### **Detailed Summary of the Educational Program, Work and Other Subject-matter Record-keeping Regulations**

---

#### **1. Regulatory Background and Authority**
These regulations are established under **subsection 29.9(2)a of the Copyright Act (S.C. 1997, c. 24, s. 18(1))**, and approved by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council** on the recommendation of the **Minister of Industry**. The regulations are issued by the **Copyright Board of Canada** and are designed to ensure proper record-keeping related to the copying of certain copyrighted works and programs—specifically for educational use.

The primary purpose is to **establish accountability and transparency** in the copying of copyrighted material by educational institutions, ensuring that royalties are accurately tracked and paid to appropriate collective societies when required.

---

#### **2. Scope of Application**
These regulations apply to two specific types of copies made under the Copyright Act:

- **Copies of news programs and news commentary programs** made under **paragraph 29.6(1)(a)** of the Act.
- **Copies of works and other subject-matter** made under **paragraph 29.7(1)(a)** of the Act.

> These include educational or research-related copies made for teaching, study, or other non-commercial purposes.

---

#### **3. Key Definitions**
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Copyright Act* of Canada. |
| **Collective Society** | A body that collects royalties on behalf of copyright holders (e.g., for music or audiovisual content), operating under an approved tariff certified under paragraph 73(1)(d) of the Act. |
| **Copy Identifier** | A unique number or reference code assigned to each copy of a program, work, or subject-matter. |
| **Educational Institution Identifier** | A unique number or reference code assigned to an educational institution. |
| **Institution** | An educational institution or a person acting under its authority. |

---

#### **4. Mandatory Record-Keeping Requirements**

##### **A. Assignment of Identifiers**
- Every institution making a copy must assign a **copy identifier** to each copy of a program, work, or subject-matter.
- A **collective society** may assign an **educational institution identifier** to an institution (used for tracking and reporting purposes).

##### **B. Marking of Copies**
- Every copy must be **marked** (on the copy itself or its container) with:
  - The **copy identifier**.
  - If applicable, the **educational institution identifier**.

> This marking ensures traceability and helps verify the origin and use of the copy.

##### **C. Completion of Information Records**
- Institutions must complete an **information record** in the prescribed format (as outlined in the Schedule) for each copy made, including:
  1. Details of the **copying** (title, duration, broadcast date/time, source broadcaster).
  2. All **public performances** of the copy where royalties are payable (under sections 29.6(2) or 29.7(2) or (3)).
  3. The **destruction** of the copy.

> The record must be completed in **legible format** and must be accurate and complete.

> **Exception**: If a copy is destroyed within **72 hours** of being made, in compliance with **section 7 of the Copyright Act**, then **subsection (2)** does not apply — no record is required for that particular copy.

---

#### **5. Destruction of Copies**
- A copy is considered destroyed when:
  - The **medium** (e.g., DVD, digital file) is physically destroyed, or
  - The copy is **erased** from the medium (e.g., deleted from a hard drive or server).

> Destruction must be documented in the information record.

---

#### **6. Reporting Obligations to Collective Societies**

##### **Initial Reporting (Within 30 Days)**
- Within **30 days** of the Copyright Board certifying a new **approved tariff** (under paragraph 73(1)(d)), the institution must send to **each collective society**:
  - A copy of **every information record** covering the period from the **date the regulations came into force** to the **date the tariff was certified**.

##### **Ongoing Annual Reporting**
- After the initial report, institutions must submit a copy of every information record (for the previous four months) by the following dates each year:
  - **January 31**
  - **May 31**
  - **September 30**

> These reports must include all entries made during the **four months prior** to the reporting date.

##### **Post-Destruction Reporting**
- Once a copy is destroyed, the institution may send the **original information record** to a collective society.
- The institution is **not required** to retain the record indefinitely.

---

#### **7. Record Retention**
- Institutions must **retain the original information record** for a period of **two years after the copy is destroyed**, **unless** they have already sent it to a collective society during that time.

> This ensures that records are preserved for audit, compliance, or royalty verification purposes.

---

#### **8. Effective Date**
- These regulations come into force on the **30th day after registration** with the federal government.

---

#### **9. Information Record Form (Summary of Key Fields)**

The form must include the following elements:

| Field | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Educational Institution Identifier (if assigned)** | Unique code assigned by a collective society. |
| **Name and Address of Institution** | Full legal name and contact details. |
| **Contact Name, Phone, Fax, Email** | For communication with collective societies. |
| **Copy Identifier** | Unique reference for the specific copy. |
| **Title of Program, Work, or Subject-matter** | Full title. |
| **Other Identifying Information** | Episode title, segment description, song titles, etc. |
| **Duration of Segment Copied** | In minutes. |
| **Date of Broadcast (yy/mm/dd)** | When the original was aired. |
| **Time of Broadcast** | Time of original broadcast. |
| **Name, Network, Call Sign or Identifier** | Source of the original content. |
| **List of Performances (with Royalties Payable)** | Date ranges for public performances (e.g., yy/mm/dd – yy/mm/dd). |
| **Certification of Destruction** | Signed statement confirming the copy has been destroyed, including: |
| &nbsp;&nbsp;• Name | Of the person certifying |
| &nbsp;&nbsp;• Title | Of the person certifying |
| &nbsp;&nbsp;• Signature | Of the certifying individual |
| &nbsp;&nbsp;• Date of Destruction (yy/mm/dd) | When destruction occurred |

> **Note**: Additional performances should be listed on a separate sheet.

---

#### **10. Key Exceptions and Limitations**
- **No record required** if a copy is destroyed within **72 hours** of being made, **provided** the destruction complies with **section 7** of the Copyright Act (which allows for temporary, non-commercial use and immediate destruction).
- **No obligation to report** if the copy is not used or performed publicly (unless royalties are payable under the Act).
- Institutions are **not required** to report records for copies that are not made or used for educational purposes.

---

#### **11. Purpose and Rationale**
These regulations serve several key functions:
- **Ensure fair royalty collection** by providing a transparent system for tracking copies and performances.
- **Support compliance** with the Copyright Act, especially regarding the licensing and payment of royalties to rights holders through collective societies.
- **Promote accountability** in educational institutions using copyrighted materials.
- **Facilitate audits** and enforcement by the Copyright Board and collective societies.

---

#### **12. Enforcement and Compliance**
- Institutions that fail to comply with these requirements may face administrative or legal consequences, including penalties or disputes over royalty payments.
- The **Copyright Board** may inspect records or conduct audits to verify compliance.
- The information provided is **confidential** and used solely for royalty tracking and compliance purposes.

---

### **Conclusion**
The *Educational Program, Work and Other Subject-matter Record-keeping Regulations* provide a structured, enforceable framework for educational institutions to responsibly copy and manage copyrighted materials for teaching and research. By requiring the assignment of identifiers, marking of copies, completion of detailed records, and timely reporting to collective societies, the regulations ensure that royalties are fairly and accurately collected when required under the Copyright Act.

These rules balance the need for **educational access** to copyrighted content with the **protection of intellectual property rights** and the **financial interests of copyright holders** through collective management systems.

--- 

✅ **Key Takeaways for Institutions**:
- Always assign a **copy identifier**.
- Mark every copy with the **copy identifier** and **institution identifier** (if applicable).
- Complete and maintain a **detailed information record** for every copy.
- Report records **within 30 days** of a new tariff approval and **annually** by January 31, May 31, and September 30.
- Destroy copies properly and **document destruction**.
- Retain records for **two years** after destruction unless sent to a collective society.

This regulation is a critical tool in maintaining compliance and fairness in the educational use of copyrighted works in Canada.
