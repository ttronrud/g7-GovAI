## ðŸ” Definition of "Independent" Director (for Paragraph 8(1)(d))

A person is **not considered independent** if they meet **any of the following conditions**, which may interfere with their ability to exercise objective, unbiased judgment:

| Condition | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| 1. **Recent director, officer, or employee role** | Has been a director, senior officer, or employee of the CPA, a member, or an affiliate of a member within the **last 3 years**. |
| 2. **Day-to-day management involvement** | Involved in the day-to-day management or direction of the CPA, a member, or an affiliate of a member. |
| 3. **Relationship with a regulated entity** | Is a director, officer, or employee of a person referred to in subsection 4(2) of the Act (e.g., a financial institution subject to regulatory oversight), or of a subsidiary or corporation controlled by that person. |
| 4. **Spousal or common-law relationship** | Is a spouse or common-law partner of a senior officer (of CPA, member, or affiliate), or resides with such a senior officer. |
| 5. **Family financial or personal relationships** | Is a family member in a financially dependent, interdependent, or personal relationship with a senior officer, where such a relationship could reasonably interfere with independent judgment. |
| 6. **Contractual or business relationship** | Has had a contractual or business relationship with the CPA, a member, or an affiliate of a member within the **last 3 years**, and such a relationship could interfere with independence. |
| 7. **Advisor or consultant relationship** | Has been an advisor, consultant, or senior officer/director of such an advisor or consultant within the last 3 years, where such a relationship could impair independence. |
| 8. **Compensation over $75,000** | Has received **any part of a calendar year (within the last 3 years)** of compensation exceeding **$75,000** from the CPA, a member, or an affiliate â€” **excluding** shares, share options, or retirement plan benefits â€” if such compensation could reasonably interfere with independent judgment. |
