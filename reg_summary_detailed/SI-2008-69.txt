**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Overpayments of Canada Education Savings Grants Remission Order**

---

**Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**  
This regulation is issued under the authority of the *Financial Administration Act*, specifically subsection **23(2.1)a**, which empowers the Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of Human Resources and Skills Development and the Treasury Board) to make orders related to the remission of overpayments in respect of certain government grants. The regulation is formally enacted under **S.C. 1991, c. 24, s. 7(2)**.

---

**Purpose of the Regulation**  
The primary purpose of this order is to **remit (i.e., cancel or forgive) overpayments** made to beneficiaries under the **Canada Education Savings Grant (CESG)** program. These overpayments occurred during a specific historical period and were made under the *Canada Education Savings Act* and the *Canada Education Savings Regulations*.

---

**Scope of the Remission**  
The remission applies to any beneficiary who received an overpayment of a Canada Education Savings Grant during the following period:

To qualify for the remission, a beneficiary must meet **all of the following conditions**:

1. **Designation as a Beneficiary under the Income Tax Act**  
   The individual must be a person designated in respect of a **Registered Education Savings Plan (RESP)** under **section 146.1 of the Income Tax Act**.

2. **Grant Paid During the Specified Period**

3. **Grant Has Not Been Repaid**  
   The overpayment must **not have been previously repaid** by the beneficiary.

**Key Implications**  
- This regulation serves as a **corrective measure** to address administrative or calculation errors in the CESG program during a defined period.  
- It provides **financial relief** to individuals who were overcharged due to technical or procedural errors, without requiring them to repay the overpayment.  
- The condition regarding minimum contributions suggests that the government is protecting the integrity of the program by ensuring that grants are only forgiven when they were not tied to valid, compliant contributions.  
- The regulation is **not retroactive beyond June 30, 2005**, and does not apply to overpayments outside the specified timeframe.
