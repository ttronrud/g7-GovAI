### **Detailed Summary of General Export Permit No. 41 – Dual-use Goods and Technology to Certain Destinations**

**Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**  
This regulation is issued by the **Minister of Foreign Affairs of Canada** under **subsection 7(1.1)a** and **10(1)b** of the *Export and Import Permits Act* (EIPA), as amended. It constitutes a **General Export Permit (GEP-41)** that authorizes the export or transfer of specific dual-use goods and technologies to designated countries.

- **Statutory References**:
  - *S.C. 2004, c. 15, s. 56* – Original enabling provision for general export permits.
  - *S.C. 2006, c. 13, s. 113* – Amendment expanding authority.
  - *R.S., c. E-19* – Current version of the Export and Import Permits Act.

- **Date of Issue**: July 15, 2015  
- **Effective Date**: Upon registration with the Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development (DFATD)

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Eligible Destination** | A list of 35 countries, including major industrial and allied nations such as Australia, Austria, Belgium, Canada’s key trading partners, EU members, Japan, South Korea, and the United States. These are the only countries to which this permit applies. |
| **Export Controls Division (ECD)** | The Canadian government body responsible for reviewing and overseeing export compliance under the EIPA. Responsible for receiving reports, verifying compliance, and enforcing restrictions. |
| **Guide** | Refers to the *Export Control List* (ECL), which categorizes goods and technologies by their potential for military or strategic use. The term "Guide" here has the same meaning as defined in Section 1 of the ECL. |
| **Composite, Development, Matrix, Production, Software, User-accessible Programmability** | These technical terms are defined in the ECL under Groups 1 and 2 and are used to describe specific characteristics of goods or technologies that may affect their classification and export restrictions. |

---

## **Authorized Exports Under This Permit**

Under this permit, any **resident of Canada** may export or transfer:

1. **Any item listed in Group 1 of the Export Control List (ECL)**  
   → This includes a broad range of dual-use goods and technologies (e.g., components, equipment, software) that have both civilian and military applications.

2. **Specific items in the ECL under sub-items 5504.2.a to 5504.2.g**  
   → These are specific technologies related to surveillance, communications, or defense systems. Examples may include certain sensors, signal processing equipment, or data transmission systems.

> ⚠️ **Important Note**: This permit does **not** cover all dual-use items — only those explicitly referenced in Group 1 or in items 5504.2.a–g.

---

## **Prohibited Exports and Restrictions**

This permit **does not authorize** exports or transfers of goods or technologies if any of the following conditions apply:

### 1. **Destination Restrictions**
- To a country **not on the eligible destination list**.
- To a country listed in the **Area Control List** (e.g., countries with significant human rights violations or security concerns).
- To a country subject to sanctions under:
  - The *Special Economic Measures Act* (SEMA) — e.g., countries or entities designated due to human rights or terrorism concerns.
  - The *United Nations Act* — e.g., countries or entities under UN sanctions.

### 2. **End-Use Restrictions**
- Goods or technologies intended for use in a **non-eligible destination**.
- Goods or technologies listed in the **Schedule to this Permit** (which may include specific prohibited items or applications).
- Goods or technologies referenced in **any item of the Guide except those in Section 2** — unless **additional authorization** is obtained from another permit under subsection 7(1.1) of the EIPA.

### 3. **Specific Prohibited Technologies**
- **Items 5504.2.h and 5504.2.i** of the Guide (which relate to advanced surveillance or data processing technologies) are **prohibited** if:
  - The software is **specially designed or modified** for development or use of these technologies.
  - The technology is **specially designed or modified** for the development or production of these technologies.

> ⚠️ This means even if the goods themselves are exported, **customized software or specialized technology** for these specific items cannot be exported.

### 4. **Range and Application Restrictions**
- No export or transfer is allowed for:
  - Rocket systems or unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) with a **range of 300 km or greater**.
  - Any goods or technologies intended for the **development, production, or use** of such systems.

## **Compliance Requirements for Exporters**

All Canadian residents exporting or transferring goods under this permit must meet strict reporting and record-keeping obligations.

### 1. **Initial Registration (Before First Export in a Calendar Year)**
Must provide written information to the Export Controls Division (ECD), including:
- Name, address, phone, fax, and email of the exporter.
- If a corporation:
  - Business number (from the Minister of National Revenue),
  - Name and contact details (address, phone, fax, email) of a designated contact person.

### 2. **Periodic Reporting (Every Six Months)**
Within **30 days** after each six-month period ending on:
- **January 31** or **July 31**,  
Must submit a report containing:
- Name, address, phone, fax, and email of each **consignee** (recipient).
- If the consignee is a corporation:
  - Name and title of a contact person who knows about the export.
  - Contact details (phone, fax, email).
- Description of each good or technology exported.
- Item number from the Guide (e.g., 5504.2.a).
- Quantity and value of each export, **by country of destination**.

### 3. **Response to Requests**
Within **15 days** of receiving a request from ECD, must provide:
- Records related to the export/transfer as specified in Section 5 (e.g., invoices, shipping documents, contracts).

### 4. **End-Use Certification Requirement**
Before any export or transfer, the exporter must obtain a **written statement from each consignee** that includes:
- The **country of end use** of the goods or technology.
- Whether the consignee is:
  - An **end-user** (direct recipient),
  - A **distributor**, or
  - If neither, a **description of their role** in the supply chain.

> This ensures accountability and helps prevent diversion to prohibited uses.

### 5. **Record Retention**
All exporters must retain records for **six years** after the year in which the export or transfer occurred. Records must include:
- Date of export/transfer
- Name and address of each consignee
- Quantity and value of goods transferred
- Name, part number, unique identifier, and technical specifications of the good or technology
- Item number from the Guide and a **comparison of technical specifications** between the Guide and the actual product
- Copy of the **end-use statement** obtained from the consignee
- Copy of any relevant **contract, invoice, or shipping document** (if available)

---

## **Labeling and Reporting Requirements**

- If the exported good is subject to reporting under the **Customs Act**, the exporter must **insert "GEP-41" or "LGE-41"** in the appropriate field of the customs form.  
  → "GEP" = General Export Permit; "LGE" = License for General Export (used in French version).

---

## **Cancellation Clause**

- The permit includes **cancellation clauses** (marked as "[Cancellation]") — indicating that it may be revoked or amended in the future if circumstances change (e.g., new sanctions, changes in security threats).  
- However, no specific cancellation date or trigger is provided in this version.

---

## **Effective Date**

- The permit comes into force on the day it is **registered** with the appropriate government authority.

---

## **Summary of Key Points**

| Feature | Summary |
|--------|---------|
| **Purpose** | To allow Canadian exporters to freely export dual-use goods and certain technologies to 35 eligible countries. |
| **Scope** | Covers Group 1 of the ECL and specific items 5504.2.a to 5504.2.g. |
| **Eligible Countries** | Includes EU nations, Japan, South Korea, U.S., Australia, and others. |
| **Prohibited Uses** | No exports to non-eligible countries, no UAVs or rockets with >300 km range, no specialized software/technology for items 5504.2.h/i. |
| **Compliance Requirements** | Mandatory initial registration, biannual reporting, end-use certification, and 6-year record retention. |
| **Enforcement** | Monitored by the Export Controls Division; failure to comply may result in penalties or revocation. |

---
