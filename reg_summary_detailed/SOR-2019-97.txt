**Detailed Summary of the *Corded Window Coverings Regulations* (S.C. 2016, c. 9, s. 67)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Corded Window Coverings Regulations* are a set of safety standards introduced under the *Canada Consumer Product Safety Act (CCPSA)*. These regulations were enacted by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Health, pursuant to **section 37a of the CCPSA**, which empowers the government to establish safety requirements for consumer products.

The primary objective of these regulations is to **prevent strangulation hazards** associated with corded window coverings‚Äîparticularly those that pose a risk to young children. The regulations apply to all indoor window coverings (such as blinds, shades, or curtains) that are equipped with at least one cord.

These rules were **repealed and replaced** by the *Corded Window Coverings Regulations* in 2016, and they came into force on the **second anniversary of their publication in the Canada Gazette, Part II** (effective May 1, 2021).

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Cord**  
   - Any component that can fold in every direction when tension is removed.  
   - Includes bands, ropes, straps, strings, chains, wires, or combinations of such components connected end-to-end.  
   - The definition emphasizes flexibility and potential for entanglement.

2. **Corded Window Covering**  
   - An indoor window covering that has **at least one cord** attached to it.

3. **Loop**  
   - A shape formed by a reachable cord that creates a **completely bounded opening** (e.g., a circular or oval shape formed by a cord).  
   - A loop is considered hazardous if it can be accessed by a child and is large enough to pose a strangulation risk.

4. **Reachable**  
   - Refers to any part of a cord that a person (especially a child) can physically touch when the window covering is installed‚Äîregardless of whether it is fully open, closed, or in between positions.

### **Safety Requirements**

#### 1. **Detachment Resistance for Small Parts**
- Every part of a corded window covering that is:
  - Accessible to a child, and  
  - Small enough to fit entirely within a **small parts cylinder** (as illustrated in *Schedule 1*)  
  must be **affixed securely** to the covering.
- The part must **not detach** when subjected to a force of **90 N** applied in any direction.

> üîç *Purpose*: Prevents small detachable parts (e.g., plastic clips, beads, or small components) from becoming loose and posing a choking hazard.

---

#### 2. **Lead Content Limit**
- All **external components** of corded window coverings must not contain more than **90 mg/kg of lead**.
- Testing must follow the **OECD Principles of Good Laboratory Practice (ENV/MC/CHEM(98)17)**, specifically the English version dated January 21, 1998, and the French version dated March 6, 1998.

> üîç *Purpose*: Limits toxic lead exposure, which can be harmful, especially to children.

---

#### 3. **Non-Reachability of Cords**
- A cord that is **not reachable** (i.e., out of a child‚Äôs reach) must **remain unreachable** throughout the entire useful life of the product‚Äîwhether the window covering is fully open, closed, or in any intermediate position.

> üîç *Purpose*: Prevents children from accessing cords during normal use or accidental movement.

---

#### 4. **Maximum Cord Length (Reachable Cords)**
- **Cord with one free end**:
  - Must not exceed **22 cm** in length when pulled gradually with a force of **35 N**.
- **Cord with no free end** (e.g., looped or fixed):
  - Must not exceed **22 cm** between two consecutive contact points under the same 35 N pull.

> üîç *Purpose*: Reduces the risk of a child being able to grasp and pull a long cord, which could lead to entanglement or strangulation.

---

#### 5. **Loop Size Limit**
- When a reachable cord is pulled with **35 N**, the **perimeter of any loop** (existing, created, or enlarged) must not exceed **44 cm**.

> üîç *Purpose*: Prevents the formation of large loops that could trap a child‚Äôs neck or head.

---

#### 6. **Connection of Two Reachable Cords**
- If two cords (each with one free end) can be connected end-to-end after being pulled with 35 N:
  - The **combined length** must not exceed **22 cm**.
  - The **perimeter of the resulting loop** must not exceed **44 cm**.

> üîç *Purpose*: Ensures even if cords are joined, the total length and loop size remain safe.

---

### **Labeling and Information Requirements**

All required information must be:

- **In both English and French** (bilingual compliance).
- **Legible, prominent, and clearly displayed**.
- **High contrast** between text and background (e.g., black text on white background).
- **Visible throughout the product‚Äôs useful life** under normal transportation, storage, sale, and use.
- **Indelibly printed** on the product itself or on a **permanently affixed label**.

#### **Typeface Requirements**
- Must use a **standard sans-serif font**.
- Must be:
  - **Not compressed, expanded, or decorative**.
  - Have a **large x-height** relative to ascenders or descenders (as shown in *Schedule 2*).
  - Measured using uppercase or lowercase letters with ascenders or descenders (e.g., ‚Äúb‚Äù or ‚Äúp‚Äù).

#### **Minimum Font Sizes**
- **"Warning" and "Mise en garde"**:
  - Must be in **bold, uppercase**, and **not less than 5 mm** in height.
- **All other required information**:
  - Must be in **type not less than 2.5 mm** in height.

### **Instructions Required**

Every corded window covering must include instructions in text, drawings, or photographs (or a combination):

1. **Assembly instructions** (if sold unassembled) including a **quantitative list of parts**.
2. **Installation instructions**.
3. **Operating instructions** (how to open/close, adjust, etc.).

> üîç *Purpose*: Empowers consumers to install and use the product safely.

---
