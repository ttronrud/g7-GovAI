**Detailed Summary of the Canada Gazette (1978) Special Issue Regulations**

---

### **Overview**

The *Canada Gazette (1978) Special Issue Regulations* are a set of administrative rules issued by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Justice, under specific provisions of the *Statutory Instruments Act*. These regulations govern the publication and distribution of a special issue of **Part II of the Canada Gazette**, which is published in conjunction with the *Consolidated Regulations of Canada, 1978*.

This special issue is not a routine publication but a targeted, official release of consolidated federal regulations, intended to ensure timely and accessible dissemination to key government officials, legal institutions, and parliamentary bodies.

---

### **Key Provisions and Interpretations**

#### 1. **Definition of "Special Issue"**
- The term *"special issue"* refers specifically to the publication of **Part II of the Canada Gazette** (which contains the consolidated federal regulations) issued **on the order of the Clerk of the Privy Council**, under **Section 13 of the Statutory Instruments Regulations**.
- This special issue is distinct from regular gazette publications and is designed to provide a comprehensive, official version of federal regulations for specific stakeholders.

#### 2. **Free Distribution to Designated Individuals and Offices (Exception to Standard Charges)**

**Notwithstanding subsection 18(2) of the Statutory Instruments Regulations**, which typically requires a fee for copies of statutory instruments, **copies of the special issue are provided free of charge** to the following categories of persons:

##### **A. Persons holding offices specified in Schedule I**
- These are senior federal officials and key government appointees.
- Examples include:
  - The Governor General
  - The Prime Minister
  - The Speaker of the Senate
  - The Speaker of the House of Commons
  - The President of the Privy Council
  - The Minister of Justice
  - The Leader of the Opposition in the Senate
  - The Leader of the Opposition in the House of Commons
  - The Chief Justice of Canada
  - The Chief Justice of the Federal Court of Canada
  - The Associate Chief Justice of the Federal Court of Canada
  - The Clerk of the Privy Council
  - The Deputy Minister of Justice
  - The Assistant Clerk of the Privy Council
  - The Registrar of Statutory Instruments
  - Members of the Statute Revision Commission
  - Chairpersons or Co-Chairpersons of Committees established under Section 26 of the Statutory Instruments Act

> **Purpose**: Ensures that top-level policymakers, legal leaders, and administrative heads have immediate access to official regulatory content for decision-making and oversight.

##### **B. Persons holding offices specified in Schedule II (for use during office tenure)**
- These individuals receive the special issue **while they are actively holding the office**.
- This provision ensures continuity of access during their term of office, even if they are not currently in active duty or are on leave.

##### **C. Persons holding offices or performing functions specified in Schedule III (on request)**
- These individuals may **request** a copy of the special issue **for use while holding office or performing duties**.
- This includes:
  - Members of Parliament
  - Officials of the Senate and House of Commons directly involved in the **administration, review, or scrutiny** of federal regulations
  - The Parliamentary Librarian
  - The Librarian and Archivist of Canada
  - Chief librarians of:
    - The United Nations
    - Provincial legislatures and territorial councils
    - Law schools in Canada
    - Courthouses in Canada
    - Universities in Canada
    - Public libraries in Canada

> **Purpose**: To support legislative scrutiny, legal research, public accountability, and institutional knowledge management.

---

#### 3. **Commercial Sale of Volumes (with a fee)**

**Notwithstanding Section 19 of the Statutory Instruments Regulations**, which normally prohibits commercial sale of statutory instruments, **each volume of the special issue may be sold to any person for $34.50**.

> **Purpose**: To allow broader public access and facilitate research, academic study, or private use, while still generating revenue to support the production and dissemination of official publications.

---

#### 4. **Reciprocal Arrangements with Other Countries**

The regulations also include a provision allowing for **reciprocal distribution arrangements** with other countries:

- If **reciprocal agreements exist** between Canada and another country for the **free distribution of government publications**, then:
  - Members of that country’s government
  - Officials of its legislatures
  - University and institutional representatives

> **May receive copies of the special issue free of charge**, provided such reciprocal arrangements are in place.

> **Purpose**: Promotes international cooperation in the sharing of regulatory and legislative information, especially among allied or partner nations.

---

### **Legal Basis and Authority**

- The regulations are made under:
  - **Subsection 13(2)** of the *Statutory Instruments Act* – authorizing the issuance of statutory instruments based on recommendations.
  - **Paragraph 27(m)** of the *Statutory Instruments Act* – which permits special provisions for the publication of gazette issues when necessary for public service or administrative efficiency.

These provisions allow for a flexible and targeted approach to regulation dissemination, balancing accessibility with administrative practicality.

---

### **Significance and Implications**

1. **Enhanced Access for Key Stakeholders**  
   The free distribution ensures that senior officials, judges, legislators, and legal institutions receive timely and accurate information on federal regulations—critical for effective governance, judicial review, and legislative oversight.

2. **Promotion of Transparency and Accountability**  
   By making the regulations accessible to Members of Parliament and parliamentary officials, the regulations support parliamentary scrutiny and democratic accountability.

3. **Support for Legal and Research Institutions**  
   The inclusion of chief librarians from universities, law schools, and courts ensures that legal professionals and researchers can access up-to-date regulatory content, supporting academic and judicial work.

4. **International Cooperation**  
   The reciprocal distribution clause reflects Canada’s commitment to international standards in government transparency and information sharing.

5. **Commercial Viability**  
   The $34.50 sale price provides a sustainable funding mechanism for the production and distribution of the special issue, without compromising access for essential public and governmental users.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Canada Gazette (1978) Special Issue Regulations* establish a structured, equitable, and transparent system for the dissemination of consolidated federal regulations. By providing **free access** to key government and legal institutions and allowing **limited commercial sale**, the regulations ensure both **operational efficiency** and **public accountability**. They represent a targeted application of statutory authority to improve access to critical legal information in a democratic and institutionalized manner.

These regulations are a foundational element in Canada’s regulatory transparency framework, ensuring that those who need the regulations most—judges, legislators, administrators, and researchers—can access them promptly and without financial barrier.
