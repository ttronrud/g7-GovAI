**Detailed Summary of the By-laws of the College of Patent Agents and Trademark Agents**

---

### **Overview**

The By-laws of the College of Patent Agents and Trademark Agents (the "College") were adopted under specific provisions of the *College of Patent Agents and Trademark Agents Act* and related regulations, including sections 75a(2)b, 87c, 88c, and section 18 of the College’s Regulations (SOR/2021-129), effective as of **April 4, 2023**. These by-laws establish the internal governance, operational procedures, financial oversight, director and committee elections, licensing processes, and professional conduct standards for the College.

They serve as a comprehensive framework to ensure accountability, transparency, professional integrity, and effective governance in the regulation of patent agents and trademark agents in Canada.

---

## **I. Key Definitions**

The by-laws begin with a clear set of definitions essential to understanding the rules:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | The *College of Patent Agents and Trademark Agents Act* |
| **CEO** | Chief Executive Officer, appointed by the Board under section 23 of the Act |
| **CIPO** | Canadian Intellectual Property Office (includes Patent Office and Registrar of Trademarks) |
| **Class 1 Licence** | Standard licence (patent or trademark agent) not restricted by section 47 |
| **Class 2 Licence** | Licence subject to restrictions under section 47 (e.g., limited practice or supervision) |
| **Class 3 Licence** | Licence for individuals in training (patent or trademark agent in training) |
| **Class 4 Licence** | Licence issued to someone not practising publicly or employed in the profession |
| **Code** | Code of Professional Conduct for Licensees under section 33(1) of the Act |
| **Foreign Practitioner** | Individual described in subsections 19(1) or 20(1) of the Regulations |
| **Professional Regulatory Body** | A statutory body regulating another profession (not under the Act) |
| **Register** | Register of Patent Agents or Trademark Agents under sections 28 and 31 of the Act |
| **Regulations** | The College of Patent Agents and Trademark Agents Regulations |
| **Supervisor** | Authorized individual who supervises a Class 3 licensee |

## **II. Definitions of Professional Standards**

Two key professional standards are defined:

- **Incompetence**: Failure to apply relevant knowledge, skills, or judgment in a manner consistent with the standards in the *Code of Professional Conduct* while practising as a patent or trademark agent.

- **Professional Misconduct**: Conduct inconsistent with the Code that would reasonably be regarded as unprofessional or discredit the profession. Includes:
  - Contravening the Act, Regulations, By-laws, or Code;
  - Behaviour that brings discredit to the profession or undermines public confidence;
  - Conviction or guilty plea to an offence listed in paragraph 73(d) of the Act;
  - Being found by a professional regulatory body to have committed professional misconduct, incompetence, incapacity, or unfitness to practise.

---

## **III. Fiscal Year and Meeting Procedures**

- The **fiscal year** runs from **January 1 to December 31** each year.

### **Annual General Meeting (AGM)**
- The CEO must give **public notice at least 60 days** before the AGM, including:
  - Date, means of attendance, and location (if applicable).
- The **agenda must include**:
  1. CEO’s presentation of the annual report and audited financial statements;
  2. Chairperson’s address to the meeting;
  3. Questions from licensees to the Board;
  4. Any additional matters added by the Board.

---

## **IV. Board of Directors’ Duties**

The Board is responsible for the strategic and financial governance of the College. Key duties include:

### **Financial Oversight**
- Appoint an **independent auditor** annually and approve audited financial statements.
- Monitor financial performance and ensure **multi-year financial planning**.
- Designate a **bank** (from Schedule I of the Bank Act) for the College’s banking needs.
- Approve **annual budgets** before each fiscal year.
- Establish an **investment policy** and appoint an investment dealer (on CEO’s recommendation).
- Approve **terms of reference and skills matrix** for committees.

### **Policy and Governance Support**
- Consult with working groups established to assist in fulfilling Board duties.
- Ensure compliance with all legal and regulatory requirements.

### **CEO Oversight**
- Provide **direction and performance expectations**.
- Conduct **regular performance reviews** and implement **succession planning**.
- Establish **working groups** to support the CEO.
- Monitor the CEO’s effectiveness in engaging with members and the public.

---

## **V. Director Compensation and Tenure**

- **Remuneration** for directors is set out in **Schedule 2**.
- Directors are entitled to **reimbursement of reasonable expenses** incurred in performing duties.
- **Term of office**:
  - Begins at the conclusion of the first AGM after election.
  - Ends on the **earlier of**:
    - The day after the **third anniversary** of the start date; or
    - The conclusion of the **fourth AGM** after election.
- The Board must **publish the election process** on the College’s website.

### **Balance of Representation**
- The number of directors is **divided evenly** between patent agents and trademark agents.

### **Vacant Director Positions**
- The CEO annually determines if any director positions will be vacant at the next AGM.
- If so, an election must be held **before** the meeting to fill the required number.

---

## **VI. Election Process for Directors**

### **Election Commissioner**
- The Board appoints an **Election Commissioner** (on CEO’s recommendation) to resolve disputes during the election process, including:
  - Disputes over nominee ineligibility;
  - Other election-related disputes.
- A ruling by the Election Commissioner is **final and not reviewable** by the Board.

### **Eligibility Criteria for Directors (Section 14 of the Act and By-laws)**
An individual is **not eligible** to be elected if:
1. In the **past five years**, they were found by the Discipline Committee to have committed professional misconduct or been deemed incompetent;
2. In the past five years, they were found by a **professional regulatory body** to have committed misconduct or been incompetent;
3. They are the subject of an action under subsection 37.1(1) of the Act (Investigations Committee);
4. They are the subject of an application to the Discipline Committee under subsection 49(1) of the Act;
5. They have already served as a director for **six years**, and less than two years have passed since the end of that term;
6. They are employed by a **department under the Financial Administration Act**;
7. They have **not provided a conflict-of-interest declaration** to the CEO;
8. They have **not attended an orientation** for prospective candidates.

> **Conflict of Interest**: No director may be appointed or elected if it would create a conflict of interest.

### **Nomination and Election Process**
- The CEO must **confirm the eligibility** of each nominee.
- Any dispute about eligibility is referred to the **Election Commissioner**, who makes a decision based on the CEO’s reasons and the nominee’s submission.

### **Election Outcome**
- If, at the end of the nominations period, the number of eligible nominees is **less than or equal to** the number of vacant positions, the CEO declares those nominees elected and notifies them and members.

### **Withdrawal**
- A candidate must **withdraw in writing** to the CEO.

### **Voting Rights**
- Only licensees whose licences are **not suspended** are eligible to vote.
- Voting is conducted **electronically**.

### **Dispute of Election Results**
- A candidate may file a **notice of dispute** with the Election Commissioner within **five days** of the election results being published online.
- The Election Commissioner must hold an inquiry if there are **reasonable grounds** to doubt the validity of the process.
- The Commissioner prepares a **written report** and submits it to the Board.
- The Board may then:
  - **Declare the result valid**; or
  - **Declare it invalid** and either:
    - Elect another candidate; or
    - Order a new election.
> **Note**: A minor procedural defect does **not** invalidate the election.

---

## **VII. Removal of Directors**

Directors may be removed by resolution if:
- A motion is made and **at least two-thirds of directors present** vote in favour;
- Notice of the motion is provided to the Board and the director **at least two weeks** before the vote.

### **Removal of Appointed Directors**
- The Board may request the **Minister** to remove an appointed director for cause under the same conditions.

### **Automatic Ineligibility**
- An elected director who is absent from **two consecutive Board meetings** is deemed ineligible under subparagraph 17(h)(iii) of the Act.

---

- **Quorum**: Five directors are required.
