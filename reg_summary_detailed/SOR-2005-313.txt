## üîç **Comprehensive Summary of the Commercial Vehicle Drivers Hours of Service Regulations**

### üìå **Background and Legal Authority**
These regulations were made under **section 16.1 of the Motor Vehicle Transport Act, 1987a**, following:
- Consultation with provincial governments (pursuant to subsection 3(1) of the Act),
- Public consultation and opportunity for stakeholder input (via publication in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*),
- Amendments to the Act (e.g., S.C. 2001, c. 13).

The regulations establish **mandatory limits on driving and on-duty time** for commercial vehicle drivers to enhance **road safety**, prevent driver fatigue, and ensure compliance with federal and provincial transportation standards.

## üö´ **Exemptions (When Regulations Do Not Apply)**

The following commercial vehicles are **exempt** from these HOS rules:
1. **Farm, forest, sea, or lake transport vehicles**:
   - Used to transport primary products (e.g., crops, timber, fish) **if the driver or carrier is the producer**.
   - Return trips after transporting such products (if empty or carrying only operational supplies).
2. **Emergency vehicles** (e.g., ambulances, fire trucks).
3. **Vehicles providing relief during public welfare emergencies** (as defined in the *Emergencies Act*).

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note: These exemptions are limited and apply only when the vehicle is used in direct connection with the producer‚Äôs operations.*

## üö® **Prohibited Driving Conditions**

No motor carrier may request or allow a driver to drive, and no driver may drive if:
- Driving would **jeopardize public safety, health, or employee safety**;
- The driver is subject to an **out-of-service declaration**;
- The driver would **violate any HOS regulation**.

## üöó **Daily Driving and On-Duty Time Limits**

### 1. **Daily Driving Time Limit**
- A driver may not drive more than **13 hours in a single day**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Exception: If the driver takes at least 8 consecutive hours of off-duty time before driving again.*

### 2. **Daily On-Duty Time Limit**
- A driver may not be on-duty for more than **14 hours in a day**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Exception: If the driver takes at least 8 consecutive hours of off-duty time before driving again.*

### 3. **Minimum Off-Duty Time Requirement**
- A driver must take **at least 10 hours of off-duty time per day**.
- This includes:
  - **8 consecutive hours** of off-duty time (mandatory),
  - **At least 2 additional hours** of off-duty time that are **not part of the 8-hour block**.

### 4. **Distribution of Off-Duty Time**
- Off-duty time (excluding the mandatory 8-hour block) may be distributed in **blocks of at least 30 minutes**.
- The total off-duty time must be **at least 10 hours** per day.

## üîÑ **Deferral of Off-Duty Time (Limited Exception)**

A driver may **defer up to 2 hours of off-duty time** to the next day **only if**:
- The deferred time is **not part of the mandatory 8-hour block**;
- Total off-duty time over two days is **at least 20 hours**;
- The deferred time is **added to the 8-hour block on the second day**;
- Total driving time over two days does **not exceed 26 hours**;
- A clear **declaration in the record of duty status** confirms the deferral and specifies whether the driver is operating under Day 1 or Day 2.

> ‚ùó This deferral is **not allowed** for the mandatory 8-hour block.

## üö¢ **Ferry Crossings (Over 5 Hours)**

For drivers crossing a ferry that takes **more than 5 hours**, the **mandatory 8-hour off-duty block** is **waived** if:
- The time spent resting in the sleeper berth (at terminal, on ferry, or at a rest stop within 25 km of disembarkation) **totals at least 8 hours**;
- The rest time is recorded as **off-duty time in the sleeper berth**;
- The driver retains a **receipt** for ferry and rest accommodation fees;
- The receipt **matches** the entry in the record of duty status.

> ‚úÖ This applies only to rest in sleeper berths ‚Äî not general off-duty time.
