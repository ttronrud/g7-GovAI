### **Detailed Summary of the Canadian Aviation Security Regulations, 2012**

The **Canadian Aviation Security Regulations, 2012** (the "Regulations") were enacted by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Transport**, under specific provisions of the **Aeronautics Act** (particularly sections 4.71a, 4.9b, 7.6(1)(a)c and (b)c, and 7.7d). These Regulations serve as the **principal regulatory framework** for aviation security in Canada, designed to **prevent, detect, respond to, and recover from acts or attempted acts of unlawful interference with civil aviation**.

---

## **1. Purpose and Legislative Context**

- The Regulations **supplement the legislative framework** established in Sections 4.7 to 4.87 of the Aeronautics Act.
- Their primary objectives are:
  - To **enhance preparedness** for unlawful interference (e.g., terrorism, sabotage).
  - To **facilitate detection, prevention, response, and recovery** from such threats.
- The Regulations are **not standalone**; they are **supplemented over time** by:
  - Other aviation security regulations
  - Security measures
  - Interim orders
  - Ministerial orders
  - Emergency directions

| Part | Subject Matter |
|------|----------------|

| **Part 4** | Security at aerodromes listed in Schedule 1 (high-risk or designated airports) |
| **Part 5** | Security at aerodromes listed in Schedule 2 (medium-risk or specific categories) |
| **Part 6** | Security at aerodromes listed in Schedule 3 and at any other place designated by the Minister |
| **Part 7** | Security at other aerodromes (non-designated) |
| **Part 8** | Aircraft security (including aircraft protection and crew safety) |
| **Part 9** | Reserved |
| **Part 10** | Reserved |
| **Part 11** | Cargo and mail on flights departing from Canadian aerodromes |
| **Part 12** | Cargo on flights departing from outside Canada to a Canadian aerodrome |
| **Part 13** | Ministerial powers and duties (e.g., emergency powers, enforcement) |
| **Part 14** | Administrative monetary penalties for violations |

> *Note: Parts 9 and 10 are reserved, indicating future potential use.*

## **4. Part 1: Screening Officers, Authorities, and Persons Subject to Screening**

This part establishes **strict requirements** for screening officers, screening authorities, and individuals subject to screening. It supplements **Section 4.85 of the Aeronautics Act**, which prohibits certain screening practices.

### **Applicability**
- Applies to:
  - Aerodromes listed in the **CATSA Aerodrome Designation Regulations**
  - Any other location designated by the **Minister under subsection 6(1.1)** of the CATSA Act

### **Requirements for Screening Officers**

A screening officer must meet **all of the following conditions** to be permitted to screen persons or goods:

1. **Age**: At least 18 years old  
2. **Citizenship/Residency**: Must be a Canadian citizen or permanent resident (as defined in the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act*)  
3. **Language Ability**: Must be able to communicate effectively in **one or both official languages (English/French)**  
4. **Security Clearance**: Must have a valid security clearance  
5. **Training**: Must have completed:
   - **Initial training** under **Section 20**
   - **Ongoing training** under **Section 23** (if applicable)
6. **Evaluation**: Must successfully complete an evaluation under **Section 22** (and subsection 23(4), if applicable), demonstrating competence in screening duties  
7. **Certification**: Must be certified by the screening authority as meeting requirements under paragraphs (a) to (f)  
8. **Designation**: Must be officially designated by the **Minister under Section 4.84**

> *Note: These requirements ensure that only qualified, trained, and accountable personnel perform screening.*
