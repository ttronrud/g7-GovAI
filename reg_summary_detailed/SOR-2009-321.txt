## üö® **Scope of Application**
- Applies to **all domestic ferries and ferry facilities** listed in **Schedules 1 and 2**.
- **Does NOT apply** to ferries that are:
  - Dismantled
  - Laid-up (not in active service)

> This ensures the regulations focus on **operational, active ferry services**.

## üèóÔ∏è **Part 1: General Matters**

### 1.1 **Obligation of Operators**
- Every operator (ferry or facility) must ensure compliance with all provisions of these regulations.

### 1.2 **Maintenance of MARSEC Levels**
- Operators must maintain **MARSEC Level 1 at all times**.
- A higher MARSEC level (Level 2 or 3) may be required **only if the Minister issues a formal security measure** under **Section 7 of the MTSA**, based on assessed threats.

### 1.3 **Prohibition on Tampering with Security Systems**
- No person may:
  - Tamper with
  - Damage
  - Disrupt the normal operation of
  Any security equipment or system covered by these regulations.

> This is a strict legal prohibition to protect critical infrastructure.

## üìÑ **Part 2: Security Documentation Requirements**

### 2.1 **Operational Requirement for Ferries**
> **No domestic ferry may operate without one of the following:**
- An **Interim Domestic Ferry Security Certificate** issued within the **previous 90 days**, OR
- A **Security Certificate** issued under paragraph 19(1)(a)

#### Exceptions (Grace Periods):
- **Routes in Part 1 of Schedule 1**: No requirement before **January 15, 2010**
- **Routes in Parts 2‚Äì4 of Schedule 1**: No requirement before **April 1, 2010**

#### Deemed Compliance:
An operator is considered compliant if the ferry holds:
- A valid **International Ship Security Certificate (ISSC)** or **interim ISSC** (under MTSA s. 202(1)/(3))
- A valid **Canadian Vessel Security Certificate** (issued under s. 202(2)) or **interim Canadian Vessel Security Certificate** (s. 202(3))

> This allows a transition period to align with international standards.

### 2.2 **Operational Requirement for Ferry Facilities**
> **No ferry facility may operate without a Statement of Compliance** issued under paragraph 19(1)(b)

#### Exceptions:
- **Facilities in Part 1 of Schedule 2**: No requirement before **January 15, 2010**
- **Facilities in Parts 2‚Äì4 of Schedule 2**: No requirement before **April 1, 2010**

#### Deemed Compliance:
Operator is deemed compliant if the Minister has issued a **Letter of Approval** under s. 352(1) of the MTSA.

---

### 2.3 **Procedure for Issuing Security Documentation**

#### For Domestic Ferries:
1. Submit a **security assessment report** (s. 15(1))
2. Receive a **Security Assessment Letter of Approval** (s. 16(1))
3. Submit a **security plan** (s. 17(1))
4. Receive an **Interim Domestic Ferry Security Certificate** (s. 18(1))
5. Receive a **Security Certificate** (s. 19(1)(a))

#### For Domestic Ferry Facilities:
1. Submit a **security assessment report** (s. 15(2))
2. Receive a **Security Assessment Letter of Approval** (s. 16(2))
3. Submit a **security plan** (s. 17(2))
4. Receive a **Statement of Compliance** (s. 19(1)(b))

> All submissions must be **signed in ink** by the operator.

---

### 2.4 **Approval Criteria for Security Assessments**
- The Minister will approve a security assessment if it:
  - Meets the requirements of **Sections 66 to 70** of the MTSA
  - Includes comprehensive analysis and recommendations

> These sections cover risk assessment, threat identification, vulnerability analysis, and mitigation strategies.

---

### 2.5 **Approval Criteria for Security Plans**
- The Minister will approve a security plan if it:
  - Addresses the **analysis and recommendations** from the approved assessment
  - Meets the requirements of **Sections 71 to 73** (for ferries) or **71, 72, 74** (for facilities)
  - Contains adequate systems to:
    - Detect security threats
    - Prevent security breaches
    - Respond to security incidents

> The plan must be **practical, realistic, and operationally feasible**.

---

### 2.6 **Validity Period of Approved Security Plans**
- Valid for a period **not exceeding five years** from the date of approval.
- The Minister determines the validity period based on:
  - **Ferry operations and industry context**
  - **Ports of call and usual routes**
  - **Operator‚Äôs security record**
  - **Complexity of the security plan and procedures**

> This allows for periodic review and adaptation to changing conditions.
