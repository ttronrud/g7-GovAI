**Detailed Summary of the Rules of the Court of Appeal of Quebec in Criminal Matters (Effective January 1, 2019)**

---

### **Overview and Legal Basis**

On May 23, 2018, the judges of the **Court of Appeal of Quebec** unanimously adopted and approved the updated **Rules of the Court of Appeal of Quebec in Criminal Matters**, replacing the previous version (SI/2006-142). These rules came into force on **January 1, 2019**, pursuant to **section 482 and 482.1 of the Criminal Code (R.S.C. 1985, c. C-46)**. 

The rules are **supplementary to the Criminal Code**, meaning they do not create new legal rights or obligations but instead provide procedural guidance. They are to be **interpreted and applied in accordance with the principles and provisions of the Code**.

---

### **Key Definitions**

The rules establish clear definitions of key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Authorities** | Includes statutory texts, regulations, case law, legal doctrine, or extracts thereof. |
| **Brief** | A document containing an argument and three schedules (mémoire). |
| **Chief Justice** | The head of the Court of Appeal (Juge en chef). |
| **Clerk** | A public servant appointed under the *Courts of Justice Act* (CQLR, c. T-16) to serve the Court (Greffier). |
| **Court** | A panel of three judges; may be increased by the Chief Justice. |
| **Facilitation Conference** | A judge-presided meeting with counsel to seek partial or definitive resolution of an appeal. |
| **Fast-Track** | A streamlined appeal process without formal briefs, conducted within a reduced time frame. |
| **Judge** | A judge of the Court of Appeal. |
| **Management Conference** | A conference where a judge presides to define issues in dispute and simplify proceedings. |
| **Motion** | A written request submitted to the Court, a judge, or the clerk. |
| **Offices of the Court** | Two physical locations: Montreal (Ernest Cormier Building) and Quebec City (300 Jean-Lesage Blvd). |
| **Working Day** | Weekdays (Monday to Friday), excluding statutory holidays under the *Code of Penal Procedure* (CQLR, c. C-25.01). |

### **Procedural and Conduct Requirements**

#### **Dress Code**
- **Counsel**: Must wear a gown, bands, white collared shirt, and dark garment.
- **Articling students**: Gown and dark garment.
- **Clerks and ushers**: Gown and dark garment.
- **Simple, unadorned attire** is acceptable for all non-counsel.
- **Videoconference counsel must be gowned** during hearings.

#### **Court Ushers and Conduct**
- A **court usher** must be present at all hearings.
- Responsible for opening and closing sessions and ensuring orderly conduct.
- Judges must enforce decorum and respect among all participants.
- **All electronic devices must be turned off** during hearings.

---

### **Confidentiality and File Management**

#### **Confidential Information Requirements**
- Every pleading (notice of appeal, motion, brief) **must explicitly state** that the file contains **no confidential information**.
- If any part is confidential:
  - The pleading must **clearly identify** the confidential content.
  - It must **reference the legal basis** (e.g., section of the Code or order) for confidentiality.
  - The **respondent must indicate any corrections** needed.

#### **Marking Confidentiality**
- The word **“CONFIDENTIAL”** must appear **below the court record number**.
- **Red binding or red band** indicates confidentiality.
- Confidential portions of a brief must be filed in a **separate volume**.

#### **Access to Confidential Documents**
- Only authorized persons may access confidential documents:
  - Parties, their counsel,
  - Persons authorized by law,
  - Individuals with a **legitimate interest** who have obtained **prior authorization from the Court or a judge** (subject to conditions and procedures).

#### **Technological Version (USB Key)**
- Parties must attach a **USB key** to each brief or to documents filed in lieu of briefs in fast-track cases.
- The USB key must:
  - Allow **keyword searches**.
  - Include **hyperlinks** from the table of contents to sections and from arguments to schedules.
- The USB key must be **identified identically to a pleading** (file number, party designation, title, confidentiality references).
- **Exception**: If a party is authorized to file Schedule III only as a technological version, they must still submit a **single paper copy** for archival purposes.
- **Pagination must match** the paper version exactly.

---

### **Office Hours and Clerk’s Duties**

- **Office Hours**: Monday to Friday, 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. (local time), unless otherwise posted.
- The **clerk maintains a computerized docket** (register) containing:
  - Contact information of parties and counsel,
  - Receipt of documents,
  - Matters arising during the appeal.
- The clerk uses the **last known contact information** to communicate with parties.
- Parties must **immediately notify the clerk** of any change in contact details.
- Each pleading must include:
  - Name, law firm, and full contact information (email, permanent code, locker number, if applicable).
- **Change of counsel**:
  - Must be notified to all parties, the clerk, and former counsel.
  - Notification includes name, address, phone, and email.
  - A change of counsel or withdrawal of representation **does not affect the hearing date** unless a judge orders otherwise.

#### **File Access and Photocopies**
- The clerk supervises access to files and removal of documents.
- **Photocopies** of non-confidential documents may be provided upon **payment of a fee**.

---

### **Pleading Format and Requirements**

#### **Paper and Text Formatting**
- **Paper size**: 21.5 cm × 28 cm (standard letter paper); 21.5 cm × 35.5 cm for motions or fast-track exhibits.
- **Text formatting**:
  - One side per sheet.
  - Minimum **1.5 line spacing** (except for quotations, which are single-spaced and indented).
  - Font: **12-point Arial** throughout.
    - Exceptions: 11-point Arial for quotations, 10-point Arial for footnotes.
  - Margins: **No less than 2.5 cm** on all sides.

#### **Signatures and Designations**
- All pleadings must be **signed by the party or their counsel**.
- Below each party’s name, the following must be indicated:
  - **Status in appeal** (in uppercase),
  - **Status in first instance** (in lowercase).
- Designations:
  - **Appellant, Respondent, Implicated Party** based on role in first instance.
  - **Intervener** only applies to parties intervening **during appeal**.
  - In **extraordinary remedies appeals**, the decision-maker in Superior Court is designated **Implicated Party**.
  - In **extradition judicial review**, the person sought is the **Petitioner**, the Minister is the **Respondent**, and the requesting state is the **Implicated Party**.

#### **Pleading Heading**
- Must include:
  - Date,
  - Filing party,
  - Nature of pleading,
  - If applicable, the specific provision (section) on which the request is based.

#### **Amendments**
- Must be clearly marked using:
  - Vertical lines in the margin,
  - Underlining,
  - Strikethrough notation.

### **Service of Pleadings**

- Governed by the **Code of Civil Procedure (CQLR, c. C-25.01)**.
- **Notice of appeal** and **motion for leave to appeal** must be served by **bailiff or peace officer**.
- Other pleadings may be served unless otherwise specified.

#### **Filing Deadline**
- Notice of appeal or motion for leave to appeal must be filed **within 30 days** of the judgment.
- If the **accused is the appellant and unrepresented**, the clerk serves the pleading on the respondent.
- In appeals by the **prosecutrix**, the notice must be served **personally** on the respondent **within 15 days** of filing (unless ordered otherwise by a judge).

#### **Required Information in Notice of Appeal or Motion**
- The offence,
- The sentence imposed (if any),
- Date of verdict, judgment, and sentence,
- Place and duration of trial (in days),
- Trial court and file number (and Superior Court file number, if applicable),
- Concise facts and grounds of appeal (maximum **10 pages** — excluding party designations and conclusions),
- Contact details (address and email) of:
  - Appellant and counsel,
  - Respondent and other parties and their counsel.

#### **Filing and Delivery of Pleadings**
- The original must be filed at the appropriate Court office with:
  - **One copy for the clerk**,
  - **Two copies for the trial court office**,
  - **One copy for the respondent** (if appellant is unrepresented).
- If required under CQLR, c. C-25.01, notice to the **Attorney General** is delivered per those provisions
