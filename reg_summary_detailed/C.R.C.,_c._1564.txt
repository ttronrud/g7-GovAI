**Detailed Summary of the Canal Regulations**

---

### **General Overview**

The *Canal Regulations* are a comprehensive set of rules governing the safe, lawful, and efficient operation of vessels navigating canals under the jurisdiction of the **Department of Transport** (now part of Transport Canada). These regulations apply to all canals listed in **Schedule I** and are designed to ensure safety, protect canal infrastructure, regulate traffic flow, and establish clear procedures for vessel operations, permits, and charges.

The regulations are cited as the **Canal Regulations** and must be kept on board every vessel navigating the canals. They also establish specific definitions of key terms used throughout the document.

## **Applicability and General Provisions**

- **Scope**: Applies to all canals under the Department’s jurisdiction as listed in **Schedule I**.
- **Mandatory Compliance**: A copy of these regulations must be on board every vessel navigating the canals.
- **Customs Clearance**: Vessels must show customs clearance papers to a Superintendent or lockmaster upon request; passage may be refused otherwise.

---

## **Vessel Registration and Licensing Requirements**

### **General Rule (Section 1)**  
No vessel (other than a canoe or skiff) that is not equipped for propulsion by sail or mechanical means may pass through a lock unless:
1. It is **registered under the Canada Shipping Act**;  
2. It has a **valid licence issued under the Canada Shipping Act**; or  
3. It is **registered or licensed and marked according to the laws of another country**.

> This ensures only legally compliant and properly registered vessels operate on the canals.

---

## **Permit Requirements (Sections 6–9)**

### **Permits for Specific Canals (St. Ours, Chambly, Ste. Anne, Carillon, Rideau, Trent Canals)**  
- Vessels **other than canoes or skiffs** must have a **permit issued under Section 7** to pass through locks in Quebec or Ontario canals.
- A vessel may **apply for a permit** if it does not proceed beyond the lock station where permits are issued.

### **Issuance of Permits (Section 7)**  
A permit must:
- Clearly show the **name or number of the vessel**;
- For non-seasonal permits, have the **expiry date endorsed by a lockmaster** on the first day of use;
- Be **produced upon request** by the operator or master for inspection by an officer.

> Permits are **non-transferable** and apply only to the vessel specified.

### **Special Permits (Exempt from Toll)**  
1. **Federal, Provincial, or Municipal Agency Vessels**  
   - Issued upon written request for official business.  
   - No toll required.

2. **Senior Citizens (Age 65+)**  
   - Vessels ≤24 feet in length, owned/operated by Canadian citizens aged 65 or over.  
   - No toll required upon written request and proof of age.

3. **Non-Profit Community Safety/Security Vessels**  
   - Used for official community safety or security purposes.  
   - No toll required upon written request and proof of use.

> These provisions promote access for public service and senior citizens while maintaining regulatory oversight.

---

## **Reporting and Documentation Requirements (Section 10)**

- All vessels (except pleasure craft ≤40 ft) must submit a **detailed report** to the statistical officer:
  - Signed by master or owner;
  - On the standard **Ship’s Report form**;
  - Must include:
    - Registered gross and net tonnage;
    - Nature and quantity of cargo;
    - Destination;
    - Certified copies of bills of lading or vessel manifest (if required).

> This enables accurate traffic monitoring, cargo tracking, and statistical analysis.

---

## **Safety and Operational Controls**

### **Vessel Character and Condition (Section 11)**  
The Chief, Superintending Engineer, or Superintendent may **deny passage** if:
- The cargo, hull, equipment, or machinery poses a risk to canal structures;
- It causes delays;
- It threatens to obstruct the canal.

### **Crew Competency (Section 12)**  
No vessel may enter a canal if:
- Its crew is **insufficient or incompetent** in the opinion of the Chief, Superintending Engineer, or Superintendent.  
- The master must follow instructions from these officials.

### **Inspection Authority (Section 13)**  
Officials may:
- Stop any vessel in the canal;
- Board the vessel;
- Examine crew, permits, manifests, licenses, or reports.

> This provides enforcement and safety assurance.

---

## **Draft Marks and Water Depth Requirements (Sections 14–16)**

### **Draft Marking Requirement**  
- All vessels drawing **5 feet or more** must have **distinct, correct draught marks** at the **bow and stern** showing exact depth fore and aft.
- A **duly sworn certificate** from the last drydock must be produced to verify correctness.

### **Minimum Water Depth Requirement**  
- A vessel may only enter or pass through a lock if the **depth of water at the controlling point exceeds the vessel’s maximum draught by at least three inches**, or by another amount determined by the Chief.
- **Schedule II** sets maximum draught limits for each canal.
- The Chief may **adjust draught limits** as needed.

> Ensures safe passage and prevents structural damage.

---

## **Cargo and Equipment Safety (Section 17)**

- Vessels must:
  - Arrange cargo and equipment so as **not to damage canal structures or other vessels**;
  - Have **all discharge pipes covered with hoods** to prevent discharge above the lock coping.

> Prevents pollution and physical damage to canal infrastructure.

---

## **Fenders and Tow Operations (Sections 18–19)**

### **Fenders**  
- Must be either:
  - Made of **floating materials**, or  
  - **Securely fastened** with steel cable or two manilla ropes.

> Prevents damage during docking or maneuvering.

### **Tow Limits and Conditions**  
- No vessel may tow more than **one other vessel** without permission from the Superintending Engineer or Superintendent.
- If towed, **two adequate tugs** must be used — one forward and one aft — when required.
- A vessel may be towed only if:
  - The **towing vessel has an unobstructed view** of the full deck and water surface 400 feet ahead of the towed vessel, or  
  - There is a **deckhand on the towed vessel** to signal directions.

> Ensures safe and controlled towing operations.
