**Detailed Summary of the N.R.C. Sales and Loans Regulations**

---

### **Regulatory Context and Authority**

The *National Research Council of Canada (N.R.C.) Sales and Loans Regulations* are issued under the authority of **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, based on the recommendation of the **Minister designated for the purpose of the National Research Council Act** and the **Treasury Board**, pursuant to **Section 52 of the Financial Administration Act**.

This regulatory instrument establishes specific rules governing the **sale of non-surplus research material** and the **gratuitous loan of scientific equipment** by the National Research Council of Canada (the "Council"). These provisions ensure fiscal accountability, proper use of assets, and compliance with public sector financial and administrative standards.

---

### **Key Definitions**

- **Council**: Refers to the National Research Council of Canada.
- **Equipment**: Scientific equipment owned by the Council that:
  - Was specially manufactured by or for the Council, or
  - Is otherwise not readily available to the general public or other borrowers.
- **Non-surplus research material**: Any material that is part of the Council’s inventory or stores and is not surplus to current needs (i.e., not excess or obsolete).

---

### **1. Sales of Non-Surplus Research Material**

#### **Eligibility and Conditions (Schedule I)**
- The Council may sell non-surplus research material **only to individuals or entities engaged in research or development work**.
- Sales must occur **at the invitation of the Council** and **on property owned by the Council**.

#### **Authorization Requirements**
- Each sale must be **authorized in writing by a Divisional Director** of the Council.
- The sale price must be **sufficient to recover the cost incurred by the Crown** (i.e., the government) in acquiring or maintaining the material.

#### **Use Condition**
- The material sold **must be used exclusively in the research or development activities** of the recipient.

#### **Financial Limitations**
- The **maximum value of any single sale is capped at $1,000**.

#### **Documentation Requirements**
Each sale must be accompanied by a **Bill of Sale** that includes the following details:
1. Name of the person who authorized the sale  
2. Date of authorization  
3. Date of the sale  
4. Description and quantity of the material sold  
5. Purpose of the sale  
6. Sale price  
7. Name of the person to whom the material is sold  

> *Purpose*: Ensures transparency, traceability, and accountability in transactions involving government-owned research assets.

---

### **2. Gratuitous Loans of Equipment**

#### **Eligible Recipients (Schedule II)**
The Council may make **gratuitous (free) loans** of equipment to:
- Her Majesty in right of Canada  
- A province  
- Municipal corporations  
- Hospitals  
- Universities  
- Other educational institutions  
- Persons (individuals or organizations)  

**Only if** the loan is for the purpose of **teaching, research, or development**.

#### **Authorization Requirements**
- Each loan must be **authorized by a Divisional Director** of the Council.

#### **Purpose Requirement**
- The loan must be **directly related to teaching, research, or development**.

#### **Documentation Requirements**
A **written loan agreement** must be signed and include the following elements:
1. **Duration of the loan** – Maximum of **two years**; no longer than that.
2. **Right to temporary return or termination** – The Council reserves the right to:
   - Require the **temporary return** of equipment at its discretion, and  
   - **Terminate the loan** at any time.
3. **Borrower Responsibilities** – The borrower is responsible for all costs associated with the equipment, including but not limited to:
   - Transportation  
   - Handling  
   - Packing  
   - Installation  
   - Removal  
   - Operation  
   - Replacement  
   - Maintenance  
   - Repair  
   - Insurance  
4. **Indemnification Clause (for institutions)** – If the loan is made to a **municipal corporation, hospital, university, or educational institution**, the borrower:
   - Shall **bear all claims or liens** arising from or related to the loan or use of the equipment  
   - Shall **indemnify and save harmless Her Majesty** from and against all such claims or liabilities  
5. **Condition Statement** – The borrower must:
   - Sign a statement at the start of the loan describing the **condition of the equipment**  
6. **Return Obligation** – Upon termination of the loan, the borrower must:
   - Return the equipment **in the same condition as described**, with **fair wear and tear excepted**

#### **Value Limitations**
- The **maximum value of any single piece of equipment** for a loan to any one borrower **shall not exceed $1,000,000**.
- **Exception**: If the loan involves equipment valued over $1,000,000, **prior approval from the Treasury Board** is required.

> *Note*: This cap ensures that large-scale or high-value equipment is subject to higher levels of oversight and financial scrutiny.

---

### **Overall Objectives and Governance Principles**

The regulations are designed to:

1. **Preserve public assets** by ensuring that equipment and research materials are used for legitimate scientific and educational purposes.
2. **Ensure accountability** through mandatory authorization, documentation, and clear terms.
3. **Prevent misuse or exploitation** of government-owned scientific equipment by requiring:
   - Use in research or development
   - Financial responsibility of the borrower
   - Indemnification for institutional borrowers
4. **Balance access and control** – allowing institutions to benefit from scientific tools while maintaining government oversight and financial responsibility.

---

### **Key Regulatory Features and Implications**

| Feature | Significance |
|-------|-------------|
| **Sales capped at $1,000** | Prevents large-scale asset sales without oversight; limits risk and ensures cost recovery. |
| **Loan value cap of $1M** | Protects public funds; requires Treasury Board approval for high-value loans. |
| **Mandatory condition statements** | Ensures transparency and fairness in equipment transfer and return. |
| **Indemnification clauses** | Protects the Crown from liability arising from borrower misuse or damage. |
| **Use limitation** | Ensures equipment is only used in approved research or educational contexts. |
| **Documentation requirements** | Provides audit trails and supports compliance with financial administration laws. |

---

### **Conclusion**

The *N.R.C. Sales and Loans Regulations* provide a clear, structured, and accountable framework for the Council to manage its scientific equipment and non-surplus research materials. By limiting sales and loans to qualified recipients, enforcing strict conditions, and mandating detailed documentation, the regulations ensure that these assets are used efficiently, ethically, and in alignment with public interest and fiscal responsibility.

These rules reflect a balance between enabling research collaboration and safeguarding government property, making them essential for maintaining the integrity and effectiveness of Canada’s national research infrastructure.
