### **2. Purpose of the Regulations**

The primary purpose of these regulations is to **define and classify what constitutes an "alternative fuel" and a "motor vehicle"** under the Alternative Fuels Act. By establishing clear, legally binding definitions, the regulations provide a consistent framework for:

- Determining eligibility for incentives or subsidies,
- Regulating fuel use and vehicle performance,
- Supporting the transition from conventional fossil fuels to cleaner, more sustainable energy sources.

### **4. Definition of "Motor Vehicle"**

The regulations define which types of vehicles are considered **motor vehicles** under the Act. These are:

- **Automobiles** (passenger cars)  
- **Passenger vans** (e.g., minivans, SUVs)  
- **Buses** (public or private transit vehicles)  
- **Light duty trucks** (e.g., pickup trucks, small cargo vehicles)  
- **Medium duty trucks** (e.g., delivery trucks, service vehicles with higher payload capacity)

> âœ… *Exclusions:* Heavy-duty trucks (e.g., long-haul freight trucks) or non-road vehicles (e.g., construction equipment) are **not included** in this definition unless otherwise specified in future amendments.

This classification ensures that fuel policies and incentives are targeted at vehicles commonly used in personal and public transportation, with a focus on reducing emissions and promoting cleaner technologies.

### **5. Effective Date**

The regulations come into force on **September 24, 1996**.

This date marks the official start of the legal application of the definitions and provisions outlined in the regulations, enabling enforcement, compliance, and implementation of related policies and programs.
