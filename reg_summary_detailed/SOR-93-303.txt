## ğŸ¯ **Core Purpose of the Regulations**

To **manage and regulate the use of inland water resources** in the Northwest Territories and Yukon Territory in a way that:

- Protects the environment;
- Prevents adverse impacts on ecosystems, communities, and other water users;

- Balances economic activity (e.g., mining, power generation, agriculture) with environmental protection.

## ğŸŒ Geographical Scope: Water Management Areas

The regulation establishes **specific geographic zones** in the NWT and Yukon as **Water Management Areas (WMAs)**, defined in **Schedule I**. These areas include:

1. **Great Slave Lake and all waters draining into it**
2. **Great Bear Lake and its tributaries (including the Great Bear River)**
3. **Mackenzie River and all its tributaries**
4. **Mainland waters draining into Hudson Bay**
5. **All other waters draining into the Arctic Ocean or adjacent waters**

> These zones are critical for environmental protection and are subject to strict water use and waste management rules.

## âœ… **Exemptions from Licensing (No Licence Required)**

Certain activities may proceed **without a licence** if they meet strict environmental and operational criteria:

### 1. **Low-Impact Uses**
- Uses or waste deposits with **no significant adverse environmental effects**.
- Do **not interfere** with existing rights of other water users or waste depositors.
- Meet the **criteria in column II of Schedules IV to VIII** (by undertaking type):
  - Agricultural, conservation, recreational, municipal, industrial, mining/milling, power.

> For example, small-scale farming or seasonal camp operations may qualify.

### 2. **Residential Sewage Waste**
- Individuals may deposit **sewage from residential buildings** under the **General Sanitation Regulations** of the NWT.

### 3. **Vessel Waste Deposition**
- Vessels (as defined in the *Canada Shipping Act, 2001*) may deposit waste **if not prohibited under Part 9** of that Act.

> This ensures shipping operations do not violate marine pollution standards.
