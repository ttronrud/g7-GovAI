**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

---

### **Regulatory Instrument**
**Title:**  
*Order Respecting the Withdrawal from Disposal of Certain Lands in the Northwest Territories (Ezodziti, N.W.T.)*

### **Issuing Authority**
- **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**,  
  on the **recommendation of the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development**,  
  pursuant to **paragraph 23(a) of the Territorial Lands Act**.

---

### **Purpose of the Order**
The primary objective of this regulation is to **withdraw specific tracts of land in the Northwest Territories from disposal** (i.e., from being available for sale, lease, or other forms of commercial development) in order to **facilitate the establishment of the Ezodziti area as a heritage resource**.

This action ensures that the land is preserved for cultural, historical, or environmental significance, protecting it from potential industrial development such as mining or oil and gas exploration.

---

### **Scope and Duration of Withdrawal**
- The withdrawal applies to the **specific parcels of land** listed in the **Schedule** attached to the Order.
- The withdrawal is **temporary** and effective from:
  - The **date the Order is made** (not specified in the text but implied to be the date of publication or issuance),
  - To **September 30, 2007**.
- This means that during this period, the lands **cannot be offered for disposal** (e.g., through leasing or sale) by the Government of Canada or the Northwest Territories.

### **Exclusions from the Withdrawal (Section 2 Exemptions)**
Despite the general withdrawal, the following activities are **explicitly exempted** and **not affected** by the Order:

1. **Prospecting Permits**  
   - A claim located by a holder of a prospecting permit **granted before the Order came into force** remains valid and can proceed.

2. **Recorded Claims**  
   - Claims that were **already recorded** prior to the Orderâ€™s date are not affected.

3. **Leases under Canada Mining Regulations**  
   - A lease granted to a person with a **recorded claim** (where the lease covers part of that claim) is not invalidated.

4. **Significant Discovery Licences**  
   - A significant discovery licence issued to a holder of an **exploration licence issued before the Order date**, covering an area subject to that exploration licence, is not affected.

5. **Production Licences**  
   - A production licence issued to a holder of a significant discovery licence (as above) or to a holder of an exploration licence or significant discovery licence **issued before the Order date**, where the licence covers an area subject to the prior licence, is not affected.

6. **Surface Leases**  
   - A surface lease granted to a claimant under the Territorial Lands Act (for a recorded claim or interest) **to allow exercise of rights** under the claim or interest is exempt.

7. **Renewals of Interests**  
   - Renewals of existing mining or petroleum interests (e.g., exploration or production rights) are not impacted.

> âœ… These exclusions ensure that **existing rights and ongoing operations** are not disrupted by the withdrawal, preserving continuity in legitimate, pre-existing activities.

### **Legal and Policy Context**
- This Order is grounded in **section 23(a) of the Territorial Lands Act**, which allows the government to withdraw land from disposal for specific purposes, including **cultural heritage, environmental protection, or Indigenous interests**.
- The designation of Ezodziti as a **heritage resource** suggests it may have:
  - Cultural or historical significance (possibly related to Indigenous communities),
  - Ecological value,
  - Or both.

> The withdrawal serves as a **proactive measure** to protect the area from industrial development during a defined period, allowing time for:
> - Cultural assessments,
> - Community consultations,
> - Environmental studies,
> - and formal designation as a heritage site.
