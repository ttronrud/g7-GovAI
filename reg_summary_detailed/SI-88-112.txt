### **Regulation Summary: Transfer of Powers and Functions from Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development to Minister of Western Economic Diversification**

**Legal Basis**:  
The regulation is issued under **Section 2 of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act**, by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Prime Minister**. ## **Detailed Breakdown of Transferred Powers, Duties, and Functions**

### **1. Powers under Paragraph 6(b) of the Department of Regional Industrial Expansion Act**

- **Original Transfer**:  
  Under **Order in Council P.C. 1987-1613 (August 4, 1987)**, certain powers related to entering into **Economic and Regional Development Agreements** were transferred from the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development to the Minister of Western Economic Diversification.

- **Scope of Transfer**:  
  The powers relate to agreements made with the **Governments of Manitoba, British Columbia, Alberta, and Saskatchewan**.

- **Exclusions (Where Agreements Are Not Transferred)**:  
  The transfer **does not apply** to agreements that:
  - Affect only **one firm** (i.e., single-entity projects),
  - Deal with the **general advancement of science and technology**,
  - Deal only with a **comprehensive restructuring of an industrial sector**, or
  - Relate to the **development of northern areas** or the **tourism sector**.

> ✅ **Summary**: The Minister of Western Economic Diversification now has authority to enter into regional development agreements with the four Prairie and BC provinces—except for those involving single firms, science/tech, sector-wide restructuring, or northern/tourism initiatives.
