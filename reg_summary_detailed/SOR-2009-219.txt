### **Detailed Summary of the Companies’ Creditors Arrangement Regulations (S.C. 2007, c. 36, s. 82)**

These regulations, made under **section 62a of the Companies’ Creditors Arrangement Act (CCAA)** by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Industry, provide detailed procedural and administrative requirements for the initiation, administration, and public disclosure of proceedings under the CCAA. The regulations ensure transparency, consistency, and accountability in the management of debtor companies undergoing restructuring.

---

## **I. Purpose and Legal Basis**
- The regulations are enacted to implement specific provisions of the **Companies’ Creditors Arrangement Act (CCAA)**, particularly those related to the **initial order**, **public disclosure**, **monitoring**, and **creditor notification**.
- They are issued pursuant to **section 62a of the CCAA**, which empowers the Governor General to make regulations to carry out the Act’s provisions.
- The regulations come into force on the date that **section 124 of the Wage Earner Protection Program Act (S.C. 2005, c. 47)** comes into force. If registered later, they take effect upon registration.
- As of September 18, 2009, these regulations were in force (SI/2009-68).

---

## **II. Key Definitions and Prescribed Entities**

### 1. **"Act"**
- Refers to the **Companies’ Creditors Arrangement Act (R.S.C., c. C-36)**.

### 2. **"Initial Order"**
- Defined as the order made upon the **initial application** filed in respect of a debtor company.

### 3. **Prescribed Entities for Specific Definitions**
The following entities are designated as "prescribed" for specific purposes in the CCAA:

| Purpose | Prescribed Entities |
|--------|---------------------|
| Income Trust (subsection 2(1)) | Stock exchanges regulated by federal or provincial Acts |
| Pension Plan (subsection 6(6)) | Pension plans regulated by federal or provincial Acts |
| Representations (paragraph 10(2)(b)) | Representations set out in a prescribed table |
| Subsection 11.1(1) | Stock exchanges, Investment Industry Regulatory Organization of Canada (IIROC), and Mutual Fund Dealers Association of Canada (MFDA) |
| Public disclosure (section 23(1)(a)(ii)) | Web page created by the monitor |

> These designations ensure that only authorized, regulated bodies are recognized for determining eligibility or compliance in specific contexts.

---

## **III. Public Disclosure Requirements (Section 23)**

The regulations establish **mandatory public disclosure** of key documents and information in proceedings under the CCAA, enhancing transparency for creditors and the public.

### A. **Mandatory Public Disclosure of Information**
The following information must be made publicly available:

| Document/Information | Method of Disclosure | Timeframe |
|----------------------|------------------------|----------|
| Initial Order and Application | Posted on monitor’s Web page | Within **2 business days** of filing |
| Initial Order and Amendments | Filed via facsimile/electronic transmission | Within **2 business days** after monitor receives them |
| Debtor Company Information Summary (Commencement) | Filed electronically | Within **5 business days** after monitor receives initial order |
| Debtor Company Information Summary (Post-Monitor Discharge) | Filed electronically | Within **5 business days** after court discharges monitor |
| Documents filed with court (reports, proposed compromises, orders, communications) | Posted on monitor’s Web page | Within **2 business days** of filing, receipt, or sending |

> **Note**: The monitor is required to create a **dedicated Web page** for each proceeding to host all public disclosures.

### B. **Prescribed Manner of Service**
- For **notices to creditors**, the prescribed methods include:
  - Personal service
  - Mail
  - Courier
  - Facsimile
  - Other electronic transmission

> This ensures that all creditors receive notice in a reliable, accessible manner.

---

## **IV. Filing Requirements (Section 23(1)(f))**

The regulations specify **which documents must be filed** with the **Superintendent of Bankruptcy** and **when**.

| Document | Filing Method | Deadline |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **Form 1 – Information Pertaining to Initial Order** | Facsimile/electronic transmission | Within **1 business day** of initial order being made |
| **Initial Application, Initial Order, Amendments** | Facsimile/electronic transmission | Within **2 business days** after monitor receives them |
| **Form 2 – Debtor Company Information Summary (Commencement)** | Facsimile/electronic transmission | Within **5 business days** after monitor receives initial order |
| **Form 3 – Debtor Company Information Summary (Post-Discharge)** | Facsimile/electronic transmission | Within **5 business days** after court discharges monitor |
| **Documents posted on monitor’s Web page** | Submitted on **CD, DVD, or other data storage medium** | Within **30 days** after court discharges monitor |

> All filings are required to be submitted electronically or via physical medium to ensure record-keeping and accessibility.

---

## **V. Required Documents and Disclosure (Section 23(1)(j))**

The following documents are **prescribed** as required to be filed with the court and made publicly available:

1. **Monitor’s Reports**  
   - Includes exhibits and cash-flow statements  
   - **Exclusion**: Any part subject to a court order restricting public release

2. **Proposed Compromises or Arrangements**  
   - Including amendments

3. **Court Orders**

4. **Written Communications and Notifications**  
   - Sent by the monitor to all creditors

> **Public Availability**: All such documents must be posted on the **monitor’s Web page** within specified timeframes.

### Timeframes for Public Posting:
| Document Type | Posting Deadline |
|-------------|------------------|
| (a) & (b): Initial Order, Application | Within **2 business days** of filing |
| (c): Reports, proposed arrangements | Within **2 business days** of monitor receiving them |
| (d): Communications to creditors | Within **2 business days** of sending |

---

## **VI. Recordkeeping and Duration (Sections 26)**

### A. **Section 26(1): Information on Form 1**
- The information on **Form 1** (initial order details) is preserved for **10 years** after being received by the Superintendent of Bankruptcy.

### B. **Section 26(2): Records of Proceedings**
- All records of proceedings are retained for **10 years** after being received by the Superintendent of Bankruptcy.

> This ensures long-term accessibility for regulatory oversight, legal challenges, or future reference.

---

## **VII. Notice of Delegation (Section 31(2))**

- A **notice of delegation** (e.g., when a monitor or legal representative is delegated authority) must be given to relevant parties.
- The prescribed method is **personal service, mail, courier, facsimile, or electronic transmission**.

---

## **VIII. Notice to Disclaim or Resiliate an Agreement (Section 32(1))**

### A. **Form 4 – Notice by Debtor Company**
- **Prescribed form**: Form 4 of the schedule titled *"Notice by Debtor Company to Disclaim or Resiliate an Agreement"*
- The debtor company may give notice of its intention to **disclaim or resiliate** (i.e., terminate or withdraw) a prior agreement.

### B. **Manner of Giving Notice**
- By **personal service, registered mail, courier**, or **facsimile/electronic transmission** if the recipient agrees.

### C. **Effect of Notice**
- If **no application** is filed within **15 days** of the notice:
  - The agreement is **automatically disclaimed or resiliated** on the **30th day** after the notice is given.

> This provides a clear, time-bound process to resolve disputes over agreements without prolonged litigation.

---

## **IX. Foreign Proceedings (Paragraph 53(b))**

When a debtor has proceedings in a foreign jurisdiction, the following information must be disclosed:

| Information Required |
|----------------------|
| Name and contact info of foreign representative |
| Name of debtor company and name under which it operates in Canada |
| Name of court, legislative provision, and date of foreign order |
| Country where foreign proceeding is filed |
| Whether it is a **foreign main** or **foreign non-main** proceeding |
| Name and contact info of legal counsel for foreign representative |

> This ensures that stakeholders are fully informed about cross-border restructuring activities.

---

## **X. Forms and Information to Be Filed with Superintendent of Bankruptcy**

The regulations include **three detailed forms** to be filed with the Superintendent of Bankruptcy at different stages of the process.

---

### **Form 1 – Initial Order Information**
*Filed when initial order is made*

- Name of court, judicial district, and court file number  
- Date of order  
- Debtor’s head office address, phone, and website  
- Business names (if different from legal name)  
- Monitor’s name and website  
- Monitor’s representative (name, address, phone, email)  
- Web page address for the proceeding  
- Dated and signed by the monitor

---

### **Form 2 – Debtor Company Information Summary (Commencement)**
*Filed after initial order is received by monitor*

- Full name(s)
