### **Detailed Summary of the Designer Remission Order, 2001 (S.C. 1997, c. 36)**

This regulation, known as the **Designer Remission Order, 2001**, was enacted by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance under **section 115 of the Customs Tariff**. It provides a **customs duty remission** for certain imported fabrics used exclusively in the production of **original designer apparel** in Canada. The Order is designed to support the Canadian fashion industry by reducing the cost of raw materials for high-end, innovative, and limited-run designer clothing.

---

## **I. Purpose of the Regulation**

The primary objective of this Order is to **encourage innovation and the development of original designer apparel** in Canada by reducing the customs duties paid or payable on specific fabrics imported into Canada. This supports small and medium-sized fashion businesses, especially those producing **limited-run, innovative, and original designs** that reflect Canadian creativity and craftsmanship.

The remission applies to **fabrics used in the manufacture of original designer apparel** during a defined period (December 13, 2001 – December 31, 2014), with a special extension for swimwear starting November 1, 2002.

---

## **II. Key Definitions**

The Order includes precise definitions to clarify eligibility and scope:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Apparel** | Includes clothing and accessories under specific tariff headings (e.g., chapters 61, 62, 65) and subheadings such as 4203.10, 4303.10, 4818.50, and certain tariff items (e.g., 3926.20.92–99, 4015.19.90, 4015.90.90). Also includes clothing or accessories under subheading 4304.00. |
| **Apparel Designer** | An individual with a formal education in fashion/apparel design or equivalent proficiency who engages in the full creative process of designing innovative apparel (e.g., sketching, fabric selection, sample development, record-keeping). Must be directly responsible for these activities or oversee them. |
| **Collection** | A product line of original designer apparel presented for a specific fashion season. |
| **Fabric** | Fabrics listed in chapters 51–59 (excluding those with aramid fibres), and all fabrics in chapter 60. Excludes fabrics containing aramid fibres (e.g., Kevlar). |
| **Innovative Apparel** | Apparel created through a continuous creative process involving new relationships among fabric, colour, assembly, shape, and function. |
| **Original Designer Apparel** | Innovative apparel available in **limited quantities**, created by a recognized designer or design house, and bearing a **permanent trademark** identifying the designer or house. |
| **Recognized Apparel Designer** | An apparel designer who meets the criteria for an apparel designer *and* has control over the use of their trademark (directly or through an entity). |
| **Recognized Apparel Design House** | A company or partnership that employs at least one recognized apparel designer and controls the use of its trademark. |
| **Trademark** | A word, symbol, or design used to distinguish goods or services in the marketplace. |
| **Winter Outerwear** | Lined and insulated clothing worn over other garments for cold-weather protection. |

> ⚠️ *Note:* Some terms such as "limited quantities," "name," and "limited quantities" were **repealed** in later amendments (SOR/2003-128 and SOR/2004-94), indicating that the definition of "limited quantities" was no longer used in the current version.

---

## **III. Eligibility Criteria for Producers**

To qualify for duty remission, a producer must meet **all of the following conditions**:

1. **Engagement in Designer Apparel Activities**  
   The producer must be involved in:
   - Creation
   - Manufacture
   - Contracting of manufacture
   - Sale of original designer apparel

2. **Legal Structure**  
   Must be a:
   - Sole proprietorship
   - Corporation
   - Partnership  
   That employs **at least one** recognized apparel designer or is itself a recognized apparel designer.

3. **Revenue Requirement**  
   At least **85% of the producer’s total net sales revenue** must come from the sale of **original designer apparel** in each calendar year for which a remission claim is submitted.

> This ensures that only businesses genuinely focused on original designer fashion benefit from the remission.

---

## **IV. Scope and Duration of Remission**

### **Main Period (General Apparel)**
- **Start:** December 13, 2001  
- **End:** December 31, 2014  
- Applies to fabrics used in manufacturing **original designer apparel** in chapters 61, 62, and 65 (e.g., men’s and women’s clothing).

### **Swimwear Exception (Extended Period)**
- **Start:** November 1, 2002  
- **End:** December 31, 2014  
- Applies to fabrics used in swimwear under specific subheadings:
  - 6112.31, 6112.39, 6112.41, 6112.49  
  - 6211.11, 6211.12  

> This extension recognizes the unique needs of swimwear producers and provides targeted support.

---

## **V. Conditions for Remission**

A claim for remission must meet **all of the following conditions**:

1. **Fabric Use Restriction**  
   The fabric must be used **exclusively** for manufacturing the producer’s own **men’s or women’s original designer apparel** (chapters 61, 62, 65).  
   **Excluded uses** include:
   - Hosiery (heading 61.15)
   - Men’s underpants/briefs (6107.11, 6107.12, 6107.19, 6207.11, 6207.19)
   - Women’s briefs/panties (6108.21, 6108.22, 6108.29, 6208.91, 6208.92, 6208.99)
   - Women’s foundation undergarments (62.12)
   - Winter outerwear with outer shells from chapters 54, 55, 59 or headings 52.10, 52.11, 52.12
   - Workwear (apparel for physical protection in work environments)
   - Employer-mandated uniforms worn exclusively during work hours

2. **Minimum Value Threshold**  
   The fabric must have a **duty value of at least $14.00 per square meter** (indexed annually).  
   - The index is updated each year according to **administrative guidelines** issued by the **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)** to account for inflation.  
   - This ensures that the remission remains economically relevant over time.

3. **Timely Claim Submission**  
   A claim must be submitted to the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** within **four years** of the date of importation.

4. **Waiver Requirement (if not the importer)**  
   If the producer is **not the importer of record**, they must provide a **waiver** from the importer, waiving the importer’s right to claim a **drawback, refund, or remission** of duties on the fabric.

5. **Reporting Obligations**  
   Producers must submit **semi-annual reports** to the Minister containing:
   - Volume and value of fabrics used
   - Use of fabrics in production
   - Any other relevant information needed for administrative oversight

6. **Audit Compliance**  
   Producers agree to be **audited by the CBSA** to verify:
   - Accuracy of remission amounts claimed
   - Completeness and accuracy of eligibility information
   - Whether the facts used to determine eligibility remain unchanged in all material respects

7. **Audit Outcome Requirement**  
   If audited, the information must be found to be:
   - Accurate and complete
   - The facts relied upon must remain unchanged in all material respects

---

## **VI. Enforcement and Administration**

- The **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)** is responsible for:
  - Administering the remission
  - Conducting audits
  - Verifying claims and eligibility
- The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** is the official responsible for receiving claims and processing them.

---

## **VII. Effective Date**

The Order comes into force on the **date of registration** with the federal government.

---

## **VIII. Key Notes and Implications**

### ✅ **Support for Canadian Design Innovation**
- Encourages Canadian designers to create unique, high-quality, limited-run fashion lines.
- Reduces input costs (especially for high-value fabrics), enabling more competitive pricing and creative freedom.
