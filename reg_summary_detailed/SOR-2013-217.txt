### **Detailed Summary of the CCOFTA Verification of Origin Regulations**

---

#### **1. Background and Legal Basis**

These regulations are a formal implementation of **Customs Notice 11-013**, issued on **August 10, 2011**, and are designed to establish a clear, transparent, and enforceable framework for verifying the **origin of goods** under the **Canada-Colombia Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement (CCOFTA)**.

- **Legal Authority**:  
  The regulations are made under:
  - **Section 42.1(c)** of the *Customs Act* (S.C. 1992, c. 28, s. 31(1)),  
  - **Paragraphs 164(1)(i)** and **(j)**, and **subsection 164(1.1)** of the *Customs Act*,  
  - **Paragraph 167.1(b)a** of the *Customs Act*,  
  - And pursuant to **section 42.1** of the *Customs Act* (S.C. 2009, c. 6, s. 24; S.C. 1992, c. 28, s. 30(1); S.C. 2012, c. 18, s. 30).

- **Effective Date**:  
  The regulations come into force on **August 15, 2011**, as specified in Customs Notice 11-013.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined to ensure clarity and consistency in the application of the regulations:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Customs Act* of Canada. |
| **Competent Authority** | The Colombian authority responsible for customs and trade matters, currently:  
- *Ministerio de Comercio, Industria y Turismo* (Ministry of Commerce, Industry, and Tourism)  
- *Dirección de Impuestos y Aduanas Nacionales* (Directorate of Taxes and Customs)  
  *If Colombia notifies Canada of a replacement, the successor entity shall apply.* |
| **Customs Administration** | The Canadian authority responsible for administering customs laws (e.g., Canada Border Services Agency – CBSA). |
| **Goods** | Goods for which preferential tariff treatment under CCOFTA is claimed. |
| **Material** | As defined in Article 318 of the CCOFTA. |
| **Verification Letter** | A formal request for information about the origin of goods. |
| **Verification Questionnaire** | A document containing questions to gather origin-related information. |
| **Verification Visit** | A physical inspection of premises to verify the origin of goods. |

---

#### **3. Methods of Origin Verification**

A verification of origin may be conducted through **one or more of the following methods**:

1. **Verification Questionnaire**  
   - Completed by:
     - The **exporter or producer** of the goods, or  
     - A **producer or supplier** of the material.  

2. **Written Response to a Verification Letter**  
   - A formal reply to a verification letter sent by Canadian customs authorities.

3. **Other Relevant Information**  
   - Any additional information received from the exporter, producer, or supplier.

> These methods are alternatives to a physical visit and are used when a full inspection is not required.

---

#### **4. Requirements for Verification Letters and Questionnaires**

All verification letters and questionnaires must include:

- Identification of the **customs administration** and the **name and title** of the officer sending the document.
- A **clear description** of the goods or materials under review.
- A **specified period** for response (minimum of **30 days**, beginning from the date of receipt).

> This ensures timely and meaningful responses from the parties involved.

---

#### **5. Request for Extension of Response Period**

- A person receiving a verification letter or questionnaire may request a **single extension** of the response period.
- The extension is granted for **up to an additional 30 days**.
- This provision allows for practical circumstances such as complex documentation or data collection.

---

#### **6. Verification Visits – Access to Colombian Premises**

Canadian customs officers may enter the following **Colombian premises** for verification purposes:

- Premises of an **exporter or producer** of goods.
- Premises of a **producer or supplier** of a material.

> This ensures that physical evidence of origin can be examined in the country where the goods were manufactured or processed.

---

#### **7. Conditions for Conducting a Verification Visit**

A verification visit **cannot be conducted** until the following conditions are met:

1. A **written notice** of the visit is sent to:
   - The **person whose premises** are to be visited,
   - The **competent authority** in Colombia,
   - *(If requested by Colombia)*, the **Embassy of Colombia in Canada**.

2. The **person whose premises** are to be visited **consents in writing** to the visit.

> These safeguards ensure transparency, respect for due process, and bilateral cooperation.

---

#### **8. Content of the Written Notice of Visit**

The notice must specify:

- Identity of the **customs administration** conducting the visit,
- **Name and title** of the officer,
- Name of the **person whose premises** are being visited,
- **Date and location** of the visit,
- **Object and scope** of the visit (including description of goods/materials),
- **Legal authority** under which the visit is being conducted.

> This ensures clarity, accountability, and compliance with Canadian legal standards.

---

#### **9. Postponement of Verification Visits**

- If, within **15 days** of receiving the notice, the **competent authority in Colombia** sends a **written request to the officer**, the visit is **postponed**.
- The postponement period:
  - Cannot exceed **60 days** from the date the notice is received,
  - May be extended by mutual agreement between the Canadian customs administration and Colombia.
- Similarly, the **person whose premises** are to be visited may request a postponement **within 15 days** of receiving the notice.
- The postponement period is also capped at **60 days** (or longer by mutual agreement).

> This provides flexibility to accommodate logistical, operational, or administrative challenges.

---

#### **10. Observers During Verification Visits**

- A person may designate **up to two observers** to be present during the visit.
- Observers are **limited to observation only** — they may not participate in interviews, document reviews, or decision-making.
- The person must **identify the observers** to the officer conducting the visit.

> This promotes transparency and trust between Canada and Colombia, allowing Colombian stakeholders to monitor the process.

---

#### **11. Recordkeeping and Compliance Obligations**

Under **subsection 42.1(2)** of the *Customs Act*, exporters and producers are required to:

- **Maintain records** relevant to determining the origin of goods, as required by Colombian law.
- **Provide access** to these records to the customs officer conducting the verification.
- **Respond promptly** to verification letters or complete and return verification questionnaires within the time frame specified (minimum 30 days).

> This ensures that origin claims are supported by verifiable, documented evidence.

---

#### **12. Written Consent to Visit**

- The **exporter or producer** must provide **written consent** to the verification visit within **30 days** of receiving the notice under paragraph 6(1)(a)(i).

> This reinforces the requirement for informed and voluntary participation.

---

#### **13. Notification of Origin Re-Determination**

- If a customs officer determines that goods are **not eligible** for preferential tariff treatment under CCOFTA (e.g., due to non-compliance with origin rules), they must:
  - Send a **written notice** to the exporter or producer.
  - The notice must:
    - Specify the **date after which preferential treatment will be denied or withdrawn**.
    - State the **period during which the exporter or producer may submit written comments or additional information** regarding the origin of the goods.

> This provides a fair opportunity for the exporter to respond and correct any errors.

---

#### **14. Method of Delivery and Receipt Confirmation**

- All verification letters, questionnaires, and notices must be sent via a method that **produces confirmation of receipt** (e.g., email with read receipt, registered mail, courier tracking).
- This ensures accountability and traceability in communications.

---

#### **15. Final Effective Date**

- These regulations are **deemed to have come into force on August 15, 2011** — the date specified in Customs Notice 11-013.

---

### **Summary of Key Objectives and Impacts**

| Objective | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Ensure Origin Verification** | Provides a structured, fair, and legally sound process to verify that goods qualify for preferential tariff treatment under CCOFTA. |
| **Promote Bilateral Cooperation** | Establishes clear procedures for collaboration between Canadian customs and Colombian authorities. |
| **Enhance Transparency and Accountability** | Requires written notices, consents, and confirmations of receipt; allows for observer participation. |
| **Protect Trade Parties’ Rights** | Provides rights to request extensions, postpone visits, and respond to findings. |
| **Support Compliance with CCOFTA** | Ensures that origin claims are based on verifiable facts, reducing the risk of fraud or misrepresentation. |
