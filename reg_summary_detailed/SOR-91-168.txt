**Detailed Summary of the Canadian Heraldic Authority Fee Order (SI/90-153, 1990)**

**Regulation Title:**  
*Canadian Heraldic Authority Fee Order*  
(Referenced as SI/90-153, 1990, in the Canada Gazette, Part II, p. 4613)

**Issuing Authority:**  
The Prime Minister of Canada, pursuant to *Order in Council P.C. 1990-2224*, dated October 11, 1990.

**Effective Date:**  
February 27, 1991 (date of publication in the Canada Gazette)

**Purpose of the Regulation:**  
This Order establishes and prescribes the fee that must be paid by applicants seeking the processing and granting of coats of arms through the Canadian Heraldic Authority (CHA).

---

### Key Provisions:

1. **Fee Amount:**  
   The fee payable by an applicant for the processing of an application to the Canadian Heraldic Authority for the grant of a coat of arms is **$435**.

2. **Method of Payment:**  
   - The fee must be paid by **cheque**.  
   - The cheque must be **payable to the Receiver General for Canada**.  
   - Payment must be made **in conjunction with** the applicant’s submission of a letter acknowledging receipt of notice from the CHA that a warrant has issued authorizing the grant of a coat of arms.

3. **Timing of Payment:**  
   - The fee is not required to be paid at the initial submission of the application.  
   - It is due **upon receipt of notice** from the Canadian Heraldic Authority that a warrant has been issued authorizing the grant of the coat of arms.  
   - This ensures that payment occurs only after the CHA has formally authorized the grant, aligning the financial obligation with the official decision-making process.

4. **Inclusion with Submission:**  
   - The fee payment (via cheque) must be **included with the letter** that acknowledges receipt of the warrant notice.  
   - This requirement ensures transparency and accountability in the administrative process and helps prevent non-payment or delays in processing.

---

### Context and Significance:

- The Canadian Heraldic Authority is responsible for granting coats of arms, badges, and other heraldic symbols to individuals, organizations, and institutions in Canada, typically in recognition of service, achievement, or historical significance.
- This fee serves as a formal administrative charge to cover the costs associated with reviewing applications, conducting research, and issuing official heraldic grants.
- The fee structure is standardized and applies uniformly to all applicants seeking a coat of arms through the CHA, ensuring consistency in administrative practices across the country.

---

### Legal Basis:

- The Order is issued under the authority of **Order in Council P.C. 1990-2224**, which granted the Prime Minister the power to establish fees for services provided by the Canadian Heraldic Authority.
- This regulatory action is part of the broader framework of Canadian heraldic law and administrative governance, ensuring that the CHA can operate efficiently and sustainably.

---

### Citation and Reference:

- The Order may be cited as: **Canadian Heraldic Authority Fee Order**  
- Official reference: **SI/90-153, 1990 Canada Gazette, Part II, p. 4613**

---

**Conclusion:**  
This regulation establishes a clear, transparent, and standardized fee of $435 for the processing of coat of arms applications by the Canadian Heraldic Authority. It outlines specific procedural requirements for payment—via cheque to the Receiver General for Canada—and ties payment to the issuance of a warrant, ensuring that fees are collected only after formal authorization. The regulation reflects a balance between administrative necessity and procedural fairness in the heraldic grant process.
