**Detailed Summary of the Farm Improvement Loans Regulations**

---

### **1. Regulation Title and Citation**
The regulations are officially titled the **Farm Improvement Loans Regulations** and may be cited as such. These regulations establish the framework for **guaranteed farm improvement loans** under the **Farm Improvement Loans Act (Act)**. The primary objective is to provide accessible, government-guaranteed financing to farmers for essential agricultural improvements, equipment purchases, and land development.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Farm Improvement Loans Act* (Loi), which provides the legal foundation for the loan program. |
| **Owner** | A person with either a legal or equitable interest in a farm, or a veteran who has a subsisting contract under the *Veterans‚Äô Land Act* for a farm. |
| **Addition** | Includes adding rooms or storeys, constructing attached garages/outbuildings, installing foundations/basements, and purchasing plumbing, heating, ventilation, or air conditioning systems. |
| **Alteration** | Structural changes to improve or modernize farm buildings (e.g., plumbing, electrical, heating systems, relocation of equipment). |
| **Major** | In the context of repair or overhaul, refers to expenditures of **$400 or more**, as estimated by the borrower. |
| **Repair** | Covers repairs to farm buildings, structures, implements, and systems (e.g., fencing, wells, plumbing, family dwellings, barns, summer cottages used as farm enterprises). |
| **Responsible Officer of the Bank** | The manager, assistant manager, credit committee, or any person duly authorized by the bank to supervise loan approvals. |
| **Works for Drainage** | Includes ditches, tiling, pumping, diking, and soil conservation measures to prevent erosion. |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: The terms *averaging period* and *interest period* have been **revoked** (SOR/78-138, s. 1), indicating they are no longer in effect.

---

### **3. Scope of Application**
These regulations apply specifically to **guaranteed farm improvement loans** authorized under the Farm Improvement Loans Act. The loans must meet specific criteria to qualify as "guaranteed" and thus eligible for government-backed financing.

---

### **4. Designated Eligible Loan Classes**

The following types of loans are prescribed and eligible for guaranteed funding:

| Loan Type | Purpose |
|---------|--------|
| **Agricultural Implement Loans** | Financing for purchasing agricultural implements and bee-keeping equipment. |
| **Livestock Loans** | Financing for purchasing livestock or bee-stock (excluding short-term feeder steers). |
| **Agricultural Equipment Loans** | Financing for purchasing or installing agricultural equipment or farm electric systems. |
| **Building Loans** | For construction, renovation, alteration, repair, or extension of any farm building or structure. |
| **Additional Land Loans** | For purchasing additional land on a farm, including any existing farm buildings. |
| **Improvement or Development Loans** | For projects such as: <br> ‚Ä¢ Farm electric system upgrades <br> ‚Ä¢ Drainage works <br> ‚Ä¢ Fencing <br> ‚Ä¢ Land clearing, irrigation, reclamation <br> ‚Ä¢ Tree planting, shelter belts <br> ‚Ä¢ Road or driveway construction |
| **Major Repair or Overhaul Loans** | For major repairs or overhauls of agricultural implements, bee-keeping equipment, or farm electric systems. |

---

### **5. Qualification Criteria for Guaranteed Loans**

Each loan type has specific eligibility requirements:

#### ‚úÖ **General Requirements (All Loan Types)**  
- The loan must be made to the **owner or tenant** of the farm.  
- The borrower must provide **evidence of actual use** of funds (as required by normal banking standards).  
- The bank must verify that funds were used **exactly as stated in the application**.

#### üîç **Loan-Specific Limits (Maximum Loan Amounts)**

| Loan Type | Maximum Loan Amount | Exclusions |
|---------|---------------------|----------|
| **Agricultural Implement / Bee-Keeping Equipment** | 75% of the cost price (as per contract or sale evidence) | Excludes labour costs |
| **Livestock / Bee-Stock** | 75% of estimated purchase cost (as per application) | Excludes labour costs |
| **Agricultural Equipment / Farm Electric System** | 75% of estimated cost (including installation, excluding labour) | Excludes labour |
| **Building Loan** | 90% of estimated cost (construction, renovation, etc., excluding labour) | Excludes labour |
| **Additional Land Loan** | 90% of the market value of the land and any farm buildings | Excludes labour |
| **Improvement/Development Loan** | 90% of estimated cost (excluding labour) | Applies to drainage, fencing, electric systems, etc. |
| **Major Repair/Overhaul Loan** | 75% of estimated cost of major repair/overhaul (excluding labour) | Applies only to specific assets |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è All calculations exclude **borrower labour costs**. The borrower must provide documentation to support cost estimates.

---

### **6. Security Requirements**

A bank must take security for the loan at the time of disbursement. The acceptable forms of security include:

- **Chattel mortgage or commercial pledge** (for movable assets)  
- **Mortgage or hypothec on real property** (on the farm)  
- **Assignment of rights under an agreement for sale**  
- **Written undertaking** by the borrower to provide security as required under the above (if needed)

> üìå **Additional Security**: If the responsible officer deems it necessary, the bank may take **further security**.

> üìå **Ongoing Security**: Banks must maintain **adequate security throughout the loan term**, consistent with normal banking practice.

---

### **7. Borrower‚Äôs Written Promise to Repay**

At the time of disbursement, the borrower must sign a **written promise to repay** that includes:

- Principal amount of the loan  
- Basis for interest rate (e.g., 1% + prime rate)  
- Repayment terms (frequency, amount, schedule)

This document is essential for legal and administrative tracking.

---

### **8. Repayment Terms**

- Repayments are due **at least annually**, though the bank may allow more frequent payments (e.g., semi-annually).
- Terms must reflect the borrower‚Äôs **financial ability**, including:
  - Type of farming operation  
  - Marketing conditions  
  - Other financial obligations  
  - Relevant economic circumstances

> üîÅ **Adjustment of Terms (with Borrower Approval)**  
If the borrower is in default, anticipates default, or wishes to take additional loans, the bank may **modify terms** with the borrower‚Äôs approval, provided:
- Repayment period is extended within the **maximum limits** set by the Act or these regulations  
- Instalment amounts or frequencies are changed  
- **No instalments are due less than annually**

> ‚ùå **Prohibited Change**: No modification may extend repayment beyond the maximum allowed unless **Ministerial approval** is obtained.

> ‚úÖ **Ministerial Approval Required**: Any change resulting in a longer repayment term than allowed must be approved by the Minister.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: Even if the Minister approves a term change, the **Minister‚Äôs liability to the bank under the Act is not discharged**.

---

### **9. Interest Rate Regulations**

#### For Banks Under the Bank Act or Alberta Treasury Branches Act:
- Maximum interest rate = **1% + the prime lending rate** in effect on the date the loan agreement is signed.

#### For Other Banks (not under federal Bank Act or Alberta Treasury Branches Act):
- Maximum interest rate = **1% + the average of the prime lending rates** of the following **seven chartered banks**, calculated on the **third Wednesday of the month preceding** the month in question:
  1. Bank of Montreal  
  2. Bank of Nova Scotia  
  3. Royal Bank of Canada  
  4. Toronto Dominion Bank  
  5. Banque Canadienne Nationale  
  6. Banque Provinciale  
  7. Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce  

> üî¢ Rate is **rounded to the nearest 1/8 of 1%**, and if equidistant, the **lower** value is selected.

> üì¢ The Minister must **immediately notify** all affected banks of any rate change.

#### Rate Adjustments:
- For **Bank Act or Alberta banks**: Interest is revised **whenever the prime rate changes**.  
- For **other banks**: Interest is revised on the **first day of each month**.

---

### **10. Default and Enforcement Measures**

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è Default Conditions:
- Failure to make a required payment  
- Borrower advises that default is likely  
- Borrower is **convicted of an offence under the Act**

#### Consequences of Default:
- The **entire outstanding balance** becomes immediately due and payable at the bank‚Äôs option.

#### Bank Actions Upon Default:
The bank may take any action it deems appropriate, including:
- Legal proceedings to recover the loan  
- Obtaining additional security  
- Realizing on existing security (e.g., selling mortgaged property)  
- Compromising with third parties or granting concessions (to non-borrowers)

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: These actions **do not discharge the Minister‚Äôs liability** to the bank under the Act.
