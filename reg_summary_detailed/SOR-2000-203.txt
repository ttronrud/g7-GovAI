## 2. ðŸš« **Exclusions Where Regulations Do Not Apply**

The regulations **do not apply** to radiation exposure in the following scenarios:

- **Medical treatment or diagnosis** (as directed by a qualified medical practitioner under provincial law)
- **Voluntary participation in biomedical research** supervised by a qualified medical professional
- **Caregiving** (support provided to a person receiving nuclear therapy)

> âœ… This recognizes that radiation exposure in medical contexts is subject to medical justification and informed consent, and is not subject to the same strict dose controls as occupational exposure.

## 3. ðŸ›¡ï¸ **Mandatory Radiation Protection Program**

Every licensee must implement a **comprehensive radiation protection program** that includes:

- **Management control over work practices** (e.g., minimizing exposure through engineering controls)
- **Personnel qualification and training** (ensuring staff understand risks and safety procedures)

  - Via **direct monitoring**, or
  - Via **estimation** if direct measurement is impractical (e.g., due to time/resource constraints)

### B. **Action Levels**

- If such a level is reached:

    2. Restore the effectiveness of the radiation protection program
