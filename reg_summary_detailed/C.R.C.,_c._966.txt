**Detailed Summary of the *Automotive Manufacturing Assistance Regulations***

---

### **1. Title and Citation**
These regulations are officially titled the **Automotive Manufacturing Assistance Regulations** and may be cited as such. They form a key piece of Canadian federal legislation aimed at supporting the domestic automotive supply chain and related industries through financial assistance and risk mitigation mechanisms.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

#### **Automobile**
- A four-wheeled passenger vehicle with a seating capacity of **not more than 10 persons**.
- Excludes commercial or off-road vehicles.

#### **Automotive Program**
- Refers to the **Canada-United States Agreement on Automotive Products (CUAAP)**, specifically:
  - **Order in Council 1965-99**, which established the framework for trade and production sharing.
  - **Letters of Commitment** from Canadian automotive manufacturers to the Minister of Industry, outlining production and investment commitments.
- Together, these constitute the "automotive program" under which the regulations operate.

#### **Bus**
- A passenger motor vehicle with **seating for more than 10 persons**, or a chassis for such a vehicle.
- **Exclusions**:
  - Electric trackless trolley buses
  - Amphibious vehicles
  - Tracked or half-tracked vehicles
  - Motor vehicles designed primarily for off-highway use

#### **Eligible Manufacturer**
A company qualifies as an *eligible manufacturer* if it meets **one or more** of the following criteria:

1. **Part or Accessory Manufacturer**:
   - Produces significant quantities of parts, accessories (including tires and tubes), or components used as original equipment in:
     - Automobiles
     - Buses
     - Off-highway vehicles
     - Specified commercial vehicles

2. **Supplier of Materials or Tooling**:
   - Supplies significant quantities of raw materials to part or accessory manufacturers.
   - Supplies tooling used in manufacturing parts or accessories (e.g., dies, molds, jigs, fixtures).

3. **Tooling Manufacturer**:
   - Manufactures significant quantities of tooling (e.g., dies, gauges, patterns) used in the production of parts or accessories for vehicles.

4. **Bus or Off-Highway Vehicle Manufacturer**:
   - Manufactures a significant number of buses, off-highway vehicles, or specified commercial vehicles.

> **Note on Holding Companies**: A company is considered a "holding company" only if it owns a manufacturer as a **subsidiary** (as defined under the Income Tax Act). Subsidiary relationships include:
> - Direct control
> - Control through one or more intermediaries
> - Control through two or more intermediaries
> - Ownership of a subsidiary of a subsidiary

> **Tooling** includes patterns, jigs, fixtures, molds, dies, gauges, and punches.

#### **Off-Highway Vehicle**
- Self-propelled trucks designed primarily for use in:
  - Quarries
  - Mines
  - Gravel/sand pits
  - Construction sites
  - Logging operations
- **Excludes**:
  - Machines used for felling, skidding, piling, loading, or processing logs (even if modified)
  - Items required to be valued separately under tariff item 43803-1

#### **Specified Commercial Vehicle**
- Includes motor trucks, ambulances, hearses, or their chassis.
- **Excludes**:
  - Buses
  - Electric trolley buses
  - Fire trucks
  - Amphibious vehicles
  - Tracked or half-tracked vehicles
  - Golf or invalid carts
  - Straddle carriers
  - Off-highway vehicles
  - Items required under tariff item 43803-1 to be valued separately

#### **Subsidiary Wholly-Owned / Controlled Corporation**
- Defined under the **Income Tax Act**:
  - A *wholly-owned subsidiary* is one where the parent company owns 100% of the shares.
  - A *controlled subsidiary* is one where the parent company holds a majority of voting power or control over the subsidiary.

---

### **3. Succession of the Board**

- The **General Adjustment Assistance Board (Board)** is declared the **successor** to the former **Adjustment Assistance Board**.
- All **property, rights, obligations, and liabilities** of the old Board as of **June 29, 1971**, are automatically transferred to the new Board.

---

### **4. Board Responsibilities**

- Administer **loans and guarantees** under these regulations.
- Perform **additional duties** assigned by the **Minister of Industry, Trade and Commerce**.
- May, after **June 30, 1973**, with **Governor in Council approval**, delegate certain administrative functions to other **government departments or agencies**.

---

### **5. Loan Authorization and Modifications**

- **Before July 1, 1973**, the Board may:
  - Authorize a **change in security** (e.g., collateral) for existing loans.
  - Enter into an **amendment agreement** with Her Majesty (the Crown) to modify terms and conditions of the loan.
- **Restrictions**:
  - **No increase in principal amount**, except through **compounding of interest in arrears**.
  - **No amendment** of terms or conditions that were **originally prescribed by these regulations**.

> This ensures that the core regulatory framework remains intact and that financial terms are not arbitrarily altered.

---

### **6. By-Laws and Operational Procedures**

- The Board may adopt **by-laws and regulations** necessary for:
  - Conducting meetings
  - Managing administrative affairs
  - Fulfilling its duties

- The Board will **use existing government staff and facilities** to carry out its functions.
- To the extent possible, these resources will be **made available** to the Board without duplication or cost.

---

### **7. Loan and Guarantee Conditions**

#### **When a Loan May Be Made**
A loan or guarantee may be provided to an **eligible manufacturer** if:
- The manufacturer is **already indebted** under a prior loan and:
  - There is a **default** or **event of default**, or
  - The Crown’s security is in **imminent danger** of being lost.
- **Financing is not available** from other sources.
- The loan or guarantee would:
  - Provide the manufacturer with a **reasonable prospect of profitability**, or
  - Allow for the **continuation of operations** (partial or full).
  - Protect the Crown’s security.

> This provision acts as a **financial safety net** during downturns caused by the automotive program.

#### **Application Process**
- An eligible manufacturer must submit a **formal application** to the Board.
- Must provide **all information** the Board deems necessary.

#### **Agreement with the Crown**
- Upon approval, the applicant may enter into an agreement with Her Majesty.
- The agreement must be in a form and contain provisions considered **necessary by the Board**.

---

### **8. Funding and Interest Rates**

- **Funding Source**: All loans and guarantee payments are made from **Parliamentary appropriations**.
- **Interest Rate**:
  - Set at the **prevailing rate charged to Crown corporations** at the time of authorization, **plus 2%**.
  - Applies to loans of similar term.
- **Guarantee Fee**:
  - 1% per annum of the guaranteed loan amount.
  - Paid by the borrower under terms set by the Board.

---

### **9. Security for Loans and Guarantees**

The Board may take, accept, or acquire various forms of security to secure loans or guarantees, including:

- **Securities**:
  - Stocks, bonds, debentures of municipal or other corporations
  - Canadian, provincial, municipal, British, or foreign government securities (secured by mortgage or otherwise)
- **Negotiable Instruments**:
  - Warehouse receipts
  - Bills of lading
- **Physical Assets**:
  - Goods, wares, merchandise
- **Mortgages/Hypothecs**:
  - On real or personal property (movable or immovable)
- **Trustee/Receiver Certificates**:
  - Issued by trustees or receivers of an eligible manufacturer’s assets

> This broad security clause ensures that the Crown can protect its interest in case of default.

---

### **10. Loan and Guarantee Terms**

- **Loan Term**:
  - **Up to 20 years** if the loan is for:
    - Acquisition
    - Modernization
    - Development
    - Conversion
    - Expansion of buildings or real property
  - **Up to 10 years** for all other purposes.

- **Guarantee Term**:
  - Fixed by the Board, with no fixed maximum.

- **Early Repayment**:
  - A loan may be repaid **in full or in part** before maturity **without notice or penalty**.
  - Method of repayment is prescribed by the Board.

---

### **11. Insurance Requirements**

- Borrowers must maintain **insurance coverage** as prescribed by the Board during the term of the loan or guarantee.

---

### **12. Security Surrender or Re-transfer**

- Any security given for a loan or guarantee may be:
  - **Surrendered**
  - **Retransferred**
  - **Reconveyed** (reassigned)
- In exchange for **other security**.
- Requires **Board or designated advisory committee approval** (as appointed by the Minister).
