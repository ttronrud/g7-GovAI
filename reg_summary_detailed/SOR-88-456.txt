### **4. Criteria for Validity of Exemption Claims (Section 11 of the Act)**

For a claim for exemption from disclosing information under **section 11** of the Act to be valid, the Minister must consider **three core criteria**:

#### âœ… **Primary Criteria (Must be met for validity)**:
1. **Not Publicly Available**  
   The information must not be accessible to the general public or otherwise available through open channels.

2. **Reasonable Measures to Maintain Confidentiality**  
   The claimant must have taken reasonable steps to ensure the information remains confidential (e.g., internal access controls, non-disclosure agreements).

3. **Actual or Potential Economic Value**  
   The information must have **actual or potential economic value** to the claimant or their competitors due to its non-public nature. Disclosure would result in:
   - A **material financial loss** to the claimant, OR
   - A **material financial gain** to the claimantâ€™s competitors.

#### âœ… **Additional Criterion (For Validity Only â€“ Not for Rejection)**:
- The **cost and business resources** expended by the claimant to develop the information must be **substantial** in the circumstances.

> ðŸ” **Important Note**: This additional criterion is **not used to deny** a claim â€” it only strengthens the case *for* exemption when present.

### **5. Confidentiality Exception â€“ What Does â€œNot Publicly Availableâ€ Mean?**

Even if the information is known to certain individuals, it **does not cease to be non-publicly available** if:
- The individuals obtained it **in confidence** (expressly or impliedly),
- Are **contractually or legally bound** (by trust, law, or equity) to maintain confidentiality, or
- Possess it **without lawful authority** (i.e., without right to possess it).
