**Detailed Summary of the Newfoundland and Labrador Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations**  
*Statutory Reference: S.C. 2004, c. 10 (as amended), effective December 13, 2004; came into force on December 17, 2004*

---

### **1. Purpose and Legal Authority**
These regulations are issued by the **Lieutenant Governor in Council** of Newfoundland and Labrador under **subsection 18(1) of the Sex Offender Information Registration Act (SOIRA)**. The primary purpose is to establish the procedures, authorized personnel, reporting mechanisms, and geographic scope for the registration and management of information related to sex offenders within the province.

The regulations provide the operational framework for the **Newfoundland and Labrador Sex Offender Information Registration Centre (NLSOIRC)**, ensuring that sex offender information is collected, registered, and maintained in a standardized, accessible, and secure manner across the province.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

- **Act**: Refers to the *Sex Offender Information Registration Act* (SOIRA), which provides the legal basis for the registration and reporting of sex offenders.
- **NLSOIRC**: The **Newfoundland and Labrador Sex Offender Information Registration Centre**, administered by the **Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP) “B” Division headquarters**. This is the central body responsible for receiving, processing, and maintaining sex offender information in the province.
- **RCMP**: The **Royal Canadian Mounted Police**, the federal law enforcement agency responsible for policing and information management under this regulation.

---

### **3. Reporting and Notification Requirements**

#### **A. Reporting by Sex Offenders**
- A **sex offender whose main residence is in Newfoundland and Labrador** may:
  - Make reports required under **paragraph 4.1(a) or (b)** or **section 4.3** of the SOIRA (e.g., updates to registration details, changes in address, employment, or contact information).
  - Provide notifications required under **section 6** of the Act (e.g., relocation, change in status, or other significant life events).
- These reports or notifications **can be submitted by telephone** directly to the **NLSOIRC**.

> This provision ensures that individuals living in the province can easily and conveniently report updates to their registration status without needing to visit a physical office or submit written forms.

---

### **4. Authorized Persons to Collect Information**

The following individuals or entities are **authorized** in Newfoundland and Labrador to collect information for the purposes of the SOIRA:

1. **A member of the RCMP** – Any RCMP officer is authorized to collect sex offender information.
2. **A police officer within the meaning of paragraph 2(l) of the Royal Newfoundland Constabulary Act, 1992** – This includes officers of the **Royal Newfoundland Constabulary (RNC)**, as defined under the RNC Act. The definition is inclusive and allows for all RNC personnel who meet the statutory criteria.
3. **A person employed at the NLSOIRC whose duties include collection of information under the Act** – Staff at the NLSOIRC who are specifically assigned to gather data related to sex offenders are authorized to collect such information.

> This ensures that both federal (RCMP) and provincial (RNC) law enforcement agencies have the legal authority to collect information during investigations, routine checks, or as part of their duties.

---

### **5. Authorized Persons to Register Information**

- **Persons employed at the NLSOIRC whose duties include the registration of information** are authorized to:
  - Register sex offender information in the official database under the Act.

> This ensures that data collected by authorized personnel is properly recorded and maintained within the centralized NLSOIRC system, maintaining accuracy and accountability.

---

### **6. Designated Registration Centres and Service Areas**

- The **Schedule** to these regulations lists specific **RCMP district offices and detachments**, as well as **Royal Newfoundland Constabulary headquarters**, that are designated as **registration centres** in the province.
- **Key Points**:
  - The **designated area of service** for each registration centre is the **entire Province of Newfoundland and Labrador**.
  - This means that **any sex offender or law enforcement officer** can access or submit information to any of these centres, regardless of location within the province.

> This broad service area ensures nationwide accessibility and equitable access to registration services across remote and rural communities.

---

### **8. Effective Date**

- These regulations come into force on the **later of two dates**:
  1. The date on which the *Sex Offender Information Registration Act* (chapter 10 of the Statutes of Canada, 2004) comes into force.
  2. The date on which these regulations are officially registered in the *Newfoundland and Labrador Gazette*.

- The regulations were **registered on December 13, 2004**, and came into force on **December 17, 2004**.

> This staggered implementation ensures that the legal framework is fully operational before enforcement begins.

---

### **9. Key Objectives and Implications**

| Objective | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Accessibility** | Sex offenders and law enforcement can easily report or collect information via phone or in person at designated centres across the province. |
| **Interagency Cooperation** | Both RCMP and RNC are empowered to collect and register information, promoting coordination between federal and provincial agencies. |
| **Geographic Coverage** | Every region of Newfoundland and Labrador—urban, rural, and remote—is covered, ensuring no community is left out. |
| **Privacy and Compliance** | The regulations are designed to balance public safety with individual rights, requiring only specific, legally mandated reporting. |
| **Centralized Database** | All information is managed through the NLSOIRC, enabling consistent tracking, monitoring, and public access (where permitted under privacy laws). |

---

### **10. Legal and Policy Context**

These regulations are part of a broader national framework established under the **Sex Offender Information Registration Act (SOIRA)**, which was introduced to:
- Improve public safety by enabling better tracking of individuals convicted of sex offences.
- Ensure transparency and accountability in how sex offender data is collected and maintained.
- Support community awareness and protection, especially in areas with high vulnerability to sexual crime.

They align with federal standards while adapting to Newfoundland and Labrador’s unique geographic and demographic challenges—particularly its vast, sparsely populated, and remote regions.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Newfoundland and Labrador Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations* provide a clear, practical, and accessible framework for managing sex offender information in the province. By defining authorized personnel, establishing a comprehensive network of registration centres, and allowing telephonic reporting, the regulations ensure that:
- Law enforcement agencies can efficiently collect and maintain data.
- Sex offenders can comply with reporting obligations without significant barriers.
- Public safety is enhanced through timely and accurate information sharing.

These measures reflect a balanced approach to protecting communities while respecting individual rights and operational realities in a geographically diverse province.
