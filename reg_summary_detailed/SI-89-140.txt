**Detailed Summary of the Statutes of Canada Distribution Direction, No. 2**

**Regulatory Authority and Context:**

The regulation is issued under the authority of **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of Supply and Services**. This issuance is grounded in **subsection 10(2) of the Publication of Statutes Act**, which empowers the Governor General to issue directions regarding the distribution of Acts of Parliament.

This particular direction—**Statutes of Canada Distribution Direction, No. 2**—establishes a formal, binding procedure for the distribution of official copies of the **Acts of the Parliament of Canada** to specific parliamentary bodies.

---

**Key Provisions of the Direction:**

1. **Distribution Requirement:**
   - **Four copies** of each Act of the Parliament of Canada must be delivered or sent **without charge** to the appropriate chairperson(s) of a specific parliamentary committee.

2. **Receiving Bodies:**
   - The copies must be sent to:
     - The **chairperson of the Committee of the House of Commons**, or
     - One of the **joint chairpersons** of the **Committee of the Senate**, or
     - One of the **joint chairpersons** of the **joint committee** of both Houses of Parliament.

3. **Purpose of the Committee:**
   - The committee established for this purpose is specifically tasked with:
     - **Reviewing** statutory instruments (i.e., regulations and orders made under the authority of Acts of Parliament)
     - **Scrutinizing** these instruments to ensure they are consistent with the original legislation and constitutional principles
     - Providing oversight and accountability in the legislative process

4. **No Cost to Recipients:**
   - The distribution is **free of charge**. This ensures that the reviewing bodies have full and timely access to the primary legislation without financial barriers.

5. **Citation of the Direction:**
   - The direction is officially titled: **Statutes of Canada Distribution Direction, No. 2**. This allows for clear identification and reference in legal and administrative contexts.

---

**Objectives and Significance:**

- **Transparency and Accountability:** By ensuring that the legislative text is accessible to the committees responsible for reviewing statutory instruments, the direction supports transparency in how laws are implemented and interpreted.
  
- **Effective Scrutiny:** The provision enables the committees to conduct thorough reviews of regulations and other instruments, ensuring they are properly aligned with the intent of the original Acts.

- **Legal Consistency:** The requirement to distribute the full text of Acts ensures that all reviewing bodies operate with accurate and authoritative source material.

- **Administrative Efficiency:** The directive establishes a standardized, repeatable process for distributing legislation, reducing ambiguity and ensuring consistency across parliamentary oversight bodies.

---

**Limitations and Scope:**

- The direction applies **only to the Acts of the Parliament of Canada**, not to bills, proposed legislation, or draft laws.
- It does not extend to the distribution of **statutory instruments** themselves, only to the **Acts** that authorize them.
- The requirement applies to **all Acts** of Parliament, regardless of subject matter or date.

---

**Conclusion:**

The *Statutes of Canada Distribution Direction, No. 2* is a procedural regulation that ensures the timely and free distribution of the full text of Canadian Acts of Parliament to the key committees responsible for reviewing statutory instruments. It strengthens parliamentary oversight, supports legislative accountability, and upholds the principles of transparency and accessibility in Canada’s legislative process. The direction is a practical implementation of the broader framework established by the *Publication of Statutes Act*, reinforcing the rule of law and the integrity of Canada’s legislative system.
