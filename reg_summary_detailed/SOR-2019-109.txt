### **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**
These regulations establish criteria for identifying **"low-materiality fees"** â€” fees that are deemed to have minimal financial significance to federal government revenue. The purpose is to:
- Improve transparency and accountability in fee structures.
- Reduce administrative burden on federal departments by exempting certain small fees from detailed reporting and oversight requirements.
- Promote cost-effectiveness in fee management while ensuring compliance with fiscal responsibility principles.

The regulations are made under **paragraph 22(2)(a)** of the *Service Fees Act*, on the recommendation of the **President of the Treasury Board**.

### **2. Key Definitions**
- **Act**: Refers to the *Service Fees Act*.
- **Report**: A formal report submitted under **subsection 20(1)** of the Act, which details annual revenue generated from a particular fee.

> These reports are critical for assessing whether a fee meets the threshold for being classified as low-materiality.

### **3. What Constitutes a Low-Materiality Fee?**

A fee is considered a **low-materiality fee** if it meets **any one** of the following conditions:

#### âœ… Condition A: Less than $51
Any fee **under $51** is automatically classified as a low-materiality fee.

#### âœ… Condition B: $51 or more but less than $151 â€” with specific criteria
A fee between **$51 and $151** qualifies as low-materiality **only if**:
1. The **three most recent reports** (prior to the start of the current fiscal year) show **no more than $500,000 in annual revenue** from that fee.
2. The fee has been **adjusted since the last report**, and the same revenue cap ($500,000) applies.
3. **No three consecutive reports** have shown more than $500,000 in annual revenue.
4. The fee has **never reached $151 or more** since these regulations came into force, nor has it been listed in **Schedule 2**.
5. The fee was previously classified as low-materiality under paragraph (a), (i) to (iii), or (c), and has **not ceased** to be so under section 3.

> This condition is designed to prevent fees from being reclassified as high-materiality due to minor fluctuations or temporary spikes in revenue.

#### âœ… Condition C: Fees Listed in Schedule 1
All fees **explicitly listed in Schedule 1** are automatically considered low-materiality fees â€” regardless of their amount â€” **unless otherwise excluded**.

> This list includes numerous operational, administrative, and service-related fees across federal departments and agencies.

### **4. Exclusions from Low-Materiality Status**

The following fees are **exempt** from being classified as low-materiality fees:

- **Fees determined by formula** (e.g., based on usage, volume, or performance metrics).
- **Fees listed in Schedule 2** â€” which includes fees that are considered high-materiality or subject to special scrutiny.

> These exclusions ensure that fees with dynamic or revenue-generating potential (like those tied to usage) are not automatically exempted.

### **5. When a Low-Materiality Fee Ceases to Be One**

A fee **ceases to be low-materiality** on any of the following dates:

| Trigger | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Fee increased to $151 or more** | The moment the fee reaches or exceeds $151. |
| **Third consecutive report exceeds $500,000** | If the fee is between $51 and $151, and **three consecutive reports** show **more than $500,000** in annual revenue. |
| **Adjustment increases fee to $51â€“$151**, but revenue exceeds $500,000 | After an adjustment, if the three most recent reports show revenue over $500,000. |
| **Added to Schedule 2** | Once a fee is moved into Schedule 2 (high-materiality list), it loses low-materiality status. |
| **Removed from Schedule 1** | If a fee is removed from Schedule 1 and: <br> - Is $151 or more, OR <br> - Is $51â€“$151 and three recent reports show >$500,000 in revenue. |

> These triggers ensure that fees that grow in revenue or are reclassified are promptly brought under stricter oversight.

### **7. Exception to the $51â€“$151 Rule (Critical Provision)**

> This is a **critical safeguard** to prevent fees with significant revenue streams from being incorrectly classified as low-materiality.
