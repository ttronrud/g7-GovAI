**Detailed Summary of the Canadian Livestock Feed Board Headquarters Regulations**

**Regulation Title:**  
Canadian Livestock Feed Board Headquarters Regulations

**Citation:**  
These Regulations may be cited as the *Canadian Livestock Feed Board Headquarters Regulations*.

**Key Provision Summary:**

The regulation establishes the official location of the headquarters of the Canadian Livestock Feed Board (CLFB). Specifically, it designates the **City of Montreal, in the Province of Quebec**, as the city where the headquarters of the CLFB shall be situated.

**Detailed Interpretation and Implications:**

1. **Purpose of the Regulation:**  
   This regulation serves a foundational administrative function by formally establishing the geographic location of the CLFB’s central office. This designation is critical for legal, operational, and jurisdictional clarity, ensuring that the Board has a recognized and official seat of governance.

2. **Designated Location:**  
   - The City of Montreal is explicitly named as the location of the headquarters.  
   - Montreal is located in the province of Quebec, which is a key agricultural and industrial region in Canada, particularly in livestock and feed production.  
   - This choice may reflect strategic considerations such as proximity to key agricultural markets, access to expertise, infrastructure, and regional stakeholder networks.

3. **Legal and Administrative Significance:**  
   - The designation provides legal certainty regarding where the Board conducts its primary administrative, regulatory, and policy functions.  
   - It may influence matters such as jurisdictional authority, service delivery, reporting requirements, and compliance obligations.  
   - It also affects the Board’s relationship with provincial and federal authorities, as well as with stakeholders in Quebec and beyond.

4. **Scope and Application:**  
   - The regulation applies to all official activities, communications, and administrative functions of the Canadian Livestock Feed Board.  
   - It does not alter the Board’s mandate or scope of operations but establishes a physical and legal base for its operations.

5. **Potential Broader Implications:**  
   - The headquarters location may impact the Board’s ability to engage with regional livestock producers, feed manufacturers, and government agencies.  
   - It may also influence the distribution of resources, staffing, and regional outreach efforts.  
   - The choice of Montreal could reflect a balance between centralization and regional accessibility, given Quebec’s importance in Canada’s agricultural sector.

**Conclusion:**  
The *Canadian Livestock Feed Board Headquarters Regulations* is a concise but essential piece of administrative legislation that formally designates the City of Montreal, Quebec, as the official seat of the Canadian Livestock Feed Board. This designation provides a clear and legally binding foundation for the Board’s operations, ensuring consistency in governance and facilitating coordination with stakeholders across Canada. While the regulation is narrow in scope, its impact on the Board’s structure, jurisdiction, and operational effectiveness is significant.
