**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Regulation Text:**  
*Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, pursuant to paragraph (c.1)a of the definition "appropriate Minister" in section 2 of the Financial Administration Act, hereby designates the Minister of Health as the appropriate Minister with respect to the Assisted Human Reproduction Agency of Canada for the purposes of the Financial Administration Act. S.C. 1992, c. 1, s. 69(2)*

---

**1. Purpose of the Regulation:**  
This regulation formally designates the **Minister of Health** as the **"appropriate Minister"** for the **Assisted Human Reproduction Agency of Canada (AHRA)** under the **Financial Administration Act (FAA)**. This designation is critical because it establishes the minister responsible for overseeing the financial administration and accountability of the AHRA, including the approval of expenditures, financial reporting, and compliance with federal financial regulations.

---

**2. Legal Authority and Basis:**  
- The designation is made under **section 2 of the Financial Administration Act**, which defines the term "appropriate Minister."
- Specifically, the regulation invokes **paragraph (c.1)a** of that definition, which provides that an "appropriate Minister" is a minister designated by the Governor in Council (i.e., the federal Cabinet) for a specific agency or organization.
- The Governor General in Council acts on the **recommendation of the Prime Minister**, indicating that the decision is part of the standard federal process for ministerial appointments and designations, reflecting political and administrative consensus.

---

**3. Scope and Application:**  
- The designation applies **only to the Assisted Human Reproduction Agency of Canada (AHRA)**.
- It is **specific and limited in scope**—it does not extend to other agencies or departments.
- The designation is effective **for the purposes of the Financial Administration Act**, meaning it governs financial oversight, budgetary processes, financial reporting, audit requirements, and compliance with federal financial rules and procedures.

---

**4. Implications of the Designation:**  
- The **Minister of Health** becomes the principal financial authority for the AHRA, responsible for:
  - Approving the agency’s financial plans and budgets.
  - Ensuring compliance with financial administration rules.
  - Receiving and reviewing financial reports from the AHRA.
  - Acting as the point of contact for financial audits or oversight by the Auditor General or other federal financial bodies.
- This designation ensures that the AHRA operates within a clear financial accountability framework, with a minister who has both policy and financial oversight responsibilities.

---

**5. Historical and Contextual Background:**  
- This regulation was enacted in **1992** (S.C. 1992, c. 1, s. 69(2)) as part of the establishment of the AHRA, which was created to regulate and oversee assisted human reproduction in Canada.
- The AHRA was established to ensure that assisted reproductive technologies (such as in vitro fertilization, surrogacy, and embryo storage) are conducted safely, ethically, and in compliance with federal standards.
- The 1992 designation reflects the alignment of financial governance with the health sector’s mandate, placing the AHRA under the purview of the Minister of Health, who is best positioned to oversee health-related regulatory and ethical issues.

---

**6. Key Legal and Administrative Significance:**  
- The regulation ensures **legal clarity** regarding financial accountability for the AHRA.
- It enables the federal government to apply uniform financial administration standards across all federal agencies, including those in the health and reproductive services sector.
- It supports **transparency and public accountability**, as financial decisions involving taxpayer funds are subject to ministerial review and oversight.

---

**Conclusion:**  
This regulation formally assigns the **Minister of Health** as the **appropriate Minister** responsible for the financial administration of the **Assisted Human Reproduction Agency of Canada** under the **Financial Administration Act**. It is a foundational step in ensuring that the AHRA operates with proper financial oversight, accountability, and alignment with broader health policy objectives. The designation is legally grounded, politically justified, and operationally significant for the agency’s governance and compliance with federal financial standards.
