## üîç **Overview of the Regulation**

**Title:**  
*New Harmonized Value-added Tax System Regulations, No. 2*

**Issued by:**  
Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance.

**Legal Basis:**  
Sections 236.01a, 277b, and 277.1c of the *Excise Tax Act*.

**Purpose:**  
To implement and refine the **Harmonized Sales Tax (HST)** system in Canada by introducing specific rules governing the taxation of **imported taxable supplies**, particularly those involving **provincial investment plans** and **stratified investment plans**, and to clarify how **taxes are applied based on location, usage, and type of supply**.

This regulation expands and adapts existing HST rules to address unique tax treatment for financial institutions with provincial-specific investment offerings and to ensure equitable tax collection across provinces.

## üöó Vehicle/Allowance Tax Rules (Paragraph 174(f))

For **allowances paid for motor vehicle use**:

- If **all or substantially all** of the supplies or vehicle use occurs in **one participating province**, the tax rate is:
  > HST rate (from subsection 165(1)) + **the province‚Äôs HST rate**

- If the supplies or use occur in **two or more provinces**, the tax rate is:
  > HST rate + **the lowest HST rate** among the participating provinces.

> This ensures that the tax burden is proportional to where the activity occurs.

---

## üö´ Exclusion of Certain HST Rules (Subsections 178.3(5), (6), 178.4(5), (6))

> These rules **do not apply** to **supplies of exclusive products made by independent sales contractors**.

> This means that HST rules related to **tax on internal charges or expenses** do **not apply** in such cases ‚Äî simplifying compliance for independent contractors.

---

## üöò Vehicle Importation Rules (Description of I in Paragraph 202(4)(b))

For **bringing a vehicle or aircraft into a participating province**:

- **If brought from another participating province**:
  - Tax rate = **HST rate of destination province** ‚Äì **HST rate of origin province**
  - This creates a **tax differential** based on provincial rates.

- **In any other case** (e.g., from non-participating province or domestic movement):
  - Tax rate = **0%**

> This reflects the principle that **inter-provincial movement between HST provinces** is taxed at the difference between the rates, while movement into non-HST provinces is exempt.

## üì¶ Application of HST to Imported Supplies (Section 218.1(1))

### When a recipient is a **Provincial Investment Plan or Stratified Investment Plan**

If a person receives an imported taxable supply (property or service) and is a **provincial investment plan or stratified investment plan** at the time the consideration becomes due or is paid, **additional HST must be paid**.

#### Formula:
> **Additional Tax = (Tax Rate for Province) √ó (Value of Consideration) √ó (Extent of Use in Province)**

Where:

| Case | Extent of Use |
|------|---------------|
| **Provincial Stratified Investment Plan** | Sum of all percentages (from section 51 of the Attribution Method Regulations) indicating how much of the property/service was used for activities related to a **provincial series** in that province |
| **Provincial Investment Plan (for the province)** | 100% |
| **Provincial Investment Plan (for another province)** | 0% |

> Applies to both **intangible personal property** (e.g., software, licenses) and **physical goods** (e.g., equipment, machinery) under paragraph (b) of *imported taxable supply*.

---

## üö® Threshold for Exemption (Paragraph 218.1(1)(a))

If a **provincial stratified investment plan** receives an imported supply of intangible personal property or service, and the **total extent of use** in participating provinces is **less than 10%** of total use across all provinces, then:

> **No additional HST is payable** under section 218.1(1)(a).

> Formula to determine threshold:
> > (Total extent of use in participating provinces) / (Total extent of use in all provinces) < 10%

> This provides **exemption** for minor or non-provincial usage.

> Additional tax must be paid on **internal and external charges** and **qualifying consideration** ‚Äî **only to the extent** that the outlay or expense relates to activities in a **particular participating province**.
