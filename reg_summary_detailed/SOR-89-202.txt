## üìú **Regulatory Summary: Revocation and Establishment of the Export Control List (S.C. 1987, c. 15, s. 26)**

### üîç **Overview**
On **July 14, 1987**, Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, acting on the recommendation of the Secretary of State for External Affairs, **revoked the previous Export Control List** (C.R.C., c. 601) and **established a new, updated Export Control List** under **Section 3 and Section 6 of the Export and Import Permits Act**.

This regulation defines the scope, applicability, and exceptions for **goods and technologies subject to export controls** in Canada. It reflects international agreements and national security interests, particularly in the areas of **dual-use items, nuclear materials, firearms, and agricultural products**.

## üö® **Export Control Scope (Subject to Control)**

The following goods and technologies are subject to export control **based on their intended destination and classification**:

### ‚úÖ **Group 1: Goods under Wassenaar Arrangement**
- All goods and technologies described in **Group 1 of the Guide**.
- Canada has agreed to control these items **in accordance with the Wassenaar Arrangement**.
- Applies to **all destinations** unless otherwise specified.

> üìå This group includes dual-use technologies (e.g., advanced electronics, chemicals, software) that could be used for both civilian and military purposes.

### ‚úÖ **Group 2: Firearms and Related Items**
Applies to **firearms and related technologies**, with specific exceptions and conditions:

#### a) **General Controls**
- All firearms (as defined in the *Criminal Code*, Section 2) **except antique firearms** (as defined in subsection 84(1) of the *Criminal Code*).
- Excludes **antique firearms** (i.e., those manufactured before 1938, unless otherwise defined).

#### b) **Prohibited or Restricted Firearms**
- Includes firearms:
  - Listed in **Part 1 of the Firearms Regulations** (prohibited or restricted);
  - Designed to discharge a projectile at:
    - Muzzle velocity **> 152.4 m/s**, or
    - Muzzle energy **> 5.7 joules**.
- Also includes **smooth-bore weapons**, **automatic weapons**, and **projectors** described in **items 2-1 and 2-2.a of the Guide**.

#### c) **Exemptions for Pre-1938 Weapons**
- **Smooth-bore weapons, automatic weapons, or projectors**:
  - Used for **hunting or sporting purposes**;
  - Manufactured **before 1938**;
  - Are **not antique firearms**; or
  - Use **non-centre fire cased ammunition**.

> üîç *Note: These exceptions are limited and require verification of origin, use, and compliance with the Inter-American Convention on Firearms.*

### ‚úÖ **Group 3: Nuclear Materials and Technologies**
Applies to **nuclear-related goods and technologies** under two international frameworks:

#### a) **Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons (NPT)**
- Canada is a signatory to the NPT (entered into force March 5, 1970).
- Controls all nuclear-related items in accordance with the treaty.

#### b) **Nuclear Suppliers Group (NSG) Guidelines**
- Applies to transfers governed by:
  - **INFCIRC/254/Rev.14/Part 1** (for nuclear materials and equipment);
  - **INFCIRC/254/Rev.11/Part 2** (for nuclear-related dual-use equipment, materials, software, and technology).

#### ‚ùå **Exclusions (Non-Controlled Items)**
Certain items containing small amounts of nuclear materials are **exempt** from control, including:

| Exempt Item | Conditions |
|-----------|------------|
| Natural or depleted uranium ‚â§ 4 grams | In a sensing component (e.g., detectors) |
| Alloys < 5% thorium | For non-nuclear use |
| Thorium-containing ceramics | Manufactured for non-nuclear use |
| Medicinal substances | Containing source material |

#### ‚úÖ **Deuterium and Heavy Water**

### ‚úÖ **Group 4: Nuclear-Related Dual-Use Equipment and Technology**
- Applies to goods and technologies under the **NPT** and **NSG Guidelines (INFCIRC/254/Rev.11/Part 2)**.

> üìå This group overlaps with Group 3 but focuses more on **equipment and technology** rather than raw materials.
