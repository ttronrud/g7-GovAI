**Detailed Summary of the Notice of Relocation Regulations (S.C. 2019, c. 16, s. 12 & s. 27(2); R.S., c. 3 (2nd Supp.))**

---

### **Overview**

These regulations, made under the authority of the *Divorce Act* (the "Act"), implement and prescribe specific requirements for the **notice of relocation** and **objection to relocation** in family law proceedings involving children of a marriage. The regulations are designed to ensure transparency, fairness, and clarity when a parent or person with parenting responsibilities intends to move, or when another party objects to such a move.

They came into force on **March 1, 2021**, as referenced in the note (SI/2019-82), though they may have been registered later and would then take effect upon registration.

---

## **Key Provisions and Purpose**

The primary purpose of these regulations is to provide a standardized, structured process for:

1. **Notifying** a personâ€™s intention to relocate (a parent or guardian),
2. **Allowing others with parenting responsibilities** to object to the relocation,
3. **Enabling contact persons** (under a contact order) to notify their intention to change residence,
4. **Ensuring all relevant parties are informed** of key details about the relocation, including timelines, addresses, and proposed arrangements for parenting time or contact.

This helps courts assess the impact of relocation on children and supports the best interests of the child standard.

## **Core Requirements by Scenario**

### **1. Notice of Relocation (Under Section 16.9(2)(d))**

A person who intends to relocate must provide a formal notice using **Form 1 of the Schedule**. This form includes the following prescribed information:

#### **Information Relating to the Person Giving the Notice:**
- Full name of the person intending to relocate.
- Current place of residence and contact information (e.g., phone number, email).
- Name of any child of the marriage who is relocating (if applicable).
- Name of any other child of the marriage for whom the person has **parenting time or decision-making responsibility**.
- Name of any person who has **parenting time, decision-making responsibility, or contact** under a contact order in relation to any child of the marriage (referred to in points a or b above).

#### **Information Relating to the Intended Relocation:**
- Expected date of relocation.
- Address of the new place of residence.
- Contact information of the person or child (as applicable) at the new address.
- A **proposal** for how parenting time, decision-making responsibility, or contact will be exercised after the move (e.g., visitation schedule, communication methods).

### **2. Notification Upon Receiving a Relocation Notice (Under Section 16.91(2)(d))**

When a person receives a notice of relocation, they must provide the following information in writing:

- Full name of the person who gave the relocation notice.
- Their current place of residence and contact information.

> ðŸ” *Purpose:* Allows the receiving party to verify the identity and current status of the relocating person and to prepare a response (e.g., to object or negotiate terms).

### **3. Objection to Relocation (Under Section 16.91(1)(b)(i)(A))**

Any person who wishes to **object to a relocation** must submit a formal objection using **Form 2 of the Schedule**. The form requires:

#### **Information Relating to the Person Objecting:**
- Full name of the person objecting.
- Current place of residence and contact information.
- **Reasons for the objection** (e.g., impact on childâ€™s education, health, or emotional well-being).
- **Views on the proposed parenting arrangements** (e.g., disagreement with visitation schedule, concern about contact frequency or quality).

> ðŸ” *Purpose:* Provides a structured mechanism for non-relocating parents or caregivers to raise valid concerns and contribute to a fair and balanced outcome.

### **4. Notice of Change in Residence for a Contact Person (Under Section 16.96(2))**

A person who has **contact with a child under a contact order** and intends to **change their place of residence** must provide notice using **Form 3 of the Schedule**. This includes:

#### **Information Relating to the Person with Contact:**
- Full name of the person with contact.
- Current place of residence and contact information.
- Name of the child specified in the contact order.
- Name of any person who has parenting time or decision-making responsibility in respect of that child.

#### **Information Relating to the Intended Change in Residence:**
- Expected date of the change in residence.
- New address and contact information.
- A **proposal for how contact could be exercised** in light of the move (e.g., modified visitation, alternative communication methods).

> ðŸ” *Purpose:* Ensures that contact persons are not caught off guard and that changes in location are properly communicated to the childâ€™s other caregiver(s) or parent(s), allowing for adjustments to the contact schedule.

## **General Rules on Effective Date**
