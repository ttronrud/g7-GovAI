- **Purpose**: To **revise and replace** the outdated *Phosphorus Concentration Control Regulations, C.R.C., c. 393*, with updated, science-based limits on phosphorus content in household and commercial cleaning products.

### **2. Exemption: Cleaning Products in Transit**
- **Section 117 of CEPA 1999 does not apply** to cleaning products that are:
  - In **transit through Canada**,
  - From a place **outside Canada** to another place **outside Canada**.
- This means that **phosphorus concentration limits do not apply** to products being transported internationally through Canada, as long as they are not intended for sale or use within Canada.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note: This exemption applies only to transit; if a product is imported into Canada for sale or distribution, the phosphorus limits apply.*

### **3. Definition of Phosphorus for Regulatory Purposes**
- **Paragraph 118(1)(a)** of CEPA 1999 defines **phosphorus and its compounds** as **nutrients**.
- This classification is significant because:
  - Excess nutrients in waterways contribute to **eutrophication** (algal blooms, oxygen depletion, dead zones).
  - Phosphorus is a key driver of water pollution, especially in sensitive aquatic ecosystems.
  - Therefore, limiting phosphorus in detergents helps protect water quality and biodiversity.
