### **Regulatory Summary: Designation of Ministers under the Preclearance Act, 2016 (S.C. 2017, c. 27)**

#### **1. Legal Authority and Context**
The regulation is issued under **subsection 4(1)** of the *Preclearance Act, 2016* (S.C. 2017, c. 27), which empowers the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister) to designate a Minister of the Crown as the **"responsible Minister"** for the purposes of the Act.

This designation establishes a formal administrative responsibility for overseeing the implementation and enforcement of the Act, particularly in relation to the preclearance of goods and persons entering Canada through certain international ports or border facilities.

---

#### **2. Designation of the Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness (PSEP)**

**Under subsection 4(1):**  
- The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness (PSEP)** is designated as the **responsible Minister** for the purposes of the entire *Preclearance Act, 2016*.

**Implications:**
- The Minister of PSEP holds overarching authority and accountability for all aspects of the preclearance regime as defined in the Act.
- This includes policy development, intergovernmental coordination, regulatory oversight, and ensuring compliance with the Act’s provisions related to the preclearance of goods, passengers, and other entities at designated international entry points (e.g., airports, seaports, land border crossings).
- As a member of the Queen’s Privy Council, the Minister has the constitutional standing to act on behalf of the Crown in this regulatory capacity.

#### **3. Additional Designations under Subsections 4(2) and (3) – Sector-Specific Responsibilities**

The Act allows for **sector-specific ministerial designations** under subsections 4(2) and (4)(3), enabling more targeted oversight in areas where infrastructure or transportation systems are involved.

##### **a. Designation under subsection 4(2): Highways, Roads, Bridges, Tunnels, and Paths**
- The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness (PSEP)** is designated as the Minister responsible for the purposes of **Sections 6 to 8** of the Act in circumstances involving:
  - Highways
  - Roads
  - Bridges
  - Tunnels
  - Paths

**Scope of Responsibility:**
- This designation applies when preclearance or related activities (e.g., security screening, access control, emergency response planning) pertain to land-based transportation infrastructure.
- The Minister of PSEP will oversee coordination between public safety, emergency management, and infrastructure stakeholders in these domains.
- This includes ensuring that preclearance protocols are aligned with emergency preparedness standards and that infrastructure is secured against threats such as terrorism, smuggling, or natural disasters.

##### **b. Designation under subsection 4(2): Marine Navigation, Infrastructure, Aerodromes, and Railways**
- The **Minister of Transport** is designated as the Minister responsible for the purposes of **Sections 6 to 8** of the Act in circumstances involving:
  - Marine navigation
  - Marine infrastructure
  - Aerodromes (airports)
  - Railways and railway terminals

**Scope of Responsibility:**
- This designation applies to preclearance activities related to maritime and air transport infrastructure.
- The Minister of Transport is responsible for ensuring that preclearance measures are integrated with the operational and safety standards of the transportation sector.
- This includes coordination with maritime authorities, aviation regulators, and rail operators to ensure that security and preclearance protocols are effective, efficient, and compliant with national and international standards.

> **Note:** Sections 6 to 8 of the Act typically relate to the **provisions on the use of preclearance facilities**, including access rights, security requirements, and the responsibilities of relevant authorities in managing and operating such facilities.
