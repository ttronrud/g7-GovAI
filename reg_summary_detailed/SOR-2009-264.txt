### 1. **Manufacturing & Importation**
A person **must not manufacture or import** any architectural coating listed in **Column 1 of the Schedule** if its VOC concentration exceeds the limit in **Column 2**, **unless**:
- The coating is **diluted before use** according to **written instructions** (in both official languages) from the manufacturer, importer, or seller, resulting in a VOC concentration **equal to or below** the limit in Column 2; **OR**
- The person holds a **permit under Section 10**.

> - If a coating is labeled for use in a different category, the **most restrictive VOC limit applies**.

## ğŸ“ Methodology for Determining VOC Concentration

### General Formula (for most coatings)
>  
> **VOC concentration (g/L)** =  
> \[
> \frac{(W_{\text{volatiles}} - W_{\text{excluded}})}{(V_{\text{coating}} - V_{\text{water}} - V_{\text{excluded}})}
> \]
>  
> Where:
> - \( W_{\text{volatiles}} \): weight of volatile organic compounds (g)
> - \( W_{\text{excluded}} \): weight of excluded compounds (g)
> - \( V_{\text{coating}} \): volume of coating (L)
> - \( V_{\text{water}} \): volume of water (L)
> - \( V_{\text{excluded}} \): volume of excluded compounds (L)

> âœ… **Excludes any colourant added after the base coating is manufactured**.

---

### Special Case: Low Solids Coatings
>  
> **VOC concentration (g/L)** =  
> \[
> \frac{(W_{\text{volatiles}} - W_{\text{excluded}})}{(V_{\text{coating}} + V_{\text{water}} + V_{\text{excluded}})}
> \]
>  
> Includes water and excluded compounds in the denominator.
