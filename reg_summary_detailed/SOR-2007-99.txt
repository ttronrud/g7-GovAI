## ğŸ—ï¸ **5. Certificate Issuance Criteria**

### (1) **New Vessels (Canadian)**
Issuance of an **International Load Line Certificate** requires:
- Compliance with **Chapter II of Annex I** of the 1966 Convention (conditions of assignment).
- Maintenance in accordance with **classification society standards**.
- Freeboard assignment:
  - Determined under **Chapter III of Annex I**, or
  - Adjusted (if necessary) to account for **vesselâ€™s structural strength**.
- Marking in accordance with **Chapter I of Annex I** (deck line and load lines).

> *New vessels built on or after the regulation came into force* follow the same rules as above.

---

### (2) **Existing Vessels (Canadian)**
Issuance of an **International Load Line Certificate** requires:
- Compliance with **Part I of Schedule I** of the Rules (conditions of assignment).
- Maintenance per classification society requirements.
- Freeboard assignment:
  - **Non-sailing, non-tanker vessels**: Part II of Schedule I (with **Part IV** if carrying timber deck cargo).
  - **Sailing vessels**: Part III of Schedule I.
  - **Tankers**: Part V of Schedule I.
- Marking in accordance with **Part VI of Schedule I**.

---

### (3) **Local Load Line Certificate**
Issued when:
- The **intended operation** provides **equivalent or greater safety** than the international requirements.
- All other conditions (e.g., maintenance, marking, structural integrity) are met.

> This is a **safety-based exemption** allowing vessels to operate under less stringent load line rules if justified by operational needs.

---

### (4) **International Load Line Exemption Certificate**
Issued when:
- The **Board** has granted an exemption under **section 13** of the Act.
- The vessel meets the **conditions of assignment** and **safety requirements** set by the Board.
- All **design, construction, or equipment-related safety requirements** set by the Board are satisfied.

> This is a **special case** for vessels with unique designs or operations that pose different risks.
