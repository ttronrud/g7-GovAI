### **Detailed Summary of the Carriers Information Regulations (S.C., 1996, c. 10)**

The **Carriers Information Regulations**, made under **Section 50 of the Canada Transportation Act**, are a set of administrative rules that establish the requirements for **air carriers, general aviation operators, and other transportation entities** to report detailed operational, financial, and safety-related data to **Transport Canada**. These regulations are designed to enhance transparency, support policy development, monitor market activity, and ensure compliance with national and international transportation standards.

---

## **I. Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**

- The regulations were **made by the Minister of Transport** and **approved by His Excellency the Governor in Council**.
- Authority stems from **Section 50 of the Canada Transportation Act**, which empowers the Minister to issue regulations to collect information from carriers.
- The regulations were originally enacted on **July 1, 1996**, and remain in force as amended (with some provisions repealed or updated over time).

## **III. Classification of Air Carriers**

Carriers are categorized into five levels based on volume of operations or revenue, which determines the scope and frequency of reporting obligations.

### **1. Large Air Carrier**
- Operates **charter or scheduled services** that have transported **2 million or more passengers worldwide** in each of the two prior calendar years.
- **Reporting obligations** include:
  - Detailed **operational data** on every scheduled flight (take-off/landing) in Canada or abroad.
  - **Financial information** (annual summary of assets and financial position).
  - **Monthly reporting** of flight details (including delays, cancellations, diversions, and denied boarding).

> *Note*: Includes flights operated by regional operators on behalf of the large carrier.

### **2. Level I Air Carrier**
- Canadian carrier that transported **≥2,000,000 revenue passengers or ≥400,000 tonnes of cargo** in the prior calendar year.
- Must provide:
  - Full **financial statements** (assets, depreciation, cash flows).
  - **Operational data** on all scheduled flights (origin, destination, passenger/cargo counts, aircraft details).
  - **Fuel consumption** data (domestic and international).
  - **Itinerary details** for passengers (if over 600,000 revenue passengers transported in two years).

### **3. Level II Air Carrier**
- Transported **100,000–1,999,999 revenue passengers** or **50,000–399,999 tonnes of cargo**.
- Must provide:
  - Aggregated **quarterly passenger and freight data** by city-pair.
  - **Flight destination data** (non-stop flights from each aerodrome).
  - **Operational details** for scheduled flights (if operating aircraft >25,000 kg).

### **4. Level III Air Carrier**
- Not a Level I or II carrier; but generated **≥$2 million in gross revenue** from air services.
- Reporting obligations:
  - Financial statements (annual).
  - Operational data if operating aircraft >25,000 kg or providing scheduled services.

### **5. Level IV Air Carrier**
- Not a Level I–III carrier; gross revenue < $2 million.
- Reporting obligations:
  - Financial statements (annual).
  - Operational data only if operating aircraft >25,000 kg or providing scheduled services.

> **Note**: Levels V–VI, *local*, and *regional* carriers have been **repealed** (SOR/2013-196, s.5).

### **6. Small Air Carrier**
- Any carrier (not large) operating a charter or scheduled service with **at least one aircraft carrying ≥70 passengers**.
- Excludes aircraft used solely for charter services where seats are not resold to the public.

### **7. Revenue Passenger**
- A passenger for whom the carrier receives payment.
- Includes:
  - Passengers on promotional offers.
  - Loyalty program redemptions.
  - Corporate discounts.
  - Compensation for denied boarding.

### **8. Non-Revenue Passenger**
- Passengers traveling for free (e.g., employees, agents) or without a seat (e.g., infants).

### **9. Scheduled Service**
- Service where seats or cargo space are sold to the public on a per-seat or per-unit basis.

### **10. Charter Service**
- Transportation under a contract where a third party reserves seats or cargo space for use or resale.

## **IV. Reporting Requirements by Carrier Type**

### **A. Large Air Carriers (Level I)**
Must provide:
- **Monthly flight-level data** (for all passenger flights operating in or to Canada):
  - Aircraft registration.
  - Flight number, origin, destination.
  - Scheduled and actual departure/arrival times.
  - Take-off/landing times.
  - Delay reasons (in minutes).
  - Whether passengers were given the chance to disembark during a tarmac delay (>3 hours).
  - Whether flight was cancelled or diverted.
- **Aggregated flight segment data** (by route segment):
  - Number of passengers denied boarding.
  - Number who gave up seats voluntarily or involuntarily.
  - Number who boarded.
- **Operational data** per flight:
  - Passenger and cargo capacity.
  - Number of revenue/non-revenue passengers.
  - Mass of freight and mail.
  - Checked luggage (pieces and mass).
  - Aircraft type (passenger/freighter).

### **B. Level II, III, IV Carriers & Foreign Air Carriers**
- **Scheduled Service (aircraft ≤25,000 kg)**:
  - Quarterly aggregated data:
    - Revenue passengers by city-pair (origin–destination).
    - Freight and mail by route.
    - Non-stop destinations from each aerodrome.
- **Charter Service (aircraft ≥5,670 kg)**:
  - Per-flight operational data:
    - Date/time, origin/destination, passenger/cargo capacity.
    - Number of revenue/non-revenue passengers (on-board or enplaned/deplaned).
    - Mass of freight/mail.
    - Checked luggage.
    - Whether flight terminates at origin.
    - Charter permit code (issued by Canadian Transportation Agency).

### **C. Level I, II, III, IV Carriers (Cargo)**
- If transported **>10,000 tonnes of cargo** in prior year:
  - Must report **supply chain details**:
    - If part of **Cargo 2000 program** (IATA): date/time/location of each shipment step.
    - Otherwise: delivery and pickup dates/locations.

### **D. General Aviation Operators**
- Must report on aircraft used for non-recreational general aviation:
  - **If any aircraft ≥5,670 kg**:
    - Per-flight data:
      - Departure/arrival aerodromes.
      - Aircraft model and registration.
      - Operator certificate type.
      - Number of passengers and cargo on board.
  - **If all aircraft <5,670 kg**:
    - Aggregated quarterly data per aircraft:
      - Model, registration.
      - Number of passengers.
      - Types of operator certificates.
      - Top 10 aerodromes.
      - Take-offs/landings (by route).
      - Average distance flown.
      - Expenses (maintenance, fuel, fees, etc.).
      - Number of aircraft and fuel consumption.

## **V. Data Submission Requirements**

### **1. Method of Submission**
- All required information must be submitted **electronically**.
- Use of **Transport Canada’s Electronic Collection of Air Transportation Statistics (ECATS)** program is mandatory.
- **Exception**: If information is collected directly by **Statistics Canada** under the *Statistics Act*, it may be submitted via the means used by Statistics Canada.

> This ensures data integrity, reduces administrative burden, and enables real-time monitoring.

### **2. Timeliness and Forms**
- Reporting is structured in **Schedule I**, which specifies:
  - **Column I**: Type of carrier (e.g., Level I, general aviation).
  - **Column II**: Form(s) required (e.g., Form A, Form B).
  - **Column III**: Reporting period (e.g., annual, quarterly).
  - **Column IV**: Deadline (e.g., by end of day on May 31).
  - **Column V**: Specific form and method (if required).

> **Note**: If two forms are listed, either may be submitted.
