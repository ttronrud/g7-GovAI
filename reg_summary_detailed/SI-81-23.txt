### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Order No. NP-MO-13-80 (August 29, 1980)**

---

#### **1. Regulatory Context and Legal Basis**

This regulation, formally titled **Order No. NP-MO-13-80**, was issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Minister responsible for the Northern Pipeline Agency**, under **subsection 20(4) of the Northern Pipeline Act (S.C. 1977–78, c. 20)**.

The primary purpose of this order is to **modify and restructure the socio-economic and environmental terms and conditions** governing the construction and operation of a natural gas pipeline in **Saskatchewan**, specifically for **Foothills Pipe Lines (Sask.) Ltd. (Foothills)**, which holds a **Certificate of Public Convenience and Necessity** issued on April 13, 1978.

The order **rescinds certain undertakings** previously deemed part of Schedule III to the Act and **adds new, comprehensive socio-economic and environmental terms and conditions** tailored specifically to Saskatchewan.

---

#### **2. Key Actions Taken by the Order**

##### **A. Rescission of Condition 7 of Schedule III (Saskatchewan-Specific)**
- The order **rescinds Condition 7** of Schedule III to the Northern Pipeline Act **as it applies to Foothills (Sask.)**.
- This condition previously contained commitments related to social and economic development, environmental protection, and fisheries/agricultural impacts.
- **Exception**: The **compensation undertaking** referenced in *Appendix 5-2, page 9 of Volume 3 of the National Energy Board’s Reasons for Decision (June 1977)* is **retained** and remains binding.

##### **B. Addition of New Terms and Conditions**
- A new set of **Northern Pipeline Socio-Economic and Environmental Terms and Conditions for the Province of Saskatchewan** is added to Schedule III.
- These conditions apply **only to Foothills (Sask.)** and **specifically to the construction and operation of the pipeline within Saskatchewan**.
- The new terms are designed to ensure:
  - Environmental protection
  - Social equity
  - Economic benefits to local communities
  - Indigenous inclusion
  - Public transparency and consultation

#### **4. Core Obligations and Provisions**

##### **A. Compliance with Laws**
- Foothills must comply with:
  - Canadian federal laws
  - Provincial laws of Saskatchewan

##### **B. Contractual Requirements**
- Any contractor or subcontractor engaged in pipeline work must **agree to abide by these terms and conditions** as a condition of contract.
- **Exemptions**: The designated officer may exempt Foothills from this requirement for specific provisions, upon application.

##### **C. Responsibility for Contractor Breaches**
- Foothills is **personally liable** for any breach of these conditions by its contractors or subcontractors — as if the breach had been committed directly by Foothills.

##### **D. Cooperation with Government and Public Access**
- Foothills must provide **reasonable access** to government officials (especially provincial) for monitoring and inspection purposes.
- Employees or agents of the provincial government may access the pipeline right-of-way for duty purposes.

##### **E. Indemnification and Liability**
- Foothills **must indemnify Her Majesty (the Crown)** against:
  - Claims arising from construction or operation
  - Gas leaks, explosions, or environmental damage on Crown lands
  - Acts or omissions by Foothills, contractors, or employees
  - Acts or omissions by Crown personnel (excluding gross negligence)

- Foothills must **pay the Crown** for any loss or damage resulting from these events.

##### **F. Emergency Preparedness**
- Before construction begins, Foothills must establish **emergency procedures** acceptable to the designated officer.
- Must **designate and train personnel** to manage emergencies.
- Must maintain emergency equipment in good working order.
- Must **immediately notify** the designated officer of any emergency and the actions taken.

##### **G. Commitment to Public Consultation**
- Foothills must:
  - Consult with:
    - Government of Saskatchewan
    - Local communities
    - Indigenous groups (Indian, Métis, non-status Indigenous)
    - Special interest groups (if they have a bona fide need)
  - Provide **informed access** to information about the pipeline.
  - Hold public meetings/workshops **upon request** from the designated officer.
  - Submit **reports** on significant consultation outcomes within 30 days.
  - Report significant consultations with labour organizations affecting construction progress.

##### **H. Information Disclosure and Public Access**
- Foothills must provide information in a form satisfactory to the designated officer, including:
  - Route alignment and construction schedules
  - Anticipated environmental and community impacts
  - Economic opportunities for local residents
  - Use of land/waterbodies
  - Potential environmental harm
  - Business opportunities in the province
  - Other matters specified by the designated officer
- Must **facilitate public access** to this information, especially for communities near the pipeline.
- Must identify a **designated liaison person** to assist with:
  - Disseminating information
  - Arranging community consultations
  - Liaising with communities

##### **I. Information Plan**
- Foothills must submit an **Information Plan** for approval, outlining how it will implement sections 17–19 and 22–23 (related to public access, consultation, and transparency).

##### **J. Manpower and Employment Planning**
- Must consult with the **provincial government and labour organizations** when preparing a **detailed manpower plan** (as per Schedule III).
- Must take measures to **discourage migration** of workers into nearby areas (to prevent social disruption).

##### **K. Orientation Program for Workers**
- All workers must undergo an **orientation program** that includes:
  - Environmental awareness
  - Working conditions
  - Potential environmental risks

- Must submit an **Orientation Plan** for approval.

##### **L. Opportunity Measures for Marginalized Groups**
- Must ensure **fair and competitive access** for:
  - Indigenous peoples (Indian, Métis, non-status)
  - Women
- Provide **supportive measures** (training, recruitment, employment) to help these groups participate fully.
- Must ensure Indigenous communities have access to **training and employment** in pipeline activities.

##### **M. Supply of Goods and Services**
- Must provide **fair and competitive opportunities** for businesses in Saskatchewan to supply goods and services to the pipeline.
- Must **cooperate with the provincial government** in supporting local businesses.

---

#### **5. Enforcement and Oversight**

- The **Designated Officer** (Northern Pipeline Agency) retains authority to:
  - Approve or reject plans (e.g., information plan, orientation plan, opportunity measures plan)
  - Modify or vary approved plans or schedules
  - Require reporting on consultations and compliance
  - Issue directions to Foothills regarding public engagement or environmental measures

- Foothills must submit a **Plan Schedule** within 30 days (or later date set by the officer), outlining when each required plan will be submitted.

---

#### **6. Significance and Policy Implications**

This order represents a **significant shift in regulatory oversight** of pipeline projects in Canada, particularly in relation to:

- **Environmental protection** (emphasizing monitoring, emergency preparedness, and impact assessment)
- **Social equity** (ensuring inclusion of Indigenous and women in employment and business opportunities)
- **Transparency and public participation** (mandating consultation, public access, and reporting)
- **Accountability** (placing Foothills directly liable for contractor breaches and environmental harm)
