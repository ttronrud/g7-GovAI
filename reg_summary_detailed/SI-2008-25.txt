## üîç **Comprehensive Summary of the Agreement Between Canada and Japan on Social Security**

### üìú **1. Legal Basis and Entry into Force**

- **Agreement Signed**: February 15, 2006, in Tokyo.
- **Purpose**: To establish reciprocal social security arrangements between Canada and Japan, ensuring fair treatment of individuals who live or work in both countries.
- **Entry into Force**:
  - The Agreement entered into force on **March 1, 2008**, following the completion of an exchange of diplomatic notes confirming that both countries had fulfilled their constitutional and statutory requirements.
  - This date was determined by **Article 19** of the Agreement, which stipulates that the Agreement shall take effect on the **first day of the fourth month after the month in which the Parties complete the exchange of diplomatic notes**.
  - The exchange of notes was completed in **November 2007**, thus triggering the March 1, 2008 entry into force.
- **Legal Authority**:
  - The entry into force was formalized through **Order in Council P.C. 2008-191**, issued on January 31, 2008, and proclaimed by the Governor General on **February 20, 2008**.
  - The Order was authorized under **subsection 42(2) of the Old Age Security Act**, which allows for the automatic entry into force of a regulation after 30 days of being laid before Parliament, unless revoked.

### üåç **4. Principles of Equal Treatment and Mobility**

#### ‚úÖ **Equal Treatment for Non-Nationals**
- Persons who are or have been subject to one country‚Äôs pension system and **ordinarily reside in the other country** shall receive **equal treatment** as nationals of that country in the application of the other country‚Äôs legislation.
- This applies to:
  - Workers
  - Spouses
  - Dependents
- **Exception**: Does **not** override Japan‚Äôs rules requiring a person aged 60‚Äì65 to reside in Japan for entitlement to Disability or Survivors‚Äô Basic Pensions.

#### ‚ùå **Prohibition of Residency-Based Restrictions**
- No provision in either country‚Äôs law shall restrict entitlement to benefits **solely because a person resides outside** the territory of that country.
- **Exceptions**:
  - Japan‚Äôs requirement for persons aged 60‚Äì65 to reside in Japan for disability or survivor benefits.
  - Canada‚Äôs requirement that a person must complete a **minimum period of residence in Canada** to qualify for OAS when living abroad.

> ‚úÖ **Totalization of Periods**:
> - Canada‚Äôs OAS requirement for residency can be met by **totalizing**:
>   - Periods of residence in Canada
>   - Periods of coverage under Japan‚Äôs pension systems
>   - This is governed by **Article 6, paragraphs 3 and 4(a)**.
> - This allows individuals who live abroad to build up residency and contribution history across both countries.

> ‚úÖ **Future Benefits**:
> - Any new benefits (e.g., allowances, guaranteed income supplements) introduced after March 1, 2008, and agreed upon by both parties, will be applied equitably.

### üöÄ **5. Employment and Work Status Rules**

#### ‚úÖ **General Rule**
- A person working as an employee or self-employed in one country is **only subject to the legislation of that country** (i.e., no dual coverage).

#### üîÅ **Temporary Detachments (Employment)**
- If an employee from **Canada** is sent by their employer to work in **Japan**:
  - The employee remains subject to **Canadian pension laws** as if working in Canada.
  - This exemption applies **only if the detachment does not exceed five years**.
  - If the stay exceeds five years, the **Japanese competent authority** may grant further exemption **with prior agreement** from the Canadian competent authority.
  - For **related companies** (e.g., a Canadian parent company sending staff to a Japanese subsidiary), the employer and subsidiary are deemed the **same employer**.

> üîÅ Applies similarly when a Japanese employee is sent to work in Canada.

#### üìù **Self-Employment (Temporary)**
- A self-employed person from Canada working temporarily in Japan:
  - Is subject only to **Canadian pension rules**.
  - Exemption applies **only if the stay is ‚â§5 years**.
  - If longer, the Japanese authority may grant an extension with prior agreement.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è These rules **do not apply** to government employees (see below).

### üèõÔ∏è **6. Government Employees**

- **Canadian government employee sent to Japan**:
  - Is subject only to **Canadian pension legislation**.
- **Japanese civil servant or person treated as such sent to Canada**:
  - Is subject only to **Japanese pension legislation**.

> ‚úÖ This ensures that public servants are not subject to the pension laws of the host country during temporary assignments.

### üß© **7. Exceptions and Flexibility**

- The competent authorities (Canada and Japan) may **agree to exceptions** to the general rules for **specific individuals or categories** (e.g., due to health, family, or hardship).
- Such exceptions must be **approved jointly** and do not override the core principles of the Agreement.

### üìä **9. Benefit Calculation Rules**

#### üìå **Old Age Security (OAS) ‚Äì Residence Periods**

- **If a Canadian is present in Japan**:
  - Any period of time spent in Japan during which they are covered under the **Canada Pension Plan (CPP)** or a provincial pension plan **counts as a period of residence in Canada**.

  - That time **does not count** as residence in Canada **unless** the spouse or dependents are covered under Canada‚Äôs CPP or provincial plan.

> ‚úÖ This enables Canadians working in Japan to build residency for OAS entitlements.
