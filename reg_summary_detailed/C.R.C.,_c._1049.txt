**Detailed Summary of the Military Rules of Evidence**

The *Military Rules of Evidence* are a comprehensive set of procedural rules governing the admissibility, presentation, and evaluation of evidence in **court martial proceedings** within the Canadian Armed Forces. These rules are designed to ensure fair, consistent, and just trials while aligning with both military discipline and fundamental principles of criminal justice. Below is a detailed, structured summary of the key provisions and their implications.

---

### **1. Scope and Applicability**
- **Title**: These Rules may be cited as the *Military Rules of Evidence*.
- **Applicability**: They apply to **all court martial proceedings**, regardless of geographical location.
- **Reference to Other Laws**: Words and phrases used in these Rules have the same meaning as in the *National Defence Act (NDA)* and the *Queen‚Äôs Regulations and Orders (QR&O)*, unless otherwise specified.
- **Governing Law for Unaddressed Questions**: Where a question of evidence is not covered by these Rules, it shall be determined by the **law of evidence applicable in civil courts in Ottawa**, provided it is not inconsistent with these Rules.

> ‚úÖ *Purpose*: Ensures uniformity in evidence handling across military courts and aligns with broader Canadian legal standards.

### **3. General Principles of Evidence**

#### **A. Admissibility of Evidence**
- **General Rule**: The court **shall not admit irrelevant evidence**.
- **Relevant Evidence**: All **relevant evidence must be admitted and considered**.
- **Exceptions**: Evidence may be excluded only if it is irrelevant or prejudicial.

> ‚öñÔ∏è *Principle*: Evidence must be directly connected to the facts at issue.

---

#### **B. Requirements for Consideration of a Fact**
A fact may only be considered by the court **if evidence has been adduced through one of the following methods**:
1. Oral testimony of a witness in court (Parts III‚ÄìIV).
2. Production and inspection of documents in court (Parts III‚ÄìIV).
3. Inspection or viewing of real evidence (Part IV).
4. Admission by the prosecutor of a fact to narrow the issue (for procedural convenience).
5. Judicial confession (under section 37).

> ‚ùå *Exception*: The court **shall not consider a fact unless evidence has been presented**‚Äîno presumptions or assumptions.

---

#### **C. Burden of Persuasion**
- **Default Burden**: The **prosecutor bears the burden** of persuading the court **beyond reasonable doubt** of every essential element of the charge.
- **Exceptions**:
  - If the accused seeks **acquittal on grounds of insanity**, the **accused bears the burden** of proving the required type and degree of insanity.
  - If a material fact (other than insanity) requires the accused to bear the burden under the *Criminal Code* or NDA, that burden applies in court martial proceedings.
  - Where the NDA specifically assigns a burden, it applies.

> ‚úÖ *Burden of Persuasion*:
> - The accused satisfies the burden if they establish the **probable truth or existence** of the fact.
> - The **burden of producing evidence** initially lies with the party having the burden of persuasion.
> - This burden **shifts to the opposing party** when:
>   - The first party presents evidence that reasonable men would consider sufficient to prove the fact; or
>   - The fact is established by a **rebuttable presumption of law** (under section 13).

> üîÅ *Shifting Burden*: Encourages active evidence production and promotes fairness.

---

#### **D. Rebuttable Presumptions of Law (Section 13)**
- Applies when the offence is in issue.
- A presumption arises when a certain fact is proven (e.g., a person was in a place at a time), and another fact is presumed to follow (e.g., they committed an act).
- The presumption **can be rebutted** by evidence showing the fact is unlikely.

> üìå Example: If a soldier is found at a location during a crime, a rebuttable presumption may arise that he was involved‚Äîunless proven otherwise.

### **4. Judicial Notice (Section 15‚Äì18)**

Judicial notice allows courts to accept facts as true without proof.

#### **Mandatory Judicial Notice (Section 15)**  
The court **must** take judicial notice of:
- Accession and death of the Sovereign
- Title and sign manual of the Sovereign
- Constitution of Canada
- Great Seal of Canada
- Acts and resolutions of Parliament and provincial legislatures
- Territorial limits of Canada and provinces
- Existence of a declared emergency
- Status of active service units
- Status of foreign governments

#### **Permissible Judicial Notice (Section 16)**  
The court **may** take judicial notice of:
- Contents of **proclamations, orders in council, ministerial orders, warrants, letters patent, rules, regulations, or by-laws** issued under authority of a public Act (including QR&O and orders from the Chief of the Defence Staff).
- Law reports of the **Court Martial Appeal Board** and appeal courts.
- Canada Gazette and provincial official gazettes.
- Records of findings/sentences at courts martial (but **not evidence adduced**).
- Dispositions on appeals, reviews, or petitions for new trial.
- Official departmental reports, forms, documents, and publications (printed by Queen‚Äôs Printer).
- Books and publications authorized for military use.
- Matters of **general service knowledge**.
- Facts or propositions of **general knowledge** that are:
  - Well known in the community where the offence occurred
  - Not subject to reasonable dispute
  - Capable of immediate, accurate verification

> ‚úÖ *Purpose*: Saves time and ensures accurate factual background in military trials.

#### **Process for Judicial Notice**
- Parties may **request** judicial notice.
- The **judge advocate** decides whether a fact is within section 15 or 16.
- If in doubt, the judge advocate **rules against** taking judicial notice.
- Once taken, the fact is **conclusively true**‚Äîno contradictory evidence is admissible.
- Parties have the **right to submit informal evidence or arguments** regarding competence or propriety of taking judicial notice.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Limitation*: Judicial notice **does not apply to publication or sufficiency of notification** (only content).
