**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

---

### **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**

This regulation establishes the formal transfer and assignment of specific public lands in Ontario to the **Minister of the Environment** for the purposes of **wildlife research, conservation, and interpretation**.

- The authority for this action is derived from **subsection 4(1) of the Canada Wildlife Act**.
- The Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Minister of the Environment) has determined that these lands are essential for the protection and study of wildlife.
- The decision is based on the Governor General’s satisfaction that the lands are required for **wildlife research, conservation, or interpretation**, which are core objectives of the Canada Wildlife Act.

---

### **2. Jurisdiction and Scope**

- The regulation applies **specifically in the Province of Ontario**.
- It pertains to a designated area known as the **Long Point National Wildlife Area (NPWA)**.

---

### **3. Assignment of Authority**

- The **Minister of the Environment** is formally assigned the **administration, management, and control** of the described public lands.
- This includes all powers and responsibilities related to:
  - Conservation of wildlife
  - Protection of habitats
  - Scientific research
  - Public education and interpretation
  - Enforcement of environmental regulations
  - Management of access and use (subject to restrictions)

---

### **4. Description of the Lands (Legal Boundaries)**

The lands are described in four main parcels, each referencing specific land patents and survey plans. These are detailed below:

---

#### **Firstly:**
- The **entire Block 1**
- The **eastern three-fourths (easterly 3/4)** of Block 2
- The **entire Blocks 3 through 5 inclusive**
- The **entire Lot 1** of Block 7

> **Legal Reference:**  
> Patented under **Instrument No. 380362**, recorded in the **Land Registry Office for the Land Registry Division of Norfolk (No. 37)** at Simcoe, dated **December 29, 1977**.

---

#### **Secondly:**
- The **entire Block 6**
- The **entire Lots 2 to 11 inclusive** of Block 7
- The **entire Blocks 8 through 12 inclusive**

> **Exception:**  
> From Block 12, the portion designated **"Part 1"** on **Plan 37R-1354**, deposited in the Land Registry Office, is **excluded**.

> **Legal Reference:**  
> Patented under **Instrument No. 390158**, recorded in the same Land Registry Office, dated **April 11, 1979**.

---

#### **Thirdly:**
- A specific portion of **Block 16**, designated as **"Part 1"** on **Plan 37R-1303**, deposited in the Land Registry Office on **July 18, 1978**.

> **Legal Reference:**  
> Also patented under **Instrument No. 390158**, same date and office as above.

---

#### **Fourthly:**
- The **entire Part 1, Part 2, and Part 3** on **Plan 37R-2507**, deposited in the Land Registry Office.

> **Identification:**  
> These parts are identified in the plan as **Parcels "B" and "BB"**.

> **Legal Reference:**  
> Also patented under **Instrument No. 390158**, same date and office.

---

### **5. Inclusion of Adjacent Lands**

- All lands **adjacent** to the parcels described above are **included**, provided they:
  - Were **outside the original survey traverse lines** of Long Point as defined in a 1856 survey.
  - Were shown on a **plan of survey by James Black**, the Provincial Land Surveyor, dated **April 24, 1856**.
  - Were **outside the formal boundary lines** of Long Point as surveyed on that date.

> This ensures that the area is not limited strictly to the surveyed boundaries but includes historically contiguous lands that were part of the original Crown grant.

---

### **6. Exemption: Water’s Edge Strip**

- A **strip of land** along the **water’s edge** of:
  - **Lake Erie**
  - **Long Point Bay**
  is **exempted** from the assignment.

- **Depth of exemption:** 132 feet from the water’s edge.

- **Purpose of exemption:**
  - The strip is **reserved for fishing purposes**.
  - **Free public access** is maintained across the strip, as per the **original Crown grant** from which the land was granted.

> This provision balances conservation with public use, ensuring that fishing rights are preserved while protecting the core wildlife area.

---

### **7. Key Implications and Significance**

| Aspect | Significance |
|-------|-------------|
| **Conservation Focus** | The land is designated for wildlife conservation, research, and public education, aligning with national environmental goals. |
| **Legal Clarity** | The detailed parcel descriptions using land patents and survey plans provide a clear, legally enforceable boundary for management. |
| **Historical Continuity** | The inclusion of lands outside original survey lines reflects a commitment to preserving the integrity of the original Long Point area. |
| **Public Access** | While the core area is protected, fishing access is preserved, promoting public engagement with the natural environment. |
| **Management Responsibility** | The Minister of the Environment now has full authority over land use, development, and activities to ensure ecological sustainability. |

---

### **8. Conclusion**

This regulation formally designates the **Long Point National Wildlife Area** as a protected zone under the Canada Wildlife Act. It transfers administrative and management control to the **Minister of the Environment**, ensuring that the area is preserved for the long-term benefit of wildlife and the public. The detailed legal descriptions and specific exemptions (notably for fishing access) demonstrate a balanced approach to conservation and public use.

This designation supports biodiversity, scientific study, and environmental education, while respecting historical land grants and traditional public rights.

---

**Final Note:**  
This regulation is a key example of how federal environmental law is applied at the provincial level to protect ecologically significant areas through clear legal descriptions, public access provisions, and dedicated management.
