## üìö **Comprehensive Summary of the Indian Oil and Gas Regulations (S.C. 2009, c. 7)**

These regulations govern the exploration, exploitation, and management of oil and gas resources on First Nation lands in Canada, with a focus on **indigenous rights, environmental protection, transparency, and equitable participation**. They are designed to balance the economic interests of oil and gas development with the **cultural, environmental, and legal interests of First Nations**.

---

## üîç 1. **Scope and Legal Basis**

- The regulations are made under **Section 4.1a and subsection 21(1)b of the Indian Oil and Gas Act**.
- They apply to **oil and gas operations** on **First Nation lands**, particularly those involving **subsurface rights (e.g., drilling, production)** and **surface rights (e.g., access, leases)**.
- The regulations are **binding on all contract holders**, operators, and First Nations involved in oil and gas activities.

## üìù 3. **Communication and Record Access**

- **Submission Format**: Notices, documents, or information must be in **paper or electronic form** or published on **Petrinex** (or its successor).
- **Address for Service**: Contract holders must provide a physical or electronic address for service in a **prescribed form** and notify the Minister of any change.
- **Receipt of Notices**:
  - **Paper notices**: Deemed received **4 days after mailing**.
  - **Electronic notices or publications on Petrinex**: Deemed received **on the day of transmission or publication**.
- **Record Search Requests**:
  - First Nations may apply (in prescribed form) for access to non-confidential, electronic contractual records.
  - Fee is set in **Schedule 1**.
- **No Obligation to Disclose Information**: A person is **not required** to submit information if the Minister or Petrinex already has it.
- **Prescribed Forms**: If no form is specified, any form may be used. If a format is required, an alternative may be accepted if the Minister confirms they can read and use it.

## ‚úÖ 4. **Eligibility to Hold a Contract**

A person may be granted a contract if:
1. They are:
   - A **corporation authorized** to do business in the province, or
   - An **individual of majority age** in that province.
2. They are **not in default** under subsection 111(5) of the Act.
3. (For corporations) **No conviction** within 2 years prior to:
   - **Public tender**: Date of bid
   - **Negotiated contract**: Date of application

> This ensures accountability and reduces risk of fraud or misconduct.

## ‚öñÔ∏è 5. **Liability and Environmental Protection**

- **Absolute Liability** applies to:
  - Every **contract holder** and person with a working interest
  - Every **operator**, **well licensee**, **pipeline licensee**, and **facility licensee**
  
> This means **full legal and financial responsibility** for any environmental damage caused by operations.

- **Insurance Requirements**:
  - **Comprehensive General Liability Insurance**:
    - Minimum $5,000,000 per occurrence (covers bodily injury, death, property damage, occupier‚Äôs, employer‚Äôs, contractor‚Äôs, products, completed operations, etc.)
  - **Automobile Liability Insurance**:
    - Minimum $5,000,000 per occurrence.
  - **Aircraft Liability Insurance** (if used):
    - Minimum $10,000,000 per occurrence.
  - **Deductibles** must not exceed **5% of the policy amount**.
  - **Subrogation Waiver**: The insurer must **waive subrogation rights** in favor of the Minister.
  - **Notice Requirements**: Must notify the Minister immediately of any termination or cancellation (with 30-day notice before cancellation).

> **Self-Insurance Option**: A letter of self-insurance (in prescribed form) may satisfy insurance requirements if the holder:
> - Acknowledges full liability
> - Declares financial capacity to cover damages.

- **Subcontractor Insurance**: All non-employee contractors must obtain and maintain adequate insurance for operations.

## üó∫Ô∏è 6. **Contract Area Boundaries**

- Boundaries must match:
  - **Surveyed legal land divisions** (if surveyed), or
  - **Anticipated boundaries** (if not surveyed).
- If lands are surveyed during the contract term, the **Minister must amend the contract** to reflect the actual boundaries after consultation with the holder and the First Nation council.
- **Exception**: Applies only if the configuration of First Nation lands **prevents compliance**.

> Ensures accuracy and legal clarity in land use.

---

## üìê 7. **Survey Requirements**

- All required survey plans must:
  - Be plotted under the **Canada Lands Surveys Act**
  - Be **approved by the Surveyor General of Canada**
  - Be **recorded in the Canada Lands Survey Records**

> Exceptions:
> - Exploration work surveys
> - Surveys under treaty land entitlement or claim settlement agreements

- In case of **boundary or well location disputes**, the Minister may order a survey as soon as feasible.

---

## ü§ù 8. **First Nation Council Engagement**

- A First Nation council may request **one annual meeting** with the contract holder to discuss:
  - Operations underway or planned
- The request must be submitted in **prescribed form**.
- The Minister must **notify the holder**.
- The holder must **organize and hold the meeting within 90 days**.
- Multiple holders may designate one representative.
- **All meeting-related expenses** are borne by the party that incurs them.

> Promotes transparency, accountability, and community involvement.
