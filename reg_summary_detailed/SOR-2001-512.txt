## üîç **Comprehensive Summary of the Canada Business Corporations Regulations, 2001**

**Regulatory Authority:**  
These regulations were issued by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Industry, under **subsection 261(1)a of the Canada Business Corporations Act (CBCA)**. They provide detailed definitions, exemptions, and procedural rules to implement specific provisions of the CBCA, particularly concerning corporate names, reporting issuers, going-private transactions, and disclosure requirements.

## üìå I. Key Definitions and Interpretations

### 1. **Distributing Corporation** *(Section 2(1) of CBCA)*
A "distributing corporation" is defined as:
- A **corporation that is a reporting issuer** under any legislation listed in **Schedule 1, Column 2** of the regulations (e.g., provincial securities laws or foreign jurisdiction laws), **or**
- A corporation that:
  - Has filed a **prospectus or registration statement** under provincial or foreign securities law;
  - And whose **securities are listed and traded on a stock exchange in or outside Canada**, **or**
  - Is involved in, formed for, resulting from, or continues after an **amalgamation, reorganization, arrangement, or statutory procedure**, where **one of the participating bodies corporate** qualifies under (a) above.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exemption Clause**:  
> A corporation that is subject to a **provincial securities exemption** or an **order from a provincial regulator** stating it is *not a reporting issuer* is **not considered a distributing corporation** for purposes of this definition.

> ‚úÖ **Purpose**:  
> This definition identifies entities subject to heightened disclosure obligations, especially in transactions involving shareholding changes or public market participation.

---

### 2. **Going-Private Transaction** *(Section 2(1) of CBCA)*
A **going-private transaction** is:
- An **amalgamation, arrangement, consolidation, or other transaction** involving a distributing corporation;
- That **terminates the interest** of a holder of participating securities **without consent**;
- And **without substituting** an interest of **equivalent value** in:
  - Participating securities of the same corporation, or
  - A successor body corporate that continues the business.

> ‚ùå Excluded: Acquisitions of shares under **Section 206 of the CBCA**.

> üìå **Participating Securities**:  
> These are securities that give holders:
> - A right to share in **earnings**;
> - A right to share in **assets after liquidation, dissolution, or winding up**.

> ‚úÖ **Purpose**:  
> This definition ensures that **shareholders are not unfairly deprived** of their rights in private transactions, protecting public interest and investor confidence.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Repealed: Sections related to this definition were repealed in **SOR/2010-128** and **SOR/2022-40**, suggesting that the concept may have been superseded by newer legislation or regulatory frameworks.

## üìÑ II. Disclosure and Notice Requirements (Sections 252.2 to 252.5)

### 1. **Prescribed Notices, Documents, or Information** *(Section 252.2)*
Under **section 252.2 of the CBCA**, the prescribed notices/documents are those referenced in **sections 48 to 81 of the CBCA** ‚Äî covering standard disclosures such as:
- Prospectuses
- Financial statements
- Proxy statements
- Shareholder communications

> ‚úÖ Purpose: Ensures compliance with mandatory disclosure obligations for public and reporting issuers.

---

### 2. **Consent Requirements** *(Section 252.3)*
- **Consent must be in writing** (paragraph 252.3(2)(a)).
- **Electronic delivery** is permitted if:
  - The document is posted on a **generally accessible electronic source** (e.g., website);
  - The addressee is **not required** to receive it at a specific system;
  - The addressee is **notified in writing** of the document‚Äôs availability and location.

> ‚úÖ Purpose: Allows flexibility in communication while maintaining accountability and traceability.

---

### 3. **Revocation of Consent** *(Section 252.3(3))*
- An addressee may **revoke consent in writing** at any time.

> ‚úÖ Purpose: Protects individuals‚Äô rights to withdraw from receiving disclosures.

---

### 4. **Concurrent Delivery to Multiple Addressees** *(Sections 252.4(b), 252.5(2)(b))*
- When delivering notices to multiple parties, the document must be provided **concurrently** ‚Äî regardless of the method used (email, postal, electronic).

> ‚úÖ Purpose: Prevents delays or unequal access to critical information.

---

### 5. **Electronic Document Delivery & Receipt**
- A document is **deemed provided** when it:
  - Leaves the originator‚Äôs or a representative‚Äôs **information system**.
- A document is **deemed received** when:
  - It enters the **addressee‚Äôs designated information system**, or
  - It is posted on a public website **and** the addressee receives **written notice** of its availability (or electronic notice enters their system).

> ‚úÖ Purpose: Establishes clear standards for electronic communication and ensures enforceability in digital environments.

## üè° III. Definition of "Resident Canadian" *(Section 2(1) of CBCA)*

Certain categories of individuals are **prescribed as resident Canadians** for the purposes of corporate residency and reporting obligations:

1. **Full-time government employees**:
   - Of the Government of Canada, a province, or a Crown corporation;
   - Where their **primary reason for residence abroad** is to work.

2. **Employees of a body corporate**:
   - Where **>50% of voting shares** are beneficially owned or controlled by resident Canadians;
   - Or where **a majority of directors** are resident Canadians;
   - Or where the company is a **subsidiary** of such a body.

3. **Full-time students**:
   - At a university recognized by majority of Canadian provinces;
   - Who have been **residing outside Canada for fewer than 10 consecutive years**.

4. **Employees of international organizations** where Canada is a member (e.g., UN, WHO).

5. **Individuals who were previously resident in Canada**:
   - At age 60 or older;
   - And have resided outside Canada for **fewer than 10 consecutive years**.

> ‚úÖ Purpose: Clarifies who qualifies as a "resident Canadian" for tax, reporting, and regulatory purposes ‚Äî especially in cross-border corporate structures.
