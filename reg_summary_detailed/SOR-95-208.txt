**Detailed Summary of the National Energy Board Rules of Practice and Procedure, 1995**

---

### **Overview**

On **April 25, 1995**, the **National Energy Board (NEB)**, under the authority of **Section 8 of the National Energy Board Act**, formally **revoked the previous Rules of Practice and Procedure (C.R.C., c. 1057)** and **issued new, comprehensive rules** in their place. These rules are now known as the **National Energy Board Rules of Practice and Procedure, 1995**.

The purpose of these rules is to establish a **clear, fair, transparent, and consistent procedural framework** for all proceedings before the NEB — including hearings, applications, complaints, interventions, and the conduct of public and private hearings — ensuring that all parties have equitable access to the process and that proceedings are conducted efficiently and with due regard to public interest and fairness.

---

## **Key Definitions and Terms**

The rules begin by defining key terms used throughout:

- **Act**: Refers to the *National Energy Board Act*.
- **Affidavit**: Includes a written affirmation.
- **Application**: A formal submission to the Board, which may include a complaint.
- **Complaint**: A formal allegation that an act or omission violates the Act or its regulations.
- **Hearing Officer**: A person authorized to conduct oral hearings on behalf of the Secretary.
- **Intervenor**: An interested party who:
  - Establishes interest under Section 28;
  - Files an answer to a complaint under Section 19;
  - Is an applicant in a multi-application proceeding;
  - Is a purchaser of gas who files an objection under Section 29.
- **Party**: A person who makes an application or is an intervenor.
- **Proceeding**: Any written or oral hearing initiated under the Act or its regulations — from the date of filing or when the Board issues procedural directions.

---

## **Scope of Application**

These rules apply to the following types of proceedings:

1. **Proceedings under subsection 15(3)** of the Act (Board-initiated inquiries).
2. **Proceedings under subsection 21(1)** (applications for decisions or orders).
3. **Proceedings under Part III** of the Act — **subject to Section 48**.
4. **Proceedings under Part IV** of the Act.
5. **Proceedings under Section 104** (e.g., matters involving export/import of oil/gas).
6. **Proceedings under Division II of Part VI of the Act** — **except Section 18**.
7. **Proceedings under the NEB Part VI (Oil and Gas) Regulations** — specifically those concerning:
   - Applications for export/import of oil or gas;
   - Amendments to gas export sales or import purchase contracts.

> **Exception**: The Board may, at its discretion, **extend or limit the application** of these rules to other proceedings if public interest and fairness so require.

---

## **Flexibility and Discretion of the Board**

The Board retains significant **discretion** to adapt procedures as needed:

- **May dispense with, vary, or modify** any rule or part thereof.
- **May extend or abridge time limits** (e.g., deadlines for filing, response, or hearings) — either on its own initiative or upon motion by a party.
- Such changes must be **promptly communicated** to all parties and interested persons.
- The Board shall issue **specific directions** to ensure procedural fairness and clarity.

> **Important Note**: Time limits are set with due consideration for the **fair conduct** of the proceeding. "Days" refer to **calendar days**, and if a deadline falls on a **holiday or Saturday**, the deadline is extended to the **next business day**.

---

## **Service of Documents**

Service is a critical procedural requirement. The rules define how documents must be served:

- **Methods allowed**:
  - Hand delivery
  - Mail
  - Courier
  - Telex
  - Facsimile (fax)
  - Other electronic communication (e.g., email), **if the recipient has the capability to receive it**.

- **Document Requirements**:
  - Must include:
    - Name(s) of each party to be served
    - Board file number
    - Hearing order number
    - A brief summary of the nature of the proceeding

- **Date of Service**:
  - Is the day the recipient or their representative **receives** the document.
  - If received **after 5 p.m.**, service is deemed to occur on the **next business day**.

- **Proof of Service**:
  - The person serving must file an **affidavit** with the Board stating:
    - Who was served
    - How service was effected

- **Multiple Parties**:
  - Service on all parties must occur on the **same day**.

- **During Oral Hearings**:
  - Documents may be made available to parties present at the hearing.
  - Copies may be provided to other parties upon request.

> **Special Rules for Personal Service**:
> - Personal service on **Her Majesty in Right of Canada** must follow the **Crown Liability and Proceedings (Provincial Court) Regulations**.
> - Personal service on **a province** requires leaving a copy with a lawyer at the **provincial Department of Justice or Attorney General**.

> **Electronic Service**:
> - If a document is served electronically, the sender must provide an **original hard copy** within a **reasonable period**.

---

## **Filing of Documents**

- **Filing Methods**:
  - Hand delivery, mail, courier, telex, fax, or electronic means are acceptable **if the Board has the capacity to receive** them.

- **Date of Filing**:
  - Is the day the Secretary or hearing officer receives the document.
  - If received **after regular business hours (8 a.m. to 5 p.m.)**, it is considered filed on the **next business day**.

- **Document Requirements**:
  - Must include:
    - Board file number
    - Hearing order number
    - A short phrase summarizing the nature of the proceeding

- **Filing Before a Hearing**:
  - If a document is filed **less than five days before a hearing**, the filer must:
    - Bring a copy to the hearing for the Board
    - Provide a copy to any party present if service is required

- **Filing and Service Together**:
  - When a document must be filed and served, both actions must occur on the **same day**.

- **Electronic Filing**:
  - Must include an **original hard copy** sent to the Board within a reasonable time.

---

## **Amendments to Documents**

- **Minor Amendments**:
  - Parties may amend any document **without leave** of the Board.

- **Substantive Amendments**:
  - Require **leave of the Board**.

> **Amendment Requirements**:
> - Each amended page must:
>   - Indicate the **date of amendment**
>   - Show a **vertical line, asterisk, or similar mark** in the margin indicating the changed portion
>   - Be accompanied by a **statement explaining the nature of the amendment**

> **Verification by Affidavit**:
> - The Board may require verification of any document or part thereof by affidavit.
> - In oral hearings: The Board will **orally inform** the person of the required document and deadline.
> - In other cases: A **written notice** is issued with the required document and deadline.
> - Failure to verify may result in the **document being struck out**.

> **Every affidavit must be filed with the Board**. If based on belief, the grounds for that belief must be stated.

---

## **Board’s Discretion on Legal Questions**

- If the Board determines that a **question of law, jurisdiction, or procedure** arises during a proceeding, it may:
  - Decide it **itself**; or
  - Refer it to the **Federal Court of Appeal** for hearing and determination.

> **Consequence of Reference**:
> - The Board may **stay (suspend)** the proceeding **pending** the decision on the issue.

---

## **Application Requirements**

Every application must:

1. Be **in writing**, signed by the applicant or their authorized representative.
2. Contain:
   - A **concise statement of relevant facts**
   - The **specific provisions of the Act or regulations** under which the application is made
   - A clear **nature and justification** for the decision or order sought
3. Include **any additional information** that supports the application (e.g., reference to Board policies or guidelines)
4. Provide **contact details** (name, address, phone numbers) of the applicant and their representative
5. Be **divided into consecutively numbered paragraphs**, each focusing on a distinct subject matter

> **Section 15 and 16 Information**:
> - If any information required by Sections 15 or 16 is not filed, the applicant must:
>   - If already in the Board’s possession: Identify it and explain how it came into the Board’s possession
>   - If unavailable: State the date when it will be filed
>   - If objected to: State the objection and grounds
> - If the Board finds the objection insufficient, it will **require the applicant to file the information**
