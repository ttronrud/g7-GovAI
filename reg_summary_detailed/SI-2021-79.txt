The regulation you have provided is a **provincial government transfer of ministerial powers and responsibilities** under the *Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act*, specifically concerning the **Federal Economic Development Agency for Northern Ontario (FEDANO)**.

---

### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

**Regulatory Text:**
> *Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, pursuant to paragraph 2(a)a of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act, transfers from the Minister of Economic Development and Official Languages to the Minister of Indigenous Services the powers, duties and functions of the Minister of Economic Development and Official Languages with respect to the Federal Economic Development Agency for Northern Ontario.*

**Statutory Citation:**  
S.C. 2003, c. 22, s. 207  
R.S., c. P-34 (Revised Statutes of Canada, Chapter P-34 – Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act)

---

### **Key Elements of the Regulation**

1. **Governing Authority**  
   - The transfer is made by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Prime Minister**.  
   - This reflects the constitutional and administrative framework under which the federal government can reorganize ministerial responsibilities through formal legislative or statutory authority.

2. **Legal Basis**  
   - The transfer is authorized under **paragraph 2(a)a of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act (PSTDA)**.  
   - This paragraph empowers the Governor in Council to transfer powers, duties, and functions between federal ministers, provided such transfers are in the public interest and consistent with the structure and purpose of the federal government.

3. **Subject of Transfer**  
   - The **powers, duties, and functions** of the **Minister of Economic Development and Official Languages** are being transferred **specifically with respect to the Federal Economic Development Agency for Northern Ontario (FEDANO)**.  
   - This means that while the Minister of Economic Development and Official Languages retains other responsibilities, their oversight and authority over FEDANO are now transferred.

4. **Receiving Minister**  
   - The **Minister of Indigenous Services** now assumes responsibility for FEDANO.  
   - This reallocation reflects a strategic shift in federal policy priorities, particularly in recognizing the importance of **Indigenous economic development** and **northern regional development** in the context of Indigenous self-determination and partnership.

5. **Nature of the Transfer**  
   - It is a **functional transfer**, not a reorganization of the agency itself.  
   - FEDANO continues to operate as a federal agency under the **Department of Indigenous Services** (or its predecessor), but now under the **policy and administrative oversight of the Minister of Indigenous Services**.

---

### **Background and Context**

- **FEDANO** was established in 1995 to promote economic development in Northern Ontario, focusing on infrastructure, business development, and job creation in remote and northern communities.
- The transfer in 2003 (S.C. 2003, c. 22) was part of a broader federal government effort to **align economic development initiatives with Indigenous priorities**.
- By transferring FEDANO’s responsibilities to the Minister of Indigenous Services, the government signaled a commitment to:
  - Ensuring that economic development in northern Canada is **co-developed with Indigenous communities**.
  - Strengthening **Indigenous self-governance and economic sovereignty**.
  - Improving coordination between economic development and Indigenous rights and reconciliation efforts.

---

### **Implications and Significance**

- **Policy Alignment**: The transfer supports the federal government’s commitment to **Indigenous reconciliation** and **inclusive economic development**.
- **Accountability**: The Minister of Indigenous Services now bears responsibility for ensuring that FEDANO’s programs are culturally appropriate, community-driven, and aligned with Indigenous priorities.
- **Operational Impact**: FEDANO may adapt its programming to better reflect Indigenous values, language, and community needs, especially in northern regions where many Indigenous communities reside.
- **Interdepartmental Coordination**: The transfer may require enhanced coordination between Indigenous Services and other departments (e.g., Natural Resources, Infrastructure, or Northern Development) to ensure continuity and effectiveness.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation represents a significant **restructuring of federal ministerial responsibilities** to better serve Indigenous communities in northern Canada. By transferring oversight of FEDANO from the Minister of Economic Development and Official Languages to the Minister of Indigenous Services, the federal government affirms that **Indigenous economic development is a core component of national economic policy**. The move is both administrative and symbolic, reflecting a broader shift toward **Indigenous-led development and partnership-based governance**.
