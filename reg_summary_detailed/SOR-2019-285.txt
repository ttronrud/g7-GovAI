The *Physical Activities Regulations* are a set of regulatory provisions under the *Impact Assessment Act* (IAA) that **designate specific physical activities as "designated projects"** requiring environmental assessment and public review under the IAA. These regulations define which types of development, infrastructure, and industrial activities trigger mandatory environmental assessments â€” particularly those located in sensitive ecological or protected areas.

These regulations were enacted to **implement the IAA**, which replaced the previous *Canadian Environmental Assessment Act, 2012 (CEAA 2012)*, and establish a more robust, transparent, and science-based process for assessing the environmental effects of major projects.
