**Detailed Summary of the Minister Designation Order (Nuclear Liability Act):**

The *Minister Designation Order (Nuclear Liability Act)* is a regulatory instrument issued under the authority of the Canadian government to designate a specific minister as the official representative responsible for administering and enforcing provisions of the *Nuclear Liability Act* (NLA).

**Key Provisions and Content:**

1. **Title and Citation:**  
   The Order is formally titled "The Minister Designation Order (Nuclear Liability Act)" and may be cited accordingly. This designation ensures that the Order is formally recognized and referenced within legal and administrative frameworks related to nuclear liability.

2. **Designation of the Minister:**  
   The Minister of Energy, Mines and Resources is officially designated as the member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada who is authorized to act as the *Minister for the purposes of the Nuclear Liability Act*.  
   - This designation is not merely a procedural formality; it confers the minister with the legal authority to perform all ministerial functions under the NLA, including making decisions, issuing directives, and exercising powers related to nuclear liability claims, compensation, and regulatory oversight.

3. **Legal Authority and Role:**  
   - The Minister of Energy, Mines and Resources is empowered to act in a representative capacity for the federal government in matters governed by the Nuclear Liability Act.  
   - This includes responsibilities such as:
     - Receiving and managing claims arising from nuclear incidents or accidents.
     - Determining liability for nuclear events.
     - Approving or rejecting compensation claims.
     - Coordinating with other federal departments, provincial authorities, and international bodies as required.
     - Ensuring compliance with the Act’s provisions regarding insurance, liability limits, and the establishment of a compensation fund.

4. **Purpose of the Designation:**  
   The primary purpose of this Order is to provide clarity and legal certainty regarding who holds the authority to act under the Nuclear Liability Act.  
   - It ensures that there is a single, clearly identified minister responsible for nuclear liability matters, thereby streamlining decision-making and enhancing accountability.  
   - This designation helps prevent ambiguity or potential delays in responding to nuclear incidents by establishing a clear chain of command and responsibility.

5. **Legal Effect and Scope:**  
   - The Order is binding and effective under Canadian law.  
   - It operates within the framework of the *Nuclear Liability Act*, which was enacted to establish a clear, transparent, and internationally aligned regime for managing liability in the event of nuclear incidents, such as those involving nuclear power plants, research reactors, or nuclear materials.  
   - The designation does not alter the substantive provisions of the Act but clarifies the administrative and executive body responsible for its implementation.

6. **Governance and Oversight:**  
   - While the Minister is designated as the primary acting authority, the Order does not imply that the Minister acts independently of other oversight mechanisms.  
   - The Minister remains accountable to Parliament and is subject to parliamentary scrutiny, especially in matters involving public safety and financial liability.

**Significance and Implications:**

- This Order ensures that the federal government has a clear and consistent point of contact for nuclear liability matters, which is essential given the high stakes and potential public impact of nuclear incidents.  
- It reinforces Canada’s commitment to international obligations under the *Convention on Nuclear Liability* and strengthens domestic governance of nuclear activities.  
- The designation supports effective coordination between the Department of Energy, Mines and Resources and other agencies, such as the Canadian Nuclear Safety Commission (CNSC), in managing nuclear safety and liability.

**Conclusion:**  
The *Minister Designation Order (Nuclear Liability Act)* formally assigns the Minister of Energy, Mines and Resources as the designated minister to act on behalf of the Crown in all matters under the Nuclear Liability Act. This designation provides legal clarity, ensures accountability, and strengthens the federal government’s capacity to manage nuclear liability in a timely, transparent, and responsible manner. It is a critical administrative tool that supports the effective implementation of Canada’s nuclear liability framework.
