### **Detailed Summary of the First Nations Land Registry Regulations**  
*(S.C. 1999, c. 24 – Made under the First Nations Land Management Act, pursuant to subsection 25(3))*

---

## **Overview**

The **First Nations Land Registry Regulations** establish the administrative framework for how documents affecting First Nations lands are registered and recorded in Canada. These regulations implement the **First Nations Land Management Act (FNLM Act)** and govern the operations of the **First Nations Land Registry**, ensuring transparency, consistency, and legal integrity in land transactions involving First Nations territories.

The regulations define key terms, outline procedures for registration and recording, specify eligibility and requirements for documents, and establish priority rules for interests and rights in First Nations land. They also emphasize public access, procedural fairness, and the protection of First Nations land rights — particularly in relation to community purposes, member rights, and land descriptions.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *First Nations Land Management Act* (FNLM Act). |
| **Applicant** | A person who submits an application for registration or recording of a document. |
| **First Nations Land Registry** | The official office responsible for maintaining records of land interests and documents. Located in the National Capital Region. |
| **General Abstract** | An electronic summary of all registered interests or rights and recorded documents affecting the entire reserve. |
| **Parcel Abstract** | An electronic summary of registered interests or rights and recorded documents affecting a specific parcel of First Nations land. |
| **Official Plan** | A legally confirmed graphical description of land boundaries prepared from field surveys under the *Canada Lands Surveys Act*. |
| **Registration Plan** | A graphical description of land boundaries created by the Surveyor General under the *Canada Lands Surveys Act*. |
| **Textual Description** | A metes-and-bounds or other non-parcel-based description of land (not limited to a single parcel on a plan). |
| **Register** | The central database of all registered and recorded documents related to First Nations land. |
| **Registrar** | The officer in the Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (DIAND) responsible for managing the Registry. |
| **Land Description** | The legal description of land used in documents (e.g., metes and bounds, parcel numbers, plan references). |

## **Location and Accessibility**

- The **First Nations Land Registry** is located in **Gatineau, Quebec**, at:  
  *10 Wellington Street, Gatineau, Quebec K1A 0H4*  
- It is open **Monday to Friday**, from **8:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. EST or EDST**, excluding holidays.
- The office is **open to the public** for inspections and inquiries.

---

## **Public Access and Information Rights**

- Any person may inspect the **electronic image** of any registered or recorded document during office hours.
- Upon request, the Registrar must provide:
  - A **copy or certified copy** of a registered or recorded document.
  - A **certificate** listing all interests (or rights in Quebec) and other documents recorded on a specified parcel’s abstract.

---

## **Types of Abstracts Maintained**

The Register includes two types of abstracts:
1. **General Abstract** – For each First Nation reserve, summarizing all interests and documents affecting the entire reserve.
2. **Parcel Abstract** – For each individual parcel of First Nations land, summarizing all interests and documents affecting that specific parcel.

> **Important Restriction**:  
> A parcel abstract may only be created if it refers **exclusively** to a lot described on an **official plan** or **registration plan**.

---

## **How to Submit a Document for Registration or Recording**

Documents may be submitted in **three ways**:

### 1. **In Person**
- Deliver the document and a completed application to:
  > Registrar  
  > First Nations Land Registry  
  > 10 Wellington Street  
  > Gatineau, Quebec K1A 0H4

### 2. **By Mail**
- Send the document and completed application to the same address.

### 3. **Electronically**
- Complete an online application at: **www.ainc-inac.gc.ca**
- Attach the document as an electronic file in one of the following formats:
  - **Adobe Acrobat (.pdf)**
  - **Tagged Image File (.tif)**
- The file must be:
  - Scanned at **200 dpi or higher**
  - A single file containing one or more images of the original document

> ❌ **Fax is not permitted** for submission.

---

## **Requirements for Applications**

Each application must include:
- Names of all parties to the document  
- Name and address of the applicant  
- Type of document (e.g., lease, mortgage, easement)  
- Date of execution (or signing in Quebec) — use the most recent date if multiple  
- Province where the land is located  
- Name of the First Nation, reserve name, and lot number (if applicable)  
- Land description  
- List of supporting documents (if any)  
- If multiple documents are submitted together, **instructions on the order** in which they should be processed

---

## **Acceptable Substitute Documents (Copies May Be Submitted)**

The following **certified copies** may be accepted instead of original documents:
- Certified copy of a **judgment or court order**
- Certified copy of a document from a **provincial land titles system**
- Certified copy of:
  - Death certificate
  - Marriage certificate
  - Name change certificate
  - Power of attorney
  - Will or probate of will
  - Appointment of administrator
- Certified copy of a **corporate amalgamation or name change** (certified by the responsible agency)
- Certified copy of an **Order in Council** (certified by Clerk of the Privy Council)
- Certified copy of a **ministerial order**
- Certified copy of a document issued by a **First Nation** (certified by person in custody of original)

> These copies must be certified by the appropriate authority.

---

## **Documents That Cannot Be Registered or Recorded**

A document will **not be registered or recorded** if:
- It has **no date**
- It is **not legible**
- It does **not identify all parties**
- The information in the application **conflicts** with the document
- The **land description fails** to meet requirements under subsection 17(1), section 18, or 19

> These are strict procedural requirements to ensure accuracy and legal validity.

---

## **Restrictions on Types of Documents That May Be Registered**

The following types of documents **must meet strict land description requirements** to be registered or recorded:

### 1. **No Land Interests or Licences for 10+ Years (unless to a member)**
- Documents granting:
  - An interest or right in First Nations land
  - A licence for **10 or more years**
  - A strata title, condominium interest, or divided co-ownership right
  - Transfer of lawful possession to a First Nation member
  - Expropriation for community purposes under section 28 of the FNLM Act  
  → **Must refer exclusively to one or more complete parcels** on an **official plan or registration plan**

> ⚠️ This restriction applies even if the document includes renewal or extension rights.

> ✅ **Exception**: Easements and servitudes (in Quebec) are **exempt** from this rule.

---

### 2. **Exchange of Land or Boundary Definition**
- Documents that:
  - Exchange First Nations land for land that will become First Nations land (under section 27 of the Act)
  - Define the exterior boundaries of First Nations land  
  → **Must use a land description derived from an official plan or registration plan**

---

### 3. **Textual Descriptions Are Restricted**
- A document with a **textual description** (e.g., metes and bounds not tied to a parcel) may only be registered if:
  - It grants an interest or right in **unencumbered land**, or
  - It grants a **licence to a utility company** for service to reserve residents

> Otherwise, such descriptions are not permitted.

---

## **Processing Order and Timeliness**

- Subject to section 21, documents are processed **in the order received**.
- If multiple documents affect the same parcel and are submitted simultaneously, they are processed **in the order specified in the application**.

> This ensures fairness and prevents conflicts in processing.

---

## **When a Document is Considered Registered or Recorded**

- **Electronically**: Registered at the time and date indicated on the **electronic acknowledgment of receipt**.
- **By Mail**: Registered at the time and date of **receipt stamped by the Registrar**.

> The Registrar provides an **acknowledgment letter** and a **tracking number** to the applicant and the First Nation.

---

## **Information Recorded in the Abstract**

Upon registration or recording, the following details are recorded in the appropriate abstract:
- Names of all parties
- Type of document
- First Nation land affected
- Date of execution/signing (most recent date if multiple)
- Tracking number
- Date and time of registration or recording

> For a **reserve-wide document**, information is recorded in the **general abstract**.  
> For a **specific parcel**, information is recorded in the **parcel abstract**.

---

## **Notification Requirements**

- The Registrar must **notify the applicant and the First Nation** without delay upon registration or recording.
