## **Detailed Summary of the Assets (Foreign Companies) Regulations**

### **1. Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**
- **Issued by:** Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance.
- **Legal Authority:** Pursuant to **Section 610a of the Insurance Companies Act (ICA)**.
- **Regulatory Text Reference:**  
  - S.C. 2001, c. 9, s. 447  
  - S.C. 1991, c. 47  
- **Definition:** In these regulations, "Act" refers to the *Insurance Companies Act*.

---

### **2. Scope and Applicability**
- **Applies to:** Foreign life insurance companies and foreign property and casualty (P&C) or marine insurance companies operating in Canada.
- **Exclusions:**
  - These regulations **do not apply to associations** (e.g., mutual or non-profit associations).
  - Applies only to companies engaged in insurance business **in Canada**.

---

### **3. Core Requirement: Minimum Canadian Assets**
Every foreign insurance company must maintain **minimum assets in Canada** to ensure financial stability and protect policyholders. This requirement is **in addition to** the general "margin of assets over liabilities in Canada" mandated under **Section 608 of the Insurance Companies Act**.

> This ensures that foreign insurers have sufficient capital *within Canada* to meet their obligations, even if their operations are based abroad.

---

## **4. Asset Requirement by Insurance Class**

### **A. For Life Insurance, Accident and Sickness Insurance, Credit Protection Insurance, and Other Approved Products**
Foreign life companies must maintain Canadian assets equal to the **aggregate of the following**:

1. **Reserve for Actuarial and Other Policy Liabilities**  
   - Determined using the **same accounting principles** as those applied in the companyâ€™s annual return (specifically, **subsection 331(4) of the ICA**).  
   - This includes future claims and obligations arising from policies in force.

2. **Minus Advances Made Against Policy Values**  
   - Deduct the amount of **all advances** made by the company:
     - On the **security** of life policies, or  
     - Against the **cash surrender value** of life policies  
     - These are included in the annual return and are considered temporary liabilities.

3. **Provision for Unpaid Claims**  
   - Must include the **amount of provision for claims incurred but not yet paid** (i.e., outstanding claims).

4. **Other Liabilities**

## **5. Reduction of Required Assets (Allowable Exceptions)**

The total required assets may be **reduced** under certain conditions, as determined by the **Superintendent of Insurance**.

### **(a) Receivables from Agents, Brokers, and Policyholders**
- The required asset amount may be reduced by **any amounts receivable** from:
  - Insurance agents  
  - Insurance brokers  
  - Policyholders  
  - For policies currently in force  
- The Superintendent may determine which receivables qualify.

> This allows for a practical reduction if the company has outstanding receivables that are expected to be collected in Canada.
