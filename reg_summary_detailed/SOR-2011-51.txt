## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Regulations Implementing United Nations Resolution 1970 (2011) and Related Measures**

### üìå **1. Background and Legal Basis**

These regulations were enacted by the **Governor in Council** of Canada, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**, under two key pieces of Canadian legislation:

- **Section 4(1) to (3) of the Special Economic Measures Act (SEMA)**  
- **Section 2 of the United Nations Act**

They implement **United Nations Security Council Resolution 1970 (2011)**, adopted on **February 26, 2011**, which called for **economic sanctions** against Libya due to its actions threatening international peace and security.

The Governor in Council concluded that:
- The situation in Libya constituted a **grave breach of international peace and security**.
- The measures in Resolution 1970 were necessary to **prevent further destabilization** and to **address serious international risks**.

Thus, these regulations provide the **domestic legal framework** to enforce those UN sanctions in Canada.

### ‚ö†Ô∏è **3. Prohibited Activities (Main Provisions)**

These regulations prohibit a wide range of actions by **any person in Canada or a Canadian abroad**, including financial, trade, and logistical support to Libya or its designated actors.

#### ‚úÖ **Prohibition 1: Dealing with Designated Persons‚Äô Property**
> **Section 3(a)‚Äì(e)**  
It is prohibited to:
- Knowingly **deal in property** (directly or indirectly) owned, held, or controlled by a **designated person** or by someone acting on their behalf.
- Enter into or facilitate any transaction related to such dealing.
- Provide **financial or related services** for such dealings.
- Make available property or financial services to a designated person or entity controlled by them.
- Provide such services for the benefit of any person/entity listed above.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception (Humanitarian Exceptions)**  
These prohibitions **do not apply** to:
- Humanitarian assistance provided by:
  - The United Nations (including its agencies and programs)
  - International organizations
  - Humanitarian organizations with UN observer status
  - Bilateral/multilateral NGOs participating in UN humanitarian plans
  - UN-coordinated humanitarian groups (including employees, grantees, subsidiaries, and partners)
- Any other individual/entity authorized by a UN Security Council committee.

> üìå **Exception for Property Affected Before September 16, 2011**  
Sections 3(a)‚Äì(c) only apply if the property was affected **on or after September 16, 2011**.  
This applies specifically to:
- The **Libyan Investment Authority**
- The **Libyan Africa Investment Portfolio**

> ‚ùå **Exception for Designated Persons**  
Sections 3(d) and (e) **do not apply** to the above two entities.

#### ‚úÖ **Prohibition 2: Export and Import of Arms and Related Material**
> **Section 4**  
It is prohibited to:
- **Export, sell, supply, or transfer** arms and related material **destined for Libya** or any person in Libya.
- **Import or acquire** arms and related material **from Libya** or any person in Libya.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception: Non-lethal Equipment**  
Prohibitions do **not apply** to:
- Non-lethal military equipment intended solely for **humanitarian or protective use**.
- Protective clothing (e.g., flak jackets, helmets) temporarily exported **to UN personnel, media, or humanitarian workers** for personal use.
- Small arms and light weapons temporarily exported to Libya **for personal use** by UN personnel, media, or humanitarian workers ‚Äî **only if** the Committee of the Security Council has been notified and does not oppose it.
- Equipment intended solely for **security or disarmament assistance** to the Government of Libya.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception: Security and Humanitarian Needs**  
Prohibitions **do not apply** to:
- Equipment for **security or disarmament assistance** to the Government of Libya.

---

#### ‚úÖ **Prohibition 3: Technical Assistance Related to Arms or Military Activities**
> **Section 5 & 6**  
It is prohibited to:
- Provide **technical assistance** (training, advice, know-how) related to:
  - The **sale, supply, transfer, manufacture, maintenance, or use** of arms and related material.
  - **Military activities** in Libya.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception**  
These prohibitions **do not apply** to:
- Technical assistance for **non-lethal equipment** used for humanitarian or protective purposes.
- Assistance for **disarmament or security programs** approved by the Security Council.

---

#### ‚úÖ **Prohibition 4: Services for Designated Vessels**
> **Section 7**  
It is prohibited to:
- Provide **bunkering services**, stevedoring, lighterage, or other services related to the operation or maintenance of **designated vessels** (those listed under UN Resolution 2146), **unless necessary for human life safety**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception**  
- Services required to **return a designated vessel to Libya** are permitted **if** the provider notifies the Minister within **48 hours**.

---

#### ‚úÖ **Prohibition 5: Carrying Arms and Related Material on Canadian Vessels/Aircraft**
> **Section 8**  
It is prohibited for:
- Canadian vessel owners or masters
- Canadian aircraft operators
- Canadian vessel owners or masters
- Canadian aircraft operators

to **carry, cause to be carried, or permit** the carriage of arms and related material **destined for Libya** or **originating from Libya**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception**  
- Applies only to **arms and related material**, not to non-lethal equipment under humanitarian use.

### üõ°Ô∏è **4. Exemptions and Certificates**

A person wishing to engage in a prohibited activity may apply for a **certificate of exemption** to the Minister of Foreign Affairs.

#### üîπ Conditions for Exemption:
- The **Security Council did not intend** to prohibit the activity.
- The **Security Council or its Committee** has **approved** the activity in advance.

#### üîπ Exemptions for Property:
A person whose property is affected by Section 3 may apply for a certificate if:
- The property is needed for **basic or extraordinary expenses**.
- It is subject to a **lien, mortgage, security interest, hypothec, charge**, or **judicial/administrative/arbitral decision**.

> ‚è±Ô∏è **Timeframes for Certificate Issuance**:
| Property Type | Timeframe | Conditions |
|--------------|----------|-----------|
| Basic expenses | 15 working days | No Security Council opposition |
| Extraordinary expenses | 30 working days | Security Council approves |
| Lien/mortgage/charge | 90 working days | - Created or issued **before** designation<br>- Not for benefit of designated person<br>- Notified to Committee of Security Council |

#### üîπ Exemptions for Contracts/Transfers:
A party to a contract or gratuitous transfer may apply to receive payments or transfers from a designated person ‚Äî **if**:
- The contract was **entered before designation**.
- Payments are **not received by** a designated person, their agent, or entity controlled by them.
