**Detailed Summary of the *Racing Products Transfer and Loan Regulations***

---

### **Regulatory Context and Authority**
The *Racing Products Transfer and Loan Regulations* were enacted by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Agriculture** and the **Treasury Board**, under **section 52 of the Financial Administration Act**. This legislative action grants the government authority to regulate the transfer or loan of specific types of racing-related photographic and video materials.

These regulations are formally titled the **Racing Products Transfer and Loan Regulations** and may be cited as such.

---

### **Key Definitions**
The regulation establishes clear definitions of key terms used throughout:

- **Agreement**: A written agreement entered into between the Director and a product user (as defined in subsection 3(1)). This is the legal mechanism through which products are transferred or loaned.
  
- **Association**: As defined in subsection 188(9) of the *Criminal Code*, referring to a group of individuals who form a criminal association (e.g., for illegal activities). This definition is included to ensure that only legitimate, non-criminal organizations can receive products.

- **Contractor**: A person who has formally contracted with the Minister to produce photographic or video materials during horse races using photo-finish or video cameras.

- **Director**: The Director of the Race Track Division of the Department of Agriculture, or any person designated by him to act on his behalf. The Director holds primary authority over the transfer or loan of products.

- **Minister**: Refers to the current Minister of Agriculture, responsible for overseeing the policy and implementation of these regulations.

- **Officer**: A person appointed by the Minister under subsection 188(3) of the *Criminal Code*. Officers have the authority to enforce compliance with the regulations and may require the return of products.

- **Product**: Any photographic material, video material, or video signal produced by a contractor during a horse race using a photo-finish or video camera for the Minister. This includes raw or processed footage related to the race.

- **Product User**: A contractor or an association that receives a product under an agreement. These are the intended recipients of the materials.

---

### **Core Authority and Purpose**
Subject to the conditions set forth in the regulations, the **Director is authorized to transfer or loan products** to product users. This authority is exercised through a **written agreement** with each product user.

#### Key Provisions of Transfer or Loan:
- The Director may enter into a written agreement with any product user to transfer or loan products.
- The agreement may allow for **repeated transfers or loans** over time, depending on the terms agreed upon.

---

### **Conditions for Termination and Return of Products**
To ensure compliance and protect the integrity of the materials, the agreement must include the following mandatory clauses:

1. **Right to Terminate**:  
   The Director may **terminate the agreement at any time** if a product is used in violation of the conditions specified in **section 4** of the regulations.

2. **Obligation to Return Products**:  
   If a product user uses a product in breach of section 4, they must **immediately deliver the product to an officer** upon request from the Director. This ensures swift enforcement and prevents misuse.

---

### **Conditions Governing the Use of Products**
The Director is **prohibited from transferring or loaning a product** unless it is used **only for viewing or reproduction** (or both), and only for **one or more of the following approved purposes**:

### **Requirement for Agreements**
The Director **shall not loan a product** to a product user unless that user has entered into an agreement that fully complies with **subsection 3(3)** of the regulation. This means:
- The agreement must include all required conditions (especially the termination clause and return obligation).
- The agreement must be in writing.
- The agreement must ensure compliance with the approved uses listed above.

---

### **Revocation Note**
The regulation was **revoked by SOR/86-388, section 3**.  
This indicates that the current version of the *Racing Products Transfer and Loan Regulations* is **no longer in force** as of the date of revocation. Therefore, **this regulation is not currently applicable or enforceable** in Canadian law.

### **Conclusion**
The *Racing Products Transfer and Loan Regulations* were a framework designed to **protect intellectual property and ensure responsible use** of sensitive racing footage. They established clear guidelines for who could access such materials, how they could be used, and what obligations users had to comply with.

However, due to their **revocation in 1986 (SOR/86-388, section 3)**, the regulations are **no longer valid or enforceable**. As such, any current handling of racing photographic or video products would be governed by **other laws**, such as the *Criminal Code*, *Copyright Act*, or more recent policies related to sports media and data rights.
