## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Marine Machinery Regulations (C.R.C., c. 6 (3rd Supp.))**

### üìå **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**
The regulation serves to **revise and replace** four outdated and obsolete sets of rules:
- *Liquefied Petroleum Gas Regulations, C.R.C., c. 1437*
- *Steamship Machinery Construction Order, C.R.C., c. 1490*
- *Steamship Machinery Construction Regulations, C.R.C., c. 1491*
- *Steamship Machinery Inspection Regulations, C.R.C., c. 1492*

These are **revoked and replaced** by the new **Marine Machinery Regulations**, which provide modern, comprehensive, and safety-focused standards for:
- The **construction**
- **Installation**
- **Inspection**
of marine machinery on Canadian-registered ships.

This update ensures alignment with current international maritime safety standards and technological advancements in shipboard systems.

> **Legal Authority**: Section 338 of the *Canada Shipping Act*, which empowers the Governor General in Council to issue or amend regulations concerning marine machinery.

### üìå **4. Scope and Applicability**

#### ‚úÖ Applies to:
- All ships **required to be registered under the Canada Shipping Act** (Part XV of the Act)

#### ‚ùå Does NOT Apply to:
- **Pleasure yachts** (except for specific cases)

#### Exceptions:

2. **Non-passenger, non-self-propelled ships**:

### üìå **5. Construction and Installation Standards**

#### General Principle:
Any machinery listed in **Schedules I to XV** must be constructed and installed according to:
1. **Rules or codes** issued by an **approved classification society** at the time of construction.
2. **Design specifications** from:
   - Part I of the applicable schedule (I‚ÄìXV)
   - General design specifications in **Schedule XVI**

#### Priority Rule:
- If there is **inconsistency** between the rules/codes and the design specifications in the schedules, **the design specifications prevail**.

#### Flexibility for Existing Machinery:
- Machinery constructed **before or after** the regulation‚Äôs coming into force can be **repaired or reinstalled** in accordance with:
  - The standards **in force at the time of repair** (not necessarily original standards)

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception**: If major repairs are **impractical or inappropriate**, they may be carried out using standards from the **original construction era** (i.e., rules in force when machinery was first built).

### üìå **6. Material Testing Requirements**

#### Mandatory Steps:
1. **Specimen Testing**:
   - Any material used in construction or repair must have **specimen pieces tested** before installation.
   - Testing must be conducted **in the presence of an inspector**.

2. **Equipment Verification**:
   - Before testing, the inspector must be provided with **evidence** that all testing equipment is:
     - In correct operational condition
     - Properly adjusted
   - Acceptable evidence includes **current reports or certificates** from an organization accredited by the **Standards Council of Canada (SCC)**.

3. **Permanent Marking**:
   - After successful testing, the material must be **permanently marked** with:
     - Information from the **Material Test Certificate** (as per Schedule XVII under "MARKING ‚Äî MARQUES")
     - The **Federal Identity Symbol** (a government-issued stamp)

4. **Alternative Marking (if required)**:
   - If marking at standard locations is **physically impossible, unsafe, or not visible**, another suitable location may be accepted.

5. **Witnessing by Experts**:
   - An **exclusive surveyor** from an approved classification society OR a **metallurgist certified by a provincial government** may witness the test and marking.
   - If so:
     - The **material test certificate** must include:
       - All test data
       - Employer identification symbol
       - Signature of the witness
     - The **permanent marking** must also include the employer‚Äôs identification symbol.

### üìå **7. Component Inspection and Certification**

#### Requirement:
- After construction, an **inspector** must issue a **Component Inspection Certificate** (in form per Schedule XVIII) for **each component** built in accordance with the regulations.

#### Pre-issuance Checks:
- The component must be **marked** with:
  - All information from the certificate (under "MARKING ‚Äî MARQUES")
  - The **Federal Identity Symbol**

#### Marking Requirements:
- Marking must be:
  - **Conspicuous**
  - **Not easily removed or covered**
  - Located at:
    - **Hot-water or steam boilers**: adjacent to access/inspection opening  
    - **Unfired pressure vessels**: on end plate  
    - **Shafts**: on flange rim  
    - **Propellers**: on hub  
    - **Other components**: adjacent to manufacturer‚Äôs permanent marking  

> ‚ö†Ô∏è If marking at the listed location is **impossible or unsafe**, the inspector may accept a **different, suitable location**.
