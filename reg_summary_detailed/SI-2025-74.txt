**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Regulation Text:**  
*Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, under subsection 5(1)a of the Salaries Act, designates the Department of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness to provide support to the Minister of Emergency Management and Community Resilience in the carrying out of that Minister’s responsibilities.*  
— S.C. 2018, c. 18, s. 3; R.S., c. S-3

---

**1. Legal Authority and Source:**  
This regulation is issued under the authority of the *Salaries Act* (specifically subsection 5(1)a), which empowers the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister) to designate government departments or agencies to provide administrative or operational support to specific ministers. The regulation is formally published in the *Statutes Canada* as **Statutory Instrument 2018, c. 18, section 3**, and is referenced in the *Regulations of the Salaries Act* under *R.S., c. S-3*.

---

**2. Key Provisions and Meaning:**

- **Designation of Department:**  
  The **Department of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness (DPSEP)** is officially designated by the federal government to provide support to the **Minister of Emergency Management and Community Resilience (MEMCR)**.

- **Purpose of Support:**  
  The primary purpose of this designation is to enable the Minister of Emergency Management and Community Resilience to effectively carry out their statutory and administrative responsibilities. This includes, but is not limited to:
  - Coordinating emergency response efforts;
  - Developing and implementing national emergency management strategies;
  - Enhancing community resilience to natural disasters, public health emergencies, and other crises;
  - Overseeing intergovernmental coordination during emergencies.

- **Nature of Support:**  
  The support provided by DPSEP is not limited to administrative or financial assistance. It includes:
  - Access to expertise, data, and operational resources;
  - Technical and policy advice;
  - Coordination of emergency preparedness programs;
  - Facilitation of communication and collaboration between federal, provincial, and territorial agencies.

---

**3. Significance and Implications:**

- **Enhanced Interdepartmental Collaboration:**  
  This designation strengthens the operational link between the Department of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness and the Minister of Emergency Management and Community Resilience. It ensures that emergency preparedness and response efforts are well-coordinated and aligned with national priorities.

- **Improved Responsiveness:**  
  By assigning DPSEP as a key support department, the government ensures that the Minister has timely access to critical resources and institutional knowledge, particularly during large-scale emergencies such as wildfires, floods, pandemics, or infrastructure failures.

- **Alignment with National Policy Goals:**  
  The move reflects Canada’s broader commitment to building resilient communities and improving national emergency management systems. It supports the government’s vision of a more proactive, coordinated, and adaptive approach to public safety.

---

**4. Legal and Administrative Framework:**

- The designation is not a reorganization or transfer of functions, but rather a formal assignment of support responsibilities under existing statutory authority.
- It operates within the framework of the *Salaries Act*, which governs the structure and responsibilities of federal departments and ministers in relation to staffing, budgeting, and administrative support.
- The Prime Minister’s recommendation ensures political accountability and alignment with government priorities.

---

**5. Effective Date and Applicability:**  
The regulation came into force with the enactment of the *Statutory Instrument 2018, c. 18* (an act that includes amendments to the Salaries Act and related regulations). It applies to all activities and responsibilities of the Minister of Emergency Management and Community Resilience that require operational or technical support from the Department of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness.

---

**Conclusion:**  
This regulation establishes a formal, legally grounded relationship between the Department of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness and the Minister of Emergency Management and Community Resilience. By designating DPSEP as a key support entity, the federal government enhances the capacity of the Minister to lead and manage emergency preparedness and response efforts across Canada, ensuring a more effective, unified, and resilient national safety framework.
