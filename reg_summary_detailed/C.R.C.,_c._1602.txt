**Detailed Summary of the Veterans Allowance Regulations**

---

### **Overview**
The *Veterans Allowance Regulations* are a set of rules established under the *War Veterans Allowance Act* (Act) to govern the application, assessment, administration, and review processes for veterans’ allowances. These regulations define eligibility criteria, procedural requirements for applications, the determination of allowance amounts, and the rights of applicants and recipients, including the right to appeal decisions.

> **Note:** Several provisions in the regulations have been **repealed** (cancelled or removed), including references to "guaranteed income supplement," "spouse’s allowance," "old age security pension," "deduction form," "application form," and specific sections related to appeals and administrative processes. These are noted throughout the summary for clarity.

---

## **I. Definitions and Key Terms**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | The *War Veterans Allowance Act*, which provides the legal basis for the allowance program. |
| **Deduction** | A portion of the allowance that may be paid to a provincial or municipal government when a recipient receives an advance, assistance, or welfare payment. |
| **Old Age Security Benefit** | A benefit payable under the *Old Age Security Act*. This term is referenced in the regulations but is **repealed** as of SOR/2004-68, s.1. It is retained here for context in relation to eligibility and interaction with the allowance. |
| **Spouse’s Allowance** | Repealed (SOR/2004-68, s.1). |
| **Military Service Details** | Required information to establish eligibility based on service history. |
| **Blindness** | Defined as visual acuity of 20/200 (6/60) or less in both eyes with proper lenses, or a visual field of less than 20 degrees. Determined via tangent screen or perimeter tests. |
| **Residence** | A person resides where they make their home and ordinarily live. Temporary absence of up to 183 consecutive days or cumulatively in a payment period does not interrupt residence in Canada. |
| **Change in Circumstances** | Any event affecting the recipient or spouse/common-law partner that may alter eligibility or allowance amount (e.g., income changes, marital status, relocation). |

## **II. Application Process**

### **1. How to Apply**
An individual may apply for an allowance in one of two ways:

- **In person**: Attend a Department office or contact a representative officer and provide:
  - Full name and address
  - Information enabling the Department to locate records
  - On request, information specified in subsection (4)

- **By filing a notice**: Submit a written notice indicating intent to apply, including:
  - Full name and address
  - Any other details needed to locate records

> *Note:* The original "application form" and "deduction form" have been **repealed** (SOR/84-784 and SOR/84-510).

### **2. Representation**
If the applicant is unable to apply due to physical or mental disability, a **responsible person** (e.g., family member, caregiver) may submit the application on their behalf.

### **3. Required Information**
All applicants must provide the following information:

- Details of military service
- Names of spouse or common-law partner and dependent children
- Income of the applicant and spouse/common-law partner
- Whether the applicant is receiving a disability pension related to military service or has accepted a commuted pension
- Any other information required by the Minister to assess eligibility

### **4. Declaration of Truth**
Every application must include a **signed declaration** stating that all information provided is **true and complete**.

### **5. Completion of Application**
An application is considered **completed** when:
- All required information is submitted
- The declaration is signed

---

## **III. Minister’s Investigation and Eligibility Determination**

- The Minister may conduct investigations into the facts stated in the application or any related matters to determine eligibility.
- If necessary, the Minister may require:
  - A medical examination
  - Medical evidence or reports
  - To verify whether the applicant qualifies as a person described in paragraph 4(1)(c) of the Act (e.g., disabled veteran)

> *Note:* Section 3 of the Act does **not apply** to this process (i.e., no income or residency rules are used in determining eligibility for the allowance itself).

### **Age Determination**
- The applicant must provide a **certificate of birth or baptism**, or alternative documentary evidence (e.g., school records, passport), when requested.
- If the applicant cannot provide such evidence, the Minister will **endeavour to obtain it**.

### **Spousal/Partnership Status**
- Valid proof includes:
  - Marriage certificate
  - Any document establishing spousal status
  - Evidence of common-law partnership (e.g., cohabitation, shared finances, joint accounts)

> *Note:* The provision allowing reliance on a statutory declaration by a reliable witness has been **repealed** (SOR/2004-68, s.8).

---

## **IV. Payment of Allowance**

### **1. Start Date of Payment**
- If the application is made **in person**: Allowance begins on the **first day of the month** in which the application is made.
- If the application is made by **filing a notice**: Begins on the **first day of the month** in which the notice is filed.

### **2. Deferred Payment Due to Temporary Non-Compliance**
If, at the time of adjudication, a requirement under the Act or these regulations is **not yet satisfied**, but the Minister believes it will be satisfied within a **reasonable time**, the allowance may be awarded **from the first day of the month when the requirement is satisfied**.

### **3. Changes in Circumstances**
- If the Minister receives information about a change in the recipient’s or spouse’s circumstances and completes all necessary investigations:
  - The Minister will **re-adjudicate** the right to the allowance
  - Determine the **amount of allowance**, if any

### **4. Effect of Changes in Class or Income Factor**
When a change in circumstances alters the recipient’s class (column I of the schedule) or income factor (column II of the schedule):

| Scenario | Start Date |
|--------|------------|
| Change results in **equal or higher** income factor | First day of the month when change occurs |
| Change results in **lower** income factor | First day of the **following month** |

> *Purpose:* To prevent sudden reductions in allowance due to minor income fluctuations.

### **5. Payment During Ineligibility**
If a person ceases to be eligible during a month, the allowance is **paid as if they were eligible for the entire month**.

---

## **V. Deductions (from Allowance)**

Deductions are allowed only under specific conditions and are limited to **not exceeding the amount by which**:

> **Advance/Assistance/Welfare Payment**  
> **exceeds**  
> **One month’s allowance**

### **Conditions for a Deduction to Be Allowed**

1. **Amount to be deducted must exceed $100**
2. **Minister must determine that the deduction will not cause undue hardship**
3. **Minister must notify the provincial or municipal authority** that made the advance/welfare payment
4. **Within 30 days**, the authority must provide a **written declaration** stating:
   - The recipient **consented** in writing to the deduction
   - The deduction does not exceed the amount of the advance
5. **The recipient must forward a statement** to the Minister confirming:
   - Receipt of the advance/assistance/welfare payment
   - The amount of the payment
6. **If the recipient fails to provide the statement**, the Minister must **verify**:
   - That the payment was received
   - That the recipient was **informed by mail** of the deduction

### **Timing of Payment to Government**
- No payment to a provincial or municipal authority can be made until **60 days after** the recipient is **informed in writing** of the deduction and its amount.

### **Requirements for the Declaration**
- Must clearly identify:
  - The recipient
  - The authority making the payment
- Must state that the recipient **consented** to a deduction not exceeding the amount of the payment

> *Note:* Several sections related to deductions and appeals have been **repealed** (e.g., SOR/2004-68, s.11).

---

## **VI. Notification of Changes in Circumstances**

Every recipient must **immediately notify** the Minister of any change that affects:
- The recipient’s class (column I of the schedule)
- The recipient’s income factor (column II of the schedule)

> *Note:* This provision has been **repealed** (SOR/2009-225, s.22).

---
