### ðŸ”¹ **Regulatory Prohibitions**

#### 1. **Building and Structure Restrictions**
> No person may:
- Erect or construct a building, structure, or object (or an addition to an existing one) on land subject to these regulations **if any part of it exceeds**:
  - The **take-off/approach surface**,
  - The **outer surface**,
  - The **transitional surface**, or
  - The **strip surface**.

> â— *This applies to all structures, including towers, antennas, trees, or even small sheds.*

#### 2. **Interference with Aeronautical Signals**
> No owner or lessee of land **within the outer surface** may:
- Use or develop land in a way that causes **interference with signals or communications** to or from:
  - Aircraft,
  - Airport facilities (e.g., radar, radio navigation systems).

> This ensures reliable communication and navigation safety.

#### 3. **Natural Growth Restrictions**
> No owner or lessee may allow any **natural growth** (e.g., trees, shrubs, vines) on land to grow **above the elevation** of any of the following surfaces:
- Take-off/approach surface,
- Outer surface,
- Transitional surface,
- Strip surface.

> âš ï¸ Even tall trees or vegetation must not exceed these elevation limits.

#### 4. **Bird Hazard Zone Prohibitions**
> No owner or lessee within the **bird hazard zone** may:
- Conduct or permit **activities that attract birds** (e.g., open fields, water features, feeding areas, waste dumping), which could pose a **risk to aviation safety**.

> This is critical for preventing bird strikes â€” a major cause of aviation accidents.

### ðŸ”¹ **Outer Surface**
- A broad, imaginary surface that defines the **maximum allowable elevation** for all development.
- Extends beyond the runways and take-off surfaces.
- Used to set **overall height limits** for all structures and vegetation.

> This surface is critical for **preventing visual obstructions** that could interfere with flight operations.

- An inclined surface that extends **from the lateral edges** of the strip and take-off/approach surfaces.
- Ensures a **smooth transition** between the airport and surrounding areas.
- Prevents sudden changes in terrain or height that could affect aircraft approach or departure
