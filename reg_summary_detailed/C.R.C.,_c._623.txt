**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: "Import of Dairy Products for Personal Use Permit"**

This regulation establishes a general import permit specifically designed to facilitate the personal importation of dairy products into Canada. Below is a comprehensive and detailed summary of its key provisions:

---

### **1. Title and Citation**
- The permit is officially titled: **"Import of Dairy Products for Personal Use Permit"**.
- It is formally cited as **General Import Permit No. 1**.
- This designation allows for consistent reference and enforcement by Canadian customs authorities.

---

### **2. Scope of Authorization**
- **Who may import**: Any individual (natural person) is authorized to import dairy products into Canada.
- **Source of importation**: Dairy products may be imported from **any country** worldwide.
- **Purpose of importation**: The products must be intended for the **personal use** of the importer and their **household members**.
- **Value limit**: The total value of dairy products imported in a single transaction (importation) must not exceed **CAD $20**.

### **3. Eligibility and Usage Conditions**
- The permit is not intended for commercial purposes or resale.
- The importation must be strictly for **personal consumption** and not for distribution, sale, or use in business operations.
- The importer and household members must be the intended recipients of the dairy products.

### **5. Enforcement and Compliance**
- The permit is a **general authorization**, meaning it does not require pre-approval or detailed product specifications.
- However, compliance with the **value limit ($20 per importation)** and **personal-use requirement** is mandatory.
- Failure to meet these conditions may result in:
  - Refusal of entry,
  - Imposition of duties or taxes,
  - Penalties or enforcement actions by Canadian Customs and Revenue Agency (CRA).

---

### **6. Key Limitations and Exclusions**
- The permit **does not cover**:
  - Dairy products imported for commercial or business use.
  - Bulk or large-scale imports exceeding $20 in value.
  - Products that are not classified as dairy (e.g., non-dairy alternatives).
- The $20 cap is **per importation**, meaning multiple shipments of dairy products (e.g., one shipment of $10 and another of $15) would each count as separate importations and must individually comply with the $20 limit.

### **7. Practical Implications**
- This permit is intended to support travelers, residents, or individuals who wish to bring small quantities of dairy products (e.g., cheese, milk, yogurt) from abroad for personal consumption.
- It simplifies the customs process for individuals by eliminating the need for detailed permits or declarations for low-value, personal-use goods.
