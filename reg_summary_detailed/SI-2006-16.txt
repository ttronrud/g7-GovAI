**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

**Regulatory Text:**
> "Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, hereby, pursuant to paragraph 2(a)a of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act, transfers the control and supervision of the Office of the Registrar of Lobbyists from the Minister of Industry to the President of the Treasury Board, and orders that the President of the Treasury Board shall preside over the Office of the Registrar of Lobbyists, effective February 6, 2006. S.C. 2003, c. 22, s. 207"

---

**1. Legal Authority and Basis:**
The regulation is issued under the authority of the **Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act**, specifically **paragraph 2(a)a**. This provision grants the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister) the power to transfer administrative control and supervision of certain public service offices or functions between different federal ministers or departments.

This legislative framework enables structural reorganization of government departments and functions to improve efficiency, accountability, and alignment with broader policy goals.

---

**2. Subject of the Transfer:**
The **Office of the Registrar of Lobbyists** — a key regulatory body responsible for overseeing and monitoring lobbying activities in Canada — is the subject of this transfer.

The Office is tasked with:
- Registering individuals and organizations that engage in lobbying.
- Ensuring compliance with the Lobbying Act and related regulations.
- Maintaining transparency in the influence of interest groups on government decision-making.
- Promoting public confidence in the integrity of the policy process.

---

**3. Change in Oversight and Control:**
- **Prior Oversight:** The Office of the Registrar of Lobbyists was previously under the administrative control and supervision of the **Minister of Industry**.
- **New Oversight:** As of **February 6, 2006**, the control and supervision of the Office are transferred to the **President of the Treasury Board**.

This shift reflects a broader government strategy to centralize and strengthen oversight of public service institutions, particularly those related to transparency, accountability, and ethical standards.

---

**4. Role of the President of the Treasury Board:**
- The President of the Treasury Board is now designated as the **presiding officer** of the Office of the Registrar of Lobbyists.
- This role includes:
  - Providing strategic direction and policy guidance.
  - Ensuring the Office operates in accordance with federal accountability and transparency standards.
  - Overseeing the administrative and operational functions of the Office.
  - Serving as the primary point of contact for interdepartmental coordination and reporting.

This appointment elevates the Office’s status within the federal public service hierarchy and aligns it more closely with core government accountability mechanisms.

---

**5. Effective Date:**
The transfer of control and the assignment of presiding authority become effective on **February 6, 2006**. All administrative, operational, and reporting responsibilities of the Office are assumed by the President of the Treasury Board from that date.

---

**6. Significance and Implications:**
- **Enhanced Accountability:** Placing the Office under the Treasury Board — a central body responsible for public service governance — strengthens oversight and ensures that lobbying activities are managed with greater transparency and consistency.
- **Policy Alignment:** The move aligns with broader government efforts to improve integrity in public decision-making and to reduce conflicts of interest.
- **Institutional Stability:** It provides a more stable and consistent administrative structure for the Office, reducing potential policy drift or inconsistent enforcement due to ministerial changes.
- **Public Confidence:** By centralizing oversight, the government aims to build public trust in the fairness and openness of the policy process.

---

**7. Legislative Citation:**
The regulation is formally cited as **S.C. 2003, c. 22, s. 207**, indicating it is a statutory amendment to the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act, enacted in 2003 and coming into force in 2006.

---

**Conclusion:**
This regulation represents a significant administrative reorganization within the Canadian federal government. By transferring the control of the Office of the Registrar of Lobbyists from the Minister of Industry to the President of the Treasury Board, the government enhances the oversight, transparency, and accountability of lobbying activities. The effective date of February 6, 2006, marks a pivotal moment in the institutionalization of lobbying regulation as a key component of public service integrity.
