**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

---

**Regulation Title:**  
Transfer of Powers, Duties, and Functions from the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development to the Minister of State to Assist the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development

**Issuing Authority:**  
Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, pursuant to **paragraph 2(a)(a)** of the *Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act*.

---

### **Overview of the Regulation**

This regulation formally transfers specific **powers, duties, and functions** of the **Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (MIAND)** to the **Minister of State to assist the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development**. This reorganization is part of a broader public service restructuring aimed at improving efficiency, accountability, and administrative capacity in managing Indigenous affairs and northern development.

The transfer does **not** diminish the overall responsibilities of the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development. Instead, it delegates certain operational and legislative responsibilities to a junior minister (the Minister of State), enabling more focused oversight and specialized management of complex and often geographically dispersed programs and legislation.

---

### **Key Legislative Acts Transferred**

The following Acts are explicitly cited in the regulation as being subject to the transfer of powers, duties, and functions:

#### 1. **S.C. 2003, c. 22, s. 207**  
- This section of the *Indian Act* (amended in 2003) governs the administration of Indigenous rights, land claims, and certain governance structures.  
- The transfer ensures that provisions related to Indigenous governance and land rights are now managed under the oversight of the Minister of State, with support from the senior minister.

#### 2. **R.S., c. P-34 – *Indian Affairs and Northern Development Act***  
- The foundational statute for the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development.  
- The transfer includes all powers and responsibilities under this Act, including the administration of Indigenous services, land claims, and northern development programs.

#### 3. **Arctic Waters Pollution Prevention Act (R.S., c. A-12)**  
- Regulates pollution in Arctic waters, including prevention, monitoring, and enforcement of environmental standards.  
- The Minister of State now oversees environmental protection in the Arctic region, particularly in relation to Indigenous communities and shared resource management.

#### 4. **Canada Lands Surveys Act (R.S., c. L-6)**  
- Governs land surveying and mapping activities across Canada, including in northern and Indigenous territories.  
- The transfer ensures that land surveying operations—critical for land claims, land use planning, and infrastructure development—are now under the jurisdiction of the Minister of State.

#### 5. **Canada Oil and Gas Operations Act (R.S., c. O-7)**  
- Regulates oil and gas operations, including environmental safeguards and Indigenous consultation requirements.  
- The Minister of State now has responsibility for overseeing operations that may affect Indigenous lands and communities in the North.

#### 6. **Territorial Lands Act (R.S., c. T-7)**  
- Governs land tenure, land use, and land administration in the territories (Nunavut, Northwest Territories, Yukon).  
- This transfer strengthens the Minister of State’s role in territorial land governance, including land claims, land titles, and surface rights.

#### 7. **Dominion Water Power Act (R.S., c. W-4)**  
- Deals with the development and regulation of water power projects, including hydroelectric projects in northern regions.  
- The Minister of State now manages hydroelectric development and water resource use in Indigenous and northern communities.

#### 8. **Canada Petroleum Resources Act (R.S., c. 36 (2nd Supp.))**  
- Provides a framework for the exploration, development, and regulation of petroleum resources in Canada.  
- The transfer ensures that petroleum-related activities in northern and Indigenous regions are coordinated under the Minister of State.

#### 9. **Gwich’in Land Claim Settlement Act (S.C. 1992, c. 53)**  
- A key land claim settlement agreement between the Gwich’in people and the federal government.  
- The Minister of State now manages implementation, monitoring, and compliance with the Gwich’in settlement, including environmental and cultural protections.

#### 10. **Nunavut Act (S.C. 1993, c. 28)**  
- Establishes the legal and constitutional framework for the territory of Nunavut.  
- The Minister of State now has oversight of Nunavut’s administrative and governance structures, including its relationship with the federal government.

#### 11. **Nunavut Land Claims Agreement Act (S.C. 1993, c. 29)**  
- Implements the land claims agreement between the Inuit of Nunavut and the federal government.  
- The Minister of State is responsible for managing land claim implementation, land title, and self-government provisions.

#### 12. **Sahtu Dene and Metis Land Claim Settlement Act (S.C. 1994, c. 27)**  
- Addresses land claims and self-government for the Sahtu Dene and Métis communities.  
- The Minister of State now oversees the implementation and monitoring of this settlement.

#### 13. **Yukon First Nations Land Claims Settlement Act (S.C. 1994, c. 34)**  
- Resolves land claims of Yukon First Nations.  
- The Minister of State now manages the administration and enforcement of land rights and title for Yukon First Nations.

#### 14. **Yukon Surface Rights Board Act (S.C. 1994, c. 43)**  
- Establishes the Yukon Surface Rights Board to manage surface rights and land use in Yukon.  
- The Minister of State now oversees the Board’s operations and decision-making.

#### 15. **Mackenzie Valley Resource Management Act (S.C. 1998, c. 25)**  
- Regulates resource development in the Mackenzie Valley, including oil, gas, and mining.  
- The Minister of State is responsible for managing resource development in a manner that respects Indigenous rights and environmental protection.

#### 16. **Yukon Act (S.C. 2002, c. 7)**  
- The foundational law for the Yukon Territory.  
- The transfer includes responsibilities related to territorial governance, land administration, and Indigenous self-government.

#### 17. **Nunavut Waters and Nunavut Surface Rights Tribunal Act (S.C. 2002, c. 10)**  
- Establishes a tribunal to resolve disputes over water and surface rights in Nunavut.  
- The Minister of State now oversees the tribunal’s operations and adjudicative functions.

#### 18. **Tlicho Land Claims and Self-Government Act (S.C. 2005, c. 1)**  
- Addresses land claims and self-government for the Tlicho people in the Northwest Territories.  
- The Minister of State manages the implementation of land rights and self-governance arrangements.

#### 19. **Nunavut Planning and Project Assessment Act (S.C. 2013, c. 14, s. 2)**  
- Provides a framework for environmental and social assessment of projects in Nunavut.  
- The Minister of State now leads project assessments that affect Indigenous communities and the environment.

#### 20. **Northwest Territories Act (S.C. 2014, c. 2, s. 2)**  
- Governs the administration and governance of the Northwest Territories.  
- The Minister of State now has responsibility for key administrative functions under this Act, including land claims and Indigenous self-government.

---

### **Purpose and Rationale of the Transfer**

1. **Improved Governance and Efficiency**  
   - By transferring these powers to a Minister of State, the federal government enhances administrative efficiency. The Minister of State can focus on specialized, technical, and operational aspects of land claims, environmental management, and northern development.

2. **Enhanced Indigenous Self-Government Support**  
   - The transfer strengthens federal support for Indigenous self-governance, land claims, and territorial autonomy by ensuring dedicated oversight and implementation of land claim settlements.

3. **Geographic and Jurisdictional Clarity**  
   - The reorganization clarifies responsibilities in the Arctic and northern territories, where jurisdiction overlaps between Indigenous communities, territorial governments, and federal agencies.

4. **Compliance with Legal and Treaty Obligations**  
   - The transfer ensures that land claims, environmental protection, and resource management are implemented in accordance with legal frameworks and treaty obligations.

5. **Accountability and Oversight**  
   - The Minister of State serves as a direct point of accountability for implementation, allowing for more transparent reporting and monitoring of Indigenous rights and environmental outcomes.

---

### **Implications**

- **For Indigenous Communities:**  
  The transfer strengthens federal commitment to land claims and self-determination, with more focused and responsive governance.

- **For Northern Territories:**  
  The Minister of State will play a key role in managing land, resource development, and environmental protection in Nunavut, the Northwest Territories, and Yukon.

- **For Federal Administration:**  
  The reorganization streamlines decision-making and improves coordination between departments involved in Indigenous affairs and northern development.

- **For Legal and Policy Continuity:**  
  All existing rights, obligations, and legislative provisions remain intact. The transfer is administrative and does not alter the substance of the laws or rights under them.
