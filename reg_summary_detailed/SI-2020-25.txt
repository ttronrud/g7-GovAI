**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Regulation Text:**  
*Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, pursuant to section 4 of the College of Patent Agents and Trade-mark Agents Act, designates the Minister of Industry, who is a federal minister, to be the Minister for the purposes of that Act.*  
S.C. 2018, c. 27, s. 247

---

**1. Legal Authority and Source:**  
This regulation is issued under the authority of the *College of Patent Agents and Trade-mark Agents Act* (the "Act"). Specifically, it invokes **section 4** of that Act, which empowers the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister) to designate a federal minister to serve as the "Minister" for the purposes of the Act.

This provision is part of **Statutory Commission 2018, Chapter 27**, which is the enactment of the *College of Patent Agents and Trade-mark Agents Act* and its associated amendments.

---

**2. Key Provision:**  
The regulation designates the **Minister of Industry** (a federal cabinet-level minister) as the official "Minister" responsible for administering and overseeing the College of Patent Agents and Trade-mark Agents under the Act.

---

**3. Purpose and Function:**  
The designation serves several key purposes:
- It formally assigns a federal minister to act as the principal government representative in relation to the College.
- It establishes the minister's authority to:
  - Approve the College’s bylaws and rules.
  - Exercise oversight and regulatory functions related to the College’s operations.
  - Make decisions regarding membership, discipline, licensing, and professional standards of patent agents and trade-mark agents in Canada.
- It ensures that the College operates under the authority of a designated government minister, thereby maintaining accountability and alignment with federal policy and regulatory frameworks.

---

**4. Interpretation of "Minister" in the Act:**  
Under section 4 of the Act, the term "Minister" is not limited to a specific ministerial title but refers to a federal minister designated by the Governor General in Council. This regulation explicitly names the **Minister of Industry** as that designated minister. This reflects a policy decision to assign oversight of intellectual property professionals to a minister with expertise in industry, innovation, and economic development.

---

**5. Legal Effect and Scope:**  
- The designation is effective from the date of publication in the *Canada Gazette* (as is standard for such regulations).
- It applies to all matters falling under the jurisdiction of the College of Patent Agents and Trade-mark Agents Act.
- It supersedes any prior or conflicting designations or interpretations of who serves as the Minister under the Act.

---

**6. Context and Rationale:**  
The Minister of Industry is well-positioned to oversee the regulation of patent and trade-mark agents because:
- The industry is closely tied to innovation, technology, and economic growth.
- The Minister of Industry is responsible for policies related to science, technology, and innovation.
- This designation ensures that the College’s regulatory functions are aligned with national innovation and intellectual property strategy.

---

**7. Implications for the College:**  
- The College will now operate under the direct authority of the Minister of Industry.
- The minister may issue directions, request reports, or intervene in matters related to College governance or compliance.
- It strengthens the connection between the federal government and the professional body, enhancing regulatory coherence and public confidence.

---

**Conclusion:**  
This regulation formally assigns the **Minister of Industry** as the designated Minister under the *College of Patent Agents and Trade-mark Agents Act*. It ensures that the governance and regulatory functions of the College are carried out under the oversight of a federal minister with relevant expertise in industry and innovation. The provision is a key mechanism for maintaining accountability, consistency, and alignment with broader federal policy goals in intellectual property and professional regulation.

This designation is binding and effective under the authority of section 4 of the Act and is part of the broader legislative framework governing the regulation of patent and trade-mark agents in Canada.
