**Detailed Summary of the Government Property Traffic Regulations**

---

### **1. Regulation Title and Citation**
The regulations are officially titled the **Government Property Traffic Regulations** and may be cited as such. These regulations govern traffic operations on **government property** and **highways located thereon**, with specific provisions tailored to certain designated areas and jurisdictions.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

The regulations define several key terms to ensure clarity and consistency in application:

- **Commissioner**: Refers to the **Commissioner of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)**.
- **Confederation Bridge**: The bridge and associated real property located over the Northumberland Strait, specifically defined in **Schedule B**, as amended, registered on October 5, 1993, in Prince County, Prince Edward Island.
- **Constable**: Includes:
  - Members of the RCMP,
  - Members of provincial or municipal police forces,
  - Any individual authorized by a Minister, Deputy Minister, Assistant Deputy, Acting Deputy, or the Commissioner (or officers under subsection 6(1) of the RCMP Act) to enforce these regulations.
- **Department**: Any department listed in **Schedule A to the Financial Administration Act**, any **Crown corporation** (as defined in section 83 of that Act), or any other division or branch of the Canadian public service.
- **Driver**: A person who is **driving** or has **actual physical control** of a vehicle.
- **Government Property**: Property owned or occupied by **Her Majesty in right of Canada**.
- **Highway**: Includes roads, streets, bridges, parkways, driveways, squares, places, viaducts, trestles, or other surfaces **designed or used for vehicular passage**.
- **Minister**: 
  - For departments: the Minister presiding over that department,
  - For Crown corporations: the President or Chief Executive Officer,
  - For other public service divisions: the appropriate Minister under the Financial Administration Act.
- **Park (Parking)**: The standing of a vehicle (occupied or unoccupied) **except when loading/unloading passengers or goods**.
- **South Klondike Highway**: The portion of the Klondike Highway in **British Columbia**, between 60°N 134°40’W and 59°39’N 135°9’W, bordering Yukon and Alaska.
- **Vehicle**: Any device used to transport persons or property on a highway, **excluding devices used solely on stationary rails or tracks**.

---

### **3. Scope of Application**

These regulations apply to:
- Government property and highways located thereon,
- **Except**:
  - National Parks,
  - The Jacques Cartier and Champlain bridges (including their approaches and portions of the Bonaventure Autoroute),
  - Territorial lands under the **Territorial Lands Act**,
  - Indian reserves,
  - Property under the control of the **National Capital Commission (NCC)**,
  - Property subject to the **Airport Traffic Regulations** or **Traffic on the Land Side of Airports Regulations**.

> **Exception for Canadian Forces**: Members of the Canadian Forces are **exempt** from these regulations when operating a government-owned vehicle with due authority.

---

### **4. Licensing and Registration Requirements**

#### **General Rule (Section 5):**
Unless exempted, **no person may operate a vehicle on a highway** unless:
- They hold all **licences and permits** required by **provincial and municipal laws** in the area where the highway is located,
- The vehicle is **registered and equipped** as required by those provincial and municipal laws.

#### **Special Provisions for Specific Highways:**
- **South Klondike Highway**: Operation requires compliance with **Yukon Territory laws** (licences, registration, equipment).
- **Confederation Bridge**: Operation must comply with **Prince Edward Island laws**.

#### **General Compliance Rule (Section 6):**
No person may operate a vehicle on a highway **except in accordance with the laws of the province and municipality** where the highway is situated.

> **Exceptional Precedence Clause (Section 7):**  
> In case of conflict between:
> - The Government Property Traffic Act and these regulations, and
> - Provincial or municipal traffic laws,
> **These Regulations and the Government Property Traffic Act prevail** over the provincial/municipal laws.

> This ensures **uniformity and control** over traffic on government property, even if local laws differ.

---

### **5. Traffic Sign and Device Authority**

#### **Authority to Install Signs (Section 10):**
The **Minister of Public Works**, the **Minister of any department managing government property**, or the **Commissioner** may:
- Install traffic signs or devices to regulate:
  - Speed limits,
  - Parking (including prohibited areas, designated parking zones),
  - Load limits,
  - Prohibitions or restrictions on vehicle use,
  - One-way designations,
  - Stopping areas,
  - Pedestrian traffic,
  - Other traffic control measures.

#### **Restrictions on Unauthorized Sign Installation:**
- **No person** (other than authorized Ministers or the Commissioner) may:
  - Mark or erect traffic signs or devices on government property **without authorization**.

#### **Prohibition on Removal or Defacement:**
- No person may **remove or deface** any traffic sign or device on government property without the **authority of the Minister or Commissioner**.

#### **Presumption of Legitimacy:**
- Any sign or device bearing the words **“Government of Canada”** or an abbreviation thereof, or indicating it was erected by a Minister or the Commissioner, is **deemed to have been installed under these Regulations**.

> This provides legal standing to existing signs and prevents challenges based on lack of formal authority.

#### **Pre-existing Signs (Section 11):**
- Signs erected **before September 17, 1952**, under the former *Act to Provide for the Regulation of Vehicular Traffic on Dominion Property* or under lawful authority, are **deemed to be established under these Regulations**.

---

### **6. Compliance with Traffic Directions**

- **Drivers must obey**:
  - All traffic signs and devices on government property,
  - Traffic directions given by a **constable**.

---

### **7. Driver and Vehicle Documentation Requirements**

Every person on government property must produce upon demand to a constable:
- A **permit issued under these Regulations**,
- A **valid licence or permit** authorizing them to drive,
- A **certificate of registration** for the vehicle.

> This ensures accountability and verification of legal status for drivers and vehicles.

---

### **8. Accident Reporting Obligations**

#### **General Rule (Section 12):**
- Every driver involved in an accident on government property must **report it promptly** as required by the **laws of the province where the accident occurred**.

#### **Special Provisions:**
- On the **South Klondike Highway**: Report as required by **Yukon Territory laws**.
- On the **Confederation Bridge**: Report as required by **Prince Edward Island laws**.

#### **Additional Reporting Requirement:**
- If government property is damaged by the accident, the driver must **immediately report the incident to**:
  - A member of the **RCMP**, or
  - The person in **charge, control, or occupation** of the damaged property.

> This ensures prompt response and accountability in cases involving public property.

---

### **9. Parking Rules**

#### **Prohibited Parking (Section 13):**
- No person may park in an area **designated by a sign as prohibited**.

#### **Permitted Parking with Permits:**
- In areas where parking is **reserved for permit holders** or **prohibited except with a permit**:
  - A vehicle may only be parked if it displays a **label attached to the vehicle** and **exposed** as required by the permit.

#### **Time-Limited Parking:**
- In areas where parking is allowed for a **specific time period**, no person may park longer than the time **indicated on the sign**.

#### **Class-Based Parking:**
- Parking reserved for a specific class (e.g., business, school, service) is **only available to members of that class**.

#### **Disabled Persons Parking:**
- Parking reserved for disabled persons is only allowed if a **valid disabled parking permit** issued by a provincial or municipal authority is **displayed on the vehicle**.

> These rules promote fairness, accessibility, and efficient use of parking space.

---

### **10. Permit and Label Issuance**

- **Ministers or authorized persons** may issue:
  - Permits and labels for parking or access on government property.
- A permit may be **revoked** at the discretion of the issuing authority.
- A permit is valid for the **period stated**, and the attached label is valid **only during that period**.

> This ensures that parking rights are temporary, verifiable, and subject to administrative oversight.

---

### **11. Enforcement and Removal of Vehicles**

- A constable may:
  - Remove a vehicle parked in violation of these regulations,
  - Store the vehicle in a suitable place **if necessary to protect it or the owner’s interests**.

> This provides enforcement tools for compliance with parking rules.

---

### **12. Speed Limit Compliance**

- No person may drive a vehicle on a highway at a speed **exceeding the posted speed limit** indicated by a traffic sign.

> Speed regulation is a core component of traffic safety on government property.
