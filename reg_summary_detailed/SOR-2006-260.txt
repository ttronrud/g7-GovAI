## ðŸ“š **Detailed Summary of the Pest Control Products Incident Reporting Regulations**

### **1. Legal Authority and Purpose**
- These regulations were made by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Health**, under **subsection 67(1) of the Pest Control Products Act (PCA)**.
- **Purpose**: To establish a formal, mandatory reporting system for incidents involving pest control products (PCPs) that pose potential health or environmental risks.
- The goal is to ensure **timely, accurate, and comprehensive reporting** of adverse events to the Minister, enabling risk assessment, regulatory oversight, and public protection.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Pest Control Products Act*. |
| **Animal** | Any invertebrate or vertebrate, excluding humans. |
| **Domestic animal** | An animal under human control and dependent on humans for survival (e.g., dogs, cats, livestock). |
| **Incident** | An event involving exposure to a pest control product with health or environmental effects. **Exclusions**: <br> - Acts or activities that constitute offences under the *Criminal Code*; <br> - Exposure of a targeted subject (e.g., pests being treated). |

| **Individual of a species at risk** | *Repealed* by SOR/2019-173, s. 2. |
| **Scientific study** | A study (human epidemiological or scientific investigation) sponsored by the registrant/applicant that identifies: <br> - New health/environmental hazards; <br> - Greater risks than those at registration; <br> - Previously undetected components or derivatives of the product. |

### **3. Classification of Incidents**

Incidents are categorized based on **subject** (human, animal, plant) and **effect** of exposure:

| Category | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **(a)** Human death | Death of a human due to exposure. |
| **(b)** Major human effect | Human symptoms indicating life-threatening conditions, adverse reproductive/developmental effects, or chronic disability. |
| **(c)** Other human effect | Any human health effect not falling under (a) or (b). |
| **(d)** Animal death | Death of any animal; if in the U.S., specifically a **domestic animal**. |
| **(e)** Animal effect (non-death) | Any adverse effect on animals other than death. |
| **(f)** Effect on plants | Harm to terrestrial or aquatic plants. |
| **(g)** Package failure | A failure in packaging that could lead to human exposure or injury. |
| **(h)** Scientific study findings** | Study (ongoing, discontinued, or concluded) sponsored by the registrant/applicant showing: <br> - New hazards; <br> - Greater risk than at registration; <br> - Undetected components or derivatives. |

> ðŸ” These categories determine **reporting obligations**, timeframes, and reporting thresholds.

### **4. Prescribed Information for Incident Reports**

All incident reports must include the following **core information** (as required by sections 7, 8, and 16 of the PCA):

#### **Mandatory Information (Section 3(1))**
- Contact information of the registrant or applicant  
- Date of incident occurrence  
- Date registrant/applicant received the information  
- Location: city, province/state, country  
- Identification of the pest control product  
- Application details: site, method, date of application  
- Incident category (as per section 2)  
- Exposure circumstances:  
  - Site, date, route of exposure  
  - Weather conditions  
  - Duration  
  - Subject details (for humans): age, gender, pregnancy status *(restricted)*  
- Incident and effect details: symptoms, duration, outcome  
- Scientific test information (if applicable):  
  - Sample details  
  - Method of analysis  
  - Results  

#### **Scientific Study Information (Section 3(2))**
- Title and date of the study  
- Name of author  
- Product identification  
- Incident category  
- Full scientific study and test data  
- Type of study (e.g., epidemiological, toxicological)  
- Reason for submission (from subparagraphs 2(f)(i) to (iii))  
- Findings regarding new hazards, increased risk, or undetected components

### **5. Deemed Receipt of Information**

- Information is deemed to have been **received by the registrant/applicant** on a certain date if:
  - Received by an **employee or agent** of the registrant/applicant; **or**
  - Received by an **employee or agent of a related corporation** (as defined under *Income Tax Act*, subsection 251(2)) â€” whether located in Canada or abroad.

### **6. Language Requirements**

- All incident reports must be submitted in **English or French** (official languages of Canada).
- If a scientific study is published in a non-English/French language:
  - If **translation cannot be completed within the required timeframe**, the registrant must provide a **summary in English or French** with the incident report.
  - The **full translated study must be provided as soon as feasible** after the incident report is submitted.

### **7. Reporting Obligations by Category**

Every registrant or applicant must report incidents within specified timeframes:

| Incident Category | Reporting Deadline |
|------------------|--------------------|
| **Human death** or **major human effect** | **Within 15 days** of receiving information |
| **Incident identified in a scientific study** | **Within 30 days** of receiving information |
| **Animal death (in Canada)** | **Within 90 days** of receiving information |
| **Incidents in the U.S. with domestic animal death** | **Within 90 days** (only if applicable) |
| **Other incidents** (e.g., non-death effects on humans, animals, plants, package failure) | **Accumulated and reported annually** per the reporting table below |

### **8. Special Reporting Obligations (Transition and Legacy)**

#### **For Registrants Before the Regulations Came Into Force**
- A registrant who, in the **year before the regulations came into force**, had accumulated information under the previous rules **must submit**:
  - Incident reports for the **portion of the reporting period immediately before** the new regulations took effect.
  - Must be submitted by **February 28, 2022**.

#### **Upon Cessation of Registration**
- If a registrant **ceases to be the registrant** of a product:
  - Must provide all incident reports and annual summaries **before ceasing** or **within 15 days after cessation**.
  - The **reporting period is deemed to end** on the day of cessation.

---

### **9. Annual Summary Requirement**

#### **When Required**
A registrant must provide an **annual summary** for an active ingredient if:
- The active ingredient is part of a PCP involved in an incident during the year; **and**
- It is implicated in **at least 10 incidents** of the same type of exposure (human, animal, or plant) as defined in section 2.

> ðŸ” "Same type of subject" means incidents involving the same exposure category (e.g., human, animal, plant).

#### **What the Summary Must Include**
- For each incident: the **category of incident** (as per section 2)
- A **concise critical analysis** of all data related to the active ingredient, including:
  - Comparison with past years
  - Commentary on changes in the **risk profile** of the product(s) containing that ingredient

#### **Deadline**
- Must be submitted by **March 31 of the following calendar year**.
