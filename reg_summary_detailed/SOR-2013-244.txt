## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Air Canada Pension Plan Funding Regulations, 2014**

### üîπ **Regulatory Background and Legal Authority**
These regulations were issued under the authority of the **Pension Benefits Standards Act, 1985 (PBSSA)**, specifically:
- Subsection 9(1)a
- Paragraphs 10.1(2)(c)b and (d)b
- Subparagraphs 28(1)(b)(iv) and (b.1)(ii)c
- Subsection 29(6)d
- Section 39e of the PBSSA

They were made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, and came into force on **January 1, 2014**.  
They were **repealed (ceased to be in force)** on **December 31, 2020**, except for certain transitional provisions.

## üîç Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Air Canada pension plan (or "plan")** | A defined benefit pension plan administered by Air Canada that was established *before May 2, 2013*, excluding multi-employer plans. |
| **Solvency deficiency** | The difference between a plan‚Äôs solvency liabilities (determined via a solvency valuation) and the market value of its assets related to defined benefit provisions (also determined via solvency valuation). In other words: <br> **Solvency Deficiency = Solvency Liabilities ‚Äì Market Value of Assets** |

> If the solvency deficiency is positive, the plan is underfunded.

All other terms used in these regulations have the same meaning as in the *Pension Benefits Standards Regulations, 1985*, unless otherwise defined.

## üéØ Scope and Application

- Applies to **all Air Canada pension plans** established before May 2, 2013.
- Does **not render the Pension Benefits Standards Regulations (PBSSR) inapplicable** overall.
- However, **Section 9 of the PBSSR (except subsection 9(14)) does not apply** to these plans.
- **Section 9(14)** of the PBSSR *does* apply, but with a specific interpretation:
  - A "special payment" includes the amount required under **paragraph 5(1)(b)** of the regulations.
- For clarity: The amounts required under paragraph 5(1)(b) are considered "**other payments**" under paragraph 10(2)(d) of the PBSSR.

## ‚úÖ Funding Requirements (Core Principle)

Every Air Canada pension plan must be funded in each plan year by **three components**:

1. **Normal cost** of the plan  
   ‚Üí The actuarial cost of providing pension benefits to employees (based on current service, salary, and benefit formulas).

2. **An additional amount determined under subsection (2)**  
   ‚Üí This is a calculated payment to address solvency deficiencies.

3. **Amount required to be paid under a defined contribution provision** in the plan  
   ‚Üí If the plan includes a defined contribution component (e.g., employer contributions to a separate fund), this amount must also be paid.

## üí∞ Calculation of the Additional Funding Amount (Subsection 2)

The amount required under subsection (2) depends on whether the **aggregate solvency deficiency of all Air Canada plans** exceeds **$150 million**.

### Case 1: Aggregate solvency deficiency ‚â• $150 million  
Use the formula:

> **Amount to pay = (Solvency Deficiency of Plan) √ó (150,000,000 / Aggregate Solvency Deficiency of All Plans)**

This is a **proportional share** of the total deficiency.

### Case 2: Aggregate solvency deficiency < $150 million  
Use the formula:

> **Amount to pay = (Solvency Deficiency of Plan) √ó (Aggregate Solvency Deficiency of All Plans) / (Aggregate Solvency Liabilities of All Plans)**

This is a **ratio-based formula** that considers both deficiency and liabilities.

> This ensures that plans with higher relative underfunding contribute more proportionally.

## ‚è≥ Special Requirement for the 2020 Plan Year (Subsection 3)

Unless the entire plan is terminated or Air Canada provides formal notice under subsection 6(1), **a specific additional payment must be made in the 2020 plan year**.

### What is this payment?
It is calculated under **subsection (4)** and must be paid **by December 31, 2020**.

### Formula (based on the aggregate solvency deficiency):

#### Case A: Aggregate deficiency ‚â• amount determined by formula A  
> **Amount = (Solvency Deficiency of Plan) √ó (Formula A) / (Aggregate Solvency Deficiencies of All Plans)**  
> Where Formula A =  
> - Number of plan years after 2013  
> - Plus the sum of additional payments made in those years (beyond normal cost)  
> - Multiplied by the solvency deficiency of the plan

#### Case B: Aggregate deficiency < amount determined by formula G  
> **Amount = (Solvency Deficiency of Plan) √ó (Formula G) / (Aggregate Solvency Deficiencies of All Plans)**  
> Where Formula G = same as above, but includes solvency liabilities in the denominator.

> This formula adjusts for the **cumulative underfunding** over time and ensures that plans contribute to closing the gap based on historical funding shortfalls.

> **Note**: This payment is designed to address long-term underfunding and is not a one-time fix.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Interest on Late Payments

If an employer fails to make the required payment under subsection (3) (the 2020 special payment) **within the prescribed time**, the unpaid amount will bear **interest at the rate used to determine the plan‚Äôs solvency liabilities**.

This encourages timely compliance and provides a financial incentive to meet funding obligations.

## üö´ Discontinuation of Funding (Subsection 1)

Air Canada may **choose to discontinue funding** of all Air Canada pension plans under these regulations for a given plan year.

### How it works:
- Air Canada must provide **written notice to the Superintendent** on or before:
  - The day the actuarial report is filed, **or**
  - June 30 of the plan year
- The regulations **cease to apply** to the plans on **December 31 of the previous plan year**.

> Example: If notice is given in 2020, the regulations stop applying to the plans on **December 31, 2020**.

### Notification to Members and Beneficiaries
- Within **30 days** of giving notice, Air Canada must send a **written notice** to:
  - All plan members and beneficiaries
  - Their spouses (or common-law partners, if cohabiting)

> This ensures transparency and allows members to understand the change.

---

## üí° Adjustment of Solvency Ratio (Post-Notice)

In the plan year when the notice is provided, the **average solvency ratio** for each plan must be adjusted:
- **Solvency assets are increased** by the amount determined under subsection (3) (the special payment).

> This adjustment ensures that the solvency ratio reflects the actual funding that was made in that year, even if the plan is no longer being funded under the regulations.

---

## üö´ Exclusion of Section 9.3 of PBSSR

- **Section 9.3 of the PBSSR (except subsection 9.3(2)) does not apply** to Air Canada pension plans.
- This means the standard solvency ratio rules (e.g., requiring a minimum ratio of 1.0) are not applied.

> However, the **prescribed solvency ratio level is set at 1.0** for the purposes of **paragraphs 10.1(2)(c) and (d)** of the PBSSA.

> This means that the plan must maintain a solvency ratio of **at least 1.0** (assets ‚â• liabilities), as determined by the solvency valuation.

## üö™ Special Payment on Termination

If a plan is **terminated**, the special payment due under **paragraph 29(6)(b)** of the PBSSA is calculated as:

> **Sum of:**
> 1. The amount that would have been required under paragraph 5(1)(b) for the period from the day of termination to the end of the final plan year  
> 2. The amount determined under subsection 5(4)

> The amounts used are based on the **last actuarial report before termination**.

> This ensures that even in the case of closure, the plan is properly funded to the extent possible.
