## **1. Purpose of the Regulation**

This Order establishes a **levy system** to fund the **promotion and research activities** of the **Canadian Beef Cattle Research, Market Development and Promotion Agency (Agency)**. The levies are imposed on the sale or importation of beef cattle and beef products to ensure ongoing investment in:

- Market development  
- Consumer promotion  
- Research into beef production and sustainability  
- Enhancing the competitiveness and visibility of Canadian beef in domestic and international markets  

The levies are directly tied to the implementation of a national promotion and research plan approved by the Government in Council.

---

## **2. Legal Authority and Approval Process**

- The Agency was created under **subsection 39(1)b of the Farm Products Agencies Act** via a Proclamation.
- The Order falls under **paragraph 7(1)(d)d** of the FPA Act, which allows for levies when necessary for implementing a promotion and research plan.
- The Order is a **class of regulation** authorized under **section 2 of the Agencies’ Orders and Regulations Approval Order**, meaning it requires prior approval by the **National Farm Products Council (NFPC)**.
- The NFPC **approved the Order** after determining that the levies are necessary and appropriate for the Agency’s mandate.

> Therefore, the Agency is legally empowered to implement the levies under **paragraphs 42(1)(d)b and (e)b** of the FPA Act and **section 10 of the Agency’s Proclamation**.

## **4. Levy Structure and Application**

### **A. Domestic Sales (Interprovincial Trade)**

A **resident of a province** who sells beef cattle in interprovincial trade must pay a levy **per head** based on the type of cattle sold. The levies are set in the **table under Section 4** (not fully shown in your text, but implied in the structure). The table includes:

> The **levy is deducted from the purchase price** by the **purchaser**.

#### **Purchaser Obligations:**
- Must **deduct the levy** from the purchase price.
- Must **remit the levy** to the **collector** for their **province of residence** within the time frame required by provincial law.
- Must provide the **seller** with a document that includes:
  - Purchase price  
  - Amount of levy deducted  
  - Breakdown by seller’s province of residence  
  - Breakdown by type of cattle  
  - (If required by provincial law) Contact details of each seller involved

#### **Seller Obligations:**
- **Not required to pay** the levy if it has been deducted by the purchaser.
- Must **retain records** of the sale and provide them to the Agency upon request.
- Must **prove** that the levy was deducted if requested by the Agency.

#### **Failure to Deduct Levy:**
- If the purchaser fails to deduct the levy, the **seller must remit it to the Agency within 90 days** of the sale.

> **Records must be kept for 7 years** after the sale date and made available for inspection by the Agency.

---

### **B. Importation of Beef Cattle and Beef Products**

Every **importer** must pay a levy for:

- Each **head of beef cattle imported**
- Each **unit of imported beef product** (classified under specific tariff numbers)

#### **Levy Amount:**
- Determined by **Schedule 1** (not fully provided in your text), which sets a **per-head or per-carcass equivalent** rate.
- For imported beef products, the levy is calculated using a **formula**:
  > **Levy (in cents)** = (Tariff rate in cents per kg) × (Weight in kg)  
  This applies to each product classified under a specific **Customs Tariff item number** and class.

#### **Payment Requirements:**
- Must be paid **within 15 calendar days** of the invoice date.
- Payment must be made via **negotiable instrument in Canadian dollars**.
- Must include a **statement** containing:
  - Importer’s name, address, phone, email  
  - Number of heads of beef cattle imported (by delivery destination)  
  - Weight of imported beef products (by Customs Tariff item number)  
  - Total levy amount (broken down between cattle and products)  
  - Date of payment

#### **Refund Policy:**
- The Agency **must refund** a levy if:
  - The importer can prove the levy was **not required** under subsection 12(1), or  
  - The levy paid exceeds **$1.00 per head of beef cattle or per carcass equivalent of imported beef products**

> **Records must be kept for 7 years** after importation and made available for Agency inspection.

## **5. Provisions on Review and Sunset Clause**

- The Agency is **required to review this Order** during its **review of the promotion and research plan**, as mandated under **section 13 of the Agency Proclamation**.
- This ensures the levy system remains aligned with evolving market needs and research priorities.

> **Sunset Clause:**  
> - **Section 4 (domestic sales levies)** and **subsection 12(1) (import levies)** **cease to have effect on June 30, 2026**.  
> - This indicates a temporary, time-limited levy regime, likely to be replaced or revised after 2026.
