### **Detailed Summary of the Explosives Regulations, 2013**  
*(Pursuant to Section 5a of the Explosives Act, S.C. 2004, c. 15, s. 37; R.S., c. E-17)*

---

## **Overview**

The *Explosives Regulations, 2013* are a comprehensive set of rules established by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Natural Resources, under **Section 5a of the Explosives Act**. These regulations govern the **acquisition, possession, use, storage, transportation, manufacturing, and sale of explosives** in Canada. They are structured into **20 Parts**, each addressing specific aspects of explosive management.

Importantly, **these regulations do not apply universally**â€”certain types of explosives are exempt from many provisions, particularly those involving high-risk or restricted materials.

---

## **Scope and Applicability**

### **General Application**
- The regulations apply to **all persons** who are involved in any activity involving explosives or who are in the vicinity of explosives.
- The term **"activity involving an explosive"** includes:
  - Acquiring, possessing, selling, offering for sale, storing, manufacturing, transporting (including in transit), importing, exporting, or delivering an explosive.
  - Using fireworks (as defined in the regulations).

### **Exemptions from Certain Provisions**
The following explosives are **exempt from most provisions** of the Explosives Act and these Regulations:

| Exempt Explosive Type | Reason for Exemption |
|------------------------|------------------------|
| Safety and strike-anywhere matches | Commonly used for safety purposes |
| Life-saving devices (e.g., flares, signals) carried in vehicles/aircraft | Necessary for safe operation |
| Explosives classified as **UN 3268** under UN Model Regulations on Transport of Dangerous Goods | Already regulated internationally |
| Explosives diluted to **less than 1% by weight** (e.g., reagents, training kits for sniffer dogs) | Low hazard, used for testing or education |
| Christmas crackers containing **less than 2 mg** of explosive substance | Minimal risk, festive use |

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Explosive substance** | A solid or liquid (or mixture) capable of producing gas at high temperature, pressure, and speed that can cause structural damage. Includes substances that produce heat, light, sound, smoke, or a combination via a self-sustaining exothermic reactionâ€”even without gas production. |
| **Explosive article** | Any object containing one or more explosive substances. |
| **Military device** | A shell, bomb, projectile, mine, missile, rocket, shaped charge, grenade, or perforator manufactured exclusively for military or law enforcement use. |
| **Activity involving an explosive** | Includes acquiring, possessing, selling, storing, manufacturing, transporting, importing, exporting, delivering, or using fireworks. |
| **Attended** | Constantly monitored by a person (including via electronic means). |
| **Compatible** | A material that will not react chemically with an explosive or degrade it, nor increase the risk of ignition. |

## **Part 2: General Requirements and Prohibitions**

This part sets out **mandatory safety standards and prohibitions** applicable to all individuals involved in explosive-related activities.

### **Age Requirement**
- A person must be **at least 18 years old** to carry out an activity involving an explosive.
- **Exemptions**:
  - A person may acquire **small arms cartridges** for **personal use**.
  - A person under 18 may possess small arms cartridges or black powder cartouches at a **federal, provincial, or territorial site** with a **Division 2 factory licence**, if the site is conducting **sanctioned demonstrations or historical re-enactments**.

### **Prohibited Activities**
- **Selling or transferring** an explosive if there is **reasonable suspicion** that:
  - The recipient is not authorized under the Explosives Act or these Regulations.
  - The explosive will be used for **criminal purposes**.
  - The recipient is under the influence of **alcohol or a performance-diminishing substance**.

- **Acquiring an explosive** with restrictions only if:
  - The recipient is a person or body specified in the authorization.
  - The intended use matches the specified purpose in the authorization.

- **Under the influence of alcohol or performance-diminishing substances** is strictly prohibited when handling explosives.

> âœ… **Exception**: A person taking a **prescription drug** may handle explosives if they have **medical proof** that:
> - The drug is medically necessary.
> - It will not impair their ability to perform the activity safely.

### **Smoking Prohibition**
- **Smoking is prohibited**:
  - While carrying out an explosive activity.
  - Within **8 meters (26 feet)** of an explosive.

### **Labeling and Alteration Rules**
- **No alteration or defacement** of printing or labels on explosives or packaging **unless ordered by an inspector** to correct an error.
- **Exception**: After removing an explosive from packaging, the label may be altered to reflect the **remaining quantity**.

### **Truthful Documentation**
- All documents required by the regulations must be **true and not misleading**.
- Failure to disclose facts makes a document **false or misleading**.

### **Safety Awareness and Risk Mitigation**
- Any person conducting an activity involving explosives must ensure that:
  - They and those under their supervision understand:
    - The nature of the activity.
    - Measures to prevent **accidental ignition**.
    - Measures to **limit fire spread or explosion extent**.
    - Measures to **protect people from fire or explosion effects**.

- Must take **practical safety measures** to minimize harm to people or property.
