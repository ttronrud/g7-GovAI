### **Regulatory Summary**

**Regulation Title:**  
Designation of the Staff of the Non-Public Funds, Canadian Forces, as a Separate Employer under the Public Service Staff Relations Act

**Legal Authority:**  
- **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of National Defence**  
- Under **subsection 62(4)a** of the *Public Service Staff Relations Act* (PSA)

**Effective Provision:**  
- **Section 19(2)** of *S.C. 1999, c. 26* (Statutory Instrument 1999-26)

---

### **Core Provision and Purpose**

This regulation designates the **staff of the Non-Public Funds, Canadian Forces** as a **separate employer** for the purposes of **paragraph 62(1)(a)** of the *Public Service Staff Relations Act*.

#### What does this mean?

- The **Canadian Forces**, specifically those working within the **Non-Public Funds** (i.e., personnel funded through non-public sources such as the Department of National Defence budget, not through general public funding or government-wide public service funding), are legally recognized as operating under a **distinct employer status**.
- This designation allows the **Canadian Forces** to be treated separately from the broader federal public service in matters of **collective bargaining, labour relations, and dispute resolution**, as defined under the PSA.

#### Key Legal Basis:
- **Subsection 62(4)a** of the PSA empowers the Governor General in Council (on ministerial recommendation) to designate certain public service staff as belonging to a separate employer.
- **Paragraph 62(1)(a)** of the PSA defines the scope of the Act’s application — specifically, it governs the relationship between the federal public service and its employees. By designating the Non-Public Funds Canadian Forces staff as a separate employer, this regulation ensures that those staff members are **not subject to the general provisions of the PSA** in the same way as other federal public service employees.

---

### **Implications of the Designation**

1. **Separate Labour Relations Framework**  
   - Employees of the Non-Public Funds, Canadian Forces (e.g., military personnel, civilian staff in military departments) are **not covered** by the general labour relations rules of the *Public Service Staff Relations Act* as applied to the broader public service.
   - Instead, their labour relations may be governed by **other applicable legislation**, such as:
     - The *Canadian Forces Staff Relations Act* (if applicable)
     - The *Labour Relations Act* of the province or territory (if relevant)
     - Or other military-specific employment frameworks

2. **Exemption from Certain PSA Provisions**  
   - The designation **exempts** these staff members from key provisions of the PSA, including:
     - Collective bargaining obligations
     - Union recognition processes
     - Dispute resolution mechanisms under the PSA
   - This is consistent with the principle that **military personnel and their civilian support staff** operate under a unique operational and administrative framework.

3. **Administrative and Jurisdictional Clarity**  
   - The regulation provides **legal clarity** on which employees fall under which employer category, preventing overlap or confusion in labour relations matters.
   - It supports the **autonomy** of the Department of National Defence in managing its workforce, particularly in areas involving national security, operational readiness, and military discipline.

### **Clarification on Legislative Reference (Important Interpretive Provision)**

> *"Unless the context requires otherwise, every reference to the Public Service Labour Relations Act in any provision of a regulation, as defined in section 2 of the Statutory Instruments Act, made under an Act of Parliament, other than a provision referred to in subsection (1) is to be read as a reference to the Federal Public Sector Labour Relations Act."*

#### Interpretation:

- This provision serves as a **key interpretive rule** to avoid confusion between two similarly named acts:
  - *Public Service Labour Relations Act* (PSLRA) — an older, now largely superseded statute
  - *Federal Public Sector Labour Relations Act* (FPSLRA) — the current, modern legislation governing labour relations in the federal public service

- **Effect:**  
  - In any regulation (especially those made under federal Acts), **if a reference is made to the "Public Service Labour Relations Act"**, it is **automatically interpreted as referring to the Federal Public Sector Labour Relations Act**, **unless the context clearly indicates otherwise**.
  - This ensures **consistency and legal coherence** across federal regulations, especially in light of the replacement of the older PSLRA with the FPSLRA.

- **Purpose:**  
  - Prevents misapplication of outdated or obsolete legislation.
  - Aligns all federal labour regulations with the current legal framework.
