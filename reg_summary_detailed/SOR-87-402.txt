### üîç **1. Purpose and Background**
These regulations were introduced to **modernize and standardize the rules governing government procurement** in Canada. They establish a clear framework for how federal government contracting authorities enter into contracts for goods, services, and construction work ‚Äî including procedures for bidding, security requirements, and ethical compliance.

The regulations are issued under the authority of the **Financial Administration Act (FAA)**, specifically **subsection 34(1)**, which allows the Governor in Council (on the recommendation of the Treasury Board) to amend or replace existing procurement rules.

> **Note**: The original version of these regulations was **repealed in 2019 (SOR/2019-199, s. 2)**, and this document represents the **current version** that was enacted in 1984 and remains in force as a replacement.

### üìè **3. Scope of Application**

These regulations apply to **all government contracts involving payment by His Majesty**, including:

- Goods contracts
- Service contracts
- Construction contracts

**However, they do NOT apply to the following specific types of contracts:**

| Excluded Contracts | Reason |
|-------------------|--------|
| Contracts entered into by the **National Film Board** | Exempted by special status |
| Construction contracts under the **Veterans‚Äô Land Act** | Specific statutory exemption |
| Contracts under the **Indian Act** involving "Indian moneys" | Indigenous-specific financial arrangements |
| Contracts for **legal services** | Governed separately by the Minister of Justice |
| Service contracts for **expert advice/testimony** in litigation or disputes | Not considered standard procurement |
| Contracts to implement **settlement agreements or court orders** in class proceedings | Legal or judicial process exceptions |
| Fit-up of offices/residential accommodations under **Federal Real Property and Federal Immovables Act** | Covered by specific legislation |
| **Interchange Canada Agreements** | Covered under intergovernmental arrangements |
| **Interim defence contracts** | For operational necessity or defence logistics |
| Contracts with **government entities** (provincial, municipal, foreign) | Intergovernmental or non-federal scope |
| Contracts involving **national security exceptions** | Where the national security exception under **Canadian International Trade Tribunal Procurement Inquiry Regulations, s.10(3)** is invoked |

### üìù **4. Bidding Requirements**

#### ‚úÖ General Rule:
Before entering into any contract, a **contracting authority must solicit bids** in accordance with **Section 7**.

#### Methods of Solicitation:
1. **Public Notice**: A formal call for bids must be issued in a manner consistent with accepted trade practices.
2. **Invitation from Suppliers‚Äô List**: Bids may be invited directly from suppliers already listed in the authority‚Äôs suppliers‚Äô list.

> üîÅ **Note**: The requirement to solicit bids has been **repealed in 2023 (SOR/2023-82, s.3)** ‚Äî this indicates a potential shift toward more flexible procurement methods, though the current version still retains the requirement.

#### Exceptions to Bidding Requirement:
A contracting authority may enter into a contract **without soliciting bids** if one of the following applies:

| Exception | Thresholds |
|---------|------------|
| **Pressing Emergency** | Delay would be injurious to public interest |
| **Estimated Expenditure** | <br> ‚Ä¢ Goods contract: **$25,000** <br> ‚Ä¢ Services for international development (planning/design): **$100,000** <br> ‚Ä¢ Services for construction/repair: **$100,000** <br> ‚Ä¢ Other contracts: **$40,000** |
| **Nature of Work** | Would not be in the public interest to solicit bids |
| **Only One Supplier Capable** | No alternative supplier exists |

> üí° These thresholds reflect a balance between transparency and operational necessity.

### üí∞ **5. Security Requirements (Bid & Contract Security)**

All security deposits and bonds must be managed in accordance with strict procedures.

#### A. **Bid Security**
- Must be in the form of a **bid bond** or **security deposit**.
- Must be **held by the contracting authority** until the bid is accepted or rejected.

#### B. **Contract Security (Required upon Award)**
- If required, the contracting authority must:
  - **Hold the original bid security** until contract security is provided.
  - Upon provision of contract security, **return the bid security** unless it becomes part of the contract security.

#### C. **Return of Security Deposits**
- If a bid is **rejected**, the bid security is returned.
- If a bid is **accepted but no contract security is required**, the bid security is returned.
- If a bid is **accepted and contract security is provided**, the bid security is **returned or retained** as part of the contract security.

#### D. **Negotiable Security Deposits (e.g., Bill of Exchange)**
- Must be **immediately deposited into the Consolidated Revenue Fund** if received as contract security or retained under paragraph 13(2)(b).
- For **other negotiable deposits**:
  - Held in a safe place, or
  - Immediately forwarded to the **Receiver General** for safekeeping.
