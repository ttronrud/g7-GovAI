**Detailed Summary of the Citizenship Regulations, No. 2 (S.C. 2014, c. 22, s. 18 and s. 26; R.S.C. 1985, c. C-29)**  
*Ottawa, May 28, 2015*

---

### **1. Purpose and Authority**
The Minister of Citizenship and Immigration issued these **Citizenship Regulations, No. 2** under specific provisions of the *Citizenship Act*:
- **Subsection 21.1(5)** and **(7)a** – Authority to make regulations regarding applications for citizenship.
- **Section 27.2b** – Power to prescribe the form, content, and requirements for applications under the Act.

These regulations provide detailed procedural and substantive requirements for various types of citizenship applications, including naturalization, adoption-based citizenship, and renunciation of citizenship.

---

| Term | Meaning |

## **3. Application Requirements by Type**

---

### **A. General Application for Citizenship (Subsection 5(1))**

An application under subsection 5(1) of the Act must be submitted in the **prescribed form**, filed according to the Minister’s instructions, and include the following:

1. **Date and Place of Birth**  
   - Evidence (e.g., birth certificate) establishing the applicant’s date and place of birth.

2. **Date of Permanent Residence**  
   - A document from Canadian immigration authorities or other evidence showing when the applicant became a permanent resident.

3. **Meeting Citizenship Requirements**  
   - Proof that the applicant meets the criteria in **paragraph 5(1)(c)(i)** of the Act (typically, having lived in Canada for a minimum period and meeting language and character requirements).

4. **Tax Number (Most Recent)**  
   - One of the following must be provided:
     - Social Insurance Number (SIN)
     - Individual Tax Number (ITN) from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)
     - Temporary Tax Number (TTN) from CRA  
   - If the applicant has one of these numbers, they must provide **consent** to share their tax information with the Minister.

5. **Declaration if No Tax Number**  
   - If no such number exists, the applicant must submit a **declaration** stating:
     - They do not have a SIN, ITN, or TTN
     - They were **not required** to file a tax return under the *Income Tax Act* for **each of the five years** immediately preceding the application date

6. **Language Proficiency (for applicants aged 18–54)**  
   - Must demonstrate adequate knowledge of **one of Canada’s official languages** (English or French).  
   - Acceptable evidence includes language test results or other verified proof.

7. **Photographs**  
   - Two recent photographs in the format specified on the prescribed form.

8. **Payment of Fees**  
   - Evidence that the required fees under **sections 31 and 32** have been paid.

---

### **B. Application by a Former or Current Member of the Canadian Forces (Subsection 5(1))**

Specific requirements apply to individuals who have served in the Canadian Armed Forces:

1. **Date and Place of Birth**  
   - As above.

2. **Permanent Residency Date**  
   - Evidence of when they became a permanent resident.

3. **Service Requirement**  
   - Must have completed **at least three years of service** in the Canadian Forces within the **six years before** the application date.

4. **Tax Number**  
   - Same as above (SIN, ITN, or TTN), with consent required if applicable.

5. **Honourable Discharge (if released)**  
   - If released from service, must provide evidence of **honourable discharge**.

6. **Language Requirement**  
   - Applies to applicants aged 18–54: must meet official language proficiency criteria.

7. **Photographs and Fees**  
   - Two photographs and proof of payment of required fees.

> *Note:* The language and fee requirements are identical to those in the general application.

---

### **C. Application by a Person Attached or Seconded to the Canadian Forces (Subsection 5(1))**

For individuals who were temporarily attached or seconded to the Canadian Forces:

1. **Date and Place of Birth**  
   - Required.

2. **Service Requirement**  
   - Must have completed **at least three years of service** in the Canadian Forces within the **six years before** the application date.

3. **Language Requirement**  
   - Applies to those aged 18–54: must meet official language criteria.

4. **Photographs and Fees**  
   - Two photographs and proof of payment of required fees.

> *Note:* No tax number or permanent residency requirement is included, as this group is not considered a general applicant.

---

### **D. Application for Citizenship of a Child (Subsection 5(5)) – Child Born to a Citizen**

An application under subsection 5(5) must include:

1. **Birth Certificate or Alternative Evidence**  
   - Proof of the child’s date and place of birth.

2. **Birth Parent Was a Citizen at Birth**  
   - Evidence that the child’s birth parent was a Canadian citizen at the time of birth.

3. **Child is Stateless**  
   - Must prove the child has **always been stateless** (no nationality).

4. **Photographs**  
   - Two photographs of the child in the prescribed format.

> *Note:* This applies to children who are not born to Canadian citizens but are stateless and meet the criteria under the Act.

---

### **E. Application on Behalf of a Minor Child (Subsection 5(2))**

When applying on behalf of a minor child:

- Must be **filed by a parent or legal guardian**.
- **Countersigned by the child** if:
  - The child is **14 years or older** on the application date
  - The child is **not prevented** from understanding the significance of the application due to a mental disability
- Required materials:
  1. Child’s date and place of birth
  2. Evidence the child is the child of a Canadian citizen
  3. If not the parent, a **court order or written agreement** proving custody
  4. Evidence of permanent residency (if applicable)
  5. If the child is 14+ and did not countersign, proof that they are **mentally disabled** and unable to understand the application
  6. Two photographs of the child
  7. Proof of payment of the fee under **section 31**

> *Note:* A provision previously required a declaration about the child’s residence; this was **repealed in SOR/2017-191, s. 2**.

---

### **F. Application for Citizenship by Adoption (Subsection 5.1(1)) – Child Adopted on or After January 1, 1947**

For a minor child adopted in Canada:

- Must be filed by a **parent or legal guardian**
- **Countersigned by the child** if:
  - Age ≥14 and not mentally impaired
- Required materials:
  1. Birth certificate or alternative evidence of the child’s date and place of birth
  2. Evidence that a **parent was a citizen at the time of adoption**
  3. If applicant is not a parent or guardian, proof of parent/guardian relationship
  4. If child is 14+ and did not countersign, proof of mental disability
  5. Evidence that the **adoption occurred while the child was a minor**
  6. Two photographs
  7. Proof of payment of the fee under section 31

> *Note:* The adoption must have occurred on or after **January 1, 1947**.

---

### **G. Application for Citizenship by Adoption (Subsection 5.1(2)) – Adult Adopted at Age 18 or Older**

For an adult who was adopted at age 18 or older:

- Must provide:
  1. Birth certificate or alternative evidence
  2. Evidence that:
     - If adopted on or after Jan. 1, 1947: parent was a citizen at time of adoption
     - If adopted before Jan. 1, 1947: parent became a citizen on that date
     - If adopted before April 1, 1949: parent became a citizen on that date following Newfoundland’s union with Canada
  3. Proof that adoption occurred **when the person was 18 or older**
  4. Two photographs
