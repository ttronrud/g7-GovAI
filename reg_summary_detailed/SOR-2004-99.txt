## **Summary of the Tax Court of Canada Rules of Procedure Respecting the Customs Act (Informal Procedure)**

### **1. Legal Basis and Approval**
- These rules were **proposed** by the Rules Committee of the Tax Court of Canada under **subsection 22(3)a of the Tax Court of Canada Act** and published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **August 30, 2003**.
- The proposal invited public comment from interested parties.
- The rules were **formally adopted** by the Governor in Council on **December 16, 2003**, based on the recommendation of the Minister of Justice under **subsection 20(1)c of the Tax Court of Canada Act**.
- The rules are effective upon publication in the *Canada Gazette*.
- The rules are cited as:  
  **"Tax Court of Canada Rules of Procedure Respecting the Customs Act (Informal Procedure)"**

---

### **2. Scope of Application**
- These rules apply specifically to **appeals under Part V.1 of the Customs Act**, **except** where the **General Procedure Rules** apply.
- This is an **informal procedure** designed for streamlined and efficient resolution of certain customs-related appeals, particularly those involving assessments or objections.

---

### **3. Judicial Authority**
- **Associate Judges** have **full powers** of a regular judge under these rules.
- An **associate judge may sit and act anywhere in Canada** at any time.
- When an associate judge sits, they **constitute the full Court** for the purposes of the proceeding.

---

### **4. Filing Procedures**
#### **Filing Methods for a Notice of Appeal**
A notice of appeal may be filed by:
- Depositing it with the **Registry**;
- Sending it by **mail** to the Registry; or  
- Sending it by **fax** or **electronic filing** to the Registry.

#### **Filing of Other Documents**
All other documents (e.g., replies, expert reports) must be filed using one of the following:
- Deposit with the Registry;
- Mail;
- Fax; or  
- Electronic filing.

#### **Date of Filing**
- For documents filed with the Registry, by mail, or by fax:  
  → The **date shown on the receipt stamp** placed by the Registry.
- For documents filed electronically:  
  → The **date shown on the acknowledgment of receipt** issued by the Court.

#### **Original Version of Documents**
- The **printed copy** of a document provided by the Registry is deemed to be the **original version**.
- Parties must provide a **paper copy** if required by the rules or requested by a party or the Court.

#### **Failure to File**
- If the Registry has **no record of receipt**, the document is **deemed not filed**, unless the Court directs otherwise.

---

### **5. Address for Service**
- The notice of appeal must include the **appellant’s address for service**.
- This may be:
  - The appellant’s address; or  
  - The address of the appellant’s **counsel or agent**.
- A **written notice of any change** in address must be submitted **immediately** to the Registry.
- Until such a change is recorded, service is deemed valid if sent to the address in the original notice of appeal.

---

### **6. Reply to Notice of Appeal**
A reply must contain:
- Facts admitted;
- Facts denied;
- Facts the respondent lacks knowledge about and puts in issue;
- Findings or assumptions made by the Minister in the assessment;
- Any other material facts;
- Issues to be decided;
- Statutory provisions relied upon;
- Reasons for the respondent’s position;
- Relief sought.

#### **Service of Reply**
- The Minister must serve a copy of the reply by **registered mail** to the appellant’s address for service within **five days** of filing.

---

### **7. Expert Witnesses**
- A party intending to call an expert witness must file a **signed report** at least **10 days before the hearing**.
- The report must include:
  - Expert’s name, address, and qualifications;
  - Substance of the testimony.
- An expert **may not testify** unless granted **leave by the presiding judge**.

---

### **8. Discontinuance of Appeal**
- An appellant may **discontinue the appeal** at any time by submitting a written notice in the form set out in **Schedule 8**.

---

### **9. Disposition of Proceedings**
- The Court shall:
  - **Render a judgment** for appeals involving **substantive rights**;
  - **Issue an order** for all other interlocutory or procedural matters.
- A **judgment is dated on the day it is signed** and is pronounced on that day.
- The judgment and its reasons (if any) must be **deposited with the Registry without delay**.

---

### **10. Costs and Taxation**
#### **General Authority**
- The Court has discretion to determine:
  - The amount of costs;
  - Allocation of costs;
  - Who pays them.

#### **Cost Awards to Respondent**
- The Court may award costs **only to the respondent** if the appellant has **unduly delayed** the resolution of the appeal.
- The award **cannot exceed the amounts listed in Section 10** of the rules.

#### **Fixed Cost Orders**
- The Court may direct a **fixed sum** payment instead of taxed costs.

#### **Fees for Counsel Services**
The following fees may be allowed for legal services:
- Preparation of notice of appeal or advice: **$185**
- Preparation for hearing: **$250**
- Conduct of hearing: **$375 per half day or part thereof**
- Taxation of costs: **$60**

#### **Disbursements for Non-Counsel Advisers**
- If an appellant is assisted by an adviser (not counsel), disbursements may be allowed **up to half** of the amounts listed in Section 10.
- Other disbursements may be allowed **only if essential** and if proven to be incurred or owed.

#### **Taxes on Counsel Fees**
- All **GST, PST, or other taxes** paid on counsel fees or disbursements may be allowed **if not otherwise reimbursed**, including input tax credits.

#### **Witness Fees**
- **Non-expert witnesses**:
  - **$75 per day** + reasonable transportation and living expenses.
  - Payment only if the **appellant is called to testify**.
- **Expert witnesses**:
  - Up to **$300 per day** for preparation and testimony.
  - May be adjusted by the Court.

---

### **11. Taxation of Costs**
- Costs are taxed by the **Registrar** or a person designated by the **Chief Justice**.
- The appellant must file a **bill of costs** (in the form of Schedule 14) with the Registrar.
- The Registrar sends a **true copy** of the bill to the respondent’s counsel immediately.
- After taxation, the Registrar issues a **certificate of taxation** to each party.
- Any party may **appeal the taxation** within **20 days** of the certificate’s mailing.
- The Court may **extend this period** if justified.

#### **Costs in Cases of Ministerial Orders**
- If the Minister has made an order under **Section 19.1 of the Customs Act**, costs may be awarded **against the person subject to the order**.

---

### **12. Election to Use Informal Procedure**
- A person who has not explicitly elected to use the **informal procedure** (Sections 18.3001 and 18.3003 to 18.302 of the Customs Act) may make such an election **within 90 days** after the reply is served.
- The election must be in the form set out in **Schedule 18**.
- The Court may allow **additional time** for such an election on **motion for special reasons**.

---

### **13. Applications to Extend Time to File Objection**
A person may apply to the Court to extend time to file a **notice of objection** under the following conditions:
- The Minister has **refused** the application; or
- 90 days have passed since the application was made and the Minister has not responded.

#### **Limitations**
- The application **cannot be made after 30 days** from when the Minister’s decision was mailed.
- The application must be filed with the Registry in **three copies**, accompanied by:
  - Three copies of the notice of objection;
  - Three copies of the Minister’s decision (if any).

#### **Conditions for Granting the Application**
The Court may grant the extension **only if**:
- The application is made **within one year** of the expiry of the time specified in Part V.1 for filing a notice of objection;
- The applicant demonstrates:
  - They were **unable to act** or to give a mandate;
  - Or had a **bona fide intention** to object;
  - The delay was **just and equitable**;
  - The application was made as soon as circumstances allowed.

#### **Filing Date**
- The application is deemed filed on the **date of receipt by the Registry**, even if documents are not attached, **provided** that:
  - The documents are filed within **30 days** of receipt; or  
  - Within a **reasonable time** set by the Court.
