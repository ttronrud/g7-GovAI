### **Detailed Summary of the Controlled Access Zone Order (Halifax, Esquimalt and Nanoose Harbours)**

This regulation, issued under the authority of **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council** on the recommendation of the **Minister of National Defence**, establishes and governs **Controlled Access Zones (CAZs)** in three key Canadian naval and defence locations: **Halifax Harbour**, **Esquimalt Harbour**, and **Nanoose Harbour**. The purpose of the Order is to ensure the **safety and security** of defence establishments, military materiel, visiting forces, and personnel by restricting access to certain areas of water and airspace.

---

## **1. Key Definitions**

- **Controlled Access Zone (CAZ):**  
  A defined zone — both **on the surface (water and land)** and **in the airspace above** — designated by the Minister under section 2 of the National Defence Act. It includes all airspace above and water/land below the zone.

- **Security Guard:**  
  - An officer or non-commissioned member of the Canadian Armed Forces employed to enforce this Order; or  
  - A person authorized by the **Chief of the Defence Staff (CDS)** to enforce the Order.

- **Defence Establishment, Materiel, Minister, Non-Commissioned Member, Officer:**  
  These terms are defined consistently with their meanings under **subsection 2(1) of the National Defence Act**.

---

## **2. Authority and Scope of Designation**

### **Who Can Designate a CAZ?**
- The **Minister of National Defence**, acting on the **recommendation of the Chief of the Defence Staff**, may designate a CAZ.

### **What Areas Can Be Designated?**
- Only areas or parts of areas of water listed in **Column 2 of the Schedule** at locations specified in **Column 1** (i.e., Halifax, Esquimalt, and Nanoose Harbours).
- The designation must be **reasonably necessary** to ensure the safety or security of:
  1. A **defence establishment**;
  2. **Materiel or property** belonging to the Canadian Forces or the Department of National Defence (outside a defence establishment);
  3. A **vessel, aircraft, or other property** under the control of a visiting force legally present in Canada (via the *Visiting Forces Act* or otherwise);
  4. **Any person** in, on, or about any of the above.

> ⚠️ *Note:* The CAZ must not extend beyond what is **reasonably necessary** to protect the safety or security of the above entities or persons.

---

## **3. Types and Duration of CAZs**

- A CAZ may be:
  - A **fixed zone** (static boundary); or  
  - A **zone surrounding** a moving or stationary object (e.g., a ship at anchor, a vessel, or aircraft).

- Designations can be:
  - For a **determinate period** (e.g., 30 days, 6 months); or  
  - For an **indeterminate period** (ongoing until revoked).

- The Minister may **amend, vary, or cancel** any designation at any time.

---

## **4. Public Notification Requirements**

### **General Rule:**
- The Minister must **notify all persons who may be affected** by the designation (or its renewal, amendment, variation, or cancellation) **as soon as possible** using appropriate means (e.g., public notice, press release, direct communication), **unless**:
  - It is **inadvisable** due to **international relations**, **national defence**, or **national security**; or  
  - The designation is for **less than 30 days** or cancellation occurs within **30 days** of designation; or  
  - The designation relates to a CAZ under **paragraph 4(b)** (i.e., areas involving visiting forces).

### **Publication in Canada Gazette:**
- The Minister must **publish a notice** in the *Canada Gazette* within **23 days** of:
  - Making a designation,  
  - Renewing, amending, varying, or cancelling it.

> ⚠️ Exceptions apply under subsection (2), particularly for short-term or sensitive designations involving international forces.

---

## **5. Access Permissions by the Chief of the Defence Staff (CDS)**

The CDS has significant discretion to manage access to CAZs:

- **Permit access** to persons or classes of persons:
  - **Without conditions**; or  
  - **With conditions** deemed appropriate based on security and safety needs.

- **Prohibit access** to certain persons or classes.

> These decisions are made based on the **safety or security** of:
> - Defence establishments, materiel, visiting forces, or persons.

### **Notification of Access Rules:**
- The CDS must notify affected persons **as soon as possible** via appropriate means (e.g., public notice, direct communication), **unless** it is inadvisable due to international relations or national security.

---

## **6. Search and Enforcement Provisions**

### **Entry/Exit Search Requirement:**
- Every person **entering or exiting** a CAZ must **submit to a search** of their person or any property under their control **upon demand by a security guard**.

### **Refusal to Submit to Search:**
- If a person refuses:
  - **Entering the zone:** May be **refused entry**.
  - **Exiting the zone:** The person or their property may be **searched** by a security guard using **only the force necessary** for the purpose.

### **Gender-Specific Search Rules:**
- A search of a person must be conducted **only by a security guard of the same sex** — **unless** there are **reasonable grounds** to believe that an **immediate search is necessary** to protect safety or security.

### **Unwarranted Search of Property:**
- A security guard may **search any property or thing** in a CAZ **without a warrant** if they have **reasonable grounds** to believe it:
  - Contains or may contain something that endangers the safety or security of:
    - Defence establishments, materiel, visiting forces, or persons.

> This power is a key tool for rapid response to potential threats.

---

## **7. Compliance and Removal from CAZ**

### **With Permission:**
- A person in a CAZ with **permission** must:
  - Comply with **all conditions of access**;
  - Obey all directions from a security guard.
- If they fail to comply, the **security guard may remove** the person or their property from the zone.

### **Without Permission:**
- A person in a CAZ **without permission** must:
  - Comply with all directions from a security guard.
- Failure to comply results in **immediate removal** by a security guard.

### **Use of Force:**
- A security guard may use **only such force as is necessary** to remove a person or property from the CAZ.

---

## **8. Authorization of Officers**

- The Chief of the Defence Staff may **authorize an officer** to act on their behalf in enforcing this Order — **except for section 2** (which likely refers to the designation process).

---

## **9. Specific CAZ Boundaries (Schedule)**

The Order designates the following **specific zones** with precise geographic coordinates:

---

### **A. Halifax Harbour**
- The area of water in **Halifax Harbour** and the **contiguous area** bounded by a straight line joining:
  - 44°30.19′N, 63°31.19′W  
  - 44°35.55′N, 63°26.61′W  

> This zone covers a major naval and defence hub in Nova Scotia.

---

### **B. Esquimalt Harbour**
- **Northwest boundary:**  
  Straight line between:
  - 48°27.13′N, 123°27.23′W  
  - 48°27.36′N, 123°27.01′W  

- **Contiguous area** bounded by:
  - 48°25.31′N, 123°25.21′W  
  - 48°23.21′N, 123°25.21′W  
  - 48°23.03′N, 123°28.79′W  

> This zone includes the **Canadian Forces Base Esquimalt**, a key naval facility in British Columbia.
