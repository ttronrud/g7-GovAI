**Detailed Summary of the *Small Businesses Loans Regulations***

---

### **Overview**

The *Small Businesses Loans Regulations* (the "Regulations") are a set of administrative rules governing **guaranteed business improvement loans** under the *Small Businesses Loans Act* (the "Act"). These regulations establish the eligibility criteria, loan classifications, security requirements, repayment terms, interest rates, default provisions, and loss recovery mechanisms for loans provided to small business enterprises.

The Regulations apply **only to guaranteed business improvement loans authorized under the Act and made before April 1, 1993**. As such, they are not applicable to loans made after that date or to other types of loans such as farm or fisheries improvement loans.

---

## **Eligible Loan Types**

The Regulations prescribe **four classes of guaranteed business improvement loans**, each with specific eligibility criteria:

---

### **1. Fixed Equipment Loans**
- Purpose: Financing purchase, installation, renovation, improvement, or modernization of **equipment affixed to real or immovable property** (e.g., machinery on a factory floor).
- **Eligibility Conditions**:
  - The borrower must be the **owner or tenant** of the premises where the equipment is located or will be placed.
  - Loan amount must not exceed the **greater of**:
    - 80% of the **estimated cost** of equipment (excluding labour), as stated in the application;
    - 80% of the **actual cost** (excluding labour), as proven by documentation.
  - The lender must have required, and the borrower must have provided, **evidence of proper use** of funds (i.e., used for the stated purpose).

---

### **2. Movable Equipment Loans**
- Purpose: Financing purchase, renovation, or modernization of **equipment not affixed to real property** (e.g., trucks, office equipment).
- **Eligibility Conditions**:
  - Loan amount must not exceed the **greater of**:
    - 80% of the **estimated cost** (excluding labour);
    - 80% of the **actual cost** (excluding labour), as documented.
  - Must have **evidence of proper fund use** as required by normal banking practices.

---

### **3. Premises Loans**
- Purpose: Financing renovation, improvement, modernization, extension, construction, or purchase of **premises** (buildings or structures).
- **Eligibility Conditions**:
  - Borrower must be the **owner or tenant** of the premises in the case of renovation or extension.
  - In the case of construction or purchase, the borrower must be an owner or someone who will become one through purchase or construction.
  - Loan amount must not exceed the **greater of**:
    - 90% of the **estimated cost** (excluding labour);
    - 90% of the **actual cost** (excluding labour).
  - Must have **evidence of proper fund use**.

---

### **4. Land Loans**
- Purpose: Financing the **purchase of land** necessary for business operations.
- **Eligibility Conditions**:
  - Loan amount must not exceed the **greater of**:
    - 90% of the **estimated cost** of land (including any structure on it);
    - 90% of the **actual cost** (including any structure), as documented.
  - Must have **evidence of proper fund use**.

> âœ… All loans require **proof of purpose** and **evidence of expenditure** in line with the application.

## **Security Requirements**

### **Mandatory Security**
- A lender **must acquire and perfect a security interest** (in a form consistent with normal banking practice) in:
  - Fixed equipment,
  - Movable equipment,
  - Premises, or
  - Land,
  depending on the loan type.

### **Alternate Security for Tenants**
- If the borrower is a **tenant**, the lender may instead acquire **alternate security** (e.g., a leasehold interest or other collateral), rather than a security interest in the premises.

> âš ï¸ **Note**: Even if normal banking practice would not require security, the Regulations **still mandate** it for guaranteed business improvement loans.

### **Security After Release**
- A lender **may release** security, but must maintain **adequate security** throughout the remaining term of the loan.

### **Additional Security**
- A lender **may take additional security** beyond the required one.

## **Repayment Terms**

### **General Requirements**
- Repayments must be made in **installments** payable **at least annually**.
- Lenders may offer **more frequent payments** (e.g., semi-annually), at their discretion.

### **First Payment Deadline**
- For loans made in **multiple disbursements**, the **first principal installment** must be paid **no later than one year** from the date of the **initial disbursement**.

### **Flexibility in Repayment Terms**
- The lender may, with **borrower approval**, alter or revise repayment terms if:
  - The borrower is in default or warns of potential default;
  - The borrower wants to take out additional loans;
  - The lender believes it is appropriate to adjust terms based on the borrowerâ€™s financial obligations.

#### **Allowed Changes**:
- Extend the repayment term (within maximum limits set in the Act);
- Change the amount of periodic instalments;
- Change the frequency of payments (but **no more frequently than annually**).

> âš ï¸ **Critical Limitation**:  
> If the revised terms result in a **longer repayment period than allowed under paragraph 3(2)(e) of the Act**, the **Ministerâ€™s prior written approval is required**.

## **Interest Rate Cap**

The **maximum annual interest rate** is set as:

> **1% + prime lending rate**, where the prime rate is determined as follows:

| Type of Lender | Prime Rate Basis |
|---------------|------------------|
| **Bank (Bank Act)** | Prime lending rate of that bank on the day of the loan |
| **Credit Union, Caisse Populaire, Cooperative** | Prime rate of one of the following: <br> - Bank of Montreal <br> - Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce <br> - National Bank of Canada <br> - Bank of Nova Scotia <br> - Royal Bank of Canada <br> - Toronto-Dominion Bank <br> (Selected by the lender at loan origination) |
| **Trust, Loan, or Insurance Corporation** | Prime rate of a Bank Act-chartered bank (selected by the lender) |

> âœ… The rate is **not fixed** and is based on the **daily prime rate** during the loan term.

## **Default and Enforcement**

### **Automatic Default**
- If a borrower **fails to make a payment**, the **entire outstanding balance** becomes due and payable at the lenderâ€™s option.

### **Conviction Under the Act**
- If a borrower is **convicted of an offence under the Act**, the entire balance becomes immediately due and payable.

### **False Statements or Misuse of Funds**
- If a lender discovers:
  - A **false statement** in the loan application; or
  - The borrower used proceeds **for an unauthorized purpose**,
  - The lender may:
    - Take any action it deems appropriate;
    - Immediately report the matter to the **Minister**;
    - The Minister may then require further action.

---

## **Loss Recovery and Claims**

### **Claim Submission Timeline**
- A lender may **only claim loss** from the Minister **after 90 days** of default.
- The claim must be submitted **within 19 months** of the date the default occurred.
