### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

This regulation, issued under the **Canada Wildlife Act** (*S.C. 1973-74, c. 21*), formally designates specific public lands across Canada as **National Wildlife Areas (NWAs)** and assigns administrative, management, and control responsibilities to the **Minister of the Environment**. The authority for this designation stems from the **Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the Minister of the Environment, pursuant to **subsection 4(1)** of the Canada Wildlife Act.

---

## **1. Legal Basis and Purpose**

- **Legal Authority**:  
  The Governor General in Council (acting on the advice of the Minister of the Environment) determines that certain public lands are essential for:
  - Wildlife research
  - Conservation
  - Interpretation (public education and awareness)

- **Purpose of the Regulation**:  
  To establish and designate specific parcels of land as **National Wildlife Areas**, which are protected for the conservation of wildlife and their habitats, and to assign full administrative and management authority to the **Minister of the Environment**.

- **Applicability**:  
  The regulation applies to specific geographic areas in **Ontario, Saskatchewan, and British Columbia**.

### **B. Saskatchewan**

#### **1. Stalwart National Wildlife Area**
- **Location**: Township 26, Range 25, West of the Second Meridian
- **Legal Description**:
  - Includes specific sections and legal subdivisions across multiple sections (e.g., sections 2, 3, 10, 11, 14, 15, 21, 22, 23, 26, 27, 28, 34)
  - Specific parcels defined by reference to **plans 71MJ07236 and 71MJ02135** in the Land Titles Office at Moose Jaw
  - Excludes areas covered by **Lake No. 2** (natural water boundaries)
  - Excludes **road rights-of-way** as shown on plans T.1133, S.3319, and 82MJ14723

- **Area**: Not explicitly stated, but extensive (multiple sections and subdivisions)

> *Purpose*: A large, diverse area supporting wetland, grassland, and forest habitats; likely critical for migratory birds, waterfowl, and native species.

> **Key Note**:  
> - All **mines, minerals, and mineral oils** within the area are **reserved to the Crown in right of Saskatchewan**, **except** for those located within the **most easterly 402.336 metres of the northeast quarter of section 4 in township 44, range 24** — these are **exempt**.

#### **3. Raven Island National Wildlife Area**
- **Location**: Township 40, Range 21, West of the Second Meridian
- **Legal Description**:
  - Legal subdivisions 3, 5, 6, and 11 of section 17
  - East half of subdivision 4
  - Parts of Raven Island in subdivisions 7, 10, and 15 (excluding areas covered by **Lenore Lake**)
  - East half of subdivision 12
  - East half and southwest quarter of subdivision 14

> **Key Note**:
