**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: By-Laws Nos. 6 and 8 of VIA Rail Canada Inc. (1978)**

This regulation, enacted by His Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of the Canadian Transport Commission under **Section 233 of the Railway Act**, establishes a comprehensive set of rules governing passenger conduct, safety, and operational discipline on VIA Rail Canada Inc. trains, stations, and related premises. It was adopted on **September 20, 1978**, and remains a foundational by-law for passenger behavior and service management.

---

### **1. Ticketing and Passenger Authentication Requirements**

- **Mandatory Ticketing**:  
  No individual may board any VIA Rail train without possessing a valid ticket issued by VIA Rail or by a company or person duly authorized by VIA Rail to sell tickets.

- **Ticket Presentation and Delivery**:  
  Every passenger must produce their ticket or ticket receipt when requested by the conductor or any authorized employee or agent. Upon request, passengers must either:
  - Deliver their ticket, or  
  - Pay the fare demanded for their journey.

- **Enforcement of Ticket Compliance**:  
  If a passenger refuses to pay the fare or produce a valid ticket, the conductor or authorized personnel may:
  - Expel the individual from the train and remove them with their baggage at any **usual stopping place**.
  - The train must first come to a stop, and **unnecessary force must not be used**.

- **Prohibition of Alteration or Forgery**:  
  No person may alter, deface, or attempt to use a ticket after it has been issued and while it is still valid and in use. This prevents fraud and ensures fare integrity.

---

### **2. Movement and Access Restrictions During Train Operation**

- **Prohibited Actions During Motion**:  
  No person—except a train employee or agent performing official duties—may:
  - Open a vestibule door,  
  - Stand on steps or platforms,  
  - Enter or exit (entrain/detrain) a car while it is in motion.

- **Unauthorized Access to Non-Passenger Cars**:  
  No person may travel or remain in a baggage car or any car not designed for passenger use without explicit authorization from a company officer or agent.

---

### **3. Passenger Conduct and General Behavior**

- **Seat and Space Usage**:  
  Passengers must not:
  - Occupy more than one seat space,  
  - Place feet on seats,  
  - Place baggage or personal items on seats in a way that excludes or inconveniences other passengers,  
  - Place unreasonably bulky or heavy items in parcel racks.

- **Health and Safety Restrictions**:  
  - No person suffering from or apparently suffering from an **infectious or contagious disease** may enter or remain on any VIA Rail train, station, or property without prior authorization from a duly authorized officer or agent.  
  - Custodians or caregivers of such individuals are prohibited from allowing them to enter or remain.

- **Intoxication and Unfit Conditions**:  
  No person in a state of **intoxication or otherwise unfit or improper condition** to be a passenger may enter or remain on VIA Rail premises.  
  Violators may be **immediately removed** from the premises in addition to facing other legal consequences.

---

### **4. Conduct and Public Order Prohibitions**

- **Prohibited Language and Behavior**:  
  No person may:
  - Use threatening, abusive, indecent, profane, or offensive language or gestures,  
  - Behave in an abusive, disorderly, indecent, or offensive manner,  
  - Commit a nuisance or interfere with the comfort or convenience of others.

- **Immediate Removal for Violations**:  
  Any person violating this rule may be **immediately removed** from the premises, in addition to any other liability.

---

### **5. Prohibitions on Items and Animals**

- **Prohibited Items**:  
  No person may bring into a car, train, station, or property:
  - Any item that may cause **inconvenience, annoyance, or damage** to others or to company property.  
  - Any **dog, bird, reptile, or other animal** without the **prior permission** of a VIA Rail employee or agent acting in the course of duty.

- **Prohibited Dangerous or Offensive Substances**:  
  No person may bring into any station or vehicle:
  - A **loaded firearm**,  
  - Any **inflammable, corrosive, offensive, explosive**, or otherwise dangerous substance or article.

---

### **6. Smoking and Fire Safety**

- **Smoking Prohibitions**:  
  Smoking is strictly prohibited in:
  - Any car or station,  
  - Any area not designated for smoking,  
  - Any part of the property where employees have requested that persons refrain from smoking.

- **Prohibition of Littering and Fire Hazards**:  
  No person may:
  - Throw or drop any litter or object from a train or car,  
  - Cause or allow anything to fall that may **ignite a fire or endanger persons or property**.

---

### **7. Graffiti and Property Damage**

- **Prohibited Acts**:  
  No person may:
  - Write obscene or offensive words,  
  - Make signs or pictures on walls or any part of a car, train, station, platform, or property,  
  - Commit a nuisance,  
  - Damage, mark, or deface any VIA Rail property or any part thereof.

---

### **8. Advertising and Solicitation**

- **Prohibited Commercial Activities**:  
  Except with the **prior permission** of a company officer or agent, no person may:
  - Advertise, sell, or expose goods,  
  - Distribute advertising or literature,  
  - Solicit business, trade, occupation, employment, or alms,  
  - Canvass or solicit donations or rewards.

- This rule ensures that commercial activity does not disrupt passenger comfort or create disorder.

---

### **9. Spitting Prohibitions**

- **Prohibited Act**:  
  No person may spit on:
  - The floor of a car or vehicle,  
  - The platform of a station,  
  - The floor or walls of any station room or passage.

---

### **10. Vehicle Parking Rules**

- **Prohibited Parking**:  
  The parking of **private or public vehicles** is banned on station or company premises **except at designated locations** established by a VIA Rail employee or agent acting in the course of duty.

- **Compliance with Parking Instructions**:  
  All vehicle operators must obey parking directions issued by company personnel.

---

### **11. Penalties and Enforcement Powers**

- **Penalty for Violations**:  
  Any violation of these by-laws is punishable by a **fine not exceeding $40** upon summary conviction.

- **Summary Intervention for Serious Violations**:  
  If a violation poses a **danger to the public or impedes the lawful operation of the railway**, VIA Rail may, under **Section 236 of the Railway Act**, **summarily intervene** and use **reasonable force** to:
  - Prevent the violation, or  
  - Enforce compliance.

- This power allows the company to act swiftly to maintain safety and order without being bound by traditional court processes.

---

### **Adoption and Authority**

- The by-laws were **passed and adopted** as an official regulation of **VIA Rail Canada Inc.** on **September 20, 1978**.  
- The regulation is issued under the authority of the **Railway Act**, specifically **Section 233** (which authorizes the Governor General in Council to sanction by-laws based on recommendations from the Canadian Transport Commission), and **Section 236** (which grants enforcement powers in emergencies or dangerous situations).

---

### **Key Objectives of the Regulation**

1. **Ensure Revenue Integrity** – Prevents fare evasion through strict ticketing and verification rules.  
2. **Maintain Public Safety** – Prohibits dangerous substances, intoxication, and infectious diseases from spreading.  
3. **Ensure Order and Decorum** – Prevents disorder, noise, and nuisance that could disrupt service.  
4. **Protect Property and Infrastructure** – Prevents damage from items, animals, or graffiti.  
5. **Enable Operational Efficiency** – Controls access, movement, and behavior to ensure smooth train operations.  
6. **Provide Legal Enforcement Tools** – Gives VIA Rail the authority to act swiftly and with reasonable force when necessary.

---

### **Relevance and Legacy**

Though issued in 1978, these by-laws reflect enduring principles of passenger conduct and railway safety. While modern versions of these rules may have been updated or revised in subsequent years (especially with changes in technology, public behavior, or health regulations), this 1978 regulation remains a foundational document in VIA Rail’s operational governance. It sets a precedent for how transportation companies balance passenger rights with the need for safety, order, and financial accountability.

---

**In Summary**:  
This regulation is a comprehensive code of conduct for all passengers and visitors to VIA Rail properties. It establishes clear expectations regarding ticketing, behavior, prohibited items, and safety, while empowering VIA Rail to enforce compliance through fines and, when necessary, the use of reasonable force. It remains a cornerstone of passenger regulation in Canadian rail transport.
