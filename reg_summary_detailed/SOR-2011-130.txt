**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

**Regulation Title:**  
Expiry of the Application of Section 12.1 of the Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act, 2006 Regulations

**Legal Authority:**  
The regulation is issued under the authority of **Section 102 of the Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act, 2006** (S.C. 2006, c. 13). It is made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of National Revenue**.

---

**Key Provisions:**

1. **Termination of Section 12.1**:  
   Section 12.1 of the *Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act, 2006* is **declared to cease to be in force** on **July 1, 2011**. This means that the specific provisions outlined in Section 12.1, which likely relate to export charge exemptions, conditions, or administrative requirements for softwood lumber products, are no longer applicable after that date.

2. **Effective Date of the Regulation**:  
   These regulations come into force on **July 1, 2011**. This date marks both the end of the applicability of Section 12.1 and the official commencement of the regulatory instrument that formalizes this expiry.

---

**Context and Purpose:**

- The *Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act, 2006* was enacted to manage and regulate export charges on softwood lumber products, primarily to address trade imbalances and protect domestic industries, particularly in the Canadian forestry sector.
- Section 12.1, which was likely designed to provide certain exemptions, transitional arrangements, or specific conditions for exporters (e.g., for certain types of lumber, shipments, or regions), was deemed to no longer be necessary or appropriate after a defined period.
- The expiry of this section reflects a policy decision to **phase out or sunset** a specific regulatory mechanism, possibly due to changes in trade dynamics, economic conditions, or the effectiveness of the original provisions.

---

**Implications:**

- After July 1, 2011, **no longer applies** to softwood lumber products exported under the Act.
- Exporters and importers are no longer subject to the obligations, conditions, or exemptions specified in Section 12.1.
- This change may have led to a simplification of the export charge regime, reducing administrative burdens or aligning the regulatory framework with updated trade policies.

---

**Legal Significance:**

- This regulation is a formal, legally binding amendment to the Act, ensuring clarity and continuity in the application of the law.
- It demonstrates the legislative process of periodic review and adjustment of trade regulations to respond to evolving economic and policy needs.

---

**Conclusion:**  
This regulation effectively **ends the legal application of Section 12.1** of the Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act, 2006, with full effect on **July 1, 2011**. It reflects a deliberate policy shift to modernize and streamline the export charge regime, removing outdated or no longer relevant provisions. The regulation is issued through a formal, constitutionally recognized process involving the Minister of National Revenue and the Governor General in Council, ensuring its legal validity and enforceability.
