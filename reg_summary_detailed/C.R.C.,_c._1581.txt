**Detailed Summary of the *Pensioners Training Regulations***

---

### **1. Title and Citation**
The regulations are officially titled the **Pensioners Training Regulations** and may be cited as such. These regulations establish the framework under which eligible pensioners (primarily veterans) may receive approved training to assist in their rehabilitation and reintegration into employment.

### **3. Eligibility for Training Approval**

A pensioner may be approved for a course of training **if**:

- The pensioner is receiving a disability pension due to **World War I, World War II**, or service in a **Special Duty Area**, **and**
- The pensioner is **unable to pursue any of their previous regular or secondary occupations** for a period of **one year or more**, due to **pensionable or non-pensionable disability** (or both), **or**
- The pensioner is **fit for one or more previous occupations**, but **placement is not feasible or will not be possible within a reasonable time**, **or**
- The pensioner is receiving a pension under **subsection 21(2) of the Pension Act**, and a full-time medical officer certifies that they are **unable to follow their regular or secondary occupation(s)** for one year or more due to **pensionable disability**.

### **5. Allowances for Training**

Allowances are paid to pensioners and dependants, subject to strict conditions:

#### **For Full-Time Training:**
- **Pensioner Allowance**:  
  Base allowance from the schedule **minus** any income from the training program that exceeds **$100 per month**.
- **Dependant Allowance**:  
  Total (pension + allowance) must **not exceed** the amount payable to dependants in the schedule.

#### **No Allowances for:**
- Home instruction
- Correspondence course training

### **6. Fees, Costs, and Charges Covered**

The Minister may pay the following costs **on behalf of the pensioner**, **only for full-time training**:

#### **University Training:**
- Tuition fees  
- University registration fees  
- Library fees  
- Instrument rental fees  
- Regular examination fees  
- "Student activity" fees (as specified in university calendars)

#### **Secondary or Vocational Training:**
- Tuition fees  
- Athletics and student activity fees  
- Prescribed textbooks (not included in fees)  
- Expendable materials supplied by the institution

#### **Correspondence or Home Instruction:**
- Only the **cost of prescribed textbooks**

### **7. Travel Expenses**

Pensioners may be reimbursed for travel related to training, depending on the situation:

#### **General Travel Allowance (for interviews or counselling):**
- Travel from residence to consultation and back (as per schedule rates)

#### **For Vocational Training in Canada:**
- One trip to the training location and one return trip (or alternative location deemed appropriate by the Minister for rehabilitation reasons)

#### **Travel Expense Components:**
| Component | Rate |
|--------|------|
| Railway transport (including sleeping berth) | Covered |
| Other modes of transport | Reasonable charges, supported by voucher |
| Private car transport | $0.045 per mile (regardless of passengers) |
| Meals (by train) | $2 per meal (if not provided by transport) |
| Meals (other than train) | $1.50 per meal (if not provided) |
| Board and lodging | $1.25 per meal (board), up to $5 per night (lodging, if supported by voucher) |

> âœ… Travel must be via the **most direct and appropriate route**.

---

### **8. Additional Living and Transportation Allowances**

- **Extra living expenses**:  
  A pensioner receiving dependant allowance who lives away from home during training may receive an **additional $65 per month** for living costs.

- **Daily transportation allowance**:  
  If daily travel to/from the training site is advantageous and the distance is significant, the pensioner may receive **daily transport at a total cost not exceeding $7.50 per week** (or actual cost, whichever is lower).

---

### **9. Compensation for Accidents or Industrial Diseases**

This section provides **significant protection and compensation rights** for pensioners and their dependants in cases of injury or illness arising from training.

#### **Eligibility for Compensation:**
A pensioner or their dependants are entitled to **compensation** (including medical, hospital, and other benefits) **at the same rate and under the same conditions** as **workmen employed by private persons** in the province where the training is taking place â€” **if**:

- The injury or illness results from an **accident** or **industrial disease** related to vocational training, and
- The pensioner is not injured or disabled due to their own wilful or intentional act.

#### **Definitions:**
- **Accident**: Includes a **fortuitous event** (natural or physical cause) or a **non-wilful act** by a third party; **excludes wilful or intentional acts** by the pensioner.
- **Industrial disease**: Any disease for which compensation is payable under provincial workmenâ€™s compensation laws.

#### **Determining Compensation:**
- The compensation is determined by the **same board, officers, or authority** that administers workmenâ€™s compensation in the province.
- The Minister, with **Governor in Council approval**, may determine the **monthly wage** of the pensioner for compensation purposes.

#### **Claim Against Third Parties:**
- If a pensioner or dependant can sue a third party (e.g., employer, contractor) for injury or illness, they may claim compensation from that party.
- If the recovery is **less than the amount due under these regulations**, the **difference is paid directly** to the pensioner or dependant.

#### **Subrogation Rights:**
- If compensation is recovered from a third party, **Her Majesty (the Crown)** is **subrogated** to the rights of the pensioner or dependant.
- The Crown may **maintain a legal action** in the name of Her Majesty and any recovery is paid into the **Consolidated Revenue Fund**.

#### **Excess Recovery:**
- If the recovery **exceeds** the amount due under these regulations, the pensioner or dependant may receive a **portion of the excess**, subject to Ministerial approval (with Treasury Board consent).
