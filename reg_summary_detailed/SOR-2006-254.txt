**Detailed Summary of the *First Nations Oil and Gas and Moneys Management Voting Regulations* (S.C. 2005, c. 48)**

---

### **Overview**

These regulations, made under **paragraph 62(a) of the First Nations Oil and Gas and Moneys Management Act (FNOGMMA)**, establish a comprehensive and transparent framework for how First Nations can conduct votes on critical matters related to oil and gas management and financial governance. The regulations ensure that voting is conducted fairly, impartially, and in accordance with legal and procedural standards, with a strong emphasis on **voter awareness, accessibility, and accountability**.

The regulations apply specifically to votes held under **sections 17 or 18** of the FNOGMMA, which pertain to:
- Ratification of oil and gas codes and financial codes;
- Approval of transfers of oil and gas exploration and exploitation management to a First Nation;
- Approval of payments of moneys to a First Nation based on a financial code.

## **Scope and Application**

These regulations apply to **all votes conducted under sections 17 or 18 of the FNOGMMA**, including:
- Ratification of oil and gas and financial codes;
- Approval of management transfer of oil and gas activities;
- Approval of financial payments to the First Nation.

---

## **Voting Process: Step-by-Step Summary**

### **1. Designation of Officers**
- The **First Nation council** must:
  - Designate an **electoral officer** (under paragraph 3(1)(a)) and
  - Forward a **written resolution to the Minister** confirming:
    - Intent to hold the vote
    - Name of the electoral officer
- The **electoral officer** must designate **one or more deputy electoral officers**.

> These officers must:
> - Supervise the vote fairly and impartially
> - Perform necessary administrative duties
> - Act with integrity and transparency

---

### **2. Question to Be Voted On**

The question presented to voters depends on the nature of the vote:

| Vote Type | Question in Schedule |
|---------|------------------------|
| **Section 10 Vote** | Ratification of oil and gas code & financial code; approval of transfer of oil and gas management |
| **Section 11 Vote** | Ratification of financial code; approval of payment of moneys |
| **Combined Vote** | All three elements above — oil/gas code, financial code, transfer, and payments |

> The exact wording is detailed in **Parts 1, 2, and 3 of the Schedule**.

---

### **3. Voter List Preparation**

At least **49 days before the vote**, the First Nation must provide the electoral officer with a **complete and accurate list of eligible voters**.

#### **Who provides the list?**
- If the First Nation has **assumed control of membership** (under section 10 of the *Indian Act*): provided by the First Nation
- If the Band List is maintained by **Department of Indigenous Services (DIS)**: provided by the Registrar

#### **What must the list include?**
- Full names of eligible voters in **alphabetical order**
- First Nation membership number OR registry number
- If no membership/registry number: **date of birth**

#### **Verification & Corrections**
- On request, the electoral officer may verify whether a name is on the list.
- The list must be **revised** if:
  - An eligible voter is **omitted**
  - A name is **incorrectly listed**
  - An **unqualified person** is included

> To prove eligibility:
> - Present evidence (from Registrar or First Nation) that the person is on the Band List and meets the age requirement
> - Present evidence that the person is **not on the Band List** or has not reached voting age

---

### **4. Addresses for Off-Reserve Voters**

At least **49 days before the vote**, the electoral officer must receive the **latest known addresses** of eligible voters who **do not reside on the reserve**.

> This ensures that off-reserve voters can receive mail-in ballots and notices.

---

### **5. Public Notice and Voter Communication**

Between **14 and 42 days before the vote**, the electoral officer or deputy must:

#### **Post on Reserve (in at least one conspicuous place):**
- Notice of the vote
- List of eligible voters

#### **Mail or deliver to off-reserve eligible voters:**
- Notice of the vote
- **Mail-in ballot** (initialled by electoral officer or deputy)
- **Outer, postage-paid envelope** (pre-addressed to the electoral officer)
- **Inner envelope** marked “Ballot” for ballot insertion
- **Voter Declaration Form**
- **Letter of instruction** on how to vote by mail-in ballot
- **Information package** about the vote

> If an eligible voter requests these materials at least **7 days before the vote**, they must be provided.

#### **Recordkeeping**
- The electoral officer must **record**:
  - Date and address to which each mail-in ballot was sent
  - Mark the voters list to indicate that a ballot was provided

> **Important**: An eligible voter who receives a mail-in ballot **cannot vote in person** at a polling station unless they return the ballot or provide proof of loss.

---

### **6. Information Meeting**

A mandatory **information meeting** must be held **before the vote**.

#### **Requirements:**
- Must be held **on or before the vote date**
- Must include:
  - **Legal adviser** (qualified under provincial law)
  - **Financial adviser** (qualified in financial matters)
  - **Interpreter** (to assist non-English or non-French speakers)

#### **Advisers must provide:**
- Overview of the **voting process and procedures**
- **Legal and financial consequences** of:
  - Ratifying oil and gas or financial codes
  - Approving transfer of oil and gas management
  - Approving payments of moneys
- Clear explanation of the **question being voted on**
- Explanation of **positive vs. negative outcomes**

#### **Advisers must:**
- Answer questions from eligible voters
- Provide clarity on all relevant topics

#### **Post-Meeting Reporting to the Minister**
The council must send to the Minister, **before the vote**, the following:
- Time and location of the meeting
- Names and addresses of legal and financial advisers
- **Signed confirmation** from both advisers stating they advised eligible voters on the required topics

---

### **7. Ballot Preparation and Distribution**

The electoral officer must:
- Prepare **sufficient ballots** with the correct question
- Ensure the **electoral officer or deputy’s initials** are on the back of each ballot
- Procure **enough ballot boxes**
- Deliver ballots and **lead pencils** to polling stations **before polls open**

---

### **8. Voting by Mail-in Ballot**

An eligible voter may vote by mail-in ballot by:

1. **Marking the ballot** with a cross, check mark, or other clear indication of their choice
2. **Folding the ballot** to conceal the question and marks but expose the initials
3. **Placing it in the inner envelope** and sealing it
4. **Completing and signing the voter declaration form** in the presence of a **witness aged 18 or over**
5. **Placing the inner envelope and signed form into the outer envelope**
6. **Delivering or mailing the outer envelope** to the electoral officer **before polls close**

> If unable to mark or sign the ballot independently, the voter may **enlist another person** to:
> - Mark the ballot according to their instructions
> - Complete and sign the declaration form

> **Witness must attest to:**
> - That the person signing is the one named on the form
> - (If assistance was used) That the voter is the one named and that the ballot was marked as directed

> - Voter may obtain a replacement by returning the spoiled ballot to the electoral officer
