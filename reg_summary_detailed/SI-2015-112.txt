**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

This regulatory instrument, issued under the authority of the Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Prime Minister), establishes and modifies the designated **Ministers responsible** for various federal Acts and statutory provisions. It reflects the allocation of ministerial responsibilities under specific legislation, ensuring that designated Ministers have legal authority to act on behalf of the Government of Canada in the administration and enforcement of these laws. The regulation is effective **November 4, 2015**, and applies to a range of federal statutes across sectors including trade, heritage, finance, industry, energy, and cultural institutions.

---

### 1. **Agreement on Internal Trade Implementation Act (S.C. 1996, c. 17)**
- **Provision**: The Minister of Industry is designated as the **Minister for the purposes of any provision** of the *Agreement on Internal Trade Implementation Act*.
- **Rationale**: This designation ensures that the Minister of Industry has the authority to oversee and implement the provisions of the Agreement on Internal Trade, which aims to reduce trade barriers and promote fair and efficient internal market operations across Canada.
- **Legal Basis**: Section 8 of the Act.
- **Significance**: Assigns a clear point of accountability and administrative responsibility for trade-related policy implementation.

---

### 2. **Canada Travelling Exhibitions Indemnification Act (S.C. 1999, c. 29)**
- **Provision**: The Minister of Canadian Heritage is designated as the **Minister for the purposes of this Act**.
- **Rationale**: This Act provides indemnification to organizations that host travelling exhibitions in Canada. The Minister of Canadian Heritage is responsible for overseeing cultural programming and ensuring that such exhibitions operate within federal policy frameworks.
- **Legal Basis**: Definition of "Minister" in section 2 of the Act.
- **Significance**: Ensures that the Minister of Canadian Heritage is the point of contact for claims, approvals, and financial support related to travelling exhibitions.

---

### 3. **Financial Administration Act (R.S., c. F-11)**
- **Provision**: Paragraphs (b) of **Order in Council P.C. 1993-1450 (SI/93-104)** and **P.C. 1993-1982 (SOR/93-536)** are **repealed**.
- **Legal Basis**: 
  - Paragraph (d) of the definition "appropriate Minister" in section 2 of the *Financial Administration Act*.
  - Subparagraph (a)(ii) of the definition "appropriate Minister" in subsection 83(1) of the same Act.
- **Rationale**: The repeal removes outdated or obsolete provisions that previously assigned ministerial responsibilities in financial administration. The modern definitions now allow flexibility in designating the appropriate Minister based on the nature of the financial activity or function.
- **Significance**: Streamlines and modernizes ministerial responsibilities in financial governance, aligning with current administrative practices and accountability structures.

---

### 4. **Industrial Design Act (R.S., c. 10 (4th Supp.), s. 20(2); R.S., c. I-9)**
- **Provision**: The Minister of Industry is designated as the **Minister for the purposes of the Industrial Design Act**.
- **Legal Basis**: Definition of "Minister" in section 2 of the Act.
- **Rationale**: The Act governs the registration, protection, and enforcement of industrial designs. Assigning this responsibility to the Minister of Industry ensures coordination with industrial policy and innovation strategies.
- **Significance**: Provides a clear administrative link between design protection and industrial development policy.

---

### 5. **Mackenzie Gas Project Impacts Fund Act (S.C. 2013, c. 40, s. 282)**
- **Provision**: The **Minister of the Canadian Northern Economic Development Agency (CNEDA)** is designated as the **Minister for the purposes of this Act**.
- **Rationale**: The Mackenzie Gas Project Impacts Fund is designed to compensate Indigenous communities and stakeholders affected by the development of natural gas projects in northern Canada. Assigning responsibility to the CNEDA Minister ensures that Indigenous and regional economic interests are properly represented and addressed.
- **Legal Basis**: Section 3 of the Act.
- **Significance**: Reflects a commitment to regional economic equity and Indigenous engagement in resource development.

---

### 6. **Museums Act (S.C. 2014, c. 20, s. 194; S.C. 1990, c. 3)**
- **Provision**: The Minister of Canadian Heritage is designated as the **Minister responsible for the following institutions**:
  - National Gallery of Canada  
  - Canadian Museum of History  
  - Canadian Museum of Nature  
  - National Museum of Science and Technology  
  - Canadian Museum for Human Rights  
  - Canadian Museum of Immigration at Pier 21  
- **Legal Basis**: Definition of "Minister" in section 2 of the *Museums Act*.
- **Rationale**: This designation ensures centralized oversight and policy direction for major national museums, aligning their operations with national cultural priorities and heritage objectives.
- **Significance**: Strengthens the role of the Minister of Canadian Heritage in managing and supporting Canada’s cultural institutions, including those focused on Indigenous history, science, human rights, and immigration.

---

### 7. **Nuclear Liability and Compensation Act (S.C. 2015, c. 4, s. 120)**
- **Provision**: The **Minister of Natural Resources** is designated as the **Minister referred to in the Act**.
- **Rationale**: This Act governs liability and compensation for nuclear incidents, including those involving nuclear facilities and materials. The Minister of Natural Resources is responsible for overseeing nuclear safety, regulation, and emergency response.
- **Legal Basis**: Section 4 of the Act.
- **Significance**: Assigns clear accountability to the Minister of Natural Resources for managing nuclear liability matters, ensuring coordination with the Canadian Nuclear Safety Commission and other relevant bodies.

### **Overall Summary and Key Themes**

| Financial Administration | Repealed outdated provisions | Modernize ministerial designations |
| Industrial Design | Minister of Industry | Protect and promote industrial design rights |
| Northern Economic Development | Minister of CNEDA | Manage impacts and compensation from gas projects |

---

### **Effective Date**
- The Order becomes effective on **November 4, 2015**.

---

### **Purpose and Significance**
This regulation consolidates and clarifies ministerial responsibilities across key federal legislation. It:
- Ensures **clarity and accountability** in governance.
- Reflects **sector-specific priorities** (e.g., trade, heritage, energy, Indigenous rights).
- Aligns with **modern administrative practices** by repealing outdated designations.
- Supports **policy coherence** and interdepartmental coordination.

It is a foundational tool in Canada’s administrative framework, enabling the government to operate efficiently and responsively across diverse policy domains.

---

**Note**: All designations are made under the authority of the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, and are consistent with the constitutional and statutory framework of the Canadian government. The Minister of Canadian Heritage is prominently featured due to the cultural and heritage-related mandates, while the Minister of Industry and Natural Resources are central to economic and energy policy.
