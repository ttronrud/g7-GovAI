**Detailed Summary of the *Notices of Uninsured Deposits Regulations (Trust and Loan Companies)***

---

### **Regulatory Background and Authority**

The *Notices of Uninsured Deposits Regulations (Trust and Loan Companies)* were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, under **subsection 413.1(3)a of the Trust and Loan Companies Act**. This provision grants the government the authority to establish regulations that ensure transparency regarding the insurance status of deposits held with trust and loan companies.

These regulations are part of a broader legislative framework designed to protect depositors and ensure informed decision-making, particularly in cases where financial institutions are **not insured** by the **Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC)**.

---

### **Legal Basis and Statutory Reference**

- **Primary Statute**: *Trust and Loan Companies Act*  
- **Relevant Sections**:
  - **Section 413.1(1)**: Requires that a notice informing customers that deposits are not insured by CDIC must be provided to individuals opening accounts.
  - **Section 413.1(2)(a)**: Requires that a notice be posted in physical branches.
  - **Section 413.1(2)(b)**: Requires that a similar notice appear in advertisements promoting deposit facilities.
- **Amending Legislation**:  
  - *S.C. 2007, c. 6, s. 358* (Statutes of Canada, 2007, Chapter 6)  
  - *S.C. 1991, c. 45* (original foundation of the Act)

---

### **Key Provisions of the Regulations**

#### **1. Delivery of the Notice to Account Openers**

- The notice informing customers that deposits are **not insured by CDIC** must be:
  - Provided **either as part of the account agreement** or as a **separate document**.
- If the company shares premises with a **member institution** of the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (under **section 2 of the CDIC Act**), the notice must include an **additional statement** clarifying:
  - The company’s **business is separate and distinct** from that of the member institution.
- In such shared premises scenarios, the company must:
  - **Verbally explain** all information contained in the notice to the person opening the account.
  - Obtain a **signed declaration** from the customer confirming that:
    1. The person has **received and read** the notice;
    2. The company has **verbally explained** the information;
    3. The person **understands** that deposits with the company are **not insured** by CDIC.

> **Purpose**: To prevent confusion between a trust/loan company and a CDIC-insured institution, especially in shared office spaces, and to ensure informed consent.

---

#### **2. Posting of the Notice in Branches**

- A notice must be **prominently displayed** in the public area of each branch.
- **Physical dimensions**:
  - **Height**: 27.94 cm  
  - **Width**: 43.18 cm  
- **Typography requirements**:
  - **Heading**: 120-point font size
  - **Body text**: 50-point font size
- The notice must be clearly visible and easily readable to the general public.

> **Purpose**: To ensure that potential customers can immediately recognize that their deposits are not covered by CDIC insurance upon visiting a branch.

---

#### **3. Posting of the Notice on Websites**

- The notice must be **prominently displayed** on the company’s website.
- It must be **clearly visible** to users accessing the site.
- While no specific font or size is mandated for websites, the notice must be **easily accessible and legible**.

> **Purpose**: To ensure digital customers are equally informed about the lack of CDIC insurance, especially given the increasing use of online banking and deposit services.

---

#### **4. Display in Advertisements**

- Any advertisement that promotes deposit facilities or solicits deposits must include a notice in the form specified in **subsection 2(1)** of the regulations.
- The notice must appear **prominently** in such advertisements.

> **Purpose**: To prevent misleading claims about insurance coverage and to ensure transparency when marketing deposit products.

---

### **Effective Date and Registration**

- The regulations come into force on the day that **section 358 of S.C. 2007, c. 6** (the amending legislation) comes into force.
- If the regulations are **registered after that date**, they will take effect on the **date of registration**.

> **Note**: These regulations were in force as of **March 8, 2008**, as indicated by SI/2008-33.

---

### **Purpose and Significance**

This regulation serves several critical functions:

1. **Consumer Protection**: Ensures that depositors are fully informed about the lack of insurance coverage for their funds in trust and loan companies.
2. **Transparency**: Prevents misleading or ambiguous information about insurance status, especially in shared premises or marketing materials.
3. **Risk Awareness**: Helps customers understand the financial risks associated with non-CDIC-insured institutions.
4. **Regulatory Compliance**: Provides a standardized, enforceable format for notices to ensure consistency across all trust and loan companies.

---

### **Key Takeaways for Financial Institutions**

- **Mandatory Notices**: All trust and loan companies must provide, post, and advertise a clear, standardized notice about uninsured deposits.
- **Shared Premises**: Special attention required to distinguish operations from CDIC-insured institutions.
- **Verbal Disclosure & Signature**: Required in shared premises scenarios to confirm customer understanding.
- **Design Standards**: Strict formatting requirements (size, font) ensure visibility and readability.
- **Digital Presence**: Websites must not only include the notice but ensure it is clearly visible and accessible.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Notices of Uninsured Deposits Regulations (Trust and Loan Companies)* are a critical component of financial consumer protection in Canada. By mandating clear, visible, and comprehensible disclosures about the uninsured nature of deposits, the regulations empower individuals to make informed financial decisions and reduce the risk of unexpected loss due to the failure of non-insured institutions. The rules emphasize both physical and digital transparency, with special provisions to address potential confusion in shared office environments.
