**Detailed Summary of the Veterans Burial Regulations, 2005**

---

### **Overview**

The *Veterans Burial Regulations, 2005* are a set of rules established under the **Department of Veterans Affairs Act**, specifically pursuant to **section 5a**, and issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Veterans Affairs**. These regulations govern the provision of **financial assistance and gravemarker installation** for eligible deceased veterans, pensioners, civilians, and other individuals who served in military or war-related capacities.

The regulations aim to ensure that eligible individuals receive **adequate support** for the final expenses associated with their deathâ€”including last sickness, funeral, burial, cremation, and gravemarker costsâ€”particularly when such costs exceed the financial capacity of the deceasedâ€™s estate or family.

## **Financial Assistance for Final Expenses**

### **Eligibility Criteria**

Financial assistance is available for the following deceased individuals:

#### **1. In Canada or Abroad (Conditions Apply):**
- A **pensioner** who dies in Canada or abroad.
- A **veteran**, **income-qualified civilian**, or **overseas service civilian** who dies in Canada.
- A **veteran buried or cremated in Canada**.
- A **veteran** who dies outside Canada and was undergoing a required medical examination at the time of death.
- An **overseas service civilian** who dies outside Canada.
- A **veteran or civilian** who dies outside Canada and was undergoing a medical examination required by the Minister or Veterans Review and Appeal Board.

#### **2. Specific Cases Based on Service-Related Conditions:**
- A pensioner whose death results from an injury, disease, or aggravation of a condition incurred during service.
- A person receiving treatment under the *Veterans Health Care Regulations* for acute care related to a pensioned condition.
- A person receiving treatment for a condition where it is uncertain whether it is service-related.
- A person who dies from a service-related or non-service-related injury or disease aggravated by service (as per the *Veterans Well-being Act*).

### **Types of Costs Covered**

The regulations cover the following expenses:

1. **Medical costs of last sickness**  
   - Costs not paid by the Minister and not covered by private or provincial health plans.

2. **Funeral services**  
   - Preparation of remains  
   - Provision of a casket (meeting Ministerial standards) or urn  
   - Ceremonial services  
   - Death notices  
   - Transportation of remains from place of death to funeral home and burial/cremation site  
   - Transportation requiring two funeral homes (due to geographic separation)  
   - Special preparation for viewing (if needed)  
   - Single grave and reasonable opening/closing costs  
   - Perpetual care of the grave  
   - Cost of a **grave liner** (decomposable or non-decomposable), if required by cemetery authorities  

3. **Cremation costs**  
   - Full cost of cremation is covered.

The total financial assistance is the **least of** the following:

| Special preparation of remains | $210 |
| Decomposable grave liner | $200 |
| Non-decomposable grave liner | $570 |
| Cremation costs (if applicable) | Included in the $7,376 cap |

---

### **Conditions for Payment**

- **No duplicate payments**: Financial assistance is not payable if the Government of Canada has already provided similar support for the same deceased person.
- **Application timeline**: An application must be submitted **within one year** of the remains becoming available for interment.
- **Suspension of applications**: If the eligibility for a disability award, pain and suffering compensation, or a pension is under review, the application is **suspended** until a decision is made.
- **Automatic favorable treatment**: If a person is later determined to be entitled to a disability award, pain and suffering compensation, or a pension, the financial assistance application is treated as if the initial eligibility determination was favorable.

## **Determination of Insufficiency of Funds**

A determination of whether funds are insufficient is made by the **Corporation (Last Post Fund)** using a formula based on the **net assets of the survivor and succession**.

### **Deductible Amounts from Net Assets**

If the deceased has a **survivor or dependent child**, the following are deducted from the sum of net assets:

- **$35,279** for the survivor (or dependent child if no survivor)
- **$700 per additional dependent child**
- Any amount already paid for burial or funeral costs (not previously considered in net asset calculation)
- The **value of a gravemarker** (if not provided under section 5):
  - Lesser of actual cost or value of marker that would have been provided
  - Minus any prior payments
- A **reasonable amount for perpetual care** of the gravemarker (if not already accounted for)
- The deceasedâ€™s **monthly income before death**
- Market value of:
  - A motor vehicle used by the family
  - The familyâ€™s residence
  - Household effects

> âŒ If there is **no survivor or dependent child**, the above deductions do **not apply**. Instead, the **net assets of the succession as a whole** are considered.

### **Insufficiency Determination**

- If the calculated amount after deductions is **nil or negative**, then there is an **insufficiency of funds**â€”and financial assistance is payable.
