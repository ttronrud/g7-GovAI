### **Detailed Summary of the Export Permits Regulations (Softwood Lumber Products 2006)**

---

#### **1. Legal Authority and Purpose**
The **Export Permits Regulations (Softwood Lumber Products 2006)** were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**, under two key pieces of legislation:

- **Section 12a of the Export and Import Permits Act (EIPA)** – which empowers the government to establish rules for issuing export permits.
- **Section 108 of the Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act, 2006b** – which specifically authorizes regulations governing the export of softwood lumber products, including requirements for permits, documentation, and compliance.

**Purpose**:  
These regulations establish the detailed procedures, definitions, and documentation requirements for exporting **softwood lumber products** to the **United States**, with a focus on ensuring traceability, compliance, and transparency in the supply chain.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Export and Import Permits Act* (EIPA), the primary legislation governing export controls. |
| **Board Foot** | Defined as per *Section 2 of the Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act, 2006*. A unit of volume used to measure lumber (1 board foot = 1 inch thick × 12 inches wide × 12 inches long). |
| **Date of Shipment** | The time when the softwood lumber product is considered to have been exported, as defined in *Section 5 of the Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act, 2006*. This is critical for determining export charge applicability and timing. |
| **Permit** | An official export permit issued under the EIPA for the export of softwood lumber products. |
| **Region** | Includes the following Canadian provinces and territories: <br> - Nova Scotia <br> - New Brunswick <br> - Prince Edward Island <br> - Newfoundland and Labrador <br> - Yukon <br> - Northwest Territories <br> - Nunavut <br> Also includes any region defined in subsection 6.3(1) of the Act. |
| **Region of First Primary Processing** | The region where the softwood lumber undergoes its first "primary processing" (e.g., sawing, planing, or cutting into usable lumber), as defined in Section 2 of the Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act. |
| **Softwood Lumber Products** | Goods listed under **Item 5104 of Group 5** in the *Schedule to the Export Control List*. These include various types of sawn softwood lumber, such as boards, planks, and dimensional lumber. |

---

#### **3. Application Requirements for Export Permits to the United States**

Any exporter (or their agent/mandatary) seeking to export softwood lumber products to the **United States** must submit a complete and accurate application to the Minister, including a statement that all information provided is **true, correct, and complete**.

**Required Information in the Application**:

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Exporter & Agent Details** | Name and address of the exporter; if applicable, name and address of the agent or mandatary. |
| **Exporter Identifier Number** | A unique number assigned by the Minister to identify the exporter. |
| **Official Language** | The language chosen for official communications with the exporter (e.g., English or French). |
| **Residency Status** | Whether the exporter and/or agent/mandatary are residents of Canada. |
| **Product Description** | A detailed description of the softwood lumber products being exported. |
| **Tariff Classification** | The specific tariff classification under the *Customs Tariff*, which determines duties and regulatory treatment. |
| **Quantity in Board Feet** | Total volume of the product expressed in board feet. |
| **Region of First Primary Processing** | Name of the region where the initial processing occurred. |
| **Mill Business Number** | The official business number assigned by the **Minister of National Revenue** for the mill that performed the first primary processing. |
| **Export Price** | The price of the products in either **Canadian dollars (CAD)** or **United States dollars (USD)**. |
| **Date of Shipment** | The date the product is considered exported (as defined in the Act). |
| **Mode of Transportation** | How the product will be transported (e.g., sea, air, truck). |
| **Port of Entry in the U.S.** | The U.S. port where the product will enter. |
| **Date of Entry into the U.S.** | The actual date the product arrives and is recorded in U.S. customs. |
| **Importer/Consignee Details** | Name and address of the U.S. importer or consignee. |

---

#### **4. Special Requirements for Maritime Provinces**

If the softwood lumber products originate from one of the following provinces:

- Nova Scotia  
- New Brunswick  
- Prince Edward Island  
- Newfoundland and Labrador  

Then the application **must include** the **Maritime Lumber Bureau Certificate of Origin** number for those products.

> **Purpose**: This ensures origin verification and supports trade compliance, particularly with U.S. import requirements related to regional sourcing and product authenticity.

---

#### **5. Requirements for Non-Canadian Exporters**

If the **exporter is not a resident of Canada**, the application **must be submitted by a Canadian resident agent or mandatary**.

> **Rationale**: Ensures accountability, facilitates oversight, and allows Canadian authorities to verify compliance with export controls and regulations.

---

#### **6. Special Provisions for "Single Family Home Package or Kit" Exclusions**

There is a specific exemption for **single family home packages or kits** that are **excluded from Item 5104 of the Export Control List** (i.e., not subject to the standard export permit requirements).

**Requirement for Retention and Disclosure**:

Any person exporting such a package or kit must **retain and provide upon request** the following documents to **U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP)**:

1. **Copy of Home Design Plan or Blueprint**  
   - Must match the customs entry in the United States.  
   - Proves that the product corresponds to the design specified in the package.

2. **Purchase Contract**  
   - Must be signed by a **customer who is not affiliated** with the exporter or importer.  
   - Ensures that the product is being sold to a genuine end-user, not resold or diverted.

3. **Inventory Listing**  
   - A complete list of all parts included in the package or kit that enters the U.S.  
   - Must conform to the original home design package.

4. **Additional Listing (for Multiple Shipments)**  
   - If multiple shipments are made under the same contract, a separate listing must identify **which items are included in the specific shipment under review**.

> **Purpose**: Prevents fraud, ensures product authenticity, and supports U.S. customs enforcement of legitimate home-building product trade.

---

#### **7. Repeal Clause**

The phrase "[Repeal]" appears in the regulation, indicating that this provision was intended to **replace or cancel a prior regulation**. However, no specific prior regulation is named. This likely means that the current regulations **supersede older rules** related to softwood lumber exports.

---

#### **8. Effective Date**

These regulations came into force on **October 12, 2006**.

> This date marks the start of enforcement for all requirements outlined above.

---

### **Key Objectives and Implications**

| Objective | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Traceability** | By requiring detailed information on origin, processing location, mill number, and shipment details, the regulations ensure that every export can be traced back to its source. |
| **Compliance & Accountability** | The requirement for truthful statements and documentation helps prevent illegal or unauthorized exports. |
| **Trade Facilitation** | Clear, standardized requirements make it easier for Canadian exporters to comply with U.S. customs and trade laws. |
| **Anti-Fraud Measures** | The special rules for home kits prevent misuse of export controls and ensure that these products are genuinely being sold to end consumers. |
| **Regional Protection** | The emphasis on regional processing and origin supports Canada’s economic interests in the Maritime provinces by reinforcing their role in the domestic lumber industry. |

---

### **Conclusion**

The **Export Permits Regulations (Softwood Lumber Products 2006)** provide a comprehensive, legally binding framework for the export of softwood lumber products to the United States. They balance **regulatory oversight** with **practical trade needs**, ensuring that:

- Exports are properly documented,
- Origin and processing are verifiable,
- Fraud and misuse are minimized,
- Canadian exporters (especially in the Maritime provinces) are supported,
- And U.S. customs authorities can verify product authenticity and compliance.

These regulations remain a cornerstone of Canada’s export control system for softwood lumber and continue to play a vital role in managing trade flows between Canada and the United States.
