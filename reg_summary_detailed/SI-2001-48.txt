## üîç **Comprehensive Summary of the Canada-Austria Supplementary Convention (1999)**

### üìå **1. Background and Purpose**
This supplementary convention amends and expands the original **Convention between Canada and the Republic of Austria for the Avoidance of Double Taxation and the Prevention of Fiscal Evasion with Respect to Taxes on Income and on Capital**, signed in Vienna on December 9, 1976.

- **Objective**: To eliminate double taxation and prevent tax evasion by clarifying tax treatment of key types of income (dividends, interest, royalties, capital gains) and establishing rules for tax withholding, information exchange, and enforcement.
- **Scope**: Applies to individuals and companies resident in either Canada or Austria, particularly in cross-border situations involving income derived from or paid in one country to a resident of the other.
- **Entry into Force**:
  - **Ratified**: June 15, 1999 (Vienna)
  - **Entered into Force**: 60 days after ratification (i.e., August 15, 1999)
  - **Effective Dates**:
    - For **tax withheld at source** on payments to non-residents: from the **first day of the second month after entry into force** (September 1, 1999)
    - For **all other taxes**: for **taxation years beginning on or after September 1, 1999**

### üìå **2. Key Amendments and New Provisions**

#### ‚úÖ **Article 10 ‚Äì Dividends**
- **Original Rule**: Dividends were generally taxed in the country of the paying company.
- **New Rule**:
  - Dividends may be taxed in the **residence country of the company paying them**, but **if the beneficial owner is a resident of the other country**, the tax imposed **shall not exceed**:
    - **5%** of the gross amount of dividends **if**:
      - The beneficial owner is a company that controls **directly or indirectly at least 10% of the voting power** in the dividend-paying company.
      - The dividend-paying company is a **non-resident-owned investment corporation resident in Canada**.
    - **15%** of the gross amount in **all other cases**.
  - **Exception**: The provisions do **not affect the taxation of the company** on its profits.

> üîç **Impact**: Limits tax on foreign dividends to a cap (5% or 15%), protecting residents from excessive taxation, especially for passive investments.

#### ‚úÖ **Article 10 ‚Äì Additional Tax on Earnings from Permanent Establishments or Immovable Property**
- **New Provision**:
  - A Contracting State may impose **additional tax** on:
    - Earnings from permanent establishments (e.g., branches or offices)
    - Alienation (sale) of immovable property in that State by a company carrying on trade in real estate
  - However, the **additional tax rate must not exceed**:
    - The percentage limitation under **paragraph 2 of Article 10**, applied to earnings **not previously taxed** in prior years.
  - **Deductions allowed** from taxable earnings:
    - Business losses (including from property alienation)
    - Taxes already paid in the country
    - Profits reinvested in the country (computed per domestic law)
    - A **maximum deduction of $500,000 CAD or its Austrian equivalent**, less any amount deducted:
      - By the company
      - By a related person engaged in a similar business

> üîç **Impact**: Prevents excessive taxation on foreign profits and introduces a floor for deductions, especially for real estate or business operations.

#### ‚úÖ **Article 11 ‚Äì Interest**
- **Original Rule**: Interest was generally taxable in the country where it arose.
- **New Rule**:
  - Interest may be taxed in the country where it arises, but **if the beneficial owner is a resident of the other country**, the tax **shall not exceed 10%** of the gross amount.

> üîç **Impact**: Caps interest taxation at 10%, protecting residents from undue tax burdens on interest income.

#### ‚úÖ **New Subparagraphs (e) and (f) ‚Äì Interest in Pension/Employee Benefit Plans**
- **Subparagraph (e)**:
  - Interest arising in a Contracting State and paid to a resident of the other country **who is a beneficiary of a pension, retirement, or employee benefits plan** shall **not be taxable in the first-mentioned State**, provided that:
    - The recipient is the **beneficial owner** and is generally **exempt from tax** in the other country
    - The interest is **not derived from trade, business, or related persons**

- **Subparagraph (f)**:
  - Interest shall be taxable **only in the other Contracting State** if:
    - The recipient is an **enterprise of the other State**
    - The interest is paid on **indebtedness arising from the credit sale** of merchandise or industrial/commercial/scientific equipment to an enterprise in the first State
    - **Exception**: Not applicable if the sale or debt is between **related persons**

> üîç **Impact**: Provides tax relief for pension income and limits interest taxation in specific trade-related scenarios, promoting fairness in employee benefit plans.

#### ‚úÖ **Article 12 ‚Äì Royalties**
- **Original Rule**: Royalties were generally taxable in the country where they arose.
- **New Rule**:
  - Royalties may be taxed in the country where they arise, but **if the beneficial owner is a resident of the other country**, the tax **shall not exceed 10%** of the gross amount.

- **New Exclusion Clause**:
  - **Copyright royalties and similar payments** (excluding motion pictures, films, or TV-related content) and **royalties for use of software, patents, or industrial/commercial/scientific information** (excluding rental or franchise-related info) arising in one country and paid to a resident of the other shall be **taxable only in the other country**.

> üîç **Impact**: Ensures that intellectual property and copyright income are taxed only once, in the country of the resident beneficiary, protecting creators and innovators.

#### ‚úÖ **Article 13 ‚Äì Capital Gains on Alienation of Property**
- **New Rule**:
  - Gains from the alienation (sale) of:
    - **Shares** in a company whose value is derived **principally from immovable property in the other country**
    - A **substantial interest in a partnership or trust** whose value is derived **principally from immovable property in the other country**

> üîç **Clarification**: "Immovable property" includes shares in such companies or interests in partnerships/trusts, but **excludes business property** used in operations.

> üö´ **Exclusion**: Does **not** cover property used in the business of the company/partnership.

> üîç **Impact**: Allows capital gains from real estate-linked investments to be taxed in the country where the property is located, preventing double taxation.

#### ‚úÖ **New Paragraph 7 ‚Äì Tax Treatment for Individuals Changing Residency**
- **Rule**:
  - If an individual **ceases to be a resident** of one Contracting State and **immediately becomes a resident** of the other, and is taxed in the first country as if they had sold and repurchased the property:
    - The individual may **elect to be treated as having sold and repurchased** the property at its **fair market value** at the time of residency change.
    - This election applies for **taxation purposes in the new country**.

> üîç **Impact**: Prevents tax on capital gains due to a residency shift, ensuring fairness and avoiding windfall taxation.
