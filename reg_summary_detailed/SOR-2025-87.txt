**Detailed Summary of the Order Declaring an Amnesty Period (2025)**  
*Under subsection 117.14(1)a of the Criminal Code (S.C. 1995, c. 39, s. 139; R.S., c. C-46)*

---

### **Overview**

This regulatory order, made by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Justice, declares a **specific amnesty period (2025)** under **section 117.14(1)a of the Criminal Code**. The purpose of this amnesty is to provide legal relief and flexibility for certain individuals and organizations who legally owned or possessed **"specified firearms"**â€”a category of prohibited firearms defined in the Firearms Regulationsâ€”and who meet specific eligibility criteria.

The amnesty period is designed to allow these persons to **safely and lawfully dispose of, store, transport, or deactivate** their specified firearms without facing criminal prosecution or penalties under the Firearms Act or the Criminal Code, provided they meet the defined conditions.

---

### **Key Definitions**

- **"Specified firearm"**:  
  A prohibited firearm listed in **items 201 to 240 of Part 1 of the Schedule to the Regulations Prescribing Certain Firearms and Other Weapons, Components and Parts of Weapons, Accessories, Cartridge Magazines, Ammunition and Projectiles as Prohibited or Restricted**.  
  These are firearms that were previously classified as **restricted or prohibited**, and are now subject to strict controls due to their potential for misuse.

---

### **Eligible Persons and Circumstances**

The Order identifies **six categories of individuals or entities** who are eligible for the amnesty period. Each category has specific eligibility requirements and associated rights.

---

#### **1. Individuals Who Own or Possess a Specified Firearm and Hold a Valid Licence**

Eligibility conditions:
- On the day the Order comes into force, the person:
  - Owns or possesses a specified firearm;
  - Holds a licence issued under the **Firearms Act**.
- During the amnesty period:
  - Is in possession of the firearm;
  - Continues to hold the licence while in possession;
- If the firearm was a **restricted firearm** on the day before the Order came into force:
  - Held a valid registration certificate issued under the Firearms Act, **or**
  - Is issued a new registration certificate **on or after** the Orderâ€™s commencement that would have been eligible under **section 13 of the Firearms Act** had the firearm remained restricted.

ðŸ‘‰ **Rights during the amnesty period**:
- Deactivate the firearm so it is no longer a firearm.
- Deliver the firearm to a police officer for destruction or other disposal.
- If the person entered into an agreement with the Government of Canada to destroy the firearm:
  - Deliver it to a designated destruction entity.
- If the person is an individual:
  - Deliver it to a **carrier** (as defined in the Firearms Act) for shipping to a deactivation facility.
- If not the owner:
  - Deliver it to its owner.
- Export the firearm in accordance with all applicable laws (including destination country regulations).
- Return the firearm to the manufacturer.
- Deliver it to a designated entity (e.g., for destruction or deactivation).
- Transport the firearm by vehicle **only under strict conditions**:
  - Firearm must be **unloaded** with **no ammunition** in the vehicle.
  - Must be in the **trunk** or **not visible from outside** (if no trunk).
  - Vehicle must **not be left unattended**.
  - Route must be **reasonably direct**.
- For individuals who own a **non-restricted firearm** on the day before the Order came into force:
  - May use it for **hunting** in accordance with **section 35 of the Constitution Act, 1982** (Indigenous rights) or to sustain themselves or their family until they can obtain another firearm.

  - Is issued a registration certificate that would have been eligible under section 13 of the Firearms Act had the firearm remained restricted.
- During the amnesty period:
  - Is in possession of the firearm;

#### **5. Individuals in Possession of a Specified Firearm for Destruction**

Eligibility conditions:
- At any time during the amnesty period:
  - In possession of a specified firearm **solely for destruction**.
- Either:
  - Entered into an agreement with the **Government of Canada** to destroy the firearm, **or**
  - Is acting in the course of duties (as an employee, agent, or mandatary) on behalf of someone who has entered such an agreement.

ðŸ‘‰ **Rights**:
- May deliver the firearm to a police officer or other authorized entity for destruction.
- May transport the firearm under conditions that ensure it is **unloaded, not visible, and not left unattended**.

#### **6. Individuals Delivering a Firearm to a Destruction Entity**

Eligibility conditions:
- At any time during the amnesty period:
  - In possession of a specified firearm **for delivery to a person** (referred to in paragraph 1(e)) to be destroyed.
- Must be:
  - An **organization authorized under an Act of Parliament** to transport firearms, or
  - An **employee, contractor, agent, or subcontractor** of such an organization acting in the course of duties or under contract.

ðŸ‘‰ **Rights**:
- May transport the firearm for destruction.
- May possess the firearm **before** transportation, as long as it is stored in compliance with business storage regulations (Section 5 of the Storage, Display and Transportation of Firearms and Other Weapons by Businesses Regulations).

---

#### **7. Businesses Deactivating Specified Firearms**

Eligibility conditions:
- At any time during the amnesty period:
  - In possession of a specified firearm **for the sole purpose of deactivating it**.
- The business:
  - Is a **licensed business** under the Firearms Act authorized to possess prohibited firearms for deactivation, or
  - Is an employee of such a business.

ðŸ‘‰ **Rights**:
- May possess the firearm before deactivation.
- May deliver it to a destruction entity.
- May transport it for destruction or deactivation.
- Must store it in compliance with **Section 6 of the Storage, Display and Transportation of Firearms and Other Weapons by Businesses Regulations**.

### **Purpose of the Amnesty Period**

The primary objectives of this amnesty are:

1. **To provide legal certainty and relief** to individuals and organizations who legally held or transferred specified firearms and who now face compliance challenges due to changes in firearm classification or regulations.

2. **To allow safe and lawful disposal** of prohibited firearms through:
   - Deactivation (rendering the firearm non-functional);
   - Destruction (by police or authorized entities);
   - Export (to countries with appropriate legal frameworks);
   - Return to manufacturers;
   - Transfer to licensed entities (e.g., for deactivation or destruction).

3. **To facilitate transport** of firearms only when strictly necessary and under conditions that ensure public safety:
   - No ammunition;
   - Firearm in trunk or not visible;
   - Vehicle not left unattended;
   - Reasonably direct route.

4. **To support Indigenous rights**:
   - Individuals with a non-restricted firearm may use it for hunting in accordance with **section 35 of the Constitution Act, 1982**, as a recognized right.

5. **To support business operations**:
   - Licensed businesses may legally store, transport, and deactivate firearms without fear of prosecution.

6. **To promote compliance and reduce illegal firearm activity** by offering a structured, lawful pathway for the removal of prohibited weapons from circulation.
