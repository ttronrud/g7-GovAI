**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

This regulation, issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council** on the recommendation of the **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration**, serves two primary purposes:

---

### **1. Repeal and Replacement of an Outdated Order**
- **Repeals**: Order in Council P.C. 157-11/848 dated June 20, 1957, which previously governed health care coverage for certain immigrants and refugees.
- **Replaces with**: A new, modernized **Order Respecting the Interim Federal Health Program, 2012**, which establishes the framework for federal financial support for health care services and medical examinations for specific groups of individuals under the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)*.

This update reflects evolving immigration policies, health care needs, and the growing complexity of refugee and immigration claims in Canada.

---

### **2. Key Definitions (Crucial for Understanding Coverage)**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act* (IRPA). |
| **Condition of Public Safety Concern** | A mental health condition diagnosed by a Canadian-licensed physician, where the individual is likely to cause harm to others. |
| **Disease Positing a Risk to Public Health** | A communicable disease on Canada’s list of notifiable diseases (as defined by the Public Health Agency of Canada), requiring public health intervention or immunization under Canadian medical standards, or as specified in the Interim Federal Health Program Policy. |
| **Expanded Health Care Coverage** | Includes hospital services, services from licensed Canadian health professionals (doctors, nurses, etc.), translation services, lab/diagnostic services, ambulance services, and supplemental services/products. Covers *more services* than basic coverage. |
| **Health Care Coverage** | Limited to urgent or essential services: hospital care, physician/nurse services, lab/diagnostic services, and immunization/medication only if needed to prevent/treat a public health disease or public safety condition. |
| **Immigration Medical Examination** | A medical exam required under IRPA section 16(2)(b) and defined in the IRPR Regulations. |
| **Person Whose Refugee Claim Has Been Rejected** | Someone whose refugee claim was finally rejected by the Immigration and Refugee Board (IRB) and whose right to judicial review or appeal is exhausted, or whose claim is deemed rejected under IRPA subsections 105(3), 108(3), or 109(3). |
| **Protected Person** | Same as in IRPA subsection 95(2); includes refugees, claimants, and others granted protection status. |
| **Public Health or Public Safety Health Care Coverage** | Covers hospital services, physician/nurse services, lab/diagnostic services, and immunization/medication only when required to diagnose, prevent, or treat a public health disease or public safety condition. |
| **Refugee Claimant** | A person whose refugee claim is pending before the IRB, including those whose appeal rights have not yet been exhausted. Excludes those whose claims were abandoned or withdrawn. |
| **Resettled Refugee** | A person who has been granted refugee protection under IRPA paragraph 95(1)(a). |

### **3. Authority and Scope of the Minister**

The **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration** is authorized to administer the **Interim Federal Health Program (IFHP)** under this Order, with specific powers to:

#### ✅ Payments Authorized:
1. **For Protected Persons (excluding resettled refugees)**  
   - Pays for health care coverage during a period set out in the IFHP Policy (as amended).

2. **For Resettled Refugees**  
   - Pays for health care coverage **only while under sponsorship** under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations (IRPR).

3. **For Refugee Claimants**  
   - Pays for health care coverage and immigration medical examinations, **except** when:
     - Their claim is **suspended** under IRPA subsections 103(1) or 105(1).
     - They are nationals of a country **designated under IRPA subsection 109.1(1)** (e.g., countries with high risks of terrorism or security threats).

4. **For Persons Whose Claims Were Rejected**  
   - Pays for public health or public safety health care coverage (but **not** if removal has been enforced under section 240 of IRPR).

5. **For Specific Vulnerable Groups**  
   - **Persons under subsection 25.1(1) or 25.2(1) of IRPA** (e.g., those receiving government income support) – eligible for expanded health care coverage.
   - **Temporary Resident Permits (TRPs)** holders who are victims of human trafficking – eligible for expanded health care and medical exams, if determined by ministerial instructions under IRPA subsection 24(3).
   - **Resettled refugees** receiving income support – eligible for expanded health care coverage for a defined period.

6. **Exceptional and Compelling Circumstances**  
   - The Minister may, on **his or her own initiative**, pay for:
     - Health care coverage
     - Public health or public safety care
     - Immigration medical exams
     - Immunization or medication (only if needed to prevent/treat a public health disease or public safety condition)
   - This applies in **exceptional cases**, such as when exercising powers under IRPA subsection 25.2(1), and for a period determined by the Minister.

7. **Medical Examinations Outside Canada**  
   - The Minister may pay for immigration medical exams, medication, or immunization **outside Canada** (prior to entry into Canada) if:
     - The individual is a **resettled refugee**, or
     - The person is a foreign national for whom the Minister exercises a power under subsection 25.2(1) of IRPA.
   - This is done only if the Minister, in their judgment, deems it **necessary** for health and safety reasons.

8. **Detained Foreign Nationals or Permanent Residents**  
   - The Minister may pay for health care services and products provided to individuals detained under IRPA, in accordance with the IFHP Policy.

### **4. Limitations and Exclusions (Critical Restrictions)**

The following are **explicitly excluded** from coverage under this Order:

| Exclusion | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Provincial Health Insurance Coverage** | The Minister **cannot pay** for services or products that a person is eligible for under any provincial health plan, whether or not they have applied. This includes both public and private plans. |
| **Private Insurance Claims** | The Minister **cannot pay** for services covered by private insurance, regardless of recovery amounts. |
| **Canadian Citizens** | The Minister **cannot make any payments** to Canadian citizens under this Order. |
| **Refugee Claimants with Suspended Claims** | No payment for health care coverage while claims are suspended under 103(1) or 105(1). |
| **Nationals of Designated Countries** | No payment for health care coverage when the individual is from a country designated under IRPA 109.1(1). |
| **Persons Eligible for Provincial Coverage** | Even if a person is not currently enrolled, if they are eligible under a provincial plan, federal payment is denied. |

> **Note**: These exclusions are designed to avoid duplication of benefits and to ensure that provincial health systems are not undermined.

---

### **5. Implementation and Effective Dates**

- **Effective Date**: The Order comes into force on **June 30, 2012**.
- **Key Provisions Take Effect on December 15, 2012**:
  - Subsection 4(3) and paragraph 6(b) of the Order
  - Section 12 of the *Balanced Refugee Reform Act* (2010)
  - This aligns with the implementation of the Balanced Refugee Reform Act, which modernized refugee claim procedures.

> **Note**: This date is referenced in the statutory instrument SI/2012-96.

---

### **6. Transitional Provisions**

- **Continuity of Prior Law**:  
  Order P.C. 157-11/848 (1957) **continues to apply** for health care services and products provided **until June 29, 2012**.
- After June 29, 2012, all health care costs must be paid under this new Order.

> This ensures a smooth transition from outdated to modernized policy without gaps in service.

---

### **7. Benefit Grids and Payment Mechanism**

- All payments are made **in accordance with the benefit grids** outlined in the **Interim Federal Health Program Policy**, as amended from time to time.
- These grids determine eligibility and the amount of financial support based on the individual’s status, type of service, and other factors.

---

### **8. Policy Objectives and Rationale**

This regulation aims to:

- Ensure that vulnerable individuals (especially refugees, claimants, and victims of trafficking) receive timely and necessary health care.
- Prevent public health risks by supporting immunizations and treatment for communicable diseases.
- Maintain national health and safety by restricting coverage to urgent, essential, or public safety-related needs.
- Avoid duplication with provincial health programs and private insurance.
- Provide flexibility in exceptional circumstances to protect both the individual and Canadian public health.
