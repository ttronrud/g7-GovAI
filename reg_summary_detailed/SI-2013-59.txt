### **Duration of Withdrawal**

### **Exclusions Where Section 2 Does Not Apply**

Despite the general withdrawal, the Order explicitly **excludes certain mineral-related activities** from being affected by the withdrawal. These are critical for ensuring continuity in existing mineral rights and operations.

The following activities are **not impacted** by the withdrawal:

1. **Mineral Prospecting Permits**
   - A person who **held a prospecting permit before the Order came into effect** may still locate a mineral claim.

2. **Recording of Mineral Claims**
   - Any mineral claim that was **already located or referred to in the permit** before the Order is made may be recorded without being affected.

3. **Leasing of Recorded Claims**
   - A person with a **recorded mineral claim** may be granted a lease under the **Northwest Territories and Nunavut Mining Regulations**, provided the lease covers land within that claim.

4. **Significant Discovery Licences (Canada Petroleum Resources Act)**
   - If a significant discovery licence is issued to a holder of an **exploration licence issued before the Order**, and it covers land subject to that exploration licence, the licence is not blocked.

5. **Production Licences**
   - Production licences issued under the Canada Petroleum Resources Act to holders of:
     - A **significant discovery licence** (issued before the Order), or
     - An **exploration licence** (issued before the Order),
     and covering land subject to those licences, are **not affected**.

6. **Surface Leases**
   - A surface lease may be issued to a holder of a recorded mineral claim or interest (under mining or petroleum regulations), **if required to allow the holder to exercise rights** under the claim or interest.

7. **Renewal of Interests**
   - Renewals of mineral interests (e.g., exploration or production rights) are not blocked by the withdrawal.
