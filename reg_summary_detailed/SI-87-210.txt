### **Regulation Summary: Fee and Charge Authorization for Environmental Services**

#### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
- The regulation is issued under **paragraph 13(b) of the Financial Administration Act (FAA)**.
- It is authorized by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, based on the recommendation of:
  - The **Minister of the Environment**
  - The **Treasury Board**
- This grants the **Minister of the Environment** the power to **prescribe fees or charges** for specific services or uses of facilities provided by the **Department of the Environment (DoE)**.

> üîç *Purpose:* To establish a transparent, cost-based framework for charging users of environmental services, ensuring that the Government recovers reasonable costs while maintaining service accessibility and public interest.

#### **2. Definitions of Key Terms**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Department** | Refers to the **Department of the Environment** (DoE). |
| **Direct Costs** | All personnel, operating, and capital expenses **directly attributable** to delivering a specific service. Examples: salaries of staff involved in data collection, equipment maintenance, travel related to service delivery. |
| **Indirect Costs** | Costs not directly tied to a service but **attributable** to its provision. Examples: administrative overhead, shared IT systems, office space, general management. |
| **Whole Cost** | The **total cost** of providing a service = Direct Costs + Indirect Costs. |
| **Government of Canada** | Used interchangeably with "Department" as defined in section 2 of the FAA ‚Äî refers to the federal government as a whole. |
| **Person** | Broadly defined to include any individual, partnership, corporation, university, or **government entity other than the Government of Canada**. |
| **Special Service** | Any service, information, or access to facilities related to: <br> - Weather and climate <br> - Water and ice conditions <br> - Wildlife <br> - Land resources <br> - Environmental protection <br> - Resource conservation |

> ‚úÖ *Note:* This definition ensures that a wide range of environmental data and services (e.g., climate reports, wildlife monitoring data, snowpack forecasts) are covered under this fee regime.

#### **3. Fee Setting Principles**

##### **General Rule (Section 3):**
> The Minister may prescribe a fee or charge equal to the **whole cost** of providing a special service to a person ‚Äî **unless specific exceptions apply**.

This means that, by default, users are expected to pay for the full cost of the service delivered, including both direct and indirect expenses.

##### **Exceptions to Whole Cost Recovery (Section 3 ‚Äì Exceptions):**
The Minister may prescribe a **partial fee** (i.e., less than the whole cost) in **any of the following circumstances**:

1. **Serious Detriment to User Income**  
   ‚Üí If charging the full cost would **severely harm** the financial well-being of the user (e.g., a small business or non-profit relying on free access to climate data).  
   ‚Üí The fee must be set at the **highest possible partial level** that avoids this harm.

2. **Reduction in Service Use to the Point of Failure**  
   ‚Üí If charging at whole cost would **drastically reduce demand** for the service, thereby undermining the **objective of providing the service** (e.g., if critical climate data becomes unavailable due to cost).  
   ‚Üí Again, the fee must reflect the **maximum feasible partial recovery** to preserve access and utility.

3. **Existence of a Commercially Available Equivalent Service**  
   ‚Üí If a **commercially available service** (offered by a private company) is available and comparable in nature and quality.  
   ‚Üí In such cases, the fee must be set at the **commercial rate** for the equivalent service.

> üìå *Key Principle:* These exceptions are designed to **balance fiscal responsibility with equitable access**, especially for vulnerable users or essential public services.

#### **4. Application of Partial Fees**

When a partial fee is prescribed, the Minister must ensure:

- In cases of **income harm or service reduction** (paragraphs 3(a) and 3(b)):  
  ‚Üí The fee must be the **highest possible partial cost recovery** that still avoids the negative outcome.

- In cases of **commercial competition** (paragraph 3(c)):  
  ‚Üí The fee must be **equal to the commercial rate** for the equivalent or similar service.

> ‚öñÔ∏è *Implication:* This ensures fairness and market alignment ‚Äî users pay no more than what a private provider would charge for the same service.

#### **5. Exemption for Registered Charities**

> The Minister must **waive any prescribed fee or charge** for:
- A **registered charity** (as defined under the Income Tax Act),
- **Except universities**.

> üí° *Rationale:* Charities often operate on tight budgets and serve public interest missions (e.g., environmental education, conservation programs). Waiving fees ensures that these organizations can access vital environmental data without financial barriers.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note:* Universities are excluded ‚Äî likely because they may have their own funding mechanisms, research grants, or institutional access agreements.

#### **7. Practical Implications**

- **For Users (e.g., businesses, researchers, municipalities):**  
  - Must pay a fee based on the **full cost** of the service unless they fall into one of the three exceptions.  
  - If they are a **charity**, they pay **nothing**.

- **For the Department of the Environment:**

#### **8. Limitations and Constraints**

- **No automatic fee increases** ‚Äî fees are prescribed in writing and must be justified under the conditions outlined.
- **No fees for universities** ‚Äî even if they are non-profits, universities are not eligible for waivers.
- **No fee for Government of Canada entities** ‚Äî the regulation explicitly excludes government bodies from being charged.
- **Transparency required** ‚Äî the Minister must publish the rationale for any fee or exemption.
