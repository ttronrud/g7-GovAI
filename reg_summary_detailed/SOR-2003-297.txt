**Detailed Summary of the *Disclosure of Charges (Retail Associations) Regulations***

---

### **Regulatory Background and Authority**

The *Disclosure of Charges (Retail Associations) Regulations* were made by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, under specific provisions of the **Cooperative Credit Associations Act (CCAA)**:

- **Section 385.09a** â€“ Relates to disclosure obligations of retail associations regarding charges.
- **Section 385.28a** â€“ Supports transparency in financial services provided by retail associations.
- **Section 463b** â€“ Empowers the government to regulate disclosure of fees and charges to ensure consumer transparency.

These regulations were originally enacted in:
- **S.C. 2001, c. 9, s. 313 and s. 339** (amendments to the CCAA)
- **S.C. 1991, c. 48** (original framework)

The purpose of these regulations is to **enhance financial transparency** by requiring retail credit associations (such as co-operative banks or credit unions) to clearly disclose all charges associated with deposit accounts and related services to their customers and the public.

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Controlled Access Automated Teller Machine (ATM)**  
   - An ATM located either:
     - In a branch or point of service of a retail association, or  
     - In an enclosed area adjacent to such a location.  
   - Access is **controlled by a system** that restricts entry to authorized users (e.g., via PIN, card, or authentication).  
   - This definition ensures that only users with proper access can use these machines, and thus, disclosures must be tailored accordingly.

2. **Point of Service (POS)**  
   - A physical location where the public can access services and open or initiate the opening of **personal deposit accounts** (e.g., savings or checking accounts).  
   - Examples include branches, kiosks, or community service centers operated by the retail association.

## **Core Provisions**

---

### **1. Disclosure of Charges Applicable to Personal Deposit Accounts**

**Requirement**:  
Retail associations must **disclose all charges** related to **personal deposit accounts** (e.g., checking or savings accounts) to customers and the public.

**Where Disclosure Must Be Made**:
- At each **branch** where products and services are offered.
- At each **point of service**.
- On the retail associationâ€™s **websites** offering products or services in Canada.

**When Charges Are Increased or New Charges Are Introduced**:

#### For Customers Who Receive a Statement of Account:
- A **written notice** must be sent **at least 30 days before** the effective date of the charge change.
- If the customer has written instructions to receive notices on behalf of themselves, the notice must be sent to that designated person.

#### For Customers Who Do Not Receive a Statement of Account:
- A **public notice** must be displayed for **at least 60 days** before the effective date at:
  - All branches,
  - All points of service,
  - All **controlled access ATMs** (where the associationâ€™s name or identifying information is displayed),
  - On the associationâ€™s **websites**.
- Additionally, a **notice (electronic or otherwise)** must be provided to all **non-controlled access ATMs** (i.e., open-access ATMs) for a period of **at least 60 days** before the change.  
  - This notice must inform customers of:
    - The increase or new charge,
    - How to obtain further details about the charge.

### **2. Disclosure of Charges for Non-Personal Deposit Account Services**

Retail associations must also disclose charges related to **non-personal deposit account services**, such as:

| Service | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Acceptance of deposits | When a customer deposits cash or instruments |
| Acceptance of coins, cheques, or Bank of Canada notes | For depositing currency or checks |
| Issuance of cheques | Fees for issuing checks |
| Certification of cheques | Fees for certifying a cheque as valid |
| Handling of returned cheques (insufficient funds) | When a cheque is returned due to overdraft |
| Holding cheques for deposit | Time delay in processing a cheque |
| Handling of U.S. dollar cheques | For foreign currency transactions |
| Stop payment processing | To cancel a cheque after issuance |
| Handling of returned customer cheques (insufficient funds) | Similar to above |
| Supply of coins and Bank of Canada notes | For cash withdrawals or deposits |
| Handling of overdrafts | Fees or charges for overdrafts |
| Transfers between accounts | Fees for moving funds within the same account |
| Supply of account statements | Fees for receiving statements |
| Handling of account confirmations | Fees for account verification |
| Conducting account searches | For balance or transaction inquiries |
| Provision of account balance information | Access to current balance |
| Closing an account | Fees associated with closing an account |
| Handling of unclaimed balances | Fees or procedures for inactive accounts |

**Disclosure Method**:
- A **written statement** must be displayed and made available at:
  - Each branch,
  - Each point of service,
  - On the associationâ€™s websites in Canada.

**Statement Content Requirements**:
- The statement must either:
  - Clearly indicate that it **sets out all charges** for these services, OR  
  - State that it **does not include all charges** and provide a clear method for obtaining information on missing charges.

### **3. Disclosure of Charge Increases for Non-Personal Deposit Services**

When a retail association increases a charge for any of the services listed above:

#### For Customers Receiving a Statement of Account:
- A **written notice** must be sent **at least 30 days before** the effective date to each customer whose account is affected.
- If the customer has designated another person, the notice must go to that person.

#### For Customers Not Receiving a Statement:
- A **public notice** must be displayed for **at least 60 days** before the effective date at:
  - All branches,
  - All points of service,
  - On the associationâ€™s websites.

### **4. Exemption for Written Agreements**

**Section 5 Exception**:  
If a customer has **agreed in writing** to a charge that differs from the amount required to be disclosed under subsection 4(1), the regulation **does not apply** to that customer.

> ðŸ“ *Example*: A customer signs a form agreeing to a higher monthly fee for account maintenance. The association is not required to disclose that fee under the standard rules, as the customer has explicitly consented to it.

> âš ï¸ *Note*: This exemption must be clearly documented in writing and must not mislead or conceal fees.

---

### **5. Maintenance of a Charges List**

Retail associations must **maintain a list** at each of the following locations:

- All branches,
- All points of service,
- On all websites offering products or services in Canada.

**List Content**:
- Must include:
  - All charges applicable to deposit accounts,
  - The **usual amount** charged for services typically provided.

**Accessibility**:
- The list must be **available for inspection during business hours** at each branch and point of service.
- Customers and the public can request and review the list upon request.

> ðŸ“š *This ensures ongoing access to up-to-date fee information and supports consumer empowerment.*
