**Detailed Summary of the Arctic Council Ministerial Meeting 2015 — Privileges and Immunities Order**

---

**1. Legal Authority and Issuance**

This regulation is issued under the authority of **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs**. It is grounded in **paragraph 5(1)(c)a of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act (FMIOA)**. This provision enables the Canadian government to grant privileges and immunities to representatives of foreign states participating in specific international meetings or organizations, when such privileges are necessary for the proper functioning of those meetings.

The Order is formally referenced in the following statutory provisions:
- **S.C. 2002, c. 12, s. 3(2)** – This section authorizes the Governor General in Council to make orders under the FMIOA to grant privileges and immunities to foreign mission representatives.
- **S.C. 1991, c. 41** – The original enactment of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act, which provides the legal framework for the privileges and immunities of international representatives.

---

**2. Purpose of the Order**

The primary purpose of this Order is to **grant specific privileges and immunities to foreign government representatives** attending the **Arctic Council Ministerial Meeting** scheduled for **April 24 and 25, 2015**, in **Iqaluit, Nunavut**.

These privileges and immunities are essential to ensure that participants can carry out their official duties without undue interference, obstruction, or legal liability from Canadian authorities.

---

**3. Definition of Key Terms**

- **"Meeting"**: Refers specifically to the **Arctic Council Ministerial Meeting** held in Iqaluit, Nunavut, on **April 24 and 25, 2015**. This is a high-level diplomatic gathering of Arctic nations and observer states, where ministers from member states discuss regional issues such as environmental protection, sustainable development, and security in the Arctic.

---

**4. Privileges and Immunities Granted**

The representatives of foreign states participating in the Meeting are granted the following privileges and immunities, **to the extent required for the exercise of their official functions in Canada**:

These are drawn from **Article IV of the Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations**, specifically:

- **Sections 11(b) and (e)**:  
  - **Section 11(b)**: Immunity from civil and criminal jurisdiction in respect of acts done in the performance of official duties.  
  - **Section 11(e)**: Immunity from legal process, including service of legal documents, in relation to acts performed in the course of their official duties.

- **Sections 14 to 16**:  
  - **Section 14**: Immunity from taxation in respect of income derived from official duties.  
  - **Section 15**: Immunity from search and seizure of property or documents related to official duties.  
  - **Section 16**: Immunity from interference with the freedom of movement and access to information required for official functions.

These provisions are **set out in Schedule III** of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*, which serves as the official repository of the UN Convention’s privileges and immunities as applied in Canada.

---

**5. Scope and Limitations**

- The privileges and immunities apply **only to the extent necessary** for the representatives to perform their official functions during the Meeting.
- They are **not granted for personal activities** or non-official conduct.
- The immunity is **limited to the duration of the Meeting** (April 24–25, 2015) and does not extend beyond that period or to other activities in Canada.
- The immunity is **conditional upon the representative’s participation in the Meeting** and their performance of official duties related to it.

---

**6. Legal Effect and Enforcement**

- The Order is **not a permanent or general grant** of privileges but is **specifically tailored** to the Arctic Council Ministerial Meeting of 2015.
- The Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** with the Canada Gazette, meaning it becomes legally effective immediately upon official publication.
- Canadian authorities (e.g., courts, law enforcement, customs) are bound by the provisions of this Order insofar as they pertain to the conduct of foreign ministers during the Meeting.

---

**7. Significance and Context**

- This Order reflects Canada’s commitment to facilitating international cooperation in the Arctic region by ensuring that diplomatic participants can operate freely and effectively.
- It underscores Canada’s recognition of the **Arctic Council as a key multilateral forum** for addressing shared regional challenges.
- By granting UN-style privileges and immunities, Canada aligns with international diplomatic norms and ensures that the Meeting proceeds without legal or procedural barriers.

---

**Conclusion**

This Order is a **temporary, targeted legal instrument** that enables foreign ministers attending the 2015 Arctic Council Ministerial Meeting in Iqaluit to operate with the necessary legal protections and immunities to carry out their official duties in Canada. It is grounded in international law and Canadian statutory authority, and it serves both practical and diplomatic purposes in supporting effective multilateral dialogue in the Arctic.

It is important to note that such orders are **not renewable or transferable** beyond the specific event and are not intended to establish a precedent for future meetings unless formally extended or renewed by similar legislation.
