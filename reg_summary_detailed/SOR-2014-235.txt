## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Special Economic Measures (South Sudan) Regulations**

### üìå **Regulatory Background and Purpose**

The **Governor in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**, has enacted these regulations under **subsections 4(1) to (3) of the Special Economic Measures Act (SEMA)**. The primary rationale is the **grave breach of international peace and security** in **South Sudan**, which has resulted or is likely to result in a **serious international crisis**.

These measures are designed to:
- Isolate individuals and entities involved in undermining peace and stability in South Sudan.
- Enforce accountability for violations of the **Cessation of Hostilities Agreement (CoHA)**.
- Apply targeted economic sanctions to prevent financial support for armed conflict or violations of peace agreements.

### ‚ö†Ô∏è **Prohibited Activities (Section 3)**

Any person in Canada or a Canadian living outside Canada is **prohibited from**:

1. **Dealing in property** held by or on behalf of a designated person, wherever located.
2. **Entering into or facilitating** (directly or indirectly) any transaction related to such property.
3. **Providing financial or related services** in respect of the prohibited dealings.
4. **Making goods available** to a designated person.
5. **Providing financial or related services** to or for the benefit of a designated person.

> üí° *This creates a broad prohibition on trade, finance, and access to goods or services involving designated persons.*

---

### ‚úÖ **Exemptions (Section 3 ‚Äì Exceptions)**

Certain transactions are **exempt** from the prohibitions above, including:

| Exception | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Pre-existing contractual payments** | Payments due under a contract entered **before** the person became designated, provided the payment is **not** made to or for the benefit of the designated person. |
| **Pension payments** | All pension payments to individuals in Canada or Canadians abroad are exempt. |
| **Diplomatic mission transactions** | Transactions required for a diplomatic mission to perform its diplomatic functions (per Article 3 of the Vienna Convention), or to maintain mission premises if temporarily or permanently recalled. |
| **Transactions with international organizations** | Includes dealings with: <br> - UN agencies <br> - International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement <br> - Canadian NGOs with grant or contribution agreements with the Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development (DFATD). |
| **Transfers of Canadian-held assets** | Canadians may transfer accounts, funds, or investments held by a designated person **on the day they became designated**, to a non-designated person. |
| **Legal services for enforcement** | Financial services required to obtain legal services in Canada related to enforcing these regulations. |
| **Loan repayments (pre-existing or third-party)** | <br> - Repayments to Canadians for loans taken **before designation** with a designated person, including enforcement of security or payments by guarantors. <br> - Repayments for loans taken with **non-designated persons**, including enforcement and security realization. |

> ‚öñÔ∏è These exemptions ensure that essential humanitarian, diplomatic, and financial obligations are not disrupted.

### üö´ **Prohibition of Facilitation or Assistance**

It is **prohibited to knowingly cause, facilitate, assist in, or intend to cause or assist in** any activity prohibited under Section 3.  
This includes **indirect involvement** such as providing advice, enabling access, or structuring transactions to circumvent sanctions.

> üõ°Ô∏è This clause strengthens enforcement by targeting both direct and indirect facilitation.

### üîç **Obligation of Financial Institutions and Entities to Monitor**

Certain entities must **continuously monitor** whether they are in possession or control of property owned or controlled by a designated person. These include:

| Entity Type | Scope |
|-----------|-------|
| **Authorized foreign banks** | Under the *Bank Act*, in Canada or banks subject to it. |
| **Cooperative credit societies, credit unions, caisses populaires** | Regulated by provincial legislation or the *Cooperative Credit Associations Act*. |
| **Foreign companies** | In respect of their **insurance business in Canada**. |
| **Insurance companies, provincial companies, societies** | As defined in *Insurance Companies Act*. |
| **Fraternal benefit societies** | In respect of insurance activities. |
| **Trust and loan companies** | Under *Trust and Loan Companies Act*. |
| **Loan companies** | Regulated by provincial law. |
| **Entities under Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act (PCMLTFA)** | If they open accounts for clients. |
| **Securities dealers, portfolio managers, investment advisers** | Authorized under provincial legislation. |

> üìù These entities must actively screen and report suspicious activity involving designated persons.

### üì¢ **Reporting Obligations to RCMP**

Every person in Canada or a Canadian abroad must **disclose without delay** to the **Commissioner of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)**:

1. The existence of property in their possession or control that they have **reason to believe** is:
   - Owned or controlled by a designated person, or
   - Owned or controlled by an entity owned or controlled by a designated person.

2. Any **transaction or proposed transaction** involving such property.

> ‚úÖ This creates a **duty to report** ‚Äî a critical tool for early detection of illicit financial flows.

> ‚öñÔ∏è **No legal liability** arises for disclosures made in **good faith** under this provision. This protects individuals from prosecution for reporting suspected violations.

### üîÑ **Review and Removal from Schedule**

A designated person may apply in writing to the **Minister of Foreign Affairs** to have their name **removed from the Schedule**.

#### Process:
- The Minister must decide within **90 days** of receiving the application whether there are **reasonable grounds** to recommend removal to the Governor in Council.
- The Minister must **notify the applicant without delay** of the decision.
- If there has been a **material change in circumstances** since the last application, the person may submit a **new application**.

> üîÑ This allows for periodic review and ensures that sanctions are not applied indefinitely without justification.

---

### üö´ **Challenge to Designation**

A person who believes they are **not a designated person** may apply to the Minister for a **certificate stating they are not designated**.

- If the Minister determines the person is **not** a designated person, they must issue the certificate within **30 days** of receiving the application.

> üìù This provides a **due process mechanism** for appeal and correction of erroneous designations.
