**Regulation Summary:**

**Title:** Designation of the Minister for International Trade as the Minister for the Purpose of the North American Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act

**Legal Authority:**  
The regulation is issued under **section 11 of the North American Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act** (*N.A.F.T.A. Implementation Act*), which grants the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister) the authority to designate a minister of the Crown to serve as the designated Minister responsible for administering specific provisions of the Act.

**Key Provisions of the Regulation:**

1. **Designation of the Minister:**  
   The **Minister for International Trade** is officially designated as the **Minister for the purposes of sections 1 to 9 and Parts I and III** of the North American Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act.

2. **Effective Date:**  
   This designation takes effect on **January 1, 1994**. This date marks the beginning of the implementation phase of the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA), aligning the federal government’s administrative structure with the new trade obligations under the agreement.

3. **Role and Jurisdiction:**  
   The designated Minister has full authority and responsibility to:
   - Interpret, administer, and enforce the provisions of sections 1 to 9 of the Act;
   - Oversee implementation of the obligations under Parts I and III of the Act, which typically cover:
     - The establishment of mechanisms for dispute resolution;
     - The implementation of trade rules and procedures;
     - The coordination of government actions related to trade policy and compliance with NAFTA commitments;
     - The management of trade-related regulatory and policy issues.

4. **Membership in the Queen’s Privy Council:**  
   The Minister for International Trade is formally recognized as a member of the **Queen’s Privy Council for Canada**. This designation signifies that the individual holds a formal, high-level role in the federal government and has the necessary standing to act on behalf of the Crown in the administration of the Act.

5. **Legal Basis and Process:**  
   The regulation is issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Prime Minister**, which reflects the standard constitutional process for such appointments. This ensures that the designation is both politically and legally sound, grounded in executive authority and ministerial responsibility.

6. **Purpose and Significance:**  
   This designation ensures that the federal government has a clearly defined and accountable minister responsible for the day-to-day administration and implementation of NAFTA-related policies. It provides legal clarity, enhances coordination among federal departments, and supports compliance with international trade obligations.

**Context and Background:**  
This regulation was enacted in the lead-up to the full implementation of the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA), which officially came into force on January 1, 1994. The Act established a legal framework for Canada’s participation in the agreement, including mechanisms for trade policy, regulatory alignment, and dispute resolution. By designating a specific minister, the government ensured that there was a single point of responsibility for overseeing compliance and implementation.

This regulation formally appoints the Minister for International Trade as the designated Minister responsible for administering and enforcing key provisions of the North American Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act, effective January 1, 1994.
