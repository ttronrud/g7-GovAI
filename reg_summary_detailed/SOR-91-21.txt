## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
- The regulation was made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, under **subsection 277(1)** of the *Excise Tax Act*.
- It serves to **prescribe specific conditions** under which a corporation ("the other corporation") is considered a **"prescribed corporation"** in relation to another corporation ("the particular corporation").
- This is primarily for **tax purposes**, specifically to determine when a corporation is deemed to be **closely related** to another, which affects how excise taxes are applied (especially under **paragraph 128(1)(b)** of the Act).

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Note: This regulation was **repealed** by **SOR/2011-56, section 2**, meaning it is **no longer in force** as of the date of repeal. However, the summary remains relevant for historical or interpretive understanding.

### **2. Key Definitions**

#### ‚úÖ *Act*  
Refers to the *Excise Tax Act*, the primary legislation governing excise taxes in Canada.

#### ‚úÖ *Specified Share*  
- A share of capital stock of a corporation that:
  - Is **issued and outstanding**.
  - Has **full voting rights under all circumstances**.
- These shares are the focus of ownership and control analysis in the regulation.

> Example: A common share with voting rights, not a non-voting or preference share.

### **3. When Is a Corporation a "Prescribed Corporation" in Relation to a Particular Corporation?**

A corporation ("the other corporation") is considered a **prescribed corporation** in relation to a "particular corporation" if **one or more of the following conditions are met**:

---

#### üîπ **Condition 1: Ownership by a Closely Related Corporation (Paragraph 128(1)(a))**

The total value and number of **specified shares** of the other corporation, owned by the particular corporation, must be held by:
- A **corporation closely related** to the particular corporation under **paragraph 128(1)(a)** of the Act.

> üìå *Note:* "Closely related" is defined elsewhere in the Act and generally includes situations such as:
> - Parent-subsidiary relationships
> - Shared ownership or control
> - Common shareholders
> - Ownership through trusts or joint ventures

---

#### üîπ **Condition 2: Ownership by an Employee or Related Entity**

The shares must be owned by:
- An **employee** of the other corporation,
- Or an employee of a corporation that is **closely related** to the other corporation (under paragraph 128(1)(a)),
- Or an employee of a corporation referred to in clause (A) or (B) (likely referring to certain types of trusts or holding companies).

**Additional Requirements:**
- The shares must be **non-tradable on a stock exchange**.
- The ownership must have arisen **in respect of the employee‚Äôs employment** (i.e., not through investment or inheritance).
- The employee must hold **qualifying voting control**.
- The employee must own **at least 90%** of the total value and number of all specified shares.

> üìå *Purpose:* To prevent tax avoidance through complex ownership structures involving employees or closely held entities.

---

#### üîπ **Condition 3: Trust Ownership for Benefit of Employee or Corporation**

The shares may be held in **trust** for the benefit of:
- The other corporation, or
- An employee referred to in subclause (C)(I)

**Requirements:**
- The trust must hold shares that:
  - Are **non-tradable on a stock exchange**.
  - The **beneficial ownership** arose from the employee‚Äôs employment.
- The trust must represent **at least 90%** of the total value and number of specified shares of the other corporation.

> üìå *Purpose:* To ensure that employee-related ownership structures (e.g., employee benefit trusts) are properly recognized for tax purposes.

---

#### üîπ **Condition 4: Threshold Requirement for Ownership (50% Rule)**

Even if the above ownership conditions are met, the **total value and number of specified shares** of the other corporation that are owned by:
- A corporation referred to in clause (i)(A) or (B) (likely meaning a closely related or employee-related entity),

must represent **at least 50%** of the total value and number of all specified shares of the other corporation.

> üìå *Purpose:* To ensure that significant ownership or control is present, not just minor holdings.

---

#### üîπ **Condition 5: Qualifying Voting Control**

The particular corporation would be deemed to hold **qualifying voting control** over the other corporation **if** it owned:
- All specified shares (full voting rights) that fall under clauses (i)(A) to (D), **and**
- All non-specified shares (non-voting shares) that would be included in those clauses **if they were specified shares**.

> üìå *Definition of "qualifying voting control":*  
This means the particular corporation has **effective control** over the other corporation, even if it does not own all shares. It includes:
- Ownership of **at least 90%** of specified shares,
- Ownership by a **closely related corporation**,
- Ownership by a **prescribed corporation** in relation to the particular corporation,
- Or a **combination** of the above.

> üìå *Purpose:* To determine whether the particular corporation (or its associates) has **de facto control** over the other corporation for tax purposes.
