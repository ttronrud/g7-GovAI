## **Detailed Summary of the Entity Associated with a Foreign Bank Regulations**

### **1. Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**
- **Enacted by:** Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance.
- **Legal Authority:** Pursuant to **subsection 507(17)a of the Bank Act**.
- **Statutory Citation:**  
  - S.C. 2001, c. 9, s. 132 (amending the Bank Act)  
  - S.C. 1991, c. 46 (original reference to the Bank Act)

> This regulation establishes exemptions from the definition of "entity associated with a foreign bank" under the Bank Act, thereby reducing regulatory exposure for certain foreign-related entities that do not engage in prohibited financial activities in Canada.

---

### **2. Key Definitions and Scope**
- **"Act"** refers to the *Bank Act*.
- The regulation applies to **entities associated with a foreign bank**, and defines specific classes of such entities that are **exempt from being classified as "associated"** under the Bank Act — even if they are linked to a foreign bank.

---

### **3. Exempt Classes of Entities Associated with a Foreign Bank**

The following classes of entities are **exempt** from the status of being "associated with a foreign bank" under the Bank Act:

---

#### **Class 1: Government Entities (Foreign Governments or Political Subdivisions)**
- Entities that are:
  - The **government of a foreign country**, or
  - A **political subdivision** of a foreign country (e.g., provincial or municipal governments),
- **And** do **not** carry on business in Canada involving any of the activities listed in **paragraphs (a) to (g)** of the definition of *“financial services entity”* in subsection 507(1) of the Bank Act.

> **Exemption Reason:** These entities are considered sovereign or governmental bodies, and if they are not involved in financial services (e.g., banking, investment, insurance), they are not subject to the same regulatory scrutiny as private financial institutions.

---

#### **Class 2: Government-Controlled Entities (Non-Bank, Non-Associated)**
- Entities that are:
  - **Controlled by a foreign government or political subdivision**,  
  - **Are not themselves foreign banks**,  
  - **Are not controlled by a foreign bank**,  
  - **Do not engage in Canada** in any activity listed in **paragraphs (a) to (h)** of the definition of *“financial services entity”*, and  
  - **Have not been deemed under subsection 507(3)** of the Bank Act to be associated with a foreign bank.

> **Exemption Reason:** These are non-banking entities (e.g., state-owned enterprises) that operate only outside Canada and do not participate in Canadian financial markets or activities. They are exempt because they lack direct financial service operations in Canada and are not linked to a foreign bank through control.

---

#### **Class 3: Entities Associated Only by Control of the Foreign Bank**
- Entities that:
  - Are associated with a foreign bank **only because they control** that foreign bank, and  
  - Are entities described in **paragraphs 468(1)(a) to (f)** of the Bank Act.

> **Note:** Paragraphs 468(1)(a) to (f) refer to specific types of entities such as:
> - Insurance companies
> - Investment companies
> - Trusts
> - Certain financial holding companies
> - Entities engaged in financial services under specific conditions

> **Exemption Reason:** These entities are only linked to a foreign bank through ownership (control), not through operations or business activities in Canada. Since they are not engaged in financial services in Canada, they are exempt.

---

#### **Class 4: Entities Controlled by Class 2 Entities (Government-Controlled Entities)**
- Entities that:
  - Are **controlled by an entity exempted under Class 2**, and  
  - Are **not a foreign bank described in paragraph 508(1)(a)** of the Bank Act, **or**  
  - Are **not controlled by a foreign bank described in paragraph 508(1)(a)**.

> **Exemption Reason:** This provides a "pass-through" exemption. If a foreign entity is exempt (e.g., a government-controlled non-bank), and a Canadian or foreign entity is controlled by it, that entity is also exempt — as long as it does not fall into the prohibited categories (e.g., being a foreign bank in Canada or controlled by one).

---

#### **Class 5: Canadian Entities Associated Only by Control of a Foreign Bank**
- Canadian entities that:
  - Are **not covered by Class 3 or Class 4**,  
  - Are associated with a foreign bank **only because they control** the foreign bank,  
  - **Except where**:
    - The foreign bank is described in **paragraph 508(1)(a)** of the Bank Act (i.e., a foreign bank that is deemed to be "associated" due to its presence in Canada), **and**
    - Either:
      - The foreign bank, or
      - An entity controlled by it,
      Engages in any of the following in Canada:
        - Conducts business in Canada (other than through holding, managing, or dealing with real property),
        - Maintains a branch in Canada (except an office under section 522),
        - Establishes, maintains, or acquires an automated banking machine (ABM), remote service unit, or similar automated service,
        - Accepts data from such machines or services in Canada (outside of exceptions in sections 511 or 512),
        - Acquires or holds control of, or a substantial investment in, a Canadian entity, or
        - Acquires or holds any share or ownership interest in a Canadian entity that:
          - Is controlled by, or has a substantial investment in, an entity associated with the foreign bank,
          - Or would be controlled by, or have a substantial investment in, if the foreign bank and associated entities were consolidated as one person.

> **Exemption Reason:** Most Canadian entities that only own a foreign bank are exempt — **but with strict exceptions**. If the foreign bank (or its affiliates) is actively operating in Canada (e.g., via branches, ABMs, or investments), then the Canadian parent entity is **not exempt** and is considered associated with a foreign bank.

> This ensures that **active financial operations in Canada** trigger regulatory oversight, even if the ownership is indirect.

---

#### **Class 6: Entities Controlled by Canadian Entities Exempted under Class 5**
- Entities that:
  - Are **controlled by a Canadian entity exempted under Class 5**, and  
  - Are **not a foreign bank described in paragraph 508(1)(a)**, or  
  - Are **not controlled by a foreign bank described in paragraph 508(1)(a)**.

> **Exemption Reason:** Extends the exemption to entities that are downstream of exempt Canadian entities. As long as they are not themselves foreign banks or controlled by a foreign bank in Canada, they are not considered associated with a foreign bank.

---

### **4. Minister’s Power to Deem Entities Not Associated**
- The **Minister of Finance** may, by **order**, and subject to any **terms and conditions** deemed appropriate, **deem any entity not to be associated with a foreign bank** for the purposes of any provision of the Bank Act.

> **Purpose:** Provides flexibility to address specific, unique cases where an entity may be linked to a foreign bank but does not meet the criteria for association due to operational or structural reasons.

> This power allows for **case-by-case exemptions** based on factual circumstances, which helps avoid overreach in regulation.

---

### **5. Effective Date**
- These regulations **come into force** on the day that **subsection 507(17)** of the Bank Act comes into force.
- This was enacted by **section 132 of the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada Act, chapter 9 of the Statutes of Canada, 2001**.
- **Effective Date:** October 24, 2001 (as noted in SI/2001-102).

---

### **6. Key Regulatory Objectives and Implications**

| Objective | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Reduce Regulatory Burden** | Exempts certain foreign-owned or government-controlled entities from being classified as "associated with a foreign bank" if they do not engage in financial services in Canada. |
| **Protect Sovereign Entities** | Ensures that foreign governments and their political subdivisions are not subject to Canadian banking regulations unless they operate in financial services. |
| **Prevent Regulatory Overlap** | Prevents unnecessary application of Canadian banking rules to entities that are passive or only indirectly linked to foreign banks. |
| **Ensure Operational Clarity** | Clearly defines when an entity is or is not associated with a foreign bank, especially in cases involving ownership and control. |
| **Maintain Oversight of Active Operations** | The exceptions (e.g., branches, ABMs, investments) ensure that any **active financial presence in Canada** triggers regulatory scrutiny. |

---
