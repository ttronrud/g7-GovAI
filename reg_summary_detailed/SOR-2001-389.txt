## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Financial Leasing Entity Regulations**

### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
The regulations were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, under specific provisions of four major Canadian financial regulatory acts:

- **Section 978a of the Bank Act**  
- **Section 463 of the Cooperative Credit Associations Act**  
- **Section 1021 of the Insurance Companies Act**  
- **Section 531 of the Trust and Loan Companies Act**

These provisions grant the regulatory authority to define and govern the operations of **financial leasing entities** (FLEs) ‚Äî institutions that engage in financial leasing and conditional sales of personal property.

> üìå *Reference: S.C. 2001, c. 9, s. 183; S.C. 1991, c. 46, 48, 47, 45 ‚Äî these are statutory citations to prior legislation that support the regulatory framework.*

---

### **2. Purpose of the Regulations**
To establish a clear and consistent regulatory framework for **financial leasing entities**, ensuring:
- Fairness and transparency in leasing and conditional sales arrangements,
- Protection of consumers (especially natural persons),
- Financial stability and sound return on investment,
- Prevention of misleading or unsuitable financial product practices.

The regulations aim to **prevent financial leasing entities from engaging in activities that resemble consumer credit or retail sales**, which could lead to consumer exploitation or financial risk.

---

### **3. Key Definitions**

#### ‚úÖ **Estimated Residual Value**
- Definition: The value of personal property **immediately after the end of a financial lease agreement**, as **estimated by the financial leasing entity at the time the agreement is entered into**.
- Purpose: Used to calculate the return on investment and to determine the expected recovery value of leased assets.

#### ‚úÖ **Gross Vehicle Weight (GVW)**
- For a motor vehicle, it is:
  - The **manufacturer-specified loaded weight** of the vehicle, or
  - For vehicles designed to pull trailers: the **weight of the vehicle when towing a trailer**.
- Purpose: To establish a **minimum threshold** for motor vehicles that can be leased or conditionally sold.

#### ‚úÖ **Personal Household Property**
- Personal property that:
  - Is **leased by a natural person** under a financial lease agreement, or
  - Is **purchased by a natural person** under a conditional sales agreement,
  - And is **intended primarily for personal use or enjoyment** of the lessee or purchaser (or a family member not dealing at arm‚Äôs length).
- Purpose: To **exclude commercial or business-use assets** from financial leasing, protecting consumers from being subjected to leasing for non-personal purposes.

### **4. Permitted Activities of a Financial Leasing Entity**

A financial leasing entity may carry out the following activities **only if compliant with the restrictions** in Sections 5‚Äì8:

1. **Entering into and accepting assignment of conditional sales agreements** in respect of personal property.
2. **Administering financial lease agreements and conditional sales agreements** on behalf of any person.
3. **Raising and investing funds** to finance its leasing activities (e.g., through loans or equity).

> ‚ö†Ô∏è These activities are **subject to strict limitations** outlined in Sections 5‚Äì8.

### **5. Prohibited Activities (Strict Restrictions)**

A financial leasing entity **may not**:

| Prohibition | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Direct customers to specific dealers** | Cannot influence or steer customers toward particular dealers for personal property (e.g., cars) under conditional sales. Prevents conflicts of interest and promotes fair competition. |
| **Enter into conditional sales agreements for motor vehicles with GVW < 21 tonnes** | Only vehicles with **gross vehicle weight of 21 tonnes or more** are eligible. This excludes light vehicles (e.g., sedans, SUVs), limiting FLEs to large commercial or heavy-duty vehicles. |
| **Enter into agreements for personal household property with natural persons** | Cannot lease or sell personal items (e.g., furniture, appliances) to individuals for personal use. This protects consumers from predatory leasing of household goods. |
| **Enter into financial lease or conditional sales agreements that are not primarily for extending credit** | The primary purpose must be **credit extension**, not resale, investment, or other commercial ends. |
| **Enter into agreements for personal property that is: <br> (a) selected by the lessee/purchaser and acquired at their request, or <br> (b) previously acquired under another agreement** | FLEs cannot lease property they did not originally acquire or select. This prevents circumvention of rules and ensures transparency. |
| **Assume responsibility for installation, promotion, servicing, cleaning, maintenance, or repair** | The financial leasing entity must **not be responsible** for ongoing maintenance or service of the leased property. This avoids creating obligations that could lead to consumer liability. |

> ‚ùó These restrictions ensure that FLEs remain **purely financial intermediaries**, not service providers or product sellers.

---

### **6. Warranty and Guarantee Provisions**

Every financial lease or conditional sales agreement must include a clause that:

- **Assigns to the lessee or purchaser** the **benefits of all warranties, guarantees, or undertakings** made by the **manufacturer or supplier** of the personal property.

> ‚úÖ This ensures that the lessee/purchaser receives full protection from the manufacturer (e.g., defects, performance, safety), not the financial leasing entity.

---

### **7. Return on Investment Requirements**

A financial lease agreement must yield:

#### (a) **Minimum Return**
- A return to the financial leasing entity that is **not less than its full investment** in the property.

#### (b) **Reasonable Return**
- The rate of return must be **reasonable**, considering:
  - The **term** of the lease,
  - The **other terms** (e.g., rental amount, residual value),
  - And the **rate of return** offered by other lessors for similar property under similar conditions.

> üîç This ensures financial viability and prevents underpricing of leases, which could lead to losses.

---

### **8. Calculation of Return (Subsection 7(1))**

The return is calculated using **three components**:

| Component | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Rental charges** | All payments due under the lease agreement (actual or future). |
| **Estimated tax benefits** | Includes tax credits and capital cost allowance claims (e.g., CCA deductions) that the entity may claim. |
| **Purchase price or residual value (whichever is lesser)** | Either: <br> - If the lessee or a third party (dealing at arm‚Äôs length) has **contracted to buy** the property or **guaranteed its resale value** at lease end, then use the **purchase price or resale value**; <br> - Otherwise, use the **estimated residual value** or **25% of the cost of acquisition**, whichever is **smaller**. |

> üìå This ensures that the return is **realistic and tied to actual or probable outcomes**, not speculative.

---

### **9. Residual Value Limitation (Critical Rule)**

> ‚ùó **No financial leasing entity may hold a total estimated residual value** of leased properties (as defined in subparagraph 7(2)(c)(ii)) that exceeds:

> **10% of the total cost of acquisition** of those properties.

#### Example:
- If a financial leasing entity acquires personal property worth $1 million in total:
  - The **sum of all estimated residual values** of the leased assets must not exceed **$100,000**.

> ‚úÖ This prevents overvaluation of future returns and protects against excessive risk in the event of asset depreciation.

---

### **10. Effective Date and Enforcement**

- The regulations **come into force on the day** that the following provisions of the **Financial Consumer Agency of Canada Act (2001)** come into force:
  - Section 127 (Bank Act)
  - Section 314 (Cooperative Credit Associations Act)
  - Section 426 (Insurance Companies Act)
  - Section 550 (Trust and Loan Companies Act)

> üìå *Note: The regulations were enacted on October 24, 2001 (SI/2001-102), and thus became effective on that date.*

---

### **11. Repeal Note**
- The "[Repeal]" clause indicates that these regulations **replace or supersede** any prior rules or provisions related to financial leasing entities.
