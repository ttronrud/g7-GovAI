### ðŸ“Œ **1. Regulatory Background and Authority**
- These regulations were established under **section 44 of the Nuclear Safety and Control Act (NSCA)**.
- They were **approved by the Governor in Council** on the recommendation of the **Minister of Natural Resources**.
- The regulations were issued by the **Canadian Nuclear Safety Commission (CNSC)** on **May 31, 2000**, and came into force on the day of approval.
- The primary objective is to **prevent the proliferation of nuclear weapons** by controlling the import, export, and transit of sensitive nuclear materials, equipment, and information.

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Nuclear Safety and Control Act* (NSCA). |
| **Controlled Nuclear Substance** | Includes specific nuclear materials listed in the schedule (e.g., fissionable materials, source materials, deuterium, tritium, etc.). |
| **Controlled Nuclear Equipment** | Equipment specifically designed or prepared for use in nuclear reactors, reprocessing, fuel fabrication, or isotope separation (e.g., centrifuges, reactor vessels, pumps). |
| **Controlled Nuclear Information** | Technical data, designs, or documentation related to controlled substances or equipment, unless publicly disclosed. |
| **Transit** | Movement of a substance, equipment, or information through Canada from a foreign point of origin to a foreign destination, with no final destination in Canada. |

  - Controlled nuclear information
- Applies to all transactions involving these items, whether for commercial, research, or industrial use.

### ðŸ“Œ **5. Exemptions from Licence Requirements**

| Exempt Activity | Conditions |
|----------------|-----------|

| **Import** of controlled nuclear information related to equipment/substances in A.3 or Part B | No licence required. |
| **Import** for **transit** purposes (through Canada) | No licence required. |
| **Export** after transit | No licence required. |
| **Import** of up to **200 kg per shipment** of a controlled substance in **paragraph A.1.3** (non-reactor use) | No licence required. |
