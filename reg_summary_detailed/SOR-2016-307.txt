## ðŸ“Œ Reporting Obligations: Who Must File?

### âœ… **1. Receiving Contributing Oil (Import or Transfer)**

**Applies to**:
- Oil imported by sea into Canada (in bulk) and destined for unloading at a terminal or port in Canada;
- Oil shipped to the U.S. by sea, then imported into Canada via non-maritime transport (e.g., rail, truck), and destined for unloading in Canada;
- Oil shipped from Canada or offshore installations (within Canadaâ€™s exclusive economic zone) and destined for unloading in Canada.

**Reporting Threshold**:
- A person must file an **information return** if they receive **more than 150,000 metric tons** of contributing oil in a calendar year.

**Deadline**:
- No later than **February 28 of the following calendar year**.

**What Must Be Included**:
- Name, mailing address, email, phone, and fax (if applicable) of the person.
- Type of contributing oil and **total quantity** of each type received.
- Name and address of **associated persons** who also received contributing oil.

> *Note*: "Associated persons" include entities with shared control, ownership, or operational ties.

### âœ… **2. Receiving Contributing Cargo (Hazardous Substances)**

**Applies to**:
- Any cargo imported into Canada by sea or shipped from Canada/offshore installations to be unloaded in Canada.

**Reporting Thresholds**:
A receiver must file a return if they receive:
- **Liquefied Natural Gases (LNG)** (as defined in paragraph 2(b) of Article 16 of the *Hazardous and Noxious Substances Convention*) â€” **any quantity**;
- **Liquefied Petroleum Gases (LPG)** (as defined in paragraph 2(c) of Article 16) â€” **over 17,000 metric tons**;
- **Any hazardous and noxious substances** listed in paragraphs 5(a)(ii), (iii), (v), (vi), and (vii) of Article 1 of the *Hazardous and Noxious Substances Convention* â€” **over 17,000 metric tons**.

**Deadline**:
- No later than **February 28 of the following calendar year**.

**What Must Be Included**:
- Name, address, email, phone, and fax of the receiver.
- Type and total quantity of each type of contributing cargo received.
- If acting as an **agent or mandatary** for a principal or mandator:
  - Name of the principal/mandator and type/quantity of cargo received.
- If receiving cargo from an **agent or mandatary**:
  - Name of agent/mandatary and type/quantity of cargo received.
- Name and address of **associated persons** involved.

> *Note*: This provision ensures traceability of hazardous materials, especially for high-risk substances.

### âœ… **3. Exporting Contributing Oil or Non-Persistent Oil**

**Applies to**:
- Oil or non-persistent oil exported by sea from Canada or offshore installations, destined for a location **outside Canada**.

**Reporting Thresholds**:
- Must file a return if:
  - **Contributing oil** exceeds **150,000 metric tons** in a calendar year; or
  - **Non-persistent oil** exceeds **17,000 metric tons**.

**Deadline**:
- No later than **February 28 of the following calendar year**.

**What Must Be Included**:
- Name, address, email, phone, and fax of the exporter.
- Types and total quantities of contributing oil and non-persistent oil exported.
- Name and address of **associated persons** involved in export.
- If exporting **non-persistent oil as an agent or mandatary** for a Canadian principal:
  - Name of principal/mandator and type/quantity exported.
- If the person is a **principal or mandator** in Canada and exported via an agent:
  - Name of agent/mandatary and type/quantity exported.

> *Note*: Non-persistent oil refers to oil that does not remain in the environment for long periods (e.g., low-sulfur or refined products), and its reporting is critical due to environmental risks.
