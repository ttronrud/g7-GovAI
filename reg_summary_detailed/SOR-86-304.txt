## ðŸ—ï¸ **Core Principles and Scope**

### 1. **Applicability**
- Applies to **all employers and employees** in workplaces where safety and health risks exist.
- **Exception**: Does **not apply** to:
  - Employees on **trains in operation**.
  - Employees on **aircraft in flight**.
  - Employees on **ships**.
  - Employees working in oil/gas exploration or production in frontier lands (covered by the *Oil and Gas Occupational Safety and Health Regulations*).
  - Workplaces excluded by an order under section 123.1 of the Canada Labour Code.

> This ensures that the regulation does not overreach into areas governed by other specialized laws.

### 2. **Application to Trainees and Contractors**
- Applies to **non-employees** (e.g., trainees, apprentices, contractors) who perform work **to gain knowledge or experience**, as if they were actual employees.
- All provisions must be interpreted and applied **as if the person were an employee**.

> This ensures that even temporary or learning-based workers are protected under the same safety standards.

### 3. **Recordkeeping Requirements**
- Employers must **maintain and keep accessible** records of workplace safety incidents, hazards, and compliance activities.
- Records must be **readily available** for inspection by:
  - The **Head of Compliance and Enforcement**
  - The **Workplace Committee**
  - The **Health and Safety Representative** (if one exists)

> Ensures transparency, accountability, and continuous improvement in safety practices.

### 4. **Conflict Resolution: Precedence of Provisions**
- If there is a **conflict between a standard incorporated by reference** (e.g., CSA, ANSI) and a direct provision in the regulation, **the direct provision in the regulation prevails**.

> This prevents ambiguity and ensures that the core safety objectives of the regulation are not undermined by conflicting standards.

## ðŸ¢ **Building and Structural Safety Standards**

This section establishes **minimum safety standards for buildings and structures**, with a strong emphasis on **fall prevention, clearances, and structural integrity**.

### Key Provisions:

#### 1. **Compliance with National Building Codes**
- All **new buildings** (construction beginning on or after March 31, 1986) must meet the **National Building Code of Canada (1995)**.
- Existing buildings must **if feasible**, meet these requirements.
- **Renovations** must also meet the code if feasible.
- If not feasible, the employer must **notify** the workplace committee or health and safety representative **before starting work**.

> Ensures that all physical workspaces are designed with safety in mind from the outset.

#### 2. **Door and Passageway Safety**
- **Double-action swinging doors** in high-traffic areas (e.g., wheelchair access) must allow users to **see people on both sides**.
- The **area of hazard** created by an open door must be clearly marked.
- If an open door **reduces passageway width below code requirements**, either:
  - A **warning sign** must be posted, or
  - A **highly visible barricade** must be placed to block access.

> Prevents accidents due to unexpected movement or blocked pathways.
