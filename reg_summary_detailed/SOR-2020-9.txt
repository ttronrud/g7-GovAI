**Detailed Summary of the Cannabis for Medical Purposes Remission Order**

**Regulatory Source:**  
- *Cannabis Act* (S.C. 2018, c. 16)  
- Made under subsection 144(1) of the *Cannabis Act*  
- Issued by the **Minister of Health**  
- **Date of Issue:** January 6, 2020  
- **Date of Coming Into Force:** January 22, 2020  

---

### **Purpose of the Order**
This regulation establishes a **remission (exemption)** of certain fees for holders of medical cannabis licences who meet specific conditions related to the **sale of cannabis in Canada** exclusively to **eligible clients or named responsible adults**, or to persons receiving medical exemptions under section 140 of the *Cannabis Act*. The goal is to support the medical cannabis system by reducing financial burdens on licensed operators who serve only medically authorized consumers.

---

### **Key Definitions Used in the Order**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Cannabis revenue** | As defined in Section 1 of the *Cannabis Fees Order* – refers to the net income derived from the sale of cannabis, calculated as revenue from sales minus costs of purchases. |
| **Client** | As defined in subsection 1(2) of the *Cannabis Regulations* – a person who has been prescribed cannabis for a medical purpose and is registered with the government. |
| **Named responsible adult** | As defined in subsection 264(1) of the *Cannabis Regulations* – a person (typically a family member or caregiver) who is legally authorized to access cannabis on behalf of a client who is unable to manage their own use due to health reasons. |

---

### **Eligibility for Fee Remission**

A licence holder is eligible for a full remission of the fee under subsections 7(1) or 8(2) of the *Cannabis Fees Order* **if both of the following conditions are met**:

#### **1. Sales Must Be Conducted Only to Eligible Recipients**
All cannabis sold during the applicable fiscal year under the relevant licence (as listed in **Column 1 of Schedule 2 to the Cannabis Fees Order**) must have been:
- Sold **in Canada** to a **client** or a **named responsible adult**, or  
- Sold to a person to whom an **exemption under section 140 of the Cannabis Act** was granted for a **specific medical purpose**, and this exemption applies to the **type of cannabis or class of cannabis** sold.

> *Note:* This means that sales to non-medical or international buyers are not eligible for remission.

#### **2. Submission Requirements to the Minister**

Depending on when the fiscal year ends relative to the coming into force of the Order, the licence holder must submit different documentation:

---

#### **Case 1: Fiscal Year Ends BEFORE the Order Comes Into Force**

If the fiscal year ends prior to January 22, 2020, the holder must submit:

1. **A written request** signed by the holder, requesting remission of the fee.
2. **A statement of cannabis revenue** for that fiscal year, including:
   - Amount received from sales of cannabis **in Canada**
   - Amount paid for the purchase of cannabis used in those sales
3. **A supporting document** confirming that:
   - All sales were made to a person who was either:
     - A client or named responsible adult **at the time of sale**, or  
     - To whom a medical exemption under section 140 of the Cannabis Act was granted **at the time of sale**, and that the exemption was relevant to the cannabis or class of cannabis sold.

> ⏱️ **Deadline:** The request and supporting documents must be submitted **within 30 days after the Order comes into force** (i.e., by February 22, 2020).

---

#### **Case 2: Fiscal Year Ends ON OR AFTER the Order Comes Into Force**

For fiscal years starting on or after January 22, 2020, the holder must submit:

1. **A written request** signed by the holder, indicating their intention to sell **all cannabis during the fiscal year only** to:
   - Clients, named responsible adults, or individuals with a medical exemption under section 140 (as defined above).
2. **A statement of cannabis revenue** for the fiscal year, including:
   - Revenue from sales in Canada
   - Cost of purchases used to calculate revenue
3. **A supporting document** confirming that:
   - All sales were made to a person who was:
     - A client or named responsible adult **at the time of sale**, or  
     - To whom a medical exemption under section 140 was granted **at the time of sale**, and applicable to the cannabis sold.

> ⏱️ **Deadline for Statement:** The revenue statement and supporting documentation must be submitted **not later than April 30 of the year following the fiscal year** (e.g., for a fiscal year ending December 31, 2020, the statement must be submitted by April 30, 2021).

---

### **Recordkeeping and Audit Requirements**

To ensure transparency and compliance, the licence holder must:

- **Retain records for at least seven years** after the date the documents are prepared.
- The records must be stored in a format that allows for **timely audit** by the Minister.

**Required Records Include:**
1. Documents showing:
   - Amount received from cannabis sales (in Canada)
   - Amount paid for the purchase of cannabis
   - Name of the person from whom cannabis was purchased
2. Documents confirming that:
   - Every sale was made to a **client**, **named responsible adult**, or to a person with a valid **section 140 medical exemption** at the time of sale, and that the exemption was relevant to the specific cannabis or class sold.

> *Note:* These records are not just for internal use—they are critical for potential audits and verification by the Minister.

---

### **Key Provisions and Implications**

| Feature | Significance |
|--------|--------------|
| **Fee Remission** | The licence holder is **not required to pay** the fee under subsections 7(1) or 8(2) of the *Cannabis Fees Order* if the above conditions are met. |
| **Exclusivity Requirement** | Sales must be **exclusively** to medical users or those with valid exemptions. No sales to general consumers, international buyers, or non-medical users are permitted. |
| **Time Sensitivity** | Submission deadlines are strict and vary by fiscal year timing. Early compliance is critical. |
| **Documentation Burden** | Holders must maintain detailed, verifiable records for seven years—this increases administrative responsibility. |
| **Audit Readiness** | The requirement for audit-ready records ensures accountability and helps prevent fraud or misuse. |

---

### **Why This Regulation Exists**

- Supports the **medical access model** of cannabis by reducing financial barriers for licensed operators who serve only medically authorized patients.
- Encourages **domestic, regulated distribution** of medical cannabis.
- Ensures that **only legitimate medical uses** are supported financially, aligning with the intent of the *Cannabis Act* to promote safe, accessible, and medically supervised cannabis use.
- Reinforces **transparency and accountability** through strict documentation and audit requirements.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Cannabis for Medical Purposes Remission Order* provides a **clear, structured pathway** for medical cannabis licence holders to avoid paying certain fees—provided they meet strict criteria regarding the **destination of sales** and **documentation**. It strengthens the integrity of the medical cannabis system by ensuring that financial incentives are tied directly to legitimate medical use, while also placing significant compliance obligations on licence holders to maintain accurate, auditable records.

This Order reflects a balance between **supporting medical access** and **ensuring regulatory oversight**, reinforcing public trust in the Canadian medical cannabis framework.
