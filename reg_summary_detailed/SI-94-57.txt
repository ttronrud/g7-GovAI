**Regulation Summary:**

**Regulation Title:** Authorization for the Minister of Transport to Prescribe Charges for Pleasure Craft Plate Issuance and Compliance Notices  
**Source:** S.C. 1991, c. 24, s. 6 (Statutory Citation)  
**Governing Authority:** His Excellency the Governor General in Council  
**Recommendation Basis:** Minister of Transport and the Treasury Board  
**Legal Authority:** Paragraph 19(1)(b) of the *Financial Administration Act*  

---

**Purpose of the Regulation:**

This regulation authorizes the Minister of Transport to issue orders that establish specific charges to be collected from owners or operators of pleasure craft (such as motorboats, personal watercraft, and other non-commercial recreational vessels) under the following conditions:

1. **For the issuance of plates (license plates)** to pleasure craft, based on:
   - The **recommended safe limits of engine power**  
   - The **recommended gross load capacity**  

2. **For the issuance of compliance notices** related to:
   - Adherence to **construction standards** (e.g., safety, structural integrity, materials, and design requirements for vessels)

---

**Key Elements Explained:**

- **"Pleasure craft"** refers to recreational vessels used for personal or non-commercial purposes, not commercial or commercial-like operations (e.g., fishing boats used for income, or cargo transport).

- **"Recommended safe limits of engine power and gross load capacity"**:
  - These are technical thresholds established by the Minister of Transport (often in consultation with safety experts, marine engineers, or standards bodies) to ensure that vessels remain safe for operation.
  - Vessels exceeding these limits may be subject to higher fees or restrictions to prevent risks such as instability, overloading, or unsafe maneuvering.

- **"Charges to be paid"**:
  - The regulation gives the Minister the power to set fees for:
    - Applying for and receiving license plates (a regulatory requirement for legal operation on waterways).
    - Receiving notices that inform owners or operators about compliance with construction standards (e.g., if a vessel fails to meet safety requirements).

- **"Prescribe by order"**:
  - The Minister of Transport is not required to set these charges through legislation but can issue a formal *regulatory order* (a government rule) that specifies:
    - The exact amount of the fee.
    - The conditions under which the fee applies (e.g., based on engine horsepower or gross tonnage).
    - The types of vessels affected.
