**Detailed Summary of the Post Office Savings Bank Regulations**

---

### **1. Title and Citation**
These regulations are officially titled the **Post Office Savings Bank Regulations** and may be cited as such. They establish the operational framework, administrative procedures, and rules governing the activities of the **Post Office Savings Bank (the "Bank")**, a financial institution operated under the authority of the Canada Post Corporation.

---

### **2. Definitions of Key Terms**
The regulations define several key terms used throughout the document:

- **Account**: The official accounting record maintained at Head Office of all transactions involving a depositor and the interest earned on their deposits.
- **Act**: Refers to the *Post Office Act*, which provides the legal foundation for the Bank’s operations.
- **Bank**: The Post Office Savings Bank.
- **Deposit**: Any monetary contribution made by or on behalf of a depositor into the Bank.
- **Depositor**: A person who holds an account in the Bank.
- **Director**: The Director of Accounting, Canada Post Corporation — the primary administrator and controller of the Bank’s operations.
- **Head Office**: The central administrative office of the Bank located in Ottawa.
- **Pass-book**: A physical book issued to a depositor that records deposits, withdrawals, and interest earned.
- **Withdrawal**: The act of withdrawing money from a depositor’s account.

*Note: Several sections have been repealed (e.g., SOR/71-176, s. 1, 4, 5, 6, 10, 11; SOR/81-843, s. 2), meaning their provisions are no longer in force. These are marked accordingly in the summary.*

---

### **3. Administration and Control of the Bank**
The **Director of Accounting** is responsible for:
- Administering and controlling all operations, transactions, and activities of the Bank.
- Maintaining accurate and complete records of all accounts.

This includes overseeing account management, withdrawal procedures, record retention, and compliance with statutory requirements.

---

### **4. Account Closure and Unclaimed Balances**
The Director may:
- **Close any account upon demand** by the depositor or a person legally authorized to act on their behalf (subject to certain conditions).
- **Transfer unclaimed balances** of accounts with a balance **less than $25** to the **Receiver General of Canada** at the end of each fiscal year — *only if*:
  - No transaction has occurred for **30 years**.
  - No statement of account has been requested by or on behalf of the depositor.
- **Maintain indefinitely** accounts with a balance of **$25 or more**.
- Carry out any incidental operations related to these actions.

> ⚠️ *Note: This provision has been repealed (SOR/71-176, s. 4), so it is no longer applicable.*

---

### **5. Business Hours**
The Bank operates during the **same hours as the Canada Post Corporation**, with the possibility of **exceptional hours** being prescribed by the Director.

> ⚠️ *Note: This section has been repealed (SOR/71-176, s. 5).*

---

### **6. Types of Accounts Maintained**
The Bank maintains the following types of accounts:

| Account Type | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Individual Personal Accounts** | Opened in the name of individual depositors. |
| **Trust Accounts** | Held in the name of one or more persons *in trust* for a specified beneficiary or beneficiaries. |
| **Joint Accounts — All to Withdraw** | Two or more persons co-own the account; **all joint holders must jointly apply** for withdrawal. |
| **Joint Accounts — Any to Withdraw** | Two or more persons co-own the account; **any one holder may withdraw funds**. |
| **Societies’ Accounts** | For clubs, schools, churches, credit unions, cemeteries, estates, or charitable institutions. |

> ⚠️ *Note: The original structure of this section has been partially repealed or amended (SOR/71-176, s. 6).*

---

### **7. Withdrawal Requirements for Joint Accounts**

- For **"All to Withdraw"** accounts:  
  → **All joint depositors must jointly apply** for withdrawal.

- For **"Any to Withdraw"** accounts:  
  → **Any one of the joint holders** may apply.

---

### **8. Succession Upon Death of a Joint Depositor**

Upon the death of a joint depositor:
- Control of the account **automatically transfers to the surviving joint holder(s)**.
- This transfer requires:
  1. Production of the **pass-book**.
  2. Evidence of **death** of the deceased depositor (satisfactory to the Director).
  3. Proof of **identity of the survivor(s)**.

> ⚠️ *Note: This section is largely intact but may be subject to repeal or amendment.*

---

### **9. Record Retention and Destruction of Documents**

The Director may **destroy records** related to an account **only if**:
- The documents are **dated more than 15 years prior** to the date of destruction.
- The records are **no longer relevant** and contain no current transactions.

**Retention Rules**:
- For accounts with a balance **less than $25**:  
  → Signature declarations and signing authorities must be **retained until the balance is transferred** to the Receiver General (as per paragraph (b) of section 4).
- For accounts with a balance **$25 or more**:  
  → Signature declarations and signing authorities **must not be destroyed**.

> ⚠️ *Note: Section 6 has been repealed (SOR/71-176, s. 6), so this retention rule is no longer in effect.*

---

### **10. Name Changes**
- A depositor must **notify the Director immediately** of any change in name.
- The depositor must **submit evidence** of the name change as required by the Director.

---

### **11. Access to Pass-Books**
- A depositor may be **required to produce** their pass-book for inspection upon request by the Director.
- When closing an account, the depositor must **submit the pass-book** to the Director.

---

### **12. Lost Pass-Book Procedures**
If a pass-book is lost:
- The depositor must:
  1. **Notify the Director without delay**, providing:
     - Where and when the first and last deposits and withdrawals occurred.
     - As much information as possible about the account and the circumstances of the loss.
  2. **Sign an undertaking** to:
     - Return the pass-book if recovered.
     - Indemnify the Canada Post Corporation against misuse.

---

### **13. Conditions for Withdrawals**
Withdrawals are permitted only under the following conditions:
- **At Head Office only** (no withdrawals allowed at other locations).
- **On presentation of**:
  - The pass-book, or
  - Any other acceptable evidence of right to withdraw (as determined by the Director).

> ⚠️ *Note: This rule is still applicable, though the original section may have been revised.*

---

### **14. Account Closure**
An account is **closed only when the full balance is withdrawn**.

---

### **15. Withdrawal of Deposits After Death of a Depositor**

#### **(a) Spouse (if deposit ≤ $50)**  
A spouse may withdraw the deposit if:
- A signed request is submitted.
- The pass-book (or a declaration of loss + undertaking to surrender it) is provided.
- Proof of **death** and **marital relationship** is submitted.

#### **(b) Other Claimants (if deposit ≤ $500)**  
Any person (not spouse) may withdraw the deposit if:
- Signed request, pass-book (or loss declaration + undertaking), proof of death, and legal authority to claim are provided.
- An **authenticated or notarial copy** of the last will (if any) is submitted.
- A **written statement** confirming no intention to apply for:
  - Letters of Probate (if will exists),
  - Letters of Administration (if no will),
  - Act of Curatorship or Tutorship.
- A **statutory declaration and indemnity agreement** signed by:
  - All other beneficiaries named in the will, or
  - All other persons entitled to share in the estate under intestacy.
- A **bond of indemnity** with two sureties for **twice the amount** of the deposit and accrued interest (if deemed necessary by the Director).

#### **(c) Relatives (if deposit ≤ $2,500)**  
The wife, husband, child, father, mother, brother, or sister may withdraw the deposit if:
- All documents required in (a) to (g) are submitted.

#### **(d) Executor, Administrator, Trustee, Tutor, or Curator**  
These individuals may withdraw the deposit if they provide:
- Signed request.
- Pass-book or loss declaration + undertaking.
- Proof of death.
- Applicable documents (as specified in subsection 5), such as:
  - Probate of will or Letters of Administration.
  - Notarial copy of the will (if in Quebec form).
  - If deceased died outside Canada: probate or equivalent documents from a competent foreign authority.

> ✅ **Payment Method**:
> - In cases (a), (b), (c): Deposit paid by **cheque payable to the claimant**.
> - In case (d): Deposit paid by **cheque payable to the estate**.
