**Detailed Summary of the Safe Working Practices Regulations (Canada)**

---

### **Regulation Title and Scope**

The **Safe Working Practices Regulations** are a set of safety standards established under the *Canada Shipping Act* to ensure the safety and health of personnel engaged in maritime operations. These regulations apply to all work conducted in **working areas** associated with ships—whether in Canadian waters or on Canadian-flagged vessels operating abroad.

The regulations cover a broad range of activities, including maintenance, repair, loading/unloading, and operations involving machinery, lifting equipment, hot work, confined spaces, and hazardous materials.

---

## **Key Definitions**

To ensure clarity and consistency, several key terms are defined:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Canada Shipping Act*, the foundational legislation under which these regulations operate. |
| **Board** | The *Board of Steamship Inspection*, established under Section 369 of the Act, responsible for overseeing compliance with safety standards. |
| **Hot Work** | Any activity involving flame or spark production (e.g., welding, grinding, drilling, burning). |
| **Inspector** | A person appointed by the Act or designated under Section 91 to inspect ships and ensure compliance. |
| **Marine Chemist** | A qualified professional with formal education (chemical engineering or chemistry major) and at least three years of relevant experience (minimum 150 supervised hours) in testing and inspecting tank vessels for gas hazards. |
| **Owner** | The individual or entity that has general management and control over a working area, structure, machinery, or equipment. |
| **Qualified Person** | For work requiring a license or certificate: the holder of such authority. For other work: a person deemed by employer to have the necessary knowledge and experience to perform the task safely. |
| **Scaffold** | A working platform supported from below. |
| **Scaffolding** | The structural support system for scaffolds. |
| **Stage** | A working platform supported from above (e.g., a suspended platform). |
| **Working Area** | Includes: (1) any area where work is being performed on board a ship; (2) adjacent areas for maintenance/repair personnel; and (3) onshore areas within reach of cranes/derricks during loading/unloading. *Excludes* sheds, warehouses, or parts of wharfs beyond mooring lines. |

## **Core Obligations and Provisions**

### **1. Employer Responsibilities**

Every employer must:
- Ensure that work is carried out in a manner that does **not endanger** the safety or health of employees.
- Implement and follow **reasonable procedures and techniques** to prevent or reduce the risk of employment injury.
- Ensure full compliance with the **safe working practices** outlined in these regulations.

> *Note: This does not relieve employers of duties imposed by Section 4 of the Canada Shipping Act.*

### **2. Owner Responsibilities**

Every owner (manager or operator) of a working area, structure, machinery, or equipment must:
- Maintain the working area, structure, or equipment in a **safe condition**.
- Ensure compliance with these regulations.

### **3. Qualified Person Requirements**

Every working area must have a **qualified person in charge** at all times. Additionally:
- The person in charge must conduct **periodic inspections** of all working areas, structures, and equipment to verify ongoing safety.

### **4. Employee Responsibilities**

All employees must:
- Take **reasonable and necessary precautions** for their own and others’ safety.
- Use **personal protective equipment (PPE)** provided by the employer or required by regulation.

### **5. Prohibitions on Unsafe Use of Equipment**

- No person may use a working area, structure, or equipment for any purpose other than its intended use.
- Equipment reassembled after dismantling must be **examined and certified by a qualified person** before use.
- **Tampering with safety devices** (e.g., guards, alarms) is strictly prohibited.
- **Emergency exits must not be obstructed** or rendered ineffective.

### **6. Reporting Unsafe Conditions and Accidents**

- Any person observing an unsafe condition must **immediately report it** to the person in charge.
- Corrections to unsafe conditions must be made **only by a qualified person**.
- In emergencies, **any person may take necessary actions** to prevent loss of life or injury.
- Employers must verify **periodically** that isolated workers have not been involved in accidents.
- All **accidents or injuries** must be reported immediately to the person in charge.

### **7. Restrictions on Alcohol, Drugs, and Disabilities**

- No person may work in a working area if their ability to perform duties is **impaired by alcohol or drugs**.
- A person with a **disability** may not be assigned to work if the nature or location of the work poses a risk to themselves or others.

### **8. Operation of Lifting Equipment**

Operators of lifting appliances (cranes, hoists, mobile equipment) must:
- Be a **qualified person**.
- Comply with **traffic laws** in the working area.
- Follow all **signs and notices** posted by the owner or employer.

**Requirements for lifting appliances:**
- A **permanent notice** showing the **safe working load** must be affixed.
- No person may be on the equipment during operation unless:
  - Safe accommodation is available, or
  - Essential maintenance/repair is being done with safe access.
- Mobile equipment must have **guards** to protect operators from falling objects or shifting loads (unless impractical).
- Equipment must be **secured when unattended** to prevent movement.

### **9. Ventilation and Internal Combustion Engines**

- All enclosed working areas using internal combustion engines must be **ventilated** so that carbon monoxide (CO) levels do **not exceed 50 ppm**.
- At least **one other person must be present** at the entrance when operating such equipment.
- Refueling is prohibited in:
  - The ship’s hold,
  - When the engine is running,
  - When there is any ignition source nearby.

**Permitted refueling in holds/tanks:**
- Only personnel directly involved in refueling are allowed inside.
- One attendant must have a **suitable fire extinguisher** ready.
- Only the **minimum required fuel** should be taken into the space at once.
- For liquefied gas: refueling must involve **cylinder replacement**, not transfer.
- Fuel must **not be transferred** into containers other than the fuel tank.

### **10. Safety Equipment and Lighting**

- Powered mobile equipment must have:
  - An **effective audible warning device**.
  - **Sufficient lighting** for safe operation in darkness or dim conditions.

### **11. Hatch and Opening Safety**

- All **hatches or openings leading to compartments over 1.5 meters deep** must be:
  - Secured with a **900 mm high fence or cover**, or
  - Fitted with a **760 mm coaming** (edge protection).
- Openings leading to compartments **1.5 meters or less deep** must be covered, fenced, or marked to prevent injury or loss of life.

**Hatch Components:**
- Be **permanently marked** to indicate deck, hatch, and section.
- Must only be fitted in the **specific location** indicated by the markings.
- When removed, must be placed **clear of working areas** and securely stowed.
- If not stowable between decks, must be moved to the **upper deck** and secured.

**Safety Rule:** Personnel must **stand clear** of overhead openings during hatch removal or replacement.

- A **tarpaulin or hatch cover** may only be spread over a hatch if all beams, covers, and plugs are **securely fitted**.

### **12. Entry into Confined Spaces**

- No person may enter a **pump room, cofferdam, ballast tank, oil tank, or similar compartment** without wearing a **breathing apparatus**.

**Exceptions:**
- If the compartment:
  - Has **not contained a vaporizing hazardous substance** and has been **ventilated to the satisfaction of a qualified person**, OR
  - Has contained a hazardous gas and has been:
    - **Gas-freed** and tested by a **marine chemist**, OR
    - Tested by a **qualified person** trained in gas testing (with at least 3 years of experience and 150 supervised hours in tank inspections), and found safe.

**Requirements for Entry:**
- A **certificate or statement** must be completed and made available to all personnel entering.
- The certificate must include:
  - Name of the ship,
  - Any special precautions,
  - Subsequent testing requirements.
- Must be **signed by the person responsible** for ventilation or testing.
- **Effective ventilation must continue** during the work period.

**Attendance Rule:**  
- A **second person must remain at the entrance** at all times while someone is inside the compartment.
- The attendant must **remain until the person exits**, unless an emergency occurs.

**Before Sealing:**  
- The person in charge must confirm that **no one is inside** the compartment before sealing it.
