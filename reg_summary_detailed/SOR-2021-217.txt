## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Canadian Pork Promotion-Research Levies Order (SOR/2020-282)**

### üìå **Background and Purpose**

This regulation establishes a **levy system** to fund the **promotion and research activities** of the **Canadian Pork Promotion-Research Agency (CPPRA)**, which was created under the *Farm Products Agencies Act*.

- The CPPRA was established by proclamation under **subsection 39(1)b** of the Farm Products Agencies Act.
- It is empowered to implement a **promotion and research plan** to support the Canadian pork industry.
- This Order is a **class of agency order** authorized under **paragraph 7(1)(d)d** of the Farm Products Agencies Act, as permitted by **section 2 of the Agencies‚Äô Orders and Regulations Approval Act**.
- The Order was submitted to the **National Farm Products Council (NFPC)** for review and approval, which it granted after determining that the levies are necessary for effective implementation of the promotion and research plan.

> ‚úÖ **Objective**: To generate revenue to fund national-level pork promotion (e.g., marketing, consumer education) and research initiatives that benefit Canadian pork producers, processors, and exporters.

## üí∞ **Levy Structure and Payment Obligations**

### 1. **Interprovincial Trade (Hogs Sold Between Provinces)**

| Scenario | Levy Amount |
|--------|-------------|
| A seller sells hogs **produced in a province listed in column 1 of the table** | Levy = amount set in **column 2** (per hog) |
| A seller sells hogs **produced in Quebec** | **$1.274 per 100 kg dressed weight** (regardless of destination) |

> üí° *The levy is based on the province of origin, not the province of sale.*

### 2. **Producers Who Process Hogs in Another Province**

| Scenario | Levy Amount |
|--------|-------------|
| Producer grows hogs in a province (column 1), processes them elsewhere | Levy = amount in **column 2** (based on province of origin) |
| Producer grows hogs in **Quebec**, processes elsewhere | Levy = **$1.274 per 100 kg dressed weight** |

> üîç *The levy is tied to where the hogs were produced, not where they are processed.*

---

### 3. **Imported Hogs and Pork Products**

- Every **importer** must pay a levy per imported hog or pork product.
- The levy amount is specified in **Schedule 1**.
- The levy is calculated based on:
  - The **customs tariff item number** (from Schedule 1.1 or 2)
  - The **weight (in kg)** of the product

> üìå **Formula**:  
> Levy (in cents per kg) = Tariff rate (from Schedule 2) √ó Weight (in kg)

> ‚úÖ **Payment Deadline**: Within **21 days** of receiving the invoice from the Agency.

> üìÑ **Invoice Requirements**:
> - Importer‚Äôs name and mailing address  
> - Number of imported hogs (by tariff item) or pork products (by tariff item)  
> - Total levy amount

## üìù **Reporting and Recordkeeping Requirements**

### 1. **Who Must Report?**
- Purchasers (who deduct levies)
- Processors (who pay levies on behalf of producers)
- Importers

### 2. **What Must Be Reported?**

All levies must be accompanied by a **statement** that includes:

| Case | Required Information |
|------|------------------------|
| **Hogs from provinces (non-Quebec)** | - Number of hogs purchased or processed <br> - Total levy amount <br> - Name, address, phone, email of purchaser/processor and seller/producer |
| **Hogs from Quebec** | - Total weight in kilograms <br> - Total levy amount <br> - Name, address, phone, email of purchaser/processor and seller/producer |

> ‚úÖ These statements must be submitted **through the collector** in the province where the hogs were produced.

---

### 3. **Recordkeeping Obligations**

All parties involved must:

- Keep **records** related to:
  - Sale or processing of hogs
  - Invoices
  - Levy deductions or payments
- Maintain records for **seven years** after the sale or processing date
- Make records available upon **request by the Agency or collector**

## üö® **Enforcement and Liability Provisions**

### 1. **Failure to Deduct or Pay Levies**

| Failure | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| Purchaser fails to deduct levy from purchase price | **Seller must pay the levy directly** to the Agency |
| Processor fails to pay levy on behalf of producer | **Producer must pay the levy directly** to the Agency |

> ‚öñÔ∏è **Joint and Several Liability**:
> - If **both** the purchaser and seller fail to comply (in the case of non-deduction), they are **jointly and severally liable** (i.e., each can be held responsible for the full amount).
> - Similarly, if **both** the processor and producer fail to comply (in processing), they are **jointly and severally liable**.

> üí° This ensures that levies are collected regardless of which party fails to act.
