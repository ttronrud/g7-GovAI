## ğŸ“Š 4. **Quota System (Core Mechanism)**

### What is a Quota?
- A **quota** is the number of pounds of turkey meat a producer is entitled to market in **interprovincial or export trade** during a specified period.
- Quotas are assigned to producers or classes of producers to prevent oversupply and ensure market stability.

### How Quotas Are Assigned:
- The CTMA establishes a **quota system** in each province.
- Quotas are assigned based on the **total market capacity** of that province, which includes:
  1. Turkey meat produced and marketed in **interprovincial trade** via the Agency or provincial boards.
  2. Turkey meat produced and marketed in **intraprovincial trade** via provincial boards or commodity boards.
  3. Turkey meat **anticipated** to be marketed (not yet authorized) in the same year.

> The total of these three components must **equal the maximum allowable volume** for that province as set out in **Section 3 of the Plan** (a detailed table in the schedule).

### Key Principles in Quota Assignment:
- **No increase in total marketable turkey volume** beyond the approved limit (as per Section 3 of the Plan) without justification.
- The Agency **must consider** the following factors before increasing quotas:
  - **Comparative advantage** in production (which province produces more efficiently)
  - Changes in **market demand**
  - Failures by producers to market their quota
  - Feasibility of increased production
  - Existing **production and storage capacity**
  - **Transportation costs** from different sources

> â— **No order or regulation may be made** that increases total marketable turkey beyond the approved annual limit unless the Agency has **demonstrated significant changes** in market size or conditions.

## ğŸ›¡ï¸ 6. **Price Protection and Anti-Price Dumping Rules**

To prevent unfair competition and price manipulation, the CTMA enforces **price controls** in both regulated and unregulated areas.

### A. **In the Regulated Area (from regulated provinces to other provinces)**:
- No person may market turkeys in a province **other than the province of origin** at a price **lower than**:
  1. The price charged at or around the same time for turkeys of **equivalent variety, class, or grade** in the **province of origin**  
  2. Plus **reasonable transportation costs** to the market

> This prevents **price dumping** and protects producers from undercutting prices in other regions.

### B. **From Regulated Area to Unregulated Area**:
- The same rule applies: turkeys shipped from regulated provinces into unregulated areas cannot be sold at a price below:
  - The price in the province of origin
  - Plus reasonable transportation costs

> This ensures **fair pricing across regional boundaries** and protects producers from exploitation.
