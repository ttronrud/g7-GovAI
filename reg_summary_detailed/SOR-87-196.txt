**Detailed Summary of the *Canadian Arsenals Limited Pension Protection Regulations***  
*(S.C. 1986, c. 20)*

---

### **1. Background and Purpose**

The *Canadian Arsenals Limited Pension Protection Regulations* were enacted by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Supply and Services** and the **Treasury Board**, under **Section 12 of the Canadian Arsenals Limited Divestiture Authorization Act** (*Act*).

The primary purpose of these regulations is to **ensure continuity and fairness in pension benefits** for employees of *Canadian Arsenals Limited* during the process of divestiture — that is, the transfer or sale of the company's assets and operations — without disrupting their retirement entitlements.

This regulatory framework specifically addresses how pension rights are calculated and recognized for employees who transition from employment with *Canadian Arsenals Limited* (or its amalgamated successor) into the broader public service or other employment contexts.

---

### **2. Citation and Scope**

- **Title**: These Regulations may be cited as the *Canadian Arsenals Limited Pension Protection Regulations*.
- **Applicable Legislation**: The regulations apply to individuals who make an election under **paragraph 12(1)(e) of the Act**.
- **Applicable Acts**: All provisions of the following federal pension legislation apply to such individuals:
  - *Public Service Superannuation Act* (PSSA)
  - *Supplementary Retirement Benefits Act* (SRBA)
- **Exception**: These provisions are **modified or adapted** in accordance with **Sections 4 and 5** of the Act to reflect the unique circumstances of employees transitioning from Canadian Arsenals Limited.

---

### **3. Key Adaptations to the Public Service Superannuation Act (PSSA)**

The regulations adapt specific provisions of the PSSA to ensure that service with *Canadian Arsenals Limited* or its amalgamated corporation is treated as equivalent to public service for pension purposes.

#### **Adaptation of Subparagraphs 9(7)(b)(i) and (9(7)(b)(ii)) of the PSSA**
- **Original Provision**: These subparagraphs determine the year in which a person ceases to be employed in the Public Service for pension calculation purposes.
- **Change Introduced**:
  - The year in which a person **ceases to be employed in the Public Service** is now considered to be the **same year** they **cease to be employed by Canadian Arsenals Limited or its amalgamated corporation**.
- **Rationale**: This ensures that the pension calculation period does not jump or misalign due to the timing of transition between public service and private-sector employment (or vice versa), especially when employees move from a non-public service employer to the public service.

#### **Adaptation of Sections 11 and 12 of the PSSA**
These sections govern pensionable service and retirement benefits.

- **Key Changes**:
  1. **Pensionable Service Periods**:
     - The number of years of pensionable service and any periods of employment are **expanded to include**:
       - Any service or employment with the **Corporation or the amalgamated corporation** during the period:
         - Beginning on the day the person **ceases to be employed in the Public Service**, and
         - Ending on the day **immediately before** the person **ceases to be employed by the Corporation or amalgamated corporation**.
     - This ensures that service in the Canadian Arsenals Limited entity is counted as part of the pensionable service record, even if it occurs after leaving public service.
  2. **Cessation Date for Service**:
     - For all purposes **except subsection 11(4)**, the **day on which a person ceases to be employed in the Public Service** is considered to be the **same day** they cease to be employed by the Corporation or amalgamated corporation.
     - This alignment prevents discrepancies in pension eligibility and retirement date calculations.

> ⚠️ *Exception*: Subsection 11(4) remains unaffected — it continues to apply as originally written.

---

### **4. Key Adaptations to the Supplementary Retirement Benefits Act (SRBA)**

The SRBA provides for supplementary retirement benefits, including contributions to a retirement account. These adaptations ensure that the transition from Canadian Arsenals Limited to public service is treated consistently.

#### **Adaptation of Subsection 4(3) of the SRBA**
- **Original Provision**: Defines the retirement year or month for pension purposes.
- **Change Introduced**:
  - The **retirement year or month** of a person is now considered to be:
    - **1986**, or
    - **May 1986**, depending on the individual’s situation.
- **Rationale**: This adjustment aligns retirement dates with the **timing of the divestiture process**, which was finalized in 1986. It ensures that employees are treated fairly under the SRBA, regardless of whether they left the Corporation earlier or later in the year.

#### **Adaptation of Subparagraphs 6(2)(b)(i) and (ii) of the SRBA**
- **Original Provision**: Defines the year in which a person ceases to contribute to the Supplementary Retirement Benefits Account.
- **Change Introduced**:
  - The year in which a person ceases to contribute to the account is now considered to be:
    - The year in which they **cease to be employed by the Corporation or the amalgamated corporation**.
- **Rationale**: This ensures that contribution periods are accurately tied to employment with Canadian Arsenals Limited, preventing misclassification of contribution years and protecting the employee’s entitlement to benefits.

---

### **5. Effective Date and Applicability**

- **Effective Date**: The regulations come into force on **May 10, 1986**.
- **Publication Note**: This date is **before** the official publication of the regulations in the *Canada Gazette*, which is standard practice in Canadian regulatory law — the regulations are effective upon enactment and implementation, even before formal publication.

---

### **6. Significance and Impact**

These regulations serve as a **critical bridge** between the public service pension system and the private-sector employment of Canadian Arsenals Limited. They ensure that:

- Employees who transitioned from the Corporation to the public service (or vice versa) are not disadvantaged in terms of pension eligibility.
- Service periods with Canadian Arsenals Limited are **recognized and counted** as valid pensionable service.
- Retirement dates and contribution periods are **aligned with the actual employment timeline** of the employee.
- The **divestiture process does not undermine** the pension security of employees.

This legal framework reflects a commitment to **fairness, consistency, and continuity** in retirement benefits during organizational change.

---

### **7. Conclusion**

The *Canadian Arsenals Limited Pension Protection Regulations* are a targeted and practical piece of legislation designed to protect the pension rights of employees during a major corporate restructuring. By adapting key provisions of the *Public Service Superannuation Act* and the *Supplementary Retirement Benefits Act*, the regulations ensure that service with Canadian Arsenals Limited is treated as equivalent to public service for pension purposes — particularly in terms of:

- Cessation dates
- Service period inclusion
- Retirement years and contribution timelines

These adaptations provide **legal clarity and stability**, safeguarding employees from potential benefit erosion due to the divestiture of Canadian Arsenals Limited.

---

**Final Note**: These regulations are a historical example of how Canadian government institutions use targeted regulatory tools to maintain employee benefits during privatization or restructuring events. They remain relevant in discussions about pension portability and transitional fairness in public-private sector transitions.
