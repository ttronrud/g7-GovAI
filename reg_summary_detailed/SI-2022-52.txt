**Regulation Summary:**

**Title:**  
Designation of the Minister of Industry as the Minister for the Purposes of the Civil Lunar Gateway Agreement Implementation Act

**Legal Authority:**  
The designation is made under **Section 5** of the *Civil Lunar Gateway Agreement Implementation Act* (S.C. 2022, c. 10), which empowers the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister) to appoint a designated Minister to act on behalf of the Government of Canada in relation to the implementation of the Civil Lunar Gateway Agreement.

**Key Provisions:**

1. **Designation of the Minister of Industry:**  
   Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, designates the **Minister of Industry** as the official Minister responsible for the purposes of the *Civil Lunar Gateway Agreement Implementation Act*. This means that the Minister of Industry has the legal authority and responsibility to perform the duties, functions, and powers conferred by the Act.

2. **Effective Date:**  
   The designation takes effect on **October 26, 2022**. This date marks the beginning of the Minister of Industry’s formal role in overseeing the implementation of the Act.

3. **Legal Basis and Authority:**  
   The power to designate a Minister under Section 5 of the Act is derived from the structure of the legislation, which establishes a clear chain of accountability and governance for the implementation of Canada’s participation in the international Civil Lunar Gateway (CLG) initiative. The Governor General in Council acts as the formal executive authority, exercising the powers granted by Parliament through the Act, and does so on the advice of the Prime Minister.

4. **Purpose of the Designation:**  
   The Minister of Industry is designated because the Civil Lunar Gateway Agreement involves significant aspects of industrial development, space technology, innovation, and economic participation. The Minister of Industry is well-positioned to oversee the development of Canadian industries, support private-sector involvement, manage research and development partnerships, and ensure alignment with national innovation and economic strategy.

5. **Scope of Responsibilities:**  
   As the designated Minister, the Minister of Industry will be responsible for:
   - Coordinating Canada’s participation in the international Civil Lunar Gateway program;
   - Ensuring alignment with Canada’s national space policy and science and technology objectives;
   - Facilitating collaboration between government agencies, academic institutions, and private companies;
   - Overseeing the allocation of public funds and resources for space-related projects;
   - Promoting the development of Canadian space capabilities and workforce readiness;
   - Reporting to Parliament on progress and challenges in implementation.

6. **Legal and Institutional Significance:**  
   This designation formalizes the role of the Minister of Industry within the framework of international space cooperation. It reflects Canada’s commitment to active, structured, and accountable engagement in the Artemis program and the broader lunar exploration initiative led by NASA and international partners.

7. **Governance and Accountability:**  
   The designation ensures that Canada’s involvement in the Civil Lunar Gateway is managed through a clear, accountable, and policy-aligned governmental structure. The Minister of Industry, as a member of the King’s Privy Council for Canada, is a senior official with the necessary authority and standing to represent Canada in intergovernmental and international space forums.

**Conclusion:**  
This regulation establishes the Minister of Industry as the official point of responsibility for implementing the *Civil Lunar Gateway Agreement Implementation Act*. It reflects Canada’s strategic investment in space exploration and technological advancement, with a focus on leveraging domestic industry, innovation, and public-private partnerships. The effective date of October 26, 2022, ensures that Canada’s role in the lunar initiative is promptly and formally operationalized within the national governance framework.
