**Detailed Summary of the Ontario Soya-Bean Marketing Levies Order (SOR/80-183, 1980)**

---

### **Overview**
The *Ontario Soya-Bean Marketing Levies Order*, made under **Section 4 of the Ontario Soya-Bean Order** and published in the *Canada Gazette, Part II*, page 645 on May 3, 1980, establishes a regulatory framework for the **fixing, imposition, and collection of marketing levies** on soya-beans produced in Ontario. The purpose of this Order is to ensure that producers contribute to the costs associated with the **marketing of Ontario-grown soya-beans in interprovincial and export markets**.

This Order is part of a broader system governed by the *Farm Products Marketing Act* of Ontario, which enables the establishment of commodity marketing boards to manage the marketing and pricing of agricultural products.

---

### **Key Definitions**

- **Act**: The *Farm Products Marketing Act of Ontario* – the enabling legislation that authorizes the creation and operation of commodity marketing boards.
  
- **Commodity Board**: The *Ontario Soya-Bean Growers’ Marketing Board* – the statutory body responsible for administering the marketing of soya-beans in Ontario. It is established under the Act and has the authority to set marketing policies, collect levies, and manage the marketing plan.

- **Plan**: A marketing plan developed and periodically amended under the Act and any associated regulations. This plan outlines the rules, procedures, and objectives for the marketing of soya-beans, including pricing, distribution, and marketing support mechanisms.

- **Producer**: Any individual or entity engaged in the actual production of soya-beans within the Province of Ontario.

- **Soya-beans**: Refers specifically to soya-beans grown and produced in Ontario.

---

### **Scope of Application**

- Applies **only** to:
  - The **marketing** of soya-beans in **interprovincial trade** (i.e., sale to other provinces) and **export trade** (i.e., sale to foreign buyers).
  - Persons and property **located within the Province of Ontario**.

> *Note*: The levy does **not apply** to the internal use, processing, or local sale of soya-beans within Ontario.

---

### **Levy Structure**

- **Rate of Levy**:  
  Every producer must pay a levy of **$1.00 per tonne** of soya-beans sold by them or on their behalf.

- **Payment Requirement**:  
  The levy is **in addition to licence fees** that may be required under the marketing plan.

> This levy is a **direct financial contribution** from producers to the Commodity Board to help cover the costs of marketing, promotion, transportation, and market access for Ontario soya-beans.

---

### **Payment Deadline and Method**

- All levies must be paid to the **Commodity Board’s office at 175 Keil Drive South, Chatham, Ontario**.
- Payment is due **not later than the 15th day of the month following the month in which the soya-beans were sold**.

> For example:
> - If soya-beans are sold in June 1980, the levy must be paid by **July 15, 1980**.

This creates a **clear and predictable timeline** for levy collection, ensuring timely revenue flow to the Board to fund marketing activities.

---

### **Purpose of the Levy**

The primary objectives of the levy are:

1. To **cover the costs** of marketing Ontario soya-beans in both domestic (interprovincial) and international markets.
2. To support **market development**, including:
   - Promotion and branding of Ontario soya-beans.
   - Participation in trade fairs and export programs.
   - Market research and price information systems.
3. To ensure **fair and equitable access** to markets for producers, especially small and medium-sized farms.
4. To provide a **stable and predictable funding mechanism** for the marketing of a key agricultural commodity.

---

### **Legal Authority and Citation**

- The Order is made **pursuant to Section 4 of the Ontario Soya-Bean Order**, which grants the Commodity Board the power to establish and regulate levies.
- The Order may be cited as: **The Ontario Soya-Bean Marketing Levies Order**.
- It is a **regulatory instrument** issued under the *Farm Products Marketing Act*, ensuring compliance with provincial agricultural policy and market development goals.

---

### **Significance and Context (Historical Background)**

- This Order was enacted in **1980**, during a period of growing emphasis on organized marketing for Ontario’s agricultural products.
- At the time, soya-beans were becoming increasingly important in Canadian agriculture, with rising demand in food processing and animal feed industries.
- The establishment of a levy system helped ensure that producers shared the financial burden of marketing their products beyond local markets, particularly in export channels where access and visibility are critical.

---

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Ontario Soya-Bean Marketing Levies Order* is a foundational regulatory measure that ensures equitable and sustainable marketing of Ontario-grown soya-beans. By imposing a simple, transparent, and time-bound levy, it enables the Ontario Soya-Bean Growers’ Marketing Board to fund essential marketing activities that enhance the competitiveness and visibility of Ontario soya-beans in national and international markets. It reflects a balanced approach to agricultural policy—supporting producers while ensuring market access and economic viability for the entire industry.
