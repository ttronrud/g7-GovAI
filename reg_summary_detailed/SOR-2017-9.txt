### ðŸ”¹ **3. Application Fees by Type and Schedule**

Fees are structured based on the **type of application** and the **nature of the active ingredient** or component involved.

#### ðŸ“Œ **Schedule 1: General Pest Control Products (Non-Semiochemical, Non-Microbial)**
- Applies to applications involving **non-microbial, non-semiochemical** active ingredients.
- **Fee Structure**:
  - The fee is set in **column 2** of Schedule 1 for each component listed in column 1.
  - If the application includes **multiple components**, the total fee is the **sum** of all applicable fees.

> Example: If an application includes two components (e.g., insecticide and fungicide), the total fee is the sum of the fees for each.

#### ðŸ“Œ **Schedule 2: Semiochemicals and Microbial Agents**
- Applies to applications involving **semiochemicals** (e.g., pheromones) or **microbial agents** (e.g., bacteria, fungi).
- **Fee Structure**:
  - Fee is set in **column 2** per type of application (e.g., registration, amendment).
  - If **multiple types of applications** are filed for the **same product at the same time**, the fee is the **highest** of the applicable fees in column 2.

> This prevents overcharging and ensures fairness when multiple applications are submitted together.

#### ðŸ“Œ **Schedule 3: Special or Non-Standard Applications**
- Includes applications like research authorizations, specific use authorizations, and export-only registrations.
- **Fee Structure**:
  - Fee is set per type of application in column 2.
  - If multiple types are filed simultaneously, the fee is the **highest** of the applicable fees.

> This schedule covers niche or specialized applications not covered in Schedules 1 or 2.
