### 1. **Definition of "Distributing Cooperative" (Subsection 2(1) of the CCA)**

A **distributing cooperative** is defined as:

#### ‚úÖ Case 1: Reporting Issuer
A cooperative that is a **"reporting issuer"** under any legislation listed in **Column 2 of Schedule 4** of the CCA (e.g., securities laws of provinces or foreign jurisdictions).

#### ‚úÖ Case 2: Non-Reporting Issuer
If a cooperative is **not** a reporting issuer, it is still a distributing cooperative if:
- It has filed a **prospectus or registration statement** under **provincial legislation** or **foreign jurisdiction laws**, and
- Any of its securities are **listed and traded** on a stock exchange **in or outside Canada**, **OR**
- It is involved in, formed for, results from, or continues after:
  - An **amalgamation**
  - A **reorganization**
  - An **arrangement**
  - A **statutory procedure**
  ‚Ä¶where **one of the participating entities is a cooperative** that meets the criteria in Case 1 or Case 2 above.

#### ‚ùå Exemption from Definition
A cooperative that is subject to a **provincial securities exemption** or an **order from a provincial securities regulator** stating it is **not a reporting issuer** is **not considered a distributing cooperative** for purposes of this definition.

> üí° *Purpose*: To identify cooperatives that are publicly traded or subject to significant disclosure obligations, ensuring transparency and regulatory oversight.

### 3. **Consent and Electronic Delivery (Section 361.3 of the CCA)**

#### ‚úÖ Written Consent Required
- Under **paragraph 361.3(2)(a)**: All **consent** must be in **writing**.

#### ‚úÖ Electronic Delivery (Section 361.3(2)(b))  
A notice, document, or information **not required to be sent to a specific place** may be delivered electronically via:
- A **generally accessible electronic source** (e.g., a website)
- **Providing written notice** to the addressee of:
  - The **availability**
  - The **location** of the electronic document

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Condition*: The document must be **posted or made available** on a public website or similar platform.

#### ‚úÖ Revocation of Consent
- Under **subsection 361.3(3)**: An addressee may **revoke consent in writing** at any time.

#### ‚úÖ Concurrent Delivery to Multiple Addressees
- Under **paragraphs 361.4(b) and 361.5(2)(b)**: When providing information to **multiple addressees**, it must be delivered **concurrently**, regardless of method.

#### ‚úÖ When an Electronic Document is Considered "Provided" or "Received"
- **Provided**: When the document **leaves the originator‚Äôs or a third party‚Äôs information system**.
- **Received**:
  - If delivered to the **designated information system** of the addressee ‚Äî when it **enters** that system.
  - If posted online ‚Äî when the **notice of availability** is received by the addressee, or when that notice **enters** the designated system (if sent electronically).

> üí° *Purpose*: To modernize regulatory communication and allow flexibility in digital delivery while preserving accountability and traceability.

### 6. **Cooperative Name Prohibitions (Section 23 of the CCA)**

A cooperative name is **prohibited** if it causes **confusion** with:

#### ‚úÖ Confusion with Trademark or Official Mark
- If the name is **identical** to the trademark or official mark, or
- If the **use of both names** leads the public to believe they are **one business**, even if the nature of the business is different.

#### ‚úÖ Confusion with Trade-name
- If the name is **identical** or if the use leads the public to believe the businesses are **one**, regardless of nature.

> üìå **Factors Considered** in assessing confusion:
1. Inherent distinctiveness of the mark/trade-name
2. Duration of use
3. Nature of goods/services
4. Nature of trade
5. Degree of resemblance (appearance, sound, concept)
6. Geographic area of use

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception**: If the **name is reserved** under section 22, it is **not prohibited** unless the **reserving party consents in writing**.
