**Detailed Summary of the New Brunswick Rules of Practice Respecting Reduction in the Number of Years of Imprisonment without Eligibility for Parole**

---

### **Background and Legal Authority**
These Rules were issued by the **Chief Justice of The Court of Queen’s Bench of New Brunswick** on **November 19, 2004**, under **subsection 745.64(1)a of the Criminal Code of Canada (S.C. 1996, c. 34, s. 2(2))**.  
They establish a formal procedural framework for individuals serving a sentence without eligibility for parole to apply for a **reduction in the number of years of imprisonment** under **section 745.6 of the Criminal Code**, which allows for a reduction in the term of imprisonment without granting parole eligibility.

The rules are specifically designed to govern the process for such applications, ensuring fairness, transparency, and consistency in the judicial review of eligibility for reduced sentences.

---

## **Key Definitions**
The following terms are defined and used throughout the rules:

- **Act**: Refers to the *Criminal Code of Canada*.
- **Applicant**: The person making the application, including counsel acting on their behalf.
- **Application**: A formal request under section 745.6(1) of the Act for a reduction in sentence without eligibility for parole.
- **Attorney General**: The Attorney General of New Brunswick and any counsel acting for them.
- **Chief Justice**: The head of The Court of Queen’s Bench of New Brunswick.
- **Clerk**: The clerk of the Court of Queen’s Bench for the judicial district where the original sentence was imposed.
- **Judge**: A judge of The Court of Queen’s Bench.
- **Presiding Judge**: The judge designated by the Chief Justice under section 745.61(5) to empanel a jury in a parole eligibility hearing.

---

## **1. Requirements for Filing an Application**

An application must be:
- **In writing** and include the following information:

| Element | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Personal Information | Full given names, surname, alternate names, and date of birth |
| Detention Details | Name and location of the institution where the applicant is currently detained |
| Conviction Details | Offence, date of conviction, and original sentence imposed |
| Incarceration Duration | Length of time served for the offence |
| Additional Sentences | Any other sentences being served (with date, place, and offence) |
| Grounds for Application | All legal or factual reasons supporting the application |
| Relief Sought | Clear statement of what the applicant is asking for (e.g., reduction of sentence) |
| Address for Service | Contact address for service of documents |
| Evidence Outline | Summary of evidence the applicant intends to present |

> **Filing Requirement**: The application and any additional materials (as required by section 745.61(1)(b) and (c)) must be filed with the **clerk of the Court of Queen’s Bench** in the relevant judicial district.

---

## **2. Service of the Application**

Upon filing, the applicant must serve the application on the following parties:
- **Solicitor General of Canada** – only for notice (not a party)
- **Attorney General of New Brunswick** – for notice (not a party)
- **Officer in charge of the detention institution** – for notice (not a party)
- **Any other person/institution** as directed by the Chief Justice

> **Method of Service**: Registered mail is the standard method.  
> **Deemed Service**: Service is deemed complete on the **tenth day after mailing**.

> **Proof of Service**: Can be established via:
- Affidavit of the person who served the documents, OR
- Any other method acceptable to the Chief Justice.

> **Deadline for Proof of Service**: Must be filed with the clerk **within 10 days** of service being completed.

---

## **3. Initial Review by the Chief Justice or Judge**

Upon receipt of the application, additional materials, and proof of service, the **Chief Justice or a judge designated under section 745.61(1)** will determine whether **section 745.6 of the Criminal Code applies** to the applicant.

### Outcomes:
- **If section 745.6 does not apply** → The application is **dismissed**.
- **If section 745.6 applies** → The court orders:
  - A **parole eligibility report** to be prepared and filed within a specified period.
  - The report is delivered to the **applicant and the Attorney General**.

---

## **4. Preparation and Content of the Parole Eligibility Report**

The report is prepared by a person **designated by the Solicitor General of Canada** and must contain:

1. **Social and Family Background** – Summary of applicant’s upbringing, relationships, family structure.
2. **Classification and Discipline Evaluations** – Records of institutional behavior and disciplinary actions.
3. **Conduct Reports** – Regular assessments of the applicant’s behavior in custody.
4. **Psychological or Psychiatric Assessments** – Any evaluations conducted by mental health professionals.
5. **Other Relevant Information** – Any additional details that provide a complete picture of the applicant’s character and conduct.

> The report may include **any other information** relevant to parole eligibility.

> **Deadline**: The author must file the report with the clerk within the time specified in the order.

> **Delivery**: A copy is delivered to the **applicant and the Attorney General** within the same timeframe.

---

## **5. Hearing Schedule and Notice**

Upon receipt of the report, the **Chief Justice or judge** determines:
- The **date and place** of a formal hearing under section 745.6(8) of the Act.

> **Notice Requirements**:
- A written notice of the hearing is sent to:
  - Solicitor General of Canada
  - Attorney General
- A copy of this notice is filed with the clerk.

> **Solicitor General’s Obligation**:
- Must send a **written notice** (by registered mail or other acceptable means) to:
  - The applicant
  - The officer in charge of the institution
- A copy of this notice is filed with the clerk.

> **Hearing Location**: Must occur within the **judicial district** where the original sentence was imposed.

---

## **6. Participation at the Hearing**

- The **applicant** and **Attorney General** may attend and be heard.
- **No other parties** may participate or present evidence.
- **Evidence allowed**: Only evidence referred to in **paragraphs 745.61(1)(b) or (c)** of the Act (e.g., medical records, character references, behavioral reports).

> **Attendance of Applicant**:  
The officer in charge of the institution must ensure the applicant is present at the hearing.

---

## **7. Determination of Reasonable Prospect of Success**

At the hearing, the judge determines whether the applicant has shown a **reasonable prospect that the application will succeed**.

### Outcomes:
- **No reasonable prospect** → Application is **dismissed with reasons**.
- **Reasonable prospect exists** → The application, along with all supporting materials and the parole eligibility report, is **forwarded to the presiding judge** for further proceedings.

---

## **8. Pre-Hearing Conference (Before the Main Hearing)**

The **presiding judge** (the judge who will hear the application) must:
- Determine the **date and place** of a **pre-hearing conference**.
- Send written notice to the Solicitor General of Canada and the Attorney General.

> **Timing**:
- The conference must occur **not earlier than 30 days** after the report is delivered to the applicant and Attorney General.
- The place must be within the **judicial district** where the original sentence was imposed.

> **Service of Notice**:
- Solicitor General sends notice to:
  - Applicant
  - Officer in charge of the institution
- A copy is filed with the clerk.

> **Attendance**: The institution officer ensures the applicant is present.

### Purpose of Pre-Hearing Conference:
- The presiding judge determines:
  - **Means of presenting evidence**
  - **Names of witnesses** (if any)
  - **Date and place of the final hearing**
- The **transcript of the trial and sentencing** is admissible as evidence.

> **Dispute Over Report**:
- If either party disputes any part of the parole eligibility report, they may, with **leave of the presiding judge**, require the **author of the report to attend** for cross-examination.

> **Decision on Evidence**:
- The presiding judge decides which parts of the report and additional evidence will be presented at the final hearing.

> **Adjournment**:
- The presiding judge may adjourn the conference, but only **within the judicial district**.

---

## **9. Disclosure and Pre-Hearing Obligations**

- **No later than 30 days before the hearing**, both parties must ensure **full disclosure** of all documents to each other.

> This includes all evidence, reports, and records relevant to the application.

---

- The **certified transcript of trial and sentencing** is admissible.
