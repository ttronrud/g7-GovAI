### **Detailed Summary of the Regulations Implementing United Nations Resolution 2653 (2022) on Haiti**

These regulations, made under **Section 2 of the United Nations Act (R.S., c. U-2)**, implement **United Nations Security Council Resolution 2653 (2022)**, adopted on **October 21, 2022**, concerning the situation in **Haiti**. The purpose of the regulations is to enable Canada to enforce the measures outlined in the UN resolution by prohibiting certain transactions and activities involving individuals, entities, and property linked to persons designated by the UN Security Council.

---

## **I. Background and Legal Authority**

- The **Governor in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**, enacted these regulations to give effect to the measures in **UN Security Council Resolution 2653 (2022)**.
- This is done under **Section 2 of the United Nations Act**, which authorizes the federal government to make regulations to implement UN resolutions.
- The regulations are **not retroactive** in their application but take effect upon **registration** in the *Canada Gazette* and apply **before publication** under the Statutory Instruments Act.

---

## **III. Prohibited Activities (Sections 2–5)**

### **Section 2: Prohibition on Dealing with Designated Persons**

It is **prohibited** for any person in Canada or any Canadian abroad to:

1. **Deal in property** owned, held, or controlled (directly or indirectly) by a designated person or by someone acting on their behalf.
2. **Enter into or facilitate transactions** related to such dealings.
3. **Provide financial or related services** for such dealings.
4. **Make available property or financial services** to:
   - A designated person;
   - A person acting on behalf of or at the direction of a designated person;
   - An entity owned, held, or controlled by a designated person.

> **Exception**: These prohibitions **do not apply** to:
> - **Humanitarian assistance** necessary to meet basic human needs.
> - Activities carried out by:
>   - The **United Nations** (including its programs, funds, specialized agencies, and related bodies);
>   - **International organizations**;
>   - **Humanitarian organizations** with UN observer status;
>   - **Bilaterally or multilaterally funded NGOs** participating in UN humanitarian response plans or refugee appeals;
>   - Any other person authorized by a UN Security Council committee acting within its mandate.

> **Interest payments**: Interest or earnings from transactions that occurred **before** a person became designated are **not prohibited**, but **become subject to the ban** once the person is designated.

---

### **Section 3: Prohibition on Exporting Arms and Related Material**

It is **prohibited** to:
- Export, sell, supply, or transfer **arms and related material** (anywhere in the world) to **Haiti or any person in Haiti**;
- Provide **technical assistance or financial assistance** related to:
  - The sale, supply, transfer, manufacture, maintenance, or use of arms.

> **Exception**: Prohibitions do **not apply** to:
> - Activities intended solely for the **United Nations** or authorized missions (e.g., Multinational Security Support Mission – MSS, UN Integrated Office in Haiti – BINUH);
> - Security units operating under the **Haitian Government** (e.g., Haitian National Police, Armed Forces of Haiti);
> - Activities **pre-approved in advance** by the Committee of the Security Council;
> - **Non-lethal military equipment** intended solely for humanitarian or protective purposes and related technical assistance.

---

### **Section 4: Prohibition on Providing Technical or Financial Assistance for Military Activities**

It is **prohibited** to provide **technical or financial assistance** related to **military activities** in Haiti or to any person in Haiti.

> **Exception**: Applies only to:
> - Activities authorized by the UN or its designated bodies;
> - Activities already approved by the Committee of the Security Council.

---

### **Section 5: Prohibition on Carrying Arms on Canadian Vessels or Aircraft**

It is **prohibited** for:
- A **Canadian vessel owner or master**;
- A **Canadian aircraft operator**;
- A **Canadian owner/master of a vessel** or **operator of an aircraft**;

to **carry, cause to be carried, or permit to be carried** arms and related material **destined for Haiti or a person in Haiti**.

> **Exception**: Applies only to activities authorized under UN mandates or approved by the Committee.

---

## **IV. General Prohibitions (Section 6)**

Any person in Canada or abroad who knowingly:
- **Causes, facilitates, or assists** in any activity prohibited under Sections 2 to 5;
- Is **intending to cause, facilitate, or assist** in such prohibited activities;

is **prohibited** from doing so.

This creates a broad, proactive ban on **indirect or enabling** involvement in prohibited activities.

---

## **V. Obligations of Financial and Business Entities (Section 7)**

Certain financial and business entities must **continuously monitor** their holdings to determine if they are in possession or control of property owned or controlled by a designated person.

These entities include:
- Banks (domestic and authorized foreign banks);
- Credit unions, co-ops, caisses populaires;
- Insurance companies and provincial insurers;
- Fraternal benefit societies;
- Trust and loan companies;
- Entities involved in securities dealing or portfolio management;
- Any entity opening client accounts.

> **Purpose**: To ensure early detection and reporting of illicit financial links to designated persons.

---

## **VI. Disclosure Requirements (Section 8)**

Every person and entity listed in Section 7 must:
- **Disclose without delay** to either:
  - The **Commissioner of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)**; or
  - The **Director of the Canadian Security Intelligence Service (CSIS)**;
  
  - The existence of property in their possession or control that they believe is owned or controlled by a designated person;
  - Any transaction or proposed transaction involving such property.

> **Legal Protection**: No legal action (civil or criminal) lies against a person for a disclosure made in **good faith**.

---

## **VII. Exemptions via Ministerial Certificate (Sections 9–12)**

A person wishing to engage in a prohibited activity may apply to the **Minister of Foreign Affairs** for a **certificate of exemption**.

### **Exemption Conditions**:
- The activity was **not intended to be prohibited** by the Security Council; or
- The activity was **approved in advance** by the Committee of the Security Council.

### **Exemptions for Property**:
A person whose property is affected by Section 2 may apply for an exemption if:
- The property is necessary for **basic or extraordinary expenses**; or
- It is subject to a **lien, mortgage, security interest, hypothec, charge, or judicial/administrative/arbitral decision**.

> **Timeframes for Approval**:
- **Basic expenses**: Within **15 working days** if the Committee does not oppose the application.
- **Extraordinary expenses**: Within **30 working days** if the Committee approves.
- **Lien/mortgage/charge**: Within **90 working days** if:
  - The lien was created **before** the person became designated;
  - It is **not for the benefit of a designated person**;
  - It was **brought to the Committee’s attention** by the Minister.

- Receipt of payments from a designated person;
- A transfer to a designated person.
