**Detailed Summary of the Application of Provincial Laws Regulations (S.C. 1996, c. 7, s. 37)**

---

### **Overview**

This regulation, made under **section 65.1a of the Contraventions Act**, establishes how provincial laws apply to the prosecution of **contraventions**‚Äîminor offences such as parking violations, traffic infractions, or other low-level violations‚Äîacross Canada‚Äôs provinces and territories. It was enacted on **August 1, 1996**, and serves as a mechanism to standardize and streamline the legal framework for handling such offences by aligning provincial statutes with the national Contraventions Act.

The regulation applies **differently in each province or territory**, based on specific effective dates, and includes **key modifications** to provincial laws to reflect the nature of contraventions as distinct from traditional criminal offences.

---

## **Core Principles and Key Provisions**

### **1. General Application of Provincial Laws**
- The laws of a province referred to in the schedule apply **as amended from time to time** to the prosecution of **contraventions designated under the Contraventions Regulations**.
- These laws apply **only to the extent and with the specific adaptations** outlined in the schedule.

> ‚úÖ **Purpose**: To allow provinces to use their existing legal frameworks (e.g., provincial offences acts) to manage minor violations without requiring new legislation.

---

### **2. Reference to the Contraventions Act**
- A **reference to the Contraventions Act** may be made in any notice, form, or document issued in connection with the application of provincial laws.
- However, **the validity of a proceeding is not affected** if such a reference is missing.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: This ensures procedural flexibility and prevents invalidation of enforcement actions simply because the Contraventions Act is not explicitly cited.

---

### **3. Modifications to Provincial Laws (General Rule)**
In all provinces, when applying the relevant provincial statutes to contraventions, the following **key terms are redefined or substituted**:

| Term in Provincial Law | Replaced With | Purpose |
|------------------------|--------------|--------|
| "Offence" | "Contravention" | Aligns terminology with the legal category of minor infractions |
| "Prosecutor" | Includes the **Attorney General** | Expands enforcement authority to include provincial legal representatives |
| "Provincial offences officer" | "Enforcement authority" | Broadens who can issue or enforce tickets (e.g., municipal officers, police) |
| "Set fine" | Fine established by Governor in Council under **paragraph 8(1)(c) of the Contraventions Act** | Ensures uniformity in fines across jurisdictions |
| "Offence notice" / "Parking infraction notice" | "Ticket" | Standardizes terminology for notices issued to individuals |
| "Certificate of offence" / "Certificate of parking infraction" | "Ticket" | Clarifies that filings with courts are treated as tickets |

> üîç These modifications ensure consistency and clarity in how contraventions are treated under provincial law.

## **Provincial-Specific Applications**

Each province has its own **effective date** and **specific statutes** that are applied to contraventions. Below is a province-by-province breakdown.

---

### **Ontario (Effective August 1, 1996)**

#### **Applicable Statutes**:
- *Provincial Offences Act (R.S.O. 1990, c. P.33)*
- Any regulations under that Act
- Any provincial acts referenced in that Act relating to proceedings for offences created by provincial laws

#### **Key Modifications**:
- "By-law" ‚Üí "Enactment" (except in sections 17(5) and 18.6(1))
- "Infraction" or "parking infraction" ‚Üí refers to a **contravention** related to **unlawful parking, standing, or stopping**
- "Clerk of a municipality" ‚Üí refers to the clerk with whom the **Minister has entered into an agreement** under **section 65.2(2) and 65.3(1)** of the Contraventions Act
- "Person designated by regulations" ‚Üí refers to persons designated under **Part II of the Provincial Offences Act** for municipalities in agreements

#### **Exclusions**:
- Subsections **12(1), 17(5), and 18.6(5)** of the Provincial Offences Act **do not apply** in contravention prosecutions.

#### **Jurisdictional Deemed Location**:
- Contraventions related to **unlawful parking, standing, or stopping** are **deemed to have occurred in a municipality designated by regulations** under Part II of the Provincial Offences Act.

#### **Collection Authority**:
- Municipalities that have entered into agreements under section 65.2(2) and 65.3(1) of the Contraventions Act may **collect fines** for parking-related contraventions.

#### **Language Services**:
- Administrative services or communications under sections 5.1, 7, or 17.1 of the Provincial Offences Act must be provided in **both official languages (English and French)** if there is significant demand, **as if provided by a federal institution** under the *Official Languages Act*.

#### **Criminal Code Integration**:
- The enactments are read as if **sections 530 to 530.1 of the Criminal Code** (relating to victims‚Äô rights, sentencing, etc.) were included‚Äî**with modifications as needed**.
