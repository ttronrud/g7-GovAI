**Detailed Summary of the *Board of Steamship Inspection Scale of Fees* Regulations**

---

### **1. Introduction and Citation**
These regulations are officially titled the **Board of Steamship Inspection Scale of Fees** and serve as a comprehensive framework for determining fees charged by the **Marine Safety Branch** of the Department of Transport under the *Canada Shipping Act* (Act). The regulations govern fees for inspections, certifications, and related services performed on ships—both Canadian and non-Canadian—under various statutory provisions.

> **Citation**: These Regulations may be cited as the *Board of Steamship Inspection Scale of Fees*.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**
The regulations define key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Canada Shipping Act*, the primary legislation governing maritime safety and regulation in Canada. |
| **Administration** | The government of the state whose flag the ship is flying; relevant for international inspections. |
| **Certificate** | Any certificate issued under sections 318 or 319 of the Act (e.g., safety, load line, or machinery certificates). |
| **First Inspection** | All inspections conducted before the issuance of the first certificate for a vessel. |
| **Inspector** | A steamship inspector appointed under the Canada Shipping Act. |
| **Length** | Defined differently based on vessel type:<br>• For Canadian-registered ships: from stem to stern post (or rudder stock if no post);<br>• For double-ended ships: from forward to aft rudder stock;<br>• For non-Canadian ships: horizontal distance between perpendiculars at hull ends. |
| **Non-Canadian Ship** | A vessel registered outside Canada. |
| **Safety Convention Certificate** | A certificate issued under the *International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea (SOLAS)*, 1974. |

---

### **3. Scope of Application**
- **Applies to Canadian ships** (except Part III, which is not included in this summary).
- **Applies to non-Canadian ships** as well.
- **Note**: Certain provisions have been repealed (e.g., SOR/88-630, SOR/95-267, SOR/2021-59), meaning some fees or clauses are no longer in force.

---

### **4. General Fee Payment Rules**

#### **Fee Recipient**
- All fees for services provided by the **Marine Safety Branch** must be paid to **any officer of that Branch**.

---

### **5. Fees for Re-inspections (Within 12 Months of Last Annual Inspection)**

> **Repealed**: This section was repealed by SOR/88-630, s.1.

**Summary**:  
If a ship is inspected within 12 months of its last annual inspection, a new 12-month certificate may be issued, and the owner pays **one-twelfth of the annual fee** for each month or part thereof that has passed since the last annual inspection.

> ⚠️ *This provision is no longer active.*

---

### **6. Fees for Extensions of Short-Term Certificates or Letters of Compliance**

- A **fee of $100** applies for extending:
  - A short-term certificate, or
  - A letter of compliance (excluding specific types listed in column I of items 7 or 8 of the table under section 30).

> Applies to inspections under sections 10 to 14, 24, 30, 31, and 34.

---

### **7. Fees for Ship Inspections Outside Canada**

- In addition to standard inspection fees, an **extra $400 per day (or part thereof)** is charged for each day an inspector spends **outside Canada** conducting the inspection.

> This applies to inspections under sections 10 to 14, 24, 25, 28–31, and 34.

---

### **8. First Inspection Fees (by Gross Tonnage)**

#### **For Ships Under 25 Gross Tons (G.T.)**
Fee = **$286 +**  
- **Passenger Ship**: Twice the fee in column II of the corresponding Table I item (based on G.T.).  
- **Non-Passenger Ship** (excluding specified types): Twice the fee in column II of Table II.  
- **Unmanned, Non-Self-Propelled Ships** (e.g., deck barges, oil barges): Fee in column II of Table II.

#### **For Ships 25 Tons or More**
Fee = **$1,152 +**  
- Same multiplier rules apply as above (twice column II fee for passenger/non-passenger ships; column II fee for unmanned vessels).

> The fee structure is based on **gross tonnage**, with reference to **Table I (passenger vessels)** and **Table II (non-passenger vessels)**.

---

### **9. Re-inspection Fees (After Re-registration in Canada)**

For ships previously registered in Canada, then registered elsewhere, and then re-registered in Canada:

#### **For Ships Under 25 Tons**
Fee = **$286 +**  
- Passenger Ship: 1.5 × column II fee  
- Non-Passenger Ship: 1.5 × column II fee  
- Unmanned/Non-self-propelled: ¾ × column II fee

#### **For Ships 25 Tons or More**
Fee = **$1,152 +**  
- Passenger Ship: 1.5 × column II fee  
- Non-Passenger Ship: 1.5 × column II fee  
- Unmanned/Non-self-propelled: ¾ × column II fee

> These fees are reduced due to the vessel's re-entry into Canadian registry after being registered abroad.

---

### **10. Periodic Inspection Fees (Annual, Quadrennial, Quinquennial)**

#### **Self-Propelled Ships Subject to Annual Inspection**
- Passenger Ship (G.T. in Table I): Fee = column II of the relevant item  
- Non-Passenger Ship (G.T. in Table II): Fee = column II

#### **Self-Propelled Ships Not Subject to Annual Inspection**
- Passenger Ship: Twice column II fee  
- Non-Passenger Ship: Twice column II fee

#### **Non-Self-Propelled Ships Subject to Annual Inspection**
- Passenger Ship: Half of column II fee  
- Non-Passenger Ship: Half of column II fee  
- Unmanned/non-self-propelled: Half of column II fee

#### **Non-Self-Propelled Ships Not Subject to Annual Inspection**
- Passenger Ship: Full column II fee  
- Non-Passenger Ship: Full column II fee  
- Unmanned/non-self-propelled: Half of column II fee

> All fees are **rounded to the nearest whole dollar**, with ties rounded **up**.

---

### **11. Special Provision: Non-Passenger, Non-Self-Propelled Ships Without Hull Periodic Inspection**

> **Notwithstanding** standard rules, if a non-passenger, non-self-propelled ship is **not subject to periodic hull inspections**, the fee for annual/quadrennial/quinquennial inspections is simply the **column II fee** (no reduction or scaling).

---

### **12. Coast Trade Inspections (Coasting Trade Act)**

- For ships authorized to operate in the **coasting trade** (limited routes, short voyages):
  - If the period of operation is **7 days or less**, and the ship complies with IMO conventions:
    - The fee for first inspection is **reduced by 90%**.
    - The fee for periodic/partial inspections is reduced by 90% (as per section 11 or 12).

> Applies to both first and periodic inspections.

---

### **13. Safety Convention Certificate (SOLAS) Inspections for Non-Canadian Ships**

- **Fee for obtaining a Safety Convention Certificate**: As specified in section 11 (i.e., based on gross tonnage and vessel type).
- **Fee for extending the certificate**: **$1,000**
- **Fee for inspecting life-saving, fire-fighting, lights, and sound signals only** (for Cargo Ship Safety Equipment Certificate):
  - ≤1,600 G.T.: **$1,000**  
  - 1,601–3,000 G.T.: **$1,500**  
  - >3,000 G.T.: **$2,000**

> **Exception**: If the inspection is conducted **concurrently** with a section 14 inspection (e.g., general safety or machinery), **no additional fee** is charged for the equipment inspection.

---

### **14. Clearance Inspections for Non-Canadian Ships**

- A **fee of $1,000** applies for each visit by an inspector to:
  - Authorize a non-Canadian ship to proceed on a voyage from Canada.
  - **Conditions**:
    - Not conducted under sections 14, 15, or 16 (for issuing or extending certificates).
    - Not a standard inspection under section 11 or 12.

> ⚠️ This provision was **repealed** by SOR/2021-59, s.24.
