## ğŸ” **Overview of the Regulation**

The **Tobacco Products Regulations (Plain and Standardized Appearance)**, made under **sections 7a and 33b of the Tobacco and Vaping Products Act (TVP Act)**, establish strict **design, appearance, and labelling standards** for tobacco products and their packaging in Canada. The primary objective is to **reduce the appeal of tobacco products**, especially among youth, by eliminating branding, attractive colours, and misleading visual elements that may encourage use.

These regulations are part of a broader public health strategy to **promote harm reduction, prevent youth initiation, and increase transparency** about the health risks of tobacco use.

- **Enacted under:** Sections 7a and 33b of the *Tobacco and Vaping Products Act* (S.C. 2018, c. 9, s. 11; s. 44; and S.C. 1997, c. 13; S.C. 2018, c. 9, s. 2).
- **Applies to:** All tobacco products intended for **retail sale in Canada**, including:
  - Primary packages (direct retail packages)
  - Secondary packages (e.g., cartons containing multiple primary packages)
- **Also applies** (under section 60 of the TVP Act) to **empty or non-tobacco packages** provided by manufacturers for future use (e.g., pre-packaged boxes for retail placement).
- **Exclusions:**
  - Packages intended for **distribution to manufacturers or retailers only** (not for consumer retail sale).
  - Packages that are **not sold directly to consumers**.

## ğŸ“ Design and Appearance Standards

### 1. **Colour Requirements**
- **All interior and exterior surfaces** of primary and secondary packages must be **drab brown** (Pantone 448), **except** where:
  - A **health warning** or **required information** is displayed (which may use gray or other approved colours).
  - Interior surfaces of **metal or wood** packages may show the **natural colour** of the material.
- **No other colours** may be used for branding or decorative purposes.

### 2. **Finish and Texture**
- All surfaces must have a **matte finish**.
- **Exception:** Pouches or soft packages with **transparent films** may have a **glossy finish**.
- All surfaces must have a **smooth texture** â€” no embossing, ridges, bulges, raised features, or irregularities.
- **Exception:** A **perforated strip** for opening may leave serrations (but only on cartons).

### 3. **Prohibited Features**
- **No brand elements** (logos, names, mascots, imagery, slogans).
- **No features that change appearance** (e.g., heat-activated ink, fold-out panels).
- **No features that allow remote access** to promotions via electronic means (e.g., QR codes for marketing).
- **No scent or sound emission** (except natural scent from wood packaging).
- **No cut-out windows** that allow product visibility without opening.
- **No stickers or tabs** (except under specific exceptions).

> âœ… **Exception:** A single **irremovable sticker** may be placed on the exterior **only if**:
> - It does not obscure required information.
> - It displays only required or authorized information.
> - It is considered part of the surface it covers.
> - Only sections 9â€“12, 13(1), 14(1), 17, and 20 apply to it.

> âœ… **Exception:** A **resealable tab** may be included on soft packages (pouches) **only if**:
> - It does not conceal required information.
> - It does not display brand elements or branding.
> - Only specific provisions apply (e.g., 10(1), 11, 12, 13(1), 14(1), 17(1), 20).

### 4. **Packaging Shape and Structure**
- **Primary packages** must be **rectangular cuboids** when closed â€” six flat surfaces meeting at right angles, with **rigid, straight edges** (no curves or bevels).
- **Secondary packages (cartons)** must also be **rectangular cuboids** with rigid, straight edges.
- **Slide-and-shell packages** must have:
  - A **lower slide-flap** (folds down and is hidden when closed).
  - An **upper slide-flap** (folds up and becomes visible when opened).
