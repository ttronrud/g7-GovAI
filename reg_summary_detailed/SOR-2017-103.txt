### **1. Purpose and Background**

This regulation is a **prohibitive order** issued by the **Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (MIAND)**, under the authority of the **Northwest Territories Act (NWT Act)**.

#### Key Reasons for the Order:
- The prohibition is **required to settle an Aboriginal land claim** or to **implement an Aboriginal land claim agreement, treaty, settlement agreement, or self-government agreement**.
- This reflects the **legal and policy imperative to protect Aboriginal rights and title**, particularly in relation to land use and control in the South Slave Region.

> The Order specifically targets the **Central and Eastern Portions of the South Slave Region**, a geographically and culturally significant area with deep ties to Indigenous communities, especially the **Athabasca Denesuline**.

### **2. Legal Authority**

The Order is made under:
- **Section 58 of the Northwest Territories Act**, which empowers the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development to consult with relevant parties before making land-related decisions.
- **Paragraph 56(b) of the NWT Act**, which authorizes the Governor General in Council to make orders restricting the issuance of interests in public lands when such restrictions are necessary for the settlement of Aboriginal land claims or implementation of agreements.

Additionally, the Minister has **consulted**:
- The **member of the Executive Council responsible for public lands**.
- **Any affected Aboriginal party**, including the Athabasca Denesuline and other Indigenous groups.

This consultation ensures **legitimacy, transparency, and respect for Indigenous rights** and is a key component of the **modern reconciliation framework** in Canada.

### **3. Scope of the Prohibition**

#### **What is Prohibited?**
Under **Section 1**, the following actions are **prohibited** for a period of **five years** from the date the Order comes into force:

| Law | Prohibited Action |
|-----|-------------------|
| **Northwest Territories Lands Act (S.N.W.T. 2014, c. 13)** | Issuance of **surface rights** to lands in **Schedule 1** (surface rights only). |
| **Petroleum Resources Act (S.N.W.T. 2014, c. 15)** | Issuance of **surface and subsurface rights** to lands in **Schedule 2**. |

> This includes **new grants, leases, or transfers** of surface or subsurface rights in these designated tracts.

#### **What is NOT Prohibited?**
Several specific activities are **exempted** from the prohibition, meaning they can proceed as normal:

| Exemption | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Disposal of substances/materials** | Allowed under the **Quarrying Regulations (N.W.T. Reg. 017-2014)**. |
| **Staking of mineral claims** | Allowed for holders of **prospecting permits granted before the Order came into force**. |
| **Recording of mineral claims** | Allowed if the claim was **recorded or staked before the Order came into force**. |
| **Grants of leases to recorded claim holders** | Allowed if the lease covers an area within a **pre-existing recorded claim**. |
| **Significant discovery licences** | Allowed to be issued to holders of **pre-existing exploration licences** if the licence covers the same area. |
| **Production licences** | Allowed to be issued to holders of **pre-existing exploration or significant discovery licences** (if covering the same area). |
| **Surface leases** | Allowed if issued to holders of **recorded claims or interests** to enable them to exercise their rights. |
| **Renewals of interests** | All existing interests (including renewal options) are **unaffected** and may be renewed normally. |

> These exemptions ensure **continuity of existing operations and rights**, particularly for long-standing exploration and mining activities.

### **4. Duration of the Prohibition**

- The prohibition begins on the **day the Order is registered**.

- After five years, the prohibition **automatically expires**, unless extended by further order.

> This temporary nature reflects a **pragmatic, transitional approach** to land use, balancing Indigenous rights with economic development potential.

### **5. Geographic Scope**
