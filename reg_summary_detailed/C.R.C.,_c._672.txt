**Detailed Summary of the Advance Payments for Crops Guarantee Assignments Regulations**

---

**Regulation Title:**  
*Advance Payments for Crops Guarantee Assignments Regulations*

---

**Purpose of the Regulation:**  
This regulation establishes the administrative framework for the designation of a specific official responsible for handling financial obligations related to assignments of rights under the *Advance Payments for Crops Act*. It also clarifies the legal classification of certain rights under that Act for purposes of federal financial administration.

---

**Key Provisions and Detailed Explanation:**

1. **Designation of Paying Officer under the Financial Administration Act**  
   - **Who is designated?**  
     Any person holding the position of **Assistant Deputy Minister, Food Production and Marketing Branch, Department of Agriculture** is formally designated as the **paying officer**.  
   - **Legal Basis:**  
     This designation is made under **Part IX of the Financial Administration Act (FAA)**, which governs the authority and responsibilities of officials in the payment of Crown debts and obligations.  
   - **Scope of Authority:**  
     The designated officer is empowered to carry out all duties related to the **payment of amounts** arising from assignments of rights under **subsection 12(1) of the Advance Payments for Crops Act**.  
     - This includes processing, authorizing, and disbursing payments when such rights are assigned to a third party (e.g., a farmer, processor, or other organization).  
     - The officer has the legal standing to act on behalf of the Government in financial transactions tied to these assignments.

2. **Classification of Rights as a Prescribed Class of Crown Debts**  
   - **Relevant Provision:**  
     Under **paragraph 81(1)(b) of the Financial Administration Act**, certain rights or obligations of the Crown are categorized as "prescribed classes of Crown debts."  
   - **What is being classified?**  
     The **rights of an organization** under **subsection 12(1) of the Advance Payments for Crops Act** are formally recognized as a **prescribed class of Crown debts**.  
   - **Significance of Classification:**  
     - This classification ensures that the rights in question are treated as **government financial obligations** under federal financial administration rules.  
     - It enables the use of standardized procedures for accounting, reporting, audit, and payment processing.  
     - It provides clarity in the hierarchy and treatment of these obligations within the governmentâ€™s financial system.  
     - It ensures that these rights are subject to the same financial oversight, transparency, and accountability measures as other Crown debts.

---

**Interpretation and Practical Implications:**

- The regulation ensures that the **Department of Agriculture** has a clearly defined and legally recognized official (the Assistant Deputy Minister) responsible for managing financial payments related to crop advance guarantees.  
- It streamlines administrative processes by eliminating ambiguity about who is authorized to act on payments tied to these assignments.  
- By classifying the rights under subsection 12(1) of the *Advance Payments for Crops Act* as a prescribed class of Crown debts, the government ensures compliance with federal financial governance standards, including transparency, auditability, and proper debt management.  
- This classification also facilitates the tracking of these financial instruments in government financial databases and reports.

---

**Background Context (Implied):**  
The *Advance Payments for Crops Act* allows agricultural producers to receive advance payments for crops before harvest, typically to support farm operations and market stability. These advances are often assigned (transferred) to third parties (e.g., processors or lenders) under specific conditions. This regulation ensures that when such assignments occur, the financial obligations associated with those rights are properly administered and accounted for under federal financial law.

---

**Conclusion:**  
The *Advance Payments for Crops Guarantee Assignments Regulations* serve a critical administrative and legal function by:  
- Designating a specific government official as the paying officer for assignments of crop advance rights;  
- Legally classifying those rights as a prescribed class of Crown debts under the Financial Administration Act;  
- Ensuring transparency, accountability, and consistency in the financial management of advance payments in the agricultural sector.  

This regulation strengthens the integrity of financial operations related to crop advance guarantees and aligns them with broader federal financial governance standards.
