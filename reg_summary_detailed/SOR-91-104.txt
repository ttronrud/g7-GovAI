## **Detailed Summary of the Mining Near Lines of Railways Regulations**

### **1. Regulatory Basis and Legal Authority**
- These regulations are made under **Section 50 of the Railway Safety Act** (*R.S., c. 32 (4th Supp.)*).
- The Governor in Council enacted these regulations **on the recommendation of the Minister of Transport**, pursuant to **paragraph 24(1)(b)** of the Railway Safety Act.
- The proposed regulations were **first published in the Canada Gazette, Part I, on December 24, 1988**, providing a **reasonable opportunity for interested parties to submit representations** to the Minister of Transport.

### **3. Key Definitions**
- **Non-railway works**:

### **4. Scope of Application**
- These regulations apply to:
  - Any **owner, operator, or lessee** of a non-railway work.
- The regulations govern the **construction, alteration, or operation** of such non-railway works **after the coming into force** of these regulations.

### **5. Geographical Boundary of Application**
- The regulations apply to any non-railway work located within a defined area:
  - A **vertical plane** extending **50 meters from each side** of a railway line.
  - Measured **horizontally at right angles to the nearest rail**.
- This creates a **buffer zone** of 100 meters total width (50 meters on each side) along the railway line.

> üîç **Example**: If a railway line runs east-west, any mine or well located within 50 meters of either the north or south side of the track is subject to these rules.

---

### **6. Prohibition of Threats to Safe Railway Operations**
- It is **prohibited** for any owner, operator, or lessee of a non-railway work to:
  - Construct, alter, or operate the work in a manner that **constitutes a threat to safe railway operations**.
- This includes activities that could:
  - Cause subsidence, ground shifting, landslides, vibrations, or structural instability.
  - Interfere with track alignment, signaling, or operational safety.

---

### **7. Mandatory Notice Requirements**
Before any construction, alteration, or operation occurs, the owner, operator, or lessee **must provide written notice** to:
- The **railway company** that owns the railway line at the location of the works.
- **At the same time**, a copy of the notice must be sent to:
  - The **Director of the Regional Railway Safety Directorate** responsible for the area where the railway is located.

> ‚úÖ **Notice must be given at least 60 days in advance** of any planned activity.

---

### **8. Contents of the Required Notice**
The notice must include the following information:
1. **Plan and profile** of:
   - The proposed or existing non-railway work.
   - The portion of the railway line adjacent to the work.
2. **Description of the method** of:
   - Construction, alteration, or operation.
3. **Assessment of any threat** to safe railway operations.
4. **Measures taken or proposed** to ensure safe railway operations (e.g., engineering controls, monitoring, mitigation strategies).
5. **Any other information** that the railway company may reasonably require to evaluate the potential risk.

> üìå This ensures transparency and allows the railway operator to assess and respond to potential safety risks.

---

### **9. Conflict Resolution Mechanism**
- If, after discussions, the **railway company and the non-railway work owner/operator** are unable to resolve concerns about a potential threat to railway safety:
  - The railway company must **advise the Minister of Transport in writing** of the identified threat.
- This formal notification triggers further review by the Minister and may lead to:
  - Additional safety assessments.
  - Mandatory mitigation measures.
  - Potential suspension or prohibition of the activity.
