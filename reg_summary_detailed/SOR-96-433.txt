## üîç **Overview of the Regulation**

### ‚úÖ Title:
**Canadian Aviation Regulations (CARs)**  
Cited as: *R.S., c. 33 (1st Supp.), s. 1*  
Enacted via:  
- S.C. 1992, c. 4, s. 5(1), s. 7, s. 14, s. 15(2), s. 20  
- S.C. 1996, c. 20, s. 101, s. 102  

These regulations establish the **rules governing aviation operations in Canada**, including aircraft design, airworthiness, flight operations, air traffic management, safety procedures, and regulatory compliance. They form the primary regulatory framework for all aspects of civil aviation in Canada.

## üìö **Legal Authority and Basis**

The CARs are made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Transport**, under specific provisions of the **Aeronautics Act**. The key sections cited include:

| Section/Subsection | Purpose |
|-------------------|--------|
| 4.3(2)a, 4.4(1)a, (2)a, 6.9(2)a, 7(2)a, 7.1(2)g, 7.7(1)h, 8.7(3)a, 9(1)a | Authority to regulate aircraft operations, air traffic, and safety |
| 4.6(2)a, 4.7(2)b | Regulation of airworthiness, maintenance, and operational limitations |
| 4.9c, 4.91d, 5e | Standards for aircraft equipment, flight manuals, and technical documentation |
| 6.71(2)f | Air traffic control services and airspace management |

These provisions allow the government to define standards for **aircraft certification, flight operations, air traffic services, and safety management**, ensuring compliance with both national and international aviation standards.

### üõ´ **Flight Operations and Procedures**

| Term | Definition & Significance |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Air transport service** | Commercial flights transporting people, cargo, or goods between two points. Requires an **air operator certificate**. |
| **Aerial work** | Commercial services other than transport or flight training (e.g., surveying, photography, agriculture). Requires a **special flight operations certificate**. |
| **Aerial work zone** | Area where aerial work occurs, especially over built-up areas or where people gather. Requires a **risk management plan** (aerial work zone plan). |
| **Aerobatic manoeuvre** | Any manoeuvre involving a bank angle >60¬∞, abnormal attitude, or abnormal acceleration. Requires special certification and safety protocols. |
| **Contact approach** | IFR approach using visual references on the ground. Used when instrument approaches are not available. |
| **Air time** | Time from take-off until landing (in flight). Used for technical recordkeeping. |

### üõ©Ô∏è **Airspace and Air Traffic Management**

| Term | Definition & Significance |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Controlled airspace** | Fixed airspace with air traffic control (ATC) service. Defined in the *Designated Airspace Handbook*. |
| **Control zone** | Up to 3,000 feet AGL; includes all controlled airspace below that height. Exceptions may apply. |
| **Control area** | Specific zones like Arctic, Northern, or Southern Control Areas (previously included; *Arctic Control Area was repealed in 2006*). |
| **Air route / Airway** | Designated corridors for flight paths, either in controlled or uncontrolled airspace. |
| **Appropriate frequency** | Frequency used by pilots for communication with ATC. Based on: <br> - ATS unit assignment <br> - Mandatory frequency at aerodromes <br> - Canada Air Pilot or Canada Flight Supplement |
| **Air traffic advisory services** | Includes weather info, serviceability reports, but **excludes IFR ATC messages**. |

### üìú **Certifications and Documentation**

| Term | Definition & Significance |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Air operator certificate (AOC)** | Authorizes an entity to operate commercial air services. Issued under Part VII. |
| **ATS operations certificate** | Authorizes operation of air traffic control or flight services units. |
| **Company operations manual (COM)** | Required by air operators; contains policies, procedures, and safety management systems for daily operations. |
| **Aircraft flight manual** | Contains operational data for a specific aircraft; may be mandated by the Minister under Part V. |
| **Canadian Technical Standard Order (CAN-TSO)** | Canadian airworthiness standard for appliances (e.g., avionics, instruments). Published by the Minister or adopted from foreign agreements. |
| **Airworthiness directive (AD)** | Mandatory maintenance or operational action issued by the Minister or a civil aviation authority to ensure safe operation. |
| **Airworthiness limitation** | Mandatory maintenance or life limits (e.g., engine hours) tied to type certificate. |

### üå§Ô∏è **Weather and Safety Systems**

| Term | Definition & Significance |
|------|---------------------------|
| **AWOS (Automated Weather Observation System)** | Electronic system collecting and disseminating real-time weather data. Used at airports. |
| **Alert height** | Height above runway where a CAT III precision approach must be discontinued if ground equipment or landing system fails. |
| **CAT II / CAT III minima** | Minimum visibility and runway conditions required for precision approaches (specified in Canada Air Pilot). |

| **APU (Auxiliary Power Unit)** | Provides power or compressed air without direct propulsion. Used for systems like avionics or cabin pressurization. |

### üßë‚Äç‚úàÔ∏è **Personnel and Safety**

| Term | Definition & Significance |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Crew member** | Any person assigned to duty during flight time. Includes pilots, flight attendants, and other flight personnel. |
