**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

---

### **Regulatory Background and Legal Authority**

The regulation in question is a set of **Rules of the Federal Court of Canada** governing the **practice and procedure** for:

- Applications for **leave** to commence an application for **judicial review**,
- Applications for **judicial review**, and  
- Appeals to the **Federal Court of Appeal** from decisions of the Federal Court.

These rules were **approved by His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Employment and Immigration**, under **subsection 84(1) of the Immigration Act**. The rules were issued on **January 7, 1993**, and are codified in **S.C. 1992, c. 49** (an Act to amend the Immigration Act and related legislation).

The rules were originally developed by the **Chief Justice of the Federal Court of Canada** and reflect the procedural framework necessary to ensure fairness, transparency, and consistency in judicial review and appeals processes under immigration and citizenship law.

> ⚠️ **Important Note**: This regulation has been **repealed or amended** over time:
> - The entire regulation was **repealed by SOR/2002-232, s. 2** (2002).
> - Certain provisions were further amended or repealed by **SOR/2015-20**, **SOR/2021-149**, and others.
> - As of now, **this 1993 regulation is no longer in force** and has been superseded by updated rules.

Nevertheless, this summary provides a **detailed historical and procedural analysis** of the original 1993 rules, which remain instructive for understanding the evolution of judicial review procedures in Canadian immigration law.

---

## **Key Definitions in the Rules**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Appeal** | An appeal under section 10.7 or 22.2(d) of the Citizenship Act, or section 74(d) of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act. |
| **Application for Judicial Review** | A request under section 22.2 (Citizenship Act) or section 74 (Immigration and Refugee Protection Act) to review a decision made by an administrative body. |
| **Application for Leave** | A request under section 22.1 (Citizenship Act) or section 72 (Immigration and Refugee Protection Act) to obtain permission to file a judicial review. |
| **Court** | Refers to either the **Federal Court of Appeal** (including a single judge) or the **Federal Court** (including a prothonotary). |
| **Registry** | A registry within the meaning of the Federal Courts Act. |
| **Tribunal** | Any administrative body that made a decision, determination, or order that is the subject of an application for leave or judicial review. |
| **Written Reasons** | Includes a transcript of oral reasons given by the tribunal. |

---

## **Scope of Application**

These rules apply to:

1. Applications for leave to commence judicial review;
2. Applications for judicial review themselves;
3. Appeals from judgments of the Federal Court to the Federal Court of Appeal.

> ✅ **Applicability**: These rules are **not exclusive**. They operate **in conjunction with**:
> - The **Citizenship Act** and **Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)**;
> - **Parts 1 to 3, 5.1, 6, 7, 10, and 11** of the **Federal Courts Rules**;
> - Specific rules (e.g., **Rule 302, 383–385**) of the Federal Courts Rules.

> ❌ **Exclusion**: **Rule 133 of the Federal Courts Rules** (which governs service of documents) **does not apply** to applications for leave or judicial review.

---

## **Service of Documents**

A document is deemed to be **received on**:

- **Ordinary mail**: 10 days after mailing;
- **Registered mail or courier**: On the day of delivery indicated on the receipt;
- **Electronic transmission or fax**: On the day it is sent (as per transmission slip).

> ⚠️ **Holiday Adjustment**: If the day of deemed receipt is a holiday, or if the document is sent after **5 p.m.** at the recipient’s local time, it is deemed received on the **next business day**.

---

## **Form and Content of an Application for Leave (Form IR-1)**

An application for leave must include:

1. Full names of all parties;
2. Date and details of the decision/order/measure/question that is the subject of the review, and the date the applicant became aware of it;
3. Name of the tribunal and, if applicable, the names of all members;
4. Tribunal’s file number (if any);
5. Precise relief sought in the judicial review;
6. Grounds for the relief (with reference to statutes or rules);
7. Proposed place and language of the hearing;
8. Whether the applicant has received written reasons;
9. Electronic address (if consented to) for electronic service;
10. Name, address, and phone number of any person who prepared the application for a fee or consideration;
11. Signature, name, address, and phone number of the applicant’s solicitor or, if acting in person, their signature and contact details.

---

## **Respondent in an Application for Leave**

- **Under the Citizenship Act**: The **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration**;
- **Under the IRPA**: **Each Minister responsible** for administering the Act in relation to the matter.

> ⚠️ The respondent must file a **notice of appearance** within **10 days** of being served.

> ❌ If a respondent fails to file a notice of appearance, they **lose the right to be served with any further documents** in the proceeding.

---

## **Request for Extension of Time**

- A request to extend time to file or serve the application must be included in the **original application for leave**.
- The request is **determined at the same time** as the application.
- The same materials and standards apply.

---

## **Request for Anonymity Order (Rule 8.1)**

- A party may request that the Court **amend or redact documents** to protect their identity.
- The request must be made in **Form IR-5**.
- The opposing party may **object** in the same form, stating grounds.
- The request is **decided at the same time** as the application for leave.
- The Court may grant anonymity **only if it determines** that the public interest in open proceedings is outweighed by the need for privacy.
- The order **continues in effect** until the Court orders otherwise — including during **any appeal** or after final judgment.

---

## **Service of Written Reasons (Rule 9)**

If the applicant claims **not to have received written reasons**, the **Registry must immediately** send a **request in Form IR-3** to the tribunal.

Upon receiving the request, the tribunal must:

- Send the **decision and written reasons** (certified) to all parties and two copies to the Registry; **or**
- Send a **notice** to parties and the Registry indicating that **no reasons were given** or that reasons were given but not recorded.

---

## **Perfecting the Application for Leave**

The applicant must complete the application within **30 days** of:

- Filing the application (if written reasons were received), or  
- Receiving the written reasons or the notice of non-reasons (if not received).

---

## **Required Record to Be Served (Rule 10)**

The applicant must serve on each respondent a **record** containing, in order:

1. The application for leave;
2. The decision or order at issue;
3. The written reasons (or notice of absence of reasons);
4. Any request for anonymity;
5. Supporting affidavits (if any);
6. A **memorandum of argument** (concise facts and legal submissions);
7. A statement on the **language** of the hearing (English, French, or both).

> This record must be **filed with proof of service**.

---

## **Opposition to the Application**

A respondent may:

- Serve **one or more affidavits**;
- Serve a **memorandum of argument** with concise facts and legal submissions.

> These must be filed and served within **30 days** of the applicant’s documents being served.

> ✅ No cross-examination of affidavit deponents is allowed **unless a judge specifically orders it**.

---

## **Hearing and Disposition of Application for Leave**

If any party fails to serve or file required documents within the time limit, or if the applicant’s reply memorandum is filed or time expires, the **judge may determine the application without further notice**.

> ⚖️ If the judge considers that **documents held by the tribunal** are necessary, the judge may issue an order specifying which documents must be produced and filed.

> The **Registry must send a copy of the order** to the tribunal immediately.

> The tribunal must then **send the specified documents** (certified) to all parties and two copies to the Registry.

---
