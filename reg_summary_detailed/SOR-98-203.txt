### **3. Scope of Application**
The regulations apply to:
- **Public service agencies** (e.g., police forces, government departments, police academies)
- **Public agents** employed by or acting under these agencies

---

### **4. Safe Storage Requirements**

#### **For Agency Firearms (when not in use):**
- Must be stored in a **secure container, vault, safe, or room**:
  - Controlled by the agency
  - Locked securely
  - Constructed so it **cannot be easily broken open**
- **Exception**: May be stored in a **dwelling house** only if **authorized in writing** by the agency.

#### **Storage in a Dwelling House:**
- If stored at home:
  - Must comply with the **Storage, Display, Transportation and Handling of Firearms by Individuals Regulations**
  - OR follow **specific written instructions** from the agency

#### **For Controlled Items (e.g., ammunition):**
- Must be stored in a **securely locked container or receptacle** that cannot be easily broken open
- OR follow **written instructions** from the agency

> ðŸ” **Agency Responsibility**: All agencies must ensure that all assigned firearms and controlled items are stored safely, either in secure facilities or under authorized home storage.

---

### **5. Training Requirements**
- Every public service agency must ensure that **all public agents** who handle, transport, possess, or use firearms receive **training appropriate to their duties** before doing so.
- Training must cover safe handling, storage, transportation, and use.

---

### **6. Identification and Reporting Requirements**

#### **Public Agency Identification Number (PAID)**
- Agencies must apply for a PAID to cover themselves and all units.
- Application must include:
  - Name, address, phone number, fax number, and email (if applicable)

#### **Firearm Identification Number (FIN) Assignment**
- The **Registrar** assigns:
  - A PAID to any agency/unit applying or reporting possession of a firearm
  - A FIN to any agency firearm or protected firearm **without a prior FIN**
- For a protected firearm **without a serial number or FIN**, the Registrar issues a **sticker** with the assigned FIN

### **7. Inventory Reporting (Mandatory by Deadline)**

#### **Mandatory Inventory Report (by October 31, 2009)**
- Every public service agency must submit a report to the Registrar **by October 31, 2009**, detailing:
  - Inventory of **all agency firearms and protected firearms** as of **October 31, 2008**
- **Exclusion**: Protected firearms intended for disposal **by October 31, 2009** are **not included**

#### **Report Content (for each firearm):**
- **Agency firearm**:
  - Serial number (if any)
  - Make
  - Manufacturer (if different from make)
  - Model (if known)
  - Type
  - Action
  - Calibre/gauge
  - Barrel length (if <470 mm)
  - Magazine capacity
- **Protected firearm**:
  - Serial number (if any)
  - Type
  - Barrel length (if <470 mm)
  - Case/file reference (if applicable)

> ðŸ“ **Note**: This inventory requirement was a major compliance step to improve transparency and accountability in firearm possession.

### **8. Ongoing Reporting Requirements**

#### **Reporting of Protected Firearms After October 31, 2009**
- Any protected firearm **still in possession after October 31, 2009** must be reported **without delay** to the Registrar.
- Must include the same information as in subsection 8(3)

#### **Reporting of New Agency Firearms (after Oct 31, 2008)**
- Any firearm **coming into possession** after October 31, 2008 and used as an agency firearm must be reported **without delay**.
- Includes:
  - Agency name and PAID
  - FIN or alternative details (if no FIN)
  - Import status (if applicable)

#### **Reporting of Protected Firearms Acquired After Oct 31, 2008**
- Any firearm **kept as a protected firearm** after that date must be reported **within 30 days** of coming into possession.
- Exceptions:
  - Firearms held for less than 72 hours
  - Transactions required under Act or other sections (e.g., seizures, amnesties)

#### **Reporting for Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)**
- If CBSA acquires a firearm (not through abandonment, seizure, or forfeiture) and keeps it for **over 90 days**, a report must be filed **without delay** after 90 days.
- Report includes:
  - Agency name and PAID
  - FIN or details
  - How it was obtained (found, detained, surrendered, etc.)
  - Case/file reference
  - Import status (if applicable)

> âŒ **Exclusions**: Transactions under subsection 26(1) of the Act or section 13 of these regulations are exempt.

---

### **9. Modifications to Firearms**

- Any **modification** that changes the **class** of a firearm (e.g., from restricted to prohibited) must be reported to the Registrar **within 30 days** of the change.

---

### **10. Transfer and Disposition of Firearms**

#### **Permitted Transfers (with restrictions):**
- Agencies may **sell, barter, give, lend, or rent** firearms to:
  - Other public service agencies
- **Exception**: No transfer to **private individuals** or foreign governments without specific authorization.
- **Foreign transfers**: May lend to **foreign government departments, national agencies**, or police forces **for judicial evidence purposes only**

#### **Return of Defective Firearms**
- Agencies may return a defective agency firearm to the **original supplier**

#### **Return of Protected Firearms**
- May return a protected firearm to the **person entitled to it** (e.g., a licensed owner)

> âœ… **Note**: Nothing in the regulations prevents return of protected firearms to rightful owners.

### **11. Reporting of Transactions**

All transfers (sales, barter, gift, loan, rent, return) must be reported to the Registrar:

| Transaction Type | Reporting Requirement |
|------------------|------------------------|
| **Sold, bartered, given, returned** | Without delay |
| **Lent or rented** | Without delay if >180 days; or after 180 days if duration exceeds 180 days |
| **Return of loaned/rented firearm** | Without delay |
| **Return of protected firearm to owner** | Without delay |

> - Names and PAIDs of parties involved

> - Import status (if applicable)
