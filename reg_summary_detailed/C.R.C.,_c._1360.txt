### 1. **Definitions and Key Terms**

### 2. **Monthly Contributions by Participants**
- **General Rule**: All participants must pay their required contributions **monthly by reservation from their salary**, unless otherwise specified.
- **Absence from Duty**:
  - If absent **without pay**, the participant must pay contributions in a **lump sum within 30 days of return to duty**, or
    - By **equal monthly instalments** from salary over **twice the period of absence**.
  - If such payment would cause **financial hardship**, the participant may choose to pay in **equal instalments over a maximum of**:
    - **Three times the period of absence**, or
    - **15 years** (whichever is shorter).

---

### 3. **Advance Payments for Specific Service Situations**
Participants must pay contributions **in advance annually** if they are:
- Serving with an **international organization**;
- Serving with the **government of a country other than Canada**;
- Serving as a **full-time paid official** of:
  - A public service employee organization;
  - A credit union;
- Serving on an **inquiry established under Part I of the Inquiries Act**, or with any **board or agency acting as an agent of His Majesty in right of Canada**.

> This ensures that contributions are not deferred during such temporary or international assignments.

---

### 4. **Unpaid Contributions Upon Leaving Public Service**
If a participant ceases employment and has unpaid contributions:
- **For annuities or annual allowances**:
  - Contributions are **deducted from the monthly payments** of the annuity or annual allowance:
    - Equal to the amount of the monthly instalment otherwise payable under section 6, or
    - 30% of the gross monthly annuity/allowance, whichever is **lesser**.
  - Or, the participant may **elect to pay in a lump sum** at the time the annuity begins.
- **For all other benefits** (e.g., lump-sum payments): Deducted **in a lump sum** at the time the benefit becomes payable.

> This ensures that the superannuation obligation is not left unpaid and is integrated into the retirement benefit structure.

---

### 5. **Contributions for Seasonal and Sessional Employees**
- For seasonal or sessional employees, contributions must be paid during periods of **non-duty** in the **same manner as if they were regular participants** under section 6.
- This ensures that temporary employees are not exempt from superannuation obligations during their off-duty periods.

---

### 6. **Educational Leave and Seasonal Lay-Offs**
- If a participant on **educational leave or seasonal lay-off** receives **less salary** than they would have received had they not been on leave:
  - They are **deemed to have been employed** during that period.
  - The **salary deemed received** is the amount they would have received had they not been on leave.
- As a result, contributions are payable **as if they were employed** during that time.

---

### 7. **Elective Participant Contributions (Post-1995)**
Every **elective participant** (those who choose to contribute voluntarily) must pay:
- **$0.15 per $1,000 of basic benefit** each month, under the following conditions:
  - Entitled to an **immediate annuity** upon leaving public service;
  - Entitled to and has opted for an **annual allowance** after March 31, 1995;
  - Entitled to an immediate annuity or annual allowance after ceasing to contribute to the Retirement Compensation Arrangements Account (RCRA);
  - Was an elective participant on March 31, 1995, and was receiving an annual allowance since within 30 days of leaving service or was entitled to an immediate annuity.

> This applies to a broad category of retirees who opted into the system.

---

### 8. **Reduction for Participants Age 65 or Older**
- For participants **65 years of age or older**, the monthly contribution is **reduced by $1.50 per month** if:
  - Entitled to an immediate annuity;
  - Entitled to an immediate annual allowance;
  - Entitled to an immediate annuity upon ceasing RCRA contributions.
- The reduction **begins on the earlier of**:
  - **April 1st** or
  - **October 1st** after the participant reaches age 65.

> This provides a financial relief mechanism for older retirees.

---

### 9. **Annual Advance Contributions (Non-Annuitant Periods)**
- Every elective participant (except those in subsection 10(1)) must pay **annual advance contributions**:
  - **During periods when no annuity or pension is being paid**:
    - Amount set out in **Part I of Schedule I** (per $2,000 of basic benefit).
  - **During periods when an annuity or pension is being paid**:
    - **12 times** the amount in **Part II of Schedule I** (per $2,000 of basic benefit).

> This ensures that contributions are paid in advance during active service and during retirement, depending on benefit status.

---

### 10. **Due Dates for Annual Contributions**
- First contribution must be paid **within 30 days** of ceasing employment.
- Subsequent contributions due **annually on or before the 30th day after the anniversary** of the date of leaving service.
- **Exception**: If a participant received **erroneous or misleading information** from a public service employee (e.g., a payroll or HR officer), they must pay the contribution **within 3 months** of receiving correct information. However, **subsequent due dates remain unchanged**.

---

### 11. **Reserving Contributions from Annuities/Allowances**
- If an elective participant is entitled to an annuity or annual allowance under Part I of the Act:
  - Contributions are **automatically reserved** from the benefit **unless the participant provides a written direction**.
- If the annuity or allowance is **insufficient** to cover contributions:
  - The participant must pay the **full amount in advance annually**.

> Participants may **write and deliver a direction** to the Minister to opt out of reserving contributions. The direction is effective on the **first day of the month following receipt**.

---

### 12. **Pro Rata Refund for Overlapping Contributions**
- If an elective participant pays a contribution for a period longer than one month and then **becomes a participant under Part II of the Canadian Forces Superannuation Act or the Public Service Superannuation Act**, they are entitled to a **pro rata refund**.
- The refund is calculated as:
  - **Numerator**: Number of complete calendar months remaining until the next due date.
  - **Denominator**: Total number of calendar months for which the contribution was paid.

> This prevents double contribution when transitioning between systems.

---
