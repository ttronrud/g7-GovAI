**Detailed Summary of the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC) Application for Deposit Insurance By-law (Ottawa, September 27, 2006)**

This by-law, issued under **paragraphs 11(2)(g)a and (i)** and **subsection 18(1)b** of the *Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation Act* (CDICA), establishes the detailed requirements and procedures for a provincial financial institution to apply for deposit insurance from the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC). The by-law outlines the mandatory information, documentation, and compliance standards that must be included in an application for deposit insurance. It is designed to ensure that only financially sound, well-governed, and transparent institutions are granted deposit insurance coverage, thereby protecting depositors and maintaining public confidence in the Canadian financial system.

---

### **1. Purpose and Legal Authority**
The by-law serves as a regulatory framework to guide applicants through the process of applying for CDIC deposit insurance. It is issued by the Board of Directors of CDIC pursuant to its statutory authority under the CDICA, which empowers it to regulate and oversee the granting of deposit insurance to eligible institutions.

The primary objective is to **assess the financial stability, governance quality, risk management practices, and legal compliance** of an applicant before approving its eligibility for deposit insurance.

---

### **2. Key Definitions and Interpretations**

The by-law provides comprehensive definitions of key terms used throughout the application process:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation Act*. |
| **Applicant** | A provincial institution seeking deposit insurance. |
| **Associate** | A person who beneficially owns >10% of voting securities, is a partner, or has substantial beneficial interest in a trust/estate. |
| **Beneficial Ownership** | Includes ownership through intermediaries (e.g., trustees, agents). |
| **Common-law Partnership** | Cohabitation for at least one year in a conjugal relationship. |
| **Corporation** | Any incorporated body, regardless of jurisdiction. |
| **Direction** | Power to exercise or direct voting rights on securities. |
| **Entity** | Broad category including corporations, trusts, partnerships, governments, and agencies. |
| **Financial Statements** | Balance sheet, income statement, retained earnings, and changes in financial position. |
| **Forecast** | Forward-looking projections based on management’s judgment of probable future economic conditions. |
| **Influence** | Power to affect management or policy decisions (direct or indirect). |
| **Material** | Matters that could reasonably impact CDIC’s decision on approval. |
| **Misrepresentation** | False statements or omissions of material facts. |
| **Officer** | Includes CEOs, presidents, treasurers, and other designated individuals. |
| **Person** | Individual or entity, including legal representatives. |
| **Promoter** | Person who participates in founding/organizing the business or receives ≥5% of securities or proceeds (excluding commissions or property transfers without active involvement). |
| **Regulatory Authority** | Supervisory body overseeing financial institutions, capital markets, or securities. |
| **Relative** | Blood, adopted, married, or common-law partner. |
| **Subsidiary** | A corporation controlled by another (via ownership >50% of voting rights). |

#### **Control and Ownership Rules**
- A person **controls** a corporation if they beneficially own >50% of voting securities and can elect a majority of directors.
- For unincorporated entities (e.g., partnerships), control exists if >50% ownership is held and the person can direct operations.
- In a **limited partnership**, the **general partner controls** the limited partnership.
- A person **controls an entity** if their direct or indirect influence would result in *de facto* control.
- **Control is transitive**: If a person controls one entity, they are deemed to control any entity controlled by that entity.
- **Aggregate control** applies: If a person and all entities they control together hold sufficient ownership, they collectively control the applicant.

### **3. Required Application Components**

An application must include the following elements, all in one of Canada’s official languages (English or French):

---

#### **A. Applicant Information**
The applicant must provide the following details:

1. **Legal Name and Language Form**  
   - Full legal name, including the form in the other official language.

2. **Addresses and Contact Information**  
   - Head office  
   - Principal place of business (if different from head office)  
   - All current and proposed branches

3. **Incorporation and Business Timeline**  
   - Date of incorporation  
   - Jurisdiction of incorporation  
   - Date business began or will begin

4. **Directors and Officers**  
   - Name, title, office, and address for each current and proposed director/officer  
   - Copies of curriculum vitae/resumes

5. **Relationships Among Officers**  
   - Whether any director/officer is an **associate** or **relative** of another director/officer or of:
     - A director/officer listed in section 13(e), or  
     - An individual referenced in section 14

6. **Criminal and Regulatory Disciplinary History**  
   For each director/officer:
   - Convictions for **material criminal offences** or **regulatory violations**  
   - Proceedings initiated against them in any jurisdiction for such offences or violations

7. **Licence and Registration Status**  
   - Suspension or revocation of any licence related to the applicant’s business  
   - Suspension or cancellation of any registration  
   - Ongoing investigations into such licences or registrations

8. **External Auditor**  
   - Legal name, address, phone, email, fax of current or proposed external auditor

9. **Promoters**  
   - Name, address, contact details of any person who was a promoter within the **24 months before submission**

10. **Authorized Representative**  
   - Name, title, address, contact details of the individual authorized to represent the applicant

---

#### **B. Financial Statements and Auditing Standards**

Financial statements must be current as of **no more than 12 months before submission**.

**Required Statements:**

- If incorporated for **less than one year**:
  - Audited opening balance sheet
  - Unaudited interim financial statements (if any)

- If incorporated for **one or more years**:
  - Unaudited interim statements (for the year following the most recent audited year)
  - Audited financial statements for:
    - All completed financial years from incorporation (if 1–3 years old), or  
    - The **last three financial years** (if 3+ years old)
  - Latest annual report (if available)

**Auditing and Accounting Standards:**

- **Primary standard**: Financial statements must be prepared in accordance with:
  - Accounting principles and auditing standards of the **jurisdiction of incorporation**, **or**
  - **Canadian Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (Canadian GAAP)**, **audited per CICA standards**

- **For foreign-controlled applicants**:
  - Statements must be prepared under **local accounting standards** and include a **comparison to Canadian GAAP**, or
  - Prepared under **Canadian GAAP** and **audited per CICA standards**

> This ensures consistency and comparability across jurisdictions while allowing flexibility for foreign entities.

---

#### **C. Business Plan (Three-Year Outlook)**

The applicant must submit a detailed business plan covering the **three-year period starting from the proposed launch date** of the deposit-taking business. The plan must include:

1. **Business Rationale**  
   - Why the applicant wants to offer deposit-taking services

2. **Market Analysis and Strategy**  
   - Target markets  
   - Business objectives  
   - Short- and long-term strategies  
   - Reasoning for expected success

3. **Product and Service Offering**  
   - Overview of each line of business  
   - Products and services offered in each market  
   - Expected levels of business volume

4. **Competitive Threat Analysis**  
   - Identification of competitive threats  
   - How the applicant plans to respond  
   - Justification for effectiveness of response

5. **Detailed Forecast**  
   - Pro forma financial statements (income, expenses, cash flow, balance sheet)  
   - Assumptions made in preparing the forecast

6. **List of Assumptions**  
   - Explanation of assumptions used in (b) to (e) above  
   - Justification for why those assumptions are reasonable

7. **Off-Balance Sheet Activities**  
   - Description of proposed off-balance sheet activities (e.g., guarantees, derivatives)

8. **Outsourcing Arrangements and Contingency Plans**  
   - Description of outsourcing (e.g., IT, operations)  
   - Contingency plans in case of disruption

9. **Sensitivity Analysis**  
   - Evaluation of financial results under alternative scenarios (e.g., economic downturns, interest rate changes)

10. **Staffing Plan**  
   - Projected number of employees over the three-year period

11. **Governance-Experience Correlation**  
   - Explanation of how the **background and expertise** of each director/officer aligns with the applicant’s business objectives

> *Additional business plans* prepared within the **24 months prior to submission** must also be included, with an explanation of **significant differences** between them.
