### **Detailed Summary of the Supreme Court of Newfoundland and Labrador — Court of Appeal Criminal Appeal Rules (2002)**

The **Criminal Appeal Rules (2002)** are a set of procedural regulations made by the **Supreme Court of Newfoundland and Labrador — Court of Appeal**, pursuant to **subsection 482(1) of the Criminal Code**. These rules govern the filing, service, conduct, and management of criminal appeals in the Court of Appeal, including appeals from conviction, sentence, acquittal, and cross-appeals. The rules also provide specific provisions for prisoner appeals, time limits, transcript requirements, intervention, and the handling of documents and records.

---

## **1. Definitions and Interpretation**

The rules begin with a comprehensive list of key definitions that clarify the terminology used throughout:

- **Appeal**: Includes applications for leave to appeal and cross-appeals.
- **Appellant**: The person appealing from conviction or sentence (or both); in appeals by the Crown, this includes the Queen represented by the Attorney General or a cross-appellant.
- **Attorney General**: Refers to the Attorney General of Newfoundland and Labrador, including counsel instructed by them, and the Queen in Crown appeals.
- **Chief Justice**: The Chief Justice of Newfoundland and Labrador or the next senior judge if absent.
- **Code**: Refers to the *Criminal Code* of Newfoundland and Labrador.
- **Court**: The Supreme Court of Newfoundland and Labrador — Court of Appeal, or a judge or judges sitting in that capacity.
- **Judge**: Includes any judge of the Trial Division sitting ex officio as a judge of the Court.
- **Judgment**: The formal decision of the Court, including orders for judgment.
- **Notice of Appeal**: Includes notices of applications for leave to appeal and cross-appeals.
- **Prisoner Appeal**: An appeal by a person in custody without counsel.
- **Provincial Court Judge**: As defined in the Criminal Code.
- **Registrar**: The deputy registrar or a clerk discharging that role.
- **Respondent**: The Queen (represented by the Attorney General) in appeals against conviction/sentence; otherwise, the person whose acquittal or sentence is appealed.
- **Time Prescribed**: Any time limit set by these rules, a judgment, or order.
- **Trial Judge**: The judge who presided over the original trial.

> **Interpretation Note**: The interpretation principles of the Criminal Code apply to these rules.

---

## **2. Scope of Application**

These rules apply to:

- Appeals under **Part XXI** of the Criminal Code.
- Appeals under **sections 784, 830, and 839** of the Code.
- All other criminal appeals filed in the Court of Appeal involving matters processed under criminal procedures.

> **Exclusion Clause**: Where these rules conflict with the Code, other statutes, or uniform rules made by the Governor in Council under subsection 482(5) of the Code, the Code takes precedence.

---

## **3. Application to Prisoner Appeals**

- The rules on prisoner appeals **apply with necessary modifications** to any person detained in custody who appeals under **section 672.72** of the Code, even if not yet convicted.
- The **senior official of every penal institution** must provide prisoners with forms of notice of appeal upon request.

---

## **4. Filing of Notice of Appeal**

### **General Time Limits**
- Appeals from **conviction or sentence** must be filed within **30 days** of:
  - Sentence being imposed (if convicted), or
  - Acquittal (if acquitted).
- If a person is acquitted of one offence but convicted of an **included offence**, the appeal from acquittal must be filed within 30 days of the sentence for the included offence.
- For appeals involving **multiple counts in an indictment**, the notice must be filed within 30 days of the acquittal or sentence for **any count**.
- Appeals under **sections 784 or 839** must be filed within 30 days of:
  - The pronouncement of the decision, or
  - The filing of written reasons (if the decision is reserved).

### **Filing Procedures by Appellant Type**

| Appellant Type | Filing Method |
|---------------|----------------|
| **Convicted person without counsel** | Must use **Form B**; notice must be filed in the prescribed manner. |
| **All other convicted persons, Attorney General, or informants** | Must use **Form A**. |
| **Prisoner appeal** | Delivered directly to the **senior official of the penal institution**. The official endorses the date of receipt, returns a copy to the prisoner, and forwards the original to the Registrar. |
| **Non-prisoner appeal (Attorney General not appellant)** | Must be filed with the Registrar: <br> - Original + four copies, OR <br> - Mailed via **prepaid registered mail**. |

### **Service of Notice of Appeal**

- Upon receipt (Rules 4(1) and (2)), the Registrar **forwards a copy** to:
  - The Attorney General
  - The court from which the appeal is taken

> **For appeals by the Attorney General**:
- Service on the respondent must occur within **30 days** of filing.
- Methods include:
  - Personal service
  - Service on legal counsel (if accepted or already appears)
  - Service on senior official if respondent is in custody
  - Verified facsimile transmission
  - Any other method directed by the Court
- **Proof of service** must be filed with the Registrar immediately.

> **Cross-appeal**: Must be filed within **30 days** of receiving the notice of appeal and served in accordance with Rule 4.

---

## **5. Intervention in Appeals**

Any person (including the Attorney General) with a legitimate interest may **intervene** in an appeal by applying for leave of the Court.

### **Requirements for Intervention Application**:
- Brief description of the intervenor and their interest.
- Identification of the position the intervenor will take.
- Submissions to be advanced and their relevance to the appeal.
- Reasons why those submissions are useful and different from those of the parties or other intervenors.

> The Court has discretion to grant, refuse, or postpone leave.

---

## **6. Leave to Appeal**

- Arguments on leave are typically heard at the **hearing of the appeal**, unless:
  - The appellant or respondent applies for a **pre-hearing determination** with supporting materials, or
  - The Court, on its own motion, orders a **separate hearing** to determine leave.

> The Court may:
- Grant leave
- Refuse leave
- Postpone the decision until the appeal hearing

---

## **7. Trial Judge’s Report**

- If the Court requests a report from the trial judge, notice must be given to the parties.
- Parties may submit on:
  - Whether the report should be furnished
  - The scope of the report requested
- If the Court orders a report, the **Registrar** forwards copies to all parties upon receipt.

---

## **8. Written Arguments (Factums)**

- An appellant may submit arguments in writing instead of appearing personally or by counsel:
  - Must state this intention in the **notice of appeal**
  - May include points of argument or file a **factum** as required by the rules
- A respondent may also submit written arguments:
  - Must inform the Registrar and the appellant of their intention
  - Must file and serve a factum in the prescribed manner and within the prescribed time

---

## **9. Extension or Abridgement of Time**

- Any time prescribed by the rules (e.g., for filing a notice of appeal) **may be extended or shortened** by the Court.
- **Notice of application** must be given to the opposing party unless:
  - Made by consent, or
  - Directed otherwise by the Court

### **Application to Extend Time (for Appeal)** Must Include:
- Evidence of potential merit (including legal issues)
- Explanation for failure to file within the time limit
- Whether the applicant demonstrated an intention to appeal
- Any prejudice to the respondent or third parties
- Special circumstances that would cause injustice
- Any other relevant information

> **For unrepresented appellants**:
- May include an application for extension with the proposed Form B notice.
- Court may consider it **on notice to the Attorney General** and after giving the AG an opportunity to respond.
- Registrar sends a copy of the Court’s order to all parties.

> **If a judge dismisses** an extension application, the applicant may file a **written notice within 7 days** to have the matter heard by a **panel of the Court**.

---

## **10. Consequences of Non-Compliance**

- **Non-compliance does not void the appeal**, but the Court may issue directions or orders to achieve the intent of the rules.

> **Failure to perfect the appeal within 6 months** (after filing of transcript) or **12 months** (if no transcript) results in the Court having power to:
- Strike out the appeal
- Direct the appellant to perfect it within a specified time
- Fix a date for hearing
- Make any other just order

> **Deemed Abandonment**:
- Applies to appeals filed **before January 1, 2000**, where:
  - 12 months have passed since the last step was taken
  - No other order has been made under these rules
- The appeal is **deemed abandoned** and the Registrar files a notice of abandonment.

> **Notice of Deemed Abandonment**:
- Sent by **ordinary mail or facsimile** to:
  - Counsel of record
