## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Investment Limits (Banks) Regulations**

**Regulatory Authority & Legal Basis**  
These regulations were issued under **sections 467a, 479a, and 978b of the Bank Act**, and are formally enacted as **S.C. 2001, c. 9, s. 127** and **s. 183**, with reference to earlier statutes including **S.C. 1991, c. 46**. The purpose is to establish rules governing a bank‚Äôs investment in real property and related interests, particularly in relation to capital adequacy, risk management, and compliance with regulatory thresholds.

The regulations come into force on the date that **sections 467 and 479 of the Bank Act** (as amended by section 127 of the *Financial Consumer Agency of Canada Act*, S.C. 2001, c. 9) come into force ‚Äî **October 24, 2001** (as noted in SI/2001-102).

## üìä Valuation of Assets in Real Property

For the purposes of sections 8 to 11 of the Bank Act, the **book value** of an asset representing a bank‚Äôs interest in real property is defined as:

- **If the asset is real property**:  
  Gross book value of the property *minus* accumulated depreciation, as reported on a balance sheet prepared in accordance with the **Superintendent of Financial Institutions' accounting principles** (as per subsection 308(4) of the Bank Act).

- **If the asset is a security or loan**:  
  The book value as reported on the bank‚Äôs balance sheet under the same accounting standards.

> This ensures consistency in valuation and aligns with standard financial reporting practices.

---

## üö´ Exemptions from Sections 476 to 478 of the Bank Act

Sections 476 to 478 (which normally impose investment limits on banks in real property) **do not apply** to the following:

1. **Widely held banks** with **equity of $1 billion or more**;  
2. **Banks with $1 billion or more in equity** that are controlled by any of the following:  
   - A widely held bank holding company  
   - A widely held insurance holding company  
   - An *eligible financial institution* (as defined in subsection 370(1) of the Bank Act), *except foreign banks*  
   - A *foreign bank that is widely held*

> This provides a **risk-based exemption** for large, well-capitalized institutions, especially those under the control of large holding companies or foreign entities.

---

## üìå Prescribed Subsidiaries

For the purposes of sections 476 and 477, a **prescribed subsidiary** of a bank is a subsidiary that is **not**:

- An insurance company  
- A securities dealer  
- A subsidiary of an insurance company or securities dealer  

> Thus, only certain subsidiaries (e.g., real estate investment firms, infrastructure companies) are subject to the investment limits.

---

## üî¢ Prescribed Percentages

- **Section 476 and 477**: The prescribed percentage is **70%**.  
  ‚Üí This means that if a bank or its prescribed subsidiary holds more than 70% of a real property entity, it may be subject to investment limits.

- **Section 478**: The prescribed percentage is **100%**.  
  ‚Üí This applies when a bank or its subsidiary has full ownership (100%) in a real property entity.

> These thresholds help determine whether a bank has a "substantial" or "controlling" interest in a real property entity.

---

## üí∞ Valuation of Participating Shares & Ownership Interests

For sections 477 and 478, the **value of participating shares and ownership interests** beneficially owned by a bank or its subsidiaries is equal to their **book value** as reported on the **consolidated balance sheet** of the bank.

> This ensures that valuation is consistent with financial reporting and avoids overvaluation or subjectivity.

## üè¢ Interests of a Bank in Real Property ‚Äì Scope and Valuation

The regulations define **what counts as an "interest in real property"** and how it is valued.

### 1. **Direct Ownership**
- Real property beneficially owned by the bank or a designated entity controlled by the bank  
- A debt obligation issued to finance or improve real property, where the bank or its controlled entity is liable

> **Valuation**:  
> - For real property: book value  
> - For debt obligation: amount by which the book value of the debt exceeds the book value of the real property

---

### 2. **Interests via Joint Ventures or Related Entities**
- Real property beneficially owned by a **related real property entity** that is a **joint venture**  
- Shares/ownership interests in a related real property entity (not a joint venture)

> **Valuation**:  
> - For real property: book value  
> - For shares/interests: book value of those shares/interests

---

### 3. **Debt Obligations and Loans to Related Entities**
- Debt obligation issued by a related real property entity and beneficially owned by the bank or its controlled entity  
- Loan made by the bank or its controlled entity to a related real property entity  
- Loan to a real property entity where a securities dealer or insurance company *controlled by the bank* has a substantial investment  
- Loan to a real property entity controlled by another real property entity (as defined)

> **Valuation**:  
> - For debt/loan: book value  
> - For **guaranteed debt/loan**:  
>   - If the entity owns real property (and is an interest of the bank), then:  
>     ‚Üí *Amount by which face value of guarantee exceeds value of real property*  
>   - Otherwise: face value of the guarantee

---

### 4. **Secured Loans/Debts by Third Parties**
A loan or debt obligation is considered an interest in real property **if it is secured by**:

- Real property owned by a third party, *in conjunction with*:  
  - The bank  
  - A designated entity controlled by the bank  
  - A related real property entity  
  - A securities dealer or insurance company controlled by the bank  
  - An entity controlled by such a dealer/company  
  - A real property entity described in paragraph 10(1)(c)(i) or (ii)

OR

- Shares/ownership interests in an entity that owns real property, and those interests are held by a third party, *in conjunction with*:
  - The bank  
  - A related real property entity  
  - A designated entity
