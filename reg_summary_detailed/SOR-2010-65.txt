### üîπ **Who Must Pay? ‚Äì Eligibility Criteria**

A telecommunications service provider (TSP) is required to pay **three types of fees annually** if both of the following conditions are met:

1. **Operational Requirement**:  
   The provider was in operation on **April 1 of the calendar year**.

2. **Revenue Threshold**:  
   The provider had **at least $10 million in Canadian telecommunications services revenues** for its **fiscal year ending in the prior calendar year**, OR  
   It is part of a **group of related telecommunications service providers** whose **aggregate revenues** (across the group) meet or exceed $10 million for that fiscal year.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Note: The $10 million threshold applies to **contribution-eligible revenues**, not all revenues.  
> üîÑ Related entities are treated as a single unit for revenue aggregation purposes.

> ‚úÖ This ensures that only large, significant players in the telecommunications sector contribute to regulatory funding.

### üîπ **Three Components of the Annual Fee**

Each eligible provider must pay three components annually:

---

#### 1. **Annual Fee**  
**Purpose**: Base contribution based on the provider‚Äôs share of total regulatory costs.

**Formula**:
\[
\text{Annual Fee} = \left( \frac{\text{Provider's Contribution-Eligible Revenues}}{\text{Aggregate Contribution-Eligible Revenues of All Eligible Providers}} \right) \times \text{Estimated Total Regulatory Costs}
\]

- **Provider‚Äôs contribution-eligible revenues**: Revenue from Canadian telecom services, calculated using the CRTC 2019-396 formula.
- **Aggregate contribution-eligible revenues**: Total of all eligible providers‚Äô contribution-eligible revenues.
- **Estimated total regulatory costs**: The total cost of the CRTC‚Äôs **Telecommunications Activity**, as outlined in the Government of Canada‚Äôs **Expenditure Plan** (Part III of the Estimates).

> üìå This fee reflects a **proportional share** of the CRTC‚Äôs regulatory costs, based on revenue.

---

#### 2. **Supplementary Fee**  
**Purpose**: Covers additional, non-core regulatory costs that are deemed supplementary to the main telecommunications activities.

**Formula**:
\[
\text{Supplementary Fee} = \left( \frac{\text{Provider's Contribution-Eligible Revenues}}{\text{Aggregate Contribution-Eligible Revenues}} \right) \times \text{Supplementary Estimated Regulatory Costs}
\]

- **Supplementary estimated regulatory costs**: As listed in the **Supplementary Estimates of the Government of Canada**.
- These may include costs related to policy development, public consultations, or administrative support not directly tied to telecom operations.

> üìå This fee ensures that providers contribute to broader administrative and policy-related functions.

---

#### 3. **Annual Adjustment**  
**Purpose**: To correct for differences between **estimated** and **actual** regulatory costs in a given fiscal year.

**Formula**:
\[
\text{Annual Adjustment} = \left( \frac{\text{Provider's Contribution-Eligible Revenues}}{\text{Aggregate Contribution-Eligible Revenues}} \right) \times \left( \text{Estimated Total Regulatory Costs} + \text{Supplementary Estimated Costs} - \text{Actual Total Regulatory Costs} \right)
\]

- **Actual total regulatory costs**: The real, incurred costs of the CRTC‚Äôs telecommunications activities during the fiscal year.
- If **estimated costs were higher than actual**, the adjustment is a **credit** (to be refunded).
- If **estimated costs were lower than actual**, the adjustment is a **debit** (to be charged).

> ‚úÖ This mechanism promotes **financial accountability** and ensures that the system adjusts dynamically to actual spending.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Important: The adjustment is **not a disbursement** ‚Äî it is applied to the **next year‚Äôs invoice**. No cash is paid or refunded directly by the Commission.

## üìö Key Principles and Objectives of the Regulation

| Principle | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Revenue-Based Contribution** | Providers contribute based on their revenue, not fixed rates or profits. This aligns costs with economic activity. |
| **Proportionality** | Larger providers pay a larger share of regulatory costs, ensuring fairness. |
| **Transparency** | All cost estimates and formulas are publicly disclosed. |
| **Accountability** | Annual adjustments ensure actual costs are reflected, preventing over- or under-funding. |
| **Group Treatment** | Related entities are aggregated, allowing for more accurate cost allocation across groups. |
| **Fiscal Year Alignment** | All calculations are based on the fiscal year ending in the prior calendar year. |
