### ðŸ”¹ **6. Pre-Trial Conferences (Mandatory)**
#### A. When Held:
- Required for all cases **tried with a jury**.
- Scheduled by a judge or at further dates/time as ordered by the presiding judge.

#### B. Who Must Attend:
- The **accused**
- The **counsel for the accused**
- The **prosecutor** or **senior counsel in charge of prosecutions**

> The court registry must notify all parties of the date, time, and place.

#### C. Notification:
- If accused is **not in custody**:
  - Notify by letter to the **address on bail recognizance** or any more recent address given in writing.
- If accused is **in custody**:
  - Notify the **custodian** by letter, stating the accused must attend.

#### D. Purpose:
- To promote a **fair and expeditious trial**.
- Full and free discussion of issues without prejudice to rights.

#### E. Procedure:
- Conducted in **chambers**.
- Full discussion of issues (e.g., motions, evidence, trial schedule).
- **Tape recording** of the conference is required by the registry.

#### F. Disclosure and Planning:
- Crown counsel must provide the **indictment or draft** to the conference.
- Conference clerk records:
  - Any orders made.
  - Special arrangements (e.g., interpreters, electronic equipment).

#### G. Pre-Trial Motions and Matters:
- Counsel must disclose:
  - Any **preliminary motions** they intend to make.
  - Any **trial matters** (e.g., jury instructions, sentencing issues) that would normally be handled after jury swearing.
- The judge may:
  - Direct the motion to be **reduced to writing** and heard before jury selection.
  - Or hear it at the **outset of trial**.
- The judge may also:
  - Direct that a matter be dealt with **before jury selection**.
  - Or after jury swearing, in the absence of the jury.

#### H. Adjournments:
- The judge may adjourn the conference as needed.
- If adjourned, the **custodian** must be notified (if accused is in custody).

#### I. Record Endorsement:
- Upon completion, the judge **endorses the indictment or a true copy** with the date of the conference.

#### J. Additional Conferences:
- The court may conduct **other informal pre-trial conferences** beyond the mandatory one.
- The **Chief Justice** may issue **practice directions** to ensure the rules are effectively implemented.
