**Detailed Summary of the Mushuau Innu First Nation Band Order**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Context**

This regulation is issued under the authority of the **Indian Act** (Canada), specifically:

- **Paragraph (c) of the definition "band" in subsection 2(1)** of the Indian Act, which allows for the creation of a new band based on specific criteria related to ancestry and residency.
- **Subsection 73(3)** of the Indian Act, which empowers the Governor in Council (i.e., the federal government) to make a band order when a group of individuals meets the conditions for band status.

The order is made **on the recommendation of the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development**, reflecting a process that involves federal policy and consultation with Indigenous communities.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Order**

The primary purpose of this Band Order is to **formally recognize and establish the Mushuau Innu First Nation** as a distinct band under the Indian Act. This recognition provides the community with legal status, rights, and responsibilities under federal law, including access to services, land rights, and governance structures.

---

### **3. Definition of "Adoption"**

In this Order, the term **"adoption" includes adoption in accordance with Innu custom**. This is significant because it acknowledges and respects the traditional practices of the Innu people, particularly their own cultural and familial definitions of adoption, which may differ from formal legal adoption under Canadian law. This ensures that cultural continuity and family ties are preserved and recognized in the legal framework.

---

### **4. Composition of the Mushuau Innu First Nation Band**

The band consists of two categories of persons:

#### **(a) Persons Identified in Schedule 1**
- This includes individuals who were **previously registered** in the Indian Register and are explicitly named in **Schedule 1**.
- These individuals form the core of the established band membership.

#### **(b) Additional Persons of Canadian Innu Ancestry (Expanded Membership Criteria)**

Every person of **Canadian Innu ancestry** who, as of **November 19, 2002**, met **one or more of the following conditions** is included in the band:

1. **Residency in Davis Inlet (with temporary absence not exceeding 12 months):**
   - Was ordinarily resident in Davis Inlet (as described in Schedule 2).
   - Was absent for **no more than 12 months**.
   - Was **ordinarily resident** in the location before the absence.

2. **Residency with longer absence for valid reasons:**
   - Was absent for **more than 12 months** due to:
     - Education
     - Employment
     - Incarceration
     - Health reasons (including medical treatment)
   - But was **ordinarily resident** in Davis Inlet before the absence.

3. **Absence due to adoption or foster care:**
   - Was adopted or placed in foster care or a similar arrangement.
   - At the time of adoption or placement, **either the individual or their parents** were **ordinarily resident** in Davis Inlet.

4. **Family relationships to members of the band:**
   - Is a **blood or adoptive relative** of someone included in any of the above categories. Specific relationships include:
     - Parent, grandparent, great-grandparent
     - Child, grandchild, great-grandchild
     - Brother, sister, half-brother, half-sister
     - Aunt, uncle, niece, nephew
     - First cousin

> This provision ensures that **extended family ties** are preserved and recognized in the band membership, promoting cultural continuity and community cohesion.

---

### **5. Geographic Location and Land Base**

The Mushuau Innu First Nation is defined by its **territorial boundaries**, which are:

- **Iluikoyak Island**, located at **Sango Bay**, within the **Electoral District of Torngat Mountains**, **Newfoundland and Labrador**.
- The island is described in detail on the **1:50,000 scale National Topographic Series (NTS) Map Sheets No. 13N/14 and 13N/15**.
- The island’s approximate centroid is:
  - **55°53′ Latitude**
  - **61°00′ Longitude**

#### **Boundaries of the Island**
- The boundaries follow the **Ordinary High Water Mark** of:
  - Sango Bay
  - Daniel Rattle
  - Davis Inlet
  - The Labrador Sea
- The island includes the **village of Davis Inlet**, which serves as the community’s central settlement.

#### **Legal Record of the Boundaries**
- A formal map showing the island and its boundaries has been recorded in the **Atlantic Office of Natural Resources Canada, Legal Surveys Division** as **Plan No. 2001-001 RSAtl**.
- This plan serves as the official legal description of the land base and is binding under Canadian law.

---

### **6. Effective Date**

The Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** with the federal government. This ensures that the legal status of the band is effective upon official publication and recognition.

---

### **7. Access to Schedules**

- The **Schedule to this Order** (which contains the detailed lists of individuals and geographic descriptions) is **no longer available online**.
- For access to the full list of individuals or other supporting documentation, interested parties must contact the **Justice Access to Information and Privacy Office**.

---

### **8. Key Implications and Significance**

- **Recognition of Indigenous Self-Determination**: The Order affirms the Mushuau Innu as a distinct First Nation with legal standing under federal law.
- **Inclusion of Historical and Cultural Continuity**: By including individuals who were absent due to education, employment, or health, and by recognizing Innu custom in adoption, the Order respects the lived experiences and cultural values of the community.
- **Family and Ancestral Ties**: The inclusion of relatives ensures that the band reflects a broader community of kinship, strengthening cultural identity and intergenerational connections.
- **Land Rights and Governance**: As a recognized band, the Mushuau Innu First Nation may now exercise rights related to land use, cultural preservation, and self-governance, subject to applicable laws and agreements.

### **Conclusion**

The **Mushuau Innu First Nation Band Order** is a foundational legal instrument that formally establishes a distinct Indigenous community under the Indian Act. It recognizes the historical, cultural, and familial ties of the Innu people of Davis Inlet and provides a clear, legally binding framework for band membership and territorial boundaries. The Order balances federal legal standards with respect for Innu traditions and community needs, marking a significant step in the recognition of Indigenous rights and self-governance in Canada.
