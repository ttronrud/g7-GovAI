### **3. Determination of "Estimated Federal Sales Tax" (Under Section 121(1))**

This section defines how the **estimated federal sales tax** is calculated for a residential complex when applying for a rebate under section 121 of the Act.

#### **General Rule:**
The estimated federal sales tax is calculated using a formula based on the **value of the complex**, depending on the type of property and the nature of the rebate claim.

#### **A. For Non-Condominium Residential Complexes**
- The estimated tax is based on the **interior floor space (in square metres)** of the complex.
- Formula:
  > **Number of square metres of interior floor space of the complex**

#### **B. For Condominium Units**
- The estimated tax is based on a **combined measure** of:
  1. The **interior floor space of the unit itself**, and  
  2. A **proportional share** of the common area space, calculated as:
     >  
     > *(Total interior floor space of common areas in the complex)*

- This ensures that the value of common areas (e.g., hallways, stairwells, parking, etc.) is fairly allocated to individual units based on their size relative to the total.

> ‚úÖ **Purpose**: Prevents overvaluation or undervaluation of common areas by distributing them proportionally among unit owners.

#### **C. Alternative Formula (Other Cases)**
If the above formulas do not apply, the estimated tax is determined by:

| Case | Value Used |
|------|-----------|

### **4. Interior Floor Space Definition (Critical for Calculations)**

The regulations define **interior floor space** in a precise way to ensure consistency in measurement:

#### ‚úÖ Includes:
- The **width of enclosing walls** that are:
  - **Not adjacent** to any other complex or unit ‚Üí **entire width** included
  - **Adjacent** to another complex or unit ‚Üí **half the width** included

> üìå Example:  
> A wall shared between two units ‚Üí only half the width counts toward each unit‚Äôs interior floor space.

#### ‚ùå Excludes:
The following areas are **not included** in interior floor space:
1. **Storage rooms, attics, and basements**  
   ‚Üí **Only if** they are **finished to a standard comparable** to living areas  
   ‚Üí Otherwise, they are excluded

2. **Parking areas**  
   ‚Üí Fully excluded

3. **Areas set aside for equipment**  
   ‚Üí Such as:
   - Heating systems
   - Water, gas, or electricity supply systems  
   ‚Üí Excluded regardless of use

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception**: If the complex is a **specified single-unit residential complex**, the builder (who supplies it) must determine the interior floor space.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è In all other cases, the **builder of the complex** is responsible for determining the interior floor space.

## üîç Key Principles and Rationale

1. **Fairness and Proportionality**  
   - The formula ensures that common areas are fairly distributed among units based on size, preventing overvaluation of shared spaces.

2. **Tax Neutrality and Accuracy**  
   - The use of **interior floor space** as a proxy for value avoids subjective valuation and ensures consistency across similar properties.

3. **Builder Accountability**  
   - Builders are responsible for determining the value and interior space, especially in cases involving limited partnerships or deemed tax collection.

4. **Prevention of Abuse**  
   - The rule that the amount applied for must be based on fair market value (or the formula) prevents applicants from submitting inflated or fictitious values.

5. **Clarity in Complex Structures**  
   - Specific definitions of what constitutes "interior floor space" and what is excluded help reduce ambiguity in valuation, especially in multi-unit developments.

- The rules are **not applicable to commercial properties** or non-residential complexes.

The *Federal Sales Tax New Housing Rebate Regulations* provide a **clear, standardized, and equitable method** for calculating the estimated federal sales tax on new residential housing complexes when claiming a rebate under section 121 of the *Excise Tax Act*.
