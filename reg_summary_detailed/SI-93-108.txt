**Regulatory Summary:**

This official instrument, issued under the authority of the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister), designates the **Minister for International Trade** as both:

1. **The Minister for the purposes of the Export Development Act**,  
   and  
2. **The appropriate Minister with respect to Export Development Canada (EDC) under the Financial Administration Act.**

---

### 1. **Designation under the Export Development Act**

- **Legal Basis**: Section 2 of the *Export Development Act* defines the term "Minister" as referring to a designated individual who holds the office of Minister responsible for overseeing the operations and functions of Export Development Canada (EDC).
- **Action Taken**: The Governor General, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, formally designates the **Minister for International Trade** as the "Minister" for the purposes of this Act.
- **Significance**: This designation ensures that the Minister for International Trade has the legal authority and responsibility to act on behalf of EDC under the Export Development Act. This includes making decisions related to EDC’s operations, funding, policy direction, and administrative functions as outlined in the Act.

### 2. **Designation under the Financial Administration Act**

- **Legal Basis**:
  - Paragraph (d) of the definition of "appropriate Minister" in section 2 of the *Financial Administration Act* specifies that an "appropriate Minister" is a Minister who has responsibility for the administration of a federal agency or program.
  - Subparagraph (a)(ii) of subsection 83(1) of the same Act further defines that the "appropriate Minister" with respect to Export Development Canada is the Minister who has responsibility for international trade and economic development, particularly those functions directly related to EDC’s mandate.
- **Action Taken**: The Minister for International Trade is formally designated as the **appropriate Minister with respect to Export Development Canada** under the Financial Administration Act.
- **Significance**: This designation enables the Minister for International Trade to:
  - Approve certain financial transactions involving EDC (such as expenditures, contracts, or funding allocations);
  - Exercise oversight and accountability over EDC’s financial operations;
  - Ensure compliance with federal financial administration rules and procedures;
  - Serve as the principal point of contact between EDC and the federal government for financial and administrative matters.

- **Accountability**: It establishes a clear chain of accountability, ensuring that EDC operates under the direction of a Minister with relevant expertise and authority.

This regulation formally assigns the **Minister for International Trade** the roles of:
