**Detailed Summary of the Canadian Vickers Dry Dock Regulations**  
*Approved by Order in Council P.C. 1956-760, revoked and replaced by SOR/56-175, Canada Gazette, Part II, Vol. 90, No. 11 (17 May 1956)*

---

### **Overview**
This regulation, known as the **Canadian Vickers Dry Dock Regulations**, was issued by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Public Works, under **Section 18 of the Dry Dock Subsidies Act**. It formally **revokes the prior Dry Dock Rules and Regulations** approved in 1956 (P.C. 1956-760) and replaces them with updated, more detailed rules governing the use of dry docks operated by **Canadian Vickers Limited (the "Company")**.

These regulations establish clear procedures, fees, responsibilities, and liabilities for vessels using the Company’s dry docks—specifically, **The Duke of Connaught Dry Dock** or **The General Georges P. Vanier Dry Dock**—and define the rights and obligations of both the vessel owner and the Company.

---

## **Key Definitions**
- **Company**: Refers to *Canadian Vickers Limited*, the operator of the dry docks.
- **Dock-master**: Any person in charge of the dry dock—such as a manager or supervisor—whose authority is central to vessel operations.
- **Dry Dock**: Either *The Duke of Connaught Dry Dock* or *The General Georges P. Vanier Dry Dock*, depending on which is being used.

---

## **Fees and Charges**

### 1. **Fees for Use of Dry Dock**
- Fees are set out in **Schedule A** and are payable **by the vessel owner** to the Company.
- Fees are calculated based on:
  - **Per day** rates: Charged for each 24-hour period or part thereof.
  - **Per ton** rates: Based on **gross tonnage** from the *Lloyd’s Register of Shipping* or vessel certificate; for **naval vessels**, fees are calculated by **displacement**.

### 2. **Fee Calculation Rules**
- **Per-day fees** include:
  - Time required for **cooling engines** before docking.
  - Time required for **warming engines** before departure.
- **No fees** are charged for any **Sunday or holiday** if **no work is performed** on the vessel during that day.

### 3. **Exclusions**
- Fees **do not cover**:
  - Pilotage charges.
  - Costs of preparing or shifting keel blocks, bilge blocks, shores, or staging for painting or repairs.

---

## **Vessel Entry and Admission Requirements**

### 1. **Mandatory Pre-Entry Steps**
A vessel may only enter a dry dock if:
- A formal **application** is submitted in the prescribed form (**Schedule B**).
- The **time, manner of entry, purpose, and duration of stay** are agreed upon with the Company.
- The vessel is **entered in the Company’s official entry book** (which is open to public inspection).
- The **entry fee** (a deposit) is paid to the Company.

### 2. **Entry Fee**
- Acts as a **deposit** toward full dockage fees.
- If the vessel fails to arrive at the scheduled time:
  - The entry fee is **forfeited**.
  - The owner must pay:
    - Full dockage fees for **one day**.
    - For each day beyond the first, fees equal to what would have been charged had the vessel occupied the dock.
    - Any **additional costs or damages** incurred by the Company due to the delay.

### 3. **Vessel Priority**
- **Her Majesty’s ships of war** and **vessels in distress** (especially those at risk of foundering) have **priority** over all other vessels.
- Vessels are generally docked in the **order of booking**, but the Company may alter this in **unusual circumstances**.

---

## **Operational and Safety Requirements**

### 1. **Vessel Preparation and Crew**
- The vessel must have **sufficient crew** to maneuver safely during entry/exit.
- All orders from the **dock-master** regarding movement must be **obeyed** by the captain and crew.
- Orders must be in **writing** if requested by the captain.

### 2. **Movement into/Out of Dry Dock**
- A vessel may **only enter under signal from the dock-master**.
- **No self-propelled entry** allowed unless specifically authorized.
- If self-propelled, vessel must be **warped under the control of the dock-master** or moved by tugs as directed.
- **Propellers or side wheels must not be operated** without explicit permission.

### 3. **Vessel Position and Stability**
- The vessel must be **trimmed and upright** to the **reasonable satisfaction** of the dock-master.
- No changes to:
  - Weight distribution.
  - Water levels in tanks.
  - Steam or water removal from boilers or tanks.
- **No steam blowing off** or **water removal** permitted.
- **No anchors or cables** may be lowered to the pontoon deck or dock floor without prior permission.

---

## **Liability and Risk Management**

### 1. **Owner’s Liability**
- The owner bears **all risks** associated with:
  - Abnormal docking conditions.
  - Damage to the dry dock or Company property.
- If risks are deemed high, the owner must:
  - Pay a **20% surcharge** on applicable fees.
  - Pay for **extra preparation** of the dry dock.
  - Provide a **bond or guarantee** to protect the Company from losses.

### 2. **Damage and Injuries**
- The owner is **liable for**:
  - Any **injuries to dock workers** caused by employee negligence or carelessness.
  - Any **damage to the dry dock or Company property** due to negligence.
- The owner must **pay for repairs**, **compensate for lost dockage fees**, and cover **consequential damages** during repair periods.

### 3. **Fire and Emergency Situations**
- If a fire occurs on board due to **crew or officer negligence**, the owner pays for:
  - Removal of the vessel.
  - Any damage to Company property.
- The Company may **remove a vessel** in emergencies:
  - To prevent danger to the vessel or Company property.
  - In the event of a fire on board or within the dock.
- In such cases, the owner pays all associated costs.

---

## **Cargo and Safety Provisions**

### 1. **Explosives and Flammable Materials**
- **No vessel may enter** with:
  - **Explosives on board**.
  - **Oil with a flash point below 23°C** (i.e., flammable oil).
- Exception: Vessels owned and operated by the **Government of Canada** are exempt.
- For vessels carrying flammable oil:
  - The owner must provide a **certificate from a competent analyst** proving that all tanks and holds are **free of inflammable vapours**.
  - Tanks must be **cleared, steamed out, and ventilated** before entry.
  - A **certificate of safety** must be provided before repairs begin.

### 2. **Discharge and Waste**
- **No discharge** from soil pipes or scuppers is allowed while in the dry dock.
- **Exception**: If connected to a **dry dock sewage system**.
- **Ashes and refuse** must be disposed of as directed by the dock-master; associated costs are borne by the vessel owner.

---

## **Repair and Maintenance**

- All repairs must be carried out **only by employees of the Company**, unless otherwise specially arranged.
- The owner must provide a **cargo manifest** upon request to verify the quantity of cargo for dockage fee calculation.

---

## **Re-entry and Re-docking**

- If a vessel leaves the dry dock due to unforeseen circumstances (e.g., extended repairs), it may **re-enter** as soon as a dry dock becomes available.
- Upon re-entry:
  - **No entry fee** is charged.
  - **No fees** are charged for:
    - Pumping out the dock.
    - Submerging the dock.
- This applies only to the **second entry** after a prior departure.

---

## **Enforcement and Penalties**

- If crew or officers **refuse to comply** with the regulations:
  - The Company may **float the vessel** and remove it from the dry dock.
  - All costs of removal are borne by the owner.
- The Company may **remove a vessel** in emergencies involving:
  - Fire.
  - Risk to vessel or property.

---

## **Application Form (Schedule B)**

A formal application must be submitted to the Company at its Montreal office:

**To: Canadian Vickers Limited**  
Notre Dame Street East, 19  
Montreal, Quebec

The form requires the following information:

| Field | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Name of Vessel | Identifies the vessel |
| Owner | Legal owner of the vessel |
| Gross Tonnage | For fee calculation |
| Dimensions | Length overall, between perpendiculars, breadth, draughts |
| Type | Screw, paddle, etc. |
| Keel Type | Bar keel or flat keel |
| Rise of floor amidships | Structural detail |
| Probable time dock required | Estimated duration |
| Purpose of docking | E.g., repair, painting, cleaning |
| Special features | Includes length of "cut up" or keel camber
