### **Detailed Summary of the Manitoba Milk Marketing Levies Order, 1991 (SOR/90-271, 1990 Canada Gazette Part II, p. 1998)**

---

#### **1. Background and Legal Authority**

This regulation, known as the **Manitoba Milk Marketing Levies Order, 1991**, was issued by the **Manitoba Milk Producers’ Marketing Board (Commodity Board)** under the authority of:

- **Section 4** of the *Manitoba Milk Order*, C.R.C., c. 155  
- **Section 2** of the *Agricultural Products Marketing Act*

It serves to **revise and replace** the previous *Manitoba Milk Marketing Levies Order, C.R.C., c. 156*, which had governed the levies on milk and cream marketing in Manitoba.

The Order is officially dated **May 3, 1991**, and is published in the *Canada Gazette, Part II*, on May 3, 1991. It may be cited as the **Manitoba Milk Marketing Levies Order, 1991**.

---

#### **2. Scope of Application**

The Order applies to:

- **Milk and cream** marketed in:
  - **Interprovincial trade** (sales to other provinces)
  - **Export trade** (sales outside Manitoba)

It does **not apply** to milk or cream marketed **within Manitoba** (intra-provincial trade), which is exempt from levies under this regulation.

---

#### **3. Definitions (Key Terms Used in the Order)**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | The *Natural Products Marketing Act* of Manitoba (referred to as "Loi") |
| **Commission** | The **Canadian Dairy Commission** (responsible for dairy subsidies and programs) |
| **Commodity Board** | The **Manitoba Milk Producers’ Marketing Board** (the regulatory body overseeing milk marketing) |
| **Cream** | Milk containing **more than 6% butterfat** (as defined in the Manitoba Milk Order) |
| **Cream Annual Permitted Quantity** | The amount of cream (in kg of butterfat) a producer can market **outside Manitoba**, calculated as: <br> *Cream Quota – Intra-provincial cream marketed* |
| **Cream Plan** | The *Cream Marketing Plan Regulation*, established under the Act, which sets cream quotas and marketing rules |
| **Cream Producer** | A person allocated a cream quota by the Commodity Board |
| **Cream Quota** | A maximum amount (in kg of butterfat) allocated to a cream producer under the Cream Plan |
| **Dairy Year** | A 12-month period from **August 1 to July 31** of the following year |
| **Deemed Number of Production Days** | A number assigned by the Commodity Board per calendar month, representing the number of days a milk producer is deemed to have marketed milk during that month |
| **Milk** | Milk with **6% or less butterfat** |
| **Milk Monthly Permitted Quantity** | Calculated as: <br> *(Milk Quota × Deemed Production Days × Monthly Eligible Percentage) – Intra-provincial milk marketed* |
| **Milk Plan** | The *Manitoba Milk Producers’ Marketing Plan Regulation*, which sets milk quotas and marketing rules |
| **Milk Producer** | A person allocated a milk quota by the Commodity Board |
| **Milk Quota** | A maximum amount (in litres) allocated to a milk producer under the Milk Plan |
| **Monthly Eligible Percentage** | The percentage of milk marketed in a month that the Commodity Board estimates was used for **manufacturing purposes** (eligible for subsidy under Canadian Dairy Commission programs) |

---

#### **4. Levies Imposed Under the Order**

The Order establishes **different levies** based on the type of product (milk or cream), the volume, and whether the producer has a quota.

---

##### **A. Milk Levies**

| Levy Type | Rate | Conditions |
|---------|------|-----------|
| **General levy on all milk marketers** | **$1.50 per hectolitre (hl)** | Applies to **any person** marketing milk in interprovincial or export trade |
| **Producer levy within permitted quantity** | **$3.44 per hl** | Applies to **milk producers** who market milk **within their monthly permitted quantity** (i.e., within their allocated quota adjusted for production and eligibility) |
| **Producer levy in excess of permitted quantity** | **$40 per hl** | Applies to **milk producers** who market milk **beyond their monthly permitted quantity** (i.e., over-quota sales) |
| **Levy on unallocated milk marketers** | **$40 per hl** | Applies to **any person** marketing milk who has **not been allotted a milk quota** |

> **Note**:  
> - 1 hectolitre = 100 litres  
> - The **monthly permitted quantity** is a calculated limit based on the producer’s quota, production days, and eligible percentage.  
> - The $40 per hl levy on over-quota and unallocated sales acts as a **penalty or disincentive** to exceed marketing limits.

---

##### **B. Cream Levies**

| Levy Type | Rate | Conditions |
|---------|------|-----------|
| **General levy on all cream marketers** | **$0.085 per kg of butterfat** | Applies to **any person** marketing cream (interprovincial or export) |
| **Producer levy in excess of permitted quantity** | **$4.00 per kg of butterfat** | Applies to **cream producers** who market cream **beyond their annual permitted quantity** (i.e., over-quota sales) |
| **Levy on unallocated cream marketers** | **$4.00 per kg of butterfat** | Applies to **any person** marketing cream who has **not been allotted a cream quota** |

> **Note**:  
> - The **annual permitted quantity** is calculated as: *Cream Quota – Intra-provincial cream marketed*  
> - The $4.00 per kg levy on over-quota and unallocated cream marketing is **higher than the general levy**, indicating a stronger penalty for exceeding limits.

---

#### **5. Payment and Due Dates**

- **When levies are due**: At the **time of marketing** (i.e., when milk or cream is sold in interprovincial or export trade).
- **Payment deadline**: Within **seven (7) days** after the marketing event.
- **Payment address**:  
  To the **Commodity Board** at:  
  **36 Scurfield Boulevard, Winnipeg, Manitoba R3T 3N5**

---

#### **6. Purpose and Rationale**

This Order was introduced to:

1. **Modernize and replace outdated levies** from the previous *Manitoba Milk Marketing Levies Order*.
2. **Ensure fair and equitable pricing** for milk and cream marketed outside Manitoba.
3. **Encourage efficient use of quotas** by introducing tiered levies:
   - Lower rates for within-quota sales (to support producers)
   - Higher rates for over-quota sales (to discourage oversupply and market distortion)
4. **Support the Canadian Dairy Commission’s subsidy programs** by aligning eligibility with manufacturing use (via the monthly eligible percentage).
5. **Protect Manitoba’s dairy producers** by ensuring that interprovincial and export sales are properly regulated and funded.

---

#### **7. Key Features and Implications**

| Feature | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| **Tiered levies** | Encourages producers to stay within their quotas; discourages overproduction and export without proper allocation |
| **Quota-based system** | Ensures that only producers with allocated quotas are subject to lower levies, promoting fairness |
| **Different rates for milk vs. cream** | Reflects differences in product value, butterfat content, and market dynamics |
| **Penalties for unallocated marketers** | Prevents unregulated or unauthorized marketing of milk/cream |
| **Link to manufacturing eligibility** | Monthly eligible percentage ties marketing to actual manufacturing use, supporting subsidy eligibility |

---

#### **8. Summary of Key Provisions (Concise)**

| Product | Within Quota | Over Quota / Unallocated | General Rate |
|--------|--------------|---------------------------|-------------|
| **Milk** | $3.44 per hl | $40 per hl | $1.50 per hl |
| **Cream** | N/A | $4.00 per kg butterfat | $0.085 per kg butterfat |

> All levies are **payable within 7 days of marketing** to the Commodity Board.

---

#### **9. Conclusion**

The *Manitoba Milk Marketing Levies Order, 1991* establishes a **structured, quota-based, and tiered levy system** to regulate the interprovincial and export marketing of milk and cream from Manitoba. It balances **economic efficiency**, **producer fairness**, and **government revenue collection** by:

- Applying different rates based on production status and quota allocation  
- Imposing penalties for over-marketing or unallocated sales  
- Ensuring alignment with national dairy policy and subsidy programs  

This regulation reflects a move toward **market-based regulation** that supports both producer sustainability and the broader Canadian dairy industry framework.
