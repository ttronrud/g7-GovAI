**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Regulation Title:**  
*Remission Order in Respect of Certain Fees for the Issuance of Identity and Travel Documents (Hurricane Fiona)*

**Legal Authority:**  
This order is made under **subsection 23(2.1)a of the Financial Administration Act** (S.C. 1991, c. 24, s. 7(2); R.S., c. F-11), which allows the Governor in Council (on the recommendation of the Treasury Board, the Minister of Citizenship and Immigration, and the Minister of Foreign Affairs) to remit certain fees when it is in the public interest to do so.

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**
The primary purpose of this regulation is to **provide financial relief** to individuals who experienced the adverse effects of **Hurricane Fiona**—a major natural disaster that impacted parts of **Quebec and the Atlantic provinces** in Canada between **September 24, 2022, and March 24, 2023**.

Specifically, the regulation grants **fee remission** (i.e., a waiver or cancellation of fees) for certain identity and travel document services that were affected by the hurricane, particularly when documents were lost, damaged, destroyed, or rendered inaccessible due to the storm.

---

### **Definition of "Document"**
The term *document* includes the following types of official government-issued identity or travel documents:
1. **Passport** – as defined in Section 2 of the *Canadian Passport Order*.
2. **Certificate of Citizenship** – as defined in subsection 2(1) of the *Citizenship Act*.
3. **Permanent Resident Card** – issued under subsection 53(1) of the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations*.
4. **Certificate of Identity**.
5. **Refugee Travel Document** – issued under the *United Nations Convention Relating to the Status of Refugees* (1951) and its 1967 Protocol.

---

### **Fees Covered by Remission**
Remission applies to fees associated with the **issuance or replacement** of the above documents, specifically under the following regulatory provisions:

- **Items 3 to 8, 14, and 15** of the *Schedule to the Passport and Other Travel Document Services Fees Regulations* (covers fees for passport applications, replacements, etc.).
- **Section 4 of the Consular Services Fees Regulations** (covers fees for consular services, such as issuing travel documents abroad).
- **Item 6 of the Schedule to the Citizenship Regulations** (covers fees for issuing a certificate of citizenship).
- **Subsection 308(2) of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations** (specifically for the replacement of a permanent resident card).

> *Note:* The remission only applies to fees that have **not already been waived** under **Section 11 of the Passport and Other Travel Document Services Fees Regulations**, which provides for general fee remission in certain circumstances.

---

### **Conditions for Eligibility (All Must Be Met)**

To qualify for fee remission under this order, a person must meet **all of the following conditions**:

1. **Residence or Presence During Hurricane Fiona Period**  
   At some point between **September 24, 2022, and March 24, 2023**, the person:
   - Was **in** or had their **residence in an area affected by Hurricane Fiona** in:
     - **Quebec**, or
     - The **Atlantic provinces** (Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, and Newfoundland and Labrador).

2. **Document Loss or Damage Due to Hurricane Fiona**  
   The person’s document (passport, citizenship certificate, permanent resident card, etc.) was:
   - **Lost, damaged, destroyed, or rendered inaccessible** as a direct result of Hurricane Fiona while it was still valid.

3. **Application for a Replacement Document**  
   During the same period (September 24, 2022 – March 24, 2023), the person or someone acting on their behalf:
   - Made a formal application for a new document, **with the same expiry date** as the original document (if applicable), in accordance with applicable regulations.

4. **Declaration of Document Loss Due to Hurricane Fiona**  
   The application must include:
   - A **written declaration** stating that the original document was lost, damaged, destroyed, or rendered inaccessible **because of Hurricane Fiona**.
   - **Proof** that, at the time of loss, the person was:
     - A **resident** of an area affected by Hurricane Fiona, **OR**
     - **Physically present** in such an area.

5. **No Prior Fee Remission**  
   The fees in question must **not have already been remitted** under Section 11 of the *Passport and Other Travel Document Services Fees Regulations*.

---

### **Key Features and Implications**

- **Public Interest Justification**: The order is made on the grounds that it is in the public interest to assist individuals who suffered significant hardship due to a natural disaster.
- **Geographic Scope**: Only areas in **Quebec and the Atlantic provinces** are covered, reflecting the known path and impact of Hurricane Fiona.
- **Time Frame**: The eligibility window is strictly defined as **September 24, 2022, to March 24, 2023**, which aligns with the period when Hurricane Fiona made landfall and caused widespread damage.
- **Document-Specific**: The remission applies only to the **replacement of documents**, not to initial issuance or other services.
- **Evidence Requirement**: Applicants must provide **proof of residency or presence** during the hurricane period and a **clear declaration** linking the document loss to the storm.
- **Exclusivity**: The remission is **not retroactive** and only applies to fees not already waived under other provisions.

---

### **Who Is Affected?**
This regulation primarily benefits:
- Canadians with valid passports, citizenship certificates, or permanent resident cards that were damaged or lost due to Hurricane Fiona.
- Individuals who were displaced or affected by flooding, wind damage, or infrastructure collapse in the affected regions.
- People who may have been unable to access their documents due to storm-related disruptions (e.g., power outages, destroyed homes, transportation breakdowns).

---

### **Implementation and Enforcement**
- The fee remission will be processed by **Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)** and/or **Consular Services**.
- Applications must be submitted through official channels in accordance with existing regulations.
- The burden of proof lies with the applicant to demonstrate the required conditions.

---

### **Conclusion**
This remission order is a targeted, humanitarian measure designed to **support individuals affected by Hurricane Fiona** by waiving specific fees related to the replacement of identity and travel documents. It recognizes the real-world impact of natural disasters on personal documentation and ensures that affected individuals are not burdened with administrative or financial costs when their documents were lost or damaged due to the storm. The regulation balances administrative feasibility with public interest, ensuring that relief is both accessible and properly justified.

> **Note**: This order is **not a general fee reduction** or a broad policy change. It is a **one-time, disaster-specific measure** applicable only to individuals meeting the defined criteria and within the specified time and geographic boundaries.
