**Detailed Summary of the Petro-Canada Limited Transactions Authorization Order, 1991**

---

### **Background and Legal Basis**

This regulation is issued under the **Financial Administration Act (FAA)**, specifically:

- **Section 122**: Allows Petro-Canada to submit a corporate plan outlining proposed transactions.
- **Section 91(6)**: Empowers the Governor in Council (the federal government) to authorize certain corporate actions when it is satisfied that the entity (Petro-Canada and its subsidiaries) is otherwise legally empowered to carry them out.

The Order was made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of:
- The **Minister of Energy, Mines and Resources**
- The **Treasury Board**

It is formally titled:

### **Purpose of the Order**

The primary purpose of this Order is to **authorize Petro-Canada Limited and its wholly-owned subsidiaries** to undertake a range of specific corporate transactions during the calendar year **1991**, without requiring additional ministerial approval or formal investment approvals, provided that all transactions comply with the **strict conditions** outlined in the schedule.

These transactions are intended to support Petro-Canada‚Äôs operational objectives, such as diversification, asset optimization, or strategic restructuring ‚Äî while minimizing financial risk and maintaining fiscal discipline.

### **Scope of Authorization**

Petro-Canada Limited and its wholly-owned subsidiaries are authorized to conduct the following types of transactions during 1991, **subject to the terms and conditions** specified in the schedule:

---

#### 1. **Incorporation of New Corporations**

**Permitted Activity:**  
Petro-Canada or its wholly-owned subsidiaries may incorporate new corporations **for the purpose of carrying out the objectives** of Petro-Canada or its subsidiary.

**Key Condition:**  
- The investment in each such new corporation (i.e., the amount of capital or equity contributed) must **not exceed 0.5% of Petro-Canada Limited‚Äôs consolidated gross assets at the end of its 1990 financial year**.

> ‚úÖ *Example:* If Petro-Canada‚Äôs 1990 consolidated gross assets were $10 billion, the maximum investment allowed in any one new subsidiary would be $50 million (0.5%).

#### 2. **Acquisition of Shares in Existing Corporations**

**Permitted Activity:**  
Petro-Canada or its wholly-owned subsidiaries may acquire shares in existing corporations **for the purpose of carrying out the objectives** of Petro-Canada or its subsidiary.

**Key Condition:**  
- The investment in each such acquisition must **not exceed 0.5% of Petro-Canada Limited‚Äôs consolidated gross assets as of the end of 1990**.

> ‚úÖ *Example:* A share purchase valued at $50 million would be permitted only if Petro-Canada‚Äôs 1990 gross assets were at least $10 billion.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note:* The acquisition must be directly related to Petro-Canada‚Äôs business objectives.

#### 3. **Acquisition of All or Substantially All Assets**

**Permitted Activity:**  
Petro-Canada or its wholly-owned subsidiaries may acquire all or substantially all of the assets of an existing corporation **for the purpose of carrying out the objectives** of Petro-Canada or its subsidiary.

**Key Condition:**  
- The **purchase price** for the assets must **not exceed 0.5% of Petro-Canada Limited‚Äôs consolidated gross assets at the end of 1990**.

> ‚úÖ *Example:* A $50 million asset purchase is allowed only if the 1990 gross assets of Petro-Canada were $10 billion or more.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note:* This includes mergers, asset purchases, or takeovers where the full or major portion of a company‚Äôs operations is acquired.

#### 4. **Sale or Disposition of Shares in Wholly-Owned Subsidiaries**

**Permitted Activity:**  
Petro-Canada may sell or otherwise dispose of shares in its wholly-owned subsidiaries.

**Key Condition:**  
- The **proceeds** from each such sale or disposition must **not exceed 0.5% of Petro-Canada Limited‚Äôs consolidated gross assets at the end of 1990**.

> ‚úÖ *Example:* A $50 million sale of a subsidiary‚Äôs shares is allowed if Petro-Canada‚Äôs 1990 gross assets were $10 billion.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note:* This applies only to sales of shares in wholly-owned subsidiaries, not to third-party investments.

#### 5. **Dissolution or Amalgamation of Wholly-Owned Subsidiaries**

**Permitted Activity:**  
Petro-Canada may dissolve or amalgamate (merge) its wholly-owned subsidiaries.

**Key Condition:**  
- The **net worth** (i.e., total assets minus total liabilities) of each subsidiary being dissolved or amalgamated must **not exceed 0.5% of Petro-Canada Limited‚Äôs consolidated gross assets at the end of 1990**.

> ‚úÖ *Exception:* This condition **does not apply** to:
> - Petro-Canada Inc.
> - ICG Propane Inc.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note:* This ensures that only small, low-value subsidiaries are dissolved or merged ‚Äî protecting larger, core operations.

#### 6. **Disposal by Wholly-Owned Subsidiaries**

**Permitted Activity:**  
Wholly-owned subsidiaries of Petro-Canada may sell or otherwise dispose of:
- Their own shares, or
- All or substantially all of their assets.

**Key Condition:**  
- The **proceeds** from each such sale or disposition must **not exceed 0.5% of Petro-Canada Limited‚Äôs consolidated gross assets at the end of 1990**.

> ‚úÖ *Example:* A subsidiary selling its assets for $50 million is allowed only if Petro-Canada‚Äôs 1990 gross assets were $10 billion.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note:* This gives subsidiaries some autonomy to manage their own operations, as long as the financial exposure remains within the cap.

### **Key Financial Threshold**

All transactions are governed by a **uniform cap**:
- **0.5% of Petro-Canada Limited‚Äôs consolidated gross assets as of the end of its 1990 financial year**.

This cap acts as a **fiscal safeguard** to:
- Prevent large-scale or speculative investments.
- Ensure that any transaction remains within a manageable and predictable financial framework.
- Avoid overexposure or risk to the parent company‚Äôs balance sheet.

> üîç *Gross assets* typically include total assets such as property, plant, equipment, inventory, receivables, and cash ‚Äî but exclude liabilities.

---

### **Purpose and Strategic Rationale**

The Order reflects a **pragmatic and risk-managed approach** to corporate governance. By authorizing limited transactions under strict thresholds, the government allows Petro-Canada to:
- Conduct necessary internal restructuring.
- Explore new business opportunities.
- Optimize asset utilization.
- Improve operational efficiency.

At the same time, it **prevents large-scale, uncontrolled expansions or acquisitions** that could pose financial or strategic risks to the federal government or national energy interests.

---

### **Limitations and Exclusions**

- **No authorization** is granted for:
  - Transactions involving third-party investments beyond the 0.5% cap.
  - Acquisitions or sales exceeding the financial thresholds.
  - Transactions not directly related to Petro-Canada‚Äôs core business objectives.
- **Exclusions from net worth limits**:
  - Petro-Canada Inc.
  - ICG Propane Inc.

This suggests that these two entities are considered strategically significant and are not subject to the same financial constraints.

---

### **Duration and Applicability**

- The authorization is **limited to the year 1991**.
- It applies only to **Petro-Canada Limited and its wholly-owned subsidiaries**.
- The Order does **not extend** to affiliated companies or non-wholly-owned entities.

---

### **Conclusion**

The **Petro-Canada Limited Transactions Authorization Order, 1991** is a targeted, risk-controlled regulatory instrument that enables Petro-Canada to manage internal corporate affairs efficiently and strategically during a specific fiscal period. By setting a clear, uniform financial cap (0.5% of 1990 consolidated gross assets), the Order ensures that all transactions remain financially prudent, transparent, and aligned with public interest and corporate governance standards.

It balances operational flexibility with fiscal responsibility ‚Äî a hallmark of sound regulatory policy in publicly accountable corporate entities.
