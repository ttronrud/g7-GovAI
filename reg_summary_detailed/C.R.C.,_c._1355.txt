**Detailed Summary of the *Atlantic Pilotage Authority Pension Regulations***

---

### **Regulation Title**  
*Atlantic Pilotage Authority Pension Regulations*  
These regulations establish the transition of pension obligations from two historical pilotage pension funds‚Äî**the Halifax Pilots‚Äô Pension Fund** and **the Saint John Pilots‚Äô Pension Fund**‚Äîto the **Public Service Superannuation Account** under the *Public Service Superannuation Act* (the "Act").

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Public Service Superannuation Act*, which governs the superannuation (pension) system for public service employees in Canada. |
| **Authority** | The *Atlantic Pilotage Authority*, a government entity responsible for managing pilotage services in Atlantic Canada. |
| **Employee** | A pilot employed by the Authority who: <br> - Became a contributor under the Act on **February 1, 1973**; <br> - Was a contributor to either the *Halifax Pilots‚Äô Pension Fund* (on December 31, 1966) or the *Saint John Pilots‚Äô Pension Fund* (on January 31, 1973). |
| **Halifax By-law** | The *Halifax Pilotage District General By-law*, confirmed by P.C. 1961-70. |
| **Saint John By-law** | The *Saint John Pilotage District General By-law*, confirmed by P.C. 1961-1739. |
| **Superannuation Account** | The central pension account established under the Act for public service employees. |

---

## **Core Provisions and Timeline**

### **1. Transfer of Funds and Securities (Effective by September 30, 1973)**

- **Mandatory Transfer Deadline**: On or before **September 30, 1973**, the Authority must:
  - Pay **all moneys** (including accrued interest) in both the **Halifax Pilots‚Äô Pension Fund** and the **Saint John Pilots‚Äô Pension Fund** to the **Minister of Finance**.
  - Transfer **all securities** held in either fund (including accrued interest) to the Minister of Finance.

> ‚úÖ This ensures that all assets from the legacy pilotage pension funds are consolidated into the national public service superannuation system.

---

### **2. Consolidation into the Superannuation Account**

- The **Minister of Finance** must:
  - Receive the funds and securities from the Authority.
  - **Pay all moneys** received into the **Superannuation Account**.
  - **Liquidate all securities** and pay the proceeds (realized value) into the Superannuation Account.
  - Complete all actions **by September 30, 1973**.

> ‚úÖ This marks the formal integration of the pilotage pension funds into the broader public service pension framework.

---

### **3. Continuation of Pension Benefits (Post-Transfer)**

- Starting on the **first day of the month following September 30, 1973**, the Superannuation Account shall:
  - Pay out **all existing pension benefits** that were being paid from the Halifax or Saint John funds on that date.
  - Pay out **any additional pension benefits** that would have been due under the *Halifax By-law* or *Saint John By-law* as they existed on **February 1, 1973**, for pilots who:
    - Contributed under those by-laws;
    - Retired or were retired **before February 1, 1973**.

> üîç This provision ensures **continuity of pension rights** for pilots who retired prior to the transition date, protecting their entitlements under the old system.

---

### **4. Terms and Conditions of Pension Payments**

- All pension benefits paid under this regulation must be **subject to the same terms and conditions** as they would have been under:
  - Sections 26, 27, and 28 of the *Halifax By-law*;
  - Part II of the *Saint John By-law*,
  as those laws read on **February 1, 1973**.

> ‚úÖ This preserves the original pension structure and eligibility rules, ensuring fairness and consistency in benefit calculation.

---

### **5. Recognition of Pensionable Service (as of February 1, 1973)**

Each employee is deemed to have **pensionable service under the Act** as of **February 1, 1973**, equal to the **most recent portion** of their service during which they contributed under either the Halifax or Saint John By-law.

This service is calculated based on the following assumptions:

- **Service Period**: The period of active service during which the pilot contributed under the By-law.
- **Contribution Rate**: The amount of contributions made is calculated using **twice the rate of contributions** applicable under the Act for a comparable period of public service.
- **Salary Assumption**: The salary used for calculating pension entitlements is equal to the **actual compensation** the pilot received during that service period.
- **Interest Assumption**: Interest is assumed to have been paid on contributions at a rate **equal to the interest rate** under the Act during that period, calculated from the **middle of each fiscal year** to **February 1, 1973**.

> üìå This creates a fair and equitable way to value prior service under the old by-laws in terms of the new public service pension system.

---

### **6. Accumulated Contributions (Calculation and Treatment)**

For the purpose of determining pension entitlements, **accumulated contributions** are defined as:

> The total amount contributed to the Halifax or Saint John fund, **plus 3% annual interest**, compounded annually, calculated from the **middle of each fiscal year** during the period of active service until **February 1, 1973**.

> üí° This interest rate (3%) is a fixed, conservative rate applied to ensure fair valuation of past contributions.

---

### **7. Excess Contributions (Bonus Payment)**

- If the **accumulated contributions** (calculated under subsection 5(2)) **exceed** the amount required to purchase the full pensionable service under subsection 5(1), then:
  - The **excess amount** is paid directly to the employee.

> ‚úÖ This provides a **financial windfall** to pilots whose contributions were greater than what was needed to cover their service, recognizing their actual investment.

---

### **8. Optional Election to Purchase Remaining Service (Post-Transfer)**

If an employee has **unaccounted service** (i.e., service during which they contributed under the By-law that is not recognized as pensionable under subsection 5(1)), they may choose to **purchase that service** by paying a calculated amount.

#### Two Options:

| Option | Conditions |
|-------|------------|
| **(a)** Within 6 months of being informed of the remainder of service | Pay an amount equal to what would have been calculated under subsection 5(1), using the same methodology as in subsection 5(1). |
| **(b)** After 6 months | Pay an amount as if **Clause 5(1)(b)(iii)(K)** of the *Public Service Superannuation Act* applied to them. |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Clause 5(1)(b)(iii)(K) likely refers to a provision allowing for special treatment or enhanced contribution rates for certain service periods.  
> This gives employees flexibility to "buy back" unused service, though only if they act within a defined window.

> üìù This provision acknowledges that some service may not have been fully recognized and allows employees to **recoup value** through a financial contribution.

## **Key Objectives and Impacts**

| Objective | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Transition of Pension System** | Merges two local pilotage pension funds into the national public service pension system. |
| **Continuity of Benefits** | Ensures retired pilots continue to receive pensions under familiar terms and conditions. |
| **Fair Valuation of Service** | Uses a standardized method to convert historical contributions into equivalent pensionable service under the Act. |
| **Recognition of Past Contributions** | Includes interest and actual salary to reflect true value of past service. |
| **Employee Flexibility** | Offers a path for employees to claim value from unused service periods. |

---

## **Significance and Context**

- These regulations were introduced during a period of **restructuring** in Canada‚Äôs public service pension systems.
- The **Atlantic Pilotage Authority** was a specialized employer with unique pension arrangements prior to 1973.
- The transition ensures that **pilotage workers**, who were previously covered by local by-laws, are now integrated into the broader, standardized public service pension system.
- The use of **fixed interest rates**, **salary-based assumptions**, and **service period calculations** ensures consistency and fairness in pension entitlements.

---

## **Conclusion**

The *Atlantic Pilotage Authority Pension Regulations* represent a comprehensive and fair mechanism to:

1. **Transfer pension assets** from local pilotage funds to the national Public Service Superannuation Account,
2. **Preserve existing pension benefits** for those who retired before February 1, 1973,
3. **Recognize and value prior service** under the old by-laws in terms of the new Act,
4. **Provide financial incentives** for employees with excess contributions,
5. **Offer flexibility** for employees to purchase unused service periods.
