**Detailed Summary of the Proclamation by Her Excellency Michaëlle Jean, Governor General of Canada (27 September 2005)**

This proclamation, issued by **Her Excellency the Right Honourable Michaëlle Jean**, serves as an official public announcement confirming her appointment as **Governor General and Commander-in-Chief of Canada**. It is a formal, constitutional document that marks the beginning of her duties in the role and ensures continuity in the functioning of government institutions under the Crown.

---

### **1. Legal and Constitutional Context**

- The proclamation begins by referencing the **Royal Commission under the Great Seal of Canada**, dated **September 10, 2005**, which formally appointed Michaëlle Jean as Governor General and Commander-in-Chief in and over Canada.
- It cites the **Letters Patent of 1947**, which establish the powers, duties, and constitutional framework of the Office of the Governor General. These Letters Patent remain the foundational legal instrument governing the office.
- The proclamation affirms that Michaëlle Jean was authorized to exercise **all powers and directions** contained in these Letters Patent, including any subsequent amendments or additions made in later instruments.

---

### **2. Formalization of Appointment and Oath of Office**

- The document emphasizes that the appointment was **read and published with full solemnity** before:
  - The **Chief Justice or another Supreme Court of Canada judge**, and
  - Members of the **Queen’s Privy Council for Canada**.
- This public ceremony is a constitutional requirement to validate the appointment and demonstrate the legitimacy of the office within Canada’s legal and ceremonial framework.
- Michaëlle Jean has taken the **oaths prescribed by the Letters Patent**, affirming her commitment to uphold the laws, traditions, and constitutional duties of the office.

---

### **3. Purpose and Content of the Proclamation**

The primary purpose of this proclamation is **to formally announce**:
- Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II’s appointment of Michaëlle Jean as Governor General.
- Her assumption of office and the commencement of her duties as **Commander-in-Chief of Canada**.

It also serves as a **constitutional directive** to ensure continuity and stability in government operations.

---

### **4. Key Provisions and Commands**

- **Continuity of Government Functions**:  
  The Governor General commands that all **Her Majesty’s Officers and Ministers** in Canada continue to perform their duties in their respective offices, places, and employments. This ensures that public administration, legislative processes, and executive functions proceed without interruption.

- **Notice to the Public**:  
  The proclamation instructs **all subjects and stakeholders** (including citizens, institutions, and public bodies) to take notice and govern themselves accordingly. This establishes the legal and practical authority of the new Governor General and reinforces the Crown’s role as a unifying and constitutional figure.

---

### **5. Constitutional Significance**

- This proclamation is a **key constitutional act** that:
  - Confirms the **legitimacy of the Governor General’s office**.
  - Demonstrates the **continuity of the Crown’s representation** in Canada.
  - Reinforces the **separation of powers** and the role of the Governor General as a ceremonial and constitutional head of state, acting on the advice of the elected government.
- As Commander-in-Chief, Michaëlle Jean holds a symbolic and formal role in national defense and military matters, though actual military command is exercised by the Canadian Armed Forces under civilian leadership.

---

### **6. Date and Venue**

- **Date**: 27 September 2005  
- **Location**: Ottawa, the capital of Canada  
- This date marks the official start of her term as Governor General, following the formal appointment process.

- The document is **signed with the Privy Seal**, a formal and traditional symbol of the authority of the Crown in Canada.

---

### **7. Broader Implications**

- This proclamation reflects the **constitutional continuity** of Canada’s parliamentary democracy, where the Governor General serves as a constitutional figurehead.
- It underscores the **independence of the Crown** from political influence, even as the office is exercised in accordance with the advice of the Prime Minister and Cabinet.
- It also highlights the **ceremonial and legal importance** of formal oaths and public proclamations in Canadian governance.

---

### **Conclusion**

This proclamation is a foundational document in the constitutional life of Canada. It formally announces the appointment of Michaëlle Jean as Governor General and Commander-in-Chief, confirms the legal basis of her office through the 1947 Letters Patent, and ensures that all government institutions and citizens operate under the recognized authority of the Crown. It is a vital expression of Canada’s constitutional order, emphasizing continuity, legality, and the symbolic role of the Governor General in national life.

---

**Note**: While the document is largely ceremonial, it carries significant legal weight within Canada’s constitutional framework and is a key part of the transition of office for a new Governor General.
