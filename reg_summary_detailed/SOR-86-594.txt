## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Civil Aviation Tribunal Rules (Effective June 1, 1986)**

### üìå **Background and Legal Basis**
- These rules were **approved by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Transport**, pursuant to **subsection 29(3)** of the *Aeronautics Act*.
- The regulation was enacted under the authority of the *Transportation Appeal Tribunal of Canada Act* (S.C. 1985, c. 28, s. 5).
- **Note**: This regulation has since been **repealed** by *SOR/2017-202, s. 2*, meaning it is no longer in force as of 2017. However, this summary provides a historical and legal analysis of its content and purpose.

These rules apply to:
1. **Requests for review** and **appeals** brought before the Tribunal under statutes listed in **subsection 2(2) or (3)** of the *Act*.
2. **Applications** referred to in **section 10** of the *Act* (e.g., applications for relief or orders).

## üìÆ Service of Documents

- **General Rule**: Documents (except summons under section 14) must be served by:
  - **Personal service**, or  
  - **Registered mail**.
- **Date of Service**:
  - For **registered mail**: The date of **receipt** of the document is considered the date of service.
- **Service of Summons**:
  - Must be **personal service** at least **48 hours** before the scheduled appearance.

## üìÑ Applications Before the Tribunal

### General Requirements:
- Must be in **writing** unless the Tribunal determines **exceptional circumstances** allow an alternative method.
- Must:
  - Clearly state the **grounds** upon which the application is based, and  
  - Specify the **relief or order** being sought.

### Notice and Opportunity to Respond:
- Upon receiving an application, the Tribunal must:
  - **Serve notice** on all other parties, and  
  - Provide each party with a **reasonable opportunity to make representations**.

### Tribunal‚Äôs Discretion:
- The Tribunal may:
  - Decide the application based on **material submitted by all parties**, or  
  - Decide **on the basis of material submitted by the applicant alone**, if the Tribunal finds **exigent circumstances** (urgent or pressing reasons).

### Determination of Application:
- The Tribunal must render a **written determination**.
- This determination must be **served immediately** on all parties after being rendered.

### Extension or Abatement of Time Limits:
- The Tribunal may **extend or shorten** time limits for any action or act **on terms it deems just**.

## ü§ù Conference and Consultation Powers

The Tribunal may:
- **Orally or in writing** direct parties to:
  - Hold a **conference** with a member of the Tribunal at a specified date, time, and place, or  
  - **Consult each other** and submit written suggestions on matters such as:
    1. Admission or proof of certain facts  
    2. Procedural issues  
    3. Exchange of documents and exhibits  
    4. Need to call particular witnesses  
    5. Any other matter that may **simplify evidence or expedite disposition** of the case

> üí° Purpose: To promote efficiency, reduce unnecessary hearings, and streamline evidence presentation.

## üìù Adjournment of Proceedings

- The Tribunal may **adjourn** a proceeding **at any time**:
  - Upon **application by a party**, or  
  - On its **own motion**  
- The adjournment shall be granted **on terms that seem just**.

## üìÑ Witness Summons and Appearance

### Issuance of Summons:
- A party may request the **registrar to issue a blank summons** for a witness.
- The party completing the summons is responsible for filling it in.

### Service of Summons:
- Must be served **personally** at least **48 hours** before the scheduled appearance.

### Witness Fees and Expenses:
- The party requesting the witness must **pay witness fees and travel expenses** in accordance with **Rule 42 of the Federal Courts Rules**.

---

## üîç Enforcement for Non-Appearance

If a witness fails to appear:
- The requesting party may apply to the Tribunal for a **warrant** directing a **peace officer** to:
  1. **Apprehend** the witness anywhere in Canada,  
  2. **Detain them in custody**, and immediately bring them before the Tribunal until their testimony is no longer needed; or  
  3. **Release them on recognizance** (with or without sureties), **conditional on their appearance** at a specified date, time, and place.

> üìù **Application Requirements**:
> The application must show that:
> - The witness was **served with the summons** (per Rule 14(2)),  
> - The witness was **paid or offered fees and travel expenses** (per Rule 14(3)),  
> - The witness **failed to appear** as required, and  
> - The witness‚Äôs presence is **material to the proceeding**.

## üìö Evidence and Procedure

### Examination of Witnesses:
- Witnesses must be examined and cross-examined **orally** on **oath or solemn affirmation**.

### Exclusion of Witnesses:
- The Tribunal may **order a witness to be excluded** from the proceeding until they are called to give evidence.

### Proof by Affidavit:
- With the **consent of all parties**, the Tribunal may order that a fact be proved by **affidavit**.

### Inspection of Property:
- The Tribunal may **inspect any property or thing** for the purpose of evaluating evidence.

### Written Arguments:
- The Tribunal may **direct a party to submit written arguments** in addition to oral arguments.

---

## üì£ Appeals to the Tribunal

### Filing a Request for Appeal:
- Must be in **writing** and filed with the Tribunal.
- Must include a **concise statement of the grounds** upon which the appeal is based.

### Service of Appeal Request:
- Within **10 days of filing**, the Tribunal must serve a copy of the request on **all other parties**.

### Notice of Hearing:
- Upon filing, the Tribunal must:
  - Serve a **notice of the date, time, and place** of the appeal hearing, and  
  - Serve a **copy of the record** (as defined in section 20 of the Act) related to the appeal.

### Determination of Appeal:
- The Tribunal shall issue a **written determination or decision**:
  - At the **conclusion of the hearing**, or  
  - As soon as **feasible** after the hearing.

### Timing of Appeal Period:
- For the purpose of calculating appeal deadlines:
  - The **determination is deemed to be made on the day it is served** on the party.

### Service of Determination:
- A copy of the decision is **immediately served** on each party after it is rendered.
