**Detailed Summary of the Women Deliver 2019 Global Conference — Privileges and Immunities Order**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Origin**
This regulation is issued under the authority of the **Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act (FMIOA)**, specifically **paragraph 5(1)(c)a** of that Act. This provision allows the Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs) to grant privileges and immunities to international conferences or events involving foreign state representatives, provided such privileges are necessary for the proper functioning of the event and in accordance with international norms.

The Order is formally registered under **S.C. 2002, c. 12, s. 3(2)** and **S.C. 1991, c. 41**, which are foundational statutes governing the legal framework for the recognition and treatment of foreign missions and international organizations in Canada.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Order**
The primary purpose of this Order is to **grant specific privileges and immunities to official delegates from foreign states** attending the **Women Deliver 2019 Global Conference**, which is a major international event focused on global women’s health, rights, and gender equality.

These privileges and immunities are provided to ensure that delegates can perform their official duties without undue interference or disruption, in a manner consistent with international diplomatic standards.

---

### **3. Definition of the Conference**
In this Order, the term **"Conference"** specifically refers to the **Ministerial portion** of the Women Deliver 2019 Global Conference. This portion is defined as the formal gathering of high-level government officials and ministers from various countries, taking place in **Vancouver, British Columbia**, from **June 3 to 5, 2019**.

Note: The Order does not extend to non-ministerial or public-facing portions of the conference (e.g., public forums, workshops, or media events), which may be subject to different rules.

---

### **4. Scope of Privileges and Immunities**
From **June 1 to July 7, 2019**, representatives of foreign states who are **official delegates** to the Conference are entitled to the following privileges and immunities:

#### **A. Privileges and Immunities from Article IV of the United Nations Convention (as set out in Schedule III to the FMIOA):**
These are the core rights granted under international law, specifically **paragraphs 11(a) to (f)** and **Section 12** of Article IV of the **Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations**.

- **Paragraph 11(a)**: Immunity from civil and criminal jurisdiction in Canada for acts performed in the course of official duties.
- **Paragraph 11(b)**: Immunity from arrest or detention for acts performed in the course of official duties.
- **Paragraph 11(c)**: Immunity from service of process (e.g., legal subpoenas or court orders) in relation to official functions.
- **Paragraph 11(d)**: Immunity from search or seizure of personal property or official documents in the course of official duties.
- **Paragraph 11(e)**: Immunity from liability for acts performed in the course of official duties.
- **Paragraph 11(f)**: Immunity from liability for expenses incurred in the performance of official duties.
- **Section 12**: Protection from interference with official communications and the right to conduct official business without disruption.

#### **B. Additional Immunities (as set out in Sections 14 to 16 of Article IV):**
These are more specific immunities related to:
- **Section 14**: Protection of official communications (e.g., telegrams, correspondence) from inspection or disclosure.
- **Section 15**: Protection of official documents and records from seizure or inspection.
- **Section 16**: Protection of the right to use official premises and facilities without interference.

> These provisions ensure that delegates can operate freely and securely, without fear of legal action or intrusion, while carrying out their official responsibilities during the Conference.

---

### **5. Time Period and Applicability**
The privileges and immunities apply from **June 1 to July 7, 2019** — a seven-day window extending **before and after** the official Ministerial session (June 3–5). This extension allows for:
- Travel arrangements
- Pre-conference preparations
- Post-conference follow-up activities

The inclusion of the pre- and post-conference periods ensures that delegates can conduct necessary official business without legal or administrative barriers during the entire duration of their participation.

---

### **6. Jurisdiction and Enforcement**
- The privileges and immunities are **recognized and enforceable in Canada** under Canadian law.
- They are granted **only to official delegates** of foreign states, not to private individuals, journalists, or non-governmental participants.
- The privileges are **limited to the functions directly related to the Conference** and do not extend to personal conduct or unrelated activities.

---

### **7. Entry into Force**
This Order comes into force on the **day it is registered** with the Canada Gazette, which is the official publication of federal laws and regulations. This ensures that all relevant government agencies, courts, and officials are aware of and bound by the provisions from the moment of registration.

---

### **8. Key Legal and Diplomatic Implications**
- This Order reflects Canada’s commitment to international norms and diplomatic practice.
- It enables the Women Deliver 2019 Conference to operate effectively by ensuring that high-level government officials can participate in discussions and negotiations without fear of legal repercussions.
- It also demonstrates Canada’s recognition of the importance of gender-based global policy discussions and its support for multilateral diplomacy.

---

### **Conclusion**
The *Women Deliver 2019 Global Conference — Privileges and Immunities Order* is a targeted, legally grounded regulation that provides essential diplomatic protections to foreign government delegates attending a key international conference on women’s health and rights. By aligning with established international standards (specifically the UN Convention on Privileges and Immunities), the Order ensures the smooth and respectful conduct of high-level diplomatic engagement in Canada, while maintaining the integrity of Canadian legal and public order.

This Order is a practical application of Canada’s international obligations and serves as a model for how similar events involving foreign state participation can be legally and diplomatically managed.
