**Detailed Summary of the Court of Appeal Criminal Appeal Rules (Saskatchewan)**  
*Effective Date: November 25, 2010*  
*Issued by: Court of Appeal for Saskatchewan under sections 482a and 482.1b of the Criminal Code (S.C. 2002, c. 13, s. 17 & 18; R.S., c. C-46)*

---

### **I. Purpose and Scope of the Rules**

The **Court of Appeal Criminal Appeal Rules (Saskatchewan)** are designed to ensure the **orderly, efficient, and fair administration of justice** in criminal appeals within the jurisdiction of the Court of Appeal for Saskatchewan. These rules govern all criminal appeals, including appeals from conviction, acquittal, sentence, or orders under the *Summary Offences Procedure Act, 1990*, and are aligned with the procedural framework of the *Criminal Code (Canada)* and the *Court of Appeal Act, 2000*.

---

### **II. Key Definitions**

- **Appellant**: The person bringing the appeal.
- **Respondent**: The person against whom the appeal is brought (e.g., the convicted offender or the Crown).
- **Judge**: A judge as defined in the *Court of Appeal Act, 2000*.
- **Registrar**: The official responsible for managing the filing and administrative aspects of appeals.
- **Notice of Appeal (NOA)**: The formal document that initiates an appeal.
- **File**: To submit a document with the registrar.
- **Represented**: When a person is represented by legal counsel.
- **Offender**: A person convicted of a criminal offence.
- **Code**: The *Criminal Code (Canada)*.
- **Court**: The Court of Appeal for Saskatchewan.

> *Note:* Definitions in sections 2 and 673 of the Code are incorporated into these rules.

---

### **III. Applicability of the Rules**

These rules apply to:
1. All criminal appeals initiated under **section 482(1)** and **section 482.1** of the *Criminal Code*, including appeals from:
   - Convictions
   - Acquittals
   - Sentences
   - Orders made in summary conviction proceedings
2. Appeals from **summary conviction matters** under the *Summary Offences Procedure Act, 1990*, specifically under **section 839** of the Code.

> *General Principle:* Where appropriate, the *Court of Appeal Rules for Civil Matters* (as amended) apply, with necessary modifications.

---

### **IV. Flexibility and Judicial Discretion**

The Court or a judge may:
- Issue **practice directives** on any matter within the rules' scope.
- **Waive compliance** or relieve parties from non-compliance if it serves justice.
- **Enlarge or abridge time periods** for filing documents, with or without prior notice.
- Make such orders **before or after** the fixed deadline.

---

### **V. Filing a Notice of Appeal (NOA)**

#### **Timing and Who Must File**
- **Offenders** must file a NOA within **30 days** of the **imposition of sentence**.
- **The Attorney General (Crown)** must file a NOA within:
  - 30 days of **acquittal**, or
  - 30 days of **sentence imposition** (if appealing from conviction or sentence).

#### **Key Clarifications**
- For appeals from **conviction and sentence**, including appeals under **Part XXIV (Dangerous Offenders and Long-term Offenders)**:
  - The 30-day period starts from the **date of sentence imposition**.
  - Only **one Notice of Appeal** is allowed.

#### **Form of the NOA**
- **Form A**: Used by offenders (represented or self-represented, in or out of custody).
- **Form B**: Used by the Attorney General.

> **Special Provision**: Every penal institution must provide inmates with a copy of Form A upon request.

#### **Self-Representation and Retention of Counsel**
- If an offender is initially self-represented and later hires counsel:
  - Counsel may **amend or file a new NOA** at any time before the factum is filed.
  - The amendment or new NOA must be filed before the offender's factum is submitted.

---

### **VI. Filing of Facts (Factums)**

#### **General Requirement**
- Every appellant and respondent must file a **factum** (written argument) unless self-represented.
- A factum must be filed **within specific timeframes**, depending on the type of appeal.

#### **Filing Deadlines by Appeal Type**

| Appeal Type | Appellant Deadline | Respondent Deadline |
|-----------|--------------------|----------------------|
| Appeal from sentence (not Part XXIV) | 20 days after receipt of transcript | 10 days after receipt of appellant’s factum |
| Summary conviction appeal (section 839) | 30 days after NOA | 30 days after receipt of appellant’s factum |
| All other appeals (conviction, acquittal, Part XXIV, mixed matters) | 60 days after receipt of transcript | 30 days after receipt of appellant’s factum |

> *Note:* Deadlines are subject to judicial modification.

#### **Content of a Factum (Seven Required Parts)**

1. **Introduction**  
   - Brief summary of the context and background of the appeal.

2. **Jurisdiction and Standard of Review**  
   - Appellant: States the basis for appeal, jurisdiction of the Court of Appeal, and appellate standard (e.g., correctness, reasonableness).  
   - Respondent: States its position on jurisdiction and standard of review.

3. **Summary of Facts**  
   - Appellant: Concisely outlines key facts.  
   - Respondent: Challenges or clarifies facts, especially those not in the transcript.

4. **Points in Issue**  
   - Appellant: Identifies legal or factual issues raised.  
   - Respondent: States its position on the points it intends to challenge.  
   - *If the respondent wishes to uphold the decision on grounds not raised by the appellant*, it must explicitly state this intention.

5. **Argument**  
   - Concise statement of legal or factual arguments.  
   - Must reference:
     - Specific **page and line numbers** in the transcript.
     - **Authorities** cited (case law, statutes).
   - Arguments must be well-structured and directly tied to the facts.

6. **Relief**  
   - Precise order the party seeks (e.g., reversal of conviction, sentence reduction, dismissal).

7. **Authorities**  
   - Alphabetical table of cited cases.
   - Must cite **Supreme Court Reports (SCR)** where possible.
   - If citing electronic databases, **must also provide print citation**.

> All paragraphs in Parts I–VI must be **numbered consecutively**.

---

#### **Appendices to Factums**

**For Appeals (not under section 839):**
- Must include:
  - Notice of Appeal
  - Information/indictment or youth sentence order
  - Written reasons of the lower court judge (if not in transcript)
  - Paper exhibits relied upon by counsel

**For Appeals under section 839 (Summary Conviction):**
- Must include:
  - NOA filed in the Queen’s Bench
  - Transcript of Provincial Court proceedings (if appeal is on unreasonable verdict or lack of evidence)
  - Relevant parts of transcript
  - Written reasons of the Provincial Court judge (if not in transcript)
  - Certificate of conviction or youth sentence order
  - NOA
  - Written reasons or transcript of Queen’s Bench judge (if no reasons exist)

> *Note:* If the appellant is **self-represented**, the respondent must include these appendices.

---

#### **Formatting Requirements**

- **Cover**:
  - Appellant: **Buff** color
  - Respondent: **Green** color
  - Must include:
    - Court number
    - Style of cause (e.g., *R. v. Smith*)
    - Whether appellant or respondent
    - Names if multiple parties

- **Printing**:
  - One side only, with **pages facing up on the left**
  - **12-point font**
  - **1.5 line spacing** (except for quotations, which are **indented and single-spaced**)
  - **Margins of at least 3.0 cm (1.5 inches)**

- **Appendices**:
  - Must be printed on **both sides** of the paper

- **Table of Contents**:
  - Required immediately after cover
  - All pages numbered consecutively
  - Bound in the sequence outlined in **Rule 20**

- **Signature**:
  - The **counsel responsible** must sign the factum.

---

### **VII. Service of Documents**

- **The Attorney General** is responsible for serving documents.
- Service is:
  - **Personal** unless:
    - The appellant/respondent is **represented**, in which case service may be made per *Queen’s Bench Rules, Part Three*; or
    - A **court order** is obtained under **section 678.1 of the Code**.
- **Proof of Service**:
  - The Attorney General must file a **Certificate of Service in Form D**.
