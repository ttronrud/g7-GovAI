## üîç **Comprehensive Summary of the Federal Real Property Regulations (S.C. 1991, c. 50)**

### üìå **Background and Purpose**
This regulation was issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Justice** and the **Treasury Board**, pursuant to **subsections 15(2) and 16(2) of the Federal Real Property and Federal Immovables Act (the "Act")**.

It **revokes and replaces** the following outdated regulations:
- *Government Land Purchase Regulations* (P.C. 1978-1749)
- *Public Lands Leasing and Licensing Regulations* (P.C. 1982-2718)
- *Public Lands Sale Regulations (Transport)* (C.R.C., c. 1327)
- *Public Works Leasing Regulations* (C.R.C., c. 1364)

The new regulations establish a **modern, coherent, and centralized framework** for how the Government of Canada acquires, disposes of, manages, and administers **federal real property and immovables** ‚Äî including land, buildings, and other tangible assets under Crown ownership.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note:** These regulations were **repealed in 2020** (SOR/2020-134, s. 2), meaning they are **no longer in force**. However, this summary provides a detailed historical and legal analysis of their content and intent, which may still be relevant for understanding the evolution of federal property governance.

## üö´ **Exclusions and Exceptions**

These regulations **do not apply** in the following situations:
1. **Expropriations** of real property or immovables by the Crown.
2. **Dispositions** (other than by lease, easement, or servitude) where the **full purchase price or consideration is not received by the Crown at or before completion**.

**Exception:**  
Even if full payment is not received, the regulations **do apply** to dispositions to **Crown corporations** (as defined under subsection 83(1) of the *Financial Administration Act*), **provided**:
- Their incorporation instruments specifically limit their activities to acquiring, leasing, holding, managing, selling, or disposing of real or personal property.

## ‚úÖ Core Powers and Responsibilities of Ministers

A **Minister** (of any department) has broad authority to:
1. **Enter into acquisitions or dispositions** of federal real property or immovables.
2. **Give or acquire licences** for use of federal property.
3. **Relinquish or accept relinquishment** of licences (as licensor or licensee).
4. **Provide utilities and services** (e.g., water, electricity) on or from federal property under their administration, and **impose fees, charges, and rates** for such services.
5. **Transfer administration and control** of federal real property or immovables:
   - To another Minister
   - To an **agent corporation** authorized under another Act of Parliament to acquire or dispose of such property
6. **Transfer or accept administrative responsibility** for a licence.
7. **Accept retrocession or reversion** (return) of administration/control to the Crown from a province ‚Äî even if the original transfer required an Order in Council or written notice.

> üîê **Note:** All such transfers and acceptances must be:
> - In **writing**
> - Signed by both parties
> - **Countersigned by the Minister of Justice**
> - **Consented to in writing** by the agent corporation (if involved)

## üí∞ Financial Provisions: Payments and Interest

### 1. **Payment for Acquisitions**
- A Minister may agree to pay:
  - Legal fees and disbursements reasonably incurred by the owner (approved by Minister)
  - Taxes and other adjustments
- If the acquisition is **unreasonably delayed** (due to no fault of the owner), interest may be paid at a rate **not exceeding 1.5% above** the average accepted tender rate of **3-month Treasury Bills** (announced by the Bank of Canada).
  - This rate is the **last tender rate** announced before the contract was executed (or signed in Quebec).

### 2. **Payment Conditions**
- **No payment** is made until **good title** is obtained and is **satisfactory to the Minister of Justice**.
- A **partial payment** may be made **before completion**, under an arrangement approved by the Minister of Justice, **with a guarantee of repayment** if title is not satisfactory.

### 3. **Exemptions**
- Does **not apply** to:
  - **Options for acquisition**
  - Acquisitions by:
    - Minister of **CSIS** (for security investigations)
    - Minister of **RCMP** (for security or criminal investigations)

### 4. **Foreign Acquisitions**
- The **Minister of Foreign Affairs** may make payments for property **outside Canada**, if:
  - Payment follows **commercial practice** in the jurisdiction
  - Ensures **immediate possession** upon full payment
  - Ensures **repayment** if good title cannot be obtained or certified
- **Exception:** In jurisdictions where title cannot be determined or certified, payment may still be made if:
  - It follows commercial practice
  - Immediate possession is guaranteed

## üìù Disposition Process and Approval

- Every disposition must be **referred to the Minister of Justice** for:
  - Settlement
  - Approval of the **form and legal content** of the Crown grant
- This requirement applies **except**:
  - When the Minister of Foreign Affairs is authorized to perform legal services under the *Government Contracts Regulations*
  - When a disposition involves property acquired by CSIS or RCMP for investigations (as per paragraph 8(3)(b))

> ‚úÖ **Conclusive Evidence:** If a Crown grant is countersigned by the Minister of Justice, it is **conclusive proof** that the Minister of Justice has approved its form and content.

## üõ†Ô∏è Option Agreements and Entry Rights

If a Minister is granted an **option to enter and conduct tests** on real property:
- May agree with the option-granting party to:
  - **Indemnify** them from claims arising from entry
  - **Repair or compensate** for damage to their **non-option property**
  - In the event the option is **not exercised**, repair or compensate for damage to the **subject property**

## üìö Recordkeeping Requirements

The **Minister of Justice** is responsible for establishing and operating a **Document Depository** at the Department of Justice to store copies of key instruments and acts, including:

### Required Instruments:
1. **Grants or concessions** of federal real property or immovable (excluding):
   - Letters patent
   - Notifications
   - Leases
   - Dispositions of property acquired under paragraph 8(3)(b) of the Act
2. **Transfers of administration and control** of real property or immovable
3. **Acceptances** of such transfers
4. **Transfers of administration** of federal property
5. **Acceptances** of such transfers
6. **Transfers of administrative responsibility** for a licence and acceptances thereof

### When Must Copies Be Sent?
A copy must be sent **immediately** to the depository when:
- A Minister **ceases to administer** federal property due to:
  - A grant or concession (paragraph 5(1)(b))
  - Transfer of administration and control to the Crown (not in right of Canada)
  - Transfer to an agent corporation
- A Minister **acquires administration** via:
  - Transfer of administration and control to the Crown
  - Transfer from another Minister or agent corporation
- A Minister **acquires administrative responsibility** for a licence via transfer

> üìù All instruments, acts, and related information may be recorded or stored using **any means** (digital, physical, etc.)
