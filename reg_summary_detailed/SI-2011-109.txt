### **Detailed Summary of Rule 82 – Criminal Appeal, Prince Edward Island Court of Appeal (Effective January 1, 2012)**

This regulation, issued by the **Prince Edward Island Court of Appeal** on **November 30, 2011**, repeals **Rule 67** of the Supreme Court and Appeal Division Rules and replaces it with **Rule 82 – Criminal Appeal**, which came into force on **January 1, 2012**. The rule governs the procedural and practical aspects of criminal appeals in Prince Edward Island, including the filing of notices of appeal, service of documents, transcript preparation, appeal books, and the conduct of arguments.

The rule is grounded in the **Criminal Code (R.S.C. 1985, c. C-27)** and applies to appeals under **Part XXI**, **Part XXVI**, and **section 839 of the Code**, as well as any other criminal appeal filed in the Court related to proceedings governed by criminal procedures. It is consistent with the broader framework of the Code and supersedes prior rules, streamlining and clarifying procedures for criminal appeals in PEI.

---

## **1. Key Definitions**

The rule begins with a comprehensive list of definitions that establish the meaning of key terms used throughout:

- **Appeal**: Includes both an application for leave to appeal and a cross-appeal.
- **Appellant**: The person appealing from conviction, sentence, or both. In a Crown appeal, it refers to Her Majesty the Queen via the Attorney General.
- **Attorney General**: Includes the Attorney General and any counsel instructed by them; also includes the Queen in a Crown appeal.
- **Chief Justice**: The Chief Justice of PEI or the next senior judge if the Chief Justice is absent.
- **Code**: Refers to the *Criminal Code*, R.S.C. 1985, c. C-27.
- **Court**: The Prince Edward Island Court of Appeal.
- **Chief Court Reporter / Court Reporter / Court Transcriber**: Designated personnel under the *Court Reporters Act* (R.S.P.E.I. 1988, Cap. S-10.1).
- **Judge**: A judge of the Court, including a judge of the Supreme Court sitting ex-officio.
- **Judgment**: The formal decision of the Court, including orders for judgment.
- **Notice of Appeal**: Includes notices for leave to appeal and cross-appeal.
- **Prisoner Appeal**: An appeal by a detained individual not represented by counsel.
- **Provincial Court / Youth Justice Court**: Courts in which criminal trials occur.
- **Registrar**: The official of the Court responsible for administrative functions.
- **Respondent**: The Queen (represented by the Attorney General) in appeals against conviction or sentence; otherwise, the person whose acquittal or sentence is appealed.
- **Trial Court**: The court from which the appeal is taken.
- **Trial Judge**: The judge who presided over the trial.
- **Youth Justice Court**: Refers to courts under the *Youth Criminal Justice Act*.

> **Note**: Interpretation and definitions from sections 2 and 673 of the Criminal Code apply to this rule.

---

## **2. Scope of Application**

Rule 82 applies to:

- Appeals under **Part XXI** (appeals from conviction or sentence),
- **Part XXVI** (appeals from acquittal or sentence),
- **Section 839** of the Code (appeals from decisions of summary conviction appeal courts),
- Any other criminal appeal filed in the Court in relation to a matter processed under criminal procedures.

> **Exception**: The rule does not conflict with provisions of the Code or other statutes, nor with uniform rules made by the Governor in Council under subsection 482(5) of the Code.

Additionally, the provisions on **prisoner appeals** are extended to apply to individuals **detained in custody**, even if not yet convicted, who appeal under **section 672.72** of the Criminal Code.

---

## **3. Filing of Notice of Appeal**

An appeal must be commenced by filing a **notice of appeal** that sets out the **grounds of appeal**.

### **Form Requirements**:
- **Prisoner Appeals**: Use **Form 82B**.
- **All Other Appeals (convicted person or Crown)**: Use **Form 82A**.

### **Filing Deadlines**:
| Type of Appeal | Deadline |
|---------------|---------|
| From conviction or conviction + sentence | Within **30 days** of sentence |
| From acquittal | Within **30 days** of acquittal |
| Acquitted but convicted of an included offence | Within 30 days of sentence for the included offence |
| Appeal from one or more counts in an indictment | Within 30 days of acquittal or sentence for any count |
| Under section 784 or 839 of the Code | Within 30 days of decision or, if reserved, after written reasons are filed |

### **Service of Notice**:

#### **Prisoner Appeals**:
- Delivered to the **senior official of the penal institution**.
- The official must **endorse the date of receipt** and return a copy to the prisoner.
- The **original is forwarded to the Registrar**.

#### **Non-Prisoner Appeals (Crown or Convicted Person)**:
- Filed with the **Registrar**:
  - Original + **four copies**, or
  - Sent by **prepaid registered mail**.

> Upon receipt, the Registrar forwards a copy to:
- The **Attorney General**
- The **trial court**

#### **Appeal by the Attorney General**:
- Filed with the Registrar.
- Service on the respondent and other parties must occur within **30 days** and may be done via:
  - Personal service
  - Service on legal counsel (if accepted)
  - Service on senior official if respondent is in custody
  - Verified facsimile transmission
  - Any other method directed by the Court or a judge

> **Proof of service** must be filed with the Registrar promptly.

---

## **4. Cross-Appeal**

- A notice of **cross-appeal** must be filed **within 30 days** of receiving the notice of appeal.
- Must be served in accordance with this Rule.

---

## **5. Leave to Appeal**

Leave is required in most cases, and arguments on leave are presented at the **hearing of the appeal**, unless:

1. The appeal is from **sentence only**, and the appellant has applied under **section 679** of the Code for release from custody pending appeal;
2. The appellant or respondent requests **prior determination of leave**, with supporting materials;
3. The Court **orders a separate hearing** to determine leave.

> The Court may:
- Grant leave
- Refuse leave
- Postpone the decision until the appeal hearing

---

## **6. Trial Judge’s Report**

If the Court requests a report from the trial judge:

- The parties are given **opportunity to submit** on:
  - Whether the report should be furnished
  - The **scope** of the report

> Upon receipt, the **Registrar forwards copies** to the parties.

---

## **7. Written Arguments (Factums)**

Parties may present arguments in writing instead of appearing in person or by counsel.

### **Appellant**:
- Must state intention in notice of appeal.
- May:
  - Include arguments in the notice of appeal, or
  - File and serve an **appellant’s factum** within time prescribed under **section 82.14(1)**

### **Respondent**:
- Must file and serve a **respondent’s factum** and a **written notice** stating they do not intend to appear, within the time prescribed under **section 82.14(3)**

---

## **8. Transcript of Proceedings**

Only **necessary portions** of the transcript must be filed, unless otherwise ordered.

### **When a Full Transcript is Required**:
- Only in specific cases:
  - Prisoner appeal
  - Appeal from a **summary conviction appeal court**
  - Order by a judge

> In all other cases, only **portions necessary to determine the appeal issues** are required.

### **Filing Requirements**:
- Appellant must file a **request for transcript and certificate (Form 82C)** with the notice of appeal.
- Within **15 days**, must file a **certificate of Chief Court Reporter or Court Reporter (Form 82E)** confirming receipt of the request.

### **Prisoner Appeals**:
- Attorney General must:
  - Send request for transcript and certificate (Form 82C) and certificate (Form 82E)
  - File copies with Registrar
  - Forward copies to prisoner

---

## **9. What is Excluded from the Transcript**

The transcript **must not include** the following, unless ordered otherwise:

- Proceedings on challenge for cause of a juror
- Opening address of the trial judge
- Opening and closing addresses of counsel
- Proceedings in absence of the jury (except for):
  - Rulings on admissibility of evidence after voir dire
  - Submissions on charge content and trial judge’s rulings
  - Objections to charge and judge’s rulings
  - Objections to evidence (only the objection is noted; full ruling included unless grounds relate to the appeal)

> **Exception**: If the Court or parties agree, or if the Court orders, specific portions may be included.

---

## **10. Transcript in Summary Conviction Appeal Cases**

- The transcript must include:
  - The transcript as submitted to the summary conviction appeal court
