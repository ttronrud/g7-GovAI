**Detailed Summary of the *Shipbuilding Temporary Assistance Program Regulations***

---

### **1. Title and Citation**
The regulation is officially titled the **Shipbuilding Temporary Assistance Program Regulations** and may be cited as such. It serves as a framework for providing financial assistance (shipbuilding grants) to Canadian shipbuilders for the construction of eligible vessels.

### **3. Scope and Historical Context**
- These regulations apply **only to vessels whose construction began before November 27, 1970**, and whose **contract date is on or before March 31, 1975**.
- The regulation has been **revoked** (SOR/78-842, s.1), indicating it is no longer in force â€” likely superseded by newer legislation or programs.

### **4. Grant Eligibility and Application Process**

#### **Eligibility Conditions**
- A vessel must:
  - Be constructed in Canada by a Canadian shipbuilder.
  - Be registered in a country **other than Canada** (exceptionally allowed).
- The Minister may declare a vessel eligible even if it will be registered abroad, provided certain conditions are met.

#### **Ministerial Considerations Before Declaration**
Before declaring a vessel eligible, the Minister must assess:
1. Benefits to Canada from using Canadian materials and components.
2. Whether the shipbuilder can fulfill the contract without over-expanding facilities.
3. Availability of shipbuilding capacity for domestic needs.
4. Long-term competitive strength of the shipbuilder and Canadian shipbuilding industry.
5. Market development implications of the contract.

#### **Application Requirements**
- Applications must follow the form prescribed by the Minister.
- Must be submitted **within 60 days of the contract date**, or a longer period allowed by the Minister.

### **6. Determination of Approved Cost**

The approved cost is determined as the **lower of two values**:
1. The **maximum approved cost** (set by the Minister).
2. The **cost verified by audit** under paragraph 7(b).

#### **Maximum Approved Cost Formula**
The Minister calculates the maximum approved cost by dividing the **contract price** by a multiplier, depending on the grant category:

### **7. Grant Payment Structure**

All agreements must include the following provisions:

#### **Progress Payments**
- Up to **80% of the total grant** may be paid **before completion** of the vessel, based on the **maximum approved cost**.
- For **multi-vessel contracts**, an additional progress payment is made **after each vessel is completed**, subject to interim audit by the **Audit Services Bureau (Department of Supply and Services)**.
- The amount of such payments must be reasonable and provide adequate protection to Her Majestyâ€™s interests.

#### **Final Payment**
- The **remaining balance** of the grant is paid **after all vessels are completed** and a full audit has been conducted.

#### **Audit Requirements**
- A full audit must be performed post-completion.
- For multi-vessel projects, **interim audits** are required during construction.
- Audits **exclude profit** when determining approved cost â€” ensuring only actual construction costs are considered.

#### **Mandatory Undertakings**
- Shipbuilders must commit to using **Canadian materials, components, and equipment** whenever they are available and internationally competitive.

#### **Other Provisions**
- The Minister may include additional terms deemed necessary to protect public interests or achieve the program's objectives.

### **9. Adjustment for Replacement Vessels**

In cases where:
- A vessel is replaced using **proceeds of disposition** (e.g., proceeds from sale), or
- A deposit is paid out under Income Tax Act provisions (subsections 13(19)),

The **approved cost of the replacement vessel** is reduced by the amount used or paid out â€” **proportional to the original proceeds or deposit**.

> This prevents double-counting of funds and ensures that the grant is not inflated by asset replacements.
