## ðŸ¡ Taxable Supply Criteria: Excluded Renovated Housing Supply

A **taxable supply** is treated as an **excluded renovated housing supply** under specific conditions when the property is a **substantially renovated residential complex**.

### Conditions for an Excluded Renovated Housing Supply:
The supply qualifies **only if all of the following are met**:

1. **Renovation Status**:
   - The residential complex is a **substantially renovated residential complex**.
   - The renovation must have occurred **after** the complex was previously used as a residential complex.

2. **Pre-Renovation Use**:
   - The complex was **held or used as a residential complex immediately before** the beginning of the substantial renovation.

3. **Post-Renovation Use (Sale or Interest Transfer)**:
   - **Case A**: Sale of the complex to a **non-builder**.
     - The complex must **not** have been occupied by an individual as a place of residence or lodging **after the substantial renovation is substantially completed**.
   - **Case B**: Supply of an **interest in the complex** (e.g., lease, ownership interest).
     - Must meet the conditions in **subparagraphs (b)(i) and (ii)** of subsection 256.2(3.1) of the Act.

> âœ… This exclusion allows for **tax relief** on the sale or transfer of residential complexes that undergo major renovations, provided they were previously used as residential units and are not occupied post-renovation.
