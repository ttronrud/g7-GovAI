**Detailed Summary of the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Reporting of Goods Regulations**

---

### **Overview**

These regulations, made under **subsection 39.38(1)a, 39.39(1)a, and 73(1)b** of the *Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act (the "Act")*, establish detailed requirements for the **reporting, record-keeping, and retention of information** related to goods imported or exported when such transactions are linked to **proceeds of crime, money laundering, or terrorist financing**. The regulations are designed to support financial transparency and accountability by ensuring that persons or entities involved in specific types of trade retain comprehensive records and comply with reporting obligations.

The regulations were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance** and the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness**, and came into force on **April 1, 2025**, as noted in the note at the end.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Reporting of Goods Under the Act (Section 39.02)**

#### **General Requirement**
Any person or entity that imports or exports goods **for a purpose referred to in subsection 39.02(6)** of the Act must make a **declaration under subsection 39.02(1)** of the Act **at the same time and in the same manner** as the reporting of imported or exported goods under **sections 12 or 95 of the Customs Act**.

> This ensures consistency between customs reporting and anti-money laundering (AML) or terrorist financing (TF) reporting obligations.

#### **Special Case: Non-Commercial Passenger Conveyances**
Goods imported on board a **non-commercial passenger conveyance** (as defined in the *Cross-border Currency and Monetary Instruments Reporting Regulations*) may be declared by the **person in charge of the conveyance** if:
- The customs reporting is done **by radio or telephone**, or  
- The goods are in the **actual possession or form part of the baggage** of a person who presents themselves **by telephone** in accordance with the *Presentation of Persons (2003) Regulations*.

> This exception recognizes the practical realities of international travel and allows for timely declarations in emergency or non-traditional scenarios.

---

### **2. Record-Keeping Obligations (Until Sixth Anniversary)**

All persons or entities involved in importing, exporting, producing, supplying, distributing, or consuming goods for a purpose under **subsection 39.02(6)** must retain specific records **until the sixth anniversary of the day the goods were imported or exported**.

#### **For Importers and Exporters (General Records)**  
Must keep:
- Records of the **origin, marking, purchase, importation, costs, and value** of the goods  
- Records of **payment for the goods**  
- Records of **sale or other disposal** of the goods in Canada  
- **Books of account** required under **section 230 of the Income Tax Act**

> These records are essential for tracing the flow of goods and detecting potential illicit financial activity.

---

#### **Additional Records for CSA Importers**
If the person or entity is a **CSA importer** (as defined in the *Accounting for Imported Goods and Payment of Duties Regulations*), they must also keep:
- Records describing the goods or specifying their quantity  
- Records of **receipt of the goods**  
- Records of **credits and adjustments** used to determine the amount paid for the goods  
- Records of **accounting for the goods**  
- Records of **refunds, drawbacks, or duty re-determinations**  
- A **list of all vendors and consignees**  
- Records needed to complete the **"CSA Revenue Summary" form** and to pay duties

> These enhance transparency in customs and revenue administration.

---

#### **Sufferance Warehouse Operators**
If a person or entity holds a **licence under paragraph 24(1)(a) of the Customs Act** to operate a **sufferance warehouse**, they must keep records until the **sixth anniversary of the day the goods were received** into the warehouse, including:
- Records of **receipt and removal** of goods

---

#### **Bonded Warehouse Operators**
If a person or entity holds a **licence under subsection 91(1) of the Customs Tariff** to operate a **bonded warehouse**, they must keep records until the **sixth anniversary of the day the goods were removed from the warehouse**, including:
- Descriptions of goods at time of receipt or removal  
- Accounting records for goods during storage  
- Inventory records and transactions while stored  
- Records of **transfer of ownership**  
- Records of **transfer to another bonded warehouse, sufferance warehouse, or duty-free shop**  
- Records of **unpacking, packing, manipulation, alteration, or combining** of goods

> These provisions ensure full traceability of goods in bonded environments, which are often used for storage, processing, or re-export.

---

#### **For Exporters (Additional Requirements)**  
Must keep:
- Records of origin, purchase, importation, cost, and value  
- Records of payment for goods  
- Records of **usage of goods in Canada**  
- Records of **exportation**  
- Records of **source of all materials (including indirect materials)** used in production  
- If a **certificate under subsection 97.1(1)** has been completed and signed:
  - Records of **applications or receipt of advance rulings** from the customs administration of the applicable free trade partner  
  - If not the producer, written representations from the producer stating that goods meet **rules of origin** under the free trade agreement

> These support compliance with international trade rules and origin verification, which is critical for preventing fraud or misrepresentation.

---

#### **For Producers (with Certificate)**  
Must keep records of:
- Purchase, cost, value, and payment for goods  
- Purchase, cost, value, and payment for all materials (including indirect materials)  
- Applications or receipt of advance rulings (as above)

> Ensures full traceability of inputs and outputs in production processes.

---

#### **For Suppliers, Distributors, or Consumers (with Certificate)**  
Must keep:
- Books of account and records required under **section 230 of the Income Tax Act**

> Ensures financial accountability throughout the supply chain.

---

#### **General Retention Period**
- **For records and books of account under section 230 of the Income Tax Act**: Must be kept in the **manner specified in section 230** (e.g., as per tax accounting standards).  
- **For all other records**: Must be kept in the **manner prescribed under subsection 40(3) of the Customs Act** (i.e., in a form and format that allows for audit and verification).

> This ensures both compliance with tax and customs standards.

---

### **4. Retention Notice Requirements (Section 39.03)**

A **retention notice** must be provided to the relevant person or entity to inform them of the records that must be retained.

#### **For Individuals**:
- Hand-delivered to the individual or  
- Sent by **registered mail** to their **last known address**

#### **For Entities**:
- Left at the **head office or place of business** with an officer or person appearing to be in control, or  
- Sent by **registered mail** to the head office or place of business

> Ensures that the notice reaches the responsible party in a legally valid way.

---

#### **Timeline for Issuing the Notice**
- Must be issued **within 60 days** of the day the goods are imported or exported.

> Provides a clear deadline to ensure timely compliance.

---

#### **Retention Period After Notice**
- If goods are imported or exported by **courier or mail**: **30 days** after the notice is sent  
- In **all other cases**: **7 days** after the notice is sent

> This shortens the retention period for fast-moving or mail-based shipments, reducing administrative burden while maintaining oversight.

---

### **5. Penalties for Violations**

Any person or entity that **violates subsections 39.02(1), (5), (6), or (8)** of the Act is liable to a penalty under **section 14** of the Act.

#### **Penalty Amounts**
- **If full disclosure is made** and there are **no reasonable grounds to believe the violation was intentional**:
  - Penalty range: **$150 to $500**

- **In all other cases** (e.g., intentional violation, failure to disclose):
  - Penalty is the **greater of**:
    1. The **fair market value** of the imported or exported goods  
    2. The **declared value** of the imported or exported goods  
    3. The **value of the financial transaction** used to pay for the goods

> This creates a strong disincentive for non-compliance and ensures penalties are proportionate to the scale of the transaction.

---

#### **Multiple Violations on the Same Conveyance**
If multiple violations occur involving goods on **the same conveyance** (as defined in subsection 2(1) of the Customs Act), they are treated as **a single violation** for the purposes of determining penalty and enforcement.

> Prevents excessive penalties for multiple minor infractions on one shipment.
