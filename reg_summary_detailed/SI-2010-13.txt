## üìö **Overview of the Convention**

This is a **bilateral social security convention** between **Canada** and the **Kingdom of Morocco**, aimed at ensuring **cooperation and mutual recognition** of social security rights for individuals who have worked or resided in both countries. It establishes rules for the **coordination of benefits** (such as pensions, disability payments, survivor benefits, and death allowances) and ensures that insured persons are not disadvantaged due to cross-border employment or residence.

The Convention was signed on **July 1, 1998**, and entered into force on **March 1, 2010**, following compliance with statutory requirements by both parties and formal notification to each other.

---

## üîç Key Background and Legal Framework

### 1. **Entry into Force Process**
- The Convention was declared to enter into force under **Order in Council P.C. 1998-1805**, laid before Parliament on October 23, 1998 (House of Commons) and October 27, 1998 (Senate).
- No motion to revoke the Order was filed within 20 sitting days.
- By **subsection 42(2) of the Old Age Security Act**, the Order came into force on **February 3, 1999**.
- The written notifications of compliance from both parties were exchanged in **November 2009**.
- The Convention officially entered into force on the **first day of the fourth month** after the month in which both parties received notification ‚Äî i.e., **March 1, 2010**.
- This date is confirmed by **Order in Council P.C. 2010-113**, issued on February 2, 2010.

> ‚úÖ **Key Takeaway**: The Convention‚Äôs entry into force was not automatic; it required mutual notification and parliamentary approval, ensuring democratic legitimacy.

## üìö Scope of Application

### 1. **Applicable Canadian Legislation**
- **Old Age Security Act** (and its regulations)
- **Canada Pension Plan (CPP)** (and its regulations)

### 2. **Applicable Moroccan Legislation**
- Social Security Scheme (limited to **long-term benefits** and **survivor death allowances**)
- Work accident and occupational illness provisions
- Special social security schemes covering **salaried workers** or those considered salaried, including current risks and benefits

> üö® **Exclusions**: The Convention does *not* cover all Moroccan social security laws ‚Äî only those related to long-term benefits and survivor benefits.

### 3. **Amendments and Extensions**
- The Convention applies to any **amendments, supplements, consolidations, or supersessions** of the listed legislation.
- Applies to new laws or regulations that extend coverage to **new categories of beneficiaries or new benefits**, **unless** one party objects within **three months** of entry into force.

## üîÑ Mutual Recognition and Obligations

### 1. **Subject to Legislation of the Other Party**
- Any person covered under Article 3 (i.e., subject to Canadian or Moroccan legislation) shall be:
  - **Subject to the obligations** of the other country‚Äôs legislation.
  - **Eligible for benefits** under that legislation under the **same conditions** as a citizen or national of that country.

> üéØ Example: A Moroccan national working in Canada may be entitled to a Canadian CPP pension if they meet the contribution requirements.

### 2. **No Reduction or Suspension of Benefits**
- Benefits under either country‚Äôs legislation **shall not be reduced, modified, suspended, or cancelled** solely because the beneficiary resides in the other country.
- Benefits are **paid in the territory of the other country**.

> ‚úÖ This protects individuals from being penalized for living abroad.

## üöß Exceptions and Special Rules

| Scenario | Rule |
|--------|------|
| **Employed Person Working in a Party‚Äôs Territory** | Subject only to that Party‚Äôs legislation. |
| **Self-Employed Person** | Subject only to the legislation of the Party where they **ordinarily reside**. |
| **Services for the Same Employer** | If an employed person performs services in the other Party‚Äôs territory for the same employer, they are subject only to the legislation of the **first Party**, as though services were performed within its territory. |
| **Maximum Period of Detachment** | Can be extended beyond 36 months up to **60 months**, with prior consent from both competent authorities. |
| **Seafarers/Aircraft Crew** | A crew member is subject only to the legislation of the country where they **ordinarily reside**: Canada if residing in Canada; Morocco if residing in Morocco. |
| **Harbour Workers** | Subject to the legislation of the Party whose **harbour** they work in. |
| **Government Employees** | Subject to the legislation of the host country **only if they are a citizen or ordinarily reside there**. They may elect to be subject to the first country‚Äôs legislation. |

> üîç The competent authorities of both parties may **modify these rules** by mutual agreement for specific persons or categories.

## üìä Benefit Calculation Rules

### 1. **Old Age Security Act (OASA) ‚Äì Residence Periods**
- **If a person is subject to CPP or provincial pension plans during residence in Morocco**:
  - That period counts as **residence in Canada** for the person and their spouse/dependants (who are not subject to Moroccan legislation due to employment/self-employment).
- **If a person is subject to Moroccan legislation during residence in Canada**:
  - That period **does not count** as residence in Canada for the person or their dependants (unless they are covered under CPP or provincial plans).

> ‚úÖ This ensures that contributions in Morocco can count toward Canadian residence requirements.

---

### 2. **Canada Pension Plan (CPP) ‚Äì Contribution Periods**
- A calendar year in which a person has **at least 3 months (or 78 days)** of insurance under Moroccan legislation is considered a **year of contributions** under the CPP.

> üìå This allows Moroccan employment to contribute toward CPP eligibility.

---

### 3. **Moroccan Legislation ‚Äì Contribution Equivalents**
- A calendar year of CPP contributions counts as **312 days** of contributions under Moroccan law.
- A day of Old Age Security Act (OASA) contributions that is **not part of a CPP period** counts as **one day** of Moroccan contributions.

> üìå This enables **partial credit** of Canadian contributions toward Moroccan benefits.

---

### 4. **Totalizing of Insurance Periods**
- If a person does **not qualify** due to insufficient insurance periods under one country, their entitlement is determined by **totalizing**:
  - Periods under both Canada and Morocco
  - Periods under a **third country** with which both parties have social security agreements allowing period totalization.

> ‚úÖ This allows individuals to build up benefits across multiple countries.

---

### 5. **Minimum Thresholds (No Benefit if Below)**
- **Canada**: If total insurance periods under Canadian law are **less than one year**, no benefit is payable.
- **Morocco**: If total insurance periods under Moroccan law are **less than 312 days**, no benefit is payable.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è These thresholds prevent benefits from being granted for minimal or non-qualifying contributions.
