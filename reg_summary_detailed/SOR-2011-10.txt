  - **Section 160**: Authority to make regulations under CEPA.

This regulation establishes **national emission standards** for marine spark-ignition engines, vessels, and off-road recreational vehicles, aligning Canada‚Äôs standards with those of the **U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)**.

## üéØ **Purpose of the Regulations**

The primary objectives are:

1. **Reduce air pollution** from:
   - Hydrocarbons (HC)
   - Oxides of nitrogen (NOx)
   - Carbon monoxide (CO)

2. **Limit emissions of toxic substances** including:
   - Formaldehyde
   - 1,3-Butadiene
   - Acetaldehyde
   - Acrolein
   - Benzene

3. **Establish emission standards and testing procedures** that are **aligned with U.S. EPA standards** (specifically CFR parts 1045, 1051, and 1060).

## üîß **Prescribed Classes of Engines and Vehicles**

The regulation defines specific classes of engines and vehicles subject to emission standards under **section 149 of CEPA 1999**.

### ‚úÖ **Classes of Engines**
- Outboards
- Inboard engines (including sterndrives and jet boat engines)
- Personal watercraft (PWC) engines
- Engines operating under **Otto cycle characteristics** with spark ignition

> ‚ùå **Excluded**:
> - Engines designed **exclusively for competition** (e.g., superior performance, no public sale, no warranty)
> - Engines using **natural gas** rated at 250 kW or more
> - Engines with **compression ignition** (covered by separate regulations)
> - Engines or vehicles used **only for military or combat purposes**
> - Engines or vehicles **being exported** without evidence of non-use in Canada
> - Vehicles with **dry weight <20 kg**

### ‚úÖ **Classes of Vessels**
- Vessels (excluding amphibious vehicles) that are:
  - Designed to be propelled by a prescribed engine
  - Have a fuel line or fuel tank installed

### ‚úÖ **Classes of Off-Road Recreational Vehicles**
- Snowmobiles
- Off-road motorcycles
- All-terrain vehicles (ATVs)
- Utility vehicles

> ‚ùå **Excluded**:
> - Competition-grade vehicles (e.g., no lights, no spark arrestor, no warranty, etc.)
> - Vehicles regulated under **On-Road Vehicle and Engine Emission Regulations**
> - Electrically powered vehicles
> - Vehicles with only compression-ignition engines (covered by other regulations)

## üè≠ **Conformity and Manufacturing Requirements**

### 1. **Model Year Determination**
- A manufacturer may choose the **calendar year** during which production occurs or the **next calendar year** if production spans across January 1.
- The period of production **must include only one January 1**.

### 2. **Conformity Requirements (Sections 153 & 154)**
- All prescribed engines, vessels, and vehicles must meet **emission standards** as defined in the regulations.
- Compliance must be demonstrated through:
  - Testing procedures aligned with **EPA CFR standards** (e.g., CFR 1045, 1051, 1060)
  - Evidence of conformity under **section 35**

### 3. **Exemptions**
- Vehicles or engines used **solely for exhibition, demonstration, evaluation, or testing** are **not subject** to the prescribed emission standards under section 152.

## üè∑Ô∏è **National Emissions Mark (NEM) System**

### üìù **Application Process**
- Any company wishing to apply the **National Emissions Mark (NEM)** must submit an application to the Minister, signed by an authorized representative, including:
  - Company head office and mailing address
  - Class of engine, vessel, or vehicle
  - Location where the mark will be applied
  - Evidence of capability to verify compliance with the standards

### ‚úÖ **Authorization**
- Upon approval, the Minister assigns a **unique authorization number** to the company.

### üìå **Eligibility for Pre-2012 Models**
- A company may apply the NEM to:
  - Engines manufactured **before** the regulation came into force
  - Vehicles with main assembly completed **before** the regulation came into force
- **Only if**:
  - The engine or vehicle complies with standards for the **2012 model year**
  - The company meets all regulatory requirements

### üñºÔ∏è **Mark Display Requirements**
- The **NEM** must be:
  - At least **7 mm in height** and **10 mm in width**
  - Displayed **immediately below or to the right** of the emission control information label (or in a visible, accessible location if no such label exists)
- The **authorization number** must be:
  - Displayed immediately below or to the right of the NEM
  - In **figures at least 2 mm in height**
