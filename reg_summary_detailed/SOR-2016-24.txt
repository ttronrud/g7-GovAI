## **Detailed Summary of the Canada-Nova Scotia Offshore Petroleum Financial Requirements Regulations**

### **1. Legal Basis and Context**

- **Governing Act**:  
  The regulations are made under the *Canada-Nova Scotia Offshore Petroleum Resources Accord Implementation Act* (the "Act"), specifically under **subsection 153(1)c**, which authorizes the Governor in Council to make regulations regarding financial requirements for offshore petroleum operations in the Canada-Nova Scotia offshore region.

- **Publication and Consultation**:
  - The proposed regulations were first published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **July 11, 2015**, giving interested parties a reasonable opportunity to submit representations to the Minister of Natural Resources.
  - Under **subsection 6(1)b** of the Act, the Minister consulted the **Provincial Minister of Nova Scotia**.
  - The Provincial Minister **approved** the regulations, confirming their alignment with provincial interests and legal frameworks.

- **Final Adoption**:  
  The Governor in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Natural Resources, formally enacted the regulations on **July 15, 2015**, under **sections 90(1) to (3)** of the Act.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Regulations**

The primary purpose of these regulations is to establish **clear, enforceable financial requirements** for offshore petroleum operators in the Canada-Nova Scotia offshore area. These requirements ensure that operators can:

- Demonstrate sufficient financial capacity to cover potential liabilities (e.g., spills, environmental damage, operational costs).
- Provide adequate financial security for environmental protection and emergency response.
- Maintain transparency and accountability in financial reporting and risk management.

### **3. Financial Resource Requirements for Applicants**

#### **3.1 Proof of Financial Capacity**
Under **subsection 167.1(1)** of the Act, applicants must prove they have the necessary financial resources to meet their obligations. This is done through a **statement of net assets or funding arrangements** that demonstrates to the Board’s satisfaction the applicant’s ability to pay the required amounts.

#### **3.2 Required Supporting Documents**
To substantiate the financial statement, the applicant must provide **one or more** of the following documents:
- Most recent **audited annual financial statement**
- Current **credit rating** from a recognized credit rating agency (with documentation)
- **Promissory note**
- **Insurance policy** or **certificate of insurance**
- **Escrow agreement**
- **Letter of credit**
- **Line of credit agreement** (where funds are available)
- **Guarantee agreement**
- **Security bond, pledge agreement, indemnity bond, or suretyship agreement**

> *Note*: These documents serve as tangible proof of financial strength and risk mitigation.

#### **3.3 Optional Audit Requirement**
For greater certainty and assurance:
- The Board **may require** that the financial statement and supporting documents be **audited by a qualified independent auditor**.
- The applicant must provide a **signed audit report** from the auditor.

This strengthens the credibility of the financial claims and ensures compliance with professional standards.

### **4. Pooled Fund Requirements (Under Section 168 of the Act)**

A **pooled fund** may be established for the purposes of **subsection 168(1.01)** of the Act to support financial obligations related to environmental protection and spill response.

#### **4.1 Location and Administration**
- The pooled fund must be **located and administered in Canada**.
- This ensures national oversight, regulatory compliance, and accountability.

#### **4.2 Permitted Uses of Funds**
The fund may be used for the following purposes:
1. **Primary Purpose**: To make payments under **subsection 168(2)** of the Act (e.g., for spill response or environmental remediation).
2. **Additional Uses (Conditional)**:
   - Under **subsection 160(2)** of the *Provincial Act* — **only if** the fund is also established under **subsection 160(1A)**.
   - Under **subsection 163(2)** of the *Canada–Newfoundland and Labrador Atlantic Accord Implementation Act* — **only if** established under **subsection 163(1.01)**.
   - Under **subsection 158(2)** of the *Newfoundland and Labrador Act* — **only if** established under **subsection 158(1.1)**.
   - Under **subsection 27(2)** of the *Canada Oil and Gas Operations Act* — **only if** established under **subsection 27(1.01)**.

> This conditional structure ensures that pooled funds are used only in specific, authorized contexts and not broadly or without oversight.

#### **4.3 Payment and Reporting Obligations of the Administrator**
The administrator of the fund has strict obligations:

| Obligation | Details |
|----------|--------|
| **Annual Financial Statement** | Must provide an **audited financial statement annually** showing that the fund is maintained at a **minimum of $250 million**, or at a **higher amount set by regulation**. |
| **Participant Changes** | Must notify the Board within **24 hours** of any participant addition, withdrawal, or change in fund balance (excluding interest charges or banking fees). |
| **Contravention Notification** | Must inform the Board within **24 hours** of becoming aware of a participant’s breach of obligations under **sections 168(1.1), (1.2), or (5)** of the Act. |
| **Contact Information** | Must provide the Board with the **phone number, email, and mailing address** of their designated contact person. |

These requirements ensure transparency, timely reporting, and effective oversight.

#### **4.4 Reimbursement into the Fund**
- Any amount **paid out of the pooled fund** must be **reimbursed into the fund within seven days** of the payment being made.
- This ensures the fund remains financially stable and available for future obligations.

### **5. Board Recommendation on Liability Assessment (Section 168.1)**

The Board may make a **recommendation to the Federal Minister** under **subsection 168.1(1)** of the Act if it determines that:

> The **estimated total of losses, damages, costs, and expenses** (excluding non-use value losses) for which the applicant may be liable under **paragraphs 167(1)(b) and (2)(b)** of the Act is **less than the amount specified in subsection 167(2.2)** of the Act.

#### **5.1 Content of the Recommendation**
The recommendation must include:
- The **estimated total of losses, damages, costs, and expenses** (excluding non-use value).
- The **recommended amount** (which must be less than the threshold in subsection 167(2.2)).
- A **summary of reasons** for the recommendation.
- A **summary of relevant information provided by the applicant** to the Board.
- Any **information previously shared with the Provincial Minister** during approval under subsection 168.1(1).
- Any **information requested by the Federal Minister**.

#### **5.2 Additional Information**
- The Board may submit **any other pertinent information** to the Federal Minister, allowing for flexibility in risk assessment and context-specific analysis.

> This process allows for a **risk-based evaluation** of an applicant’s financial and operational capacity to manage potential environmental or safety incidents.
