## üìö **Overview of the Agreement**

The **Agreement on Social Security between Canada and the Republic of India** (hereinafter referred to as the *Agreement*) is a bilateral social security treaty designed to **ensure that individuals who work or reside in both countries can access pension, disability, and survivor benefits without being penalized due to cross-border residency or employment**. It establishes rules for **credit accumulation**, **benefit eligibility**, **totalization of periods**, **mutual recognition of contributions**, and **cooperation between authorities**.

The Agreement entered into force on **August 1, 2015**, following the exchange of written notifications by both governments confirming compliance with all requirements for entry into force, as stipulated in Article 28 of the original 2012 Agreement signed in New Delhi.

---

## üîç Key Provisions and Detailed Summary

### 1. **Purpose and Scope of the Agreement**
- The primary objective is to **cooperate in the field of social security** and provide **mutual recognition of benefits** for individuals who have worked or lived in both Canada and India.
- It applies specifically to:
  - **Old-age and survivors‚Äô pensions** for employed persons in India.
  - **Permanent Total Disability (PTD) pensions** for employed persons in India.
  - **Old Age Security (OAS)** and **Canada Pension Plan (CPP)** in Canada.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note:* The Agreement does **not cover** public assistance, unemployment insurance, or health care.

### 3. **Application of the Agreement**

#### a) **Applicable Legislation**
- **Canada**:  
  - *Old Age Security Act (OASA)* and its regulations  
  - *Canada Pension Plan (CPP)* and its regulations  
- **India**:  
  - Old-age and survivors‚Äô pension for employed persons  
  - Permanent Total Disability (PTD) pension for employed persons  

#### b) **Exclusions and Extensions**
- The Agreement applies to:
  - Amendments, supplements, consolidations, or supersessions of the above legislation.
  - New categories of beneficiaries or new benefits ‚Äî **unless India or Canada formally objects within 6 months of implementation**.

#### c) **Who Is Covered?**
- Any person who:
  - Has been subject to Canadian or Indian legislation.
  - Derives rights from such a person under the applicable laws.

> Individuals covered have the **same rights and obligations** as nationals of the respective country.

### 4. **Benefit Rights and Protection from Reduction**

#### ‚úÖ **No Reduction or Suspension Due to Residency**
- Benefits paid under either country‚Äôs legislation to a person covered by the Agreement **shall not be reduced, modified, suspended, or cancelled solely because the person is residing in the other country**.

> This protects individuals from being denied benefits just because they live abroad.

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è **Special Provisions for Canada (Outside Canada)**
- **Old Age Security (OAS) and Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS)**:
  - Can only be paid **to the extent permitted by the OAS Act**.
  - A person outside Canada must meet the **minimum residency requirement** (totalized under Chapter 1) to qualify.

> Example: A Canadian citizen living in India may qualify for OAS only if their total periods of residence in Canada (including periods in India under CPP) meet or exceed the required threshold.

### 5. **Employment and Jurisdiction Rules (Article 6)**

| Scenario | Rule |
|--------|------|
| **Employed person working in a Contracting State** | Subject only to the legislation of that State. |
| **Self-employed person working in the other country** | Subject only to the legislation of the **first State of residence**. |
| **Person sent by employer to work abroad** | Subject only to the legislation of the **first State** (residence), even if working abroad. This can be extended beyond 60 months with mutual consent. |
| **Seafarers** | - If crew member, subject only to legislation of **State of residence**.<br>- If ship flies Indian flag ‚Üí subject only to **India‚Äôs legislation**. |
| **Government employees posted abroad** | Subject only to the legislation of the **first State** (where posted). |

> These rules prevent double coverage and ensure clarity in jurisdiction.

### 6. **Totalization of Creditable Periods (Key Benefit Mechanism)**

This is the **core mechanism** of the Agreement, allowing individuals to **combine contributions from both countries** when determining eligibility for pensions.

#### a) **For OAS Eligibility (Canada)**
- A **creditable period under Indian legislation** is treated as a **period of residence in Canada**.
- This applies to:
  - The person themselves
  - Their spouse or common-law partner
  - Dependants living with them

> Even if the person is not employed in Canada, time spent in India may count toward Canadian residency requirements.

#### b) **For CPP Eligibility (Canada)**
- A **calendar year with at least 3 months of creditable periods under Indian legislation** counts as **one full year** of CPP contribution.
- This helps individuals with part-time or intermittent work in India accumulate enough contributions to qualify for CPP.

#### c) **For Indian Pension Eligibility**
- A **calendar year under CPP** is treated as **12 months** of creditable periods under Indian law.
- A **month under OAS Act** (not part of CPP) counts as **one month** of creditable time under Indian law.

> This allows Indian citizens working in Canada to build pension eligibility in India using their Canadian contributions.

---

### 7. **Eligibility Determination (Article 12)**

If a person is **not eligible** due to insufficient creditable periods under one country:

- Totalize all creditable periods from:
  - Both Canada and India
  - Any third country with which both nations have social security agreements (totalization agreements)

> This ensures that individuals are not denied benefits due to gaps in one country‚Äôs coverage.

---

### 8. **Minimum Period Requirement (OAS Outside Canada)**

- A person outside Canada **cannot receive OAS** unless their **totalized periods of residence in Canada** (including time in India under CPP) meet or exceed the **minimum residency requirement** set by the OAS Act.

> This ensures that individuals do not receive benefits without meeting basic residency thresholds.

---

### 9. **Benefit Calculation Rules**

#### a) **For Canada (OAS/Partial Pension)**
If a person qualifies **only through totalization**:
- **Earnings-related portion** of the pension is calculated **based only on CPP pensionable earnings**.
- **Flat-rate portion** is calculated as:
  $$
  \text{Flat-rate} = (\text{CPP flat-rate}) \times \left(\frac{\text{Contributions in Canada}}{\text{Minimum qualifying period}}\right)
  $$
  - The ratio **cannot exceed 1.0** (i.e., no more than full flat-rate).

> This prevents overpayment and ensures fair calculation.

#### b) **For India (Old Age, Survivor, or Disability Benefits)**
- The **theoretical amount** of benefit would be calculated **as if all periods were accumulated under Indian law alone**.
- The **actual benefit** is then adjusted using the ratio:
  $$
  \text{Actual Benefit} = \text{Theoretical Amount} \times \left(\frac{\text{Periods under Indian law}}{\text{Total periods under both countries}}\right)
  $$

> This ensures a fair, proportional distribution of benefits based on actual contribution history.

### 10. **Administrative Cooperation and Information Exchange**

#### a) **Mutual Communication**
- Competent authorities and institutions must:
  - Share **necessary information** for benefit determination.
  - Provide **free assistance** in determining eligibility or benefit amounts (as if it were their own legislation).
  - Communicate **changes in laws or policies** affecting the Agreement promptly.

#### b) **Confidentiality**
- All information exchanged under the Agreement is:
  - **Confidential**
  - Used **only for implementing the Agreement**
  - Not disclosed to third parties without **prior notification from the sending country**

> This protects privacy
