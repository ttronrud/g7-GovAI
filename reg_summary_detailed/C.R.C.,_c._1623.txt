**Detailed Summary of the *Memorial Cross Order (World War II)***

---

### **Overview**
The *Memorial Cross Order (World War II)* is a Canadian regulatory instrument established to honor and commemorate individuals who served and died during World War II. It establishes the criteria under which a **Memorial Cross** (also known as the *Memorial Cross*) may be awarded to certain family members of Canadian and Newfoundland service personnel who made the ultimate sacrifice in service to their country.

This Order is formally cited as the *Memorial Cross Order (World War II)* and is administered under the authority of the **Minister of Veterans Affairs**. Its primary purpose is to provide a formal, dignified, and respectful memento of personal loss and sacrifice to the mothers, widows, and, in some cases, children and siblings of those who died in service during World War II.

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Cross**  
   - Refers to the *Memorial Cross*, a specific silver cross with the following design elements:
     - **Silver cross pattée** (a cross with arms that are wide and flat at the ends)
     - Suspended by a **purple ribbon**
     - At the **end of the upright**: a **crown**
     - At the **foot** and at the **end of each arm**: a **maple leaf**
     - In the **center**, within a **wreath of laurel**, the **Royal Cypher** of the reigning monarch
     - **Engraved** with:
       - The **number, rank, and name** of the individual commemorated (sailor, soldier, airman, merchant seaman, or fire fighter)

2. **Fire Fighter**  
   - Defined as any person who served during WWII as a member of the **Corps of (Civilian) Canadian Fire Fighters for Service in the United Kingdom**.
   - Must have been:
     - On active service while receiving pay and allowances
     - Outside the continents of **North and South America**, their adjacent islands, and territorial waters
     - Including service in **Greenland, Iceland, or the Aleutian Islands**
   - **Excluded** from eligibility:
     - Service in **Newfoundland, Bermuda, or the West Indies**

3. **Merchant Seaman**  
   - Any person who served on:
     - A ship **registered in Canada**
     - A **UK-registered ship** registered in Canada or elsewhere
     - A registered ship of an **allied country** during WWII
   - Eligibility condition:
     - Must have been **born, domiciled in Canada or Newfoundland**, or an **ordinary resident** in Canada or Newfoundland **between September 9, 1929, and the date they began service**

4. **Sailor, Soldier, or Airman**  
   - Any person who served in the **naval, army, or air forces** of:
     - His Majesty (the Crown) **raised in Canada**
     - His Majesty (outside Canada) or one of the **allied nations** during WWII
   - Eligibility condition:
     - Must have been **born, domiciled in Canada or Newfoundland**, or an **ordinary resident** in Canada or Newfoundland **between September 9, 1929, and the date of service commencement**

5. **Mother**  
   - The **natural mother**  
   - If not raised by the natural mother, then the woman **who raised the individual from childhood**, as determined by the **Minister of Veterans Affairs**

6. **Widow**  
   - Includes:
     - The **legal widow or widower** of the deceased service member
     - Any person who is **pensionable as a widow** under the *Pension Act* or the *Civilian War Pensions and Allowances Act* due to the death of the service member
     - Anyone who **would have been considered a widow** under those laws, **if applicable**
   - **Excludes**:
     - Anyone who was **divorced, judicially separated, or legally separated** from the service member at the time of death

7. **World War II**  
   - Defined as per subsection 3(1) of the *Pension Act*, meaning the global conflict from 1939 to 1945

---

### **Eligibility for the Memorial Cross**

A Memorial Cross may be issued **only** to the following individuals, in order of priority:

1. **Mother** – if alive  
2. **Widow** – if alive  
3. **Eldest living child** – if no mother or widow is alive  
4. **Father** – if no mother, widow, or eldest child is alive  
5. **Eldest brother or sister** – if no mother, widow, child, or father is alive  

> The eligibility is **strictly hierarchical** and based on familial relationships and the survival of the individual.

---

### **Who Is Eligible for the Cross?**

The Cross may be issued in respect of the following categories of service members, **subject to specific conditions**:

#### 1. **Sailors, Soldiers, or Airmen**
- Eligible if they:
  - Were **killed in action**
  - **Died while on active service**
  - **Died or died from causes attributable to service** during WWII
- Includes deaths occurring:
  - While on active service
  - Subsequent to discharge from service
- **Exception**:
  - If death occurs **after discharge**, the Cross may **only** be issued to the **widow or her next of kin** **if she was married to the service member at the time of discharge**

#### 2. **Merchant Seamen**
- Eligible if:
  - Lost their life through **enemy action**
  - Died or died from **causes attributable to service** as a merchant seaman during WWII
- **Exception**:
  - Cross may only be issued to the **widow or her next of kin** if she was **married to the merchant seaman at the time he sustained the injury or disability** leading to death

#### 3. **Fire Fighters**
- Eligible if:
  - Died during service while receiving pay and allowances in the UK or allied territories (excluding Newfoundland, Bermuda, West Indies)
  - Died or died from **causes attributable to service**
- **Exception**:
  - Cross may only be issued to the **widow or her next of kin** if she was **married to the fire fighter at the time he sustained the injury or disability** resulting in death

> Note: The exception applies **only** to deaths resulting from injury or disability during service.

---

### **Requirements for Issuance**

A Memorial Cross **shall not be issued** unless the recipient provides **proof of**:

1. The **death** of the service member  
2. The **service** of the individual during WWII  
3. The **eligibility** of the recipient under the rules outlined in the Order

This ensures that the award is both **accurate and legitimate**, preventing misuse or inappropriate claims.

---

### **Key Principles and Intent**

- **Honoring Sacrifice**: The Order recognizes the profound personal and national loss experienced by families of those who died in service.
- **Family-Centered Recognition**: It prioritizes the **mother and widow** as primary recipients, reflecting their central role in the emotional and social impact of loss.
- **Strict Eligibility Criteria**: The definitions of who qualifies as a service member and who is eligible to receive the cross are **precise and legally defined**, ensuring consistency and fairness.
- **Exclusions and Exceptions**: The rules explicitly exclude certain relationships (e.g., divorced individuals) and impose conditions on post-discharge deaths, emphasizing the importance of marital status at the time of injury or death.
- **Geographic and Temporal Limits**: The scope is limited to WWII (1939–1945), specific service roles, and defined geographic areas (e.g., exclusion of Newfoundland, Bermuda, West Indies for fire fighters).

---

### **Administrative Authority**

- The **Minister of Veterans Affairs** is responsible for:
  - Determining eligibility of recipients
  - Approving applications
  - Verifying service and death records
  - Ensuring compliance with the Order’s provisions

---

### **Legal and Historical Context**

- The Order was originally enacted in **1981** (SI/81-31) and later amended in **1991** (SI/91-69), reflecting evolving administrative practices and legal interpretations.
- It operates in conjunction with existing legislation such as the *Pension Act* and the *Civilian War Pensions and Allowances Act*, which define widowhood and pension eligibility.
- The Memorial Cross is a **symbolic, not monetary**, recognition — intended to provide **emotional and historical closure** to families.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Memorial Cross Order (World War II)* is a carefully structured, respectful, and legally grounded regulation designed to:

- Commemorate the service and sacrifice of Canadian and Newfoundland citizens who died during WWII
- Provide a formal, dignified memento to the **mothers, widows, and surviving relatives** of those individuals
- Ensure that only **eligible, verified individuals** receive the cross
- Maintain clarity in eligibility through precise definitions and conditions

It stands as a testament to Canada’s commitment to honoring the human cost of war and supporting the families who endured it.
