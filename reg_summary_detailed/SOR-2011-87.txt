## **1. Regulatory Background and Authority**

- **Issuance**: These regulations were made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Labour** and the **Minister of Transport**.
- **Legal Basis**: Enacted under specific sections of the *Canada Labour Code*:
  - Sections **125a, 125.1b, 125.2c, 126d, and 157e**.
- **Purpose**: To establish health and safety standards for employees working on **aircraft in operation**, and for individuals granted access to aircraft, ensuring their protection from occupational hazards including noise, electrical risks, contamination, and exposure to hazardous substances like asbestos.

---

## **2. Scope of Application**

The regulations apply to:

- **Non-employee individuals** who perform work on an aircraft to gain knowledge or experience (e.g., trainees, apprentices), **as if they were employees**.
- **All employees employed on board aircraft during operation**.
- **Persons granted access** to aircraft by the employer (e.g., maintenance staff, support personnel).

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Key Principle**: All provisions of these regulations must be interpreted and applied as if the non-employee were an actual employee.

## **4. Noise Exposure and Hearing Protection (Part on Acoustics)**

### **4.1 Measurement Requirements**
- Sound exposure must be measured using:
  - A sound level meter **recommended in CSA CAN/CSA-Z107.56-13** (Clause 4.3).
  - The instrument must be **certified** by a Standards Council of Canada-accredited body to meet **Clause 4 requirements**.
- Measurement must be conducted:
  - **Instantaneously** during normal working conditions.
  - Using the **slow response setting** of the SLM.

### **4.2 Exposure Limits**
- **No employee may be exposed to**:
  - An A-weighted sound pressure level **exceeding the limits in Schedule** (column 1) for durations over those specified (column 2).
  - A **noise exposure level (Lex,8) exceeding 87 dBA** in any 24-hour period.

### **4.3 High-Risk Exposure (‚â•84 dBA)**
If an employee may be exposed to **‚â•84 dBA for a period likely to endanger hearing**, the employer must:
1. **Immediately appoint a qualified person** to conduct an investigation.
2. **Notify** the workplace committee or health and safety representative (HSR) of:
   - The investigation.
   - The name of the appointed person.

### **4.4 Investigation Requirements**
The investigation must assess:
- Sources of sound on board.
- Likely A-weighted sound pressure levels and exposure durations.
- Existing noise-reduction methods.
- Whether exposure exceeds the **87 dBA limit**.
- Whether exposure may reach **‚â•84 dBA**.

### **4.5 Report Requirements**
After consultation with the HSR or committee, the investigator must:
- Document observations and findings.
- Provide **recommendations** for:
  - Engineering controls to reduce exposure (e.g., sound barriers, quieter equipment).
  - Use of hearing protectors for exposure between **84‚Äì87 dBA**.
- The report must be:
  - **Kept by the employer** for **10 years**.
  - **Accessible to affected employees**.

### **4.6 Employee Notification & Hearing Protection**
If the report indicates exposure ‚â•84 dBA:
- The employer must:
  - Provide **written information** on the hearing risks.
  - Make the report **readily available** to the employee.
  - **Post a notice** in a conspicuous, accessible location indicating where the report can be reviewed.

### **4.7 Hearing Protection Requirements**
If engineering controls are **not feasible** to reduce exposure below the limit:
- The employer must:
  - Provide **hearing protectors** that:
    - Are certified by an accredited body to meet **CSA Z94.2-02 (R2007)** standards.
    - Prevent exposure to the hazardous sound level.
  - **Train employees** in fitting, care, and use of protectors (in consultation with HSR or committee).

## **5. Electrical Safety (Section 3.3)**

### **5.1 Work on Live Electrical Equipment**
- **Only a qualified person** may perform testing or work on electrical equipment on board aircraft.
- If there is a risk of **hazardous electrical shock**, employees must use:
  - **Insulated tools and protection equipment**.

### **5.2 Conditions for Working on Live Equipment**
An employee may work on live or potentially live equipment **only if**:
- The employer has **instructed** the employee in safe procedures for working on live conductors.
- A **safety ground** is connected to the equipment.
- The equipment is **disconnected** from all sources of electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, or other energy that could make it dangerous.

### **5.3 Guarding and Coordination**
- **Guarding** of live electrical equipment is required.
- If **two or more employees** are working on or near electrical equipment, the employer must ensure **safe coordination** and full communication.

### **5.4 Reporting Defects**
- Any employee identifying a defect in electrical equipment must:
  - **Mark or tag** the equipment as unsafe.
  - **Immediately report** the defect to the **person in charge of the aircraft**.

## **6. Hygiene and Sanitation (Washrooms and Galley Areas)**

### **6.1 Washroom Requirements**
- **Ideal**: A washroom with **toilet and washbasin** for employee use.
- **Fallbacks** (in order of feasibility):
  1. Toilet-only room.
  2. Washbasin-only room.
- **Washrooms must be**:
  - **Enclosed** to provide reasonable privacy.
  - **Cleaned at least once every 24 hours** when used.
  - **Maintained in a clean and sanitary condition**.

### **6.2 Equipment and Supplies**
- **Toilet paper** must be provided.
- **Menstrual products** (tampons, pads) must be available in washrooms with toilets.
- If not feasible in washrooms, they must be provided in **another accessible, private location** under employer control.
- **Soap or cleaning agent** must be provided in dispensers at each washbasin.
- **Single-use hand towels** must be available in sufficient quantity.
- **Non-combustible container** for used towels.
- **Hot water** (if provided) must be ‚â§ **43¬∞C**.
- If hot water is not feasible, **antiseptic agent** must be provided.

### **6.3 Water Supply and Potable Water**
- Employees must have **sufficient potable water** for:
  - Drinking.
  - Personal washing.
