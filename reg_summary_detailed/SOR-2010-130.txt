**Detailed Summary of the 13th IAAF World Junior Championships in Athletics Remission Order, 2010**

---

### **Overview**

This regulation, known as the **13th IAAF World Junior Championships in Athletics Remission Order, 2010**, was issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, under **section 115a of the Customs Tariff**. Its purpose is to provide **customs duty and excise tax remissions** for goods and equipment imported into Canada in connection with the **13th IAAF World Junior Championships in Athletics**, held in **Moncton, New Brunswick**, from **July 19 to July 25, 2010**.

The Order aims to facilitate the smooth operation of the event by reducing financial burdens on key participants and organizers, including athletes, officials, sponsors, and the local organizing committee (LOC), while ensuring compliance with Canadian customs and tax laws.

## **Scope of Remission (Tax and Duty Relief)**

The Order grants remission (i.e., exemption or reduction) of customs duties and excise taxes under specific conditions. These are divided into several categories:

---

### **1. Remission for Championships Family Members**

- **Applies to**: Individuals (non-residents) who hold IAAF accreditation and participate in the event or serve in official roles.
- **Covered goods**: Goods imported **temporarily** into Canada for **exclusive use** by the individual in connection with the Championships.
- **Relief granted**:
  - **Customs duties** on such goods.
  - **Excise tax** under Part IX of the *Excise Tax Act* (for the imported goods).

> ‚úÖ Purpose: To allow international athletes, coaches, and officials to bring necessary personal equipment into Canada without incurring customs or excise costs.

---

### **2. Remission for Goods and Equipment Imported by LOC, Media Rights Holders, Sponsors, or Suppliers**

- **Applies to**: The LOC, media rights holders, sponsors, suppliers, or their agents/representatives.
- **Covered goods**:
  - Goods for **display** (e.g., trophies, banners, signage), including apparatus used to display them.
  - **Equipment** used exclusively in connection with the Championships.
- **Relief granted**:
  - **Customs duties** on imported goods and equipment.
  - A **portion of the excise tax** under Part IX of the Excise Tax Act.

> üîç **Tax Calculation Rule (Section 2)**:
> - If the importer is **not a resident of Canada** and is **not a registrant** under subsection 123(1) of the Excise Tax Act:  
>   ‚Üí Full excise tax is remitted.
> - In all **other cases** (e.g., Canadian residents or registrants):  
>   ‚Üí Remission equals the **difference** between:  
>     (a) Total excise tax payable, and  
>     (b) The tax on **1/60th of the value of the goods or equipment per month** (or partial month) that the goods remain in Canada.

> ‚úÖ Purpose: To reduce the tax burden on temporary imports used for event operations, with a progressive reduction based on how long the goods stay in Canada.

---

### **3. Remission for Low-Value Goods (Unit Value ‚â§ $60)**

- **Applies to**: Media rights holders, sponsors, suppliers, LOC, IAAF, or their agents.
- **Goods**: Items imported for **free distribution** during the event.
- **Relief granted**:
  - **Customs duties** on goods with unit value of **$60 or less**.

> ‚úÖ Purpose: To enable promotional and marketing materials to be distributed at no cost to participants or the public.

---

### **4. Remission for Gifts or Awards to Residents of Canada**

- **Applies to**: Goods imported by the LOC or a Championships family member.
- **Recipient categories**:
  - A Championships family member;
  - An agent or representative of the LOC;
  - A resident of Canada participating in the Championships;
  - A resident acting in an official capacity related to the event.
- **Relief granted**:
  - **Customs duties**;
  - **Excise tax** on the imported goods.

> ‚úÖ Purpose: To allow fair and accessible distribution of prizes, awards, or recognition items to Canadian participants and officials.

---

### **5. Remission for Certified Athletic Equipment (Donated to Public Institutions)**

- **Applies to**: Athletic equipment imported by the LOC.
- **Conditions**:
  1. Equipment must be **certified by the IAAF** as meeting international competition standards for the sport.
  2. Equipment must be **required by an athlete** solely for training or competition at the Championships.
  3. Equipment must be **donated** to:
     - A not-for-profit sports club,
     - A sports federation,
     - A registered charity,
     - An educational or public institution,
     - A municipality, town, or city.
  4. Equipment must **not be sold or disposed of** within **two years** after importation.

> ‚úÖ Purpose: To support post-event community development by enabling the transfer of high-quality, certified equipment to public or nonprofit entities.

---

### **6. Remission for Donated or Free-Provided Clothing**

- **Applies to**: Clothing imported into Canada.
- **Conditions**:
  1. Donated **by a sponsor or supplier** to the LOC.
  2. Provided **free of charge** to LOC volunteers to wear as uniforms during official duties.
  3. The volunteer recipient **keeps the clothing after the Championships**.

> ‚úÖ Purpose: To support volunteer operations with appropriate uniforms, while allowing for cost-effective and sustainable use of donated clothing.

---

## **Conditions for Remission**

To qualify for any remission under this Order, the following **conditions must be met**:

1. **Import Date**: Goods or equipment must be imported into Canada **between March 1, 2010, and July 25, 2010** (the event period).

2. **Claim Deadline**: A claim for remission must be submitted to the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** **within two years** of the day when the goods are accounted for under **section 32 of the Customs Act**.

3. **Evidence Requirement**: The importer must provide the **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)** with **evidence or information** proving entitlement to remission under this Order.

---

## **Final Disposition Requirements**

For remission to be granted under **sections 3 and 4**, the goods or equipment must meet one of the following conditions **by December 31, 2010**:

- **Exported from Canada**, or  
- **Destroyed in Canada** under the supervision of a CBSA officer, **at the expense of the importer**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è This ensures that temporary imports do not remain in Canada indefinitely and are either used or properly disposed of.

---

## **Effective Date**

This Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** with the Canada Gazette.
