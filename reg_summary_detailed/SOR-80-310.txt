### ðŸ”¹ **Overview and Purpose**
The *Railway Costing Regulations* (1980) were enacted under **Section 330 of the Railway Act** to establish a standardized, transparent, and legally sound methodology for determining **railway operating costs** for the purposes of assessing **actual loss**, **rate justification**, and **financial viability** of rail operations.

These regulations apply specifically to **cost submissions** filed by railway companies under the jurisdiction of the Canadian Transport Commission (CTC), particularly for financial years **1979 and beyond**, and are designed to ensure that:
- Costs are fairly and consistently calculated,
- Losses due to discontinuation or abandonment of services are accurately measured,
- Rates are justified based on actual, recoverable costs,
- The financial health and operational realities of rail services are reflected in regulatory decisions.

## ðŸ”¹ **Cost Definitions and Methodology**

### 1. **Definition of "Costs" for Actual Loss Calculations**
Costs are defined in two key contexts:

#### A. For **Branch Lines**:
> The costs that would have been **avoided** if the company had not maintained or operated the branch line in a given financial year. This includes:
- Variable costs of carrying traffic originating or terminating on the line (regardless of timing, method, or entity).
- Costs are adjusted after a **reasonable period of time** to account for operational adjustments.

#### B. For **Passenger-Train Services**:
> The costs that would have been **avoided** if the service had not been operated. Includes all costs related to passenger carriage, regardless of when or how incurred.

> âœ… Key Principle: Costs are not based on historical or average expenses, but on **what would have been avoided** in the absence of the service or line.

## ðŸ”¹ **Variable Costs for Goods Carriage (Section 278)**

- **Standard Basis**: Variable costs are calculated based on **carloads of 30,000 pounds** using standard railway equipment.
- **Alternative Routes**: If goods can be transported via **two or more railway companies**, variable costs are based on the **lowest cost rail route**.
- This ensures that companies are not unfairly charged for more expensive routes when alternatives exist.

Depreciation is included in cost calculations to reflect the **decline in value** of assets over time, especially when lines or services are discontinued.

### 1. **General Depreciation Allowance (for continued operation)**

## ðŸ”¹ **Allowances for Cost of Capital**

The cost of capital is a critical component in calculating actual loss and justifying rates. It reflects the **opportunity cost** of using capital in rail operations.

### A. **For Sections 253, 260 (actual loss and passenger services)**  
Two options available:

1. **Option 1 (Salvage Value-Based)**  
   - Apply the **rate of return on capital** (including income tax) to the **salvage value** of road property to be retired.  
   - Cap at **net book investment** (computed under group depreciation plan).

2. **Option 2 (Net Book Investment-Based)**  
   - Apply the rate to the **net book investment** of road property to be retired, **only if** the company can demonstrate depreciation and net investment calculations that reflect the **specific aging characteristics** of the assets.

3. **Additional Allowance**  
   - Apply the same rate to the **net book value of all other property** necessary for operation (e.g., buildings, equipment).

> âœ… The CTC determines the appropriate rate based on company-specific conditions and financial realities.

### B. **For Sections 256, 258, 261 (branch lines and services)**  
More detailed rules:

1. **For road property to be retired**:
   - Apply the rate of return to the **salvage value** of road property, **not exceeding net book investment** (based on group depreciation plan).

2. **For new assets required for continued operation**:
   - Apply the rate to the **net book value** of such investments, **at the time of investment**.

3. **For all other necessary property**:
   - Apply the rate to the **net book value** of property required for continued operation.
