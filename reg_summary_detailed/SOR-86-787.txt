### üîç **Overview**

This regulation establishes the procedural framework governing **public hearings conducted by an Arbitration Committee** under section 75.16 of the *National Energy Board Act*. It supersedes the outdated rules from 1983 (P.C. 1983-529) and provides detailed guidelines on how notices of arbitration, replies, service, hearings, document production, issue formulation, and procedural conduct must be carried out to ensure fairness, transparency, and efficiency.

> **Key Purpose**: To ensure that arbitration proceedings related to pipeline projects ‚Äî particularly those involving land use, compensation, and damages ‚Äî are conducted in a timely, fair, and orderly manner while protecting the rights of all parties involved.

> **Scope**: Applies to all hearings conducted by the Arbitration Committee under section 75.16 of the Act.

> **Status**: **Revoked in 1993** (SOR/93-240, s. 2), meaning these rules are no longer in force. However, this summary remains valuable for historical, legal, and policy analysis.

### 1. **Notice of Arbitration Requirements**

#### A. When served by a **Company** (on a landowner):
- Must be:
  - Dated and signed by counsel or authorized officer.
  - Clearly state grounds, relevant facts, and nature of decision sought.
  - Divided into **consecutively numbered paragraphs**, each addressing a distinct issue.
  - Include:
    - Name and address of the company (and counsel if applicable).
    - Description and location of the lands, accompanied by a **plan**.
    - Amount of compensation offered (if any).
    - An **appraisal report** that includes:
      - Value of the land (excluding improvements).
      - Value of improvements and things grown on the land.
      - Estimated damage to remaining lands.
    - Certified copy of title or current abstract of title.
    - Names and addresses of all known parties with interest in the land.
    - A 30-day deadline for the respondent to file a reply.
    - If settling claims for damages, must include full description and evaluation of damages.

#### B. When served by a **Landowner** (on a company):
- Must be:
  - Dated and signed by the owner or counsel.
  - Include same elements as above, but:
    - Focus on **estimated relocation expenses** (if applicable).
    - Must include a clear statement of damages due to the company‚Äôs operations.
    - No compensation offer is required ‚Äî only a claim for damages.

> ‚úÖ **Common Elements**:
> - All notices must be concise, fact-based, and structured clearly.
> - The use of numbered paragraphs ensures clarity and avoids ambiguity.

---

### 2. **Service of Documents**

#### A. Who must receive the notice?
- One copy to the **Minister**.
- One copy to the **respondent**.
- One copy to **each person known to have an interest** in the land.

#### B. Service of Notice to the Minister:
- By **registered mail**.
- Date of service = **second day after posting** at a post office.

#### C. Service on a Person:
- **Personal service** is required unless impractical.
- Methods:
  - Leave with the person.
  - Leave with an officer of a company.
  - Leave with warden, reeve, mayor, or clerk (for municipal corporations).

#### D. Substituted Service (when personal service fails):
- Only allowed if:
  1. Personal service is **impractical**.
  2. There is a **reasonable possibility** the person will receive notice.
- Required application includes:
  - Affidavit detailing:
    - Efforts made to serve personally.
    - Prejudice from further attempts.
    - Last known address or places frequented.
    - Contacts with the person.
- **Methods of substituted service**:
  - Leave with an adult at residence, workplace, or frequented place.
  - Leave with someone in communication with the person.
  - Send by registered mail to last known address.
  - Publish in general-circulation publications in the area.
- **Effective date** of service:
  - The day **after** each method is carried out.
  - Or a later date set by the Committee in its order.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Proof of Service**:
> - For **landowners**: Filed with the Committee **at the time of hearing** (unless served directly to Minister or Committee).
> - For **companies**: Filed within **10 days** of service.

### 3. **Reply by the Respondent**

#### A. General Requirements:
- Must be:
  - Dated and signed by counsel or authorized officer.
  - Divided into numbered paragraphs.
  - Include:
    - Name and address of the company or owner.
    - Clear reasons for opposing the decision sought.
    - Admission or denial of facts in the notice.
    - Description and plan of lands (if disputed).
    - Compensation offer (if applicable), with an **appraisal report**.
    - Certified copy of title or abstract.
    - Names and addresses of all parties with interest.
    - Full description and evaluation of damages (if applicable).

#### B. If Respondent is the Landowner:
- Must:
  - State reasons for opposing the company‚Äôs claim.
  - Admit or deny facts.
  - Include estimated relocation costs.
  - Describe damages (if applicable).

#### C. Deadline:
- Must be filed within **30 days** of service of the notice of arbitration.

> ‚ùå Failure to respond within 30 days may result in default, though the Committee may allow extensions.

---

### 4. **Document Production and Inspection**

#### A. Availability of Documents:
- The Committee must make all documents filed with it **available for inspection** during regular business hours.
- Parties may make copies at their own expense.

#### B. Request to Inspect Documents:
- Any party may request a document held by another party or their counsel.
- The party holding the document must allow inspection and copying.

#### C. Failure to Produce:
- If a party fails to produce a document:
  - The Committee will **refuse to admit it as evidence** unless it can prove **sufficient reason** for non-compliance.
- The requesting party may introduce **secondary evidence** (e.g., a copy from a third party).

#### D. Committee‚Äôs Authority:
- May request any party to provide information or documents necessary for a determination.
- Parties are required to comply.

---

### 5. **Formulation of Issues in Dispute**

#### A. When the Committee may intervene:
- If the notice or reply **fails to clearly raise the issues**, the Committee may order the parties to **formulate the issues**.

#### B. If parties fail to do so:
- The Committee may **formulate the issues itself**.

#### C. Purpose of Issue Formulation:
To:
- Simplify complex disputes.
- Clarify or limit the scope of the issues.
- Consider:
  - Admissions of facts.
  - Use of affidavits or public documents.
  - Procedure for the hearing.
  - Exchange of exhibits and supporting documents.

#### D. Conference or Written Submissions:
- The Committee may order parties or their counsel to:
  - Appear in person before **three members** of the Committee.
  - Or submit written submissions.
- Purpose: To streamline evidence, reduce burden, and improve decision-making.

---

### 6. **Stay of Proceedings**

- If the Committee requests additional information or documents, it may **order a stay** of proceedings for a reasonable period.
- Stay duration is determined by the Committee based on circumstances.
