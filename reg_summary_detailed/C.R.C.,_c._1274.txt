**Detailed Summary of the *Armed Forces Postal Regulations***

---

### **1. Regulation Title and Citation**
The regulation is officially titled the **Armed Forces Postal Regulations** and may be cited as such. It governs postal services and mailing procedures specifically for members of the Canadian Forces and certain civilians associated with them when mail is sent to or from outside Canada.

> **Note**: This regulation has been significantly revised or repealed over time, with several key terms and provisions (e.g., "first class mail," "book," "C.O.D. Service," "parcel post") being officially **repealed**. The current version reflects only the surviving, active provisions relevant to the postal operations of the Canadian Forces.

---

### **2. Key Definitions (Relevant to the Current Scope)**

#### **Canadian Forces Post Office (CFPO)**
- A military post office operated **outside Canada** by the **Canadian Forces Postal Service**.
- Provides postal services to:
  - Members of the Canadian Forces stationed abroad.
  - Civilians who normally receive mail through a CFPO.

#### **Fleet Mail Office (FMO)**
- A military post office operated by the Canadian Forces Postal Service to serve **Canadian Forces vessels** (ships or vessels of war) at sea.
- Enables mail transmission to and from military ships operating internationally.

#### **Forces Air Letter (Lettre-avion pour militaire)**
A specially authorized **stationery item** designed for use by:
- Any member of the Canadian Forces who is served through a CFPO or FMO and sends or receives mail in Canada.
- Any person in Canada who writes to such a member.

### **3. Scope of Application**

These regulations apply to mail sent **to or by**:
1. **Any member of the Canadian Forces** stationed **outside Canada** who is served through:
   - A Canadian Forces Post Office (CFPO)
   - A Fleet Mail Office (FMO)
2. **Any civilian** who normally receives mail through a CFPO.

> This means that only mail involving these specific individuals or those receiving mail via military postal facilities is covered under these rules.

---

### **4. Rates and Fees**

- The **rates** applicable to mail under these regulations are set out in the **Schedule** attached to the regulation.
- These rates govern the cost of mailing items (such as letters or parcels) when posted through CFPOs or FMOs.
- The schedule applies to all mail posted under these regulations, including both domestic and international mail.

> âš ï¸ Note: Many prior classifications (e.g., first class mail, fourth class mail, parcel post) have been **repealed** and are no longer in effect.

---

### **5. Registration of Mail**

- **Letter mail**, **letters**, and **Forces Air Letters** may be registered if they meet the requirements set out in the **Special Services and Fees Regulations** for registered mail in Canada.
- The **registration fee** applies to items posted in Canada for delivery within Canada.
- Specifically, the **rate in Schedule VII** of the *Special Services and Fees Regulations* applies to such registered items.

> Registration allows for tracking and proof of delivery, which is especially important for sensitive or valuable correspondence.

---

### **6. Customs and Duty Requirements (for International Mail)**

When a letter or letter-post item may be subject to **customs duties** in the destination country, the sender must comply with the following:

#### **A. Green Label Requirement**
- A **completed green label** (as defined in paragraph 7.1(1)(a) of the *International Letter-post Items Regulations*) must be affixed:
  - On the **address side**, in the **upper left corner**
  - Under the senderâ€™s name and address
- Alternatively, a **printed green facsimile** of the label may be used, provided it is:
  - 74 mm in length
  - 52 mm in width

> This label alerts customs authorities to the potential duty implications of the item.

#### **B. Additional Declaration (if value exceeds $500)**
If the value of the item exceeds **$500 CAD**:
1. The sender must complete the **Customs Declaration Dispatch Note** (as set out in **Schedule III** of the *International Letter-post Items Regulations*).
2. The declaration must be **securely affixed** to the item, preferably on the address side.
3. The item must be **hand-delivered to a postal employee at a post office** for processing.

> This requirement ensures compliance with international customs laws and prevents unauthorized or undeclared high-value shipments.

---

### **8. Key Principles and Objectives**

1. **Simplified and Secure Communication**  
   - The Forces Air Letter provides a standardized, secure, and cost-effective method for military personnel to communicate with family and friends in Canada.

2. **Compliance with International Standards**  
   - The regulation aligns with the *International Letter-post Items Regulations*, particularly regarding customs declarations and labeling.

3. **Customs Compliance and Risk Mitigation**  
   - Clear rules for high-value items prevent smuggling and ensure legal compliance with customs laws.

4. **Modernization of Postal Services**  
   - The removal of outdated classifications reflects a shift toward more efficient, transparent, and internationally harmonized postal systems.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Armed Forces Postal Regulations* provide a **targeted, practical, and legally compliant framework** for postal services involving Canadian Forces personnel and their dependents or civilian associates abroad. The regulation emphasizes:

- Standardized use of **Forces Air Letters** with strict formatting and content rules.
- Clear **customs and duty obligations** for international mail, especially for high-value items.
- Alignment with broader Canadian postal and international postal standards.
- A modernized structure that reflects the **repeal of obsolete classifications** and the adoption of current postal practices.

While the regulation is narrow in scope (limited to military and associated civilian mail), it plays a critical role in maintaining communication between military members and their families, while ensuring legal, financial, and logistical compliance with both domestic and international postal laws.
