## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Regulation:  
"Regulations Prescribing Games of Chance and Prescribing Registrants Making Supplies of a Right to Participate in Games of Chance"**

### **1. Legal Basis and Context**
- **Authority**: The regulation was made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, under **subsection 277(1)** of the *Excise Tax Act*.
- **Purpose**: To prescribe:
  - Which entities are considered **"registrants"** (i.e., authorized gaming bodies);
  - What constitutes a **"prescribed game of chance"**;
  - Key **definitions** and **tax rules** applicable to supplies of rights to participate in games of chance, especially those involving provincial gaming authorities.
- **Status**: This regulation was **repealed in 2011** (SOR/2011-56, s. 8), meaning it is **no longer in force**. However, it remains historically significant and may have influenced current regulatory frameworks.

> **Net Tax = (Net Tax from Gaming Activities) + (Net Tax from Non-Gaming Activities)**

### C. **Adding Amounts to Net Tax (When a Bet is Placed)**

When a person places a bet with a PGA (excluding purchasing an instant win ticket from a distributor), the authority **adds** to its gaming net tax the amount:

> **Added Amount = (Tax Rate Base) √ó (100% + %A) √ó (Bet Amount) / (Tax Amount by Person)**

Where:
- **Tax Rate Base** =  
  - If bet placed in a **participating province**: *Rate in subsection 165(1) + provincial tax rate*  
  - Else: *Only rate in subsection 165(1)*  
- **%A** = Percentage determined for A (likely a fixed or variable rate, e.g., 100% for full tax inclusion)  
- **Bet Amount** = Total amount bet, including tax paid by the person  
- **Tax by Person** = Any tax the person pays on the bet under provincial law

> üîç This rule ensures that **all bets** are taxed at a rate that reflects both federal and provincial tax obligations.

### D. **Adding Amounts for Instant Win Tickets**

When a PGA delivers or agrees to deliver an instant win ticket to a distributor, and the distributor pays the authority during a reporting period, the authority adds:

> **Added Amount = (Tax Rate Base) √ó (100% + %A) √ó (Face Value of Ticket) / (Tax by Distributor)**

Where:
- **Face Value** = Price shown on the ticket, inclusive of tax
- **Tax by Distributor** = Any tax paid by the distributor on the ticket

> ‚ö†Ô∏è This ensures that **instant win tickets** are taxed as if they were part of a bet, even though they are sold to distributors.

### E. **Credit for Prizes or Winnings (Non-Instant Win)**

When a prize is payable (e.g., from a bet), the authority receives a **credit** equal to:

> **Credit = (Tax Rate Base) √ó (100% + %A) √ó (Prize Amount)**

Where:
- **Tax Rate Base** = Same as above (provincial + federal if applicable)
- **Prize Amount** = Actual amount paid as prize

> ‚úÖ This credit offsets the tax added when the bet was placed.
