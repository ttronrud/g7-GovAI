**Detailed Summary of the Canadian Industrial Renewal Regulations**

---

### **Overview**

The *Canadian Industrial Renewal Regulations* (CIRR) are a set of administrative rules established under the authority of the *Department of Industry, Trade and Commerce Act* and the *Appropriation Act No. 1, 1980–81*, specifically Vote 1a (Industry, Trade and Commerce). These regulations were enacted by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Industry, Trade and Commerce, the Minister of Regional Economic Expansion, and the Treasury Board.

The primary objective of the regulations is to **support and revitalize Canadian manufacturing and processing industries**, particularly those in decline or facing international competition, through **loans, insurance, and contributions**. The program focuses on enhancing competitiveness, promoting job retention, encouraging technological advancement, and supporting business restructuring in designated communities.

---

## **Key Definitions**

To ensure clarity and consistency, the regulations define several key terms:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Application** | A loan, insurance, or contribution application submitted under these regulations. |
| **Business Enterprise** | An eligible manufacturer or a designated business. |
| **Contribution** | Financial support authorized under a vote of an Appropriation Act. |
| **Designated Business** | A business engaged in manufacturing or processing activities in a designated community. |
| **Designated Community** | A geographic area designated under the *Department of Regional Economic Expansion Act* for economic revitalization purposes. |
| **Eligible Manufacturer** | A manufacturer engaged in or about to engage in an eligible manufacturing or processing activity. |
| **Eligible Manufacturing or Processing Activity** | Activities classified under specific SIC (Standard Industrial Classification) codes, including: <br>• Rubber footwear (SIC 1624) <br>• Leather tanneries (SIC 172) <br>• Shoe factories (SIC 174) <br>• Glove/ mittens manufacturing (SIC 175) <br>• Textile industries (excluding hygiene products) <br>• Knitting, clothing, and sporting goods (SIC 3931 and 3993) <br>• Coated fabrics (non-rubberized) |
| **External or Other Business** | A business operating outside a designated community, or one planning to move into a community with fewer than 100,000 residents that has been severely affected by layoffs or shutdowns in eligible manufacturing. |
| **Manufacturer** | Any person engaged in manufacturing or processing in Canada. |
| **Manufacturing or Processing Activity** | Any activity involving transformation of raw materials into goods, including chemical, physical, or biochemical changes, excluding growth or decay. |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Regional Economic Expansion (responsible for administering the program). |
| **Private Lender** | A financially sound lender that is not a government entity, provincial government, municipal corporation, or agency thereof. |
| **Restructure** | A significant change in operations—products, methods, markets, or management—such as acquisition of working capital, machinery, or buildings. |

## **Core Provisions and Objectives**

### **1. Administration and Authority**
- The **Minister of Regional Economic Expansion** is responsible for administering all loans, insurance, and contributions under these regulations.
- The Minister may enter into agreements, require documentation, and impose stipulations to ensure compliance and effectiveness.
- The Minister must act to **maximize economic benefits** and **minimize losses** to Her Majesty’s government.

---

### **2. Loan Programs**

#### **(a) General Loan Criteria**
- A loan may only be granted if the applicant would **likely be unable to implement the project without the Minister’s assistance**.
- Loans are only provided when the applicant **cannot obtain financing on reasonable terms** from other sources.

#### **(b) Loan Amounts and Terms**
- Maximum loan amount: **$1.5 million** for mergers, acquisitions, or amalgamations involving a business enterprise.
- For other cases, the loan amount is not capped, but must be justified.
- **Interest rate**: 
  - Must be **at least equal to the interest rate charged by the Government of Canada to Crown corporations** for similar loans.
  - Cannot exceed **3% above that rate**.
- **Term**: Up to **20 years**.
- **Repayment**: Can be repaid early without penalty or bonus.
- **Security**: The Minister may require security and may retransfer or amend it as needed. A trustee or receiver may be appointed.

#### **(c) Loan for Restructuring**
- The Minister may provide a loan to support a business’s restructuring (e.g., changes in production, market, or management) if it improves international competitiveness.

#### **(d) Loan for Mergers/Acquisitions**
- A person may apply for a loan to:
  - Purchase effective ownership control of a business enterprise.
  - Acquire whole or significant portions of its assets.
  - Effect a merger or amalgamation.
- Loan cap: **$1.5 million**.
- The feasibility study and business plans must be developed with a qualified consultant, unless the applicant proves they can conduct the study independently.

---

### **3. Insurance on Private Lender Loans**

#### **Purpose of Insurance**
Insurance is provided to protect private lenders against loss in cases where:
- The business is restructuring to improve international competitiveness.
- The business is in financial distress and needs time to sell assets or transfer ownership.
- A business is acquiring another business or assets.
- A service provider is enhancing services to support a competitive business.

#### **Conditions for Insurance**
- The business must require the loan to:
  - Restructure operations.
  - Engage in international trade-competitive production.
  - Acquire a business or its assets.
  - Continue operations during a transition period (e.g., asset sale).
- The acquisition or restructuring must:
  - Prevent serious disruption.
  - Improve efficiency, productivity, or international competitiveness.
  - Not proceed without the Minister’s insurance.
  - Be limited in liability to **10% of the loan amount** for the borrower.

#### **Liability and Guarantees**
- The borrower must provide:
  - A **10% guarantee** to the private lender.
  - Appropriate **security on assets** acquired or on the business being acquired.
- The private lender must agree to these conditions.

#### **Insurance Limits**
- **Section 12, 14, or 15**: Insurance **cannot exceed 90%** of the loan amount.
- **Section 13 or 16**: Insurance **cannot exceed 100%** of the loan amount.

#### **Insurance Fee**
- **1% per annum** of the insured amount, paid in **semi-annual installments**.
- The Minister may **waive or reduce** the fee in exceptional circumstances (e.g., hardship or public interest).

#### **Repayment of Loss**
- If a private lender demands repayment:
  - For **sections 12 or 15**: Repayment capped at **90% of the loss**.
  - For **sections 13 or 16**: Repayment capped at **100% of the loss**.
  - For **section 14**: Repayment capped at **90% of the loss**, **excluding recoveries from guarantees**.

#### **Periodic Advances**
- If a loan is advanced in installments, the Minister may provide insurance on each advance.

---

### **4. Contribution Programs**

Contributions are financial supports provided to help businesses undertake specific activities that enhance competitiveness, innovation, or feasibility.

#### **Eligible Contribution Uses**
1. **Feasibility Studies & Planning**:
   - Costs of engaging a qualified consultant to develop a proposal for a loan or contribution.
   - Market feasibility studies or market strategy development.
2. **Product Development**:
   - Costs of developing or improving **technologically advanced products or services** with commercial potential.
   - Must be scientifically and technically feasible but carry **technical risk**.
3. **Industrial Design**:
   - Costs of designing new mass-produced durable products, directed by a qualified industrial designer.
4. **Product Testing**:
   - Market testing or product testing to validate new offerings.
5. **Productivity Enhancement Projects**:
   - Projects that involve a **significant departure from traditional practices** using existing technology.
   - Must show **demonstrable uncertainty** and **potential for significant gains** in productivity or efficiency.
6. **Protection of Crown Interest**:
   - Costs to protect the government’s interest in loans, insurance, or contributions.

#### **Contribution Limits**
- Contributions must be **sufficient to avoid significant economic hardship** for the business.
- No specific dollar cap is mentioned, but amounts are strictly tied to necessity and feasibility.

---

## **Eligibility and Application Process**

### **Application Requirements**
- Any person seeking a loan, insurance, or contribution must:
  - Submit a formal application to the Minister.
  - Provide **all necessary information** to assess eligibility and feasibility.

### **Approval Criteria**
- The Minister will approve a request **only if**:
  - The applicant cannot obtain financing on reasonable terms.
  - The project aligns with the goals of industrial renewal (e.g., competitiveness, job retention, innovation).
