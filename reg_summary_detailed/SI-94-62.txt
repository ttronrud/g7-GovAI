### **Detailed Summary of the Canadian Volunteer Service Medal Order**

This regulation, issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council** on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, **revokes the original Order in Council P.C. 8160 of October 22, 1943**, which established the *Canadian Volunteer Service Medal*, and replaces it with a **revised, comprehensive Order** that defines the criteria, eligibility, design, and award procedures for the **Canadian Volunteer Service Medal (CVSM)**. The new Order provides a detailed and structured framework for recognizing individuals who contributed voluntarily during **World War II** in various capacities—military, civilian, and auxiliary roles.

---

## **1. Legal Authority and Citation**
- The Order is issued under the authority of the Governor General in Council, based on the recommendation of the Prime Minister.
- It may be cited as the **Canadian Volunteer Service Medal Order**.
- This Order supersedes the previous 1943 regulation and reflects updated eligibility standards and administrative clarity.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**
The Order introduces precise definitions to ensure consistent interpretation:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Active Service** | Service during World War II (September 1, 1939 – September 1, 1945). |
| **Common-Law Partner** | A person who has cohabited with the individual in a conjugal relationship for at least one year. |
| **Medal** | The Canadian Volunteer Service Medal (CVSM). |
| **Survivor** | In order of precedence: spouse or common-law partner → eldest child → father → mother → eldest brother or sister. |
| **World War II** | The period from September 1, 1939, to September 1, 1945. |

---

## **3. Design of the Medal**
- **Shape and Material**: Circular, made of silver.
- **Obverse (Front)**:
  - Depicts marching figures representing wartime volunteers.
  - Inscribed with:
    - "CANADA" above
    - "VOLUNTARY SERVICE VOLONTAIRE" below
- **Reverse (Back)**:
  - Features the **Canadian Coat of Arms**.
- **Suspension Ribbon**:
  - Width: **1¼ inches**.
  - Divided into **five stripes**:
    - Central stripe: **Royal Blue**
    - Left and right of central stripe: **Scarlet** (each 3/16 inch wide)
    - Outer stripes: **Green** (each 3/16 inch wide)

> *Note: The ribbon design symbolizes the unity and sacrifice of volunteers, with blue representing Canada, scarlet denoting courage, and green symbolizing peace and growth.*

---

## **4. Eligibility for Awarding the Medal**

The CVSM may be awarded to individuals who meet one of the following criteria:

### **A. Armed Forces Personnel (Voluntary Service)**
- Must have served **voluntarily** on active service during WWII.
- Must meet one of these conditions:
  1. **Minimum 18 months of service**, ending on or before **March 1, 1947**, and was **honourably discharged or retired**.
  2. **Minimum 6 months of service**, any part of which was **outside Canada**, and was honourably discharged or retired.
  3. Discharged or retired due to an **injury or disease** (or aggravation) **attributable to or incurred during service**.

### **B. Posthumous Awards**
- To the **survivor** of a person who:
  - Was killed in action or died from injury/disease/aggravation incurred during service.
  - Meets the eligibility criteria above.

### **C. Youth Service (Under 19 at Start of War)**
- A person who:
  - Was **under 19 years old** on **September 2, 1945**,
  - Was **voluntarily serving** in the armed forces,
  - Was **compulsorily honourably discharged** before completing 18 months of service.

### **D. Canadian Merchant Mariners**
- Must be within the meaning of **subsection 21.1(4) of the Pension Act**.
- Eligible if:
  1. Served for **18 months or more**, ending by March 1, 1947, and was honourably discharged or retired.
  2. Served for **6 months or more**, any part outside Canada, and was honourably discharged or retired.
  3. Discharged or retired due to injury/disease related to service.

### **E. Merchant Navy Veterans (War Veterans Allowance Act)**
- Must be within the meaning of **subsection 37(7.3) of the War Veterans Allowance Act**.
- Eligible if:
  - Served for **6 months or more**, any part outside Canada, and was honourably discharged or retired.

### **F. Auxiliary & Support Personnel**
- Individuals employed by recognized organizations (e.g., Canadian Legion, YMCA, Knights of Columbus, Salvation Army) as:
  - Authorized field representatives,
  - Helpers,
  - Overseas Headquarters Staff.
- Must have served **at least 6 months**, any part outside Canada, and been honourably discharged or retired.
- Eligible if service resulted in injury/disease attributable to service.

### **G. Canadian Fire Fighters Serving in the UK**
- Must have served during WWII.
- Minimum **6 months**, any part outside Canada.
- Must have been honourably discharged or retired due to injury/disease related to service.

### **H. Nursing and Medical Personnel**
- Must have served under:
  - Canadian Red Cross Society or St. John Ambulance Brigade.
- Eligible if:
  - With Royal Canadian Army Medical Corps for **18 months** (with approval of Adjutant-General) and honourably discharged.
  - Outside Canada as a welfare worker, nursing aid, orthopaedic surgeon, nurse, ambulance driver, etc., for **6 months or more**, and honourably discharged.
  - Discharged due to injury/disease related to service.

### **I. Aircrew Employed by UK Air Ministry**
- Must have:
  - Been domiciled in Canada at the start of employment.
  - Served in **Number 45 Wing, 45 Group, or Atlantic Ferrying Organization** of the RAF.
  - Made **at least one trans-oceanic flight**.
  - Served for **6 months or more continuously**.
  - Was honourably discharged or retired due to injury/disease related to service.

### **J. British Commonwealth Air Training Plan (BCATP) Instructors**
- Must have:
  - Provided air crew instruction during WWII.
  - Served for **18 months or more**.
  - Been honourably discharged or retired due to injury/disease related to service.

### **K. Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)**
- Members or reserve/special constables (under prior Orders P.C. 2484 and P.C. 3444) who:
  - Served **voluntarily** on full-time basis during WWII.
  - Total service (not including time after March 1, 1947):
    - At least **18 months** (honourable discharge or retirement).
    - At least **6 months**, any part outside Canada (honourable discharge or retirement).
    - Discharged or retired due to injury/disease related to service.

### **L. Posthumous Awards to Survivors**
- To the **survivor** of any person mentioned in (d) to (j.1) if:
  - The person was killed in action or died from injury/disease related to service.
- Also, to the **survivor** of someone eligible under (a) or (c) to (j.1) but **not awarded** the medal.

### **M. Replacement Awards**
- To any person who previously received the medal but it was **lost, stolen, or destroyed**.
- The recipient must **pay the cost of replacement**.

> ⚠️ **Exclusion**: A person who received the **Newfoundland Second World War Volunteer Service Medal** is **not eligible** for the CVSM.

---

## **5. Exclusions from Eligibility**
The Medal shall **not be awarded** to individuals who:
- Were **discharged for misconduct**.
- Were a **deserter or absent without leave** at the end of WWII.
- Have been **convicted of an indictable offence** without a pardon.

---

## **6. Service Period Calculations**
Certain periods are **excluded** from qualifying service:
- Periods of **absence without leave (AWOL)**.
- Time spent in **penal servitude, field punishment, detention, or imprisonment** (including arrest awaiting trial).
- **Leave without pay**.
- Service **after September 1, 1945** (i.e., post-WWII service is not counted).

> *Note: This ensures only genuine wartime service is recognized.*

---

## **7. Service Additions (Combining Service Periods)**
- **Foreign Armed Forces Service**: Service in armed forces of other realms (e.g., UK, Australia, UK colonies) or the US during WWII may be **added** to subsequent Canadian service to meet qualifying periods.
- **Merchant Navy Service Additions**:
  - Analogous service in foreign merchant navies (excluding Canada or US).
  - Service in foreign armed forces (excluding cases where the individual already qualifies via such service).
