### **Regulatory Summary: Repeal and Designation of the Minister of Industry as Designated Minister and Appropriate Minister**

#### **1. Legal Authority and Basis**
The regulation is issued under the authority of:
- **Section 2 of the Business Development Bank of Canada Act (BDBCA)** – specifically, the definition of "Designated Minister."
- **Paragraph (d) of the definition "appropriate Minister" in Section 2 of the Financial Administration Act (FAA)**.
- **Subparagraph (a)(ii) of the definition "appropriate Minister" in subsection 83(1) of the Financial Administration Act**.

These provisions establish the legal framework for designating a minister responsible for overseeing the operations, governance, and financial administration of the **Business Development Bank of Canada (BDBC)**.

#### **2. Key Actions Taken in the Regulation**

##### **(a) Repeal of Prior Order in Council**
- The regulation **repeals Order in Council P.C. 2023-1066**, issued on **October 27, 2023**.
- This prior order had previously designated a different minister or set out a different administrative structure for the BDBC.
- By repealing it, the government formally **ends the previous designation**, removing any prior ministerial assignment or authority related to the BDBC under that order.

##### **(b) Designation of the Minister of Industry as the Designated Minister**
- The **Minister of Industry** is formally designated as the **Designated Minister** under the **Business Development Bank of Canada Act**.
- This role is defined in **Section 2** of the BDBCA and is responsible for:
  - Providing strategic direction to the BDBC.
  - Ensuring alignment of the BDBC’s mandate with national economic and industrial policy goals.
  - Acting as the primary point of contact between the federal government and the BDBC.
  - Approving major decisions, such as changes in governance, strategic plans, or funding priorities.
- This designation reflects the BDBC’s core mandate—supporting small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), innovation, and industrial development—areas directly aligned with the responsibilities of the Minister of Industry.

##### **(c) Designation of the Minister of Industry as the Appropriate Minister under the Financial Administration Act**
- The **Minister of Industry** is also designated as the **appropriate Minister** with respect to the **Business Development Bank of Canada** under:
  - **Section 2 of the Financial Administration Act (FAA)** (specifically paragraph (d)), and
  - **Subsection 83(1)(a)(ii)** of the FAA.
- This designation grants the Minister of Industry specific powers and responsibilities under the FAA, including:
  - Approving financial administration matters related to the BDBC (e.g., budgetary allocations, financial reporting, and financial oversight).
  - Authorizing the BDBC to enter into contracts, issue loans, or conduct financial operations.
  - Ensuring compliance with federal financial management standards and accountability requirements.
- This ensures that the BDBC operates within a clear and accountable financial governance framework, with the Minister of Industry as the key federal official responsible for financial oversight.

#### **3. Legal and Policy Significance**

- **Continuity and Clarity**: The regulation provides legal clarity by formally assigning responsibility to the Minister of Industry, replacing a previous, potentially ambiguous or outdated designation.
- **Policy Alignment**: The choice of the Minister of Industry reflects a strategic alignment with the BDBC’s mission—fostering innovation, supporting SMEs, and advancing Canada’s industrial competitiveness.
- **Accountability and Oversight**: By designating the Minister of Industry as both the Designated Minister and the appropriate Minister, the government ensures that the BDBC is subject to strong ministerial oversight and accountability, consistent with federal financial and administrative standards.

#### **4. Effective Date and Legal Status**
- The regulation is published as **S.C. 1995, c. 28** (Statutes of Canada, 1995, Chapter 28) and **R.S., c. F-11** (Revised Statutes of Canada, Chapter F-11).
- It is a **statutory instrument** (SI/2023-73), meaning it is a delegated legislative instrument issued under the authority of an Act of Parliament.
- It comes into force upon publication in the Canada Gazette and is binding on all relevant federal departments and agencies.

#### **5. Implications for the Business Development Bank of Canada**
- The BDBC will now operate under a clear and consistent ministerial framework.
- The Minister of Industry will have direct responsibility for:
  - Strategic oversight.
  - Financial accountability.
  - Ensuring that BDBC programs support national economic priorities.
- This strengthens the integration of BDBC activities with broader federal economic and industrial policy initiatives.

### **Conclusion**
This regulation effectively **repeals a prior administrative designation** and **formally assigns the Minister of Industry as both the Designated Minister and the appropriate Minister** for the Business Development Bank of Canada. This action ensures that the BDBC is properly aligned with the government’s economic development goals, operates under clear legal authority, and is subject to robust financial and strategic oversight. It represents a significant step in clarifying governance and accountability for Canada’s key financial institution supporting small businesses and innovation.
