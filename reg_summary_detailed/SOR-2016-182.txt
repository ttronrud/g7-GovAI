### ðŸ”¹ **3. Safety Requirements for Matches**

#### âœ… **General Requirements (Section 2)**
All matches must meet the following criteria:

1. **No White Phosphorus**  
   - Matches must **not contain white phosphorus**, which poses a significant health and safety risk (e.g., respiratory irritation, toxic fumes, spontaneous combustion).

2. **Uniform Application of Ignition Compound**  
   - The ignition compound must be **evenly and consistently applied** across the match head. Any unevenness may lead to inconsistent ignition or failure to ignite.

3. **Match Head Integrity**  
   - The match head must be:
     - Free from **chipping, cracking, or crumbling**.
     - Not **adhering to the head of another match** (prevents dangerous sticking or transfer of flame).

4. **Wood Match Splint Requirements**  
   - Must be made from **good-grade, long-fibre wood**.
   - Must **not be broken, split, or crushed**â€”ensuring durability and safe handling.

5. **No Spontaneous Ignition**  
   - Matches must **not ignite spontaneously** when tested according to **Schedule 1** (a standardized test method involving heat exposure).

6. **Ignition Compound Behavior**  
   - When ignited, the ignition compound must **not**:
     - Crack,
     - Explode, or
     - Drop glowing particles (which could cause burns or fires).

7. **Splint Glow Duration**  
   - After the flame is extinguished, the splint must **not glow for more than 3 seconds**. This prevents prolonged fire hazards after use.

8. **Breaking Resistance of Splints (Tested per Schedule 2)**

### ðŸ”¹ **4. Matchbook Requirements (Section 3)**

Matchbooks must meet the following design and safety standards:

1. **Cover Must Fully Enclose Match Heads**  
   - When closed, the cover must **completely cover all match heads** to prevent accidental contact or exposure.

2. **Fastening Wire Safety**  
   - Any wire used to secure the cover to the common base must:
     - **Not pierce** the striking surface.
     - **Not loosen** the striking surface during use or handling.

3. **Friction Paint Proximity Rule**  
   - Any friction paint (used to create the striking surface) on the **inside of the cover** must be **at least 2.54 cm (1 inch)** away from the match heads.

4. **Striking Surface Orientation**  
   - The striking surface must be on the **side opposite to the opening** of the matchbook. This ensures that users rub the surface only when opening the book, reducing the risk of accidental ignition during storage or transport.

  - The **manufacturer**, if the matches are produced in Canada; or
  - The **importer or distributor**, if the matches are imported.

> This ensures traceability and accountability for product safety.
