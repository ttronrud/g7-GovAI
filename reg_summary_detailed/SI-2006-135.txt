### **Regulatory Summary: Transfer of Responsibilities from the Department of Industry to the Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development**

#### **Legal Authority**
The regulation is issued under the authority of **paragraph 2(a)(a)** of **Section 207 of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act (S.C. 2003, c. 22)**.  
This provision allows the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister) to transfer control, supervision, powers, duties, and functions between federal departments and agencies, in order to improve administrative efficiency, alignment with policy goals, or better serve specific populations.

---

#### **Effective Date**
The transfer becomes effective on **December 1, 2006**.

---

### **Key Transfers of Control and Supervision**

#### 1. **Aboriginal Business Canada (within the Regional Operations Sector)**
- **Current Department**: Department of Industry  
- **Transferred To**: Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (DIAND)  
- **Nature of Transfer**: Control and supervision of the Aboriginal Business Canada unit are transferred from the Department of Industry to DIAND.  
- **Purpose**: This move reflects a strategic realignment to ensure that policies and programs supporting Indigenous economic development are managed within a department with direct responsibility for Indigenous peoples.  
- **Implication**: Aboriginal Business Canada will now operate under the oversight of DIAND, which is responsible for Indigenous relations, economic development, and cultural initiatives in Canada. This enhances coordination between economic development initiatives and Indigenous communities.

#### 2. **First Nations SchoolNet (within the E-Learning Directorate)**
- **Current Department**: Department of Industry  
- **Location**: Information Highway Applications Branch, Spectrum, Information Technologies and Telecommunications Sector  
- **Transferred To**: Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (DIAND)  
- **Nature of Transfer**: Control and supervision of the First Nations SchoolNet program are transferred from the Department of Industry to DIAND.  
- **Purpose**: First Nations SchoolNet is a technology-based education initiative designed to provide access to digital learning tools and online educational resources for First Nations schools.  
- **Implication**: By moving this program to DIAND, the federal government ensures that Indigenous education needs are directly addressed by a department with statutory responsibility for education and cultural programs in Indigenous communities. This improves relevance, cultural sensitivity, and responsiveness in delivering educational technology services.

### **Transfer of Powers, Duties, and Functions**

#### 1. **National Aboriginal Economic Development Board (NAEDB)**
- **Established By**: Order in Council P.C. 1990–374, dated February 23, 1990  
- **Original Minister**: Minister of Industry  
- **Transferred To**: Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development  
- **Nature of Transfer**: All powers, duties, and functions of the Minister of Industry related to the NAEDB are transferred to the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development.  
- **Purpose**: The NAEDB was created to promote and support Aboriginal economic development across Canada. This transfer ensures that Indigenous economic development policy is now managed by a minister with direct responsibility for Indigenous affairs.  
- **Implication**:  
  - The board will now operate under the policy and administrative framework of DIAND.  
  - This strengthens the integration of economic development initiatives with broader Indigenous self-determination, cultural preservation, and community-based planning.  
  - It supports a more holistic and culturally appropriate approach to Indigenous economic growth.

### **Overall Rationale and Policy Significance**

This regulatory transfer is part of a broader **government strategy to centralize and strengthen Indigenous affairs** within the federal structure. Key objectives include:

- **Improved Policy Alignment**: Ensuring that economic development, education, and Indigenous governance are coordinated under a single department with dedicated mandate and responsibility.
- **Enhanced Accountability and Responsiveness**: Indigenous communities benefit from programs and policies being managed by a department that understands their unique needs, values, and contexts.
- **Strengthening Self-Determination**: By placing Indigenous economic and educational initiatives under the purview of DIAND, the government supports Indigenous self-governance and community-led development.
- **Administrative Efficiency**: Reducing duplication and improving interdepartmental coordination in areas of overlap between economic development and Indigenous affairs.

### **Conclusion**

This regulation marks a significant shift in the federal government’s organizational structure, moving key Indigenous-focused programs and policy responsibilities from the Department of Industry to the Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development. The transfers of **Aboriginal Business Canada**, **First Nations SchoolNet**, and the **powers related to the National Aboriginal Economic Development Board** reflect a commitment to:

- Decentralizing Indigenous affairs under a dedicated ministry,  
- Ensuring culturally appropriate and community-driven solutions,  
- Strengthening the federal government’s capacity to support Indigenous economic and educational advancement.

The effective date of **December 1, 2006** signals the formal implementation of these changes, with lasting impacts on federal policy delivery to First Nations, Métis, and Inuit communities.
