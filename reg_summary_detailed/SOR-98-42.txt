**Detailed Summary of the *Used or Second-hand Motor Vehicles Regulations*, S.C. 1997, c. 36**

---

### **Overview**

The *Used or Second-hand Motor Vehicles Regulations* (S.C. 1997, c. 36) are a set of rules made by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance, under **subparagraph 132(1)(m)(ii)** of the *Customs Tariff*. These regulations establish specific exemptions from standard customs duties and import restrictions for certain used or second-hand motor vehicles.

The primary purpose of these regulations is to **allow the importation of used vehicles without paying customs duties or being subject to full tariff obligations**, under defined and limited circumstances. This promotes practicality, economic efficiency, and fairness in cases where new vehicles are not feasible or available, particularly in specialized or personal contexts.

These regulations **come into force on January 1, 1998**, and are intended to apply only to motor vehicles that meet specific eligibility criteria.

---

### **Key Provisions and Eligible Categories**

The following categories of used or second-hand motor vehicles are explicitly exempt from standard tariff treatment (i.e., from being subject to duties under tariff item 9897.00.00):

---

#### **1. Vehicles Used in Oil & Gas Exploration or Development**
- The vehicle must:
  - Be manufactured **before the calendar year** of importation.
  - Have **machinery or apparatus permanently mounted** on it.
  - Be imported for use in:
    - Exploratory or discovery work related to oil or natural gas wells.
    - Development, maintenance, testing, depletion, or production of a well **up to and including the wellhead assembly**.
    - Drilling for water.
- **Exemption condition**: A similarly equipped vehicle must **not be readily available in Canada**.

> *Rationale*: Supports the energy sector by enabling access to specialized equipment that may be difficult or expensive to source domestically.

---

#### **2. Vehicles Received as Gifts from Relatives/Friends Abroad**
- Imported by a **resident of Canada** as a **gift from a relative or friend residing outside Canada**.
- The vehicle must be used for **personal purposes only** and **not for commercial activity**.

> *Rationale*: Facilitates personal connections and family transfers across borders without imposing import duties.

---

#### **3. Non-Resident Vehicles for Summer/Vacation Use**
- Imported by a **non-resident** for **permanent use** at a **summer or vacation residence** in Canada.
- The residence must be **occupied by the non-resident**.

> *Rationale*: Allows seasonal use of vehicles by foreign residents who live in Canada temporarily, avoiding unnecessary duty charges.

---

#### **4. Defence-Related Imports by Citizens of Other Countries**
- Imported by a **citizen of another country** who:
  - Is **not a resident of Canada**.
  - Is **employed in a defence establishment of the government of their home country** in Canada, or
  - Is on **official military service in Canada**.

> *Rationale*: Supports international defence cooperation and military operations in Canada.

---

#### **5. Temporary Importations Under Existing Rules**
- Vehicles imported under the **Non-residentsâ€™ Temporary Importation of Baggage and Conveyances Regulations**.

> *Rationale*: Aligns with temporary entry provisions for personal or official travel-related items.

---

#### **6. Construction and Maintenance Contractors at U.S. Leased Bases**
- Imported by a **contractor** engaged in the construction and maintenance of a **leased base established in Newfoundland by the U.S. Government**.
- The vehicle is used exclusively for construction/maintenance work at these bases.

> *Rationale*: Supports U.S.-Canada defence and infrastructure cooperation in Newfoundland.

---

#### **7. Heavy-Duty Diesel Dump Trucks**
- Must be:
  - Diesel-powered.
  - Self-propelled.
  - Mounted on **rubber-tired wheels or rubber-tired half-tracks**.
  - Have a **rated capacity of at least 7.2 cubic metres (by struck volume)** and **15 tonnes (by payload weight)**.
- Imported for **off-highway use** to transport minerals, excavated materials, or construction materials at:
  - Mines, quarries, gravel or sand pits, or construction sites.

> *Rationale*: Addresses the need for heavy-duty off-road equipment in resource extraction and development industries.

---

#### **8. Former Residents Returning to Canada**
- A **former resident of Canada** returning to resume residence in Canada qualifies if:
  - Was a resident of another country for **at least 12 consecutive months** immediately before returning.
  - Was **continuously absent from Canada for at least six months**, and during that absence:
    - Owned the vehicle for **at least six months**, or
  - Emigrated or was assigned to extended duty abroad and returned due to:
    - Illness, unemployment, educational needs, or other personal reasons of a similar nature.

> *Rationale*: Provides a pathway for returning individuals to bring personal property (including vehicles) without import duties.

---

#### **9. Residents Returning to Canada**
- A **resident of Canada** returning to Canada qualifies if:
  - Was continuously absent for **at least 12 consecutive months**.
  - Was absent for **at least six months**, during which they owned the vehicle for **at least six months**, or
  - Was assigned to extended duty abroad and returned due to illness, unemployment, educational needs, or similar personal reasons.

> *Rationale*: Similar to former residents, this supports reintegration of individuals into Canadian life with their personal property.

---

#### **10. Vehicles at Least 15 Years Old**
- The vehicle must be **not less than 15 years old**.

> *Rationale*: Exempts older, lower-value vehicles from duty, reducing the cost of importing used vehicles that are no longer technologically relevant.

---

#### **11. Vehicles Manufactured Before January 1 of the Model Year**
- Must be:
  - Manufactured **before January 1** of the model year.
  - Imported **after January 1** and **before December 31** of that same year.

> *Rationale*: Allows importation of pre-model-year vehicles (i.e., used vehicles from the previous year) that may have been available in the market before the new model was released.

---

#### **12. Formula or Sports Racing Cars**
- Imported as **formula or sports racing cars**.
- **May not be licensed for use on public highways**.

> *Rationale*: Exempts racing vehicles from general road use restrictions and duties, though they are not permitted for public road use.

---

#### **13. Replacement Vehicles After Damage Abroad**
- Imported by a **resident of Canada** who acquired the vehicle **outside Canada** as a **replacement** for another vehicle that:
  - Was damaged in an accident **outside Canada**.
  - Damage was so severe that **repair was impracticable**.

> *Rationale*: Supports individuals in recovering from accidents abroad without being burdened by import duties.

---

#### **14. Vehicles Covered by Foreign Aircraft Servicing Equipment Remission Order (1992)**
- Vehicles that fall under the **Foreign Aircraft Servicing Equipment Remission Order, 1992** upon importation.

> *Rationale*: Continues existing exemptions for equipment used in foreign aircraft servicing.

---

#### **15. Vehicles Subject to Temporary Entry Remission Orders**
- Vehicles imported **temporarily** under:
  - A **temporary entry remission order**, or
  - Any other order permitting **temporary entry for commercial purposes**.

> *Rationale*: Supports temporary business or operational needs without full importation duties.

---

#### **16. Vehicles Forfeited Under the Controlled Drugs and Substances Act**
- Vehicles forfeited under the **Controlled Drugs and Substances Act**.

> *Rationale*: Allows disposal or re-importation of vehicles tied to illegal activities under legal forfeiture procedures.

---

#### **17. Vehicles Imported Under Tariff Items 9802.00.00 or 9803.00.00**
- Imported temporarily under these tariff items (which typically cover certain types of temporary or specialized goods).

> *Rationale*: Maintains consistency with existing temporary importation frameworks.

---

#### **18. Vehicles Covered by Akwesasne Residents Remission Order**
- Goods (including vehicles) where **sections 4 and 7 of the Akwesasne Residents Remission Order** apply upon importation.

> *Rationale*: Supports Indigenous rights and community-based remission orders for residents of Akwesasne.

---

#### **19. Original Purchaser Imports for Personal Use**
- Imported by the **original purchaser** of the vehicle, provided:
  - The vehicle is imported for **personal use** of the purchaser or their household.
  - It is **not used for business, manufacturing, or as contractor equipment**.
  - The **bill of sale does not state** that the vehicle was used as a **demonstration vehicle** or by a **car rental agency**.

> *Rationale*: Prevents abuse of the exemption by commercial entities; ensures the exemption is reserved for genuine personal use.

---
