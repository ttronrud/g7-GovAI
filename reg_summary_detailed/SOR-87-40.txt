# **Proclamation Establishing the Canadian Hatching Egg Producers (Canadian Hatching Egg Producers Proclamation, 1986)**

---

## **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**

This Proclamation establishes the **Canadian Hatching Egg Producers (CHEP)**, a marketing agency for **broiler hatching eggs and chicks** used in chicken production. It is authorized under **subsection 17(1) of the Farm Products Marketing Agencies Act**, which allows the Governor in Council to create such an agency when:

- The marketing of a farm product in **interprovincial and export trade** is **not regulated** by the Canadian Wheat Board Act or the Canadian Dairy Commission Act; **and**
- A **majority of producers** in Canada are in favour of establishing the agency.

> ‚úÖ The Governor in Council has determined that a majority of producers of **broiler hatching eggs and chicks** support the creation of this agency.

---

## **2. Name and Structure of the Agency**

- **Official Name**: *Canadian Hatching Egg Producers* (CHEP)
- **Type**: A marketing agency established under the *Farm Products Agencies Act* (Act)
- **Membership**: 8 members, appointed as follows:

### **Appointment of Members**
| Category | Appointment Mechanism |
|--------|------------------------|
| **Initial Members** | Existing directors of the *Canadian Broiler Hatching Egg Producers Association* who are from **signatory provinces** (Ontario, Quebec, Manitoba, BC, Saskatchewan, Alberta). These members serve until the end of the first fiscal year of the agency. |
| **Provincial Representatives** | Each **signatory province** appoints **one member** from among its Commodity Board directors who are actively involved in broiler hatching egg production. The hatchery of the member must be licensed by the provincial Commodity Board or covered by a Quebec-accredited hatchery association. |
| **Federation Representatives** | If there are **4 or fewer** provincial members: Canadian Hatchery Federation appoints **1 member**.<br>If there are **5 or more**: Federation appoints **2 members**:<br> - One representing the **region west of the Ontario-Manitoba border**<br> - One representing the **region east of the Ontario-Manitoba border** |

> üîç **Eligibility Requirements**:
> - Must be a **resident of Canada**
> - Must be an **active member of a hatchery staff**
> - Must be from a **licensed hatchery in a signatory province**
> - Must **not be employed by the same corporate entity or family** as another member

### **Temporary Substitute Members**
- Appointed by a Commodity Board or the Canadian Hatchery Federation to replace a member who is absent, unable to act, or elected Chairperson.
- Serve at the **discretion of the appointing body** (i.e., "during pleasure").

## **3. Leadership and Governance**

- The members **elect a Chairman and Vice-Chairman** at their first meeting and annually at the first meeting after each annual general meeting.
- The Chair and Vice-Chair serve until their successors are elected.

---

## **4. Head Office Location**

- The **head office** of CHEP is located in the **National Capital Region**, as defined in the *National Capital Act* (Schedule).

## **5. Scope of Authority: Farm Products Covered**

CHEP has authority over:

### ‚úÖ **Broiler Hatching Eggs and Chicks** produced for chicken production.

#### **Marketing Coverage**:
| Type | Scope |
|------|-------|
| **Signatory Provinces** (Ontario, Quebec, Manitoba, BC, Saskatchewan, Alberta) | Broiler hatching eggs and chicks produced **within** these provinces and marketed in **interprovincial or export trade** |
| **Non-Signatory Provinces** (NS, NB, PEI, NL, Yukon, NWT, Nunavut) | Broiler hatching eggs and chicks produced **within** these provinces and **shipped into** signatory provinces for **interprovincial trade only** (not for export) |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important Limitation**: CHEP **does not** have authority to exercise powers under **Section 22** of the Farm Products Agencies Act **except** for:
> - Implementing a **quota system** for marketing chicks from non-signatory provinces into signatory provinces
> - Establishing a **licensing system** for marketing chicks
> - Requiring marketers to **deduct and remit levies or charges** to CHEP

## **6. Quota Systems (Core Regulatory Tool)**

CHEP may establish **quota systems** to control the volume of broiler hatching eggs and chicks marketed in interprovincial or export trade.

### **A. Quota System for Signatory Provinces**
- CHEP allocates quotas to **producers** in signatory provinces.
- Quotas are set such that:
  - The **total number of broiler hatching eggs** authorized for marketing (via quotas and intraprovincial sales) **plus** anticipated non-quota sales
  - **Equals the total number** of eggs set out in a provincial table (attached in the schedule).
- **No increase or decrease** in total marketing volume (quota + non-quota) is allowed unless the Agency follows the **allocation system** in **Schedule B** of the *Federal-Provincial Agreement* (P.C. 1986-2653).

### **B. Quota System for Non-Signatory Provinces**
- CHEP allocates quotas to individuals in non-signatory provinces who market eggs into signatory provinces (interprovincial only).
- The total number of eggs authorized under quotas **must not exceed** the number set in the provincial table.
- **Proportional Adjustment Rule**:
  - If CHEP increases or decreases the **total number of chicks** allowed to be marketed from a non-signatory province, it must do so by:
    - The **same percentage** as the change in broiler hatching eggs for that province, **or**
    - The **average percentage** change across all provinces in the area (if multiple provinces are involved)

> üìå This ensures that quota changes in chicks are **proportionally aligned** with changes in egg production, preventing market distortion.

## **7. Licensing System**

CHEP may establish a **licensing system** for persons involved in marketing broiler hatching eggs or chicks.

### **Scope**:
- **Signatory Provinces**: Licensing applies to marketing **in interprovincial or export trade**
- **Non-Signatory Provinces**: Licensing applies to marketing **into signatory provinces** (interprovincial only, not export)

### **Licensing Requirements**:
- Must include:
  - Compliance with CHEP orders, regulations, and the National Farm Products Marketing Council‚Äôs rules
  - Mandatory **collection and reporting of marketing data**
  - Conditions for **suspension or cancellation** of licences
- CHEP determines:
  - Criteria for granting, refusing, or renewing licences
  - Fees associated with each licence or class of licence

---

## **8. Levies and Charges**

CHEP may impose **levies or charges** on marketers of broiler hatching eggs.

### **Types of Charges**:
| Charge Type | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **General Levies** | Imposed on all marketers to cover **administrative and marketing costs** of the agency. Levels are set to ensure sufficient funding for annual operations. |
| **Excess Quota Charges** | Imposed on marketers who sell **more than their authorized quota**. This is a penalty for over-marketing. |

> üí° The levies are designed to:
> - Fund agency operations (e.g., administration, pricing, compliance)
> - Prevent over-supply and market instability
> - Ensure financial sustainability

> üìù CHEP may create **reserves** and account for **essential expenses** when estimating costs.

---

## **9. Pricing Authority**

CHEP may establish a **pricing system** to determine the price at which broiler hatching eggs may be marketed.

- Applies to:
  - Any **variety, class, or grade** of broiler hatching eggs
  - In **signatory provinces**, for marketing in **interprovincial or export trade**

> üîç This gives CHEP significant control over market prices to stabilize supply and prevent price volatility.

---

## **10. Prohibitions and Limitations**

- CHEP **cannot**:
  - Increase or decrease the **total number of broiler hatching eggs** authorized for marketing (quota + non-quota) beyond the levels set in the provincial tables **unless** it follows the allocation rules in Schedule B of the Federal-Provincial Agreement.
  - Alter the total number of chicks marketed from non-signatory provinces **without proportional alignment** with egg production changes.

---

## **11. Legal Citation and Enforcement**

- This Proclamation and its schedule are to be cited as:  
  **Canadian Hatching Egg Producers Proclamation**

- All persons (including producers, marketers, and government bodies) are **required to take notice** and **govern themselves accordingly**.

- The Proclamation is issued by the **Governor in Council**, on the advice of the **Privy Council**, and affixed with the **Great Seal of Canada**.
