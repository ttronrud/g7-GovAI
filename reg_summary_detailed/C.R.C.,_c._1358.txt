## 4. KINDS OF SUPERANNUATION OR PENSION BENEFITS

Two types of benefits are recognized:

### a) Benefit under Judges Act
- Paid from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund** or other government funds.
- Related to service period.
- Paid in **instalments during lifetime and after** (if provided).

### b) Benefit from Boards, Commissions, or Corporations (Schedule A)
- Paid from **non-Consolidated Revenue Fund** accounts.
- Must be:
  - Related to service period,
  - Paid in instalments during lifetime and after.

> These benefits are **not** considered "pensionable" under the Superannuation Act if they are not tied to service or paid from specific funds.

## 6. DEFAULT AND RECOVERY MECHANISMS

### a) Default on Installment
If a contributor misses a payment:
- Minister **demands payment immediately** (except during leave without pay).
- Amount in default, **with interest at 4% per annum**, becomes due.

#### Payment Options:
- **Lump sum immediately**, or  
- **Monthly instalments** for:
  - The **life of the contributor**, or  
  - The **remainder of the original payment period** (whichever is shorter).

> If contributor does **not respond within 30 days**, defaults to the **monthly instalment option**.

---

### b) Alternative Payment Plan (After Default)
A contributor may, before expiry of 30-day window:
- Pay **all defaults** with interest (4% per annum from due date).
- Over a period starting:
  - 30 days after the end of the period when contributions were paid without default, or  
  - The last day of the month when the election is made â€” whichever is **later**.

> If contributor **dies before full payment**, unpaid amounts with interest continue to be payable into the Superannuation Account.

---

### c) Error in Payment
If an amount is paid in error:
- Minister **immediately demands repayment** of the erroneous amount.

---

### d) Response to Demand
A person receiving a demand must:
- Pay the amount in **one lump sum**, or  
- Arrange to pay in **monthly instalments** within 30 days.
