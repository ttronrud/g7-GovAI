**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

This regulation is a **Government Order** issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Justice**, under the authority granted in **subsection 2(5)c of the Divorce Act**. The purpose of the Order is to formally designate the **Province of Manitoba** as a jurisdiction that will be governed by specific, comprehensive child support guidelines when determining child support obligations under the federal **Divorce Act**.

---

### **1. Legal Context and Purpose**

- **Section 26.1a of the Divorce Act** (as referenced in the preamble) deals with the determination of child support and requires the use of "applicable guidelines" to ensure consistency and fairness in child support calculations across Canada.
- The **Divorce Act** (specifically subsection 2(1)) defines the term *"applicable guidelines"* to refer to the guidelines established by a province or territory for determining child support.
- This Order serves to **formally designate Manitoba** as the jurisdiction whose guidelines will be recognized and applied under the federal Divorce Act when child support is being determined in cases involving Manitoba residents or where Manitoba law is relevant.

---

### **2. Key Provisions of the Order**

#### **(a) Designation of Manitoba**
- The **Province of Manitoba** is officially designated as the jurisdiction whose guidelines apply under the definition of *"applicable guidelines"* in **subsection 2(1) of the Divorce Act**.
- This designation means that when a court or family dispute resolution body is determining child support under the federal Divorce Act, and one of the parties resides in or has a connection to Manitoba, the **Manitoba Child Support Guidelines** will be used as the governing framework.

#### **(b) Applicable Guidelines**
- For the purposes of **subsection 2(5)** of the Divorce Act, the **Child Support Guidelines Regulation, Man. Reg. 58/98** is the law that constitutes the "comprehensive guidelines" for Manitoba.
- This regulation provides a detailed, objective, and formula-based method for calculating child support payments based on factors such as:
  - The income of both parents
  - The number of children
  - The custody arrangement (primary residence)
  - The cost of living and other relevant economic factors
- The regulation is designed to ensure fairness, consistency, and transparency in child support calculations.

#### **(c) Effective Date**
- The Order comes into force on **June 1, 1998**.
- This date marks the beginning of the formal application of the Manitoba Child Support Guidelines Regulation (Man. Reg. 58/98) as the authoritative source for child support determinations under the federal Divorce Act in Manitoba.

---

### **3. Significance and Implications**

- **Federal-Province Coordination**: This Order reflects the federal government’s recognition of provincial child support systems and their integration into federal family law. It allows for a harmonized approach to child support across Canada while respecting provincial jurisdiction and policy.
- **Consistency and Fairness**: By adopting Manitoba’s comprehensive guidelines, the federal framework ensures that child support calculations in Manitoba are consistent with those in other provinces and territories, reducing disparities and legal uncertainty.
- **Legal Authority**: The designation gives the Manitoba Child Support Guidelines Regulation (Reg. 58/98) formal legal standing under the Divorce Act, meaning it is binding in federal child support cases involving Manitoba residents or where Manitoba law applies.

---

### **4. Background and Legal Basis**

- The Order is made under **subsection 2(5)c of the Divorce Act**, which empowers the Governor General in Council to designate a province or territory as having "applicable guidelines" for child support.
- The reference to **S.C. 1997, c. 1, s. 11** and **R.S., c. 3 (2nd Supp.)** indicates the legislative framework and amendments that support the authority of this Order.
- The regulation is part of a broader national effort to standardize child support calculations across Canada, with each province having its own guidelines that are recognized under federal law.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation formally establishes the **Manitoba Child Support Guidelines Regulation (Man. Reg. 58/98)** as the official, federally recognized set of rules for determining child support in Manitoba under the **Divorce Act**. It ensures that Manitoba’s well-developed and comprehensive child support framework is integrated into the national child support system, promoting fairness, consistency, and predictability in family law proceedings involving child support.

**Effective Date**: June 1, 1998  
**Applicable Law**: Child Support Guidelines Regulation, Man. Reg. 58/98  
**Jurisdiction**: Province of Manitoba  
**Legal Authority**: Divorce Act, subsection 2(5)c and 2(1)  

This Order remains a foundational element in Manitoba’s child support regime and continues to influence child support decisions in both federal and provincial family courts.
