### ðŸ”¹ **Overview and Legal Basis**
These regulations establish the **standards for pension benefits** in private pension plans in Canada. They were enacted by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, pursuant to:
- Subsections **4(6)** and **9(6)**, and
- **Section 39** of the *Pension Benefits Standards Act, 1985*.

The regulations are officially titled:
> **Pension Benefits Standards Regulations, 1985**  
> (May be cited as the *Pension Benefits Standards Regulations, 1985*)

They came into force on **January 1, 1987**, and form a key part of the regulatory framework governing the **valuation, funding, and benefit structure** of pension plans in Canada.

## ðŸ” Key Definitions and Concepts

The regulations define numerous terms essential to understanding pension plan valuation, governance, and financial obligations.

---

### 1. **Accepted Actuarial Practice**
- Refers to the **standards of actuarial practice** as outlined in **paragraph 9(2)(b)** of the *Pension Benefits Standards Act*.
- May be **modified or specified** by the **Superintendent of Financial Institutions** under that paragraph.
- Ensures consistency and fairness in actuarial valuations across plans.

---

### 2. **Accountant**
- A person **authorized to act as an accountant** under provincial laws.
- Used in the context of financial reporting and audits related to pension plans.

---

### 3. **Act**
- Refers to the *Pension Benefits Standards Act, 1985* (the "Act").
- The primary legislative foundation for these regulations.

---

### 4. **Actuarial Report**
- A formal report filed with the **Superintendent** under:
  - Subsection **9.01(5)** or **12(2)** of the Act, or
  - A copy provided under **subsection 9.01(6)**.
- Required for valuations and determining plan liabilities and assets.

- Used to assess whether a plan has sufficient assets to meet its defined benefit obligations upon termination.

---

### 6. **Average Solvency Ratio**
- Determined using **subsections 9(8) to (11)** of the Act.
- Reflects the ratio of solvency assets to liabilities over time, based on recent actuarial reports.

---

### 7. **Book Value**
- The **cost of acquisition** of an asset, including all **direct acquisition costs**.
- Used in asset valuation for internal accounting purposes.

---

### 8. **Bridging Benefit**
- A **temporary periodic payment** provided to a **former member** after retirement.
- Purpose: To **supplement** the memberâ€™s pension until:
  - They become eligible for **Old Age Security (OAS)**, or
  - They begin receiving **retirement benefits** under the **Canada Pension Plan (CPP)** or **Quebec Pension Plan (QPP)**.
- Ensures continuity of income during transition periods.

---

### 9. **Canadian Resource Property**
- Defined as per **paragraph 66(15)(c) of the Income Tax Act**.
- Includes mineral rights and other natural resource assets in Canada.

---

### 10. **Deferred Life Annuity**
- A life annuity that:
  - Begins **no earlier than one year after purchase**,
  - Provides **equal periodic payments** or payments adjusted by reference to:
    - OAS pension,
    - CPP or provincial pension,
    - Consumer Price Index (CPI),
    - Value of assets in a **segregated fund**,
  - Issued by a **Canadian life insurance company** authorized to conduct life insurance business.

> *Contrast with Immediate Life Annuity (see below).*

---

### 11. **Immediate Life Annuity**
- Begins **within one year of purchase**.
- Same payment structure and adjustment mechanisms as deferred annuities.
- Also issued by a **Canadian life insurance company**.

> These annuities are common in retirement income solutions.

---

### 12. **Insured Plan**
- A plan where **all benefits are paid via annuities or insurance contracts** issued by a Canadian life insurer.
- The insurer is **obligated to pay all benefits** as set out in the plan.
- Implies a **higher level of risk transfer** to the insurer.

---

### 13. **Investment Fund**
- A fund established by a **corporation, limited partnership, or trust**.
- Purpose: To **invest pooled money** from multiple investors.
- Shares or units are allocated **proportionally** to each investorâ€™s interest.

---

### 14. **Letter of Credit**
- A financial instrument that meets the requirements under **subsection 9.1(5)** of the Act.
- Used as a **funding mechanism** for pension liabilities.

---

### 15. **Life Income Fund**
- A **registered retirement income fund (RRIF)** under the Income Tax Act that meets specific requirements in **section 20.1**.
- Designed to provide **stable, lifelong income** to retirees.

---

### 16. **Locked-in Registered Retirement Savings Plan (LRP)**
- A **registered retirement savings plan (RRSP)** that meets the requirements in **section 20**.
- Funds are **locked in** and cannot be withdrawn without specific conditions (e.g., retirement, disability).
- Prevents early access and ensures long-term savings.

---

### 17. **Marketplace**
- Includes:
  - Exchanges,
  - Quotation and trade-reporting systems,
  - Any entity that:
    - Brings together buyers and sellers of securities or derivatives,
    - Aggregates orders from multiple parties,
    - Uses **established, non-discretionary methods** for trade execution.
- Defines the **market infrastructure** for investment activities.

---

### 18. **Market Value**
- The **price** at which an asset would be bought or sold in an **open market**.
- Assumed to be between **prudent, knowledgeable, and willing parties**.
- Used in valuation for assets not held in fixed or book value form.

---

### 19. **Member Choice Account**
- An account under a plan (as defined in **subsection 8(4.2)** of the Act) where members can make **investment choices**.
- Allows **greater control** over how their pension funds are invested.
- Common in modern pension plans with investment flexibility.

---

### 20. **Normal Cost**
- The cost of benefits accruing during a plan year, based on a **going concern valuation**.
- Excludes special payments.
- Includes:
  - A **provision for adverse deviations**:
    - At least **5%** in **negotiated contribution plans**,
    - Otherwise, a provision for adverse deviations if any.
- Reflects the **expected cost** of benefits under normal market conditions.

> *Note: "Plan year" and "simplified pension plan" were repealed in 2011 and 2015.*

---

### 21. **Plan**
- Refers to a **pension plan** under the Act.
- Includes defined benefit, defined contribution, and other types.

---

### 22. **Valuation Dates**
- **Prior valuation date**: one year before the current valuation date.
- **Prior second valuation date**: two years before the current valuation date.
- Used for **trend analysis** and **comparative valuation** over time.

---

### 23. **PRPP (Pooled Registered Pension Plan)**
- A plan registered under **section 12 of the Pooled Registered Pension Plans Act (PRPP Act)**.
- Allows pooling of contributions from multiple participants into a single fund.

---

### 24. **Restricted Life Income Fund**
- A **registered retirement income fund (RRIF)** meeting requirements in **section 20.3**.
- More restrictive than standard RRIFs; may have **limited access** or **specific withdrawal rules**.

---

### 25. **Restricted Locked-in Savings Plan**
- A **locked-in RRSP** meeting requirements in **section 20.2**.
- Restricts access to funds and limits withdrawal options.

---

### 26. **Segregated Fund**
- A fund established by a **corporation authorized to operate** such funds.
- Assets are held **exclusively** for the benefit of one or more pension plans.
- Provides **isolation and protection** of plan assets from general corporate liabilities.
