## ğŸ” Section 1: Criteria for Determining Whether a Pardon Would Bring Justice into Disrepute

Under **paragraph 4.1(3)(d)** of the *Criminal Records Act*, the **Pardon Board** must assess whether granting a pardon would bring the administration of justice into disrepute. The Board may consider the following factors:

### 1. **Threat to Safety or Security of Canada**
- If the offence posed a threat to national security or public safety.

### 2. **Offence Against the Administration of Law and Justice**
- Must be an offence prosecuted by **indictment** (i.e., a serious criminal charge) under **Part IV of the Criminal Code** (e.g., fraud, corruption, obstruction of justice).

### 3. **Serious Personal Injury Offence**
- As defined in **section 752 of the Criminal Code**, such as offences involving physical harm, assault, or injury resulting in death or long-term disability.

### 4. **Offence Motivated by Bias, Prejudice, or Hate**
- Based on race, national or ethnic origin, language, colour, religion, sex, age, mental or physical disability, sexual orientation, or similar grounds â€” particularly relevant in hate crimes.

### 5. **Service Offences**
- Specifically defined in:
  - **Sections 73 to 82 of the National Defence Act** (e.g., misconduct by military personnel), where the applicant received a **life sentence**.
  - **Section 130 of the National Defence Act** (offences involving misuse of authority or duty), **if** it also falls under any of the following:
    - (a) to (d) or (f) to (h) of this section (i.e., includes bias, violence, harm to children/vulnerable persons, fraud, etc.)

### 6. **Serious Physical or Psychological Injury to Another Person**
- Offences that caused significant harm to individuals, whether physical or mental.

### 7. **Fraudulent Transactions Relating to Contracts and Trade (Part X of the Criminal Code)**
- Applies if **any** of the following conditions are met:
  - The value of the fraud exceeds **$1 million**;
  - The fraud adversely affected or threatened the **stability of the Canadian economy, financial system, or investor confidence**;
  - The fraud involved a **large number of victims**;
  - The offender exploited their **high public reputation**;
  - The offence involved **cruelty or harm to children or vulnerable persons**;
  - The applicant has a **criminal record outside Canada** for an offence that, if committed in Canada, would have been prosecuted by indictment;
  - The applicant demonstrates a **pattern of criminal activity** (as defined in subsections 462.37(2.04) and (2.05) of the Criminal Code) or a **pattern of increasing gravity** of offences.

> This section is particularly significant as it establishes **strict thresholds** for pardons in cases involving financial fraud, economic harm, or systemic harm â€” even if a pardon is granted.

### âœ… Understanding of Consequences of Consent

3. The **police force or body will disclose** the information to the applicant.
4. **With written consent**, the information may also be disclosed to the **organization or person who requested the verification**.

> This ensures that **pardon status does not erase the record** for the purposes of background checks in sensitive roles.
