### üîπ **Overview**
These regulations establish detailed rules governing **appointment priorities** in the Canadian public service, particularly in cases involving:
- Military service
- Disability
- Death of a service member
- Spousal or common-law partner relocation
- Incumbent-based appointments
- Employment equity

They are designed to ensure **fairness, equity, and priority access** to positions for individuals who have faced significant personal or professional disruptions due to service, illness, or family circumstances ‚Äî while also aligning with broader employment equity and human rights principles.

> **Legal Authority**: Section 22 of the *Public Service Employment Act*

### 1. **Incumbent-Based Appointments & Acting Appointments ‚Äì No Priority Entitlement**

> **Section**: Entitlement to priority under sections 5‚Äì10 (e.g., seniority, service, disability) **does not apply** to:
- Incumbent-based appointments
- Acting appointments
- Appointments made under employment equity programs for members of designated groups (e.g., women, Indigenous peoples, persons with disabilities), **unless** the person entitled under those provisions is also a member of a designated group in the program.

> **Purpose**: Ensures that internal promotions and temporary assignments do not trigger traditional priority entitlements that may distort equity or seniority-based selection.

---

### 2. **Employment Equity Appointments ‚Äì Exception to Priority Rules**

> A member of a designated group (e.g., women, Indigenous peoples, persons with disabilities) may be appointed **without regard to standard priority entitlements** under sections 39.1, 40, 41(1), and (4) **if**:
- The appointment is made under an approved employment equity program.
- The person entitled to priority under those sections is **also** a member of a designated group **to which the program applies**.

> **Purpose**: Supports targeted recruitment and inclusion in underrepresented groups while maintaining fairness through targeted programs.

---

### 3. **Priority for Veterans (Canadian Armed Forces)**

> Individuals released from the **Canadian Forces** due to medical reasons attributable to service are entitled to **priority appointment** in any public service position where they meet essential qualifications (paragraph 30(2)(a) of the Act).

#### Conditions for Priority:
- The person must **request the priority within five years** of release.
- The person must **not be employed** in the public service for an indeterminate period at the time of request.
- Within five years of release, they must be certified by a competent authority as ready to return to work on a specified day.
- That day must fall within five years of release.

#### Entitlement Period:
- Begins on the **later of**:
  - The day they are certified as ready to return to work
  - The day the Minister of Veterans Affairs determines they were released due to service-related illness
- Ends on the **earliest** of:
  - Fifth anniversary of the start date
  - Day they are appointed to an indeterminate position
  - Day they decline an appointment without good and sufficient reason
  - If employed on a **specified term**, ends when:
    - Period is converted to indeterminate under subsection 59(1) of the Act, **or**
    - They request continuation of a specified term despite 59(1)

> **Note**: Applies to:
> - Regular Force
> - Reserve Force
> - Special Force

> **Class B/C Reserve Service**: Defined under Queen‚Äôs Regulations and Orders for the Canadian Forces.

### 4. **Priority for RCMP Members Discharged Due to Medical Reasons**

> A member of the **Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)** or **Reserve of the RCMP** (if medical reasons are service-related) is entitled to priority appointment.

#### Conditions:
- Request made within five years of discharge
- Certification within five years of readiness to return to work
- Readiness date within five years of discharge

#### Entitlement Period:
- Begins on the day certified as ready to return to work
- Ends on the earliest of:
  - Fifth anniversary of start
  - Appointment to indeterminate position
  - Decline of appointment without good reason
  - If on a specified term: conversion to indeterminate or request to maintain specified term

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Repealed (SOR/2015-115, s. 6)**: This provision was **repealed** in 2015.

### 5. **Priority for Spouses/Common-Law Partners of Deceased Service Members**

> If a person dies in the line of duty (employee, regular/reserve/special force, RCMP, or RCMP Reserve), their **spouse or common-law partner** is entitled to priority in **advertised external appointments** where they meet essential qualifications.

#### Conditions for Priority:
- Spouse/common-law partner is **not employed** in the public service for an indeterminate period at time of request
- Qualifies under a federally or provincially legislated compensation plan due to death in service
- Makes request **within five years** of qualifying for compensation

#### Special Provision (Post-2020):
> If the spouse/common-law partner:
> - Qualified for compensation between **April 1, 2020 and March 31, 2023**
> - Did **not** make a request within the time limit as it read on March 31, 2025
> - Then must make a request **by April 1, 2028**

#### Entitlement Period:
- Begins on the day of request
- Ends on the earliest of:
  - Second anniversary of request
  - Appointment to an indeterminate position
  - Decline of appointment without good reason
  - If on a specified term: conversion to indeterminate or request to maintain specified term

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Repealed (SOR/2015-115, s. 6)**: This section was **repealed** in 2015.

### 6. **Priority for Employees on Spousal Relocation Leave**

> An employee on a leave of absence due to **spouse or common-law partner relocation** (not entitled under subsection 41(1)) is entitled to **priority appointment** in any public service position where they meet essential qualifications.

#### Conditions:
- Leave is granted due to relocation of spouse/common-law partner
- Not entitled to appointment under section 41(1)

#### Entitlement Period:
- Begins on the start of the leave
- Ends on the earliest of:
  - End of leave period
  - Appointment to an indeterminate position
  - Decline of appointment without good reason
  - If on a specified term: conversion to indeterminate or request to maintain specified term

> Purpose: Supports work-life balance and family stability in public service appointments.

---
