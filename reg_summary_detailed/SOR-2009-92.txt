### **Detailed Summary of the Regulations Implementing the United Nations Resolutions on Somalia**

---

#### **1. Introduction and Legal Basis**

These regulations are a **domestic implementation** of United Nations Security Council (UNSC) resolutions concerning Somalia, specifically designed to enforce the measures outlined in key resolutions related to **arms control, financial restrictions, and humanitarian exemptions**.

- The regulations were made by **Her Excellency the Governor in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**, under **Section 2 of the United Nations Act**.
- The authority to enact these rules stems from the requirement to **effectively apply** measures established in various UNSC resolutions, particularly those aimed at preventing the proliferation of weapons, supporting stability in Somalia, and curbing support for armed groups.

---

#### **2. Key UN Resolutions Referenced**

The regulations reference a series of critical UNSC resolutions, including:

| Resolution | Date | Key Focus |
|---------|------|---------|
| **733 (1992)** | Jan 23, 1992 | Initial response to the Somali crisis; calls for peacekeeping and arms control. |
| **751 (1992)** | Apr 24, 1992 | Establishes a Committee of the Security Council and outlines measures to prevent support for armed factions. |
| **1356 (2001)** | Jun 19, 2001 | Focuses on the prohibition of arms and military activities in Somalia. |
| **1425 (2002)** | Jul 22, 2002 | Reinforces restrictions on arms and military assistance. |
| **1725 (2006)** | Dec 6, 2006 | Addresses support for armed groups and strengthens financial controls. |
| **1744 (2007)** | Feb 20, 2007 | Establishes a framework for monitoring and sanctions. |
| **1772 (2007)** | Aug 20, 2007 | Further strengthens prohibitions on arms and military support. |
| **1844 (2008)** | Nov 20, 2008 | Introduces designation of individuals and entities involved in armed conflict; establishes specific prohibitions on dealing with designated persons. |
| **2111 (2013)** | Jul 24, 2013 | Authorizes limited military and technical assistance under strict conditions. |

> âš ï¸ **Important Note**: Several of these resolutions have been **repealed or replaced** by later amendments (e.g., SOR/2020-120, s.1), particularly **Security Council Resolutions 733, 751, 1356, 1425, 1744, 1772, and 2036**, which are now **no longer applicable** under the current version of these regulations. Only **Resolution 1844 (2008)** and **Resolution 2111 (2013)** remain central to the current legal framework.

#### **3. Definitions and Key Terms**

The following definitions clarify the scope and application of the regulations:

- **AMISOM**: The African Union Mission in Somalia â€” a peacekeeping force deployed to support stability.
- **Arms and related material**: Includes weapons, ammunition, military vehicles, paramilitary equipment, and spare parts.
- **Canadian**: A citizen of Canada or an entity established under Canadian or provincial law.
- **Committee of the Security Council**: The body established under Resolution 751 to oversee implementation and designate individuals/entities.
- **Designated person under paragraph 3 or 8**: Individuals or entities designated by the Committee of the Security Council under paragraphs 3 and 8 of Resolution 1844. These persons are subject to strict financial and trade restrictions.
- **Entity**: Includes corporations, trusts, partnerships, funds, unincorporated associations, and foreign states.
- **Military activities**: Any actions by state or non-state armed forces, armed mercenaries, or activities that support the operational capacity of armed groups.
- **Official**: A person employed, engaged, or holding a position of responsibility in the federal or provincial government of Canada.
- **Person**: An individual or entity.
- **Property**: Any asset, including financial or physical assets.
- **Security Council**: The main decision-making body of the United Nations.
- **Somalia**: The Federal Republic of Somalia, including its political subdivisions, government bodies, and agencies.
- **Technical assistance**: Training, consulting, instruction, know-how transfer, or advice related to technology or operations.
- **Working day**: A day that is not a Saturday or a public holiday.

#### **4. Prohibited Activities**

The core prohibitions in these regulations are designed to **prevent Canada from supporting or enabling armed conflict or illicit activities in Somalia**.

##### **(a) Prohibition on Dealing with Designated Persons (Paragraphs 3 & 8)**

Any person in Canada or a Canadian outside Canada is **prohibited from**:

- **Dealing in property** (directly or indirectly) that is owned, held, or controlled by:
  - A designated person under paragraph 3 of Resolution 1844;
  - A designated person under paragraph 8 of Resolution 1844.

- **Entering into or facilitating** transactions related to such dealings.

- **Providing or acquiring financial or related services** for such dealings.

- **Making available property** to or for the benefit of:
  - A designated person under paragraph 3;
  - A designated person under paragraph 8 (or someone acting on their behalf).

> âœ… **Exception**: Payments of **interest or earnings** that arose before the designation of the person are **not prohibited**, provided the amount becomes subject to the prohibition after the designation.

##### **(b) Prohibition on Arms and Related Material**

It is prohibited to:

- **Export, sell, supply, or transfer** arms and related material (anywhere in the world) to:
  - Somalia or any person in Somalia;
  - A designated person under paragraph 8.

- **Provide technical assistance or financial assistance** related to the sale, supply, transfer, manufacture, maintenance, or use of arms and related material to:
  - Somalia or any person in Somalia;
  - A designated person under paragraph 8.

##### **(c) Prohibition on Military Activities**

It is prohibited to provide **technical assistance or financial assistance** to:

- Somalia or any person in Somalia;
- A designated person under paragraph 8;

**for any military activity**, including training, logistics, or operational support.

> ðŸš« This includes support for armed groups, mercenaries, or any activity that strengthens military capabilities.

##### **(d) Prohibition on Transport of Arms**

Canadian vessels (as defined in the *Canada Shipping Act, 2001*) or aircraft registered in Canada are **prohibited from carrying** arms and related material destined for:

- Somalia or any person in Somalia;
- A designated person under paragraph 8.

> âœ… **Exception**: Arms carried for **defensive purposes** on a Canadian vessel are exempt.

##### **(e) Prohibition on Import of Charcoal**

Any person or Canadian is **prohibited from importing** charcoal from Somalia or from any person in Somalia.

> âš ï¸ This is likely intended to prevent illicit trade or exploitation of natural resources by armed groups.

#### **5. Exemptions and Exceptions**

Several critical **exemptions** are provided to ensure humanitarian and legitimate operational needs are not unduly restricted.

##### **(a) Humanitarian Assistance**

The prohibitions **do not apply** to:

- Activities necessary to ensure the **timely delivery of humanitarian assistance**, including:
  - The United Nations (including its programs, funds, agencies, and specialized bodies);
  - International organizations;
  - Humanitarian organizations with observer status under the UN General Assembly;
  - Bilaterally or multilaterally funded NGOs participating in UN humanitarian response plans;
  - Any other entity authorized by a UN Security Council committee.

> âœ… This ensures that Canada does not block life-saving aid during crises.

##### **(b) Limited Military and Technical Assistance**

Certain activities are exempted under specific conditions:

| Exemption | Conditions |
|--------|----------|
| **AMISOM activities** | Any activity supporting AMISOM (African Union Mission in Somalia) is exempt. |
| **Strategic partners of AMISOM** | Only if operating within the context of Security Council Resolution 2111. |
| **UN personnel (e.g., UNSOM)** | Activities of UN personnel, media, and humanitarian workers. |
| **EU Training Mission (EUTM)** | Activities related to EUTM. |
| **Non-lethal military equipment** | If intended solely for humanitarian or protective use and **noted to the Committee of the Security Council 5 working days in advance**. |
| **Protective clothing** | Flak jackets, helmets, temporarily exported to Somalia for personal use by UN, media, or humanitarian staff. |
| **Security sector development** | If intended solely to develop Somaliaâ€™s security sector and **noted 5 working days in advance** by the Committee. |
| **Anti-piracy operations** | Activities supporting Member States or regional organizations in combating piracy off Somalia
