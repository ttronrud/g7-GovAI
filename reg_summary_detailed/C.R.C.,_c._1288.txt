**Detailed Summary of the *Posting Abroad of Letter-Post Items Regulations***

---

### **1. Regulation Title and Citation**
The regulation is formally titled:  
**"Posting Abroad of Letter-Post Items Regulations"**  
It may be cited as such and serves to govern the handling of letter-post items (mail) that are sent *outside of Canada* to an addressee *in Canada* under specific conditions involving volume or purpose.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

#### **(a) "Large Quantities" (Grande quantitÃ©)**
- Refers to the volume of letter-post items posted in a single day or over a month.
- **Thresholds:**
  - **More than 50 letter-post items** posted on **any one day** by or on behalf of a person.
  - **More than 300 letter-post items** posted in **any one month** by or on behalf of the same person.
- This threshold is designed to identify bulk mailing activities that may be used to exploit postal rate differences.

#### **(b) "Letter-Post Item"**
- Defined consistently with the **International Letter-Post Items Regulations**.
- Typically refers to standard postal items such as letters, printed cards, envelopes, or wrappers that are sent via postal services.
- Excludes other forms of mail (e.g., parcels, registered mail, or non-letter items).

#### **(c) "Person"**
- Includes any of the following:
  - Individual
  - Sole proprietorship
  - Partnership
  - Incorporated entity (corporation or legal business structure)
- The regulation applies to all such entities or individuals when they engage in posting letter-post items abroad.

---

### **3. Applicable Situation**

The regulation applies when:
- A **letter-post item** is posted **in a country other than Canada**.
- The item is sent **to an addressee in Canada**.
- The sender is **a person resident in Canada** (i.e., the sender has a Canadian address or legal presence).
- The posting qualifies as **"large quantities"** (i.e., exceeds the thresholds defined above).

---

### **4. Regulatory Actions When Conditions Are Met**

If the above conditions are met, the **Corporation** (referring to Canada Post or the Canadian postal service) has two possible responses:

#### **Option 1: Return the Items to the Country of Origin**
- If the Corporation has **reasonable grounds to believe** that:
  - The **sole purpose** of posting the items abroad is to **profit from lower postage rates** in the foreign country,  
    *(i.e., the sender is using foreign postal rates to reduce costs and gain a financial advantage)*,
- Then the Corporation may **return the letter-post items to the country of origin** (where they were sent from).

#### **Option 2: Require Payment of Full Canadian Postage**
- If the items are **printed papers** (such as cards, envelopes, or wrappers) that:
  - Are **addressed for return to an address in Canada**,  
    *(indicating a potential return or refund mechanism)*,
- Then the Corporation may **require the sender to pay full postage**.
- This postage must be calculated at the **rate that would have applied if the items had been posted directly in Canada**.

---

### **5. Determining the "Post Office of Posting" for Postage Calculation**

For the purpose of calculating the required Canadian postage under paragraph (1)(e):

- The **Canadian post office** where the letter-post items are **received from the foreign country** is deemed to be the **"post office of posting"**.
- This ensures consistency in the application of rates and prevents manipulation of the calculation process.

> Example: If a Canadian sender mails 100 letters to Canada via a foreign postal service, and those letters are later received by a Canadian post office in Toronto, then the Toronto post office is treated as the "post office of posting" for calculating Canadian postage.

---

### **6. Enforcement and Consequences of Non-Compliance**

If the sender **refuses to pay** the required Canadian postage:
- The Corporation will treat the letter-post items as **undeliverable mail**.
- This action is governed by the **Undeliverable and Redirected Mail Regulations**.
- As a result:
  - The mail will be **declared undeliverable**.
  - It may be **returned to the sender** or **destroyed**, depending on postal policy.
  - The sender may face **financial penalties or administrative consequences**, depending on the jurisdiction and enforcement mechanisms.
