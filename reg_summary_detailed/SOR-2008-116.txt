**Detailed Summary of the Lobbyists Registration Regulations (S.C. 2006, c. 9, s. 79 and ss. 81(g); R.S., c. 44 (4th Supp.); S.C. 2006, c. 9, s. 66)**

---

### **1. Introduction and Authority**
These **Lobbyists Registration Regulations** were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **President of the Treasury Board**, under **section 12a of the Lobbying Act**.  
This means the regulations are a formal administrative implementation of the Lobbying Act, providing specific rules for how lobbyists must register and report their activities.

The regulations came into force on **July 2, 2008**, and are designed to ensure transparency, accountability, and compliance with the Lobbying Act in relation to lobbying activities by individuals and organizations.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

- **Act**: Refers to the *Lobbying Act*, which establishes the legal framework for regulating lobbying in Canada.
- **Commissioner**: The official appointed under subsection 4.1(1) of the Lobbying Act to oversee the registration and enforcement of the Act.
- **Consultant Lobbyist**: An individual who is required to file a return under **section 5(1)** of the Lobbying Act. This typically includes outside consultants who engage in lobbying activities on behalf of clients.
- **In-House Lobbyist**: An employee of a corporation or organization who is named in a return filed under **section 7(1)** of the Act. These are internal staff who perform lobbying duties as part of their job.
- **Member of a Transition Team**: Any person referred to in **subsection 2(3)** of the Lobbying Act. This relates to individuals involved in government transition processes (e.g., during changes in leadership or administration), who may be subject to specific lobbying restrictions.

---

### **3. Filing Requirements: Electronic vs. Paper Format**

#### **General Rule**
- A return must be filed **electronically**, unless an exemption applies.

#### **Exemptions for Paper Filings**
A person may file a return in **paper format** if:
1. They have a **disability** that prevents them from filing electronically; or  
2. They **do not have access** to a computer system capable of electronic filing.

#### **Required Forms for Paper Filings**
- **Form 1** – For a **consultant lobbyist** filing under section 5(1).
- **Form 2** – For an **in-house lobbyist** filing on behalf of a **corporation** under section 7(1).
- **Form 3** – For an **in-house lobbyist** filing on behalf of an **organization** under section 7(1).

#### **Delivery and Receipt Dates**
- **Mail or Hand Delivery**: Return is deemed received on the **date of receipt stamped** by the Commissioner’s office.
- **Facsimile (Fax)**: Return is deemed received on the **date indicated by the receiving apparatus**.
- **Electronic Filing**: Return is deemed received on the **date it enters the registry** of the Commissioner’s office.

#### **Validity of Filing**
- A **fax copy** of the return is considered **the original** for all purposes.

---

### **4. Additional Information Required in Returns**

#### **For Consultant Lobbyists (Section 5(1) Return)**
In addition to standard information required under section 5(2), the consultant must provide:
1. **Board Membership**:  
   - If the client is a corporation: Whether the consultant is a member of the **board of directors**.
   - If the client is an organization: Whether the consultant is a member of, or a member of the **board of directors** of, the organization.

2. **Five-Year Prohibition Status (Section 10.11)**:  
   - If the consultant **ceased to hold a designated public office on or after July 2, 2008**, and files the return **less than five years later**, they must state:
     - Whether they are **subject to the five-year lobbying prohibition** under section 10.11.
     - If **not subject**, provide the **reason** (e.g., exemption or waiver).
   - If the consultant **ceased to be a transition team member on or after January 24, 2006**, and files the return less than five years after that date, they must similarly indicate:
     - Whether they are subject to the five-year prohibition.
     - If not, provide the reason.

3. **Client’s Principal Representative**:  
   - The **name and title** of the client’s principal representative with whom the consultant interacts.

> *Purpose*: To ensure transparency regarding potential conflicts of interest, public office connections, and compliance with post-office lobbying restrictions.

---

#### **For Oral Communications (Section 5(3))**
Certain types of **oral communications** initiated **in advance** are defined as requiring special reporting:

- Any communication under subparagraphs **5(1)(a)(i) to (vi)** that is **initiated by someone other than a public office holder**.
- Any communication under **subparagraphs 5(1)(a)(v) or (vi)** that is **initiated by a public office holder**.

> *Note*: These are communications that involve direct engagement with public officials and are considered high-risk due to potential influence.

#### **Additional Information Required in Section 5(3) Returns**
In addition to the standard information under paragraph 5(3)(a), the consultant must include:
- The **position title** of the designated public office holder.
- The **name of the branch/unit** and **department/institution** where the public official works at the time of the communication.

> *Purpose*: To provide clear details about the nature and context of interactions with government officials.

---

#### **For In-House Lobbyists (Section 7(1) Return)**

The officer responsible (i.e., the employee) must include, in addition to the required information under section 7(3):
- If the in-house lobbyist **ceased to hold a designated public office on or after July 2, 2008**, but filed the return **less than five years after**, they must state:
  - Whether they are subject to the **five-year lobbying prohibition** under section 10.11.
  - If not, the **reason**.
- If the in-house lobbyist **ceased to be a transition team member on or after January 24, 2006**, and filed the return less than five years after, they must similarly indicate:
  - Whether they are subject to the five-year prohibition.
  - If not, the reason.

> *Purpose*: To track the transition from public service to private lobbying and ensure compliance with post-service restrictions.

---

#### **For Oral Communications (Section 7(4))**
Similar to section 5(3), the following oral communications are defined as requiring reporting:
- Any communication under subparagraphs **7(1)(a)(i) to (v)** initiated by someone **other than a public office holder**.
- Any communication under **subparagraph 7(1)(a)(v)** initiated by a **public office holder**.

#### **Additional Information Required in Section 7(4) Returns**
In addition to the required information under paragraph 7(4)(a), the officer must provide:
- The **position title** of the designated public office holder.
- The **name of the branch/unit** and **department/institution** where the official is employed at the time of the communication.

> *Purpose*: To ensure transparency in internal lobbying activities involving government officials.

---

### **5. Response to Commissioner Requests**

#### **Clarification Requests**
- The individual who submitted the return must provide a **clarification** within **30 days** of receiving the Commissioner’s request.
- The request is deemed made on:
  - The **postmark date** (if mailed),
  - The **date of delivery** (if hand-delivered),
  - The **date indicated by the receiving device** (if faxed),
  - The **date of transmission** (if sent electronically).

#### **Correction Requests**
- The individual must provide a **correction** within **10 days** of receiving the request.
- The correction is deemed received on:
  - The **date of receipt stamped** (if delivered by mail or hand),
  - The **date indicated by the receiving apparatus** (if faxed),
  - The **date of transmission** (if sent electronically).

> *Purpose*: To ensure accuracy and timeliness in data reporting, and to maintain the integrity of the registration system.

---

### **6. Repeal Clause**
The regulation includes a **repeal clause** indicating that these regulations **do not supersede or replace** any prior versions, but rather are the current version effective from **July 2, 2008**. This suggests a transition from earlier rules to a more detailed and structured system.
