- The Agreement was **signed on May 24, 2001**, under Article 28 of the Agreement on Social Security between Canada and the Czech Republic.
- It was **ratified and entered into force on January 1, 2003**, following the exchange of formal written notices by both parties confirming compliance with their respective legal requirements.
- The **entry into force** was governed by a specific timeline:
  - The Order in Council (P.C. 2001-1371) declared the Agreement would enter into force on the **first day of the fourth month** after the final month in which both parties exchanged ratification notices.
  - This date was **January 1, 2003**.
- The **Governor in Council** issued a **Proclamation (P.C. 2002-2174)** on December 12, 2002, officially notifying the public of the entry into force of the Agreement.

#### üìå Rule 6: Employment in a Party's Territory
- An **employed person** working in a Party‚Äôs territory is **subject only to that Party‚Äôs legislation**.

#### üìå Rule 7: Self-Employed Persons
- A self-employed person who would otherwise be subject to both Parties‚Äô laws is subject **only to the legislation of the Party where they reside**.

#### üìå Rule 8: Temporary Detachments
- If an employee is sent **temporarily** to work in the other Party‚Äôs territory (e.g., for a related employer), they remain subject to the **first Party‚Äôs legislation**.

#### üìå Government Employment

### üîπ **5. Creditable Periods and Totalization Rules**

This section outlines how periods of contributions/residence are combined to determine eligibility for benefits.

#### ‚úÖ Totalization of Creditable Periods:
- **For OAS (Canada)**:
  - A period of **presence/residence in the Czech Republic** where the person contributed to the **Canada Pension Plan (CPP)** or a provincial pension plan is counted as a **period of residence in Canada** for the person and their spouse/common-law partner and dependants (if not subject to Czech employment/self-employment).
  - Conversely, a period of residence in Canada where the person contributed to the Czech Republic‚Äôs legislation **does NOT count** toward Canadian residence for OAS eligibility (unless under specific conditions).

> üîç Only **contributions due to employment or self-employment** count.

#### ‚úÖ CPP Eligibility:
- A **calendar year** with **at least 90 days** of creditable periods under Czech legislation counts as a **creditable year** under the CPP.

#### ‚úÖ Czech Republic Eligibility:
- A **calendar year** of CPP contributions is treated as a **creditable period** under Czech law.
- A period of residence under the **Old Age Security Act (OAS)** that is **not part of a CPP creditable period** is also treated as a creditable period under Czech law.
- For **invalidity or survivor benefits**, CPP creditable years are treated as creditable under Czech law.

> ‚úÖ This enables cross-border recognition of contributions and promotes fairness.

#### ‚úÖ Final Eligibility Determination:
- If a person does **not meet eligibility** due to insufficient creditable periods, totalization is applied **across all periods** (Canada, Czech Republic, and third countries with mutual agreements).
- However, **if total duration is less than one year**, and **no benefit exists under the legislation**, the competent institution **does not have to pay** a benefit under this Agreement.

> ‚ùå A key safeguard to prevent overpayment or eligibility based on minimal contributions.

### üîπ **6. Benefit Calculation and Payment Rules**

#### ‚úÖ For OAS and Allowances (Outside Canada):
- A person outside Canada **must have totalized residence periods equal to or exceeding** the minimum required by Canadian OAS law to qualify for a pension.
- An **allowance or Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS)** is paid **only to the extent permitted** by Canadian law.

#### ‚úÖ For People Eligible via Totalization:
- **Earnings-related portion** of the benefit is calculated **based only on CPP pensionable earnings**.
- **Flat-rate portion** is calculated by:
  - Multiplying the flat-rate amount under CPP
  - By the **ratio of actual CPP contributions to the minimum qualifying period**.
  - **Maximum ratio = 1** (i.e., no more than full benefit).

> üìå Example: If a person contributed for 60% of the required period, they receive 60% of the flat-rate portion.

#### ‚úÖ For Czech Republic Benefits:
- If eligibility is met **without using totalization**, the benefit is calculated **solely on Czech legislation**.
- If eligibility **depends on totalization**, the Czech institution:
  1. Calculates the **theoretical benefit** if all periods were under Czech law alone.
  2. Applies a **ratio** of Czech creditable periods to totalized periods to determine the actual benefit.

> üîç This ensures fair and proportional outcomes.

#### ‚úÖ Assessment Base:
- Creditable periods under **Canadian legislation are excluded** when calculating the assessment base for Czech benefits.

> ‚öñÔ∏è Ensures no double-counting or overvaluation.
