**Detailed Summary of the *Gros Morne National Park of Canada Snowshoe Hare Regulations* (S.C. 2000, c. 32)**

---

### **Overview**

These regulations, made under **Section 17 of the Canada National Parks Act**, govern the **traditional harvesting of snowshoe hares** in **Gros Morne National Park of Canada**. The regulations establish a formal, controlled system for **snaring snowshoe hares**, ensuring sustainability, ecological protection, and community-based access. They were introduced to balance **traditional Indigenous and local harvesting practices** with **environmental conservation and park management**.

The regulations came into force on **October 1, 2005**, and remain in effect as of the latest amendment (SOR/2009-322, s. 27 — which repealed a specific provision).

## **Scope and Applicability**

- These regulations **apply exclusively to Gros Morne National Park of Canada**.
- In the event of conflict with other regulations under the *Canada National Parks Act*, **these regulations prevail**.
- **Exemptions**: Sections 5 and 6 (which may otherwise restrict certain activities) **do not apply** to:
  - The Superintendent, park wardens, enforcement officers, or peace officers
  - When performing duties related to **harvesting management** or **scientific research** on snowshoe hares

> This ensures that park staff and researchers can operate without restriction during official duties.

---

## **Legal Basis and Status of Snaring**

- Snaring snowshoe hares is designated as a **traditional renewable resource harvesting activity**, recognizing its cultural and historical significance to local communities.

---

## **Permit Requirements**

### **1. Who Can Apply?**
Only **eligible persons** may apply for a snare permit.

- Must be a **permanent resident** of one of the designated communities in Newfoundland and Labrador.
- Must have been **at least 19 years old on August 13, 1973**, and a permanent resident of the park or community at that time.
- Or, must be the **child** of such a person.

> This establishes a **generational link** to traditional harvesting practices.

### **2. Application Process**
- Must be submitted to the **Superintendent** using a form provided by them.
- Required information:
  - Full name, date of birth, and permanent address
  - **Written evidence** proving eligibility

### **3. Permit Issuance**
- The Superintendent may issue a snare permit upon application, **subject to**:
  - Any determinations under Sections 15–17 (management and conservation decisions)
  - The applicant not already holding a valid or suspended permit for the season
  - No prior revocation of a permit within the **12 months before application**

> Prevents overuse and ensures responsible access.

---

## **Permit Conditions and Restrictions**

Every snare permit includes the following mandatory elements:

- Name, date of birth, and address of the permit holder
- A **clear statement that transfer or assignment of the permit is prohibited**
- A description of the **duties and responsibilities** of the permit holder (Sections 12–14)
- A statement that **contravention of permit conditions is an offence under subsection 24(3) of the Act**
- Any **supplementary conditions** set by the Superintendent (e.g., area restrictions, snare limits, duration)

---

## **Duration and Expiry of Permits**

A snare permit expires on the **earliest of**:
- The expiry date stated on the permit
- The day the permit is revoked
- The day the permit holder is no longer a permanent resident of the park or a community

> Ensures that permits are only valid while the holder remains in the community and eligible.

---

## **Prohibited Activities by Permit Holders**

Permit holders **must not**:
1. **Transfer or assign** their permit to another person
2. Use **non-metallic wire snares** (only metallic wire allowed)
3. Set snares to capture animals **other than snowshoe hares**
4. **Bait snares** with anything other than **naturally occurring flora**
5. **Cut flora** with trunk diameter >7.5 cm at 15 cm above ground (to protect vegetation)
6. **Cut or damage** white pine, yellow birch, black ash, or red maple trees (key forest species)
7. Leave snares **unattended for more than 48 hours**
8. Snare in areas **closed by the Superintendent** under Section 16
9. Use snared hares for any purpose **other than personal or household use** (e.g., gifts, food, trade)

> These rules protect biodiversity, prevent overharvesting, and minimize environmental impact.

---

## **Duties and Responsibilities of Permit Holders**

Permit holders must:
- **Carry their snare permit** at all times while snaring or in possession of hares
- **Immediately produce** their permit, snares, and any hares upon request by park officials
- **Notify the Superintendent in writing** of any change in permanent address
- **Report immediately** to a park warden if a non-snowshoe hare is accidentally caught and is **wounded or dead**
- **After snaring half of their permit limit**, reduce the number of snares so that the total number **does not exceed the remaining half of the limit**

> This promotes sustainable and monitored harvesting.

---

## **Removal of Snares and Reporting**

- Permit holders must **remove all snares, line indicators, and trail markers** when:
  - The hare limit is reached, **or**
  - Before the permit expires (if limit not reached)

> Failure to comply results in the Superintendent removing and disposing of the snares.

- The permit holder is **responsible for all costs** incurred by the park due to this removal.

---

## **Return of Permit**

Within **30 days after the permit expires**, the holder must:
- Return the permit to a park warden
- On the **back of the permit**, provide:
  - Number of snares set
  - Number of hares snared
  - Areas where hares were snared
  - Number of days of snaring activity
  - Any other required information under permit conditions

> This ensures accurate record-keeping for ecological monitoring.

---

## **Management Authority of the Superintendent**

The Superintendent has broad authority to:
- **Determine which areas** in the park allow or restrict snaring (under Sections 15 and 16)
- **Close areas temporarily** for public safety, ecological protection, or conservation reasons
- Set:
  - The **annual limit** of snowshoe hares that may be snared per permit
  - The **maximum number of permits** to issue per area

> This ensures dynamic, science-based management of harvesting levels.

---

## **Penalties for Violations**

- **Suspension**: A permit may be suspended if the holder violates any regulation or condition.
- **Reinstatement**: A suspended permit may be reinstated if:
  - The violation is corrected
  - 30 days pass after suspension (unless proceedings are started)
  - The person is found not guilty or proceedings are discontinued
- **Revocation**: A permit is revoked if:
  - The holder is **convicted of a violation**
  - The permit has been **suspended twice** during its term (except when reinstated under specific conditions)

> Provides accountability and enforces compliance.

---
