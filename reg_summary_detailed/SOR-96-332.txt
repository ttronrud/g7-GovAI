## üìö **Comprehensive Summary of the Employment Insurance Regulations (S.C. 1996, c. 23)**

These regulations implement and clarify provisions of the **Employment Insurance Act (EIA)**, specifically governing who is considered an *insured person*, what constitutes *insurable employment*, and the definitions of key terms such as *family member*, *care*, *support*, and *critically ill individuals*. The regulations also define exceptions to insurable employment and outline the reasonable efforts required for claimants seeking reemployment.

---

## üîç I. **Legal Authority and Purpose**

- The regulations were **approved by the Governor General in Council** based on recommendations from the Minister of Employment and Immigration.
- They are issued under specific sections of the **Employment Insurance Act**, including:
  - Sections 5, 16, 19, 24, 36, 37, 42, 50, 54, 55, 60, 69, 109, 110, 111, 114, 116, 121, 123, 139, 143, 153.1, and 167.
  - Paragraphs in Schedule II (particularly **paragraph 5(6)(g)** and **section 14** of Schedule II).
- Purpose: To **define and clarify** who is covered under Employment Insurance (EI), what types of work are insurable, and how benefits are administered in relation to health-related family circumstances.

## üìö II. **Key Definitions**

### 1. **General Definitions**
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Employment Insurance Act*. |
| **Pay Period** | The time during which an insured person receives or enjoys earnings (e.g., weekly, monthly). |
| **Self-employed person** (except in sections 30 and 35) | Defined as per subsection **152.01(1)** of the EIA ‚Äî generally includes individuals operating independently without employer-employee relationships. |

---

### 2. **International Organization**
An entity that:
- Is a **specialized agency of the United Nations** (under Article 63 of the UN Charter), or  
- Is a **member organization** whose primary purpose is maintaining international peace or promoting economic/social well-being among nations.

> *Purpose: To determine whether employment with such organizations is insurable under EI.*

---

### 3. **Family Member** *(Extensive Definition)*
A person considered a "family member" for EI purposes includes:

- Spouse or common-law partner  
- Children (of self or partner)  
- Parents (or their spouse/common-law partner)  
- Grandparents or grandchildren  
- Spouse/common-law partner of a child or parent  
- Parents or spouses of a spouse/common-law partner  
- Children of a parent's child or spouse  
- Uncles/aunts and nephews/nieces  
- Foster parents/children or guardians/ward  
- Any person the individual *considers to be like a close relative* ‚Äî **even if not related by blood, marriage, or law**

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note:** This broad definition ensures that family members affected by illness or disability can access EI benefits.

---

### 4. **Care and Support**
- **Care** = All care required due to the health condition of a family member (e.g., critically ill child/adult), **excluding care provided by health professionals**.
- **Support** = All psychological or emotional support needed due to a family member‚Äôs illness or injury.

> These are key for qualifying for **family care benefits** under EI (e.g., under sections 23.1, 23.2, 23.3).

---

### 5. **Critically Ill Child**
- A person **under 18 years old** on the start date of the period in question.
- Their baseline health has **significantly changed**, and their **life is at risk** due to illness or injury.

> Applies to EI benefits for family care and support.

---

### 6. **Critically Ill Adult**
- A person **18 years or older** on the start date of the period.
- Their health has significantly changed, and their **life is at risk** due to illness or injury.

> Also qualifies for EI family care/support benefits.

## üè¢ III. **What Constitutes Insurable Employment?**

This section establishes **which types of work are covered** under EI, with exceptions.

---

### ‚úÖ **Employment Included in Insurable Employment**

1. **Government Employment (Provincial Level)**
   - Employment by a province (via Her Majesty in right of a province) is included **if**:
     - The provincial government enters into a written agreement with the Commission to **waive exclusions** and **insure all employees** in such roles.
   - Includes employees appointed under provincial public service laws or in corporations/commissions acting as agents of the province.

2. **Employment by Foreign Governments or International Organizations**
   - Included **if** the foreign government or international organization **consents in writing** to inclusion.
   - **Exception**: Consents given under the **pre-1996 Unemployment Insurance Regulations** remain valid **unless revoked**.

3. **Maritime Employment (Ships Outside Canada)**
   - Included if:
     - Ship is **Canadian-registered or licensed**, **except** if it regularly operates between ports outside Canada and is chartered to a foreign resident.
     - Ship is **not Canadian-registered**, but:
       - Charters are to a **Canadian resident**;
       - Operates from a **Canadian port**;
       - Operations are **primarily controlled in Canada**;
       - Owner or managing owner resides or has a business in Canada;
       - Or employment is governed by an **agreement between Canada and the ship‚Äôs registry**.

4. **Employment Outside Canada**
   - Included if:
     - Person **ordinarily resides in Canada**;
     - Employment is **outside or partly outside Canada**;
     - Employer is **resident or has a place of business in Canada**;
     - The job would be insurable **if performed in Canada**;
     - The employment is **not insurable under the host country‚Äôs unemployment insurance law**.

5. **Specific Types of Employment (Included)**
   - Union members working for their union in **union business** (excluding picketing).
   - Apprentices/trainees ‚Äî even if they don‚Äôt perform services.
   - Clergy or members of religious orders.
   - Barber/hairdressers ‚Äî **if not owners/operators** of the business.
   - Taxi, bus, or school bus drivers ‚Äî **if not owners of >50% of vehicle or operators**.
   - Public service officers (federal or provincial) in departments listed in **Schedule I‚ÄìV of the Financial Administration Act**, **provided**:
     - Appointed under provincial public service law;
     - Province has agreed to insure all such employees;
     - Employment is in a provincial agency or union/association elected by popular vote.
   - Employees placed through **placement/employment agencies** ‚Äî remunerated by the agency.

## ‚ùå IV. **Employments Excluded from Insurable Employment**

### A. **Permanent Exclusions (Original)**
- **Religious orders**: If a person has taken a vow of poverty and receives remuneration directly or personally to the order.
- **Unemployment insurance in U.S. states**:
  - Under the **Canada‚ÄìU.S. Unemployment Insurance Agreement (1942)**;
  - Under the **U.S. Railroad Unemployment Insurance Act**.
- **Employment abroad** where premiums are paid under another country‚Äôs unemployment insurance law (e.g., foreign nationals working in Canada).
- **Rescue operations** ‚Äî only if not regularly employed by the employer.
- **Self-employment or Job Creation Partnerships benefits** ‚Äî excluded from EI coverage.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note: Some exclusions have been repealed (e.g., circus/fair work, referendum/election work).*

---

### B. **Conditional Exclusions (Subject to Subsections 2‚Äì4)**

1. **Circus, Fair, Parade, Carnival, etc.**
   - Excluded if:
     - Not regularly employed;
     - Employed for **less than 7 days per year**.

2. **Public Office Referendum/Election Work**
   - Excluded if:
     - Not regularly employed;
     - Employed for **less than 35 hours per year after 1998**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è These exclusions are **temporary** and apply only to short-term, non-regular roles.
