**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

---

**Title:**  
Proclamation Establishing a National Day of Remembrance for Victims of Air Disasters

**Date of Proclamation:**  
December 23, 2020  
(Issued under Order in Council P.C. 2020-1130)

**Issuing Authority:**  
The Government of Canada, by and with the advice of the Privy Council, through the Governor in Council.

**Signed by:**  
Julie Payette, Governor General of Canada (acting on behalf of Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II)

**Jurisdiction:**  
Canada (nationwide)

---

### **Background and Context:**

This proclamation was enacted in response to the tragic downing of **Ukraine International Airlines Flight 752** on **January 8, 2020**, which resulted in the deaths of **176 people**, including:

- **55 Canadian citizens**
- **30 Canadian permanent residents**
- Numerous other individuals with ties to Canada

This incident stands as one of the **worst aviation disasters in Canadian history** in terms of loss of Canadian lives.

The proclamation also acknowledges the broader historical context of air disasters that have deeply affected Canada and the global community, including:

- **Air India Flight 182 (1988)** – A terrorist bombing that killed 329 people, including many Canadians.
- **Ethiopian Airlines Flight 302 (2019)** – A crash that killed 257 people, highlighting global safety concerns in aviation.

These tragedies have left lasting emotional and societal impacts on Canadians, particularly within families and communities who have lost loved ones.

---

### **Purpose of the Proclamation:**

The primary objective of this regulation is to:

1. **Honour the memory of victims** of air disasters, especially those who were Canadian citizens or residents.
2. Provide a **formal, national opportunity** for Canadians to reflect on the human cost of aviation tragedies.
3. Promote **national remembrance, empathy, and awareness** of aviation safety and the importance of preventing future incidents.
4. Recognize the **collective grief** and resilience of Canadian society in the face of such loss.

---

### **Key Provision:**

The Government of Canada **proclaims January 8 of every year as a National Day of Remembrance for Victims of Air Disasters**.

This day is designated to:

- Be observed across Canada by individuals, communities, schools, organizations, and government institutions.
- Include activities such as memorial services, educational programs, public statements, and moments of silence.
- Serve as a day of reflection, remembrance, and advocacy for improved aviation safety standards.

---

### **Legal Basis:**

The proclamation is issued under the authority of the **Governor in Council**, which is the formal mechanism through which the federal government enacts laws and proclamations with the advice of the Privy Council.

It follows **Order in Council P.C. 2020-1130**, dated December 18, 2020, which authorized the issuance of the proclamation.

---

### **Legal Effect and Obligations:**

- The proclamation is **not a law** in the traditional sense (it does not create new rights or obligations under statute law).
- However, it carries **moral, symbolic, and ceremonial weight**.
- It is **binding upon all persons and institutions in Canada** to recognize and observe the day as a day of remembrance.
- The Government encourages, but does not mandate, specific actions or ceremonies.

---

### **Significance and Impact:**

- This day marks the **first official national day of remembrance** dedicated specifically to victims of air disasters in Canada.
- It provides a **structured, annual opportunity** for Canadians to collectively acknowledge the pain and loss caused by aviation accidents.
- It strengthens national identity and unity by recognizing shared trauma and resilience.
- It may also contribute to public discourse on aviation safety, transparency, and accountability in the industry.

---

### **Conclusion:**

The Proclamation Establishing a National Day of Remembrance for Victims of Air Disasters is a **symbolic and meaningful act of national mourning and remembrance**. It honors the lives lost in the 2020 crash of Ukraine International Airlines Flight 752 and other past aviation tragedies, while reinforcing Canada’s commitment to safety, compassion, and the dignity of those affected. Though not a regulatory statute with enforcement powers, it serves as a powerful tool for education, emotional healing, and national unity.

---

**Note:**  
This proclamation does not impose penalties or legal requirements. Its purpose is **commemorative, educational, and cultural**, reflecting the values of remembrance, respect, and national solidarity.
