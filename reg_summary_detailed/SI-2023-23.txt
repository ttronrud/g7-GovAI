**Detailed Summary of the "Certain Fees Paid or Payable for the Acquisition of Permanent Resident Status, Travel Document Services and Consular Services (Resettlement from Afghanistan) Remission Order"**

---

### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
This regulation is issued under **subsection 23(2.1)a of the Financial Administration Act (S.C. 1991, c. 24, s. 7(2))**, and is made by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, based on the recommendations of:
- The **Treasury Board**  
- The **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration**  
- The **Minister of Foreign Affairs**

This authority allows the government to remit (i.e., waive or forgive) certain fees in the public interest, particularly in response to humanitarian and emergency circumstances.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Order**
The primary purpose of this remission order is to **provide financial relief** to individuals who:
- Were affected by the fall of Kabul in August 2021,  
- Were in Afghanistan or en route to Canada,  
- Required urgent travel to reach Canada,  
- And applied for permanent residency, citizenship, or travel documents during a critical period.

The remission is intended to support the **resettlement of Afghan nationals and their families** who were displaced due to the collapse of the Afghan government and the subsequent evacuation efforts.

---

### **3. Scope of Fees Remitted**

#### **A. Permanent Resident Status Fees (Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations – Section 303(1))**
Remission is granted for fees associated with the acquisition of **permanent resident status**, for individuals whose applications were processed under any of the following **temporary public policies**:

1. **Temporary public policy for the issuance of permanent resident visas for families of Afghan nationals who came to Canada under previous public policies**  
2. **Amended temporary public policy to facilitate permanent residence for certain citizens of Afghanistan issued temporary resident permits**  
3. **Temporary public policy to facilitate permanent residence for certain citizens of Afghanistan**  
4. **Temporary public policy for the issuance of permanent resident visas for families of Afghan nationals who came to Canada under previous public policies — intake cap increase**  
5. **Temporary public policy for extended families of former language and cultural advisors**

> *Note:* These policies were implemented during the 2021–2022 period to expedite the processing of applications from Afghans and their families who were evacuated from Afghanistan.

✅ **Remission applies** to those who applied under any of these specific temporary policies.

---

#### **B. Certificate of Citizenship Fees (Citizenship Regulations – Section 31(1) and Section 315 of the IRPR Regulations)**
Remission is granted for the fees associated with an application for a **Certificate of Citizenship**, under the following conditions:

- The application was made **abroad** during the period **August 25, 2021 – December 31, 2023**  
- The applicant is a **Canadian citizen or permanent resident**  
- The applicant was **in Afghanistan or in another country en route to Canada**  
- The applicant **urgently needed to travel** due to the fall of Kabul in August 2021  
- If the applicant was **outside Afghanistan** when applying, their urgent travel was **facilitated by Canada**

> *Note:* This includes individuals who were displaced or in transit and needed to return to Canada for citizenship purposes.

---

#### **C. Travel Document Services Fees (Passport and Other Travel Document Services Fees Regulations – Schedule Items 9, 10, and 14)**
Remission is granted for fees related to the following services, when applied for **abroad** during the specified period:

- **Item 9**: Application for a **temporary passport**  
- **Item 10**: Application for a **certificate of citizenship** (if applicable)  
- **Item 14**: Application for a **travel document** (likely including emergency travel documents)

✅ **Eligibility conditions**:
- Application must have been made **abroad** between **August 25, 2021, and December 31, 2023**  
- The person must have been in **Afghanistan or en route to Canada**  
- The person **urgently needed to travel** due to the fall of Kabul in August 2021

> *Note:* The requirement that the application be made "abroad" ensures that the remission is targeted at those who were outside Canada when they needed travel documents.

---

#### **D. Consular Services Fees (Consular Services Fees Regulations – Section 4)**
Remission is granted for the **fees for issuing a temporary passport**, under the following conditions:

- The application was made **at a Canadian diplomatic mission abroad**  
- During the period **August 25, 2021 – December 31, 2023**  
- The person **urgently needed to travel** as a direct result of the fall of Kabul in August 2021

> *Note:* This provision specifically supports individuals who were in a foreign country and needed a temporary passport to return to Canada or travel for urgent reasons.

---

### **4. Key Conditions for Eligibility**

All remissions are subject to the following **core conditions**:

| Condition | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Timeframe** | Applications must have been made between **August 25, 2021, and December 31, 2023** |
| **Urgent Travel Need** | The individual must have **urgently needed to travel** due to the **fall of Kabul in August 2021** |
| **Location at Time of Application** | Must have been in **Afghanistan or en route to Canada** at the time of application |
| **Application Location** | For travel document services, the application must have been made **abroad** |
| **Travel Facilitated by Canada** | If the applicant was outside Afghanistan, their urgent travel must have been **facilitated by Canada** (e.g., through Canadian consular support or emergency assistance) |

> These conditions ensure that the remission is targeted at those who were directly impacted by the crisis and required urgent assistance to return to Canada.

---

### **5. Types of Services Covered**

| Service Type | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Permanent Residency Fees** | Fees for applying for permanent residence under specific temporary policies for Afghans and their families |
| **Certificate of Citizenship Fees** | Fees for applying for citizenship when abroad due to the Kabul crisis |
| **Temporary Passport Fees** | Fees for emergency travel documents (items 9, 10, 14 in the Schedule) |
| **Consular Temporary Passport Issuance** | Fees for temporary passports issued at Canadian missions abroad |

---

### **6. Effectiveness and Timeline**

- **Date of Coming Into Force**: The Order **comes into force on the day it is made** (i.e., the date of publication in the Canada Gazette).
- **Duration**: The remission applies **only during the specified period** (August 25, 2021 – December 31, 2023), and is **not retroactive** beyond that window.
- **No Future Extensions**: This remission is **time-limited** and does not apply to applications made after December 31, 2023.

---

### **7. Public Interest Justification**
The government asserts that this remission is in the **public interest** because:
- It supports the safe and timely resettlement of Afghans and their families following the collapse of the Afghan government  
- It recognizes the **humanitarian crisis** and the urgent need for individuals to return to Canada  
- It reduces financial barriers for vulnerable populations who were displaced and required emergency travel  
- It demonstrates Canada’s commitment to supporting those affected by the Kabul evacuation

---

### **8. Key Takeaways and Implications**

- **Targeted Relief**: This is not a blanket fee waiver; it is narrowly focused on individuals affected by the fall of Kabul and who applied during a defined window.
- **Humanitarian Focus**: The policy reflects a humanitarian response to a major crisis, prioritizing urgent needs over administrative or financial considerations.
- **Administrative Clarity**: The regulation clearly defines eligibility criteria, making it easier for applicants to understand whether they qualify.
- **Consistency with Government Policy**: Aligns with broader government efforts to support Afghan resettlement and protect vulnerable populations.

---

### **Conclusion**

This remission order is a **targeted, time-bound, and humanitarian measure** designed to support individuals who were displaced from Afghanistan or in transit to Canada following the fall of Kabul in August 2021. It waives fees for permanent residency, citizenship, and emergency travel document applications made abroad during the period August 25, 2021, to December 31, 2023, under strict eligibility conditions tied to urgent travel needs and geographic circumstances.

It reflects Canada’s recognition of the exceptional circumstances faced by Afghans and their families and underscores the government’s commitment to providing accessible, equitable, and compassionate support during times of crisis.
