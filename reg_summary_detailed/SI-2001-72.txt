## üîç **OVERVIEW OF THE REGULATION**

This is a **bilateral social security agreement** between **Canada** and the **Republic of Slovenia**, signed on **May 17, 1998**, and entering into force on **January 1, 2001**. The agreement establishes rules for the **recognition, eligibility, and payment of social security benefits** (such as pensions, disability benefits, and survivor benefits) for individuals who have worked or resided in both countries.

The purpose is to **avoid double taxation, double coverage, or benefit denial** due to cross-border residency or employment, and to ensure that individuals are treated fairly under the social security systems of both nations.

## üìú **KEY PROVISIONS AND STRUCTURE**

### 1. **Entry into Force**
- The Agreement entered into force on **January 1, 2001**.
- This date follows the **first day of the fourth month after the month in which both parties exchanged ratification instruments**, which occurred on **September 15, 2000**.
- The Order in Council (P.C. 1998-1806) was laid before Parliament on **October 23, 1998**, and came into force on **February 2, 1999**, due to lack of parliamentary objection within the required timeframe.

> ‚úÖ **Key Takeaway**: The Agreement became legally binding in 2001 after formal ratification and mutual notification.

### 3. **Scope of Application**

#### A. Primary Legislation Covered
- **Canada**:
  - Old Age Security Act (OAS)
  - Canada Pension Plan (CPP)
- **Slovenia**:
  - Pension and Disability Insurance Act (excluding benefits for reduced working capacity)

#### B. Extended Application
- Applies to **amendments, supplements, consolidations, or supersessions** of the above legislation.
- Applies to **new beneficiaries or new benefits** unless one party objects within **3 months** of entry into force.

> ‚úÖ **Inclusion Rule**: The agreement covers individuals and their **dependants and survivors** under either country‚Äôs laws.

### 4. **Eligibility and Benefit Rights**

#### A. General Principle
> Unless otherwise stated, **a person who is or has been subject to either country‚Äôs legislation is eligible for benefits from the other country under the same conditions as a citizen of that country**.

#### B. No Reduction or Suspension of Benefits
- Benefits under either country‚Äôs legislation **shall not be reduced, suspended, canceled, or confiscated solely because the beneficiary resides in the other country**.
- Benefits must be **paid in the territory of the other country**.

#### C. Third Country Residency
- Citizens of one country residing in a **third country** are treated the same as citizens of the first country residing there.

> ‚úÖ **Key Protection**: Residency does not affect eligibility or payment of benefits.

### 6. **Creditable Periods and Totalization Rules**

This section governs how periods of contribution or residence are **counted across borders** to determine eligibility.

#### A. OAS Act ‚Äì Creditable Periods in Slovenia
- If a person is contributing to **Canada‚Äôs CPP or provincial pension plans** while residing in Slovenia:
  - That period counts as **residence in Canada** for the person and their **spouse/dependants** (if not employed/self-employed in Slovenia).
- If a person is contributing to **Slovenia‚Äôs system** while in Canada:
  - That period **does not count** as residence in Canada for spouse/dependants (unless covered by CPP or provincial plan).

#### B. CPP ‚Äì Creditable Periods in Slovenia
- A **calendar year with at least 3 months** of creditable periods in Slovenia counts as a **full year** of CPP contributions.

#### C. Slovenia ‚Äì Creditable Periods from Canada
- A **calendar year** of CPP contributions counts as **12 months** of creditable periods in Slovenia.
- A **month** of OAS eligibility (not part of a CPP period) counts as **1 month** of Slovenian creditable periods.

#### D. Disability & Survivor Pensions
- Same rule as above: **1 CPP year = 12 Slovenian months**.

#### E. Totalization of Periods
- If a person has **insufficient creditable periods** under either country, eligibility is determined by **totalizing all creditable periods** from both countries and any third country with reciprocal agreements.

> ‚úÖ **Note**: Periods cannot overlap when calculating totalization.

### 8. **Special Provisions for Residents Outside Canada**

- **Old Age Security (OAS) Pension**:
  - Must have **at least the minimum residency period** in Canada (totalized under Chapter 1).
  - Otherwise, **no OAS pension is payable**.

- **Spouse‚Äôs Allowance & Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS)**:

> ‚ùå **No OAS or GIS outside Canada** without meeting minimum residency requirements.

### 9. **Administrative and Information Sharing**

#### A. Mutual Communication
- Competent authorities and institutions must:
  - Share **necessary information** for benefit determination.
  - Provide **free assistance** in assessing eligibility or benefit amounts (as if it were their own system).
  - Notify each other of **legislative changes** that affect the agreement.

#### B. Confidentiality
- All information exchanged under the agreement is **confidential** and **only for administrative purposes**.
- Cannot be disclosed unless required by law.

#### C. Document Exemptions
- Documents required for benefit applications are **exempt from diplomatic or consular authentication**.
- **Tax exemptions** and **legal fees** provided under one country‚Äôs law extend to the other country‚Äôs documents.
