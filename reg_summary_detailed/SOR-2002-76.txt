**Detailed Summary of the *Marine Activities in the Saguenay-St. Lawrence Marine Park Regulations* (S.C. 1997, c. 37)**

---

### **Overview**

These regulations, made under **Section 17 of the Saguenay-St. Lawrence Marine Park Act**, establish the legal framework governing all marine-related activities within the **Saguenay-St. Lawrence Marine Park (SSMLMP)**. The primary objective is to **protect marine ecosystems, particularly marine mammals**, while allowing sustainable, regulated tourism and scientific activities. The regulations define key terms, outline permit requirements, set operational standards, and establish enforcement mechanisms.

> **Note**: Several definitions and provisions were repealed in 2016 (SOR/2016-257), including "disturbance of a marine mammal," "endangered marine mammal," "Fjord," and "commercial vessel." This summary reflects only the current, active provisions as of the latest amendments.

---

## **I. Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined and used throughout the regulations:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Saguenay-St. Lawrence Marine Park Act*. |
| **Marine mammal** | Any cetacean (whales, dolphins, porpoises) or pinniped (seals, sea lions). |
| **Vessel** | Any watercraft used or capable of being used for navigation, including air cushion vehicles. |
| **Aircraft** | As defined in the *Aeronautics Act*. |
| **Operator** | The person in command or control of a vessel. |
| **Marine tour business** | Any activity involving observation of marine life, landscape, or cultural resources from water or air (e.g., cruises, kayaking, diving), excluding official park management duties. |
| **Class 1** | Business offering *directed marine mammal watching tours* on non-human-powered vessels. |
| **Class 2** | Business offering *non-directed marine mammal watching or other marine observations* on non-human-powered vessels. |
| **Class 3** | Business operating on *human-powered vessels* (e.g., kayaks, canoes). |
| **Cruise ship** | A passenger vessel offering overnight accommodations for at least 100 persons (excluding crew). |
| **Shuttle service** | A paid water-based passenger transport service. |
| **Special activity** | Temporary events such as parades, regattas, film shoots, or flying below 609.6 m (2,000 ft). |
| **Observation mode** | When a vessel approaches a cetacean within less than 400 meters. |
| **Observation zone** | A circular area (radius = 0.5 nautical miles = 926 meters) around a vessel in observation mode. |
| **Speed** | Speed over the ground. |
| **Year** | A 12-month period beginning April 1. |

---

## **II. Exemptions and Exceptions**

Certain individuals or activities are exempt from the full scope of these regulations:

1. **Park officials** (superintendent, wardens, enforcement officers, peace officers) are exempt while performing official duties.
2. **Persons acting under direct instructions from the Minister** for environmental protection, public safety, or park management are exempt.
3. **Fishing activities** are exempt under specific conditions:
   - Holders of **commercial fishing licences** or **aboriginal communal fishing licences**.
   - Holders of **seal fishing licences**.
4. **Scientific research** is exempt from some restrictions **only if**:
   - It involves marine mammals.
   - Approaching them closer than the minimum distance is necessary for research.
5. **Cargo ships** are exempt from certain provisions (e.g., observation mode restrictions) under section 14.

> These exemptions ensure that essential operations (e.g., emergency response, scientific study, fishing) are not unduly restricted.

---

## **III. Prohibitions**

No person may operate or conduct activities in the park unless authorized:

- **Marine tour business**, **cruise ship**, or **shuttle service** must be operated by a **permit holder** or with **written authorization** from a permit holder.
- **Scientific research** or **special activities** must be conducted by a **permit holder** or with **written authorization** from one.

> This ensures accountability and prevents unauthorized or unregulated operations that could harm marine mammals or ecosystems.

---

## **IV. Permit Requirements**

### **Types of Permits**
Permits are issued by the Minister under Section 10 of the Act and include:

- Class 1, 2, or 3 permits (for marine tour businesses)
- Shuttle service permit
- Scientific research permit
- Cruise ship permit
- Special activity permit

### **Application Requirements**
All applications must be submitted in writing and include:

| Requirement | Details |
|----------|--------|
| **Applicant details** | Name, contact info; business info if different |
| **Type of permit** | Clearly specified |
| **Equipment details** | Number and registration numbers of vessels |
| **Operational areas** | Description of zones where operations will occur |
| **Legal documentation** | Proof vessels are authorized and capable of the activity |
| **Services offered** | For class 1/2/3 or shuttle permits |
| **Research details** | Objectives, methodology, duration, equipment, dates, times, places; if marine mammals are involved, must state whether closer approach is necessary |
| **Special activity details** | Description, objectives, dates, locations |
| **Cruise dates** | For cruise ship permits |

> Applications must also include the **applicable fee** as set by the Minister under the *Parks Canada Agency Act*.

---

## **V. Permit Conditions and Objectives**

The Minister may impose conditions in permits to achieve the following **four core objectives**:

1. **Protection and management of the park**
2. **Protection of ecosystems** (including reducing vessel numbers)
3. **Protection of submerged cultural resources**
4. **Public health, safety, and access** for education, recreation, and science

### **Permit Conditions May Include**:
- Time/place restrictions on operations
- Fueling practices
- Safety protocols
- Passenger information requirements
- Communication methods (e.g., for Class 1 vessels)
- Mandatory on-board personnel (e.g., park warden)
- Reporting obligations:
  - Number of vessel uses annually
  - Ecosystem incidents (Class 3 permits)
  - Research activity reports (scientific permits)
  - Client limits per guide (Class 3)
  - Guide qualifications (Class 3)
- Restrictions on approaching marine mammals in research activities to **minimize disturbance**

> Conditions can be **amended** if needed to meet these objectives.

## **VI. Suspension, Reinstatement, and Cancellation of Permits**

| Event | Grounds |
|------|--------|
| **Suspension** | - Failure to comply with permit conditions<br>- Reasonable belief of contravention of Act, Regulations, or other laws<br>- Failure to notify Minister of changes in application info |
| **Reinstatement** | - Breach corrected<br>- 30 days passed without prosecution<br>- Found not guilty of contravention |
| **Cancellation** | - Convicted of contravention<br>- Suspended 3 times during permit term (except where reinstated under (b) or (c)) |

> **Important Provisions**:
- The Minister must give the permit holder an **opportunity to be heard** before cancellation.
- A suspended permit holder is **ineligible** to apply for new permits during suspension.
- A cancelled permit holder is **ineligible** for 12 months after cancellation.

---

## **VII. Transfer of Permits**

- A permit may be transferred **only** if:
  - The current holder **notifies the Minister in writing** of:
    - Name and contact of new owner
    - Proposed transfer date
    - Any changes to business name, vessel registration, or other details
  - The Minister **approves** the transfer
- Approval is granted **only if** the changes do **not affect existing permit conditions**.
- If ownership changes but the permit is not transferred properly, the permit **expires on the date of transfer**.

> This ensures continuity and accountability during business transitions.

---

## **VIII. Marine Activities Certificates**

Required for **operators and guides** under certain permits:

- **Class 1, 2, 3 permits**
- Scientific research involving marine mammals
- Special activities involving marine mammals

### **Issuance and Renewal**
- Issued after passing a **Minister-approved training course**
- Must pass an **annual examination** to renew
- Must pay applicable fees (as set by the Minister)

### **Obligations**
- Must **carry the certificate at all times** during operations

### **Suspension & Cancellation**
- **Suspension**: Reasonable belief of contravention
- **Cancellation**:
  - Convicted 3 times of contravention
  - Suspended 3 times
- **No new certificate** issued to someone whose certificate has been previously cancelled
- Opportunity to be heard before cancellation
