## üîç **Detailed Summary of the National Parks Garbage Regulations (Canada)**

### üìå **Background and Legal Authority**
- The regulation was issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of the Environment**, under **subsection 7(1) of the National Parks Act**.
- It **revokes the previous National Parks Garbage Regulations (C.R.C., c. 1123)** and replaces them with updated, more detailed rules governing the **management, storage, collection, and disposal of garbage in Canadian national parks**.
- The regulation came into force as part of a modernization effort to improve environmental protection, ensure public health, and promote sustainable waste management in national parks.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: This regulation was **repealed in 2001 (SOR/2001-449, s. 2)** and is no longer in force. However, the summary below reflects its content as it existed at the time of enactment, which may still be relevant for historical or policy analysis.

## üö´ **Applicability and Exclusions**

- **Applies to**: National Parks and **park reserves** (as if they were parks), **subject to Sections 40 and 41 of the Canada National Parks Act**.
- **Does NOT apply**:
  - In the **Town of Banff** or **Town of Jasper** (these are excluded due to separate municipal waste systems).
- **Exclusions**:
  - Trade waste accumulation is allowed only under a permit (see below).
  - Yard rubbish is restricted from sidewalks/streets (except during designated collection days).

---

## üì¶ **Provisions on Garbage Handling and Storage**

### 1. **Container Requirements**
- Owners must:
  - Provide and maintain **sufficient, serviceable, sanitary containers**.
  - Use only containers listed in **Column I of Schedule I** and meeting the **specifications in Column II**.
  - **Drain and wrap wet garbage** before placing it in containers.

### 2. **Storage Between Collections**
- **In Banff, Glacier, Jasper, Kootenay, Mount Revelstoke, and Yoho National Parks**:
  - Garbage must be stored in **self-dumping containers** that meet the specifications in **Schedule I, Item 4, Column II**.
- **In all other parks**:
  - Must be stored in an **approved enclosure** or in **steel refuse containers** (Schedule I, Item 2, Column II).

### 3. **Preparation for Collection**
- **Combustible rubbish** must be securely tied in bundles as directed by the Superintendent.
- **Yard rubbish** must be prepared according to Superintendent‚Äôs instructions.
- **Ashes/coals** must be extinguished before collection.
- **Bulky waste** may only be set out during times designated by the Superintendent.
- **Liquid, trade, special, animal, and construction/demolition wastes** are **prohibited** from being set out for collection by the Superintendent.

### 4. **Street and Premises Cleanliness**
- Owners must **keep streets, sidewalks, and boulevards adjacent to their premises free of garbage**.
- No garbage may be discarded or deposited anywhere in a park **except where authorized** by the Superintendent.

## üöö **Collection and Transport Rules**

- The Superintendent may:
  - Determine **which portions of a park** and **when** garbage will be collected.
  - Post **signs at disposal areas** requiring a **permit** for delivery of solid or liquid waste.
- **Permit Requirement**:
  - Any person delivering waste to a disposal area must have a permit from the Superintendent.
  - Permit holders must **show the permit** upon request.
- **Vehicle Requirements**:
  - Garbage must be transported in vehicles that are **properly constructed and covered** to prevent spillage.
  - If garbage escapes, the **owner of the vehicle is responsible for immediate cleanup**.

---

## üöÆ **Yard Rubbish Regulations**

- **Prohibited**: Discarding yard rubbish on sidewalks or streets.
- **Exception**: On days designated by the Superintendent, yard rubbish may be deposited on sidewalks/streets **only if it does not impede pedestrian or vehicular traffic**.

---

## üìù **Permit to Accumulate Trade Waste**

- Owners may accumulate **trade waste** (e.g., scrap metal, machinery, vehicles) **only if**:
  - They apply to the Superintendent and receive a written permit.
- The permit must specify:
  - Type and nature of trade waste.
  - Location and method of accumulation.
  - Method of removal.
- **Permits are denied** if the Superintendent has **reasonable grounds** to believe that accumulation would:
  - Harm the park‚Äôs appearance, natural resources, or amenity.
  - Affect the safety, comfort, or enjoyment of residents or visitors.

## üí∞ **Charges and Fees for Garbage Collection & Disposal**

Charges are based on **volume, type of property, and usage**. The following formulas apply:

---

### ‚úÖ **General Formula (for most lots)**  
For owners of lots in parks with garbage collection services:

> **Charge = (Lot Volume Value) √ó (Total Operating & Maintenance Cost / Total Volume Value)**

Where:
- **Lot Volume Value** = sum of volume values of units on the lot √ó (months of occupancy / 12)
- **Total Operating & Maintenance Cost** = annual cost of collecting and disposing of garbage in the park
- **Total Volume Value** = sum of all lot volume values in the park

> üí° This is a **volume-based fee**, ensuring that users pay according to their actual garbage output.
