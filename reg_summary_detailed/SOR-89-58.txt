**Detailed Summary of the Fresh Fruits and Vegetables (United States Tariff) Import Regulations**

---

### **Regulatory Background and Legal Authority**

The *Fresh Fruits and Vegetables (United States Tariff) Import Regulations* were established by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, pursuant to **subsection 60.2(10)** of the *Customs Tariff*. This regulatory instrument grants certain fresh fruits and vegetables the benefit of the **United States Tariff**, allowing them to be imported into Canada under preferential tariff treatment.

- **Effective Date**: January 1, 1989  
- **Statutory Citation**:  
  - S.C. 1988, c. 65, s. 97  
  - S.C. 1987, c. 49  
- **Regulation Title**: *Fresh Fruits and Vegetables (United States Tariff) Import Regulations*  
- **Purpose**: To regulate the importation of specific fresh fruits and vegetables that are eligible for preferential tariff treatment under a bilateral agreement with the United States.

---

### **Scope of the Regulations**

These regulations apply specifically to **fresh fruits and vegetables** that fall within certain tariff headings and subheadings of the *Customs Tariff* and are entitled to the **benefit of the United States Tariff**. This preferential treatment reduces or eliminates import duties on these goods when imported into Canada.

#### **Eligible Goods (Tariff Headings and Subheadings)**

The following products are included:

- **Heading 07.01 to 07.09** (excluding turnips)  
  - Includes: Apples, pears, grapes, citrus fruits, berries, and other fresh fruits and vegetables (e.g., onions, carrots, potatoes, etc.)  
  - **Exclusion**: Turnips are excluded from this category.

- **Heading 08.09 and 08.10** (excluding cranberries and blueberries)  
  - Includes: Other fresh vegetables such as beets, radishes, and leafy greens  
  - **Exclusion**: Cranberries and blueberries are excluded from this category.

- **Subheadings 0806.10 and 0808.20**  
  - These subheadings cover specific types of fresh vegetables (e.g., certain root vegetables or leafy greens) eligible for preferential treatment.

> **Note**: The regulations define "goods" as any fresh fruit or vegetable falling under these specific classifications that are entitled to the United States Tariff.

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Container**  
   - Refers to any receptacle, package, wrapper, or confining band used in the transportation or storage of the goods.  
   - Examples: crates, boxes, plastic bags, netting, or any enclosing material.

2. **Goods**  
   - Defined as fresh fruits or vegetables classified under the specified tariff headings/subheadings that qualify for the United States Tariff.

3. **Point of Production**  
   - Refers to the **state of the United States**, the **District of Columbia**, or **Puerto Rico** where the goods were produced.  
   - This is a critical element for determining eligibility and verifying origin under the preferential tariff regime.

---

### **Accounting and Documentation Requirements**

Every person who accounts for goods under **subsection 32(1)** of the *Customs Act* or who makes an **interim accounting** under **subsection 32(2)** of that Act is required to provide a **detailed statement in duplicate**, signed by both the **vendor** and the **purchaser** (or their authorized representatives), **before the goods are released** for entry into Canada.

#### **Mandatory Information in the Statement**

The statement must include the following details for **each container** in the shipment:

1. **Names and Addresses**  
   - Vendor  
   - Purchaser  
   - Ultimate consignee  
   - Authorized representatives of each party  

2. **Carrier Information**  
   - Name and address of the carrier  
   - Identification number of the conveyance (e.g., truck, ship, or aircraft) transporting the goods  

3. **Description of Goods**  
   - Full description of the goods  
   - The specific tariff item (heading or subheading) under which the goods are classified  

4. **Weight and Container Details**  
   - Weight of the goods in each container  
   - Number and size of all containers in the shipment  

5. **Point of Production**  
   - Must clearly state the U.S. state, D.C., or Puerto Rico where the goods were produced  

6. **Value for Duty**  
   - Determined in accordance with the *Customs Act*  
   - This is the value used to calculate customs duties (if any)  

7. **F.O.B. Purchase Price**  
   - The price at which the goods were purchased, **Free On Board** (F.O.B.) the point of shipment in the U.S.  
   - This is a key value benchmark for documentation and compliance  

8. **Delivered Price (if available)**  
   - The price at which the goods were delivered to the purchaser (if known)  
   - Not mandatory if unavailable, but must be noted if provided  

9. **Dates**  
   - Date of purchase of the goods  
   - Date of shipment from the point of production  

---

### **Purpose and Implications**

- **Preferential Tariff Treatment**: The regulations allow Canadian importers to benefit from reduced or eliminated tariffs on certain U.S.-origin fresh produce, promoting trade and economic efficiency between Canada and the United States.

- **Transparency and Accountability**: The detailed accounting requirements ensure that:
  - The origin of goods is verifiable (to confirm eligibility)
  - All transactional data is transparent and traceable
  - Customs authorities can verify compliance with origin rules and tariff obligations

- **Compliance Mechanism**: The requirement for a **dual-signed statement** (by vendor and purchaser) strengthens accountability and reduces the risk of fraud or misrepresentation.

- **Enforcement**: Failure to provide the required documentation or to provide false information may result in penalties, denial of importation, or customs audits.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Fresh Fruits and Vegetables (United States Tariff) Import Regulations* establish a clear, structured framework for the importation of eligible fresh produce from the United States into Canada. By specifying eligible products, defining key terms, and mandating detailed accounting documentation, the regulations ensure that preferential tariff treatment is applied fairly, transparently, and in compliance with Canadian customs law.
