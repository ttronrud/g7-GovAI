**Detailed Summary of the Radiocommunication Act Exemption Order (Jammers — Royal Canadian Mounted Police)**  
*Issued by the Minister of Industry under subsection 14(1)a of the Radiocommunication Act*  
*Ottawa, May 17, 2024*  
*Replaces SOR/2019-269; Comes into force upon registration*

---

### **Overview**

This regulation, known as the **Radiocommunication Act Exemption Order (Jammers — Royal Canadian Mounted Police)**, grants specific exemptions from key provisions of the *Radiocommunication Act* (RCA) to enable the **Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)** and certain associated government entities to legally install, use, possess, manufacture, import, distribute, or sell **radio frequency jammers** — devices that intentionally interfere with or block radio communications — for authorized and critical purposes.

The exemption is narrowly tailored to ensure national security, public safety, and law enforcement operations, while still respecting the regulatory framework designed to prevent unauthorized interference with radiocommunication services.

---

## **Key Provisions and Exemptions**

### **1. Who Is Exempt?**

The following individuals and entities are exempt from the application of **subsection 4(4)** and **paragraph 9(1)(b)** of the Radiocommunication Act:

#### **(1) RCMP Employees (Technical Investigation Services & Other Units)**
- Employees of the **RCMP Technical Investigation Services (TIS)** and other RCMP employees who are required to:
  - Install, use, possess, manufacture, import, distribute, or sell jammers
  - In the course of their **duties or training**
- Are exempt from the restrictions in subsection 4(4) and paragraph 9(1)(b) of the Act.

> *Note:* This exemption applies only when the activity is directly related to official duties or training.

#### **(2) The Crown (via the Minister of Public Works and Government Services)**
- The **Minister of Public Works and Government Services (MPWGS)** and employees of the Department of Public Works and Government Services (DPWGS) who are required to:
  - Import jammers **on behalf of the RCMP**
- Are exempt from subsection 4(4) of the Act in respect of that importation.

> *Purpose:* Enables procurement and logistics support for RCMP jammer operations without violating radiocommunication regulations.

#### **(3) Contractors and Vendors in Government Contracts**
- Any **person or entity** that:
  - Has entered into a contract with the Government of Canada to provide jammers or related services to the RCMP
  - Performs work under that contract or a related subcontract
- Is exempt from subsection 4(4) and paragraph 9(1)(b) of the Act **if** they are required to:
  - Install, use, possess, manufacture, import, distribute, offer for sale, or sell a jammer
  - In accordance with the terms of the contract

> *This includes subcontractors and suppliers involved in the supply chain.*

#### **(4) Bidding Process for Jammers**
- A person or entity may offer a jammer for sale **without violating subsection 4(4)** if:
  - The offer is made in response to a **formal solicitation of bids** (e.g., a government procurement process)
  - The bid is submitted to the Government of Canada for provision to the RCMP

> *Purpose:* Allows competitive bidding for jammer procurement without triggering regulatory barriers.

---

## **Purpose of the Exemption**

The exemptions are granted solely for the following **authorized purposes**:

1. **National Security**  
2. **Public Safety**  
   - Including operations at **penitentiaries and prisons**  
3. **International Relations**  
4. **Investigation or Prosecution of Offences**  
   - Including the **preservation of evidence**  
5. **Protection of Property or Prevention of Serious Harm to Persons**

> These purposes are strictly defined and serve as the legal justification for the use of jammers — ensuring that such tools are not used for unauthorized or general interference.

---

## **Conditions and Requirements for Compliance**

To ensure responsible and lawful use, several **mandatory conditions** apply:

### **A. Notification and Reporting Requirements (RCMP Obligations)**

Before any exemption under subsection (1) is activated, the **RCMP must provide written notice to the Minister** containing:

- Postal and email addresses, and telephone numbers of:
  - The **headquarters or centre of operations** where jammer responsibilities are primarily exercised
  - The **contact persons** at that location
  - The **resource persons** responsible for jammers

> *This ensures transparency and accountability.*

Additionally, the RCMP must:

- Provide **updated information** as soon as feasible when any of the above details change
- **Confirm the accuracy** of the information annually, by the anniversary of the Order’s coming into force

---

### **B. Training Requirement**

- Every employee carrying out an activity under the exemption in subsection (1) must:
  - Have received or be receiving **specialized training** in relation to the use of jammers

> *Ensures that only qualified personnel operate such high-risk equipment.*

---

### **C. Access to Directives and Procedures**

- The RCMP must ensure that:
  - TIS employees have access to **TIS-specific directives** on jammers
  - Other employees have access to the **directives of their respective operational units** regarding jammer use

> *Promotes consistency and adherence to internal operational standards.*

---

### **D. Minimization of Interference and Exposure**

All users must:

- **Minimize** the jammer’s interference to:
  - The **smallest physical area**
  - The **fewest radio frequencies**
  - The **appropriate power level**
  - The **minimum duration** required to achieve the intended purpose

- **Minimize unwanted emissions** and **exposure of persons to radiofrequency (RF) fields**

> *Protects civilian and public communications and health.*

---

### **E. Flexibility and Control of Jammers**

- Any jammer used under the exemption must:
  - Allow for **adjustable power levels**
  - Allow for **adjustable radio frequencies** that can be interfered with

> *Enables precise, targeted use and prevents overreach or broad-spectrum disruption.*

---

### **F. Security and Access Controls**

- Jammers must be:
  - **Accessible only** to employees or entities **exempt under the Act**
  - **Turned off and stored securely** when not in use
  - Stored in a **secure location or manner**, including during transportation

> *Prevents unauthorized access or misuse.*

---

### **G. Recordkeeping Requirements**

The RCMP must maintain detailed records for **each use of a jammer**, including:

- Name of the employee or entity using the jammer  
- Location, date, and (if possible) time of use  
- Radio frequencies interfered with or obstructed  
- Purpose of use (from the list in subsection 2(7))

> *Ensures accountability, auditability, and compliance with oversight.*

---

### **H. Restrictions on Use by Contractors and Vendors**

A person or entity referred to in subsection (6) (contractors) may **only use a jammer** under the following conditions:

1. The use must occur **under the direct supervision of an RCMP employee** referred to in subsection (1)  
2. The purpose must be one of:
   - Testing the jammer’s functionality  
   - Performing maintenance  
   - Providing training on jammers  

> *Prevents independent or unregulated use of jammers by third parties.*

---

## **Repeal of Previous Order**

- This Order **repeals the previous exemption order** (SOR/2019-269)  
- Ensures that the current, updated, and more detailed framework governs all future jammer-related activities involving the RCMP.

---

## **Effective Date**

- The Order comes into force on the **day it is registered** (i.e., upon official publication in the *Canada Gazette*).

---

## **Key Takeaways and Implications**

| Aspect | Summary |
|-------|--------|
| **Legal Basis** | Section 14(1)a of the Radiocommunication Act |
| **Scope** | Limited to RCMP, its contractors, and authorized government departments |
| **Purpose** | National security, public safety, law enforcement, evidence preservation |
| **Compliance** | Requires training, reporting, recordkeeping, and strict operational controls |
| **Safety & Accountability** | Emphasis on minimizing interference, protecting public health, and ensuring transparency |
| **No General Authorization** | Jammers are not available for public or commercial use — only for authorized, purpose-driven operations |

---

## **Conclusion**

This exemption order represents a **carefully balanced regulatory exception** to the otherwise strict rules governing radio frequency interference in Canada. It allows the RCMP to use jammers in critical investigative and operational contexts — such as preventing terrorist communications, securing prison environments, or preserving evidence — while maintaining strong safeguards to:

- Prevent misuse  
- Protect public communications  
- Ensure accountability  
- Limit exposure to harmful RF fields  

By requiring detailed reporting, training, supervision, and technical controls, the Order ensures that the use of jammers remains both **legally defensible** and **operationally responsible**.

This is a significant example of how regulatory frameworks can adapt to meet the needs of national security and law enforcement, while still upholding public interest and technical standards.
