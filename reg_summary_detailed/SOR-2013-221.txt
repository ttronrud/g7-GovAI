## ðŸ“Œ **Overview of the Regulation**

### **Purpose**
The *Electronic Commerce Protection Regulations* aim to:
- Promote the use of electronic means in commercial activities by **reducing the prevalence of unsolicited commercial electronic messages (UCEMs)**.
- Ensure that electronic communication in commerce is efficient, transparent, and respectful of individuals' privacy.
- Balance the interests of businesses and consumers by establishing clear rules around **consent, opt-out mechanisms, and exemptions** for legitimate or necessary messaging.

This regulation implements **Section 6 of the Act**, which prohibits unsolicited commercial electronic messages (e.g., spam), while providing **specific exemptions** for messages that are legitimate, consensual, or required by law.

### 1. **Definitions in the Regulations**

#### âœ… *Family Relationship*
- Exists between two individuals if:
  - They are related by **marriage, common-law partnership**, or **parent-child relationship**.
  - They have had **direct, voluntary, two-way communication**.

> *Purpose:* To recognize close personal ties where unsolicited messages may be inappropriate or unnecessary.

#### âœ… *Personal Relationship*
- Exists if:
  - Two individuals have had **direct, voluntary, two-way communication**.
  - It would be **reasonable to conclude** they have a personal relationship based on shared interests, experiences, opinions, frequency, duration, or in-person meetings.

> *Purpose:* To allow for a flexible, context-based determination of personal connections beyond formal family ties.

### 3. **First Commercial Message Exception**

> Paragraph 6(1)(a) of the Act does **not apply** to the **first commercial electronic message** sent by a person **after a referral** from someone who has a qualifying relationship with either the sender or the recipient.

#### Qualifying Relationships:
- Existing business relationship  
- Existing non-business relationship  
- Family relationship  
- Personal relationship  

#### Conditions:
- The message must:
  - Be sent **after a referral** from someone with one of these relationships.
  - Disclose the **full names of the individuals who made the referral**.
  - Clearly state that the message is sent **as a result of the referral**.

> *Purpose:* To encourage referrals and build trust in commercial interactions without penalizing the first contact made through a personal connection.

### 4. **Consent and Withdrawal Rules (Section 10 of the Act)**

#### âœ… Express Consent on Behalf of an Unknown Person

If a person obtains **express consent** from someone whose identity is unknown, they may authorize others to use that consent **under specific conditions**:

1. The **consenter must be identified** in every commercial message.
2. The **authorized sender must provide** an unsubscribe mechanism that:
   - Meets all requirements under **Section 11** of the Act.
   - Allows the original person to **withdraw consent** from the person who obtained it or any authorized user.

#### âœ… Notification Requirements Upon Withdrawal

When an authorized person receives a withdrawal of consent:
- They must **immediately notify** the person who obtained the consent.
- The person who obtained consent must then:
  - Notify the **original consenter**.
  - Notify the **authorized sender**.
  - Notify **any other authorized user** who used the consent.

> *Purpose:* To ensure **transparency and accountability** in consent management, especially when consent is granted indirectly.

#### âœ… Timely Notification of Withdrawal

- The person who obtained consent must **without delay** inform any person referred to in paragraph (2)(c) of the Act (likely referring to a third party or recipient) of the withdrawal.

> *Purpose:* To protect the privacy and autonomy of individuals and ensure timely revocation of consent.

#### âœ… Effect of Withdrawal

- The withdrawal must be **implemented immediately** in accordance with **subsection 11(3)** of the Act.
- If applicable, the person referred to in paragraph (2)(c) must also give effect to the withdrawal.

> *Purpose:* To ensure that consent is not used indefinitely and that individuals can control their data.

### 5. **Exemptions for Specific Programs (Subparagraph 10(8)(a)(vi))**

Certain **software or security programs** installed by telecommunications service providers are exempt from restrictions on data use or message transmission:

| Exempt Program Type | Description |
|--------------------|-------------|
| **Security programs** | Installed solely to protect network security from **current and identifiable threats** to availability, reliability, or efficiency. |
| **Upgrade/Update programs** | Installed to update or upgrade network systems. |
| **Failure correction programs** | Installed solely to **correct a failure** in network operation or software. |

> *Purpose:* To allow service providers to maintain network integrity and performance without violating electronic commerce rules.

### 6. **Definition of Membership (Paragraph 10(13)(c))**

- **Membership** means being accepted as a member of a club, association, or voluntary organization **in accordance with its membership requirements**.

> *Purpose:* To define who qualifies as a member for purposes of receiving communications.
