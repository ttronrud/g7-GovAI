**Detailed Summary of the *Protection of Residential Mortgage or Hypothecary Insurance Regulations* (S.C. 2011, c. 15, s. 20)**

---

### **Overview**

These regulations, made under the *Protection of Residential Mortgage or Hypothecary Insurance Act* (the "Act"), establish detailed requirements for **qualified mortgage lenders**, **approved mortgage insurers**, and **reinsurance practices** in Canada. The primary objective is to ensure **financial stability, transparency, and consumer protection** in the residential mortgage and hypothecary insurance market by setting strict eligibility and operational standards.

The regulations came into force on the day that **Section 20 of the Supporting Vulnerable Seniors and Strengthening Canadaâ€™s Economy Act** came into force, or, if registered later, on the date of registration. As of January 1, 2013, these regulations were in force (as noted in SI/2012-87).

## **1. Qualification of Mortgage Lenders**

A mortgage or hypothecary lender must meet one of the following criteria to be designated a **qualified mortgage lender**:

### **(a) General Criteria (Section 3(1))**
- Must be a **corporation** whose **articles of incorporation do not restrict its ability to lend** in the jurisdictions where it operates.

### **(b) Specific Types of Entities (Section 3(1)(b))**
The lender must be one of the following:
1. **A financially sound institution** with:
   - At least **$3,000,000 in unencumbered paid-up capital**
   - Incorporated by or under an Act of **Parliament or a provincial legislature**

2. **A federal financial institution** or **authorized foreign bank**, as defined in **Section 2 of the Bank Act**

3. **A trust, loan, or insurance corporation** incorporated and regulated by a provincial legislature

4. **A cooperative credit society** incorporated and regulated by a provincial legislature

> âœ… *Note:* A cooperative credit society **does not need to meet the underwriting or administration requirements** under subsections (2) and (3) if it was **already designated as a qualified mortgage lender** under an agreement defined in **Section 43 of the Act** before these regulations came into force.

## **2. Requirements to Underwrite Loans**

To **underwrite** residential mortgage or hypothecary loans, a lender must meet **additional criteria** beyond being a qualified lender:

- Must have:
  - **At least 3 years of experience** underwriting such loans in Canada
  - **Capability and resources** to underwrite and commit to such loans

**OR**

- Be a **subsidiary** of a parent corporation that:
  - Is a qualified mortgage lender
  - Meets the criteria in (a) above
  - **Undertakes to underwrite** the subsidiaryâ€™s loans in Canada
  - Is **accountable to the approved mortgage insurer** for performance on those loans

**OR**

- Have:
  - **At least $5,000,000 in paid-up capital**
  - At least **two mortgage officers**, each with:
    - **Minimum of 10 years of underwriting experience**
    - Responsible for underwriting all residential mortgage and hypothecary loans in Canada

> âš ï¸ *Note:* The underwriting experience must be in **residential mortgage or hypothecary loans** and must be directly relevant to the lenderâ€™s operations.

## **4. Reinsurance Provisions**

An **approved mortgage insurer** may only:

- **Cause itself to be reinsured** against a risk it has underwritten **if the reinsurer is also an approved mortgage insurer**

## **5. Loan Replacement Restrictions**

An approved mortgage insurer **must not replace** an insured mortgage or hypothecary loan with another loan under the same insurance coverage **unless**:

1. **Both loans are made to the same borrower**, and  
   â†’ The new loan is intended to **discharge the outstanding balance** of the original loan (i.e., refinancing)

2. **The new loan is part of a loan workout** designed to:
   - Reduce or avoid losses on a real or potential insurance claim

## **6. Commitment to Insure a Portfolio**

An approved mortgage insurer that makes a **commitment to insure a portfolio of loans up to a specified total value** must:

- **Not insure any individual loan** under that commitment **later than one year after the commitment is made**

> â±ï¸ Ensures that commitments are acted upon within a reasonable timeframe, preventing long-term delays in underwriting.

## **7. Prescribed Relationship (Section 13(1)(c))**

A person or entity is considered to be in a **prescribed relationship** with an approved mortgage insurer if:

- The mortgage insurer and/or its controlled entities (under Section 3 of the *Insurance Companies Act*) **beneficially own more than 20% of the voting shares** of a qualified mortgage lender, **OR**
- Another person/entity and its controlled entities:
  - Beneficially own **more than 20% of the voting shares** of the qualified mortgage lender, **AND**
  - Also beneficially own **more than 20% of the voting shares** of the approved mortgage insurer

> ğŸ” This creates a **conflict-of-interest threshold** â€” if ownership exceeds 20%, the relationship is flagged for regulatory scrutiny.

## **8. Deemed Joint Ownership (Section 5)**

Two or more persons/entities are **deemed to be a single person or entity** for ownership purposes if they:

- Have **agreed (formally or informally)** to act **jointly or in concert** in respect of:
  - Shares in a qualified mortgage lender or approved mortgage insurer  
  - Shares in any entity that owns shares in a qualified mortgage lender or approved mortgage insurer  
  - Shares in any entity that controls such an entity

- Agreements to **veto** board proposals
- Agreements that **no proposal may be approved without consent** from one or more parties

> âŒ **Exception:**  
Ownership is **not presumed** to be joint or in concert simply because:
- One is a proxyholder of another  
- They vote in the same way (e.g., all vote "yes" or "no")
