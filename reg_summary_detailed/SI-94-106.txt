### **Regulation Summary: Minister of Communications (Library and Archives of Canada) Authority to Prescribe Fees Order**

#### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
- The regulation is issued under **paragraph 19(1)(b) of the Financial Administration Act (FAA)**.
- It is approved by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of:
  - The **Minister of Communications**
  - The **Treasury Board**
- This action reflects a formal administrative process where the federal government delegates authority to prescribe fees through a legislative-style order.

#### **2. Revocation of Previous Order**
- The regulation **revokes** the prior Order in Council:
  - **P.C. 1989-1595**, dated **August 24, 1989**
  - Officially published in the *Canada Gazette, Part II*, on **page 4000** (SI/89-181)
- This earlier order granted the Minister of Communications the authority to prescribe fees for users of the **National Archives of Canada**.
- The revocation signifies a **change in administrative structure and responsibility**, specifically renaming and updating the institution from the "National Archives of Canada" to the **Library and Archives of Canada (LAC)**.

#### **3. New Authority Established**
- The new regulation establishes the **Minister of Communications (Library and Archives of Canada) Authority to Prescribe Fees Order**.
- This Order grants the **Minister of Communications** the power to:
  - **Prescribe fees** for users or **classes of users** of any service or facility provided by the **Library and Archives of Canada (LAC)**.
- The authority is **not limited to specific services** but applies broadly to all services and facilities operated by LAC.

#### **4. Scope of Fees**
- The fees are to be applied to:
  - Users of **services** (e.g., document access, research assistance)
  - Users of **facilities** (e.g., archival storage, reading rooms, public exhibitions)
  - Specific **classes of users** (e.g., individuals, institutions, researchers, corporations)

#### **5. Fee Determination Framework**
- Fees must be determined in accordance with the **Treasury Board Policy on External User Charges**:
  - Original policy issued in **August 1986**
  - Revised in **December 1989**
- This policy provides a **standardized, transparent, and financially responsible framework** for setting user charges, ensuring that:
  - Fees are **reasonable and justified**
  - They reflect **cost recovery** and **service value**
  - They are **consistent with public accountability and fiscal responsibility**

#### **6. Citation and Reference**
- The new Order may be cited as:
  > **Minister of Communications (Library and Archives of Canada) Authority to Prescribe Fees Order**

#### **7. Purpose and Implications**
- **Modernization**: Reflects the rebranding and reorganization of the institution from the "National Archives of Canada" to the **Library and Archives of Canada**, which better reflects its broader mandate (including library services and cultural heritage).
- **Accountability and Transparency**: By aligning with Treasury Board policy, the fee structure becomes more accountable, transparent, and aligned with federal financial governance standards.
- **Flexibility**: Allows the Minister to adapt fees over time based on changing operational costs, demand, or service delivery needs—without requiring new legislation.
