**Detailed Summary of the Investment Limits (Cooperative Credit Associations) Regulations**

---

### **Overview**

The *Investment Limits (Cooperative Credit Associations) Regulations*, made under sections 389a, 403a, and 463b of the *Cooperative Credit Associations Act* (the "Act"), establish regulatory limits on the extent to which cooperative credit associations (CCAs) may invest in real property and other assets. These rules are designed to ensure financial stability, reduce systemic risk, and protect members and depositors by imposing prudent investment and capital adequacy requirementsâ€”particularly in relation to real estate exposure.

The regulations came into force on **October 24, 2001**, following the enactment of sections 314 and 339 of the *Financial Consumer Agency of Canada Act*, S.C. 2001, c. 9.

## **Exemption from Sections 401 and 402**

Sections 401 and 402 of the Act impose investment limits on CCAs based on their exposure to real property and equity. However:

> **If the sum of the associationâ€™s equity and minority interests in subsidiaries (as reported in consolidated financial statements) is $5 billion or more**, then **sections 401 and 402 do not apply**.

This provides a threshold exemption for large, well-capitalized associations, recognizing that they are more resilient and better able to manage risks.

---

## **Prescribed Subsidiaries**

For the purposes of investment limits, a *prescribed subsidiary* is a subsidiary of the association *excluding*:
- Insurance companies;
- Securities dealers;
- Subsidiaries of insurance or securities dealers.

This means that CCAs can have significant exposure to other types of subsidiaries (e.g., real estate development firms, property management companies) without triggering the investment limitsâ€”provided they are not in the regulated financial sectors.

## **Key Investment Ratios: Equity Percentage and Real Property Percentage**

These ratios determine how much of the associationâ€™s regulatory capital may be allocated to real property investments.

### **1. Real Property Percentage**
- Formula:  
  $$
  \text{Real Property Percentage} = \frac{\text{Total value of all interests in real property (excluding those excluded by section 397)}}{\text{Regulatory Capital}}
  $$
- Includes:
  - Real property beneficially owned;
  - Debt obligations issued for acquisition or improvement of real property;
  - Interests in related real property entities (RRPEs) and joint ventures;
  - Debt obligations or loans secured by real property in RRPEs or third parties.

### **2. Equity Percentage**
- Formula:  
  $$
  \text{Equity Percentage} = \frac{\text{Total value of participating shares and ownership interests (excluding those in "permitted entities" or excluded by section 397)}}{\text{Regulatory Capital}}
  $$
- Excludes:
  - Participating shares in permitted entities where the association has a substantial investment;
  - Ownership interests excluded under section 397.

## **Valuation of Interests in Real Property**

The value of an interest in real property is determined based on **book value** as reported in the associationâ€™s consolidated balance sheet under the Superintendentâ€™s accounting standards (subsection 292(4)).

### **Types of Interests in Real Property**

1. **Direct Ownership**
   - Real property beneficially owned by the association or a designated entity.
   - Value = Book value of real property.

2. **Debt Obligations for Real Property**
   - Debt issued to acquire or improve real property.
   - Value = Book value of debt *minus* book value of real property (i.e., excess).

3. **Interests in Related Real Property Entities (RRPEs)**
   - Real property held by a joint venture or RRPE (controlled by the association or a designated entity).
   - Value = Book value of real property.
   - Shares/ownership interests in RRPEs = Book value of shares/interests.

4. **Loans or Debt Obligations to RRPEs or Controlled Entities**
   - Loans to RRPEs or to entities where a securities dealer or insurance company controlled by the association has a substantial investment.
   - Value = Book value of the loan.

5. **Guarantees or Agreements**
   - **Guarantee** to complete development of real property owned by the association, a designated entity, or an RRPE.
     - Value = Estimated cost of completing development.
   - **Agreement to support operating/financing costs**.
     - Value = Amount of funds advanced.

6. **Secured Loans or Debt Obligations (Third Party Secured)**
   - A loan or debt obligation secured by real property or ownership interests of a third party.
   - Value = **Lesser of**:
     - Net realizable value of the third partyâ€™s interest in the real property or shares;
     - A calculated formula based on:
       - Book value of the loan or guarantee (if held by or guaranteed by the association);
       - Total net realizable value of other security interests;
       - Value of the underlying real property;
       - Total value of outstanding shares/interests.

   > This ensures that the value of a secured interest is not overstated and reflects actual market value.

## **Special Provisions: Interests in Real Property from Realization of Security Interests**

Under subsection 11(3) of the Act, certain interests in real property arise when a security interest is realized on a loan or debt obligation.

These include:
- Real property acquired through the realization of a security interest;
- Debt obligations issued to finance real property acquisition or improvement.

Their value is calculated as per the rules above.

---

## **Exclusions and Special Rules**

- **Section 397 Exclusions**: Certain interests in real property are excluded from valuation (e.g., shares in permitted entities with substantial investment). These are not included in real property percentage or equity percentage calculations.
- **Permitted Entities**: Entities in which the association has a substantial investment are exempt from being counted in equity or real property percentages if they are deemed "permitted" under section 397.
