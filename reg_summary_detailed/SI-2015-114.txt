**Detailed Summary of the Regulation on the Procedure of the Court of Québec (O.C. 1099-2015)**

---

### **Overview and Purpose**

This regulation, approved by the *Gouvernement du Québec* on December 9, 2015, through an Order in Council made by the *Conseil exécutif*, establishes detailed procedural rules governing the administration and operation of the **Court of Québec**, in accordance with the following key statutes:

- **Code of Civil Procedure (C-25.01)**  
- **Criminal Code (R.S.C. 1985, c. C-46)**  
- **Code of Penal Procedure (C-25.1)**  
- **Youth Criminal Justice Act (S.C. 2002, c. 1)**  
- **Youth Protection Act (P-34.1)**  
- **Civil Code of Québec (provisions on adoption)**

The regulation is published in the *Canada Gazette* under subsections **482(4)** and **482.1(6)** of the Criminal Code, affirming its legal authority and nationwide applicability within Québec.

---

### **Scope of Application**

- Applies to **all judicial districts of Québec**, unless specific rules are established for the **District of Québec** or **Montreal**.
- Ensures **proper compliance with procedural rules**, efficient case management, and the **effective functioning of each division** of the Court of Québec (Civil, Criminal, Penal, and Youth Divisions).
- Aims to promote **fairness, transparency, efficiency, and accessibility** in judicial proceedings.

---

### **Key Provisions and Detailed Explanations**

#### **1. Flexibility in Rule Application**
- Judges may **modify rules** or **exempt parties** from procedural requirements based on the **specific circumstances** of a case.
- This ensures procedural adaptability while maintaining consistency with justice principles.

#### **2. Information Technology (IT) and Digital Access**
- The regulation extends traditional procedural terms (e.g., *pleading*, *exhibit*, *transcript*, *filing*) to include **technology-based versions** and **digital access**.
- Courts may adopt digital tools for:
  - Document filing
  - Testimony
  - Recordkeeping
- **Technology-based documents** must:
  - Allow **keyword searches** as a core function
  - Include an **index with hyperlinks** if multiple documents are filed in one file
  - Disclose **all functions** of the document to ensure transparency and prevent unintended bias or interference in court operations

#### **3. Office Hours**
- Court offices are open **Monday to Friday (excluding holidays)** from **8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.**
- Extended hours may occur when the court is sitting.

#### **4. Custody and Management of Court Records**
- All **registers, records, orders, and judgments** required under applicable laws must be **kept in court offices**.
- Custody and access are governed by the **directives of the Chief Judge**.
- Special rules apply to records under:
  - The **Youth Protection Act (P-34.1)**
  - The **Youth Criminal Justice Act (S.C. 2002, c. 1)**
  - **Adoption provisions** in the *Civil Code of Québec*
- These records must be stored in accordance with **Schedule I** of the regulation.

#### **5. Access to Records and Exhibits**
- Any person may access court records during office hours, **subject to legislative provisions or judicial orders**.
- Specific procedures govern:
  - **Access conditions**
  - **Removal of exhibits from records**
- These procedures vary by division of the court (e.g., civil, criminal, youth).

#### **6. Contact Information Requirements**
- **Parties and their lawyers/notaries** must provide and **keep current** their contact details (name, address, postal code, phone, email, fax).
- For **unrepresented parties**, especially in small claims matters, full contact information must be provided.
- Changes must be reported **immediately** to the court office.
- For lawyers, a **change in the master file** is sufficient.

#### **7. Format and Typeface for Pleadings**
- All pleadings must be:
  - Printed on **one side only** of a **letter-sized sheet (21.5 × 28 cm / 8½ × 11 inches)**
  - Using **Arial 12-point font**
- Handwritten pleadings must be **legible**
- **Reverse side or back** must include:
  - Record number
  - Names of parties
  - Nature/object of the pleading
  - Amount in dispute (if applicable)
- For lawyers/notaries:
  - Name, address, postal code, phone, fax, email, and **permanent court number**
- For unrepresented parties:
  - Full contact information (name, address, postal code, phone, email, fax)

#### **8. Signature Requirements**
- Every pleading must be **signed** by:
  - The party
  - The party’s lawyer or notary
  - Or the lawyer/notary’s partnership
- In **small claims proceedings**, the pleading must be signed by the party or their **mandatary** (agent)

#### **9. Designation of Parties**
- The **order and designation** of parties in pleadings must **match the original application** to maintain consistency and clarity.

#### **10. Exhibits**
- Exhibits must be:
  - **Enumerated and identified** in a list
  - Numbered with a **classification code** using a letter prefix per party (e.g., "A1", "B2")
  - The same code must be used throughout the proceeding
- The **record number and classification code** must appear on the exhibit or its reverse side

#### **11. Expert Reports**
- In **non-small claims proceedings**, a party submitting an expert report must also provide:
  - The **author’s curriculum vitae**
  - An **invoice** for expert fees up to date and for trial-related fees (if claimed as legal costs)
- This ensures transparency in expert costs and accountability.

#### **12. Filing of Pleadings**
- The **clerk** is responsible for:
  - Numbering the pleading
  - Marking the **date and time of receipt**
  - Entering it into the **court register**

#### **13. Medical and Psychosocial Records in Sealed Envelopes**
- Reports from physicians, psychologists, or social workers (or other psychosocial experts) filed in sealed envelopes:
  - Are **kept confidential**
  - May **only be accessed** by authorized persons with **judicial permission**
  - The **nature of the documents** must be clearly stated on the envelope
- **Access rights** include the right to **make copies at the person’s expense**

#### **14. Record of Access to Sealed Documents**
- The clerk must **record**:
  - The name and title of every person who consults or requests a copy of a sealed document

#### **15. Amendments to Pleadings**
- Changes to pleadings must be:
  - **Underlined** or marked with a **vertical stroke** (for additions)
  - **Dotted lines in parentheses** (for deletions)
- If changes are ordered, a **new pleading** must be filed within the prescribed time, following the same procedure.

#### **16. Technology-Based Documents and Testimony**
- Courts may allow **technology-based documents or testimony** (e.g., video, digital recordings) when feasible.
- Such documents must:
  - Support **keyword searches**
  - Include an **index with hyperlinks** for multi-document files
- Filing parties must **disclose all features** of the technology-based document to avoid operational disruption.

#### **17. Official Version of the Roll**
- The **official version** of the roll (list of parties) is the one **posted in courthouses**.
- In case of discrepancies, the **courthouse version prevails**.

#### **18. Decorum and Conduct in Court**
- The judge may issue orders to maintain:
  - **Serene hearings**
  - **Good order**
  - **Decorum**
  - **Respect for rights of all parties, lawyers, witnesses, and staff**
- **Court bailiffs and special constables** are responsible for:
  - Enforcing decorum
  - Ensuring silence
  - Managing seating arrangements
  - Assisting the judge in applying this regulation and technology guidelines

#### **19. Dress Code**
- All individuals in the courtroom must be **suitably dressed**.
- **Judges (criminal division)**:
  - Wear a **black robe** (closed or with jacket), white shirt, collar and bands, dark clothing, and appropriate footwear
- **Lawyers (criminal division)**:
  - Same attire as judges
- **Lawyers/notaries (civil cases)**:
  - Wear **trousers, jacket, shirt, tie** (men); **skirt or trousers with blouse/jacket or plain dress** (women)
  - **No bands** for articling students
- **Clerks, bailiffs, and officers**:
  - Wear **black robes** with **dark, plain clothing** and **closed footwear**

#### **20. Hearing Schedules**
- Hearings begin at **9:30 a.m. and 2:00 p.m.**, unless otherwise ordered by the judge or chief judge.
