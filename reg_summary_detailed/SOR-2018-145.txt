### **Detailed Summary of the Industrial Hemp Regulations (S.C. 2018, c. 16)**

These regulations, made under **subsection 139(1) of the Cannabis Act**, establish the framework for the legal cultivation, processing, importation, exportation, and commercialization of **industrial hemp** in Canada. Industrial hemp is defined as a cannabis plant â€” or any part of it â€” in which the concentration of **delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol (THC)** is **0.3% w/w or less** in the flowering heads and leaves. Importantly, this determination must account for the **potential conversion of tetrahydrocannabinolic acid (THCA) to THC**, ensuring that the final THC level remains within the legal limit.

## **2. Authorized Activities Under a Licence**

A licence holder may engage in one or more of the following activities, depending on the type of licence:

- Sell industrial hemp  
- Import or export seed or grain  
- Cultivate industrial hemp  
- Propagate industrial hemp (only for plant breeders)  
- Possess seed or grain for cleaning  
- Possess grain for processing  
- Obtain seed by preparing it (e.g., cleaning, drying)

> âœ… **Offering to conduct** an authorized activity is permitted if the licence allows it.

> âœ… **Possession, transfer, transport, or delivery** of industrial hemp is allowed to the extent necessary to perform the licensed activity.

## **5. Import Restrictions**

### **Seed Importers** may only import:
- Seed of **pedigreed status** and **approved cultivar** recognized by:
  - OECD Seed Schemes, or  
  - Association of Official Seed Certifying Agencies (AOSCA)  
- **Plant breeders** may import seed of:
  - A variety listed in their licence  
  - Or germplasm named/numbered in their licence  

### **Grain Importers** may only import from:
- Countries participating in **OECD Seed Schemes**, or  
- Countries with an agency that is a member of **AOSCA**
