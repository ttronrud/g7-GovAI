Certainly. Below is a **detailed, structured summary** of the **Specific Claims Tribunal Rules of Practice and Procedure** (S.C. 2008, c. 22), as adopted on **June 1, 2011**, by a committee of Tribunal members under subsection 12(1) of the *Specific Claims Tribunal Act*.

---

## **Comprehensive Summary of the Specific Claims Tribunal Rules of Practice and Procedure**

### **1. Purpose and Authority of the Rules**
- The Rules were established under **subsection 12(1) of the Specific Claims Tribunal Act (S.C. 2008, c. 22)**.
- The **Chairperson of the Tribunal**, appointed under subsection 6(3) of the Act, has authorized a committee of Tribunal members to develop and adopt these procedural rules.
- The Rules aim to ensure:
  - **Justice**
  - **Timeliness**
  - **Cost-effectiveness**
  - **Cultural sensitivity**  
  in the resolution of specific claims involving First Nations.

> **Core Principle**: The Tribunal must balance procedural efficiency with respect to the **cultural diversity** and **distinctive character** of Indigenous claims.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | The *Specific Claims Tribunal Act* |
| **Chairperson** | The superior court judge appointed to preside over the Tribunal |
| **File** | To submit a document to the Registrar |
| **Legal Counsel** | A member of a provincial or territorial Bar |
| **Letter Size Paper** | 21.5 cm × 28 cm (8½ in × 11 in) |
| **Registrar** | The official responsible for administrative functions under subsection 10(2) of the Act |
| **Representative** | A party’s legal counsel or a person authorized under subrule 7(2) |

---

### **3. General Principles and Flexibility**
- **Interpretation and Application**: Rules must be interpreted to promote **just, timely, and cost-effective resolution** of claims, with due regard to cultural context.
- **Discretionary Powers of the Tribunal**:
  - May **vary, dispense with, or supplement** any Rule if necessary for just resolution.
  - May provide directions in cases of **technological difficulties** (e.g., email filing issues).
  - May apply rules by **analogy to the Federal Courts Rules** for matters not explicitly covered.
- **Time Limits**: Governed by **Sections 26–29 of the Interpretation Act**, with **Saturday considered a holiday**.

---

### **4. Representation in Proceedings**
- **Mandatory Representation**:
  - All parties and First Nations must be represented by **legal counsel** unless special circumstances apply.
- **Exceptions**:
  - The Tribunal may grant **leave** for a claimant or First Nation to be represented by someone **not legal counsel** (e.g., community representative).
  - **Individuals** are **not required** to be represented by legal counsel.
- **Termination of Representation**:
  - A representative ceases to serve upon filing **written notice** and serving it on all parties.

---

### **5. Hearing Order and Procedure**
- The **Chairperson may set the order of hearings** regardless of filing date.
- If both **validity of the claim** and **compensation** are in issue, the Tribunal may order **separate stages** of hearing.

---

### **6. Filing Documents**
- **Mandatory Methods**:
  - All documents must be filed **by email** (subject to exceptions).
  - **Electronic filing only** (PDF or any format convertible to letter size paper).
- **Filing Timing**:
  - Documents filed by email or fax **after 17:00 Ottawa time** are deemed filed on the **next non-holiday day**.
- **Electronic Documents**:
  - Filed by email or fax are considered **the original** of the document.
- **Paper or Fax Filings**:
  - Allowed only with **Tribunal leave**.
  - Must be printed on **letter size paper**.
  - May retain existing format if **cannot be printed** on letter size.
  - Must be **consecutively numbered**.
- **Fax Requirements**:
  - Must include a **cover sheet** with:
    - Filer’s name, address, phone
    - Date/time of transmission
    - Total pages (including cover)
    - Contact for transmission issues
- **Email Service**:
  - Must be in PDF or printable format.
  - Must include an **electronic message** with:
    - Filer’s name, address, phone
    - Description of document
    - Date/time of transmission
    - Contact for transmission issues

---

### **7. Service of Documents**
- Must be served **personally**, by **email**, **fax**, or **registered mail**.
- Service is complete when:
  - For a **party**: delivered to their representative
  - For an **intervenor**: delivered to their representative or personally, if unrepresented
- **Late service (after 17:00 local time)** is deemed delivered on the **next non-holiday day**.
- **Fax Service**:
  - Must be on letter size paper.
  - **No more than 50 pages** without recipient’s **prior consent**.
  - Must include a cover sheet with same details as above.
- **Proof of Service**:
  - Must be provided via:
    - **Affidavit of Service** (must include receipt confirmation)
    - Or **admission by the served party**

---

### **8. Applications to the Tribunal**
- **General Rule**: All applications must be made in writing.
- **Exceptions**: Oral applications may be permitted with **Tribunal leave**.
- **Who Heats the Application**:
  - If before the **pre-hearing conference**: heard by the Tribunal member presiding over the **case management conference**.
  - If on or after the **pre-hearing conference**: heard by the member presiding over the **hearing**.
  - If the required member is unavailable, any other member may hear it.
- **Directions**: The Tribunal may set time and manner for arguments and evidence.
- **Notice of Application**:
  - Must state:
    - Relief sought
    - Grounds for application
    - Whether any parties have consented
- **Response Deadline**: Within **10 days** of service.
- **Response and Notice of Application**:
  - Must be **no more than 20 pages**, legible, single-sided on letter size paper, in **12-point or larger font**.
  - **Rules 33 and 36 do not apply** to **ex parte applications**.

---

### **9. Declaration of Claim and Response**
- A **declaration of claim** must:
  - Identify the First Nation and the applicable definition under section 2 of the Act
  - Provide representative’s contact info
  - Confirm fulfillment of **conditions precedent** (subsection 16(1))
  - State the **grounds** under subsection 14(1)
  - Briefly state the facts
  - State that **compensation sought does not exceed $150 million**
- **Crown Response (within 30 days)** must include:
  - Whether the Minister decided not to negotiate (and date)
  - Position on **validity of claim**
  - Which of **paragraphs 20(1)(e)–(h)** may support compensation
  - Admission, denial, or lack of knowledge for each fact in the declaration
  - Brief statement of Crown facts
  - Relief sought
  - Email for document service

---

### **10. Party Status and Interventions**
- **Applications for party status or leave to intervene**:
  - Must be made **within 60 days** of service under subsection 22(1).
  - If filed **after 60 days**, the Tribunal must consider whether the applicant made **reasonable efforts** to act timely.
- **Notice of Intervention** must include:
  - Name, address, phone, and representative (if any)
  - Description of participation and how it assists the Tribunal
  - Party whose position they intend to support
  - Language to be used in proceedings
- **Tribunal may provide directions** to intervenors on role and procedures, **only after giving parties an opportunity to submit submissions**.

---

### **11. Case Management Conferences**
- **Mandatory** before proceedings begin or at intervals as needed.
- **Notice to parties** must be provided by the Registrar.
- **Attendance**: Representatives must attend **in person**.
- **Leave may be granted** for alternative attendance.
- **Matters to be discussed**:
  - Condition precedent under subsection 16(1)
  - Crown’s position on claim validity
  - Preparation for compensation
  - Intent to use **oral history** evidence
  - Impact on provinces, First Nations, or individuals
  - Interest in **mediated settlement**
  - Proportionality of proceedings (based on amount in dispute and complexity)
- **Ongoing Discussions** at each conference:
  - Protocols for oral history and expert evidence
  - Nature and type of evidence
  - Disclosure issues (privilege, confidentiality)
  - Resolved vs. unresolved issues
  - Related claims (same facts or lands)
  - Witness testimony
  - Procedural timeline

  - Statements and disclosures made during case management are **without prejudice**.
