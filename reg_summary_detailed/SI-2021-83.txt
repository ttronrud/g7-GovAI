**Nature of the Regulation:**

This regulation constitutes a **formal transfer of ministerial responsibilities** within the federal government. Specifically, it transfers certain **powers, duties, and functions** originally held by the **Minister of Health** to the **Minister of Mental Health and Addictions** and also designates the latter as the **Associate Minister of Health**.

   - The Minister of Mental Health and Addictions is simultaneously designated as the **Associate Minister of Health**, indicating a shared or integrated role in health-related policy, particularly in areas related to mental health and substance use.

2. **Statutory Basis:**
   - The transfer is authorized under **paragraph 2(a)(a)** of the *Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act*, which allows for the reorganization of ministerial responsibilities within the federal government to improve efficiency, coherence, or policy alignment.
   - This provision enables the government to reallocate responsibilities based on strategic policy goals, such as strengthening mental health and substance use services.

3. **Subject Matter of the Transfer:**
   - The transfer specifically pertains to the **Canadian Centre on Substance Abuse Act (CCSA Act)**.
   - The CCSA is a federal agency established to conduct research, provide policy guidance, and support evidence-based approaches to substance abuse prevention, treatment, and harm reduction.
   - By transferring these functions, the government is aligning substance use policy more closely with mental health priorities, recognizing the strong interconnection between mental health and substance use disorders.

4. **Policy and Strategic Rationale:**
