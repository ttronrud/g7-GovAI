**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: "Imports of Certain Textile and Apparel Goods From Costa Rica – Customs Duty Remission Order" (S.C. 1997, c. 36)**

---

### **1. Purpose and Authority**
This regulation establishes a **customs duty remission** for specific textile and apparel goods imported from **Costa Rica** into Canada. It was issued under the authority of:

- **Section 115 of the Customs Tariff Act**,  
- On the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**,  
- By **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**.

The primary objective is to **promote trade** between Canada and Costa Rica by reducing or eliminating customs duties on qualifying textile and apparel products, consistent with the **Canada-Costa Rica Free Trade Agreement (C-CRFTA)**.

---

### **2. Definitions (Key Terms)**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Apparel** | Goods in Chapters 61 and 62 of the List of Tariff Provisions that are cut or knitted to shape and sewn or assembled in Costa Rica using fabric or yarn produced or obtained outside the free trade area. |
| **Fabric and Made-Up Goods** | Includes: <br> - Cotton or man-made fibre fabrics and made-up textile goods in Chapters 52–55 (excluding those with 36% or more wool/fine animal hair), and in Chapters 58, 60, and 63, provided they are woven or knitted in Costa Rica from yarn produced or obtained outside the free trade area, or knitted from yarn spun in Costa Rica from fibre from outside the free trade area. <br> - Wool fabrics and made-up goods (with 36% or more wool/fine animal hair) in the same chapters, under the same production conditions. <br> - Finished and cut/finished garments (subheadings 9404.90) made from fabrics listed in specific subheadings (e.g., 5208.11 to 5208.29, etc.) produced or obtained outside the free trade area. |
| **Free Trade Area** | The geographic region comprising **Canada and Costa Rica**. |
| **Spun Yarn** | Cotton or man-made fibre yarns (headings 52.05–52.07 or 55.09–55.11) spun in Costa Rica from fibre (headings 52.01–52.03 or 55.01–55.07) produced or obtained outside the free trade area. |

---

### **3. Scope of Duty Remission**

- **Remission applies to**:  
  - **Apparel**,  
  - **Fabric and made-up goods**,  
  - **Spun yarn**  
  imported from **Costa Rica**.

- **Eligible imports** must be:
  - Produced or assembled **in Costa Rica**,
  - Using **fabric or yarn** sourced **outside the free trade area** (i.e., not produced in Canada or Costa Rica).

- **Remission is granted** on or after the **effective date** of Section 37 of the *Canada-Costa Rica Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act* and Chapter 28 of the *Statutes of Canada, 2001*.

---

### **4. Conditions for Remission**

The duty remission is **not automatic** and is subject to the following conditions:

#### **(a) Annual Quantitative Limits**
- Remission is limited to an amount **not exceeding the annual quantitative limits** set out in:
  - **Schedules 6.B.1, 6.B.2, and 6.B.3** of **Appendix III.1.6.1 to Annex III.1 of Chapter III** of the C-CRFTA.
- These limits cap the volume of eligible goods that can be imported under this remission each year per product category.

#### **(b) Certification and Documentation Requirements**
Importers or owners must provide the following documentation **at specific times**:

1. **At the time goods are accounted for** under subsections 32(1), (3), or (5) of the *Customs Act*, or when applying for a refund:
   - A **certificate issued under the Export and Import Permits Act and Import Certificate Regulations**, identifying the quantity available for remission or refund under Chapter III of the C-CRFTA.

2. **At the same time**:
   - A **written declaration** (in English or French) confirming that the certification required in paragraph (c)(i) is in the importer’s possession.

3. **At the time of release of goods**, or **at any time after** providing the declaration:
   - A **certification of the exporter**, in English, French, or Spanish, in the form specified in the schedule.
   - Accompanied by:
     - A copy of the **applicable invoice or sales agreement**.
     - If the certification is in **Spanish**, an **English or French translation** must be provided.

> ⚠️ This ensures compliance with the C-CRFTA’s requirements and verifies that goods meet the agreed-upon origin and production standards.

---

### **5. Effective Date and Legal Status**

- The regulation **comes into force** on the day that **Section 37 of the Canada-Costa Rica Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act** and **Chapter 28 of the Statutes of Canada, 2001**, come into force.
- As of **November 1, 2002**, this regulation was in force (noted in SI/2002-146).

---

### **6. Exporter Certification (Form)**

An **exporter certification form** is attached to the regulation, requiring the exporter to:

> *"I, the exporter of the goods referred to in the attached invoice or sales agreement, hereby certify that those goods comply with the applicable requirements specified in Appendix III.1.6.1 of Annex III.1 to Chapter III of the Canada-Costa Rica Free Trade Agreement."*

The form includes fields for:
- Name  
- Title  
- Company  
- Telephone and fax numbers  
- Invoice number  

This certification serves as a **legal declaration** of compliance with the origin rules and is a prerequisite for duty remission.

---

### **7. Key Implications and Objectives**

| Objective | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Promote Trade** | Reduces the cost of importing textile and apparel goods from Costa Rica, encouraging Canadian businesses to source from Costa Rica. |
| **Support Origin Rules** | Ensures only goods meeting specific production criteria (e.g., fabric/yarn from outside the free trade area, produced in Costa Rica) qualify for remission. |
| **Prevent Abuse** | Annual limits and documentation requirements prevent overuse or circumvention of the remission. |
| **Enforce Compliance** | Requires detailed documentation to verify origin, production, and compliance with C-CRFTA terms. |

---

### **8. Summary in Plain Language**

This regulation allows Canadian importers to **receive a refund of customs duties** on certain textiles and apparel goods imported from Costa Rica — **only if**:
- The goods are made in Costa Rica using materials (fabric or yarn) from outside Canada and Costa Rica,
- The goods fall within specific tariff classifications,
- The importer has not exceeded annual limits,
- All required documentation (certifications, invoices, declarations) is submitted at the correct time.

This policy supports **free trade** between Canada and Costa Rica by reducing trade barriers for qualifying goods, while maintaining **origin controls** to ensure fair and transparent trade.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation is a **key component** of the Canada-Costa Rica Free Trade Agreement, designed to:
- Facilitate trade in textiles and apparel,
- Encourage value-added production in Costa Rica,
- Ensure compliance with origin rules,
- Provide a structured, transparent, and accountable mechanism for duty remission.

It reflects a balanced approach — promoting economic integration while protecting the integrity of the trade agreement through clear definitions, limits, and documentation requirements.
