**Detailed Summary of the *Meetings and Proposals (Banks and Bank Holding Companies) Regulations***  
*Issued under the Bank Act (Canada), S.C. 2005, c. 54*

---

### **Overview**

The *Meetings and Proposals (Banks and Bank Holding Companies) Regulations* are a set of administrative rules made by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance, pursuant to specific sections of the *Bank Act*. These regulations establish detailed procedural requirements for **annual meetings**, **shareholder proposals**, and **voting processes** involving banks and bank holding companies in Canada.

The regulations aim to ensure **transparency, fairness, and consistency** in shareholder engagement, particularly in relation to the submission, review, and voting on proposals at meetings. They clarify timelines, eligibility criteria, and procedural standards for proposals, while also allowing for modern methods of voting (e.g., electronic or telephonic).

---

### **Key Provisions and Explanations**

#### 1. **Applicability and Legal Basis**
- These regulations apply to **banks and bank holding companies** operating under the *Bank Act*.
- They are enacted under several sections of the *Bank Act*, including:
  - Section 137a, subsection 138(1)b
  - Section 143c, subsections 144(1)d and 151(5)e
  - Section 726f, subsection 727(1)g
  - Section 732h and subsections 733(1)i, 740(5)j, and 978(1)k
- These provisions relate to **shareholder meetings**, **proposal submission**, **voting rights**, and **record date management**.

---

#### 2. **Record Date Requirements**

- **General Rule (excluding specific exceptions):**  
  For most purposes under sections 137(5) and 726(5) of the *Bank Act*, the **record date** (the date used to determine who is entitled to vote) must be **no more than 60 days** before the action (e.g., a vote or meeting).

- **Exception for Specific Paragraphs (137(5)(c), (d) and 726(5)(c), (d)):**  
  When a proposal is related to certain types of matters (e.g., changes to governance or structural issues), the record date must be:
  - **At least 21 days** and
  - **No more than 60 days**
  before the meeting date.

> This ensures sufficient time for shareholders to make informed decisions while maintaining timely governance processes.

---

#### 3. **Notice of Record Date**

- Under sections 137(7) and 726(7), the **notice of the record date** must be given **at least 7 days** before the date fixed for the record date.

> Ensures shareholders have reasonable notice to verify their eligibility to vote.

---

#### 4. **Notice of Meeting**

- Under sections 138(1) and 727(1), the **notice of the meeting** must be sent:
  - **No less than 21 days** and
  - **No more than 60 days** before the meeting.

> Balances the need for timely communication with adequate time for shareholders to prepare.

---

#### 5. **Timeframe for Proposal Submission (Subsection 141.1(2))**

- Under subsection 141.1(2), the **maximum time allowed** for a shareholder to submit a proposal is **90 days** from the date of the meeting.

> Gives shareholders a reasonable window to submit proposals before the meeting.

---

#### 6. **Eligibility of Proposers (Subsections 143(1.1) and 732(1.1))**

A person may submit a proposal if they meet **one of the following two criteria**:
- They hold **voting shares** equal to **1% of the total outstanding voting shares** of the bank or bank holding company as of the date the proposal is submitted; **OR**
- Their **fair market value** of shares (as determined at the close of business the day before the proposal is submitted) is **at least $2,000**.

> This ensures that only significant shareholders or investors with meaningful financial interest can propose changes.

Additionally:
- The person must have been a **registered holder or beneficial owner** during the **six-month period immediately before** the proposal is submitted.

> Prevents opportunistic or short-term submissions by ensuring proposers have sustained ownership.

---

#### 7. **Request for Proof of Eligibility (Subsections 143(1.4), 144.1(5), 732(1.4))**

- A bank or bank holding company may **request proof** of eligibility within **14 days** of receiving a proposal.
- The proposer must then provide the required proof within **21 days** of receiving the request.

> Provides a mechanism to verify eligibility and prevent fraudulent or non-compliant proposals.

---

#### 8. **Length of Proposal and Statement (Subsections 143(3), 144.1(6), 732(3))**

- The **proposal and its supporting statement together** must not exceed **500 words**.

> Ensures clarity, brevity, and focus on essential issues, reducing complexity and enhancing accessibility for shareholders.

---

#### 9. **Timeframe for Response to Proposal (Subsections 143(5), 144.1(8), 732(5))**

- **Paragraphs 143(5)(a), 144.1(8)(a), 732(5)(a):**  
  The bank or bank holding company must respond to a proposal within **90 days**.

- **Paragraphs 143(5)(c), 144.1(8)(c), 732(5)(c):**  
  A proposal must receive **support from at least 3%, 6%, or 10%** of voting shares depending on how many annual meetings it has been presented at:
  - **1 meeting:** 3% of shares voted
  - **2 meetings:** 6% of shares voted at the last presentation
  - **3 or more meetings:** 10% of shares voted at the last presentation  
  *(Applies only to federal credit unions)*

> This establishes a **threshold for proposal viability** based on prior engagement, discouraging frivolous or low-impact proposals.

- **Paragraphs 143(5)(d), 144.1(8)(d), 732(5)(d):**  
  The **minimum support** required is:
  - 3%, 6%, or 10% of shares voted at the last presentation (as above)
  - And the **period of support must be at least 5 years** (for proposals introduced at 3+ meetings)

> Ensures that proposals have **long-term backing** and are not based on short-term sentiment.

---

#### 10. **Timeframe for Review and Response (Subsection 143(5.1), 144.1(9), 732(5.1))**

- The bank or bank holding company must respond to a proposal within **two years** of its submission.

> Provides a clear deadline for consideration and response, ensuring accountability.

---

#### 11. **Notification of Shareholders (Subsections 144(1), 144.2(1), 733(1))**

- A bank or bank holding company must **notify shareholders** of a proposal within **21 days** of receiving it.

> Ensures timely dissemination of information to shareholders for informed voting.

---

#### 12. **Voting via Electronic or Telephonic Means (Subsections 144(1), 144.2(1), 733(1), 151(6)(a), 740(5))**

- A vote may be conducted **via telephonic, electronic, or other communication facilities** if:
  1. The system enables votes to be **gathered in a verifiable manner**; and  
  2. The **tallied votes are presented without identifying individual shareholders, members, or groups**.

> Allows for **modern, efficient voting** while preserving **confidentiality and integrity** of the process.

> This supports remote participation and improves accessibility, especially for geographically dispersed shareholders.

---

#### 13. **Coming into Force**

- These regulations **come into force on the day they are registered** (i.e., upon publication in the Canada Gazette).

> Ensures immediate applicability once formally adopted.

---

### **Key Objectives and Impacts**

| Objective | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Transparency** | Clear timelines and eligibility rules ensure fair and predictable processes. |
| **Shareholder Protection** | Only significant shareholders can propose changes, reducing frivolous or speculative proposals. |
| **Efficiency** | Word limits, notice periods, and electronic voting streamline meeting operations. |
| **Accountability** | Mandatory response timelines and support thresholds ensure proposals are seriously considered. |
| **Modernization** | Allows electronic voting while preserving privacy and verification standards. |
