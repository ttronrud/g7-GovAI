## üîç **Overview of the Regulation**

This regulation establishes a **uniform system of accounting** for gas pipeline companies operating in Canada. It replaces the previous *Gas Pipeline Uniform Accounting Regulations* (which were repealed by SOR/2020-50, s. 6) and introduces a new, detailed framework for how gas pipeline companies must maintain, record, and report their financial and operational data.

The primary objective is to ensure **transparency, consistency, and accountability** in the financial reporting of gas pipeline operators, enabling regulators to assess financial health, operational efficiency, and compliance with national energy policy objectives.

The regulation applies only to companies authorized by the **Canadian Energy Regulator (CER)** ‚Äî formerly the National Energy Board (NEB) ‚Äî to **construct or operate a gas pipeline**.

## üè¢ Application to Companies: Group 1 vs. Group 2

### ‚úÖ **Group 1 Companies**  
*(Companies listed in Schedule VIII)*

These are the primary regulated operators and must comply with **all requirements** of the Regulations:

1. **Keep separate books of account** in Canada, in accordance with **generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP)**.
2. **Maintain accounts as prescribed** in these Regulations unless otherwise directed by the Commission.
3. **Maintain a full system of accounts** as outlined in the Regulations.

### ‚ö†Ô∏è **Group 2 Companies**  
*(All other companies that construct or operate gas pipelines)*

These companies are subject to **limited compliance**, with exemptions unless specifically required:

1. Must keep **separate books of account** in accordance with GAAP **until one year after the Commission grants leave to abandon** the pipeline.
2. Must **file audited financial statements** with the Regulator within **120 days** after the end of each fiscal year.
3. Must comply with subsections **6(1), (7), (8), and (9)** ‚Äî likely related to specific accounting treatments.
4. Are **exempt from most provisions** of the Regulations **except** as prescribed in paragraphs (a) to (c).

> This distinction reflects a phased approach to regulation, recognizing that Group 2 companies may be in transition or operating under temporary authorizations.

## üìÇ Accounting Records and Requirements

### General Principles:
- Records must provide **sufficient detail** to fully explain all entries.
- All records ‚Äî including **minute books, capital stock records, correspondence, computer printouts, tapes, and card decks** ‚Äî are included.
- Records must be **readily accessible** for inspection by Regulator representatives.
- Where a pipeline is held in **undivided interest**, the **operator must maintain records** that allow **easy identification and access** for each beneficial owner.

### Record Retention:
- Records must be retained for **one year after the Commission grants leave to abandon** the pipeline.

## üìä Financial Statement Structure (Schedules I‚ÄìVII)

### 1. **Balance Sheet Accounts (Schedule I: 100‚Äì293)**
- Reflect the company‚Äôs **financial position** at a given date.
- Include:
  - Assets
  - Liabilities
  - Share capital
  - Retained earnings

### 2. **Income Accounts (Schedule II: 300‚Äì341)**
- Summarize revenue, expenses, and **extraordinary items**.
- **Extraordinary items** are defined as:
  - Not typical of normal operations
  - Not expected to recur
  - Not part of routine business evaluation
- Must be recorded in:
  - **Account 331**: Extraordinary Income
  - **Account 341**: Extraordinary Income Deductions

### 3. **Retained Earnings Accounts (Schedule III: 350‚Äì357)**
- Track changes in retained earnings annually and include:
  - Balance transferred from income
  - Prior period adjustments
  - Miscellaneous adjustments
  - Dividend appropriations
  - Income tax on retained earnings adjustments

### 4. **Plant Accounts (Schedule IV: 401‚Äì498)**
- Subsidiary to balance sheet accounts 100‚Äì107.
- Classify all plant assets and are supported by **subsidiary accounts** that record:
  - Costs by location (e.g., main gathering systems, compressor stations, storage facilities, pipelines)
  - Expenditures for **additions, replacements, and retirements** approved by the Commission

> Plant accounts must separately track expenditures for each authorized project.

### 5. **Operating Revenue Accounts (Schedule V: 500‚Äì579)**
- Classify all **revenue from pipeline operations**.

### 6. **Operating Expense Accounts (Schedule VI: 610‚Äì729)**
- Cover operating expenses related to pipeline operations.

### 7. **Maintenance Expense Accounts (Schedule VII: 810‚Äì889)**
- Cover maintenance costs for pipeline operations.

## üèóÔ∏è Service Functions and Expense Allocation

The service functions are defined as:

| Function | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Gathering | Collection of natural gas from wells |
| Products Extraction | Processing of natural gas liquids (NGLs) |
| Gas Supply (operating only) | Delivery of gas to customers |
| Underground Storage | Storage of gas in underground facilities |
| Transmission | Moving gas through pipelines |
| General | General administrative or support functions |
| Administrative and General (operating only) | Administrative costs directly tied to operations |

> Expenses common to multiple accounts must be **equitably apportioned** and supported by **adequate records** showing the basis of allocation.

> Subsidiary accounts must record expenses **by location** where practicable (e.g., per major facility).

## üìù Capitalization of Construction Costs

All costs directly related to the **acquisition, construction, or improvement** of plant must be **capitalized** and recorded in plant accounts.

### Key Elements of Capitalized Costs:

| Cost Element | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Labour** | Includes wages and employee benefits. Pay for employees **specifically assigned** to construction is included. Pay for incidental employees is not. |
| **Materials & Supplies** | Purchase price at free delivery, taxes, transportation, inspection. Discounts are accounted for. Allowances are made for: <br> - Unused portions <br> - Salvage from temporary structures <br> - Recovered small tools |
| **Special Equipment (e.g., shovels, dredgers)** | Includes labour, materials, depreciation, supplies, and rental/operation costs. If equipment is used **only on a project**, its cost is capitalized and not depreciated until after completion. |
| **Transportation** | Includes transport of people, equipment, and materials. Cost of material delivery to the company is included in material cost if practicable. |
| **Contract Work** | Payments made to third parties for work performed under contract. |
| **Insurance** | Covers plant during construction, fire/willful destruction, injuries, and property damage. Recovered insurance is credited to the relevant cost account. |
| **Privileges** | Compensation for temporary use of public or private property. |
| **Engineering Services** | Fees for planning, design, supervision, inspection, or advice. |
| **Earnings & Expenses During Construction** | Revenues from property included in construction cost and expenses attributable to those revenues. |
