## üìö **Overview of the Regulation**

### **Regulation Title**
*Official Languages (Communications with and Services to the Public) Regulations*  
(Canada Gazette, 1991; later amended and partially repealed)

### **Legal Basis**
- Made under **Section 32 of the Official Languages Act (OLA)**, which empowers the Governor in Council (on the recommendation of the Treasury Board) to make regulations concerning communications with and services to the public in either official language.
- The regulation was developed in response to **Section 84 and 85 of the OLA**, which require:
  - Public consultation with linguistic minority communities and the general public.
  - Drafting and publication of proposed regulations before the House of Commons and in the *Canada Gazette*.

### **Purpose**
To ensure that **federal institutions provide services and communications in both official languages** (English and French) where there is **significant demand** from the English or French linguistic minority populations in a given geographic area.

This regulation defines **when and where** such bilingual services are required, based on demographic, geographic, and service-specific criteria.

## üéØ Core Principle: When Is Bilingual Service Required?

The regulation sets out **specific conditions** under which a federal institution must provide services in **both official languages**. These conditions are based on **demographics, geographic location, service type, and public demand**.

The key criterion is whether there is **"significant demand"** for services in one or both official languages.

---

### üåç **2. Where There Is Demand in One Language Only (Non-Bilingual Service)**

- If an office or facility is located in **Canada** and there is **significant demand in one official language** (but not both), then **services must be provided in that language**.
- This applies **only if** there is **no significant demand in both languages**.

> This ensures that linguistic minorities are not left out even if their population is small.

---

### üåê **3. Offices Outside Canada**

- If a federal office or facility is **outside Canada**, and over a year **at least 5% of public demand** is in a particular official language, then services must be provided in that language.

> This applies to overseas offices (e.g., in the Caribbean, Africa, etc.).

---

### üîÅ **Recurrence Rule: ‚ÄúCurrent Significant Demand‚Äù**

- For any scenario listed in (1)(b), (c), (g), (h), (i), (j), (l), (m), (o), (p), (q), **significant demand is considered current** if it exists under **any one of those conditions**.

> This means that if even one of the listed criteria is met, bilingual services are required.
