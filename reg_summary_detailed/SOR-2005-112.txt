**Detailed Summary of the International Cospas-Sarsat Programme Privileges and Immunities Order**

---

### **Overview**
This regulation, known as the *International Cospas-Sarsat Programme Privileges and Immunities Order*, was issued by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of Canada’s Ministers of Foreign Affairs and Finance, under **Section 5a of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act**. It grants specific **privileges and immunities** to the **International Cospas-Sarsat Programme (COSPAS-SARSAT)** and its personnel operating in Canada.

The Order is a legal instrument that enables the Cospas-Sarsat Programme — a global satellite-based search and rescue system — to function effectively in Canada by providing it with the necessary legal status and protections, consistent with international norms and the framework of the United Nations Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations (the "Convention").

---

## **Key Definitions**

1. **Arrangement**  
   The agreement among Canada, France, the Russian Federation, and the United States regarding the establishment of the Cospas-Sarsat Programme’s headquarters in Montreal, Quebec.

2. **Convention**  
   The *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, as set out in Schedule III of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act.

3. **Expert**  
   An individual who is not a representative of a Member State or an official, but who performs missions for the Organization (e.g., attending official meetings or conferences). Includes experts from associated states or organizations.

4. **Official**  
   The Head of Secretariat or any other individual employed full-time by the Organization.

5. **Organization**  
   The International Cospas-Sarsat Programme, consisting of the Council and the Secretariat established under the *Cospas-Sarsat Programme Agreement*.

6. **Programme Agreement**  
   The 1988 agreement signed by Canada, France, the former USSR, and the U.S., which entered into force on August 30, 1988. The Russian Federation assumed the former USSR’s responsibilities.

7. **Representative of a Member State**  
   A representative of a state party to the Programme Agreement.

---

## **Legal Status of the Organization in Canada**

- The **Organization is granted the legal capacity of a body corporate** under Canadian domestic law.
- The **Head of Secretariat** serves as the **legal representative** of the Organization in Canada with respect to its functions under the Programme Agreement.
- This legal status allows the Organization to own property, enter into contracts, and conduct business in Canada without being treated as a mere foreign entity.

> *Note: This legal capacity does not override the privileges and immunities granted under this Order.*

---

## **Privileges and Immunities by Category**

### **1. The Organization (General Immunities)**

| Privilege/Immunity | Description |
|-------------------|-------------|
| **Inviolability of Premises** | The headquarters premises in Montreal are inviolable. Canadian authorities may only enter with the consent of the Head of Secretariat — except in emergencies (e.g., fire) where consent is presumed. |
| **Immunity from Legal Process** | Immune from all legal actions (e.g., lawsuits, arrests) in Canada, except where immunity has been expressly waived. Waivers are limited to enforcement measures and require explicit consent from the Organization. |
| **Immunity of Property and Assets** | Immune from search, requisition, confiscation, expropriation, or interference by any government body (executive, judicial, legislative). |
| **No Use as Refuge** | The headquarters cannot be used as a safe haven for individuals avoiding arrest or legal process. |
| **Inviolability of Archives** | All documents, records, and archives of the Organization are protected from seizure or access. |
| **Tax Exemptions** | Exempt from: <br> - Direct taxes (e.g., corporate income tax) <br> - Customs duties and import/export restrictions for official use <br> - Excise duties and sales taxes on publications (including audio-visual materials) <br> *Exception:* Exemptions do not apply to charges for public utility services. |
| **Freedom of Communication** | Official communications (mail, cables, telegrams, telephones, etc.) receive treatment **at least as favourable as that given to any other government**. No censorship applies. |
| **Use of Codes and Courier Services** | The Organization may use codes, ciphers, and courier services (bags, couriers) with the same immunities as diplomatic couriers. |

---

### **2. Representatives of Member States**

These individuals are granted immunities while attending meetings or conferences convened by the Organization.

| Privilege/Immunity | Description |
|-------------------|-------------|
| **Immunity from Arrest/Seizure** | Immune from personal arrest, detention, or seizure of personal belongings. Immune from legal process for acts performed in their official capacity. |
| **Inviolability of Papers** | All documents and papers are protected. |
| **Right to Use Codes & Couriers** | May use codes and receive correspondence via sealed courier bags. |
| **Exemption from Immigration Restrictions** | Exempt from immigration controls, alien registration, and national service obligations. |
| **Baggage Immunity** | Same rights as diplomatic envoys regarding personal baggage. |
| **No Duty Exemption on Goods** | Cannot claim exemption from customs duties or excise on goods (except in personal baggage). |
| **Freedom of Speech** | Immunity from legal process for words spoken or written during their duties — even after they cease being representatives. |
| **Waiver Rights** | A Member State may waive immunity if it determines that doing so would impede justice. Waiver does not affect the purpose of the immunity. |

> *Purpose:* To ensure representatives can speak freely and perform duties without fear of legal action.

---

### **3. Officials of the Organization**

These are full-time employees of the Organization.

| Privilege/Immunity | Description |
|-------------------|-------------|
| **Immunity from Legal Process** | Immune from legal process for acts performed in their official capacity. |
| **Tax Exemption** | Exempt from taxation on salaries and emoluments paid by the Organization. |
| **Immunity from National Service** | Immune from national service obligations in Canada. |
| **Immunity from Immigration Restrictions** | Spouses and dependents are immune from immigration and alien registration requirements. |
| **Repatriation Rights** | Same repatriation facilities during international crises as diplomatic envoys. |
| **Free Import of Furniture & Effects** | May import personal effects, furniture, and vehicles duty-free upon first arrival in Canada. |

> *Purpose:* To ensure staff can work effectively without interference from Canadian law.

---

### **4. Head of Secretariat (Special Status)**

- In addition to the privileges of an official, the **Head of Secretariat** and their **spouse and minor children** are granted **full diplomatic privileges and immunities** under the *Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations*.
- This includes:
  - Immunity from arrest and legal process
  - Immunity from taxation on income
  - Immunity from immigration restrictions
  - Full protection of personal belongings
- These privileges are granted **only to the Head of Secretariat and their immediate family**.

> *Note:* The Council (the governing body) may waive immunity for the Head of Secretariat if needed to serve justice.

---

### **5. Experts**

Experts (non-officials, non-representatives) performing missions for the Organization receive the following:

| Privilege/Immunity | Description |
|-------------------|-------------|
| **Immunity from Arrest/Seizure** | Immune from personal arrest or detention. |
| **Immunity from Legal Process** | Immune from legal process for acts performed in their official mission — even after the mission ends. |
| **Inviolability of Papers** | All documents and papers are protected. |
| **Right to Use Codes & Couriers** | May use codes and receive correspondence via sealed bags. |
| **Currency & Exchange Flexibility** | Same exchange restrictions as foreign government representatives on temporary missions. |
| **Baggage Immunity** | Same as diplomatic envoys. |

> *Purpose:* To ensure experts can conduct their work independently and without fear of legal interference.

---

## **Key Provisions and Limitations**

### **1. No Tax Liability for Canadian Citizens or Residents**
- Canadian citizens or permanent residents are **not exempt** from taxes or duties imposed by Canadian law.
- The privileges and immunities do **not extend to tax obligations**.

### **2. National Security Exceptions**
- Canadian authorities retain the **right to deny entry** to any individual (including representatives, officials, or experts) for **national security reasons**.
- The Organization must be **immediately informed** if such action is taken.

### **3. No Binding Force Under International Law**
- This Arrangement is **not binding under international law**.
- It only becomes effective when:
  - All four parties (Canada, France, Russia, U.S.) sign it, **and**
  - The Order in Council is **published in the Canada Gazette** recognizing the Organization’s legal personality and granting privileges.

### **4. Duration and Termination**
- The Arrangement **automatically terminates** when the *Programme Agreement* ends.
- It remains in effect **as long as the Programme Agreement is in force**.
- Can be **terminated by either Canada or the Organization** after **one year’s written notice**.
- May be **amended by mutual consent** of all signatories.
