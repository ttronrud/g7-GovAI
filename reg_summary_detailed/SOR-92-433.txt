**Detailed Summary of the Federal Referendum Fees Tariff (SOR/92-430, 1992)**

---

### **Overview**

The **Federal Referendum Fees Tariff**, published under **SOR/92-430** in the *Canada Gazette, Part II*, on July 1, 1992, establishes a comprehensive framework for the payment of **fees, costs, allowances, and expenses** to individuals and entities involved in the conduct of a **referendum under the Referendum Act**. This regulation was enacted by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, based on recommendations from the **President of the Privy Council** and the **Chief Electoral Officer**, pursuant to specific provisions of the *Canada Elections Act* and the *Referendum Act*.

The Tariff applies to all persons employed or engaged in the administration of a referendum and sets out **detailed payment rates** based on the **population of the electoral district**, the **nature of the services rendered**, and **geographic or operational circumstances** (e.g., urban vs. rural travel).

It is formally titled the **Federal Referendum Fees Tariff** and is effective from **July 1, 1992**.

---

### **Key Definitions**

- **Act**: Refers to the *Referendum Act*.
- **Supervising Deputy Returning Officer**: An individual who supervises the deputy returning officers of **five or more polling stations** located in a **central polling place**.

---

### **Scope of Application**

The Tariff applies to:
- Returning officers
- Assistant returning officers
- Deputy returning officers
- Poll clerks
- Enumerators
- Revising officers and agents
- Printers
- Support staff
- Messengers
- Interpreters
- Constables
- Stand-by personnel

**Exclusions**:
- Individuals covered by the **Special Voting Rules Referendum Fees Tariff** (a separate, more limited tariff for specific circumstances).
- Any person not directly involved in the conduct of the referendum.

---

## **Core Components of the Tariff**

---

### **1. Returning Officer Fees (Based on Electoral District Population)**

The returning officer is paid a **base fee** depending on the population of the electoral district and whether a poll is held or enumeration occurs.

| Population Range | Poll Held? | Poll Not Held? |
|------------------|-----------|----------------|
| ≤ 60,000         | Yes       | Yes (Enumeration) |
| >60,000 to ≤70,000 | Yes       | Yes (Enumeration) |
| >70,000 to ≤90,000 | Yes       | Yes (Enumeration) |
| >90,000 to ≤110,000 | Yes       | Yes (Enumeration) |
| >110,000         | Yes       | Yes (Enumeration) |

**Note**: For **enumeration-only** cases (no poll), fees are lower and depend on whether enumeration occurred.

---

### **2. Returning Officer Expenses**

- **Incidental Expenses**: Covers stationery, postage, telegrams, office equipment, furniture, phone services, and transportation of referendum supplies.
- **Office Rental**: For the returning officer’s office used during the referendum.
- **Travel Expenses**: For necessary travel related to the referendum (based on population and location).

---

### **3. Support Staff Compensation**

Support staff required for ballot box handling and result tabulation are paid based on **population size**:

| Population | Number of Employees | Max Hours per Employee |
|-----------|---------------------|------------------------|
| ≤60,000   | 1                   | 338 hours              |
| >60,000 to ≤90,000 | 2              | 338 hours each         |
| >90,000 to ≤110,000 | 3             | 338 hours each         |
| >110,000  | 4                   | 338 hours each         |

**Bilingual Requirement**: If services are provided in both official languages, an additional amount is paid.

---

### **4. Accountable Advances for Part-Time Staff**

The returning officer receives **advance payments** for:
- **Part-time support staff** (salary cost): Based on population.
- **Staff for mailing enumeration cards**: Advance for salary cost.
- **Staff for affixing/removing labels** on enumeration cards: Advance for salary cost.

These advances are **accountable** (must be justified and reimbursed if not used).

---

### **5. Special Assignments**

If a returning officer is requested to perform a **special assignment**:
- Paid **per hour** at a specified rate.
- Travel and living expenses are covered.

---

### **6. Training and Debriefing Sessions**

Returning officers and assistant officers are paid:
- **Per day** for attendance at:
  - Training sessions
  - Referendum debriefing sessions
- **Travel, accommodation, and living expenses** if the session is not at their residence.

---

### **7. Coordinator for Preliminary Lists (Computerized System)**

- Paid **hourly**, with a cap on the rate (not exceeding a specified amount per hour).

---

### **8. Printing Costs (Printer Fees)**

| Service | Fee Structure |
|--------|---------------|
| **Notice of Enumeration** |  
- First 100 copies: Fixed amount  
- Each additional 50 copies (or part thereof): Fixed amount  
|  
| **Ballot Papers** (typesetting, printing, binding) |  
- First 1,000 ballots: Fixed amount  
- Each additional 1,000 ballots: Fixed amount  
|  
| **Reproduction of Documents** (preliminary lists, notice of grant, etc.) |  
- Per original page, up to a fixed rate |

---

### **9. Enumerator Compensation**

Each enumerator is paid the **greater of**:
- A flat rate (based on population)
- A tiered rate:
  - **First 200 electors**: Fixed amount
  - **Each elector beyond 200**: Additional amount per elector

**Additional Payments**:
- For typing preliminary lists (if requested)
- For attending training sessions
- For travel:
  - First 70 km: Fixed rate
  - Each km beyond 70 km: Fixed rate
- For **revision work in rural divisions**

> **Note**: If an enumerator works in **multiple polling divisions**, the fee is calculated based on **total electors across all divisions** in the preliminary list.

---

### **10. Stand-by and Supervisory Enumerators**

- **Stand-by Enumerator**:
  - Fee for agreeing to stand-by
  - For attending training sessions

- **Supervisory Enumerator**:
  - Up to **30 hours per week** at hourly rate
  - Travel and incidental expenses (same as enumerator)

---

### **11. Revising Officer and Agent Fees**

- **Revising Officer**:
  - Fee = greater of:
    - One fee per polling division in the revisal district
    - A fixed base amount + postage
- **Revising Agent / Stand-by Revising Agent**:
  - Paid per hour of work
  - Travel and incidental expenses: capped at:
    - Number of kilometers traveled
    - Or a fixed amount (whichever is greater)

---

### **12. Rent for Revisal Offices**

- Owner of building used as revisal office is paid:
  - **Per day** for use
  - Maximum daily rent (fixed amount)

---

### **13. Deputy Returning Officers (Advance Poll & Polling Stations)**

- **Advance Poll Deputy**: Paid for all services, including vote counting on ordinary day.
- **Polling Station Deputy / Counting Clerk**: Paid for services at polling station or in returning officer’s office.
- **Supervising Deputy (Central Polling Place)**: Paid for supervisory duties.
- **Training Sessions**: Paid per session attended (with travel/living expenses if applicable).

---

### **14. Poll Clerks**

- **Advance Poll Clerk**: Paid for all duties, including vote counting.
- **Polling Station Clerk**: Paid for duties at polling station or in office.
- **Stand-by Poll Clerk**: Paid for training sessions (with travel/living expenses if needed).

---

### **15. Rent for Advance Polling and Polling Stations**

- **Advance Polling Station**:
  - Rent (including heating, lighting, cleaning, furniture) for **two days** (advance poll) + **one day** (ordinary polling day)
  - Maximum per advance poll (fixed amount)

- **Polling Station**:
  - Rent per day (maximum fixed amount)

---

### **16. Messengers**

- Paid **per hour worked**
- Travel and living expenses (outside home residence)

---

### **17. Interpreters and Constables**

- **Interpreters**:
  - Paid per hour at a fixed rate (advance poll or polling station)

- **Constables**:
  - Paid per hour at a fixed rate (advance poll or polling station)

---

### **18. Security Guards**

- Returning officers may hire security guards with **Chief Electoral Officer’s prior authorization**.
- Paid for services rendered.

---

### **19. Witnesses at Vote Addition**

- Each elector called to be a witness (when no representative from both sides is present) is paid:
  - A fee **per hour worked**
