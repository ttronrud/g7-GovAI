**Detailed Summary of the Rules of the Provincial Court of Newfoundland and Labrador in Criminal Proceedings (Effective November 1, 2004)**

---

### **1. Introduction and Authority**
The *Rules of the Provincial Court of Newfoundland and Labrador in Criminal Proceedings* were adopted by the Provincial Court of Newfoundland and Labrador, with the concurrence of its judges, under **subsection 482(2) and (3) of the Criminal Code**. These rules govern all criminal proceedings, applications, and motions within the jurisdiction of the Provincial Court of Newfoundland and Labrador.

- **Title**: The rules are officially titled *The Rules of the Provincial Court of Newfoundland and Labrador in Criminal Proceedings*.
- **Effective Date**: November 1, 2004.
- **Scope**: Applies to all prosecutions, proceedings, and applications involving criminal matters, including those incidental to such matters.

---

### **2. Structure and Interpretation of Rules**
The rules follow a standardized structure:
- **Rules** are numbered with a decimal point (e.g., Rule 1.01 to Rule 1.06).
- **Subrules**, **clauses**, and **subclauses** are further subdivisions (e.g., subrule 1.01(1), clause 1.01(2)(a), subclause 1.01(2)(c)(i)).
- In practice, references can be made simply as "Rule 1.01", "Rule 1.01(2)", or "Rule 1.01(2)(c)".

**General Principles**:
- The rules are intended to ensure **fairness, simplicity, and efficiency** in criminal proceedings.
- They are to be **liberally construed** to eliminate unjustifiable delays and expenses.
- If a matter is not explicitly covered, the court may apply principles by **analogy**.
- If an accused is not represented by counsel, all procedural duties normally assigned to counsel shall be performed by or on behalf of the accused.

### **4. General Principles and Flexibility**
- **Flexibility in Procedure**: Where the rules do not provide specific guidance, the court may apply principles by analogy.
- **Non-Nullity of Irregularities**: Failure to comply with rules is not a ground for nullifying a proceeding or order. The court may:
  - Grant **amendments or relief** under **Rule 2.02** to ensure just determination.
  - Only **set aside** a proceeding or document if necessary in the **interests of justice**.
- **Dispensing with Rules**: The court may, in the interests of justice, **waive compliance** with any rule at any time.
- **Time Calculations**:
  - **Clear days** between events: Days on which events occur are **excluded**.
  - **Less than six days**: Holidays are **not counted**.
  - **Expiry on a holiday**: The deadline may be extended to the **next non-holiday**.
  - **Service after 4 p.m. or on a holiday**: Deemed served on the **next non-holiday**.
  - **Local time**: Time references are interpreted according to the local time in the court centre.

### **5. Filing and Service of Documents**
#### **Filing Requirements**
- All documents must be filed in the **court office** where the proceeding began, is pending, or is being heard — unless otherwise directed.
- **Affidavits, transcripts, records, or factums** for hearings must be filed in the office where the hearing is to occur.
- Documents may be filed:
  - By **hand delivery** to the court office, or
  - By **mail** with the prescribed fee.
- **Mail filing**:
  - The **filing date** is deemed to be the **date of the filing stamp** on the document, unless the court orders otherwise.
  - If the court office has **no record of receipt**, the document is deemed **not filed**, unless the court orders otherwise.

#### **Document Format and Content**
- **Physical Format**:
  - Must be **legibly printed, typewritten, or written**.
  - On **durable white paper** measuring **279 mm × 216 mm**.
  - Must have a **left margin** (and right margin if reverse side is used).
- **Headings**:
  - Must include:
    - Name of the court and information number.
    - Title of the proceeding.
    - If more than two parties, a short title using the names of the first parties followed by “and others”.
- **Mandatory Content**:
  - Title of the document.
  - Date of the document.
  - For party-filings:
    - Name, address, and telephone number of counsel (or party if unrepresented).
  - For clerk-issued documents:
    - Address of the court office.

#### **Access to Documents**
- A person entitled to view a document may request a **certified copy** from the clerk, **paid for at a prescribed fee**.

### **6. Affidavits – Rules and Requirements**
- **Form**: Must be in **Form 2**.
- **Tone**: Must be in the **first person**.
- **Content**:
  - Must state the **full name of the deponent**.
  - If the deponent is a party, officer, director, member, or employee of a party, this must be stated.
  - Divided into **numbered paragraphs**, each limited to one factual statement.
- **Signature**: Must be **sworn or affirmed before a person authorized to administer oaths**.
- **Beliefs**: May include statements of belief about non-contentious facts, **provided the source and basis of belief are specified**.
- **Exhibits**:
  - If attached: Must be **attached and filed with the affidavit**.
  - If shown to deponent: Not attached; left with the clerk for court use. After the matter is resolved and appeal period ends, the exhibit is returned to the filing party or counsel unless ordered otherwise.
  - A **copy of the exhibit** must be served with the affidavit unless impractical.
- **Multiple Deponents**:
  - Each requires a **separate jurat** unless all swear together at the same time, in which case a single jurat stating “Sworn (or Affirmed) by the above-named deponents” is acceptable.
- **Corporate Affidavits**:
  - May be signed by an officer, director, or employee of the corporation.
- **Illiteracy or Blindness**:
  - If a deponent is illiterate or blind, the person administering the oath must certify:
    - The affidavit was read to the deponent, who understood it.
    - The deponent signed or marked it in their presence.
  - Without such certification, the affidavit is **not admissible** without **court leave**.
- **Alterations**:
  - Any interlineation or erasure must be **initialled by the person taking the affidavit**.
  - Without initialling, the affidavit is **not admissible** without **presiding judge’s leave**.
