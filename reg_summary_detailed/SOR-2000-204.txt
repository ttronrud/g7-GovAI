## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Class I Nuclear Facilities Regulations**

### üîπ **1. Regulatory Framework and Legal Basis**
- These regulations were **approved under Section 44 of the Nuclear Safety and Control Act (NSCA)**.
- They were issued by the **Canadian Nuclear Safety Commission (CNSC)** on May 31, 2000.
- The regulations apply specifically to **Class I nuclear facilities**, which are defined as the most sensitive and high-risk category of nuclear installations due to their potential impact on public safety, environmental protection, and national security.

> ‚úÖ *Legal Authority*: The NSCA provides the overarching legal framework for nuclear safety, control, and regulation in Canada. These regulations implement specific requirements for licensing, operations, safety, and environmental protection for Class I facilities.

### üîπ **3. Scope of Application**
- These regulations **apply exclusively to Class I nuclear facilities**.
- They **do not apply to Class IB nuclear facilities** ‚Äî meaning Class IB facilities are governed by different rules (e.g., under *Class II Nuclear Facilities and Prescribed Equipment Regulations* or other lower-tier regulations).
- The regulations govern the **entire lifecycle** of a Class I facility: site preparation, construction, operation, decommissioning, and abandonment.

#### ‚úÖ **General Requirements (All Licence Applications)**
All licence applications (except for "abandonment") must include the following **additional information** beyond what is required in Section 3 of the *General Nuclear Safety and Control Regulations*:

| Requirement | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Site Description** | Location of the facility, including exclusion zones and structures within them. |
| **Facility Plans** | Detailed plans showing location, perimeter, areas, structures, and systems. |
| **Ownership or Authority** | Evidence that the applicant owns the site or has legal authority to operate it. |
| **Management System** | A system promoting a strong safety culture. |
| **Human Performance Program** | Measures ensuring workers are fit for duty. |
| **Hazardous Substances** | Name, form, characteristics, and quantity of hazardous substances present. |
| **Worker Health & Safety Policies** | Procedures to protect worker well-being. |
| **Environmental Protection Policies** | Procedures to prevent environmental harm. |
| **Effluent & Environmental Monitoring Programs** | Plans for monitoring emissions and environmental impacts. |
| **Information under Nuclear Security Regulations** | If the facility is subject to nuclear security rules (e.g., certain reactors). |
| **Public Notification Plan** | How the community will be informed about potential environmental or health effects. |
| **Decommissioning Plan** | Proposed strategy for decommissioning the facility or site. |
