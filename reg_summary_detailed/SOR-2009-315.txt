### 2. **Board Powers and Authority**

The **National Energy Board (NEB)** is granted broad powers under these Regulations, including:

- **Allocation of Areas**: The Board may determine spacing units and well production rates to manage drilling and production.
- **Well Designation**: Can assign names, classifications, or statuses to wells and change them as needed.
- **Zone Designation**: Can designate zones for regulatory purposes.
- **Pool/Field Naming and Boundary Definition**: Can name pools or fields and define their boundaries for identification.

> This gives the Board significant control over how resources are developed and managed across Canada.

### 3. **Management System Requirements**

Every applicant for an **authorization** must develop and submit an **effective management system** that integrates operations with financial and human resource management to ensure compliance with the Act and these Regulations.

#### Required Elements of the Management System:

1. **Policies and Goals**
   - Clear policies on safety, environmental protection, and waste prevention.
   - Processes for setting improvement goals.

2. **Hazard Identification and Risk Management**
   - Procedures to identify hazards and evaluate associated risks.
   - Risk mitigation strategies.

3. **Personnel Training and Competency**
   - Ensures staff are trained and competent to perform duties.

4. **Facility Integrity**
   - Maintains integrity of all safety, environmental, and waste prevention systems (e.g., equipment, structures, installations, support craft).

5. **Internal Reporting and Corrective Actions**
   - Reports on hazards, minor injuries, incidents, and near-misses.
   - Takes corrective actions to prevent recurrence.

6. **Documentation**
   - All processes must be documented clearly and systematically.
   - Documents must be current, approved by appropriate authority, and accessible.

7. **Periodic Reviews and Audits**
   - Conduct regular audits or reviews.
   - Take corrective action if non-conformities or improvement opportunities are found.

8. **Coordination Among Stakeholders**
   - Clearly defines roles and responsibilities among:
     - Owner of the installation
     - Contractors
     - Operator

9. **Accountability**
   - Must designate:
     - A person accountable for establishing and maintaining the system.
     - A person responsible for implementing it.

> âœ… The system must be **proportional** to the size, nature, complexity, and risks of the operations.
