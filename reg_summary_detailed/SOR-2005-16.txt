**Detailed Summary of the New Brunswick Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations**  
*Enacted under subsection 18(1) of the Sex Offender Information Registration Act (S.C. 2004, c. 10)*  
*Effective Date: January 13, 2005*

---

### **Overview**
These regulations, made by the Lieutenant-Governor in Council of New Brunswick under subsection 18(1) of the *Sex Offender Information Registration Act*, establish the procedures, definitions, and authorized personnel for the registration and collection of information about sex offenders within the province. The regulations provide a legal framework to ensure that information about individuals convicted of sex offences is collected, maintained, and shared in a secure and systematic manner to support public safety and offender monitoring.

---

### **Key Definitions**

- **Act**: Refers to the *Sex Offender Information Registration Act*, which provides the legislative foundation for these regulations.
- **RCMP**: Stands for the Royal Canadian Mounted Police. This designation grants the RCMP specific authority under the regulations.
- **Police officer and police force**: Defined consistently with *section 1 of the Police Act* (chapter P-9.2 of the Acts of New Brunswick, 1977, as amended). This ensures alignment with existing police authority and jurisdictional definitions.

---

### **Reporting and Notification Requirements**

1. **Reporting by Sex Offenders in New Brunswick**  
   A sex offender whose **main residence is located in New Brunswick** may fulfill any reporting obligation under:
   - Paragraph **4.1(a) or (b)** of the Act (which typically relates to periodic reporting of changes in address, employment, or other personal details), or  
   - **Section 4.3** of the Act (which may involve reporting of changes in residence or other relevant information)  
   **by telephone**.

   > This provision simplifies compliance for residents of New Brunswick by allowing them to report directly via phone, reducing the need for in-person visits to registration centres.

2. **Information Collection Authority**  
   Members of the **Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)** and **police officers** are authorized in New Brunswick to **collect information** for the purposes of the Act.  
   This includes gathering data from individuals, institutions, or other sources as required under the Act.

---

### **Authorized Persons to Register Information**

The following individuals or entities are authorized in New Brunswick to **register information** related to sex offenders:

- A **member of the RCMP**  
- A **police officer**  
- A **person employed at the New Brunswick Sex Offender Information Registration Centre**, administered by the RCMP’s “J” Division, whose duties specifically include the **registration of information**

> This ensures that only trained and designated personnel can officially record and maintain data, enhancing data integrity, accountability, and compliance with privacy and legal standards.

---

### **Designated Registration Centres**

The regulations designate specific locations as **official registration centres** in New Brunswick. These centres are listed in a table with two columns:

- **Column 1**: The name or address of the registration centre  
- **Column 2**: The designated **area of service** (i.e., geographic jurisdiction or region where the centre serves)

> This ensures that individuals and law enforcement personnel know where to submit reports or access registration services based on their location.

---

### **Effective Date**

These regulations come into force on the **day they are registered** (January 13, 2005), meaning they are immediately operational upon official publication and registration.

---

### **Purpose and Significance**

These regulations serve several key functions:

- **Public Safety**: By centralizing and standardizing the registration of sex offender information, the province enhances its ability to monitor and respond to potential risks.
- **Accessibility**: The provision allowing phone reporting improves access for sex offenders residing in New Brunswick, promoting compliance without requiring travel.
- **Accountability and Authority**: Limiting registration to authorized personnel (RCMP, police officers, and designated staff at the RCMP “J” Division) ensures that sensitive data is handled responsibly and in accordance with legal and ethical standards.
- **Geographic Clarity**: The designation of registration centres with defined service areas ensures that individuals in different parts of the province can access the appropriate service point.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *New Brunswick Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations* establish a clear, structured, and accessible system for collecting, managing, and disseminating information about sex offenders within the province. By defining key roles, streamlining reporting processes, and designating authorized centers, the regulations balance the need for public safety with practical implementation and individual rights. The framework remains aligned with both federal and provincial laws and supports ongoing efforts to protect communities through informed and responsible offender management.
