## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Public Lands Mineral Regulations (S.C. 1991, c. 50)**

### üìå **Overview**
These regulations govern the **granting, terms, conditions, and administration of mineral leases** on certain public lands in Canada. They were introduced to modernize and streamline the leasing process for mineral exploration and extraction, while ensuring **resource conservation**, **economic efficiency**, and **protection of Crown interests**.

The regulations were promulgated by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Natural Resources and the Treasury Board**, under **paragraphs 16(2)(a) and (j) of the Federal Real Property Act**.

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Lease** | A grant from Her Majesty (the Crown) to a person (the Lessee) of the exclusive right to search for, win, and take one or more minerals within, upon, or under Crown-owned lands. |
| **Mineral** | Any naturally occurring, homogeneous constituent of the lithosphere (e.g., metals, rock-forming substances), **but excludes**: <br> - Petroleum, natural gas, or related hydrocarbons <br> - Soil, peat, groundwater, or non-hydrocarbon gases |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Natural Resources of Canada (or any person authorized to act on their behalf). |

## üìù Core Provisions and Regulatory Framework

### 1. **Grant of Leases**
- The Minister may grant a lease to any individual or entity for the **exclusive right** to search for, win, and extract one or more minerals located on Crown-owned land in any province.
- **Lease duration**: Initially **10 years**, renewable under specific conditions.

> ‚úÖ *This replaces the older regulations and provides a modern, standardized framework.*

---

### 2. **Tender Requirement (Competitive Bidding)**
- **By default**, the Minister must **call for tenders** and grant the lease to the person offering the **highest bid**.
- This ensures **fair competition** and **transparent allocation** of mineral rights.

> üö´ Exception: A lease may be granted **without a tender** if the applicant is the **registered owner of the surface rights** of the land.

---

### 3. **Lease Form and Minimum Rental**
- All leases must follow the **form set out in Schedule I**.
- **Minimum annual rental**: **$1 per acre per year**, payable in advance.
  - First payment due upon execution of the lease.
  - Subsequent payments due on the same date each year.

> üîç *This ensures a baseline revenue stream for the Crown, regardless of the mineral value.*

---

### 4. **Geological Limitations and Mineral Grouping**
- A lease may **limit the right to search for and extract minerals** to a specific:
  - Geological zone
  - Volume of rock
  - Area within the province
- If multiple minerals are involved, the lease may restrict rights to those minerals **only when found together** (e.g., gold and silver in the same ore body).

> üí° This promotes targeted exploration and reduces overreach.

---

### 5. **Flexibility for Special Circumstances**
- The Minister may **deviate from the standard lease form** in rare cases (e.g., unique geology, environmental concerns, or technological needs), **provided**:
  1. The **minimum rental of $1/acre/year is not reduced**.
  2. A **royalty other than the standard one** may be included **only if**:
     - The Minister deems it necessary
     - To implement an agreement under **section 6** (consolidation or joint development)

> üìå This allows for tailored arrangements without undermining core revenue protections.

---

### 6. **Consolidation and Joint Development Agreements**
- The Minister may enter into **agreements with mineral operators** to:
  - Consolidate their interests
  - Merge or combine operations
  - Promote joint ventures or cooperative development
- Such agreements may be used to:
  - Improve efficiency
  - Reduce environmental impact
  - Facilitate large-scale development

> üèóÔ∏è Examples: Joint ventures between multiple companies; unit operations (shared infrastructure).

---

### 7. **Amendments to Leases**
- With **Minister‚Äôs consent**, a lease may be amended to:
  - Reflect changes in operations
  - Adjust operational terms
- **But**:
  - The **minimum rental of $1/acre/year cannot be reduced**.
  - Amendments cannot include new royalty structures unless tied to a section 6 agreement.

> üîê Ensures financial stability and prevents erosion of Crown revenue.

## üìÑ Lease Document Structure (Schedule I)

The lease is a formal, legally binding document executed in duplicate and includes:

1. **Parties**:
   - Her Majesty the Queen in right of Canada (as the Crown)
   - The Lessee (the mineral rights holder)

2. **Grant of Rights**:
   - Exclusive right to search for, win, and take designated minerals within specific lands in a province.

3. **Payment Obligations**:
   - Annual rental: **$1 per acre**, payable in advance
   - Royalty: Based on **provincial laws** governing mineral royalties
     - If no provincial royalty law exists on January 1st of a year ‚Üí royalty is determined as if the minerals were taken from **provincial lands** (i.e., based on provincial rules)

4. **Covenants and Obligations of the Lessee**:
   - Pay all rentals and royalties
   - Use lands only for mineral exploration and extraction
   - Maintain all wells, boreholes, mines, and structures in good repair
   - Prevent avoidable damage to the land
   - Safely close and abandon all excavations
   - Comply with all provincial laws and regulations
   - Dispose of waste materials without harming public or private interests
   - Keep accurate records of:
     - Quantity, weight, pitmouth volume, and sales value of minerals
   - Permit inspection of records by the Minister at reasonable times
   - Provide written statements (verified by affidavit) upon request about operations
   - Allow the Minister and their agents to enter the land for inspection or surveys
   - Indemnify the Crown against claims arising from Lessee‚Äôs operations

5. **Transfer and Subletting Restrictions**:
   - **No transfer, subletting, or assignment** without **prior written consent** from the Minister.
   - Any transfer must be **unconditional**.
   - If transferred to **two or more parties**, one must be appointed as **agent** to act on behalf of all (with corporate seal if applicable).

6. **Default and Termination**:
   - **Termination triggers** if:
     - Rent or royalty is **overdue by more than 30 days**, OR
     - A default in performance (e.g., failure to meet covenants) persists for **90 days** after written notice from the Minister.
   - Upon default, the Minister may:
     - Declare the lease terminated
     - Enter and distress (seize) the land or assets
     - Recover unpaid amounts plus costs and expenses

7. **Provincial Taxes and Filings**:
   - Lessee must pay all **provincial rates, assessments, taxes**, and municipal fees (e.g., drainage, irrigation).
   - Must deliver to the Minister **copies of all returns, plans, daily reports, and other documents** filed with the provincial government.

8. **Surrender and Removal of Equipment**:
   - Lessee may **surrender** the lease at any time, or with Minister‚Äôs consent, any portion of it.
   - Within **60 days** of termination or surrender, the Lessee may remove:
     - Buildings, fixtures, and equipment
     - **Exception**: Cannot remove:
       - Supports, pillars, timbers, frameworks, or casings essential to shafts, tunnels, or openings
       - Any structure that could cause collapse or hazard
   - Failure to remove within 60 days ‚Üí **forfeiture** of all equipment to the Crown.

9. **Property Rights of Surface Structures**:
   - Buildings or works erected on the surface **with the surface owner‚Äôs agreement** and used in general operations (not directly tied to mining openings) **remain the property of the Lessee**.

10. **Renewal of Leases**:
    - At the end of the 10-year term:
      - If the Minister determines that the land has **commercial potential** and the Lessee has fully complied with all covenants:
        - The lease may be **renewed for another 10 years**.
      - Renewals may continue **as long as production remains commercially viable** and the Minister is satisfied.
    - Renewal terms and conditions are set by the Minister.
