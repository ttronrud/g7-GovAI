**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

---

### **Regulation Title:**  
*Remission Order in Respect of Fees for the Replacement of Passports, Permanent Resident Cards and Citizenship Certificates Whose Loss, Damage or Destruction Resulted From the Wildfires in Fort McMurray and Surrounding Areas in Alberta in May 2016*

---

### **Legal Authority:**
This regulation was issued under the authority of **subsection 23(2.1)a of the Financial Administration Act** (S.C. 1991, c. 24, s. 7(2); R.S. c. F-11).  
The Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Minister of Citizenship and Immigration, the Minister of Foreign Affairs, and the Treasury Board) determined that it is in the **public interest** to provide fee remission for individuals affected by the 2016 Fort McMurray wildfires.

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation:**
To **grant a full remission (i.e., waiver) of fees** for the replacement of:
- Passports  
- Permanent Resident Cards  
- Citizenship Certificates  

**Where the loss, damage, or destruction** of these documents was **directly caused by the wildfires** that occurred in **Fort McMurray and surrounding areas in May 2016**.

This measure aims to provide **support and relief** to individuals who experienced significant disruption due to the natural disaster, particularly in terms of travel, immigration status, and legal documentation.

---

### **Eligibility Conditions:**

To qualify for the remission, applicants must meet **all of the following conditions**:

1. **Residence or Presence During the Wildfires:**
   - The applicant must have **resided in, or been present in**, Fort McMurray or its surrounding areas **during the occurrence of the wildfires in May 2016**.

2. **Cause of Document Loss:**
   - The **loss, damage, or destruction** of the passport, permanent resident card, or citizenship certificate must have been **directly caused by the wildfires**.

3. **Application Timing:**
   - For **passports**:  
     The application for replacement or reimbursement of fees must have been **delivered in person or by registered mail during, or sent by regular mail with a postmarked date within** the period from **May 3, 2016, to September 6, 2016**.
   - For **permanent resident cards or citizenship certificates**:  
     The application must have been submitted during or after the period ending on **August 3, 2016** (with the same delivery or postmark requirements).

4. **Supporting Documentation:**
   - The application must be accompanied by:
     - **Proof of residence or presence** in Fort McMurray or surrounding areas at the time of the wildfires (e.g., utility bills, rental agreements, police reports, or other official records).
     - A **declaration**, signed by the applicant or by someone acting on their behalf, stating that:
       - The loss, damage, or destruction of the document resulted from the wildfires.

---

### **Scope of Fee Remission:**
The remission applies to fees payable under the following regulatory provisions:
- **Passport and other Travel Document Services Fees Regulations** – Section 2 (fees for passport replacements)
- **Consular Services Fees Regulations** – Section 4 (fees for consular services related to travel documents)
- **Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations** – Subsection 308(2) (fees for replacement of permanent resident cards)
- **Citizenship Regulations** – Subsection 31(1) and Item 6 of the Schedule (fees for replacement of citizenship certificates)

This means that **all applicable fees** associated with the replacement of these documents are **waived** for eligible individuals.

---

### **Key Features and Implications:**

- **No financial burden** on individuals affected by the disaster due to the loss of essential identity and travel documents.
- **Time-bound application window** ensures that the relief is targeted and practical, focusing on those who applied during the immediate aftermath of the wildfires.
- **Verification of causation** requires a formal declaration linking the loss of the document to the wildfire, ensuring that the remission is not applied broadly but only where the disaster was a direct cause.
- The regulation reflects a **government commitment to disaster response and humanitarian support**, recognizing that natural disasters can severely disrupt personal and legal documentation.

---

### **Exclusions:**
- Individuals who did not reside or visit Fort McMurray or surrounding areas during the wildfires.
- Individuals whose documents were lost or damaged due to reasons other than the wildfires (e.g., theft, negligence, or natural wear and tear).
- Applications submitted outside the specified timeframes (e.g., after September 6, 2016, for passports; after August 3, 2016, for permanent resident cards or citizenship certificates).

---

### **Conclusion:**
This regulation is a targeted, compassionate policy response to a major natural disaster. It provides **practical and financial relief** to individuals whose personal documents were lost or destroyed due to the 2016 Fort McMurray wildfires. By requiring clear documentation and adherence to specific timelines, the government ensures accountability and fairness in the distribution of this benefit, while still offering meaningful support to those most affected.

--- 

**In short:**  
*Affected individuals who lived in or were present in Fort McMurray during the 2016 wildfires and applied for replacement of passports, permanent resident cards, or citizenship certificates within the specified timeframes (May 3 to September 6, 2016 for passports; August 3, 2016, for other documents), and who can prove the documents were lost due to the fires, are exempt from all associated fees.*
