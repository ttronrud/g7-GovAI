### **3. Communication Requirements**
- All official communication with the Division must be directed to the **registry office** specified by the Division.
- Parties must ensure that **contact information is accurate and up-to-date**.
- If contact information changes (e.g., address, phone number), the party must **notify the Division and the Minister in writing immediately**, unless detained.

### **5. Withdrawal of Admissibility Hearing Request**
- **Withdrawal is generally an abuse of process** if it would harm the integrity of the proceedings.
- **Exception**: If **no substantive evidence has been accepted**, withdrawal is **not** an abuse of process.
- **Procedure**:
  - If no substantive evidence has been accepted: The Minister may **withdraw orally at the hearing or in writing**.
    - If written, a copy must be sent to the other party.
  - If **substantive evidence has been accepted**: The Minister must submit a **written application** to reinstate the request.
- The Division may **allow reinstatement** if:
  - A principle of natural justice was violated, or
  - It is otherwise in the **interests of justice**.

### **6. Decision at Admissibility Hearing**
- If the decision is **in favour of the person**:
  - The member must **date and sign a notice of decision** and provide a copy to both parties.
- If the decision is **against the person**:
  - The member must issue a **signed order** citing relevant provisions of the Act.
  - The person must be notified of:
    - Right to appeal to the **Immigration Appeal Division (IAD)**, or
    - Right to file a **judicial review in the Federal Court** (if no appeal right exists).

> ðŸ“ **Effect of Decision**:
> - Oral decisions take effect when the member **states the decision**.
> - Written decisions take effect when the member **signs and dates** the document.

### **9. Early Detention Review Applications**
- A party may apply **before the expiry** of the 7-day or 30-day period.
- The Division may **grant the application** if new facts justify an earlier review.

### **10. Notification of Removal from Canada**
- The **Minister must notify the Division immediately** if a person is **removed from Canada** before a scheduled detention review.

### **12. Representation by Counsel**
- A person represented by counsel must:
  - Provide **counselâ€™s contact information in writing** to the Division and the Minister.
  - Update the Division **immediately** if contact details change.
- **Counsel of record** status is established when:
  - Counsel agrees to a date for a proceeding, or
  - Counsel becomes counsel after a date is fixed.
- To **withdraw as counsel of record**:
  - Must notify the Division and Minister **in writing**.
  - Counsel is no longer of record upon receipt of notice.
- To **remove counsel**:
  - The person must notify the Division and Minister in writing.
  - Counsel is removed upon receipt of notice.

### **13. Changing Language of Proceedings**
- A party may request a change to English or French:
  - **Orally or in writing** for 48-hour, 7-day reviews, or admissibility hearings held at the same time.
  - **In writing only** for all other cases.
- **Timing**:
  - For 48-hour/7-day hearings: As soon as possible.
  - For all other cases: At least **5 days before** the hearing.

---

### **14. Interpreter Requirements**
- A party must **notify in writing** of interpreter needs (language/dialect).
- **Timing**:
  - As soon as possible for 48-hour/7-day reviews or co-located hearings.
  - At least 5 days before for all other cases.
- The interpreter must:
  - Take an oath or make a solemn affirmation.
  - Interpret accurately and fairly.
