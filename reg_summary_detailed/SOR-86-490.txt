**Detailed Summary of the Cree-Naskapi Long-Term Borrowing Regulations, 1986**

---

### **Background and Legal Authority**
The *Cree-Naskapi Long-Term Borrowing Regulations, 1986* were introduced by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development**, under two specific provisions of the *Cree-Naskapi (of Quebec) Act*:

- **Section 98** of the Act: Grants the Governor General the authority to make or revoke regulations related to the governance and financial affairs of the Cree and Naskapi bands.
- **Subsection 198(2)**: Authorizes the making of regulations concerning long-term borrowing by Indigenous bands under the Act.

These Regulations **revoke** the earlier *Cree-Naskapi Long-Term Borrowing Regulations* made by Order in Council **P.C. 1985-793** on March 14, 1985, and replace them with updated, more detailed rules governing long-term borrowing by the Cree bands and the Naskapi band in Quebec.

The Regulations came into force on **April 1, 1986**, and are officially cited as:  
**Cree-Naskapi Long-Term Borrowing Regulations, 1986**  
(Statutory Citation: S.C. 1984, c. 18; SOR/85-248, 1985 Canada Gazette Part II, p. 1580)

---

### **Key Provisions of the Regulations**

#### **1. Requirements for a Valid By-law Authorizing Long-Term Borrowing**
Under **subsection 97(1)** of the *Cree-Naskapi (of Quebec) Act*, any by-law authorizing a long-term borrowing must include, in addition to standard information, the following specific details:

- **Name and address of the lender**  
  The borrowing must clearly identify the individual, corporation, or institution providing the funds.

- **Interest, commissions, premiums, and other similar costs**

- **Source of revenue and repayment mechanism**  
  The by-law must specify:
  - The **source of revenue** (e.g., land lease income, resource royalties, tourism revenues) from which the borrowing will be repaid.
  - The **specific portion of that revenue** that will be allocated to repay the loan and cover associated costs.

- **Security (if any)**  
  If the band is providing any form of collateral (such as land, assets, or future revenue rights), this must be clearly outlined in the by-law. This includes details on what is pledged and under what conditions.

---

#### **2. Obligation to Use Revenue for Repayment**
A band is **legally required** to apply **sufficient funds** from the revenue source specified in the by-law to:
- Repay the long-term borrowing
- Cover all related costs (e.g., interest, commissions, administrative expenses)

This obligation is not discretionary. The band must ensure that the designated revenue is used solely for these purposes.

---

#### **3. Prohibition on Misuse of Borrowing Funds**
No council member, officer, employee, or agent of the band may:
- **Knowingly apply or assist in applying** funds from the specified revenue source to anything other than the repayment of the long-term borrowing and related costs.

This includes:
- Using funds for personal benefit
- Allocating them to non-essential expenditures
- Paying for unrelated operations or projects

> This provision establishes a strict accountability framework to protect Indigenous financial autonomy and prevent corruption or financial mismanagement.

---

#### **4. Penalties for Violations**
The Regulations specify penalties for contravention of **Section 4**, which deals with the improper use of borrowing funds or failure to comply with the requirements of the by-law.

- **Maximum fine**: $2,000  
- **Maximum term of imprisonment**: 6 months  

These penalties are enforceable under **Section 198** of the *Cree-Naskapi (of Quebec) Act*, which provides the legal basis for administrative and criminal sanctions.

### **Purpose and Significance**

The 1986 Regulations represent a significant step in strengthening **financial accountability and transparency** within Cree and Naskapi bands. Key objectives include:

- **Protecting Indigenous financial sovereignty** by ensuring that borrowing is only approved through transparent, well-documented, and accountable processes.
- **Preventing misuse of funds** by clearly defining revenue sources and prohibiting diversion of funds.
- **Promoting sustainable development** by tying borrowing to verifiable, long-term revenue streams.
- **Supporting legal and administrative clarity** by providing clear regulatory standards for borrowing decisions.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Cree-Naskapi Long-Term Borrowing Regulations, 1986* establish a robust and enforceable framework for long-term borrowing by Cree and Naskapi bands in Quebec. By mandating transparency in lender identification, cost disclosure, revenue source specification, and strict repayment obligations, the regulations promote responsible financial management and protect the interests of the bands and their members.

The inclusion of penalties ensures that compliance is not merely procedural but carries real legal and financial consequences, reinforcing trust in the governance and financial integrity of Indigenous communities.
