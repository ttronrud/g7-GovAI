### ğŸ”— **3. Control and Affiliation Rules**

These rules define who controls an entity and how affiliations are determined:

- A **person controls** a corporation if they beneficially own **more than 50% of the voting shares** and can elect a majority of directors.
- A person controls an **unincorporated entity** if they own >50% of ownership interests and can direct its affairs.
- The **general partner** of a limited partnership controls it.
- A **trustee** controls a trust.
- **Control is transitive**: If A controls B, and B controls C, then A is deemed to control C.
- **Affiliation** exists when two entities are controlled by the same person or one controls the other.

### ğŸš« **5. Prohibitions on Concentrated Investments**

To prevent overexposure and reduce risk, the Board **must not directly or indirectly invest more than 10% of the total market value of its assets** in:

- **Any one person**;
- **Two or more associated persons**;
- **Two or more affiliated corporations**.

#### âœ… Exceptions to the 10% Rule:

The restriction **does not apply** to:

1. **Subsidiaries of the Board**;
2. **Securities issued or fully guaranteed by**:
   - Government of Canada or a province;
   - Government of the United States or its agencies;
3. **Mortgage-backed securities funds** fully guaranteed by the federal or provincial government;
4. **Funds that substantially replicate a broad market index** (e.g., S&P/TSX Composite);
5. **Segregated funds, mutual funds, or pooled funds** that comply with the *Pension Benefits Standards Regulations, 1985*.

> This allows strategic exposure to government-backed or index-based products without violating concentration limits.

### ğŸš« **6. Prohibition on Holding More Than 30% of Voting Shares**

The Board **shall not directly or indirectly invest in securities of a corporation** where it would hold **more than 30% of the voting rights** (i.e., shares with voting power).

#### Exceptions:

- **Subsidiaries of the Board** are exempt.
- **Investment corporations** are exempt **if**:
  - They do not engage in transactions with related parties that would violate section 16 (conflict of interest);
  - Their investments are limited to those authorized under these regulations;
  - They do **not** invest in other investment corporations with >30% voting shares;
  - They provide the Board with an **undertaking** (a binding commitment) to:
    - Avoid prohibited transactions with related parties;
    - Avoid investing in other investment corporations with >30% voting shares;
    - Limit investments to those authorized under these regulations.

- **Real estate corporations** are exempt **if**:
  - Activities are limited to acquiring, holding, leasing, or managing real property (owned or held on behalf of the Board, or other real estate corporations with >30% voting shares);
  - Investments outside real estate are restricted;
  - No transactions with related parties violate section 16;
  - No investment in other real estate corporations with >30% voting shares;
  - They provide the Board with an undertaking to avoid such violations.

- **Resource corporations** are exempt **if**:
  - Activities are limited to Canadian resource properties (owned or held on behalf of the Board or affiliated resource corporations);
  - Investments outside resource properties are restricted;
  - No borrowing for purposes other than income from resource properties;
  - No transactions with related parties that violate section 16;
  - No investment in other resource corporations with >30% voting shares;
  - They provide the Board with an undertaking to avoid such violations.

> These exemptions allow strategic investment in specialized entities (like real estate or resource companies) under strict conditions and oversight.
