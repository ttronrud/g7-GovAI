## üìù Application Requirements for Registration Certificates

### 1. **Verification Requirement**
- Every application for a registration certificate **must be accompanied by evidence** that the information has been verified by an **approved verifier**.
- **Exception**: If:
  - The firearm was previously verified;
  - The description (make, class, type, action, calibre/gauge) has not changed;
  - The Registrar determines, based on prior verification methods, that **a new verification is unnecessary**.

> This promotes efficiency while maintaining accuracy and accountability in firearm classification.

## üìÑ Registration Certificate Requirements

### 1. **Firearm Identification Number**
- The Registrar **must indicate** the firearm identification number on the issued registration certificate.
- If the Registrar has made a determination under **section 6** (e.g., serial number insufficient), the certificate must include a **sticker** bearing the firearm identification number.

> This enables tracking and traceability of firearms.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Conditions Attached to Registration Certificates (Section 4)

The Registrar must attach specific conditions to registration certificates depending on the type of firearm and modifications:

| Condition | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Modification that changes class** | Holder must notify Registrar within **30 days** of any modification that alters the firearm‚Äôs class (e.g., converting a non-restricted to restricted). |
| **Modification making frame/receiver capable of discharging** | Applies only to firearms registered as frames or receivers only. Must be reported within 30 days. |
| **Modification to an altered automatic firearm** | Must be reported within 30 days. |
| **Modification causing firearm to cease being a firearm** | Must be reported within 30 days. |
| **Modification to type, action, calibre, or gauge** | <br> - If **permanent**: report within 30 days; <br> - If **temporary but still exists after 30 days**: report without delay. |
| **Business under paragraph 22(f) of Firearms Licences Regulations** | If modification occurs, must be reported **within 13 months** after the modification, **if still present one year later**. |

> These conditions ensure ongoing compliance and prevent unauthorized or dangerous modifications.

## üîç Special Rules for Commencement Day Firearms

Firearms lawfully possessed on **December 1, 1998** are subject to special marking and verification rules:

- Holder must ensure that:
  - The **sticker** with the firearm identification number is attached to a visible place on the frame or receiver **within 60 days** of certificate issuance; **OR**
  - The firearm identification number is **permanently and legibly stamped or engraved** on the frame/receiver **within 90 days**.

> This ensures that legacy firearms are properly tracked and marked, aligning with national firearm inventory standards.

## üõ†Ô∏è Exceptions for Non-Visible Marking

The Registrar may allow a holder to place the identification number or sticker in a **non-visible location** (e.g., behind a panel) if:

1. It is consistent with the **manufacturer‚Äôs current practices** for that model;
2. The firearm **lacks a visible space** suitable for a sticker or engraving;
3. The firearm is **rare**; or
4. The firearm has an **unusually high value**, and visibility would significantly reduce its market value.

> This balances practicality, preservation of collectible items, and regulatory compliance.

## üìé Sticker Management and Reporting

- Holders must:
  - **Keep the sticker attached** and ensure the number remains **legible**.
  - **Notify the Registrar immediately** if:
    - The sticker becomes detached;
    - It is obscured;
    - The number becomes illegible.

> Upon notification:
- The Registrar issues a **new sticker without delay**.
- The holder must attach the new sticker **without delay** upon receipt.

> Ensures continuous traceability and prevents loss or tampering.

## ‚ùå Revocation of Registration Certificates

A registration certificate may be **revoked** if:

1. The holder **violates any condition** attached to the certificate (including those in sections 4‚Äì6); or  
2. The Registrar is **advised** of:
   - A modification that changes the firearm‚Äôs class; or
   - A modification that makes the firearm capable of discharging or ceases to be a firearm (as per sections 4(1)(b) or 4(2)).

> This provides a strong enforcement mechanism to protect public safety.
