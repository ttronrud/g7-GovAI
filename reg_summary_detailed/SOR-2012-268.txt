### **Detailed Summary of the Federal Credit Union Conversion Regulations**

These regulations, established under **section 216.14a of the Bank Act** and made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance**, provide a comprehensive framework governing the **conversion of a federal credit union into a bank with common shares**. The process is governed by **section 216.08 of the Bank Act**, which allows federal credit unions to become banks through the issuance of letters patent by the Minister.

The regulations came into force on **December 19, 2012**, and are designed to ensure that such a conversion is conducted transparently, fairly, and in the best interests of members, shareholders, and the broader Canadian financial system.

---

## **1. Purpose and Scope**
The primary purpose of these regulations is to:
- Ensure **transparency and fairness** in the conversion process.
- Protect the **rights and interests** of members and shareholders.
- Promote **informed decision-making** by requiring detailed disclosure.
- Maintain **financial stability** and public confidence in the Canadian financial system.

These regulations apply specifically to the **conversion of a federal credit union into a bank with common shares**, as defined under section 216.08 of the Bank Act.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**
| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Bank Act* |
| **Board Authorization** | A formal decision by the board of directors to pursue conversion |
| **Conversion** | The process where a federal credit union becomes a bank with common shares via issuance of letters patent |
| **Converted Credit Union** | The entity that results from the conversion |
| **Converting Credit Union** | The credit union proposing conversion |
| **Market Terms and Conditions** | As defined in subsection 501(2) of the Bank Act – fair and reasonable terms based on market standards |
| **Related Party** | As defined in section 486 of the Bank Act – individuals or entities with a close financial or personal relationship |
| **Value of the Converting Credit Union** | Market value of the credit union (including membership shares and shares, if any), as of the conversion date or a date specified by the Superintendent, excluding capital contributions and conversion expenses |
| **Valuation Expert** | An independent, qualified professional who provides an objective valuation of the credit union |

> Any reference to "estimated value" is interpreted as a **range of estimated values** to reflect uncertainty.

## **3. Requirements for a Conversion Proposal**

A conversion proposal must be **clear, simple, and not misleading**, and must include the following information:

### **A. Core Conversion Details**
- **Proposed date of conversion** or description of an event (e.g., merger, acquisition) coinciding with conversion.
- **Proposed name** of the converted credit union.
- **Advantages and disadvantages** of conversion for the credit union and its members/shareholders.

### **B. Alternatives Considered**
- Description of other options considered (e.g., staying as a credit union, merging with another institution).
- Reasons why conversion is deemed in the best interest of the credit union and its stakeholders.

### **C. Business Plan (3-Year Outlook)**
- Services to be offered or discontinued.
- Services to be provided by other entities (name, relationship to the credit union and related parties).
- Changes to geographic service areas.

### **D. Financial and Ownership Changes**
- Value and type of securities (shares or cash) to be issued to members/shareholders.
- Number of members/shareholders who will become common or other shareholders post-conversion.
- Whether the converted bank will be controlled by a shareholder or have a significant shareholder interest within three years.

### **E. Governance Changes**
- Changes in the number of directors.
- Changes in member/shareholder rights (e.g., voting rights, election of directors, control of the bank).

### **F. Shareholder Rights (Section 5 Requirements)**
- Description of any changes to shareholder rights under section 5 of the Bank Act.

### **G. Related Party Disclosure (One Year Before Board Authorization)**
- List of related parties and any changes in ownership.
- Description of all transactions between the credit union and related parties.

### **H. Fairness Assessment**
- A discussion of the fairness of the proposal to members/shareholders.
- An **independent fairness opinion** from a qualified professional with relevant experience.

### **I. Valuation Report Summary**
- Summary of valuation report including:
  - Estimated value of the credit union, membership shares, and shares.
  - Methodology and key assumptions used.
  - Day(s) on which values will be adjusted and how.
  - Certification that values and assumptions are reasonable.

### **J. Pro Forma Financial Statements**
- Based on either:
  - Most recent quarterly financial statement (if annual statement is over 120 days old), or
  - Most recent annual financial statement.
- Prepared in accordance with annual reporting standards.
- Accompanied by an auditor’s report or comments.

### **K. Capital Requirements**
- Expected capital needs over the next three years.
- Anticipated sources of capital (e.g., issuance of shares, retained earnings).

### **L. Securities Offerings**
- Description of any planned public offerings of securities during the three-year period.

### **M. Corporate Reorganization**
- Description of any anticipated corporate restructuring.

### **N. Dividend and Patronage Policy**
- Comparison of dividend and patronage allocation policies before and after conversion.

### **O. Resolutions and By-Laws**
- Copy of any special resolution to be passed by members/shareholders.
- Proposed by-laws for the converted credit union.

### **P. Auditor and Transfer Agent**
- Name of proposed auditor and transfer agent.

### **Q. Withdrawal Clause**
- Statement that the board may **withdraw the proposal at any time before conversion**.

### **R. Restrictions on Conversion**
- Summary of restrictions under **section 9** (e.g., on share transfers, issuance, etc.).

### **S. Tax Consequences**
- Summary of tax implications for members and shareholders.

## **4. Submission and Approval Process**

### **To the Superintendent**
Before the conversion can proceed, the converting credit union must submit to the **Superintendent of Financial Institutions** the following documents:
- Proposed name and date of conversion.
- Certified board resolution authorizing conversion.
- Certificate from the board that the proposal is fair and reasonable to members/shareholders.
- Copy of any special resolution to be considered.
- Proposed letters patent of conversion.
- Proposed by-laws.
- Description of any additional information to be provided to members/shareholders.
- Details of information sessions and public advertising.
- Pro forma financial statements and auditor’s report.
- Valuation report with certification of reasonableness.
- Independent fairness opinion.
- List of individuals expected to own >5% of common shares (and whether they are related parties).
- Offering documents (for any public securities offering, especially within 30 days of conversion).

> The Superintendent may:
> - Specify the valuation date.
> - Require information sessions or set rules for them.
> - Direct additional disclosures (e.g., extra valuations, fairness opinions).
> - Exempt certain provisions (e.g., valuation or public disclosure) if appropriate.

---

## **5. Restrictions on Payments and Issuances**

### **During the Period from Board Authorization to Conversion**
The credit union **must not pay**:
- **To related parties or employees**:
  - Except for regular fees and compensation on **market terms and conditions**.
  - Member and shareholder benefits as specified in the proposal.
- **To members or shareholders**:
  - Except for:
    - Interest on deposits and securities (on market terms).
    - Dividends on shares and membership shares (on market terms, per by-laws and section 12.1).
    - Patronage allocations and interest rebates (on market terms).
    - Any benefits specifically provided in the conversion proposal.

### **From Board Authorization to One Year Post-Conversion**
The credit union **must not issue or provide**:
- Shares, share options, or rights to acquire shares to:
  - Directors, officers, or employees of the **converted bank**.
  - Any person who was a director, officer, or employee of the **converting credit union during the year before conversion**.

> **Exception**: Issuance of membership shares to new applicants or for patronage allocations is allowed.

---

## **6. Restrictions on Membership and Share Issuance**

- **No new membership applications or issuance of new membership shares** may begin **30 days before the valuation report date**.
- **No issuance of membership shares or securities** (except as allowed under exceptions) begins on the day of board authorization.

---

## **7. Public Disclosure Requirements**

As soon as feasible after board authorization, the credit union must:
- Publish **weekly announcements** for **four consecutive weeks** in:
  - *The Canada Gazette*.
  - A newspaper of general circulation in each location where members normally reside.
- Post **continuously for four consecutive weeks** on its official website.
- Announcements must include:
  - The proposed conversion.
  - The restrictions under sections 8 and 9.
  - The policy on accepting new members (from board authorization until new membership is prohibited).

---

## **8. Application for Conversion (Under Section 216.08)**

An application to the Minister must include:
- All information in the conversion proposal.
- The approved conversion proposal.
- A statement confirming Superintendent approval and date.
- Special resolutions passed by members/shareholders (with board certification of vote results).
- All material required under paragraphs 6(k) to (m).
- The credit union’s response to any Superintendent requirements
