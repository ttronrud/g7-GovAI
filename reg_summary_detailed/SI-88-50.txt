**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Title:**  
*Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985 Distribution Direction*  
**Statutory Authority:**  
Pursuant to subsection 8(2) of the *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985 Act* (S.C. 1985, c. 48)  
**Issuing Body:**  
Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Justice  
**Date of Publication:**  
1987 (as enacted in S.C. 1987, c. 48)  
**Citation:**  
Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985 Distribution Direction  

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**

The primary objective of this Direction is to ensure the timely, equitable, and free distribution of the *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985* to designated individuals and institutions. The *Revised Statutes* are the official, consolidated version of Canadian federal laws, and this Direction establishes a formal mechanism to make them accessible to the public and relevant stakeholders.

---

### **Key Provisions**

1. **Distribution Requirement**  
   The *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985* must be distributed **without charge** to the persons and classes of persons listed in the attached Schedule (Schedule to the Direction).

2. **Timing of Distribution**  
   Distribution must occur **as soon as practicable** after the Revised Statutes become available. This ensures that the public and authorized entities receive the updated legal text in a timely manner, minimizing delays in legal, academic, and administrative use.

3. **Scope of Recipients**  
   The specific individuals and organizations entitled to receive the statutes are detailed in the Schedule. While the full list is not included in this summary, typical recipients under such directions may include:
   - Courts and judicial officers
   - Legal professionals (e.g., lawyers, legal advisors)
   - Law schools and universities
   - Government departments and agencies
   - Legislative bodies (e.g., Parliament, legislative committees)
   - Legal research institutions and libraries
   - Public interest organizations and NGOs involved in legal advocacy

4. **No Cost to Recipients**  
   The distribution is explicitly **free of charge**. This ensures accessibility to all eligible parties, regardless of financial resources, promoting transparency and equal access to the law.

5. **Legal Authority**  
   The Direction is issued under subsection 8(2) of the *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985 Act*, which grants the Minister of Justice the authority to direct the dissemination of the statutes. The Governor General in Council (the formal executive authority of the Crown) acts on this recommendation, giving the Direction legal force and binding effect.

---

### **Significance and Implications**

- **Promotes Legal Accessibility:** Ensures that legal professionals, educators, and the public have access to the most current version of Canadian federal law.
- **Supports Judicial and Administrative Functioning:** Enables courts, legal practitioners, and government bodies to operate with accurate and up-to-date legal references.
- **Encourages Legal Literacy and Research:** Facilitates academic study and public understanding of the Canadian legal system.
- **Ensures Consistency and Accuracy:** Provides a standardized, official source of federal statutes, reducing reliance on outdated or unofficial versions.

---

### **Limitations and Notes**

- The Direction does **not** extend to private individuals or the general public unless they are specifically listed in the Schedule. Distribution is targeted to those with a legitimate legal or institutional need.
- The Direction does not alter the content of the *Revised Statutes* but only governs their dissemination.
- It is a procedural and administrative instrument and does not have legislative effect in changing the law.

---

### **Conclusion**

This Regulation is a foundational administrative measure designed to ensure that the *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985*—the authoritative compilation of federal laws—are efficiently and freely distributed to key stakeholders in the legal and public sectors. By establishing clear criteria and timelines for distribution, it enhances access to the law, supports the rule of law, and strengthens the functioning of Canada’s legal institutions.

> **Note:** For full compliance and implementation, the actual Schedule must be consulted to identify the precise list of persons and classes of persons entitled to receive the statutes.
