**Detailed Summary of Regulation: General Import Permit No. 6 — Roses for Personal Use**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Source**
- The regulation is issued by the **Minister of Foreign Affairs** under **subsection 8(1.1)a of the Export and Import Permits Act**.
- It is based on **Section 108(1)** of **S.C. 1994, c. 47, the Export and Import Permits Act**, which empowers the Minister to issue general import permits for specific goods under certain conditions.

---

### **2. Effective Date and Implementation**
- The permit comes into force on the date that **Section 45 of the Canada-Israel Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act** (Chapter 33 of the Statutes of Canada, 1996) comes into effect.
- **Note**: The permit is effective from **January 1, 1997**, as indicated in the note (SI/97-9 — Statutory Instrument 97-9).

---

### **3. Scope of the Permit**
This permit authorizes the import of **roses** into Canada for **personal use or household use**, specifically from:
- **Israel**, or
- **Another CIFTA beneficiary** (a country participating in the Canada–Israel Free Trade Agreement or the broader CIFTA framework — Canada–Israel Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act).

---

### **4. Definition of "Roses"**
The term *"roses"* is clearly defined and includes:
- **Cut roses** and **rose buds**, which are:
  - **Fresh**
  - **Suitable for bouquets or ornamental purposes**
- These are classified under:
  - **Tariff item 0603.10.11**, or
  - **Heading 98.04 or 98.26** in the **List of Tariff Provisions** set out in the **Schedule to the Customs Tariff**.

> This ensures that only specific types of fresh cut roses (not dried, processed, or commercial floral products) are covered under this permit.

---

### **5. Quantity Limitation**
- Any **resident of Canada** may import:
  - **No more than two dozen (24) roses per importation**.
- This limit applies **per individual importation**, meaning that a person cannot bring in more than 24 roses in a single shipment, regardless of how many times they import over time.

> This restriction is designed to prevent large-scale commercial importation and to ensure the permit remains focused on **personal or household use**.

---

### **6. Reporting Requirement**
- If the import of roses under this permit is subject to **reporting requirements under the Customs Act**, the importer must include the following statement on the prescribed form:
  - In **English**: *"Imported under the authority of General Import Permit No. 6 — Roses for Personal Use"*
  - In **French**: *"Importé en vertu de la Licence générale d’importation no 6 — Roses pour usage personnel"*

> This ensures transparency and compliance with customs procedures, allowing customs officials to verify that the import is authorized under this specific permit.

---

### **7. Purpose of the Permit**
- The permit is intended to:
  - Facilitate the **personal importation** of roses by Canadian residents.
  - Support **consumer access** to fresh cut roses for domestic use (e.g., for home decoration, personal gifts, or household arrangements).
  - Align with the **Canada–Israel Free Trade Agreement (CIFTA)**, which aims to reduce trade barriers and promote trade between Canada and Israel and other CIFTA partner countries.

---

### **8. Key Policy Implications**
- **Non-commercial intent**: The permit is strictly limited to **personal or household use**, not for resale, commercial distribution, or bulk supply.
- **Simplified process**: It streamlines the import process for small quantities of roses, reducing bureaucratic hurdles for individuals.
- **Compliance with international trade agreements**: Reflects the provisions of the **Canada–Israel Free Trade Agreement**, particularly those related to the liberalization of certain goods and the facilitation of personal imports from partner countries.

---

### **9. Exclusions and Limitations**
- The permit **does not apply** to:
  - Dried or processed roses
  - Commercial or wholesale imports
  - Imports for resale or commercial purposes
- The **two-dozen limit** applies per importation, not per year or per household.
- The permit does **not cover other floral products** or goods outside the defined tariff classifications.

---

### **Conclusion**
General Import Permit No. 6 — Roses for Personal Use is a targeted, consumer-focused regulation that enables Canadian residents to import fresh cut roses (or rose buds) from Israel or other CIFTA beneficiary countries for personal or household use. It operates under strict quantity limits (24 roses per importation), requires proper reporting, and is aligned with Canada’s trade obligations under the Canada–Israel Free Trade Agreement. The permit ensures that trade benefits are accessible to individuals while maintaining customs control and compliance with tariff and regulatory standards.

---

**Final Note**: This permit is a **non-renewable, time-bound** authorization that became active on **January 1, 1997**, and remains valid until revoked or amended by the Minister.
