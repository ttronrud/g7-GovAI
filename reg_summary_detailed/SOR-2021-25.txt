### ðŸ”¹ **3. Core Regulatory Objectives**

The primary goals of these regulations are:

1. **Ensure Safe and Environmentally Responsible Cross-border Movement**  
   Prevent pollution and protect human health and ecosystems during the transport, transit, and disposal of hazardous waste and recyclable materials.

2. **Compliance with International Agreements**  
   Align Canadaâ€™s practices with:
   - The **Basel Convention** (1992) â€“ controls transboundary movement of hazardous waste.
   - The **Canada-USA Agreement** â€“ facilitates cooperation on waste movement.
   - **OECD Decision C(2001)107/FINAL** â€“ sets standards for waste recovery operations.

3. **Strengthen Accountability and Transparency**  
   Require detailed **notifications** and **movement documents** with specific line entries to ensure traceability and oversight.

4. **Promote Safe Handling and Transport**  
   Apply strict rules on carriers, consignors, consignees, and facilities to ensure safe handling and compliance with national and international standards.

5. **Support Sustainable Recycling**  
   Regulate the movement of recyclable materials to ensure they are properly processed and do not become environmental hazards.
