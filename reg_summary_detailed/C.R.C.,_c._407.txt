## üìå Overview of the Regulation (Prior to Repeal)

This regulation established reporting obligations for certain **liquid fossil fuels** originating from **crude oil, coal, or bituminous sands** in Canada. It required producers and importers to report specific information to the **Minister of the Environment** (or relevant federal department) on a periodic basis, particularly concerning fuel types and additives used in their production or importation.

## üìù Reporting Obligations

### 1. **General Reporting Requirement (Section 1)**  
Every person who:
- Produces in Canada **or**
- Imports into Canada  
more than **400 cubic metres** of a fuel listed in **Form 1 of the Schedule**  
must submit, **for each quarter** of the calendar year, the information required by **Form 1**.

‚úÖ **Deadline**:  
The information must be submitted **by January 31st** of the year following the calendar year in which the fuel was produced or imported.

> Example: If fuel was produced in 2023, the report must be submitted by January 31, 2024.

### 2. **Additive Reporting Requirement (Section 2)**  
Every person who produces or imports a fuel containing **any additive other than lead or lead compounds** must:

- Submit information required by **Form 2 of the Schedule**  
- **Within 60 days** of selling a cumulative total of **400 cubic metres** of that fuel containing the additive during a calendar year.

> Note: This applies only to **new or previously unreported additives**. Once reported, no further reporting is needed for that additive in future years (unless there‚Äôs a change).

### 3. **Change Reporting Requirement (Section 4)**  
If any **change occurs** in the information previously submitted under:
- Section 1 (Form 1)
- Section 2 (Form 2)  
then the person must report the change to the Minister **within 60 days** of the change occurring.
