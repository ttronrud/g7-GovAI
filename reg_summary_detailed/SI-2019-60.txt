**Regulation Summary:**

**Title:** Designation of the Minister for the Purpose of the Federal Prompt Payment for Construction Work Act  
**Legal Citation:** S.C. 2019, c. 29, s. 387  
**Issuing Authority:** Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister  
**Governing Provision:** Section 3 of the *Federal Prompt Payment for Construction Work Act*  

---

**Summary:**

This regulation designates the **Minister of Public Works and Government Services** as the **Minister responsible for the administration and implementation of the Federal Prompt Payment for Construction Work Act**.

Key Details:

1. **Legal Basis:**  
   The designation is made under **Section 3 of the Federal Prompt Payment for Construction Work Act**. This section empowers the Governor General in Council (acting on the advice of the Prime Minister) to appoint or designate a minister to serve as the official point of responsibility for the Act.

2. **Purpose of the Designation:**  
   The primary purpose is to establish clear administrative accountability and authority for enforcing the prompt payment obligations under the Act. By designating a specific minister, the government ensures that there is a designated government official responsible for:
   - Overseeing the implementation of the Act;
   - Receiving and processing complaints or disputes related to delays in payment;
   - Ensuring compliance with the Act’s requirements regarding timely payment of construction work;
   - Coordinating with relevant departments and agencies to enforce prompt payment practices.

3. **Designated Minister:**  
   The **Minister of Public Works and Government Services** is appointed as the Minister for the purposes of the Act. This minister is also a member of the **Queen’s Privy Council for Canada**, which affirms their status as a senior, official government representative with the authority to act on behalf of the Crown in matters of legislation and policy.

4. **Significance of the Appointment:**  
   - Provides a clear line of responsibility within the federal government for construction payment timelines.
   - Enhances transparency and accountability in public infrastructure projects where contractors are often subject to payment delays.
   - Supports the Act’s goal of improving financial predictability and reducing disputes in the construction sector by ensuring prompt payments to contractors and subcontractors.

5. **Effective Date:**  
   The regulation is part of **Statutory Code 2019, Chapter 29**, which came into force on the date of enactment. The designation is effective immediately upon publication in the Canada Gazette and is binding under the Act.

6. **Scope of Authority:**  
   The designated Minister has the authority to:
   - Issue directives or guidelines related to the Act;
   - Monitor compliance with prompt payment requirements;
   - Liaise with federal departments, provincial governments, and construction stakeholders;
   - Report on implementation and outcomes to Parliament, as required.

---

**Conclusion:**  
This regulation establishes a formal and legally binding mechanism to assign leadership responsibility for the Federal Prompt Payment for Construction Work Act. By designating the Minister of Public Works and Government Services, the federal government strengthens its commitment to fair, timely, and transparent payment practices in public construction projects—ultimately benefiting contractors, promoting project efficiency, and supporting economic stability in the construction sector.
