**Detailed Summary of the Criminal Procedure Rules of the Court of Queen’s Bench of New Brunswick (2014)**

---

### **Overview and Purpose**

The *Criminal Procedure Rules of the Court of Queen’s Bench of New Brunswick*, numbered 1 to 12, were adopted on **September 5, 2014**, by a majority of the judges of the Court of Queen’s Bench of New Brunswick under **subsections 482(1) and (3)** of the *Criminal Code of Canada*. These rules came into effect upon publication in the *Canada Gazette* as required by subsection 482(4) of the *Criminal Code*.

The primary objective of these rules is to ensure **fair, efficient, and uniform** handling of all criminal proceedings within the jurisdiction of the Court. They are designed to:

- Promote **justice and fairness** in criminal proceedings;
- Ensure **simplicity and uniformity** in procedure;
- Eliminate **unjustifiable expense**;
- Avoid **unnecessary delay**;
- Provide **flexibility** where needed for the interests of justice.

These rules are **intended to be liberally interpreted** where gaps exist in the *Criminal Code* or where specific procedural matters are not addressed.

---

### **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Application** | A proceeding initiated by a *Notice of Application*, whether under statutory authority or another legal basis. |
| **Clerk’s Office** | The office of the Clerk of the Court in the judicial district where the proceeding is commenced, pending, or being heard. |
| **Court** | The Court of Queen’s Bench of New Brunswick, including any judge presiding. |
| **Judge** | A Justice of the Court. |

> These rules apply to **all criminal proceedings** within the jurisdiction of the Court.

---

### **General Principles and Interpretation**

1. **Flexibility and Fairness**  
   - Where a matter is not covered by the *Criminal Code* or these rules, the Court may determine procedure or refer to the *Rules of Court of New Brunswick*.
   - The rules are to be **liberally construed** to achieve fairness, simplicity, and efficiency.

2. **Representation of Accused**  
   - If an accused appears *in person* (without counsel), any procedural requirement that applies to counsel may be fulfilled by the accused.  
   - However, any procedural privilege granted to counsel (e.g., to file or withdraw) is **not automatically available** to the accused unless explicitly allowed.

3. **Interpretation**  
   - The **interpretation sections of the Criminal Code** apply to these rules.

4. **Practice Notes**  
   - The **Chief Justice** may issue **practice notes** that are consistent with these rules. These notes have the **same force and effect** as the rules themselves.

5. **Forms**  
   - The **Appendix of Forms** must be used where applicable, with reasonable variations based on circumstances.

6. **Failure to Comply**  
   - A failure to comply with these rules is considered an **irregularity**, but **does not render a proceeding, order, or document null**.
   - The Court may:
     - Grant **amendments or relief** under **Rule 2.02** to secure a just outcome; or
     - Set aside a step, document, or order **only if necessary in the interests of justice**.

7. **Discretionary Powers**  
   - The Court may:
     - **Dispense with** any rule at any time if it deems it just;
     - **Extend or abridge time limits** on reasonable terms, especially in the interests of justice.
   - Time extensions may be granted **before or after** the original deadline.

8. **Document Standards**  
   - All documents must be:
     - Printed, typed, or reproduced **legibly** on **good quality paper**.
     - Contain:
       - Name of the Court, judicial district, and court file number;
       - Style of the proceeding (using Form 1), or a short title (e.g., "Smith and others") if more than two parties;
       - Date of the document;
       - Name, address, telephone number, and (if available) email/fax number of counsel or party filing.

9. **Certified Copies**  
   - A person entitled to inspect a document may request a **certified copy** upon payment of a prescribed fee.

### **Service of Documents**

1. **Manner of Service**  
   - Service must generally be in **writing**.
   - Documents are to be filed with the **Clerk’s Office**, unless served during a hearing or otherwise provided.

2. **Filing by Mail**  
   - A document filed by mail is deemed filed on the **date of the filing stamp** in the Clerk’s office.
   - If the Clerk’s office has **no record of receipt**, the document is deemed **not filed**.

3. **Affidavits**  
   - An affidavit must contain:
     - Facts known personally to the deponent;
     - Statements of information or belief about non-contentious facts, **only if the source is clearly specified**.
   - For **corporations**, an affidavit may be signed by an officer, director, or employee.

4. **Service Methods**  
   - **Personal Service**:
     - On individuals: leave with the individual.
     - On corporations: leave with an officer, director, agent, or person in control of a business location.
     - On the **Attorney General of Canada**: leave with a Crown Prosecutor employee or via **fax**.
     - On the **Attorney General of New Brunswick**: leave with a Crown Prosecutor employee or via **fax**.
   - **No original document need be carried** during personal service.

5. **Alternative to Personal Service**  
   - Service may be made via:
     - **Mail** to last known address + **Acknowledgment of Receipt Card (Form 3A)**.
     - **Fax or telephone transmission** (with specific requirements).
     - **Electronic mail (email)** to counsel or authorized employee.

6. **Effective Service via Fax or Email**  
   - **Fax transmission** requires:
     - Cover page with sender details, recipient, date/time, number of pages, transmission number, and contact info.
     - Acknowledgment of receipt card (Form 3B).
     - Service is effective **only if counsel endorses and returns** the card.
   - **Email transmission**:
     - Requires confirmation of receipt by counsel or authorized employee.
     - Service is effective only upon such confirmation.

7. **Acceptance of Service**  
   - When counsel accepts service, they are deemed to represent that they have **authority from their client** to accept service.

8. **Service on Accused (Unrepresented)**  
   - Must be by **personal service** unless:
     - The Court **approves an alternative method**; or
     - The Court **dispenses with service**.

9. **Defenses Against Default**  
   - Even if a document is served properly, a person may apply to:
     - Set aside consequences of default;
     - Request an extension of time;
     - Ask for adjournment.
   - Grounds include:
     - The document was **not brought to their attention**; or
     - It was received **after the time of service**.

10. **Invalid Service – Court Validation**  
    - If service is made improperly, the Court may **validate it** if it is satisfied that:
      - The document came to the person’s notice; or
      - It would have come to their notice **except for their own attempts to evade service**.

11. **Proof of Service**  
    - Service may be proved by:
      - An affidavit in **Form 4A or 4B**; or
      - A **written admission or acceptance** by counsel (which is sufficient and does not require verification).

12. **Substituted or Dispensed Service**  
    - If prompt service is impractical, the Court may:
      - Order **substituted service** (with a specified effective date); or
      - **Dispense with service** if necessary in the interests of justice.

---

### **Commencement of an Application**

1. **Notice of Application (Form 1)**  
   - All applications or motions must be commenced by a **Notice of Application in Form 1**.
   - Must be filed with the **Clerk’s Office**.

2. **Notification of Third Parties**  
   - If a third party’s interests may be affected, the applicant must **notify them** as if they were a party.

3. **Ex Parte Applications**  
   - A party may apply *ex parte* for directions if there is uncertainty about whether a non-party should be served.

4. **Intervention**  
   - Any person with a legitimate interest may **intervene** with the **leave of the Court**, subject to terms and rights determined by the Court.

5. **Content of Notice of Application**  
   Must include:
   - Place and date of hearing;
   - Precise relief sought;
   - Grounds (with reference to statutes or rules);
   - Documentary, affidavit, and other evidence to be presented;
   - Whether any order is required to **abridge or extend** time for service or filing.

6. **Service and Filing Requirements**  
   - Notice of Application and supporting evidence must be served on **all parties** and filed with the Clerk’s Office.
   - **Minimum filing deadline**: **45 days** before the hearing or trial (whichever comes first),
