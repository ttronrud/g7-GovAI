**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Proclamation of Canadian Multiculturalism Day**

---

**Title:**  
Proclamation Declaring June 27 as "Canadian Multiculturalism Day"

**Issuing Authority:**  
Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II, by and with the advice of the Privy Council for Canada, through the Governor in Council.  
Formally issued by the Governor General of Canada, Adrienne Clarkson, on behalf of the Crown.

**Date of Issuance:**  
November 14, 2002  
(Year: 2002; in the 51st year of Her Reign)

**Location:**  
Government House, Ottawa, Canada

---

### **Background and Purpose**

This proclamation establishes **June 27 of each year as "Canadian Multiculturalism Day"**, a national day dedicated to:

- Celebrating the **diversity of Canada’s population**;
- Recognizing the **valuable contributions** of Canadians from all cultural, ethnic, racial, linguistic, and religious backgrounds to Canadian society;
- Reinforcing the principle that **multiculturalism is a foundational element of Canadian identity and heritage**.

The proclamation is rooted in the understanding that Canada’s strength and unity are built upon the rich diversity of its people, whose unique traditions, perspectives, and experiences enrich national life.

---

### **Key Provisions**

1. **Establishment of a National Holiday**  
   The Governor in Council, acting under the authority of the Crown, formally declares **June 27 as "Canadian Multiculturalism Day"** annually.

2. **Purpose of the Day**  
   The day is intended to:
   - **Honor and acknowledge** the contributions of diverse communities to Canada’s social, economic, cultural, and political development;
   - Promote **awareness and appreciation** of Canada’s multicultural character;
   - Encourage **inclusion, equity, and mutual respect** among all Canadians;
   - Provide an opportunity for **public events, educational programs, community gatherings, and cultural celebrations**.

3. **Legal Basis**  
   The proclamation is issued pursuant to **Order in Council P.C. 2002-1869**, dated October 31, 2002. This order authorized the Governor in Council to issue a proclamation declaring a specific date as a national observance in recognition of multiculturalism.

4. **Legal Effect**  
   The proclamation is binding upon all persons and institutions in Canada. It is a formal declaration of intent and is recognized as a **non-statutory but legally recognized national observance**, meaning it carries official status and is expected to be observed by federal, provincial, territorial, and municipal governments, schools, businesses, and communities.

---

### **Historical and Political Context**

- The proclamation reflects Canada’s long-standing commitment to **multiculturalism**, a policy enshrined in the **Canadian Multiculturalism Act (1988)** and reinforced through national policy and public education.
- June 27 was chosen as the date because it was the **original date of the first official Canadian Multiculturalism Day**, established in 1997 as a public holiday in recognition of the country’s diversity.
- The 2002 proclamation formalized and elevated this observance to a **permanent, annual national day**, ensuring continuity and institutional recognition.

---

### **Significance and Impact**

- **Symbolic Importance:** The day serves as a public affirmation of Canada’s identity as a multicultural nation, where diversity is not only accepted but celebrated.
- **Educational Value:** Schools and institutions are encouraged to use the day to teach students about cultural traditions, histories, and the importance of inclusion.
- **Community Engagement:** Local governments and organizations are encouraged to host events such as cultural fairs, food festivals, art exhibitions, storytelling sessions, and intercultural dialogues.
- **Policy Alignment:** The day supports broader government initiatives related to equity, diversity, inclusion, and anti-racism.

---

### **Legal and Constitutional Framework**

- The proclamation is issued under the **Royal Prerogative**, exercised by the Governor in Council with the advice of the Privy Council.
- It does not create new legislation but serves as a **proclamation of policy and recognition**, consistent with Canada’s constitutional framework and the principles of multiculturalism enshrined in the *Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms* and the *Multiculturalism Act*.
- It is not a statutory holiday with paid leave, but rather a **cultural and civic observance**.

---

### **Conclusion**

The Proclamation of Canadian Multiculturalism Day (June 27) is a significant and enduring expression of Canada’s commitment to diversity, inclusion, and national unity. By formally designating this date, the Canadian government affirms that the contributions of all Canadians—regardless of background—are central to the nation’s identity and progress. The day serves both as a celebration and a call to action, urging Canadians to engage in respectful dialogue, embrace cultural differences, and build a more inclusive society.

---

**Note:**  
This proclamation is a **non-binding but official national observance**. While not a statutory holiday with legal entitlements (such as paid leave), it is widely observed across Canada and recognized by federal, provincial, and municipal authorities as a day of cultural significance.
