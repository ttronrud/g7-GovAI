### **4. List of Accidents or Incidents That Must Be Reported**

The regulations define **25 specific categories** of events (labeled (a)(i) to (xxv)) that constitute a **military-civilian occurrence** and must be reported. These include:

| Category | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **(i)** | Injury, illness, or death of a person due to exposure to aircraft systems (jet blast, propeller, rotor downwash), or being on board, in contact with aircraft, or during maintenance |
| **(ii)** | Structural damage or system failure affecting flight characteristics or performance requiring repair/replacement |
| **(iii)** | Aircraft going missing or becoming inaccessible |
| **(iv)** | Engine failure or precautionary shutdown |
| **(v)** | Transmission gearbox malfunction |
| **(vi)** | Smoke or fire not related to normal operation |
| **(vii)** | Loss of control due to system failure, weather, wake turbulence, vibrations, or flight envelope violation |
| **(viii)** | Landing/takeoff outside intended area or with landing gear retracted |
| **(ix)** | Aircraft dragging any part |
| **(x)** | Crew unable to perform duties due to physical incapacitation or life-support failure |
| **(xi)** | Depressurization requiring emergency descent, activation of emergency systems, or use of emergency life-support |
| **(xii)** | Fuel shortage forcing diversion or priority landing |
| **(xiii)** | Incorrect or contaminated fuel/lubricant used during servicing |
| **(xiv)** | Collision (not part of normal operations) between aircraft or aircraft and terrain/object |
| **(xv)** | Aircraft coming dangerously close to a collision, compromising safety |
| **(xvi)** | Distance between two aircraft less than minimum separation standards (as per Canadian Domestic Air Traffic Control Standards) |
| **(xvii)** | Crew declaring an emergency or indicating need for priority handling |
| **(xviii)** | Malfunction of emergency devices (e.g., ejection seats, bombs, jammers, cartridges, targets) |
| **(xix)** | Unintentional release of slung load (precautionary or emergency) |
| **(xx)** | Release of dangerous goods (as defined in *Transportation of Dangerous Goods Act, 1992*) |
| **(xxi)** | Defect or deterioration not expected from normal use or maintenance |
| **(xxii)** | Damage to aeronautical product during maintenance |
| **(xxiii)** | Unauthorized deviation from approved maintenance program |
| **(xxiv)** | Object found in aircraft out of place (could cause damage) |
| **(xxv)** | Tool lost during maintenance |

### **5. When Must a Report Be Made?**

#### **Mandatory Reporting Conditions**:
- A civilian must report **immediately** if they have **direct knowledge** of a military-civilian occurrence listed above.
- The report must be made **as soon as feasible** and via the **quickest available means** (e.g., phone, email, in person).

#### **Exception to Reporting**:
A civilian **does not need to report** if:
1. They have already reported it to the **Canadian Transportation Accident Investigation and Safety Board (CTAISB)** under subsection 2(1) of the *Transportation Safety Board Regulations*;  
   OR  
2. The Authority has **already received** a report from another source.

> This prevents duplication and ensures efficiency in the reporting chain.

---

### **6. Required Information in Reports**

When reporting, the civilian must provide the following information:

| Information | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Aircraft details | Type, model, registration mark, country of registration |
| Personnel details | Owner, pilot-in-command, lessee (if applicable) |
| Flight history | Last point of departure, date/time of departure, intended destination |
| Time of incident | Date and time of the occurrence |
| Air traffic services provider | Name of person providing air traffic services |
| Casualties (if applicable) | Number of crew, passengers, injuries, deaths |
| Location | Geographically defined (e.g., nearest landmark, latitude/longitude) |
| Description of event | Full account of what happened, extent of damage to aircraft/environment |
| Dangerous goods | Shipping name, UN number, consignor/consignee (if any) |
| Missing/inaccessible aircraft | Last known position, time, and actions taken to locate |
| Access attempts | Actions taken to gain access to the aircraft |
| Protective measures | Actions taken to protect people, property, environment |
| Reporter details | Name, title, phone number, address |

> All information **available at the time** of the event must be reported.  
> Any **additional information** that becomes available later must be reported **as soon as possible**.

---

### **7. Reporting by Non-Civilian Entities**

- Any **non-civilian** (e.g., military personnel, government agencies) who has information about a military-civilian occurrence or a related incident may report it to the Authority **by any means** (e.g., email, phone, written letter).

> This ensures broad access to information and supports transparency and safety.

---

### **8. Protection of Reporter Identity**

- The **identity of a civilian reporter is protected** under the regulations.
- This protection applies **unless** the civilian **waives it in writing**.

> This encourages reporting without fear of retaliation or exposure, promoting a culture of safety and accountability.

---

### **9. Preservation of Evidence**

- Every person who has **possession or control of evidence** related to a military-civilian occurrence must **preserve that evidence** until the Authority determines it is no longer required.

> This ensures that critical data remains available for investigation.

- **Exemption from preservation** may be granted if **another party has already preserved** the evidence.

> This prevents redundant or conflicting evidence collection.

---

### **10. Safety Measures and Reporting Requirements**

- The regulations **do not prevent** individuals from taking **necessary safety measures** (e.g., evacuating, securing aircraft, protecting environment).
- However, **before** taking such actions, individuals must:
  - **Record evidence** (to the best of their ability),
  - **Advise the Authority** of their actions as soon as feasible.

> This balances immediate safety with regulatory accountability.
