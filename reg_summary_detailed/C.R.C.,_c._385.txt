## üìå IV. Contribution Calculation Rules

### A. Base and First Additional Contributions (for qualifying payments)

- **Qualifying Payment**: Portion of remuneration that is ordinary remuneration from pensionable employment.
- **Formula**:
  $$
  \text{Contribution} = (\text{Payment} - \text{Basic Exemption}) \times (\text{Base Rate} + \text{First Additional Rate})
  $$
- **Rounding Rule**: Rounded to nearest $0.01; if equidistant, rounded **up**.

> Example: If an employee earns $200 in a pay period with a $14.58 basic exemption, and rates are 9.9% and 1.5%, the contribution is calculated accordingly.

## üöÄ V. Second Additional Contribution (for high earners)

- **Applies only when**:
  $$
  \text{Payment} > \text{Total remuneration to date} \quad \text{and} \quad \text{Total remuneration} > \text{Max Pensionable Earnings}
  $$
- **Formula**:
  $$
  \text{Second Additional Contribution} = (\text{Payment} - \text{Total Remuneration to Date}) \times \text{Second Additional Rate}
  $$
  (Only if result > 0)

> This is designed to capture contributions from **high-income earners** who exceed the standard pensionable earnings limit.

- **Annual cap**: Total second additional contributions **cannot exceed** the year‚Äôs maximum second additional contribution.

## üîÅ VI. Coordination with Provincial Pension Plans

To avoid **double contribution**, the CPP adjusts contributions when employees already contribute under provincial plans.

### A. For Base & First Additional Contributions:
- The employee‚Äôs CPP contribution **cannot exceed**:
  $$
  (\text{Max Base + Max First Additional}) - (\text{Previous contributions under provincial plan})
  $$
- Adjustment factor:  
  $$
  \text{Ratio} = \frac{\text{CPP Rate (base + first)}}{\text{Provincial Plan Rate (base + first)}}
  $$

> So if a provincial plan has a lower rate, the CPP contribution is reduced proportionally.

### B. For Second Additional Contributions:
- Similarly, the CPP contribution **cannot exceed**:
  $$
  \text{Max Second Additional} - (\text{Previous provincial contributions})
  $$
- Adjustment factor:  
  $$
  \text{Ratio} = \frac{\text{CPP Second Rate}}{\text{Provincial Second Rate}}
  $$
