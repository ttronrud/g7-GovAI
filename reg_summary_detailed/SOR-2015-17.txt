The regulation is enacted under **Section 19a** of the *Hazardous Products Act* (HPA), which mandates consultation with provincial governments, worker representatives, employer organizations, and supplier organizations before introducing new regulatory measures.  
It is formally adopted by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Health**, pursuant to **subsection 15(1)** of the HPA.  
This regulation establishes detailed classification, labeling, and evaluation criteria for hazardous products in Canada, aligning with international standards such as the **Globally Harmonized System (GHS)** and the **United Nations Model Regulations**.

## ğŸ§ª **Classification Principles and Procedures**

### 1. **Health Professionals**
- Defined as:
  - Physicians registered and practicing under provincial medical laws.
  - Nurses registered or licensed and practicing under provincial nursing laws.

> Used in the context of health hazard evaluations, especially for biological agents.

---

### 2. **Use of "Should"**
- In referenced or incorporated texts, **"should" is interpreted as imperative** (i.e., mandatory), unless the context clearly indicates otherwise.

> Ensures strict compliance with regulatory requirements.

---

### 3. **Order of Hazard Severity in Classification Tables**
- In each subpart of Parts 7 and 8 (which cover hazard classifications), categories and subcategories are listed in **order of decreasing severity**.
- **Exception**: For Subpart 5 of Part 7 (related to biological hazards), the order may differ.

> This ensures that the most severe hazard is always evaluated first.

---

### 4. **Classification Rule: Most Severe Hazard Wins**
- If a product meets criteria for **multiple hazard categories/subcategories**, it must be classified in the **one representing the most severe hazard**.
- Once a category is assigned, **no further evaluation** is required for less severe categories in the same table.
- If a product is classified in a **more severe category**, it **does not need to be evaluated** for less severe ones.

> Prevents over-classification and ensures hazard clarity.

---

### 5. **Prescribed Classification in Schedule 4**
- If a product, mixture, or substance is **prescribed** in **Schedule 4** (a list of substances with fixed classifications), it **must be classified in that specific category/subcategory**.
- Additionally, the product **must be evaluated** for **all other hazard classes** (e.g., physical, health, environmental) using the relevant sections (e.g., 2.1, 2.2, 2.7).

> Ensures full hazard assessment even when one hazard is predefined.

---

### 6. **Mixtures with More Severe Ingredients**
- If a mixture contains one or more ingredients classified in a **more severe hazard category** within the same classification table, the **mixture must be classified in that more severe category**.
- This applies only when the more severe ingredient is present in a concentration above the threshold specified in the hazard class.

> Ensures that mixtures are not downgraded due to less dangerous components.

---

### 7. **Impurities, Stabilizers, and Additives**
- **Known impurities, stabilizing solvents, or additives** that are:
  - Classified as hazardous, and
  - Present at a concentration **above the threshold** specified in a hazard class,

> Must be considered during classification.

> This ensures that additives or impurities do not reduce the hazard classification of a product.

---

### 8. **Packaging of Multiple Products**
- If **two or more individually packaged products** are combined in an outer container for sale or import, the **assemblage is not considered a single product**.
- Each product must be evaluated **separately** and classified independently.

> Prevents misclassification due to packaging.

---

### 9. **Animal Data Relevance**
- Animal data from a specific species **cannot be used** to classify a mixture or substance in any health hazard class (Subparts 1â€“10, 12 of Part 8) **if it has been proven scientifically** that the mechanism of action in that species is **not relevant to humans**.

> Promotes human-relevant data and avoids misleading hazard assessments.
