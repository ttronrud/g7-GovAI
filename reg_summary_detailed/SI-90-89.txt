**Regulation Summary:**

**Title:** Specification of the National Capital Region as the Head Office Location for Key Canadian Museums  
**Date of Effectiveness:** July 1, 1990  
**Governing Authority:** His Excellency the Governor General in Council, acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister  
**Legal Basis:** Section 25 of the *Museums Act* (S.C. 1990, c. 3)  

---

**Overview:**

This regulation formally designates the **National Capital Region (NCR)**—as defined in the Schedule to the *National Capital Act*—as the official and exclusive location for the head offices of four of Canada’s most prominent national museums. The decision was made pursuant to statutory authority under Section 25 of the *Museums Act*, which empowers the federal government to specify the location of a museum’s head office.

---

**Key Provisions:**

1. **Museums Affected:**
   The regulation applies specifically to the following four national museums:
   - The National Gallery of Canada  
   - The Canadian Museum of Civilization  
   - The Canadian Museum of Nature  
   - The National Museum of Science and Technology  

2. **Designated Location:**
   The head office of each of these institutions shall be located within the **National Capital Region (NCR)**.  
   The boundaries and definition of the NCR are formally established in the **Schedule to the National Capital Act**, which typically includes Ottawa and its surrounding areas (such as the city of Ottawa, the National Capital Commission’s designated area, and key federal and provincial zones within the region).

3. **Effective Date:**
   The regulation comes into force on **July 1, 1990**. This date marks the official transfer or confirmation of the head office locations for the four institutions.
