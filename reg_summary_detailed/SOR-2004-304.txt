**Detailed Summary of the Saskatchewan Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations (S.C. 2004, c. 10)**  
*Effective Date: December 8, 2004 (came into force on December 15, 2004)*

---

### **Overview**
The *Saskatchewan Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations* were made by the Lieutenant Governor in Council on the recommendation of the Saskatchewan Minister of Justice and Attorney General, under subsection 18(1) of the *Sex Offender Information Registration Act* (SOIRA). These regulations provide the operational framework for how sex offenders in Saskatchewan must register information, how that information is collected, and where registration can occur.

The regulations are designed to ensure transparency, accountability, and public safety by establishing clear procedures for the registration of sex offenders and the collection and management of related data within the province.

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Act**  
   Refers to the *Sex Offender Information Registration Act*, which provides the legal foundation for the registration and management of information about individuals convicted of certain sex offences.

2. **Police Service**  
   Defined as having the same meaning as in paragraph 2(q) of *The Police Act, 1990*, as amended. This includes all provincial police services operating in Saskatchewan (e.g., municipal police services).

3. **RCMP**  
   Stands for the Royal Canadian Mounted Police — the national federal law enforcement agency.

---

### **Reporting and Notification Requirements**

#### 1. **Telephone Reporting for Sex Offenders Residing in Saskatchewan**
- A sex offender whose **main residence is located in Saskatchewan** may fulfill any reporting requirement under:
  - Paragraph 4.1(1)(a) or (b) of the Act (initial or periodic registration reports)
  - Section 4.3 of the Act (updates or changes to registration information)
- These reports **can be made by telephone**, making it accessible and convenient for offenders to comply with registration obligations.

#### 2. **Notification Under Section 6 of the Act**
- Any notification required under **Section 6** (e.g., changes in address, contact information, or other personal details) may be provided:
  - **In person**, or  
  - **By telephone**
- This flexibility supports compliance while reducing barriers to participation.

---

### **Authorized Persons to Collect Information**

The following individuals or entities are authorized in Saskatchewan to **collect information** for the purposes of the Act:

- **Members of the RCMP** who serve in Saskatchewan  
- **Members of a police service** (municipal or other)  
- **Persons employed by the RCMP or a police service** whose duties include collecting information under the Act

> **Important Note**:  
> Any person authorized under **Section 3 of the Act** (which grants authority to collect information) is also authorized in Saskatchewan to **register information** under the Act. This ensures consistency and accountability in data handling.

---

### **Designated Registration Centres in Saskatchewan**

The following locations are officially designated as **registration centres** in the province:

1. **Saskatchewan Sex Offender Central Registration Centre**  
   - Administered by the RCMP “F” Division  
   - Address: 6101 Dewdney Avenue, Regina  
   - Serves as the central hub for managing and processing registration data across the province.

2. **Each RCMP Detachment**  
   - All RCMP detachments operating in Saskatchewan are authorized to serve as registration centres.

3. **Municipal Police Service Offices in the Following Cities**:  
   - Estevan  
   - Moose Jaw  
   - Prince Albert  
   - Regina  
   - Saskatoon  
   - Weyburn  

4. **Each Office of the File Hills First Nations Police Service**  
   - Recognized as a registration centre due to its role in serving Indigenous communities.

> **Service Area**:  
> The **designated area of service** for each registration centre is the **entire Province of Saskatchewan**. This means that any sex offender residing in Saskatchewan can register or update their information at any of these centres, regardless of location.

---

### **Effective Date**

These regulations come into force on the **later of two dates**:
- The date on which the *Sex Offender Information Registration Act* (chapter 10 of the Statutes of Canada, 2004) comes into force  
- The date on which these regulations are officially registered in the *Statutes of Saskatchewan*

> The regulations were registered on **December 15, 2004**, and thus came into force on that date.

---

### **Purpose and Implications**

- **Public Safety**: Enables authorities to track and monitor individuals convicted of certain sex offences, helping to protect communities.
- **Accessibility**: Allows offenders to report and update information via telephone or in person, reducing administrative burdens and improving compliance.
- **Coordination**: Establishes a consistent, provincially managed system involving both federal (RCMP) and local (municipal police, First Nations) law enforcement bodies.
- **Inclusivity**: Includes First Nations police services, reflecting the importance of community-based policing and cultural sensitivity in enforcement.

---

### **Key Takeaways**

| Feature | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Applicability** | Applies to all sex offenders whose main residence is in Saskatchewan |
| **Reporting Methods** | Can be done by phone or in person |
| **Authorized Collectors** | RCMP members and police personnel with designated duties |
| **Registration Centres** | Central RCMP office in Regina + all RCMP detachments + municipal offices in 6 cities + File Hills First Nations Police Service |
| **Service Coverage** | Entire province of Saskatchewan |
| **Effective Date** | December 15, 2004 |

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Saskatchewan Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations* establish a clear, accessible, and inclusive system for the registration and management of sex offender information. By leveraging both federal and local law enforcement resources, and by allowing flexible reporting methods, the regulations aim to balance public safety with practical compliance. The designation of multiple registration centres ensures that individuals across the province — including in rural and Indigenous communities — can participate in the system without undue difficulty.

These regulations remain a key component of Saskatchewan’s broader strategy to manage sexual offender information responsibly and transparently.
