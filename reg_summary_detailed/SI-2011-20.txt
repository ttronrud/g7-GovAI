**Detailed Summary of the Rules of the Court of Queen’s Bench for Saskatchewan (2011) – Summary Conviction Appeals**

---

### **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**
These Rules were established by the **Court of Queen’s Bench for Saskatchewan** under:
- **Section 482a of the Criminal Code (R.S.C. 1985, c. C-46)** – which grants appellate jurisdiction over summary conviction appeals;
- **Section 28 of the Queen’s Bench Act, 1998 (S.S. 1998, c. Q-1.01)** – empowering the court to make procedural rules for appeals.

The Rules govern the **procedural framework for appeals from summary conviction courts** in Saskatchewan, ensuring consistency, fairness, and efficiency in appellate proceedings.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Appeal** | An appeal from a summary conviction court under Part XXVII of the Criminal Code and the *Summary Offences Procedure Act, 1990*. |
| **Appeal Court** | The Court of Queen’s Bench for Saskatchewan. |
| **Authority** | Entities with jurisdiction to issue bylaws, including municipalities, Meewasin Valley Authority, Wakamow Valley Authority, Wascana Centre Authority, and the University of Regina or Saskatchewan. |
| **File** | To submit documents to a local registrar. |
| **Form** | Standardized forms (Appendix to the Rules) used for filing notices and memoranda. |
| **Judge** | A judge of the appeal court. |
| **Local Registrar** | Includes deputy registrars; responsible for receiving and managing appeal filings. |
| **Prosecutor** | Defined differently depending on the nature of the offence:<br>• For *Criminal Code* offences: Attorney General of Saskatchewan or informant/ticket issuer.<br>• For *Saskatchewan Acts* or *federal Acts*: AG of Canada or informant/ticket issuer.<br>• For *bylaws*: The authority or its authorized representative. |

### **3. Filing a Notice of Appeal**

#### **Who Must File**
- A **defendant** appealing a conviction or order.
- A **prosecutor** appealing a decision or sentence.

#### **Timing**
- Must file within **30 days** after:
  - The **pronouncement of the order under appeal**, or
  - The **imposition of sentence**, whichever is **later**.

#### **Required Information on Notice of Appeal**
- Full address for service (including **telephone number**, **fax number**, and **email address** if applicable).
- Any change to this information must be **notified in writing** to the local registrar.

#### **Service and Filing**
- **Filing with the local registrar constitutes service** on the prosecutor.
- The local registrar must **forward a copy** of the notice to the prosecutor.

---

### **4. Service of the Notice of Appeal**

#### **Prosecutor’s Obligations**
- Must serve the **defendant personally** within **10 days** of filing.
- Service must be by **personal delivery** unless otherwise directed.

#### **Substituted Service**
- If personal service is not possible, the prosecutor may apply **ex parte** to the appeal court for an order authorizing substituted service (e.g., mail, email, fax).
- If the court approves substituted service, the defendant is **deemed served**.
- The prosecutor must **file proof of service** with the local registrar.

#### **Court Authority**
- The appeal court may:
  - **Set aside or vary** an order for substituted service.
  - Make any order regarding service of the notice or other documents.

---

### **5. Transmission of Trial Materials**

Within **14 days** of serving the notice, the appellant must provide the local registrar with **proof** that transcripts of trial proceedings have been ordered.

Once the transcript and reasons for judgment are received:
- The appellant must file and serve on the respondent:
  - **Original and one copy of the transcript of evidence**
  - **Reasons for judgment** (if appeal is against an order other than sentence)

#### **If the appeal is against sentence:**
- File and serve:
  - Transcript of sentencing proceedings (including prosecution and defence submissions)
  - Reasons for sentence

#### **If appeal is against both sentence and other orders:**
- File and serve all required materials within 10 days of receiving the transcript.

> ⚠️ *Exception:* If the appellant applies for a **trial de novo** under section 822(4) of the Criminal Code, the rules above do not apply.

---

### **6. Application for Trial De Novo**

- Must be filed within **30 days** of serving the notice of appeal.
- At least **seven days before hearing**, the appellant must serve a copy of the notice and supporting materials on the respondent.
- No date, time, or place for the hearing is required in the notice of appeal.

---

### **7. Hearing Schedule**

- The local registrar sets the **date, time, and place** of the appeal hearing **after receiving the transcript** and unless otherwise ordered by the court.
- Upon setting the hearing, the registrar must **notify both parties** in writing.

---

### **8. Memoranda of Argument**

#### **Appellant’s Memorandum (Form 3)**
Must be filed at least **30 days before the hearing** and served on the respondent. Includes:
- **Part 1: Introduction** – Summary of appeal context
- **Part 2: Jurisdiction & Standard of Review** – Positions on:
  - Source of right to appeal
  - Court’s jurisdiction
  - Standard of appellate review
- **Part 3: Summary of Facts** – Concise factual statement
- **Part 4: Points in Issue** – Key legal or factual issues
- **Part 5: Argument** – Detailed legal reasoning with references to:
  - Page and line of transcript
  - Authorities cited
  - Statutes, regulations, bylaws (copied as appendix)
- **Part 6: Relief** – Desired order from the court
- **Part 7: Authorities** – Alphabetized table of authorities (in compliance with the *Citation Guide for the Courts of Saskatchewan*)

#### **Respondent’s Memorandum (Form 4)**
Must be filed at least **15 days before the hearing** and served on the appellant. Includes:
- **Part 1: Introduction** – Summary of context
- **Part 2: Jurisdiction & Standard of Review** – Position on same matters as appellant
- **Part 3: Summary of Facts** – Accepts, modifies, or supplements facts
- **Part 4: Points in Issue** – Responds to appellant’s points and may raise new ones
- **Part 5: Argument** – Concise legal reasoning with references and authorities
- **Part 6: Relief** – Desired order
- **Part 7: Authorities** – Alphabetized table (same citation standards)

> ⚖️ Both parties must cite authorities according to the **Citation Guide for the Courts of Saskatchewan**.

---

### **9. Applications to Dismiss Appeal**

#### **Grounds for Dismissal**
The respondent may apply to dismiss the appeal if:
- The appellant has **failed to pursue the appeal diligently**, or
- The appellant has **failed to comply with these Rules**

#### **Process**
- The respondent must serve the appellant with **14 days’ notice** of the hearing.
- If the local registrar determines the appellant has failed to comply or acted negligently, they may **refer the matter to the appeal court**.
- The registrar must serve both parties with **14 days’ notice** of the hearing of the reference.

#### **Court Discretion**
- The appeal court may **dismiss the appeal** or make any other order it considers just.

---

### **10. Failure to Comply with Rules**

If an appellant:
- Fails to file a **memorandum of argument** as required, or
- Fails to **appear at the hearing** on the date and time set,

The appeal court may **dismiss the appeal** or make any other order it deems just.

---

### **11. Abandonment of Appeal**

An appeal may be abandoned by:
- Signing and filing a **Notice of Abandonment in Form 5**, or
- Informing the court **in person or by counsel** that the appeal is abandoned.

> ⚠️ If signed by the appellant, an **Affidavit of Execution** must be attached, stating:
> - The appellant was personally present and signed the document
> - The witness knows the appellant and is over 18 years of age

---

### **12. Extensions of Time**

Any judge may, on **application**, extend or shorten time limits for:
- Filing notices
- Submitting documents
- Appearing in court

> This power applies even if the application is made **after the deadline** has passed.

---

### **13. Applications for Release, Stay, or Relief**

An appellant may apply for:
- Release from custody
- Stay of probation
- Stay of driving prohibition
- Other stay orders

#### Required Documents:
- **Notice of Application (Form 6)**
- **Affidavit** verifying facts
- Any other supporting material

#### Service:
- Must serve the **prosecutor** upon filing.

#### Hearing Process:
- Local registrar must:
  - Set a hearing date within **three days** of receiving the application
  - Notify both parties of the date, time, and place
