## üîç **Comprehensive Summary of the Regulation: Repeal and Replacement of General Radio Regulations (SOR/2021-40, s. 2)**

### üìå **Overview and Purpose**
This regulation, issued under the authority of the **Radiocommunication Act** and the **Financial Administration Act**, formally **repeals four outdated regulations**:
1. *General Radio Regulations, Part I, C.R.C., c. 1371*
2. *General Radio Regulations, Part II, C.R.C., c. 1372*
3. *Interference-causing Equipment Regulations* (P.C. 1993-408)
4. *Radio Operators‚Äô Certificate Regulations* (March 9, 1978)

It replaces them with a **new, consolidated set of radiocommunication rules** governing:
- Radio authorizations and exemptions
- Operation of radio apparatus
- Interference-causing and radio-sensitive equipment
- Licensing conditions and technical standards

These new rules are effective upon publication in the *Canada Gazette, Part II*, and represent a significant modernization of Canada‚Äôs radio spectrum governance framework.

### 2. **Licensing Terms and Restrictions**

#### ‚úÖ **Core Terms of a Radio Licence**
A radio licence grants the holder the right to:
- Install, operate, or possess radio apparatus to perform **any of the following services**:
  - Aeronautical
  - Amateur radio
  - Public information
  - Developmental
  - Fixed
  - Intersatellite
  - Land mobile
  - Maritime
  - Radiodetermination
  - Fixed point-to-point

- Operate at **fixed stations, mobile stations, or space stations**, as authorized.

#### üîí **Restrictions on Activities**
- Licence activities must be **limited strictly to those services specified in the licence**.
- **Radiocommunication service providers** must provide services **without unjust discrimination**.

#### üö´ **Service-Specific Usage Restrictions**
- **Aeronautical Service**: Only for:
  - Aircraft safety and navigation
  - General aircraft operations
  - Public message exchange

- **Maritime Service**: Only for:
  - Ship safety and navigation
  - General vessel operations
  - Public message exchange

- **Developmental Service**: Only for:
  - Experiments, research, tests, or demonstrations related to the service

### 3. **Eligibility to Hold a Radio Licence**

Individuals, corporations, or government entities may apply for a licence as a **radiocommunication user or service provider**, **except in the amateur radio service**, which has separate rules.

#### Eligible Applicants:
- **Individuals**:
  - Canadian citizens
  - Permanent residents
  - Non-residents with employment authorization under the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act*

- **Corporations**:
  - Incorporated under Canadian or provincial law

- **Partnerships, Joint Ventures, or Trusts**:
  - Only if all partners, co-venturers, or trustees are eligible under the above

- **Government Entities**:
  - Federal, provincial, or local governments or their agencies

- **Foreign Governments**:
  - Countries that are signatories to the *Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations* (1961)

- **Ownership-Based Eligibility**:
  - Owner of a Canadian-registered aircraft ‚Üí may operate a station on board
  - Owner of a Canadian-registered ship/vessel ‚Üí may operate a station on board

- **Special Cases**:
  - Residents of non-Canadian countries who wish to:
    - Establish a station for interconnection with a public switched network
    - Require a licence for a limited-duration special event

> ‚ùå *Note: The amateur radio service is excluded from this general eligibility list.*

### 4. **Licensing and Transfer Prohibitions**

- **Licences cannot be transferred or assigned** without **Ministerial authorization**.
- A **radiocommunication service provider** must provide **each subscriber or lessee** with a copy of the applicable terms and conditions of the licence.

### 5. **Exemptions from Radio Licensing (Subsection 4(1) of the Radiocommunication Act)**

Certain equipment or operations are **exempt from requiring a radio licence** under specific conditions.

#### ‚úÖ **Exemptions by Equipment Type**

| Exemption | Conditions |

| **Broadcasting Certificate-exempt Equipment** | Equipment listed in the *Broadcasting Certificate-exempt Radio Apparatus List (October 2010)* that meets technical requirements |

#### ‚úÖ **Exemptions for Mobile Equipment on Aircraft or Vessels**

| Scenario | Conditions |
|--------|-----------|
| **Aircraft (Aeronautical or Radiodetermination Service)** | - Operation within Canada<br>- Outside Canada in another country<br>- In a country with reciprocal agreement with Canada<br>- Must comply with technical requirements for mobile stations in aeronautical service (Section 34.1) |
| **Vessels (Maritime or Radiodetermination Service)** | - Operation within Canada<br>- Outside Canada in another country<br>- In a country with reciprocal agreement with Canada<br>- Must comply with technical requirements for mobile stations in maritime service (Section 34.2) |
| **Amateur Radio Service (Mobile or Fixed)** | - Operator must hold one or more valid amateur radio certificates (Section 42)<br>- Must comply with technical requirements (Section 45) |
