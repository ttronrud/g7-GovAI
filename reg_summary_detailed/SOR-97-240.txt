### **1. Legal Authority and Enactment**
- These regulations were issued by the **Treasury Board** under **section 10a of the Financial Administration Act (SC 1996, c. 18, s. 4)**.
- The regulations came into force on **April 23, 1997**.
- They establish rules governing the issuance, use, replacement, and oversight of **paper cheques** (excluding electronic forms) issued by the **Receiver General of Canada** and **departmental Ministers**.

### **3. Requirements for Issuing a Cheque (General Rules)**

Every cheque issued must:
- Bear the **signature** of either:
  - The **Receiver General** or a **signing officer**, or
  - The **Deputy Receiver General** or a **signing officer**.
- Include the **name of the payee**.
- Be for a **specific, stated amount**.
- **Not have any alterations** in the amount or payee name.

### **4. Dating Requirements for Cheques**

Subject to subsection (3), every cheque must bear:
- The **day it is issued**, OR
- The **day the payment is due**, OR
- The **day the payment is to be made**.

#### Exception â€“ Authorization to Date by Month and Year Only
The Receiver General may authorize dating a cheque **only by month and year** (without specifying the day) if:
1. The cheque is **not released before the month in which the payment is due**;
2. It represents a payment under an **Act of Parliament** that **does not specify a due day**;
3. It is a **recurring payment in a fixed amount** authorized by an Act of Parliament; and
4. The amount is due **no more than once per month**.

### **5. Replacement Cheques (When Original is Lost, Stolen, or Not Received)**

#### General Rule:
If a payee or holder for value reports:
- The cheque was **not received**, or
- It was **lost, destroyed, or stolen**,

Then the **Receiver General or a signing officer** may issue a **replacement cheque** for the **same amount**, provided the payee/holder:
- Submits a **form of undertaking** (as defined below).

#### Requirements for the Form of Undertaking:
- Must include:
  - Name and address of the payee/holder;
  - Amount, date of issue, and purpose of the original cheque;
  - An undertaking that:
    - The payee/holder will **return the original cheque** if found;
    - Will **indemnify and save harmless Her Majesty in right of Canada** from any loss or expense related to the replacement.
- Must be **signed by the payee/holder before a witness**, and **signed by the witness**.
- Must be **forwarded to the Receiver General**.

#### Special Requirement for Amounts Over $5,000:
If the original cheque is for **more than $5,000**, the payee/holder must also provide:
- An **affidavit or statutory declaration** sworn or declared before a **commissioner for oaths, justice of the peace, or other legally authorized person** in Canada or abroad, confirming the non-receipt, loss, destruction, or theft.

> âš–ï¸ This is a **safeguard against fraud** for large-value payments.

#### Exception: No Undertaking Required
A replacement cheque may be issued **without requiring an undertaking** if:
- The payee requests it in writing, and
- The cheque represents:
  - A **recurring payment in a fixed amount authorized by an Act of Parliament**, or
  - A payment under a **program where the payee is already required to provide an undertaking** as part of the program terms.

#### Emergency Exception (No Undertaking Required)
If the **Receiver General is notified** by:
- An employee or public officer,
- Canada Post,
- A carrier,
- RCMP,
- Or a provincial/municipal police force,

That the cheque **cannot be delivered** due to:
- Act of God,
- Fire,
- Theft,
- Loss or accidental destruction during delivery,
- Inaccessibility (likely to cause delay beyond due date),

Then a replacement cheque may be issued **without any undertaking**.

> ðŸš¨ This ensures timely payments are not delayed due to unforeseen events.

### **6. Departmental Bank Accounts**

#### When Can a Departmental Bank Account Be Established?
An **appropriate Minister** may apply to the **Receiver General** for the establishment of a departmental bank account when:
- Expenditures need to be made in locations where **normal Receiver General cheque facilities are not immediately available**.

#### Types of Authorized Expenditures from a Departmental Bank Account:
1. Emergency salary advances  
2. Accountable travel advances (where travel credit cards or travellerâ€™s cheques are not viable alternatives)  
3. Reimbursement of travel and removal expenses  
4. Payment of supplies/services acquired under **local purchase authority** where immediate payment is required  
5. Reimbursement of petty cash advances  
6. Brokerage fees and customs duties payable to an independent customs broker  
7. Postage  
8. Payment of salaries/wages to **part-time and term employees** engaged locally under the *Locally-Engaged Staff Employment Regulations, 1995*, who are not paid via:
   - Canadian Forces,
   - RCMP,
   - Public service pay systems (under section 12 of the Department of Public Works and Government Services Act)

> â— All these expenditures must be **authorized and properly documented**.

#### Signature Requirements:
- **Two signatures** are required on any cheque drawn from the account.
- **Exception**: The **appropriate Minister** may request the **Treasury Board** to authorize a **single signature**.

#### Expenditure Limitations:
- **No expenditure exceeding $5,000** may be made from a departmental bank account **without prior Treasury Board approval**.

#### Exceptions to the $5,000 Limit:
Expenditures over $5,000 may be made **without Treasury Board approval** if:
1. Paying **locally engaged employees** (if the department has authority to make such payments), or  
2. Issuing an **emergency salary advance** under paragraph 4(b) of the *Accountable Advances Regulations*.

#### Requesting Additional Authorization
A Minister may apply to the **Treasury Board** for authorization to make **other types of expenditures** beyond those listed in section 7(2).

> Upon approval:
- The Treasury Board must issue a **written authorization** that specifies:
  - The **classes of expenditures** allowed;
  - The **number of signatures required** on cheques for those expenditures.

> âœ… This allows flexibility while maintaining accountability.

### **7. Oversight and Control by the Receiver General**

Despite the establishment of a departmental bank account or any authorization, the **Receiver General retains the power to**:
- Direct the financial institution to **refuse payment** of any cheque drawn on the account;
- **Withdraw authority** from any person to draw cheques from the account;
- **Discontinue** the account and **transfer the balance** to the Receiver General.

> ðŸ” This ensures **centralized control and financial accountability**.

### **8. Requirements for Cheques Drawn on Departmental Bank Accounts**

Every cheque drawn on a departmental bank account must:
- Bear the signature of **one or more signing officers** (depending on authorization);
- Include the **name of the payee**;
- Be for a **specific amount**;
- **Not be altered** in amount or payee name;
- Bear the **date of issue** or the **due date** of the payment.
