**Detailed Summary of the Deposit Insurance Application Fee By-law (Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation)**

**Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis:**  
The Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC) Board of Directors enacted this by-law under two specific provisions of the *Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation Act*:

- **Paragraph 11(2)(g)a**: Grants the Board the authority to establish and amend by-laws related to the administration of deposit insurance, including fees associated with applications.
- **Subsection 18(1)b**: Empowers the Board to prescribe fees that must be paid in connection with applications for deposit insurance.

This by-law serves to formally establish a mandatory, non-refundable fee for certain deposit insurance applications.

**Key Provision:**  
The by-law prescribes a **fixed application fee of $20,000** that must accompany any application for deposit insurance submitted by a **provincial institution** under **subsection 17(1.1)** of the *Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation Act*.

**Scope of Application:**  
- Applies specifically to **provincial financial institutions** (e.g., provincial credit unions or other institutions chartered or regulated under provincial law) that are seeking deposit insurance coverage.
- The fee is required when such institutions apply under the specific provision in the Act (subsection 17(1.1)), which governs the eligibility and process for provincial institutions to obtain deposit insurance.

**Nature of the Fee:**  
- **Non-refundable**: The $20,000 fee is not refundable under any circumstances, regardless of the outcome of the application or whether the institution is approved for insurance coverage.
- This reflects a policy decision to ensure that the administrative and operational costs of processing applications are covered and to provide a stable revenue stream for CDIC to manage the application review process.

**Effective Date:**  
The by-law comes into force on the **day it is registered** with the appropriate statutory authority (typically the Canada Gazette or the relevant government registry). This means its provisions are immediately applicable upon formal publication and registration.

**Legal References:**  
- **R.S. c. 18 (3rd Supp.), s. 51**: Refers to the statutory framework that allows the Board to adopt by-laws for administrative purposes.
- **S.C. 1999, c. 28, s. 100**: This is the enabling legislative provision that authorizes the Board to make by-laws, including those concerning fees.

**Purpose and Implications:**  
- The fee ensures financial accountability and helps fund the administrative costs of evaluating applications and assessing risk.
- It provides a clear, predictable cost for provincial institutions seeking deposit insurance, reducing uncertainty in the application process.
- The non-refundable nature underscores the finality of the fee, aligning with standard administrative fee practices in financial regulation.

**Conclusion:**  
This by-law establishes a standardized, non-refundable fee of **$20,000** for provincial institutions applying for deposit insurance under subsection 17(1.1) of the CDIC Act.
