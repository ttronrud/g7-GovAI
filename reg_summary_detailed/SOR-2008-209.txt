**Detailed Summary of the *Exploitation of the Donkin Coal Block (Natural Resources) Regulations* (S.C. 2007, c. 33)**

---

### **1. Purpose and Authority**
The *Exploitation of the Donkin Coal Block (Natural Resources) Regulations* were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of Natural Resources**. This action is grounded in **subsections 13(2) and (3)** of the *Donkin Coal Block Development Opportunity Act* (the "Act").

These regulations serve to **establish specific rules and conditions governing the exploitation of the Donkin coal block** in Nova Scotia. The Donkin coal block is a designated area of coal reserves, and these regulations provide the legal framework for how that resource may be developed, managed, and regulated.

---

### **2. Scope and Applicability**
The Regulations apply specifically to the **exploitation of the Donkin coal block**, and are designed to ensure that development occurs in a manner consistent with environmental protection, public safety, and the broader regulatory framework of Nova Scotia.

#### **Key Definitions and Interpretation**
- **"Nova Scotia laws"** refers to all Acts and regulations of Nova Scotia as amended from time to time, as listed in **Schedule 1**.
- For clarity and consistency, these laws **include any subsequent regulations made under those Acts after the coming into force of these Regulations**, unless explicitly excluded.

#### **Interpretation Principles**
- The *Nova Scotia Interpretation Act* (R.S.N.S. 1989, c. 235), as amended, governs the interpretation of the Nova Scotia laws.
- A specific **adaptation to interpretation rules** is provided in **Schedule 2**, which modifies how certain provisions of the Interpretation Act apply in this context. This ensures that the regulations are interpreted in a way that is appropriate to the unique circumstances of coal block development.

---

### **3. Incorporation of Relevant Laws**
The Regulations **incorporate by reference** key Nova Scotia statutes and regulations governing natural resources, **with specific exclusions** to avoid conflicts or inapplicability.

#### **Included Laws (with exceptions):**
- **Mineral Resources Act (S.N.S. 1990, c. 18)** and its regulations — **except**:
  - **Sections 81 and 112** of the Mineral Resources Act are **excluded**.
  - This exclusion likely reflects the need to allow for special provisions tailored to coal development, such as permitting processes, environmental assessments, or royalty arrangements not covered under general mineral resource rules.

- **Petroleum Resources Act (R.S.N.S. 1989, c. 342)** and its regulations — **except**:
  - **Sections 7 and 10** of the Petroleum Resources Act
  - **Subsection 14(2)** of the *Petroleum Resources Regulations (N.S. Reg. 178/85)*
  - **Subsection 20(2)** of the same regulations
  - These exclusions are critical because:
    - The Donkin coal block is a **coal resource**, not a petroleum resource.
    - Petroleum-specific provisions (e.g., licensing, drilling standards, or environmental controls) are not relevant or applicable to coal mining.
    - Excluding these sections prevents overreach or misapplication of petroleum regulations to a non-petroleum resource.

> **Note**: The exclusion of petroleum-related sections ensures that the regulations remain focused and legally appropriate for coal exploitation.

---

### **4. Key Provision Clarification**
- In **Section 22** of the Regulations, the reference to **“Sections 20 and 21”** is **reinterpreted** as referring only to **Section 21**.
  - This is a typographical or interpretive correction to ensure clarity and prevent unintended application of Section 20, which may not be relevant or applicable to the context of coal exploitation.

---

### **5. Effective Date**
The Regulations **come into force on the day they are registered** with the Office of the Registrar of Regulations. This means they are immediately enforceable upon official publication and registration, allowing for prompt implementation of regulatory requirements for coal development activities.

---

### **6. Significance and Implications**
- These regulations provide a **legally binding framework** for coal development in the Donkin area.
- They ensure that coal exploitation is conducted under a clear, consistent, and transparent set of rules.
- By selectively incorporating general natural resource laws while excluding irrelevant or conflicting provisions, the regulations maintain **legal precision and regulatory relevance**.
- The exclusion of petroleum-related provisions underscores the **distinct nature of coal development**, which involves different geological, environmental, and operational considerations.

---

### **Conclusion**
The *Exploitation of the Donkin Coal Block (Natural Resources) Regulations* (S.C. 2007, c. 33) are a targeted, legally sound set of rules designed to govern the development of the Donkin coal resource. They:
- Are grounded in statutory authority,
- Incorporate relevant Nova Scotia laws with careful exclusions to maintain relevance,
- Provide clear interpretive guidance,
- And ensure that development proceeds within a safe, sustainable, and legally compliant framework.

This regulatory instrument is a key component in the broader strategy of managing Nova Scotia’s natural resources in a way that balances economic development with environmental stewardship and regulatory integrity.

--- 

**Note**: For full legal effect, these regulations should be read in conjunction with the *Donkin Coal Block Development Opportunity Act* and the relevant schedules and amendments.
