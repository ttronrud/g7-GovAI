### üîç **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**

The **Vessel Pollution and Dangerous Chemicals Regulations** (S.C. 2005, c. 29, s. 16(1)) are a set of Canadian regulations designed to **prevent pollution from ships** and to **ensure safe handling of dangerous chemicals** at sea and in Canadian waters.

These regulations:

- **Supplement and complement** existing international standards, particularly:
  - The **International Convention for the Prevention of Pollution from Ships (MARPOL), 1973**, and its **1978 Protocol and 1997 Protocol**.
  - The **International Convention on the Control of Harmful Anti-Fouling Systems on Ships, 2001 (Anti-Fouling Systems Convention)**.
- Are enacted under specific powers in the *Canada Shipping Act, 2001*, including:
  - **Subsection 7(2)** ‚Äì Authority to make regulations for the safety and pollution prevention of vessels.
  - **Subsection 35(1)a** ‚Äì Authority to regulate pollution prevention.
  - **Subsection 120(1) and (2)** ‚Äì Authority to regulate pollution control and hazardous substances.
  - **Paragraph 182(a)** ‚Äì Authority to regulate pollution from vessels.
  - **Section 190** ‚Äì Authority to regulate pollution from vessels carrying dangerous chemicals.
  - **Paragraphs 207(2)(a) and 244(a)** ‚Äì Authority to regulate the handling and discharge of hazardous substances.

> ‚úÖ **Key Objective**: To ensure that Canadian vessels and operations comply with both international standards and national environmental protection goals, particularly in preventing marine pollution from oil, chemicals, waste, and emissions.

### üìö **2. Scope and Applicability**

The regulations apply to **all vessels operating in Canadian waters**, including:

- **Canadian vessels** (built or registered in Canada)
- **Canadian pleasure crafts** (pleasure boats licensed under Part 10 of the Act or operated primarily in Canada)
- **Foreign vessels and foreign pleasure crafts** (those not registered in Canada)

#### Key Categories Covered:
| Vessel Type | Definition |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Cargo Vessels** | Any vessel not a passenger vessel or pleasure craft (e.g., tankers, bulk carriers) |
| **Passenger Vessels** | Vessels carrying more than 12 passengers |
| **Chemical Tankers** | Vessels designed for carrying dangerous chemicals in bulk |
| **Oil Tankers** | Vessels primarily designed to carry crude oil or oil in bulk |
| **Gas Carriers** | Vessels designed to carry liquefied gases or other gases listed in the IMO LPG Code |
| **Combination Carriers** | Vessels that carry both oil and solid cargoes in bulk |
| **Noxious Liquid Substances (NLS) Tankers** | Vessels carrying substances categorized as X, Y, or Z under IBC Code |
| **Offshore Support Vessels** | Vessels operating in offshore environments (e.g., drilling rigs, supply vessels), subject to Resolution A.673(16) |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: The regulations apply to **all stages of a vessel‚Äôs life cycle**, including construction, conversion, operation, and decommissioning.
