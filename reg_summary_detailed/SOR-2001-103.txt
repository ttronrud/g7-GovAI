## ðŸ“Š APR Calculation Rules (Section 600 of the Act)

### For **loans not involving credit cards or lines of credit**:
The APR must be calculated using the following formula:

> **APR = (Total Cost of Borrowing / Principal) Ã— (100 / Term in Years)**

Where:
- **Total Cost of Borrowing** = Sum of all borrowing costs (interest, fees, etc.) over the loan term
- **Principal** = Average outstanding balance at the end of each period (before subtracting payments)
- **Term** = Duration of the loan in years (to at least two decimal places)

### Key Rules for APR Calculation:
| Rule | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Rounding** | APR may be rounded to the **nearest eighth of a percent** (e.g., 5.125%) |
| **Payment Order** | Each payment is applied **first to accumulated cost of borrowing**, then to principal |
| **Time Periods** | - 1 month = 1/12 year<br>- 1 week = 1/52 year<br>- 1 day = 1/365 year |
| **Variable Rates** | Must use the **annual rate in effect on the day of calculation** |
| **No Installments** | If no payments exist, APR is calculated assuming **lump-sum repayment at maturity** |
| **New Credit Agreements** | Even if the loan carries an outstanding balance from a prior agreement, it counts as a **new credit agreement** for APR purposes |

### For **credit cards or lines of credit**:
- **Fixed interest rate**: APR = the fixed annual interest rate
- **Variable interest rate**: APR = the annual rate **on the date of disclosure**

> If there is **no cost of borrowing beyond interest**, the APR equals the interest rate.

## âŒ What Is **Excluded** from Cost of Borrowing?

The following charges are **not included** in the cost of borrowing:

- Insurance that is **optional** or where the **borrower is the beneficiary** and the insured asset reflects the value of collateral
- **Overdraft fees**
- Fees to **register documents** or obtain information from public registries about security interests
- **Penalty charges** for early repayment
- Legal fees (unless required by the foreign company)
- Insurance against **defects in title** (if paid directly by the borrower)
- Appraisal/inspection services provided **directly to the borrower**
- Insurance against **default on a high-ratio mortgage**
- Maintenance fees for tax accounts (required or optional)
- Fees to **discharge a security interest**
- **Default charges**

> These exclusions prevent overstatement of costs and protect borrowers from misleading disclosures.

## ðŸ“„ Disclosure Requirements

All foreign companies must provide a **written disclosure statement** to the borrower before or at the time of entering into a credit agreement.

### Form of Disclosure Statement:
- Can be:
  - A **separate document**
  - A **part of the credit agreement or application**

### Formatting and Presentation Rules:
| Requirement | Details |
|-----------|--------|
| **Consolidated Presentation** | For agreements or applications, the disclosure must be in a **single, clear location** |
| **Information Box** | Must appear at the **beginning** of the agreement or application, containing key data from one of **Schedules 1 to 5** |
| **Text Legibility** | - Font size: **minimum 12 points**<br>- Bold for titles, numbers, interest rates, dates, amounts<br>- 10-point font for other text<br>- Standard spacing<br>- Margins above/below/sides<br>- Dark text on light background (high contrast) |

> The goal is **clarity, simplicity, and readability**.

### Assumptions and Estimates:
- If the information is based on an **assumption or estimate**:
  - It must be **reasonable**
  - Must be **clearly labeled** as an estimate
  - Must be **known to be unverifiable at the time of disclosure**

> This protects borrowers from being misled by speculative or unverifiable data.

### Language and Clarity:
- All disclosures must be:
  - **Clear**
  - **Simple**
  - **Not misleading**

> The language must be accessible to the average consumer.

## ðŸ“¬ Delivery and Timing of Disclosure

### By Mail:
- A disclosure statement sent by mail is considered **provided on the fifth business day after the postmark date**

### For Multiple Borrowers:
If a foreign company enters into a credit agreement with **two or more borrowers**:
- Must provide the disclosure to **all borrowers**
- **Exception**: If **all borrowers consent** (in writing or orally) to having it provided to **one borrower on their behalf**, the company may provide it to that one.
- **If only some consent**, the company may provide it to one borrower **only if** it also provides it to **all others who did not consent**
- **Oral consent** must be **confirmed in writing** (paper or electronic)

> This promotes efficiency without compromising transparency.

## â±ï¸ Timing of Initial Disclosure Statement

The initial disclosure must be provided **on or before**:

- The **earlier of**:
  - The **first payment** (other than a disbursement charge) made by the borrower
  - **Two clear business days before** the agreement is signed (for mortgages or hypothecs)
  - **On the day the agreement is signed** (for all other types)

> **Exceptions** (when disclosure is not required before signing):
- Borrower **consents** to receiving the statement later
- Borrower receives **independent legal advice**
- The agreement includes a **rescission period of at least two business days**
- The agreement offers **favourable terms** that reduce the cost of borrowing

> These exceptions allow flexibility in cases where borrowers are fully informed or given time to reconsider.
