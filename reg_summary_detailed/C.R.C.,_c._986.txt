## 3. **Modified Work Schedules (Sections 170 & 172)**

  - Days off

## 5. **Exemptions from Overtime (Section 174)**

Section 174 does **not apply** in these situations:

- Employees work beyond standard hours to **change shifts** (e.g., rotating shifts)
- Employees work beyond standard hours to **exercise seniority rights** under a collective agreement
- Employees work beyond standard hours due to **shift exchange** with another employee

> This recognizes operational flexibility in certain industries.

## 6. **Work Schedules and Rest Days**

- If employees work **in excess of maximum hours** (as per section 171), the schedule must include **no fewer days of rest** than the number of weeks in the schedule.
- Under **section 176 (permits)**, the Head of Compliance and Enforcement may issue a permit allowing:
  - Flexibility in rest days
  - Alternative days of rest
  - Deviation from section 173 (rest day requirements)
- During averaging periods, work schedules **do not need to follow section 173** (rest day rules)

## 7. **Employment of Minors (Under 18 Years)**

An employer may hire someone under 18 **only if**:

## 8. **Apprenticeship Exemption (Section 178)**

- Employers are **exempt** from section 178 (overtime rules) for **apprentices** who:
  - Are registered under a provincial apprenticeship act
  - Are paid according to a schedule established under that act

> This supports training and development programs.

## 9. **Call-In Work (Section 253.3)**

- If an employee reports for work **at the call of the employer**, they are entitled to **at least 3 hours of wages at their regular rate**, even if no actual work is performed.

> This protects workers from being exploited for "showing up."
