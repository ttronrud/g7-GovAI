**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Service of Documents Required or Authorized to Be Served Under Sections 53 to 57 of the Conflict of Interest Act**

---

### **Regulatory Authority and Context**
This regulation was issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Prime Minister**, under **Section 54 of the Conflict of Interest Act**. It provides specific procedural guidelines for how documents required or authorized under **Sections 53 to 57** of the Conflict of Interest Act may be served upon a public office holder.

The regulation is part of the **Conflict of Interest Act (CIA)** framework, which aims to ensure transparency, accountability, and the avoidance of conflicts of interest among public officials. This particular regulation addresses the **method, timing, and proof of service** of documents related to such disclosures or obligations.

---

### **Key Provisions**

#### **1. Methods of Serving Documents**
A document may be served on a public office holder in one of the following ways:

- **Personal Delivery**  
  A copy of the document may be delivered directly to the public office holder at:
  - Any place where they are located, or  
  - If the office holder is not conveniently accessible, to a person who appears to be at least 18 years of age at the office holder’s **last known address or usual place of residence**.

- **Registered Mail or Courier Service**  
  The document may be sent via:
  - Registered mail, or  
  - Courier service  
  to the office holder’s **last known address, usual place of residence, or place of work**.

- **Electronic Transmission**  
  The document may be transmitted electronically (e.g., via **fax or email**) to the office holder’s **last known address, usual place of residence, or place of work**.  
  ⚠️ **Important Requirement**: If served electronically, a **copy must also be sent by registered mail** to ensure physical record and verification.

---

#### **2. Date of Service**
The date on which service is deemed complete depends on the method used:

- **Personal Delivery**: The day on which the document is physically delivered.
- **Registered Mail or Courier**: The **10th day after** the date indicated on the receipt issued by the postal or courier service.
- **Electronic Transmission**: The day on which the document is **sent electronically**.

> This timing ensures a clear, verifiable, and consistent timeline for service, particularly in legal or compliance contexts.

---

#### **3. Proof of Service**
Acceptable evidence that a document has been properly served includes:

- An **acknowledgment of service** signed by or on behalf of the person served; or  
- An **affidavit of service** that includes:
  - The name of the person who served the document,  
  - The date, place, and manner of service.

> These forms of proof are designed to provide a legally valid and verifiable record of service, which may be necessary for compliance audits or judicial review.

---

#### **4. Effective Date**
The regulation comes into force on:
- The day that **Section 54 of the Conflict of Interest Act** (as amended by Section 2 of Chapter 9 of the Statutes of Canada, 2006) comes into force, **or**  
- If later, on the day the regulation is officially **registered** in the Canada Gazette.

> This ensures alignment with the legislative framework and provides a clear start date for implementation.

> **Note**: These regulations were in force as of **July 9, 2007**, as referenced in **SI/2007-75** (Statutory Instrument 2007-75).

---

### **Purpose and Significance**

This regulation establishes a **clear, accessible, and flexible framework** for serving documents related to conflict of interest obligations. It ensures that public office holders receive required disclosures or notices in a timely and verifiable manner, while also accommodating modern communication methods such as email and fax.

Key benefits include:
- **Accessibility**: Allows service to be conducted through multiple channels, including personal delivery, mail, and electronic means.
- **Clarity**: Defines the exact date of service, which is critical for compliance timelines and legal accountability.
- **Verification**: Requires either an acknowledgment or affidavit, ensuring that service is not merely assumed but documented.
- **Compliance with Modern Practices**: Includes electronic transmission, reflecting current administrative realities and improving efficiency.

---

### **Application and Scope**

- Applies to **all public office holders** (e.g., ministers, department heads, elected officials, civil servants) who are subject to the requirements of **Sections 53 to 57** of the Conflict of Interest Act.
- Covers documents such as:
  - Conflict of interest disclosures,
  - Notices of potential conflicts,
  - Requests for declarations,
  - Compliance notices.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation provides a **practical, enforceable, and legally sound** procedure for serving essential conflict of interest-related documents. By outlining specific methods, defining service dates, and requiring verifiable proof, it strengthens transparency and accountability in public office. It balances traditional methods (like personal delivery and registered mail) with modern tools (like email and fax), ensuring that public officials are effectively and fairly notified of their obligations under the Conflict of Interest Act.

It is a key component of Canada’s broader efforts to promote ethical governance and public trust in public office.
