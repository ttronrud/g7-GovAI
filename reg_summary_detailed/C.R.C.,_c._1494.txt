**Detailed Summary of the Tackle Regulations (Canada)**

---

### **Overview**

The **Tackle Regulations**, formally titled *"The Tackle Regulations"* and cited as such, are a set of safety regulations under the **Canada Shipping Act** (Act), designed to ensure the safety of workers and the integrity of cargo-handling operations during loading, unloading, and handling of cargo, bunker coal, ship‚Äôs stores, and ship/cargo fittings. These regulations apply specifically to **ship-to-shore operations** and related activities, emphasizing the safety of personnel, equipment, and structural components involved in the processes.

The regulations are administered by the **Board of Steamship Inspection (Board)**, which is established under Part VIII of the Canada Shipping Act. The Board has the authority to approve gear, prescribe safety standards, and issue exemptions when appropriate.

## **Scope of Application**

These regulations apply to:

1. **Ships** engaged in loading, unloading, or handling cargo, bunker coal, ship‚Äôs stores, or ship/cargo fittings **within Canada**.
2. **Shore-based equipment** (e.g., cranes, derricks) and attached gear when used in cargo operations.
3. **Areas on shore** within the scope of any lifting equipment (e.g., crane or derrick) and the immediate approaches to such areas ‚Äî **excluding**:
   - Sheds or warehouses
   - Any part of a wharf forward or aft of the ship‚Äôs mooring lines

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exemptions**:
> - Fishing vessels are **exempt**.
> - Ships where:
>   - The number of workers involved at any time is **less than 10**, and
>   - The maximum weight hoisted at any one time does **not exceed 455 kg**  
>   are **not subject** to full compliance.

> ‚úÖ **Exemptions by Board**:  
> The Board may, at its discretion, **exempt** any ship or cargo gear owner from full compliance if it determines that doing so is "proper and reasonable."

## **Compliance Obligations**

### **1. Compliance by Ship Owner, Master, or Officer in Charge (Part III)**  
- Must comply with all safety requirements in **Part III** ‚Äî *except* when:
  - A hatch is **taken over** by workers for the processes, and
  - It is **formally reported in writing** to the owner/master/officer in charge as being **completed**, and
  - The hatch has been **fenced and covered** as required under Section 42, or
  - It has been **reclaimed by the ship‚Äôs owner** and reported accordingly.

> ‚úîÔ∏è Upon receiving such written notice, the owner/master/officer must **immediately provide a written acknowledgment** in the prescribed form.

### **2. Life Saving Equipment Requirements (Loading/Unloading from Boom, Raft, or Crib)**

When loading/unloading from a **boom, raft, or crib**:

- A **life-saving boat** must be kept:
  - Near the working place
  - Equipped with:
    - Oars
    - A boat hook
    - An **approved lifebuoy with 27 meters of heaving line**

> üîÅ **If water current ‚â• 3 knots**:
> - The life-saving boat must be:
>   - **Manned**
>   - Fitted with a **motor**

> üîÅ **For unfenced open scows or scows with fencing < 760 mm above load**:
> - Must provide:
>   - One approved lifebuoy with 27 m of line
> - If current ‚â• 3 knots:
>   - Must keep a **manned, motorized life-saving boat** with oars and boat hook

### **3. Safe Access to Ship and Wharf (Section 8)**

Safe access must be provided for workers to move between the ship and wharf, unless conditions make it unnecessary due to low risk.

#### Requirements:
- **Ship‚Äôs accommodation ladder, gangway, or similar**:
  - Minimum width: **560 mm**
  - Must be:
    - Properly secured
    - Fenced to a **clear height of 915 mm** (with upper/lower rails, taut ropes, or equivalent)
    - Exception: On a ship‚Äôs side, fencing only required on one side if the other is protected by the ship‚Äôs hull
  - Constructed from **suitable, durable, non-slip material**
  - Must prevent slipping

- **Alternative ladder** (if not a ship ladder):
  - Must be **adequately strong, properly secured**, and prevent slipping

- **If ladder rests on bulwark or deck**:
  - Must have **suitable handholds** from bulwark to deck

- **Safety net or equivalent**:
  - Required if workers might fall between ship and wharf

- **Alongside hatches**:
  - Similar safety precautions required on the side of the ship adjacent to the wharf

- **Between ships**:
  - Safe access must be provided if ships are alongside each other ‚Äî unless risk is negligible

- **Highest freeboard vessel**:
  - Responsible for providing safe access

- **In holds deeper than 1.52 m**:
  - Safe access required from deck to hold

#### Ladder Specifications (for deep holds):
- Must provide:
  - **Foothold depth**: ‚â•115 mm (including space behind)
  - **Width**: ‚â•250 mm
  - **Firm handhold**
- Should be in **continuous vertical line** from deck to bottom of hold
- If interrupted, must have a **safe landing** at the end to prevent workers from stepping off
- At hatch coamings:
  - Must have **cleats or cups** for secure handhold and foothold (same dimensions as above)

#### Alternative access:
- Sloping ladders or stairways are acceptable **if** they meet the requirements of a vertical ladder and provide adequate footholds

#### For undecked vessels:
- Must provide safe access (ladder or rope ladder)
- Rope ladders must have:
  - **Flat wooden treads**
  - Be **non-twisting**

#### General Requirements:
- **Room must be maintained** between any winch or obstruction and access point
- **Shaft tunnels** must have adequate handholds or footholds on both sides
- All areas where workers are employed or must go must be **efficiently lit** during operations
- Lighting must **not interfere with vessel navigation**
