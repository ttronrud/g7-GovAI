### **1. Title and Citation**
- These regulations are officially titled the **Supplementary Retirement Benefits Regulations** and may be cited as such.
- The primary legislation referenced is the **Supplementary Retirement Benefits Act (the "Act")**.

The regulations specify how the **Pension Index** is calculated for the purposes of subsection 2(1) of the Act.

#### **Calculation Process:**
- The Pension Index for a given year (starting with 1970) is derived from the **Consumer Price Index (CPI)**.
- Specifically:
  - The CPI for each month in the **12-month period ending on September 30 of the immediately preceding year** is collected.
  - The **aggregate (sum)** of these 12 monthly CPI values is divided by 12 to obtain an average.
- This average is then **adjusted to one digit after the decimal point** (e.g., 1.2 or 1.23 becomes 1.2).

#### **Rounding Rules for Fractions Less Than One:**
- If the resulting quotient has a fractional part less than 1:
  - It must be expressed as a **decimal with two digits after the decimal point**.
  - **If the second digit is less than 5**, simply drop it (e.g., 1.234 â†’ 1.23 â†’ 1.2).
  - **If the second digit is 5 or greater**, increase the first digit after the decimal by 1 and drop the second digit (e.g., 1.25 â†’ 1.3; 1.26 â†’ 1.3).

### **5. Recovery of Overpayments (Amounts Paid in Error)**
When an individual receives an **incorrect or overpayment** of supplementary retirement benefits under the Act:

#### **General Rule:**
- The overpayment must be **repaid** through:
  1. **Deductions from the supplementary retirement benefit**, or
  2. **Deductions from the personâ€™s pension**, if the benefit is insufficient to cover the repayment.

> âš ï¸ The repayment must occur **only after the person is formally notified in writing** of the overpayment.

### **6. Method of Repayment â€“ Monthly Installments**
Subject to certain exceptions, the overpayment is recovered via **monthly instalments** calculated as follows:

#### **Key Elements:**
- The **first deduction** occurs in the month **following** the month when a 30-day grace period after written notice expires.
- Subsequent deductions are made **monthly** thereafter, in **equal amounts**.
- The repayment period is the **lesser of two durations**:
  1. The **life expectancy** of the person to whom the overpayment was made (based on life tables), or  
  2. The period required to repay the full amount through monthly instalments equal to **2% of the gross monthly pension** payable to that person.

### **7. Alternative Recovery Options (With Approval)**
The person to whom the overpayment was made may, **at any time**, choose an alternative method of repayment **with the approval of the individual**:

#### **Option A: Larger Monthly Installments**
- Monthly payments calculated **in the same way** as under subsection (2), but **larger** than the standard instalments.

#### **Option B: Lump Sum + Remaining Balance via Monthly Payments**
- Pay the full amount in a **lump sum**, or
- Pay the remainder in **monthly instalments** (same calculation method as above), with the total repayment period **not exceeding** the maximum period defined in subsection (2)(b) (i.e., the 2% of pension rule).

### **8. Lump-Sum Payment Option**
- Any person who has been **notified in writing** of an overpayment may **choose to pay the full amount in one lump sum** at any time.
- This option is available **without needing approval** from the government or a third party, offering financial relief to those who can afford it immediately.

---

### **9. Timing of Deductions**
- The **first deduction** is made in the month **after** the 30-day notice period ends.
- Subsequent deductions are **monthly**, continuing until:
  - The overpayment is fully repaid, or
  - The person dies (whichever comes first).
- **Exception:** The **last instalment** may be **less than the previous ones** if the balance is smaller.

> ðŸ“… Example:  
> If the overpayment is $5,000 and the monthly instalment is $60, the final payment may be $10 if the remaining balance is $10.

### **Summary in Plain Language**

These regulations govern how overpayments of supplementary retirement benefits are recovered and how benefit adjustments are calculated using inflation data. Key points:

- Inflation is measured using the **Consumer Price Index**, averaged over a 12-month period ending September 30.
- The average is rounded to **one decimal place** (with specific rules for fractions).
- Benefit and ratio calculations use **more decimal places** (three or four) for precision.
- If someone is overpaid:
  - They are notified in writing.
  - The overpayment is repaid through **monthly deductions** from their pension or benefit.
  - The repayment lasts **no longer than the life of the person** or until the amount is paid via 2% of their monthly pension.
  - They can **choose to pay in full at once** or opt for **larger monthly payments** or a **lump sum + monthly balance** with approval.
  - The first deduction starts **30 days after notice expires**, and subsequent ones are monthly, with the final one possibly smaller.
