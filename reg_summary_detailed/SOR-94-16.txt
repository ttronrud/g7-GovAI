## üîç **Regulatory Summary: Determining the Country of Origin for Marking Imported Goods from Non-CUSMA Countries**

### üìå **Legal Basis and Authority**
- **Enacted by:** His Excellency the Governor General in Council  
- **On the recommendation of:** The Minister of Finance  
- **Under authority of:** Subsection 63.1(1)a of the *Customs Tariff*  
- **Effective date:** Upon the coming into force of **Section 133 of the *North American Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act*** (S.C. 1993, c. 44, s. 133)  
- **Statutory reference:** *Regulations under R.S., c. 41 (3rd Supp.)*

> This regulation applies to **goods imported into Canada from countries outside the Canada‚ÄìUnited States‚ÄìMexico Agreement (CUSMA)** and requires certain goods to be **marked with their country of origin** to ensure transparency and compliance with trade rules.

---

### üåç **Key Definitions and Concepts**

#### ‚úÖ **CUSMA Country**
- A "CUSMA country" refers to a party to the **Canada‚ÄìUnited States‚ÄìMexico Agreement (CUSMA)**.
- Goods imported from **non-CUSMA countries** are subject to these marking rules.

#### ‚úÖ **Applicability**
- **Applies to:** Goods listed in **Schedule I** that are imported from a country other than a CUSMA country.
- **Does NOT apply to:** Goods listed in **Schedule II**.

> The marking requirement is **only triggered for goods in Schedule I**, and **exempted for those in Schedule II**.

---

### üìã **Schedule I ‚Äì Goods That Must Be Marked**

These goods **must be marked with their country of origin** in accordance with the *Marking of Imported Goods Regulations*.

#### üîç **Definition of "Country of Origin"**
- The **country of origin** is defined as:
  > *"The country in which the goods were substantially manufactured."*
- This means the country where the **majority of the value-added production** (e.g., manufacturing, processing, assembly) occurred.

> *Note:* "Substantially manufactured" implies that the product has undergone significant transformation or processing in that country.

---

### üìö **Schedule II ‚Äì Exempt Goods**

The following goods are **exempt from the marking requirement**:

| Item | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Item 16** | "Production" includes: growing, mining, harvesting, fishing, trapping, hunting, manufacturing, processing, or assembling. This clarifies that even if a good is produced in one country but processed in another, the definition of "production" is broad. |
| **All other items** | The full list includes a wide range of goods such as hardware, novelties, sporting goods, paper products, apparel, horticultural products, and steel/aluminum goods. |

> These goods are **not required to be marked** under this regulation.
