## ðŸ” **Regulation Summary: Reducing or Offsetting Excise Tax with Refunds or Rebates**

### **1. Legal Basis and Purpose**
- **Authority**: The regulation was made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of National Revenue**, under:
  - Subsection **228(7)** and **277(1)** of the *Excise Tax Act*.
- **Objective**: To allow **corporations that are members of a closely related group** to **offset or reduce** the excise tax they are required to remit (or have already paid) by the amount of a **refund or rebate** that another member in the group is entitled to under the Act.
- **Key Principle**: Tax owed by one member can be reduced by the amount of a refund or rebate due to another member within the same group â€” promoting tax efficiency and avoiding double taxation among related entities.

### **3. Scope of Application**
These regulations apply to:
- Excise tax **required to be remitted** under:
  - Subsection **228(2)** or **228(2.3)** of the Act.
- Excise tax **paid** under:
  - Subsection **228(4)** or Division IV or IV.1 of Part IX of the Act.

> This means the rules cover both **remittances** (tax payments due) and **payments made** (such as in cases of overpayment or rebate claims).

### **4. Prescribed Circumstances (When Offset Is Allowed)**

For a tax reduction or offset to be valid under subsection 228(7), the following must be true:
- The **corporations** involved must be **corporations** (not individuals).
- All such corporations are considered **members** of the closely related group.

### **5. Prescribed Conditions (Requirements for Valid Offset)**

The following conditions must be met for the offset to be valid:

| Condition | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Designation of Coordinator** | One member must be formally designated as the **coordinator** to act on behalf of all members in filing joint applications, returns, and refund/rebate applications. |
| **Filing of Joint Application** | The coordinator must file a **joint application** in the prescribed form, reducing or offsetting tax by the amount of a refund or rebate due to any other member. |
| **Copy of Designation Filed** | A copy of the coordinator designation must be attached to the joint application. |
| **Filing of Direction** | For each member entitled to a refund or rebate, the coordinator must file a **direction** stating that the refund/rebate should be applied to reduce the tax of another member. |
| **Acknowledgement from CRA** | The coordinator must receive **written acknowledgment** from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) confirming receipt of the joint application or revised application. |
| **Identical Reporting Periods** | All members must have the **same reporting period** (e.g., same fiscal year or quarter). |
| **No Overlap with Other Groups** | A member cannot belong to **more than one closely related group** that has filed a joint or revised application and has not filed a notice of withdrawal. |
| **New Member Joins** | If a corporation becomes a member after a joint application is filed, the coordinator must file a **revised application** to include it. |
| **Revised Application Acknowledgement** | A revised application must be accompanied by CRA acknowledgment. |
| **No Direction or Return for New Member Until Acknowledgement** | Until the CRA acknowledges the revised application, **no direction, return, or refund application** may be filed for the new member. |
| **Notice on Member Exit** | When a corporation ceases to be a member, the coordinator must file a **notice** of withdrawal. |

### **6. Prescribed Rules (Operational Requirements)**

#### **(a) Content of Applications**
- **Joint Application**: Must include information proving that **each applicant is a member** of the group.
- **Revised Application**: Must confirm that the newly added corporation is a **member** of the group.

#### **(b) When Offset Is Allowed**
- The tax remittable by a member may be reduced or offset by the amount of a refund or rebate (or portion thereof) from another member **during or after**:
  - The reporting period when the CRA acknowledges the joint or revised application.
- But **before** the earliest of the following:
  1. A reporting period when a corporation **ceases to be a member**.
  2. A reporting period when a member **fails to comply** with the Act or these regulations.
  3. A reporting period in which the coordinator files a **notice** stating that the members **no longer intend** to offset tax using refunds or rebates.

> This ensures the offset is only active during active, compliant participation.

#### **(c) Conditions for Filing a Direction**
A direction (i.e., instruction to apply a refund/rebate) may only be followed if:
- The member has **no tax to remit**, **or**
- The tax remittable by the member has **already been reduced or offset** under subsection 228(6) of the Act (i.e., by a refund or rebate due to the member).

> Prevents over-application or misuse of refunds.

#### **(d) Filing Requirements with the Minister**
The coordinator must file with the Minister:
- **All joint and revised applications**.
- **All directions, returns, and refund/rebate applications** for each reporting period, **together with a statement** that includes:
  - The **tax to be remitted** by each member.
  - The **amount of refund or rebate** each member is entitled to.
- Additionally, for each reporting period, a **list** must be filed that includes:
  - The **name of each member** entitled to a refund or rebate and the **content of their direction**.
  - The **name of each member** who may reduce or offset tax, and the **amount** of reduction.
  - The **order** in which refunds or rebates are applied to reduce tax (if multiple members are involved).

> Ensures transparency and accountability in the offset process.

#### **(e) Tax Remittance Obligations**
- The coordinator must **remit the tax** for each member.
- For members who reduce or offset tax via a direction:
  - The remittance amount is **adjusted downward** by the amount of the refund or rebate applied.

> Ensures accurate tax payment reflecting actual entitlements.
