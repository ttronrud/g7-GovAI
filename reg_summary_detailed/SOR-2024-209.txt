**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

---

### **Regulatory Instrument**
**Title:**  
*Ministerial Conference on the Human Dimension of Ukraine’s 10-Point Peace Formula – Privileges and Immunities Order*

**Legal Basis:**  
This regulation is issued under the authority of the **Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act (FMIOA)**, specifically:

- **Paragraph 5(1)(c)a** and **(f)b to (h)** of the Act  
- **Section 3(2) and 3(3)** of S.C. 2002, c. 12 (the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*)  
- **S.C. 1991, c. 41** (the original Act)

This means the Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs) is granting temporary privileges and immunities to participants in a specific international meeting under the framework of international diplomatic law.

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**
The purpose of this Order is to **grant temporary privileges and immunities** to participants in the **Ministerial Conference on the Human Dimension of Ukraine’s 10-Point Peace Formula**, which is a high-level international diplomatic event aimed at advancing peace negotiations related to Ukraine.

These privileges and immunities are granted **only during a defined period** and are strictly limited to the functions of the participants in relation to the Meeting.

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Convention**  
   Refers to the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, as set out in **Schedule III** of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*.  
   → This is the legal foundation for the privileges granted, particularly in relation to diplomatic missions and international organizations.

2. **Meeting**  
   Defined as the **Ministerial Conference on the Human Dimension of Ukraine’s 10-Point Peace Formula**, scheduled to be held in **Montréal, Quebec, on October 30 and 31, 2024**.  
   → This is a diplomatic forum focused on human rights and humanitarian aspects of the Ukraine conflict, specifically addressing the "10-Point Peace Formula" proposed by Ukraine.

3. **Relevant Period**  
   The time frame from **October 27, 2024, to November 1, 2024**.  
   → This period includes the days before, during, and immediately after the Meeting, allowing for logistical and operational preparation and follow-up.

---

### **Privileges and Immunities Granted During the Relevant Period**

#### 1. **Official Delegates from Foreign States**
- **Privileges and immunities** granted:  
  Paragraphs **11(a) to (f)** and **Sections 12, 14, and 16** of **Article IV of the UN Convention**.
- **Scope**:  
  These privileges are granted **only to the extent required** for the exercise of their official duties in connection with the Meeting.
- **Examples of such privileges** (based on Article IV of the Convention):
  - Immunity from legal process (e.g., arrest, prosecution)
  - Immunity from search or seizure
  - Freedom of movement and communication
  - Protection of official correspondence
  - Exemption from taxes and duties (where applicable)

> This is equivalent to the diplomatic immunity granted to UN delegates, but tailored to the specific context of a ministerial-level peace conference.

#### 2. **Senior Officials of International Organizations**
- **Privileges and immunities**:  
  Comparable to those granted to **diplomatic agents under the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations (VCDR)**, **excluding**:
  - Duty relief (e.g., exemption from work duties)
  - Tax relief (e.g., tax exemptions)
- **Scope**:  
  Only to the extent required for their functions in relation to the Meeting.
- **Rationale**:  
  Senior officials (e.g., heads of departments, technical leads) are granted diplomatic-level status to facilitate their participation and ensure they can operate without interference, but without full tax or duty exemptions.

#### 3. **Other Officials of International Organizations**
- **Privileges and immunities**:  
  As set out in **Paragraphs 18(a), (d), and (e) of Article V of the Convention**.
- **Scope**:  
  Limited to functions related to the Meeting.
- **Examples**:
  - Immunity from legal proceedings (in certain cases)
  - Protection of personal and official documents
  - Freedom to travel and communicate

> These are more limited than those of senior officials but still provide essential protections for operational staff.

#### 4. **Experts Performing Missions for International Organizations**
- **Privileges and immunities**:  
  As provided in **Article VI of the Convention**.
- **Scope**:  
  To the extent required for the performance of their expert functions.
- **Examples**:
  - Immunity from legal action for statements made during the Meeting
  - Protection of expert reports and data
  - Freedom to travel and communicate without interference

> Experts are granted limited immunity to ensure they can provide independent, objective assessments without fear of legal repercussions.

---

### **Key Principles and Limitations**

- **Temporary and Limited Scope**:  
  All privileges are **temporary**, effective only during the **relevant period (October 27 – November 1, 2024)**.  
  After this period, the privileges cease.

- **Function-Based Granting**:  
  Privileges are granted **only to the extent required** for the performance of official duties related to the Meeting.  
  → No broader or unrelated privileges are conferred.

- **No Full Diplomatic Status**:  
  While senior officials are granted privileges comparable to diplomatic agents, they are **not** given full diplomatic immunity (e.g., no duty or tax relief).

- **No Immunity from Criminal Prosecution**:  
  The Order does **not** grant immunity from criminal charges, which remains under national jurisdiction.

- **Compliance with International Law**:  
  The Order ensures that the Meeting operates within internationally recognized norms of diplomatic conduct and protection, respecting the rights of all participants.

---

### **Effective Date**
- The Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** (i.e., officially published in the Canada Gazette).

---

### **Significance and Context**

This Order is a **diplomatic and legal enabler** for a high-level international peace dialogue. By granting appropriate privileges and immunities, Canada ensures that:

- International participants (including foreign ministers and experts) can engage freely and without fear of legal or administrative interference.
- The Meeting can proceed in a neutral, secure, and professional environment.
- Canada upholds its international obligations under the UN Convention and the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations.

It reflects Canada’s commitment to supporting multilateral peace efforts, particularly in the context of the Ukraine conflict, by facilitating dialogue on the human dimension (e.g., humanitarian access, protection of civilians, war crimes, and transitional justice).

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation is a **targeted, temporary, and functionally limited** measure that provides essential diplomatic protections to participants in a critical peace conference. It is grounded in international law, respects national sovereignty, and supports the legitimacy and effectiveness of the Ministerial Conference on the Human Dimension of Ukraine’s 10-Point Peace Formula. The measures are narrowly tailored, time-bound, and strictly aligned with the functions of the participants, ensuring legal clarity and accountability.

--- 

**In short:**  
Canada temporarily grants diplomatic privileges and immunities to foreign delegates, senior officials, and experts attending a key peace conference on Ukraine’s 10-Point Peace Formula in Montréal from October 27 to November 1, 2024 — ensuring safe, effective, and legally protected participation in the dialogue.
