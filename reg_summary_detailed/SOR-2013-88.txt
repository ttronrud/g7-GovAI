## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Export of Substances on the Export Control List Regulations**

### üìå **1. Regulatory Background and Legal Authority**

- **Legal Basis**:  
  These regulations were made under **subsection 332(1)a** of the *Canadian Environmental Protection Act, 1999 (CEPA 1999)*, which empowers the Minister of the Environment to regulate the export of certain hazardous substances.

- **Publication and Public Consultation**:  
  On **August 6, 2011**, the Minister of the Environment published a **proposed version** of these regulations in *Canada Gazette, Part I*.  
  This allowed stakeholders to submit **comments or objections**, including requests for a **board of review**, to ensure transparency and public participation in environmental policy.

- **Final Adoption**:  
  After review and public input, the **Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of the Environment and the Minister of Health**, formally adopted the regulations under **subsection 102(1) of CEPA 1999**.

### üéØ **3. Purpose of the Regulations**

The primary objectives are:

1. **Prohibit or regulate the export** of substances listed in the **Export Control List (ECL)**.
2. **Implement international agreements**:
   - *Rotterdam Convention* (PIC procedure)
   - *Stockholm Convention* (POPs)
   - *Minamata Convention* (mercury control)
3. Ensure **transparency, accountability, and environmental safety** in international trade of hazardous substances.

### üö® **6. Export of Persistent Organic Pollutants (POPs)**

- **Definition**: Substances listed in **Annex A or B of the Stockholm Convention**, **excluding** those added by amendments not in force for Canada.

#### ‚úÖ Conditions for Exporting POPs (from Part 2 or 3 of ECL):

A person may export a POP **only if** one of the following applies:

1. **Specific exemption or acceptable purpose** exists in Annex A/B of the Stockholm Convention:
   - The destination **must be a Stockholm Party**.
   - The Party must have **registered** the exemption in its official register.
   - Export must comply with the terms of the exemption.

2. **Export to non-Stockholm Parties**:
   - Annual certification (as per paragraph 2(b)(iii) of Article 3 of the Stockholm Convention) must be transmitted to the Secretariat.
   - Export must comply with the exemption terms.

3. **Export under Article 6(1)(d)**:  
   ‚Üí Export is authorized for specific purposes (e.g., research, analysis, disposal).

4. **Research or analytical use**:
   - Total quantity exported for lab use ‚â§ **10 kg/year**

5. **Incidental presence in trace amounts** in the product.

6. **Legacy products**:
   - Product manufactured **on or before** the entry into force of a ban in Canada.
   - Canada has provided a **public notification** to the Secretariat (as per note (ii) in Annex A/B), which has been made publicly available.

> ‚ùå **Exclusion**:  
> Does **not apply** to POPs contained in **hazardous waste or recyclable materials** regulated under the *Cross-border Movement of Hazardous Waste and Hazardous Recyclable Material Regulations*.
