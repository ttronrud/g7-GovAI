**Detailed Summary of the United Nations Remission Order (Effective January 2, 1952)**

---

### **Overview**
The *United Nations Remission Order* is a regulatory instrument that grants specific tax exemptions and immunities to the United Nations (UN) and its agents when importing or purchasing goods into Canada for official use. It is designed to facilitate the UN’s operational activities by removing or reducing certain customs, excise, and consumption taxes, while maintaining compliance with Canadian trade and fiscal laws. The Order is cited as the *United Nations Remission Order* and operates under the authority of the *Privileges and Immunities (International Organizations) Act*.

---

### **Key Provisions and Detailed Explanations**

#### **1. Exemption from Excise Stamp Tax on Official Cheques**
- **Exemption Condition**:  
  Cheques drawn on the **official funds of the United Nations** are **exempt** from the excise stamp tax.
- **Non-Exemption Condition**:  
  Cheques issued against **personal accounts** (i.e., individual or non-official accounts) are **subject to the excise stamp tax**.
- **Purpose**: Ensures that financial transactions conducted by the UN using its official funds are not burdened by administrative fees, promoting operational efficiency.

---

#### **2. Exemption from Customs Duties and Taxes on Goods for Official Use**
- **Scope of Exemption**:  
  The UN and its agents are exempt from:
  - Customs duties
  - Consumption or sales taxes (as imposed under the *Customs Tariff and Excise Tax Act*)
  - Prohibitions and restrictions on imports and exports
- **Conditions**:
  - The goods must be imported or purchased **for the official use of the United Nations**, not for personal or commercial benefit.
  - "Official use" means use in furtherance of the UN’s mission (e.g., humanitarian aid, peacekeeping, development programs), and **must not provide financial benefit to any individual or private party**.

---

#### **3. Disposal of Goods Admitted Free After One Year**
- **Exemption upon Disposal**:  
  Goods that have been admitted into Canada **free of duty and tax** and have remained in the possession or use of the importer for **at least one year** may be **sold or disposed of in Canada without paying customs duties or taxes**.
- **Otherwise**:  
  If goods are sold or disposed of **within one year** of purchase, they are subject to the **ordinary provisions** of the *Customs Tariff and Excise Tax Act*, including applicable duties and taxes.
- **Rationale**: Prevents abuse of exemptions by enabling the UN to retain and later dispose of goods without tax liability only after a reasonable holding period.

---

#### **4. Required Endorsement on Customs Entries**
- **Mandatory Statement**:  
  On all import and export entries, the importer must **endorse** the following statement:  
  > “Free under provisions of the Privileges and Immunities (International Organizations) Act”
- **Purpose**:  
  To clearly identify the nature of the transaction and ensure transparency and compliance with Canadian customs procedures.

---

#### **5. Full Exemption for Publications, Printed Matter, Films, and Sound Recordings**
- **Scope of Exemption**:  
  The UN and its agents are fully exempt from:
  - Customs duties
  - Sales or excise taxes
  - Prohibitions or restrictions on import, export, use, or sale
- **Application**:  
  These materials may be:
  - Imported
  - Exported
  - Used
  - Sold
  — all **without payment of duty or tax**, provided the usual import/export entries are completed.
- **Endorsement Requirement**:  
  The endorsement from Section 4 must be applied to entries involving such materials.

---

#### **6. Tax Remission or Refund for Purchased Goods (Under Excise Tax Act)**
- **Eligibility**:  
  When the UN purchases goods from licensed manufacturers or wholesalers in Canada, it may claim **remission or refund** of:
  - Excise tax
  - Consumption or sales tax
  — **if the goods are purchased for official use**.
- **Conditions for Claim**:
  - The purchase must be made under an **appropriate certificate**.
  - The UN must include a **signed certificate** from a senior officer or authorized agent stating:
    1. The account will be paid from the **funds of the United Nations**.
    2. The exemption is **properly allowable** under the *Privileges and Immunities (International Organizations) Act*.
- **Tax Liability Exception**:  
  If goods are sold or otherwise disposed of **within one year** of purchase (except for publications, printed matter, films, or sound recordings), they are subject to **existing tax rates**, and the **vendor remains liable** for such taxes.

---

#### **7. Use of Codes and Courier Immunities**
- **Right to Use Codes**:  
  The UN is granted the right to use **special codes** for identification in communications and logistics.
- **Immunities for Correspondence**:
  - The UN may dispatch and receive correspondence via **courier services or bags**.
  - These couriers and bags enjoy the **same immunities and privileges** as diplomatic couriers and bags.
- **Security Note**:  
  Nothing in this provision prevents the UN and Canadian government from agreeing on **appropriate security measures** to protect sensitive information or prevent misuse.

---

#### **8. Handling of Despatch Bags or Packages**
- **Automatic Forwarding Rule**:  
  If a despatch bag or package arrives in Canada:
  - Addressed to the UN or a senior UN official, and
  - From external examination (without breaking the seal), appears to contain **only official documents**
  → It shall be **forwarded directly to the intended recipient** by customs authorities **without delay or inspection**.
- **Purpose**:  
  Ensures swift and secure handling of official communications, reducing bureaucratic delays and preserving the integrity of UN operations.

---

### **General Principles and Limitations**

| Principle | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Official Use Only** | Exemptions apply strictly to activities advancing UN objectives. Personal or commercial gain disqualifies goods from exemption. |
| **One-Year Rule** | After one year of use, goods may be sold tax-free; otherwise, taxes apply. This prevents indefinite tax avoidance. |
| **Vendor Liability** | Vendors are liable for taxes if goods are sold within one year (except for specific media). |
| **Compliance Required** | All exemptions require proper documentation, including signed certificates and customs endorsements. |
| **No Precedent for Abuse** | The regulations are designed to balance operational needs with fiscal accountability. |

---

### **Legal Basis**
- The Order derives its authority from **Article II, Section 8** of the *Privileges and Immunities (International Organizations) Act*.
- It operates within the framework of Canadian customs and excise law, but provides specific carve-outs for international organizations acting in the public interest.

---

### **Significance and Impact**
- **Facilitates UN Operations**: Enables the UN to operate efficiently in Canada by eliminating unnecessary tax burdens on official purchases and communications.
- **Promotes International Cooperation**: Recognizes the UN’s unique status as a global body with distinct operational needs.
- **Balances Fiscal Responsibility**: Includes safeguards (e.g., one-year rule, vendor liability) to prevent misuse of exemptions.

---

### **Conclusion**
The *United Nations Remission Order* establishes a clear, structured, and legally grounded framework for tax remission and operational immunities for the United Nations in Canada. It ensures that the UN can function effectively without being hindered by domestic tax and customs regulations, while maintaining accountability through defined conditions, documentation, and time-based restrictions. This Order remains a foundational example of how states accommodate international organizations while preserving fiscal integrity and regulatory oversight.
