## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Consolidation Regulations (SOR/2005-168, s. 2 ‚Äì Repealed, SOR/89-158, s. 6; SOR/92-578, s. 5)**

> **Note:** This regulation is a set of procedural rules governing the filing, administration, and enforcement of **consolidation orders** under **Part X of the Bankruptcy and Insolvency Act (BIA)**. It outlines the duties of the **clerk of court**, the rights and obligations of **debtors and creditors**, and the process for managing consolidated debt claims. Many sections have been **repealed or amended**, but the core procedural framework remains relevant.

---

### üîπ 1. **Definition of Key Terms**
- **"Act"** refers to **Part X of the Bankruptcy and Insolvency Act**, which deals with the orderly payment of debts through consolidation or restructuring.
- **"Court"** is defined differently by province:
  - Saskatchewan: Her Majesty‚Äôs Court of Queen‚Äôs Bench
  - Nova Scotia: County Court
  - British Columbia: Supreme Court and designated County Courts
  - Northwest Territories: Supreme Court of the Northwest Territories
  - Prince Edward Island: Supreme Court of Prince Edward Island

---

### üîπ 2. **Application Fee and Receipt**
- A debtor applying for a **consolidation order** must pay a **non-refundable application fee of $20**.
- The **clerk must issue a receipt** for this fee.
- This fee is **separate from all other court funds** and not subject to general court revenue.

---

### üîπ 3. **Fees and Financial Management**
- All fees collected under the BIA must be kept in a **separate account** from funds collected under other statutes (e.g., bankruptcy or insolvency laws).
- **Financial transparency** is required:
  - The clerk maintains:
    - A **cash book** recording:
      - Date of payment
      - Consolidation order number
      - Debtor‚Äôs name
      - Amount of payment
      - Particulars of the payment
    - A **separate consolidation account sheet**
    - A **separate register sheet** for each debtor
- Payments are deposited with:
  - A **bank**, or
  - A **non-bank corporation** if:
    - Deposits are insured by the **Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC)**, or
    - Protected under a **provincial deposit insurance scheme**

> ‚úÖ Purpose: Ensures financial accountability and prevents commingling of funds.

---

### üîπ 4. **Affidavit as Application**
- An **affidavit filed by the debtor** under subsection 219(2) of the Act **automatically constitutes an application** for a consolidation order.
- The affidavit must be filed in **duplicate**.
- The affidavit includes:
  - Full disclosure of:
    - All assets and liabilities
    - Creditors and amounts owed
    - Security held by creditors
    - Personal and spouse‚Äôs income, occupation, and dependents
    - Monthly living expenses
  - A statement of **take-home income** after deductions
  - Duration of unemployment (if any)

> ‚ö†Ô∏è This is a foundational document that sets the basis for the consolidation order.

---

### üîπ 5. **Creditor Relationships and Security**
- If a **creditor is related to the debtor**, the clerk may **require an affidavit from the creditor** proving the debt before entering the creditor‚Äôs name in the register.
- A **registered creditor holding security** may, at any time, **elect to rely on that security** under **section 232 of the BIA**.
- Conditions for relying on security:
  - Must file an **affidavit stating the value** of the security.
  - If the proceeds from repossession or sale **exceed the claim**, the **excess must be paid into court** and applied to other creditors.
  - If proceeds are **less than the claim**, the creditor retains the balance.
- **Exception**: Security exempt under provincial law (e.g., chattels not subject to seizure) is not subject to this rule.
- **Creditor liability**: If a creditor fails to comply with valuation or disclosure requirements, the court may **order an independent appraisal** of the security.

---

### üîπ 6. **Consolidation Account and Disbursement**
- Upon filing the debtor‚Äôs affidavit and entering its details in the register:
  - The clerk **opens a consolidation account** in the debtor‚Äôs name.
  - All **moneys paid into court** under the consolidation order are **credited to this account**.
- Before distributing funds:
  - The clerk may **deduct 15%** to cover **administrative and operational costs** (e.g., court fees, postage, legal services).
- The clerk may **deduct postage costs** for mailing notices and forms before distribution.

---

### üîπ 7. **Transfer of Proceedings**
- The clerk may **transfer the consolidation proceedings** to another location if it is determined that administration would be more effective:
  - **Within the same province**: To a different judicial district or centre.
  - **Across provinces**: To a province where the BIA is in force, **only after**:
    - Notifying the debtor and registered creditors
    - Distributing all funds already collected
- This allows for **geographic flexibility** in managing complex or dispersed debt cases.

---

### üîπ 8. **Hearing and Notice Procedures**
- All applications to the court must be made to the **next sitting** of the court unless:
  - The sitting is within **7 days** of the application, or
  - The court orders otherwise, or
  - The parties agree
- The **judge may hear matters in chambers** (private session), especially for procedural or administrative issues.
- A **notice of motion** under the Act:
  - Does **not require an affidavit** unless the clerk deems one necessary.
  - If required, the **affidavit must be served** with the notice.
- Notices must be served on:
  - The debtor
  - All registered creditors
- **Default or objection** must be raised within **20 days** of receiving the **Notice to Creditors**.

---

### üîπ 9. **Notice of New Claims**
- If the clerk issues notice of a **new claim** against the debtor (under subsection 231(1)):
  - A **30-day period** is allowed for **disputing the claim**.
  - Creditors must formally object within that time.

---

### üîπ 10. **Variation of Consolidation Orders**
- If the consolidation order is varied (under sections 227(1), 231(3), or 234(4)):
  - A **Notice of Variation of Consolidation Order (Form 11)** must be issued to:
    - The debtor
    - All registered creditors
  - A copy of the notice must be **attached to the court file**
  - The original consolidation order must be **endorsed** with:
    - Date and terms of variation
    - Name and title of the authority (e.g., judge) ordering the variation

---

### üîπ 11. **Termination of Consolidation Orders**
A consolidation order ends under **either** of the following conditions:
1. **Default by the debtor**:
   - The clerk issues a **Notice of Default (Form 18)**.
2. **Court order**:
   - Under paragraph 233(3)(b), the court permits **all registered creditors to proceed independently** to enforce their claims.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Once terminated, the debtor is no longer bound by the consolidation order, and creditors may pursue individual enforcement actions.

---

### üîπ 12. **Applications for Continuance on Grounds of Force Majeure**
- If a debtor applies to **continue under the consolidation order** due to circumstances beyond their control (e.g., illness, natural disaster):
  - The application must be made via a **notice of motion**.
  - The court will consider whether the hardship justifies continuation.

---

### üîπ 13. **Forms and Documentation**
- The **forms in Schedule I** are the official forms used in consolidation proceedings, including:
  - Affidavit of the debtor
  - Notice to Creditors
  - Notice of Objection
  - Notice of Motion
  - Consolidation Order
- The **forms in Schedule II** are used for **accounting and record-keeping**.
- **Electronic accounting systems** are permitted **if they contain the same information** as the required paper forms.

---

### üîπ 14. **Interest and Administrative Costs**
- Every consolidation order carries **interest at 5% per annum**.
- **Costs of proceedings** are at the **absolute discretion of the court**, but must not exceed those in a similar civil action.
- **No costs** are incurred for hearings before the clerk (unless ordered by the court).

---
