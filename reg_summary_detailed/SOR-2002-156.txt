**Detailed Summary of the Rules of the Supreme Court of Canada (as of April 15, 2002, R.S., c. 34 (3rd Supp.), s. 7)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Rules of the Supreme Court of Canada*, adopted on April 15, 2002, pursuant to **subsection 97(1)a of the Supreme Court Act**, govern all proceedings before the Court. These rules provide a comprehensive framework for the conduct of litigation, including procedural requirements, service of documents, filing procedures, interpretation of time periods, representation, and the handling of sensitive or confidential materials.

These rules apply **universally to all proceedings** in the Supreme Court of Canada unless otherwise specified by statute or another Act of Parliament.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Definitions**

The rules begin with a clear list of key definitions that shape the interpretation and application of the rules:

- **Act**: Refers to the *Supreme Court Act*.
- **Affidavit**: Includes both an affirmation and a statutory declaration.
- **Agent**: A lawyer practicing in the National Capital Region, as defined under the *National Capital Act*.
- **Application for Leave to Appeal**: Refers to motions under Rule 25 and Section 40 of the Act.
- **Attorney General**: Includes the Attorney General of Canada, a provincial attorney general, or a territorial minister of justice.
- **Authorities**: Encompasses legislative enactments, case law, articles, texts, treaties, and excerpts.
- **Commissioner for Oaths**: Any person authorized (federal or provincial) to conduct oath examinations.
- **Counsel**: A member of the provincial bar.
- **Electronic Filing Portal**: The official online portal of the Court or any website designated by the Court.
- **Holiday**: As defined in the *Interpretation Act*, including Saturday.
- **Last Known Address/Email/Fax Number**: The most recently filed address or contact details (from documents filed in the Court or the lower court or a notice of change under Rule 17.1).
- **Party**: A person named in the style of cause (Rule 22), including those added or substituted under Rule 18. When referring to the lower court, it means a party in that court.
- **Person**: Includes bodies politic or corporate, His Majesty the King, and an attorney general.
- **Printing**: Reproduction via any method (e.g., photocopying, scanning), but does **not** include handwriting.
- **Proceeding**: Includes appeals, applications for leave to appeal, motions, or references before the Court, a judge, or the Registrar.

> *Note: These definitions are foundational and ensure consistent interpretation across all proceedings.*

---

### **2. Flexibility and Discretion**

The Court, a judge, or the Registrar may:

- **Adopt alternative procedures** where the rules do not provide specific guidance (provided they are not inconsistent with the Act or rules).
- **Issue directions** upon motion or on their own initiative.
- **Impose terms and conditions** in orders or directions as they deem appropriate.
- **Make orders that are proportionate** to the complexity of the proceeding and the importance of the issues involved.

> This reflects a principle of judicial discretion and proportionality, ensuring procedural fairness without rigidity.

---

### **3. Time Calculation**

Time periods are governed by the *Interpretation Act*, with specific adjustments:

#### **General Rule**
- Time periods are calculated **excluding the starting day or event**, and **including the last day of the last seven-day period**.

#### **Exclusions**
- **Holidays**: Not included in periods of **less than six days**.
- **July**: The month of July is **excluded** from time calculations **except** for:
  - Service and filing of a notice of intervention (Rule 33(4))
  - Filing of a factum, record, or book of authorities in an appeal or cross-appeal (Rules 35–37)
  - Motion for intervention under paragraph 56(b), including responses or replies
- **December 23 to January 3**: This period (the Christmas/New Year period) is **excluded** from time calculations **except** for:
  - Service and filing of a notice of constitutional question under Rule 33(2)

> This ensures that time is not artificially extended during holidays or summer months, unless necessary for specific procedural steps.

#### **Extensions or Abrasions**
- The Court, a judge, or Registrar may **extend or abridge** time periods on motion or their own initiative.
- An affidavit must be filed supporting any request for extension or abridgement, explaining the **reason for delay or urgency**.

#### **Adjournments**
- The Chief Justice or any other judge may **adjourn hearings** on motion or at their own initiative.
- An affidavit must state the **facts or reasons** for adjournment.

#### **Excusing Non-Compliance**
- A party may be **excused** from complying with rules on motion or at the discretion of the Court, judge, or Registrar.

> These provisions provide flexibility for practical and unforeseen circumstances.

### **4. Filing and Service Procedures**

#### **Filing Methods**
A document may be filed through any of the following methods:
1. **Electronic filing portal** (preferred method)
2. **Fax transmission**
3. **Email**
4. **Hand delivery**
5. **Mail or courier**

> *Note: Hand delivery, mail, or courier is required for documents that must be bound (e.g., pleadings, motions, affidavits).*

#### **Filing Dates**
- **Hand delivery, mail, courier**: Deemed filed on the date of the Court’s filing stamp.
- **Electronic, fax, email**: Deemed filed on the day received, **unless that day is a holiday**, in which case it is deemed filed on the **next business day**.
- All electronic filings are timestamped in **Eastern Time Zone**.

#### **Binding Requirements**
- Within **five business days** of filing (via electronic, fax, or email), the original and required copies must be **bound** and filed by hand delivery, mail, or courier.
- This ensures consistency, security, and accessibility of records.

#### **Service of Documents**
Service must be made to:
- A party’s **counsel or agent** at their **last known address, fax number, or email address**, or
- To the **party personally** (if not represented)

**Methods of Service**:
- Personal service (any day except holidays)
- Ordinary mail (except for originating documents or documents filed in support)
- Registered or certified mail or courier
- Fax transmission (with exceptions):
  - Not allowed for originating documents or documents filed in support
  - Not allowed for documents over 40 pages unless the recipient consents
- Email
- Leaving a copy with a party’s counsel, agent, or employee

> **Exception**: If service is attempted and fails in a province or territory, a copy may be left on the premises.

#### **Electronic Service**
- For email service:
  - Must include title, sender info, recipient info, date/time, and number of attachments or access instructions.
- For fax:
  - Must include title, sender details, recipient details, date/time, page count, fax number, and contact for transmission issues.

#### **Deemed Service**
- A document is deemed served on the day it is received or admitted to be received.
- **Exception**: If received between **5:00 p.m. and 12:00 a.m.** or on a **holiday**, service is deemed to occur on the **next business day**.
- **Ordinary mail**: Deemed served on the **fifth business day** after mailing.

#### **Proof of Service**
Proof must be filed within **two days** of service (unless ordered otherwise), including:
- For **ordinary mail**: Affidavit of service in Form 20 + post office receipt or proof of delivery
- For **registered/certified mail or courier**: Affidavit of service in Form 20 + receipt or signature

> This ensures accountability and enforceability of service.

---

### **5. Confidentiality and Sealing**

Documents that are:
- Subject to a **sealing or confidentiality order** from a lower court or the Supreme Court
- Classified as **confidential by legislation**

Must be:
- **Sealed in an envelope**
- Accompanied by:
  - A **covering letter**
  - A **copy of the sealing order or legal provision**

#### Specific Requirements for Sealed Documents:
- If a document contains or reveals confidential information:
  - Must be sealed and include:
    - One **redacted electronic version** (if required)
    - Two **redacted printed versions**
- If a redacted version cannot be filed, a **motion to seal** must be submitted to the Registrar.

> This protects sensitive information (e.g., national security, personal data, trade secrets).

---

### **6. Representation and Counsel**

#### **General Representation**
- A party may act **in person** or be represented by **counsel**.
- **Counsel** is defined as a member of the provincial bar.

#### **Exceptions to Representation by Counsel**
- A party may continue to be represented by a **non-counsel** (e.g., a legal assistant, paralegal) if:
  - That person represented them in a lower court and they choose to continue.
  - A judge grants leave under applicable provincial or federal legislation.
