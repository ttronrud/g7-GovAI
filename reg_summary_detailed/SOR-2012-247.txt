## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Patented Medicine Prices Review Board Rules of Practice and Procedure (2012)**

### üîπ **Overview and Legal Basis**
- The **Patented Medicine Prices Review Board (PMPRB)**, a federal regulatory body under the *Patent Act*, has adopted these **Rules of Practice and Procedure**.
- These rules were approved by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Health**, under **subsection 96(2)a of the Patent Act**.
- The rules govern the **procedural framework** for all hearings and proceedings conducted by the PMPRB in relation to the pricing and information requirements of patented or protected medicines.

> **Purpose**: To ensure that proceedings before the PMPRB are conducted **fairly, expeditiously, and transparently**, while allowing flexibility in procedure to meet the unique nature of pharmaceutical pricing disputes.

## üèõÔ∏è General Principles and Procedural Flexibility

1. **Applicability**  
   These rules apply to **all proceedings** before the PMPRB.

2. **Quorum**  
   A quorum consists of **two members** of the Board.

3. **Defects in Form or Procedure**  
   A proceeding **cannot be defeated** solely due to a procedural defect ‚Äî this ensures procedural fairness without undermining the process.

4. **Discretionary Authority**  
   The Board may:
   - Vary, supplement, or dispense with any rule
   - Deal with **unforeseen procedural matters** as it sees fit
   - Ensure the **fair and expeditious conduct** of proceedings

5. **Authority to Receive Evidence**  
   The Board may:
   - Receive any evidence it considers appropriate
   - Take judicial notice of facts (e.g., widely accepted scientific or technical facts)
   - Take notice of facts related to patented or protected medicines
   - Add parties at any stage
   - Allow amendments to documents
   - Decide questions of procedure

6. **Directives and Affidavits**  
   The Board may:
   - Require parties to provide documents or information (in paper or electronic format)
   - Require a particular fact to be established by **affidavit**

> ‚úÖ All affidavits must be **filed with the Secretary**.

7. **Witness Testimony**  
   - Witnesses must testify **under oath or solemn affirmation**
   - May be examined by the calling party and **cross-examined by adverse parties**

8. **Expert Witness Evidence**  
   - **Not admissible** unless:
     - The issue was raised in pleadings or pre-hearing conference
     - The expert is called to **rebut** another party‚Äôs expert
   - A party may not call **more than two expert witnesses per issue** without **Board leave**
   - Required expert witness documentation includes:
     - Statement of issues addressed
     - Qualifications of the expert
     - Curriculum vitae (attached as schedule)
     - Facts and assumptions underlying opinions
     - Summary of opinions
     - Agreement/disagreement with other experts (if applicable)
     - Reasons for opinions
     - Literature or documents relied upon
     - Summary of methodology
   - Must file a **signed Expert Witness Declaration (Form 1)** with the Secretary
   - Expert must be **available for examination and cross-examination**

9. **Time Limits**  
   - Examination in chief of an expert **cannot exceed 90 minutes without Board consent**

10. **Service and Filing Deadlines**  
    - If a deadline falls on a **Saturday, Sunday, or holiday**, it is extended to the **next business day**
    - Documents served after **5:00 p.m.** are deemed served at **9:00 a.m.** the next business day

11. **Service of Documents**  
    Must be done by:
    - Personal service (per Rule 11)
    - Courier (deemed served on day of delivery)
    - Electronic transmission (deemed served on day of transmission)
    - Or by any method directed by the Board

12. **Filing Requirements**  
    - Must be filed with the Secretary via:
      - Personal service
      - Courier or certified mail
      - Electronic transmission (PDF or authorized format)
    - **Electronic documents must:**
      - Have identical cover page, table of contents, and page numbers as paper version
      - Include a filename with:
        - Name of medicine/company (if applicable)
        - Name of filing party
        - Title of document
        - Date
      - Be in **PDF format** or authorized format
    - Date marked by Secretary is deemed **filing date**
    - **Three paper copies** must be filed with Secretary if served; if electronic, **within 48 hours**
    - On request, Secretary must provide **filing confirmation**

13. **Confidentiality**  
    - Any document containing confidential information must clearly label:
      > **‚ÄúCONFIDENTIAL / CONFIDENTIEL‚Äù**  
      prominently on the cover and pages

## üì£ Initiation of Proceedings

1. **Notice of Hearing**  
   - Issued by the Secretary and signed
   - Must be served personally on:
     - The respondent
     - All concerned ministers
     - Any other person directed by the Board

2. **Contents of Notice of Hearing**  
   - Must include:
     - Date and time of the **teleconference** (per Rule 22)
     - Statement of **purpose of the hearing**
     - Statement that the hearing will be **public**, unless:
       - The respondent shows that **specific, direct, and substantial harm** would result from disclosure
     - A **schedule of events**
     - Any other information the Board considers appropriate

3. **Public Notice**  
   - The Secretary must publish notice in the **Canada Gazette** and any other manner directed by the Chairperson

4. **Record of Proceedings**  
   - Must be maintained and include:
     - Notice of hearing
     - All documents filed by parties
     - Board decisions, orders, directions
     - Transcripts of pre-hearing conferences and hearings
     - Any other documents directed by the Board

## ‚öñÔ∏è Responses and Replies

1. **Respondent‚Äôs Response**  
   - Must be filed **within 20 days** of receiving the notice of hearing
   - Must be:
     - Dated and signed
     - In consecutively numbered paragraphs
     - Include:
       - Admission or denial of each material fact or ground in the allegation/application
       - Grounds for opposing the proposed order
       - Material facts relied upon
       - Address of person for service

2. **Failure to Respond**  
   - If no response is filed within 20 days:
     - The Board may **make findings and issue an order** under **section 83 of the Act**, provided:
       - A copy of the notice was served
       - Evidence shows the respondent was required to provide information

3. **Board Staff‚Äôs Reply**  
   - Must be filed within **20 days** of receiving the response
   - Must:
     - Be in consecutively numbered paragraphs
     - Include admission or denial of each fact in the response
   - If no reply is filed, it is **deemed to deny all facts and grounds** in the response

## üö© Leave to Intervene

- Any person with a **legitimate interest** in the subject matter may apply for **leave to intervene**
- Motion must include:
  - Name and address of intervener and counsel
  - Nature of interest and reason for intervention
  - Facts supporting the motion
  - Issues the intervener intends to address
- Must be filed and served per Rule 10
- Parties may submit written submissions
- The Board may:
  - Grant or deny intervention
  - Impose conditions or restrictions
- Considerations include:
  - Whether the person has a **sufficient interest**
  - Whether intervention would **harm other parties**
  - Whether it would **interfere with fair and expeditious conduct**
