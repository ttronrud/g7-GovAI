**Detailed Summary of the Canadian Payments Association Reporting Requirements Regulations**

---

### **Regulatory Context and Authority**

These regulations were issued under the authority of **subsection 35(1)a of the Canadian Payments Act (CPA)**, by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**. The regulations are part of **S.C. 2014, c. 39, s. 356(1)** and are codified under **R.S., c. C-21** and **S.C. 2001, c. 9, s. 218**.

The primary purpose of these regulations is to establish **specific reporting obligations** for the **Canadian Payments Association (CPA)**, ensuring transparency, accountability, and financial sustainability in its operations and long-term planning.

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Act**: Refers to the *Canadian Payments Act* — the foundational legislation governing the operations, structure, and responsibilities of the Canadian Payments Association.

2. **Five-Year Corporate Plan**: A comprehensive strategic and financial document required to be submitted by the CPA Board. It outlines the Association’s financial plans and performance expectations over a five-year period.

---

### **Mandatory Content of the Five-Year Corporate Plan**

The five-year corporate plan must include the following elements for each relevant fiscal year:

#### **(a) For the Fiscal Year for Which the Plan Is Submitted**
- The **operating budget** and **capital expenditures budget** for that fiscal year.

#### **(b) For Each of the Next Four Fiscal Years (Following the Submission Year)**
- **Operating budget forecasts** and **capital expenditures budget forecasts** for each of the subsequent four years.

#### **(c) For the Fiscal Year Prior to the Submission Year**
- **Audited financial statements**; if audited statements are not available, the Association must provide:
  - An **updated operating budget**
  - An **updated capital expenditures budget**

#### **(d) For the Fiscal Year Before the One Required Under (c)**
- **Audited financial statements** (i.e., two years prior to the submission year)

> *Note: This ensures a consistent historical baseline for financial review and comparison.*

#### **E. Explanations of Major Variances**
- A detailed explanation of any **major variance** between:
  - The amounts proposed in the current plan and those previously provided in the same fiscal year (under paragraphs a–d).
  - The amounts proposed in the current plan and those previously provided in the **same fiscal year** in the **previous five-year corporate plan**.

> *This provision promotes transparency and accountability by requiring justification for significant changes in budgetary assumptions or financial projections.*

---

### **Submission and Approval Timeline**

- The **Board of the Canadian Payments Association** must submit the five-year corporate plan to the **Minister of Finance** for approval **no later than three months after the start of the fiscal year** for which the plan is submitted.

- Any **significant amendment** to an already approved five-year corporate plan must also be submitted to the Minister for **re-approval**.

> This ensures that the government maintains oversight and can assess the financial feasibility and strategic direction of the Association.

---

### **Annual Financial Report (Section 24 of the CPA)**

- The Association must prepare and **publish on its official website** a report on its financial performance for each fiscal year.

- This report must be published **no later than five months after the end of the fiscal year**.

> This enhances public access to financial information and supports transparency and trust in the Association’s operations.

---

### **Effective Date and Registration Provisions**

- These regulations come into force on the day that **subsection 356(1) of the Economic Action Plan 2014 Act, No. 2** comes into force.

- If the regulations are **registered (published in the Canada Gazette) after that date**, they will take effect on the **date of registration**.

> This provides a clear and predictable enforcement timeline, with a fallback mechanism in case of delayed publication.

---

### **Note on Enforcement**

- These regulations were **in force as of June 5, 2015**, indicating that they were formally adopted and implemented by that date.

---

### **Purpose and Significance**

These regulations serve several critical functions:

1. **Financial Accountability**: By requiring detailed budgets, forecasts, and audited financial statements, the regulations ensure that the Canadian Payments Association operates with fiscal responsibility and transparency.

2. **Strategic Planning**: The five-year corporate plan enables long-term strategic planning and helps align the Association’s activities with national payment system goals.

3. **Government Oversight**: The requirement to submit plans and amendments to the Minister of Finance ensures that the government can monitor and influence the Association’s financial direction and performance.

4. **Public Transparency**: By mandating public publication of financial reports, the regulations increase access to information for stakeholders, including the public, regulators, and financial institutions.

5. **Performance Monitoring**: The variance explanations allow for early identification of financial or operational issues and support performance improvement.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Canadian Payments Association Reporting Requirements Regulations* establish a robust, transparent, and accountable framework for the financial management and reporting of the Canadian Payments Association. By mandating comprehensive five-year financial planning, timely submissions, and public disclosure, the regulations enhance the integrity, stability, and public trust in Canada’s payment systems.

These rules are essential for ensuring that the CPA operates effectively and efficiently in support of Canada’s broader financial infrastructure objectives.
