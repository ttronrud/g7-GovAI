**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: *Officers Authorized to Exercise Powers or Perform Duties of the Minister of National Revenue Regulations* (S.C. 1986, c. 1)**

---

### **Overview and Background**

This regulation, formally titled *Officers Authorized to Exercise Powers or Perform Duties of the Minister of National Revenue Regulations*, was enacted under **subsection 164(3)** of the *Customs Act* and published in *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **June 28, 1986**. The publication provided a reasonable opportunity for interested parties to submit written representations to the Minister of National Revenue.

Following this public consultation, **Her Excellency the Governor in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the Minister of National Revenue, approved and enacted the regulation under **paragraph 164(1)(a)** of the *Customs Act*. The regulation is effective upon the coming into force of paragraph 164(1)(a) of the *Customs Act*.

The primary purpose of this regulation is to **authorize specific officers or classes of officers within the Department of National Revenue (DNR), Customs and Excise** to exercise certain powers and perform specific duties normally reserved for the **Minister of National Revenue** under the *Customs Act*, subject to defined limitations.

---

### **Regulation Title and Citation**

- **Title**: Officers Authorized to Exercise Powers or Perform Duties of the Minister of National Revenue Regulations  
- **Citation**: S.C. 1986, c. 1  
- **Reference**: Often cited as "the Officers Regulations" or "the Authorized Officers Regulations"

---

### **Key Definitions**

The regulation establishes several key definitions to clarify scope and terminology:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Customs Act* |
| **Chief Officer of Customs (for an area or place)** | The manager of the customs office(s) serving that specific area or location |
| **Region** | One of six designated geographic regions of the DNR: <br> - Atlantic <br> - Quebec <br> - Northern Ontario <br> - Southern Ontario <br> - Prairie <br> - Pacific |
| **Regional Collector** | The manager of customs offices within a given region |

These definitions are essential for identifying the geographic and administrative structure under which officers operate.

### **Authorization of Officers to Exercise Ministerial Powers**

The regulation authorizes **various officers and positions** to exercise powers and perform duties of the Minister of National Revenue under the *Customs Act*, **with important exclusions**.

#### **1. Deputy Minister of National Revenue for Customs and Excise**
- May exercise **all powers and duties of the Minister**, **except**:
  - Subsections 9(1) and (2)
  - Paragraphs 9(5)(h) and 24(1)(c)
  - Subsection 51(3)
  - Sections 59 and 105
  - Subsections 106(3) and 109(1)
  - Sections 134, 149, and 165 of the *Customs Act*

> **Note**: This is the highest-ranking non-ministerial officer with broad authority, excluding powers related to specific enforcement, assessments, and certain administrative functions.

---

#### **2. Assistant Deputy Minister (ADM)**
- May exercise **all powers and duties of the Minister**, **except** the same exclusions as the Deputy Minister above.

> **Note**: This position has significant delegated authority, similar to the Deputy Minister, but at a lower level of the hierarchy.

#### **3. Director General – Commercial Operations Directorate**
- May exercise powers under:
  - Section 8 (general powers)
  - Paragraph 9(5)(e)
  - Subsections 10(1), 12(6), 22(2), 24(2), 32(2), 35.02(3), 37(2), 40(1)/(2), 43(1)

  - Subsections 95(4), 97.2(1), 99(4)

  - Section 147
  - Subsection 166(2)

> **Scope**: Focuses on commercial operations, customs procedures, and trade compliance.
