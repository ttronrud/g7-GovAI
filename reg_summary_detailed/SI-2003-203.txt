### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

**Title:**  
Transfer of Control and Supervision of Functions and Powers Under the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act

**Issuing Authority:**  
Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, pursuant to **paragraph 2(a) of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act**.

**Effective Date:**  
**December 12, 2003**

---

## **I. Transfer of Control and Supervision from the Department of Human Resources Development (HRD) to the Department of Human Resources and Skills Development (HRSD)**

The following units, branches, and functions of the **Department of Human Resources Development (HRD)** are transferred to the **Department of Human Resources and Skills Development (HRSD)**:

---

### **1. Human Investment Programs Branch**  
*All functions except:*  
- Those related to the **Social Development Partnership Program** (retained in HRD).

> *Rationale:* This transfer reflects a strategic realignment to better integrate social development and investment initiatives under a broader skills and social development framework.

---

### **2. Office for Disability Issues**  
*Entirely transferred*  
> *Rationale:* The Office’s focus on disability inclusion and accessibility aligns with HRSD’s broader mandate in skills development, employment equity, and inclusive economic participation.

---

### **3. Canada Student Loans Program (CSLP) Call Centres and Web-Service Functions**  
*All functions related to call centres and web services are transferred*  
> *Rationale:* This reflects a move toward centralized, modernized service delivery and improved access to student financial support services under HRSD’s oversight.

---

### **4. Employment Programs Branch**  
*All functions except:*  
- Those administering **employment programs for persons with disabilities** (retained in HRD).

> *Rationale:* This partial transfer allows HRSD to manage general employment initiatives while preserving disability-specific programming under HRD to ensure targeted support.

---

### **5. Insurance Programs Branch**  
*All functions except:*  
- Activities related to **streamlining and automation of services and benefits processing**, including:  
  - *Modernizing Services for Canadians*  
  - *Government on Line (GoL)* initiatives  

> *Rationale:* The automation and digital service improvements are retained in HRD to ensure continuity in service modernization efforts. This preserves HRD’s role in technological transformation within social insurance services.

---

### **6. Social Insurance Number (SIN) and Social Insurance Register Operations**  
*All functions transferred*  
> *Rationale:* The SIN and Register are critical to identity and benefit administration. Their transfer to HRSD strengthens integration with broader social development and employment systems.

---

### **7. Channel Management Activities**  
*All channel management related to call centres and web services*  
> *Rationale:* Ensures consistency in customer service delivery across programs and aligns with HRSD’s broader responsibility for service delivery and digital platforms.

---

### **8. Strategic Policy Branch**  
*All functions related to:*  
- **Labour market policy**  
- **Learning policy**  
> *Rationale:* These policy areas are central to workforce development and skills training—core components of HRSD’s mandate.

---

### **9. National Secretariat for Homelessness**  
*Entirely transferred*  
> *Rationale:* Homelessness policy and response are now integrated into HRSD’s social development portfolio, reflecting a broader focus on social inclusion and community well-being.

---

### **10. All In-Person Services Functions**  
*All in-person services related to HRD programs and services*  
> *Rationale:* Consolidates service delivery under HRSD to improve coordination, reduce duplication, and enhance client access to support services.

---

## **II. Transfer of Powers, Duties, and Functions from the Minister of Human Resources Development to the Minister of State for Human Resources and Skills Development**

The following powers, duties, and functions are transferred from the **Minister of Human Resources Development** to the **Minister of State for Human Resources and Skills Development**:

### 1. Powers and Duties Related to Employment Insurance Boards of Referees  
Under **Part VI of the Employment Insurance Act** (S.C. 1996, c. 23):  
- Authority over the **employment insurance boards of referees**  
- Powers conferred upon the Minister of Human Resources Development under the Employment Insurance Act and the Department of Human Resources Development Act (S.C. 1996, c. 11)

> *Rationale:* This transfer strengthens the role of the Minister of State in employment insurance dispute resolution, ensuring better coordination between employment services and insurance administration.

---

## **Key Implications and Rationale for the Transfer**

1. **Reorganization for Strategic Alignment:**  
   The transfer reflects a broader government strategy to consolidate and streamline social services under a more cohesive **human resources and skills development** framework.

2. **Improved Service Delivery:**  
   Centralizing in-person services, call centres, and digital platforms under HRSD enhances efficiency, consistency, and client experience.

3. **Focus on Skills, Employment, and Inclusion:**  
   The inclusion of labour market and learning policy, disability issues, and homelessness underscores a shift toward a more holistic approach to workforce and social development.

4. **Preservation of Key Exceptions:**  
   Certain programs (e.g., disability-specific employment, service modernization, and the Social Development Partnership Program) are retained in HRD to maintain targeted, specialized support.

5. **Enhanced Oversight of Employment Insurance:**  
   The transfer of referee board powers to the Minister of State ensures greater accountability and coordination in employment insurance disputes.

---

## **Conclusion**

This regulation represents a **major structural reorganization** within the Canadian public service. It redefines the responsibilities of two key departments—HRD and HRSD—by:

- Transferring core functions related to employment, skills, disability, and social inclusion to HRSD  
- Strengthening the role of the Minister of State in employment insurance administration  
- Aligning policy and service delivery with evolving social and economic needs  

The move supports a more integrated, responsive, and inclusive approach to human resources and social development in Canada.
