**Detailed Summary of the United Nations Peacekeeping Defence Ministerial Conference (Vancouver 2017) — Privileges and Immunities Order**

---

### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
This Order is issued under the authority of **section 5a of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*** (FMIOA), which empowers the Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs) to grant privileges and immunities to participants in international conferences or meetings, particularly those involving the United Nations or other international organizations.

The Order is based on the framework established in:
- **S.C. 2002, c. 12, sections 3 and 10** – provisions related to the application of privileges and immunities to international missions and conferences.
- **S.C. 1991, c. 41** – the original *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*, which provides the foundational legal structure for recognizing and protecting the rights of foreign missions and international bodies.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Order**
The primary purpose of this regulation is to **grant specific privileges and immunities** to participants in the **United Nations Peacekeeping Defence Ministerial Conference (Vancouver 2017)**, held on **November 14 and 15, 2017**, in Vancouver, British Columbia. These privileges and immunities are designed to ensure that delegates and officials can perform their duties effectively and without undue interference from Canadian authorities.

---

### **3. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Conference** | Refers specifically to the intergovernmental meeting known as the *United Nations Peacekeeping Defence Ministerial Conference*, held in Vancouver on November 14–15, 2017. |
| **Convention** | Refers to the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, as set out in **Schedule III** of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*. This is the international legal instrument that governs the rights and protections of UN officials. |
| **Official Delegate** | Any individual designated by a foreign state, foreign government, or international organization to attend the Conference. This includes Ministers of Defence, their representatives, Chiefs of Defence Staff, and their representatives. |
| **Relevant Period** | The time frame from **November 12, 2017, to November 17, 2017** — this includes the days before the Conference begins (to allow for travel and preparation) and extends beyond the official meeting dates (to cover the end of the mission and transition). |

---

### **4. Privileges and Immunities Granted**

#### **A. For Official Delegates of Foreign States or Foreign Governments**
During the relevant period, official delegates (including Defence Ministers, their representatives, and Chiefs of Defence Staff and their representatives) shall have in Canada:
- The **privileges and immunities** specified in **Sections 11, 12, 14, and 15 of Article IV of the Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations**.

These privileges typically include:
- **Immunity from legal process** (e.g., protection from being sued or arrested in civil or criminal matters related to their official duties).
- **Exemption from taxation** (in some cases, though this may be limited).
- **Freedom of movement** and **freedom from interference** with their official functions.
- **Exemption from search or seizure** of personal effects or official documents during the performance of duties.
- **Protection of official communications** and correspondence.

> These rights are granted **only to the extent required** for the exercise of their functions in relation to the Conference.

#### **B. For Official Delegates of International Organizations**
The Order provides differentiated treatment based on the rank and role of the official:

1. **Heads of International Organizations and Senior Officials**  
   - Granted privileges and immunities **comparable to those of diplomatic agents** under the *Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations (VCDR)*.  
   - This includes:
     - Immunity from criminal and civil jurisdiction.
     - Protection of official communications.
     - Exemption from certain laws (e.g., customs, entry/exit, taxation).
     - Freedom of movement and residence within Canada.

2. **Other Officials (non-heads, non-senior)**  
   - Granted the **privileges and immunities set out in Section 18 of Article IV of the Convention**.  
   - These include:
     - Immunity from legal proceedings arising from acts performed in the course of their official duties.
     - Protection of official documents and communications.
     - Exemption from certain administrative and procedural requirements.

> Again, these immunities are **limited to what is necessary** for the performance of their functions in connection with the Conference.

#### **C. For Experts Performing Missions for International Organizations**
Experts (such as technical advisors, analysts, or specialists) engaged by international organizations to support the Conference are granted:
- The **privileges and immunities set out in Article VI of the Convention**.

Article VI of the Convention typically includes:
- **Immunity from legal proceedings** for acts performed in the course of their official duties.
- **Exemption from customs duties** and **entry/exit restrictions**.
- **Freedom from interference** with their work and personal activities related to the mission.

> These rights are also **limited to the extent required** for the performance of their functions during the relevant period.

---

### **5. Scope and Limitations**
- The privileges and immunities are **temporary and time-bound**, applying only during the **relevant period (November 12–17, 2017)**.
- The rights are **not absolute** — they are granted **only to the extent necessary** for the performance of official functions in Canada.
- The Order does **not extend to all individuals** attending the Conference; only those who are officially designated as delegates or experts performing official missions.
- The Order **does not confer immunity** for personal misconduct unrelated to official duties or for acts committed outside the scope of the Conference.

---

### **6. Legal Effect and Entry into Force**
- The Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** with the Statutes of Canada (i.e., upon official publication in the Canada Gazette).
- It is a **temporary, specific legislative instrument** designed solely for the purpose of facilitating the smooth operation of the Vancouver 2017 Ministerial Conference.

---

### **7. Significance and Context**
This Order reflects Canada’s commitment to:
- Recognizing the importance of international cooperation in peacekeeping and defence matters.
- Respecting international norms and legal frameworks (particularly the UN Convention and the Vienna Convention) to ensure that high-level officials can engage in diplomatic and strategic discussions without fear of legal or administrative obstacles.
- Supporting multilateral dialogue on peacekeeping operations, defence cooperation, and the role of national militaries in international peace efforts.

It also demonstrates how Canada applies **existing international legal instruments** to specific, time-limited events — a common practice in the conduct of international diplomacy.

---

### **Conclusion**
This regulation is a **targeted, temporary, and legally grounded** measure that ensures the **effective functioning** of the United Nations Peacekeeping Defence Ministerial Conference in Canada. By granting specific privileges and immunities to official delegates and experts, it enables secure, uninterrupted, and respectful engagement among national defence ministers and international organization representatives. The Order is consistent with Canada’s obligations under international law and reflects a balanced approach to protecting diplomatic functions while maintaining national legal sovereignty.

--- 

**Note:** This Order is not a permanent statute. It expires upon the end of the relevant period and is not intended to establish long-term privileges for similar future conferences unless formally renewed or extended.
