## ðŸ”¹ **Core Obligations of Pipeline Companies**

### 1. **Membership in One-Call Centres**
- If a pipeline operates in a geographical area where a **one-call centre** exists:
  - The pipeline company **must be a member** of that centre.
- **Purpose of One-Call Centres**:
  - Receive locate requests from the public.
  - Notify members (including pipeline companies) of proposed construction or ground-disturbing activities.
  - Protect underground infrastructure and ensure public safety.

---

### 2. **Response to Requests for Written Consent**
- When a person requests written consent to:
  - Construct a facility across, on, along, or under a pipeline, or
  - Engage in an activity causing ground disturbance,
- The pipeline company must:
  - Respond **within 10 working days**.
  - **Inform the requester** whether consent is granted or refused.
  - If refused, **provide clear reasons** for the refusal.

> âœ… **Note**: Consent may be granted **subject to conditions** necessary to protect:
> - Public and employee safety
> - Environmental integrity
> - Pipeline safety and security

---

### 3. **Conditions and Amendments During Construction**
- At any time during construction or activity:
  - The pipeline company may **add or amend conditions** to ensure pipeline safety and security.
- These amendments must be justified and based on real-time risk assessment.

---

### 4. **Providing Information for Authorization Applications**
- If a person intends to apply for **Regulator authorization** and requests information from the pipeline company:
  - The pipeline company must provide:
    - All necessary information to prepare the application.
    - All **reasonable assistance** required.
  - This must happen **within 10 working days** of receiving the request.

---

### 5. **Locate Requests and Marking of Pipelines**
When a person requests to locate a pipeline:
- The pipeline company must:
  - **Within 3 working days** (or longer period agreed upon):
    - Provide written **safety practices** for working near the pipeline.
    - **Mark the location** of pipes at **maximum 10-meter intervals** along each pipe.
    - Ensure markings are **clearly visible and distinct** from other markings.
    - Provide **clear explanation** of the significance of the markings.

### 6. **Notification of High-Risk Agricultural Zones**
Even if a location is covered by the general "no-ground-disturbance" rule:
- If **agricultural operations** (e.g., plowing, farming) could impair pipeline safety:
  - The pipeline company must:
    - **Identify specific locations** where such activities pose a risk.
    - **Notify in writing**:
      - Landowners of those locations.
      - Farmers or service providers who operate or lease land there.

### 7. **Inspections During Ground Disturbance Activities**
Pipeline companies must:
- Conduct **mandatory inspections** during any activity causing ground disturbance within the **prescribed area**.
- Inspect **all exposed pipes** before backfilling to ensure:
  - No damage occurred.
  - The pipe remains intact and secure.

> - Any unusual events that may affect pipeline safety or security

### 9. **Suspension of Consent**
A pipeline company may **suspend consent** if:
- The contractor fails to comply with:
  - Technical details or conditions in the consent (e.g., from CERA Regulations)
  - Instructions provided (e.g., safety procedures)
- Work practices are found to **threaten pipeline safety or security**

### 10. **Reporting Obligations to the Regulator**
Pipeline companies must **report immediately** to the Regulator on:
- Every **contravention** of the damage prevention regulations.
- Any **damage to the pipeline** resulting from:
  - Construction
  - Maintenance or removal of facilities
  - Ground disturbance activities
  - Operation of vehicles or mobile equipment across the pipeline
- Any activity that the company believes **could impair pipeline safety or security**

> ðŸ“ **Report must include**:
> - Details of the contravention or damage (cause, nature)
> - Any concerns about pipeline safety or security
> - Any intended actions or requests (e.g., investigation, repair)
