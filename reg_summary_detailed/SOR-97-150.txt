### **1. Regulatory Basis and Authority**
- These regulations were made under **subsection 7(1)b of the National Parks Act**.
- Issued by the **Governor General in Council**, based on a recommendation from the **Minister of Canadian Heritage**.
- The regulations are codified in **S.C. 1995, c. 11, s. 26** and **S.C. 1996, c. 10, s. 246**.

---

### **2. Scope and Application**
- Applies to **all national parks** in Canada.
- **Subject to sections 40 and 41 of the National Parks Act**, the regulations also extend to **park reserves** as if they were parks.
- **Exemptions**:
  - **Non-motorized aircraft** (e.g., sailplanes, gliders) that **cannot transport persons** are excluded.
  - **Non-motorized paragliders and hang-gliders** are explicitly excluded.

### **4. Prohibited Aircraft Operations**
#### **General Prohibition**
- It is **prohibited** for any person to conduct a take-off or landing in a national park **unless**:
  - The park is listed in **Column I of the Schedule** (specific park names), and
  - The landing occurs at a **take-off and landing location** specified in **Column II** of the Schedule.

#### **Prohibited in Specific Parks (Items 1 to 9, 11, 12)**
- No take-off or landing is allowed in any park listed in **items 1 to 9, 11, or 12** of Column I **unless**:
  - The person holds a valid **aircraft access permit**.

#### **Special Prohibitions**
- **Item 13 (Park Name Not Specified)**:
  - Landing is only permitted for **non-commercial recreational purposes** if the person holds a permit.
  - **Exception**: Landing is allowed in an emergency (diversion or other emergency).

- **Item 14 (Park Name Not Specified)**:
  - Landing is **only allowed in emergency situations** (diversion or other emergency).
  - No recreational or commercial take-offs or landings permitted.

### **5. Mandatory Reporting and Authorization Requirements**
In the case of a landing under **paragraph (3)(b)** or **subsection (4)** (emergency/diversion):
- The person must:
  1. **Notify the Superintendent** as soon as feasible of:
     - The landing location (item 13 or 14, Column II)
     - The nature of the diversion or emergency
  2. **Obtain the Superintendentâ€™s authorization before take-off**.

> This ensures accountability, safety, and coordination with park management.

---

### **6. Permitting Process**
#### **Who Can Apply?**
- Any person may apply for an aircraft access permit under **subsection (4)**.

#### **Application Requirements (Form and Content)**
The application must include:
- Name, address, and phone number of the **applicant**
- Name, address, and phone number of the **aircraft owner**
- Name, address, and phone number of a **non-applicant emergency contact**
- Make, model, and **registration number** of the aircraft
- Proposed **dates or periods** for take-off/landing
- Proposed **locations** for take-off/landing

#### **Commercial Operators**
- Must submit a **copy of their operating licence** under:
  - Section 4.1 or subsection 10.1(2) of the *National Parks of Canada Businesses Regulations*

---

### **7. Superintendentâ€™s Considerations Before Issuing a Permit**
Before granting a permit, the Superintendent must consider:
- The **natural and cultural resources** of the park
- The **safety, health, and enjoyment** of visitors and residents
- The **preservation, control, and management** of the park

> This ensures that aircraft access does not compromise ecological or public values.

---

### **8. Contents of the Issued Permit**
The permit must specify:
- Name, address, and phone of the **permit holder**
- Name, address, and phone of the **aircraft owner**
- Name, address, and phone of an **emergency contact (non-permit holder)**
- Aircraft make, model, and registration number
- Authorized **dates or periods** for take-off/landing
- Authorized **locations** for take-off/landing
- Any **additional conditions** necessary for park preservation and management

---

### **9. Suspension, Revocation, and Refusal of Permits**
- **Suspension**: A permit may be suspended if the holder fails to comply with the regulations.
- **Reinstatement**: A suspended permit may be reinstated if the violation is corrected.
- **Refusal to Issue**: A person convicted of a contravention of these regulations or the *Aeronautics Act* may be denied a permit for a period **not exceeding one year**.

> This provides enforcement and accountability mechanisms.

---

### **10. Authorized Exceptions to Prohibitions**
The Superintendent may authorize take-off or landing at any location in a park for the following **authorized purposes**:
- **Natural or cultural resource management and protection**
- **Other management or control functions** (e.g., monitoring, research, patrols)
- **Public safety**
- **Law enforcement**

> These exceptions allow critical operational needs to proceed without requiring a permit, provided they serve park administration objectives.
