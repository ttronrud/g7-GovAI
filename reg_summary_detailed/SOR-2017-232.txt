**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

---

### **Regulation Title:**  
*29th Meeting of the Parties to the Montreal Protocol and Associated Meetings – Privileges and Immunities Order*

---

### **Legal Basis and Authority**

This regulation is issued under the authority of **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the **recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs**, pursuant to specific provisions in the **Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act**:

- **Paragraphs 5(1)(c)a and (f) to (h)b** of the Act.

These provisions empower the Government to grant privileges and immunities to representatives of foreign states and international organizations participating in specific meetings, ensuring they can carry out their duties without undue interference or obstruction.

The regulation is published in **Statutes Canada (S.C. 2002, c. 12, s. 3(2) and s. 3(3))**, and is referenced under **S.C. 1991, c. 41** — the original version of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act.

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**

The primary purpose of this Order is to **grant privileges and immunities** to participants in a series of key international meetings related to **ozone layer protection**, specifically under the **Montreal Protocol** and the **Vienna Convention for the Protection of the Ozone Layer**. These privileges and immunities are essential to ensure that delegates, officials, and experts can conduct their work efficiently, securely, and without fear of legal or administrative interference.

---

### **Privileges and Immunities Granted**

The regulation grants specific privileges and immunities during the relevant period, tailored to different categories of participants:

#### **1. Representatives of Foreign States**
- **Privileges and immunities** are granted under **Sections 11, 12, 14, and 15 of Article IV** of the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*.
- These provisions typically include:
  - **Immunity from legal process** (e.g., no arrest or prosecution for acts done in the course of official duties),
  - **Exemption from taxation**,
  - **Immunity from search and seizure**,
  - **Freedom of movement and communication**,
  - **Protection of official communications**.

> This ensures that foreign delegates can participate in discussions and negotiations without fear of legal repercussions.

---

#### **2. Heads of International Organizations**
- Heads (e.g., directors, presidents, CEOs) and their **alternates and senior officials** of international organizations (such as the United Nations Environment Programme or the World Meteorological Organization) are granted privileges and immunities **comparable to those of diplomatic agents** under the *Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations*.
- This includes:
  - Immunity from arrest or prosecution,
  - Exemption from local laws and regulations,
  - Protection of official correspondence and travel,
  - Immunity from civil and criminal liability for official acts.

> This recognition treats these leaders as "diplomatic agents" in the context of these meetings, even if they are not from a sovereign state.

---

#### **3. Other Officials of International Organizations**
- All other officials (non-heads, mid-level staff) are granted privileges and immunities as set out in **Section 18 of Article V of the Convention**.
- These typically include:
  - Immunity from civil and criminal liability for acts performed in the course of their duties,
  - Protection of personal and official documents,
  - Exemption from certain forms of legal jurisdiction.

> These protections allow staff to operate effectively without fear of personal liability.

---

#### **4. Experts Performing Missions for International Organizations**
- Experts (e.g., scientists, technical advisors) who are assigned to perform missions for international organizations are granted privileges and immunities under **Article VI of the Convention**.
- These include:
  - Immunity from civil and criminal liability for acts performed in the course of their expert duties,
  - Protection of their personal and professional information,
  - Freedom to travel and communicate without restriction.

> This ensures that technical and scientific experts can provide objective, unbiased advice without legal risk.

---

### **Scope and Duration**

- The privileges and immunities apply **only during the relevant period**: **November 16 to November 26, 2017**.
- The Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** in the Canada Gazette (i.e., upon official publication).
- The protections are **limited in scope** to the specific meetings listed and are not extended to other events or periods.

---

### **Significance and Context**

This Order is part of Canada’s commitment to international environmental cooperation. By granting diplomatic-level privileges and immunities, Canada:

- Facilitates **effective international dialogue** on climate and environmental protection,
- Supports the **implementation of global environmental agreements** (Montreal Protocol and Vienna Convention),
- Ensures **technical and diplomatic participation** is free from legal or bureaucratic barriers,
- Demonstrates Canada’s role as a responsible and cooperative member of the international community.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation is a targeted, legally sound instrument that enables smooth and secure participation in critical international environmental meetings. It provides clear, structured protections for foreign delegates, international organization officials, and experts, ensuring that the work of these meetings — focused on protecting the ozone layer — can proceed without interference. The provisions are consistent with international norms and the framework established by the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*, reflecting Canada’s adherence to global diplomatic and environmental standards.

--- 

**In short:**  
This Order grants diplomatic privileges and immunities to participants in key ozone layer-related meetings in Montreal in 2017, ensuring that delegates, officials, and experts can perform their duties freely and without legal risk during the relevant period.
