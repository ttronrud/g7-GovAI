### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Supply of Publications by a Non-Resident Registrant**

#### **1. Regulatory Background and Authority**
- **Issuing Body**: His Excellency the Governor General in Council.
- **Recommendation**: Made on the recommendation of the **Minister of National Revenue**.
- **Legal Basis**: 
  - Paragraph **143(2)(a)** and **subsection 277(1)** of the **Excise Tax Act**.
- **Purpose**: To establish rules governing the **supply of certain publications by non-resident registrants** in Canada, particularly in relation to **taxation and compliance requirements** under the Excise Tax Act.

> This regulation applies specifically to **non-resident entities or individuals** (i.e., those not physically located in Canada) that supply printed or audio-based publications into Canada.

---

#### **2. Regulatory Text and Key Definitions**

##### **Definition of "Act"**
- "**Act**" refers to the *Excise Tax Act*, which governs excise taxes, including the Goods and Services Tax (GST) and Harmonized Sales Tax (HST).

##### **Definition of "Registration Number"**
- A **registration number** is the unique identifier assigned to a person under **Section 241 of the Excise Tax Act**.
- This number is required for compliance with tax reporting and supply tracking.

---

#### **3. What Constitutes "Tangible Personal Property" Under Section 143.1 of the Act?**

The regulation prescribes which types of publications are considered **tangible personal property** for the purposes of **section 143.1** of the Excise Tax Act. This classification triggers GST/HST tax obligations.

**Included (Subject to Tax):**
- A **book**, **newspaper**, **periodical**, **magazine**, or any similar **printed publication**.
- **Excluded**: Any publication listed in **Section 1 of Schedule VII** to the Excise Tax Act (e.g., certain government or exempt publications).

**Additional Inclusion:**
- An **audio recording** that:
  - Is related to a publication included in the above category (e.g., a CD or audio cassette accompanying a magazine),
  - And is **submitted together with** the publication to **Canada Post Corporation** or a **customs officer**.

> âš ï¸ Note: The audio recording is only included if it is **physically attached** to the publication during shipment.

#### **4. Compliance Requirements When a Supply is Deemed to Have Been Made in Canada**

If a **non-resident person** supplies a publication (as defined above) and such supply is **deemed to have occurred in Canada** under **section 143.1** of the Excise Tax Act, the supplier must provide the **registration number** in one of the following ways:

| Method | Conditions |
|-------|-----------|
| **In the masthead** of the publication | If the masthead is on the first five pages. If not, it must appear on one of the first five pages. |
| **On the back cover** | Only if the address of the person appears on that cover. |
| **On the mailing label** | Attached to the publication when sent via Canada Post or courier. |
| **On the packaging** | On the outer packaging of the publication. |
| **On a separate document** | Accompanying the publication when sent to Canada Post or a customs officer. |

> âœ… **All of these methods are acceptable**, and the supplier must ensure **one of them is used**.

> â— **Critical Exception**:  
> If the person **does not yet have a registration number** at the time of mailing or courier delivery, they must provide **evidence** on a separate accompanying document (e.g., a letter or form) that they have **applied for a registration number**.
