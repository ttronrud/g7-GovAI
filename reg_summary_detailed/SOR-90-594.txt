- The **Veterans Health Care Regulations** were introduced to replace the outdated *Veterans Care Regulations* (approved by Order in Council P.C. 1984-2971 of August 31, 1984).
- The revocation and substitution were approved by the **Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Veterans Affairs** and the **Treasury Board**, under **subsection 5(1) of the Department of Veterans Affairs Act**.
- The new regulations came into force on **September 1, 1990**, marking a significant modernization of veterans‚Äô health care access and eligibility criteria.

> This regulatory update reflects evolving needs in veteran health care, particularly in relation to aging veterans, chronic illness, and improved access to community-based services.

### 1. **Core Health Care Service Categories (Types of Health Needs)**

These classifications determine the level of care a veteran or eligible individual may receive:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Type I Health Need** | Personal care and supervision required due to decreased physical or mental faculties, but the individual remains ambulant or independently mobile. <br> ‚Üí Service provided in a **residential care facility**. |
| **Type II Health Need** | Requires personal care under health professional supervision; the person has a functional disability, has reached recovery limits, and needs minimal diagnostic or therapeutic services. <br> ‚Üí Service provided in **intermediate care**. |
| **Type III Health Need** | Requires ongoing personal care, diagnostic, nursing, and therapeutic services from health professionals. Applies to chronically ill individuals or those with stable but severe functional disabilities. <br> ‚Üí Service provided in **chronic care**. |

> These types form the foundation for determining which health care setting (residential, intermediate, or chronic) is appropriate for a client.

### 2. **Who Qualifies as a ‚ÄúClient‚Äù?**

A broad definition of ‚Äúclient‚Äù includes various categories of individuals who are eligible for health care under the regulations:

#### A. **Veterans**
- Those who served during:
  - World War I or II (active service in naval, army, or air forces of Canada or Newfoundland forces)
  - In a theatre of actual war (as defined in the War Veterans Allowance Act)
  - As Canadian merchant mariners in WWI or WWII (excluding merchant navy veterans under specific exclusions)
- Service in Korea as part of the special force
- Special operators or supervisors deemed veterans under specific Acts
- Allied veterans as defined in the War Veterans Allowance Act
- Individuals previously approved for:
  - Adult residential care
  - Veterans independence program services
  - Chronic care in community facilities

#### B. **Civilian Eligible Persons**
- **Civilian pensioners**: Entitled to pensions under:
  - Parts I‚ÄìIII or VI‚ÄìX of the *Civilian War-related Benefits Act*
  - *Civilian Government Employees (War) Compensation Order*
- **Income-qualified civilians**:
  - Receive allowances under the Civilian War-related Benefits Act
  - Or would qualify if not receiving Old Age Security (OAS) or similar foreign benefits
- **Income-qualified overseas service civilians**:
  - Overseas service civilians who meet income qualification criteria
- **Newfoundland Special Award Pensioners**:
  - Recognized for disability incurred during WWI or WWII, despite not qualifying under the Canada-Newfoundland union terms

#### C. **Other Eligible Groups**
- **Military service pensioners**:
  - Entitled to disability pensions from the Pension Act, excluding:
    - Active service in WWI or WWII
    - Service in theatres of operations
    - Special duty service
- **Flying accident pensioners**:
  - Compensation from *Flying Accidents Compensation Regulations*
- **Red Cross pensioners**:
  - Entitled to pensions under the 1952 Memorandum of Agreement between the Crown and the Canadian Red Cross
- **Special duty service pensioners**:
  - Pension awarded for disabilities incurred during special duty service (as defined in the Pension Act)
- **Prisoners of war**:
  - Entitled to basic compensation under subsection 71.2(1) of the Pension Act

#### D. **Eligibility for Compensation**
- **Entitled to a disability award**: Includes individuals who:
  - Have received a disability award under the *Veterans Well-being Act*
  - Would have received one but failed to meet a condition before April 1, 2019 (unless a pain and suffering compensation decision has been made)
  - Would have received one but for a pre-2019 condition in section 53 of the Veterans Well-being Act
- **Entitled to pain and suffering compensation**:
  - Has received compensation under sections 45(1) or 47(1)
  - Is referred to in section 45(1) but disability assessment not yet complete
  - Would have received compensation but for section 56.4(1) of the Veterans Well-being Act

> These provisions ensure that individuals who were previously denied benefits due to procedural or threshold issues can still access care based on their actual medical condition.

### 4. **Service Delivery and Facilities**

- **Community Facilities** are the primary settings for care delivery.
- Services are categorized by **type of health need** (I, II, III), which directly determines:
  - Level of supervision
  - Required health professional involvement
  - Duration and intensity of care
- The Minister retains authority to approve or disapprove facilities.
- **Contract beds** are reserved for specific veterans, ensuring targeted access to care based on need and eligibility.

### 6. **Exclusions and Clarifications**

- **Pension does not include**:
  - Compassionate awards under section 34 of the *Veterans Review and Appeal Board Act*
  - Pensions awarded under subsection 22(2) of the *Pension Act*
- **Health** is defined as a state of **physical, mental, and social well-being**, not merely the absence of disease.
- **Health professionals** include physicians, dentists, nurses, and other practitioners approved by the Minister.

---

### 7. **Purpose and Scope of the Regulations**

The primary objectives of the Veterans Health Care Regulations are:

1. **To provide equitable and accessible health care** to eligible veterans and civilians based on medical need.
2. **To classify care needs** into three levels (Type I, II, III) to match appropriate care settings.
3. **To ensure continuity of care** for individuals with long-term disabilities or chronic conditions.
