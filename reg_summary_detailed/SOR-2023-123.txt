**Detailed Summary of the Vaping Products Reporting Regulations**

---

### **Overview**

The *Vaping Products Reporting Regulations* are a regulatory framework established under **Section 7.8a of the Tobacco and Vaping Products Act (TVP Act)**, as amended by *S.C. 2018, c. 9, s. 12* and *S.C. 1997, c. 13*. These regulations mandate **transparency and accountability** in the production, sale, and distribution of vaping products in Canada. They require manufacturers to report detailed information about their vaping productsâ€”including ingredients, sales volumes, and geographic distributionâ€”to the **Minister of Mental Health and Addictions** (in consultation with the Associate Minister of Health).

These regulations are designed to:
- Improve public health oversight of vaping products,
- Enable effective monitoring of product safety and composition,
- Support policy development related to youth access, nicotine exposure, and market regulation,
- Ensure compliance with federal excise duties and product labeling standards.

---

## **Key Definitions**

The regulations define key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Tobacco and Vaping Products Act*. |
| **Assortment** | A package containing two or more types of vaping products, or two or more brands of the same type with different characteristics (e.g., flavours, nicotine levels). |
| **Civic Address** | Full address details (unit, number, street, municipality, province/state, postal code/country) for addresses in or outside Canada. |
| **GTIN** | Global Trade Item Number assigned by GS1 to identify a brand or assortment. |
| **Manufacturer** | Excludes entities that only package, label, or distribute products on behalf of a manufacturer. |
| **Net Sales** | Total sales value minus returns during a specified period. |
| **Net Volume** | Total volume of vaping substance sold minus returns. |
| **Unique Product Identifier (UPI)** | Either the GTIN or an alphanumeric code (â‰¤10 characters) for brands without GTIN. |
| **Vaping Device** | Devices that generate aerosol (e.g., e-cigarettes), as defined in Section 2 of the TVP Act. |
| **Vaping Part** | Components such as tanks, cartridges, pods, or batteries. |
| **Vaping Substance** | The liquid or aerosol contained in devices (e.g., e-liquids). |

---

## **Scope of Application**

### **Products Covered**
The regulations apply to all vaping products intended for **retail sale**, including:

1. **Vaping parts** that:
   - Contain or are intended to contain a source of energy (e.g., batteries),
   - Are reservoirs (e.g., tanks, cartridges) with aerosol-generating components,
   - Contain microprocessors.

2. **Vaping devices** that:
   - Do not contain a vaping substance,
   - Contain a vaping substance.

3. **Vaping substances** (e-liquids).

4. **Assortments** of the above products intended for retail sale.

> âš ï¸ **Exclusions**:
> - Products covered by a **Food and Drugs Act authorization** (e.g., licenses for specific products),
> - Assortments that include a vaping product already authorized under the Food and Drugs Act.

- Required for **each brand of vaping product sold in Canada**, including those in assortments.

#### **Information Required for Each Brand**

| Item | Details |
|------|--------|
