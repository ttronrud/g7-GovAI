**Detailed Summary of the *Northern Mineral Exploration Assistance Regulations***

---

### **Overview**

The *Northern Mineral Exploration Assistance Regulations* (NMER) establish a framework for the Canadian government to provide financial assistance—specifically, grants—to individuals or corporations conducting exploratory work in northern Canada. The primary objective is to promote economic development in northern regions by supporting the discovery and evaluation of mineral and hydrocarbon resources while ensuring accountability, transparency, and public interest.

These regulations are administered by the **Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development**, with day-to-day oversight by the **Director, Northern Policy and Program Planning Branch**.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Applicant** | A person or group applying for a grant to fund exploratory work. |
| **Application** | A formal request for a grant, submitted under oath and including detailed plans. |
| **Canadian securities dealer** | A firm licensed to trade shares in Canada, used as a criterion for public corporation eligibility. |
| **Director** | Head of the Northern Policy and Program Planning Branch. |
| **Exploratory work** | Includes surface surveys, geological/geophysical/geochemical studies, drilling (e.g., diamond drill holes or exploratory test wells), and associated infrastructure (roads, airstrips). |
| **Gas** | Natural gas only. |
| **Grant** | Financial assistance authorized by the Minister, capped at 40% of eligible expenditures. |
| **Holdings** | A broad category of rights including mineral claims, leases, permits, and prospecting rights under various federal and territorial laws. |
| **Minerals** | Metals, ores, coal, and other naturally occurring substances capable of mining—excluding oil, gas, and quarry products. |
| **Oil** | Crude petroleum and hydrocarbons from oil sands, bitumen, oil shale, etc., but not gas. |
| **Program expenditure** | All costs directly related to exploratory activities. |
| **Recipient** | An approved applicant who receives the grant. |
| **Year** | A 12-month period ending March 31. |

---

## **Eligibility for Grants**

### **1. Individual Applicants**
- Must be:
  - A Canadian citizen aged 18 or over.
  - Entitled to enter and conduct exploratory work on the holdings in question (via beneficial ownership, lease, or option agreement).

### **2. Corporate Applicants**
Only certain Canadian corporations qualify:

#### **(a) Private Corporations**
- Shares must be beneficially owned:
  - By Canadian citizens,
  - Or by one or more corporations meeting the public corporation criteria (see below),
  - Or a mix of both, such that **at least 50% of voting power** is held by Canadian citizens or qualifying public corporations.

#### **(b) Public Corporations**
- Must have:
  - Common shares listed on a Canadian stock exchange, OR
  - Shares offered publicly through a Canadian securities dealer.

#### **(c) Special Case – Corporations under Income Tax Act Subparagraphs 66(15)(h)(i) and (ii)**
- These are typically large, foreign-owned or non-Canadian entities.
- May qualify **only if**:
  - They commit to **substantially all** their exploratory expenditures being made in northern Canada.
  - Their ownership structure does **not exceed 15% voting power** from other qualifying corporations.
  - They meet the private/public ownership thresholds (b(i), (ii), or (iii)).

> **Key Requirement**: The corporation must demonstrate **beneficial ownership or priority to become the beneficial owner** of the holdings in question.

### **3. Groups of Persons**
- Must:
  - Form a written agreement approved by the Minister.
  - Appoint a single agent (member or third party) to receive communications and grant payments.
  - Agree to be **jointly and severally liable** for all obligations under the regulations.

---

## **Application Process**

An applicant must submit a detailed application under oath, including:

1. **Description of areas and holdings** involved.
2. **Estimates of program expenditure**:
   - By class (e.g., labor, equipment, travel).
   - With justification for each category.
3. **If work is staged**:
   - Estimated timelines for each stage.
   - Detailed breakdown of work per stage.
4. **Three copies** of preliminary reports, maps, photos, and data supporting the proposal.
5. **Additional documents/undertakings** as required by the Minister.

> **Completion of Application**: Occurs when:
> - All required information is submitted, and
> - The application meets the eligibility requirements.

---

## **Minister’s Approval and Grant Authorization**

Upon receiving a complete application, the Minister may:

- **Approve the application** if it contributes to the economic development of northern Canada.
- **Determine the total program expenditure**.
- **Set a completion deadline** for the exploratory work.
- **Authorize a grant** equal to **up to 40% of the lesser of**:
  - The Minister’s determined program expenditure, and  
  - The portion of that expenditure borne by the recipient.

> **Flexibility**: Before the full grant is paid, the Minister may **vary** the amount, date, or expenditure limits based on new information provided by the recipient.

---

## **Mandatory Drilling Requirements**

A grant is only authorized **if** the recipient demonstrates:

- For **oil/gas**: Drilling of an **exploratory test well**.
- For **minerals**: Drilling of **diamond drill holes or equivalent** in a number deemed "satisfactory" by the Director.

> **Waiver Clause**: If the Minister determines, based on results, that drilling is **not warranted**, they may **waive** the drilling requirement.

---

## **Reporting and Expenditure Verification**

### **Post-Completion Reporting (Within 90 days)**

The recipient must submit a **statement of program expenditure** under oath, including:

- Description of areas and holdings.
- Details of exploratory work conducted.
- Full breakdown of expenditures and their purposes.
- Three copies of reports, maps, photos, and other supporting materials.
- Any additional information required by the Director.

### **Interim Statements**

- If successive reports would exceed 3 months apart, the recipient may submit an **interim statement** at least **3 months after the last report**.

### **Director’s Approval of Expenditure Statement**

The Director will:
- Review the statement and any supporting data.
- Approve it **only if**:
  - It accurately reflects the work and expenditures.
  - It aligns with the approved application.
- Then determine the **amount of grant payable** for that period.

> **Note**: The Director may approve a statement **even if the recipient is not the beneficial owner of all holdings**, **only if**:
> - The Minister is satisfied the recipient is entitled, **in priority**, to become the beneficial owner of any such holdings.

---

## **Discontinuance of Work**

- No grant payment is made after discontinuance **unless** the Minister **consents in writing**.
- The Minister may approve discontinuance if further exploration is **not warranted**.

---

## **Obligations of Recipients (Mandatory Undertakings)**

Recipients must agree to the following conditions:

1. **Payment of Owing Amounts**  
   - Must repay the **total grant amount** (plus interest) to Her Majesty in Right of Canada **if**:
     - A breach of any regulation occurs, or
     - A false or misleading statement is made in the application or related documents.

2. **Public Disclosure**  
   - Authorize the Minister to **publish** information disclosed to the Director **after 2 years** from the completion date of the program.

3. **Restrictions on Transfer of Holdings**  
   - Cannot assign, transfer, mortgage, or abandon holdings **without prior written consent** from the Minister (or after notice is given).

4. **No Implied Findings**  
   - Cannot represent or imply that government approval or grant payment **indicates** the presence of oil, gas, or minerals.

5. **Repayment of Grants (Section 12)**  
   - Repay **the full grant amount plus 10% simple interest per annum** if a breach occurs.

6. **Employment of Local Residents**  
   - Must employ as many **local residents** as possible in the area of exploration, considering availability and qualifications.

---

## **When Repayment Becomes Due**

- **Upon commencement of production for gain** on any of the holdings in the application.
- The Director determines:
  - Whether production has started.
  - The date it began.

> **Interest on Repayment**:
- Simple interest at **2% + the average interest rate** on long-term Government of Canada bonds from the **prior calendar year**.

> **Payment Schedule**:
- Recipient must pay **at least 10% of the outstanding amount or the balance**, whichever is lesser, **on each anniversary** of when repayment became due.
- The balance is paid in full thereafter.

---

## **Alternative Payment Method – Issuance of Shares**

For **public corporations** (shares listed on a Canadian stock exchange or offered via a Canadian securities dealer):

- The recipient may, **with Minister’s consent**, pay the outstanding amount by issuing **fully paid common shares** to Her Majesty in Right of Canada.

### **Valuation of Shares**:
- **Listed shares**: Closing market price on the day of agreement on the principal exchange.
- **Unlisted shares**: Closing market price in a daily newspaper in the city where trading occurs.
