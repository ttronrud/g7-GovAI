**Detailed Summary of the Annual Statement (Trust and Loan Companies) Regulations**

---

**Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis:**

The *Annual Statement (Trust and Loan Companies) Regulations* are made under the authority of the **Trust and Loan Companies Act** (TLC Act), specifically pursuant to:

- **Subsection 313(2)a** of the TLC Act, which empowers the Governor in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance) to prescribe the format and content of annual financial statements required by trust and loan companies.
- **Subsection 531(1)b** of the TLC Act, which provides further authority to establish the requirements for the submission of financial reports by these institutions.

These provisions are designed to ensure transparency, accountability, and consistency in the financial reporting of trust and loan companies, thereby protecting investors and maintaining public confidence in the financial system.

---

**Regulatory Objective:**

The primary objective of these regulations is to **standardize and prescribe the mandatory financial statements** that trust and loan companies must prepare and file annually. This ensures uniformity in reporting across all such companies, facilitating effective oversight by regulators, investors, and the public.

---

**Prescribed Financial Statements:**

The regulations require trust and loan companies to prepare and submit the following five core financial statements for each financial year:

1. **Balance Sheet as at the End of the Financial Year**  
   - A statement that presents the company’s financial position at a specific point in time (the end of the fiscal year).  
   - It lists all assets, liabilities, and shareholders’ equity.  
   - Provides a snapshot of what the company owns (assets), what it owes (liabilities), and the net worth (equity) of the company.

2. **Statement of Income for the Financial Year**  
   - Reports the company’s revenues, expenses, and net income (or loss) over the course of the fiscal year.  
   - Includes key line items such as interest income, non-interest income, operating expenses, and other gains or losses.  
   - Enables stakeholders to assess profitability and operational performance.

3. **Statement of Cash Flows for the Financial Year**  
   - Details the inflows and outflows of cash during the year, categorized into operating, investing, and financing activities.  
   - Helps assess the company’s liquidity, financial flexibility, and ability to generate cash from its core operations.  
   - Provides insight into the company’s cash management practices beyond profit and loss.

4. **Statement of Changes in Shareholders’ Equity for the Financial Year**  
   - Tracks the changes in shareholders’ equity over the year, including contributions, dividends, retained earnings, and any adjustments.  
   - Clarifies how equity has evolved due to business performance, capital injections, or distributions to shareholders.

5. **Statement of Comprehensive Income for the Financial Year**  
   - Extends the income statement to include all gains and losses that are not included in net income, such as foreign exchange gains, unrealized gains or losses on investments, and other comprehensive income (OCI) items.  
   - Provides a more complete picture of the company’s total financial performance, including non-operating and non-cash items.

**Legal References and Historical Context:**

- The regulations are referenced in:
  - **S.C. 2005, c. 54, s. 434** – Section 434 of the *Statutes of Canada 2005*, which formally enacts the regulations.
  - **S.C. 2005, c. 54, s. 449** – Section 449, which may provide additional procedural or administrative details.
  - **S.C. 1991, c. 45** – A foundational reference to earlier financial reporting requirements, indicating continuity and evolution of financial disclosure standards in the sector.

These provisions reflect a long-standing regulatory framework aimed at ensuring financial transparency in the trust and loan company sector.

---

**Effective Date:**

The regulations come into force on **June 1, 2011**.  
This date marks the beginning of their application to all trust and loan companies operating in Canada under the TLC Act.  
Companies were required to comply with the prescribed format and content of the financial statements starting from their annual financial year ending on or after June 1, 2011.

---

**Key Implications:**

- **Enhanced Accountability:** Mandates regular, standardized reporting to improve transparency and accountability to regulators, shareholders, and the public.
- **Regulatory Oversight:** Enables the Financial Services Regulatory Authority (or relevant regulator) to monitor financial health, detect potential risks, and ensure compliance with financial standards.
- **Investor Confidence:** Provides investors with reliable, comparable, and consistent financial information to make informed decisions.
- **Compliance Requirement:** Failure to submit the prescribed statements in the required format or within the prescribed timeframe may result in penalties or enforcement actions.

---

**Conclusion:**

The *Annual Statement (Trust and Loan Companies) Regulations* establish a comprehensive and standardized framework for financial reporting by trust and loan companies in Canada. By requiring the preparation and submission of five core financial statements—balance sheet, income statement, cash flow statement, equity changes, and comprehensive income—the regulations promote financial transparency, strengthen regulatory oversight, and support market confidence. Enacted under the authority of the Trust and Loan Companies Act and effective from June 1, 2011, these regulations represent a critical component of Canada’s financial regulatory infrastructure for non-bank financial institutions.
