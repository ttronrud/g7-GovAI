### **4. Representations of the Design**

#### **Mandatory Requirements**
- A design representation must:
  1. **Fully disclose** the design, considering the name of the finished article and any statement under sections 17 or 18.
  2. Be in one of the following forms:
     - Photographs
     - Graphic reproductions
     - Any other form **approved by the Minister or Commissioner**
  3. Be of **sufficient quality** to allow clear and accurate identification of design features.
  4. Include **at least one image showing the design or finished article in isolation**.

#### **Acceptable Formats**
- Must be presented in the **form specified** by CIPO (e.g., high-resolution, properly scaled, unaltered).

#### **Exclusions and Limitations**
- **Dotted/broken lines**: Features shown in dotted or broken lines are **not considered part of the design**, unless a statement explicitly includes them.
- **Blurring or colouring**: If used to indicate the feature is **not part of the design**, it is not considered part of the design.
- **No utilitarian descriptions**: Any statement must **not describe**:
  - Utilitarian function
  - Manufacturing method or construction principle

> ğŸ“ Example: A photo showing a chair with a unique armrest shape is acceptable. A description saying "the armrest improves ergonomics" is **not allowed**.

### **5. Scope of the Application**

#### **General Rule**
- An application must relate to **only one design** applied to:
  - A **single finished article**, or
  - A **set of variants** applied to a **single finished article or set**.

#### **Limitations on Scope**
- The application **relates only to features** that:
  - Appeal to and are judged **solely by the eye** (i.e., aesthetic features).
- If the applicant **clearly states** the application relates only to **some features** (e.g., only the handle), then it only covers those.
- Features shown in **dotted lines or blurred areas** are **not included**, unless otherwise stated.

> ğŸš« A design application cannot claim protection for functional elements (e.g., how a product works).

### **6. Divisional Applications**

#### **When Can a Divisional Application Be Filed?**
A divisional application may be filed **only if**:
- The design in question was **disclosed in the original application** on:
  - The **filing date** (for non-divisional original applications), or
  - The **date the original application was received** (for divisional original applications), and
  - In the **earliest original application in the series**, on its **filing date**.

> âœ… This ensures that no new design is introduced after the original filing.

#### **Mandatory Statement**
- The application **must contain a statement** identifying the corresponding original application.
- This statement must be submitted **within three months** of the date the Minister receives the divisional application.

#### **Separate Application Status**
- A divisional application is a **separate application**:
  - With its own **filing date**
  - With its own **fee structure**
  - With its own **examination and registration process**

#### **Filing Deadline**
- Must be filed **within two years** of:
  - The filing date of the original application (if not a divisional application), or
  - The filing date of the **earliest original application** in the series (if the original was a divisional application)

#### **Exception: Re-filing After Objection**
- If:
  1. The Minister sends a **refusal report** under subsection 22(2) stating the original application fails to meet the requirements;
  2. The applicant **amends the original application** to remove the design in question;
  3. The divisional application is filed **within six months of the amendment**,
- Then the divisional application is **not subject to the two-year rule**.

> âš ï¸ This exception applies **only to non-Hague applications** and **does not apply to Hague applications or divisionals from them**.
