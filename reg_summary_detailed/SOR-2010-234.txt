## ğŸ“Œ Disclosure Requirements for Adjustable Policies (Section 2(1) and 464.1(2))

### Definition of "Adjustable Policy"
An **adjustable policy** is a **non-participating individual life insurance policy** issued by a life company that allows the insurer to **unilaterally modify** the following at its sole discretion:

- Premium or insurance charge  
- Amount of insurance coverage  
- Surrender value  

**Exclusions** from this definition:
- Policies where premium, coverage, or surrender values are **fixed in the policy or schedule**.
- **Group insurance policies**, including creditor group policies.
- **Annuity contracts** (including deferred annuities or savings plans where the insurer guarantees an annuity).
- **Reinsurance policies**.

### Mandatory Notice for Policy Changes
If a company makes a change to an adjustable policy **within the previous 12 months**, it must:

- Send a **notice to the policyholder** no later than the **end of the 30-day period after the policy anniversary** that follows the 12-month period.
- The notice must contain a **brief summary** of the change (e.g., increase in premium, change in coverage, or modification to surrender value).

> âœ… Purpose: Protects policyholders from unexpected changes and ensures they are informed of material modifications to their policy terms.
