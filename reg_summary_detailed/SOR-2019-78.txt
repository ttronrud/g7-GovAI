### **2. Key Definitions**

#### **(a) "Act"**
- Refers to the *Excise Act, 2001*, the primary legislation governing excise taxes.

#### **(b) "Base Amount"**
The base amount is the foundation for calculating the excise duty on a cannabis product and varies depending on:
- **How the product is obtained** (by purchase vs. otherwise)
- **Which specified province** the product is delivered to or produced in.

##### **Case 1: Delivered or Made Available Without Purchase**
- If a person obtains cannabis **not through a purchase** (e.g., gifted, given, or received as a result of a non-commercial transaction), the **fair market value** of the product is the base amount.

#### **(d) Exclusions from "Cannabis Product"**
Certain items are **excluded** from being classified as cannabis products for duty purposes:
- **Test kits** (as defined in the Cannabis Regulations) that have been **issued a registration number but not cancelled**.
- **Reference standards** (highly purified, standardized substances used in labs or research) that:
  - Contain known quantities of cannabis chemicals (e.g., THC, CBD)
  - Are used for **analytical, medical, industrial, educational, law enforcement, or research** purposes
  - Are **not intended for human consumption**

> These exclusions ensure that lab tools and scientific reference materials are not taxed as cannabis products.

### **3. Dutiable Amount Determination**

#### **(a) For Products Delivered or Made Available Without Purchase**
- The **dutiable amount** equals the **fair market value** of the cannabis product.
- This applies when the product is given as a gift, received as a donation, or otherwise not acquired via a formal sale or purchase.

#### **(b) For Products Acquired Through Purchase**
- The dutiable amount is calculated using the **base amount formula** above.
