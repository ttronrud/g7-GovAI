## üîç **Regulation Summary: Accounting for Imported Goods and Payment of Duties**

### üìå **Overview**
This regulation establishes the procedures, requirements, and exceptions for **accounting for imported goods** and **paying duties** under the *Customs Act* (S.C. 1986, c. 1). It was introduced following a public consultation period, where a draft version was published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on March 29, 1986, allowing stakeholders to submit written representations to the Minister of National Revenue.

The regulation came into force upon the coming into effect of specific sections of the *Customs Act*, including sections 32, 33, 35, 88, 89, 92, and paragraphs 164(1)(d), (i), and (j), as well as section 166.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important Note**: This regulation has been **repealed or amended over time** (e.g., SOR/2001-197, SOR/2005-210, SOR/2006-152, SOR/2011-208, SOR/2018-121, SOR/2024-41). Many key terms and provisions have been removed or replaced. The current version reflects a **historical snapshot** of customs accounting rules from the late 1980s to early 2020s, and should not be interpreted as current law without checking the most recent version.

## üìù Core Requirements for Accounting & Duty Payment

### 1. **Mandatory Electronic Accounting (General Rule)**

> **Applicable to**: All persons required by subsections 32(1), (3), or (5) of the *Customs Act* to account for goods.

- Accounting **must be done electronically** using the **Electronic Commerce Client Requirements Document (ECCRD)**.
- The ECCRD sets out the **technical specifications, formats, and procedures** for electronic data interchange (EDI).
- This ensures standardized, secure, and efficient data transmission between importers and customs authorities.

> ‚úÖ **Exception**: The Minister may allow alternative methods (non-electronic) if:
- The electronic infrastructure is **inadequate or incompatible**;
- A **natural disaster, national crisis, or exceptional circumstance** prevents reliable use of electronic systems;
- It is **impracticable** for the importer due to circumstances beyond their control.

---

### 2. **Interim Accounting (for goods released under subsection 32(2)(a))**

- Must be submitted **in writing** at the customs office where goods were released.
- **Alternative options**:
  - **Orally** via telephone or other telecommunication at a customs office designated by the Minister (only for persons with special authorization under the *Presentation of Persons (2003) Regulations*).
  - **Electronically**, in accordance with ECCRD.

> üîç This provision allows flexibility for small businesses or those without immediate access to electronic systems.

---

### 3. **CSA Importers and Electronic Accounting**

- All CSA importers must account for goods released under subsection 32(2) **electronically**, per ECCRD requirements.

> ‚ùå **Exception**: This rule **does not apply** to goods released under paragraph 32(2)(b) (e.g., certain exempt or temporary releases).

---

### 4. **Documents Required at Time of Accounting**

Every person accounting for goods must provide, **before release**, any required certificates, licenses, permits, or information under:
- The *Customs Act*;
- Other federal or provincial legislation;
- Regulations made under those laws.

These documents are necessary to verify compliance with import restrictions or regulations.

## üì¶ Specific Requirements by Type of Goods

### A. **Casual Goods** (Personal, non-commercial imports)

- Must provide:
  - A **commercial invoice**, **price list**, **bill of sale**, or similar document that:
    - Describes the goods;
    - Includes sufficient detail to determine **tariff classification** and **duty value**.

- **Conditional Release Prior to Duty Payment**:
  - Goods may be released **before full duty payment** if the importer provides:
    1. A **credit card remittance** (where the importer is cardholder or authorized user), and the card issuer has a formal agreement with the Government of Canada;
    2. A **traveler‚Äôs cheque**, **money order**, or **certified cheque**.

- **Duty Payment Deadline**:
  - If released conditionally, the person must pay duties **within five days** of release.

---

### B. **Commercial Goods**

- Must provide:
  - **If estimated duty value exceeds $3,300**:
    - A **completed customs invoice**, OR
    - A commercial invoice with **same information as a completed customs invoice**, OR
    - A commercial invoice **plus a partially completed customs invoice** (together containing full data).
  - **If estimated duty value ‚â§ $3,300**:
    - A commercial invoice, price list, bill of sale, or similar document that:
      - Describes the goods;
      - States the number of units;
      - Enables officers to determine tariff classification and duty value.

> ‚ùå **Exception**: Does **not apply** to CSA importers for goods released under paragraph 32(2)(b).
