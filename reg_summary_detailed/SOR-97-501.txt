**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Title:**  
*Privileges and Immunities of the Secretariat of the Convention on Biological Diversity Order*  
**Statutory Authority:**  
Pursuant to paragraphs 5(1)(a), (b), (c), (f), (g), (h) and subsections 5(2)a and (3) of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act* (FMIOA), and on the recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs and the Minister of Finance.  
**Citation:**  
S.C. 1995, c. 5, par. 25(1)(m); S.C. 1991, c. 41  

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**

This Order grants legal privileges and immunities to the **Secretariat of the Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD)** and its associated personnel, as well as representatives of foreign states that are Parties to the CBD, in Canada. It establishes the legal framework under which these international bodies and individuals can operate in Canada without undue interference from Canadian authorities, consistent with international norms and obligations under the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations* (the "Convention").

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Agreement**  
   Refers to the *Headquarters Agreement* between the Government of Canada and the Secretariat of the Convention on Biological Diversity, signed in New York on October 25, 1996, and in force since that date. This agreement outlines the specific terms under which Canada will host and support the Secretariat’s operations in Canada.

2. **Convention**  
   Refers to the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, as set out in *Schedule III* of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*. This is a foundational international treaty that establishes the legal privileges and immunities granted to diplomatic missions and international organizations.

3. **Secretariat**  
   The Secretariat established under **Article 24** of the *Convention on Biological Diversity*, adopted at the Rio Earth Summit on June 5, 1992, and entered into force on December 29, 1993. The Secretariat is responsible for implementing the CBD, including organizing meetings, managing technical programs, and supporting the implementation of biodiversity conservation goals.

---

### **Privileges and Immunities Granted**

#### 1. **The Secretariat (as a Legal Entity)**  
- The Secretariat shall have **the legal capacity of a body corporate** in Canada. This means it can enter into contracts, own property, sue and be sued, and otherwise function as a legal entity within Canadian law.  
- It shall also enjoy the **privileges and immunities** specified in **Articles II and III of the Convention**, to the extent provided in **Articles 3 to 6 of the Agreement**.  
  - These privileges include immunity from legal process, protection of communications, and freedom from interference in its operations.  
  - The scope is limited by the terms of the Headquarters Agreement, which defines the specific areas in which these immunities apply.

#### 2. **Representatives of Foreign Parties to the CBD**  
- Representatives of foreign states that are Parties to the CBD shall have in Canada the **privileges and immunities** set out in **Article IV of the Convention**, to the extent specified in **Article 10 of the Agreement**.  
  - These include immunity from arrest and prosecution, protection of personal and official communications, and freedom from interference in their official duties.  
  - The Agreement limits these privileges to the scope of their participation in the CBD.

#### 3. **Executive Secretary of the Secretariat**  
- The Executive Secretary (the chief administrative officer of the Secretariat) shall have the **same privileges and immunities as diplomatic agents** under the *Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations*.  
- These privileges are defined in **Schedule I** of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*.  
  - Examples include:  
    - Immunity from arrest or prosecution in civil or criminal matters related to official duties.  
    - Immunity from search or seizure of official documents.  
    - Freedom from interference in official communications.  
  - This treatment is granted under **paragraph 2 of Article 11 of the Agreement**, which specifically recognizes the Executive Secretary’s diplomatic status.

#### 4. **Officials of the Secretariat**  
- All other officials of the Secretariat (non-Executive Secretary staff) shall have the **privileges and immunities** set out in **Section 18 of Article V of the Convention**.  
  - These include:  
    - Immunity from legal proceedings in respect of acts performed in the course of official duties.  
    - Protection of official communications.  
    - Immunity from search or seizure of personal or official property.  
  - These privileges are subject to the terms of **Article 11 of the Agreement**, which defines the scope and conditions under which they apply.

#### 5. **Experts Performing Missions for the Secretariat**  
- Experts (such as scientific, technical, or advisory personnel) engaged to perform missions on behalf of the Secretariat shall have the **privileges and immunities** set out in **Article VI of the Convention**, to the extent specified in **Article 12 of the Agreement**.  
  - These include:  
    - Immunity from legal process for acts performed in the course of their official duties.  
    - Protection of their personal and official communications.  
  - These privileges are limited to the scope of their expert engagement under the Agreement.

---

### **Legal Basis and Authority**

- The Order is issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the advice of the Ministers of Foreign Affairs and Finance.  
- It is authorized under specific provisions of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*, particularly those related to recognizing the legal status and immunities of international organizations and their personnel.  
- The provisions are consistent with international law, especially the *Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations* and the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, which serve as the legal foundation for the privileges granted.

---

### **Effective Date**

- The Order comes into force on **November 20, 1997**.  
- This date marks the official start of the application of the privileges and immunities in Canada for the Secretariat and its personnel.

---

### **Significance and Implications**

- This regulation enables Canada to host and support the **Secretariat of the Convention on Biological Diversity** effectively, allowing it to carry out its mandate—promoting biodiversity conservation, sustainable use of biological resources, and fair benefit-sharing—without legal or administrative obstacles.  
- It ensures that international experts, officials, and representatives can operate freely and safely in Canada, which is essential for global environmental cooperation.  
- The framework balances international obligations with Canadian sovereignty, ensuring that privileges are not unlimited and are strictly limited to official duties and as defined in the Agreement and international law.  
- It reflects Canada’s commitment to multilateral environmental governance and its role in global biodiversity protection.

---

### **Conclusion**

This Order is a critical piece of Canadian legislation that legally recognizes the status of the **CBD Secretariat** and its personnel in Canada. By granting appropriate privileges and immunities—consistent with international standards—it enables the Secretariat to function efficiently and independently, thereby supporting global efforts to protect biodiversity. The Order is both a practical and diplomatic instrument, reinforcing Canada’s engagement in international environmental governance.
