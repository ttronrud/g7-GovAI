## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Tobacco Reporting Regulations**

### üîç **Background and Legal Authority**

The Tobacco Reporting Regulations were enacted under the authority of the **Tobacco and Vaping Products Act (Act)**, specifically **Sections 7 and 33**, and were approved by the **House of Commons** on June 8, 2000, following a recommendation by the Minister of Health and a report from the Standing Committee on Health. The regulations were officially made by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council on May 12, 2000 (S.C. 1997, c. 13).

> **Purpose**: To ensure transparency and accountability in the manufacturing, marketing, and distribution of tobacco products by requiring manufacturers to provide detailed, verifiable, and standardized information to the Government of Canada.

## üìù Core Reporting Requirements

All manufacturers of consumer tobacco products must submit specific reports to the Minister of Health. These reports are required to be:

- **Accurate**
- **Complete**
- **Submitted in legible electronic format**
- **Backed by verifiable test data**

---

### ‚úÖ General Reporting Requirements (Applicable to All Reports)

Every report must include the following **mandatory information**:

1. **Name and contact details** of the manufacturer:
   - Full name
   - Telephone number
   - Email address
   - Civic address of principal place of business in Canada

2. **Name and contact details** of the individual who prepared the report:
   - Name
   - Phone and email
   - If employed by a third party, name and contact details of that entity

3. **Date of submission**

4. **Period covered** by the report (e.g., January 1 ‚Äì December 31)

5. **Section of the Regulations under which the report is made**

6. **Attestation Statement**:
   - The individual must attach a sworn statement affirming that all information is **true, complete**, and provided in **good faith**.

---

### üîÅ Brand Name Consistency and Changes

- Manufacturers must use a **unique brand name** for each consumer tobacco product in all reports.
- The brand name must be used **uniformly** across all reports for the same product.
- If a manufacturer changes the brand name, they must submit a new report that:
  - Identifies the **previous brand name**
  - Identifies the **new brand name**

> This ensures traceability and avoids confusion in product tracking.

---

### üìö Exemptions

- **No reporting is required** for consumer tobacco products **manufactured solely for export**.

---

## üß™ Data Sources and Laboratory Requirements

All data used in reports must come from **tests performed on actual tobacco products** sampled during the reporting period.

> üî¨ **Testing Lab Requirements**:
- Must be:
  - Accredited by the **Standards Council of Canada (SCC)**, or
  - Accredited by another body that is a signatory to the **ILAC Mutual Recognition Arrangement (ILAC MRA)**,
- And must meet the **most recent version of ISO/IEC 17025**, which sets standards for testing and calibration laboratories.

> ‚úÖ **Lab must provide**:
- A document proving accreditation for the **specific test methods** required by these regulations.

> üìé For reports under **Section 12, 14, or 14.2**, the manufacturer must attach:
1. A **laboratory report** specifying:
   - Type of tobacco product tested
   - Brand name
   - Data used
2. A **document proving lab accreditation** at the time of testing

## üìé Specific Report Types and Content

---

### 1. **Profile Report (on Manufacturer)**

**When to Submit**: On or before the day the manufacturer begins selling tobacco products.

**If the product was sold before the regulation came into force**:
- Must be submitted by the **180th day after the regulation comes into force**.

**Content**:
- List of **every type of tobacco product** sold
- For **non-consumer tobacco products** (e.g., cigarette papers, filters), list of brand names used
- Name and civic address of **each warehouse** in Canada used for storage

**Digital Images (for each brand of consumer tobacco product)**:
- Must show:
  - All sides of the **package** when closed
  - Any **interior promotions**
  - Any **overwrap** with promotions (used as packaging or carton)
  - Any **brand elements** on the product unit

**Image Requirements**:
- Taken on a **white background**
- Must be **clear**, with **accurate colours**
- Must display **legible text**
- Must include the **brand name**
- Must include a **graphical scale in millimetres**

> üîÅ **Changes to appearance**:
- If a package, promotion, overwrap, or brand element is changed:
  - Must be reported **within 30 days** of first sale using the changed item
  - Must include:
    - Previous digital image
    - Description of change
    - New digital image

> üöÄ **New brand launch**:
- A manufacturer must submit digital images **on or before the day** they first sell a new brand.

---

### 2. **Report on Manufacturing Processes**

**Applies to**: All consumer tobacco products and components (e.g., cigarette paper, filters, mouthpieces, tips)

**Content (by brand)**:
- Detailed description and **schematic of sequential manufacturing steps**
- Conditions and parameters (e.g., temperature, humidity, time) for each step
- **Processing aids** (e.g., emulsifiers, preservatives):
  - Common name
  - Chemical name
  - Brand name
  - CAS registry number (if applicable)

**Specific Requirements for Cigarettes, Kreteks, Little Cigars**:
- Type of **cigarette paper** used, with specifications
- Type of **filter** used, with:
  - Specifications
  - Pressure drop
  - **Filter efficiency with respect to nicotine** (determined via **Official Method T-106**, dated Dec 31, 2017)
- An **engineering drawing** of the product
