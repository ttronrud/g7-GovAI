### **Detailed Summary of the Chesterfield Inlet Airport Zoning Regulations**

#### **1. Legal Basis and Adoption Process**

These regulations are established under the **Aeronautics Act**, specifically **Section 5.4** and **Section 5.5**, which govern the process for adopting zoning regulations at Canadian airports.

- A **proposed version** of the zoning regulations was first published in two successive issues of the *Canada Gazette, Part I* on **August 19 and 26, 1989**, and later in two issues of the local newspaper *News/North* on **January 2 and 8, 1990**.
- This publication provided **a reasonable opportunity for interested parties** (e.g., residents, developers, aviation stakeholders) to submit formal representations or objections to the Minister of Transport.
- Based on this public consultation, the **Governor in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the Minister of Transport, formally adopted the regulations.

> âœ… **Key Outcome**: The regulations are now legally binding and apply to the Chesterfield Inlet Airport area in the Northwest Territories.

- These regulations apply to **all lands adjacent to or in the vicinity of the airport**, including public road allowances.

- The boundary of the outer limits is defined as a **circle with a radius of 4,000 meters**, centered on the **Airport Reference Point (ARP)**.

#### **5. Prohibited Activities**

No person may:

- Erect or construct any **building, structure, or object**;
- Add any **extension or modification** to an existing structure;

**on any land covered by these regulations**, if the **highest point** of such construction would exceed:

1. The **approach surface**;
2. The **outer surface**;
3. The **transitional surface**.

#### **6. Purpose and Safety Rationale**

These zoning rules are designed to:

- **Ensure aviation safety** by maintaining clear flight paths and approach corridors.
- Prevent **visual obstructions** that could compromise aircraft navigation.
- Maintain **minimum clearances** for aircraft during critical phases of flight (takeoff, landing, and approach).
- Provide a **consistent, measurable, and enforceable framework** for land use near the airport.

The design of the approach surfaces, transitional surfaces, and outer surface follows international aviation standards (similar to those used in ICAO and FAA guidelines), adapted to the remote and often rugged terrain of the Northwest Territories.

#### **9. Enforcement and Compliance**

- The regulations are **enforceable by the Minister of Transport**.
- Any construction or development within the designated area must comply with these height limits.
- Violations could result in **fines, project halts, or legal action**.
- Local authorities or developers must consult with Transport Canada before initiating any construction.

---

#### **10. Summary in Plain Language**

These regulations establish strict **height limits** for all buildings and structures near Chesterfield Inlet Airport. The goal is to keep the airspace clear so that aircraft can safely take off and land, especially in challenging weather or visibility conditions.

- **No building can rise higher than**:
  - A sloped surface (1:30) extending 2.5 km from each end of the runway;
  - A flat surface 45 meters above the airport reference point (or 9 meters above ground if lower);
  - A sloped transition zone (1:7) from the runway edges to the outer surface.
- The entire area within a **4-kilometer radius** of the airport centerline is regulated.
- The rules are based on a 1988 zoning plan and were made public for public comment before being adopted.
