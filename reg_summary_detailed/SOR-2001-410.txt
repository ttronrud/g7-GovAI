---

### **2. Purpose of the Regulations**

The primary purpose of these regulations is to **establish clear conditions under which an insurance company or an insurance holding company may use its name** in various financial documents related to securities transactions. This includes:

- Prospectuses  
- Offering memorandums  
- Takeover bid circulars  
- Advertisements  
- Any other document associated with a securities-related transaction  

The rules are designed to prevent misleading or unauthorized use of company names, which could mislead investors or compromise the integrity of financial disclosures.

A person (such as an underwriter, broker, or promoter) may use the name of:

- An **insurance company**, or  
- An **insurance holding company**

in connection with a securities-related transaction **only if one of the following two conditions is met**:

#### **Condition 1: Required by Law**
The use of the name is **mandated by law**. This means that if a legal requirement (such as a statutory obligation under securities laws or regulatory rules) necessitates the inclusion of the companyâ€™s name in a document, such use is permitted.

#### **Condition 2: Written Permission from the Company**
The insurance company or insurance holding company must have **given written consent** for the use of its name in the document. This consent must be explicitly granted and documented in writing.

### **4. Scope of Application**

The regulations apply to all documents used in connection with **securities-related transactions**, including but not limited to:

- Initial public offerings (IPOs)  
- Private placements  
- Mergers and acquisitions  
- Takeover bids  
- Shareholder communications

### **6. Repeal Note**

The regulations are **not retroactive** and are only in force from their effective date. There is no indication of a repeal or replacement in the current text provided. However, the note in the regulation states:

> [Repeal]  
> These Regulations come into force on the day on which section 1026 of the Insurance Companies Act comes into force.

This phrasing suggests that these regulations are **temporary or transitional** and are intended to operate only until the relevant statutory provisions are fully implemented. In practice, such regulations often serve as a bridge to more comprehensive reforms.

---

### **7. Key Implications and Significance**

- **Investor Protection:** Prevents misleading or deceptive use of corporate names, which could create confusion about ownership or responsibility.
- **Transparency and Accountability:** Requires clear documentation (written permission) to ensure that all name usage is legitimate and properly authorized.
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Provides a clear, enforceable standard for financial institutions and intermediaries to follow when communicating with investors.
- **Alignment with Broader Financial Regulation:** Part of a larger effort by the Canadian government to harmonize securities and insurance regulations under the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC).

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Name Use in Securities-related Transactions (Insurance Companies and Insurance Holding Companies) Regulations* establish a strict and transparent framework for when an insurance company or its holding company may use its name in financial disclosures or marketing materials. The use is permitted only if:

1. It is legally required, or  
2. It has been explicitly authorized in writing by the company.

These rules were enacted on **October 24, 2001**, and are designed to uphold investor confidence, ensure regulatory compliance, and prevent misuse of corporate identities in financial transactions.

They represent a key step in aligning insurance and securities regulation in Canada to promote fairness, transparency, and accountability in the marketplace.
