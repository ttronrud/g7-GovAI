**Detailed Summary of the Supreme Court of Newfoundland, Trial Division Rules for Orders in the Nature of Certiorari, Habeas Corpus, Mandamus and Prohibition (S.C. 1994, c. 44, s. 35)**  
*Effective Date: June 1, 2000*  
*Issued in Saint John’s, Newfoundland, April 18, 2000*

---

### **1. Purpose and Scope of the Rules**
These rules establish the procedural framework governing applications for **orders in the nature of certiorari, habeas corpus, mandamus, and prohibition** in the **Supreme Court of Newfoundland, Trial Division**. They are enacted under **subsection 482(1)a of the Criminal Code**, which authorizes the Court to make such rules to ensure effective and fair access to judicial remedies.

The rules apply specifically to **applications made to the Court, to a judge in chambers, or during trial**, and govern how these extraordinary remedies are initiated, served, heard, and executed.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**
- **Application**: Any originating or interlocutory request made to the Court, a judge in chambers, or during a trial.
- **Code**: Refers to the *Criminal Code* of Newfoundland.
- **Court**: The Supreme Court of Newfoundland, Trial Division.
- **Judge**: A judge of the Trial Division.
- **Registrar**: The Registrar of the Court and their lawful deputy.

> *Note: The interpretation sections of the Criminal Code apply to these rules, ensuring consistency with statutory interpretation principles.*

---

### **3. General Procedural Principles**
- **Default Applicable Rules**: Where no specific provision is made in these rules, the **Criminal Appeal Rules** of the Trial Division apply, with necessary modifications.
- **Flexibility in Timeframes**: The Court may, in the **interests of justice**, extend or abridge any time period required by these rules or any Court order. This includes extending deadlines even if the application for extension is filed *after* the deadline has expired.
- **Consent Extensions**: Periods for serving, filing, or amending pleadings or documents may be extended by **written consent of the parties**.

---

### **4. General Requirements for Applications**
- **Notice Requirements**: 
  - A notice of application and all supporting documentation must be served at least **seven clear days before the hearing date** on every person who is **interested or likely to be affected** by the proceedings.
  - The Court may, in the interests of justice, **require notice to be served on additional persons** not previously identified.
- **Service in Quashing Cases**:
  - If the application seeks to **quash a conviction, order, or warrant**, notice must also be served on:
    - The **Attorney General of Newfoundland**;
    - The **Provincial Court Judge or justice** who issued the conviction, order, or warrant.
- **Right to Participate Without Service**:
  - A person not served with notice may apply to the Court to show they are affected and may be allowed to participate in the proceedings as if they had been served.
- **Application to Bring a Party Before the Court**:
  - A party confined or otherwise affected may apply for permission to be brought before the Court.
  - Such an application must be supported by an **affidavit** detailing the facts.
  - The judge may, by **written order**, grant this request.

---

### **5. Time Limits for Applications**
- **Certiorari Applications**:
  - Must be filed and served within **six months** of the conviction, acquittal, order, or warrant to which they relate.
  - This rule is subject to exceptions under Rule 10 (see below).

---

### **6. Service of Notice and Return of Records – Certiorari Process**
- **Form A Notice**:
  - The notice of application for certiorari must be **endorsed on the application** and addressed to the appropriate **Provincial Court Judge, justice, officer, or clerk**.
- **Return of Record**:
  - Upon receiving the notice (endorsed in accordance with Rule 9), the Provincial Court official must **immediately return** the full record to the Registry of the Supreme Court, including:
    - The conviction, acquittal, or order;
    - The information, evidence, exhibits, and any notice served on them.
  - The return must be accompanied by a **certificate** in the form specified in **Form B of the Schedule**.
- **Effect of Certificate**:
  - The certificate has the **same legal effect as a return to a writ of certiorari**.
- **Transmission by Officer**:
  - If the record is transmitted to the correct officer, the officer shall **certify the transmission and date** on the notice, rather than issuing a certificate.
- **Failure to Receive Record**:
  - If the record is not received by the designated office, the officer or clerk must return a **certificate to the Provincial Court official** stating that the record was not received.
- **Flexibility**:
  - The Court may, in the interests of justice, **dispense with the return of evidence or exhibits** or parts of them.

> *Note: A copy of these rules must appear on or be attached to the application served on the Provincial Court official.*

---

### **7. Ex Parte Quashing of Convictions**
- **Exception to Time Limit**:
  - The Court may **quash a conviction, order, or warrant ex parte** upon receiving an application from the Crown, **without requiring prior notice or waiting for the six-month period**.
  - This provides a mechanism for urgent correction of judicial errors when the Crown acts on its own initiative.

---

### **8. Mandamus – Requirements and Effect**
- **Oath Requirement**:
  - No mandamus order may be granted unless an **affidavit is produced** stating that the applicant is acting **on behalf of the person named in the application**.
- **Immunity from Prosecution**:
  - No action or prosecution may be brought against any person for anything done in **good faith in accordance with a mandamus order** issued by the Court or a judge.
- **Nature of the Order**:
  - The order may compel performance of a duty **immediately or after a fixed time**.
  - It may include **specific terms** and conditions.
  - Mandamus is not available for mere requests; it requires a clear duty owed by a public body or official.

---

### **9. Habeas Corpus – Procedure and Service**
- **Application to a Judge**:
  - An application for habeas corpus may be made directly to a judge and may be **joined with other applications** (e.g., certiorari or mandamus).
- **Form and Issuance**:
  - The order may be issued in **Form C of the Schedule**, or with **variations not affecting substance**, based on the nature of the case and the interests of justice.
  - The order may be granted **ex parte** (without notice).
- **Service Requirements**:
  - In addition to serving the application (as per Rule 6), the order must also be served on:
    - The **person in custody** of the applicant;
    - The **Attorney General of Newfoundland**.
- **Post-Order Procedures**:
  - After hearing and issuing the order, the judge may:
    1. **Dispose of the matter as soon as possible**, either in the presence of the applicant or with their consent in absence;
    2. **Direct that an order be immediately drawn up for the prisoner’s discharge**, which serves as a **sufficient warrant** for any gaoler, constable, or officer to release the prisoner.

---

### **10. Sample Forms and Orders (Summary)**

#### **Form A – Notice to Provincial Court Official**
- Requires return of full record (conviction, information, evidence, exhibits) to the Supreme Court Registry.
- Must be endorsed on the application and addressed to the relevant Provincial Court official.

#### **Form B – Certificate of Return**
- Signed by the Provincial Court official certifying that all records have been returned and are fully described.

#### **Form C – Habeas Corpus Order**
- Template for issuing a release order, including:
  - Name of applicant;
  - Name of custodian;
  - Date and time of hearing;
  - Order for discharge;
  - Certificate of documents in custody.

#### **Order to Bring a Person Before the Court**
- Example:
  > "You are hereby ordered to bring A.B. before the presiding judge at the Court House on [date] at [time], so that such judge may cause to be done with A.B. what is right and just according to law."

  > "You shall deliver immediately to the Registry true copies of all orders, warrants, committals, or documents pursuant to which A.B. is confined, together with your certificate confirming the documents listed."

---

### **11. Key Principles and Judicial Discretion**
- The Court retains broad **discretion** to:
  - Extend deadlines;
  - Allow participation of non-served parties;
  - Waive service of certain documents;
  - Grant ex parte relief where necessary.
- The rules are designed to **balance procedural fairness** with the **efficiency of justice**, especially in urgent or complex cases.

---

### **12. Effective Date**
- These rules came into force on **June 1, 2000**.
