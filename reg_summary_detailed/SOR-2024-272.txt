### âš–ï¸ **3. Regulatory Requirements for Applications**

Under **Section 298(2)** of the CERA, every application for authorization must demonstrate that the proposed work will:
- Be conducted safely and securely;  
- Protect property and the environment;  
- Comply with all applicable laws on safety, security, and environmental protection;  
- Take into account the **impacts of climate change**.

#### Required Information and Documents in an Application:
1. **Authorized Representative**  
   - Name and contact details of the operatorâ€™s designated representative.

2. **Development Concept**  
   - Overall project design, including:  
     - Plans for overlapping or concurrent authorizations  
     - Phased development (staged work)  
     - Expansion or modification over time

3. **Description of Work & Activities**  
   - Objectives, scope, and maps showing preferred and alternative operations sites.

4. **Support Craft & Equipment Use**  
   - Description of vessels, aircraft, vehicles, and associated systems (e.g., cranes, vessels for installation).

5. **Execution Plan & Schedule**  
   - Detailed timeline for activities, including:  
     - Phasing of work  
     - Need for overlapping authorizations  
     - Timeline for implementation

6. **Survey & Monitoring Results**  
   - Data from prior surveys or monitoring programs (including past projects), including:  
     - Geophysical surveys  
     - Environmental assessments  
     - Marine life studies  
     - Ice or seabed conditions  

7. **Use of Data in Planning**  
   - How previous data was used to inform current planning decisions.

8. **Compliance with Laws**  
   - Clear description of how safety, security, and environmental laws will be followed.

9. **Impact on Other Users**  
   - How the project may affect other users of the operations site (e.g., fishing, shipping, marine activities).

10. **Permits & Authorizations**  
   - List of all required permits (e.g., from fisheries, marine protection, local governments).

11. **Target Levels of Safety, Security & Environmental Protection**  
   - Specific, measurable goals for safety and environmental performance, aligned with Section 30 of the Act.

12. **Proposed Changes from Previous Applications**  
   - If changes are made, a detailed rationale and assessment of their impact must be provided.

### ðŸ” **4. Mandatory Conditions of Authorization**

Every authorization is subject to **four core conditions** under Section 298(9):

#### (a) **Management System**  
Must meet the requirements in **Section 8** of the Act and include:

- A **comprehensive framework** for reducing risks to safety, security, and the environment.
- Must be:
  - **Proportional** to the scope, nature, and complexity of the project and its hazards.
  - **Comprehensive**, covering all authorized activities.
  - **Explicit, proactive, and culture-driven** (fostering a "safety culture").
- Must identify the **standards** it is based on (e.g., ISO 45001, ISO 14001).
- Must include policies and processes for:
  - **Hazard identification** (including human and organizational factors)
  - **Risk assessment and mitigation**
  - **Inventory and ongoing updates** of hazards and controls
  - **Internal and external communication** of safety and environmental risks
  - **Coordination with suppliers, contractors, and service providers**
  - **Auditing and evaluation** of performance against measurable goals
  - **Inspection, testing, and maintenance** of facilities and systems
  - **Continuous improvement** through:
    - Internal reporting of hazards/incidents
    - Root cause analysis
    - Trend analysis
    - Corrective and preventive actions
    - Feedback loops and sharing of lessons learned
  - **Change management** (evaluation of changes that could affect safety/environment)
  - **Public engagement** (receiving and responding to public concerns)
  - **Documentation and recordkeeping** (approved, reviewed, updated, and accessible)
  - **Compliance monitoring** (to prevent non-compliance with authorization conditions or laws)

> This system is the **central governance mechanism** for managing safety, security, and environmental performance.

---

#### (b) **Safety Plan**  
Must meet requirements in **Section 9** and include:

- Integration with the management system and alignment with regulatory obligations.
- Summary of:
  - How safety hazards were identified (including nearby activities)
  - How risks were assessed
- Description of:
  - Identified hazards and risk assessments
  - Measures to **anticipate, monitor, avoid, and minimize** safety risks (especially ice/icing hazards)
  - Evaluation of effectiveness of these measures in meeting target safety levels
- Communication methods for informing affected persons
- Details of **critical safety facilities and equipment** (e.g., lifeboats, emergency power) and their inspection/maintenance protocols
- Full description of **all support craft and vessels** used
- Training requirements for personnel (experience, qualifications, competencies)
- Safe diving operations (with standards appropriate to depth and type)
- Compliance and performance monitoring (data collection, audits, inspections)
- Organizational structure and chain of command for safety
- Change management processes (for safety-related changes)

> If the operator uses **third-party service providers**, the safety plan must also include:
> - How procurement considers past safety performance  
> - Service providersâ€™ roles and responsibilities  
> - Oversight mechanisms and compliance checks  
> - Communication protocols for safety issues

#### (c) **Environmental Protection Plan**  
Must meet requirements in **Section 10** and include:

- Integration with the management system and alignment with environmental obligations.
- Summary of:
  - How environmental hazards were identified (including nearby operations)
  - How environmental risks were assessed
- Description of:
  - Identified environmental hazards and risk assessments
  - Measures to prevent, monitor, and minimize environmental risks
  - Evaluation of effectiveness in meeting target environmental protection levels
- Communication methods to inform affected parties
- Details of **critical environmental protection facilities and equipment** (e.g., spill response systems) and their maintenance
- Organizational structure and chain of command for environmental protection
- **Procedure for discovering archaeological or burial sites** (e.g., immediate halt, reporting, consultation with Indigenous groups, archaeological investigation)
- Compliance and performance monitoring (data, audits, inspections)
- Change management processes for environmental risks

> For service providers:
> - How procurement considers past environmental performance  
> - Roles and responsibilities  
> - Oversight and compliance  
> - Communication on environmental issues
