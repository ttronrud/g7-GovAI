## 1. **Regulation Title and Status**
- **Title**: *Seeds Regulations*  
- **Citation**: "These Regulations may be cited as the Seeds Regulations."
- **Repeal Status**:
  - Several sections have been **repealed**:
    - **SOR/96-252, s. 2**: Repeals the original definition of "biotechnology".
    - **SOR/2007-223, s. 1**: Repeals the "Seeds Fees Notice" and "Department" sections.
    - **SOR/2018-108, s. 401**: Repeals references to "Registrar" and other administrative roles.
  - **Important**: The core definitions and standards (e.g., on seed grading, purity, and exemptions) remain largely in effect despite these repeals.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Interpretation**: The current version of the regulations is a **revised, partially updated** version of the original, with some administrative and procedural elements removed or replaced.

## 2. **Key Definitions (Applicable to the Regulations)**

| Term | Definition | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Biotechnology** | Application of science/engineering to use living organisms or their parts/products in natural or modified forms. | Includes genetic modification, tissue culture, etc. Seed derived via biotechnology is included in the definition of "seed". |
| **Conformity Verification Body** | Independent body with agreement with the Agency to assess and audit seed establishments, operators, graders, samplers, or labs. | Ensures compliance with seed standards through independent evaluation. |
| **Seed** | Includes seed derived through biotechnology. | Broadens scope to include genetically modified or biotechnologically enhanced seeds. |
| **Act** | Refers to the *Seeds Act*. | The primary legislation governing seed regulation in Canada. |
| **Agency** | Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA). | Responsible for enforcing seed regulations, issuing certifications, and overseeing testing. |
| **Association** | Canadian Seed Growers‚Äô Association (CSGA). | Key stakeholder that sets varietal purity standards and issues crop certificates. |
| **Canadian Wheat Board Area** | Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Alberta, and parts of British Columbia (Peace River District, Creston-Wynndell Area). | Region where the Canadian Wheat Board has jurisdiction over seed certification. |
| **Certified Status** | Seed meets varietal purity standards: <br> - Grown in Canada: crop certified ‚ÄúCertified‚Äù by Association; <br> - Grown abroad: certified by approved foreign agency and meets Association standards. | A quality assurance designation. |
| **Condition** | Preparation of seed via cleaning, processing, packing, treating, or altering its nature. | Includes any physical or chemical modification of seed lots. |
| **Crop Certificate** | Document issued by the Association certifying a crop meets varietal purity standards. | Required for foundation, registered, and certified status. |
| **Domestic Tag** | Official tag for seed grown in Canada and graded with a Canada pedigreed grade name (e.g., Foundation, Registered, Certified). | Indicates domestic origin and quality. |
| **Fastened** | Package sealed so it cannot be opened easily without evidence of opening. | Used to prevent tampering and ensure integrity. |
| **Flower Seeds** | Seeds of herbaceous plants grown for blooms, foliage, or ornamental features. | Excluded from certain grading requirements. |
| **Foundation Status** | Seed derived from a crop with a ‚ÄúFoundation‚Äù certificate issued by the Association and meets purity standards. | Highest quality tier; used as the base for breeding. |
| **Grade** | Determining or assigning a grade name based on sample analysis and prescribed standards. | Based on purity, germination, and variety. |
| **Inter-agency Certification Tag** | Tag for seed certified by a foreign official agency and graded with a Canada pedigreed name. | For internationally certified seed entering Canada. |
| **Licensed Sampler** | Individual licensed under section 13.1(2.1) to sample seed. | Must follow recognized sampling methods. |
| **Lot Designation** | Number, mark, symbol, or test number identifying a seed lot. | Used for traceability. |
| **Lot Number (for pedigreed seed)** | Includes the pedigreed seed declaration number. | Links seed to declaration records. |
| **Official Certifying Agency** | Foreign agency approved by the Association that certifies seed varietal purity. | Must be a member of OECD seed certification schemes or AOSCA. |
| **Official Sample** | Sample drawn by an inspector or under supervision of a licensed sampler, prepared per standard methods. | Used for testing and compliance. |
| **Officially Recognized Laboratory** | Lab designated by the Minister as accredited under the Act. | Conducts official seed testing. |
| **Officially Recognized Sample** | Sample drawn and prepared per standard methods by a licensed sampler, government body, or accredited agency/laboratory. | Ensures validity and traceability. |
| **Officially Recognized Test** | Test performed on an officially recognized sample in an officially recognized lab or by an accredited grader. | Must follow recognized standard methods. |
| **Pedigreed Seed Declaration** | Form used to declare processing of pedigreed seed. | Required for seed with pedigreed status. |
| **Pedigreed Seed Declaration Number** | Unique number assigned to the declaration. | Used to track seed processing. |
| **Pedigreed Status** | Seed is of foundation, registered, certified status, or approved as breeder or select seed. | Core quality designation; allows exemption from some requirements. |
| **Pedigreed Status Tag** | Tag for seed grown in Canada but not graded. | Indicates pedigree without formal grading. |
| **PPTM Varietal Blend** | Blend designed to maintain plant pest tolerance characteristics. | Used in pest management strategies. |
| **President** | Head of the Agency. | Oversees operations and appointments. |
| **Progeny** | Seed or seed-like crops (cereals, oilseeds, pulses). | Includes offspring of parent plants. |
| **Recognized Standard Method** | One of the following:<br> - Canadian Methods and Procedures for Testing Seed (CMPTS)<br> - Rules for Testing Seed (AOSA)<br> - International Rules for Seed Testing (ISTA) | Basis for all official testing. |
| **Registered Status** | Seed derived from a ‚ÄúRegistered‚Äù crop certificate and meets purity standards. | Second-tier quality; used in commercial production. |
| **Registrar** | Person designated by the President to accredit graders or license samplers. | Administrative role (repealed in SOR/2018-108). |
| **Seed Lot** | Quantity of seed that is uniform in purity, germination, variety, type, and quality. | Basis for grading and testing. |
| **Specialty Seed** | Seed mixed with fertilizers, soil, compost, moss, plastic, paper, etc. Includes artificial seeds. | May have different handling and labeling rules. |
| **Varietal Blend** | Mixture of two or more varieties of pedigreed seed. | Must maintain pedigreed status. |
| **Variety** | As defined by the International Union of Biological Sciences (ICBN); a distinct group of plants with common characteristics that are stable upon reproduction. | Includes hybrids from controlled cross-pollination. |
| **Variety Name** | Word, number, or letter/number combination used to identify a variety. | Must be standardized and registered. |

## 3. **Fees and Access Requirements**

> **Repealed**: The "Seeds Fees Notice" (SOR/2007-223, s.1) and "Department" sections have been repealed.

- **Current Requirement**:
  - **No service, right, privilege, or facility** under these regulations may be provided to a person **unless**:
    - The person includes the **applicable fee** with the application or request, **or**
    - The person has **agreed in writing to pay the fee upon receipt of an invoice** from the Agency.

> ‚úÖ **Implication**: All applications for grading, certification, sampling, or testing require payment of applicable fees.
