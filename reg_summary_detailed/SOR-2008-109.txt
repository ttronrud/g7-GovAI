### **Detailed Summary of the Aircraft Objects Regulations**

  The regulation was issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of Finance**.

#### **2. Purpose of the Regulation**
- The primary purpose of these **Aircraft Objects Regulations** is to **establish specific rules for the registration, treatment, and regulatory status of aircraft objects**—defined as property attached to or used in aircraft—particularly in relation to the application of certain provisions of the Bank Act.

#### **3. Exemption from Bank Act Provisions (Key Provision)**
- **Sections 426 to 436 of the Bank Act do not apply** to aircraft objects that:
  - Were **not registered under those sections prior to the coming into force of these Regulations**.
- This means that if an aircraft object (e.g., a component, engine, or part of an aircraft) was not registered under the Bank Act’s registration regime before the Regulations came into effect, it is **not subject** to the requirements of those sections (such as registration, reporting, or financial disclosure obligations).

#### **4. Transition and Sunset Clause**
- **Five-Year Sunset Provision**:  
  Five years after the day these Regulations come into force, the provisions of **Sections 426 to 436 of the Bank Act will cease to apply** to aircraft objects that were **already registered under those sections before that day**.

  - This provides a **transition mechanism** to gradually phase out the Bank Act’s jurisdiction over aircraft objects, allowing for a smooth shift to a more specialized or international framework.
