## **Overview of the Regulation**

This regulation establishes a formal **tariff (schedule) of fees, costs, allowances, and expenses** for personnel involved in conducting a **referendum** under the *Special Voting Rules* (Schedule II to the *Canada Elections Act*). It was issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **President of the Privy Council**, pursuant to specific provisions of the *Special Voting Rules* and the *Referendum Act*.

The regulation is enacted to ensure **fair, consistent, and transparent compensation** for key administrative and operational roles during a national referendum — particularly those involving the management of voting processes, supervision, and record-keeping.

It is officially known as the **"Special Voting Rules Referendum Fees Tariff"** and applies to all individuals employed directly or indirectly in connection with a referendum under the Special Voting Rules.

---

## **Legal Authority and Basis**

The regulation is grounded in the following legal provisions:

1. **Section 11(1)** of the *Special Voting Rules (Schedule II to the Canada Elections Act)*  
   – Grants authority to the Chief Electoral Officer to establish fees and allowances for special returning officers and other personnel.

2. **Section 7 of the Referendum Act**  
   – Authorizes the Chief Electoral Officer to make regulations related to the conduct of referendums, including financial arrangements for staff.

3. **Subsection 12(2)** of the *Special Voting Rules*  
   – Provides that the rules may be adapted by regulation to apply to referendums, as authorized under the Referendum Act.

4. **Subsection 7(1) of the Referendum Act**  
   – Confirms that the Special Voting Rules may be applied to referendums, thereby enabling the establishment of this tariff.

Thus, the regulation is both **legally authorized** and **operationally necessary** to manage the financial aspects of referendum operations.

## **Scope and Applicability**

The Tariff applies to the following personnel employed **at or with respect to a referendum** under the *Special Voting Rules*:

| Position | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Special Returning Officer** | Senior official responsible for overseeing the conduct of voting in a specific electoral district or region. |
| **Chief Assistant** | Senior support officer to the special returning officer, assisting with administrative and logistical duties. |
| **Scrutineer** | Individual responsible for verifying and recording votes, ensuring accuracy and integrity of the vote count. |
| **Clerical Assistant** | Support staff handling record-keeping, data entry, and other clerical tasks. |

> The tariff applies to all services rendered during the duration of a referendum, including travel, training, and special assignments.

## **Detailed Fee and Allowance Provisions**

### 1. **Special Returning Officer**

- **Base Compensation**:  
  Paid for **all services performed** by the special returning officer during the entire duration of the referendum.

- **Special Assignments**:  
  If requested by the Chief Electoral Officer to perform a **special task** (e.g., overseeing a remote polling station, resolving a dispute, or managing a crisis), the officer is **paid additional compensation** for those specific duties.

- **Training and Debriefing Sessions**:  
  If requested to attend a **training session** or a **referendum debriefing session**, the officer is entitled to **payment** for those activities, recognizing the time and effort required.

> *Note: This reflects the principle that employees are compensated not only for routine duties but also for additional responsibilities or professional development required during a referendum.*

---

### 2. **Chief Assistant**

- **Base Compensation**:  
  Paid for **all services performed** during the referendum.

- **Special Assignments**:  
  If assigned to perform a **special task** by the Chief Electoral Officer, the chief assistant is **paid accordingly**.

- **Training and Debriefing Sessions**:  
  Attendance at **training or debriefing sessions** is **compensated**, recognizing the importance of professional training and post-referendum analysis.

> This ensures that support staff are fairly compensated for both core and ad hoc duties.

---

### 3. **Scrutineer**

- **Base Compensation**:  
  Paid for **all services performed** during the referendum, including vote verification, supervision of counting, and record-keeping.

> No additional allowances are specified beyond the base rate for routine duties.

---

### 4. **Clerical Assistant**

- **Base Compensation**:  
  Paid for **all services performed** during the referendum, including data entry, filing, and administrative support.

> No special assignments or training are explicitly mentioned as being paid, suggesting that compensation is limited to core duties.

## **Meals and Incidental Expenses (Travel Allowance)**

This provision applies **only in the voting territories** defined in **subsections 5(1) and (5)(2)** of the *Special Voting Rules*.

- **Eligible Personnel**:  
  Special returning officers, chief assistants, and scrutineers.

- **Allowance**:  
  Paid for **meals and incidental expenses** incurred when traveling **away from their place of residence** in connection with the conduct of the referendum.

> This allowance is intended to cover reasonable costs associated with fieldwork, such as meals, lodging, transportation, and other necessary expenditures during travel to polling stations or reference locations.

> **Note**: This allowance does **not apply** to clerical assistants, indicating a distinction in operational roles and travel demands.

## **Purpose and Objectives**

The primary objectives of this Tariff are:

1. To **ensure fair and equitable compensation** for individuals who play critical roles in managing referendums.
2. To **support efficient and accurate vote counting and administration** by providing adequate resources to personnel.
3. To **facilitate training and debriefing** as part of democratic process improvement.
4. To **recognize the time and effort** involved in both routine and exceptional duties.
5. To **promote public confidence** in the integrity and professionalism of referendum operations.

## **Limitations and Exclusions**

- **Clerical assistants** are not entitled to:
  - Special assignment payments
  - Training or debriefing session payments
  - Travel allowances (meals/incidental expenses)

- The **tariff does not cover**:
  - Compensation for personal time not directly related to referendum duties
  - Overtime or performance-based bonuses
  - Benefits such as health insurance or retirement contributions

- Applies **only to referendums conducted under the Special Voting Rules**, not to regular elections.
