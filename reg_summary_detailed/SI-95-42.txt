### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

This regulation, issued under the **Canada Wildlife Act** (S.C. 1994, c. 23), establishes the administrative authority for three designated **National Wildlife Areas (NWAs)** in Canada. The primary purpose is to protect and manage public lands for **wildlife research, conservation, and public interpretation**. Below is a comprehensive, structured summary of the regulation.

---

## **1. Legal Basis and Authority**

- **Governing Legislation**: The regulation is issued under the **Canada Wildlife Act**, specifically **subsection 4(1)**.
- **Issuing Body**: The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet), acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of the Environment**.
- **Purpose**: To assign administrative responsibility for specific public lands to the **Minister of the Environment** for the purposes of **wildlife research, conservation, and public interpretation**.
- **Legal Justification**: The Governor in Council is satisfied that these lands are essential for the protection and study of wildlife and ecosystems.

> This reflects a federal mandate to designate and manage lands critical to biodiversity and ecological integrity, ensuring that such areas are protected from inappropriate development or human activity.

---

## **2. Designated National Wildlife Areas**

The regulation designates **three National Wildlife Areas** located in different regions of Canada. Each area is defined by detailed land descriptions, including legal references to property plans, deeds, and cadastre records.

---
