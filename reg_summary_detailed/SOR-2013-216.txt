**Detailed Summary of the CPFTA Verification of Origin Regulations**

---

### **1. Background and Legal Basis**

These **CPFTA Verification of Origin Regulations** are a set of rules designed to implement and operationalize the **origin verification procedures** under the **Canada–Peru Free Trade Agreement (CPFTA)**. The regulations are grounded in the **Customs Act** and were introduced in response to a public announcement known as **Customs Notice 09-018**, issued on **July 30, 2009**.

- **Legal Authority**:  
  The regulations are made under:
  - **Section 167.1(b)a** of the *Customs Act* (which authorizes the Minister to make regulations to give effect to public announcements),
  - **Section 42.1(c)**, **paragraphs 164(1)(i)d and (j)**, and **subsection 164(1.1)e** of the *Customs Act*, which relate to the administration of origin verification and the powers of customs officers.

- **Enactment Date**:  
  The regulations come into force on **August 1, 2009**, as specified in Customs Notice 09-018.

- **Statutory References**:  
  - S.C. 1992, c. 28, s. 31(1) – General provisions of the Customs Act  
  - R.S., c. 1 (2nd Supp.) – Relevant amendments to customs legislation  
  - S.C. 2009, c. 6, s. 24 – Enactment of the regulations  
  - S.C. 1992, c. 28, s. 30(1) – Authority to make regulations  
  - S.C. 2012, c. 18, s. 30 – Subsequent amendments or clarifications  

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined to ensure clarity and consistency in the application of the regulations:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Customs Act* |
| **Competent Authority** | The Peruvian government body responsible for administering customs and tax matters. Initially, this includes:<br> - *Ministerio de Comercio Exterior y Turismo* (Ministry of Foreign Trade and Tourism)<br> - *Superintendencia Nacional de Administración Tributaria* (National Tax Administration Superintendency)<br> *If Peru notifies Canada of a replacement, the successor bodies will take over.* |
| **Customs Administration** | The Canadian authority (e.g., Canada Border Services Agency – CBSA) responsible for administering Canadian customs laws. |
| **Goods** | Products for which preferential tariff treatment under the CPFTA is claimed. |
| **Material** | A component, part, or ingredient used in the production of goods. |
| **Verification Letter** | A formal request sent to an exporter, producer, or supplier asking for information about the origin of goods. |
| **Verification Questionnaire** | A structured document containing specific questions about the origin of goods. |
| **Verification Visit** | An on-site inspection conducted by a Canadian customs officer to verify the origin of goods. |

---

### **3. Methods of Origin Verification**

A verification of origin may be conducted through **three methods**, in addition to a physical verification visit:

1. **Verification Questionnaire**  
   - Completed by:
     - The **exporter or producer** of the goods, or  
     - A **producer or supplier of a material** used in production.

2. **Written Response to a Verification Letter**  
   - A response provided by the same parties to a formal request for origin information.

3. **Other Relevant Information**  
   - Any additional information received from the exporter, producer, or supplier.

> These documents serve as the primary tools for assessing compliance with origin rules, especially when a physical visit is not feasible.

---

### **4. Requirements for Verification Letters and Questionnaires**

All verification letters and questionnaires must:

- Clearly identify:
  - The **customs administration** and the **name and title** of the officer sending the document.
- Describe:
  - The **specific goods or materials** under review.
- Specify:
  - A **minimum response period of 30 days**, beginning on the date of receipt.

> This ensures that parties have sufficient time to provide accurate and complete information.

---

### **5. Request for Extension of Response Period**

- A recipient may request **one extension** within the specified period.
- The extension is granted for **no more than 30 additional days**.
- This provision allows flexibility in cases where documentation or data collection takes longer than expected.

---

### **6. Conditions for Conducting a Verification Visit**

A Canadian customs officer **may not conduct a verification visit** until the following conditions are met:

1. A **written notice of intent** is sent to:
   - The **person whose premises are to be visited**,
   - The **Peruvian competent authority**, and
   - (If requested by Peru) the **Peruvian Embassy in Canada**.

2. The **person whose premises are to be visited provides written consent** to the visit.

> These safeguards ensure transparency, respect for privacy, and cooperation between Canada and Peru.

---

### **7. Content of the Verification Visit Notice**

The notice must include the following details:

- Identity of the **customs administration** sending the notice  
- Name and **title of the officer** conducting the visit  
- Name of the **person whose premises are being visited**  
- **Date and location** of the visit  
- **Object and scope** of the visit (including description of goods/materials)  
- The **legal authority** under which the visit is being conducted  

> This ensures clarity and accountability for all parties involved.

---

### **8. Postponement of Verification Visits**

A visit may be **postponed** under two circumstances:

- **By Peru**: If Peru sends a **written request within 15 days** of receiving the notice, the visit is postponed.
- **By the Person Being Visited**: If the person whose premises are being visited sends a **written request for postponement within 15 days** of receiving the notice.

> The postponement period:
- Is **limited to a maximum of 60 days** from the date of notice,
- May be extended for a **longer period** if agreed upon by both Canada and Peru.

> This provides flexibility due to logistical, operational, or scheduling constraints.

---

### **9. Observers During the Visit**

- A person may **designate up to two observers** to be present during the visit.
- Observers are **not allowed to participate in the verification process**; their role is **limited to observation only**.
- The person must **identify the observers** to the officer conducting the visit.

> This promotes transparency and trust in the verification process, allowing stakeholders to monitor the inspection.

---

### **10. Obligations of Exporters and Producers**

Under **subsection 42.1(2)** of the *Customs Act*, exporters and producers are required to:

1. **Maintain records** relevant to determining the origin of goods, in accordance with **Peruvian laws**.
2. **Provide access** to these records to the customs officer during a verification.
3. **Respond promptly** to:
   - Verification letters (within 30 days),
   - Verification questionnaires (within the specified period).

> These obligations ensure that origin claims are supported by verifiable documentation.

---

### **11. Consent to Verification Visit**

- Written consent to the visit must be provided **within 30 days** of receiving the notice under paragraph 6(1)(a)(i).
- Failure to provide consent may result in the visit being denied or postponed.

---

### **12. Re-Determination of Origin**

If a customs officer determines that goods are **not eligible** for preferential tariff treatment under the CPFTA, they must:

- Send a **written notice** to the exporter or producer stating that preferential treatment will be denied or withdrawn.
- The notice must include:
  - The **date after which preferential treatment will be withdrawn**,
  - The **period during which the exporter or producer may submit written comments or additional information**.

> This provides a fair opportunity for the exporter to respond and potentially appeal the decision.

---

### **13. Delivery and Receipt of Documents**

- All verification letters, questionnaires, and notices must be sent via a method that **produces confirmation of receipt** (e.g., email with read receipt, certified mail, electronic tracking).
- This ensures that all parties have a verifiable record of communication.

---

### **14. Effective Date**

- These regulations are **deemed to have come into force on August 1, 2009**, as stated in the public announcement (Customs Notice 09-018).

---

### **Summary of Key Objectives and Impacts**

| Objective | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Ensure Compliance** | Verifies that goods claimed for preferential tariff treatment under the CPFTA actually originate in Peru, as required by the agreement. |
| **Promote Transparency** | Requires clear documentation, written consent, and observer participation. |
| **Respect for Peruvian Sovereignty** | Involves Peruvian authorities and allows for bilateral coordination. |
| **Fair Process** | Provides clear timelines, extension rights, and opportunities to respond to findings. |
| **Operational Flexibility** | Allows for postponements and alternative verification methods (questionnaires, letters). |
