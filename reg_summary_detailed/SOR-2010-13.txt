**Detailed Summary of the G8 Summit Privileges and Immunities Order, No. 2010-2**

---

### **Overview**

The *G8 Summit Privileges and Immunities Order, No. 2010-2* is a Canadian regulatory instrument issued under the authority of the **Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act (FMIOA)**. It grants specific **privileges and immunities** to participants in the **G8 Summit** and related preparatory meetings, enabling them to carry out their official duties in Canada without undue interference or legal barriers.

This Order was made by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**, pursuant to **Section 5a of the FMIOA**, which allows the government to confer privileges and immunities on international organizations and their representatives during specific events.

The Order came into force upon registration and applies to a defined period from **January 15, 2010, to July 4, 2010**, covering the planning and execution of the G8 Summit and its associated meetings.

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Convention**  
   Refers to the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, as set out in **Schedule III** of the FMIOA. This international treaty forms the legal basis for the privileges and immunities granted under the Order. The provisions of the Convention are adapted to the G8 context in this Order.

2. **Experts performing missions for the Organization**  
   Governmental or intergovernmental experts invited by the G8 to attend the **Meeting** and its **preparatory meetings**. These are not diplomats but technical or policy advisors engaged in specific missions.

3. **Meeting**  
   The main G8 summit meeting scheduled to take place in **Huntsville, Ontario**, from **June 25 to 27, 2010**.

4. **Organization**  
   The **G8**, an intergovernmental conference of the eight leading industrialized countries (Canada, United States, United Kingdom, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, and Russia).

5. **Preparatory Meetings**  
   A comprehensive list of meetings held in Canada during the period from January 15, 2010, to June 24, 2010. These include:
   - **Political Directors' Meetings** (January–June 2010)
   - **Sherpas’ Meetings** (personal representatives of Heads of State/Government)
   - **Ministers of Finance Meetings** (February 2010)
   - **Sous-sherpas’ Meetings** (personal representatives of Foreign Ministers)
   - **G8 Accountability Senior Level Working Group** meetings (March and April 2010)
   - **Ministers of Foreign Affairs Meeting** (March 2010)
   - **Africa Personal Representatives and African Partnership Forum** meetings (April and June 2010)

   *Note:* The Order includes all such meetings occurring in Canada during the specified timeframe.

---

### **Legal Capacity and Privileges of the Organization**

The **G8 (as an Organization)** is granted **legal capacity as a body corporate** in Canada during the period from **January 15, 2010, to July 4, 2010**. This means the G8 can:
- Enter into contracts,
- Own property,
- Be sued or sued in its own name,
- And otherwise function as a legal entity.

It is granted the **privileges and immunities** set out in **Sections 2 to 5 of Article II of the UN Convention**, which include:
- Immunity from legal process (except in cases of serious crime),
- Immunity from taxation,
- Immunity from search and seizure,
- Protection of official communications and documents.

These privileges are extended to the G8 as a whole during its operations in Canada.

---

### **Privileges and Immunities for State Representatives**

Representatives of G8 member states (e.g., ambassadors, ministers, heads of state) are granted specific immunities depending on the timing and nature of their participation:

#### **1. Periods of Immunity (by phase):**
- **January 29 – February 13, 2010**  
  Immunities under **Sections 11, 12, and 14–16 of Article IV of the Convention** are granted. These apply specifically to the **preparatory meetings of the Ministers of Finance and Foreign Affairs**.

- **March 22 – April 6, 2010**  
  Same privileges (Sections 11, 12, 14–16) are granted for the **Ministers of Finance and Foreign Affairs** meetings.

- **June 15 – July 4, 2010**  
  Again, the same immunities are granted during the final phase of the preparatory process.

#### **2. Extended Immunity for Other Preparatory Meetings**
- From **January 15, 2010, to July 4, 2010**, representatives are granted **Section 11(d) of Article IV of the Convention** for all other preparatory meetings (e.g., political directors, sherpas, accountability groups, Africa meetings).

> **Note:** These immunities are **only to the extent required** for the performance of official duties. They do not extend to personal activities or private conduct.

---

### **Privileges and Immunities for Experts**

Experts performing missions for the G8 (e.g., technical advisors, economic analysts, environmental specialists) are granted:
- The full set of privileges and immunities under **Article VI of the UN Convention**, during the period from **January 15, 2010, to July 4, 2010**.
- These include:
  - Immunity from arrest or detention for acts performed in the course of their official duties.
  - Immunity from legal proceedings for statements made during official functions.
  - Protection of personal documents and communications.

> This ensures that experts can work freely and without fear of legal repercussions while contributing to G8 policy development.

---

### **Scope and Duration**

- **Start Date:** January 15, 2010  
- **End Date:** July 4, 2010  
  (Note: The Meeting ends on June 27, but the Order extends to July 4 to cover final administrative and logistical closure.)

- The Order applies to **all meetings** of the G8 and its associated forums (e.g., Africa Partnership Forum) held in Canada during this period.

---

### **Purpose and Significance**

This Order serves several critical functions:

1. **Facilitates Diplomatic Functioning**  
   Ensures that G8 officials and experts can conduct meetings, negotiate, and exchange sensitive information without fear of legal interruption.

2. **Respects International Norms**  
   Aligns Canada’s legal framework with established international conventions on diplomatic immunity, particularly the UN Convention.

3. **Supports Multilateral Cooperation**  
   Enables effective coordination among G8 nations on global issues such as economic policy, climate change, security, and development.

4. **Protects National Security and Confidentiality**  
   Prevents unauthorized access to sensitive documents or communications during high-level negotiations.

5. **Provides Legal Clarity**  
   Clearly defines which parties (G8, ministers, experts) are entitled to which privileges and under what conditions.

---

### **Repeal Clause**

The Order includes a **repeal clause**, indicating that it is a temporary measure. Once the specified period ends (July 4, 2010), all privileges and immunities granted under this Order **cease automatically** and revert to standard Canadian laws.

---

### **Legal Basis**

- **Section 5a of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act (FMIOA)**  
  Grants the Governor General the power to make orders granting privileges and immunities to international organizations and their representatives.

- **Schedule III of the FMIOA**  
  Contains the full text of the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, which serves as the foundational legal instrument.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *G8 Summit Privileges and Immunities Order, No. 2010-2* is a targeted, time-limited, and legally grounded instrument that enables the smooth and secure conduct of a major international summit and its preparatory processes within Canada. By granting appropriate immunities to the G8, its officials, and experts, the Order supports Canada’s role as a host nation in multilateral diplomacy while ensuring compliance with international legal standards.

It reflects Canada’s commitment to international cooperation, diplomatic neutrality, and the rule of law in the context of global governance.

--- 

**Final Note:** This Order is not a permanent legal change. It is a **temporary measure** enacted solely for the purpose of hosting the G8 Summit and its related activities in 2010.
