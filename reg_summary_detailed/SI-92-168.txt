**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

**Regulatory Instrument:**  
Transfer of Public Service Functions under the *Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act*

**Issuing Authority:**  
His Excellency the Governor General in Council, acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, pursuant to **Section 2 of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act**.

**Effective Date:**  
August 31, 1992

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**
This regulation establishes the formal transfer of specific functions and responsibilities from the **Department of External Affairs** to the newly established **Canada Employment and Immigration Commission (CEIC)**. The transfer is part of a broader government restructuring aimed at improving the efficiency, coordination, and administrative focus of Canada‚Äôs immigration and employment-related policies and services.

The objective is to centralize and streamline immigration and employment services under a dedicated commission, ensuring greater accountability, expertise, and responsiveness in managing immigration, employment, and related public service functions.

---

### **Scope of the Transfer**

The transfer includes the **control and supervision** of the following portions of the Department of External Affairs:

#### 1. **Consular, Immigration and Passport Affairs Branch**  
- **Excluded:** The **Passport Office** is explicitly *not* included in this transfer.  
- **Excluded Portions:**  
  - Any part of the **Consular and Immigration Affairs Bureau** that relates to **consular services** (e.g., issuing diplomatic or consular documents, assisting foreign nationals in consular matters).  
  - Any part of the **Consular, Immigration and Refugee Policy Bureau** that deals with **consular services**.  

> ‚úÖ **Included:** All functions related to **immigration affairs** within this branch, excluding passport issuance and consular services.

#### 2. **Immigration Sections of the Programs Divisions**  
These sections are located within the following geographic branches of the Department of External Affairs:

- **Africa and Middle East Branch**  
- **Asia and Pacific Branch**  
- **Europe Branch**  
- **Latin America and Caribbean Branch**  
- **United States Branch**

> ‚úÖ **Included:** The **immigration sections** within each of these branches, including their **related units at missions abroad** (i.e., embassies, consulates, and other foreign diplomatic posts).

### **Key Implications and Context**

1. **Reorganization of Government Functions**  
   The transfer reflects a strategic shift in government structure to place immigration and employment services under a single, specialized body‚Äîthe Canada Employment and Immigration Commission‚Äîenhancing coordination and policy coherence.

2. **Separation of Functions**  
   - **Consular services** (such as passport issuance and assistance to foreign nationals) were retained within the Department of External Affairs to preserve diplomatic and international obligations.  
   - **Immigration processing and policy development** were transferred to CEIC to allow for more focused, professional management.

3. **Geographic Coverage**  
   The transfer applies to all overseas missions, ensuring that immigration-related functions (e.g., application processing, visa issuance, program implementation) are now managed under CEIC‚Äôs oversight, even in foreign countries.

4. **Legal Authority**  
   The transfer is authorized under **Section 2 of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act**, which empowers the Governor in Council (on the advice of the Prime Minister) to reassign responsibilities among public service departments for administrative efficiency and policy effectiveness.

### **Outcome and Significance**

- The Canada Employment and Immigration Commission (CEIC) became the central body responsible for:
  - Immigration policy development and implementation
  - Processing of immigration applications
  - Management of international immigration programs
  - Oversight of immigration operations abroad

- This reorganization laid the foundation for future reforms in Canada‚Äôs immigration system, eventually contributing to the establishment of the modern **Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)** in later years.
