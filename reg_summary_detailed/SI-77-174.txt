## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Rules Governing Criminal Appeals to the Court of Appeal of Alberta**

These rules define key terms, procedural timelines, filing requirements, service obligations, and substantive procedures for appeals in criminal cases in Alberta. They apply to appeals under:

- **Part XVIII of the Criminal Code**,  
- **Section 771 of the Criminal Code**, or  
- **The Summary Convictions Act of Alberta**.

They are supplemented by the *Rules of Court* for civil appeals, with specific modifications (e.g., no cross-appeals, Rule 509 does not apply).

---

## 1. **Definitions of Key Terms**

| Term | Definition |

| **Appellate Judge** | A judge of the Court of Appeal of Alberta, including ex officio judges. |

## 2. **Scope of Application**

These rules govern appeals to the **Court of Appeal of Alberta** under:

- Part XVIII of the *Criminal Code* (appeals from conviction/sentence),
- Section 771 of the *Criminal Code* (appeals from acquittal or insanity findings),
- The *Summary Convictions Act of Alberta* (summary conviction appeals).

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: The rules are not exhaustive. In matters not covered, the *Rules of Court* for civil appeals apply, **mutatis mutandis**, with two key exceptions:
> - **No cross-appeals** are permitted.
> - **Rule 509** (which governs certain procedural matters in civil appeals) **does not apply**.

---

## 3. **Non-Compliance and Time Extensions**

- **Non-compliance** with these rules does **not void** a proceeding.
- The Court or an Appellate Judge may **amend, set aside, or otherwise deal with** non-compliant proceedings as justice demands.
- **Time limits** for filing appeals may be **extended or shortened** by the Court or an Appellate Judge.
- **Notice to the opposing party** must be given **two clear days in writing** unless:
  - The application is made on **consent**, or
  - The Court orders otherwise.

> üìå **Exception**: If the Court dismisses an application to extend or shorten time, the appellant may **file a notice of appeal within 7 days** of the dismissal.

---

## 4. **Abandonment of Appeals**

- **Conviction appeals** and **Attorney-General appeals under s. 676 of the Code** are **deemed abandoned** if:
  - **Four calendar months** pass after the Appeal Books are ready, and
  - The appellant **fails to file and serve** their factum (formal written submission) on the respondent.

> ‚öñÔ∏è This rule acts as a time-bar to prevent stale appeals and ensures active participation.

---

## 5. **Continuance of Appeals**

- The Court or an Appellate Judge may, on **proper grounds**, **continue** an appeal (e.g., due to illness, delay, or other justifiable reasons) **before or after** the time limit.
- Such continuation is granted on **terms that are just**.

---

## 6. **Production of Evidence**

- If the appellant or respondent obtains **leave to call further evidence**, the rules for **civil matters** (as per the *Rules of the Supreme Court*) apply:
  - Witnesses and documents may be obtained via the same procedures.
  - All applicable civil rules apply to evidence production in appeals.

---

## 7. **Formal Judgment and Corrections**

- After judgment, the **registrar shall promptly enter a formal judgment**.
- **Clerical errors or omissions** may be corrected by a **single member of the Court** pronouncing the judgment.

### üìù **Filing and Service Procedures**

#### (A) **Prisoner Appeal**
- Serve **three (3) copies** on the **warden or designated officer** of the institution.
- The officer must:
  - **Endorse** the date of receipt,
  - **Return one copy** to the appellant,
  - **Forward one copy** to the registrar,
  - **Retain one copy**.

#### (B) **Convicted Person (not a prisoner)**
- File **three (3) copies** with the registrar **or** mail them by **registered mail** to the registrar.

#### (C) **Appeal by the Attorney-General**
- File **two (2) copies** with the registrar.
- Serve **personally** on the accused (or their counsel), **unless ordered otherwise** by an Appellate Judge.
- If **substitutional service** is ordered, **leave of Court is required** before the appeal can be argued.
- On such application, the Court may give **further directions** on service.

> ‚úÖ Upon receipt (except for Attorney-General appeals), the registrar must **immediately forward a copy** to:
> - The Attorney-General or his counsel,
> - The prosecutor or his counsel,
> - The judge (in cases tried before a Provincial Judge),
> - The clerk of the Court in which the trial occurred.

---

## 9. **Form of Notice of Appeal**

- In appeals by **unrepresented persons**, the notice must be in **Form A** or equivalent.
- In all other appeals, it must be in **Form B** or equivalent.
- Where an unrepresented person **subsequently retains counsel**, they must:
  - **Immediately notify** the registrar and the respondent.
  - After that, all rules applicable to represented appeals apply.

> ‚úÖ Every notice must:
> - Clearly state the **grounds of appeal** or application,
> - Be **signed by the appellant or their counsel**.

---

## 10. **Hearing Scheduling**

- **No time or place for hearing** is stated in the notice of appeal.
- Upon receipt (except for leave applications), the registrar shall:
  - **Enter the case on the lists** for the **next sittings** of the Court.
  - Issue **directions** regarding hearing (e.g., date, time, format).

> üìç **Hearing Location**:
> - Appeals from **Southern Judicial Districts** ‚Üí Heard in **Calgary**.
> - All other appeals ‚Üí Heard in **Edmonton**.
