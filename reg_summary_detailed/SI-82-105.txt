### üîπ **3. Scope of Application**

The Agreement applies to the following categories of individuals:

1. **Nationals** of either country  
2. **Refugees**  
3. **Stateless persons**  
4. **Others** who derive rights from nationals, refugees, or stateless persons  
5. **Nationals of non-Contracting States** who are not otherwise included (but only if they fall under the above categories)

#### Equal Treatment Principle:
- Individuals designated in Article III (above) who reside in either country shall receive **equal treatment** in terms of **benefit eligibility and payment** as nationals of that country.

#### Residency and Benefits:
- Nationals of a Contracting State who live **outside both countries** shall receive benefits from the other country **under the same conditions** as that country‚Äôs nationals living abroad.

#### Exclusion of Residency-Based Rules:
- Laws that require **residence or presence** in a country to qualify for benefits **do not apply** to individuals covered under the agreement who reside in the other country.

### üîπ **4. Employment and Contribution Rules**

#### General Rule:
> An employed person working in one country shall be subject to **only the laws of that country**.

#### Exception ‚Äì Work in the Other Country (Up to 60 Months):
- If an employee is covered under the laws of one country (e.g., Canada) and is **required by their employer to work in the other country (U.S.)**, they remain subject to **only the first country‚Äôs laws**, as if the work were performed in that country.
- This applies **only if the period of work in the U.S. does not exceed 60 months**.

> üîç **Intermittent Work**: If the person works in the U.S. for **short, separate periods**, each period counts as a **separate period of work**.

> üö© **Mutual Consent Exception**: With prior agreement between the Competent Authorities, this rule may apply even if:
- The employer does **not have a place of business** in the first country, or
- The period of work **exceeds or is expected to exceed 60 months**

#### Government Employees:
- This rule **does not apply** to government employees (as defined in the Vienna Conventions on Diplomatic and Consular Relations).
- Exceptions:
  - A government employee sent to work in the other country is subject to **only the first country‚Äôs laws**.
  - A person hired locally in the other country is subject to **only the other country‚Äôs laws**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Immunity Clause**: Government officials (diplomats, consular staff) are **exempt** unless they have **waived immunities** regarding social security contributions.

#### Special Cases ‚Äì Ships and Aircraft:
- For officers or crew members of ships or aircraft:
  - If covered under both U.S. and CPP rules, the person is subject to:
    - **Only the Canada Pension Plan (CPP)** if they are a **resident of Canada**.
    - **Only U.S. laws** otherwise.

#### Self-Employment:
- If a person would be covered under both countries due to self-employment:
  - They are subject to **only Canada‚Äôs rules** if they are a **resident of Canada**.
  - Otherwise, subject to **U.S. laws**.

> üîÑ **Activity Classification**: If one country treats an activity as self-employment and the other as employment, the rules for **self-employment** apply.
