## ðŸ“ CORE PROVISIONS (SUMMARIZED)

### 1. **Eligibility for Direct Loans**
A qualifying student may receive a **direct loan** if:
- They are enrolled as a **full-time student** at a designated educational institution.
- They have been issued (or are issued) a **certificate of eligibility**.
- They submit **confirmation of enrolment** to the Minister **within 30 days** of receiving it, **no later than the end of the confirmed period**.
- They meet all conditions listed in the regulation (e.g., income limits, enrollment status).

> ðŸ“Œ The confirmation of enrolment must include the studentâ€™s **Social Insurance Number (SIN)** and be signed by an **officer of the designated educational institution** (e.g., registrar or student aid officer).

---

### 2. **Submission of Confirmation of Enrolment**
- Must be submitted to:
  - The **Minister** (for direct loans),
  - The **lender** (if the student has a risk-shared loan or guaranteed student loan).
- **Deadline**: Within 30 days of obtaining confirmation, and **no later than the end of the confirmed period**.

> âš ï¸ If the student was previously a full-time student and then stopped (under section 8), and the confirmation is submitted **more than 6 months later**, special interest payment rules apply.

---

### 3. **Interest Accrual and Payment Rules (Post-Return to Full-Time Status)**

If a student returns to full-time status after having ceased being one:

#### a) If more than 6 months have passed since ceasing full-time status:
- If indebted under **risk-shared or guaranteed loans**:
  - Must pay **interest accrued** from the day after they stopped being full-time until the day before the confirmed period begins.
- If indebted under a **direct loan**:
  - Must pay **interest accrued** to the day before the confirmed period begins.

#### b) If confirmation is submitted **within 6 months** of the return to full-time status:
- Can choose to either:
  - Pay the accrued interest, or
  - **Add the unpaid interest to the principal balance** in a consolidated loan agreement.

> This provision allowed flexibility for students returning to studies after a break, particularly those with existing loan obligations.

---

### 4. **Becoming a Full-Time Student Again**
A student becomes a full-time student on the **later of**:
- The day the confirmation of enrolment is received by the Minister, **or**
- The day they enter into a **full-time direct loan agreement**.

> For students with **outstanding loans** from full-time status:
- If they meet the conditions in subsection (1)(e) or (f), they become full-time students **on the day they meet those conditions**.
- Otherwise, they **continue as full-time students** from the day **after** they would have otherwise ceased.

> This ensures continuity of student status and loan eligibility.

---

### 5. **Loan Agreements and Consolidation**
- **Consolidated Loan Agreements**:
  - Replace multiple older loans (e.g., risk-shared or guaranteed loans).
  - Must be in prescribed form.
  - Include the borrowerâ€™s SIN.
  - Replace all prior agreements and include total principal and repayment terms.
- Types include:
  - **Consolidated Risk-Shared Loan Agreement**
  - **Consolidated Direct Loan Agreement**
  - **Consolidated Guaranteed Student Loan Agreement**

> These were designed to simplify repayment and reduce administrative complexity for borrowers with multiple loans.

---

### 6. **Special Status for Students with Disabilities**
- A student with **permanent or persistent/prolonged disability** who is enrolled in **40â€“59% of a full course load** may **elect to be considered a full-time student**.
- The **certificate of eligibility** must specify whether the student is classified as full-time or part-time.

> This provision recognizes the challenges faced by students with long-term impairments and provides greater flexibility in enrollment status.

---

### 7. **Refund of Fees**
- The designated educational institution may be authorized to **forward any fee refund** (paid with loan proceeds) to the Minister for **credit against future direct loans**.

> This ensures that loan funds are used efficiently and that institutions are not overcharged.
