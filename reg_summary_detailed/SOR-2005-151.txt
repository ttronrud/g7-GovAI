## ðŸ“š **Detailed Summary of the Canada Education Savings Regulations (S.C. 2004, c. 26)**

These regulations establish the administrative framework governing the **Registered Education Savings Plan (RESP)** system in Canada, including rules for **CES grants**, the **Canada Learning Bond (CLB)**, **educational assistance payments (EAPs)**, **withdrawals**, **repayments**, and **trustee responsibilities**. The goal is to ensure that contributions to RESPs are used effectively for post-secondary education and that government subsidies are properly administered and safeguarded.

### ðŸ”¹ 2. **Order of Withdrawal of Contributions**

When both assisted and unassisted contributions are in an RESP, withdrawals are **prioritized** as follows:

1. **Assisted contributions** are withdrawn **before** unassisted contributions.
2. Among unassisted contributions:
   - Those made **after 1997** are withdrawn **before** those made **before 1998**.

> This ensures that government-subsidized contributions are used first, preserving the value of the CES grant.

### ðŸ”¹ 5. **Eligibility for Multiple Beneficiaries (EAP and Grant Rules)**

The Minister may pay a CES grant **only if**:
- The RESP has **one beneficiary**, OR
- All beneficiaries are **brothers or sisters** of each other.

> This prevents the grant from being used for non-related beneficiaries and maintains the family-based nature of the program.

---

### ðŸ”¹ 6. **Restrictions on Withdrawals After March 22, 2004**

If **assisted contributions** are withdrawn after **March 22, 2004**, the Minister **will not** pay a CES grant for that individual **during the period** that begins on the day of withdrawal and ends on the last day of the **second year following the withdrawal**, **unless**:

1. At the time of withdrawal, **at least one beneficiary is eligible** to receive an **EAP**.
2. The withdrawal is an **eligible transfer** (e.g., to another RESP or to a parent).
3. The withdrawal is part of an **excess contribution** (to reduce tax under Part X.4) and the excess is **not greater than $4,000**.

> This rule protects the grant system from misuse and ensures that withdrawals do not disqualify future grant eligibility.

---

### ðŸ”¹ 7. **Ineligibility for Beneficiaries (Post-Withdrawal Rule)**

If contributions made **before 1998** are withdrawn **after February 23, 1998**, any beneficiary of the RESP **during the period from February 24, 1998, to the date of withdrawal** is considered an **ineligible beneficiary** for the period starting on the day of withdrawal and ending on the last day of the second year following the withdrawal.

> This prevents beneficiaries from receiving benefits after a certain date, especially to avoid misuse of the system during a transitional period.

- The total withdrawal for the year is **$200 or less**.
- The withdrawal occurs when **at least one beneficiary is eligible** for an EAP.
- It is an **eligible transfer**.
- It is part of an **excess contribution** (â‰¤ $4,000).

> These exceptions allow for small, legitimate withdrawals (e.g., for emergency expenses or transfers to another RESP).

### ðŸ”¹ 9. **Canada Learning Bond (CLB) Rules**

The Minister may pay a CLB **only if**:

- A **trustee agreement** is in place with the required terms (Section 8).
- The application is made by the **trustee at the request of a subscriber**.
- The RESP **complies with registration rules** under subsection 146.1(2) of the Income Tax Act.
- The RESP has **only one beneficiary**, or all beneficiaries are **brothers or sisters**.
- The RESP has a **CLB account** for each beneficiary.
- The trustee complies with all regulatory terms.

> The CLB is paid upon the **birth of the beneficiary** or when the RESP is established (whichever comes first), and is **non-refundable**.
