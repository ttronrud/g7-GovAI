### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
- The regulations were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Natural Resources**, pursuant to specific provisions of the *National Energy Board Act*:
  - Subsection **48.12(6)a**
  - Subsection **48.13(7)a**
  - Subsection **48.14(3)a**

> This means the regulations are legally binding and enforceable under federal energy regulation law, specifically governing financial obligations of pipeline operators.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

#### **"Authorized" Pipeline**
A pipeline is considered "authorized" if:
- Its **construction and operation have been approved** under **Part III of the Canadian Energy Regulator Act (CERA)**;
- It is **not under construction** or has not begun construction;
- It is **not deactivated, decommissioned, or abandoned** by order of the Commission or with Commission leave.

> This definition excludes pipelines that are in early stages of development or have been permanently shut down.

### **4. Risk Value Calculation**

The **risk value** is a key metric used to determine financial liability and is calculated as:

> **Risk Value = (Maximum Outside Diameter in mm)¬≤ √ó (Maximum Operating Pressure in megapascals)**

- If a company operates **two or more pipelines**, the risk value is taken from the **pipeline with the highest risk value**.
- This ensures that the most hazardous or high-risk pipeline drives the financial requirement.

> Example: A pipeline with a 1.2 m diameter and 10 MPa pressure will have a much higher risk value than a smaller or lower-pressure one.

---

### **5. Determining the Applicable Limit of Liability for Multiple Commodities or Varieties**

If a company operates pipelines transporting **multiple commodities or varieties**, the limit of liability is determined as follows:

| Scenario | Rule |
|--------|------|
| **Multiple commodities** | Apply the limit as if the company were transporting only the commodity with the **highest liability** |
| **Multiple varieties of the same commodity** | Apply the limit as if transporting only the variety with the **highest liability** |
| **Unconnected pipelines transporting different commodities** | Apply the limit as if operating only the pipeline with the **highest liability** |

> This ensures that companies are held to the **most financially significant risk** in their operations.

### **6. Types of Financial Resources Required (Under Section 138(2) of the Act)**

The Commission may require companies to maintain one or more of the following financial resources to meet their obligations:

- Insurance policy  
- Escrow agreement  
- Letter of credit  
- Line of credit  
- Participation in a **pooled fund** (under subsection 139(1))  
- Parent company guarantee  
- Surety bond, pledge agreement, indemnity bond, or suretyship agreement  
- Cash or cash equivalents  

> These are the **authorized forms** of financial assurance that can satisfy regulatory requirements.

---

### **7. Accessibility Requirements for Financial Resources**

The regulations specify **how much of the financial resources must be readily accessible** (i.e., immediately available to the Commission in case of emergency or claim):

| Company Type | Minimum % of Financial Resources Must Be Readily Accessible |
|------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Companies operating pipelines under **paragraphs 137(5)(a)** or **2(1)(a) to (d)** | **At least 5%** |
| Companies operating pipelines under **paragraphs 2(1)(e) to (i)** | **At least 2.5%** |

> "Readily accessible" means funds must be **available on demand** and not locked in long-term investments or illiquid instruments.

---

### **8. Commission‚Äôs Authority to Specify Accessible Financial Resources**

If the Commission specifies which types of financial resources must be readily accessible, it must choose from the following:

- Letter of credit  
- Line of credit  
- Participation in a pooled fund  
- Cash or cash equivalents  

> This gives the Commission flexibility to ensure that funds are both **secure and usable** in emergencies.

---

### **9. Pooled Funds ‚Äì Requirements and Oversight**

A **pooled fund** is a shared financial mechanism where multiple pipeline operators contribute to a single fund to collectively meet financial requirements.

#### Key Requirements:
- Must be **located in Canada**  
- Must be used **solely** for meeting financial requirements under the Act  
- Must be administered by a **representative approved by the Commission**  
- Terms of administration (and changes to those terms) must be **approved by the Commission**  
- **Minimum readily accessible amount**: $250,000,000  
- If the amount falls below $250,000,000, it must be **restored within 10 business days**

> This promotes cost-sharing and risk mitigation among operators.

---

### **10. Reporting Obligations for Pooled Fund Administrators**

The administrator of a pooled fund must:

- **No later than April 30 each year**:
  - Provide **audited financial statements**  
  - Demonstrate that **readily accessible funds** are maintained per the regulations  
- **Notify the Commission within 1 business day** of:
  - Addition or withdrawal of a participant  
  - Any reduction in readily accessible funds (except for interest, banking fees, or administrative costs authorized under the fund‚Äôs terms)  
- Provide the Commission with:
  - Contact person‚Äôs **telephone number, email, and mailing address**

> This ensures **transparency, accountability, and timely oversight**.
