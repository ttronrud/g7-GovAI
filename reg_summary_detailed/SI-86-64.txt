### **1. Designation of the Civil Aviation Tribunal as a Department under the Financial Administration Act**

**Legal Basis:**  
Paragraph (b) of the definition of "department" in **Section 2 of the Financial Administration Act (FAA)**.

**Key Provision:**  
The Governor General in Council designates the **Civil Aviation Tribunal** as a "department" for the purposes of the Financial Administration Act.

**Purpose and Significance:**  
- This designation means that the Civil Aviation Tribunal is recognized as a federal department within the scope of the Financial Administration Act.  
- As a result, the Tribunal is subject to the financial, administrative, and reporting requirements of the Act, including:
  - The requirement to maintain financial records and accounts.
  - The obligation to comply with financial management and procurement rules.
  - The need to submit annual financial reports to the Department of Finance.
  - The application of federal financial regulations and audit standards.
- This formal recognition ensures that the Tribunal operates under a structured, accountable, and transparent financial framework consistent with other federal departments.

### **2. Designation of the Minister of Transport as the "Appropriate Minister" under the Financial Administration Act**

**Legal Basis:**  
Paragraph (b) of the definition of "appropriate Minister" in **Section 2 of the Financial Administration Act**.

**Key Provision:**  
The Minister of Transport is designated as the **appropriate Minister** with respect to the Civil Aviation Tribunal.

**Purpose and Significance:**  
- The "appropriate Minister" is responsible for overseeing the financial and administrative functions of a department under the FAA.  
- This designation grants the Minister of Transport authority to:
  - Approve certain expenditures or financial decisions on behalf of the Tribunal.
  - Provide direction on financial policy and compliance matters.
  - Serve as the primary point of contact between the Tribunal and the Department of Finance.
- It also establishes accountability: the Minister of Transport is responsible for ensuring the Tribunal complies with federal financial administration standards and reporting obligations.

### **3. Designation of the Minister of Transport as the "Minister" under the Aeronautics Act**

**Legal Basis:**  
**Section 31* of the Aeronautics Act**.

**Key Provision:**  
The Minister of Transport, who is a member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada, is designated as the **Minister for the purposes of Section 31** of the Aeronautics Act.

**Purpose and Significance:**  
- Section 31 of the Aeronautics Act typically provides for the appointment of a Minister to act as the official authority responsible for the operation and oversight of the Civil Aviation Tribunal in matters related to aviation regulation and dispute resolution.
- This designation ensures that:
  - The Minister of Transport has formal authority to issue directions, make decisions, or intervene in matters involving the Tribunal.
  - The Tribunal operates under the legal and policy framework established by the Aeronautics Act, with the Minister of Transport serving as the key government representative.
  - The Tribunal's decisions and functions are aligned with national aviation policy and regulatory objectives.

### **Background and Context**

- The regulation was enacted on **April 1, 1985**, under **S.C. 1985, c. 28, s. 5** (a Statutory Instrument of Canada).
- The Civil Aviation Tribunal was established to provide an independent, fair, and efficient mechanism for resolving disputes involving civil aviation matters, such as:
  - Licensing of pilots and air operators.

  - Disputes between airlines and regulatory bodies.
- By formally recognizing the Tribunal as a department and assigning the Minister of Transport as the appropriate Minister and Minister under the Aeronautics Act, the government ensures:

This regulation establishes a clear administrative and financial framework for the Civil Aviation Tribunal by:
