### ðŸ“ **5. Reporting Requirements**

#### A. Annual Reporting (Schedule 3)
> Every person must submit **information** to the Minister **before using more than 10 grams** of a toxic substance (or product) in a calendar year.

- **Submission frequency**: Only **once per calendar year** per substance/product.
- **When to submit**: As soon as feasible **before** exceeding 10g of use.
- **Information required** (in Schedule 3):
  - Name and identity of the toxic substance/product
  - Anticipated period of use
  - Estimated quantity (in grams or other units) per year
  - Identification of each proposed or actual use
  - For products: estimated concentration of the substance

> ðŸ“Œ This ensures transparency and helps monitor exposure levels.

#### B. Special Reporting for Existing Users (Schedule 2.1)
> If a person was using a substance listed in **column 1 of Schedule 2.1** on a specific date (e.g., March 14, 2013), and used **more than 10g** of that substance in the calendar year of that date:
- Must submit the required information **within 60 days** of that date.
- Only once per year per substance.

> ðŸ“Œ This applies to legacy users who may have been operating before the regulation was fully implemented.

### ðŸ›¡ï¸ **6. Permits for Continued Use**

#### When Can a Permit Be Issued?
A permit under **section 10** may be granted to allow continued use of a prohibited substance **only if**:
1. There is **no technically or economically feasible alternative** (except substances regulated under these regulations).
2. The applicant has taken **measures to minimize or eliminate harm** to human health or the environment.
3. A **compliance plan** is submitted with a timeline of implementation **no longer than 3 years** from the permit date.

#### Conditions for Permit Denial
The Minister may **refuse** a permit if:
- The applicant provided **false or misleading information**.
- Required information under subsection 9(4) is **missing or insufficient**.

#### Permit Renewal
- A permit may be **renewed twice**.
- Renewal must follow the same conditions as the original permit.
- Renewal applications must include the same information as initial applications.

#### Permit Revocation
- A permit may be **revoked** if:
  - The conditions (e.g., no harm, compliance plan) are no longer met.
  - There is **false or misleading information**.
- **Before revocation**, the Minister must:
  - Provide **written reasons**.
  - Give the permit holder an **opportunity to respond in writing**.

> âš–ï¸ This ensures due process and accountability.
