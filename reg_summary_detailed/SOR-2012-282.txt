## üìö **Overview of the Insurable Housing Loan Regulations (2012)**

These regulations establish the **eligibility criteria and underwriting standards** for housing loans to be insured by the **Corporation (the Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation ‚Äì CMHC)** under the *National Housing Act*. The regulations apply to all housing loans **not previously insured** under a contract entered into before December 7, 2012.

The primary objective is to **ensure financial stability, protect borrowers, and promote responsible lending** by setting clear thresholds for loan structure, borrower qualifications, property value, debt levels, and creditworthiness.

## üîç Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *National Housing Act* |
| **Credit Score** | Issued by a federally or provincially incorporated credit reporting agency; must be substantially equivalent to scores used on June 26, 2011 |
| **Eligible Residential Property** | One to four family housing units (e.g., single-family homes, townhouses, condominiums), including associated rights, structures, and facilities |
| **First-Time Home Buyer** | An individual who has never owned a residential property in Canada; or who has been living separate and apart from a spouse/common-law partner for at least 90 days due to a breakdown of the relationship |

| **Gross Debt Service Ratio (GDS)** | % of gross annual income required to cover housing payments (e.g., mortgage, taxes, insurance) |
| **Total Debt Service Ratio (TDS)** | % of gross annual income required to cover all housing and other debt payments |
| **High Ratio Loan** | A loan where the principal amount (plus prior balances) exceeds 80% of the property value at approval |
| **Low Ratio Loan** | A loan where the principal amount (plus prior balances) is ‚â§80% of the property value at approval |
| **Newly Built** | Property never occupied for residential use (or only temporarily during interim possession) |

## üö¶ Scope and Applicability

- Applies to **all housing loans not previously insured** under a pre-existing CMHC-approved contract.
- A loan becomes a **new loan** for regulatory purposes if:
  - It is modified to **replace security with a new property and increase balance**, or
  - It involves a **modification requiring an additional insurance premium**.
- Such modifications are assessed **as if approved on the day of the change**, and must meet current criteria.
- **Exceptions**:
  - Modifications **not requiring additional insurance premiums** do not affect insurance eligibility.
  - The Corporation may provide insurance for loans in **loan workout programs** (to reduce losses) if:
    - It meets the original loan criteria (e.g., under paragraph 4(a))
    - No additional insurance premium is required

### ‚úÖ General Requirements for All Insured Loans
1. **Must be underwritten and administered by an approved lender** or held in a **Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP)** or **Registered Retirement Income Fund (RRIF)** administered by an approved lender.
2. **Must be secured in first or second priority** by an eligible residential property (i.e., no junior liens).

## üîç High Ratio Loan (HRL) Requirements

A high ratio loan is defined as one where the **loan-to-value (LTV) exceeds 80%** at approval.

| Requirement | Details |

| **Purpose** | Must either:<br>‚Ä¢ Purchase the property, or<br>‚Ä¢ Discharge a prior **uninsured low ratio loan** |
| **Amortization Period** | - Maximum **25 years**<br>- Can extend to **30 years** if: <br>‚ÄÉ‚Ä¢ Borrower is a **first-time home buyer**, or<br>‚ÄÉ‚Ä¢ Property is **newly built** |
| **Property Value Limit** | Must be **less than $1,500,000** |
| **Payment Recalculation** | If variable interest rates are allowed, payments must be **recalculated every 5 years** to align with original amortization schedule |
| **Start of Payments** | Must begin on:<br>‚Ä¢ Day of funding<br>‚Ä¢ Closing of purchase agreement<br>‚Ä¢ Completion of improvement/conversion |
| **Credit Score** | At least **one borrower or guarantor must have a credit score ‚â•600** |

| **Occupancy Requirement** | At least one family unit must be occupied by the borrower or a related person (spouse, common-law partner, parent-child) |
| **Reasonable Repayment Likelihood** | Must be reasonably likely to be repaid based on borrower‚Äôs income, debts, and obligations; based on **reasonable assumptions about peak payments** |
| **Securities Guarantee (Pool Loans)** | If part of a pool of loans used to issue marketable securities, **any securities issued after July 1, 2016 must be guaranteed under subsection 14(1) of the Act** |

### Exceptions to Credit Score Requirement (Paragraph 1(g))
- If **no more than 3%** of the lender‚Äôs **high ratio or low ratio loans** approved and funded during any of the following periods had **no borrower/guarantor with a score ‚â•600**:
  - First 4 quarters of the prior 5 quarters
  - First 4 quarters of the prior 6 quarters
  - First 4 quarters of the prior 7 quarters

> This allows for **temporary leniency** in credit scoring if a lender maintains a strong overall credit profile.

### Income Verification Requirement
- Lenders or CMHC must make **reasonable efforts** to verify borrower income and employment status.
- For self-employed borrowers, plausibility of reported income must be assessed.
