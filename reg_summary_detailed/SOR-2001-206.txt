**Detailed Summary of the Radiocommunication Act (Paragraph 9(1)(c)) Exemption Order**

---

**1. Legal Basis and Authority**  
This exemption order is issued under the authority of the *Radiocommunication Act* of Canada. Specifically, it is made by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Industry, pursuant to **subsection 3(2)a** of the Act. This provision allows the government to issue exemptions when certain provisions of the Act are not applicable to specific circumstances, particularly in the context of radio and telecommunications regulation.

---

**2. Purpose of the Order**  
The primary purpose of this Order is to **grant a legal exemption** from the application of **paragraph 9(1)(c)** of the *Radiocommunication Act* for specific Canadian government-operated vessels and ships.  

Paragraph 9(1)(c) of the Act generally prohibits the reception of encrypted subscription programming signals (such as cable or satellite TV content) without authorization. This exemption allows members of the Canadian Armed Forces and Department of National Defence (DND)-operated ships or vessels to receive such signals **without violating the Act**, provided certain conditions are met.

---

**3. Scope of the Exemption**  
- **Applicable Entities**: The exemption applies to **any ship or vessel** that is:
  - Under the **direction or control** of the **Department of National Defence (DND)**, and  
  - **Outside the coverage area** of any satellite that carries an **encrypted subscription programming signal** distributed by a **lawful distributor**.

> *Note*: This means the exemption only applies to government-operated vessels (e.g., naval ships, military transport, or DND-supported vessels) and only when they are operating beyond the reach of commercial satellite coverage that delivers encrypted content.

---

**4. Who is Exempt?**  
The exemption is granted to:  
- **Her Majesty in right of Canada**, as represented by the **Department of National Defence** and **members of the Canadian Forces**.  
This includes personnel serving on DND-operated vessels and authorized individuals on board such vessels during operations or missions.

---

**5. Conditions of the Exemption**  
The exemption is **not absolute** and comes with strict conditions:

- **Decoding Requirement**: If any encrypted subscription programming signal is **decoded** (i.e., decrypted or unblocked to view the content), such decoding must be:
  - Performed **under and in accordance with** an authorization from a person who:
    - Has a **lawful right to transmit** the signal, and  
    - Has the **legal authority to authorize its decoding**.

> This ensures that even though the vessel is exempt from the general prohibition on receiving encrypted signals, the act of decrypting the content is still subject to legal and regulatory oversight. Unauthorized or illicit decoding is prohibited.

---

**6. Effective Date**  
The Order comes into force on the **day it is registered** in the Canada Gazette. This is the standard method by which Canadian regulatory instruments become legally effective.

---

**7. Key Implications and Significance**  
- **Operational Flexibility**: Allows military personnel and DND vessels to access media content (such as news, educational programming, or entertainment) during long voyages or overseas missions, especially where satellite coverage is limited or unavailable.
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Balances operational needs with compliance with the *Radiocommunication Act*, ensuring that the exemption does not enable unauthorized access to copyrighted or encrypted content.
- **Security and Accountability**: The requirement for authorization before decoding ensures that such access is not abused and remains within the bounds of lawful use and government oversight.

---

**8. Limitations and Exclusions**  
- The exemption does **not apply** to:
  - Vessels not under DND control.
  - Vessels operating within the coverage area of a satellite carrying encrypted programming (unless otherwise authorized).
  - Any unauthorized or unlicensed decoding of signals.
- The exemption does **not extend** to private individuals or non-DND personnel on board.

---

**Conclusion**  
This Order is a targeted, narrowly defined exemption designed to support the operational and logistical needs of the Canadian Armed Forces by allowing them to receive encrypted subscription programming on government vessels abroad â€” while maintaining strict compliance with Canadian telecommunications law. It reflects a balance between national security, operational necessity, and regulatory integrity, ensuring that access to media content remains lawful, authorized, and accountable.
