**Detailed Summary of the Eastern Townships Wood Producers’ Levies (Interprovincial and Export Trade) Order**

---

### **1. Regulatory Context and Legal Basis**

This regulation is issued under the authority granted by **sections 3 and 4 of the Quebec Wood Order, 1983** (Order in Council P.C. 1983-2885, dated September 22, 1983). It is a subordinate legislative instrument that implements specific levies on wood producers operating in the **Eastern Townships Region of Quebec**, particularly when their wood is marketed **interprovincially or exported**.

- **Legal Source**: The Order is made pursuant to the *Quebec Wood Order, 1983*, which empowers the *Eastern Townships Region Wood Producers’ Syndicate* (the "Commodity Board") to establish and collect levies on wood trade.
- **Citation**: The Order is officially cited as the *Eastern Townships Wood Producers’ Levies (Interprovincial and Export Trade) Order*, published in the *Canada Gazette, Part II*, on **May 13, 1994** (SOR/85-1067), and originally established under SOR/83-713.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Order**

The primary objective of this Order is to:

- **Fix and impose levies** on producers in the Eastern Townships Region who market wood outside Quebec (i.e., in interprovincial or export trade).
- **Establish a mechanism for collecting these levies** from producers to fund specific programs and initiatives related to the wood industry.
- Ensure that producers contribute financially to the economic and operational sustainability of the regional wood sector through a structured, transparent levy system.

---

### **3. Key Definitions**

The Order defines several key terms to clarify scope and application:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Commodity Board** | Refers to the *Syndicat des producteurs de bois de l’Estrie* (Eastern Townships Region Wood Producers’ Syndicate). This body is responsible for administering the levies and managing the funds collected. |
| **Plan** | Refers to the *Plan conjoint des producteurs de bois de l’Estrie*, a regulatory framework under *Regulation M-35.1, r. 102* of the Quebec Regulation Code. This Plan sets out the rules governing participation, obligations, and cooperation among producers in the region. |
| **Producer** | A person or entity that is subject to the Plan and is involved in either the **production or marketing** of wood. This includes both those who grow timber and those who sell or distribute it. |
| **Wood** | Wood that is **produced within the Eastern Townships Region** and is **subject to the Plan**. This ensures that only regionally sourced wood is covered under the levy system. |

---

### **4. Levies Applicable to Producers**

Every producer who markets wood in **interprovincial or export trade** is required to pay the following levies:

1. **Regulation on Producer Contributions (Règlement sur les contributions des producteurs de bois de l’Estrie)**  
   - *Reference*: R.Q. c. M-35.1, r. 75.1  
   - Purpose: Funds general operational costs, administrative expenses, and shared industry activities.  
   - This levy is typically a percentage of the value of the wood marketed and is designed to support the overall functioning of the industry.

2. **Regulation on the Research and Protection Fund (Règlement sur le fonds de recherche et de protection des producteurs de bois de l’Estrie)**  
   - *Reference*: R.Q. c. M-35.1, r. 99  
   - Purpose: Supports **research initiatives** and **protection measures** for producers, such as environmental safeguards, sustainable forestry practices, and innovation in wood processing.  
   - This fund helps ensure long-term viability and competitiveness of the sector.

3. **Regulation on the Working Capital Fund (Règlement sur le fonds de roulement du Syndicat des producteurs de bois de l’Estrie)**  
   - *Reference*: R.Q. c. M-35.1, r. 100  
   - Purpose: Provides **short-term financial support** (working capital) to producers to cover operational costs such as transportation, storage, and marketing expenses.  
   - Helps producers manage cash flow, especially during periods of fluctuating demand or market volatility.

> These levies are **not fixed at a single rate** but are **amended periodically** in accordance with the applicable regulations, reflecting changes in market conditions, inflation, or industry needs.

---

### **5. Payment and Collection Requirements**

- **Who Pays**: All producers who are **subject to the Plan** and engage in **interprovincial or export marketing** of wood must pay the levies.
- **Where to Pay**: Levies must be paid to the **Commodity Board at its headquarters in Sherbrooke, Quebec**.
- **Payment Deadline**: Producers must remit the levies **within the time limits** specified in the relevant regulations (e.g., within 30 days of the end of a fiscal period or upon notification of sale).
- **Enforcement**: Failure to pay on time may result in penalties or administrative action by the Commodity Board.

---

### **6. Scope and Limitations**

- **Geographic Scope**: Applies only to wood **produced in the Eastern Townships Region** of Quebec.
- **Market Scope**: Applies only to wood marketed **outside Quebec** (interprovincial trade or export).
- **Exclusions**: Producers who do not participate in the Plan or who market wood solely within Quebec are not subject to these levies.

---

### **7. Oversight and Accountability**

- The **Commodity Board** (Syndicat) is responsible for:
  - Administering the levy system.
  - Ensuring compliance with payment deadlines.
  - Managing and reporting on how levy funds are used (e.g., research, protection, working capital).
- Funds collected are **not distributed directly to individual producers** but are pooled and used for collective industry benefits.

---

### **8. Legal and Regulatory Framework**

This Order operates within a broader regulatory ecosystem:

- The **Quebec Wood Order, 1983** provides the foundational legal authority.
- The **Plan conjoint des producteurs de bois de l’Estrie** sets out the operational framework for producer participation.
- The **three specific regulations** (r. 75.1, r. 99, r. 100) define the structure, rates, and purposes of the levies.

All regulations referenced are subject to periodic review and amendment to adapt to changing economic and environmental conditions.

---

### **Conclusion**

This Order establishes a **mandatory, regionally focused levy system** to support the economic sustainability and long-term development of the wood industry in the Eastern Townships of Quebec. By requiring producers to contribute to a shared fund for research, protection, and working capital, the Order promotes:

- Industry resilience,
- Environmental stewardship,
- Financial stability for producers engaged in out-of-province trade.

It represents a key example of **cooperative governance** in natural resource sectors, where regional producers pool resources to manage market risks and invest in sustainable development.

---

**Note**: This Order remains in force as of the date of publication and is subject to amendment or revocation through further Orders in Council or regulatory updates. Producers are advised to monitor updates to the referenced regulations for changes in levy amounts, payment schedules, or fund allocations.
