**Detailed Summary of the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC) Policy By-law (October 27, 1993)**

---

### **Overview**

The regulation in question is a **policy by-law** adopted by the **Board of Directors of the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC)** under **paragraph 11(2)(g)** and **subsection 18(3)** of the *Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation Act* (CDIC Act). It establishes the **form and provisions of the CDIC’s deposit insurance policy**, outlining the rights, obligations, and reporting requirements of member financial institutions.

> **Note:** This by-law was originally issued on **October 27, 1993**, and has since been **repealed or modified** by subsequent amendments, including **SOR/2008-293** (s. 2, s. 5, s. 6) and **SOR/2005-115** (s. 1). As such, it is **no longer in full force**, but this summary provides a detailed historical and structural analysis of its content and purpose as it existed at the time.

---

## **Key Legal Basis and Purpose**

- **Authority**: The by-law is enacted pursuant to:
  - **Paragraph 11(2)(g)** of the CDIC Act: Authorizing the Board to prescribe the form and content of the deposit insurance policy.
  - **Subsection 18(3)** of the CDIC Act: Empowering the Board to prescribe the terms of the policy of deposit insurance.

- **Purpose**:
  - To define the **scope, conditions, and obligations** of member financial institutions (e.g., banks, credit unions, trust companies) in relation to CDIC deposit insurance.
  - To ensure **transparency, financial stability, and accountability** by requiring regular reporting, compliance with governance and risk standards, and timely disclosure of material information.
  - To support **risk-based supervision** and **resolution planning** in the event of financial distress.

## **Core Obligations of Member Institutions**

### 1. **Deposit Insurance Coverage**
- CDIC insures deposits up to the **maximum limit** (historically $100,000 per depositor, per institution, per currency) as defined in the Act, by-laws, and this policy.

---

### 2. **Compliance with Laws and Regulations**
Member institutions must:
- Comply with the **CDIC Act**, other applicable federal and provincial laws, by-laws, their **incorporation documents**, and any **undertakings or agreements** with CDIC.

---

### 3. **Governance, Risk, and Capital Management**
Institutions must maintain:
- **Appropriate, effective, and prudent** practices in:
  - **Corporate governance**
  - **Risk management**
  - **Liquidity and capital management**
- **Robust operational controls** across all business functions.

---

### 4. **Premium Payments**
- **Annual Premium**: Paid in accordance with **section 21 of the CDIC Act**, due at times specified in **subsection 22(2)**.
- **Initial Membership Premium**: Paid in the year of membership under **section 23(1)**, due per **subsection 23(2)**.
- **Late Payments**: Interest accrues from the due date to the date of payment under **section 25**.
- **Premium Surcharge**: If assessed under **section 25.1**, the institution must pay the surcharge and any interest on it under **section 25.1(3)**.

---

### 5. **Return of Insured Deposits**
- Must be completed and **submitted by July 15 each year**, or at any time requested by CDIC.
- The institution must use a **method specified in the Return** to estimate the total insured deposits.

---

### 6. **Confidentiality of Prescribed Information**
- **General Rule**: Member institutions **shall not disclose prescribed information**.
- **Exceptions**:
  - Disclosure to **affiliates, directors, officers, employees, auditors, or legal counsel** is allowed **if confidentiality is ensured**.
  - Disclosure is **permitted without restriction** if:
    - The examiner **directly communicated** a rating (paragraph e) or information (paragraph h) to the institution.
  - This exception supports transparency in internal oversight and audit processes.

---

### 7. **Annual Financial Reporting (Within 120 Days After Year-End)**
Must provide:
- **Consolidated financial statements** (including comparative data from prior year), **approved by the board**, and **audited**.
- A **current list** of:
  - Subsidiaries and affiliates
  - Directors and officers (with titles, business addresses, email, phone numbers)

---

### 8. **Response to CDIC Requests (On Demand)**
When requested by CDIC for monitoring, compliance, or resolution planning, institutions must provide:
- **Financial statements** (consolidated/unconsolidated) for a specified period, including balance sheets for foreign branches.
- A **statement certified by an officer** containing:
  - Profile and analysis of insured and uninsured deposits
  - Details of deposits solicited via agents/brokers
  - Description of deposit data collection systems
  - Accounting and control systems for deposit liabilities
  - Detailed list of on- and off-balance sheet assets, liabilities, derivatives, and commitments
- A **business plan** (approved by board) covering the remainder of the financial year (from date of request to end of next year), within **60 days**.
- Any **other requested reports or documents**.

---

### 9. **Resolution Plan Information (Upon Request)**
If CDIC requests a resolution plan, institutions must provide:
- Audited or unaudited financial statements (consolidated and unconsolidated), including foreign branch balance sheets
- Current list of subsidiaries, affiliates, and group entities (with jurisdiction, licenses, regulatory authorities, and contact details)
- List of directors and officers of subsidiaries/affiliates (with contact details)
- **Legal corporate structure** (ownership relationships)
- **Financial interconnections** (e.g., intra-group exposures, guarantees, funding, cross-defaults, liquidity support)
- **Payment, clearing, and settlement systems**
- **Business functions and operations** (including outsourcing)
- **Business continuity and crisis management plans**
- Detailed on- and off-balance sheet positions
- Legal and regulatory frameworks
- Any other information relevant to the resolution plan

> This provision supports **stress testing, scenario planning, and systemic risk mitigation**.

---

### 10. **Analysis of Requested Information**
Institutions must provide **analysis** of any specific information requested in a resolution plan or compliance review, where applicable.

---

### 11. **Exemptions from Disclosure**
- Institutions are **not required** to disclose information if:
  - It is **prohibited by Canadian legislation** or
  - It is **prohibited by foreign jurisdictional law**

> This protects legal and regulatory compliance obligations.

---
