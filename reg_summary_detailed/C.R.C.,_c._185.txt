These regulations apply **only** to:

- A licence to buy tobacco **expires on June 30th of the year immediately following the year in which it was issued**.

### **6. Criteria for Issuance of a Licence**

A licence to buy tobacco may be **refused** if the applicant:
- **Lacks sufficient experience** in the buying or handling of flue-cured tobacco;  
- **Does not demonstrate adequate financial responsibility** (e.g., sufficient capital, creditworthiness, or financial history to support purchases);  
- **Does not possess appropriate equipment** (e.g., storage, handling, transportation, or quality inspection facilities) to safely and effectively engage in the buying of tobacco.

### **7. Grounds for Suspension, Revocation, or Refusal of Licence**

A licence to buy tobacco may be:
- **Suspended**,  
- **Revoked**, or  
- **Refused** (if not already issued)

in the following circumstances:
- **Failure to comply** with any provision of:
  - The *Farm Products Marketing Act*;  
  - Regulations made under the Act; or  
  - Any **order or direction issued by the Commodity Board**.

- Breaching quality standards;
- Failing to report required information;
- Engaging in fraudulent or unethical practices;
- Misusing tobacco or failing to maintain proper records;

This provision gives the Commodity Board significant enforcement power to maintain market integrity and protect the interests of tobacco growers.

3. **Transparency and Accountability**:

The *Ontario Flue-Cured Tobacco Licensing (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations* establish a structured, regulated framework for the buying and marketing of raw flue-cured tobacco produced in Ontario.
