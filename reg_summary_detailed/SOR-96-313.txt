### **Detailed Summary of the Contraventions Regulations (S.C. 1996, c. 7, s. 4)**

---

#### **1. Legal Authority and Purpose**
The Contraventions Regulations were issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Justice**, under **section 8a of the Contraventions Act**. These regulations came into force on **August 1, 1996**.

The primary purpose of these regulations is to **define specific offences** (known as *contraventions*) and to establish:
- The **short-form description** of each offence,
- The **maximum fine amount** applicable to each contravention,
- Special provisions for **young persons** and **quotas/limits**.

These rules streamline enforcement by allowing administrative or ticket-based penalties for violations, particularly in areas such as port operations, environmental protection, land use, and wildlife management.

---

#### **2. Structure of the Regulations**

The regulations are organized into **schedules**, each listing a column of offences. The structure follows a standardized format:

| Column I | Column II | Column III |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Provision in an enactment** | **Short-form description of the offence** | **Fine amount (or range)** |

Each row represents a distinct contravention. The key elements are:

---

#### **3. Key Provisions Explained**

##### **A. Designation of Offences as Contraventions**
Any violation of a provision listed in **Column I** of the Schedules is treated as a **contravention** under the *Contraventions Act*.

> This allows the government to apply a simplified legal process (e.g., through tickets) rather than full criminal proceedings.

---

##### **B. Short-Form Description (Column II)**
For administrative and procedural purposes, each offence is given a **clear, concise description** (e.g., "Enter, leave or proceed within a port or traffic control zone without having obtained a traffic clearance").

> This description is used in:
> - Ticketing notices,
> - Notices of violation,
> - Public awareness campaigns,
> - Enforcement records.

---

##### **C. Fine Amounts (Column III)**
The **amount of the fine** that may be imposed in proceedings initiated via a **ticket** is set in Column III.

- The fine is **not automatic** â€” it depends on the specific offence.
- Where multiple amounts are listed (e.g., base amount + additional amount), the **additional amount applies only if the quota or limit is exceeded**.

> Example:  
> If a fine has a base amount of $50 and an additional $100 if a limit is exceeded, then:
> - Only the base fine applies if the violation does not exceed the limit.
> - The additional fine applies **only when the first item that exceeds the quota/limit** is involved.

> This prevents punitive fines for minor or unintentional breaches.

---

##### **D. Special Rule for Young Persons**
If a contravention is committed by a **young person** (typically defined as someone under 18 or under 19, depending on jurisdictional interpretation), the fine is **deemed to be the lesser of**:
- **$100**, or  
- The amount specified in Column III.

> This reflects a policy of **proportionality and rehabilitation** for youth offenders.

### **4. Major Categories of Offences and Examples**

#### **I. Port and Waterway Operations**
These offences relate to safety, navigation, and environmental protection within ports, harbours, and the **Seaway** (a major waterway in Canada, particularly in Ontario).

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Enter/leave port without traffic clearance | Unauthorized entry/exit without permission |
| Fail to maintain communication with port authority | Risk to safety due to lack of contact |
| Fail to follow port procedures | Violation of established safety practices |
| Lack of radio frequency capacity | Inability to communicate with port control |
| Interfere with navigation | Actions that disrupt vessel movement |
| Cause silting or reduce water depth | Environmental degradation affecting navigation |
| Cause nuisance or damage to ships | Unacceptable impact on port infrastructure |
| Adversely affect air, soil, or water quality | Environmental harm |
| Interfere with authorized port activities | Disruption of legitimate operations |

> *Note:* Both the **public port** and **private port** (e.g., Seaway) are covered. The rules are applied consistently across different types of facilities.

---

#### **II. Environmental and Watercourse Protection**
Offences related to waterways and natural systems.

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Divert river/stream flow | Alters natural hydrology |
| Causes or affects currents | Disrupts natural water movement |
| Causes silting or accumulation | Reduces navigability and ecosystem health |
| Reduces water depth | Impacts vessel access and safety |

> These are serious environmental violations with direct consequences for ecosystems and infrastructure.

---

#### **III. Reporting Obligations (Critical Safety Rules)**
Failure to report incidents promptly may result in fines.

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Fail to notify port official of: | - Injury to a person<br>- Environmental damage<br>- Material loss<br>- Explosion<br>- Fire<br>- Accident<br>- Grounding/stranding |
| Fail to provide: | - Description of spilled material<br>- Location of spill<br>- Efforts made to remove spill |

> These are **mandatory reporting requirements** to ensure public safety and environmental protection.

---

#### **IV. Storage Tanks and Infrastructure**
Prohibits unauthorized construction, modification, or removal of storage tanks.

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Build/install/modify/replace/dismantle/decommission a tank without authorization | Unauthorized structural changes |
| Fail to comply with conditions of authorization | Violation of approved terms (e.g., spill prevention, containment) |

> Requires **written authorization** from the relevant authority (e.g., port or environmental agency).

---

#### **V. Excavation and Material Removal**
Prohibits unauthorized digging or removal of materials.

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Excavate or remove material without authorization | Unauthorized land disturbance |
| Fail to comply with conditions of authorization | Violation of approved plans or safety measures |

> Prevents environmental damage and structural instability.

---

#### **VI. Cargo Transhipment and Loading/Unloading**
Restricts where cargo can be loaded, unloaded, or transhipped.

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Tranship, load, or unload cargo outside designated areas | Unauthorized movement of goods |
| Fail to comply with conditions of authorization | Breach of approved procedures |

> Ensures cargo handling occurs in safe, controlled, and designated zones.

---

#### **VII. Navigation Devices**
Prohibits unauthorized placement, alteration, or removal of devices used for navigation.

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Place, alter, remove, or relocate navigation device without authorization | Disrupts maritime safety systems |

> These devices are essential for safe vessel movement.

---

#### **VIII. Construction and Structures**
Prohibits building or modifying structures without authorization.

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Build, place, rebuild, repair, alter, move, or remove any structure without authorization | Unauthorized development |

> Applies to all types of structures, including temporary ones.

---

#### **IX. Fire and Blasting Activities**
Prohibits unauthorized use of fire or explosives.

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Cause a fire or explosion without authorization | Risk to public safety |
| Conduct blasting or set off fireworks without authorization | High-risk activities |

> Requires strict permits and conditions to prevent accidents.

---

#### **X. Harbours and Waterways (General)**
Similar to port offences but applied more broadly to **harbours**.

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Obstruct or threaten harbour areas | Physical blockage |
| Interfere with authorized activities | Disruption of legitimate operations |

> Covers both physical and environmental impacts.

---

#### **XI. Wildlife and Natural Resources (Protected Areas)**
Enforces rules to protect flora, fauna, and archaeological sites.

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Disturbing, removing, defacing, damaging, or destroying archaeological sites or historical resources | Cultural heritage protection |
| Removing or damaging flora, fauna, or natural objects | Biodiversity protection |
| Touching, feeding, or enticing wildlife | Harmful human-wildlife interaction |
| Using poison, gas, or drugs to capture/injure/destroy wildlife | Illegal and dangerous practices |
| Possessing prohibited poisons | Illegal possession of hazardous materials |
| Bringing domestic animals into buildings | Risk of disease or disturbance |

> These provisions aim to preserve ecological and cultural integrity.

---

#### **XII. Land Use and Permits**
Regulates land access and use, especially in parks and protected areas.

| Offence | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Using land, occupying land, camping, residing, or erecting structures without a permit | Unauthorized land use |
| Permitting another person to use/occupy/reside/camp/erect a structure for valuable consideration | Illegal sharing or commercial use |
| Entering a park within one year of removal | Re-entry after removal |

> Prevents unauthorized occupation and ensures proper land management.
