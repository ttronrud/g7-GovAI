### **Overview**
This regulation, originally enacted on March 15, 1986, and published in *Part I of the Canada Gazette*, establishes the rules governing **special customs services**â€”specifically, services performed by customs officers outside their normal duties or locations. It was introduced under the authority of **sections 166 and 167 of the Customs Act**, following a public consultation period during which stakeholders were given the opportunity to submit comments.

> **Important Note**: This regulation has been **repealed** by *SOR/2024-41, section 10*, effective as of the date of the repeal. Therefore, it is no longer in force and does not apply to current customs operations.

## **Key Definitions and Provisions**

### 1. **Definition of "Chief Officer of Customs"**
- The term **"chief officer of customs"** refers to the **manager of the customs office or offices** serving a particular area or place.
- This role is central to determining jurisdiction and responsibilities in relation to special services.

## **Fees for Special Services**

### A. **General Fee Structure (for officers not listed in Section 5)**  
Every person requesting a special service from an officer **not specified in Section 5** must pay:

- **$54** for the **first two hours or portion thereof** spent on the service  
- **$27 per additional hour or portion** of service time

### B. **No Charges for Meals and Rest**
- **No fee** is charged for time spent by officers on **meals or rest breaks** during the performance of a special service.

---

### C. **Multiple Officers Required**
- If **more than one officer** is involved in providing a special service, the **total chargeable time** is the **sum of all individual service times**.
- This means fees are calculated based on aggregate effort, not per officer.

---

## **Responsibility for Transportation, Accommodation, and Meals**

### A. **General Duty of the Requesting Person**
- The person requesting the special service must provide:
  - Transportation  
  - Accommodation  
  - Meals  

> This is required **unless** the service is being performed under exceptional circumstances.

---

### B. **Quality Standard**
- The provided transportation, accommodation, and meals must be **comparable in quality** to what would normally be authorized under the **Treasury Board Travel Directive** at the time of the service.

> This ensures fairness and consistency with government travel standards.

---

### C. **Fees When Provision Is Not Made**

If the requesting person fails to provide necessary support (transportation, accommodation, meals), the person **must pay**:

#### 1. **For Accommodation, Meals, or Transportation Not Provided**
- The charge is based on the **rates and allowances** set out in the **Treasury Board Travel Directive** in effect at the time of the service.

#### 2. **For Use of Private or Government-Owned Conveyance**
- If the officer must use:
  - A **privately owned vehicle**, or  
  - A **government-owned or leased vehicle**
  
> The requester must pay the **greater of**:
- The allowance for private vehicle use under the Treasury Board Travel Directive, **or**
- **$5**

> Example: If the travel allowance is $10, the requester pays **$10** (the higher amount).

---

## **Security Requirement for Payment**

### When Is Security Required?
- If the **chief officer of customs** determines that **security is needed** to guarantee payment of fees incurred during the performance of a special service, the requesting person **must provide security**.

### How?
- Security must comply with the **Financial Security (Electronic Means) Regulations**.
- The amount of security is **determined by the Minister of National Revenue**.

> This provision protects the government from non-payment risks in high-value or high-risk situations (e.g., urgent cross-border shipments or sensitive operations).

## **Regulatory Context and Authority**

- The regulation was made under **sections 166 and 167 of the Customs Act**, which empower the Minister of National Revenue to establish rules for special customs services.
- The process included **public notice and opportunity for comment**, ensuring transparency and stakeholder input before finalization.

## **Key Objectives of the Regulation**

| Objective | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Cost Recovery** | Ensures that the government recovers reasonable costs when officers perform non-routine duties. |
| **Operational Flexibility** | Allows officers to respond to urgent or off-hours needs without violating standard procedures. |

| **Humanitarian Exceptions** | Exempts life-saving or human remains-related releases from fees. |
| **Risk Management** | Requires security for high-risk or high-cost services. |

This regulation established a clear, structured framework for when, how, and at what cost customs officers could perform special services beyond their regular duties.
