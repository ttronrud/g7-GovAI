## üìö OVERVIEW OF THE REGULATION

This is a **bilateral social security agreement** between **Canada** and the **Republic of Latvia**, designed to ensure that individuals who have worked or lived in both countries are entitled to **social security benefits** (such as pensions, disability benefits, and survivor benefits) under the laws of either country, without discrimination or undue reduction due to cross-border residence or employment.

The agreement is rooted in **Article 27 of the Agreement on Social Security** and was formally activated on **November 1, 2006**, following a legal and procedural process involving parliamentary approval and the exchange of mutual notifications confirming compliance with entry-into-force requirements.

#### a) **Primary Legislation Covered**

  - Canada Pension Plan (CPP)

  - Act on State Social Insurance
  - Act on State Pensions
  - Act on State Funded Pensions

The Agreement applies to:

1. Any person who:
   - Is or has been subject to the legislation of Canada or Latvia.
2. Any person who derives rights under a Party‚Äôs legislation from someone described in (1).

### 5. **ELIGIBILITY AND BENEFIT RIGHTS**

#### a) **General Principle**
> Subject to Article 7‚Äì9:  
> A person who is or has been subject to the legislation of one Party is **eligible for benefits under the other Party‚Äôs legislation under the same conditions as a national of that Party**.

#### b) **No Reduction Due to Residence**
- A benefit under a Party‚Äôs legislation **shall not be reduced, suspended, or cancelled** simply because the person resides in the territory of the other Party.
- Benefits are paid **when the person is in the territory of the other Party**.

#### c) **Benefits in Third Countries**
- A person residing in a **third country** shall receive the benefit under the legislation of a Party **on the same terms and in the same amount** as a national of that Party residing in that third country.

---

### 6. **CONTRIBUTION AND EMPLOYMENT RULES**

#### a) **Employed Persons**
- An employed person working in the territory of a Party is **subject only to that Party‚Äôs legislation** for that employment.

#### b) **Self-Employed Persons**
- A self-employed person who resides in one Party and carries out self-employment in the other Party (or both) is **subject only to the legislation of the first Party** (where residence is established).

#### c) **Employment Abroad (Temporary Assignments)**
- An employee sent by an employer with a business presence in one Party to work in the other Party is **subject only to the legislation of the first Party**, as if working within its territory.
- This coverage is **limited to 60 months** without prior consent from both competent authorities.

---

### 7. **EXCLUSIONS AND EXCEPTIONS**

- **Diplomatic & Consular Personnel**:
  - A person employed by a diplomatic or consular mission of the other Party is subject only to the legislation of the **first Party** (where the mission is located).
  - **Exception**: If the person has previously contributed under the legislation of the employing Party, they may, within **6 months of employment or entry into force of the Agreement (whichever is later)**, choose to be subject only to the legislation of the **employing Party**.

- **Common Agreement**:
  - The competent authorities may, by mutual agreement, make **exceptions** to Articles 6‚Äì8 for any person or category of persons.

### 8. **CREDITABLE PERIODS AND ELIGIBILITY CALCULATIONS**

#### a) **OAS Act ‚Äì Residence in Latvia**
- If a person is subject to CPP or provincial pension plans during a period of residence in Latvia:
  - That period counts as **residence in Canada** for the person and their spouse/common-law partner/dependants (who are not subject to Latvia‚Äôs legislation due to employment or self-employment).

- If a person is subject to Latvia‚Äôs legislation during a period in Canada:
  - That period **does not count** as residence in Canada for the person or their dependants.

#### b) **Conditions for "Subject to Legislation"**
- A person is considered "subject to":
  - **Canada Pension Plan** only if they **make contributions** due to employment or self-employment in Latvia.
  - **Latvian legislation** only if they or their employer make contributions due to employment or self-employment in Canada.

#### c) **Totalizing of Creditable Periods**
- If a person is not eligible due to insufficient creditable periods, eligibility is determined by **totalizing**:
  - All creditable periods from both countries.
  - Periods specified in Articles 2‚Äì5 (e.g., residence, employment).
  - **But only if the periods do not overlap**.
