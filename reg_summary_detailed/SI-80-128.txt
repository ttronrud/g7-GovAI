**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

### **Regulatory Context and Background**

This regulation, issued under the **Northern Pipeline Act** by the **Northern Pipeline Agency (Designated Officer)**, addresses the modification of the terms and conditions governing the construction and operation of a natural gas pipeline in southern British Columbia. The specific action involves:

- **Rescinding** certain obligations originally included in **Condition 7 of Schedule III** to the Northern Pipeline Act.
- **Adding new socio-economic and environmental terms and conditions** tailored specifically to Foothills Pipe Lines (South B.C.) Ltd. ("Foothills").
- The regulation is part of a broader regulatory framework designed to ensure that pipeline development in Canada respects environmental protection, Indigenous rights, community interests, and economic equity.

The original Certificate of Public Convenience and Necessity was issued on **April 13, 1978**, to Foothills (South B.C.) for a pipeline route in southern British Columbia. The regulatory action stems from earlier public consultations and undertakings made during a 1976 hearing before the **National Energy Board (NEB)** regarding the "Alaska Highway Pipeline Project," which included commitments related to social, economic, and environmental matters.

Under **subsection 21(2)** of the Northern Pipeline Act, such undertakings are deemed to be terms or conditions of the certificate and are incorporated into **Schedule III** â€” the regulatory framework governing pipeline operations.

Under **subsection 20(4)**, the Designated Officer (Northern Pipeline Agency) has the authority to **rescind, amend, or add** to these terms and conditions with the approval of the Governor in Council.

### **Key Actions Taken in This Regulation**

#### 1. **Rescission of Condition 7 of Schedule III (in part)**
- The Designated Officer **rescinds** the original Condition 7 of Schedule III **as it applies to Foothills (South B.C.)**.
- This condition had previously included broad undertakings related to:
  - Social and economic development
  - Environmental protection
  - Fisheries, agriculture, and wildlife
- However, **one specific undertaking is preserved**:  
  â†’ The **compensation undertaking** listed in **Item 7 under "Compensation" in Appendix 5-2, page 9 of Volume 3 of the NEBâ€™s Reasons for Decision (June 1977)**.

> This means that while general social and environmental commitments are removed, the obligation to provide compensation (likely to landowners or affected communities) remains in force.

#### 2. **Addition of New Terms and Conditions**
A comprehensive new set of **Socio-Economic and Environmental Terms and Conditions** is added to Schedule III and becomes binding on Foothills for the specified pipeline segment.

These terms are titled:  
ðŸ‘‰ **"Northern Pipeline Socio-Economic and Environmental Terms and Conditions for Southern British Columbia"**

They apply only to the **specific portion of the pipeline** running from:
- Near **Coleman** on the Alberta-British Columbia border
- In a **south-westerly direction** across southern BC
- To the **International Boundary Line** near **Kingsgate**, USA

This ensures geographic specificity and avoids overreach into other regions.

### **Definitions in the Regulation**

The regulation includes precise definitions of key terms used throughout, including:
- **Agricultural land** (cleared, under cultivation, or in reserves)
- **Agricultural land reserve** (under BCâ€™s Agricultural Land Commission Act)
- **Big game animals**, **fur-bearing animals**, **wildlife**, **rare/endangered species**
- **Borrow pits** (excavation sites for fill material)
- **Construction** (activities from clearing to opening; excludes survey lines)
- **Environmental impact** (any change in physical or biological conditions)
- **Facility** (temporary or permanent installations)
- **Fuel** (hydrocarbons and petroleum products)
- **Hazardous material** (toxic, explosive, or damaging substances)
- **Indian** (as defined under the Indian Act)
- **Inspection and monitoring** (verification and data collection)
- **Operation** (activities after pipeline opening)
- **Waterbodies** (lakes, rivers, streams, beds)
- **Waste** (solid, liquid, human, or industrial debris)

All other terms are interpreted as per the Northern Pipeline Act.

### **Core Obligations Imposed on Foothills**

#### 1. **Compliance with Laws**
Foothills must comply with:
- Federal laws (Canada)
- Provincial laws (British Columbia)

#### 2. **Contract Requirements**
- Any contract with a contractor (or subcontractor) must **require compliance** with these terms and conditions.
- Foothills may apply for **exemption** from compliance in rare cases, but only with written approval from the Designated Officer.

#### 3. **Liability for Contractor Breaches**
- Foothills is **personally liable** for any breach by contractors or subcontractors â€” as if the breach had been committed directly by Foothills.

#### 4. **Cooperation with Regulatory Oversight**
- Must provide **reasonable assistance** and **information** to the Designated Officer and relevant provincial agencies.
- Government employees (especially provincial) have **reasonable access** to pipeline right-of-way and construction areas.

#### 5. **Indemnification and Damage Liability**
- Foothills must:
  - **Save, indemnify, and keep harmless** Her Majesty (the Crown)
  - Against claims arising from:
    - Construction or operation of the pipeline
    - Gas leaks, explosions, or spills on Crown land
    - Acts or omissions by Foothills or its agents
    - Acts or omissions by Crown officials (excluding gross negligence)

- Must **pay any losses or damages** incurred by the Crown due to pipeline-related events.

#### 6. **Emergency Preparedness**
- Must establish **emergency procedures** satisfactory to the Designated Officer.
- Designate and train personnel to manage emergencies (e.g., fires, spills).
- Must **immediately notify** the Designated Officer and provincial agency of any emergency.
- In the case of a **forest fire**, must provide:
  - Personnel
  - Equipment or aircraft for suppression

#### 7. **Public Consultation and Information Sharing**
- Must provide **information** to:
  - Government of Canada
  - Government of BC
  - Nearby communities
  - Indigenous, MÃ©tis, and non-status Indigenous organizations
  - Special interest groups (only if they have a "bona fide need")

> Information must be provided in a **form and manner satisfactory to the Designated Officer** and must include:
> - Route alignment and construction schedule
> - Expected environmental and community impacts
> - Economic opportunities
> - Use of land/waterbodies
> - Potential benefits to local businesses
> - Any other matter specified by the Designated Officer

- Must **facilitate public access** to this information and ensure **local communities have equitable access**.

- Must **consult** with communities, Indigenous groups, and special interest groups upon request.

- Must **report significant results** of consultations to the Designated Officer.

- Must **identify and assign a liaison person** to:
  - Disseminate information
  - Arrange community consultations
  - Perform liaison duties

### **Regulatory Oversight and Review Mechanisms**

- **Plan Approval Process**:
  - Foothills must submit **plans** (e.g., information plan, orientation plan, opportunity measures plan) for approval.
  - Once approved, these plans become binding.
  - The Designated Officer may **modify or vary** any plan with reasonable notice.
  - Foothills must submit a **Plan Schedule** within 30 days (or later as directed), listing when each plan will be submitted.

- **Review and Modification Rights**:
  - The Designated Officer retains the authority to **review, revise, or reject** any plan or proposal.
