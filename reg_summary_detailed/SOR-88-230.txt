### **Detailed Summary of the Frontier Lands Registration Regulations (1988)**

The **Frontier Lands Registration Regulations**, made under the *Canada Petroleum Resources Act* (S.C. 1986, c. 45), came into force on **April 28, 1988**, following a public notice and opportunity for comment published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **January 23, 1988**. These regulations establish a formal system for the **administration, registration, and record-keeping of interests and instruments in relation to frontier lands** in Canada—specifically, lands that are not yet fully developed for petroleum or mineral exploration and development.

These regulations are designed to ensure **transparency, accountability, and legal certainty** in the ownership, transfer, and management of rights over frontier lands, particularly in relation to petroleum and mineral interests. They define the responsibilities of the **Registrar**, the **procedures for registration**, the **information to be recorded**, and the **fees applicable** to such services.

---

## **1. Legal Basis and Authority**

- The regulations are made under:
  - **Section 9(1)** and **95(1)** of the *Canada Petroleum Resources Act* (C.P.R.A.)
  - **Section 100** and **107** of the C.P.R.A.
- The Governor in Council enacted them on the recommendation of:
  - The **Minister of Energy, Mines and Resources**
  - The **Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development**
- The regulations may be cited as the **Frontier Lands Registration Regulations**.

---

## **2. Role and Structure of the Registrar**

- The **Registrar** is responsible for supervising the operation of the registration system established under **Part VIII of the Canada Petroleum Resources Act**.
- In the absence of the Registrar, the **Deputy Registrar** performs the duties and powers of the Registrar.
- If no person is designated as Registrar, the Deputy Registrar assumes full responsibilities.

### **Key Responsibilities of the Registrar:**

| Responsibility | Description |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Maintain the Register** | Prepares and enters abstracts of interests and instruments into a central register. |
| **Maintain the Daybook** | Records all documents received, including type, date, submitter, and time of receipt. |
| **Stamps Documents** | Each document submitted is stamped with the date and time of receipt. |
| **Keep Original Records** | Maintains original copies of all registered interests and instruments, the register, and the daybook. |
| **Correct Errors** | Corrects omissions or inaccuracies in abstracts when discovered. |
| **Cancel Errors** | Cancels registrations made in error and notifies affected parties. |
| **Issue Certified Copies** | Provides certified exact copies upon request and payment of fees. |

---

## **3. Registration of Interests and Instruments**

### **Abstract of an Interest**
When an interest is registered, the Registrar must prepare a detailed **abstract** and immediately enter it into the register. The abstract must include:

1. **Type, effective date, and term** of the interest  
2. **Name(s) of interest owner(s)**  
   - If multiple holders, names of each holder and the name of their **designated representative** (as per section 9 of the Act)  
3. **Particulars of shares** in the interest  
4. **Description of the frontier lands, geological formations, or substances** to which the interest applies  

> ⚠️ *Note: This section was repealed by SOR/2007-226, section 2.*

### **Additional Notations in Abstracts**
The abstract must also contain:

- A **notation of any instrument** registered under **section 91** of the Act (i.e., instruments applying to the interest by virtue of that section).
- **Changes** to information recorded in the abstract (excluding corrections under subsection 9(2)).
- **Orders, decisions, or actions** of the Minister that are expressly subject to **section 106** of the Act.
- **Orders under section 12** of the Act.
- **Notices under subsections 36(1) or 105(1)** of the Act.
- **Extensions of the interest term** under any provision of the Act.

### **Consolidation of Interests**
When two or more interests are consolidated under **subsection 25(3)** or **section 39** of the Act:
- The Registrar prepares a **new abstract** of the consolidated interest.
- This abstract includes **all notations** from the original abstracts being consolidated.

---

## **4. Record-Keeping and Access**

### **Daybook**
- A chronological record maintained at the Registrar’s office.
- Includes:
  - Type and date of document
  - Name of person submitting
  - Date and time of receipt
- If a document is **refused registration**, the refusal is recorded immediately.
- If a document is **accepted**, the registration is recorded, and the daybook is updated.

### **Public Access and Inspection**
- Any person may inspect:
  - The **daybook**
  - The **register**
  - Copies of registered interests or instruments
- Access is granted **on request and upon payment of the prescribed fee** (as set out in the Schedule).

### **Certified Copies**
- The Registrar must provide a **certified exact copy** of:
  - Any registered interest or instrument
  - Any abstract of an interest
- The Registrar may certify that an **enlarged print from a microfilm or electronic/magnetic recording** is an exact copy.

---

## **5. Cancellation and Corrections**

- The Registrar may **cancel a registration in error**.
- Must **notify**:
  - The interest owner
  - The representative (if appointed under section 9)
  - Any other person directly affected by the cancellation

- If any required information is **missing or incorrect**, the Registrar must **correct it promptly** upon discovery.
- A **corrected abstract** is sent to:
  - The interest owner or representative
  - The **secured party** under any security notice related to the interest, **if not fully discharged** at the time of correction

---

## **6. Appointment and Termination of Representatives**

- Interest holders may appoint a **representative** under subsection 9(1) of the Act.
- A notice of appointment must be submitted by **each interest holder** to the Registrar.
- Any change in the **name or address** of the representative must be reported **immediately**.
- To terminate the appointment, interest holders must provide a **notice of termination** to the Registrar. The termination becomes effective upon receipt.

> This ensures that there is a clear legal representative for communication and administrative purposes, especially when dealing with complex ownership structures.

---

## **7. Jurisdiction and Service of Notices**

- **Concurrent Original Jurisdiction**:
  - The **Federal Court**, the **Court of Queen’s Bench of Alberta**, and the **Superior Court of Justice of Ontario** have concurrent original jurisdiction over disputes involving frontier lands under Part VIII of the Act.

- **Service of Notices**:
  - Unless otherwise specified in the Act, notices from the Minister or Registrar must be served by:
    - **Personal service**, or
    - **Registered mail** sent to the **latest address filed** with the Registrar.

---

## **8. Surrender of Interests**

- An interest may be **surrendered** with respect to:
  - All or any portion of the frontier lands subject to the interest
- **Surrender of full interest**:
  - Must be executed by the interest owner or each holder (if more than one)
- **Surrender of a portion**:
  - Must be executed by the owner or each holder of shares in that portion

Upon surrender:
- The Registrar must:
  - **Endorse a memorandum** on the original copy of the interest
  - **Make a notation of surrender** in the abstract

---

## **9. Fees and Payment**

- Fees are set out in a **Schedule** attached to the regulations.
- The fee for each service is specified in **Column II** of the schedule, corresponding to the service listed in **Column I**.
- Fees are payable **at the time the service is rendered**.
- All fees are payable to the **Receiver General of Canada**.

> This ensures transparency and accountability in financial transactions related to registration and administration.

---

## **10. Key Principles and Objectives**

| Principle | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Transparency** | All registrations, changes, and notices are recorded and accessible to the public. |
| **Accountability** | The Registrar maintains detailed records and is required to correct errors and notify affected parties. |
| **Legal Certainty** | Clear rules govern the registration, modification, and termination of interests. |
| **Efficiency** | Standardized procedures reduce administrative burden and ensure consistency. |
| **Public Access** | Citizens and stakeholders can inspect records and obtain certified copies. |

---

## **11. Repeal and Amendments**

- The requirement to include a description of shares and portions of frontier lands in the abstract was **repealed** by **SOR/2007-226, section 2**, indicating that the regulation has been updated or superseded by newer legislation or administrative practices.

---
