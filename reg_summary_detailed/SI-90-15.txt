**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

This regulation establishes the deployment of Canadian military personnel to support the **United Nations Observer Group in Central America (ONUCA)**, in accordance with a United Nations Security Council resolution and Canada’s commitment to international peacekeeping efforts.

---

### **Background and Legal Basis**

1. **United Nations Security Council Resolution 644 (1989)**  
   - On November 7, 1989, the UN Security Council adopted Resolution 644, which authorized the creation of the **United Nations Observer Group in Central America (ONUCA)**.  
   - The primary mandate of ONUCA is to conduct **on-site verification** of two key objectives:
     - The **cessation of aid** to irregular forces and insurrectionist movements in Central America.
     - The **cessation of the use of one State’s territory** for attacks on other States within the region.  
   - This resolution was a response to ongoing regional instability and armed conflict, particularly involving state-sponsored insurgencies and cross-border attacks.

2. **Canada’s Commitment to ONUCA**  
   - The **Secretary-General of the United Nations** formally requested Canada to provide specific military support to ONUCA.  
   - Canada has accepted this request and committed to supplying:
     - **Military aircrew and maintenance personnel**
     - **Communications personnel**
     - **Headquarters personnel**  
   - This support is critical for ONUCA’s operational effectiveness, particularly in monitoring and verifying compliance with the ceasefire and non-aggression mandates.

3. **Policy Justification**  
   - It is considered advisable and in Canada’s national interest to contribute to the on-site verification efforts in Central America.  
   - This involvement supports broader international efforts to promote peace, stability, and the rule of law in a region affected by prolonged conflict.

---

### **Legal Authority and Implementation**

- The regulation is issued under **paragraph 31(1)(b)** of the **National Defence Act** (R.S., c. 31 (1st Supp.), s. 60 (Sch. I, item 14)).  
  - This provision authorizes the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Minister of National Defence) to place Canadian Forces personnel on active service **beyond Canada’s borders** for peacekeeping, monitoring, or other international operations, provided such service is in accordance with international obligations or agreements.

- The personnel being deployed include:
  - **Officers and non-commissioned members of the Regular Force** of the Canadian Forces
  - **Officers and non-commissioned members of the Reserve Force** of the Canadian Forces  
  - These individuals must be **part of or serving in immediate support of ONUCA** operations.

---

### **Nature and Scope of Deployment**

- The deployment is **temporary and mission-specific**, focused on:
  - On-site verification of compliance with the ceasefire and non-aggression obligations.
  - Monitoring of military and logistical activities that could support insurgent movements or cross-border attacks.
- Personnel will operate in **Central America**, under the supervision and coordination of the United Nations.
- The deployment does **not** involve combat operations or the use of force; it is strictly observational and verification-based.

---

### **Key Objectives of the Regulation**

1. **Support UN Peacekeeping Efforts**  
   - Canada’s contribution strengthens the credibility and effectiveness of ONUCA by providing essential logistical, technical, and operational support.

2. **Promote Regional Stability**  
   - By verifying the cessation of aid to insurgents and attacks, Canada helps prevent escalation of violence and supports long-term peace in Central America.

3. **Demonstrate International Commitment**  
   - The regulation reflects Canada’s role as a responsible global actor in peace and security matters, fulfilling its obligations under international law and multilateral agreements.

4. **Compliance with Domestic Legal Framework**  
   - The deployment is authorized through a clear, established legal pathway under the National Defence Act, ensuring accountability and adherence to Canadian law.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation formally authorizes the **deployment of Canadian military personnel beyond Canada’s borders** to support the United Nations Observer Group in Central America (ONUCA). It is grounded in a UN Security Council resolution, Canada’s voluntary commitment to peacekeeping, and domestic legal authority under the National Defence Act. The mission is strictly observational and verification-based, aimed at promoting peace, stability, and compliance with international ceasefires in Central America. The deployment underscores Canada’s ongoing role in global peace and security initiatives.
