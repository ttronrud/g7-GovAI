**Detailed Summary of the Alexander First Nation Treaty Land Entitlement Remission Order**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Purpose**

This regulation, known as the *Alexander First Nation Treaty Land Entitlement Remission Order*, was issued under the authority of **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**. It is based on **subsection 23(2)a of the Financial Administration Act**, which allows the government to grant tax remissions in the public interest.

The primary purpose of this Order is to **remit (i.e., forgive or cancel)** certain taxes, interest, and penalties that were incurred or would have been incurred by the **Alexander First Nation (Alexander)** in connection with specific transactions related to its treaty land entitlements. This remission is intended to support the implementation of a historic settlement agreement and to recognize the financial impact of acquiring land under that agreement.

---

### **2. Statutory Basis**

- **Legal Source**: The Order is made under **section 7(2) of S.C. 1991, c. 24, the Excise Tax Act**.
- The Excise Tax Act governs excise taxes on certain transactions, including land transfers and the acquisition of property interests.
- The remission applies specifically to taxes under **Division II of Part IX** of the Excise Tax Act, which deals with excise taxes on the supply of land or property interests.

---

### **3. Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined to ensure clarity and consistency in the application of the Order:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Excise Tax Act*. |
| **Alexander** | The *Alexander Indian Band No. 438*, also known as the *Alexander First Nation*. |
| **Settlement Agreement** | The *Alexander First Nation Treaty Land Entitlement Settlement Agreement*, signed on **September 3, 1998** by Alexander and on **October 13, 1998** by Her Majesty the Queen in right of Canada. This agreement established the terms under which Alexander acquired land. |
| **Settlement Land** | Land (including any mines and minerals beneath or within it) that was **purchased or acquired by or on behalf of Alexander** pursuant to the Settlement Agreement. |
| **Third Party Interest** | As defined in **Article 1.1(cc)** of the Settlement Agreement — refers to any interest (e.g., lease, ownership, or right) held by a third party in settlement land. |
| **Other Terms** | All other terms are interpreted as in **subsection 123(1)** of the Excise Tax Act, which provides general definitions of common legal and financial terms. |

---

### **4. Scope of Remission**

The Order grants a **full remission** (i.e., cancellation or forgiveness) of the following:

#### **(a) Excise Taxes and Related Charges**
Remission is granted for the following types of transactions:

1. **Supply of Settlement Land**  
   - The tax paid or payable under Division II of Part IX of the Excise Tax Act on the value of consideration paid or payable by Alexander or its agent for the **supply (transfer) of settlement land**.  
   - **Limitation**: The total amount of settlement land involved must not exceed **10,000 acres** cumulatively.

2. **Supply or Cancellation in Favour of Alexander of a Third Party Interest**  
   - Tax remission applies when Alexander or its agent receives or is granted a **third-party interest** in settlement land (e.g., through a transfer or cancellation of a lease or right held by a third party).

3. **Supply of Tangible Personal Property on Settlement Land**  
   - Tax remission applies when Alexander or its agent acquires **tangible personal property** (e.g., buildings, equipment) that is **situated on settlement land at the time of acquisition**.  
   - **Condition**: Title to the tangible personal property must be transferred to Alexander or its agent.

4. **Costs Incurred in Connection with the Transactions**  
   - All **reasonable and direct costs** incurred by Alexander or its agent in connection with any of the above transactions (e.g., legal fees, surveying, administrative expenses) are eligible for remission.

5. **Interest and Penalties**  
   - Interest and penalties paid or payable under Part IX of the Excise Tax Act in respect of any of the above transactions are also remitted.

> ⚠️ **Note**: This remission applies only to taxes, interest, and penalties related to the specified transactions involving settlement land.

---

### **5. Conditions for Remission**

To qualify for remission, the following conditions must be met:

1. **No Prior Rebate or Credit**  
   - The tax, interest, or penalties must **not have already been**:
     - Rebated,
     - Credited,
     - Refunded, or
     - Remitted to any person under the Excise Tax Act or the Financial Administration Act.

2. **Written Claim to the Minister of National Revenue**  
   - A **formal, written claim** must be submitted to the **Minister of National Revenue**.

3. **Time Limit for Claims**  
   - **For taxes, interest, or penalties paid BEFORE the Order came into force**:  
     The claim must be submitted within **two years** of the date the Order came into effect.  
   - **For taxes, interest, or penalties paid ON OR AFTER the Order came into force**:  
     The claim must be submitted within **two years** of the **date the tax or penalty was actually paid**.

4. **Confirmation from the Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (DIAND)**  
   - The claim must be accompanied by a **written confirmation** from DIAND that it is being made **with respect to settlement land** under the Settlement Agreement.

> ❗ This condition ensures that the remission is only applied to transactions tied to the legitimate treaty land entitlements and not to unrelated or speculative property dealings.

---

### **6. Key Implications and Objectives**

- **Support for Indigenous Self-Determination**:  
  The remission recognizes the historical and financial challenges faced by Indigenous communities in acquiring land rights through treaty settlements. It provides financial relief to the Alexander First Nation, enabling them to manage and develop their lands without the burden of excise taxes.

- **Encouragement of Land Development and Stewardship**:  
  By removing tax burdens on land acquisition and related activities, the Order encourages the First Nation to invest in infrastructure, housing, economic development, and environmental stewardship on its settlement land.

- **Alignment with Treaty Settlement Principles**:  
  The Order is consistent with broader government efforts to honor treaty agreements and support Indigenous communities through financial and administrative accommodations.

- **Clarity and Accountability**:  
  The strict conditions (especially the two-year claim window and DIAND confirmation) ensure that remissions are applied fairly, transparently, and only to legitimate, documented transactions.

---

### **7. Limitations and Exclusions**

- **No Remission for Transactions Beyond 10,000 Acres**:  
  The cumulative total of settlement land involved is capped at **10,000 acres**. Any transaction exceeding this limit is not eligible.

- **No Remission for Non-Settlement Land or Non-Settlement Transactions**:  
  The remission applies **only** to land and interests directly linked to the Settlement Agreement.

- **No Remission for Taxes Already Refunded or Offset**:  
  If the tax or penalty was previously refunded or offset elsewhere, the remission will not apply.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Alexander First Nation Treaty Land Entitlement Remission Order* is a targeted, principled, and legally grounded measure designed to:

- Provide **financial relief** to the Alexander First Nation in connection with its treaty land entitlements,
- Promote **Indigenous land development and self-governance**, and
- Uphold the **public interest** by ensuring that treaty settlements are implemented without undue financial barriers.

It represents a significant step in recognizing the historical and ongoing contributions of Indigenous peoples and aligning federal tax policy with the goals of reconciliation and equitable land rights.

--- 

✅ **In essence**: This Order forgives excise taxes, interest, and penalties on specific land and property transactions involving the Alexander First Nation, provided the transactions are within the 10,000-acre limit, properly documented, and claimed within two years — with confirmation from DIAND. It is a tool of financial support rooted in treaty justice and public interest.
