**Detailed Summary of the Proclamation Regarding the Canada-Australia Income Tax Convention**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Issuance**
This document is an **official Proclamation** issued under the authority of **Queen Elizabeth II**, as the Head of the Commonwealth and Governor General of Canada. It is signed and sealed by the **Governor General of Canada**, acting on the advice of the **Privy Council for Canada**, which is the formal body responsible for advising the Crown on matters of national policy and legislation.

- **Date of Proclamation**: August 26, 1981  
- **Place of Issuance**: Ottawa, Canada  
- **Legal Status**: This proclamation serves as the formal public notice that the Canada-Australia Income Tax Convention has entered into force in Canada.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Proclamation**
The primary purpose of this proclamation is to **officially announce** and **publicly declare** the effective date of the **Canada-Australia Income Tax Convention**, which was enacted to:
- Avoid **double taxation** of income earned by individuals and entities in one country that is taxed in the other.
- Prevent **fiscal evasion** (tax avoidance or fraud) related to income tax.

This notice is required under **Section 7 of the Canada-Australia Income Tax Convention Act, 1980**, which mandates that the Governor in Council issue a proclamation in the *Canada Gazette* to inform the public of both:
- The date the Convention comes into force
- The date it ceases to be effective (if applicable)

---

### **3. Key Background and Legal Basis**

#### **Section 7 of the Canada-Australia Income Tax Convention Act, 1980**
- This section establishes a procedural requirement: the Government of Canada must issue a **proclamation in the Canada Gazette** to formally notify the public of the Convention’s effective date.
- The Act was passed on **April 23, 1981**, and became part of the Statutes of Canada, 1980–81 (Part II, Chapter 56).

#### **Article 27 of the Convention**
- Article 27 sets the **conditions under which the Convention comes into force**.
- It stipulates that the Convention will enter into force on the date when **both governments exchange formal notes** (official communications) confirming that all necessary domestic legal and administrative measures have been completed to give the Convention full legal effect within their respective jurisdictions.

---

### **4. Timeline of Implementation**

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **April 28, 1981** | The **Government of Australia** notifies the Government of Canada that all necessary steps to implement the Convention within Australia have been finalized. |
| **April 29, 1981** | The **Government of Canada** responds by notifying Australia that all necessary measures have been completed within Canada. |
| **April 29, 1981** | As a result of this mutual notification, **Article 27 is satisfied**, and the Convention officially comes into force on this date. |

This mutual confirmation marks the **effective date** of the Convention.

---

### **5. Content of the Proclamation**

The proclamation explicitly states:

> "We, by and with the advice of Our Privy Council for Canada, do by this Our Proclamation give notice that the said Convention between Canada and Australia for the avoidance of double taxation and the prevention of fiscal evasion with respect to taxes on income came into force on April 29, 1981."

This declaration serves as:
- A **public legal notice** to all Canadian citizens, residents, businesses, and other stakeholders.
- A **formal record** of the Convention’s entry into force under Canadian law.
- A **binding legal instrument** that enables the application of the Convention’s provisions in Canadian tax law.

---

### **6. Legal and Practical Implications**

#### For Canadian Tax Authorities:
- The Convention allows for **tax credits or exemptions** for income earned in Australia by Canadian residents, and vice versa.
- It provides a framework for resolving disputes over tax liability, ensuring that individuals and companies are not taxed twice on the same income.

#### For Individuals and Businesses:
- Taxpayers with income from Australia (e.g., from employment, investments, or business activities) can now claim relief from double taxation under the Convention.
- The rules on **tax residency**, **source of income**, and **taxable benefits** are defined in the Convention and now apply from April 29, 1981.

#### For International Tax Relations:
- This Convention strengthens the **tax cooperation** between Canada and Australia.
- It reflects Canada’s commitment to international tax harmonization and mutual respect for fiscal policies.

---

### **7. Signature and Authentication**

The proclamation is authenticated by:
- **The Governor General of Canada**, who is the representative of the Crown and has the authority to issue such proclamations.
- **Witnesses**:
  - **The Honourable Julien Chouinard**: A Puisne Judge of the Supreme Court of Canada (a senior judicial officer), and a member of the Privy Council.
  - **Edward Richard Schreyer**: Chancellor of the Order of Canada and Commander of the Order of Military Merit, who also holds the Canadian Forces’ Decoration.

These individuals are included to affirm the **legitimacy, authority, and integrity** of the proclamation.

---

### **8. Conclusion**

This proclamation is a **formal, legally binding public announcement** that:
- The **Canada-Australia Income Tax Convention** officially entered into force on **April 29, 1981**.
- It fulfills a statutory obligation under Section 7 of the Canada-Australia Income Tax Convention Act, 1980.
- It provides clarity and certainty to taxpayers, tax administrators, and international partners regarding the effective date of the Convention.
- It marks a significant step in Canada’s international tax policy and bilateral relations with Australia.

---

### **Final Note**
This document is not a new law or policy change, but rather the **official public declaration** of the implementation of an existing international treaty. It ensures that the Convention’s provisions are recognized and can be applied in Canadian tax law from the date of entry into force.
