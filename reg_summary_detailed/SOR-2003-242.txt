### **Definition of "Financial Intermediary Activities"**

The regulation hinges on the concept of **financial intermediary activities** â€” those that involve taking deposits, making loans, trading securities, or engaging in other activities that expose the entity to **market or credit risk**.

- If such activities are **restricted to internal group use**, they are considered non-risky and do not constitute financial intermediation in the traditional sense.
- Any service that involves **external exposure** (e.g., lending to non-group customers, accepting public deposits, trading in financial instruments for third parties) would fall outside the scope of this exemption.

### **Conclusion**

The **Exemption from Approval for Certain Investments in Intragroup Service Entities (Bank Act) Regulations** provides a clear, risk-based exemption from regulatory approval for banks, foreign banks, and bank holding companies when they invest in or acquire control of service entities that:

- Provide services **exclusively to the bank or its group**; and  
- Do **not engage in financial intermediary activities** that expose them to material market or credit risk.

This regulation streamlines internal governance, enhances operational efficiency, and supports the stability of the financial system by ensuring that only non-risky, group-specific services are exempt from approval.
