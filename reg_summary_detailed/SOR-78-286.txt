**Detailed Summary of the *Railway Passenger Services Contract Regulations***

---

### **Overview**

The *Railway Passenger Services Contract Regulations* (commonly referred to as the "Railway Passenger Services Contract Regulations") are a set of administrative rules established by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Minister of Transport** and the **President of the Treasury Board**. These regulations govern the framework under which the **Government of Canada** enters into a contract with **VIA Rail Canada Inc. (VIA)** for the provision of railway passenger services.

The regulations were enacted under **Transport Vote 52d of the Appropriation Act No. 1, 1977**, which authorizes funding for railway passenger services. The primary purpose of these regulations is to ensure that:

- The provision of passenger rail services is financially sustainable,
- Public accountability and transparency are maintained,
- The government retains oversight over service quality, pricing, and cost structures,
- The operations of VIA are aligned with broader transportation policy objectives, and
- The financial incentives and cost controls are fair, transparent, and subject to appropriate governmental review.

### **Conditions for Contract Approval**

Before a railway passenger service contract can be entered into, the following conditions **must be met**:

1. **Treasury Board Approval**: The contract must be formally approved by the Treasury Board.
2. **Implementation Plan Approved by CN and CP**: VIA must submit an implementation plan for its involvement in passenger services, which must be jointly approved by Canadian National (CN) and Canadian Pacific (CP)â€”the two major freight rail operators.
3. **Agreement with CN and CP on Cost Review**: VIA must reach an agreement with CN and CP such that any payments made to them (including financial incentives) are subject to a **review process** approved by the Minister. This review ensures that:
   - The actual costs incurred by VIA do **not exceed** those calculated using **CTC Order R-6313**, a standard developed by the Canadian Transport Commission (CTC).
   - The cost benchmark is **indexed** (adjusted annually) based on official CTC indices to account for inflation and economic changes.

### **Subsidiary Service Requests: Key Provisions**

A *subsidiary service request* is a formal, written request by the Minister to VIA to modify or expand service delivery. These requests are subject to strict conditions:

#### 1. **Pre-Request Planning and Projections**
- Before issuing a subsidiary service request, the Minister **must require VIA to submit**:
  - Detailed plans for the proposed service,
  - Financial projections (including revenue and cost estimates),
  - Proposed fare structures (if requested by the Minister).

#### 2. **Consultation on Fare Structures**
- The Minister may consult with:
  - Provincial governments,
  - Members of the motor carrier industry (e.g., bus companies),
  - Other relevant stakeholders.
- This consultation ensures that fare decisions consider regional economic conditions, competition, and public interest.

#### 3. **Treasury Board Approval Thresholds**
- **No subsidiary service request** may be issued without **Treasury Board approval** if:
  - It involves a payment exceeding **$10 million** in a single year, **or**
  - It relates to passenger services that are **substitutes or ancillary** to services on railway lines (e.g., shuttle services, regional connections).
- This threshold protects against unilateral or large-scale service expansions without proper fiscal oversight.

#### 4. **No Inconsistent Undertakings**
- Any subsidiary service request must **not contain undertakings that conflict with the terms of the main railway passenger service contract**.

#### 5. **Ministerial Limitations on Payments and Service Quality**
The Minister has the authority to impose limits on:
- **Maximum total payments** to VIA,
- **Pricing conditions** (i.e., caps or rules on how fares can be set),
- **Minimum level and quality of service** (e.g., frequency, reliability, comfort standards).

### **Mandatory Undertakings by VIA (Under the Contract)**

Every railway passenger service contract must include the following obligations by VIA:

1. **Compliance with Subsidiary Service Requests**  
   - VIA must comply with all subsidiary service requests issued by the Minister after consultation with the Minister.

2. **Transparency and Accountability**  
   - VIA must make its **financial accounts and records open to audit and inspection** by an authorized representative of the Minister.

3. **Reporting Requirements**  
   - VIA must provide **regular reports** to the Minister, containing information as requiredâ€”such as performance metrics, financial data, passenger numbers, and service changes.

4. **Public Invitation to Tender Policy**  
   - VIA must adopt a **contract policy for public invitation to tender** that is acceptable to the Minister.  
   - This ensures that future procurement of services (e.g., equipment, maintenance) is done through transparent, competitive processes.

---

### **Prohibited Undertakings in the Contract**

No railway passenger service contract may contain any express or implied terms that:

1. **Require the Minister to pay VIA for costs incurred** (except for those covered under the **compensation method** in Section 7 of the contract).  
   - This prevents VIA from claiming reimbursement for operating expenses, which would undermine government control over spending.

2. **Preclude the Minister from participating in**:
   - Setting objectives,
   - Developing service concepts,
   - Establishing pricing policies,
   - Implementing service improvements.

### **Payment Mechanism and Financial Incentives**

Payments to VIA under the contract or subsidiary service requests are governed by specific principles:

#### 1. **Financial Incentive Measures Are Allowed**
- Payments may include **financial incentives** designed to:
  - Encourage VIA to make **cost-effective decisions** (e.g., balancing passenger attraction with cost reduction),
  - Adjust payment terms **route-by-route** based on:
    - Competitive conditions (e.g., more competition on certain routes may reduce profitability),
    - Government policy goals (e.g., promoting rural access or environmental sustainability).

#### 2. **Payments Are Not Based on Actual Costs**
- Payments are **not calculated on actual operating costs** incurred by VIA.
- Instead, they are determined **at the time of contract or request** based on:
  - **Estimated costs** (e.g., capital and operating expenses),
  - **Estimated revenues**,
  - Subject to:
    - Minimum service quality standards,
    - Pricing conditions set by the Minister.

> ðŸ“‰ This means VIA cannot claim "cost recovery" or "loss compensation" beyond the contracted amount.

#### 3. **Cost Cap Based on CTC Order R-6313**
- The estimated costs used to determine payments **must not exceed** those derived from **CTC Order R-6313**, which provides a standardized method for estimating railway operating costs.
- This cap is **subject to indexing** (annual adjustment) provided by the **Canadian Transport Commission (CTC)** to reflect inflation and economic changes.
- **Exception**: Any cost estimate that exceeds the CTC benchmark **requires Ministerial approval** before being implemented.

### **Conclusion**

The *Railway Passenger Services Contract Regulations* establish a **balanced, transparent, and accountable framework** for the federal governmentâ€™s relationship with VIA Rail. The regulations:

- Protect public funds by limiting payments to cost benchmarks and requiring oversight,
- Empower the Minister to shape service delivery through pricing, quality, and operational controls,
- Ensure that VIA operates efficiently and in the public interest,
- Maintain fairness and transparency through stakeholder consultation and audit rights.
