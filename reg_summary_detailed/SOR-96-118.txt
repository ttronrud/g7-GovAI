### ðŸ”¹ **Fire and Ignition Safety Provisions**

#### ðŸ”¥ Prohibited Proximity of Flame-Type Equipment
No flame-type equipment (e.g., heaters, torches, welders, boilers, open flame appliances) may be placed or operated within:
- **25 meters** of:
  - A well
  - An oil storage tank
  - Any source of ignitable vapors

**Exceptions:**
- **Water supply wells** or **water injection wells** with:
  - A suitable packer
  - Surface casing annulus vented outside any building
- **Emergency work** only, when:
  - Wellhead valves and blowout preventers are **closed**

#### ðŸ”¥ Prohibited Proximity to Process Vessels
- Flame-type equipment **cannot be placed within 25 meters** of a process vessel (e.g., heaters, separators, dehydrators) **unless**:
  - It is fitted with an **adequate flame arrester**.

#### ðŸ”¥ Building Proximity Rules
- Flame-type equipment **cannot be located in the same building** as a process vessel or ignitable vapor source unless:
  1. Air intakes and flues are located **outside** the building.
  2. Relief valves, safety heads, and vapor sources are **vented above roof level**.
  3. The building is **adequately cross-ventilated**.

> These rules reduce the risk of ignition due to vapors or hot surfaces.

---

#### ðŸ”¥ Venting and Flame Arresters
- All **process vessels** and equipment that may emit ignitable vapors must be **vented to the atmosphere**.
- All **storage tank vent lines** going to flare pits or stacks must have:
  - **Flame arresters** or **equivalent safety devices** to prevent fire propagation.

> Prevents uncontrolled fires or explosions from flammable vapors.

---

#### ðŸ”¥ Exhaust Pipe Requirements
- Exhaust pipes from internal combustion engines within 25 meters of a well, tank, or process vessel must:
  - Prevent flame emergence along the pipe or at the end.
  - Have the **end at least 6 meters** from the vertical centerline of the well.
  - Be directed **away from the well**.

> Prevents flame spread and ignition of nearby flammable materials.

All equipment at or near wells, tanks, or process vessels must comply with:
- **Part I of the Canadian Electrical Code (CEC Part I)**

> Ensures electrical safety in high-risk environments.
