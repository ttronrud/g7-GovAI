## üîç **Overview of the Regulation**

This document is a **proclamation** issued by the Government of Canada, under the authority of the *Old Age Security Act*, confirming that the **Agreement on Social Security between Canada and the Republic of Serbia** officially comes into force on **December 1, 2014**.

The agreement establishes a framework for **cooperative social security arrangements** between Canada and the Republic of Serbia, enabling citizens of both countries to access pensions, disability benefits, and other social security benefits ‚Äî even when living or working in the other country ‚Äî under mutually recognized rules.

It is a **bilateral social security agreement** designed to prevent benefit "double-dipping" and ensure fair, transparent, and reciprocal treatment of individuals who have contributed to either country‚Äôs social security systems.

## üìú Key Background and Legal Pathway

1. **Origin of the Agreement**  
   - Signed in Belgrade on April 12, 2013 (Article 28 of the Agreement).
   - The agreement entered into force upon the **exchange of diplomatic notes** confirming both parties had met all requirements.

2. **Entry into Force Process (as per Canadian law)**  
   - The Order in Council (P.C. 2013-1381) declaring the agreement‚Äôs entry into force was laid before:
     - House of Commons: January 31, 2014
     - Senate: February 4, 2014
   - No motion to revoke it was introduced within 20 sitting days.
   - Under **subsection 42(2) of the Old Age Security Act**, the Order came into force on the **30th sitting day after laying**, which was **April 4, 2014**.
   - Diplomatic notes were exchanged in **August 2014**.
   - The agreement entered into force on the **first day of the fourth month after the exchange of notes**, i.e., **December 1, 2014**.

3. **Final Proclamation**  
   - On **September 18, 2014**, the Governor in Council issued **Order P.C. 2014-944**, directing the issuance of a proclamation.
   - This proclamation formally **notifies the public and the institutions of Canada** that the Agreement is now in force as of **December 1, 2014**.

## üéØ Purpose and Scope of the Agreement

The primary goal is to **harmonize social security benefits** (pensions, disability, work-related injury) between Canada and Serbia by:

- Recognizing contributions made in one country as valid for benefit eligibility in the other.
- Ensuring that individuals who have worked or lived in both countries are not disadvantaged.
- Facilitating cross-border access to benefits without discrimination or duplication.

## üåç Who Is Covered?

The Agreement applies to:

- Any person who has been or is currently subject to the legislation of **either or both** countries.
- Persons who **derive rights** from such individuals (e.g., spouses, common-law partners, dependants).

> üìå **Key Principle**: These individuals are treated as if they were **citizens of the other country**, and are eligible for benefits under the same conditions.

## üí∞ Benefits and Payment Rules

### General Rule:
> Unless otherwise stated, **benefits are not reduced, suspended, or canceled** simply because the recipient is in the territory of the other country.

### Specific Rules:

#### 1. **Benefits Paid Abroad**
- **Canada**:
  - An **OAS pension** is paid only if the person‚Äôs total residence periods (totalized as per Part III of Chapter 1) meet or exceed the **minimum residency requirement** in Canada.
  - **Allowance and Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS)** are paid **only to the extent permitted** by Canadian law.

- **Serbia**:
  - Benefits are paid to individuals residing in a **third country**, **provided** Serbia has a social security agreement with that third country.

#### 2. **Third Country Residency**
- If a person (or someone deriving rights from them) resides in a third country, benefits from **both countries** may still be paid ‚Äî **but only if** the third country has a social security treaty with Serbia.

## üìÖ Creditable Periods and Benefit Calculations

### For OAS (Canada):
- A period of **employment or self-employment in Serbia** during which a person contributes to the **Canada Pension Plan (CPP)** counts as **residence in Canada**.
- This applies to the person and their **spouse/common-law partner and dependants** (if not subject to Serbian employment/self-employment).
- **Reverse rule**: A period in Canada where a person contributes to Serbian legislation **does NOT** count as residence in Canada for OAS purposes.

### For CPP (Canada):
- A **calendar year** that includes **at least 3 months** of creditable periods in Serbia counts as a **creditable year** under CPP.

### For Serbian Pension Eligibility:
- A **calendar year** of CPP contributions counts as **12 months** of creditable periods in Serbia.
- A **month** of OAS contributions (not part of a CPP period) counts as **1 month** of creditable time in Serbia.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è These rules apply only when totalizing periods under **Chapter 1** (residence/contribution periods).
