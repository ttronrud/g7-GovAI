**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

**Regulation Title:**  
Notice of Entry into Force of the Canada-Mexico Income Tax Convention

**Date of Notice:**  
June 3, 1992  

**Legal Basis:**  
Pursuant to **Section 18 of the Canada-Mexico Income Tax Convention Act, 1991** (S.C. 1992, c. 3, Part III), the Government of Canada has issued a formal notice confirming that the **Canada-Mexico Income Tax Convention** has officially come into force.

---

**Key Provisions and Significance of the Notice:**

1. **Entry into Force Date:**  
   The Convention between the Government of Canada and the Government of the United Mexican States for the Avoidance of Double Taxation and the Prevention of Fiscal Evasion with respect to Taxes on Income entered into force on **May 11, 1992**.  
   This date marks the official start of the legal obligations and benefits established under the treaty.

2. **Purpose of the Convention:**  
   The primary objectives of the Convention are:
   - To **avoid double taxation** of income earned by individuals and businesses in either Canada or Mexico.
   - To **prevent fiscal evasion** (tax avoidance or fraud) by ensuring that income is taxed only once and in accordance with fair and transparent rules.
   - To promote economic cooperation and investment between Canada and Mexico by providing clarity on tax treatment of income from cross-border activities.

3. **Applicable Tax Regime:**  
   The Convention specifically addresses **taxes on income**, including:
   - Dividends
   - Interest
   - Royalties
   - Capital gains
   - Business profits
   - Employment income

   It establishes rules for determining the **source of income**, the **taxable amount**, and **tax credits or exemptions** that may be granted to prevent double taxation.

4. **Legal Authority:**  
   The notice is issued under **Section 18** of the *Canada-Mexico Income Tax Convention Act, 1991*, which empowers the Canadian government to officially announce when a tax treaty comes into force. This section ensures that the public and tax authorities are formally informed of the treaty’s operational commencement.

5. **Public Notification:**  
   The notice serves as a formal public announcement to:
   - Inform Canadian taxpayers, tax professionals, and government agencies about the availability of treaty benefits.
   - Ensure compliance with treaty provisions by both domestic and foreign entities.
   - Enable the Canadian Revenue Agency (CRA) to update its guidance, forms, and procedures to reflect treaty rules.

6. **Implementation Implications:**  
   Upon entry into force:
   - Canadian residents may claim **foreign tax credits** or **exemptions** for taxes paid to Mexico on income sourced in Mexico.
   - Mexican residents may similarly claim benefits for income earned in Canada.
   - Taxpayers may apply for **treaty-based relief** in cases of double taxation, such as reduced tax rates or exemptions for certain types of income.

7. **Relevance to Tax Policy:**  
   This treaty strengthens Canada’s international tax framework and supports bilateral economic relations. It enhances predictability and fairness in cross-border taxation, which is especially important for multinational corporations and individuals with income in both jurisdictions.

---

**Conclusion:**  
This notice is a critical administrative and legal step that brings into effect a key international agreement designed to promote tax fairness and economic cooperation between Canada and Mexico. By confirming the treaty’s entry into force on May 11, 1992, the Canadian government ensures that the rules of the Convention are now enforceable and accessible to taxpayers and tax authorities, thereby supporting a more equitable and transparent international tax environment.
