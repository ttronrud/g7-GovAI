This document is a **Proclamation of Appointment** issued by **Her Excellency the Right Honourable Mary May Simon**, who serves as the **Governor General and Commander-in-Chief of Canada**. It is a formal, constitutional instrument that officially announces her appointment and the commencement of her duties in the role. Below is a **detailed summary** of the regulation:

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Basis of the Proclamation**

- The proclamation is issued under the authority of **Letters Patent** issued by **Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II** on **September 8, 1947**, which established the Office of the Governor General and Commander-in-Chief in and over Canada.
- The Queen's Commission, dated **July 21, 2021**, formally appointed Mary May Simon as Governor General and Commander-in-Chief in and over Canada.
- This proclamation is an official announcement that the appointment has been made and that the Governor General has been duly authorized to exercise all powers and duties associated with the office, as defined in the Letters Patent and any subsequent amendments.

---

### **2. Constitutional and Ceremonial Significance**

- The proclamation is issued in accordance with constitutional conventions and legal procedures:
  - It is read and published **in the presence of the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of Canada** and **members of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada**, affirming its legitimacy and adherence to the rule of law.
  - The Governor General has taken the **oaths required by the Letters Patent**, which are essential to the constitutional duties of the office.

---

### **3. Purpose and Content of the Proclamation**

The primary purposes of this proclamation are:

#### a) **To Announce the Appointment**
- It publicly declares that Mary May Simon has been appointed by Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II as Governor General and Commander-in-Chief in and over Canada.
- This serves as a formal notification to all Canadians and institutions that a new Governor General has taken office.

#### b) **To Confirm the Commencement of Duties**
- The proclamation affirms that the Governor General has **entered upon the duties** of her office, effective from the date of issuance (July 26, 2021).
- This marks the official start of her term, which is typically for a five-year term (or until the next appointment), subject to the Royal Pleasure.

#### c) **To Ensure Continuity of Government**
- The Governor General **commands all Her Majesty’s Officers and Ministers** to continue in their respective offices and responsibilities.
- This ensures **constitutional continuity** during the transition period, preventing any disruption in the functioning of federal institutions, departments, and services.

#### d) **To Inform the Public and All Stakeholders**
- The proclamation instructs "all and singular Her Majesty’s loving subjects and all others whom these Presents may concern" to **take notice and govern themselves accordingly**.
- This serves as a public notice that the new Governor General is now in office and that all government operations should proceed under her constitutional authority.

---

### **4. Formal Elements and Legal Structure**

- The document is **formally authenticated** with:
  - The **Governor General’s Hand** (signature)
  - The **Seal of Office** (a formal symbol of authority)
- It is issued in **Ottawa**, the seat of the Canadian government, on **July 26, 2021**, the 26th day of July in the year 2021 and the **seventieth year of Her Majesty’s Reign**.

---

### **5. Constitutional Role of the Governor General**

While the Governor General is a **ceremonial and constitutional figure**, this proclamation affirms the following key roles:

- **Representative of the Crown** in Canada
- **Commander-in-Chief** of the Canadian Armed Forces
- **Head of State** in a constitutional monarchy (acting on the advice of the Prime Minister and Cabinet)
- **Appointer of key officials**, including judges, senators, and federal ministers
- **Duty to perform constitutional functions**, such as giving royal assent to legislation, dissolving Parliament, and appointing the Prime Minister (in the event of a vacancy)

---

### **6. Significance in Canadian Governance**

- This proclamation is a **cornerstone of Canada’s constitutional order**.
- It demonstrates the **separation of powers** and the **role of the Crown** as a neutral, constitutional institution.
- It reinforces the **principle of continuity** in government, even during leadership transitions.
- It reflects Canada’s status as a **constitutional monarchy** with a parliamentary system, where the Governor General acts on the advice of elected officials.

This proclamation is not a law or policy directive but a **constitutional instrument** that officially marks the beginning of Mary May Simon’s tenure as Governor General and Commander-in-Chief of Canada. It is a formal, legally binding announcement that:
