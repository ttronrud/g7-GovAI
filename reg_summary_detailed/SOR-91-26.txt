### 1. **"Act"**
- Refers to the *Excise Tax Act*.

### 2. **"Employee Life and Health Trust", "Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP)", etc.**
- These terms have the same meaning as defined in **subsection 248(1) of the Income Tax Act**.
- This ensures consistency with tax law definitions, particularly regarding trust and savings plan structures.

### 3. **"Person at Risk" (Definition in Section 4)**
- A person who is **financially at risk** due to:
  - Acquisition, ownership, or issuance of a financial instrument.
  - Providing a guarantee, acceptance, or indemnity in respect of the instrument.
- **Exclusions**:
  - A person who becomes at risk **only by authorizing a transaction** or **supplying a clearing/settlement service** is **not** considered a "person at risk".

> This definition is crucial for determining when certain services are **exempt** from being classified as financial services under paragraph (t).

### ðŸ”¹ Subparagraph (q)(ii): Management/Administrative Services to Persons in Paragraph (q)
> **Applies when supplied by a supplier providing management or administrative services** to a person falling under paragraph (q) of the definition of "financial service".

#### Prescribed Services Include:
1. **Issuance or transfer of ownership** of a financial instrument (e.g., shares, bonds, accounts) from the supplier to the person.
2. **Operation or maintenance** of a savings, chequing, deposit, loan, charge, or other account held by the person with the supplier.
3. **Arranging for issuance, renewal, variation, or transfer of ownership** of a financial instrument for a person who is:
   - In a **self-directed RDSP**, **self-directed REES**, **self-directed RRIF**, **self-directed RRSP**, or **self-directed TFSA**.

> **Purpose**: These services are deemed financial services because they involve direct administrative or operational involvement in managing financial instruments.

> **Key Term Definitions**:
- *Acquirer*, *issuer*, *payment card*, *payment card network*, and *payment card network operator* have the same meanings as in **Section 3 of the Payment Card Networks Act**.

### ðŸ”¹ Paragraph (r.6): Services Related to Payment Card Transactions
> These services are prescribed for **paragraph (r.6)** of the definition of "financial service".

#### The prescribed services include:

1. **Service provided by a payment card network operator as acquirer**:
   - Supplied to:
     - The person who accepted the payment card, or
     - A payment service provider engaged by that person.

2. **Service rendered to a holder of a payment card**:
   - Supplied by the network operator in its **capacity as issuer** of the card.

3. **Settlement services**:
   - From **acquirer to issuer**, or from **issuer to acquirer** (in settlement of a transaction).
   - **Only if** the issuer supplies a corresponding settlement service (i.e., paying the network operator the amount charged to the card).

4. **Settlement service where the network operator pays the acquirer**:
   - For the amount charged to the payment card.
   - **Only if** the issuer has paid the network operator the same amount.

> **Purpose**: These services are essential to the functioning of the payment card ecosystem and are explicitly included as financial services to ensure proper taxation.

> **Note**: The term *instrument* includes:
> - Money
> - An account
> - A credit card voucher
> - A charge card voucher
> - A financial instrument

## ðŸ”¹ Paragraph (t): Administrative and Information Services (General)

> **Prescribed services** for **paragraph (t)** of the definition of "financial service" include:

1. **Transfer, collection, or processing of information**.
2. **Any administrative service**, including:
   - Payment or receipt of dividends, interest, principal, claims, benefits, or other amounts.
   - **Exception**: Solely making a payment or taking a receipt is **not** a prescribed service.

> **Purpose**: These services are considered financial in nature due to their administrative role in managing financial relationships.

## âŒ Exclusions from Paragraph (t) â€“ When a Service is NOT Prescribed

A service **is not** a prescribed service under paragraph (t) **if it is supplied by**:

1. A **person at risk** (as defined above),  
2. A **member of the same closely related group** as a person at risk â€” **if the recipient is not** the person at risk or another member of the same group, or  
3. An **agent, salesperson, or broker** who arranges for:
   - Issuance, renewal, variation, or transfer of ownership of an instrument for a person at risk or a member of the same closely related group.

> **Rationale**: To prevent double taxation or overreach in cases where services are provided in a personal, advisory, or intermediary capacity rather than as a core financial service.

> This exclusion protects individuals and closely related entities from being taxed on services that are not core to financial operations.
