**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

**Regulatory Instrument:**  
Transfer of Public Service Functions under the *Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act*

**Issuing Authority:**  
Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister

**Legal Basis:**  
Pursuant to **paragraph 2(a)** of the *Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act*, which empowers the federal government to reorganize and transfer responsibilities within the public service to improve efficiency, accountability, and service delivery.

**Effective Date:**  
**December 12, 2003**

---

**Subject of Transfer:**  
The control and supervision of specific units and divisions within the **Chief Information Officer (CIO) Branch** of the **Treasury Board Secretariat (TBS)** are being transferred to the **Department of Public Works and Government Services (DPWGS)**.

These units include:

1. **GOL Analysis**  
   – Responsible for analyzing Government of Canada (GOL) data and trends to support policy and decision-making.  
   – Provides strategic insights into the performance, structure, and effectiveness of government operations.

2. **Gateways and Clusters**  
   – Manages the design, implementation, and oversight of government digital service gateways and service clusters.  
   – Focuses on integrating and streamlining digital services across federal departments.

3. **GOL Assistant Secretary’s Office**  
   – Supports the Assistant Secretary of the CIO Branch, providing administrative, strategic, and coordination functions.  
   – Acts as a liaison between the CIO Branch and senior leadership within the Treasury Board Secretariat.

4. **Service Delivery Improvement Division**  
   – Leads initiatives to enhance the quality, accessibility, and efficiency of public services.  
   – Implements performance improvement strategies and evaluates service delivery models across government.

5. **Secure Channel Client Engagement and Secure Channel Project Management Office**  
   – Manages secure communication channels for government clients and partners.  
   – Oversees the development, deployment, and management of secure digital platforms for sensitive government communications.

6. **Organizational Readiness Division**  
   – Assesses and supports the readiness of federal organizations to adopt new technologies and digital transformation initiatives.  
   – Provides guidance on organizational change management, training, and capacity building.

7. **Communications and Public Relations**  
   – Oversees internal and external communications related to government digital services, technology initiatives, and public engagement.  
   – Develops messaging, manages public outreach, and ensures consistent communication across government digital platforms.

---

**Purpose of the Transfer:**  
The transfer was implemented to:

- Improve coordination between digital service delivery and public sector operations.  
- Strengthen the integration of technology and service delivery within government.  
- Align the functions of the CIO Branch with the broader mandate of the Department of Public Works and Government Services (DPWGS), which is responsible for managing public infrastructure, services, and government operations.  
- Enhance accountability and ensure that digital transformation efforts are effectively supported by both technical and organizational capabilities.

---

**Implications of the Transfer:**

- **Change in Oversight:** The transferred units will now fall under the administrative and strategic direction of DPWGS, rather than the Treasury Board Secretariat.  
- **Continuity of Functions:** The day-to-day operations and responsibilities of the transferred divisions remain unchanged; only the supervisory authority and reporting lines are affected.  
- **Strengthened Integration:** DPWGS, with its broader mandate for public service delivery and infrastructure, is better positioned to ensure that digital services are aligned with broader government service goals.

---

**Context and Background:**  
This transfer reflects a broader government initiative in the early 2000s to modernize federal services through digital transformation. It was part of a larger effort to centralize and streamline public service functions related to information technology and service delivery, ensuring that technology investments directly contribute to improved citizen outcomes.

---

**Conclusion:**  
This regulation marks a significant reorganization within the federal public service. By transferring key digital service and information management functions from the Treasury Board Secretariat to the Department of Public Works and Government Services, the government aimed to enhance the effectiveness, coherence, and accountability of digital public service delivery. The move underscores the growing importance of digital transformation in shaping modern government services.
