**Detailed Summary of the Rules of the Board of Arbitration (Agriculture and Agri-Food)**  
*Based on S.C. 1995, c. 40, s. 28 and s. 32(1); R.S., c. 20 (4th Supp.); Ottawa, July 4, 2000*

---

### **1. Purpose and Authority**
The **Board of Arbitration (Agriculture and Agri-Food)** has been authorized under **subsection 8(3)c of the Canada Agricultural Products Act (the "Act")** to establish and adopt these **Rules**. These rules govern the procedures for resolving disputes related to agricultural products, including complaints about quality, quantity, grading, shipment, and compensation.

The rules were approved by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food**, and are formally enacted as part of the regulatory framework under the Act.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**
The following terms are defined and apply throughout the rules:

- **Act**: Refers to the *Canada Agricultural Products Act*.
- **Complainant**: A person who files a formal complaint against a defendant under subsection 9(1) of the Act.
- **Defendant**: A person against whom a complaint is filed.
- **Party**: Includes the complainant, defendant, or any intervenor.
- **Secretary**: The official administrative officer of the Board responsible for processing submissions and communications.

---

### **3. General Principles of Interpretation and Fairness**
- **Liberality in Interpretation**: The rules are to be **liberally construed** to ensure the **fairness, efficiency, and cost-effectiveness** of dispute resolution.
- **Unfairness Exception**: If applying a rule would result in **unfairness to a party**, the Board may **waive compliance** with that rule.
- **Tolerance for Technical Defects**: Minor **formal or technical irregularities** may be overlooked by the Board.
- **Time Periods**: All days are counted in calculating time limits, **except** if the deadline falls on a **Saturday, Sunday, or statutory holiday** — in which case, the time is extended to the **next business day**.
- **Time Extensions**: The Board may **extend time limits** before or after their expiration at its discretion.

---

### **4. Document Handling and Confidentiality**
- **Public Documents**: All documents filed with the Board are treated as **public documents** unless a party requests confidentiality.
- **Confidentiality Requests**: A party must provide **clear and detailed reasons** for seeking confidentiality. If harm is alleged, the reasons must specify the **nature and extent** of that harm.
- **Duplicate Submission**: All documents sent to the Board must be sent in **duplicate**.
- **Submission Methods**: Documents must be submitted by **hand, registered mail, courier, or facsimile (fax)**.
- **Facsimile Requirement**: If a document is sent by fax, the **original and a copy must be sent by mail without delay**.
- **Board Responsibility**: The Board is responsible for ensuring that all submitted documents are **copied and sent to all parties**.
- **Delivery of Communications**: Written communications from the Board may be delivered by **hand, registered mail, courier, or fax**.
- **Change Notification**: A party must **notify the Board immediately** of any change in fax number or address.

---

### **5. Meetings and Hearings**
- **Teleconferences Allowed**: The Board may conduct **teleconferences** for all purposes **except** for conducting **hearings**.
- **Representation**: A party may be represented by **counsel or an authorized agent** (with written authorization).
- **In Camera Hearings**: A party (complainant or defendant) may request a **closed (in camera) hearing** if they can demonstrate to the Board that the circumstances require confidentiality.
- **Exclusion of Witnesses**: The Board may **exclude a witness** from the hearing until they are called to give evidence.
- **Notice Taking**: The Board may take notice of any matter to **expedite proceedings**.

---

### **6. Conflict of Interest and Impartiality**
- **Disclosure of Bias**: If a party believes a Board member is **not impartial**, they must **notify the Board in writing immediately**, stating the reason.
- **Exclusion of Member**: If the Board determines the reason is valid, the member is **excluded from considering** the complaint.
- **Conflict of Interest**: If a member has a **conflict of interest** related to the complaint, the member is **automatically excluded**.

---

### **7. Filing a Complaint**
A complaint must be:
- Filed with the **Secretary**;
- Include **copies of all relevant documents**;
- Contain a **statement** indicating whether a **hearing is requested**;
- Be **signed** by the complainant, their counsel, or an authorized agent.

**Required Information in the Complaint:**
1. A **precise statement of facts** and the **grounds** of the complaint, including the specific regulation allegedly violated;
2. Full **contact details** of the defendant and any agent;
3. **Quantity, quality, or grade** of each agricultural product shipped;
4. **Dates of shipment and arrival**;
5. **Identification of shipping vehicles**;
6. **Shipping and destination points**;
7. **Nature of the order** and **amount of compensation requested**.

**Secretary’s Review**: After reviewing the complaint, the Secretary may require the complainant to provide **additional information** within a set time.

- If the complainant fails to provide it **within the deadline** and offers **no reasonable excuse**, the Board may proceed with the complaint based on existing information.

**Frivolous or Vexatious Complaints**: If the Board determines the complaint is **frivolous, vexatious, or lacks a valid basis**, it will **decline to consider** it and notify the complainant.

---

### **8. Defendant’s Response (Defence)**
- Within **30 days** of receiving the complaint, the defendant may file a **defence** with the Secretary.
- The defence must include:
  - A **precise statement of facts** forming the basis of the defence;
  - A statement of which facts in the complaint are **admitted, denied, or unknown**.
- The defence must be **signed** by the defendant, counsel, or agent, with **authorization documentation** if an agent signs.

**Supporting Documents**: The defence must include copies of any **relevant documents not already submitted** by the complainant.

**Counterclaim (if applicable)**:
- If the complainant is a **licensed dealer** under the Act, the defendant may file a **counterclaim** within 30 days.
- The counterclaim must contain the **same information as a complaint** and be accompanied by relevant documents and a request for a hearing.
- The counterclaim must also be signed by the defendant or authorized representative.

**Secretary’s Notification**: The Secretary must send the defence and any counterclaim (with supporting documents) to the **complainant**.

**Defendant’s Additional Information**: The Secretary may require the defendant to provide **additional information**. If not provided within the deadline and without a reasonable excuse, the Board may proceed with the complaint.

---

### **9. Intervention by Third Parties**
- Any person with a **direct financial interest** in the proceedings may request to **intervene**.
- To intervene, the person must:
  - Submit a **written request**;
  - Clearly describe their **financial interest**;
  - Provide a **precise statement of facts and grounds** for intervention;
  - Include copies of relevant documents;
  - Be signed by the person or their agent (with authorization if an agent signs).

**Board’s Decision**:
- If the Board determines the person has a **direct financial interest** and the intervention would be **useful**, the Board authorizes the intervention and sends the materials to all parties.
- If the Board denies the request, the materials are **returned** to the requester.

---

### **10. Hearing Procedures**
If a hearing is requested:
- The Secretary sets a **date, time, and place** and notifies all parties in writing.
- Within **30 days** of receiving the notice, each party must:
  - Notify the Board in writing whether they plan to **attend** or **present arguments in writing**.
  - If attending, provide a **written statement** of the names of witnesses and the **nature of their evidence** within 30 days.
- The Secretary sends this statement to the other parties.

**Summons Requests**:
- Within 30 days of the hearing notice, a party may request a **summons** to compel a witness to appear or produce documents.
- The Board may **refuse** the summons if it determines the evidence or documents are **not relevant** to the subject matter.

**Pre-Hearing Conference**:
- If the Board deems it appropriate, it may arrange a **pre-hearing conference** (by phone or in person) to:
  - Simplify issues;
  - Clarify admitted/denied facts;
  - Consider amendments to pleadings;
  - Determine hearing procedures;
  - Exchange proposed evidence;
  - Address other matters to streamline the process.
- After the conference, the Secretary must **summarize agreements** and send the summary to all parties.
- **No communication** about pre-hearing statements may be made to the Board outside the summary.
