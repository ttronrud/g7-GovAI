### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation (SOR/78-408, 1978 – Canada Gazette Part II, p. 2162)**

This regulation, issued under the **Canada Wildlife Act**, formally assigns administrative control over specific public lands to the **Minister of the Environment** for the purpose of **wildlife research, conservation, and interpretation**. It is a statutory instrument that designates certain areas as **National Wildlife Areas (NWAs)** and establishes the legal framework for their management.

---

## **1. Legal Basis and Authority**

- **Governing Statute**: Canada Wildlife Act  
- **Section Invoked**: Subsection 4(1) of the Canada Wildlife Act  
- **Issuing Body**: The Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of the Environment)  
- **Purpose**: To ensure that designated public lands are preserved and managed for the protection, study, and public interpretation of wildlife.

> The Governor General in Council acts on the recommendation of the Minister of the Environment, indicating a policy-driven, expert-led allocation of land management responsibilities.

---

## **2. Key Provisions of the Regulation**

### **A. Assignment of Management Authority**
- The **Minister of the Environment** is granted full administrative, management, and control authority over:
  - The public lands described in **Schedule I** (specifically, the designated National Wildlife Areas).
  - In substitution for lands previously designated in **Order in Council P.C. 1978-1439**, the lands described in **Schedule II** are now included.

> This provision reflects a **restructuring or replacement** of earlier land designations, ensuring continuity and updated alignment with current conservation priorities.

---

- **Location**: County of Cumberland  
- **Boundary**: Bounded by a heavy line on **Plan 66185** in the Canada Lands Surveys Records (CLSR) at Ottawa

- **Purpose**: Conservation and interpretation of wildlife in a key wetland or riparian habitat.
