## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Weights and Measures Regulations (Repealed Sections Noted)**

This regulation establishes definitions, exemptions, and procedural requirements for devices used in trade under the *Weights and Measures Act* (Act). It governs the accuracy, approval, examination, marking, and use of measuring devices such as scales, tanks, meters, and packaging systems. Several sections have been **repealed**, and certain exemptions are conditional or time-limited.

## üö´ II. Exemptions from the Act (Where Devices Are Not Regulated)

### A. **General Exemptions (Where Class, Type, or Design is Exempt)**

A class, type, or design of device is exempt from the Act if:
1. It is subject to verification under the *Electricity and Gas Inspection Act*; or  
2. It is designed for use **only in laboratories or scientific research**, and **not approved under section 3** of the Act.

---

### B. **Exemptions Based on Use (Specific Devices or Activities)**

#### 1. **Downstream Petroleum Trade**
- Exempt from section 8 and subsection 15(1) if used for:
  - Gauging **tanker ship holds or tanks >55,000 L** (12,000 gallons) in downstream petroleum trade (refineries ‚Üí wholesalers ‚Üí retailers).
  - Gauging ship holds used in **wholesale trade of mining products**.

#### 2. **Specific Device Types Exempt from Section 8 & Subsection 15(1)**

These devices are exempt **regardless of use**:
- Water meters  
- Parking meters  
- Taxi meters  
- Odometers of motor vehicles  
- Coin-operated machines that weigh persons  
- Payment-operated machines dispensing liquid **without recording dispensed volume** (mechanical/electrical indicators not used)  
- Clocks, watches, chronometers, and time-measuring devices  
- Milk measuring tanks for **farm use**  
- Measuring tanks mounted on **railway cars**  
- Tanks with capacity **exceeding 55,000 L or 12,000 gallons**  
- Meters for measuring **grease or similar flowable commodities**  
- Static measures used as **containers for sale**  
- Packing devices  
- Laboratory or scientific weighing machines (used for precious metals or high-value commodities), **if**:  
  - They conform to applicable performance, installation, and use specifications;  
  - Were **examined and certified before December 31, 1981**  
- Linear static measures  
- Devices used in **downstream petroleum trade** to measure liquids in **permanent closed piping systems** (e.g., from refinery to storage), **except** when the product reaches a consumer or processing point where it is no longer considered a hydrocarbon.

#### 3. **Exemption for Standard Quantity Commodities and Crude Petroleum**

A device is exempt from section 8 and subsection 15(1) if it is used **exclusively** to:
- Measure **standard quantity commodities** (e.g., identical-sized packages of cereal)  
- Measure **crude petroleum products**

> ‚ö†Ô∏è This exemption applies only if the device is used **exclusively** for these purposes.

---

### C. **Exemptions for Measuring Tanks**

A measuring tank is exempt from **paragraph 8(a)** and **subsection 15(1)** if it conforms to:
- Design, composition, construction, and performance specifications in:
  - Divisions I, V, X, and XII of **Part V** of the regulations  
  - Any additional specifications established under **section 13**

---

### D. **Exemption for Gold, Silver, or Diamond Trade**

A device is exempt from section 8(b) and subsection 15(1) if:
- Used **exclusively** in the **wholesale trade of gold, silver, or diamonds**, **after initial refining**

> Applies only to **wholesale trade** and after refining.

---

### E. **Exemption for Replaced Meter Registers (No Compensation)**

- A meter register **approved under section 3** that:
  - Has **no automatic temperature, pressure, or density compensation**, or
  - Has such compensation **not in use**
- Is installed as a **replacement** on a previously examined and certified volumetric liquid meter
- Is exempt from section 8(b), subsection 15(1), and paragraph 26(1)(c)

> ‚ùå **Exception**: If the register is altered to include automatic compensation, this exemption **does not apply**.

---

### F. **Exemption for Trade Transactions (Written Notice to Minister)**

Under **subsection (1)**, a trade transaction may be exempt from section 8, subsection 15(1), section 23, paragraph 24(b), and section 33 **if**:

1. The parties give **written notice** to the Minister that:
   - The approved device is **not available** for the intended transaction;
   - They wish to use a **different device**;
   - It would be **impractical or unreasonably expensive** to obtain approval or comply with examination requirements.

2. The exemption **only applies if**:
   - The parties provide the Minister with required information about the device;
   - The device conforms to all Act and regulation requirements **except** design, composition, construction, and performance specs;
   - A **written contract ‚â§3 years** is entered into, specifying:
     - Limits of error applicable to the device and commodity;
     - Calibration method and interval;
   - A **true copy** of the device-use section of the contract is submitted to the Minister;
   - The device measures **within the stated limits of error**.

> ‚úÖ This exemption is **conditional** and limited to specific circumstances.

---

### G. **Exemptions for Devices Approved Under Previous Enactments**

- A device or class/type/design **approved under a previous enactment** (pre-1970‚Äì72) is exempt from **paragraph 8(a)** of the Act (subject to section 10).
- A device **examined and certified under a previous enactment** but **not approved under section 3** is also exempt from paragraph 8(a).

> This allows continuity of use for legacy equipment.

---

### H. **Exemptions for Altered Devices (Approved Before 1989)**

If a device or class/type approved under section 3 or a previous enactment, or examined and certified by an inspector **on or before December 31, 1989**, is altered to measure in **metric units** (Schedule I), it is exempt from paragraph 8(a)
