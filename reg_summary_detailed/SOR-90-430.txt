**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Title of the Order:**  
*The Dundee Estates Limited Sale Authorization Order*

**Legal Basis:**  
This regulation is issued under the authority of **subsection 91(3)** of the *Financial Administration Act* (FAA), which empowers the Governor in Council (i.e., the federal Cabinet, acting on the advice of the Minister) to authorize the sale of assets of a federal entity or its subsidiary when deemed appropriate for public interest or administrative efficiency. The Order is made pursuant to the **Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency Act (ACOA Act)** and the **Treasury Board** recommendations, indicating that the decision aligns with broader federal policy objectives related to economic development and asset optimization in Atlantic Canada.

**Key Facts of the Regulation:**

1. **Entity Involved:**  
   - **Dundee Estates Limited** – A wholly-owned subsidiary of **Enterprise Cape Breton Corporation** (ECC), a federally incorporated corporation operating in Cape Breton, Nova Scotia.  
   - The entity is being authorized to sell *all* of its assets, meaning a complete divestiture.

2. **Buyer of Assets:**  
   - **Odyssey Cape Breton Tourist Attractions Limited** – A private entity that will acquire all assets of Dundee Estates Limited. The transaction is likely aimed at transitioning ownership of tourism-related properties or infrastructure to a company with a focus on tourism development and operations.

3. **Purpose of the Authorization:**  
   - The sale is authorized to facilitate the transfer of Dundee Estates’ assets to a company better positioned to manage and operate them in a tourism context.  
   - This action supports the strategic goals of the Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency (ACOA), which promotes economic growth, job creation, and regional development in Atlantic Canada.  
   - The Treasury Board’s involvement indicates that the transaction is being evaluated for fiscal responsibility, administrative efficiency, and alignment with government policy.

4. **Legal Authority and Process:**  
   - The Governor in Council has determined, based on the recommendation of the Minister, that Dundee Estates Limited is otherwise empowered to conduct the sale.  
   - The authorization is issued under **subsection 91(6)** of the *Financial Administration Act*, which allows the Governor in Council to confirm that a corporation or subsidiary has the legal capacity to engage in asset sales.  
   - The final authorization under **subsection 91(3)** confirms the sale as a formal, government-approved transaction, ensuring compliance with financial administration standards and accountability.

5. **Scope of the Order:**  
   - The Order authorizes Dundee Estates Limited to sell **all of its assets** to Odyssey Cape Breton Tourist Attractions Limited.  
   - The sale is not limited to specific assets; it is a full transfer of ownership and operational rights, which may include real estate, land, facilities, or related intangible assets tied to tourism or recreational use.

6. **Citation:**  
   - The Order may be cited as: *The Dundee Estates Limited Sale Authorization Order*, providing a formal and official reference for legal and administrative purposes.

**Implications and Context:**

- This transaction likely represents a strategic shift in the management of Cape Breton’s tourism infrastructure, with the goal of improving service delivery, enhancing visitor experience, and supporting local economic development.  
- The transfer from a government-owned subsidiary (Enterprise Cape Breton Corporation) to a private operator (Odyssey) may improve operational efficiency and responsiveness to market demands.  
- The federal government’s involvement underscores the importance of such transactions in advancing regional economic development goals under the ACOA framework.

**Conclusion:**  
This regulation formally authorizes the complete sale of all assets of Dundee Estates Limited to Odyssey Cape Breton Tourist Attractions Limited. It is grounded in federal financial administration laws, supported by ministerial and Treasury Board recommendations, and aligned with the broader economic development objectives of the Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency. The Order ensures transparency, legal validity, and accountability in the transfer of assets, with the ultimate aim of improving tourism operations and regional prosperity in Cape Breton.
