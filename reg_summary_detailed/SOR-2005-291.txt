**Detailed Summary of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change Privileges and Immunities Order**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Purpose**

This regulation is issued under the authority of **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, based on the recommendations of two key Canadian ministers:
- The **Minister of Foreign Affairs**
- The **Minister of Finance**

It is enacted pursuant to **Section 5a of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act (FMIOA)**, which empowers the Government of Canada to grant privileges and immunities to international organizations and their representatives when they are conducting official activities within Canadian territory.

The primary purpose of this Order is to **recognize and confer specific legal privileges and immunities** upon the **United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC)** and its associated activities during a defined period, in order to facilitate the smooth operation of its meetings and preparatory sessions in Canada.

---

### **2. Applicable Legal Framework**

- The **Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act (FMIOA)** provides the foundational legal basis for granting privileges and immunities to international bodies.
- Specifically, **Schedule III** of the FMIOA contains the full text of the **Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations**, which forms the basis for the privileges and immunities granted in this Order.
- The Order draws directly from **Articles II, IV, V, and VI** of this Convention.

---

### **3. Key Definitions**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Convention** | Refers to the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, as set out in Schedule III of the FMIOA. This establishes the standard framework for diplomatic and official privileges. |
| **Organization** | Refers to the **intergovernmental conference of state parties** to the **UNFCCC**, established in New York in 1994, and the **Kyoto Protocol** (a legally binding agreement under the UNFCCC), including its **Secretariat** as established under Article 8 of the Convention. |
| **Meeting** | The main conference of the Organization held in **Montreal, Quebec**, from **November 28 to December 9, 2005**. This is the central event during which member states negotiate climate change policies. |
| **Preparatory Meetings** | A series of smaller meetings held in Montreal from **November 23 to 25, 2005**, including:  
- Expert Group on Technology Transfer  
- Executive Board of the Clean Development Mechanism (CDM)  
- Expert Meeting on Tools and Methodologies for Assessing the Impacts of Climate Response Measures |
| **Relevant Period** | The time frame from **November 20, 2005**, to **December 12, 2005**. This period encompasses the preparation, hosting, and conclusion of both the main meeting and preparatory sessions. |

---

### **4. Privileges and Immunities Granted**

#### **(a) Legal Capacity of the Organization**
> *During the relevant period, the Organization shall have in Canada the legal capacity of a body corporate and the privileges and immunities set out in Article II of the Convention.*

- The UNFCCC is recognized as a **legal entity with corporate status** in Canada during the relevant period.
- This allows the Organization to:
  - Enter into contracts
  - Own property
  - Be sued or sue others
  - Conduct business and administrative functions in Canada
- The privileges and immunities under **Article II** include:
  - Immunity from legal process (e.g., suits for torts or civil claims)
  - Immunity from taxation
  - Immunity from search and seizure
  - Freedom from interference with its operations

#### **(b) Privileges and Immunities of State Representatives**
> *Representatives of states and governments that are members of the Organization shall have in Canada, to such extent as may be required for the exercise of their functions in Canada in relation to the Meeting and Preparatory Meetings, the privileges and immunities set out in Article IV of the Convention.*

- Diplomatic envoys and government delegates attending the meetings are granted:
  - **Immunity from arrest or detention** (except in cases of serious criminal offenses)
  - **Immunity from civil and criminal liability** in Canada while performing official duties
  - Protection from service of process (e.g., subpoenas or court orders)
  - Freedom to conduct official business without fear of legal action
- These protections are **limited in scope** and apply only to actions directly related to the Meeting and Preparatory Meetings.

#### **(c) Privileges and Immunities of Organization Officials**
> *Officials of the Organization shall have in Canada, to such extent as may be required for the exercise of their functions in Canada in relation to the Meeting and Preparatory Meetings, the privileges and immunities set out in Article V of the Convention.*

- Staff members of the UNFCCC Secretariat and related bodies (e.g., technical officers, coordinators, administrators) are granted:
  - Immunity from civil and criminal liability for acts performed in the course of their official duties
  - Protection from arrest or detention during official work
  - Freedom from interference with their professional activities
- These rights are strictly tied to their role in organizing and conducting the meetings.

#### **(d) Privileges and Immunities of Experts**
> *Experts invited by the Organization to attend the Meeting and Preparatory Meetings shall have in Canada, to such extent as may be required for the exercise of their functions in Canada in relation to those Meetings, the privileges and immunities set out in Article VI of the Convention.*

- Experts (e.g., scientists, economists, technical advisors) invited to participate in the meetings are granted limited immunities:
  - Immunity from civil and criminal liability for statements or actions made in the course of their official participation
  - Protection from legal action arising from their expert contributions
- These immunities are **conditional** and only extend to activities directly related to the meetings.

---

### **5. Limitations and Exclusions**

> *The privileges and immunities referred to in section 2 do not apply to Canadian citizens and permanent residents of Canada in respect of liability for taxes or duties imposed by any law in Canada.*

- **No immunity from taxation** applies to:
  - Canadian citizens
  - Permanent residents
- This means that individuals from Canada are **not protected from tax obligations** (e.g., income, property, or capital gains taxes) simply because they are involved in or attend the meetings.
- This exclusion ensures that Canada maintains full fiscal control and compliance with domestic tax laws.

---

### **6. Effective Date and Legal Status**

- The Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** with the Statutes of Canada.
- It is a **temporary, time-bound** measure, valid only during the **relevant period** (November 20, 2005 – December 12, 2005).
- It is **not a permanent or ongoing status**; it is specifically designed to support a short-term international event.

---

### **7. Significance and Context**

- This Order was issued in preparation for the **first major UNFCCC conference held in Canada**, marking a significant moment in Canada’s engagement with global climate policy.
- By granting privileges and immunities, Canada:
  - Facilitated international cooperation
  - Ensured that diplomatic and technical activities could proceed without disruption or legal interference
  - Demonstrated Canada’s commitment to multilateral environmental governance
- It reflects Canada’s adherence to international norms and the principle of **diplomatic immunity** under the UN Charter and the FMIOA.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation is a **targeted, temporary legislative instrument** that enables the **United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change** and its associated participants to operate freely and securely in Canada during a critical period in 2005. It provides a clear legal framework for the **legal capacity of the Organization**, **immunity for state representatives, officials, and experts**, while explicitly **excluding tax liabilities** for Canadian nationals. The Order underscores Canada’s role as a responsible host nation in international environmental diplomacy and aligns with established international law governing diplomatic privileges.
