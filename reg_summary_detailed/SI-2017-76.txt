### **3. General Application and Procedure**

#### **Applicable Rules**:
- These Rules apply to **all criminal or summary conviction appeal proceedings**, unless otherwise provided in statute.
- Unless otherwise stated, all applications must be made on **Form CC1**.

#### **Filing and Service Requirements**:
- Applications must be filed with the **judicial centre** where the criminal proceeding is being or will be heard.
- **Urgent applications** may be brought **without notice**, solely for scheduling purposes (e.g., setting dates for service or hearing).
- Applications must be heard on the date and time **set by the Court** in that judicial centre.

#### **Supporting Documents (Rule 7)**:
At least **seven days before the hearing**, the applicant must:
1. File:
   - An **affidavit**, **transcript**, and/or **detailed particulars of evidence**.
   - A **summary of the legal argument** supporting the relief sought.
2. Serve these documents on:
   - Every party to the proceeding.
   - Every person who could be **directly affected** by the proposed order.

> Failure to appear at the hearing may result in the application being **dismissed as abandoned**.

### **5. Specific Applications and Procedures**

#### **A. Mandamus, Certiorari, Habeas Corpus, Prohibition**
- Governed by:
  - **Part 3, Division 2, Subdivision 2 of the Alberta Rules of Court**
  - **Division 1 of these Criminal Procedure Rules**
- In case of **conflict**, **Division 1 prevails**.

> This ensures consistency and prioritizes the criminal procedure rules over general civil court rules in criminal appeals.

#### **B. Judicial Interim Release (Rule 8)**
- Applications must be filed using **Form CC3**.
- Must be served on the respondent at least **two days before** the hearing date.

#### **C. Detention Review (Section 525 of the Criminal Code)**
- Must be filed by the **institution of detention** using **Form CC4**.
- Filed in the judicial centre where the accused is in custody or where trial is to occur.
- Upon receipt:
  - The Court must **advise of the hearing date and time**.
  - Provide a copy of **Form CC5** to designated counsel or to the institution (if no counsel is designated), to be shared with the accused.

#### **D. Charter Rights Applications**
- Must be made on **Form CC1** and follow Division 1 procedures.
- However, **supporting documents must be filed and served at least**:
  - **7 days before the pre-trial conference**, or  
  - **60 days before trial**, whichever is earlier â€” or as directed by the Court.

> This ensures sufficient time for review and preparation when rights under the *Charter* are challenged.

#### **E. Challenges to Jurors (Section 638(1)(b))**
- Must be filed at least **60 days before jury selection**.
- Must include:
  - A **notice of challenge** with a **ground** in accordance with **Section 639** and **Form 41** of the Code.
- The **court coordinator** must schedule a **pre-trial conference** between:
  - Trial judge or case management judge
  - Prosecutor
  - Accused
- Purpose: To resolve challenges and avoid unnecessary delays.

- Publication bans

- Must be made under **Division 1** unless required by statute.
- Requires additional submissions:

  - A **draft of the requested order**

#### Jurisdiction:
- **Sealing entire files or setting aside sealing orders**:
  - Must be made to:
    - Chief Justice
    - Associate Chief Justice
    - A judge designated by the Chief Justice
- **All other applications**:
  - Must be made to the **trial judge** or, if no trial judge assigned, to another judge.

#### Filing and Public Notice Requirements:
- Must file **three paper copies** of **Form CC6** with the clerk.
- Provide **one electronic copy** to the clerk.
- If the clerk has a list of registered media organizations:
  - Forward the electronic copy to them.
- The clerk must **post a copy** of Form CC6 in a public notice area at the courthouse.

> This balances **privacy concerns** with **public accountability**, especially in sensitive cases involving witness protection or victim safety.
