**Detailed Summary of the United Nations University International Network on Water, Environment and Health – Privileges and Immunities Order**

---

### **Overview**

This regulation, known as the *United Nations University International Network on Water, Environment and Health – Privileges and Immunities Order*, is a legal instrument issued under the authority of the **Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act (FMIOA)**. It grants specific **privileges and immunities** to the **International Network on Water, Environment and Health (INWEH)** — a specialized body established through an agreement between Canada and the United Nations University (UNU).

The Order is enacted by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs** and the **Minister of Finance**, pursuant to specific provisions of the FMIOA:

- Subsection **5(1)a** and **(2)b** – Authority to grant privileges and immunities to international organizations.
- Subsection **13(3)c** – Authority to provide legal status and immunities to international bodies operating under agreements with Canada.

This Order was first introduced in **S.C. 2002, c. 12, ss. 3(1) to (4)** and is referenced in other statutory provisions, including:
- S.C. 1995, c. 5, par. 25(1)(n): Recognition of international organizations under Canadian law.
- S.C. 2002, c. 12, s. 8: Framework for implementation of privileges and immunities.
- S.C. 1991, c. 41: General framework for diplomatic and international organization immunities.

### **Legal Status and Privileges and Immunities**

#### 1. **Legal Capacity of INWEH**
- INWEH is granted the **legal capacity of a body corporate**.
- This means it can:
  - Enter into contracts,
  - Own property,
  - Sue and be sued,
  - Hold assets and liabilities in its own name.
- This status enables INWEH to operate independently and formally within Canadian legal frameworks.

#### 2. **Privileges and Immunities of INWEH (under the UN Convention)**
- INWEH is entitled to the full range of **privileges and immunities** as outlined in **Articles II and III of the United Nations Convention**.
- These include:
  - Immunity from legal process (e.g., lawsuits) in Canada.
  - Exemption from taxation.
  - Freedom from interference in its operations.
  - Protection of its property and communications.
- These privileges are granted because INWEH is recognized as a **United Nations-related international organization** operating under the UN system.

#### 3. **Director and Family Members**
- The **Director** of INWEH and **members of their household** (who are not Canadian citizens or permanent residents) are granted **privileges and immunities comparable to those of diplomatic agents** under the **Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations**.
- This includes:
  - Immunity from arrest and detention.
  - Protection from search or seizure of personal property.
  - Exemption from certain laws (e.g., criminal jurisdiction).
- The exemption applies **only to non-Canadian citizens or permanent residents** — Canadian nationals are not covered under this provision.

#### 4. **Officials of the University**
- Officials of the University (i.e., staff of the UNU) who fall within the scope of **Section 19 of Article V of the UN Convention** and whose families form part of their household are entitled to:
  - Privileges and immunities **comparable to those of diplomatic agents** under the Vienna Convention.
- Specifically, these include:
  - Immunity from criminal prosecution.
  - Protection of personal and official communications.
  - Exemption from service of legal process.
- These rights are limited to those specified in **Sections 18 and 19 of Article V** and **Article VII** of the UN Convention.

#### 5. **Privileges and Immunities of Officials (Section 18 of Article V)**
- All officials of the University are granted the privileges and immunities set out in **Section 18 of Article V of the UN Convention**.
- This includes:
  - Immunity from civil and criminal liability in the performance of official duties.
  - Protection of official documents and correspondence.

#### 6. **Experts of the University**
- Experts (scientific, technical, or advisory personnel) are granted the privileges and immunities set out in **Article VI of the UN Convention**.
- These include:
  - Immunity from legal proceedings related to their professional activities.
  - Protection of their personal and professional communications.
  - Freedom from interference in their work.

#### 7. **Personnel of INWEH**
- INWEH staff (personnel) are granted privileges and immunities **to the extent specified in Section 4 of Article IX of the Agreement**.
- These rights are **comparable to those of diplomatic agents** under:
  - **Articles 31 and 35** of the Vienna Convention (which cover immunity from arrest and protection of property and communications).
  - **Paragraph (1)(b) of Article 36** of the Vienna Convention (which deals with immunity from legal proceedings in the performance of official duties).
- This means INWEH personnel may enjoy protection from arrest, search, or legal action while carrying out their duties, consistent with diplomatic standards.

### **Purpose and Significance**

This Order serves several key purposes:

1. **International Cooperation Facilitation**  
   It enables INWEH — a global network focused on critical environmental and public health issues — to operate effectively in Canada without being subject to undue legal or administrative interference.

2. **Legal Recognition and Sovereignty**  
   By granting INWEH the status of a body corporate and full privileges and immunities, Canada recognizes INWEH as a legitimate international entity under international law.

3. **Protection of Experts and Officials**  
   The provision of diplomatic-level immunities ensures that researchers, scientists, and administrators can carry out their work without fear of legal retaliation or disruption — crucial for high-level scientific collaboration.

4. **Alignment with International Standards**  
   The Order ensures that Canada’s treatment of INWEH aligns with the **Vienna Convention** and the **UN Convention**, reinforcing Canada’s commitment to international norms in diplomacy and multilateral cooperation.

5. **Support for Global Environmental and Health Goals**  
   INWEH focuses on water, environment, and health — pressing global challenges. This legal framework supports Canada’s role in addressing these issues through international partnerships.

---

### **Limitations and Exclusions**

- **Canadian citizens and permanent residents** are **not entitled** to the diplomatic-level immunities for the Director or officials — the protection applies only to non-Canadians.
- Privileges are **not absolute** — they are limited to the scope defined in the UN Convention and the Agreement.
- Immunities do **not extend to criminal acts unrelated to official duties** or to violations of public order.
- The Order does **not confer immunity from tax obligations** in all cases — only those specifically listed in the UN Convention.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation establishes a formal, legally binding framework that grants the **International Network on Water, Environment and Health (INWEH)** — a specialized UN-affiliated body — the **status of a body corporate** and the **privileges and immunities** of a diplomatic entity under international law. It ensures that key personnel, including the Director, officials, experts, and INWEH staff, can operate freely and securely in Canada, supporting scientific collaboration and international environmental health initiatives.

By aligning with the **Vienna Convention** and the **UN Convention on Privileges and Immunities**, the Order reflects Canada’s adherence to international norms and strengthens its role in global environmental and public health governance
