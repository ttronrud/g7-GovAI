### **Detailed Summary of the Cross-border Currency and Monetary Instruments Reporting Regulations**

These regulations, made under **subsection 73(1)a of the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act (PCMLTFA)**, establish a comprehensive framework for the **reporting of cross-border movements of currency and monetary instruments** into or out of Canada. The goal is to **prevent money laundering and terrorist financing** by requiring transparency in the importation and exportation of high-value financial assets.

---

## **I. Purpose and Legal Basis**

- The regulations are enacted by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, pursuant to **section 73(1)a of the PCMLTFA**.
- The primary objective is to **identify and monitor suspicious cross-border transactions involving currency and monetary instruments** to detect potential illicit financial activity.

---

## **II. Key Definitions**

### **A. Definitions in the Act and Regulations**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Currency** | Coins under the *Currency Act*, Bank of Canada notes intended for circulation in Canada, or coins/banknotes from other countries. |
| **Monetary Instruments** | Bearer instruments such as: <br> - Securities (stocks, bonds, debentures, treasury bills) <br> - Negotiable instruments (bank drafts, cheques, promissory notes, travellers’ cheques, money orders) <br> *Excluded*: warehouse receipts or bills of lading, especially those with restrictive endorsements or clearing stamps. |
| **Courier** | A commercial carrier engaged in scheduled international transport of goods (not mail). |
| **Cargo Ship** | A commercial vessel transporting goods (not mail). |
| **Commercial Passenger Conveyance** | A vehicle (aircraft, ship, vehicle) carrying passengers who have paid for passage. |
| **Non-Commercial Passenger Conveyance** | Vehicles without paying passengers (e.g., private aircraft, pleasure craft). |
| **Conveyance** | Any vehicle, aircraft, or watercraft used to move persons, goods, currency, or instruments. |
| **Cruise Ship** | A commercial vessel with sleeping facilities for over 70 non-crew passengers, excluding ferry services. |
| **Transfer Agent** | A corporation-appointed entity responsible for maintaining stock records, issuing certificates, and distributing dividends. |

> **Note**: The definition of *monetary instruments* excludes instruments with restrictive endorsements or clearing stamps, which are typically used in formal financial systems.

---

## **III. Reporting Threshold**

- The **prescribed reporting amount** is **$10,000 CAD**.
- This amount is **in Canadian dollars**, or **in equivalent foreign currency**, using:
  - The **Bank of Canada’s published exchange rate** at the time of importation/exportation; or
  - The **ordinary business exchange rate** used by the person/entity if no official rate is published.

> This threshold applies to both **importation and exportation** of currency or monetary instruments.

---

## **IV. Who Must Report and When**

Reports must be made by specific persons/entities under **paragraphs 12(3)(a) to (e)** of the PCMLTFA:

| Person/Entity | Reporting Requirement |
|-------------|------------------------|
| **(a)** Person transporting currency/instruments (e.g., courier, passenger, freight crew) | Must submit a written report at the customs office upon arrival or departure. |
| **(b)** Entity transporting on behalf of another | Reports required under Schedule 2. |
| **(c)** Entity acting as a transfer agent | Reports required under Schedule 2. |
| **(d)** Person or entity involved in mail or courier services | Reports required under Schedule 3. |

> **General Rule**: All known information relevant to each item in the schedule must be reported — even if multiple items are involved.

---

## **V. Reporting Procedures by Mode of Transport**

### **1. Arrival in Canada – General Rule**
- Reports must be submitted **without delay** at the **customs office at the place of importation**.
- If the office is closed, submit to the **nearest open customs office**.

### **2. Arrival on a Commercial Passenger Conveyance (e.g., plane, ship)**
- If destination is **another place in Canada**, the report may be submitted at the destination customs office **if**:
  - The person does **not disembark** in Canada.
  - The currency/instruments are **not removed** from the conveyance.
  - If transferred to a **designated holding area**, the person and instruments remain there until reloaded for departure.

> Applies to both importation and exportation.

### **3. Arrival on a Non-Commercial Conveyance (e.g., private plane, yacht)**
- If customs reporting is allowed via **radio or telephone** (under the *Presentation of Persons (Customs) Regulations*):
  - The person or person in charge must **provide Schedule 1, 2, or 3 information** upon arrival.
  - Upon request, they must **present themselves and make the instruments available for examination**.

> Alternative reporting by phone is permitted **only** if the person is authorized under the 2003 Regulations.

### **4. Freight Train Crew Member**
- Must submit report **without delay** at the **customs office specified** by the officer upon presentation under section 11 of the *Customs Act*.

### **5. Courier Transporting Instruments into Canada**
- If destination is **another place in Canada**, the report must be submitted at the **customs office at the destination airport** (as per air waybill).
- Instruments must **not be removed** from the aircraft at arrival, unless transferred under customs control to a **designated holding area**.
- If in a holding area, they may not be removed except to board another flight.

### **6. Mail-Related Transactions**
- **Importation by mail**:  
  - Include an **importation report inside the mail item**.  
  - Affix the **customs declaration form** (as per Universal Postal Convention) to the outside, clearly indicating the presence of currency/instruments.

- **Exportation by mail**:  
  - Include an **exportation report inside the mail item**.  
  - Submit a **copy of the report** to the nearest customs office **before or at the time of mailing**.

> **Exception**: If the mail item is **transiting through Canada** from outside to outside, and will not leave the postal route until after leaving Canada, **no report is required**.

---

## **VI. Exceptions to Reporting Requirements**

| Exception | Condition |
|---------|----------|
| **Traveling to or from outside Canada** | If the person or instruments are on a **commercial passenger conveyance** and do not disembark or remove instruments in Canada (except for transfer under customs control). |
| **Cruise ships or cargo ships** | Instruments transported to a destination outside Canada are **not required to be reported** if they are **not removed from the vessel while in Canada**. |
| **Bank of Canada operations** | Reports are **not required** for currency movement by the Bank of Canada for **distribution, processing, or testing** of banknotes. |
| **Financial entities or transfer agents** | If currency or securities are imported by a **financial entity or securities dealer** (as defined in the PCMLTFA Regulations), or by a **transfer agent**, **no report is required**. |

---

## **VII. Retention of Reports and Notices**

- **Retention Period**:
  - For **courier or mail transactions**: **30 days** after the notice is given or sent.
  - For **all other cases**: **7 days** after notice is given or sent.

- **Notice of Retention (Section 14)**:
  - An officer must provide **written notice** (in person or by registered mail) to the person/entity.
  - Notice must be given within **60 days** of importation or exportation.
  - The notice must include:
    - The person/entity’s name, address, citizenship, date of birth, contact info.
    - Jurisdiction and country of document issuance.
    - Type of transaction (import/export).
    - Dates, departure/arrival points, transit points, purpose.

---

## **VIII. Penalties for Non-Compliance**

Penalties under **section 18(2)** of the PCMLTFA depend on the severity of the violation:

| Violation Type | Penalty |
|---------------|--------|
| **No concealment**, full disclosure, **no prior seizures** | 5% of value, up to **$2,500** |
| **Concealment (not via false compartment)**, false statement, or **prior seizure** | 25% of value |
| **Concealment via false compartment**, or **prior seizure for concealment/false statement** | 50% of value |

> Penalties are applied to the **value of seized currency or instruments**.
