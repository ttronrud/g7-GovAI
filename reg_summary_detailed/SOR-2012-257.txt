## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Refugee Appeal Division Rules (2012)**

### **1. Legal Authority and Approval**
- These rules were **made by the Chairperson of the Immigration and Refugee Board (IRB)** under **subsection 161(1) of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)**.
- The rules were **approved by the Governor in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration**.
- The Chairperson prepared the rules **in consultation with the Deputy Chairpersons and the Director General of the Immigration Division**.
- The rules are binding and govern all procedures related to appeals before the **Refugee Appeal Division (Division)** of the IRB.

> **Source**: S.C. 2001, c. 27 (original IRPA framework)

### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act* (IRPA). |
| **Appellant** | A person appealing a decision, or the Minister making an appeal from the Refugee Protection Division. |
| **Contact Information** | Name, postal address, telephone number, fax, and email. For counsel, also includes the name of the legal body and membership ID (if counsel is from a recognized bar or organization). |
| **Division** | The Refugee Appeal Division of the IRB. |
| **Interested Person** | A person who has been granted permission to participate in an appeal under **Rule 46**. |
| **Party** | In an individual appeal: the appellant and, if the Minister intervenes, the Minister. In a Ministerial appeal: the appellant and the Minister. |
| **Proceeding** | Includes conferences, applications, or appeals ‚Äî decided with or without a hearing. |
| **Registry Office** | A physical office or business location of the Division where documents are filed. |
| **Regulations** | Refers to the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations* (IRPR), which set time limits and procedural requirements. |
| **Respondent** | In a Ministerial appeal, the person whose decision is being challenged. |

### **3. Filing an Appeal (by an Individual)**

#### ‚úÖ Requirements:
- The appellant must submit **three copies** of a **written notice of appeal** to the Division.
- The Division must **immediately forward** a copy of the notice to the Minister.

#### üìù Contents of the Notice of Appeal:
1. Appellant‚Äôs name and telephone number; address for document delivery.
2. If represented by counsel:
   - Counsel‚Äôs contact information.
   - Any limitations on the retainer (e.g., fee cap).
3. Identification number issued by the Department of Citizenship and Immigration (DCI).
4. Refugee Protection Division (RPD) file number.
5. Date of the notice of decision being appealed.
6. Date the appellant received the written reasons for the decision.
7. Language preference (English or French).
8. Contact information of the designated representative (if any), and any proposed change.

#### ‚è±Ô∏è Timeliness:
- The notice must be received by the Division **within the time limit set in the Regulations** (e.g., 30 days from receipt of decision).

### **4. Perfecting an Appeal (Submitting the Appellant‚Äôs Record)**

#### ‚úÖ Requirements:
- The appellant must submit **two copies** of the **appellant‚Äôs record** to the Division.
- The Division must **immediately forward** a copy to the Minister.

#### üìö Contents of the Appellant‚Äôs Record (in order on consecutively numbered pages):
1. **Notice of decision and written reasons** from the RPD.
2. **Transcript of RPD hearing** (if relied upon), with a **signed declaration** by the transcriber stating:
   - Transcriber‚Äôs name.
   - That the transcript is accurate.
3. **Documents refused by RPD** as evidence (if relied upon).
4. **Written statement** indicating:
   - Whether the appellant is relying on evidence under **subsection 110(4)** of IRPA (e.g., new evidence).
   - Whether a **hearing is requested** under **subsection 110(6)**.
     - If so, whether a **change of hearing location** is requested under **Rule 66**.
     - The language/dialect for interpretation (if needed).
5. **Documentary evidence** to be relied upon.
6. **Legal authorities** (laws, case law, etc.) to be relied upon.
7. **Memorandum** containing:
   - Full and detailed submissions on:
     - The **errors** that form the grounds of appeal.
     - Where those errors are located (in written reasons, transcript, or recordings).
     - How documentary evidence meets **110(4)** requirements and relates to the appellant.
     - The **decision the appellant seeks**.
     - Why a **hearing is necessary** under 110(6).

#### üìè Page Limit:
- **Maximum 30 pages** if typed on one side.
- **Maximum 15 pages** if typed on both sides.

### **5. Minister‚Äôs Intervention in an Appeal**

#### ‚úÖ When:
- The Minister may intervene **at any time before a decision is made**.

#### üìù Requirements:
- Must provide:
  - First to the **appellant**, then to the **Division**:
    - A **written notice of intervention**.
    - Any documentary evidence to be relied upon.
- The notice must include:
  - Counsel‚Äôs contact information.
  - Appellant‚Äôs identification number, name, RPD file number, date of decision, and date of receipt of reasons.
  - Whether relying on evidence under **110(3)** and its relevance.
  - Whether requesting a hearing under **110(6)**, with justification.
    - If so, whether a **change of hearing location** is requested (under Rule 66).

#### üìö Additional Documents (Minister‚Äôs Intervention Record):
- Transcript of RPD hearing (if not included in appellant‚Äôs record), with **signed declaration** of accuracy.
- Legal authorities relied upon.
- **Memorandum** containing:
  - Full and detailed submissions on:
    - Grounds of appeal.
    - The decision the Minister seeks.
- **Page Limit**: Same as above ‚Äî **30 pages (one side)** or **15 pages (two sides)**.

#### üìé Proof Requirement:
- Must provide **proof** that the documents were delivered to the appellant.

#### ‚è±Ô∏è Deadline:
- All documents must be received by the Division **within 15 days** of the appellant receiving the Minister‚Äôs notice of intervention or any additional documents.

### **6. Responding to Minister‚Äôs Intervention**

#### ‚úÖ Requirements:
- The appellant must provide:
  - First to the **Minister**, then to the **Division**:
    - A **reply record**.

#### üìö Contents of Reply Record:
1. Transcript of RPD hearing (if relied upon and not previously provided), with **signed declaration** of accuracy.
2. New documentary evidence (not in original record or Minister‚Äôs submission).
3. New legal authorities (not previously submitted).
4. **Memorandum** containing:
   - Only the grounds raised by the Minister.
   - How new evidence meets **110(4)** or **110(5)** requirements and relates to the appellant.
   - Why a hearing is needed under **110(6)** (if not previously requested).
   - Whether a **change of hearing location** is requested (under Rule 66).

#### üìè Page Limit:
- Same as above: **30 pages (one side)** or **15 pages (two sides)**.

#### üìé Proof Requirement:
- Must provide **proof** that the reply record was delivered to the Minister.

  - Extension to **respond** to a Minister‚Äôs intervention.

#### üìù Requirements:

  - Counsel‚Äôs contact info (if applicable) and retainer limitations.

  - RPD file number, date of decision, and date of receipt of reasons.

#### üîÅ Specific Requirements:
