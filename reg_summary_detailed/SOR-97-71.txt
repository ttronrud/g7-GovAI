### **Detailed Summary of the Exporters' and Producers' Records Regulations**

---

#### **1. Regulatory Background and Legal Basis**

The **Exporters' and Producers' Records Regulations** are a set of administrative rules implemented under the **Customs Act** (Canada), specifically to fulfill obligations arising from the **Canada-International Free Trade Agreements (CIFTA)** and related trade agreements such as NAFTA, CCFTA, CEFTA, and others.

- These regulations **do not introduce new substantive changes** to existing requirements but rather **implement a provision of CIFTA**.
- As such, they fall under **paragraphs 164(4)(a.03)a and (d)** of the Customs Act, which state that regulations that make no material substantive change to existing rules are **not required to be published under subsection 164(3)** of the Act.
- The regulations were made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of National Revenue**, pursuant to:
  - Subsection **97.2(1)c** of the Customs Act,
  - Paragraph **164(1)(i)d**,
  - Subsection **164(1.1)e** of the Customs Act.

This means the regulations were issued through a standard administrative process, without requiring public consultation or publication under the usual notice provisions due to their minor, interpretive nature.

---

#### **2. Purpose of the Regulations**

The primary purpose of these regulations is to establish **specific record-keeping obligations** for **exporters and producers of commercial goods**, particularly in the context of **origin rules and compliance with free trade agreements (FTAs)**.

These records are essential for:
- Demonstrating compliance with **rules of origin**,
- Supporting **advance rulings** (formal determinations by customs authorities on whether goods meet origin criteria),
- Enabling **verification visits** by customs administrations of free trade partners,
- Ensuring transparency and accountability in the production and export processes.

---

#### **3. Key Definitions**

The regulations define several key terms to ensure clarity and consistency in application:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Customs Act*. |
| **Advance ruling** | A formal determination by the customs authority of a free trade partner (e.g., under Article 509 of NAFTA, Article 5.8 of CIFTA, or similar provisions in other FTAs) that goods meet the rules of origin. |
| **Commercial goods** | Goods exported for sale or for industrial, commercial, institutional, or other uses ‚Äî not just for personal or non-commercial purposes. |
| **Material** | Any good used in the production of another good, including parts, ingredients, or components. |
| **Indirect material** | Goods not physically incorporated into the final product but used in production, testing, or maintenance. Examples include: <br> - Fuel and energy <br> - Tools, dies, molds <br> - Spare parts and lubricants <br> - Safety equipment <br> - Testing/inspection equipment <br> - Catalysts and solvents <br> - Any other item reasonably demonstrable as part of production. |
| **Producer** | A person who grows, mines, harvests, fishes, traps, hunts, manufactures, processes, or assembles a good. |
| **Production** | Any of the activities listed above in the definition of "producer". |

These definitions are critical because they determine what constitutes a "material" or "indirect material" for record-keeping purposes and help clarify the scope of required documentation.

---

#### **4. Record-Keeping Obligations**

##### **A. General Exporters (Who Export Commercial Goods)**

Every person who exports commercial goods and is required under **subsection 97.2(1)** of the Customs Act to maintain records must keep the following for **six years after the export date**:

1. **Origin, purchase, importation, costs, and value** of the commercial goods;
2. **Payment records** related to the commercial goods;
3. **Usage of the goods in Canada** (e.g., whether they were sold, used in manufacturing, etc.);
4. **Exportation details** (how and when the goods were exported);
5. **Source of all materials**, including both **direct and indirect materials**, used in the production of the goods.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note:* This includes detailed documentation of all inputs ‚Äî even those not physically part of the final product (e.g., lubricants, tools, energy).

---

##### **B. Exporters with an Advance Ruling Certificate**

An exporter who has completed and signed a certificate under **subsection 97.1(1)** of the Act must, in addition to the above records, maintain for **six years from the date the certificate was signed**:

1. **Records relating to applications for or receipt of advance rulings** from the customs administration of the applicable free trade partner;
2. **If the exporter is not a producer**, they must also keep **written representations from the producer** stating that the goods meet the rules of origin under the applicable FTA.

> üí° This ensures that exporters can demonstrate compliance with origin rules when challenged by a free trade partner.

---

##### **C. Producers of Commercial Goods**

A producer who has signed a certificate under subsection 97.1(1) and is required to keep records must maintain for **six years from the date of the certificate**:

1. **Purchase, cost, value, and payment records** for the commercial goods themselves;
2. **Purchase, cost, value, and payment records** for all materials and indirect materials used in production;
3. **Applications for or receipt of advance rulings** from the customs administration of the applicable free trade partner.

> üîç This places a strong obligation on producers to document every input into the production process, including indirect materials, which are often overlooked.

---

#### **5. Availability of Records for Verification**

Any person required to maintain records under these regulations must:

- **Provide access** to those records upon **written notification** from the customs administration of a free trade partner requesting a **verification visit**;
- Allow inspection at **all reasonable times**;
- Provide a **facility for inspection** (e.g., a secure office or digital system).

> ‚úÖ This enables free trade partners to verify compliance with origin rules and ensures transparency in the supply chain.

---

#### **6. Format and Storage of Records**

The regulations allow flexibility in how records are stored, provided they are reliable and accessible:

- **Machine-sensible data media** (e.g., digital files, databases) are acceptable **if**:
  - They can be traced back to original source documents; and
  - The system can produce **accessible and readable copies** (e.g., PDFs, printouts).

- **Microfilm** is also permitted **if** it complies with **National Standard of Canada CAN2-72.11-79** ("Microfilm as Documentary Evidence"), published by the Canadian General Standards Board and amended from time to time.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note:* The microfilm provision was later **repealed** (removed), indicating that digital formats are now preferred and sufficient.

---

#### **7. Effective Date**

These regulations come into force on the day that **Section 38 of the Canada-Israel Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act (S.C. 1996, c. 33)** comes into force.

- As noted in the footnote, **January 1, 1997**, is the effective date of the regulations in force.

---

#### **8. Repealed Provisions**

- A prior provision (repealed by **SOR/97-330, s. 6**) was previously included but has been removed.
- The microfilm requirement, though listed, was later **repealed**, reflecting a shift toward digital record-keeping.

---

### **Key Takeaways and Implications**

| Aspect | Significance |
|-------|-------------|
| **No Major Substantive Change** | These regulations are interpretive, not transformative ‚Äî they clarify existing obligations under CIFTA and the Customs Act. |
| **Focus on Origin Compliance** | The rules ensure that both exporters and producers can prove that goods meet origin requirements under FTAs. |
| **Comprehensive Record-Keeping** | Covers not just direct inputs but also indirect materials (e.g., lubricants, tools, energy), which are often overlooked. |
| **Advance Ruling Support** | Requires documentation of advance rulings, which are critical for certainty in cross-border trade. |
| **Transparency & Verification** | Enables free trade partners to verify compliance through inspections, promoting trust in trade flows. |
| **Digital Flexibility** | Allows modern storage solutions (digital files) as long as traceability and accessibility are maintained. |

---

### **Conclusion**

The **Exporters' and Producers' Records Regulations** provide a clear, enforceable framework for maintaining accurate, detailed, and accessible records related to the production and export of commercial goods under Canada‚Äôs free trade agreements. While the regulations are technically minor in scope, their practical impact is significant in ensuring **compliance with rules of origin**, **facilitating trade**, and **building confidence among trading partners**.

They represent a foundational element of Canada‚Äôs compliance regime in international trade, emphasizing **transparency, accountability, and traceability** throughout the supply chain ‚Äî especially for goods subject to origin rules.
