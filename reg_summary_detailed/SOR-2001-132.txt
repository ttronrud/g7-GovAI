## **Agriculture and Agri-Food Administrative Monetary Penalties Regulations Respecting the Pest Control Products Act and Regulations**  
**(S.C. 1995, c. 40; SI/2000-94; March 29, 2001)**

---

### **1. Regulatory Authority and Purpose**

This regulation was issued by the **Minister of Health** under:
- **Subsection 4(1)** of the *Agriculture and Agri-Food Administrative Monetary Penalties Act (the "Act")*, and
- The **Order Transferring Powers from the Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food to the Minister of Health** regarding enforcement of the Pest Control Products Act.

**Purpose**:  
To establish administrative monetary penalties (penalties) for violations of the *Pest Control Products Act* and related regulations (e.g., Sales Information Reporting, Incident Reporting), with clear definitions, classifications, and penalty amounts based on the nature and severity of the violation.

The regulation provides a framework for enforcing compliance with pest control product laws, including provisions related to safety, labeling, sales reporting, and incident reporting.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Agriculture and Agri-Food Administrative Monetary Penalties Act*. |
| **Minor Harm** | Actual or potential harm whose effects can be reversed at a cost **not exceeding $1,000**. Applies to minor environmental, health, or financial impacts. |
| **Serious Harm** | Actual or potential harm that **does not constitute minor harm**. Includes harm exceeding $1,000 in monetary loss or significant health/environmental damage. |

> These definitions are used to classify violations as minor, serious, or very serious.

---

### **3. Violations Subject to Penalties**

Any of the following constitute a violation and may be proceeded with under the Act:

| Source | Provisions Covered |
|-------|--------------------|
| **Pest Control Products Act** | Provisions listed in **Column 1 of Part 1 of Schedule 1** |
| **Pest Control Products Regulations** | Provisions listed in **Column 1 of Part 2 of Schedule 1** |
| **Pest Control Products Sales Information Reporting Regulations** | Provisions listed in **Column 1 of Part 3 of Schedule 1** |
| **Pest Control Products Incident Reporting Regulations** | Provisions listed in **Column 1 of Part 4 of Schedule 1** |

> These provisions cover unauthorized use, improper labeling, failure to report sales or incidents, and other regulatory breaches.

---

### **4. Short-Form Descriptions and Violation Classification**

- Each violation has a **short-form description** (in Column 2 of Schedule 1) to simplify enforcement notices.
- However, **in case of any discrepancy**, the **legislative provision itself takes precedence** over the short-form description.

#### Violation Classification:

| Type of Violation | Classification |
|------------------|----------------|
| Causes or could cause **minor harm** (e.g., low-level environmental or health risk, under $1,000 loss) | **Minor violation** |
| Could cause **serious harm** (e.g., significant health/environmental risk, loss >$1,000) | **Serious violation** |
| Causes **serious harm** (e.g., major harm to health, environment, or people) | **Very serious violation** |

> The classification determines the applicable penalty amount.

---

### **5. Penalty Amounts**

Penalties vary based on:
- The **type of violator** (individual vs. business),
- Whether the violation was **committed for financial gain**,
- The **severity** of the violation (minor, serious, very serious).

#### A. Individual Violators (Not in Business or for Financial Gain)

| Violation Type | Penalty |
|---------------|--------|
| Minor violation | $500 |
| Serious violation | $800 |
| Very serious violation | $1,300 |

#### B. Violators in Business or for Financial Benefit

| Violation Type | Penalty |
|---------------|--------|
| Minor violation | $1,300 |
| Serious violation | $6,000 |
| Very serious violation | $10,000 |

> **Adjustments** to these penalties are applied based on the **total gravity value** established under **Schedule 2**, which considers:
- Prior violations,
- Degree of intent or negligence,
- Harm caused.

---

### **6. Determining Total Gravity Value (for Serious/Very Serious Violations)**

The total gravity value is calculated by assessing three criteria:

| Criterion | Source in Schedule 3 |
|--------|------------------------|
| **History of Prior Violations** | Column 2 of Part 1 of Schedule 3 |
| **Degree of Intent or Negligence** | Column 2 of Part 2 of Schedule 3 |
| **Harm Caused or Potentially Caused** | Column 2 of Part 3 of Schedule 3 |

**Process**:
1. Assign a gravity value (from Column 1) for each criterion.
2. Sum the values to determine the **total gravity value**.
3. This total is used to adjust the base penalty (e.g., $6,000 or $10,000) upward or downward.

> Example: A person with a history of prior offenses, high intent, and significant environmental harm would receive a higher gravity value and thus a higher penalty.

---

### **7. Compliance Agreements and Penalty Reductions**

- If a person enters into a **compliance agreement** under **subsection 10(1)** of the Act:
  - The penalty is **reduced by $1 for every $2** the person spends on corrective actions.
  - **Maximum reduction is to nil** (i.e., no penalty if full compliance costs are met).

> This encourages corrective action and rehabilitation.

---

### **8. Service of Notices and Documents**

#### A. Service on Individuals
- **Personal delivery**: To the individual at any place.
- **Delivery to household member**: If the individual is not present, deliver to an adult household member; service is deemed effective on the day left.
- **Mail options**: Registered mail, courier, or electronic means (e.g., email, fax).
- **Electronic service**: A copy must also be sent by **registered mail**.

#### B. Service on Businesses or Organizations
- Sent to head office or agent via:
  - Registered mail, courier, or fax.
  - Left at head office with an officer in control or management.
  - Electronic means (not fax) to any individual named in the business.

> For fax/electronic service: A copy must be sent by registered mail.

---

### **9. Certification of Service**

A person who signs a **certificate of service** (approved by the Minister) is deemed to have served the document:
- **By registered mail or courier**: On the **10th day after** the date on the receipt.
- **By fax or electronic means**: On the **day it is sent**.

---

### **10. Payment of Penalties**

- **Payment Deadline**:  
  - For **penalties**: Within **15 days** after service of the notice (unless otherwise specified).
  - For **default payments**: Within **30 days** after service (under subsection 9(1)).

- **Payment Amounts**:
  - A person may pay **half the penalty** if they pay within **15 days** of service.

- **Accepted Payment Methods**:
  - Certified cheque
  - Money order
  - Made payable to **Receiver General for Canada**
  - Can be delivered:
    - In person
    - By regular mail
    - By registered mail
    - By courier

- **Payment Timing**:
  - **In person**: On the day of delivery.
  - **Regular mail**: On the date of the postmark.
  - **Registered mail or courier**: On the date indicated in the receipt.

---

### **11. Requests for Review or Compliance Agreements**

#### A. Review of Facts (Under Subsection 8(1) of the Act)
- A person may request a **review of the facts** of the violation.
- Must be submitted in writing **within 30 days** of service of the notice.

#### B. Review or Compliance Agreement (Under Subsection 9(2) of the Act)
- For violations with penalties of **$2,000 or more**, a person may:
  - Request a **review of facts**, or
  - Enter into a **compliance agreement**.
- Must be submitted in writing **within 30 days** of service.

> A **compliance agreement** must include a **detailed proposal** outlining corrective actions to ensure future compliance.
