---

## **Prospector’s Assistance Terms and Conditions Order, 1981**  
*(C.R.C., c. 338 – Revoked; Replaced by this Order)*  
**Effective Date:** April 1, 1981  
**Issued by:** His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development  
**Citation:** *Prospector’s Assistance Terms and Conditions Order*  
**Jurisdiction:** Yukon Territory and Northwest Territories  

---

### **1. Purpose and Background**
This regulation was enacted to **replace the earlier *Prospectors’ Assistance Regulations*** and to establish a formal, structured framework for providing **financial assistance to Canadian citizens and permanent residents** who are engaged in or intend to engage in **mineral prospecting activities** in the **Yukon Territory and Northwest Territories**.

The primary goal is to:
- Encourage exploration for mineral deposits (non-placer) in remote northern regions.
- Support Canadian citizens and residents in developing their skills and knowledge in prospecting.
- Ensure that financial assistance is used responsibly and effectively.
- Promote technical oversight and accountability through a structured review process.

The regulation was passed under **Vote 35 of Appropriation Act No. 1, 1979–80**, indicating it was part of a broader government budgetary decision to fund exploration activities in northern Canada.

---

### **2. Definitions**
Key terms used throughout the regulation:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Assisted prospector** | A prospector who receives financial assistance under this Order. |
| **Board** | A governing body established for each territory: <br> - Yukon: Consists of Supervising Mining Recorder (Chair), Regional Mining Engineer, Regional Geologist, and up to two members appointed by the Director. <br> - Northwest Territories: Same structure as Yukon. |
| **Director** | Head of the Northern Non-Renewable Resources Branch of the Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development. |
| **Department** | Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (DIAND). |
| **Field work** | Activities related to searching for mineral deposits other than placer (e.g., base metals, precious metals, industrial minerals). |
| **Fiscal year** | April 1 to March 31 of the following year. |

---

### **3. Scope and Applicability**
- Applies **only to prospectors prospecting or intending to prospect in the Yukon Territory or Northwest Territories**.
- Does **not apply** to placer mining (gold panning, stream sediment mining) or to any other type of mining activity.
- Does **not cover** prospecting in provinces or other Canadian jurisdictions.

---

### **4. Application Process**
Every applicant must submit a formal application **before June 1** of the fiscal year for which they are requesting assistance.

#### Required Submissions:
1. **Application Form (Schedule I)** – Must include:
   - Personal information (name, age, citizenship status)
   - Details of intended prospecting activity
   - Prospecting experience and training
   - Character references (name, occupation, address)
   - Business or occupation
   - Knowledge of economic potential of the area

2. **Sketch map** of the area(s) intended for prospecting, including:
   - Proposed base camp locations
   - Methods and procedures to be used
   - Brief summary of known geological or economic potential

#### Submission Path:
- Applications are submitted to the **Mining Recorder** of the relevant district.
- The Mining Recorder forwards the application **with recommendations** to the **Prospectors’ Assistance Board**.

---

### **5. Eligibility Requirements**

#### A. Age Requirement:
- Prospective applicants **under 21 years of age** are **not eligible** for assistance **unless**:
  - They are prospecting **in the company of an experienced prospector who is 21 years or older**.

#### B. Citizenship and Residency:
- The Board shall **give preference** to:
  - Canadian citizens
  - Permanent residents as defined under the *Immigration Act, 1976*
- Non-citizens or temporary residents are not eligible unless they meet the permanent resident criteria.

#### C. Physical/Mental Capacity:
- Applicants must disclose any physical or mental disabilities.
- If such conditions affect their ability to perform prospecting work, the Board may deny or limit assistance.

---

### **6. Establishment of the Prospectors’ Assistance Board**

#### Structure:
Each territory has a **Board of three core members plus up to two additional members**:

| Member | Role |
|-------|------|
| **Supervising Mining Recorder** | Chairperson of the Board |
| **Regional Mining Engineer** | Provides technical expertise |
| **Regional Geologist** | Offers geological guidance |
| **Up to two additional members** | Appointed by the Director from nominees of **active mining or prospecting associations** in the territory; must be acceptable to the Director |

> *Note: These members ensure technical oversight, fairness, and regional relevance.*

---

### **7. Board Responsibilities**

The Board has several key duties:

1. **Review and assess each application**:
   - Evaluate the applicant’s qualifications, experience, and proposed activity.
   - Determine the **amount of financial assistance** required.

2. **Conduct knowledge tests**:
   - May require applicants to take a **simple test** to verify their understanding of prospecting techniques and procedures.

3. **Make recommendations**:
   - Recommend **approval or rejection** of each application.
   - If approved, specify:
     - The **amount of financial aid** to be provided
     - Whether an **advance payment** will be made

4. **Notify applicants**:
   - Inform applicants of the outcome of their application.
   - If approved, provide details of the recommended assistance and advance (if any).

---

### **8. Financial Assistance Limits**

The maximum financial support allowed per applicant is:

| Expense Type | Maximum Allowable Amount |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| **All expenses (excluding travel)** | Up to **$1,500** |
| **Travel expenses** | Up to **$2,000** |

> *Note: Travel expenses include vehicle rentals, pack animals, boats, fuel, and transportation costs.*

#### Special Provision:
- A **per diem allowance of $25 per day** may be included in the $1,500 limit as a **personal labour cost** for field work days.

---

### **9. Disbursement of Funds**

The **Chairman of the Board**, acting on the Board’s recommendation, authorizes payments in two stages:

1. **At the beginning of the prospecting season**:
   - Grants the **full or partial amount** of the recommended assistance.

2. **At the end of the prospecting season**:
   - Pays **up to the remaining balance** of the recommended amount.
   - Or, a **reimbursement** may be approved based on the Review Committee’s assessment of actual field work expenses.

> *Note: Reimbursement is not automatic and requires verification of actual work.*

---

### **10. Technical Supervision and Oversight**

- The Board must **arrange for a Mining Engineer or Regional Geologist** to visit each assisted prospector **in the field**.
- These visits provide:
  - Supervision
  - Technical advice
  - Verification of work quality and compliance

---

### **11. Review Committee**

Composed of:
- The **Chairman of the Board**
- The **appropriate Regional Mining Engineer**
- The **appropriate Regional Geologist**

#### Responsibilities:
1. **Review all submitted documentation**, including:
   - Daily diaries
   - Sketch maps
   - Statements of expenditures
   - Supporting invoices and receipts

2. **Recommend reimbursement** at the end of the season if the Committee deems it necessary.

3. **Maintain records** of:
   - All payments made
   - All statements of expenditures
   - Supporting documents

> *This ensures accountability and transparency in the use of public funds.*

---

### **12. Obligations of the Assisted Prospector**

Each assisted prospector must comply with the following requirements:

#### A. Minimum Prospecting Duration:
- Must spend **at least 60 days** prospecting within the area described in the application during the fiscal year.

#### B. Daily Diary:
- Must keep a **daily diary** in a form prescribed by the Director.
- Diary must record:
  - Daily activities
  - Location and time of work
  - Tools and methods used

#### C. Field Records:
- Must maintain and submit:
  - Sketches and maps showing:
    - Mineral occurrences
    - Location of trenches and drill holes
    - Other field work performed

#### D. Financial Statement:
- At the end of the season, must submit:
  - Diary
  - Sketches and maps
  - A **statement of expenditures** (Schedule II)
  - Supporting invoices and receipts

---

### **13. Conditions and Consequences of Non-Compliance**

#### A. Failure to Meet 60-Day Requirement:
- If a prospector fails to meet the 60-day requirement, **any advance payment received must be repaid** to Her Majesty (the Crown).
- **Exception**: If the failure is due to **reasons beyond the prospector’s control** (e.g., weather, illness, road closures), the Review Committee may:
  - Approve the **retention** of previously paid funds
  - Authorize **further payment** as deemed appropriate
