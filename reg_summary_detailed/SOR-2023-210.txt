### **1. Purpose of the Regulation**
This regulation establishes the **rules of origin** for goods eligible to receive preferential tariff treatment under three distinct programs:
- **General Preferential Tariff (GPT)**
- **General Preferential Tariff Plus (GPT Plus)**
- **Least Developed Country Tariff (LDC Tariff)**

These rules determine whether a good qualifies as "originating" in a beneficiary country (GPT), beneficiary-plus country (GPT Plus), or a least developed country (LDC). Origin status is essential for eligibility for reduced or zero tariffs upon importation into Canada.

The regulation is made under the authority of the **Customs Tariff** and specifically subsection **16(2)a**, which allows the Governor General in Council to establish origin rules based on ministerial recommendation (by the Minister of Finance).

### **3. Rules for Determining Origin**

#### **A. Originating Goods in Beneficiary or Beneficiary-Plus Countries**
A good is considered to originate in a beneficiary country or beneficiary-plus country **if it meets one of the following criteria**:

1. **Natural Products**:
   - A mineral product extracted from the soil or seabed.
   - A vegetable product harvested in the country.
   - A live animal born and raised in the country.
   - A good obtained from a live animal in the country.
   - A good obtained from hunting or fishing in the country.
   - A good obtained from sea fishing or marine resources taken by a vessel of the country.

2. **Manufacturing on Board a Factory Ship**:
   - A good produced on a factory ship **exclusively from a fabric** originating in the country.

3. **Waste/Scrap**:
   - Waste or scrap derived from manufacturing operations within the country.

4. **Used Goods for Raw Material Recovery**:
   - A used good imported into Canada **for recovery of raw materials only**.

5. **Manufactured Exclusively from Originating Materials**:
   - A good produced in the country **exclusively from a product or good** that originated in the country (or in a beneficiary country or Canada) under any of the above.

> âœ… This rule applies to all goods **outside Chapters 61 to 63** of the List of Tariff Provisions.

#### **C. Deemed Origin (For Specific Goods)**
Certain materials and packaging are **deemed to originate** in the beneficiary country or beneficiary-plus country, even if they were sourced elsewhere:

- **Materials/Parts**: Any materials or parts used in manufacturing that originated in:
  - Another beneficiary country
  - Canada
- **Packing**: Any packaging required for transportation, **excluding** packaging used for sale to consumers in the beneficiary country.

#### **E. Origin in Least Developed Countries (LDCs)**

A good originates in an LDC **if the value of non-originating materials used in production is no more than 80% of the ex-factory price** of the final good (as packed for shipment).

> âœ… This is a **more lenient threshold** (80% vs. 40%) to support developing nations.

### **4. Retention of Origin Status During Transport**

When a good that qualifies as originating is transported **outside** a beneficiary country, beneficiary-plus country, or LDC, it **retains its originating status only if**:

1. The good remains **under customs control** during transit.
2. It does **not undergo any further production or processing** outside the country of origin.
3. The only allowed operations are:
   - Unloading
   - Reloading
   - Separation from bulk shipments
   - Storing
   - Preserving condition

> âŒ Any processing, manufacturing, or reassembly outside the country **destroys origin status**.

---

### **5. Grouping and Assembly Rules**

For determining origin, **each good in a shipment is considered separately**, but exceptions apply:

1. **Group, Set, or Assembly**:
   - If goods are classified under the **same tariff item**, they are treated as **one good**.

2. **Tools, Parts, and Accessories**:
   - Are considered part of the **main good** if:
     - Imported with the good
     - Customarily included in the sale of that good
     - Their price is included in the goodâ€™s price
     - No separate charge is made

3. **Unassembled Goods Imported in Multiple Shipments**:
   - If components are imported in separate shipments due to transport or production constraints, they are treated as **one good**.

> This prevents "fragmented origin" manipulation and ensures that the final product is treated as a single unit.
