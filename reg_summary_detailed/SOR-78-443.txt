## üìå OVERVIEW

This regulation was a **set of rules governing fishing activities in inland and coastal waters of Newfoundland and Labrador**, primarily focused on **angling (fishing with a hook and line)**, with specific provisions for **trout, salmon, and other native species**. It was established under the authority of the **Fisheries Act**, specifically **Section 34**, which allows the Minister to make or amend fishery regulations.

Despite being **repealed in 2003**, it provides valuable historical insight into how fishery management was structured in the province during the mid-20th century ‚Äî particularly regarding **species protection, gear restrictions, seasonal closures, and access for Indigenous communities**.

## üéØ CORE PROVISIONS

### 1. **Fishing Methods in Inland Waters**

#### ‚úÖ Allowed:
- **Angling only** is permitted in inland waters (excluding specific exceptions).
- **No netting, sweeping, raking, or foul hooking** (hooking fish outside the mouth) is allowed.
- **Exception**: Use of **gaffs, tailers, or landing nets** is allowed for landing fish.

#### ‚ùå Prohibited:
- Fishing with **nets, traps, or spears** in inland waters (except for specific Indigenous rights).
- **Foul hooking** (hooking fish in body parts other than the mouth) ‚Äî banned in inland waters.

> üîç *This reflects a strong emphasis on protecting fish populations from non-selective or injurious methods.*

---

### 2. **Indigenous Fishing Rights (Inuit and Indians in Labrador)**

- **Allowed**: Inuit and Indigenous peoples may catch fish **for food only** in inland waters.
- **Prohibited**: No commercial sale or barter of fish.
- **Gear Restrictions**:
  - Nets must not exceed **46 meters in total length** at one time.
  - Every net must have a **visible name tag** (wood or metal) attached so the operator can be identified.

> üìå This provision recognizes **traditional fishing practices** and supports **cultural rights**, while protecting against overharvesting.

---

### 3. **Net and Trawl Use in Inland Waters**

- **No one may possess a net or trawl near inland waters without a valid licence**.
- This prevents unauthorized or unregulated use of large fishing gear in sensitive ecosystems.

### 5. **Angling Gear Restrictions**

- **One line or rod and line only** at a time (no multiple lines).
- **Lines must be closely and constantly attended** by the angler.
- **In rivers listed in Schedule I**:
  - Only **artificial flies with barbless hooks** allowed.
  - **No more than one hook** per line.
- **During ice fishing**:
  - No more than **three lines/rods**.
  - Same attendance and gear rules apply.

> üéØ These rules aim to **prevent overfishing**, reduce accidental hooking, and ensure fair access to fishing opportunities.

---

### 6. **Age and Time Restrictions**

- **No person under 14 years old** may fish in rivers listed in **Schedule I** unless **supervised by someone aged 17 or older**.
- **No fishing between sunset and sunrise** in rivers (one hour after sunset to one hour before sunrise).

> üìå This protects young anglers and ensures safe, responsible fishing practices.

---

### 7. **Prohibitions on Retention and Harm to Fish**

- **No live sport fish** may be retained in inland waters (must be released).
- **Spent fish** (post-spawning) and **immature fish** (under size limits) **must be released immediately**.
- **No wilful disturbance** of fish or spawning grounds.

> üåø These rules reflect a **conservation-based approach** to protect spawning stocks and juvenile fish.

---

### 8. **Waste Disposal Restrictions**

- **No deposit or disposal of waste material** (gear, garbage, offal, debris) in inland or tidal waters or on ice, unless:
  - Disposed in **gurry grounds** (designated areas) under fishery officer authority.
  - Permitted under the **Ocean Dumping Control Act**.

> üö´ This prevents pollution and habitat degradation.
