**Detailed Summary of the 14th IAAF World Half Marathon Championships Remission Order**  
*Statutory Citation: S.C. 1997, c. 36*  

---

### **Overview**  
This regulation, issued by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance under **section 115 of the Customs Tariff**, provides for the **remission (exemption) of certain taxes and duties** on goods imported temporarily into Canada in connection with the **14th IAAF World Half Marathon Championships**, held in **Edmonton, Alberta, on October 1, 2005**.  

The purpose of this Order is to facilitate international participation in the event by reducing the financial burden on athletes, officials, sponsors, and organizers â€” particularly those who are **not ordinarily resident in Canada** â€” while ensuring compliance with Canadian customs and tax laws.

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Championships**  
   Refers specifically to the **14th IAAF World Half Marathon Championships**, scheduled to take place in **Edmonton, Alberta, on October 1, 2005**.

2. **Championships Family Member**  
   Includes any individual (not ordinarily resident in Canada) who:
   - Is an athlete, coach, trainer, official, or judge participating in or supporting the Championships; or  
   - Holds an **IAAF accreditation** issued by the Local Organizing Committee (LOC) and is either:
     - A member of the IAAF, or  
     - A member of a sports federation that is a member of the IAAF.

3. **Foreign Corporation**  
   A corporation whose:
   - Head office is located outside Canada;  
   - Does **not** have a branch or subsidiary in Canada;  
   - Has been designated as:
     - An official sponsor or supplier of the Championships, or  
     - A broadcaster of the Championships, by the LOC.

4. **IAAF**  
   The **International Association of Athletics Federations** â€” the global governing body for track and field athletics.

5. **LOC**  
   The **Edmonton World Half Marathon Championships 2005 Ltd. Local Organizing Committee** (also referred to as the "Local Organizing Committee").

### **Tax Remission Provisions**

#### **Section 1: Remission of Excise Taxes and GST for Championships Family Members**
- **Applies to**: Goods imported temporarily into Canada by a **Championships family member**.
- **Condition**: The goods must be used **exclusively** for the memberâ€™s participation or involvement in the Championships.
- **Remission**: Excise taxes and **Goods and Services Tax (GST)** are waived on such goods.
- **Limitation**: Subject to **Sections 7 and 8** (which address conditions and enforcement).

#### **Section 2: Partial Remission of GST for Foreign Corporations and LOC**
- **Applies to**:
  - Goods for **display** (including apparatus and equipment used to display them), imported temporarily by a **foreign corporation**, its agent, or representative.
  - Equipment imported temporarily by the **LOC** or a foreign corporation, its agent or representative.
- **Condition**: The goods must be used **exclusively** in connection with the Championships.
- **Remission Amount**:  
  The remitted GST equals the **difference between**:
  - The full GST paid or payable on the value of the goods, and  
  - The GST that would have been payable on **1/60th of the value of the goods per month** (or partial month) that the goods remain in Canada.

> âœ… Example: If goods remain in Canada for 3 months, the remitted GST is equal to the difference between the full GST and the GST on (3/60) of the total value.

### **Conditions for Remission**

All remissions are subject to the following **mandatory conditions**:

1. **Timeframe of Import**  
   Goods must be imported into Canada during the period:  
   **March 1, 2005 â€“ October 1, 2005**.

2. **Timely Claim Submission**  
   A claim for remission must be submitted to the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** within **two years** of the day the goods were accounted for under **section 32 of the Customs Act**.

3. **Evidence Requirement**  
   The importer must provide the **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)** with **evidence or information** proving entitlement to remission under this Order.

4. **Final Disposition of Goods**  
   Remission is granted **only if** the goods are:
   - **Exported from Canada** on or before **December 31, 2005**, **or**
   - **Destroyed in Canada** under the supervision of a customs officer, **at the importerâ€™s expense**.

> ðŸš¨ Failure to meet these conditions (e.g., no export or destruction, late claim, lack of documentation) results in **no remission**.
