### **Detailed Summary of the Saskatchewan Fishery Regulations, 1995 (Repealed by SOR/2017-58, s. 32)**

---

#### **1. Regulatory Background and Legal Authority**

These regulations were introduced by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Fisheries and Oceans**, pursuant to **Section 43 of the Fisheries Act**. This provision allows the federal government to repeal or replace provincial fishery regulations when necessary to ensure consistency with federal fisheries policy and law.

- The **Saskatchewan Fishery Regulations of 1979 (P.C. 1979-1681)** were officially repealed and replaced by the **1995 Saskatchewan Fishery Regulations**.
- The 1995 Regulations came into force on **January 1, 1991**, under **S.C. 1991, c. 1, s. 12**, and were originally issued under the authority of the **Fisheries Act (Saskatchewan), 1994**.
- These regulations were **repealed in 2017** by **SOR/2017-58, section 32**, indicating that they are no longer in force and have been superseded by newer, more current regulations.

> ‚úÖ **Key Takeaway**: This document is a historical regulatory text and is **no longer applicable** as of the 2017 repeal. It provides insight into how fishing was regulated in Saskatchewan prior to modern updates.

---

#### **2. Definitions**

The regulations define key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Director** | Refers to the Director of Fisheries Administration, appointed by the provincial Minister. Responsible for implementing and enforcing fishery rules. |
| **Indian** | Defined under **paragraph 12 of the Saskatchewan Natural Resources Transfer Agreement (1930)**, which recognizes Indigenous rights in relation to natural resources. This includes status-based Indigenous individuals with rights to fish for food. |
| **Province** | Refers specifically to the **Province of Saskatchewan**. |
| **Provincial Minister** | The provincial Minister responsible for administering the **Fisheries Act (Saskatchewan), 1994**. This is the authority with power to issue licenses and manage fishery operations. |

---

#### **3. Scope of Application**

- These regulations apply to **all waters within Saskatchewan** **except** those covered by the **National Parks Fishing Regulations** (which govern specific protected areas like national parks and reserves).

> üîç **Note**: This means that fishing in national parks or designated protected areas is governed by different rules, not these general provincial regulations.

---

#### **4. Licensing Requirements**

##### **General Rule**
- **No person may fish without a valid licence**, unless they fall under an exception (e.g., Indigenous fishers).

##### **Licence for Indigenous Persons (Section 4(2))**
- The **provincial Minister may issue a licence** to an **Indian** to fish **solely for personal or family food use**.
- This licence is **free of charge**.
- The licence is **not required** for Indigenous persons to fish using traditional methods such as:
  - Spear fishing
  - Bow and arrow
  - Angling
- These methods are allowed **without a licence**, **only for food use**.

> ‚úÖ **Purpose**: Recognizes and respects Indigenous rights to traditional fishing practices under the 1930 Agreement and the Constitution Act, 1930.

---

#### **5. Prohibition on Trade of Fish (Section 4(3))**

- **No person may buy, sell, trade, or barter** any fish caught under:
  - A licence issued under Section 4(2) (Indigenous food use)
  - Or under Section 4(3) (unlicensed personal/family fishing)

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Implication**: Ensures that fish caught for personal consumption remain within the household or community and are not commercialized, protecting traditional food practices and preventing market-based exploitation.

---

#### **6. Licence Conditions (Section 5)**

- The **provincial Minister may impose conditions** on any fishing licence to:
  - Promote **fisheries management**
  - Protect fish populations
  - Ensure **conservation and sustainability**
- Conditions may cover any matter listed in **paragraphs 22(1)(a) to (z.1)** of the *Fishery (General) Regulations* (a federal regulation).
- **All licence holders must comply** with any conditions set out in their licence.

> üîç Example: Conditions could include limits on catch size, gear restrictions, seasonal closures, or specific areas where fishing is allowed.

##### **Variation of Close Times**
- The **provincial Minister or Director** may issue an **order to vary** any close time.
- Variations must be **notified** to affected individuals or communities using **one or more of the following methods**:

  2. Published in a local newspaper
  3. Posted in the affected area
  4. Sent electronically
  5. Given orally by a fishery officer

#### **8. Release of Live Fish (Section 8)**

##### **General Rule**
- **No person may release live fish** into waters within Saskatchewan **unless authorized by a licence**.

##### **Exception**
- A fish may be **returned to the water immediately after being caught** ‚Äî no licence required.

##### **Conditions for Licence to Release Fish (Section 8(3))**
The provincial Minister may issue a licence to release live fish **only if**:
1. The release is **in keeping with fisheries management and conservation goals**
2. The fish are **free of disease or disease agents** that could harm fish populations
3. The release will **not negatively impact**:
   - Stock size
   - Genetic diversity
   - Natural fish populations

> üö´ **No fee** is charged for such licences.

> üîç **Purpose**: Prevents the introduction of disease or genetic contamination; supports responsible aquaculture or rehabilitation efforts.

### **Summary of Key Provisions**

| Provision | Key Points |
|--------|------------|
| **Authority** | Based on Section 43 of the Fisheries Act; replaced 1979 regulations |
| **Applicability** | Applies to all Saskatchewan waters except National Parks |
| **Fishing Licences** | Required for most people; free for Indigenous food use |
| **Indigenous Fishing Rights** | Unlicensed spear, bow, or angling allowed for food use |
| **No Trade of Fish** | Prohibited for fish caught under personal/family use |
| **Licence Conditions** | Can include gear, location, quantity, seasonality |
| **Close Times** | Prohibited fishing in specific lakes, rivers, and reservoirs during set times |
| **Variation Orders** | Can be issued and publicly notified |
| **Live Fish Release** | Only permitted with a licence and under strict conditions |

---

### **Legal and Policy Implications**

- **Indigenous Rights Recognition**: The regulation reflects a formal recognition of Indigenous fishing rights under the 1930 Natural Resources Transfer Agreement and the Constitution Act, 1930.
- **Conservation Focus**: Emphasis on sustainable practices through close times, licence conditions, and restrictions on fish trade.
- **Flexibility and Enforcement**: Allows for adaptive management via variation orders and public notification.
- **Public Safety and Compliance**: Requires clear communication of changes to ensure compliance and avoid overfishing.
