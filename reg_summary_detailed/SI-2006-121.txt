## ðŸ” OVERVIEW

This regulation is the **official proclamation** that brings into force the **Agreement on Social Security between Canada and the Republic of Estonia**, signed on **February 21, 2005**, in Ottawa. It establishes a framework for cooperation between Canada and Estonia in the field of social security, particularly concerning the **recognition, eligibility, and payment of pensions and benefits** for individuals who reside in or work across both countries.

The agreement ensures **mutual recognition of social security contributions and residence periods**, prevents double taxation or double liability, and promotes **fairness and equity** in pension rights for citizens and residents of both nations.

### 1. **Entry into Force of the Agreement**

- The agreement was scheduled to enter into force on **November 1, 2006**, which is the **first day of the fourth month after the month in which the Parties exchanged written notices** confirming that their domestic legal requirements had been met.
- The exchange of these notices occurred on **July 14, 2006**.
- The Canadian government laid the Order in Council (P.C. 2005-1897) before Parliament on **November 4, 2005**, but due to a parliamentary dissolution, it was re-laid on **May 2, 2006**.
- Under the **Old Age Security Act (section 42(2))**, the Order came into force on the **thirtieth sitting day after being laid before Parliament**, which was **June 20, 2006**.
- Despite this, the actual entry into force of the agreement was delayed until **November 1, 2006**, as required by the **Article XXVIII of the original Agreement**.
