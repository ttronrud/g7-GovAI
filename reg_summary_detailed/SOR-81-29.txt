### **Background and Context**

This regulation was originally enacted under the **Municipal Grants Act, 1980**, and was promulgated by the Governor in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Public Works. It establishes the **rules and definitions governing grants in lieu of real property taxes or frontage/area taxes** that may be paid by the Government of Canada to municipal or local taxing authorities.

The regulation was initially published in *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **December 5, 1980**, providing a reasonable opportunity for interested parties (such as municipalities, property owners, or other stakeholders) to submit comments or objections before the regulation came into force.

However, **this regulation has since been repealed**:
- **Section 2** of SOR/2001-494 repealed the regulation entirely.
- **Section 6** of SOR/2001-494 repealed a specific provision relating to certain federal properties being included in the definition of â€œfederal propertyâ€ for the purposes of the Act.

As such, **this regulation is no longer in force** and does not apply to current tax or grant arrangements in Canada.

---

### **Key Elements of the Regulation (Prior to Repeal)**

#### **1. Purpose of the Regulation**
To define when and how the federal government may provide **grants in lieu of real property taxes or frontage/area taxes** to local taxing authorities. These grants are intended to compensate municipalities for the cost of property taxes that would otherwise be collected from federal property that is leased or occupied by non-departmental entities.

This ensures that local governments are not financially burdened by property taxes on federal lands or buildings used by government agencies, international bodies, or diplomatic missions.

---

#### **2. Definitions Used in the Regulation**

- **Act**: Refers to the *Payments in Lieu of Taxes Act* (PILTA), which provides the legal framework for such grants.
- **International Organization**: As defined in Section 3 of the *Privileges and Immunities (International Organizations) Act* â€” includes entities like the UN, World Bank, etc., operating in Canada.
- **Visiting Force**: As defined in the *Visiting Forces Act* â€” refers to foreign military forces temporarily stationed in Canada.

---

#### **3. Criteria for Determining Taxable Attributes (Paragraph b of Subsection 2(1) of the Act)**

For the purpose of determining whether a tax levy should be applied to federal property (e.g., for financing operating costs), the following criteria may be used:

- The **actual or estimated cost of constructing a new building**
- The **number of rooms, living units, or beds** in a building
- The **number of persons occupying** the building
- Any **lot, parcel, improvement, or property component** that an assessment authority would normally use to calculate a frontage or area tax on taxable private property

These criteria are used to assess the **economic or functional value** of federal property in relation to local tax assessments.

---

#### **4. Definition of "Federal Property" (Under Subsection 2(1) of the Act)**

The regulation defines which real properties owned by Her Majesty in right of Canada qualify as **federal property** eligible for grants in lieu of property taxes. This includes:

| Category | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Domestic use by employees or Canadian Forces** | Property leased/occupied by employees or members of the Canadian Forces for domestic purposes. |
| **Property managed by a Crown minister** | Owned by the Crown and managed by a corporation listed in **Schedule III or IV** of the Act, which is exempt from real property tax. |
| **Pickering Airport site** | Real property acquired by Her Majesty as a site for the proposed Pickering Airport. |
| **Pre-existing leases/occupations** | Property leased or occupied prior to acquisition, where the lease or occupation continues post-acquisition. |
| **Specially assessed improvements** | Property specially assessed for a local improvement (e.g., road, sewer) â€” even if lease ends, such assessments may continue. |
| **Leases of one year or less** | Any property with a lease term of one year or less. |
| **Indian or Inuit occupancy** | Property used primarily for residential or farming purposes by Indigenous persons (Indian or Inuit), not on an Indian Reserve. |
| **Visiting forces** | Property leased/occupied by members of a visiting force for **residential or military purposes**. |
| **Diplomatic missions** | Property used as **chancery/offices** or **official residence** of a foreign government's diplomatic mission. |
| **Consular posts/trade commissions** | Property used as **chancery/offices** or **official residence** of a consular or trade commission. |
| **International organizations** | Property used as **chancery/offices** or **official residence of the head of a mission**. |
| **National Arts Centre (Ottawa)** | Real property in Ottawa leased to or occupied by the National Arts Centre Corporation. |
| **Agreements under Section 12 of the Act** | Property subject to an agreement under Section 12 (likely related to temporary or special use). |
| **Airport authorities** | Property owned by the Crown and leased to a designated airport authority, **unless sublet to a third party** â€” only the airport authority or a receiver-manager may occupy or sublet it. |

> **Exception**: Properties under management of a corporation in Schedule III or IV, even if occupied by a **department**, are included in federal property for PILTA purposes.

### **Key Legal Basis**

- The regulation operates under **subsection 8(1) of the Municipal Grants Act, 1980**, which authorizes the Minister of Public Works to make regulations respecting grants in lieu of real property taxes or frontage/area taxes.

### **Significance and Historical Importance**

Although now repealed, this regulation was historically significant because:

1. It established a **clear, detailed framework** for identifying federal property eligible for tax relief.
2. It recognized the **unique status** of certain federal properties (e.g., those used by Indigenous peoples, diplomatic missions, or international organizations).
3. It provided **practical criteria** for assessing taxability based on physical attributes (rooms, occupancy, construction costs), enabling fair and transparent tax equity.
4. It reflected a **principle of fiscal fairness**, ensuring that municipalities are not unfairly burdened by taxes on federal property used for public or international functions.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation, while **no longer in effect**, represents a foundational effort by the Canadian government to balance municipal taxation with federal property use. Its provisions laid the groundwork for modern tax-exempt or grant-based arrangements involving federal real estate. However, due to changes in policy and legislation (particularly SOR/2001-494), the regulation has been **fully repealed** and is not applicable today.

For current guidance on grants in lieu of property taxes, reference should be made to the **Payments in Lieu of Taxes Act** and its current amendments, which may have updated or replaced the definitions and eligibility criteria outlined in this 1980 regulation.
