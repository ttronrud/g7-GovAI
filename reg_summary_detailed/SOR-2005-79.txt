## **III. Filing and Service of Documents**

### **1. Mandatory Filing of Initiating Documents**
- All initiating documents must be **filed directly with the Board**.
- Upon receipt, the Board must provide **copies to**:
  - The other party involved;
  - Any person who may be affected by the proceeding.

### **2. Time Limits for Replies**
- The **other party** must reply to an initiating document (excluding arbitration, conciliation, or notice of adjudication reference) **within 15 days** of receiving a copy.
- Exceptions may apply based on specific provisions or urgency.

### **3. Consequences of Non-Response**
- Despite sections 16 and 17 of the Act, if a party fails to respond within the 15-day limit (in non-adjudication proceedings), the **Board or Chairperson may dispose of the matter without further notice**.

### **4. Subsequent Documents**
- Any document submitted after the initiating document must be filed with the Board and must be shared with:
  - The original filing party;
  - All parties who received a copy under section 4;
  - Any intervenors;
  - The **Canadian Human Rights Commission (CHRC)** if notice was previously given under subsections 210(1), 217(1), or 222(1) of the Act.

> âš ï¸ *Exemptions:* This rule does **not apply** to:
> - Applications for summons;
> - Documentary evidence under sections 30 and 42;
> - Additional documentation under sections 32 and 44;
> - Statements of opposition or applications to amend them.

### **5. Receipt of Documents**
- A document is deemed received on:
  - **Courier delivery**: Day of sending;
  - **Hand delivery**: Day of receipt;
  - **Electronic transmission** (email, fax): Day of sending;
  - **Mail**: Date of **postmark** or **postage meter impression**, or the **later** of the two if both appear.

> âœ… **Holiday/Weekend Rule**: If a deadline falls on a Saturday or holiday, the document may be filed on the **next business day**.

## **IV. Withdrawal of Proceedings**

- A party may **withdraw a proceeding**:
  - In person at a hearing; or
  - By submitting a **written notice of withdrawal** to the Board at any time before a decision is made.

- Upon receiving notice of withdrawal or being informed by the Chairperson or adjudicator:
  - The Board must **close the file**;
  - Notify all parties, intervenors, and (if applicable) the CHRC.

> âš ï¸ **Status Review Provision**: If a party fails to respond to a notice from the Board requesting reasons why the matter should not be deemed withdrawn, and no response is received within the time frame set by the Board, the matter will be **deemed withdrawn**.

---

## **V. Flexibility in Timelines**

Despite strict deadlines, the Board may:
- **Extend time limits** (for fairness or complexity);
- **Reduce time limits** (if the matter is urgent), **on notice to all parties**.

> This ensures procedural fairness while allowing for practical realities in complex cases.

---

## **VI. Consolidation of Proceedings**

- The Board may **consolidate two or more related proceedings** to promote efficiency and reduce duplication.

---

## **VII. Adding Parties or Intervenors**

- Any person with a **substantial interest** in a proceeding may apply to be:
  - Added as a **party**; or
  - Made an **intervenor**.

- The Board may grant such requests **after providing opportunity for representations** from all parties.

> The Board may also, at its own initiative or upon request, require that information in a document be:
- Made more **complete or specific**;
- If insufficient, the Board may **strike** incomplete or vague information from the document after allowing the party to respond.
