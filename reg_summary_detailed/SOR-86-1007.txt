**Detailed Summary of the One-Day Local Employment Seminars Fee or Charge Order**

**Regulation Title:**  
One-Day Local Employment Seminars Fee or Charge Order  
**Citation:** SI/86-179, 1986 Canada Gazette Part II, p. 4245  
**Date of Issuance:** September 23, 1986  
**Enabling Authority:** Order in Council P.C. 1986-2103, passed on September 11, 1986  
**Issuing Authority:** The Minister of Employment and Immigration  

---

**Purpose of the Regulation:**  
This regulation establishes a standardized fee to be collected from participants attending one-day local employer seminars hosted by Canada Employment Centres across Canada. The primary objective is to ensure financial sustainability for the delivery of these employer-focused training and information sessions, which aim to improve employment outcomes by promoting employer engagement in workforce development.

---

**Key Provisions:**

1. **Applicable Event:**  
   The fee applies specifically to participants attending **one-day local employer seminars** conducted by **Canada Employment Centres**. These centres are part of the federal governmentâ€™s employment services network and are responsible for facilitating job matching, skills development, and employer engagement initiatives.

2. **Fee Amount:**  
   The fee to be paid by each participant is **$25 Canadian dollars**. This amount is fixed and uniform across all regions of Canada, ensuring consistency in cost and administrative simplicity for both the centres and participants.

3. **Scope of Application:**  
   The regulation applies to all such seminars hosted by Canada Employment Centres throughout Canada. This includes both in-person and, by extension, any format that qualifies as a one-day local employer seminar.

4. **Collection Responsibility:**  
   The responsibility for collecting the $25 fee lies with the Canada Employment Centres that host the seminars. These centres are authorized to enforce the fee as part of their operational procedures and may require participants to pay the fee at the time of registration or attendance.

5. **Legal Basis:**  
   The Minister issued this Order pursuant to **Order in Council P.C. 1986-2103**, which granted the authority to establish fees or charges for specific employment-related services. This legislative instrument provides the legal foundation for the imposition of the $25 fee.

6. **Citation and Publication:**  
   The Order is officially published in the *Canada Gazette, Part II*, on September 23, 1986, and may be cited as the **One-Day Local Employment Seminars Fee or Charge Order**.

---

**Rationale and Context:**  
The one-day employer seminars are designed to support local businesses in understanding labour market trends, workforce skills gaps, and strategies for hiring and retaining talent. By charging a modest fee, the federal government ensures that these services remain financially viable while maintaining accessibility. The $25 fee is considered reasonable and proportionate to the administrative and logistical costs of organizing and delivering the seminars.

---

**Impact and Significance:**  
- Encourages employer participation by making seminars accessible and professionally supported.  
- Provides a stable revenue stream for Canada Employment Centres to fund ongoing employment services.  
- Promotes transparency and consistency in fee structure across Canada.  
- Aligns with broader federal employment policy goals of improving employment outcomes through employer engagement.

---

**Note on Validity and Review:**  
This Order is valid as a statutory instrument under the authority granted by the Order in Council. It remains in effect until amended, repealed, or superseded by a subsequent regulation. No further legislative action is required to enforce or implement the fee, as it is directly authorized by federal executive order.

---

**Conclusion:**  
The One-Day Local Employment Seminars Fee or Charge Order establishes a clear, standardized fee of **$25 per participant** for attending one-day employer seminars hosted by Canada Employment Centres. This regulation supports the effective delivery of employment services, fosters employer engagement, and ensures the long-term sustainability of local employment initiatives across Canada.
