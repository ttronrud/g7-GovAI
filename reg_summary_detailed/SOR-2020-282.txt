**Detailed Summary of the Canadian Pork Promotion-Research Agency Proclamation (P.C. 2020-844)**

---

### **Overview**
This regulation, formally titled the *Canadian Pork Promotion-Research Agency Proclamation*, is a legal instrument issued by the Governor in Council of Canada on **October 30, 2020**, and came into force on **November 25, 2020**. It establishes the **Canadian Pork Promotion-Research Agency (CPRA)** under the authority of **section 39(1)a of the Farm Products Agencies Act (FPA Act)**.

The purpose of the Proclamation is to create a dedicated agency to promote and conduct research on **hogs and pork products** in Canada, with a focus on improving market access, enhancing competitiveness, and supporting the sustainability of the pork sector both domestically and internationally.

---

## **Key Provisions and Components**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Basis**
- The creation of the CPRA is authorized under **subsection 39(1)a of the Farm Products Agencies Act**.
- This provision allows the Governor in Council to establish a promotion and research agency **if the majority of producers (and importers, where applicable)** support its formation.
- The Governor in Council has determined that **a majority of Canadian producers and importers of hogs and pork products support the establishment of the Agency**.

> **Legal Citation**:  
> S.C. 1993, c. 3, s. 12; R.S., c. F-4; S.C. 1993, c. 3, s. 2

---

### **2. Name and Purpose of the Agency**
- The Agency is officially named the **Canadian Pork Promotion-Research Agency (CPRA)**.
- It is established to:
  - **Promote** the marketing and production of hogs and pork products.
  - **Conduct and promote research** related to these farm products.
- Its mandate aligns with the broader objectives of the Farm Products Agencies Act, particularly **section 41**, which emphasizes advancing the economic viability and competitiveness of Canadian agricultural sectors.

---

### **3. Scope of Activities**
The CPRA is empowered to operate within the following areas:

#### **A. Promotion**
- Promote the **marketing and production** of hogs and pork products for:
  - Interprovincial trade
  - Export markets
  - Import trade (to support domestic supply chains)

#### **B. Research**
- Conduct and promote **research activities** relevant to:
  - Hog health and welfare
  - Production efficiency
  - Processing technologies
  - Consumer preferences
  - Sustainability and environmental impact

> These activities aim to enhance the value chain, improve product quality, and increase market visibility and demand for Canadian pork.

---

### **4. Definitions (Key Terms)**
The Proclamation includes clear definitions essential to understanding the scope and operation of the Agency:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | The Farm Products Agencies Act |
| **Agency** | The Canadian Pork Promotion-Research Agency established by this Proclamation |
| **Hog** | A live, domesticated pig marketed for the production of pork products |
| **Marketing** | Includes selling, buying, pricing, packing, processing, transporting, storing, and preparing a product for consumption or use |
| **Pork Product** | An edible product made in whole or in part from a hog |
| **Provincial Pork Association** | Specific organizations representing producers in each province: e.g., Ontario Pork Producers’ Marketing Board, Les éleveurs de porcs du Québec, Pork Nova Scotia, etc. |

---

### **5. Composition of the Agency: Membership Structure**

The CPRA consists of **12 members**, elected through a structured process:

#### **(a) Provincial Representation (9 members)**
- One member elected from each of the following provinces:
  - Ontario
  - Quebec
  - Nova Scotia
  - New Brunswick
  - Manitoba
  - British Columbia
  - Prince Edward Island
  - Saskatchewan
  - Alberta  
- Each member is elected by delegates at the **Annual General Meeting (AGM)** of the Agency.
- Candidates must be nominated by the respective **provincial pork association** representing primary producers.

#### **(b) Importer Representation (1 member)**
- One member elected to represent **importers** of hogs or pork products into Canada.
- Eligibility: Must have imported hogs or pork products in the **previous calendar year**.
- Candidates submit applications to the Agency.

#### **(c) Value Chain Representation (2 members)**
- Two members elected from individuals directly involved in the Agency’s operations.
- If no applications are received, the Agency (or its members) may invite qualified individuals from other segments of the pork value chain, including:
  - Retail
  - Restaurant and food service
  - Processing
  - Research and development

> These members ensure diverse stakeholder input and cross-sectoral engagement.

---

### **6. Term of Office and Succession**

- **Term**: One year, beginning at the close of each Annual General Meeting.
- **Resignation, removal, or death**:
  - For members elected under **(a)** (provincial producers):  
    → The **provincial pork association** appoints a **temporary substitute** until the next AGM.  
    → If the substitute cannot act, the association appoints a replacement.
  - For members elected under **(b)** or **(c)** (importers or value chain):  
    → The **Agency itself** appoints a temporary substitute.  
    → If unable to act, the Agency appoints a replacement.

> This ensures continuity and operational stability.

---

### **7. Leadership Structure**

- At the **first meeting** and **after each annual general meeting**, members elect:
  - A **Chair**
  - A **Vice-Chair**
- In case of resignation, removal, or death:
  - The remaining members elect a new chair or vice-chair at their **next meeting**.
- The chair and vice-chair serve for the **remainder of the term**.

---

### **8. Head Office Location**

- The **head office** of the CPRA is located in **Ottawa, Ontario**.
- This central location facilitates coordination with federal agencies, policy development, and access to national markets.

---

### **9. Financial and Operational Requirements**

#### **Annual Budget Submission**
- The Agency must submit an annual budget to the **Governor in Council (Council)** for approval, which includes:
  - Estimated costs for the next 12 months
  - A detailed **business program** outlining proposed activities
  - All relevant information to assess:
    - Whether the activities align with **section 7** of the FPA Act (objectives of promotion and research)
    - Whether any **existing or proposed orders** (e.g., levies) are necessary for implementation

> This ensures transparency, accountability, and alignment with public policy goals.

---

### **10. Levies and Charges (Funding Mechanism)**

To fund its operations, the CPRA may impose **levies or charges** on specific groups:

#### **Applicable Groups**:
- Persons engaged in:
  - Marketing of hogs in **interprovincial or export trade**
  - Importation of hogs or pork products into Canada

#### **Order Process**:
- The Agency may issue a formal **order** specifying:
  - Class of persons affected
  - Amount of levy or charge
  - Method of collection

#### **Funding Rules**:
- Levies must be set at levels that generate **sufficient revenue** to cover the Agency’s estimated administrative and operational costs for the year.
- All funds collected from **import levies** must be held in a **separate account** to ensure financial transparency and accountability.

#### **Default and Enforcement**:
- Levies unpaid **30 days after due date** become a **debt payable to the Agency**.
- The Agency may, with **concurrence of a provincial pork association**, appoint that association or another entity to **collect levies on its behalf**.

---

### **11. Cooperation and Collaboration**

- The Agency is required to:
  - Promote **high levels of cooperation** among:
    - Its members
    - Provincial pork associations
    - Importers of hogs and pork products
- This fosters a unified, collaborative approach to market development and policy implementation.

---

### **12. Review and Evaluation of the Plan**

- The Agency must conduct a **review of its plan every five years**:
  - First review within **five years** of the Proclamation coming into force
  - Subsequent reviews every five years thereafter
- Purpose of the review:
  - Evaluate the **effectiveness and relevance** of the promotion-research plan
  - Determine if **modifications** are needed to achieve the Agency’s objectives (as defined in section 41 of the FPA Act)

#### **Reporting Requirement**:
- Within **three months** of each review meeting, the Agency must file a **written report** with the Council:
  - Outlining findings
  - Recommending any necessary changes

> This ensures ongoing adaptability and responsiveness to market conditions and stakeholder needs.

---

### **13. Non-Preemption of Existing Powers**

- Nothing in this Proclamation **affects the powers already vested in the Agency** under **section 42 of the Farm Products Agencies Act**.
- The agency retains all powers previously granted under the Act, including authority to enter into agreements, manage funds, and carry out specific functions.
