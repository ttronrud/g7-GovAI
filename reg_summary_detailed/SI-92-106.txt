## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Rules Governing Appeals in the Court of Appeal of Manitoba**

These rules were adopted on **May 1, 1992**, by the Judges of the Court of Appeal of Manitoba, and are published in the *Canada Gazette* as required under **section 482(4) of the Criminal Code (Canada)**. They implement procedural standards for criminal appeals in Manitoba, particularly those involving **appeals from conviction, acquittal, or sentence**, and are governed by the broader interpretation and definitions found in the Criminal Code.

---

### üîç **Key Definitions and Scope**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Court** | Refers specifically to *The Court of Appeal of Manitoba* |
| **Tribunal** | Used interchangeably with "court" in the context of appeal proceedings |
| **Initiating Document** | The first document filed to commence an appeal process; includes either a *Notice of Appeal* or a *Notice of Application for Leave to Appeal* |
| **Judge** | A judge of the Court of Appeal |
| **Registrar** | The registrar or deputy registrar of the Court of Appeal |
| **Summary Conviction Appeal Court** | The Court of Queen‚Äôs Bench sitting on appeal in summary conviction matters |
| **Trial Judge** | Includes judges of the Court of Queen‚Äôs Bench or Provincial Court |

> ‚öñÔ∏è *These rules apply to all criminal appeals and are subject to the general interpretation and definitions in the Criminal Code.*

## üìù **1. Requirements for the Initiating Document**

### A. Who Must File It?
- Must be signed by:
  - The **appellant** (accused or Crown), or
  - Their **counsel or agent**

### B. Who It Is Directed To:
- Must be **directed to the registrar**

---

### C. Content of the Initiating Document (by Appellant ‚Äì Accused)

For an appeal by an accused, the initiating document must be in **Form 1 of the Schedule** and must include:

1. The **offence(s) charged**
2. The **place of trial**
3. The **date of conviction and sentence**
4. The **court that made the conviction**
5. The **sentence imposed**
6. The **age of the accused**
7. Whether **oral evidence was tendered at trial**
8. Whether the accused **wishes to be present at the appeal hearing**
9. The **current mailing address of the accused**
10. The **nature and reasons** for the appeal (e.g., request for new trial, sentence reduction)
11. Whether a **publication ban** applies (and if so, details)
12. Whether **access to the court file is restricted** (and if so, details)

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception**: The court or a judge may make any order allowed by law, even if not requested or if reasons are insufficient.

---

### D. Content of the Initiating Document (by Crown)

For a Crown appeal:
- **Appeal from acquittal**: Use **Form 2**
- **Appeal from sentence**: Use **Form 3**

Must include:
1. Offence(s) charged
2. Place of trial
3. Date of acquittal or sentence
4. Court that granted acquittal or imposed sentence
5. Age of accused
6. (In appeal from sentence) The sentence imposed
7. (In appeal from acquittal) Whether oral evidence was tendered
8. Nature and reasons for the order sought
9. Whether a **publication ban** applies (and details)
10. Whether **access to the file is restricted** (and details)

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Same exception applies: The court or judge may issue any lawful order, regardless of whether it is requested or properly reasoned.

## üìÖ **2. Filing Timelines and Procedures**

### A. Appeals from Trials Without a Jury (Indictable Offences)
- A notice of appeal may **include a request** that, if a new trial is granted, the accused be tried by **judge and jury**.

### B. Notice of Appeal and Application for Leave
- **May be combined** in one document.
- **No separate notice is needed** if leave to appeal is granted.

### C. Leave to Appeal from Summary Conviction Appeal Court
- Must file a **separate notice of appeal within 7 days** after leave is granted.

### D. Filing for Leave to Appeal (General Rules)

| Type of Appeal | Procedure |
|---------------|----------|
| From a trial by indictment | May seek leave: <br> - On a **motion in chambers** with supporting materials <br> - Or from the court **during the hearing on merits** |
| From a decision of a summary conviction appeal court | Must file a **motion for leave in chambers within 30 days** of sentence or acquittal |

---

## üì¨ **3. Filing and Service of Initiating Documents**

### A. When Filed (Accused as Appellant)
- Must be sent in **quadruplicate** by **regular lettermail** or filed in quadruplicate in the registrar‚Äôs office.
- Must be filed **no later than 30 days after sentence**.

> The registrar retains **one copy** and **delivers the other three** to:
> - The Attorney General
> - The trial court
> - The summary conviction appeal court
> - The trial judge

### B. When Filed (Crown as Appellant)
- Must be filed in **duplicate** with the registrar **within 30 days** of sentence or acquittal.
- Must be **served on the respondent** (accused or counsel) in accordance with subrule 7(2).

> The registrar retains one copy and delivers one to the trial court or summary conviction appeal court.

---

## üìÆ **4. Deemed Service of Notices**

- A notice is **deemed served** if sent by **regular lettermail** to the **last known address** of the person.

### For Crown Service (Section 676):
- Must be served **personally** on:
  - Respondent or their legal counsel (authorized to accept)
- If respondent is in custody: Sent to the **warden or jailer**, who serves it personally.
- **Substitutional service** allowed by a judge‚Äôs order.
- A **certificate from the warden/jailer** is sufficient proof of service.

---

## üìÇ **5. Transcript Requirements and Filing**

### A. When a Transcript is Needed
- If evidence was **not recorded in a way that allows a transcript**, then the **presiding judge‚Äôs notes**, certified by the judge, are **prima facie proof** of the evidence and conduct.

### B. When a Transcript *Is* Available
- The appellant must file with the registrar **at the time of filing** the initiating document:
  - Confirmation from transcription services that the transcript has been ordered, **or**
  - A **letter explaining** why the transcript has not been ordered.

> If the registrar finds the explanation **unsatisfactory**, the appellant may apply to a **judge in chambers for an extension** within **10 days** of being notified.

> If the registrar **accepts** the explanation, they will **extend the time** and notify the appellant of the new deadline.

---

### C. Abandonment of Appeal (Key Risk)

An appeal is **deemed abandoned** if:
1. The transcript is **not ordered** at filing **and no extension is granted** by registrar or judge; **OR**
2. The transcript is **not ordered within any extended time period** granted.

> This is a critical procedural safeguard to prevent stale appeals.

---

### D. Charges for Transcript
- The **appellant pays** for the transcript.
- **Exception**: If the **Crown agrees** to cover the cost (as respondent), the Crown bears the expense.

> Charges follow the same rates as for **civil cases** in the Court of Queen‚Äôs Bench.

---

## üìö **6. Appeal Book Requirements**

The **Attorney General** (on behalf of the Crown) must prepare and file an **appeal book** within:

- **30 days** after the initiating document is filed (if Crown is appellant)
- **30 days after the factum is filed** (if Crown is respondent)
- **As soon as practicable** in sentence-only appeals
- **Where an application for leave to appeal is made**

> The appeal book must contain:
> - The initiating document
> - Written exhibits relevant to the appeal
> - Reasons for decision (whether in transcript or otherwise)
> - Pre-sentence reports and other sentencing-related exhibits
> - Any other materials necessary for adjudication

> In **expedited hearings**, the appeal book must be prepared and filed **before** the hearing.

> A party may file a **supplementary appeal book** to add materials omitted from the initial one.
