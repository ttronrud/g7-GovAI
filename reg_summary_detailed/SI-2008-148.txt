## üìú **Detailed Summary of the Order Governing the Grant of the Memorial Cross**

### **1. Purpose of the Regulation**
This Order establishes the current framework for granting the **Memorial Cross** to individuals who have suffered personal loss due to the death of a member or former member of the Canadian Forces. It replaces or updates previous rules (specifically the 1950 and 2006 Orders) to reflect changes in military service structures and to provide clarity on eligibility, designation, and administrative responsibilities.

The Memorial Cross serves as a **symbol of remembrance and tribute**, honoring the sacrifice of service members who died in connection with their military duties.

### **4. Eligibility Criteria for Granting the Memorial Cross**

#### ‚úÖ **Case 1: Death in Hostilities Outside Canada (Pre-2001 Service)**  
Applies to members or former members who died **outside Canada** due to military action under international commitments.

**Eligibility Conditions:**
- The member or former member **served before October 7, 2001**.
- In the case of a **member of the Supplementary Reserve**, they **did not serve** in or with the Regular Force, Primary Reserve, Cadet Instructors Cadre, or Canadian Rangers **on or after October 7, 2001**.
- The death was **attributable to military service in an area of hostilities** outside Canada, resulting from:
  - Actions under the **Charter of the United Nations**,
  - The **North Atlantic Treaty**, or
  - Any other **collective defence agreement** entered into by Canada.
- The death occurs **on or after the date this Order comes into force**.

**Designation Rules:**
- The **member or former member** may designate up to **two recipients** (in a form approved by the Minister of Veterans Affairs).
- If no such designation exists, or the form is missing, or no designated recipients survive:
  - The **representative of the estate** may designate up to **two recipients**.
  - If only **one** recipient is granted under a prior designation, the limit is **one**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note: A recipient who receives the Memorial Cross under the 1950 Order or 2006 Order will not be eligible for a new one under this Order.*

#### ‚úÖ **Case 2: Death Due to Injury or Disease Related to Military Service (Post-2001 Service)**  
Applies to members or former members who served **on or after October 7, 2001**.

**Eligibility Conditions:**
- The member or former member served in one of the following:
  - Regular Force,
  - Primary Reserve,
  - Cadet Instructors Cadre,
  - Canadian Rangers.
- For members of the **Supplementary Reserve**, they must have served **in or with** any of the above forces **on or after October 7, 2001**.
- The death must be attributable to an **injury or disease related to military service**.

**Designation Rules:**
- The **member or former member** may designate up to **three recipients** (in a form approved by the **Chief of the Defence Staff**).
- If no designation exists, or the form is missing, or no designated recipients survive:
  - The **representative of the estate** may designate:
    - Up to **three recipients** (if no prior designation under section 7),
    - **Two recipients** (if only one was granted under section 7),
    - **One recipient** (if two were granted under section 7).

> This reflects a broader recognition of service-related health issues arising after 2001.

### **5. Special Provision for Deaths Between October 7, 2001, and December 31, 2006**

If a member or former member died **between October 7, 2001, and December 31, 2006**, and the death occurred **before this Order came into force**, the representative may still designate recipients under the following limits:

- **Three recipients**, if **no Memorial Crosses** were previously granted under the 1950 Order.
- **Two recipients**, if **only one** was granted under the 1950 Order.
- **One recipient**, if **two** were granted under the 1950 Order.

> This provision ensures continuity and fairness in cases where prior recognition was limited.

### **6. Responsibility for Granting the Memorial Cross**

| Scenario | Responsible Minister |
|--------|------------------------|
| Member dies while serving in or with the **Regular Force, Primary Reserve, Cadet Instructors Cadre, or Canadian Rangers** | **Minister of National Defence** |
| Former member of CAF (Royal Canadian Navy, Canadian Army, Royal Canadian Air Force) dies, or a member of the **Supplementary Reserve** dies while not serving with the above forces | **Minister of Veterans Affairs** |

> This division of responsibility ensures appropriate oversight based on the nature of service and status.

### **7. Transition and Continuity Provisions**

- **Designation from the 2006 Order**:  
  If a member or former member designated recipients **before this Order came into force**, using a form approved under **section 6 of the 2006 Order**, that designation is **deemed valid** under the **current Order**, but only in the form approved under **section 7** of this Order.

> This ensures that valid prior designations are not invalidated by the new rules.

- **No Double Granting**:  
  A person who has already received a Memorial Cross under either the **1950 Order** or the **2006 Order** is **not eligible** to receive one under this Order.

> Prevents duplication and ensures that the cross is awarded only once per individual.

### **8. Repeal Clause**

- The words "[Repeal]" appear twice at the end of the document.  
  This indicates that **the 1950 Order and/or earlier versions are being formally replaced** by this new Order.  
  It does not mean the old rules are fully deleted ‚Äî rather, they are **superseded** and no longer apply in their original form.

> This new Order is now the **primary legal basis** for granting the Memorial Cross.
