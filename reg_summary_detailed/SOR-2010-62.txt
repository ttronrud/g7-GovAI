**Detailed Summary of the G20 Summit Privileges and Immunities Order, 2010**

---

### **Overview**
The *G20 Summit Privileges and Immunities Order, 2010* is a regulatory instrument issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**, under **section 5a of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act (FMIOA)**. This Order grants specific **privileges and immunities** to the **G20 (Group of Twenty)** and its associated participants during a series of meetings held in Canada in 2010.

The Order is designed to ensure that the G20 and its representatives can operate effectively in Canada without undue interference from domestic laws or authorities, particularly during the preparation and conduct of high-level diplomatic meetings.

---

### **Legal Basis**
- **Section 5a of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act (FMIOA)** provides the legal authority for the Governor General to make orders granting privileges and immunities to international organizations and their representatives.
- The Order is based on the **Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations**, specifically **Schedule III** to the FMIOA, which outlines the standard framework for such privileges.

---

### **Key Definitions**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Convention** | Refers to the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, as set out in Schedule III of the FMIOA. This serves as the model for the privileges and immunities granted under this Order. |
| **Experts performing missions for the Organization** | Governmental or intergovernmental experts invited by the G20 to attend the Meeting and preparatory meetings. These are not heads of state or government, but technical or policy experts supporting the Organization’s work. |
| **Meeting** | The main G20 summit scheduled to take place in **Toronto, Ontario**, from **June 26 to 27, 2010**. |
| **Organization** | The **G20**, an intergovernmental conference of the 20 leading industrialized countries. |
| **Preparatory meetings** | A series of meetings held in Canada during the period from March 11, 2010, to June 25, 2010, including: <br> - **March 18–19, 2010** in Ottawa (Sherpa meetings) <br> - **May 24–25, 2010** in Calgary (Sherpa meetings) <br> - **June 22–24, 2010** in Toronto (Sherpa meetings) <br> *Any other meetings of the Sherpas or equivalent representatives held in Canada during this period are also included.* |

---

### **Privileges and Immunities Granted**

#### **1. Legal Capacity of the G20 (Organization) in Canada**
- **Period**: From **March 11, 2010**, to **July 4, 2010**
- **Privilege**: The G20 is granted **legal capacity as a body corporate** in Canada.
- **Immunities**: The G20 enjoys the **privileges and immunities** specified in **Sections 2 to 5 of Article II of the Convention**.
  - These typically include:
    - Immunity from legal process (e.g., arrest, search, seizure)
    - Immunity from taxation
    - Freedom from interference in its internal affairs
    - Right to conduct official business without domestic jurisdictional constraints
  - This allows the G20 to operate as a formal entity in Canada, facilitating its diplomatic and administrative functions.

#### **2. Privileges and Immunities for State Representatives (during Preparatory Meetings)**
- **Period**: From **March 11, 2010**, to **July 4, 2010**
- **Applicable to**: Representatives of G20 member states or participating governments attending preparatory meetings (e.g., Sherpas).
- **Privileges and Immunities**: As set out in **Section 11(d) of Article IV of the Convention**.
  - These include:
    - Immunity from legal proceedings related to their official duties
    - Protection from arrest or detention during the performance of their duties
    - Immunity from civil or criminal liability for actions taken in the course of their official functions
    - Protection of personal and official communications
    - Freedom to travel and reside in Canada for official purposes
  - These are **limited to the extent required for the exercise of their functions** in Canada during preparatory meetings.

#### **3. Privileges and Immunities for State Representatives (during the Main Meeting)**
- **Period**: From **June 15, 2010**, to **July 4, 2010**
- **Applicable to**: Representatives of G20 member states or participating governments attending the **main Meeting in Toronto**.
- **Privileges and Immunities**: As set out in **Sections 11, 12, and 14 to 16 of Article IV of the Convention**.
  - These are broader and more comprehensive than those for preparatory meetings and include:
    - **Section 11**: Immunity from legal process (arrest, prosecution, seizure) for acts performed in the course of official duties.
    - **Section 12**: Immunity from taxation (e.g., income, property taxes) on income derived from official duties.
    - **Section 14**: Immunity from civil liability for acts done in the course of official duties.
    - **Section 15**: Immunity from criminal liability for acts done in the course of official duties.
    - **Section 16**: Protection of official communications and documents.
  - These immunities are **only applicable to actions taken in relation to the Meeting** and are not extended to private activities.

#### **4. Privileges and Immunities for Experts Performing Missions**
- **Period**: From **March 11, 2010**, to **July 4, 2010**
- **Applicable to**: Governmental or intergovernmental experts invited to attend the Meeting and preparatory meetings.
- **Privileges and Immunities**: As set out in **Article VI of the Convention**.
  - This article generally provides:
    - Immunity from legal proceedings for acts performed in the course of official duties.
    - Protection of personal and official communications.
    - Freedom to travel and reside in Canada for official purposes.
    - Immunity from taxation on income derived from official duties.
  - These protections are **limited to the extent required for the exercise of their functions** in Canada during both the Meeting and preparatory meetings.

---

### **Scope and Duration**
- The Order is effective from the day it is **registered** (i.e., published in the *Canada Gazette*).
- The entire period of privileges and immunities spans from **March 11, 2010**, to **July 4, 2010**, covering:
  - Preparatory meetings (March–June 2010)
  - The main G20 summit in Toronto (June 26–27, 2010)
  - Post-summit activities until July 4, 2010

> Note: The G20’s legal capacity and the privileges for representatives and experts begin earlier (March 11) and extend slightly beyond the Meeting (until July 4), allowing for a full diplomatic cycle.

---

### **Purpose and Significance**
This Order ensures that:
- The G20 can conduct its meetings in Canada without disruption from domestic legal or administrative barriers.
- Diplomatic officials and experts can carry out their duties freely, safely, and efficiently.
- Canada respects international norms and agreements related to diplomatic immunity.
- The host country maintains a neutral and supportive environment for international cooperation.

It reflects Canada’s commitment to hosting major international events with the necessary legal and institutional support, while upholding the principles of international law and diplomatic immunity.

---

### **Limitations and Conditions**
- All privileges and immunities are **conditional** and **limited to the extent required** for the performance of official duties.
- They do **not extend to private activities** or personal conduct.
- The immunity does **not protect** from liability for criminal acts unrelated to official duties or for acts committed outside the scope of official functions.
- The Order applies **only to individuals and entities directly involved** in the G20 activities in Canada.

---

### **Conclusion**
The *G20 Summit Privileges and Immunities Order, 2010* is a targeted, time-bound legal instrument that enables the smooth and secure operation of the G20 in Canada during a critical period of international economic and political dialogue. By aligning with the established framework of the United Nations Convention on Privileges and Immunities, the Order ensures that diplomatic and technical participants can operate without fear of legal interference, thereby supporting effective multilateral cooperation.

It exemplifies how Canada, as a host nation, can facilitate international diplomacy through clear, lawful, and internationally recognized mechanisms.
