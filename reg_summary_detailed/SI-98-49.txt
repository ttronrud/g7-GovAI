### ðŸ” **Purpose and Context**
This Convention establishes a framework for **cooperation between Canada and the United Kingdom** in the field of **social security**, particularly regarding:
- **Liability for contributions** (i.e., who pays into social security systems),
- **Eligibility for benefits** (such as pensions, disability, and industrial accident benefits),
- **Recognition of employment and residence** across borders.

It is based on **Article XVI of the Convention on Social Security** signed on January 16, 1997, and implemented through an Order in Council (P.C. 1997-496) and a subsequent Proclamation (P.C. 1998-287).

The Convention aims to:
- Prevent double taxation or double coverage of social security contributions,
- Ensure fair and reciprocal treatment of workers and dependants,
- Provide clarity in cases where individuals work or reside in both jurisdictions.
