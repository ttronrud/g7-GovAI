**Detailed Summary of the Indian Mining Regulations**

---

### **Overview**

The *Indian Mining Regulations* are a set of administrative rules governing the exploration, development, and production of minerals on lands within Indian reserves in Canada. These regulations are issued under the authority of the *Indian Act* and are administered by the **Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (DIAND)** through its **Oil and Mineral Division**.

These regulations apply specifically to **mineral rights underlying lands in Indian reserves**, with a key exception: they **do not apply** to surrendered mines or minerals underlying lands in **British Columbia**.

---

## **Key Definitions and Interpretations**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Indian Act*, which provides the legal framework for Indigenous land and resource management. |
| **Assessment Work** | Includes geological, geophysical, geochemical surveys, core/drill drilling, removal of overburden, underground work (e.g., shaft sinking), road building, and any other work approved by the Supervisor. It is essential for evaluating mineral potential. |
| **Department** | The Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (DIAND). |
| **Division Chief** | The head of the Oil and Mineral Division or an authorized representative. Responsible for issuing permits, leases, and managing tenders. |
| **Lease** | A grant of rights to explore, develop, and produce minerals within a defined lease area. Issued under sections 5, 6, or 19 of the *Indian Act*. |
| **Permit** | A grant of rights to explore and develop minerals in a permit area. Issued under sections 5 or 6. |
| **Minerals** | Naturally occurring metallic and non-metallic minerals and rocks containing such minerals. **Excludes** petroleum, natural gas, placer deposits (e.g., gravel, sand, peat), and unconsolidated materials. |
| **Person** | Any individual aged 21 or above, or a corporation registered or licensed in Canada or a province. |
| **Supervisor** | The Supervisor of Indian Minerals, or an authorized officer. Oversees compliance and assessment work. |
| **Treatment** | Concentrating, smelting, refining, or similar processes. Does **not** include washing, screening, or handling alone. |

> *Legal reference*: Section and legal subdivisions follow the definitions in *Part II of the Canada Lands Surveys Act*.

---

## **Applicability and Jurisdiction**

- Applies to **minerals underlying lands in Indian reserves**.
- **Excludes British Columbia**: The regulations do **not apply** to surrendered mines or minerals in reserves located in BC.
- Permittees and lessees must comply with **provincial laws** related to mineral exploration, development, production, treatment, and marketing — **provided such laws do not conflict** with these regulations.

---

## **Tendering and Issuance of Permits and Leases**

### **1. Public Tenders**
- The Division Chief may issue public tenders for mineral rights.
- Tenders may be advertised or issued in other ways deemed appropriate.
- The Division Chief may issue a permit or lease to the person offering the **highest tender** or **reject all tenders**.

### **2. Special Cases – Band-Approved Leases**
- The Division Chief may issue a permit or lease for minerals beneath lands set apart for a band’s use **only with the consent of the band council**.
- The lease or permit may be subject to terms approved by the band council.

### **3. Application Requirements**
- All applications for permits or leases must include:
  - The required **fee** (as listed in the schedule).
  - A **legal description** of the land area (using section, legal subdivision, lot, or aliquot part).
  - For leases: the **first-year rental** as specified in section 24.

> *Note*: If boundaries do not align with a formal survey, the Division Chief may allow **irregular boundary descriptions**.

---

## **Permit Duration and Extensions**

### **Initial Term**
- A permit expires after **one year** from issuance.

### **Extensions**
- A permittee may apply for an extension **before expiration**.
- Extension is granted for:
  - **One year**, or
  - A shorter period requested by the permittee.
- **Conditions**:
  - The permittee must submit:
    - A summary of work done under the permit and previous extensions.
    - A summary of proposed work if the permit is extended.
- **Maximum Extensions**:
  - A permittee is entitled to **no more than three extensions**, unless:
    - The tender or permit explicitly allows more than three extensions.
    - The Supervisor determines that extensions are **necessary to complete exploration** and that assessment work has been satisfactorily performed.

### **Reinstatement**
- If a permit expires, the holder may apply within **30 days** for **reinstatement and extension**.
- The Division Chief may reinstate and extend the permit for **up to one year** upon request by the Supervisor.

---

## **Rental and Security Deposits**

### **Rental Payments**
- **Initial Permit**: $0.25 per acre per year.
- **First 3 Extensions**: $0.04 per month per acre.
- **Any Further Extensions**: $0.08 per month per acre.

> *Note*: If a lease is issued under section 19, any prior rental paid under a permit is **refunded** for the unexpired portion of that permit.

### **Security Deposit**
- Required for all permits and leases.
- Amount and form (money, bonds, promissory notes) are specified in the tender or permit.
- Must be payable to the **Receiver General** at a chartered bank or in another form approved by the Division Chief.

> **Return of Deposit**:
- Upon expiry or surrender of a permit or lease, and upon verification of compliance, the **security deposit is returned** to the permittee or lessee.

---

## **Assessment Work Requirements**

### **Mandatory Work**
- **Permit Term (Initial)**: $0.50 per acre.
- **Each Extension**: $1.00 per acre.
- **Alternative**: The value may be specified in the tender or permit.

### **Work Near Permit Area**
- If assessment work is performed **near** a permit area and evaluates its mineral potential, the Supervisor may **deem it as assessment work** for the permit area.

### **Non-Compliance**
- If required work is not performed:
  - The permittee must make a **cash payment** equal to the difference between required and actual value.
- If **more than required** work is performed:
  - The **excess value** can be **credited** toward future assessment work requirements (for extensions or future leases).

### **Reporting Requirements**
- Within **90 days** after permit expiry or extension:
  - Submit a **certified statement** (in duplicate) detailing:
    - Assessment work performed.
    - Cost of performance.
    - Any cash payments due.
- Within **six months**:
  - Submit **maps and technical reports** (in duplicate) showing:
    - Assessment work performed.
    - Results obtained.

> *Failure to comply may result in additional information being requested by the Supervisor.*

---

## **Lease Provisions**

### **Lease Issuance**
- A lessee may apply for a lease in respect of a permit area or part thereof.
- Application must be:
  - In a form satisfactory to the Division Chief.
  - Include a legal description (by section, lot, or aliquot part).
  - Include the required **fee** and **first-year rental**.

### **Survey Requirements**
- If boundaries are not aligned with a formal survey, the applicant may be required to have the land **surveyed** by a commissioned land surveyor under the instructions of the **Surveyor General of Canada**.
- If a lease is later surveyed, the **description in the lease may be amended** to match the official survey.
- The **amended description** is deemed effective on the **30th day after being sent by registered mail** to the lessee.

---

## **Lease Duration and Renewal**

### **Initial Term**
- A lease expires after **10 years** from issuance, unless otherwise specified in the tender or lease.

### **Renewal**
- Renewal may be applied for before expiration.
- Renewal term:
  - As specified in the lease (if any), or
  - For **10 years** if no term is specified.
  - Or for a **shorter period** requested by the lessee.
- Lessees may **group two or more leases** within a single reserve.

> *Note*: This provision was **revoked** by SOR/90-468, s.1 (as per the text), indicating it may no longer be in force.

---

## **Rental and Royalty Obligations**

### **Annual Rental**
- **Rate**: $2 per acre per year.
- May be adjusted in the tender or lease.
- Paid in advance to the **Receiver General**.

> *Note*: If a lease is issued under section 19, the **security deposit** is returned for unexpired portions.
