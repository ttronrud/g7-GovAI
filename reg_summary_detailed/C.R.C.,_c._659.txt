### **Core Provisions**

#### **1. Prohibition of Dumping in Interprovincial Trade (From Unregulated Area to Regulated Area)**  
When turkeys are shipped **from the unregulated area into the regulated area** for **interprovincial trade** (i.e., not for export), the following rule applies:

> No person may market turkeys in a province **other than the province where they were produced** at a price that is **less than**:
> - The price charged at or about the same time for turkeys of an **equivalent variety, class, or grade** in the province or other geographical area where they were produced; **plus**
> - Any **reasonable transportation charges** incurred by the marketer to deliver the turkeys to the place of marketing.

- The rule applies only to **interprovincial trade**, not to exports.

> - Any **reasonable transportation charges** incurred by the marketer to deliver the turkeys to the place of marketing.

### **Key Principles and Objectives**

- **Anti-Dumping Objective**: To protect Canadian turkey producers from unfair competition due to the sale of turkeys at prices below their fair value.
- **Fair Market Price Benchmark**: The rule uses a benchmark based on the **local market price** in the province of origin, adjusted for reasonable transportation costs.
- **Geographic Fairness**: The regulation ensures that prices reflect the local market conditions of the place of origin, not external or foreign pricing influences.
- **Interprovincial Trade Focus**: The rules apply to trade *within* Canada, not to international exports or imports from outside Canada (which would fall under different trade regulations).

### **Scope and Application**

- Applies to **all turkeys**, including live and slaughtered, and all parts of turkeys.
- Applies to **all marketing activities** (buying, selling, transporting, processing).
- Applies to **all provinces** within the regulated area and to interprovincial movements involving the unregulated area.
- Applies to **both producers and third-party marketers** (e.g., distributors, retailers).

### **Why These Regulations Exist**

- To maintain a **level playing field** for Canadian turkey producers.
- To prevent **unfair competition** from turkeys sold at artificially low prices from outside the regulated area.
- To protect **domestic agriculture and food supply chains** from market distortions caused by dumping.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Canadian Turkey Anti-Dumping Regulations* establish a fair pricing mechanism for turkeys moving between Canadian provinces. By requiring that turkeys be marketed at a price no lower than the price charged in their place of origin (plus reasonable transportation costs), the regulations prevent dumping and protect domestic producers from being undercut by unfairly priced imports from outside the regulated area or from within Canada when moving across provincial borders.

These rules are essential for preserving market integrity, supporting local agriculture, and ensuring that Canadian producers can compete on fair terms in the domestic market.
