## **Core Principles of the Allocation Method**

1. **Allocation Based on Historical Performance**
   - Export allocations are primarily determined by **historical export volumes** (for exporters) or **historical production volumes** (for producers with no export history).
   - This ensures that entities with a track record of exporting receive a fair share based on past behavior.

2. **Quota Distribution by Province**
   - Separate allocations are established for each province:
     - Ontario
     - Quebec
     - Manitoba
     - Saskatchewan
   - Each province has a fixed monthly export quota (the "quantity") that is distributed among eligible applicants.

3. **Eligibility and Relinquishment**
   - Any primary producer or remanufacturer may **relinquish** their export allocation for 2014 by notifying the Minister in writing by **December 9, 2013**.
   - Relinquished allocations are excluded from the total pool used in calculations.

4. **Transfers of Allocations**
   - A primary producer or remanufacturer may **transfer** a portion of their export allocation to another entity, **with Ministerâ€™s consent** under subsection 6.3(4) of the EIPA.
   - Such transfers are **only valid** if:
     - The transferred volume corresponds to a real product volume; or
     - The total volume transferred (including non-product volumes) does **not exceed 15%** of the original allocation.

> âš ï¸ Note: Transfers are not automatically counted toward the transfereeâ€™s allocation â€” they are **deemed to belong to the original producer** in terms of export volume reporting.

## **Allocation Formulas by Province**

### **1. Ontario Quantity Allocation**

- **Base Allocation Formula**:
  $$
  \text{Allocation} = \left( \frac{\text{Ontario quantity}}{\text{Total historical export volume (Ontario)}} \right) \times \text{Producer's historical export volume}
  $$
  - Denominator excludes producers who relinquished their allocations.
  - Numerator is the producerâ€™s historical export volume from Ontario to the U.S.

- **Remainder Allocation**:
  - After the base allocation is assigned, the **remaining Ontario quantity** is distributed **in order of receipt** of export permit applications.
  - Each applicant receives the amount they request until the remainder is fully allocated.
  - Applicants must **exhaust their initial allocation** before applying for the remainder.

### **2. Quebec Quantity Allocation**

Quebec allocation is more complex due to **dual criteria** (export volume vs. production volume) and a **reserve pool** mechanism to ensure minimum coverage.

#### A. **Remanufacturer Allocation (Based on Historical Export Volume)**

- Formula:
  $$
  \text{Allocation} = \left( \frac{\text{Quebec quantity}}{\text{Total historical export volume (Quebec)}} \right) \times \text{Remanufacturerâ€™s historical export volume}
  $$
  - Excludes producers who relinquished allocations.

- **Minimum Threshold Requirement**:
  - The **sum of all remanufacturersâ€™ allocations** must be **at least 7.5% of the Quebec quantity**.
  - If not met, a **reserve pool** is created to cover the shortfall.

#### B. **Reserve Pool (Section 9)**

- Formula:
  $$
  \text{Reserve Pool} = \left( \text{Quebec quantity} - \text{Sum of remanufacturer allocations} \right) + \text{Shortfall to meet 7.5\% threshold}
  $$
  - The reserve pool is used to allocate additional volumes to **primary producers**.

#### C. **Primary Producer Allocation (Based on Historic Production Volume)**

- Formula:
  $$
  \text{Allocation} = \left( \frac{\text{Reserve Pool}}{\text{Total production volume (by primary producers)}} \right) \times \text{Producerâ€™s production volume (2010â€“2012)}
  $$
  - Only applies to primary producers who:
    - Did **not** export any products to the U.S. during the reference period, **OR**
    - Produced Quebec softwood lumber between **January 1, 2010 â€“ December 31, 2012**.

- **Cap on Allocation**:
  - No primary producer can receive more than **40% of their average monthly production volume** (2010â€“2012) as an allocation.

> ðŸ” *Key Rule*: Primary producers must choose between:
> - Receiving allocation based on **historic export volume** (if they exported in the past), or
> - Receiving allocation based on **historic production volume** (if they never exported).

> ðŸ“ *Data Source*: Production volumes are based on records provided by the **Government of Quebec**, with the consent of the producer.

#### D. **Residual Non-Allocated Quantity (Section 11)**

- Formula:
  $$
  \text{Residual} = \text{Reserve Pool} - \text{Total allocated to primary producers}
  $$
  - This residual amount is **not distributed** and remains unallocated.

#### E. **Primary Producer Allocation (Based on Historic Export Volume)**

- Formula:
  $$
  \text{Allocation} = \left( \frac{\text{Producerâ€™s historical export volume}}{\text{Total historical export volume (by primary producers)}} \right) \times \text{Quebec quantity}
  $$
  - Excludes relinquished allocations.

> âš–ï¸ *Balance*: This allocation ensures that producers with export history are fairly rewarded, while the reserve pool ensures that even non-exporting producers get a fair share based on production.
