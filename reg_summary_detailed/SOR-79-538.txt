**Detailed Summary of the Consolidated Regulations Delivery Regulations**

---

### **1. Introduction and Legal Basis**

The *Consolidated Regulations Delivery Regulations* are a statutory instrument made under the authority of **paragraph 27(k) of the Statutory Instruments Act**. This regulation was issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Justice**. It establishes a formal framework for the **free distribution of copies of the Consolidated Regulations of Canada, 1978** to specific individuals and institutions.

These regulations may be cited as the **Consolidated Regulations Delivery Regulations** and are designed to ensure that key legal and governmental stakeholders have **accessible, affordable, and timely access** to the official codified version of Canadian federal regulations.

---

### **2. Definitions**

The regulation begins with two key definitions:

#### **(a) Consolidated Regulations**
- Refers to the *Consolidated Regulations of Canada, 1978*.
- This is the official, codified compilation of all federal statutes and regulations, arranged in a single, authoritative source for ease of reference.

#### **(b) Hard Cover Copy**
- A physical set of volumes containing the Consolidated Regulations.
- Features:
  - Solid binder board cover of **0.120 inch thickness**.
  - Bound with **Tanotex** (a type of binding material), specifically:
    - Bright finish quality **Number 8524**.
    - Colour **Number 490**, dark blue, with **Morocco grain finish**.
    - Manufactured by **Columbia Finishing Mills Limited**.
  - Lettered in **silver**.
- This version is durable, professionally produced, and intended for long-term preservation and official use.

#### **(c) Soft Cover Copy**
- A series of volumes containing the Consolidated Regulations.
- Features:
  - Cover made of **paper No. 1 coated cover**, S.C., C1S, coated on one side.
  - Basis: **301M**, caliber: **0.012**.
  - Manufactured by **Domtar Inc.**.
  - Bound using **perfect binding** (a wrap-around binding process).
- This version is more affordable, portable, and suitable for routine, personal, or institutional use.

---

### **3. Distribution Provisions**

The regulations specify **three distinct categories of free delivery** based on the type of copy and the recipient’s role or function:

---

#### **(i) Free Delivery of Hard Cover Copies**

> **To persons holding offices specified in Schedule I.**

- These are **officials and senior government officials** who are directly involved in the administration, interpretation, or enforcement of regulations.
- The hard cover copy is delivered **without charge** to those individuals currently holding these offices.
- **Purpose**: To provide durable, authoritative, and long-lasting access to the consolidated regulations for high-level officials who require it for official duties.

> **Schedule I includes:**
- The Governor General of Canada  
- The Prime Minister of Canada  
- The Speaker of the Senate  
- The Speaker of the House of Commons  
- The President of the Privy Council  
- The Minister of Justice  
- The Leader of the Opposition in the Senate  
- The Leader of the Opposition in the House of Commons  
- The Chief Justice of Canada  
- The Chief Justice of the Federal Court of Canada  
- The Associate Chief Justice of the Federal Court of Canada  
- The Clerk of the Privy Council  
- The Deputy Minister of Justice  
- The Assistant Clerk of the Privy Council  
- The Registrar of Statutory Instruments  
- Members of the Statute Revision Commission  
- Chairmen or Co-Chairmen of any Committee established under **Section 26 of the Statutory Instruments Act**

> **Note**: This provision emphasizes **institutional and judicial leadership** roles in the federal government and Parliament.

---

#### **(ii) Free Delivery of Soft Cover Copies (for use during office tenure)**

> **To persons holding offices specified in Schedule II.**

- Soft cover copies are delivered **without charge** to individuals currently holding these offices.
- The copy is provided **for use while they are in office**.
- **Purpose**: To support the day-to-day work of officials who need access to regulations for administrative, legislative, or policy-related functions.

> **Schedule II includes:**
- Each member of the judiciary of Canada, including:
  - Provincial court judges
  - Magistrates
- Each Attorney General, Minister of Justice, and Solicitor General of the provincial governments
- Each Deputy Attorney General, Deputy Minister of Justice, and Deputy Solicitor General of the provincial governments
- The Parliamentary Librarian
- The Librarian and Archivist of Canada
- The chief librarian of the United Nations
- The chief librarian of each provincial legislature and territorial council
- The chief librarian of each law school in Canada
- The chief librarian of each courthouse in Canada
- Each member of Parliament
- Each official of the Senate and House of Commons **directly concerned with the administration or review and scrutiny of federal regulations**

> **Note**: This group includes **judicial, legislative, and legal professionals** who regularly engage with federal regulations in their official duties. The soft cover format supports accessibility and portability.

---

#### **(iii) Free Delivery of Soft Cover Copies (on request)**

> **To persons holding offices or performing functions specified in Schedule III.**

- Soft cover copies are delivered **on request** to individuals currently holding offices or performing specific functions listed in Schedule III.
- The copy is provided **for use while they hold those offices or perform those functions**.
- **Purpose**: To extend access to key stakeholders who may not be in formal office but are engaged in regulatory or policy work.

> **Schedule III includes:**
- Members of the government of any country, **officials of their legislatures**, and **universities or other institutions**, **if reciprocal arrangements exist** for similar free distribution of government publications in Canada.

> **Note**: This provision reflects **international cooperation** in the dissemination of legal publications. It allows for **mutual exchange of government publications** between Canada and other countries, provided that a reciprocal agreement exists.

> **Example**: If a U.S. official requests a copy of a Canadian regulation, and there is a reciprocal arrangement with Canada, the Canadian government may provide a soft cover copy.

---

### **4. Key Objectives and Rationale**

The regulation serves several important purposes:

1. **Accessibility**: Ensures that key government, legal, and legislative personnel have **free access** to the authoritative version of Canadian regulations.
2. **Efficiency**: Reduces duplication and cost of obtaining regulations by centralizing distribution through official channels.
3. **Transparency and Accountability**: Supports the ability of officials and judges to review and interpret regulations accurately, enhancing transparency in governance.
4. **Judicial and Legislative Support**: Empowers judges, legislators, and legal professionals with reliable, up-to-date information.
5. **International Cooperation**: Encourages mutual exchange of government publications, strengthening international legal and institutional ties.

---

### **5. Scope and Limitations**

- **Not all individuals** are entitled to receive copies.
- Distribution is **limited to those specified in Schedules I, II, and III**.
- Copies are **not available to the general public** or private individuals unless under special arrangements.
- The **format** (hard vs. soft cover) is tied to the **nature and use** of the copy:
  - Hard cover: for permanent, official, high-level use.
  - Soft cover: for temporary, practical, or mobile use.
- **Requests for soft cover copies under Schedule III** are **conditional** and require **reciprocal arrangements**.

---

### **6. Conclusion**

The *Consolidated Regulations Delivery Regulations* are a practical and targeted mechanism to ensure that **authorized officials, judges, legislators, and institutions** have **free and timely access** to the official codified version of Canadian regulations. By clearly defining who qualifies for free delivery, specifying the format of the copies, and establishing conditions for international exchange, the regulation enhances the **operational efficiency, transparency, and legal integrity** of Canada’s regulatory and legislative systems.

It reflects a long-standing principle in public administration: that **access to the law is a public good**, especially for those who are responsible for its interpretation, enforcement, or oversight.

---

**In short**:  
These regulations ensure that key Canadian government, legal, and legislative officials can obtain free, official copies of the *Consolidated Regulations of Canada, 1978*—in both hard and soft cover formats—based on their official roles or functions, promoting transparency, accountability, and effective governance.
