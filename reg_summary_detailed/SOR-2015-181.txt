### **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**
The *Secure Air Travel Regulations* are made under **section 32 of the Secure Air Travel Act (SATA)** by the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness (MPSEP)**.  
The primary purpose is to **enhance aviation security** by implementing a system of **identity verification and passenger screening** for certain domestic and international flights, especially those involving high-risk or large-scale operations.

### **4. Information Collection and Submission Requirements**

#### **4.1. Pre-Flight Information Collection (72 Hours Before Departure)**  
Air carriers must collect the following information about each passenger expected to board:

- Citizenship or nationality  
- Passport details (number, expiry, issuing country) or permanent resident card (Canada or U.S.)  
- Canadian Travel Number (if provided)  
- Reservation record locator number  
- Unique passenger reference assigned by the air carrier  
- Flight code (airline + flight number)  
- Scheduled departure date, time, and place  
- Scheduled arrival date, time, and place  

> ðŸ“Œ This information must be collected **no later than 72 hours before scheduled departure**.  
> If a reservation is made **after 72 hours**, the information must be submitted at the time of reservation.

#### **4.2. Timeliness of Submission to the Minister**

- **For persons expected to board**:  
  - Information must be provided **within 72 hours** of departure or at time of reservation (whichever is later).  
  - Any **new information or changes** must be reported **as soon as feasible** before departure.

- **For persons on board**:  
  - Information must be provided to the Minister **within 30 minutes after departure** (e.g., boarding gate time).  
  - This includes updated details such as actual departure time or changes in passenger details.

> âœ… The information must be submitted via an **electronic communication system** compatible with the Ministerâ€™s system and capable of:
> - Receiving and acknowledging receipt of data
> - Receiving directions from the Minister under **section 9 of the Act**

> ðŸ” Air carriers must **use the same system** for cancellation notifications (see below).

### **6. Identity Verification at Check-In and Boarding**

#### **6.1. Pre-Boarding Checks (Before Boarding Pass Issuance)**

##### **If the Minister has flagged the passenger as a "listed person" (on a watchlist)**:
- The air carrier **must not allow** the person to obtain a boarding pass via:
  - Self-service kiosks  
  - Internet check-in  
- If the person visits the **check-in desk**, the carrier must:
  - Verify identity using acceptable photo ID (as defined below)
  - Compare:
    - Name, date of birth, and photo on ID with reservation record
    - Full face (if photo ID is presented) with the photo in the document

> âš ï¸ **If a discrepancy is found**, the air carrier **must not issue a boarding pass** until receiving a response from the Minister.

> âœ… **Exception**: The air carrier may issue a boarding pass **only if**:
> - No discrepancy is found, **OR**
> - A discrepancy exists but the Minister confirms the person's details still match a listed individual (i.e., no new direction issued)

> ðŸ“ž The air carrier must **contact the Minister of Transport by phone** and be informed that **no direction will be issued under subsection 9(1)** (except under paragraph 9(1)(b)) before allowing boarding.

#### **6.2. Identity Verification at Boarding Gate**

### **7. Prohibitions on Transportation**
