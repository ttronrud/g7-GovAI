### **1. Regulatory Authority and Purpose**
- The **Minister of Natural Resources** has approved the *Canada Lands Surveyors Regulations*, made by the **Council of the Association of Canada Lands Surveyors**, pursuant to **Section 62 of the Canada Lands Surveyors Act**.
- These regulations establish the **standards, procedures, and responsibilities** governing the practice of surveying in Canada, particularly for individuals licensed as **Canada Lands Surveyors (C.L.S.)**, permit holders, and members of the Association.

### **3. Code of Ethics (Section 3)**

All members ‚Äî including members, Canada Lands Surveyors, licence holders, and permit holders ‚Äî must adhere to the following ethical obligations:

#### **(1) Serving the Public**
- Serve the public with **accuracy, efficiency, and integrity** for the development and peaceful enjoyment of Canada‚Äôs lands and natural resources.

#### **(2) Honesty and Trustworthiness**
- Maintain **confidentiality** regarding client or employer affairs during and after employment.
- **Certify only work performed by them or under their supervision**.
- Enter into **fee-splitting arrangements only with client consent**.

#### **(3) Upholding the Law**
- Refrain from entering arrangements that allow the **unauthorized practice** of surveying.
- **Willfully refrain from becoming an accessory** to illegal practices of surveying.

#### **(4) Avoiding Professional Impropriety**
- Disclose **any conflict of interest** that could impair service quality.
- Avoid using their name in association with entities of **dubious ethics**.
- Accept **no compensation from more than one source** for the same service without full consent.

#### **(5) Fair Compensation**
- Charge and accept **fair and reasonable fees** based on:
  - Professional and technical complexity
  - Level of responsibility
  - Professional liability
- Make **details of fees available to clients upon request**.

#### **(6) Professional Competence and Responsibility**
- Assume **full professional responsibility** for all phases of survey work under supervision.
- Foster **integrity and understanding of professional obligations** in employees.
- Continually **advance skills through study and education**.
- Refrain from **public criticism of colleagues**.
- Report **perceived misconduct or incompetence** to the Registrar.
- Keep **adequate records** to allow peer review.
- Avoid assigning work beyond one‚Äôs competence or available resources.
- Avoid **misleading or self-laudatory advertising**.

> üîÅ *Note: One provision (about cultivating employee integrity) was repealed by SOR/2011-291, s.2.*

### **5. Registers Maintained by the Registrar**

The **Registrar** maintains three official registers:

| Register | Information Required |
|--------|------------------------|
| **Canada Lands Surveyors** | Name, commission granted, number/date, status, address |
| **Members of the Association** | Name, enrolment dates, membership category, address/phone, employer/firm, membership fees, licence status, insurance proof, claims against member |
| **Permit Holders** | Name, date of first permit, address, names of supervising members, permit status, fees, insurance, claims against permit holder |

> These registers ensure **public accountability and transparency** in the profession.

### **6. Membership and Application Process**

- **Application to Join**:
  - Must be submitted to the Registrar using a form provided by the Association.
- **Renewal**:
  - Must be filed **before the end of the fiscal year**.
  - Renewal is granted if compliant with Act and Regulations.
- **Reapplication After Lapse or Cancellation**:
  - Can apply again if compliant with requirements.

> ‚öñÔ∏è **Note**: Membership is not automatic; applicants must meet educational and ethical standards.

### **7. Path to Commission as a Canada Lands Surveyor**

#### **Eligibility Requirements**

| Eligibility Path | Requirements |
|------------------|-------------|
| **Already entitled to survey in a province** | Submit: Application form, copy of commission/licence, certification from issuing agency |
| **Registered as candidate in a province** | Submit: Application form, certification from provincial body, proof of licence/commission issuance |
| **No provincial qualification** | Submit: Application form, academic transcript, diploma/degree/certificate (post-secondary education) |

> üìå **Education Requirement**: Must have at least the level of education of a **graduate in survey technology from a Canadian college**.

> üìù **Language Requirement**: Documents not in English or French must be **translated by a certified translator**.
