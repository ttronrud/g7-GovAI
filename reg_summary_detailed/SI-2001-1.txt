**Detailed Summary of the Treaty Land Entitlement (Manitoba) Remission Order**

---

### **Overview**

The **Treaty Land Entitlement (Manitoba) Remission Order**, made under subsection 23(2)a of the *Financial Administration Act* by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of National Revenue, provides a **tax remission** (i.e., exemption or relief from taxation) to certain **First Nations** in Manitoba. This remission applies specifically to taxes, interest, and penalties related to land transactions and associated property transfers that occur under the framework of the **Manitoba Framework Agreement on Treaty Land Entitlement**.

The Order is designed to support the implementation of treaty rights and land entitlements for First Nations by reducing financial burdens associated with acquiring or transferring land or property under formal agreements.

---

### **Legal Basis**

- **Authority**: The Order is issued under **subsection 23(2)a of the Financial Administration Act**, which allows the Governor General in Council (on ministerial recommendation) to make remission orders in the public interest.
- **Relevant Act**: The *Excise Tax Act* (Act), particularly **Division II (tax on supply)** and **Division VIII (interest and penalties)** of **Part IX**, which govern the taxation of certain transactions involving land and tangible personal property.

---

### **Key Definitions**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Excise Tax Act*. |
| **First Nation** | A band recognized under subsection 2(1) of the *Indian Act*, listed in **Column 1 of Schedule 1 or Schedule 2**. |
| **Framework Agreement** | The *Manitoba Framework Agreement on Treaty Land Entitlement*, executed on **May 29, 1997**, between Her Majesty the Queen in right of Canada, Her Majesty the Queen in right of Manitoba, and the *Treaty Land Entitlement Committee of Manitoba Inc.* |
| **Qualifying Land** | Land selected or acquired by a First Nation in accordance with a *specific agreement*, and **confirmed in writing** by the Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (DIAND). |
| **Specific Agreement** | Two types:<br>1. For First Nations in **Schedule 1**: An agreement between the First Nation and the Government of Canada, entered into on a specified date, fulfilling Canada’s treaty obligation to reserve land.<br>2. For First Nations in **Schedule 2**: An agreement among the First Nation, the Government of Canada, the Government of Manitoba, and the Treaty Land Entitlement Committee, whereby the First Nation accepts the terms of the framework agreement. |
| **Third Party Interest** | As defined in subsection 1.01(91) of the Framework Agreement — typically refers to a financial or ownership interest in land held by a third party (e.g., a lease, mortgage, or right of use) that is transferred to or canceled in favor of the First Nation. |
| **Tangible Personal Property** | Physical assets (e.g., buildings, equipment) located on land described in a third-party interest at the time of the First Nation’s acquisition of that interest. |

> All other terms are interpreted according to **subsection 123(1)** of the *Excise Tax Act*.

---

### **Scope of Remission**

The Order grants a **full remission** (i.e., tax relief) to eligible First Nations for the following types of transactions:

#### 1. **Supply of Qualifying Land**
- Tax on the **value of consideration** paid or payable by the First Nation or its agent for the supply of qualifying land.
- Applies only to land up to the **maximum acreage** specified in **Column 3** of the relevant Schedule (Schedule 1 or 2).

#### 2. **Supply or Cancellation of Third Party Interests**
- Tax on the **supply of** or **cancellation in favour of** a third-party interest in land described in subparagraph (i).
- This includes situations where a First Nation acquires ownership of a land interest previously held by a third party (e.g., a lease or mortgage).

#### 3. **Supply of Tangible Personal Property**
- Tax on the supply of tangible personal property (e.g., buildings, machinery) located **on the land** described in subparagraph (i) at the time of the First Nation’s acquisition of the land interest.
- **Condition**: Title to the tangible personal property must be **transferred** to the First Nation or its agent.

#### 4. **Costs Incurred in Connection with the Transactions**
- Remission extends to **reasonable and documented costs** incurred by the First Nation or its agent in carrying out any of the above transactions (e.g., legal fees, surveying, administrative costs).

#### 5. **Interest and Penalties**
- Remission of **interest and penalties** under **Division VIII of Part IX** of the *Excise Tax Act* for any of the transactions listed above.

---

### **Conditions for Remission**

To qualify for remission, the following **conditions must be met**:

1. **No Prior Rebate or Relief**  
   The tax, interest, or penalty in question **must not have already been**:
   - Rebated,
   - Credited,
   - Refunded,
   - Or remitted  
   to any person under the *Excise Tax Act* or the *Financial Administration Act*.

2. **Timely Claim Submission**  
   A **written claim** must be submitted to the **Minister of National Revenue** within **two years** of the relevant deadline:

   - For taxes, interest, and penalties **paid before** the Order came into force:  
     → Within **two years after the Order’s coming into force**.

   - For taxes, interest, and penalties **paid on or after** the Order came into force:  
     → Within **two years after the date of payment**.

> This ensures that claims are made promptly and prevents indefinite delays in recovery or relief.

---

### **Purpose and Public Interest Justification**

The Order is made **in the public interest** to:
- Support the fulfillment of **treaty obligations** and **treaty land entitlements** for First Nations in Manitoba.
- Reduce financial barriers to land acquisition and development, enabling First Nations to exercise their rights under treaties and frameworks.
- Promote **self-determination**, **economic development**, and **cultural revitalization** by enabling First Nations to access land and property for community use, governance, and economic activities.

---

### **Key Impacts and Implications**

| Aspect | Implication |
|-------|-------------|
| **Land Access** | First Nations can acquire land without facing excise tax on the value of consideration, encouraging land-based development. |
| **Financial Relief** | Eliminates excise tax, interest, and penalties on qualifying land and property transactions, reducing administrative and financial burdens. |
| **Legal Clarity** | Defines clear criteria (e.g., qualifying land, specific agreements, third-party interests) to ensure eligibility and consistency. |
| **Compliance Requirement** | Requires **written claims** and **timely submission**, ensuring accountability and transparency in the remission process. |
| **Exclusivity** | Applies only to First Nations listed in **Schedule 1 or Schedule 2**, and only to transactions under the specified agreements. |

---

### **Conclusion**

The **Treaty Land Entitlement (Manitoba) Remission Order** is a targeted and significant policy tool that recognizes the historical and ongoing relationship between the Government of Canada and First Nations in Manitoba. By providing tax relief on land and property transactions tied to treaty land entitlements, it supports the implementation of self-governance, land rights, and economic development within Indigenous communities.

It reflects a broader commitment to **indigenous reconciliation**, **treaty fulfillment**, and **financial equity**, while operating within the legal and fiscal framework of the *Excise Tax Act* and the *Financial Administration Act*.
