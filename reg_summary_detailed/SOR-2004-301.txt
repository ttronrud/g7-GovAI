**Detailed Summary of the Mingan Archipelago National Park Reserve of Canada Snowshoe Hare Regulations**  
*(S.C. 2000, c. 32; as amended)*

---

### **1. Regulatory Context and Authority**

These regulations were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of the Environment**, under **Section 17 of the Canada National Parks Act**. This grants the government the authority to establish specific rules governing traditional harvesting activities within designated national park reserves.

The regulations specifically govern the **snaring of snowshoe hares** in the **Mingan Archipelago National Park Reserve of Canada**, a protected area in Quebec. The purpose is to balance **traditional Indigenous and local harvesting practices** with the need to **protect ecological integrity**, ensure **sustainable resource use**, and maintain **environmental conservation**.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined and used throughout the regulations:

- **Act**: Refers to the *Canada National Parks Act*.
- **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**: Has the same meaning as in subsection 2(1) of the *Parks Canada Agency Act* — i.e., the head of Parks Canada (currently the Director General).
- **Common-law partner**: A person cohabiting with another in a conjugal relationship for at least one year.
- **Eligible person**: A person who meets one of the following criteria:
  - Was a permanent resident of Longue-Pointe-de-Mingan, Mingan, Havre-Saint-Pierre, or Baie-Johan-Beetz on **June 29, 1984**;
  - Was born in one of those areas and is a **first or second-generation descendant** (by blood or adoption) of someone meeting the above criteria, and is **16 years or older** at the time of applying for a permit;
  - Is the **spouse or common-law partner** of someone meeting the above criteria.
- **Park reserve**: Refers specifically to the **Mingan Archipelago National Park Reserve of Canada**.
- **Possession**: As defined in subsection 4(3) of the *Criminal Code* — includes having control or ownership of an item, including an animal.
- **Season**: The period during which snare permits are issued (typically a defined calendar period, e.g., winter months).
- **Snare**: The act of capturing or attempting to capture an animal using a snare.
- **Snare permit**: A permit issued under Section 8 of the Act to legally snare snowshoe hares.
- **Snowshoe hare**: Refers to the species *Lepus americanus*, including any part of the animal.
- **Superintendent**: A person appointed under the *Parks Canada Agency Act* as the superintendent of the park reserve, or any officer authorized to act on their behalf.

---

### **3. Scope and Applicability**

- These regulations apply **to the Mingan Archipelago National Park Reserve**.
- They **take precedence over any conflicting regulations** made under the Canada National Parks Act.
- **Sections 5 and 6** (which generally apply to enforcement and administrative procedures) **do not apply** to:
  - The superintendent, park wardens, enforcement officers, or peace officers **when carrying out duties related to snowshoe hare harvesting**;
  - Any person **authorized by the superintendent to conduct research** on snowshoe hares or harvesting activities.

> This exception ensures that management and scientific personnel can operate without undue restrictions while performing their official duties.

---

### **4. Traditional Resource Harvesting Status**

- Snaring snowshoe hares is designated as a **traditional renewable resource harvesting activity**.
- This recognition affirms the cultural and historical significance of the practice among local communities, particularly Indigenous groups and long-standing residents of the region.

---

### **5. Permit Requirements**

#### **Who Can Apply?**
Only **eligible persons** may apply for a snare permit. Eligibility is based on:
- Residence or birth in specific communities in the park (Longue-Pointe-de-Mingan, Mingan, Havre-Saint-Pierre, Baie-Johan-Beetz) as of June 29, 1984;
- Lineage (first or second generation) from such individuals;
- Spousal or common-law partner status with an eligible person.

#### **Application Process**
- Applications must be submitted to the **superintendent** on a form provided by them.
- Required information includes:
  - Full name, date of birth, and permanent address of the applicant;
  - **Written evidence** confirming eligibility (e.g., birth certificate, residency proof, family documentation).

#### **Permit Issuance**
- The superintendent may issue a snare permit **only upon application by an eligible person**.
- The permit is subject to **determinations under Sections 15–17**, which govern area restrictions, limits, and conditions.

#### **Exclusions from Permit Issuance**
- No one can obtain a permit if:
  - They already hold a **valid or suspended permit** for the same season;
  - A previous permit issued to them was **revoked within the last 12 months**.

---

### **6. Snare Permit Contents and Conditions**

Every snare permit must include the following:

- Name and date of birth of the permit holder;
- Permanent residence address;
- A **prohibition on transfer or assignment** of the permit;
- A detailed statement of the **duties and responsibilities** of the permit holder (as outlined in Sections 12–14);
- A notice that **contravention of permit conditions constitutes an offence under subsection 24(3) of the Canada National Parks Act**;
- Any **standard conditions** set by the superintendent, including:
  - Duration of the permit;
  - Specific areas where snaring is allowed;
  - Areas where snaring is **restricted** during part of the season;
  - Maximum number of snowshoe hares that may be snared and possessed;
  - Method for **indicating snares, snaring lines, and trails**;
  - Method for **attaching tags** provided by the superintendent to each snared hare.

> [Note: A previous provision (repealed in SOR/2009-322, s.23) related to reporting requirements has been removed.]

---

### **7. Superintendent’s Discretion and Management Powers**

The superintendent has broad authority to ensure ecological protection and sustainable management:

- **Can impose supplementary conditions** on all snare permits (e.g., seasonal closures, area restrictions, snare limits) to protect snowshoe hare populations and preserve ecological integrity.
- **Determines annually**:
  - Which areas allow or restrict snaring;
  - The **annual harvest limit** (number of hares per permit);
  - The **maximum number of permits** that can be issued per area.

- **May close areas** to snaring at any time for reasons such as:
  - Ecological protection;
  - Public safety;
  - Conservation of natural resources.

> These decisions are based on scientific monitoring and environmental assessments.

---

### **8. Permit Holder Obligations**

Permit holders must comply with the following rules:

✅ **Must carry the permit** when snaring or in possession of snowshoe hares.

✅ **Must produce the permit, snares, and any hares in possession** upon request by:
- Superintendent;
- Park warden;
- Enforcement officer;
- Peace officer.

✅ **Must attach a tag** to each snared hare using the method specified in the permit.

✅ **Must report immediately** to a park warden if a snare accidentally captures a non-snowshoe hare that is **wounded or dead**.

✅ **Must reduce snare numbers** after snaring **half of the permitted limit** so that the total number of snares does not exceed the remaining half of the limit.

✅ **Must remove snares, line indicators, and trail markers** when:
- The harvest limit is reached; or
- The permit expires (within 48 hours if the limit is not reached).

> If the permit holder fails to comply, the superintendent may **remove and dispose of the snares**.

---

### **9. Permit Expiry and Return Requirements**

- A snare permit expires on the **earlier of**:
  - The date stated on the permit;
  - The date it is revoked.
- Within **60 days after expiry**, the permit holder must **return the permit** to the superintendent.
- Upon return, the holder must record on the back of the permit:
  - Number of snares set;
  - Number of hares snared;
  - Areas where hares were caught;
  - Any other information required by permit conditions.

---

### **10. Enforcement and Sanctions**

#### **Suspension of Permits**
- A permit may be **suspended** if the holder violates any regulation or condition.
- Suspension may be lifted if:
  - The contravention is corrected;
  - 30 days pass after suspension (unless legal proceedings are initiated);
  - Proceedings are dismissed or the holder is found not guilty.

#### **Revocation of Permits**
- A permit is **revoked** if:
  - The holder is **convicted of an offence** related to a violation of these regulations;
  - The permit has been **suspended twice** during its validity (except when reinstated under paragraph (b) or (c) above).
