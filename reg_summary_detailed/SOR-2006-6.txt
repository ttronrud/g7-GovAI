**Detailed Summary of the Public Service Staffing Tribunal Regulations (S.C. 2003, c. 22, ss. 12 and 13; Ottawa, December 22, 2005)**

These regulations, made under **section 109 of the Public Service Employment Act (PSEA)**, establish the procedural framework governing complaints made to the **Public Service Staffing Tribunal (PST)**. The regulations ensure that complaints related to public service employment—such as layoffs, revocations, appointments, or proposed appointments—are processed fairly, efficiently, and in a manner that respects procedural rights, accessibility, and due process.

---

### **1. Key Definitions**

The regulations begin with a clear set of definitions to ensure consistent interpretation:

- **Act**: Refers to the *Public Service Employment Act* (PSEA).
- **Contact Information**: Includes telephone numbers, mailing addresses, and electronic addresses.
- **Day**: A calendar day.
- **Intervenor**: Any person permitted under subsection 19(4) of the Act to participate in a hearing.
- **Party**: Anyone with a right to be heard under specific sections of the Act (e.g., subsection 65(3), section 75, 79(1), or 85).
- **Respondent**: 
  - In cases involving lay-offs, revocations, appointments, or proposed appointments, the **deputy head** (if authorized under section 15 of the Act); 
  - Otherwise, the **Commission** (i.e., the Public Service Staffing Tribunal or the relevant public service body).
- **Signature**: A handwritten or electronic signature (digital or symbolic) incorporated into or attached to an electronic document.
- **Writing**: For individuals with disabilities affecting written communication, includes any accessible means of expression that can be understood and retained (e.g., speech-to-text, braille, sign language).
- **Standardized Test**: *Repealed* by SOR/2011-116, s.1 — no longer applicable.
  
> **Note**: The roles of the **Canadian Human Rights Commission (CHRC)** and the **Accessibility Commissioner** are defined as participants in proceedings when they give formal notice under subsections 20(3) and 20.1(3) of the PSEA that they intend to submit on issues involving:
> - Interpretation or application of the *Canadian Human Rights Act* (for CHRC);
> - Contravention of regulations under section 117(1) of the *Accessible Canada Act* (for Accessibility Commissioner).

---

### **2. Scope of Application**

These regulations apply to all complaints filed with the Tribunal under:
- Subsection 65(1) (complaints about lay-offs, revocations, appointments)
- Section 74, subsection 77(1), or section 83 of the PSEA.

---

### **3. Receipt and Timing of Complaints**

#### **Deadline for Filing a Complaint**
A complaint must be received by the Board **no later than 15 days after**:
- The day the complainant receives notice of the relevant employment action (lay-off, revocation, appointment, etc.), **or**
- The date specified in a public notice (if applicable).

#### **When a Complaint is Considered Received**
The date of receipt is determined as follows:
- **Electronic mail, fax, or electronic means**: On the day it is sent.
- **Courier or hand-delivery**: On the day it is received.
- **Mail**: On the **later** of the postmark or postage meter impression (authorized by Canada Post), or **six days after** the postmark/postage date if both appear.

> **Exception**: The Board may establish **alternate methods** of notice delivery for fairness (e.g., for individuals with disabilities or remote access).

#### **Time Extensions**
- The Board may extend or reduce timeframes for actions or submissions.
- The Board must determine whether such extensions are **in the interest of fairness**.
- If extended, the duration must be justified and documented.

#### **Time Period Adjustments**
- Any time period ending on a **Saturday or statutory holiday** is **automatically extended** to the next day that is not a Saturday or holiday.

---

### **4. Filing Requirements for Complaints**

A complaint must be filed in **writing** and include:
1. Name and contact information of the complainant (disclosable to all parties);
2. Name and contact information of authorized representative (if any);
3. Number/identifier of the process involved;
4. Copy of the notice of the employment action (lay-off, revocation, appointment, etc.);
5. Name of the department, branch, or sector involved;
6. Reference to the specific provision of the PSEA under which the complaint is made;
7. Full factual description of the events giving rise to the complaint (to the extent known);
8. Signature of the complainant or representative;
9. Date of the complaint.

> **Note**: The standardized test definition has been **repealed** and is no longer relevant.

---

### **5. Notice and Service Procedures**

Upon receiving a complaint, the Board:
- **Acknowledges receipt** and sends a copy to the **respondent**.
- Within **10 days**, the respondent must provide the Board with:
  - Names and business addresses of all other parties;
  - Electronic mail addresses, if available.

The Board then sends the complaint and supporting documents to **all other parties**.

---

### **6. Mediation Requirements**

The Board **must schedule mediation** unless:
- The complainant notifies the Board, **within 25 days** of the acknowledgment letter, that they **do not wish to participate** in mediation; **or**
- The respondent notifies the Board, **within 25 days** of receiving the complaint, that they **do not wish to participate**.

#### **Request to Refer to Mediation**
- Either party may request mediation **before the hearing**, with the **agreement of the other party**.

#### **Exchange of Information**
- The complainant and respondent must **exchange all relevant information** as soon as feasible, **no later than 25 days** after the Board acknowledges receipt.
- If either party fails to comply, the Board may **order completion** within a specified time.
- If a party refuses to provide information, the other party may request the Board to **order disclosure**.

> **Request for Disclosure Must Include**:
> - Name and contact info of the requester;
> - File number;
> - List of documents/information requested;
> - Detailed explanation of relevance;
> - Date of request.

> **Suspension of Time Periods**: Any deadlines are **suspended** during the review of such a request.

> **Conditions for Disclosure**:
> - The Board may order disclosure only if:
>   - The information is relevant;
>   - Disclosure does **not threaten**:
>     - National security;
>     - Any person’s safety;
>     - The validity or integrity of a standardized test (though this is now **repealed**);
>     - Or distort test results unfairly.
> - Conditions may be imposed to prevent these risks and apply **before and after** the hearing or resolution.

> **Use of Information**: Information obtained under sections 16 and 17 may be used **only for the purpose of the complaint**.

---

### **7. Intervention by Third Parties**

Any person with a **substantial interest** in a proceeding may apply to intervene.

#### **Application Requirements**:
- Written notice;
- Name and contact info (for correspondence);
- Authorized representative details (if any);
- File number;
- Grounds for intervention and interest in the matter;
- Expected contribution to the proceeding;
- Signature;
- Date of application.

#### **Board’s Consideration Factors**:
- Whether the applicant is directly affected;
- Whether their position is already represented;
- Whether public interest or justice would be served;
- Whether their input will assist in decision-making.

> The Board may issue **directions** on the role and procedures of the intervenor.

---

### **8. Notification to CHRC and Accessibility Commissioner**

#### **Canadian Human Rights Commission (CHRC)**
- If a complaint raises an issue under the **Canadian Human Rights Act**, the complainant must:
  - Notify CHRC **in writing** under subsection 65(5) or section 78 of the PSEA.
  - The notice must include:
    - Copy of the complaint;
    - Complainant’s name and contact info;
    - Authorized representative info;
    - Description of the issue and alleged discriminatory practice;
    - Prohibited ground of discrimination;
    - Corrective action sought;
    - Signature and date.

> The complainant must send a copy to:
> - Other parties;
> - The Board;
> - Intervenors (if any).

> CHRC must notify the Board **within 15 days** of receiving the notice whether it intends to make submissions.

> The Board must provide a copy of CHRC’s notice to all parties and intervenors.

#### **Accessibility Commissioner**
- If a complaint involves a contravention of regulations under section 117(1) of the *Accessible Canada Act*, the complainant must:
  - Notify the Accessibility Commissioner under subsection 65(9) or section 78.1.
  - Include:
    - Copy of the complaint;
    - Name and contact info;
    - Authorized representative info;
    - Description of the issue;
    - Corrective action sought;
    - Signature and date.

> Same delivery requirements apply.

> The Commissioner must notify the Board **within 15 days** of receiving the notice.

> The Board must distribute the notice to all parties and intervenors
