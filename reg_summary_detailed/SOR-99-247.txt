### **2. Scope and Applicability**

The regulations apply to a person who:
- Ceases to be employed in the **public service** (specifically, in the RCMP);
- Becomes employed by a **new employer** as a result of an agreement between **Her Majesty in right of Canada** and that new employer.

> **Definition: "New Employer"**  
> A "new employer" is any person or body (such as a government department, agency, or private entity) that, through a formal agreement with the Government of Canada, assumes the duties and responsibilities previously carried out by a portion of the RCMP. - Sections **4 to 9** of the PSSA do not apply to individuals who have exercised an option under subsection **3(2)** (likely referring to a transfer or continuation option in superannuation).

### **5. Key Objectives and Purpose**

The primary purpose of these regulations is to:

1. **Ensure continuity of superannuation rights** for public servants transitioning from the RCMP to a new employer due to organizational restructuring or divestiture.
2. **Protect individuals** during a period of employment change by allowing them to:
   - Request a return of contributions;
   - Exercise a transfer option for pension value;
   - Receive survivor and child benefits;
   - Maintain dependant allowances.
3. **Clarify key definitions and timelines**, especially regarding service periods, age, and benefit eligibility.
4. **Provide transitional fairness** to employees who may have been displaced or reassigned due to government reorganization.
