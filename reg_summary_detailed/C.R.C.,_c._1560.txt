**Detailed Summary of the Trade Unions Regulations**

---

### **Overview**

The *Trade Unions Regulations* are a set of administrative rules that implement and operationalize provisions of the *Trade Unions Act* (Act). These regulations establish the procedures, forms, requirements, and conditions governing the **registration of trade unions**, **alterations to their rules**, **financial reporting**, **inspections**, and **cancellation of registration** in Canada. The regulations provide a structured framework to ensure transparency, accountability, and legal compliance within registered trade unions.

---

## **Key Definitions and Terminology**

1. **"Act"**  
   Refers to the *Trade Unions Act*, which provides the legal foundation for the operation and regulation of trade unions in Canada.

2. **Complete Alteration of Rules**  
   Defined as the **full replacement** of an existing set of registered rules with a new, entirely different set of rules. This is a fundamental change to the union’s governing structure.

3. **Partial Alteration of Rules**  
   Defined as:
   - Adding one or more new rules to the existing registered rules, or  
   - Replacing one or more existing rules with new ones.  
   This is a modification without replacing the entire rulebook.

---

## **Application and Registration Procedures**

### **1. Application for Registration of a Trade Union (Form 1)**

- Must be submitted by **seven members** of the proposed union.
- The application must include:
  - The **name** of the proposed trade union (as stated in Rule No.).
  - A statement confirming that **no other existing union** (registered or not) has a name identical or so similar as to cause confusion.
  - The **place of meeting** and **office address** for communications (as per Rule No.).
  - The **date of establishment** of the union.
  - A full statement of the union’s **objects and purposes** (as per Rule No.).
  - Conditions under which members become entitled to benefits (Rule No.).
  - Rules regarding **fines and forfeitures** (Rule No.).
  - Procedures for **making, altering, amending, or rescinding rules** (Rule No.).
  - Appointments and removals of officers (e.g., treasurer, trustees, committee) (Rule No.).
  - Investment of funds and audit requirements (Rule No.).
  - Provision for inspection of books and member lists by stakeholders (Rule No.).

- **Supporting Documents Attached**:
  - Two copies of the **rules**, each marked “A”.
  - A list of **officers**, marked “B”.
  - A **general statement of receipts, funds, effects, and expenditures**, marked “C”, covering:
    - Assets and liabilities as of a specific date.
    - Receipts and expenditures during the prior year, categorized by purpose.

- **Authorization Requirement**:
  - The seven signatories must confirm they have **duly authorized** the application on behalf of the union.
  - Authorization must be clearly stated (e.g., a resolution passed at a general meeting or another valid method).
  - If the union has been in operation for more than one year, this authorization requirement is mandatory.

- **Fees**:
  - A registration fee of **$4** is required upon receipt of the application.

---

### **2. Applications for Alteration of Rules**

#### **A. Partial Alteration of Rules (Form 6)**

- **Purpose**: To add or replace one or more rules within the existing registered rules.
- **Required Documents**:
  - **Form 6** (application form).
  - A **declaration in Form 5** (statutory declaration by an officer of the union).
  - **Two copies of the new rules**, marked “O”.
  - **One copy of the old rules** to be replaced.

- **Registrar’s Obligations**:
  - The Registrar **must not register** a partial alteration if the resulting rules fail to meet the requirements of the *Trade Unions Act*.
  - Upon approval, the Registrar issues a **Certificate of Registry of Partial Alteration of Rules (Form 8)**.
  - The certificate is delivered upon payment of a **$2 fee** and includes **one copy of the new rule(s)** added or substituted.

#### **B. Complete Alteration of Rules (Form 7)**

- **Purpose**: To replace the entire set of registered rules with a new, comprehensive set.
- **Required Documents**:
  - **Form 7** (application form).
  - A **declaration in Form 5** by an officer of the union.
  - **Two copies of the new set of rules**, each marked “P”.

- **Registrar’s Obligations**:
  - The new rules must comply with all statutory requirements under the Act.
  - Upon approval, the Registrar issues a **Certificate of Registry of Complete Alteration of Rules (Form 9)**.
  - The certificate is delivered upon payment of a **$2 fee** and includes **one copy of the new set of rules**.

> In both cases, the new rules must be **signed by seven members** of the union.

## **Financial and Reporting Requirements**

- **General Statement of Receipts, Funds, Effects, and Expenditures**  
  Required under Section 13 of the Act.  
  - Must be submitted in **Form 2** of the schedule.  
  - Includes:
    - Assets and liabilities as of a specific date.
    - Annual receipts and expenditures, categorized by purpose.

- **Copy of New or Altered Rules**  
  Required under Section 14 of the Act.  
  - Must be submitted in **Form 3** of the schedule.

- **Statement of Officer Changes**  
  Required under Section 14 of the Act.  
  - Must be submitted in **Form 4** of the schedule when there are changes in leadership.

---

## **Inspection and Access to Records**

- Any person may inspect documents related to a registered trade union (e.g., financial records, rulebooks, officer lists).
- Inspection is permitted upon payment of a **fee of $0.25**.

---

## **Cancellation of Registration**

The Registrar may cancel a trade union’s certificate of registry under the following circumstances:

1. **The union ceases to meet the legal definition of a trade union** under Section 2 of the Act.
2. **The registration is void** under Section 6 of the Act (e.g., due to fraud, lack of authorization, or violation of law).

### **Procedure for Cancellation**:
- At least **two months** before cancellation, the Registrar must send a **registered mail notice** to the union at its last known address, outlining the grounds for cancellation.
- The notice must be sent **only if the union is still active or registered**.

### **Withdrawal of Registration**:
- A trade union may request the **withdrawal** of its certificate of registry.
- The Registrar may grant this request **if properly evidenced**, as per the Registrar’s requirements.

---

## **Statutory Declarations and Legal Compliance**

- A **statutory declaration** (Form 5) must be signed by an officer (e.g., clerk, secretary, treasurer) of the union.
- The declaration affirms that:
  - The proposed rule changes were made in accordance with the union’s existing rules.
  - The union’s governance and procedures were followed during the rule-making process.

- This declaration is required for both **partial and complete alterations**.

---

## **Certificate of Registry**

- Upon successful registration:
  - A **Certificate of Registry** is issued by the Registrar General of Canada.
  - The certificate includes:
    - The trade union’s name.
    - The date of registration.
    - The rules (in the case of partial or complete alterations).
    - The trade union’s register number.

- The certificate is signed by the **Registrar General of Canada** and bears the official seal.

## **Key Procedural Notes**

| Element | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| **Number of Signatories** | Must be **seven members** who sign the application. |
| **Rule Signatures** | Rules must be signed by the seven signatories. |
| **Authorization** | Must be clearly documented (e.g., general meeting resolution). |
| **Fees** | - Registration: $4<br> - Partial alteration: $2<br> - Complete alteration: $2 |
| **Inspection Fee** | $0.25 per inspection. |
| **Notice Before Cancellation** | At least **two months** in advance. |

---

## **Purpose and Objectives of the Regulations**

1. **Ensure Legitimacy and Transparency**  
   By requiring detailed applications, financial statements, and rule documentation, the regulations ensure that only legitimate and transparent unions are registered.

2. **Maintain Accountability**  
   The requirement for officer changes, financial reporting, and rule compliance ensures that unions operate within legal and ethical boundaries.

3. **Protect Members and Stakeholders**  
   By allowing public inspection of records and requiring clear rule provisions, the regulations protect members’ rights and interests.

4. **Provide Clarity in Governance**  
   The structured format for rule alterations (partial or complete) ensures consistency and legal predictability in union governance.

---
