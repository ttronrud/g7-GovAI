**Detailed Summary of the Specification of Statutory Provisions (Treasury Board) Order**

---

**Title:**  
*Specification of Statutory Provisions (Treasury Board) Order*

**Purpose:**  
This Order serves to formally specify and authorize the **Treasury Board of Canada** to exercise certain powers that are otherwise granted to the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet) under specific statutes and financial appropriations. By identifying these statutory provisions, the Order enables the Treasury Board to act in a delegated capacity—particularly in matters related to policy, administration, and financial oversight—without requiring direct Cabinet intervention in routine or administrative decisions.

The Order is a **regulatory instrument** that provides legal clarity and operational authority to the Treasury Board, allowing it to fulfill its mandate under various federal laws and appropriation acts.

---

### **Key Provisions and Authorities Authorized**

The Treasury Board is authorized to exercise the powers of the Governor in Council under the following statutory and financial provisions:

---

#### 1. **Canadian Wheat Board Act**
- **Subsection 8(1)** and **Subsection 9(1)**  
  These provisions empower the Governor in Council to:
  - Approve the **establishment, structure, and operations** of the Canadian Wheat Board.
  - Make decisions regarding the **management, governance, and policy direction** of the Board.
  
  *Implication:* The Treasury Board may now act on administrative or operational matters related to the Canadian Wheat Board (e.g., staffing, budgeting, or policy implementation), provided such actions fall within the scope of these subsections.

---

#### 2. **Central Mortgage and Housing Corporation Act (CMHCA)**
- **Subsection 11(3)** in relation to the **establishment and operation of a pension fund** under **Subsection 14(3)**  
  This allows the Governor in Council to:
  - Establish and manage a **pension fund** for employees of the Central Mortgage and Housing Corporation.
  - Oversee the **funding, administration, and financial operations** of that pension fund.

  *Implication:* The Treasury Board may now manage or approve decisions related to the pension fund for CMHC employees, including contributions, investment strategies, and funding allocations, as delegated under this authority.

---

#### 3. **Diplomatic Service (Special) Superannuation Act**
- **Subsection 6(5)** and **Section 7** (in relation to powers under subsection 6(5) and section 7)  
  These provisions relate to the **superannuation (pension) benefits** of members of the diplomatic service.
  - Subsection 6(5): Grants the Governor in Council authority to make rules or regulations regarding the superannuation scheme.
  - Section 7: Allows the Governor in Council to approve the administration and funding of the scheme.

  *Implication:* The Treasury Board can now:
  - Approve administrative rules governing pension benefits for diplomatic staff.
  - Make decisions on funding, contributions, and plan modifications for diplomatic service superannuation.

---

#### 4. **Government Companies Operation Act**
- **Subsection 5(1)** and **Section 7**  
  This Act governs the operations of government-owned corporations.
  - Subsection 5(1): Authorizes the Governor in Council to establish, regulate, and manage government companies.
  - Section 7: Provides for the appointment of officers and management of such entities.

  *Implication:* The Treasury Board may now:
  - Approve the governance structure, appointments, and operational policies of government-owned corporations.
  - Exercise oversight in day-to-day management of such entities, within the bounds of the law.

---

#### 5. **Public Service Employment Act (PSEA)**
- **Section 44**  
  This section empowers the Governor in Council to:
  - Make regulations concerning the **employment of public servants**, including conditions of service, disciplinary procedures, and employment policies.

  *Implication:* The Treasury Board can now issue or approve regulations related to public service employment standards, such as leave policies, performance reviews, or disciplinary frameworks—subject to the authority granted under Section 44.

---

---

### **Legal and Administrative Significance**

- **Delegation of Authority:** This Order enables the Treasury Board to act as a **delegated body** of the Governor in Council, streamlining administrative and financial decision-making.
- **Efficiency and Accountability:** By authorizing the Treasury Board to act under these provisions, the federal government enhances administrative efficiency while maintaining accountability through established legal frameworks.
- **Continuity and Stability:** The inclusion of specific historical finance votes (dating back to the 1940s–1960s) suggests that these provisions are foundational and long-standing, reflecting the enduring nature of the Treasury Board’s role in managing federal operations.

---

### **Scope and Limitations**

- The powers granted are **specific and limited** to the provisions listed in the Schedule.
- The Treasury Board must act within the **bounds of the original legislation** and cannot expand the scope beyond what is legally authorized.
- Decisions made under this Order remain subject to **judicial review** and **public accountability** under Canadian administrative law.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Specification of Statutory Provisions (Treasury Board) Order* is a critical instrument that defines the **legal basis and scope of the Treasury Board’s authority** to act on behalf of the Governor in Council in areas including:
- Public service employment (PSEA),
- Pension and superannuation schemes (for diplomatic and CMHC employees),
- Government company operations,
- The management of the Canadian Wheat Board,
- And specific financial appropriations from historical Appropriation Acts.

By clearly specifying these statutory provisions, the Order ensures that the Treasury Board operates within a transparent, lawful, and accountable framework, enabling effective governance of key federal institutions and programs.
