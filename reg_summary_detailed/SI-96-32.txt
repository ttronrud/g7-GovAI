### 4. **Eligibility and Rights for Cross-Border Residents**
Any person (including nationals of either country) who has been or is subject to the legislation of either country is eligible for benefits from the other country **under the same conditions as nationals of that country**.

#### Key Principles:
- Benefits are **not reduced, suspended, canceled, or confiscated** simply because the person resides in the other country.
- Benefits are **paid in the territory of the other country** (e.g., a Canadian resident working in Mexico receives a CPP pension in Mexico).
- If a person resides in a **third country**, benefits are paid under the same conditions as if they were a national of that third country.

This section details how **creditable periods** are counted and combined across countries to determine eligibility and benefit amounts.

- If a person is covered by the **Canada Pension Plan (CPP)** or a **provincial comprehensive pension plan** during residence in Mexico:

- If a person is covered by Mexican legislation during residence in Canada:
  - That period is **not counted** as residence in Canada **unless** the person is covered by CPP or provincial plan.

#### B. **Creditable Periods in Mexico Counted as Canadian Periods**
- A **calendar year** of creditable periods in Mexico (with at least 13 weeks) counts as a **year of creditable service** under the **Canada Pension Plan (CPP)**.

- For **invalidity or death benefits**, a CPP year counts as **52 weeks** of creditable service in Mexico.
