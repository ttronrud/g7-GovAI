### **Regulation Summary: Designation of the Public Appointments Commission Secretariat as a Department and Appointment of Executive Director as Deputy Head**

**Legal Authority and Source:**  
This regulation is issued under the authority of **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Prime Minister**. It is derived from provisions in the **Public Service Employment Act** (PSEA), specifically:

- **Paragraph (b) of the definition "department"** in **subsection 2(1)** of the Act  
- **Paragraph (b) of the definition "deputy head"** in **subsection 2(1)** of the Act  

The regulation is published in **Statutory Instrument S.C. 2003, c. 22, sections 12 and 13**.

---

### **Key Provisions and Their Meaning**

#### **1. Designation of the Public Appointments Commission Secretariat as a "Department"**
- **What it does:**  
  The Public Appointments Commission Secretariat (PAC Secretariat) is officially designated as a **"department"** within the meaning of the Public Service Employment Act.
  
- **Legal Basis:**  
  This designation is made pursuant to **paragraph (b)** of the definition of "department" in subsection 2(1) of the PSEA.  
  (Note: The original text contains a typo — "Acta" should be "Act". This is corrected in the interpretation.)

- **Implications:**  
  - The PAC Secretariat is now treated as a formal department of the public service under the Act.  
  - This status grants it certain rights, responsibilities, and administrative powers under the PSEA.  
  - It may now be subject to the same employment rules, oversight mechanisms, and reporting requirements that apply to other public service departments.  
  - It may also be eligible for certain funding, staffing authority, and administrative autonomy consistent with departmental status.

#### **2. Designation of the Executive Director as the "Deputy Head"**
- **What it does:**  
  The **Executive Director of the Public Appointments Commission Secretariat** is formally designated as the **"deputy head"** of the Secretariat for the purposes of the Public Service Employment Act.

- **Legal Basis:**  
  This designation is made under **paragraph (b)** of the definition of "deputy head" in subsection 2(1) of the PSEA.

- **Implications:**  
  - The Executive Director now holds the formal title and responsibilities of a deputy head within the public service framework.  
  - This includes authority to assist the head of the department (in this case, the Chair of the Public Appointments Commission) in managing the Secretariat’s operations.  
  - The designation may influence internal governance, staffing decisions, performance evaluations, and accountability structures under the Act.  
  - It affirms the Executive Director’s leadership role and ensures that the Secretariat has a clear chain of command and governance structure aligned with public service standards.
