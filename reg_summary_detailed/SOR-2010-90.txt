### **Detailed Summary of the Motor Vehicle Restraint Systems and Booster Seats Safety Regulations (Canada)**

These regulations, enacted under **subsection 11(1)** of the **Motor Vehicle Safety Act (MVSA)** and based on a public consultation process initiated on **October 10, 2009**, establish comprehensive safety standards for **restraint systems and booster seats** used in Canadian vehicles. The regulations aim to ensure that all such equipment meets rigorous safety, design, labeling, and record-keeping requirements to protect children, infants, and disabled persons during vehicle travel.

---

## **1. Regulatory Background and Process**

- **Legal Authority**: The regulations were adopted by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Transport**, pursuant to **subsection 11(1)** of the *Motor Vehicle Safety Act*.
- **Public Consultation**: A proposed version of the regulations was published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **October 10, 2009**, providing a reasonable opportunity for stakeholders (e.g., manufacturers, safety organizations, consumers) to submit written representations to the Minister.
- This ensures transparency, public participation, and alignment with stakeholder input before final adoption.

## **3. National Safety Mark**

- A **prescribed national safety mark** (as shown in **Schedule 2**) is required on all compliant restraint systems and booster seats.
- **Mandatory Elements of the Mark**:
  - Must be **at least 50 mm in diameter**
  - Must be **indelibly stitched, molded, or printed** on the product or its packaging
  - Must be **fully visible** when the product is installed
- **If the restraint has a removable base**, the mark must be applied to the **seating component** (not the base)
- The mark must include:
  - The **authorization number** assigned by the Minister
  - The applicable **CMVSS number** indicating compliance:

## **6. Temporary Importation (for specific purposes)**

Per **paragraph 7(1)(a)** of the MVSA, temporary importation is permitted for:
- Exhibition
- Demonstration
- Evaluation
- Testing
- Further manufacturing prior to export

> ðŸ” The importer must submit a **declaration** stating:
> - Purpose of import
> - That it will be used **only for that purpose**
> - That it will remain in Canada for **no more than one year** (or as specified by the Minister)
> - That it will be **exported or destroyed** before the end of the period
> - That the representative signing is authorized

## **7. Recordkeeping Obligations**

All companies that:
- Apply the national safety mark, or
- Import restraint systems or booster seats

**Must maintain records** for **at least five years** after:
- Manufacturing, or
- Importation

### Required Records Include:
- Date of manufacture/import
- Model name and number
- Authorized representative details
- Compliance with standards
- Warranty claims, field reports, service reports

> ðŸ“‚ If a third party maintains records, the company must retain the **name and address** of that person.

### Response to Inspector Requests:
- Must provide records **within 30 working days** (or **45 days** if translation is needed)
- Records must be in **either official language**

## **8. Registration System for Purchasers**

To support accountability and safety communication, companies must provide each purchaser with an **information card** (in both official languages) that allows the purchaser to:

- Provide their **name, mailing address, email**, and **purchase date**
- Provide the **model name and number**, **date of manufacture**
- Include a **safety message** emphasizing the importance of providing this information

> ðŸ” This information is **strictly for registration purposes only** and must be kept for **at least five years** after purchase.
