**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: "Certain Fees in Respect of the Issuance and Replacement of Identity and Travel Documents (Crash of Ukraine International Airlines Flight PS752) Remission Order"**

---

### **1. Purpose and Context**

This regulation, issued by the Government of Canada under the authority of the *Financial Administration Act*, establishes a **fee remission** (i.e., waiver of fees) for specific travel and identity document applications related to the **January 8, 2020 crash of Ukraine International Airlines Flight PS752**.

The crash resulted in the tragic loss of 176 lives, including many Canadian citizens and permanent residents. In response to the public interest and humanitarian concerns, the Government of Canada, acting through the Treasury Board and in consultation with the Ministers of Citizenship and Immigration and Foreign Affairs, introduced this remission order to support the families of the victims by removing financial barriers to obtaining or replacing essential travel documents.

---

### **2. Legal Authority**

The Order is made under **subsection 23(2.1)a of the Financial Administration Act**, which allows the Government to waive fees when it is in the public interest to do so. This provision provides the legal basis for the temporary and targeted remission of service fees in exceptional circumstances.

---

### **3. Scope of the Remission**

The remission applies to **fees paid or payable** under the following regulatory provisions:

#### **A. Passport and Other Travel Document Services Fees Regulations**
- **Section 2(1)**: Fees for the issuance or replacement of:
  - A **passport**
  - A **certificate of identity**
  - A **refugee travel document**
  - An **emergency travel document**

#### **B. Consular Services Fees Regulations**
- **Section 4**: Fees for the issuance of a **travel document** (including, where applicable, a refugee or emergency travel document)

#### **C. Citizenship Regulations**
- **Section 31(1)** and **Schedule Item 6**: Fees for an application for a **certificate of citizenship**

#### **D. Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations**
- **Subsection 294.1(1)**: Fees for processing an application for an **electronic travel authorization (eTA)**
- **Subsections 308(1) and (2)** and **Section 315**: Fees for processing applications for:
  - A **permanent resident card**
  - A **permanent resident travel document**

> Note: The remission covers fees associated with the **issuance or replacement** of these documents.

---

### **4. Eligibility Conditions**

To qualify for the fee remission, a person must meet **all of the following conditions**:

1. **Application Timing**  
   The application must have been submitted **between January 10, 2020, and June 30, 2020**, inclusive.

2. **Family Relationship to a Victim**  
   The applicant must be:
   - A **family member** of a person who was a victim of the **January 8, 2020 crash of Ukraine International Airlines Flight PS752**, or  
   - A person acting on behalf of such a family member.

3. **Travel Purpose**  
   The applicant must have declared (in writing) that they:
   - Were required to **travel to Canada or abroad** to:
     - Attend to the **personal affairs of the victim**, or  
     - Provide **support to the family of the victim**

4. **No Prior Remission**  
   The fees in question must **not have already been remitted** under **Section 11 of the Passport and Other Travel Document Services Fees Regulations**.  
   This ensures that the remission is not applied more than once for the same application.

---

### **5. Definition of Key Terms**

- **Refugee Travel Document**: A travel document issued under:
  - The *United Nations Convention Relating to the Status of Refugees* (1951)
  - The *Protocol to that Convention* (1967)

This definition ensures that individuals seeking refugee status or travel under international refugee protections are included in the scope of the remission.

---

### **6. Key Features of the Order**

- **Temporary and Targeted**: The remission is limited to the specified time period (January 10, 2020 – June 30, 2020) and applies only to individuals directly affected by the PS752 tragedy.
- **Humanitarian Focus**: The policy recognizes the emotional and practical burdens placed on families of victims, especially when travel is required for personal or family support.
- **Administrative Efficiency**: The Government streamlines the process by requiring only a declaration of family relationship and travel purpose, reducing bureaucratic hurdles.
- **Exclusivity**: The remission is not available for general travel document applications or for unrelated individuals.

---

### **7. Public Interest Justification**

The Government of Canada determined that it is in the **public interest** to waive these fees because:
- The crash was a tragic and sudden event affecting many Canadian citizens and permanent residents.
- Families faced significant financial and logistical challenges in accessing travel documents to support their loved ones.
- Removing fees ensures that affected individuals can access essential travel documents without financial barriers, supporting their ability to fulfill personal and family obligations.

---

### **8. Implementation and Enforcement**

- The remission is to be applied by the relevant service providers (e.g., Citizenship and Immigration Canada, Passport Canada, or consular offices).
- Service providers must verify the applicant’s status as a family member of a victim and the stated travel purpose.
- Applications must include a **formal declaration** confirming the relationship and travel need.
- The fee remission will be processed automatically upon verification, with no further administrative charge.

---

### **9. Limitations and Exclusions**

- The remission **does not apply** to:
  - Applications submitted outside the specified time frame (before January 10, 2020, or after June 30, 2020)
  - Applications not involving a family member of a PS752 victim
  - Applications where the travel purpose does not relate to personal or family affairs of the victim
  - Fees already remitted under Section 11 of the Passport and Other Travel Document Services Fees Regulations

---

### **10. Conclusion**

This remission order is a **humanitarian and compassionate measure** designed to support the families of victims of the Ukraine International Airlines Flight PS752 disaster. By waiving applicable fees for travel and identity documents during a specific period, the Government of Canada acknowledges the profound impact of the tragedy and ensures that affected individuals can travel to attend to personal matters or provide support to their families without financial hardship.

It reflects Canada’s commitment to both **public accountability** and **human rights**, demonstrating a responsive and empathetic approach to national emergencies and tragedies.
