### **Detailed Summary of the Nunavut Mining Regulations (as published in the Canada Gazette, Part I, June 29, 2013)**

---

#### **1. Introduction and Legal Basis**

The **Nunavut Mining Regulations** were formally adopted by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development** and the **Treasury Board**, under several key provisions of the **Territorial Lands Act (T-7)** and the **Financial Administration Act (F-11)**.

- **Legal Authority**:
  - Section 8, 12, and 23(b) of the *Territorial Lands Act*.
  - Paragraphs 19(1)(a)(c) and 19.1(a)(c) of the Act.
  - Subsection 23(2.1)(d) of the *Financial Administration Act*, which allows the government to make regulations in the public interest.

- **Publication and Public Consultation**:
  - The proposed regulations were published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **June 29, 2013**.
  - A reasonable opportunity was provided for interested parties (including Indigenous communities, industry stakeholders, and the public) to submit representations to the Minister.

This regulatory framework governs all aspects of mineral prospecting, claim recording, mining operations, royalty obligations, environmental protection, and land rights in **Nunavut**, specifically within the **Nunavut Mining District**.

---

#### **2. Scope and Applicability**

- **Applicable Area**:  
  The regulations apply to the **Nunavut Mining District**, defined in **Schedule 2** of the *Northwest Territories Mining District and Nunavut Mining District Order*.

- **Geographic Structure**:  
  The lands are divided into **grid areas, sections, and units** as outlined in **Schedule 3**, which serves as a foundational map-based system for land administration.

> **Note**: Certain definitions (e.g., "boundary post," "corner post," "witness post," "legal post") have been **repealed** by **SOR/2020-209**, s. 1, and are no longer in effect.

#### **3. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Territorial Lands Act* (T-7). |
| **Business Day** | A day that is not a Saturday or a statutory holiday. |
| **Chief** | The Chief of Financial Analysis and Royalties Administration in the Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development. |
| **Contiguous** | Units, claims, or lands that share a common boundary (not just a point). |

| **Depreciable Assets** | Buildings, plant, machinery, and equipment. |
| **Environmental Baseline Studies** | Pre-development environmental assessments covering meteorology, hydrology, soil, ecosystems, wildlife, cultural heritage, etc., to establish a baseline for future environmental monitoring. |
| **Exploration Cost** | Costs incurred to assess mineral presence, location, extent, or economic potential — **excludes** costs to bring a mine into production. |
| **Fiscal Year** | As defined in section 249.1 of the *Income Tax Act*, applicable to the mine operator. |
| **Holder of Surface Rights** | The lessee or registered holder of surface rights on the land. |
| **Licence** | A licence to prospect under section 3 of the Act. |
| **Mine** | An undertaking producing or having produced minerals in Nunavut, including depreciable assets used in mining. |
| **Mineral** | Includes amber and naturally occurring inorganic substances (e.g., frac sand), **excluding** materials regulated under the *Territorial Quarrying Regulations*. |
| **Mining Property** | A recorded or leased claim (or group of contiguous claims) where a mine or part of a mine is located, and owned by the same person or jointly by members of a joint venture. |
| **Mining Reclamation Trust** | A trust established for mine reclamation or environmental management, as required under the *Nunavut Waters and Nunavut Surface Rights Tribunal Act* or under leases, contracts, or permits. |
| **Mining Recorder** | A person designated by the Minister to administer mining rights. |
| **Mining Royalty Valuer** | A person appointed by the Minister to determine the value of minerals or processed minerals produced. |
| **Owner** | Any person with legal or beneficial interest in a claim, mine, or property. |
| **Precious Stone** | Diamond, sapphire, emerald, or ruby. |
| **Prescribed Form** | A form prescribed by the Minister under section 28 of the Act. |
| **Processing** | Includes crushing, grinding, flotation, milling, refining, and (for precious stones) cleaning and sorting. |
| **Processing Assets** | Tailings disposal facilities and depreciable assets used exclusively in processing. |
| **Prospecting Permit** | A permit issued under the previous version of the regulations (before November 1, 2020). |
| **Related Persons** | Includes: related persons (under Income Tax Act, without reference to section 251(5)(b)); associated corporations (section 256); affiliated persons (section 251.1); or owners/operators of the same mine. |
| **Supervising Mining Recorder** | The senior official designated by the Minister to oversee the Mining Recorder. |
| **Undeducted Balance** | For depreciation: original cost minus previously claimed allowances; for development: eligible costs not yet claimed; for reclamation trust: total contributions minus previously claimed allowances. |
| **Unit** | A unit defined in **Schedule 3**. |
| **Work** | Activities performed to assess mineral potential (e.g., drilling, geochemical analysis, mapping, remote sensing, environmental baseline studies). |

> **Relatedness Rule**: If two persons are related, they are also considered related to any person to whom the other is related.

#### **4. Licensing to Prospect**

##### **Eligibility to Apply**:
- An **individual** aged **18 or older**.
- A **corporation** incorporated under:
  - The *Business Corporations Act (Nunavut)* (S.N.W.T. 1996, c. 19), or
  - The *Canada Business Corporations Act*.

##### **Application Requirements**:
Must include:
- Applicant’s full name, address, phone, and email.
- Name, address, phone, and email of each authorized representative.
- For individuals: proof of age (18+).
- For corporations: title of the incorporation act.

##### **Validity and Renewal**:
- A licence is valid from the date of issuance or renewal until **March 31 of the following calendar year**.
- Renewal must be applied for **not earlier than January 1** of the year preceding the expiry date.
- Renewal application must include the same information as the original application.

##### **Changes in Information**:
- Any change in personal or corporate details must be reported to the Mining Recorder within **10 business days**.

##### **Non-Transferability**:
- Licences are **not transferable**.

##### **Permitted Actions by Licensees**:
Only a licensee or an authorized representative may:
- Prospect for claim recording.
- Apply for claim recording, lease renewal, or lease of a recorded claim.
- Acquire a recorded claim, lease, or interest in one.
- Be issued a **Certificate of Work** (under section 47) or a **Certificate of Extension** (under subsection 49.2(1)).

---

#### **5. Prohibitions on Prospecting**

Prospecting is **prohibited** on the following types of land:

1. **Lands covered by a prospecting permit**.
2. **Lands covered by a recorded claim or leased claim**.
3. **Lands mentioned in any of the following provisions**:
   - Section 52(5), (6); 56(1), (2); 62.1(5), (6); 67(2); Section 85; Subsections 93(4) to (6).
4. **Lands withdrawn from disposal** under paragraph 23(a) of the Act.
5. **Lands set apart or appropriated** under paragraphs 23(b) to (e) of the Act.
6. **Lands where minerals have been granted by the Crown**.
7. **Lands subject to a prospecting prohibition** under:
   - A federal land use plan, or
   - A land claims agreement.
8. **Cemeteries**.

> **Exceptions**:
> - The **permittee** is exempt from the prohibition on prospecting on lands covered by a prospecting permit.
> - The **claim holder** or **lessee** is exempt from the prohibition on prospecting on lands covered by a recorded or leased claim.
