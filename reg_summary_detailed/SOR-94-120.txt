### **Detailed Summary of the Old Crow Airport Zoning Regulations**

The **Old Crow Airport Zoning Regulations** are a set of official rules established under the authority of the **Aeronautics Act** of Canada. These regulations define spatial constraints on construction and development activities near Old Crow Airport in the Yukon Territory to ensure aviation safety, particularly in relation to aircraft approach and takeoff paths.

---

## **1. Legal Basis and Adoption Process**

- **Authority**: The regulations were made pursuant to **Section 5.4 of the Aeronautics Act**, which allows the Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of Transport) to adopt zoning regulations for airports.
- **Public Consultation**: A proposed version of the regulations was published in:
  - Two successive issues of *Canada Gazette Part I* on **July 31 and August 7, 1993**,
  - Two successive issues of *The Yukon News* on **September 22 and 24, 1993**.
- This publication provided a **reasonable opportunity for interested parties** (e.g., residents, developers, aviation stakeholders) to submit written representations or objections to the Minister of Transport before final adoption.

### **B. Transitional Surfaces**

## **6. Purpose and Safety Objectives**

The primary purpose of these regulations is to:

- **Ensure aviation safety** by preventing obstacles from interfering with aircraft during takeoff, landing, and approach.
- **Maintain visual and physical clearance** for pilots, especially in remote and often challenging Arctic conditions.
- Provide **clear, measurable, and enforceable boundaries** based on established aeronautical standards.

> These measures are essential because Old Crow Airport serves as a critical transportation hub in a remote region of Canada, where weather conditions and terrain can significantly impact flight operations.
