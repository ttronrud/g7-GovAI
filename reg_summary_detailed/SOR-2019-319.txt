**Detailed Summary of Regulation: General Import Permit No. 83 — Aluminum Products**

---

### **1. Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**
- The **Minister of Foreign Affairs** has issued **General Import Permit No. 83 — Aluminum Products** under **subsection 8(1.1)a of the Export and Import Permits Act** (S.C. 1994, c. 47, s. 108(1); R.S., c. E-19).
- This permit is a formal authorization allowing specific imports into Canada without requiring individual permit applications, provided the goods fall within the scope of the permit.

---

### **2. Scope of the Permit**
- **Any resident of Canada** may import into Canada **any goods described in Item 83 of the Import Control List**.
- The goods covered are **aluminum products**, as defined in the Import Control List. While the full list is not included in this summary, Item 83 typically includes a broad range of aluminum-based materials and manufactured products (e.g., aluminum sheets, bars, extrusions, castings, or other forms of aluminum used in industrial or consumer applications).
- The permit applies to **all imports of such goods**, regardless of the quantity, value, or source, as long as they meet the criteria outlined in the regulation.

---

### **3. Reporting Requirements**
- If the imported goods are subject to reporting requirements under the **Customs Act**, the reporting form must include one of the following statements:
  - **English**: *"Imported under the authority of General Import Permit No. 83 — Aluminum Products"*
  - **French**: *"Importé en vertu de la Licence générale d’importation no 83 — Produits d’aluminum"*
- This ensures transparency and traceability of imports under this permit.

---

### **4. Compliance and Record-Keeping Obligations**

#### **a. Response to Department Requests**
- Importers must respond to **requests from the Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development (DFATD)** within **10 days**.
- The response must include the **documents and records** referenced in **section 4** of the Export and Import Permits Act, for the import period specified in the request.

#### **b. Availability for Inspection**
- Importers must:
  - Be **available at all reasonable times** for inspection by any person authorized by the Minister.
  - Provide **all reasonable assistance** to facilitate inspections of the required documents and records.

#### **c. Country of Origin, Value, and Quantity Determinations**
- Importers must provide **any documents and records necessary** for the Department to determine:
  - The **country of origin** of the aluminum products,
  - The **import value** (in Canadian dollars),
  - The **quantity** (in kilograms) of the imported goods.
- These records must be submitted **within the time period specified by the Department**.

---

### **5. Record Retention Requirements**
- Importers must **retain all relevant documents and records** for a period of **six years** after the year in which the import was made.
- The retained records must include the following information:
  1. **Name and address** of the importer or consignee.
  2. **Proof of Canadian residency**.
  3. **Date of entry** of the goods into Canada.
  4. **Quantity** of goods, expressed in **kilograms**.
  5. **Country from which the goods are imported** (i.e., the country of shipment).
  6. **Country of origin** of the goods (i.e., where the aluminum was actually produced).
  7. **Shipping document** that clearly indicates **freight and other transportation costs separately**.
  8. **Tariff classification** of the goods, as listed in the **List of Tariff Provisions** in the schedule to the **Customs Tariff**.
  9. **Import value in Canadian dollars**.
  10. A **detailed description** of the goods (including specifications, dimensions, type, etc.).

> These records are critical for compliance, audit purposes, and ensuring that the goods are properly classified and valued under Canadian trade and customs laws.

---

### **6. Effective Date**
- The permit **comes into force on September 1, 2019**.
- However, if the permit is **registered after September 1, 2019**, it will take effect on the **date of registration**.

---

### **Key Implications and Purpose**
- This permit simplifies the import process for aluminum products by eliminating the need for individual permit applications, streamlining trade for Canadian businesses.
- It supports **economic efficiency** and **industry competitiveness** in sectors that rely on aluminum (e.g., manufacturing, construction, transportation).
- The strict record-keeping and reporting requirements ensure **transparency**, **accountability**, and **compliance** with Canadian trade regulations, especially regarding:
  - Origin of goods (to prevent fraud or misclassification),
  - Value reporting (to prevent underreporting and tax evasion),
  - Trade monitoring (to support national policy and supply chain oversight).

---

### **Conclusion**
General Import Permit No. 83 — Aluminum Products is a significant regulatory tool that enables Canadian residents to import a wide range of aluminum products with minimal administrative burden, while maintaining robust compliance and transparency through mandatory record-keeping and reporting. It balances ease of trade with accountability, ensuring that aluminum imports are properly tracked, valued, and verified in accordance with Canadian customs and trade laws.

This permit reflects a strategic approach to managing the importation of essential industrial materials while safeguarding national trade interests and regulatory integrity.
