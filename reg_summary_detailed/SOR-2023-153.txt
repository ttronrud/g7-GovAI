### **Detailed Summary of the CHFTA Verification of Origin Regulations**

---

#### **1. Background and Legal Authority**

These regulations are a formal implementation of **Customs Notice 14-023**, issued on **September 24, 2014**, which outlines procedures for verifying the origin of goods eligible for preferential tariff treatment under the **Canada–Honduras Free Trade Agreement (CHFTA)**.

- The regulations come into force on **October 1, 2014**, as specified in the public announcement.
- They were adopted by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness**, pursuant to specific provisions in the **Customs Act**:
  - **Section 42.1a**
  - **Paragraphs 164(1)(i)b and (j)**
  - **Subsection 164(1.1)c**
  - **Paragraph 167.1(b)d**

These provisions empower the Canadian government to establish rules governing the verification of origin of goods under CHFTA, ensuring compliance with the agreement’s requirements for origin certification.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined to ensure clarity and consistency throughout the regulations:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Customs Act* (R.S.C., 1992, c. 28) |
| **Competent Authority** | The **Secretaría de Estado en los Despachos de Industria y Comercio (Secretariat of State of Industry and Commerce)** in Honduras, or any successor entity that Honduras formally notifies Canada of. |
| **Customs Administration** | The Canadian authority responsible for administering customs laws and regulations. |
| **Goods** | Goods for which preferential tariff treatment under CHFTA is claimed. |
| **Material** | A component or ingredient used in the production of another good. |
| **Verification Letter** | A formal request for information about the origin of goods. |
| **Verification Questionnaire** | A structured form requesting information on the origin of goods. |
| **Verification Visit** | An on-site inspection of premises to verify the origin of goods. |

---

#### **3. Methods of Origin Verification**

A verification of origin may be conducted through one or more of the following methods:

1. **Verification Visit**  
   - An on-site inspection of premises.
   - Conducted only if certain conditions are met (see Section 6).

2. **Verification Questionnaire**  
   - Completed by:
     - The **exporter or producer** of the goods, or  
     - A **producer or supplier of a material** used in the goods.
   - Must be returned within **30 days** of receipt.

3. **Written Response to a Verification Letter**  
   - A formal reply to a verification letter sent to the exporter or producer.

> *Note:* These methods are alternatives to a physical visit and may be used in lieu of or in addition to a visit.

---

#### **4. Requirements for Verification Letters and Questionnaires**

Every verification letter or questionnaire must include:

- The **identity of the Canadian customs administration** and the **name and title** of the officer sending it.
- A **clear description** of the goods or materials under review.
- A **minimum 30-day deadline** for response, beginning on the day the letter or questionnaire is received.

---

#### **5. Request for Extension of Time**

A person receiving a verification letter or questionnaire may request a **single extension** within the specified time frame.

- **Extension allowed only if**:
  - The exporter or producer is experiencing **bankruptcy, financial distress, or business reorganization**, resulting in loss of origin records; **or**
  - There is an **unforeseeable event** (e.g., fire, flood, natural disaster) causing partial or complete loss of records.

> **Important**: Extensions are **limited to 30 days** unless one of the above exceptional circumstances applies.

---

#### **6. Verification Visits – Procedures and Conditions**

A verification visit can only occur if:

1. A **written notice** is sent to:
   - The person whose premises are to be visited (the exporter or producer),
   - The **Honduran competent authority** at least **five business days** before sending it to the individual,
   - (If requested by Honduras) to the **Embassy of Honduras in Canada**.

2. The person whose premises are to be visited **consents in writing** to the visit.

##### **Content of the Notice**
The notice must specify:
- Identity of the Canadian customs administration and officer conducting the visit,
- Name and title of the officer,
- Name of the person whose premises are being visited,
- Date and location of the visit,
- Object and scope (including description of goods/materials),
- Legal authority for the visit.

##### **Request for Postponement**
- A person may request a **postponement within 15 days** of receiving the notice.
- The postponement may last up to **60 days** from the date of notice, or a longer period agreed upon by both parties.

> **Honduran Request for Postponement**:  
> If the competent authority in Honduras requests a postponement within 15 days of receiving the notice, the visit is **automatically postponed**.  
> The postponement period is also capped at **60 days**, or can be extended by mutual agreement.

##### **Observers**
- A person may designate **two observers** to be present during the visit.
- Observers may only **observe** and **may not participate** in the verification process.
- The observers must be **identified in advance** to the officer conducting the visit.

---

#### **7. Recordkeeping Obligations (Under Section 42.1(2) of the Act)**

Exporters and producers are required to:

- **Maintain records** in Honduras for **five years** after the date the Certificate of Origin is signed.
- In some cases, Honduras may require a **longer retention period**, as per **Article 5.6 of the CHFTA**.
- Provide **access to these records** to customs officers conducting a verification.
- Respond to verification letters or complete and return questionnaires **within the 30-day period** (or extended period if applicable).

> These obligations ensure that origin claims can be substantiated and audited.

---

#### **8. Written Consent to Verification Visit**

- The exporter or producer must provide **written consent** to the verification visit within **30 days** of receiving the notice.

> This is a mandatory step and failure to provide consent may result in the visit being denied or the process being challenged.

---

#### **9. Handling of Non-Eligible Goods**

If a customs officer determines that goods are **not eligible** for preferential tariff treatment under CHFTA:

- The officer must **attach a written notice** to their statement under **subsection 42.2(1)** of the Act.
- This notice must specify:
  - The **date from which** preferential treatment is to be denied or withdrawn,
  - The **period during which** the exporter or producer may submit **written comments or additional information** regarding the origin of the goods.

> This allows exporters a chance to appeal or clarify their origin claims before any tariff adjustments are applied.

---

#### **10. Delivery and Receipt Confirmation**

All verification letters, questionnaires, and notices (including visit notices) must be sent via a method that provides **confirmation of receipt**.

> This ensures traceability and accountability in the communication process.

---

#### **11. Effective Date**

These regulations are **deemed to have come into force on October 1, 2014**, the date specified in the original public announcement (Customs Notice 14-023).

> This means all provisions apply retroactively from that date, even though the regulations were formally issued in 2021 (S.C. 2021, c. 1, s. 25).

### **Purpose and Significance**

These regulations establish a **transparent, fair, and legally sound process** for verifying the origin of goods traded between Canada and Honduras under the CHFTA. They:

- Prevent fraud or misrepresentation of origin,
- Ensure compliance with trade agreement obligations,
- Provide clear procedural safeguards for both Canadian customs officials and Honduran exporters,
- Balance administrative efficiency with due process and business flexibility.
