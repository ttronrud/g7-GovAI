**Detailed Summary of the National Capital Commission Traffic and Property Regulations**

---

### **Overview**
The *National Capital Commission Traffic and Property Regulations* (commonly referred to as the "Traffic and Property Regulations") establish a comprehensive framework governing the use of land, vehicles, and public behavior on the property of the **National Capital Commission (Commission)**. These regulations apply to all areas under the Commission’s jurisdiction — including roads, driveways, parks, and recreational spaces — and are designed to ensure public safety, maintain order, protect property, and preserve the integrity of the National Capital Region.

These regulations are **not** intended to dedicate any driveway or public access to the general public, nor do they override provincial or municipal laws in areas where they are consistent. Instead, they serve as a **supplemental set of rules** that define how individuals and vehicles may operate on Commission-owned or managed land.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Commercial Vehicle** | A vehicle designed or equipped for goods delivery, including trucks, delivery bodies, buses, taxis, or vehicles with business signage (excluding RCMP, Government of Canada, or government-affiliated organizations). |
| **Commission** | The National Capital Commission, responsible for managing and enforcing these regulations. |
| **Driveway** | A portion of Commission property specifically designed and intended for vehicle use. |
| **Liquor** | Includes beer, wine, spirits, and other alcoholic beverages. |
| **Motor Vehicle** | Any vehicle propelled by non-muscular power, excluding over-snow vehicles. |
| **Operator** | The driver or person in physical control of a vehicle. |
| **Over-Snow Vehicle** | A vehicle operating on snow/ice via tracks or skis (e.g., snowmobiles or snowcats). |
| **Park** | Standing a vehicle (occupied or unoccupied) for more than a moment, not under temporary or uncontrollable circumstances. |
| **Peace Officer** | A police officer under the *Criminal Code*, or any individual authorized by the Commission to enforce these rules. |
| **Vehicle** | Any device used to transport people or goods on highways, excluding stationary rail-based equipment. |
| **Trailer** | Considered a separate vehicle and not part of the towing vehicle. |

> **Note**: The regulations emphasize that no provision constitutes a dedication of driveway access to the public.

---

## **Core Provisions and Key Rules**

---

### **1. Vehicle Operation on Driveways**
- **Licensing and Registration Requirements**:  
  No person may operate a vehicle on a Commission driveway unless they hold all required licenses and permits from the **province and municipality** where the driveway is located, and the vehicle is properly registered and equipped.

- **Compliance with Provincial/Municipal Laws**:  
  All vehicle operations must follow the laws of the province and municipality.  
  → *Exception*: Laws that conflict with or are inconsistent with these regulations are excluded.

- **Speed Limits**:  
  - Vehicles must not exceed the posted speed limit on a driveway.  
  - If no speed limit is posted, the maximum speed is **60 km/h**.  
  - If traffic is controlled by a peace officer, the operator must follow the officer’s instructions.

---

### **2. Parking on Driveways and Commission Property**
- **Parking Rules**:  
  - No parking is allowed on a driveway unless compliant with **provincial and municipal laws**.  
  - Parking is only permitted in areas designated by **traffic signs or devices** erected under Commission authority.  
  - Unauthorized parking is prohibited unless explicitly allowed.

- **Commercial Vehicle Exceptions**:  
  Certain commercial vehicles may operate on driveways without Commission consent under specific conditions:
  - Delivery or collection of goods to/from adjacent properties with **no other access**.
    - Must enter/exit via the **nearest intersecting street**.
    - Must occur between **midnight and noon**.
  - Vehicles operated by the Commission itself.
  - Taxicabs.
  - Light vans or pickup trucks:
    - Used for **personal transportation only**.
    - Gross registered weight ≤ **5,000 lbs**.
    - No advertising except owner’s name.
  - Commercial vehicles with gross weight ≤ **8,000 lbs** operating over the **Portage Bridge** between Hull and Ottawa.

> These exceptions are narrowly defined and require clear compliance with conditions.

---

### **3. Access to Driveways**
- **Entry Points**:  
  Vehicles must enter a driveway only at points where **public access is provided**.  
  - Exceptions:
    - Access via private roads or entranceways:
      - Constructed **on or before September 30, 1960**.
      - Constructed **after September 30, 1960**, with **written consent** from the Commission.

---

### **4. Operation of Specific Vehicles**
- **Commercial Vehicles**:  
  Require **Commission consent** to operate on any Commission property (except for the listed exceptions above).

- **Motor Vehicles**:  
  Not permitted on Commission property (other than driveways) without **Commission authorization**.

- **Over-Snow Vehicles**:  
  May only operate in areas **explicitly designated** by Commission signage.

---

### **5. Construction and Access Infrastructure**
- **No Unauthorized Construction**:  
  No individual may build private roads, gates, or entrances to a driveway without **written consent** from the Commission and adherence to specified conditions.

---

### **6. Bicycle Use**
- Bicycles may only be ridden:
  - On **driveways** or
  - On **designated bicycle paths**.
- Bicycles must not ride **abreast** of other vehicles or bicycles on a driveway.

---

### **7. Racing and Offensive Behavior**
- **Racing**: Prohibited unless authorized by the Commission.
- **Offensive Conduct**: No person may use blasphemous, indecent language or behave in an offensive manner on Commission property.

---

### **8. Waste and Debris**
- No person may:
  - Throw, deposit, or leave **glass, nails, tacks, or metal scraps** on a driveway (as these can damage vehicle tires).
  - Throw **refuse or debris** on Commission property unless in designated areas.

---

### **9. Traffic Signs and Devices**
- All traffic signs and devices on Commission property are presumed to be **erected by the Commission**.
- The Commission (or its officers, such as the Superintendent of Gatineau Park) may order the **installation or removal** of signs.
- Unauthorized removal, alteration, or defacement of signs is prohibited.

---

### **10. Public Entry and Use of Property**
- **General Access**:  
  No person may enter Commission property **except where public access is permitted**.
- **Prohibited Activities**:
  - Hunting, fishing, or taking game/fish **unless in designated areas**.
  - Playing golf, tennis, baseball, football, or soccer **unless in designated zones**.
  - Playing games of chance (e.g., cards, dice, wheels) **on or in Commission property**.
  - Selling goods, drinks, or advertising **without written permission**.
  - Bathing in waters **unless authorized**.
  - Making or leaving unattended fires **unless in designated areas**.
  - Camping, picnicking, or erecting tents **unless specifically allowed**.
  - Removing soil, plants, trees, or structures **without Commission approval**.
  - Cutting, breaking, or defacing buildings, fences, trees, or natural features.

> **Exception**: Activities on **leased recreational property** (e.g., leased to a municipality) are governed by the **municipality’s bylaws**.

---

### **11. Domestic Animals**
- **Keeper Definition**: Owner or responsible adult (e.g., parent of a minor).
- **Animal Access**:  
  Domestic animals may only be on Commission land **in accordance with the bylaws of the municipality** where the land is located.
- **Exceptions**:
  - Commission land governed by the *National Capital Commission Animal Regulations*.
  - Leased recreational property.

---

### **12. Liquor and Fire Use**
- Liquor may only be consumed **in accordance with provincial laws**.
- No fires may be made or left unattended **unless specifically designated**.

---

### **13. Obstruction of Waterways**
- No person may obstruct streams or bodies of water on Commission land **without written approval**.

---

### **14. Enforcement and Penalties**
- **Violation of any provision** results in:
  - A **summary conviction**.
  - A **fine not exceeding $500**, or
  - **Imprisonment for up to six months**, or
  - Both penalties.

- **Peace Officers** may:
  - Move or remove vehicles parked illegally.
  - Issue warnings or enforce compliance.

---

## **Key Exceptions and Exclusions**

| Exception | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Emergency Operations** | Police, fire, or ambulance vehicles may operate in emergency situations (e.g., chasing suspects or responding to emergencies) without violating rules. |
| **Peace Officers** | May enforce rules and issue directions without penalty. |
| **Commission-Operated Vehicles** | Vehicles operated by the Commission are exempt from many restrictions. |
| **Time-Limited Commercial Deliveries** | Between midnight and noon, deliveries to adjacent properties are allowed if they enter via the nearest street. |
| **Designated Zones** | Activities like hunting, fishing, games, or recreation are permitted only in areas explicitly authorized. |
