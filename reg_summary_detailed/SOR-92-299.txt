**Detailed Summary of the Foreign Bank Representative Offices Regulations (S.C. 1991, c. 46)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Foreign Bank Representative Offices Regulations*, introduced by His Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance, were enacted under **sections 509 and 559 of the Bank Act**. These regulations replaced the earlier *Foreign Bank Representative Offices Regulations* made by Order in Council P.C. 1981-966 (Canada Gazette, Part II, p. 1202, April 9, 1981).

The new regulations came into force on **June 1, 1992**, and established a modern, transparent, and accountable framework for the registration and operation of **foreign bank representative offices (RBOs)** in Canada. They were designed to ensure that such offices operate within strict boundaries, do not engage in prohibited financial activities, and are subject to ongoing reporting and oversight by the **Superintendent of Financial Institutions**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note:** These regulations were later **repealed** by **SOR/99-271, section 2**, effective September 29, 1999. This means the regulations are no longer in force in Canada as of that date. However, this summary provides a detailed understanding of their content and purpose during their active period.

---

## **Key Definitions and Interpretation**

- **"Act"** refers to the *Bank Act* (the primary legislation governing banks in Canada).
- A **foreign bank** is a bank incorporated or established outside of Canada.
- A **representative office (RBO)** is a non-banking entity established in Canada to promote the services of a foreign bank or act as a liaison with clients and other offices of the foreign bank or its affiliates (excluding Canadian affiliates).

---

## **1. Application for Registration of a Representative Office**

A foreign bank may apply for registration of a representative office by submitting a **written application** to the Superintendent of Financial Institutions. The application must include the following information:

| Requirement | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Name and address of the foreign bank** | Full name and head office address. |
| **Legal basis of establishment** | Reference to the laws of the country or political subdivision where the foreign bank was established. |
| **Business and operations** | Description of the foreign bank‚Äôs business activities and operations. |
| **Proposed address of the RBO** | Current or intended location of the representative office in Canada. |
| **Names and qualifications of representatives** | List of individuals assigned to the office, including their business experience and duties. |
| **Supervision mechanism** | Description of how the foreign bank will supervise the RBO‚Äôs operations. |
| **Reporting line** | Name and business address of the officer within the foreign bank to whom the chief representative reports directly. |
| **Maximum personnel** | The maximum number of employees to be employed at the RBO during the period before the first required statement is submitted (under subsection 7(2)). |
| **Name of chief representative** | The individual who will lead the RBO. |
| **Audited financial statements** | For the most recent financial year, expressed in **Canadian currency** and the **currency of the country where the head office is located**. (Subject to section 9.) |
| **Certificate from regulatory authority** | Issued by the bank regulator in the foreign country, confirming: <br> - Incorporation/establishment under local laws; <br> - Reference to those laws; <br> - That the bank is in "good standing." |
| **Board resolution** | A certified copy of a resolution authorizing the establishment of the representative office. |

> ‚úÖ **Approval Process**:  
> If the Superintendent approves the application, they issue **written notice of approval**.  
> The registration becomes effective on the **date of that notice**.

---

## **2. Reporting and Information Updates**

The regulations impose **ongoing reporting obligations** to ensure transparency and compliance:

| Event | Reporting Requirement |
|------|------------------------|
| **Changes in information** (e.g., address, personnel, structure) | Must be reported **within 45 days** of the change. |
| **Appointment of a new chief representative** | Must be notified to the Superintendent **within one month** of appointment. |

> üîç These provisions ensure that the Superintendent remains informed of any material changes in the operations or leadership of the RBO.

---

## **3. Prohibited Business Activities**

No representative office may engage in any business activity **other than**:

1. **Promoting the services** of:
   - The foreign bank itself, or
   - An affiliate of the foreign bank **not incorporated in Canada**.

2. **Acting as a liaison** between:
   - Clients of the foreign bank and
   - Other offices of the foreign bank or its **non-Canadian affiliates**.

> ‚ùå **Prohibited activities** include:
> - Offering financial products (e.g., loans, deposits, credit cards)
> - Providing investment advice
> - Engaging in direct banking services
> - Conducting any business activity that could be interpreted as commercial or financial in nature

> ‚úÖ **Exception**:  
> Subsection (2) does **not apply** to employees of a foreign bank where an **order under subsection 524(1) of the Bank Act** has been made. This allows certain flexibility in cases where the bank has been granted special authorization (e.g., for specific operations or under exceptional circumstances).

---

## **4. Employee Restrictions**

No employee of a representative office may:
- Undertake or carry out any business activity **outside the scope** of the RBO‚Äôs permitted functions (as defined in paragraph 1(a) and (b)).

> This ensures that employees are not acting as independent agents or engaging in unauthorized financial activities.

---

## **5. Annual Fees and Reporting Obligations**

### **Annual Fee**
- A foreign bank must pay an **annual fee of $2,500** per registered representative office.
- Payment is due on or before **November 1** of each year.
- The fee supports the administrative and regulatory costs associated with maintaining the registration.

### **Annual Employment Statement**
- Each representative office must submit to the Superintendent a statement by **November 1** each year indicating:
  - The **number of persons employed** at the office.

> This helps monitor staffing levels and assess operational scale.

---

## **6. Submission of Financial Statements**

### **Audited Financial Statements**
- A representative office must submit **audited financial statements** of the foreign bank for each financial year.
- The statements must be in the **same form and manner** as required under paragraph 3(b) of the regulations.
- The statements must be prepared in **Canadian currency** and the **currency of the country where the head office is located**.

> üìù **Exception ‚Äì Unaudited Statements**:
> - If audited financial statements are not available, the foreign bank or its representative office may submit **unaudited financial statements** for the year in question.
> - This applies only when audited statements are not accessible to shareholders.

> üìù **Exception ‚Äì Pre-Application Financial Statements**:
> - If the financial statements for the year immediately preceding the application are not available, the foreign bank may submit statements for the **prior year** (i.e., the year before the one in which the application is filed) for the purpose of satisfying the requirements.

> üîÅ **Duplicate Submissions**:
> - If a document (e.g., financial statements, certificate of incorporation) has already been submitted under the Bank Act, it **does not need to be resubmitted**.

---

## **Regulatory Oversight and Enforcement**

- The **Superintendent of Financial Institutions** is responsible for:
  - Reviewing applications
  - Approving or rejecting registration
  - Receiving and verifying ongoing reports
  - Ensuring compliance with the rules on prohibited activities and reporting

- The regulations serve as a **barrier against foreign banks using representative offices to circumvent Canadian banking laws** or to conduct unauthorized financial activities.

---

## **Purpose and Policy Objectives**

The regulations were designed to achieve several key objectives:

1. **Protect Canadian consumers and financial markets** by preventing foreign banks from engaging in prohibited financial activities through representative offices.
2. **Ensure transparency and accountability** through mandatory registration, reporting, and financial disclosures.
3. **Limit the scope of representative offices** to purely promotional and liaison functions, preventing them from becoming de facto banking operations.
4. **Maintain consistency with international banking standards** and Canada‚Äôs regulatory framework.
5. **Provide clear, enforceable rules** for both foreign banks and their representatives.

---

## **Key Legal and Practical Implications**

| Aspect | Implication |
|-------|-------------|
| **No banking activities** | RBOs cannot offer loans, deposits, or investment services ‚Äî they are strictly non-operational. |
| **No Canadian affiliates** | Affiliates must be incorporated in Canada to be considered in the scope of this regulation. |
| **Strict reporting** | Frequent and detailed reporting ensures continuous monitoring. |
| **Annual fee** | Provides a financial incentive for compliance and administrative oversight. |
| **Repeal in 1999** | The regulations were eventually replaced by broader reforms, reflecting changes in Canada‚Äôs international banking policy and the evolution of financial services regulation. |
