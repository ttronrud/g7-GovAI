### **Detailed Summary of New Brunswick Summary Conviction Appeal Rule 64 (Effective May 1, 1992)**

This regulation, titled **New Brunswick Summary Conviction Appeal Rule 64 with Respect to Summary Conviction Appeals to the Court of Queen’s Bench**, governs the procedural framework for appeals from summary conviction proceedings in New Brunswick. It was enacted under **Section 482 of the Criminal Code (R.S.C. 1985, c. C-46)** and was formally adopted by a majority of the Judges of the Court of Queen’s Bench of New Brunswick on **November 9, 1991**, with its provisions coming into force on **May 1, 1992**. The rule was registered in the *Canada Gazette* on January 27, 1982, as SI/82-14, and was updated in accordance with a schedule attached to the rule.

The rule applies to appeals under two primary statutes:
- **Part XXVII of the Criminal Code** (relating to appeals from summary convictions),
- The **Provincial Offences Procedure Act (S.N.B. 1987, c. P-22.1)**.

---

## **I. Scope and Application**

### **1.1 Applicable Jurisdiction**
- Applies to appeals from:
  - Convictions or sentences imposed in summary conviction courts (Provincial Court),
  - Proceedings under the Provincial Offences Procedure Act.

### **1.2 Interpretation**
- All definitions and interpretations from the **Criminal Code** apply unless otherwise specified.
- Key terms include:
  - **Appeal court**: The Court of Queen’s Bench (CQB).
  - **Clerk**: Clerk of the CQB in the judicial district where the trial occurred.
  - **Judge**: A judge of the Court of Queen’s Bench.
  - **Penal institution**: Includes both penitentiaries (R.S.C. 1985, c. P-5) and correctional institutions (R.S.N.B. 1973, c. C-26).

---

## **II. Commencement of Appeal**

### **2.1 Notice of Appeal**
An appeal must be initiated by filing or mailing a **Notice of Appeal**.

#### **Forms of Notice of Appeal**
- **Form 64A**: For a defendant appealing a conviction or sentence.
- **Form 64B**: For a prosecutor appealing a dismissal of an information or a sentence.

#### **Filing Requirements**
- **Standard filing**: The original and **two copies** must be:
  - Filed with the clerk, **or**
  - Mailed by **prepaid registered mail or courier** to the clerk.
- **For prisoners not represented by counsel**:
  - The original and **three copies** must be served on the **senior officer of the penal institution**.
  - The senior officer must:
    - Endorse the date of service on all copies,
    - Return a copy to the prisoner,
    - Forward the original and two other copies to the clerk.

#### **Clerk’s Duties Upon Receipt**
Upon receiving the Notice of Appeal, the clerk must:
1. Assign a **court file number**.
2. Enface (stamp) the original and copies with:
   - The court file number,
   - The date of issue.
3. Forward a copy to the **summary conviction court** along with a **Request for Record (Form 64C)**.
4. **If the appellant is a defendant**:
   - Retain the original,
   - Forward a copy to the **Attorney General**,
   - Return a copy to the appellant.
5. **If the appellant is the Attorney General, counsel for the AG, or an informant**:
   - Return the original to the appellant,
   - Retain a copy.

> ✅ **Service on the respondent**: The forwarding of a copy of the Notice of Appeal to the Attorney General is deemed **service on the respondent**.

#### **Counsel Retention**
If a defendant without counsel later retains legal representation, the new counsel must:
- Give **immediate written notice** to both the respondent and the clerk of their retention and address for service.

---

## **III. Time to Appeal**

An appeal must be initiated **within 30 days** of:
- The date of conviction,
- The order appealed from,
- Or the sentence appealed against.

This deadline may be extended by order of the **appeal court or a judge**.

---

## **IV. Service of Notice of Appeal**

### **For Non-Defendants (e.g., Prosecutor, Informant)**
- Must serve the Notice of Appeal on the respondent **within 15 days** of issuance (or extended time).
- Must **file the Notice of Appeal and proof of service** with the clerk **within 7 days** of service (or extended time).

> Service may be done via:
> - Rule 18 methods (originating process service), or
> - As directed by a judge.

---

## **V. Transcript of Proceedings**

### **5.1 Preparation and Transmission**
- The summary conviction court must prepare and transmit a transcript upon receiving the **Request for Record (Form 64C)**.
- **Unless ordered otherwise**, when the appeal is against sentence only, the transcript shall include:
  - Evidence given and submissions on the **sentence issue**,
  - The **reasons for sentence** given by the trial judge.

### **5.2 Omission of Transcript Parts**
- Parties may agree in writing, or a judge may order, that **part of the transcript be omitted**.

### **5.3 Trial de novo Application**
- If an application is made under **section 822(4) of the Criminal Code** for a **trial de novo**, the court may **dispense with preparing a transcript**.

### **5.4 Access to Transcript**
- A court stenographer, upon payment of fees under the **Recording of Evidence Act (S.N.B. 2009, c. R-4.5)**, provides a copy of the transcript or part thereof to any party involved in the appeal.

---

## **VI. Exhibits**

### **6.1 Retention by Summary Conviction Court**
- The court retains all exhibits received at trial until:
  - 90 days after the expiry of the time limit for filing an appeal (or any extension).

### **6.2 Forwarding to Appeal Court**
- If exhibits are forwarded to the appeal court under **section 821(1) of the Criminal Code**, the clerk retains custody until:
  - The appeal is abandoned,
  - The clerk receives a notice of appeal to the **Court of Appeal**, or
  - 30 days after the deadline for appeal to the Court of Appeal.

### **6.3 Return of Exhibits**
- When an appeal is abandoned, or after 30 days post-Court of Appeal deadline, the clerk returns the exhibits to the summary conviction court.
- If no appeal is taken within the time limit, or if exhibits are returned from the Court of Appeal, the court returns them to the parties who originally submitted them.

> ⚠️ **Note**: This rule does **not override** any existing laws regarding exhibits, seized property, or forfeiture.

---

## **VII. Setting Down for Hearing**

### **7.1 Conditions for Hearing**
The clerk shall set the appeal down for hearing when:
- Proof of service of the Notice of Appeal is received, and
- The material transmitted by the summary conviction court (including transcript) is received.

> ⚠️ **Exception**: If an application for a **trial de novo** is pending, the appeal is not entered on the Motions Day list.

### **7.2 Hearing Date**
- The date for hearing is fixed in accordance with **Rule 47.08** (standard scheduling rules).

---

## **VIII. Application for Trial de novo**

### **8.1 Grounds and Procedure**
- An application under **section 822(4) of the Criminal Code** for a **trial de novo** must be made by **motion**.
- Notice of motion must be served within **10 days** after:
  - Receiving the transcript of evidence,
  - Receiving a certificate from the summary conviction court or stenographer stating a transcript cannot be provided, or
  - Receiving an order under **Rule 64.06(4)** dispensing with transcript preparation.

### **8.2 Hearing After Motion**
- Once the motion is determined, the appeal is set down for hearing under **Rule 47.08**.

---

## **IX. Written Submissions**

### **9.1 Timing and Content**
- **Appellant** (defendant or prosecutor) must file written submissions **not later than 15 days before the hearing**.
- **Respondent** must file submissions **not later than 7 days before the hearing**.

> Submissions must:
- Include **authorities** relied upon,
- Reference **evidence** relevant to the grounds of appeal.

### **9.2 Exceptions**
- A defendant **not represented by counsel** is **not required** to file written submissions.

---
