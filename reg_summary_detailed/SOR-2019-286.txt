## **Detailed Summary of the *Authorizations Concerning Fish and Fish Habitat Protection Regulations* (S.C. 2019, c. 14, s. 31(6); R.S., c. F-14)**

---

### **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**

These regulations were enacted by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Fisheries and Oceans**, under specific provisions of the **Fisheries Act** (Act), namely:

- Paragraphs **43(1)(i.11)a**, **(i.2)a**, **(i.4)a**, **(i.6)a**, and **(i.8)a**.

The primary purpose of these regulations is to **establish a clear and enforceable framework for issuing authorizations** that allow certain works, undertakings, or activities to proceed **without causing significant harm to fish or their habitat**, while ensuring that such activities are properly assessed, mitigated, and monitored.

---

### **2. Scope and Applicability**

These regulations apply to:

- Any **application for an authorization** under **paragraph 34.4(2)(b)** or **35(2)(b)** of the Fisheries Act.
- The authorization process applies to **proposed works, undertakings, or activities** that may impact fish or fish habitat.

#### **Exemption**
- The requirement to submit a full application with financial guarantees **does not apply** if the applicant is:
  - Her Majesty in right of Canada,
  - Her Majesty in right of a province, or
  - The government of a territory.

---

### **3. Application Process for Authorizations**

#### **3.1 Requirements for a Complete Application**
An applicant must submit the following in writing:

| Component | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Basic Information** | Name, address, and telephone number of the applicant and, if applicable, their representative. |
| **Project Description** | Purpose, project context, associated infrastructure, permanent/temporary structures, and construction methods (including machinery, explosives, materials). |
| **Phases and Schedule** | Detailed description and timeline of the work, including project phases. |
| **Location Details** | Geographic coordinates, small-scale and large-scale site plans, and identification of affected watersheds, water sources, and bodies of water. |
| **Community and Regional Context** | Name of nearest community, county, district, or region, and province. |
| **Consultation Records** | Description and results of consultations with Indigenous communities, the public, and other stakeholders. |
| **Fish and Habitat Assessment** | Type of water body, fish species present, abundance estimates, and how data were collected (methods, sampling techniques). |
| **Likely Impacts** | Species affected, life stages, extent and type of habitat impacted, probability, magnitude, geographic scope, and duration of effects. |
| **Impact Mechanisms** | Explanation of how the proposed activity will lead to fish death or harmful alteration of habitat. |
| **Mitigation Measures** | Detailed plan to avoid or reduce fish deaths or habitat damage, including analysis of expected effectiveness. |
| **Monitoring Plan** | Specific measures to assess whether mitigation is effective. |
| **Contingency Plan** | Actions to take if mitigation fails. |
| **Quantitative Impact Estimates** | Specific numbers for expected fish deaths and habitat damage after mitigation measures are implemented. |
| **Offsetting Plan** | Description of habitat credits to be used to offset impacts; if not fully offset, a detailed plan including: <br> - Location (coordinates), site plans, <br> - Measures and objectives, <br> - Monitoring, contingency plans, <br> - Adverse effects, mitigation strategies, <br> - Timeline, cost estimates, <br> - Access requirements (if needed). |

> *Note: This information is required even if the applicant is a government entity, unless exempted.*

---

### **4. Timeframes and Processing Procedures**

#### **4.1 Initial Application Processing**
- The Minister must send a **confirmation of receipt** to the applicant within **24 hours** of receiving the application.
- Within **60 days** of receipt (beginning on the day of receipt), the Minister must:
  - Notify the applicant in writing whether the application is **complete, incomplete, or inadequate**.
  - If incomplete, specify what additional information or documents are required.

#### **4.2 Receiving Additional Information**
- Upon receipt of any missing documents or information, the Minister must issue a **new confirmation of receipt**.
- The 60-day processing period **restarts** from the date of the new confirmation.

#### **4.3 Final Decision on Authorization**
- Within **90 days** of receiving a notification that the application is complete, the Minister must:
  - Either **issue an authorization** or
  - **Refuse the application** in writing.

> *This 90-day period applies only if no delays occur.*

---

### **5. Extensions and Delays in Processing**

The 60-day and 90-day time limits may be **extended under specific circumstances**:

| Reason for Extension | Description |
|---------------------|-------------|
| **Amendments to the application** | If the applicant proposes changes to their original submission. |
| **Request to suspend processing** | Written request from the applicant to pause processing. |
| **Additional information needed** | Circumstances requiring new data or modifications to submitted documents. |
| **Consultation required** | Need for consultation with Indigenous groups, stakeholders, or other parties. |
| **Legal or regulatory requirements** | An Act of Parliament, regulation, or land claims agreement mandates a decision or condition before authorization can be granted. |

> When a delay occurs, the Minister must:
> - Notify the applicant in writing,
> - Explain the reason for the delay,
> - Specify any required additional information or documents.

---

### **6. Restarting the Processing Timeline**

The time limit restarts when **all of the following conditions are met**:

| Condition | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| (a) | All information supporting proposed amendments has been received. |
| (b) | The applicant has submitted a written request to resume processing. |
| (c) | Required information or documents have been obtained or amended. |
| (d) | Consultation has been completed. |
| (e) | A decision has been made or conditions have been met (as per legal requirements). |

> The Minister must notify the applicant of the **new start date** for the processing timeline.

---

### **7. Authorization Modifications, Suspensions, and Cancellations**

The **holder of an authorization** may request:

#### **7.1 Amendment**
- Must include:
  - File numbers,
  - Description of changes,
  - Updated information/documents (as per Schedule 1 or 2),
  - Detailed analysis of likely effects on fish and habitat.

#### **7.2 Suspension (in whole or part)**
- Must include:
  - File numbers,
  - Duration of suspension,
  - Reason for suspension,
  - Description of likely effects on fish and habitat.

#### **7.3 Cancellation**
- Must include:
  - File numbers,
  - Requested cancellation date,
  - Reason for cancellation,
  - Description of likely effects on fish and habitat.

> All requests must be submitted in writing and include a confirmation of receipt from the Minister.

#### **Processing of Requests**
- Within **60 days** of receipt, the Minister must notify whether the request is **complete, incomplete, or inadequate**.
- If incomplete, specify required information.
- If complete, within **90 days**, the Minister must:
  - Amend, suspend, cancel the authorization, or
  - Refuse the request in writing.

> Same extension rules apply as for applications.

> Time limit restarts when:
> - Amendments are submitted,
> - Processing is resumed,
> - Required data is obtained,
> - Consultation is completed,
> - Legal conditions are met.

### **8. Ministerâ€™s Discretionary Powers**

The Minister may, **on their own initiative**, amend, suspend, or cancel an authorization if:

| Ground for Action | Description |
|------------------|-------------|
| Conditions not met | The conditions established in the authorization have not been or will not be met. |
| Fraud or misrepresentation | Authorization was obtained through fraud, false statements, or misrepresentation. |
| New information shows greater harm | New data indicates fish death or habitat damage will be **significantly greater** than anticipated at issuance. |
| Unpaid fines | The authorization holder has not paid a fine imposed under the Act. |

> **Suspension without notice** is allowed **only if**:
> - There is an **imminent threat** to fish or habitat (i.e., immediate risk of death or destruction).
> - The suspension is necessary to prevent such imminent harm.

> If an authorization is amended, suspended, or cancelled, the Minister must:
> - Notify the holder in writing,
> - State the effective date,
> - In the case of suspension, specify the duration.

---

### **9. Reinstatement of Suspended Authorizations**

- The authorization may be **reinstated**:
  - At the **end of the suspension period**, or
  - **At any time during the suspension** upon written request, **if the reason for suspension has been resolved or corrected**.
- The Minister may require the authorization holder to submit a formal **request for amendment** to reinstate the authorization.
