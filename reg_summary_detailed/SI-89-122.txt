**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Title:**  
Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985 Distribution Direction (No. 2)  
**Statutory Authority:**  
Pursuant to subsection 8(2) of the *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985 Act*  
**Issuing Body:**  
Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Justice  
**Date of Publication:**  
1987 (S.C. 1987, c. 48)  
**Citation:**  
S.C. 1987, c. 48  

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**

This Direction establishes a formal mechanism for the distribution of the *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985* to specific parliamentary committees. Its primary objective is to ensure that members of parliamentary committees responsible for reviewing and scrutinizing statutory instruments have timely and accessible access to the official, up-to-date version of the national statutes. This promotes transparency, accountability, and informed legislative review.

---

### **Key Provisions**

1. **Distribution Requirement:**  
   Four copies of the *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985* must be distributed without charge to the chairperson of any committee established by Parliament for the purpose of reviewing and scrutinizing statutory instruments.

2. **Eligible Committees:**  
   The provision applies to any committee of:
   - The House of Commons,  
   - The Senate, or  
   - Both Houses of Parliament,  
   that has been established specifically to examine or review statutory instruments (i.e., regulations, rules, or orders made under legislative authority).

3. **Timing of Distribution:**  
   The distribution must occur "as soon as practicable after they become available." This ensures that committees receive the statutes promptly, allowing them to conduct timely and effective scrutiny of proposed regulations and other statutory instruments.

4. **No Cost to Recipients:**  
   The copies are to be provided free of charge, emphasizing the public interest in open access to legislative materials and removing financial barriers for parliamentary review.

5. **Scope of the Document:**  
   The document distributed is the *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985*, which is the official compilation of all federal laws in Canada, revised and updated to reflect current legal provisions.

---

### **Legal and Institutional Context**

- The regulation is issued under the authority of subsection 8(2) of the *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985 Act*, which empowers the Governor General in Council (on the advice of the Minister of Justice) to make directions regarding the dissemination of the Revised Statutes.
- The *Revised Statutes* serve as the authoritative source for federal legislation and are essential tools for parliamentary oversight, legal research, and judicial interpretation.
- By providing copies to committee chairpersons, the government supports the legislative process by enabling informed and rigorous scrutiny of how laws are implemented through regulations and other instruments.

---

### **Significance and Implications**

- **Enhances Parliamentary Oversight:** Ensures that committees tasked with reviewing statutory instruments have the foundational legal text necessary to assess the legality, consistency, and appropriateness of proposed regulations.
- **Promotes Transparency and Accountability:** Makes the legislative process more accessible and accountable by ensuring that review committees have direct access to the primary source of federal law.
- **Supports Legislative Quality:** Facilitates better-informed debate and decision-making by providing accurate, authoritative legal materials to committee members.
- **Operational Efficiency:** The provision is straightforward and non-disruptive—only four copies are required, and distribution is automatic upon availability.

---

### **Limitations and Exclusions**

- The regulation does not extend to other bodies such as provincial legislatures, courts, or private individuals.
- It does not apply to the distribution of draft or proposed regulations, only to the final, published *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985*.
- The requirement is limited to chairpersons of committees established for the purpose of statutory instrument review—other committee members or non-constitutional bodies are not covered.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985 Distribution Direction (No. 2)* is a targeted, practical regulation designed to strengthen the legislative review process in Canada. By ensuring timely and free access to the official version of federal statutes, it supports the integrity and effectiveness of parliamentary scrutiny, particularly in the context of regulatory development. It reflects a commitment to transparency, legal accuracy, and democratic accountability within Canada’s legislative framework.
