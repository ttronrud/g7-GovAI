### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

---

#### **Regulatory Instrument**
- **Title**: Order in Council P.C. 1994-23, Section 7(1) and Section 2  
- **Effective Date**: 1994 (as part of *Statutes of Canada, 1994, Chapter 23*)  
- **Legal Authority**:  
  - Subsection 4(1)a of the *Canada Wildlife Act*  
  - Recommendation by the Minister of the Environment  
  - Approval by the Governor in Council (i.e., the Governor General on the advice of the federal Cabinet)

---

#### **Purpose of the Regulation**
This regulation designates and assigns administrative control over specific public lands in Quebec to the **Minister of the Environment** for the purposes of **wildlife research, conservation, and interpretation**. It also amends two existing Orders in Council to reflect these changes.

---

#### **Key Actions Taken**

1. **Assignment of Administration**  
   - The **Minister of the Environment** is formally assigned the responsibility for administering the public lands described in **Part 1 of the annexed schedule**.  
   - This includes managing, protecting, and promoting sustainable use of these lands for wildlife-related activities such as scientific study, habitat preservation, and public education.

2. **Amendments to Existing Orders in Council**  
   - **Amendment to Order in Council P.C. 1980-1480 (SI/80-114)**  
     - This order originally established certain wildlife areas.  
     - The amendment updates **Schedule I** to include the newly designated lands, in accordance with **Section 1 of Part 2 of the annexed schedule**.  
     - This ensures legal consistency and formal recognition of the new lands under the *Canada Wildlife Act*.

   - **Amendment to Order in Council P.C. 1981-1373 (SI/81-90)**  
     - This order governs other designated areas.  
     - The amendment updates the schedule in accordance with **Section 2 of Part 2 of the annexed schedule**, aligning it with the new designations and exclusions.

---

#### **Designated Area: Îles de l’Estuaire National Wildlife Area**

The regulation establishes the **Îles de l’Estuaire National Wildlife Area** in the province of **Quebec**, within the **St. Lawrence River**, and includes the following components:

---

##### **1. Bicquette Island**
- **Location**: Near Du Bic Island, approximately at  
  - Latitude: 48°25′N  
  - Longitude: 68°50′W  
- **Legal Description**:  
  - Designated as **Lot 748** on the Plan and Book of Reference for the Cadastre of the Parish of Sainte-Cécile-du-Bic, in the **Rimouski Registration Division**.  
  - Described in a deed registered in the Registry Office under number **2548**.
- **Exclusion**:  
  - The parcel designated **"PARCELLE I, Partie LOT 748"**, shown on a plan prepared by Roger Boisclair (Quebec Land Surveyor) as **number 1820** of his minutes (dated September 30, 1987), and referenced as **plan DMADC-Q-05537** of the Canadian Coast Guard (Laurentian Region).  
  - This parcel is **excluded** from the wildlife area.

#### **Legal and Administrative Implications**

- **Conservation Focus**: The lands are designated for **wildlife research, conservation, and public interpretation**—meaning they are protected from industrial development, unauthorized land use, or activities that could harm native species or ecosystems.
- **Federal Oversight**: The Minister of the Environment has full administrative authority over these lands, including managing access, permitting, monitoring, and enforcing conservation standards.
- **Exclusions**: Specific parcels or zones are excluded due to their use (e.g., navigation aids, private or governmental infrastructure), ensuring that critical infrastructure remains functional while preserving ecological values.
- **Legal Clarity**: The detailed cadastral descriptions and reference plans ensure that the boundaries are clearly defined and legally enforceable, reducing ambiguity in land management and legal disputes.

---

#### **Significance of the Regulation**

- This regulation strengthens Canada’s **national wildlife area system** by formally incorporating key islands in the St. Lawrence River into protected status.
- It reflects a long-standing commitment to **biodiversity conservation** in ecologically sensitive regions of Quebec.
- It provides a **legal foundation** for scientific research and public engagement with natural heritage in these areas.
- It demonstrates how federal land management is adapted to meet environmental objectives through precise, evidence-based land designations.
