**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

This regulation, issued by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister), constitutes a formal transfer of administrative responsibility within the Canadian federal public service under the authority granted by **paragraph 2(a) of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act**.

**Key Elements of the Regulation:**

1. **Transfer of Responsibility**:  
   The regulation transfers from the **Minister of Industry** to the **Minister of the Environment** the **control and supervision** of a specific office within the public service: the **Office of Infrastructure of Canada**.

2. **Scope of the Transfer**:  
   The transfer applies specifically to the portion of the public service known as the **Office of Infrastructure of Canada**. This office is responsible for certain infrastructure-related functions, likely including policy development, planning, and coordination related to infrastructure projects, which may involve energy, transportation, or environmental considerations.

3. **Effective Date**:  
   The transfer becomes effective on **December 12, 2003**. From this date, the Minister of the Environment assumes full authority over the Office of Infrastructure of Canada, including oversight, management, and decision-making responsibilities.

4. **Presiding Officer**:  
   The **Minister of the Environment** is designated as the **presiding officer** of the Office of Infrastructure of Canada, indicating that they will have ultimate authority and responsibility for its operations and strategic direction.

5. **Legal Authority**:  
   The transfer is authorized under **paragraph 2(a) of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act**, which permits the Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Prime Minister) to reassign responsibilities among federal ministers for public service offices. This provision allows for realigning government functions to better reflect policy priorities, organizational efficiency, or interdepartmental coordination needs.

6. **Purpose and Implications**:  
   The transfer reflects a strategic shift in government priorities, likely aimed at:
   - Strengthening environmental considerations in infrastructure planning.
   - Ensuring that infrastructure development aligns with environmental protection, sustainability, and climate change goals.
   - Improving interdepartmental coordination between infrastructure policy and environmental regulation.

**Conclusion:**  
This regulation represents a significant reorganization within the federal public service, moving the oversight of infrastructure-related functions from the Ministry of Industry to the Ministry of the Environment. It underscores a broader governmental emphasis on integrating environmental sustainability into infrastructure development and reflects a shift in policy governance to ensure that infrastructure projects meet environmental standards and contribute to long-term ecological resilience. The change takes effect on December 12, 2003, and is legally grounded in the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act.
