## ðŸ“ How Ownership Rate is Calculated

The core of the regulations lies in calculating the **Canadian ownership rate** â€” the percentage of formal equity that is owned by Canadian citizens or residents.

### Step-by-Step Process:

1. **Identify All Formal Equity Units**  
   All shares, rights, or instruments representing ownership (excluding certain types) are included.

2. **Exclude Specific Units**  
   The following units are **excluded** from the calculation:
   - Shares held by a **dealer acting as underwriter** during a 60-day period after signing an underwriting agreement.
   - **Bearer units** as of February 9, 1982.
   - Units with a **fixed or determinable yield** not exceeding a "reasonable yield" compared to market yields (to avoid overvaluation).
   - Units that, if included, would result in the entity having a total equity percentage of 100% â€” these are excluded to prevent double-counting.

3. **Determine Ownership Through Unit Holders Lists**  
   Entities maintain a **unit holders list** â€” a record of registered owners. This list is used to determine who owns how many units.

4. **Aggregation of Units in a "Measured Block"**  
   - If a person has **two or more entries** on the unit holders list for the same class of equity:
     - They are treated as **one entry** if:
       - Same address, no distinguishing features (e.g., account numbers).
       - Different addresses but entity knows units are held by the same person.
       - Different account numbers but same address and entity has reason to believe they belong to the same person.
   - Only entries representing **10% or more of the measured block threshold** are counted.

5. **Threshold Application**  
   - For **equity-related applicants/investors**: threshold = **1%**.
   - For **others**: threshold = **5%**.
   - If multiple classes of equity exist, apply the threshold to the class with the **largest number of units**.

6. **Classification of Equity Interests**  
   - **Partnerships/Trusts**: All substantially similar units form one class of equity.
   - **Corporations**: All shares in the same class (or same series) form one class.
   - **Rights to Income/Revenue or Capital**: If they represent 100% of income or capital, they are treated as **100 units** owned in proportion to each interest holder.

7. **Forward Equity Rules**  
   - Units of forward equity are grouped by:
     - Identical attributes (e.g., same terms, price, date of issue).
     - Issued on the same day and at the same price.

8. **Election to Create New Classes**  
   An applicant or investor can **choose** to treat new units issued after a selected date as a **separate class of equity** â€” useful for tracking changes over time.
