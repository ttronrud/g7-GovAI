### **Detailed Summary of the Saskatchewan Uranium Mines and Mills Exclusion Regulations**

---

#### **1. Legal Authority and Purpose**
These regulations were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Labour**, after consultation with the **Canadian Nuclear Safety Commission (CNSC)**. They are grounded in two key pieces of federal legislation:

- **Section 159a of the Canada Labour Code** â€“ which allows for the exclusion of certain employment conditions from the general application of labour laws.
- **Section 8.2b of the Non-Smokersâ€™ Health Act** â€“ which provides a basis for excluding specific provisions related to smoking in workplaces.

The primary purpose of these regulations is to **exclude certain federal labour and health protections from applying to employment in uranium mines and mills in Saskatchewan**, while still ensuring that essential safety and operational standards are maintained through alternative provincial and federal laws.

---

#### **2. Scope of Application**
The regulations apply specifically to:

- **Uranium mines or mills located in the Province of Saskatchewan** that are **subject to the Nuclear Safety and Control Act (NSCA)**.
- Employment on or in connection with such facilities, including:
  - Workers employed by holders of surface leases to mine or process uranium-bearing materials under:
    - *The Provincial Lands Act* (R.S.S. 1978, c. P-31)
    - *The Forest Act* (R.S.S. 1978, c. F-19)
    - *The Forest Resources Management Act* (S.S. 1996, c. F-19.1)
  - Workers employed by operators of uranium mines or mills that mine or process uranium-bearing material for or on behalf of the above-mentioned holders.

> This definition ensures that the exclusion applies to both direct operators and those acting on their behalf.

---

#### **3. Excluded Provisions from Federal Labour and Health Laws**

The following **federal laws and their sections are excluded** from applying to employment in uranium mines and mills in Saskatchewan:

| Law | Excluded Sections/Provisions |
|-----|------------------------------|
| **Canada Labour Code** | Sections 122 to 158 (covering various employment rights, such as working hours, leave, overtime, and compensation) |
| **Non-Smokersâ€™ Health Act** | All provisions **except Section 8.2** (which allows for smoking in designated areas, as long as it does not endanger health or safety) |

> This means that workers in uranium mines and mills are **not entitled** to federal protections related to:
> - Working hours and rest periods
> - Paid leave (e.g., maternity, sick, personal)
> - Overtime pay
> - Wage guarantees
> - Other labour standards under the Canada Labour Code

> However, **Section 8.2** of the Non-Smokersâ€™ Health Act remains in force â€” allowing for designated smoking areas, provided they do not compromise health or safety.

#### **4. Applicable Laws and Regulations (Retained and Modified)**

Despite the exclusions, **certain provincial and federal acts and regulations remain in force** for uranium mine and mill operations. These are explicitly listed and include modifications to specific sections or regulations.

##### **A. Boiler and Pressure Vessel Act (R.S.S. 1978, c. B-5)**
- Applies **except Section 39**.
- Includes the following regulations:
  - *Regulations Respecting The Design, Construction, Installation and Use of Boilers and Pressure Vessels* (SR 262/57)

  - *Regulations Respecting Examinations and Certificates of Engineers and Firemen* (SR 311/77)

> This ensures that safety standards for high-pressure systems remain in place.

##### **B. Electrical Inspection Act (S.S. 1993, c. E-6.3)**

  - *Electrical Inspection Regulations* (Reg 1): **Sections 19 to 21 are excluded**

  - *Canadian Electrical Code (Adoption) Regulations* (Reg 5): Applies
