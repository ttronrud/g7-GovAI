> **"Agreement on Social Security between Canada and the Slovak Republic"**,  
> Proclaimed on **December 20, 2002**, and entering into force on **January 1, 2003**.

This agreement is a bilateral social security arrangement designed to ensure **cooperative treatment of benefits**, **avoid double contributions**, and **allow for creditable periods** (i.e., periods of employment or residence) to be recognized across national borders between Canada and the Slovak Republic.

### 2. **Scope of Application**

#### A. **Covered Legislation**
- **Canada**:
  - Old Age Security Act (OASA) and its regulations
  - Canada Pension Plan (CPP) and its regulations
- **Slovakia**:
  - Old age pensions
  - Invalidity and partial invalidity pensions
  - Pensions for widows, widowers, and orphans

#### B. **Extended Coverage**
- Applies to **amendments, supplements, consolidations, or replacements** of the above laws.
- Applies to **new beneficiaries or new benefits** unless **Slovakia or Canada objects within 3 months** of enactment.

> This ensures flexibility while protecting against unilateral expansions.

---

### 3. **Who Is Covered?**

- Any person who:
  - Is or has been subject to the legislation of **either or both** countries.
  - Derives rights from such individuals (e.g., dependants, spouses).

> **Equal Rights**: Covered persons have **the same rights and obligations** as citizens of the respective country.

---

### 4. **Benefit Payment Rules (Non-Reduction Principle)**

> **Core Principle**: Benefits are **not reduced, suspended, or canceled** solely because the person is in the territory of the other country.

- Benefits are paid **even when the person is in a third country**.
- Benefits are paid **regardless of location** â€” no territorial restrictions.

### 6. **Creditable Periods & Totalization (Eligibility Rules)**

#### A. **Old Age Security Act (Canada) â€“ Residence & Credit**
- If a person is contributing to **Canada Pension Plan (CPP)** or a provincial plan during a stay in Slovakia:
  - That period counts as **residence in Canada** for the individual and their spouse/dependants (who are not covered by Slovak employment/self-employment).
- Conversely, if a person is subject to **Slovak legislation** during a stay in Canada:
  - That period **does not** count as residence in Canada for spouse/dependants (unless they are covered by CPP or provincial plans).

> This supports **credit accumulation** for individuals living abroad.

#### B. **Eligibility Based on Creditable Periods**
- Totalize all creditable periods (from both countries) **without overlap**.
- For **old age benefits**:
  - A calendar year with **at least 90 days** of creditable periods under Slovak law = **one creditable year under CPP**.
- For **Slovak old age benefits**:
  - A CPP creditable year counts as a **Slovak creditable period**.
  - A period under OASA (not part of a CPP period) also counts as a Slovak creditable period.

#### C. **Invalidity / Survivor Benefits**
- A calendar year of CPP contributions counts as a **Slovak creditable period**.

#### D. **Totalization Threshold**
- If total creditable periods are **less than one year**, and no benefit exists under national rules:
  - The country **does not have to pay** under this Agreement.

> This prevents minimal contributions from triggering benefits.

### 7. **Pension Calculations (Amounts)**

#### A. **Old Age Security (OAS) â€“ Outside Canada**
- A person outside Canada **only qualifies** for OAS if their **totalized residence periods** meet or exceed the **minimum required in Canada**.
- Allowances and Guaranteed Income Supplements (GIS) are paid **only to the extent allowed** by OASA.

#### B. **CPP Benefits â€“ Amount Calculation**
- **Earnings-related portion**: Based solely on **CPP pensionable earnings**.
- **Flat-rate portion**: Calculated as:
  $$
  \text{Flat-rate} = (\text{CPP flat-rate}) \times \left(\frac{\text{CPP contributions}}{\text{Minimum qualifying period}}\right)
  $$
  - The fraction **cannot exceed 1** (i.e., maximum flat-rate = full amount).

> Ensures fairness in benefit calculation based on actual contributions.

#### C. **Slovak Benefit Determination**
- If eligible **without totalizing Canadian periods**:
  - Slovak institution calculates benefit **without considering Canadian contributions**.
- If eligible **only via totalization**:
  - First, calculate **theoretical benefit** as if all periods were Slovak.
  - Then, apply a **proportional reduction** based on:
    $$
    \frac{\text{Actual Slovak periods}}{\text{Total periods (Canada + Slovakia)}}

#### D. **Minimum Period Requirement**
- If Slovak creditable periods are **less than 12 months**, **no benefit is paid**.
- **Exception**: If the benefit is payable **based solely on Slovak periods**, the rule does not apply.
- **Canadian periods under 12 months** are **counted** in Slovak benefit calculations.

> Prevents benefits from being granted based on incomplete records.

#### E. **Overlap of Benefits**
- If a person receives **both** a pension from Canada and a benefit from Slovakia:
  - **No reduction** applies due to overlap.

> Avoids double-dipping or benefit erosion.
