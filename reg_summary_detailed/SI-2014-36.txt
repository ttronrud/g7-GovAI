### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Withdrawal from Disposal of Certain Tracts of Territorial Lands in the Northwest Territories (Thaidene Nene (East Arm of Great Slave Lake) National Park Reserve) Order**

---

#### **1. Legal Authority and Issuance**
This regulation is issued under the authority of the **Territorial Lands Act (R.S., c. T-7)**, specifically **paragraph 23(a)**, which allows the Governor in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development) to withdraw certain tracts of territorial land from disposal.

- **Issuing Body**: His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development.
- **Legal Basis**: Paragraph 23(a) of the *Territorial Lands Act*.
- **Regulatory Reference**: R.S., c. T-7 (Territorial Lands Act).

---

#### **2. Purpose of the Order**
The primary objective of this Order is to **protect and establish a national park** in the **Thaidene Nene (East Arm of Great Slave Lake)** area in the **Northwest Territories**.

- The withdrawal of land from disposal ensures that these lands are **preserved for ecological, cultural, and environmental protection** purposes.
- It supports the creation of a **national park reserve**, which will serve as a protected area for biodiversity, Indigenous cultural heritage, and sustainable natural resource management.

---

#### **3. Scope of Withdrawal**
The Order withdraws specific tracts of territorial land — including both **surface and subsurface rights** — from disposal for a period of **two years**, beginning on the date the Order is made.

- **Geographic Location**: The tracts are located in the **vicinity of the East Arm of Great Slave Lake**, Northwest Territories.
- **Description of the Tract**:
  - A detailed, legally defined boundary description is provided in the schedule (attached to the Order).
  - The boundary is composed of a series of straight-line bearings and distances, starting and ending at precise geographic coordinates (latitude and longitude).
  - The tract covers a large, complex area of land and water, forming a significant portion of the proposed national park.

> ✅ *This means no future disposal (e.g., sale, lease, or transfer) of this land will occur during the two-year period unless otherwise authorized by law or regulation.*

---

#### **4. Exclusions from the Withdrawal (Section 2 Exceptions)**
Despite the general withdrawal, **certain activities and interests are explicitly excluded** from being affected by the withdrawal. These exclusions are critical to ensure that existing rights, infrastructure, and mineral exploration activities are not disrupted.

The following activities and interests are **not covered** by the withdrawal:

| Activity / Interest | Explanation |
|--------------------|-----------|
| **Mineral claims under prospecting permits granted before the Order** | Holders may locate or record mineral claims prior to the Order being made. |
| **Recording of mineral claims** | Claims that were located or recorded before the Order takes effect remain valid. |
| **Leases over recorded claims** | A lease granted to a person holding a recorded claim (under mining regulations) is allowed if it enables the holder to exercise rights under the claim. |
| **Significant discovery licences** | If issued to a holder of an exploration licence issued *before* the Order, and covers the same area, the licence is not blocked. |
| **Production licences** | Production licences issued to holders of prior exploration or discovery licences are not blocked, provided they cover areas subject to those earlier licences. |
| **Surface leases** | Surface leases required to allow holders of recorded claims or interests to exercise rights under those claims or interests are permitted. |
| **Renewal of interests** | Existing rights may be renewed under applicable regulations. |
| **Substances or materials under Territorial Quarrying Regulations** | Disposition of quarrying materials is not affected. |
| **Land for transmission lines or hydroelectric infrastructure** | Interests in land needed for transmission lines or ancillary facilities related to hydroelectric projects on the **Taltson River** are exempt. |
| **Land under Acquisition Agreement (May 5, 1988)** | Interests listed in the 1988 Agreement between Her Majesty the Queen and the Government of the Northwest Territories (and related entities) are exempt. |

> ⚠️ *These exclusions ensure that existing mining operations, infrastructure projects, and Indigenous land-use rights are not undermined by the withdrawal.*

---

#### **5. Repeal Clause (Clarification and Limitation)**
The Order includes a **repeal clause** that clarifies that the withdrawal applies **only to the specified tract** and does not extend to other lands or activities.

- The repeal clause confirms that **all the described parcel and land covered by water** in the vicinity of the East Arm of Great Slave Lake are specifically withdrawn from disposal.
- This provides **legal certainty** that the withdrawal is both **targeted and limited** to the designated area.

---

#### **6. Significance and Implications**

| Aspect | Significance |
|-------|-------------|
| **Environmental Protection** | Establishes a protected area to conserve biodiversity, wetlands, and unique ecosystems in one of Canada’s most ecologically sensitive regions. |
| **Cultural and Indigenous Values** | The Thaidene Nene area is of deep cultural significance to Indigenous peoples, particularly the Inuvialuit and other First Nations. The protection supports cultural preservation and traditional land use. |
| **Sustainable Development** | Balances conservation with economic interests by excluding only certain types of development (e.g., mining, infrastructure) that do not conflict with the park’s primary purpose. |
| **Legal Clarity** | Provides a clear, legally binding mechanism for land protection while respecting existing rights and obligations. |
| **Temporary Nature** | The two-year period allows time for final planning, environmental assessments, Indigenous consultation, and formal designation of the area as a national park. |

---

#### **7. Timeline and Implementation**
- The withdrawal begins on the **date the Order is issued**.
- It remains in force for **two years**, after which it may be renewed, amended, or revoked by new legislation or orders.
- During this period, **no disposal** (sale, lease, or transfer) of the specified land is permitted unless authorized by a different legal instrument.

---

#### **8. Conclusion**
This regulation is a **critical step in the establishment of Thaidene Nene National Park Reserve**, one of Canada’s most significant protected areas in the Northwest Territories. By withdrawing a large tract of land from disposal, the Government of Canada ensures that this ecologically and culturally valuable region will be preserved for future generations.

It reflects a **balanced approach** to land use — protecting natural and cultural resources while respecting existing rights and economic interests in the region.

---

### ✅ Key Takeaways:
- **Withdrawal**: Specific land tract in East Arm of Great Slave Lake is withdrawn from disposal for **two years**.
- **Purpose**: To establish **Thaidene Nene National Park Reserve**.
- **Exclusions**: Existing mining claims, hydroelectric infrastructure, and 1988 acquisition agreements are not affected.
- **Legal Basis**: Territorial Lands Act, paragraph 23(a).
- **Significance**: Supports environmental conservation, Indigenous rights, and sustainable land use.

This Order is a foundational legal instrument in Canada’s broader effort to protect and manage its natural heritage.
