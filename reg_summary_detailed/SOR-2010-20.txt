### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation Adapting the Canada Elections Act for the Purposes of a Referendum (2010)**

This regulation, issued by the **Chief Electoral Officer of Canada** on **February 2, 2010**, amends and replaces the previous version of the *Regulations Adapting the Canada Elections Act for the Purposes of a Referendum* made on December 20, 2001 (SOR/2002-25). It provides a comprehensive legal framework to adapt key provisions of the *Canada Elections Act* specifically for the conduct of a **referendum**—a process in which Canadian citizens vote on a question of national importance, such as constitutional amendments or changes to public policy.

The regulation is structured into several key components: **definitions**, **eligibility and voting rights**, **procedural rules**, **powers and responsibilities of the Chief Electoral Officer**, and **specific provisions related to voting, conduct, and oversight**. Below is a detailed breakdown of its core elements.

---

## **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**
- The regulation is enacted **pursuant to subsections 7(3) and 7(4) of the Referendum Act**.
- It serves to **repeal the outdated 2001 regulations** and introduce a modern, clear, and enforceable set of rules tailored to referendums.
- The regulation comes into force on the day it is registered in the *Supplement to the Canada Gazette*.

> **Title**: *Canada Elections Act as Adapted for the Purposes of a Referendum*

---

## **2. Key Definitions (Applicable to Both Acts)**
The regulation introduces or modifies definitions used in both the *Canada Elections Act* and the *Referendum Act*. These definitions clarify terms critical to the functioning of a referendum.

| Term | Definition |

| **Advance Polling Station** | A designated polling station established under section 168(3). |

| **Broadcasting Arbitrator** | The official appointed under section 332(1) of the Canada Elections Act. |
| **Commercial Value** | The lowest charge for a service or property at the time of provision, either by the provider or a comparable commercial provider. If the provider is not in business, and the cost is ≤$200, value is deemed **nil**. |
| **Commissioner** | The Commissioner of Canada Elections appointed under section 509. |
| **Common-law Partner** | A person cohabiting in a conjugal relationship for at least one year. |
| **Election** | Refers to a vote for a Member of Parliament (MP). |
| **Elector** | A Canadian citizen aged 18 or older on polling day. |
| **Judge** | Defined by province; includes senior judges or designated substitutes in case of vacancy or absence. |
| **List of Electors** | A list of all electors in a polling division, including names, addresses, and identifiers. |
| **Member** | A Member of the House of Commons. |
| **Oath** | Includes solemn affirmation or statutory declaration. |
| **Official List of Electors** | Prepared by the returning officer under section 106. |

## **3. Voting Eligibility and Rights**

### **Who Can Vote?**
- **All Canadian citizens aged 18 or older** on polling day are **qualified electors**.
- **Exclusions**:
  - The **Chief Electoral Officer** and **Assistant Chief Electoral Officer** are **not entitled to vote**.
  - Any person **imprisoned in a correctional institution for two years or more** is **not entitled to vote**.

### **Prohibited Actions**
- No person may:
  - Vote or attempt to vote **knowing they are not qualified or not entitled**.
  - **Induce another person** to vote knowing they are not qualified.

### **Right to Vote**
- Every qualified elector has the right to:
  - Have their name included in the **list of electors** for their polling division.
  - Vote at the **polling station** in their polling division.

### **No Second Ballot**
- An elector who has already voted **cannot request a second ballot** at the same referendum.

## **4. Place of Ordinary Residence**
This section establishes how a person's place of residence is determined, which is crucial for determining voting eligibility and location.

### Key Principles:
- The **place of ordinary residence** is where a person:
  - Has always lived, or
  - Has adopted as their dwelling place.
- It is the place to which the person **intends to return** when away.
- A person can have **only one place of ordinary residence**, which is not lost during temporary absences.

### Special Cases:
- If someone sleeps in one place and works elsewhere, their **residence is where they sleep**.
- **Temporary residential quarters** (e.g., hostels) count as residence **only if no other place is considered home**.
- A **shelter, hostel, or social services provider** is considered the residence if the person has **no permanent dwelling**.
- If the rules in section 8 are insufficient, the **referendum officer** determines residence based on all facts of the case.

---

## **5. Special Voting Rights (Part 11)**
Certain individuals outside Canada or with special circumstances may vote under **Part 11** of the regulation:

1. **Canadian Forces electors**
2. **Federal or provincial public service employees posted outside Canada**
3. **Canadian citizens employed by international organizations (e.g., UN) and posted abroad**
4. **Persons absent from Canada for less than 5 consecutive years who intend to return**
5. **Incarcerated electors** (within the meaning of Part 11)
6. **Any other elector in Canada who wishes to vote under this part**

> Note: These provisions allow broader participation for Canadians living or working abroad or temporarily away.

## **6. Powers and Responsibilities of the Chief Electoral Officer**

The Chief Electoral Officer has broad authority to ensure the **fair, transparent, and impartial conduct** of referendums.

### Key Powers:
- **General direction and supervision** over all aspects of the referendum.
- Ensures **fairness and impartiality** of all referendum officers.
- Issues **instructions** to officers on how to administer the Act and Referendum Act.
- Performs duties and powers **necessary for referendum administration**.

### Flexibility in Emergencies:
- During a referendum period or within **30 days afterward**, the Chief Electoral Officer may:
  - **Adapt any provision** of the Act (e.g., extend deadlines).
  - **Extend voting hours** at advance polling stations (with limitations).
  - **Increase the number of referendum officers or polling stations**.

> **Exception**: The Chief Electoral Officer **may not extend voting hours** at advance polling stations **unless under exceptional circumstances**.

### Emergency Extension of Voting Hours:
- If voting is interrupted on polling day due to an emergency, and the Chief Electoral Officer determines that **a substantial number of electors would otherwise be unable to vote**, the hours may be extended.
- However, the extension must meet two conditions:
  - The polling station **must not close later than midnight** on polling day.
  - The total open time on polling day **must not exceed 12 hours**.
