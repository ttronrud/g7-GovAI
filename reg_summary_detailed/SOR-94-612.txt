## **1. Introduction and Legal Authority**

- **Enactment**:  
  These regulations were issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the **recommendation of the President of the Treasury Board**.  
  The authority derives from specific provisions of the **Pension Benefits Division Act**, including:
  - Sections 5, 7(3), 8(1), (4), (6), (7), 13(2), 14, and 16.

- **Effective Date**:  
  The regulations come into force on the **later of**:
  - The day the regulations are made (i.e., published in the Canada Gazette);
  - The day the *Pension Benefits Division Act* comes into force.

- **Regulation Title**:  
  These regulations are known as the **Pension Benefits Division Regulations** and may be cited as such.

## **3. Pensionable Service Accrual During the Period Subject to Division**

Pensionable service is calculated in a nuanced way to reflect actual contributions and service periods. It includes:

1. **Current Service**  
   - All service during the period subject to division, **excluding** leave without pay.

2. **Elective Service**  
   - Service where the member elected to contribute (e.g., voluntary service).  
   - Credit is proportional to the contributions paid during the period subject to division relative to total required contributions.

3. **Leave Without Pay**  
   - Service during leave without pay is credited in proportion to contributions made during the period subject to division versus total required contributions.

4. **Transfers from Other Pension Plans**  
   - Service credited due to transfers (e.g., reciprocal or pension transfer agreements).  
   - Credit is proportional to the period credited under the original plan during the period subject to division.

5. **Election under Clause 6(1)(b)(iii)(M) of the Public Service Superannuation Act**  
   - Service credited due to a transfer election.  
   - Proportional to the period of pensionable service included in the transfer value during the period subject to division, compared to the total period represented by that transfer value.

## **4. Application for Division of Pension Benefits**

An application must contain the following information:

### **For the Member**:
- Full name  
- Most recent known address  
- Date of birth  
- Most recent place of employment in the public service  
- Termination date (if known)  
- Employee ID, regimental number, or pension number (if known)

### **For the Spouse, Former Spouse, or Common-Law Partner**:
- Full name  
- Most recent known address

### **Additional Documents Required**:
- If acting on behalf of an interested party:  
  - Original or certified true copy of authorization document  
- If applicant is a **personal representative of a deceased interested party**:  
  - Certified copy of death certificate  
  - Authorization document  
  - Evidence that the court order or agreement remains in force  
- If the interested party is **incapable of managing affairs**, the applicant may act on their behalf.

> - For unmarried parties: Statutory declaration stating when cohabitation began and ended (or intention to end it).

## **5. Requests for Information**

Under subsection 13(2) of the Act, a request for information must be in writing and include:

### **If There Is a Court Order or Agreement**:
- Certified true copy of the order or agreement  
- Statutory declaration (by spouse or partner) stating:
  - For married parties: Date of marriage and date of cessation of cohabitation (or intention to cease)
  - For common-law partners: Start and end dates of cohabitation (or intention to cease)

### **If There Is No Court Order or Agreement**:
- Statutory declaration by the spouse or partner meeting the above requirements

> ðŸ“Œ The statutory declaration must clearly state:
> - For spouses: Marriage date and cessation of cohabitation (or intention to cease)
> - For common-law partners: Start and end of cohabitation (or intention to cease)

## **6. Information to Be Provided by the Minister**

When responding to a request for information, the Minister must provide the following:

### **General Information**:
- Date the information is prepared  
- Date when the member:
  - First received a pension (non-disability)
  - Became eligible to receive a non-age-reduced pension
  - Would have received a non-age-reduced pension (if not entitled to disability pension)

### **Period Subject to Division**:
- Determined under paragraph 8(2)(a) of the Act  
- If not determinable:  
  - Begins on **day of marriage** (for married couples)  
  - Begins on **day of cohabitation** (for common-law partners)  
  - Ends on:
    - The day parties ceased to cohabit (as per statutory declaration), **or**
    - The last day of the month before the month in which the request is made (if no cessation)
