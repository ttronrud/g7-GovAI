### **Detailed Summary of Regulation: General Export Permit No. 47 – Export of Arms Trade Treaty Items to the United States**

---

#### **1. Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**
- **Issuing Authority**: The Minister of Foreign Affairs.
- **Legal Authority**: This permit is issued under **subsection 7(1.1)a of the Export and Import Permits Act (EIPA)**.
- **Enabling Legislation**: 
  - *Export and Import Permits Act* (S.C. 2004, c. 15, s. 56; R.S., c. E-19)
  - *An Act to amend the Export and Import Permits Act and the Criminal Code (amendments permitting the accession to the Arms Trade Treaty)*, Chapter 26 of the Statutes of Canada, 2018.

This regulation implements Canada’s accession to the **Arms Trade Treaty (ATT)**, which establishes international standards for the export, import, and transfer of conventional arms.

---

#### **2. Effective Date**
- The permit comes into force on the date that **Section 6 of the 2018 Statutes of Canada (Chapter 26)** comes into force.
- If the regulation is registered (published in the Canada Gazette) after that date, it takes effect on the **date of registration**.
- **Note**: As of September 1, 2019, this regulation was in force (SI/2019-41).

---

#### **3. Definitions**
Key terms used in the regulation:
- **Act**: Refers to the *Export and Import Permits Act*.
- **Export Controls Operations Division (ECOD)**: The division within the Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development responsible for administering export controls.
- **Guide**: Has the same meaning as defined in **Section 1 of the Export Control List (ECL)** — a classification tool that categorizes goods subject to export controls.

---

#### **4. Scope of the Permit**
The permit authorizes **residents of Canada** to export certain goods to the **United States**, provided the goods fall within specific categories in the **Export Control List (ECL)**.

##### **Authorized Exports (Permitted Goods)**
Residents may export any of the following:
- Any good listed in **Item 2-1 of the Guide**.
- Any good listed in **Item 2-3 of the Guide**.
- Any good listed in **Group 9 of the Schedule to the Export Control List**.

> ⚠️ **Note**: These categories are defined in the ECL and relate to conventional arms and related items (e.g., firearms, ammunition, and certain military equipment), but only those that are **not prohibited** under Canadian law.

---

#### **5. What the Permit Does NOT Authorize**
The permit explicitly **excludes** the following exports:
- Exports to **any country other than the United States**.
- Exports of:
  - **Prohibited ammunition**
  - **Prohibited devices**
  - **Prohibited firearms**
  - **Prohibited weapons**
  > As defined in **subsection 84(1) of the Criminal Code** (e.g., weapons that are illegal under Canadian law due to design, use, or purpose).
- Exports of goods listed in:
  - **Item 2-2.a. of the Guide**
  - **Item 2-4.a. of the Guide**

> These items typically involve weapons or components that are considered high-risk or subject to stricter international controls.

---

#### **6. Requirements for Exporting Firearms**
If a resident exports a **firearm** under this permit, they must:
- Hold a **valid firearms licence** issued by the **Chief Firearms Officer of the province** where they reside.
- If the firearm is classified as a **restricted firearm**, they must also hold a **registration certificate** under the *Firearms Act*.

> This ensures that only legally licensed individuals can export firearms, aligning with domestic firearm control laws.

---

#### **7. Reporting and Record-Keeping Obligations**

##### **A. Mandatory Information Submission (Before Export)**
Every resident must provide the following **personal and contact details** to the Export Controls Operations Division **before exporting** under this permit in any calendar year:
- Full name
- Address
- Telephone number
- Email address
- Facsimile number (if applicable)

##### **B. Periodic Reporting (Every Six Months)**
Residents must submit a **report within 30 days** of each six-month period ending on:
- **June 30**
- **December 31**

Each report must include:
- Whether any goods were exported during that period.
- If so:
  - Name and address of each **consignee** (recipient)
  - **Description** of the good
  - Reference to the **provision in the ECL schedule** (e.g., Group 9, Item X)
  - **Item number** assigned in the Guide
  - **Quantity** and **value in Canadian dollars**

##### **C. Response to Requests**
Residents must provide any requested information within **15 days** upon receiving a written request from ECOD regarding exports during a specified period.

##### **D. Exception to Reporting**
- A resident who provides the required information by the end of the **six-month period ending December 31** is **not required** to submit a report for the period ending **June 30** in the same calendar year.
- This provides flexibility for individuals who complete their reporting in one cycle.

##### **E. Return of Goods**
- If a good is exported **with the intent to return it to Canada** and is **returned within two years** of export:
  - No reporting or record-keeping obligations apply.
- If the good is **not returned within two years**, the resident must submit a written report to ECOD **within 30 days of the two-year anniversary** of the export date.

> This ensures accountability for goods that remain outside Canada for extended periods.

---

#### **8. Record Retention Requirements**
Residents must retain the following records for **six years** after the year in which the good is exported:
- Date of export
- Name and address of each consignee
- Quantity and value (in Canadian dollars) of the good
- Description of the good, including:
  - Reference to the **ECL schedule provision**
  - **Item number** from the Guide

> These records are essential for audits, compliance checks, and potential investigations.

---

#### **9. Key Objectives and Implications**
This permit:
- Enables Canada to comply with its obligations under the **Arms Trade Treaty (ATT)**, which promotes transparency, accountability, and responsible arms transfers.
- Facilitates **export of specific conventional arms** to the United States — a major trading partner — while maintaining strict controls over prohibited or high-risk items.
- Balances **economic and trade interests** with **national security and legal compliance**.
- Establishes a **clear compliance framework** with reporting, licensing, and record-keeping requirements.

---

| Feature | Details |

---

#### **Conclusion**
General Export Permit No. 47 is a **targeted, legally binding instrument** that allows Canadian residents to export specific conventional arms-related items to the United States in compliance with international obligations under the Arms Trade Treaty. It includes robust **licensing, reporting, and record-keeping requirements** to ensure accountability, transparency, and alignment with Canadian criminal and export control laws. It reflects a balanced approach between facilitating legitimate trade and preventing the proliferation of prohibited or dangerous weapons.

This regulation is a key component of Canada’s international arms control policy and demonstrates its commitment to responsible, lawful, and transparent arms trade practices.
