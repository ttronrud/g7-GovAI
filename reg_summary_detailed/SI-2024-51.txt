**Detailed Summary of the National Security and Intelligence Review Agency Act Deputy Heads of the Federal Public Administration Order**

---

**1. Legal Authority and Basis**  
This Order is issued under the authority of the **National Security and Intelligence Review Agency Act (NSIRA Act)**. Specifically, it is made:

- By **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Prime Minister**;
- Under **paragraph (e) of the definition of "deputy head"** in **Section 2** of the NSIRA Act;
- And under **paragraph 55(b)** of the same Act.

These provisions empower the federal government to designate individuals as deputy heads of specific portions of the federal public administration for the purposes of oversight, accountability, and reporting under the NSIRA Act.

---

**2. Purpose of the Order**  
The primary purpose of this Order is to **designate specific individuals as deputy heads** of designated portions of the **federal public administration**. These designations are critical for enabling the **National Security and Intelligence Review Agency (NSIRA)** to carry out its statutory duties, including reviewing the activities of national security and intelligence agencies and ensuring accountability to Parliament and the public.

By formally appointing individuals as deputy heads, the Order establishes a clear chain of responsibility and ensures that NSIRA has the necessary governance structure to monitor and evaluate the operations of key federal institutions involved in national security and intelligence.

---

**3. Key Provisions of the Order**  

- **Designation of Deputy Heads**:  
  The person listed in **Column 2 of the Schedule** to this Order is designated as the **deputy head** of the corresponding portion of the federal public administration specified in **Column 1**.  
  This designation is made **for the purposes of the NSIRA Act**, meaning it governs the agency’s oversight functions, reporting requirements, and accountability mechanisms.

- **Scope of Designation**:  
  Each designation applies to a specific **portion of the federal public administration**, which may include agencies, departments, or specific functions (e.g., the Canadian Security Intelligence Service, the Communications Security Establishment, or the Department of National Defence).  
  The designation ensures that NSIRA can effectively assess the activities, policies, and practices of these entities in a structured and accountable manner.

- **Repeal of Previous Order**:  
  The **Canadian Security Intelligence Service Act Deputy Heads of the Public Service of Canada Order (SI/93-81)** is **repealed**.  
  This action removes outdated or superseded designations and ensures that current deputy head appointments are aligned with the updated framework under the NSIRA Act.

---

**4. Effective Date**  
- The Order comes into force on **October 15, 2024**.  
- However, **if the Order is made after October 15, 2024**, it will come into force on the **day it is issued**.

This provision provides flexibility in case the Order is issued later than the original date, ensuring timely implementation without requiring retroactive effect.

---

**5. Significance and Implications**  

- **Enhanced Oversight**: The designation of deputy heads strengthens NSIRA’s ability to conduct independent reviews of national security and intelligence activities, ensuring they are conducted within legal, ethical, and democratic boundaries.

- **Accountability and Transparency**: By clearly assigning responsibility to specific individuals, the Order improves transparency and accountability in the federal public administration.

- **Modernization of Governance Structure**: The repeal of the 1993 Order reflects a shift in how national security oversight is structured in Canada, aligning current practices with modern governance and legislative frameworks.

- **Inter-Agency Coordination**: The Order supports coordination between NSIRA and key federal departments and agencies, particularly those involved in intelligence, security, and public safety.

---

**Conclusion**  
This Order is a critical legislative instrument that establishes the current structure of deputy head appointments within the federal public administration for the purposes of national security and intelligence oversight. It replaces outdated provisions, ensures alignment with the NSIRA Act, and provides a clear, legally binding framework for accountability and transparency in Canada’s security institutions. Its effective date of October 15, 2024, marks a significant step in the evolution of Canada’s national security review regime.
