The regulation was introduced to standardize and improve the **delivery and collection of mail** in both urban and rural areas, particularly in commercial, apartment, and office buildings. It replaces the previous *Mail Receptacles Regulations, C.R.C., c. 1282*, which had been in effect prior to 1983.

This new set of regulations establishes **clear requirements for mail receiving and dispatching facilities**, ensures **safe and accessible mail handling**, and provides **procedural guidelines** for mail delivery, access, and service authorization. It reflects a shift toward **greater accountability, efficiency, and consistency** in postal operations across Canada.

The regulation was developed after a public consultation period, during which interested parties were invited to submit representations to the Minister. ### 1. **Authority and Installation of Receptacles**
- The **Canada Post Corporation (Corporation)** has the authority to:
  - Install, erect, relocate, or cause to be installed or relocated **any receptacle or device** for mail collection, delivery, or storage in public places (including roadways).
- **No person may relocate or remove** any such receptacle without **prior written authorization** from the Corporation.

### 2. **Collection of Mail in Commercial Buildings**

#### Eligibility Criteria:
A commercial building may be authorized for regular mail collection **only if**:
- It has a **sufficient volume of mail** to justify regular collection.
- The **mail dispatching facility and letter chute** (if present) are:
  - Constructed and installed **at no cost to the Corporation**.
  - Built in accordance with **specifications in Schedule I**.

#### Application Process:
The **owner** must submit a written application to the **local postmaster**, including:
- Copies of **building plans** showing the proposed letter chute (with accessibility and distances to the dispatching facility).
- A **description** of the building (use, number of floors, tenants, business types, etc.).
- A **map** showing parking spaces for mail vehicles and distances from the dispatching facility.
- The **proposed effective date of occupancy** (if applicable).

> ðŸ” Purpose: To assess mail volume and ensure economic feasibility of service.

#### Service Management:
The Corporation may:
- **Alter, suspend, or terminate** the collection service if:
  - The building ceases to be a commercial building.
  - The service can no longer be provided economically.
  - The owner fails to comply with the regulations.

### 3. **Mail Delivery Conditions**

Mail delivery is subject to strict conditions based on the type of building or address.

#### (a) General Delivery Rules:
- Delivery is **only permitted** to buildings with:
  - **Compliant mail receiving facilities** (as per Schedule II), or
  - **Agreements with the local postmaster** for delivery to an addressee or recognized representative.

#### (b) Apartment Buildings:
Delivery is allowed **only if**:
- The building has a **central office** adjacent to the main entrance, or
- It has a **mail box assembly** installed and constructed according to **Schedule III** specifications.

> ðŸ” Emphasis on accessibility and shared facilities.

#### (c) Parcel Compartment Units:
Delivery to parcel compartments is allowed **only if** the conditions in **Schedule IV** are met (e.g., security, accessibility, lockability).

#### (d) Business Premises Without Facilities:
Delivery is allowed **only if**:
- The post office representative can **enter the premises**.
- Mail can be delivered **personally at or near the entrance** to the addressee or representative.

#### (e) Distance Restrictions:
- Delivery is **only allowed** to residences or businesses **within 35 meters** of a municipal thoroughfare or sidewalk.
- **Exception**: If delivery was previously made before July 1, 1979, or if the addressee installs a **mail box adjacent to the thoroughfare**.

> ðŸš« No delivery beyond 35 meters unless specific exceptions apply.

#### (f) Access Requirements:
- A **suitable, safe path** must connect the building to the road/sidewalk and remain **clear and safe year-round**.
- The building must have a **building number** that matches municipal plans.

> - A **Corporation-approved lock** is installed, or
> - A **satisfactory alternative access arrangement** is made with the local postmaster.

### 4. **Notice and Compliance Requirements**

#### Pre-Service Notice:
Before initiating or extending mail delivery in an area, the Corporation must send a **notice to householders and building owners** stating:
> *"If mail receiving facilities are not provided in accordance with these regulations, mail will not be delivered and will be left with the local postmaster to be collected by the addressee."*

#### Non-Compliance Notice:
If a household or building owner fails to provide a compliant mail facility or if the facility becomes **unsuitable or unsafe**, the Corporation must issue a notice stating:
> *"If provision is not made, mail will not be delivered and will be left with the local postmaster."*

#### Consequence of Non-Compliance:
- If the owner **fails to remedy the issue within the specified period**, mail will **not be delivered** to that address.
- Delivery may be **continued temporarily** until the issue is resolved.
