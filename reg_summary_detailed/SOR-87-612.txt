### **Overview**

These regulations were established under **Part IV of the Canada Labour Code**, specifically in response to **sections 82*, 83*, 106(1)*, and 106(3)(b)***, and were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Ministers of Labour, Energy, Mines and Resources, and Indigenous Affairs and Northern Development**.

The primary purpose of these regulations is to **establish occupational safety and health standards** for employees working in **oil and gas exploration, drilling, production, conservation, processing, or transportation** on **Canada lands**, as defined in the *Canada Oil and Gas Act*.

## **Key Provisions and Scope**

### 1. **Applicability**
- Applies to **employees engaged in oil or gas activities** on **Canada lands**, including:
  - Exploration and drilling
  - Production, conservation, processing, or transportation (excluding interprovincial pipeline transportation)
- Applies to **non-employees** who perform work to gain knowledge or experience â€” treated as if they were employees.
- **Exclusions**:
  - Employees working on **ships or aircraft** are not covered.
  - Activities involving **interprovincial pipeline transportation** are governed by the **Canada Occupational Safety and Health Regulations** (as amended).

### 3. **Core Safety and Health Requirements**

#### âœ… **Fall Protection and Structural Safety**
- **Guardrails required** around work areas where there is a drop of more than **1 meter** (except on helicopter decks).
- **Guardrails must**:
  - Be between **900â€“1100 mm** high at the top.
  - Have an intermediate rail midway down.
  - Have posts no more than **3 meters apart**.
  - Withstand a **static load of 890 N** (approximately 90 kg) in any direction.
- **Where guardrails are not feasible**, **cables or chains** must be installed to prevent falls.
- **Toe boards or solid panels** required to prevent tools or objects from falling from raised platforms:
  - If tools are piled high, a **solid or mesh panel** (â‰¥450 mm high) is required.
  - If not, tools must be **tied down** or **placed in nets** to protect employees below.

#### âœ… **Ladders, Stairways, and Ramps**
- **Fixed ladders** required when moving between levels more than **450 mm** apart.
- Ladders must be:
  - **Vertical**
  - Securely fastened at top, bottom, and intermediate points
  - Have **rungs every 300 mm**, at least **150 mm from wall**
  - Have **side rails extending â‰¥900 mm above landing/platform**
- **Ladders over 6 m** must have a **protective cage** above 2 m.
- **Ladders over 9 m** must have **landings every 6 m**, each at least **0.36 mÂ²**, with guardrails.
- **Stairways and walkways** must be:
  - Free of ice, snow, and debris
  - Clearly marked and maintained for safe use

#### âœ… **Open Enclosures and Floor Openings**
- Open tops (bins, hoppers, pits) must have:
  - A **fixed ladder inside** the enclosure
  - A **grating, screen, or walkway** (â‰¥500 mm wide) with guardrails
- The covering/walkway must support:
  - The **maximum likely load**, or
  - A **live load of 6 kPa** (whichever is greater)

#### âœ… **Wall and Floor Openings**
- Any **wall opening â‰¥750 mm high and 300 mm wide** or **floor opening â‰¥300 mm** in smallest dimension:
  - Must have **guardrails** or be **covered** with material capable of supporting all possible loads.
- **Exemption**: Loading/unloading areas of docks (rail, truck, marine) are excluded.

#### âœ… **Loading and Unloading Areas**
- Docks and ramps must:
  - Be **strong enough** to support maximum expected loads
  - Be **free of surface irregularities** affecting mobile equipment
  - Have **side rails, curbs, or rolled edges** on unused sides to prevent equipment from falling
- Portable ramps and dock plates must:
  - Be **clearly marked** with maximum safe load
  - Be **securely installed** and not slide or move under load

#### âœ… **Environmental and Workplace Conditions**
- Floors in wet areas must:
  - Be covered with **dry false floors** or treated with **non-slip material**
  - Be kept **free of oil, grease, or slippery substances**
- All **dust, dirt, waste, and scrap** must be removed regularly to maintain safety.
- All **travelled surfaces** must be free of:
  - Splinters
  - Holes
  - Loose boards or tiles

#### âœ… **Heating Devices in Enclosed Spaces**
- Portable open-flame heaters (e.g., salamanders) must:
  - Not block emergency exits
  - Be located, protected, and used so as **not to ignite nearby combustibles**
  - Be used only when **adequate ventilation** is provided
  - Be protected from damage or overturning
- If incomplete combustion occurs, the heater must be equipped with **a flame arrestor or ventilation system**

### 4. **First Aid and Medical Response Requirements**

- **First Aid Attendants** must have one of the following:
  - Emergency first aid certificate (â‰¥1 day)
  - Standard first aid certificate (â‰¥2 days)
  - Advanced first aid certificate (â‰¥5 days, not marinersâ€™ course)
  - Marinersâ€™ first aid certificate (â‰¥5 days)

- **Medics** (highly trained medical personnel) must have:
  - Experience in **helicopter or fixed-wing aircraft evacuation**
  - Certification in **Advanced or Basic Cardiac Life Support (ACLS/BCLS)** recognized by Canadian Heart Foundation
  - A **registered nurse certificate** with ICU/emergency experience, or
  - A **paramedic certificate** with clinical experience, or
  - An **Occupational Qualification VIB Medical Assistant** (military) certificate

- **First Aid Rooms** must be:
  - Used exclusively for first aid or medical care
  - Readily accessible to employees

- **Records** related to safety and health must be:
  - Kept **readily available** at all times
  - Accessible to the **Head of Compliance and Enforcement** and/or **safety and health committee/representative**
