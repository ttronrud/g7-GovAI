## âœ… General Principles and Limitations

- **No Limitation on Accommodation Duties**:  
  These regulations **do not limit** the duty to accommodate under the *Canadian Human Rights Act* or other federal laws.  
  â†’ The goal is to **complement**, not replace, existing human rights obligations.

- **No Requirement to Endanger Safety or Health**:  
  A transportation provider **cannot be required** to do anything that would compromise **security, public health, or public safety**.

- **Dignity Principle**:  
  All persons with disabilities must be treated with **dignity, respect, and autonomy**.

## ðŸ—£ï¸ Communication with Passengers

Personnel must communicate with persons with disabilities with **sensitivity and awareness** of their needs:

- Consider the **nature of the disability** (especially visual, hearing, or communication impairments).
- Note if the person uses an **assistive device** (e.g., hearing aid, screen reader).
- Use **effective communication methods**, such as:
  - Sign language
  - Augmentative or alternative communication (AAC) systems
  - Plain, clear, concise language

> This ensures **inclusivity and respect for autonomy**.

  - Use **email** or

  - Telephone
  - Email
  - Third-party relay services
- Must **publish**:
  - Website address
  - Telephone number
  - Email address
  - Third-party TRS number

> This ensures **equal access** to booking, itinerary checks, and customer support.
