**Detailed Summary of the Stamping and Marking of Tobacco Products Regulations (S.C. 2002, c. 22)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Stamping and Marking of Tobacco Products Regulations*, made under **paragraphs 304(1)(f) and (o) of the Excise Act, 2001**, establish comprehensive requirements for the **labeling, stamping, packaging, and marking** of tobacco and vaping products in Canada. These regulations ensure transparency, traceability, and compliance with excise laws by mandating specific information be displayed on packages, cartons, cases, and containers — both domestically produced and imported — and by regulating how excise stamps are applied and accounted for.

The regulations apply to **tobacco products**, **cannabis products**, and **vaping products**, with distinct provisions tailored to each category. They also include provisions related to **security, financial accountability, service agreements**, and **information returns**.

---

## **I. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Excise Act, 2001* (Loi) |
| **Carton** | A paperboard box or wrapper containing ≤15 packages of cigarettes or ≤200 tobacco sticks |
| **Case** | A corrugated cardboard box used primarily for transport and protection of packages or cartons of tobacco or vaping products |
| **Manufacturer** | A company that produces tobacco products |
| **Raw leaf tobacco** | Repealed (SOR/2011-6, s.1); previously defined as being packaged when formed into a "hand" or packed for sale |
| **Prescribed package** | The smallest package in which a product is normally offered for sale to consumers |

## **II. Prescribed Information Requirements by Product Type**

### **1. For Packages of Manufactured Tobacco or Cigars (Sections 3 & 3.1)**

#### **Paragraph 34(b) of the Excise Act (Licensee Information):**
- Must include:
  - Name and address of the tobacco licensee
  - License number
  - If packaged for another person: the other person’s name and principal place of business

#### **Paragraph 35(1)(a) of the Excise Act (Manufacturer/Importer Information):**
- Must include:
  - Name and address of the manufacturer who packaged the product
  - If imported by a licensee: licensee’s name/address or license number
  - If imported by another party: name and address of the importer

> **Exception for Exported Products (to foreign duty-free shops or ships’ stores):**
> In addition to standard information, **"not for sale in Canada"** and **"vente interdite au Canada"** must be printed in a **conspicuous manner** on the package.

---

### **2. For Cases of Manufactured Tobacco or Cigars (Section 3.3)**

Must include:
- Number of cartons in the case and number of packages per carton (if packed in cartons)
- Number of packages in the case and weight of tobacco per package (if packed in packages)

> This applies to both domestic and imported products.

---

### **3. For Vaping Products (Sections 3.6, 3.7, and 158.46–158.47)**

#### **Paragraph 158.46(1)(b) & (2)(a) – Vaping Product Licensee Information:**
- Must include:
  - Vaping licensee’s name and address, or license number
  - If packaged for another: that person’s name and principal address

#### **Vaping Substance Content (per package):**
- Volume in **millilitres (ml)** of liquid vaping substance
- Weight in **grams (g)** of solid vaping substance
- Number of vaping devices and immediate containers in the package

#### **For Cases of Vaping Products (Section 3.8):**
- Number of packages in the case
- Volume and weight of vaping substance per package

#### **For Containers (Section 3.6 & 3.7):**
- Domestic containers: information set out in Section 3.6
- Imported containers: information set out in Section 3.7

---

### **4. For Tobacco Markings (Schedules 7 & 8)**

#### **Subsection 38(1) – Domestic vs. Imported Products:**
- **Domestic manufactured tobacco/cigars**: Marking per **Schedule 7**
- **Imported tobacco/cigars**: Marking per **Schedule 8**
- **Exception**: Cigars intended for **duty-free shops or ships’ stores** (even if made in Canada) must have **Schedule 8 marking**

#### **Subsection 38(2) – All Containers (Imported):**
- Requires **Schedule 8 marking** for all imported containers

> **Markings must be:**
> - Printed or affixed in a **conspicuous manner**
> - In accordance with specifications in the relevant Schedule

> **Vaping Products (Subsections 158.5(1) & (1.1)):**
> - Domestic: **Schedule 7 marking**
> - Imported: **Schedule 8 marking**
> - All containers must have marking in conspicuous, compliant format

> **Subsection 158.5(2) – All Imported Vaping Products:**
> - Must bear **Schedule 8 marking**

---

## **III. Excise Stamp Requirements**

### **Prescribed Manner of Affixing Stamps (Section 2 of the Act)**

A stamp must be:
- Affixed in a **conspicuous place** on the package
- Applied in a way that **seals the package**
- Remain **affixed after the package is opened**
- Not interfere with **security features** of the stamp
- Not obstruct any **required information under federal law**

> Applies to **tobacco**, **cannabis**, and **vaping excise stamps**

---

## **IV. Quantity Limits (Units of Product)**

### **Tobacco Products (Paragraph 32(2)(j) & 35(2)(c))**
- **Prescribed limit: 5 units**
- One unit = any of the following:
  - 200 cigarettes
  - 50 cigars
  - 200 tobacco sticks
  - 200 grams of manufactured tobacco

> This limit applies to **transport or storage** of tobacco products.

---

### **Vaping Products (Paragraphs 158.44(3)(e), 158.47(2)(c), and Section 158.56)**
- **Prescribed limit: 5 units**
- One unit = up to:
  - 120 ml of liquid vaping substance OR
  - 120 g of solid vaping substance
  - Within **no more than 12 vaping devices or immediate containers**

> This limit applies to **transport or possession** of vaping products.

---

## **V. Service Agreements for Cannabis (New Provision – Section on Service Agreements)**

> *Applies to cannabis products only; not directly to tobacco or vaping.*

### **Definition: Service Agreement**
- An agreement between two **cannabis licensees** where one licensee (the "packager") packages or stamps cannabis products for another (the "principal licensee")
- The principal licensee is **not** a producer of cannabis solely for packaging purposes

### **Authorization Requirements**
- A party may apply to the Minister to **authorize** a service agreement
- Application must:
  - Be in **prescribed form**
  - Include a **copy of the service agreement**
  - Be filed **60 days before the proposed effective date** (or later, at Minister’s discretion)

### **Minister’s Actions**
- Must **consider and decide** on the application within due time
- Must **notify the applicant in writing** of decision and, if authorized, the **effective date**

### **Conditions and Amendments**
- The Minister may impose **conditions** on authorized agreements
- If the agreement is to be **amended or terminated**, the parties must:
  - Notify the Minister **without delay**
  - File a new application for authorization of the amended agreement

### **Revocation**
- The Minister may **revoke** authorization if:
  - A party violates the agreement
  - Conditions are not met
  - Agreement is no longer valid
- Revocation must be **notified in writing** to all parties with an **effective date**

---

### **Prescribed Persons Under Service Agreements**

#### **For Paragraph 158.05(2)(c):**
- A **cannabis licensee** that:
  - Is a party to an authorized service agreement
  - Has stamps issued to the **other licensee** (the packager)
  - Is using those stamps to affix to cannabis products **in accordance with the agreement and any Minister-imposed conditions**

> This ensures accountability and prevents misuse of stamps.

---
