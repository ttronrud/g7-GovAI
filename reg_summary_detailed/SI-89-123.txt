**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

This regulation, published under **S.C. 1987, c. 48**, constitutes an official declaration by the **Governor General in Council** (acting on the recommendation of the Minister of Justice) regarding the **coming into force** of the **Third Supplement to the Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985**.

---

### **Background and Context**

1. **Statute Revision Commission’s Role**:
   - The Statute Revision Commission (SRC) is responsible for reviewing and updating Canada’s statutes to ensure clarity, consistency, and efficiency in the legislative framework.
   - The Commission completed its work on the **third supplement** to the *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985*, which consolidates, revises, and organizes existing laws to improve readability and legal certainty.

2. **Official Certification and Deposit**:
   - The final version of the 3rd Supplement was formally attested by the **Governor General**, countersigned by the **Minister of Justice**, and certified as accurate by the **Chairman of the Statute Revision Commission**.
   - This certified copy was officially deposited in the **Office of the Clerk of the Parliaments** on **April 4, 1989**, signifying its formal acceptance and readiness for enactment.

---

### **Legal Authority**

- The declaration is made **pursuant to subsection 13(2) of the Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985 Act**.
- This provision allows the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Justice, to declare that a supplement to the Revised Statutes shall come into force as if the enactments within it were enacted directly by Parliament.
- This mechanism enables the legal effect of a supplement to be immediate and binding without requiring a new Act of Parliament, streamlining the legislative process.

---

### **Key Provision of the Regulation**

- **Effective Date**: The 3rd Supplement to the Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985, shall **come into force and take effect on May 1, 1989**.
- **Legal Effect**: From that date onward, all enactments contained in the Supplement shall operate and have the force of law **as though they were enacted directly by an Act of Parliament**.
- This means that the laws included in the supplement are treated as if they were passed by the Parliament of Canada, with full legal standing, enforceability, and judicial recognition.

---

### **Purpose and Significance**

- **Legal Clarity and Accessibility**: The supplement consolidates and modernizes statutes, removing redundancies, obsolete provisions, and inconsistencies, thereby improving access to the law for legal practitioners, government agencies, and the public.
- **Efficiency in Governance**: By enabling the enactment of new or revised legal provisions through a supplement rather than through a new standalone Act, the process becomes faster and more efficient.
- **Continuity of Law**: The declaration ensures that the legal system remains stable and that changes to statutes are implemented in a timely and formal manner.

---

### **Legal and Administrative Implications**

- All courts, government departments, and legal institutions are required to recognize and apply the laws contained in the 3rd Supplement from **May 1, 1989**, as valid and binding law.
- The supplement becomes part of the official legal corpus of Canada, referenced in legal proceedings, administrative decisions, and policy development.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation marks the formal implementation of a major statutory revision effort. By declaring May 1, 1989, as the effective date of the 3rd Supplement to the *Revised Statutes of Canada, 1985*, the Government of Canada ensures that a comprehensive and up-to-date version of its statute law is now in operation. This action reflects the ongoing commitment to legal modernization, transparency, and administrative efficiency within Canada’s legislative framework.
