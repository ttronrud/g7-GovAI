**Detailed Summary of the Seaway Property Regulations (S.C. 2001, c. 4, s. 150; S.C. 1998, c. 10)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Seaway Property Regulations* are a set of administrative rules made under **Section 98a of the Canada Marine Act** by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Transport**. These regulations govern the use, management, and protection of **Seaway property**—land and other assets managed, operated, or used by the **St. Lawrence Seaway Management Corporation (Manager)**—within the **St. Lawrence Seaway**, a major international waterway connecting the Great Lakes to the Atlantic Ocean.

The primary purpose of these regulations is to ensure **safety**, **environmental protection**, **navigation integrity**, and **commercial efficiency** of the Seaway system while establishing clear rules for activities conducted on or within the Seaway.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Canada Marine Act*. |
| **Allied petroleum product** | Includes non-fuel hydrocarbons such as alcohols, glycols, thinners, solvents, organic chemicals, and inks. Excludes propane, paint, and solvents. |
| **Authorization** | A formal permission granted by the Manager under Part 3 of the Regulations. |
| **Designated area** | A specific zone identified in writing or by signage for certain activities. |
| **Fee** | A charge required under subsections 92(1) or (2) of the Canada Marine Act. |
| **Hot work** | Any activity involving flame or potential ignition (e.g., welding, cutting, heating). |
| **Manager** | The *St. Lawrence Seaway Management Corporation*, responsible for managing Seaway operations. |
| **Petroleum product** | A refined hydrocarbon mixture (at least 70% hydrocarbons) used as fuel, lubricant, or power transmitter (e.g., gasoline, diesel, kerosene). Excludes propane, paint, and solvents. |
| **Seaway property** | All land and infrastructure managed, operated, or used by the Manager in connection with the Seaway. |

---

## **Scope and Application**

- These regulations apply **within the Seaway and on Seaway property**.
- They **do not apply** to aspects of work that involve **approvals or opinions under Part I of the Canadian Navigable Waters Act (CNWA)** issued to persons other than the Manager or its agents.
- The regulations are **binding on Her Majesty in right of Canada or a province**.

---

## **Prohibited Activities (Section 5)**

Unless authorized, **no person may do or permit anything** that has or is likely to result in any of the following:

1. **Endangering safety or health** of persons in or on the Seaway.
2. **Interfering with navigation** in the Seaway.
3. **Obstructing or adversely affecting** any part of the Seaway or Seaway property.
4. **Interfering with authorized activities** under Section 26 or 30.
5. **Diverting river flow**, altering currents, causing silting, or reducing water depth.
6. **Causing a nuisance**.
7. **Damaging ships or other property**.
8. **Adversely affecting sediment, soil, air, or water quality**.
9. **Impacting Seaway operations or property**.

> This creates a broad **prohibition on harmful or disruptive conduct** without formal authorization.

---

## **Restrictions on Activities (Schedule Column 1)**

- **No activity listed in Column 1 of the Schedule may be conducted** if there is an **"X" in Column 4** (indicating it is prohibited).
- **Access to Seaway property is restricted** unless:
  - The person is conducting an authorized activity under Section 26 or 30; **or**
  - Access is not restricted by signs, fences, or other physical barriers.

> This ensures only authorized or unobstructed access to sensitive areas.

---

## **Signage and Devices (Manager’s Authority)**

The Manager may install signs or devices to:

- Ensure **safety** of people and property.
- Protect the **environment**.
- Manage **marine infrastructure** and operations commercially.

**Permitted subjects for signage include:**
- Safe operation of vehicles.
- Parking or stopping rules (including prohibitions).
- Restrictions on **vehicle weight and dimensions**.

> All persons must **comply with signage** unless authorized otherwise under Section 33.1.

---

## **Vehicle Operations on Seaway Property**

- Must comply with **local provincial and municipal laws**.
- Vehicles must **enter roadways only at designated public access points**.
- Must operate **safely** and at a speed **not exceeding the lowest of**:
  - 40 km/h,
  - Speed limits posted by the Manager,
  - Speed required due to weather or equipment movement.

> Speed limits are designed to ensure safety during variable conditions.

---

## **Vehicle Removal and Storage (Section 14)**

The Manager may move or store a vehicle if it:
- Is **abandoned**,
- Is parked or stopped in a **hazardous or obstructive manner**,
- Is parked outside **designated parking areas**, or
- Is parked outside **parking/stopping hours**.

> If a vehicle interferes with navigation, the Manager may have it removed **at the owner/operator’s expense**.

---

## **Spills, Drops, or Discharges (Section 15)**

Any person who drops, deposits, discharges, or spills:
- Refuse,
- Polluting substances,
- Objects interfering with navigation,
- Cargo or ship’s gear,

Must:
1. **Immediately attempt to remove** the substance or object (technically and economically feasible),
2. **Notify the Manager without delay**, providing:
   - Description of what was spilled,
   - Approximate location,
   - Efforts made to remove it,
3. **Submit a detailed written report** as soon as possible.

> If the spill is not removed immediately, the Manager may have it removed **at the person’s expense**.

---

## **Dangerous Situations (Section 16)**

Any person causing a dangerous situation must:
- Take **precautions** such as:
  - Posting notices,
  - Deploying lights or barriers,
  - Stationing personnel to warn others,
- Prevent injury or damage,
- **Notify the Manager immediately** of the situation and actions taken.

> This ensures prompt response to potential hazards.

---

## **Emergency Exceptions (Section 17)**

Despite other rules, a person may conduct an activity listed in Column 1 of the Schedule **without authorization** during an **emergency** if:
- The emergency threatens **safety or property/environment**, and
- The person submits a **written report** explaining why it was an emergency.

> This allows flexibility during urgent, life-threatening events.

---

## **Reporting Obligations in Emergencies**

In cases involving:
- Death, bodily injury, or environmental damage,
- Any emergency situation,

All directly involved persons (and authorized activity holders) must:
- **Notify the Manager immediately**,
- Submit a **detailed written report** as soon as possible,
- At the Manager’s request, provide copies of reports submitted to **provincial, municipal, or federal authorities**.

> Ensures transparency and coordination during crises.

---

## **Incident Reporting (Section 18)**

Any person involved in an incident resulting in:
- Material loss,
- Explosion, fire, accident, grounding, stranding, or pollution,

Must submit a **detailed written report to the Manager without delay**.

> This supports incident tracking and accountability.

---

## **Mitigation of Prohibited Results (Section 19)**

If an activity is likely to have prohibited results (Section 5) and **no authorization is required**, the Manager may:
- **Instruct the person to stop** the activity, or
- Require them to take **precautions to mitigate or prevent** the adverse effect.

> Provides regulatory intervention even for unlicensed activities.

---

## **Manager’s Duty to Mitigate (Section 20)**

If the Manager is the **proponent** of an activity listed in the Schedule that may have prohibited results, the Manager must take **appropriate measures to mitigate or prevent harm**, if technically and economically feasible, considering:
- Safety of persons and property,
- Environmental protection,
- Commercial operation of Seaway infrastructure.

> Reinforces the Manager’s responsibility as steward of the Seaway.

---

## **Construction and Modifications (Section 21)**

Subject to any agreement between Canada and the U.S. under Section 25 of the Canada Marine Act, the Manager or a person acting on its behalf may:
- Build, place, rebuild, repair, or alter works **in, on, over, under, through, or across** the navigable waters of the Seaway.

> This allows for infrastructure development, subject to broader international agreements.

---

## **Impact Assessment Requirement (Section 22)**

Before carrying out any activity under Section 24:
- The Manager must conduct a **navigation impact assessment**.
- A **written summary report** must be prepared and submitted before the work begins.

> Ensures proactive evaluation of risks to navigation.
