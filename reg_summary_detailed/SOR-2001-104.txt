## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Cost of Borrowing (Trust and Loan Companies) Regulations**

### **1. Regulatory Background and Authority**
- **Enacted by**: Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance.
- **Legal Basis**: Sections 435 to 443a and 531b of the *Trust and Loan Companies Act (TCLA)*.
- **Purpose**: To establish standardized rules for calculating and disclosing the **true cost of borrowing** to consumers, ensuring transparency and fairness in credit agreements.
- **Regulatory Reference**:  
  - S.C. 1997, c. 15, ss. 379‚Äì384  
  - S.C. 1997, c. 15, s. 408  
  - S.C. 1991, c. 45

### **2. Key Definitions**
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | The *Trust and Loan Companies Act* |
| **APR** | Annual Percentage Rate ‚Äî the cost of borrowing expressed as an annual rate on the principal |
| **Borrower** | Includes individuals to whom a loan is offered, and holders or applicants for a credit card |
| **Credit Agreement** | Any agreement involving a loan, line of credit, or credit card |
| **Disbursement Charge** | Fees for arranging, documenting, insuring, or securing a loan (excluding certain types) |

### **3. Scope of Application**
These Regulations **do not apply** to:
- Credit agreements for **business purposes** of a borrower;
- Credit agreements with **non-natural persons** (e.g., corporations, trusts);
- Loans governed by the *Canada Student Loans Act*;
- Student loans governed by any federal or provincial Act that requires interest or discount disclosure.

> ‚úÖ Applies to consumer loans, credit cards, and lines of credit.

### **4. Calculation of Annual Percentage Rate (APR)**

#### **General Formula (for loans not via credit card/line of credit):**
> **APR = (Total Cost of Borrowing / Average Outstanding Principal) √ó (100 / Term in Years)**  
Where:
- Total cost of borrowing = sum of all interest and fees over the loan term
- Average outstanding principal = average of the principal balance at the end of each payment period (before any payment is deducted)
- Term = loan duration in years (to at least two decimal places)

#### **Key Rules for APR Calculation:**
| Rule | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **Rounding** | APR may be rounded to the **nearest eighth of a percent** (e.g., 5.125%) |
| **Payment Application Order** | Each payment must first cover **accumulated cost of borrowing**, then reduce the **outstanding principal** |
| **Time Periods** | - 1 month = 1/12 year<br>- 1 week = 1/52 year<br>- 1 day = 1/365 year |
| **Variable Interest Rates** | Must use the **rate in effect on the day of calculation** |
| **No Installments** | If no payments exist, APR is calculated assuming repayment in **one lump sum at maturity** |
| **New Credit Agreements** | A loan with a balance from a prior agreement is treated as a **new credit agreement** for APR purposes |

#### **APR for Credit Cards & Lines of Credit**
- **Fixed interest rate** ‚Üí APR = that fixed rate  
- **Variable interest rate** ‚Üí APR = rate **in effect on the disclosure date**

> ‚ö†Ô∏è If there is **no cost of borrowing beyond interest**, the APR equals the interest rate.

### **5. What Is Included in the Cost of Borrowing (APR)?**

The total cost includes:
- Interest or discount under section 435(a) of the Act
- **Administrative charges** (e.g., application, account management)
- **Lawyer/notary fees** required by the company (not optional)
- **Insurance charges** (excluding those for optional coverage or asset value)
- **Broker fees** if included in the loan amount and paid directly by the lender
- **Appraisal, inspection, or surveying fees** related to property used as security (excluding those for borrower benefit)

> ‚úÖ These are **included** in the APR calculation.

---

### **6. What Is EXCLUDED from the Cost of Borrowing?**

The following are **not included** in the APR:
- **Optional insurance** (e.g., not required by the borrower)
- **Overdraft fees**
- **Registry fees** (to register security interests or obtain registry info)
- **Penalty charges** for early repayment
- **Lawyer/notary fees** not required by the company
- **Title defect insurance** (if paid by borrower)
- **Appraisal/inspection services provided directly to borrower**
- **High-ratio mortgage insurance**
- **Tax account maintenance fees** (required or optional)
- **Fees to discharge a security interest**
- **Default charges**

> ‚ùå These are **excluded** from the APR.

### **7. Disclosure Requirements**

#### **Mandatory Disclosure Statement**
- Must be provided **in writing** to the borrower.
- Must include **all required information** in a clear, simple, and non-misleading manner.
- Must be in **language the borrower can understand**.

#### **Form of Disclosure Statement**
- Can be:
  - A **separate document**
  - A **part of the credit agreement or application**

#### **Presentation Requirements (Consolidated Format)**
- If part of an agreement or application:
  - Must be presented in a **single, consolidated location**
  - The **applicable information box** (from Schedules 1‚Äì5) must appear at the **beginning** of the document

- If separate:
  - Must be provided **before** the agreement is signed or **with** the application
  - The **information box** must be at the **beginning** of the separate statement

#### **Information Box Formatting Standards**
To ensure **legibility and accessibility**:
- Font size: **Minimum 12 points** (bold for titles, rates, dates, amounts)
- Other text: **Minimum 10 points**
- Bold formatting for key numbers (interest rates, dates, dollar amounts)
- Standard spacing between words and characters
- Margins (top, bottom, left, right) to provide white space
- **Dark text on light background** for optimal contrast

> ‚úÖ Designed for **maximum readability** by all consumers.

---

### **8. Assumptions and Estimates**
- Information based on **assumptions or estimates** is acceptable **only if**:
  1. The assumption is **reasonable**
  2. It **cannot be known at the time** of disclosure
  3. The borrower is **explicitly informed** that it is an assumption or estimate

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Must not mislead or create false impressions.

---

### **9. Timing of Disclosure (Initial Statement)**

A company must provide the **initial disclosure statement**:
- **On or before the earlier of**:
  - The first payment (other than a disbursement charge) by the borrower
  - **Two clear business days before** entering into the agreement **if it is a mortgage or hypothec**
  - **On the day of entering into** the agreement in all other cases

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Exceptions:
- If the borrower **consents** to receiving the statement via another borrower (e.g., group loan)
- If the borrower obtains **independent legal advice**
- If a **rescission period of at least two business days** is included
- If the agreement includes **favourable terms** that reduce the cost of borrowing

---

### **10. Required Information in Initial Disclosure Statement**

#### **For Fixed Interest Rate, Fixed Amount Loans (e.g., mortgage, personal loan)**
Must include:
- Principal amount
- Advance schedule (when advances are made)
- Total amount of all payments
- Total cost of borrowing (expressed as an amount)
- Loan term and amortization period (if different)
- Annual interest rate and compounding method
- APR (if different from interest rate)
- Date interest begins and any grace periods
- Amount and due date of each payment
- Payment order: **first to cost of borrowing, then to principal**
- Optional services (charges, cancellation conditions)
- Rebate/penalty formula for early repayment (if applicable)
- Default charges (as per section 18)
- Security interest (property pledged)
- Broker fees (if any)
- Fee to discharge security interest (amount on disclosure date)
- All other charges (excluding interest)
