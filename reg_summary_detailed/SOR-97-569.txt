**Detailed Summary of the British Columbia Rules of Practice Respecting Reduction in the Number of Years of Imprisonment Without Eligibility for Parole**

---

### **Overview**
The regulation in question establishes the **British Columbia Rules of Practice Respecting Reduction in the Number of Years of Imprisonment Without Eligibility for Parole**, made under **subsection 745.64(1)a of the Criminal Code** by the **Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of British Columbia**. These rules govern the procedural framework for an individual who is serving a sentence of imprisonment *without eligibility for parole* to apply for a reduction in the number of years of such imprisonment.

The rules came into force on **December 1, 1997**, and replaced the earlier **SOR/92-746** version of the rules. They are designed to ensure a fair, structured, and transparent process for applicants to seek a reduction in their non-parole period, while maintaining accountability and due process.

---

## **Key Provisions and Structure**

### **1. Definitions**
The rules begin with a clear set of definitions that apply throughout:

- **Applicant**: A person making the application, or counsel acting on their behalf.
- **Application**: A formal request made under **section 745.6(1)** of the Criminal Code.
- **Attorney General**: Refers to the Attorney General of British Columbia, including counsel acting for them.
- **District Registrar**: The official responsible for administrative handling of applications in the Vancouver Judicial District.
- **Judge**: A judge designated by the Chief Justice under **section 745.61(5)** to oversee the preliminary hearing and trial of the application.

> These definitions ensure clarity and consistency in how parties and roles are understood during the process.

---

### **2. Effective Date and Repeal**
- The rules **come into force on December 1, 1997**.
- The **prior rules (SOR/92-746)** are **repealed**, meaning this document supersedes all previous versions.

---

### **3. Mandatory Application Form and Supporting Affidavit**
An application must be:
- Submitted in **Form 8**, a standardized format.
- Include the following specific information:
  - Full name and date of birth of the applicant.
  - Details of the offence and sentence (conviction date, sentencing date, place of trial).
  - Name and location of the current institution where the applicant is detained.
  - A **complete list** of all institutions where the applicant has been detained since arrest, including dates of entry.
  - Any **additional sentences** the applicant is currently serving (with date, place, and offence).
  - **Grounds** for the application (must be precise and concise).
  - **Relief sought** (i.e., the reduction in years of imprisonment).
  - Applicant’s **address for service**.

> This ensures full transparency and completeness in the application, allowing the court to assess the applicant’s history and circumstances.

Additionally:
- The application must be **supported by an affidavit in Form 9**, sworn by the applicant.
- The affidavit confirms the truth of the facts stated in the application.

---

### **4. Service of the Application**
Upon filing:
- The applicant must serve the application on **three parties**:
  1. The **Solicitor General of Canada**
  2. The **Attorney General of British Columbia**
  3. The **officer in charge of the institution** where the applicant is detained.

**Method of Service**:
- Must be sent via **registered mail**.
- Service is deemed effective on the **seventh day after mailing**.

**Proof of Service**:
- Must be supported by an **affidavit** from the person who carried out the service.
- This affidavit must be filed with the **district registrar within seven days** of service.

> This ensures that all relevant parties are formally notified and have a reasonable opportunity to respond.

---

### **5. Designation of a Judge**
- A judge must be formally designated in writing by the Chief Justice under **section 745.61(5)** of the Criminal Code.
- The designation must be filed with the **district registrar**.
- This judge will preside over the preliminary hearing and the full hearing of the application.

---

### **6. Preliminary Hearing – Key Duties of the Judge**
Upon receiving the application, the judge must:
- **Determine the date and place** of the preliminary hearing.
- Send a **written notice** of the hearing to the **Attorney General**.
- Instruct the Attorney General to ensure the applicant is present at the hearing.

Upon receiving the judge’s notice, the Attorney General must:
- Send a **written notice** of the hearing to:
  - The applicant
  - The Solicitor General of Canada
  - The officer in charge of the institution
- A copy of this notice must be filed with the **district registrar**.

---

### **7. Proceedings at the Preliminary Hearing**
At the preliminary hearing, the judge determines:
- Whether the applicant meets the requirements of **section 745.6(1)** of the Code.
- Whether the application complies with **Rule 7**.
- The **method of presenting evidence** (e.g., live testimony, documents).
- Whether evidence must be provided **in advance** to the other party and, if so, how and when.
- The **nature and extent of cross-examination**.
- The **date and place** for the full hearing of the application.

**Additional Powers**:
- The judge may **adjourn** the hearing if necessary.
- The judge may **order the preparation of a Parole Eligibility Report** (PER), based on subsection **745.63(1)** of the Code.

---

### **8. Parole Eligibility Report (PER)**
- Prepared by a person designated by the **Solicitor General of Canada**.
- Must include:
  - Summary of applicant’s **social and family background**
  - Summary of **classification and discipline evaluations**
  - Summary of **regular conduct reports**
  - Summary of **psychological or psychiatric assessments**
  - Any other information relevant to the applicant’s character and conduct

> The report is intended to provide a comprehensive view of the applicant’s behavior and suitability for parole.

**Key Provisions**:
- If the judge orders a PER, the hearing is **adjourned** to allow time for preparation.
- The report is **filed with the district registrar**.
- A copy is **delivered to the applicant and the Attorney General**.
- The judge must **notify the applicant and Attorney General** when the report is filed and the hearing is resumed.
- The judge allows **at least 30 days** for both parties to study the report before resuming the hearing.

**Disputes**:
- If either party disputes any part of the report, they may request the **author of the report to attend** the hearing for **cross-examination**.
- The judge decides which parts of the report will be presented and whether additional evidence is needed.

---

### **9. Hearing of the Application**
The hearing is conducted in accordance with **Part XX of the Criminal Code**, which governs jury trials. However, with specific modifications:

#### **Key Modifications**:
- The applicant and the Attorney General are entitled to the **same number of peremptory challenges** as they would have in a trial for the underlying offence.
- The judge may issue **any necessary orders or directions**, including:
  - Determining the **sufficiency** of the application
  - Extending or reducing time periods for evidence or presentation

#### **Evidence**:
- The judge may **admit a certified transcript** of the original trial and sentencing proceedings as evidence.

#### **Order of Presentation**:
- The **applicant presents evidence first**.
- If permitted by the judge, the applicant may present **rebuttal evidence** after the Attorney General has presented their case.

#### **Addressing the Jury**:
- After evidence is presented:
  - The applicant speaks to the jury.
  - The Attorney General then addresses the jury.
  - The applicant may **reply** to the Attorney General’s address.

> This structure ensures the applicant has the first opportunity to present their case and respond to any arguments made by the Crown.

---

### **10. Juror Decision**
- The **jury’s decision** must be based **solely on the evidence presented** at the hearing.
- The jury does **not consider** the applicant’s underlying criminal record or other non-evidentiary factors.

---

### **11. Court Format and Jurisdiction**
- The matter is filed in the **Supreme Court of British Columbia** under the heading:
  > *IN THE MATTER OF [Full Name of Applicant], an application for a reduction in the number of years of imprisonment without eligibility for parole pursuant to section 745.6 of the Criminal Code.*

- The application and affidavit are submitted in a formal, structured format, including:
  - A statement of the applicant’s request for reduction.
  - A full list of institutions and dates of detention.
  - Precise grounds for the application.
  - A sworn affidavit confirming the truth of the facts.

---

## **Purpose and Policy Rationale**
The rules serve several key objectives:
1. **Fairness and Due Process**: Ensures that individuals serving long non-parole terms have a structured, fair opportunity to seek a reduction based on their behavior, circumstances, and rehabilitation.
2. **Transparency**: Requires detailed documentation and formal service to ensure all stakeholders are informed.
