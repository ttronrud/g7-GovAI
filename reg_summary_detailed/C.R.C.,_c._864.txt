**Detailed Summary of the *Fisheries Improvement Loans Regulations***

---

### **Overview**
The *Fisheries Improvement Loans Regulations* (the "Regulations") are a set of administrative rules designed to govern the issuance, terms, repayment, and enforcement of **guaranteed loans** provided under the *Fisheries Improvement Loans Act* (the "Act"). These regulations establish the eligibility criteria, loan limits, repayment structures, interest rates, security requirements, default provisions, and claims procedures for loans intended to support fishing enterprises in Canada.

The Regulations apply specifically to **guaranteed loans** authorized under the Act and are intended to promote the development and sustainability of primary fishing activities by providing accessible financing to fishermen and fishing-related businesses.

---

## **Key Definitions and Interpretations**

The Regulations define key terms used throughout the Act and these rules:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Fisheries Improvement Loans Act* (Loi). |
| **Insured Fishing Vessel** | A vessel that is insured under the *Fishing Vessel Insurance Regulations* or has equivalent coverage ensuring indemnity for total or partial loss. |
| **Vehicle** | A motor vehicle other than a passenger car (e.g., trucks, trailers). |
| **Addition** | Includes construction of new rooms, storeys, garages, ice houses, foundations, or basements, and purchase of materials for such purposes. |
| **Alteration** | Structural improvements to buildings or structures to modernize or improve functionality; includes equipment relocation, plumbing/heating/ventilation upgrades, and material purchases. |
| **Hull** | The body of a fishing vessel. |
| **Major Repair/Overhaul** | A repair or overhaul costing at least $400 (as estimated by the borrower). |
| **Principal Occupation** | The primary activity through which a person earns income during a fishing season. |
| **Repair** | Covers repairs to water supply systems, shore installations, fishing equipment, vessels, or buildings, including painting. |
| **Superstructure** | Equipment affixed to the hull (e.g., masts, rigging, rails), excluding electronic equipment or engines. |
| **Fisherman** | A person who has or intends to acquire an equitable or legal interest in a fishing vessel or a weir/trapping device, or holds a valid licence to use such devices. |
| **Primary Fishing Enterprise** | Activities such as processing fish that are normally carried out by fishermen in a given area or done to meet specific marketing needs. |
| **Shore Installation** | Structures like piers, wharves, skidways, or stages affixed to land and used in fishing operations. |
| **Fishing Equipment** | Tools, machines not affixed to real property; skiffs, lifeboats, engines, anchors, electronic gear; vehicles, skids, toboggans; and gear like nets, trawls, traps, lines. |

> *Note: Several definitions have been revoked (e.g., "averaging period," "interest period") under SOR/78-139, s. 1 and s. 2. These are no longer in effect.*

## **Eligibility and Loan Types**

Only loans that meet specific criteria are considered **guaranteed loans** under the Act and these Regulations. The following loan types are explicitly defined and governed:

---

### **1. Purchase or Construction of a Fishing Vessel**
A guaranteed loan is available if:
- The vessel is an **insured fishing vessel**;
- The loan amount is:
  - **≤ 90%** of the purchase price (for purchases), or
  - **≤ 90%** of the estimated construction cost (for construction), or
  - **≤ actual cash expenditure** incurred by the borrower (if construction is done partially or fully by the borrower);
- The lender has required evidence (via normal lending practices) that funds were used **for the purpose stated in the application**.

> *Note: This provision ensures that the loan does not exceed a reasonable proportion of the total cost and that funds are used appropriately.*

---

### **2. Purchase or Construction of Fishing Equipment**
A guaranteed loan is available if:
- The loan amount is:
  - **≤ 75%** of the purchase price (for purchases), or
  - **≤ 75%** of the estimated construction cost (for construction), or
  - **≤ actual cash expenditure** incurred by the borrower;
- Funds are used **for the purpose specified in the application**.

> *Lower threshold (75%) reflects the higher risk and cost variability associated with equipment compared to vessels.*

---

### **3. Major Repair or Overhaul of a Vessel**
A guaranteed loan is available if:
- The vessel is insured;
- The loan amount is:
  - **≤ 75%** of the estimated cost of the major repair/overhaul, or
  - **≤ actual cash expenditure** incurred by the borrower;
- Funds are used for the stated purpose.

> *Major repairs require a minimum cost of $400, as defined in the Regulations.*

---

### **4. Purchase or Construction of Shore Installations or Buildings**
A guaranteed loan is available if:
- The loan amount is:
  - **≤ 90%** of the purchase price (for purchases), or
  - **≤ 90%** of the estimated cost (for construction, repair, alteration, or addition), or
  - **≤ actual cash expenditure** incurred (if carried out by borrower);
- Funds are used for the stated purpose.

> *Buildings and shore installations are treated similarly to vessels due to their direct operational role in fishing.*

---

### **5. Development or Improvement of a Primary Fishing Enterprise**
A guaranteed loan is available if:
- The loan amount is:
  - **≤ 90%** of the estimated cost of development/improvement, or
  - **≤ actual cash expenditure** incurred (if carried out by borrower);
- Funds are used for the stated purpose.

> *Examples include:*
> - Sinking or installing water supply systems;
> - Repairing or altering non-building structures (e.g., sheds, storage);
> - Moving and re-installing shore installations.

---

## **Loan Application Process**

- Every applicant must submit a **signed application** in the form set out in **Schedule I**.
- The form must include all required information about the borrower, the proposed use of funds, and financial details.

---

## **Repayment Terms**

- Repayments must be made **in instalments at least annually**.
- The lender may allow **more frequent payments** (e.g., semi-annually), at its discretion.
- The repayment schedule must reflect the borrower’s **financial capacity**, taking into account:
  - Type of fishing enterprise;
  - Marketing conditions;
  - Other financial obligations;
  - Other relevant circumstances.

> *The goal is to ensure repayment is realistic and sustainable for the borrower.*

---

## **Modification of Loan Terms**

A lender may revise or extend loan terms **with the borrower’s approval**, under the following conditions:
- Extension of repayment period **within the maximum limits set by the Act**;
- Change in **instalment amount**;
- Change in **frequency of instalments** (but **not less than annually**).

> *This flexibility helps borrowers facing financial hardship or changing market conditions.*

---

## **Limitations on Term Extensions**

- Loan terms **cannot be extended beyond what is prescribed in the Act or Regulation** unless:
  - The **Minister approves** the change.
- Even with Ministerial approval, the **Minister’s liability to the lender remains unchanged**.

> *This protects the lender from unlimited term extensions and ensures accountability.*

---

## **Interest Rate Provisions**

### **For Banks Under the Bank Act (e.g., major Canadian banks):**
- Maximum interest rate = **1% + prime lending rate** in effect on the date the loan agreement is signed.

### **For Other Banks (not under Bank Act):**
- Maximum interest rate = **1% + average of the prime lending rates** of the following seven chartered banks:
  1. Bank of Montreal  
  2. Bank of Nova Scotia  
  3. Royal Bank of Canada  
  4. Toronto-Dominion Bank  
  5. Banque Canadienne Nationale  
  6. Banque Provinciale  
  7. Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce  
- The average is **rounded to the nearest 1/8 of 1%**, with ties broken in favor of the **lower** value.
- The rate is **reviewed monthly** on the first day of each month.
- The Minister must **notify all banks** immediately when a new rate is set.

> *This ensures fair, transparent, and market-based interest rates while protecting borrowers from excessive charges.*

---

## **Security Requirements**

At the time of loan disbursement, the lender **must take security** for repayment through one of the following methods:
1. **Chattel mortgage or commercial pledge** (for personal property);
2. **Mortgage or hypothec on real property**;
3. **Assignment of rights under a sale agreement**;
4. **Written undertaking** by the borrower to provide security as required under any of the above.

> *Lenders may also take **additional security** if they deem it necessary.*

---

## **Borrower Promises and Default Provisions**

- The borrower must provide a **written promise to repay** the loan, including:
  - Principal amount;
  - Basis for interest rate;
  - Repayment terms.

> *This document is critical for accountability and legal enforcement.*
