### **Detailed Summary of the Presentation of Persons (2003) Regulations**

---

#### **1. Background and Legal Authority**
These regulations were enacted on the recommendation of the Minister of National Revenue, under the authority of the **Customs Act**, specifically:
- **Section 11a**, subsection **11.1(3)b**
- Paragraphs **164(1)(b)(c), (i)(d), and (j)** and **167.1(b)(e)**

They implement a public announcement made on **December 3, 2001**, and are designed to provide flexibility in how certain individuals may present themselves at Canadian borders, particularly when arriving via specific modes of transportation.

The regulations are referenced in several statutory provisions:
- **S.C. 2001, c. 25, s. 10, 11, 85(1)**
- **S.C. 1992, c. 28, s. 30(1), 31(1)**
- **R.S., c. 1, (2nd Supp.)**

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined to ensure clarity and consistency in the application of the regulations:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Customs Act* |
| **Authorization** | A formal permission issued by the Minister allowing a person to present themselves in an alternative manner at a border crossing |
| **Authorized Person** | A person who has received an authorization from the Minister |
| **Commercial Driver** | A person who operates a commercial highway conveyance (e.g., truck, delivery vehicle) |
| **Commercial Goods** | Goods imported into Canada for sale or commercial use |
| **Commercial Highway Conveyance** | A vehicle designed for hauling freight on highways |
| **Commercial Passenger Conveyance** | A vehicle used to carry passengers who have paid for passage (e.g., buses, trains) |
| **Common-Law Partner** | An individual who has cohabited with another in a conjugal relationship for at least one year |
| **Corporate Aircraft** | An aircraft used for business purposes, not carrying paying passengers, with no more than 15 persons (including crew) aboard upon arrival in Canada |
| **Designated Customs Office** | A customs office designated under the Act where individuals may present themselves, either normally or via alternative means |
| **Designated Holding Area** | A secure area where persons in transit are held before proceeding to their final destination |
| **Family Member** | A spouse, common-law partner, or dependent identified in a document confirming a military or diplomatic posting abroad |
| **Marine Pleasure Craft** | A vessel used exclusively for recreational purposes, not carrying paying passengers |
| **Non-Commercial Passenger Conveyance** | Includes corporate aircraft, private aircraft, and marine pleasure craft; does not carry paying passengers |
| **Parent** | A person with legal custody or parental authority over a child (by law, agreement, or court order) |
| **Permanent Resident of Canada** | As defined in the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act* |
| **Private Aircraft** | An aircraft other than corporate aircraft that does not carry paying passengers and has no more than 15 persons (including crew) on board upon arrival |

#### **3. Exemptions from Mandatory Presentation (Section 11(1))**

Certain individuals and situations are **exempt** from the requirement to present themselves at a designated customs office under subsection **11(1)** of the *Customs Act*. These include:

##### **(a) Arriving on a Commercial Passenger Conveyance**
- If the person **does not disembark** at the point of arrival and intends to go to **another place in Canada** with a designated customs office:
  - Must present themselves **without delay** at that destination’s customs office.
  - If the office is closed, must present at the **nearest open office**.

##### **(b) Transferred Under Customs Control**
- If transferred from a **designated holding area** to another commercial passenger conveyance:
  - For departure to **a place outside Canada**: Only required to leave the holding area to board the new conveyance.
  - For departure to **another place in Canada**: Must present at the destination’s customs office as above.

##### **(c) Arriving via Non-Commercial Passenger Conveyance**
- If arriving at a **designated customs office**, and the person in charge of the conveyance:
  - Can present passengers and crew via **radio or telephone**.
  - Notifies an officer of arrival and, if required, presents at the specified time and place.

##### **(d) Crew Members Arriving via Freight Train**
- If arriving at a designated customs office and the crew member:
  - Notifies an officer by radio or telephone of arrival.
  - If required, presents at the specified time and place.

##### **(e) Entry into Canadian Waters or Airspace**
- Any person entering Canadian waters or airspace (including inland waters or airspace) who:
  - Has a destination in Canada with a designated customs office.
  - Presents themselves **without delay** at that office.

> **Note:** In all exempted cases, individuals are **required to answer truthfully** any questions asked by officers during the performance of their duties under the Act or other federal laws.

---

#### **4. Exceptions for Transporting Passengers and Crew (Section 11(3))**

The **person in charge** of a commercial passenger conveyance is **not required** to ensure that certain passengers or crew are transported immediately to a customs office under subsection **11(3)** if:

- The individuals **do not disembark in Canada** and have a destination outside Canada, and
  - Only passengers or goods from a **designated holding area** were taken aboard during the trip in Canada.

- The individuals have a destination in Canada with a designated customs office, and:
  - Only passengers/goods from a holding area were taken aboard.
  - They present themselves **without delay** at the destination customs office (or nearest open one).

- The individuals are **transferred** under customs control to another conveyance:
  - For departure to a place outside Canada, and comply with the conditions in (2)(b)(i).
  - For departure to Canada, and comply with (2)(b)(ii).

- The conveyance arrives at a customs office where presentation can be made by **radio or telephone**:
  - The person in charge notifies the officer of arrival and lists passengers/crew.
  - If required, ensures those disembarking are transported to the specified time and place to present themselves.

> **Same applies to freight trains** — crew members are exempt from immediate transport if:
> - They arrive at a designated customs office.
> - The person in charge notifies the officer of arrival and crew details.
> - If required, ensures crew are transported to present themselves.

---

#### **5. Advance Notification Requirements (Section 12.1(1))**

For **non-commercial passenger conveyances** (excluding marine pleasure crafts), the person in charge must:
- Provide the **scheduled time and place of arrival** in Canada.
- If different, provide the **final destination and scheduled time** of arrival in Canada.

> This must be done **by telephone** to a designated customs office **between 2 and 48 hours before arrival**.

Additional obligations:
- If required by an officer, provide information about **other persons on board**.
- Notify the officer of the **actual arrival** of the conveyance.
- **Notify of any changes** to the original information **before arrival**, unless emergency circumstances exist — in which case, the change must be reported **at the time of arrival**.

---

#### **6. Authorization Process**

The Minister may issue an **authorization** to allow a person to present themselves in an **alternative manner** (e.g., by phone, biometric data, or remote presentation) under specific conditions.

##### **A. Authorization for Paragraph 11(a)** *(Alternative presentation for non-commercial travelers)*

Eligibility criteria:
- Must be a **citizen or permanent resident of Canada** or the **United States**.
- Or a citizen of another country **if**:
  - They are part of a program in that country that allows alternative entry.
  - Canada has a **reciprocal arrangement** with that country (under paragraph 13(2)(a) of the *Canada Border Services Agency Act*).
- Must be of **good character**.
- Not **inadmissible** to Canada under the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act*.
- Provide **written consent** to the use of **biometric data** (e.g., photo, iris scan) for identification and verification.
- Provide **true, accurate, and complete information** in the application.
- Resided only in **Canada, the U.S., or a foreign country** (depending on status) during the **three years prior** to application.

> **Exceptions**:
> - Citizens of Canada or the U.S. are exempt from the residency requirement.
> - A child under 18 may apply on behalf of a parent who:
>   - Was adopted outside Canada (by a Canadian citizen/permanent resident) or born outside Canada to a Canadian citizen.
>   - Was adopted outside the U.S. (by a U.S. citizen/permanent resident) or born outside the U.S. to a U.S. citizen.
