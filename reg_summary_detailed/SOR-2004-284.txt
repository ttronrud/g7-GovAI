**Detailed Summary of the Radiocommunication Act (Subsection 4(1) and Paragraph 9(1)(b)) Exemption Order (Security, Safety and International Relations), No. 2004-1**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Issuance**
This exemption order was issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Industry**, under **subsection 3(2)a of the Radiocommunication Act**.  
- **Statutory Basis**: The order is grounded in the *Radiocommunication Act*, specifically subsection 3(2)a, which allows for temporary exemptions from certain provisions of the Act when necessary for national security, safety, or international relations.  
- **Reference to Statutes**:  
  - S.C. 2004, c. 7, s. 37(2): Provides the legal basis for the issuance of the exemption.  
  - S.C. 1989, c. 17, s. 2: Establishes the general framework for exemptions under the Act.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Order**
The primary purpose of this Order is to **grant a temporary exemption** from two key provisions of the *Radiocommunication Act*:
- **Subsection 4(1)**: Prohibits the intentional interference with or obstruction of radiocommunications without authorization.
- **Paragraph 9(1)(b)**: Prohibits the use of radio apparatus to interfere with or obstruct radiocommunications, except under specific, authorized circumstances.

The exemption is granted **for security, safety, or international relations** purposes ‚Äî a narrow and high-level justification requiring exceptional justification.

### **4. Conditions for Exemption**

#### **(a) Subsection 4(1) Exemption**
- Applies **only if** the radio apparatus is used to:
  - **Interfere with or obstruct** a radiocommunication,
  - **In accordance with subsection 4(2)** of this Order,
  - And **for the purpose of security, safety, or international relations**.

#### **(b) Paragraph 9(1)(b) Exemption**
- Applies **only if** the interference or obstruction is:
  - Done for the purposes of **security, safety, or international relations**.

> üîç **Key Point**: The exemption is **not a blanket permission** to interfere with radio signals. It is strictly tied to specific, legitimate national interests.

To ensure responsible use and minimize unintended harm:
- **Every reasonable effort must be made** to:
  - **Confining interference** to the **smallest possible physical area**,
  - Limiting the number of **affected frequencies**,
  - Reducing the **duration** of the interference to the **absolute minimum** required to achieve the stated objective.

> ‚úÖ This principle reflects a **proportionality requirement**, ensuring that the government does not overreach or cause unnecessary disruption to legitimate radio services (e.g., emergency communications, aviation, or public broadcasting).
