### ðŸ” **Overview**

This regulation was introduced by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Employment and Immigration**, under **sections 11* and 17** of the *Canada Student Loans Act (CSLA)*. It revoked the outdated 1981 regulations and replaced them with updated rules governing eligibility, loan terms, repayment, and special circumstances for students receiving government-backed student loans.

The new regulations came into force on **August 1, 1993**, and were designed to:
- Clarify student status (full-time vs. part-time),
- Improve access to loans for students with disabilities,
- Introduce provisions for medical and parental leaves,
- Provide flexibility for borrowers who temporarily interrupt studies,
- Ensure fair and consistent treatment across provinces and institutions.

> âš ï¸ **Note**: These regulations were **fully repealed on April 12, 2023**, under *SOR/2023-273, s. 2*, meaning they are no longer in force. This summary reflects their historical content and intent as of their active period.

### 1. **How to Become or Remain a Full-Time Student**
A borrower must meet **all of the following conditions** to continue or regain full-time student status:

- **Obtain signature** of:
  - An officer of the educational institution, **or**
  - The provincial authority (if authorized by the institution and notified to the Minister).
- **Sign the consent and certification** portion of the confirmation of enrolment, ratifying any loans made while a minor.
- **Submit confirmation of enrolment** to the lender holding the borrowerâ€™s outstanding loans **within 30 days** of signing â€” **before the end of the confirmed period**.
- **Pay accrued interest**:
  - If more than **six months** have passed since the borrower last ceased being a full-time student, interest must be paid **to the day before the confirmed period begins**.
  - If submitted **between six and 12 months after ceasing full-time status**, the borrower must either:
    - Pay interest from the date of cessation, **or**
    - Enter into a **consolidated guaranteed student loan agreement** in which the unpaid interest is added to the principal.

> - The borrower **again becomes a full-time student** on the day they meet the requirements.
> - They **continue to be a full-time student** beyond the date they would otherwise have ceased â€” **unless otherwise specified**.

## ðŸ¥ Medical and Parental Leave Provisions (Major Update)

### 1. **Period of Postponement of Repayment**
A borrower who ceases to be a full-time student due to:
- A **medical leave** (due to a medical condition significantly interfering with studies), or  
- A **parental leave** (due to birth, adoption, or guardianship of a child)

**May apply** to the Minister for a **postponement of repayment**.

#### Application Requirements:
- Must be submitted **within six months after the end of the most recent period of studies**, but **no later than 12 months after**:
  - For medical leave: the date specified by the medical professional.
  - For parental leave: the date of birth, adoption, or becoming guardian/tutor.

#### Outcome:
- If approved, the borrower is **deemed to remain a full-time student** for a **six-month period** beginning on the **first day of the month after** the month they would have otherwise ceased being a full-time student.

#### Extensions:
- **After 6 months**: Can request extension to **12 months** (if leave is extended or new leave is granted).
- **After 12 months**: Can request extension to **18 months** (if leave is further extended or new leave is granted).
- Requests must be submitted:
  - No earlier than **30 days before** the end of the current period.
  - No later than **30 days after** the period ends.

> âš ï¸ **Restriction**: A borrower **cannot apply for a new period of postponement** within **30 days** of the start of the current confirmed period.
