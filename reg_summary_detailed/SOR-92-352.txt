## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Protection of Assets (Banks) Regulations, S.C. 1991, c. 46**

### **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**
- These regulations are designed to **protect and maintain the assets of banks** by establishing clear procedures and safeguards for the handling, custody, registration, and insurance of securities and other financial assets.
- They are made under **paragraph 559(g) of the Bank Act**, which empowers the Governor in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance) to create rules to protect bank assets.
- The regulations aim to ensure **financial integrity, accountability, and risk mitigation** in the operations of banks, particularly with respect to securities.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Bond** | A contract of insurance under which one party agrees to indemnify another for losses arising from the act of a third party (e.g., fraud or theft by an employee). |
| **Security** | A negotiable security owned or held by the bank, or held in trust by the bank. <br> ‚ùå Does **not** include securities issued **by** the bank itself. |
| **Security Transaction** | Any purchase, sale, redemption, exchange, transfer, assignment, or other transaction involving a security. |

> *Note: The term "security" is narrowly defined to exclude bank-issued instruments, focusing on external securities like bonds, stocks, or debentures.*

---

### **3. Scope and Exemptions**

The regulations **do not apply** in the following situations:

1. **Where a bank operates in a jurisdiction** and complies with local laws that are **inconsistent** with these regulations.
2. **Where the bank is fulfilling duties** related to:
   - Administration of an estate or trust; or
   - Acting as an agent (e.g., for clients in settlement or custody roles).

> This ensures flexibility for banks operating under different legal frameworks or performing non-core, fiduciary functions.

---

### **4. Record-Keeping Requirements**

#### (a) Procedures for Handling Assets
- A bank must maintain a **written record** of procedures for:
  - Handling and safeguarding its assets (e.g., physical storage, access controls, internal approvals).
- This record must be **shared with all directors, officers, employees, and agents** who have access to or involvement in asset handling.

> Purpose: Ensures transparency, accountability, and consistency in internal controls.

#### (b) Identification of All Securities
- The bank must maintain an **up-to-date record** that identifies **every security** it owns or holds.

> Ensures traceability and reduces risk of loss or misappropriation.

---

### **5. Custody of Securities**

#### General Rule:
- Every security must be kept **securely** and **prevents unauthorized access**, and must be in the custody of:
  - The **bank itself**, or
  - An **authorized entity** such as:
    - A **custodian of securities**
    - A **depository**
    - A **clearing agency**

> This ensures that assets are not left in unsecured or unregulated hands.

#### Exceptions (Where Custody is Not Required):
The rule does **not apply** to securities that are:
- Under the **control of a government** in the jurisdiction where the bank operates;
- **Pledged** as collateral for the bank‚Äôs debt or potential debt;
- **Loaned** to a person under a written agreement;
- In **transit** (e.g., being transported between locations).

> These exceptions recognize legitimate business activities and government oversight.

#### Written Custodial Agreement Requirement:
- A bank **must enter into a written custodial agreement** before placing any security with an authorized third party (e.g., depository or custodian).

> Ensures legal accountability and clarity of responsibilities.

---

### **6. Daily Deposit of Net Proceeds from Security Transactions**

A bank must **deposit daily** any net amount received from a security transaction into one of the following accounts:

1. An account **kept by the bank itself**;
2. With a **financial institution authorized** to accept deposits in the jurisdiction where it operates;
3. With a **trust company authorized** to hold money in trust in that jurisdiction;
4. With the **government** of the jurisdiction or an agency thereof authorized to act as a securities custodian;
5. With **CDS Clearing and Depository Services Inc.** (a major Canadian securities depository).

> Purpose: Prevents the accumulation of unmonitored cash and reduces liquidity risk or opportunities for misappropriation.

---

### **7. Registration of Securities**

#### General Rule:
- Every security must be **registered in the bank‚Äôs name** in the register of the issuing entity (e.g., the issuer of a bond or stock).

> This ensures legal ownership and facilitates tracking and transfer.

#### Exceptions:
The rule does **not apply** to securities that:
- Cannot be registered due to reasons **beyond the bank‚Äôs control** (e.g., technical or legal limitations);
- Are under government control;
- Are held as **collateral** or for **safekeeping**;
- Are registered in the name of a **nominee** (e.g., a designated representative);
- Are held under a **book-entry, certificateless, or immobilized system** (e.g., electronic systems where physical certificates are not issued);
- Are held temporarily by an agent, liquidator, trustee, or issuer for purposes such as:
  - Reorganization
  - Amalgamation
  - Liquidation
  - Voting

> Recognizes modern, electronic, and procedural realities in securities management.

---

### **8. Holding Securities in Bearer or Non-Bank Name**

If a security is otherwise **adequately protected** against:
- Loss
- Fraud
- Theft
- Destruction

Then the bank may:
- Hold the security in **bearer form** (where ownership is determined by physical possession), or
- Hold it in **registered form in a name other than the bank‚Äôs name** (e.g., under a nominee).

> This allows flexibility in certain secure environments (e.g., with electronic safeguards or trusted third-party custody), but only if the security is sufficiently protected.

---

### **9. Insurance Requirements**

#### (a) Insurance for Dishonest or Criminal Acts
- A bank must **acquire and maintain** one or more **bonds** (insurance policies) to indemnify the bank against losses arising from:
  - **Dishonest or criminal acts** by bank officers or employees.

> This protects the bank from internal fraud (e.g., embezzlement, misappropriation).

#### (b) Insurance for Physical Damage or Loss
- The bank must also maintain **insurance policies** to cover:
  - Damage to, destruction of, or mysterious disappearance of its assets;
  - Any other usual contingencies (e.g., fire, natural disaster, theft).

> Broad coverage to protect against both internal and external risks.

---

### **10. Conditions on Insurance Policies and Bonds**

#### (a) No Termination Without Notice
- Any bond or insurance policy acquired under section 10 **must not be cancelled or terminated** until **at least 30 days** after:
  - The **Superintendent of Financial Institutions** receives a written notice from either:
    - The insurer, or
    - The bank (insured)

> This provides a **cooling-off period** and allows time for review, audit, or appeal ‚Äî protecting the bank from sudden loss of coverage.

#### (b) Adequate Coverage Amount
- The amount of the bond or insurance policy must be determined by the **bank‚Äôs directors**, based on:
  - The **nature and value** of the assets owned or held;
  - The **handling and safeguarding procedures** in place;
  - Any other factors that could affect the **potential extent of loss**.

> Ensures coverage is proportionate and realistic, avoiding both under-insurance and excessive costs.

| Principle | Description |

| **Custody** | Assets must be held securely, either by the bank or an authorized third party (with written agreements). |
| **Accountability** | Daily deposits of net proceeds ensure liquidity and reduce risk of misuse. |
| **Registration** | Most securities must be registered in the bank‚Äôs name, with limited exceptions for electronic or temporary holdings. |
