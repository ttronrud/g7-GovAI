**Detailed Summary of the General Adjustment Assistance Regulations**

---

### **Overview**

The *General Adjustment Assistance Regulations* establish a comprehensive framework for providing financial assistance to Canadian manufacturers and other eligible entities facing economic challenges due to international trade competition, market disruptions, or structural changes. These regulations are administered by the **General Adjustment Assistance Board (Board)**, which acts on behalf of Her Majesty (the Crown) to offer a range of financial tools including **loans, loan insurance, grants for consulting assistance, and stock options**.

The primary objective of these regulations is to **protect Canadian industry from serious injury caused by foreign competition or adverse trade conditions**, while also supporting **industrial restructuring, innovation, and market access** through targeted financial support.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Board** | A 7-member body comprising 3 non-Public Service members (including a Chairman and Vice-Chairman) and 4 Public Service members appointed by the Governor in Council—each representing a key federal ministry (Industry, Finance, Employment, Immigration, Regional Economic Expansion). |
| **Consulting Assistance** | Technical or professional advice provided by a consultant acceptable to the Board, aimed at improving operational efficiency, product development, or market strategy. |
| **Letter of Credit (LC)** | A financial instrument issued by a private lender before January 1, 1978, to secure or guarantee trade transactions. |
| **Manufacturer** | Any person, firm, or corporation in Canada engaged in a manufacturing or processing activity. |
| **Manufacturing or Processing Activity** | Activities involving transformation of raw materials into finished goods through significant chemical, biochemical, or physical changes (excluding growth or decay). |
| **Private Lender** | A lender approved by the Board, excluding government entities (federal, provincial, municipal) or agencies effectively controlled by them. |
| **Restructure/Restructuring** | A significant change in operations—such as product lines, production methods, markets, or management—that may include acquisition of machinery, working capital, or facilities. |
| **Stock Option** | An agreement under which the Crown acquires an unconditional, assignable right to purchase shares in a corporation at specified terms. |

---

## **Structure and Composition of the Board**

- **Total Members**: 7
  - **3 Non-Public Service Members**: Appointed by the Governor in Council; one designated as **Chairman**, one as **Vice-Chairman**.
  - **4 Public Service Members**: One appointed by each of the following Ministers:
    - Minister of Industry, Trade and Commerce
    - Minister of Finance
    - Minister of Employment and Immigration
    - Minister of Regional Economic Expansion
- **Quorum**: The Chairman and any other three members constitute a quorum.
- **Vice-Chairman Powers**: Can act as Chairman in absence or incapacity; may also act at the Chairman’s request.
- **Ministerial Designees**: If a designated member is absent or unable to act, a senior official from their department steps in.
- **Compensation**: The Governor in Council determines emoluments and expenses for non-Public Service members.

---

## **Core Functions and Authorities of the Board**

### 1. **Administration of Financial Instruments**
The Board administers:
- Insurance on loans and letters of credit
- Grants for consulting assistance
- Stock options

### 2. **Use of Government Resources**
- Utilizes existing staff and facilities of the Department of Industry, Trade and Commerce.
- May access other Government of Canada services when necessary.

### 3. **Establishment of an Executive Committee**
- Composed of **three members** of the Board:
  - Chairman or Vice-Chairman
  - Member designated by the Minister of Industry, Trade and Commerce
  - Member designated by the Minister of Finance
- Powers include:
  - Determining eligibility for insurance or loans
  - Authorizing loans up to **$250,000**
  - Authorizing insurance of loans up to **$250,000**
  - Calling loans in case of default
  - Consent to private lenders calling loans
  - Directing private lenders to take protective actions
  - Authorizing amendments to securities to protect Crown interests
  - Approving grants for consulting assistance

> *Note: This committee serves as a decision-making body for specific, high-volume decisions with limited risk exposure.*

## **Eligibility and Types of Assistance**

### **A. Loans to Manufacturers for Restructuring**
A loan may be granted if:
1. The manufacturer:
   - Faces serious injury or threat of injury due to **foreign competition** (in terms of price, quantity, or conditions)
   - Is unable to secure financing on reasonable terms
2. The loan is intended to assist in **restructuring operations** (e.g., new production methods, market expansion, improved competitiveness).

> *Loan amount, term, and interest rate are determined by the Board.*

---

### **B. Loans for Adjusting to Foreign Market Changes**
A loan may be granted if:
1. The manufacturer:
   - Must adjust to changes in access to foreign markets caused by:
     - A **temporary import surtax** imposed by a non-Canadian country
     - Other actions (e.g., trade barriers) with similar effects
   - Is unable to obtain financing on reasonable terms
2. The loan supports adaptation to these external conditions.

---

### **C. Loans to Trustees or Successors**
If a borrower has been placed under legal supervision (e.g., trustee, receiver), the Board may:
- Provide a loan to that trustee/manager to **protect Crown interests in collateral** securing prior loans or LCs.

---

### **D. Loans to Borrowers for Protection of Assets**
If a loan or LC has already been made or insured, the Board may provide a **new loan** to the original borrower to:
- Protect the Crown’s interest in assets securing the prior loan or LC.

---

### **E. Complementary Financing (Loan-to-Loan Scheme)**
When a private lender offers a loan (up to **$200,000**) and the Board authorizes insurance on it:
- The Board may provide a **second loan** (up to **50% of the insured loan amount**) under the following conditions:
  1. The private lender must agree to the terms of the original loan.
  2. The interest rate on the Board loan must be **at least equal to the sum** of:
     - The interest charged by the private lender
     - The insurance fee on the loan
  3. The loan is only allowed if it is **essential** to prevent serious delays in project implementation.
  4. The loan must be repaid:
     - On the **first disbursement** by the private lender, or
     - On an earlier date as determined by the Board.
  5. If disbursement is delayed, the Board specifies a **repayment date** within a reasonable timeframe.

> *Purpose: To ensure timely project implementation without relying solely on private financing.*

## **Insurance of Loans and Letters of Credit**

The Board may provide insurance on loans or LCs under several circumstances:

| Scenario | Conditions |
|--------|-----------|
| **1. New Opportunities or Restructuring** | Manufacturer seeks to take advantage of a new production opportunity or improve competitiveness; unable to secure financing without insurance. |
| **2. Foreign Market Disruptions** | Facing foreign trade barriers (e.g., import surtax) or competition causing injury; cannot obtain financing. |
| **3. Service Providers** | Entities providing services (marketing, finance, etc.) to manufacturers need financing to improve Canadian manufacturing competitiveness. |
| **4. Contractors (Outside Canada)** | General contractors, engineers, builders, architects, or manufacturers require LCs as bid/performance guarantees for international contracts involving **significant Canadian content**. |
| **5. Repayment of LC Obligations** | A contractor needs a loan to repay financial obligations from a previously insured LC. |

> **Maximum Insurance Coverage**: The Board may insure loans or LCs up to **90% of the total loan amount**.

> **Canadian Content Requirement**: In cases involving international contracts, the amount of Canadian content must be **equal to or greater than** the amount of insurance provided.

## **Stock Options – Acquisition and Exercise**

- The Board may acquire a **stock option** as a condition of a loan.
- The option may be exercised or assigned **only if**:
  1. The value of the corporation’s capital stock has increased due to assistance under these regulations.
  2. Exercise would allow Her Majesty to benefit from the increased value.
- Upon acquisition, the Board must:
  - Sell or dispose of the capital stock **as soon as economically beneficial**.
  - Notify the original option holder (the person who received the option) of any bona fide offer to buy the shares.
  - Allow that person to **purchase the shares within 7 business days** at the same price and terms as the offer.
- If the Board intends to assign the option, it must:
  - Notify the original holder.
  - Give them **7 business days** to purchase the option at the same terms.

> *This ensures fair treatment of stakeholders and protects private interests.*

## **Loan Terms and Conditions**

- **Interest Rate**:
  - Must be **within a prescribed range**:
    - Not less than the rate charged by the Government of Canada to crown corporations for similar loans.
    - Not more than **2.25% above** that rate.
  - Determined by the Board at the time of approval, based on circumstances.

- **Loan Term**:
  - Maximum term of **20 years**.
