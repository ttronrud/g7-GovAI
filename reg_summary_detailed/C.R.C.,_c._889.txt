### ðŸ”¹ III. **Official Sample Requirements and Retention**

- **Retention period**:
  - **Official samples**: Must be retained from the day of grading for **not less than 6 months and not more than 120 months**.
  - **Unofficial samples**: Must be retained for **at least 20 days** after grading.

- **Purpose of retention**:
  - To allow for potential appeals, re-inspections, or resale via public tender after the retention period expires.

- **Resale of samples**:
  - Official and unofficial samples must be **sold by public tender within a reasonable time** after the required retention period ends.
  - If **no tender is received**, the sample is **discarded**.

### ðŸ”¹ V. **Inspection Certificates**

- **Grain inspection certificates** must be issued in standardized forms:
  - **On discharge from terminal elevators**: Use *Certificate Final for Grain*.
  - **All other grain**: Use *Certificate for Grain*.
  - Both forms are published and amended by the Commission.

- **Appeals under section 39**:
  - An appeal lies in respect of grain officially inspected at a **primary elevator being transferred to a terminal elevator**.
  - The Chief Grain Inspector must issue a decision **without delay**.
  - If the appeal results in a different grade, the **inspection certificate is revised with the date of the appeal decision**.

### ðŸ”¹ VI. **Exemptions from Licensing Requirements**

Certain types of grain handling operations are **exempt from licensing** under paragraph 117(a) of the Act if the following conditions are met:

1. **Feed Mill Elevator**:
   - Constructed to handle and store grain as part of a feed mill operation.

2. **Process Elevator**:
   - Does **not purchase grain from producers**.
   - Does **not assume payment obligations** to producers.
   - Allows **Commission access to records**.

3. **Seed Cleaning Plant Elevator**:
   - Handles grain **only for seed purposes**.
   - Does not operate for grain storage or purchase outside of seed use.
   - Allows Commission access to records.

4. **Agent/Mandatary Elevator**:
   - Operates only as an agent or mandatary for licensees.
   - Licensees provide **security to the Commission** for all grain transactions.
   - Allows Commission access to records.

5. **Grain Dealer Exemptions** (for western grain):
   - Does **not deal in or handle western grain** except:
     - As an agent/mandatary for licensees (with licensee security).
     - For seed purposes.
     - Without purchasing from producers or assuming payment obligations.

### ðŸ”¹ VII. **Licensing Conditions and Operational Requirements**

Every licensee operating an elevator must:

1. **Store grain only in the building described in the license application**.
2. **Notify the Commission in writing** within **15 days** of:
   - Any functional alteration to the building or grain flow systems.
   - Any addition to equipment.
3. **Notify immediately** (without delay) of:
   - Damage or destruction of the building or required equipment.
   - Damage or destruction of stored grain.
4. **Maintain good repair and working order** of:
   - Elevator buildings and all associated equipment.
   - Sampling and weighing equipment and surrounding areas (must be clean and accessible).
5. **Post the license** in a conspicuous place in the elevator.

### ðŸ”¹ IX. **Security Requirements**
