**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:  
Tax Court of Canada Rules of Procedure Respecting the Excise Tax Act (Informal Procedure)**

---

### **1. Introduction and Legal Basis**

This regulation establishes the **Tax Court of Canada Rules of Procedure Respecting the Excise Tax Act (Informal Procedure)**, which governs the conduct of appeals under **Part IX of the Excise Tax Act** that are brought through the **informal procedure** pathway. The rules were:

- **Proposed** on August 3, 1991, in *Canada Gazette, Part I*, pursuant to **subsection 22(3)** of the *Tax Court of Canada Act*.
- **Published for public comment**, allowing interested parties to submit representations to the Rules Committee.
- **Approved by the Governor in Council** on October 22, 1991, under **section 20(1)** of the *Tax Court of Canada Act*, which empowers the Court’s Rules Committee to draft procedural rules subject to Cabinet approval.

These rules came into force upon **registration under the Statutory Instruments Act** and are now binding on all parties involved in appeals under the Excise Tax Act using the informal procedure.

---

### **2. Scope and Applicability**

- **Applies to appeals** under **Part IX of the Excise Tax Act**, specifically those that do **not fall under the general procedure rules**.
- **Excludes** appeals that are governed by the *Tax Court of Canada Rules (General Procedure)*.
- The rules apply to **all procedural matters** in such informal appeals, including filing, service, evidence, hearings, costs, and appeals from decisions.

---

### **3. Judicial Authority and Powers**

- **Associate Judges** have **full judicial powers** equivalent to those of a full judge under these rules.
- An **associate judge may sit anywhere in Canada** at any time and, when so sitting, **constitutes the full Court** for the purposes of the proceeding.

---

### **4. Filing of Documents**

#### **Methods of Filing**
A party may file documents using **one of the following methods**:
- Deposit with the **Registry**;
- Mail to the Registry;
- Fax to the Registry;
- **Electronic filing** via the Court’s website (**www.tcc-cci.gc.ca**) or any other site designated by the Court.

> *Note: Electronic filing is increasingly encouraged and recognized as a valid method.*

#### **Date of Filing**
- For **paper filings** (mail, fax, deposit): The date is the **date shown on the receipt stamp** issued by the Registry.
- For **electronic filings**: The date is the **date shown on the acknowledgment of receipt** issued by the Court.

#### **Original Version of Document**
- When filed electronically, the **printed copy** provided by the Registry is deemed the **original version** of the document.

#### **Paper Copy Requirement**
- Any party filing electronically must provide a **paper copy** of the document if required by the rules or requested by a party or the Court.

#### **Failure to File**
- If the Registry has **no record of receipt**, the document is **deemed not filed**, unless the Court directs otherwise.

---

### **5. Notice of Appeal**

- Must include:
  - The **appellant’s address for service** of documents.
  - This address may be:
    - The appellant’s address;
    - The address of the appellant’s **counsel or agent**.

- A **written notice of any change** in address must be submitted **immediately** to the Registry. The new address becomes effective upon receipt.

- **Until changed**, service on the appellant is deemed valid if sent by **mail to the address in the notice of appeal**.

---

### **6. Reply to Notice of Appeal**

A reply must contain:
- Facts **admitted**, **denied**, or **in issue**;
- Findings or assumptions made by the **Minister** during the assessment;
- Any **other material facts**;
- The **issues to be decided**;
- **Statutory provisions** relied upon;
- **Reasons** for the respondent’s position;
- The **relief sought**.

> *The reply must be filed within five days of the notice of appeal being served.*

- The **Minister must serve a copy** of the reply by **registered mail** to the appellant’s address for service within five days of filing.

---

### **7. Expert Witnesses**

- A party intending to call an **expert witness** must file a **report at least 10 days before the hearing**.
- The report must include:
  - The expert’s **name, address, and qualifications**;
  - A **summary of the expert’s testimony**.
- An expert may **only testify with the leave of the presiding judge** if the report is filed.

---

### **8. Discontinuance of Appeal**

- Any party may **discontinue** an appeal at any time by submitting a **written notice** in the form set out in **Schedule 8**.

---

### **9. Disposition of Proceedings**

- The Court may:
  - **Render a judgment** on appeals involving **substantive rights** (e.g., whether the assessment is correct).
  - **Issue an order** on interlocutory or procedural matters (e.g., discovery, costs, scheduling).

- A **judgment** is dated on the day it is **signed** and that is the **date of pronouncement**.

- The **judgment and reasons** (if any) must be **deposited with the Registry without delay**.

---

### **10. Costs and Taxation**

#### **General Principles**
- The Court may **determine the amount of costs**, **allocate them**, and **identify who pays** them.

#### **Costs Awards**
- The Court may award **costs to the respondent** only if:
  - The appellant’s actions **unduly delayed** the resolution of the appeal.

- The Court may **direct a fixed sum** in lieu of taxed costs.

#### **Fees for Counsel Services**
The following fees may be allowed for **counsel services**:
- Preparation of notice of appeal or advice: **$185**
- Preparation for hearing: **$250**
- Conduct of hearing: **$375 per half-day or part thereof**
- Taxation of costs: **$60**

> *Note: These are "party and party costs" — i.e., costs incurred by both parties.*

#### **Advisor Services (Non-Counsel)**
- If an appellant is represented by an **advisor (not counsel)**, disbursements may be allowed **up to half** of the above amounts.
- Other disbursements (e.g., travel, translation) may be allowed if:
  - They were **essential** to the appeal;
  - They were **actually incurred** or the party is **liable** for them.

#### **Taxes on Counsel Fees**
- All **GST, PST, or other taxes** paid on counsel fees or disbursements may be allowed **if**:
  - The taxes were paid or are payable;
  - They are **not otherwise reimbursed** (e.g., via input tax credits).

#### **Witness Fees**
- **Non-expert witnesses**:
  - $75 per day + **reasonable transportation and living expenses**.
  - Payment **only if** the appellant is **called upon to testify** by the respondent’s counsel.

- **Expert witnesses**:
  - Up to **$300 per day** for preparation and testimony.
  - May be adjusted by the Court.

> *Note: The $300 expert fee was later repealed in 2004 (SOR/2004-103, s. 8), but the original version is included here as part of the historical regulation.*

---

### **11. Taxation of Costs**

- Costs are taxed by the **Registrar** or any person designated by the **Chief Justice** as a **taxing officer**.

- The **appellant must file a bill of costs** (in form set out in **Schedule 12**) with the Registrar.

- The Registrar sends a **true copy to the respondent’s counsel** immediately.

- After taxation, the Registrar issues a **certificate of taxation** to each party.

- A party may **appeal the taxation** within **20 days** of the mailing of the certificate.

> *The time period may be extended by a judge if justified.*

- **Costs may be awarded against a person** if the Court makes an order under **section 19.1 of the Excise Tax Act** (e.g., for non-compliance or fraud).

---

### **12. Election to Use Informal Procedure**

- A person who has **not elected** to use the informal procedure in their notice of appeal may **make such an election within 90 days** after the reply is served (or within a **shorter time** if the Court allows special reasons).

- The election must be in the form set out in **Schedule 15**.

> *This allows parties to choose the informal procedure after the initial filing, if they did not explicitly opt in.*

---

### **13. Applications to Extend Time to Object or Request**

Under specific circumstances, a taxpayer may apply to the Court to **extend time** to:
- File a **notice of objection** to an assessment;
- Make a **written request** to the Minister regarding an avoidance transaction.

#### **When an Application May Be Made**
- After the **Minister refuses** the application, or
- After **90 days** have passed from service of the application and the Minister has not responded.

> *However, no application may be made
