### **Detailed Summary of the National Energy Board Act Part VI (Oil and Gas) Regulations**

---

#### **1. Purpose and Legal Authority**
These regulations are made under the **National Energy Board Act (Act)**, specifically pursuant to **subsections 119.01(1)** and **130(2)**. They implement the provisions of **Division I of Part VI** of the Act, which governs the **exportation and importation of oil and gas** in Canada.

- The regulations are officially titled:  
  **National Energy Board Act Part VI (Oil and Gas) Regulations**  
  (Cited as S.C. 1990, c. 7, s. 34)

- The **Governor in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Natural Resources**, has issued these regulations to ensure that Canadaâ€™s energy exports and imports are conducted in a manner that protects national energy security, environmental integrity, and market stability.

#### **3. Applicability of Procedures**
- **Part I of the National Energy Board Rules of Practice and Procedure (1995)** applies to all applications and procedures related to:
  - Applying for a licence or order
  - Issuing a licence or order

This ensures procedural fairness, transparency, and consistency in decision-making.

---

#### **4. Approval Requirements for Licences**
The **Governor in Council must approve** any licence for the following activities:
- Exportation of **gas**
- Importation of **gas**
- Exportation of **heavy crude oil**
- Exportation of **oil other than heavy crude oil**

> âŒ *Note*: This requirement underscores the high level of governmental oversight for major energy trade flows.

---

#### **5. Suspension and Revocation of Orders**
The Board may:
- **Suspend** an order if a term or condition has been **violated** or **not complied with**.
- **Revoke** an order if the holder **refuses to comply** with conditions or fails to comply when lifting a suspension.

**Procedure**:
- Before suspending or revoking, the Board must:
  - Send a **written notice** to the holder specifying the violation.
  - Provide the holder with an **opportunity to be heard**.

> âœ… Exception: The Board may suspend or revoke an order **on the application or with the consent of the holder**, allowing flexibility in urgent cases.

---

#### **6. Inspection Powers**
The Board or authorized personnel may:
- Enter any premises where oil or gas is produced, recovered, exported, or imported.
- Inspect instruments, equipment, records, books, and accounts related to export/import operations.
- Conduct tests necessary for inspection.

**Requirements**:
- Authorized individuals must **produce written authorization** when requested.
- Operators or persons in charge of premises must **permit and assist** inspections.

> This ensures compliance with regulatory standards and accountability in energy trade operations.

#### **8. Definition of Natural Gas**
For the purposes of **subsection 119.01(1.1)** of the Act:
- **Natural gas** is a mixture containing **at least 85% methane**.
- May contain other hydrocarbons that are **gaseous at 15Â°C and 101.325 kPa**.
- May contain minor non-hydrocarbon gases and impurities.

#### **9. Application Requirements for Licences**

##### **For Exportation of Gas**
Applicants must provide comprehensive information to the Board, including:

1. **Terms of the Licence Request**:
   - Duration of the licence
   - Maximum daily, annual, and term quantities
   - Tolerance levels for temporary operations
   - Points of exportation

2. **Gas Supply Information**:
   - Summary of contracted or owned gas volumes (daily/annual), reserves, and termination dates
   - Copies of **pro forma contracts** for each type of purchase

3. **Market Information**:
   - Copy of every **gas export sales contract**
   - Detailed summary of terms and conditions (including Schedule I format)
   - Name of person to whom questions about contracts may be directed
   - Description of the export market to be served

4. **Supply and Demand Balance**:
   - If the gas comes from a **non-dedicated pool**, a balance must be shown on both aggregate and annual bases for the duration of exportation, identifying all firm commitments.

5. **Transportation Arrangements**:
   - Details of contracts for movement of gas in/out of Canada
   - Copies of all transportation contracts
   - Description of existing or proposed gathering, storage, or transmission facilities (or new facilities)

6. **Environmental and Social Effects**:
   - Assessment of environmental and social impacts directly related to the export

7. **Market Impact Assessment**:
   - Evaluation of whether Canadians would face difficulty in meeting energy needs at **fair market prices**

8. **Regulatory Approvals**:
   - Status of federal, provincial, or state approvals for:
     - Removal of gas from a province
     - Importation into destination country
     - Transportation services
     - Tariffs/tolls
     - Facilities
     - Environmental reviews
     - Contractual arrangements

9. **Status Sheet**:
   - A summary of all contractual arrangements and regulatory approvals, in the form specified in **Schedule II**

---

##### **For Importation of Gas**
Requirements are **similar**, with adjustments:

1. **Terms of Licence Request**:
   - Duration
   - Maximum daily, annual, term quantities
   - Tolerance levels
   - Points of importation

2. **Gas Supply Information**:
   - Summary of purchase contracts (daily/annual volumes, reserves, termination dates)
   - Copies of pro forma contracts

3. **Market Information**:
   - Copy of every **gas import purchase contract**
   - Detailed summary of terms and conditions
   - Description of market to be served

4. **Transportation Arrangements**:
   - Same as above

5. **Environmental and Social Effects**:
   - Assessment of impacts

6. **Regulatory Approvals**:
   - For:
     - Removal of gas from country of origin
     - Import into a province
     - Transportation
     - Tariffs/tolls
     - Facilities
     - Environmental reviews
     - Contractual arrangements

> All applications must demonstrate **feasibility, market viability, and minimal negative impact on domestic energy markets**.
