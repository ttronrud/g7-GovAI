This is a **bilateral social security agreement** between the **Government of Canada** and the **Government of the Republic of Chile**, signed on **November 18, 1996**, and formally entered into force on **June 1, 1998**. The agreement establishes reciprocal rights and obligations for citizens and residents of both countries regarding pensions, disability benefits, survivor benefits, and related social security entitlements.

It is designed to **prevent double taxation, reduce administrative burdens, and ensure fair access to benefits** for individuals who work or reside in both countries, particularly those who are not nationals of either country.

The agreement is grounded in **Article XXIX of the Agreement on Social Security** and was implemented through **Order in Council P.C. 1998-667**, which issued a formal proclamation to make the agreement legally effective.
