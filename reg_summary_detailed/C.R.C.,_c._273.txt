**Detailed Summary of the *Saskatchewan Hog Licensing (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations***

---

### **1. Regulation Title and Citation**
These regulations are officially known as the **Saskatchewan Hog Licensing (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations**. They serve to govern the licensing and operational standards for individuals and entities involved in the **interprovincial and export trade of hogs** originating from Saskatchewan.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

The regulations define several key terms to ensure clarity and consistency in application:

- **Act**: Refers to *The Natural Products Marketing Act, 1972* of Saskatchewan. This foundational legislation provides the legal framework under which the regulations operate.
  
- **Assembly Yard**: A designated premises where producers deliver their hogs for temporary holding, grouping, or auction. These are central to the marketing process.

- **Assembly Yard Operator**: The owner or operator of an assembly yard. Must meet specific operational and safety standards.

- **Commission**: The *Saskatchewan Hog Marketing Commission*, established under the Act and the *Saskatchewan Hog Marketing Plan*. The Commission is responsible for administering the licensing system, enforcing compliance, and overseeing the hog marketing process.

- **Hog**: Any member of the species *Sus scrofa L.* (domestic pig) that is produced in Saskatchewan. This includes all stages of the pig from birth to slaughter.

- **Order Buyer**: A person who purchases sows at a public auction in an assembly yard with the intent to resell them to another party or on their behalf. This role is distinct from general purchasing and involves resale activities.

- **Plan**: The *Saskatchewan Hog Marketing Plan*, which is established and periodically amended under the Act. It outlines the overall structure, goals, and mechanisms for hog marketing, including pricing, distribution, and market access.

- **Processor**: Any individual or entity engaged in processing hogs (e.g., slaughter, rendering) or using hogs in manufacturing processes.

- **Producer**: Includes:
  - Persons directly involved in producing or feeding hogs and making them available for slaughter;
  - Leaseholders or agreement holders who are entitled to a share of the hogs produced;
  - Individuals who take possession of hogs under a security agreement (e.g., for a debt).

- **Purchaser**: Any person buying hogs in Saskatchewan who is **not** a producer, processor, consumer, or a livestock dealer licensed in Saskatchewan. This includes order buyers.

- **Trucker**: A person who operates a vehicle for the continued commercial transport of hogs. This excludes occasional or non-commercial transport.

---

### **3. Scope of Application**
These regulations apply **only** to:
- The **marketing of hogs** in **interprovincial and export trade**;
- Persons and property located within the **Province of Saskatchewan**.

This means the rules do not extend to domestic consumption, private sales, or non-commercial activities within the province.

---

### **4. Licensing Requirement**
No individual or entity may legally act as one of the following **regulated roles** without holding a valid licence issued by the **Saskatchewan Hog Marketing Commission**:

- Purchaser  
- Processor  
- Assembly yard operator  
- Truckers  

This licensing requirement ensures accountability, transparency, and financial responsibility in the hog marketing chain.

---

### **5. Licence Expiry**
All licences issued under these regulations will **expire on December 31st of the year following the date of issue**.  
For example, a licence issued on January 1, 2025, will expire on December 31, 2026.

This creates a periodic renewal cycle, ensuring that all licensees remain active and compliant with current standards.

---

### **6. Licence Fees**
The fees for issuing licences are tiered based on the type of licence:

| Role | Fee |
|------|-----|
| **Purchaser** | $20 |
| **Processor** | $20 per processing plant (facility) |
| **Assembly Yard Operator** | $20 per assembly yard |
| **Trucker** | $10 per vehicle |

These fees are designed to generate revenue for the Commission to support the operation and oversight of the hog marketing system.

---

### **7. Financial Responsibility and Bonding**
To obtain a licence, each applicant must:
- Be **properly bonded**; and  
- Provide **adequate proof of financial responsibility** to the Commission.

This requirement ensures that licensees can meet their financial obligations, such as covering losses due to accidents, disease outbreaks, or market fluctuations, and protects both producers and the broader market.

---

### **8. Minimum Standards for Assembly Yards**
A licence for an **assembly yard operator** is only issued if each proposed assembly yard meets the following **mandatory physical and operational standards**:

1. **Protection from Wind and Sun**: Adequate shelter to protect hogs from extreme weather conditions.
2. **Concrete Floor**: A solid, non-slip concrete surface to prevent injuries and ensure hygiene.
3. **Bedding**: Sufficient and appropriate bedding material (e.g., straw, wood shavings) to maintain animal comfort and health.
4. **Water Availability**: Access to clean, readily available water at all times.
5. **Adequate Pens**: Sufficient space and design to house hogs safely and humanely, based on animal welfare standards.
6. **Loading Facilities**: Loading areas that are functional and safe under **all weather conditions** (e.g., snow, rain, wind).
7. **Tattooing Equipment**: Equipment capable of producing **clear, legible tattoos** on hogs for identification and traceability purposes.

These standards are critical for:
- Ensuring animal welfare;
- Facilitating traceability in case of disease outbreaks;
- Enabling accurate tracking of hogs from farm to market;
- Supporting compliance with food safety and marketing regulations.

---

### **Key Objectives of the Regulations**

1. **Market Integrity**: Prevents unregulated or unscrupulous actors from entering the interprovincial and export hog trade.
2. **Traceability and Accountability**: Through licensing, bonding, and tattooing, the system ensures that hogs can be traced from origin to destination.
3. **Animal Welfare**: Mandates safe, humane, and hygienic conditions in assembly yards.
4. **Financial Stability**: Requires proof of financial responsibility to mitigate risks to producers and consumers.
5. **Transparency and Fair Trade**: Limits market manipulation and ensures fair access to the interprovincial and export markets.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Saskatchewan Hog Licensing (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations* establish a comprehensive, structured, and accountable framework for the movement and marketing of hogs from Saskatchewan. By requiring licensing, setting clear operational standards, and enforcing financial responsibility, the regulations promote fair trade practices, protect animal welfare, ensure market transparency, and safeguard the interests of producers, processors, and consumers. The system is specifically tailored to the unique needs of interprovincial and export markets, where traceability, reliability, and compliance are essential.

These regulations are a vital component of the broader *Saskatchewan Hog Marketing Plan* and operate under the authority of *The Natural Products Marketing Act, 1972*.
