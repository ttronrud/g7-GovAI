**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Regulation:**  
S.C. 2019, c. 29, s. 292  
**Title:** Designation of the Minister of Citizenship and Immigration under the *College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants Act*

---

**Summary:**

This regulation, enacted under the authority of the *College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants Act*, formally designates the **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration** (a federal minister of the Canadian government) as the official "Minister referred to in that Act."

---

**Key Provisions and Context:**

1. **Legal Authority:**  
   The regulation is issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Prime Minister**. This reflects the standard constitutional and administrative process in Canada where federal ministers are appointed through the Crown-in-Council, with the Prime Minister’s recommendation providing political and policy direction.

2. **Governing Legislation:**  
   The regulation operates under **section 3 of the College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants Act**. This section typically establishes the framework for the appointment or designation of a minister responsible for overseeing the College, which is a regulatory body that governs professionals involved in immigration and citizenship services (such as consultants and advisors).

3. **Purpose of the Designation:**  
   The primary purpose is to **identify and assign official responsibility** to a specific federal minister for the administration, oversight, and policy direction of the College. This ensures clarity in accountability and enables the minister to fulfill duties such as:
   - Approving College bylaws or policies;
   - Ensuring compliance with federal immigration and citizenship standards;
   - Overseeing the regulatory functions of the College;
   - Responding to matters related to professional conduct, licensing, and public interest.

4. **Who is Designated?**  
   The **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration** is designated as the minister responsible under the Act. This role may be held by a different minister depending on the government’s portfolio structure, but in the context of this regulation, the current or designated minister in that portfolio is recognized as having the authority to act on behalf of the College.

5. **Effective Date and Legal Force:**  
   The regulation is part of **Statutory Instrument 2019, Chapter 29**, which came into force on the date of publication. It is binding and has the force of law within the scope of the *College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants Act*.

6. **Administrative and Policy Implications:**  
   - Provides a clear line of accountability between the federal government and the College.
   - Ensures that regulatory decisions and enforcement actions by the College are subject to the oversight of a designated minister.
   - Supports transparency and consistency in the regulation of immigration consultants, who play a role in advising individuals on immigration processes and eligibility.

---

**Significance:**

This designation is critical for the operational integrity of the College, as it establishes a formal link between the regulatory body and the federal government. It ensures that the College operates under the authority and direction of a responsible minister, which is essential for maintaining public trust, ensuring regulatory compliance, and aligning the College’s activities with broader immigration policy objectives.

---

**Conclusion:**

The regulation S.C. 2019, c. 29, s. 292 is a formal and legally binding designation that assigns the **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration** as the designated minister under the *College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants Act*. It reflects the government’s commitment to structured oversight of immigration-related professional services and ensures that regulatory governance remains aligned with federal policy and accountability standards.
