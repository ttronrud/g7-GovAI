**Detailed Summary of the College of Patent Agents and Trademark Agents Regulations (S.C. 2018, c. 27, s. 247)**

These regulations, made under the authority of the *College of Patent Agents and Trademark Agents Act* (the "Act"), establish detailed rules governing the licensing, training, conduct, and administrative procedures for individuals seeking to practice as patent agents or trademark agents in Canada. The regulations aim to ensure professionalism, integrity, and consistency in the provision of intellectual property services while maintaining access to qualified practitioners and supporting the development of future professionals.

---

### **1. Composition of Committees**
To ensure independence and impartiality in disciplinary and investigative processes:
- The **Investigations Committee** and the **Discipline Committee** must have a majority of members who:
  - Are **not licensees**; and
  - Are **not employed by a department** defined under the *Financial Administration Act* (i.e., federal government departments).
- This ensures that decisions are made by independent, non-partisan individuals, reducing conflicts of interest.

---

### **2. Composition of the Licensing Committee**
The committee responsible for administering licensing requirements (including qualifying exams) must:
- Be composed of individuals who can be **removed at the pleasure of the Board** (ensuring flexibility and accountability).
- **Exclude individuals** who are members of associations primarily representing patent or trademark advisors (to prevent conflicts of interest).
- Include **representatives from the Patent Office and the Office of the Registrar of Trademarks** (to ensure alignment with official IP systems).
- Be subject to a **conflict of interest policy** established by the Board (to maintain transparency and ethical standards).

---

### **3. Eligibility Requirements for Licensing Applications**

#### **Patent Agent Licence (Section 26)**
An applicant must:
- Be a **resident of Canada**.
- Meet requirements set out in the College’s by-laws, including:
  - Completion of **training**;
  - Passing **qualifying examinations**;
  - Demonstrated **good character and fitness to practise**;
  - Payment of applicable **fees**.

#### **Patent Agent in Training Licence (Section 26(2))**
An applicant must:
- Be a **resident of Canada**.
- Meet by-laws requirements, including:
  - Completion of **education**;
  - Demonstrated **good character and fitness to practise**;
  - Payment of **fees**;
  - Submission of required **administrative forms**.

#### **Trademark Agent Licence (Section 29)**
An applicant must:
- Be a **resident of Canada**.
- Meet by-laws requirements, including:
  - Completion of **training**;
  - Passing **qualifying examinations**;
  - Demonstrated **good character and fitness to practise**;
  - Payment of **fees**.

#### **Trademark Agent in Training Licence (Section 29(2))**
An applicant must:
- Be a **resident of Canada**.
- Meet by-laws requirements, including:
  - Completion of **education**;
  - Demonstrated **good character and fitness to practise**;
  - Payment of **fees**;
  - Submission of required **administrative forms**.

> All licence holders must remain **residents of Canada** and meet ongoing requirements related to training, character, and fees.

---

### **4. Application to the Federal Court (Sections 44–48)**
- Any party may file an application with the **Federal Court** via **notice of application**.
- This provides a legal mechanism for resolving disputes over sealed packages, such as documents or physical items involved in investigations.

---

### **5. Sealed Package Procedures (Sections 46–47)**
- The **prescribed period** for handling sealed packages is **10 days** after the investigator removes the sealed package.
- The **Federal Court** may issue orders regarding:
  - Retention, opening, or return of sealed packages.
- A sealed package may be opened if:
  - The **holder of a privilege** consents; or
  - The package was **returned to the original owner**.
- The **applicable period** for opening a sealed package ends on the **latest** of:
  - 30 days after the investigator took possession;
  - 30 days after the sealed package was opened (or could have been opened);
  - A mutually agreed period between the owner and the holder of a privilege.

> These provisions ensure procedural fairness and protect privacy and confidentiality.

---

### **6. Exemptions from Prohibition on Representation (Sections 70 and 71)**

#### **Section 70 (Patent Agents) – Exemptions**
A person is **not subject to the restrictions** in section 70(1) if:
- They are **permitted to represent another person** before the Patent Office under:
  - The *Patent Rules* or *Patent Act*;
  - Any other federal or provincial statute;
- They are an **employee representing their employer**, unless the employer is representing someone else;
- They are performing an **administrative activity** with **permission from a licensed patent agent** (whose licence is not suspended).

#### **Section 71 (Trademark Agents) – Exemptions**
A person is **not subject to the restrictions** in section 71(1) if:
- They are **permitted to represent another person** before the Office of the Registrar of Trademarks under:
  - The *Trademarks Regulations* or *Trademarks Act*;
  - Any other federal or provincial statute;
- They are an **employee representing their employer**, unless the employer is representing someone else;
- They are performing an **administrative activity** with **permission from a licensed trademark agent** (whose licence is not suspended).

> These exemptions allow for practical flexibility in administrative and procedural roles within IP offices.

---

### **7. Authority to Make By-Laws**
The College is authorized to create by-laws covering:
- Conditions on licences (e.g., **good character and fitness to practise**);
- Requirements for applicants (e.g., **qualifying exams, fees, training**);
- Determining what constitutes **good character and fitness**;
- Restrictions on who may provide representation under sections 27 or 30 of the Act.

> This grants the College flexibility to adapt standards over time based on evolving practice and public interest.

---

### **8. International Registration (Registers of Patent and Trademark Agents)**

#### **International Patent Agents (Paragraph 28(1)(f))**
- The **Registrar must include** in the *Register of Patent Agents*:
  - Individuals who are **residents of a country other than Canada**;
  - Who are **authorized to act as patent agents** under their national law;
  - Who have **submitted a written request** to be included.
- To **maintain inclusion**, such individuals must:
  - Continue to meet the eligibility criteria;
  - Submit an **annual statement** (within a period set by Board by-laws) confirming:
    - Their country of residence;
    - That they remain authorized as patent agents under their national law.

#### **International Trademark Agents (Paragraph 31(1)(f))**
- The **Registrar must include** in the *Register of Trademark Agents*:
  - Individuals who are **non-Canadian residents**;
  - Authorized to act as trademark agents under their national law;
  - Who have made a **written request**.
- To maintain inclusion, they must:
  - Meet ongoing eligibility;
  - Submit an **annual statement** confirming residence and authorization.

> These provisions support international collaboration while ensuring Canadian clients are protected by Canadian licensing standards.

---

### **9. Deemed Patent Agent in Training Licence (Section 26(2))**

An individual is **deemed to hold a patent agent in training licence** during a specified period if:
- They are **working in Canadian patent law and practice**, including preparing and prosecuting patent applications;
- They are **supervised** by:
  - A licensed patent agent; or
  - A legal clinic staff member associated with a Canadian law faculty;
- They are **resident in Canada**;
- They **notify the College** of compliance with the requirements.

> The period begins on the **later** of:
- The day these regulations come into force; or
- The date the individual submits notice.

> The period ends on the **earliest** of:
- The day they receive a patent agent licence or in-training licence;
- The day they surrender or have their licence revoked;
- One year after the regulations come into force.

> This provides a transitional path for individuals in training who may not yet be fully licensed.

---

### **10. Deemed Trademark Agent in Training Licence (Section 29(2))**

An individual is **deemed to hold a trademark agent in training licence** during a period if:
- They are **working in Canadian trademark law and practice**, including preparing and prosecuting trademark applications;
- They are **supervised** by:
  - A licensed trademark agent; or
  - A legal clinic staff member associated with a Canadian law faculty;
- They are **resident in Canada**;
- They **notify the College** of compliance.

> The period begins on the **later** of:
- The day the regulations come into force; or
- The date of notice submission.

> The period ends on the **earliest** of:
- Issuance of a trademark agent licence or in-training licence;
- Surrender or revocation of the licence;
- One year after the regulations come into force.

> This supports experiential learning and professional development.
