### 1. **Definition of "Letter Mail"**
The term *letter mail* is broadly defined to include:
- A letter, card, postcard, or similar communication  
- A receipt, invoice, or similar financial document  
- Any other article the sender chooses to post at a letter mail rate  
> **Condition:** The item must be posted in Canada for delivery within Canada and meet the requirements of these regulations.

### 4. **Prohibited Items in Standard Mail**
The following items **shall not be enclosed** in standard mail:
- Bottle caps  
- Loose coins  
- Foodstuffs  
- Glass  
- Jewellery  
- Keys  
- Liquids  
- Pencils  
- Pens  
- Powders  
- Seeds  
- Any fragile, perishable, or hazardous material that could:
  - Soil or damage other mail  
  - Damage postal equipment  
  - Cause injury to postal workers  

> These restrictions are designed to ensure safety, hygiene, and operational efficiency in postal processing.

---

### 5. **Prohibited or Restricted Features on Mail Items**

An item of standard mail **shall not be accepted** if:
- A **staple** is attached to the outer cover  
- It has a **pin-feed hole strip** (common in self-mailers)  
- It consists of a **punched or perforated card** not enclosed in an envelope  

> These features interfere with automated sorting and processing systems.

### 10. **Postage Meter Impression Requirements**

- Must be placed on the **front** of the item in the **upper right corner**:
  - No more than **40 mm from the top edge**  
  - No more than **100 mm from the right edge**

> Ensures standardized placement for scanning and processing.

---

### 11. **Postage Stamp Placement**

- If a **postage stamp** (not a meter impression) is used:
  - Must be placed in the **upper right corner** of the front of the item

---

### 12. **Return Address and Service Indicators**

- If present on a mail item with a postage meter:
  - Must be placed in the **upper left corner**, at least **19 mm from the bottom edge**  
- A return address may also be placed:
  - On the **back of the item**, near the top edge, centered between left and right edges

---

### 13. **Form Numbers and Graphics**

- A **form number** may be printed on the front, but only if:
  - Located **no more than 6 mm from the bottom edge**

- **Graphics or printing** are prohibited from:
  - Being **less than 19 mm from the bottom edge**  
  - Appearing in the **area designated for postage** (postage meter impression)

> Prevents visual clutter and ensures clear identification of postage and address.

---

### 14. **Weight Limits**

- **Maximum weight for letter mail:**
  - **Standard mail (item 1 of schedule):** 50 grams  
  - **All other letter mail:** 500 grams  

- **Minimum weight for self-mailers (items without outer cover):** 3 grams  

> Ensures mail items are of sufficient mass to be processed efficiently and safely.
