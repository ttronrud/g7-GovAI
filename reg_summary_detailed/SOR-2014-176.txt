**Detailed Summary of the Canada Industrial Relations Board – Status of the Artist Act Procedural Regulations (2014)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Status of the Artist Act Procedural Regulations*, made under **section 16a of the Status of the Artist Act (S.C. 2012, c. 19, s. 535)** and effective from **Ottawa, June 20, 2014**, provide a comprehensive procedural framework governing all proceedings before the **Canada Industrial Relations Board (CIRB)** under the *Status of the Artist Act*. These regulations establish clear rules for filing applications, responding, serving documents, conducting hearings, managing representation votes, and handling certification and revocation processes.

The regulations ensure transparency, fairness, and consistency in administrative processes involving artists, artists’ associations, and producers, with a strong emphasis on procedural integrity, timely submissions, and the protection of privacy and confidentiality.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Status of the Artist Act* (Loi sur le statut de l’artiste). |
| **Applicant** | An individual artist, an artists’ association, or a producer who files an application. |
| **Application** | Any formal request or complaint submitted to the Board under the Act. |
| **Application for Reconsideration** | A request to review a Board decision, *except* those involving sector-specific determinations under subsection 35(1) or certification orders under section 36. |
| **Participant** | Any applicant, respondent, or intervenor in a proceeding. |
| **Returning Officer** | An individual appointed by the Board to conduct a representation vote. |

> These definitions apply to all proceedings under the Act.

---

## **General Application and Time Periods**

- The regulations apply to **all proceedings before the Board** under the Act.
- **Time periods are calculated in calendar days**.
- If a deadline falls on a **Saturday or a statutory holiday** (as defined in subsection 35(1) of the *Interpretation Act*), the period is **automatically extended to the next working day**.
- **Any member of the Board** may sign orders or determinations.

---

## **Filing Requirements for Applications**

An application must be filed in **writing** and, unless exempted under specific provisions (e.g., sections 23(1), 28, 31, 32, 33, 38, or 39(1)), must include the following:

1. **Contact details** of the applicant and their authorized representative (name, postal address, email, phone).
2. **Contact details** of the respondent and their authorized representative (if applicable).
3. **Grounds and facts** supporting the application.
4. **Determination or order sought**.
5. **Request for oral hearing** (if any), with justification.
6. **Supporting documents**.
7. **Signature** of applicant or representative.
8. **Date of filing**.

> *Note:* Exemptions exist for certain types of applications (e.g., those under section 23(1) or 38), which may not require full disclosure.

---

## **Notice of Application**

Upon receipt, the Board must:
- Give **written notice** to all persons whose rights may be directly affected.
- Include the **file number** assigned to the application.

---

## **Response to Application (Within 15 Days)**

The respondent must file a written response within **15 days** of receiving notice and include:

1. Contact details of respondent and representative.
2. File number.
3. Full response to allegations or issues raised.
4. Position on the determination or order sought.
5. Request for oral hearing (if any), with reason.
6. Supporting documents.
7. Signature.
8. Date of filing.

> *Note:* A request for **extension** of this 15-day period must be submitted in writing with valid grounds.

---

## **Reply to Response (Within 10 Days)**

The applicant must file a **reply within 10 days** of the response and include:

1. File number.
2. Full reply to allegations.
3. Position on the respondent’s requested determination/order.
4. Request for oral hearing (if any).
5. Supporting documents.
6. Signature.
7. Date of filing.

> *Extension requests* for the reply period are also permitted with written justification.

---

## **Request for Leave to Intervene**

A third party may request leave to intervene in a proceeding under subsection 19(3) of the Act:

- Must be filed **within 15 days** of notice of application (or within time specified in a public notice under subsection 24(1) or 35(2)).
- Must include:
  - Contact details of intervener and representative.
  - File number.
  - Grounds for intervention and interest in the proceeding (including potential prejudice if denied).
  - Explanation of how intervention will assist the Board in fulfilling the purposes of **Part II** of the Act.
  - Supporting documents.
  - Signature and date.

> **Response to request** must be filed within **10 days**; **reply to response** within **5 days**.

> Extension requests for filing times are allowed with written justification.

---

## **Submission on the Merits (After Grant of Leave to Intervene)**

If leave is granted, the intervenor must file a **written submission on the merits** within **10 days** of receiving notice, including:

1. File number.
2. Full facts and grounds.
3. Position on any determination or order sought.
4. Determination or order sought.
5. Supporting documents.

> A **response to the submission** must be filed within **10 days**.  
> Extension requests are permitted.

---

## **Service of Documents**

All documents required by the regulations must be:

- **Filed with the Board** or **served on participants** using one of the following methods:
  1. Hand delivery.
  2. Mail (registered or regular).
  3. Electronic transmission with proof of receipt.
  4. Any method authorized by the Board.

> **Address for service**:
> - For the Board: Office of the Administrative Tribunals Support Service of Canada (designated Board office).
> - For others: Address in a Board notice or, if none, the **last known address**.

> **Electronic transmission** must include:
> - Sender and recipient details.
> - Date and time of transmission.
> - Total number of pages.

> **Service by registered mail**: Considered filed on **date mailed**, served on **date of receipt**.
> **Service by regular mail**: Considered served on the **fifth day after mailing**.
> **Hand delivery or electronic transmission**: Considered served on the **day of service**, if before 5:00 p.m.

---

## **Evidence Submission Before Oral Hearing**

A participant intending to present evidence at an oral hearing must file:

1. **Six copies** (or number specified by the Board) of:
   - All documents intended as evidence (including those from application, response, reply, or submission).
   - Organized in **tabbed books**.
2. A **list of witnesses**, including:
   - Names and occupations.
   - Summary of information each witness will provide.

> **Deadline**:
> - **Applicant**: No later than **10 days before** the hearing.
> - **Respondent and Intervenor**: No later than **8 days before** the hearing.

> **Failure to comply**:
> - The Board may **refuse to consider** the evidence or **hear the witness**.

> The Board may require **advance submission** of authorities and arguments relied upon.

---

## **Confidentiality of Documents**

- The Board may **place a document on the public record** if it is relevant to the proceeding.
- The Board may **declare a document confidential** if:
  - Disclosure would cause **specific direct harm** to a person.
  - The harm **outweighs the public interest** in disclosure.

> If declared confidential, the Board may:
> - Prevent the document from being placed on public record.
> - Require that any discussion involving the document be held in **private**.
> - Authorize provision of the document to designated persons.
> - Make any other appropriate order.

> **Critical Protection Clause**:
> - The Board or its authorized employee **must not disclose** information that could reveal:
>   - Membership in an artists’ association.
>   - Opposition to certification of an association.
>   - An artist’s wish to be or not to be represented by an association.
> - **Exception**: Disclosure is permitted only if it **further the purpose of Part II** of the Act.

> **Note**: Membership in an artists’ association is **evidence** that the artist wishes to be represented.

> *(Note: Certain provisions on confidentiality were repealed by SOR/2025-206, sections 10 and 13 — see below.)*

---

## **Consolidation and Case Management**

- The Board may **consolidate**, **hear together**, or **hear consecutively** two or more proceedings.
- The Board may schedule a **case management conference** to resolve procedural or administrative matters.
- Conferences may be held **in person, by teleconference, videoconference, or other electronic means**.
