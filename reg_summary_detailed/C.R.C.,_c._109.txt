**Detailed Summary of the Sault Ste. Marie Airport Zoning Regulations**

---

### **1. Title and Citation**
The regulation is officially titled:  
**Sault Ste. Marie Airport Zoning Regulations**  
It may be cited as such and forms part of the official zoning framework governing land use around Sault Ste. Marie Airport in Ontario.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

Several critical terms are defined to ensure clarity and consistency in interpretation:

- **Airport**: Refers specifically to **Sault Ste. Marie Airport**, located in the **District of Algoma, Province of Ontario**.

- **Airport Reference Point (ARP)**:  
  A designated geographic point used as a benchmark for elevation calculations.  
  - Defined in **Part I of the Schedule**.  
  - **Deemed elevation**: 611 feet above sea level (for regulatory purposes, even if not precisely measured at the point).

- **Approach Surface**:  
  An imaginary inclined plane extending outward and upward from each end of a runway strip.  
  - Slope ratio: **50 feet horizontally to 1 foot vertically** (i.e., 1:50).  
  - The base of this surface is a horizontal line perpendicular to the runway centerline at the ends of the strip.  
  - Applies only to **runways 04-22 and 11-29**.  
  - Dimensions:  
    - 500 feet on each side of the runway centerline at the strip ends.  
    - Extends 2,000 feet on each side of the **projected centerline** of the runway at its outer ends.  
    - Outer ends are located **200 feet above the elevation at the strip ends**, and measured **10,000 feet horizontally** from the strip ends.

- **Horizontal Surface**:  
  An imaginary flat plane located **150 feet above the elevation of the Airport Reference Point (ARP)**.  
  - This serves as a baseline for height restrictions across the entire airport zone.

- **Strip**:  
  A designated rectangular area of the airport‚Äôs landing surface, specifically designed for takeoff and landing in a given direction.  
  - For runways 04-22 and 11-29:  
    - **Width**: 1,000 feet (500 feet on each side of the centerline).  
    - **Length**: 8,600 feet (as shown on Plan No. T2557).  
  - These dimensions are defined in **Part II of the Schedule**.

- **Transitional Surface**:  
  An imaginary inclined plane extending upward and outward from the **outer lateral limits of the strip and its approach surface**.  
  - Slope ratio: **7 feet horizontally to 1 foot vertically** (i.e., 1:7).  
  - Extends upward to intersect the **horizontal surface** or other transitional surfaces.  
  - Also shown on **Plan No. T2557, dated February 28, 1969**, held at the Department of Transport in Ottawa.

---

### **3. Scope of Application**

#### **Applicable Lands**
These regulations apply to all lands:
- Adjacent to or in the vicinity of Sault Ste. Marie Airport.
- With outer limits described in a specific legal description:

> *Commencing at the northeasterly corner of Section 1, Township of Parke;  
> Thence westerly along the northern boundary of Sections 1, 2, 3, 4 and the northeast quarter of Section 5 to the intersection with Lake Superior‚Äôs waters edge;  
> Thence generally southeasterly, easterly, and northeasterly along Lake Superior and St. Mary‚Äôs River to the intersection with the eastern boundary of Section 12, Township of Parke;  
> Thence northerly along the eastern boundary of Section 12 and Section 1 back to the point of commencement.*

- These boundaries are illustrated on **Plan No. T2557**, dated February 28, 1969, and is on file with the Department of Transport in Ottawa.

#### **Exclusions**
- The regulations **do not apply** to lands that are part of the airport itself from time to time (i.e., active airport operations or official airport land use).

---

### **4. Height Restrictions on Buildings and Structures**

#### **General Rule (Section 5)**  
No person may erect, construct, or add to any building, structure, or object **on any land to which these regulations apply**, if the **highest point** of such structure exceeds the elevation of any of the following surfaces **at the location of the highest point**:

1. **Horizontal Surface**  
   - 150 feet above the ARP (611 feet above sea level).  
   - Acts as a general height ceiling across the entire airport zone.

2. **Approach Surfaces**  
   - Applies only to **runways 04-22 and 11-29**.  
   - Extends 500 feet on each side of the centerline at the strip ends.  
   - Extends 2,000 feet on each side of the projected centerline at outer ends (200 feet above strip ends, 10,000 feet from strip ends).  
   - This surface is designed to ensure safe aircraft approach and landing by providing a clear zone free of obstructions.

3. **Transitional Surfaces**  
   - Connects the outer edges of the strip and approach surface to the horizontal surface.  
   - Provides a gradual transition to maintain visual and operational safety during approach and departure.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: The approach surfaces and transitional surfaces are **not applicable** to other runways or areas outside 04-22 and 11-29.

#### **Exception (Section 5 ‚Äì Limited Elevation Over Horizontal Surface)**  
A person **may** construct a building, structure, or addition **that exceeds the horizontal surface** in height ‚Äî **but only if**:
- The structure‚Äôs **highest point does not exceed 30 feet above the land surface** at that location.

> This allows for minor structures (e.g., small sheds, signage, or low-height towers) to be built above the horizontal surface, provided they remain within a 30-foot limit above ground level.

---

### **5. Natural Growth and Removal Requirements**

- If **natural growth** (e.g., trees, shrubs, vegetation) on regulated land **exceeds** any of the defined surfaces (horizontal, approach, or transitional), the **Minister of Transport** may issue a direction requiring:
  - The **owner or occupier** of the land to **remove** the excessive growth.

> This ensures that natural features do not interfere with aircraft operations or safety zones.

---

### **8. Purpose and Rationale**

These regulations were designed to:
- Ensure **safety** during aircraft takeoff and landing by preventing obstructions in critical approach and departure zones.
- Maintain **clear visual and physical corridors** for pilots, especially in low-visibility or night operations.
- Provide **consistent, measurable height limits** across the airport perimeter.
- Protect the airport from **unauthorized or oversized structures** that could interfere with flight operations.
