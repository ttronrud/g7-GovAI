### **I. Legislative Background and Purpose**

#### **1. Legal Authority**
- The regulation was made under **subsection 332(1)a** of the *Canadian Environmental Protection Act, 1999* (CEPA 1999), which allows the Minister of the Environment to propose and adopt regulations to reduce emissions of harmful substances.
- The Governor in Council (i.e., the federal Cabinet) enacted the regulation **on the recommendation of the Minister of the Environment and the Minister of Health**, pursuant to:
  - **Section 93(1)**: Authority to make regulations for environmental protection.
  - **Section 286.1d**: Power to regulate emissions of substances that contribute to climate change or environmental harm.
  - **Section 330(3.2)e**: Power to designate provisions as "serious offences" for enforcement purposes.

#### **2. Regulatory Process**
- A **proposed regulation** was published in *Canada Gazette, Part I* on May 27, 2017, giving stakeholders (industry, environmental groups, etc.) a **public comment period**.
- The **National Advisory Committee** was consulted under **subsection 93(3)** to provide expert advice.
- The Governor in Council concluded that the proposed regulations:
  - Do **not conflict** with other federal laws;
  - Provide **sufficient environmental and human health protection**;
  - Are **necessary and proportionate** to reduce emissions of methane and volatile organic compounds (VOCs).

### **II. Overall Objectives**

The primary goals of these regulations are:

1. **To reduce emissions of methane and certain volatile organic compounds (VOCs)** from upstream oil and gas operations.
2. **To protect the environment and human health** by minimizing the release of greenhouse gases and pollutants that contribute to climate change and air quality degradation.
3. **To establish enforceable, measurable, and science-based standards** for emission control in the upstream sector.

### **IV. Core Provisions and Requirements**

#### **A. Hydrocarbon Gas Conservation Equipment (Section 7)**

Operators must ensure that **at least 95% of hydrocarbon gas routed to conservation equipment is captured and conserved**.

**Mandatory Requirements:**
- Equipment must operate **continuously**, except during normal servicing or repairs.
- Must be operated and maintained **in accordance with manufacturer‚Äôs recommendations**.
- **Exception**: If an operator can prove via documented records that **non-compliance with a recommendation does not affect the 95% capture rate**, that recommendation need not be followed.

**Recordkeeping Requirements:**
- For each month the equipment is used:
  - Document the **percentage of gas captured** at any given moment.
  - Include **volumetric flow rate calculations** and supporting data.
  - Record **operation and maintenance practices**, including manufacturer recommendations and actions taken.

> üìå *This ensures transparency and accountability in gas capture performance.*

---

#### **B. Use of Captured Gas (Section 8)**

Captured hydrocarbon gas must be conserved until it is used for one of the following:

1. **As fuel in a combustion device** where **no more than 5% of the gas is released** to the atmosphere.
2. **Delivered** to a downstream facility (e.g., pipeline).
3. **Injected** into an underground geological deposit (not as waste disposal).

**Recordkeeping for Combustion Devices:**
- For each month the device is used:
  - Record the **percentage of gas released** to the atmosphere.
  - Based on:
    - Tests conducted under manufacturer-recommended conditions; or
    - Measurements taken under those conditions.
- Must be supported by **test results or measurement data**.

> üîç *This ensures that combustion is efficient and emissions are minimized.*

#### **D. Flowback Gas Management (Section 10)**

For wells undergoing **hydraulic fracturing** with a **gas-to-oil ratio (GOR) of at least 53:1**:

- **Flowback gas must NOT be vented**.
- Must be **captured and routed** to either:
  - Hydrocarbon gas conservation equipment, or
  - Hydrocarbon gas destruction equipment.

**Exception:**
- If the flowback gas has **insufficient heating value** to sustain combustion, venting may be permitted.

**Recordkeeping Requirement:**
- For each well undergoing hydraulic fracturing:
  - Record the **most recent GOR** before fracturing.
  - If GOR ‚â• 53:1, demonstrate that flowback gas was captured and routed.
  - If gas was vented, record the **heating value** of the gas.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è This targets high-GOR wells ‚Äî which are typically associated with significant methane emissions ‚Äî and requires strict emission controls.
