**Detailed Summary of the Safeguard Surtax Regulations, 1995-3**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Purpose**

The *Safeguard Surtax Regulations, 1995-3* are a set of administrative regulations issued under the authority of the **Customs Tariff** of Canada, specifically pursuant to **subsection 60.01(7)** of that legislation. 

These regulations were enacted by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, and are designed to implement a **safeguard surtax** on certain agricultural goods imported from any country. The primary objective is to protect domestic agricultural producers from potentially harmful surges in foreign imports that could threaten their viability or market access.

The regulations are effective from **August 1, 1995**, and are cited as the *Safeguard Surtax Regulations, 1995-3*.

---

### **2. Legal Basis and Citation**

- **Statutory Reference**:  
  - *S.C. 1994, c. 47, s. 83* (Statutes of Canada, 1994, Chapter 47, Section 83)  
  - *R.S., c. 41 (3rd Supp.)* (Revised Statutes of Canada, Chapter 41, Third Supplement)

These provisions authorize the government to impose temporary additional duties (surtaxes) on specific agricultural products when import levels are found to be causing or threatening injury to Canadian producers.

---

### **3. Scope of the Regulations**

The regulations prescribe specific agricultural goods that are subject to a **safeguard surtax** for imports from any country. The goods are classified under the **Harmonized System (HS) tariff item numbers** listed in the annex.

The three main categories of agricultural goods are:

---

#### **A. Wheat**
The following tariff classifications cover various forms of wheat:

- **1001.10.10** – Wheat, whole, not further classified  
- **1001.10.20** – Wheat, whole, in the form of grain  
- **1001.90.10** – Wheat, whole, in other forms  
- **1001.90.20** – Wheat, whole, in other forms  

These classifications cover **raw wheat** and its basic forms.

---

#### **B. Wheat Products**
This broad category includes processed or derived products of wheat. The tariff items include:

- **1101.00.10, 1101.00.20** – Wheat flour, not further classified  
- **1103.11.10, 1103.11.20** – Wheat flour, in specific forms (e.g., for baking)  
- **1103.21.10, 1103.21.20** – Wheat flour, for specific uses (e.g., for bread)  
- **1104.19.11, 1104.19.12, 1104.29.11, 1104.29.12** – Wheat products, such as semolina, middlings, etc.  
- **1104.30.11, 1104.30.12** – Wheat products, including wheat starch  
- **1108.11.10, 1108.11.20** – Wheat products, such as wheat-based food items  
- **1109.00.10, 1109.00.20** – Wheat-based processed foods  
- **1901.20.13 to 1901.20.24** – Wheat-based products (e.g., pasta, noodles, noodles with added ingredients)  
- **1902.11.10 to 1902.19.93** – Various processed wheat products (e.g., cakes, biscuits, pastries)  
- **1902.30.11 to 1902.30.39** – Wheat-based baked goods  
- **1904.10.10 to 1904.90.62** – Wheat-based food products (e.g., breakfast cereals, crackers)  
- **1905.10.10 to 1905.90.63** – Further processed wheat products (e.g., flours, grits, ready-to-eat meals)  
- **2302.30.10, 2302.30.20** – Wheat-based industrial or food products (e.g., wheat protein, flour for animal feed)

> This category encompasses a wide range of **processed, manufactured, and semi-processed wheat-based goods**, from flour to ready-to-eat meals and industrial products.

---

#### **C. Barley**
Raw barley is covered under the following tariff items:

- **1003.00.11** – Barley, whole  
- **1003.00.12** – Barley, whole, in specific forms  
- **1003.00.91, 1003.00.92** – Barley, in other forms  

These classifications cover **raw barley** as imported into Canada.

---

#### **D. Barley Products**
Processed or derived barley products are included under:

- **1102.90.11, 1102.90.12** – Barley flour  
- **1103.19.11, 1103.19.12** – Barley products, e.g., barley flour  
- **1103.29.11, 1103.29.12** – Barley products, such as malted barley  
- **1104.11.10, 1104.11.20** – Barley-based food products  
- **1104.21.10, 1104.21.20** – Barley products (e.g., barley grits)  
- **1107.10.11, 1107.10.12, 1107.10.91, 1107.10.92** – Barley-based food items  
- **1107.20.11, 1107.20.12, 1107.20.91, 1107.20.92** – Barley products, including malt  
- **1108.19.11, 1108.19.12** – Barley-based processed foods  
- **1901.90.11, 1901.90.12** – Barley-based food products  
- **1904.10.30, 1904.10.41, 1904.10.49** – Barley-based food items  
- **1904.20.30, 1904.20.41, 1904.20.49, 1904.20.63, 1904.20.64** – Barley-based processed foods  
- **1904.90.30, 1904.90.40, 1904.90.63, 1904.90.64** – Barley-based products  
- **2302.40.11, 2302.40.12** – Barley-based industrial or food products (e.g., barley protein)

> This category includes **flours, malt, processed foods, and industrial by-products** derived from barley.

---

### **4. Key Features of the Surtax**

- **Applicability**: Applies to **all imports** of the listed agricultural goods from **any country**.
- **Trigger**: The surtax is imposed when the **import volume** of these goods is found to be causing or threatening **injury to Canadian producers**.
- **Purpose**: To provide temporary protection to domestic producers by increasing the cost of imported goods, thereby reducing their volume and allowing Canadian producers to recover and stabilize their markets.
- **Duration**: The surtax is temporary and typically reviewed periodically to determine whether it remains necessary.
- **Administration**: The **Minister of Finance** and the **Customs and Revenue Canada** are responsible for monitoring import volumes, assessing injury, and enforcing the surtax.

---

### **5. Legal and Economic Implications**

- **Protectionism**: The regulation reflects a **protectionist policy** aimed at shielding domestic agriculture from foreign competition, especially in the context of global market shifts.
- **Trade Impact**: It may lead to **higher prices** for consumers and businesses importing these goods, and could result in **trade disputes** with exporting countries.
