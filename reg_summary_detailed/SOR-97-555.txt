## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Broadcasting Distribution Regulations (1997)**

### üîπ **1. Legal Basis and Procedural Background**

The regulations are issued under **subsection 10(1) of the Broadcasting Act** (S.C. 1991, c. 11), which empowers the **Canadian Radio-television and Telecommunications Commission (CRTC)** to make regulations governing the distribution of broadcasting services.

- A **proposed version** of these regulations was first published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **July 12, 1997**, pursuant to **subsection 10(3)** of the Broadcasting Act.
- This publication provided a **reasonable opportunity** for licensees, broadcasters, and other stakeholders to submit **representations or objections**.
- Following this public consultation, the CRTC formally adopted and enacted the regulations on **December 8, 1997**, in Hull, Quebec.

> ‚úÖ This reflects a standard CRTC regulatory process: proposal ‚Üí public consultation ‚Üí final adoption.

### üîπ **2. Purpose and Scope of the Regulations**

These regulations establish **rules governing how broadcasting services are distributed** across Canada, including:

- The structure of **service packages** (basic, first-tier, discretionary)
- Requirements for **local content contributions**
- Definitions of key terms related to **programming types, ownership, and distribution models**
- Rules for **affiliation agreements**, **community programming**, and **commercial messaging**

They apply to all **licensed broadcasting distribution undertakings**, including:
- Terrestrial broadcasters (over-the-air TV/radio)
- Cable companies
- Direct-to-Home (DTH) satellite providers

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Note: Several definitions in the original text have since been **repealed or amended** (e.g., "basic band", "available channel", "Category B", "Category 1/2", etc.), indicating that this 1997 version is **historical** and **superseded** by later amendments. However, it remains a foundational document in the evolution of Canadian broadcasting policy.

### üîπ **4. Community Programming and Local Expression (Core Policy Goal)**

This is a **central pillar** of the regulations:

- **Community Programming** is defined broadly and includes:
  - Programming produced by individuals, groups, or community corporations.
  - Programming relevant to the community served.
  - Local community television or access TV.

- **Contribution to Local Expression**:
  - Mandates that licensees contribute financially to community programming.
  - **Higher contribution in non-metropolitan areas** (where local expression is more critical).
  - Ensures **financial support** for grassroots media and local voices.

- **Community Channel**:
  - A dedicated channel within a distribution undertaking for community programming.
  - Must be operated by a **community programming undertaking** or a **community television corporation**.

- **Community Television Corporation**:
  - A **not-for-profit** entity.
  - Must:
    - Be based in the licensed area.
    - Be incorporated under Canadian or provincial law.
    - Have board members drawn from the community.
    - Allow full participation and voting at annual meetings.

> üí° This reflects a long-standing CRTC commitment to **democratic media**, **cultural diversity**, and **local empowerment**.

### üîπ **5. Service Offerings and Pricing Structure**

The regulations establish a **tiered model** for service delivery:

| Tier | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Basic Service** | Core package required by law; includes public interest programming, local content. |
| **First-Tier Offering** | A higher-tier package (for digital services) that includes additional programming (e.g., more channels, regional content). |
| **Discretionary Services** | Optional services (e.g., premium channels, on-demand) sold separately. |

> üîç This structure supports **affordable access** to essential content while allowing **choice and flexibility**
