**Detailed Summary of the Farm Debt Mediation Regulations (S.C. 1997, c. 21, as amended)**  
*Ottawa, March 13, 1998*  
*Effective Date: April 1, 1998 (SI/98-52)*

---

### **Overview**
The *Farm Debt Mediation Regulations* are a set of administrative rules made under the *Farm Debt Mediation Act* (S.C. 1997, c. 21). These regulations provide detailed procedures for the operation of the Farm Debt Mediation Service (FDM Service), including definitions, notification requirements, eligibility criteria, mediation processes, appeal mechanisms, and the structure and duties of the Appeal Boards. The primary goal is to facilitate fair, timely, and equitable resolution of farm debt disputes between farmers and their creditors, particularly in cases of financial distress.

---

## **Key Components and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Definitions**
The regulations begin with a clear set of definitions that apply throughout the document:

- **Act**: Refers to the *Farm Debt Mediation Act*, which provides the legal framework for debt mediation in agriculture.
- **Business Day**: A day that is not a Saturday or a statutory holiday. This standard is used for timing deadlines and notifications.
- **Common-Law Partner**: A person who has cohabited with a farmer or creditor in a conjugal relationship for at least one year. This definition expands the concept of family ties beyond formal marriage.
- **Service**: Refers to the *Farm Debt Mediation Service*, operated by the Department of Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada.

---

### **2. Relationship Definitions (Section 20 & 22(2) of the Act)**
These rules define who qualifies as "related" to a farmer or creditor for the purposes of conflict of interest and eligibility in mediation:

#### For an Individual Farmer or Creditor:
- Spouse or common-law partner
- Child (or child of spouse/common-law partner)
- Sibling, parent, grandparent, or grandchild of the farmer or creditor or their spouse/common-law partner
- Employee or employer of the farmer/creditor at the time of initial application
- Any person who acquires property from the farmer under terms more favorable than open-market arm’s-length conditions

#### For a Corporate Farmer or Creditor (e.g., corporation, cooperative, partnership):
- Officer, director, partner, or employee at the time of the original application
- Direct or indirect ownership of 25% or more of shares (or control)

> **Purpose**: To prevent conflicts of interest and ensure that decisions are made impartially, especially when family or business connections exist.

---

### **3. Appointment of Mediators**
- An **administrator** may appoint a **mediator** under paragraph 10(1)(a) of the Act if the person:
  - Is knowledgeable about or has experience in mediating disputes.

> **Purpose**: Ensures that mediators are qualified and experienced to handle sensitive farm debt issues.

---

### **4. Extension of Stay of Proceedings**
Under certain circumstances, an administrator may extend a stay of proceedings (a temporary suspension of legal action against a farmer) if:
1. The value of the farmer’s assets will **not significantly diminish** during the extension;
2. The **majority of creditors will not be unduly prejudiced**;
3. There is **no indication of bad faith** by the farmer.

> **Purpose**: Provides flexibility for farmers facing temporary financial hardship, allowing time to negotiate a debt settlement without immediate risk of asset seizure.

---

### **5. Notification Requirements**
The regulations establish strict procedures for how farmers, creditors, and the Minister are notified of decisions or information:

- **Methods of Notification**:
  - In person or by **telephone**
  - **Priority post**, **courier service**
  - **Facsimile (fax)** or **electronic mail (email)** — *only if the recipient has consented in writing*

- **Timing of Notification**:
  - **In person or phone**: Immediate notification
  - **Email/fax**: Notified on the **next business day**
  - **Priority post/courier**: Notified on the **second business day** after delivery

> **Purpose**: Ensures timely and effective communication, with clear timelines to prevent delays in dispute resolution.

---

### **6. Appeal Board Structure and Function**

#### **Geographic Regions**:
Two regional Appeal Boards are established:
- **West Region**: Manitoba, British Columbia, Saskatchewan, Alberta, Yukon, Northwest Territories, Nunavut
- **East Region**: Ontario, Quebec, Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, Newfoundland and Labrador

> **Note**: This regional structure ensures local relevance and accessibility for appeals.

#### **Composition**:
- Each board consists of:
  - A **Chairperson**
  - Up to **four other members**, appointed by the Minister

#### **Agreements with Members**:
Each member must sign an agreement that includes:
- Remuneration for services (as per Treasury Board directives)
- Reimbursement for **travel and living expenses** incurred while performing duties

#### **Prohibited Activities**:
- Members **cannot hold any office or employment** inconsistent with their duties.
- Cannot perform roles as expert, mediator, or administrator under the Act.

#### **Temporary Appointments**:
- The Chairperson of one region may appoint a member to serve **temporarily** on another region’s Appeal Board when needed.

#### **Chairperson Powers and Responsibilities**:
- Presides over all meetings
- Supervises and directs the work of the board
- Establishes panels to hear appeals
- Allocates work equally among members
- Manages internal affairs and temporary appointments

#### **Interim Chairperson**:
- Designated by the Chairperson
- Takes over duties if the Chairperson is absent or incapacitated
- If the interim chair is unwilling or unavailable, the remaining members elect a **temporary chair by majority vote**

> **Purpose**: Ensures continuity and stability in the appeal process even during leadership gaps.

---

### **7. Filing and Processing of Appeals**

#### **When Can Appeals Be Filed?**
- Appeals must be filed **within prescribed timeframes**:
  - **Eligibility or extension of stay**:
    - Must be filed within **4 business days** after notice of initial stay or extension
  - **Termination of stay**:
    - If termination is by administrator under paragraph 14(2)(c) or (d): within **1 business day**
    - Otherwise: within **4 business days**

#### **Filing Requirements**:
- A **Notice of Appeal** must be submitted in the form established by the Minister
- Must be filed with the **same office** where the original mediation application was submitted
- Acceptable formats: original, **facsimile copy**, or **electronic version**

#### **Notification of Appeal**:
- Upon filing, the administrator must notify:
  - The farmer or creditor (as applicable)
  - The Minister (if the Minister is the guarantor of a debt owed to a creditor)

#### **Right to Respond**:
- Within **1 business day** (for termination appeals) or **3 business days** (for other appeals), a party may:
  - Request a copy of the Notice of Appeal
  - File a **written statement** outlining objections and relevant information

#### **Administrator’s Duty**:
- After the deadline for statements, the administrator must send to the **Chairperson of the Appeal Board**:
  - All information on which the administrator’s decision was based
  - All written statements and other relevant information submitted by parties or the Minister

> **Purpose**: Ensures transparency and allows the Appeal Board to make informed, fair decisions based on full disclosure.

---

### **8. Hearing and Decision of Appeals**

- Each appeal is heard by a **panel** consisting of:
  - The Chairperson
  - Two other members selected by the Chairperson

- The panel must:
  - Decide **expeditiously**
  - Use **minimal formality**
  - Base decisions on:
    - Information sent under section 10
    - Any **additional relevant information** the party was informed of and given a reasonable opportunity to respond to

- A decision is made by a **majority vote** of the panel members

- Members may participate via **telephone or other effective means** of communication

- The panel may:
  - **Dismiss** the appeal
  - **Reverse** the administrator’s decision

> **Purpose**: Promotes efficiency, fairness, and accessibility in appeal proceedings.

---

### **9. Notification of Appeal Decisions**

- The administrator must inform:
  - The farmer
  - The farmer’s creditors
  - The Minister (if the Minister is a guarantor of a debt owed to a creditor)

> **Purpose**: Ensures all stakeholders are informed of the outcome of the appeal.

---

### **10. Service of Notices to Farmers and Administrators (Section 21 of the Act)**

#### **For Individual Farmers**:
- Can be served by:
  - Leaving a copy with the farmer
  - Leaving a sealed envelope with an adult resident at the residence, and mailing another copy the same day or next day
  - Sending by **priority post, courier, or registered mail**

> **Note**: Notice served via envelope and delivery is deemed effective **7 business days after mailing**

#### **For Corporate Farmers (cooperatives, partnerships, etc.)**:
- Can be served by:
  - Leaving a copy with an officer, director, partner, or agent
