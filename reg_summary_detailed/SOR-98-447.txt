**Detailed Summary of the Regulations Defining ‚ÄúAdvertising Revenues‚Äù**

**Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**  
These regulations are issued by the **Copyright Board of Canada** under **subsection 68.1(3)(a)** of the *Copyright Act* (S.C. 1997, c. 24, s. 45). The purpose of these regulations is to provide a clear and consistent definition of the term **"advertising revenues"** as used in the context of the Copyright Act, particularly in relation to the licensing and compensation arrangements involving wireless transmission systems (e.g., broadcast television or radio services).

---

**Effective Date**  
The regulations come into force on **August 31, 1998**.

---

**Key Definitions and Provisions**

1. **Definition of "System"**  
   - The term **"system"** is defined as a **wireless transmission system**.  
   - This includes entities such as television or radio broadcasters that transmit content via wireless means (e.g., over-the-air broadcasts), and may be relevant in determining how advertising revenue is allocated and reported.

2. **Definition of "Advertising Revenues" (Under Subsection 68.1(1))**  
   - **Advertising revenues** are defined as the **total compensation** received by a system (wireless transmission system) for advertising activities.  
   - This compensation may take the form of:
     - Money (cash payments)
     - Goods (e.g., products or services provided as part of a sponsorship)
     - Services (e.g., promotional or technical support)
   - The compensation is **net of**:
     - Taxes
     - Commissions paid to advertising agencies (such as media buying or agency fees)

3. **Valuation of Goods and Services**  
   - For the purpose of calculating advertising revenues, any **goods or services** received by the system must be valued at their **fair market value**.  
   - This ensures consistency and fairness in financial reporting, preventing under- or overvaluation due to subjective or arbitrary pricing.

4. **Special Provision for Group Broadcasting Arrangements**  
   A key and nuanced provision addresses situations where **multiple systems broadcast a single event**, either simultaneously or with a delay. In such cases:

   - If one system acts on behalf of a **group of systems** (e.g., a national broadcaster coordinating a live sports or cultural event across several regional stations), and pays another system within that group for participation:
     - The **compensation paid by the lead system to a participating system** (within the group) is **included in the advertising revenues** of the **system receiving the payment** (i.e., the participating system).
     - The **difference** between:
       - The total compensation received by the lead system (the one acting on behalf of the group), and  
       - The amount paid to other systems in the group (i.e., the compensation referred to in paragraph (a))  
     - Is considered part of the **advertising revenue of the lead system** (the one acting on behalf of the group).

---

**Purpose and Rationale**  
These regulations are designed to:
- Provide **clarity and consistency** in how advertising revenues are defined and calculated under the Copyright Act.
- Ensure **fair treatment** of all systems involved in broadcasting, especially in collaborative or shared programming scenarios.
- Prevent **double counting** of payments or revenue misallocation in multi-system events.
- Support accurate licensing and royalty calculations, particularly in the context of **public interest broadcasting** or **sponsorship agreements**.

---

**Application Context**  
These regulations are primarily relevant to:
- Licensing of broadcasting services under the Copyright Act.
- Determining the **advertising revenue** that qualifies as a basis for royalty payments or compensation to content creators or rights holders.
- Regulatory oversight of how systems monetize advertising, especially in multi-system or networked broadcasting environments.
