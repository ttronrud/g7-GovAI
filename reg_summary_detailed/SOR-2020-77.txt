**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

---

### **Regulatory Context and Background**

This regulation is a **Direction issued by the Governor General in Council** (on the recommendation of the Minister of Canadian Heritage) under the **Broadcasting Act (S.C. 1991, c. 11)**, specifically in accordance with **subsection 27(2)b** of that Act. It implements provisions of the **Canada–United States–Mexico Agreement (CUSMA)**, particularly those related to cross-border broadcasting.

The Direction is rooted in the **Agreement between Canada, the United States of America, and the United Mexican States**, signed in Buenos Aires on **November 30, 2018**, and subsequently amended by the **Protocol of Amendment**, signed in Mexico City on **December 10, 2019**.

Key provisions of the Agreement under discussion are found in **Annex 15-D**, which addresses the **regulation of foreign television programming services**, including those from the United States.

---

### **Jurisdictional Basis**

- **Paragraphs 1 and 4 of Annex 15-D** of the CUSMA Agreement fall within the **jurisdiction of the Canadian Radio-television and Telecommunications Commission (CRTC)**, as defined under the *Broadcasting Act*.
- The **Minister of Canadian Heritage** has fulfilled the required consultation obligations under **subsection 27(2)b** of the Broadcasting Act, ensuring that the CRTC was consulted before this Direction was issued.

---

### **Purpose and Scope of the Direction**

The primary purpose of this Direction is to **enable the CRTC to implement specific provisions of Annex 15-D of the CUSMA Agreement**, particularly **Paragraph 4**, which concerns **United States home shopping television programming services**.

#### **Key Provisions of the Direction:**

1. **Implementation of Paragraph 4 of Annex 15-D**  
   - The CRTC is directed to **place United States-based home shopping television programming services** (including modified versions tailored for the Canadian market) on the **List of non-Canadian programming services and stations authorized for distribution**.
   - This means that these U.S. services are officially recognized and permitted to be distributed in Canada under CRTC regulations.

2. **Affiliation Rights for Distribution Undertakings**  
   - The CRTC must ensure that these U.S. home shopping programming services are able to **negotiate affiliation agreements** with **distribution undertakings** (such as cable, satellite, or internet-based providers).
   - This allows U.S. programming to be carried on Canadian distribution platforms, subject to applicable rules and conditions.

3. **Definition of "Agreement"**  
   - In this Direction, the term **"Agreement"** refers to the **Canada–United States–Mexico Agreement (CUSMA)** as defined in **Section 2 of the Canada–United States–Mexico Agreement Implementation Act (S.C. 2020, c. 1)**.

---

### **Effective Date**

- The Direction comes into force on the day that **Section 152 of the Canada–United States–Mexico Agreement Implementation Act** (S.C. 2020, c. 1) comes into force.
- If the Direction is **registered later than that date**, it will take effect on the **date of registration**.

> **Note**: This Direction came into force on **July 1, 2020**, as reflected in **Statutory Instrument SI/2020-33**, and was later amended by **SI/2020-46**.

---

### **Significance and Implications**

- **Market Access for U.S. Programming**: This Direction facilitates greater access for U.S. home shopping television services into the Canadian market, aligning with CUSMA’s goal of promoting fair and open cross-border trade in broadcasting services.
- **Regulatory Clarity**: It provides clear guidance to the CRTC on how to classify and authorize foreign programming, reducing uncertainty for broadcasters, distributors, and content providers.
- **Alignment with International Trade Agreements**: This action demonstrates Canada’s commitment to implementing CUSMA obligations in a way that respects both international trade principles and domestic regulatory frameworks.
- **Consumer Benefits**: Canadian consumers may gain access to a wider variety of U.S. home shopping programming, including niche or specialized content, through authorized distribution channels.

---

### **Legal and Regulatory Framework**

- **Primary Authority**: *Broadcasting Act (S.C. 1991, c. 11)*  
- **Relevant Provisions**:  
  - Subsection **27(1)(a)(b)** – Allows the Governor General in Council to issue directions to the CRTC on matters within its jurisdiction.  
  - Subsection **27(2)(b)** – Requires consultation with the CRTC before such directions are issued.  
- **Supporting Legislation**: *Canada–United States–Mexico Agreement Implementation Act (S.C. 2020, c. 1)* – Provides the legal basis for implementing CUSMA provisions in Canadian law.

---

### **Conclusion**

This Direction is a **key implementation step** in Canada’s compliance with the CUSMA Agreement. It ensures that U.S. home shopping television services—both original and modified for the Canadian market—are legally recognized and permitted to operate in Canada under the CRTC’s oversight. By enabling these services to be distributed and to enter into affiliation agreements, the Direction promotes cross-border media exchange, supports market competition, and reflects the spirit of mutual economic and regulatory cooperation between Canada, the United States, and Mexico.

It exemplifies how international trade agreements are translated into concrete regulatory actions that affect domestic broadcasting policy and consumer access.
