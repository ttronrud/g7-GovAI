## ğŸ—ï¸ Licensing Process

### 1. **Who Can Apply?**
- Any person may apply for a licence **provided they meet specific criteria**.
- The Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness (MPSEP) may issue a licence **only if**:
  - The applicant submits a formal application in the prescribed form;
  - The applicant provides sufficient **financial resources** to:
    - Meet the requirements under sections 11 and 12 of the *Customs Tariff*;
    - Lease or purchase the proposed warehouse site;
  - The applicant is of **good character**;
  - The proposed warehouse site is within a **customs office service area**;
  - The applicant can provide **suitable conditions for safekeeping of goods**;
  - The Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) can provide customs services to the site.

### 2. **Application Requirements**
- Must include:
  - A **detailed plan** of the proposed bonded warehouse, which must specify:
    - Whether the site exists or is to be constructed;
    - Type of construction (existing or new);
    - The specific area designated for storing goods.

> ğŸ” *Note: The plan is critical for assessing feasibility, security, and compliance.*

## ğŸ” Security and Operational Conditions

### 1. **Security Requirements**
- Security must be provided in accordance with the **Financial Security (Electronic Means) Regulations** (under subsection 91(4) of the *Customs Tariff*).
- This includes electronic monitoring, access controls, and audit trails.

### 2. **Facilities and Equipment Required**
Every licensee must ensure the bonded warehouse has:
- Sturdy doors and building components;
- Secure locks on doors and windows;
- Clear signage indicating security requirements;
- Additional storage facilities for **designated goods** (e.g., tobacco, alcohol, vaping products);
- Adequate space for **officer inspections**;
- Personnel and equipment to **make goods available for inspection**;
- Staff trained to provide **audit information** on inventory and operations.

## ğŸš« Prohibited Goods and Transactions

### 1. **Goods That Cannot Be Received into a Bonded Warehouse**
The following **domestic products** are strictly prohibited from entering a bonded warehouse:
- Cigars  
- Packaged raw leaf tobacco  
- Manufactured tobacco that is **not stamped**  
- Non-duty-paid packaged or bulk spirits  
- Non-duty-paid packaged or bulk wine  
- Non-duty-paid specially denatured alcohol  
- Vaping products that are **not stamped**

> âŒ These restrictions aim to prevent the illegal storage of unexcisable or unregulated goods.

### 2. **Imported Goods Restrictions**
#### a) **General Prohibitions**
- No licensee may receive **imported tobacco products**, **packaged spirits**, or **packaged wine** into a bonded warehouse **unless** the goods are destined for:
  - Sale to a **foreign diplomat in Canada**;
  - **Export from Canada**;
  - Sale to a **duty-free shop**;
  - Use as **shipsâ€™ stores**;
  - Supply to an **air carrier** operating international flights (licensed under sections 69 or 73 of the *Canada Transportation Act*).

#### b) **Bulk Goods Restrictions**
- No licensee may receive **bulk spirits**, **bulk wine**, or **specially denatured alcohol** into a bonded warehouse **unless** the goods are to be **exported from Canada**.

#### c) **Vaping Products**
- No licensee may receive **imported vaping products** into a bonded warehouse **unless** they are to be:
  - Sold to a **foreign diplomat in Canada**;
  - **Exported from Canada**.

> âš ï¸ These restrictions ensure that only authorized and compliant goods are stored in bonded warehouses, reducing risks of smuggling or unauthorized distribution.
