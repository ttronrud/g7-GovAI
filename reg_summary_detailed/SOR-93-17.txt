### **Detailed Summary of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police Public Complaints Commission Rules of Practice and Procedure (1992)**

---

#### **1. Legal Basis and Purpose**
The **Royal Canadian Mounted Police Public Complaints Commission (RCMP PCC) Rules of Practice and Procedure** are established under **paragraph 45.33(b)** of the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act* (R.S., c. 8 (2nd Supp.), s. 16). These rules govern the conduct of hearings and procedures related to public complaints against the RCMP.

The primary purpose of these rules is to ensure that:
- Complaints from members of the public are investigated fairly, efficiently, and transparently;
- The process is accessible, equitable, and consistent with principles of natural justice;
- Hearings are conducted in a manner that respects procedural fairness while allowing flexibility when appropriate.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**
The rules define key terms used throughout the process:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | The *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act* |
| **Commission** | The member(s) assigned to hear complaints under subsection 45.44(1) of the Act |
| **Complainant** | Any member of the public who files a complaint under subsection 45.35(1) |
| **Complaint** | As defined in subsection 45.35(1) of the Act |
| **Hearing** | A formal inquiry into a complaint initiated under paragraph 45.42(3)(c) or subsection 45.43(1) |
| **Hearing Process Officer** | A person appointed by the Commission to act on behalf of the Registrar during hearings |
| **Interested Person** | A person who has a substantial and direct interest in the complaint |
| **Party** | As defined in subsection 45.45(15) of the Act |
| **Record** | Any document, transcript, paper, tape, or electronic data containing information |
| **Registrar** | A person appointed under subsection 45.31(2) and designated by the Commission Chairman |

---

#### **3. Scope and Applicability**
These rules apply to **all proceedings involving hearings conducted by the Commission** under **section 45.45** of the Act.

The Commission has **discretionary authority** to:
- **Dispense with any rule** if circumstances or fairness require it (e.g., for informal, expedited hearings);
- **Extend or abridge time periods** (e.g., deadlines for submissions or responses) where fairness demands it.

When such dispensations occur, the Commission must **immediately inform all parties and interested persons**.

---

#### **4. Filing Records**
Records may be filed with the Commission in several ways:
- **In person**, during Commission sessions;
- **By hand delivery**, registered mail, courier, telex, facsimile, or other means **if facilities exist**.

**Filing date**:
- The day a record is received by the Commission or an authorized representative;
- If received **after 5 PM on a business day**, it is deemed filed on the **next business day**, unless the Commission is in session.

**Confidential Records**:
- Must be clearly marked **“Confidential/Confidentiel”**;
- Must be **kept separate from public records**.

---

#### **5. Service of Documents**
Documents may be served in the following ways:

##### **(a) Standard Service Methods**:
- Hand delivery, mail, courier, telex, facsimile, or other available means.

##### **(b) Alternative Service (if standard methods fail)**:
- A notice must be published **twice within 7 days** in a daily newspaper circulated in the place of the person’s latest known residence.
- The notice must:
  - State the contents of the document;
  - Confirm that the original is filed with the Commission.

##### **(c) Who May Be Served**:
- **On the Commission**: Leave a copy with an authorized representative.
- **On an adult (16+ years)**:
  - Leave with the person, their authorized counsel, or another person ≥16 years old residing or employed at the person’s address or principal office.
- **On a child (14+ years)**:
  - Leave with the child (if ≥14), and with the child’s parent, guardian, or legal custodian.

##### **Service Effective Date**:
- When received by the Commission or authorized person;
- Or, **one day after the last publication** in the newspaper (for publication-based service).

##### **Proof of Service**:
- Must be made by filing a **declaration under oath or solemn affirmation** with the Commission **immediately upon service**.

---

#### **6. Requests for Records or Information (Section 7)**
Any party or interested person may, in writing, request:
- Any record in their possession or control;
- A **full and adequate reply** to the request (especially for information).

##### **Requirements for Requests**:
- Must be addressed to the named party or interested person;
- Must be delivered or served within a period set by the Commission;
- Must be filed with the Registrar and delivered to other parties.

##### **For Information Requests**:
- Each item of information must be **numbered consecutively**.

##### **Obligations of the Respondent**:
- Must deliver a copy of the record or reply to all parties and file it with the Commission;
- For replies to information requests, each item must be responded to on a **separate page**.

##### **Non-Compliance Consequences**:
- If a person fails to comply:
  - Must state the **grounds for refusal**;
  - Provide a **statement of other relevant information** that could assist the requesting party;
  - Deliver both the grounds and statement to all parties and file them with the Commission.

##### **Right to Motion for Production**:
- A party may **move for the Commission to order** the production of a record or reply if not received.

##### **Evidence at Hearing**:
- A party **cannot introduce evidence** of a record they failed to deliver and file **unless the Commission finds sufficient justification** for non-compliance.

##### **Secondary Evidence**:
- If a party refuses to deliver a record, the requesting party may introduce **secondary evidence** (e.g., copies from third parties) **only if authorized by the Commission**.

---

#### **7. Consequences of Non-Compliance**
If any party or interested person fails to comply with these rules or a Commission direction:
- The Commission may **stay the proceedings** until compliance is achieved;
- Or take **other fair and reasonable steps** to ensure the hearing proceeds informally and expeditiously.

---

#### **8. Jurisdiction and Procedure Issues**
The Commission may:
- **Determine any issue of jurisdiction, law, or procedure** at any stage before proceeding continues;
- **Refer any legal or procedural question** to the **Federal Court** for hearing and determination;
- **Stay the proceedings** pending Federal Court decision.

---

#### **9. Conference and Teleconference Provisions (Section 12)**
At any time, the Commission may direct parties or interested persons to:
- Attend a **conference** or **teleconference** to discuss:
  - Clarifying contentious issues;
  - Admitting facts or information;
  - Language of the hearing;
  - Record exchange procedures;
  - Hearing procedures;
  - Any other matter aiding preparation.

##### **Agreements Reached**:
- In **conferences**: Recorded in minutes signed by all attendees and **countersigned by the presiding Commission member**.
- In **teleconferences**: Minutes signed by the presiding member and delivered to all participants.

##### **If No Agreement**:
- The Commission may **decide the issue** to allow the hearing to proceed informally and efficiently.

##### **Effect of Agreements or Decisions**:
- Any agreement or Commission decision **governs the hearing** and shall be delivered to all parties and interested persons.

---

#### **10. Summons to Witnesses (Section 14)**
A party or interested person may obtain a **summons in Form 1** (issued by the Commission and sealed) to summon a witness.

##### **Requirements**:
- Must be completed by the party or their counsel;
- Must contain required information (style of cause, name, address of witness).

##### **Advance Notice**:
- A party may submit the name and address of a proposed witness to the Registrar **at least 7 days before** the hearing, so the Commission can issue a summons.

##### **Request for Witness Fees**:
- A summoned person may, **before appearing**, request payment of **witness fees and allowances** (same as those payable in Federal Court).

---

#### **11. Motion Procedure**
Parties or interested persons may raise issues during proceedings via **motions**.

##### **Requirements**:
- Must be clear, concise, and include:
  - Facts;
  - Order sought;
  - Grounds for the motion.

##### **Filing and Service**:
- Written motions must be filed with the Registrar or hearing process officer and served on all parties.
- Motions may also be made **orally during hearings**, following Commission-established procedures.

##### **Disposal**:
- Motions may be disposed of **in writing or orally** by the Commission.

---

#### **12. Motion to Intervene**
A person seeking to participate in a proceeding must:
- File a **signed and dated motion**;
- Submit a **supporting affidavit** stating the facts.

##### **Contents of Motion**:
- Style of cause;
- Name and address of the intervener;
- Concise statement of matters affecting them;
