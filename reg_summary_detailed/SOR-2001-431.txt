## ðŸš« **Prohibited Activities and Restrictions**

### 1. **Prohibited Entities for Investment or Control**
A company **shall not**, through specialized financing activities:
- Acquire control of, or hold, acquire, or increase a substantial investment in:
  - Any entity listed in **paragraphs 453(1)(a) to (j)** of the TLC Act (e.g., entities involved in certain high-risk or non-financial activities).
  - An entity **primarily engaged in leasing motor vehicles in Canada** for the purpose of extending credit or financing customer acquisition.
  - An entity **primarily engaged in providing temporary possession** of personal property (including motor vehicles) for purposes other than financing acquisition.
  - An entity that **acts as an insurance broker or agent** in Canada.

> These restrictions aim to prevent companies from engaging in activities that could lead to systemic risk, fraud, or misalignment with core financial stability objectives.

---

### 2. **Maximum Holding Period: 13 Consecutive Years**
- A company **shall not** hold control of, or maintain a substantial investment in, an entity via specialized financing activities for **more than 13 consecutive years**.

> This rule prevents long-term, passive ownership in potentially risky or opaque entities and promotes diversification and market responsiveness.

> **Exception**: The period before an entity becomes a specialized financing entity is **not counted** under this rule (see below).

---

### 3. **Aggregate Balance Sheet Value Threshold: $250 Million**
- A company **shall not** acquire control of, or increase a substantial investment in, an entity if the **aggregate balance sheet value** of shares and ownership interests held (or to be held) by:
  - The company,
  - Any subsidiary of the company,
  - Any specialized financing entity of the company,
  > **exceeds $250 million**.

> This cap limits the scale of concentrated exposure to any single entity, reducing concentration risk.
