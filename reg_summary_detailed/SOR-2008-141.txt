### **Detailed Summary of the Competition Tribunal Rules (2008)**  
*Effective Date: February 11, 2008*  
*Source: R.S., c. 19 (2nd Supp.), Part I – Competition Tribunal Act, as amended*

---

## **Overview**

The **Competition Tribunal Rules**, adopted on **February 11, 2008**, and approved by the Governor in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Industry, provide the procedural framework for the operation of the **Competition Tribunal** under the *Competition Act*. These rules govern the filing, service, conduct, and management of proceedings before the Tribunal, including both **applications** and **references**.

The rules were developed following a public consultation process: a draft version was published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **May 26, 2007**, giving interested parties a reasonable opportunity to make representations. After review, the rules were formally approved and implemented.

---

## **Key Provisions and Structure**

### **1. Definitions**
The rules establish clear definitions of key terms used throughout the process:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Competition Act* |
| **Applicant** | A person filing an application under Part VII.1 or Part VIII, or a person filing a reference under section 124.2 |
| **Certified Copy** | A copy of a document certified by the Registrar or a designated person |
| **Chairperson** | The judicial member designated as Chairman of the Tribunal |
| **Commissioner** | The Commissioner of Competition appointed under section 7(1) of the Act |
| **Consent Agreement** | An agreement under sections 74.12, 105, or 106.1, with terms agreed upon by the parties |
| **Counsel** | A member of the provincial bar |
| **Document** | Any documentary material in any form (paper, electronic, audiovisual, etc.), including copies or portions |
| **Electronic Hearing** | A hearing where documents are provided electronically and presented electronically |
| **Electronic Transmission** | Includes email or transmission via the Tribunal’s website |
| **File** | To submit a document with the Registrar |
| **Intervenor** | A person granted leave to intervene (by Tribunal), an Attorney General (under sections 88 or 101), or the Commissioner (under section 103.2 or 124.2(3)) |
| **Originating Document** | A notice of application, notice of reference, or application for leave under section 103.1 |
| **Paper Hearing** | A hearing where documents are submitted and presented in paper form |
| **Party** | Either an applicant or respondent |
| **Person** | Includes corporations, partnerships, and unincorporated associations |
| **Reference** | A question referred to the Tribunal under section 124.2 |
| **Registrar** | The official responsible for administrative functions of the Tribunal |
| **Registry** | The administrative office of the Tribunal |
| **Respondent** | A person named in a notice of application |

### **2. Flexibility and Discretion**
- The Tribunal has **discretion to vary, dispense with, or supplement** any rule in individual cases to ensure fairness, informality, and expediency.
- A party may request that an application be heard **urgently or within a specified timeframe**, and the Tribunal may issue directions accordingly.

> **Time Limits**:  
> - Time limits are calculated under sections 26–30 of the *Interpretation Act*.  
> - If a deadline falls on a **holiday or Saturday**, the deadline is extended to the next **non-holiday/non-Saturday** day.  
> - For time limits **less than six days**, holidays and Saturdays are **not included**.  
> - Time limits **cannot be shortened or extended without a judicial member’s order or direction**.

---

### **3. Memorandum of Fact and Law**
Each party must submit a **memorandum of fact and law** that includes:
- A **table of contents**
- In consecutively numbered paragraphs:
  - Concise statement of facts
  - Statement of points in issue
  - Concise statement of submissions
  - Order sought (including costs)
  - List of authorities (statutes, regulations)
  - Appendix with copies of cited laws or regulations (or relevant excerpts)

This ensures clarity, consistency, and transparency in legal arguments.

---

### **4. Service of Documents**

#### **Service of Originating Documents (e.g., Notice of Application)**
- **Individual**: Leave a certified copy with the individual.
- **Partnership**: Leave with one partner during business hours.
- **Corporation**: Leave with an officer or person in charge of head office or branch in Canada during business hours.
- **Commissioner**: Leave at the Commissioner’s office during business hours.
- **If represented by counsel**: Leave with the counsel who accepts service.

> If service cannot be made in this way, the party may apply to a **judicial member** for an order allowing alternative service.  
> Once granted, the order must be served on all named parties.

> **Exception**: If service is not validly made, the Tribunal may **deem it valid** if it determines the document came to the person’s notice or would have done so absent avoidance of service.

#### **Service of Other Documents (non-originating)**
- **Unrepresented parties**:
  - Leave at usual/last known address
  - Send by **facsimile (fax)** (as per subrule 3)
  - Send by **registered mail or courier** with **acknowledgment of receipt**
  - Send by **electronic transmission** if the recipient agrees and sends **acknowledgment within 24 hours**
  - Any other method permitted by the Tribunal

- **Parties represented by counsel**:
  - Same as above, but service is directed to **counsel**:
    - Leave at counsel’s business address
    - Send by fax
    - Send by registered mail with acknowledgment
    - Send electronically with 24-hour acknowledgment
    - Any other method allowed by the Tribunal

> **Facsimile Transmission Requirements**:
> - Must include a cover page with:
>   - Sender’s name, address, phone
>   - Recipient’s name and address
>   - Date/time of transmission
>   - Total pages (including cover)
>   - Contact for transmission issues

> **Electronic Transmission Requirements**:
> - Must include an electronic message with:
>   - Sender’s name, address, phone, email
>   - Recipient’s name
>   - Date/time of transmission
>   - Title of document
>   - Contact for transmission issues

---

### **5. Proof of Service**
- **Originating documents**: Proof by **affidavit of service** (Schedule 1)
- **Other documents**: Proof by **certificate by counsel or designated person** (Schedule 3)
- For **registered mail**, a **signed and dated acknowledgment** must be attached.

---

### **6. Filing Procedures**

#### **Electronic Filing (Primary Method)**
- All parties must **file documents electronically**.
- The Tribunal may allow **paper filing or facsimile filing** in exceptional circumstances.

#### **Electronic Hearing Requirements**
- Documents filed electronically become the **original** for both electronic and paper hearings.
- In **paper hearings**, parties must provide **five paper copies** within **24 hours** of electronic filing.

#### **Document Format Requirements**
- Only documents printed on **21.5 cm × 28 cm (letter size)** paper with **numbered pages** may be filed.
- Documents not in this format may be filed **in their original format** if they cannot reasonably be converted.

#### **Facsimile Filing (Limited)**
- **Prohibited** for:
  - Originating documents and accompanying documents
  - Documents filed in multiple copies
  - Documents containing **confidential information**
- If filed, must include a **cover page** meeting the requirements of subrule 10(3)
- Documents filed after **17:00 Ottawa time** are deemed filed on the **next non-holiday/non-Saturday day**

#### **Electronic Filing Time Stamping**
- All electronic submissions must be **electronically time-stamped**
- Documents filed after 17:00 Ottawa time are deemed filed on the **next non-holiday/non-Saturday**

#### **Correction of Electronic Documents**
- Before judgment, the Tribunal may **point out irregularities** in electronic submissions and allow parties to **correct them** under fair conditions.

#### **Statements Under Oath or Affirmation**
- Can be filed electronically via a **scanned version** with:
  - Handwritten signature
  - Statement: *"The document that is being electronically submitted to the Tribunal is an electronic version of a paper document that has been signed by the affiant. The signed document in paper copy is available and will be produced if requested by the Tribunal."*
- The **original paper version must be maintained** by the filing party or intervenor until **one year after all appeal periods expire**
- Upon Tribunal request, the original must be provided for review
- The Tribunal may allow alternative filing methods for such statements

#### **Certified Copies**
- Upon request, the Tribunal may issue a **certified electronic copy** of a document stamped “certified”
