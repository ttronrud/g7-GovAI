**Detailed Summary of Rule 91 – Criminal Appeal, Nova Scotia Court of Appeal (S.C. 2002, c. 13, s. 17; R.S., c. C-46)**  
*Effective: December 31, 2008*  

---

### **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**
Rule 91 is a procedural rule made under **section 482(1) and (3)** of the *Criminal Code* (R.S. C-46), governing the process for appeals to the **Nova Scotia Court of Appeal** from criminal judgments. It provides a comprehensive, structured framework for initiating, managing, and conducting criminal appeals, including the filing of notices, service, submission of documents, and hearing procedures.

This rule applies specifically to appeals under **Part XXI of the Criminal Code**, which governs appeals from criminal convictions, acquittals, sentences, and related decisions. It also incorporates provisions from legislation that mirrors the Code’s appeal procedures.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**
The rule establishes clear definitions of key terms used throughout:
- **Appeal**: Includes appeals under Part XXI of the Code or any statute incorporating those provisions. This includes both applications for leave and appeals contingent on leave being granted.
- **Appellant**: A person who brings an appeal.
- **Attorney General**: Includes the Director of Public Prosecutions, Her Majesty the Queen (as represented), and counsel acting on their behalf.
- **Chief Justice**: The Chief Justice of Nova Scotia.
- **Code**: Refers to the *Criminal Code*.
- **Court of Appeal**: The Nova Scotia Court of Appeal.
- **Judgment**: Any decision or order from which an appeal is available — including conviction, acquittal, sentence, finding of guilt, verdict of unfit to stand trial, or not criminally responsible due to mental disorder.
- **Prisoner Appeal**: An appeal initiated by someone in prison who is not represented by counsel. It ceases when the person is released or retains counsel.
- **Prothonotary**: A judicial officer appointed by the Minister of Justice to serve in the Supreme Court.
- **Registrar**: The official appointed by the Minister of Justice to manage the Court of Appeal’s administrative functions (including deputies, assistants, associates).
- **Sentence Appeal**: An appeal that concerns only the sentence imposed, not the conviction or guilt.

> *Note: Interpretation and definition sections of the Code apply to this Rule.*

---

### **3. Applicability of Civil Procedure Rules**
- The **Civil Procedure Rules (CPR)**, particularly **Rule 90**, apply to this Rule with necessary modifications.
- Procedural rules in Rule 90 (e.g., motions to judges or the Court of Appeal) apply to motions under Rule 91.
- Non-compliance with Rule 91 **does not render a proceeding void**, but may result in:
  - Amendment of the proceeding
  - Setting aside due to irregularity
  - Other just remedies

> Time periods specified in Rule 91 may be **extended or abridged** by a judge or the Court of Appeal before or after expiry.

> A party seeking such an extension must file a motion under:
- Section 678 of the Code
- Rule 2 – General
- Or subsection (1) of this Rule

---

### **4. Filing a Notice of Appeal**
An appeal must begin with a **notice of appeal** filed with the **registrar** of the Court of Appeal.

#### Requirements of the Notice of Appeal:
- Must be entitled **"Notice of Appeal"**, dated, and signed.
- Must include:
  1. **Notice of appeal from a judgment**:
     - Type of judgment (e.g., conviction, acquittal)
     - Name of judge and court
     - Date of judgment
  2. **Application for leave to appeal** (if required), as per Rule 91.08
  3. **Names of counsel** who represented the parties before the trial court or a statement if no representation
  4. **Description of the offence** and relevant section reference
  5. **Plea entered** and any **sentence imposed**
  6. **Statutory authority** for the appeal
  7. **Concise grounds of appeal**
  8. **Order sought** at conclusion of appeal
  9. **Statement of whether the appellant is in prison** and, if so, the place of imprisonment
  10. **Delivery address**:
     - For one appellant: single address
     - For multiple appellants: designated address for all or separate addresses
  11. **Acknowledgment** that documents delivered to the address are considered received, and that further contact info is available from the registrar

#### Forms:
- May be filed in **Form 91.05(A)** (for represented appellants)
- Or **Form 91.05(B)** (for unrepresented appellants, including prisoners)

> **Prisoners' Rights**:  
> Every senior official of a penal institution must provide a copy of Form 91.05(B) upon request to any prisoner in custody.

---

### **5. Service and Timelines**
#### Deadline for Filing:
An appeal may be initiated within **25 days** of:
- The day the appellant is sentenced (if appealing conviction, guilt, or sentence)
- The day a judgment is made (if appealing acquittal or non-conviction)

> Time is calculated under **Rule 94.02** and may be extended under **section 678** of the Code or **Rule 91.04**.

#### Service Requirements (for non-prisoner appeals):
- Appellant must:
  - Provide **two copies** of the notice to the registrar when filed
  - **Serve a copy** on each respondent **before the deadline** in Rule 91.09(1) via personal service or other lawful method
  - Provide **proof of service** to the registrar
  - Send a copy to the judge whose judgment is being appealed

#### Service for Prisoner Appeals:
- Must deliver the original notice, **two copies to the registrar**, and **one copy to each respondent** to the **senior official** of the institution where the appellant is imprisoned.
- The senior official must:
  - Certify the date of delivery
  - Deliver the notice and two copies to the registrar
  - Deliver the third copy to the respondent

> The registrar must send a copy of the notice to the relevant judge.

---

### **6. Cross-Appeal**
A respondent may file a **notice of cross-appeal** within 25 days of receiving a copy of the notice of appeal.

#### Requirements of the Notice of Cross-Appeal:
- Must be entitled **"Notice of Cross-Appeal"**, dated, and signed
- Must include:
  - Same file number as the original notice
  - Names of cross-appellant and cross-respondent
  - Nature of the judgment appealed from
  - Names of judge and court, and date of judgment
  - Application for leave (if required)
  - Statutory authority, grounds, and order sought
  - Delivery address (same structure as for appeal)
  - Acknowledgment of receipt and contact info from registrar

> Rule 91.11 applies to cross-appeals as if:
- “Appeal” = “Cross-appeal”
- “Appellant” = “Respondent who cross-appeals”

---

### **7. Motion to Appoint Hearing Date and Directions**
The appellant (or Attorney General in a prisoner appeal) must:
- File a **motion to appoint a time and date** for the appeal hearing and provide directions (e.g., when transcripts, appeal books, and factums are due)
- Submit this motion **no later than 80 days** after the notice of appeal is filed

> The **judge may set a hearing date on their own motion**, even if the appeal is not yet perfected.

> The appellant or Attorney General must file a **certificate of readiness** (in compliance with Rule 90.26) **at least five days before** the motion is heard.

---

### **8. Filing of Key Documents**
#### Audio Recording:
- Appellant or Attorney General must request the **full audio recording** of the trial proceedings from the prothonotary or clerk
- Must pay the prescribed fee
- Upon payment, the prothonotary must provide:
  - Entire hearing, including:
    - Evidence
    - Oral submissions
    - Jury charge
    - All oral rulings and decisions

#### Transcript:
- Must be prepared by a **certified court reporter** in accordance with **subsection 682(4)** of the Code
- Must include:
  - Headline on each page with witness name and type of examination (direct, cross, redirect)
  - Questions numbered consecutively, beginning with “Q” and answers with “A”
  - Copies of written submissions and transcripts of submissions
  - Exhibits (documentary or electronic), indexed and numbered as at trial
  - Agreements to limit transcript or appeal book content
  - Agreed statements of facts (substituting for omitted parts)
  - Certified jury charge

> **Exception for Sentence Appeals** (Rule 91.15(2)):
- Transcript of trial evidence **not required** if only the sentence is appealed
- Must include:
  - Presentence report
  - Statement of criminal record (if applicable)
  - Copies of all orders related to the sentence (e.g., probation, conditional sentence, discharge)
