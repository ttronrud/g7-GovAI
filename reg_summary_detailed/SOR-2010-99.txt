### **4. Special Rules for Tax Calculation on Supplies After April 6, 2010 (but Before May 1, 2010)**

This section applies when a **taxable supply** (not zero-rated) is made to a **non-consumer** (e.g., a business) and the **consideration (payment)** becomes due or is paid **between April 6, 2010, and May 1, 2010**, and a **portion of that payment relates to services or periods after June 2010**.

#### ðŸ“Œ General Rule:
- The **total tax** under subsection 165(2) of the Act is calculated at **10%** on the portion attributable to the period **after June 2010**.
- However, for **collection purposes**, the registrant **deems the tax to have been calculated at 8%**.
- The **difference** between the 10% total tax and the 8% "tax collectible" is **paid by the recipient** to the government.

> ðŸ” This creates a **"tax adjustment"** mechanism to ensure compliance with the transition to the HST system.

#### âœ… Exceptions (Where the Rule Does Not Apply)

| Type of Supply | Exception Condition |
|---------------|----------------------|
| **Transportation Services** | If the transportation is part of a **continuous journey** that began **before July 1, 2010** |
| **Freight Transportation** | If the **shipper transferred possession** to the first carrier **before July 1, 2010** |
| **Other Services** | If **all or substantially all** of the service was performed **before July 1, 2010** |

> These exceptions ensure that supplies fully completed before the transition date are not subject to retroactive HST adjustments.

### **5. Application to Specific Types of Supplies**

#### (a) **Lease, Licence, or Similar Arrangements**
- Applies when rent/royalty is due **after April 6, 2010**, and **part of the payment relates to a period after June 2010**
- **Total tax** = 10% on the portion after June 2010
- **Tax collectible** = deemed at 8%
- **Difference** must be paid by the recipient

âœ… **Exemption Conditions**:
- The lease period **begins before July 1, 2010 and ends before July 31, 2010**
- The property is **intangible personal property** (e.g., software, IP), and the payment is **not tied to usage or profit**

#### (b) **Membership or Admission Fees**
- Applies when a portion of the membership/admission fee relates to a period **after June 2010**
- **Total tax** = 10% on the portion after June 2010
- **Tax collectible** = deemed at 8%
- **Difference** must be paid by the recipient

âœ… **Exemption Condition**:
- If **all or substantially all** of the membership period is **before July 1, 2010**

#### (c) **Passenger Transportation Passes**
- Applies when the **validity period** includes time **after June 2010**
- **Total tax** = 10% on the portion after June 2010
- **Tax collectible** = deemed at 8%
- **Difference** must be paid by the recipient

âœ… **Exemption Condition**:
- If the **validity period begins before July 1, 2010 and ends before August 1, 2010**

> This ensures that short-term passes with validity in the early part of the transition period are not subject to the adjustment.

#### (d) **Lifetime Membership for an Individual**
- Applies if the **total consideration** (after April 6, 2010 and before July 1, 2010) exceeds **25% of the total consideration**
- **Total tax** = 10% on the **difference between the total amount and 25% of the total consideration**
- **Tax collectible** = deemed at 8%
- **Difference** must be paid by the recipient

> This is a unique rule targeting long-term memberships with large upfront payments, ensuring that only the excess over 25% is taxed at the HST rate.
