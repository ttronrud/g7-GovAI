**Detailed Summary of the Cultural Property Export Regulations**

---

### **Overview**

The *Cultural Property Export Regulations* are a set of administrative rules designed to implement the *Cultural Property Export and Import Act* (the "Act"). These regulations establish the procedures, requirements, and conditions under which Canadian cultural property may be exported. The primary objective is to protect Canada’s cultural heritage by regulating the export of culturally significant objects and ensuring that such exports are justified, properly documented, and monitored.

---

## **Key Definitions**

1. **Act**: Refers to the *Cultural Property Export and Import Act*, which provides the legal framework for regulating the export and import of cultural property.

2. **Object**: Any object or class of objects listed in subsection 4(2) of the Act and included in the *Control List*. This includes items such as archaeological artifacts, historical documents, artworks, and other items of cultural significance.

3. **Public Record**: Any original documentary material created or received by a public authority (or its predecessor) in Canada that contains information about the organization, function, policy, or activities of that authority. This includes archives, maps, photographs, films, and sound recordings.

---

## **Application for an Export Permit**

### **General Requirements**
- An application for an export permit must be submitted in the form established by the Minister, signed by the applicant, and forwarded to a designated **permit officer**.

### **Mandatory Information to Be Included**
The following details must be provided with the application:

| Information | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Applicant details** | Name, address, and telephone number |
| **Owner details** | If the applicant is not the owner, provide the owner’s name, address, and phone number |
| **Shipper/Consignment details** | Name, address, and phone number of the person shipping or consigning the object |
| **Consignee details** | Name, address, and phone number of the recipient |
| **Object description** | A detailed description sufficient to clearly identify the object (subject to sections 3 and 4) |
| **Proposed export date** | The intended date of export |
| **Purpose of export** | Explanation of why the object is being exported |
| **Market value or selling price** | Fair market value in Canada or actual selling price (if sold) |
| **Additional information** | Any information requested by the permit officer, expert examiner, or Minister to clarify the object’s identity or purpose |

### **Photographic or Photostatic Evidence Requirements**
A photograph or photostatic copy of the object must be included in the application if the object falls into any of the following categories:

- Group I, paragraphs 2(a), (b), or (e)
- Group I, paragraphs 3(a), (b), (c), or (d)
- Group I, item 4 (collections)
- Any object in Groups II, III, IV, V, or VI
- Group VII, paragraphs 2(a), (b), 3(a), (b), 4(a), (b), or 5(a), (b)
- Item 7 of Group VII (non-printed books)

**Exception**: For objects described in paragraph (1)(e) or (f) (e.g., documents with more than 12 pages), only **12 pages representative of the object as a whole** need be submitted.

### **Photographs for Collections**
For a collection described in paragraph 2(c) of Group I (collections of specimens), a **separate photograph of each specimen** must be submitted and must be satisfactory to the Minister.

---

## **Exemptions from Application Requirements**

Certain objects are exempt from full application requirements under specific conditions:

1. **Temporary Imports (Paragraph 7(a) of the Act)**  
   - Applies to objects imported temporarily for non-resale purposes (e.g., exhibitions, research).  
   - Exemption applies **only if** the application includes:
     - A permit or document under subparagraph 6(c)(i)
     - An A.T.A. Carnet under subparagraph 6(c)(iii)
     - A declaration under subparagraph 6(c)(iv)

2. **Public Authority Objects (Paragraph 7(b) of the Act)**  
   - Exemption applies **if** the application includes the institution or public authority’s reference number used to identify or catalogue the object.

3. **Institutional Exports (Paragraph 7(c) of the Act)**  
   - Applies to objects exported by institutions or public authorities.  
   - Exemption applies **if** the application includes the object’s inventory, accession, or other reference number.

---

## **Identification of Objects in Applications**

When an object is subject to **paragraph 7(a)** of the Act (temporary import), the object may be identified by providing:

- The name of the object  
- The name of the maker  
- One of the following:
  - Reference number of an export permit issued by a foreign government (if authorized)  
  - Copy of the shipping document that entered Canada (with translation if needed)  
  - Reference number issued by a Canadian institution or public authority  
  - A copy of the most recent A.T.A. Carnet issued for the object  
  - A copy of the import declaration made by the last importer  

> **Note**: For temporary imports, applicants must also provide **documentary evidence or a signed declaration** proving:
> - The object was imported into Canada within the **35 years prior** to the application
> - It was **not previously exported under a permit** issued under the Act

---

## **Expert Examiner Review**

- If a permit officer refers an application to an **expert examiner** (under subsection 8(3) of the Act), the examiner may require the applicant to **produce the physical object** for examination to verify its authenticity, condition, or identity.

---

## **Issuance and Validity of Export Permits**

- Permits are issued in a form established by the Minister.
- A permit is **valid for only 90 days** from the date of issuance.
- **Reinstatement or amendment** is possible if the permit lapses, is suspended, or is cancelled. The applicant must:
  - Forward the original permit to the Minister
  - Submit a request with **clear reasons** for reinstatement or amendment

---

## **Obligations After Permit Issuance**

- The permit holder must provide information to the Minister upon request to verify:
  - The identity of the consignee
  - Whether the object was delivered to the consignee
  - The selling price, if the object was sold

---

## **Export Permits for Specific Purposes (Paragraph 7(c) of the Act)**

This section applies to objects exported for specific purposes, including:

- Appraisal  
- Authentication  
- Conservation  
- Exhibition  
- Loan  
- Processing  
- Research  
- Restoration or repair  
- As personal effects  

### **Conditions for Issuance**
- The applicant must **certify and establish** to the permit officer’s satisfaction that the object is being removed for one of the above purposes.

### **Duration of Permit**
- Maximum period of removal: **Five years**

### **Return Obligation**
- The applicant must include a **written undertaking** in the application stating they will return the object within the time specified in the permit.

### **Reporting Requirements During Removal**
- At any time upon request by the Minister, the permit holder must provide:
  - The **location** of the object during the period it is abroad
  - Details of any **damage, destruction, or loss**

### **Notice of Return**
- Within **15 days** of the object’s return to Canada, the applicant must:
  - Submit a **Notice of Return** (in form established by the Minister)
  - Provide **proof** (satisfactory to the Minister) that the returned object matches the one exported

### **Information Required in Notice of Return**
- Exporter’s name, address, and phone number  
- Reference number of the export permit  
- Documentary evidence of **port of entry and date of entry** into Canada

---

## **Prescribed Classes of Objects under Section 14 of the Act**

These are specifically protected and include:

### **Original Archives of National Historic Interest**
- Made in Canada, or made outside Canada by someone who resided in Canada at any time
- Must relate to the history or national life of Canada
- Includes:
  - Textual records or documents in manuscript form  
  - Maps or cartographic records in manuscript form  
  - Maps or cartographic records in printed form **prior to 1850**  
  - Designs or pictorial records in manuscript form  
  - Designs or pictorial records in printed form **prior to 1880**  
  - Photographs or photographic records **prior to 1900**

### **Original Public Records**
- Any original public record made or received by a public authority, including:
  - Graphic or textual documents  
  - Photographs  
  - Cinematographic films  
  - Sound recordings  

> These are considered high-value cultural property and are subject to strict export controls.

---

## **Export Procedures and Customs Compliance**

- Before exporting any object, the **exporter or their authorized agent** must:
  - Provide the **export permit** to the **collector of customs** at the port of export  
  - If using **Canada Post**, provide the permit to the **postmaster** at the time of posting

---
