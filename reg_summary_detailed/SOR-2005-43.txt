**Detailed Summary of the Yukon Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations**

**Regulatory Authority and Source**  
These regulations are issued under **subsection 18(1) of the Sex Offender Information Registration Act (SOIRA)**, which empowers the Commissioner of Yukon to make regulations to implement and administer the Act. The regulations were formally adopted on **February 10, 2005**, and are published under **S.C. 2004, c. 10**. They came into force on the day of registration.

---

### **Key Definitions**

The regulations begin by establishing key terms used throughout:

- **Act**: Refers to the *Sex Offender Information Registration Act*, which provides the legal framework for registering, monitoring, and managing information about individuals convicted of certain sex offences.
- **RCMP**: Stands for the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police*, the federal law enforcement agency responsible for collecting and maintaining sex offender information in Yukon.
- **YSOIRC**: The *Yukon Sex Offender Information Registration Centre*, located in Whitehorse and administered by the RCMP’s “M” Division (which handles criminal intelligence and offender management).

---

### **Notification Requirements for Sex Offenders**

- A sex offender whose **primary residence is within Yukon** may fulfill any notification obligations under **Section 6 of the Act** (which requires registration and reporting) by making the notification **by telephone** to the YSOIRC.
- This provision simplifies compliance for individuals living in Yukon, allowing them to report changes in address, registration status, or other required information without needing to submit formal written documentation.

---

### **Authorized Personnel to Collect Information**

- **Members of the RCMP** are explicitly authorized in Yukon to **collect information** for the purposes of the Act.
- This includes gathering data from individuals, courts, or other sources related to sex offences, such as conviction records or registration details.

---

### **Authorized Persons to Register Information**

The following individuals are authorized in Yukon to **register information** under the Act:

1. **A member of the RCMP**  
2. **A person employed by the RCMP whose duties include the registration of information collected under the Act**

This ensures that only trained and designated personnel within the RCMP can officially record and maintain sex offender data, maintaining accountability and data integrity.

---

### **Registration Centres and Service Areas**

- Each **RCMP detachment** operating in Yukon is designated as a **registration centre**.
- The **designated area of service** for each registration centre is the geographic area served by that detachment. This ensures local accountability and efficient service delivery.
- The **YSOIRC** is designated as a **central registration centre** for the entire territory.
- Its **designated area of service is all of Yukon**, meaning it serves as the central hub for receiving, processing, and managing sex offender information from across the territory.

---

### **Effective Date**

These regulations come into force on the **day they are registered**, which is **February 10, 2005**.

---

### **Purpose and Significance**

These regulations establish a clear, accessible, and legally sound framework for:

- Ensuring that sex offenders residing in Yukon comply with reporting requirements.
- Providing a centralized and accountable system for collecting, registering, and managing sex offender information.
- Facilitating coordination between local RCMP detachments and the central YSOIRC.
- Supporting public safety by enabling timely and accurate tracking of individuals convicted of sex offences.

By defining roles, authorizations, and service areas, the regulations ensure consistency, reduce administrative burden, and enhance the effectiveness of sex offender monitoring in Yukon.

---

**Conclusion**  
The Yukon Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations provide a practical and legally grounded system for managing sex offender information within the territory. They balance the need for public safety with administrative efficiency by clearly defining who can collect and register data, how notifications can be made, and where information is processed. The regulations remain a foundational component of Yukon’s approach to sex offender management under federal legislation.
