**Detailed Summary of the Export Permits Regulations (S.C. 1994, c. 47, s. 112(1))**

---

### **Overview**

The *Export Permits Regulations* are a set of rules established under the *Export and Import Permits Act* (the "Act") by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs. These regulations provide detailed procedural and substantive requirements for the issuance, application for, and compliance with export permits in Canada.

They came into force on **April 15, 1997**, and govern the conditions under which Canadian entities may export specific goods and technologiesâ€”particularly those subject to national or international controlsâ€”while ensuring that such exports align with foreign policy, national security, and international obligations.

## **Key Provisions and Interpretations**

### **1. Definitions**

The regulations define key terms used throughout:

- **Act**: Refers to the *Export and Import Permits Act*.
- **Applicant**: A Canadian resident applying for an export permit or an amendment to an existing one.
- **Controlled Goods**: Previously defined under the Defence Production Act (DPA), now largely replaced or repealed; the term "DPA controlled goods" is retained as referring to goods listed in Section 35 of the *Defence Production Act*.
- **Customs Office**: As defined in subsection 2(1) of the *Customs Act*â€”the designated government body responsible for customs enforcement.
- **Goods**: Includes:
  - Goods listed in the *Guide* (a reference document) intended for export to a destination specified in Section 2 of the *Export Control List*; or
  - Goods intended for export to a country on the *Area Control List*.
- **Guide**: As defined in Section 1 of the *List* (the Export Control List).
- **List**: Refers to the *Export Control List*, which categorizes goods and technologies subject to export controls.
- **Officer**: As defined in subsection 2(1) of the *Customs Act*â€”a customs official who may inspect and verify export compliance.
- **Permit**: An official export permit issued under subsection 7(1) of the *Export and Import Permits Act*.
- **United States Export Authorization**: A copy of any of the following approvals issued by the U.S. Department of State or Commerce under the *International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR)*, Titles 22, Parts 120â€“130:
  - Export licence
  - Warehousing and Distribution Agreement
  - Technical Assistance Agreement
  - Manufacturing Licence Agreement
  - Re-export authorization letter
  - U.S. export licence exemption

### **2. Exclusions from Scope**

These regulations **do not apply** to:

- **Softwood lumber products** described in **item 5104 of Group 5** of the schedule to the *Export Control List*.
- **Goods listed in items 5200 to 5210 of Group 5** of the schedule to the List.
- **Exports governed by subsection 4(1)** of the Act (which may relate to certain non-controlled or exempted goods).

### **3. Application Requirements for Export Permits**

An applicant must submit a **duly completed and signed application form** provided by the Minister, including the following information:

#### **A. Basic Applicant Information**
- Date of completion of the form
- Applicantâ€™s full name, address, phone number, email, fax, and any identifier number assigned by the Minister
- If the applicant is a corporation, contact details of a representative familiar with the application

#### **B. Third-Party Information (if applicable)**
- Name, address, contact details, and identifier number of the person or corporation who will use or export the goods
- Contact person familiar with the application

#### **C. Consignee Details**
- Name, address, phone, email, and fax of each consignee (recipient)
- Contact person at the consignee familiar with the application

#### **D. End-Use and Destination**
- Country where the goods will be used or final destination

#### **E. Goods or Technology Details (per type)**
- Country of origin
- If any U.S. origin component exists and is included in **item 5400 of Group 5**, the proportion of U.S.-origin materials to total cost (as a percentage)
- Item number in the *Guide* (if applicable)
- Detailed description of goods/technology:
  - Purpose and technical specifications
  - Must be specific enough to avoid vague terms (e.g., no trade names or general descriptions)
- Quantity, unit value, and total market value
- Factory or first shipping point in Canada
- Approximate mass, net weight, or volume

#### **F. Delivery and Communication Method**
- How the permit should be sent (e.g., by mail, email, courier)

#### **G. End-Use and Compliance Evidence**
To demonstrate that the export is consistent with the intended purpose, applicants must provide **one or more** of the following:
- International Import Certificate
- End-Use Certificate
- End-Use Statement
- Copy of sale contract (between applicant/exporter and seller or buyer)
- Copy of commercial invoice
- Copy of letter of credit or financial documentation (identifying Canadian and foreign institutions)
- Name and address of seller
- Intended end-use of the goods
- Intended end-use location (if different from consignee location)
- Import permit from destination country
- In-transit authorization

### **4. Additional Required Submissions**

Beyond the application form, applicants must also submit:

#### **A. Declaration of Compliance**
- A statement confirming that:
  - The goods will enter the economy of the country specified in the application
  - The goods will **not be transshipped or diverted** from that country

#### **B. DPA Control Requirements (for Group 2, Group 6, or item 5504 of Group 5)**
If the goods are **DPA controlled goods**:
- Proof of registration or exemption from registration under the *Defence Production Act* and *Controlled Goods Regulations*
- OR proof that the applicant/exporter is **exempt from Part 2 of the DPA** under Section 36 of that Act

If the goods are **not DPA controlled**:
- A declaration that the applicant has reviewed the DPA and confirms they are **not exporting or transferring DPA-controlled goods**

#### **C. United States Export Authorization**
Must be submitted for:
- Any DPA-controlled goods of U.S. origin
- Any goods incorporating DPA-controlled goods of U.S. origin
- Any goods manufactured in Canada using DPA-controlled goods of U.S. origin

> This requirement ensures compliance with U.S. export controls, particularly for dual-use or defense-related items.

### **6. Export Compliance and Reporting**

At the time of export (including goods containing controlled technology), the **exporter must provide the permit to a customs officer** at the customs office **within the timeframes specified in the *Reporting of Exported Goods Regulations***.

The customs officer must:
- Verify that the permit is **valid**
- Compare the information in the permit with the information in the prescribed form under the *Customs Act*

This ensures that all export activities are properly authorized and recorded.
