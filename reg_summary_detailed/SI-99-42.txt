**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Title:**  
*Proclamation Declaring Clean Air Day Canada*

**Issuing Authority:**  
Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II, by and with the advice of the Privy Council for Canada, acting through the Governor General of Canada.

**Date of Proclamation:**  
April 20, 1999

**Legal Status:**  
A formal proclamation issued under the authority of the Governor General in Council, pursuant to statutory powers related to environmental policy and public awareness.

---

### **Purpose and Background (Reasons for the Proclamation):**

The proclamation is rooted in several key concerns:

1. **Federal Priority on Clean Air:**  
   The federal government recognizes clean air as a central environmental and public health priority.

2. **Global and Local Impacts of Air Pollution:**  
   There are significant and growing threats from atmospheric pollution and greenhouse gas emissions—both globally and locally—leading to climate change, health risks, and environmental degradation.

3. **Public Health and Well-Being:**  
   Healthy air quality is essential for the physical health, social stability, and economic prosperity of all Canadians and their natural environment.

4. **Shared Responsibility:**  
   Canadians recognize that reducing harmful emissions requires coordinated action across all levels of society—including government, industry, and individuals.

5. **Establishing a National Awareness Day:**  
   To promote public awareness and encourage collective action, the government seeks to institutionalize a dedicated day focused on clean air.

---

### **Key Provision of the Proclamation:**

The Governor General in Council, by Order in Council P.C. 1999-573 dated March 25, 1999, has declared:

> **"The Wednesday of Canadian Environment Week in June of each year to be Clean Air Day Canada."**

This means that annually, on the **Wednesday during Canadian Environment Week (which typically occurs in June)**, the day is officially recognized as **Clean Air Day Canada**.

---

### **Legal and Practical Implications:**

- **Recognition and Awareness:**  
  The proclamation formally designates Clean Air Day Canada as a national observance, encouraging public, governmental, and organizational participation in promoting clean air initiatives.

- **Call to Action:**  
  It serves as a reminder to individuals, communities, businesses, and institutions to take responsibility for reducing air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions.

- **Educational and Outreach Role:**  
  The day is intended to be used for public education campaigns, community events, school programs, and environmental advocacy to raise awareness about air quality issues and sustainable practices.

- **Non-Regulatory in Nature:**  
  Importantly, this proclamation is **not a regulatory measure**. It does not impose legal obligations or emission standards. Instead, it is a symbolic and educational initiative to promote awareness and encourage voluntary action.

---

### **Significance and Legacy:**

- **Foundation for Future Environmental Policy:**  
  This proclamation laid the groundwork for Canada’s broader environmental agenda, particularly in the context of climate change and air quality management.

- **Part of a Larger Environmental Framework:**  
  It aligns with other federal environmental policies and programs, such as the *Canadian Environmental Protection Act* and efforts to meet international commitments under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC).

- **Cultural and Institutional Impact:**  
  Clean Air Day Canada became a recurring national event, helping to embed environmental consciousness into Canadian civic life.

---

### **Final Note:**

The proclamation is issued with the Great Seal of Canada and affixed by the Governor General, Roméo A. LeBlanc, who was acting on the advice of the Privy Council. It is a formal, ceremonial instrument of governance that reflects the federal government’s commitment to environmental stewardship.

---

### **Conclusion:**

This regulation is a **non-binding, symbolic proclamation** that establishes **Clean Air Day Canada** as an annual national observance on the **Wednesday of Canadian Environment Week in June**. Its primary purpose is to raise public awareness about the importance of clean air, promote environmental responsibility, and encourage collective action among individuals, communities, and institutions. While it does not contain enforceable rules or emission targets, it plays a vital role in shaping public discourse and supporting Canada’s broader environmental goals.
