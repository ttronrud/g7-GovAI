## âœ… Core Obligations for Societies

### 1. **Asset Handling Procedures**
- A society must:
  - Maintain a **written record** outlining the **procedures for handling and safeguarding** its assets.
  - **Inform all relevant personnel** (directors, officers, employees, agents) who have access to or are involved in asset management about these procedures.

> Purpose: Ensures transparency, accountability, and clear lines of responsibility in asset protection.

---

### 2. **Security Recordkeeping**
- The society must maintain an **up-to-date record** identifying **each security** it owns or holds.

> This includes details such as issuer, type, quantity, value, and current status.

> Purpose: Enables auditability and traceability of financial holdings.

---

### 3. **Secure Custody of Securities**
- **General Rule**:  
  Every security must be kept securely, in a manner that prevents unauthorized access, and in one of the following forms:
  - In the **custody of the society**; or  
  - In the custody of an **authorized entity** that:
    - Is licensed to act as a **custodian of securities**, or  
    - Is a **depository or clearing agency** authorized by law in the jurisdiction where it operates.

> âš ï¸ **Exceptions to Custody Requirement**:
  - Securities under the **control of the government** of the jurisdiction where the society operates.
  - Securities **pledged as collateral** for debt or potential debt.
  - Securities **loaned** to a person under a written agreement.
  - Securities **in transit** (e.g., being transported).

> Purpose: Balances security with practicality â€” avoids unnecessary restrictions in common scenarios.

---

### 4. **Custodial Agreement Requirement**
- A society **must not** place a security with an authorized custodian unless it has entered into a **written custodial agreement** with that entity.

> This ensures legal accountability, clarity of responsibilities, and protection against misuse.

---

### 5. **Daily Deposit of Net Proceeds**
- On a **daily basis**, any net amount received from a **security transaction** (e.g., sale, redemption) must be deposited into one of the following accounts:

| Option | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| With the society itself | (Self-held account) |
| With a **financial institution** authorized to accept deposits in the jurisdiction |
| With a **trust company** authorized to hold money in trust |
| With the **government** of the jurisdiction or its authorized agency (e.g., securities custodian) |
| With **CDS Clearing and Depository Services Inc.** (a major Canadian securities depository) |

> Purpose: Prevents misuse or misappropriation of funds by ensuring liquidity is immediately secured and accessible only through authorized channels.

### 6. **Registration of Securities**
- **General Rule**:  
  Every security must be **registered in the societyâ€™s name** in the register of the issuing entity.

> Exceptions (where registration is not required):
  - The security **cannot be registered** due to reasons **beyond the societyâ€™s control** (e.g., technical or legal barriers).
  - Under the **control of the government**.

### 7. **Bearer Form or Alternative Registration (Where Applicable)**
- If a security is **already protected against loss, fraud, theft, or destruction**, the society may:

> This exception applies only when **risk of loss is mitigated** (e.g., through physical security, insurance, or electronic safeguards).

> Purpose: Allows flexibility in holding certain securities without requiring full registration, provided risks are adequately managed.

### 8. **Dishonesty or Criminal Acts Coverage (Bond)**
- A society must acquire and maintain **one or more bonds** (insurance policies) to cover losses arising from:
  - **Dishonest or criminal acts** by officers or employees.

> These bonds must:
- Be issued by an entity authorized to insure risks under the laws of the jurisdiction where the insurer operates.
- Provide **at least 30 daysâ€™ notice** before cancellation or termination:
  - The bond **cannot be cancelled or terminated** until **30 days after** the Superintendent of Insurance receives a written notice from the insurer or the insured (the society) of their intention to cancel or terminate.

> Purpose: Protects against fraud or embezzlement by key personnel and ensures stability in the event of misconduct.

---

### 9. **General Asset Protection Insurance (Policy)**
- A society must also acquire and maintain **insurance policies** to cover losses from:
  - **Damage, destruction, or mysterious disappearance** of assets.
  - Any other **normal contingency** (e.g., fire, natural disaster, theft, administrative error).

> These policies are required for **all assets owned or held** by the society.

> Purpose: Mitigates risks of physical or financial loss due to unforeseen events.

---

### 10. **Amount of Insurance and Bond Coverage**
- The amount of the bond or insurance policy must be determined by the **supreme governing body** of the society, taking into account:
  - The **nature and value** of the assets owned or held;
  - The **arrangements and procedures** in place for handling and safeguarding assets;
  - Any **other factors** that might affect the potential extent of loss.

> This ensures coverage is **proportional and reasonable**, not excessive or arbitrary.

> Purpose: Promotes prudent risk management based on actual exposure.
