**Detailed Summary of the Crown Corporation Corporate Plan, Budget and Summaries Regulations**

---

### **Regulatory Background and Legal Authority**

The regulation is issued by the **Treasury Board of Canada** under **paragraphs 126(a) and (b) of the Financial Administration Act (FAA)**. It serves to **repeal two outdated regulations**:

1. **Crown Corporation Corporate Plan and Budget Regulations**, made under decision **T.B. 802718 of July 23, 1986**  
2. **Crown Corporation Summaries Regulations, 1986**, made under decision **T.B. 804020 of December 18, 1986**

These older regulations were replaced by a new, more comprehensive set of rules that provide clearer, more consistent, and modernized requirements for the preparation, submission, and parliamentary presentation of corporate plans, budgets, and summaries for **parent Crown corporations**.

The new regulations are officially titled:  
ðŸ‘‰ **Crown Corporation Corporate Plan, Budget and Summaries Regulations**  
They are published in the *Canada Gazette, Part II*, under **SOR/86-828 (1986)** and **SOR/87-37 (1987)**.

---

### **Key Provisions and Interpretations**

#### **1. Definition of "Act"**
- "Act" refers to the **Financial Administration Act (FAA)**, which governs the financial management and accountability of federal institutions, including Crown corporations.

---

#### **2. Scope of a Corporate Plan**

A **corporate plan** of a parent Crown corporation must cover a **defined five-year period**, structured as follows:

- The **financial year in which the plan is submitted**  
- The **financial year immediately preceding** the submission year  
- The **five financial years following** the submission year  

ðŸ‘‰ **Total coverage: 7 financial years**  
This structure ensures that plans provide both forward-looking strategic direction and historical context for performance evaluation.

> Example:  
> If a plan is submitted for the 2025 fiscal year, it must cover:  
> - 2024 (prior year)  
> - 2025 (current year)  
> - 2026, 2027, 2028, 2029, and 2030 (next five years)

This extended timeframe supports long-term planning, risk assessment, and financial sustainability.

---

#### **3. Submission Deadlines**

- A parent Crown corporation must submit its **corporate plan**, **capital budget**, and **operating budget** (if required by the FAA) to the **appropriate Minister**  
- **Deadline**: No later than **eight weeks before the start of each financial year**

> Example:  
> For the 2025 financial year, the submission must be made by **no later than October 15, 2024** (assuming financial year starts January 1).

This ensures that budgets and plans are available to ministers and policy decision-makers in time to inform annual policy, funding, and oversight decisions.

---

#### **4. Handling of Unavailable Information**

If any information normally required in a corporate plan, operating budget, capital budget, or amendment is **not available**, the corporation must:

- Clearly **state the reasons** for the unavailability of that information  
- Include this explanation in the submitted plan, budget, or amendment

This provision promotes **transparency and accountability**, ensuring that decision-makers are aware of data gaps and limitations, rather than receiving incomplete or misleading information.

---

#### **5. Language Requirements**

- **Submission in both official languages**:  
  All corporate plans, operating budgets, capital budgets, and amendments must be submitted to the Minister **in both English and French**.

- **Graphs, tables, and calculations**:  
  These may be prepared in **only one official language** (either English or French).  
  This practical flexibility reduces administrative burden while maintaining compliance with bilingualism principles.

> Note: The requirement for bilingual submission applies to the **textual content** and **descriptive elements**, but visual elements (charts, tables) can be presented in one language.

---

#### **6. Parliamentary Summaries â€“ Timing and Approval Conditions**

Summaries of corporate plans, budgets, or amendments must be laid before **each House of Parliament** (House of Commons and Senate) on **any of the first 30 days** after a House is sitting, **provided** that one of the following approvals has been obtained:

| Type of Summary | Approval Requirement |
|----------------|------------------------|
| **Summary of a corporate plan or amended plan** | Approval of the plan by the **Governor in Council** under **section 122 of the FAA** |
| **Summary of an operating or capital budget included in a corporate plan** | Approval of the plan by the **Governor in Council** (same as above) |
| **Summary of an operating or capital budget (not part of a corporate plan)** | Approval of the budget by the **Treasury Board** under **section 123 or 124 of the FAA**, as applicable |

> This ensures that parliamentary scrutiny occurs only after formal government approval, aligning with the principle of **accountability and democratic oversight**.

> The summary must be presented within **30 days of the House beginning its session**, giving Parliament sufficient time to review and debate the financial proposals.

---

### **Purpose and Significance of the Regulation**

This regulation modernizes and strengthens the financial accountability framework for **Crown corporations**â€”federally owned entities that operate in key sectors such as transportation, energy, telecommunications, and public services.

Key objectives include:

1. **Improved Transparency**  
   - Clearer timelines, required content, and language rules enhance public access and understanding.

2. **Enhanced Accountability**  
   - Mandatory submission deadlines and parliamentary summaries ensure that financial decisions are subject to ministerial and legislative review.

3. **Strategic Long-Term Planning**  
   - The 7-year plan coverage allows for sustainable, forward-looking planning and performance evaluation.

4. **Compliance with Bilingualism**  
   - Ensures equal access to information for both English and French-speaking Canadians.

5. **Efficient Oversight**  
   - Streamlines the process of budget and plan approval through defined triggers (e.g., Governor in Council or Treasury Board approval) before parliamentary presentation.

---

### **Conclusion**

The **Crown Corporation Corporate Plan, Budget and Summaries Regulations** represent a significant update to the financial governance of federal Crown corporations. By replacing outdated rules with a clear, structured, and transparent framework, the Treasury Board enhances financial accountability, supports strategic planning, and ensures that Parliament remains properly informed about the financial direction of these key public institutions.

This regulation reflects Canadaâ€™s ongoing commitment to **fiscal responsibility, transparency, and democratic accountability** in public administration.
