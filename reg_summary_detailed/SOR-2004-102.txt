---

## **Tax Court of Canada Rules of Procedure Respecting the Excise Act, 2001 (Informal Procedure)**  
*Effective Date: December 16, 2003 (Published in Canada Gazette, Part I)*

---

### **I. Background and Legal Authority**

- These rules were **proposed** by the Rules Committee of the Tax Court of Canada under **subsection 22(3)a** of the *Tax Court of Canada Act* and published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **August 30, 2003**.
- The proposal invited **public comment** from interested parties.
- After reviewing submissions, the Rules Committee, under **section 20b** of the Act and with **approval from Her Excellency the Governor in Council** (on the recommendation of the Minister of Justice), **formally adopted** the proposed rules.
- The rules came into force upon **publication in the Canada Gazette, Part I**, on **December 16, 2003**.
- The rules are **cited as**: *Tax Court of Canada Rules of Procedure Respecting the Excise Act, 2001 (Informal Procedure)*.

---

### **II. Scope of Application**

- These rules apply to **appeals under the Excise Act, 2001**, **except** where the *General Procedure Rules* of the Tax Court of Canada apply.
- The rules govern **informal procedure** in tax appeals related to excise assessments.

---

### **III. Jurisdiction and Judicial Authority**

- **Associate Judges** have **all the powers of a full judge** under these rules.
- An associate judge may:
  - Sit and act **anytime and anywhere in Canada**.
  - In their absence or when acting, constitute the **full Court** for the purposes of hearing and deciding appeals.

---

### **IV. Filing Procedures**

#### **1. Filing a Notice of Appeal**
- Must be filed using **one of three methods**:
  - Deposited with the **Registry**;
  - Sent by **mail** to the Registry;
  - Sent by **fax** or **electronic filing** to the Registry.
- The notice must include:
  - The **appellant’s address for service** of documents.
  - This address may be the appellant’s, their counsel, or an agent.

#### **2. Change of Address**
- Any change in address must be **immediately notified** to the Registry.
- Until such change is recorded, service is deemed valid if sent to the address in the original notice.

#### **3. Filing Other Documents**
- All documents (other than the notice of appeal) may be filed via:
  - Deposit with the Registry;
  - Mail;
  - Fax;
  - Electronic filing.
- **Date of filing** is determined as:
  - For physical or faxed documents: the **date shown on the Registry’s receipt stamp**.
  - For electronically filed documents: the **date shown on the Court’s electronic acknowledgment**.

#### **4. Electronic Filing**
- The **printed copy** of the document provided by the Registry is deemed the **original version**.
- Parties must provide a **paper copy** if requested by the Court or a party.
- If the Registry has **no record** of receipt, the document is deemed **not filed**, unless the Court directs otherwise.

---

### **V. Reply to Notice of Appeal**

- The respondent must file a **reply** that includes:
  1. Facts admitted;
  2. Facts denied;
  3. Facts unknown to the respondent and in issue;
  4. Findings or assumptions made by the Minister during assessment;
  5. Any other material facts;
  6. Issues to be decided;
  7. Statutory provisions relied upon;
  8. Reasons for the respondent’s position;
  9. Relief sought.
- The Minister must **serve a copy of the reply** by **registered mail** to the appellant’s address for service within **five days** of filing.

---

### **VI. Expert Witnesses**

- A party wishing to call an expert witness must:
  - File a **signed report** at least **10 days before the hearing**.
  - The report must include:
    - Expert’s name, address, and qualifications;
    - Substance of the testimony.
- The expert **may not testify** unless **leave of the presiding judge** is granted.

---

### **VII. Discontinuance of Appeal**

- Any party may **discontinue an appeal** at any time by submitting a **written notice** in the form set out in **Schedule 8**.
- This allows for withdrawal of the appeal without further proceedings.

---

### **VIII. Disposition of Appeals and Applications**

- The Court may:
  - **Render a judgment** on appeals involving **substantive rights** (e.g., final decision on tax liability).
  - **Issue an order** for interlocutory or procedural matters (e.g., discovery, costs, scheduling).
- A **judgment**:
  - Is **dated on the day it is signed**.
  - Must be **deposited with the Registry without delay**.
  - Includes **reasons** (if any).

---

### **IX. Costs and Taxation**

#### **1. Cost Awards**
- The Court may:
  - Determine the **amount of costs** for all parties;
  - Allocate costs;
  - Order specific parties to pay costs.
- **Costs may be awarded only to the respondent** if:
  - The appellant’s actions have **unduly delayed** the resolution of the appeal.

#### **2. Fixed Cost Awards**
- The Court may **direct a fixed sum** in lieu of taxed costs.

#### **3. Fees for Counsel Services**
- The following fees may be allowed (taxed on a per-service basis):
  - Preparation of notice of appeal or advice: **$185**
  - Preparation for hearing: **$250**
  - Conduct of hearing: **$375 per half-day or part thereof**
  - Taxation of costs: **$60**

#### **4. Fees for Non-Counsel Advisers**
- If an appellant is assisted by an adviser (not counsel), disbursements may be allowed at **half the listed amounts** (e.g., $92.50 for preparation).
- Other disbursements may be allowed if:
  - They were **essential** to the appeal;
  - They were **actually incurred** or the party is **liable** for them.

#### **5. Taxes on Counsel Fees**
- All **services, sales, use, or consumption taxes** paid or payable on counsel fees or disbursements may be allowed **if**:
  - They are **not otherwise reimbursed** (e.g., via input tax credits).

#### **6. Witness Compensation**
- **Non-expert witnesses**:
  - Paid **$75 per day** plus **reasonable transportation and living expenses**.
  - Payment only if the **appellant is called to testify** by the respondent’s counsel.
- **Expert witnesses**:
  - Paid **up to $300 per day** for preparation and testimony.
  - Amount may be adjusted by the Court.

#### **7. Taxation of Costs**
- Taxation is conducted by:
  - The **Registrar**, or
  - A **taxing officer** designated by the Chief Justice.
- An appellant must file a **bill of costs** (in the form of **Schedule 14**) with the Registrar.
- The Registrar:
  - Sends a **true copy** to the respondent’s counsel immediately.
  - Issues a **certificate of taxation** after taxation.
- Any party may **appeal the taxation** within **20 days** of the certificate’s mailing.
  - The time may be **extended** by the Court.

---

### **X. Special Provisions for Time Extensions**

#### **1. Extension of Time to File Objection**
- A person who has applied to the Minister to extend time to file a **notice of objection** may apply to the Court **after**:
  - The Minister **refuses** the request, **or**
  - **90 days** have passed since service and the Minister has not responded.
- **Deadline for application**: No later than **30 days after the Minister’s decision is mailed**.
- Application must include:
  - Three copies of the **application to the Minister**;
  - Three copies of the **notice of objection**;
  - Three copies of the **Minister’s decision** (if any).
- The Court may:
  - **Dismiss** the application;
  - **Grant** it, with **terms as deemed just**;
  - **Deem the notice of objection valid** from the date of the order.
- **Conditions for grant**:
  - Application must be made **within one year** of the expiry of the time allowed under the Excise Act for filing an objection.
  - The applicant must demonstrate:
    - Inability to act or give a mandate;
    - A **bona fide intention** to object;
    - That it would be **just and equitable** to grant the extension;
    - The application was made as soon as circumstances allowed.

> **Note**: The application is deemed filed on the **date of receipt by the Registry**, even without full documentation, **provided** the required documents are filed within **30 days** or a reasonable time set by the Court.
