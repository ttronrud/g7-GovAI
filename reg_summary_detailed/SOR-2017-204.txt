## **Detailed Summary of the Special Economic Measures (Venezuela) Regulations**

### **1. Legal Authority and Purpose**
- **Enacted by:** His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**, under **subsections 4(1) to (3)** of the *Special Economic Measures Act (SEMA)*.
- **Purpose:** To implement the **decision of the Association Concerning the Situation in Venezuela**, adopted on **September 5, 2017**, which condemned human rights violations, political instability, and systemic corruption in Venezuela.
- These regulations establish a list of individuals and entities (the "listed persons") whose activities are restricted due to their involvement in undermining Venezuela’s democratic institutions, human rights, or governance.

> ⚖️ **Key Legal Basis:** The *Special Economic Measures Act* empowers Canada to impose sanctions on individuals and entities linked to regimes that violate international norms or undermine democratic stability.

---

### **2. Definitions**

#### **Minister**
- Refers to the **Minister of Foreign Affairs** of Canada.

#### **Venezuela**
- Includes:
  - The **Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela** and all its **political subdivisions** (e.g., states, municipalities).
  - Its **government bodies**, including departments and agencies at all levels.
  - Any **government or department** within political subdivisions.
  - Any **agency or subdivision** of Venezuela or its political units.

> This broad definition ensures that the scope of sanctions extends to all levels of Venezuelan state institutions.

---

### **3. Who is a "Listed Person"?**

A person is listed if they meet **any of the following criteria**:

| Criterion | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **(a)** Engaged in activities that directly or indirectly undermine peace, security, or stability in Venezuela or the integrity of its democratic institutions. | Includes actions that threaten national stability or democratic processes. |
| **(b)** Engaged in activities contributing to or supporting **gross and systematic human rights violations** in Venezuela. | Focuses on severe, widespread abuse (e.g., suppression of dissent, torture, arbitrary detention). |
| **(c)** Engaged in activities contributing to **significant corruption** in Venezuela. | Includes acts that facilitate bribery, embezzlement, or abuse of power. |
| **(d)** Is a **current or former senior official** in the Government of Venezuela. | Includes high-ranking officials such as ministers, governors, or heads of state. |
| **(e)** Is a **current or former member** of the **Constituent National Assembly of Venezuela**. | The assembly was a controversial body formed during Venezuela’s political crisis. |
| **(f)** Is an **associate or family member** of someone listed under (a) to (e). | Extends sanctions to relatives and close associates. |
| **(g)** Is an **entity** (company, organization) **owned, held, or controlled directly or indirectly** by a person listed under (a) to (e). | Applies to companies or institutions tied to sanctioned individuals. |
| **(h)** Is a **current or former senior official** of an entity listed under (g). | Ensures accountability for leadership roles in sanctioned entities. |

> This definition ensures a **comprehensive and cascading impact** of sanctions — not only targeting individuals but also their networks and affiliated organizations.

---

### **4. Prohibited Activities (Section 3)**

Any person **in Canada or outside Canada** is prohibited from:

1. **Dealing in property** owned or controlled (directly or indirectly) by a listed person — *wherever located*.
2. **Entering into or facilitating transactions** related to such property dealings.
3. **Providing financial or related services** in connection with such dealings.
4. **Making available goods** (anywhere in the world) to a listed person or someone acting on their behalf.
5. **Providing financial or related services** to or for the benefit of a listed person.

> These prohibitions apply globally — even if the property is located abroad or the transaction occurs outside Canada.

---

### **5. Exceptions (Section 3 Exceptions)**

Certain transactions are **exempt** from the prohibitions, including:

| Exception | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Pre-existing contractual payments** | Payments due under a contract entered *before* the person became listed, provided they are not paid to a listed person or their representative. |
| **Transfers of accounts/funds/investments** | Required to transfer funds from a listed person to a non-listed person, where the transfer was held *on the day of listing*. |
| **Loan repayments** | Required for loans made to Canadians *before* the person became listed, including enforcement of security or payments by guarantors. |
| **Government benefits** | Payments under: <br> - Old Age Security Act <br> - Canada Pension Plan (CPP) <br> - Québec Pension Plan (CQLR, c. R-9) <br> - Superannuation, pensions, or retirement savings plans <br> - Garnishment, Attachment and Pension Diversion Act <br> - Disability benefits |
| **Legal services** | Financial services needed for a listed person to obtain legal representation in Canada regarding these sanctions. |
| **Diplomatic functions** | Transactions required by a diplomatic mission to perform its functions under the **Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations** — including during temporary or permanent recall. |
| **Transactions with international organizations** | Includes UN agencies, International Red Cross, or entities with grant agreements with DFAIT (Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development). |
| **Canada–Venezuela agreements** | Any transaction authorized under formal agreements between Canada and Venezuela. |

> These exceptions ensure **practicality and fairness**, protecting essential services, legal rights, and diplomatic operations.

---

### **6. Ongoing Compliance Obligations (Section 6)**

The following **Canadian entities** must **continuously monitor** their possession or control of property that may belong to or be controlled by a listed person:

| Entity Types | Scope |
|-----------|-------|
| Authorized foreign banks (under Bank Act) | In their Canadian business activities. |
| Cooperative credit societies, savings & credit unions, caisses populaires | Regulated under provincial laws or the *Cooperative Credit Associations Act*. |
| Foreign companies (under Insurance Companies Act) | In their insurance business in Canada. |
| Companies, provincial companies, societies | As defined in Insurance Companies Act. |
| Fraternal benefit societies | In insurance activities. |
| Trust and loan companies | Regulated under Trust and Loan Companies Act. |
| Provincial trust companies | Regulated under provincial legislation. |
| Loan companies | Regulated under provincial legislation. |
| Entities opening client accounts | Under paragraph 5(h) of the *Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act*. |
| Entities providing securities/dealing or investment advice | Authorized under provincial legislation. |

> This requirement imposes a **duty of vigilance** on financial and service providers to detect and report potential violations.

---

### **7. Disclosure Requirement (Section 7)**

Every person or entity in Canada (including Canadians abroad) must **disclose without delay** to either:

- The **Commissioner of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)**, or  
- The **Director of the Canadian Security Intelligence Service (CSIS)**  

The following information:

1. The **existence of property** in their possession or control that they **reasonably believe** is owned or controlled by a listed person.  
2. Any **transaction or proposed transaction** involving such property.

> This is a **mandatory reporting obligation** to prevent illicit financial flows.

---

### **8. Immunity for Good-Faith Disclosures**

- **No legal action** (civil or criminal) may be brought against a person who discloses information in **good faith** under this section.
- This protects individuals and entities from liability for reporting suspected violations.

> Encourages transparency and cooperation without fear of retaliation.

---

### **9. Review and Removal from List**

#### **Application to Remove Name**
- A listed person may **apply in writing** to the Minister to have their name removed from the schedule.
- The Minister must assess whether there are **reasonable grounds** to recommend removal to the Governor in Council.

#### **Material Change in Circumstances**
- If there has been a **material change in circumstances** since the last application, a person may submit a **new application**.

> Provides a **review mechanism** to ensure sanctions are not applied indefinitely without justification.

---

### **10. Name Dispute Resolution**

- A person whose name is **similar or identical** to a listed person and who claims they are not that person may apply to the Minister for a **certificate of non-identity**.
- The Minister must respond within **30 days**:
  - If the applicant is proven **not to be** the listed person → issue a certificate.
  - If not proven → provide notice of the determination.

> Prevents identity confusion and protects individuals from being wrongly targeted due to name similarity.

---

### **11. Applicability and Entry into Force**

- **Statutory Instruments Act (Paragraph 11(2)(a))**: These regulations apply **before publication in the Canada Gazette**.
- **Entry into Force**: The regulations come into effect on the day they are **registered** (i.e., when officially published and registered in the Canada Gazette).

> Ensures immediate enforceability upon enactment.
