**Detailed Summary of the RCMP External Review Committee Rules of Practice and Procedure**

---

### **Overview**

The *Rules of Practice and Procedure of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police External Review Committee* (commonly referred to as the *RCMP External Review Committee Rules*) were established under **section 29 of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act (Act)** and came into effect on **June 8, 1988**. These rules govern the procedures, conduct, and administrative processes by which the External Review Committee (the Committee) conducts reviews of matters involving RCMP personnel, including grievances, disciplinary appeals, and discharge or demotion appeals.

The rules are designed to ensure **fairness, transparency, consistency, and procedural integrity** in the Committee’s decision-making process. They define the roles of key participants, outline procedural timelines, establish standards for evidence and submissions, and provide mechanisms for conflict of interest management and hearing conduct.

---

### **Key Definitions**

The rules begin with clear definitions of key terms used throughout:

- **Act**: Refers to the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act*, which provides the legal foundation for the Committee’s authority.
- **Committee member**: A person appointed under subsection 25(1) of the Act to serve on the Committee.
- **Interested person**: A person who can demonstrate a *substantial and direct interest* in a matter being reviewed by the Committee.
- **Record**: Any form of written, recorded, stored, or reproduced information — including books, documents, tapes, cards, or digital files.
- **Registered mail**: Traceable mail that confirms the date of mailing, signature upon delivery, and delivery status — used as proof of filing.
- **Registrar**: The official appointed under subsection 27(2) of the Act and designated by the Committee Chairman to manage administrative functions.

---

### **General Provisions**

#### **1. Flexibility in Unforeseen Matters**
- If a matter arises during proceedings not covered by these rules, the Committee has the authority to take any steps it deems necessary to resolve it.

#### **2. Time Limits and Holidays**
- Time limits or deadlines that fall on a **Saturday or statutory holiday** are automatically extended to the **next working day**.

#### **3. Pre-Hearing Applications to Appear**
- A person seeking to appear before the Committee must submit a **written application** no later than **five working days before the scheduled hearing**.
- The application must clearly state the **nature of the person’s interest** in the matter.
- The **Registrar** is responsible for sending a copy of this application to all parties involved in the matter.

#### **4. Participation via Telecommunication**
- Committee members may participate in meetings **by telephone or other means of telecommunication**, allowing for flexibility in attendance.

---

### **Referral of Matters to the Committee**

#### **5. Submission of Records by the Commissioner**
- When a matter is referred to the Committee, the **Commissioner or their delegate** must send one of the following to the **Committee Chairman**:
  - A **bound copy of the record** required under subsection 33(3) of the Act (including the cause style and index), OR
  - **Three bound copies** of the record under subsections 45.15(4) or 45.25(3) of the Act (also including cause style and index).
- The Commissioner must provide a **certified affidavit** confirming that:
  - The record has been sent to the Committee Chairman.
  - In matters under section 33, both the **officer involved** and the **member presenting the grievance** have received a copy of the record.

---

### **Written Submissions and Responses**

#### **6. Submission and Response Periods**
- During the review process, the Committee Chairman may allow parties and interested persons to file **written submissions** to clarify issues.
- Upon receiving a submission, the Chairman must **forward a copy to all other parties and interested persons**.
- Any party or interested person may respond within:
  - **14 days** after receiving the submission, OR
  - A **longer period** if justified by the circumstances.

---

### **Conflict of Interest Management**

#### **7. Disclosure and Withdrawal of Committee Members**
- A Committee member must **immediately notify the Chairman** if:
  - They have had a **past or current personal or professional association** with a member whose grievance, disciplinary appeal, discharge, or demotion appeal is before the Committee; OR
  - They are an **employee, associate, counsel, or member** of a legal firm representing that member.
- If the Chairman determines a **real or potential conflict of interest** exists, the member must **withdraw from further participation** in the matter.

> This provision ensures impartiality and protects the integrity of the review process.

---

### **Filing Records with the Committee**

#### **8. Filing Requirements**
- All records required by the rules must be:
  - **Addressed to the Registrar**.
  - Sent via **registered mail** or **delivered in person** to the Registrar’s office.
- The **effective date of filing** is the **date of mailing** (for registered mail).

---

### **Requests for Record Inspection**

#### **9. Right to Request Records**
- At any time before a hearing, any party may **request in writing** that another party produce for inspection:
  - A record, and
  - Copies of that record.
- The party receiving the request must provide the record within a **reasonable time** after receiving the request.

#### **10. Consequences of Non-Compliance**
- If a party fails to produce a record:
  - The record **cannot be presented in evidence** at the hearing.
  - **Exception**: The party may present the record if they can **demonstrate sufficient cause** for the failure (e.g., unforeseen circumstances, illness, etc.).

---

### **Pre-Hearing Directions and Clarifications**

#### **11. Committee’s Authority to Direct Proceedings**
The Committee may issue **verbal or written directions** to parties to:
- Appear at a specified time and place;
- File written submissions;
- Clarify or refine issues;
- Assist in:
  - Simplifying issues;
  - Determining whether to amend submissions;
  - Assessing the admissibility of facts (e.g., via affidavit) or use of public records;
  - Setting hearing procedures;
  - Coordinating the exchange of records and exhibits;
  - Any other matter that may help streamline the process.

> This allows the Committee to proactively manage the complexity of cases and ensure clarity and efficiency.

---

### **Hearing Procedures**

#### **12. Rights of Parties and Interested Persons**
- At a hearing, parties and interested persons have the right to:
  - Make **representations**;
  - Present **evidence**;
  - **Examine and cross-examine witnesses**;
  - Conduct this in **either official language** (English or French).

#### **13. Written Arguments**
- The Committee may require a party or interested person to file a **written argument** in addition to any verbal presentation.

#### **14. Enforcement of Compliance**
- If a party fails to comply with any rule:
  - The Committee may **stay the proceedings** until compliance is achieved, OR
  - Take other **just and reasonable measures** in the circumstances.

---

### **Postponement of Hearings**

#### **15. Requesting a Postponement**
- A party may request a postponement:
  - With the **consent of all other parties** and after notifying all interested persons; OR
  - If no consent is given, the party may:
    - Send a **written notice** to all other parties and interested persons;
    - Appear at the scheduled hearing and **request a postponement**.

> This provides flexibility in case of emergencies or logistical issues.

---

### **Adjournment of Hearings**

#### **16. Right to Adjourn**
- The Committee may **adjourn a hearing at any time** to allow a party or interested person to obtain additional information necessary for a full and fair investigation.

---

### **Conduct of Hearings**

#### **17. Continuity of Hearings**
- Hearings will proceed **day by day** as practicable.
- If necessary, they may proceed **in the evenings or on Saturdays**, at the **discretion of the Committee**.

> This ensures continuity and avoids undue delays.

---

### **Repealed or Revoked Provisions**

Several sections have been **repealed or revoked** over time:

| Repealed/Revoked Provision | Date of Revocation | Reason |
|---------------------------|--------------------|--------|
| **Executive Director** | SOR/91-544, s. 1 | The position was abolished. |
| **Summons for Original Records** | SOR/91-544, s. 9 | No summons can be issued for original records if a copy can be proven under statutory rules. |
| **Claims under subsections 35(12) and 46(3)** | SOR/91-544, s. 10 | These claims were removed from the Committee’s jurisdiction. |
| **Hearing on Saturdays** | SOR/97-437, s. 3 | This provision was repealed; no longer applies. |

> These changes reflect evolving administrative practices and legal interpretations.
