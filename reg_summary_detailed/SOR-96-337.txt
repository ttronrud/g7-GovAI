### ðŸ”¹ **5. How the Agency Assesses Adequacy of Insurance**

The Agency evaluates whether coverage is adequate by:

#### ðŸ” **For All Applicants (Construction or Passenger Rail Operations):**
The Agency examines the **risks associated with the railway project**, including:
- Passenger ridership
- Passenger and freight train miles
- Volume of railway traffic
- Class and volume of **dangerous goods transported**
- Types of **population areas served**
- Number of **level crossings**
- Train speed
- Crew training standards
- Method of train control (e.g., automatic signaling)
- Overall **safety record** of the applicant

> These factors help determine the likelihood and severity of potential incidents that could result in third-party claims.

#### ðŸ” **For Self-Insurance Cases Only:**
The Agency assesses the **financial capability** of the applicant to sustain the level of self-insurance based on:
- The **three most recent annual financial reports** filed with the Agency under **section 344 of the Railway Act** (as it existed before the 1996 amendments), or
- If those reports are unavailable: **audited financial statements** for the three most recent fiscal years
- If neither is available: **other financial information** that demonstrates financial stability and ability to meet self-insurance obligations

> ðŸ“Œ This ensures that self-insurance is not used as a substitute for adequate risk management in financially unstable organizations.
