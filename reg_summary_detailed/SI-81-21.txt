### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Northern Pipeline Act – Rescission and Replacement of Condition 7 and Socio-Economic/Environmental Terms (Agency File No. 5012)**

This regulation, issued by the **Northern Pipeline Agency** (Designated Officer) on **August 29, 1980**, pertains to the **rescission of outdated terms** and the **establishment of new, comprehensive socio-economic and environmental conditions** governing the construction and operation of the **Swift River Portion** of the pipeline in **British Columbia**, specifically for **Foothills Pipe Lines (South Yukon) Ltd.** ("Foothills (South Yukon)").

The document is issued under the authority of **subsection 20(4)** of the **Northern Pipeline Act**, which empowers the Designated Officer to rescind, amend, or add to the terms and conditions set out in **Schedule III** of the Act—provided such changes are approved by the **Governor in Council**.

---

## **I. Background and Context**

### 1. **Certificate of Public Convenience and Necessity**
- On **April 13, 1978**, a Certificate of Public Convenience and Necessity was issued to **Foothills (South Yukon)** for the construction and operation of a pipeline segment known as the **"Swift River Portion"** in northern British Columbia.
- This portion lies within **Zone 2** of Foothills’ pipeline system and is detailed in **Annex II of the Agreement**.

### 2. **Historical Undertakings from Public Hearings (1976–1977)**
- In **1976**, a public hearing before the **National Energy Board (NEB)** was held in Ottawa, Inuvik, Whitehorse, and Yellowknife.
- Participants—including **Foothills (Yukon)**, **Alberta Gas Trunk Line**, **Westcoast Transmission**, and others—presented evidence, undertakings, and commitments related to the **Alaska Highway Pipeline Project**, with a focus on:
  - Social and economic development
  - Environmental protection
  - Fisheries and agriculture
  - Indigenous and community concerns

### 3. **Legal Basis for Incorporating Undertakings**
- Under **subsection 21(2)** of the Northern Pipeline Act:
  - Any undertaking made during the public hearing is deemed to be a **term or condition** of the Certificate.
  - These undertakings are incorporated into **Schedule III** of the Act as binding obligations.

---

## **II. Key Actions Taken by the Designated Officer**

### ✅ **1. Rescission of Condition 7 of Schedule III**
- The Designated Officer **rescinds Condition 7** of Schedule III **as it applies to Foothills (South Yukon)** for the **Swift River Portion**.
- This condition was originally based on commitments made during the 1976–1977 public hearings and had been interpreted as a binding environmental or social obligation.

> **Exception**: The rescission does **not** apply to the **"Compensation" undertaking** listed in **Appendix 5-2, page 9 of Volume 3** of the NEB’s *Reasons for Decision, Northern Pipelines* (June 1977).  
> → This specific compensation commitment **remains in force**.

### ✅ **2. Rescission of Other Social, Economic, and Environmental Undertakings**
- All other undertakings related to:
  - Social and economic development
  - Environmental protection
  - Fisheries
  - Agriculture
  - Indigenous rights
  - Community impacts
  are **rescinded** in relation to the Swift River Portion.

> This reflects a **reassessment** of the original commitments, likely due to:
> - Changes in policy
> - Evolving community expectations
> - Need for more flexible, enforceable, and up-to-date terms

### ✅ **3. Addition of New Terms and Conditions**
- A **new set of comprehensive socio-economic and environmental terms and conditions** is **added to Schedule III** of the Northern Pipeline Act.
- These terms are titled:
  > **"Northern Pipeline Socio-Economic and Environmental Terms and Conditions for the Swift River Portion of the Pipeline in the Province of British Columbia"**

These new terms are **binding on Foothills (South Yukon)** and apply **only to the construction and operation of the Swift River Portion** unless otherwise specified.

---

## **III. Key Provisions of the New Terms and Conditions**

### 1. **Definitions**
The regulation provides clear definitions of key terms used throughout:
- **Agricultural land**: Land cleared for pasture, under cultivation, or part of an agricultural reserve.
- **Agricultural land reserve**: Established under the *Agricultural Land Commission Act*.
- **Big game/fur-bearing animals**: As defined in the *Wildlife Act*.
- **Borrow pit**: Site used for excavation of granular material.
- **Construction**: Activities from clearing to opening the pipeline (excluding survey lines).
- **Environmental impact**: Any change in physical or biological conditions due to construction or operation.
- **Facility**: Permanent or temporary installations used in pipeline operations.
- **Fuel**: All liquid and gaseous hydrocarbons used in pipeline operations.
- **Hazardous material**: Substances that could cause environmental harm if spilled.
- **Inspection/monitoring**: Verification of compliance with design and conditions; data collection on environmental changes.
- **Operation**: Activities after leave-to-open is granted.
- **Rare and endangered species**: Species at risk due to small population or limited habitat.
- **Swift River portion**: Defined in Annex II of the Agreement, within British Columbia.
- **Waste**: Discarded materials including oil drums, ashes, human waste.
- **Waterbodies**: Lakes, rivers, streams, and their beds.
- **Wildlife**: Includes native species, migratory birds, Galliformes, raptors, and rare/endangered species.

> All other terms are defined in the Act.

---

### 2. **General Compliance Obligations**
- Foothills must **comply with all Canadian and provincial laws**.
- When entering into contracts with contractors, Foothills **must require compliance** with these terms and conditions as a condition of the contract.

### 3. **Exemptions and Breach Liability**
- **Exemption**: The Designated Officer may **exempt Foothills** from compliance with a specific provision if requested.
- **Breach Liability**: Foothills is **personally liable** for any breach by its contractors or subcontractors, as if the breach had been committed directly by Foothills.

### 4. **Cooperation and Access**
- Foothills must provide **reasonable access** to government employees, consultants, and community representatives for inspection, monitoring, or consultation purposes.
- Government agencies (provincial and federal) may access the pipeline right-of-way for duty-related purposes.

### 5. **Indemnification and Compensation**
- Foothills must **indemnify Her Majesty (the Crown)** against:
  - Claims arising from construction or operation
  - Gas leaks, explosions, or environmental damage
  - Acts or omissions by Foothills, contractors, or their employees
  - Acts or omissions by Crown officials (excluding gross negligence)
- Foothills must **pay any loss or damage** suffered by the Crown as a result of pipeline-related incidents.

### 6. **Emergency Procedures**
- Foothills must establish **emergency procedures** satisfactory to the Designated Officer to respond to environmental or human safety emergencies.
- Must **designate and train personnel** to carry out these procedures.
- Must **immediately notify** the Designated Officer of any emergency and report measures taken.

### 7. **Consultation and Public Engagement**
- Foothills must:
  - **Consult** with:
    - Government of Canada
    - Government of British Columbia
    - Northern British Columbia Advisory Council
    - Communities near the Swift River portion (including **Teslin and Watson Lake**, Yukon)
    - Indigenous organizations with traditional or cultural interests
  - Consult within **30 days** of receiving a direction from the Designated Officer.
  - Report **significant results** of all consultations to the Designated Officer.
  - Report significant outcomes of consultations with **contractors or labor organizations** affecting construction progress.

### 8. **Information Dissemination**
- Foothills must provide information to the above entities on:
  - Route alignment and construction schedules
  - Potential economic benefits to local communities
  - Use of land or waterbodies
  - Significant environmental impacts
  - Any other matters specified by the Designated Officer
- Information must be provided in a **form and manner satisfactory to the Designated Officer**.
- Must be **accessible to the public** and facilitate public access to information.

### 9. **Transportation and Logistics**
- Foothills must:
  - Minimize disruption to local transportation services.
  - **Bear the cost** of any additional transportation infrastructure requested by federal or provincial governments.
  - Ensure such infrastructure is built, maintained, and disposed of to provide **long-term benefit** to affected communities.
  - Submit a **Transportation and Logistics Plan** for approval by the Designated Officer.

### 10. **Telecommunications**
- Must ensure that:
  - Use of telecommunication services does **not adversely affect** existing provincial services.
  - Any required infrastructure improves, rather than hinders, existing services.

### 11. **Road and Utility Protection**
- Must consult with:
  - Provincial and Yukon governments
  - Regulatory authorities over roads
