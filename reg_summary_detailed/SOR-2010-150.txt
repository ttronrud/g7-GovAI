## üéØ Who is a "Prescribed Person"?

Under **subsection 278.1(2.1)** of the Act, a person (excluding selected listed financial institutions) is a **prescribed person** for a reporting period if:

1. The person is **not a charity**.
2. The person is a **large business** (as defined in subsection 236.01(1) of the ETA) at any time during the reporting period or a prior period in the fiscal year.
3. The person is a **builder** who:
   - Makes a **specified housing supply** in respect of which tax under subsection 165(1) becomes payable during the reporting period;  
   - Is the **recipient** of a supply of a qualifying residential complex and makes a supply of that complex where tax under subsection 165(2) becomes payable;  
   - Is **deemed to have collected tax** under paragraphs 51(1)(e), 52(1)(e), 53(1)(e), 58.04(1)(e), 58.05(1)(e), or 58.06(1)(e) of the New Harmonized Value-added Tax System Regulations;  
   - **Claims a transitional new housing rebate** in a return under Division V of Part IX of the ETA for the reporting period.

> This definition targets **large builders involved in high-value housing projects** and ensures they are subject to stricter reporting and compliance obligations.

## ‚öñÔ∏è Penalties for Failing to File Returns

Under **section 280.11** of the ETA:

- **First failure**: $100 penalty  
- **Each subsequent failure**: $250 penalty  

> Penalties apply to failure to file a return (including late filing or missing submissions), regardless of whether the return is for a specified return or a standard return.

## üìÑ What is a "Prescribed Return"?

Under **section 284.01** of the ETA, a prescribed return includes:

1. A return required to be filed **electronically** under subsection 278.1(2.1) of the Act;
2. For **selected listed financial institutions**, a return under Division V of Part IX of the Act;
3. A return for which the person has made an **election under section 8.1**.

> This ensures that key registrants (especially builders) file returns electronically and are subject to specific rules about what information must be reported.

## üí∞ Prescribed Amounts (for Input Tax Credit and Tax Calculations)

These are critical for determining **net tax liability**, especially for **provincial input tax credits (ITCs)** and **taxable supplies**.

### General Rule:
If a person is required to **add or deduct** a specified provincial input tax credit (as per subsection 236.01(1) of the ETA), then:

- The **total amount reasonably attributable** to:
  - Property or services **acquired in or brought into Ontario** ‚Üí is a *prescribed amount* for Ontario.
  - Property or services **acquired in or brought into British Columbia** ‚Üí is a *prescribed amount* for BC.
  - Property or services **acquired in or brought into Prince Edward Island (PEI)** ‚Üí is a *prescribed amount* for PEI.

> The term "reasonably attributable" means the amount can be logically linked to the use or consumption of the property/service in that province.

## üìå Prescribed Amounts for Builders (Tax Collection and Reporting)

### 1. **When a builder is deemed to have collected tax** (under paragraphs 51(1)(e), etc.):
- The **total amounts of tax collected** in:
  - Ontario
  - British Columbia
  - Prince Edward Island  
‚Üí are each a **prescribed amount** for the return.

> This ensures that even if tax is not directly charged, the builder is still accountable for tax collected under certain conditions.

---

### 2. **For specified housing supplies made in each province**:
- If a builder makes specified housing supplies in **Ontario, Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, BC, or Newfoundland and Labrador**, the **total consideration** for those supplies in each province is a prescribed amount for the return.

> This allows for province-specific tracking of housing supply value and tax implications.

---

### 3. **For Prince Edward Island (PEI) ‚Äì Special Rules (Post-Oct 1, 2016)**

If a reporting period includes **October 1, 2016**, the prescribed amounts are:

| Scenario | Prescribed Amount |
|--------|-------------------|
| Supplies where tax under 165(1) became payable **before Oct 1, 2016** | Total consideration |
| Supplies where tax under 165(1) became payable **on or after Oct 1, 2016** and **no tax under 165(2)** applies | Total consideration |
| Supplies where tax under 165(2) applies and became payable **on or after Oct 1, 2016** | Total consideration |

> This reflects a **transition in tax rates and rules** in PEI (from 10% to 9% HST) effective October 1, 2016.
