### ðŸ”¹ **3. Pest Risk Assessment (PRA) â€“ Core Scientific Framework**

- The Minister must conduct a **pest risk assessment** using the **FAO ISPM guidelines**.
- The assessment evaluates:
  - Whether a thing is a pest or could be infested.
  - Whether it constitutes or could constitute a **biological obstacle** to pest control.
  - Whether it poses a **significant adverse effect on the environment**.
- Based on the PRA, the Minister may:
  - Recommend actions to prevent introduction or spread.
  - Recommend control or eradication measures.
  - Determine if environmental degradation is likely.
- The goal is to **minimize environmental harm** while protecting Canadian flora.

> This marks a major shift from outdated, reactive quarantine policies to a **proactive, risk-based approach** aligned with international standards.

### ðŸ”¹ **4. Authorized Activities Involving Pests or Biological Obstacles**

Under subsection **6(1)** of the Act, a person may undertake certain activities (e.g., research, education, processing, exhibition) involving a pest or biological obstacle **only if**:
1. The Minister or an inspector determines the activity is for **scientific, educational, industrial, or exhibition purposes**.
2. The person obtains a **written authorization** from the Minister or inspector.
3. The thing is **packaged, transported, handled, and used** in a way that prevents spread.
4. The movement complies with **Part III** of the regulations.
5. Any disposal of the thing or its remains must:
   - Prevent spread of pests.
   - Destroy any pest or ensure it is non-viable.

> This ensures that even legitimate activities involving pests are strictly regulated to prevent environmental or agricultural harm.

### ðŸ”¹ **5. Authorization Process for Activities**

- The Minister or inspector may issue a **written authorization** if they believe:
  - The person is **able and willing** to comply with all conditions (packaging, handling, disposal, etc.).
  - The person will take **every precaution** to prevent pest spread.
- The authorization may include **specific conditions** (e.g., time limits, handling methods).
- The person must **comply fully** with all conditions in the authorization.

> Exception: **No authorization is required** if the person is acting on behalf of Her Majesty in right of Canada for:
> - Application of the Act.
> - Administrative purposes under other Acts.
> - Activities related to section 2 of the Act (e.g., inspections, enforcement).

### ðŸ”¹ **8. Quarantine Procedures**

#### When Quarantine Applies:
- If an inspector has **reasonable grounds** to believe a thing is a pest or could be infested, or constitutes a biological obstacle to pest control.

#### Actions Required:
- The inspector must:
  - **Send or personally deliver** a **notice of quarantine** to the owner or person in control.
  - **Attach a quarantine tag** (if applicable), which bears:
    - The **quarantine number** (same as the notice), or
    - The **permit number** if specified in the permit.

#### Restrictions:
- No person may:
  - Alter, deface, or erase information on the notice or tag.
  - Remove the tag.
- **Exception**: If impractical, an inspector may authorize a person to remove the tag.

#### Activity Restrictions:
- No activity may be undertaken in respect of the quarantined thing **except as authorized** by the inspector and **within the specified period and conditions**.

#### Release from Quarantine:
- A thing may be released if:
  - It is **not a pest** or could not be infested.
  - It does **not constitute a biological obstacle**.
  - The pest has been **eradicated**.
  - It no longer presents a biological obstacle.
- Upon release, the inspector must send a **notice of release** to the owner.

> Quarantine is a temporary, controlled measure to assess and manage pest risks.

### ðŸ”¹ **9. Inspections, Surveys, and Public Notification**

- Inspectors may conduct **investigations or surveys** to detect pests or biological obstacles.
- If a pest is detected and an area is identified:
  - The Minister may describe the area using:
    - A publicly available map or plan.
    - A farm, county, district, municipality, province, or part thereof.
  - The Minister must take **all practicable steps** to inform persons likely to be affected.

> This ensures timely public awareness and enables proactive response.

### ðŸ”¹ **10. Treatment and Processing of Infested Things or Places**

- If an inspector determines a thing or place is infested or poses a biological obstacle:
  - The inspector may **require treatment or processing**.
  - The treatment may include fumigation, heat treatment, or other approved methods.
  - The inspector may:
    - Treat or process the thing themselves.
    - Require the owner to treat or process it.

#### Requirements:
- A **written notice** must be sent specifying:
  - The treatment or process.
  - The manner of treatment.
  - The deadline for completion.

#### Compliance:
- The owner must carry out the treatment as directed and ensure:
  - The pest is eradicated.
  - Spread is prevented.

> This is a key tool for eradicating pests at source.
