**Detailed Summary of the National Parks Wildlife Regulations, C.R.C., c. 1122**

---

### **Overview**

The *National Parks Wildlife Regulations* (replacing the previous *National Parks Game Regulations*) were issued by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of the Environment, under subsection 7(1) of the *National Parks Act*. These regulations govern the protection, management, and permissible activities related to wildlife within **all Canadian National Parks except Wood Buffalo National Park**.

The regulations establish clear rules regarding hunting, possession, disturbance of wildlife, firearm use, and the protection of natural habitats. They also provide specific provisions for certain parks (e.g., Mingan Archipelago) and recognize exceptions for scientific, management, or cultural purposes.

---

## **Key Definitions**

The regulations define several key terms used throughout:

- **Exotic wildlife**: Any animal not indigenous to the park or declared as such by regulation.
- **Firearm**: Any weapon capable of discharging bullets or missiles that can injure or kill wildlife, including devices adapted for such use.
- **Guide**: A person holding a valid licence under the *National Parks Businesses Regulations* who provides guided services.
- **Hunt**: Includes trapping, chasing, shooting, stalking, or attempting to capture, kill, or injure wildlife.
- **Loaded (with respect to a firearm)**: A firearm with a live shell, cartridge, or projectile in the chamber, breach, or barrel.
- **Possession**: As defined in subsection 4(3) of the *Criminal Code*.
- **Superintendent through highway**: Specific designated roads and highways that serve as primary access routes through certain parks (e.g., Trans-Canada Highway in Banff, Yellowhead Highway in Jasper).
- **Trap**: Any device that captures wildlife but does not allow exit, including snares.
- **Vehicle**: Any device used for land transportation.
- **Vessel**: Any boat, canoe, raft, amphibious vehicle, or similar watercraft (excluding sea planes).
- **Wildlife**: All wild animals (mammals, birds, fish, insects, reptiles, amphibians, eggs, young, and parts thereof).

---

## **Scope and Application**

- Applies to **all National Parks in Canada except Wood Buffalo National Park**.
- Applies to **all park reserves as if they were parks**.
- **Precedence rules**:
  - In case of conflict between these regulations and:
    - The *National Parks of Canada Fishing Regulations* → Fishing regulations prevail.
    - The *Mingan Archipelago National Park Reserve of Canada Snowshoe Hare Regulations* → Snowshoe hare regulations prevail.

> **Exception**: These regulations do **not apply** to:
> - Superintendents, park wardens, peace officers, or authorized personnel performing park management duties.

---

## **Core Prohibitions**

**General Prohibitions (Section 1)**  
*Except as otherwise provided, no person may:*

1. **Hunt, disturb, hold in captivity, or destroy** any wildlife within or remove any wildlife from a park.
2. In specific parks (**La Mauricie, Georgian Bay Islands, St. Lawrence Islands, Mingan Archipelago**), **have wildlife in personal possession**.
3. In **any park other than those listed in paragraph (b)** or outside a park, **be in possession of wildlife killed or procured within a park**, unless:
   - The wildlife is in possession **in accordance with subsection (4)** below.

4. **Disturb or destroy** a nest, lair, den, beaver house, or dam.

5. **Touch, feed, or entice wildlife** (e.g., using food, bait, or decoys) to approach.

6. **Release exotic wildlife** into any park.

7. **Shine a movable light with voltage greater than 4.5 volts** in areas frequented by wildlife between sunset and sunrise.

---

## **Permitted Possession of Wildlife (Section 4)**

A person may possess wildlife in a park **only under the following conditions**:

### **In Mingan Archipelago National Park Reserve**:
- The ducks must have been hunted **in accordance with these regulations**.
- The person must **produce a hunting permit** issued under Section 13.1 upon request by a park warden or officer.

### **In All Other Parks**:
A person may possess wildlife if:
- They produce one of the following, upon request by a park warden or officer:
  - An **export permit** under the *Game Export Act*,
  - A **hunting or trapping licence/permit** issued by the province where the wildlife was obtained,
  - A **hunting tag** (properly affixed) issued by the province,
  - Or, if obtained as a **gift**, a valid licence allowing gift transfer, or a **signed statement** from the donor including:
    - Date of gift,
    - Type and number of wildlife,
    - Name of recipient,
    - Licence number under which wildlife was procured.

> **Purpose of possession** must be one of the following:
> - Storing at a permanent residence in the park,
> - Transporting directly on a **through highway** to a permanent residence within the park,
> - Transporting directly through the park to private property outside the park via the **sole access road**,
> - In **Prince Edward Island National Park**, transporting on the **Gulf Shore Parkway** only if entry was solely for the most direct route to outside roads.

> **Exception**: Subsection (4) does **not apply** to individuals authorized under **paragraph 15(1)(a)** to remove, relocate, or destroy wildlife.

> **Note**: Reasonable protection from biting or stinging insects is permitted.

---

## **Hunting in Mingan Archipelago National Park Reserve (Section 13.1)**

### **Eligibility for Hunting Permit**:
- Must be a **permanent resident** of:
  - Longue Pointe de Mingan,
  - Mingan,
  - Havre St-Pierre,
  - Baie John Beetz (Quebec).
- Must hold a **valid migratory game bird hunting permit** under the *Migratory Birds Regulations, 2022*.

### **Permitted Hunting**:
- Between **half an hour before sunrise and half an hour after sunset** during the open season.
- Within the **daily bag limit** set by the *Migratory Birds Regulations, 2022* for Quebec.

> **Exception**: Aboriginal peoples with existing **aboriginal or treaty rights** to hunt in the area are **not subject to this permit requirement**.

### **Permit Conditions**:
- Superintendent may specify:
  - Duration of validity,
  - Specific hunting locations,
  - Additional conditions for public safety or resource protection.

---

## **Superintendent Powers (Section 15)**

The superintendent may authorize:

1. **Removal, relocation, or destruction** of wildlife for:
   - Scientific purposes,
   - Park management purposes.

2. **Sale or public disposal** of wildlife or wildlife products from a park.

3. **Introduction of exotic wildlife** into a park (if deemed necessary).

---

## **Firearm and Trap Prohibitions (Section 16)**

### **Prohibitions**:
- No person may:
  - Use a **dog for hunting wildlife**.
  - Be in possession of a **loaded firearm** in a park unless:
    - The firearm is in a case, or
    - It is wrapped and tied securely so no part is exposed.
  - Have a **loaded firearm** in or on a **vehicle, vessel, or aircraft**.
  - **Discharge a firearm** from a vehicle, vessel, or aircraft.
  - Possess **poison, gas, narcotic, or drug** intended to capture, injure, or kill wildlife.
  - Communicate via radio, phone, walkie-talkie, or other means the **location of wildlife** to another person **if either is hunting**.
  - **Sell or offer for sale** firearms or traps in a park.

> **Exceptions**:
> - A person in **Mingan Archipelago** hunting under a valid permit.
> - A **bonded armoured car service guard** performing assigned duties.

---

## **Permitted Carrying of Firearms or Traps (Section 17)**

A person may carry a firearm or trap **only if**:
- They have a **permit from the superintendent** authorizing carrying it through the park for use **outside the park**, or
- The firearm/trap is being carried:
  - From a vehicle/vessel/aircraft to their premises, or
  - From their premises to a vehicle/vessel/aircraft.

> **No one** (except bonded guard) may carry a firearm or trap outside a vehicle, vessel, or aircraft without a permit.

---

## **Prohibitions in Specific Parks (Section 18)**

No person may possess a firearm or trap in:
- **Kejimkujik, La Mauricie, Point Pelee, or Forillon National Parks**,
- **Except when traversing the park along designated highways** (e.g., Highway 132 in Forillon),
- **Unless authorized under paragraph 15(1)(a)** to remove, relocate, or destroy wildlife.

---

## **Inspection and Compliance**

- Every person in possession of a firearm or trap must allow a **park warden or peace officer** to:
  - Inspect the firearm or trap,
  - Inspect the permit authorizing its carrying.

- **Guides** must ensure firearms or traps are carried in full compliance with these regulations.
