- These regulations were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, under **Sections 479 to 488a and 703b of the Insurance Companies Act (ICA)**.

> âœ… *Key Legal Foundation*: The regulations implement **Section 481** of the ICA, which mandates that the cost of borrowing must be expressed as an **Annual Percentage Rate (APR)**, and **Section 480**, which requires clear and timely disclosure of this cost to borrowers.

### **4. Cost of Borrowing: Calculation and Components**

#### **A. For Loans (not credit cards or lines of credit):**
The **total cost of borrowing** must be expressed as an **APR** over the term of the loan, calculated using the following formula:

> **APR = (Total Cost of Borrowing / Principal) Ã— (100 / Term in Years)**  
> Where:
> - Total cost of borrowing = sum of all interest, fees, and charges over the term
> - Principal = outstanding balance at each period (before any payment)
> - Term = loan duration in years (to at least two decimal places)

##### **Key Rules for APR Calculation:**
- **Rounding**: APR may be rounded to the **nearest eighth of a percent** (e.g., 5.125%).
- **Payment Application Order**: Each payment is applied **first to the accumulated cost of borrowing**, then to the principal (prevents negative amortization).
- **Time Periods**:
  - 1 month = 1/12 of a year
  - 1 week = 1/52 of a year
  - 1 day = 1/365 of a year
- **Variable Interest Rates**: Must use the **rate in effect on the date of calculation**.
- **No Installments?** If no payments are made, APR is calculated assuming repayment in **one lump sum** at maturity.
- **New Credit Agreements**: A loan that builds on an existing balance is treated as a **new credit agreement** for APR purposes.

#### **B. For Credit Cards or Lines of Credit:**
- **Fixed interest rate**: APR = the fixed annual interest rate  
- **Variable interest rate**: APR = the annual interest rate **on the date of disclosure**

> âœ… *Simpler rules* apply to credit cards and lines of credit â€” no need to calculate APR over time; disclosure is based on current or disclosed rate.

#### **C. What Constitutes "Cost of Borrowing"?**
Includes all **non-interest charges** that are part of the loan cost, such as:
- Administrative fees
- Lawyer or notary fees (required by the company)
- Insurance (excluding optional or borrower-beneficiary policies)
- Broker fees (if included in the amount borrowed and paid directly by the company)
- Appraisal, inspection, or surveying fees (for property used as security)

> âŒ **Excluded Charges** (not included in cost of borrowing):
- Insurance on the loan if:
  - Optional
  - Beneficiary is the borrower and covers asset value
- Overdraft fees
- Registry fees (to register security interests)
- Penalty fees for early repayment
- Lawyer/notary fees (unless required by company)
- Title defect insurance (paid by borrower)
- Appraisal/inspection services provided directly to borrower
- Default insurance on high-ratio mortgages
- Tax account maintenance fees (required or optional)
- Fees to discharge a security interest
- Default charges

> ðŸ“Œ *Important*: Only **mandatory** and **directly related** charges are included in the cost of borrowing.

### **5. Disclosure Requirements**

#### **A. Mandatory Disclosure Statement**
- Must be provided **in writing** to the borrower **before or at the time** of entering into the credit agreement.
- The statement must include:
  - Clear, simple, and non-misleading language
  - Information in a **consolidated, easily readable format**

#### **B. Format and Presentation Rules**
- **Consolidated Format**:
  - If part of the agreement: Must be presented in a **single location**.
  - If separate: Must be provided **before signing** or with the application.
- **Information Box**:
  - Must appear at the **beginning** of the agreement or disclosure statement.
  - Must contain data from **Schedule 1 to 5** (depending on loan type).
- **Text Formatting Requirements**:
  - Font: **Minimum 12-point**, bold for titles and numbers (interest rates, dates, amounts)
  - Other text: **Minimum 10-point**
  - Spacing: Standard, readable spacing
  - Margins: Adequate white space
  - Background: **Dark text on light background** for maximum visibility

> âœ… *Goal*: Ensure borrowers can **easily understand** the key financial terms without confusion.

#### **C. Assumptions and Estimates**
- If data is based on an assumption or estimate:
  - It must be clearly labeled as such
  - Must be reasonable
  - Must not be known at the time of disclosure

> âœ… *Example*: A projected interest rate based on future index movements may be disclosed as an estimate.

### **6. Timing of Disclosure**

#### **A. Initial Disclosure Statement**
Must be provided **on or before**:
- The **earlier of**:
  - The first payment (other than a disbursement charge)
  - **Two clear business days before** entering into a mortgage or hypothec agreement
  - **On the day of entry** into any other credit agreement

> âš ï¸ *Exception* (if borrower consents or favorable terms exist):
- Borrower consents to receiving the statement via another borrower
- Borrower receives **independent legal advice**
- A **rescission period of at least two business days** is included
- The agreement includes **favourable terms** that reduce the cost of borrowing

#### **B. Subsequent Disclosure Statements**
- **For fixed-rate loans**:
  - If a payment is missed or a default charge is applied, and this **increases the outstanding balance**, a **subsequent statement must be issued within 30 days** to explain the situation and consequences.

- **For variable-rate loans**:
  - **If the rate increases by more than 1%** above the last disclosed rate, a **subsequent statement must be issued within 30 days**, including:
    - New annual interest rate and effective date
    - Revised payment amounts and due dates
  - **If interest is tied to a public index** (e.g., prime rate), the company must provide a statement **at least once every 12 months**, including:
    - Beginning and ending interest rates
    - Opening and closing balances
    - Payment schedule based on the new rate

> ðŸ“Œ *Purpose*: To keep borrowers informed of changes in interest rates and their financial impact.
