## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Agreement on Social Security between Canada and Sweden**

### üîπ **Overview**
This is a bilateral social security agreement between **Canada and Sweden**, signed on **April 10, 1985**, and updated through a new agreement effective **April 1, 2003**, following changes in social security legislation and mutual cooperation needs. The agreement governs how citizens, dependants, and survivors of either country can access pension and benefit systems across borders, ensuring **recognition, portability, and equal treatment** under the rules of both nations.

The agreement was formally **proclaimed into force on April 1, 2003**, after a legal process involving Orders in Council and parliamentary procedures (discussed in the preamble).

---

## üîç **Key Provisions and Structure**

### 1. **Preamble and Entry into Force**
- The agreement entered into force on **April 1, 2003**, following:
  - Notification from both countries confirming compliance with all requirements.
  - A legal process under the *Old Age Security Act* (subsection 42(2)), which requires a 30-day sitting period after the Order in Council is laid before Parliament.
- The Order in Council (P.C. 2002-739) was first laid in May 2002, then again in October 2002 after Parliament was dissolved.
- After 30 days of the second laying, the Order came into force on **December 5, 2002**.
- The exchange of written notifications occurred on **December 18, 2002**.
- The agreement thus entered into force on the **first day of the fourth month after the month of notification**, i.e., **April 1, 2003**.

> ‚úÖ This structure ensures that the agreement only comes into force once both parties have met all procedural and legislative conditions.

### 4. **Who Is Covered? (Article III)**

- Applies to:
  - Any person who has been or is currently subject to Canadian or Swedish legislation.
  - Their **dependants and survivors** (as defined in national laws).

> Key principle: **Citizens of either country and their dependants are treated equally** when accessing benefits in the other country.

---

### 5. **Eligibility and Benefit Rights (Article IV)**

#### üîπ General Principle:
- A citizen of one country and their dependants/survivors are eligible for benefits under the other country‚Äôs legislation **under the same conditions** as citizens of that country.

#### üîπ No Reduction Due to Residence:
- Benefits **shall not be reduced, suspended, cancelled, or confiscated** solely because the person resides in the other country.
- Benefits are **paid when the person is in the territory of the other country**.

#### üîπ For Dual-Residency or Third Countries:
- Benefits are payable **even when the person (or dependant/survivor) is in a third state**.

> This ensures continuity of benefits regardless of where the beneficiary physically resides.

### 6. **Employment and Jurisdiction Rules (Article VI)**

These rules determine **which country‚Äôs social security system applies** based on employment status.

| Scenario | Jurisdiction |
|--------|-------------|
| **Employed person** working in a Party‚Äôs territory | Subject only to that Party‚Äôs legislation |
| **Self-employed person** working in the other Party‚Äôs territory | Subject only to the **first Party** (where they reside) |
| **Person sent by employer to work abroad** (same or related employer) | Subject only to the **first Party** (where employer is based) |
| **Government employees** sent to work in another country | Coverage **not limited to 60 months** |
| **Seafarers** (crew members) | Subject to:
  - Sweden if ship flies Swedish flag
  - Canada otherwise |
| **Government employees** working locally in the other country | Subject to the **first Party** |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception**: The 60-month limit applies only to non-governmental self-employed or employed individuals.

> üö® **Important Exception**: The 60-month rule does **not apply** to government employees.

### 7. **Residence and Creditable Periods (Articles VII‚ÄìX)**

#### ‚úÖ For Old Age Security Act (Canada):
- **If a person is subject to CPP or provincial pension plans during a stay in Sweden**:
  - That period counts as **residence in Canada** for:
    - The person
    - Their spouse/common-law partner
    - Dependents living with them in Sweden
    - Only if they are not subject to Swedish legislation via employment/self-employment.

- **If a person is subject to Swedish legislation during a stay in Canada**:
  - That period **does not count** as residence in Canada for the person or their dependents (if not employed/self-employed in Canada).

> This allows for **credit accumulation** in Canada during temporary stays in Sweden.

#### ‚úÖ For Swedish Legislation:
- **If a person is subject to Swedish legislation during a stay in Canada**:
  - That period counts as **residence in Sweden** for:
    - The person
    - Spouse and children under 18
    - Only if they are not subject to Canadian legislation via employment/self-employment.

- **If a person is subject to Canadian legislation during a stay in Sweden**:
  - That period **does not count** as residence in Sweden for dependents.

> This ensures **recognition of residence** for dependants under Swedish rules.

### 8. **Totalizing Creditable Periods (Article XIII)**

- If a person does **not meet eligibility thresholds** due to insufficient creditable periods:
  - Eligibility is determined by **totalizing**:
    - Creditable periods under Canadian and Swedish laws
    - Creditable periods completed under **third-party agreements** (where both countries have reciprocal arrangements)

> This allows individuals to "stack" periods from multiple systems to qualify for benefits.

---

### 9. **Eligibility Determination (Article XIV)**

#### For Old Age Security Act (OASA):
- A **creditable period under Swedish legislation** counts as **residence in Canada**.

#### For Canada Pension Plan (CPP):
- A **calendar year** with pension points credited under Swedish income-based pensions counts as a **creditable year** in CPP.

#### For Swedish Legislation:
- A **creditable year under CPP** counts as a creditable year under Swedish law.
- A **creditable period under OASA** (not overlapping with CPP) counts as a creditable period under Swedish law.

> This creates **mutual recognition of contributions** across systems.

---

### 10. **Special Rules for Pensions Outside Canada**

- A person eligible for a **full Canadian pension** but who has not lived in Canada for the minimum required period may still qualify **if outside Canada** ‚Äî **provided**:
  - Their total residence periods (under totalizing rules) meet or exceed the minimum required by OASA.

> However:
- A **pension under OASA** is **only paid outside Canada** if total residence periods equal or exceed the minimum required by OASA.
- **Allowances and Guaranteed Income Supplements** are paid only to the extent allowed by OASA.

> ‚ùå **No automatic payment** outside Canada without meeting residency thresholds.
