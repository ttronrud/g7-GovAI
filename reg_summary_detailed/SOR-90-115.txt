**Detailed Summary of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police Training Academy Fees Order (SI/89-214, 1989 Canada Gazette Part II, p. 4298)**

**Order Title:**  
*Royal Canadian Mounted Police Training Academy Fees Order*

**Issuing Authority:**  
The Solicitor General of Canada, pursuant to **Order in Council P.C. 1989-1898**, dated September 21, 1989.

**Effective Date:**  
January 26, 1990

**Scope and Purpose:**  
This regulation establishes a standardized fee structure for services and the use of facilities provided by the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP) at the **RCMP Training Academy in Regina, Saskatchewan**. The Order applies to individuals (or organizations) who receive training or access to facilities at the Academy, ensuring transparency and consistency in billing for services rendered.

---

### **Fees Prescribed Under the Order**

The Order specifies the following fees based on the type of service or facility used:

1. **Academic Training in a Classroom by an RCMP Instructor**  
   - **Fee:** $31 per hour  
   - **Description:** Covers instruction delivered by a Royal Canadian Mounted Police instructor in a formal classroom setting. This includes theoretical or academic components of training programs.

2. **Gas and Small Arms Training**  
   - **Fee:** $24 per hour  
   - **Description:** Applies to hands-on training involving firearms, explosives, or related equipment. This includes practical instruction in the use, handling, and safety of small arms and associated gas systems.

3. **Driver Training Provided by an RCMP Instructor Using an RCMP Vehicle**  
   - **Fee:** $31 per hour  
   - **Description:** Covers driver education sessions conducted by an RCMP instructor using official RCMP vehicles. This may include defensive driving, vehicle operation, or emergency response driving scenarios.

4. **Accommodation at the Training Academy**  
   - **Fee:** $17 per day  
   - **Description:** Applies to lodging provided to individuals during their stay at the Academy. This fee covers room and board (including meals, if applicable) and is charged on a daily basis.

5. **Use of a Classroom by a Person Other Than an RCMP Instructor**  
   - **Fee:** $17 per hour  
   - **Description:** Covers the rental or use of a classroom space by non-RCMP personnel (e.g., external organizations, government agencies, or private groups) for meetings, workshops, or other educational events. This does not include instruction by an RCMP instructor.

---

### **Key Provisions and Interpretations**

- **Applicability:**  
  The fees apply to any individual or entity that receives one of the listed services or uses one of the listed facilities at the RCMP Training Academy in Regina, Saskatchewan.

- **Exclusions:**  
  The Order does not cover administrative, logistical, or non-training-related services not explicitly listed. It is limited to the specific services and facilities outlined.

- **Billing and Enforcement:**  
  The fees are to be collected directly by the RCMP or its authorized representatives. The Order serves as a binding legal instrument, and failure to pay may result in enforcement actions, depending on the circumstances.

- **Transparency and Accountability:**  
  The fee structure ensures that users are clearly informed of the cost of services, promoting fairness and accountability in the use of RCMP training resources.

---

### **Background and Context**

This Order was issued in 1989 as part of broader efforts to formalize and regulate the use of RCMP training facilities and services. Prior to this regulation, there may have been inconsistent or unregulated charges for training and accommodation. By establishing clear, publicly accessible fees, the government aimed to:

- Improve financial accountability within the RCMP training system  
- Ensure equitable access to training resources  
- Provide a transparent framework for external users (e.g., government departments, academic institutions, or private entities)

---

### **Legal Authority**

The Order is made under **Order in Council P.C. 1989-1898**, which grants the Solicitor General of Canada the authority to establish fees and charges for services provided by the RCMP, particularly in the context of training and facilities use.

**Conclusion:**  
The *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Training Academy Fees Order* is a concise yet comprehensive regulatory instrument that defines the fees payable by external users for specific training and facility services at the RCMP Training Academy in Regina, Saskatchewan. It ensures transparency, fairness, and consistency in service pricing while operating within the legal authority granted by the federal government through Order in Council.

This Order remains in force unless amended or repealed by a subsequent legislative or administrative action.
