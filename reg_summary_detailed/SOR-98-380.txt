**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Order Respecting the First Legislative Assembly of Nunavut**  
*Statutory Citation: S.C. 1993, c. 28*  
*Issued under Section 76.01 of the Nunavut Act*  
*Made by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development*  
*Effective Date: July 15, 1998*

---

### **Overview**

This regulation establishes the initial structure of Nunavut’s Legislative Assembly, specifically defining the **number of members** and the **boundaries of the electoral districts** for the first election in Nunavut’s history. It serves as a foundational legal instrument under the *Nunavut Act*, ensuring that the territory has a democratically structured legislative body upon its official establishment.

---

### **Key Provisions**

#### **1. Number of Members**
- The **first Legislative Assembly of Nunavut is composed of 19 members**.
- This number was determined to reflect the population distribution and geographic diversity of the territory at the time of its creation, ensuring equitable representation across regions.

#### **2. Electoral Districts and Their Boundaries**
The regulation delineates **19 distinct electoral districts**, each with a precise geographic description based on latitude, longitude, and boundary lines. These descriptions are provided in the Schedule to the Order and are essential for electoral administration and voting procedures.

Each district is named and defined using a formal geographic description, typically following a "commencing at X, thence to Y, thence to Z" format. These descriptions are based on:
- International boundaries (e.g., between Nunavut and the Northwest Territories or Manitoba)
- Parallel and meridian lines of latitude and longitude
- Natural or administrative features (e.g., Hudson Bay, Queen Maud Gulf, Victoria Strait)
- Specific land parcels, lots, and plans (especially in rural or remote areas)

### **Detailed Description of Electoral Districts**

Below is a breakdown of each electoral district, including its geographic boundaries and key geographical references:

---

#### **1. QUTTIKTUQ**
- **Geographic Coverage**: A large district in the northern part of Nunavut.
- **Boundaries**:
  - Begins at the northern limit of Canada.
  - Follows the **110th meridian of longitude** south to the **74°15′ N** line.
  - Proceeds east along the 74°15′ parallel to the **103rd meridian**.
  - Then south along the 103rd meridian to the **72nd parallel**.
  - Proceeds east to the **91st meridian**, then south to the **71°15′ N**.
  - Continues east to the **82nd meridian**, then north to the **74°15′ N**.
  - Proceeds east to the **Greenland-Canada boundary** (approx. 71°00′ W).
  - Travels north along the Greenland-Canada border to the **60th meridian** at **82°13′ N**.
  - Returns north to the northern limit of Canada.
- **Inclusion**: All islands within this described area are included in Quttiktuq.
- **Significance**: Covers a vast, remote northern region with significant Arctic and Inuit cultural presence.

---

#### **2. KUGLUKTUK**
- **Geographic Coverage**: Central-northern region, near the 70th parallel.
- **Boundaries**:
  - Begins at the **intersection of 70°N and 110°W**.
  - Proceeds west-southwest to the **110th meridian** (approx. 65°10′ N).
  - Then travels north along the 110th meridian back to the starting point.
- **Key Feature**: Follows the Nunavut–Northwest Territories border, which forms part of its boundary.
- **Significance**: A mid-territory district with strong Inuit communities.

---

#### **3. CAMBRIDGE BAY**
- **Geographic Coverage**: Eastern and central part of Nunavut, including the Cambridge Bay area.
- **Boundaries**:
  - Begins at **74°15′ N and 110°W**.
  - Travels south along the 110th meridian to the **Nunavut–NWT border** (65°10′ N).
  - Proceeds southeasterly along the border to the **102nd meridian** (64°14′ N).
  - Travels north along the 102nd meridian to the **65th parallel**.
  - Moves west to the **102°30′ meridian**, then north to the **68°45′ N** in the Queen Maud Gulf.
  - Proceeds northeasterly to the **100th meridian** at **69°20′ N** in the Victoria Strait.
  - Travels north to the **72nd parallel**, then west to the **103rd meridian**, then north to **74°15′ N**, and finally west to the starting point.
- **Significance**: A major urban and administrative center; includes a mix of coastal and inland areas.

---

#### **4. NATTILIK**
- **Geographic Coverage**: Central-western Nunavut, near the Hudson Bay.
- **Boundaries**:
  - Begins at **72°N and 100°W**.
  - Travels south to **69°20′ N** in the Victoria Strait.
  - Moves southwesterly to the **102°30′ meridian** at **68°45′ N**.
  - Proceeds south to the **67th parallel**.
  - Travels east to the **93°10′ meridian**, then north to the **69th parallel**.
  - Proceeds east to the **91st meridian**, then north to the **72nd parallel**.
  - Finally, moves west to the starting point.
- **Significance**: A district with a mix of rural and traditional Inuit settlements.

---

#### **5. AKULLIQ**
- **Geographic Coverage**: Western-central region, extending toward the Canadian Arctic.
- **Boundaries**:
  - Begins at **71°15′ N and 91°W**.
  - Travels south to the **69th parallel**.
  - Moves west to the **93°10′ meridian**, then south to the **66th parallel**.
  - Proceeds east to the **80th meridian**, then north to the **68th parallel**.
  - Travels west to the **85th meridian**, then north to the **71°15′ N**.
  - Finally, moves west to the starting point.
- **Significance**: A geographically diverse district with coastal and inland features.

---

#### **6. BAKER LAKE**
- **Geographic Coverage**: Northern central region, near the 67th parallel.
- **Boundaries**:
  - Begins at **67°N and 102°30′W**.
  - Travels south to the **65th parallel**.
  - Proceeds east to the **102nd meridian**.
  - Travels south to the **62°20′ N**.
  - Moves east to the **93°10′ meridian**, then north to the **67th parallel**.
  - Finally, moves west to the starting point.
- **Note**: Uses the **102nd meridian** as a boundary with the Northwest Territories.
- **Significance**: A remote, sparsely populated area with important Inuit communities.

---

#### **7. ARVIAT**
- **Geographic Coverage**: Eastern Arctic, near the Hudson Bay.
- **Boundaries**:
  - Begins at **62°20′ N and 102°W**.
  - Travels south to the **60th parallel**.
  - Proceeds east along the 60th parallel to the **western shoreline of Hudson Bay** (approx. 94°49′ W).
  - Travels southeasterly along the bay to the **90th meridian** (57°02′ N).
  - Proceeds north to the **61°28′ N**.
  - Moves west to the **93°10′ meridian**, then north to the **62°20′ N**.
  - Finally, moves west to the starting point.
- **Note**: Uses the **60th parallel** as a boundary with Manitoba.
- **Significance**: A strategically important area with a strong Inuit population and access to major waterways.

---
