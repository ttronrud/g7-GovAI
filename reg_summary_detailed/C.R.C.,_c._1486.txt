## üö¢ VESSEL OPERATIONAL AND SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

### 1. **Design, Construction, and Equipment**
- The **authorized representative** must ensure the vessel is:
  - Designed and constructed to operate safely and be **seaworthy** in its area of operation.
- If the **Minister** has reasonable grounds to believe the vessel is unsafe, they may require the representative to prove compliance with safety requirements.

### 2. **Stability and Modifications**
- Any modification affecting **vessel stability** must be recorded.
- Record must be maintained in a form and manner specified by the Minister.
- For vessels that have undergone a stability assessment, the modification record is retained **until a new stability assessment** is conducted.

### 3. **Freezing Spray Conditions**
- **No operation** in areas where Environment Canada issues a **freezing spray warning** unless:
  - A **stability assessment** confirms safe operation in freezing spray with ice accretion.
- Vessels must carry **means to remove accumulated ice** from the hull.

### 4. **Pre-Operation Notification**
- Before first service, the authorized representative must inform the Minister of:
  - Intention to operate the vessel.
  - Physical characteristics (size, length, tonnage, etc.).
  - Nature of operation (e.g., trawling, purse seining).
- The representative must provide this information upon request.

### 5. **Design Limitations and Safe Operation**
- **No operation** beyond the vessel‚Äôs **design limitations**.
- **No careless operation** ‚Äî must be conducted with due care, attention, and consideration for others.

### 6. **Lifejacket Requirements**
- **All persons on board** must wear a lifejacket when:
  - Vessel has **no deck or deck structure** ‚Üí all must wear lifejackets.
  - Vessel has a **deck** ‚Üí all persons on the deck or in the cockpit must wear lifejackets.

### 7. **Weather-Exposed Openings**
- Openings exposed to weather must be **closed** when not needed for operations.
- If danger of water entry exists, they must be **closed immediately**.

### 8. **Maintenance and Tools**
- Tools and spare parts for routine maintenance and minor repairs must be:
  - Carried on board.
  - Secured in a **readily accessible location**.

### 9. **Fuel Safety (Gasoline-Powered Vessels)**
- **Engine space blower must run for at least 4 minutes** before starting the engine.
- **No fuel leakage** allowed.
- **Fuel discharge** must comply with *Vessel Pollution and Dangerous Chemicals Regulations*.
- **Fueling rules**:
  - At dockside or beached:
    - If portable tank: must be removed.
    - If fixed tank: only one person allowed on board.
  - When fueling a fixed tank:
    - All electrical equipment must be off.
    - All doors, windows, and ports must be closed.
    - All engines must be shut off.
    - All open flames (including pilot lights) must be extinguished.
- **Portable fuel tanks** must be stored:
  - Far from heat, ignition sources, machinery, and crew quarters.
- **Fueling via funnel/nozzle**:
  - Requires continuous contact between shipboard pipe and filling device during operation.

### 10. **Portable Fuel-Burning Equipment**
- Must be:
  - Used only in **well-ventilated open spaces or on open decks**.
  - **Secured** to prevent movement during use.
  - Stored in **well-ventilated, isolated areas** from heat and ignition sources.

## üîê SAFETY PROCEDURES AND DRILLS

### Required Safety Procedures (in writing, in English/French/bilingual):
1. Location and use of **all safety equipment**.
2. Measures to:
   - Prevent falls overboard.
   - Retrieve persons who fall overboard.
   - Protect limbs from rotating equipment.
   - Avoid hazards from ropes, nets, docking lines, etc.
3. **Emergency load release** (for beam trawling and purse seining).
4. Fire and explosion prevention measures.
5. **Watertight/weathertight integrity**:
   - With deck ‚Üí prevent flooding of interior spaces.
   - Without deck ‚Üí prevent swamping.
6. Safe loading, stowing, and unloading of fish, bait, and consumables.
7. Operation of **towing and lifting equipment** and **prevention of overloading**.
