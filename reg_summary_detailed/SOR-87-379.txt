**Detailed Summary of the Teleglobe Canada Pension Protection Regulations**

---

### **1. Regulatory Background and Authority**

The *Teleglobe Canada Pension Protection Regulations* were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Minister of State (Privatization)** and the **Treasury Board**, pursuant to **Section 25 of the Teleglobe Canada Reorganization and Divestiture Act** (*S.C. 1987, c. 12*).

This regulatory instrument is designed to **protect the pension rights of public service employees** who were transitioning from the federal public service to a newly established private corporation â€” Teleglobe Canada â€” as part of a broader privatization process.

These regulations may be cited as the **Teleglobe Canada Pension Protection Regulations**.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

- **Act**: Refers to the *Teleglobe Canada Reorganization and Divestiture Act*, which provides the legal framework for the restructuring and divestiture of Teleglobe Canada.
- **New Corporation**: Refers to the newly formed private corporation (Teleglobe Canada) resulting from the reorganization and divestiture under the Act.

---

### **3. Applicability of Existing Pension Laws**

The regulations state that **all provisions of the following Acts and their associated regulations apply to employees referred to in paragraph 25(1)(d) of the Act**, *except where specifically adapted* under Sections 4 and 5 of the Act:

- **Public Service Superannuation Act (PSSA)**  
- **Supplementary Retirement Benefits Act (SRBA)**  
- Regulations made under those Acts (as amended)

This means that the standard pension rules governing public servants generally continue to apply to these employees â€” but with **key modifications** to account for the transition from public service to a private entity (Teleglobe Canada).

### **4. Adaptations to the Public Service Superannuation Act (PSSA)**

#### **Section 25 of the Act â€“ Adaptation of Subparagraphs 9(7)(b)(i) and (ii)**

These provisions relate to the **determination of pensionable service years** and how retirement is calculated.

**Key Change:**
- The **year in which a person ceased to be employed in the Public Service** is now treated as the **year in which they ceased to be employed by the new corporation**.

#### **Section 25 of the Act â€“ Adaptation of Sections 11 and 12 of the PSSA**

These sections govern the **calculation of pensionable service** and **eligibility for pensions**.

**Key Changes:**

1. **Inclusion of Service with the New Corporation**:
   - The number of years of pensionable service, and any periods of employment, **shall include** any service or employment with the new corporation during the period:
     - Starting on the **day the person ceases to be employed in the Public Service**, and
     - Ending on the **day immediately preceding the day** the person ceases to be employed by the new corporation.

   ðŸ‘‰ This ensures that the **transition period** between public service and employment with Teleglobe Canada is **counted as pensionable service**, preserving pension benefits.

2. **Retirement Date Adjustment**:
   - For all purposes **except subsection 11(4)**, the **day a person ceases to be employed in the Public Service** is considered the **same day** they cease to be employed by the new corporation.

   ðŸ‘‰ This eliminates a potential discrepancy in retirement eligibility dates and ensures that pension entitlements are not disrupted by the change in employer.

> *Exception*: Subsection 11(4) of the PSSA (which may relate to specific pension benefits like early retirement or disability) is **not affected** by this adaptation.

These provisions relate to **supplementary retirement benefits**, including contributions and retirement eligibility.

---

### **6. Effective Date**

These regulations come into force **on and after April 4, 1987**.

This date coincides with the official start of the transition period from public service to Teleglobe Canada, making it a critical milestone in the privatization process.

---

### **7. Purpose and Significance**

The primary objective of these regulations is to:

- **Ensure continuity and fairness** in pension entitlements for public service employees transitioning to Teleglobe Canada.
- **Prevent loss of pension benefits** due to the change in employer status.
- **Provide clarity and consistency** in how pensionable service, retirement dates, and contribution periods are calculated during the transition.
- **Protect employees** from adverse outcomes that might otherwise result from a sudden shift from a public to a private employer.

By adapting key provisions of the PSSA and SRBA, the regulations effectively **bridge the gap** between public service pension rules and private-sector employment, ensuring that employees are not disadvantaged by the reorganization.
