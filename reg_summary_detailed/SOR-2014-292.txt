### **4. Notification Requirements When a Basic Requirement is Lost**

### **8. Grounds for Discharge (Beyond Code of Conduct Violations)**
Under paragraphs 20.2(1)(f) and (g) of the Act, discharge may be justified even if not due to a Code of Conduct breach, including:
- Having a **disability** (as defined in the *Canadian Human Rights Act*);
- Being **absent from duty without authorization**;
- Being **detained in custody** or **serving a prison sentence**;
- Being in a **conflict of interest** not listed in the Code of Conduct;
- **Losing a basic requirement** for duty performance;
- Being **convicted of an offence punishable by indictment** (or would be if committed in Canada).
