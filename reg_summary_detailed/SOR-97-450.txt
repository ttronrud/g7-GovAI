### üìå Purpose of the Order

This Order grants the **Commission for Environmental Cooperation (CEC)** and its officials **privileges and immunities** in Canada under the **Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act**, enabling the CEC to function effectively as a trilateral environmental body with legal standing and operational autonomy in Canada.

These privileges are derived from the **Agreement on Environmental Cooperation (AEC)** among Canada, the United States, and the United Mexican States, which was originally signed in **1994** and **renewed in 2018**, with the renewal coming into force on **July 1, 2020**.

### üìö Legal Capacities and Privileges and Immunities

#### 1. **The Commission as a Body Corporate**
- The CEC is granted **the legal capacity of a body corporate** in Canada.
- This allows it to:
  - Enter into contracts,
  - Own property,
  - Be sued or sue in Canadian courts,
  - Hold legal rights and obligations.

#### 2. **Privileges and Immunities of Council Members (Representatives of Foreign States)**
- Members of the Council (e.g., government representatives from Canada, the U.S., or Mexico) are granted **privileges and immunities comparable to those of diplomatic envoys** under the **Vienna Convention**.
- These include:
  - Immunity from legal process in their official capacity,
  - Exemption from certain taxes,
  - Protection from arbitrary arrest or detention.

> *Based on Articles 8 and 1 of Article 13 of the Agreement.*

#### 3. **Privileges and Immunities of the Executive Director and Secretariat Officials**
- The **Executive Director** and **Directors of the Secretariat**, along with their **families forming part of their households**, are granted privileges and immunities **comparable to those of diplomatic agents** under the **Vienna Convention**.
- This includes:
  - Immunity from legal process in official acts,
  - Exemption from taxation on salaries,
  - Immunity from national service obligations,
  - Immunity from immigration restrictions and alien registration,
  - Access to exchange facilities (e.g., currency exchange) like diplomatic missions,
  - Right to import free of duty personal effects upon arrival in Canada.

> *Based on Article 9 and paragraph 2 of Article 13 of the Agreement.*

#### 4. **Privileges and Immunities of Other Secretariat Officials**
- All other Secretariat staff are granted the following privileges and immunities under **Section 18 of Article V of the Vienna Convention**:
  - **Immunity from legal process** for words spoken or written and acts performed in an official capacity.
  - **Exemption from taxation** on salaries and emoluments.
  - **Immunity from national service obligations**.
  - **Immunity from immigration restrictions** for spouses and dependent relatives.
  - **Same exchange facilities** as diplomatic officials of comparable rank.
  - **Right to import free of duty** furniture and personal effects upon first taking up post.

> *Based on Article 10 and paragraph 2 of Article 13 of the Agreement.*

#### 5. **Privileges and Immunities of Experts Performing Missions**
- Experts engaged by the Commission (e.g., technical advisors, scientists, consultants) are granted immunities under **Article VI of the Vienna Convention**, including:
  - **Immunity from personal arrest or detention**.
  - **Immunity from legal process** for words spoken or written and acts performed in the course of their mission ‚Äî **even after they cease employment**.
  - **Inviolability of personal papers and documents**.
  - **Same immunities and facilities** for personal baggage as granted to diplomatic envoys.

> *Based on Article 11 and paragraph 3 of Article 13 of the Agreement.*
