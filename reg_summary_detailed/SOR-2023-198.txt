**Detailed Summary of the Canada-CARICOM Summit 2023 Privileges and Immunities Order**

---

### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
The regulation is issued under **subsection 5(1)a of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*** (S.C. 2002, c. 12, s. 3; S.C. 1991, c. 41).  
- It is made by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the **recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs**.  
- This authority allows the Canadian government to grant temporary privileges and immunities to foreign entities and individuals participating in specific international events, such as summits.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Order**
The primary purpose of this Order is to **grant specific privileges and immunities** to participants in the **Canada-CARICOM Summit 2023**, held in Ottawa from **October 17 to 19, 2023**.  
These provisions ensure that participants can carry out their official duties without undue interference, in accordance with international norms and practices.

---

### **3. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **CARICOM** | The Caribbean Community established under the *Treaty of Chaguaramas*, signed on July 4, 1973, in Chaguaramas, Trinidad and Tobago. This includes the CARICOM Secretariat as referenced in Article 23 of the Treaty. |
| **Convention** | Refers to the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, as set out in **Schedule III** of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*. This is the legal framework used to define the rights of international organizations and their representatives. |
| **Relevant Period** | The time frame from **October 14, 2023, to October 22, 2023** — a period that extends slightly beyond the Summit dates to cover preparation and post-summit activities. |
| **Summit** | The Canada-CARICOM Summit, hosted in Ottawa from **October 17 to 19, 2023**. |

---

### **4. Privileges and Immunities Granted**

#### **(A) CARICOM as a Legal Entity**
During the relevant period:
- CARICOM is granted **the legal capacity of a corporation**.  
  → This allows CARICOM to act independently in legal matters, enter into contracts, and hold property, which is essential for conducting official business during the Summit.
- CARICOM is granted **privileges and immunities** as specified in:
  - **Sections 2 to 5 of Article II** of the Convention (relating to immunities from legal process, property, and jurisdiction),
  - **Article III** of the Convention (relating to immunities for representatives and officials).

> *Note:* These immunities are limited to what is **necessary for the exercise of CARICOM’s functions** during the Summit.

---

#### **(B) Official Delegates from Foreign States**
Representatives of foreign states who are **official delegates** to the Summit:
- Are granted privileges and immunities under:
  - **Paragraphs 11(a) to (f)** and **Section 12 of Article IV** of the Convention.
- These rights are **subject to the limitations** specified in **Sections 14 to 16 of Article IV** of the Convention.

> *Key Implications:*
> - These provisions typically include immunity from arrest, protection of personal and official communications, and protection of documents.
> - However, **duty and tax relief** (such as exemption from certain taxes or duties) are **excluded** from these privileges — meaning delegates remain subject to Canadian tax laws and employment regulations.

---

#### **(C) Senior Officials of CARICOM**
Senior CARICOM officials participating in the Summit:
- Are granted **privileges and immunities comparable to those of diplomatic agents under the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations**, **except**:
  - They **do not receive duty and tax relief**.

> *Key Implications:*
> - This means senior officials enjoy diplomatic-level protections such as:
>   - Immunity from legal proceedings (except for crimes against persons or property),
>   - Protection of official communications,
>   - Freedom to travel and conduct official business.
> - However, they are **not exempt** from Canadian tax obligations or employment duties.

---

#### **(D) Other Officials and International Organization Representatives**
Other CARICOM officials and officials from **other international organizations** who have been **invited by Canada** to the Summit:
- Are granted privileges and immunities under:
  - **Paragraphs 18(a), (d), and (e)** of **Article V** of the Convention.

> *Key Implications:*
> - These include:
>   - Immunity from legal proceedings (in certain circumstances),
>   - Protection of personal and official correspondence,
>   - Protection of official documents and equipment.

> *Note:* This is a more limited set of immunities than those given to diplomatic agents or senior officials.

---

#### **(E) Experts Invited by Canada**
Experts invited by Canada to participate in the Summit:
- Are granted privileges and immunities under **Article VI** of the Convention.

> *Key Implications:*
> - These rights typically include:
>   - Immunity from legal proceedings in relation to their expert duties,
>   - Protection of personal and professional materials,
>   - Protection of their work during the Summit.
> - These privileges are strictly limited to the **functions of their expert role** and are not extended to personal or private matters.

---

### **5. Scope and Duration**
- The Order applies **only during the relevant period**: **October 14 to October 22, 2023**.
- The **Summit itself** occurs between October 17 and 19, 2023, and the extended period allows for:
  - Preparatory meetings,
  - Post-summit evaluations,
  - Travel and logistics coordination.

---

### **6. Entry into Force**
- The Order comes into force on the **day it is registered** in the *Canada Gazette* (official publication for federal laws).

---

### **7. Key Principles and Rationale**
- **International Norms**: The Order aligns with established international practices, particularly those of the United Nations and the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations.
- **Principle of Necessity**: Privileges and immunities are granted **only to the extent required** for the performance of official duties.
- **Balanced Approach**: While protecting the functions of delegates and officials, it **excludes certain privileges** (like tax or duty relief) to maintain Canadian legal and fiscal oversight.
- **Temporary Nature**: The Order is **not permanent** and is limited to a specific event and timeframe, ensuring accountability and compliance with Canadian law.

---

### **8. Summary Table of Privileges by Category**

| Category | Privileges Granted | Exclusions / Limitations |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------------|
| **CARICOM** | Legal capacity of a corporation; immunities under Articles II and III of the Convention | Limited to functions related to the Summit |
| **Official Delegates (Foreign States)** | Immunities under Art. IV, para. 11(a)-(f) and Sec. 12; limited by Art. IV, secs. 14–16 | No duty or tax relief |
| **Senior CARICOM Officials** | Diplomatic-level immunities (like diplomats); no duty/tax relief | Excludes tax and duty benefits |
| **Other Officials / International Orgs** | Immunities under Art. V, para. 18(a), (d), (e) | Limited scope |
| **Experts** | Immunities under Art. VI of Convention | Limited to expert functions |

---

### **Conclusion**
This Order is a **temporary, targeted, and legally grounded** measure that enables Canada to host the Canada-CARICOM Summit in a manner consistent with international diplomatic standards. It ensures the effective functioning of the Summit by providing appropriate legal protections to CARICOM, its officials, foreign delegates, and experts — all while respecting Canadian sovereignty and fiscal accountability. The provisions are narrowly tailored, strictly time-bound, and based on international legal instruments, demonstrating a balanced and principled approach to diplomatic engagement.

--- 

**Final Note**: This Order reflects Canada’s commitment to multilateral cooperation with the Caribbean region and underscores the importance of maintaining diplomatic and institutional integrity during high-level political dialogues.
