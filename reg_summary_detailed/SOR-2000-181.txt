**Detailed Summary of the DNA Data Bank Advisory Committee Regulations (S.C. 1998, c. 37)**

---

### **Overview**
The *DNA Data Bank Advisory Committee Regulations*, made under Section 12 of the *DNA Identification Act* (S.C. 1998, c. 37), establish a formal advisory body to oversee and provide guidance on the establishment, operation, and governance of Canada’s national DNA data bank. These regulations were issued by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Solicitor General of Canada, and come into force on the date when Sections 2, 3, and 12 of the *DNA Identification Act* take effect.

---

### **Key Definitions**
- **"Advisory Committee"**: Refers specifically to the *DNA Data Bank Advisory Committee*, established under Section 2 of the *DNA Identification Act*.

---

### **Establishment of the Advisory Committee**
- A formal **DNA Data Bank Advisory Committee** is created.
- The Committee is composed of:
  - **One Chairperson**
  - **One Vice-Chairperson**
  - **One representative from the Office of the Privacy Commissioner**
  - **Up to six additional members**, who may represent:
    - Police services
    - Legal profession
    - Scientific communities
    - Academic institutions

This composition ensures a balanced, multidisciplinary perspective, incorporating privacy concerns, law enforcement needs, scientific expertise, and public accountability.

---

### **Appointment and Term of Office**
- Members are **appointed by the Solicitor General of Canada**.
- Each member serves a **term of not more than five years**.
- **Reappointment is permitted** upon the expiration of a member’s term, provided they remain eligible and are recommended for continued service.

This structure allows for stability and continuity while also enabling periodic review and renewal of representation from various stakeholder groups.

---

### **Powers and Responsibilities**
The Advisory Committee has two primary functions:

1. **Advisory Role**:
   - May advise the **Commissioner of the DNA Data Bank** (the designated official under the Act) on any matter related to:
     - The **establishment** of the national DNA data bank
     - Its **operation and management**
   - This advisory function may be initiated:
     - **On its own motion**, when the Committee determines that such advice is necessary
     - **Upon request from the Commissioner**

2. **Annual Reporting Obligation**:
   - The Committee is required to **report annually** to the Commissioner.
   - Reports must be submitted **within three months after the end of each fiscal year**.
   - These reports will detail the Committee’s activities, decisions, recommendations, and any significant developments or challenges encountered during the year.

---

### **Legal Basis and Effective Date**
- The regulations are enacted **pursuant to Section 12 of the DNA Identification Act**.
- They come into force **on the date when Sections 2, 3, and 12 of the DNA Identification Act** (S.C. 1998, c. 37) come into force.

This ensures that the Advisory Committee is operational from the outset of the Act’s implementation, providing timely oversight and guidance.

---

### **Purpose and Significance**
The establishment of the DNA Data Bank Advisory Committee serves several critical purposes:
- To ensure **transparency and public accountability** in the use of DNA data.
- To **balance law enforcement needs** with **privacy rights and ethical considerations**.
- To provide **independent, expert advice** on technical, legal, and policy issues related to the DNA data bank.
- To promote **public trust** in a system that involves sensitive personal biological information.

By including representatives from privacy, law, science, and academia, the Committee acts as a bridge between government institutions and the public, helping to ensure that the DNA data bank operates within legal, ethical, and democratic frameworks.

---

### **Conclusion**
The *DNA Data Bank Advisory Committee Regulations* provide a robust, structured, and accountable mechanism for guiding the development and operation of Canada’s national DNA data bank. Through its diverse membership, annual reporting, and advisory mandate, the Committee plays a vital role in safeguarding individual rights, ensuring legal compliance, and maintaining public confidence in the use of DNA information for criminal justice purposes.

These regulations reflect a proactive approach to governance in a high-stakes area of biotechnology and public policy.
