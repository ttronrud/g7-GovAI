#### **3. Core Provisions of the Regulations**

The regulations prohibit **dumping** of chicken in interprovincial trade (non-export) under the following conditions:

> **No person may sell chicken in a province other than the province of production at a price that is less than:**
> 
> 1. The price charged at or about the same time by the trade for similar sales of chicken of an equivalent variety, class, or grade in the **province where the chicken is produced**, **plus**
> 2. Any **reasonable transportation charges** required to ship the chicken to the place of sale.

##### **Key Elements:**
- **Scope**: Applies only to **interprovincial trade** (not export).
- **Exclusions**: Does **not apply** when chicken is shipped from a non-signatory territory to a signatory province.
- **Definition of "Dumping"**: Selling below "fair market value" â€” defined as the price charged in the **province of origin** for similar chicken products.
- **Similarity Criteria**: Chicken must be of the **same variety, class, or grade** to be considered comparable.
- **Price Benchmark**: The price must be compared to the **market price** in the province of origin, adjusted for **reasonable transportation costs**.

#### **4. Purpose and Objectives**

The primary objectives of these regulations are:

- To **prevent unfair competition** by protecting domestic producers from being undercut by cheaper chicken sold from other provinces.
- To **maintain stable prices** and ensure fair returns for Canadian chicken farmers.
- To support the **implementation of a marketing plan** by ensuring that chicken prices reflect true market conditions in the region of origin.
- To promote **market integrity** and prevent price distortion that could harm the long-term viability of the Canadian chicken industry.

As such, while historically significant, they are **no longer in effect** and should not be applied in current regulatory practice.
