## ðŸ—ï¸ Designated Classes of Projects (Exempt from EIAs)

The regulation outlines **three main schedules** of exempt projects, each with specific conditions and geographic applicability.

---

### âœ… **Schedule 1 & 2: General Exemptions (Federal Lands & Parks Canada)**

#### **Applicability**
- Projects listed in **Schedule 1** are exempt when carried out on **federal lands or outside Canada**.
- Projects in **Schedule 2** are exempt when carried out on **federal lands administered by Parks Canada**.

#### **Excluded Activities (Even if in Schedule)**
The following activities **do not qualify** for exemption, regardless of the schedule:
1. Projects that change:
   - Water level of a water body
   - Alignment of a watercourse
   - Characteristics of a wetland
2. Activities involving **listed wildlife species** under the *Species at Risk Act* (e.g., their residences or critical habitats)
3. Any activity under **subsection 5(1) of the Migratory Birds Regulations, 2022**, unless specifically allowed under subsection 5(2)
4. Release of a **deleterious substance** into a water body (as defined in Fisheries Act s.34(1))
5. Activities prohibited under:
   - *Canadian Navigable Waters Act*
   - *Fisheries Act* (s.34.4(1), 35(1), 36(3))
   - *Scott Islands Protected Marine Area Regulations*
6. Removal or damage to:
   - Historical, archaeological, paleontological, or architectural resources
7. Damage to such significant structures or sites

> ðŸ” **Key Point**: Even if a project fits within a schedule, if it triggers one of these exclusions, it **must undergo a full environmental assessment**.
