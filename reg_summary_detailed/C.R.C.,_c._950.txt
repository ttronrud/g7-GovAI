**Detailed Summary of the Indian Band Council Procedure Regulations**

---

### **1. Title and Citation**
These regulations are officially known as the **Indian Band Council Procedure Regulations**. They establish a formal framework governing the operations, procedures, and governance of Indian Band Councils in Canada, as administered under the *Indian Act*.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

The regulations define several key terms to ensure clarity and consistency in their application:

- **Assistant Deputy Minister**: Refers to the Assistant Deputy Minister, Indian and Eskimo Affairs, within the Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (DIAND). This individual holds a senior administrative role overseeing Indigenous affairs.

- **Council**: The elected governing body of an Indian Band, established under **Section 74 of the Indian Act**. It is responsible for managing the day-to-day affairs and decision-making for the band.

- **Department**: The Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (DIAND), responsible for federal policy and administration related to Indigenous peoples.

- **Minister**: The federal Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development, who holds ultimate authority over the Department and its operations.

- **Secretary**: A member of the council appointed to record the minutes of council meetings. This role ensures transparency and accountability in decision-making.

- **Superintendent**: A senior field officer responsible for overseeing Indian Affairs operations in a specific region. This includes:
  - The Indian Commissioner for British Columbia,
  - Regional Supervisors,
  - Assistant Indian Agency Officers,
  - Any officer acting under instructions from the Minister or Assistant Deputy Minister.

This definition ensures that the superintendent plays a critical supervisory and advisory role in council affairs.

---

### **3. First and Regular Meetings**

- The **first meeting of a newly elected council must occur no later than one month after the election**.
- The date, time, and place must be clearly announced in writing to all council members.
- Subsequent meetings are scheduled at times and places deemed necessary for the council’s business or the band’s affairs.

> **Purpose**: Ensures timely initiation of governance and accountability from day one.

---

### **4. Attendance Requirements**

- No council member may be absent from **three consecutive meetings** without **prior authorization** from:
  - The **Chief of the Band**, or
  - The **Superintendent**, with the **consent of a majority of the council members**.

> **Rationale**: Prevents chronic absenteeism and ensures active participation in governance.

---

### **5. Special Meetings**

- The **Chief of the Band** or the **Superintendent** may call a special meeting at any time.
- A special meeting may also be summoned upon **request from a majority of the council members**.
- The **superintendent must notify all members** of the date, time, and location of the special meeting.

> **Purpose**: Allows for urgent or ad-hoc decision-making when needed.

---

### **6. Quorum Requirements**

- A **majority of all council members** constitutes a quorum.
- For councils with **nine or more members**, a **minimum of five members** is required to constitute a quorum.

> **Note**: This ensures that decisions are made with broad consensus and not by a small, potentially unrepresentative group.

- If no quorum is present within **one hour** of the scheduled meeting, the secretary shall:
  - Call the roll,
  - Record the names of those present,
  - And adjourn the meeting until the next scheduled session.

> **Purpose**: Prevents meetings from being held without sufficient representation.

---

### **7. Presiding Officers**

- The **Chief of the Band** or the **Superintendent** is the presiding officer at a meeting, unless otherwise specified.
- In the absence of the Chief or if the Superintendent is not present, the **presiding officer is elected from among the members present**.
- The presiding officer has full authority over:
  - Maintaining order,
  - Deciding procedural questions,
  - Managing the flow of debate.

> **Note**: The presiding officer is not entitled to vote unless there is a tie.

---

### **8. Order of Business at Regular Meetings**

The standard order of business during regular council meetings is as follows:

1. **Review and Adoption of Minutes**  
   - Reading and approving the minutes of the previous meeting (with possible corrections).

2. **Unfinished Business**  
   - Addressing any unresolved issues from prior meetings.

3. **Correspondence and Petitions**  
   - Reviewing incoming letters, requests, or formal petitions from individuals or organizations.

4. **Committee Reports**  
   - Hearing reports from standing or special committees.

5. **New Business**  
   - Introducing new proposals or initiatives.

6. **Hearings on Deputations**  
   - Listening to statements from individuals or groups representing community interests.

7. **Adjournment**  
   - Closing the meeting.

> **Purpose**: Provides a structured, transparent, and predictable process for council deliberations.

---

### **9. Proposal and Debate Procedures**

- Each resolution must be:
  - **Moved** by a member,
  - **Seconded** by another member,
  - Then formally presented to the presiding officer.

- Once a resolution is placed before the council, it is considered **in the possession of the council**.

- A resolution may be **withdrawn** by **consent of a majority of members present**.

- Members may only speak when addressing the **presiding officer**, and must stay focused on the current topic.

- If multiple members wish to speak at once, the **presiding officer decides who speaks first**.

- A member may be **called to order** during a speech, which suspends debate until the point of order is resolved.

- A member may **only raise a point of order once**.

- Any member may **appeal** a presiding officer’s decision, and such appeals are decided by **majority vote without debate**.

> **Purpose**: Ensures fair, orderly, and respectful debate while preventing procedural chaos.

---

### **10. Voting Procedures**

- All questions are decided by a **majority vote of members present and voting**.

- The **presiding officer does not vote** unless there is a **tie**.

- In the case of a tie:
  - If the presiding officer is **not the superintendent**, they cast the deciding vote.
  - If the presiding officer **is the superintendent**, the tie is resolved by a **majority of the council** (or by a designated alternate).

- A member may **refuse to vote**, and in such cases, they are **deemed to have voted in the affirmative**.

- When a division occurs:
  - Each member must **publicly announce their vote**.
  - The secretary shall **record the vote** upon request by any member.

> **Purpose**: Ensures transparency, accountability, and clear records of decisions.

---

### **11. Access and Participation**

- Regular meetings are **open to all members of the band**.
- No member may be excluded from a regular meeting **unless they engage in improper conduct**.

- The presiding officer may **expel or exclude** any person causing a disturbance.

> **Purpose**: Promotes inclusivity, community involvement, and democratic participation.

---

### **12. Committees**

- At its first meeting, the council may appoint the following **standing committees**:
  - **Finance**
  - **Roads and Bridges**
  - **Welfare**

- The council may also form **special committees** on matters of interest to the band.

- A **majority of committee members** constitutes a quorum.

- The **Chief of the Band** is an **ex-officio member** of all committees and has the right to vote.

- Other council members may **attend and participate in discussions** but **do not have voting rights** unless explicitly permitted by the committee.

- Committees are responsible for:
  - Reporting regularly on matters within their scope,
  - Recommending actions to the council,
  - Considering and reporting on matters referred by the council or the Chief.

- Special committee meetings may be called by:
  - The **chairman**, or
  - A **majority of the committee**, or
  - The **Chief of the Band** or **Superintendent** (in the absence of the chairman).

> **Purpose**: Enables specialized, focused management of key issues while maintaining oversight by the full council.

---

### **13. Role of the Minister’s Representative**

- A representative of the **Minister** may attend council meetings and:
  - Explain the council’s powers and duties,
  - Advise on procedural matters,
  - Provide information requested by council members on band administration.

> **Purpose**: Ensures that the council operates within the framework of federal policy and regulations.

---

### **14. Flexibility in Procedure**

- The council may adopt **additional rules of procedure** for matters not specifically addressed in these regulations, **provided such rules are not inconsistent** with these regulations.

> **Purpose**: Allows councils to adapt to local needs while maintaining compliance with federal standards.

---

### **Summary of Key Principles and Objectives**

| Principle | Objective |

| **Democratic Participation** | Ensures all members have a voice and are accountable through regular attendance and voting. |
| **Transparency** | Minutes are recorded; votes are publicly announced; decisions are documented. |
| **Order and Fairness** | Clear rules for debate, voting, and appeals prevent chaos and ensure equity. |
| **Accountability** | The superintendent and chief of the band have oversight roles; members are held responsible for attendance and conduct. |
