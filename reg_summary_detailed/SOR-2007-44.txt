### **Detailed Summary of the Regulations Implementing United Nations Security Council Resolution 1737 (2006) on Iran**

---

#### **Background and Legal Authority**

These regulations were enacted by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**, pursuant to **Section 2 of the United Nations Act**. The authority stems from the United Nations Charter, specifically **Article 41**, which empowers the Security Council to take action to maintain international peace and security, including imposing sanctions.

The regulations implement **United Nations Security Council Resolution 1737 (2006)**, adopted on **December 23, 2006**, which imposes targeted sanctions on Iran related to its nuclear activities and proliferation-sensitive programs. The purpose of these domestic regulations is to **effectively apply the measures** outlined in Resolution 1737 within Canadian jurisdiction.

> ⚠️ **Note**: This document has been partially **repealed or superseded** by subsequent amendments (e.g., SOR/2025-202), particularly as of 2025. Key provisions referenced in the original text are no longer in force. However, this summary reflects the **original content and intent** of the regulation as it stood prior to repeal.

## **Core Prohibitions**

### **1. Prohibition on Dealing with Designated Persons’ Property**

It is **prohibited** for any person in Canada or any Canadian living outside Canada to:

- Knowingly **deal in property** (directly or indirectly) owned, held, or controlled by a **designated person**, or by someone acting on their behalf or at their direction;
- **Enter into or facilitate** such transactions;
- **Provide financial or related services** (e.g., banking, payment processing) for such dealings;
- **Make property or financial services available** to a designated person or to someone acting on their behalf.

> ✅ **Exception**: This prohibition does **not apply** to humanitarian assistance provided by:
> - United Nations agencies, programs, funds, specialized agencies;
> - International organizations;
> - Humanitarian NGOs with UN observer status;
> - Bilateral/multilateral NGOs participating in UN humanitarian plans;
> - Other entities authorized by a Security Council committee.

These exceptions allow for essential aid to reach vulnerable populations in Iran without violating sanctions.

---

### **2. Prohibition on Exporting Specific Products to Iran**

Any person in Canada or abroad is prohibited from **selling, supplying, or transferring** the following products **destined for Iran, persons acting on Iran’s behalf, or persons in Iran**:

#### **A. Products Listed in INFCIRC/254 (IAEA Information Circular)**
- **Part 1**: Items related to nuclear fuel cycle (e.g., uranium enrichment, reprocessing).
- **Part 2**: Items related to nuclear reactor design, fuel fabrication, and safety systems.

> ⚠️ **Exception**: Products listed in **Annex B, Section 1** of INFCIRC/254, intended for **light-water reactors** or **low-enriched uranium** used in such reactors, may be exported **if incorporated into assembled nuclear fuel elements** — provided they are not used for enrichment or weapons purposes.

#### **B. Products Listed in the Guide (Export Control List)**
Specific items under:
- Group 1: Items 1–1.A.1 to 1–1.A.3, 1–1.C, 1–7.A to 1–7.E, 1–9.A to 1–9.E
- Group 2: Items 2–10.c and 2–10.d, 2–21.b.1, 2–21.b.3 and 2–21.b.4

These cover technologies and equipment related to **nuclear proliferation and weapon development**.

#### **C. Conventional Arms**
- Battle tanks, armored combat vehicles, large-calibre artillery, combat aircraft, attack helicopters, warships, missiles, and missile systems — as defined in the **UN Register of Conventional Arms** — including spare parts.

> ⚠️ The list may be expanded or updated by the Security Council or its Committee.

#### **D. Missile Technology**
- Any technology related to **ballistic missiles capable of delivering nuclear weapons**, including launch systems or associated components.
- Based on the **Missile Technology Control Regime (MTCR)** annex from July 16, 2015 (U.S. letter).

---

### **3. Prohibition on Uranium Mining and Nuclear Material Use in Canada**

It is **prohibited** to:

- Make available **property or provide financial services** related to:
  - Uranium mining in Canada;
  - Production or use of nuclear materials and technologies listed in **Part 1 of INFCIRC/254**;
- To **Iran**, **any person in Iran**, or **any entity owned, held, or controlled by Iran or persons acting on its behalf**.

> ❌ This includes direct or indirect transactions involving uranium mining, processing, or nuclear fuel cycle technologies in Canada.

Also prohibited:
- **Entering into or facilitating transactions** involving such activities with Iran or its affiliates.

---

### **4. Prohibition on Technical Assistance to Iran**

No person in Canada or abroad may provide **technical assistance** (including training, consulting, know-how, or data transfer) to Iran or any person in Iran related to:

- The **sale, supply, or transfer** of any product prohibited under section 4;
- The **manufacture or use** of such products in Iran or on its behalf.

> ❌ This includes sharing technical data such as blueprints, software, or engineering designs.

---

### **5. Prohibition on Importing or Carrying Prohibited Products**

It is **prohibited** to:

- **Import, purchase, or acquire** arms and related material from Iran, from persons in Iran, or from those acting on Iran’s behalf;
- **Carry** (directly or indirectly) any of the prohibited products (as defined in section 4) **on a Canadian vessel or aircraft**:
  - Destined for Iran or any person in Iran;
  - Carried by a vessel or aircraft registered in Iran or contracted to Iran.

> ❌ Applies to **owners, masters, or operators** of Canadian vessels or aircraft.

Additionally:
- **No Canadian vessel or aircraft** may carry **products prohibited under section 4** from Iran — even if the final destination is Canada — if those products were acquired from Iran.

---

### **6. Prohibition on Vessel Operations for Iran-Related Activities**

A person may **not provide** goods, materials, or services (e.g., insurance, bunkering, stevedoring, lighterage) to a vessel **registered in Iran or contracted to Iran** if:

- There are **reasonable grounds to believe** the vessel is carrying prohibited products;
- The services are not **necessary for humanitarian purposes**.

> ✅ **Exception**: Humanitarian needs may justify such services.

---

### **7. General Prohibition on Facilitating or Assisting Prohibited Activities**

It is **prohibited** to knowingly:
- **Cause, facilitate, assist in**, or **intend to cause or assist** in any activity prohibited by sections 3 to 6.

This creates a broad liability for indirect support or facilitation.
