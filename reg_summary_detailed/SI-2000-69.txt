## **Detailed Summary of the Indian Settlements Remission Order (2000)**

### **1. Purpose and Authority**
- This regulation provides **tax remission** (i.e., a refund or cancellation of tax liability) for certain taxpayers and recipients of taxable supplies related to **Indian Settlements** in Canada.
- It is issued under the authority of **subsection 23(2)a of the Financial Administration Act**, which allows the Governor in Council to make orders for the remission of taxes in the public interest.
- The decision to issue this order was made **on the recommendation of the Minister of National Revenue**, indicating a policy-driven, revenue-considered approach to addressing tax obligations on Indigenous lands.

---

### **2. Scope and Applicability**
- The Order applies to **Indian Settlements** as defined in **Schedule 1**.
- An *Indian Settlement* refers to a specific geographic area named in **Column 1** of Schedule 1 and described in **Column 2** of that Schedule.
- The Order **remains in force until** all or part of the lands constituting the Indian Settlement are formally designated as a **reserve** by an order of the Governor in Council.
  - This means that as long as the land remains classified as an "Indian Settlement" (not a reserve), the remission provisions apply.

### **3. Definitions**

### **4. Remission for Income Tax (Section 5 â€“ Income Tax Remission)**

#### **What is remitted?**
- A taxpayer whose income arises from an Indian Settlement is entitled to **remission** of any tax, interest, or penalties **paid or payable** during a taxation year or fiscal period, **if**:
  - The amount of tax, interest, and penalties **actually paid** exceeds
  - The amount that would have been payable **if the Indian Settlement had been a reserve throughout the entire year/fiscal period**.

#### **Key Conditions:**
- The remission applies to **each taxation year or fiscal period** that begins **during or after** the date specified in **Column 2 of Schedule 2** for that particular Indian Settlement.
- The calculation compares the actual tax burden to what would have been the tax burden **had the settlement been a reserve** (i.e., treated as a legally designated Indigenous reserve under the Indian Act), which typically has different tax treatment.
