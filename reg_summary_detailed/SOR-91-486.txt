## **Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Import and Export of Contaminated Fuel (Canada)**

### **1. Legal Basis and Background**

This regulation was enacted under **section 34 of the Canadian Environmental Protection Act (CEPA), 1999** (as amended), specifically pursuant to **subsection 48(1)** of CEPA, which allows the Minister of the Environment to propose regulations concerning the import and export of toxic substances. 

- The proposed regulations were first published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **March 16, 1991**.
- A **60-day public comment period** was provided under **subsection 48(2)** of CEPA, during which individuals or organizations could file objections.
- **No objections were received** within the 60-day window.
- The Governor in Council, acting on the recommendation of the Minister of the Environment and the Minister of National Health and Welfare, and after consulting the federal-provincial advisory committee under **section 6** of CEPA, concluded that:
  - The proposed regulations do **not overlap** with any existing federal or provincial regulations on substances.
  - Therefore, the regulations were formally adopted by **Governor in Council** on the basis of section 34 of CEPA.

### **2. Definition of Key Term**

**Contaminated Fuel**  
- Defined as **any fuel listed in Item 13 of the List of Toxic Substances in Schedule I to CEPA, 1999**.  
- This includes fuels that contain hazardous contaminants such as polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), heavy metals, or other toxic substances that pose environmental or health risks.

---

### **3. Prohibitions on Import and Export**

#### **Prohibition on Import (Section 4(1) and (2.1))**
- It is **prohibited** for any person to **import contaminated fuel into Canada**, unless:
  - The fuel is imported **for destruction, disposal, or recycling**, and such activities are carried out in accordance with **federal or provincial laws** governing hazardous waste management.

#### **Prohibition on Export (Section 4(2) and (2.1))**
- It is **prohibited** for any person to **export contaminated fuel out of Canada**, unless:
  - The destination country has **authorized or permitted** the importation of the contaminated fuel through its competent authority.

> âš ï¸ **Important Exception**:  
> A person does **not contravene section 3** (which governs the movement of hazardous substances) if the contaminated fuel is **in transit** through Canada from a place outside Canada to another place outside Canada, **provided there is written evidence** confirming the fuel is in transit.

### **4. Conditions for Import and Export**

#### **Import for Destruction, Disposal, or Recycling**
- Only allowed if:
  - The activity complies with applicable **federal or provincial hazardous waste laws**.
  - The fuel is not being used or sold for commercial or consumer purposes.

#### **Export to a Foreign Country**
- Requires **prior authorization** from the **appropriate authority** in the destination country.
- The exporter must maintain documentation proving that the importation into that country is legally permitted.

### **5. Definitions**

- **Appropriate Authority**:  
  Refers to any **government body, agency, or official** in a foreign country that has the legal authority under its national laws to **authorize or permit the import** of contaminated fuel.
