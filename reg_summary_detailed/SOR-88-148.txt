### **Detailed Summary of the Toronto/Buttonville Airport Zoning Regulations (S.C. 1985, c. 28, s. 1)**

---

#### **1. Background and Legal Basis**
The **Toronto/Buttonville Airport Zoning Regulations** are established under **Section 4.4 of the Aeronautics Act**, which empowers the Minister of Transport to adopt zoning regulations for airports to ensure safety, minimize hazards to aircraft operations, and protect airspace integrity. 

Prior to the final adoption of these regulations, a **proposed version** was published in:
- Two successive issues of the *Canada Gazette, Part I* (June 13 and 20, 1987),
- Two successive issues of the *Richmond Hill Liberal* and *Markham Economist* (June 17 and 24, 1987),
- Two successive issues of *Lâ€™Express de Toronto* (June 16 and 23, 1987).

This publication provided a **reasonable opportunity for interested parties** (e.g., landowners, community groups, aviation stakeholders) to submit written representations or objections to the Minister of Transport. Based on this public consultation, the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister, formally adopted the regulations.

#### **4. Elevation Datum**
- The **assigned elevation of the Airport Reference Point (ARP)** is **193 meters above sea level**.
- All other elevation references (approach surfaces, outer surface) are measured relative to this point.

#### **5. Prohibited Activities â€“ Structural and Natural Growth Restrictions**

##### **(a) Construction and Development Restrictions**
No person may:
- Erect or construct any **building, structure, or object**,
- Or make any **addition to an existing building, structure, or object**,
on any land subject to these regulations, **if the highest point of that object exceeds**:

1. The **approach surface** (for each runway end),
2. The **outer surface**, or
3. The **transitional surface**.

> âš ï¸ *This applies to all structures, including towers, antennas, trees, or buildings.*

##### **(b) Management of Natural Growth**
If any **natural growth** (e.g., trees, shrubs, bushes) on regulated land exceeds the elevation of any of the above surfaces:
- The **Minister of Transport may require** the owner or occupier to **remove the excessive growth**.
- This ensures that natural features do not create visual obstructions or hazards during flight operations.

##### **(c) Waste Disposal Restrictions**
No owner or occupier may:
- Use any land under these regulations for the **disposal of waste that is edible or attractive to birds**.

#### **6. Description of Key Features (Based on Zoning Plan No. 19-036-84-05, May 30, 1986)**

##### **A. Approach Surfaces (by runway)**
Each approach surface is a **conical-shaped inclined plane** with a specified slope and horizontal distance from the runway end:

| Runway | Slope | Horizontal Distance from End | Height of Top Line | Width of Top Line |
|--------|-------|------------------------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| 03 | 1:30 (1m vert / 30m horiz) | 1,446 m | 48.2 m above end | 174.6 m from centerline |
| 15 | 1:40 | 1,724 m | 43.1 m above end | 333.6 m from centerline |
| 21 | 1:30 | 1,203 m | 40.1 m above end | 150.3 m from centerline |
| 33 | 1:40 | 1,768 m | 44.2 m above end | 340.2 m from centerline |

> These surfaces protect aircraft during approach and landing by preventing obstructions in the critical approach zone.

##### **B. Outer Surface**
- Located at a **constant elevation of 45 meters above the ARP (193 m AGL)**.
- If this elevation would place the outer surface **below 9 meters above ground level**, it is adjusted to **9 meters above ground** to ensure safety and practicality.

> This defines the maximum allowable height for all structures and vegetation across the entire airport perimeter.

##### **C. Strips**
- **Runway 03â€“21 Strip**:
  - Width: 60 meters (30 m on each side of centerline)
  - Length: 837.93 meters
- **Runway 15â€“33 Strip**:
  - Width: 150 meters (75 m on each side)
  - Length: 1,176.19 meters

> These define the active areas where aircraft take off and land.

##### **D. Transitional Surfaces**
- Inclined planes rising at a **1:7 slope** (1 meter vertical per 7 meters horizontal).
- Extend from the **lateral edges** of the strips and approach surfaces.
- Intersect with the **outer surface** or with the transitional surface of an adjacent strip.

> These surfaces provide a smooth transition between the runway area and the outer zone, preventing sharp elevation changes that could interfere with aircraft navigation.

---

#### **8. Purpose and Safety Objectives**
These regulations are designed to:
- Prevent **visual obstructions** during aircraft takeoff and landing.
- Minimize the risk of **bird strikes** by restricting waste disposal near the airport.
- Ensure **airfield safety and operational efficiency** by maintaining clear flight paths.
- Provide a **clear, enforceable framework** for land use around the airport.
