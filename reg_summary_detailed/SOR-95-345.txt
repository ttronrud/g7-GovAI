#### **3. Definitions and Key Terms**

The regulations define several critical spatial and technical terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |

| **Airport** | Refers to Burwash Airport, located near Burwash Landing, Yukon Territory. |
| **Airport Reference Point (ARP)** | A specific point on the runway centerline, 915 meters from the threshold of runway 28. It serves as the origin for all elevation and distance measurements. |
| **Approach Surfaces** | Imaginary inclined planes extending from each end of the runway strip (runway 10â€“28), designed to ensure safe aircraft approaches. #### **5. Core Restriction: Height Limitations**

**No person may erect, construct, or expand any building, structure, or object** (including additions to existing structures) **if the highest point of that structure exceeds**:

1. The **approach surfaces**  
2. The **outer surface**  
3. The **transitional surfaces**

> âš ï¸ This restriction applies to **every point** of any structure within the regulated zone. Even small structures (e.g., antennas, towers, sheds, or windbreaks) must comply with these height limits.

This rule is designed to:
- Prevent interference with aircraft during takeoff and landing
- Minimize visual and navigational hazards
- Ensure safe flight operations by maintaining clear airspace and visibility

#### **6. Purpose and Rationale**

The primary objectives of these zoning regulations are:

- **Safety**: To protect aircraft during approach and departure by maintaining unobstructed flight paths.
- **Air Navigation**: To preserve visual flight rules (VFR) and instrument flight rules (IFR) operations by ensuring adequate clearance.
- **Environmental and Community Protection**: To prevent noise and visual pollution from nearby development.
- **Regulatory Clarity**: To provide a consistent, measurable, and enforceable framework for land use near the airport.

### **Summary in Plain Language**

The **Burwash Airport Zoning Regulations** establish strict height limits on all structures near Burwash Airport in Yukon. These limits are based on precise geometric planes (approach, transitional, and outer surfaces) that extend from the runway and are designed to ensure aircraft can safely take off and land without obstruction.

- The airport's central point (ARP) is at 793.7 meters above sea level.
- A 4,000-meter radius circle defines the area under regulation.
- Structures cannot exceed the height of any of the three defined surfaces (approach, outer, or transitional).
- The rules were developed after public notice and consultation to balance aviation safety with community development.
