### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
- These regulations are made under **subsection 65(1)b of the Assisted Human Reproduction Act (AHR Act)**.
- The **Minister of Health** proposed the regulations under **subsection 66(1)** of the AHR Act, which requires that proposed regulations be laid before each House of Parliament.
- The Governor in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister) formally enacts the regulations, thereby giving them legal force.

> **Statutory Reference**:  
> S.C. 2004, c. 2; S.C. 2012, c. 19, s. 737  
> These regulations came into force on the date that **Section 12 of the AHR Act** came into effect (as amended).

### **4. Automobile Allowance and Maximum Reimbursement**

- For **transportation by automobile** (excluding vehicles operated by transportation services that issue receipts):
  - The maximum reimbursement is calculated based on:
    - Distance driven (in kilometers),
    - The **applicable automobile allowance rate** published annually by the **Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)**.

> Example:  
> If a surrogate drives 100 km, and the CRA rate is $0.50/km, the maximum reimbursement would be $50.

> This ensures transparency and prevents overpayment.

### **6. Reimbursement Process and Responsibilities**

- **Reimbursing Party (e.g., clinic, program, or sponsor)** must:
  - **Indicate on the declaration**:
    - The amount reimbursed for each expense,
    - The date of reimbursement.
  - **Sign the declaration** to affirm the accuracy of the reimbursement details.

> This creates a clear audit trail and establishes responsibility.

### **7. Reimbursement for Loss of Work Income (Surrogate Mothers Only)**

Under **subsection 12(3) of the AHR Act**, a surrogate mother may be reimbursed for lost income during pregnancy **if**:

- She was absent from work due to a medical reason certified by a **qualified medical practitioner** (as per paragraph 12(3)(a) of the AHR Act).
- She provides the following documents:

1. A **signed declaration** that includes:
   - Her name and address  
   - Start and end dates of absence from work  
   - Amount requested for reimbursement  
   - Statement that she has not received compensation from any other source  
   - Statement that all information is accurate and complete

2. **Supporting evidence** of income she would have earned during the absence (e.g., pay stubs, employment contract)

3. A **copy of the medical certification** from a qualified practitioner confirming the medical reason for absence.

> The reimbursing party must:
> - Indicate the amount reimbursed and the date of reimbursement on the declaration  
> - Sign it to confirm accuracy

> This provision recognizes the financial hardship faced by surrogate mothers due to pregnancy-related absences.

### **8. Recordkeeping Requirements**

- **All parties who reimburse expenses** must maintain records of:
  - All documents submitted (e.g., declarations, receipts, recommendations)
  - For a period of **six years after the date of reimbursement**

> This ensures transparency and enables audits or inspections by regulatory authorities.

- **Ministerial Oversight**:
  - The **Minister of Health** may, by **written notice**, require a person to provide records or additional information related to a reimbursement.
  - The person must provide the records by the date specified in the notice.

> This gives the government authority to investigate or verify claims if needed.
