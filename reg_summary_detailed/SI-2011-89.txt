## üîç OVERVIEW OF THE REGULATION

This document is an official **proclamation** from the Government of Canada that formally **enters into force** the **Agreement on Social Security between Canada and the Republic of Macedonia**, which was signed on August 26, 2009. The agreement establishes a framework for mutual recognition and cooperation in social security matters, including pensions, disability benefits, and eligibility rules, for individuals who work or reside in both countries.

The agreement entered into force on **November 1, 2011**, following a formal process under Canadian parliamentary procedures and compliance with statutory requirements under the *Old Age Security Act*.

## üìú BACKGROUND AND LEGAL FOUNDATION

### Key Events Leading to Entry into Force:
- **August 26, 2009**: The Agreement on Social Security between Canada and the Republic of Macedonia is signed.
- **April 29, 2010**: The Governor in Council issues **Order in Council P.C. 2010-0556**, declaring that the Agreement will enter into force on the first day of the fourth month after either party receives written confirmation that all statutory requirements have been met.
- **May 3‚Äì4, 2010**: The Order is laid before both Houses of Parliament (House of Commons and Senate), as required by law.
- **No revocation motion filed** within 20 sitting days of laying the Order.
- **June 28, 2010**: The Order comes into force under **subsection 42(2) of the Old Age Security Act**, marking the official start of the implementation process.
- **July 26, 2011**: Both parties exchange written notifications confirming compliance with all statutory requirements.
- **November 1, 2011**: The Agreement officially enters into force ‚Äî the first day of the fourth month after the month in which both parties received mutual notification of compliance.

> This date is explicitly defined in Article 28 of the original Agreement and is the cornerstone of the timeline.

### Final Proclamation:
- On **October 14, 2011**, the Governor General of Canada issues a formal **Proclamation** (P.C. 2011-948) to give public notice that the Agreement has entered into force on **November 1, 2011**.

## üß© ELIGIBILITY AND BENEFIT PAYMENT RULES

### General Principle:
> A person who is subject to the legislation of one or both Parties is **eligible for benefits under the other Party‚Äôs legislation under the same conditions as a citizen of that country**.

### Key Provisions on Benefit Coverage:

| Scenario | Rule |
|--------|------|
| **Employed person working in one country** | Subject only to the legislation of that country. |
| **Self-employed person working in the other country** | Subject only to the legislation of the country where they reside (first Party). |
| **Employee sent abroad by employer** | Subject only to the legislation of the country where the employer is based (first Party), even if working abroad. <br> **Limitation**: Coverage may not exceed **60 months** without mutual consent of both competent authorities. |
| **Crew members of ships** | <br> - If residing in Canada ‚Üí subject only to **Canada‚Äôs legislation**. <br> - If residing elsewhere ‚Üí subject only to **Macedonia‚Äôs legislation**. |

> **Exception**: Provisions of the **Vienna Conventions (Diplomatic & Consular Relations)** remain fully applicable.

> **Government employees posted abroad**: Subject only to the legislation of the country where they are posted.

> **Government employees working in the other country**: Subject only to the legislation of the country where they are posted.

> **Common agreement**: Competent authorities may jointly modify Articles 6‚Äì9 for specific persons or categories.

## üìÖ CREDITABLE PERIODS AND ELIGIBILITY CALCULATIONS

### 1. **Old Age Security (OAS) ‚Äì Canada**
> Creditable periods in Macedonia are recognized as periods of residence in Canada **only if**:
- The individual made **contributions to the Canada Pension Plan (CPP)** or a provincial comprehensive pension plan during that time (via employment or self-employment).
> Similarly, periods in Canada under Macedonia‚Äôs legislation are **not** counted as residence in Canada for OAS purposes **unless** the person contributed under Canada‚Äôs system.

### 2. **Canada Pension Plan (CPP) ‚Äì Creditable Periods**
> A calendar year in which the person had **at least 3 months** of creditable periods under Macedonia‚Äôs legislation is considered **one full creditable year** under CPP.

### 3. **Macedonian Pension System ‚Äì Eligibility**
> - A calendar year of CPP contributions is treated as **12 months** of creditable time under Macedonia‚Äôs system.
> - A month of OAS contributions (not part of a CPP creditable period) is treated as **one month** of creditable time under Macedonia‚Äôs system.
> - For disability or survivor pensions: Same rule ‚Äî 1 CPP year = 12 Macedonian months.

### 4. **Totalizing of Periods**
> If a person does not meet eligibility due to insufficient creditable periods, totalization applies:
- Totalize creditable periods from both Canada and Macedonia.
- Also include periods from **third countries** where both Parties have social security agreements allowing period totalization.

> **Exception**: If only Macedonia has an agreement with a third country, Macedonian citizens may totalize periods from that third country **unless otherwise specified** in that agreement.

> **Minimum Threshold Rule**:
> - If total creditable periods under a Party are **less than one year** and no right to a benefit exists under that Party‚Äôs law, **no benefit is payable** under this Agreement.

> **Partial Pension Calculation**:
> - For individuals eligible **only through totalization**, the amount of the OAS pension is calculated:
>   - **Earnings-related portion**: Based solely on CPP pensionable earnings.
>   - **Flat-rate portion**: Multiplied by a fraction representing the ratio of actual CPP contributions to the minimum qualifying period (but capped at 100%).

> **Macedonian Pension Calculation**:
> - The **theoretical pension** is calculated as if all periods had been accumulated under Macedonia‚Äôs system alone.
> - The **actual pension** is then determined by applying the ratio of Macedonian creditable periods to the total (Canada + Macedonia).

> **Minimum Pension Guarantee**:
> - The Macedonian law‚Äôs **minimum pension guarantee** continues to apply to persons residing outside Macedonia.

## üì£ ADMINISTRATIVE AND COOPERATION MECHANISMS

### 1. **Information Exchange**
- Competent authorities and institutions must **communicate necessary information** for benefit determination.
- They must **lend good offices and provide assistance** in assessing eligibility or benefit amounts ‚Äî as if the matter were under their own legislation.

> This assistance is **free of charge**, except where reimbursement is allowed under an administrative arrangement.

### 2. **Confidentiality**
- All information exchanged under the Agreement is **confidential**.
- May only be used for the **purpose of implementing the Agreement and relevant legislation**.
- Not to be disclosed to third parties or used for other purposes.

### 3. **Document Exemptions**
- Documents required for benefit applications are **exempt from authentication** by diplomatic or consular authorities.
