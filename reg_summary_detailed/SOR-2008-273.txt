Below is a **detailed, structured summary** of the **PCB Regulations** as published under the *Canadian Environmental Protection Act, 1999* (CEPA 1999), effective from November 4, 2006, and enacted by the Governor in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of the Environment and the Minister of Health.

## üîç **Comprehensive Summary of the PCB Regulations (Canada Gazette, Part I, November 4, 2006)**

### üìå **Background and Legislative Basis**
- The regulations were made under **section 93(1)** of the *Canadian Environmental Protection Act, 1999* (CEPA 1999), following a public consultation process.
- The **Minister of the Environment** published a proposed regulation in *Canada Gazette, Part I*, November 4, 2006, inviting public comments and objections.
- The **National Advisory Committee** was consulted under section 6c of CEPA 1999.
- The Governor in Council concluded that the proposed regulations:
  - Do **not conflict** with other federal or provincial environmental laws;
  - Provide **adequate protection** to human health and the environment;
  - Are consistent with the intent of CEPA 1999 to regulate toxic substances like polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs).

> ‚úÖ **Conclusion**: The regulations are legally valid and necessary to manage PCBs safely.

## üìè **Concentration-Based Thresholds**

The regulations are **concentration-dependent**, distinguishing between:
- **Liquids** (e.g., oils, capacitors)
- **Solids** (e.g., electrical equipment, insulation materials)

### üîπ Environmental Release Prohibitions
- **No release into the environment** (except from specific equipment) if:
  - In **liquid**: ‚â• **2 mg/kg** of PCBs
  - In **solid**: ‚â• **50 mg/kg** of PCBs

> ‚ùå This applies to **all uncontrolled releases** (e.g., spills, leaks, dumping).

### üîπ Maximum Quantities Released from Equipment
- No more than **1 gram** of PCBs may be released from equipment in use, or from equipment with an extension granted under section 17.

## ‚úÖ Exemptions and Permitted Uses

### 1. **Laboratory Analysis & Research**
- PCBs may be used for **laboratory analysis or research** if:
  - Conducted in an **authorized facility**; OR
  - In a facility that follows **internationally recognized best laboratory practices**, where local authorities lack authorization mechanisms.

> üîç Research must focus on **human health or environmental effects**.

---

### 2. **Fusion-Sealed Capacitors in Consumer Products**
- May be used if:
  - Part of a **consumer product**
  - **Fusion sealed** (cannot be opened without damaging the device)
  - Would become **inoperable and irreparable** if PCBs were removed.

> ‚úÖ This allows continued use in legacy electronics (e.g., older capacitors in appliances).

---

### 3. **Transportation Equipment (Aircraft, Ships, Trains)**
- May contain PCBs **only in communication, navigation, or electronic control systems or cables**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è PCBs are not allowed in other parts of vehicles.

---

### 4. **Incidental PCBs in Colouring Pigments**
- May be manufactured, exported, imported, offered for sale, or used **if concentration is less than 50 mg/kg**.
- **Annual average concentration** of PCBs in pigment must not exceed **25 mg/kg**.

> üìå This is a critical exemption for industrial pigments where PCBs are produced unintentionally.

---

### 5. **Processing for Destruction or Recovery**
- PCBs or products may be processed (e.g., destroyed or recovered) only in an **authorized facility** authorized for such purposes.

---

### 6. **Manufacture of Solid Products (‚â§50 mg/kg)**
- A person may manufacture solid products containing PCBs at **less than 50 mg/kg** using bulk materials also at **less than 50 mg/kg**.
- These products may be used.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Applies **only to products manufactured before September 5, 2008**.

## üìù Extension of Use (Special Cases)

### When Can an Extension Be Granted?
The Minister may grant an extension **up to December 31, 2014** under the following conditions:

#### ‚úÖ Condition 1: Equipment Replacement Not Feasible
- Equipment is being replaced with **engineered-to-order** equipment.
- It is **not technically feasible** to replace the equipment by **December 31, 2009**.

#### ‚úÖ Condition 2: Facility Closure
- Facility is scheduled for **permanent closure by December 31, 2014**.

### Required Elements in Application
An application must include:
1. **Applicant details** (name, address, contact info)
2. **Technical description** of equipment:
   - Type, function, quantity of liquid/solid
   - Concentration of PCBs (mg/kg)
   - Quantity of PCBs (kg)
   - Nameplate and serial number
   - Unique ID from required label (section 29)
3. **Facility location and function**
4. **Evidence** that:
   - Replacement is not feasible, OR
   - Facility is closing by Dec 31, 2014
5. **Plan to end use** (with timelines)
6. **Monthly inspection plan** for damage that could lead to PCB release
7. **Label compliance** (equipment must bear required label under section 29)

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Conditions for Denial or Revocation
- **Denial**: If the Minister has **reasonable grounds** to believe the application contains **false or misleading information**.
- **Revocation**:
  - If conditions no longer apply during the extension
  - If false/misleading information is discovered
- **Revocation must include**:
  - Written reasons
  - An opportunity for the applicant to respond in writing
