## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Investment Limits (Canadian Societies) Regulations**

### üìå **Legal Basis and Authority**
- These regulations were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**.
- They are based on specific sections of the *Insurance Companies Act*:
  - **Section 553a**: Relates to investment limits for Canadian societies.
  - **Section 564b**: Addresses capital and investment restrictions.
  - **Section 1021c**: Concerns the valuation of real property interests and related financial instruments.
- Statutory references:
  - S.C. 2001, c. 9, s. 437, 439, 465
  - S.C. 1991, c. 47

> **Purpose**: To establish clear rules governing how Canadian insurance societies (non-profit, mutual organizations) can invest in real property and related financial instruments, ensuring financial stability, risk control, and regulatory compliance.

## üìä Valuation Principles

### 1. **Book Value of Real Property Interests**
For sections 7‚Äì10 of the regulations:
- The **book value** of an asset (real property or security/loan) is calculated based on the balance sheet of the society prepared in accordance with the **Superintendent‚Äôs accounting principles** (as defined in subsection 331(4) of the ICA).
- **Real property**: Gross book value minus accumulated depreciation.
- **Securities or loans**: Book value as reported on the balance sheet.

> This ensures consistency in financial reporting and valuation across entities.

## üîê Investment Limits (Section 563, 565, 566)

### ‚úÖ **Section 563 ‚Äì Maximum Investment in Financial Institutions**
- A society may not invest more than **15% of its total assets** in:
  - Financial institutions, or
  - Subsidiaries of financial institutions (except where the subsidiary is a financial institution only because it is a subsidiary of the society).

> **Purpose**: Prevents over-concentration of risk in financial institutions, which could threaten the solvency of the society.

### ‚úÖ **Section 565 ‚Äì Maximum Investment in Participating Shares and Ownership Interests**
- A society may not hold more than **25% of the total assets** in participating shares or ownership interests in:
  - Financial institutions, or
  - Subsidiaries of financial institutions (same exclusion rule applies).

> **Note**: These limits apply to **prescribed subsidiaries** ‚Äî i.e., those that are not financial institutions or subsidiaries of financial institutions solely due to their status as society subsidiaries.

> **Valuation**: The value of such shares and interests is taken as their **book value** on the **consolidated balance sheet** of the society.

## üè° Interests in Real Property (Part XII of the ICA ‚Äì Sections 7 to 11)

This section defines what constitutes an "interest of a society in real property" and how those interests are valued. It expands beyond direct ownership to include debts, guarantees, and loans tied to real estate.

---

### ‚úÖ Types of Interests in Real Property

#### 1. **Direct Ownership or Debt for Acquisition/Improvement**
- Real property **beneficially owned** by the society or a designated entity.
- A **debt obligation** issued by the society or a designated entity to finance real property acquisition or improvement.

> **Valuation**:
> - For real property: Book value (gross value minus depreciation).
> - For debt: The amount by which the **book value of the debt exceeds** the book value of the real property.

---

#### 2. **Interests in Related Real Property Entities (Joint Ventures or Subsidiaries)**
- Real property owned by:
  - A **related real property entity** that is a **joint venture**, or
  - An entity where a designated entity has a substantial investment.
- Shares or ownership interests in such entities held by the society or a designated entity.

> **Valuation**:
> - Real property: Book value.
> - Shares/ownership interests: Book value of the shares or interests.

---

#### 3. **Debt Obligations, Loans, and Guarantees**
These include various types of financial instruments linked to real property:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Debt issued by a related real property entity** | Owned by society or designated entity. |
| **Loan made by society or designated entity** to a related real property entity or to a real property entity where a financial institution controlled by the society has a substantial investment. |
| **Guaranteed debt obligation** | Issued by a related real property entity, beneficially owned by a third party, but **guaranteed** by the society or a designated entity. |
| **Guaranteed loan** | Made by a third party to a related real property entity, **guaranteed** by the society or a designated entity. |

> **Valuation**:
> - For debt/loan: Book value.
> - For **guaranteed obligations**:
>   - If the entity owns real property that is an interest of the society:  
>     ‚Üí Amount by which **face value of guarantee exceeds** value of real property (per paragraph 8(2)(a)).
>   - Otherwise: Face value of guarantee.

---

#### 4. **Secured Loans or Debts**
A loan or debt is considered an interest in real property **if secured** by:
- Real property beneficially owned by a third party **in conjunction with**:
  - The society;
  - A designated entity;
  - A related real property entity;
  - A financial institution controlled by the society;
  - An entity controlled by a financial institution (subparagraph iv);
  - A real property entity described in 9(1)(c)(i) or (ii);
- Or shares/ownership interests in an entity that owns real property and is in conjunction with the society, a related entity, or a designated entity.

> **Valuation**:
> - For **real property security**:
>   - Lesser of:
>     1. Net realizable value of third party‚Äôs interest in the real property at time of security creation;  
>     2. A formula that includes:
>        - Book value of the loan (if made by society or owned by society);  
>        - Face value of guarantee (if guaranteed by society);  
>        - Minus total net realizable value of other security interests given for the same loan.
> - For **shares/ownership interests as security**:
>   - Lesser of:
>     1. Net realizable value of third party‚Äôs interest in shares at time of security;  
>     2. A formula involving:
>        - Book value or guarantee face value;  
>        - Net realizable value of entity‚Äôs interest in real property;  
>        - Value of shares given as security;  
>        - Total outstanding shares.
> - For **related real property entity shares**:
>   - Same formula, with adjustments for entity-level ownership.

---

#### 5. **Guarantees and Agreements for Development or Operating Costs**
- A **guarantee** given by society or designated entity to complete development of real property beneficially owned by:
  - The society;
  - A designated entity; or
  - A related real property entity.
- An **agreement** to support third-party operating or financing costs for real property owned by the above entities.

> **Valuation**:
> - Guarantee: Estimated cost of completing development.
> - Agreement: Amount of funds advanced by the society or designated entity under the agreement.

> These are treated as **interests in real property** for regulatory purposes, even without direct ownership.
