**Detailed Summary of the *North Pacific Marine Science Organization Privileges and Immunities Order* (S.C. 1991, c. 41)**

---

### **Overview**

The *North Pacific Marine Science Organization (PICES) Privileges and Immunities Order* is a Canadian regulatory instrument enacted under the authority of **section 5 of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act**. It grants specific **privileges and immunities** to the **North Pacific Marine Science Organization (PICES)** and its representatives, officials, and experts while operating in Canada. This Order is designed to facilitate the Organization’s scientific activities by ensuring it can function effectively and without undue interference from Canadian authorities.

The Order is based on a formal **Headquarters Agreement** between Canada and PICES, and it aligns PICES’ status and rights with international standards—particularly those established by the **United Nations Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations (the "Convention")** and the **Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations (the "Vienna Convention")**.

---

### **Legal Basis and Enactment**

- **Enacted by**: His Excellency the Governor General in Council  
- **On the recommendation of**:  
  - The Secretary of State for External Affairs  
  - The Minister of Finance  
- **Authority**: Section 5 of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*  
- **Citation**: *North Pacific Marine Science Organization Privileges and Immunities Order*  
- **Primary Purpose**: To recognize and protect the legal and operational status of PICES in Canada, enabling it to carry out its scientific mandate without disruption.

---

### **Key Definitions in the Order**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Agreement** | The Headquarters Agreement between Canada and PICES, signed on January 8, 1993 (CTS 1993/3), as amended by two Exchange of Notes: one in Ottawa (May 21, 1997) and one in Sidney (May 26, 1997) (CTS 1997/21). These amendments clarify the scope of cooperation and privileges. |
| **Convention** | The *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, which provides a framework for the rights of international organizations. |
| **Organization** | The North Pacific Marine Science Organization (PICES), a regional intergovernmental scientific body focused on marine research in the North Pacific. |
| **Vienna Convention** | The *Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations*, which governs the privileges and immunities of diplomatic missions and representatives. Included in Schedule I of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act. |

### **Core Provisions and Privileges**

#### **1. Legal Capacity of the Organization**
- PICES is granted **the legal capacity of a body corporate** in Canada.
- This means it can:
  - Enter into contracts
  - Own property
  - Sue and be sued
  - Conduct business operations in Canada
- This corporate status allows PICES to operate independently and formally within Canadian legal systems.

#### **2. Privileges and Immunities of PICES (Under Article II of the Convention)**
- To the extent specified in **Articles 2, 3, 4, and 5 of the Agreement**, PICES shall enjoy the **privileges and immunities** outlined in **Article II of the Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations**.
- These include:
  - **Immunity from legal process** (in certain cases)
  - **Exemption from taxation**
  - **Freedom from interference in administrative matters**
  - **Immunity for acts performed in the course of official duties**
- These rights are **limited and conditional**, meaning they apply only to actions directly related to the Organization’s scientific mandate and as defined in the Agreement.

#### **3. Privileges and Immunities for Member State Representatives**
- Representatives of **foreign member states** serving on the **Governing Council** of PICES shall have in Canada privileges and immunities **comparable to those granted to diplomatic representatives under the Vienna Convention**.
- This includes:
  - Immunity from arrest or detention
  - Protection from search or seizure
  - Exemption from certain taxes and customs duties
- These rights are **limited to their official duties** and are subject to the terms of **Article 6 of the Agreement**.

#### **4. Privileges and Immunities for the Chairman and Vice-Chairman**
- The **Chairman of the Governing Council** and the **Vice-Chairman when acting as Chairman** shall enjoy privileges and immunities **comparable to those of diplomatic representatives under the Convention**.
- This includes:
  - Immunity from legal proceedings
  - Protection of personal and official communications
  - Exemption from certain administrative and fiscal obligations
- These rights are specified under **Article 7 of the Agreement** and are intended to ensure the effective functioning of the Council’s leadership.

#### **5. Privileges and Immunities for Officials of the Organization**
- Officials of PICES (such as administrative staff, technical personnel, and management) shall have privileges and immunities as set out in:
  - **Paragraphs (1) to (3) of Article 8**
  - **Article 10 of the Agreement**
- These rights are derived from **Section 18 of Article V of the Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations**.
- Specifically, they include:
  - Immunity from legal proceedings for acts performed in the course of official duties
  - Protection of personal property and communications
  - Exemption from certain taxes and customs duties
- These rights are **not absolute** and are limited to official functions and responsibilities.

#### **6. Privileges and Immunities for Experts Performing Missions**
- Experts (e.g., scientists, researchers) engaged to perform missions for PICES shall have the privileges and immunities set out in **Article VI of the Convention**.
- These include:
  - Immunity from arrest or detention while carrying out official duties
  - Protection of personal and professional documents
  - Exemption from certain legal and fiscal obligations
- These rights are granted **only to the extent specified in Article 11 of the Agreement**, ensuring that the benefits are tied directly to their scientific mission and not extended broadly.

### **Key Principles and Implications**

1. **International Legal Framework**  
   The Order draws heavily on international law—specifically the UN and Vienna Conventions—to ensure consistency with global standards for international organizations. This enhances PICES’ legitimacy and operational efficiency in Canada.

2. **Limited Scope of Immunities**  
   Privileges and immunities are **not unlimited**. They are strictly tied to:
   - The Organization’s official functions
   - The specific provisions of the Agreement
   - The nature of the individual’s role (e.g., official, expert, representative)

3. **Non-Diplomatic Nature**  
   PICES is **not a diplomatic mission**, but a scientific intergovernmental organization. Therefore, its privileges are **not equivalent to those of a diplomatic mission**, though they are structured to mirror them in key respects—particularly for leadership and experts.

4. **Operational Benefits**  
   By granting these rights, Canada enables:
   - Free movement of scientists and officials
   - Freedom to conduct research and data collection
   - Protection of sensitive scientific information
   - Smooth collaboration between Canadian institutions and international partners

5. **Compliance and Oversight**  
   While the Order grants immunities, it does not remove accountability. Canadian authorities retain the right to:
   - Monitor compliance with laws
   - Require reporting or audits
   - Enforce rules where activities are not in the public interest

---

### **Conclusion**

The *North Pacific Marine Science Organization Privileges and Immunities Order* establishes a clear, legally binding framework for the operation of PICES in Canada. It recognizes the Organization’s unique role in advancing marine science in the North Pacific region and ensures that its members, officials, and experts can operate with the necessary protections and legal autonomy to fulfill their scientific mandates.

By aligning with international standards under the UN and Vienna Conventions, the Order promotes:
- Scientific cooperation
- Cross-border research collaboration
- Mutual respect between Canada and its international partners

Importantly, the privileges are **conditional, limited, and purpose-specific**, ensuring that Canada maintains its sovereignty and legal integrity while supporting a vital scientific initiative.

---

**Final Note**: This Order is a key example of how Canada balances international obligations with domestic legal principles in the context of scientific and environmental cooperation. It reflects a pragmatic approach to facilitating international collaboration without compromising national laws or public interest.
