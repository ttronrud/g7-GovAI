### ðŸ“Œ **Overview and Legal Basis**
These regulations establish the conditions, procedures, and limits under which a refund of customs duties may be granted in Canada. They are derived from **paragraph 74(1)(a) to (g)** of the *Customs Act* and are administered by the Minister of National Revenue on the recommendation of the Governor in Council.

- **Legal Authority**: The regulations are made under:
  - Sections 74c to 76, 78d, and 81 of the *Customs Act*
  - Paragraph 164(1)(i)e of the *Customs Act*
- **Exemption from Publication Requirement**: The regulations are not required to be published under subsection 164(3) of the *Customs Act* because they give effect to a public announcement made on **October 27, 1997** (as per paragraph 164(4)(a.2)a), meaning they are considered "procedural" and not new policy.

### 1. **Damage, Deterioration, or Destruction (Section 74(1)(a))**
- **Perishable goods**: Written notice must be submitted within **3 days** of release.
- **Non-perishable goods**: Written notice must be submitted within **4 years** of release.

### 3. **Inferior Quality (Section 74(1)(c))**
- **Perishable goods**: Within **3 days** of release.

- Other preferential claims (e.g., CIFTA, CCRFTA, etc.): Generally within **4 years** of accounting.

---

### 2. **Short Shipment (Section 74(1)(b))**
- Refund = **Difference between**:
  - Duties paid
  - Duties payable on the **actual quantity released**

> This ensures the importer is not penalized for a shortfall in quantity.

---

### 3. **Inferior Quality (Section 74(1)(c))**
- Refund = **Difference between**:
  - Duties paid
  - Duties payable on the **value of the inferior-quality goods**

> Applies only if the quality defect is not due to expiration or rust.

---

### 4. **Preferential Tariff Claims (Sections 74(1)(c.1), (c.11))**
- Refund = **Difference between**:
  - Duties paid
  - Duties payable under **preferential tariff treatment** (e.g., NAFTA, CIFTA, CCRFTA, etc.)

> Example: If goods were eligible for 0% duty under NAFTA but paid 10%, the refund equals the 10% overpayment.

---
