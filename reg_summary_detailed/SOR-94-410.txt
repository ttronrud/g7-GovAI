> **Export Development Canada Exercise of Certain Powers Regulations**  
> (S.C. 1993, c. 26, s. 4(2); amended and updated as of SOR/2008-46)

This regulation grants specific powers to **Export Development Canada (EDC)**‚Äîa Canadian government-owned corporation established under the *Export Development Act*‚Äîto engage in certain financial, leasing, and equity-related activities, subject to ministerial oversight and conditions designed to ensure alignment with Canada‚Äôs export objectives and financial stability.

---

## üîç OVERVIEW

### **Purpose of the Regulation**
The regulation empowers Export Development Canada (EDC) to:
- Dispose of certain property by lease,
- Acquire equity interests in entities,
- Enter into domestic financial and insurance transactions,

**But only under strict conditions and with prior approval from both the Minister of International Trade and the Minister of Finance.**

These powers are exercised to support Canadian businesses engaged in international trade, while preventing EDC from engaging in activities that could undermine financial stability, create conflicts of interest, or serve non-export-related purposes.

### 2. **Definitions Used in the Regulation**

| Term | Definition |

| **Domestic insurance transaction** | An insurance, reinsurance, indemnity, or guarantee provided by EDC for a transaction not related to international business. |
| **Equity interest** | Ownership stake in an entity. |

### 3. **Power to Dispose of Property by Lease**

#### ‚úÖ Conditions for Leasing Property
EDC may lease property **only if**:
- The property is **not used outside Canada** during the lease term.
- The lease is **not entered for the primary purpose of extending credit**.
- The **Corporation is responsible** for installing, promoting, servicing, cleaning, maintaining, and repairing the property during the lease.
- EDC **does not intend to dispose of its interest** in the property during or at the end of the lease.
- The **capital cost and financing charges** will **not be recovered** by EDC.
- **No contractual arrangement exists** for the final disposal of EDC‚Äôs interest in the property, **and** the **estimated residual value** of the leased property (plus all other similarly disposed properties) is **‚â§ 20%** of the **total cost of acquisition** of all such properties.

#### üö´ Approval Requirement
- Requires **prior approval** from both the **Minister of International Trade** and the **Minister of Finance**.
- This ensures that leasing is not used as a disguised form of financing or asset management.

### 4. **Power to Acquire Equity Interests in Entities**

#### ‚úÖ General Authority
EDC may acquire equity interests in entities ‚Äî but only under specific conditions.

#### üö´ Approval Requirement (Subsection 5(2))
An acquisition requires ministerial approval **if**:
- The **value of the equity interest being acquired** (plus any equity interest EDC already holds) **exceeds 25%** of the **total value of all equity interests** in the entity at the time of acquisition.

> This threshold prevents EDC from becoming a dominant shareholder in any entity.

#### üìå How Total Equity Value is Calculated
For purposes of the 25% threshold:
- Value is calculated on a **fully diluted basis** (includes all potential shares, including those issued to future investors or held by obligations).
- Includes:
  - The equity interest being acquired.
  - Any equity interest that EDC or others are **obligated to acquire**.
- Determined using:
  1. Most recent **audited financial statements** of the entity (if available),
  2. Agreements between the entity and EDC regarding acquisition,
  3. Agreements among investors (including EDC) regarding equity purchases.

#### üö´ Exceptions Where Approval Is Not Required
Approval is **not required** in the following cases:
- Acquisitions resulting from **restructurings, reorganizations, or workout arrangements** involving entities where EDC already holds an equity interest or is a creditor.
- Acquisitions due to:
  - Dividends or in-kind distributions,
  - Exchange or substitution of equity interests as a result of **mergers, amalgamations, or similar actions**,
  - Settlement of debts arising from:
    - Credit extensions (e.g., loans),
    - Insurance, indemnity, or guarantee arrangements.

> These exceptions ensure flexibility during corporate restructuring and debt settlement.

### 5. **Power to Enter into Domestic Financial Transactions**

#### ‚úÖ General Authority
EDC may enter into domestic financial transactions (e.g., loans, lines of credit) for non-export purposes.

#### üö´ Approval Requirement (Subsection 5(2))
Approval is required **if**:
- The person seeking credit has **annual export transaction and foreign market business volume < 50%** of their **total annual business volume**.

> This ensures that EDC does not lend to businesses that are primarily domestic and not export-oriented.

#### üö´ Exception: Prior Approval
- Approval is **not required** if the Ministers previously approved a similar domestic financial transaction with the same person **within the last 48 months**.

> This allows continuity of credit relationships with trusted, stable clients.

---

### 6. **Power to Enter into Domestic Insurance Transactions**

#### ‚úÖ General Authority
EDC may provide insurance, reinsurance, indemnity, or guarantees to persons for non-export activities.

#### üö´ Approval Requirement (Subsection 6(2) & (3))
Approval is required **if**:
- The person receiving insurance has **annual export transaction and foreign market business volume < 50%** of their **total annual business volume**.

> Same threshold as financial transactions ‚Äî focused on export relevance.

#### üö´ Exception: Prior Approval
- Approval is **not required** if a similar domestic insurance transaction was previously approved with the same person within the last **48 months**.

---

### 7. **Special Provisions on Insurance Transactions**

- A domestic insurance transaction requires ministerial approval **if** it involves:
  - A class of insurance defined in **column II of the schedule** to these Regulations (specific types of insurance, e.g., property, liability, or specialized risks).
- This ensures that high-risk or complex insurance products are subject to greater scrutiny.

---

### 8. **Temporary Exemption (Repeal and Continuity Clauses)**

> These provisions address a transitional period tied to amendments in the *Export Development Act*.

#### üìå Provision:
- **Subsections 5(2), 6(2), and (3)** (i.e., the approval requirements for financial and insurance transactions) **do not apply** during the period:
  - From the day **paragraph 10(1)(a)** of the Act (as amended by subsection 260(1)) comes into force,
  - Until that paragraph is repealed.

> This was likely a transitional measure to allow for legislative changes without disrupting existing operations.

#### üìå Important Exception:
- Despite the repeal of the above provisions, **new transactions** entered into by EDC **during the period** when paragraph 10(1)(a) was in force **are still subject to the rules**.
- Even after repeal, EDC may take **any necessary steps** to implement or finalize such transactions.

> This ensures **continuity and enforceability** of transactions initiated during the transitional period.

## üîç Key Objectives and Policy Rationale

| Objective | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Promote Export-Oriented Business** | EDC‚Äôs activities are strictly limited to supporting businesses involved in international trade. |
| **Prevent Financial Risk** | Restrictions on leasing, equity acquisition, and lending prevent EDC from becoming overly exposed to non-export or speculative risks. |
| **Ensure Transparency and Oversight** | Mandatory ministerial approval ensures accountability and alignment with government policy. |
| **Protect Financial Stability** | By limiting credit and insurance to businesses with significant foreign market exposure, EDC avoids supporting domestic-only or low-risk ventures. |
| **Enable Flexibility in Restructuring** | Exceptions for reorganizations and debt settlements allow EDC to support distressed or evolving businesses. |
