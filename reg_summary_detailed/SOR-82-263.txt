**Detailed Summary of the National Historic Parks General Regulations**

---

### **Overview**

The *National Historic Parks General Regulations* are a set of administrative rules issued by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of the Environment, under the authority of two key pieces of legislation:

- **Section 7 of the National Parks Act**  
- **Section 3 of the National Historic Parks Order (C.R.C., c. 1112)**

These regulations establish comprehensive rules governing the **control, management, and protection** of Canada’s National Historic Parks. The primary objective is to preserve the **natural, cultural, historical, and scientific values** of these protected areas while allowing for **controlled public use and management activities**.

The regulations are formally known as the *National Historic Parks General Regulations* and apply to all areas designated as National Historic Parks under Part II of the National Parks Act.

---

## **Key Definitions**

Before proceeding to the substantive provisions, the regulations define key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Archaeological site** | Any site or area containing historical resources buried, partially buried, on land, or submerged in water. |
| **Director** | The Director of a Region of Parks Canada (Department of the Environment). |
| **Diver’s marker** | A floating device with a red square flag and a white diagonal stripe, indicating a dive site. |
| **Explosive** | Any substance or device (excluding firearms) that produces an explosion or pyrotechnic effect. |
| **Fauna** | All living vertebrates and invertebrates, including eggs and young, but *excluding* fossil remains. |
| **Fireplace** | A grill, stove, or similar device designed for starting or maintaining a fire. |
| **Flora** | Any plant matter (living or dead), including fungi and molds, but *excluding* fossil remains. |
| **Historical object** | Movable historical resources such as artifacts, documents, artworks, or reproductions. |
| **Historical resource** | Any natural or man-made feature of palaeontological, prehistorical, historical, cultural, natural, scientific, or aesthetic value (e.g., sites, structures, remains). |
| **Kindle** | To start or attempt to start a fire. |
| **Maintain** | To add fuel to a fire to keep it burning. |
| **Minister** | The Minister of the Environment. |
| **Moor** | To tie up or dock a watercraft to a wharf, another vessel, or to use a space at a wharf (including docking, anchoring, storing). |
| **Natural object** | Natural materials (soil, rock, mineral, sand) or phenomena not included in flora or fauna; excludes fossil remains. |
| **Park** | Any area set apart as a National Historic Park. |
| **Superintendent** | The on-the-ground manager of a specific park. |
| **Vessel** | Any boat, canoe, raft, amphibious vehicle, or other watercraft (excluding seaplanes). |
| **Wharf** | Any structure (dock, pier, jetty, quay, etc.) used to moor watercraft. |

## **Core Prohibitions and Protective Measures**

### **1. Protection of Archaeological Sites and Historical Resources**
- **Prohibition**: No person may disturb, remove, deface, damage, or destroy any archaeological site or historical resource in a Park.
- **Exception**: A **Director** may issue a permit for removal of historical resources for:
  - Park management
  - Scientific research
  - Public display

> This recognizes the importance of preserving heritage while allowing for responsible research and display.

---

### **2. Protection of Flora, Fauna, and Natural Objects**
- **Prohibition**: No person may remove, deface, damage, or destroy flora, fauna, or natural objects in a Park.
- **Exception**: A **Superintendent** may issue a permit for such actions if:
  - Required for park management
  - For scientific purposes
- **Permit Conditions**: Must specify:
  - Type and amount of items to be removed
  - Exact location
  - Any additional conditions (e.g., timing, method)

> Ensures that even necessary removals are strictly controlled and documented.

---

### **3. Protection of Public Structures and Signage**
- **Prohibition**: No person may damage, deface, or destroy:
  - Public buildings
  - Fences
  - Signs, notices, or signage placed in the Park

> Maintains the integrity of infrastructure and visitor information systems.

---

### **4. Waste Management and Pollution**
- **Prohibition**: No person may deposit snow, leaves, rubbish, or other matter in the Park **except** in designated places, times, and conditions set by the Superintendent.
- **Prohibition**: No person may pollute any watercourse in the Park.

> Prevents environmental degradation and maintains water quality.

---

### **5. Watercourse Use and Management**
- **General Rule**: No obstruction, diversion, or interference with watercourses is permitted (subject to sections 9–11).
- **Emergency Exceptions**:
  - **Superintendent** may permit water extraction during drought, fire, or contamination emergencies.
  - **Director** may issue permits (up to 10 years) for domestic or business water use by individuals.
  - **Minister** may enter into agreements with:
    - Municipalities or water districts adjacent to the Park
    - Residents or tourism operators on adjacent land
    for water supply (domestic or tourism-related)

> Balances ecological protection with practical needs during emergencies and for local communities.

---

### **6. Designation of Restricted Areas and Activities**
- **Power to Restrict**: The Superintendent may designate any activity or area as "restricted" if necessary for:
  - Preservation
  - Control
  - Management of the Park

- **Public Notification**: Must post clear notices at all information offices and entrances describing:
  - The restricted activity
  - Maps showing the affected area

- **Permitted Exceptions**:
  - A person may apply to the Superintendent to engage in a restricted activity **on specific terms and conditions**.
  - The Superintendent may **suspend or cancel** such authorizations if needed for park safety or management.

- **General Rule**: No one may enter or engage in a restricted activity **without prior authorization**.

> Provides flexibility for managing sensitive zones (e.g., near archaeological sites or fragile ecosystems).

---

### **7. Designated Activities and Authorizations**
- **Definition**:
  - A *designated activity* includes travel, entry into restricted areas, or use of park facilities.
  - Activities not mentioned in Section 12 (e.g., camping, hiking, events) may be restricted to authorized holders.

- **Authorization Requirements**:
  - The Superintendent must issue an authorization only if the activity will **not result in**:
    1. Significant harm to natural or cultural resources
    2. Harm to public safety or health
    3. Significant reduction in public enjoyment
    4. Harm to park preservation or management

- **Conditions**:
  - Authorizations include necessary terms and conditions for safety and conservation.

- **Enforcement**:
  - Only authorized individuals may carry out designated activities.
  - Holders must produce authorizations upon request.
  - The Superintendent may:
    - Suspend authorizations for non-compliance
    - Reinstate them if compliance is restored
    - Revoke them if the holder is convicted of a violation

> A structured, risk-based system to regulate public access and activities.

---

### **8. Camping Regulations**
- **General Prohibition**: No one may:
  - Use, occupy, reside, or camp on park land
  - Erect structures
  - Park vehicles for camping
  *unless authorized*

- **Exceptions**:
  - Use of **public picnic grounds** for meal preparation and consumption is allowed.

- **Camping Permits**:
  - The Superintendent may issue permits for:
    - Specific areas
    - Defined periods
    - Specific conditions
  - Permits are **non-transferable** and valid only during the specified period.

- **Cancellation Conditions**:
  - Permits may be canceled if:
    - Area is unoccupied for over 24 hours
    - Permit holder fails to maintain the area
    - False or misleading information was provided
    - Violation of permit conditions or directives

- **Cancellation Notice**:
  - Must be mailed or posted at the site.
  - Cancellation takes effect upon notice.

- **Removal Obligation**:
  - Permit holder must remove all vehicles, trailers, or structures before permit expires or is canceled.

- **Superintendent Authority**:
  - May remove unauthorized vehicles or structures.

> Ensures responsible and temporary use of park land, preventing long-term encroachment.

---

### **9. Fire Regulations**
- **Prohibited Fires**:
  - No kindling or maintenance of fire except:
    - In a **fireplace provided by the Superintendent**
    - In a **portable stove** burning fuel other than wood or charcoal
    - In a **charcoal or gas-fired barbecue**

- **Exception**:
  - Superintendent may issue a written authorization for fire use under specific conditions.

- **Additional Prohibitions**:
  - Fires must not spread beyond designated equipment
  - Lighted cigarettes, matches, or burning substances must be discarded in designated receptacles
  - No use of equipment that could ignite a fire without precautions
  - No accumulation or improper handling of brush or inflammable materials
  - No leaving fires unattended

> Prevents wildfires and protects sensitive ecosystems.
