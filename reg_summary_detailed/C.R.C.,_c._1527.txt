## üìù **Detailed Summary of the Repealed Territorial Lands Regulations (Prior to SOR/2016-131)**

### üîç **Overview**
This regulation governs the **quarrying, removal, and use of natural materials** (such as carving stone, loam, sand, gravel, and stone) from **territorial lands** under the control of the **Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development** (now the Minister of Indigenous Services Canada). It establishes procedures for **staking, applying for leases or permits**, and sets limits on the quantity and use of materials that can be taken.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important Note**: This regulation has been **repealed** by **SOR/2016-131, s. 2 and s. 3**, and many of its provisions are no longer in force. The current framework for material extraction in the Northwest Territories and Nunavut is governed by updated laws, including the *Territorial Lands Act*, *Nunavut Land Claims Agreement*, and other modernized regulations.

## üìê Staking Procedures (for Lease Applications)

A person seeking to lease land for material extraction must **"stake"** the area according to strict rules:

### 1. **Area Size Limits**
- **Loam**: Maximum of **8.1 hectares**.
- **All other materials** (e.g., sand, gravel, stone): Maximum of **64.8 hectares**.

### 2. **Shape Requirements**
- The staked area must be **rectangular**.
- Exception: If a boundary of a previously staked tract is shared, it may be used as a common boundary.

### 3. **Marking Requirements**
- **Posts**:
  - Must be at least **10 cm square** and **1.2 m above ground** when firmly planted.
  - Must bear markings showing:
    - Post number
    - Applicant‚Äôs name
    - Date of staking
    - Type of material to be removed
- **In timbered areas**, lines between posts must be clearly marked.

### 4. **Alternative Marking (No Timber)**
- If no timber exists, **rock cairns** may be used instead:
  - Must be at least **60 cm high** and **60 cm in diameter at base**.
  - Must include a **metal container** with a document containing:
    - Cairn number
    - Applicant‚Äôs name
    - Date of staking
    - Type of material to be removed

### 5. **Notice Requirement**
- The applicant must post a **written notice** on a post or cairn stating their intention to apply for a lease within the time prescribed by the regulations.

### 6. **Priority Rules**
- If **two or more people apply for the same area**, the **first to stake** the land in compliance with these rules has **priority** for lease approval.

## üìÑ Application for Lease

- Must be filed with the **territorial land agent** within **30 days** of staking.
- Application must be in **duplicate** and include:
  1. A **metes and bounds description** of the land.
  2. The **type of material** desired to be removed.
  3. A **sketch** showing:
     - Position of the parcel relative to a survey monument, topographical feature, or known point.
     - Copies of the markings on posts or cairns.
  4. An **affidavit** stating that:
     - The applicant has complied with all regulations.
     - The land contains the material in **merchantable quantities**.

## üï∞Ô∏è Lease Term and Obligations

- **Maximum lease term**: **10 years**.
- Lessee must:
  - Begin removal of material **within a time frame set by the Minister**.
  - Continue removal in a manner **satisfactory to the Minister**.
- Renewal:
  - The Minister may **renew** the lease for **up to 10 additional years**, if the lessee has complied with the lease and regulations.

## üö™ Personal Take (No Permit Required)

Any resident of the **Northwest Territories or Nunavut** may take material **without charge or permit** in a calendar year, provided:

- **No surface rights** have been licensed, leased, or otherwise disposed of by the Crown.
- The material is taken **for personal use only**, not for barter or sale.

### Quantity Limits:
- **Loam**: Up to **10 cubic metres** per year.
- **Sand, gravel, or stone**: Up to **40 cubic metres** per year.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è This exemption **does not apply** if:
> - Surface rights are already held by a third party (e.g., oil and gas lease).
> - The material is being taken for sale or barter.

## üìÑ Permit System (Alternative to Lease)

Any person may apply for a **permit** to take material from territorial lands, provided:

- The application is in the form prescribed by the Minister.
- It is accompanied by:
  - The **application fee** (as set in Schedule I).
  - **Royalties** (as set in Schedule II), unless exempt.

### Exemptions from Fees and Royalties:
The following **do not pay application fees or royalties**:
- Government departments of Canada
- Commissioners of the Northwest Territories or Nunavut
- Municipal districts in NT or Nunavut
- Educational, religious, charitable institutions, or hospitals

### Permit Duration
- Issued for a period based on estimated start and end dates.
- **Maximum duration**: **3 years**.
- **Expiration**: The permit expires on the day the **stated quantity of material** is fully quarried and removed.

### Key Prohibitions
- A permit **cannot be assigned or transferred** to another person.
- If a permittee fails to comply with regulations or permit conditions, the **Minister may cancel the permit**.
