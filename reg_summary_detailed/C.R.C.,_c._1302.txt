**Detailed Summary of the Minister Designation Order (Precious Metals Marking Act)**

**Title:**  
Minister Designation Order (Precious Metals Marking Act)

**Purpose of the Order:**  
This regulatory instrument serves to formally designate a specific federal minister as the official authority responsible for administering and enforcing the *Precious Metals Marking Act*. It ensures clarity and accountability in the implementation of the Act by clearly identifying the responsible government official.

**Key Provisions:**

1. **Designation of the Minister:**  
   The Minister of Consumer and Corporate Affairs is officially designated as the "Minister" for all purposes under the *Precious Metals Marking Act*. This designation is made pursuant to the authority granted by the Act itself, which typically empowers the government to appoint or designate a minister to oversee regulatory functions.

2. **Legal Authority and Scope:**  
   The designation grants the Minister of Consumer and Corporate Affairs the legal authority to:
   - Issue regulations, directives, and guidelines related to the marking of precious metals.
   - Enforce compliance with the requirements of the Act.
   - Receive and process reports, applications, and complaints concerning the marking of precious metals.
   - Conduct inspections or investigations into the handling and labeling of precious metal products.
   - Take enforcement actions against non-compliant entities or individuals.

3. **Applicability and Jurisdiction:**  
   This Order applies throughout Canada and is binding on all persons, businesses, and organizations involved in the manufacture, import, export, sale, or distribution of precious metals (such as gold, silver, platinum, and palladium) that are subject to the marking requirements under the *Precious Metals Marking Act*.

4. **Legal Citation:**  
   The Order may be cited as the *Minister Designation Order (Precious Metals Marking Act)*. This formal citation ensures consistency in legal references and facilitates the use of the Order in court, regulatory filings, and official communications.

5. **Legal Basis:**  
   The Order is issued under the authority of the *Precious Metals Marking Act*, which establishes the framework for ensuring that precious metals are properly marked with information such as the metal type, purity, weight, and manufacturer details. The designation of a minister is a necessary administrative step to operationalize the Act and ensure effective oversight.

6. **Effectiveness and Duration:**  
   The Order is effective immediately upon publication and remains in force until amended, revoked, or superseded by a subsequent order or legislation. It is typically subject to review and may be revised as needed to reflect changes in regulatory needs or policy objectives.

**Significance:**  
This Order is a foundational administrative step in the regulatory framework governing precious metals. By clearly assigning responsibility to the Minister of Consumer and Corporate Affairs, it provides legal certainty and ensures that regulatory functions are carried out by a designated, accountable official. This enhances transparency, strengthens consumer protection, and supports market integrity by ensuring that precious metals are accurately and consistently marked.

**Conclusion:**  
The *Minister Designation Order (Precious Metals Marking Act)* is a concise but essential regulatory instrument that establishes the Minister of Consumer and Corporate Affairs as the official responsible for administering the marking requirements of precious metals in Canada. It ensures that the Act is implemented effectively, consistently, and in accordance with federal regulatory standards.
