**Detailed Summary of the Akwesasne Residents Remission Order**

---

### **Overview**
The *Akwesasne Residents Remission Order* is a regulatory instrument issued under **section 101 of the Customs Tariff** by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the **Minister of National Revenue** and the **Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development**. This Order provides for the **remission (i.e., cancellation or exemption) of customs duties** on certain goods imported into Canada from the United States by specific individuals and entities associated with the **Akwesasne community**.

The Order recognizes the unique socio-economic and cultural circumstances of the Akwesasne people and aims to facilitate access to essential goods for residents while respecting Canadian customs and revenue laws.

---

### **Official Citation**
- **Title**: *Akwesasne Residents Remission Order*  
- **Reference**: R.S., c. 41 (3rd Supp.)

---

### **Key Definitions in the Order**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Akwesasne Reserve** | Comprises two reserves: <br> - St. Regis Reserve No. 15 (Quebec) <br> - Cornwall Island Reserve No. 59 (Ontario) |
| **Akwesasne Resident** | Any individual who has established permanent residence on the Akwesasne Reserve |
| **Authorized Identification** | An identification document issued by the **Mohawk Council of Akwesasne** to an Akwesasne resident |
| **Cornwall Customs Office** | The official customs office located at the highway border crossing on Cornwall Island, Ontario |
| **Duly Authorized Community Store** | A store on the Akwesasne Reserve primarily serving Akwesasne residents, authorized by the Mohawk Council of Akwesasne in a manner acceptable to both the Minister of National Revenue and the Council |
| **Duties** | As defined in section 66 of the Customs Tariff — generally refers to import duties, taxes, and fees |
| **Personal Use** | Any use of goods that is not income-generating; includes household, medical, educational, or personal consumption purposes |

---

### **Scope of Application**
- Applies to goods **acquired in the United States** and **imported into Canada** on or after the date the Order comes into force.
- Covers goods specifically listed in the **Schedule** (see below), with **exclusions** for certain categories.

---

### **Remission Provisions (by Category)**

#### **1. Remission for Akwesasne Residents (Section 4)**
- **Applies to**: Goods imported for **personal use** by an Akwesasne resident.
- **Conditions for Remission**:
  1. Goods must be **reported under section 12 of the Customs Act** and **accounted for under section 32** at the **Cornwall customs office**.
  2. The resident must present **authorized identification** to the customs officer.
  3. Goods must be for the **personal use** of the resident or another Akwesasne resident.
  4. Goods **cannot be sold or transferred** within **12 months** of importation — except to another Akwesasne resident for personal use.
  5. If duties were already paid, a **claim for remission must be submitted to the Minister of National Revenue within two years** of accounting under section 32.

> ✅ This provision supports individual access to essential goods without financial burden.

---

#### **2. Remission for Community Stores (Section 5)**
- **Applies to**: Goods listed in the **Schedule** imported by:
  - The **owner or operator** of a **duly authorized community store**, or
  - A person acting on behalf of such owner/operator
- **Purpose**: To sell goods to Akwesasne residents for their **personal use**.
- **Conditions for Remission**:
  1. Goods must be **reported and accounted for** at the Cornwall customs office.
  2. The store must present **evidence of authorization** to operate the store (approved by both the Minister of National Revenue and the Mohawk Council).
  3. Goods must be sold **only to Akwesasne residents** for **personal use**.
  4. A **claim for remission** must be submitted to the Minister of National Revenue within **two years** of accounting under section 32.

> ✅ Encourages community-based retail to serve residents, reducing cost of living through local access to essential items.

---

#### **3. Remission for Mohawk Council or Authorized Entities (Section 6)**
- **Applies to**: Goods imported by:
  - The **Mohawk Council of Akwesasne**, or
  - An entity **authorized by the Council**
- **Purpose**: For use in **educational, hospital, or social services** provided to Akwesasne residents.
- **Conditions for Remission**:
  1. Goods must be **reported and accounted for** at the Cornwall customs office.
  2. If imported by an authorized entity, **evidence of authorization** must be presented.
  3. Goods **cannot be sold or transferred** within **12 months** of importation — except to an Akwesasne resident for personal use.
  4. A **claim for remission** must be submitted to the Minister of National Revenue within **two years** of accounting under section 32.

> ✅ Supports vital community services (healthcare, education) by reducing the cost of importing necessary supplies.

### **Schedule of Exempt Goods**
The following goods are eligible for remission under the Order (excluding alcoholic beverages, cigars, cigarettes, and manufactured tobacco):

| Category | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| **Bedding** | Mattresses, sheets, blankets |
| **Books, Magazines & Newspapers** | Educational, recreational, or informational materials |
| **Clothing & Footwear** | Apparel, shoes, winter wear |
| **Cosmetics** | Personal hygiene and beauty products |
| **Diapers & Baby Products** | Infant care supplies |
| **First Aid Products** | Bandages, antiseptics, pain relievers |
| **Foodstuff & Edible Products** | Non-perishable foods, snacks, grains |
| **Household Cleaning Products** | Detergents, soaps, cleaners |
| **Hygiene Products** | Toiletries, sanitary products |
| **Indian Craft Articles** | Baskets, beaded work, traditional art |
| **Iron Workers’ Tools** | Tool belts, hand tools (e.g., hammers, wrenches) |
| **Light Bulbs** | Standard household lighting |
| **Light Hardware** | Nails, hammers, paint brushes, fasteners |
| **Personal Needs Products** | Items for daily personal care |
| **Sporting Goods** | Hockey sticks, fishing gear, protective sports equipment |
| **Art Supplies & Stationery** | Writing paper, pens, drawing tools |
| **Waste Disposal Bags** | Trash bags, compost bags |

> ⚠️ **Exclusions**: Alcoholic beverages, cigars, cigarettes, and manufactured tobacco are **not eligible** for remission under this Order.

### **Key Provisions and Implications**

| Feature | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| **Border Point Specificity** | All transactions must occur at the **Cornwall customs office** — ensuring compliance and oversight. |
| **Accounting Requirement** | Goods must be formally reported and accounted for under the Customs Act — maintains transparency and auditability. |
| **12-Month Non-Transfer Rule** | Prevents resale or commercialization of exempt goods, protecting the personal-use intent. |
| **Two-Year Claim Window** | Provides a reasonable timeframe for residents and entities to apply for remission after importation. |
| **Authorization Requirements** | Ensures legitimacy of community stores and entities — prevents misuse or unauthorized imports. |

---

### **Policy Objectives and Rationale**

1. **Support for Indigenous Communities**  
   The Order acknowledges the economic challenges faced by remote Indigenous communities and provides a practical mechanism to reduce import costs for essential goods.

2. **Preservation of Cultural and Traditional Goods**  
   Exemption of **Indian craft articles** helps sustain traditional arts and supports cultural preservation.

3. **Promotion of Community-Based Economies**  
   By allowing community stores to import goods for residents, the Order strengthens local economies and self-reliance.

4. **Access to Essential Services**  
   Enables hospitals, schools, and social programs to operate efficiently without bearing the full cost of imported supplies.

5. **Compliance with Canadian Customs Framework**  
   The Order operates within the legal structure of the Customs Act and Tariff, ensuring that remission does not undermine revenue collection — instead, it applies only to clearly defined, non-commercial uses.

---

### **Limitations and Exceptions**

- **No remission applies to**:
  - Alcoholic beverages
  - Cigars, cigarettes, or manufactured tobacco
- **No remission applies to income-producing uses** — only **personal use** is eligible.
- **No remission applies to resale or transfer** within 12 months (unless to another Akwesasne resident for personal use).
- **All transactions must be reported and accounted for** at the Cornwall customs office — failure to do so may result in non-compliance or denial of remission.
