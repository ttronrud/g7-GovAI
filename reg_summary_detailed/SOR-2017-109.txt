## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Environmental Violations Administrative Monetary Penalties Regulations**

### **1. Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**
- These regulations were issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the **recommendation of the Minister of the Environment**, under **Section 5a of the Environmental Violations Administrative Monetary Penalties Act (EVAMPA)**.
- The regulations are rooted in the **Environmental Violations Administrative Monetary Penalties Act (EVAMPA)**, which provides a legal framework for imposing administrative monetary penalties (AMPs) for environmental violations.
- **Statutory references**:
  - S.C. 2012, c. 19, s. 54
  - S.C. 2009, c. 14, s. 126

> üîç *Purpose*: To establish clear, enforceable rules for identifying, classifying, and penalizing environmental violations through a transparent and proportionate administrative penalty system.

### **2. Definitions and Scope of Violations**

#### ‚úÖ **Types of Violations Identified**

| Violation Type | Legal Basis |
|---------------|------------|
| **Type A, B, C, D, or E** | Defined based on specific provisions in Schedules 1‚Äì3 and the Greenhouse Gas Pollution Pricing Act |

#### **A. Contravention of Environmental Acts or Regulations (Schedule 1 ‚Äì Column 1)**
- Any violation of a provision in an Environmental Act or its regulations listed in **Column 1 of Schedule 1** is considered a **"violation"** that may be prosecuted under EVAMPA.
- Examples include violations of pollution control, waste management, or emissions standards under Acts like the *Canadian Environmental Protection Act (CEPA)*, *Municipal Solid Waste Act*, or *Canadian Environmental Protection Act, 1999*.

#### **B. Contravention of Orders or Directions (Schedule 2 ‚Äì Column 1)**
- Any breach of an order or direction issued under a provision in an Environmental Act or its regulations listed in **Column 1 of Schedule 2** constitutes a violation.
- These may include court-issued directives, enforcement orders, or regulatory instructions (e.g., to reduce emissions or stop discharges).

#### **C. Failure to Comply with Permit Conditions (Schedule 3 ‚Äì Column 1)**
- Non-compliance with a condition of a permit, licence, or authorization issued under an Environmental Act or regulation listed in **Column 1 of Schedule 3** is a violation.
- Examples: failing to install pollution control equipment, not reporting emissions, or exceeding permitted discharge volumes.

#### **D. Contravention of Greenhouse Gas Pollution Pricing Act (GHG Act)**
- Violation of **subsection 174(1)** or **paragraph 178(1)(a)** of the *Greenhouse Gas Pollution Pricing Act* (e.g., failure to pay carbon tax or report GHG emissions) is designated as a violation under EVAMPA.

### **3. Classification of Violations: Type A‚ÄìE**

Each violation is classified into one of five categories ‚Äî **Type A, B, C, D, or E** ‚Äî based on the **nature and severity** of the violation, as specified in **Columns 2 of Schedules 1‚Äì3**.

| Type | Severity Level |
|------|----------------|
| **A** | Most serious (e.g., major pollution events, large-scale non-compliance) |
| **B** | Moderate severity |
| **C** | Less serious but still significant |
| **D** | Minor or recurring violations |
| **E** | Very minor or technical breaches |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Classification determines the penalty structure and the factors used to calculate the penalty amount.*

### **4. Penalty Calculation Formulae**

Penalties are calculated using **formulae based on four key components**:

---

#### üîπ **For Type A, B, or C Violations**  
Formula:
\[
\text{Penalty} = B + H + E - G
\]
Where:
- \( B \) = **Baseline penalty amount** (from Schedule 4 or 5)
- \( H \) = **History of non-compliance amount** (if applicable)
- \( E \) = **Environmental harm amount** (if applicable)
- \( G \) = **Economic gain amount** (if applicable)

> üìå *Note:* If no harm or gain, only baseline and history are considered.

---

#### üîπ **For Type D or E Violations**  
Formula:
\[
\text{Penalty} = B + H - G
\]
Where:
- \( B \) = Baseline penalty amount
- \( H \) = History of non-compliance amount (if applicable)
- \( G \) = Economic gain amount (if applicable)

> üìå *Note:* No environmental harm component for D/E violations.

### **6. Continuation of Violations and Daily Penalties**

If a violation **continues over multiple days**, it results in **separate daily violations** under Section 12 of the EVAMPA.

Penalty increases with time:
| Time Period | Daily Penalty |
|-----------|----------------|
| Day 2 to Day 9 (after initial violation) | $0.25 |
| Day 10 to Day 20 | $0.50 |
| Day 21 and beyond | $1.00 |

> üìå *Purpose:* To deter prolonged or repeated environmental harm by increasing penalties over time.

### **7. Service of Notice of Violation**

The notice of violation must be served in a manner appropriate to the **type of violator**:

#### ‚úÖ For **Individuals**:
- Leave with the individual at their last known address or with an adult household member.
- OR send via **registered mail, courier, fax, or electronic means** to their address.
- *For minors under 18*: Serve with a parent or guardian.

#### ‚úÖ For **Persons (non-individuals)**:
- Serve with authorized representative or officer in charge of operations.
- OR send by registered mail, courier, fax, or electronic means to the representative or head office.
- OR send electronically to an individual named above.

#### ‚úÖ For **Ships or Vessels**:
- Deliver personally to the **master or person in charge**.
- Affix to the vessel in a prominent location.
- Leave with the **owner/operator‚Äôs representative**.
- Send via registered mail, courier, fax, or electronic means to owner/operator or representative.
- Electronic transmission to individuals involved.

> ‚úÖ **Proof of Service**:
- Acknowledgement signed by the recipient.
- Certificate of service (approved by Minister) stating date, method, and recipient.
- Electronic transmission record (with date/time).

> ‚è≥ **If no proof of service**:
- **Registered mail/courier**: Service effective on the **10th day after mailing**.
- **Fax/electronic**: Service effective on the **day of transmission**.

### **8. Review Rights for Ships and Vessels**

Under **Section 15 of the EVAMPA**, a ship or vessel may request a review on behalf of:
- The **owner**
- The **operator or master**
- An **authorized representative** of the owner or operator

> üîç *This ensures accountability and fairness in enforcement actions involving maritime operations.*
