### **Detailed Summary of the Regulations Respecting Customs Sufferance Warehouses (S.C. 1986, c. 1)**

This regulation, enacted under the authority of the *Customs Act*, establishes the rules governing the operation of **customs sufferance warehouses** in Canada. These warehouses are designated facilities where imported goods may be temporarily stored while awaiting clearance or payment of duties. The regulation provides a comprehensive framework for licensing, operation, security, compliance, and enforcement of such facilities.

---

## **I. Legal Basis and Enforcement**

- **Authority**:  
  The regulation was made under:
  - Section 30, subsection 37(1), paragraphs 164(1)(i) and (j), and  
  - Section 166 of the *Customs Act*.
  
- **Procedural Background**:  
  A proposed version of these regulations was published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **April 26, 1986**, giving interested parties a reasonable opportunity to submit representations to the Minister of National Revenue. Based on this public consultation, the Governor in Council approved the final regulations.

- **Effective Date**:  
  The regulations come into force on the date that the relevant provisions of the *Customs Act* (specifically section 30, subsection 37(1), and section 166) come into force.

## **II. Key Definitions**

The regulation defines several key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Customs Act* |
| **Applicant** | A person who applies for a licence to operate a sufferance warehouse |
| **Carrier** | A person authorized under the *Transportation of Goods Regulations* to transport goods |
| **Chief Officer of Customs** | The manager of the customs office serving the area where the warehouse is located |
| **Excise Warehouse Licensee** | As defined in section 2 of the *Excise Act, 2001* |
| **Firearm / Prohibited Ammunition / Prohibited Device / Prohibited Weapon / Restricted Weapon** | As defined in the *Criminal Code* (subsections 84(1)) |
| **Special Container** | As defined in section 2 of the *Excise Act, 2001* |
| **Licence** | A permit to operate a place as a sufferance warehouse |
| **Licensee** | A person who holds a licence |

## **III. Licensing Requirements**

### 1. **Application Process**
- Applications must be submitted in the **prescribed form**.
- Must include a **detailed plan** of the proposed sufferance warehouse that specifies:
  - Whether the warehouse exists or is to be constructed;
  - The type of construction (existing or new);
  - The specific area designated for storing imported goods.

### 2. **Conditions for Issuing a Licence**
The Minister may only issue a licence if:
- The applicant has **good character**;
- The applicant has **sufficient financial resources** to provide necessary facilities, equipment, personnel, and services (as required under sections 11‚Äì13 of the Act), and to lease or purchase the warehouse;
- There is a **need** in the local area due to the volume and nature of imports;
- The site is **within a reasonable distance** of major transportation routes and a customs office;
- The warehouse has **adequate storage space** for imported goods;
- The structure is **suitable for warehouse operations**;
- The **Customs Agency can provide customs services** for the warehouse.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note*: Some conditions (e.g., "good character") were previously subject to repeal (SOR/95-177, s.1), but remain in effect in this summary.

### 3. **Security Requirements**
- An applicant must provide **security** under the *Financial Security (Electronic Means) Regulations*, in an amount set by the Minister.
- The Minister may **require an increase** in security at any time if duties payable on stored goods exceed the current level.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note*: This security requirement was later repealed by SOR/2024-41, s.38.

## **IV. Licence Amendments and Cancellation**

### 1. **Amendments Allowed**
The Minister may amend a licence for the following reasons:
- Change restrictions on **classes of goods** that may be received;
- Change the **circumstances** under which goods may be received;
- Change the **name of the licensee** (if the name changes).

> üîç **Notice Requirement**: For changes to goods restrictions or circumstances, the licensee must be given **90 days‚Äô notice** before the amendment is made.

### 2. **Cancellation of Licence**
A licence may be cancelled if:
- The licensee **no longer owns or leases** the warehouse;
- The licensee **requests cancellation in writing**;
- The licensee becomes **bankrupt**.

> üîç **Suspension and Cancellation Conditions (subject to section 9)**:
- The licensee is in **receivership**;
- Fails to comply with any law or regulation regarding import/export controls;
- Acts **dishonestly** in dealings with customs brokers, importers, carriers, or government;
- Is found **incompetent** in managing the warehouse;
- The volume of goods has declined to the point where the warehouse is no longer needed;
- There is **no longer a need** for a sufferance warehouse in the area;
- The **Customs Agency can no longer provide services** to the warehouse.

### 3. **Notice and Representation Rights**
- Before cancelling a licence, the Minister must give the licensee **90 days‚Äô notice** and provide all relevant grounds.
- The licensee may submit **representations within 90 days** to request that the cancellation not occur.
- If a licence is **suspended**, the licensee receives a written notice confirming the suspension and the reasons.
- The licensee may make representations within **90 days** to request reinstatement.
- The Minister may **reinstate** a suspended licence if the cause for suspension no longer exists.

## **V. Operational Requirements for Licensees**

Every licensee must ensure the following standards are met at the sufferance warehouse:

### 1. **Facilities and Services**
- Provide **washroom facilities and offices** for customs officers, including heat, light, and cleaning services (if requested by the chief officer);
- Provide **adequate space** for examination of imported goods;
- Ensure that **goods are made available** to officers for inspection;
- Provide a **detention compound or parking area** for goods in conveyance (if requested);
- Provide **secure storage** with:
  - Sturdy doors and building components;
  - Secure locks on doors and windows;
  - Signs indicating security requirements;
  - Additional security measures if storing **designated goods** (e.g., nuclear substances, firearms, tobacco).

### 2. **Access Control**
- **No person other than the licensee, their employees, or a carrier‚Äôs employees** may enter a storage area without:
  - Written authorization, or
  - The **attendance of a customs officer**.

### 3. **Security Procedures**
- Must have **clear procedures** to:
  - Maintain the security of the warehouse;
  - Restrict access to authorized personnel;
  - Ensure staff are trained and follow these procedures.

### 4. **Charges and Transparency**
- Must publish a **schedule of charges** for services offered and make it available for inspection by any person.

### 5. **Free Services to Officers**
- All facilities, services, personnel, and equipment provided to customs officers under paragraphs 11(1)(a) to (c) must be provided **free of charge**.

### 6. **Receipt of Goods**
- Goods must be **acknowledged electronically** upon receipt.
- **Exception**: If goods are transported by a **courier** and will be released under subsection 32(4) of the *Customs Act* before duties are paid, the licensee may acknowledge receipt by:
  - Endorsing the bill of lading, waybill, or similar document;
  - Endorsing the customs reporting document; or
  - Issuing a transfer document to the carrier.

> üîç *Note*: "Courier" is defined under the *Persons Authorized to Account for Casual Goods Regulations*.

### 7. **Refusal of Goods**
- A licensee may **refuse to accept goods** for safekeeping if the person requesting it has an **unpaid account** for storage fees.

## **VI. Time Limits for Removal of Goods and Forfeiture**

### 1. **General Rule: 40-Day Limit**
- If goods are not removed from the warehouse within **40 days** of being reported under section 12 of the *Customs Act*, they may be **deposited in a place of safe-keeping** under subsection 37(1) of the Act.

### 2. **Perishable Goods: 4-Day Limit**
- Perishable goods must be removed within **4 days** of reporting; otherwise, they may be deposited in a place of safe-keeping.
