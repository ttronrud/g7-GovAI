### **2. Key Definitions (Core Terminology)**

The regulations define over 40 key terms to ensure clarity and consistency across offshore operations. These definitions are critical for interpreting the rules governing safety, environmental protection, and operational standards.

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Accidental Event** | An unplanned event that may result in loss of life or environmental damage (e.g., explosion, leak). |

| **Accommodations Area/Installation** | Living quarters, dining, medical, and recreational areas on an installation; separate from production or drilling operations. |

| **Production Installation** | Equipment used for petroleum production, processing, separation, storage, and related marine activities. |
| **Flow System** | Equipment used to measure and record fluid production (e.g., flow meters, master meters, sampling devices). |
| **Well Control** | Managing fluid movement into or out of a well to prevent blowouts or uncontrolled flow. |
| **Safety-Critical Element** | Any system or equipment essential to safety or environmental protection â€” including those preventing or mitigating major accidents. |
| **Barrier Envelope** | A combination of physical elements that prevent fluid migration from formation into wellbore or environment. |
| **Environmental Load** | Natural forces like wind, waves, ice, tides, seismic activity that affect structural integrity. |

| **Waste Material** | Garbage, cuttings, produced water, sewage, or surplus fluids generated during operations. |

| **Subsea Production System** | Equipment on or below seabed (e.g., production risers, flowlines, control systems) upstream of isolation valves. |
| **Diving Project/System** | Activities involving diving in oil/gas operations, including equipment for decompression and rescue. |
