### **Overview**

This regulation, issued by the Minister of Industry, provides a legal exemption under the *Radiocommunication Act* allowing specific individuals and entities to operate, install, possess, manufacture, import, distribute, or lease **radio frequency jammers**â€”devices that intentionally interfere with or block radio communicationsâ€”without violating the Actâ€™s general prohibition on such activities. The exemption is strictly limited to operations deemed necessary for **national security, public safety, national defence, international relations, law enforcement, and protection of property or persons**.

The Order is designed to support the operational needs of the **Department of National Defence (DND)** and the **Canadian Forces (CF)**, as well as related government and foreign military entities, while ensuring that jammer use remains **controlled, accountable, and minimally disruptive** to the broader radiocommunication environment.

---

## **Key Provisions and Structure**

The Order is structured into two main parts:

---

### **Part 1: Exemptions for DND, Canadian Forces, and Related Entities (Section 2)**

This section grants exemptions to the following persons or entities under subsection 4(4) and paragraph 9(1)(b) of the Radiocommunication Act:

#### **1. DND and Canadian Forces Personnel**
- **Who is covered**:  
  - His Majesty in right of Canada, represented by the **Minister of National Defence**, and  
  - Any **employee of DND** or **officer or non-commissioned member of the Canadian Forces** who is required in the course of their duties or training to:
    - Install, use, possess, manufacture, import, distribute, or lease a jammer  
    - For purposes specified in subsection (2)

#### **2. Other Officers or Servants of the Crown**
- **Who is covered**:  
  - Any officer or servant of the Crown (under the supervision of someone in paragraph 1(a)) who is required to carry out activities involving jammers for purposes specified in subsection (2)

#### **3. DND Public Works and Government Services (PWGSC) Employees**
- **Who is covered**:  
  - The **Minister of Public Works and Government Services** and any **employee of PWGSC** who is required to import a jammer **on behalf of DND** for a purpose specified in subsection (2)

#### **4. Foreign Military Personnel in Canada**
- **Who is covered**:  
  - Any **member of a foreign military force** present in Canada under an international arrangement, who is required to:
    - Install, use, possess, import, or distribute a jammer  
    - Under the **supervision of a person in paragraph 1(a)**  
    - For a purpose specified in subsection (3)

> âœ… **Purpose of Exemption (Section 2(2))**  
> The exemption applies for the following **authorized purposes**:
> - National security  
> - Public safety (including in penitentiaries and prisons)  
> - National defence  
> - International relations  
> - Investigation or prosecution of offences in Canada (including preservation of evidence)  
> - Protection of property or prevention of serious harm to any person  

> âœ… **Foreign Military Purpose (Section 2(3))**  
> For foreign military personnel, the exemption applies only for:
> - National defence  
> - International relations  
> - Protection of property or prevention of serious harm to any person  

---

### **Part 2: Exemptions for Contractors and Bidders (Section 3)**

This section grants exemptions to **contractors and bidders** involved in supplying jammers to DND.

#### **Who is covered**:
- Any **person or entity** that:
  - Responds to a **solicitation of bids** for the provision of jammers to DND, and offers a jammer for sale; **OR**
  - Has entered into a **contract with the Government of Canada** for the provision of jammers or related services, and:
    - Is required to install, use, possess, manufacture, import, distribute, offer for sale, or sell a jammer  
    - In accordance with the terms of the contract or any related subcontract  

> âœ… **Purpose of Exemption (Section 3(2))**  
> The exemption applies **only** for:
> - National defence  
> - International relations  

> âš ï¸ **Note**: Contractors are not permitted to use jammers for other purposes (e.g., public safety, law enforcement) unless explicitly authorized under a separate exemption.

## **Conditions and Requirements for Compliance**

To ensure responsible and lawful use, the Order imposes several **mandatory conditions** on all exempted persons and entities.

### **1. Mandatory Notification to the Minister (Section 2(1))**
Any person or entity covered under paragraph 2(1)(a), (b), or (d) must **not operate a jammer** unless they have received a **written notice** from DND or the Canadian Forces containing:
- Postal and email addresses, and telephone number of the **headquarters or centre of operations** responsible for jammers  
- Titles, postal and email addresses, and telephone numbers of **individuals responsible for jammers**  
- Titles, email addresses, and telephone numbers of **persons responsible for responding to inquiries** (including outside business hours)  

> ğŸ” **Update Requirement**: If any of this information changes, the DND or CF must **immediately update** the Minister.

---

### **2. Mandatory Training Requirement (Section 2(1))**
- All persons covered under paragraphs 2(1)(a), (b), or (d) must:
  - **Receive or be receiving training** in relation to **each activity** they perform involving jammers  
  - Training must be relevant to the specific use (e.g., installation, operation, maintenance)

> âš ï¸ **Note**: Training is required regardless of whether the activity is conducted in a formal or informal setting.

---

### **3. Accessibility of Directives and Instructions**
- **DND must ensure** that all directives, instructions, and orders related to jammer use are **reasonably accessible** to employees responsible for jammers (in paper or electronic format).  
- **Canadian Forces must ensure** similar accessibility for officers and non-commissioned members.

> ğŸ“Œ Purpose: To ensure consistent, transparent, and standardized operational guidance.

---

### **4. Minimization of Interference and Emissions (Section 2(1))**
All exempted persons must:
- **Minimize jammer interference** to:
  - The **smallest physical area**  
  - The **fewest radio frequencies**  
  - The **appropriate power level**  
  - The **minimum duration** required to achieve the intended purpose  
- **Minimize unwanted emissions** and **exposure of persons to radiofrequency (RF) fields**  
- Ensure jammers are **capable of frequency adjustment** (i.e., can be tuned to interfere only with specific frequencies)

> ğŸ” This ensures that jammer use does not cause unintended disruption to civilian or commercial radio services.

---

### **5. Access and Security Controls (Section 2(1))**
- Jammers must be:
  - **Accessible only to persons exempted under this Order** or to contractors exempted under paragraph 3(1)(b)  
  - **Turned off and stored securely** when not in use, including during transport  

> ğŸ”’ This prevents unauthorized access or misuse.

---

### **6. Recordkeeping Requirements (Section 2(1))**
The **Department of National Defence** (or Canadian Forces) must maintain a **detailed record** of every jammer use by exempted personnel or entities. Records must include:
- **Place, date, and (if possible) time** of use  
- **Radio frequencies interfered with or obstructed**  
- **Purpose** of use (as specified in paragraphs 2(2)(a) to (f))  

> ğŸ“‚ These records are critical for accountability, audits, and potential regulatory or legal review.

---

### **7. Restricted Use by Contractors (Section 3(1)(b))**
A person or entity under paragraph 3(1)(b) (i.e., a contractor) may **only use a jammer** under the following conditions:
- The use is **under the direct supervision** of a person referred to in paragraph 2(1)(a) (e.g., a DND officer)  
- The purpose is one of:
  - Testing the jammerâ€™s functionality  
  - Performing maintenance on the jammer  
  - Providing training to DND or CF personnel  

> âŒ **No other use is permitted** (e.g., testing on civilian networks, unauthorized interference).
