**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

This regulation is a formal Canadian government directive issued under the authority of the *National Defence Act*, authorizing the deployment of Canadian military personnel to participate in the **United Nations Mission for the Referendum in Western Sahara (UNMRS)**. Below is a comprehensive and detailed breakdown of the regulation:

---

### **1. Background and Context**

- **UN Security Council Resolution 690 (1991)**  
  On April 29, 1991, the United Nations Security Council adopted Resolution 690, which:
  - Recognized the right of the people of Western Sahara to self-determination.
  - Established the **United Nations Mission for the Referendum in Western Sahara (UNMRS)**.
  - Mandated the conduct of a referendum to allow the people of Western Sahara to choose between independence or integration with Morocco.
  - Required the deployment of a UN-mandated mission to ensure the security, neutrality, and integrity of the referendum process.

- **UN Secretary-General’s Report**  
  The regulation references the report submitted by the UN Secretary-General, which supported the holding of a referendum in Western Sahara. This report provided the basis for the Security Council’s decision to establish and support the UNMRS.

---

### **2. Canadian Participation**

- The regulation acknowledges that **Canada’s participation in the UNMRS is both appropriate and advisable** given its international obligations, commitment to peacekeeping, and support for self-determination.

- Canada is being formally invited to contribute personnel to the mission, aligning with its broader foreign policy principles of promoting peace, human rights, and international law.

---

### **3. Legal Authority**

- The deployment of Canadian military personnel is authorized under **paragraph 31(1)(b)** of the *National Defence Act* (R.S., c. 31 (1st Supp.), s. 60 (Sch. I, item 14)).

  - This provision allows the Governor General in Council (acting on the advice of the Minister of National Defence) to place Canadian forces on active service **beyond Canada’s borders** in support of international peacekeeping operations, including those authorized by the United Nations.

  - The authority is exercised in accordance with international obligations and under the framework of UN mandates.

---

### **4. Personnel Deployed**

The regulation specifies that the following Canadian military personnel will be deployed:

- **Regular Force Personnel**:
  - Officers
  - Non-commissioned members (NCOs)

- **Reserve Force Personnel**:
  - Officers
  - Non-commissioned members (NCOs)

These individuals are:
- **Part of** the UNMRS, or
- Serving in **immediate support roles** to the mission

This includes both direct operational roles (e.g., security, logistics, administration) and support functions essential to the mission’s success.

---

### **5. Purpose of Deployment**

The primary objectives of the Canadian deployment are:

- To assist in ensuring the **security and neutrality** of the referendum process.
- To support the **implementation of the self-determination process** in Western Sahara.
- To uphold international law and the principles of peaceful conflict resolution.
- To contribute to a stable and just political outcome for the people of Western Sahara.

---

### **6. Scope and Duration**

- The deployment is **beyond Canada**, meaning personnel will serve in Western Sahara under the authority of the United Nations.
- The regulation does **not specify a time frame or duration**, implying that the deployment may be temporary and subject to renewal or adjustment based on the evolving needs of the UNMRS and the political situation in Western Sahara.
- Deployment will be governed by the terms of the UNMRS mandate and Canadian military policy.

---

### **7. Governance and Oversight**

- The decision is made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of National Defence**.
- This reflects the standard Canadian process for deploying military forces abroad under the National Defence Act.
- The deployment is consistent with Canada’s participation in international peacekeeping operations and its adherence to UN mandates.

---

### **8. Significance and Implications**

- **Diplomatic and Moral Commitment**: Canada’s involvement demonstrates its support for self-determination and the rule of law in international affairs.
- **Peacekeeping Contribution**: This deployment reflects Canada’s long-standing role in global peace and security efforts.
- **Operational Flexibility**: The inclusion of both regular and reserve force personnel allows for a scalable and responsive deployment.
- **International Cooperation**: The mission is a collaborative effort involving multiple UN member states, with Canada contributing to a shared international goal.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation formally authorizes the deployment of Canadian military personnel to the **United Nations Mission for the Referendum in Western Sahara**. It is grounded in international law, UN mandates, and Canada’s constitutional authority under the *National Defence Act*. The deployment supports a key objective of self-determination for the people of Western Sahara and reflects Canada’s commitment to peace, justice, and international cooperation.

While the regulation does not specify the number of personnel or the exact duration of deployment, it establishes a clear legal and operational framework for Canada’s participation in this UN-mandated peacekeeping mission.
