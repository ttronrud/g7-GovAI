## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Canadian Passport Regulations (Replaced Order)**

### **1. Purpose and Legal Authority**
- The Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Secretary of State for External Affairs, has **revoked the previous Canadian Passport Regulations (C.R.C., c. 641)** and **replaced them with a new Order** governing the issuance, administration, and revocation of Canadian passports.
- This Order is issued under the authority of the **Citizenship Act** (Act) and the **former Citizenship Act** (former Act), and it establishes a modern, secure, and legally robust framework for passport administration in Canada.

> **Key Point**: The Order replaces outdated regulations and aligns with current citizenship, security, and digital governance standards.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**
- **Act**: The *Citizenship Act* (Loi).
- **Applicant**: A person aged **16 years or older** who applies for a passport.
- **Former Act**: The *Canadian Citizenship Act* (pre-2009), which is now superseded.
- **Minister**: The **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration** (current responsible minister).
- **Passport**: An official document that confirms a person‚Äôs **identity and Canadian nationality**, intended to facilitate international travel.

> **Note**: References to "Passport Canada," "Passport Office," and "point of service" have been **repealed** (removed) under statutory instruments (SI/2006-95, SI/2013-57).

---

### **3. Legal Principles and Interpretations**
- **Indictable Offences**: Any offence that could be prosecuted **summarily or by indictment** is treated as an **indictable offence** for the purposes of this Order, even if prosecuted summarily.
- **Electronic Administration**: The Minister may use **electronic means** (including automated systems) to administer, process, and make decisions under this Order.
- **Digital Biometrics**: The Minister may convert applicant information (e.g., photographs) into **digital biometric formats** for use in passports or identity verification.

---

### **4. Passport Issuance Requirements**
#### ‚úÖ Conditions for Issuance
Every passport must:
- Be in a form **prescribed by the Minister**;
- Be issued in the name of the **Minister on behalf of Her Majesty in right of Canada**;
- Remain the **property of Her Majesty in right of Canada**;
- Be issued **on the condition** that the bearer returns it to the Minister **without delay** upon request;
- Be **signed by the person to whom it is issued**.

> **Clarification**: A passport with a reference to the *Minister of Foreign Affairs* is deemed to have been issued in the name of the Minister.

---

### **5. Who Can Be Issued a Passport?**
- A passport may be issued **only to a person who is a Canadian citizen** under the *Citizenship Act*.
- **No passport may be issued to anyone who is not a Canadian citizen**.

> **Exception**: The Minister may issue a passport to a child under 16 years of age under specific conditions (see below).

### **7. Passport for Children Under 16**
A child under 16 may be issued a passport if:
- The applicant is the **parent** of the child;
- The applicant is the **custodial parent** (if parents are divorced or separated); or
- The applicant is the **legal guardian** of the child.

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è Exceptions and Restrictions:
- **If parents are divorced/separated**, a passport may **not** be issued unless:
  - The application is accompanied by **evidence** that issuing the passport is **not contrary** to a court order or separation agreement.
- **If a court order restricts movement** to a specific judicial district, a passport may **not** be issued unless:
  - The order is **revoked or varied** to allow travel.
- **Exception**: Despite the above, the Minister may issue a passport to a child under 16 **if they have reasonable grounds to believe it is in the child‚Äôs best interest**, even without a parent‚Äôs application.

> **Note**: This exception provides flexibility in cases involving child welfare, safety, or urgent travel needs.

### **9. Grounds for Refusal to Issue or Revocation of a Passport**
The Minister may **refuse to issue** or **revoke** a passport if the applicant:
#### ‚ùå General Grounds (for adults and children):
- Fails to provide a **duly completed application** or required information.
- Is **charged in Canada** with an **indictable offence**.
- Is **charged outside Canada** with an offence that would be indictable in Canada.
- Is subject to **imprisonment or restrictions** (e.g., parole, probation, work release, temporary absence) under Canadian law.
- Is subject to **similar restrictions abroad**.
- Has been **convicted** under **section 57 of the Criminal Code** (offences involving fraud, deception, or misuse of state resources).
- Is **indebted to the Crown** for repatriation or consular assistance.
- Has a **valid passport that has not expired and has not been revoked**.

#### ‚ùå Additional Grounds (for children):
- The Minister may refuse to issue a child‚Äôs passport if it is **not in the child‚Äôs best interest**.

#### ‚ùå Grounds for Refusal Based on Facilitation of Fraud:
- The applicant has **facilitated the use of a passport by someone other than the bearer** (e.g., identity theft, fraud).

#### ‚ùå Grounds for Revocation (after issuance):
- The person is **outside Canada** and is **charged with an indictable offence** abroad.
- The passport is being used to **commit an indictable offence** in Canada or abroad.
- The person **permits another to use the passport**.
- The passport was obtained through **false or misleading information**.
- The person has **ceased to be a Canadian citizen**.

> **Note**: These grounds apply equally to both adults and children.

### **10. National Security and Terrorism Powers**
- The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** may:
  - Refuse to issue or revoke a passport if there are **reasonable grounds to believe** it is necessary to:
    - Prevent a **terrorism offence** (as defined in section 2 of the *Criminal Code*).
    - Protect **national security** of Canada or a foreign country.

> **This power is critical** in preventing threats to public safety or national security.

---

### **11. Consequences of Refusal or Revocation**
- If a passport is **refused or revoked** (except for ground **g** ‚Äî indebtedness to Crown), the Minister may **refuse to deliver passport services** (e.g., renewals, replacements) for a **maximum period of 10 years**.
- The same applies to the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** when acting under section 10.1.

> **Exception**: Ground **g** (indebtedness to Crown) does **not** trigger the 10-year service restriction.

---

### **12. Cancellation of Passports**
The Minister or Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness may **cancel** a passport if:
- There is **reasonable suspicion** that cancellation is needed to prevent an act or omission under **section 7(4.1) of the Criminal Code** (e.g., acts endangering public safety).
- In the case of a child under 16, cancellation is in the **child‚Äôs best interest**.
