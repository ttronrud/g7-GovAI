### **Detailed Summary of the Order Declaring an Amnesty Period (2024)**

---

#### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
This regulation is issued under **subsection 117.14(1)a of the Criminal Code (R.S.C., c. C-46)**, which authorizes the Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of Justice) to declare an amnesty period for certain individuals who possess **prohibited firearms** under specific conditions. The authority is rooted in **section 139 of the Criminal Code (S.C. 1995, c. 39)**, which provides for the declaration of amnesty periods to facilitate compliance with firearms laws without criminal penalties.

---

#### **2. Definition of Key Terms**
- **"Specified firearm"**: A firearm that is listed in **items 97 to 200 of Part 1 of the Schedule to the Firearms Regulations** (Regulations Prescribing Certain Firearms and Other Weapons, Components and Parts of Weapons, Accessories, Cartridge Magazines, Ammunition and Projectiles as Prohibited or Restricted). These are **prohibited firearms** under Canadian law, meaning they are generally banned from ownership or possession.

---

#### **3. Scope of the Amnesty Period**
The amnesty period is designed to provide legal relief and flexibility for individuals and entities currently in possession of specified firearms, particularly those that were previously classified as **restricted firearms**. It applies to the following categories of persons:

---

##### **(a) Individuals Who Own or Possess a Specified Firearm and Hold a Valid Licence**
- On the day this Order comes into force, the person:
  - Owns or possesses a **specified firearm**;
  - Holds a **valid licence issued under the Firearms Act**;
- During the amnesty period:
  - Remains in **possession** of the firearm;
  - **Continues to hold the licence** while in possession;
- If the firearm was a **restricted firearm** on the day before the Order came into force:
  - Had a **valid registration certificate** issued under the Firearms Act, or
  - Is issued a new registration certificate **on or after the Order's coming into force**, which would have been eligible under **section 13 of the Firearms Act** had the firearm remained restricted.

> **Purpose**: To allow individuals to legally manage, deactivate, destroy, or transfer their firearms without facing criminal charges.

---

##### **(b) Individuals Who Entered into a Transfer Agreement for a Specified Firearm**
- On or before the Order’s coming into force, entered into an agreement (under section 21 of the Firearms Act) to **receive** a specified firearm that was a **restricted firearm** at the time of the Order’s coming into force;
- During the amnesty period:
  - Is issued a **registration certificate** that would have been eligible under section 13 of the Firearms Act if the firearm had remained restricted;
  - Is in **possession** of the firearm;
  - Continues to hold the licence while in possession.

> **Purpose**: To allow lawful transfer of firearms from one person to another, especially where the transfer was already in process before the Order, and the recipient now has the legal standing to possess it.

---

##### **(c) Persons Storing a Specified Firearm on Behalf of an Owner**
- At any time during the amnesty period, is in **possession** of a specified firearm **solely for storage** on behalf of an owner who qualifies under (a) or (b);
- Holds a licence that would have permitted them to possess the firearm based on its classification **on the day before the Order came into force**.

> **Purpose**: To allow licensed storage agents to legally store firearms for owners, provided the storage is authorized under the owner’s licence.

---

##### **(d) Persons Performing Repairs or Adjustments**
- At any time during the amnesty period, is in **temporary possession** of a specified firearm for the sole purpose of:
  - Performing a **repair or adjustment** (as defined in subsection 2(2)(c)(i)) of a firearm owned by someone qualifying under (a);
- Holds a licence that would have permitted possession of the firearm based on its classification on the day before the Order came into force.

> **Purpose**: To allow qualified individuals to perform necessary safety-related maintenance without violating possession rules.

---

##### **(e) Persons Delivering a Firearm for Destruction**
- At any time during the amnesty period, is in possession of a specified firearm **solely for the purpose of delivering it for destruction**;
- Is either:
  - An **authorized organization** (under an Act of Parliament) authorized to transport firearms, or
  - An **employee, contractor, agent, or subcontractor** acting in the course of their duties.

> **Purpose**: To allow authorized entities to transport firearms to be destroyed, without violating possession or transportation laws.

---

##### **(f) Businesses Deactivating Firearms**
- At any time during the amnesty period, is in possession of a specified firearm **solely for the purpose of deactivating it** (rendering it non-functional);
- Is a **business** (as defined in subsection 2(1) of the Firearms Act) that holds a licence to possess prohibited firearms for deactivation, or is an **employee of such a business**.

> **Purpose**: To enable businesses to legally deactivate prohibited firearms for safe disposal or storage.

---

#### **4. Purpose of the Amnesty Period**
The primary goals of this amnesty are to:
- Allow individuals and businesses to **legally manage, deactivate, destroy, or transfer** specified firearms without criminal liability;
- Facilitate **safe and lawful disposal** of prohibited firearms;
- Provide **flexibility in transportation and storage** under strict conditions;
- Support **cultural and constitutional rights**, particularly for Indigenous peoples.

---

#### **5. Specific Permitted Actions During the Amnesty Period**
Each category of person may perform the following actions, subject to conditions:

| Action | Conditions |
|-------|-----------|
| **Deactivate** the firearm | So it is no longer a functioning firearm |
| **Deliver** to police for destruction or disposal | Must be done lawfully |
| **Deliver to a government-authorized destruction entity** | If the individual has an agreement with the Government of Canada |
| **Deliver to a carrier** (as defined in the Firearms Act) | For shipping to be deactivated or destroyed |
| **Deliver to the owner** | If not the owner |
| **Export** the firearm | Only if all legal requirements (including destination country laws) are met |
| **Return to manufacturer** | For businesses authorized to deactivate |
| **Transport by vehicle** | Only under strict conditions: <br> - Firearm must be **unloaded**, **no ammunition present** <br> - Must be in the **trunk** or **not visible from outside** <br> - Vehicle must **not be left unattended** <br> - Route must be **reasonably direct** |
| **Store the firearm** | In compliance with applicable storage regulations based on the firearm’s classification on the day before the Order came into force |

---

#### **6. Special Provisions for Indigenous Rights**
- For individuals who are **Indigenous** and:
  - Own a specified firearm that was a **non-restricted firearm** on the day before the Order came into force;
  - Are exercising a **right recognized and affirmed under Section 35 of the Constitution Act, 1982** (e.g., hunting for traditional or cultural purposes);
- They may **use the firearm for hunting** until they can obtain another firearm for that purpose.

> This provision recognizes **Indigenous rights to self-determination and traditional practices**, including hunting.

---

#### **7. Storage Requirements**
All possession of the firearm **before** any of the permitted actions must comply with storage regulations:
- **Individuals**: Must store the firearm in accordance with **section 5 or 6** of the *Storage, Display, Transportation and Handling of Firearms by Individuals Regulations*, based on its classification on the day before the Order came into force.
- **Businesses**: Must store the firearm in accordance with **section 5** of the *Storage, Display and Transportation of Firearms and Other Weapons by Businesses Regulations*.

> These rules ensure that firearms are securely stored and not accessible to unauthorized persons.

---

#### **8. Amnesty Period Timeline**
- **Starts**: On the day the Order is made (the date of issuance).
- **Ends**: **October 30, 2026**.

> This gives a total of **approximately 2 years and 1 month** for eligible individuals and businesses to comply with the conditions and complete the required actions.

---

#### **9. Key Legal and Policy Implications**
- **No criminal liability** applies during the amnesty period for the actions described in the Order.
- The Order **does not extend to all firearms** — only those listed in items 97–200 of the Schedule to the Firearms Regulations.
- The amnesty **does not apply retroactively** to acts before the Order’s coming into force — only to actions taken **during the period**.
- It reflects a **public safety and reconciliation strategy**, balancing law enforcement needs with respect for Indigenous rights and responsible firearm management.

---

#### **10. Conclusion**
This **Amnesty Period (2024)** is a targeted, legally grounded measure designed to:
- Reduce the number of illegal or unregistered prohibited firearms in circulation;
- Provide a safe, lawful pathway for individuals and businesses to manage their firearms;
- Respect constitutional rights, particularly those of Indigenous peoples;
- Promote public safety through responsible disposal and deactivation.

It represents a **pragmatic and rights-based approach** to firearms regulation, emphasizing both enforcement and reconciliation.
