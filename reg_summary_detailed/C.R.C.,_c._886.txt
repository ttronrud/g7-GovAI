**Detailed Summary of the Airport Traffic Regulations**

---

### **1. Title and Citation**
The regulations are officially titled the **Airport Traffic Regulations** and may be cited as such. These rules govern traffic operations on airports in Canada, excluding specific areas listed in **Schedule I or II** (the "land side" of certain airports), which are governed by separate regulations.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

The regulations define critical terms used throughout the document:

- **Aerodrome**: Any land, water, or other surface used, designed, or prepared for aircraft arrival, departure, movement, or servicing, including associated buildings, installations, and equipment.
  
- **Aircraft**: Any machine used or designed for navigation in the air; *excludes* machines that derive lift from reaction against the ground (e.g., hovercrafts).

- **Airport**: An aerodrome where a Canadian aviation document issued under the *Aeronautics Act* is in force.

- **Airport Manager**: The individual in charge of the airport or their authorized representative.

- **Animal**: Includes domestic animals and poultry.

- **Apron**: Area outside the manoeuvring area used for loading/unloading, refuelling, maintenance, parking, and movement of aircraft, vehicles, and pedestrians.

- **Apron Traffic**: All vehicles, pedestrians, and equipment using the apron.

- **Commercial Passenger Vehicle**: Any vehicle (e.g., taxi, bus) used or intended for transporting passengers for compensation.

- **Constable**: Includes members of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police, provincial/municipal police, or individuals authorized by the Minister to enforce these regulations.

- **Cross-Walk**: A designated pedestrian crossing or intersection area between sidewalks.

- **Department**: The Department of Transport (Ministère des Transports).

- **Driver**: A person physically controlling a motor vehicle or bicycle.

- **Intersection**: Area where two or more roads meet at an angle, defined by curb lines or boundaries.

- **Land Side**: As defined in the *Traffic on the Land Side of Airports Regulations* (Schedule I or II).

- **Loading Area**: Publicly designated area for passenger or cargo loading/unloading.

- **Manoeuvring Area**: Area used for aircraft take-off, landing, and associated movement — excludes the apron.

- **Minister**: The Minister of Transport.

- **Motor Vehicle**: Any self-propelled land vehicle, including over-snow vehicles (e.g., snowmobiles or snow plows), including those using jet propulsion for lift.

- **Over-Snow Vehicle**: A vehicle operating on tracks or skis designed for snow or ice.

- **Owner**: Person with legal title or registration of a motor vehicle, including conditional purchasers, lessees, or mortgagors with possession.

- **Park**: Standing of a vehicle (occupied or not) except during active loading/unloading.

- **Parking Area**: Designated area for parking vehicles, marked by signs or surface markings.

- **Parking Meter**: *Repealed* (SOR/92-120, s.1).

- **Parking Permit**: Issued by the airport manager authorizing parking in a specific area under defined conditions.

- **Parking Space**: Delineated space within a parking area for motor vehicles.

- **Pay-in-Advance Parking Control System**: System requiring payment before or after parking for the anticipated duration.

- **Pedestrian**: A person walking, including invalids or children in wheelchairs.

- **Restricted Area**: Zone designated by signage for access only to persons authorized by the airport manager.

- **Road**: Any highway, street, or place designed or used for motor vehicle passage.

- **Sidewalk**: Part of a road or apron constructed for pedestrian use.

- **Special Parking Permit**: Permit issued by the airport manager allowing parking in a specific location with specific terms.

- **Valid Identification**: Document issued by the airport manager authorizing access to restricted areas.

---

### **3. Scope of Application**
- These regulations apply to **airport areas outside the "land side"** of airports listed in **Schedule I or II**.
- The Minister may authorize any person or class of persons to act as a **constable** to enforce these regulations.

---

### **4. Licensing and Registration Requirements**
- No person may operate a motor vehicle on an airport unless:
  - They hold all required **licenses and permits** under provincial/municipal laws.
  - The vehicle is **registered and equipped** per provincial/municipal standards.
- A **provincial certificate of registration** is *prima facie proof* of ownership.
- Operating a vehicle on an airport is subject to **provincial and municipal laws**, *except* where those laws conflict with or are inconsistent with the **Government Property Traffic Act** or these regulations.

---

### **5. Authority to Install Traffic Signs and Devices**
- The **Minister** may:
  - Mark or erect traffic signs/devices regulating:
    - Speed limits
    - Parking, loading, and restricted areas
    - Load limits
    - Use of roads by vehicles or persons/animals
    - One-way designations
    - Stopping zones
    - Pedestrian traffic
    - General traffic control
- **No private individual or entity** may install traffic signs or devices without Ministerial authorization.
- Any sign bearing the **Department of Transport** or "Ministère des Transports" or its abbreviation is deemed to be authorized under this Part.

---

### **6. Compliance with Traffic Signs and Directions**
- All drivers must **obey traffic signs and devices** applicable to their vehicle.
- Drivers must comply with **directions given by a constable**.
- A constable may direct or control traffic to ensure order and safety.

---

### **7. Driving Restrictions**
- **Motor vehicles** (excluding over-snow vehicles) may only be driven on **roads**.
- **Over-snow vehicles** may only be operated with **permission from the airport manager**.
- On aprons, speed must not exceed **15 mph (25 km/h)** unless otherwise authorized.
- No driver may operate a vehicle in a manner that is **dangerous** to persons, aircraft, vehicles, or equipment — considering traffic volume and conditions.

---

### **8. Parking Rules**
#### General Prohibitions:
- No parking on the **apron** without the **airport manager’s permission**.
- No parking in **restricted areas** without **valid identification**.
- No parking in **areas designated as prohibited** by signage.
- No parking on **grassed or non-designated areas** without Ministerial permission.
- No parking in **loading areas**.

#### Parking Permits and Special Permits:
- In areas reserved for parking permits or special permits:
  - Only authorized persons may park.
  - A **label or ticket** must be affixed to the driver’s side windshield or dashboard (clearly visible).
  - Parking must follow permit terms.

#### Time Limits:
- Parking is limited to the duration indicated on signs.
- Parking is prohibited during specific hours if so designated.

#### Parking Spaces:
- In areas with **delineated parking spaces**, vehicles must be parked **entirely within one space**.
- If a vehicle is longer/wider and occupies more than one space:
  - The driver must pay for **each space occupied**.
- If a vehicle exceeds standard dimensions, it must be parked in a designated **oversized vehicle zone**.

#### Obstruction:
- Parking that obstructs traffic is prohibited.
- Violation of parking rules (e.g., oversized vehicle in wrong space) is considered **obstruction**.

---

### **9. Parking Control Systems**
- **Pay-in-advance systems** require payment before or after parking.
- Vehicles parked under such systems **cannot remain beyond the time period** for which the fee was paid.

---

### **10. Special Parking Zones**
- **Handicapped parking**: Only vehicles transporting handicapped persons may park here, and must be marked accordingly.
- **Designated for specific classes**: Only eligible persons (e.g., staff, vendors) may park in such zones.

---

### **11. Enforcement and Penalties**

#### General Offences:
- Any person who contravenes a provision of these regulations is liable on **summary conviction** to:
  - A fine not exceeding the **lesser of**:
    - The maximum fine under provincial highway traffic laws (if any), or
    - **$500**
  - A term of **imprisonment not exceeding six months**, or
  - Both fine and imprisonment.

#### Parking Violations:
- Offences related to parking are subject to a **maximum fine of $50**.

#### Additional Penalties:
- A court may issue an **order prohibiting** a convicted person from driving on **federal lands** (owned by Her Majesty in right of Canada) for up to **one year**.
- Violating such an order carries a fine of up to **$500** or imprisonment for up to **six months**, or both.

---

### **12. Plea of Guilty and Payment System**
A person accused of a parking violation may **register a plea of guilty within 72 hours** (excluding holidays) and pay a **fixed fee** depending on the type of violation and the airport:

| Violation Type | Airport Type (Schedule I) | Airport Type (Schedule II, *) | Airport Type (Schedule II, not *) |
|---------------|----------------------------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Parking in prohibited area | $25 | $15 | $10 |
| Parking in loading area | $30 | $20 | $15 |
| Parking in permit/reserved area | $25 | $15 | $10 |
| Overtime parking | $15 | $10 | $5 |

> *Note
