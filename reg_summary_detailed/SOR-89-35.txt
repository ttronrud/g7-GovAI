## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Regulations Respecting the Canadian International Trade Tribunal**

> **Note**: These regulations were originally enacted in 1988 and are now **repealed** by *SOR/2017-143, s. 2*. As such, they no longer have legal effect in Canada. This summary is for historical, interpretive, or academic reference only.

### 1. **Legal Basis and Authority**
- The regulations were made by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the **recommendation of the Minister of Finance**, under **section 40 of the Canadian International Trade Tribunal Act**.
- This section grants the government authority to issue rules and procedures to implement the Act.
- The regulations are **no longer in force** as of the repeal in 2017.

---

### 2. **Definitions and Key Terms**

#### a) **"Domestic Production"**
- Refers to the **value or volume** of goods that are:
  - **Identical** to the goods at issue in a complaint, or
  - **Directly competitive** with those goods (as defined below).
- This is central to assessing whether Canadian producers are being harmed by imports.

#### b) **"Like or Directly Competitive Goods"**
- Goods that are:
  - **Identical in all respects** to the subject goods of a complaint, **or**
  - In the absence of identical goods, goods whose **uses and other characteristics closely resemble** those of the subject goods.
- This definition is critical for determining market competition and injury to domestic producers.

#### c) **"Other Interested Party"**
- A broad category of parties entitled to be heard by the Tribunal in specific procedural contexts (e.g., appeals, inquiries).
- Includes:
  - Domestic producers of like or directly competitive goods.
  - Trade unions representing workers in such industries, or associations of such unions.
  - Exporters or importers of like or directly competitive goods into or from Canada.
  - Associations of domestic producers, exporters, or importers.
  - Associations advocating for **consumer interests** in Canada.
  - Governments of countries mentioned in reports, complaints, or extension requests (e.g., when a complaint involves a foreign government).
  - Any other person whose **rights or financial interests** may be affected by the proceedings.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *This definition ensures broad stakeholder participation, especially in matters involving trade, competition, and consumer protection.*

### 3. **Quorum and Single-Member Decision-Making Authority**

#### a) **General Quorum Provision**
- The **Chairman of the Tribunal** may determine that **one member** constitutes a quorum.
- That member may:
  - Exercise all powers of the Tribunal.
  - Perform all duties and functions of the Tribunal.

#### b) **Specific Situations Where Single-Member Quorum Applies**
The Chairman may authorize a single member to act as quorum in the following specific cases:
1. **Textile Tariff Relief Inquiries**
   - Related to the **1994 Reference** made by the Minister under section 19 of the Act.
2. **Appeals under the Customs Act or Special Import Measures Act**
   - Appeals from decisions of customs or import control authorities.
3. **Appeals under Section 81.19 of the Excise Tax Act**
   - Appeals regarding **rebates under section 120** of the Excise Tax Act.
4. **Orders under Section 75.3 and subsections 75.4(8) or 75.6(7) of the Special Import Measures Act**
   - These relate to anti-dumping or countervailing duty orders.

> ‚úÖ **Purpose**: To ensure **efficient and timely decision-making** in complex or precedent-setting cases, particularly those involving urgent or routine appeals.

### 4. **Determining Domestic Producer Status (for Inquiries under Sections 26, 30.07, 30.22, 30.23, 30.25)**

To determine if a party qualifies as a **domestic producer**, the Tribunal must examine whether the party:
1. Is **actually a domestic producer** (produces like or directly competitive goods in Canada).
2. Is **related** to an exporter or importer of such goods.
3. Also acts as an **importer or exporter** of such goods.

> üîç This helps identify potential conflicts of interest or overlapping roles in trade disputes.

---

### 5. **Definition of "Related" (for Parties under Sections 30.01(4), 30.011(5), 30.012(6))**

Two parties are considered **related** if any of the following apply:
- Blood relationship, marriage, or adoption (as per subsection 251(6) of the Income Tax Act).
- One is an **officer or director** of the other.
- Both are officers/directors of the **same two corporations, associations, partnerships, or organizations**.
- They are **partners**.
- One is the **employer** of the other.
- They **directly or indirectly control or are controlled** by the same person.
- One **directly or indirectly controls** the other.
- Any person directly or indirectly owns **5% or more** of the voting stock or shares of each party.

> üí° This is a key tool to detect **interlocking corporate structures** that may distort fair competition or create conflicts of interest.

### 6. **Factors to Consider in Determining Market Impact (Under Section 20(2)(a), 27(1), 30.28(1))**

When assessing whether imported goods are causing harm to domestic producers, the Tribunal must consider:

#### a) **Volume of Imports**
- Whether there has been a **significant increase** in the volume of imports into Canada.
- Whether the increase is **absolute or relative** to domestic production of like goods.

#### b) **Price Effects**
- Whether imported goods have **significantly undercut** prices of domestic goods.
- Whether such imports have:
  - **Depressed prices** of domestic goods, or
  - **Limited price increases** for domestic producers.

#### c) **Economic Effects on Domestic Producers**
- The Tribunal must evaluate all relevant economic factors affecting domestic producers and workers, including:
  - Changes in **production levels, employment, sales, market share, profits/losses**.
  - **Productivity**, return on investment, utilization of capacity.
  - **Cash flow, inventories**.
  - **Terms and conditions of employment**.
  - **Growth potential or ability to raise capital**.

> üìä This comprehensive analysis ensures a holistic assessment of market impact beyond simple price comparisons.

The Tribunal evaluates:
- Actual volume of PRC-origin goods imported into Canada.
- Effect on prices of like or directly competitive goods.
- Impact on domestic producers.

> üîé This reflects a targeted focus on **PRC imports** in the context of trade disputes and potential unfair practices.

### 8. **Significant Diversion of Trade (Under Section 30.21(1)(b) and 30.23(6))**

To determine if an action causes or threatens a **significant diversion of trade into Canada**:

The Tribunal considers:
- Increase in **market share** of PRC goods in Canada.
- Nature and extent of the **action** (e.g., dumping, subsidies).
- Actual or imminent **increase in import volume** due to the action.
- Supply and demand dynamics in the domestic market.
- Volume of PRC goods imported into Canada and into **other WTO members** taking similar actions.

> üåê This helps assess whether trade diversion is a result of coordinated or unfair trade practices across the WTO.

### 9. **Review of Orders for Continuance (Under Section 30.08(1) ‚Äì Extension Requests)**

To determine whether an existing order (e.g., anti-dumping or countervailing duty) remains necessary:

The Tribunal must:
- Review **recent developments** in:
  - Domestic markets.
  - Global markets (especially for like or directly competitive goods).
- Evaluate the **likely effects of terminating** the order.
- Use the **factors set out in Section 5** of the Act (which includes market conditions, injury risk, etc.).

> ‚è±Ô∏è Ensures orders are not maintained indefinitely without justification.
