**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

This regulation is a formal Canadian government directive issued under the authority of the *National Defence Act*, authorizing the deployment of Canadian military personnel to participate in the **United Nations Operation in Somalia (UNOSOM)**. It reflects Canada’s international commitment to supporting peace, reconciliation, and humanitarian efforts in Somalia during the early 1990s.

---

### **Background and Context**

1. **UN Security Council Resolutions 751 (1992) and 775 (1992):**
   - **Resolution 751 (April 24, 1992):** Established the **United Nations Operation in Somalia (UNOSOM)** with the primary objectives of:
     - Promoting reconciliation and a political settlement in Somalia.
     - Providing urgent humanitarian assistance to the Somali population, which was suffering from widespread famine, civil conflict, and displacement.
   - **Resolution 775 (August 28, 1992):** Authorized an increase in the size and operational scope of UNOSOM, including expanded troop deployment and enhanced mission capabilities. This expansion was necessary to meet the growing humanitarian and security challenges in the country.

2. **Canada’s Participation:**
   - The regulation acknowledges that participation in UNOSOM is both a matter of international responsibility and a strategic alignment with United Nations goals.
   - It is deemed "advisable" that Canada contribute to this mission, reflecting Canada’s role as a responsible global actor in peacekeeping and humanitarian operations.

---

### **Legal Basis and Authority**

- The Governor General in Council (the head of the Canadian government, acting on the advice of the Minister of National Defence) has issued this directive **pursuant to paragraph 31(1)(b)** of the *National Defence Act*.
- This paragraph empowers the government to place Canadian Forces personnel on active service **beyond Canada** in support of international peacekeeping or humanitarian missions, provided such service is in accordance with international obligations and under the authority of the United Nations.

---

### **Scope of the Deployment**

The regulation authorizes the following personnel to be placed on active service:

1. **Regular Force Personnel:**
   - Officers and non-commissioned members (NCOs) of the **Regular Canadian Forces**.
   - These are full-time, professional members of the Canadian Armed Forces serving in active duty.

2. **Reserve Force Personnel:**
   - Officers and NCOs from the **Reserve Force** of the Canadian Forces.
   - These individuals are typically part-time or available for call-up and are deployed only when required.
   - The regulation specifically includes those who are:
     - **Part of** the UNOSOM mission, or
     - Serving in **immediate support** of the mission.

> This means that even reserve personnel who are not permanently deployed but are directly involved in or supporting UNOSOM operations are included under this directive.

---

### **Purpose of the Deployment**

The primary objectives of the deployment are:
- To assist in **political reconciliation** efforts in Somalia by contributing to peacebuilding and the stabilization of the country.
- To deliver **urgent humanitarian assistance**, including food, medical aid, and protection of vulnerable populations.
- To support the broader mandate of UNOSOM to restore order and security in a region affected by civil war and humanitarian crisis.

---

### **Key Legal and Operational Implications**

- **International Obligation:** Canada’s participation is framed within the context of international law and UN mandates, ensuring that the deployment aligns with international standards and objectives.
- **Operational Flexibility:** By including both regular and reserve forces, the regulation allows for a flexible and scalable response to mission needs.
- **Service Beyond Canada:** The directive explicitly places personnel "on active service beyond Canada," meaning they are temporarily assigned to international operations outside Canadian borders.
- **Temporary Nature:** While the regulation does not specify duration, such deployments under the *National Defence Act* are typically temporary and subject to review and renewal based on mission needs and international developments.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation formally authorizes Canada’s contribution to the United Nations Operation in Somalia through the deployment of both regular and reserve members of the Canadian Forces. It is grounded in international obligations, supported by UN Security Council resolutions, and enacted under the legal authority of the *National Defence Act*. The deployment serves critical humanitarian and peacebuilding purposes in Somalia during a period of intense crisis, reflecting Canada’s commitment to global peace and human rights.

This action is a historical example of Canada’s role in international peacekeeping and humanitarian intervention during the 1990s.
