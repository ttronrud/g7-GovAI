**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Revocation of the Bank Act Public Inquiry Rules of Procedure and Approval of New Rules (S.C. 1991, c. 46)**

---

### **Overview**

This regulation, published in *Statutes of Canada 1991, Chapter 46* (S.C. 1991, c. 46), amends and replaces the former *Bank Act Public Inquiry Rules of Procedure* approved by Order in Council P.C. 1982-60 (Canada Gazette, January 14, 1982). It was issued under the authority of **subsection 26(5) of the Bank Act**, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance, and approved by His Excellency the Governor General in Council.

The primary objective of this regulation is to **modernize, clarify, and streamline the procedural framework** governing public inquiries into objections to proposed bank incorporations, continuances, or amalgamationsâ€”actions regulated under the *Bank Act*.

### **Key Definitions**

The regulation establishes clear definitions for key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Bank Act*, the primary legislation governing banking institutions in Canada. |
| **Application** | A formal submission made under sections 25, 33, or 228 of the Bank Act, seeking incorporation, continuation, or amalgamation of a bank. |
| **Inquiry** | A public hearing conducted by the Superintendent of Financial Institutions to examine an objection to a bank application. |
| **Objection** | A formal challenge to a proposed incorporation, continuance, or amalgamation. |
| **Party** | Either the applicant (proposing the bank) or the objector (challenging the proposal). |
| **Presiding Officer** | The Superintendent of Financial Institutions or, in certain cases, a person appointed under section 7 of the Bank Act to lead the inquiry. |

### **Initiation of an Inquiry**

The Superintendent of Financial Institutions has discretion to initiate a public inquiry **only if satisfied** that it is **necessary and in the public interest**.

#### Key Requirements for Notice:

- **Minimum 14 daysâ€™ advance notice** before the inquiry is held.
- The notice must be:
  - Published in the **Canada Gazette** and in a **newspaper of general circulation** at or near the location of the bankâ€™s head office.
- A **notice of inquiry** must be served on both parties (applicant and objector), including:
  - A copy of the **objection**.
  - An **invitation to attend** the inquiry.

#### Contents of the Notice:

The notice must clearly state:
1. **Date, time, and place** of the inquiry.
2. **Purpose of the inquiry** and the **substance of the objection**.
3. **Name of the presiding officer**.
4. Any other information deemed appropriate by the Superintendent.

### **Cancellation of an Inquiry**

An inquiry may be **cancelled** under the following circumstances:

- The **application is withdrawn**.
- The **objection is withdrawn**, or **all objections are withdrawn** (if multiple exist).

Upon cancellation:
- The Superintendent must **serve a notice of cancellation** on all parties.
- This notice must also be **published in the Canada Gazette** (as per paragraph 4(1)(a)).

> This ensures transparency and provides timely notice to stakeholders when proceedings are discontinued.

### **Service of Documents**

Documents required under these rules may be served in one of the following ways:

1. **Personal delivery** to the recipient.
2. **Registered or certified mail** to the recipientâ€™s address for service.
3. **Last known or usual address** if no current address is available.
4. **Other method** specified by the presiding officer.

#### Address for Service:

- **For an applicant**: The address listed in the application.
- **For an objector**: The address listed in the objection.

> This ensures that all parties receive official communications in a timely and legally valid manner.

---

### **Appointment of Presiding Officer**

- The Superintendent may **appoint a person** (e.g., a legal expert or independent official) to serve as **presiding officer** in place of the Superintendent.
- This allows for greater procedural fairness or expertise in complex cases.

---

### **Participation in the Inquiry**

#### Submissions:
- Any party (applicant or objector) may:
  - Submit **written submissions**.
  - Make **oral submissions** to the presiding officer during the inquiry.

#### Representation:
- A party may attend **in person** or be represented by **legal counsel**.

#### Witness Calls:
- The presiding officer has the authority to **allow a party to call witnesses** to testify during the inquiry.

> This provides procedural flexibility while maintaining accountability and due process.

---

### **Order of Submissions**

A strict procedural order applies during the inquiry:

1. The **objector** must make submissions **first**, in support of their objection.
2. The **applicant** then makes **responses** to the objection.

> This ensures that the objection is thoroughly examined before the applicant can respond, promoting fairness and transparency.

---

### **Public Access and Transparency**

- Every inquiry is **open to the public**.
- The public may attend and observe proceedings in person.

> This enhances public confidence in the regulatory process and promotes accountability.

---

### **Access to Documents**

During a specified period, the public (and parties) may:

- **Examine** the objection.
- **Request a copy** of any document submitted in support of or in opposition to the objection.

#### Timeframe:
- Begins on the day the **notice of inquiry** is published in the *Canada Gazette*.
- Ends on the day the **Minister issues or decides not to issue** the letters patent (official approval of the bank).

#### Location:
- Examinations and requests may be made at:
  - The **Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions in Ottawa**, or
  - The **location of the inquiry**, during its conduct.

> This promotes transparency and allows interested parties to review evidence before a final decision is made.

---

### **Distribution of Inquiry Report**

After the inquiry concludes:

- The **Superintendent prepares a report** of the findings.
- This report is **immediately sent to the parties**.
- It is then made available to the public by the **Minister**, as required under subsection 26(4) of the *Bank Act*.

> This ensures that all stakeholders receive timely, detailed information about the inquiryâ€™s outcome.
