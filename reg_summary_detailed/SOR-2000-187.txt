### **1. Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**
- The **Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food** has issued these regulations under **subsection 4(1) of the Agriculture and Agri-Food Administrative Monetary Penalties Act (the "Act")**.
- These regulations establish the **framework for administrative monetary penalties** (AMPs) for violations related to **human, animal, plant health, and environmental protection**, as well as **consumer protection** through enforcement of key agricultural and food safety legislation.

### **2. Definitions**
Key terms used throughout the regulations:

- **"Act"**: Refers to the *Agriculture and Agri-Food Administrative Monetary Penalties Act*.

### **3. Designated Violations Subject to Penalties**
Violations that may be prosecuted under the Act are defined in **Schedule 1**, divided into three categories:

#### **Category 1: Contravention of Provisions in Key Acts or Regulations**
- Any breach of:
  - The **Health of Animals Act**
  - The **Plant Protection Act**
  - The **Safe Food for Canadians Act**
  - OR any **regulation made under** those Acts
- Specifically listed in **Column 1 of any Division of any Part of Schedule 1**.

#### **Category 2: Violation of Ministerial Orders**
- Breach of an **order issued by the Minister under the Plant Protection Act**, specifically those listed in **Column 1 of Division 1 of Part 2 of Schedule 1**.

#### **Category 3: Refusal or Neglect of Duty**
- Failure to perform a duty imposed by or under:
  - The Health of Animals Act
  - The Plant Protection Act
  - The Safe Food for Canadians Act
  - OR any regulation under those Acts
- As specified in **Column 1 of any Division of any Part of Schedule 1**.

### **5. Classification of Violations: Minor, Serious, Very Serious**
Each violation is classified based on its severity using **Column 3 of Schedule 1**:
- **Minor violation**: Less serious, low risk, minimal harm.
- **Serious violation**: Significant risk, potential harm to health/environment or economic impact.
- **Very serious violation**: Severe consequences, including widespread harm or major financial loss.

> This classification determines the **penalty amount** and influences enforcement strategy.

### **7. Total Gravity Value Calculation (for Business/Financial Gain Violations)**

The penalty for serious or very serious violations involving business or financial benefit is **adjusted** based on a **total gravity value** derived from three criteria:

#### **Criteria and Associated Gravity Values (from Schedule 3):**

| Criterion | Source in Schedule 3 | Description |
|--------|------------------------|-----------|
| **(a) History of Prior Violations** | Part 1 of Schedule 3 | Includes prior convictions or violations. Higher history = higher gravity value. |
| **(b) Degree of Intention or Negligence** | Part 2 of Schedule 3 | Determines whether the violation was intentional, reckless, or negligent. |
| **(c) Harm Caused or Potentially Caused** | Part 3 of Schedule 3 | Assesses actual or potential harm to human/animal/plant health, environment, or persons. |

#### **Process:**
1. For each criterion, assign a **gravity value** from Column 1 of the corresponding part of Schedule 3.
2. Sum the values from all three criteria.
3. The **total gravity value** determines the final penalty amount (up to a maximum of $10,000).

> This ensures penalties are **proportional to the severity and context** of the violation.

### **8. Penalty Reduction for Compliance Agreements**

- If a person enters into a **compliance agreement** with the Minister:
  - The penalty amount is **reduced by $1 for every $2 spent** by the person.
  - **Maximum reduction**: Penalty can be reduced to **zero**.
- The agreement must include **specific corrective actions** to ensure future compliance.
- The request to enter into such an agreement must be made **within 30 days** of the notice being served.

> This encourages voluntary compliance and rehabilitation over punitive measures.

### **10. Payment of Penalties**

#### **General Payment Deadline (Section 9(1))**
- A person named in a notice of violation must pay the penalty **within 30 days** of service.

#### **Payment in Lieu of Penalty (Section 9(2))**
- A person may pay **half the penalty amount** instead of the full amount, **within 15 days** of service.

> This provides a **flexible compliance option** for individuals facing financial hardship.

#### **When Payment is Deemed Made**
- **By regular mail**: On the date indicated in the **postmark**.
- **By registered mail or courier**: On the date shown in the **receipt issued** by the post office or courier.

### **11. Right to Request Review or Compliance Agreement**

#### **Request for Review of Facts (Section 8)**
- A person may request a **review of the facts** of the violation by:
  - The Minister, or
  - The Tribunal (administrative review body)
- Must be submitted in **writing** within **30 days** of the notice being served.

#### **Request for Compliance Agreement (Section 9)**
- If the penalty is **$2,000 or more**, the person may request a **compliance agreement**.
- Must be submitted in writing within **30 days** of service.
- The request must include a **detailed proposal** of corrective actions to ensure future compliance.

> This allows for **structured remediation** rather than immediate enforcement.
