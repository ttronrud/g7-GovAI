### **Key Definitions Used in the Regulations**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Baggage** | Personal items brought into Canada for personal use (as defined in section 4). |
| **Beer** | Includes beer or malt liquor as defined in the Excise Act. |

| **Resident** | A person whose settled routine includes living, residing, and being ordinarily present in Canada. |
| **Temporary resident** | A non-resident who enters Canada temporarily for one of the following purposes: <br> - Studying at an educational institution; <br> - Employment for up to 36 months; <br> - Performing preclearance activities for the U.S. Government under the 2015 Land, Rail, Marine, and Air Transport Preclearance Agreement; <br> - Spouse, common-law partner, or dependant of someone in the above categories. |
| **Visitor** | A non-resident and non-temporary resident who enters Canada for a stay not exceeding 12 months. |

### **Eligibility and Conditions for Temporary Importation**

A non-resident may temporarily import baggage or conveyances classified under **tariff item 9803.00.00** (personal use goods) **only if**:

#### âœ… General Conditions:
1. **The baggage or conveyance is not used by a resident** of Canada.
2. **For conveyances**, they must **not be used** for:
   - Transporting passengers or goods for compensation;
   - Transporting goods for sale;
   - Soliciting sales or subscriptions on behalf of a Canadian business.
3. **For consumable goods** (e.g., alcohol, tobacco, vaping products), the following apply:
   - **For students or employees** (temporary residents): The goods must accompany the person upon **initial arrival in Canada**.
   - **For any other temporary resident**: Goods must accompany the person upon **initial arrival** or **return to Canada after absence abroad**.
   - **For visitors**: Goods must be declared at arrival with a **specific departure date** or a **series of visits** with a final departure date within 12 months.
4. **Non-consumable baggage or conveyances** must be **exported or destroyed under customs supervision** before the time limit in section 5 expires.
5. **Visitors** must produce a customs form (issued on a prior visit) if requested by a customs officer when arriving again during the 12-month period.

### **Duration of Stay for Imported Goods**

The time during which imported goods may remain in Canada depends on the **status of the person importing**:

| Importer Type | Duration Limit |
|--------------|----------------|
| **Visitor** | Until the **earlier of**: <br> - The date declared for departure (under subsection 3(e)(i) or (ii)), or <br> - 12 months after importation |
| **Temporary resident studying** (student) | Until the **day they complete their studies** at an educational institution |
| **Temporary resident working** (employee) | Until the **earlier of**: <br> - Completion of employment in Canada, or <br> - 36 months after arrival in Canada |
| **Temporary resident performing U.S. preclearance duties** | Until the **day they complete their employment with the U.S. Government** |

> ðŸ“Œ *Note: The 36-month limit applies only to employment, not to study or preclearance roles.*

### **Extension of Time Limits (Exceptional Cases)**

In cases where it is **impossible or impractical** for a visitor or temporary resident to meet the time requirements (e.g., due to medical issues, travel delays, or unforeseen circumstances), the **Minister of National Revenue may extend** the period the goods may remain in Canada:

| Case | Maximum Extension |
|------|-------------------|
| **Visitor** | Up to **18 months** after importation |
| **Temporary resident** (student or employee) | Up to **6 months after termination of studies or employment** |

> âœ… Extensions are granted at the discretion of the Minister and require justification.

---

### **Security Requirement**

The Minister may require **security** (in the form of **cash or a certified cheque**) for any imported baggage or conveyances under tariff item 9803.00.00.  
- The amount of security **cannot exceed** the duties that would have been payable if the tariff item had not applied.

> This is a safeguard to ensure that goods are not misused or retained beyond their intended temporary use.

### **Key Objectives and Policy Implications**

1. **Facilitates Temporary Travel**  
   The Regulations allow non-residents (students, workers, preclearance staff) to bring personal items into Canada without permanent importation status or full customs duties.

2. **Prevents Abuse**  
   Strict limits on alcohol, tobacco, and vaping products prevent misuse and ensure that only reasonable personal needs are met.

3. **Protects Canadian Tax Base**  
   By limiting the amount and duration of imports, the regulations prevent tax evasion or circumvention of excise laws.

4. **Supports International Cooperation**  
   The inclusion of preclearance workers reflects Canadaâ€™s cooperation with the U.S. under the 2015 Transport Preclearance Agreement.

5. **Ensures Accountability**  
   Requirements for declarations, forms, and extensions promote transparency and compliance.

### **Conclusion**

The *Non-residentsâ€™ Temporary Importation of Baggage and Conveyances Regulations* provide a **clear, structured, and balanced framework** for allowing non-residents to bring personal items into Canada temporarily. It strikes a balance between **practicality for travelers** and **regulatory control** over alcohol, tobacco, and other consumables.
