**Detailed Summary of the 2010 Olympic and Paralympic Winter Games Remission Order**

---

### **Overview**

The *2010 Olympic and Paralympic Winter Games Remission Order* is a regulatory instrument issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, under **section 115a of the Customs Tariff**. This Order provides **customs duty, excise tax, and Goods and Services Tax (GST) remissions** for goods and vehicles imported temporarily into Canada in connection with the **2010 Olympic Winter Games** (held February 12–28, 2010) and the **2010 Paralympic Winter Games** (held March 12–21, 2010), both hosted in and around **Vancouver and Whistler, British Columbia**.

The primary objective of this Order is to facilitate the smooth operation of the Games by enabling the importation of essential goods and equipment—such as media equipment, athlete gear, vehicles, and promotional items—without the burden of customs duties and GST, while ensuring compliance with Canadian trade laws.

> **Legal Authority**:  
> - S.C. 2005, c. 38, par. 142(e) and 145(2)(j)  
> - S.C. 1997, c. 36 (Customs Act and related provisions)

> **Exclusions**:  
> The Order **does not apply to alcoholic beverages or tobacco products**.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Authorized Importer of Goods Other Than Vehicles** | A broadcast rights holder, NOC, NPC, official sponsor, OBS S.A., OBS Vancouver, VANOC, or their agent/representative acting as importer of non-vehicle goods for the Games. |
| **Authorized Importer of Vehicles** | A broadcast rights holder, OBS Vancouver, VANOC, or an official vehicle importer, acting as importer of vehicles for the Games. |
| **Broadcast Rights Holder** | A person granted rights by the IOC (Olympics) or VANOC (Paralympics) to broadcast the Games. |
| **Games** | Refers to either the Olympic or Paralympic Games, as applicable. |
| **Games Family Member** | A non-resident individual holding a Games accreditation issued by VANOC and who is an athlete, coach, official, technician, judge, or employee of IOC, IPC, NOC, NPC, or international federation. |
| **Media Accreditation Holder** | A non-resident individual with media accreditation from VANOC to cover the Games. |
| **NOC / NPC** | National Olympic Committee / National Paralympic Committee of a participating country. |
| **Non-Resident Non-Registrant** | A foreign entity (e.g., IOC, IPC, broadcast rights holder, sponsor) not resident in Canada and not registered under subsection 123(1) of the Excise Tax Act. |
| **OBS S.A. / OBS Vancouver** | Olympic Broadcasting Services S.A. (IOC subsidiary) and its Vancouver subsidiary. |
| **Official Sponsor** | A party granted rights to use Olympic/Paralympic marks in exchange for financial or material support. |
| **Official Vehicle Importer** | A person designated by VANOC or OBS Vancouver to import Games-related vehicles. |
| **VANOC** | Vancouver Organizing Committee for the 2010 Olympic and Paralympic Winter Games (incorporated in 2003). |

## **Remission Provisions**

### **1. Remission for Games Family Members and Media Accreditation Holders**
- **Applies to**: Goods imported temporarily by a **Games family member** or **media accreditation holder**.
- **Conditions**:
  - Goods must be used **exclusively** by that individual for purposes related to the Games.
- **Tax Relief**:
  - Full remission of **customs duties** and **GST**.

> *Purpose*: To support athletes, officials, and media personnel with essential personal or professional equipment without incurring import taxes.

---

### **2. Remission for Authorized Importers of Goods (Non-Vehicles) and IOC/IPC**
- **Applies to**:
  - Goods (other than vehicles) imported by an **authorized importer of goods**, or by the **IOC or IPC**, for exclusive use in connection with the Games.
- **Tax Relief**:
  - Remission of **customs duties**, **excise taxes**, and **GST** (as specified below).

#### **GST Calculation Rule**:
- **If imported by a non-resident non-registrant**:
  - Full remission of GST paid on the importation.
- **In all other cases**:
  - Remission equals the **difference** between:
    - Total GST paid on importation, and  
    - GST payable on **1/60 of the value of the goods per month** (or partial month) that the goods remain in Canada.

> *Example*: If goods worth $60,000 are imported and stay in Canada for 3 months, GST remitted = Total GST – (1/60 × $60,000 × 3) = Total GST – $3,000.

> *Purpose*: To prevent tax overpayment for goods used over time, especially those that are temporary and used during the Games period.

---

### **3. Remission for Low-Value Goods (Unit Value ≤ $60)**
- **Applies to**:
  - Goods imported by a **non-resident non-registrant** (excluding official vehicle importers), or by **authorized importers**, for **free distribution** in connection with the Games.
- **Conditions**:
  - Unit value must be **$60 or less**.
- **Tax Relief**:
  - Full remission of **customs duties** and **GST**.

> *Purpose*: To allow free distribution of promotional items, merchandise, or giveaways to the public or participants without tax burden.

---

### **4. Remission for Gifts and Awards**
- **Applies to**:
  - Goods imported by **VANOC** or a **Games family member**.
- **Conditions**:
  - Goods have a **unit value of $60 or less**.
  - Intended for distribution as:
    - Gifts or awards to:
      - A Games family member;
      - VANOC or its agent;
      - A resident of Canada participating in the Games;
      - A resident acting in an official capacity.
- **Tax Relief**:
  - Full remission of **customs duties** and **GST**.

> *Purpose*: To enable equitable and accessible distribution of awards and recognition to participants and staff.

---

### **5. Remission for Donated Goods (Athletic Equipment & Ancillary Items)**
- **Applies to**:
  - Goods imported by **VANOC** or an **official sponsor**.
- **Conditions**:
  - Goods must be:
    - Ancillary to hosting/staging the Games; or
    - Athletic equipment required by an athlete for training or competition and certified to meet **international competition standards**.
  - At the end of the Games, the goods must be:
    - Donated to one of the following:
      - Whistler Legacies Society (a BC-based not-for-profit);
      - A sports federation;
      - A registered charity;
      - An educational or public institution;
      - A municipality, town, or city.
  - The goods **must not be sold or disposed of within two years** after importation.

- **Tax Relief**:
  - Full remission of **customs duties**.

> *Purpose*: To ensure that high-quality, game-related equipment is preserved for long-term community benefit after the Games.

---

### **6. Remission for Uniform Clothing**
- **Applies to**:
  - Clothing supplied to **VANOC** by an **official sponsor**.
- **Conditions**:
  - Supplied **free of charge** to:
    - VANOC employees or Games volunteers.
  - Worn as **uniforms** during official duties.
  - Kept for **personal use** after the Games.
- **Tax Relief**:
  - Full remission of **customs duties**.

> *Purpose*: To support staff and volunteers with uniforms without incurring import costs.

---

## **Conditions for Remission**

All remissions under this Order are subject to the following **mandatory conditions**:

1. **Import Timing**:
   - Goods or vehicles must be imported **between January 1, 2008, and March 21, 2010**.

2. **Claim Deadline**:
   - A claim for remission must be submitted to the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** **within two years** of the day the goods were accounted for under **section 32 of the Customs Act**.

3. **Evidence Requirement**:
   - The importer must provide the **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)** with **evidence or information** proving entitlement to remission under the Order.

4. **Final Disposition of Goods/Vehicles**:
   - By **December 31, 2010**, the goods or vehicles must either:
     - Be **exported from Canada**, or  
     - Be **disposed of in a manner acceptable to the Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness**, at the importer’s expense.

> *Note*: This ensures that temporary imports do not remain in Canada indefinitely and are properly accounted for post
