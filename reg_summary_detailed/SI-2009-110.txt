**Detailed Summary of the Right of Permanent Residence Fees Remission Order**

---

### **Regulatory Background and Authority**

This regulation, formally issued under the authority of **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, is based on the recommendation of the **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration** and the **Treasury Board**, and is enacted pursuant to **subsection 23(2.1)a of the Financial Administration Act**. 

The purpose of this Order is to provide a **remission (exemption)** of the fees associated with the acquisition of permanent resident status under the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations (IRPR)* for specific individuals who have been directly involved in supporting Canada’s mission in Kandahar Province, Afghanistan.

The regulation was introduced in **1991** (S.C. 1991, c. 24, s. 7(2)) and is codified under **R.S., c. F-11**, reflecting its long-standing role in supporting humanitarian and diplomatic personnel.

---

### **Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined and used throughout the Order:

1. **Canadian Mission Worker**  
   A citizen or resident of Afghanistan who:
   - Works as an employee or independent contractor for a **federal department or agency**; or  
   - Works as an employee or independent contractor for a **person or entity** that has entered into a contract with a federal department or agency.  
   The work must be directly related to supporting the Canadian mission in **Kandahar Province**.

2. **Common-law Partner**  
   Defined in accordance with **subsection 1(1) of the Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations**. This refers to a person who lives with another in a relationship that constitutes a de facto marriage or partnership, as recognized under Canadian immigration law.

3. **Joint Referral Committee (JRC)**  
   A federal body established by the federal public administration, composed of senior officials. The JRC plays a critical role in verifying employment relationships and assessing claims of risk, injury, or death related to work in Kandahar.

4. **Spouse**  
   As defined in the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations*, this refers to a person legally recognized as a spouse under Canadian immigration standards (e.g., married or in a formal marital relationship).

---

### **Purpose of the Remission**

The Order provides a **remission of the fees** required under **subsection 303(1) of the IRPR** for individuals applying to obtain permanent resident status in Canada. These fees are typically charged when a person applies for permanent residence through family sponsorship or other pathways.

The remission is **not automatic** and is granted only to individuals who meet **strict eligibility criteria** tied to their connection with Canadian operations in Kandahar and the risks they or their spouses/common-law partners have faced.

---

### **Eligibility Criteria for Remission**

A person is eligible for fee remission **if they meet all of the following conditions**:

#### **Condition 1: Canadian Mission Worker with Risk or Injury**
- The applicant must be a **Canadian mission worker** who:
  - Carried out duties in **Kandahar Province** for **at least 12 months**, consecutive or non-consecutive, **since October 9, 2007**.
- Due to that work, the individual:
  - Faces **extraordinary and individualized risk** (e.g., life-threatening danger from conflict, violence, or instability), **or**
  - Has suffered a **non-accidental or non-natural injury** that:
    - Places their life at risk, or  
    - Prevents them from working.

#### **Condition 2: Spouse or Common-law Partner of a Canadian Mission Worker**
- The applicant must be a **citizen and resident of Afghanistan** and:
  - Be the **spouse or common-law partner** of a person who:
    - Is a Canadian mission worker who carried out duties in Kandahar for at least 12 months since October 9, 2007, **and**
    - Has faced **extraordinary and individualized risk**, or
    - Has suffered a **non-accidental or non-natural injury** that endangers life or prevents work.

#### **Condition 3: Spouse or Common-law Partner of a Mission Worker Who Died in the Line of Duty**
- The applicant must be a **citizen and resident of Afghanistan** and:
  - Be the **spouse or common-law partner** of a Canadian mission worker who:
    - Carried out duties in Kandahar for at least 12 months since October 9, 2007, **and**
    - Suffered a **non-accidental or non-natural death** as a result of that work.

> Note: The definition of “non-accidental or non-natural injury” implies harm not due to natural causes or accidents (e.g., trauma from violence, disease, or exposure to hazardous conditions).

---

### **Application and Review Process**

To qualify for remission, the applicant must:

1. **Submit an application** to the **Joint Referral Committee (JRC)** using the **official form** provided by the federal government.
   - The application must be submitted **on or before December 31, 2011**.
   - This deadline is critical — the Order is time-bound and no applications after this date are eligible.

2. **JRC Verification**
   - The JRC must confirm:
     - The **employment or contractual relationship** of the named Canadian mission worker.
     - The **validity of claims** regarding risk, injury, or death related to their duties in Kandahar.

3. **Immigration Application Approval**
   - The applicant’s **immigration application must be approved** by the **Department of Citizenship and Immigration** (now known as Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada – IRCC).

4. **Additional Evidence (if required)**
   - At the request of either the **JRC** or the **Department of Citizenship and Immigration**, the applicant must provide **any additional documentation or evidence** to support their claim.
   - This may include medical reports, employment records, witness statements, or official reports confirming risk or injury.

---

### **Key Features and Implications**

- **Humanitarian Focus**: The Order recognizes the dangers faced by Canadian mission workers and their families in high-risk environments such as Kandahar, where conflict and instability were prevalent after 2007.
- **Family Sponsorship Protection**: It extends protection to spouses and common-law partners, ensuring that family members of mission workers are not financially burdened by immigration fees due to the risks they face.
- **Time-Bound Eligibility**: The **December 31, 2011** deadline limits eligibility to individuals who applied within a specific window, emphasizing the importance of timely action.
- **Verification by Senior Officials**: The involvement of the JRC ensures that claims are thoroughly reviewed and verified, reducing the risk of fraud or improper claims.
- **Exclusion of General Cases**: This is not a broad policy for all Afghan nationals or all mission workers. It applies only to those with specific ties to Kandahar and documented risks or injuries.

---

### **Limitations and Scope**

- **Geographic Limitation**: Only applies to **Kandahar Province**, Afghanistan.
- **Time Limitation**: Only applies to mission workers who served **since October 9, 2007**.
- **Application Deadline**: Only individuals who submitted applications **by December 31, 2011** are eligible.
- **No Retroactive Application**: Applications filed after the deadline are not accepted.
- **No Automatic Remission**: Remission is conditional and requires full compliance with all procedural and evidentiary requirements.

---

### **Conclusion**

This Right of Permanent Residence Fees Remission Order serves as a **targeted, humanitarian measure** to support individuals who have been directly affected by the risks associated with Canada’s diplomatic and development work in Kandahar, Afghanistan. By waiving immigration fees for qualifying spouses, common-law partners, and mission workers, the Order acknowledges the dangers faced in that region and promotes family reunification and inclusion in Canada.

It reflects a balance between fiscal responsibility (remitting fees only when justified) and compassion (recognizing exceptional circumstances). However, due to its strict eligibility criteria and time-bound nature, it is a **limited and specific policy** rather than a general immigration support tool.

---

**Note**: This Order is no longer actively enforced in current immigration policy, as it was issued in 2011 and predates modern immigration frameworks. It may serve as a historical reference or precedent for future policies addressing risks faced by diplomatic and development personnel in conflict zones. For current eligibility, individuals should consult the most up-to-date guidance from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC).
