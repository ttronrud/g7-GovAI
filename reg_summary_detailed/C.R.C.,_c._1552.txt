**Detailed Summary of the Timber Marking Rules**

---

### **Overview**
The *Timber Marking Rules* establish the procedural and substantive requirements for the registration of timber marks in Canada. These rules govern how applicants submit applications, what documentation is required, how applications are processed, and the conditions under which a timber mark may be legally registered. The rules are designed to ensure clarity, authenticity, and enforceability of timber marks—distinctive identifiers used to mark timber products to indicate origin, quality, or ownership.

---

### **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

#### **1. Citation and General Reference**
- The regulation is officially titled the **Timber Marking Rules**.
- It applies to all applications for the registration of timber marks in Canada and governs the process from submission to registration.

---

#### **2. Form and Submission Requirements**
- An application to register a timber mark must be submitted in **duplicate** using **Form 1**.
- The application must be submitted **by correspondence** and addressed to:
  > *The Commissioner of Patents, Ottawa, Canada.*

> **Note:** This indicates that the process is administrative and conducted via postal communication, not in person or through a centralized digital portal.

---

#### **3. Correspondence Rules**
- **Scope of Communication:** Correspondence related to a specific application must:
  - Be **exclusively about that application**.
  - Not discuss unrelated matters (e.g., other patent applications or general business inquiries).
- **Authorized Parties:** Only the **applicant** or a person **duly authorized in writing by the applicant** may correspond on the matter.
- This ensures accountability, prevents unauthorized third-party involvement, and maintains the integrity of the application process.

---

#### **4. Format and Presentation of Documents**
- All documents (except drawings) must:
  - Be **clearly and legibly typewritten or printed**.
  - Be on sheets of **white paper** of **acceptable quality**.
  - Be of standard dimensions: **8 inches wide by 13 inches long**.
  - Include a **clear one-inch margin on the left-hand side** for readability and formatting consistency.

> **Special Requirement – "Ribbon Copy":**
> - One of the two copies of a typewritten document must be the **ribbon copy**.
> - This is likely a reference to a specific copy intended for official records or internal use (possibly for archival or verification purposes).

---

#### **5. Drawing Requirements**
- Drawings must be:
  - On sheets of **good-quality pure white paper** or **tracing cloth**.
  - Of the same dimensions: **8 inches wide × 13 inches long**.
  - Clearly drawn in **black lines**.
  - On a **sufficiently large scale** to be easily readable.
- **Prohibited Elements:**
  - The **title** of the timber mark or any **descriptive matter** (e.g., words explaining the mark) must **not appear** on any part of the drawing.
- **Permitted Element:**
  - Each drawing sheet may bear the **signature of the applicant or their authorized agent** in the **lower right-hand corner**.

> This ensures that the drawing remains a neutral, visual representation of the mark, avoiding confusion or misrepresentation.

---

#### **6. Assignment of Timber Marks**
- An assignment (transfer of ownership) of a registered timber mark must be:
  - The **original document**, or
  - A **typed or printed copy certified before a notary public** as a true and accurate copy.

> **Important Procedure:**
> - If the assignment is accompanied by:
>   - A **duplicate** of the original, or
>   - A **notarially certified copy**,  
>   then the Commissioner will return the duplicate or copy **with a certificate of registration**.
> - If the assignment is **not accompanied** by such a duplicate or certified copy:
>   - The Commissioner will **notify the person who submitted it** with:
>     - The **number** of the timber mark,
>     - The **date** of registration,
>     - And the **details of the mark** against which it was registered.

> This provision protects applicants from fraudulent or incomplete assignments and ensures that only properly authenticated transfers are recognized.

---

#### **7. Application Form Content (Form 1 – To Be Completed in Duplicate)**

The application form includes the following essential elements:

1. **Applicant’s Information:**
   - Full post office address(es) of the applicant(s) must be provided.

2. **Request for Registration:**
   - The applicant declares that they are requesting registration of a timber mark in their name.
   - The applicant **declares that the mark was not and is not in use by any other person** at the time of adoption.

3. **Description of the Timber Mark:**
   - A clear and accurate **description** of the timber mark must be included.
   - This description must be specific enough to distinguish the mark from others and must reflect its visual characteristics.

4. **Attachment of a Drawing:**
   - A **drawing of the timber mark** must be attached and included with the application.

5. **Signature and Witnesses:**
   - The application must be **signed by the applicant or their authorized agent**.
   - The signature must be made **in the presence of two witnesses**.
   - The witnesses must sign the application to verify the authenticity of the signature.

6. **Submission to the Commissioner:**
   - The completed form is submitted to:
     > *The Commissioner of Patents, Ottawa, Canada.*

---

### **Purpose and Legal Significance**
These rules serve several key functions:
- **Prevent fraud and misuse** of timber marks by requiring clear documentation and verification.
- **Ensure transparency and traceability** in the registration process.
- **Protect intellectual property rights** associated with distinctive timber marks (e.g., for branding, origin, or quality identification).
- **Support enforceability** of timber mark ownership through proper assignment and record-keeping.

---

### **Key Takeaways for Applicants**
| Requirement | Key Point |
|-----------|----------|
| **Form** | Must be submitted in duplicate on Form 1 |
| **Submission Method** | By mail only to the Commissioner of Patents, Ottawa |
| **Correspondence** | Must be limited to the application; only applicant or authorized agent may communicate |
| **Document Format** | Typewritten/print on 8x13" white paper with 1" left margin |
| **Drawings** | Black lines, clearly drawn, no titles or descriptions; signature only in lower right corner |
| **Assignment** | Must be original or notarized copy; duplicates must be returned with certificate |
| **Witness Requirement** | Two witnesses required for signature on Form 1 |

---

### **Conclusion**
The *Timber Marking Rules* provide a structured, formalized, and legally sound framework for the registration of timber marks in Canada. By emphasizing clarity, authenticity, and documentation integrity, the rules help prevent unauthorized use, ensure proper ownership transfer, and support the commercial and legal identification of timber products. They are particularly relevant for industries relying on brand identity, traceability, or quality assurance in timber goods.

> **Note:** These rules appear to be historical in nature and may reflect a bygone era of patent and trademark administration. Modern equivalents may now be governed under the *Canadian Intellectual Property Office (CIPO)* or *Trademarks Act*, depending on the context. However, this document remains a detailed and instructive example of how timber marks were formally registered in Canada.
