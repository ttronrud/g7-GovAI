### **3. Core Consumer Protections and Obligations**

#### âœ… **Statement of Account**
- An institution **must send a statement of account** to the borrower **after the last day of each billing cycle**.
- This ensures borrowers are informed of their balances, charges, and due dates in a timely and transparent manner.

#### âœ… **Minimum Payment Deadline**
- A borrower **cannot be required to make a minimum payment before 21 days after the last day of the billing cycle**.
- This prevents aggressive or premature collection pressures and gives consumers reasonable time to plan payments.

#### âœ… **Grace Period for Due Dates**
- If the due date for a minimum payment falls on a **Saturday or public holiday**, a payment made on the **next business day** is considered **on time**.
- This ensures fairness during non-working days.

#### âœ… **Interest-Free Payment on Full Balance**
- If a borrower pays the **entire outstanding balance in full** by the due date, **no interest is charged** on purchases made during that billing cycle.
- This encourages responsible use of credit and rewards timely repayment.

#### âœ… **Allocation of Overpayments (When Multiple Interest Rates Apply)**
When a credit card has different interest rates on different balances (e.g., cash advances vs. purchases), any overpayment beyond the minimum must be allocated using one of two methods:
1. **Highest rate first**: Allocate the excess payment to the balance with the highest interest rate, then move down in descending order.
2. **Proportional allocation**: Distribute the overpayment in proportion to each balance relative to the total outstanding balance.

### **4. Debt Collection Practices (Critical Consumer Protection Provisions)**

#### âœ… **Information to Be Provided to Debtors**
When contacting a debtor to collect a debt, institutions must inform them of:
- The **amount owed** and **type of debt**
- The **identity** or **unique identifier** of the person collecting the debt
- Their **relationship** to the institution

> This promotes transparency and prevents deception.

#### âœ… **Prohibited Communication Methods (Harassment Prohibitions)**

- Relatives
- Neighbors
- Friends or acquaintances

- The person has **guaranteed** to pay the debt and is being contacted in relation to that guarantee; **or**
