**Detailed Summary of the *Cree-Naskapi Band Elections Regulations* (S.C. 1984, c. 18)**

---

### **Overview**
The *Cree-Naskapi Band Elections Regulations* are a set of administrative rules established under the **Cree-Naskapi (of Quebec) Act** to govern the procedures for electing members of the council in Cree and Naskapi Bands in Quebec. These regulations provide a comprehensive, structured framework for conducting democratic elections‚Äîboth general elections and specific positions such as chief and councillors‚Äîensuring transparency, fairness, and cultural appropriateness within Indigenous governance systems.

The regulations were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development**, pursuant to specific statutory provisions (Sections 67, 78(3), and 198 of the Cree-Naskapi (of Quebec) Act).

These regulations may be cited as:

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Cree-Naskapi (of Quebec) Act* ‚Äì the foundational legislation governing the governance and electoral structure of Cree and Naskapi Bands. |
| **Band Election** | A general election or an election for a specific council member position (chief or councillor), as defined in Sections 74‚Äì77 of the Act. |
| **Electoral List** | A list of eligible voters in alphabetical order, maintained and updated by the Returning Officer. |
| **Land of the Band** | Refers to Category IA or IA-N lands designated for the band under the Act. |
| **Representative** | A person appointed under Section 27 to represent the band in certain administrative or electoral functions. |
| **Returning Officer** | The official appointed by the band (under subsection 71(1) of the Act) to oversee all aspects of the election process. |

## **Structure of the Council**

- The council of a band must consist of:
  - **One chief**
  - **Not fewer than four and not more than twelve councillors**
- The number of councillors is determined based on population: **one councillor for every 100 members** of the band.

> Example: A band with 500 members would have 5 councillors (rounded to the nearest whole number), not fewer than 4 and not more than 12.

---

## **Term of Office**

- The term of office for any council member is **two years**, beginning on the day **after** the election.

---

## **Initiating a Band Election**

Two types of elections may be called:

1. **By the Council**:  
   The band council may call an election and determine:
   - The **date of the poll**
   - Whether an **advance poll** (early voting) or **travelling poll** (voting outside the band‚Äôs land) will be held.

2. **By the Returning Officer (under Section 77)**:  
   If the Returning Officer initiates the election (e.g., due to vacancy or other reasons), they determine:
   - The **date of the poll**
   - Whether advance or travelling polls will occur

---

## **Nomination Meeting Process**

### **Timing**
- Must occur between **five clear days after** the posting of the notice of election and **five clear days before** the poll date.

### **Requirements**
- Must last **at least two hours**.
- May have **up to two sessions**; if insufficient candidates exist before the first session ends, the meeting may be adjourned and reconvened two days later.

### **Nomination Methods**
- **Written nomination**:  
  - Must be on a form (Schedule II)  
  - Signed by the candidate and **three other electors**  
  - Submitted to the Returning Officer **before** the nomination meeting

- **Oral nomination**:  
  - Proposed by one elector and seconded by another  
  - Candidate must **accept or refuse** the nomination **before the meeting ends**  
  - If absent, acceptance/refusal must be communicated via **telephone, radio, or other appropriate means**  
  - The Returning Officer must inform all attendees of the requirements for valid nominations

### **Key Rules**
- A person **cannot run for both chief and councillor** in the same election.
- If there is **only one candidate** for chief or if the number of councillor candidates equals or exceeds the number of positions, the position is filled by **acclamation** (unanimous recognition).
- If there are **multiple candidates**, a **poll is held** on the date specified in the election notice.

---

## **Polling Procedures**

### **Notice of Poll**
- Must be signed by the Returning Officer and posted:
  - In the **head office of the band**
  - In **one or more public places within the land of the band**
- Must also be sent to the **band secretary**

### **Polling Stations**
- Must be located **within the land of the band**
- Each station must have:
  - One **voting compartment** that allows secret voting
  - Clear **directions for voting**
  - Identical **pens/pencils** used across stations
  - Sufficient **ballots** and **ballot boxes**
  - A **poll book** per station for record-keeping

> Ballots are provided in two forms:
> - **Chief election ballot** (Schedule IV)
> - **Councillor election ballot** (Schedule V)

### **Ballot Requirements**
- Lists candidates in **alphabetical order**
- May include a candidate‚Äôs **common name** (e.g., ‚ÄúJohn Smith‚Äù instead of ‚ÄúJ.S.‚Äù)
- **No additional information** (no photos, biographies, or political statements)

### **Ballot Boxes**
- Made of **durable material**
- Equipped with a **padlock**
- Cannot be opened without unlocking ‚Äì ensures integrity

### **Polling Hours**
- Open for voting **continuously for at least 8 hours**, between **6 a.m. and 10 p.m.**
- In cases of **natural disaster, blackout, or extraordinary circumstances**, the Returning Officer may:
  - Postpone closing of the poll by up to **2 hours**
  - Postpone the entire poll by up to **4 weeks**
  - Interrupt and resume within **4 weeks**
- Any changes must be **reasonably communicated** to electors

---

## **Voting Process**

### **Candidate Representation**
- A candidate may appoint:
  - **One representative per polling station** during voting hours
  - **Two representatives** during vote counting

### **Voting Procedure**
- At the polling station:
  1. The Returning Officer verifies the elector‚Äôs name is on the **electoral list** and that they have not already voted.
  2. The officer initials the back of the ballot.
  3. The elector proceeds to a voting compartment to mark their choice.
  4. Only **one person** may be in a compartment at a time.

### **Assistance for Disabled Voters**
- Electors who cannot read or vote independently due to **blindness, language barriers, or physical disability** may be assisted:
  - By the Returning Officer
  - Or by a **friend or relative** (if accompanied)

### **Ballot Return and Handling**
- After marking, the ballot is returned to the Returning Officer.
- The officer checks whether it was **initialled**.
- If not initialled:
  - The ballot is marked ‚Äú**declined**‚Äù and placed in the ballot box.
- If damaged:
  - Marked ‚Äú**damaged**‚Äù and placed in the ballot box.
- An elector whose ballot was declined or damaged is **entitled to a replacement ballot**.

### **Poll Book Records**
- The Returning Officer must record in the poll book:
  - Name of each elector who received a ballot
  - If assistance was provided and the reason
  - If an elector left without returning the ballot

> Important: If an elector leaves without returning the ballot, they are **considered to have voted**.

> If the elector later returns the ballot, it is marked ‚Äúdeclined‚Äù and placed in the ballot box.

### **Voting at Closing Time**
- Electors present at the polling station at the **closing time** may vote **before** the station is closed.

---

## **Counting the Votes**

### **Ballots Rejected**
The Returning Officer **rejects** any ballot that:
- Was not initialled by the Returning Officer or deputy/assistant
- Is **blank**
- Has **more votes than positions available**
- Contains **marks identifying the voter**
- Has been marked ‚Äúdeclined‚Äù or ‚Äúdamaged‚Äù

### **Invalid Votes**
- Any vote that does **not clearly indicate the voter‚Äôs intention** is rejected.

### **Objections**
- If a candidate or representative objects to a vote count:
  - The Returning Officer must:
    - Decide the objection
    - Record it in the poll book
    - Write ‚Äúallowed,‚Äù ‚Äúallowed in part,‚Äù or ‚Äúrejected‚Äù on the ballot
    - Initial the ballot

### **Sorting Ballots**
- After counting:
  - Chief and councillor ballots are separated
  - Ballots are sorted into two lots:
    - **Allowed or allowed in part**
    - **Rejected**

---

## **Final Election Outcome**

### **Chief Election**
- The candidate with the **highest number of votes** is elected chief.

### **Councillor Elections**
- The candidates with the **highest number of votes**, up to the number of positions available, are elected.
