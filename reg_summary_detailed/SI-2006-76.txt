**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Regulation Reference:**  
S.C. 2003, c. 22, s. 207  
**Title:** Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act  
**Effective Date:** July 1, 2006  
**Issuing Authority:** Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister  

---

**Summary:**

This regulation formally transfers administrative control and supervision of specific components within the federal public administration from the **Privy Council Office (PCO)** to the **Treasury Board**.

Specifically, the regulation transfers to the Treasury Board the **control and supervision** of the **Regional Offices** located within the **Communications and Consultations Secretariat** of the Privy Council Office.

---

**Key Elements of the Transfer:**

1. **Scope of Transfer:**  
   - The transfer applies only to the **Regional Offices** under the **Communications and Consultations Secretariat**.  
   - These offices are responsible for regional coordination, public engagement, and communication functions related to federal policy and government operations.

2. **Functional Context:**  
   - The **Communications and Consultations Secretariat** historically served as a central unit within the Privy Council Office to manage public communications, consultation processes, and outreach to stakeholders across Canada.  
   - The Regional Offices functioned as regional extensions of this secretariat, facilitating dialogue between federal government and regional communities.

3. **Transfer of Authority:**  
   - The **Treasury Board**, as the principal body responsible for managing federal public administration, personnel, and the overall performance of federal departments and agencies, assumes responsibility for:
     - Oversight of operations
     - Financial management
     - Performance monitoring
     - Human resources and administrative policies
     - Accountability and compliance

4. **Rationale (Implied):**  
   - The transfer reflects a broader administrative restructuring aimed at improving efficiency, accountability, and coordination within federal public service operations.  
   - By placing regional public engagement functions under the Treasury Board, the government seeks to align these activities more closely with broader public service governance, performance standards, and departmental accountability frameworks.

5. **Effective Date:**  
   - The transfer became effective on **July 1, 2006**, meaning all administrative, operational, and supervisory responsibilities for the Regional Offices in the Communications and Consultations Secretariat were transferred to the Treasury Board on that date.

---

**Conclusion:**

This regulation is a formal administrative reorganization under the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act. It transfers the operational and supervisory control of the Regional Offices within the Communications and Consultations Secretariat from the Privy Council Office to the Treasury Board, effective July 1, 2006. The change enhances centralized oversight and aligns regional public engagement activities with broader federal public service governance objectives.
