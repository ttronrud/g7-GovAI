### **Detailed Summary of the Rules of Procedure for Boards of Review under the Canadian Environmental Protection Act, 1999 (CEPA)**

The regulation in question is a formal set of procedural rules issued by the **Minister of the Environment** under **section 341 of the Canadian Environmental Protection Act, 1999 (CEPA)**, in accordance with the earlier publication of proposed rules in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **July 27, 2002**. These rules govern the operations of **Boards of Review** established under **section 333 of CEPA** to conduct inquiries into environmental matters, particularly when a notice of objection is filed under section 332(1).

This summary provides a comprehensive, structured breakdown of the key provisions, objectives, definitions, procedural requirements, and enforcement mechanisms within the Rules of Procedure.

---

## **1. Legal Basis and Purpose**

- **Statutory Authority**:  
  The rules are made pursuant to:
  - **Section 332(1)**: Allows the Minister to publish proposed rules for public comment.
  - **Section 341**: Grants the Minister the authority to adopt and promulgate final rules for the operation of Boards of Review.

- **Purpose**:  
  To establish a fair, transparent, and efficient process for reviewing objections to decisions made by the Minister under CEPA — particularly those involving the regulation of substances or activities that may pose environmental or health risks.

- **Public Participation**:  
  The initial proposal was published to allow stakeholders to submit comments or file **notices of objection**, requesting that a Board of Review be established to examine the merits of a regulatory decision.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Canadian Environmental Protection Act, 1999* (S.C. 1999, c. 33). |
| **Applicant** | A person who has filed a notice of objection under the Act. |
| **Board** | A Board of Review established under section 333 of the Act. |
| **Interested Person** | A government, association, or individual with a legitimate, non-frivolous or vexatious interest in the outcome of the inquiry. |
| **Minister** | The Minister or Ministers responsible for establishing a Board under section 333. |
| **Party** | Includes both the applicant and any interested person who has obtained leave to intervene under section 10 and is granted the opportunity to appear before the Board. |

> *Note*: These definitions clarify who has standing to participate in the inquiry and what constitutes a valid interest.

---

## **3. Scope and Flexibility of the Rules**

- **Applicability**:  
  The rules apply generally to all inquiries conducted by Boards of Review, **except where specific rules are made by the Minister for a particular inquiry under section 341**.

- **Flexibility Clause**:  
  At any time during an inquiry, if it is **just, fair, and in the public interest**, the Board may:
  - Vary or dispense with any provision of these rules.
  - Modify procedures to suit the circumstances of the case.

- **Notification Requirement**:  
  Any variation or dispensation must be:
  - Notified **without delay** to the **Minister and all parties**.
  - Accompanied by **clear directions** on how the modified procedures will be implemented.

> This ensures accountability and transparency while allowing adaptability to complex or unique cases.

---

## **4. Quorum and Attendance**

- A **majority of the Board members** constitutes a **quorum**.
- The **absence of one or more members does not invalidate** the Board’s proceedings or prevent action by the quorum.

> This ensures that decisions can be made even if not all members are present.

---

## **5. Time Periods**

- All timeframes (e.g., deadlines for submissions, hearings) are calculated as **consecutive calendar days**.
- This avoids ambiguity and ensures consistency in scheduling and compliance.

---

## **6. Pre-Hearing Conferences**

The Board may direct a **conference** (formal meeting) to address a range of issues, including:

- Clarifying and simplifying the issues under review.
- Admitting or verifying facts via affidavit.
- Discussing the use of public documents.
- Determining procedural arrangements (e.g., format of hearing).
- Exchanging written submissions, exhibits, and other materials.
- Identifying content that may contain **confidential information**.
- Determining:
  - Who may access confidential information.
  - The number of witnesses or time limits for evidence presentation.
  - Whether electronic communication (e.g., teleconferences, videoconferences) can be used.
- Resolving any other relevant procedural matters.

> These conferences promote early collaboration, reduce procedural delays, and help ensure a more efficient and informed inquiry.

---

## **7. Notice of Hearing**

- The Board must provide **written notice of the hearing** to:
  - The Minister
  - All parties

- The notice must include:
  - **Subject matter** of the inquiry
  - **Time and place** of the hearing
  - Instructions on how to **present evidence or make representations**
  - Whether the hearing will be **oral, written, or a combination**

> This ensures all parties are fully informed and prepared.

---

## **8. Pre-Hearing Submissions**

### **General Submission Requirement**
- At least **7 days before the hearing**, the Minister or a party must file a **written submission** that includes:
  - A **summary of facts and evidence** to be presented.
  - The **names of witnesses** and a **summary of the evidence** each will offer.

### **Expert Witness Reports**
- A party intending to introduce **expert witness testimony** must serve a **report** on the Minister and other parties and file it with the Board at least:
  - **20 days before the hearing begins** (if responding to a report).
- If a party wishes to **rebut** a report submitted by another party, they must serve a report of the expert at least **10 days before the hearing**.

> Reports must include:
> - The **expert’s qualifications**
> - A **full statement of the evidence** to be presented

### **Minister’s Expert Reports**
- If the Minister intends to use expert testimony, they must serve and file the report within the same timeframes as above.

> These requirements ensure transparency and allow all parties to prepare adequately and evaluate expert input.

---

## **9. Intervenor Status**

An **interested person** may apply to intervene in the inquiry by filing a written application with the Board and serving it on the Minister and all parties. The application must:

- State whether the person intends to **appear at the hearing**.
- Establish that the person has a **genuine, non-frivolous interest** in the outcome.
- Specify the **issues** they intend to address.

### **Board’s Considerations in Granting Intervenor Status**
The Board must consider:
- The **nature of the inquiry** and its issues.
- Whether the person has a **genuine interest**.
- Whether the person can make a **useful and different contribution** to the Board’s understanding.
- Any **delay or prejudice** caused by the intervention.

### **Opportunity for Representation**
- The parties and the Minister are given the opportunity to make representations on whether intervenor status should be granted.

### **Notification**
- The Board must **notify the Minister and parties** of its decision.

### **Access to Prior Information**
- The Board may require the Minister or a party to provide an intervenor with **information or evidence** previously submitted to the Board.

> This mechanism ensures that stakeholders with legitimate interests can participate meaningfully.

---

## **10. Representation at Hearings**

- The **Minister, parties, or witnesses** may be represented by **counsel**.
- On motion, the Board may authorize an **agent** (e.g., a representative or advisor) to represent a party or witness, provided the agent is **competent** to do so.

> This supports legal representation and ensures parties can effectively advocate their positions.

---

## **11. Mode of Evidence Submission**

- Evidence may be accepted in various forms:
  - In person
  - In writing
  - In electronic form (including **teleconference or videoconference**)

> This promotes accessibility and efficiency, especially for remote participants.

---

## **12. Service and Filing of Documents**

- Documents may be served or filed via:
  - Hand delivery
  - Mail
  - Electronic means (e.g., email, secure portal)

- **Electronic service**:
  - Requires a **paper copy** to be provided to the recipient **within seven days** of electronic service.
  - The recipient may **waive** this requirement in writing.

- **Date of service** is defined as the date the document is **received** by the person being served or their authorized representative.

> This ensures clarity and enforceability in service.

---

## **13. Public Access and Confidentiality**

- **Public Hearings**:  
  Unless information is treated as **confidential** under CEPA (paragraphs 52(a), (b), or (c)) or contains **personal information** as defined in the *Privacy Act*, all hearings are **public**.

- **Public Record**:  
  All information provided to the Board for the inquiry is placed on the **public record**.

> This promotes transparency and accountability in environmental decision-making.

> **Exceptions**:
> - Confidential information (e.g., trade secrets, sensitive personal data)
> - Personal information protected under the *Privacy Act*
