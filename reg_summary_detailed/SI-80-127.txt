### **Overview**

**Order No. NP-MO-1-80**, issued by the **Northern Pipeline Agency** on **June 12, 1980**, is a regulatory decision under the **Northern Pipeline Act (NPA)** that modifies the terms and conditions governing the operation of the **Foothills Pipe Lines (Alta.) Ltd. (Foothills)** pipeline in **Alberta**. This order specifically:

- **Rescinds** certain environmental, social, and economic undertakings previously deemed part of Schedule III to the Northern Pipeline Act;
- **Adds new, comprehensive socio-economic and environmental terms and conditions** tailored to the Province of Alberta;
- Reaffirms the obligations of Foothills to comply with both federal and provincial laws;
- Establishes detailed requirements for public consultation, environmental protection, community engagement, employment equity, emergency preparedness, and reporting.

This order was issued in response to public interest concerns and evolving policy priorities regarding environmental stewardship, Indigenous rights, community development, and accountability in large-scale infrastructure projects.

---

## **Key Background and Context**

1. **Certificate of Public Convenience and Necessity (CPCN):**
   - The certificate was originally issued on **April 13, 1978**, to Foothills Pipe Lines (Alta.) Ltd. under the Northern Pipeline Act.
   - It authorized construction and operation of a pipeline segment within **Alberta**, as detailed in **Annex I of Schedule I** to the Act.

2. **Public Hearings and Undertakings (1976â€“1977):**
   - A public hearing was held in **Ottawa, Inuvik, Whitehorse, and Yellowknife** (1976â€“1977) involving multiple pipeline companies, including Foothills (Yukon), Alberta Gas Trunk Line, Westcoast Transmission, and others.
   - These hearings addressed the **"Alaska Highway Pipeline Project"**, and companies made various **social, economic, and environmental undertakings**.
   - Under **subsection 21(2) of the Northern Pipeline Act**, such undertakings are deemed to be **terms or conditions** in **Schedule III** of the Act, binding on each company in relation to its specific route.

3. **Subsection 20(4) Authority:**
   - The Designated Officer (Northern Pipeline Agency) has the power, with **Governor in Council approval**, to **rescind, amend, or add** terms and conditions in Schedule III.
   - This order exercises that power to **revise and expand** the conditions applicable to Foothills (Alta.) in Alberta.

### **2. Addition of New Terms and Conditions**
A new set of **socio-economic and environmental terms and conditions** is added to **Schedule III** of the Northern Pipeline Act, specifically for **Foothills (Alta.)** in **Alberta**.

These conditions are titled:  
> **"Northern Pipeline Socio-Economic and Environmental Terms and Conditions for the Province of Alberta"**

They are binding on Foothills during **construction and operation** of the pipeline in Alberta, unless otherwise specified.

## **Core Components of the New Terms and Conditions**

### **1. Definitions**
Key terms are clearly defined to ensure legal clarity and consistency:
- **Agricultural land**: Land used for crops or livestock.
- **Big game animals, fur-bearing animals, fur-bearing carnivores**: As defined in Albertaâ€™s *Wildlife Act*.
- **Borrow pit**: Site for excavation of granular material.
- **Environmental impact**: Any change in physical or biological conditions due to construction or operation.
- **Facility**: Temporary or permanent fixed installations.
- **Fuel**: All petroleum products used in pipeline activities.
- **Hazardous material**: Substances that could cause environmental harm if spilled or misused.
- **Indian**: As per the *Indian Act*.
- **Inspection & Monitoring**: Verification of compliance with design and conditions; data collection on environmental changes.
- **Operation**: Activities after leave-to-open is granted.
- **Visual resources**: Landscape features (land, water, vegetation, wildlife).
- **Waste**: Discarded materials including oil drums, ashes, human waste.
- **Waterbodies**: Lakes, rivers, streams.
- **Wildlife**: Includes native species, migratory birds, and specific bird orders.

> All other terms retain their meaning as defined in the Act.
