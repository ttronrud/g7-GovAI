**Detailed Summary of the Exemption from Restrictions on Investments (Banks, Bank Holding Companies and Foreign Banks) Regulations**

---

### **Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**

The regulations are issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of Finance**, under specific provisions of the **Bank Act**:

- **Paragraphs 474(a)a and (b)a** of the Bank Act  
- **Paragraph 522.23(b)b** of the Bank Act  

These provisions provide the legal authority to exempt certain investment activities involving banks, bank holding companies, and foreign banks from specific restrictions that would otherwise apply under the Bank Act.

The regulations are codified under:
- **S.C. 2001, c. 9, s. 127** (Section 127 of the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada Act)
- **S.C. 2001, c. 9, s. 132** (Section 132 of the same Act)
- **S.C. 1991, c. 46** (Reference to prior regulatory framework)

---

For the purposes of **subparagraphs 468(3)(d)(ii)** and **930(3)(d)(ii)** of the Bank Act, the following provisions **do not apply** when determining whether a bank is permitted to acquire a **substantial investment** in another entity:

- **Subsections 468(4) to (6)** of the Bank Act

### **Purpose and Policy Rationale**

The primary objectives of these regulations are:

1. **To promote financial market efficiency and integration** by allowing banks (both domestic and foreign) greater flexibility in acquiring investments, especially in financial institutions.
2. **To support cross-border financial cooperation** by removing unnecessary barriers for foreign banks to invest in or control Canadian financial entities.
3. **To ensure consistency** between the Bank Actâ€™s investment rules and the broader financial stability framework, particularly in light of evolving financial market dynamics.
4. **To maintain prudential oversight** while allowing for strategic investments that support innovation, competition, and access to capital.

---

### **Limitations and Scope**

- The exemptions apply **only to specific types of transactions** under the Bank Act.
- The rules **do not eliminate all regulatory oversight** â€” other provisions of the Bank Act (e.g., capital adequacy, risk management, consumer protection) still apply.
- The exemptions are **targeted**, not general â€” they do not allow banks to bypass all restrictions on investments or control.
- The exemption applies **only when evaluating whether a transaction is permitted**, not to the actual conduct of the transaction.

### **Conclusion**

This regulation establishes a **targeted exemption** from restrictive provisions of the Bank Act that would otherwise limit a bankâ€™s ability to acquire a substantial investment in another entity â€” both domestically and internationally. It enhances the ability of Canadian banks and foreign banks to engage in strategic investments, while maintaining the integrity of financial regulation and systemic stability.
