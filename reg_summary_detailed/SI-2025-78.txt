The regulation you have provided appears to be a **government transfer of ministerial powers and responsibilities** under the **Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act**, specifically involving the reassignment of duties related to the **Federal Economic Development Agency for Southern Ontario (FEDAA)**.

### **Regulatory Text Summary**

> *"Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, under paragraph 2(a)a of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act, transfers from the Minister of Industry to the Minister of Artificial Intelligence and Digital Innovation the powers, duties and functions of the Minister of Industry in relation to the Federal Economic Development Agency for Southern Ontario."*  
> — *S.C. 2003, c. 22, s. 207; R.S., c. P-34*

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Basis**

- The transfer is authorized under **paragraph 2(a)a of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act** (commonly referred to as the "PSRTDA").
- This Act provides the legal framework for reorganizing government departments and transferring powers, duties, and functions between ministers, often to improve efficiency, align with strategic priorities, or reflect changes in government structure.
- The Governor General in Council (acting on the advice of the Prime Minister) exercises the authority to make such transfers, reflecting the constitutional convention that executive decisions are made by the government, with formal legislative backing.

### **2. Key Change in Ministerial Responsibility**

- **Transfer of Responsibility**:  
  The **Minister of Industry** is transferring all powers, duties, and functions related to the **Federal Economic Development Agency for Southern Ontario (FEDAA)** to the **Minister of Artificial Intelligence and Digital Innovation**.

- **Nature of the Transfer**:  
  This is not a change in the agency itself (FEDAA remains a federal agency), but rather a **shift in ministerial oversight**. The Minister of AI and Digital Innovation now holds the authority to:
  - Approve or direct policy decisions affecting FEDAA,
  - Allocate funding or resources,
  - Set strategic priorities for FEDAA’s operations,
  - Issue directives or interventions in the agency’s activities.
