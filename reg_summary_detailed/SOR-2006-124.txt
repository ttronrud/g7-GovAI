## ðŸ” **Comprehensive Summary of the Pest Control Products Regulations (S.C. 2002, c. 28)**

### ðŸ“Œ **Overview**
These regulations establish the legal framework governing the **manufacture, distribution, labeling, use, importation, and safety** of pest control products in Canada. They are issued under **Section 67 of the Pest Control Products Act (PCA)**, on the recommendation of the Minister of Health, and provide detailed definitions, exemptions, labeling requirements, and conditions for the use of pest control products.

The regulations aim to:
- Ensure the **safety** of pest control products for humans, animals, and the environment.
- Promote **clear labeling and transparency** in product information.
- Provide **exemptions** for certain low-risk or specific-use products.
- Regulate **importation** of foreign products with defined conditions.
- Protect public health and safety during transport, storage, and emergency response.

## ðŸ“š **What Constitutes a Pest Control Product?**

Under subsection 2(1) of the PCA, the following are classified as pest control products:

1. **Devices** that control, destroy, attract, repel pests, or mitigate pest-related harm.
2. **Compounds/substances** added to or used with pest control products to enhance or modify their physical/chemical properties or effects on host organisms.
3. **Active ingredients** used to control pests or mitigate pest damage.
4. **Treated articles** (inanimate products treated during manufacturing).
5. **Treated seeds** (seeds with pest control product incorporated or applied).

> âœ… This broad definition ensures that even non-agricultural uses (e.g., industrial cleaning, antifouling, disinfection) are covered.
