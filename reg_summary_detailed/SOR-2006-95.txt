**Detailed Summary of the Order Declaring an Amnesty Period (2006)**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Source**
This Order is issued under **subsection 117.14(1)a of the Criminal Code of Canada**, by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Justice**.  
It is formally published in the *Statutes of Canada* (S.C. 1995, c. 39, s. 139), which provides the statutory basis for amnesties related to firearms offences.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Order**
The primary purpose of this amnesty period is to provide a **legal and administrative pathway** for individuals who were in violation of Canadian firearms laws â€” specifically those involving the possession of non-restricted firearms without proper registration or licensing â€” to **rectify their status** without facing criminal prosecution or penalties during a defined time frame.

The amnesty aims to:
- Facilitate compliance with firearms legislation.
- Encourage individuals to legally register firearms or obtain proper licences.
- Allow safe and lawful disposal of firearms (e.g., destruction, export, transfer).
- Reduce the burden of enforcement for firearms-related offences that were committed during a specific historical period.

---

### **3. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Non-restricted firearm** | A firearm that is not classified as a *prohibited firearm* or a *restricted firearm*. This includes common types such as handguns, rifles, and shotguns that are legally owned under certain conditions. |
| **Public service agency** | As defined in *Section 1 of the Public Agents Firearms Regulations*, this refers to government agencies or institutions (e.g., police, fire departments, museums, or educational institutions) that are authorized to possess firearms under specific regulations. |

---

### **4. Who Is Covered by the Amnesty?**

An individual is eligible for the amnesty period if, **at any time between May 17, 2006, and May 16, 2015**, they meet **one of the following conditions**:

#### **Condition 1: Possession with a Valid Licence but No Registration Certificate**
- The individual is in possession of a **non-restricted firearm**.
- They **hold a licence to possess firearms** or a **licence to possess and acquire firearms**.
- However, they **do not hold a registration certificate** for that firearm.

> âœ… **Outcome**: The individual may obtain a registration certificate for the firearm.

#### **Condition 2: Possession Without a Licence or Registration, with an Expired or Expiring Licence**
- The individual is in possession of a **non-restricted firearm**.
- They **do not hold a registration certificate**.
- Their licence to possess or acquire firearms **expired during the period from January 1, 2004, to May 16, 2006**, **or** will have expired during the period from **May 17, 2006, to May 16, 2015**.

> âœ… **Outcome**: The individual may obtain both a **licence and a registration certificate** for the firearm.

> âš ï¸ Note: The expired licence must have been valid prior to the expiry date within the specified window.

---

### **5. What Actions Are Permitted During the Amnesty Period?**

Individuals covered by the amnesty may perform **one or more of the following actions** during the period (May 17, 2006 â€“ May 16, 2015):

| Action | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Obtain a registration certificate** | For individuals with a valid licence but no registration. |
| **Obtain a licence and registration certificate** | For individuals who previously held a valid licence that expired during the specified timeframe. |
| **Deactivate the firearm** | Physically modify the firearm so that it is no longer functional as a weapon (e.g., rendering it inoperable). |
| **Export the firearm** | Export the firearm to another country, provided all applicable legal requirements (including import/export laws of the destination country) are met. |
| **Turn in the firearm** | Surrender the firearm to a **police officer** or a **firearms officer** for **destruction or other lawful disposal**. |
| **Sell or give the firearm** | Transfer ownership to:  
- A **public service agency** (e.g., museum, police force),  
- A **business** that holds a licence to acquire firearms (e.g., gun shop, collector),  
- An **individual** who holds a valid **possession and acquisition licence** for firearms. |
| **Possess the firearm before performing one of the above actions** | The individual may legally possess the firearm **prior to** taking any of the above steps. |

> ðŸ“Œ All actions must be carried out **within the amnesty period** and in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

---

### **6. Amnesty Period Timeline**

- **Start Date**: May 17, 2006  
- **End Date**: May 16, 2015  
- **Duration**: Exactly 10 years

> This period is **closed** after May 16, 2015. Any actions taken after this date would not be eligible under this amnesty.

---

### **7. Effective Date**

- The Order comes into force on the **day it is officially registered** in the *Canada Gazette* (the official journal of federal laws).

---

### **8. Legal Significance and Implications**

- **No Criminal Liability**: Individuals covered by this amnesty are **not subject to prosecution** for the possession of a non-restricted firearm during the period, provided they comply with the conditions outlined.
- **Compliance Pathway**: It offers a structured and legal way for individuals to bring their firearms into compliance with the *Criminal Code* and the *Firearms Act*.
- **Historical Context**: The amnesty specifically targets a gap in licensing and registration that existed between 2004 and 2006, when many individuals may have lost their licences or failed to register firearms in time.
- **Public Safety Focus**: By enabling lawful disposal or registration, the Order supports public safety by reducing unlicensed, unregistered firearms in circulation.

---

### **9. Limitations and Exclusions**

- The amnesty **does not apply** to:
  - Prohibited firearms (e.g., military-grade weapons, certain handguns).
  - Restricted firearms (e.g., handguns, certain rifles).
  - Individuals who committed offences outside the defined conditions.
- The individual must have **legal possession** of the firearm at the time of the amnesty period.
- The individual must **not have been previously convicted** of a firearms offence that would otherwise bar them from participating in the amnesty.

---

### **Conclusion**

The **Amnesty Period (2006)** is a targeted, time-limited legislative measure designed to help individuals resolve past non-compliance with Canadian firearms laws. It provides a safe, legal, and enforceable pathway for individuals to:
- Register firearms,
- Obtain valid licences,
- Dispose of firearms lawfully (through destruction, export, or transfer),
- Avoid criminal charges for past violations.

By closing the gap in registration and licensing requirements during a critical period (2004â€“2015), this Order strengthens public safety and compliance with firearms legislation in Canada.
