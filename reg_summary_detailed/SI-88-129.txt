**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

This regulation establishes the deployment of Canadian military personnel to serve as part of the **United Nations Iran-Iraq Military Observer Group (UNIIMOG)**, in accordance with United Nations Security Council Resolution 619 (1988), and under Canada’s obligations as a contributing member state.

---

### **Background and Context**

1. **United Nations Security Council Resolution 619 (August 9, 1988)**  
   - The resolution authorized the establishment of the **UNIIMOG** to:
     - Verify, confirm, and supervise the **cease-fire** between Iran and Iraq.
     - Monitor and ensure the **withdrawal of military forces** by both parties from the conflict zone.
   - This was a critical step in maintaining peace and stability in the region following the protracted Iran-Iraq War (1980–1988), which had resulted in significant human and material costs.

2. **Canada’s Commitment to UNIIMOG**  
   - The **Secretary General of the United Nations** formally requested Canada to provide **military observers and communications personnel** to support the operations of UNIIMOG.
   - Canada accepted this request and committed to contributing personnel to support the mission’s objectives.

3. **Rationale for Canadian Involvement**  
   - It is deemed advisable for Canada to participate in the verification and supervision of the cease-fire and withdrawal of forces, consistent with international obligations and Canada’s role in global peacekeeping efforts.

---

### **Legal Authority**

The deployment is authorized under **paragraph 31(1)(b) of the National Defence Act**.  
This provision allows the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Minister of National Defence) to place Canadian Armed Forces personnel on active service **beyond Canada’s borders** for international peacekeeping, monitoring, or other operations, including those under United Nations mandates.

---

### **Key Provisions of the Regulation**

- **Personnel Deployed**:  
  The regulation authorizes the deployment of:
  - Officers and non-commissioned members (NCMs) from the **Regular Force** of the Canadian Forces.
  - Officers and NCMs from the **Reserve Force** of the Canadian Forces.

- **Deployment Purpose**:  
  These personnel will serve **as part of or in immediate support of UNIIMOG**. Their roles will include:
  - Observing and verifying compliance with the cease-fire agreement.
  - Confirming the withdrawal of military forces from the border areas.
  - Monitoring the demilitarization of zones and reporting any violations to the United Nations.
  - Providing communications support to ensure operational coordination and real-time reporting.

- **Scope of Service**:  
  The deployment is **beyond Canada**, meaning the personnel will be stationed in the Iran-Iraq conflict zone (primarily in the border regions) to carry out their duties under the UN mandate.

---

### **Governor General in Council’s Role**

- The Governor General in Council (the head of state, acting on the advice of the Minister of National Defence) formally **orders the placement of personnel on active service**.
- This action is a formal, legal mechanism to activate military personnel for international operations, ensuring accountability and adherence to domestic legal frameworks.

---

### **Significance and Implications**

- **International Peacekeeping Contribution**: Canada’s participation reflects its commitment to multilateral peace and security efforts through the United Nations.
- **Operational Flexibility**: The inclusion of both regular and reserve force personnel allows for a scalable and responsive deployment.
- **Diplomatic and Strategic Value**: Canada’s involvement enhances its role in regional stability and reinforces its image as a responsible global actor in conflict resolution.
- **Compliance with International Law**: The deployment aligns with the United Nations Charter and the principles of peacekeeping, including impartiality, neutrality, and non-use of force.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation formally authorizes Canada to deploy military observers and communications personnel to the Iran-Iraq border region as part of the UNIIMOG. It is grounded in international obligations under UN Security Council Resolution 619 and supported by domestic legal authority under the National Defence Act. The deployment aims to verify and supervise the cease-fire and withdrawal of military forces, contributing to regional peace and stability. It underscores Canada’s role in international peacekeeping and its commitment to upholding international law and humanitarian principles.
