**Detailed Summary of the Selkirk Marine Railway Dry Dock Regulations, 1989**

---

### **Overview**
The *Selkirk Marine Railway Dry Dock Regulations, 1989* (SOR/82-467, as amended) were enacted by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Public Works and the Treasury Board, pursuant to sections 24, 28, and 29 of the *Public Works Act*. These regulations replace the earlier *Selkirk Marine Railway Dry Dock Regulations* made in 1982 (P.C. 1982-1309) and establish a comprehensive framework governing the **management, operation, access, use, and financial obligations** related to the Marine Railway Dry Dock at Selkirk, Manitoba.

These regulations are designed to ensure the **safe, efficient, and accountable use** of a federally managed marine dry dock facility, while protecting public property, ensuring environmental and safety standards, and providing clear procedures for vessel entry, exit, repairs, and charges.

---

## **Key Definitions**

The regulations define several key terms to ensure clarity and consistency:

- **Agent**: The individual or entity responsible for submitting an application on behalf of a vessel.
- **Application**: A formal request for dry docking, structured in the form outlined in *Schedule I*.
- **Day**: A 24-hour period used for calculating dock charges.
- **Dock Charge**: Fees (tolls or dues) specified in *Schedule II*, Column II, for use of the dry dock or services provided.
- **Dry Dock**: The Marine Railway Dry Dock at Selkirk, Manitoba.
- **Dry Dock Property**: The land and associated government-owned infrastructure (e.g., cradles, cranes, gutters) used in connection with the dry dock, excluding leased property.
- **Entry Book**: A record-keeping log that documents authorized vessel entries, durations, and other operational details.
- **Gas Hazard Control Standards (TP 3177E)**: Safety standards issued by the Department of Transport to manage gas hazards in vessels undergoing repairs.
- **Gross Tonnage**: Measured according to the *International Convention on Tonnage Measurement of Ships, 1969*; otherwise, the "gross registered ton."
- **Manager**: The Department officer based in Edmonton, Alberta, responsible for overall dry dock operations and maintenance.
- **Superintendent**: The officer stationed at Selkirk responsible for day-to-day management and supervision of the dry dock and its property.
- **Winter Term**: A defined period from **November 1 to May 14** of each year.

---

## **Core Provisions and Operational Framework**

### **1. Authorization to Enter the Dry Dock**
- **No vessel may enter the dry dock without prior authorization from the Superintendent.**
- Authorization requires:
  - A completed and submitted application (Schedule I).
  - Agreement between the **agent** and **Superintendent** on:
    - The **date of entry**.
    - The **estimated duration** of stay.
  - The entry details must be **recorded in the Entry Book**.
  - The **agent must sign** the entry book.
  - The **dock charge** (from Schedule II, Item 1) must be paid.
  - A **cash deposit or bond** (with two sureties) may be required if the Superintendent deems it necessary.

> *Note:* The Superintendent may require a bond or deposit to cover potential damages or unpaid charges.

---

### **2. Consequences of Failure to Enter on Scheduled Date**
- If a vessel fails to enter on the agreed date:
  - Its name is **removed from the Entry Book**.
  - It **cannot re-enter** under the original authorization.
  - The agent must pay:
    - The **vacancy charge** (Schedule II, Item 2) for each day the dock remains unused.
    - Any applicable charge for **preparation services** (e.g., cleaning, pumping).

> *Exception:* This does not apply if:
> - The delay was due to **permission granted by the Superintendent for another vessel**, or
> - The delay was caused by **weather conditions**.

> *Re-entry possibility:* If the dry dock is available, the Superintendent may **re-record** the vessel’s entry with a new date.

---

### **3. Priority Access for Emergency Repairs**
- The Superintendent may allow a vessel to enter **before others** if:
  - It is in a condition requiring **immediate repair**.
- In such cases, the Superintendent must **report the reason** to the Manager.

---

### **4. Damage Liability**
- If damage occurs to the dry dock or property due to:
  - The vessel’s crew,
  - The agent or its employees,
  - Independent contractors or their employees,
  - Equipment failure by the agent or contractor,
- The agent must **deposit funds or post a bond** sufficient to cover the cost of damage.

---

### **5. Post-Entry Inspection and Duration Adjustment**
- Within **three days** after the dry dock is pumped out, the agent must:
  - Inspect the vessel.
  - **Revise the estimated duration of stay** if necessary.
  - Report the revised duration to the Superintendent for **recording in the Entry Book**.

---

### **6. Safety and Lighting Requirements**
- Vessels must display **adequate lighting** at **each end of every gangway** between sunset and sunrise.

---

### **7. Tugboat Availability**
- The agent must ensure **sufficient tugboats** are available to safely handle the vessel during entry and exit.

---

### **8. Duration of Stay**
- A vessel **cannot remain in the dry dock longer than**:
  - The original or revised estimated duration (from Entry Book or Section 8), or
  - Any additional time **permitted by the Superintendent** under Section 13.

> *Exception:* If repairs cannot be completed within the estimated time and **no other vessel is scheduled**, the Superintendent may allow **further time** to complete repairs.

> *Re-entry for repairs:* If a vessel leaves early (before repairs are complete), the agent must pay:
> - The **dock charge for re-entry** (Schedule II, Item 3).

> *Keel and bilge block installation:* A charge (Schedule II, Item 2) applies for **any re-entry** involving keel or bilge block installation.

---

### **9. Exit and Cleaning Requirements**
Before a vessel leaves the dry dock, the agent must:
1. Remove all **equipment, machinery (except permanently affixed items), materials, and scaffolding**.
2. Remove **all oil and residues** from gutters, sumps, valves, and pumps.
3. Remove **all sand from sandblasting** and other **refuse** generated during repairs.
4. Perform **any additional cleaning** required by the Superintendent.

> *Purpose:* Prevent environmental contamination and maintain the integrity of dry dock infrastructure.

---

### **10. Safety Restrictions for Hazardous Materials**
- **Explosives**: A vessel carrying explosives **cannot enter** the dry dock until the explosives are removed.
- **Oils with flash-point below 23°C**: A vessel that carried such oil on its last voyage must submit a **certificate from a marine chemist** (per Gas Hazard Control Standards) before entry.
> *Exception:* If the oil is in a **bunker tank** and no work is being done on the tank or its systems, this restriction does not apply.

> *Note:* Delays due to compliance with this rule result in a **vacancy charge** (Schedule II, Item 5) for each day the dry dock remains idle.

---

### **11. Use of Dry Dock Property (Keel/Bilge Blocks, etc.)**
- No person may use **keel blocks, bilge blocks, or other dry dock property** without **Superintendent authorization**.
- The Superintendent may authorize use **only if the property is available**.
- **Prohibited acts**:
  - Damaging or setting adrift any block, pole, shore, machine, pipe, or scaffolding.
  - Throwing any object or material from the cradle or walls.

---

### **12. Bunkering (Fueling) in the Dry Dock**
- **No bunkering is allowed without Superintendent authorization**.
- Authorization requires:
  - A **fire watch** at the fuelling connection.
  - All **work on the vessel’s exterior** must be suspended during bunkering.

---

### **13. Vessels Afloat or Moored**
- Any vessel **lying afloat or moored** (including at wharves or dry dock entrances) must be **ready to move immediately** upon request by the Superintendent.

---

### **14. Loading, Unloading, and Storage**
- **No person (other than authorized by the Superintendent)** may:
  - Load, unload, or place cargo or equipment on dry dock property.
  - Store cargo, building materials, equipment, or machinery destined for or originating from a vessel using the dry dock.
> *Exception:* The Superintendent may authorize storage **only if** the items are **necessary for repairing or building** a vessel.

---

### **15. Crew and Sanitation**
- If crew live on board:
  - All **sewage, garbage, sweepings, ashes, or refuse** must be removed from the dry dock property.
- The **entrances to water-closets and urinals** must be **locked or fastened**.
- **No person may use** the water-closets or urinals while the vessel is in the dry dock.

---
