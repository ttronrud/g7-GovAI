**Detailed Summary of the Repealed Regulation: Coal Mining Regulations (SOR/2003-116, s. 2)**

> **Note:** This regulation was **repealed** by *SOR/2003-116, section 2*, and is no longer in force. The summary below provides a comprehensive, detailed interpretation of its original content and purpose, as it existed prior to repeal.

---

## **Overview**

This regulation governs the **staking, licensing, permitting, and operation of coal mining activities** on **territorial lands in the Northwest Territories (NWT)**, prior to the 2003 repeal. It established a formal framework for how individuals, corporations, government entities, and Indigenous communities could explore for and mine coal, with clear rules on land staking, application procedures, fees, royalty obligations, surface rights, and operational restrictions.

It is important to note that this regulation **only applied to territorial lands under the jurisdiction of the Minister of Northern Affairs** and was **not applicable to lands outside of the NWT or to areas already under other regulatory regimes**.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |

| **Lessee** | Holder of a lease for coal mining. |
| **Licence** | A permit issued by the Chief to explore for coal on territorial lands. |
| **Licensee** | Holder of a licence to explore for coal. |

| **Mineral Claim Staking Sheet** | A standardized map used to define claim boundaries:<br>• Between 60°N and 68°N latitude: 15-minute latitude bands, 30-minute longitude bands.<br>• North of 68°N: 15-minute latitude bands, 1-degree longitude bands. |

## **Scope and Application**

- These regulations **only apply to territorial lands** under the control and administration of the Minister via the *Territorial Lands Act*.
- **No person may mine coal without authority under these regulations.**
- The regulations **do not affect rights or interests acquired before December 16, 1954**, ensuring continuity of pre-existing land rights.

---

## **Eligibility to Stake a Location**

- Any person **18 years of age or older**, whether acting individually or on behalf of others or a corporation, may stake a location for coal mining.
- **Excluded lands** are not available for staking:
  - Cemeteries
  - Municipal districts, municipalities, or local improvement districts
  - Lands reserved for Indian Reserves, national parks, game sanctuaries, military use, or public purposes
  - Lands reserved under the *Dominion Water Power Act*

---

## **Surface Rights and Consent Requirements**

- If surface rights are owned or occupied by another party, **prior consent or security** must be obtained from the owner/occupant before staking:
  - **Consent** (written or verbal) from the surface rights holder
  - Or **security** in an amount deemed adequate by the recorder (in money or bearer bonds of Canada or Canadian National Railway Company, unconditionally guaranteed by the Crown)
- The staker must **compensate** the surface rights owner or occupant for any loss or damage caused by staking.

---

## **Staking a Location – Form and Marking Requirements**

- The location must be staked as a **rectangle**, with **length not exceeding four times the width**.
- **Area limitations**:
  - For a **lease**: maximum **640 acres**
  - For a **permit**: maximum **1 acre**

### **Marking the Location (on the ground)**

Two methods are allowed:

#### **1. Timber Posts**
- Four posts at corners:
  - Made of **sound timber**, firmly placed in the ground or from a rooted tree
  - Squared/faced at least **18 inches from the top**, with each side measuring **at least 3 inches**
  - **Mounded with stone** (≥4 stones) or **earth** (conical, ≥2 ft diameter at base, ≥1 ft high)

#### **2. Metal Tubes**
- Four metal tubes (≥4 inches long, ½ inch diameter), closed at both ends
- Placed in the center of a mound:
  - **Stone mound** (≥4 stones)
  - **Earth mound** (conical, ≥3 ft diameter at base, ≥2 ft high)

### **Labeling the Corners**
- Northeast corner → **No. 1**
- Southeast corner → **No. 2**
- Southwest corner → **No. 3**
- Northwest corner → **No. 4**

### **Required Information on Markings**
- Number of post/tube
- Name of person staking
- Date and time of staking
- "CML" if applying for a **lease**
- "CMP" if applying for a **permit**

---

## **Boundary Marking for Visibility**

To ensure long-term recognition of boundaries:

- **Timbered areas**:
  - Mark every **100 feet** along the boundary
  - Three blazes per tree:
    - One facing the boundary
    - One on each side in the direction of the boundary
  - Underbrush must be cleared where necessary

- **Non-timbered areas**:
  - Clear brush where needed
  - Erect **cairns** (≥4 stones, conical, ≥2 ft diameter, ≥1 ft high) or **earth mounds** (same dimensions)

---

## **Application for Lease or Permit**

### **Time Limits for Application**
- If location is **within 10 miles** of the recorder’s office:
  - Must apply within **15 days** of staking
- If location is **more than 10 miles away**:
  - 15 days + **1 day per additional 10 miles** (or fraction thereof)
- If the deadline falls on a **Sunday or closed day**, the application may be submitted on the **next open day**

> **Applications submitted after the deadline are not valid.**

### **Priority in Disputes**
- In disputes over staking time, **priority is determined by the order in which the northeast corner post/tube was placed** (subject to validity of records and compliance with regulations).

---

## **Application Procedures**

### **For a Lease (Form 1)**
- Required documents:
  - Application in Form 1
  - **$5 fee**
  - Sketch of location
  - **First-year rental payment** (set at $1 per acre)

> The recorder may inspect the location and forward the application to the Chief if compliant.

### **Lease Issuance and Terms**
- The Minister may issue a lease for **21 years** at **$1 per acre annually**, payable in advance.
- **Renewal** is possible for additional 21-year terms if the lessee proves compliance with lease conditions.
- **Royalty**: $0.10 per ton of **merchantable coal** mined.
- **Prohibitions**:
  - No assignment, transfer, or subletting without **written Ministerial consent**
  - No mining within **60 feet** of a location boundary

### **Lessee Rights**
- Full right to **coal on leased land**
- Right to enter and use the surface land **only for efficient mining operations**
- Must **compensate** surface rights owners for any loss or damage

### **Annual Reporting and Royalty Payments**
- Within **one month after each year**, submit a **sworn statement** showing:
  - Amount of coal extracted
  - Pay accrued royalties to the recorder

### **Mandatory Operations Start**
- Must begin active operations within **one year** of receiving notice from the Minister to start.
- The Minister will specify a **minimum annual coal production** (maximum of **10 tons per acre per year**).
- This quantity may be increased with **30-day notice**.
- If operations are not started or coal not produced, the lease may be **cancelled at the Minister’s discretion**.

### **Assignment and Abandonment**
- A lessee may **assign** the lease:
  - Up to the date of Ministerial consent under section 16
- May **abandon** the lease:
  - Up to the date the Minister is notified in writing of abandonment

### **Expenditure Waiver for Remote Areas**
- If a lessee operates on land **more than 10 miles from a railway** and can prove:
  - Actual prospecting and development costs incurred **before railway access**
  - Amounts equal to or exceeding the **annual rental**
- The Minister may **waive rental payments** for that year or apply the payment toward future rentals.
- **Limitation**: Such expenditures can count toward rental waiver for **no more than five years** of the lease term.

### **Shared Expenditure Across Locations**
- If a lessee holds multiple adjoining locations, **expenditures on one location** may be applied
