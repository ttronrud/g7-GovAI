### **Regulatory Summary: Transfer of Powers Under the Canadian Tourism Commission Act**

#### **1. Authority and Legal Basis**
- The regulation is issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Prime Minister**.
- It is grounded in **paragraph 2(a)(a) of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act** (commonly referred to as the "Public Service Rearrangement Act").
- This provision allows for the transfer of powers, duties, and functions between federal ministers within the Public Service, typically to realign responsibilities based on policy priorities or administrative efficiency.

> *Legal Reference:*  
> - **S.C. 2003, c. 22, s. 207** (Statutory authority for ministerial transfers)

#### **2. Nature of the Transfer**
- The **powers, duties, and functions** previously held by the **Minister of Economic Development and Official Languages** under the **Canadian Tourism Commission Act** are being **transferred** to a new ministerial office.
- The receiving minister is the **Minister of State (Tourism) and Associate Minister of Finance**.

> This transfer is **not a creation of new legislation**, but a reassignment of existing responsibilities within the existing legal framework of the Canadian Tourism Commission Act.

---

#### **3. Historical Context of the Transfer**
- The powers in question were **originally transferred** from the Minister of Economic Development and Official Languages to the **Minister of State (Tourism)** via **Order in Council P.C. 2019-1368**, issued on **November 20, 2019**.
- This earlier Order in Council established the initial reallocation of tourism-related responsibilities.
- The current regulation **confirms and formalizes** that transfer under the broader authority of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act.

> *Note:* The current regulation serves as a **reaffirmation or updating** of the 2019 transfer, ensuring continuity and legal clarity in ministerial responsibilities.

---

#### **4. Scope of the Transfer**
The transfer includes:
- All **powers** granted to the Minister of Economic Development and Official Languages under the *Canadian Tourism Commission Act*.
- All **duties and functions** associated with the operation, oversight, and strategic direction of the **Canadian Tourism Commission (CTC)**.
- This includes responsibilities such as:
  - Promoting Canadian tourism domestically and internationally.
  - Coordinating tourism policy across government departments.
  - Managing the CTC’s budget, programs, and public engagement.
  - Liaising with provinces, territories, and private sector stakeholders.

> The transfer does **not** include the powers related to official languages, which were previously under the jurisdiction of the Minister of Economic Development and Official Languages, unless specifically re-assigned in this context.

---

#### **5. Rationale and Implications**
- The reallocation reflects a **strategic shift in government priorities**, emphasizing tourism as a key economic driver.
- By assigning tourism responsibilities to the **Minister of State (Tourism)**—who is also an Associate Minister of Finance—there is a **cross-functional integration** of tourism policy with fiscal planning and economic development.
- This enhances coordination between tourism promotion and financial oversight, potentially improving the efficiency and impact of tourism-related investments.

---

#### **6. Legal and Administrative Significance**
- The transfer is **legally binding** and effective upon publication in the Canada Gazette.
- It ensures **clarity in ministerial accountability** and avoids ambiguity in who is responsible for tourism policy and implementation.
- It operates within the established framework of federal ministerial restructuring, which allows for flexibility in responding to changing economic and policy landscapes.

---

### **Conclusion**
This regulation formally transfers the powers, duties, and functions of the **Minister of Economic Development and Official Languages** related to the **Canadian Tourism Commission** to the **Minister of State (Tourism) and Associate Minister of Finance**, pursuant to the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act. The transfer is a continuation and formalization of a prior Order in Council (P.C. 2019-1368), reflecting a strategic realignment to strengthen the federal government’s capacity to promote and manage Canada’s tourism sector through integrated policy and financial oversight.
