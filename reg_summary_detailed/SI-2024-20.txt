**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Title:**  
*Remission Order in Respect of Certain Fees for the Issuance of Identity and Travel Documents (Russian Invasion of Ukraine)*

**Legal Basis:**  
This regulation is issued under **subsection 23(2.1)a of the Financial Administration Act** (S.C. 1991, c. 24, s. 7(2); R.S., c. F-11), which empowers the Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Treasury Board, the Minister of Citizenship and Immigration, and the Minister of Foreign Affairs) to make remission orders for certain fees related to the issuance of identity and travel documents in the public interest.

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**
The primary purpose of this remission order is to **provide financial relief to individuals affected by the Russian invasion of Ukraine** by waiving specific fees associated with the issuance of identity and travel documents. This measure supports the humanitarian response to the crisis, enabling urgent travel for Canadians and Ukrainian nationals who were displaced or required immediate movement due to the conflict.

---

### **Definition of "Document"**
The term *document* includes the following:
1. **Passport** – as defined in Section 2 of the *Canadian Passport Order*.
2. **Certificate of Citizenship** – as defined in subsection 2(1) of the *Citizenship Act*.
3. **Travel document issued to a permanent resident** – under subsection 31(3) of the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act*.
4. **Emergency travel document** – issued in urgent circumstances.

These are the types of documents for which fees may be remitted.

---

### **Fees Subject to Remission**
Remission is granted for fees payable under the following regulatory provisions:

#### 1. **Passport and Other Travel Document Services Fees Regulations**
- Paragraphs **7(c)**, **9(a)** and **(b)**, and **items 10 and 14** of the schedule.

#### 2. **Consular Services Fees Regulations**
- **Section 4** (fees for consular services).

#### 3. **Citizenship Regulations**
- **Item 6** of the schedule (fees related to citizenship applications).

#### 4. **Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations**
- **Section 315** (fees for travel documents for permanent residents).
- Subsections **296(1), 298(1), 299(1), 300(1), 303.2(1), 305(1), and 315.1(1)** – which cover fees related to various applications and travel documents under immigration and refugee provisions.

---

### **Conditions for Remission**

#### **For Fees under Paragraphs 2(a) to (d)**  
*Applicable to Canadian citizens and permanent residents who were impacted by the Russian invasion of Ukraine.*

**Eligibility Conditions:**
- The person must be a **Canadian citizen or a permanent resident**.
- The individual must have been **in Ukraine or another country** and had to travel **on an urgent basis** as a direct result of the Russian invasion of Ukraine.
- **In the case of paragraph 2(d)** (fees for travel *to Canada*), the person must be traveling to Canada.
- The application for a document must have been submitted **between February 22, 2022, and March 31, 2023**, either:
  - **Electronically**, or  
  - **In person** at one of the following Canadian diplomatic missions:
    - Warsaw, Poland  
    - Bucharest, Romania  
    - Budapest, Hungary  
    - Vienna, Austria  
- The fees in question **must not have already been remitted** under **Section 11** of the *Passport and Other Travel Document Services Fees Regulations* (i.e., this is a first-time remission).

> ⚠️ *Note:* This provision ensures that only those who applied during the specified time window and through the designated consular offices are eligible.

---

#### **For Fees under Paragraph 2(e)**  
*Applicable specifically to Ukrainian nationals.*

**Eligibility Conditions:**
- The application must have been submitted by a **Ukrainian national** to the **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration**.
- The application must have been made **between February 22, 2022, and March 14, 2022**.

> ⚠️ *Note:* This provision is more narrowly focused and applies only to Ukrainian nationals applying during the first phase of the crisis, with a strict end date of March 14, 2022.

---

### **Key Features and Implications**

| Feature | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Humanitarian Focus** | Designed to respond to urgent needs caused by the war in Ukraine, ensuring individuals can move safely and legally. |
| **Geographic Scope** | Applies only to applications submitted at specific Canadian embassies/consulates in Central and Eastern Europe. |
| **Time Limit** | Applications must be submitted between **February 22, 2022, and March 31, 2023** (for most cases), with a cutoff of **March 14, 2022** for Ukrainian nationals. |
| **Exclusivity** | Remission is only granted if the fee has not already been waived under Section 11 of the Passport Fees Regulations. |
| **Travel Purpose** | Emphasis on "urgent" travel due to the invasion — travel for safety, family reunification, or medical reasons is considered. |
| **Exclusions** | Does not cover all fees; only specific ones listed in the referenced regulations. |

---

### **Public Interest Justification**
The Governor General in Council has determined that it is in the **public interest** to waive these fees because:
- The Russian invasion of Ukraine created widespread displacement and emergency situations.
- Canadians and Ukrainians required urgent access to identity and travel documents to leave conflict zones or return home.
- Waiving fees reduces barriers to timely travel and supports national and international humanitarian efforts.

---

### **Implementation and Enforcement**
- The remission is **automatic** for individuals who meet the eligibility criteria and submit applications within the specified timeframes and locations.
- The **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration** is responsible for processing applications from Ukrainian nationals.
- The **consular offices** in Warsaw, Bucharest, Budapest, and Vienna are responsible for receiving and processing applications from Canadians and permanent residents.

---

### **Conclusion**
This remission order is a targeted, time-limited, and humanitarian measure that provides fee relief to:
- Canadians and permanent residents who were in Ukraine or elsewhere and needed urgent travel due to the war.
- Ukrainian nationals who applied to obtain travel documents before March 14, 2022.

By waiving specific fees under key regulations, the government ensures that individuals can access essential travel documents without financial barriers during a time of crisis, reflecting a commitment to both immigration policy and humanitarian response.

---

**Final Note:**  
This regulation is **not a permanent policy** but a temporary measure specific to the context of the Russian invasion of Ukraine. It expires at the end of the specified time periods and is not intended to apply to future events or general travel needs.
