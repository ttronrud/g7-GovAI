## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Petroleum and Gas Revenue Tax Regulations**

### üìå **Overview**
These regulations, made under **section 103 of the Petroleum and Gas Revenue Tax Act (PGRTA)**, provide detailed definitions, interpretations, and rules governing the taxation of petroleum and gas production in Canada. They clarify how certain expenses, royalties, production revenues, and projects are treated for tax purposes‚Äîparticularly with respect to **Canadian exploration and development costs**, **prescribed projects**, **royalty obligations**, and **tax instalments**.

The regulations are primarily concerned with:
- Defining key terms (e.g., Canadian exploration expenses, joint exploration corporations),
- Prescribing which expenses are eligible for deduction,
- Determining when certain activities qualify as "prescribed projects" or "prescribed reservoirs",
- Specifying eligible deductions and allowances,
- Setting rules for royalty treatment,
- Establishing tax instalment bases for individuals and corporations.

They were issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance** and the **Minister of National Revenue**, and are cited as the **Petroleum and Gas Revenue Tax Regulations**.

## üìå Key Provisions and Their Implications

---

### 1. **Prescribed Amounts for Tax Purposes (Section 5(1)(e))**

> **Purpose**: To define which Canadian exploration and development overhead expenses are eligible for inclusion in computing taxable income.

#### Key Rules:
- A **prescribed amount** is defined as:
  - The taxpayer‚Äôs **Canadian exploration and development overhead expense** for a taxation year,
  - **Excluding**:
    - Amounts related to **financing**,
    - Amounts that are **incentives under the Petroleum Incentives Program Act** or **payments from the Alberta Petroleum Incentives Program Fund**,
    - Amounts incurred **after September 30, 1986**.

> **Effect**: This restricts the deductibility of certain overhead costs, especially those tied to financing or post-1986 expenses, and excludes incentives to prevent double-counting or tax avoidance.

---

### 2. **Renunciation of Expenses (Section 5(2))**

> **Purpose**: To address the tax treatment of expenses **renounced** by a joint exploration corporation to a shareholder corporation.

#### Key Rules:
- If a joint exploration corporation **renounces** a Canadian exploration or development expense (not related to financing) to a shareholder corporation, and such expense is **deemed** to be the shareholder‚Äôs expense under subsection 66(10.1) or (10.2) of the Income Tax Act:
  - The expense is **deemed incurred by the shareholder** at the time of renunciation.
  - The expense is **no longer** a Canadian exploration or development overhead expense of the **joint exploration corporation** after that time.

> **Effect**: Ensures that the economic transfer of exploration costs is properly reflected in the tax treatment of the shareholder, preventing double counting or shifting of costs between entities.

---

### 3. **Prescribed Royalty Amounts (Section 5(1)(a))**

> **Purpose**: To define when royalties paid to Her Majesty in right of Canada are considered "prescribed" for tax purposes.

#### Key Rules:
- The following amounts are **prescribed royalties**:
  - Royalties, taxes, lease rentals, or bonuses paid to **Her Majesty in right of Canada** for the use and benefit of:
    - A **band or bands** under the *Indian Act*,
    - **Petro-Canada Limited**.

> **Note**: This is a special rule to ensure that royalty payments to Indigenous bands or Petro-Canada are treated as taxable income under the Act.

> **Revocation Note**: The provision stating that royalties before October 1, 1986, related to production are prescribed **was revoked by SOR/89-553, s.3**. This suggests a narrowing of scope over time.

---

### 4. **Prescribed Purposes (Section 5(2)(b))**

> **Purpose**: To define **taxable purposes** for which income from petroleum/gas production may be earned or used.

#### Key Purposes:
- **Earning income** from a source described in paragraph 5(1)(a) or (b) of the Act (i.e., from oil/gas production).
- **Use or consumption of gas** from wells **not part of a gas business**, provided:
  - The total production from such wells **does not exceed 1,000 gigajoules** in the year.

> **Effect**: Limits the tax deductibility of gas used for non-commercial purposes, especially when production is small and not part of a business.

---

### 5. **Prescribed Deductions for Production Revenue (Section 5(2)(e))**

> **Purpose**: To allow specific deductions for production revenue based on time periods and source of production (especially bituminous sands).

#### Key Deductions:
| Period | Deduction Rate | Conditions |
|-------|----------------|-----------|
| After December 31, 1981 | 25% | Of production revenue attributable to that period |
| After Dec 31, 1982 and before June 1, 1983 | 20.45% | From bituminous sands deposits |
| After May 31, 1983 | 25% | From bituminous sands deposits |

> **Conditions for Deduction**:
- The taxpayer must **not have claimed** any deduction under:
  - Paragraph 82(2)(e) or subsection 82(3.2) of the Act (before Dec 12, 1988),
  - Any resource royalty payments,
  - Any inclusion of royalty amounts in production revenue under subsection 82(5),
- Paragraph 82(1)(b.1) must be read **without reference to "or resource royalty"**.

> **Effect**: Encourages investment in bituminous sands by providing a temporary, targeted deduction, while preventing double-deduction or royalty-related manipulation.

---

### 6. **Prescribed Projects and Reservoirs (Sections 5(8), 79, 82, 82.1)**

> **Purpose**: To identify projects and reservoirs that qualify for special tax treatment under enhanced recovery rules.

   - OR the project is located in **Ontario**.
