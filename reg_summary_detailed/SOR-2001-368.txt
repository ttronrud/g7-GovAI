**Detailed Summary of the Commercial Loan (Insurance Companies, Societies and Insurance Holding Companies) Regulations**

---

### **1. Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**

These regulations were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, under **section 1021a of the Insurance Companies Act (Act)**.  
- **Statutory Reference**: S.C. 2001, c.9, s.465; S.C. 1991, c.47  
- The regulations are designed to define and clarify key terms and thresholds related to **commercial loans** and the **regulatory treatment of subsidiaries** for insurance companies, insurance societies, and insurance holding companies.

### **3. Definition of "Widely Distributed" Debt Obligations**

This section defines when a debt obligation qualifies as "widely distributed," which is a key factor in determining whether a loan is considered a commercial loan and thus subject to certain regulatory treatment.

#### **Applicable Criteria (for debt obligations):**

The debt obligations must meet **at least one** of the following conditions:

1. **Distribution Threshold (90% Rule)**  
   - At least **90% of the maximum authorized principal** of the debt obligations is held by **persons other than the company and its subsidiaries**.  
   - **AND**  
   - The debt was issued to **at least 25 persons other than the company and its subsidiaries** within **six months** of the first issuance.  
   *(Note: Applies only if issued in a single issuance)*

2. **Continuous Issuance (Average Holder Threshold)**  
   - If issued continuously, there must be, **on average**, **at least 25 holders** (excluding the company and its subsidiaries).

3. **Initial Distribution Criteria (Three of Four)**  
   At the time of initial distribution, the debt obligations must meet **at least three** of the following:
   - Initial term **less than one year**  
   - Rated by a **rating agency**  
   - Distributed through a person **authorized to trade in securities**  
   - Distributed in accordance with an **offering circular, memorandum, or similar document**

#### **Applicable Criteria (for other securities)**

For non-debt securities (e.g., equity or other instruments), the following applies:

- The securities must be **listed and posted for trading** on a **recognized stock exchange**, **OR**  
- A **prospectus** relating to the issuance of the securities was filed under the laws of **Canada, a province, or a jurisdiction outside Canada**.

ðŸ‘‰ **Purpose**: To distinguish between loans that are broadly distributed (and thus more likely to be commercial in nature) and those that are concentrated or issued to a small group of investors, which may fall outside the scope of commercial loan rules.

---

### **4. Definition of "Maximum Authorized Principal"**

- In relation to any debt obligation, **maximum authorized principal** means the **maximum amount of debt** that may be issued under the terms of the debt obligation or any related agreement.

ðŸ‘‰ **Purpose**: Provides a clear benchmark for determining the size and scope of debt instruments when assessing whether they qualify as widely distributed or commercial loans.

### **6. Prescribed Subsidiaries â€“ Section 505 (Property and Casualty & Marine Companies)**

A **prescribed subsidiary** of a property and casualty (P&C) or marine company is a subsidiary that is **not**:

- A **financial institution**  
- A **bank holding company** or an **insurance holding company**  
- A subsidiary of a financial institution (unless the subsidiary is only a financial institution due to its parentage)  
- A subsidiary of a bank holding company or insurance holding company

ðŸ‘‰ **Purpose**: Excludes certain types of financial subsidiaries from being counted in regulatory thresholds (e.g., for asset concentration or capital requirements), unless they are directly tied to financial operations.

#### **Prescribed Percentage (Section 505)**  
- The **prescribed percentage** is **5%** â€” meaning that only subsidiaries holding **5% or more** of a companyâ€™s total assets are considered for regulatory scrutiny.

ðŸ‘‰ **Note**: This threshold applies to both P&C and marine companies.
