## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Non-Mailable Matter Regulations (C.R.C., c. 1289)**

### üìå **Background and Legal Authority**
- The regulation was **approved by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Regional Industrial Expansion**, under **subsection 19(1) of the Canada Post Corporation Act**.

- These regulations are effective **January 1, 1990** and may be cited as the **Non-Mailable Matter Regulations**.

### üìö **Key Definitions and Scope**

#### 1. **Definition of "Letter-Post Item"**
- The term "letter-post item" is defined **identically to that in the International Letter-Post Items Regulations**.

#### 2. **Non-Mailable Matter**
- The items listed in the **Schedule** to these regulations are classified as **non-mailable matter**.
- This means they **cannot be accepted or transported** by Canada Post under normal postal operations.

### üì¶ **Special Handling Rules for Items Returned by Customs Officers**

When non-mailable matter is **returned to Canada Post by a customs officer**:

- **General Rule**:  
  The item is held for:
  - **30 days**, or  
  - **Until final disposition of legal proceedings** (if such proceedings are ongoing), after which it is disposed of per **Section 4** of the regulations.

- **For items containing intoxicating beverages**:
  - Can be:
    1. **Returned to the country of origin** if the addressee requests it and pays return postage; or  
    2. **Held for 30 days**, then **destroyed**, unless the addressee requests return and pays postage within that period.

### üö® **Specific Prohibited Items (Excluded from Mailing)**

The following items are explicitly **non-mailable** due to safety, health, or international treaty compliance:

| Category | Explanation |
