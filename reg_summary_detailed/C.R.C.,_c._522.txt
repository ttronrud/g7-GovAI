**Detailed Summary of the *Customs Diplomatic Privileges Regulations***

---

### **1. Regulation Title and Citation**
The regulation is formally titled the **Customs Diplomatic Privileges Regulations** and may be cited as such. It establishes the legal framework governing the importation of goods into Canada by certain diplomatic, consular, and official representatives of foreign governments, including exemptions from customs duties under specific conditions.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

- **Chief Officer of Customs (agent en chef des douanes):**  
  Refers to the manager or managers of the customs office(s) serving a particular area or place. This individual is responsible for processing applications and enforcing the regulations within their jurisdiction.

- **Collector (Revoked):**  
  This term was previously used but has been **revoked** by *SOR/88-86, section 1*. It is no longer applicable.

- **Duty / Taxes (Revoked):**  
  These terms were also **revoked** by *SOR/88-86, section 1*. They are no longer used in the regulation.

- **Deputy Minister:**  
  Refers to the **Deputy Minister of National Revenue for Customs and Excise**. This official holds authority over the processing and approval of certain applications, particularly those involving motor vehicles.

- **Of Career (de carriÃ¨re):**  
  Defines individuals who are:
  - Nationals or citizens of the country they represent,
  - Not engaged in any business or profession outside of their diplomatic or consular duties.  
  This ensures that only those genuinely serving in official capacity are entitled to privileges.

---

### **3. Privilege of Importation at Free Rates of Customs Duties**

Under **Section 4**, the privilege of importing goods at **free rates of customs duties** is granted to the following categories of individuals:

#### **Eligible Individuals:**
- Ambassadors, Ministers, High Commissioners, and Consuls General of career (i.e., serving in official capacity).
- Members of the diplomatic corps and staff of high commissionersâ€™ offices listed in the Department of External Affairsâ€™ diplomatic list.
- Consuls of career.
- Vice Consuls of career.
- Trade Commissioners of career.
- Assistant Trade Commissioners of career.

> *Note:* The term "of career" ensures that these individuals are not involved in commercial activities and are serving in official diplomatic or consular roles.

---

### **4. Application Process**

#### **For Goods Other Than Motor Vehicles:**
- Must be submitted **in writing** to the **Chief Officer of Customs**.
- A **separate application** is required for each importation.

#### **For Motor Vehicles (Post-First Arrival):**
- Must be submitted in the **form specified in the Schedule** (attached to the regulation).
- The application must be **forwarded directly to the Deputy Minister** of National Revenue (Customs and Excise), not just to the local customs office.

> *Rationale:* This reflects the higher level of oversight required for motor vehicles due to their value and potential for misuse.

---

### **5. Approval and Authorization Requirements**

Once an application is approved:
- The applicant receives a **duplicate Free Rate of Customs Duties Importation Authorization**.
- The applicant must **present both copies** of this authorization to the **Chief Officer of Customs** at the customs office where the vehicle is reported.

---

### **6. Signature Requirements by Category**

Applications must be signed by an authorized official depending on the applicant's role:

| Applicant Type | Required Signatory |
|---------------|--------------------|
| Consul of career | Appropriate Consul General or Consul in charge (if no Consul General) |
| Vice Consul of career | Consul General or Consul in charge (if no Consul General), or Vice Consul in charge (if no Consul) |
| Trade Commissioner of career | Senior Trade Commissioner or Trade Commissioner in charge (if no senior commissioner) |
| Assistant Trade Commissioner of career | Trade Commissioner in charge or Assistant Trade Commissioner in charge (if no Trade Commissioner) |

> *Purpose:* Ensures accountability and official endorsement of the application by a responsible authority within the diplomatic or consular structure.

---

### **7. Personal and Household Effects (Including Motor Vehicles)**

- The privilege of importing **personal and household effects**, including **motor vehicles**, at free rates of customs duties applies upon **first arrival in Canada** to:
  - Representatives of foreign governments (e.g., ambassadors, ministers, consuls) sent to Canada,
  - Their **families**.

> *Exclusion:* Spirituous liquors are **not included** in this exemption.

---

### **8. Sale or Disposal of Imported Goods**

#### **General Rule (Goods):**
- Goods imported at free rates may be **sold or disposed of in Canada without paying customs duties** if:
  - They have been in the **use and possession** of the importer in Canada for **at least one year**.

#### **Motor Vehicles:**
- Motor vehicles imported at free rates may be sold or disposed of without customs duties if:
  - They have been in **use and possession** for **at least two years**.

> *Note:* This rule protects the intended use of the goods and prevents exploitation of the privilege for resale.

---

### **9. Waiver in Case of Death or Departure**

- If a person entitled to the privilege (as per Section 4) **dies or transfers away from Canada** before the two-year period expires:
  - The **Minister** may **authorize a waiver** of customs duties upon **recommendation from the Secretary of State for External Affairs**.
  - This ensures flexibility in exceptional circumstances where the vehicle or goods are no longer in use or ownership by the original recipient.

---

### **10. Official Supplies from Foreign Governments**

- Uniforms, office equipment, stationery, and similar supplies sent by a foreign government for the **official use** of its officers in Canada may be imported at **free rates of customs duties**.

> *Purpose:* Supports the operational needs of diplomatic and consular missions without incurring customs costs.

---

### **11. Revoked Provisions**

The following provisions have been **revoked** by *SOR/88-86, section 1 and section 7*:
- "Duty or duties"
- "Tax or taxes"
- "Collector"
- "Application for duty or tax" (section 7)

> *Impact:* These outdated or redundant provisions have been removed, streamlining the regulation and aligning it with current administrative practices.

### **Key Principles and Objectives of the Regulation**

| Principle | Objective |
|--------|----------|
| **Diplomatic Immunity & Practical Use** | To allow diplomatic and consular personnel to bring personal and official equipment into Canada without customs barriers. |
| **Accountability & Oversight** | Requires formal applications, signatures from authorized officials, and clear declarations to prevent misuse. |
| **Use-Based Exemption** | Goods and vehicles are only exempt from duties if used in Canada for a specified period (1 year for goods, 2 years for vehicles). |
| **Prevention of Resale** | Restricts resale within a defined timeframe to prevent exploitation of the free importation privilege. |
| **Flexibility in Exceptional Cases** | Allows for waiver of duties upon death or departure, ensuring fairness and humanitarian considerations. |

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Customs Diplomatic Privileges Regulations* provide a structured, transparent, and accountable framework for granting **free importation privileges** to diplomatic, consular, and trade mission officials in Canada. By clearly defining eligibility, application procedures, approval mechanisms, and conditions for resale or disposal, the regulation balances the practical needs of foreign representatives with the integrity of Canadaâ€™s customs system.

Despite the revocation of several outdated terms, the core principles remain relevant and are essential for maintaining diplomatic relations while ensuring fair and consistent customs enforcement.
