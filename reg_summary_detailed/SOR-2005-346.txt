### **Detailed Summary of the Protection of Passenger Information Regulations**

The **Protection of Passenger Information Regulations** (commonly referred to as the "Passenger Name Record Information Regulations") are a set of administrative rules made under the **Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)** by the **Government of Canada**, specifically through the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness**. These regulations govern how the **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)** collects, retains, uses, and discloses **passenger name record (PNR) information** for the purposes of national security, immigration enforcement, and border management.

---

## **1. Legislative Basis and Process**

- The regulations were **proposed** and laid before both Houses of Parliament under **subsection 5(2)a of the IRPA**, which allows the Minister to table proposed regulations for parliamentary review.
- The final regulations were **made by Her Excellency the Governor in Council**, on the recommendation of the Minister, under:
  - **Subsection 5(1)** of the IRPA (power to make regulations),
  - **Section 150.1c** of the IRPA (specific authority to regulate the use of passenger information).
- The regulations came into force on the **day of registration** in the Canada Gazette.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act* (IRPA). |
| **Agency** | The **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)**. |
| **Commercial vehicle** | Any vehicle carrying persons or goods by air, water, or land. Defined in accordance with the IRPR (Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations). |
| **Day of departure** | The day a commercial vehicle departs from the last point of embarkation before arriving in Canada. Applies to all modes of transport. |
| **Time of departure** | The time of take-off (air) or departure (water/land) from the last point of embarkation. |
| **Passenger name record information (PNR)** | Information specified in **paragraph 269(1)(e)** of the IRPA — includes details such as name, nationality, travel dates, destination, contact information, and other passenger details provided to the CBSA. |
| **Serious transnational crime** | A crime punishable by at least 4 years in prison that: <br> - Occurs in more than one country; <br> - Is planned or controlled in another country; <br> - Involves an organized criminal group active across borders; <br> - Has substantial effects abroad; or <br> - Is committed with intent to travel through or into Canada. |
| **Terrorism offence** | Includes acts or omissions: <br> - For political, religious, or ideological purposes with intent to intimidate or compel action; <br> - Intended to cause death, serious harm, endanger public safety, cause major property damage, or disrupt essential services; <br> - Involves participation in or support of terrorist groups; <br> - Includes financing, facilitating, or conspiring to commit such acts. <br> *Defined under subsection 83.01(1) of the Criminal Code.* |
| **Terrorist group** | As defined in subsection 83.01(1) of the Criminal Code. |
| *(Note: "Advance passenger information," "enforcement database," "PAXIS system," and "intelligence official" have been repealed by SOR/2016-38, s. 1 and s. 2.)* |

## **3. Core Principles and Limitations**

### **A. Use of PNR Information**
- The CBSA may **only use** PNR information **in accordance with these Regulations** and **only for purposes under the IRPA**.
- Use is strictly limited to:
  - Preventing, detecting, investigating, or prosecuting **terrorism offences** or **serious transnational crimes**.
  - Conducting **trend analysis** or developing **risk indicators** to support national security or border safety.

### **B. Retention of PNR Information**

| Retention Period | Conditions |
|------------------|-----------|
| **Default retention period**: **3 years and 6 months** after the **day of departure** of the commercial vehicle carrying the passenger. | Applies to all individuals unless otherwise extended. |
| **Extended retention (up to 6 years)**: | If the information is needed to identify someone **reasonably suspected** of having committed a **terrorism offence** or **serious transnational crime**. Retention must be **proportional and justified**, and only as long as necessary for that purpose. |

> ⚠️ **Note**: Retention beyond 6 years is **not permitted** under any circumstances.

---

## **4. Access to PNR Information by CBSA Officials**

Access is strictly controlled and time-limited.

### **(1) General Access (for identifying terrorism or transnational crime)**
- Officials may access PNR data to:
  - Identify individuals who may have committed a **terrorism offence** or **serious transnational crime**.
  - Conduct **trend analysis** or develop **risk indicators**.
- Access is **only allowed if the official's duties require it**.

### **(2) Access During 72 Hours to 2 Years After Departure**
- During the period from **72 hours after departure** to **two years after day of departure**:
  - Officials may access the **name** of a person who was on board (only for identifying potential threats).
  - Access is **only permitted if confirmed necessary** for the purpose.

### **(3) Access After 2-Year Period (until 3 years 6 months)**
- After the 2-year window, access is permitted **only if authorized by the President of the CBSA**.
- Access is **only allowed** to identify someone **reasonably suspected** of committing a terrorism offence or serious transnational crime.

### **(4) Access During Extended Retention Period (under subsection 3(2))**
- During the period when PNR data is retained under extended conditions (i.e., for terrorism or transnational crime), access is **limited to identifying persons** who may have committed such offences.

> ✅ **All access must be justified** and tied to the official’s function.

---

## **5. Recordkeeping Requirements**

When access is granted, the following **must be recorded in a register** and **retained for at least two years**:

| Information Required |
|----------------------|
| Name of the official requesting access |
| Reason for the request |
| Name of the person being investigated |
| Dates: request made, authorization granted, and date of access |

> This ensures **transparency, accountability, and auditability** of access to sensitive personal data.

---

## **6. Disclosure of PNR Information**

The CBSA may disclose PNR information **only under strict conditions**, primarily to other government bodies or foreign authorities.

### **A. To Federal or Provincial Government Departments**
Disclosures are allowed **only if**:
- There are **reasonable grounds** to believe the information will assist in preventing, investigating, or prosecuting a **terrorism offence** or **serious transnational crime**.
- The department has **functions directly related** to such investigations.
- The department has committed to applying **standards of protection** at least as strong as those in these Regulations.
- The department agrees **not to further disclose** the information without CBSA permission (unless legally required).
- Only the **necessary information** is disclosed.

### **B. To Foreign Government Authorities**
Disclosures are allowed **under international agreements** if:
- There are **reasonable grounds** to believe the information will assist in preventing, investigating, or prosecuting a terrorism or transnational crime.
- The foreign authority has functions directly related to such crimes.
- The authority has committed to applying **standards of protection** at least equivalent to those in these Regulations — or, alternatively, to those negotiated with the **European Union**.
- Only **necessary information** is disclosed.

> ✅ **Important**: Disclosure under these provisions is **case-by-case** and subject to rigorous review.

### **C. Legal Compliance**
- Nothing in the regulations prevents the CBSA from disclosing PNR information in response to a **subpoena, warrant, or court order** issued by a Canadian court or body with jurisdiction.

---

## **7. Repealed Provisions**

Several earlier definitions and provisions have been **repealed** by **SOR/2016-38, s. 1 and s. 2**, including:
- Advance passenger information
- Enforcement database
- PAXIS system
- Intelligence official

These were either outdated or superseded by new frameworks (e.g., the 2016 amendments to PNR regulations, which modernized data handling and strengthened privacy protections).

---

## **8. Key Objectives and Purpose**

The primary goals of these regulations are:

1. **Enhance national security** by enabling the CBSA to identify potential threats related to terrorism or organized crime.
2. **Support effective border management** through timely access to passenger data.
3. **Ensure compliance with privacy and data protection standards** by imposing strict limits on:
   - Retention periods,
   - Access rights,
   - Disclosure conditions,
   - Recordkeeping.
4. **Balance security needs with individual privacy rights** by requiring justification, oversight, and transparency.

---
