### **Detailed Summary of the Canada Pension Plan Investment Board Regulations (S.C. 1997, c. 40)**

The **Canada Pension Plan Investment Board Regulations** (the "Regulations") are a set of administrative rules established under **section 53 of the Canada Pension Plan Investment Board Act (the "Act")**. These regulations govern how the Canada Pension Plan Investment Board (the "Board") manages and invests the assets of the Canada Pension Plan (CPP), ensuring that investments are made in a manner consistent with sound financial principles, risk management, transparency, and accountability.

---

## **I. Enactment and Approval Process**

### **1. Legislative Basis**
- The Regulations do **not take effect automatically**.
- Under **subsection 53(2) of the Act**, the Regulations must be approved by the **appropriate provincial Ministers** of **at least two-thirds of the participating provinces**, and these provinces must collectively represent **at least two-thirds of the total population** of all participating provinces.
- This requirement ensures that the regulations reflect broad provincial consensus and are not imposed unilaterally by federal authorities.

### **2. Approval Condition Met**
- The preamble confirms that this threshold has been met — the required provincial ministers have approved the Regulations.

### **3. Formal Adoption**
- Based on this approval, **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, formally enacts the Regulations under **section 53 of the Act**.

> **Result**: The Regulations come into force on the day they are registered in the *Statutes of Canada*.

## **III. Control and Affiliation Rules**

### **1. Control**
- A person **controls** a corporation if they beneficially own **more than 50% of the voting shares** and those shares are sufficient to elect a majority of directors.
- A person **controls** an unincorporated entity (excluding limited partnerships) if they own **more than 50% of ownership interests** and can direct its business.
- The **general partner** of a limited partnership controls the partnership.
- A **trustee** controls a trust.

### **2. Deemed Control**
- If a person controls an entity, they are **deemed to control any entity controlled by that entity**.

### **3. Affiliation**
- Two entities are **affiliated** if one controls the other or both are controlled by the same person.

### **4. Substantial Investment**
- In an **unincorporated entity**: ownership >25%.
- In a **corporation**:
  - Voting rights >10% of total voting rights, **or**
  - Shares represent >25% of shareholders’ equity.

### **5. Association**
A person is **associated with**:
- A corporation they control and all its affiliates;
- A person who controls them;
- A partner with a substantial investment in a partnership where they have a substantial investment;
- A trust or estate in which they have a substantial interest or serve as trustee;
- Their spouse;
- Their brother, sister, child, or descendant, or spouse of any of them.

> These definitions are critical for **related-party transaction rules** and **conflict-of-interest safeguards**.

## **IV. Investment Policy and Standards**

### **1. Investment Policy Statement**
- The Board must establish and maintain a **written statement** of investment policies, standards, and procedures under **section 35 of the Act**. This includes:
  - Categories of investments;
  - Use of derivatives (options, futures, etc.);
  - Portfolio diversification;
  - Asset mix and expected return;
  - Risk management policies (credit, market, liquidity);
  - Liquidity of investments;
  - Lending of cash or securities;
  - Voting rights retention or delegation;
  - Valuation methods for non-traded investments;
  - Criteria for determining whether a related-party transaction is “nominal.”

### **2. Link to CPP Funding**
- The policy statement must **describe factors affecting CPP funding and financial obligations**, and how these relate to investment decisions.

### **3. Review Frequency**
- The Board must **review and confirm or amend** the investment policy **at least once per financial year**.

> This ensures ongoing alignment with changing economic, demographic, and fiscal conditions.

---

## **V. Investment Restrictions**

### **1. Limit on Holdings (10% Rule)**
- The Board **shall not directly or indirectly invest more than 10% of the total book value of its assets** in:
  - The securities of **any one person**;
  - The securities of **two or more associated persons**; or
  - The securities of **two or more affiliated corporations**.

> **Exemptions**:
- Investments in **subsidiaries of the Board**;
- Securities issued or fully guaranteed by:
  - The Government of Canada;
  - A provincial government;
  - An agency of either;
- Funds composed of mortgage-backed securities fully guaranteed by the above;
- Funds that replicate a widely recognized index of securities traded on a public exchange;
- Segregated, mutual, or pooled funds compliant with the *Pension Benefits Standards Regulations, 1985*.

> These exemptions allow for strategic investments in government-backed or diversified funds without violating the concentration limits.

### **2. Voting Share Limit (30% Rule)**
- The Board **shall not invest in securities of a corporation** where more than **30% of the voting rights** are attached.
- **Exemption**: Investments in securities of a **subsidiary of the Board**.

> This prevents undue influence over corporate governance and protects against potential conflicts of interest.

---

## **VI. Related-Party Transactions**

### **1. Definition and Trigger**
- If a transaction is entered into with a person who the Board knows **will become a related party**, that person is considered a related party **for the purpose of the transaction**.

### **2. General Prohibition**
- Subject to **sections 17 and 18**, the Board **shall not enter into a transaction with a related party**.

### **3. Post-Exit Period**
- For **12 months after a person ceases to be a related party**, the Board **shall not enter into a transaction** with that person.

### **4. Exceptions**
The Board **may enter into a transaction with a related party** if:
- The transaction is **necessary for the operation or administration** of the Board; **and**
- The terms and conditions are **not less favourable than market terms and conditions**.

### **5. Investment in Related Party Securities**
- The Board **may invest in securities of a related party** if those securities are **acquired on a public exchange**.

### **6. Nominal Transactions**
- The Board **may enter into a transaction with a related party** if the **value is nominal**.
- For valuation purposes, **multiple transactions with the same related party are considered a single transaction**.
