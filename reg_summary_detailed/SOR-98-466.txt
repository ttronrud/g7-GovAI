**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: "Regulations Prescribing Public Officers"**

**Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**  
The regulation is issued by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Justice, under **paragraph 117.07(2)(g)a of the Criminal Code of Canada**. This provision of the Criminal Code establishes specific categories of individuals who are considered "public officers" for the purposes of certain criminal liability and protection provisions—particularly those related to the handling of firearms, evidence, and sensitive government functions.

The regulation is formally enacted as **S.C. 1995, c. 39, s. 139**, and came into force on **December 1, 1998**.

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**  
The primary purpose of these regulations is to **identify and define who qualifies as a "public officer"** under paragraph 117.07(2)(g)a of the Criminal Code. This designation is significant because it determines whether certain individuals are protected from criminal prosecution under specific provisions of the Code—particularly those involving the **misuse or improper handling of firearms, evidence, or other regulated items**—and may also influence the application of immunity or procedural protections in criminal investigations.

---

### **Who Is Considered a Public Officer Under This Regulation?**  

The regulation defines the following **classes of individuals** who are deemed public officers for the purposes of paragraph 117.07(2)(g)a:

1. **Employees responsible for court exhibits and evidence**  
   - Individuals who are employed in the public service of Canada or by a provincial or municipal government and who have responsibility for:  
     - Examinations  
     - Inventory  
     - Storage  
     - Maintenance  
     - Transportation  
   of **court exhibits and evidence**.

2. **Police or public service personnel handling firearms and weapons**  
   - Employees of police forces or other public service agencies who are responsible for:  
     - Acquisition  
     - Examination  
     - Inventory  
     - Storage  
     - Maintenance  
     - Issuance  
     - Transportation  
   of the following:  
     - Firearms  
     - Prohibited weapons  
     - Restricted weapons  
     - Prohibited devices  
     - Prohibited ammunition  
     - Explosive substances  

3. **Technicians, laboratory analysts, and scientists in forensic or research laboratories**  
   - Professionals working in **forensic or research laboratories** who perform scientific analysis or technical work related to evidence or crime investigations.

4. **Armourers and firearms instructors**  
   - Personnel who work at **police academies** or similar institutions designated under **subparagraph 117.07(2)(e)(ii)** of the Criminal Code.  
   - Also includes those employed by:  
     - Federal or provincial departments responsible for **natural resources, fisheries, wildlife, conservation, or the environment**  
     - **Revenue Canada**

5. **Park wardens and environmental enforcement officers**  
   - Employees of federal or provincial departments who are responsible for **enforcing laws and regulations** related to:  
     - Natural resources  
     - Fisheries  
     - Wildlife  
     - Conservation  
     - Environment  

6. **Immigration officers**  
   - Officers employed by immigration authorities who are involved in the processing, inspection, or enforcement of immigration laws.

7. **Security personnel in Parliament**  
   - Security personnel employed by:  
     - The **House of Commons**  
     - The **Senate**  
     - The **Service**, as defined in **section 79.51 of the Parliament of Canada Act**  
   *(Note: This refers to the "Service" under the Parliament of Canada Act, which typically includes the security and protection services of Parliament)*

8. **Aircraft pilots employed by public service agencies**  
   - Pilots working for the **Department of Transport** or other public service agencies that operate government aircraft.

---

### **Definition of "Public Service Agencies"**  
For the purposes of this regulation, the term **"public service agencies"** is defined **identically to its meaning in section 1 of the Public Agents Firearms Regulations**.  
This ensures consistency with existing legal definitions and frameworks governing the regulation of firearms and public officials.

---

### **Key Legal Implications**  
- The designation of these individuals as "public officers" under paragraph 117.07(2)(g)a of the Criminal Code may provide them with **certain protections** or **immunities** from criminal liability when acting in the course of their duties.  
- It also clarifies **who is subject to specific criminal provisions** related to the handling of dangerous or regulated materials, thereby helping to prevent misuse or negligence in sensitive areas like evidence management, firearms control, or environmental enforcement.  
- The regulation helps ensure **accountability and consistency** in how public officers are identified across federal, provincial, and municipal governments.

---

### **Effective Date**  
The regulations came into force on **December 1, 1998**.

---

### **Conclusion**  
This regulation provides a **clear, structured, and comprehensive list** of individuals who qualify as public officers under the Criminal Code for the purposes of specific legal protections and responsibilities. By defining who falls under this category, it enhances legal clarity, strengthens oversight in sensitive areas (such as evidence handling and firearms management), and supports the integrity of public service operations across Canada.

It is a key tool in the broader framework of **public officer protections and accountability**, ensuring that individuals entrusted with managing high-risk or high-stakes materials are appropriately recognized and protected under Canadian criminal law.
