### **Detailed Summary of the Proclamation and Agreement on Social Security Between Canada and Jamaica (1983)**

This document is a **Proclamation issued by the Government of Canada** on **September 16, 1983**, declaring the **Agreement on Social Security between Canada and Jamaica**, signed in Kingston on **January 10, 1983**, to be **in force effective June 3, 1983**. It is rooted in the **Old Age Security Act (O-6)** and governed by **section 22.3 and subsections 22.4(1) and (2)**, which outline the procedures for international social security agreements.

The agreement establishes a **mutual recognition and coordination framework** for social security benefits (such as old age pensions, survivor benefits, disability pensions, and child benefits) between Canada and Jamaica, ensuring that individuals who live in or work in either country can access benefits under the laws of either nation without being subject to arbitrary reductions or denials due to residency.

---

## **I. Key Legal and Procedural Background**

### **1. Legislative Authority**
- The **Old Age Security Act (Chapter O-6)** provides the legal basis for such international agreements.
- **Section 22.3** authorizes the Governor in Council to declare an international social security agreement in force.
- **Section 22.4** sets out the parliamentary process:
  - The Order in Council (P.C. 1983-865) declaring the agreement in force was laid before Parliament on **June 3, 1983**.
  - No motion was filed in either House within 20 sitting days.
  - By **subsection 22.4(2)**, the Order came into force on **June 3, 1983** ‚Äî the same day it was laid before Parliament.
- A **Proclamation (P.C. 1983-1973)** issued on **June 30, 1983**, formally gives notice that the Agreement is in force from **June 3, 1983**.

> ‚úÖ **Conclusion**: The agreement entered into force on **June 3, 1983**, without requiring parliamentary debate or amendment, due to the absence of opposition or motion under the Act.

---

## **II. Scope and Objectives of the Agreement**

The primary goal of the Agreement is to **prevent double taxation, benefit denial, or reduction** of social security benefits based solely on where a person resides. It ensures **mutual recognition** of benefits and residency periods across national borders.

### **1. Parties and Applicable Legislation**
| Party | Relevant Legislation |
|------|------------------------|
| **Canada** | Old Age Security Act (OASA), Canada Pension Plan (CPP), and related regulations |
| **Jamaica** | National Insurance Act and regulations made thereunder |

The Agreement applies to:
- All amendments, consolidations, or extensions of the above legislation.
- Extensions to new categories of beneficiaries, subject to mutual agreement.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: Provincial social security programs (e.g., provincial pensions) are addressed separately under **Article XX**.

## **III. Key Provisions of the Agreement**

### **1. Definitions (Article I)**
The Agreement defines critical terms to ensure clarity and consistency:
- **Competent Authority**: Minister responsible for social security in each country.
- **Territory**: Canada and Jamaica respectively.
- **Legislation**: As defined in Article II.
- **Credited Period**: Time of contribution, employment, or residence used to qualify for a benefit. In Canada, this includes disability pension periods under CPP.
- **Government Employment**: Includes public service roles in either country (e.g., government agencies, statutory corporations).
- **Pension/Benefit**: Includes supplements, increases, or adjustments.
- **Old Age Benefit**: 
  - Jamaica: Old age pension under National Insurance Act (including wage-related increases).
  - Canada: Old age pension under OASA (excluding income-tested supplements and spouse‚Äôs allowance).
- **Spouse‚Äôs Allowance**: 
  - Jamaica: Increase for dependent spouse.
  - Canada: Spouse‚Äôs allowance under OASA (including Guaranteed Income Supplement equivalent).
- **Survivor‚Äôs Benefit**: 
  - Jamaica: Widow/widower pension.
  - Canada: Survivor‚Äôs pension under CPP.
- **Invalidity Benefit**: Disability pension in both countries.
- **Children‚Äôs Benefit**: Orphan or special child benefit.
- **Death Benefit**: Lump-sum funeral grant.

> üîç **Important**: Undefined terms take meaning from applicable domestic legislation.

### **2. Mutual Recognition of Benefits (Article VIII)**

#### **Core Principle**:
> *Benefits acquired under one Party‚Äôs legislation shall not be reduced, suspended, or canceled simply because the beneficiary resides in the other Party‚Äôs territory.*

- Benefits are **payable in the territory of the other Party**.
- If eligibility can be established under Articles VIII, IX, X, or XI, the benefit shall be **paid in lieu of the original grant**.

#### **Exception**:
- A person may be subject to **only one country‚Äôs legislation** depending on employment or residency (see below).

---

### **3. Employment and Coverage Rules (Article VI)**

#### **General Rule**:
> An employed person working in one country is subject **only** to that country‚Äôs legislation.

#### **Exception: Services for the Same Employer**
> If an employee works in the territory of one Party for the same employer, they remain subject to the **legislation of their home country** (i.e., the country where they are employed under its labor laws).

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Limitation**: This coverage may **not exceed 24 months** without prior approval from both competent authorities.

#### **Special Case: Crew Members of Ships or Aircraft**
> A crew member who would otherwise be covered by both Canada‚Äôs CPP and Jamaica‚Äôs legislation:
- Is subject **only to Jamaica‚Äôs legislation** if a **Jamaican national** and resides in Jamaica.
- Is subject **only to Canada‚Äôs CPP** if not a Jamaican national.

#### **Government Employment**
> An individual performing government duties in the other country is subject to the **other country‚Äôs legislation only if**:
- They are a **national** of that country, or
- They **ordinarily reside** there.

> ‚úÖ However, they may **elect** to be covered by the first country‚Äôs legislation if they are a national.

> üîÅ **Flexibility**: The competent authorities may jointly modify these rules for specific persons or categories.

### **4. Residency and Credit Rules (Article VII)**

These provisions determine how **residence periods** in Canada and Jamaica are counted toward eligibility for benefits under the **Old Age Security Act (OASA)**.

#### **A. Credit for Residency in Jamaica (for Canadians)**
> A Canadian who resides in Jamaica during a period of time:
- Is **not** counted as residing in Canada **for OASA purposes**, **unless**:
  - They are not employed.
  - Their spouse or dependant is residing with them and not employed.
  - The period is **not** due to employment under Jamaica‚Äôs legislation.

> ‚úÖ **Exception**: If the spouse or dependant is contributing to CPP due to employment, that period **is** counted as residence in Canada.

> ‚ùå **No credit**: If the person is employed in Jamaica, the period is **not** counted as residence in Canada.

#### **B. Credit for Residency in Canada (for Jamaicans)**
> A Jamaican who resides in Canada:
- Is **not** counted as residing in Canada **for OASA purposes**, unless:
  - They are not employed.
  - Their spouse or dependant is with them and not employed.

> ‚úÖ **Exception**: If spouse/dependant is contributing to CPP, the period is counted.

> ‚ùå **No credit**: If employed in Canada, the period is not counted.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Overlap Rule**: If a person holds multiple jobs, the employment period does **not** count as residence.

---

### **5. Entitlement to Old Age Benefits (Article VIII)**

#### **Case 1: Entitlement under Jamaica‚Äôs Law**
> If a person is entitled to an old age benefit under Jamaica‚Äôs National Insurance Act:
- The benefit shall be **paid in Canada**.

#### **Case 2: Entitlement under Canada‚Äôs OASA**
> If a person is entitled to an old age benefit under Canada‚Äôs OASA:
- The benefit shall be **paid in Jamaica** **only if**:
  - They have accumulated **at least 20 years of residence in Canada**.

> ‚ùå If less than 20 years of residence, **partial benefit** is payable **outside Canada** if:
  - Totalized residence in Canada and Jamaica equals **at least 20 years**.
  - Calculated according to **subsections 3(1.1) to 3(1.4)** of the OASA (partial pension rules).

> ‚úÖ **Partial pension** is payable **outside Canada** if total residence meets 20 years.

> ‚ùå **Subsection 3(1)** of the OASA **does not apply** in these cases.
