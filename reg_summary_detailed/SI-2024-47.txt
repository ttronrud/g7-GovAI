**Detailed Summary of the Jasper National Park of Canada Rent and Fee Remission Order**

---

### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
This regulation is issued under **subsection 23(2.1)a of the Financial Administration Act (FAA)**, which allows the Governor in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Treasury Board and the Minister of the Environment) to make orders granting rent and fee remissions in the public interest.  
- **Statutory Reference**:  
  - S.C. 1991, c. 24, s. 7(2)  
  - R.S., c. F-11  
- The Order is specifically designed to provide financial relief to individuals and entities affected by the **wildfires that occurred in July 2024 in Jasper National Park of Canada**.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**
- **"Regulations"** refers to the *National Parks of Canada Lease and Licence of Occupation Regulations* (SOR/92-25), which govern leasing and occupation of properties within national parks.
- All terms used in this Order are interpreted according to their definitions in the Regulations unless otherwise specified.

---

### **3. Scope and Purpose**
The primary purpose of this Order is to **mitigate economic hardship** experienced by:
- The Municipality of Jasper (as a local government entity),
- Lessees and licence holders of cottages and other properties in the park,
- Individuals affected by the 2024 wildfires.

It provides **partial or full remission (i.e., cancellation or reduction)** of rental payments for specific periods and conditions, particularly in response to **damage caused by the July 2024 fires**.

---

## **Detailed Provisions by Section**

---

### **Section 1: Remission for the Municipality of Jasper**
- **What**: A full remission of rents paid or payable for the period **April 1, 2024, to March 31, 2027**.
- **Applies to**: Rents for leases, licences of occupation, and other agreements under **Section 7.3 of the Municipality of Jasper Agreement**.
- **Rationale**: This supports the municipal government during a period of financial strain due to the impact of the 2024 fires and related recovery efforts.

---

### **Section 2: Remission for Lake Edith Cottage Lessees – Habitable Units**
Applies to **Lake Edith cottages** in the resort subdivision referenced in **Item 1 of Schedule II of the Regulations**.

#### **A. For Habitable Cottages (after July 2024 fires):**
- **Remission Amounts**:
  - **8.33%** of rent for the period **April 1, 2024 – March 31, 2025** (under subsection 8(1) of the Regulations).
  - **14.29%** of rent for the period **April 1, 2024 – October 31, 2024** (under subsection 8(2) of the Regulations).
- **Applies to**: Leases granted under **paragraph 3(1)(a)** of the Regulations.
- **Rationale**: Provides modest relief to lessees whose cottages remain habitable post-fire, recognizing the disruption caused by the event.

---

### **Section 3: Remission for Non-Habitable Cottages (after July 2024 fires)**
Applies to cottages **no longer habitable** after the July 2024 fires.

#### **Remission Breakdown by Period and Subsection**:
| Period | Subsection | Remission Percentage |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| April 1, 2024 – March 31, 2025 | 8(1) | **66.67%** |
| April 1, 2024 – October 31, 2024 | 8(2) | **42.86%** |
| April 1, 2025 – earlier of (resumed occupancy or March 31, 2028) | 8(1) or (2) | **100%** |

- **Applies to**: Leases granted under **paragraph 3(1)(a)** of the Regulations.
- **Rationale**: Offers substantial relief to lessees of damaged properties. The 100% remission for the 2025–2028 period is intended to support recovery and eventual reoccupation.

---

### **Section 4: Remission for Lessees and Licence Holders under Paragraphs 3(1)(b) or (e)**
Applies to:
- Lessees under **paragraph 3(1)(b)** or (e) of the Regulations,
- Licence of occupation holders under **paragraph 18(1)(b)** or (e) of the Regulations.

#### **Remission for Period: April 1, 2024 – March 31, 2025**
- **66.67%** of the rent or fee under subsections **11(1)** or **18(7)**.

#### **Remission for Period: April 1, 2025 – March 31, 2026**
- **Amounts are capped**:
  - If total rent/fee is **less than $50,000**: remission equals the **full amount**.
  - If total rent/fee is **$50,000 or more**: remission is **$50,000**.
- **Exception (Gross Revenue Loss Clause)**:
  - If the lessee or licence holder suffers a **gross revenue loss >10%** compared to their **previous gross revenue** (defined below), the remitted amount is:
    - The **greater of**:
      - $50,000, or  
      - (Total rent/fee) × (percentage of gross revenue loss).
  - **Requirement**: The lessee must submit a **signed attestation** of gross revenue loss within **six months after the end of their fiscal year**.

#### **Definition of "Previous Gross Revenue"**:
- Refers to the gross revenue from the **fiscal year ending on or before July 24, 2024**.
- Adjusted using the **Consumer Price Index (CPI) for 2023** to account for inflation.

> This provision ensures that those with significantly reduced income due to the fires receive enhanced relief proportional to their actual financial impact.

---

### **Section 5: Remission for Deferred Payments**
- Applies to **anyone who had their rent or fee payments deferred** due to a **Parks Canada announcement in August 2024**.
- Remission is granted **in full or in part** for the interest (i.e., accrued interest) on any deferred payments.
- **Scope**: Covers all affected parties, regardless of property type or lease status.

> This provision ensures that individuals who were unable to pay due to the fire-related disruptions do not face additional financial penalties for interest on delayed payments.

---

## **Key Themes and Policy Objectives**

| Objective | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Support for Affected Communities** | Direct financial relief to the Municipality of Jasper and residents impacted by the fires. |
| **Equitable Relief Based on Damage** | Different levels of remission based on whether a cottage is habitable or not. |
| **Incentivization of Recovery** | The 100% remission for non-habitable units during 2025–2028 supports long-term recovery and reoccupation. |
| **Inflation and Revenue Adjustment** | Use of CPI-adjusted revenue ensures fair comparisons across years. |
| **Prevention of Financial Hardship** | Caps and thresholds prevent overpayment while protecting vulnerable operators. |
| **Transparency and Accountability** | Requires attestation of revenue loss to prevent abuse and ensure eligibility. |

---

## **Implementation Notes**
- The Order is **binding and enforceable** under the Financial Administration Act.
- All remissions are **calculated based on the specific lease or licence agreement**, including the relevant subsections and dates.
- The **Minister of the Environment** is responsible for administering and verifying claims, especially those involving gross revenue loss.
- The **six-month deadline** for submitting revenue loss attestation is critical—failure to meet it may result in ineligibility.

---

## **Conclusion**
This **Jasper National Park Rent and Fee Remission Order** is a targeted, multi-layered policy response to the **economic consequences of the July 2024 wildfires** in Jasper National Park. It balances:
- **Public interest** (supporting recovery and community resilience),
- **Fairness** (proportional relief based on property condition and financial impact),
- **Administrative feasibility** (clear eligibility criteria and timelines).

By providing **full or partial rent remissions** to municipalities, cottage lessees, and licence holders—especially those affected by fire damage—the Order aims to stabilize the local economy, encourage recovery, and uphold the principle of equitable access to park accommodations during times of crisis.

--- 

**Final Note**: This Order reflects a proactive and compassionate approach by the Canadian government to support residents and businesses in one of Canada’s most iconic natural environments during a period of environmental and economic disruption.
