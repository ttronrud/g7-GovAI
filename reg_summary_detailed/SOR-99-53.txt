## ðŸ” **Detailed Summary of the Competency of Operators of Pleasure Craft Regulations**

### ðŸ“Œ **Overview**
These regulations establish the **minimum competency standards** for individuals who operate pleasure craft (motorized boats) in Canadian waters for recreational purposes. They were introduced to ensure public safety by requiring operators to demonstrate knowledge of boating safety, responsibilities, and emergency procedures.

The regulations came into force on **April 1, 1999**, and apply to **motorized pleasure craft** operating in Canadian waters **excluding the Northwest Territories and Nunavut**. They do **not apply to seaplanes**.

### Exceptions (Subsection 2)

A person is **exempt** from the above requirements if they:

1. Are operating under the **supervision of an instructor** as part of an accredited course;  
2. Are **not residents of Canada** and the pleasure craft is in Canada for **less than 45 consecutive days**; or  
3. Have **proof on board** of a certificate of competency, training certificate, endorsement, or other equivalency listed in the schedule (now repealed).

## ðŸŒ **Non-Resident Operators**

A non-resident person is competent to operate a pleasure craft if:

- They have **proof of competency** (e.g., PCOC, foreign certificate); **or**  
- They hold a **certificate or document** from their home country/state that meets the requirements of Canadian boating safety standards.

> This allows international visitors to operate boats legally, provided their credentials are recognized.

## ðŸ“š **Accreditation of Boating Safety Courses**

### How to Apply:
- A course provider must submit a **written request** and **4 copies of the course material** to the **Department of Transport (DOT)**.

### Criteria for Accreditation:
A course is accredited if it includes:
- Definitions of boating terms and expressions;  
- Responsibilities of pleasure craft operators (e.g., carrying required equipment, charts, maps);  
- Safety procedures (including emergency responses);  
- General boating safety knowledge;  
- Review of relevant regulations and statutes, including:
  - *Canada Shipping Act, 2001*  
  - *Contraventions Act* (related to shipping violations)  
  - *Criminal Code*  
  - *Vessel Operation Restriction Regulations*  
  - *Navigation Safety Regulations, 2020*  
  - *Collision Regulations*  
  - *Small Vessel Regulations*

### Suspension & Reinstatement:
- The DOT may **suspend accreditation** if a course no longer meets the criteria.  
- Accreditation may be **reinstated** if the issues are resolved.

## ðŸ“ **Test Administration Requirements**

Only a course provider or their agent may administer a test **if**:

1. The test is administered **in accordance with an approved test protocol** submitted and approved by the Minister;  
2. The test meets the requirements of subsections (3) and (4).

### Approval Criteria for Test Protocol:
The protocol must ensure:

- **Identity and age verification** of each candidate;  
- Clear instructions provided **before the test begins** on:
  - Maximum duration of the test;  
  - Passing grade (minimum 75%);  
  - Procedures to complete the test;  
- During the test, candidates must:
  - Not communicate with anyone except the test administrator;  
  - Not access documents other than the test and instructions;  
  - Not copy, remove, or send the test or any portion of it;  
  - Not consume alcoholic beverages;  
- The test site must be:
  - Designated **solely** for candidates and administrators;  
  - Owned, leased, or controlled by the course provider (not a candidateâ€™s residence);  
  - Accessible to DOT agents upon request;  
  - Conforms to the providerâ€™s description in the protocol;  
  - If located in a commercial/sporting area, **physically separated** (e.g., by walls/partitions) so candidates cannot see outside the test area;  
- No candidate may take a test **more than once per day**;  
- Any candidate violating rules is **removed immediately**, and their answers are **automatically rejected**;  
- The **ratio of candidates to administrators** must not exceed **20:1**;  
- A **copy of the test protocol** must be available at the test site for inspection by DOT agents.
