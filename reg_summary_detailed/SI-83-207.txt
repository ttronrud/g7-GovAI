**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Regulation Title:**  
*Statutes of Canada Distribution Direction*  
(Replaces and revokes Order in Council P.C. 1953-609 of 22 April 1953)

**Issuing Authority:**  
His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Supply and Services and the President of the Treasury Board.  
This action is authorized under:  
- Subsection 10(2) of the *Publication of Statutes Act*  
- Section 52 of the *Financial Administration Act*

**Purpose of the Regulation:**  
To establish a modern, equitable, and efficient system for the free distribution of the Acts of Parliament of Canada to designated individuals and institutions, ensuring timely access to legislative materials for key governmental, judicial, and parliamentary stakeholders.

---

### Key Provisions:

1. **Revocation of Previous Order:**  
   This Direction revokes the outdated Order in Council P.C. 1953-609, which had governed the distribution of parliamentary acts. The new Direction reflects updated needs and administrative practices in the Canadian government.

2. **Title and Citation:**  
   The new regulation is officially titled the *Statutes of Canada Distribution Direction* and may be cited as such.

3. **Timing and Delivery of Acts:**  
   - The Acts of the Parliament of Canada shall be delivered **without charge** as soon as practicable **after the close of each parliamentary session**.  
   - The delivery is intended to ensure timely access to new legislation, enabling prompt legislative review, implementation, and public awareness.

4. **List of Recipients (Eligible Recipients):**  
   The Acts are to be distributed free of charge to the following individuals and institutions:

   - **Federal Government Officials and Parliamentarians:**  
     - Governor General  
     - Lieutenant Governors of the Provinces  
     - Speaker of the Senate  
     - Speaker of the House of Commons  
     - Prime Minister  
     - Leader of the Opposition in the Senate  
     - Leader of the Opposition in the House of Commons  
     - Ministers of the Government of Canada  

   - **Members of Parliament (MPs):**  
     - All Members of Parliament of Canada who are not otherwise included on the list (i.e., all MPs, regardless of party or office).

   - **Judicial Officials:**  
     - All members of the judiciary of Canada, including provincial court judges and magistrates.

   - **Provincial Government Legal Officials:**  
     - Attorneys General  
     - Ministers of Justice  
     - Solicitors General  
     - Of the Governments of the Provinces  

   - **Provincial Legal and Library Officials:**  
     - Chief or senior legislative counsel of each Province  
     - Chief librarians of court houses in Canada  
     - Chief librarians of law schools in Canada  

   - **Foreign Government Officials and Academic Institutions (Conditional):**  
     - Members and officials of the Government of any foreign country  
     - Persons holding positions in universities and other institutions in that country  
     - **Distribution is permitted only if one of the following conditions applies:**  
       (a) There exists a **reciprocal arrangement** with Canada whereby similar free distribution of statutes is provided to Canadian officials or institutions; or  
       (b) The distribution is **recommended by one of the following authorities**:  
         - The Minister of Justice  
         - The Secretary of State for External Affairs  
         - The Librarian and Archivist of Canada  
         - The Parliamentary Librarian  

     > *Note:* This provision ensures that foreign distribution is not automatic or uncontrolled. It requires formal justification or mutual agreement, promoting fairness and consistency in international legislative access.

---

### Key Features and Implications:

- **Free Access:** The regulation ensures that no recipient is charged for receiving the Acts of Parliament, promoting transparency and accessibility in the legislative process.

- **Timeliness:** Distribution occurs "as soon as practicable after the close of each session," ensuring that legislative changes are available to those who need them promptly.

- **Inclusivity and Equity:** The list includes both federal and provincial officials, judicial personnel, and legal professionals, recognizing their critical roles in interpreting, applying, and implementing laws.

- **Reciprocity Principle:** The conditional access for foreign entities emphasizes mutual respect and cooperation, preventing unilateral distribution and ensuring that access is balanced and justified.

- **Administrative Oversight:** The requirement for recommendations by key officials (e.g., Minister of Justice, Librarian and Archivist) ensures that the distribution process remains accountable and aligned with national policy and archival standards.

---

### Exclusions and Limitations:

- The regulation does **not** extend to the general public or private individuals. Access to legislation is limited to those with a direct role in governance, law, or public service.  
- Foreign distribution is **not automatic** and requires either a reciprocal agreement or formal recommendation by designated officials.

---

**Note:**  
This Direction is **not published in the Canada Gazette, Part II**, which may indicate it is a non-public administrative directive intended for internal use by government departments and institutions. However, it remains legally binding and enforceable within the framework of the *Publication of Statutes Act* and *Financial Administration Act*.

---

**Conclusion:**  
The *Statutes of Canada Distribution Direction* establishes a clear, fair, and legally grounded mechanism for the free and timely delivery of parliamentary legislation to essential government, judicial, and academic bodies. It replaces an outdated policy with a more responsive, equitable, and internationally sensitive framework, ensuring that those who need the law have timely and accessible access to it.
