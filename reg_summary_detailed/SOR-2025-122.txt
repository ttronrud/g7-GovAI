### **Detailed Summary of the United States Surtax Remission Order (2025)**

This regulation, issued under **section 115a of the Customs Tariff** (S.C. 2005, c. 38, par. 145(2)(j); S.C. 1997, c. 36), grants **remission (i.e., exemption)** of **surtaxes** imposed under specific U.S. surtax orders — namely:

- *United States Surtax Order (2025-1)*  
- *United States Surtax Order (Steel and Aluminum 2025)*  
- *United States Surtax Order (Motor Vehicles 2025)*  

The purpose of this Order is to **facilitate the importation of critical goods** into Canada for **health care, public health, public safety, national defence, and national security** purposes, while also supporting key domestic industries such as agriculture, food processing, and manufacturing. The remission is subject to strict conditions and timelines.

---

## **I. Scope of Remission**

Remission is granted in several distinct categories, each tied to specific **uses**, **entities**, or **tariff classifications**.

---

### **1. Remission for Health Care and Public Health Entities**

**Eligible entities** that may import goods without paying surtaxes:

- Government or clinical health research organizations  
- Organizations producing or storing **medical countermeasures** (e.g., pharmaceuticals, medical devices)  
- Offices of public health officials (as defined in *Food and Drug Regulations*, subsection C.10.001(1))  
- Emergency response services (ambulance, emergency medical services)  
- Firefighting services  
- Law enforcement agencies  
- Federal or provincial correctional services  
- Department of National Defence (DND)  
- Canadian Security Intelligence Service (CSIS)

> *Note:* This includes both direct use and support functions essential to public safety and health infrastructure.

---

### **2. Remission for Use in Medically Necessary Health Care Services**

Goods may be exempt if imported **for use in providing medically necessary health care services**, including at:

- Hospitals  
- Health care or dental clinics  
- Medical, dental, or diagnostic laboratories  
- Long-term care facilities  

Additionally, remission applies to goods used by:

- Entities providing products or services related to **blood, cells, tissues, or organs** (for medically necessary care)  
- Federal, provincial, local, or Indigenous health authorities  

> *Purpose:* Ensures timely access to essential medical supplies and services during public health emergencies or routine operations.

---

### **3. Remission for Manufacturing, Agriculture, and Food Packaging**

Remission applies to goods imported into Canada for use in:

- Manufacturing or processing of any good  
- Production of agricultural products  
- Packaging of food products or beverages  

> *Rationale:* Supports domestic supply chains and food security, especially during times of global supply disruption.

---

### **4. Remission Based on Tariff Classification (Schedules 1–4, 4.1, and 5)**

The Order provides **specific tariff classification numbers** under which surtaxes are waived, depending on the schedule:

#### **Schedule 1**  
- Goods classified under the following tariff numbers receive remission:  
  - 2202.99.39.10  
  - 2202.99.39.20  
  - 1901.90.34.90  
  - 1902.19.11.00  
  - 1902.19.12.30  
  - 1902.19.12.90  
  - 1902.19.19.00  
  - 1902.30.11.00  
  - 1902.30.19.00  
  - 2106.90.35.00  
  - 2106.90.39.90  
  - 2106.90.95.90  

> *Examples of goods:*  
> - Blood bank or plasma freezers  
> - Laboratory freezers  
> - Pharmaceutical refrigerators  
> - Blood bank refrigerators, pass-thru refrigerators  
> - Laboratory refrigerators  
> - Pharmaceutical or laboratory pass-thru refrigerators  

> *Note:* These are critical for storing temperature-sensitive medical products.

---

#### **Schedule 2 and 3**  
- Goods classified under **tariff numbers listed in column 1** of these schedules are exempt **if** they are imported under the specified conditions.

> *Examples of goods (partial list):*  
> - Steel bars and rods (e.g., 1018, 1020, 1045 grades)  
> - Cold-rolled steel strips (SAE 1095, 0.9 mm thick, 500 mm wide)  
> - Continuous cast iron bars (65-45-12, 65-40-18, etc.)  
> - Special quality bars (ASTM 105, 108, SA105)  
> - Free-cutting steel bars (1.46", 1.312", 1", 1.078")  
> - Cast iron bars (cold-formed, rectangular or circular cross-section)  
> - Cold-rolled bars (C1018, C1045, up to 4 inches thick, 20 ft long)  
> - Alloy steel bars (4130, 4140, 4340, 52100, 8620, 9310)  
> - Tubes of carbon steel (1026, ASTM A513, A106, A519)  
> - Drawn steel tubes (various diameters and wall thicknesses)  
> - Welded steel pipes (circular or rectangular cross-sections)  

> *Conditions:*  
> - Must meet specified **grade, dimensions, strength, and coating** requirements  
> - Must be imported **before a specified date** (e.g., September 1, 2025, or a date in Schedule 3)  
> - Must conform to product descriptions  
> - Must be imported in accordance with specified conditions (e.g., coating, finish, testing)

---

#### **Schedule 4.1**  
- Applies to specific **alloy steel and high-performance steel products** with precise technical specifications.

> *Examples:*  
> - Bars and rods of maraging steel (250M, 300M)  
> - Alloy steel bars (4130, 4140, 4340, 52100, 8620, 9310)  
> - Cold-formed bars with specified dimensions and grades  
> - Steel tubes with specific dimensions and yield strength requirements  

> *Conditions:*  
> - Must be imported by a person with a **specific business number** listed in column 1  
> - Must conform to description in column 3  
> - Must be imported during a **specific period** (as specified in column 4)  
> - Must comply with conditions in column 5 (e.g., testing, certification)

---

#### **Schedule 5**  
- Applies specifically to **motor vehicles** under the *Motor Vehicles 2025* surtax order.

> *Eligible vehicles:*  
> - Motor vehicles classified under tariff numbers in Schedule 5  
> - All-terrain vehicles (ATVs), including utility terrain vehicles (UTVs) and side-by-side vehicles  
> - Hearses and hearse limousines  

> *Note:* This supports emergency response, transportation of medical personnel, and funeral services.

---

## **II. Conditions for Remission**

Remission is **not automatic** and is subject to the following **mandatory conditions**:

1. **Import Date Limitations**  
   - For goods under sections 1–3 (health, safety, defence):  
     → Must be imported **before December 16, 2025**  
   - For goods under Schedule 3:  
     → Must be imported **before the date specified in column 3 of Schedule 3**  
   - For goods under Schedule 4 or 4.1:  
     → Must be imported **before September 1, 2025** (Schedule 4)  
     → Must be imported **during a specified period** (Schedule 4.1)  

2. **Importer Qualifications**  
   - For Schedule 4 and 4.1 goods:  
     → Must be imported by a **person with a business number listed in column 1**  
     → Must conform to **product description** (if applicable)  

3. **Compliance with Technical Specifications**  
   - Goods must meet **exact dimensional, material, and performance criteria** (e.g., yield strength, wall thickness, coating, grade)  
   - Must conform to **ASTM, SAE, or other technical standards** as specified  

4. **No Prior Claims for Relief**  
   - No other surtax relief claim can have been granted for the same good under the Customs Tariff  

5. **Timely Claim Submission**  
   - The importer must **submit a claim for remission to the Minister
