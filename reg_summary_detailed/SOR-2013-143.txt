**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: "Regulations Establishing the Periods Within Which Eligible Authors, Eligible Performers and Eligible Makers Not Represented by Collective Societies Can Claim Private Copying Remuneration"**

---

### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
- These regulations are issued by the **Copyright Board of Canada** under **paragraph 83(13)(b)a of the Copyright Act** (S.C. 1997, c. 24, s. 50; R.S. c. C-42).
- The authority allows the Board to establish specific timeframes within which eligible creators (authors, performers, and makers) who are **not represented by collective societies** can claim **private copying remuneration**.
- Private copying remuneration is a payment made to rights holders when individuals make personal copies of copyrighted works (e.g., for personal use), as part of a compensation mechanism to balance the impact of private copying on the market.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Regulation**
To define **clear and enforceable deadlines** for eligible individuals (authors, performers, and makers) to claim their entitlements under the **Private Copying Tariff** system. This ensures:
- Fair and timely distribution of remuneration,
- Legal certainty for rights holders,
- Compliance with the requirements of subsection 83(11) of the Copyright Act, which grants such entitlements,
- A structured timeline that accounts for different types of creators and different tariffs.

---

- The **longer deadline for performers** reflects the extended period during which performance rights are used in private copying (e.g., in music or live performances).
- The **shorter deadline for makers** may reflect the shorter lifecycle or different market dynamics of recorded works.
- All parties have a **fixed endpoint** (December 31) for the standard tariffs, with the exception of new tariffs, which are governed by a fixed number of years after the tariff ends.

---

### **5. Definition of Key Terms**
- **Eligible Author**: A person who created a work covered by the private copying tariff and is not represented by a collective society (e.g., a composer, writer, or artist not part of a music or literary collective).
- **Eligible Performer**: A person who performed a work (e.g., a singer, actor, or musician) and is not represented by a collective society.
- **Eligible Maker**: A person who produced a sound recording or other tangible work (e.g., a record producer or sound engineer) and is not represented by a collective society.
- **Private Copying Tariff**: A tariff established by the Copyright Board to determine the amount of remuneration payable to rights holders when private copying occurs (e.g., making a copy of a song at home).
- **Cessation of Effectiveness**: The date when a tariff is officially replaced or withdrawn by the Board.

---

### **6. Enforcement and Compliance**
- These regulations are **binding and enforceable** under the Copyright Act.
- Rights holders must **exercise their entitlement within the specified period**; failure to do so results in **loss of claim** to private copying remuneration.
- The regulation applies **only to individuals not represented by a collective society** (e.g., those not covered by organizations like the Canadian Association of Music Publishers or the Canadian Society of Musicians).

---

### **7. Effective Date**
- The regulations come into force on the **day they are registered** in the Canada Gazette.
- This ensures that the rules are immediately applicable upon official publication.

---

### **8. Significance and Impact**
- Provides **clarity and predictability** for rights holders who may otherwise be uncertain about when they can claim compensation.
- Encourages **proactive claims** by setting clear deadlines.
- Balances the interests of rights holders and the public by ensuring that remuneration is paid only within reasonable timeframes.
- Reflects the **different timelines** of various creative sectors (e.g., music performance vs. written works vs. recordings).

---

### **Conclusion**
This regulation establishes a **structured, time-bound framework** for eligible creators (authors, performers, and makers) who are not part of collective societies to claim private copying remuneration. It differentiates the deadlines based on the type of creator and the tariff period, with authors and performers receiving longer windows (up to 7 years) and makers receiving a shorter window (up to 5 years). The rules ensure that claims are made within a defined period, promoting fairness, legal certainty, and effective administration of the private copying remuneration system under Canadian copyright law.
