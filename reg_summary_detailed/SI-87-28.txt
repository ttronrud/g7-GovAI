**Detailed Summary of the Criminal Appeal Rules of the Supreme Court of Newfoundland, Trial Division (1986)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Criminal Appeal Rules of the Supreme Court of Newfoundland, Trial Division*, adopted on **July 4, 1986**, by the majority of Judges of the Supreme Court of Newfoundland, Trial Division under **Section 438 of the Criminal Code of Canada**, govern the procedural framework for appeals from **summary conviction courts** under **Section 748 of the Criminal Code**. These rules establish a clear, structured process for filing, serving, and conducting criminal appeals in Newfoundland, ensuring consistency, fairness, and efficiency in appellate proceedings.

These rules are formally titled *"The Criminal Appeal Rules of the Supreme Court of Newfoundland, Trial Division"* and came into force on **September 2, 1986**.

---

## **Key Definitions and Interpretations**

The rules define several key terms used throughout:

- **Appeal**: An appeal from a summary conviction court under **Part XXIV of the Criminal Code**.
- **Appeal Court**: The Judicial Centre of the Supreme Court of Newfoundland, Trial Division, nearest to where the original trial occurred.
- **Registrar**: A clerk performing the functions of the Registrar in the Appeal Court.
- **Code**: Refers to the *Criminal Code of Canada*.
- **Judge**: Refers to the judge of the appeal court unless preceded by "trial," in which case it refers to the judge or magistrate of the summary conviction court.

---

## **Core Procedural Requirements**

### **1. Filing and Serving a Notice of Appeal**

An appeal must be initiated by a **written notice of appeal**, which must be:
- **Dated and signed** by the appellant or their solicitor.
- **Directed to the respondent** (the person or entity against whom the appeal is being made).

#### **Content of the Notice of Appeal**
The notice must include the following elements:
- Name of the summary conviction court that issued the conviction/order/sentence.
- The conviction, order, or sentence appealed against.
- The offence charged and the place where it was committed.
- The location where the trial was held.
- The date of conviction, order, or sentence.
- Whether the appeal is from conviction, sentence, order of dismissal, or both.
- A concise and precise statement of the **relief sought** (e.g., reversal of conviction, reduction of sentence), along with **reasons** for requesting it.
- Whether the appellant (if in custody) wishes to be present in person during the hearing.
- The appellantâ€™s address for service.

#### **Forms for Different Appellants**
- If the **appellant is the defendant**, the notice may use **Form 1** in the schedule.
- If the **appellant is the prosecutor**, the notice may use **Form 2**, modified as needed.

#### **Filing and Service Deadlines**
- **Appellant (Defendant):**
  - Must file the notice within **30 days** after the conviction or sentence, whichever is later.
  - Must serve a copy **personally or by pre-paid registered mail** on the respondent or their solicitor/agent.
  - Must file **proof of service** with the Registrar **no later than seven clear days after the last day of service**.

- **Appellant (Prosecutor):**
  - Same 30-day filing deadline.
  - Must serve the notice on the respondent or their representative.
  - Must file proof of service within **seven clear days** after service.
  - The appeal court may, on **ex parte application**, order alternative service (e.g., to a third party) if the defendant is uncooperative or unavailable.

### **2. Transmission of Materials to the Appeal Court**

Upon filing, the **Registrar shall immediately send a copy of the notice of appeal** to the summary conviction court.

#### **Summary Conviction Courtâ€™s Obligations**
- Must transmit to the Registrar, within **10 days** of receiving the notice, the material required under **subsection (1) of Section 754 of the Criminal Code**, including:
  - The record of the trial, judgment, and sentence.

#### **Transcript of Evidence Requirement**
- The appellant must **notify the summary court in advance** that **four copies** of the transcript of evidence and the reasons for judgment and sentence are required.

#### **Court Reporter Certificate (Form 3)**
- If evidence was taken by a **duly sworn court reporter** or recorded via sound apparatus:
  - The appellant must file a **certificate (Form 3)** confirming that four copies of the transcript and reasons have been ordered and will be provided.
  - The court reporter must proceed with **reasonable diligence** to prepare the transcript.
  - Upon completion, the reporter must send **four certified copies** (certified as correct and authentic) to the Registrar.

> âœ… *This certificate is **prima facie proof** of the accuracy and authenticity of the transcript.*

#### **Alternative: Trial Notes**
- If evidence was **not recorded** or taken by a court reporter:
  - The trial judge must provide **four copies of his notes** of the evidence and proceedings.
  - These notes, when **verified by the judge**, are **prima facie proof** of what occurred at trial.

### **3. Filing of Statements of Facts and Law**

- The appellant must file a **signed, concise statement** of the points of fact and law to be argued, referencing:
  - The evidence.
  - Relevant legal authorities.
- A copy must be delivered to the respondent.

- The respondent must file a **similar statement** within **14 days** of receiving the appellantâ€™s statement and deliver a copy to the appellant.

- In their statement, the respondent should:
  - Accept or reject facts stated by the appellant.
  - Provide a reply to the points of argument.

> âš ï¸ **Note:** Filing statements is **not mandatory** for perfecting an appeal. However, **omission or delay may result in adverse cost consequences**.

---

### **4. Time Extensions**

- The appeal court may, upon application:
  - **Extend deadlines** for filing or hearing.
  - Allow **ex parte applications** (without notice to the other party), though the court may require service if it deems it necessary.
- The application may be made **before or after** the deadline.

---

### **5. Application for Trial de Novo**

Under **Section 755(4) of the Code**, an appellant may apply for a **new trial** (trial de novo) if:
- The appeal is from conviction.
- The appellant receives the transcript or a certificate stating that one cannot be provided.

#### **Procedure for Application**
- Must be made by **notice of motion with supporting material**.
- Must be filed **within seven days** of receiving the transcript or certificate.
- Service on the respondent must occur **no later than seven days before the return date** of the motion.
- Proof of service must be filed with the Registrar **no later than two days before the return date**.

#### **Hearing of the Motion**
- Evidence must be presented **viva voce** (in person).
- With the **approval of the appeal court**, affidavits may be used as support.

- The time and place for the hearing **do not need to be stated in the notice of appeal**.

  - The required materials (including transcript, if applicable),
  - And the transcript is not dispensed with,
- The Registrar shall:
  - **Enter the case on the list** of appeals.
  - **Fix the time and place** for hearing, subject to:
    - Directions by the judge.

### **8. Submission of Written Arguments**

- Any party may submit **written arguments** at any time before the hearing.
- If **all parties submit written arguments**, the appeal court may **dispense with a formal hearing**.

---

### **9. Post-Hearing Procedures**

- Immediately after disposing of the appeal, the Registrar shall send a copy of the **judgment and reasons** (if any) to the trial judge.

---

### **10. Abandonment of Appeal**

- An appellant may **abandon the appeal** by serving a **notice of abandonment** on the respondent and filing a copy with the Registrar **before the hearing date**.

#### **Conditions for Validity of Abandonment**
- The notice may be signed by:
  - The appellant or their solicitor.
- If the appellant signs, their signature must be:
  - **Verified by affidavit**, or
  - **Witnessed by**:
    - The Registrar,
    - A solicitor, or
    - An officer of the institution in which the appellant is confined.
