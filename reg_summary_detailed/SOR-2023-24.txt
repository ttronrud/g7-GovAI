### **Detailed Summary of the Copyright Board Rules of Practice and Procedure (2023)**

The **Copyright Board Rules of Practice and Procedure**, adopted on **January 13, 2023**, and approved by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Industry under **section 66.6a of the Copyright Act (S.C. 2018, c. 27, s. 294; R.S., c. C-42)**, establish a comprehensive and fair procedural framework for the operation of the Copyright Board in matters involving proposed tariffs, royalty rate applications, and related proceedings.

This summary provides a detailed breakdown of the rules, organized by key thematic areas: **purpose, definitions, scope, procedural flexibility, service and filing, notice requirements, case management, evidence and hearings, confidentiality, expert witnesses, and public access**.

---

## **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**

- The rules are **made under section 66.6a of the Copyright Act**, which empowers the Copyright Board to adopt rules of practice and procedure.
- The rules were developed following a **public consultation period** initiated on June 18, 2022, when a draft version of the proposed rules was published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, allowing interested parties to submit written representations.
- The rules are designed to ensure **fairness, transparency, efficiency, and consistency** in the Board’s decision-making process, particularly in tariff and royalty rate disputes involving collective societies and users.

---

## **2. Definitions**

Key terms used throughout the rules include:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Copyright Act* (R.S., c. C-42). |
| **Chair** | The presiding officer of the Board, appointed under subsection 66(1). |
| **Member** | A member of the Board appointed under subsection 66(1). |
| **Intervener** | A person granted leave to participate in a proceeding. |
| **Objector** | A person who files an objection to a proposed tariff. |
| **Party** | In tariff matters: collective society filing the tariff, an objector, or intervener. In individual royalty applications: a collective society or user under subsection 71(1). |

---

## **3. Scope of Application**

These rules apply to the following types of proceedings:

1. **Proposed Tariffs** filed by collective societies regarding:
   - Rights under sections 3, 15, 18, 19, or 21 of the Act (e.g., music, sound recordings, performances).
   - Royalties under subsections 29.7(2) and (3) and paragraph 31(2)(d) of the Act (e.g., mechanical royalties, digital royalties).
   - Levies under section 82 of the Act (e.g., fees for use of certain rights).

2. **Applications to fix royalty rates or related terms/conditions** under subsection 71(1) of the Act (e.g., individual licensing cases).

> *Note:* The rules are not limited to specific industries but cover all rights and uses governed by the Act.

---

## **4. Flexibility and Discretion**

The Board is granted **broad discretion** to adapt or modify the rules in certain circumstances:

- To **deal with matters informally or expeditiously** (e.g., small-scale disputes).
- To **ensure meaningful participation** of stakeholders with a legitimate interest.
- To **address special circumstances** (e.g., emerging technologies, pandemic-related disruptions).

Additionally:
- The Board may **issue directions** on procedural issues not explicitly covered in the rules.
- The Board may **vary, supplement, or dispense with compliance** with any rule if it deems it appropriate.

---

## **5. Time Limits and Business Days**

- **Non-business days are excluded** from time calculations:
  - Days from **December 21 to January 7** (inclusive) in one year are **not counted** in any time limit.
- A **"business day"** is defined as any day **other than Saturday, a statutory holiday, or a day in the December 21–January 7 period**.
- If a time limit falls on a non-business day, it is **automatically extended to the next business day**.
- **Filing deadline**: Documents filed after **17:00 Ottawa local time** are deemed filed on the **next business day**.

---

## **6. Service of Documents**

- Service is primarily conducted via **email** to the party or their authorized representative.
- If email is not possible, service may occur via:
  - Personal delivery
  - Mail to the address for service
  - Any other method authorized by the Board
- **Address for service**:
  - The most recent address used by the party in communication with the Board (for tariffs or applications).
  - If no such address exists, the last address used by the Board to send a notice or listed in a public notice.
- **Presumption of receipt**: An email is presumed to have been received on the date and time it was sent.
- If received after 17:00 Ottawa time, it is deemed received on the **next business day**.
- **Proof of service**: The person serving a document must file a **proof of service** with the Board, unless the email itself constitutes sufficient proof.

---

## **7. Language Requirements**

- Proceedings must be conducted in the **official language(s) chosen by the parties** (English or French).
- Documents in languages other than English or French must be:
  - Accompanied by a **translation into English or French**.
  - Supported by an **affidavit attesting to the accuracy of the translation**.

> This ensures equal access and understanding across linguistic communities.

---

## **8. Notice of Grounds for Proposed Tariff**

A collective society filing a proposed tariff must submit a **notice of grounds** within **seven days** of filing. The notice must include:

- A description of the **uses covered** by the tariff.
- The **basis for proposed royalty or levy rates**.
- Any **other information required** by the Board.

> **Special requirement**: If the proposed tariff covers the **same or substantially the same uses** as the last approved or proposed tariff, the society must also file a **comparative document** showing the differences.

- The Board must **publish** both the notice of grounds and the comparative document in a manner it deems appropriate.

---

## **9. Objections to Proposed Tariffs**

- Any person may file an **objection** to a proposed tariff under subsection 68.3(2) of the Act, accompanied by a **notice of grounds for objection**.
- The notice must:
  - Explain why the Board should **not approve** the tariff, even with modifications.
  - Set out **specific grounds for objection**.
  - Include any other required information.
- At the same time, the objector may file **additional information** for the Board’s consideration.
- The collective society must file a **reply to the objection within 14 days** of receiving the notice of grounds.

---

## **10. Case Management and Joint Statements of Issues**

To promote efficiency and clarity, the rules establish a **case management process**:

- **Joint Statement of Issues**:
  - Required when a proceeding is commenced and an objection has been filed.
  - Must be filed within **90 days** of commencement (or as directed by the Board/case manager).
  - Must include:
    - A description of the issues to be considered.
    - A proposal to **consolidate** the proceeding with one or more other related proceedings (e.g., other tariffs or royalty applications).
    - Any other information required by the Board.

- If the parties **cannot agree**, each must file a **separate statement** outlining:
  - Their position on each issue.
  - Any consolidation proposals.
  - Other required information.

> The Board may **consider additional issues** not raised in the statements, ensuring comprehensive review.

---

## **11. Role of the Case Manager**

- The **case manager** is assigned by the Chair under subsection 66.504(1) of the Act.
- The case manager has **significant powers**, including:
  - Giving directions or making orders to **adapt, restrict, or exclude** any rule.
  - Conducting **case management conferences** to address procedural matters.
- Topics that may be discussed during a conference include:
  - Clarifying or simplifying issues.
  - Facilitating agreement on issues.
  - Determining whether legal questions should be decided by the Board.
  - Selecting official language(s).
  - Deciding on interrogatories (questions to be asked of parties).
  - Determining filing requirements (e.g., legal briefs, case records).
  - Setting hearing schedules and durations.
  - Defining confidentiality or expert testimony parameters.
- After the conference, the case manager may issue an **order** on any matter discussed.
- A party may **request to vary** a case management order, with the request requiring:
  - A clear reason.
  - A statement of whether other parties have consented.
- The case manager may **grant or deny** such requests based on **compelling reasons**.

> **Note**: Assignment of a member as case manager does **not prevent** that member from participating in the final decision on the merits.

---

## **12. Case Records and Evidence**

- A **case record** is required in oral hearings and must include:
  - A statement of position.
  - Documentary exhibits (including expert reports and sworn statements).
