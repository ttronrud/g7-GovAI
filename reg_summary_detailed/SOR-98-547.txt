### **Detailed Summary of the Leamy Lake Navigation Channel Regulations**

---

#### **1. Regulatory Authority and Basis**
The **Leamy Lake Navigation Channel Regulations** were enacted by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Canadian Heritage**, pursuant to **Section 20 of the National Capital Act**. This provision empowers the federal government to establish rules for the management and use of navigable waters within the National Capital Region, particularly those that serve public safety, environmental protection, and orderly navigation.

These regulations specifically govern the use of the **Leamy Lake Navigation Channel**, a key waterway connecting Leamy Lake to the Gatineau River and Lac de la Carrière in the **City of Gatineau, Quebec**.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined for clarity and consistency throughout the regulations:

- **Authorized Vessel** – *Repealed* (SOR/2007-113, s.1): This definition was previously used but has been formally removed from the regulations.
  
- **Boat** – A watercraft of any type capable of navigation, regardless of propulsion method (e.g., sail, motor, paddle). This broad definition includes all vessels used on the channel.

- **Channel** – Refers to the specific navigation channel described in **Schedule 1**, which includes:
  - The **north passage** linking Leamy Lake to the Gatineau River.
  - The **south passage** linking Leamy Lake to Lac de la Carrière.
  
  The exact boundaries are defined by a detailed series of geographic coordinates.

- **Commission Employee** – Any individual acting on behalf of the **Commission** (likely the National Capital Commission or a similar body) under contract or agreement. This includes staff managing operations and enforcement.

- **Control Booth Operator** – A Commission employee stationed at the **Gatineau River entrance** to the channel who manages access and traffic flow.

- **Motorboat** – A motorized vessel that:
  - Is used **exclusively for pleasure**, or
  - Is designed to carry **fewer than 10 passengers** and is used **for remuneration** (e.g., private tours).

- **Motor Fuel** – Includes **gasoline, fuel oil, and diesel fuel**.

- **Peace Officer** – Includes:
  - Members of the **Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)** designated as peace officers under paragraph 7(1)(d) of the RCMP Act;
  - Police officers from the **City of Gatineau**;
  - Supernumerary special constables appointed under the RCMP Act for this purpose and designated as peace officers.

- **Shuttle Boat** – A motorized vessel designed to carry **10 or more passengers** and used **for remuneration** (e.g., scheduled passenger transport).

- **Waiting Area** – A designated area within the channel described in **Schedule 2** where boats may legally stop (outside of emergencies).

> **Note**: These regulations **do not apply** to peace officers or Commission employees performing their duties, **except** for the **control booth operator**, who is subject to the rules under Sections 10–12.

---

#### **3. Prohibited Activities in the Channel**

No person may engage in the following activities **within the navigation channel**:

1. **Swimming or bathing** – Strictly prohibited to protect public safety and prevent accidents.
2. **Unreasonable interference** – No activity that disrupts or hinders another person’s lawful use of the channel.
3. **Behaviors endangering public safety** – Includes reckless actions, intoxication, or dangerous maneuvers.
4. **Stopping outside the waiting area** – Boats may not stop anywhere in the channel unless in an **emergency**.
5. **Anchoring** – Anchoring is prohibited except in an emergency.
6. **Dumping waste** – Prohibition includes:
   - Sewage or other waste;
   - Motor fuel or motor oil.
7. **Selling or supplying fuel** – No one may sell, supply, or offer motor fuel or oil to motorboats or shuttle boats.
8. **Fueling boats** – Fueling a motorboat or shuttle boat with motor fuel or adding motor oil to engines is **prohibited**, except in an emergency.

> These prohibitions apply to all persons, including operators, passengers, and visitors.

---

#### **4. Operational Requirements for Boat Operators**

Every person operating a boat in the channel must:

- **Comply with instructions** from:
  - A **peace officer**;
  - The **control booth operator**;
  - Any other Commission employee acting in the course of their duties.

- **Obey signal lights** in the channel (e.g., traffic signals, lights indicating entry/exit or movement), **unless overridden** by a peace officer or control booth operator.

- **Minimize wake** – Operate the vessel in a way that reduces wake to:
  - Prevent endangering public safety;
  - Avoid damage to:
    - Shorelines;
    - Other boats;
    - Structures or objects.

---

#### **5. Control Booth Access Rules (Entry Restrictions)**

Access to the channel is **strictly regulated** by the **control booth operator**, who manages entry based on traffic conditions.

##### **Entry from the Gatineau River:**
- A person may only enter the channel **from the Gatineau River** if the control booth operator **grants permission**.

##### **Conditions for Permission:**

- **For Motorboats**:
  - The channel must have **fewer than 20 motorboats**.
  - The **total number of motorboats in the channel and Lac de la Carrière** must be fewer than **25**.

- **For Shuttle Boats**:
  - There must be **no shuttle boats** in the channel.
  - There must be **no more than one shuttle boat** in Lac de la Carrière.

##### **Denial of Entry (When Permission is Refused):**
The control booth operator may deny entry if:
- The operator has **reasonable grounds to believe** the person is **impaired by alcohol or drugs**;
- The person is **behaving in a manner that endangers public safety**;
- The channel is **blocked by a disabled or non-operational boat**;
- An **emergency** exists in the channel or Lac de la Carrière.

> These rules ensure safe and orderly flow of traffic, especially during peak times or adverse conditions.

---

#### **6. Penalties for Violations**

Any person who violates **Sections 4 to 7 and Section 9** (prohibited activities and access rules) is liable to:

- A **fine of up to $500**, or
- **Imprisonment for up to six months**, or
- **Both** a fine and imprisonment.

These penalties are enforced on **summary conviction**, meaning they can be heard in a less formal court process without a full trial.

---

#### **7. Effective Date and Jurisdiction**

- **Effective Date**: The regulations came into force on **October 29, 1998**.
- **Geographic Scope**:
  - The channel is defined by precise **geographic coordinates** that describe:
    - The **north passage** (connecting Leamy Lake to the Gatineau River);
    - The **south passage** (connecting Leamy Lake to Lac de la Carrière);
    - Specific boundary lines drawn from defined points (latitude and longitude).
  - A second delineated area includes a portion of Leamy Lake near the southern end, bounded by coordinates from 45°27′09″ to 45°27′10″.

> This ensures that only specific, clearly defined areas are governed by these rules.

---

#### **8. Key Objectives of the Regulations**

1. **Public Safety** – Prevent accidents, drownings, collisions, and environmental harm.
2. **Environmental Protection** – Prohibit dumping of fuel and waste to preserve water quality.
3. **Orderly Navigation** – Regulate traffic flow via access controls and wake management.
4. **Traffic Management** – Use of a central control point (control booth) to manage entry and prevent overcrowding.
5. **Clear Accountability** – Define roles of peace officers, Commission employees, and operators.

---

#### **9. Important Notes and Interpretations**

- The regulations are **not absolute** — exceptions exist for **emergencies** (e.g., medical, mechanical failure, natural disaster).
- The **control booth operator** acts as the central authority for access decisions and traffic flow.
- The rules are designed to **balance recreational use** with safety and environmental stewardship.
- The **prohibition on fueling** is particularly significant — it prevents unauthorized fueling operations and reduces pollution and fire risks.
- The **waiting area** provides a safe, designated zone for boats to dock or wait, reducing congestion and risk.

---

### **Conclusion**

The **Leamy Lake Navigation Channel Regulations** provide a comprehensive framework for the safe, responsible, and orderly use of a critical waterway in the National Capital Region. By clearly defining prohibited activities, regulating access through a centralized control system, and imposing enforceable penalties, the regulations protect public safety, prevent environmental damage, and ensure that the channel remains accessible and usable for recreational and commercial purposes — all while maintaining strict oversight by authorized personnel.

These rules reflect a balance between public access and operational control, making them an essential component of waterway management in Gatineau and the broader National Capital Region.
