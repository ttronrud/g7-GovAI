**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:  
Order Respecting the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization and Its Institute for Statistics**  
*(S.C. 2002, c. 12, s. 3(2) & (3); S.C. 1991, c. 41)*

---

### **Overview**

This regulation, made under the authority of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act* (the "Act"), grants specific **privileges and immunities** to the **United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO)** and its **Institute for Statistics (Institute)** operating in Canada. It is based on the *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations* (the "Convention"), and applies to UNESCO’s operations, personnel, and property within Canada.

The Order ensures that UNESCO can carry out its mandate—promoting education, science, and culture—without undue interference from Canadian authorities, while respecting the principle that such privileges are granted **for the institution’s operational independence**, not for personal benefit.

---

## **Key Provisions and Structure**

### **1. Definitions and Scope**

- **Act**: The *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act* provides the legal basis for this Order.
- **Convention on Privileges and Immunities**: The core framework governing UNESCO’s immunities, derived from the United Nations Convention.
- **Vienna Convention**: The *Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations* is referenced as a model for diplomatic immunity standards.
- **UNESCO**: Recognized as a **body corporate** with full legal capacity.
- **Institute**: The UNESCO Institute for Statistics (located in Montreal) is included under the scope of this Order.
- **Permanent Resident**: Defined under the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act*.
- **Representatives of a Member**: Includes delegates, deputy delegates, advisers, technical experts, and secretaries of foreign states that are members of UNESCO.

---

## **II. Privileges and Immunities of UNESCO (the Organization)**

These apply to UNESCO itself, its property, assets, and income, wherever located.

### **A. Immunity from Legal Process**
- UNESCO, its property, and assets are **immune from all legal processes** (e.g., lawsuits, arrests, seizures).
- **Exception**: Immunity can be **waived expressly** by UNESCO in specific cases.
- Waiver **does not extend to enforcement measures** (e.g., fines, asset seizure) unless UNESCO consents explicitly.

### **B. Immunity from Interference**
- Immune from:
  - Search
  - Requisition
  - Confiscation
  - Expropriation
  - Any executive, administrative, judicial, or legislative interference
- Applies to all locations and holders of UNESCO property.

### **C. Financial and Monetary Flexibility**
- **No financial controls, moratoria, or restrictions** apply:
  - Can hold **funds, gold, or currency** in any form or currency.
  - Can **transfer funds** freely between Canada and other countries or within Canada.
  - Can **convert currencies** at will.
- This ensures operational autonomy and financial stability.

### **D. Inviolability of Premises and Archives**
- **Premises**: UNESCO’s offices and facilities are **inviolable**, subject only to fire and safety regulations.
- **Archives and Documents**: All documents, records, and papers belonging to UNESCO are **inviolable**, regardless of where they are stored.

### **E. Tax Exemptions**
- **Exempt from direct taxes** (e.g., income tax), **except** those that are essentially **public utility charges** (e.g., water, electricity).
- **Exempt from customs duties and import/export restrictions**:
  - For official use (e.g., equipment, supplies) — **provided** these items are not sold in Canada without prior government agreement.
  - For **UNESCO publications** (e.g., reports, journals).
- **Excise duties and property taxes**:
  - Generally **not exempt**.
  - **Exception**: When UNESCO makes **important official purchases**, the Government of Canada will make administrative arrangements to **remit or return** applicable duties or taxes.

### **F. Communication Privileges**
- UNESCO enjoys **treatment equivalent to that of a foreign government** in matters of:
  - Mail, cables, telephones
  - Press rates (for information to media and radio)
- **No censorship** of official correspondence or communications.

### **G. Courier and Code Rights**
- UNESCO may use **codes** and dispatch/receive correspondence via **couriers or sealed bags**, with the same immunities and privileges as diplomatic couriers.

## **III. Privileges and Immunities of Representatives of Member States**

These apply to delegates, technical experts, and officials from foreign states participating in UNESCO activities in Canada.

### **A. Immunity and Inviolability**
- Immunity from:
  - Personal arrest or detention
  - Seizure of personal baggage
  - Legal process for words spoken or written, or acts performed in their official capacity
- Inviolability of all papers and documents
- Right to use codes and receive correspondence via courier or sealed bags

### **B. Immigration and National Service Exemptions**
- Exempt from:
  - Immigration restrictions
  - Alien registration requirements
  - National service obligations
- While visiting Canada for official duties

### **C. Currency and Exchange Facilities**
- Same rights as diplomatic envoys on temporary official missions (e.g., no exchange restrictions)

### **D. Personal Baggage Immunity**
- Same immunities as diplomatic envoys

### **E. Residence and Taxation**
- Time spent in Canada performing duties is **not considered residence** for tax purposes.
- Therefore, they are **not subject to Canadian residence-based taxes**.

### **F. Purpose of Privileges**
- Privileges are granted **not for personal benefit**, but to ensure **independence and freedom of speech** in carrying out their duties.

### **G. Waiver of Immunity**
- A foreign state has the **right and duty** to waive immunity if it would **impede justice**.
- Waiver does **not affect the purpose** of the immunity (i.e., protecting free expression and independence).

> **Note**: Subsections (3) to (5) do **not apply** to **Canadian citizens or permanent residents**.

---

## **IV. Privileges and Immunities of Designated Senior Officials**

These are individuals designated under **paragraph 5(1)(f)** of the Act.

### **Who Qualifies?**
- The **Director-General** of UNESCO
- Any official holding a position **equivalent to a Deputy Secretary-General of the United Nations**

### **Privileges and Immunities**
- Comparable to those of **diplomatic agents** under the *Vienna Convention*.
- Include:
  - Immunity from legal process for official acts
  - Immunity from arrest or detention
  - Immunity from seizure of personal baggage
  - Exemption from taxes on salaries (if not Canadian citizens/permanent residents)
  - Exemption from immigration and national service obligations
  - Same repatriation rights during international crises as diplomatic envoys
  - Exchange facilities equivalent to those of diplomatic officials
  - Right to import **furniture and effects free of duty** upon first arrival in Canada

### **Waiver Rights**
- The **Director-General** may waive immunity if it would impede justice — **without prejudice to UNESCO’s interests**.
- The **General Conference of UNESCO** may also waive immunity.

> **Purpose**: Privileges are granted **in the interest of UNESCO**, not for personal gain.

---

## **V. Privileges and Immunities of Designated Officials**

Designated under **paragraph 5(1)(g)** of the Act.

### **Who Qualifies?**
- Director-General and officials equivalent to Deputy Secretary-General **if they are Canadian citizens or permanent residents**
- All other UNESCO officials not designated under (f)

### **Privileges and Immunities**
- Immunity from legal process for official acts
- Exemption from taxes on salaries (if not Canadian citizens/permanent residents)
- Immunity from immigration and national service obligations (for self and dependents)
- Same repatriation rights as diplomatic envoys
- Exchange facilities equivalent to diplomatic officials
- Right to import **furniture and effects free of duty** upon first assignment in Canada

### **Waiver Rights**
- Director-General may waive immunity if it would impede justice — **without prejudice to UNESCO’s interests**

> **Purpose**: Again, privileges are for **institutional function**, not personal benefit.

---

## **VI. Privileges and Immunities of Designated Experts**

Designated under **paragraph 5(1)(h)** of the Act.

### **Who Qualifies?**
- Experts performing missions for UNESCO

### **Privileges and Immunities**
- Immunity from:
  - Personal arrest or detention
  - Seizure of personal baggage
- Immunity from legal process for acts and words related to their mission
- Inviolability of all papers and documents
- Right to use codes and receive correspondence via courier or sealed bags
- Same currency/exchange facilities as diplomatic envoys on temporary missions
- Same baggage immunities as diplomatic envoys

> **Key Point**: Immunity continues **even after the mission ends** (i.e., after they are no longer employed by UNESCO)

### **Waiver Rights**
- Director-General may waive immunity if it would impede justice — **without prejudice to UNESCO’s interests**

> **Purpose**: Ensures experts can operate independently and freely in their work, free from legal or administrative interference.
