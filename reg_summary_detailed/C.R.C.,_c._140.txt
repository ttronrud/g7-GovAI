**Detailed Summary of the British Columbia Egg Order**

---

**1. Title and Citation**  
The regulation is formally titled the *British Columbia Egg Order* and may be cited as such. It serves as a statutory instrument that establishes specific rules and powers for the regulation of egg marketing in British Columbia, particularly in interprovincial and export trade.

---

**2. Key Definitions**  
The Order defines several key terms to ensure clarity and consistency in its application:

- **Act**: Refers to the *Natural Products Marketing (British Columbia) Act* (Loi), which provides the legal foundation and authority for the Commodity Board’s regulatory functions.
  
- **Commodity Board**: Refers to the *British Columbia Egg Marketing Board* (Office de commercialisation), an agency established under the Act to oversee the marketing of eggs in the province.

- **Eggs**: Defined as "eggs of a domestic hen produced in British Columbia" (oeuf). This definition limits the scope of the regulation to eggs originating from hens raised within the province, excluding imported or non-domestic eggs.

---

**3. Regulatory Authority of the Commodity Board**  
The Commodity Board is granted broad regulatory authority over the marketing of eggs in **interprovincial trade** and **export trade**. This authority extends to the same powers it holds for regulating local egg marketing under **Section 17 of the Natural Products Marketing (British Columbia) Act**.

This means that the Board can implement measures such as pricing, quality standards, and market controls in markets outside British Columbia and in international markets—similar to how it manages local sales and distribution within the province.

> *Note: The provision under "Revoked, SOR/80-90" indicates that a prior version of this regulation was revoked in 1980 (SOR/80-90), and the current version is the updated or reissued authority.*

---

**4. Power to Impose Levies and Charges**  
A central function of the Commodity Board under this Order is the ability to:

- **Fix, impose, and collect levies or charges** from individuals and entities operating within British Columbia who are involved in either:
  - The **production** of eggs, or  
  - The **marketing** of eggs (including sale, distribution, or export).

These levies are not arbitrary; they are structured based on **classification of persons into groups**.

- The Board may **classify producers or marketers into distinct groups** (e.g., small farms, large commercial operations, processors, etc.).
- Each group may be assigned a **different levy or charge**, based on factors such as production volume, market role, or financial capacity.

This tiered system ensures that the burden of levies is proportionate and equitable across different types of producers and businesses.

---

**5. Use of Levies and Charges**  
The funds collected through these levies and charges are not restricted in their use. The Commodity Board may apply them for a range of purposes, including:

- **Creation of reserves** (funds set aside for future use, such as during market downturns or supply shocks),
- **Payment of operational expenses** related to egg marketing (e.g., logistics, advertising, market analysis),
- **Covering losses** incurred from the sale or disposal of eggs (e.g., due to market collapse or price drops),
- **Equalizing or adjusting payments** among egg producers.

Specifically, the Board may use the collected funds to:
- **Equalize moneys** realized from egg sales across different producers during specified time periods.
- **Adjust financial outcomes** to ensure fairer distribution of profits or compensation, especially during periods of fluctuating market prices.

This mechanism helps stabilize the market and supports equitable returns to producers, particularly during times of volatility.

---

**6. Duration and Flexibility**  
The Board has discretion over:
- The **periods of time** during which levies or adjustments are applied.
- The **specific periods** for which equalization or adjustment of funds will occur.

This flexibility allows the Board to respond dynamically to market conditions, such as seasonal fluctuations, supply disruptions, or changes in demand.

---

**7. Scope and Application**  
The powers granted under this Order apply only to:
- Persons and property **situated within the Province of British Columbia**.
- Activities related to the **production or marketing of eggs**.

This ensures that the regulation remains focused on domestic producers and traders and does not extend beyond provincial boundaries.

---

**8. Legal Basis and Enforcement**  
All powers conferred in this Order derive from **Section 3 of the Natural Products Marketing (British Columbia) Act**, particularly as it relates to interprovincial and export marketing. The Board acts under delegated authority, and its orders are enforceable through the legal framework of the Act.

---

**Summary in Plain Language**  
This regulation empowers the British Columbia Egg Marketing Board to manage the sale and distribution of eggs both outside the province and internationally. It allows the Board to impose fair, group-based fees on egg producers and marketers to fund market stability, cover losses, and ensure fair returns during price fluctuations. These funds can be used to create financial reserves, pay operating costs, and balance income among producers—especially during difficult market times. The system is designed to protect producers, maintain market integrity, and support a sustainable egg industry in British Columbia.

---

**Key Takeaways**  
- The Order applies only to eggs produced in BC by domestic hens.  
- Levies are tiered based on producer type or size.  
- Funds are used to stabilize markets and support producers.  
- The Board has flexibility in timing and scope of financial adjustments.  
- It is a key tool for economic stability in the provincial egg industry.  

This regulation is a critical component of British Columbia’s agricultural marketing framework, ensuring that producers are protected from market volatility while maintaining fair and transparent trade practices.
