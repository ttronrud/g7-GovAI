**Detailed Summary of the Vancouver Organizing Committee for the 2010 Olympic and Paralympic Winter Games (GST/HST) Remission Order**

---

### **Overview**
This regulation, made under the authority of the *Financial Administration Act* (specifically subsection 23(2)a), grants a **tax remission** to the **Vancouver Organizing Committee for the 2010 Olympic and Paralympic Winter Games (VANOC)** with respect to **Goods and Services Tax (GST)** and **Harmonized Sales Tax (HST)** collected or collectible in connection with its obligations under the *Host City Contract* with the International Olympic Committee (IOC) and the International Paralympic Committee (IPC).

The purpose of the remission is to **support the public interest** in hosting the 2010 Olympic and Paralympic Winter Games in Vancouver and Whistler, British Columbia, by reducing the financial burden on VANOC during the planning, organization, staging, and broadcasting of the Games.

---

### **Legal Basis**
- **Authority**: The Governor General in Council, acting on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance, pursuant to **subsection 23(2)a of the Financial Administration Act**.
- **Statutory Reference**:  
  - S.C. 1991, c. 24, s. 7(2)  
  - R.S., c. F-11 (Financial Administration Act)

This gives the government the power to make remissions of tax for public benefit when it is in the public interest.

### **Core Provisions of the Remission**

#### **Section 2: Remission of GST/HST on Supplies to IOC or IPC**
- **What is remitted?**  
  VANOC is entitled to a **full remission** of any GST/HST collected or collectible **on supplies of property or services** made to the **IOC or IPC** under the Agreement.
  
- **Purpose of the supply**:  
  The supplies must be made **solely for the purpose of planning, organizing, staging, or broadcasting** the Games.

- **Conditions for remission**:
  1. The tax must have been **collected or become collectible** after **July 1, 2003**, and before **January 1, 2012**.
  2. The remission is **only granted if the amount has not already been rebated, remitted, credited, or refunded** under:
     - The *Financial Administration Act*, or
     - Any other Act of Parliament.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: This means VANOC cannot claim remission for taxes already compensated through other mechanisms.

#### **Section 3: Remission of Interest and Penalties**
- If VANOC has **paid interest or penalties** on any amount that qualifies for remission under Section 2, then **interest and penalties are also remitted**.
- **Conditions for remission of interest and penalties**:
  1. A **written application** must be filed with the **Minister of National Revenue** within **two years** of the later of:
     - The day the Order comes into effect (date of issuance); and  
     - The day the interest and penalties were actually paid by VANOC.

> üîç This ensures timely claims and prevents retroactive or delayed claims.

#### **Section 4: Application Requirements (Timing)**
- For any amount **included in VANOC‚Äôs net tax calculation** in a return filed under **Division V of Part IX of the Excise Tax Act** (i.e., in a tax return reporting net tax payable):
  - A **written application for remission** must be submitted **within two years** of the later of:
    - The day the Order is made; and  
    - The end of the reporting period to which the return relates.

> üìù This creates a **strict deadline** for claiming remission of tax that was included in a tax return.

#### **Section 5: Limitation on Remission**
- Remission is **not available** if the tax liability has already been:
  - Reimbursed,
  - Remitted,
  - Credited,
  - Or refunded  
  to VANOC or any other party under the *Financial Administration Act* or any other federal statute.

> This prevents double benefits and ensures fiscal accountability.

> The two-year window ensures that claims are made promptly and helps maintain administrative efficiency.

---

### **Purpose and Public Interest Justification**
- The remission is justified as being in the **public interest** due to:
  - The significant investment required to host the Games.
  - The economic, social, and cultural benefits expected from hosting major international events.
  - The need to ensure VANOC can operate without undue financial strain related to GST/HST on essential services and supplies provided to the IOC and IPC.

---

### **Scope and Limitations**

‚úÖ **What is covered**:
- Supplies of **property or services** directly tied to the planning, organization, staging, or broadcasting of the Games.
- Payments made to **IOC or IPC** under the Agreement.

‚ùå **What is excluded**:
- Any supply not directly related to the Games.
- Tax on supplies made to third parties unrelated to the Games.
- Tax collected before July 1, 2003, or after January 1, 2012.
- Any tax already refunded, rebated, or credited elsewhere.
- Interest or penalties not claimed within the two-year deadline.

---

### **Administrative Process**
1. VANOC must **identify** GST/HST liabilities arising from supplies to IOC or IPC.
2. Confirm that the supply was made **for the Games** and within the date range (July 1, 2003 ‚Äì January 1, 2012).
3. **File a written application** with the Minister of National Revenue within two years of the relevant deadline.
4. The Minister will process the application and grant remission if all conditions are met.

---

### **Conclusion**

This remission order is a **targeted fiscal measure** designed to:
- Support the successful delivery of the 2010 Winter Olympics and Paralympics.
- Reduce the financial burden on VANOC by eliminating GST/HST liabilities on qualifying supplies made to the IOC and IPC.
- Ensure transparency and accountability through clear eligibility criteria, time limits, and application requirements.

It reflects a balance between **public benefit** and **fiscal responsibility**, allowing a major public event to proceed without being constrained by standard tax obligations.
