### **Detailed Summary of the Fort McKay First Nation Oil Sands Regulations (S.C. 2005, c. 53)**

---

#### **Background and Legal Basis**

These regulations are established under **section 3 of the *First Nations Commercial and Industrial Development Act* (FN-CIDA)**, which empowers the Minister of Indigenous Services to make regulations for First Nations engaged in commercial and industrial development. Specifically:

- The **Fort McKay First Nation** submitted a resolution to the Minister requesting that the federal government recommend the creation of specific oil sands development regulations.
- A **joint Intergovernmental Agreement** between the **federal government**, the **Province of Alberta**, and the **Fort McKay First Nation** was concluded, outlining how the regulations will be administered and enforced by **provincial officials and bodies** as specified in the regulations.
- As a result, **Her Excellency the Governor in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Indigenous Services**, formally enacts the *Fort McKay First Nation Oil Sands Regulations*.

---

#### **Purpose of the Regulations**

The primary objective is to **implement a legal regime on the project lands that is harmonized with Alberta’s existing laws governing oil sands mining and related activities**, ensuring consistency while respecting First Nation governance and rights.

---

### **Key Definitions and Scope**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Federal Minister** | The Minister of Indigenous Services (formerly Indian and Northern Affairs). |
| **First Nation** | The Fort McKay First Nation. |
| **Project Lands** | A specific area of 3,381.48 hectares (8,355.8 acres) located in **Theoretical Township 96, Range 9, West of the 4th Meridian**, within **Fort McKay Indian Reserve No. 174C**. Precisely described as: <br> - Lot 1, Block 1: 1,304.11 ha<br> - Lot 1, Block 2: 830.57 ha<br> - Lot 2, Block 2: 1,109.11 ha<br> - Lot 3, Block 2: 137.69 ha |
| **Incorporated Laws** | Alberta laws listed in **Schedule 1**, as amended, with specific adaptations detailed in **Schedule 2**. These laws govern oil sands operations on the project lands. |
| **Regulation** | Defined as per the *Alberta Regulations Act* (RSA 2000, c. R-14), as amended. |

> ⚠️ **Important Note**: The term “enactment” in Alberta’s *Interpretation Act* and provincial offences procedures is interpreted to include the incorporated laws.

---

### **Application of Alberta Laws**

- **General Rule**: The incorporated laws apply to the project lands **subject to the adaptations in Schedule 2**.
- **Interpretation**: All incorporated laws must be interpreted in accordance with the **Alberta Interpretation Act (RSA 2000, c. I-8)**.
- **Key Clarifications**:
  - A reference to “enactment” in Alberta legislation includes the incorporated laws.
  - For enforcement purposes, “enactment” includes the incorporated laws.
  - **No legislative power** is conferred by these regulations on any individual or body.
  - All **non-legislative powers, duties, or functions** of Alberta laws (e.g., regulatory, administrative) are preserved for entities under those laws, **subject to Schedule 2 adaptations**.
  - Bodies and funds referenced in the regulations are the same as those under the applicable Alberta law.

---

### **Exclusions and Special Provisions**

#### 1. **Exclusion of the *Indian Oil and Gas Act***
- This Act **does not apply** to the project lands.

#### 2. **Prevalence Over Conflicting Regulations**
- These regulations **prevail over any conflicting provisions of the *Indian Act***.

#### 3. **Excluded Provisions from Alberta Laws**
Certain Alberta laws and their regulations are **excluded or modified** for the project lands:

| Act / Regulation | Excluded Provisions / Exceptions |
|------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Environmental Protection and Enhancement Act (EPEA)** | Sections 3, 21–24, 54, 55, 64, 144, 183–186, 224; clauses 53(b)(c); subclauses 134(f)(iv); Emissions Trading, Mercury Emissions, Oil Sands Monitoring, Pesticide Rules |
| **Oil Sands Conservation Act** | Full application applies |
| **Pipeline Act** | Section 40 is excluded |
| **Responsible Energy Development Act** | Sections 62–66; Enforcement of Private Surface Agreement Rules |
| **Safety Codes Act** | Sections 3, 47(4), 55(2) are excluded |
| **Surface Rights Act (SRA)** | Does **not apply** to project lands |
| **Public Lands Act (PLA)** | Does **not apply** to project lands |
| **Geothermal Resource Development Act** | Applies only with federal ministerial approval |
| **Historical Resources Act (HRA)** | Applies only with federal and First Nation agreement; excludes certain provisions related to sales/leases/dispositions of archaeological/paleontological resources |
| **Hydro and Electric Energy Act (HHEA)** | Applies only to transmission lines and power plants used in oil sands mining; public highway definition excludes Crown/local authority ownership |

### **Governance and Authority**

- The **Fort McKay First Nation acts through its Band Council** in all matters under these regulations.
- The **federal Minister and the First Nation** are considered **equal stakeholders** in decision-making processes.

#### Key Governance Clauses:
- **Section 9 (Environmental Protection and Enhancement Act)**:
  - A Commission decision specifically affecting the project lands is deemed to **directly and adversely affect** the rights of the federal Minister and the First Nation.
  - The term “person” includes both the federal Minister and the First Nation.
  - Both parties are **entitled to file applications** under sections 10 and 24(2).
  - The Commission **cannot order His Majesty in Right of Canada** to pay costs, penalties, or expenses under sections 21 and 25.
  - “Interest in land” is interpreted as **leasehold interest in land**.

- **Section 24 of the Conservation and Reclamation Regulation (AR 115/1993)**:
  - The Alberta Energy Regulator (AER) uses forfeited security to carry out **conservation and reclamation** of the land.
  - The reference to “Minister” is read as “Alberta Energy Regulator.”
  - Clause 24(2)(b), (4), and (7) are **excluded**.
  - Subsection 24(4) is read as **subsection 24(3)**.

- **Geothermal Resource Development Act (G-5.5)**:
  - Any order or direction by the Regulator must be **approved by the federal Minister**.
  - The federal Minister or First Nation may request an inquiry into matters related to the Act’s purpose.
  - The Regulator **cannot direct His Majesty in Right of Canada** to pay costs under sections 17, 21(3), or 24(4).
  - “Interests in land” = **leasehold interests**.
  - Regulations on entry onto private land without consent **do not apply**.

- **Historical Resources Act (HRA)**:
  - No power to exercise, give notice of intention to exercise, or affect **historic, archaeological, or palaeontological resources** under sections 16, 20, 30, 33, 34, 37 **without prior agreement** of the federal Minister and the First Nation.
  - The Act **does not affect property rights** in archaeological or palaeontological resources.
  - Records prepared by the Crown in right of Alberta remain **vested in the Crown**.
  - Regulations on sale, lease, exchange, or disposition of archaeological/palaeontological resources **do not apply**.

- **Hydro and Electric Energy Act (HHEA)**:
  - Applies only to **transmission lines and power plants** used in oil sands operations.
  - The definition of “public highway” excludes ownership by Crown or local authority.
  - Only the **federal Minister or First Nation** may approve actions under subsection 34(2).
  - Regulations on entry without consent **do not apply**.

- **Oil and Gas Conservation Act (OGCA)**:
  - A direction by the Lieutenant Governor in Council is **effective only upon written agreement** of both the federal Minister and the First Nation.

### **Procedural and Enforcement Requirements**

#### 1. **Consent and Approval**
- Any **consent, authorization, or approval** required under an incorporated law must be granted **only by the First Nation**.
- Any **notice or document** required to be given to an owner or registered owner must also be sent to:
  - The **federal Minister**
  - The **First Nation**

#### 2. **Financial Obligations**
- **No obligation to pay fees or charges** applies to **His Majesty in Right of Canada**.
- **No liability** for owners or registered owners applies to the federal government.
