## **Comprehensive Summary of the Human Pathogens and Toxins Regulations (S.C. 2009, c. 24)**

### **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**
The regulations are made under **Section 66 of the Human Pathogens and Toxins Act (Act)**, and are intended to:
- Regulate the possession, use, transfer, and handling of **human pathogens and toxins** in Canada.
- Ensure **public health and safety** through strict biosafety and biosecurity standards.
- Provide a framework for **licensing, security clearances, and oversight** of activities involving dangerous biological agents.

The proposed regulations were laid before both Houses of Parliament under **Section 66.1** of the Act and were formally enacted by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Health**.

### **3. Licence Duration and Renewal**

The **maximum duration** of a licence depends on the **risk level** of the pathogen or toxin involved:

| Risk Level / Toxin Type | Maximum Licence Duration |
|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Risk Group 2 human pathogen, Risk Group 3 prion, or non-prescribed toxin | **5 years** |
| Risk Group 3 (non-prion) human pathogen or prescribed toxin | **3 years** |
| Risk Group 4 human pathogen | **1 year** |

- **Renewal**: The Minister may renew a licence upon application by the holder, for further periods specified in subsection (2).
- **Duration factors**: The Minister must consider:
  - The applicant’s **compliance history** over the past 10 years (with the Act, regulations, Department of Health Act, Human Pathogens Importation Regulations, Health of Animals Act, and Health of Animals Regulations);
  - The **risks associated** with the controlled activities;
  - Any **other relevant factor** affecting public health or safety.

---

### **4. Licensing Conditions**

Every licence must include the following mandatory conditions:

#### **(a) Non-Obstruction of Biological Safety Officer**
- Licence holders and those conducting controlled activities **must not obstruct** the biological safety officer in carrying out their duties.

#### **(b) Pre-Notification of Key Activities**
Before conducting any of the following, a person must **notify the biological safety officer in writing**:
- Importing or exporting a human pathogen or toxin;
- Possessing a pathogen/toxin received from another licence holder or from someone conducting controlled activities;
- Transferring a pathogen/toxin to another licence holder or person conducting controlled activities.

#### **(c) Pre-Transfer Safeguards**
Before transferring a pathogen/toxin, the transferor must ensure:
- The recipient is **exempt from licensing**, or
- The recipient will conduct activities in a facility covered by a **valid licence** for that pathogen/toxin.

#### **(d) Pre-Export Compliance**
Before exporting, the exporter must ensure the **intended recipient** will conduct activities in accordance with **applicable biosafety and biosecurity standards** in the foreign jurisdiction.

#### **(e) Responsibility for Unreceived Materials**
- The recipient must make **reasonable efforts to locate** the material if not received within a reasonable time.
- Must **inform the biological safety officer without delay**.

#### **(f) Inadvertent Possession**
If a person discovers they are **inadvertently in possession** of a human pathogen/toxin **not listed in Schedule 5** (i.e., not authorized under the licence):
- Must **immediately inform** the biological safety officer;
- Handle and store it **appropriately**;
- **Dispose of or transfer it to a licensed facility within 30 days**.

#### **(g) Prescribed Toxins – 24-Hour Rule**
For **toxins prescribed under Section 10**:
- If the recipient does **not receive** the material within **24 hours** of the expected date/time:
  - Must make reasonable efforts to locate it;
  - Inform the biological safety officer **without delay**;
  - Provide **any relevant information** to prevent public risk.

---

### **5. Notification Requirements for Changes in Activities**

A licence holder must **notify the Minister in writing**:
- Of any **intention to increase** the virulence, pathogenicity, communicability, resistance, or toxicity of a human pathogen or toxin.
- Of any **change to physical structure**, equipment, or standard operating procedures that could affect **biocontainment** (especially for Risk Group 3 or 4 pathogens or prescribed toxins).
- Of any **change to their name** within a reasonable time.

---

### **6. Security Clearance Requirements**

#### **Eligibility**
- Only individuals **18 years or older** may be issued a security clearance.
- **Ineligible circumstances**:
  - The Minister **refused** to issue a clearance within the last 5 years.
  - The Minister **revoked** a clearance within the last 5 years.
- **Exception**: If the basis for refusal/revocation is later proven to be **false or inaccurate**, subsection (2) does not apply.

#### **Application Requirements**
An application must be:
- Signed and dated by the applicant.
- Include the following information and documents:
  - Full name, aliases, name changes;
  - Gender, height, weight, eye/hair color;
  - Date and place of birth; birth certificate (if born in Canada), or entry details (if born abroad);
  - Home and work addresses, phone numbers, email;
  - Addresses lived in over the past 5 years;
  - Names and addresses of employers and post-secondary institutions;
  - Fingerprints (from Canadian police, accredited private company, or Government of Canada agency);
  - Two valid government-issued photo IDs;
  - For non-Canadians: CV, valid visa, police record checks from all jurisdictions lived in over past 5 years;
  - Travel records (for trips >90 days outside country of residence, excluding Government business);
  - Spouse or common-law partner details (name, DOB, birth certificate, address, date of death if deceased or relationship ended);
  - A **statement signed by applicant and either licence holder or licence applicant**, certifying:
    - The need for the clearance;
    - The specific part of the facility for which access is required.

> **Note**: Each **separate facility** requires a separate statement under paragraph (2)(m).

> **Exception**: An inspector under Section 40 of the Act does **not** need to include this statement.

---

### **7. Security Clearance Evaluation Process**

Upon receipt of an application, the Minister conducts the following **record checks** to assess risk under Section 14:

- **Criminal record check**
- **Review of law enforcement files** (including intelligence)
- **Canadian Security Intelligence Service (CSIS) security assessment**
- **Credit check**

The Minister may **request additional information** to assess risk.

---

### **8. Criteria for Issuing or Refusing a Security Clearance**

The Minister must issue a clearance **only if** the applicant does **not pose an undue risk** to public health and safety, after considering:

| Factor | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| **Nature and relevance** of past events/convictions | Seriousness, frequency, recency, and sentence |
| **Involvement in harmful activities** | Includes: <br> - Use of pathogens/toxins for criminal acts or violence; <br> - Threats to national security; <br> - Membership in terrorist or criminal organizations; <br> - Association with individuals involved in such activities |
| **Positional risk** | Whether the applicant is in a position where they could be **induced or pressured** to commit an act posing undue risk |
| **Prior clearance history** | Previous suspension or revocation |
| **False/misleading information** | Any intentional deception in application |
| **Foreign clearance status** | Whether a foreign jurisdiction refused, suspended, or revoked clearance — and the reason |
| **Outstanding criminal charges** | If a charge would trigger a risk under Section 14, the application is **postponed** until the charge is resolved. |

> The Minister may **postpone** processing if an outstanding charge would constitute a risk under Section 14.

---

### **9. Validity Period of Security Clearance**

- The validity period is **determined by the level of risk** assessed.
- **Maximum validity: 5 years**.
