### üîπ **2. Definition of "Eligible Agricultural Goods"**
The term **"eligible agricultural goods"** refers to specific agricultural products listed in the **Schedule** attached to the permit. These are the only goods authorized for import under this general permit.

The list includes:
- Poultry (live birds, eggs, meat, offal, fat, sausages)
- Bovine and swine products (meat, cuts, carcasses)
- Dairy products (milk, cream, butter substitutes, whey, etc.)
- Prepared food items derived from these commodities

Each product is defined by:
- **Species** (e.g., *Gallus domesticus* for poultry)
- **Weight or size limits** (e.g., live broilers ‚â§ 185 g)
- **Tariff classification numbers** (e.g., 0105.11.22, 0207.11.92) from the *Customs Tariff* schedule
- **Specific exclusions or conditions** (e.g., origin restrictions)

### üîπ **3. Scope of Authorization**
- **Who Can Import?** Any **resident of Canada** may import eligible agricultural goods into Canada from **any country**.
- **No Requirement for Individual Permits**: This is a **general permit**, meaning no individual import permit is required under section 8 of the EIPA for these goods ‚Äî **except** where otherwise restricted.

> ‚ùå **Key Restriction**: A resident **may not** import eligible agricultural goods under this permit **if** they are also importing the same goods under a permit issued under **section 8.3** of the EIPA. This prevents overlap and ensures compliance with priority-based or special-purpose permits.

### üîπ **5. Exclusion Provisions (Critical Limitations)**

#### ‚ùå **Exclusion During Force of General Permit No. 20**

- This is a **temporary restriction** to avoid duplication or conflict with a more specific permit for grains and cereals.

> üìå **Exception**: 
> - This exclusion **does not apply** to eligible agricultural goods listed in items 68‚Äì98 that were **already imported** but:
>   - Were **not accounted for** under section 5 of Permit No. 20
>   - Were **not placed in a bonded warehouse** under section 5 of Permit No. 20
> 
> ‚Üí These goods are **still permitted** under this general permit, even if they were previously imported without proper documentation.
