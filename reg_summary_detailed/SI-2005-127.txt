# **Regulation of the Municipal Courts of Québec – Summary (O.C. 950-2005, 19 October 2005)**  
*An Act respecting municipal courts (R.S.Q., c. C-72.01), Courts of Justice Act (R.S.Q., c. T-16), Criminal Code (R.S.C., c. C-46)*

---

## **I. Background and Legislative Framework**

This regulation establishes the **Rules of the Municipal Courts of Québec**, adopted under the authority of several key statutes:

1. **Act respecting municipal courts (R.S.Q., c. C-72.01)**  
   - Section 56.2 empowers a **majority of municipal judges**, in agreement with the **chief judge**, to adopt **uniform rules of practice** applicable across all municipal courts.
   - These rules must be:
     - Compatible with the *Act respecting municipal courts*,
     - Compatible with the *Code of Civil Procedure* (R.S.Q., c. C-25) and *Code of Penal Procedure* (R.S.Q., c. C-25.1),
     - Approved by the **Government of Québec**,
     - Subject to the provisions of the *Regulations Act (R.S.Q., c. R-18.1)*, except sections 21 to 24.

2. **Courts of Justice Act (R.S.Q., c. T-16)**  
   - Section 98, 3rd paragraph, subpara. 2, assigns authority to the **associate chief judge of the Court of Québec** responsible for municipal courts to perform functions normally exercised by the chief judge in relation to municipal courts.

3. **Criminal Code (R.S.C., c. C-46)**  
   - Section 482(2) allows **each municipal court** to adopt its own rules of court, provided they are **not inconsistent** with the Code or other federal laws, and **subject to approval by the Lieutenant Governor in Council**.

---

## **II. Adoption and Approval Process**

- A **draft of the Rules** was developed in April 2004 by a majority of municipal judges, in consultation with the **associate chief judge** responsible for municipal courts.
- The draft was **published in Part 2 of the Gazette officielle du Québec on 2 June 2004**, with a **45-day period** for government review and approval (as per sections 10 and 11 of the Regulations Act).
- After review and consultation, the **Government approved** the rules with amendments.
- The final rules were **formally adopted by Order in Council on 19 October 2005**.
- The rules **come into force on the 15th day after publication** in the *Gazette officielle du Québec*.

---

## **III. Key Provisions of the Rules**

### **1. Definitions**
Standardized definitions apply throughout the rules:
- **Case**: Any stage of a criminal, penal, or civil proceeding, including motions or applications.
- **Court**: A municipal court in Québec.
- **Clerk**: Includes deputy clerk or replacement clerk.
- **Judge**: A municipal court judge.
- **President judge**: A judge appointed by the Government to preside over a full-time municipal court; includes the associate president judge when acting in place.
- **Judge responsible for the court**: Designated judge in multi-judge courts.
- **Parties**: Includes the Queen, prosecutor, defendant, intervener, impleaded party, or opposing party.

---

### **2. Time Limits and Exemptions**
- A president judge, judge responsible for the court, or a judge may **shorten time limits** or **grant exemptions** if there are **serious reasons** (e.g., illness, emergency, unforeseen circumstances).

---

### **3. Powers of Judges**
- Powers assigned to a **president judge** or **judge responsible for the court** **cannot be exercised by any other judge** in the court, unless absent or unable to act.

---

### **4. Access to Records and Exhibits**
- **Records and exhibits** may only be consulted **in the presence of the clerk or a person designated by the clerk**.
- **Copies of documents** may be obtained **only upon payment of required fees**.
- **Removal of records** is permitted **only at the request or with authorization of the judge or clerk**.
- After **expiry of the appeal period**, a party may **remove an exhibit** (with receipt), **unless the exhibit has been seized**.

---

### **5. Courtroom Management**
- The **president judge or judge responsible** determines:
  - The **use and purpose** of available courtrooms.
  - The **assignment of judges** to preside in courtrooms.
- Cases are **assigned by the president judge or judge responsible**.

---

### **6. Filing Requirements**
- All **motions or applications** must be filed **at the court office at least 3 clear juridical days before the hearing date**.
- **Oral applications** for inscription on the roll must also be submitted within this time limit.

---

### **7. Roll of the Court**
- Prepared by the **clerk under the authority** of the president judge, judge responsible, or a judge.
- Must include:
  - Name of presiding judge,
  - Name of clerk,
  - Record numbers,
  - Names of parties,
  - Name of attorney (if applicable),
  - Nature of offence, motion, or application,
  - Date, time, and courtroom number of the hearing.
- A **copy of the roll** must be delivered to the judge and **made available to parties** before the hearing.
- The roll must be **posted at the entrance to the courtroom** or at another location designated by the judge or court authority.

---

### **8. Sittings and Scheduling**
- Sittings are **fixed by the president judge, judge responsible, or judge**, after **consulting the clerk**.
- Sittings occur in the **morning, afternoon, or evening after 6:00 p.m.**, or at any time set by the judge after consultation with the clerk.

---

### **9. Minutes of Hearings**
- Prepared by the **clerk** and must include:
  - Names of parties, attorneys, and witnesses,
  - Exhibits and documents filed,
  - Amendments and admissions,
  - Objections and rulings,
  - Decisions rendered,
  - Any other matter the judge deems necessary.
- Exhibits are **classified by letter** (e.g., A, B) and **numbered in numerical order**.

---

### **10. Conduct and Decorum in Court**
- **All persons present must rise when the judge enters** and remain standing until seated.
- At recess or adjournment, they must rise again until the judge retires.
- At the start of a session or adjournment, the clerk announces:
  > “Silence. All rise please.”
  > “The Municipal Court of [Name] is now in session, the Honourable [Name] presiding.”
- After the judge is seated, persons are invited to sit.
- The clerk announces any recess or resumption.

---

### **11. Language and Demeanour**
- All persons speaking in court must be **courteous and respectful**.
- Anyone addressing the judge or a witness must **rise and remain standing**, unless **permitted otherwise by the judge**.

---

### **12. Prohibited Conduct**
- No conduct that **interferes with the dignity or good order** of the court is allowed.
- During hearings:
  - No conversation with others,
  - No addressing the clerk,
  - No consulting records,
  - No reading newspapers,
  - No taking photographs, films, audio or video recordings,
  - No use of pagers or cell phones in a resonant mode,
  - No eating, drinking, or chewing gum.

> ⚠️ **Media Note**: Media are **authorized to record proceedings and decisions on audiotape**, but **no broadcasting** of such recordings is permitted unless the judge specifically allows it.

---

### **13. Dress Code**
- All persons appearing before the court must be **suitably attired**.
- **Attorneys and articled students** must wear:
  - **Male**: Conservative trousers, jacket, shirt, tie, or black robe.
  - **Female**: Conservative skirt or trousers with blouse and jacket, or dress, or black robe.
- This requirement applies **unless otherwise ordered by the judge**.

---

### **14. Adjournments**
- If a party anticipates being unable to appear on the scheduled date, they must:
  - Notify the **other parties and their attorneys** in writing,
  - State the **reason**,
  - Request an adjournment.
- The judge may **exempt** a party from this requirement.

> ✅ **Requirement**: If an adjournment is granted, the **reasons must be recorded in the minutes**.

---

  - Indicate the **relevant pages**,
  - Highlight the **cited extracts**.
