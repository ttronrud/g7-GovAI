## üìù **Detailed Summary of the Aboriginal Communal Fishing Licences Regulations**

### üîπ **Regulatory Background & Purpose**
- **Revocation and Replacement**:  
  These regulations **revoked** the previous *Aboriginal Fisheries Agreements Regulations* (Order in Council P.C. 1992-1456), dated June 26, 1992.  
  They **replace** it with new, updated rules governing **aboriginal communal fishing licences** in Canada.

- **Legal Authority**:  
  The regulation is enacted under **Section 43 of the Fisheries Act**, which empowers the Minister of Fisheries and Oceans to establish rules for aboriginal fishing rights and activities.

- **Regulation Title**:  
  The new rules are officially known as the **Aboriginal Communal Fishing Licences Regulations**.

---

### üîπ **Scope and Applicability**

#### ‚úÖ **Where the Regulations Apply**
The regulations apply to fishing activities in the following designated areas:
1. **Canadian fisheries waters in and adjacent to**:
   - Ontario
   - Quebec
   - Nova Scotia
   - New Brunswick
   - Prince Edward Island
   - Newfoundland
   - Northwest Territories

2. **Tidal waters in and adjacent to**:
   - Manitoba

3. **Tidal waters in and adjacent to**:
   - Yukon Territory  
     *(Specifically for fish of anadromous stocks including:)*
     - Chum salmon
     - Coho salmon
     - Chinook salmon
     - Pink salmon
     - Sockeye salmon
     - Rainbow trout
     - Family Coregonidae (whitefish and cisco)
     - Arctic char

4. **Pacific Fishery Management Area (PFMA)**:
   - Fisheries in the waters of the **Areas enumerated and described in Schedule II** to the *Pacific Fishery Management Area Regulations*.
   - **Salmon fisheries in British Columbia**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exclusion**:  
> The regulations **do not apply** in **national parks**, where fishing is generally prohibited.

### üîπ **Issuance of Communal Fishing Licences**

- The **Minister** may issue a **communal fishing licence** to an **aboriginal organization** to allow it to carry out fishing and related activities.

- The Minister may **designate** in the licence:
  - Specific **persons** who may fish under the licence.
  - Specific **vessels** that may be used for fishing.

- **If not designated by the Minister**, the aboriginal organization may:
  - **Designate persons in writing** (for fishing).
  - **Designate vessels in writing** (for use in fishing).

> This provides flexibility and self-governance to aboriginal communities while maintaining oversight.

---

### üîπ **Conditions in the Licence**

To ensure **proper fishery management, conservation, and protection**, the Minister may impose conditions in the licence covering a wide range of matters ‚Äî including but not limited to:

1. **Species and quantities of fish** that may be taken or transported.
2. **Notification procedures**:
   - How and when designated persons/vessels must notify the Minister.
   - What documents constitute proof of designation.
   - When and to whom such proof must be produced.
   - What documents or information must be carried on board and when they must be presented.
3. **Vessel and gear marking**:
   - Requirements for marking and identifying vessels and fishing gear.
4. **Landing rules**:
   - Specific **locations and times** when fish may be landed.
   - Methods used for landing and how the **quantity of fish** is measured.
5. **Pre-fishing reporting**:
   - Designated persons or vessel masters must report:
     - Where and when fishing will occur.
     - Reporting method, timing, and recipient.
6. **Inspection requirements**:
   - Locations and times for inspections of vessel holds.
   - Procedures for conducting inspections.
7. **Limits on participants**:
   - Maximum number of persons or vessels that may be designated.
   - Maximum number of designated persons who may fish at any one time.
8. **Fishing gear restrictions**:
   - Type, size, and quantity of gear permitted.
9. **Fish marking**:
   - Conditions under which fish must be marked for scientific or administrative purposes.
10. **Disposal of fish**:
   - Rules governing how fish caught under the licence may be used (e.g., sold, kept, shared).

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: The general authority to impose these conditions is **not restricted**, allowing the Minister to tailor conditions to local ecological, cultural, and operational needs.

---

### üîπ **Prohibited Actions and Compliance Requirements**

- **No contravention**:  
  Any person carrying on fishing or related activities under a licence **must comply fully** with all conditions set out in the licence.

- **Exclusive access**:  
  Only **designated persons** may fish under the authority of the licence.  
  **No one else** (non-designated individuals) may fish under the licence.

- **Close Time Prohibition**:
  - A **close time** is established from **December 29 to December 31**.
  - During this period, **no person** authorized under a licence may:
    - Fish for
    - Catch, or
    - Retain any species of fish
    in the waters referenced in subsection 3(1) of the Quebec Fishery Regulations, 1990.
  - This close time applies **separately and individually** to each species in each body of water.

> This provision is intended to protect fish stocks during critical spawning or migration periods.

The *Aboriginal Communal Fishing Licences Regulations* represent a **significant step in recognizing and enabling Indigenous fishing rights** in Canada. By allowing aboriginal organizations to obtain communal fishing licences with defined conditions, the regulation:
