## ğŸ” 1. **Purpose and Scope**

### Purpose:
To establish **minimum health and safety standards** for employees working on trains while in operation, particularly focusing on:
- Lighting
- Noise exposure
- Use of elevating devices (elevators, escalators)
- Electrical safety
- Sanitary conditions in on-board accommodations and food preparation areas

- Applies to **employees on trains while in operation**.
- Applies to **all persons granted access** to trains by an employer (e.g., contractors, trainees, visitors).
- Applies to **non-employees** who perform work to acquire knowledge or experience â€” treated as if they were employees for the purposes of these regulations.

### ğŸ”¹ **3.2 Noise Exposure Standards**

#### Objective:
Protect employees from hearing damage due to prolonged exposure to high noise levels.

#### Key Metrics:
- **A-weighted sound pressure level (dBA)**: Measures human-perceivable noise.
- **Noise exposure level (Lex,8)**: Time-integrated exposure over 24 hours.

#### Exposure Limits:
| Condition | Limit |
|--------|-------|
| In any 24-hour period | No exposure to A-weighted sound pressure level exceeding the limit in **Schedule** (Column I) for more than the duration in **Column II** |
| At any time | No exposure to **more than 87 dBA** |

#### Actions Required:
1. **If exposure â‰¥ 84 dBA**:
   - Employer must **appoint a qualified person** to investigate.
   - Investigator must:
     - Identify noise sources
     - Assess exposure duration
     - Evaluate current controls
     - Determine if exposure exceeds limits (e.g., 84â€“87 dBA)
     - Recommend engineering controls or hearing protectors
   - Report must be **signed, dated, and kept for 10 years**.
   - Posted in a **conspicuous place** and provided to affected employees.
   - Provide written information about the risks of high noise exposure.

2. **If feasible**, employers must use **engineering controls** (e.g., sound barriers, enclosure) to reduce noise **before** relying on hearing protectors.

3. **If not feasible**:
   - Employer must **write a report** to:
     - Head of Compliance and Enforcement
     - Workplace committee or health and safety representative
   - Must provide **hearing protectors** (meeting CSA CAN/CSA-Z94.2-02 standards) to employees.

4. **Training**:
   - Employers must develop and implement a **training program** on:
     - Proper fit
     - Care and maintenance
     - Use of hearing protectors

5. **Non-employees** granted access to noisy areas must **use approved hearing protectors**.

6. **Warning Signs**:
   - Must be posted in conspicuous places if noise exceeds 87 dBA.

### ğŸ”¹ **3.3 Elevating Devices (Escalators, Elevators)**

#### Safety Rules:
- **No load exceeding design capacity** may be carried.
- **No operation** if any safety device is inoperative.
- **No alteration or interference** with safety devices without authorization.

#### Inspection and Testing:
- Must be conducted by a **qualified person** at:
  1. Before first operation
  2. After any modification
  3. **Every 12 months**
- Record must include:
  - Date and location
  - Observations on safety and condition
  - Signature of qualified person
- Records must be **kept for 2 years**

#### Maintenance:
- Must be performed by a **qualified person appointed by the employer**

### ğŸ”¹ **3.4 Electrical Safety**

#### General Rule:
- Where work is performed on **live electrical equipment**, strict safety procedures apply.

#### Requirements:
- Employees must be **instructed in safe procedures** for working on live conductors.
- Equipment must be **guarded** (covered, shielded, or inaccessible) when live or potentially live.
- If guarding is not feasible:
  - Equipment must be **insulated** from the employee or the employee from ground.

#### Control Devices:
- Must be:
  - Easily accessible
  - Designed for **quick and safe operation**
  - Located so access paths are **free of obstruction**

#### Lockout/Tagout:
- If only authorized personnel operate switches:
  - Switches must have **locking devices** accessible only to authorized persons.

> âš ï¸ If work is not covered by the above, **Part VIII of the Canada Occupational Health and Safety Regulations** applies.

### ğŸ”¹ **3.5 Sanitary and Hygienic Conditions**

#### On-Board Accommodation & Food Preparation Areas:
- Must be **clean, sanitary, and well-maintained**.
- Used in a way that maintains cleanliness and hygiene.

#### Key Requirements:
- **Separation** between food preparation areas and sleeping quarters.
- **Garbage disposal**:
  - Must have **tight-fitting covers**
  - Be **leak-proof and easy to clean**
  - Be emptied **at least once daily**
- **Potable water, heating, ventilation, and sewage systems** must be provided in all on-board accommodations.
- **Toilet rooms** must be constructed and maintained to prevent health hazards.

#### Vermin Prevention:
- All enclosed areas (including accommodations, toilets, food prep) must be designed to **prevent vermin entry**.
- If vermin enter:
  - Employer must **eliminate them immediately** and prevent future entry.

#### Storage:
- No person may store equipment in on-board accommodation, toilet, or food preparation area **unless**:
  - Storage is approved and does not compromise safety or hygiene
