**Detailed Summary of the *Possession and Export of Elvers Regulations* (S.C. 2019, c. 14, s. 31(1) to (11); R.S., c. F-14)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Possession and Export of Elvers Regulations* are a set of federal regulations made under **subsection 43(1)a of the Fisheries Act** by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Fisheries and Oceans**. These regulations establish strict controls on the **possession, transfer, and export of elvers**â€”defined as American eels less than 10 cm in lengthâ€”within Canada and in Canadian fisheries waters.

The primary objectives of these regulations are:

- To **protect and conserve fish populations**, particularly American eels, which are vulnerable due to overharvesting and habitat degradation.
- To **ensure proper management and traceability** of elvers from capture to export.
- To **prevent illegal or unregulated trade** in elvers, including those caught illegally or obtained from unauthorized sources.
- To **support sustainable aquaculture practices** through regulated access to elvers for cultivation.

These regulations come into force on **March 1, 2025**.

## **1. General Prohibition on Possession of Elvers**

> **Section 3(1)**  
> It is generally **prohibited** for any person to be in possession of elvers **unless authorized** by a licence issued under these Regulations.

This means that **no individual or entity may legally hold elvers** without first obtaining a formal licence from the Minister of Fisheries and Oceans.

---

## **2. Exceptions to the Possession Prohibition**

There are specific circumstances under which a person may possess elvers **without a licence**, including:

### **(a)** Persons who caught elvers under a **Fishery (General) Regulations licence (Section 52)**  
- Individuals who legally caught elvers under a valid licence issued under Section 52 of the *Fishery (General) Regulations*.

### **(b)** Transfer to aquaculture facilities or release into natural habitats  
- Possession for the purpose of transferring elvers to an **aquaculture facility** or **releasing them into fish habitat**, as authorized under the **Fisheries Act** or **provincial legislation**.

### **(c)** Persons who caught elvers under a **Fisheries Act licence**  
- Applies **only while transporting elvers** from the point of capture to a designated holding facility, **as specified in the licence**.  
- This exception is **conditional** and limited in scopeâ€”only applies during transport, not for long-term possession.

### **(d)** Commercial transporters  
- A commercial transporter may possess elvers **in the course of their business**, provided they have **no direct or indirect ownership or interest** in the elvers.

### **(e)** Airport personnel  
- Staff working at airports engaged in handling goods for air transport may possess elvers **in the course of their duties**.

### **(f)** Aquaculture facility operators  
- Operators holding an **aquaculture licence** may possess elvers **for cultivation** until they grow to **10 cm or more in length**.

### **(g)** Government personnel  
- Personnel of the **CBSA, CFIA, DFO, or provincial agencies** acting in the course of their duties may possess elvers.

### **(h)** Law enforcement officers  
- Individuals acting under federal or provincial legislation may obtain possession of elvers during law enforcement activities.

> âš ï¸ **Important Note**: The exception in (c) is **time-limited** and applies **only during transport** to a specified holding facility under a valid licence.

## **3. Prohibitions on Transfer of Elvers**

> **Section 3(3)**  
> It is **prohibited** to transfer possession of elvers to anyone **unless**:
> - The recipient is authorized to possess elvers under a licence issued under these Regulations, **or**
> - The transfer falls under one of the exceptions listed in **paragraphs 3(2)(b), (d), (e), (f), or (h)**.

This ensures that **only authorized individuals** can move elvers, maintaining traceability and compliance.

> âœ… **Exception**: A person who caught elvers under a valid licence (as in 3(2)(a) or (c)) may **transfer possession to any other person authorized to fish under the same licence**.  
> This promotes cooperation among licence holders and supports shared access to elvers for research, aquaculture, or other permitted uses.

---

## **4. Licence Requirements**

### **Issuance of Licences**
- The **Minister of Fisheries and Oceans** may issue **two types of licences**:
  - **Licence to possess elvers**
  - **Licence to export elvers**

### **Conditions That May Be Imposed**
Licences may include **any conditions consistent with these regulations**, including:

#### **Possession & Storage Conditions**
- Location and facility where elvers must be stored
- Time and manner of bringing elvers into possession
- Requirements for storage and handling

#### **Reporting Obligations**
- Must provide information to the Department, including:
  - Who the elvers were obtained from
  - Who they were transferred to
  - Quantity and circumstances of elver deaths during possession

#### **Transportation Requirements**
- Specific rules on:
  - How elvers are transported
  - Containers used (must be appropriate, sealed, and properly marked)
  - Marking, labelling, sealing, and unsealing procedures
  - Must be done by the licence holder, a fishery officer, a fishery guardian, or DFO personnel

#### **Recordkeeping Requirements**
- Must maintain detailed records, including:
  - Elvers possessed at any time
  - Source and destination of transfers
  - Number of elvers that died and the reasons for death
- Records must be **kept in a specific format** and **produced to the Department** upon request
- Records must be **retained for at least five years** after the licence expires or is cancelled

#### **Verification of Shipments**
- Any shipment of elvers must be **verified** by a DFO fishery officer, fishery guardian, or DFO personnel.
- Verification includes:
  - Packaging process
  - Weight of individual elvers
  - Total weight of the package

## **5. Export of Elvers**

> **Section 3(4)**  
> It is **prohibited to export elvers** unless authorized by a **licence to export elvers** issued under these Regulations.

> **Prohibited Exports Include**:
- Elvers caught **illegally** in Canadian fisheries waters
- Elvers whose possession originated from a person **not authorized** to possess them under these regulations

### **Export Licence Conditions**
The Minister may impose conditions in export licences, including:

- **Packaging location and time** for export
- **Information to be provided** to the Department
- **Declaration to CBSA** for each shipment (including shipment details)
- **Records** must include:
  - Name of the commercial transporter to whom the shipment was entrusted
  - Tracking number or similar unique identifier
- Records must be maintained and **produced to the Department** as required

> ðŸ”’ **Seal Integrity Requirement**:  
> No person may **unseal** or **tamper with** a container of elvers that has been sealed for export. This ensures integrity and prevents unauthorized access or diversion.

---

## **6. Prohibited Mixing of Elvers**

> **Section 3(5)**  
> A person must **not combine** in the same container:

1. **Elvers caught outside Canadian fisheries waters** with **elvers caught inside Canadian waters**  
2. **Legally caught elvers** with **illegally caught elvers**

This rule prevents mixing of legally and illegally sourced elvers, ensuring traceability and compliance with conservation goals.
