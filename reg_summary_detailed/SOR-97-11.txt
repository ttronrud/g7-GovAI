**Detailed Summary of the *Credit Information (Insurance Companies) Regulations* (S.C. 1996, c. 6, s. 80; S.C. 1991, c. 47)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Credit Information (Insurance Companies) Regulations* are a regulatory framework established under the *Insurance Companies Act* (specifically sections 489a and 607) to govern how **insurance companies and foreign companies** may use **customer credit information** in Canada. These regulations aim to:

- Prevent misuse of sensitive consumer financial data,
- Protect individuals' privacy,
- Ensure transparency and fairness in insurance marketing,
- Limit the use of credit information for promoting insurance products,
- Promote responsible and ethical data-sharing practices between financial institutions and insurers.

The regulations apply only to **credit-related insurance** and certain types of insurance where a company or foreign company has obtained credit information from its customers.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Authorized Type of Insurance** | Specific insurance products that are deemed acceptable for use with credit information. Includes: <br> • Credit or charge card-related insurance <br> • Creditors’ disability insurance <br> • Creditors’ life insurance <br> • Creditors’ loss of employment insurance <br> • Creditors’ vehicle inventory insurance <br> • Export credit insurance <br> • Mortgage insurance <br> • Travel insurance |
| **Credit Customer** | A natural person who has applied for or received a loan from a company or foreign company, excluding loans used to purchase insurance or secured by a policy. Also includes loans under the *National Housing Act* or *Small Business Loans Act* from approved lenders. |
| **Credit Information** | Any information obtained about a customer (or their employee/member/partner) due to a loan, credit card use, or trust/loan corporation involvement. Excludes loans for purchasing insurance or secured by policies. |
| **Credit or Charge Card-Related Insurance** | Insurance provided automatically to cardholders without individual risk assessment, covering: <br> • Loss/damage to goods purchased with the card <br> • Warranty claims from manufacturers <br> • Rental vehicle liability when paid via card |
| **Creditors’ Disability Insurance** | Group policy that pays part of a debt if the debtor or their spouse/common-law partner suffers bodily injury, illness, or disability. Applies to guarantors, directors, officers, or key individuals in entities. |
| **Creditors’ Life Insurance** | Group policy that pays part of a debt or credit limit upon death of the debtor, spouse, guarantor, director, officer, or key person in a business entity. |
| **Creditors’ Loss of Employment Insurance** | Pays part of a debt if the debtor or guarantor becomes involuntarily unemployed. |
| **Creditors’ Vehicle Inventory Insurance** | Covers loss or damage to vehicles held in stock for sale, especially when financed by the company. |
| **Export Credit Insurance** | Covers exporters against non-payment for exported goods/services. |
| **Group Policy** | A policy covering multiple individuals with individual certificates of insurance. |
| **Insurance Company** | Any entity (domestic or foreign) approved, registered, or authorized to offer insurance under federal or provincial legislation. |
| **Line of Credit** | Flexible lending with no fixed repayment schedule; total outstanding balance does not exceed a reasonable credit limit. |
| **Loan Company** | A body corporate under the *Trust and Loan Companies Act* or a trust/loan corporation under provincial law. |
| **Mortgage Insurance** | Covers losses due to default on loans secured by real property (e.g., homes). |
| **Personal Accident Insurance** | Covers bodily injury or death due to accidents, or hospitalization days. |
| **Small Business** | A business that qualifies as a small business corporation under subsection 248(1) of the *Income Tax Act*. |
| **Travel Insurance** | Covers trip cancellations, personal property loss, baggage delays, medical expenses, death, or hospitalization during travel. Applies to individuals or group policies. |

## **Core Provisions and Restrictions**

### **1. Use of Credit Information for Promotion (Main Restriction)**

> **No company or foreign company may use credit information to promote an insurance company, agent, or broker in Canada unless:**

#### **Option A: Only deals in authorized types of insurance**
- The company only offers one of the listed authorized insurance types.
- This ensures that promotion is limited to products where the use of credit data is appropriate and risk-appropriate.

#### **Option B: Promotion occurs outside the company’s offices and targets specific groups**
- Promotion must occur **outside the company’s physical offices**.
- It must be directed to one of the following:
  - All holders of credit or charge cards who receive regular statements,
  - All credit customers who receive regular statements,
  - The general public.

> **Purpose**: Prevents targeted, unsolicited advertising that could be seen as intrusive or manipulative. Limits use to broad, non-personalized outreach.

---

### **2. Promotion of Specific Insurance Policies or Services**

> **A company may promote a policy or service only if:**

- The policy is of an **authorized type of insurance**, or
- The policy is a **personal accident insurance policy**, and the promotion occurs **outside the company’s offices**, or
- The promotion occurs **outside the offices** and is directed to:
  - Cardholders receiving regular statements,
  - Credit customers receiving regular statements, or
  - The general public.

> **Key Point**: Even for personal accident insurance, promotion must be non-intrusive and not tied to individual credit history.

---

### **3. Exclusions from Promotion (Permitted Exceptions)**

A company may **exclude individuals** from promotional activities if:

1. The promotion would **contravene a federal or provincial law** (e.g., privacy, anti-discrimination, or consumer protection laws),
2. The individual has **writtenly declined** to receive promotional material, or
3. The individual holds a **credit card account not in good standing** (e.g., overdue, delinquent).

> **Note**: This provides flexibility while maintaining consumer autonomy and data integrity.

---

### **4. Prohibitions on Sharing Credit Information with Insurers**

> **No company or foreign company may:**

- **Directly or indirectly provide** credit information to an insurance company, agent, or broker,
- **Allow subsidiaries** to provide such information to insurers,
- **Share credit information with affiliates that are not subsidiaries**, unless:
  - The company ensures that the affiliate will **not share the information** with any insurer, agent, or broker.

> **Purpose**: Prevents unauthorized or indirect data sharing that could lead to misuse, profiling, or exploitation of consumer data.

---

### **5. Exception for Pre-Approved Arrangements**

> **Subsection (1) does not apply** (i.e., restrictions do not apply) if:

1. The company or foreign company **explicitly informs** the insurer or agent that the information is **credit information**,
2. The company has **established clear procedures** to ensure that the credit information will **not be used** to promote insurance business or products in Canada,
3. The insurer or agent provides a **written undertaking** (in a form acceptable to the Superintendent of Financial Services) that they will **not use the information** for insurance promotion in Canada.

> **Purpose**: Allows for **legitimate, mutually agreed-upon data-sharing arrangements** between financial institutions and insurers, provided safeguards are in place.

---

## **Key Principles and Objectives**

| Principle | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Privacy Protection** | Prevents insurers from gaining access to sensitive consumer financial data without consent or clear justification. |
| **Consumer Autonomy** | Allows customers to opt out of receiving promotional materials. |
| **Ethical Marketing** | Restricts promotion to non-intrusive, broad-based outreach (outside offices), reducing targeted advertising. |
| **Risk-Based Use** | Only allows promotion of specific, authorized insurance types, aligning with the nature of credit-based risks. |
| **Transparency** | Requires clear communication about what data is being shared and how it will be used. |
| **Regulatory Oversight** | The Superintendent of Financial Services has authority to approve undertakings and ensure compliance. |

---

## **Regulatory Context and Enforcement**

- These regulations are part of broader efforts by the **Office of the Superintendent of Financial Services (OSFS)** to ensure fair, transparent, and ethical practices in the insurance and financial sectors.
- Violations may result in **fines, penalties, or enforcement actions** by the Superintendent.
- The rules are designed to balance **the legitimate business needs** of financial institutions (e.g., underwriting risk) with **consumer privacy and rights**.

---

## **Limitations and Scope**

- **Does not apply** to:
  - Promotions of non-authorized insurance types (e.g., health, auto, life insurance not linked to credit).
  - Use of credit information for internal risk assessment or underwriting (which is generally allowed under other regulations).
- **Applies only to**:
  - Use of credit information **for promoting insurance products or services** in Canada.
  - Does not restrict the **use of credit data** for internal operations, such as credit risk evaluation or lending decisions.

---

## **Conclusion**

The *Credit Information (Insurance Companies) Regulations* establish a **strict and balanced framework** governing how financial institutions can use customer credit information for insurance marketing. By:

- Limiting promotion to authorized insurance types,
- Requiring non-intrusive, broad-based outreach,
- Prohibiting direct sharing with insurers,
- Allowing only pre-approved data-sharing agreements
