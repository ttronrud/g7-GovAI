**Detailed Summary of the Commissioner’s Standing Orders (Conduct) – Royal Canadian Mounted Police**

---

### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**

The Commissioner of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP) has issued these **Standing Orders (Conduct)** under specific provisions of the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act* (the "Act") and related regulations. The authority is derived from:

- **Paragraphs 21(2)(k) to (m)a** of the Act  
- **Sections 39.1b and 39.2b** of the Act  
- **Subsections 46(4)c and 47.1(3)d** of the Act  

These provisions empower the Commissioner to establish internal administrative procedures governing disciplinary actions—commonly referred to as the *conduct process*—for members of the RCMP who are alleged to have violated the **Code of Conduct**.

This regulation was issued on **November 25, 2014**, and is part of the broader regulatory framework established under **S.C. 2013, c. 18**, particularly sections 14(2), 29, 36(2), and 37, as well as the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Regulations, 2014* (the "Regulations").

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

To ensure clarity and consistency, the following terms are defined:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act*. |
| **Conduct Process** | Any administrative action, decision, or procedure related to an alleged breach of the Code of Conduct by a member. Includes investigations, hearings, decisions, and remedial or corrective measures. |
| **Parties** | Refers to the individuals involved in a conduct process—specifically, the subject member and the conduct authority. |
| **Regulations** | The *RCMP Regulations, 2014*. |
| **Subject Member** | A member who is the subject of a conduct process. |

---

### **3. Designation of Conduct Authorities**

Conduct authorities are individuals designated by the Commissioner to manage conduct processes. These individuals are typically officers or managers with command responsibilities.

#### **Who is designated as a Conduct Authority?**

1. **Detachment Commanders and Direct Reports**  
   - Officers or personnel who command a detachment or report directly to a manager or equivalent managerial position.

2. **Officers or Equivalent Managerial Positions**  
   - Includes mid-level supervisors and managers.

3. **Division Commanders**  
   - Officers in charge of a Division (a major operational unit).

> **Note:** The Commissioner may establish **minimum requirements** (e.g., qualifications, training, experience) that a person must meet to serve as a conduct authority.

> The Commissioner may **revoke** a designation at any time via **written notice**.  
> - Upon revocation, **any ongoing conduct process is suspended** until another conduct authority takes over.  
> - The revocation takes **immediate effect** upon service.

#### **Special Requirement for Senior Officers**

If a conduct authority is a **senior officer** (as defined in subsection 2(1) of the *Public Servants Disclosure Protection Act*), the process must comply with the **provisions of that Act**, including protections against unfair or retaliatory disciplinary actions.

### **4. Types of Conduct Measures**

Conduct measures are categorized based on severity and are imposed by conduct authorities depending on the level of the violation.

---

#### **A. Remedial Conduct Measures** *(Imposed by all conduct authorities)*  
These are used for minor or less serious breaches. The measures include:

- Admonishment  
- Direction to work under **close supervision** (up to 1 year)  
- Direction to undergo **training**  
- Direction to undergo **medical treatment** (as determined by a Health Services Officer)  
- Direction to attend **counselling sessions** or complete a **rehabilitative program**  
- Direction to complete a **program or engage in an activity**  
- **Removal, restriction, or modification of duties** (up to 1 year)  
- **Reassignment** to another position (no relocation or demotion)  
- **Reprimand**  
- **Financial penalty** of up to **8 hours of pay** (deducted from salary)

> The conduct authority and subject member may **agree on any other conduct measure**, **except**:
> - Financial penalties  
> - Corrective or serious conduct measures (which require higher authority)

---

#### **B. Corrective Conduct Measures** *(Imposed by officers or managerial personnel)*  
These apply to more serious violations and may be added to remedial measures. Include:

- **Ineligibility for promotion** (up to 1 year)  
- **Deferment of pay increment** (up to 1 year)  
- **Suspension without pay** (up to 80 hours)  
- **Financial penalty** (up to 80 hours of pay)  
- **Forfeiture of annual leave** (up to 80 hours)  
- Any **combination** of the above, totaling **no more than 80 hours**

> These measures are not available to conduct authorities who are only in a supervisory role (e.g., detachment-level officers).

---

#### **C. Serious Conduct Measures** *(Imposed by Division Commanders or higher)*  
These are for severe or repeated breaches and may result in long-term consequences. May be imposed in addition to remedial and corrective measures:

- Removal, restriction, or modification of duties (up to **3 years**)  
- Ineligibility for promotion (up to **3 years**)  
- Deferment of pay increment (up to **2 years**)  
- **Reduction to the next lower rate of pay** (up to 2 years)  
- **Demotion** (for a period of up to 3 years or **indefinitely**)  
- **Transfer to another work location**  
- **Suspension without pay**  
- Forfeiture of annual leave (up to **160 hours**)  
- Financial penalty (deducted from pay)

> **Demotion Specifics**:
> - Upon expiration of the demotion period, the member’s **rate of pay reverts** to their previous rate, **subject to any applicable adjustments** for rank or level.

> A conduct authority imposing a demotion must **specify the period** (maximum 3 years) during which the member is ineligible for promotion.

---

### **5. Conduct Boards and Review Authority**

#### **Conduct Boards**
- Composed of senior officers or appointed individuals.
- May review decisions made by conduct authorities and impose measures as appropriate.

> Conduct boards may:
> - **Rescind or amend** findings or conduct measures  
> - **Substitute** findings (e.g., from "no contravention" to "contravention")  
> - **Initiate a formal hearing** if the original decision is found to be unreasonable or disproportionate

#### **Review Authority**
- May be designated by the Commissioner to review decisions made by conduct authorities.

> **Review Authority Powers**:
> - May review a decision independently to determine if:
>   - A finding is **clearly unreasonable**, or  
>   - A conduct measure is **clearly disproportionate** to the nature and circumstances of the breach  
> - If in the **public interest**, the review authority may:
>   - **Rescind** a finding of no contravention and **impose a new finding** of contravention  
>   - **Amend or substitute** a conduct measure with one that is **proportionate**  
>   - **Rescind** a measure and **initiate a formal hearing** under the Act

> Before making such a change, the review authority must:
> - Serve the subject member with a **notice of intention**  
> - Allow the member **14 days** to submit written responses  
> - Render a **written decision** after considering submissions  
> - Include **reasons** in the decision if it amends or substitutes a measure

> **Important Limitation**:  
> If a review authority initiates a hearing, **any information not part of the original investigation** (including admissions or statements) **cannot be shared with the conduct board** unless the member requests it.

---

### **6. Hearing Procedures Before a Conduct Board**

The process is designed to be **informal, expeditious, and fair**, in accordance with the principles of procedural fairness.

#### **Pre-Hearing Requirements**

- Within **30 days** of being served with the notice of hearing (or as directed by the board), the subject member must:
  - Submit a **written admission or denial** of each alleged contravention  
  - Provide **written submissions**  
  - Submit **evidence, documents, or reports** (excluding the investigation report)

> The member may **request a further investigation**.

> The board may **order additional information or documents** to be provided.

> **Privilege Protection**:  
> Parties are **not required** to disclose information protected by:
> - Legal privilege  
> - Subsection 47.1(2) of the Act  
> - Subsection 56(3) of the Regulations

#### **Pre-Hearing Conference**

- The board may **hold a pre-hearing conference** (in any manner it deems appropriate)  
- Any **agreements, directions, or undertakings** reached must be **recorded in writing** and shared with both parties
