**Detailed Summary of the *Federal Prompt Payment for Construction Work Regulations (Dispute Resolution)***

---

### **Overview**

The *Federal Prompt Payment for Construction Work Regulations (Dispute Resolution)*, made under **Section 22 of the Federal Prompt Payment for Construction Work Act (S.C. 2019, c. 29, s. 387)**, provide a clear, structured, and time-bound framework for resolving disputes arising in federal construction contracts. These regulations establish a formal **adjudication process** designed to ensure **prompt, fair, and efficient resolution** of payment disputes between contractors and government entities, thereby promoting timely payments and reducing delays in the construction industry.

The regulations were issued by the **Minister of Public Works and Government Services** on **November 10, 2023**, and came into force on **December 9, 2023**, following registration under the Statutory Instruments Act.

---

## **Key Components of the Regulations**

---

### **1. Definitions**

- **Act**: Refers to the *Federal Prompt Payment for Construction Work Act*, which provides the legal foundation for prompt payment and dispute resolution.
- **Party**: Any entity involved in a dispute (e.g., contractor, government entity, or subcontractor).

---

### **2. Requirements for Proper Invoicing**

In addition to fulfilling contractual obligations, all **invoices** submitted for construction work must include the following information:

| Element | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Date of invoice** | Must be clearly stated. |
| **Contractor details** | Name, street address, mailing address, telephone number, and email address of the contractor who performed the work. |
| **Period of supply** | The timeframe during which materials or services were provided. |
| **Contract reference** | Contract number or authorization under which the work was carried out. |
| **Description of work** | Detailed description including quantity (if applicable). |
| **Amount payable and payment terms** | Total amount due and the agreed payment schedule. |
| **Payee details** | Name, title, street address, mailing address, telephone number, and email address of the party to whom payment is due. |

> *Purpose*: Ensures transparency, traceability, and reduces ambiguity in payment claims.

---

### **3. Notice of Non-Payment**

A notice of non-payment must include:
- All information required under **Section 13 of the Act**, plus
- The **amount that is not in dispute** (i.e., the portion of the invoice that the recipient agrees is valid and payable).

> *Purpose*: Allows the contractor to identify and address only the disputed portion of the invoice, streamlining the dispute resolution process.

---

### **4. Powers, Duties, and Functions of the Adjudicator Authority**

The **Adjudicator Authority** is responsible for overseeing the adjudication system and ensuring fairness, integrity, and efficiency. Its key functions include:

| Function | Details |
|--------|--------|
| **Training and Education** | Develops and delivers initial and continuing education programs for adjudicators. |
| **Certification Management** | Issues, renews, suspends, or cancels adjudicator certifications. |
| **Eligibility Oversight** | Ensures adjudicators meet all eligibility criteria. |
| **Public Register** | Maintains a publicly accessible list of adjudicators with their qualifications. |
| **Fee Scheduling** | Establishes and maintains a fee schedule based on adjudicator experience and dispute complexity. |
| **Code of Conduct** | Develops and enforces a code of conduct for adjudicators. |
| **Complaint Handling** | Establishes procedures to receive, investigate, and resolve complaints against adjudicators. |
| **Consent to Dismissal** | Evaluates whether an adjudicator should be exempted from determining a dispute under specific circumstances (e.g., conflict of interest, personal hardship). |
| **Replacement Appointment** | Appoints a new adjudicator if the original one can no longer fulfill their duties due to circumstances listed in Section 5 of the Regulations. |

> *Purpose*: To maintain high standards, accountability, and impartiality in the adjudication process.

---

### **5. Eligibility Criteria for Adjudicators**

To qualify as an adjudicator, an individual must meet **all of the following requirements**:

| Requirement | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Accredited member in good standing** | Must be a current, active member of the Adjudicator Authority. |
| **10+ years of relevant experience** | Must have at least 10 years of professional experience in the construction industry. |
| **No indictable offence** | Must not have been convicted of an indictable offence in Canada or a comparable offence abroad. |
| **Not an undischarged bankrupt** | Must not be currently bankrupt or have an undischarged bankruptcy status. |
| **Appropriate security clearance** | Must possess the necessary security clearance to handle sensitive or classified information (if applicable). |

> *Purpose*: Ensures that adjudicators have both technical expertise and personal integrity to make fair decisions.

---

### **6. Powers and Duties of an Individual Adjudicator**

Each dispute is determined by a **single adjudicator**, who has broad powers to ensure a fair and efficient process:

| Power or Duty | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Impartiality** | Must remain impartial and independent; no conflicts of interest. |
| **Fact and Law Determination** | Must ascertain relevant facts and applicable laws. |
| **Scheduling Directions** | Can issue directions on when hearings or meetings should occur. |
| **Submission Restrictions** | May impose limits on the length of written or oral submissions. |
| **Request for Documents** | Can require any party to provide supporting documents or evidence. |
| **Meetings and Inspections** | Can meet with parties and their representatives. With consent, may conduct site visits or inspections. |
| **Testing/Experiments** | Can carry out tests or experiments (with party consent). |
| **Appointment of Experts** | Can appoint experts or assessors to assist in resolving factual issues. |
| **Inference from Conduct** | Can draw reasonable inferences from the parties’ conduct before or during the process. |
| **Issue Directions** | May issue any other directions to promote expeditious resolution. |

> *Important Note*:  
> If a party fails to comply with an adjudicator’s request or direction, the adjudicator may **proceed in their absence** and make a decision based on available evidence.

---

### **7. Time Limits and Exclusions**

- **Time for adjudication**:
  - A determination must be made **within 20 days** of receiving the response from the opposing party.
  - If no response is received, the deadline remains **20 days from the due date**.
  - The deadline **may be extended by up to 5 days** at the discretion of the adjudicator.
  - A **longer extension** is possible with **consent of all parties and the adjudicator**.

- **Excluded periods** from the time calculation:
  - Any **holiday** as defined in subsection 35(1) of the *Interpretation Act*.
  - **Saturdays**.
  - The **nine-day period from December 24 to January 1** (Christmas to New Year).
  - Any **provincial construction holiday** recognized by a provincial government.

> *Purpose*: To prevent time delays due to public holidays or seasonal breaks, while still maintaining strict timelines.

---

### **8. Electronic Submission Requirement**

All documents required under these regulations must be submitted **electronically**.

> *Purpose*: Promotes efficiency, reduces administrative burden, and ensures secure and accessible records.

---

### **9. Scope of Adjudication**

- Each adjudication must address **only one matter**.
- **Consolidation of disputes** is possible under the following conditions:
  - The parties **agree** to consolidate their disputes into a single adjudication.
  - A contractor may **require consolidation** by notifying the other parties and the adjudicators.

#### **Rules for Consolidation**:
- When a dispute is consolidated:
  - The adjudicator appointed to the original dispute is considered to have **resigned** from that adjudication upon being informed of the consolidation.
  - A new adjudicator must be appointed to handle the consolidated case.
  - The new adjudicator may be appointed under **Section 13, 14, or 15** of the Regulations.

> *Purpose*: To avoid duplication of effort and reduce costs when multiple disputes are related.

---

### **10. Representation and Appointment Process**

- A party may act **in person** or be represented by:
  - Legal counsel, or
  - Another authorized representative.

#### **Joint Appointment Process**:
- Parties seeking a joint adjudicator must communicate in writing within **4 days** of receiving the notice of adjudication.
- The adjudicator must respond in writing (consent or refusal) within **4 days** of receiving the request.
- If refused, parties may request another adjudicator.
- The new adjudicator must also respond within **4 days**.

#### **Appointment by Adjudicator Authority**:
- If joint appointment fails, **either party** may request the Adjudicator Authority to appoint an adjudicator.
- The Authority must appoint the adjudicator within **5 days** of receiving the request.
- Within **2 days** of appointment, the Authority must inform the parties of:
  - The adjudicator’s name, address, phone number, and email.

> *Purpose*: Provides a clear, transparent, and accessible pathway to appointing a neutral third party.
