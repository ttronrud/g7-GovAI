### **Detailed Summary of the Information and Management of Time Limits Regulations (S.C. 2019, c. 28, s. 1)**

These regulations, made under **Section 112 of the Impact Assessment Act (IAA)**, establish detailed procedures for the **management of time limits** and the **requirements for project descriptions** in the context of environmental impact assessments (EIAs) in Canada. They were issued on **July 19, 2019**, and came into force on **August 28, 2019**, with a formal registration as **SOR/2019-283**. The regulations are designed to ensure transparency, consistency, and accountability in the federal impact assessment process, particularly regarding timelines, information requirements, and Indigenous and public engagement.

---

## **I. Purpose and Legal Authority**

- **Legal Basis**: The regulations are made by the **Minister of the Environment** under **Section 112 of the Impact Assessment Act**, which empowers the Minister to prescribe rules related to the timing and content of information submissions and assessments.
- **Purpose**: To provide clarity on:
  - When time limits may be suspended;
  - What information must be submitted by proponents;
  - How engagement with Indigenous peoples, other jurisdictions, and the public must be structured;
  - The format and language of submissions.

---

## **II. Suspension of Time Limits**

Under specific circumstances, time limits set by the Act (e.g., for submitting information or responding to requests) may be **suspended**, allowing proponents or the Agency more time to complete required work.

### **Situations Where a Time Limit May Be Suspended**:

1. **Request to Designate a Physical Activity or Project**  
   - A time limit may be suspended **if the proponent submits a written request** to the Agency for an extension.

2. **Changes to a Designated Project‚Äôs Plans**  
   - If a proponent proposes a **change in design, construction, or operation** of a designated project, and:
     - The Agency or review panel determines that the change would **alter the potential environmental effects** of the project; and
     - There is **insufficient information available** to conduct a full impact assessment or prepare a report.
   - In such cases, the time limit for completing the assessment may be suspended while the proponent collects necessary data.

3. **Non-Payment of Fees or Charges**  
   - If a proponent fails to pay required fees, charges, levies, or costs (as defined in **Section 76 of the IAA**) within the time limit set out in **Section 80**, the time limit for processing the request may be suspended until payment is made.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è *Note: This suspension mechanism is intended to prevent undue delays due to unforeseen data gaps or financial issues, but it does not waive the obligation to eventually meet the deadline.*

## **III. Requirements for Project Descriptions**

The regulations define the **detailed content** that must be included in the **initial** and **detailed descriptions** of a designated project. These descriptions are critical for initiating and guiding the impact assessment process.

### **A. Initial Description of a Designated Project (Under Subsection 9(5) of the IAA)**

**Mandatory Elements (as set out in Schedule 1)**:
1. **Project Basics**  
   - Name, type/sector, and proposed location.

2. **Proponent Information**  
   - Name and contact details of the proponent and their **primary representative**.

3. **Engagement Summary**  
   - Summary of engagement with other jurisdictions or parties (key issues raised, outcomes).  
   - Brief plan for future engagement.

4. **Indigenous Peoples Affected**  
   - List of Indigenous groups potentially affected.  
   - Summary of engagement with Indigenous peoples of Canada (issues raised, outcomes).  
   - Brief description of future engagement plans.

5. **Regional Studies & Assessments**  
   - Any relevant studies or plans conducted in the region (e.g., under Sections 92 or 93 of the IAA), including regional assessments or those by Indigenous governing bodies ‚Äî **only if publicly available**.

6. **Strategic Assessments**  
   - Any strategic assessments conducted under **Section 95** of the IAA.

7. **Project Purpose and Need**  
   - Statement of purpose and need, including potential benefits.

8. **Physical Activity Reference**  
   - Provisions from the **Schedule to the Physical Activities Regulations** describing the project (in whole or part).

9. **Project Components**  
   - List of all activities, infrastructure, permanent/temporary structures, and physical works involved in construction, operation, and decommissioning.

10. **Production Capacity & Processes**  
   - Estimate of maximum production capacity.  
   - Description of production processes.

11. **Project Schedule**  
   - Anticipated timeline for construction, operation, decommissioning, and abandonment ‚Äî including any planned expansions.

12. **Alternatives Considered**  
   - List of **technically and economically feasible alternatives** to carry out the project (e.g., different technologies).  
   - List of **direct alternatives** to the project itself (e.g., not building at all).

13. **Proposed Location Details**  
   - Geographic coordinates (including for linear projects: major ancillary facilities and spatial boundaries of the study corridor).  
   - Site maps at appropriate scale.  
   - Legal description of land (title, deed, water lot authorization, if already acquired).  
   - Proximity to:
     - Permanent, seasonal, or temporary residences.
     - Affected communities.
     - Land used for traditional purposes by Indigenous peoples (reserves, First Nation land, land under claims or self-government agreements).
     - Federal lands.

14. **Environmental and Social Context**  
   - Brief description of the **physical and biological environment** (based on publicly available data).  
   - Brief description of the **health, social, and economic context** in the region (based on public data or engagement).

15. **Financial Support**  
   - Description of any financial support provided or potentially provided by federal authorities.

16. **Federal Lands Use**  
   - List of federal lands that may be used.

17. **Jurisdictional Powers**  
   - List of jurisdictions with powers, duties, or functions related to assessing environmental effects.

18. **Potential Environmental Impacts (Non-Negligible)**  
   - List of non-negligible adverse changes to:
     - **Fish and fish habitat** (Fisheries Act);
     - **Aquatic species** (Species at Risk Act);
     - **Migratory birds** (Migratory Birds Convention Act, 1994).
   - List of non-negligible adverse changes on **federal lands**.
   - List of non-negligible adverse changes to the **marine environment outside Canada** due to pollution.
   - List of non-negligible adverse changes to **interprovincial, boundary, or international waters** due to pollution (as defined in Canada Water Act).

19. **Impacts on Indigenous Peoples**  
   - Description of any non-negligible adverse impacts on:
     - Physical and cultural heritage;
     - Traditional use of land and resources;
     - Historical, archaeological, paleontological, or architectural sites.
   - Based on public information or engagement.

20. **Impacts on Indigenous Health, Social, and Economic Conditions**  
   - Description of any adverse changes to Indigenous health, social, or economic conditions.

21. **Federal Work or Undertaking**  
   - If the project is on federal lands or a federal work/undertaking (under CEP Act, s. 3(1)), a list of non-negligible adverse effects must be provided.

22. **Greenhouse Gas Emissions**  
   - Estimate of greenhouse gas emissions associated with the project.

23. **Waste and Emissions**  
   - List of types of waste and emissions (air, water, land) generated during all phases of the project.

24. **Plain-Language Summary**  
   - A plain-language summary of all information required in items 1‚Äì24, in both **English and French**.

### **B. Detailed Description of a Designated Project (Under Subsection 15(1.1) of the IAA)**

The detailed description expands on the initial one, with **more specificity and depth**, particularly in areas like:

- **More detailed descriptions** of activities, infrastructure, and components.
- **More precise information** on alternatives and production processes.
- **More comprehensive descriptions** of environmental and social contexts.
- **More detailed descriptions** of potential adverse effects and mitigation plans.

> üîç **Key Difference**: While the initial description sets the foundation, the detailed description is a more thorough, technically rigorous version used to support the full impact assessment.

1. **Tailored Guidelines**
