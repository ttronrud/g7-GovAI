**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

**Title of Regulation:**  
Transfer and Amalgamation of Departments under the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act

**Issuing Authority:**  
Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister.

**Legal Basis:**  
The regulation is issued pursuant to **paragraph 2(a)(a)** and **paragraph 2(b)** of the *Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act* (S.C. 2003, c. 22, s. 207).

---

**Key Provisions and Actions:**

1. **Transfer of Control and Supervision:**  
   - The **control and supervision** of all portions of the federal public administration that were previously under the **Department of Social Development (DSD)** are **transferred** to the **Department of Human Resources and Skills Development (DHRSD)**.  
   - This means that the administrative oversight, operational management, and day-to-day governance of the DSDâ€™s programs, services, and staff are now managed by the DHRSD.

2. **Transfer of Powers, Duties, and Functions:**  
   - All **powers, duties, and functions** of the **Minister of Social Development** under any Act of Parliament are **transferred** to the **Minister of Human Resources and Skills Development (MHRSD)**.  
   - This includes statutory responsibilities, policy-making authority, decision-making powers, and the ability to issue directives or regulations related to social development matters.

3. **Amalgamation of Departments:**  
   - Pursuant to **paragraph 2(b)** of the Act, the **Department of Social Development and the Department of Human Resources and Skills Development are formally amalgamated** into a single, unified department.  
   - This new combined department operates under the leadership of:
     - The **Minister of Human Resources and Skills Development** (as the head of the department).
     - The **Deputy Minister of Human Resources and Skills Development** (as the deputy head, responsible for day-to-day administration and coordination).

4. **Effective Date:**  
   - All of the above changes take effect on **February 6, 2006**.

---

**Purpose and Implications:**

- This regulatory action represents a major **restructuring of federal government departments** aimed at improving efficiency, reducing duplication, and streamlining governance.
- By merging the two departments, the government seeks to:
  - Create a more cohesive and integrated approach to human resource management and social development.
  - Enhance coordination between policies related to employment, training, workforce development, and social welfare.
  - Improve accountability and resource allocation by consolidating functions under a single ministerial leadership.

- The transfer of powers and responsibilities from the Minister of Social Development to the Minister of Human Resources and Skills Development signals a shift in policy priorities, potentially reflecting a broader government strategy to align social services with economic development and workforce readiness.

---

**Conclusion:**  
This regulation marks a significant administrative reorganization within the federal government. It consolidates the Department of Social Development into the Department of Human Resources and Skills Development, transfers all ministerial responsibilities related to social development to the new minister, and establishes a unified departmental structure effective February 6, 2006. The move reflects a strategic effort to strengthen interdepartmental coordination and improve the delivery of public services in the areas of human resources, skills development, and social support.
