## ðŸ“Œ III. **Panel Designation and Procedures**

### Panel Selection:
- The Commissioner may designate a person to select a **three-person panel** (from Level 1 officers) to hear a grievance when:
  - The decision/act/omission is likely to generate **numerous grievances**;
  - The relevant law or policy is **vague, ambiguous, or contradictory**;
  - The law or policy is **internally inconsistent**.

### Panel Chairperson:
- One of the three panel members must be designated as **chairperson**.
- The chairperson manages day-to-day operations, including communication with:
  - The OCGA;
  - The parties (grievor and respondent).

### Decision-Making:
- A **majority vote** decides matters before the panel.
- In case of a **tie**, the **chairperson has a second vote**.

## ðŸ“ IV. **Filing a Grievance**

### At the Initial Level:
The grievor must submit a **grievance form** to the OCGA or their supervisor containing:

1. Grievorâ€™s **name and employee number**  
2. **Particulars** of the decision, act, or omission in question  
3. **Name of the person** responsible (or their replacement)  
4. **Date** the grievor became aware of the issue  
5. **Concise grounds** for the grievance  
6. **Prejudice suffered** (e.g., loss of opportunity, demotion, stress)  
7. **Redress requested** (e.g., reinstatement, compensation)

> The form must be filed **as soon as feasible** after the grievor becomes aware of the issue.

### At the Final Level:
The grievor must file with the OCGA or supervisor:

1. **Reasons why the initial-level decision is flawed**, including:
   - Violation of **principles of procedural fairness**;
   - **Error of law**;
   - **Clearly unreasonable** outcome;
2. **Redress requested**;
3. A **copy of the initial-level decision** (to allow review).

> This level is for appeals against an initial-level decision that is found to be unfair, legally incorrect, or unreasonable.

## ðŸ“® V. **Obligations of Supervisors and OCGA**

- **Supervisor** must forward the grievance form (and any request under subsection 8(1)) to the **OCGA** as soon as feasible.
- **OCGA** must:
  - Forward a copy of the grievance form to:
    - The **respondent**;
    - The **first officer in the chain of command** (or the person named in the initial decision).
  - If the grievor objects to forwarding to the first officer, they must submit a **written request** with supporting reasons.
- The OCGA forwards:
  - The **request** and the **grievance form** to the **adjudicator**.
- The adjudicator renders a decision on the request **as soon as feasible**, with reasons, and names an appropriate person to receive the grievance form if the request is granted.

> All decisions are **served on the parties**.

## ðŸ’¬ VI. **Informal Resolution Process**

### Mandatory Step:
- As soon as feasible after receiving the grievance form, the **respondent must contact the grievor** to discuss resolving the issue **informally**.

### If the grievor refuses:
- The respondent must provide **written notice** to the OCGA and serve a copy on the grievor.

### Continuation of Informal Efforts:
- The parties may **continue attempts** to resolve the dispute informally until the adjudicator issues a written decision.

### Adjudicator Powers in Informal Resolution:
- May:
  - **Review** a proposed agreement;
  - **Approve** an agreement;
  - **Resolve issues** related to implementation.

### Confidentiality Rules:
All communications during the informal resolution process are **confidential** and may only be disclosed if:
- Agreed to by both parties;
- Required by law;
- Needed to reach or implement an agreement;
- Required to protect health or safety.

> This protects privacy and encourages open dialogue.

## ðŸ“‚ VII. **Adjudicator Powers and Responsibilities**

### General Powers:
- May:
  - **Dismiss** a grievance if it is:
    - Moot, frivolous, vexatious, or an abuse of process;
    - Already adjudicated;
  - **Render a single decision** on a grievance and any substantially similar one;
  - **Consolidate** grievances (with consent of grievors) and designate a respondent;
  - **Sever** a grievance into multiple distinct ones;
  - Determine whether requested information is a **standardized test** or falls under Section 5 (e.g., personal records).

### Decision-Making Principles:
- Must decide **as informally and expeditiously as possible**, while respecting **principles of procedural fairness**.

### Handling Unforeseen Matters:
- If a new issue arises not covered by the Act, Regulations, or this Part, the adjudicator may give **any appropriate direction**.

### Enforcement of Compliance:
- May **remedy failures** by any party to comply with this Part.

### Case Conferences:
- May hold a **case conference** (on their own initiative or at request) to discuss the grievance.
- Communications during the conference are **confidential** and only discloseable under the same conditions as informal resolution.
- The adjudicator is **not prevented** from exercising other powers.

### Submissions:
- Parties may make submissions on matters in dispute.
- The adjudicator decides **how and when** submissions are made.
- A decision may be rendered **without submissions** if reasonable notice is given.
