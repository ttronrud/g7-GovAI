### **Detailed Summary of the Social Security Tribunal Rules of Procedure (2022)**

The **Social Security Tribunal Rules of Procedure**, adopted by the Chairperson of the Social Security Tribunal under **subsection 45.1(2)a of the Department of Employment and Social Development Act (DESDA)**, provide a comprehensive and accessible framework for the appeal process in Canada’s social security programs. These rules are designed to ensure **fairness, simplicity, transparency, and flexibility** in how appeals are handled before the Tribunal. They apply to appeals related to income security and employment insurance benefits and are structured to promote **access to justice** for all parties involved.

---

## **1. Legal Authority and Background**

- The rules were approved by the **Minister of Employment and Social Development** under **subsection 45.1(2)a of the DESDA**.
- The rules were issued on **November 3, 2022**, and are part of the broader regulatory framework governing administrative tribunals in Canada.
- The rules are **repealed in certain sections** under **SOR/2025-91, s. 3 and s. 8**, indicating that future amendments or replacements may be introduced (as of 2025).

---

## **2. Structure and Purpose of the Rules**

The Rules are organized into **nine Parts**, each addressing specific aspects of the appeal process. The overall goal is to:

- Ensure **clarity and simplicity** in the appeal process.
- Promote **fairness, participation, and efficiency**.
- Enable **full participation** by individuals—even those without legal representation.
- Provide **flexibility** to adapt procedures to individual circumstances.

> **Key Principle**: The Tribunal must interpret and apply the rules so that the process is **simple, quick, fair, and responsive to the unique needs of each case**.

---

## **3. Definitions (Part 1)**

This section defines key terms used throughout the rules to ensure consistency and clarity. Key definitions include:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Appeal** | A formal request to challenge a reconsideration decision (General Division) or a General Division decision (Appeal Division). Includes applications for permission to appeal to the Appeal Division. |
| **Appellant** | The person or entity (e.g., Minister, Commission) who files the appeal. At the Appeal Division, the appellant may be the Minister or Commission. |
| **Argument** | A written or oral submission outlining a party’s position on what the Tribunal should decide. |
| **Business Day** | A day that is not a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday. |
| **Commission** | The Canada Employment Insurance Commission. |
| **Contact Information** | Includes address, phone number, and email address. |
| **Evidence** | Any material presented to prove a fact—documents, photos, recordings, or testimony at hearings. |
| **Income Security Appeal** | Appeal from a reconsideration decision by the Minister under the Canada Pension Plan (CPP), Canada Disability Benefit Regulations (CDBR), or Old Age Security Act (OAS). |
| **Employment Insurance Appeal** | Appeal from a reconsideration decision by the Commission under the Employment Insurance Act (EI). |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Employment and Social Development. |
| **Participant** | Includes parties, their representatives, support persons, witnesses, and interpreters. |
| **Party** | The appellant and the opposing party (Minister or Commission), depending on the type of appeal. |
| **Permission to Appeal** | Leave granted to appeal a General Division decision to the Appeal Division under sections 58 or 58.1 of DESDA. |
| **Reconsideration Decision** | A decision by the Minister or Commission to review or amend a prior decision (e.g., denial of benefits). |
| **Reconsideration Request** | A formal request to have a decision reviewed under relevant legislation. |

## **4. General Principles of the Appeal Process (Part 2)**

### **Core Principles**
The Tribunal must ensure that:
- The process is **simple, quick, and fair**.
- The Tribunal **considers the unique circumstances** of each party.
- All parties can **participate fully**, even without legal representation.
- The process is **flexible** and tailored to the specific facts of each case.

### **Active Adjudication**
The Tribunal uses **active adjudication**—a proactive approach where it:
- Identifies key issues to be addressed.
- Determines appropriate procedures.
- Explains the rules and laws to parties.
- Helps participants understand the process.
- Decides the order of evidence or arguments at hearings.
- Asks questions during oral hearings.

> This ensures that parties are not passive recipients of decisions but active participants in shaping the outcome.

### **Communication with Parties**
- The Tribunal uses **contact information on file** to reach parties.
- It may **continue the appeal process even if it cannot reach a party**, without further notice—this is permitted under the rules.

## **5. Roles and Responsibilities of Participants (Part 3)**

### **Participants Must:**
- Be respectful and maintain a safe environment.
- Be honest.
- Comply with the rules, regulations, and appeal process.

### **Parties’ Responsibilities:**
- Prepare and file documents.
- Present evidence and arguments.
- Attend conferences or hearings.
- Question witnesses.
- Respond to submissions from other parties.

### **Representatives (Under Section 63.1 of DESDA):**
- Act on behalf of the party (e.g., present arguments).
- May represent the party for all or part of the appeal.
- **Do not need to be a lawyer**.
- Are responsible for full participation in the process, just like a party.
- Must share all information received from the Tribunal with their client.
- Must notify the Tribunal of any changes in contact information or of their withdrawal.

### **Support Persons (Part 3):**
- Assist parties during oral hearings by:
  - Organizing documents.
  - Taking notes.
  - Providing emotional or moral support.
- Do **not represent** the party.
- May attend as an observer.
- The Tribunal does **not communicate directly** with support persons outside of hearings.
- The party is responsible for sharing Tribunal communications with the support person.

### **Witnesses:**
- Provide testimony at oral hearings.
- Must answer all questions **truthfully**.
- Can be questioned by any party, representative, or the Tribunal.

## **6. Filing Documents (Part 4)**

### **Methods of Filing:**
A party may file documents with the Tribunal via:
1. **Email** (using the official Tribunal email address).
2. **Mail** (using the address on the Tribunal’s website).
3. **Electronic filing** (via the Tribunal’s online portal).

> The Tribunal may require documents in a **specific form or format**.

### **Filing Date:**
- A document is considered **filed on the date the Tribunal receives it**.
- The Tribunal **indicates the receipt date** on each document.

### **Deadlines:**
- If a deadline falls on a **non-business day**, it is **extended to the next business day**.

### **Document Handling:**
- When a party files a document to support their position, the Tribunal:
  - Adds it to the **appeal record**.
  - Sends a **copy to all other parties** as soon as possible.

### **Public Access:**
- The public may access documents in the appeal record **unless limited** under **section 3 of the Social Security Tribunal Regulations, 2022** (e.g., due to privacy or sensitive information).

### **Electronic Documents:**
- An **electronic copy is considered the original**.
- The Tribunal may:
  - Make an electronic copy.
  - Provide an electronic copy.
  - **Certify** an electronic copy as a true and accurate version.

### **Delivery and Receipt:**
| Method | Date of Receipt |
|-------|-----------------|
| Regular mail | 10 days after mailing |
| Registered mail or courier | On delivery confirmation |
| Email or electronic method | Next business day |

> The Tribunal applies **sections 22(1) to (3)** (likely related to procedural fairness) unless a party provides a valid reason to waive them.

## **7. How to Appeal (Part 5)**

### **A. Appeal from a Reconsideration Decision (General Division)**

To appeal a reconsideration decision (e.g., denial of CPP, disability, or OAS benefits), the appellant must file a **Notice of Appeal** with the **General Division**. The notice must include:
- Full name of the appellant.
- Contact information.
- Reasons for appealing.
- A copy of the reconsideration decision or its date.

> The Tribunal may require the appellant to provide:
- A **social insurance number (SIN)**.
- A **Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) business number**.
- Or any other identifying number.

**Deadline**: Must be filed by the deadline set in **section 52 of DESDA**.

**Notification**: The Tribunal notifies the appellant upon receipt.

### **B. Appeal from a General Division Decision (Appeal Division)**

To appeal a General Division decision, the appellant must file an **Application for Permission to Appeal** with the **Appeal Division**. The application must include:
- Full name.
- Contact information.
- Reasons for appealing.
- A copy of the General Division decision, date, or file number.
