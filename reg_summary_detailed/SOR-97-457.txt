## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Copyright Regulations (S.C. 1993, c. 15, s. 8; S.C. 1997, c. 24, s. 37(2))**

### üìå **Overview**
These regulations, made under **sections 59a and 62b of the Copyright Act**, establish the procedural and administrative framework governing the **registration of copyrights** in Canada. They define key terms, outline requirements for applications and correspondence, specify formatting and submission standards, set out fees, and provide rules for handling defective submissions. The regulations were officially **implemented on October 1, 1997**, and replaced the previous *Copyright Rules (C.R.C., c. 422)*.

## üì¨ **Rules for Receiving Correspondence**

The regulations establish clear rules for when correspondence is considered **received by the Copyright Office**, whether delivered physically or electronically.

### 1. **Physical Delivery During Ordinary Business Hours**
- If delivered during the Copyright Office‚Äôs **normal business hours**, it is considered **received on the day of delivery**.

### 2. **Physical Delivery Outside Ordinary Hours**
- If delivered **outside business hours**, it is deemed received **on the next business day** when the Office is open.

### 3. **Delivery to a Designated Establishment**
- The Commissioner may designate certain locations (e.g., branches or offices) where correspondence may be delivered.
- If delivered during **normal hours** at such a location and the Copyright Office is open that day ‚Üí received **on that day**.
- If delivered during **normal hours** but the Office is **closed** ‚Üí received on the **next open day**.
- If delivered **outside normal hours** at a designated establishment ‚Üí considered delivered during normal hours on the **next business day** when the establishment is open.

### 4. **Electronic Transmission**
- Correspondence may be sent **at any time** via electronic or other means specified in the **Canadian Patent Office Record** (e.g., email, secure portals).
- **Time of receipt** is determined by the **local time** of the Copyright Office‚Äôs location:
  - If delivered when the Office is open ‚Üí received on that day.
  - If delivered when the Office is closed ‚Üí received on the **next day when the Office is open**.

> ‚úÖ **Key Principle**: The regulations ensure **consistency and fairness** in determining when a communication is received, regardless of time or place of delivery.

- **For a work** (e.g., literary, artistic, musical, dramatic):  
  ‚Üí Must be submitted under **section 55 of the Act**.  
  ‚Üí Covers **only one work**.

- **For a performer‚Äôs performance, sound recording, or communication signal**:  
  ‚Üí Must be submitted under **section 56 of the Act**.  
  ‚Üí Covers **only one such item**.

> ‚ùå No application may register multiple works or multiple performances/sound recordings in a single submission.

### 2. **Required Application Components**
Each application must include:
- A **fee** (as specified in the Schedule, Column 2, Item 1).

## üìù **Request for Assignment or Licence**

A request to register:
- An **assignment of copyright**, or
- A **licence granting an interest in a copyright**

Must be:
- **In writing**, and
- Contain the following information:
  1. **Names and addresses** of:
     - Assignor (or licensor) and assignee (or licensee)
  2. **Description of the interest being transferred** (e.g., exclusive, non-exclusive, duration)
  3. **Title of the work, performance, sound recording, or signal**
  4. **Registration number** (if available)

### 3. **Required Supporting Evidence**
- Must include the **evidence required by paragraph 57(1)(a) of the Act** (e.g., proof of ownership, contract, or agreement).

### 4. **Fees**
- Must be accompanied by the **fee listed in Column 2 of Item 2 of the Schedule**.

## üö´ **Defective Applications and Remedies**

If the Commissioner determines that an application or request is **defective** (e.g., missing information, incomplete details):

1. The Commissioner will issue a **written notice** of deficiency.
2. The applicant has **60 days** from the date of notice to **correct the defect**.
3. If the defect is **not cured within 60 days**, the application or request is **rejected**.
4. No further action can be taken unless:
   - A **new application or request** is filed, and
   - The **correct fee** is paid.

> ‚úÖ This creates a **clear process for correction**, promoting accuracy and compliance.

## üìù **Formatting and Submission Standards**

All applications and correspondence must meet strict formatting requirements:

- Must be **legible and clear**.
- If submitted on paper:
  - Size: **21 cm √ó 28 cm to 22 cm √ó 35 cm** (on one side only).
  - Margins: **At least 2.5 cm on the left and top**.
  - Paper must be **white**.

> ‚ùå Applications on non-white paper, double-sided, or improperly formatted may be rejected.

## üí∞ **Fees for Services**

Fees are set out in the **Schedule** and vary by service type. Key fee categories include:

| Service | Fee Components |
|--------|----------------|
| **Application for copyright registration** | Base fee (from Schedule, Item 1, Column 2) |
| **Request for assignment or licence** | Base fee (from Schedule, Item 2, Column 2) |
| **Certification services** | Base fee + **per page charge** |
| **Copy services** | Base fee + **per copyright charge** |
| **Physical copy provision** | Additional fee **per physical medium** (after the first) |
| **Use of Copyright Office equipment** | Fee applies if user makes copy themselves |
| **Copyright Office makes copy** | Fee applies per request, plus per copyright |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: Fees are clearly tied to the nature and volume of the service requested.

## üöÄ **Online Submission Rules**

Applications and requests may be submitted **online** via the **Canadian Intellectual Property Office (CIPO) website**.

- **Online submission** is accepted under:
  - Section 55 (for works)
  - Section 56 (for performances/sound recordings)
  - For assignment/licence requests

> ‚úÖ Online submission simplifies access, reduces processing time, and ensures secure and traceable records.

> ‚ùå In all other cases (offline, paper, or non-online), submissions must follow the standard procedures outlined in the regulation.
