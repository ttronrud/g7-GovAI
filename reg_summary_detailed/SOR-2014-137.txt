**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

This regulation, issued under the authority of **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, pursuant to **Section 3 of the Bretton Woods and Related Agreements Act (R.S., c. B-7, s. 1; c. 24 (1st Supp.), s. 3)**, establishes the **Privileges and Immunities Order** for three key international financial institutions:

1. **The International Development Association (IDA)**  
2. **The International Finance Corporation (IFC)**  
3. **The Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA)**

---

### **Legal Basis and Authority**

- The regulation is enacted under **Section 3 of the Bretton Woods and Related Agreements Act**, which grants the Governor General in Council (acting on the advice of the Minister of Finance) the power to give effect to the provisions of international agreements concluded under the Bretton Woods system.
- These agreements are incorporated into Canadian law through this Order, giving them **domestic legal force** in Canada.

---

### **Key Provisions with Force of Law in Canada**

#### 1. **International Development Association (IDA)**  
- **Sections 2 to 9 of Article VIII** of the Articles of Agreement of the IDA (as set out in **Schedule III** to the Bretton Woods and Related Agreements Act) are given **full force and effect under Canadian law**.
- These provisions typically cover:
  - **Privileges and immunities** of IDA officials and staff.
  - **Immunity from legal process** in Canada (e.g., protection from arrest, search, or legal proceedings).
  - **Exemption from taxation** in Canada (for certain staff and operations).
  - **Non-applicability of Canadian laws** that would interfere with the operations of the IDA.
  - **Freedom of movement** and **non-discrimination** in dealings with Canadian entities.
  - **Immunity from civil and criminal liability** arising from official duties.

#### 2. **International Finance Corporation (IFC)**  
- **Sections 2 to 9 and Section 11 of Article VI** of the Articles of Agreement of the IFC (as set out in **Schedule IV** to the Act) are given **force of law in Canada**.
- These provisions provide:
  - **Privileges and immunities** for IFC officials, employees, and agents.
  - **Immunity from legal proceedings** in Canada for acts performed in the course of official duties.
  - **Exemption from Canadian taxes** (e.g., income tax, corporate tax) for certain personnel and operations.
  - Protection against **arbitration or litigation** in Canadian courts over actions taken in the performance of IFC functions.
  - **Freedom from interference** by Canadian authorities in the IFC’s operations or management.

> Note: Section 11 of Article VI specifically addresses **immunity from liability for acts done in the exercise of official functions**, which is a critical protection for institutional operations.

#### 3. **Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA)**  
- **Articles 44 to 48 and Article 50 of Chapter VII** of the Convention establishing MIGA (as set out in **Schedule V** to the Act) are given **force of law in Canada**.
- These provisions include:
  - **Immunity from legal process** for MIGA officials and staff acting in their official capacity.
  - **Exemption from taxation** in Canada for certain employees and operations.
  - **Protection from civil and criminal liability** for actions taken in the course of guaranteeing investments.
  - **Non-interference by Canadian authorities** in MIGA’s operations, including its guarantee and risk management functions.
  - **Freedom of movement and residence** for MIGA personnel in Canada.

---

### **Purpose and Significance**

This Order ensures that the operations of these international development institutions are **not unduly hindered by Canadian laws or judicial processes**, enabling them to:

- Operate effectively in Canada and with Canadian partners.
- Provide financial support to developing countries without fear of legal or fiscal retaliation.
- Maintain the integrity and independence of their functions, such as investment guarantees, technical assistance, and development financing.

By incorporating these international agreements into Canadian law, Canada recognizes and respects the **sovereign status and operational autonomy** of these multilateral institutions under international law.

---

### **Effective Date**

- The Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** in the Canada Gazette.  
- This ensures immediate legal effect upon publication, allowing Canadian courts, government agencies, and institutions to recognize and apply the privileges and immunities provided.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation is a critical instrument of Canada’s participation in the global financial architecture established at Bretton Woods. It enables Canada to:

- Fully implement international development and investment support mechanisms.
- Protect the legal and operational independence of IDA, IFC, and MIGA in Canada.
- Uphold Canada’s international obligations under the agreements governing these institutions.

It reflects Canada’s commitment to multilateralism, development cooperation, and the rule of law in international financial institutions.
