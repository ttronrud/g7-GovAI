### üìù II. **General Requirements for Documents and Procedures**

#### 1. **Prescribed Documents**
- All documents required by the Act or these Rules must conform to the **form prescribed**.
- Modifications may be allowed if necessary due to circumstances.
- Deviations are permitted **only if authorized under section 32 of the Interpretation Act**.

#### 2. **Ordinary Court Procedure**
- In cases not explicitly covered by the Act or these Rules, courts may apply **ordinary procedural rules** within their jurisdiction‚Äî**provided they do not conflict** with the BIA or these Rules.

#### 3. **Time Periods (Less Than 6 Days)**
- When a period of less than six days is specified for an act or proceeding:
  - **Saturdays and holidays are excluded** from the calculation.
  - Only weekdays (Mon‚ÄìFri) are counted.

#### 4. **Service of Documents Outside Business Hours**
- A notice or document received by a Division Office **outside business hours** is deemed received on:
  - The **next business day** if received between **end of business and midnight** on a business day.
  - The **next business day** if received on a **Saturday or holiday**.
  - **At the beginning of business hours** if received between **midnight and opening time** on a business day.
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception**: This rule **does not apply** to documents filed by **facsimile** in Part III proceedings.

#### 5. **Method of Service**
- Unless otherwise specified:
  - Notices must be served **personally**, by **mail**, **courier**, **facsimile**, or **electronic transmission**.
- **Minimum Time Requirements**:
  - For **personal delivery, facsimile, or electronic transmission**: Must be received **at least 4 days before** the event.
  - For **mail or courier**: Must be sent **at least 10 days before** the event.
- **Proof of Service**:
  - The person giving or sending the notice (e.g., trustee, receiver) must prepare and retain an **affidavit** or proof of service in their files.

#### 6. **Exemptions and Flexibility**
- The court may, on an **ex parte application**,:
  - Exempt a party from time limits.
  - Order **custom terms or conditions**, including adjustments to deadlines.

### üìÇ III. **Filing and Court Proceedings Requirements**

#### 1. **Filing of Applications**
- All applications to the court must be made by **motion**, unless the court orders otherwise.
- The Superintendent may **intervene** in any application by filing a notice of intervention.

#### 2. **Filing of Motions**
- A party must file with the court at least **one day before the hearing**:
  - The original notice of motion or motion.
  - Every affidavit supporting the motion.
  - Proof of service (if applicable).

#### 3. **Examination and Discovery**
- A party may, with **leave of court**,:
  - Examine another party or individual.
  - Require the production of documents.
  - Require a person to appear for examination on an affidavit filed with the court.
- An application for leave may be made **ex parte**.

#### 4. **Court Orders and Filings**
- All court proceedings must:
  - Be **dated**.
  - Be **entitled** with the name of the court and the phrase **‚Äúin Bankruptcy and Insolvency‚Äù**.

- Unless otherwise directed by the Chief Justice, Associate Chief Justice, or Commissioner under section 184 of the BIA:
  - All documents required to be filed in court must **first be filed at the registrar‚Äôs office**.

#### 6. **Transfer of Proceedings**
- If proceedings are transferred from one court to another under subsections 187(7) or (10) of the BIA:
  - The **registrar of the original court** must send the file to the **registrar of the new court**, with a **copy of the transfer order attached**.

### ‚öñÔ∏è IV. **Appeals and Review**

#### 1. **Appeal from Registrar‚Äôs Order**
- Must be made by **motion** to a judge.
- Must be filed within **10 days** of the order or decision, or within a further time set by the judge.
- The notice of motion must state the **grounds of appeal**.

#### 2. **Appeal to Court of Appeal**
- Under subsection 183(2) of the BIA:
  - Must be filed within **10 days** of the order or decision.
  - Must include a request for leave to appeal (if applicable).
- The registrar of the appealed court transmits the notice and file to the **court of appeal**.

#### 3. **Request for Instructions**
- The **official receiver** may request instructions from:
  - The registrar (if not the official receiver), or
  - The judge (if the official receiver is also the registrar).
- This applies in cases of doubt regarding interpretation of the Act, Rules, or directives.

### üí∞ V. **Bill of Costs and Taxation**

#### 1. **When Taxation is Required**
- All bills of costs for legal services **exceeding $2,500 in aggregate** (excluding federal/provincial taxes) must be **taxed by the taxing officer**.
- Bills below $2,500 are exempt.

#### 2. **Requirements for a Valid Bill of Costs**
- Must describe the **nature of legal services** in a **fair, reasonable, and detailed** manner.
- Must be taxed only if:
  - The **trustee is present** at the taxation, OR
  - The bill includes a **declaration signed by the trustee** stating that:
    - The services were **duly authorized and rendered**.
    - The charges are **reasonable in the trustee‚Äôs opinion**.

#### 3. **Taxing Officer‚Äôs Authority**
- The officer must determine that:
  - Services were **duly rendered**.
  - Charges are **reasonable** and **in accordance with the tariff** (if applicable).
  - Services are **accounted for** and not ones the trustee should have performed.
  - Services were **authorized and approved** under the Act (if required).

#### 4. **Effect of Taxed Bill**
- Once taxed, the taxing officer:
  - Makes a **statement of taxation**.
