## ğŸ¯ **Primary Objective**
To **minimize environmental and human health risks** associated with the use of **tetrachloroethylene (PCE)** in dry cleaning by:
- Enforcing strict operational standards,
- Mandating waste management practices,
- Requiring detailed reporting and recordkeeping,
- Promoting safer technologies (e.g., closed-loop systems).

Tetrachloroethylene is a **persistent organic pollutant (POP)** and a known **carcinogen**; its release into air, water, or soil poses significant health and ecological risks.

## ğŸšš **Waste Management Requirements**

### 1. **Wastewater Handling**
Owners/operators must ensure **wastewater is either**:
- **Transported to a licensed waste management facility no less than once every 12 months**, **OR**
- **Treated on-site** using a full on-site treatment system that includes:
  - A **second PCE-water separator** (to recover PCE from effluent),
  - An **initial activated carbon filter** (to remove PCE),
  - A **monitor-alarm system** that automatically shuts down the system when the filter becomes saturated,
  - A **second activated carbon filter** (to further purify effluent after saturation detection).

> âš ï¸ When transporting wastewater, **all wastewater present in the facility at the time of transport must be moved**, including any not treated on-site.

---

### 2. **Residue Handling**
- All **residue** (solid/liquid waste containing PCE) must be **transported to a waste management facility at least once every 12 months**.
- All residue present in the facility at the time of transport must be removed.

## ğŸ“ **Reporting and Recordkeeping Requirements**

All persons involved in PCE use, import, recycling, or sale must maintain records and submit annual reports to the **Minister of the Environment** by **April 30 of the following calendar year**.

### 1. **Importers of PCE**
- Maintain records of:
  - Name, address, contact details
  - Authorized representative
- Report annually:
  - Total quantity imported
  - Quantity used for dry cleaning (own use and for others)
  - Quantity used for degreasing, chemical feedstock, or other purposes
- Include a **confidentiality statement** if applicable (under CEPA 313)

---

### 2. **Recyclers of PCE**
- "Recycling" means **recovering and cleaning** PCE (e.g., reclaiming), **excluding** re-use in dry-cleaning machines.
- Must report:
  - Total quantity recycled
  - Quantity recycled for dry cleaning, degreasing, or other uses
- Maintain records and include confidentiality request if needed

---

### 3. **Sellers of PCE**
- Must report:
  - Total quantity of **virgin** and **recycled** PCE sold to dry-cleaning facilities
  - For each facility:
    - Name and address of owner/operator
    - Quantity and date of each sale (virgin and recycled)

---

### 4. **Dry-Cleaning Facility Operators**
- Must report annually for **each facility**:
  - Purchases of PCE
  - Transport of wastewater and residue to waste management facilities
  - Treatment of wastewater via on-site systems
- Include:
  - Dates and quantities of all transactions
  - Names and contact details of transporters and waste management facilities
