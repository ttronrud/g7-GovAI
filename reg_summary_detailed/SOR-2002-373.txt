**Detailed Summary of the Members of Parliament Disability Allowance Regulations**

---

### **Regulatory Background and Legal Authority**

The *Members of Parliament Disability Allowance Regulations* were issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Minister of State and Leader of the Government in the House of Commons**, under **subsection 71.1(4)a of the Parliament of Canada Act** (S.C. 2001, c. 20, s. 11). This regulatory instrument establishes the procedural framework for determining eligibility and administering the **disability allowance** for members of Parliament (MPs) who are unable to perform their duties due to disability.

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Clerk**  
   - Refers to the **Clerk of the Senate** in the case of a Senator.  
   - Refers to the **Clerk of the House of Commons** in the case of a Member of Parliament.  
   *Role*: Acts as the administrative gateway for disability allowance applications and oversees the initial processing of claims.

2. **Disability Allowance**  
   - The financial allowance provided under **Section 71.1 of the Parliament of Canada Act** to members of Parliament who are unable to carry out their duties due to a recognized disability.

3. **Member**  
   - Includes any current or former member of the Senate or House of Commons.  
   - Specifically includes former members who resigned from Parliament due to disability.

4. **Minister**  
   - Refers to the member of the Queen’s Privy Council designated as the **Minister for the purposes of the Parliament of Canada Act**.  
   - This individual is responsible for reviewing the Clerk’s recommendation and forwarding it to the Governor in Council for final determination.

---

### **Application Process for Eligibility Determination**

To qualify for a disability allowance, a member must submit a formal **written application** to the Clerk. The application must include:

- **Medical Examination Report**:  
  - A report from a **physician authorized to practice medicine** in the jurisdiction where the examination was conducted.  
  - The report must be **no more than six months old** (i.e., performed within the six months immediately preceding the date of application).  
  - The report must **certify that the member is incapable of performing their duties as a member due to a disability**.

- **Proof of Age**:  
  - Written documentation confirming the member’s age.

> ⚠️ **Incomplete or Inaccurate Applications**:  
> If the application or medical report is incomplete or imprecise, the Clerk may issue a **written request** requiring the member to:  
> - Supply missing information, or  
> - Undergo one or more **supplementary medical examinations**.  

> ❌ **Failure to Comply**:  
> If the member fails to respond to the Clerk’s request within the specified time (or within any extended time granted under subsection 9(2)), they are deemed to have **abandoned their application**.

---

### **Eligibility Determination Process**

1. **Clerk’s Recommendation**:  
   - The Clerk evaluates the application and any supporting documents and issues a **recommendation** to the Minister on whether the member is eligible for the disability allowance.

2. **Minister’s Request**:  
   - Based on the Clerk’s recommendation, the Minister formally requests that the **Governor in Council** make a final determination of eligibility.

3. **Final Determination by Governor in Council**:  
   - The Governor in Council makes the **final decision** on eligibility.  
   - This determination is **final and binding** for the specific application.  
   - A **written copy** of the determination is provided to the member.

---

### **Reapplication Rights**

- A member who **abandons** their application, or who was previously **determined ineligible**, or who **revokes** their election to receive the allowance, is **not barred** from submitting a **new application** in the future.  
- This ensures that individuals can reapply if their circumstances change or if new medical evidence becomes available.

---

### **Election to Receive the Allowance**

Once eligibility is confirmed, a member may **elect to receive** the disability allowance within **90 days** of the Governor in Council’s determination.

#### Requirements for Election:
- The member must send a **notice to the Clerk** indicating their election.  
- The notice must be:  
  - **Signed by the member**  
  - **Witnessed**  
  - **Dated**  
- The notice must also include **details of the member’s resignation** from Parliament (if applicable), as this is a key administrative requirement.

> ⚠️ **Election Before Eligibility Determination**:  
> If a member wishes to elect the allowance **before** the Governor in Council makes a determination, they must:  
> - Submit a notice to the Clerk, and  
> - Include the documents required under **paragraphs 2(2)(a) and (b)** (i.e., the medical report and proof of age).

---

### **Extensions for Unforeseen Circumstances**

A member who is unable to comply with a Clerk’s request (e.g., for medical examination or election) due to **reasons beyond their control** (e.g., illness, disability, or other emergencies) may request a **time extension**.

- The request must be made **in a timely manner** and include **evidence supporting the reason**.  
- The Clerk may grant the extension **only if** the request and supporting evidence demonstrate that the delay is justified and necessary.

---

### **Payment of the Allowance**

- The disability allowance is paid in accordance with the **standard payment procedures** of either the **Senate or the House of Commons**, depending on the member’s chamber.  
- This ensures consistency and administrative efficiency in disbursement.

---

### **Revocation of Election**

- A member who has elected to receive the disability allowance may **revoke that election at any time**.  
- Revocation must be made via **written notice** sent to the Clerk.

---

### **Representation by Authorized Person**

- If a member is unable to act personally (e.g., due to disability), a **person authorized in writing** may act on their behalf.  
- The authorization must be **provided to the Clerk** to ensure transparency and accountability.

---

### **Effective Date**

- These regulations come into force on the **day of registration** with the Statutes of Canada.

---

### **Key Principles and Implications**

| Principle | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| **Medical Certification Requirement** | Ensures that disability claims are based on objective, verifiable medical evidence. |
| **Time Limits & Accountability** | Prevents indefinite delays and ensures timely processing. |
| **Finality of Determination** | The Governor in Council’s decision is binding, providing legal certainty. |
| **Flexibility for Future Applications** | Allows members to reapply after abandonment or ineligibility, promoting fairness. |
| **Procedural Safeguards** | Includes witness signatures, documentation, and extension provisions to protect vulnerable individuals. |
| **Administrative Clarity** | Clearly defines roles of Clerk, Minister, and Governor in Council. |

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Members of Parliament Disability Allowance Regulations* provide a **clear, structured, and equitable process** for determining disability allowance eligibility. The regulations balance **administrative efficiency** with **human rights considerations**, ensuring that members who are genuinely unable to perform their duties due to disability receive appropriate financial support. The process includes safeguards for medical validity, procedural fairness, and individual rights—such as the ability to reapply or revoke an election—while maintaining accountability throughout the system.

This regulatory framework reflects a commitment to both **parliamentary dignity** and **the well-being of members** who face significant personal challenges.
