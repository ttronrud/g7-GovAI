## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Softwood Lumber Products Export Permit Fees Regulations**

### üîπ **Background and Purpose**
The regulations are a direct implementation of the **Softwood Lumber Agreement (SLA)**, signed on **May 29, 1996**, between the **Government of Canada** and the **Government of the United States**. The agreement aims to resolve long-standing trade disputes over softwood lumber exports and establishes a framework for managing export volumes and associated fees to ensure fair competition and market stability.

This regulation establishes **export permit fee structures** for Canadian exporters of softwood lumber products destined for the United States. The fees are designed to:
- Recoup administrative costs of permit issuance.
- Encourage compliance with export quotas.
- Prevent over-exportation beyond agreed limits.
- Provide flexibility through tiered pricing and remission mechanisms.

## üö´ Exemptions from Fees

An exporter is **exempt from the volume-based fee** if:
1. **Production in the prior calendar year** was **less than 10 million board feet**.
2. **Production dropped by at least 25%** in the prior calendar quarter due to:
   - Worker strike
   - Mill fire
   - Forest fire
   - Any other **force majeure event**
   - The exporter must **notify the Government of Canada** within 60 days of the event.
   - Canada must provide documentation to the U.S. government within 60 days.

> Exemptions are intended to protect producers facing unforeseen disruptions.

## üìâ Fee Reductions Based on Market Prices

**Fees are waived for an "additional quantity" (92 million bf)** if, during any calendar quarter, the **average price** in *Random Lengths* meets or exceeds:
- **$405 per 1,000 bf** (April 1, 1996 ‚Äì March 31, 1998)
- **$410 per 1,000 bf** (after March 31, 1998)

> This incentivizes exporters to export when prices are high (i.e., during strong market conditions), reducing the burden during price downturns.

> ‚ùå **Second Additional Quantity (72.5 million bf)**: **No fee waiver** applies ‚Äî fees are always charged.

## üíµ Fee for Exporters Without Export Level

An exporter **without an export level** (i.e., not participating in the quota system) pays:
- **$100 per 1,000 board feet**.

> This is a higher rate and reflects the lack of quota protection.
