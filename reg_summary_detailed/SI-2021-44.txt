**Regulation Summary:**

**Title:** Designation of the President of the Pacific Economic Development Agency of Canada as Deputy Head

**Date of Issuance:** August 6, 2021  
**Legal Authority:**  
- Paragraph 11(2)(a)(a) of the *Financial Administration Act* (S.C. 2003, c. 22, s. 8; R.S., c. F-11)  
- This provision empowers the Governor General in Council (acting on the advice of the Prime Minister) to designate individuals to serve in leadership roles within federal agencies, including the appointment of deputy heads.

1. **Designation of Individual:**  
   The President of the Pacific Economic Development Agency of Canada (PEDA) is officially designated as the **Deputy Head** of the Agency.

2. **Effective Date:**

3. **Scope of Role:**

   - Implementing policy and strategic objectives
   - Representing the Agency in intergovernmental and public forums
   - Ensuring compliance with federal regulations and financial administration standards

4. **Legal Basis:**  
   The designation is authorized under **paragraph 11(2)(a)(a)** of the *Financial Administration Act*, which allows for the appointment of deputy heads in federal agencies. This provision supports the efficient and accountable management of public institutions by enabling clear leadership structures.

5. **Agency Background:**  
   The Pacific Economic Development Agency of Canada (PEDA) is a federal agency established to promote economic growth and development in the Pacific region of Canada (including British Columbia, Yukon, and the Northwest Territories). Its mandate includes supporting local economic initiatives, fostering innovation, and enhancing regional prosperity through targeted investments and partnerships.

6. **Governance and Accountability:**
