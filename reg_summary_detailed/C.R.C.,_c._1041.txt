### üìå 2. **Authorized Possession of Narcotics**

A person may legally possess a narcotic under the following conditions:

#### ‚úÖ General Authorization (for business/profession)

- **Licensed dealer**, **pharmacist**, or **practitioner registered and entitled to practise** in the province where the narcotic is possessed.
- **Practitioner in another province** may possess for **emergency medical purposes only**.
- **Hospital employee or practitioner** in a hospital may possess narcotics.
- **Personal use** is allowed **only if**:
  - Obtained from a **practitioner** under a valid prescription (not issued in violation of regulations), or
  - Obtained from a **pharmacist** under section 36 of the CDSA (e.g., for personal use).
- **Practitioner of medicine** who received a narcotic under subsection 68(1) or (2) may possess it **only to provide or deliver** it to a person specified in subsection 68(3).
- **Agent or mandatary** of such a practitioner may possess it **to provide or deliver** it to the same person.
- **Government employees** (inspectors, RCMP, police constables, peace officers, technical/scientific staff of federal/provincial governments or universities) may possess narcotics **in connection with their employment**.
- **Exempted individuals** may possess narcotics **only if** they are exempted under section 56 of the CDSA and the possession is for a purpose specified in the exemption.
- The **Minister of Health** may possess narcotics as part of official duties.

> üîç **Key Principle**: Possession is **not automatic**. It must be tied to a legitimate purpose ‚Äî professional, medical, emergency, or governmental ‚Äî and supported by legal justification.

#### ‚úÖ Agent or Mandatary Exception

- An agent or mandatary may possess a narcotic **if they have reasonable grounds to believe** the principal is a person authorized to possess it under any of the above paragraphs (e.g., a licensed dealer or practitioner), and their possession is **to assist in administration or enforcement** of laws.

> üìå This allows for chain-of-command support in law enforcement or regulatory operations.
