**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Regulation Title:**  
*Fees for the Addition of an Observation to or for the Replacement of Travel Documents, Permanent Resident Cards and Citizenship Certificates – Remission Order*

**Legal Authority:**  
The Order is made under **subsection 23(2.1)a of the Financial Administration Act**, on the recommendation of the **Treasury Board**, the **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration**, and the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**. This provision allows the government to regulate fees related to administrative services when it is in the public interest.

**Purpose of the Regulation:**  
The primary purpose of this remission order is to **provide fee remission (exemption)** for certain individuals who applied for or updated specific government-issued documents between specified dates and whose documents included an **"X" as a sex or gender identifier**. This reflects a commitment to **gender inclusivity**, **non-discrimination**, and **respect for individual identity**, particularly in the context of gender diversity and the recognition of non-binary or gender-diverse identities in official documentation.

---

### Key Definitions:

- **Refugee Travel Document**:  
  A travel document issued under the **United Nations Convention Relating to the Status of Refugees (1951)** and its **Protocol (1967)**. This includes documents such as identity cards or travel permits issued to refugees.

---

## Fee Remission Provisions (by Document Type)

### 1. **Remission for Issuance of a Certificate of Identity or Refugee Travel Document**
- **Applies to**: Replacement or issuance of a certificate of identity or refugee travel document.
- **Conditions for Remission**:
  - The application was made **in Canada** between **June 4, 2019, and June 4, 2020**.
  - On the day the application was submitted, the person held a **valid certificate of identity or refugee travel document** that was **valid for at least six months**.
  - The newly issued document must have the **same validity period** as the one being replaced.

> **Rationale**: Ensures that individuals who have been recognized as having a gender identity marked with "X" are not financially burdened when updating their official identity documents, especially during a transition period.

---

### 2. **Remission for Addition of an Observation in a Passport or Other Travel Document**
- **Applies to**: Adding an observation (e.g., a gender identifier) to a passport or other travel document.
- **Conditions for Remission**:
  - The application was made **in Canada or in a foreign mission** between **August 31, 2017, and June 4, 2019**.
  - On the day of application, the passport or travel document was **valid for at least six months**.

> **Note**: This provision covers a **broader timeframe** (starting earlier than the others) and applies to **passports and other travel documents**, not just refugee documents. It reflects a recognition of the need to update gender markers in travel documents over time.

> **Rationale**: Supports the inclusion of gender diversity in travel documents, especially during a period when gender identity recognition was being formalized in official processes.

---

### 3. **Remission for Issuance of a Travel Document (e.g., for non-citizens)**
- **Applies to**: Issuance of a travel document (e.g., a visa or travel permit) with a gender identifier "X".
- **Conditions for Remission**:
  - Application made in Canada or abroad between **June 4, 2019, and June 4, 2020**.
  - On the date of application, the person held a **valid travel document** valid for **at least six months**.
  - The new document issued must have the **same validity period** as the one being replaced.

> **Rationale**: Ensures that individuals with gender identities marked as "X" are not charged fees when replacing or updating their travel documents, promoting equitable access to travel and international mobility.

---

### 4. **Remission for Processing a Permanent Resident Card**
- **Applies to**: Application for a permanent resident card (PRC) with a gender identifier "X".
- **Conditions for Remission**:
  - Application made in Canada between **June 4, 2019, and June 4, 2020**.
  - On the day of application, the person held a **valid permanent resident card** that was valid for **at least six months**.

> **Rationale**: Recognizes that permanent residents who identify as non-binary or gender-diverse should not face financial barriers when updating their residency documentation, which is essential for legal and social inclusion.

---

### 5. **Remission for Application for a Certificate of Citizenship**
- **Applies to**: Application for a certificate of citizenship with a gender identifier "X".
- **Conditions for Remission**:
  - Application made between **June 4, 2019, and June 4, 2020**.
  - On the day of application, the person held a **valid certificate of citizenship**.

> **Note**: Unlike other provisions, this one does **not require the document to be valid for six months**—only that it was valid at the time of application. This may reflect the nature of citizenship certificates, which are often not tied to a fixed validity period like travel documents.

> **Rationale**: Supports the inclusion of gender identity in citizenship documentation, reinforcing the recognition of gender diversity in Canada’s foundational identity documents.

---

## General Principles Underlying the Regulation:

1. **Gender Inclusivity**:  
   The use of the "X" as a gender identifier is a key feature of this regulation. It acknowledges that gender is not binary and that individuals may identify outside of traditional male/female categories.

2. **Public Interest Justification**:  
   The government determines that remission of fees is in the **public interest**, particularly in promoting **equality, dignity, and respect** for all individuals, regardless of gender identity.

3. **Time-Limited Eligibility**:  
   Each remission is tied to a specific time window (e.g., 2017–2019 or 2019–2020), indicating that this is a **targeted policy** aimed at supporting individuals during a period of transition in gender identity recognition in Canadian government services.

4. **Validity Requirement**:  
   Most provisions require that the **original document be valid for at least six months** at the time of application. This ensures that individuals are not applying to replace expired or invalid documents, which could be considered a misuse of service.

5. **No Fee for Replacements**:  
   The remission applies to the **fees associated with the issuance or replacement** of the documents, meaning individuals are **not charged** for these administrative actions under the specified conditions.

---

## Effective Date:

- The Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** with the Statutes of Canada. This is standard practice for regulatory instruments and ensures immediate effect upon publication.

## Implications and Significance:

- **Inclusion and Equity**: This regulation is a significant step toward recognizing gender diversity in official government records. It aligns with broader Canadian efforts to promote inclusion, such as the **Gender Identity and Expression (GIE) policy** in immigration and citizenship services.
  
- **Legal Precedent**: It sets a precedent for future fee remissions related to gender identity, potentially expanding to other services or document types.

- **Support for Marginalized Communities**: Individuals who identify as non-binary or gender-diverse often face barriers in accessing government services due to outdated forms or fees. This regulation removes a financial barrier, improving access and dignity.

- **Administrative Clarity**: The regulation clearly defines the conditions under which remission applies, making it easier for applicants and service providers to understand eligibility.

---

## Conclusion:

This remission order is a **forward-thinking, inclusive policy** that addresses the financial and administrative challenges faced by individuals who identify with gender identities beyond the traditional binary. By waiving fees for specific document replacements or additions involving the "X" gender identifier during defined periods, the Canadian government demonstrates a commitment to **human rights, equity, and gender inclusivity** in public services. It reflects both a practical and moral obligation to ensure that all citizens—regardless of gender identity—can access and maintain official documentation without financial hardship.

> **Bottom Line**: This regulation ensures that individuals who identify with a gender identity marked as "X" are not charged fees when updating or replacing key identity documents (passports, permanent resident cards, citizenship certificates, refugee travel documents) during specified timeframes, provided they meet basic validity conditions. It is a tangible step toward inclusive governance in Canada.
