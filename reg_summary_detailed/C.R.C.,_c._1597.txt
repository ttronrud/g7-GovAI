**Detailed Summary of the *Visiting Forces Attachment and Serving Together Regulations***

---

### **Regulation Title**  
*Visiting Forces Attachment and Serving Together Regulations*  
These Regulations may be cited as the **Visiting Forces Attachment and Serving Together Regulations**.

---

### **Applicable Legal Framework**  
- The Regulations are made under the authority of the **Visiting Forces Act (the "Act")**, specifically **Part VI** of that Act.  
- The term "**Act**" in these Regulations refers to the *Visiting Forces Act*, and all references to "Part VI" pertain to the provisions governing the temporary attachment of members of foreign armed forces to the Canadian Forces and vice versa.

---

### **Core Purpose of the Regulations**  
To establish the legal framework for the **temporary attachment of members of the Canadian Forces to foreign armed forces** and the **reciprocal attachment of foreign military personnel to the Canadian Forces**, ensuring that such attachments are governed by clear rules of service, duty, and rightsâ€”while respecting the sovereignty and operational needs of both parties.

Additionally, the Regulations define when two armed forces are considered to be **"serving together"**, which has implications for jurisdiction, command, and legal treatment under Canadian law.

### **1. Canadian Forces Members Attached to Foreign Armed Forces**

Every member of the **Canadian Forces** (CF) shall, **unless otherwise directed by the Chief of the Defence Staff (CDS)**, be placed at the disposal of the **service authorities** of any country where **Part VI of the Visiting Forces Act has been declared applicable**.

This means that Canadian military personnel may be temporarily attached to a foreign armed force under the following conditions:

- On the **staff of or attending** a school or training unit of that foreign force.
- On the **staff of or as a patient in** a hospital or convalescent establishment operated by that foreign force.
- **Appointed to or detailed for duty** in:
  - A ship, vessel, formation, unit, detachment, or establishment.
  - Or in **transit** or **awaiting passage** in any ship, vessel, or other conveyance.
- All of the above must be part of or under the **control of an armed force of that country**, whether serving **within or outside** that country.

### **2. Foreign Military Personnel Attached to the Canadian Forces**

Similarly, members of an armed force from a country where **Part VI of the Act is applicable** may be placed at the disposal of the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal government) by the **service authorities of that country** for temporary attachment to the **Canadian Forces**.

Under these circumstances:

- The foreign military member shall be considered **temporarily attached to the Canadian Forces** for the duration of their assignment.
- The attachment applies whether they are serving **within or outside Canada**.
- The conditions for attachment are the same as for Canadian personnel:
  - On the staff of or attending a school/training unit.
  - On the staff of or as a patient in a hospital or convalescent establishment.
  - Appointed to or detailed for duty in:
    - A formation, station, unit, or detachment.
    - Or in transit or awaiting passage in any ship, vessel, or conveyance.
  - All must be under the **control of the Canadian Forces**.

- The **statutes relating to the Canadian Forces** (e.g., the *Canadian Forces Act*, *Service Regulations*, etc.) shall generally **apply to foreign personnel temporarily attached to the Canadian Forces**.

## **Key Principles and Objectives**

1. **Reciprocity**  
   The Regulations ensure that both Canadian and foreign military personnel can serve temporarily with each other under the same rules, promoting cooperation and mutual understanding.

2. **Operational Flexibility**  
   Enables temporary deployment of personnel to support joint training, peacekeeping, or crisis response efforts without requiring permanent status or long-term integration.

3. **Legal Clarity**  
   Defines when personnel are considered attached or serving together, which is essential for determining jurisdiction, command, and legal treatment.

4. **Practical Limitation**  
   Excludes foreign personnel from Canadian retirement and veteran benefitsâ€”ensuring financial and administrative boundaries are respected.

5. **Sovereignty and Control**  
   The Governor in Council and the Chief of the Defence Staff retain authority to override or direct attachments, preserving national control over military operations.

The *Visiting Forces Attachment and Serving Together Regulations* provide a **structured, legally sound framework** for the temporary deployment of military personnel between Canada and other nations where Part VI of the *Visiting Forces Act* applies.
