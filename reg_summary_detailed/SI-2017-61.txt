This document is a **Proclamation of Appointment** issued by **Her Excellency the Right Honourable Julie Payette**, who served as **Governor General and Commander-in-Chief in and over Canada** from 2017 to 2023. - The proclamation is issued under the authority of **Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II**, who formally appointed Julie Payette as Governor General through a **Commission under the Great Seal of Canada**, dated **September 20, 2017**.
- This appointment is based on the **Letters Patent** dated **September 8, 1947**, which establish the powers, duties, and constitutional role of the Governor General of Canada. These Letters Patent are foundational legal instruments that define the office.
- The proclamation affirms that Payette has been authorized to exercise all powers and duties inherent in the office, including those added, amended, or substituted by later Letters Patent.

### **3. Purpose and Content of the Proclamation**
The proclamation serves several key constitutional and administrative functions:

#### a. **Notification of Appointment**
- It publicly announces that Julie Payette has been appointed as Governor General and Commander-in-Chief in and over Canada.
- It confirms that she has **entered upon the duties** of the office, effective from the date of issuance (October 2, 2017).

#### b. **Continuity of Government Functions**
- It commands that **all Her Majesty’s Officers and Ministers** in Canada **continue in the performance of their respective duties**.
- This ensures governmental continuity and stability during the transition of office, especially in a constitutional monarchy where the Governor General acts as a ceremonial and constitutional head of state.

#### c. **Public Notice and Guidance**
- It instructs **all subjects and citizens** of Canada to take notice of the appointment and to govern themselves accordingly.
- This serves as a legal and formal notice to the public, reinforcing the legitimacy of the office and the constitutional framework.

### **4. Legal and Constitutional Significance**
- The proclamation is a **public instrument of state**, issued under the authority of the Crown, and is binding on all persons and institutions within Canada.
- It reflects the **constitutional monarchy** structure of Canada, where the Governor General performs ceremonial and constitutional duties on behalf of the monarch, acting as the **representative of the Crown**.
- The Governor General's role includes:
  - Giving Royal Assent to legislation.
  - Appointing federal and provincial officials.
  - Summoning and dissolving Parliament.
  - Exercising reserve powers (e.g., in times of crisis or political deadlock).
- While the Governor General does not exercise political power, this proclamation ensures that the office is properly established and recognized.
