**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Title:**  
Order Approving the Proposed Regulations Repealing the Laurentian Pilotage Authority District No. 3 Regulations

**Date of Publication in the Canada Gazette, Part I:**  
December 10, 2011

**Date of Approval and Entry into Force:**  
October 7, 2014

---

### **Background and Context**

This regulatory order is a formal approval by the **Minister of Transport** of a proposed set of regulations that sought to **repeal** (i.e., officially cancel or replace) the *Laurentian Pilotage Authority District No. 3 Regulations*.

The process followed under the **Pilotage Act** (S.C. 1998, c. 10, s. 150; R.S., c. P-14) includes a mandatory public and administrative review process to ensure transparency, stakeholder input, and legal compliance before any regulation is enacted or repealed.

---

### **Key Provisions of the Regulatory Process**

1. **Publication of Proposed Regulations**  
   On December 10, 2011, the **Laurentian Pilotage Authority (LPA)** published the proposed *Regulations Repealing the Laurentian Pilotage Authority District No. 3 Regulations* in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*. This publication served as a formal notice to the public and interested parties that a change to the regulatory framework was being considered.

2. **Notice of Objection**  
   Under **subsection 21(1)** of the Pilotage Act, any person or organization with a direct interest in the matter could file an objection to the proposed regulations. Notices of objection were submitted to the **Minister of Transport**, ensuring that the regulatory change was subject to scrutiny from potentially affected stakeholders.

3. **Investigation and Consultation**  
   As required by **subsection 21(2)** of the Pilotage Act, the Minister appointed a designated official (an investigator) to conduct a formal investigation into the proposed regulations. This investigation included:
   - Reviewing the rationale and technical details of the repeal.
   - Engaging in consultations with relevant parties, including:
     - Shipping companies
     - Maritime operators
     - Local port authorities
     - Pilotage professionals
     - Regulatory bodies
   - Assessing whether the repeal would have a reasonable impact on safety, efficiency, or compliance in the maritime sector.

4. **Investigation Report**  
   The appointed investigator completed the review and submitted a formal report to the Minister of Transport under **subsection 21(4)** of the Pilotage Act. This report provided findings, recommendations, and conclusions regarding the merits of the proposed repeal.

5. **Final Approval by the Minister**  
   Based on the investigation and report, the **Minister of Transport**, acting under **subsection 21(4)** of the Pilotage Act, issued this Order Approving the Proposed Regulations. This is the final step in the legal process, authorizing the repeal of the outdated or no longer necessary District No. 3 regulations.

---

### **Outcome of the Order**

- The **proposed Regulations Repealing the Laurentian Pilotage Authority District No. 3 Regulations** are officially **approved** and will take effect on the day the Order is made — **October 7, 2014**.
- As a result, the **previous regulations governing District No. 3 of the Laurentian Pilotage Authority are formally repealed**.
- This action reflects a regulatory modernization effort, likely to:
  - Remove outdated or redundant rules.
  - Align the pilotage framework with current maritime practices.
  - Simplify the regulatory environment for shipping and pilotage operations in the region.

---

### **Legal and Administrative Significance**

- This Order is a key example of how the **Pilotage Act** ensures that regulatory changes are not made unilaterally but are subject to:
  - Public notice
  - Stakeholder input
  - Formal review
  - Ministerial approval
- It demonstrates the Canadian government’s commitment to **transparency, accountability, and stakeholder engagement** in maritime regulation.

---

### **Conclusion**

The Order Approving the Proposed Regulations Repealing the Laurentian Pilotage Authority District No. 3 Regulations is a legally binding instrument that formally ends the regulatory regime for District No. 3. It was developed through a structured, transparent process involving public notice, objection periods, stakeholder consultation, and formal investigation. Its approval on October 7, 2014, signifies the government’s decision to streamline and modernize pilotage regulations in the Laurentian region, ensuring alignment with current operational needs and legal standards.

This repeal likely reflects a broader effort to consolidate or restructure pilotage districts under a more efficient, unified system.
