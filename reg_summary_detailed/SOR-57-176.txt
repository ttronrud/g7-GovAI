**Detailed Summary of the Proclamation (April 11, 1957)**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Context**

This document is a **Proclamation issued by the Governor General of Canada**, acting on the advice of the **Privy Council for Canada**, under the authority granted by **Section 22 of the Penitentiary Act (Chapter 206 of the Revised Statutes of Canada, 1952)**.

- **Section 22 of the Penitentiary Act** empowers the Governor in Council to, by proclamation, designate specific tracts of land within Canada as "penitentiaries" for the purposes of the Act.
- The proclamation serves to legally define and formalize the status of certain land parcels as **government-controlled penal institutions**, subject to the provisions of the Penitentiary Act.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Proclamation**

The primary purpose of this proclamation is to:

- **Formally declare** a specific parcel of land in the Township of Pittsburgh, County of Frontenac, Province of Ontario, as a **penitentiary**.
- Establish the **legal boundaries** and **ownership status** of the land in question.
- Assign it the official name: **Joyceville Institution**.
- Notify the public and relevant authorities that the land is now under the jurisdiction of the provincial government for use as a correctional facility.

---

### **3. Description of the Land Designated**

The land declared as a penitentiary consists of six distinct parcels totaling approximately **613.21 acres**, with specific descriptions provided in detail:

#### **Parcel 1: Lot 15 (Fifth Concession)**
- **Location**: Northeast corner of Lot 15 in the Fifth Concession.
- **Boundary Description**:
  - Begins at the northeast angle of Lot 15.
  - Runs south 4°21′ east for 6,756.1 feet to the south limit of Lot 15.
  - Then south 79°45′ west for 1,279.3 feet to the southwest angle of the lot.
  - Then north 4°21′ west for 6,814.9 feet to the northwest angle of the lot.
  - Finally north 82°23′ east for 1,274.6 feet back to the point of commencement.
- **Area**: 194.21 acres.
- **Note**: This portion is fully included in the designation.

#### **Parcel 2: Lot 16 (Fifth Concession)**
- **Full lot** in the Fifth Concession.
- **Area**: 200 acres.

#### **Parcel 3: West Half of Lot 17 (Fifth Concession)**
- Only the **western half** of Lot 17.
- **Area**: 100 acres.

#### **Parcel 4: Southeast Quarter of Lot 15 (Sixth Concession)**
- The **southeast quarter** of Lot 15 in the Sixth Concession.
- **Area**: 50 acres.

#### **Parcel 5: West Half of Lot 15 (Sixth Concession), South of Rideau Canal**
- Only the **west half** of Lot 15 in the Sixth Concession.
- **Location**: South of the Rideau Canal.
- **Historical Note**: Formerly owned by Andrew Guild.
- **Area**: Approximately 19 acres.

#### **Parcel 6: West Fifty Acres of Lot 16 (Sixth Concession), South of Rideau Canal**
- A portion of Lot 16 in the Sixth Concession, specifically the **west 50 acres**.
- Located **south of the Rideau Canal**.

> **Total Area**: Approximately **613.21 acres** (calculated from individual parcel areas).

---

### **4. Exclusion Clause**

A specific **exclusion** is noted:
- A **strip of land 86 feet wide**, known as **No. 15 Highway**, which runs from the **west limit of Lot 15** to the **east limit of the west half of Lot 17**, is **excluded** from the penitentiary designation.
- This highway remains **outside the boundaries** of the Joyceville Institution and is not subject to the same restrictions or use.

---

### **5. Legal and Administrative Significance**

- The proclamation is **binding and enforceable** under Canadian law.
- The land is now legally recognized as a **penitentiary** for the Province of Ontario.
- It is officially named the **Joyceville Institution**, indicating its function as a correctional facility.
- The **original survey plan** showing the boundaries is filed in the **Office of the Chief Engineer** under reference number **J-S-1**.
- The red outline of the designated land appears on the compiled survey plan, providing a visual and official record.

---

### **6. Legal Process and Governance**

- The proclamation was issued **with the advice of the Privy Council**, which is the highest decision-making body in Canada for matters of policy and legislation.
- The **Governor General**, Vincent Massey (then Commander-in-Chief of Canada), affixed the **Great Seal of Canada** to authenticate the proclamation.
- The proclamation is issued in the name of **Queen Elizabeth II**, as Head of the Commonwealth, reflecting the constitutional framework of Canada’s monarchy.

---

### **7. Public Notice and Compliance**

- The proclamation is **published in the Canada Gazette**, the official government publication, to ensure public awareness.
- All individuals, entities, and authorities affected by the designation are **required to take notice** and to **govern themselves accordingly**.
- This includes:
  - Landowners (who may lose rights to use or sell the land).
  - Contractors or developers (who must not encroach).
  - Government agencies (who must comply with the use of land for correctional purposes).

### **8. Historical and Geographic Context**

- The Township of Pittsburgh lies in **Frontenac County, Ontario**, a region historically associated with early settlement and agricultural development.
- The designation of this land as a penitentiary reflects a **shift in land use** from private or agricultural to **public institutional use**.
- The inclusion of land south of the **Rideau Canal** suggests the area's proximity to transportation infrastructure, which may have influenced the location of the institution.

### **9. Significance of the Name: "Joyceville Institution"**

- The name "Joyceville" appears to be a **newly assigned name** for the institution, likely chosen to reflect a sense of place or to honor a person (possibly a local figure or benefactor).
- The name is not referenced in earlier records, indicating it was established during this period as a formal correctional facility.

### **Conclusion**

This proclamation marks a formal and legally binding step in the establishment of the **Joyceville Institution** as a correctional facility in Ontario. It defines the precise boundaries of the land, excludes a critical access route (the highway), and ensures that the land is now under the control and use of the provincial government for penitentiary purposes. The document reflects the constitutional and legislative mechanisms by which Canada designates land for public institutions, particularly in the realm of justice and corrections.

It is a foundational document in the administrative history of the institution and serves as a reference point for future land use, legal disputes, or institutional developments related to the Joyceville Institution.
