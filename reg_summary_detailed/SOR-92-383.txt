**Detailed Summary of the Prince Edward Island Rule of Practice Respecting Reduction in the Number of Years of Imprisonment Without Eligibility for Parole**

---

### **Overview**

The regulation, issued on **June 15, 1992**, by the **Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of Prince Edward Island — Trial Division**, establishes a formal procedural framework for applications seeking a **reduction in the number of years of imprisonment without eligibility for parole** under **subsection 745(1) of the Criminal Code of Canada**. This rule is designed to ensure a fair, transparent, and structured process for individuals currently serving sentences without parole eligibility to apply for a reduction in their non-parole period — a mechanism intended to promote justice, rehabilitation, and proportionality in sentencing.

The rule came into force on **September 1, 1992**, and is formally titled:  
**"Prince Edward Island Criminal Rule of Practice Respecting Reduction in the Number of Years of Imprisonment Without Eligibility for Parole."**

---

## **Key Definitions**

The rule defines several key terms to ensure clarity and consistency throughout the process:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Applicant** | A person making the application, including counsel acting on their behalf. |
| **Application** | A formal request submitted under subsection 745(1) of the Criminal Code. |
| **Attorney General** | The provincial Attorney General of Prince Edward Island, including counsel acting for them. |
| **Chief Justice** | The head of the Supreme Court of Prince Edward Island — Trial Division. |
| **Judge** | A judge designated by the Chief Justice to preside over the preliminary hearing and hearing of the application. |
| **Registrar** | The official responsible for receiving, processing, and filing applications and related documents. |

---

## **Application Requirements**

### 1. **Form and Content**
An application must be submitted in **Form 79.03A** and must contain the following elements:

- Full name (given names, surname), date of birth of the applicant  
- A complete list of institutions where the applicant has been detained since arrest, including dates of entry  
- Details of the offence: nature of the offence, sentence imposed, dates of conviction and sentencing, and location of the trial  
- Number of years of imprisonment without eligibility for parole  
- Full criminal record (including prior convictions and sentences)  
- Precise and concise grounds supporting the application  
- Statement of relief sought (i.e., reduction in non-parole period)  
- Applicant’s address for service  

### 2. **Supporting Affidavit**
The application must be accompanied by an **affidavit in Form 79.04A**, sworn by the applicant, confirming the truth of the facts stated in the application.

---

## **Filing and Service Procedures**

- The application and affidavit must be **filed with the registrar** of the Supreme Court of Prince Edward Island.
- The applicant is required to **serve the application** on three parties:
  1. The **Solicitor General of Canada**  
  2. The **Attorney General of Prince Edward Island**  
  3. The **officer in charge of the institution** where the applicant is detained  

### Service Method:
- Service must be made by **registered or certified mail**.
- Service is deemed effective on the **seventh day after mailing**.
- Proof of service may be established by:
  - Filing an affidavit from the person who made the service, or  
  - Any other method acceptable to the Chief Justice.

---

## **Initial Review by the Chief Justice**

Upon receipt of the application, the **Chief Justice** performs the following steps:

1. Determines whether **subsection 745(1)** of the Criminal Code applies to the applicant.
   - If **not applicable**, the application is **dismissed**.
   - If **applicable**, the application is **sent to the judge** for further proceedings.

> ⚖️ *Note: Subsection 745(1) allows for a reduction in the number of years of imprisonment without eligibility for parole, provided the applicant meets certain criteria, such as having served a significant portion of their sentence and demonstrating rehabilitation.*

---

## **Preliminary Hearing (Conducted by the Judge)**

Once the application is forwarded to the judge, the following procedures are followed:

### 1. **Setting the Hearing**
- The judge sets the **date and place** of the preliminary hearing.
- A **written notice** of the hearing is sent to:
  - The Attorney General  
  - The applicant  
  - The Solicitor General of Canada  
  - The officer in charge of the institution  

> All notices are sent by **registered mail** and must be filed with the registrar.

### 2. **Purpose of the Preliminary Hearing**
The hearing serves to:
- Ensure a fair and expeditious process  
- Allow both parties to inform the judge of the evidence they intend to present  
- Allow for procedural orders (e.g., adjournments, evidence rules)

### 3. **Evidence by Affidavit**
- If either party intends to present evidence via affidavit, the judge may:
  - Require the **deponent to attend** for cross-examination  
  - Issue directions on how the affidavit will be used  

### 4. **Adjournment**
- The judge may **adjourn the hearing** as appropriate.

### 5. **Parole Eligibility Report (PER)**
- The judge may **order the preparation of a Parole Eligibility Report** if it is deemed relevant.
- The report is prepared by a person designated by the **Solicitor General of Canada** and must include:
  - Summary of social and family background  
  - Classification and disciplinary records  
  - Conduct reports from institutional staff  
  - Psychological or psychiatric evaluations  
  - Any other information relevant to the applicant’s character and conduct  

> The report is **not limited** to facts directly related to parole eligibility but may include any information relevant to the issue.

### 6. **Filing and Distribution of the Report**
- The report is filed with the registrar.
- A **copy is delivered to the applicant and the Attorney General**.

### 7. **Resumption of Hearing**
- Upon receipt of the report, the judge notifies both parties that the hearing will resume.
- The judge sets the resumption date with **at least 30 days** for both parties to review the report.

### 8. **Disputes Over the Report**
- If either party disputes any part of the report, they may request the **author of the report to appear** for cross-examination.
- The judge decides:
  - Which parts of the report will be presented  
  - Whether additional evidence is needed  

---

## **Final Hearing Before a Jury**

### 1. **Jurisdiction and Jury Empanelment**
- The judge may:
  - Order a **jury to be empanelled** to determine whether the reduction in non-parole period is warranted  
  - Or, if subsection 745(1) does not apply, refer the application back to the Chief Justice  

> Jurors are empanelled in accordance with **Part XX of the Criminal Code**, with modifications as required by the circumstances.

### 2. **Peremptory Challenges**
- The applicant and the Attorney General are entitled to the **same number of peremptory challenges** as they would have in a criminal trial for the offence at issue.

### 3. **Procedure at the Hearing**
- The judge may:
  - Conduct a **voir dire** on the admissibility of the Parole Eligibility Report  
  - Admit evidence that is **credible and trustworthy**, subject to standard rules of evidence (as in sentencing hearings)  
  - Order any necessary investigations  

- The hearing may be **adjourned** at the judge’s discretion.

### 4. **Evidence Rules**
- Only the **applicant and the Attorney General** may present evidence.
- The applicant presents evidence **first**, and may present **rebuttal evidence** after the Attorney General has finished, if permitted by the judge.

### 5. **Judicial Decision After Evidence**
- If the judge determines that **subsection 745(1) does not apply**, the application is **referred back to the Chief Justice**, who may dismiss it.
- If the judge finds that **subsection 745(1) applies**, the applicant and the Attorney General will each address the jury.

### 6. **Judge’s Address to the Jury**
- After both parties address the jury, the judge delivers a **closing address** on:
  - The applicable law  
  - The evidence presented  

### 7. **Jury Decision**
- The jury’s decision must be **based solely on the evidence presented** during the hearing.

---

## **Judicial Powers and Flexibility**

The judge has broad discretionary powers to ensure the process is just and efficient:

- Can **set time limits** for the proceedings  
- Can **require the applicant to be brought before the court**  
- Can issue **any other orders necessary in the interest of justice**

Additionally, the judge may:
- Order proceedings to be held **in camera** (privately) if necessary for public morals, order maintenance, or justice  
- Impose a **ban on publication** of any evidence presented  
- Apply **Section 527 of the Criminal Code** (which governs confidentiality in proceedings) with appropriate modifications

---

## **Transparency and Recordkeeping**

- A **complete transcript** of all proceedings (preliminary hearing, jury hearing, etc.) must be prepared and **filed with the registrar**.

---

## **Final Outcomes**

| Outcome | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Application Dismissed**
