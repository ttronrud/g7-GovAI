**Detailed Summary of the Cannabis Act (Police Enforcement) Regulations (S.C. 2018, c. 16)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Cannabis Act (Police Enforcement) Regulations*, made under **section 139 of the Cannabis Act**, provide a comprehensive legal framework that **exempts certain law enforcement personnel and their associates from criminal liability** when they come into possession of cannabis during a *particular investigation*. These exemptions are designed to **support legitimate law enforcement activities** involving cannabis, including investigations into illicit trafficking, production, or distribution, while ensuring that such activities do not constitute violations of the Cannabis Act.

The regulations also establish **procedures for the handling, transfer, reporting, and disposal of forfeited cannabis**, and include **accountability mechanisms** to ensure transparency and compliance with public safety and oversight requirements.

---

## **Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined and apply throughout the regulations:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Cannabis Act* (SC 2018, c. 16). |
| **Appropriate Officer** | - For the **RCMP**: Assistant Commissioner in charge of drug enforcement.<br>- For other police forces: Most senior officer responsible for operations.<br>- For military police: Canadian Forces Provost Marshal. |
| **Chief** | Senior police officer in charge of a police force (not the RCMP). |
| **Military Police** | Designated military police under subsection 2(2) of the Act. |
| **Particular Investigation** | A primary investigation conducted under the Cannabis Act or another federal Act, including any investigations arising from it. |
| **Police Force** | A police force designated under subsection 2(1) of the Act. |
| **Provincial Minister** | The provincial minister responsible for policing. |
| **RCMP** | Royal Canadian Mounted Police. |

## **Key Provisions and Exemptions**

### **1. Exemptions for Law Enforcement Officers (Sections 3–8)**

#### **Section 3: Officers in Active Service During a Particular Investigation**
- A **member of a police force or military police** is **exempt from sections 8, 9–14 of the Cannabis Act** if:
  - They are in **active service**; and
  - They engage in activities involving cannabis (excluding forfeited cannabis, imported/exported under exemptions, or produced under exemptions) **that they have come into possession during a particular investigation**.
- This exemption applies **only to cannabis acquired during a legitimate investigation**.

#### **Section 4: Personnel Assisting Officers**
- A **person** is exempt from sections 8–14 if:
  - They act **under the direction and control** of a qualified police officer or military police member who meets the conditions in **paragraphs 3(a) and (b)**; and
  - They assist the officer in the course of the investigation.

> **Note**: The exemption applies only to cannabis not forfeited to Her Majesty, not imported/exported under section 17, or produced under section 19.

#### **Section 5: Notification Requirement (Import/Export)**
- Any officer or person exempt under Section 3 or 4 must **notify the Assistant Commissioner of the RCMP (in charge of drug enforcement)** in writing or electronically **before** importing or exporting cannabis.
- If notification is not feasible before the activity, it must be made **as soon as feasible afterward**.

---

### **2. Exemptions for Specific Offences (Conspiracy, Attempt, Accessory, Counselling) – Sections 9–10**

#### **Section 6: Officers Engaging in Activities (e.g., section 9, 10, 11, 12)**
- A police officer or military police member is exempt from the application of **sections 9, 10, 11, or 12** (offences related to attempts, conspiracies, etc.) if:
  - In active service; and
  - Acting in the course of their responsibilities for a particular investigation.

#### **Section 7: Persons Assisting Officers**
- A person is exempt from such offences if:
  - Acts under the direction and control of a qualified officer; and
  - Assists the officer in the course of the investigation.

> This protects individuals who assist law enforcement in lawful investigative actions, even if those actions involve cannabis.

#### **Section 8: Exemption for Offering to Engage in Activities**
- An officer or person who **offers to engage** in an activity prohibited under sections 9–12 is exempt **only for the act of offering**, provided:
  - In active service; and
  - Acting in the course of duties during a particular investigation.

> This prevents criminalization of preliminary or exploratory actions during investigations.

---

### **3. Exemptions from Cannabis and Hemp Regulations (Section 10)**

- Any person or officer exempt from sections 8–14 of the Cannabis Act is **also exempt from the Cannabis Regulations and Industrial Hemp Regulations** in respect of the same activity.
- This ensures that law enforcement personnel are not subject to additional regulatory restrictions when acting within their investigative duties.

---

### **4. Certificates for Handling Forfeited Cannabis or Foreign-Obtained Cannabis (Sections 14, 17, 19)**

#### **Section 14: Exemption for Forfeited Cannabis (Section 12)**
- A police officer or military police member may be exempt from **section 12** (regarding possession or use of cannabis) if:
  - The cannabis was **forfeited to Her Majesty**;
  - It was **imported under section 17** or **produced under section 19**;
  - The officer has a **certificate** issued by the appropriate officer.

> **Certificate Conditions**:
> - Officer must be in active service;
> - Acting in the course of a particular investigation.

> **Duration**: Maximum of **one year**.

#### **Section 17: Exemption for Importing Cannabis from Foreign States**
- A person is exempt from section 11 (importing cannabis) if:
  - Acts under the direction of a qualified officer;
  - Cannabis was **requested and obtained directly from a foreign state**;
  - The activity is for **law enforcement purposes** (e.g., identifying individuals involved in offences).

> **Note**: Cannabis that passes through a foreign state **for investigative purposes** (e.g., identifying suspects) is **not** considered imported under this section.

> **Certificate Issuance**:
> - Issued by the Assistant Commissioner of the RCMP;
> - For officers involved in investigations where the RCMP participates;
> - Maximum duration: **six months**.

#### **Section 19: Exemption for Producing Cannabis (for Investigation)**
- A person is exempt from section 11 if:
  - Produces cannabis under a certificate;
  - The cannabis is for investigative use;
  - Done under the direction of a qualified officer.

> **Certificate Conditions**:
> - Officer must be in active service;
> - Acting in the course of a particular investigation (RCMP participation required).

> **Duration**: Six months.

> **Note**: The exemption applies only to cannabis produced for **law enforcement investigations**, not for commercial or personal use.

---

### **5. Certificate Requirements and Revocation**

- All certificates issued under Sections 14, 17, or 19 must:
  - Identify the officer;
  - Specify the **duration** of exemption;
  - Indicate the **particular investigation** involved.

- A certificate is **revoked on the earliest of**:
  1. Revocation by the appropriate officer;
  2. Officer no longer in active service;
  3. Officer no longer acting in the investigation;
  4. Investigation completed;
  5. Certificate expires.

- The officer must be **notified in writing** of revocation if it occurs under (a), (c), (d), or (e).

---

### **6. Management and Transfer of Forfeited Cannabis (Section 23)**

#### **Section 23: Transfer of Cannabis Between Officers**
- The **chief or appropriate officer** is exempt from section 9 of the Act if they **transfer cannabis** (forfeited to Her Majesty) to another chief or appropriate officer **for the purpose of a particular investigation**.

> **Reporting Requirements**:
> - Officer **making the transfer** must inform the Minister as soon as feasible after receiving the request.
> - Officer **receiving the cannabis** must inform the Minister upon receipt.

#### **Section 24: Disposal of Unneeded Cannabis**
- If cannabis is **no longer required** for investigations:
  - The chief or appropriate officer must **seek directions from the Minister** before disposing of or otherwise dealing with it.
  - Must dispose of it **in accordance with the Minister’s directions**.

> **If no longer needed**:
> - Must **in writing** seek directions from the Minister.
> - Must dispose of cannabis **in accordance with those directions**.

> **Notification Requirement**:
> - Must notify the Minister in writing **within 60 days** of disposal.

---

### **7. Annual Reporting Obligations (Section 25)**

#### **Mandatory Annual Reports**
- The **chief or appropriate officer** must submit a report to:
  - The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness**;
  - The **Minister of National Defence** (for military police);
  - (For police forces other than RCMP) to the **provincial minister**.
