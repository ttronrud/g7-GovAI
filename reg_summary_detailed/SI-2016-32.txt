**Detailed Summary of the Banff National Park of Canada Siksika Nation Castle Mountain Land and Timber Claim Settlement Agreement Rent and Fee Remission Order**

---

### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
This Order is issued under the authority of **subsection 23(2.1)a of the Financial Administration Act** (S.C. 1991, c. 24, s. 7(2); R.S., c. F-11).  
- The Governor General in Council (the federal executive authority) has exercised its power to make this Order based on a recommendation from two key departments:
  - The **Minister of the Environment**
  - The **Treasury Board**

This legislative action reflects a policy decision that it is in the **public interest** to provide financial relief to the Siksika Nation through rent and fee remission for certain land and occupancy rights within Banff National Park.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Order**
The primary purpose of this Order is to **grant rent and fee remissions** to the Siksika Nation and its affiliated entities for specific land and occupancy rights related to the **Castle Mountain and Storm Mountain areas** in Banff National Park.  

This measure is part of a broader **Settlement Agreement** between the Government of Canada and the Siksika Nation, aimed at recognizing and addressing historical land claims, promoting reconciliation, and supporting Indigenous economic development and self-determination.

---

### **3. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Licence of Occupation** | A permit granted under the *National Parks of Canada Lease and Licence of Occupation Regulations* for lands adjacent to either the **Castle Mountain Chalets site** or the **Storm Mountain Lodge site**. |
| **Regulations** | Refers to the *National Parks of Canada Lease and Licence of Occupation Regulations*, which govern leasing and occupancy rights in national parks. |
| **Settlement Agreement** | The formal agreement between Canada and the Siksika Nation regarding land rights, including land claims, timber rights, and economic benefits related to the Castle Mountain and Storm Mountain areas. |
| **Siksika Entity** | A legal entity (such as a corporation or limited partnership) that is fully owned or beneficially held in trust for the members of the Siksika Nation. This ensures that only Indigenous-owned or -controlled organizations benefit from the remission. |
| **Special Lease** | A specific type of lease granted under paragraph 3(1)(e) of the Regulations for the following sites: <br> - Castle Mountain Chalets site <br> - Storm Mountain Lodge site <br> - Castle Mountain Campground site <br> - Castle Mountain Hostel site |

---

### **4. Financial Relief Provided (Remission)**

#### **(a) For Special Leases**
- A **full remission** of rent payments is granted to any **Siksika entity** that holds a **special lease** for one of the four named sites.
- **Exception**: The remission does **not** apply to **$1.00 per year** in rent.
- This means that while all rent obligations are waived, the lessee must still pay a minimal annual fee of **$1.00**.

#### **(b) For Licences of Occupation**
- A **full remission** of the **fee** payable under **section 18 of the Regulations** is granted to any **Siksika entity** holding a **licence of occupation** for land adjacent to either the Castle Mountain Chalets or Storm Mountain Lodge sites.
- Again, the **exception applies**: the lessee must pay **$1.00 per year** as a fee.

> ✅ **Key Point**: The remission applies only to **Siksika entities**, ensuring that the benefit flows directly to Indigenous-owned or -controlled organizations. Non-Siksika entities are not eligible.

---

### **5. Effective Date**

- The Order comes into force on the **day the Settlement Agreement is fully executed**.
- If the Settlement Agreement is executed **before** the Order is made, the Order takes effect on the date of full execution.
- If the Order is made **after** the Settlement Agreement is signed, it comes into force on the **date of issuance** of the Order.

> ⚠️ **Note**: This Order is in force as of **August 15, 2016**, indicating that the Settlement Agreement was likely executed or finalized by that date.

---

### **6. Significance and Implications**

- **Indigenous Reconciliation**: This Order is a tangible step toward recognizing Indigenous land rights and historical injustices by providing financial relief and formalizing economic participation.
- **Economic Support**: It enables the Siksika Nation to operate and maintain facilities (such as chalets, lodges, and campgrounds) without the burden of significant rental or occupancy fees, thereby supporting local economic development and employment.
- **Cultural and Environmental Stewardship**: By allowing the Siksika Nation to manage and operate these sites, the Order supports Indigenous stewardship of natural and cultural resources within Banff National Park.
- **Legal Clarity**: The Order provides clear, enforceable terms for rent and fee obligations, reducing uncertainty for both the Government of Canada and the Siksika Nation.

---

### **7. Limitations and Exclusions**

- The remission **does not cover** the **$1.00 annual fee** for rent or occupancy fees.
- The remission applies **only** to Siksika entities — not to other corporations or individuals.
- The relief is **limited to specific sites** (Castle Mountain, Storm Mountain, Campground, Hostel) and does not extend to other areas or types of land use.

---

### **Conclusion**

This Order is a significant regulatory and policy instrument that:
- Formalizes financial support for the Siksika Nation in accordance with a comprehensive land and timber settlement agreement,
- Promotes Indigenous self-governance and economic sustainability,
- Balances public interest with Indigenous rights and historical justice,
- And ensures compliance with federal financial and land-use regulations.

It stands as a model of collaborative governance between the Government of Canada and Indigenous nations, reflecting a commitment to reconciliation, environmental protection, and equitable access to natural resources.
