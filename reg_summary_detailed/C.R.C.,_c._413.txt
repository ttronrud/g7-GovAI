## ğŸš¢ Permitted Activities Under Licence (Main Provisions)

The Minister may issue a licence authorizing a foreign fishing vessel and its crew to:

### 1. **Commercial Fishing**
- Engage in fishing for commercial purposes.

### 2. **Transhipment & Supplies**
- Take on board fish, outfit, or supplies while at sea.
- Transport fish from fishing grounds.

### 3. **Processing at Sea**
- Process fish at sea (e.g., cleaning, freezing, packaging).

### 4. **Port Activities**
- Purchase or obtain bait, outfits, or supplies at Canadian ports.
- Effect repairs at Canadian ports.
- Purchase, load, unload, transship, sell, or process fish/fish products at Canadian ports.
- Unload, land, re-embark, or tranship equipment (of the vessel or another vessel of the same flag state) at Canadian ports.
- Grant shore leave to crew.
- Discharge or take on board crew members (of the vessel or another vessel of the same flag state).

### 5. **Passing Through Canadian Waters**
- Vessels may **pass through specific channels** during voyages outside Canadian waters, including:
  - Between Vancouver Island and mainland BC (between 49th parallel and a line from Cape Sutil to Cape Caution)
  - Fitz Hugh Sound
  - Finlayson Channel
  - Laredo Channel
  - Princess Royal Channel
  - Principe Channel
  - Grenville Channel

> These are **specific maritime corridors** defined by straight-line boundaries.

### 6. **Scientific Research**
- Vessels may enter Canadian waters for scientific research or any activity listed in subparagraphs (a)(ii) to (xi) or subsection (1.1) related to research.

> This includes research on fish stocks, migration, environmental impacts, etc.

## ğŸš« Conditions Preventing Licence Issuance

The Minister **shall not issue a licence** under paragraph (1)(a) or subsection (1.1) if:

1. The vessel is **not licensed or authorized** by its flag state for fisheries activities.
2. The vessel is **not compliant** with relevant **conservation and management measures** (e.g., NAFO, NPFC, WCPFC).
3. The vessel is providing supplies to a foreign vessel that is **non-compliant** with conservation measures.
4. The proposed activity is **not compatible** with or will **undermine** conservation measures.
5. The activity is **not consistent with sustainable use** of fisheries resources or will lead to **excess harvesting or processing capacity**.

> These are **strict compliance thresholds** designed to prevent overfishing and ensure sustainable management.
