**Detailed Summary of the Canadian Transportation Agency Rules (Dispute Proceedings and Certain Rules Applicable to All Proceedings)**  
*Issued: April 29, 2014*  
*Regulation: S.C. 1996, c. 10, under section 17 of the Canada Transportation Act*

---

### **1. Purpose and Scope of the Rules**

These Rules govern the procedures for **dispute proceedings** before the **Canadian Transportation Agency (CTA)**. They apply to all contested matters (i.e., disputes) initiated by an application to the Agency, **except those that are subject to mediation**.

The Rules are designed to ensure:
- **Proportionality** in proceedings based on the importance and complexity of the issues and the relief sought.
- **Efficiency** and **fairness** in resolving disputes.
- **Optimal use of Agency and party resources**.
- **Promotion of justice** through timely and accessible procedures.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

The Rules establish clear definitions of key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Canada Transportation Act*. |
| **Affidavit** | A written statement confirmed by oath or solemn declaration. |
| **Applicant** | A person who files an application with the Agency. |
| **Application** | A formal document initiating a proceeding before the Agency. |
| **Business Day** | A day when the Agency is open for business (excluding weekends and holidays). |
| **Dispute Proceeding** | Any contested matter initiated by application. |
| **Document** | Any information recorded in any form (including electronic, physical, or digital). |
| **Intervener** | A person whose request to intervene has been granted by the Agency. |
| **Party** | Includes applicants, respondents, or any person named by the Agency. |
| **Person** | Includes partnerships and unincorporated associations. |
| **Proceeding** | Any matter initiated by an application, whether contested or not. |
| **Respondent** | A person named as a respondent in an application or later named by the Agency. |

---

### **3. Quorum and Conduct of Proceedings**

- **One member of the Agency constitutes a quorum** for all meetings and decisions.
- The Agency must conduct proceedings in a **proportionate, fair, and efficient manner**, tailored to the nature and complexity of the dispute.
- The Rules are to be interpreted to **facilitate the most expeditious determination** of disputes, promote justice, and ensure efficient use of resources.

---

### **4. Filing Documents and Procedures**

#### **4.1 Filing Requirements**
- All documents must be filed with the **Secretary of the Agency**.
- Documents are placed on the **public record** unless a **request for confidentiality** is filed under Section 31 at the same time.
- A person must send a **copy of the document** to each party (or their representative) **on the same day** it is filed — **except** in the following cases:
  - The document is a confidential version of a document with a confidentiality request filed.
  - The document is an application.
  - The document is a position statement.

#### **4.2 Methods of Filing**
Documents may be submitted via:
- Courrier (mail)
- Personal delivery
- Email
- Facsimile (fax)
- Other electronic means approved by the Agency

#### **4.3 Filing Timelines and Deadlines**
- **Email, fax, or electronic submissions** are considered filed and received on:
  - The **date of transmission** if sent **on or before 5:00 p.m. Gatineau time on a business day**.
  - The **next business day** if sent after 5:00 p.m. or on a non-business day.
- **Courier or personal delivery** is considered filed and received on:
  - The **date of delivery** if delivered **by 5:00 p.m. on a business day**.
  - The **next business day** otherwise.

#### **4.4 Prohibited Filings**
- A document **cannot be filed after the deadline** unless a **request under subsection 30(1)** is filed and **granted** by the Agency.
- A document **not authorized by these Rules** cannot be filed unless a **request under subsection 34(1)** is filed and granted.

> ⚠️ **Consequence of Violation**: Any document filed in breach of these rules will **not be placed on the Agency’s record**.

#### **4.5 Language Requirements**
- All documents must be in **English or French**.
- If a document is in another language, a **full English or French translation** must be filed **at the same time**.
- The **translation is treated as the original** for all purposes in the proceeding.

#### **4.6 Amending Previously Filed Documents**
- Any **substantive amendment** to a previously filed document requires a **formal request under subsection 33(1)**.
- All amendments must:
  - Be clearly identified.
  - Include the word **"AMENDED"** in **capital letters** in the top right corner of the first page.

#### **4.7 Verification of Document Content**
- The Agency may, by notice, require a person to verify the contents of a document via:
  - **Affidavit** (if the document is subject to Schedule 2)
  - **Witnessed statement** (if subject to Schedule 3)
- The verification must be filed within **five business days** of the notice.
- Failure to file verification results in the **document being struck from the record**.

---

### **5. Representation and Legal Authority**

- A person who is **not a member of the bar** of a province may be represented by another person **only if they file the information in Schedule 4** authorizing that person to act on their behalf.

- Any change in contact information must be **reported to the Agency and all parties immediately**.

---

### **6. Filing of Applications**

- Every application must include the information in **Schedule 5**.
- If the application is **complete**, the parties are notified in writing that it has been accepted.
- If **incomplete**, the applicant is given **20 business days** to provide missing information.
- If the applicant fails to do so, the file is **closed**.
- A closed file may be reopened with a **new application** on the same matter.

---

### **7. Responses to Applications**

#### **7.1 Respondent’s Answer**
- Must be filed within **15 business days** of the acceptance notice.
- Must include information in **Schedule 6**.

#### **7.2 Applicant’s Reply**
- Must be filed within **5 business days** of receiving the answer.
- Must include information in **Schedule 7**.
- **Restriction**: Cannot raise new issues or introduce new evidence unless a **request is filed and granted**.

---

### **8. Interventions and Position Statements**

#### **8.1 Intervention**
- An intervener may file an intervention within **5 business days** of being granted leave to intervene.
- Must include information in **Schedule 8**.
- Participation is **limited to rights granted by the Agency**.

#### **8.2 Response to Intervention**
- An applicant or respondent in adverse interest may file a response within **5 business days** of receiving the intervention.
- Must include information in **Schedule 9**.

#### **8.3 Position Statement**
- May be filed **before the close of pleadings**.
- Must include information in **Schedule 10**.
- **No participation rights**.
- **No right to receive notices** or to be heard during the proceeding.

---

### **9. Requests for Written Questions or Document Production**

A party may request:
- Written questions about facts or issues.
- Production of documents in their possession or control.

#### **9.1 Timing**
- For **written questions**: Before close of pleadings.
- For **document production**: Within **5 business days** of becoming aware of the documents or before close of pleadings — **whichever comes first**.

#### **9.2 Response Requirements**
- The responding party must file a **complete response** within **5 business days** of receiving the notice.
- Response must include information in **Schedule 12**.

#### **9.3 Objections**
- If a party objects to a request, they must:
  - Provide a **clear, concise explanation** of the objection (including relevance and availability).
  - Submit **supporting documents**.
  - Provide any **additional relevant information**.

---

### **10. Expedited Process**

The Agency may apply an **expedited process** at the request of a party under Section 28.

#### **10.1 Expedited Answers and Replies**
- **Answer**: Within **5 business days** (instead of 15).
- **Reply**: Within **3 business days** (instead of 5).

#### **10.2 Expedited Responses to Requests**
- **Response to request**: Within **2 business days**.
- **Reply to response**: Within **1 business day**.

> ⚠️ The request must demonstrate that adherence to normal timelines would cause **financial or other prejudice**.

#### **10.3 When Expedited Process Applies**
- To an answer and reply.
- To a request for documents or questions.

---
