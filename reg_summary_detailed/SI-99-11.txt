## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Agreement on Social Security Between Canada and Grenada**

### üîπ **1. Background and Legal Context**
- The Agreement was signed on **January 8, 1998**, under **Article XXIV of the Agreement on Social Security** between Canada and Grenada.
- The Agreement entered into force on **February 1, 1999**, following the exchange of ratification instruments on **October 29, 1998**.
- The entry into force was governed by a **procedural timeline**:
  - The Order in Council (P.C. 1998-427) was laid before Parliament on **April 2, 1998**.
  - No motion to revoke it was filed before the 20th sitting day.
  - By **subsection 42(2) of the Old Age Security Act**, the Order came into force on the **30th sitting day after being laid**, which was **June 5, 1998**.
  - The Agreement officially entered into force on the **first day of the fourth month after the exchange of ratifications**, i.e., **February 1, 1999**.
- This was formalized through **Order in Council P.C. 1999-58**, which issued a **Proclamation** notifying the public of the Agreement‚Äôs effectiveness.

---

### üîπ **2. Purpose of the Agreement**
The primary objective is to **promote cooperation in the field of social security** between Canada and Grenada by:
- Ensuring **mutual recognition** of benefits and eligibility for individuals who have worked or lived in both jurisdictions.
- Preventing **double taxation or denial of benefits** due to residency or employment status.
- Establishing **rules for creditable periods**, **benefit totalization**, and **cross-border entitlements**.

### üîπ **4. Scope of Application**
- Applies to:
  - All persons who are or have been subject to the legislation of **either Canada or Grenada**.
  - Their **dependants and survivors**, as defined in applicable legislation.
- **Eligibility**: A person subject to one Party‚Äôs legislation is **eligible for benefits under the other Party‚Äôs legislation** under the **same conditions as a citizen of that Party**.

> ‚úÖ Example: A Canadian resident working in Grenada may qualify for a Grenadian invalidity benefit if they meet residency and contribution requirements.

### üîπ **5. Residency and Work Status Rules**

#### A. **Employed Persons**
- An employed person working in the territory of one Party is subject **only** to the legislation of that Party.
- **Exception**: If the same person works for the **same employer** in the territory of the other Party, they are subject **only** to the legislation of the **first Party** (the one where they were originally employed).
- **Assignment Limitation**: This coverage (i.e., being subject to one Party‚Äôs legislation) may not exceed **60 months** without prior consent from both competent authorities.

#### B. **Self-Employed Persons**
- A self-employed person who **ordinarily resides** in one Party and works for their own account in the other Party is subject **only** to the legislation of the Party where they reside.

#### C. **Seafarers / Crew Members**
- A crew member of a ship, vessel, or aircraft who would otherwise be subject to both Parties‚Äô legislation is subject **only** to:
  - Canada if they **ordinarily reside in Canada**.
  - Grenada if they **ordinarily reside in Grenada**.

#### D. **Government Employees**
- A government employee performing duties in the territory of the other Party is subject **only** to the legislation of that Party **if**:
  - They are a citizen of that Party, or
  - They **ordinarily reside** there.
- However, they may **elect** to be subject only to the legislation of the first Party (home Party), even if they reside abroad.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: The competent authorities may **modify these rules** by mutual agreement for specific categories of persons.

### üîπ **6. Creditable Periods and Totalization Rules**

#### A. **Old Age Security Act (OASA) ‚Äì Residency Calculation**
- **If a person contributes to CPP or provincial pension plans while residing in Grenada**:
  - That period is **counted as residence in Canada** for the person and their **spouse/dependants** (who are not subject to Grenadian employment/self-employment).
- **If a person is subject to Grenadian legislation while residing in Canada**:
  - That period is **not counted** as residence in Canada for the person or their dependants (unless they contribute to CPP/provincial plans).
- **Exception**: A person who is **ordinarily resident in Grenada** and employed in Canada under a CPP or provincial plan:
  - Their period of presence and employment in Canada is **counted as residence in Canada** (only for purposes of this Agreement).

#### B. **Creditable Periods in Grenada for CPP Eligibility**
- A **calendar year** under the Canada Pension Plan (CPP) that includes **at least 13 weeks** of creditable periods under Grenadian legislation is treated as a **full year of contributions** for CPP purposes.

#### C. **Creditable Periods for Grenadian Benefits**
- **For age benefits**:
  - **1983**: A creditable year under CPP = **39 weeks** of contributions in Grenada.
  - **From January 1, 1984 onward**: A creditable year under CPP = **52 weeks** of contributions in Grenada.
  - **Weeks after April 4, 1983** (not part of CPP creditable period) = **counted as one week** of contribution in Grenada.

- **For invalidity or survivors‚Äô benefits**:
  - Same rules as for age benefits:
    - 1983 = 39 weeks
    - 1984+ = 52 weeks

> üìå These rules allow **equivalent credit** for contributions made in one country toward entitlements in the other.

#### D. **Totalization of Periods**
- If a person is **not eligible** due to insufficient creditable periods under one Party:
  - Eligibility is determined by **totalizing**:
    1. Creditable periods under Canada and Grenada
    2. Creditable periods under a **third country** with which both Parties have social security agreements (totalization provisions).
- **Exception**: If the total duration of creditable periods under a Party is **less than one year**, and no benefit exists under that legislation alone, the competent institution **shall not be required** to pay benefits under this Agreement.

### üîπ **7. Benefit Payments and Calculations**

#### A. **Old Age Security (OAS) Pension**
- If a person is eligible **only through totalization**, the amount is calculated:
  - **Earnings-related portion**: Based on **pensionable earnings under CPP**.
  - **Flat-rate portion**: Multiplied by a **fraction**:
    - Ratio of actual CPP contributions to the **minimum qualifying period**.
    - This fraction **cannot exceed 1** (i.e., maximum = full flat-rate).

#### B. **Spouse‚Äôs Allowance and Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS)**
- Paid **only to the extent permitted** by the OASA.
- A person outside Canada is eligible **only if total residency periods meet the minimum required by OASA**.
- Spouse‚Äôs allowance and GIS are **not automatically available** in all cases.
