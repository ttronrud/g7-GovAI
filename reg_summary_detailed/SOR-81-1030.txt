## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Regulation on Grants in Lieu of Taxes**

### üìå **Overview**
This regulation, made under the authority of the *Municipal Grants Act, 1980*, establishes the framework for **corporations included in Schedule III or IV** of the Act to make **payments in lieu of real property tax, frontage or area tax, and business occupancy tax** ‚Äî taxes they would otherwise be exempt from due to their status as federal entities.

The regulation defines key terms, sets minimum payment amounts, outlines procedures for making payments, and provides mechanisms for dispute resolution. It also includes provisions for special arrangements and adjustments in cases involving school-related taxes or service obligations.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important Note**: This regulation was **repealed** by:
> - **SOR/2001-494, s. 8** (full repeal)
> - **SOR/2006-324, s. 1** (partial repeal)
> - **SOR/92-506, s. 1** (partial repeal)
> - **SOR/97-103, s. 4** (partial repeal)

Thus, **this regulation is no longer in force** as of the dates listed above. However, this summary provides a full understanding of its historical content and intent.

## üèõÔ∏è **Legal Basis and Context**

- **Authority**: The regulation was issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Public Works**, under **subsection 8(1) of the Municipal Grants Act, 1980**.
- **Purpose**: To ensure that federal corporations (e.g., Crown corporations, port authorities) that are exempt from municipal property and business taxes still contribute fairly to local government revenues through **equivalent payments** in lieu of those taxes.
- **Notice**: A proposed version was published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **December 5, 1980**, giving interested parties a reasonable opportunity to make representations.

## üìÖ **Effective Date and Transitional Provisions**

### 1. **No New Lower Payments Allowed**
> On the coming into force of these regulations, **no corporation** exempt from real property or business occupancy tax may enter into a **special arrangement** with a local government or other authority to pay **less than the amount required under these regulations**.

- This ensures **fairness and consistency** in tax equivalency.
- Prevents corporations from negotiating below-market or below-regulatory levels.

### 2. **Continuation of Existing Agreements**
> If a corporation is already party to a **special arrangement** at the time of implementation, that arrangement may **continue until its expiration**.

- Provides **stability and predictability** for ongoing agreements.

### ‚úÖ 1. **Payment in Lieu of Real Property Tax**

- **Minimum Payment**:  
  Must be **not less than**:
  > (Corporation effective rate) √ó (Corporation property value)

- **Special Case ‚Äì School-Related Taxes**  
  If real property tax is levied at different rates:
  - By religious denomination
  - By class of property  
  Then the effective rate used is adjusted as follows:

  - **For non-school purposes**: Use the portion of the effective rate applied to non-school property.
  - **For school purposes**:  
    - If applicable: Divide the school tax portion by the total assessed value of all taxable property under the jurisdiction.  
    - If applicable per class: Divide the school tax portion by the assessed value of all property of that class.

> üéØ Purpose: To ensure equitable treatment of school tax burdens across denominations or property types.

- **Deductions Allowed** (in determining payment amount):
  1. Amount established under a **special arrangement** for education services.
  2. Amount established under a **special arrangement** for alternative compensation.
  3. Reasonable expenditures incurred or expected by the corporation to provide services if the taxing authority refuses to do so.
  4. Any **cancellation, reduction, or refund** of real property tax that the corporation believes would have applied if it were taxable.

> ‚öñÔ∏è Exception: Despite Section 8, a corporation may deduct **reasonable expenditures** incurred by **Her Majesty in right of Canada** or the corporation to provide the service related to the tax.

## üì• **Payment Procedures**

### General Rules:
- Payments must be made **only to the taxing authority** in the jurisdiction where the property is located.
- Must be made **within 50 days** of receiving an application (for real property and frontage taxes).
- For business occupancy taxes: Must be made **within a reasonable time** after receiving the application.

### Interim Payments:
- If a corporation cannot determine the final payment amount within the deadline, it must make an **interim payment** equal to the **estimated total**.

> üí° Ensures timely contribution and avoids default.

## ü§ù **Dispute Resolution Mechanism**

Where two corporations disagree on the amount of a payment (real property, frontage, or business occupancy tax):

| Scenario | Resolution Path |
|--------|-----------------|
| Both corporations responsible to the **same Minister** | Dispute referred to **that Minister** |
| Otherwise (different Ministers) | Dispute referred to the **Minister of Public Works and Government Services** |

> Ensures a clear, centralized process for resolving inter-corporation disputes.
