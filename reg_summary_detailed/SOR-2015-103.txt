## üìö **Comprehensive Summary of the Rules of the Review Tribunal (Canada Agricultural Review Tribunal)**

### üîπ **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
- These Rules are established under **subsection 8(3)(a)** of the *Canada Agricultural Products Act*.
- The **Review Tribunal** (commonly known as the *Canada Agricultural Review Tribunal*) is empowered to make these rules by virtue of **subsection 4.1(1)** of the same Act.
- The rules are **approved by the Governor in Council** on the recommendation of the **Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food**.
- In case of any inconsistency between these Rules and an Act of Parliament or a regulation made under such an Act, **the Act or regulation prevails**.

---

### üîπ **2. Interpretation and General Principles**
- **"Holiday"** is defined as:
  - A Saturday, Sunday, or any day designated as a holiday under **subsection 35(1) of the Interpretation Act**.
- The rules are to be interpreted and applied so as to ensure:
  - **Justice**
  - **Speed and expediency**
  - **Cost-effectiveness**
- The Tribunal has **discretion** to resolve any procedural matter not covered by these rules, provided it remains consistent with the rules.
- **Time limits** that end on a holiday are **automatically extended** to the next day that is **not a holiday**.
- The Tribunal may **extend time limits** before or after their expiry at its discretion.

---

### üîπ **3. Language of Proceedings**
- Proceedings are conducted in **English or French**, depending on the **language chosen by the applicant**.
- Once a language is selected, **all oral and written communications** (including documents and exhibits) must be in that language ‚Äî **unless parties consent otherwise**.
- If the applicant does **not specify a language** in their request, the **language of the request** is deemed the official language of the proceeding.
- **Interpretation Services**:
  - Required parties must **notify the Tribunal at least 7 days before a hearing** in writing.
  - Must indicate whether interpretation is needed from a language **other than English or French**.
  - Parties requiring non-English/French interpretation must **pay for the costs** of those services.
- The Tribunal may **relieve a party from a rule** if its application would cause **unfairness**.
- The Tribunal may **draw attention to gaps in evidence or non-compliance** with the rules.
- On request, the Tribunal may allow a party to **correct evidence gaps or non-compliance** before the end of proceedings, subject to conditions deemed just.

---

### üîπ **4. Confidentiality of Documents**
- Parties may request that documents or exhibits be treated as **confidential**.
- Such a request must include:
  - Clear **reasons** for confidentiality.
  - A description of the **nature and extent of potential harm** if disclosed.
- The Tribunal may only grant confidentiality if it is **satisfied** that:
  - The documents are genuinely confidential.
  - The **public interest in open and accessible proceedings** is balanced against the need for confidentiality.

### üîπ **5. Filing and Service of Documents**
#### ‚úÖ **Filing Methods**:
A party may file a document with the Tribunal by:
- Hand delivery to the Tribunal‚Äôs head office
- Registered mail to the mailing address
- Courier to the mailing address
- Fax or electronic means
- Ordinary mail to the mailing address

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Filing after 5:00 p.m.** (local time) is deemed received on the **next non-holiday**.

#### ‚úÖ **Service Methods**:
A document may be served on a party by:
- Hand delivery
- Registered mail
- Courier
- Fax or electronic means
- Ordinary mail

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Service after 5:00 p.m.** is deemed received on the **next non-holiday**.

#### ‚úÖ **Effective Date of Filing/Service**:
- **Registered mail or courier**: Effective on the **date of receipt** (as shown on post or courier receipt).
- **Fax/electronic means**: Effective on the **day it is sent**.
- **Ordinary mail**: Effective on the **postmark date**, or **receipt date** if the postmark is illegible.

---

### üîπ **6. Representation and Participation**
- **Individuals** may act **in person** or be represented by legal counsel or another **duly authorized representative**.
- **Corporations, partnerships, or unincorporated associations** must be represented by:
  - Legal counsel, or
  - An officer, partner, or member.
- A party must **notify the Tribunal within 7 days** of any change in:
  - Contact information (e.g., phone, email, address)
  - Legal counsel or representative
- A change in legal representation must be **notified within 7 days** of the change.

---

### üîπ **7. Public Nature of Proceedings**
- All proceedings are **public** by default.
- The Tribunal may order a proceeding to be held **in camera** (privately) if a party shows that:
  - The circumstances **warrant privacy** (e.g., sensitive personal or commercial information).
- The Tribunal may conduct hearings **via teleconference, videoconference, or other electronic means**, at its discretion.

---

### üîπ **8. Presentation of Evidence**
- A party may present evidence by **affidavit**:
  - In **written submissions proceedings**: within **30 days** after expiry of rules 36 or 52.
  - In **oral hearings**: at least **15 days before the hearing date**.
- The deponent of an affidavit must be **available for cross-examination**.
  - Timing of cross-examination is decided by **mutual consent** or by the Tribunal if no agreement is reached.
- The **transcript of cross-examination** must be filed with the Tribunal within **7 days** of the cross-examination.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è The Tribunal may take notice of any matter to **expedite proceedings**.

---

### üîπ **9. Order of Proceedings and Witness Management**
- The Tribunal **establishes the order of evidence** at the start of the oral hearing.
- The Tribunal may **exclude witnesses** from the hearing while others are being called.
- Hearings may be **recorded**.

---

### üîπ **10. Oaths and Examination**
- All persons to be examined orally must take an **oath or make a solemn affirmation** before being examined.
- A party has the right to:
  - Examine their own witnesses
  - Cross-examine witnesses of the other party
  - Re-examine their own witnesses on matters raised during cross-examination

---

### üîπ **11. Conflict of Interest and Impartiality**
- Any party may **immediately notify the Tribunal in writing** if they believe a Tribunal member:
  - Is not in a position to act impartially, or
  - Has a conflict of interest.
- The **Chairperson of the Tribunal must issue a decision within 7 days** of receiving such notice.
- If the Chairperson determines the member is unfit:
  - The member is **excluded** from the matter.
  - The Chairperson may:
    - Order a **rehearing with a differently constituted Tribunal**, or
    - Order a **new hearing**.
- If a Tribunal member **self-discloses a conflict**, the Chairperson must direct the matter to be **reconvened with a different member** or a new hearing.
