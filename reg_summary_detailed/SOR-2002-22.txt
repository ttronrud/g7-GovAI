## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Canadian Chicken Licensing Regulations (SOR/2002-1)**

### üìå **Background and Legal Authority**

- The **Governor in Council** established **Chicken Farmers of Canada (CFC)** under **subsection 16(1)(b)** of the *Farm Products Agencies Act* (S.C. 1993, c. 3, par. 13(b)) via the **Chicken Farmers of Canada Proclamation** (SOR/79-158; SOR/98-244).
- CFC was empowered to implement a **marketing plan** under the same proclamation.
- Based on this authority, CFC issued these **Canadian Chicken Licensing Regulations** under:
  - **Paragraph 22(1)(f)** of the *Farm Products Agencies Act*
  - **Section 11(d)** of the *Schedule to the Chicken Farmers of Canada Proclamation*

> These regulations govern the **marketing of chicken in interprovincial and export trade**, ensuring compliance with quota systems, licensing, reporting, and quality standards.

## üö´ **Prohibited Activities Without a Licence**

Any person (producer, primary processor, processor, dealer, retailer, or transporter) **may not engage in interprovincial or export marketing of chicken** unless they:

1. Hold an appropriate licence issued by CFC;
2. Pay the required licensing fee (as set in **section 6**);
3. Comply with all conditions of the licence (as outlined in **section 5**).

> This establishes a **mandatory licensing regime** to ensure traceability, compliance, and fair market access.

## ‚úÖ **Licensing Process & Requirements**

### üìù Application Requirements
An application must include:
- Full contact details (name, address, phone, fax, email)
- Type of licence sought
- Statement of conditions the licence is subject to
- Expiry date

### üìÖ Issuance Timeline
- CFC must issue a licence **within 30 days** of receiving:
  - A completed, approved application form
  - Payment of the applicable fee

> This ensures timely access to the market for qualified participants.

### üîÅ Licence Expiry
- All licences (except market development or specialty chicken) expire on **December 31 of the fifth year** after issuance.
- **Exception**: A licence may be revoked earlier if conditions are breached.

### üö´ Non-Transferability
- Licences are **not transferable**.
- If a licensee sells, disposes of, or discontinues their business **before expiry**, they must:
  - Return the licence to CFC
  - CFC must **revoke** the licence immediately.

## üìù General Conditions for All Licences

Every licence (except market development or specialty) is subject to the following conditions:

| Condition | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Weekly Marketing Reporting** | Within 7 days after each week ends, report: <br> - Number of head and total live weight of chicken marketed <br> - For interprovincial trade: origin and destination provinces <br> - For export trade: origin province and destination country <br> - Names and addresses of buyers and sellers |
| **Quota Notification Reporting** | Within 7 days of receiving a quota document from the provincial Commodity Board, provide a copy to CFC |
| **Compliance with CFC Rules** | Must follow all CFC orders, regulations, and the *Farm Products Agencies Act* |
| **Accurate Bookkeeping** | Maintain complete and accurate records related to chicken marketing (live or eviscerated) and retain them for **6 years** |
| **No Unlicensed Marketing** | May not market live chicken to persons without valid licences |
| **No Non-Quota Chicken** | May not market chicken not raised by producers with quotas under the *Canadian Chicken Marketing Quota Regulations* |
| **Quota Compliance** | May not market more chicken than the quota allotted by the provincial Commodity Board |
| **No Marketing Excess Quota Chicken** | May not market chicken from producers exceeding their quota |
| **Levy Remittance** | Must remit levies imposed under **sections 3 and 4 of the Canadian Chicken Marketing Levies Order** |
| **Compliance with Commodity Board Marketing Scheme** | Must follow the marketing scheme and rules of the Commodity Board (as determined by the board) |

## üè¢ **Market Development Licence Conditions**

Issued to **primary processors** who use federally inspected slaughtering facilities.

### üîë Required Conditions:
1. **Eligibility**: Must be a primary processor using federally inspected facilities.
2. **Commitment Form Submission**: Must submit a **Market Development Commitment Form (Schedule 1)** to CFC or an authorized Commodity Board **at least 7 days before** the allocation of the quota.
3. **Prohibited Marketing**: May not market chicken not raised by producers with valid federal market development quotas.
4. **Marketing Obligation (during commitment period)**:
   - Must market the **total live weight equivalent** of the number of kg stated in the commitment form.
   - Live weight equivalent is calculated using **coefficients in column 2 of Schedule 2**.
   - Must market to buyers listed in **Schedule 2, section 3** (specific end-uses).
   - Must receive one of the following verification documents from the buyer:
     - Market Development Policy Volume Confirmation Letter (from DFA, Trade and Development)
     - Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) Certificate of Inspection (Meat Products)
     - Official Meat Inspection Certificate (for fresh meat, by-products, etc.)

5. **Compliance Reporting (within 21 days of each reporting period)**:
   - Must submit a detailed report including:
     - Complete product description
     - End-use product description
     - Identity of purchaser
     - Province of origin
     - Date of marketing
     - Eviscerated weight (kg)
     - Live weight equivalent (kg)
     - Any other information designated by CFC
   - Must retain these documents for **6 years**

6. **Document Retention (for review)**:
   - Must retain the following documents for 6 years:
     - Export Declaration (for export trade)
