## ðŸ” **Overview of the Regulation**

This regulation designates specific **physical activities** that are subject to mandatory **environmental assessment (EA)** under the *Canadian Environmental Assessment Act, 2012 (CEAA 2012)*. It defines what constitutes a "designated project" and specifies which types of projects must undergo a formal environmental review before proceeding.

The regulation came into force on **July 6, 2012**, and was initially issued under the authority of **paragraphs 84(a) and (e)** of CEAA 2012, which empower the Minister of the Environment to designate certain physical activities requiring environmental assessment.

Importantly, **many definitions in the regulation have been repealed** (e.g., paper product, pulp, pulp and paper mill, right of way, abandonment, decommissioning, Class IA/IB nuclear facilities), indicating that the scope of the regulation has been streamlined or replaced by more current legislation. This reflects a broader legislative shift toward focusing on major infrastructure and resource development projects with significant environmental impacts.
