## **1. Legal Basis and Purpose**

This by-law, titled **Canadian Payments Association By-law No. 9 â€” Lynx**, was established under the authority of **section 18(1)a and 18(2)c of the Canadian Payments Act (CPA)**. It governs the **clearing and settlement of payment messages** through **Lynx**, the electronic funds transfer system owned and operated by the Canadian Payments Association (CPA).

The primary objectives of this by-law are:
- To define the rules and procedures for operating Lynx.
- To ensure the **efficiency, safety, and soundness** of the payment system.
- To establish clear responsibilities between the **Association, participants (financial institutions), and the Bank of Canada (Bank)**.
- To provide a framework for **real-time settlement**, **collateral management**, **intraday and overnight lending**, and **risk mitigation**.

## **3. Scope and Application**

- This by-law applies to **all clearing and settlement activities** of payment messages through Lynx.
- All **participants** must comply with both this by-law and the **Rules**.
- **No legal rights or liabilities** are created, modified, or imposed by this by-law or the Rules unless explicitly stated.
- The **Association is responsible** for managing, operating, and maintaining Lynx to ensure it functions as required.

## **4. Participant Access and Participation**

### **Application to Join Lynx**
- A member may apply to the **President of the CPA** to become a participant.
- Approval is granted only if:
  - The member meets **technical and testing requirements**;
  - The member has **paid applicable fees**;
  - For non-Bank members:  
    - One Lynx account per settlement mechanism;  
    - One intraday loan account;  
    - Agreements with the Bank regarding accounts, intraday loans, overnight advances, and collateral pledging.

### **Suspension of Participation**
The President may suspend a participantâ€™s access if:
1. The **Bank notifies** that the participant no longer has access to its Lynx accounts or intraday loan account;
2. The participant **fails to meet technical or testing requirements**;
3. The participant **fails to pay required fees**.

> âœ… **Notice Requirement**: Before suspending a participant, the President must **notify the Bank** of the intention.

### **Suspension Due to Financial Instability**
If a regulator, government agent, or supervisor takes control of a participant or declares it **non-viable or insolvent**, the President may suspend participation **only with concurrence of the Minister of Finance and the Governor of the Bank**, if further participation could harm the **efficiency, safety, or soundness** of Lynx.

### **Reinstatement**
- A suspended participant or revoked member may apply to have their status reinstated.
- The President must reinstate the permission if the **circumstances causing suspension or revocation no longer exist**.

### **Revocation by the Board**
The Board may revoke a memberâ€™s approval if:
- The participant no longer has access to Lynx accounts or intraday loan accounts;
- The participant no longer meets technical or testing requirements.

> âœ… **Notice Requirement**: The Board must notify the Bank before revocation.

> After revocation:
> - The President must **immediately notify** the member and **promptly notify all other participants**.

## **5. Operational Requirements**

### **Account Structure**
- The Association must operate the following accounts on behalf of the Bank:
  - **One Lynx account per settlement mechanism** for each participant;
  - **One intraday loan account** for each participant (except the Bank).

### **Daily Operation**
- Lynx must operate **every business day** in accordance with the **Lynx operating schedule**.
- All participants **must have access to Lynx daily**, unless technical issues prevent connection.
- Participants must **connect to Lynx daily** unless prevented by technical difficulties.

### **Collateral and Credit Limit Management**

#### **Pledging Collateral**
- Each participant (other than the Bank) must **pledge collateral**.
- Before each business dayâ€™s payments processing cycle begins, the **Bank assesses the value** of the pledged collateral.

#### **Allocation of Collateral**
- Participants must **allocate part or all of the assessed collateral value** as security for their intraday loan.
- This allocation can be changed **before the finalization window**, but:
  - The **assessed value of allocated collateral must not fall below** the outstanding intraday loan amount at the time of change.

#### **Credit Limit Establishment**
- Before the start of each business dayâ€™s processing cycle:
  - The Bank establishes a **credit limit** equal to the assessed value of the allocated collateral.
  - The credit limit is **provided to the Association**.

> ðŸ” If a participant changes its collateral allocation, a **new credit limit** is established and provided.

#### **Minimum Fund Apportionment**
- The Association may, in accordance with the Rules, set a **minimum amount** of funds a participant must apportion to a settlement mechanism.
- If such a minimum exists, the participant must allocate **at least that amount** to its Lynx account for that mechanism.

## **6. Intraday and Overnight Financing**

### **Intraday Loan**
- Every business day, the Bank makes an **intraday loan** to each participant equal to the **total amount of funds** apportioned to all settlement mechanisms by the participant.

- A participant may repay part or all of the intraday loan **before the finalization window** by transferring funds from any of its Lynx accounts to its intraday loan account.
