**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: "Regulations Respecting Research, Market Development and Technical Assistance (Wheat and Barley)"**

---

### **1. Regulatory Authority and Basis**
- The regulation was issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food of Canada**, under **Section 83.2a of the Canada Grain Act**.
- It is part of **S.C. 2011, c. 25, s. 27**, and is codified in **R.S., c. G-10** (Canada Grain Act Regulations).
- This regulation establishes specific rules governing the collection, remittance, and use of funds derived from cash purchase tickets for wheat and barley grown in certain Canadian provinces.

---

### **2. Scope and Applicability**
- These regulations apply to **wheat and barley** grown in the following regions:
  - **Manitoba**
  - **Saskatchewan**
  - **Alberta**
  - The **Peace River District of British Columbia**
- The regulation specifically targets **cash purchase ticket systems** used under the Canada Grain Act to ensure fair pricing and support for farmers through research, market development, and technical assistance.

---

### **3. Key Agency and Responsibility**
- The **Alberta Barley Commission (Commission)** is designated as the **designated agency** responsible for receiving and managing the collected deductions.
- Under **Section 83.1 of the Canada Grain Act**, amounts collected via deductions from cash purchase tickets are to be remitted to the Commission.

---

### **4. Deduction Requirements for Cash Purchase Tickets**
- When a person is required to issue a cash purchase ticket under the Canada Grain Act, they must collect a **deduction** from the buyer based on the type of grain:
  - **Wheat**: **$0.48 per tonne**
  - **Barley (outside Alberta)**: **$0.56 per tonne**
  - **Barley grown in Alberta**: **$0.04 per tonne** (a significantly lower deduction, reflecting regional differences or cost structures)

> This deduction is intended to fund ongoing research, marketing, and technical support for wheat and barley producers.

---

### **5. Remittance and Reporting Obligations**
- The collected deductions must be **remitted to the Commission within 30 days** of the end of the month in which they were collected.
- Along with the remittance, a **detailed report** must be submitted indicating:
  - The total amount deducted for **wheat**
  - The total amount deducted for **barley**
  - The breakdown by **individual producer**

This ensures transparency and accountability in fund collection and distribution.

---

### **6. Refund Process for Producers**
- Any **producer** who believes they have been overcharged or incorrectly deducted may apply for a **refund** from the Commission.
- To qualify, the application must:
  - Be submitted in the **appropriate form** provided by the Commission
  - Be **signed and dated**
  - Include the producer’s:
    - Full name
    - Mailing address
    - Telephone number
- **Frequency**: A producer may apply for a refund **only once per crop year**.
- **Deadline for submission**: Applications must be submitted by **July 31** of the crop year.
- **Refund timing**: The Commission must remit the refund **not later than 90 days after the end of the crop year**.

> This provision ensures fairness and provides a mechanism for producers to correct errors or over-deductions.

---

### **7. Annual Reporting Requirements**
The Commission is required to submit an **annual report to the Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food** no later than **120 days after the end of the crop year**. The report must include detailed information on:

1. **Total amounts of deductions** collected for wheat and barley.
2. **Research funding**:
   - Total amounts paid for research into **new and improved varieties** of wheat and barley
   - Names of **recipients** of the funds
   - A **description** of the research activities conducted
3. **Marketing and promotion funding**:
   - Total amounts paid for **marketing and use promotion**
   - Recipients of funds
   - Description of promotional activities
4. **Technical assistance funding**:
   - Total amounts paid for **technical support** related to wheat and barley use
   - Recipients of funds
   - Description of technical assistance activities
5. **Refund details**:
   - Total amounts of refunds issued
6. **Administrative costs**:
   - A breakdown of the Commission’s operational and administrative expenses

> This comprehensive reporting ensures that public funds are used transparently and effectively for the benefit of producers and the broader agricultural sector.

---

### **8. Effective Date**
- These regulations come into force on **August 1, 2012**.

---

### **Key Objectives and Implications**
- **Support for Innovation**: Funds support research into improved crop varieties, helping farmers adapt to climate change, disease resistance, and yield improvements.
- **Market Development**: Promotes the competitiveness of Canadian wheat and barley in domestic and international markets.
- **Technical Assistance**: Provides farmers with access to expert knowledge on best practices in planting, harvesting, storage, and use of crops.
- **Fairness and Accountability**: The refund process and reporting requirements ensure that producers are treated fairly and that public funds are used transparently.
- **Regional Differentiation**: The lower deduction for Alberta barley reflects regional economic or operational realities, potentially reducing the financial burden on Alberta producers.

---

### **Conclusion**
This regulation establishes a structured, transparent, and equitable system for collecting and utilizing funds from wheat and barley producers to support agricultural advancement. By directing deductions from cash purchase tickets to the Alberta Barley Commission, it ensures that research, market development, and technical assistance are funded in a way that benefits producers across key Canadian grain-producing regions—while maintaining accountability through mandatory reporting and a clear refund mechanism.

It represents a key component of the Canada Grain Act’s broader goal of promoting sustainable, productive, and resilient grain farming in Canada.
