### âœ… Who Are the Regulated Parties?

## ðŸ“Œ Part I: Policy Committees and Workplace Committees

### 1. **Selection of Committee Members (Non-Union Employees)**

- Employees **not represented by a trade union** must select committee members by **majority vote**.
- **Employer-selected members** must be employees who exercise **managerial functions** (e.g., supervisors, department heads).

> This ensures a balance of power between employee and employer representation.

---

### 2. **Chairperson Rotation and Responsibilities**

- Chairpersons selected under subsection **135.1(7)** of the Act must **alternate** (i.e., rotate) regularly.
- **Responsibilities of Chairpersons** include:
  - Scheduling meetings and notifying members.
  - Preparing the agenda for each meeting.
  - Ensuring that every item discussed at a meeting concludes with a **clear decision**.
  - Ensuring the committee fulfills its intended functions.

> The rotation promotes fairness, accountability, and shared leadership.

---

### 3. **Vacancy Management**

When a committee member resigns or leaves, and the composition no longer meets the legal requirements under **section 135.1**, a new member must be appointed:

| Committee Type | Timeframe for Appointment |
|---------------|----------------------------|
| **Policy Committee** | Within **60 days** of vacancy |
| **Workplace Committee** | Within **30 days** of vacancy |

> This ensures continuity and compliance with statutory requirements.

---

### 4. **Quorum Requirements**

A quorum is required for a committee to conduct valid business:

- Must consist of the **majority of members**.
- At least **half of the members must be employees**.
- At least **one member must be an employer representative**.

> This ensures balanced participation and protects against domination by either side.

---

### 5. **Minutes and Approval Process**

After each meeting:

1. **Minutes must be provided** to both chairpersons for review and approval.
2. If an approval document is issued, it must be **attached to the minutes**.
3. The chairperson **selected by employer members** must provide a copy of the minutes and approval document to:
   - The employer
   - Each committee member

4. The **employer must provide**:
   - To the **workplace committee** (if itâ€™s a policy committee meeting)
   - To the **policy committee** (on request) (if itâ€™s a workplace committee meeting)

5. The employer must make a **copy of the minutes and approval document readily available to all employees** for a period of **one month**.

> This ensures transparency, employee access, and accountability.

---

### 6. **Retention of Records**

The employer must **retain copies** of minutes and approval documents for **two years** after the meeting date at the following locations:

| Committee Type | Location of Record Retention |
|---------------|------------------------------|
| **Policy Committee** | At the employerâ€™s **head office** |
| **Workplace Committee** | At the **head office** OR at the **workplace** |

> This provides a permanent record for audits, inspections, and dispute resolution.

---

### 7. **Annual Reporting (Workplace Committees Only)**

- On or before **March 1 each year**, the chairperson selected by employer members must submit an **annual report** to the **Head of Compliance and Enforcement**.
- The report must:
  - Be in the **form specified in the schedule**
  - Include all required information
  - Be **signed by both chairpersons**

- After submission, the chairperson must provide a copy to the employer.

- The employer must then:
  - Post a copy of the report in a **conspicuous place** (as defined in paragraph 125(1)(z.17) of the Act)
  - Keep it posted for **two months**

> This ensures ongoing oversight, public accountability, and compliance with labour standards.

## ðŸ©º Part II: Health and Safety Representatives

### 1. **Selection of Health and Safety Representatives**

- Employees not represented by a trade union must select their health and safety representatives by **majority vote**.

> Ensures democratic and fair selection.

---

### 2. **Term of Office**

- The term of office for a health and safety representative is **two years**.

> Provides stability and allows for planned review and renewal.

---

### 3. **Vacancy Filling**

- If a representative ceases to serve, the vacancy must be filled within **30 days** of the vacancy occurring.

> Ensures continuous protection of employee health and safety.

---

## ðŸ“š Training Requirements

### Mandatory Training for Committees and Representatives

Employers are required to develop and deliver training programs for:

1. **The Canada Labour Code and relevant regulations**
2. **Means to fulfill responsibilities under the Act**
3. **Rules of operation for each committee or representative**
4. **Principles of consensus-building** in health and safety matters

> This ensures that committee members and representatives are equipped with the knowledge and tools to perform their duties effectively and collaboratively.

### Training Review and Updates

- The training program must be:
  - **Reviewed and updated at least every three years**
  - **Updated whenever there is a significant change in circumstances** (e.g., new legislation, workplace hazards, organizational changes)

> Promotes relevance, adaptability, and responsiveness to evolving workplace conditions.
