### **Detailed Summary of the Baie Verte Mines Inc. Regulations and Agreement (1982)**

The document in question is a comprehensive **governmental agreement and regulatory framework** established in 1982 between **the Government of Canada** and **Baie Verte Mines Inc. (BVM)**, a subsidiary of Transpacific Asbestos Inc., to facilitate the **reactivation of an asbestos mine and milling facility in Baie Verte, Newfoundland**. It combines regulatory provisions (the *Baie Verte Mines Inc. Regulations*) with a detailed financial and operational agreement, reflecting both legal authority and policy objectives related to regional economic development, environmental responsibility, and public-private partnerships.

This summary is structured into **three main sections** for clarity:  
1. **Regulatory Framework (The Regulations)**  
2. **Core Agreement Between Canada and Baie Verte Mines Inc.**  
3. **Key Provisions, Conditions, and Outcomes**

---

## **1. Regulatory Framework: The Baie Verte Mines Inc. Regulations**

These regulations were enacted under the authority of the **Appropriation Acts of 1980–81 and 1981–82**, specifically under Industry, Trade and Commerce Votes 1a and L35. They provide the formal legal basis for financial support to Baie Verte Mines Inc. and define key terms and procedures.

### **Key Elements of the Regulations:**

#### **Definition of Key Parties**
- **Baie Verte Mines Inc. (BVM):**  
  A company incorporated under Ontario law with its head office in Toronto. It is a wholly owned subsidiary of Transpacific Asbestos Inc.

- **Private Lender:**  
  Any lender *excluding*:
  - The Government of Canada
  - Provincial governments of Canada
  - Agencies of such governments or companies effectively controlled by them
  - Municipal corporations

#### **Financial Support from the Minister of Industry, Trade and Commerce**
- **Loans:**  
  The Minister may provide **up to $13 million in aggregate** in operating capital loans to BVM, based on a draft agreement between the Minister of Regional Economic Expansion and BVM (as outlined in the schedule).

- **Insurance for Private Lenders:**  
  The Government may provide **insurance up to $1 million in aggregate** to cover losses arising from loans made by private lenders to BVM.  
  - The insurance amount payable to a private lender is **limited to the amount of insurance in effect on the date of demand** for repayment.

#### **Application Process**
- Applications for loans or insurance must be submitted to the Minister of Industry, Trade and Commerce.
- Applications must include all information and documentation required by the Minister.

#### **Effective Date**
- The regulations come into force on **September 20, 1982**.

---

## **2. Core Agreement Between Canada and Baie Verte Mines Inc.**

This is a formal **public-private development agreement**, approved under the **Department of Regional Economic Expansion Act**, and supported by a **Monitoring Committee** to ensure accountability and performance.

### **Background and Rationale**
- The agreement was entered into due to:
  - The economic decline of the Baie Verte region.
  - The need to re-activate the abandoned asbestos mine and milling operations previously run by Advocate Mines Limited.
  - A shared commitment between Canada and the Province of Newfoundland to improve employment and regional viability.
- The Government of Canada designated the **Baie Verte Peninsula** as a **Special Area** (Schedule A) requiring special measures from August 1, 1982, to March 31, 1987.

### **Key Definitions in the Agreement**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Control Period** | May 13, 1982 – March 31, 1987 |
| **Special Area** | The Baie Verte Peninsula in Newfoundland, as defined on Statistics Canada Map 4 (1981) |
| **Approved Plans** | Operational plans approved by the Monitoring Committee |
| **Approved Levels** | Employment levels approved by the Monitoring Committee |
| **Eligible Expenditures** | Costs approved for reimbursement by the Monitoring Committee (excluding land acquisition or acquisition-related costs) |
| **Operating Capital** | Funds for overburden removal and shortfalls in operations |
| **Line of Credit** | Funds to build up milled fibre inventory |
| **Minister** | Minister of Regional Economic Expansion of Canada (or authorized representative) |
| **Monitoring Committee** | Body established to oversee program implementation and fund disbursement |

## **3. Key Provisions, Conditions, and Outcomes**

### **A. Financial Assistance from Canada**
Canada will provide **up to $13 million in direct loans** to BVM, structured as follows:

| Period | Loan Amount |
|--------|-------------|
| Up to March 31, 1984 | $9 million |
| Fiscal Year Ending March 31, 1985 | Up to $4 million (subject to Minister’s discretion) |
| **Total Maximum** | **$13 million** |

- Canada may **accelerate disbursement** up to the $13 million cap at its sole discretion.

> **Note:** In addition, a **federal loan guarantee of $1 million** is provided to the Royal Bank of Canada or other authorized institutions. This guarantee covers **up to 25% of line of credit funds** drawn down by BVM, but **not exceeding $1 million**. A separate loan guarantee agreement will be signed after banking arrangements are finalized and with Minister of Finance approval.

---

### **B. Repayment Terms of the Loan**

- **Interest Calculation:**
  - Simple interest at **Bank of Canada rate + 1% per annum**, calculated quarterly.
  - Interest accrued to **March 1, 1987**, is **capitalized** and added to the principal.
  - The total amount (principal + accrued interest) is treated as a single sum, and interest is charged at a **weighted average rate** of all payments.

- **Monthly Payments (from March 1, 1987 onward):**
  - The Company must pay **30% of its pre-tax profits** from the prior fiscal year.
  - Payments are applied in this order:
    1. First to **accrued unpaid interest**
    2. Second to **outstanding principal**
  - The final payment is capped at the amount needed to repay the **outstanding principal and unpaid interest**.

- **Prepayment Rights:**
  - The Company may **prepay all or part of the loan at any time without penalty or notice**.
  - However, **prepayments cannot be applied to principal while interest is still outstanding**.

---

### **C. Termination and Default Conditions**

#### **Termination Events:**
- The agreement terminates on **March 31, 1987**, regardless of performance.
- Canada may terminate the agreement **earlier** if:
  - The operation is no longer viable (as assessed annually by the Minister).
  - The Company makes a **significant change to the program** without prior written approval.
  - The Company **substantially reduces employment below approved levels** without prior approval.

#### **Consequences of Default:**
- If default occurs, Canada may:
  - Terminate obligations under the agreement.
  - Require the Company (or Transpacific Asbestos Inc.) to **repay all amounts paid** under the agreement immediately.
  - The amount becomes **immediately due and owing**, and must be repaid promptly.

---

### **D. Monitoring and Oversight**

#### **Role of the Monitoring Committee**
- Established jointly by Canada and the Province of Newfoundland.
- Responsibilities:
  - Monitor and analyze technical, financial, and marketing aspects of operations.
  - Assess the **short- and long-term viability** of the operation.
  - Evaluate monthly cash needs and recommend disbursements.
  - Review annual status reports and recommend continued funding.
  - Ensure compliance with the agreement terms.
  - **Cannot interfere with day-to-day operations** of BVM.

#### **Reporting Requirements**
- The Company must submit:
  - **Monthly progress claims** with detailed financial and operational data.
  - **Audited annual financial statements** within 120 days of fiscal year-end.
  - **External auditor reports** certifying:
    - Familiarity with the agreement’s covenants.
    - No material breaches of covenants observed.
- At the end of the program, auditors must provide a final report (Schedule F).

#### **Access and Cooperation**
- The Company must allow the Monitoring Committee **reasonable access** to the Baie Verte facility.
- The Company agrees to **cooperate fully** with Canada and the Province on a **public information program** to promote transparency and community engagement.

---

### **E. Environmental and Financial Provisions**

- **Environmental Compliance:**  
  All environmental protection measures must meet or exceed **pollutant control standards** set by relevant federal or provincial governments.

- **Profit Attribution and Interest Payments:**
  - The Company’s income is treated as part of **Transpacific Asbestos Inc.’s consolidated adjusted pre-tax profits**.
  - BVM is required to **pay Transpacific interest** on its 11% debenture due March 31, 1987, from its net income (after required payments).
  - Transpacific is entitled to **reimbursement** for eligible expenditures made on behalf of BVM.

- **Funds Usage:**
  - All funds provided to or generated by BVM must be used for **BVM’s operations**.
  - Funds **cannot be transferred** to Transpacific
