**Detailed Summary of the Billiard Cloth Remission Order, 1996**

---

**1. Regulatory Authority and Legal Basis**  
The Billiard Cloth Remission Order, 1996, is issued under the authority of **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of Finance**. This action is authorized specifically under **Section 101 of the Customs Tariff** (*R.S., c. 41 (3rd Supp.)*). Section 101 empowers the federal government to make orders that remit (i.e., reduce or eliminate) customs duties on certain goods, typically for economic or industrial policy reasons.

---

**2. Order Title and Citation**  
The Order is officially titled:  
**"The Billiard Cloth Remission Order, 1996"**  
It may be cited as: *Billiard Cloth Remission Order, 1996*.

---

**3. Purpose of the Order**  
The primary objective of this regulation is to **remit (waive) customs duties** on **billiard cloth** that is imported into Canada for the purpose of **manufacturing or repairing billiard tables**. This measure aims to support domestic industries involved in the production and maintenance of billiard tables by reducing the cost of essential raw materials.

---

**4. Scope of the Remission**  
The remission applies to:  
- **Billiard cloth** classified under **tariff item numbers**:  
  - **5111.19.00**  
  - **5112.19.10**  
  These classifications are found in **Schedule I to the Customs Tariff**, which lists dutiable goods subject to specific tariff rates.  

The remission specifically covers **billiard cloth used in the manufacture or repair of billiard tables**, not for other purposes such as general textile use or non-billiard applications.

---

**5. Conditions for Eligibility**  
Remission is **not automatic** and is subject to two key conditions:

**Condition 1: Import Period**  
The billiard cloth must have been **imported into Canada during a specific timeframe**:  
- From **July 1, 1995**, to **December 31, 1997**, inclusive.  
This means goods imported outside this window are **not eligible** for remission under this Order.

**Condition 2: Timely Claim Submission**  
A claim for remission must be **submitted to the Minister of National Revenue within three years** of the date of importation.  
- The claim must be filed **no later than three years after the date the goods entered Canada**.  
- Failure to file within this period results in **loss of eligibility** for the remission.

---

**6. Legal and Administrative Implications**  
- The remission is granted under **Section 2** of the Customs Tariff, which allows for duty remission in specific cases, often tied to industrial or economic objectives.  
- The Minister of National Revenue is the responsible authority to process claims and determine eligibility.  
- The Order is **non-renewable** and limited to the specified time frame (1995â€“1997), indicating a temporary, targeted policy intervention.

---

**7. Policy Rationale**  
This Order reflects a **supportive policy toward the domestic billiard industry**, which may have been experiencing challenges due to high input costs. By reducing the cost of imported billiard cloth, the government aims to:  
- Support small and medium-sized manufacturers of billiard tables;  
- Encourage domestic production and repair services;  
- Maintain the competitiveness of Canadian-based billiard table manufacturing and maintenance businesses.

---

**8. Key Limitations**  
- **Time-bound**: Only applies to imports between July 1, 1995, and December 31, 1997.  
- **Specific use only**: Only applies to cloth used in **manufacture or repair** of billiard tables.  
- **Strict claim deadline**: Claims must be filed within three years of importation.  
- **No extension or retroactive application**: The Order does not extend beyond the defined period or apply to goods imported before or after the specified dates.

---

**Conclusion**  
The *Billiard Cloth Remission Order, 1996* is a targeted, temporary regulatory measure designed to alleviate the financial burden on Canadian manufacturers and repairers of billiard tables by waiving customs duties on specific types of imported billiard cloth. It operates within a clear legal framework and imposes strict eligibility conditions to ensure fiscal accountability and targeted support. While narrow in scope, it serves as an example of how Canadian trade policy can be used to support niche domestic industries.
