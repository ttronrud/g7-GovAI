### **Detailed Summary of the National Energy Board Cost Recovery Regulations (S.C. 1990, c. 7, s. 13)**

---

#### **Overview and Purpose**

The **National Energy Board Cost Recovery Regulations**, effective **January 1, 1991**, were enacted under **section 24.1 of the National Energy Board Act** and with the **approval of the Treasury Board**. These regulations establish a formal framework for the **recovery of certain operational and administrative costs** from regulated companies operating in Canadaâ€™s energy sectorâ€”specifically pipelines and power lines.

The primary objective is to ensure that the **National Energy Board (NEB)** can recover reasonable and attributable costs associated with its regulatory responsibilities, including oversight, enforcement, and program delivery, without relying solely on government funding. This system provides a **cost-based, tiered mechanism** for cost recovery based on the size and type of the regulated entity.

## **Cost Determination Methodology (Section 6)**

The total costs attributed to the NEB are calculated as follows:

> **Total attributable costs = 95% of:**
> - **1/4 of estimated program costs** (from the *Expenditure Plan* in the Government of Canadaâ€™s Estimates for the **previous fiscal year**)  
> - **3/4 of forecasted program costs** (from the *Expenditure Plan* for the **next fiscal year**)

This formula blends **past and future spending projections** to provide a forward-looking, budgetary basis for cost recovery.

---

## **Annual Financial Adjustments (Section 7)**

The Board must annually assess whether actual expenditures differ from projected costs:

1. **Compare actual expenditures** with the total program costs calculated under Section 6.
2. **Adjust the total costs** by:
   - Subtracting any **over-recovery** (if actual spending was less than projected)
   - Adding any **under-recovery** (if actual spending exceeded projections)
   - Subtracting **all administration levies** already collected from:
     - Large commodity pipeline companies ($50,000)
     - Intermediate commodity pipeline companies ($10,000)
     - Small commodity pipeline companies ($500)
     - Border accommodation pipelines ($500)

This ensures that the NEBâ€™s cost recovery is **accurate, transparent, and not double-counted**.
