### **Detailed Summary of the Public Service Staff Relations Board (P.S.S.R.B.) Regulations and Rules of Procedure, 1993**

This regulation, issued by the **Public Service Staff Relations Board (P.S.S.R.B.)** under specific provisions of the *Public Service Staff Relations Act*, formally **revokes the previous Regulations and Rules of Procedure** enacted on November 7, 1990 (SOR/90-771), and **replaces them with the updated version adopted in 1993**. The new regulations are effective upon publication in the *Canada Gazette* and are officially cited as:

> **P.S.S.R.B. Regulations and Rules of Procedure, 1993**

These regulations govern the procedures for all proceedings before the Board, including complaints, applications for certification, revocation of certification, declarations on the legality of strikes, and consent applications under section 107 of the Act. The rules are designed to ensure procedural fairness, transparency, and efficiency in resolving labor relations matters within the Canadian public service.

---

## **I. Legal Authority and Purpose**

The Board enacted these regulations pursuant to several key sections of the *Public Service Staff Relations Act*, including:

- **Sections 5.2(1)** and **22(1)**: Authority to regulate procedures.
- **Section 28**: Power to make rules.
- **Subsections 29(1)** and **37(1)**: Authority to define procedural matters.
- **Section 78.5**: Powers related to hearings and hearings on applications.
- **Subsection 92(2)**: Authority to conduct investigations.
- **Section 95**: Powers regarding the filing and handling of documents.
- **Subsection 99(1)** and **Section 100**: Powers to manage hearings and applications.

These provisions empower the Board to establish clear, consistent, and fair procedures for all labor-related disputes involving public service employees and their employers.

---

## **II. Key Provisions and Functional Elements**

### **1. Replacement of Outdated Rules**
- The 1990 version of the P.S.S.R.B. Regulations and Rules of Procedure is **officially revoked**.
- The **1993 version supersedes** the 1990 version, incorporating modernized procedures, clarified timelines, and enhanced procedural safeguards.

---

### **2. Definitions and Key Terms**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Public Service Staff Relations Act*. |
| **Application** | Includes a complaint, application for certification, revocation of certification, declaration of a strike as lawful or unlawful, or consent under section 107. |
| **Board** | The Public Service Staff Relations Board. |
| **Person** | Includes employee organizations, councils of employee organizations, and employers. |
| **Secretary** | The Board’s Secretary and any assistant secretary. |
| **Terminal Date** | A deadline set between **10 and 30 days** after an application is filed. This period excludes Saturdays and holidays. |

> **Note:** All time periods specified in the regulations are computed **excluding weekends and statutory holidays**.

### **3. Board’s Discretionary Powers**

The Board is granted broad discretion to manage proceedings, including:

#### a) **Requesting Clarification of Documents**
- The Board may request that information in a filed document be made **more complete or specific**.
- After providing the party with an opportunity to respond, the Board may **strike out** information that is incomplete or insufficient.

#### b) **Consolidating Proceedings**
- The Board may **consolidate** multiple proceedings into one to promote efficiency and reduce duplication.

#### c) **Adjournment of Hearings**
- The Board may **adjourn** a hearing and specify the day, time, place, and terms of continuation.

#### d) **Extension or Reduction of Deadlines**
- The Board may:
  - **Extend** time limits for filing documents, providing notices, or performing acts.
  - **Reduce** time limits on notice or filing, provided it is **notified to all parties**.

#### e) **Adding Parties or Documents**
- The Board may **add a person as a party** to a proceeding or **direct that a party be provided with a document**.

#### f) **Dismissal of Applications**
- The Board may **dismiss an application** if it lacks jurisdiction.
- Before dismissing, the Board must:
  - Request written arguments from the parties within a specified timeframe, or
  - Hold a **preliminary hearing**.
- If dismissed, the Board must provide **a written decision and reasons** to the parties.

#### g) **Review Requests**
- A party may file a **request for review** within **25 days** of receiving the decision.
- The request must include a **concise statement of facts and grounds**.
- The Board will:
  - Issue a **notice of hearing to show cause** why the review should be granted,
  - Either **rescind the decision** and order the proceeding to continue, or
  - **Confirm** the original decision dismissing the application.
- If a hearing is held, the Board will act accordingly based on the evidence presented.

### **4. Filing and Service of Documents**

#### a) **Filing of Documents**
- A document is deemed filed when:
  - Sent by **registered mail** to the Secretary, on the **date sent**; or
  - Received by or on behalf of the Secretary, on the **day received**.
- If received **after 4:00 p.m. on a business day**, it is deemed filed on the **next business day**.

#### b) **Notice of Hearing**
- A notice of hearing must be provided to parties **at least five days before** the scheduled hearing.
- If a party **fails to attend**, the Board may proceed with the hearing and dispose of the matter **without further notice**.

#### c) **Complaints (Section 23 of the Act)**
- Must be filed with the Secretary in **duplicate** using **Form 2** of the schedule.
- The Secretary must provide a copy to each **respondent** named in the complaint.

#### d) **Reply to Complaints**
- Respondents must file a **reply in duplicate** within **10 days** of receiving the complaint.
- The Secretary must provide the complainant with a copy of the reply.

#### e) **Hearing Notice**
- A notice of hearing is issued when:
  - The respondent fails to file a reply within the time specified; or
  - The complainant receives a copy of the reply.

### **5. Certification Proceedings (Applications under Section 19)**

#### a) **Filing of Application**
- Applications must be filed in **duplicate** using **Form 3** of the schedule.

#### b) **Notice to Employer**
- The Secretary must:
  - Provide the employer with a copy of the application.
  - Fix a **terminal date** (between 10–30 days).
  - Notify all parties of the terminal date.

#### c) **Notice Posting Requirements**
- Employers must:
  - Post notices **immediately upon receipt** in **conspicuous locations**.
  - Keep them posted **until after the terminal date**.
  - File a **statement of compliance** after the terminal date.

#### d) **Employer’s Reply**
- The employer must file a **reply in duplicate** using **Form 5** by the terminal date.

#### e) **Intervention by Employee Organizations**
- Any employee organization that represents affected employees may file an **intervention** in duplicate using **Form 6** by the terminal date.
- If seeking certification as a bargaining agent, the organization must file an **intervener’s application** in **triplicate** using **Form 7** by the terminal date.

#### f) **Intervenor’s Application (Section 20 & 21)**
- Applies with **modifications based on circumstances**.
- If the Board sets a **separate terminal date**, the Secretary must fix and notify the parties.

#### g) **Statement of Opposition**
- Any employee or group of employees affected by the application may file a **concise statement of opposition** by the terminal date.
- Must be:
  - Signed by the employee or each member of the group.
  - Include a mailing address.

#### h) **Amendments**
- Any application, reply, intervention, or statement may be **amended with leave of the Board**.

#### i) **Authority of Councils**
- If a council of employee organizations files an application, it must submit **documents proving each member has delegated authority** to act as a bargaining agent.

#### j) **Employer’s Position Lists**
- The Board may require the employer to file:
  - A list of **all employees** in the proposed bargaining unit with **specimen signatures**.
  - A list of employees in **managerial or confidential positions**.

#### k) **Response to Position Lists**
- The applicant or intervener must file a **statement within 10 days** of receiving the list, indicating whether they **object** to any position being identified as managerial or confidential.

#### l) **Evidence of Majority Support**
- All or part of the **documentary evidence** must accompany the application to show that a **majority of employees** in the proposed unit wish to be represented by the applicant or intervener.
- Any missing evidence must be filed by the **terminal date**.
