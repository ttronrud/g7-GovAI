### **Detailed Summary of the Federal Court Rules, 1998**

The **Federal Court Rules, 1998** are a set of procedural regulations governing the administration and conduct of all proceedings in the **Federal Court of Appeal** and the **Federal Court of Canada**, as established under the *Federal Courts Act* (S.C. 1990, c. 8). These rules were formally approved by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Justice**, pursuant to **subsection 46(1)b of the Federal Court Act**, following public consultation.

---

## **I. Background and Approval Process**

- The proposed rules were first published in the *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **September 20, 1997**, in accordance with **subsection 46(4)a** of the Federal Court Act.
- Interested parties (including legal professionals, litigants, and the public) were invited to submit written representations or comments.
- Based on that feedback, the **Rules Committee of the Federal Court** developed the final version on **January 26, 1998**.
- The Governor General in Council formally approved the rules, effective as of the date of publication.

> **Legal Authority**:  
> - S.C. 1990, c. 8, s. 14(4) – Authority to make rules  
> - S.C. 1990, c. 8, s. 14(1) – General rule-making power

---

## **II. Scope and Application**

### **1. Applicability**
- These Rules apply to **all proceedings** in:
  - The **Federal Court of Appeal**
  - The **Federal Court**
- **Unless otherwise provided by or under an Act of Parliament**, the rules govern all matters.

### **2. Precedence of Statutes and Regulations**
- In the event of **inconsistency** between these Rules and an Act of Parliament or a regulation made under such an Act, **the Act or regulation prevails** to the extent of the inconsistency.

A comprehensive set of definitions is provided to ensure clarity and consistency across all proceedings. Key terms include:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Federal Courts Act* |
| **Action** | A proceeding initiated under Rule 169 (e.g., civil litigation) |

| **Administrator** | The Chief Administrator of the Courts Administration Service Act, or someone acting on their behalf |
| **Admiralty Action** | Proceedings under Section 22 of the Act involving maritime law |
| **Appeal** | A proceeding under Rule 335 (e.g., appeal from a decision of the Federal Court) |
| **Applicant** | In a class proceeding: representative applicant for common issues; individual member for personal issues |
| **Application** | A proceeding under Rule 300 (e.g., motion for leave, reference) |
| **Assessment Officer** | A Registry officer or referee assigned to a reference |
| **Case Management Judge** | A judge or prothonotary assigned under Rule 383 or 383.1 to manage complex cases |
| **Certified Copy** | A copy of a Registry document certified by an officer of the Registry |
| **Court** | May refer to either the Federal Court of Appeal (including a single judge) or the Federal Court (including a prothonotary) |
| **Court File** | The official record maintained under Rules 23 or 24 |
| **Dispute Resolution Conference** | A conference ordered under Rule 386 to resolve conflicts |
| **Filed** | A document accepted for filing under Rule 72 |
| **Garnishee** | A person subject to an order attaching a debt to a judgment debtor under Rule 449 |
| **Hague Convention** | The 1965 Convention on Service Abroad of Judicial Documents |
| **Hearing** | Includes any conference or meeting under these Rules |
| **Holiday** | Includes Saturdays, statutory holidays, and specific adjustments for New Year’s Day, Canada Day, Remembrance Day, and Christmas Day |
| **Intervener** | A person granted status under Rule 109 to participate in proceedings |
| **Issued** | A document dated, signed, sealed, and assigned a Court file number by the Administrator |
| **Local Office** | A Registry office outside the principal office (e.g., Calgary, Toronto, Winnipeg) |
| **Motion** | A request to the Court under or to enforce these Rules |
| **Oath** | Includes a solemn affirmation under the Canada Evidence Act |
| **Order** | Includes judgments, decisions, and determinations of references under Section 18.3 |
| **Originating Document** | A document referenced in Rule 63 (e.g., statement of claim) |
| **Party** | Varies by proceeding: plaintiff/defendant in actions; applicant/respondent in applications; appellant/respondent in appeals |
| **Person** | Includes tribunals, unincorporated associations, and partnerships |
| **Plaintiff** | In class actions: representative plaintiff for common issues; individual member for personal issues |
| **Pleading** | A document initiating, defining, defending, or responding to a claim |
| **Principal Office** | Head office of the Registry in Ottawa |
| **Referee** | A person to whom a matter is referred under Rule 153 |
| **Registry** | Repealed in 2004; functions now performed by officers |
| **Seasonal Recess** | December 21 to January 7 each year |
| **Sheriff** | Includes marshals, peace officers, and in Quebec, members of the Ordre professionnel des huissiers de justice du Québec |
| **Simplified Action** | A streamlined proceeding under Rule 292 |
| **Solicitor** | A person authorized under the Act |
| **Solicitor of Record** | A solicitor registered under Rules 123 or 124 |
| **Specially Managed Proceeding** | A case managed under Rules 383–385 |
| **Statement of Claim** | The document initiating a civil action |
| **Summer Recess** | July and August each year |
| **Third Party** | Includes any fourth or subsequent party |
| **Tribunal** | Same as federal board, commission, or other tribunal under the Act |
| **Unincorporated Association** | A group operating under a common name for a common purpose |
| **Writ of Execution** | Includes seizure and sale, possession, delivery, and sequestration |

> Note: Several terms (e.g., *business day*, *Christmas recess*) have been **repealed** due to changes in policy or administrative structure (SOR/2015-21, SOR/2021-244).

## **IV. Principles of Procedure**

### **1. Guiding Principles**
The Rules emphasize:
- **Justice**, **expedience**, and **cost-effectiveness** in every proceeding.
- **Proportionality** — considering:
  - The complexity of the case
  - The importance of the legal issues
  - The amount in dispute

> This ensures that procedures are tailored to the specific needs of each case, promoting fairness and efficiency.

### **2. Flexibility and Judicial Discretion**
- The Court may, **on motion**, provide for any procedural matter not covered in the Rules or an Act by:
  - **Analogy** to existing rules
  - **Reference to superior court practice** in the province most closely related to the subject matter
- Rules may incorporate **necessary variations** when forms are used.

## **V. Time and Deadlines**

### **1. Time Calculation**
- Time periods are governed by **Sections 26–30 of the Interpretation Act**, unless otherwise directed.
- **Holidays**:
  - Do **not count** in periods of less than 7 days.
  - Are **excluded** from time calculations for filing, amending, transmitting, or serving documents **if the period is less than 7 days**.
- **Seasonal Recess**:
  - Days within the summer (July–August) or seasonal recess (Dec 21 – Jan 7) **do not count** in time for filing or service unless otherwise ordered.
- **Extensions**:
  - May be granted **once** by **written consent of all parties**.
  - Maximum extension: **half of the original period**.
  - **No extension** is allowed:
    - For periods fixed by an order of the Court
    - Under specific provisions (e.g., subsections 203(1), 304(1), 339(1))
  - The Court may, **on motion**, extend or shorten any period.

> A motion for time extension may be filed **before or after** the deadline.

---

## **VI. Court Administration and Registry Functions**

### **1. Court Registrar**
- The Administrator must ensure that a **duly qualified person** is present at every Court sitting to:
  - Manage the sitting efficiently and orderly
  - Maintain records of all material events
  - Keep and manage all books, records, and exhibits filed during the sitting

### **2. Seal of the Court**
- The seal of both the Federal Court of Appeal and the Federal Court must be:
  - Approved by the Chief Justice
