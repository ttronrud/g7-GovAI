### **Detailed Summary of the FIFA Men's U-20 World Cup Canada 2007 Remission Order**

---

#### **1. Introduction and Legal Basis**
This regulation, known as the **FIFA Men's U-20 World Cup Canada 2007 Remission Order**, was issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, under **section 115a of the Customs Tariff**. It provides for the **remission (exemption)** of certain customs duties, excise taxes, and goods and services tax (GST) on goods imported into Canada in connection with the **FIFA Men's U-20 World Cup Canada 2007**.

- **Statutory References**:
  - *S.C. 2005, c. 38, par. 145(2)(j)* – Authority for remission of duties and taxes.
  - *S.C. 1997, c. 36* – Reference to the Customs Act and related provisions.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **CSA** | Canadian Soccer Association Incorporated (ACS) – the national governing body for soccer in Canada. |
| **FIFA** | Fédération Internationale de Football Association – the international governing body of football. |
| **Foreign Corporation** | A corporation whose head office is outside Canada and has no branch or subsidiary in Canada. Applies specifically to entities acting as: <br> - Media rights holders <br> - Sponsors <br> - Suppliers |
| **Media Rights Holder** | A corporation granted broadcasting rights to the U-20 World Cup by either the CSA or FIFA. |
| **Sponsor** | Any official sponsor of the U-20 World Cup designated by FIFA. |
| **Supplier** | Any official supplier to the U-20 World Cup designated by the CSA or FIFA. |
| **U-20 World Cup** | The FIFA Men’s U-20 World Cup Canada 2007, held from **June 30 to July 22, 2007**, in Canada. |
| **U-20 World Cup Family Member** | An individual (not ordinarily resident in Canada) who is: <br> - An athlete, coach, team official, support staff member, or technical official; or <br> - A member of FIFA holding an accreditation issued by the CSA and approved by FIFA. |

> **Note**: This Order **does not apply** to alcoholic beverages or tobacco products.

---

#### **3. Scope and Purpose of Remission**

The primary objective is to facilitate the smooth organization and participation in the U-20 World Cup by **removing or reducing import taxes and duties** on goods essential to the event, while ensuring compliance with Canadian customs regulations.

---

### **4. Specific Provisions of Remission**

---

#### **(1) Remission for U-20 World Cup Family Members**
- **Applies to**: Goods imported temporarily by a **U-20 World Cup family member**.
- **Conditions**:
  - Goods must be for the **exclusive use** of that individual in connection with the U-20 World Cup.
- **Taxes Remitted**: Customs duties, excise taxes, and GST.

> *Purpose*: To allow athletes, officials, and international staff to bring necessary personal items without incurring import costs.

---

#### **(2) Remission for CSA or Foreign Corporations (Media, Sponsorship, Equipment)**
- **Applies to**:
  - Goods for **display** (including apparatus and equipment) imported by the CSA or a foreign corporation (or their agent/representative).
  - Equipment imported by the CSA or a foreign corporation (or their agent/representative).
- **Conditions**:
  - Goods must be used **exclusively** in connection with the U-20 World Cup.
- **Taxes Remitted**:
  - **Customs duties** (full remission).
  - **Portion of GST** calculated as follows:
    > GST remitted =  
    > (GST on full value of goods)  
    > − (GST on 1/60th of the value of goods for each month or portion of a month the goods remain in Canada)

> *Example*: If goods are imported for 3 months, GST remitted = GST on full value − (GST on 1/60th × value) × 3  
> *Rationale*: This formula incentivizes timely export or destruction of goods to avoid prolonged tax liability.

---

#### **(3) Remission for Media Rights Holders, Sponsors, Suppliers, FIFA**
- **Applies to**: Goods imported by media rights holders, sponsors, suppliers, FIFA, or their agents/representatives.
- **Conditions**:
  - Unit value of goods must be **$60 or less**.
  - Goods must be **freely distributed** at the U-20 World Cup.
- **Taxes Remitted**: Customs duties.

> *Purpose*: To enable free distribution of promotional or branded items (e.g., merchandise, giveaways) during the tournament without tax barriers.

---

#### **(4) Remission for Gifts or Awards to U-20 World Cup Family Members**
- **Applies to**: Goods imported by a U-20 World Cup family member.
- **Conditions**:
  - Unit value ≤ $60.
  - Goods are distributed as:
    - Gifts or awards to:
      - Another U-20 World Cup family member;
      - An agent/representative of the CSA;
      - A resident of Canada participating in the tournament;
      - A resident of Canada acting in an official capacity related to the event.
- **Taxes Remitted**: Customs duties, excise taxes, and GST.

> *Purpose*: To support the exchange of awards and recognition among participants and officials without tax burden.

---

#### **(5) Remission for Athletic Equipment**
- **Applies to**: Athletic equipment imported by the CSA.
- **Conditions**:
  - Equipment must be:
    1. **Certified by FIFA** as meeting international competition standards;
    2. Required **exclusively** by an athlete for training or competition in the U-20 World Cup;
    3. **Donated** after the tournament to:
       - A not-for-profit sport club;
       - A sport federation;
       - A registered charity;
       - An educational or public institution;
       - A municipality, town, or city;
    4. **Not sold or disposed of** within **two years** of importation.
- **Taxes Remitted**: Customs duties.

> *Purpose*: To ensure that high-quality, standardized equipment is available for athletes while promoting post-event community benefit.

---

#### **(6) Remission for Clothing Donations and Volunteer Uniforms**
- **Applies to**: Clothing imported into Canada.
- **Conditions**:
  - Clothing must be:
    1. **Donated to the CSA by a sponsor or supplier**;
    2. **Provided free of charge** to CSA volunteers to wear as uniforms during official duties;
    3. **Kept by the individual volunteer recipient** after the U-20 World Cup.
- **Taxes Remitted**: Customs duties.

> *Purpose*: To support volunteer operations and promote inclusivity and recognition of volunteer contributions.

---

### **5. Conditions for Remission (General Requirements)**

All remission claims must meet the following **mandatory conditions**:

1. **Import Timing**:
   - Goods must be imported into Canada **between February 1, 2007, and July 22, 2007** (the event period).

2. **Claim Submission Deadline**:
   - A claim for remission must be submitted to the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** **within two years** of the date the goods were accounted for under **section 32 of the Customs Act**.

3. **Evidence Requirement**:
   - The importer must provide **evidence or information** to the **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)** demonstrating entitlement to remission under this Order.

4. **Final Disposition of Goods**:
   - Remission is granted **only if** the goods are:
     - **Exported from Canada** on or before **October 1, 2007**; **or**
     - **Destroyed in Canada** under the supervision of a CBSA officer, at the importer’s expense.

> *Note*: This condition ensures that goods are not retained indefinitely in Canada and prevents potential misuse or tax evasion.

---

### **6. Enforcement and Compliance**

- The **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)** is responsible for verifying claims and ensuring compliance.
- The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** is the designated authority for processing remission claims.
- The Order is **not retroactive** — only applies to goods imported during the specified window (Feb 1 – July 22, 2007).

---

### **7. Effective Date**

- The Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** with the Canada Gazette.
