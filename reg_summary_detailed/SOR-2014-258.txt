**Detailed Summary of the Railway Operating Certificate Regulations**

---

### **Overview**

The *Railway Operating Certificate Regulations* were made by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Transport, under **section 17.9a of the Railway Safety Act** (R.S., c. 32 (4th Supp.), S.C. 2012, c. 7, s. 12). These regulations establish the requirements for issuing, varying, and managing **railway operating certificates** in Canada. The regulations are designed to ensure that all railway operators â€” whether full-service railway companies or local operators â€” meet rigorous safety standards, particularly in areas such as employee fitness, equipment safety, rule compliance, and operational management.

---

### **Key Definitions**

- **"Rule"** refers to any safety rule that has been formally approved by the Minister of Transport under **section 19 of the Railway Safety Act**. This ensures that only recognized, vetted safety procedures are used in operations.

---

### **Applicability of the Regulations**

The regulations apply to two main types of applications:

1. **Applications for the issuance of a railway operating certificate** authorizing a **railway company** to operate and maintain a railway.
2. **Applications for the issuance of a railway operating certificate** authorizing a **local railway company** to operate railway equipment on a railway.
3. **Applications for variation** (amendments) of an existing certificate.

Sections 4 and 5 govern the first type; Sections 7 and 8 govern the second. The rules for variation are outlined in a separate section.

---

## **I. Application for Issuance of a Railway Operating Certificate (Full Railway Operator)**

### **Submission Requirements**
An application must be submitted to the Minister via:
- **Email**, or  
- **Registered mail**

The application must include the following elements:

1. **Legal Name of the Applicant**  
   The official legal name of the company applying for the certificate.

2. **Description of the Applicantâ€™s Railway**  
   A detailed description of the railway system, broken down by **province and territory**.

3. **Description of the Applicantâ€™s Operations**  
   A clear outline of the types of operations the company will conduct (e.g., passenger transport, freight, maintenance, etc.).

4. **List of Rules in Place**  
   A comprehensive list of the rules adopted by the applicant to meet the conditions of issuance. These must be approved under section 19 of the Railway Safety Act.

5. **Attestation by Senior Officer**  
   A sworn statement by the **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)** or the **most senior officer** of the company, affirming that:
   - The application is **complete and accurate**;  
   - The company has the **necessary human and financial resources** to operate and maintain its railway at the **highest level of safety**; and  
   - The company either currently has or will establish a **safety management system** compliant with the *Railway Safety Management System Regulations, 2015*.

---

### **Conditions of Issuance (Mandatory Requirements)**

To qualify for a full railway operating certificate, the applicant must meet **all** of the following conditions:

| Condition | Details |
|--------|--------|
| **1. Rule Compliance** | Must have rules that **reproduce the Canadian Rail Operating Rules (CROR)** or provide an **equivalent level of safety**. |
| **2. Fatigue Management** | Must have rules governing the **management of employee fatigue** for operating personnel. |
| **3. Medical Fitness for Duty** | Must have rules ensuring employees in **critical safety positions** are medically fit for duty. |
| **4. Employee Qualifications** | Must have rules defining and verifying the **qualifications** of employees. |
| **5. Track Safety (if applicable)** | If operations include **line works (track maintenance or construction)**, must have rules setting **minimum safety standards for track**. |
| **6. Passenger Safety (if applicable)** | If transporting passengers, must have: <br> - Rules for **safe handling of passengers** <br> - Minimum safety standards for: <br>   â€¢ Passenger cars <br>   â€¢ Train brakes <br>   â€¢ Locomotives |
| **7. Freight Safety (if applicable)** | If transporting freight, must have minimum safety standards for: <br> â€¢ Freight cars <br> â€¢ Train brakes <br> â€¢ Locomotives <br> â€¢ Retroreflective materials (e.g., for visibility) and their application on equipment |
| **8. Resource Capacity** | Must demonstrate **sufficient human and financial resources** to operate and maintain the railway at the **highest level of safety**. |

## **II. Application for Issuance of a Certificate for a Local Railway Operator (Operating Equipment on a Railway)**

This type of certificate allows a **local operator** (e.g., a contractor or small entity) to operate railway equipment on a railway system.

### **Submission Requirements**
The application must include:

1. **Legal Name of the Applicant**  
2. **Description of Railways**  
   By province and territory, specifying where the applicant will operate.  
3. **Description of Operations**  
   What types of operations the applicant will perform.  
4. **List of Rules**  
   Rules adopted to meet the conditions of issuance (must be approved under section 19).  
5. **Attestation by Senior Officer**  
   As in the full operator case: confirms completeness, accuracy, and that the applicant has the **resources and a safety management system** compliant with the *Railway Safety Management System Regulations, 2015*.

### **Additional Requirement: List of Safety Instruments**
The applicant must also provide a **detailed list of all instruments** that impose safety requirements on their operations. This includes:

- Agreements with railway companies  
- Provinces or territories' laws and regulations  

The list must be:
- **Organized by instrument type** (e.g., contracts, provincial statutes)  
- For each instrument, must specify:
  - The name and nature of the instrument  
  - Any requirements related to:
    - **Fatigue management**  
    - **Medical fitness for duty** (for critical roles)  
    - **Employee qualifications**

> This ensures transparency and compliance with external safety obligations.

---

To receive a certificate for operating equipment, the applicant must meet the following:

| Condition | Details |
|--------|--------|
| **1. Rule Compliance** | Must have rules that **reproduce CROR** or provide **equivalent safety**. |
| **2. Passenger Safety (if applicable)** | If transporting passengers: <br> - Rules for safe handling of passengers <br> - Minimum safety standards for: <br>   â€¢ Passenger cars <br>   â€¢ Train brakes <br>   â€¢ Locomotives |
| **3. Freight Safety (if applicable)** | If transporting freight: <br> - Minimum safety standards for: <br>   â€¢ Freight cars <br>   â€¢ Train brakes <br>   â€¢ Locomotives <br>   â€¢ Retroreflective materials and their application |
| **4. Resource Capacity** | Must demonstrate **sufficient human and financial resources** to operate railway equipment at the **highest level of safety**. |

> âš ï¸ *Note: Local operators are not required to meet track safety standards unless they are directly involved in line work.*

## **III. Application for Variation of a Railway Operating Certificate**

A variation request (e.g., changing operations, expanding services, updating rules) must include:

1. **Legal Name of the Applicant**  
2. **Certificate Number**  
3. **Description of the Variation and Reason**  
   Why the change is being requested (e.g., new route, updated equipment, revised staffing).  
4. **Attestation by Senior Officer**  
   Confirmation that:
   - The application is complete and accurate; and  
   - The **conditions of issuance continue to be met** after the variation.

> The Minister will assess whether the variation maintains or improves safety and whether the applicant still meets all required standards.

---

## **Entry into Force**

- These regulations come into force on the day that **Section 12 of the Safer Railways Act** comes into force.  
- If the regulations are **registered after that date**, they will come into force on the **date of registration**.

|--------|-------------|
| **Enhanced Safety Standards** | Mandates alignment with CROR or equivalent safety standards, ensuring consistency across the rail network. |
| **Employee Safety Focus** | Requires rules on fatigue, medical fitness, and qualifications â€” protecting workers and preventing human error. |
