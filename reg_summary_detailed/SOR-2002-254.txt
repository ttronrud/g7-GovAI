## ğŸš¨ **Exemptions and Exceptions**

The regulations **do not apply** to diesel fuel in the following cases:

1. **In Transit**  
   - Fuel moving from outside Canada to outside Canada, with written evidence of transit.

2. **For Export**  
   - Fuel produced or sold for export, with proof of export intent.

3. **Imported for Specific Uses**  
   - Fuel imported for use in off-road engines, small/large stationary engines, or conveyances (land, water, air), **if**:
     - Sulphur concentration exceeds the limit in section 3,
     - And the fuel will meet the requirements **before use or sale**,
     - And is accompanied by written evidence.

4. **Scientific Research**  
   - The regulations **do not apply** to diesel fuel used in scientific research (except for section 1, which sets general limits).

5. **North of Latitude 81Â°N**  
   - Paragraphs (1)(f) and (g) (sulphur limits) **do not apply** for diesel fuel used **north of 81Â°N**.

## ğŸ“ **Measurement Method**

- Sulphur concentration must be measured using **ASTM D5453-16** (Ultraviolet Fluorescence method).
- For **reporting purposes**, an **equivalent method** may be used **only if**:
  - The method is described in writing to the Minister,
  - It is validated against ASTM D6708-16 (statistical validation of method equivalence),
  - And the results are proven equivalent to ASTM D5453-16.

> âš ï¸ **Important**: The equivalent method is **only for reporting** â€” it **cannot** be used to verify compliance with sulphur limits.

## ğŸ“ **Reporting and Recordkeeping Requirements**

### 1. **Annual Reports (for Producers & Importers)**  
- Must be submitted to the Minister **within 45 days after the end of each calendar year**.
- Reports must include:
  - Name and address of producer/importer.
  - Name and address of each facility where fuel is produced or imported.
  - Volume of diesel fuel and sulphur concentration for each facility and province.

### 2. **Method for Calculating Sulphur Concentration**  
- Use of **ASTM D5453-16** or an **equivalent method** validated per ASTM D6708-16.
- Equivalent method is **only for reporting**; not for compliance.

### 3. **Pre-Production/Import Notification (Schedule 2)**  
- Any person intending to **produce or import diesel fuel** must submit a report to the Minister **no later than 5 days before first production/import**.
- The report includes:
  - Intended use (e.g., on-road, off-road, research).
  - Facility details.
  - Volume estimates.

### 4. **Change Notification**  
- If changes occur (excluding annual volume or authorized official), notify the Minister **within 5 days**.

### 5. **Update Requirement (Post-2017)**  
- If a person previously submitted a report before the amendment came into force, they must submit a **new report within 60 days** if the fuel is intended for:
  - Vessel engines (any type)
  - Small or large stationary engines
  - Scientific research

> âš ï¸ This update was introduced to ensure compliance with the 2014 sulphur reduction timeline.
