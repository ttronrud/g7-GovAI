### **Regulatory Summary**

**Regulation:**  
*Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, under paragraph 2(a)(a) of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act, transfers from the Minister of Indigenous Services to the Minister of Industry the powers, duties and functions of the Minister of Indigenous Services with respect to the Federal Economic Development Agency for Northern Ontario.*

**Statutory Citation:**  
S.C. 2003, c. 22, s. 207  
Relevant Statute: *R.S., c. P-34* (Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act)

1. **Authority for Transfer**  
   The transfer is authorized by **paragraph 2(a)(a)** of the *Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act*. This provision allows the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister) to reassign powers, duties, and functions between federal ministers, provided it serves the public interest and is consistent with the structure and purpose of the federal public service.

2. **Parties Involved**

   - **Receiving Minister:** Minister of Industry  
   - The transfer specifically concerns **functions related to the Federal Economic Development Agency for Northern Ontario (FedDev Ontario)**.

   The transfer includes **all powers, duties, and functions** of the Minister of Indigenous Services **with respect to FedDev Ontario**.

   - Policy direction and oversight of FedDev Ontario’s operations
   - Strategic planning and funding decisions affecting the agency
   - Interdepartmental coordination involving FedDev Ontario
   - Any administrative or financial responsibilities previously held by the Minister of Indigenous Services

   The **Federal Economic Development Agency for Northern Ontario (FedDev Ontario)** is a federal agency established to promote economic development in Northern Ontario. It provides funding, technical support, and infrastructure development to regional businesses and communities. The agency operates in partnership with provincial governments and Indigenous communities.

6. **Legal and Procedural Validity**
