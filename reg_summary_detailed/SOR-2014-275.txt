## ğŸ” Key Definitions (Core Terms)

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Grade Crossing** | A road crossing at grade (same level) with railway tracks, or multiple road crossings where railway lines are within 30 meters of each other. |
| **Public Grade Crossing** | A crossing where the road is opened or maintained by a **road authority** and is designed for public use. |
| **Private Grade Crossing** | A crossing not operated or maintained by a public road authority. May be owned or managed by a private entity (e.g., a company or property owner). |
| **Existing Grade Crossing** | A crossing where actual construction began **before November 28, 2014**. |
| **New Grade Crossing** | A crossing where construction begins **on or after November 28, 2014**. |
| **Average Annual Daily Railway Movements (AADRM)** | Total railway movements per year divided by 365 days. Measures rail traffic volume. |
| **Average Annual Daily Traffic (AADT)** | Total motor vehicle crossings per year divided by 365 days. Measures road traffic volume. |
| **Cross-Product** | Product of AADRM Ã— AADT. Used to assess risk and determine required safety measures. |
| **Design Vehicle** | A standardized vehicle (from the *Geometric Design Guide for Canadian Roads*, 1999) used to determine road and crossing design parameters. |
| **Railway Design Speed** | Speed at which railway equipment is designed to operate (e.g., 97 km/h for passenger trains). |
| **Road Crossing Design Speed** | Speed at which motor vehicles are expected to travel across the crossing (based on road design). |
| **Stopping Sight Distance (SSD)** | Distance a driver needs to see ahead to stop safely, calculated per Grade Crossings Standards. |
| **Storage Distance (D)** | Shortest distance between the nearest rail and the edge of the intersecting road, measured along the centerline. |
| **Sightlines** | Visibility lines required for drivers to see approaching trains and warning signs. Must remain unobstructed. |
| **Traffic Control Devices** | Signs and systems including: Stop sign, Stop Ahead sign, Railway Crossing Ahead sign, Advisory Speed Tab, Prepare to Stop sign, and interconnected traffic signals. |
| **Warning System** | Automated system (not an interconnected signal) that alerts road users to approaching trains via lights, bells, gates, or mechanisms. |

> **Note**: Two adjacent roads crossing the same railway lines are treated as **separate grade crossings**.
