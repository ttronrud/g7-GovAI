**Detailed Summary of the Canadian Industrial Hemp Promotion-Research Agency Proclamation (P.C. 2024-1147)**

---

### **Overview**

This regulation, formally known as the **Canadian Industrial Hemp Promotion-Research Agency Proclamation**, was issued on **November 7, 2024**, by the Governor in Council of Canada, acting on the advice of the Privy Council. It establishes the **Canadian Industrial Hemp Promotion-Research Agency (CIHPRA)** under the authority of **section 39(1)** of the *Farm Products Agencies Act*.

The proclamation is a formal legislative instrument that creates a new government-backed agency to promote and conduct research on **industrial hemp products** in Canada. It outlines the structure, governance, mandate, financial mechanisms, and accountability framework of the agency.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Legal Basis and Purpose**

- **Legal Authority**: The creation of the Agency is authorized under **section 39(1)** of the *Farm Products Agencies Act*, which allows the Governor in Council to establish a promotion and research agency for one or more farm products if a majority of producers (and importers, where applicable) support such an agency.
  
- **Support Requirement**: The Governor in Council has determined that a **majority of Canadian producers and importers of industrial hemp products** support the establishment of this agency.

- **Objective**: The Agency’s primary purpose is to:
  - Promote the **marketing and production** of industrial hemp products and derivatives.
  - Conduct and promote **research** related to those products.
  - Support the growth of the industrial hemp sector through **interprovincial trade, exports, and imports**.

---

### **2. Name and Legal Citation**

- The agency is officially named the **Canadian Industrial Hemp Promotion-Research Agency (CIHPRA)**.
- The proclamation may be cited as the **"Canadian Industrial Hemp Promotion-Research Agency Proclamation"**.

---

### **3. Composition and Governance of the Agency**

The Agency consists of **nine members**, elected or appointed through a structured process:

#### **Election Process (by Province and Sector):**

| Member Type | Election Criteria |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Six members** | One per province: Ontario, Quebec, Manitoba, British Columbia, Saskatchewan, Alberta. Elected by producers who: <br> - Have notified the Agency of candidacy <br> - Hold a valid licence under the *Industrial Hemp Regulations* for cultivating industrial hemp <br> - Have cultivated industrial hemp in the **current year or the two preceding years** in that province. |
| **One member** | Represents producers in the **Atlantic provinces** (Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, Newfoundland and Labrador). Elected by producers from any of these provinces who meet the same eligibility criteria (candidacy, licence, cultivation in the past three years). |
| **One member** | Represents **industrial hemp importers**. Elected by importers who have imported industrial hemp products or derivatives in the current or previous two years. |
| **One member** | Elected from among members elected under (a) to (c) who are involved in the **supply chain** (e.g., processors, distributors, marketers). |

#### **Appointment in Case of Vacancy**

- If no candidate is nominated for any election, the Agency may **appoint** a member to fill the vacancy.

#### **Term of Office**

- Each member serves a **two-year term**, beginning on the **last day of the annual general meeting** at which they are elected or appointed.

#### **Chair and Vice-Chair**

- At the first meeting and at the first meeting after each annual general meeting, members elect a **Chair** and a **Vice-Chair** from among themselves.
- If either the Chair or Vice-Chair is unable to serve or resigns, the remaining members must **re-elect** a new Chair or Vice-Chair for the remainder of the term.

#### **Head Office Location**

- The **head office** of the Agency is located in **Calgary, Alberta**.

---

### **4. Mandate and Powers**

The Agency is empowered to:

1. **Promote marketing and production** of industrial hemp products and derivatives, including:
   - Selling, buying, pricing, packaging, transporting, storing, and preparing products for sale.
   - Facilitating **interprovincial trade**, **export**, and **import** activities.

2. **Conduct and promote research** on industrial hemp products and derivatives, including:
   - Scientific studies, innovation, and development of new applications (e.g., textiles, construction materials, bioplastics, health products).

---

### **5. Budget, Business Program, and Oversight**

- The Agency must submit **annually** to the **Governor in Council (via the Council)**:
  - A **budget** outlining projected costs for 12 months.
  - A **business program** detailing:
    - The proposed activities and business operations.
    - All relevant information to assess whether:
      - The activities align with **section 7** of the *Farm Products Agencies Act* (which governs public interest and economic development).
      - The activities are consistent with the **Agency’s stated object** under **section 41** of the Act (which emphasizes promoting the development and use of industrial hemp).
      - Any **existing or proposed orders** (e.g., levies) are necessary for the implementation or administration of the plan.

> This ensures **transparency and accountability** to the federal government.

---

### **6. Levies and Charges (Funding Mechanism)**

To fund its operations, the Agency may impose **levies or charges** on:

- **Marketers** involved in:
  - Interprovincial trade
  - Export trade
- **Importers** of industrial hemp products or derivatives.

#### Key Features of Levies:

- **Classification**: The Agency may group persons into **classes** (e.g., by volume, type of product, region) and set **different levies** for each class.
- **Collection**: The Agency may appoint a person (e.g., a collector or agent) to collect the levies on its behalf.
- **Payment Deadlines**: Levies are due within 30 days of being assessed. **Unpaid levies become a debt** owed to the Agency.
- **Funding Requirement**: Levies must be set at levels that ensure the Agency receives **sufficient returns** to cover its **estimated administrative and operational costs** each year.

> These levies are not a tax but a **user fee** tied to market activity.

#### Financial Accountability:

- All funds collected from **importers** must be **retained in a separate, dedicated account** to ensure transparency and prevent misuse.

---

### **7. Cooperation and Collaboration**

- The Agency is required to take **all reasonable steps** to:
  - Foster **cooperation among its members**.
  - Encourage collaboration between producers, importers, and supply chain participants.

> This promotes a unified and efficient industry ecosystem.

---

### **8. Review and Evaluation of the Plan**

To ensure ongoing effectiveness and relevance, the Agency must:

- Hold a **review meeting within five years** of the Proclamation coming into force.
- Hold **subsequent reviews every five years** thereafter.

#### Review Process:

- Within **three months** of each review meeting, the Agency must file a **written report** with the Council that includes:
  - An assessment of the **terms and effectiveness** of the promotion and research plan.
  - **Recommendations** for any modifications to the plan to better achieve the Agency’s objectives (as defined in section 41 of the *Farm Products Agencies Act*).

> This ensures the plan evolves with market needs and technological advances.

---

### **9. Limitations and Exclusions**

- **No Impact on Existing Powers**: Nothing in this proclamation affects the powers already vested in the Agency under **section 42** of the *Farm Products Agencies Act*.  
  (This means the Agency retains its core statutory powers even if not explicitly listed here.)

---

### **10. Definitions (Key Terms in the Schedule)**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Farm Products Agencies Act*. |
| **Agency** | The Canadian Industrial Hemp Promotion-Research Agency established by this proclamation. |
| **Derivative** | As defined in subsection 2(3) of the *Industrial Hemp Regulations* (e.g., hemp-based plastics, textiles, food products). |
| **Industrial Hemp** | As defined in subsection 1(2) of the *Industrial Hemp Regulations* (cannabis with less than 0.3% THC). |
| **Industrial Hemp Product** | Industrial hemp plus any product listed in **Schedule 2 of the Cannabis Act** (e.g., fiber, seed, oil, bioplastics). |
| **Marketing** | Includes selling, buying, pricing, packaging, transporting, storing, and preparing products for sale. |
| **Plan** | The promotion and research plan set out in Part 2 of the schedule. |

---

## **Significance and Implications**

### ✅ **Positive Outcomes**
- Creates a **dedicated, independent body** to support the growth of Canada’s industrial hemp sector.
- Provides **funding through targeted levies**, ensuring sustainability without relying on general government funds.
- Encourages **research and innovation**, helping Canada compete globally in sustainable, high-value agricultural products.
- Promotes **interprovincial and international trade** through coordinated marketing efforts.
- Includes **diverse representation** (producers, importers, supply chain actors) to ensure inclusivity and legitimacy.
