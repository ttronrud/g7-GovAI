**Detailed Summary of the Criminal Proceedings Rules of the Manitoba Court of Queen’s Bench (2016)**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Purpose**

The *Criminal Proceedings Rules of the Manitoba Court of Queen’s Bench* are enacted under **subsection 482(1)a and (3)b** and **section 482.1c** of the *Criminal Code* (R.S.M. 2000, c. C-46), and were officially adopted on **June 7, 2016**. These rules are binding and apply to all criminal proceedings within the jurisdiction of the Manitoba Court of Queen’s Bench.

#### **Key Objectives:**
- To ensure the **just determination** of every criminal proceeding.
- To promote **simplicity, fairness, and efficiency** in court administration.
- To eliminate **unjustifiable expense and delay**.
- To ensure procedural consistency and access to justice.

> The rules must be interpreted in a way that supports these goals. If not explicitly provided, practice is to be determined by **analogy** to the rules.

---

### **2. Definitions**

The rules define key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Appeal** | An appeal from a decision of a summary conviction court under Part XXVII of the Code. |
| **Assignment Court** | A sitting of the Court when trial and pre-trial proceedings are scheduled. |
| **Charter** | The *Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms*. |
| **Code** | The *Criminal Code*. |
| **Counsel** | The counsel of record or counsel specifically authorized to act on behalf of the counsel of record. |
| **Court** | The Court of Queen’s Bench of Manitoba. |
| **Holiday** | Any day the Court office is closed. |
| **Judge** | A judge of the Court. |
| **Pre-trial Conference** | Same as a pre-hearing conference under section 625.1 of the Code. |
| **Proceeding** | Includes trials, applications, appeals, or any process arising from or incidental to prosecution of an offence under the Code. |
| **Summary Conviction Appeals Assignment Court** | A sitting of the Court when summary conviction appeals are scheduled. |

### **3. General Principles and Interpretation**

- **Interpretation of Rules**: The interpretation sections of the *Criminal Code* apply unless otherwise stated.
- **Judicial Discretion**: A judge may **dispense with compliance** with any rule if it is necessary in the **interests of justice**.
- **Non-Compliance with Filing Rules**:
  - If a motion or application fails to comply with filing requirements, it **will not be heard** unless the presiding judge grants **leave** after considering:
    - Nature and reason for non-compliance;
    - Explanation provided;
    - Merits of the application;
    - Notice given to other parties;
    - History of proceedings and need for expeditious resolution.
- **Costs for Non-Compliance**:
  - Counsel who fail to comply without **reasonable excuse** may be ordered to pay **personal costs**.
  - These costs are payable to the **Minister of Finance, Manitoba**.
- **Compliance Obligations**:
  - Counsel for the accused and the prosecutor are **deemed to comply** with all time limits set by a pre-trial, case management, or other judge.
- **Time Limit Calculations**:
  - **Clear days**: Both the start and end days are **excluded** (e.g., a 5-day period excludes both day 1 and day 6).
  - **Less than 7 days**: Holidays during that period are **excluded**.
  - **Time expires on a holiday**: The act may be completed on the **next non-holiday**.
- **Extension of Time Limits**:
  - The Court may **extend or abridge** time limits by order, on just terms.
  - An application for extension may be made **before or after** the deadline.
- **Venue Rules**:
  - A proceeding must be **commenced** in the **judicial centre nearest** to where the offence occurred.
  - **Accessibility** is a key factor in determining proximity.
  - Proceedings are generally heard in the **original judicial centre**, unless transferred by order.
  - **Change of venue** is governed by the Code’s change-of-venue provisions.
  - Failure to follow venue rules does **not invalidate** the proceeding, but the Court may **transfer** it or make other orders as it deems just.
- **Filing Requirements**:
  - All documents must be filed at the **court office in the judicial centre where the proceeding began**, unless filed during a hearing.
  - Documents may be filed by:
    - Leaving them in the office; or
    - Mailing them with a prescribed fee.
  - A **mail-filing** is deemed filed on the **date of the filing stamp**.
- **Registrar’s Refusal to Accept Documents**:
  - A document may be refused if:
    - It does not comply with format;
    - It is illegible; or
    - It is not securely bound.
  - Refusal is **final unless directed by a judge**.

### **4. Document Formatting and Structure**

All documents must meet strict formatting standards to ensure consistency and readability.

#### **General Requirements:**
- **Paper**: 216 mm × 279 mm (A4 size), white, good quality.
- **Text**:
  - Double-spaced, **except for quotations**, which are single-spaced.
  - Margins: **~25 mm** on all sides.
  - **Only one side** printed, except for **books of authorities**, which must be **double-sided**.

#### **Covering Page (Form 1)**:
Required for every document and must include:
- Title of the proceeding;
- Name of the court, judicial centre, and file number;
- For affidavits: deponent’s name and date sworn;
- Title of the document;
- Filer’s name, address, email, and phone/fax numbers.

#### **Covering for Briefs, Factums, and Books of Authorities**:
- **Applicant/Appellant**: Blue cover stock (front and back).
- **Respondent**: Beige cover stock (front and back).
- **Intervener**: Grey cover stock (front and back).

#### **Motion or Application Brief** (must be in consecutively numbered paragraphs):
- **Part 1**: Introduction/overview.
- **Part 2**: Concise statement of facts.
- **Part 3**: List of issues.
- **Part 4**: Argument and relief requested.
- **Part 5**: List of authorities.
- **Schedule A**: Text of relevant legislation.

> **Limit**: Brief (excluding Schedule A) must not exceed **30 pages** unless directed otherwise.

#### **Summary Conviction Appeal Factum**:
- Same structure as above, with additional parts:
  - **Part 4**: Standard of review.
  - **Part 5**: Argument and relief.
  - **Part 6**: List of authorities.
- Applies to **subrules 5.07(2) and (3)**.

#### **Books of Authorities**:
- Must include:
  - An index.
  - Authorities relied on (with headnotes if applicable), **highlighted or sidebarred**.
- Authorities must be:
  - Separated by **numbered tabs**.
  - Printed on **both sides** of each page.
- **No duplication** of authorities already filed by another party.
- **No inclusion** of cases that establish well-known legal principles unless there is something **specifically relevant** to the proceeding.

---

### **5. Orders and Proceedings**

- All orders must be in **Form 2 of the schedule** and include:
  - Name of the judge;
  - Date of the order;
  - Recital of particulars: hearing date, parties present, those absent, and any undertakings made.

---

### **6. Confidentiality and “B” Files**

- Documents filed during **resolution, pre-trial, or case management conferences**, and their **transcripts**, are **confidential** and **not part of the public record**.
- These are stored in a separate file known as a **“B file”**.
- **Access Rights**:
  - The prosecutor, counsel for each accused, and any **self-represented accused** have the right to access the B file.

---

### **7. Service of Documents**

#### **Personal Service (Required in Certain Cases)**:
- Required when:
  - The document is served on a **non-party** (e.g., a motion to the prosecutor).
  - A party has **no counsel** (self-represented).
- **Service Methods**:
  - **Individual**: Give a copy directly.
  - **Corporation**: Give to an officer, director, agent, or person in charge.
  - **Judge of Provincial Court or Justice of Peace**: Serve the court administrator in the region.
  - **Attorney General of Canada**: Leave at Winnipeg regional office.
  - **Attorney General of Manitoba**: Leave at the prosecutor’s office in the judicial centre.

> The person effecting service is **not required to have the original**.

#### **Alternative Service (When Personal Service is Not Feasible)**:
- If prompt personal service is **impractical**, the Court may issue an **order for substituted service** or **dispense with service** if in the interests of justice.
- In substituted service, the Court must specify **when service becomes effective**.
