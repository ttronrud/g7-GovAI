**Detailed Summary of the British Columbia Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations (2004)**

---

### **Overview**

The *British Columbia Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations*, made on **December 15, 2004**, pursuant to **subsection 18(1) of the Sex Offender Information Registration Act (SOIRA)**, S.C. 2004, c. 10, establish the administrative framework for the registration, reporting, and management of information related to sex offenders within the Province of British Columbia.

These regulations define key terms, outline who is authorized to collect and register information, specify the designated registration centres, and set the effective date of the regulations. The primary goal is to ensure public safety by enabling timely and accurate reporting of sex offender information to a centralized authority.

---

### **Key Provisions and Detailed Explanation**

#### **1. Applicability and Legal Authority**
- The regulations are issued by the **Lieutenant Governor in Council** (i.e., the provincial government acting on behalf of the Crown).
- They are enacted under **subsection 18(1) of the Sex Offender Information Registration Act (SOIRA)**, which empowers the government to make regulations necessary to implement the Act.
- The Act itself was passed in 2004 as **Chapter 10 of the Statutes of Canada, 2004**, and these regulations provide the operational details for its implementation in British Columbia.

---

#### **2. Definitions**

The regulations begin with a clear set of definitions to ensure consistency and understanding:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Sex Offender Information Registration Act*, S.C. 2004, c. 10. |
| **BCSOIRC** | Stands for the *British Columbia Sex Offender Information Registration Centre*, which is administered by the **RCMP "E" Division**. This is the central body responsible for receiving, storing, and managing sex offender registration data. |
| **RCMP** | The *Royal Canadian Mounted Police*, the national police force responsible for law enforcement in Canada, including in British Columbia. |

> These definitions clarify the legal and institutional structure of the system, ensuring accountability and jurisdictional clarity.

---

#### **3. Who May Report or Provide Information**

**Eligible individuals or entities** who may report sex offender information to the BCSOIRC include:

- **Sex offenders whose primary residence is in British Columbia** may report under any of the following:
  - Paragraphs **4.1(1)(a) to (b.2)** of the Act (which cover various types of reporting obligations, such as initial registration, changes in address, or new offences).
  - **Section 4.3** of the Act (which allows for reporting of certain offences or changes in status).
  - **Section 6 of the Act**, which permits **telephone notification** of relevant information to the BCSOIRC.

> This provision ensures that individuals living in BC can comply with reporting requirements, even if they are not formally required to register through a formal process.

---

#### **4. Authorized Persons to Collect and Register Information**

The regulations identify the **authorized persons and entities** in British Columbia who are legally permitted to collect and register information under the Act:

1. **RCMP Members or Reservists**  
   - Any active or reserve member of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police is authorized to collect and register information related to sex offenders.

2. **Provincial, Municipal, or Designated Constables**  
   - As defined under **Section 1 of the Police Act (Chapter 367 of the Revised Statutes of British Columbia, 1996, as amended)**, these include:
     - Provincial constables
     - Municipal constables
     - Designated constables  
   - These officers have the legal authority to gather and register information in accordance with the Act.

3. **Special Municipal Constables**  
   - A special municipal constable who:
     - Was previously a member of the RCMP, or
     - Was previously a constable referred to in paragraph (b) above  
   - Is authorized to collect and register information.

4. **Employees of Registration Centres**  
   - Any person employed at a **registration centre** whose duties include **the registration of sex offender information** is authorized to register such information.

> This broad authorization ensures that law enforcement and public safety personnel across different levels of government can act promptly and legally when sex offender information comes to light.

---

#### **5. Designated Registration Centres**

- The **Schedule** to the regulations (not included in the excerpt) lists the **specific locations** designated as official **registration centres** in British Columbia.
- Each of these centres is authorized to receive and process reports and notifications under the Act.

> This ensures that all sex offender information is centrally managed and accessible to relevant authorities, regardless of where the incident or individual is located.

---

#### **6. Area of Service**

- The **designated area of service** for each registration centre is the **entire Province of British Columbia**.
- This means that **any person or agency in BC** can report information to any of the designated centres, and the information will be accepted and processed without geographic limitations.

> This provision enhances the reach and efficiency of the reporting system, promoting uniformity and accessibility across the province.

---

#### **7. Effective Date**

- These regulations come into force on the **later of two dates**:
  1. The date on which the *Sex Offender Information Registration Act* (SOIRA) comes into force.
  2. The date on which these regulations are officially registered in the *Canada Gazette* or published in the provincial register.

> This ensures that the regulations only take effect once both the enabling legislation and the detailed rules are in place and legally recognized.

> **Note**: The regulations are noted as coming into force on **December 21, 2004**, which is the later of the two dates mentioned.

---

### **Purpose and Public Safety Implications**

The overarching purpose of these regulations is to:
- Improve the **timeliness and accuracy** of sex offender information collection.
- Ensure that **public safety is protected** through a coordinated, accessible, and legally sound reporting mechanism.
- Provide a **clear framework** for law enforcement and other authorized personnel to act in accordance with the law.
- Support the **centralized management** of sex offender data through the BCSOIRC, which helps in monitoring, tracking, and public notification where appropriate.

---

### **Key Takeaways**

| Feature | Summary |
|--------|--------|
| **Authority** | Issued by the Lieutenant Governor in Council under SOIRA, s. 18(1) |
| **Central Body** | BCSOIRC (administered by RCMP "E" Division) |
| **Reporting Eligibility** | Sex offenders residing in BC may report under specific Act provisions or by phone |
| **Authorized Collectors** | RCMP members, provincial/municipal constables, special municipal constables, and staff at registration centres |
| **Registration Centres** | Designated locations across BC (as per Schedule) |
| **Service Area** | Entire Province of British Columbia |
| **Effective Date** | December 21, 2004 (later of SOIRA’s coming into force and registration date) |

---

### **Conclusion**

The *British Columbia Sex Offender Information Registration Regulations* establish a clear, accessible, and legally robust system for the collection, registration, and management of sex offender information. By defining key roles, authorizing a wide range of public safety personnel, and ensuring nationwide coverage within BC, the regulations support public safety while maintaining legal compliance and operational efficiency.

These regulations remain a foundational component of BC’s sex offender monitoring framework and continue to inform policy and practice in the area of public safety and criminal justice.
