## üîç **Regulation Summary: Retirement Compensation Arrangements Regulations, No. 2**

### **1. Purpose and Context**
These regulations establish a **special early retirement compensation arrangement** for certain public servants who were made surplus due to workforce reductions or position abolitions between **April 1, 1995, and March 31, 1998**. The goal is to provide financial support to eligible employees who are no longer employed in the Public Service, particularly those who would otherwise have lost pension benefits due to early departure.

The regulations were introduced in response to significant workforce restructuring initiatives, including the **Work Force Adjustment Directive (1991)** and the **Executive Employment Transition Policy (1992)**, and aim to ensure that affected employees receive fair compensation based on their pensionable service and contributions.

## üìÖ Eligibility Criteria

### ‚úÖ **Eligible Groups**

#### **Group A: Surplus Employees (Part I of Schedule)**
- Must have been employed in a portion of the Public Service listed in **Part I of the Schedule**.
- Must have received a **notice of surplus status** between **April 1, 1995, and March 31, 1998**.
- Must have met one of the following conditions **on April 1, 1995**:
  - Was required to contribute to the Superannuation Account (subsection 5(1) of PSA),
  - Contributed to the Superannuation Account via election (section 5.2 of PSA), or
  - Was on leave of absence without pay and could have made or had made an election under subsection 5.3(1) of PSA.

#### **Group B: Surplus Employees (Part II of Schedule)**
- Must have been employed in a portion of the Public Service listed in **Part II of the Schedule**.
- Must have received a notice of surplus status **on or after the day that the portion was added to Part II**, up to **March 31, 1998**.
- Must have met one of the same three conditions as above **on the day the portion was added to Part II**.

#### **Group C: Governor in Council Appointees**
- Must have been appointed to a position that was **abolished during the relevant period**.
- Must have been employed in the Public Service on the day of abolition.
- Must meet the same eligibility conditions as above (contribution or election status).

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exclusions from Surplus Status:**
> - Employees who are transferred to prevent surplus status (only if both transfers are effective on the same day and pay difference ‚â§ 4%).
> - Employees under EETP who are not eligible due to failing to meet new Treasury Board conditions (August 15, 1995).
> - Employees who receive a reasonable job offer (under WFAD) or an alternative employment offer (under EETP).
> - Employees who receive early departure incentives or negotiated settlements.

### **For Surplus Employees (Groups A & B)**

| Condition | Details |
|--------|--------|
| **1. Election** | Must elect to receive the annual benefit **within 60 days** of receiving the first notice of surplus status. |
| **2. Employment Termination** | Must cease employment **within 6 months** of the first notice, or within a longer period determined by the deputy head (to meet operational needs). |
| **3. No Alternative Employment** | Must not have received: <br> ‚Ä¢ A reasonable job offer (under WFAD), or <br> ‚Ä¢ An offer of alternative employment (under EETP), or <br> ‚Ä¢ An offer of contiguous employment from an external employer due to transfer of function. |

| **5. Pension Service Requirements** | At time of termination, must have: <br> ‚Ä¢ Been employed full-time (as defined in clause 13(1)(c)(ii)(C) of PSA) for **at least 10 years total**, <br> ‚Ä¢ Be **aged between 50 and 55 years**, and <br> ‚Ä¢ Have exercised the option to receive an annual allowance under clauses 13(1)(c)(ii)(B) or (D) of PSA. |

> üîÅ **For Repeated Surplus Status:** If a person receives another notice of surplus status during the period, the 6-month window starts from the **date of the subsequent notice**.

### **For Governor in Council Appointees**

| Condition | Details |
|--------|--------|
| **1. Termination Deadline** | Must cease employment **within 6 months** of the position being abolished. |
| **2. Service & Age Requirements** | Must have: <br> ‚Ä¢ Been employed full-time for **at least 10 years**, <br> ‚Ä¢ Be **aged between 50 and 55 years** at termination. |
| **3. Election** | Must elect to receive the benefit **within 30 days of ceasing employment**. |
| **4. Annual Allowance Option** | Must exercise the option to receive an annual allowance under clauses 13(1)(c)(ii)(B) or (D) of PSA **within 30 days of ceasing employment**. |
| **5. No Lump Sum Settlement** | Must not have received a lump-sum termination settlement. |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note:** This group has a shorter time frame (6 months) and a shorter election window (30 days) compared to surplus employees.

## üí∞ Structure of the Annual Benefit

The annual benefit is calculated as follows:

> **Annual Benefit = [Annuity payable under PSA para. 11(1)(a) on full pensionable service] ‚Äì [Annual allowance under clause 13(1)(c)(ii)(B) or (D)] ‚Äì [Any benefit under Regulation No. 1, section 35]**

- The annuity amount is based on what would have been paid if the employee had retired under normal pension rules.
- The benefit is **not a pension**, but a **compensation for early retirement**.
- It is **equal in nature and subject to the same conditions** as an annual allowance under the PSA.

## üîÑ Special Provisions

| Provision | Explanation |
|--------|------------|
| **Spousal Reduction** | If a participant makes an election under section 13.1 of the PSA (spouse joining after termination), the annual benefit is **reduced by the same proportion and for the same period** as the annual allowance is reduced under section 68 of the PSA regulations. |
| **Surviving Spouse Benefit** | If the surviving spouse is entitled to an immediate annual allowance under section 13.1(3) of the PSA, they receive **a benefit equal to the annual benefit amount multiplied by the percentage of the participant‚Äôs original allowance** (before any reduction under section 68). |
| **Re-employment** | The annual benefit **ends immediately** when the participant is re-employed in the Public Service and becomes a contributor under section 64 of the PSA. |
| **No Reinstatement** | Once the benefit ends due to re-employment, it **does not resume** if the person later becomes unemployed again. |
| **Immediate Annuity** | If a participant receives an immediate annuity under subparagraph 13(1)(d)(ii) of the PSA, they **cease to receive the annual benefit**. |
