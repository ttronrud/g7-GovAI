**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Designation of the Immigration Consultants of Canada Regulatory Council (ICCRC) under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)**

---

### **Regulatory Context and Legal Authority**

This regulation was issued by the **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration** under two specific provisions of the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)*:

- **Subsection 91(5)a**: Grants the Minister the authority to designate a body to act as a regulatory entity for immigration consultants.
- **Subsection 91(7)a**: Provides the power to designate a body whose members in good standing may represent or advise individuals in connection with immigration or refugee proceedings.

The regulation serves to formally **designate the Immigration Consultants of Canada Regulatory Council (ICCRC)** as the official body under paragraph **91(2)(c)** of the IRPA. This paragraph allows individuals to be represented or advised by members of a designated body in matters related to immigration or refugee applications.

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**

The primary objective of these regulations is to:

- Establish a **recognized, credible, and regulated body** through which qualified immigration consultants can represent or advise individuals before immigration authorities.
- Ensure that only **professionally qualified and properly licensed consultants** are authorized to provide representation or advice in immigration and refugee matters.
- Provide a **smooth transition** from the previous regulatory framework (the Canadian Society of Immigration Consultants â€“ CSIC) to the new ICCRC, ensuring continuity of service and legal validity for existing members.

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Act**  
   Refers to the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act* (IRPA), the primary legislation governing immigration and refugee decisions in Canada.

2. **ICCRC (Immigration Consultants of Canada Regulatory Council)**  
   A newly incorporated regulatory body established under **Part II of the Canada Corporations Act**, officially incorporated on **February 18, 2011**. It is the successor to the Canadian Society of Immigration Consultants (CSIC), which was incorporated on October 8, 2003.

---

### **Designation of the ICCRC**

Under the regulation, the ICCRC is **designated as the body** whose members in good standing may:

- Represent or advise a person  
- Offer to represent or advise a person  
- In connection with any **proceeding or application under the IRPA**, such as:
  - Applications for permanent residence
  - Refugee claims
  - Citizenship applications
  - Visas and work permits

This designation ensures that individuals seeking legal representation in immigration matters can rely on consultants who are members of a recognized, regulated body.

---

### **Automatic Transition of Members from CSIC to ICCRC**

A critical transitional provision allows for the **automatic transfer of membership** from the **Canadian Society of Immigration Consultants (CSIC)** to the ICCRC.

#### Key Provisions:
- Any person who was a **member in good standing** of the CSIC **on the date these regulations come into force** is automatically considered a member of the ICCRC **for the purposes of the IRPA**.
- This automatic status lasts until the **earlier of two dates**:
  1. **120 days** after the regulations come into force
  2. The date on which the individual **discontinues their membership in the ICCRC**

> This transitional period ensures that existing consultants are not suddenly excluded from representing clients during a critical transition phase.

#### Membership Fee Waiver:
- During the transitional period (as defined above), members who are transferred from CSIC to ICCRC are **not required to pay membership fees** to the ICCRC.
- This provision supports continuity and avoids financial barriers for consultants during the transition.

---

### **Effective Date and Registration Requirements**

- The regulations **come into force on the day that Section 1 of the *Act to amend the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act*, Chapter 8 of the Statutes of Canada, 2011, comes into force**.
- If the regulations are **registered later than that date**, they will come into force on the **date of registration**.

> This ensures that the regulation is implemented in a timely and legally sound manner, with flexibility for administrative processing.

---

### **Legal Significance and Implications**

1. **Regulatory Oversight**  
   The ICCRC now acts as the official regulatory body for immigration consultants in Canada. This enhances accountability, professional standards, and public trust in the immigration advisory process.

2. **Legal Validity of Representation**  
   Only members of the ICCRC in good standing can legally represent or advise individuals in immigration or refugee proceedings. This prevents unqualified or unlicensed individuals from offering professional services.

3. **Professional Standards and Accountability**  
   The ICCRC is expected to establish and enforce professional standards, including continuing education, ethical conduct, and disciplinary procedures, to maintain the integrity of the profession.

4. **Continuity of Service**  
   The transitional clause ensures that existing consultants can continue to serve clients without disruption, preserving access to legal representation for applicants.

---

### **Reference to Prior Statutory Instruments**

- **S.C. 2011, c. 8, s. 1**: The enabling provision under which the Minister issued the regulation.
- **S.C. 2001, c. 27**: Refers to the original IRPA amendments that laid the groundwork for regulatory designation.
- **Note**: The regulation is listed as **SI/2011-57**, effective June 30, 2011, and is referenced in official publications.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation marks a significant step in the **professionalization and regulation of immigration consultancy** in Canada. By formally designating the **Immigration Consultants of Canada Regulatory Council (ICCRC)** as the authorized body for representation in immigration and refugee matters, the government strengthens the integrity of the immigration system and ensures that individuals receive advice from qualified, accountable professionals.

The transitional provisions ensure a **smooth, equitable, and practical shift** from the previous CSIC framework to the new ICCRC structure, protecting both consultants and applicants during the changeover.

---

**In essence**:  
This regulation establishes the ICCRC as the official regulatory body for immigration consultants under the IRPA, with a clear pathway for existing members to transition into the new system without financial or legal barriers, ensuring professional standards, accountability, and continuity in immigration advisory services.
