### âš ï¸ **Exemptions and Scope Limitations**

These regulations **do not apply** in the following situations:

1. **Transfer to Another Siding After Interswitching**:  
   If a terminal carrier has already interswitched traffic to a siding under these rules, and then **transfers the traffic to a different siding at the request of a shipper or another railway company**, the regulation does **not apply** to that second transfer.

2. **Railway Companies with High Interswitching Revenue**:  
   If a railway company derived **at least 90% of its gross freight revenues from interswitching** in the **immediately preceding three-year period**, based on company returns filed under Section 50 of the Act, then these regulations **do not apply** to that company.

> This exemption recognizes that companies heavily involved in interswitching may operate under different financial and operational models and thus require different regulatory treatment.

A terminal carrier must provide **the same level of service** to interswitched traffic as it provides to its own **line haul traffic** (i.e., long-distance freight movement).

> This ensures **fairness and consistency**â€”interswitched traffic is not treated as second-class or inferior in terms of reliability, timeliness, or availability.

### ðŸšš **Empty Car Handling (No Charges)**

- A railway company that has a car **interswitched** by a terminal carrier must **deliver an empty car** back to the terminal carrier at the interchange for delivery to a siding for loading.
- **No charge** shall be made by the terminal carrier for:
  - Delivering an empty car from the interchange to a siding for loading  
  - Returning an empty car to the interchange after unloading

> This promotes **efficiency and cost predictability** in the rail network. It removes the cost burden on shippers for empty car handling, which would otherwise increase freight costs.
