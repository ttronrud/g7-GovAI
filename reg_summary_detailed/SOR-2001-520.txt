**Detailed Summary of the Canada Industrial Relations Board Regulations, 2001**

The *Canada Industrial Relations Board Regulations, 2001* (the "Regulations") are a set of administrative rules adopted by the **Canada Industrial Relations Board (CIRB)** under **section 15a of the Canada Labour Code (the "Code")**. These regulations establish detailed procedural rules governing how applications, complaints, and other matters are filed, processed, and resolved before the Board. They provide clarity on document requirements, timelines, notice procedures, intervenor rights, evidentiary standards, and the handling of sensitive or confidential information.

---

### **1. Definitions and Key Terms**

The Regulations begin with a comprehensive list of definitions that apply throughout the document:

- **Affidavit**: A written statement sworn under oath or solemn declaration.
- **Application**: Any formal request, complaint, question, or dispute made in writing under the Code and referred to the Board.
- **Code**: Refers to the *Canada Labour Code*.
- **Complaint**: A written complaint filed under specific sections of the Code (e.g., 97(1), 110(3), 133(1)).
- **Day**: A calendar day.
- **Intervenor**: A person whose request to intervene has been granted.
- **Party**: Any applicant, respondent, or intervenor.
- **Person**: Includes employers, employer organizations, trade unions, councils of trade unions, employees, or groups of employees.
- **Registrar**: An employee of the Administrative Tribunals Support Service of Canada authorized by the Board to act on its behalf.
- **Reply**: The final written response by the applicant to the respondent’s response.
- **Response**: The written reply by the respondent to the applicant’s application.
- **Returning Officer**: The individual appointed by the Board to conduct a representation vote.

> **Note**: Certain provisions have been **repealed** (e.g., sections related to disclosure, summons, and notice periods) due to amendments in subsequent legislation (e.g., SOR/2011-109, SOR/2012-305, SOR/2014-243). These are marked in the original text and should be considered obsolete in current practice.

---

### **2. Authority and Signatory Powers**

- Only the **Chairperson**, a **Vice-Chairperson**, or another **Board member** may sign final orders or decisions.
- The **Registrar** may sign decisions in specific limited circumstances, including:
  - Amendments to certification orders due to a name change;
  - Applications for certification under section 24;
  - Successor rights, privileges, and duties under section 43;
  - Name changes in successor employer applications (sections 44–46);
  - Withdrawal of complaints or applications prior to referral to a panel.

> This delegation allows for efficient handling of uncontested or routine matters without requiring full Board involvement.

---

### **3. Filing and Service of Documents**

All documents filed with or served on the Board must be submitted in accordance with strict procedures:

#### **Who Can Sign?**
- **Trade unions, councils, or employer organizations**: Signed by president, secretary, two other officers, or an authorized individual.
- **Employers**: Signed by the employer, general manager, CEO, or authorized representative.
- **Employees**: Signed by the employee or an authorized individual.

> The Board may require written authorization to be filed with the Board if needed.

#### **Methods of Service**
Documents may be served via:
- **Hand delivery**
- **Mail** (to the address for service)
- **Fax** (with proof of receipt)
- **Other means authorized by the Board**

#### **Address for Service**
- For the **Board**: Address of an office of the Administrative Tribunals Support Service of Canada designated as a Board office.
- For any other person: Address as listed in a Board notice, or the latest known address if no address is provided.

#### **Fax Requirements**
A faxed document must include:
- Sender’s and recipient’s names, addresses, phone/fax numbers
- Date and time of transmission
- Total number of pages

#### **Date of Filing**
- If sent by **registered mail**: Filing date is the date of mailing.
- Otherwise: Filing date is the date the document is **received** by the Board.

> **Time Extensions**: If a deadline falls on a Saturday or holiday (as defined in subsection 35(1) of the *Interpretation Act*), it is extended to the next day.

---

### **4. Content Requirements for Applications, Responses, and Replies**

#### **Application (General Case – Not Covered by Expedited Process)**
Must include:
- Names, postal and email addresses, phone/fax numbers of applicant and legal counsel/representative
- Names, contact details of respondent
- Reference to the specific Code provision under which the application is made
- Full factual details, relevant dates, and grounds for the application
- Copies of supporting documents
- Date and description of any prior Board orders or decisions
- Whether a hearing is requested and reasons for it
- Description of the order or decision sought

> **Notice to Affected Employees**: If employee rights are at stake, the Board may require:
> - Posting notices in employee-accessible locations for a reasonable period
> - Notifying employees through other effective means

> The **date employees are deemed to have received notice** is the earliest of:
> - Date of notice from the Board
> - Date of posting
> - Date of notification via other means

#### **Response and Reply**
- **Response** (to an application):
  - Must be filed within:
    - **10 days** for certification applications
    - **15 days** for all other applications
- **Reply** (to a response):
  - Must be filed within **10 days** of the response being filed

> Both responses and replies must include:
> - Names and contact details of respondent and counsel
> - Board file number
> - Full facts, dates, and grounds
> - Supporting documents
> - Position on the order/decision sought
> - Whether a hearing is requested and reasons
> - Description of the order or decision sought

#### **Request to Intervene**
- Must be filed:
  - Within **10 days** for certification applications
  - Within **15 days** for all other applications
- Must include:
  - Name, contact details, and counsel info
  - Description of interest in the matter
  - Explanation of prejudice if denied
  - How intervention will assist the Board in fulfilling Code objectives

> **Response to Intervention Request**: Within 10 days of receipt  
> **Reply to Submissions**: Within 10 days of receiving the intervenor’s written submissions

> Submissions must include:
> - File number
> - Facts, dates, grounds
> - Supporting documents
> - Position on the order/decision
> - Hearing request and reasons
> - Description of the order/decision sought

### **5. Expedited Process**

Certain types of applications are subject to an **expedited process** to ensure timely resolution:

#### Applies to:
- Applications for **interim orders** under section 19.1 of the Code
- Applications to **file a decision or order in Federal or provincial courts** under sections 23 or 23.1
- Referrals to the Board by the Minister under section 80, 87.4(5), or 107
- Applications for **declaration of invalid strike or lockout vote** under 87.3(4) and (5)
- Applications for **declaration of unlawful strike or lockout** under sections 91 and 92
- Unfair labour practice complaints (dismissals for union activity under 94(3), prohibited use of services under 94(4)/(6))
- Complaints respecting dismissals under section 133

#### Key Features:
- The application must be **served on the respondent at the same time** it is filed with the Board.
- This constitutes **notice that a hearing may be held immediately**.
- Parties must respond, reply, or intervene **within five days** of receiving notice or the response.

> **Note**: This process is **not available** for all matters and is specifically tailored to urgent or high-impact cases.

---

### **6. Interim Orders and Affidavits**

- For applications under **section 19.1 (interim orders)**, a **personal knowledge affidavit** is required.
- If the individual lacks personal knowledge, they must state:
  - The source of information or belief
  - The basis for relying on that information
- The Board may specify terms of cross-examination or reply by the deponent.
- **Interim orders remain in effect until the matter is finally resolved** by the Board.

> The Board may require any party to **verify by affidavit** the contents of a document within a reasonable time.

---

### **7. Consolidation and Hearing Procedures**

- The Board may **consolidate**, **hear together**, or **hear consecutively** two or more proceedings.
- A party seeking disclosure of documents must:
  - Request disclosure directly from the other parties first
  - Only apply to the Board for an order if direct negotiation fails

> If parties reach a **document disclosure agreement**, the Board may require them to file a copy with the Board.

> **Public Record**: The Board must place relevant documents on the public record unless they are declared confidential.

> **Confidentiality**:
> - The Board may declare a document confidential if disclosure would cause **specific direct harm** to a person.
