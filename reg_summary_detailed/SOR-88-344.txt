**Detailed Summary of the Office of the Registrar General Fees for Services Order (SI/88-68, 1988)**

---

**Regulatory Background and Authority**  
This regulation, known as the *Office of the Registrar General Fees for Services Order*, was issued on **June 23, 1988**, by the **Minister of Consumer and Corporate Affairs**. It is authorized under:

- **Paragraph 13(b) of the Financial Administration Act**, which empowers the Minister to establish fees for services provided by federal departments and agencies.
- **Order in Council P.C. 1988-672**, dated April 14, 1988, which provides the legal basis for the Minister to prescribe fees for services related to the issuance of documents by the Office of the Registrar General of Canada.

The Order became effective on **July 1, 1988**, and may be cited as the *Office of the Registrar General Fees for Services Order*.

---

**Purpose of the Order**  
The primary purpose of this regulation is to establish a standardized, transparent, and legally enforceable fee structure for services provided by the Office of the Registrar General of Canada. These services include the issuance and reproduction of various official documents, primarily related to the administration of the Railway Act, the Canadian National Railway Act, and the Bell Canada Act.

---

**Fees Prescribed by the Order**

The Order outlines specific fees for different types of documents and services. Below is a detailed breakdown:

1. **Certificate of Authentication**  
   - **Fee**: $100 per certificate  
   - **Description**: This certificate verifies the authenticity of a document, typically used to confirm that a document (such as a railway or telecommunications record) is genuine and issued by an authorized body. It is a standalone service.

2. **Certificate of Deposit (under Railway Act, Canadian National Railway Act, or Bell Canada Act)**  
   - **First Certificate**: $100  
   - **Each Additional (related) Certificate**: $35  
   - **Description**:  
     - A "Certificate of Deposit" is issued to confirm the existence or status of a deposit made under specific federal acts.  
     - The fee structure reflects a tiered model: a higher initial fee for the first certificate, with a reduced rate for subsequent related certificates (e.g., additional deposits or updates).  
     - "Related certificates" are those that pertain to the same transaction or deposit, such as multiple entries in a single record.

3. **Copies of Documents Deposited under the Railway Act, Canadian National Railway Act, or Bell Canada Act**  
   - **Fee**: $1.00 per page  
   - **Description**:  
     - This applies to reproducing original records that have been filed under the aforementioned acts.  
     - The cost is per page, meaning that a 10-page document would incur a fee of $10.  
     - This service is limited to documents deposited under these specific statutes.

4. **Copy of Any Other Document**  
   - **Fee**: $10.00 per copy  
   - **Description**:  
     - Applies to copies of documents not covered under the Railway, Canadian National Railway, or Bell Canada Acts.  
     - Examples may include personal identification records, birth or death certificates (if not specifically covered), or other non-transportation-related records.  
     - The fee is a flat rate regardless of document length or complexity.

5. **Certified Copy of Any Document**  
   - **Fee**: $35.00 per certified copy  
   - **Description**:  
     - A "certified copy" is a reproduction of a document that is stamped or signed by an official to verify its accuracy and authenticity.  
     - This service is more formal and carries a higher fee due to the verification process.  
     - Applies to any document, whether under the railway acts or other categories.

---

**Key Features and Implications**

- **Clarity and Transparency**: The Order provides a clear, itemized fee schedule, making it easier for applicants to understand and budget for the costs associated with obtaining official documents.
- **Tiered Pricing**: The use of tiered fees (e.g., $100 for the first certificate, $35 for each additional one) encourages efficient use of services while preventing overcharging for related requests.
- **Legal Basis**: The fees are grounded in federal administrative law, ensuring that they are enforceable and consistent with financial governance standards.
- **Scope**: The fees are limited to services provided by the Office of the Registrar General of Canada and do not extend to other government departments or services.
- **Effective Date**: The Order took effect on **July 1, 1988**, meaning all fees apply from that date forward.

---

**Application and Enforcement**  
- The fees are payable directly to the Office of the Registrar General of Canada when requesting the issuance or reproduction of documents.
- The Office is responsible for collecting the fees and ensuring compliance with the prescribed rates.
- The Order is enforceable under the Financial Administration Act and can be amended or revoked by the Minister with proper authority.

---

**Conclusion**  
The *Office of the Registrar General Fees for Services Order* establishes a fair, consistent, and legally sound fee structure for services related to the issuance and reproduction of official documents. It supports transparency in government operations, ensures adequate funding for administrative services, and provides clear guidance for individuals and organizations seeking to access records under federal legislation. The tiered and categorized fees reflect both administrative efficiency and the varying levels of service required for different types of documents.

This Order remains a foundational regulatory instrument in the administration of federal record-keeping and authentication processes in Canada.
