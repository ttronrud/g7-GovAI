### **Detailed Summary of the Yukon Territory Supreme Court Rules for Pre-Hearing Conferences in Criminal Matters**

---

#### **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**
These Rules were established by the **Supreme Court of the Yukon Territory** under **section 438 of the Criminal Code** and with the **concurrence of a majority of the judges present** at a meeting on **July 25, 1988**. They implement and govern the procedures for **pre-hearing conferences** in criminal matters, as mandated by **section 553.1 of the Criminal Code**.

The rules aim to:
- Promote a **fair, just, and expeditious** trial.
- Facilitate early resolution of issues.
- Reduce trial delays and costs.
- Allow for meaningful discussion between the parties and the court before trial begins.

These Rules are formally titled:  
**"Yukon Territory Supreme Court Rules for Pre-Hearing Conferences in Criminal Matters"**  
and may be cited accordingly.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**
- **Court**: Refers to the Supreme Court of the Yukon Territory.  
- **Judge**: A judge of the Court.

---

#### **3. Timing and Location of Pre-Hearing Conferences**
- The conference must be held at a time, date, and place **as directed by the presiding judge**.
- The judge may **reschedule** the conference or hold additional ones if necessary.
- The conference is **not mandatory** in terms of timing, but must occur when the judge deems appropriate.

---

#### **4. Nature of the Conference**
- By default, the pre-hearing conference is **informal and conducted in chambers**.
- It is designed to allow **full and free discussion** of issues without prejudicing the rights of the parties in any subsequent proceedings.
- The informal nature ensures open dialogue while preserving procedural fairness.

---

#### **5. Attendance**
- **Counsel for the Crown and counsel for the accused** (or the accused if unrepresented) must attend **if fully briefed** on the issues to be discussed.
- The judge may require that an accused **be available for consultation with counsel** prior to or during the conference.

> *Note:* This ensures that all parties are adequately prepared and have the opportunity to engage in meaningful discussion.

---

#### **6. Topics for Discussion**
The presiding judge may inquire into and discuss the following matters:

| Topic | Purpose |
|------|--------|
| **Disclosure** | Assess the extent of disclosure by the Crown and whether further disclosure is needed by the accused or counsel. |
| **Resolution of Issues** | Explore the possibility of resolving charges—e.g., through a plea deal, dismissal, or agreement—especially regarding individual counts. |
| **Simplification of Issues** | Identify and streamline complex or overlapping issues to make trial more manageable. |
| **Admissions and Agreements** | Encourage the exchange of admissions or agreements to facilitate a fair and timely outcome. |
| **Estimated Trial Duration** | Determine how long the trial is expected to take. |
| **Fixing a Trial Date** | Set or confirm a trial date if not already scheduled. |
| **Further Conferences** | Consider holding additional pre-hearing conferences if needed. |

> These discussions are intended to **identify potential efficiencies** and **avoid unnecessary litigation**.

---

#### **7. Anticipated Pre-Hearing Motions**
- The judge may, at their discretion, **order that a pre-hearing motion** (e.g., for particulars, amendment, severance) be heard **before trial**.
- The motion may be scheduled at a time convenient for counsel and the court.

---

#### **8. Discretionary Orders**
- The judge has **broad discretion** to issue any orders deemed necessary or appropriate to ensure:
  - Fairness
  - Justice
  - Efficiency
- Such orders may include procedural, evidentiary, or scheduling directives.

---

#### **9. Post-Conference Requirements**
Upon completion of the conference, the judge must:
1. **Complete a Pre-Hearing Conference Report in Form I**  
   - A copy of this report must be provided to:
     - Counsel for the Crown
     - Counsel for the accused (or the accused if unrepresented)
   - The report is **tentative** and contains **no binding commitments**.

2. **Endorse the indictment** with the date of the conference.  
   - This serves as a formal record of the conference and helps track procedural timelines.

> **Important Note**:  
> The report is **not binding**. Positions stated are **tentative**, and all agreements are:
> - Without prejudice
> - Purely for the assistance of the court in resolving trial problems
> - Subject to prompt review and adjustment if necessary

---

#### **10. Flexibility and Additional Conferences**
- The rules do **not preclude** the judge from conducting **additional informal pre-hearing conferences** with the **consent of counsel or the accused**.
- These can occur on terms the judge considers appropriate and may be used to address specific concerns not covered in the initial conference.

---

#### **11. Effective Date**
- These Rules come into force on the day that **section 553.1 of the Criminal Code** (as amended by **section 127 of the Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1985, S.C. 1985, c. 19**) comes into effect.

> **Note**: These rules were officially in force as of **September 1, 1988**, as noted in SI/88-125.

---

#### **12. Pre-Hearing Conference Report (Form I) – Summary of Contents**
This standardized form guides the discussion and documentation of key procedural and evidentiary issues. It includes the following sections:

---

##### **A. Procedural History**
- Dates of alleged offences
- Dates of original information
- Date and length of preliminary inquiry
- Status of transcript (ready/not ready)
- Whether an indictment has been filed
- Whether a trial date has been set
- Tentative trial duration
- Accused’s custody status

---

##### **B. Mode of Trial**
- Whether the accused has elected:
  - Trial by judge alone
  - Trial by judge and jury
- Whether there will be a **re-election** (retrial) for a different mode
- Whether consent is required and whether it will be given

---

##### **C. Disclosure**
- Requests for further disclosure
- Sufficiency of the indictment (under sections 510(1) and 510(3) of the Criminal Code)
- Requests for particulars
- Whether a motion for particulars will be filed
- Requests to amend the indictment or counts

---

##### **D. Joinder and Severance**
- Motion to sever certain counts from others
- Motion to sever accused from others
- Basis for such motions

---

##### **E. Jury Composition**
- Challenge to the jury array
- Prospective juror challenges for cause
- Whether proposed questions will be submitted to prospective jurors

---

##### **F. Special Pleas**
- Whether any special pleas (e.g., insanity, self-defence) will be raised
- Details of the basis for each plea

---

##### **G. Issue of Fitness**
- Whether fitness is in issue (by Crown or defence)
- Disclosure of medical or other reports
- Whether copies will be made available to the trial judge
- Whether fitness was raised at preliminary inquiry
- Whether fitness will be contested at trial
- Estimated duration of fitness evidence

---

##### **H. Evidentiary Matters**
- **Similar Fact Evidence**: Whether the Crown will introduce such evidence and whether the defence will contest it.
- **Statements**: Whether statements by the accused to persons in authority will be admitted; whether a **voir dire** (cross-examination) will be requested (on grounds of voluntariness, Charter compliance, etc.); estimated duration.
- **Hearsay**: Whether evidence will be contested on hearsay grounds or whether exceptions will be claimed.
- **Expert Evidence**: Whether expert testimony will be introduced by Crown or defence; on what issue; whether admissibility will be contested.
- **Privilege**: Whether any claim of privilege (e.g., legal, medical) will be asserted.
- **Search and Seizure**: Whether evidence will be challenged under **section 8 of the Charter**.
- **Intercepted Communications**:
  - Whether evidence will be admitted
  - Whether a **voir dire** is required
  - Whether admission is based on:
    - Authorization
    - Consent
    - Section 178.16(1)(a) or (b) of the Criminal Code
  - Whether the adequacy of notice under section 178.16(4) will be contested
  - Whether applications will be made in voir dire (e.g., to open sealed packets, set aside authorizations, allege fraud, coercion)
  - Estimated duration of the voir dire
- **Trial Issues**:
  - Voice identification
  - Tape accuracy, integrity, continuity
  - Use and admissibility of transcripts

---

##### **I. Competency of Witnesses**
- Allegations that Crown or defence witnesses are:
  - Incompetent due to age or mental capacity
  - Capable only of giving unsworn testimony

---

##### **J. Procedural Issues**
- Likelihood of a **directed verdict** (judgment at end of Crown’s case)
- Other procedural matters the trial judge may need to rule on

---

##### **K. Length of Trial**
- Estimated duration of the trial

---

##### **L. Positions of the Parties**
- **Crown Position**:
  - On each accused
