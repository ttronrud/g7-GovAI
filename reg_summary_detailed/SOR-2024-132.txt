### ðŸ“Œ **1. Legal Basis and Authority**

The Feeds Regulations, 2024 are made under the following statutes:

- **Feeds Act (S.C. 2015, c. 2, s. 56; R.S., c. F-9)**  
  Primary legislation governing the regulation of feeds in Canada.

- **Health of Animals Act (subsection 64(1)c)**  
  Provides authority to regulate animal health-related aspects of feed, particularly concerning disease prevention and treatment.

- **Food and Drugs Act (S.C. 2015, c. 2, ss. 95(1) to (6); S.C. 1990, c. 21)**  
  Supports regulation of medicating ingredients and safety standards related to human and animal health.

- **Food and Drug Regulations (S.C. 2023, c. 26, ss. 506(1); R.S., c. F-27)**  
  Provides specific rules on drug use, labeling, and safety of medicating ingredients.

These regulations are issued by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the **recommendation of the Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food and the Minister of Health**, reflecting a collaborative approach between agri-food and public health governance.

### ðŸ„ **3. Designation of Livestock (Under Section 2 of the Act)**

Animals designated as **livestock** for regulatory purposes include:

- Cattle, sheep, goats  
- Bison, water buffalo, cervids (e.g., deer), llamas, alpacas  
- Swine (pigs)  
- Poultry (chickens, turkeys, ducks, geese)  
- Ratites, pigeons, pheasants, partridges, quail, grouse, guinea fowl, pea fowl  
- Horses and rabbits  
- Bees  
- Finfish and molluscs intended for human consumption as food  

> This definition is critical for determining which feeds are subject to regulation and which exemptions apply.
