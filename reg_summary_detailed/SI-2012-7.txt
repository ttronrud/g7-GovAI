**Detailed Summary of the Criminal Proceedings Rules for the Superior Court of Justice (Ontario), Effective March 1, 2012**

---

### **1. Background and Legal Authority**
The *Criminal Proceedings Rules for the Superior Court of Justice (Ontario)* were enacted on **February 3, 2012**, and came into force on **March 1, 2012**, pursuant to **subsection 482(1) and (3) of the Criminal Code** of Canada. These rules replace and repeal a number of outdated and fragmented rules governing criminal proceedings in Ontario’s Superior Court of Justice.

The legislation is rooted in the **Criminal Code**, which grants the Superior Court of Justice jurisdiction over all criminal matters. This regulatory framework ensures consistency, fairness, and procedural clarity in the administration of criminal justice across Ontario.

---

### **2. Repeal of Previous Rules**
The new rules **repeal several prior sets of rules and regulations**, including:
- *Part I of the Rules Respecting Criminal Proceedings* (SI/85-152)
- *Ontario Supreme Court Rules Respecting Pre-hearing Conferences in Criminal Matters* (SI/86-145)
- *Ontario District Court Rules Respecting Pre-hearing Conferences in Criminal Matters* (SI/86-214)
- *Ontario County and District Court Judges’ Criminal Courts Summary Conviction Appeal Rules* (SI/77-213)
- *Ontario Court of Justice Criminal Proceedings Rules* (SI/92-99)
- *Ontario Rules of Practice Respecting Reduction in the Number of Years of Imprisonment Without Eligibility for Parole* (SOR/92-270)
- *Rules Amending the Ontario Court of Justice Criminal Proceedings Rules* (SI/97-121)

This repeal consolidates and modernizes the procedural framework for criminal proceedings in the Superior Court of Justice, eliminating redundancy and creating a unified, streamlined system.

---

### **3. Scope and Application**
These rules apply to:
- Prosecutions
- Criminal proceedings
- Applications (e.g., motions, requests for relief)
- Appeals

They govern all matters of a criminal nature or arising from or incidental to such proceedings, including pre-trial, trial, and post-trial stages.

**Jurisdiction**: The rules apply within the jurisdiction of the Superior Court of Justice, which includes all counties, districts, and regions in Ontario.

---

The rules define key terms used throughout the document, ensuring clarity and consistency. Important definitions include:

### **5. General Principles and Objectives**
The rules are designed to:
- Ensure **fairness and justice**
- Promote **simplicity and efficiency** in procedure
- Eliminate **unjustifiable expense and delay**
- Be **liberally construed** where gaps exist

Where specific procedures are not provided, courts are expected to apply **analogy** to existing rules.

**Special Provision for Unrepresented Accused**:  
If an accused appears in person (without a solicitor), they may perform all acts that would normally require a solicitor — provided the rules permit or require such actions.

---

### **6. Interpretation and Compliance**
- The **interpretation sections of the Criminal Code** apply to these rules.
- The **Appendix of Forms** provides standard templates (e.g., Form 1, Form 2, Form 4) that must be used where applicable, with reasonable adaptations based on circumstances.
- A judge may **dispense with compliance with any rule** only if it is **necessary in the interests of justice**.

---

### **7. Time Calculations and Service Rules**
Time periods are calculated under specific guidelines:
- **Between two events**: Days are **excluded from the first event** and **included in the second** (e.g., from May 1 to May 5 = 4 days).
- **Less than 7 days**: Holidays are **not counted**.
- **Expiration on a holiday**: The deadline may be extended to the **next non-holiday**.
- **Service after 4 p.m. or on a holiday**: Deemed to have been served on the **next non-holiday**.

**Time of Day**: Local time is used for all references.

**Extension of Time**:
- A judge may extend or abridge time periods under **Rule 2.01**.
- Applications for extensions may be made **before or after** the deadline.
- Time may also be extended by **consent in writing**, signed by the relevant party or as directed by the court.

---

### **8. Document Standards and Format Requirements**
All documents must meet strict formatting standards to ensure consistency and readability:

#### **General Document Requirements**
- **Size**: 216 mm × 279 mm (standard letter size)
- **Paper quality**: Good quality paper
- **Text formatting**: 
  - Legible, printed, typewritten, or handwritten
  - Double-spaced
  - Left margin: approximately **40 mm**
- **Headings**:
  - Must include:
    - Name of court and court file number
    - Title of proceeding (per Rule 6 or 40)
    - If more than two parties: "and others" after the first two names
- **Backsheet (Form 3)**:
  - Must include:
    - Short title of proceeding
    - Name of court and file number
    - Deponent’s name and date of swearing (for affidavits)
    - Location of court office
    - Title of document
    - Name, address, and phone number of solicitor or party filing

#### **Certified Copies**
- At request and upon payment of a prescribed fee, the **registrar may issue a certified copy** of any document in a court file.

#### **Filing Requirements**
- All documents must be filed in the **court office where the proceeding began**, unless otherwise directed.
- Documents filed by mail:
  - The **filing stamp date** is deemed the filing date.
  - If the court office has no record of receipt, the document is **deemed not filed**, unless the court orders otherwise.
- Documents used in hearings (e.g., affidavits, transcripts) must be filed in the **court office where the hearing is held**.

---

### **9. Affidavit Requirements**
Affidavits must:
- Be in **Form 4**
- Be written in the **first person**
- State the **full name of the deponent**
- If the deponent is a party or representative, state that fact
- Be divided into **numbered paragraphs**, each focused on a single fact
- Be **signed and sworn/affirmed** before a person authorized to administer oaths
- Be limited to **facts known personally** or evidence the deponent could give in court
- May include **statements of belief or information** only if:
  - The source and basis of belief are clearly stated
  - No such statements are allowed where the rules prohibit them

**Exhibits**:
- If attached: must be physically attached to the affidavit
- If shown to the deponent: not attached; left with the registrar and returned upon conclusion of the matter (unless ordered otherwise)
- For documents: a copy must be served with the affidavit unless impractical

**Multiple Deponents**:
- Each requires a **separate jurat**
- If all deponents swear together at the same time, one jurat may state: “Sworn (or affirmed) by the above-named deponents”

**Corporate Affidavits**:
- A corporation may be represented by an officer, director, or employee
