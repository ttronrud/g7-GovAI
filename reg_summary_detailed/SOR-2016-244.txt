**Detailed Summary of the Global Fund Replenishment Conference — Privileges and Immunities Order**

---

### **1. Legal Basis and Authority**
This regulation is issued under the authority of **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs**. It is grounded in specific provisions of the **Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act (FMOA)**:

- **Paragraph 5(1)(c)a** – Allows for the granting of privileges and immunities to international conferences and organizations.
- **Paragraph 5(1)(g)** – Authorizes the granting of privileges and immunities to representatives of foreign states participating in international conferences, where such privileges are necessary for the effective performance of their functions.

This Order is a formal legislative instrument that confers specific legal privileges and immunities on participants in a particular international conference.

---

### **2. Reference to the Conference**
The Order applies to the **Global Fund Replenishment Conference**, an intergovernmental meeting convened to strengthen the Global Fund’s efforts in combating **AIDS, Tuberculosis, and Malaria**. Key details:

- **Official Name**: Global Fund Replenishment Conference to fight AIDS, Tuberculosis and Malaria  
- **Location**: Montréal, Quebec  
- **Dates**: September 15 to 17, 2016  

The term “Conference” is defined within the Order to refer specifically to this event.

---

### **3. Privileges and Immunities Granted**

#### **A. During the Conference (September 15–17, 2016)**
Representatives of **foreign states** participating in the Conference are granted the following privileges and immunities **in Canada**, to the extent required for the performance of their official duties:

- **Paragraph 11(b) of Article IV of the United Nations Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations**  
  → Grants immunity from **civil and criminal jurisdiction** in Canada for acts performed in the course of their official duties during the Conference.

- **Paragraph 11(e) of Article IV**  
  → Grants immunity from **civil and criminal liability** for actions taken in connection with the Conference, including the use of official documents or communications.

- **Sections 14 to 16 of Article IV**  
  → These provisions provide:
  - Immunity from **searches and seizures** of personal property or official documents.
  - Protection of **official communications** (e.g., correspondence, records) from being disclosed or subject to legal process.
  - Protection of **official functions** carried out during the Conference.

These privileges are **limited in scope** and are only applicable to actions directly related to the Conference and to the extent necessary for the representatives to perform their official duties.

> **Note**: These immunities are consistent with international norms and are granted under the framework of the United Nations Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations, which is incorporated into Canadian law via Schedule III of the FMOA.

---

#### **B. During Travel to and from the Conference**
Representatives of foreign states who are acting in their **official capacity** as:

- A **head of state**
- A **head of government**
- A **minister**

are granted the following privileges and immunities **during their journey to and from the Conference**:

- **Paragraph 11(d) of Article IV of the UN Convention**  
  → Provides **immunity from criminal jurisdiction** in Canada for acts committed during travel, provided they are related to the official duties of the representative.  
  → This includes protection from arrest or prosecution for acts committed while traveling in Canada in connection with the Conference.

> This immunity is **not extended** to personal acts or private conduct; it is strictly tied to official duties.

---

#### **C. Heads of International Organizations**
Heads of international organizations (e.g., CEOs, executives, or designated officials of the Global Fund or other participating entities) participating in the Conference are granted:

- **Paragraph 18(d) of Article V of the UN Convention**  
  → Provides **immunity from criminal jurisdiction** during travel to and from the Conference, for acts performed in the course of their official duties.

> This provision protects the personal and official activities of organizational leaders during travel, ensuring they can attend and participate without fear of legal interference.

---

### **4. Scope and Limitations**
- The privileges and immunities are **time-bound** and apply **only during the specific period** of the Conference (September 15–17, 2016) and the **travel to and from** that period.
- They are **limited to the functions directly related to the Conference**.
- The privileges are **not absolute**; they do not extend to personal conduct, private property, or acts unrelated to official duties.
- The Order does **not** confer immunity from **taxation**, **financial liability**, or **civil claims** unrelated to official functions.

---

### **5. Legal Effect and Implementation**
- The Order is **registered** with the Governor in Council and becomes effective **on the day it is registered** (i.e., upon publication in the *Canada Gazette*).
- It is a **temporary, specific-purpose instrument**, not a permanent or general rule.
- It operates under the **principles of international law**, particularly the UN Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations, which Canada has adopted through its domestic legislation.

---

### **6. Purpose and Significance**
The Order serves several key purposes:

- **Facilitates diplomatic engagement**: Ensures that foreign government officials and international organization leaders can participate fully and without legal impediments in a high-level international health conference.
- **Respects international norms**: Aligns Canada’s legal treatment of international participants with established international practices and treaty obligations.
- **Supports global health cooperation**: Enables effective coordination among countries and organizations to address the global burden of AIDS, tuberculosis, and malaria.

---

### **Conclusion**
The *Global Fund Replenishment Conference — Privileges and Immunities Order* is a targeted, legally sound, and internationally consistent measure that grants temporary and function-specific privileges and immunities to foreign state representatives and heads of international organizations during a critical global health conference. These protections are essential for enabling diplomatic and administrative functions to proceed without undue legal barriers, while remaining within the bounds of Canadian law and international treaty obligations.

> This Order exemplifies how Canada balances its obligations under international law with the practical needs of multilateral diplomacy.
