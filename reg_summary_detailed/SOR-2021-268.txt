## ğŸ“ Determining VOC Concentration

The VOC concentration is calculated using a formula:

$$
\text{VOC Concentration (\% w/w)} = \frac{W_S}{W_{EX}} \times 100
$$

Where:
- **W_S** = Total weight (in grams) of substances that **volatize** during testing.
- **W_EX** = Weight of substances **excluded** from the calculation (based on type and category).

## ğŸ”„ Compliance Unit Trading System (CUTS)

To support compliance, a **compliance unit trading system** is introduced, allowing companies to:
1. **Generate compliance units** (1 unit per kg of reformulated product).
2. **Transfer unused units** to other companies.
3. **Use units** to offset excess VOCs in products that exceed the maximum allowed concentration.

### How Units Are Generated:
- A company must submit a **notice by October 1** of the first year of participation.
- The notice includes:
  - Company and representative details
  - Product name, category, and subcategory
  - Pre-reformulation VOC concentration, reformulation date, post-reformulation concentration
  - Expected annual production (excluding exports)

> **Formula for Units Generated**:
$$
\text{Compliance Units} = \sum \left( \frac{(C_{\text{max}} - C_{\text{actual}})}{C_{\text{max}}} \times Q \right)
$$
Where:
- $C_{\text{max}}$ = Maximum allowed VOC concentration (from Schedule 1)
- $C_{\text{actual}}$ = Actual VOC concentration after reformulation
- $Q$ = Quantity of product (kg) manufactured/imported in a year (excluding exports)

> For the **first year**, the base period starts from **the date of notice submission or reformulation**, whichever is later.

### Validity of Compliance Units:
- Valid for **2 years**, starting on **January 1 of the year after generation**.

> The Minister must issue **written confirmation** of unit availability within **60 days** of receiving a report under section 14.
