### **Detailed Summary of the ACOA Loan Insurance Regulations (R.S., c. 41 (4th Supp.), Part I)**  
*Issued: Moncton, May 7, 1990*  
*Cited as: ACOA Loan Insurance Regulations*  
*Effective under the Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency Act (ACOA Act), pursuant to paragraphs 20(1)(b) and (2)(a), with approval of the Minister of Finance*

---

## **I. Purpose and Authority**

These regulations establish the framework for **loan insurance** provided by the **Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency (ACOA)** under its **Action Program** to support commercial development in Atlantic Canada. The Minister of ACOA, with the approval of the Minister of Finance, enacted these rules to ensure that loan insurance is granted only for projects that contribute meaningfully to regional economic development.

The regulations are designed to:
- Encourage private investment in targeted commercial enterprises.
- Reduce financial risk for lenders who would otherwise be hesitant to fund certain types of projects.
- Promote economic growth in Atlantic Canada through strategic, high-impact investments.

## **III. Eligibility Criteria for Loan Insurance**

A project may qualify for loan insurance **only if it meets one of the following conditions**:

### ‚úÖ **1. Projects in Scheduled Sectors**
- The establishment, expansion, or modernization of a commercial enterprise engaged in activities listed in the **Schedule** (e.g., agriculture, fishing, manufacturing, tourism).

### ‚úÖ **2. Strategic Investments Not in the Schedule**
- Activities not listed in the Schedule but represent a **strategic investment** with clear economic benefits to Atlantic Canada.

### ‚úÖ **3. Asset Purchases (Under Specific Conditions)**
- Purchase of assets from an existing commercial enterprise **only if**:
  - The enterprise has ceased or is about to cease operations due to circumstances **beyond the owner‚Äôs control**.
  - The transaction is **arm‚Äôs length** and involves a buyer **unrelated** to the current or immediate predecessor owner.
  - The purchase was **not made solely to obtain loan insurance**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Excluded Projects**:
> - Wholesale or retail trade
> - Real estate development
> - Government service industries
> - Personal or social services
> - Mergers, refinancing, or acquisitions (except under paragraph 3(c))

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Food/Drink Enterprises**:
> - If the project serves food or drink, it must fall under **Sector 3: Tourism** (e.g., hotels, restaurants, campgrounds).

## **IV. Conditions for Approval of an Application**

The Agency may approve a loan insurance application **only if** all of the following conditions are met:

1. **Project Location**: Must be in Atlantic Canada.
2. **Loan Amount & Term**:
   - Minimum loan: **$100,000**
   - Maximum term: **15 years**
3. **Use of Funds**:
   - Entire loan amount must be used to finance the project.
4. **Loan Application**:
   - Loan must be applied **only to capital costs**.
5. **Security Interest**:
   - Lender must have an **enforceable security interest** (e.g., mortgage, lien), consistent with standard banking practices.
6. **Lender‚Äôs Need for Insurance**:
   - The applicant cannot obtain a loan or one on reasonable terms without the insurance.
7. **Project Viability**:
   - The project (or combined with existing operations) must be **commercially viable** within reasonable risk bounds.
8. **Economic Contribution**:
   - The project must represent a **significant contribution** to the economic benefit of Atlantic Canada.

## **V. Loan Insurance Agreement**

Upon approval of an application, the Agency may enter into a **loan insurance agreement** with the lender. This agreement outlines:
- The percentage of the loan that will be insured (maximum **90%** of the outstanding principal).
- The terms of insurance coverage, including claim conditions and limits.

---

## **VI. Insurance Fee Structure**

Lenders must pay an **advance insurance fee** calculated using the following formulas:

| Fee Type | Formula |
|--------|--------|
| **On first disbursement** | Fee = (Insurance Percentage √ó Disbursement Amount) |
| **On each subsequent disbursement** | Fee = (Insurance Percentage √ó Disbursement Amount) |
| **On each anniversary date** (until earlier of formal demand or loan repayment) | Fee = (Insurance Percentage √ó Outstanding Loan Balance on anniversary date) √ó (Number of days remaining until next anniversary) / 365 |

> üîç The fee is based on the **percentage of the loan insured** and the **amount and timing** of disbursements.

## **VII. Claim Process and Payment Limits**

### üîç **When Can a Claim Be Paid?**
- A claim **cannot be paid** until:
  1. A **formal demand** has been made by the lender to the applicant.
  2. The lender provides **evidence** that it has taken **all reasonable steps** to protect its rights and realize on the security.

### üìÑ **Claim Requirements**
- Must include all information required by the Agency to assess the loss amount.

### üí∞ **Maximum Payment Amount**
The Agency will pay the **lesser of**:
1. The **percentage of loss** incurred by the lender (as defined below), **or**
2. The **percentage of the outstanding principal** on the date of formal demand.

---

## **VIII. Definition of "Loss"**

The total loss is calculated as follows:

> **Loss =**  
> (a) Principal amount of loan + unpaid interest (as of date of formal demand)  
> + (b) Reasonable legal fees, disbursements, and collection costs  
> + (c) Disbursements to protect security  
> + (d) Interest on (a‚Äìc), calculated at the rate in the loan agreement:  
> - For up to **180 days** after formal demand  
> - For an **additional 180 days** (if agreed upon by Agency and lender)  
> - After that, interest is calculated at **half the original rate**  
>  
> **Minus**  
> (e) Net proceeds from sale of security  
> (f) Any recovery by the lender after the formal demand date exceeding other debts owed by the applicant

> ‚úÖ Interest is capped at **180 days** (with optional extension), and reduced to **50%** after that.
