## **2. Prescribed Interest Rate for Payments to the Receiver General for Canada**

This section defines the **interest rate** that must be paid to the **Receiver General for Canada** when the Act requires interest to be paid (e.g., on late payments or tax liabilities).

### **Calculation Method**:
The prescribed interest rate for a given quarter is the **sum of two components**:

#### **Component 1: Average Yield of Treasury Bills**
- **Basis**: The **simple arithmetic mean** (average) of the yields (expressed as a percentage per year) of **Government of Canada Treasury Bills**.
- **Criteria**:
  - Bills that mature **approximately three months** after their issue date.
  - Sold at **auctions** during the **first month of the quarter immediately preceding** the current quarter.
- **Calculation**:
  - All such auction yields are averaged.
  - The average is expressed as a **percentage per year**.
  - If the average is not a whole number, it is **rounded up to the next higher whole percentage**.
- **Example**: If the average yield is 3.4%, it becomes 4%.

#### **Component 2: Fixed Addition**
- A fixed **4%** rate is added to the average yield from Component 1.

### **Final Interest Rate Formula**:
> **Prescribed Rate = (Average Yield of 3-month Treasury Bills) + 4%**

> This means the total interest rate is **at least 4%**, and increases depending on the yield of Treasury Bills.

### **Application**:
- Applies to **all provisions of the Act** that require interest to be paid to the Receiver General for Canada.

> Example: If the average yield of 3-month T-bills in the first month of Q1 (January) is 2.8%, the prescribed rate for Q1 is 2.8% + 4% = **6.8%** (rounded up to 7% if not already whole).

## **4. Revenue Thresholds (Key Financial Thresholds)**

These thresholds determine whether a company is subject to the digital services tax.

### **Global Revenue Threshold**
- **Amount**: **€750,000,000** (750 million euros)
- **Purpose**: A company is subject to the tax **only if its global revenue (from all sources) exceeds €750 million** in a given fiscal year.
- **Note**: This is the **overall threshold** that determines eligibility for the tax.

### **In-Scope Revenue Threshold**
- **Amount**: **$20,000,000** (20 million Canadian dollars)
- **Purpose**: This threshold determines whether a company's **digital services revenue** (e.g., revenue from online platforms, advertising, etc.) is included in the tax calculation.
- **Application**: A company must have **at least $20 million in digital services revenue** to be considered "in scope" for the tax.

> Note: Even if a company exceeds the global threshold, it may not be subject to the tax if its digital revenue is below $20 million.

---

## **5. Registration Threshold (Part 6 of the Act)**

- **Amount**: **$10,000,000**
- **Purpose**: Applies under **Part 6** of the Act, which governs **registration requirements** for digital service providers.
- **Requirement**: A company must register with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) if its **annual revenue from digital services exceeds $10 million**.
- **Note**: This threshold is **lower** than the in-scope threshold, meaning registration may be required even if the company is not yet subject to the tax.

> Example: A company earning $12 million annually in digital services must register under Part 6, regardless of whether it exceeds the in-scope threshold.
