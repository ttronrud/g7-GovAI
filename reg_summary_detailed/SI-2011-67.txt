**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

**Regulation Title:**  
Transfer of Control and Supervision of Specific Units from the Department of Public Works and Government Services to Shared Services Canada

**Legal Authority:**  
The regulation is issued under **paragraph 2(a)(a) of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act** (S.C. 2003, c. 22, s. 207), as amended or referenced in the *Revised Statutes of Canada, Chapter P-34* (R.S., c. P-34). This provision empowers the Governor General in Council (acting on the recommendation of the Prime Minister) to reorganize federal public administration by transferring control and supervision of certain units to a newly established or designated entity—here, **Shared Services Canada**.

---

**Key Provisions of the Regulation:**

1. **Transfer of Units:**  
   The Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, transfers to **Shared Services Canada** the control and supervision of the following specific units within the **Department of Public Works and Government Services (DPWGS)**:

   - **Services Transition and Major Projects Unit**  
     Responsible for managing the transition of public services and overseeing major infrastructure or program projects across federal departments.

   - **Chief Technology Officer Unit**  
     Oversees the strategic direction of technology policy, innovation, and digital transformation across the federal government.

   - **Enterprise Services Unit**  
     Manages enterprise-wide information systems, service delivery platforms, and shared technology solutions used across multiple departments.

   - **Service Management and Delivery Unit**  
     Focuses on the design, delivery, and performance management of public services, including service standards, customer experience, and operational efficiency.

   - **Email, Data Centre and Network Services Unit**  
     Provides core IT infrastructure services such as email systems, data centre operations, and network management for federal departments.

   - **Email, Data Centre and Network Services Support Unit**  
     Offers technical support and operational assistance for the services managed by the above unit, ensuring reliability and uptime.

2. **Governance and Leadership:**  
   The **Minister of Public Works and Government Services** is formally designated as the **presiding officer (or head)** of Shared Services Canada. This establishes a direct line of accountability between the federal minister responsible for DPWGS and the newly structured shared services entity.

3. **Effective Date:**  
   The transfer of control and supervision, as well as the appointment of the Minister as presiding officer, takes effect on **August 4, 2011**.

---

**Purpose and Strategic Rationale:**

- **Centralization of Shared Services:** The transfer aims to consolidate key IT and service delivery functions into a centralized, cross-departmental entity (Shared Services Canada) to improve efficiency, reduce duplication, and ensure standardized service delivery across the federal government.

- **Enhanced Technology and Service Delivery:** By grouping technology leadership and service management under one umbrella, the federal government seeks to streamline digital transformation, improve cybersecurity, and deliver better public services through scalable, interoperable systems.

- **Improved Oversight and Accountability:** Assigning the Minister of Public Works and Government Services as the presiding officer ensures that these critical functions remain aligned with broader public service goals and are subject to political and ministerial oversight.

- **Support for Federal Modernization:** The units transferred are foundational to modernizing federal operations—particularly in areas such as data infrastructure, network services, and service delivery—making this transfer a key component of broader government digital transformation initiatives.

---

**Legal and Administrative Significance:**

- This regulation represents a significant reorganization within the federal public service, reflecting a shift toward **shared services models** common in large-scale government operations.
- It demonstrates the government’s commitment to **efficiency, cost savings, and service standardization** by removing silos and enabling cross-departmental collaboration.
- The use of the Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act underscores the constitutional authority of the federal government to restructure public service operations in the public interest.

---

**Conclusion:**

This regulation marks a pivotal moment in the evolution of federal public administration by transferring six key units responsible for technology, service delivery, and infrastructure management from the Department of Public Works and Government Services to **Shared Services Canada**. The Minister of Public Works and Government Services is appointed as the presiding officer, ensuring continuity and accountability. Effective August 4, 2011, the move supports a more unified, efficient, and modern federal government, laying the groundwork for improved digital services and operational performance across departments.
