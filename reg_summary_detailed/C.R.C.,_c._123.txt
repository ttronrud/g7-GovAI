**Detailed Summary of the Windsor Airport Zoning Regulations**

---

### **1. Regulation Title and Citation**
- The regulations are officially titled: **Windsor Airport Zoning Regulations**.
- They may be cited as such and are binding within the jurisdiction of the Province of Ontario.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Airport** | Refers specifically to **Windsor Airport**, located in Windsor, Ontario. |
| **Airport Reference Point (ARP)** | A fixed geographical point used as a reference elevation for zoning and height control. It is defined in **Part I of the Schedule** and is **deemed to be 606 feet above sea level** for all regulatory purposes. |
| **Strip** | A rectangular area of the airport‚Äôs landing surface, **1,000 feet wide**, specifically designated for aircraft takeoff and landing in a particular direction (e.g., Runway 7-25). |
| **Approach Surface** | An imaginary inclined plane that begins at the end of a strip and slopes upward from the strip‚Äôs end to a horizontal surface. The slope is defined by specific dimensions depending on the runway. |
| **Horizontal Surface** | An imaginary flat plane centered on the ARP, located **150 feet above its elevation** (i.e., 606 + 150 = 756 feet above sea level). This surface serves as the baseline for height restrictions. |
| **Transitional Surface** | An imaginary inclined plane extending outward from the outer edges of the strip and its approach surface, rising at a **1:7 slope** (1 foot vertically for every 7 feet horizontally) until it meets the horizontal surface or another transitional surface. |

---

### **3. Scope of Application**

- These regulations apply to **all lands**, including **public road allowances**, that are:
  - Adjacent to or in the **vicinity of Windsor Airport**.
  - More specifically described in **Part II of the Schedule**.

> This includes not only private property but also public roads and land buffers around the airport.

---

### **4. Height Restrictions (Prohibited Construction)**

No person may:
- Erect or construct any building, structure, or object;
- Add to an existing building, structure, or object;

**If the highest point of such structure exceeds the elevation of any of the following surfaces at the location of that point:**

#### **(a) Horizontal Surface**
- A defined perimeter line that forms a horizontal plane 150 feet above the ARP (i.e., 756 feet above sea level).
- The boundary of this surface is described in a detailed **legal description** (a compass traverse) starting from the **southerly angle of Lot 107** on Plan 1489 and proceeding through multiple intersections with roads, lot boundaries, and railway rights-of-way.
- It encircles key areas around the airport, including sections of Howard Avenue, Dougall Avenue, Stanley Street, Gladstone Avenue, Ypres Avenue, Walker Road, Tecumseh Road, Lauzon Road, and others.

#### **(b) Approach Surfaces**
- These are **inclined planes** extending from each end of certain runways:
  - **Runway 2-20 and 12-30**:
    - 500 feet wide on each side of the centerline at the strip ends.
    - Expands to 1,250 feet on each side at outer ends, which are **200 feet above the strip end elevation** and **10,000 feet horizontally** from the strip end.
  - **Runway 7-25**:
    - 500 feet wide at the ends.
    - Expands to **2,000 feet** on each side at outer ends (same 200 ft elevation and 10,000 ft horizontal distance).
- These surfaces are designed to ensure aircraft have clear visibility and unobstructed flight paths during approach and departure.

#### **(c) Transitional Surfaces**
- These are **inclined planes** rising from the outer lateral limits of the strips and their approach surfaces.
- Slope: **1 vertical foot per 7 horizontal feet** (1:7 ratio).
- Extend upward until they intersect the horizontal surface or another transitional surface.
- Designed to provide a smooth, gradual transition from the runway edge to the horizontal surface.

> All these surfaces are **illustrated and referenced** on **Plan No. 2586**, dated October 3, 1968, held in the Department of Transport in Ottawa.

---

### **5. Exception: Natural Growth**

- If a **natural object** (e.g., tree, bush, hill) on regulated land **exceeds the height** of any of the above surfaces:
  - The **Minister of Transport** may issue a **direction** requiring the **owner or occupier** to **remove or trim** the excessive growth.

> This provision allows for flexibility in dealing with natural features while still protecting aviation safety.

- This means the **current version is no longer in force** as of the date of revocation.

### **8. Purpose and Intent**

The primary objectives of these regulations were:

1. **Ensure aviation safety** by preventing obstructions to aircraft during takeoff, landing, and approach.
2. **Maintain clear flight paths** by limiting structures and natural features above specified elevation thresholds.
3. **Provide a legally defined, measurable boundary** for height control using precise geographic descriptions and elevation references.
4. **Allow flexibility** through the natural growth exception and ministerial direction.
