### **Context and Background**

- The **Office of Indian Residential Schools Resolution of Canada (OIRSRC)** was established in 2004 as part of Canada’s response to the legacy of the **Indian Residential School System**, a system of government-funded and church-operated schools that forcibly removed Indigenous children from their families between the 1830s and 1996, resulting in widespread physical, emotional, and cultural harm.
- The office was created to:
  - Develop and implement a **national framework for reconciliation**.
  - Lead the **process of redress** for survivors and their families.
  - Coordinate with Indigenous communities, governments, and other stakeholders.
  - Support the development of a **formal apology**, **compensation**, and **cultural healing initiatives**.

- Prior to this transfer, the office was under the authority of the President of the Queen's Privy Council, a largely symbolic and constitutional position. By transferring it to the Solicitor General, the government made a **strategic decision to place reconciliation work under a minister with direct policy and legislative authority**, particularly one with responsibility for public safety and justice.
