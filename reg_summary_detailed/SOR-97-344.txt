> **"CCFTA Textile and Apparel Extension of Benefit Order"**,  
> issued by the Minister of Finance on **July 4, 1997**, under **section 25.6a of the Customs Tariff**, and published in *S.C. 1997, c. 14, s. 51* and *R.S., c. 41 (3rd Supp.)*.

## üîç **Overview of the Regulation**

This regulation implements a **specific extension of tariff benefits** under the **Canada-Chile Free Trade Agreement (CCFTA)**. It allows certain textile and apparel goods imported from **Chile into Canada** to be **exempt from customs duties**, provided they meet strict origin and compliance requirements.

The Order is part of the broader framework of the CCFTA, which aims to reduce trade barriers and promote economic integration between Canada and Chile. This particular regulation focuses on **textile and apparel products** that are **originally produced or processed in Chile** using raw materials (fabric, yarn) sourced **outside the free trade area** (i.e., outside Canada and Chile).

### 1. **Definition of Key Terms**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Apparel** | Goods in Chapters 61 or 62 of the Customs Tariff that are cut or knit to shape and sewn or assembled in **Chile** from fabric or yarn produced or obtained **outside the free trade area** (Canada and Chile). |
| **Fabric and Made-Up Goods** | Includes: <br> - Cotton or man-made fiber fabrics and made-up textile goods in Chapters 52‚Äì55 (excluding those with 36% or more wool/fine animal hair); <br> - Goods in Chapters 58, 60, and 63 that are woven/knitted in Chile from yarn produced or obtained outside the free trade area, or from yarn spun in Chile from fiber sourced outside the free trade area; <br> - Specific finished goods under subheadings 5208.11 to 5208.29, etc., cut and sewn from fabrics produced outside the free trade area; <br> - Wool-based goods (with ‚â•36% wool/fine hair) in Chapters 51‚Äì55 and 58, 60, 63, if woven/knitted in Chile from yarn from outside the free trade area or spun in Chile from foreign fiber. |
| **Free Trade Area** | The geographic area comprising **Canada and Chile**. |
| **Spun Yarn** | Cotton or man-made fiber yarns (under headings 52.05‚Äì52.07, 55.09‚Äì55.11) that are **spun in Chile** from fiber (under 52.01‚Äì52.03, 55.01‚Äì55.07) **produced or obtained outside the free trade area**. |

> These definitions establish the **origin criteria** for eligibility under the tariff benefit.

### 2. **Scope of the Benefit**

- The **duty-free treatment** (tariff benefit) under the CCFTA is extended to:
  - **Apparel**
  - **Fabric and made-up goods**
  - **Spun yarn**

- **Only up to the annual quantitative limits** set in the CCFTA Annex C-00-B, Appendix 5.1, Schedules 5.B.1, 5.B.2, and 5.B.3.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Importance of Quantitative Limits**:  
> This means that even if goods meet all other criteria, the **total volume** of these goods that can be imported from Chile annually is capped. These limits prevent over-reliance on Chilean exports and maintain balance in trade.

---

### 3. **Conditions for Eligibility (Compliance Requirements)**

To qualify for the tariff benefit, importers or owners must satisfy **two key procedural and documentary conditions**:

#### ‚úÖ Condition 1: **Written Declaration by Importer**
- At the time the goods are:
  - Accounted for under **subsection 32(1), (3), or (5)** of the *Customs Act*, **or**
  - Subject to a **refund application under paragraph 74(1)(d)** of the *Customs Act*,
- The importer must submit a **written declaration** (in English or French) stating that:
  > *"The certification referred to in paragraph (b) is in the possession of the importer or owner."*

> This ensures that the required exporter certification is available and not lost or withheld.

#### ‚úÖ Condition 2: **Exporter Certification**
- At the time of **release of goods**, or **at any time after** the importer provides the declaration above, the importer must present:
  - A **certification from the exporter** (in English, French, or Spanish), containing:
    - The **statement specified in the form** (attached as a schedule),
    - All **requested information** (e.g., origin, production details, fiber source, etc.).
  - A **copy of the applicable invoice or sales agreement**.
- If the certification is in **Spanish**, it must be **translated into English or French** upon request by a customs officer.

> This certification is critical to verify that the goods:
> - Were produced in Chile,
> - Used raw materials from outside the free trade area,
> - Comply with the technical and origin rules of the CCFTA.

---

### 4. **Effective Date**

- The Order comes into force on the day that **Section 51 of the Canada-Chile Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act** (S.C. 1997, c. 14) comes into force.

> As noted in the note: *Regulations in force July 5, 1997* ‚Äî this means the regulation became effective on **July 5, 1997**.

### 5. **Exporter Certification Form (Summary)**

The form includes the following elements:

> **I, the exporter of the textile or apparel goods referred to in the attached invoice or sales agreement, hereby certify that those goods comply with the requirements specified in respect of those goods in Appendix 5.1 of Annex C-00-B to Chapter C of the Canada-Chile Free Trade Agreement.**

Required fields:
- **Name**
- **Title**
- **Company**
- **Telephone**
- **Fax**
- **Signature**
- **Date**

> This form serves as **legal proof of origin** and compliance with the CCFTA‚Äôs rules of origin.

## üéØ Purpose and Significance

This regulation:

1. **Promotes trade** between Canada and Chile by reducing tariffs on textiles and apparel.
2. **Ensures origin compliance** through strict documentation and verification.
3. **Protects Canadian industries** by limiting the volume of goods that can enter under duty-free terms (via quantitative limits).
4. **Supports the integrity of the free trade agreement** by preventing "non-originating" goods from benefiting from tariff reductions.

---

## ‚öñÔ∏è Key Legal and Trade Principles Enforced

- **Rules of Origin**: Goods must be produced or processed in Chile using foreign-origin raw materials.
- **Quantitative Restrictions**: Prevents abuse or overuse of tariff benefits.
- **Documentary Compliance**: Requires verifiable evidence (invoices, certifications) to ensure legitimacy.
- **Language Flexibility**: Allows Spanish certification with translation, accommodating Chile‚Äôs linguistic reality.

## üîç Summary in One Paragraph

This regulation extends duty-free treatment under the Canada-Chile Free Trade Agreement to apparel, fabric and made-up textile goods, and spun yarn imported from Chile into Canada‚Äî**only up to annual quantitative limits**. To qualify, goods must originate in Chile using raw materials sourced outside the free trade area (Canada and Chile), and importers must provide a written declaration confirming the availability of an exporter certification. At release or upon request, the importer must submit a **certification from the exporter** (in English, French, or Spanish) with supporting documentation, including an invoice or sales agreement. If the certification is in Spanish, it must be translated. The Order takes effect on **July 5, 1997**, and is designed to ensure **compliance with origin rules**, **protect Canadian interests**, and **maintain fair trade balance**.
