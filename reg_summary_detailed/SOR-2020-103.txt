**Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Order Amending the Canadian Chicken Marketing Levies Order**

---

### **1. Background and Context**

This regulation, officially titled *Order Amending the Canadian Chicken Marketing Levies Order*, is a formal administrative decision issued by **Chicken Farmers of Canada (CFC)** under the authority granted by federal legislation. It amends the *Canadian Chicken Marketing Levies Order* to adjust or expand the framework for how marketing levies are collected, administered, and used in the Canadian chicken industry.

The regulation is rooted in a broader legal and policy framework designed to support the stability and sustainability of the domestic chicken market through coordinated marketing and pricing mechanisms.

---

### **2. Legal Authority and Procedural Framework**

#### **a. Establishment of Chicken Farmers of Canada (CFC)**
- The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal government) established **Chicken Farmers of Canada (CFC)** via the *Chicken Farmers of Canada Proclamation* under **subsection 16(1)b of the Farm Products Agencies Act**.
- This Proclamation legally recognizes CFC as a designated agency responsible for managing the marketing and economic interests of Canadian chicken producers.

#### **b. Power to Implement a Marketing Plan**
- CFC has been granted the authority to develop and implement a **marketing plan** for the Canadian chicken industry, as permitted by the Proclamation.
- This marketing plan is intended to ensure stable prices, promote market access, and protect producers from market volatility.

#### **c. Regulatory Classification and Approval Process**
- The proposed amendment falls under a specific class of orders governed by **paragraph 7(1)(d)d of the Farm Products Agencies Act**.
- Under **section 2 of the Agencies’ Orders and Regulations Approval Order**, such orders are subject to review and approval by the **National Farm Products Council (NFPC)**.
- The amendment was formally submitted to the NFPC in accordance with **paragraph 22(1)(f)** of the Farm Products Agencies Act.

#### **d. NFPC Approval**
- After a thorough review, the **National Farm Products Council approved** the proposed amendment.
- The Council concluded that the amendment is **necessary for the effective implementation of CFC’s marketing plan**.
- This approval is critical because it confirms that the proposed changes serve a legitimate and essential purpose in the industry’s economic management.

---

### **3. Purpose of the Amendment**

The primary objective of this Order is to **authorize Chicken Farmers of Canada to implement and administer the marketing levies** for the Canadian chicken sector more effectively.

Key aspects of the amendment include:
- Clarifying or adjusting the **rules governing the collection, allocation, and use of marketing levies**.
- Ensuring that funds collected from producers are used in a transparent, accountable, and strategic manner to support market stability, consumer access, and producer income.
- Enabling CFC to respond to market conditions, such as supply fluctuations, international competition, or price volatility.

---

### **4. Legal Basis for the Order**

The Order is issued under:
- **Paragraph 22(1)(f)** and **(g)** of the *Farm Products Agencies Act*: These provisions empower CFC to make orders (including amendments) after receiving approval from the NFPC.
- **Section 12g of the Schedule to the Chicken Farmers of Canada Proclamation**: This section provides the specific legal authority for CFC to issue the amendment order.

Thus, the Order is not merely administrative—it is a legally binding instrument grounded in federal statutes and procedural safeguards.

---

### **5. Effective Date**

- The amended Order comes into force on **May 10, 2020**.
- This date allows stakeholders (producers, processors, regulators, and consumers) time to adjust operations, understand new requirements, and comply with the updated levy framework.

---

The Order references several key statutes and regulatory instruments, including:
- *Farm Products Agencies Act* (FPA Act)
- *Agencies’ Orders and Regulations Approval Order*
- *Chicken Farmers of Canada Proclamation*
- *Canadian Chicken Marketing Levies Order* (original and amended)
- Relevant statutory amendments such as:
  - S.C. 2015, c. 3, s. 85 and s. 88

These references confirm the historical and legal continuity of the regulatory framework.

---

### **7. Significance and Implications**

- **Industry Stability**: The amendment strengthens the ability of CFC to manage market dynamics and protect producers from price crashes or surges.
- **Transparency and Accountability**: By requiring NFPC approval, the process ensures that changes are not arbitrary and are aligned with broader agricultural interests.
- **Producers’ Interests**: Farmers benefit from a more predictable and responsive system that helps stabilize incomes and supports long-term investment in the sector.
- **Consumer Impact**: Stable pricing and efficient marketing help ensure affordable and accessible chicken products for consumers.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation represents a critical step in the operationalization of Canada’s chicken marketing system. By amending the *Canadian Chicken Marketing Levies Order*, Chicken Farmers of Canada, with proper legal authority and after rigorous oversight by the National Farm Products Council, enhances its capacity to implement a strategic marketing plan. The amendment ensures that marketing levies are used effectively to support the economic health of the chicken industry, maintain market stability, and serve both producers and consumers.

The Order is a well-structured, legally sound, and procedurally transparent exercise of delegated authority, reflecting the federal government’s commitment to supporting agricultural sectors through sound regulatory governance.
