**Detailed Summary of the *Saskatchewan Hog Information (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations***

---

### **1. Regulation Title and Citation**
The regulation is formally titled:  
**"Saskatchewan Hog Information (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations"**  
It may be cited as such and governs the collection, reporting, and transparency requirements related to the marketing of hogs in Saskatchewan, specifically in **interprovincial trade and export transactions**.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

The regulation provides clear definitions of key terms used throughout:

- **Act**: Refers to *The Natural Products Marketing Act, 1972* of Saskatchewan (Loi). This foundational legislation establishes the legal framework for hog marketing in the province.

- **Assembly Yard**: A designated premises where producers can deliver their hogs for temporary holding, processing, or distribution before slaughter. These serve as central hubs in the hog supply chain.

- **Assembly Yard Operator**: The individual or entity (owner or operator) responsible for managing an assembly yard. This includes those who manage operations, storage, and distribution of hogs.

- **Commission**: The *Saskatchewan Hog Marketing Commission*, established under the Natural Products Marketing Act and operating according to the *Saskatchewan Hog Marketing Plan*. The Commission is responsible for overseeing compliance, ensuring transparency, and regulating hog marketing practices.

- **Hog**: Any member of the species *Sus scrofa L.* (domestic pig) that is produced within Saskatchewan. This includes all stages of the pig from birth to slaughter.

- **Plan**: The *Saskatchewan Hog Marketing Plan*, which is established and periodically amended under the Act. The Plan sets out the rules, objectives, and mechanisms for managing hog marketing, including pricing, quotas, and market access.

- **Processor**: Any individual or business engaged in the processing of hogs (e.g., slaughter, rendering, or manufacturing using hogs). This includes those involved in transforming hogs into meat products or other by-products.

- **Producer**: A broad category that includes:
  - Persons directly involved in producing or feeding hogs and making them available for slaughter;
  - Employers of such persons;
  - Individuals who have a contractual right (e.g., via lease or agreement) to a share of hogs produced by another;
  - Individuals who take possession of hogs as security for a debt (e.g., in a loan agreement).

- **Small Processor**: A processor that, in the prior calendar year, processed **less than 2% of all hogs slaughtered in Saskatchewan**, as reported by the **Department of Agriculture of Canada**. This classification is used to identify smaller operations that may have different reporting obligations than larger processors.

---

### **3. Scope of Application**

These regulations apply **only** to:
- The **marketing of hogs** in **interprovincial trade** (sales between provinces) and **export trade** (sales outside of Canada);
- Persons and property located **within the Province of Saskatchewan**.

ðŸ‘‰ *Note*: The regulations do not apply to domestic sales within Saskatchewan or to non-marketing activities such as breeding, feeding, or transportation unless those activities are directly tied to interprovincial or export marketing.

---

### **4. Recordkeeping and Reporting Obligations**

#### **General Requirement for All Hog Marketing Participants**
Every person engaged in the marketing of hogs must:
- **Keep complete and accurate books and records** of all marketing activities related to hogs (e.g., sales, purchases, quantities, prices, dates, and parties involved);
- **Retain these records for at least three years** from the date of the transaction.

#### **Inspection and Access Rights**
- Any **member or authorized representative of the Commission** may:
  - **Request** the production of books and records;
  - **Inspect** any premises used for hog marketing at any reasonable time.

#### **Duty to Permit Inspection**
Every person in possession or control of premises used for hog marketing must:
- **Allow inspection** by Commission members or representatives;
- **Provide reasonable information** about hog marketing activities as requested.

ðŸ‘‰ This ensures transparency, accountability, and enables the Commission to monitor compliance with marketing rules, detect fraud, and ensure fair market practices.

---

### **5. Specific Reporting Requirements**

#### **(a) Weekly Reporting by Small Processors**
Every **small processor** must submit a **weekly statement** to the Commission containing the following information:

1. **Producer Information**:
   - Name, address, and **farm registration number** of every producer from whom the processor purchased hogs.

2. **Producer or Consumer Information (for custom processing)**:
   - Name, address, and (where applicable) farm registration number of:
     - The producer or consumer on whose behalf the hog was custom-processed.

3. **Hog Details**:
   - Quantity, variety, grade, class, or size of the hogs purchased or processed.

4. **Pricing Information**:
   - The price paid for each batch or unit of hogs.

ðŸ‘‰ This requirement enhances traceability in the supply chain, particularly for small-scale processors, and helps the Commission monitor pricing trends, market dynamics, and potential market distortions.

#### **(b) Weekly Reporting by Assembly Yard Operators (for Sow Auctions)**
Every operator of an **assembly yard who conducts auctions of sows** must submit a **weekly statement** indicating:
- The **number of sows sold** at each auction.

ðŸ‘‰ This provision supports monitoring of breeding stock movement, which is critical for managing supply, preventing overproduction, and ensuring sustainable hog production in the province.

---

### **6. Purpose and Objectives of the Regulations**

The primary goals of these regulations are:

- To **ensure transparency and accountability** in the interprovincial and export hog marketing system;
- To **promote fair competition** and prevent market manipulation;
- To **enable the Commission to monitor supply and demand**, price trends, and producer/processor relationships;
- To **support data-driven decision-making** in the development and amendment of the Hog Marketing Plan;
- To **protect producers and consumers** by providing traceability and oversight in the marketing chain.

---

### **7. Enforcement and Compliance**

- The Commission has the authority to inspect premises and require documentation.
- Non-compliance with recordkeeping or reporting obligations may result in penalties or enforcement actions, although specific penalties are not detailed in this regulation (they may be outlined in the underlying *Natural Products Marketing Act*).
- The reporting system is designed to be **practical and proportionate**, especially for small processors and assembly yard operators, recognizing their operational scale.

---

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Saskatchewan Hog Information (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations* establish a robust, transparent, and accountable system for tracking and managing hog marketing activities outside of Saskatchewan. By requiring detailed recordkeeping, regular reporting, and inspection rights, the regulations ensure that the hog market operates fairly, sustainably, and in alignment with the goals of the *Saskatchewan Hog Marketing Plan*. The focus on small processors and sow auctions reflects a balanced approach that supports both large-scale and small-scale producers while maintaining market integrity.
