## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Agreement on Social Security between Canada and the Republic of Trinidad and Tobago**

### üîç **Overview**
This agreement establishes a bilateral social security framework between **Canada** and the **Republic of Trinidad and Tobago**, aimed at ensuring **cooperation and mutual recognition of social security rights** for individuals who work or reside in both jurisdictions. It facilitates the **recognition, portability, and totalization of contributions and residence periods** for key benefits such as retirement, disability, survivors‚Äô and death benefits.

The agreement entered into force on **July 1, 1999**, following ratification by both governments and compliance with the procedural requirements under the **Old Age Security Act** and the **Agreement on Social Security** signed on April 9, 1997.

### 2. **Scope of Application**
The agreement applies to:
- The specified legislation in both countries.
- Amendments, supplements, consolidations, or replacements of those laws (unless objected to by the other Party within 3 months of entry into force).
- **New categories of beneficiaries or benefits** unless one Party objects within 3 months of their introduction.

> **Exception**: The agreement applies only to persons who are or have been subject to the legislation of one or both Parties, or to individuals deriving rights from such persons.

---

### 3. **Eligibility and Rights of Persons Covered**
Any person described in **Article III** (those subject to legislation of either country) shall:
- Be **subject to the obligations** of the other Party‚Äôs legislation.
- Be **eligible for benefits** under that legislation **on the same conditions as citizens** of that country.

> This ensures **equal treatment** and **non-discrimination** in access to social security benefits.

---

### 4. **Non-Reduction of Benefits Due to Residency**
Unless otherwise specified:
- Benefits payable under the legislation of a Party to a person residing in the other Party‚Äôs territory **shall not be reduced, suspended, or cancelled** solely due to residency in the other country.
- These benefits shall be **paid in the territory of the other Party**.

> This ensures **portability** of benefits across borders.

---

### 5. **Application of Legislation Based on Employment Type**
#### a) **Employed Persons**
- An employed person working in the territory of one Party shall be subject **only** to the legislation of that Party.
- **Exception**: If the same employer provides services in both territories, the person is subject only to the legislation of the **first Party** (where the employment began).
- **Assignment Limitation**: For assignments (temporary work), coverage under one Party‚Äôs legislation may not exceed **60 months** without prior approval from both competent authorities.

#### b) **Self-Employed Persons**
- A self-employed person who ordinarily resides in one Party and works in the other (or both) is subject only to the legislation of the **first Party** (where they reside).

#### c) **Work on Continental Shelf Installations**
- A person working on installations in the continental shelf (e.g., for seabed or mineral exploration) is treated as if the installation is located in the territory of the Party whose seabed rights it operates under.

> **Definition**: The continental shelf includes areas beyond territorial seas where a Party has rights under international law or domestic law.

#### d) **Seafarers**
- A seafarer who would otherwise be subject to both Parties‚Äô legislation is subject to:
  - **Canada**, if they **ordinarily reside in Canada**.
  - **Trinidad and Tobago**, otherwise.

#### e) **Government Employment**
- An employed person performing government duties in the other Party‚Äôs territory is subject **only** to the legislation of the **other Party** if:
  - They are a **citizen** of that Party, or
  - They **ordinarily reside** there.
- However, they may **elect** to be subject only to the legislation of the first Party (where they reside), even if not a citizen.

> This allows flexibility in employment-based eligibility.

#### f) **Common Agreement on Exceptions**
- The competent authorities of both Parties may, by mutual agreement, modify the application of these rules for specific individuals or categories.

### 6. **Creditable Periods and Totalization Rules**

#### a) **Totalization of Creditable Periods**
If a person does not meet the required creditable periods under one Party‚Äôs legislation, their entitlement is determined by **totalizing**:
- Creditable periods under both Parties,
- Creditable periods under a **third State** with which both Parties have a separate social security agreement (totalization allowed).

> This enables individuals to build up sufficient contribution history across borders.

#### b) **Application of Totalization to Specific Benefits**

##### i) **Old Age Security Act (OASA) ‚Äì Canada**
- **Periods in Trinidad and Tobago**:
  - If a person is subject to the **Canada Pension Plan (CPP)** or a provincial pension plan during a period in Trinidad and Tobago, that time counts as **residence in Canada** for the person and their spouse/dependants (who are not covered by Trinidad and Tobago employment/self-employment).
- **Periods in Canada**:
  - If a person is subject to Trinidad and Tobago legislation during a period in Canada, that time **does not** count as residence in Canada for the person or their dependants (unless covered by CPP or provincial plan).
- **Exception for Persons from Trinidad and Tobago**:
  - If a Trinidadian resident works in Canada and is subject to CPP or provincial plan, that period counts as **residence in Canada** for purposes of OASA.

##### ii) **Canada Pension Plan (CPP) ‚Äì Canada**
- A **calendar year** in which the person has at least **13 creditable weeks** under Trinidad and Tobago‚Äôs legislation is considered as **one year of contributions** under the CPP.

##### iii) **Trinidad and Tobago Retirement Benefits**
- **Year 1972** (creditable under CPP): counts as **40 weeks** of contributions under Trinidad and Tobago legislation.
- **Years from January 1, 1973 onward** (creditable under CPP): count as **52 weeks** of contributions under Trinidad and Tobago legislation.
- **Weeks under OASA (post-April 10, 1972)** not part of CPP creditable periods: count as **one week** of contributions under Trinidad and Tobago legislation.

##### iv) **Survivors‚Äô Benefits (Trinidad and Tobago)**
- Same rules as retirement benefits:
  - 1972: 40 weeks
  - 1973 onwards: 52 weeks

> These rules ensure that contributions made in Canada (especially pre-1973) are recognized as equivalent to contributions in Trinidad and Tobago.

#### c) **Limitations on Totalization**
- Creditable periods under Canada are **only counted to the extent necessary** to establish entitlement to a benefit under Trinidad and Tobago legislation.
- **Exception**: Totalization does **not apply** for:
  - Disability pensions under the Canada Pension Plan.
  - Invalidity benefits under Trinidad and Tobago legislation.

> This protects the integrity of disability and invalidity systems.

> This prevents minimal or non-contributory claims.
