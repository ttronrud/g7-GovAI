### üîπ **2. Purpose of the Surtax**
- To impose a **25% surtax** on certain **steel and aluminum products** imported from the **United States**.
- The surtax is applied **on top of the standard customs duty** determined under sections 47 to 55 of the *Customs Act*.
- This measure is intended to:
  - Support domestic manufacturing and industry,
  - Address concerns about trade imbalances and over-reliance on U.S. steel and aluminum,
  - Align with broader trade policy objectives under **CUSMA** and Canada‚Äôs strategic industrial goals.

### üîπ **3. Scope of Goods Subject to the Surtax**

The following goods are **subject to a 25% surtax** if they originate in the United States:

#### ‚úÖ **(A) Goods classified under tariff items in Schedule 1 or Schedule 2**
- These are primary categories of steel and aluminum products.
- The surtax applies directly to goods falling under these schedules.

#### ‚úÖ **(B) Goods classified under tariff items in Schedule 3**
- These items are **otherwise classifiable** under a tariff heading in **Schedule 1**.
- Example: A product may be technically classified under a Schedule 3 item but is actually a steel product that fits within a Schedule 1 category.
- In such cases, the surtax still applies due to the "otherwise classifiable" rule.

#### ‚úÖ **(C) Goods classified under tariff items in Schedule 4**
- These items are **otherwise classifiable** under a tariff heading in **Schedule 2**.
- This ensures that even if a product is classified under a less specific heading, it still falls under the scope of the surtax if it is essentially a steel or aluminum product.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: The term ‚Äúotherwise classifiable‚Äù means that despite the specific classification, the product‚Äôs nature and composition align with a product in Schedule 1 or 2.

### üîπ **4. Definition of "Originating in the United States"**

- For a good to be considered "originating in the United States" for the purposes of this surtax:
  - It must be **eligible to be marked as goods of the United States**.
  - This eligibility is determined according to the **Determination of Country of Origin for the Purpose of Marking Goods (CUSMA Countries) Regulations**.
- This ensures that only goods that meet the **CUSMA origin rules** (e.g., sufficient processing or transformation in the U.S.) are subject to the surtax.

> üìå This prevents non-U.S. origin goods from being unfairly targeted.

### üîπ **5. Exemptions from the Surtax**

The following goods are **exempt** from the 25% surtax, regardless of their classification:

#### ‚ùå (A) Goods under Chapter 98 or 99 of the List of Tariff Provisions:
- If the tariff item is **not listed in Schedule 3** (for Chapter 98) or **Schedule 4** (for Chapter 99), even if the product would otherwise fall under a Schedule 1 or 2 item.
- This exemption applies to:
  - **Chapter 98**: Goods in transit, spare parts, or temporary entries.
  - **Chapter 99**: Miscellaneous goods, including those for personal use or non-commercial purposes.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è This means that **only specific items** (those listed in Schedules 3 or 4) are subject to the surtax ‚Äî others are excluded.

#### ‚ùå (B) Goods in Transit to Canada
- Any goods **in transit** to Canada **on the day the Order comes into force** (March 13, 2025) are **not subject** to the surtax.
- This provides a grace period for goods already en route.

#### ‚ùå (C) Personal Imports from the U.S. via Campobello Island, New Brunswick
- Goods imported from the U.S. at **Campobello Island** are exempt **if**:
  1. The importer **ordinarily resides** on Campobello Island,
  2. The person is **returning after an absence of less than 24 hours**,
  3. The goods are **in their personal possession or form part of their baggage**, and
  4. The goods are for **personal or household use**.

> ‚úÖ This exemption supports the principle of **personal travel and non-commercial imports**.
