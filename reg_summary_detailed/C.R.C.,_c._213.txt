**Detailed Summary of the *Ontario Greenhouse Vegetable Information (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations***

---

### **1. Regulation Title and Citation**
The regulation is officially titled:  
**Ontario Greenhouse Vegetable Information (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations**  
It may be cited as such and governs the marketing, reporting, and handling of greenhouse vegetables in Ontario, specifically in interprovincial and export trade.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Appointed Shipper** | A person who holds a valid and current *certificate of appointment* issued by the **Commodity Board** under the *Ontario Greenhouse Vegetable Appointed Shippers’ Procedures (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations*. This role gives the shipper authority to act on behalf of producers in selling or transporting greenhouse vegetables. |
| **Commodity Board** | The **Ontario Greenhouse Vegetable Producers’ Marketing Board** (OGVPMB), which oversees the marketing, pricing, and regulation of greenhouse vegetables in Ontario. It acts as a regulatory and administrative body responsible for ensuring fair trade practices and market stability. |
| **Greenhouse Vegetables** | Specifically includes **tomatoes, cucumbers, and lettuce** grown in Ontario within a greenhouse or any enclosed structure (e.g., under glass, plastic, or other materials) designed to control temperature and protect plants. |
| **Producer** | Any individual or entity engaged in the actual cultivation and production of greenhouse vegetables. |
| **Product** | Any quantity of greenhouse vegetables, regardless of size or volume. |
| **Shipper** | Any person who offers for sale, sells, receives, assembles, packs, ships for sale, or transports a greenhouse vegetable product. **Exclusions** include: <br> - Servants driving vehicles owned by producers or shippers; <br> - Railway companies; <br> - Individuals transporting products by motor vehicle as agents of a producer. |

> Note: The term "shipper" is central to this regulation and defines who is responsible for compliance with reporting and documentation requirements.

---

### **3. Scope of Application**
These regulations apply **only** to:
- The **marketing** of greenhouse vegetables in **interprovincial trade** (sales between provinces) and **export trade** (sales outside of Canada).
- Persons and property **located within the Province of Ontario**.

> This means the rules do not apply to domestic retail sales within Ontario or to non-greenhouse vegetable products.

---

### **4. Reporting Requirements**

#### **A. Mandatory Reporting by Shippers**
Every shipper must provide the Commodity Board with specific information as outlined in **Column I** of the Schedule, using the method specified in **Column II**, and submitting it by the deadline in **Column III**.

- The information likely includes details such as:
  - Volume and variety of vegetables shipped
  - Dates of shipment
  - Shipper and producer details
  - Sales data
- The purpose is to ensure accurate market tracking, price monitoring, and transparency in the supply chain.

#### **B. Reporting by Producers Selling Outside Appointed Shippers**
If a producer sells greenhouse vegetables **without** using an appointed shipper, they must:
- Report all such sales to the Commodity Board on forms provided by the Board.
- Deliver the report **no later than Wednesday of the week following the week in which the product was sold**.

> This ensures that even non-shipper sales are tracked and accounted for in the overall market data.

---

### **5. Receipt Requirements Upon Delivery**

Every shipper must issue a **written receipt** to the producer at the time of delivery containing the following:

1. **Name and address of the shipper**  
2. **Name of the producer** who delivered the product  
3. **Date of delivery**  
4. **Number and type of containers** for each variety of product  
5. **Weight of all tomatoes** and **number of all cucumbers** delivered

> This receipt serves as a formal record of transaction and is essential for traceability and dispute resolution.

#### **Optional Stamping Clause**
The receipt may be stamped with one of the following:
- “Price subject to change in accordance with any order of The Ontario Greenhouse Vegetable Producers’ Marketing Board”  
- “Prix sujet à modification conformément à toute ordonnance de The Ontario Greenhouse Vegetable Producers’ Marketing Board”

> This informs buyers that the price may be adjusted based on decisions made by the Commodity Board (e.g., price controls, market adjustments).

---

### **6. Availability of Sales Invoices**

- **Copies of all sales invoices** must be made available for inspection by a **Commodity Board representative** at any time during **normal business hours**, starting **after 10 a.m. on the day following the sale**.

> This promotes transparency and allows the Board to verify sales data and ensure compliance with reporting obligations.

---

### **7. Claim Notification by Purchasers**

- Every purchaser who receives a product must **notify the shipper within six hours** of receiving the product at the agreed delivery location of any claim for compensation (e.g., due to damage, spoilage, or quality issues).

> The six-hour window is a strict deadline designed to ensure prompt action and prevent delays in resolving disputes.

---

### **8. Shipper’s Obligations Upon Receiving a Claim**

When a claim is received, the shipper must:
1. **Notify the producer** of the claim within 24 hours of receiving the purchaser’s notice.
2. **Notify the Commodity Board** of the claim and **complete and deliver a claim form** provided by the Board **within 24 hours of the close of business** on the day the notice was received**.

> This establishes a clear chain of communication and ensures that claims are formally documented and processed by the Board.

---

### **9. Evidence Required Before Claim Approval**

Before the Commodity Board approves a payment for a claim, the shipper must:
- Deliver to the Board the **original manifest** prepared at the time of shipment (which includes details of the shipment).
- Provide **evidence satisfactory to the Board** that the product was **cooled in accordance with the Ontario Greenhouse Vegetable Handling (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations**.

> Cooling is critical to preserving quality and preventing spoilage, especially during interprovincial and export transport. Compliance ensures food safety and maintains product integrity.

---

### **10. Settlement Reporting**

On the day of any **settlement of a claim** (whether paid or not), the shipper must:
- Furnish the Commodity Board with **full written particulars** of the settlement.
- Include a **copy of the inspection certificate** issued by an inspector under the **Canada Agricultural Products Standards Act**.

> This ensures that all claims are properly documented, inspected, and verified for compliance with national food safety and quality standards.

---

### **Purpose and Objectives of the Regulations**

These regulations are designed to:
- Ensure **transparency and accountability** in the marketing of greenhouse vegetables.
- Promote **fair pricing and market stability** through centralized reporting and oversight by the Commodity Board.
- Maintain **product quality and safety** through strict handling and cooling requirements.
- Facilitate **traceability** from producer to buyer, which supports quality control, dispute resolution, and regulatory compliance.
- Protect both producers and consumers by establishing clear procedures for sales, claims, and record-keeping.

---

### **Key Implications for Stakeholders**

| Stakeholder | Key Responsibilities |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Producers** | Report all non-shipper sales; maintain accurate records; ensure product is properly cooled. |
| **Appointed Shippers** | Issue receipts; report sales; manage claims; provide manifests and inspection certificates; comply with reporting timelines. |
| **Purchasers** | Notify shippers of claims within 6 hours of delivery. |
| **Commodity Board** | Oversees compliance; receives reports and claims; verifies data; ensures fair market practices. |

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Ontario Greenhouse Vegetable Information (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations* form a comprehensive framework for the **regulated marketing** of tomatoes, cucumbers, and lettuce grown in Ontario. By mandating detailed reporting, standardized documentation, and strict procedures for claims and product handling, the regulations aim to ensure **market integrity, quality control, and equitable trade practices** in both interprovincial and export markets.

They reflect a balance between enabling efficient trade and protecting producers, shippers, and consumers through transparency, traceability, and adherence to quality standards. The rules are particularly important in a perishable agricultural product sector where timely and accurate data are essential to managing supply chains and responding to market fluctuations.

--- 

**Note**: These regulations operate in conjunction with other related legislation, such as the *Ontario Greenhouse Vegetable Handling (Interprovincial and Export) Regulations* and the *Canada Agricultural Products Standards Act*, to ensure full compliance with food safety and handling standards.
