**Detailed Summary of the Fenner Dunlop (Bracebridge) Inc. Remission Order (S.C. 1997, c. 36)**

---

### **1. Legal Authority and Issuance**
- **Issuing Body**: Her Excellency the Governor General in Council.
- **Recommendation**: Made on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance.
- **Legal Basis**: Pursuant to **Section 115 of the Customs Tariff Act**.
- **Purpose**: To grant a remission (i.e., a partial or full refund) of customs duties paid or payable on specific imported goods.

---

### **2. Scope of the Remission**
- **Goods Covered**: Certain **woven fabrics** classified under **tariff item 5906.99.22** of the Customs Tariff.
- **Importer**: **Fenner Dunlop (Bracebridge) Inc.**
- **Time Period**:  
  The remission applies to imports made into Canada during the period from **January 14, 1999, to May 19, 2004**.

---

### **3. Conditions for Remission**
The remission is **not automatic** and is subject to the following conditions:

1. **Use in Manufacturing Conveyor Belts**  
   The woven fabrics must have been, or will be, used **in the manufacture of conveyor belts** by Fenner Dunlop (Bracebridge) Inc.  
   → This condition ensures that the remission is tied to a specific, legitimate industrial application, supporting the principle that duties are only waived when the goods serve a domestic manufacturing purpose.

2. **Timely Claim Submission**  
   A claim for remission must be submitted to the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** (the relevant minister responsible for customs and trade matters) **within twelve months** of the Order coming into force.  
   → This establishes a strict deadline to ensure accountability and timely processing.

3. **Submission of Evidence to CBSA**  
   The importer must provide the **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)** with any evidence or information demonstrating entitlement to remission under this Order.  
   → This requirement ensures transparency and supports verification of compliance with the conditions.

4. **No Prior Claims for Refund or Drawback**  
   No claim for **refund or drawback** under the **Customs Act** or the **Customs Tariff** has been made with respect to the goods in question.  
   → This prevents double claims and ensures that the remission is granted only where no other remedy has already been pursued.

---

### **4. Effective Date**
- The Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** with the appropriate government office (typically the Canada Gazette or official registry).  
  → This ensures the Order is legally effective upon formal publication and registration.

---

### **5. Key Regulatory Principles Underlying the Order**
- **Industrial Justification**: The remission is granted based on a specific use (conveyor belt manufacturing), which aligns with policies promoting domestic industrial activity and economic efficiency.
- **Administrative Accountability**: The conditions (timely claims, evidence submission, no prior claims) ensure that the remission is not abused and is properly monitored.
- **Compliance with Tariff Provisions**: The Order operates within the framework of Section 115 of the Customs Tariff Act, which allows for remissions in cases of specific economic or industrial benefit.

---

### **6. Implications and Significance**
- **For Fenner Dunlop (Bracebridge) Inc.**:  
  This Order provides financial relief by refunding customs duties paid on specific woven fabrics used in the production of conveyor belts—a key product in industrial and infrastructure sectors.
- **For Canadian Trade Policy**:  
  Demonstrates the government’s use of targeted remission mechanisms to support domestic manufacturing, particularly in industries reliant on specialized textiles.
- **For CBSA and the Minister**:  
  Establishes a clear administrative pathway for reviewing and processing remission claims, with defined timelines and documentation requirements.

---

### **Conclusion**
This remission order is a targeted, conditionally granted refund of customs duties for specific woven fabrics used in conveyor belt manufacturing by Fenner Dunlop (Bracebridge) Inc. It reflects a balance between supporting domestic industry and maintaining fiscal discipline through strict eligibility criteria and procedural safeguards. The Order remains enforceable only if all specified conditions are met and claims are submitted within the prescribed timeframe.
