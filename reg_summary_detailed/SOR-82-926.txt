# **Chrysler Canada Ltd. Regulations, 1982**  
*(SOR/80-453, 1980 Canada Gazette Part II, p. 2214)*

---

## **1. Purpose and Background**

This regulation was issued by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Industry, Trade and Commerce**, under **Industry, Trade and Commerce Vote 1a of Appropriation Act No. 1, 1980–81**.

It serves to:
- **Revoke** the previous *Chrysler Canada Ltd. Regulations* made by Order in Council P.C. 1980-1621 (June 13, 1980).
- **Establish new insurance provisions** for loans made by private lenders to **Chrysler Canada Ltd.** during a specific period, aimed at supporting key restructuring and investment projects in Canada.

The primary objective is to **ensure financial stability and operational viability** of Chrysler Canada Ltd. and its associated entities (especially DCI Diesel Canada Inc.) during a critical period of plant conversion and retooling, particularly at the Windsor Assembly and Engine Plants.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Chrysler Canada Ltd.** | A Canadian corporation incorporated under that name, with its registered office in Windsor, Ontario. |
| **Chrysler Corporation** | The U.S.-based parent company incorporated in Delaware, headquartered in Highland Park, Michigan. |
| **DCI Diesel Canada Inc.** | A Canadian company in which Chrysler Canada Ltd. holds 97% equity and Perkins Engines Canada Ltd. holds 3%. |
| **Minister** | Refers to the Minister of Industry, Trade and Commerce. |
| **Private Lender** | Any lender approved by the Minister who is *not*: <br> - The Government of Canada <br> - A provincial government <br> - An agency of any such government (or a company effectively controlled by them) <br> - A municipal corporation |

---

## **3. Scope and Authority**

- The Minister has authority to provide **insurance for loans** (up to **$200 million in aggregate**) made by **private lenders** to **Chrysler Canada Ltd.**
- This power is derived from **Industry, Trade and Commerce Vote 1a of the Appropriation Act 1980–81**.
- Insurance is **not available to government entities or municipal corporations** — only to private lenders meeting strict criteria.

---

## **4. Conditions for Loan Insurance**

Any insurance agreement between the Government of Canada and a private lender must meet the following **mandatory conditions**:

### **4.1 Insurance Fee**
- The private lender pays an **annual insurance fee of 1%** of the total amount of insurance in effect at any time.

---

### **4.2 Use of Insured Loans**
The loan funds must be used exclusively for one of two major projects:

| Project | Maximum Amount | Purpose |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| **Windsor Assembly Plant Conversion** | Up to **$150 million** | Convert the plant into a **van/wagon production facility** to begin public sales in **autumn 1983**. |
| **Windsor Engine Plant Conversion** | Up to **$50 million** | Retool and reequip the engine plant to produce **240,000 diesel engines per year** by **December 31, 1985**. |

> *Note: These are the only permitted uses of the insured funds.*

---

### **4.3 Timing of Insurance Agreements**
- All loan insurance agreements must be entered into **on or after January 1, 1983**, and **no later than September 1, 1984**.

---

### **4.4 Repayment Schedule**
- **Windsor Assembly Plant loan** (van/wagon project):  
  Repaid in full on **April 30, 1986** (over a 5-year period).
- **Windsor Engine Plant loan** (diesel engine project):  
  Repaid in full on **December 31, 1986** (over a 5-year period).

---

### **4.5 Business and Employment Conditions**

#### a) **Operating and Financing Plan**
- Chrysler Canada Ltd. must operate under an **Operating and Financing Plan approved by the Minister**.

#### b) **Employment Levels**
- **1980–1981**: Employment level must be **9%** of Chrysler Corporation’s U.S. employment.
- **1982–1986**: Employment level must be **at least 11%** of U.S. employment.
- **1987–1988**: Average employment must be **at least 11%**, with **no level below 9%** in either year.

> *This ensures job preservation and economic stability during restructuring.*

#### c) **Diesel Engine Plant Employment Target**
- The diesel engine plant (Windsor Engine Plant) must reach **1,000 employees by 1988**.
- This level must be **maintained until the later of 1990 or the repayment of the engine plant loan**.

---

### **4.6 Investment Requirement**
- Chrysler Canada Ltd. must invest a **total of $821 million** in:
  - Windsor Assembly Plant
  - Windsor Engine Plant
  - Other Canadian operations
- This investment must occur between **1980 and 1986**.

---

### **4.7 Loan Restructuring with Bankers**
Chrysler Canada Ltd. must reach an agreement with its bankers to restructure existing loans, including:

- **Principal repayment postponed until September 30, 1986**, then repaid in **equal quarterly installments over five years**.
- **Interest rate capped at 12.5% per annum**.
- **7% of interest accrued before September 30, 1986 is deferred** until the **fourth anniversary** of the restructuring date.

---

### **4.8 Trust Indenture and Security Sharing Agreement**

A binding trust indenture and security sharing agreement must be entered into with:

- **The Canada Trust Company**
- **Private lenders**
- **The Minister**
- **Her Majesty in Right of Ontario**
- **Ontario Development Corporation (ODC)**

#### Purpose of the Agreement:
To secure, via **first charge**, the following obligations:

1. **Restructured loans** (from paragraph 4.7)
2. **Insured loans** (under this regulation)
3. **Loans guaranteed by ODC** ($33 million)
4. **Repayable contributions by ODC** ($10 million)
5. **Repayable contributions by Government of Canada** ($12 million)

#### Distribution of Proceeds from Asset Realization:

| Asset Type | Distribution Order |
|----------|--------------------|
| **Fixed assets in Pillette Road Plant** | <br>1. First, use proceeds to reduce **insured loans by $50 million** <br>2. Then, retire **restructured and insured loans pari passu** <br>3. Remaining proceeds retire ODC-guaranteed debt and repayable contributions |
| **Other fixed assets (excluding Pillette Road)** | <br>1. First, retire **restructured and insured loans pari passu** <br>2. Remaining proceeds retire ODC-guaranteed debt and repayable contributions |
| **Non-fixed assets** | <br>1. First, retire **restructured loans** <br>2. Remaining proceeds retire **insured loans**, ODC-guaranteed debt, and repayable contributions |

> *This ensures a priority-based repayment structure, with insured loans and restructuring obligations protected.*

---

### **4.9 Licensing Agreements**

#### a) **Chrysler Canada Ltd. – Chrysler Corporation**
- Grants **exclusive worldwide license** to produce **T115 van/wagons** in Canada.
- Grants **exclusive Canadian marketing rights** for these vans.

#### b) **DCI Diesel Canada Inc. – Chrysler Corporation**
- Grants **exclusive rights** to:
  - Manufacture, use, and sell **diesel engines** (listed in schedule)
  - From **January 1, 1983**, until the **later of January 1, 1994 or the repayment date of the engine plant loan**

---

### **4.10 Government Commitment and Loan Drawdown**

- The **U.S. government has committed to guarantee up to $1.5 billion USD** in loans to Chrysler Corporation.
- Chrysler Corporation has already **drawn down at least $1.2 billion USD** under this commitment **before** the loan insurance agreement takes effect.

> *This demonstrates strong U.S. financial backing, reducing perceived risk to Canadian investors.*

---

### **4.11 Project Implementation**

- Chrysler Canada Ltd. must carry out the **conversion and retooling of the Windsor Engine Plant** through **DCI Diesel Canada Inc.**

---

### **4.12 Anti-Blocking Clause (Perkins Engines Canada Ltd.)**
