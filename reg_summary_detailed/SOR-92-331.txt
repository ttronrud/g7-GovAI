### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation:  
"Regulations Respecting the Extent to Which a Company May Undertake the Business of Insurance and Relations Between Companies and Entities That Undertake the Business of Insurance, Insurance Agents and Insurance Brokers"**  
*(Effective June 1, 1992; Repealed by SOR/2002-270, s. 2)*

---

## **1. Regulatory Context and Authority**

- **Enacted by:** His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance.
- **Legal Basis:** Section 416 of the *Trust and Loan Companies Act* (*Act*).
- **Purpose:** To define the scope and limitations of insurance-related activities that trust and loan companies (and affiliated entities) may engage in within Canada, particularly in relation to:
  - The types of insurance they can administer,
  - Their relationships with insurance companies, agents, and brokers,
  - Restrictions on promotion and data sharing,
  - Prohibitions on indirect promotion or access to sensitive customer information.

> ⚠️ **Note:** This regulation was **repealed in 2002** (SOR/2002-270, s. 2), meaning it is no longer in force. However, this summary provides a detailed historical and interpretive analysis of its provisions as they were in effect from June 1, 1992.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | The *Trust and Loan Companies Act* |
| **Authorized Type of Insurance** | Specific insurance products approved for administration by companies under this regulation. Includes: <br> - Credit or charge card-related insurance <br> - Creditors’ disability insurance <br> - Creditors’ life insurance <br> - Creditors’ loss of employment insurance <br> - Creditors’ vehicle inventory insurance <br> - Export credit insurance <br> - Mortgage insurance <br> - Travel insurance |
| **Company Web Page** | A web page used by a company in its Canadian business operations. Must be accessible to the public or customers. Excludes internal pages only accessible to employees or agents. |
| **Credit or Charge Card-Related Insurance** | Insurance provided automatically to cardholders without individual risk assessment, covering: <br> - Loss/damage to goods purchased with the card <br> - Warranty extension for manufacturer <br> - Contractual liabilities from vehicle rentals paid via card |
| **Creditor’s Disability, Life, and Employment Insurance** | Group policies that pay part or all of a debtor’s debt upon: <br> - Disability/injury (disability insurance) <br> - Death (life insurance) <br> - Unemployment (loss of employment insurance) <br> Applies to debtors, guarantors, directors/officers, or key individuals essential to financial obligations |
| **Vehicle Inventory Insurance** | Covers direct and accidental loss/damage to vehicles held in stock for sale (some financed by the company) |
| **Export Credit Insurance** | Covers exporters against non-payment for exported goods/services |
| **Group Insurance Policy** | A policy covering a group of identifiable individuals who hold certificates of insurance; administered by the company or its affiliate |
| **Insurance Company** | Any entity legally authorized to underwrite insurance under federal or provincial legislation |
| **Line of Credit** | A flexible lending commitment up to a predetermined limit, based on reasonable credit needs |
| **Mortgage Insurance** | Covers the company or its affiliate against default by a debtor on a mortgage secured by real property |
| **Personal Accident Insurance** | Covers bodily injury or death due to accidents, or hospitalization days; applies to individuals |
| **Provincial Trust or Loan Company** | A provincially incorporated trust or loan corporation |
| **Small Business** | A business that would qualify as a small business corporation under subsection 248(1) of the *Income Tax Act* |
| **Travel Insurance** | Covers trips away from home or province, including: <br> - Trip cancellation/interruption <br> - Loss/damage to personal property <br> - Delayed baggage <br> - Illness/disability during trip <br> - Accidents, dental care, and return of remains after death |

## **3. Scope of Insurance Activities Allowed**

### ✅ **Permitted Activities**
1. **Administering Authorized Insurance Types and Personal Accident Insurance**
   - Companies may administer:
     - All authorized types of insurance (as defined above)
     - Personal accident insurance (for individuals)

2. **Administering Group Insurance Policies for Specific Employee Groups**
   - For employees of:
     - The company itself or entities the company controls (under s. 453 of the Act)
     - Other companies that control the company or entities they control (under s. 453)
     - Banks or bank holding companies that control the company (under s. 468 or 930 of the *Bank Act*)
     - Entities controlled by the company’s affiliates (under s. 522.07, 522.08, or 522.32(1)/(3) of the *Bank Act*)
     - Employees of authorized foreign banks that are affiliates of the company
     - Employees of foreign bank affiliates (if the foreign bank is under s. 522.17 or 522.18 of the *Bank Act*)

3. **Providing General Advice**
   - Companies may offer general advice about:
     - Authorized types of insurance
     - Services related to authorized insurance
   - **Restriction:** Advice must not be:
     - Specific to a particular risk, life insurance policy, proposal, or service
     - Targeted to a specific insurance company, agent, or broker
     - A referral to a particular insurer, agent, or broker

4. **Promotion of Insurance Products (with Strict Conditions)**
   - **Permitted promotions**:
     - **Only if** the insurance product is an **authorized type** of insurance
     - **Only if** the promotion occurs **outside a branch** and targets:
       - Holders of credit or charge cards who receive regular statements
       - All natural-person customers who receive regular statements
       - The general public
     - **Exception:** Promotion of personal accident insurance may occur outside a branch
     - Promotion of travel insurance may occur outside a branch and target the general public

5. **Promotion on Company Web Pages**
   - A company **may** promote authorized insurance products or agents on its web page **if**:
     - The promotion is for an insurance company or broker that deals **only** in authorized types of insurance
     - The promotion relates to **only authorized types** of insurance
   - **Prohibited**:
     - Promotion of insurance from companies or brokers that do **not** deal only in authorized types
     - Promotion of non-authorized insurance policies or services

6. **Exclusions from Promotion**
   - A company may exclude individuals from promotion if:
     - Promotion would violate a federal or provincial law
     - The individual has written opt-out
     - The individual holds a credit card account that is not in good standing

## **4. Prohibitions and Restrictions**

### ❌ **Strict Prohibitions**

| Prohibition | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Sharing Customer Information** | No company may provide, directly or indirectly, to an insurance company, agent, or broker: <br> - Information about a customer in Canada <br> - Information about an employee of a customer in Canada <br> - Any member of a Canadian entity with members <br> - Any partner in a Canadian partnership |
| **Subsidiary Information Sharing** | Subsidiaries may not share customer or employee data with insurers, agents, or brokers |
| **Affiliate Information Sharing** | Affiliates (non-subsidiaries) may only receive information if the company ensures that the affiliate will **not** share it with insurers/agents/brokers for promotional purposes |
| **Subsidiary Trust/Loan Company Sharing** | A provincial trust or loan company subsidiary may not share customer or employee data with insurers, agents, or brokers |
| **Telecommunications Devices** | No company may provide a telecommunications device (e.g., phone, app, chat) that links a customer directly to an insurance company, agent, or broker |
| **Premises Adjacency** | A company may not operate on premises adjacent to those of an insurance company, agent, or broker unless it clearly indicates to customers that its business is separate and distinct |

---

## **5. Exceptions and Special Provisions**

### ✅ **Exception for Existing Policies (May 6, 1992)**
- Companies that administered an insurance policy on **May 6, 1992**, and whose administration was **not permitted under section 4** of the Act, may **continue** administering that policy for any person who had coverage under it on that date.

> This was a transitional provision to allow continuity of existing insurance arrangements that were previously allowed but fell outside the new regulatory framework.

---

## **6. Compliance and Oversight**

- The regulation implies **compliance with the Superintendent of Insurance**:
  - Affiliates must provide **written undertakings** to insurers/agents that they will not use customer data for promotion in Canada.
  - These undertakings must be in a form acceptable to the Superintendent.
