### **Detailed Summary of the Special Economic Measures (Belarus) Regulations**

The **Special Economic Measures (Belarus) Regulations**, made under the **Special Economic Measures Act (SEMA)**, are a set of targeted economic sanctions imposed by the Government of Canada in response to **gross and systematic human rights violations** committed in Belarus. These regulations were introduced on **June 21, 2017**, and came into force upon registration in the Canada Gazette.

This summary provides a comprehensive, structured analysis of the regulation’s **purpose, definitions, prohibitions, exceptions, compliance obligations, and enforcement mechanisms**.

---

## **1. Purpose and Background**

- **Primary Objective**: To impose targeted economic sanctions on individuals and entities linked to the Belarusian government responsible for **gross and systematic human rights abuses**, particularly those undermining democratic governance and international norms.
- **Legal Basis**: The measures are authorized under **sections 4(1)a, (1.1)b, (2), and (3)** of the *Special Economic Measures Act (SEMA)*.
- **Governor in Council’s Authority**: The Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs) determines that the violations constitute a sufficient basis for economic action.
- **Triggering Event**: The decision is grounded in documented evidence of widespread human rights violations in Belarus, including suppression of dissent, arbitrary arrests, and crackdowns on civil society.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Belarus** | Refers to the Republic of Belarus and includes all its political subdivisions, government bodies, departments, agencies, and their sub-units. |
| **Consumer Communication Device** | Includes computers, mobile phones, SIM cards, printers, monitors, digital cameras, modems, keyboards, and software (excluding encryption source code). |
| **Minister** | Refers to the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**. |
| **Restricted Goods and Technologies List** | A dynamic list maintained by the Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development (DFATD), published online and updated periodically. It specifies goods and technologies subject to restrictions. |
| **Technology** | Any form of technical data, know-how, training, consulting, or instruction related to the design, manufacture, or use of goods. |

---

## **3. Who is a "Listed Person"?**

A person is listed in **Schedule 1** if the Governor in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister, is satisfied that there are **reasonable grounds to believe** the individual or entity:

- Has participated in **gross and systematic human rights violations** in Belarus;
- Engages in activities that **facilitate, support, fund, or obstruct**:
  - Violations of Ukraine’s sovereignty or territorial integrity;
  - Work of international organizations in Ukraine;
- Is a **current or former senior official** of the Government of Belarus;
- Is an **associate** of someone listed under any of the above categories;
- Is a **family member** of a listed person;
- Is an **entity owned, held, or controlled** directly or indirectly by a listed person or by Belarus itself;
- Is a **senior official** of an entity listed under the above.

> *Note: The list includes both individuals and entities, with broad definitions that extend to associates and family members.*

---

## **4. Prohibited Actions (General Prohibitions)**

Any person in Canada or a Canadian residing outside Canada is prohibited from:

### **A. Dealing with Listed Persons’ Property**
- Dealing in **any property** (worldwide) owned, held, or controlled by a listed person or by someone acting on their behalf.
- Entering into or facilitating **transactions** related to such dealings.
- Providing **financial or related services** (e.g., banking, payments, transfers) for such transactions.

### **B. Financial Transactions with Belarus or Entities Controlled by Belarus**
- **Dealing in transferable securities or money market instruments** (e.g., treasury bills, CDs, commercial paper) issued by:
  - Belarus or any entity controlled by Belarus;
  - A person acting on behalf of or at the direction of such an entity.
> ⚠️ *Repealed by SOR/2023-71, s. 1*

### **C. Long-Term Debt Transactions**
- **Transacting in, financing, or dealing in debt** with a maturity of **over 90 days** (e.g., bonds, loans, letters of credit) related to:
  - Belarus or entities controlled by Belarus;
  - Persons acting on behalf of such entities.
> ⚠️ *Repealed by SOR/2023-71, s. 2*

### **D. Insurance or Reinsurance**
- Providing **insurance or reinsurance** to or for the benefit of:
  - Belarus or entities controlled by Belarus;
  - Persons acting on behalf of such entities.
- Providing insurance to **any person in Belarus** for goods listed in **Chapter 88 of the Harmonized Commodity Description and Coding System (HCDS)** (e.g., weapons, surveillance equipment, or military-related goods) or for **technology used in such goods**.

> ✅ *Exception*: Existing insurance or reinsurance is exempted for **30 days** after the regulation comes into force.

---

## **5. Prohibitions on Trade and Technology Exports**

### **A. Importing or Acquiring Goods from Belarus**
- Prohibited to **import, purchase, acquire, ship, or otherwise deal in**:
  - Any good listed in **Part 1 of Schedule 2** (exported from Belarus);
  - Any good listed in **Part 2 of Schedule 2** (exported from Belarus).

> These goods are generally high-risk items, including surveillance equipment, communications technology, and dual-use items.

### **B. Exporting Goods or Technology to Belarus**
- Prohibited to **export, sell, supply, or ship**:
  - Any good listed in the **Restricted Goods and Technologies List** (RGT List) or in **Schedule 3**;
  - Any **technology** listed in the RGT List or Schedule 3.

> This includes advanced software, hardware, and technical know-how that could be used for surveillance or repression.

---

## **6. Exceptions and Exemptions**

Several important **exceptions** are provided to ensure the regulation does not unduly impact legitimate trade, humanitarian efforts, or international cooperation.

| Exception Type | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Media Use** | Goods temporarily exported for use by **media representatives** from Canada or partner countries listed in Annex 1 of the RGT List are exempt. |
| **Nuclear Safeguards** | Goods used in **international nuclear verification** (e.g., inspections, monitoring) are exempt. |
| **Government Use** | Goods for use by **Canadian or partner country government departments or agencies** listed in Annex 1 are exempt. |
| **Chemical Weapons Inspections** | Goods used in inspections under the **Chemical Weapons Convention (CWC)** are exempt. |
| **International Space Station (ISS)** | Goods used in ISS operations are exempt. |
| **Software Updates** | Software updates for **civilian entities** (e.g., Canadian or partner country-owned companies) are exempt. |
| **Civil Aircraft** | Civil aircraft registered in Canada or the U.S. departing temporarily are exempt. |
| **Aircraft/Ship Supplies** | Equipment and supplies necessary for safe operation (spare parts, food, fuel) are exempt. |
| **Personal Effects** | Personal effects taken by individuals leaving Canada or Belarus for personal use (not for sale or long-term retention) are exempt. |
| **Consumer Communication Devices** | Devices generally available to the public and designed for **user-installed, self-support** use are exempt. |

> ⚠️ *Note*: These exemptions are **not automatic**—they require justification and must be applied in accordance with the regulation.

---

## **7. Specific Schedules and Prohibitions**

### **Schedule 4 – Goods Prohibited from Export to Belarus**
- Prohibits export to Belarus of goods listed in **column 1 of Part 1 and Part 2 of Schedule 4**.
- **Part 1**: Includes high-risk items such as surveillance, communications, and dual-use technology.
- **Part 2**: Includes goods that may be used for repression or military purposes.

> ✅ *Exemptions apply* to personal effects, media, government use, and aircraft operations.

### **Schedule 5 – Additional Goods Prohibited from Export**
- Prohibits export of goods listed in **column 1 of Schedule 5** to Belarus.

> ⚠️ *Effective 60 days after coming into force* (not immediately).

---

## **8. Exceptions to Prohibitions (Section 3)**

Certain transactions are **exempt** from the general prohibitions, including:

- Payments due under contracts **entered into before** the person became listed;
- Transactions necessary for **transferring accounts, funds, or investments** from a listed person to a non-listed person on the day they were listed;
- Loan repayments or enforcement actions related to loans made **before** listing;
- Government benefits (e.g., Old Age Security, CPP, disability payments);
- Legal services required by listed persons;
- Transactions necessary for **diplomatic missions** (per Vienna Convention);
- Transactions with **international organizations** (UN, Red Cross, etc.);
- Transactions under **Canada–Belarus agreements**.

> These exceptions are intended to **avoid economic disruption** to legitimate financial obligations and diplomatic functions.
