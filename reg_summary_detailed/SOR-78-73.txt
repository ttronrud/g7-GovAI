### **2. Purpose of the Regulations**

The primary objective of these regulations is to establish a **clear, consistent, and internationally aligned method** for converting values originally expressed in **gold francs** (a historical unit of value tied to gold) into **Canadian dollars** for use in the administration of the **Canada Shipping Act**.

This is necessary because:
- The Canada Shipping Act includes provisions that reference values in gold francs, particularly in relation to ship tonnage, insurance, and shipping fees.
- Gold francs were a pre-1971 international currency standard, and their use in legal and financial contexts had become outdated.
- The international monetary system had evolved, with the **Special Drawing Rights (S.D.R.)** becoming the preferred reference for exchange rate calculations.

Thus, the regulations modernize and standardize the valuation process by anchoring gold franc values to internationally recognized financial instruments.

---

### **3. Key Definitions**

- **S.D.R. (Special Drawing Rights):**  
  A reserve asset created and maintained by the **International Monetary Fund (IMF)**. It is used as a measure of value in international monetary affairs and serves as a benchmark for exchange rate calculations.  
  In this regulation, S.D.R. is used as an intermediary unit to convert gold francs into Canadian dollars.

### **4. Conversion Methodology**

The regulations establish a **two-step conversion process** to determine the Canadian dollar equivalent of gold francs:

#### **Step 1: Convert Gold Francs to S.D.R.**
- **Rate:** 15.075 gold francs = 1 S.D.R.
- This means that **1 gold franc = 1 / 15.075 S.D.R.**

- This rate is **not fixed** and is updated periodically by the IMF to reflect current international monetary conditions.

### **5. Legal and Practical Implications**

- **Replacement of Outdated Currency Reference:**  
  The previous gold franc-based system, rooted in the Bretton Woods system, is no longer viable due to the collapse of the gold standard and the transition to fiat currencies. This regulation modernizes the legal framework.

- **Clarity and Predictability:**  
  By using a defined, two-step conversion path, the regulations provide clarity for legal, financial, and maritime stakeholders (e.g., shipping companies, insurers, port authorities) who must calculate values under the Canada Shipping Act.

- **Alignment with International Standards:**  
  Using S.D.R. as an intermediary unit ensures that the Canadian valuation system aligns with international financial practices and the IMFâ€™s authoritative exchange rate data.

- **No Direct Gold Franc to Dollar Rate:**  
  The regulation avoids setting a direct gold franc to Canadian dollar rate. Instead, it relies on a **third-party international benchmark (S.D.R.)**, which reduces the risk of arbitrary or outdated valuation assumptions.

### **6. Scope and Applicability**

These regulations apply specifically to:
- **Provisions of the Canada Shipping Act** that reference values in gold francs.
- Situations involving:
  - Ship tonnage assessments
  - Insurance calculations
  - Regulatory fees
  - Valuation of maritime claims or obligations

They do **not** apply to other areas of finance, currency, or trade policy outside the scope of the Canada Shipping Act.
