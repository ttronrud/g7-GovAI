### **8. Approach Surfaces (Detailed)**
Each runway end has a dedicated approach surface with a specific profile:

- **Initial Slope**: 1:60 (1 meter vertical rise per 60 meters horizontal)
- **Secondary Slope**: 1:50 (1 meter vertical rise per 50 meters horizontal)
- Extends **15,000 meters horizontally** from the runway end
- Outer ends of the horizontal line are **2,400 meters** from the runway centerline
- Vertical height of the final horizontal line:
  - Runway 03: **291.2 m** above strip end elevation
  - Runway 21: **291.0 m**
  - Runway 12L & 12R: **291.0 m**
  - Runway 30L & 30R: **291.4 m**

> These surfaces ensure that aircraft have a clear view of the runway during approach, especially in poor visibility or adverse weather.

### **9. Transitional Surfaces**
- Extend from the **lateral edges** of each strip and its approach surfaces.
- Slope at **1:7** (1 meter vertical rise per 7 meters horizontal).
- Extend upward and outward until they meet:
  - The **outer surface**, or
  - The transitional surface of an **adjacent strip**

> This ensures a **smooth, continuous buffer zone** between adjacent runways, preventing obstructions from interfering with aircraft navigating between runways.
