**Detailed Summary of the *Canadian Ownership and Control Determination Forms Order, 1985***  
*(S.C. 1980-81-82-83, c. 107; SOR/84-412, 1984 Canada Gazette Part II, p. 2312; Ottawa, August 16, 1985)*

---

### **Overview**

This regulation, known as the *Canadian Ownership and Control Determination Forms Order, 1985*, was issued by the **Minister of Energy, Mines and Resources** under the authority of the *Canadian Ownership and Control Determination Act* (the "Act") and its associated *Regulations*. It **revokes the previous 1984 Order** and establishes updated, more detailed requirements for how applications for a certificate under the Act must be submitted — specifically, the **form, content, and documentation** required in applications filed **on or after August 29, 1985**.

The Order is designed to ensure consistency, clarity, and completeness in determining the **Canadian ownership rate** and **control status** of entities and individuals, particularly for purposes such as eligibility for certain federal programs, tax benefits, or regulatory compliance.

---

### **Key Provisions and Structure**

#### **1. Scope and Applicability**
- Applies to **all applications filed on or after August 29, 1985**.
- Replaces the *Canadian Ownership and Control Determination Forms Order, 1984*.
- This Order may be cited as the *Canadian Ownership and Control Determination Forms Order, 1985*.

---

#### **2. Definitions**
The Order defines key terms used throughout:
- **"Annex"**: Refers to the schedule(s) in Schedule II, which contain specific forms or information requirements for different types of applicants.
- **"Act"**: The *Canadian Ownership and Control Determination Act*.
- **"Class of capitalization"**: A formal equity class (e.g., common shares, preferred shares) or a forward equity class (e.g., options, warrants).
- **"Form"**: Refers to the standardized forms listed in Schedule I.
- **"Regulations"**: The *Canadian Ownership and Control Determination Regulations, 1984*.

All other terms retain their meaning as defined in the Act and Regulations.

---

#### **3. General Requirements for Applications**
Where a provision requires an application to be submitted in a specific **Form**, the application may be:
- Submitted in that Form, or
- In the form of a letter or other appropriate format.

However, **regardless of format**, the application must:
1. **Include all information specified in the Form**;
2. **Include or be accompanied by any other information or documentation required by this Order**; and  
3. **Be certified as indicated in the Form**.

This ensures that even if applicants use a non-standard format, the essential data remains complete and verifiable.

---

#### **4. Application Forms by Applicant Type**

The Order specifies different forms based on the nature and size of the applicant:

| Applicant Type | Required Form |
|---------------|---------------|
| **Individual** | Form A-I |
| **Small Applicant** | Form A-S |
| **Entity (non-small)** | Form A |
| **Corporation that elects under subsection 30(7) of the Regulations** | Form A-AML (Alternative Measurement and Listing) |
| **Entity meeting the criteria in Form A-C** | Form A-C (instead of Form A) |

> **Note**: "Small applicant" is defined in the Regulations and generally refers to entities with limited assets or operations, which may require simplified reporting.

---

#### **5. Special Requirements for Certain Entities**

- **Cooperative Corporation with 100% Canadian Ownership (under paragraph 12(1)(g))**  
  Must include or be accompanied by:
  - Information in **Annex 1**
  - A completed certificate in Annex 1, as specified.

- **Life Insurance Company**  
  Must include or be accompanied by:
  - Information in **Annex 2**

- **Mutual Company (not a life insurance company)**  
  Must include or be accompanied by:
  - Information in **Annex 3**

- **Entities with 100% Canadian Ownership (under subsection 12(1)) or referred to in subsection 16(5)**  
  Must include:
  - Relevant portions of **Annex 4**
  - The corresponding certificate in Annex 4, completed as directed.

> These provisions reflect special treatment for entities with unique ownership structures or regulatory status.

---

#### **6. Mandatory Information for Determining Canadian Ownership Rate**

Where any of the following information is **necessary** to determine the Canadian ownership rate of an applicant or investor, it must be included in the application:

- Description of each class of capitalization  
- Summary of nominee client information  
- Summary of measured block information  
- Summary of beneficial Canadian ownership calculation per class of formal equity  
- Summary of Canadian ownership rate calculation  
- Any other relevant information, including:
  - Identification of a class of capitalization  
  - Exclusion of a class from measurement  
  - Beneficiaries of trusts  
  - Investor status (primary, secondary, or equity-related)

> This ensures transparency and accuracy in assessing ownership, especially when dealing with complex ownership structures involving trusts, nominees, or indirect holdings.

---

#### **7. Required Documentation for Ownership/Control Determination**

For applications involving Form A or Form A-AML, the following documents must be included **if relevant**:

- **Copies of all prospectuses** filed by or on behalf of the applicant with a securities regulatory authority **on or after October 28, 1980**  
- **Copies of all other offering documents** used to issue securities after that date  
- **Financial statements** of the applicant for its most recently completed fiscal period — **only if required to measure the Canadian ownership rate**  
- **Unit holders list** (or portion thereof) showing entries where:
  - The number of units is **equal to or greater than 10% of the measured block threshold** for each class of formal equity  
  - The beneficial Canadian ownership rate is calculated under **section 25 of the Regulations**

> This requirement ensures that the regulatory body has access to sufficient data to assess ownership accurately, especially for entities with large or concentrated holdings.

---

#### **8. Flexibility for Small Applicants**

- For applications in **Form A-S** (small applicants), applicants may include **one copy of financial statements** that show the assets relevant to determining total assets under **section 9 of the Regulations**.

> This provides a simplified path for smaller entities without requiring full financial disclosures.

---

#### **9. Control Status Applications**

Under **section 39 of the Act**, when an application is made for a certificate stating:
- Canadian ownership rate and control status, or  
- Control status alone,

the application must include **all necessary information and documentation** to determine the **control status** of the person.

> Control status is distinct from ownership rate and involves assessing whether a person has the ability to direct or influence the management or policies of an entity.

---

#### **10. Filing Procedures**

Applications must be filed by:
- **Sending by mail**, or  
- **Delivering in person** to one of the following:
  - **Central Registry of the Petroleum Incentives Administration**, City of Calgary  
  - **Central Registry of the Petroleum Incentives Administration**, City of Ottawa

> This establishes a clear and accessible filing process, with two designated locations to ensure geographic accessibility.

---

### **Significance and Purpose**

This Order serves several key regulatory functions:

1. **Modernization and Clarity**  
   Replaces outdated forms and procedures with a more structured, transparent, and user-friendly system.

2. **Improved Accuracy in Ownership Determination**  
   By mandating detailed disclosures about capitalization classes, nominee clients, financial statements, and ownership calculations, the Order enhances the reliability of ownership rate assessments.

3. **Tailored Treatment for Special Cases**  
   Recognizes the unique structures of life insurance companies, mutuals, cooperatives, and entities with 100% Canadian ownership.

4. **Compliance with Regulatory Standards**  
   Aligns with the *Canadian Ownership and Control Determination Regulations, 1984*, ensuring consistency with statutory and regulatory frameworks.

5. **Support for Federal Policy Objectives**  
   Facilitates the implementation of federal programs (e.g., petroleum incentives, tax credits) that require verification of Canadian ownership and control.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Canadian Ownership and Control Determination Forms Order, 1985* is a comprehensive and practical regulatory instrument that streamlines and standardizes the process for applicants to submit applications for Canadian ownership and control determinations. It balances **simplicity for small applicants** with **rigor and detail for larger or more complex entities**, ensuring that all applications contain the necessary information to support accurate and fair assessments of ownership and control.

By clearly defining forms, required disclosures, and filing procedures, this Order strengthens regulatory oversight, promotes transparency, and supports Canada’s broader goals of encouraging Canadian ownership in key industries.
