## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Ozone-depleting Substances and Halocarbon Alternatives Regulations**

### üîç **1. Regulatory Context and Legal Basis**

- **Enabling Legislation**:  
  These regulations are made under **section 93(1)** of the *Canadian Environmental Protection Act, 1999 (CEPA)*, which allows the Governor in Council to regulate substances that deplete the ozone layer.

- **Legal Process**:
  - The **Minister of the Environment** published a **proposed regulation** in *Canada Gazette, Part I*, on **March 21, 2015**.
  - A public comment period was opened, allowing stakeholders to submit **comments or objections**.
  - The **National Advisory Committee** was consulted under section 6c and subsection 93(3) of CEPA.
  - The **Governor in Council** concluded, under subsection 93(4), that the proposed regulations do **not conflict** with other federal environmental laws and provide **sufficient protection** to human health and the environment.

> ‚úÖ Therefore, the regulations were formally adopted and published on **March 21, 2015**, as the final version.

### üéØ **3. Purpose of the Regulations**

The primary objectives are:

1. **Implement Canada‚Äôs obligations under the Montreal Protocol** on substances that deplete the ozone layer.
2. **Regulate ozone-depleting substances (ODS)** and **products containing or designed to contain** them.
3. **Set rules for halocarbon alternatives** (e.g., HFCs, HCFCs) as replacements for ODS.
4. **Ensure safe handling, use, and disposal** of ODS to protect human health and the environment.

---

### üìÇ **4. Scope of Application**

#### ‚úÖ **Applies to**:
- Substances (including isomers) listed in **Schedule 1**, specifically on the **List of Toxic Substances** under CEPA.
- **Products** that contain or are designed to contain these substances.

#### ‚ùå **Does NOT Apply to**:
1. **Incidental byproducts**:
   - Substances produced accidentally during manufacturing of non-regulated substances.
   - Substances present incidentally in mixtures, products, or equipment.

2. **Small quantities left in containers**:
   - Residue in a container after emptying, **not exceeding 10% of the container‚Äôs total capacity** (by weight).

3. **Substances in transit**:
   - Moving through Canada (from outside Canada to outside Canada, or vice versa) **if**:
     - Destination is known on entry/exit date.
     - The substance is **not stored**, **repackaged**, **sorted**, **altered**, or **sold** during transit.

4. **Automobiles in transit**:
   - Vehicles moving through Canada from abroad to abroad.

### üìù **6. Permits for Export and Import**

#### üîπ Export Permits (Under Section 6)
- **Permitted purposes**:
  1. Destruction
  2. Disposal (if imported by mistake)
  3. Use listed in **Column 3 of Table 1** (specific essential or approved uses)
  4. Reclamation (for CFCs, bromofluorocarbons, bromochlorodifluoromethane)  
  5. Any other purpose compliant with the **importing Party‚Äôs laws** (for CFCs, bromofluorocarbons, bromochlorodifluoromethane)

- **Special exemptions**:
  - **Reclaimed substances** may be exported regardless of purpose:
    - Bromochloromethane
    - HBFCs
    - Reclaimed CFCs, tetrachloromethane, 1,1,1-trichloroethane

- **Timeframe for re-export**:
  - For **reclaimed CFCs or similar substances**, must be **re-exported to country of origin within 6 months** of importation.

---

#### üîπ Import Permits (Under Section 11)
- **Permitted purposes**:
  1. Destruction
  2. Use listed in **Column 3 of Table 1**
  3. Reclamation (for CFCs, tetrachloromethane, 1,1,1-trichloroethane, HBFCs, bromochloromethane)

- **Special exemptions**:
  - Reclaimed bromofluorocarbons or bromochlorodifluoromethane may be imported regardless of purpose.

- **Timeframe for re-export or destruction**:
  - If not re-exported within 6 months:
    - Must be **sent for destruction** within **3 months** of the 6-month deadline.
    - Destruction must occur at a facility operating under the **Montreal Protocol Handbook**.

### üö´ **7. Prohibitions on Use and Sale**

#### General Prohibitions:
- **No use or sale** of substances listed in **Table 1 of Schedule 1** unless:
  1. The substance is **recovered, recycled, or reclaimed**.
  2. The substance is **sold for destruction**.
  3. The substance was **manufactured or imported before** the following cutoff dates:
     - Tetrachloromethane: **January 1, 1995**
     - 1,1,1-Trichloroethane: **January 1, 1996**
     - CFCs: **January 1, 1996**
     - Bromochlorodifluoromethane, bromotrifluoromethane, dibromotetrafluoroethane: **July 1, 1994**
     - Bromofluorocarbons (excluding above): **January 1, 1994**
     - HBFCs: **January 1, 1996**
     - Bromochloromethane: **January 1, 2002**
  4. The substance was manufactured or imported for a **specific use listed in Column 3 of Table 1** and is used for that **same purpose**.
  5. **Tetrachloromethane** used in **chlor-alkali plants** (as diluent for nitrogen trichloride) or as **feedstock** or for **laboratory use**.

#### Prohibited Use of Recovered Substances:
- **No use for any other purpose** after recovery from a product used in an approved essential use (e.g., refrigeration, pest control).
- **No sale** for any other use.
