### **4. What Constitutes a "Secure Electronic Signature"?**

According to **subsection 31(1)** of PIPEDA, a secure electronic signature must result from the **sequential application of the following steps**:

1. **Hash Function Application**  
   - The data in the electronic document is passed through a hash function to generate a **message digest** â€” a unique fingerprint of the data.

2. **Private Key Encryption**  
   - The message digest is encrypted using the **private key** of the signer. This creates a digital signature that is uniquely tied to both the data and the signer.

3. **Attachment to the Document**  
   - The encrypted message digest is incorporated into, attached to, or associated with the electronic document.

4. **Transmission with Certificate or Access Means**  
   - The document and the encrypted message digest are transmitted together with either:
     - The **digital signature certificate (DSC)**, or
     - A **means of access** to the DSC (e.g., a secure URL or password-protected file).

5. **Verification Upon Receipt**  
   After receiving the document and the encrypted message digest:
   - The recipient uses the **public key** from the DSC to decrypt the message digest and recover the original digest.
   - The recipient applies the hash function again to the original data to generate a **new message digest**.
