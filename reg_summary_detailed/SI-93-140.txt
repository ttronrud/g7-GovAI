**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

This regulatory instrument, issued under the authority of the **Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act**, formally transfers specific functions and responsibilities from two existing government departments to the **Department of Agriculture**. The transfer is made by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the recommendation of the **Prime Minister**, pursuant to **paragraph 2(a)** of the Act, which authorizes the reorganization and reallocation of public service duties.

### Key Elements of the Regulation:

1. **Purpose of the Transfer**:
   - The primary objective is to enhance the effectiveness and efficiency of government oversight in the **agri-food sector** by consolidating key responsibilities under a department with direct expertise and policy focus in agriculture.
   - This realignment aims to improve coordination between policy development, food safety, processing standards, and consumer information related to agricultural products.

2. **Departments Involved in the Transfer**:

   - **From the Department of Industry, Science and Technology**:
     - The **Food Products Branch**.
     - Specifically, the portion relating to **agri-food processing and manufacturing**.
     - This includes regulatory functions, standards, inspections, and policy development related to how agricultural products are processed, manufactured, and brought to market.

   - **From the Department of Consumer and Corporate Affairs**:
     - The **Food Division** within the **Consumer Products Branch**.
     - Specifically, the portion relating to **agri-food and labelling**.
     - This involves responsibilities related to food labelling requirements, consumer information, product authenticity, and compliance with labelling standards for agricultural and food products.

3. **Transfer of Control and Supervision**:
   - The regulation explicitly transfers **control and supervision** of the specified portions to the **Department of Agriculture**.
   - This means that the Department of Agriculture will now have authority over:
     - Policy formulation and implementation in agri-food processing.
     - Oversight of manufacturing standards and practices.
     - Enforcement and monitoring of agri-food labelling requirements.
     - Consumer protection aspects tied to food product information.

4. **Legal Authority**:
   - The transfer is authorized under **paragraph 2(a)** of the *Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act*, which permits the government to reassign public service functions to better align with sectoral priorities, improve governance, and ensure more effective delivery of public services.

5. **Implications**:
   - **Streamlined Governance**: The agri-food sector will benefit from a more integrated and specialized oversight body, reducing duplication and improving policy coherence.
   - **Enhanced Accountability**: The Department of Agriculture will now be directly accountable for ensuring food safety, processing standards, and truthful labelling in the agricultural supply chain.
   - **Stakeholder Impact**: Industry players, farmers, processors, and consumers will experience clearer regulatory frameworks and more consistent enforcement under a dedicated agricultural department.

6. **Effective Date**:
   - While not explicitly stated in the provided text, such transfers typically take effect upon publication in the official government gazette or upon formal notification. The effective date would be determined by the issuing authority and may follow the date of publication.

This regulation represents a strategic reorganization of government functions to place agri-food processing and labelling oversight under the Department of Agriculture.
