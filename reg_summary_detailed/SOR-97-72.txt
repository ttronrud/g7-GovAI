### **Detailed Summary of the NAFTA and CIFTA Advance Rulings Regulations**

---

#### **1. Regulatory Background and Legal Basis**

These regulations implement a provision of the **Canada-Israel Free Trade Agreement (CIFTA)** and are designed to establish a formal process for **advance rulings** on the **origin and tariff treatment** of goods imported from **free trade partners**, particularly under the **North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA)** and **CIFTA**.

- The regulations **do not introduce material substantive changes** to existing customs rules.
- As such, they are **not required to be published under subsection 164(3) of the Customs Act** due to paragraphs **164(4)(a.03)a and (d)**, which allow for non-publication of regulations that make no significant change to existing provisions.

- The regulations are issued under **section 43.1c** of the *Customs Act*, along with **paragraphs 164(1)(i)d and (j)** and **subsection 164(1.1)e**, which authorize the Minister of National Revenue to issue advance rulings and related administrative rules.

---

#### **2. Purpose of the Regulations**

The primary purpose is to provide **clarity, consistency, and predictability** in determining whether goods imported from **free trade partners** (such as NAFTA countries, CIFTA partners, and others) qualify for **preferential tariff treatment** under the applicable free trade agreements. This includes:

- Determining whether goods meet **origin criteria** (e.g., regional value content, production location, use of materials).
- Ensuring compliance with **rules of origin** under NAFTA, CIFTA, and other agreements.
- Protecting importers and producers from uncertainty by allowing them to **seek advance rulings** before importing goods.

---

#### **3. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Customs Act* (the primary legislation governing customs procedures). |
| **Advance Ruling** | A formal determination by a customs officer that goods (or materials used in their production) meet the origin requirements for preferential tariff treatment under the *Customs Act* (specifically under sections 43.1(1)(a) or (b)). |
| **Material** | A good used in the production of another good â€” includes parts or ingredients. |

---

#### **4. Who Can Apply for an Advance Ruling?**

An application may be made by any of the following:

1. **Importers** of the goods in Canada.
2. **Persons authorized to account for the goods** under section 32(6)(a) or subsection 32(7) of the *Customs Act*.
3. **Exporters or producers** of the goods in a **free trade partner other than Canada**.
4. **Producers of a material** used in the production of the goods, **if**:
   - The goods are produced in a **NAFTA country other than Canada**, or in **Chile, Costa Rica, Peru, Colombia, Jordan, Panama, or Honduras**.
   - The goods are produced in **Israel or another CIFTA beneficiary**, or in the **United States**, where a material is used in production.

> *Note: The provision for producers in Israel or U.S. is limited to materials used in production.*

---

#### **5. Timing and Language Requirements**

- An application must be submitted **at least 120 days before** the date of importation.
- The application must be made in **English or French**, the official languages of Canada.

---

#### **6. Evaluation Process and Supplemental Information**

- If an officer determines that the application lacks sufficient information, they may:
  - **Request supplemental information**.
  - Specify a **minimum 30-day period** for the applicant to provide it.

> This ensures that rulings are based on complete and accurate data.

---

#### **7. Principles of Consistency and Transparency**

- **Consistency**: Officers must provide **consistent rulings** for applications involving **identical facts and circumstances**.
- **Transparency**: Officers must provide a **full written explanation** of the reasons for the ruling.
- **Language**: The ruling must be issued in the **same language** as the original application.

---

#### **8. Effectiveness and Scope of Advance Rulings**

- An advance ruling becomes effective on:
  - The date it is issued, **or**
  - A later date specified in the ruling, **but not later than the date of importation**.

- The ruling applies to goods:
  - That are the subject of the ruling.
  - Imported **on or after the effective date**.

---

#### **9. Conditions for Maintaining an Advance Ruling**

An advance ruling remains valid and is honored by the Minister if all of the following conditions are met:

1. **No change** in the material facts or circumstances on which the ruling is based.
2. **Full compliance** with all terms and conditions of the ruling.
3. The ruling has **not been revoked**.

> This ensures that rulings remain reliable and enforceable over time.

---

#### **10. Situations Where an Ruling May Be Postponed**

An officer may **postpone issuing** an advance ruling if the evaluation involves one of the following processes, which could affect the ruling:

- A **verification of origin** under section 42.1 of the *Customs Act*.
- A **re-determination or further re-determination** of origin under sections 59, 60, or 61.
- A **hearing before the Canadian International Trade Tribunal (CITT)** or any court.

> This allows time for judicial or administrative review to impact the final ruling.

---

#### **11. Situations Where an Ruling May Be Declined or Postponed**

- If the applicant **fails to provide supplemental information** within the 30-day period specified.

---

#### **12. Grounds for Modification or Revocation of an Advance Ruling**

An advance ruling may be **modified or revoked** if any of the following conditions apply:

| Ground | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| **Error in fact** | Misstatement or factual inaccuracy in the basis of the ruling. |
| **Error in tariff classification** | Incorrect classification of goods or materials. |
| **Error in regional value content (RVC)** | For goods exported from NAFTA countries (Chile, Costa Rica, Honduras), if the RVC requirement is misapplied under relevant chapters (e.g., Chapter Four of NAFTA, Chapter D of CCFTA). |
| **Misapplication of origin rules** | Errors in determining whether goods qualify as "goods of a NAFTA country" under Annex 300-B, 302.2, or Chapter Seven of NAFTA. |
| **Misapplication of qualifying goods rules** | Errors in determining if goods qualify as "qualifying goods" under Chapter Seven of NAFTA. |
| **Misapplication of re-entry rules** | Errors in determining if goods re-entering Canada after export for repair/alteration qualify for duty-free treatment under relevant articles (e.g., Article 307 of NAFTA). |
| **Disagreement with agreed interpretations** | If the ruling contradicts an **interpretation agreed upon by**:
  - NAFTA partners (Chapters 3/4),
  - Canada & Chile (Chapters C/D of CCFTA),
  - Canada & Costa Rica (Chapters III/IV of CCRFTA),
  - Canada & EFTA states (Annex C to CEFTA),
  - Canada & Peru (Chapters 2/3 of CPFTA),
  - Canada & Colombia (Chapters 2/3 of CCOFTA),
  - Canada & Jordan (Chapters 2/4 of CJFTA),
  - Canada & Panama (Chapters 2/3 of CPAFTA),
  - Canada & Honduras (Chapters 3/4 of CHFTA). |
| **Change in material facts** | New facts emerge that alter the basis of the ruling. |
| **Denial or withdrawal of preferential treatment** | Under subsection 42.1(2) or (3) of the *Customs Act*. |
| **Non-compliance with terms** | Exporters/producers fail to comply with RVC or other conditions of the ruling. |
| **Inconsistent operations** | Exporter's operations do not align with the facts used in the ruling. |
| **Incorrect supporting data** | Errors in cost allocation or value computation used to determine RVC. |
| **Changes in law or agreement** | Modifications to:
  - Chapter 3, 4, 5, or 7 of NAFTA,
  - Chapter C, D, or E of CCFTA,
  - Chapter Three of CIFTA,
  - Chapters III, IV, V of CCRFTA,
  - Chapter II or Annex C to CEFTA,
  - Chapters 2, 3, 4 of CPFTA, CCOFTA, CJFTA, CPAFTA, or CHFTA,
  - Or decisions by Canadian courts or tribunals. |

> These grounds ensure that rulings remain **accurate, legally sound, and aligned with evolving trade agreements**.

---

#### **13. Notice and Effect of Modification or Revocation**

- Officers must issue **written notice** of any modification or revocation, including:
  - The **effective date** of the change.
- The modification or revocation becomes effective on:
  - The date issued, **or**
  - A later date specified in the notice (not later than the importation date).

> This provides legal certainty and allows stakeholders to respond appropriately.
