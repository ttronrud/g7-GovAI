**Detailed Summary of the Criminal Appeals and Bail Applications Rules for the Court of Appeal for the Northwest Territories (under Section 438 of the Criminal Code)**

---

### **Overview**
These Rules are issued under **Section 438 of the Criminal Code** by the Judges of the **Court of Appeal for the Northwest Territories (NWT)**. They establish a comprehensive framework governing **criminal appeals** and **bail applications pending appeal** in the territory. The Rules apply to appeals from trial dispositions (convictions, acquittals, sentences, or findings of unfitness to stand trial or “not guilty by reason of insanity”) under:
- **Part XXI of the Criminal Code**,  
- **Section 839 of the Code**, or  
- Any other statute or ordinance granting a right of appeal in summary conviction matters.

These Rules are designed to ensure procedural consistency, fairness, and efficiency in appellate proceedings while providing flexibility where appropriate.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Appellant** | Any person entitled to appeal — includes individuals appealing against conviction/sentence or those appealing from findings of insanity or unfitness to stand trial. Also includes the **Attorney-General**. |
| **Appellate Judge** | A judge of the Court of Appeal, including ex officio judges. |
| **Attorney-General** | As defined in s. 2 of the Code; includes counsel instructed by the AG for appeal purposes. |
| **Code** | The Criminal Code of Canada. |
| **Court** | The Court of Appeal for the Northwest Territories. |
| **Prisoner Appeal** | An appeal filed by a person in custody who is not represented by counsel. |
| **Sentence Appeal** | An appeal involving only the sentence, not the conviction or verdict. |
| **Trial Disposition** | A conviction, sentence, acquittal, or finding that the accused is unfit to stand trial or “not guilty by reason of insanity.” |
| **Trial Judge** | The judge who presided over the trial — includes Territorial Court or Supreme Court judges and deputy judges. |
| **Warden** | The official in charge of any custodial institution (e.g., gaol, penitentiary, remand centre, mental hospital). |
| **Registrar** | The Registrar of the Court of Appeal in Yellowknife, and includes deputies/assistants. Maintains records of appeals at a designated office in Edmonton, Alberta. |

## **General Principles and Procedural Framework**

### **1. Applicability of the Rules**
- These Rules govern appeals to the Court under:
  - Part XXI of the Criminal Code (appeals from summary conviction),
  - Section 839 of the Code (appeals from certain summary conviction convictions),
  - Any other statute or ordinance allowing appeal in summary conviction matters.
- In matters not specifically addressed, the **Rules of Court for civil appeals** apply *mutatis mutandis*, **except that cross-appeals are not permitted**.

### **2. Non-Compliance and Time Extensions**
- **Failure to comply with the Rules does not render proceedings void**.
- However, the Court or an Appellate Judge may **amend, set aside, or otherwise deal with** non-compliance as justice requires.
- Any time limit specified in the Rules may be **extended or shortened** by the Court or an Appellate Judge.
- A **two-day written notice** must be given to the opposing party unless:
  - The extension is agreed upon (consented to), or
  - The Court orders it otherwise.
- If an appeal is dismissed due to failure to appear, the order may be **vacated on special application** to the Court.

### **3. Evidence in Appeals**
- If the Court grants **leave to call further evidence**, the rules for evidence in civil matters (under the Rules of the Supreme Court) apply.
- Evidence may be obtained through:
  - Witness attendance,
  - Production of documents,
  - Use of depositions (under s. 540), or
  - Other methods permitted under civil rules.

## **Notice of Appeal and Filing Procedures**

### **Time Limits for Filing a Notice of Appeal**
All notices of appeal must be filed and served within **60 days** of the following events:
- Conviction or conviction and sentence,
- Acquittal,
- A finding of “not guilty by reason of insanity” (s. 675(3)),
- A finding or verdict that the accused is unfit to stand trial (s. 675(3) or s. 676(3)).

> *Note: These deadlines are strict and non-extendable unless specifically allowed.*

---

### **Filing and Serving of the Notice of Appeal**

| Type of Appeal | Filing & Serving Procedure |
|---------------|----------------------------|
| **Prisoner Appeal** (unrepresented) | Serve **3 copies** on the **warden or designated officer** of the institution. The officer must: <br> - Endorse receipt date, <br> - Return one copy to the appellant, <br> - Forward one to the Registrar, <br> - Retain one copy. |
| **Other Appeals (convicted person)** | File **3 copies** with the Registrar or mail them by **registered mail** to the Registrar. |
| **Appeal by Attorney-General** | File **2 copies** with the Registrar and **personally serve** the notice on the accused (or respondent), **unless ordered otherwise** by an Appellate Judge. <br> ⚠️ **If substitutional service is ordered**, no argument may occur without **leave of the Court**, which may issue further directions. |

> Upon receipt (except for AG appeals), the **Registrar must immediately forward a copy** to:
> - The Attorney-General or his counsel,
> - In cases tried before a Territorial Court judge → immediately forward to that judge,
> - In all other cases → forward to the **clerk of the trial court**.

---

### **Formal Requirements for Notices of Appeal**

- In **prisoner appeals** or **unrepresented appeals**, the notice must be in **Form A** or equivalent.
- In all other appeals, it must be in **Form B** or equivalent.
- Every notice must:
  - State the **grounds for appeal**,
  - Be **signed by the appellant or their counsel**.

> **No time or place for hearing is stated** in the notice. The Registrar will:
> - Enter the case on the **lists for the next sitting** of the Court,
> - Issue directions for hearing as the Court or a judge deems appropriate.

---

### **Notice of Leave to Appeal**

- **Applications for leave to appeal** are made via filing a notice of appeal.
- **No separate notice is required**.
- If the Court grants leave:
  - It may **determine the appeal on the merits immediately**, or
  - Direct that it be heard at a later date.

> For **applications to an Appellate Judge**:
> - A notice of appeal is filed,
> - A **notice of motion** is filed contemporaneously or thereafter,
> - Must be **served on the respondent at least 2 clear days before the return of the motion**, unless ordered otherwise.

> If an **Appellate Judge refuses leave** to appeal:
> - In **s. 675(1)(a)(ii)** (application for certificate under s. 675(1)(a)(ii)) → the applicant may file a notice within **7 days** to have the refusal reviewed by the Court.
> - In **s. 675(1)(b)** (sentence appeal) → the refusal is **final**, and no further appeal lies.

---

## **Certificate Applications (s. 675(1)(a)(ii))**

- An application for a certificate (to allow appeal from conviction) must be made within **30 days of sentence**.
- May be made **ex parte** or with **at least two clear days’ notice to the Attorney-General**.
- If notice is required:
  - Notice may be returnable **before or after** the 30-day period,
  - A copy must be **mailed to or filed with the Registrar**,
  - If the certificate is granted, the notice of appeal must be filed within:
    - 30 days of sentence, or
    - 2 days after the certificate is granted — **whichever is later**.

---

## **Sentence Appeals – Special Procedures**

### **Filing of "Sentence Material" (for convicted persons with counsel)**

- The appellant must file **6 copies** of the **Sentence Material** with the Registrar at least **14 days before the Court opens**.
- A **further copy** must be served on the respondent immediately.

**Sentence Material must include:**
1. The Information or Indictment and sentence,
2. A Statement of Facts as related to the trial judge,
3. Any pre-sentence or post-sentence reports,
4. Transcript of the trial judge’s Reasons for Judgment and sentence,
5. The convicted person’s criminal record (if disclosed to the trial judge).

> If an **appeal book** is filed, the Sentence Material need not include material already in it.

---

### **Respondent’s Supporting Material**

- The respondent must file **6 copies** of written material supporting their argument with the Registrar **7 days before the Court opens**, unless ordered otherwise.
- A copy must be **immediately served on the appellant**.
