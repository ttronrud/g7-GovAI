### **Regulation Summary: First Nations Tax Commissioner Appointment Regulations**  
**Statutory Authority:**  
- **First Nations Fiscal and Statistical Management Act** (formerly known as the First Nations Fiscal Management Act)  
- Specific provisions:  
  - **Subsection 20(3)**: Authorizes the appointment of a First Nations Tax Commissioner  
  - **Paragraph 140(a)**: Provides the basis for establishing procedures related to tax commissioner appointments  

**Governing Body:**  
- **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council**, acting on the **recommendation of the Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development**  
- This indicates that the appointment authority is exercised at the federal level, with the Minister providing the formal recommendation to the Governor General, who formally issues the regulation.

---

### **Core Purpose of the Regulation**  
The regulation establishes the **procedures and mechanism** for appointing a **First Nations Tax Commissioner** in accordance with the legal framework set out in the First Nations Fiscal and Statistical Management Act.

---

### **Key Provision: Appointment Mechanism**  
- **The Commissioner shall be appointed by the Indigenous Law Centre at the University of Saskatchewan.**  
  - This is a significant and distinctive feature of the regulation.  
  - It designates a specific, independent, and academically grounded institution—**the Indigenous Law Centre (ILC)**—as the body responsible for identifying and recommending qualified individuals for the position.  
  - The ILC is well-positioned to provide legal, cultural, and community-based expertise, ensuring that the commissioner is not only legally competent but also culturally appropriate and accountable to First Nations communities.

---

### **Scope and Application**  
- The regulation applies specifically to **subsection 20(3)** of the First Nations Fiscal Management Act.  
- It defines the **process** by which a tax commissioner is appointed, ensuring transparency, accountability, and community engagement.  
- The commissioner is responsible for managing tax-related matters within First Nations, including ensuring compliance with fiscal and tax laws, promoting financial accountability, and supporting the financial self-governance of First Nations communities.

---

### **Effective Date**  
- The regulations **come into force on the day they are registered** in the Canada Gazette.  
- This means there is no delay in implementation—once officially published, the appointment process is immediately operational.

### **Significance and Implications**  
1. **Community-Centered Approach**:  
   - By involving the Indigenous Law Centre, the regulation emphasizes **Indigenous leadership and knowledge** in fiscal governance.  
   - It supports the principle of **self-determination** by enabling First Nations to have trusted, culturally competent professionals manage their tax affairs.

2. **Transparency and Accountability**:  
   - The appointment process is clearly defined and institutionalized, reducing potential for bias or arbitrary decisions.

3. **Legal Clarity**:  
   - The regulation removes ambiguity about who is responsible for appointing the commissioner, providing a clear legal pathway under federal law.

4. **Support for Financial Capacity Building**:
