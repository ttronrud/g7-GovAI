### ðŸ”¹ **2. Purpose of the Regulations**
The primary purpose is to **determine the contribution rates** for the Canada Pension Plan, including:
- The **base contribution rate**
- **Additional contribution rates** (for new or increased benefits)
- Adjustments based on **actuarial projections** over time

These rates are set to ensure **long-term financial sustainability** of the CPP by maintaining a **stable ratio of assets to expenditures**, especially over a long-term horizon (up to 2098).

#### âœ… **(A) Base Contribution Rate (Subparagraph 115(1.1)(c)(i))**
The base contribution rate is the **smallest multiple of 0.0001 percentage points** that ensures the **projected ratio of assets to expenditures** at **Year 60** after the review period is **not lower** than the ratio at **Year 10** after the review period.

> This ensures long-term solvency â€” the plan wonâ€™t run out of money in the future.

**Formula:**
\[
\frac{A_{60}}{E_{60}} \geq \frac{A_{10}}{E_{10}}
\]
Where:
- \( A_{60} \): Projected value of **base CPP assets** on Dec 31 of Year 60
- \( A_{10} \): Projected value of **base CPP assets** on Dec 31 of Year 10
- \( E_{60} \): Projected expenditures (contributions) in Year 61
- \( E_{10} \): Projected expenditures in Year 11

> ðŸ“Œ The formula is applied to **all contributions**, including those related to increased or new benefits (if they exceed zero).

#### âœ… **(B) Contribution Rate for Increased or New Benefits (Subparagraph 115(1.1)(c)(ii))**
This rate includes:
- A **permanent increase** (long-term adjustment)
- A **temporary increase** (short-term, phased-in adjustment)

**Permanent Increase**:
- Smallest multiple of 0.0001 pp that satisfies:
\[
\text{PV of Assets (for new benefits)} \leq \text{PV of Contributions} + \text{PV of Extra Costs}
\]
Where:
- PV of assets = present value of assets related to new benefits
- PV of contributions = present value of contributions due to the permanent increase
- PV of extra costs = present value of additional costs from the new benefits

> This ensures that the **long-term financial burden** of new benefits is sustainable.

**Temporary Increase**:
- Applies for a period consistent with **common actuarial practice**
- Smallest multiple of 0.0001 pp that satisfies:
\[
\text{PV of Assets (before benefit start)} \leq \text{PV of Contributions (temporary)} + \text{PV of Extra Costs (before benefit start)}
\]
> This ensures that the **short-term impact** of new benefits does not exceed the funding capacity.

> âš ï¸ **If the contribution rate is less than 0.02 percentage points**, it is **deemed zero** for that year and all future years (to prevent negligible or negative contributions).

### ðŸ”¹ **5. Additional Contribution Rates (for the "Additional CPP")**

These apply to **new or expanded benefits** under the **Additional Canada Pension Plan (ACPP)**, which is a separate component of the CPP.

#### (a) First and Second Additional Contribution Rates
- These are the **smallest multiples of 0.0001 percentage points** that satisfy two conditions:

1. **Present Value Condition**:
   - The present value of **projected expenditures** (for the ACPP) must be **less than or equal to**:
     - Present value of projected contributions (without new benefits)
     - Plus projected value of assets (without new benefits)

2. **Asset-to-Expenditure Ratio Condition**:
   - The **ratio of assets to expenditures at Year 60** must be **no lower** than the ratio at **Year 50** after the review period.

> âš ï¸ **If the 60th year after the review period is earlier than 2098**, it is **deemed 2098**, and the 50th year is **deemed 2088**.

---

#### (b) Permanent and Temporary Increases for Additional Rates
- **Permanent Increase**:
  - Calculated as the difference between:
    - The actual first/second additional rate (under section 4)
    - The rate that would result **if new benefits were excluded** from asset and cost projections
  - Applies only when the new benefits are financially significant.

- **Temporary Increase**:
  - Applies for a period consistent with actuarial standards
  - Must satisfy a similar formula to the permanent increase, but **before** the benefits start
  - The second additional rate is **scaled by the additional contribution rate ratio** (i.e., multiplied by the ratio from paragraph 46(1)(c)/(b))

> âŒ **No temporary increase** if the permanent increase is less than **0.02 percentage points**
