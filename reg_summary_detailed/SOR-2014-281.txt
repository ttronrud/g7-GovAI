**Detailed Summary of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police Regulations, 2014**

---

### **Introduction and Legal Authority**
The *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Regulations, 2014* are enacted by **His Excellency the Governor General in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness**, under specific provisions of the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act* (RCMP Act). These provisions include:

- Subsections 11(1)a, 21(1)b, 23(4)c, 31(7)d, and 33(4)d  
- Section 38d of the RCMP Act  

These regulations provide detailed administrative, operational, and procedural guidelines for the organization, structure, duties, personnel management, and conduct of members within the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP).

The regulations are published under:
- S.C. 2013, c. 18, s. 11  
- S.C. 2013, c. 18, s. 14(1)  
- R.S., c. 8 (2nd Supp.), s. 14  
- R.S., c. 8 (2nd Supp.), s. 16  
- R.S., c. R-10  

---

## **1. Key Definitions**

The regulations establish precise definitions of key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act* |
| **Civilian member** | A person appointed to a level in the Force under subsection 7(1) of the Act |
| **Common-law partner** | A person cohabiting with another in a conjugal relationship for at least one year |
| **Post** | A place where a member is assigned, either permanently or temporarily |
| **Regular member** | A person appointed to a rank; includes a special constable |
| **Reserve** | The RCMP Reserve established under subsection 11(1) of the Act |
| **Reservist** | A person appointed to the Reserve under subsection 7(2) |
| **Special constable** | A person appointed *before June 30, 1988* to the rank of special constable |
| **Special constable member** | A person appointed *on or after June 30, 1988* to the rank of special constable member |

> *Note: This distinction reflects a historical shift in the structure and status of special constables, with the latter being integrated into the formal hierarchy.*

## **2. Organizational Structure of the Force**

### **Divisions and Command Structure**
- The **Minister** may divide the RCMP into **divisions**.
- Each division includes members and personnel as directed by the **Commissioner**.
- The **Commanding Officer** of each division may be designated by the **Governor in Council**.
- Headquarters of divisions are located where the Minister directs.
- The **Commissioner** has authority to:
  - Divide a division into **organizational components** (e.g., units or branches)
  - Assign names and locations to these components
  - Group divisions or components together for operational efficiency

### **Command Precedence**
- In the absence of a commanding officer, command of a post follows a **prescribed order of precedence** (set out in section 12 of the Act).
- Precedence among **regular members** (excluding special constables) is based on rank:
  1. Commissioner  
  2. Deputy Commissioner  
  3. Assistant Commissioner  
  4. Chief Superintendent  
  5. Superintendent  
  6. Inspector  
  7. Corps Sergeant-Major  
  8. Sergeant-Major  
  9. Staff Sergeant-Major  
  10. Staff Sergeant  
  11. Sergeant  
  12. Corporal  
  13. Constable  

> **Note:** Precedence is determined by **date of appointment or promotion**. The Commissioner must determine precedence between:
> - Special constables  
> - Special constable members  
> - Civilian members

## **3. The RCMP Reserve**

### **Establishment and Structure**
- The **RCMP Reserve** is formally established under the Act.
- The **Commissioner** organizes the Reserve as directed and appoints members up to a maximum number set by **Treasury Board**.

### **Appointment and Tenure**
- Reservists may be appointed for a **maximum of three years** and may be **revoked at any time**.
- Appointment requires:
  - Good character  
  - Meeting other qualifications determined by the Commissioner

### **Call-up and Duties**
- The Commissioner may call reservists for **training or duty** when necessary.
- Reservists called up for duty are paid **Treasury Board-determined pay and allowances**.
- If designated as a **peace officer**, they have the same duties as regular officers under:
  - Section 18 of the Act  
  - Section 14 of the Act  

### **Resignation**
- A reservist may resign by providing **written notice** to the Commissioner.
- Resignation becomes effective upon the Commissioner’s written acceptance.
- The Commissioner may **refuse** the resignation and must provide **written reasons**.

## **4. Duties of Members (Peace Officers)**

All members who are peace officers have the following duties:

1. **Enforce laws** – All Acts of Parliament and regulations.
2. **Render assistance** – To any department of the Government of Canada as directed by the Minister.
3. **Maintain law and order** in:
   - Yukon, Northwest Territories, Nunavut
   - National parks
   - Provinces and municipalities with which the Minister has entered into arrangements under section 20 of the Act
4. **Guard and protect**:
   - Government property designated by the Minister
   - Specific individuals including:
     - The Governor General  
     - The Prime Minister  
     - Judges of the Supreme Court of Canada  
     - Ministers of the Crown  
     - Canadian citizens or permanent residents (as defined in the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act*) designated by the Minister  
     - Internationally protected persons (as defined in the *Criminal Code*)  
     - Other foreign nationals designated by the Minister  
5. **Ensure security** of:
   - Meetings of first ministers and the Prime Minister (not held on Parliament Hill)  
   - Cabinet meetings (not held on Parliament Hill)  
   > *These duties are carried out in accordance with the RCMP’s assessment of threat or risk.*

## **5. Service of Documents**

The regulations define how documents required under the Act must be served:

### **Methods of Service**
- **Personal service**:
  - Delivered to the individual or a person of majority age (not legally disabled) residing at the individual’s address.
  - If minor or disabled, delivered to a guardian or representative.
- **By mail or courier**:
  - Ordinary mail with receipt card  
  - Registered mail to last known address  
  - Courier delivery to last known address  
  - For minors/disabled: sent to or delivered to the representative
- **Electronic means**:
  - Sent to the individual’s email address  
  - If minor/disabled: sent to representative’s email

### **Proof of Service**
- Required proof includes:
  - Signed receipt (individual or representative)
  - Post office receipt (registered mail)
  - Affidavit of service  
- **In all cases**, an affidavit of service is required.

### **Deemed Service**
- If no proof is available, service is deemed to have occurred on the **seventh day after**:
  - Mailing date  
  - Courier delivery date  
  - Electronic transmission date  

### **Refusal and Good Faith Exceptions**
- If a person refuses personal service, it is deemed effective at the time of refusal **if**:
  - The refusal is recorded  
  - A copy is left with the individual  
- If the individual proves they acted in **good faith** but failed to receive the document due to circumstances beyond their control, the decision-maker may:
  - Set a different service date  
  - Extend the service period  

### **Alternate Methods**
- The Commissioner may permit **alternate service methods** when personal service is not feasible.

## **6. Appeals and Grievances**

### **Appeals to the Committee**
Any adjudicator (under the Commissioner’s Standing Orders) must refer the following appeals to the **Committee** before deciding:

1. Appeals by a complainant against a written decision under:
   - Subsection 6(1) and paragraph 6(2)(b) of the *Investigation and Resolution of Harassment Complaints* Standing Orders  
2. Appeals against decisions to:
   - Revoke a member’s appointment (under section 9.2 of the Act)  
   - Discharge or demote a member under:
     - Paragraph 20.2(1)(e): disability (under Canadian Human Rights Act)  
     - Paragraph 20.2(1)(g): absence from duty, conflict of interest  
   - Order stoppage of pay and allowances (under paragraph 22(2)(b))

> *Note: This ensures a formal review process for significant disciplinary or administrative actions.*

---

## **7. Code of Conduct**

- All members must comply with the **Code of Conduct** as set out in the **Schedule** to the regulations.
- This includes standards of behavior, integrity, impartiality, and professionalism.
