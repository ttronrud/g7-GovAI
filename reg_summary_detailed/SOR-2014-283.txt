### **Detailed Summary of the CJFTA Verification of Origin Regulations**

---

#### **1. Background and Legal Basis**

These regulations implement provisions of **Customs Notice 12-027**, issued on **September 28, 2012**, which outlines amendments to Canada’s customs regulations related to the **Canada-Jordan Free Trade Agreement (CJFTA)**. Specifically, the regulations provide the administrative framework for verifying the **origin of goods** eligible for preferential tariff treatment under the CJFTA.

The regulations come into force on **October 1, 2012**, as specified in the notice.

They are made under the authority of the **Customs Act** and specifically pursuant to:
- **Section 42.1a** of the Customs Act,
- **Paragraphs 164(1)(i)b and (j)**,
- **Subsection 164(1.1)c**, and
- **Paragraph 167.1(b)d**.

This legislative basis enables Canada to verify the origin of goods to ensure compliance with CJFTA requirements and to prevent fraud or misrepresentation in claims for preferential tariff treatment.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined to ensure clarity and consistency in the application of the regulations:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Customs Act*. |
| **Competent Authority** | The Ministry of Industry and Trade of Jordan or the Jordan Customs Department (or their successors), as designated by Jordan to Canada. Canada must receive written notice from Jordan identifying this authority. |
| **Customs Administration** | The Canadian authority responsible for administering Canada’s customs laws (e.g., the Canada Border Services Agency – CBSA). |
| **Goods** | Goods for which preferential tariff treatment under the CJFTA is claimed. |
| **Material** | As defined in **Article 1-7 of the CJFTA**, meaning raw materials, components, or inputs used in the production of goods. |
| **Verification Letter** | A formal communication requesting information about the origin of goods subject to a verification of origin. |
| **Verification Questionnaire** | A structured form requesting information about the origin of goods. |
| **Verification Visit** | A physical inspection of premises (in Jordan) to verify the origin of goods. |

---

#### **3. Methods of Origin Verification**

A verification of origin may be conducted through **one or more of the following methods**:

1. **Verification Visit**  
   A physical inspection of the premises where goods are produced or stored.

2. **Review of a Verification Questionnaire**  
   Completed by:
   - The **exporter or producer** of the goods, or  
   - A **producer or supplier of a material**.

3. **Written Response to a Verification Letter**  
   A response from the exporter, producer, or supplier to a formal request for origin information.

4. **Other Relevant Information**  
   Any additional information received from the exporter, producer, or supplier.

> *Note:* These methods can be used individually or in combination.

---

#### **4. Requirements for Verification Letters and Questionnaires**

All verification letters and questionnaires must:

- Clearly identify the **Canadian customs administration** and the **name and title** of the officer sending the document;
- Clearly describe the **goods or materials** being verified;
- Specify a **minimum 30-day response period**, beginning on the date of receipt.

Additionally, the recipient has the right to:
- Request a **single extension** of up to **30 additional days** within the specified period.
- This extension must be submitted in writing and is subject to approval by the customs administration.

---

#### **5. Prescribed Premises for Verification Visits**

The following entities in **Jordan** are considered **prescribed premises** for a verification visit:

- An **exporter or producer** of goods;
- A **producer or supplier** of a material.

> *Only these entities are eligible to be the subject of a physical verification visit.*

---

#### **6. Conditions for Conducting a Verification Visit**

An officer may only conduct a verification visit if **all of the following conditions are met**:

1. A **written notice** is sent to:
   - The person whose premises are to be visited,
   - The **competent authority in Jordan**, and
   - (If requested by Jordan) the **Canadian Embassy in Jordan**.

2. The person whose premises are to be visited **consents in writing** to the visit.

---

#### **7. Content of the Verification Visit Notice**

The written notice must include:

- Identity of the **Canadian customs administration**;
- Name and title of the **officer conducting the visit**;
- Name of the **person whose premises are being visited**;
- **Date and place** of the visit;
- **Object and scope** of the visit (including description of goods/materials);
- **Legal authority** justifying the visit (e.g., reference to section 42.1(1)(a) of the Customs Act).

---

#### **8. Right to Postpone a Verification Visit**

The person receiving the notice may:
- Request a **postponement** in writing within **15 days** of receiving the notice;
- The postponement may be for **up to 60 days** from the date of notice, or for a longer period **agreed upon by both parties**.

> *Note:* If the **Jordanian competent authority** sends a written request for postponement within 15 days of receiving the notice, the visit is automatically postponed under the same conditions.

---

#### **9. Right to Designate Observers**

The person whose premises are being visited may:
- Designate **two observers** to be present during the visit;
- Observers are **limited to observation only** (no participation in interviews or record review);
- The observers must be **identified in writing** to the officer conducting the visit.

---

#### **10. Obligations of Exporters and Producers (Under Section 42.1(2))**

For the purpose of verifying origin under paragraph 42.1(1)(a) of the Customs Act, exporters and producers must:

1. **Maintain records** relevant to determining the origin of goods, in accordance with the laws of Jordan (as applicable);
2. **Provide access** to those records to the customs officer;
3. **Respond promptly** to verification letters or complete and return verification questionnaires within the **30-day period** specified in the letter or questionnaire.

> *This obligation applies regardless of whether a physical visit occurs.*

---

#### **11. Consent to Verification Visit**

The **exporter or producer** must provide **written consent** to the verification visit within **30 days** of receiving the notice under paragraph 6(1)(a)(i).

Failure to provide timely consent may result in the visit being denied or delayed.

---

#### **12. Re-Determination of Origin (Under Section 59(1))**

If a customs officer determines that goods are **not eligible** for preferential tariff treatment under the CJFTA, they must:

- Send a **written notice** to the exporter or producer;
- Include a **statement indicating the goods are ineligible**;
- Specify the **date from which preferential treatment may be denied or withdrawn**;
- Provide a **period during which the exporter or producer may submit written comments or additional information** regarding the origin of the goods.

This allows for a fair opportunity to appeal or clarify the origin claim.

---

#### **13. Delivery and Receipt Confirmation**

All verification letters, questionnaires, and notices (including those under paragraph 6(1)(a) and section 10) must be sent via a method that **produces confirmation of receipt** (e.g., email with read receipt, certified mail, tracked delivery).

> This ensures accountability and traceability in the verification process.

---

#### **14. Effective Date**

These regulations are **deemed to have come into force on October 1, 2012**, the date specified in the original public announcement.

---

### **Summary of Key Provisions and Implications**

| Key Feature | Significance |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Origin Verification Mechanism** | Ensures that goods claiming CJFTA preferential treatment genuinely originate in Jordan. |
| **Flexibility in Verification Methods** | Allows Canada to use letters, questionnaires, or physical visits depending on circumstances. |
| **Transparency and Accountability** | Requires written notices, response periods, and confirmation of receipt. |
| **Respect for Jordanian Legal Framework** | Requires compliance with Jordan’s domestic record-keeping and origin laws. |
| **Fair Process for Respondents** | Includes rights to request extensions, postpone visits, and have observers present. |
| **Enforcement Mechanism** | Enables denial of preferential treatment if origin is found to be false or misstated. |

---

### **Conclusion**

The **CJFTA Verification of Origin Regulations** establish a **transparent, fair, and legally sound process** for verifying the origin of goods imported into Canada under the Canada-Jordan Free Trade Agreement. By balancing the need for regulatory enforcement with respect for the rights of exporters and producers, the regulations support the integrity of the CJFTA while promoting trade efficiency and compliance.

These rules are critical for maintaining the credibility of preferential tariff treatment and ensuring that only genuinely Jordan-origin goods benefit from reduced or zero tariffs under the agreement. They also reflect Canada’s commitment to international trade cooperation and mutual respect in customs administration.
