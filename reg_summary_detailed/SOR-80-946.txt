### **4. Closure and Prohibition Notices**

When a superintendent determines that fire prevention or management requires action, they may:

- Close an area or highway to public use or travel  
- Prohibit smoking  
- Prohibit starting or maintaining fires in certain areas  

#### Requirements for Posting Notices:
- The notice must be **prominently displayed** in the affected area, on highways, or in other conspicuous locations.
- The notice must clearly state one or more of the following:
  - Area or highway is closed to public use or travel  
  - Smoking is prohibited  
  - Starting or maintaining fires is prohibited  

#### Legal Consequences of Violating a Notice:
- **No person may**:
  - Enter the closed area or highway  
  - Smoke in the prohibited area  
  - Start or maintain a fire in the prohibited area  
- Any person in a closed area **must leave immediately** upon closure.

### **10. Fire Prevention Inspections and Enforcement**

#### Right to Enter Buildings:
- Owners or occupants of buildings, structures, or places in parks must **permit entry** by a fire prevention inspector at any reasonable time for inspection purposes.

#### Inspection and Orders:
- A fire prevention inspector may issue an order requiring:
  - **Alterations, renovations, or repairs** to prevent fire hazards.
  - **Closure of buildings** to the public or habitation.

#### Closure Orders:
- **Public-access buildings**: Must be closed **immediately** upon order.
- **Non-public buildings**: Must be closed to habitation and use **30 days after service of the order**, and remain closed until compliance is achieved.

#### Notification of Orders:
- The inspector must notify the owner/occupant **in writing** by:
  - Registered mail  
  - Personal service  

#### Compliance:
- Owners or occupants are **legally required** to comply with any order issued under sections 10 or 11.
