## **1. Purpose and Legal Basis**

This regulation establishes the **types of passenger information** that Canadian aviation operators and authorities may provide to **foreign governments or agencies** under specific conditions, as authorized by **Section 4.83 of the Aeronautics Act**. It also specifies the conditions under which such information may be shared ‚Äî particularly with the **United States Department of Homeland Security (DHS)**.

The regulation is designed to:
- Facilitate international cooperation in aviation security and passenger screening.
- Ensure that foreign authorities have access to relevant passenger data when an aircraft is scheduled to land in a foreign state or fly over the United States.
- Maintain compliance with international obligations and aviation safety standards.

---

## **2. Scope of the Regulation**

The regulation applies in two main scenarios:

---

### **A. Aircraft Due to Land in a Foreign State**

Under **Section 4.83 of the Aeronautics Act**, information about persons on board or expected to be on board an aircraft may be shared with a **competent authority in a foreign state** ‚Äî **only if**:
- The foreign state and the competent authority are listed in **Schedule 3** of the regulation.

#### ‚úÖ Information That May Be Provided:
The following details about passengers are eligible for disclosure to the foreign authority:

| Category | Specific Information |
|--------|------------------------|
| **Personal Identifiers** | Surname, first name, initial(s) |
| **Demographics** | Date of birth, gender |
| **Citizenship/Travel Documents** | Citizenship or nationality; if not available, the country that issued the travel document (e.g., passport) |
| **Passport Details** | Passport number; if applicable, visa or resident alien card number |
| **Passenger Name Record (PNR) Creation** | Date when the PNR was created |
| **Ticketing & Travel Arrangements** | Notation if passenger arrived at the departure gate with a ticket but no reservation; names of travel agency/agent; date ticket was issued; whether ticket was exchanged; date payment was due or reservation queued |
| **Ticket Details** | Ticket number; whether it is one-way; validity (e.g., valid for one year, no dates/flight numbers assigned); origin city/country of travel |
| **Itinerary** | All embarkation and disembarkation points (itinerary cities) |
| **Operator Information** | Name of the aircraft operator; names of other operators involved in segments of the journey (including any non-ticket-issuing operators); operator code and flight identification number |
| **Flight Details** | Destination in the foreign state; travel date; seat assignment (if selected); number of checked bags; baggage tag numbers; class of service; stated seat requests |
| **Contact Information** | Phone numbers of passenger and/or travel agency; address of passenger and/or travel agency |
| **Payment Information** | Method of payment; whether paid by someone other than the ticket holder |
| **Itinerary Gaps** | Notation if there are gaps requiring undetermined travel methods |
| **Routing Information** | Departure and arrival points, operator codes, stops, and surface segments (e.g., ground transport) |
| **Electronic Ticketing** | Notation if the ticket is electronic and stored in the operator‚Äôs reservation system |

> The information listed in **Schedule 2** (which includes most of the above) may **only be provided upon request** by the foreign state or its competent authority.

### **B. Aircraft Due to Fly Over the United States**

Under **Section 4.83 of the Aeronautics Act**, Canadian operators may provide specific passenger data to the **United States Department of Homeland Security (DHS)** when an aircraft is scheduled to **fly over U.S. airspace**.

#### ‚úÖ Information That Must Be Provided to DHS:

| Category | Specific Information |
|--------|------------------------|
| **Passenger Name** | Full name |
| **Demographics** | Date of birth, gender |
| **Passport Details** | Passport number; country of issuance |
| **Passport Expiry** | Expiry date of passport |
| **Airport Codes** | IATA airport code ‚Äî place of origination; IATA airport code ‚Äî port of first arrival |
| **Air Carrier Details** | Air carrier code |
| **Flight Information** | Flight number; departure date and time; arrival date and scheduled arrival time |
| **Reservation Details** | Reservation control number; record sequence number; record type; passenger update indicator |
| **Traveler Reference** | Traveler reference number (also known as "redress number" or "known traveler number") |

> üîç **Note**: This list is more limited than the full Schedule 2 data and is tailored specifically for U.S. border and security screening purposes.

## **3. Key Conditions and Limitations**

| Condition | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Foreign State Authorization** | Disclosure is permitted only if the foreign state and its competent authority are explicitly listed in **Schedule 3**. |
| **Request-Based Disclosure** | Information in Schedule 2 (for foreign states) is **not automatically shared** ‚Äî it must be provided **upon formal request**. |
| **No Mandatory Sharing** | The regulation does **not require** sharing of information unless a foreign authority makes a request or the flight is over U.S. airspace. |
| **Data Minimization** | Only necessary data is shared, with emphasis on protecting privacy and ensuring relevance to security purposes. |
| **Operator Responsibility** | The airline or operator is responsible for collecting, maintaining, and transmitting accurate passenger data in accordance with these rules. |

## **4. Purpose and Rationale**

This regulation supports:
- **International aviation security cooperation**, especially in preventing terrorism and illegal activities.
- **Efficient border control and passenger screening**, particularly in the U.S. and other designated countries.
- **Compliance with international agreements**, such as those under the **International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO)** and bilateral security arrangements.

It balances the need for **security and transparency** with the **protection of individual privacy rights** and **data protection principles**.

---

## **5. Privacy and Legal Considerations**

- The regulation is part of a broader framework that respects **privacy laws** and **data protection standards**.
- It is **not a blanket data-sharing policy** ‚Äî it is strictly limited to specific flights, specific authorities, and specific data fields.
- Canadian law (including the *Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act* or PIPEDA) may apply to the handling of personal data, and operators must ensure compliance with privacy obligations when sharing information.
