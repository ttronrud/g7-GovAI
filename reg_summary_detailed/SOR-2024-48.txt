**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

---

### **Regulation Title:**  
*Fourth Session of the Intergovernmental Negotiating Committee on Plastic Pollution (Ottawa 2024 – INC-4) Privileges and Immunities Order*

---

### **Legal Basis and Authority**
This regulation is issued under **subsection 5(1)a of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act*** (FMIOA), which empowers the Governor General in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs) to grant privileges and immunities to representatives and officials of international organizations participating in specific meetings.

The regulation is grounded in the **Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations** (the "Convention"), as set out in **Schedule III to the FMIOA**. It applies to a specific diplomatic and multilateral negotiation event — the **Fourth Session of the Intergovernmental Negotiating Committee on Plastic Pollution (INC-4)** — held in Ottawa in 2024.

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**
The primary purpose of this Order is to **ensure the independence, security, and operational effectiveness** of the INC-4 meetings by providing legal protections — including immunity from legal action, inviolability of premises, and protection of personal and official records — to participants and officials involved in the negotiations on a globally binding international instrument to combat plastic pollution, including in marine environments.

This is essential to maintain the integrity of diplomatic processes and to allow participants to conduct negotiations freely and without fear of interference or legal liability.

---

### **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Agreement** | The formal agreement between the United Nations, represented by the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP), and the Government of Canada, concerning the Fourth Session of the Intergovernmental Negotiating Committee on Plastic Pollution (INC-4). |
| **Convention** | The *Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations*, as codified in Schedule III of the FMIOA. |
| **Meetings** | Two events: (1) Regional preparatory consultations on April 21, 2024, in Ottawa; and (2) The main INC-4 session from April 23 to 30, 2024, in Ottawa. |
| **Relevant Period** | The time frame from **April 19, 2024, to May 3, 2024** — encompassing the lead-up to and the duration of the meetings. |

---

### **Core Provisions and Privileges and Immunities**

#### **1. Inviolability of Premises and Archives**
- During the relevant period, the **premises** (as defined in Article 3 of the Agreement) and the **archives and documents** of the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) are **inviolable**.
- This means:
  - No search, seizure, or interference with UNEP’s physical facilities or official records.
  - These assets are protected from any unauthorized access or disruption, ensuring the confidentiality and integrity of the negotiation process.

> *Legal basis: Section 3 of the Convention.*

---

#### **2. Privileges and Immunities for Participants**
Participants in the meetings — defined under **Article 2 of the Agreement** — are granted privileges and immunities under **Article IV of the Convention**, specifically **Sections 11 and 12**, to the extent provided in **Sections 14 to 16** of that Article.

This includes the following categories of individuals:

- **Representatives of foreign states**  
- **Representatives of members of the United Nations**  
- **Representatives of members of specialized agencies or related organizations of the United Nations**

> These individuals are granted:
> - Immunity from legal proceedings (e.g., civil or criminal suits) arising from their participation in the meetings.
> - Protection of their personal and official communications.
> - Exemption from certain taxes and duties.
> - Protection of their personal status and freedom of movement during the relevant period.

> *Note:* The privileges are **limited to the extent required for the exercise of their functions** in relation to the meetings — meaning they do not extend beyond the scope of their participation.

---

#### **3. Privileges and Immunities for UN Officials**
- **Officials of the United Nations** (e.g., staff, delegates, administrators) are granted privileges and immunities under **paragraphs 18(a) and (c) to (f)** of **Article V of the Convention**.
- These include:
  - Immunity from legal proceedings related to their official duties.
  - Protection of their personal and official communications.
  - Exemption from arrest or detention without due process.
  - Immunity from civil and criminal liability for acts performed in the course of their official duties.

> *Scope:* Applies only to the extent necessary for their functions during the meetings.

---

#### **4. Privileges and Immunities for Experts**
- **Experts** (non-officials) who are performing missions for the United Nations or its specialized agencies or related organizations are granted privileges and immunities under **Article VI of the Convention**.
- This includes:
  - Immunity from legal proceedings related to their expert functions.
  - Protection of their personal and professional information.
  - Freedom to travel and conduct work without interference.

> *Note:* This provision applies **only to experts**, not to officials or participants, and is limited to the extent required for their specific roles in the meetings.

---

### **Duration and Effective Date**
- The Order comes into force on the **day it is registered** with the Statutes of Canada (i.e., upon official publication in the *Canada Gazette*).
- It is **time-limited** and only applies during the **relevant period** (April 19 to May 3, 2024), which includes the preparatory consultations and the main INC-4 session.

---

### **Legal and Diplomatic Significance**

This Order is a critical tool in facilitating international diplomacy by:

- Ensuring **diplomatic neutrality** and **freedom of expression** during negotiations.
- Preventing **domestic interference** or legal actions that could disrupt the negotiation process.
- Providing **legal certainty** to participants from various countries and international bodies.
- Upholding international norms and standards for the treatment of foreign missions and international organizations.

It reflects Canada’s commitment to multilateral environmental governance and its role as a key host in global environmental negotiations.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation establishes a legally binding framework of **privileges and immunities** for the participants and officials of the INC-4 session on plastic pollution. It ensures that the negotiations in Ottawa in 2024 can proceed in a secure, neutral, and effective environment, consistent with international diplomatic standards. By protecting the premises, records, and individuals involved, it supports the development of a globally binding international instrument to address plastic pollution — a critical environmental challenge of our time.
