## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Rules Governing Appeals from the Territorial Court of Yukon (Yukon Summary Conviction Appeals Rules)**

### üìå **Overview**
These rules govern the procedural requirements for filing and conducting appeals from decisions of the **Territorial Court of Yukon** (e.g., conviction, acquittal, sentence, or order) under the **Summary Convictions Part of the Criminal Code**. The appeals are heard by the **Supreme Court of Yukon (Appeal Court)**.

The rules are designed to ensure timely, fair, and accessible appellate processes, with clear timelines, service obligations, and procedural safeguards for both appellants and respondents.

## üîπ Filing of the Notice of Appeal (Rule 2)

### ‚úÖ Requirements:
- Must be in **Form 1**.
- Must be **signed by the appellant, their counsel, or an authorized agent**.
- Must be **filed with the Clerk of the Appeal Court in Whitehorse**.

### üìù Contents of the Notice of Appeal (Must Include):
1. **Name of the Territorial Court judge or justice** who made the decision.
2. **Location of the Territorial Court** where the decision was rendered.
3. **Particulars of the conviction, acquittal, sentence, or order** being appealed.
4. **Grounds upon which the appeal is taken** (e.g., error in law, evidence, procedure).
5. **Nature of relief sought** (e.g., reversal of conviction, new trial, reduction of sentence).
6. **Plea at trial** ‚Äì whether guilty or not guilty.
7. **Custody status** ‚Äì whether the appellant is in custody and, if so, since when.
8. **Appellant‚Äôs address for service** (including phone, fax, and email if available).

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: The notice must be filed **within 30 days** of the conviction, acquittal, sentence, or order, **whichever is later**. This period does **not include** the date of the event or the filing date itself.

## üîπ First Appearance Date (Rule 2(3))

- Upon filing, the **Clerk must endorse** the **date, time, and place** of the first appearance in Supreme Court criminal chambers.
- The **first appearance date must be within 30 days** of filing.
- This date is critical for scheduling the hearing and is binding unless extended.

## üîπ Service of the Notice of Appeal

### üìå When the Appeal is by the **Prosecutor**:
- The prosecutor must **personally serve** a copy of the notice of appeal on the **respondent (defendant)** within the 30-day period.
- If personal service is not possible:
  - The prosecutor may **apply to the Appeal Court without notice** for directions on service.
- The prosecutor must **file proof of service** (Form 2) with the Clerk **within 10 days** of the last day of service.

### üìå When the Appeal is by the **Defendant**:
- The Clerk **automatically forwards** a copy of the notice of appeal to the **prosecutor‚Äôs office** within **10 days** of filing.
- The Clerk completes and issues a **Certificate of Service (Form 3)**, which serves as **proof of service** on the prosecutor.

> ‚úÖ In both cases, service must occur within the 30-day window.

## üîπ Transmission of Trial Materials (Rule 4(5))

- Within **15 days** of the notice of appeal being filed, the **Clerk of the Territorial Court** must transmit to the Appeal Court:
  - Information or summary offence ticket
  - Any orders made by the Territorial Court
  - Documentary exhibits
  - Pre-sentence reports
  - Victim impact statements

> üìù If the respondent‚Äôs address is unknown, the materials are provided to them on the **first appearance date**.

---

## üîπ Filing of the Transcript of Evidence (Rule 5)

- The appellant must file a **transcript of the evidence** with the Appeal Court and serve a copy on the respondent **within 60 days** of filing the notice of appeal.
- **Exception**: If the appeal is only from **sentence**, only the **submissions of the parties** and the **reasons of the trial judge** need to be included.
- If the appellant **cannot afford** the cost of a transcript:
  - They may apply to the Appeal Court under **Rule 11(3)** for **indigent status**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è Failure to file a transcript within the time limit may result in referral to the Appeal Court.

---

## üîπ Hearing Scheduling (Rule 6)

- The **first appearance date** is the **minimum** time for the hearing to be scheduled.
- The Appeal Court **must not schedule** a hearing **before 30 days** after receiving the documents and materials from the Territorial Court.
- **Exception for custody cases**:
  - If the appellant is **in custody** due to a sentence and appeals the conviction or sentence:
    - On application, the Clerk shall **set the matter for hearing before the next available sitting** of the Appeal Court.
    - The Appeal Court may:
      - Dismiss the appeal on the merits; or
      - Make any other order appropriate.

> üí° This ensures that incarcerated individuals are not denied timely appellate access.

---

## üîπ Withdrawal of Guilty Plea (Rule 8)

- If the appeal is from a **conviction** and the appellant **entered a guilty plea**:
  - The Clerk shall **set the matter over** for a preliminary hearing on whether the appellant can **withdraw the guilty plea**.

## üîπ Filing of Brief Outlines of Argument (Rule 9)

### ‚úÖ Required for both parties:
- A **brief outline of argument** must be filed and served before the hearing.
- Must include:
  - Relevant **case law** and other legal authorities.
  - Specific **references to evidence** to be discussed.

### ‚è±Ô∏è Timelines:
- **Appellant‚Äôs outline**: Must be filed and served **at least 30 days** before the hearing.
- **Respondent‚Äôs outline**: Must be filed and served **at least 15 days** before the hearing.

### ‚ùå Exceptions:
- No outline is required if:
  - The party is **not represented by counsel**; or
  - A **new trial has been ordered** under Rule 5.

> ‚öñÔ∏è **No party may refer to unfiled or unserved materials** without **leave of the Appeal Court**.

## üîπ Abandonment of Appeal (Rule 10)

- An appellant may **abandon the appeal** by signing and filing **Form 5**.
- Upon abandonment:
  - The appeal is **deemed abandoned**.
  - If the appellant was **released from jail pending appeal** or a **stay was granted**, the **original sentence or order takes effect immediately**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è This rule protects the integrity of the criminal justice process by preventing stale appeals.

---

## üîπ Applications for Release or Stay (Rule 11)

- Until the appeal is heard and disposed of, an appellant may apply for:
  - **Release from jail**; or
  - A **stay of a sentence or order**.

### Required Documents:
- A **notice of application in Form 4**.
- An **affidavit** stating the facts supporting the application.
- Any **additional relevant material** (e.g., medical reports, affidavits).

### Clerk‚Äôs Responsibilities:
- Forward the application to the prosecutor.
- Obtain a hearing date **within 3 days** of notification.
- Notify both parties of the hearing date.

### Appeal Court Powers:
- May grant any order, including:
  - Requiring the appellant to enter into an **undertaking or recognizance**.
  - Requiring the deposit of **money or security** with the Clerk.

---

## üîπ Extensions of Time (Rule 12)

- The Appeal Court may **extend or shorten time** for any procedural requirement.
- A **two-day written notice** must be given to the opposite party **unless the application is made on consent**.
- If the deadline falls on a **holiday**, the time is extended to the **next business day**.
