### 1. **Common-Law Partner**
- A person who cohabits with another in a **conjugal relationship** for **at least one year**.
- **Exception**: If the inability to cohabit is due to **persecution or penal control**, the individual is still considered a common-law partner.
- This definition supports recognition of long-term, non-marital relationships in immigration assessments.

> *Purpose*: To ensure equitable treatment of individuals in long-term relationships without formal marriage.

---

### 2. **Family Member (for sponsorship purposes)**
A person is considered a family member if they are:
- The **spouse or common-law partner** of the sponsor;
- A **dependent child** of the sponsor, their spouse, or common-law partner;
- A **dependent child of a dependent child** (i.e., a grandchild who is dependent).

> *Note*: Excludes certain provisions (e.g., section 12 and paragraph 38(2)(d)) and specific regulations (e.g., 7.1(3)(a), sections 159.1 and 159.5).

> *Purpose*: Defines who qualifies as a dependent for sponsorship under the IRPA.

### 4. **Health and Social Services Definitions**
- **Health Services**: Any health service (including devices, lab tests, pharmaceuticals) where **more than half the funding comes from government sources**.
- **Social Services**: Residential or institutional care (including devices) recommended by a health professional, where **more than half the funding is government-supported**.

> **Excessive Demand**:
- A demand that would:
  - Cause **anticipated costs to exceed triple** the average Canadian per capita health/social service cost over the 5 years after the last medical exam; **OR**
  - Add to existing waiting lists and **increase morbidity or mortality** due to lack of timely services for Canadians or permanent residents.

> *Purpose*: Prevents overburdening the Canadian health and social systems by screening for unreasonable or unsustainable demands.

### 5. **Key Financial and Administrative Definitions**
- **Administration Fee**: Covers costs associated with processing foreign nationals, including:
  - Examinations
  - Detention
  - Investigations and admissibility hearings
  - Fingerprinting, photo verification, document checks
  - Translation/interpretation
  - Proceedings before the Immigration Division

> *Purpose*: Establishes a transparent cost base for processing applications.

- **Minimum Necessary Income**:
  - Based on the **Statistics Canada low-income cut-off (LICO)** for urban areas of 500,000+ people.
  - Determines the **lowest income** required to support:
    - Sponsor and their family
    - Sponsored foreign national and their family
    - Any other person (including spouse/common-law partner) with active undertakings

> *Purpose*: Ensures sponsors can financially support their families without undue hardship.

### 6. **Work and Employment Definitions**
- **Work**: Any activity involving wages or commissions, or one that competes directly with Canadian citizens/permanent residents in the labor market.
- **Work Permit**: A written authorization issued by an officer allowing a foreign national to work in Canada.

> *Purpose*: Ensures foreign workers do not displace Canadian citizens in the labor market.

- **TEER Category**:
  - A classification system in the **National Occupational Classification (NOC)** indicating the level of training, education, and experience required for a job.
  - Used to assess eligibility for work permits and skilled immigration.

> *Purpose*: Standardizes occupational assessment for skilled immigration and work authorization.

### 7. **Transportation and Crew Definitions**
- **Commercial Vehicle**: Used for commercial purposes (air, land, water).
- **Commercial Transporter**: Owns, operates, chartsers, or manages a commercial vehicle or international infrastructure (e.g., tunnels, bridges).
- **Member of a Crew**:
  - Includes those employed to perform duties during a voyage or in port (e.g., cabin crew, stewards).
  - **Excludes**:
    - Persons whose fare is waived in exchange for work
    - Maintenance staff under service contracts
    - Anyone on board for non-crew purposes
    - In-flight security officers

> A person **ceases to be a crew member** if:
- They desert or are believed to have deserted
- They are hospitalized and fail to return or leave Canada
- They are discharged and unable/unwilling to perform duties

> *Purpose*: Clarifies who qualifies as a crew member for immigration and entry purposes.

### 8. **Adoption and Family Relationships**
- **Adoption** means a legal process that creates a **parent-child relationship** and **severs prior relationships**.
- **Adoption is invalid** if:
  - Entered into **primarily to gain status or privilege** under the IRPA
  - Does **not create a genuine parent-child relationship**

> **Exception**: Paragraphs 117(1)(g), subsections 117(2) and (4) are exempt from this rule.

> *Purpose*: Prevents fraudulent or transactional adoptions for immigration benefits.

---

### 9. **Spousal and Conjugal Relationship Validity**
A foreign national **shall not be considered**:
- A spouse, common-law partner, or conjugal partner if:
  - The relationship was entered into **primarily to gain status or privilege** under the Act
  - The relationship is **not genuine**
- A spouse if:
  - The foreign national or the person was previously married to another person
  - The person has lived separate and apart for at least one year and is in a common-law relationship with someone else
- A spouse if:
  - At the time of marriage, one or both were not physically present (unless due to Canadian Forces service and the marriage is valid under both Canadian and foreign law)

> *Purpose*: Prevents fraudulent or exploitative marriages for immigration purposes.
