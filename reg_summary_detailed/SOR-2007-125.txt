## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Vessel Clearance Regulations**

### **1. Regulatory Authority and Basis**
- **Enacted by:** Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Transport, Infrastructure and Communities.
- **Legal Authority:** Pursuant to **paragraph 244(a) of the Canada Shipping Act, 2001**.
- **Purpose:** To establish the requirements for vessels to obtain clearance for departure, particularly focusing on safety, pollution prevention, and compliance with international standards.

> These regulations are part of the broader framework governing vessel operations in Canada and ensure that vessels meet mandatory safety, environmental, and operational standards before embarking on voyages.

### **4. Required Certificates and Documents for Vessel Clearance (Section 214 of the Act)**

#### ‚úÖ General Requirements (All Vessels Departing on International Voyages)

The following documents are required **for all vessels** departing on an international voyage **unless exempted**:

| Document | Applies To | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Load Line Certificate** | Required if load line regulations apply | Acceptable types: <br> - Great Lakes & Inland Waters of Canada Load Line Certificate <br> - U.S. Title 46, Chapter I, Part 45 Load Line Certificate <br> - International Load Line Certificate or Exemption Certificate |
| **Fitness to Proceed Certificate** | Required under Cargo, Fumigation and Tackle Regulations | Confirms the vessel is safe to proceed on its voyage |
| **International Oil Pollution Prevention (OILPOL) Certificate** | Applies to all vessels | Required for pollution prevention from oil discharges |
| **International Pollution Prevention Certificate (NOXIOUS LIQUID SUBSTANCES)** | For vessels carrying noxious liquid substances in bulk | Ensures safe handling of hazardous liquids |
| **Certificate of Fitness for Carriage of Dangerous Chemicals in Bulk** | For chemical tankers | Either Canadian or International Certificate |
| **Certificate of Fitness for Offshore Support Vessel** | For vessels handling limited hazardous/noxious liquids | Applies if IMO Resolution A.673(16) guidelines are relevant |
| **International Sewage Pollution Prevention Certificate** | Required for all vessels | Ensures safe disposal of sewage |
| **International Air Pollution Prevention Certificate** | Required for all vessels | Controls emissions from ship engines and systems |
| **International Anti-fouling System Certificate or Declaration** | Required | Prevents marine pollution from hull biofouling |
| **International Energy Efficiency Certificate** | Required | Measures and certifies energy efficiency of the vessel |
| **Safe Manning Document or Equivalent** | Required | Confirms the vessel has sufficient qualified crew |

> üîç **Exception:**  
> If the vessel‚Äôs master holds a **Certificate of Fitness for Offshore Support Vessels**, then the **Certificate of Fitness for Carriage of Dangerous Chemicals in Bulk** is **not required**.

#### ‚úÖ Special Requirements for Canadian Vessels (Less Than 500 GT)

- A **safety certificate issued under Section 13 of the Vessel Safety Certificates Regulations** is required for:
  - Canadian cargo vessels **under 500 GT** departing on an **international voyage (non-inland)**, or
  - All Canadian vessels departing on an **inland voyage**.

> This ensures that even smaller Canadian vessels meet baseline safety standards before departure.

---

#### ‚úÖ Requirements for Foreign Vessels

- A **certificate issued by or under the authority of a foreign government** is required if:
  - The vessel is a **cargo vessel under 500 GT** departing on an international voyage (non-inland), or
  - The vessel is departing on an **inland voyage**.

> This allows for international compliance while respecting the flag state‚Äôs jurisdiction.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception:**  
> If the vessel's flag state has **not ratified** a relevant international convention (e.g., SOLAS, MARPOL), then a **Certificate of Compliance** issued by that state must be submitted instead of the full certificate.

> This provision ensures that vessels from non-ratifying states are still held to international safety standards through alternative compliance documentation.

### **5. Document Requirements**

- **Original documents and valid certificates** are required.
- **No copies or expired certificates** are accepted.
- This ensures authenticity and enforceability of compliance.
