### **Detailed Summary of the Rules of Procedure for Hearings Before the Military Police Complaints Commission, 2022**

These Rules of Procedure, made under **section 250.15a of the National Defence Act (Act)** by the Chairperson of the **Military Police Complaints Commission (MPCC)**, govern the conduct of hearings conducted under **subsection 250.38(1)** of the Act. The Rules are designed to ensure that hearings are **fair, effective, expeditious, and proportionate** to the nature and complexity of the complaint, while also respecting public interest and efficient use of resources.

---

## **1. Scope and Applicability**
- These Rules apply to **all hearings** conducted under **section 250.38(1)** of the National Defence Act.
- They provide a structured framework for procedural conduct, including document handling, service, scheduling, motions, hearings, and decision-making.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**
The Rules define essential terms used throughout:
- **Act**: Refers to the *National Defence Act*.
- **Commission counsel**: Counsel formally designated by the Chairperson to represent the Commission during hearings.
- **Counsel**: A member of the bar of a province (i.e., licensed legal practitioner).
- **Day**: A calendar day.
- **Document**: Any written or electronic material — including affidavits, submissions, correspondence, maps, photos, recordings, digital files, and copies — regardless of format.
- **Electronic transmission**: Includes email or the Commission’s website; **excludes fax**.
- **File**: To submit a document to the Registrar.
- **Intervenor**: A person granted leave by the Commission to participate in a hearing under section 44 of the Act.
- **Party**: A complainant, the subject of a complaint, or any person with a **substantial and direct interest** in the proceeding (as defined in section 250.44 of the Act).
- **Person**: Includes individuals, corporations, partnerships, and unincorporated associations.
- **Registrar**: The official responsible for administrative and procedural matters of the Commission.

---

## **3. Purpose and Principles**
- The primary goal is to ensure **fairness, efficiency, and proportionality** in proceedings.
- Proceedings must be **proportionate** to the significance of the issues and the complexity of the complaint.
- The Commission may deviate from these rules if necessary to achieve fairness or expediency.
- **Non-compliance** with a rule is considered an **irregularity**, but **does not nullify** any proceeding, document, or order.
- The Commission has broad discretion to:
  - **Vary or supplement** rules;
  - **Dispense with compliance** with a rule;
  - Issue **practice directions** consistent with the Rules’ purpose.

> *Note:* If a matter is not covered by these Rules, the practice will be determined based on the **purpose of the Rules** and any **practice directions** issued under section 29.

---

## **4. Time and Service of Documents**

### **Computation of Time**
- Time periods are governed by **sections 26 to 29 of the Interpretation Act**.

### **Service of Documents**
Service must be carried out in one of the following ways:
1. **Individuals**: Leave a copy with the individual.
2. **Partnerships, corporations, or associations**: Leave a copy with a partner, officer, or director.
3. **If represented by counsel**: Leave a copy with counsel, with **proof of service** as required under section 11.

**Additional authorized methods**:
- **Electronic transmission** (email or Commission website)
- **Fax transmission**
- **Registered mail**
- Any other method authorized by the Commission

#### **Electronic Transmission Requirements**
- Must be in **PDF format** or another format approved by the Commission.
- Must include:
  - Sender’s name, phone, email, mailing address
  - Name of person being served
  - Date and time of transmission
  - Title/description of document
  - Contact details for transmission issues

> **Rule**: Documents transmitted after **5 p.m. local time** are deemed served on the **next business day** (not Saturday or holiday).

#### **Fax Transmission Requirements**
- Must include a cover page with:
  - Sender’s name, phone, fax, email, mailing address
  - Name of recipient
  - Date/time of transmission
  - Total number of pages (including cover)
  - Title/description of document
  - Contact details for transmission issues

> **Rule**: Same time-of-day rule applies — transmission after 5 p.m. is deemed served on the next business day.

#### **Proof of Service**
- Service is proven by:
  - **Acknowledgement signed by the recipient**, or
  - **Affidavit of service** stating:
    - Who served the document
    - Date, place, and method of service
    - For electronic/fax/registered mail: **receipt confirming successful delivery**

> **Requirement**: The person serving documents must **retain proof** and provide it upon request.

#### **Filing Requirements**
- A document may only be filed if a **copy has been served** on all parties and intervenors.
- When filed, it must be accompanied by **proof of service** meeting section 11 requirements.

> **Electronic filing only** is required for parties and intervenors.

#### **Exceptions to Electronic Filing**
- The Commission may allow **paper or fax filing** if justified by circumstances.

> **Filing format**:
- Must be on **letter-sized paper** with **numbered pages**.
- If not feasible, documents may be filed in existing format.

> **Electronic version is deemed the original** for oral/written hearings.

> **Original documents must be produced** upon request by the Commission.

> **Certified copies**: The Registrar may issue a **paper copy** of an electronic document stamped “certified copy” upon request.

> **Public access**: Subject to confidentiality orders under section 55, the public may access documents in the **public record** (in a format set by the Registrar).  
> **Private hearings**: Documents filed in private hearings **do not form part of the public record**.

---

## **5. Oaths and Affirmations**
- Statements made under **oath or solemn affirmation** may be filed electronically.
- A **scanned version** must be submitted with:
  - A document attesting that:
    1. The electronic version is a copy of a **signed paper document**;
    2. The original paper document is available and will be produced upon request.
- The **original signed document must be retained for one year** after the final report is issued.
- The Commission may request the **original** for review.

---

## **6. Panel and Hearing Management**

### **Panel Composition**
- The **panel** consists of members of the Commission assigned by the Chairperson.
- The Chairperson of the Commission designates **one member** to act as **Chairperson of the panel**, responsible for managing daily operations, communications, and procedural matters.

### **Absence or Incapacity**
- If a panel member is absent or incapacitated, or if a position becomes vacant:
  - The hearing may continue with the **remaining members**.
  - The Chairperson may **assign additional members** to the panel.

> **Note**: Absence or vacancy **does not invalidate** the proceeding.

### **Single-Member Panel**
- If the panel has only one member and that member is absent or incapacitated, the Chairperson may assign **one or more additional members**.

### **Submissions on Panel Changes**
- When a new member is assigned:
  - Parties and intervenors must submit **written submissions** on how the hearing should proceed.
  - The Chairperson may, after considering submissions and interests of justice, determine:
    - Whether to **rehear evidence**
    - Whether to use **transcripts of previously given evidence**

---

## **7. Decision-Making**
- A decision is made by a **majority of the panel**.
- In the case of a **tie**, the **Chairperson of the panel** has the **deciding vote**.

### **Interim and Preliminary Questions**
- The Chairperson or their delegate may:
  - Determine **interim or preliminary questions** (e.g., motions)
  - Exercise powers necessary to resolve such questions

### **Questions of Law, Jurisdiction, or Procedure**
- The Commission may:
  - Determine any such question **during the proceeding**
  - **Stay** the proceeding temporarily until the question is resolved

### **Reference to the Federal Court**
- Under **section 18.3 of the Federal Courts Act**, the Commission may refer:
  - Questions of law, jurisdiction, or procedure
  - To the **Federal Court** for determination

> **After reference**:
- The Commission may:
  - Proceed with hearing **on remaining issues** (excluding those referred)
  - **Stay** the hearing on referred issues and **reserve decision** pending Federal Court outcome

---

## **8. Case Management Conferences**

The Commission may conduct **one or more case management conferences**, presided over by a panel member conducting the hearing.

### **Purpose**
To streamline proceedings and ensure efficiency by addressing key issues early.

### **Matters to be Considered**
The Commission may include in directions:
- Dates, duration, and location of the hearing
- Whether the hearing will be **oral, written, or hybrid**
- Format of document presentation (electronic vs. paper)
- Deadlines for motions and document exchanges
- Issues of **confidentiality**
- **Clarification, simplification, or elimination** of issues
