**Detailed Summary of the Telecommunications and Electronic Maintenance Services Fees Order (SI/81-61, 1981)**

---

### **Overview of the Regulation**

The *Telecommunications and Electronic Maintenance Services Fees Order* (SI/81-61, 1981), published in the *Canada Gazette, Part II*, on April 8, 1981, is a statutory instrument issued by the **Minister of Transport** under the authority of **Order in Council P.C. 1981-928**, dated April 2, 1981.

This Order establishes a formal framework for **charging fees or charges** for specific **telecommunications and electronic maintenance services** provided by the **Department of Transport** under agreements made pursuant to Order P.C. 1981-928.

The Order serves as a **regulatory mechanism** to define the fees applicable to services delivered by the Department in the context of national telecommunications and electronic infrastructure maintenance, particularly in support of transportation-related systems.

---

### **Legal Basis and Authority**

- The Minister of Transport exercised powers under **Order in Council P.C. 1981-928**, which authorized the Department of Transport to enter into agreements for the provision of telecommunications and electronic maintenance services.
- The present Order (SI/81-61) is a **subsidiary regulation** that specifies the **fees to be charged** under those agreements.
- It is issued pursuant to the principle of **fee transparency and accountability**, ensuring that service providers and users understand the cost structure for services rendered.

---

### **Scope and Applicability**

The fees established in this Order apply to:

- **Telecommunications services**
- **Electronic maintenance services**

Provided by the **Department of Transport** as a result of **any agreement** entered into under Order P.C. 1981-928.

These services are typically related to the **operation, maintenance, and support of electronic and communication systems** used in transportation infrastructure — such as air traffic control, railway signaling, maritime communications, or transportation management systems.

---

### **Key Provisions**

1. **Fees Are Prescribed in Schedule**
   - The actual fee amounts and structures are detailed in the **Schedule** attached to the Order.
   - The Schedule outlines specific fees based on:
     - Type of service (e.g., installation, maintenance, monitoring, troubleshooting)
     - Duration of service
     - Complexity of the electronic or telecommunications system involved
     - Location or jurisdiction of service delivery
     - Frequency of service (e.g., routine vs. emergency)

2. **Charging Mechanism**
   - Fees are to be charged **in accordance with the schedule**.
   - The Department of Transport is authorized to **collect these fees directly** from users or contracting parties who receive such services.
   - Charges are not arbitrary or discretionary but are **prescribed and fixed** under the Order, ensuring consistency and predictability.

3. **Purpose of the Fees**
   - To **recoup the cost of providing services** to users.
   - To ensure **financial sustainability** of the Department’s telecommunications and electronic maintenance operations.
   - To promote **accountability and transparency** in service delivery.

---

### **Significance and Context**

- This Order reflects Canada’s early efforts to **establish a structured and accountable system** for managing public-sector telecommunications and electronic services.
- It aligns with broader government initiatives during the 1980s to modernize transportation infrastructure and improve the efficiency of communication networks.
- The use of **Order in Council** to authorize such fees indicates that the decision was made at a high level of government, likely due to the strategic importance of transportation communications.

---

### **Limitations and Considerations**

- The Order applies **only to services provided under agreements made under P.C. 1981-928**.
- It does **not cover** general telecommunications services provided by other departments or private entities.
- The fees are **non-negotiable** and based on a fixed schedule — users must pay the amount specified, regardless of perceived value or service quality.
- There is **no provision for appeals or review** of fees within this Order, suggesting that the fees are intended to be final and binding.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Telecommunications and Electronic Maintenance Services Fees Order* (SI/81-61) is a foundational regulatory instrument that:

- Authorizes the Department of Transport to charge fees for telecommunications and electronic maintenance services;
- Establishes a transparent, schedule-based fee structure;
- Operates within a legal framework set by Order in Council P.C. 1981-928;
- Aims to ensure cost recovery, operational sustainability, and accountability in the delivery of critical transportation infrastructure support services.

While outdated in today’s context (given the evolution of telecommunications and the shift to private-sector and digital platforms), this Order remains historically significant as an early example of government regulation in the domain of public electronic services.
