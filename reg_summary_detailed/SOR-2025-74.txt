**Detailed Summary of the Employment Insurance Board of Appeal Regulations (2025)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Employment Insurance Board of Appeal Regulations*, approved on **February 21, 2025**, by Her Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Employment and Social Development, implement a clear, accessible, and efficient appeal process under the *Employment Insurance Act* (EIA) and the *Department of Employment and Social Development Act*. These regulations govern how individuals and employers can appeal decisions made by the Canada Employment Insurance Commission (Commission) under **section 113** of the EIA, specifically in response to a **reconsideration decision** made under **section 112**.

The regulations are designed to ensure fairness, simplicity, accessibility, and operational efficiency while respecting the **principles of natural justice**.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Legislative Authority and Purpose**
- The regulations are made under:
  - **Section 68.2a** of the *Department of Employment and Social Development Act* (DESDA)
  - **Paragraph 114(2)(b)** of the *Employment Insurance Act* (EIA)
- Purpose: To establish a transparent, timely, and accessible procedure for appealing reconsideration decisions by the Commission.

---

### **2. Definitions**
The following terms are defined for clarity and consistency throughout the regulations:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Department of Employment and Social Development Act* |
| **Appellant** | A person who appeals a reconsideration decision — either the claimant or the employer of the claimant |
| **Claimant** | As defined in subsection 2(1) of the EIA — the individual claiming EI benefits |
| **Employer** | As defined in subsection 2(1) of the EIA — the person or entity responsible for the claimant’s employment |
| **Party** | Includes the appellant, the Commission, or any person added to the appeal under section 16 |
| **Reconsideration Decision** | A decision by the Commission under section 112 of the EIA, including decisions on extending time to file a reconsideration request |

---

### **3. General Principles of Interpretation and Application**
- The regulations must be interpreted and applied in a way that:
  - Ensures the appeal process is **as simple and efficient as the principles of natural justice allow**
- If a matter arises outside the scope of these regulations, the **Board of Appeal** may determine the procedure **by analogy**, provided it does not violate natural justice.

---

### **4. Obligations of Parties**
- All parties must:
  - Comply with the Board’s directions
  - Respect deadlines
  - Communicate as required
- Communication methods (other than filing documents) include:
  - Mail
  - Courier
  - Telephone
  - Electronic means (e.g., email, online portal)
- The Board **must use contact information** in the appeal record when contacting parties.
- Parties must **notify the Board immediately** of any changes in contact details.
- If a party fails to respond to communications using provided contact information, the Board **may continue the process without further notice**, provided natural justice principles are respected.

---

### **5. Language and Accessibility**
- Appellants must choose **English or French** for the appeal.
- Other parties may participate in their **preferred official language**.
- The Board **must provide interpretation services** upon written request (as soon as feasible).
- A party may **hire their own interpreter at their own expense**.
- Parties with disabilities or special needs must be **accommodated to the extent possible** to ensure full participation.

---

### **6. Expense Reimbursement (Section 43.15)**
A party (other than the Commission) may be reimbursed for travel expenses or receive an allowance if:
- They must travel **more than 100 km** from the postal address in the appeal record to the hearing location
- The request is **in writing**
- The request is **approved by the Executive Head of the Board of Appeal** before the hearing date

> This ensures accessibility for individuals in remote or rural areas.

---

### **7. Hearing Location Assignment (Section 43.16)**
- Hearings are assigned to the **most suitable region** based on the appellant’s postal address (as listed in **column 2 of the Schedule**).
- Exceptions may be authorized by the **Executive Head** if:
  - The appeal would not be heard in a timely manner
  - Travel would be facilitated
  - No panel is available in the designated region
  - The appeal involves multiple parties across regions
  - The appellant requests it
  - Other operational or efficiency-related reasons exist

---

### **8. Hearing Format and Participation**
- **In-person hearing**:
  - Appellant must request it
  - Other parties may:
    - Attend in person (at their own expense)
    - Participate via videoconference or teleconference
    - Choose not to attend
- **Videoconference/teleconference**:
  - If the appellant requests it and does not wish to attend
  - If the appellant fails to select a format and cannot be reached
  - Parties may participate via video or teleconference or choose not to attend
- **Exceptional Circumstances**:
  - If in-person hearing raises **security, health, or operational concerns** that cannot be mitigated, the Executive Head may authorize a virtual format
- **Absence of a Party**:
  - The Board may proceed without a party who fails to appear without prior notice, provided the hearing is otherwise conducted fairly.

---

### **9. Decision-Making and Hearing Procedure**
- **No hearing required** if:
  - The Commission and the appellant are the only parties
  - The Commission **concedes the appeal**
- The Board may hold **part of a hearing in private** if there is a serious risk that public disclosure would:
  - Cause undue hardship (e.g., medical information)
  - Threaten a person’s life, liberty, or security
  - Undermine the fairness of the proceeding
  - Pose a public security risk
- **Presiding Member’s Powers**:
  - May **exclude individuals** during oral evidence related to sexual or other harassment
  - Must provide the excluded party with:
    - A copy of the audio recording of the evidence
    - An opportunity to respond to it

---

### **10. Filing of Documents**
- **Notice of Appeal** can be filed:
  - In person at a Service Canada office
  - By mail or courier (using postal address on the Board’s website)
  - Electronically (via the Board’s online portal)
- **Requirements**:
  - **Claimant**: Full name, postal address, phone/email, and social insurance number (or identifying number from the reconsideration decision)
  - **Employer**: Full name, postal address, phone/email, business name, business number (from CRA), and identifying number
- **Presumption of Filing Date**:
  - In person: Date of receipt stamp
  - Ordinary mail: Date of receipt stamp
  - Registered mail/courier: Date on delivery confirmation
  - Electronic: Date/time recorded in the system

---

### **11. Notification and Document Handling**
- The Board must:
  - Notify the appellant and Commission **as soon as feasible** upon receiving a notice of appeal
- Within **seven business days** (or with additional time granted), the Commission must file:
  - Copy of the request for reconsideration
  - Copy of the reconsideration decision
  - Its arguments (if any)
  - All relevant documents in its possession
- The Board must:
  - Add to the appeal:
    - Any claimant with a direct interest (if appellant is an employer)
    - Any employer notified by the Commission
  - May add other employers on its own initiative or upon request
- **Late Notice**:
  - If filed after the deadline (subsection 43.11(1)), the appellant must provide **reasons**
  - Regional coordinator may request additional information

---

### **12. Joining Appeals**
- Appeals may be **joined** if:
  - They raise a **common legal question**
  - Joining is **not unfair** to any party
- A **single notice of appeal** may represent a group of appellants if:
  - The appeal involves a common issue
  - The Commission previously dealt with each case in a reconsideration decision
- The notice must:
  - Identify **one reconsideration decision** as the subject (binding on all parties)
  - Name the **group representative**
  - Include:
    - Full name, SIS number, and decision ID for each appellant
    - Signed consent from each appellant to join

---

### **13. Filing of Written Arguments and Evidence**
- All written arguments must be filed **before the scheduled hearing date**
- The Board **cannot consider evidence filed after the hearing** unless:
  - The Board **requests it**
  - A party requests it at the hearing and the Board **accepts the request and sets a filing date**

---
