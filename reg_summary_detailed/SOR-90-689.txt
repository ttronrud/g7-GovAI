### **Detailed Summary of the Regulation: Tax Court of Canada Rules of Procedure Respectfully Concerning the Canada Pension Plan**

This regulation, issued on **September 7, 1990**, constitutes a formal **revocation and replacement** of the former *Pension Appeals Board Rules of Procedure (Contributions and Coverage)* under *C.R.C., c. 389*, with new, comprehensive *Tax Court of Canada Rules of Procedure respecting the Canada Pension Plan*. The rules are effective **January 1, 1991**, and apply exclusively to appeals under the *Canada Pension Plan (CPP)* Act.

---

## **I. Background and Legal Authority**

- The **rules committee of the Tax Court of Canada** published a notice on **April 21, 1990**, proposing:
  - The **revocation** of the *Pension Appeals Board Rules of Procedure (Contributions and Coverage)*, **except for appeals or proceedings initiated before January 1, 1991**.
  - The **introduction** of new *Tax Court of Canada Rules of Procedure* for CPP appeals, substantially in the form annexed to the notice.

- The notice invited **public comment** from interested parties.

- The rules committee, acting under **section 20 of the Tax Court of Canada Act**, and subject to **approval by the Governor in Council**, formally:
  - Revoked the old rules (with exception for pre-1991 cases).
  - Adopted and implemented the new rules effective **January 1, 1991**.

- The **Governor in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Justice**, approved the changes under **subsection 20(1)** of the Act.

---

## **II. Key Provisions of the New Rules**

### **1. Title and Applicability**
- These rules are titled:  
  **"Tax Court of Canada Rules of Procedure respecting the Canada Pension Plan"**  
  and may be cited as such.

- They apply **only to appeals under section 28 of the Canada Pension Plan Act**.

---

### **2. Definitions of Key Terms**
The rules define several key terms to ensure clarity and consistency:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Canada Pension Plan Act* |
| **Appellant** | An employee, employer, or their representative appealing a decision by the Minister under section 28 |
| **Electronic filing** | Filing documents via the Court’s website (www.tcc-cci.gc.ca) or any website directed by the Court |
| **Fax** | Transmission of a printed document via facsimile |
| **Intervener** | A person affected by a Ministerial determination under section 27 who has formally intervened in the appeal |
| **Minister** | The Minister of National Revenue |
| **Registrar** | The official appointed by the Chief Administrator of the Courts Administration Service to manage the Court’s administrative functions |
| **Registry** | The Court’s central office at 200 Kent Street, Ottawa (with additional local offices as notified) |

> **General Principle**: The rules are to be **liberally construed** to ensure **just, cost-effective, and expeditious resolution** of appeals.

---

### **3. Jurisdiction and Judicial Authority**
- **Associate Judges** have **all the powers of a full judge** under these rules.
- An associate judge may **sit and act anywhere in Canada** at any time.
- When an associate judge sits, they **constitute the full Court** for the purposes of the appeal.

---

### **4. Filing of Appeals**
- An appeal must be initiated **within 90 days** of the date the Minister’s decision is communicated to the appellant.
- **Extensions** may be granted under specific conditions (see below).

#### **Filing Methods**
An appeal must be filed by:
1. **Depositing** with the Registry;
2. **Sending by mail** to the Registry; or  
3. **Sending by fax or electronic filing** to the Registry.

> *Note: A special form (Schedule 5) is provided as an example, but no mandatory pleading format is required.*

---

### **5. Date of Filing**
- **For paper or fax filings**: The date is the **date shown on the receipt stamp** from the Registry.
- **For electronic filings**: The date is the **date shown on the acknowledgment of receipt** issued by the Court.
- The **printed copy** of an electronically filed document is deemed the **original version**.
- If required, a party must provide a **paper copy** of an electronically filed document.

> If the Registry has **no record** of receipt, the document is **deemed not filed**, unless the Court directs otherwise.

---

### **6. Application for Extension of Time**
An applicant may request an extension of the 90-day appeal period if:
- The application is made **within 90 days of the 90-day deadline** (i.e., within 180 days of the decision date).
- The applicant demonstrates:
  - **Inability to act** or **inability to instruct a representative** during the initial 90 days, OR
  - A **good faith intention** to appeal.
- The extension is **just and equitable** given the circumstances.
- The application is made **as soon as possible** after the obstacle arises.
- There are **reasonable grounds** for appealing the decision.

#### **Process**:
- The application must be filed with **three copies** of the application and **three copies of the notice of appeal**.
- The Court will consider the application **after giving the Minister an opportunity to respond**.
- The Registrar must **notify both the applicant and the Minister** of the decision.
- The application is deemed filed on the **date of receipt by the Registry**, even if the notice of appeal is filed later — **provided it is filed within 30 days** of the application date or within a reasonable time set by the Court.

---

### **7. Service of Documents**
#### **Service of Notice of Appeal**
- The Registrar must serve:
  - The Minister and
  - The appellant (or intervener)
- Service can be:
  - **Personal service** (personal service on the Commissioner of Revenue is deemed service on the Minister), OR
  - **By mail** to the Minister.

> If served by mail, the **date of service is the date it is mailed**, and the date is taken as the one shown on the envelope.

#### **Service of Minister’s Response**
- The Minister must:
  - Serve a copy of the notice of appeal and the Registry details to **every person** who received a notification under section 27(5) of the Act.
  - Serve notice of the **name and address** of each such person at the Registry.
  - Serve copies of:
    - The application to determine a question under section 27(1)(a), or
    - The assessment and appeal under section 27(2), and
    - The notification under section 27(5).

> Service is by **mail or filing at the Registry**. Date of service is the **mail date** (as shown on the envelope).

---

### **8. Intervenor Rights**
- Any person affected by a Ministerial decision may **intervene** in an appeal.
- A **notice of intervention** must be filed with the Registry **within 45 days** of being served with the notice of appeal.

#### **Intervention Requirements**
- The notice may state:
  - That the intervener intends to rely on the reasons in the appellant’s notice of appeal, or
  - On the reasons of another intervener.

#### **Service of Intervention Notice**
- The Registrar must serve the **Minister and the appellant** with the notice.
- Service is by **personal service** or **mail**; mail date is the date of mailing.

---

### **9. Consolidation of Appeals**
- If multiple appeals involve a **common legal or factual issue**, the Court may:
  - **Consolidate** them;
  - **Heard at the same time**;
  - **Heard consecutively**;
  - Or **stay** one appeal until another is decided.

> This may be requested by the Minister, an appellant, or an intervener.

- Similarly, if multiple interveners have similar claims, the Court may allow **one notice of intervention** to represent all, if:
  - There is a **common legal or factual issue**, or
  - It is **desirable in the interests of justice**.

---

### **10. Minister’s Response to Appeal or Intervention**
- The Minister must respond **in writing** within **60 days** of receiving the notice of appeal or intervention.
- Extensions may be granted by the Court within that 60-day window.

#### **Response Requirements**
- The reply must:
  - **Admit or deny** the facts stated in the appeal or intervention;
  - Include **any additional facts** on which the Minister intends to rely.

#### **Application for Extension of Time**
- Must include:
  - Date the notice was served;
  - Additional time needed;
  - Reasons for the delay.
- May be filed by:
  - **Filing with the Registry**, or
  - **Sending via telegram, telex, fax, or mail**.
- If sent by **telecom**, the date of service is the **transmission date**.
- If by **mail**, the date is the **postmark date** (earliest if multiple).
- The Court reviews the application **after allowing the appellant or intervener to make representations**.
- The Registrar notifies the Minister and the party.
