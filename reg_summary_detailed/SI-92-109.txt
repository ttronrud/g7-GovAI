**Detailed Summary of the Regulation**

**Regulation Title:**  
Notice of Entry into Force of the Canada-Mexico Tax Information Exchange Convention  

**Date of Notice:** June 3, 1992  
**Issuing Authority:** Government of Canada  
**Legal Basis:** Section 24 of the *Canada-Mexico Tax Information Exchange Convention Act, 1991* (S.C. 1992, c. 3, Part IV)  

---

**Summary:**

This notice formally announces that the *Convention between the Government of Canada and the Government of the United Mexican States for the Exchange of Information with Respect to Taxes* has officially come into force on **April 27, 1992**.

The Convention is a bilateral agreement designed to facilitate the exchange of tax-related information between Canada and Mexico. Its primary objective is to promote transparency, prevent tax evasion, and ensure compliance with tax laws by enabling both countries to share relevant financial and tax information in a structured, confidential, and legally protected manner.

Under Section 24 of the *Canada-Mexico Tax Information Exchange Convention Act, 1991*, the Government of Canada is required to issue a public notice confirming the entry into force of the Convention. This notice serves as formal legal and administrative confirmation that the treaty has been ratified and is now operational between the two countries.

---

**Key Provisions and Implications:**

1. **Entry into Force Date:**  
   The Convention entered into force on **April 27, 1992**, which marks the date when the mutual obligations and rights under the treaty begin to apply. This date is significant as it establishes the effective date for the exchange of tax information between the two jurisdictions.

2. **Purpose of the Convention:**  
   - To enable the exchange of information related to taxation, including financial accounts, income, assets, and tax filings.  
   - To support mutual tax enforcement efforts, particularly in cases involving cross-border transactions, tax evasion, or non-compliance.  
   - To strengthen tax cooperation and enhance confidence in the integrity of tax systems in both countries.

3. **Scope of Information Exchange:**  
   The Convention allows both countries to request and receive information from each other’s tax authorities when necessary to fulfill tax obligations, investigate potential tax violations, or prevent fraud. The information exchanged is typically limited to what is strictly necessary and must be processed in a confidential and secure manner.

4. **Legal and Administrative Framework:**  
   The Convention operates under the authority of the *Canada-Mexico Tax Information Exchange Convention Act, 1991*, which provides the legislative basis for implementing the treaty in Canadian law. The Act ensures that the exchange of information complies with Canadian privacy laws, legal standards, and international obligations.

5. **Public Notification:**  
   The notice issued on June 3, 1992, is a formal public announcement that informs Canadian citizens, businesses, tax professionals, and other stakeholders that the bilateral tax information-sharing mechanism is now active. This transparency helps ensure public awareness and facilitates compliance with the treaty’s requirements.

---

**Significance:**

- This Convention is a cornerstone of Canada’s international tax cooperation efforts, particularly within the North American context.  
- It strengthens the economic and legal ties between Canada and Mexico, two major trading partners in North America.  
- It provides a legal framework for cross-border tax enforcement and supports the broader goals of international tax transparency and fairness.

---

**Conclusion:**

The notice confirms that the Canada-Mexico Tax Information Exchange Convention has officially entered into force on April 27, 1992, as required by Section 24 of the *Canada-Mexico Tax Information Exchange Convention Act, 1991*. This marks the beginning of a formal, legally binding process for exchanging tax-related information between Canada and Mexico, aimed at improving tax compliance, preventing fraud, and fostering economic cooperation in the region.

This regulatory action reflects Canada’s commitment to international tax transparency and effective tax administration in a globalized economy.
