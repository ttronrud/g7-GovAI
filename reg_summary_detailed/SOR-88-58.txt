## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Air Transportation Regulations**

### **1. Regulatory Background and Authority**
- The **Governor in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Transport**, has approved the *Air Transportation Regulations* under **Section 102 of the National Transportation Act, 1987**.
- These regulations establish the legal framework governing **air transportation services** in Canada, including licensing, operational classifications, insurance requirements, and exemptions.
- The regulations are formally cited as the **Air Transportation Regulations**.

### **3. Exemptions from Part II of the Act**

Part II of the *National Transportation Act* (which governs licensing and operational standards) **does not apply** to the following air services:

- Air cushion vehicle services  
- Organ retrieval transportation (for human transplants)  
- Aircraft demonstration services  
- External heli-transports  
- Hot air balloon services  

> ‚úÖ These services are exempt from standard licensing and regulatory oversight under Part II of the Act, though they may still be subject to other safety or operational rules.

---

### **4. Exemption for Non-Crew Personnel on Board**

An air carrier is **exempt** from requiring a domestic or non-scheduled international licence if it transports persons **not part of the air crew** who are **required for the operation** of the service (e.g., medical staff, technicians, or event personnel). This exemption applies only when such persons are necessary for the execution of the air service.

### **5. Classes of Aircraft Allowed Under Licences**

The regulations define **four classes of aircraft** that Canadian air carriers may operate under appropriate licences:

| Class | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Small Aircraft** | Up to 39 passenger seats |
| **Medium Aircraft** | More than 39 but not more than 89 passenger seats |
| **Large Aircraft** | More than 89 passenger seats |
| **All-Cargo Aircraft** | Designed exclusively for goods transport |

> ‚úÖ Each air carrier holding a licence for a specific class of aircraft is **assigned a designation** matching that class (e.g., "Domestic Service, Large Aircraft").

### **6. Classes of Air Services by Licence Type**

The regulations establish distinct classes of **air services** that may be operated under different types of licences:

#### A. **Domestic Services** (under a domestic licence)
- Domestic service, small aircraft  
- Domestic service, medium aircraft  
- Domestic service, large aircraft  
- Domestic service, all-cargo aircraft  

#### B. **Scheduled International Services** (under a scheduled international licence)
- Scheduled international service, small aircraft  
- Scheduled international service, medium aircraft  
- Scheduled international service, large aircraft  
- Scheduled international service, all-cargo aircraft  
- Scheduled international service (for non-Canadian carriers)  

#### C. **Non-Scheduled International Services** (under a non-scheduled international licence)
- Non-scheduled international service, small aircraft  
- Non-scheduled international service, medium aircraft  
- Non-scheduled international service, large aircraft  
- Non-scheduled international service, all-cargo aircraft  
- Non-scheduled international service (for non-Canadian carriers)  

> ‚úÖ Each air carrier and its licence are **assigned a designation** matching the class of service operated.

### **7. Insurance Requirements (Mandatory)**

All air carriers operating domestic or international services must maintain **two types of liability insurance**:

#### (a) **Passenger Liability Insurance**
- Must cover injuries or deaths of passengers during boarding, disembarking, or on board.
- **Minimum coverage amount** =  
  **$595,000 √ó number of passenger seats** on board  
  (adjusted every 5 years via CPI formula)

#### (b) **Third-Party Liability Insurance**

### **8. Restrictions on Insurance Exclusions**

Air carriers **cannot exclude or waive** coverage below the required minimums **unless** the exclusion meets one of the following conditions:

1. **Standard Industry Exclusions** covering:
   - War, hijacking, terrorism
   - Noise, pollution, aviation radioactive contamination

2. **Exclusion for liability arising from contracts** ‚Äî if the liability would have existed even without the contract.

3. **Fraud or misrepresentation clause** ‚Äî the policy is void if the carrier concealed material facts or committed fraud.

> ‚ùå Any exclusion that reduces coverage below the minimums is invalid unless it falls under one of these approved categories.

---

### **9. Comprehensive Single Limit Coverage**

- Air carriers **may use a single policy** (or combination of primary and excess policies) to cover all liability risks.
- However, **no single limit** can be less than the **combined minimums** required under (a) and (b) above.

---

### **10. Working Day Definition**

- A **working day** for filing documents with the National Transportation Agency is defined as a day when **Public Service of Canada offices are generally open** in the province where the head office or regional office is located.
