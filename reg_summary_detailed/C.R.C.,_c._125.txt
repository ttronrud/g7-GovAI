### **Detailed Summary of the Yarmouth Airport Zoning Regulations**

---

#### **1. Regulation Title and Citation**
These regulations are officially titled the **Yarmouth Airport Zoning Regulations** and may be cited as such. They establish zoning and land-use restrictions specifically designed to ensure the safe operation of aircraft at Yarmouth Airport by controlling the height and development of structures, natural growth, and land use near the airport.

#### **3. Scope of Application**

These regulations apply to all lands **adjacent to or in the vicinity of Yarmouth Airport**, with outer boundaries precisely described in a detailed legal description (a "metes and bounds" description). The boundary includes:

- Starting at the intersection of the **southwesterly boundary of Wyman Road** and the **westerly boundary of a road leading to Pinkney Point**.
- Proceeding through various lot lines, shoreline boundaries (Yarmouth Sound, Yarmouth Harbor, Milo Lake, Telfry Lake, Chebogue River), and highways.
- Ending at the **intersection of the southeasterly boundary of Grove Road and the southwesterly boundary of Vancouver Street**, and including **Bunker Island**.

#### **4. Height Restrictions on Buildings and Structures**

##### **General Prohibition (Section 5)**
No person may erect, construct, or expand any building, structure, or object **on land subject to these regulations** if the **highest point** of such structure exceeds the elevation of any of the following surfaces **at the location of that point**:

1. **Horizontal Surface** (150 ft above ARP = 256 ft above sea level)  
2. **Approach Surfaces**:
   - For **Runway 06â€“24**:
     - 600 ft on each side of the centerline at the strip ends.
     - 2,000 ft on each side of the projected centerline at outer ends (200 ft above strip end elevations).
     - Extends **10,000 ft horizontally** from the strip ends.
   - For **Runway 15â€“33**:
     - 500 ft on each side at strip ends.
     - 1,100 ft on each side at outer ends (200 ft above strip end elevations).
     - Extends **8,000 ft horizontally** from the strip ends.
3. **Transitional Surfaces** (1:7 slope from strip edges to horizontal surface)

> âŒ Violation: Any structure exceeding these surfaces is prohibited.

##### **Exception (Permitted Structures)**
A building, structure, or addition **may exceed the horizontal surface** (i.e., go above 256 ft) **only if**:
- Its highest point is **no more than 30 feet above the land surface** at that location.
- This exception allows for minor structures (e.g., small towers, antennas, or landscaping features) to rise above the horizontal surface, as long as they remain within a 30-foot buffer above ground level.

#### **5. Prohibition of Interference with Aeronautical Operations**

No owner or lessee of land under these regulations may:
- Use or develop land in a way that **interferes with signals or communications** to or from aircraft.
- Interfere with **any aeronautical facilities** (e.g., radar, radio towers, navigation aids, emergency services).

> ðŸ” This ensures uninterrupted air traffic control, flight safety, and communication.

#### **6. Management of Natural Growth**

If **natural vegetation** (e.g., trees, shrubs) grows to a height that exceeds any of the defined surfaces (approach, transitional, or horizontal), the **Minister** may issue a requirement for the **removal of the excess growth**.

### **Key Regulatory Objectives**

1. **Flight Safety**  
   Prevents obstructions to aircraft during takeoff, landing, and approach phases by limiting structure heights near runways and approach paths.

2. **Clearance Zones**  
   Establishes defined geometric surfaces (approach, transitional, horizontal) to provide safe visual and physical clearance for aircraft.

3. **Land Use Control**  
   Restricts development near the airport to prevent interference with air traffic operations and communication systems.

4. **Enforceable Boundaries**  
   Uses precise legal descriptions and reference points to ensure clarity and consistency in enforcement.

5. **Flexibility for Minor Developments**  
   Allows limited height above the horizontal surface (up to 30 ft) for small structures or natural features, balancing safety with local development needs.
