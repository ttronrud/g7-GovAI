### **Purpose of the Regulation**

This regulation designates specific provisions of the *Canada Wildlife Act* as being **enforceable under paragraph 13(1)(b)** of the Act.  

#### Key Interpretation:
- **Paragraph 13(1)(b)** of the Canada Wildlife Act provides that a violation of a provision of the Act may be enforced **if it is a provision designated under this regulation**.
- This designation allows enforcement authorities (e.g., Environment and Climate Change Canada, provincial agencies) to apply penalties, issue fines, or take legal action against individuals or organizations that breach the designated provisions.

The regulation essentially **clarifies which specific provisions of the Canada Wildlife Act are subject to enforcement action**, ensuring legal certainty and operational clarity for regulators and the public.

---

### **Provisions Designated for Enforcement**

The following provisions of the *Canada Wildlife Act* are formally designated for enforcement under paragraph 13(1)(b):

1. **Paragraphs 3(1)(a) to (f), (k) to (n), (p) to (s), and (u)**  
   - These cover a range of prohibitions related to:
     - Unauthorized possession, hunting, or killing of wildlife (e.g., trapping, taking, or possessing wildlife without a permit)
     - Prohibitions on the use of certain methods or equipment (e.g., snares, poisons, or firearms without authorization)
     - Restrictions on the transport or sale of wildlife or wildlife products
     - Prohibitions on disturbing wildlife in protected areas
     - Prohibitions on possessing wildlife in violation of licensing or permit conditions

2. **Subsection 3(2)**  
   - This provision typically addresses the **prohibition on the taking of wildlife without a license**, including the requirement that all hunting, trapping, or collecting must be authorized under a valid permit.

3. **Subsection 3.8(4)**  
   - This provision usually relates to **prohibitions on the possession or use of certain wildlife-related equipment or materials** (e.g., snares, traps, or tools) without proper authorization or registration.

4. **Paragraph 8.1(3)(b)**  
   - This provision generally addresses **prohibitions on the importation, exportation, or movement of wildlife or wildlife products** across provincial or national borders without proper authorization. It may include restrictions on the movement of species listed under the Act or protected under international agreements.
