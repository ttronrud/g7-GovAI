**Detailed Summary of the Government Airport Concession Operations Regulations (SOR/76-538)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Government Airport Concession Operations Regulations* (SOR/76-538), issued by His Excellency the Governor General in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Transport under sections 25 and 26 of the *Department of Transport Act*, **revoke the previous regulations** (Order in Council P.C. 1976-2076) and **introduce a comprehensive framework** governing the operation of commercial and other transportation services at government-controlled airports in Canada.

These regulations establish clear rules for the **issuance, enforcement, and supervision of permits** for commercial passenger vehicles and courtesy vehicles operating at airports, with a focus on **public safety, order, convenience, and compliance**. They apply specifically to airports under the direct management or ownership of the federal government.

---

## **Key Definitions**

To ensure clarity, the regulations define several key terms:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Airport** | An aerodrome with an official airport licence issued by the Minister under the Air Regulations. |
| **Airport Manager** | A Department of Transport official responsible for managing an airport or their authorized representative. |
| **Board** | A group of persons appointed by the Minister to advise on the issuance or cancellation of permits. |
| **Bus** | A commercial passenger vehicle operating on a per-passenger fare basis with a specific route or destination. |
| **Commercial Passenger Vehicle** | A vehicle used to transport people for compensation; excludes medical transport, urban transit buses, and transit services for disabled persons. |
| **Courtesy Vehicle** | A vehicle operated by a commercial enterprise to transport its own customers between the airport and the enterprise’s office. |
| **Designated Airport** | An airport designated by the Minister where permits are required for certain vehicles. |
| **Designated Vehicle** | A specific class of vehicle (e.g., limousine or bus) specified by the Minister that requires a permit. |
| **Dispatch Service** | A service that directs taxicabs or limousines to passengers who request them. |
| **Driver** | The person driving or in control of a motor vehicle. |
| **General Vehicle Station** | A designated area for parking courtesy or commercial passenger vehicles (excluding taxis or limousines). |
| **Ground Transportation Inspector** | A person appointed by the airport manager to monitor and enforce compliance. |
| **Limousine** | A commercial vehicle with ≤10 seats (including driver), used on a zone fare basis. |
| **Taxicab** | A commercial vehicle with a meter, not a bus or limousine. |
| **Operator** | The person in charge of a vehicle, whether or not they are driving. |
| **Permit** | A license issued by the airport manager allowing operation of a vehicle at an airport. |
| **Permit Holder** | The operator named in the permit. |
| **Plate** | A physical device (card, sticker, etc.) issued to identify a vehicle with an active permit. |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Transport or an authorized representative. |

> **Exclusions**: Commercial passenger vehicles do **not** include:
> - Vehicles primarily used for medical transport (to/from medical facilities),
> - Buses providing regular urban transit (not to/from airports), or
> - Buses serving persons with disabilities.

---

## **Scope of Application**

These regulations apply to:
1. All **government-managed airports**, and  
2. Airports **belonging to or occupied by Her Majesty in right of Canada**.

The Minister has the authority to designate any airport where commercial or courtesy vehicle operations are regulated.

---

## **Designation Powers of the Minister**

- The Minister may **designate any airport** where commercial or courtesy vehicles must obtain a permit.
- The Minister may **specify the types of vehicles** (e.g., limousines, buses) that require a permit at such airports.
- These designations or specifications can be **revoked at any time** by the Minister.

---

## **Appointment of Ground Transportation Inspectors**

- The **airport manager** may appoint one or more individuals as **ground transportation inspectors** to monitor and enforce compliance.
- **No person may obstruct** a ground transportation inspector in the performance of their duties.

---

## **Designation of Parking Areas (Section 6)**

Airport managers may designate areas at the airport as:
- **Open Taxicab Station**
- **Open Limousine Station**
- **General Vehicle Station**

**Rules for Operation:**
- **General Vehicle Station**: Only courtesy vehicles or non-taxi/commercial passenger vehicles may pick up or unload passengers/goods here.
- **Open Taxicab or Limousine Station**: Only taxis or limousines may pick up or unload passengers/goods at these designated stations.

This ensures **orderly and safe access** to transportation services at the airport.

---

## **Permit Requirements (Section 8)**

**Any person operating a business or commercial service at an airport must:**
- Have a valid **permit**, or
- Have a **lease, licence, or contract** with the Minister for that operation.

**Additionally**, any person who **fixes, installs, or places equipment** at the airport for a commercial purpose must also have a permit or such a contract.

> **Exception**: A person may transport passengers **from outside the airport to inside** without a permit **if**:
> - The transportation does not violate provincial trespass laws.

---

## **Transportation from Airport to Outside (Section 9)**

To transport passengers **from an airport to outside** the airport, the operator must:
- Have a **licence agreement** with the Minister, or  
- Hold a **valid permit** and operate in accordance with its terms and conditions.

> This ensures that only authorized operators provide inter-airport transportation.

---

## **Emergency and Irregular Flight Exceptions (Section 10)**

The airport manager may authorize the operation of a commercial vehicle **in emergency situations**, including:
- Public safety, convenience, or necessity (e.g., medical emergency),
- Weather or technical disruptions causing inadequate ground transport,
- Transport at remote locations not served by authorized services.

This allows flexibility during unforeseen circumstances.

---

## **Conditions for Operation of Commercial Passenger Vehicles (Section 11)**

All commercial passenger vehicles must comply with the following conditions:

1. **Direct Route Requirement**:  
   A driver who transports a passenger from outside the airport to inside must **leave the airport by the most direct route**.

2. **Refusal of Transport**:  
   When a commercial vehicle (non-bus) reaches the front of a waiting line, the driver **cannot refuse** to transport a passenger unless:
   - The passenger is, in the opinion of a peace officer or ground transportation inspector, **intoxicated or behaving disorderly**.

3. **Fare Limitation**:  
   The only fee that can be charged for a ride from the airport to outside is:
   - The fee set by a **provincial or municipal tariff**, or
   - A tariff **approved by the Minister**.

4. **Inspection Requirement**:  
   Upon request by a ground transportation inspector:
   - The vehicle must **stop**,
   - The inspector must be allowed to **inspect the vehicle**, and
   - The operator must **produce the permit** for examination.

5. **Compliance with Permit Terms**:  
   The operator and driver must follow all terms and conditions of the permit.

6. **Taxi Licensing Requirement**:  
   A taxicab operator must hold a **valid provincial or municipal taxi licence**.

---

## **Establishment of Dispatch Services**

- The airport manager may establish a **dispatch service** (e.g., a service that directs taxis or limousines to passengers) at a designated airport.

---

## **Issuance of Permits (Section 12–14)**

- Operators may apply in writing to the airport manager for a permit to operate at a designated airport.
- The airport manager may issue the permit **subject to conditions** deemed necessary.
- A **plate** is provided to the permit holder upon issuance.

> **Permit Validity**:  
> Permits are valid for a period specified in the permit.

> **Expiry**:  
> Upon expiry, the permit must be **returned to the airport manager**.

---

## **Board Review of Permit Applications (Section 13)**

- The airport manager may **refer permit applications to a Board** for advice on:
  - Whether the permit should be issued,
  - And under what terms and conditions.

> The Board’s advice is only binding **if**:
> - A **hearing** is held,
> - The applicant was a party to the hearing,
> - The airport manager or representative had an opportunity to be heard.

> The airport manager **must consider** the Board’s advice before making a decision.

---

## **Suspension of Permits (Section 15)**

- The airport manager may **suspend a permit for up to 30 days** if there is reasonable belief that:
  - The permit holder or driver has violated the regulations or permit terms.

> **Notice Requirements**:
> - Written notice (by hand or registered mail) must be delivered to the permit holder stating:
>   - Reason for suspension,
>   - Duration of suspension,
>   - That the permit holder may:
>     - Appear in person within 10 days to make representations, or
>     - Submit written reasons why suspension should not occur.

> The suspension decision is made **after considering** the representations or reasons.

> The airport manager must **notify the permit holder in writing** of the decision.
