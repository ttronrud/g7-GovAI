#### **Nature of the Transfer**
The specific transfer involves:
- The **powers, duties, and functions** of the **Minister of Canadian Heritage**

#### **Legal Basis of the Official Languages Act**
The **Official Languages Act** (R.S., c. 31 (4th Supp.)) is the primary legislation governing Canada’s official language policy. It establishes:
- English and French as the official languages of Canada
- The obligation of federal institutions to provide services in both official languages
- The role of the federal government in promoting linguistic duality and protecting the rights of francophones

The transfer of these powers to the Minister of La Francophonie reflects a strategic realignment of federal governance to better align with the priorities of linguistic minority communities, particularly in French-speaking regions.
