## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Special Economic Measures (Russia) Regulations**

### üìå **Background and Purpose**
The regulation was enacted by the Governor in Council (on the recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs) in response to the **Russian Federation‚Äôs actions** that are deemed to constitute a **grave breach of international peace and security**, posing a serious risk of international crisis. This includes actions such as military aggression against Ukraine and systemic human rights violations.

The primary objective is to **apply targeted economic sanctions** against individuals, entities, and goods linked to Russia, in order to:
- Impose economic pressure on the Russian state;
- Prevent support for actions undermining Ukraine‚Äôs sovereignty;
- Protect Canadian interests and international norms;
- Support the stability and security of international institutions.

These measures are grounded in **subsections 4(1) to (3)** of the *Special Economic Measures Act*, which empower the government to impose restrictions on trade, finance, and technology related to designated persons or entities.

## üìö **Schedules and Designated Lists**

### üîπ **Schedule 1 ‚Äì Designated Persons**
A person listed in Schedule 1 is considered a **"designated person"** if the Governor in Council (on Minister‚Äôs recommendation) is satisfied there are reasonable grounds to believe the individual:
- Has engaged in activities that **facilitate, fund, or support violations of Ukraine‚Äôs sovereignty or territorial integrity**;
- Has participated in **gross and systematic human rights violations in Russia**;
- Is a **senior official of the Russian government**;
- Is an **associate** of someone listed in any of the above;
- Is a **family member** of someone listed in any of the above;
- Owns, controls, or acts on behalf of a person listed in (a)‚Äì(f);
- Is an entity owned or controlled by Russia or acting on its behalf.

‚û°Ô∏è **Consequence**: Prohibits **all dealings** with such persons ‚Äî including transactions, financial services, and supply of goods.

---

### üîπ **Schedule 1.1 ‚Äì Ships with Restricted IMO Numbers**
A ship with an IMO number listed in Schedule 1.1 is considered a **"restricted vessel"** if the Governor in Council is satisfied it is:
- Transporting or has transported goods **on behalf of or for the benefit of Russia**.

‚û°Ô∏è **Consequence**: Prohibits **docking or passage through Canada** unless necessary for:
- Navigational safety;
- Safeguarding human life.

Also, **services related to such vessels** (e.g., maintenance, chartering) are prohibited for Canadians or Canadians abroad.

## üö´ **Prohibited Activities and Transactions**

### 1. **Dealing with Designated Persons (Schedule 1)**
Any person in Canada or abroad **cannot**:
- Deal in property owned by or on behalf of a person in Schedule 1;
- Enter into or facilitate transactions related to such dealings;
- Provide financial or related services;
- Make available goods to such persons or their agents.

> üîç This includes **direct or indirect financial transactions**, **sales**, **transfers**, and **services**.

---

### 2. **New Debt Restrictions**
| Schedule | Maturity Threshold | Instruments Prohibited |
|--------|--------------------|------------------------|
| 2 | >30 days | Bonds, loans, credit extensions, letters of credit, treasury bills, commercial paper, etc. |
| 3 | >90 days | Same instruments, but higher threshold |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exemption**: Existing debt instruments are not affected. New debt only begins to be restricted **after listing**.

### 3. **Securities Transactions (Schedule 2)**
Prohibits **new issuance or trading of securities** (e.g., shares, ownership interests) in relation to persons in Schedule 2.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exemption**: Activities before listing are not affected.

### 4. **Export of Specific Goods to Russia (Schedules 4, 5, 5.01, 5.02, 7)**
#### üî∏ **Schedule 4** ‚Äì Prohibited Goods
Prohibits exporting or supplying to Russia:
- Goods for **offshore oil exploration or production at depth >500 meters**;
- Goods for **Arctic oil exploration or production**;
- Goods for **shale oil exploration or production**.

> ‚úÖ **Exemption**: Contracts entered into **before the regulation came into force** are not affected.

#### üî∏ **Schedule 5** ‚Äì Import Prohibitions
Prohibits importing from Russia:
- Goods listed in column 1 of Schedule 5 (e.g., certain industrial equipment, high-tech components).

> ‚úÖ **Exemption**:
> - Goods imported under contracts **entered into ‚â•60 days before** the regulation came into force;
> - Goods imported **within 120 days after** the regulation came into force.

#### üî∏ **Schedule 5.01** ‚Äì Import from Russia (with conditions)
Same as Schedule 5, but applies to goods **exported from Russia at least 60 days before** the regulation came into force.

> ‚úÖ **Exemption**: Applies only to goods that meet **both**:
> - Contract entered ‚â•60 days before;
> - Import within 120 days after.

#### üî∏ **Schedule 5.02** ‚Äì Export to Russia
Prohibits exporting to Russia:
- Goods listed in column 1 of Schedule 5.02 (e.g., certain defense-related or energy equipment).

> ‚úÖ **Exemptions**:
> - **Usual and reasonable quantities** of supplies for aircraft/ship consumption;
> - Goods under contracts entered ‚â•60 days before and shipped within 120 days after.

### 5. **Restricted Goods and Technologies List (RGTL)**
Any good or technology listed in the **RGTL** or **Schedule 5.1** is:
- **Prohibited from being exported to Russia or persons in Russia**;
- **Prohibited from being provided to Russia or persons in Russia** (including technical services).

> ‚úÖ **Exemptions**:
> - Goods/services for:
>   - Media representatives (temporary export);
>   - International nuclear safeguards;
>   - Inspections under the Chemical Weapons Convention;
>   - International Space Station activities;
>   - Software updates for Canadian-owned or partner-country entities;
>   - Civil aircraft (temporary sojourns);
>   - Equipment and spare parts necessary for operation;
>   - Usual and reasonable supplies for aircraft/ship;
>   - Personal effects (for individual use only);
>   - Urgent health, safety, or environmental mitigation.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Note**: Exemptions are narrowly defined and require specific justification.

- Technology used in those goods
