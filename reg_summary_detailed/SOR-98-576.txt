**Detailed Summary of the Regulation:**

**Regulation Title:**  
Capacity Plates and Conformity Plates Charges Order  
**Statutory Authority:**  
Pursuant to Order in Council P.C. 1994-642 of April 21, 1994, and under paragraph 19(1)(b)b of the *Financial Administration Act*  
**Statutory Reference:**  
S.C. 1991, c. 24, s. 6  
**Date of Issuance:**  
November 26, 1998  
**Effective Date:**  
April 1, 1999  

---

### **Purpose of the Regulation**
This regulation establishes the fees (charges) that must be paid by individuals or entities seeking to obtain either a **Capacity Plate** or a **Conformity Plate** for certain vehicles or equipment, particularly those subject to regulatory standards related to safety, performance, and construction.

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Capacity Plate**  
   - *Definition:* A plate that indicates the **recommended safe limits** of engine power and the **recommended gross load capacity** of a vehicle or piece of equipment.  
   - *Purpose:* Ensures that operators do not exceed safe operational parameters, helping to prevent mechanical failure, safety hazards, or damage to infrastructure.  
   - *Applicability:* Typically issued for heavy-duty vehicles, such as trucks, trailers, or off-road machinery, where power and load capacity are critical for safe operation.

2. **Conformity Plate**  
   - *Definition:* A notice in the form of a plate that certifies compliance with **construction standards**.  
   - *Purpose:* Confirms that a vehicle or equipment has been built or modified in accordance with established safety, structural, and regulatory standards.  
   - *Applicability:* Used to verify that the physical construction of a vehicle or machine meets prescribed technical specifications.

---

### **Fees (Charges)**

- **Charge for a Capacity Plate:**  
  $5.00  
  - This fee is levied when a user applies for or receives a Capacity Plate that specifies engine power and gross load limits.

- **Charge for a Conformity Plate:**  
  $5.00  
  - This fee is levied when a user applies for or receives a Conformity Plate confirming compliance with construction standards.

> Both charges are identical in amount and are intended to cover administrative and regulatory processing costs associated with issuing these plates.

---

### **Legal Basis**

- The regulation is made under the authority of **Order in Council P.C. 1994-642**, which was issued on April 21, 1994.  
- It is authorized under **paragraph 19(1)(b)b of the Financial Administration Act**, which allows the Minister of Transport to establish charges for administrative services, including the issuance of regulatory plates.

This provision enables the government to generate revenue to support the administrative costs of regulating vehicle safety and performance standards.

---

### **Effective Date**

- The Order comes into force on **April 1, 1999**.  
- All applications for Capacity Plates or Conformity Plates made on or after this date are subject to the $5 fee structure outlined in this regulation.

---

### **Significance and Implications**

- **Regulatory Clarity:** Provides clear, standardized fees for issuing two key safety-related regulatory plates, improving transparency for users.
- **Cost-Effectiveness:** The low fee of $5 reflects a balance between administrative efficiency and accessibility, ensuring that small operators or businesses are not unduly burdened.
- **Safety and Compliance:** Reinforces the importance of adhering to safe operating limits and construction standards, thereby reducing risks of accidents, equipment failure, or non-compliance with transportation laws.
- **Administrative Oversight:** Allows the Minister of Transport to maintain control over the issuance of these plates, ensuring only properly evaluated and approved vehicles receive them.

---

### **Conclusion**

This regulation is a concise and practical administrative measure that establishes a uniform fee of $5 for the issuance of both Capacity and Conformity Plates. It supports the broader regulatory framework aimed at ensuring vehicle safety, operational efficiency, and compliance with national standards. By clearly defining the purpose and cost of these plates, the regulation promotes accountability, transparency, and consistency in transportation safety oversight.
