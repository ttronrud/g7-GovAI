**Detailed Summary of the Regulations Respecting Excise Licences and Registrations**  
*Statutory Citation: S.C. 2002, c. 22 (as amended)*  
*Made under subsection 304(1) of the Excise Act, 2001*

---

### **1. Purpose of the Regulations**
These regulations establish the criteria, procedures, and conditions governing the issuance, renewal, suspension, and cancellation of **excise licences and registrations** in Canada. They are administered by the Minister of National Revenue and apply to entities involved in the production, distribution, sale, or storage of alcohol, tobacco, cannabis, and vaping products.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Excise Act, 2001* (Loi sur les produits excisés, 2001). |
| **Licence** | A formal authorization issued under sections 14, 19, 20, or 22 of the Act. This includes licences for producing, possessing, or distributing alcohol, tobacco, cannabis, or vaping products. |
| **Registration** | A formal authorization issued under sections 15 to 18 of the Act. Typically granted for businesses engaged in retail or other operations involving excisable goods, without the need for full production or warehousing capacity. |

---

### **3. Eligibility Requirements for a Licence (Sections 2–3)**

To qualify for a licence (excluding those under section 22), applicants must meet the following criteria:

#### **General Eligibility Criteria**
- The applicant is **not subject to a receivership** in respect of their debts.
- In the **five years prior to application**, the applicant has:
  - Not failed to comply with any federal or provincial law related to taxation or controls on:
    - Alcohol
    - Tobacco
    - Cannabis
    - Vaping products
    - Or any regulations made under such laws
  - Not acted to **defraud Her Majesty** (i.e., committed fraud or dishonesty).

#### **Individual Applicants**
- Must be **at least 18 years of age**.
- Must have **sufficient financial resources** to operate the business responsibly.

#### **Partnerships or Unincorporated Bodies**
- **All individuals** in the partnership must meet the individual eligibility requirements (age and financial responsibility).
- The **partnership itself** must meet the financial responsibility requirement (subparagraph c)(ii).
- **If composed only of corporations**:
  - Each corporation must meet the financial responsibility requirement.
- **If composed of both individuals and corporations**:
  - Individuals must meet age and financial requirements (c)(i).
  - The partnership and each corporation must meet the financial responsibility requirement (c)(ii).

#### **Corporate Applicants**
- The corporation must meet the financial responsibility requirement (c)(ii).

> **Note**: Section 3(3) adds a **province-specific requirement** for licences under section 19 (authorizing possession of non-duty-paid packaged alcohol in an excise warehouse):
> - If the province where the warehouse is located has legislation on alcohol warehousing, the applicant must be **authorized by that province or its liquor authority** to warehouse alcohol.
> - This requirement **does not apply** if the applicant is an existing alcohol licensee who is producing or packaging alcohol in that province.

> **Note**: This provision was **repealed in 2005** (SOR/2005-355, s. 1), so it is no longer in force.

---

### **4. Eligibility Requirements for a Registration (Section 4)**

To qualify for a registration, applicants must:
- Not be subject to a receivership in respect of their debts.
- In the five years prior to application, have:
  - Not failed to comply with any federal or provincial law related to taxation or controls on:
    - Alcohol
    - Tobacco products
  - Not acted to defraud Her Majesty.

> *Note*: The requirements for registration are less stringent than those for a licence, as registration typically applies to retail or smaller-scale operations.

---

### **5. Validity Periods of Licences (Section 5)**

The duration of a licence is limited as follows:

| Licence Type | Validity Period |
|-------------|------------------|
| **Cannabis licence** | Up to **five years**, but must end **on or before the expiry date of the cannabis permit under subsection 62(1) of the Cannabis Act** |
| **Vaping product licence** | Up to **three years** |
| **All other licences** | Up to **two years** |

> *Note*: The five-year limit for cannabis licences reflects the longer-term regulatory framework established under the Cannabis Act.

---

### **6. Security Requirements (Section 23(3)(b))**

Applicants must provide security to ensure compliance with tax and financial obligations. The required amount is **not less than $5,000**, with specific caps and conditions depending on the product type.

| Licence Type | Security Requirement |
|-------------|------------------------|
| **Spirits licence** | Must be sufficient to cover all bulk spirits duties (as per sections 104–112 of the Excise Act) up to a **maximum of $2 million** |
| **Tobacco or vaping product licence** | Must cover the duty amount (paragraph 160(b) of the Excise Act) up to a **maximum of $5 million per licence** |
| **Cannabis licence** | <ul><li>If the licensee has **quarterly reporting periods** (under subsection 159.2(2) of the Cannabis Act):</li><li>Must cover **one-third of the duty amount** (up to $5 million)</li><li>If reporting periods are otherwise (e.g., annual): must cover the full duty amount (up to $5 million)</li></ul> |

> *Note*: The security must be provided in one of the following acceptable forms:
> - Bank draft
> - Certified cheque
> - Canada Post money order
> - Bond issued by:
>   - A licensed fidelity/surety insurer recommended by the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions
>   - A member of the Canadian Payments Association
>   - A corporation with deposits insured by:
>     - Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC)
>     - Régie de l’assurance-dépôts du Québec (up to statutory limits)
>   - A credit union (as defined in subsection 137(6) of the Income Tax Act)
>   - A corporation accepting public deposits, with repayment guaranteed by the province

---

### **7. Reporting and Notification Obligations**

All licencees and registrants must:

- **Immediately notify the Minister in writing** of any changes to:
  - Personal or business information
  - Premises used in operations
- **Licencees only** must also:
  - Notify the Minister of any changes to their **fiscal months** (e.g., when financial reporting occurs)

> *Purpose*: Ensures the Minister maintains accurate, up-to-date records and can conduct effective oversight.

---

### **8. Amendments to Licences or Registrations**

- If the Minister receives a **notification under section 6** (e.g., changes in business structure or premises), the Minister may **amend** the licence or registration accordingly.

---

### **9. Renewal of Licences (Section 10)**

- A licensee must submit a **completed renewal application** at least **30 days before the licence expires**.
- Renewal is **only permitted** if the licensee has **not ceased to meet the applicable requirements** of section 2 (eligibility criteria).

---

### **10. Suspension of Licences or Registrations (Section 11)**

The Minister may suspend a licence or registration if any of the following grounds exist:

| Ground for Suspension |
|------------------------|
| Failure to meet any of the requirements in sections 2, 3, 6, 7, or 13 |
| Failure to meet the conditions of the licence or registration |
| Bankruptcy |
| Ceasing to carry on the business for which the licence was issued |
| Failure to comply with any Act of Parliament (other than the Excise Act or provincial laws on alcohol, tobacco, cannabis, or vaping) |
| Acting to defraud Her Majesty |

> Upon suspension:
> - The Minister must **notify the licensee or registrant in writing** immediately.
> - The notice must include **all relevant grounds** for suspension.

> The licensee or registrant may **make representations within 90 days** to request reinstatement.

> The licence or registration is **reinstated automatically** when the grounds for suspension cease to exist.

---

### **11. Cancellation of Licences or Registrations (Section 12)**

The Minister may cancel a licence or registration if:

| Ground for Cancellation |
|-------------------------|
| The licensee or registrant **requests cancellation in writing** |
| The applicant is **bankrupt** |
| The applicant **ceases to meet eligibility requirements** (section 2 or 3) |
| The applicant **ceases to carry on the business** |
| The applicant **fails to comply** with any applicable law (other than the Excise Act or provincial excise laws) |
| The applicant **acts to defraud Her Majesty** |

> Before cancellation:
> - The Minister must provide **90 days’ written notice**.
> - The notice must include **all relevant information** about the grounds for cancellation.

> The licensee or registrant may **make representations within 90 days**
