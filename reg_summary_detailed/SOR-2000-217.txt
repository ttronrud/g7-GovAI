## ðŸ“Œ Authorized Possession of Targeted Substances (Part 2 of Schedule 1)

A person may lawfully possess a targeted substance listed in **Part 2 of Schedule 1** (typically benzodiazepines and other prescription drugs) under the following conditions:

### 1. **For Business or Profession**
- Must be:
  - A **licensed dealer**
  - A **pharmacist**
  - A **practitioner** (e.g., doctor, nurse, midwife, podiatrist) registered and entitled to practise in the province where they possess the substance.

### 2. **Emergency Medical Purposes**
- A practitioner registered and entitled to practise in a province **other than** where they are possessing the substance may possess it **only for emergency medical purposes**.

### 3. **Hospital Employees or Practitioners**
- Employees or practitioners working in a **hospital** (licensed, government-owned, or operated) may possess targeted substances.

### 4. **Personal or Responsible Use**
- Possession for **personal use** or for **another person or animal** for whom they are responsible, provided:
  - The substance was obtained:
    - From a **practitioner**, or
    - Through a **prescription** that was **not issued or obtained in contravention** of these regulations.

### 5. **Government Employees**
- Individuals employed as:
  - Inspector
  - RCMP member
  - Police constable
  - Peace officer
  - Technical/scientific staff of federal/provincial government or university
- May possess targeted substances **in connection with their employment**.

### 6. **Exemptions under Section 56 of CDSA**
- Possession is authorized if exempt under section 56 (e.g., for specific legitimate purposes), and the use is for a purpose listed in the exemption.

### 7. **Importation under Section 68**
- Possession is authorized if the substance was **imported legally** under section 68 for personal or another personâ€™s use.

### 8. **Transit or Transhipment**
- A person responsible for the substance during **transit or transhipment in Canada** may possess it under a valid **permit for transit or transhipment**.

### 9. **Ministerâ€™s Authority**
- The **Minister of Health** may possess targeted substances for official purposes.

### 10. **Agents or Mandatories**
- A person acting as **agent or mandatary** of someone authorized under any of the above conditions may possess the substance.

### 11. **Assisting in Enforcement**
- A person may possess a targeted substance **to assist another** in administering or enforcing laws, **if they have reasonable grounds to believe** the other is a person referred to in paragraph (1)(e) above.

### 12. **Exporting**
- An individual may possess a targeted substance **for the purpose of exporting** it in accordance with **section 69** of the CDSA.

> ðŸ” **Important**: Possession is only lawful if it is **authorized by the regulations**, not just because the substance is controlled.

## ðŸ§ª Test Kit Regulations

### 1. **Eligibility for Registration**
A test kit may be registered if:
- It contains a targeted substance and a reagent system or buffering agent.
- It is used for **medical, laboratory, industrial, educational, law enforcement, or research purposes**.
- Its contents are **not intended or likely to be consumed** by humans or animals.

### 2. **Labeling Requirements**
The label must include:
- The **registration number** (preceded by "TK")
- In cases **not subject to Medical Devices Regulations**:
  - Name and address of **manufacturer or assembler** (or custom order recipient)
  - **Brand name**
- **No need** to display the symbol required by Food and Drug Regulations if the registration number is present.

> âœ… This ensures traceability and transparency while preventing misuse.

### 3. **Application Process**
To apply for registration:
- Submit a detailed application to the Minister including:
  - Brand name
  - Design and construction details
  - Identity and quantity of targeted substance and any other substances
  - Salt name (if applicable)
  - Proposed use
  - Directions for use
- Application must be:
  - Signed and dated by an authorized representative
  - Include an attestation that all information is correct and complete

### 4. **Review and Approval**
- Upon review, the Minister **must issue a registration number (TK-xxxx)** if:
  - The test kit will only be used for **authorized purposes** (as listed)
- The Minister **must refuse registration** if:
  - The test kit poses a **risk to public health or safety**, including:
    - Total amount of substance is **too high**
    - Adulterating/denaturing agent fails to prevent consumption
    - Likely to be used for **non-authorized purposes**
- Before refusal, the Minister must:
  - Provide a **notice with reasons**
  - Allow the applicant an **opportunity to be heard**

### 5. **Cancellation of Registration**
The Minister may cancel a registration number if:
- Holder ceases all activities
- The test kit is no longer approved under **Medical Devices Regulations**
- One of the grounds for refusal applies (e.g., risk to public health)
