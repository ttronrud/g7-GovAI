**Detailed Summary of the North American Leaders’ Summit 2016 — Privileges and Immunities Order**

---

**1. Legal Authority and Origin**  
This regulation is issued under the authority of the *Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act* (FMOA), specifically pursuant to **paragraph 5(1)(c)a** of that Act. This provision allows the Governor General in Council—on the recommendation of the Minister of Foreign Affairs—to establish privileges and immunities for participants in certain international conferences or summits, provided they are similar in nature to diplomatic missions or international organizations.

The Order is formally enacted as a **statutory instrument** under the authority of the FMOA and is registered under the following legislative references:  
- **S.C. 2002, c. 12, s. 3(2)** – This section of the FMOA authorizes the Governor General in Council to make orders relating to privileges and immunities for participants in international events.  
- **S.C. 1991, c. 41** – The original enactment of the FMOA, which provides the foundational legal framework for such orders.

---

**2. Purpose of the Order**  
The primary purpose of this Order is to grant **temporary privileges and immunities** to foreign government representatives participating in the **North American Leaders’ Summit 2016**. These privileges are designed to ensure that delegates can carry out their official duties in Canada without undue interference or legal obstacles, consistent with international diplomatic norms.

---

**3. Definition of Key Terms**  
- **"Summit"**: Refers specifically to the **intergovernmental conference known as the North American Leaders’ Summit**, scheduled to take place in the **National Capital Region (NCR)** of Canada on **June 29, 2016**.  
  - The Summit is a high-level meeting among leaders of Canada, the United States, and Mexico, focusing on regional cooperation, economic policy, security, and other shared interests.

---

**4. Scope of Privileges and Immunities**  
The Order grants to **representatives of foreign states participating in the Summit** the following rights and immunities, **to the extent required for the exercise of their official functions in Canada**:

These privileges are drawn from **Article IV of the Convention on the Privileges and Immunities of the United Nations**, specifically:

- **Sections 11(b) and (e)**:  
  - **Section 11(b)**: Immunity from civil and criminal jurisdiction in respect of acts performed in the course of official duties.  
  - **Section 11(e)**: Immunity from arrest or detention for acts performed in the course of official duties.  
  - These provisions protect delegates from being sued or detained in Canada for actions taken during their participation in the Summit.

- **Sections 14 to 16**:  
  - These sections provide protections related to:
    - **Immunity from service of process** (i.e., legal documents such as subpoenas or court orders cannot be served on delegates).
    - **Immunity from search or seizure** of personal property or official documents.
    - **Freedom from interference with official communications** (e.g., telephone calls, messages, or electronic communications) during the Summit.

These immunities are **limited in scope** and are **only applicable to the extent necessary** for the performance of official duties related to the Summit. They do not extend to personal conduct or private activities outside the official functions of the Summit.

---

**5. Application and Timing**  
- The privileges and immunities apply **only on June 29, 2016**, during the Summit event in the National Capital Region.  
- The Order is **not a permanent or ongoing provision**; it is a **temporary, event-specific measure** tailored to the needs of the Summit.  
- The Order **comes into force on the day it is registered** with the appropriate government authority (e.g., the Department of Justice or the Registrar of Statutes).

---

**6. Legal and Diplomatic Significance**  
- This Order reflects Canada’s commitment to international norms and diplomatic practice by recognizing the importance of high-level intergovernmental meetings.  
- By granting privileges similar to those of United Nations missions, Canada ensures that participating leaders can operate freely and effectively, enhancing the legitimacy and efficiency of the Summit.  
- It also demonstrates the application of international law in a domestic regulatory context, aligning Canada’s practices with established diplomatic conventions.

---

**7. Limitations and Conditions**  
- The immunities are **not absolute** and are strictly limited to actions taken in the course of official duties during the Summit.  
- Delegates remain subject to Canadian law in all other matters, including personal conduct, financial obligations, or actions unrelated to the Summit.  
- The Order does **not extend to non-governmental representatives**, such as observers or private sector participants.  
- The immunities are **not transferable** to third parties or to the personal assets of the delegates.

---

**Conclusion**  
The *North American Leaders’ Summit 2016 — Privileges and Immunities Order* is a targeted, temporary legislative instrument that enables foreign government representatives to participate in a major regional summit without facing legal or procedural barriers in Canada. By drawing on established international diplomatic principles—specifically those of the United Nations Convention on Privileges and Immunities—the Order ensures a fair, efficient, and respectful environment for high-level intergovernmental dialogue. It exemplifies how Canada balances national sovereignty with international cooperation through the use of well-defined, legally grounded privileges and immunities.

---  
*Note: This Order is no longer in force, as the Summit took place in 2016 and such temporary orders typically expire upon the conclusion of the event.*
