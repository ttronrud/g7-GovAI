**Detailed Summary of the National Parks Land Rents Remission Order, No. 2**

---

### **Legal Basis and Authority**
This regulation is issued under the authority of **subsection 23(2.1)a of the Financial Administration Act**, which allows the Governor in Council (i.e., the federal government) to make orders remitting land rents when it is in the public interest. The decision is based on the recommendation of two key federal departments:
- The **Minister of Canadian Heritage**
- The **Treasury Board**

This demonstrates a collaborative approach between heritage policy and financial administration to address land rent issues in national parks.

---

### **Purpose of the Order**
The primary objective of the **National Parks Land Rents Remission Order, No. 2** is to **provide financial relief to leaseholders** of public lands located within designated national parks. Specifically, it offers **partial remission (reduction or cancellation)** of rent obligations that would otherwise be due in the year 2000 and for subsequent years, under specific lease conditions.

The remission is designed to:
- Alleviate financial hardship for leaseholders during a period of significant rent adjustments.
- Promote public interest by ensuring sustainable land use in national parks without placing undue burden on private operators or leaseholders.
- Reflect the economic realities of changing rental rates over time, particularly in a transition from higher to lower rates.

---

### **Scope of Application**
The order applies to:
- **Leaseholders** who hold leasehold interests in **public lands situated within national parks** listed in **Schedule I to the Canada National Parks Act**.
- Leases that are governed by:
  - The **terms of the applicable lease agreement**
  - The **National Parks Lease and Licence of Occupation Regulations (1991)**

Thus, only those lease arrangements that are formally recognized and regulated under the current national parks framework are eligible.

### **Conclusion**

The **National Parks Land Rents Remission Order, No. 2** is a targeted, time-limited financial relief measure enacted in **1991** to assist leaseholders in national parks facing declining rental rates during the early 2000s. It operates under a clear, transparent formula based on the difference between initial and final rental rates, with two distinct provisions for **longer-term (10-year)** and **shorter-term (2-year)** leases.

This regulation reflects a pragmatic approach to balancing:
- **Environmental conservation** (by maintaining land access within national parks)
- **Economic fairness** (by mitigating rent reductions for leaseholders)

While it is a historical policy from 1991, it remains a notable example of how federal government can intervene in land lease arrangements to support both public interest and private stakeholders.
