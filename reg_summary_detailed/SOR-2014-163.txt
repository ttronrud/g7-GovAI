#### **Background and Legal Basis**

These regulations are a direct implementation of United Nations Security Council (UNSC) resolutions adopted in 2013â€“2014 in response to the deteriorating security situation in the **Central African Republic (CAR)**. The key resolutions referenced are:

- **UNSCR 2127 (2013)** â€“ December 5, 2013  
- **UNSCR 2134 (2014)** â€“ January 28, 2014  
- **UNSCR 2149 (2014)** â€“ April 10, 2014  

These resolutions established measures to:
- Prevent the flow of weapons and mercenaries into the CAR;
- Prohibit support for armed groups and military activities;
- Strengthen international efforts to stabilize the CAR through humanitarian and security mechanisms.

Under **Section 2 of the United Nations Act**, the Governor in Council (i.e., the federal government of Canada) has the authority to adopt regulations to implement these UNSC measures. This legislation was enacted on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**, and it forms part of Canadaâ€™s international obligations under the UN Charter.

## **Core Prohibitions**

### **Section 1: Prohibition on Dealing with Designated Persons**

Any person in Canada or a Canadian living abroad is **prohibited from**:

1. **Dealing in property** owned, held, or controlled by a **designated person** or someone acting on their behalf or at their direction.
2. **Entering into or facilitating transactions** related to such dealings.
3. **Providing or acquiring financial or related services** for such dealings.
4. **Making available property** to or for the benefit of a designated person or entity owned by or controlled by them.
5. **Providing financial services** to such persons or entities.

> âœ… **Exception**: Payments of **interest or earnings** that occurred **before** the individual became a designated person are not prohibited, provided the amount becomes subject to the restriction.

---

### **Section 2: Prohibition on Humanitarian and Human Rights Support**

Despite the prohibitions above, the following activities are **exempt**:

- **Humanitarian assistance** necessary to meet basic human needs in CAR, including:
  - Deliveries by the **United Nations**, its agencies, specialized bodies, and international organizations.
  - Humanitarian organizations with **observer status** under the UN General Assembly.
  - Bilateral or multilateral NGOs participating in UN humanitarian or refugee response plans.
  - Employees, grantees, subsidiaries, and partners of such NGOs acting in those roles.
- Any person **authorized by a UN Security Council committee** to carry out such activities.

> ğŸ“Œ This ensures that humanitarian efforts are not disrupted by sanctions.

### **Section 3: Prohibition on Arms and Related Material**

It is prohibited to:

- **Export, sell, supply, or transfer** arms and related material (including spare parts) to CAR or to any person in CAR.
- **Provide technical assistance or financial assistance** related to:
  - The sale, supply, transfer, manufacture, maintenance, or use of arms in CAR.

> - **Support for MINUSCA, EU training missions, French forces, or allied forces** â€” **if advance notice is given**.

### **Section 4: Prohibition on Military Activities and Mercenaries**

It is prohibited to:

- Provide **technical or financial assistance** related to **military activities** in CAR.
- Provide **armed mercenaries** to or for the benefit of CAR.

> âŒ This includes any support that enables or facilitates armed conflict or destabilization.

### **Section 5: Prohibition on Transport of Arms**

Any **Canadian-owned vessel** or **aircraft registered in Canada** is prohibited from:

- Carrying, causing to be carried, or permitting to be carried **arms and related material** destined for CAR or any person in CAR.

## **Obligations of Financial Institutions and Entities**

Certain entities must **continuously monitor** their holdings to determine whether they possess or control property belonging to or on behalf of a **designated person**. These include:

- **Authorized foreign banks** and banks regulated under the Bank Act
- **Cooperative credit societies, savings and credit unions, caisses populaires**
- **Foreign companies** involved in insurance business in Canada
- **Insurance companies, provincial companies, societies**
- **Fraternal benefit societies** and entities offering insurance
- **Trust and loan companies**
- **Loan companies**
- **Entities involved in securities dealing or portfolio management** (if they open client accounts)

> ğŸ” These institutions are required to **report immediately** to the **Commissioner of the RCMP** or the **Director of CSIS** if they have reason to believe they hold such property.

## **Disclosure Obligations**

All persons and entities listed above must:

- **Disclose without delay**:
  1. The existence of property they believe is owned, held, or controlled by a designated person.
  2. Any transaction or proposed transaction involving such property.

> âœ… **No liability** arises in Canada for a disclosure made in **good faith** under this provision.

## **Exemptions and Certifications**

A person wishing to engage in a prohibited activity must apply in writing to the **Minister of Foreign Affairs** for a **certificate of exemption**.

### **When a Certificate is Issued**:
- If the **UN Security Council did not intend** the activity to be prohibited.
- If the **Security Council or its Committee has approved** the activity in advance.

> ğŸ“ This allows flexibility for legitimate, authorized, or humanitarian purposes.
