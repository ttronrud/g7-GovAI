**Detailed Summary of the Rules of Practice of the Superior Court of the Province of Quebec, Criminal Division, 2002**

---

### **1. Legal Basis and Scope**
These rules were established by the **Superior Court of Quebec** under **Section 482 of the Criminal Code**, and they govern the conduct of criminal proceedings in all judicial districts of Quebec.  
- The rules came into force **15 days after publication in the Canada Gazette**.  
- They **supersede** all previous rules of practice for the Criminal Division of the Quebec Superior Court, including the *Quebec Superior Court Rules of Practice Respecting Criminal Matters*, **except for cases commenced before the effective date** of these rules, which continue to be governed by the prior rules.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**
- **"Code"** refers to the *Criminal Code of Canada*.
- **"Decision"** includes convictions, verdicts, orders, or sentences.
- **"Clerk"** refers to the clerk of the Superior Court, Criminal Division, for the district where the appeal is filed.
- **"Clerk of the court of first instance"** is the official with legal custody of proceedings before the lower court from which the appeal arises.

---

### **3. Court Session Procedures**
- Sessions begin at **9:30 a.m.** or at a time set by the court.
- All persons present must:
  - **Stand when the judge enters** the courtroom and remain standing until the judge is seated.
  - **Stand again at adjournment** and remain standing until the judge retires.
  - The same applies to **jurors**.
- Upon entry, the **court usher announces**:  
  *"Silence. All rise please. The Superior Court is now in session, the Honourable [Judge] presiding."*
- Once seated, the usher invites others to sit.

---

### **4. Dress Code and Conduct**
- **Mandatory attire during sessions**:
  - **Counsel**: Black gown, bands, white collar, dark clothing.
  - **Articling students**: Black gown and dark clothing.
  - **Court clerk and usher**: Black gown and dark clothing.
- **Exception**: Gowns are **not required in July and August**, except during **jury trials**.
- All persons appearing before the court must be **suitably attired**.
- **Standing required** when addressing the court, unless permitted otherwise.
- **Prohibited behaviors** that interfere with dignity or order:
  - Reading newspapers
  - Taking photographs, filming, or using cellular phones or pagers
- **Media recording**:
  - **Allowed** on **audiotape** (including recordings of decisions), **unless the judge orders otherwise**.
  - **Broadcasting or public dissemination** of any recording (including audio or video) is **strictly prohibited**.
- **Camera use** in courthouses is **restricted** and only permitted in designated areas, as determined by **directives from the Chief Justices**.

---

### **5. Accused and Representation**
- The **accused must remain in the prisoners’ dock** throughout trial unless **authorized by the judge** to sit elsewhere.
- **Counsel who represented the accused at committal** continues to represent them **unless they formally terminate the mandate**.
- To withdraw from representation:
  - Must serve **written notice** on both the accused and the prosecutor.
  - The original notice and proof of service must be **deposited with the clerk**.
  - Withdrawal is only permitted:
    - **Within 14 days before the start of the term** or **during the term**, with:
      - A **motion** filed at least **one clear juridical day in advance**.
      - The motion must state **clear reasons** for withdrawal.
      - The accused must be **ensured of presence** if in custody.
    - The judge must **grant permission**.

---

### **6. Motions and Applications**
- All motions and applications must:
  - Be **in writing**.
  - Be **served on the opposing party or counsel** with **at least one clear juridical day’s notice**, unless the law provides otherwise.
  - Clearly state:
    - **Factual and legal grounds**.
    - **Conclusions sought**.
- Parties relying on **case law or jurisprudence** must:
  - File **copies of relevant pages** with **highlighted extracts**.
- Parties relying on **regulatory or statutory provisions** (other than those in the *Constitution Act, 1982*, *Criminal Code*, *Canada Evidence Act*, *Controlled Drugs and Substances Act*, *Food and Drugs Act*, or *Young Offenders Act*) must:
  - Submit **highlighted copies** of the relevant provisions in the court record.
- Only **motions filed in the clerk’s office in compliance with these rules** are placed on the practice roll (unless otherwise provided).

---

### **7. Use of Videolink and Telephonic Conferences**
- **Videolink hearings** are allowed in districts with suitable equipment:
  - Parties must submit a **written application** to the **Judge Administrator of the Criminal Division**, with a copy to the other parties.
  - In **urgent cases**, applications may be made **by telephone**.
- If granted:
  - Either party may plead from any **available video facility** in their jurisdiction.
  - The party may choose to address the court **in person** at the hearing location.
- The court may impose **conditions** (e.g., technical requirements, time limits) for such proceedings.

---

### **8. Applications Under Sections 520 and 522 of the Criminal Code**
- These applications (e.g., for interim release or bail) **require written notice** from the accused to the prosecutor:
  - **Two clear juridical days’ written notice** required, unless the prosecutor **waives it**.
- The accused’s affidavit must include:
  - Date and place of arrest.
  - Residential addresses (past 10 years) and intended residence (if interim release is sought).
  - Marital status and cohabitation.
  - Occupation, employer, and employment duration.
  - Full criminal record (including foreign convictions).
  - Pending charges (in Canada or abroad).
  - Passport status.

---

### **9. Extraordinary Remedies (Certiorari, Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Procedendo, Prohibition)**
- Must be filed as a **motion supported by one or more affidavits**.
- Affidavits must:
  - Attest to the **truth of facts**.
  - Set out the **relief sought**.
- Must be **served on**:
  - The responsible judge or official.
  - All parties.
- Must include **at least one clear juridical day’s notice** of presentation.
- **No motion filed after 30 days** from the decision or proceeding it challenges can be heard **unless extended by the judge**.
- **Service of the motion suspends** proceedings, but the judge may **order continuation**.
- Upon service, the respondent must **transmit the case record** to the clerk.

---

### **10. Written Submissions for Extraordinary Remedies**
- Parties must file **written submissions** within the time set by the judge.
- Submissions must:
  - Reference **relevant transcript passages**.
  - Be supported by **legal arguments** and **case law**.
- Applies to all extraordinary remedies.

---

### **11. Habeas Corpus in Criminal Matters**
- **Articles 851–855 of the Code of Civil Procedure** apply to criminal habeas corpus proceedings, **with modifications** based on circumstances.

---

### **12. Appeals**
- Appeals must be filed in the **Superior Court of Quebec in the district where the decision was rendered**.
- **Notice of appeal** must be:
  - Signed by the appellant or their counsel.
  - Include:
    - The offence charged.
    - The sentence imposed (if any).
    - Date and place of decision or sentencing.
    - Name and record number of the court of first instance.
    - **Precise and concise grounds of appeal** and conclusions sought.
    - Addresses of appellant, counsel, respondent, and their counsel.
- **Additional grounds** must be submitted in writing **no later than 15 days before the hearing**, with proof of service.
- **Time limit**:
  - Appeals must be commenced **within 30 days** of the decision, **unless extended** under subsection 815(2) of the Code.
- **Filing requirement**:
  - Notice of appeal must be filed in the clerk’s office within 30 days.
- Upon receipt, the clerk must:
  - Send copies to:
    - Counsel from the first instance.
    - The judge who rendered the decision.
    - Clerk of the first instance court.
- For **prosecutorial appeals**, the notice must be served personally on the respondent (unless ordered otherwise).
- Respondent counsel must file a **written appearance within 10 days** (or as extended by the judge).

---

### **13. Practice Roll and Preparatory Proceedings**
- Upon receipt of the notice, the clerk:
  - Calls for the **record of the first instance**.
  - Places the case **pro forma on the practice roll within 30 days** (or as directed).
  - Notifies parties of the date.

  - By telephone at the judge’s option.
