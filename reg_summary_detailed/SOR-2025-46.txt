### **Detailed Summary of the Rules of Practice and Procedure of the Chief Military Judge**

These Rules of Practice and Procedure, established under **Section 165.3a of the National Defence Act (NDA)** and approved by **Her Excellency the Governor General in Council on January 9, 2025**, govern all proceedings before a **military judge** in Canadaâ€™s armed forces. They are designed to ensure **fairness, transparency, efficiency, and consistency** in military justice proceedings, while aligning with both the NDA and the broader legal framework of Canadian law.

The rules were developed through consultation with a **rules committee** established by the Governor in Council, and they apply to all court martial proceedings presided over by a military judge.

---

## **I. Scope and Applicability**

- **Applicability**: These rules apply to all proceedings under the **National Defence Act (Act)** that are conducted before a military judge.
- **Purpose**: To standardize procedures for service of documents, scheduling, hearings, evidence, and access to information, ensuring procedural fairness and operational efficiency.

## **III. Service of Documents**

### **1. Methods of Service**
Documents may be served by:
- **Personal service** (on the day left with the person or their authorized representative),
- **Registered mail** (on the day of delivery shown on the post office receipt),
- **Fax** (on the day of transmission shown on the confirmation receipt),
- **Email** (only after consent is obtained).

> âš ï¸ **Email service is not permitted without prior consent** from the recipient.

---

### **2. Requirements for Fax Service**
- Must be printed on **letter size paper**.
- **Not permitted** if the document exceeds **50 pages** without the recipientâ€™s **prior written consent**.
- Must include a **cover sheet** with:
  - Name, address, and phone number of the serving party;
  - Date and time of transmission;
  - Total number of pages (including cover);
  - Contact for transmission issues.

---

### **3. Requirements for Email Service**
- Must be in **PDF format**.
- Email must contain:
  - Name, address, phone, and email of the sender;
  - Name and counsel (if any) of the recipient;
  - Date and time of transmission;
  - Title of each document, number of attachments, and page count per attachment.

> âœ… **Consent is required** before email service can occur.

---

### **4. Consent to Email Service**
- **Form 1 (Schedule)**: A formal consent form must be submitted to the **Court Martial Administrator** and served on all other parties.
- **Effective date**: The day the form is served.
- **Counsel appointed by Director of Military Prosecutions or Defence Counsel Services** is **deemed to have given consent**, though this consent can be **withdrawn**.

---

### **5. Withdrawal of Consent**
- Must be done via **Form 2 (Schedule)**, submitted to the Court Martial Administrator and served on all parties.
- **Effective upon service**.

---

### **6. Proof of Service**
A party must provide **proof of service** if:
- Required by the rules; or
- The military judge orders it.

**Acceptable forms of proof include**:
- A **certificate of service** (Form 3);
- An **affidavit** or document demonstrating service under local law;
- A **written acknowledgment** from the recipient or their counsel;
- An **acknowledgment or acceptance in open court** before the military judge.

> âœ… **No proof required** if the recipient provides an acknowledgment or acceptance in open court.

## **IV. Custody Review Hearings (Section 158â€“159 of NDA)**

### **1. Notification by Custody Review Officer**
If the officer **does not release** the person in custody, the **representative of the Canadian Forces** must notify the **Court Martial Administrator** as soon as practicable.

### **2. Information to Be Provided (Before Hearing)**
The custody review officer must provide the following to the representative, accused, and Court Martial Administrator:
- Service number, rank, and name of the person in custody;
- List of charges, including whether each is a **designated offence** (as defined in Section 153);
- Date and time of commitment to custody;
- Whether counsel was retained, requested, or declined;
- Location of detention;
- Name and contact of responsible personnel;
- Name, rank, and contact of the custody review officer;
- Date of completion of the review;
- Name and contact of the representative of the Canadian Forces and confirmation of notification;
- Location and contact of the nearest **video conference facility**.

### **3. Telecommunications Hearing (if directed)**
If the military judge orders a hearing via telecommunications:
- All parties must provide:
  - Evidence to be presented;
  - Copies of statutes, regulations, and authorities relied upon;
- The **representative of the Canadian Forces** must provide:
  - The **report of custody** and accompanying documents (as per Sections 158.1(1) and (5));
- All information must be submitted **no later than the day before** the scheduled hearing.

## **V. Pre-Trial and Coordinating Conferences**

### **1. Coordinating Conference**
- Required **before trial** to:
  - Schedule the trial;
  - Discuss matters affecting trial duration or conduct.
- **Timing**:
  - Must be scheduled **within 45 days** of receiving the charge sheet.
  - For proceedings where the accused may choose the type of court martial (under Section 165.193), the 45-day period starts on the **later of**:
    - Receipt of the charge sheet;
    - Receipt of the completed delivery confirmation (under QRO 111.023(3)).
- Must be held **within 15 days** of being contacted by the Court Martial Administrator.
- **Mode**: By **telephone** unless the military judge directs otherwise (e.g., in person or via video).

### **2. Pre-Trial Conference**
- May be ordered **at the request of a party or by the military judge**.
- Purpose: To discuss legal or evidentiary issues that may facilitate trial.
- Mode: Same as coordinating conference â€” **telephone by default**.

## **VI. Applications and Notices**

### **1. General Requirements for Applications**
All applications (except those under QRO 112.04(1)) must include:
- Sufficient detail to allow the opposing party to respond without adjournment;
- Evidence to be presented;
- Estimated time to present the application;
- Proposed time, date, and location of hearing;
- Proposed method of hearing (e.g., in person, by video).

> âœ… Must be **served on all parties** and **a copy with proof of service** provided to the Court Martial Administrator **no later than 5 days before the hearing**.

### **2. Special Requirements**
- **Notice under QRO 112.04(1)** must also include:
  - Proposed time, date, and location;
  - Proposed method of hearing.
- Must be sent to the Court Martial Administrator **within 5 days** of the proposed hearing date.

### **3. Application for a Plea of Guilty (Section 189.1(2))**
- Must be submitted using **Form 5**.
- Must be served on the **prosecutor** and a copy provided to the Court Martial Administrator **no later than 5 days before the accused is ordered to appear**.

### **4. Written Reply to an Application (Form 6)**
- Must include:
  - Position on the issues, grounds of argument, and matters not disputed;
  - Evidence to be presented;
  - Estimated time required.
- Must be sent to all parties and the Court Martial Administrator **no later than the day before the hearing**.

### **5. Withdrawal of Application (Form 7)**
- Must clearly identify the application being withdrawn.
- Must be sent to all parties and the Court Martial Administrator **before the scheduled hearing**.

## **VII. Counsel Withdrawal**

### **1. Withdrawal Before Court Martial is Convened**
- Must serve a notice (Form 8) on:
  - The accused;
  - The Director of Military Prosecutions;
- Must provide a copy to the Court Martial Administrator with **proof of service within 5 days** of service.
