### **Detailed Summary of the Flin Flon Airport Zoning Regulations**

The **Flin Flon Airport Zoning Regulations** are a set of legally binding rules established under the **Aeronautics Act** of Canada to manage land use and development around Flin Flon Airport in Manitoba. These regulations were enacted to ensure the safety and operational efficiency of the airport by restricting structures, natural growth, and waste disposal in areas that could interfere with aircraft operationsâ€”particularly during takeoff and landing.

## **7. Prohibition on Waste Disposal**

- No owner or occupier of regulated land may **use the land for the disposal of any waste that is edible or attractive to birds**.
- This restriction is critical because **bird attraction increases the risk of bird strikes**, which can lead to catastrophic aviation accidents.

## **8. Geographical Boundary**

- The **outer boundary** of the regulated zone is defined as a **circle with a radius of 4,000 meters**, centered on the **Airport Reference Point (ARP)**.

## **9. Purpose and Safety Objectives**

The primary goals of these regulations are:

- To **ensure aviation safety** by eliminating obstacles that could interfere with aircraft approaches and departures.
- To **protect aircraft operations** by maintaining clear zones around runways and approach paths.
- To **prevent environmental hazards**, such as bird attraction, which could lead to dangerous bird strikes.
- To **provide a structured, enforceable framework** based on engineering standards and public consultation.
