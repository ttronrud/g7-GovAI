### **Detailed Summary of the *Reporting of Exported Goods Regulations* (S.C. 2001, c. 25, s. 55)**

These regulations, made under **section 95a of the Customs Act**, establish a comprehensive framework for the **reporting of exported goods** in Canada. The objective is to ensure transparency, compliance with trade laws, and timely collection of export data for statistical and regulatory purposes. The regulations define key terms, specify who must report exports, when and how reports must be submitted, and outline exceptions where reporting is not required.

---

## **I. Key Definitions**

The regulations begin by defining essential terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Act** | Refers to the *Customs Act* (Loi sur les douanes). |
| **Bulk goods** | Goods that are loose or in mass, confined only by permanent structures (e.g., containers or transport units), with no intermediate packaging. |
| **Carrier** | A person (other than the exporter) who transports goods from Canada. |
| **Chief Officer of Customs** | The manager of customs offices serving a specific area or place. |
| **Commercial goods** | Goods exported for sale or for industrial, commercial, institutional, or occupational use. |
| **Customs Service Provider** | Includes customs brokers, freight forwarders, agents, or representatives who provide customs-related services (excluding transport). |
| **Exporter** | A business holder with a business number who exports commercial goods or causes them to be exported. |
| **Export Reporting Office (ERO)** | A designated customs office where export reports must be filed. |
| **Homogeneous goods** | Goods that closely resemble each other in material and characteristics and are intended for the same purpose. |
| **Restricted goods** | Goods whose export is prohibited, controlled, or regulated under the *Customs Act* or another federal Act. |
| **Time-sensitive goods** | Goods that lose value or utility if not exported immediately (e.g., perishables, just-in-time inventory). |

---

## **II. Who Must Report Exports?**

Under **section 3**, the following classes of persons are required to report exported goods:

1. **Exporters**  
2. **Carriers** (transporting the goods)  
3. **Customs Service Providers** (e.g., brokers, freight forwarders)

> *Note:* This applies generally to all exports unless an exception applies.

---

## **III. When and How Must Reports Be Filed?**

### **General Rule (Section 3, Subsection 1)**  
All exported goods must be reported in writing **at an Export Reporting Office (ERO)**, with specific deadlines based on the mode of transport:

| Mode of Export | Deadline |
|---------------|---------|
| **By mail** | Not less than **2 hours before** delivery to the post office |
| **By vessel** | Not less than **48 hours before** loading onto the vessel |
| **By aircraft** | Not less than **2 hours before** loading |
| **By rail** | Not less than **2 hours before** the railcar is assembled into a train |
| **By any other means** | **Immediately before** exportation |

### **Exceptions to General Rule (Live Animals, Bulk, Homogeneous, Time-Sensitive Goods)**  
Unless restricted, the following goods may be reported **immediately before exportation**:

- Live animals  
- Bulk goods  
- Homogeneous goods  
- Time-sensitive goods  

> *Note:* These exceptions apply only if the goods are not restricted.

---

## **IV. Special Reporting Requirements for Bulk or Homogeneous Goods**

An exporter may report **bulk or homogeneous goods monthly** (instead of per shipment), **only if**:

1. The exporter has received **written confirmation** from the **Chief Officer of Customs** that:
   - The goods qualify as bulk or homogeneous goods under the regulations.
2. If the goods are **restricted**, the exporter must also receive **written confirmation** from the appropriate official responsible for the relevant federal Act (e.g., Agriculture, Environment) that export reporting is permitted under that Act.

> *Reporting frequency:* Monthly reports must be submitted to the **Chief Statistician of Canada** within **five business days after the end of the month** in which the goods were exported.

---

## **V. Reporting for Restricted Goods**

If goods are **restricted** (e.g., controlled chemicals, weapons, endangered species):

- The exporter must provide **evidence** that the export complies with the relevant federal Act.
- This evidence, along with a copy of the export report, must be:
  - Provided to the **Chief Officer of Customs** within the time frame specified in the regulation.
  - Submitted to the **place specified in the export permit**; if no place is specified, then to the **nearest ERO** to the point of exit from Canada.
  - The goods must be made available for inspection at that location.

---

## **VI. Exemptions from Reporting (Section 6)**

Certain types of goods **do not require reporting** if they are **not restricted**:

| Exempt Goods | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Personal/household effects** | Not for resale or commercial use (excluding emigrants) |
| **Commercial goods under $2,000 CAD** | Value threshold applies |
| **Conveyances** | Classified under tariff items 9801.10.00, 9801.20.00, or 9801.30.00 (e.g., vehicles used for transport) |
| **Reusable containers** | Skids, drums, pallets, straps used in international transport |
| **Diplomatic personnel goods** | For personal or official use by embassies or missions |
| **Personal gifts/donations** | Excludes conveyances |
| **Transit goods** | Imported into Canada and exported en route to a non-Canadian destination |
| **Transhipment goods** | Manufactured in Canada, exported for transshipment to another Canadian destination |
| **Goods for repair/warranty** | Will be returned to Canada |
| **Shipsâ€™ stores** | For use by Canadian carriers |
| **Goods from bonded/sufferance warehouses** | Removed for export from bonded facilities |
| **Goods returned within 12 months** | Not for further processing |

> *Note:* These exemptions apply only when the goods are **not restricted**.

---

## **VII. Exemptions Based on Bilateral Agreements (Section 7)**

Goods exported under a **written government-to-government arrangement** may be exempt from reporting **if**:

- The destination country collects export data as part of its import statistics.
- That data is **shared with Canada**.

> *Purpose:* To support international trade data sharing and avoid duplication.

---

## **VIII. Reporting When Suspected Violation of Law (Section 8)**

If, at the time of export, a customs officer **suspects on reasonable grounds** that goods are being exported in violation of a federal Act (e.g., export controls, environmental laws), the exporter, carrier, or customs service provider **must report the goods immediately**.

> *Applies to:* Exporters, carriers, and customs service providers.

---

## **IX. Carrier Reporting Requirements (Section 9)**

### **1. General Carrier Reporting (for goods not the conveyance itself)**  
All goods exported by a carrier (other than the conveyance) must be reported **before exportation** at the **nearest ERO** to the loading location.

### **2. Special Reporting for Conveyances**  
For goods transported via a **conveyance** (vessel, railcar, aircraft), the **carrier must report**:

| Mode | Reporting Location |
|------|--------------------|
| **Vessel** | ERO closest to loading place |
| **Railcar** | ERO closest to assembly into train |
| **Aircraft** | ERO closest to place of exit |

> *Exceptions:*  
> - Regularly scheduled aircraft  
> - Highway conveyances (unless export is suspected to violate a law)

### **3. Post-Export Reporting (if permitted)**  
For certain goods exported via non-highway conveyance (e.g., vessel, aircraft), the carrier may report **after exportation** if:

1. The carrier gives a **written undertaking** to an officer that it is exporting only the specified goods.
2. The officer **authorizes** the reporting in writing.

> *Reporting deadlines after export:*
> - **Vessel**: Within **3 business days** after departure
> - **Rail**: Within **1 business day** after train assembly
> - **Aircraft**: Within **1 business day** after departure

> *Additional requirement:* For vessels, carriers must also report to the **Chief Statistician of Canada** within **5 business days after the end of the month** of departure.

---

## **X. Carrier Reporting for Transit Goods (Section 10)**

Goods that were **imported into Canada** and are **exported en route** to a non-Canadian destination must be reported by the **carrier**:

| Mode | Reporting Location |
|------|--------------------|
| **Mail** | ERO closest to post office |
| **Vessel** | ERO closest to loading place |
| **Aircraft** | ERO closest to departure point |
| **Rail** | ERO closest to train assembly |
| **Other** | ERO nearest to point of exit |

> *Exception:* Highway conveyances are **not required** to report unless there is a reasonable suspicion of violation.
