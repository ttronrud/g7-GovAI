**Detailed Summary of the *Centennial Flame Research Award Act***

---

### **Overview of the Act**

The *Centennial Flame Research Award Act* is a Canadian legislative measure enacted by Her Majesty, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons, to establish a fund and a research award aimed at supporting disabled Canadians in conducting research on the contributions of disabled individuals to Canadian public life and to the activities of Parliament.

The Act is formally known as the **Centennial Flame Research Award Act**, and it establishes a dedicated financial mechanism to promote visibility, recognition, and scholarly inquiry into the roles and achievements of disabled persons in Canadian society.

---

### **Key Definitions**

1. **Award**  
   Refers to the monetary grant established under Section 3. It is intended to support research and report writing by disabled individuals.

2. **Committee**  
   Composed of relevant committees from both the Senate and the House of Commons responsible for matters concerning the status of disabled persons. This Committee serves as the administrative body overseeing the award process, including reviewing applications and selecting recipients.

3. **Disabled Persons**  
   Defined broadly to include individuals who:
   - Consider themselves disadvantaged due to any persistent physical, mental, psychiatric, learning, or sensory impairment; or  
   - Believe that a potential employer would likely perceive them as disadvantaged.  
   This definition emphasizes both lived experience and societal perception, ensuring inclusivity and recognition of diverse forms of disability.

4. **Fiscal Year**  
   Refers to the 12-month period beginning on **April 1** and ending on **March 31** of the following year. This is the financial cycle used for fund collection and reporting.

---

### **Establishment of the Fund**

- A new fund, the **Centennial Flame Research Award Fund**, is created.
- The fund receives two primary sources of revenue:
  1. **All money collected from the Centennial Flame monument** located on Parliament Hill in Ottawa.
  2. **Any other donations** made directly to the fund.

> The Centennial Flame, a symbolic monument on Parliament Hill, is historically associated with Canada’s national identity and commemoration. The income generated from it is redirected here to support research on disability in Canadian society.

---

### **Purpose of the Fund and Award**

The primary objective of the fund is to:
- Provide **monetary awards** to **disabled Canadian citizens**.
- Enable them to **conduct research and prepare reports** on the contributions of one or more disabled individuals to:
  - The **public life of Canada** (e.g., politics, culture, education, social movements), or  
  - The **activities of Parliament** (e.g., legislative processes, representation, accessibility initiatives).

This promotes greater awareness, inclusion, and historical documentation of disabled people's roles in shaping Canadian institutions and society.

---

### **Eligibility and Application Process**

- **Eligibility**: Any **Canadian citizen** who identifies as a **disabled person** is eligible to apply.
- **Application**: Applicants must submit a formal application to the **Committee**.
- The award is granted **annually**, meaning a new recipient is selected each year.

---

### **Selection and Approval Process**

1. The **Committee** receives all applications.
2. It evaluates each application based on:
   - Whether the applicant qualifies under the definition of "disabled person" (Section 2);
   - The relevance, quality, and potential impact of the proposed research;
   - Other criteria deemed appropriate by the Committee.
3. The Committee selects **one recipient** per year.
4. The final decision must be **approved by the Speakers of the Senate and the House of Commons**.

> This dual approval ensures accountability and cross-party legitimacy in the selection process.

---

### **Supervision of Fund Collection**

- The **Board of Internal Economy of the House of Commons** is responsible for:
  - Supervising the collection of funds (especially from the Centennial Flame monument);
  - Ensuring compliance with the provisions of this Act;
  - Making regulations as necessary to carry out the Act’s purposes.

> This oversight ensures transparency and financial integrity in the fund’s operations.

---

### **Renewability of the Award**

- The award may be **renewed annually** if:
  - The **Speaker of the Senate** and the **Speaker of the House of Commons** approve a recommendation from the Committee.
- Renewal allows for continued research and sustained contribution to public knowledge about disability in Canada.

> This provision supports long-term, in-depth studies rather than one-off projects.

---

### **Reporting and Accountability**

1. **Annual Report Requirement**:
   - Within a timely period after each fiscal year ends, the **Chairman of the Committee** must prepare and submit an **annual report** to both Houses of Parliament.

2. **Content of the Annual Report**:
   - Must include the **report prepared by the award recipient** as required under Section 5(3).

3. **Recipient Report**:
   - The award recipient must produce a **detailed report** on their research findings.
   - The report may be presented in **any medium or format** deemed appropriate by the recipient (e.g., written paper, digital document, video, presentation).

4. **Public Engagement**:
   - The recipient report is laid before the Committee.
   - The Committee has the authority to **call the recipient as a witness** during hearings or debates.

> This ensures transparency, public access to findings, and opportunities for dialogue on disability-related issues.

---

### **Significance and Implications**

- **Promotes Inclusion**: By funding research by disabled individuals on the contributions of disabled people, the Act affirms the value and agency of disabled persons in public life.
- **Historical and Social Awareness**: Encourages documentation of underrepresented contributions, helping to correct historical omissions and build a more inclusive national narrative.
- **Policy and Legislative Influence**: Findings from the reports may inform future policy, accessibility reforms, and legislative changes related to disability rights.
- **Symbolic Value**: The use of the Centennial Flame monument ties the initiative to Canada’s national heritage and centennial legacy, reinforcing the importance of inclusivity in national institutions.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Centennial Flame Research Award Act* is a forward-thinking piece of legislation that combines symbolic heritage with practical action. It establishes a sustainable, accountable, and inclusive mechanism to empower disabled Canadians to research and share their experiences and achievements. By funding research on the contributions of disabled individuals to Canadian public life and Parliament, the Act not only supports individual growth but also advances societal understanding, equity, and representation.

It stands as a meaningful step toward realizing the principles of inclusion, accessibility, and recognition in Canadian democracy.
