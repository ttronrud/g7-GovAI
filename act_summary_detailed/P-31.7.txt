**Detailed Summary of the *Public Sector Pension Investment Board Act***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *Public Sector Pension Investment Board Act* establishes the **Public Sector Pension Investment Board (PSPIB)** as a standalone, independent body responsible for managing and investing pension funds for key federal public service groups in Canada. The Act is designed to ensure that these pension funds are managed in the best interests of contributors and beneficiaries, with a focus on achieving sustainable returns while minimizing financial risk.

This Act provides a comprehensive legal framework governing the structure, governance, operations, independence, and accountability of the Board. It emphasizes **independence from federal public administration**, **transparency**, **financial prudence**, and **protection of beneficiaries’ interests**.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Name and Citation**  
- The Act is titled the **Public Sector Pension Investment Board Act** and may be cited accordingly.

---

### **2. Definitions**  
Key terms are defined to clarify scope and application:  

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Board** | The Public Sector Pension Investment Board established under Section 3. |
| **By-law** | A rule or regulation made by the Board (only applies in French). |
| **Entity** | Includes bodies corporate, trusts, partnerships, unincorporated associations, Her Majesty in right of Canada or a province, or any foreign government or its political subdivisions. |
| **Fund** | Refers to specific pension funds under three major Acts: <br> - Canadian Forces Pension Fund / Superannuation Investment Fund (Canadian Forces Superannuation Act) <br> - Public Service Pension Fund / Superannuation Investment Fund (Public Service Superannuation Act) <br> - Royal Canadian Mounted Police Pension Fund / Superannuation Investment Fund (RCMP Superannuation Act) <br> *Note: If regulations are made under Section 59.1 of the Canadian Forces Act, a new fund may be created.* |
| **Minister** | The President of the Treasury Board (i.e., the Minister responsible for public finances). |
| **Prescribed** | As defined by regulation. |
| **Subsidiary** | A corporation wholly owned directly or indirectly by the Board through a chain of subsidiaries. |

---

### **3. Establishment of the Board**  
- A **body corporate** named the **Public Sector Pension Investment Board** is created.  
- The Board is **not an agent of Her Majesty** and is **independent of federal public administration**.  
- Its **directors, officers, employees, and agents are not part of the federal public service**.  
- The **head office** is located in the **National Capital Region**, as defined in the Schedule to the *National Capital Act*.  
- The **Canada Not-for-Profit Corporations Act does not apply** to the Board.  
- **Part X of the Financial Administration Act (FAA)** does **not apply**, except for the following sections:  
  - Sections 89.8 to 89.92, 113.1, 132 to 147, and 154.01.  
- **Exception**: Any reference to **Section 131 of the FAA** is read as a reference to **Section 35 of this Act**.  

> This ensures the Board operates with **greater autonomy** from standard financial administration rules.

---

### **4. Reporting and Oversight**  
- Reports or information provided to the **Minister (Treasury Board)** under Sections 132–147 of the FAA must also be shared with:  
  - The **Minister of National Defence** (for Canadian Forces funds)  
  - The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** (for RCMP funds)  
- The Minister may only require a **special examination** under subsection 138(2) of the FAA **after consulting** with those two Ministers.

---

### **5. Capital and Share Structure**  
- The **capital of the Board is $100**, divided into **10 shares of $10 each**.  
- The shares are **issued to the Minister** and held **on behalf of Her Majesty in right of Canada**.  
- The shares are **registered in the name of the Minister** by the Board.  

> This structure ensures the Board is fully funded and legally established without direct ownership by the government, while maintaining accountability through the Minister.

---

### **6. Objects and Mandate**  
The Board’s core purposes are:  

1. **To manage pension amounts** transferred to it under specific sections of the three pension Acts (Canadian Forces, Public Service, RCMP), **in the best interests of contributors and beneficiaries**.  
2. **To invest assets** with the goal of achieving a **maximum rate of return**, while **avoiding undue risk of loss**, taking into account:  
   - The **funding status** and **financial obligations** of the pension plans.  
   - The **ability of those plans to meet future obligations**.  

> This dual mandate balances **investment performance** with **long-term financial stability** of pension plans.

---

### **7. Funding of Operational Costs**  
- All **operational costs** of the Board are **paid from the funds** (i.e., the pension funds themselves).  
- The **Minister determines the source** of funding, but:  
  - **No withdrawals** can be made from:  
    - The **Canadian Forces Pension Fund** or **Superannuation Investment Fund** (or any fund under Section 59.1 of the Canadian Forces Act) **without consulting the Minister of National Defence**.  
    - The **RCMP Pension Fund** or **Superannuation Investment Fund** **without consulting the Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness**.  

> This ensures **protection of key pension funds** from operational drain and maintains **inter-ministerial accountability**.

---

### **8. Legal Capacity and Powers**  
- The Board has the **capacity and rights of a natural person**, subject to this Act.  
- It **cannot engage in activities inconsistent** with its objects or prohibited by this Act.  
- No act of the Board is invalid **solely because it lacked capacity or power** at the time.  

> This strengthens the Board’s legal authority and operational flexibility.

---

### **9. Investment and Operational Requirements**  
The Board must:  
- Establish **annual written investment policies, standards, and procedures** for each fund it manages.  
- **Monitor officers and employees** to ensure compliance with these policies.  
- Prepare **quarterly and annual financial statements** in accordance with this Act.  
- Establish procedures for identifying and resolving **conflicts of interest**.  
- Adopt a **code of conduct** for all officers and employees.  
- Designate a **committee** to monitor conflict-of-interest procedures and the code of conduct.  

> These requirements promote **transparency, accountability, and ethical governance**.

---

### **10. Delegation of Powers**  
- The Board may delegate powers to:  
  - The **Chairperson**  
  - A **committee**  
  - An **officer**  
- **However, the Board may NOT delegate** the following powers:  
  - Adoption, amendment, or repeal of **by-laws**  
  - Establishment of **investment policies, standards, or procedures**  
  - Filling a vacancy in a **directors’ committee**  
  - Appointment of **officers** or fixing their **remuneration**  
  - Approval of **annual or other financial statements**  

> This preserves key governance and oversight functions with the Board itself.

---

### **11. Board Composition and Appointment**  
- The Board consists of **13 directors**, including a **Chairperson**.  
- **Disqualifications for directors** include:  
  - Being under 18  
  - Being of unsound mind (as found by court)  
  - Being a bankrupt  
  - Not being a natural person  
  - Being an employee, agent, or officer of the Board  
  - Being a member of Parliament, provincial legislature, or Senate  
  - Receiving a pension or superannuation benefit under any of the three pension Acts  
  - Being a member of an advisory committee under the relevant Acts  
  - Being an employee or agent of a foreign government  
  - Not being a resident of Canada  

> This ensures **independence, integrity, and financial neutrality**.

---

### **12. Nominating Committee**  
- The **Minister establishes a nominating committee of 8 members**, appointed as follows:  
  1. **Independent Chairperson** (appointed by Minister after consulting Defence and Public Safety Ministers) who:  
     - Is not entitled to any pension under the three pension Acts  
     - Is not entitled to a benefit payable from the Consolidated Revenue Fund  
     - Is not subject to a pension plan that could entitle them to a benefit  
  2. **Two members** (one from public service, recommended by Public Service advisory committee)  
  3. **One member** receiving a pension under any of the three pension Acts  
  4. **Two members** appointed by Minister of National Defence (from advisory committee)  
  5. **Two members** appointed by Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness (from advisory committee)  

- The committee holds office for **five years**, with **reappointment possible**.  
- The Minister may **remove any member at any time**.  

> This ensures **diversity, balance, and representation** of key stakeholders.
