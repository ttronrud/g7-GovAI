**Detailed Summary of the *National Security and Intelligence Review Agency Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *National Security and Intelligence Review Agency Act* establishes the **National Security and Intelligence Review Agency (NSIRA)**, a federal oversight body designed to ensure accountability, transparency, and compliance with the law in the operations of key national security and intelligence entities. The Act grants the NSIRA broad powers to review activities, investigate complaints, and assess the legality and necessity of actions taken by intelligence and security agencies, while balancing the need for operational confidentiality and national security.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Definitions**

The Act provides precise definitions of key terms used throughout:

- **Appropriate Minister**:  
  Refers to the relevant federal minister responsible for a given department, division, or corporation. For example:
  - For departments listed in *Schedule I* of the *Financial Administration Act*, it is the Minister presiding over that department.
  - For divisions or branches listed in *Schedule I.1*, it is the minister named in Column II.
  - For corporations in *Schedule II*, it is the minister designated by the Governor in Council.
  - For parent Crown corporations, it follows the definition in subsection 83(1) of the *Financial Administration Act*.
  - For the Canadian Forces, it is the **Minister of National Defence**.

- **Department**:  
  Includes departments, divisions, branches, corporations, parent Crown corporations, and the Canadian Forces — **excluding**:
  - Review bodies (e.g., Civilian Review and Complaints Commission for the RCMP)
  - The office of the Intelligence Commissioner

- **Deputy Head**:  
  The senior official responsible for a given entity:
  - For departments: Deputy Minister
  - For Canadian Forces: Chief of the Defence Staff
  - For RCMP: Commissioner
  - For CSIS: Director
  - For other entities: Designated by the Governor in Council
  - For inquiries under the *Inquiries Act*: Single commissioner or designated commissioner

- **Director**:  
  Specifically refers to the **Director of the Canadian Security Intelligence Service (CSIS)**.

- **Review Agency**:  
  The NSIRA itself — established under Section 3 — consisting of a **Chair and between 3 and 6 other members**.

- **Review Body**:  
  Two specific bodies:
  1. The **Civilian Review and Complaints Commission (CRCC)** for the RCMP (established under s. 45.29(1) of the RCMP Act)
  2. The **National Security and Intelligence Committee of Parliamentarians (NSICP)**

---

### **2. Establishment and Composition of the NSIRA**

- The NSIRA is established as a **statutory body** with a **Chair and 3 to 6 members**.
- Members are appointed by the **Governor in Council** on the **recommendation of the Prime Minister**.
- Appointment process includes **mandatory consultation** with:
  - The Leader of the Government in the Senate and Government Representative in the Senate
  - The Leader of the Opposition in the Senate
  - The Leader or Facilitator of every recognized party or parliamentary group in the Senate
  - The Leader of the Opposition in the House of Commons
  - The leader in the House of Commons of each party with **at least 12 members**

> This ensures **broad political representation** and promotes **accountability and transparency** in appointments.

- **Term of Office**:  
  - Initial term: **No more than 5 years**
  - Reappointment: Eligible for **one further term** after expiry of first term.

- **Chair and Vice-Chair**:
  - Designated by the Governor in Council on the Prime Minister’s recommendation.
  - Can serve on a **full-time or part-time basis**.
  - If not designated as Chair or Vice-Chair, all other members serve **part-time**.

- **Acting Chair**:
  - If the Chair is absent or incapacitated, the **Vice-Chair acts as Chair**.
  - If the Vice-Chair is also absent or vacant, the Chair may designate another member to act, or the Agency may designate one if no designation is made.
  - **No member may act as Chair for more than 90 days without Governor in Council approval** — a safeguard against prolonged interim leadership.

- **Remuneration and Expenses**:
  - Part-time members receive:
    - Fixed daily remuneration
    - Reasonable travel and living expenses (as per Treasury Board directives)
  - Full-time Chair and Vice-Chair receive:
    - Same remuneration and expenses
  - All members are deemed **employees** for:
    - *Government Employees Compensation Act*
    - *Aeronautics Act* (for employment in federal public administration)
    - *Public Service Superannuation Act* (for full-time members)

- **Internal Procedures**:
  - The Agency may **determine its own procedures** for carrying out reviews or fulfilling duties — providing flexibility in operational design.

---

### **3. Mandate of the NSIRA**

The NSIRA has four core mandates:

#### **(a) Review of CSIS and CSEC Activities**
- Must review **any activity** carried out by:
  - The **Canadian Security Intelligence Service (CSIS)**
  - The **Communications Security Establishment (CSEC)**

#### **(b) Review of Departmental National Security and Intelligence Activities**
- Must review any activity by a **federal department** that relates to **national security or intelligence**.

#### **(c) Review of Matters Referred by Ministers**
- Can review any matter related to national security or intelligence that a **Crown minister refers** to the Agency.

#### **(d) Investigation of Specific Complaints**
The Agency must investigate:
- Complaints under:
  - **Section 16(1)**, **17(1)**, or **18(3)** of the CSIS Act (e.g., about conduct, decisions, or security clearances)
- Complaints referred under:
  - **Section 45.53(4.1)** or **45.67(2.1)** of the **Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act**
- Reports made under **Section 19 of the Citizenship Act**
- Matters referred under **Section 45 of the Canadian Human Rights Act**

> These complaints cover issues such as misuse of power, improper conduct, or breaches of human rights.

---

### **4. Annual Review Requirements**

- **CSIS Performance Review**:
  - Each calendar year, the NSIRA must review **at least one aspect** of CSIS’s efforts to **reduce threats to Canada’s security**.

- **Review of New or Modified Ministerial Directions**:
  - Must review the **implementation** of significant new or revised ministerial directions issued to:
    - CSIS
    - CSEC
    - Any other department if the direction relates to **national security or intelligence**

- **Review of Avoiding Complicity in Mistreatment by Foreign Entities Act**:
  - Must annually review implementation of all directions issued under this Act — aimed at preventing foreign influence and complicity in mistreatment.

---

### **5. Access to Information**

Despite other Acts of Parliament, the NSIRA has **broad access to information** to fulfill its mandate — a critical feature for effective oversight.

#### **General Access**:
- May access **any information** in the possession or control of a department **in a timely manner**, **subject to section 12**.

#### **Privilege Exceptions**:
- **No access** to information protected by:
  - Law of evidence
  - Solicitor-client privilege
  - Professional secrecy of advocates and notaries
  - Litigation privilege  
> **Important**: Disclosure of such information **does not waive** these privileges.

#### **Specific Access Rights**:
- For complaints under:
  - **Section 16(1)**, **17(1)**, or **18(3)**: Access to information relating to the complaint held by CSIS or CSEC
  - **Section 18(3)**: Access to information held by the deputy head, CSIS, or CSEC
  - **RCMP complaints (s. 45.53(4.1) or 45.67(2.1))**: Access to information held by CRCC, RCMP, CSIS, or CSEC

#### **Additional Access**:
- Under **Sections 9 and 10**, the Agency may:
  - Request documents and explanations from the deputy head or employees of the relevant department
  - Decide **on its own** whether information relates to the complaint or review

> **Conflict Resolution**: If there is a conflict between this Act and another law, **Sections 9 or 10 prevail**.

#### **Confidentiality Limitation**:
- **Cannot access** information that constitutes a **confidence of the Queen’s Privy Council** — disclosure of which would be refused under **section 39 of the Canada Evidence Act**.
