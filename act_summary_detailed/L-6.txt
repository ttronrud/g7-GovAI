**Detailed Summary of the *Canada Lands Surveys Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *Canada Lands Surveys Act* is a federal statute that establishes the legal framework for conducting, managing, and verifying land surveys of **Canada Lands**â€”that is, lands belonging to Her Majesty in right of Canada or under the Government of Canadaâ€™s power to dispose of themâ€”located in **Yukon, the Northwest Territories, and Nunavut**. It governs the authority, procedures, and responsibilities related to surveying these lands, including the use of monuments, measurement standards, record-keeping, and dispute resolution mechanisms.

The Act was significantly amended and partially repealed in 1998 (e.g., sections on "Board," "candidate," "examination," "Secretary," "prescribed," and "commission" were repealed), reflecting modern administrative restructuring. The current version focuses on **survey integrity, accountability, and consistency**, with strong emphasis on **professional standards, oversight, and public accountability**.

## **Core Provisions and Functional Structure**

### **1. Administration and Oversight**

- **Minister of Natural Resources (or Northern Affairs)** has **overall administrative, directional, and controlling authority** over all surveys under this Act.
- The **Surveyor General** manages day-to-day survey operations and holds **custody of all original survey records**, including:
  - Field notes
  - Journals
  - Plans
  - Maps
- The Minister may authorize **an employee of the Department of Natural Resources** (who is a qualified surveyor) to perform duties of the Surveyor General.
- The **Governor in Council** may issue **orders and regulations** as needed to implement the Act or address gaps.

> âœ… *This ensures centralized control and accountability while allowing flexibility in implementation.*

### **2. Surveyor Accountability and Verification**

- Every surveyor must **verify and affirm by oath or otherwise** (to the satisfaction of the Surveyor General) that their survey was conducted **faithfully and correctly** in accordance with:
  - This Act
  - Instructions issued by the Surveyor General
- If a **court finds a survey was improperly executed**, the **Attorney General of Canada** may initiate legal proceedings to recover **costs from the surveyor** who provided the false verification.
- Surveyors must **maintain exact, regular field notes** and **file them with the Surveyor General in chronological order**.

### **3. Measurement Standards**

  - **Alternative methods of measurement** authorized by the Surveyor General based on specific circumstances.

- The **measure of length** used is the **Canadian measure of length**, as defined by the *Weights and Measures Act*.

- The plan must show:

### **10. Special Surveys (for Territorial Lands)**

Special surveys may be conducted for:
1. Correcting errors in existing surveys
2. Subdividing land not previously subdivided
3. Fixing road or highway locations
4. Establishing boundary lines where monuments are lost or unclear
5. Any other purpose deemed necessary by the Minister or Commissioner
