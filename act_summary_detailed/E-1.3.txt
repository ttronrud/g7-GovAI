**Detailed Summary of the *Economic Development Agency of Canada for the Regions of Quebec Act***  

---

### **Overview**

The *Economic Development Agency of Canada for the Regions of Quebec Act* (commonly referred to as the **EDACRQ Act**) establishes a dedicated federal agency to promote economic development and diversification in the **regions of Quebec**, particularly those experiencing **slow economic growth, low income levels, or inadequate employment opportunities**. The Act provides a legal framework for the creation, operation, and governance of the **Economic Development Agency of Canada for the Regions of Quebec (EDACRQ)**, outlining its mandate, structure, powers, funding mechanisms, accountability measures, and relationship with provincial and private stakeholders.

This Act was enacted by **Her Majesty, on the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons of Canada**, and came into force in stages — with most provisions effective **October 5, 2005**, and **Section 27** (on employee status and transition) effective **June 23, 2005**.

---

## **Key Components and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Name and Citation**
- The Act is formally titled:  
  **"The Economic Development Agency of Canada for the Regions of Quebec Act"**  
- It may be cited as the **EDACRQ Act**.

---

### **2. Definitions**
The Act introduces several key definitions used throughout:
- **Agency**: Refers to the newly established *Economic Development Agency of Canada for the Regions of Quebec* (established under Section 8).
- **Enterprise**: Includes both traditional businesses and **social economy enterprises** (e.g., cooperatives, not-for-profit enterprises focused on social goals).
- **Minister**: The head of the Agency, appointed by commission under the Great Seal, serving at the pleasure of the Governor in Council.
- **President of the Agency**: The chief executive officer (CEO), appointed by the Governor in Council as Deputy to the Minister.

---

### **3. Purpose of the Act**
> *"To promote the development and diversification of the economy of the regions of Quebec."*

The central objective is to address regional disparities in economic performance by:
- Supporting **small- and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs)**,
- Encouraging **entrepreneurial talent**,
- Promoting **sustainable employment and income creation**,
- Improving **economic conditions** in underdeveloped or disadvantaged areas.

---

### **4. Appointment of the Minister**
- The **Minister** is appointed by commission under the Great Seal.
- Holds office **at the pleasure of the Governor in Council** (i.e., can be removed or reappointed at any time).
- Has **broad powers and duties** over all matters within federal jurisdiction not assigned to another department, board, or agency — specifically related to the **economic development of Quebec’s regions**.

---

### **5. Powers, Duties, and Functions of the Minister**
The Minister is empowered to:
1. **Guide, promote, and coordinate** federal policies and programs related to regional economic development in Quebec.
2. **Lead and coordinate** federal cooperation with:
   - The Government of Quebec,
   - Business sectors,
   - Labour organizations,
   - Public and private bodies in Quebec.
3. **Establish advisory and other committees**, with defined membership, duties, and operations.
4. **Enter into agreements** with:
   - The Government of Quebec,
   - Any federal, provincial, or private entity,
   - Including sector-specific cooperation agreements.

> *Note:* These agreements may cover distinct economic sectors (e.g., agriculture, technology, tourism).

---

### **6. Committee Members and Remuneration**
- Members of committees are:
  - Paid **remuneration fixed by the Governor in Council**.
  - Entitled to **reimbursement** for:
    - Travel,
    - Living expenses,
    - Other costs incurred while attending committee meetings.
- Reimbursement follows **Treasury Board directives**.
- Full-time members must be absent from their ordinary place of work; part-time members from their ordinary residence.

---

### **7. Structure and Leadership of the Agency**

#### **Minister and President**
- The **Minister** presides over the Agency.
- The **President of the Agency** is appointed by the Governor in Council as the **deputy to the Minister**.
- The President is the **chief executive officer (CEO)** of the Agency and has:
  - Control and supervision over all Agency officers and employees.
  - Authority to manage programs, projects, and services.

#### **Vacancy or Absence of the President**
- If the President is absent or incapacitated, the Minister may appoint an acting President.
- **No acting President may serve beyond 90 days** without **Governor in Council approval**.

#### **Remuneration**
- The President is paid the remuneration set by the Governor in Council.

---

### **8. Object of the Agency**
> *"To promote the long-term economic development of the regions of Quebec, with special attention to areas where slow economic growth or inadequate productive employment opportunities exist."*

The Agency’s mission includes:
- Fostering **cooperation and complementarity** with Quebec and local communities.
- Supporting **enterprise development**, especially SMEs.
- Enhancing **entrepreneurial capacity**.
- Improving the **overall economic environment** in Quebec.

---

### **9. Key Activities and Functions of the Agency**

The Agency may carry out the following actions to fulfill its mandate:

| Function | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Policy Development** | Formulate and implement federal policies, plans, and integrated approaches in cooperation with other federal ministers and agencies. |
| **Cooperative Planning** | Develop mechanisms to facilitate coordination with Quebec and local communities. |
| **Enterprise Support** | Plan, implement, and manage programs to: <br> - Establish, develop, support, and promote enterprises (especially SMEs); <br> - Develop entrepreneurial talent; <br> - Promote economic prosperity; <br> - Strengthen communities. |
| **Economic Environment Improvement** | Offer services such as: <br> - Support to business associations, trade shows, market research; <br> - Development of economic opportunity data banks and networks; <br> - Improved business communication and cooperation; <br> - Promotion of business and investment knowledge. |
| **Information Gathering & Dissemination** | Collect, analyze, coordinate, and disseminate data on regional economic development. |
| **Investor Assistance** | Assist investors (especially SMEs) in establishing enterprises in Quebec, **in compliance with federal investment requirements** and **approved by the Treasury Board**. |
| **Financing Tools** | Make **loans** to individuals or enterprises for enterprise development (especially SMEs). |
| **Guarantees and Insurance** | Provide **loan guarantees** or **credit insurance** for financial obligations related to enterprise development. |
| **Grants and Contributions** | Make grants and contributions to support Agency or Minister-led programs and projects. |
| **Agreements and Arrangements** | Enter into contracts, memoranda of understanding, or other arrangements — including cooperation agreements — in the name of Her Majesty or the Agency. |
| **Other Functions** | Perform any other functions assigned by order of the Governor in Council. |

> *Note:* The Agency may also perform additional functions as specified by the Governor in Council.

---

### **10. Support to the Minister**
The Agency assists the Minister in:
- Exercising powers and performing duties under this Act and other Acts.
- Guiding federal policies and coordinating cooperation with Quebec and private stakeholders.
- Administering agreements under Section 5(2).
- Exercising powers granted under Section 7.
- Compiling **detailed performance data** on programs and services to monitor trends and progress in regional economic development.

---

### **11. Staffing and Operations**
- Officers and employees are appointed in accordance with the **Public Service Employment Act**.
- The **head office** of the Agency is located in **the Province of Quebec** (ensuring proximity to target regions).

---

### **12. Legal Standing and Jurisdiction**
- Any contract, memorandum, or arrangement entered into by the Agency **in its own name** is **binding on Her Majesty in right of Canada** to the same extent as it binds the Agency.
- Legal actions (suits, claims) related to rights or obligations of the Agency may be brought **in the name of the Agency** in any court that would have jurisdiction if the Agency were a corporation.
- The Agency is **not considered an agent of Her Majesty** in legal proceedings.

---

### **13. Regulatory Authority**
The Minister may make regulations under the Act to:
- Specify **additional programs, projects, and services** to improve the economic environment in Quebec.
- Define key terms such as:
  - "Community"
  - "Small- and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs)"
  - "Project"
  - "Demonstration project"
- Specify **classes of SMEs and activities** eligible for Agency support.
- Generally implement the purposes and provisions of the Act.

> *With Minister of Finance’s approval*, the Minister may also regulate:
- Conditions for **loans, guarantees, and loan insurance**.
- Procedures for **acquiring, exercising, assigning, or selling stock options or warrants** received as conditions of loans, contributions, or guarantees.

> **Important Note:**  
> Loan insurance or credit insurance under this Act is considered a **guarantee** under the **Financial Administration Act**.

---
