### **1. Purpose and Context of the Agreement**

This agreement is a formal **ratification and confirmation** of a prior understanding between the Government of Canada and the Government of Nova Scotia concerning the status, ownership, and future management of **Indian reserve lands** in Nova Scotia.

#### Background:
- Since 1867 (under the British North America Act), certain lands originally set aside for Indigenous peoples (specifically, "Indian reserves") have been **surrendered to the Crown** by Indigenous bands.
- Over time, the Crown issued **Letters Patent** (legal instruments under the Great Seal of Canada) conveying these surrendered lands to private individuals.
- However, two decisions by the **Judicial Committee of the Privy Council** (the highest court of appeal for Canada at the time) concluded that such conveyances were **invalid** because they were made without the **explicit authority of the Province of Nova Scotia**.
- As a result, the grantees of these lands hold **defective titles**, which creates legal uncertainty, hardship, and inconvenience for both Indigenous communities and the Crown.

#### Objective:
To **settle outstanding legal and administrative issues** related to Indian reserves in Nova Scotia and to establish a clear, legally sound framework for Canadaâ€™s future dealings with reserve lands, including:
- Clarifying ownership rights,
- Resolving title defects,
- Establishing procedures for land surrenders and sales,
