### 5. **Board Membership and Appointment**

#### General Appointment Process:
- Members (except chairperson) are appointed by the **federal Minister of Northern Affairs**.
- Appointments follow procedures outlined in **Parts 2 to 5** of the Act (which include criteria for nomination, consultation, and qualifications).

#### Alternate Members:
- For most boards (except Wekeezhii):
  - Alternate members can be selected:
    - By a **first nation** (nominated by a first nation)
    - Or through **consultation with first nations**
  - These act in case of absence or incapacity.
- For other members, **territorial Minister** may agree to alternate appointments.

#### Chairperson Appointment:
- **General Rule**: Chairperson is appointed by the federal Minister from a nominee of a **majority of members**.
- If no majority nominee is acceptable within a reasonable time, the Minister may appoint **any person** as chair.
- **Wekeezhii Land and Water Board**: Chair is appointed **jointly by federal Minister and Tlicho Government**, based on member nominations.

#### Term of Office:
- All members serve a **three-year term**.
- Reappointment is allowed in the same or different capacity.

#### Removal of Members:
- A member may only be removed **after consultation**:
  - With the board
  - With the territorial Minister (where applicable)
  - With the first nation or Tlicho Government that nominated the member
- **Exception**: Members of the Wekeezhii Board appointed by Tlicho Government must be removed only after consultation with the **Board and federal Minister**.

#### Conflict of Interest:
- A member **cannot act** on an application or participate in a decision if it would create a **material conflict of interest**.
- However, **status or entitlements** under land claim agreements (e.g., hunting rights) **do not automatically create a conflict**.

> This ensures transparency and impartiality in decision-making.
