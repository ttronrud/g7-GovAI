**Detailed Summary of the *Railway Belt and Peace River Block Act*, 1930**

---

### **Overview of the Act**

The *Railway Belt and Peace River Block Act*, enacted on **February 20, 1930**, is a foundational piece of Canadian legislation that formalizes the **re-transfer of certain Crown lands** from the **Government of Canada** to the **Province of British Columbia**. This re-transfer is rooted in historical agreements made during the Confederation of Canada in 1871, specifically under the *Terms of Union*, and is driven by both legal and practical considerations â€” including the completion of a vital railway infrastructure and the need for fair, equitable administration of land in British Columbia.

The Act establishes a comprehensive framework for the transfer of lands known collectively as the **Railway Belt and Peace River Block**, while preserving key federal interests, protecting environmental and cultural resources, and ensuring continuity in financial and administrative obligations.

1. **Origins in the Terms of Union (1871)**

2. **The Railway Belt and Peace River Block**  
   - These lands were granted by the Province of British Columbia to Canada as a condition of railway development.
   - The lands became known as the **Railway Belt** (for railway access and right-of-way) and the **Peace River Block** (for broader development and settlement).

3. **Completion of the Railway**

## **Core Provisions of the Act**

### **1. Re-transfer of Lands**
- All **interests of Canada** in the lands originally granted by British Columbia (the Railway Belt and Peace River Block) are **re-transferred to the Province**.
- The transfer takes effect **upon the coming into force of the Act**.
- The transferred lands are to be **subject to the laws of British Columbia** governing Crown land administration.

> âœ… **Key Point**: The transfer is not a simple reversal of a contract; it is framed as a **just and equitable resolution** based on fairness, not strict legal obligation.

### **2. Financial Obligations**
- **Annual Payment of $100,000** continues to be paid by Canada to British Columbia, **on a half-yearly basis** (January 1 and July 1).
- This payment is **independent of the land transfer** and is explicitly preserved as a fulfillment of the original *Terms of Union*.
- **No accounting required** for payments made before the Actâ€™s coming into force.
- Payments made after the Actâ€™s implementation are retained by the Province with **no obligation to account to Canada**.

### **3. Administration of Transferred Lands**
- The Province assumes full administrative control over the transferred lands.
- The Province must:
  - Carry out all existing contracts for **purchase or lease** of land interests.
  - Fulfill all obligations to **railway companies** (e.g., Canadian Pacific Railway) or individuals who have rights based on prior agreements.
  - Administer land grants for **railway right-of-way, stations, workshops, ballast pits, etc.** as per existing statutes.

> âœ… **Key Point**: The Province assumes responsibility for all existing rights and obligations tied to the land, ensuring continuity and stability.

### **4. Transfer of Powers and Authorities**
- Any powers or rights previously reserved to federal officers (e.g., Minister of Interior, Governor in Council) may now be exercised by:
  - The **Lieutenant-Governor in Council** of British Columbia, or
  - Equivalent provincial officers authorized under provincial Crown land laws.

> âœ… **Key Point**: Federal administrative powers are delegated to provincial authorities, enhancing local governance.

### **5. Exclusions and Reservations**

#### **A. Ordinance and Admiralty Lands**
- Lands designated as **ordnance or admiralty lands** (military or naval use) are **not transferred** to British Columbia.
- These lands remain under **federal control** for their original purposes.
- **Exception**: All prior alienations of such lands by the UK government (or its successors) are recognized and confirmed by Canada.
- **Boundary Determination**:
  - Two persons (one appointed by the Governor General in Council, one by the Lieutenant-Governor in Council) determine boundaries.
  - If they disagree, an **umpire** is appointed jointly by the Minister of Justice (Canada) and Attorney-General of BC.

> âœ… **Key Point**: Federal control over strategic and military lands is preserved.

#### **B. Wharf Sites**
- Canada retains **wharves and wharf sites** within the Railway Belt (specified in **Schedule One**).
- The boundaries of these sites are to be **defined by agreement** between Canada and British Columbia.
- **Reversion Clause**: If a wharf is no longer needed, the site **reverts to the Province**.

#### **C. Foreshores and River Beds**
- The **foreshores and beds of harbours** established in the Railway Belt remain **vested in Canada**.
- Canada also retains:
  - The **foreshores and beds of the Fraser River and Pitt River** above New Westminster Harbour and below defined points (e.g., Kanaka Creek and Pitt Lake exit).
- This preserves federal control over navigable waters and river ecosystems.

> âœ… **Key Point**: Federal jurisdiction over waterways and river systems is preserved for environmental and navigational reasons.
