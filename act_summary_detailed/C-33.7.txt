**Detailed Summary of the *College of Patent Agents and Trademark Agents Act***

---

### **1. General Overview**

The *College of Patent Agents and Trademark Agents Act* establishes a self-governing, independent professional body—the **College of Patent Agents and Trademark Agents (the "College")**—to regulate individuals who provide legal services related to patents and trademarks in Canada. The Act is designed to ensure public confidence in the quality and integrity of patent and trademark representation by setting high standards for professional conduct, competence, and accountability.

The College operates independently of the federal government and is not part of the public administration. It is governed by a Board of Directors, with members including licensed patent and trademark agents, and is empowered to regulate the practice of patent and trademark agents through licensing, disciplinary procedures, and oversight mechanisms.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

- **Board**: The governing body of the College, consisting of at least seven directors (including a Chairperson), appointed or elected by the Minister or the College members.
- **College**: The regulatory body established under this Act to oversee and regulate patent agents and trademark agents.
- **Investigator**: An individual designated to conduct investigations into alleged misconduct.
- **Licence**: A formal authorization issued by the Registrar allowing an individual to practice as a patent or trademark agent.
- **Licensee**: Any individual holding a patent agent licence or trademark agent licence (or in training).
- **Minister**: A federal minister designated under the Act to oversee the College’s operations and provide policy direction.
- **Privileged**: Information protected by legal privileges such as solicitor-client privilege, professional secrecy, or specific provisions in the Patent or Trademarks Acts.
- **Registrar**: The official responsible for maintaining the Registers of Patent and Trademark Agents and issuing licences.
- **Patent Agent**: An individual authorized to represent clients in patent applications and prosecution.
- **Trademark Agent**: An individual authorized to represent clients in trademark registration and prosecution.

---

### **3. Legal Status and Jurisdiction**

- The College is a **corporation without share capital**, established under federal law.
- It is **not subject to the Canada Not-for-Profit Corporations Act**, except where otherwise provided by regulation.
- The Act is binding on **Her Majesty in right of Canada or any province**.
- The **Official Languages Act applies** to the College, ensuring equal access to services in both official languages (English and French).

---

### **4. Purpose and Mandate**

The primary purpose of the College is to:
- Regulate patent and trademark agents in the **public interest**;
- Enhance the public’s ability to secure and protect intellectual property rights under the *Patent Act* and *Trademarks Act*;
- Ensure that only qualified and ethical professionals provide representation before government offices (e.g., the Patent Office and Office of the Registrar of Trademarks).

---

### **5. Governance Structure**

#### **Board of Directors**
- Minimum of **seven directors**, including a Chairperson.
- The **Minister may by order**:
  - Fix the total number of directors;
  - Appoint directors (with a cap on the number of appointed directors to ensure majority rule);
  - Appoint directors who are not licensees or government employees.
- **Elected directors** must include:
  - At least one patent agent;
  - At least one trademark agent.
- **Eligibility criteria** for directors:
  - Must be at least 18 years old;
  - Not bankrupt;
  - Not a member of an association primarily representing patent/trademark advisors;
  - Not a member of a governing body or steering committee of such an association in the past 12 months;
  - Appointed directors cannot be licensees or government employees;
  - Elected directors cannot have a suspended licence or meet other ineligibility criteria in the by-laws.

#### **Term of Office**
- Directors serve **no more than three-year terms**, renewable.
- Terms may vary by appointment or election; not all directors must serve on the same calendar dates.
- Appointed directors serve **during good behaviour** and may be removed for cause by the Minister.
- Elected directors may be removed per by-laws.

#### **Vacancies**
- If an elected director resigns or is removed before their term ends, the Board may appoint a temporary replacement for the remainder of the term.

#### **Chairperson**
- Elected by the Board from among directors.
- Presides over Board meetings and performs duties as defined in by-laws.
- May be removed by the Board in accordance with by-laws.

#### **Board Meetings**
- Must meet **at least once per calendar year**.
- Meetings are **open to the public**, unless otherwise restricted by by-laws.

#### **Authority of the Board**
- The Board may act on behalf of the College and delegate powers to:
  - Directors;
  - Committee members;
  - Registrar;
  - Investigators;
  - Officers and employees.

---

### **6. Committees**

Two key committees are established:

#### **Investigations Committee**
- Responsible for investigating complaints or initiating investigations into licensee conduct.
- May take **provisional actions** if it has reasonable grounds to believe a licensee has committed:
  - Professional misconduct;
  - Incompetence.
- Actions include:
  - Imposing conditions on a licence;
  - Restricting representation rights;
  - Suspending a licence.
- Must notify the licensee in writing and provide a **30-day period** for written response.
- All actions are **provisional** and automatically cease if:
  - The Discipline Committee revokes or amends the action;
  - The investigation is dismissed;
  - The appeal is withdrawn;
  - The Discipline Committee exercises its powers or renders a decision.

#### **Discipline Committee**
- Reviews and makes final decisions on disciplinary matters.
- Has authority to:
  - Confirm, amend, or revoke actions taken by the Investigations Committee;
  - Determine whether professional misconduct or incompetence occurred.
- Must issue a **written decision** with clear reasons.
- May dismiss a complaint if it finds no reasonable grounds to believe misconduct occurred.

---

### **7. Licensing and Registration**

#### **Licensing Process**
- The **Registrar** must issue:
  - A **patent agent licence**;
  - A **trademark agent licence**;
  - A **patent agent in training licence**;
  - A **trademark agent in training licence**;
  — upon application by an individual who meets the requirements set out in regulations.

#### **Conditions and Restrictions**
- All licences are subject to conditions imposed under the Act.
- A licensee whose licence is **not suspended** may:
  - Represent clients in patent or trademark applications;
  - Engage in other business before the relevant government offices.

#### **Registration Requirements**
The **Register of Patent Agents** and **Register of Trademark Agents** must include:
- Name and contact information of each agent and any firm they are part of;
- Any conditions or restrictions on their licence;
- Suspension status (with date if suspended);
- Disciplinary actions taken against them;
- Names of individuals whose licences were surrendered or revoked;
- Other information required by regulations or by-laws.

#### **Public Access**
- The Registers must be **available online** in a **searchable format** on the College’s website.
- The Registrar must provide the **Commissioner of Patents** and **Registrar of Trademarks** with up-to-date information (excluding details about suspended licences).

---

### **8. Professional Conduct and Standards**

- All licensees must adhere to a **Code of Professional Conduct**.
- The **Minister must by regulation** establish this code.
- Only the **Board** may amend or repeal the code, **with prior written approval from the Minister**.

#### **Professional Liability Insurance**
- Licensees must be insured against professional liability, **unless**:
  - They are employed by Her Majesty in right of Canada or a province;
  - They are working within the scope of that employment.
- The by-laws may exempt certain individuals from this requirement.

---

### **9. Suspension and Revocation of Licences**

The Registrar may suspend a licence if a licensee fails to:
- Pay annual or other required fees;
- Provide required information or documents;
- Comply with continuing professional development (CPD) requirements;
- Meet conditions imposed on their licence;
- Maintain professional liability insurance;
- Comply with any other by-law requirement.

- A **written notice** must be issued to the licensee.
- Suspension continues **until** the licensee complies with the required actions or until the licence is revoked.
- The Registrar may **revoke** a suspended licence after a specified period (as defined in by-laws).
- A licensee may **surrender** their licence voluntarily via application under by-laws.

---

### **10. Complaints and Investigations**

#### **Complaint Process**
- The Registrar receives all complaints about professional misconduct or incompetence.
- The Registrar may **dismiss** a complaint for any reason specified in regulations.
- If not dismissed, the complaint is **referred to the Investigations Committee**.

#### **Appeal Process**
- If the Registrar dismisses a complaint, the complainant has the right to **appeal within 30 days** to the Investigations Committee.
- The Investigations Committee may:
  - Dismiss the appeal;
  - Allow it and proceed with review.
- All complaints referred by the Registrar or appeals are **considered by the Investigations Committee**, which makes a final determination.

#### **Outcome of Investigation**
- If the Investigations Committee finds **no reasonable grounds** for misconduct or incompetence, it **dismisses the complaint** and notifies the parties.
