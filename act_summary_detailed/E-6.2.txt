### **2. Purpose of the Act**
The primary purpose of this Act is to provide **financial assistance to individuals and organizations** to help reduce energy costs, particularly in the context of housing and public infrastructure. It achieves this through:
- Refund-style payments to individuals who overpaid income tax due to a specific tax rule (related to energy costs),
- Direct funding for energy efficiency programs in housing,
- Support for public transit infrastructure development,
- Funding for energy efficiency initiatives under the *Energy Efficiency Act*.

This legislation reflects a government effort to **alleviate energy-related financial burdens** on low- and moderate-income households and to promote energy efficiency in housing and transportation.

  - The overpayment must include a portion that relates to the **"description of C"** under subsection 122.61(1), which refers to **energy costs** (e.g., heating, electricity, etc.) for a household.

#### **Section 3: Supplement and Allowance Payments (Old Age Security and Disability Benefits)**

**Objective:**  
To provide **direct financial support** to seniors and individuals receiving social benefits due to energy cost burdens.

**Key Details:**

- **Eligibility:**
  - The person must be entitled to:
    - A **supplement under subsection 11(1) of the Old Age Security Act**, or
    - An **allowance under subsection 19(1) or 21(1) of the Old Age Security Act** (e.g., for disability or spousal support).
  - The benefit must be payable for **any month in the payment quarter beginning January 1, 2006**.

- **Payment Amount:**
  - The **Minister of Employment and Social Development** may pay **$125** to the person.

- **Exclusion Clause:**
  - **No payment** will be made if:
    - The person or their **cohabiting spouse or common-law partner** (as defined in section 122.6 of the *Income Tax Act*) has **already received** or **can reasonably be expected to receive** a payment under Section 2 (the tax refund).
