## üîç **Detailed Summary of the Canada‚ÄìNewfoundland and Labrador Atlantic Accord Implementation and Offshore Renewable Energy Management Act**

### üìå **1. Purpose and Context**
This Act serves as the **legal framework for implementing the Atlantic Accord** between the Government of Canada and the Government of Newfoundland and Labrador (NL), specifically concerning **offshore petroleum resource management and revenue sharing**. It establishes a new regulatory body and sets out the governance, operational, and cooperative principles governing activities in the **offshore area** ‚Äî including both traditional petroleum and emerging offshore renewable energy projects.

Importantly, while the Atlantic Accord originally focused on **petroleum**, this Act **explicitly excludes it from applying to offshore renewable energy resources**, creating a distinct legal regime for clean energy development.

### üèõÔ∏è **3. Establishment of the Regulator**

#### ‚úÖ Creation
- A **joint regulatory body** ‚Äî the **Canada‚ÄìNewfoundland and Labrador Offshore Energy Regulator (Regulator)** ‚Äî is established by the **joint operation** of this Act and the *Provincial Act* (R.S.N.L. 1990, c. C-2).
- The Regulator is **not a federal or provincial agency**, but rather a **corporation with legal capacity** under the *Canada Business Corporations Act*.

#### ‚úÖ Powers and Legal Status
- Has **all corporate powers** of a corporation incorporated under the *Canada Business Corporations Act*, including those in section 21 of the *Interpretation Act*.
- Is treated as being established **under provincial law** for all purposes, meaning it has **provincial legal standing**.
- May be **dissolved only by joint action** of a federal Act of Parliament and a provincial Act of the Legislature of NL.

#### ‚úÖ Composition and Appointment
- **Total Members**: 7
  - 3 appointed by **Federal Government**
  - 3 appointed by **Provincial Government**
  - 1 **Chairperson** appointed jointly by both governments
- **Vice-Chairpersons**: One or two members may be designated as vice-chairpersons, **jointly by both governments**.
- **Alternate Members**: Each government may appoint one alternate to serve in case of absence or incapacity.
- **Dual Appointment**: A member may be appointed by **both governments** (e.g., Chairperson or vice-chair).

#### ‚úÖ Appointment Process
- **Consultation begins 6 months before** the incumbent Chairperson‚Äôs term ends or upon notice of death/resignation.
- If no agreement is reached within **3 months**, a **panel of three members** is formed:
  - One appointed by each government within 30 days.
  - The **panel chair** is appointed:
    - Jointly by the two panel members, within 30 days of their appointments; or
    - By the **Chief Justice of Newfoundland and Labrador** if they fail to agree.
- The **panel selects the Chairperson within 60 days** of the panel chair‚Äôs appointment.
- The **panel‚Äôs decision is final and binding** on both governments.

#### ‚úÖ Terms and Removal
- All members serve **six-year terms**.
- May be **reappointed** for additional terms after expiration.
- **Removal for cause**:
  - For members appointed by one government: removed by that government.
  - For members appointed by both governments: removed by both governments.
- No member may be employed in the **Public Service of Canada** or as a **civil servant** in NL during their term.

> üîç *Definition of "civil servant"*: As defined in the Provincial Act.

#### ‚úÖ Conflict of Interest
- Members and the Chief Executive Officer (CEO) are **subject to joint conflict of interest guidelines** established by the **Federal Minister and Provincial Minister**.
- They are **not subject to federal conflict of interest rules**.

### üìú **4. Regulatory and Governance Provisions**

#### ‚úÖ Federal-Provincial Consultation and Approval
- **Before any regulation is made** under certain sections (e.g., development plans, boundary lines, environmental approvals), the **Federal Minister must consult the Provincial Minister**.
- The regulation **cannot be enacted without the Provincial Minister‚Äôs approval**.
- This applies to regulations under:
  - Sections 2.1, 29.1, 41(7), 56.1, 64, 67(2), 96.1, 96.2(2), 118, 122(1), 125(1), 149(1), 162(2.3), 163(1.02), 183.19(6), 183.25(1), 183.29, 183.3(3), 202.01(1), and 203.

- **For occupational health and safety regulations** (e.g., under section 205.001 or 205.124):
  - The Federal Minister must consult the **Provincial Minister responsible for occupational health and safety**.
  - Approval from that minister is required.

#### ‚úÖ Boundary and Offshore Area Definitions
- The **Federal Government** may make regulations:
  - To **prescribe lines** enclosing areas adjacent to NL for the offshore area (to clarify boundaries).
  - To **amend the definition** of the offshore area in section 2.
- **Charts** issued by the Federal Minister are **conclusive proof** of offshore boundaries, **without requiring proof of signature or official status**.

#### ‚úÖ Dispute Resolution Mechanism
- If a dispute arises between NL and another province (a party to an agreement) over a prescribed line or portion of the offshore area:
  - The Government of Canada must first attempt **negotiation**.
  - If unresolved within a reasonable time, the dispute is referred to an **impartial tribunal or body**.
- The **Federal Minister determines** the tribunal‚Äôs constitution, membership, and procedures.
- If arbitration is used, the arbitrator applies **principles of international law on maritime boundary delimitation**, with necessary modifications.
- Once a dispute is settled, **any regulation based on that settlement is not subject to the consultation process under section 7**.

### üö´ **5. Exclusions and Precedence**

#### ‚ùå Offshore Renewable Energy is Excluded from Atlantic Accord
- The **Atlantic Accord does not apply** to offshore renewable energy resources.
- This allows for **separate, flexible regulatory frameworks** for clean energy development.

#### ‚ùå Exclusion of Key Federal Laws
- The following laws and regulations **do not apply** within the offshore area:
  - *Canada Petroleum Resources Act*
  - *Canada Oil and Gas Operations Act*
  - *Part 5 of the Canadian Energy Regulator Act* and any regulations made under it.
- This ensures **regulatory independence** for the offshore zone.
