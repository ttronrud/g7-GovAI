**Detailed Summary of the National Capital Act**

---

### **Overview**

The *National Capital Act* (often cited as the **National Capital Act**) is a foundational piece of Canadian federal legislation that establishes the **National Capital Commission (NCC)** as the governing body responsible for the planning, development, conservation, and improvement of the **National Capital Region (NCR)** — the seat of the Government of Canada and its surrounding area. The Act provides a comprehensive framework for the Commission’s structure, powers, responsibilities, financial operations, and relationship with other levels of government and institutions.

This summary provides a detailed, organized breakdown of the key provisions of the Act, including definitions, governance, powers, operational rules, and geographic scope.

---

## **I. Key Definitions**

The Act begins by defining essential terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Chairperson** | The head of the Commission; appointed by the Governor in Council. |
| **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)** | The executive leader of the Commission; also appointed by the Governor in Council. |
| **Commission** | The National Capital Commission, continued under section 3. |
| **Department** | Includes federal departments, divisions, commissions, boards, or bodies acting as agents of Her Majesty. |
| **Highway** | Includes streets, roads, lanes, thoroughfares, and driveways. |
| **Local Municipality** | A municipality wholly or partly located within the National Capital Region. |
| **Member** | A member of the Commission (excluding Chairperson and CEO). |
| **Minister** | Initially the Prime Minister, but may be designated by the Governor in Council. |
| **National Capital Region (NCR)** | A specifically defined geographic area in Ontario and Quebec, described in detail in the Schedule. |
| **Property** | Real or personal property, or any interest in it. |
| **Property of the Commission** | Property under the control or ownership of the Commission. |
| **Public Lands** | Real property under the control or management of a federal department. |
| **Work** | Any structure, construction, or undertaking. |

> *Note:* The position of **Vice-Chairperson** was repealed in 2006. The provision regarding the **Public Service Superannuation Act** does not apply unless otherwise directed by the Governor in Council.

## **II. Governance Structure of the Commission**

### **1. Composition and Appointment**

- The Commission consists of **15 members**, including:
  - **1 Chairperson**
  - **1 Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**
  - **12 other members**

- **Appointment Process**:
  - **Members (non-Chair, non-CEO)**:
    - **3** from local municipalities in **Ontario**, at least **2 from Ottawa**.
    - **2** from local municipalities in **Quebec**, at least **1 from the part of Gatineau west of the Gatineau River**.
    - **8** from **Canada generally**, excluding those from municipalities mentioned above.

  - **Chairperson and CEO**:
    - Appointed directly by the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet), for a term considered appropriate by them.

- **Term of Office**:
  - Regular members serve **up to four years**.
  - Terms are staggered so that **no more than half** of the members expire in any one year.
  - A person who has served **two consecutive terms** is **not eligible for reappointment** for **12 months** after completing their second term.

- **Vacancies**:
  - Do **not impair** the ability of the remaining members to act.

---

### **2. Leadership and Committees**

- **Executive Committee**:
  - Composed of:
    - Chairperson
    - CEO
    - **Three other members**, at least **one from Quebec**
  - Powers:
    - Exercises delegated powers of the Commission.
    - Prepares and submits minutes of its proceedings to the full Commission at each meeting.

- **National Capital Planning Committee**:
  - May be established by the Commission as needed for administrative purposes.

- **Member Compensation**:
  - All members (including those on committees) are entitled to **reasonable travel and other expenses** incurred in carrying out duties.
  - The **Governor in Council** may authorize **allowances or special remuneration** for members with specific responsibilities.

- **General Manager**:
  - Appointed by the Governor in Council during pleasure.
  - Paid a salary set by the Governor in Council.

- **Employment**:
  - The Commission may employ officers, employees, consultants, and advisers.
  - Remuneration and terms of employment are subject to **approval by the Governor in Council**.
  - The Commission may propose:
    - A **plan of organization** (for job classifications and structure)
    - **Compensation rates** for each position
    - Other desirable employment terms

> *Note:* The Governor in Council may also make regulations under the Financial Administration Act to adapt to the Commission’s needs.

---

## **III. Core Objectives and Purposes**

The primary **object and purpose** of the Commission is:

> To **prepare plans** for and **assist in the development, conservation, and improvement** of the National Capital Region so that the **nature and character of the seat of Government of Canada** reflects its **national significance**.

This includes ensuring the region is sustainable, accessible, and representative of Canada’s heritage and identity.

---

## **IV. Powers and Authorities**

The Commission is granted broad powers to carry out its mandate, including:

### **1. Property Management**
- May **acquire, hold, administer, or develop** any property.
- May **sell, grant, convey, lease, or dispose of** property, subject to conditions deemed necessary.
- May **construct, maintain, and operate**:
  - Parks, squares, highways, parkways, bridges, buildings, and other works.
- May **maintain and improve** property under the control of a department upon request from the responsible Minister or authority.

### **2. Cooperation with Municipalities**
- May **cooperate or make grants** to local municipalities for improvement, development, or maintenance of property.

### **3. Recreation and Public Facilities**
- May **construct, maintain, and operate**, or grant **concessions for**, places of entertainment, amusement, recreation, refreshment, or public accommodation on Commission property.

### **4. Historic Preservation**
- May **administer, preserve, and maintain** any historic place or museum.

### **5. Research and Planning**
- May conduct **investigations and research** related to planning in the NCR.

### **6. Incidental Powers**
- May do and authorize **any acts incidental or conducive** to achieving its objectives.

## **V. Development and Land Use Controls**

### **1. Project Approval Requirements**

Before any project involving public lands in the NCR may begin, a **proposal must be submitted to the Commission for approval** in the following cases:

- A **department** proposes to:
  - Erect, alter, extend, or demolish a building or structure.
- A **private person** proposes to:
  - Erect, alter, extend, or demolish a building or structure on public lands.
- A **department or person** proposes to **change the use** of public lands.

#### **Factors Considered by the Commission in Approval Decisions**:
- **Site, location, design, and plans** of the proposed work.
- **Use** of the building or land after alteration or demolition.
- **Existing use and proposed use** of public lands in cases of land-use change.

> ⚠️ **Exception**: Interior alterations **not required** unless they accommodate a change in use.

### **2. Approval Process**
- **No project may commence** without prior submission and **Commission approval**.
- Proposals for **sale of public lands** must be submitted to the Commission **before sale**.
- **No public land in the NCR may be sold without Commission approval**.

### **3. Governor in Council Authority**
- If the Commission **does not approve** a proposal under sections 12, 12.1, or this section, the **Governor in Council may approve it**.
- Such approval by the Governor in Council is **deemed to be an approval by the Commission** for the purposes of the Act.
- Any approval (by Commission or Governor in Council) may include **terms and conditions**.

---

## **VI. Railway and Transportation Powers**

- The Commission may:
  - Construct a **railway and related facilities** in the NCR, in accordance with approved plans.
  - **Sell, convey, or lease** such facilities to a railway company.
  - Enter into agreements for:
    - Use (sole, joint, or shared)
    - Maintenance
    - Operation of the railway or facilities

> ⚠️ **Important Limitation**: 
> - The Commission is **not considered a railway company** under this section — only for the purpose of leasing or operating.
> - **Part III of the Canada Transportation Act** applies, with necessary modifications.

---

## **VII. Land Acquisition and Expropriation**

- If the Commission believes it must acquire land or an interest in land **without the owner’s consent**, it must **advise the appropriate Minister** under **Part I of the Expropriation Act**.

- For **Expropriation Act purposes**, land required for public works or the Commission’s activities is deemed to be for a **public purpose**.

- A reference to the **Crown** in the Expropriation Act is **construed as a reference to the Commission**.
