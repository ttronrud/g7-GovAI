**Detailed Summary of the *Agriculture and Agri-Food Administrative Monetary Penalties Act***  

---

### **Overview**

The *Agriculture and Agri-Food Administrative Monetary Penalties Act* (the "Act") establishes a **fair, efficient, and proportionate administrative monetary penalty (AMP) system** to enforce compliance with key Canadian agri-food legislation. It serves as an **alternative to criminal prosecution** and a **supplement to existing enforcement mechanisms**, allowing for faster, more flexible, and cost-effective resolution of violationsâ€”particularly those that do not constitute serious criminal offences.

The Act applies to a range of agri-food-related laws, including the *Farm Debt Mediation Act*, *Feeds Act*, *Fertilizers Act*, *Health of Animals Act*, *Pest Control Products Act*, *Plant Protection Act*, and *Safe Food for Canadians Act*. Its primary goal is to improve regulatory compliance while minimizing the burden on individuals and businesses, especially small operators.

---

## **Purpose of the Act**

To establish a **modern, efficient, and equitable administrative penalty system** that:
- Provides a **non-criminal alternative** to enforcement,
- Is **proportionate** to the nature and severity of the violation,
- Encourages compliance through **early intervention and remediation**,
- Reduces the need for costly and time-consuming court proceedings.

---

## **Regulatory Authority of the Minister**

The Minister has broad power to make **regulations** to define and govern the AMP system, including:

1. **Designating violations** that can be enforced under this Act:
   - Contravention of specific provisions of agri-food Acts or their regulations.
   - Contravention of specific orders issued by the Minister under the *Plant Protection Act*.
   - Refusal or neglect to perform specified duties under the *Plant Protection Act*, *Health of Animals Act*, *Pest Control Products Act*, or *Safe Food for Canadians Act*.

2. **Classifying violations** into three categories:
   - **Minor violation**
   - **Serious violation**
   - **Very serious violation**

3. **Establishing penalties** for each violation, including:
   - A **range of penalties** (e.g., $5,000 to $25,000).
   - **Maximum penalties**:
     - For individuals (not in business, not for financial gain): **$2,000**
     - For all other cases:
       - Minor: **$5,000**
       - Serious: **$15,000**
       - Very serious: **$25,000**

4. **Setting criteria for increasing or reducing penalties**, including:
   - Degree of **intent or negligence**.
   - **Harm caused** by the violation.
   - **History of prior violations or convictions** within the 5 years before the current violation.

5. **Specifying procedures** for:
   - Serving notices of violation.
   - Proof of service.
   - Review processes (oral or written).
   - Compliance agreements.
   - Payment timelines and reduced payment options.

6. **Prescribing any other matters** necessary to implement the Act.

## **Who Can Issue Notices of Violation?**

The Minister may designate **specific individuals or classes of persons** (e.g., inspectors, officers) to issue **notices of violation**. These notices must:
- Name the person,
- Identify the violation,
- Include a **warning** (if applicable), or
- State the **penalty amount**, payment deadline, and **option for a reduced payment** (if allowed by regulation).

---

## **Content and Rights in a Notice of Violation**

Every notice must:
- Clearly summarize, in **plain language**, the **rights and obligations** of the person served, including:
  - Right to request a **review of the facts** by the Minister or the Tribunal.
  - Procedure for making such a request.

### **Two Types of Notices**

| Type | Content |
|------|--------|
| **With Warning** | The person is warned they have committed a violation. They may request a review by the Minister or Tribunal within the prescribed time. If no review is requested, they are **deemed to have committed the violation**. |
| **With Penalty** | Specifies the penalty amount, payment deadline, and optional reduced payment. |

## **Options for the Person Served**

Instead of paying the penalty, the person may:
1. **Request a compliance agreement** (only if the penalty is $2,000 or more).
2. **Request a review by the Minister** of the facts.
3. **Request a review by the Tribunal** of the facts.

> âŒ If the person **fails to pay the penalty or make a request** within the prescribed time, they are **deemed to have committed the violation**.

---

## **Compliance Agreements**

- A person may request a **compliance agreement** with the Minister to ensure future compliance.
- The Minister may enter into such an agreement **on terms and conditions** that are satisfactory, including:
  - Requiring **reasonable security** (e.g., bond or deposit) as a guarantee of compliance.
  - **Reducing the penalty** in whole or in part.
- Upon entering into the agreement:
  - The person is **deemed to have committed the violation**.
- If the person **complies with the agreement**, the Minister:
  - Serves a **notice of compliance**.
  - The proceedings end.
  - Any security is **returned** to the person.
- If the person **fails to comply**:
  - The Minister serves a **notice of default** stating:
    - The person must pay **twice the original penalty**, or
    - The security is **forfeited** to Her Majesty in right of Canada.
- Upon payment of the default amount:
  - The amount is accepted as full satisfaction.
  - Proceedings end.

## **Review Process**

### **1. Review by the Minister (Under Section 8)**
- After a request for review, the Minister determines whether the violation occurred.
- If the violation is confirmed, the person may:
  - Pay the penalty, or
  - Request a **review by the Tribunal**.

### **2. Review by the Tribunal (Under Section 13 & 14)**
- The Tribunal may:
  - **Confirm, vary, or set aside** the Ministerâ€™s decision.
  - Determine whether the violation occurred.
  - If the violation occurred but the penalty was **not properly established**, the Tribunal **corrects the penalty amount**.
- The Tribunalâ€™s decision is **final** and binding.
- The person must pay the penalty as ordered.
- Upon payment, the Minister accepts it as full satisfaction, and the proceedings end.

## **Debts and Enforcement**

### **Debts Due to Her Majesty in Right of Canada**
The following amounts are considered **debts** and may be recovered in the **Federal Court**:
1. Penalty amount from the date the notice is served.
2. Amounts under a compliance agreement from the date of agreement.
3. Amount in a notice of default from the date it is served.
4. Penalty from a Ministerial decision (under section 13) from the date of notice.
5. Penalty from a Tribunal order from the expiration of the payment period.
6. Reasonable expenses under section 22 (from the date incurred).

> â±ï¸ **Time Limit for Recovery**: No proceedings may be commenced **later than five years** after the debt becomes payable.

> âœ… **Finality of Debt**: Once a debt is established, it is **final and not subject to review or restraint**, except as provided in sections 9 to 14.
