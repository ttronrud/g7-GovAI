**Detailed Summary of the National Anthem Act**

The *National Anthem Act* is a legislative instrument enacted to formally establish and recognize the national anthem of Canada. Below is a detailed summary of its key provisions:

---

**1. Citation of the Act**  
The Act is officially titled the *National Anthem Act*. This designation allows the legislation to be formally referenced in legal, governmental, and public discourse using a standardized name.

---

**2. Designation of "O Canada" as the National Anthem**  
The central provision of the Act declares that the words and music of the song *“O Canada”*, as precisely set out in the *Schedule* attached to the Act, shall be recognized and designated as the **national anthem of Canada**.  

- The Schedule contains the official, authoritative version of the lyrics and musical notation of *“O Canada”*, including the exact wording of the verses, the melody, and any official musical arrangement.  
- This formalization ensures that there is a single, legally recognized version of the national anthem, which helps prevent confusion or unauthorized variations from being used in official contexts.

---

**3. Public Domain Status**  
The Act explicitly declares that the words and music of the national anthem are **in the public domain**.  

- This means that the composition of *“O Canada”*—including its lyrics and musical score—is freely available for use, reproduction, adaptation, and dissemination without requiring permission, royalties, or licensing.  
- The public domain status removes any copyright restrictions on the anthem, allowing individuals, institutions, and organizations to use it in educational, cultural, ceremonial, or commercial settings without legal concern.  
- This provision supports the anthem’s role as a symbol of national identity and promotes accessibility, especially in schools, public events, and government functions.

---

**Purpose and Significance**  
The National Anthem Act serves several important functions:  
- It provides legal clarity and consistency in the use of Canada’s national anthem.  
- It reinforces national unity and cultural identity by formally recognizing a shared national symbol.  
- It ensures that the anthem remains accessible to all Canadians, regardless of background or location.  
- It supports the use of the anthem in official ceremonies, public education, and international representation.

---

**Conclusion**  
The *National Anthem Act* is a concise but significant piece of legislation that formally designates *“O Canada”* as Canada’s national anthem and removes any copyright barriers to its use. By establishing a clear, legally recognized version of the anthem and placing it in the public domain, the Act promotes inclusivity, national pride, and the free and equitable use of a foundational cultural symbol in Canadian society.
