**Detailed Summary of the *Department of Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *Department of Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Act* (DCIRNA Act) is a foundational piece of legislation enacted by the Government of Canada to institutionalize and strengthen **nation-to-nation, government-to-government, and Inuit-Crown relationships** with First Nations, Métis, and Inuit peoples. It establishes a new federal department — the **Department of Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs (DCIRNA)** — to advance reconciliation, implement Indigenous rights, and promote self-determination, well-being, and sustainable development in the Canadian North.

This Act reflects Canada’s commitment to fulfilling its obligations under **Section 35 of the Constitution Act, 1982**, which recognizes and affirms Indigenous rights, and to implementing the **United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (UNDRIP)**.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Purpose and Principles**

The Act is grounded in several core principles:

- **Reconciliation**: The Government is committed to achieving reconciliation through respectful, collaborative, and equitable relationships with Indigenous peoples.
- **Recognition of Section 35 Rights**: It affirms the rights of Indigenous peoples as recognized and affirmed under Section 35 of the *Constitution Act, 1982*.
- **Implementation of UNDRIP**: The Act supports the implementation of the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples.
- **Nation-to-Nation Relationships**: It emphasizes partnership, mutual respect, and shared decision-making between the Crown and Indigenous peoples.
- **Self-Determination**: The Act promotes Indigenous self-governance, self-reliance, prosperity, and well-being.

---

### **2. Establishment of the Department**

- A new federal department is created: the **Department of Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs (DCIRNA)**.
- The **Minister of Crown-Indigenous Relations** presides over the department and is responsible for leading Canada’s engagement with Indigenous peoples.

> **Definition of Key Terms**:
> - **Indigenous Peoples**: Defined as "aboriginal peoples of Canada" under Section 35(2) of the Constitution Act, 1982.
> - **Indigenous Governing Body**: A council, government, or entity authorized to act on behalf of an Indigenous group with rights under Section 35.
> - **Indigenous Organization**: Any entity representing Indigenous interests.
> - **Minister**: Refers to the Minister of Crown-Indigenous Relations.

---

### **3. Structure and Leadership**

#### **Minister of Crown-Indigenous Relations**
- Appointed by commission under the Great Seal; serves at the pleasure of the Governor in Council.
- Holds overall responsibility for:
  - Leading national policy on Indigenous rights and self-determination.
  - Negotiating treaties and agreements with Indigenous peoples.
  - Advancing reconciliation through renewed relationships.
  - Ensuring implementation of Section 35 rights and treaty obligations.

#### **Deputy Minister**
- Appointed by the Governor in Council; serves at pleasure.
- Acts as deputy head of the department and supports the Minister.

#### **Associate Deputy Minister**
- Appointed by the Governor in Council; serves at pleasure.
- Operates under the Deputy Minister and may be delegated specific duties or functions by the Minister.

> These roles allow for structured leadership, delegation, and operational efficiency within the department.

---

### **4. Powers, Duties, and Functions of the Minister**

The Minister has broad authority over all matters within Parliament’s jurisdiction **not assigned to another department**, specifically relating to:

- **Indigenous peoples’ rights** (especially under Section 35).
- **Treaty negotiations and implementation**.
- **Reconciliation efforts** through nation-to-nation and government-to-government relationships.

Key responsibilities include:
- Leading national policy on Indigenous rights and self-determination.
- Negotiating new treaties and agreements that advance Indigenous self-governance.
- Promoting public awareness and understanding of reconciliation.

---

### **5. Collaboration with Indigenous Services Canada**

- The Department may **provide services to and receive services from the Department of Indigenous Services Canada (DISC)**.
- A formal **written agreement** between the Ministers of Crown-Indigenous Relations and Indigenous Services will govern this cooperation.
- The Minister may **delegate any powers, duties, or functions** (except the power to delegate under this section) to the Minister of Indigenous Services, for any period and under terms deemed suitable.

> This ensures coordination and shared responsibility in delivering services to Indigenous communities.

---

### **6. Reporting Requirements**

- The Minister must table a **report in each House of Parliament** within **three months** after the end of each fiscal year (or on any of the first 15 days of the next parliamentary session if the House is not sitting).
- The report must detail:
  - Measures taken to advance **Indigenous self-determination**.
  - Progress in **reconciliation efforts** with Indigenous peoples.

> This ensures transparency, accountability, and public oversight of Indigenous policy and implementation.

---

### **7. Minister of Northern Affairs**

A separate role is established for the **Minister of Northern Affairs**, with distinct responsibilities focused on the **Canadian North** (Yukon, Northwest Territories, Nunavut).

#### **Appointment**
- The Minister of Northern Affairs may be appointed by commission under the Great Seal.
- If no Minister is appointed:
  - The **Minister of Crown-Indigenous Relations** automatically assumes the powers and duties of the Minister of Northern Affairs.
  - All references to the Minister of Northern Affairs in legislation or instruments are **read as references to the Minister of Crown-Indigenous Relations**, unless context otherwise requires.

#### **Deputy Minister of Northern Affairs**
- May be designated by the Governor in Council.
- If not designated, the **Deputy Minister of Crown-Indigenous Relations** assumes the role.
- All references to the Deputy Minister of Northern Affairs are read as references to the Deputy Minister of Crown-Indigenous Relations.

---

### **8. Powers and Responsibilities of the Minister of Northern Affairs**

The Minister of Northern Affairs has authority over all matters within Parliament’s jurisdiction related to:

1. **The Canadian North**:
   - Yukon, Northwest Territories, and Nunavut.
   - Their resources, affairs, and development.

2. **Policy and Program Development**:
   - Coordinates activities across federal departments, boards, and agencies in the territories.
   - Develops and implements policies to promote **social, economic, and political development** in the North.
   - Supports **scientific research and technology** to improve knowledge of and development in the North.

3. **Land and Property Administration**:
   - Administers lands in Nunavut (except those under other ministers or agent corporations).
   - Administers public real property (Yukon) and public lands (Northwest Territories), excluding those under other ministers or territorial commissioners.

> This ensures centralized, coordinated management of federal interests in the North while respecting territorial autonomy.

---

### **9. Authority to Delegate and Establish Advisory Bodies**

- The Minister or Minister of Northern Affairs may:
  - **Delegate powers and duties** (except the power to delegate under this section).
  - **Appoint special representatives** to provide advice or represent them in consultations with Indigenous groups.
  - **Establish advisory and other committees** to advise on specific issues.

> These mechanisms allow for flexibility and deeper engagement with Indigenous communities during negotiations and policy development.

---

### **10. Access to Information and Data**

- The Minister or Minister of Northern Affairs may:
  - **Collect, analyze, and interpret** data and documents related to their responsibilities.
  - **Disclose or permit disclosure** of such information to:
    - The Department of Indigenous Services;
    - Federal departments or agencies listed in Schedule I or I.1 to the *Financial Administration Act*;
    - Indigenous organizations;
    - Not-for-profit entities controlled by Indigenous individuals.

> **Important Restrictions**:
> - Disclosure of **personal information** is only permitted if:
>   - It is publicly available;
>   - The individual consents;
>   - The disclosure serves a purpose under the *Privacy Act* (e.g., public interest, legal proceedings);
>   - It is protected by **solicitor-client privilege**, **professional secrecy**, or **litigation privilege**;
>   - It is restricted under other Acts (e.g., Access to Information Act);
>   - It is a **confidence of the Queen’s Privy Council**.

> This balances transparency with privacy and legal protections.

---

### **11. Transitional Provisions (Effective Date: November 30, 2017)**

This section ensures a smooth transition from the former **Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development (DIAND)** to the new DCIRNA.

#### Key Transitions:
- **Ministers**:
  - The former **Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development** is deemed to be the **Minister of Crown-Indigenous Relations**.
  - The former **Minister of State to assist** is deemed to be the **Minister of Northern Affairs**.
  - The former **Deputy Minister of Indian Affairs and Northern Development** is deemed to have been appointed as the **Deputy Minister of Crown-Indigenous Relations**.

- **Positions**:
  - All individuals in DIAND are deemed to have moved to DCIRNA, maintaining their positions and responsibilities.

- **Appropriations**:
  - Unexpended funding allocated to DIAND in the fiscal year when the Act came into force is **deemed appropriated** to DCIRNA.
