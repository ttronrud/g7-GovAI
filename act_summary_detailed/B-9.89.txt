## üèõÔ∏è **Detailed Summary of the Building Canada Act**

### **1. Purpose and Legislative Context**

The *Building Canada Act* (Bill C-34) was enacted to **accelerate the development of nationally significant infrastructure projects** across Canada ‚Äî particularly in underserved or remote regions such as the North ‚Äî by streamlining regulatory processes while maintaining environmental protections and respecting Indigenous rights.

#### **Core Objectives:**
- Strengthen **national economic security**, **sovereignty**, and **energy resilience**.
- Enhance **trade corridors**, **economic connectivity**, and **market access**.
- Create **good-paying, unionized jobs**.
- Promote **clean growth** and **climate change mitigation**.
- Improve **regulatory certainty** and **investor confidence**.
- Ensure **transparency**, **accountability**, and **public participation**.

The Act responds to growing recognition that Canada needs faster, more efficient pathways for major infrastructure projects ‚Äî especially those critical to energy, transportation, and resource development ‚Äî without compromising environmental or Indigenous rights.

### **3. Process for Designating National Interest Projects**

#### **Step 1: Determining National Interest**
- The **Governor in Council** may designate a project as a **national interest project** by **amending Schedule 1**.
- Before doing so, the Governor must:
  - Publish a **30-day public notice** in the *Canada Gazette*.
  - Obtain **written consent from the provincial or territorial government** if the project falls within exclusive provincial/territorial jurisdiction (e.g., land use, water rights).
- The order must include **specific, measurable criteria** for why the project is in the national interest.

> üîç **Criteria Considered:**
> - Strengthens national autonomy, resilience, and security.
> - Provides economic or other benefits to Canada.
> - Has a high likelihood of successful execution.
> - Advances Indigenous peoples‚Äô interests.
> - Contributes to clean growth and climate goals.

#### **Step 2: Time Limits and Accountability**
- The Governor **must issue the order within 15 days** of the Act coming into force.
- If no order is issued within that time, the **Minister must table a report** in both Houses of Parliament explaining the delay and outlining a revised timeline.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **No designations allowed during prorogation or dissolution of Parliament**, nor after the **fifth anniversary** of the Act‚Äôs commencement.

#### **Step 3: Amendments and Removal**
- The Governor can:
  - **Add** a new project to Schedule 1 (with public notice and consultation).
  - **Amend** the name or description of an existing project.
  - **Remove** a project if it is no longer in the national interest.
- However, **removal is prohibited once a formal authorization document has been issued** for the project.

> üö´ **Important Limitation**: Once a document (authorization) is issued under Section 7, the project cannot be removed from Schedule 1.

### **4. Regulatory and Authorization Process (Section 7)**

This is the **core mechanism** of the Act ‚Äî a streamlined process to issue **deemed authorizations** for national interest projects.

#### **Key Provisions:**

##### ‚úÖ **Deemed Authorization**
- The Minister issues a **single document** that is **deemed to be all required authorizations** under applicable laws (e.g., environmental, safety, land use).
- This document **does not replace** the need for compliance with existing laws ‚Äî it only **accelerates** the process.

##### ‚úÖ **Conditions in the Document**
- The document must contain **clear, enforceable conditions** for each authorization.
- Conditions must be **practicable and legally permissible** under the relevant enactment.
- Conditions are **deemed to be valid** as if issued under the original law.

##### ‚úÖ **Public Transparency Requirements**
- The document must be **publicly accessible** (via the Internet).
- All **studies, impact assessments, recommendations, and decisions** must be made public **before issuance**.
- A **comparative analysis** of accepted vs. rejected recommendations must be included.
- An **assessment of risks** from ignoring rejected recommendations must be provided.
- A **description of the normal regulatory process** (if the project had not been designated) must be included.

> üìù **Public Disclosure Timeline**:
> - The Minister must make the full information public **no later than 30 days before** the document is issued.
> - The information must be tabled in **both Houses of Parliament**.
> - At the request of **10 or more members**, the Minister must appear before a parliamentary committee to explain decisions.

##### ‚úÖ **Consultation with Indigenous Peoples**
- **Mandatory consultation** with Indigenous peoples whose rights under **Section 35 of the Constitution Act, 1982** may be affected.
- A **meaningful, active participation process** must be established.
- A **report on the consultation process and outcomes** must be made public within **60 days** of document issuance.

> üõ°Ô∏è This ensures Indigenous voices are not tokenized and that their rights are respected.

##### ‚úÖ **National Security Reviews**
- A **national security review** is required for:
  - State-owned investments.
  - Foreign investments from **hostile countries**.
- The review ensures national security is not compromised.

##### ‚úÖ **Foreign Investment Safeguards**
- For foreign investments, the Minister must confirm that:
  - National security interests are protected.
  - No risks to health, safety, or international obligations exist.

> üîê Specific requirements apply under:
> - **Nuclear Safety and Control Act** ‚Üí Requires confirmation from the **Canadian Nuclear Safety Commission (CNSC)**.
> - **Canadian Energy Regulator Act** ‚Üí Requires confirmation from the **Canadian Energy Regulator (CER)**.

> ‚ùå No document can be issued without such confirmation.

- No designations allowed after the **fifth anniversary** of the Act‚Äôs coming into force.
