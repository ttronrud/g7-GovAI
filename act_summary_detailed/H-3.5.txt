**Detailed Summary of the *Heritage Railway Stations Protection Act***

### **Overview**

The *Heritage Railway Stations Protection Act* is a Canadian statute designed to protect historically significant railway stations and their key features from unauthorized alteration, destruction, or disposal. The Act establishes a formal process for designating heritage railway stations and heritage features, and it imposes strict legal safeguards to ensure that these important cultural assets are preserved for future generations. It operates under the authority of the Minister of Parks Canada, with oversight and advisory input from the Historic Sites and Monuments Board of Canada (the "Board").

The Act applies specifically to railway companies that are subject to *Part III of the Canada Transportation Act*, ensuring that it targets major national transportation operators rather than private or regional entities.

### **Scope of Application**

- The Act applies to **railway companies** that fall under *Part III of the Canada Transportation Act*, which includes federally regulated rail operators such as Canadian National Railway (CN), Canadian Pacific Railway (CP), and others.

### **Designation Process**

- The **Minister may designate**:
  - Railway stations as **heritage railway stations**;
  - Specific **features** within those stations as **heritage features**.

- These designations are made **on the recommendation of the Board**, ensuring that decisions are based on expert historical and architectural assessment.

### **Prohibitions on Unauthorized Actions**

Unless authorized by the Governor in Council, **no railway company may**:

1. **Remove, destroy, or alter** a heritage railway station (or any part of it) that is owned or under its control;  
2. **Sell, assign, transfer, or otherwise dispose of** a heritage railway station;  
3. **Alter any heritage feature** of a heritage railway station.
