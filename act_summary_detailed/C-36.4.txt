**Detailed Summary of the Competition Tribunal Act (This Part)**

---

### **Overview**
This Part of the *Competition Tribunal Act* establishes the legal framework for the **Competition Tribunal**, a federal body created to administer and adjudicate matters related to competition law under the *Competition Act*. It defines the structure, composition, powers, procedures, and operational rules of the Tribunal, ensuring it operates with independence, fairness, and efficiency while maintaining alignment with judicial standards and public accountability.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Citation and Definitions**

- **Citation**: This Part is cited as the *Competition Tribunal Act*.
- **Key Definitions**:
  - **Judicial Member**: A member of the Tribunal appointed by the Governor in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Justice. These are typically experienced judges from the Federal Court.
  - **Lay Member**: A member appointed by the Governor in Council on the recommendation of the Minister. Lay members are non-judicial individuals with expertise in economics, industry, commerce, public affairs, or related fields.
  - **Minister**: Refers to the **Minister of Industry** (though in practice, the Minister of Justice and/or Minister of Consumer and Corporate Affairs may also play roles).
  - **Tribunal**: The body established under subsection 3(1), tasked with hearing and deciding competition-related disputes.

---

### **2. Establishment of the Tribunal**

- A **Competition Tribunal** is formally established.
- The Tribunal is composed of:
  - **Not more than six judicial members**, appointed by the Governor in Council based on the **Minister of Justice’s recommendation**.
  - **Not more than eight lay members**, appointed by the Governor in Council on the recommendation of the **Minister of Industry**.

> This structure ensures a balance between judicial expertise and lay representation from diverse sectors of Canadian society.

---

### **3. Advisory Council for Lay Member Appointments**

- The Governor in Council may establish an **advisory council** (up to 10 members) to advise the Minister on lay member appointments.
- The council must consist of individuals knowledgeable in:
  - Economics
  - Industry
  - Commerce
  - Public affairs
- Members may be drawn from:
  - Business communities
  - Legal profession
  - Consumer groups
  - Labour organizations
- **Requirement**: The Minister must **consult** with this advisory council before making any recommendation for a lay member.

> This provision enhances transparency, ensures diversity of perspective, and improves the legitimacy of lay member appointments.

---

### **4. Chairmanship of the Tribunal**

- The Governor in Council shall designate **one judicial member** as the **Chairman** of the Tribunal.
- The Chairman:
  - Has **supervision and direction** over the Tribunal’s work.
  - Oversees the **allocation of work** among members.
- In the event of:
  - Vacancy of the Chairman’s office
  - Absence or incapacity of the Chairman
  → The **senior judicial member** present in Canada and able/willing to act **exercises the Chairman’s powers**.

> Ensures continuity and leadership even during temporary absences or vacancies.

---

### **5. Terms of Office and Reappointment**

- **Judicial Members**:
  - Appointed for a term **not exceeding seven years**.
  - Hold office **as long as they remain a judge of the Federal Court** (i.e., their term continues until retirement or resignation).
- **Lay Members**:
  - Appointed for a term **not exceeding seven years**.
  - Hold office during **good behaviour**.
  - May be **removed by the Governor in Council for cause** (e.g., misconduct, failure to perform duties).

> Both types of members are eligible for **reappointment** after their initial term ends.

---

### **6. Continuation in Proceedings After Term Ends**

- A member may **continue to act** in any matter in which they were involved during their term of office, even after their term expires.
- This ensures **procedural continuity** and prevents disruption in ongoing cases.

---

### **7. Temporary Substitutes**

- If a member is absent or incapacitated, the Governor in Council may appoint a **temporary substitute member** for a period determined by the Governor.
- The substitute acts temporarily until the original member returns or is replaced.

> Provides flexibility in managing absences without halting proceedings.

---

### **8. Remuneration and Allowances**

- **Lay members** receive remuneration fixed by the Governor in Council.
- All members (judicial and lay) are entitled to:
  - **Travel allowances** equivalent to those paid to judges under the *Judges Act*.
- Lay members are deemed to be **employed in the federal public administration** for the purposes of:
  - *Government Employees Compensation Act*
  - *Aeronautics Act* (regulations under section 9)

> Ensures financial support and legal protections for members.

---

### **9. Oath of Office**

- Every member must take an **oath** before assuming office:
  - To **duly and faithfully** perform their duties.
  - To act in the **best of their skill and knowledge**.
- The **Chairman** takes the oath before the **Chief Justice of the Federal Court**.
- Other members take the oath before the **Chairman** (or another member if the Chairman is absent or incapacitated).

> Reinforces integrity, impartiality, and professional commitment.

---

### **10. Jurisdiction of the Tribunal**

The Tribunal has jurisdiction to:
- Hear and dispose of:
  - Applications under **Part VII.1 or VIII of the Competition Act** (e.g., complaints about anti-competitive practices, mergers, etc.).
  - Any related matters.
  - Matters under **Part IX** of the Competition Act that are **referred under subsection 124.2(2)** of that Act (e.g., matters involving the Commissioner or other government bodies).

> This gives the Tribunal broad authority over competition enforcement and dispute resolution.

---

### **11. Powers Comparable to a Superior Court**

The Tribunal has **all powers, rights, and privileges** of a **superior court of record**, including:
- Attendance and swearing in witnesses
- Production and inspection of documents
- Enforcement of its orders
- Other procedural powers necessary to carry out its duties

> This grants the Tribunal significant procedural autonomy and judicial authority.

---

### **12. Contempt of the Tribunal**

- A person **cannot be punished for contempt** unless a **judicial member** determines that:
  - The finding of contempt is appropriate.
  - The punishment is justified in the circumstances.
- This protects individuals from arbitrary or punitive actions.

> Ensures due process and judicial restraint.

---

### **13. Awarding of Costs**

- The Tribunal may award **costs** in proceedings under Parts VII.1 and VIII of the Competition Act:
  - On a **final or interim basis**.
  - In accordance with the *Federal Court Rules, 1998*.
- The Tribunal may:
  - Direct **who pays** the costs.
  - Determine **who taxes and allows** the costs.
- **Exception**: The Tribunal **shall not award costs against Her Majesty in right of Canada** unless:
  - It is satisfied that the award is **necessary to maintain confidence in the administration of justice**, or
  - The **absence of an award would substantially harm the other party’s ability to conduct business**.
- **Important Exception**: Costs awarded to Her Majesty in right of Canada **cannot be disallowed or reduced** simply because:
  - The counsel involved was a **salaried officer** of the Crown.
  - The counsel was not entitled to recover costs under the rules.
- Any costs awarded to Her Majesty shall be **paid to the Receiver General**.

> Balances fairness with administrative efficiency and protects government interests.

---

### **14. Status of the Tribunal**

- The Tribunal is a **court of record**.
- It has an **official seal**, which is **judicially noticed** (i.e., presumed true in legal proceedings).

> Confers formal legal status and enhances its authority.

---

### **15. Informal and Expedient Proceedings**

- All proceedings shall be conducted as **informally and expeditiously as possible**, consistent with **fairness and due process**.
- This promotes efficiency without compromising justice.

---

### **16. Intervention Rights**

- Any person may **intervene** in proceedings before the Tribunal (except those under Part VII.1 of the Competition Act).
- Intervention is allowed **with leave of the Tribunal**.
- The purpose is to allow parties affected by the matter to make **relevant representations**.

> Enhances public participation and ensures broader stakeholder input.

---

### **17. Summary Proceedings**

- On a motion by a party, a judicial member may hear and decide an application **in a summary manner**, following applicable rules on summary dispositions.
- The member may:
  - **Dismiss** the application if no genuine basis exists.
  - **Allow** it in whole or in part if no genuine basis exists for the response.
- This streamlines processing of straightforward or routine applications.

> Promotes efficiency in handling less complex cases.

---

### **18. Composition of Hearings**

- **General Rule**: Every application must be heard by **not less than three and no more than five members**.
  - At least **one judicial member**.
  - At least **one lay member**.
- **Exception**: In certain cases, the Tribunal may hear matters alone (see below).

> Ensures balanced decision-making with both legal and non-legal perspectives.
