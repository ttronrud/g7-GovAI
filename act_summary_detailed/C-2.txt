**Detailed Summary of the *Canada Council for the Arts Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Canada Council for the Arts Act* establishes the **Canada Council for the Arts (CCA)** as a federal corporation dedicated to fostering, promoting, and supporting the arts in Canada. The Act outlines the structure, governance, funding mechanisms, objectives, and operational powers of the Council, ensuring its independence from direct government control while providing it with financial and administrative support.

---

### **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

#### **1. Citation and Definitions**
- The Act is known as the *Canada Council for the Arts Act*.
- **"Arts"** is broadly defined to include:
  - Architecture
  - Theatre arts
  - Literature
  - Music
  - Painting
  - Sculpture
  - Graphic arts
  - And other similar creative and interpretative activities  
  *(This broad definition ensures inclusivity across artistic disciplines.)*

- **"Council"** refers to the corporation established under Section 3.

---

#### **2. Establishment of the Council**
- A **corporation** called the *Canada Council for the Arts* is created.
- It consists of:
  - A **Chairperson**
  - A **Vice-Chairperson**
  - Up to **nine other members**
- All members are appointed by the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet).

---

#### **3. Terms of Office and Eligibility**
- **Chairperson and Vice-Chairperson**:
  - Appointed for terms **not exceeding five years**.
  - Terms may be renewed.
- **Other members**:
  - Appointed for **four-year terms**.
- **Restriction on Reappointment**:
  - A person who has served **two consecutive terms** in any of the following roles:
    - Chairperson
    - Vice-Chairperson
    - Member (appointed under subsection 4(2))
  - Is **not eligible** to be re-appointed to the Council in the same capacity for **twelve months** after completing their second term.
  - This provision promotes diversity and prevents long-term dominance by individuals.

---

#### **4. Appointment of the Director**
- The Council appoints a **Director**, who serves at the pleasure of the Governor in Council.
- The **Director is the chief executive officer** of the Council, responsible for day-to-day management and implementation of the Council’s mandate.

---

#### **5. Compensation and Expenses**
- **Salary/remuneration** for the Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, and Director is set by the Governor in Council.
- **Members** (non-officer members) receive a daily allowance for attending Council meetings.
- All members and the Director are entitled to **reasonable travel and living expenses** incurred while performing duties away from their ordinary place of residence.

---

#### **6. Employment and Public Service Status**
- The **Director and employees** of the Council are deemed to be **employed in the public service** for the purposes of the *Public Service Superannuation Act*.
- The Council is considered a **Public Service corporation** under Section 37 of that Act, which provides benefits such as superannuation and pension coverage.
- However, **the Council is not an agent of Her Majesty** (the Crown), and:
  - Its members, Director, and employees are **not part of the federal public administration**.
  - This establishes the Council’s **independence** from direct government bureaucracy and administrative oversight.

---

#### **7. Core Objectives of the Council**
The Council is empowered to achieve the following goals:
1. **Foster and promote** the study, enjoyment, and production of the arts.
2. **Assist, cooperate with, and enlist** organizations with similar objectives.
3. **Provide grants, scholarships, or loans** to:
   - Individuals in Canada for study or research in the arts (domestic or international).
   - Individuals abroad for study or research in Canadian arts.
4. **Make awards** to Canadian artists for outstanding accomplishments.
5. **Sponsor exhibitions, performances, and publications** of artistic works.
6. **Provide capital grants** to universities and similar institutions for **building construction projects**.
7. **Exchange knowledge and information** about the arts with other countries or organizations.
8. **Represent and interpret Canadian arts internationally**.

> These objectives are broad and flexible, allowing the Council to adapt to evolving artistic and cultural needs.

---

#### **8. International Engagement**
- The Governor in Council may assign the Council **functions related to the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO)**.
- This allows the Council to participate in global cultural diplomacy and international cooperation in the arts.

---

#### **9. By-Laws and Internal Governance**
- The Council may adopt **by-laws** to:
  - Regulate its meetings and procedures
  - Govern its operations
  - Appoint honorary officers and advisory committees
- This gives the Council flexibility in internal management while maintaining accountability.

---

#### **10. Meeting Requirements**
- The Council must meet **at least three times per year** in **Ottawa**.
- Meetings can occur on days fixed by the Council and may be held at other times or places as deemed necessary.

---

#### **11. Funding and Financial Mechanisms**

##### **Endowment Fund**
- The **Minister of Finance** is authorized to contribute **C$50 million** to the Council from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund**.
- This sum forms the **Endowment Fund**, which is the primary source of long-term funding.
- **Expenditures** (except for specific grants under paragraph 8(1)(e)) may be funded from:
  - The **return on investments** in the Endowment Fund
  - **Gifts, bequests, or other property** available for the purposes of the Act

> This ensures sustainable, non-recurring funding through investment returns.

##### **University Capital Grants Fund**
- The Council establishes a separate fund called the **University Capital Grants Fund**.
- The **same C$50 million** is allocated to this fund by the Minister of Finance.
- **Grants made under paragraph 8(1)(e)** (i.e., capital assistance for university construction) may be paid from this fund, but with strict limits:
  - For any **individual project**, the grant may not exceed **50% of total project expenditures**.
  - For any **province**, the amount granted must be in the **same proportion** as the province’s population relative to the total population of all provinces with universities.

> This ensures equitable distribution of funds based on population and institutional presence.

##### **Investment Rules**
- Investments in the Endowment Fund or University Capital Grants Fund may only be made in:
  - **Government of Canada bonds or securities**
  - Or securities **guaranteed by the Government of Canada**
- Proceeds from the sale or disposal of such investments are **recredited to the original fund** (i.e., no net loss or diversion).

> This ensures financial stability and risk mitigation.

##### **Other Property and Gifts**
- The Council may:
  - Acquire, hold, manage, and dispose of **real and personal property**.
  - Invest money (including gifts or bequests) in any manner it sees fit — **subject to advice from the Investment Committee**.
- It may **expend, administer, or dispose of** any property not part of the Endowment or University Capital Grants Fund, **subject to any conditions** attached to the gift or bequest.

> This provides flexibility in managing additional assets while respecting donor intent.

---

#### **12. Tax Status**
- For the purposes of the *Income Tax Act*, the Council is deemed to be a **registered charity**.
- This status allows it to:
  - Receive tax-exempt donations
  - Deduct charitable contributions
  - Operate without corporate income tax on qualifying activities

---

#### **13. Financial Accountability and Reporting**
- The Council’s **accounts and financial transactions are audited annually** by the **Auditor General of Canada**.
- The audit report is submitted to:
  - The Council
  - The **member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada** designated under subsection 21(1)

> This ensures transparency and accountability to the government and Parliament.

##### **Annual Report Requirement**
- The **Chairperson** must submit to the designated Privy Council member:
  - A report of all proceedings under the Act for the fiscal year
  - Including:
    - Financial statements
    - Auditor General’s report
- This report must be **laid before Parliament** within **15 days** of receipt (or on the first day after Parliament resumes if not sitting).
- Parliament is required to **review** the report.

> This creates a formal mechanism for parliamentary oversight.

---

#### **14. Transition and Repeal of Investment Committee**
- Every person who was a member of the **Investment Committee** immediately before the coming into force of **Section 9** of this Act **ceases to hold office** upon that date.
- This reflects a structural reform to ensure updated governance and independence in investment decisions.

> The repeal of the Investment Committee’s continuity ensures that the Council can adapt its investment strategy without legacy constraints.

---

| Feature | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| **Independence** | The Council is not part of federal public administration, allowing it to act autonomously in cultural policy. |
| **Broad Definition of "Arts"** | Covers diverse creative fields, ensuring inclusivity and relevance. |
| **Endowment and Capital Grants** | Provides long-term financial stability and targeted support for university infrastructure. |
| **Equitable Funding** | University grants are distributed based on population, promoting fairness. |
