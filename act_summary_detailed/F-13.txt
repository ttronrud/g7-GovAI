**Detailed Summary of the *Freshwater Fish Marketing Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Freshwater Fish Marketing Act* establishes the **Freshwater Fish Marketing Corporation (Corporation)** as a federal agency tasked with promoting and regulating the marketing, trade, and distribution of freshwater fish and related products in Canada. The Act provides a comprehensive framework for the Corporation’s operations, including its governance structure, powers, financial mechanisms, trade restrictions, and enforcement provisions.

It was designed to ensure **orderly interprovincial and international trade in freshwater fish**, **increase returns to commercial fishermen**, and **promote sustainable and fair market conditions**. The Act also includes provisions for inspection, compliance, penalties, and cooperation between federal and provincial governments.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Definitions and Key Terms**

The Act defines several key terms used throughout:

- **Board**: The Board of Directors of the Corporation.  
- **Chairperson**: The Chair of the Board (note: this office was repealed in 2006; the current term is "Chairperson" in English only).  
- **President**: The chief executive officer and general manager of the Corporation.  
- **Corporation**: The Freshwater Fish Marketing Corporation established under section 3(1).  
- **Minister**: A designated member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada, responsible for overseeing the Act.  
- **Participating Province**: A province with which the Government of Canada has entered into an agreement under section 24.  
- **Container**: Any packaging, wrapper, or confining band used in packing or marketing fish.  
- **Fish**: Round, dressed, or filleted freshwater fish (of species listed in the Schedule), whether fresh or frozen, packaged or not, fished commercially in a participating province. Includes parts of such fish.  
- **Fisherman**: A person licensed under the *Fisheries Act* to fish commercially in a participating province, or someone acting on behalf of two or more such licensed individuals.

---

### **2. Establishment of the Corporation**

- The Corporation is established as a **corporation with a Board of Directors** composed of:
  - One **Chairperson**
  - One **President**
  - One director for **each participating province**
  - Four additional directors (non-provincial)

#### **Appointment of Directors**
- **Chairperson and President**: Appointed by the **Governor in Council** for a term deemed appropriate (during "pleasure").
- **Other Directors**:
  - Appointed by the **Minister**, with **Governor in Council approval**.
  - Term **not exceeding four years**, with a policy to ensure **no more than half of the directors’ terms expire in any one year**.
  - Directors for participating provinces are **recommended by the Lieutenant Governor in Council** of that province.
- **Age Limit**: No person over **70 years of age** may be appointed as a director. A director ceases to serve upon reaching age 70.
- **Re-appointment**: A director who is not disqualified by age may be reappointed after their term ends.

#### **Vacancies**
- A vacancy on the Board **does not impair** the functioning of the remaining directors.
- If the vacancy involves a **provincial director**, it must be filled **as soon as practicable** in accordance with the rules.

#### **Temporary Substitutes**
- If a director (excluding Chairperson or President) is absent or incapacitated, the **Governor in Council** may appoint a **temporary substitute** under prescribed conditions.

#### **Presiding Officers**
- The **Chairperson presides** at Board meetings.
- If absent or incapacitated, or if the office is vacant, the **President presides**.

#### **Roles and Responsibilities**
- The **President** is the **chief executive officer** and has **full authority** to manage the Corporation’s business, except where specifically reserved for the Board.
- In the event of the President’s absence or vacancy, the **Board may authorize an officer or director** to act as President for up to **60 days**, but only with **Governor in Council approval**.
- The President is paid a **salary set by the Governor in Council**.
- Other directors receive **fees for attending meetings**, also set by the Governor in Council.
- Directors are entitled to **travel and living expenses** for duties, as fixed by **Board by-law**.

---

### **3. Powers and Functions of the Corporation**

The Corporation is granted broad powers to carry out its mandate of marketing and trading freshwater fish. These include:

1. **Buying and Processing Fish**:
   - Purchase fish and perform dressing, filleting, freezing, packaging, or other preparation for market.

2. **Producing Fish Products and By-Products**:
   - Manufacture or produce fish products and by-products and prepare them for market.

3. **Storage, Transportation, and Disposal**:
   - Store, ship, insure, import, export, market, sell, or otherwise dispose of fish and products.

4. **Real Property**:
   - Purchase, lease, or hold real property; sell or otherwise deal with it.

5. **Branches and Agents**:
   - Establish branches or employ agents in Canada or abroad.

6. **Investments**:
   - Invest surplus funds in **Government of Canada securities** (or those guaranteed by the Government), and reinvest proceeds.

7. **Borrowing and Lending**:
   - Borrow money from banks on the Corporation’s credit.
   - Make **seasonal working capital loans** to commercial fishermen in participating provinces.

8. **Other Powers**:
   - Do all other acts necessary or incidental to fulfilling its functions.

> The Corporation may also:
> - Enter into agreements with governments or private parties.
> - Receive grants, rights, franchises, privileges, or concessions.
> - Employ officers and employees.
> - Set duties, terms, and conditions of employment via **Board by-laws**.
> - Pay employees based on Board by-laws.

---

### **4. Public Service Status**

- The **President and officers/employees** are deemed to be **employed in the public service** under the *Public Service Superannuation Act*.
- The Corporation is deemed a **Public Service corporation** under section 37 of that Act.
- For **Government Employees Compensation Act** and *Aeronautics Act* purposes, the President and employees are considered part of **federal public administration**.
- **Exception**: Directors and officers are **not considered part of the federal public administration** unless they are employees.

---

### **5. Board By-Laws**

The Board may adopt by-laws on matters including:
- Calling and conducting meetings (including committee formation and delegation of duties).
- Fixing travel and living expenses for directors (subject to **Treasury Board approval**).
- Defining duties and conduct of directors.
- General management and governance of the Corporation.

---

### **6. Head Office and Jurisdiction**

- The **head office** is located in **Winnipeg, Manitoba**, or in the immediate vicinity.
- Board meetings may be held **anywhere in Canada** as determined by the Board.

---

### **7. Status as an Agent of Her Majesty**

- The Corporation is treated as an **agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada** for all purposes under this Act.

---

### **8. Financial Self-Sufficiency**

- The Corporation operates on a **self-sustaining financial basis** and **does not receive direct appropriations from Parliament**.

---

### **9. Grants in Lieu of Taxes**

- The Corporation may make **grants to municipalities** (in Canada) to replace taxes that would otherwise be levied on Corporation-owned land.
- Grants are limited to the amount of taxes that would have been collected if the Corporation were not an agent of the Crown.

---

### **10. Financial Support from the Minister of Finance**

With **Governor in Council authorization**, the **Minister of Finance** may:
- **Guarantee repayment** of loans made by banks to the Corporation (including interest).
- **Lend money** to the Corporation.

> **Limitation**: The **total outstanding debt** (loans from banks + loans from Minister of Finance) **shall not exceed CAD 50 million** at any time.

*(Note: These provisions were repealed in 2010, c. 12, s. 1738 — this appears to be a typographical or structural error in the original text, as the section is marked for repeal. It may be an outdated or obsolete clause.)*

---

### **11. Restrictions on Trade (Interprovincial and Export Trade)**

**No person (other than the Corporation or its agents)** may:
- Export fish from Canada.
- Send or carry fish from one participating province to another or outside Canada.
- Receive fish in a participating province for transport outside that province.
- Sell or buy fish in a participating province for delivery in another province or outside Canada.

> **Exception**: These restrictions apply **only if not done in accordance with a licence issued by the Corporation**.

#### **Licence Authority**
- The Board may issue **licences** for trade activities.
- By-laws may prescribe the **form, terms, and conditions** of such licences.

#### **Regulatory Flexibility**
- The Governor in Council may, by regulation:
  - Add or remove fish species from the Schedule.
  - Exempt any species, area, transaction, or person from the restrictions **conditionally or unconditionally**, for a specified period or generally.

---

### **12. Exclusive Right to Trade in Fish**

- The Corporation has the **exclusive right** to trade in and market fish in:
  - **Intraprovincial trade**
  - **Export trade**
