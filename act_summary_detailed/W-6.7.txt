### **Detailed Summary of the Western Arctic (Inuvialuit) Claims Settlement Act**

---

#### **Overview**

The **Western Arctic (Inuvialuit) Claims Settlement Act** is a foundational piece of Canadian legislation that legally enacts and gives effect to the **Inuvialuit Claims Agreement** of 1984 â€” a landmark land and resource settlement between the **Inuvialuit people** and the **Government of Canada**. The Act formalizes the surrender of Inuvialuit native title claims in exchange for a comprehensive package of rights, benefits, and compensation, particularly in the **Inuvialuit Settlement Region (ISR)** of the **Northwest Territories and Yukon**.

This Act was enacted to provide legal certainty, recognize the Inuvialuitâ€™s traditional rights, and ensure their full and meaningful participation in economic, cultural, and social life in the Arctic region.

### **Key Provisions and Detailed Analysis**

#### **1. Background and Context (Whereas Clauses)**

The Act begins with several "whereas" clauses that establish the historical, legal, and policy context:

- **Traditional Use and Occupancy**: The Inuvialuit have historically occupied and used lands in the Northwest Territories and Yukon based on traditional practices (hunting, fishing, trapping).
- **Government Assumption of Obligations**: In return for surrendering their native title, the Government of Canada assumed specific obligations under the Agreement â€” including land grants, hunting/fishing rights, cultural preservation, and financial compensation.
- **Scope of Rights and Benefits**: The Agreement includes:
  - Granting of land (for settlement and development).
  - Rights to hunt, fish, trap, and engage in commercial activities within the ISR.
  - Protection of Inuvialuit cultural identity and values.
  - Equal participation in the northern and national economy and society.
  - Environmental protection of Arctic wildlife, ecosystems, and biological productivity.
  - Financial compensation to the Inuvialuit.
- **Surrender of Native Claims**: In exchange for these benefits, the Inuvialuit formally surrender all native claims, rights, title, and interests in the Territory.
- **Consultation with Territorial Governments**: The governments of the Northwest Territories and Yukon were consulted and participated in discussions, recognizing their jurisdiction over local matters.
- **Legislative Necessity**: It is deemed expedient for Parliament to approve, validate, and give legal force to the Agreement.

#### **3. Legal Effect of the Agreement**

- **Approval and Validity**: The Act **formally approves, gives effect to, and declares valid** the Agreement. This means that the Agreement is now a binding legal instrument under Canadian law.
- **Rights Upon Extinguishment of Claims**: Once native claims are extinguished, the beneficiaries (the Inuvialuit) are entitled to **all rights, privileges, and benefits** outlined in the Agreement â€” including:
  - Land titles granted under **Section 7(1) and (2)** of the Agreement.
  - Rights to hunt, fish, trap, and conduct commercial activities.
  - Cultural and environmental protections.

#### **4. Extinction of Native Title**

- **Full Surrender of Claims**: All native claims, rights, title, and interests of Inuvialuit individuals or groups in the Territory are **formally extinguished**.
- **Important Exception**: Despite the extinguishment of native title:
  - Inuvialuit retain their status as **Canadian citizens**.
  - They continue to enjoy **all rights and benefits of citizenship**, including access to public services, social programs, and federal protections.
  - Their **aboriginal status** remains intact, and they continue to benefit from **other relevant legislation** (e.g., the *Indian Act*, *Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Canada* policies, and other aboriginal rights laws).
