**Detailed Summary of the Statutes Repeal Act**

---

### **Overview**
The *Statutes Repeal Act* is a legislative measure introduced by the Government of Canada to systematically review and repeal outdated or obsolete Acts of Parliament. It establishes a structured, annual process by which the Government identifies and repeals provisions of laws that have remained unenacted for a significant periodâ€”specifically, nine years or moreâ€”since their assent, and that were never brought into force by proclamation or order.

The primary objective of the Act is to **modernize the statute book**, eliminate obsolete or redundant legislation, reduce legal complexity, and improve legislative clarity and efficiency in Canadian law.

---

### **Key Provisions and Detailed Explanation**

#### **1. Citation of the Act**
- The Act is officially titled: **"The Statutes Repeal Act"**.
- This title reflects its purpose: to repeal statutes that are no longer in force or have become obsolete.

---

#### **2. Annual Report Requirement**
- **Who**: The Minister of Justice is responsible for preparing and delivering the annual report.
- **When**: The report must be laid before both the **Senate and the House of Commons** on **any of the first five days** on which either House sits in a given calendar year.
- **Content of the Report**:
  - The report must list every **Act of Parliament** or **provision within an Act** that:
    1. Was **assented to nine years or more before the December 31 of the year immediately preceding the laying of the report**; and  
    2. Has **not come into force by December 31 of that preceding year**.
    - In other words, the law was passed and approved (assented to) at least nine years ago, but it was never activated (i.e., never came into force) by proclamation or order from the Governor in Council.

#### **3. Automatic Repeal of Listed Acts/Provisions**
- Every Act or provision listed in the annual report is **repealed on December 31 of the year in which the report is laid**, **unless**:
  - It comes into force **on or before December 31** of that year; or  
  - During that year (i.e., within the calendar year the report is laid), **either House of Parliament adopts a resolution** stating that the Act or provision **should not be repealed**.

#### **4. Publication in the Canada Gazette**
- The Minister of Justice must publish **each year** in the *Canada Gazette* a list of all Acts or provisions that were **repealed on the preceding December 31** under this Act.
- This ensures **public transparency** and provides a clear, official record of legislative changes.

#### **5. Exemption from Repeal (Section 2 Exclusion)**
- **Section 2 does not apply** to:
  1. Any provision that was **amended by an Act that was assented to during the nine calendar years before the year this Act comes into force**; or  
  2. Any provision that is **necessary for the amended provision to have effect**, until the calendar year following the **ninth anniversary of the assent** of the amending Act.

### **Purpose and Significance**

The *Statutes Repeal Act* serves several important functions:

1. **Legal Modernization**: Removes laws that are no longer relevant, outdated, or redundant, thereby streamlining the legislative framework.
2. **Transparency and Accountability**: The annual report and public publication in the *Canada Gazette* ensure that the public and Parliament are informed about which laws are being removed.
3. **Parliamentary Oversight**: The ability of either House to block a repeal through a resolution gives Parliament a mechanism to preserve laws deemed important or necessary.
4. **Protection of Legislative Integrity**: The exemption for recently amended laws prevents the loss of critical legal changes due to automatic repeal.
5. **Efficiency in Law Reform**: By focusing on laws that have been dormant for over nine years, the Act targets obsolete statutes without disrupting current legal systems.

---

### **Limitations and Considerations**

- **No automatic repeal of active laws**: Only laws that have not come into force and were passed more than nine years ago are affected.
- **Parliamentary discretion**: The power to block repeal through a resolution ensures that important or practical laws can survive even if they are long dormant.
- **Exclusion of amendments**: The protection for provisions amended within the last nine years ensures that legal reforms are not undone by the repeal mechanism.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Statutes Repeal Act* is a well-designed, proactive tool for legislative streamlining. It enables Canada to maintain a clean, up-to-date statute book by identifying and repealing long-dormant laws. Through a structured, transparent, and accountable process involving the Minister of Justice, Parliament, and the public, the Act balances the need for legal modernization with the need to preserve essential or recently updated provisions.

It reflects a broader commitment to **legal efficiency**, **good governance**, and **public accountability** in the Canadian legal system.
