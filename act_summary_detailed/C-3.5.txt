### **Detailed Summary of the Canada Disability Savings Act (2007)**

---

#### **Overview and Purpose**

The **Canada Disability Savings Act**, enacted under **Section 136 of Chapter 35 of the Statutes of Canada, 2007**, came into force on **December 1, 2008** (as per SI/2008-63). The Act is designed to **encourage long-term financial savings** for individuals with severe and prolonged physical or mental impairments through **registered disability savings plans (RDSPs)**. Its primary objective is to **enhance the financial security** of persons with disabilities by enabling them to build a savings pool that can support their future needs.

The Act establishes two key financial instruments:
- **Canada Disability Savings Grant (CDSG)**
- **Canada Disability Savings Bond (CDSB)**

These are provided by the federal government to help beneficiaries (individuals with disabilities) grow their savings over time, with eligibility based on income, age, and residency status.

---

## **Key Definitions and Key Terms**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Canada Child Benefit (CCB)** | Repealed in 2016. Previously used in eligibility criteria. |
| **Canada Disability Savings Bond (CDSB)** | A fixed or variable amount paid into an RDSP, based on income thresholds. |
| **Canada Disability Savings Grant (CDSG)** | A grant paid into an RDSP to encourage contributions, with tiered rates based on contribution levels and income. |
| **Family Income** | Determined by the Minister using adjusted income from the Income Tax Act (Section 122.6), adjusted for the relevant year. |
| **First Threshold** | The income level defined in paragraph 117(2)(b) of the Income Tax Act, adjusted annually. |
| **Phase-out Income** | A transitional income level used before 2017; after 2017, replaced by a new threshold in subsection 122.61(1)(q). |
| **Second Threshold** | The higher income limit defined in paragraph 117(2)(b) of the Income Tax Act, adjusted annually. |
| **Eligible Individual / Qualified Dependents** | As defined in Section 146.4 of the Income Tax Act. |
| **DTC-Eligible Individual** | A person who qualifies under the Disability Tax Credit system. |
| **Specified Disability Savings Plan (SDSP)** | A plan designated by the Minister as not eligible for CDSG or CDSB. |

> All terms not otherwise defined have the same meaning as in the Income Tax Act unless otherwise specified.

## **Core Provisions and Mechanisms**

---

### **1. Canada Disability Savings Grant (CDSG)**

#### **Eligibility Criteria**
A CDSG may be paid if the beneficiary meets **one of the following conditions**:
- An individual aged **18 or older** on December 31 of the year before the relevant year, and whose **family income ≤ second threshold** for that year.
- A **qualified dependant** of an eligible individual whose **adjusted income (for Canada child benefit purposes)** in January of the year ≤ second threshold.
- A person receiving a **special allowance** under the *Children’s Special Allowances Act* for at least one month in the year.

> *Note:* The Canada Child Benefit was repealed in 2016, but its income definitions are still referenced in eligibility rules.

#### **Grant Amounts (Tiered Structure)**
The CDSG amount depends on the total contributions made in the year and the beneficiary’s eligibility:

| Contribution Level | Grant Rate |
|-------------------|-----------|
| Up to $500 | 300% of contributions |
| Over $500 to $1,500 | 200% of contributions |
| Over $1,500 | 100% of total contributions (up to a maximum of $1,000) |

> This means:
> - For contributions ≤ $500: 300% = e.g., $150 for $500
> - For contributions between $501–$1,500: 200% = e.g., $200 for $1,000
> - For contributions > $1,500: only 100% up to $1,000 max

#### **Allocation of Contributions (Backward Allocation)**
Contributions made **after 2010** can be **allocated retroactively** to up to **10 prior years** (after 2007), provided:
- The plan was **not** a specified disability savings plan during those years.
- The beneficiary was **resident in Canada** during those years.
- The allocation follows a **priority order**:
  1. Up to $500 per year for beneficiaries meeting condition (2)(a)
  2. Up to $1,500 per year for those same beneficiaries
  3. Up to $1,000 per year for others

> Contributions from **2008–2010** are considered allocated to the year they were made.

#### **Limits**
- **Lifetime maximum**: Not more than **$70,000** in CDSGs.
- **Annual maximum**: Not more than **$10,500** per year.

> The $10,500 cap is derived from the sum of the maximum grants across the tiered structure and is applied only when the beneficiary qualifies under the full tiered formula.

### **2. Canada Disability Savings Bond (CDSB)**

#### **Eligibility Criteria**
A CDSB may be paid if the beneficiary meets one of the following:
- An individual aged **18+** on Dec 31 of the year before the year, and **family income ≤ phase-out income**.
- A qualified dependant with adjusted income ≤ phase-out income in January of the year.
- A person receiving a special allowance for at least one month in the year.

> If the beneficiary’s income is **above phase-out income but below first threshold**, a **formula-based bond** is calculated.

#### **Bond Amounts**
- **If income ≤ phase-out income**: $1,000
- **If income between phase-out and first threshold**:  
  Formula:  
  $$
  \text{Bond Amount} = \left( \frac{\text{Family Income} - \text{Phase-out Income}}{\text{First Threshold} - \text{Phase-out Income}} \right) \times (\text{First Threshold} - \text{Phase-out Income}) + \text{Phase-out Income}
  $$  
  → This results in a **linear increase** from $1,000 to $1,000 + (proportional increase based on income)

> Example: If phase-out = $20,000, first threshold = $50,000, and family income = $30,000, then:
> - (30,000 – 20,000) / (50,000 – 20,000) = 1/3
