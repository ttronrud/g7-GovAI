**Detailed Summary of the *Olympic and Paralympic Marks Act* (Canada)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Olympic and Paralympic Marks Act* is a Canadian legislative instrument enacted to protect the integrity and exclusive use of official Olympic and Paralympic marks. The Act establishes legal rules governing the use, adoption, and commercialization of marks associated with the Olympic and Paralympic Games, ensuring that these prestigious symbols are not misused or exploited for commercial gain by unauthorized entities. It balances the need for brand protection with exceptions for legitimate usesâ€”such as by organizing committees, government bodies, athletes, and in non-commercial contexts like news reporting or artistic expression.

The Act came into force on **December 17, 2007**, with certain provisions (notably Section 13) taking effect on **December 31, 2010**.

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Citation and Purpose**
- The Act is titled: **The Olympic and Paralympic Marks Act**.
- Its primary purpose is to **protect the distinctiveness and integrity** of Olympic and Paralympic marks by restricting unauthorized commercial use.

---

### **2. Definitions**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **COC** | The **Canadian Olympic Committee**, a corporation incorporated under the *Companies Act, 1934*. |
| **CPC** | The **Canadian Paralympic Committee**, a corporation incorporated under the *Canada Corporations Act*. |
| **Court** | Refers to either the **Federal Court** or a **provincial superior court**. |
| **Olympic or Paralympic mark** | A mark listed in **Schedule 1 or 2**. This includes the official Olympic and Paralympic emblems and associated designations. |
| **Organizing Committee** | Any organization recognized by the COC and a host city as responsible for planning, financing, and staging the Olympic or Paralympic Games. |
| **General Meaning** | Words and expressions are interpreted according to the *Trademarks Act*, unless otherwise defined. |

> *Note:* The Act recognizes that certain marks will expire after a specified date (as per Schedule 2), and those expired marks are no longer considered Olympic or Paralympic marks.

### **3. Prohibition of Unauthorized Use**

#### **Section 3: Prohibition of Adoption or Use**
> No person may:
> - Adopt or use in connection with a business (as a trademark or otherwise) an **Olympic or Paralympic mark**;
> - Use a mark that **so closely resembles** an Olympic or Paralympic mark that it is likely to be mistaken for it.

#### **Section 4: Prohibition of Translations**
> No person may use a **translation in any language** of an Olympic or Paralympic mark in connection with a business.

### **4. Prohibition of Misleading Promotion (Section 5)**

> No person may promote or direct public attention to their business in a way that:
> - Misleads the public into believing their business is **approved, authorized, or endorsed** by:
>   - An organizing committee,
>   - The COC,
>   - The CPC,
>   - Or any business association with the Games.

### **5. Remedies for Infringement (Section 6)**

If a court finds an act violates Section 3 or 4, it may issue any appropriate remedy, including:
- **Injunction** (preventing future use),
- **Damages or profits**,
- **Punitive damages**,
- **Corrective advertising** (to retract false claims),
- **Destruction, exportation, or other disposition** of offending goods, packaging, labels, and advertising materials,
- **Destruction or removal of equipment** used to apply the prohibited mark.

> âš ï¸ **Limitation on Time**: No remedy can be awarded for acts committed **more than three years before the filing of the action**.
