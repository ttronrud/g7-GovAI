**Detailed Summary of the *Canada–EFTA Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *Canada–EFTA Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act* (the "Act") is a Canadian legislative instrument enacted to implement the **Canada–European Free Trade Association (EFTA) Free Trade Agreement**, signed on **January 26, 2008**, between Canada and the four EFTA member states: **Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, and Switzerland**.

This Act provides the legal framework, definitions, enforcement mechanisms, and administrative responsibilities necessary for Canada to fulfill its obligations under the Agreement and its associated bilateral agreements—particularly those related to agriculture.

The Act was passed by the **Parliament of Canada** with the advice and consent of both the **Senate and House of Commons**, and it came into force on **July 1, 2009**, as noted in the statutory instrument (SI/2009-38).

---

### **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

#### **1. Citation and Purpose**
- The Act is formally cited as the **Canada–EFTA Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act**.
- Its **primary purpose** is to implement the **Free Trade Agreement (FTA)** and the **three bilateral agriculture agreements** between Canada and each EFTA state.

The objectives of the Agreement and the Act include:
- Establishing a **free trade area** between Canada and the EFTA states.
- Promoting **reciprocal trade** to foster economic development in both Canada and the EFTA countries.
- Ensuring **fair and non-discriminatory conditions of competition** in trade.
- Creating a **framework for future cooperation**, especially in services and investment.
- Contributing to the **global expansion of world trade** by removing trade barriers.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

The Act establishes clear definitions essential to its interpretation and application:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Agreement** | The main Free Trade Agreement between Canada and the EFTA states (Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, Switzerland), signed on January 26, 2008. |
| **Bilateral Agreement** | Three separate agreements on agriculture signed with each EFTA country on the same date: <br> - Canada–Iceland <br> - Canada–Norway <br> - Canada–Switzerland |
| **EFTA State** | A member of the European Free Trade Association: <br> - Republic of Iceland <br> - Principality of Liechtenstein <br> - Kingdom of Norway <br> - Swiss Confederation |
| **Federal Law** | Any Act of Parliament or regulatory instrument (orders, regulations, etc.) made under the authority of an Act of Parliament. |
| **Joint Committee** | A committee established under **Article 26 of the Agreement** to oversee implementation and resolve disputes. |

> These definitions ensure clarity and consistency in how the Act and the Agreement are interpreted.

---

#### **3. Publication of the Agreement**
- The **Agreement and the bilateral agriculture agreements** are to be **published in the Canada Treaty Series**, making them publicly accessible and legally binding.

---

#### **4. Interpretation Principle (For Greater Certainty)**

- Any provision of this Act, any Act amended or enacted under **Part 2**, or any other federal law that implements the Agreement or bilateral agreements **must be interpreted in a manner consistent with the Agreement or bilateral agreement**.
- This principle ensures that Canadian law aligns with the terms of the trade agreement and prevents conflicts or divergences.

---

#### **5. Legal Binding and Enforcement Provisions**

- The Act is **binding on Her Majesty in right of Canada**, meaning it has full legal force and effect.
- **No individual or entity** may bring legal proceedings or enforce rights arising solely from:
  - **Part 1 of the Act** (which contains general provisions)
  - The **Agreement or bilateral agreements**
- Such enforcement must be authorized by the **Attorney General of Canada**.
- This provision is designed to **prevent private litigation** over trade obligations under the Agreement, ensuring that disputes are handled through formal, diplomatic, or institutional channels (e.g., the Joint Committee or arbitration).

> This reflects a common feature in international trade agreements: the **non-enforceability of individual rights** to avoid undermining the stability and negotiation process of the agreement.

---

#### **6. Exclusions and Limitations**

- The Act **does not apply to natural surface or ground water**, whether in liquid, gaseous, or solid form.
- This explicitly excludes environmental or natural resource rights related to water from the scope of the Agreement.
- The Act **does not limit Parliament’s authority** to pass new laws to implement the Agreement or fulfill Canada’s obligations.
- This ensures that **Parliament retains full legislative power** to adapt domestic laws as needed to meet international trade requirements.

---

#### **7. Approval of the Agreement**

- The **Agreement and the bilateral agreements are formally approved** by this Act, signifying their legal acceptance and integration into Canadian law.

---

#### **8. Role of the Minister**

- The **Minister for International Trade** is designated as the **principal representative of Canada on the Joint Committee**.
- The Minister has key responsibilities:
  - Appointing **representatives to subcommittees** established under Article 9 of the Agreement or under Article 26 (Joint Committee).
  - Designating a **government agency, division, or branch** to provide **administrative support** to arbitral tribunals established under **Chapter VIII** of the Agreement (which deals with dispute resolution).
- The Minister may appoint individuals to serve on **subcommittees or working groups** as needed.

---

#### **9. Financial Obligations**

- The **Government of Canada is responsible for paying**:
  - Its **appropriate share** of the **expenses incurred by the Joint Committee**.
  - The **remuneration and expenses** of members of:
    - Arbitral tribunals
    - Subcommittees
    - Working groups
  - The **general operating costs** of these bodies.

> This ensures that Canada contributes fairly to the administrative and operational costs of implementing and managing the Agreement.

---

#### **10. Suspension of Benefits or Obligations (Governor in Council Powers)**

- The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the Cabinet, acting on ministerial advice) may, under **Article 31 of the Agreement**, issue orders to **suspend or modify** certain benefits or obligations in specific circumstances.
- The powers include:
  - Suspending rights or privileges granted to an EFTA state or its goods under the Agreement or federal law.
  - Modifying or suspending the application of federal laws enacted under Part 2 (i.e., laws implementing the Agreement) for EFTA states or their goods.
  - Extending the application of federal laws to EFTA states or their goods.
  - Taking any other measure deemed necessary.

> This provision provides **flexibility in times of crisis**, such as trade disruptions, public health emergencies, or market instability, allowing Canada to protect domestic interests without violating the spirit of the Agreement.

- Such orders remain in effect **until revoked** and are valid for the duration specified in the order.

---

#### **11. Commencement of the Act**

- The provisions of the Act **come into force on a day or days to be fixed by order of the Governor in Council**.
- The Act officially came into force on **July 1, 2009**, as confirmed in the statutory instrument SI/2009-38.

---

### **Significance and Context**

This Act is a cornerstone of Canada’s engagement with **EFTA countries**, particularly in the area of **agricultural trade**. While Canada is not part of the European Union, it has established formal trade relationships with key non-EU European economies through this Agreement.

Key features of the Act reflect modern international trade law principles:
- **Non-discrimination** and **fair competition**
- **Cooperative dispute resolution mechanisms** (via Joint Committee and arbitration)
- **Clarity in roles and responsibilities**
- **Legal certainty and stability** through defined interpretation rules
- **Government accountability** through financial and procedural obligations

It also demonstrates Canada’s commitment to **economic integration** and **global trade liberalization**, even with non-EU partners.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Canada–EFTA Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act* serves as the **legal backbone** for Canada’s trade relationship with Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, and Switzerland. It enables Canada to:
- Implement the Free Trade Agreement and its agriculture-specific components,
- Ensure fair and transparent trade conditions,
- Establish structured cooperation and dispute resolution mechanisms,
- Maintain flexibility in response to changing economic or political conditions,
- And uphold the rule of law and consistency in international trade obligations.

By clearly defining roles, responsibilities, and enforcement mechanisms, the Act supports a stable, predictable, and mutually beneficial trade relationship between Canada and the EFTA states.
