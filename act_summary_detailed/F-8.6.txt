### **Detailed Summary of the Federal Sustainable Development Act**

---

#### **1. Act Title and Citation**
The Act is formally titled the **Federal Sustainable Development Act** and may be cited as such. It establishes a comprehensive legal framework to guide and govern the Government of Canada’s approach to sustainable development across federal institutions and departments.

---

#### **2. Definitions**
Key terms are defined to ensure clarity and consistency throughout the Act:

- **Appropriate Minister**: Refers to the Minister responsible for a specific area under the *Financial Administration Act*, as defined in section 2 of that Act. This ensures accountability and responsibility for specific entities or portfolios.
  
- **Commissioner**: Refers to the **Commissioner of the Environment and Sustainable Development**, appointed under subsection 15.1(1) of the *Auditor General Act*. The Commissioner plays a key oversight role in reviewing the sustainability of government actions.

- **Designated Entity**: Entities listed in the **Schedule** to the Act. These are federal bodies or corporations that are subject to the requirements of the Act.

- **Entity**: Includes:
  - Any body named in Schedules I to II of the *Financial Administration Act*;
  - Any **Crown corporation** as defined in section 2 of that Act.

- **Minister**: Generally refers to the **Minister of the Environment**, unless the context otherwise specifies (e.g., when a different minister is responsible for a designated entity).

- **Precautionary Principle**: This was originally included but was **repealed in 2019** (via *Bill C-2*, s.1). The Act no longer explicitly references it.

- **Sustainability**: Defined as the capacity of a thing, action, activity, or process to be maintained **indefinitely**.

- **Sustainable Development**: Defined as development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs — echoing the Brundtland Report.

---

#### **3. Purpose of the Act**
The primary objective of the Federal Sustainable Development Act is to:

- Establish a **legal framework** for developing and implementing a **Federal Sustainable Development Strategy (FSDS)**;
- Make decision-making related to sustainable development **more transparent and accountable** to Parliament;
- Promote **coordinated action** across all levels of the federal government;
- Respect Canada’s **domestic and international obligations** regarding sustainable development;
- Improve the **quality of life** for Canadians through environmentally sound, socially equitable, and economically viable policies.

---

#### **4. Binding Nature**
The Act and any regulations made under it are **binding on Her Majesty in right of Canada**, meaning they have full legal force and effect across all federal institutions.

---

#### **5. Core Principles of Sustainable Development**
The Act emphasizes several foundational principles that must guide the development and implementation of sustainable development strategies:

1. **Efficient Use of Resources**  
   Encourages integration of environmental, economic, and social factors into all government decisions.

2. **Evolution of the Concept**  
   Sustainable development is not static — it evolves with new scientific understanding, societal needs, and technological advances.

3. **Means of Achieving Sustainable Development**  
   Includes:
   - Protection of ecosystems
   - Prevention of pollution
   - Human health protection
   - Promotion of equity
   - Conservation of cultural heritage
   - Fulfillment of domestic and international commitments
   - Recognition of intergenerational responsibility

4. **Application of Key Principles**  
   The Act explicitly recognizes the importance of:
   - **Precautionary Principle** *(repealed in 2019)*
   - **Polluter Pays Principle**
   - **Internalization of Costs** (e.g., factoring environmental damage into economic decisions)
   - **Continuous Improvement** (ongoing efforts to enhance performance)

5. **Intergenerational Equity**  
   Ensures that current decisions do not compromise the well-being or opportunities of future generations.

6. **Openness and Transparency**  
   Requires public access to information to support accountability and public engagement.

7. **Involvement of Aboriginal Peoples**  
   Acknowledges the unique traditional knowledge and deep connection Aboriginal peoples have with Canada’s lands and waters, and mandates their inclusion in sustainable development processes.

8. **Collaboration**  
   Emphasizes stakeholder engagement and joint efforts among government, industry, civil society, and communities.

9. **Results and Delivery Approach**  
   A structured method to:
   - Set measurable objectives
   - Develop strategies to achieve them
   - Use indicators to track progress
   - Establish accountability mechanisms

---

#### **6. Oversight Structure**

##### **a. Privy Council Committee**
- A **committee of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada**, consisting of a Chairperson and other members, is established to provide **oversight** over the development and implementation of the Federal Sustainable Development Strategy.
- This committee ensures high-level political accountability and strategic direction.

##### **b. Sustainable Development Office (SDO)**
- The **Minister of the Environment** establishes a **Sustainable Development Office** within the Department of the Environment.
- Responsibilities include:
  - Developing and maintaining systems to **monitor progress** on sustainable development goals.
  - Preparing **progress reports** every **three years**, starting from the Act’s coming into force (or from **November 10, 2017**).
  - Reports are submitted to the Minister and then **tabled in each House of Parliament** within the first 15 days of a sitting session after receipt.

> *Note:* A report tabled in Parliament is **automatically referred** to the standing committee responsible for sustainable development.

---

#### **7. Sustainable Development Advisory Council (SDAC)**

##### **Establishment**
- The Minister appoints the SDAC, which includes:
  - One representative from each **province and territory**;
  - Six representatives from **Aboriginal peoples**;
  - Three representatives from each of the following:
    - Environmental non-governmental organizations (NGOs)
    - Business organizations
    - Labour organizations

> *Note:* The **appointment of representatives is to reflect demographic diversity**, including age and gender.

##### **Chairmanship**
- The **Minister of the Environment** serves as **Chair** of the Council.

##### **Mandate**
- To **advise the Minister** on all matters related to sustainable development.
- May receive referrals from the Minister.

##### **Expenses**
- Members may be paid **reasonable expenses** incurred in carrying out Council duties, subject to **Treasury Board directives**.

---

#### **8. Development of the Federal Sustainable Development Strategy (FSDS)**

##### **Timeline**
- The Minister must develop the **FSDS within two years** of the Act coming into force.
- Thereafter, a new FSDS must be developed **at least once every three years**, beginning from **November 10, 2017**.

##### **Contributions**
- All **designated entities** (and their presiding ministers, if applicable) must contribute to the development of the FSDS.

##### **Review and Public Consultation**
- The Minister submits a **draft FSDS** to:
  1. The **Sustainable Development Advisory Council**
  2. The **appropriate committees of each House of Parliament**
  3. The **public**

> A **minimum 120-day review period** is required for public and parliamentary feedback.

- The draft is also submitted to the **Commissioner of the Environment and Sustainable Development** for review and comment on:
  - Whether each **target is measurable**
  - Whether each target includes a **clear time frame**

> Again, a **120-day period** is allowed for the Commissioner’s review.

##### **Final Approval**
- The Minister submits the final FSDS to the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the Cabinet) for formal approval.
- Once approved, the strategy becomes the **official Federal Sustainable Development Strategy**.

##### **Parliamentary Tabling**
- The official FSDS is **tabled in each House of Parliament** within the period following submission or on any of the first 15 days after that period.

> A strategy tabled in Parliament is **automatically referred** to the relevant standing committee.

---

#### **9. Requirement for Designated Entities to Develop Their Own Strategies**

##### **Timeline**
- Within **one year** of the official FSDS being tabled in Parliament, **every designated entity** (except those specifically excluded) must:
  - Prepare a **sustainable development strategy** that:
    1. Contains clear **objectives and plans**
    2. Complies with the federal strategy and contributes to achieving its goals
    3. Takes into account the entity’s **mandate**
    4. Considers applicable **Treasury Board policies or directives** on sustainable development
    5. Incorporates **public and stakeholder feedback** from the review process

- The strategy must be submitted to the **appropriate Minister**.

##### **Parliamentary Tabling**
- The appropriate Minister must **table the strategy** in each House of Parliament within the first 15 days of a sitting session after receiving it.

##### **Progress Reporting**
- The designated entity must submit **progress reports** at least once every **two years** after the strategy is tabled.
- These reports are also **tabled in Parliament** within 15 days of receipt by the Minister.

> *Note:* For entities presided over by a minister (e.g., Crown corporations), the **minister** prepares the strategy and reports on progress.
