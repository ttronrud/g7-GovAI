## üèõÔ∏è KEY INSTITUTIONAL REFORMS (TRANSITION AND RESTRUCTURING)

The Act facilitates the **restructuring of several key national security institutions** into new, more transparent, and accountable bodies. These transitions are designed to preserve institutional continuity while improving oversight and public accountability.

---

### 1. **Establishment of the National Security and Intelligence Review Agency (NSIRA)**

#### Background:
- Replaces the **Security Intelligence Review Committee (SIRC)**, which was established under the Canadian Security Intelligence Service (CSIS) Act.
- SIRC faced criticism for lack of transparency and public accountability, especially after concerns were raised about its powers and operations.

#### Key Provisions:
- **Definition**: "New Agency" refers to the **National Security and Intelligence Review Agency**.
- **Continuity of Personnel**:
  - The **Chairman** of the former SIRC continues in office as a **member** of NSIRA for the remainder of their term.
  - All **former members** of SIRC continue in office as members of NSIRA.
- **Appointment of Chair**:
  - The Governor in Council must designate a Chair from among those continuing in office or appoint a new one within a reasonable timeframe after the Act comes into force.
- **Transfer of Functions and Assets**:
  - All rights, obligations, property, and contracts of the former SIRC are transferred to NSIRA.
  - All **information under SIRC‚Äôs control** (including complaints, investigations, and reviews) is transferred to NSIRA.
  - Any **complaints or reports** made to SIRC before the transition are deemed to have been made to NSIRA.
- **Legal Continuity**:
  - Pending legal actions or administrative proceedings against SIRC can be continued against NSIRA with the same effect.
  - No compensation or indemnity claims are available to former SIRC members for their role in the transition.

> ‚úÖ **Purpose**: To create a more transparent, independent, and accountable body that reviews intelligence and security activities without compromising operational effectiveness.

---

### 2. **Establishment of the Intelligence Commissioner (IC)**

#### Background:
- Replaces the **Commissioner of the Communications Security Establishment (CSE)**, who previously had oversight responsibilities.
- The role of the CSE was restructured to align with broader intelligence oversight goals.

#### Key Provisions:
- **Definition**: "New Commissioner" refers to the **Intelligence Commissioner** under the *Intelligence Commissioner Act*.
- **Continuity of Personnel**:
  - The individual who held office as the former CSE immediately before the Act comes into force becomes the **new Intelligence Commissioner** as if appointed under the new Act.
  - All employees of the former CSE retain their positions in the new office.
- **Transfer of Rights and Obligations**:
  - All rights, property, and obligations of the former CSE are transferred to the new office.
  - Unexpended funds allocated to the former CSE are deemed to be allocated to the new office.
  - Contracts with the former CSE are deemed to be contracts with the new Commissioner.
- **Continuity of Powers**:
  - Any powers or duties previously authorized by the former CSE are retained by the new Commissioner.
- **Legal Continuity**:
  - Pending legal actions (excluding those related to complaints or reviews) can be brought against the new Commissioner.
- **No Compensation Claims**:
  - Former CSE officials have no right to claim compensation, damages, or indemnity due to the transition.

> ‚úÖ **Purpose**: To establish a dedicated, independent, and civilian oversight body for the CSE, ensuring accountability for surveillance and data collection practices.

---

### 3. **Reorganization of the Communications Security Establishment (CSE)**

#### Background:
- The CSE is responsible for protecting Canada‚Äôs communications and information systems from cyber threats and ensuring the security of government and private sector data.

#### Key Provisions:
- **Definition**: "Former department" = CSE before the reform; "New department" = CSE after reform.
- **Continuity of Leadership**:
  - The **Chief of CSE** continues in office for the remainder of their term.
- **Continuity of Employees**:
  - All employees retain their positions in the new CSE.
- **Funding and Expenditures**:
  - Unexpended appropriations for the former CSE are deemed to be for the new CSE.
- **Transfer of Powers and Functions**:
  - All powers, duties, and functions previously held by the CSE are now exercised by the new CSE.
  - Authorizations issued under the National Defence Act (e.g., for access to information) continue to be valid.
- **Legal and Regulatory References**:
  - All references to the "former department" in laws, regulations, schedules, and acts are deemed to refer to the "new department."
  - Specific schedules (e.g., Access to Information Act, Privacy Act, Financial Administration Act) are updated to reflect the change.

> ‚úÖ **Purpose**: To ensure continuity in national cybersecurity operations while enabling better governance, oversight, and public accountability.

## üîê DATA PROTECTION AND SURVEILLANCE REFORMS

### Section 111 ‚Äì Dataset and Collection Rules

- **Definition**:
  - A **dataset** is defined as a collection of electronic information with a common subject matter.
  - A **Service** refers to the Canadian Security Intelligence Service (CSIS) as defined in the CSIS Act.

- **Key Provision**:
  - Any dataset collected by CSIS **before the commencement day** (date when section 96 comes into force) that would have been subject to **sections 11.02 and 11.05** of the CSIS Act (on data retention and access) is **deemed to have been collected under section 11.05** on the commencement day.

> ‚úÖ **Purpose**: To ensure consistency and legal clarity in how data collected before reform is treated under new data governance rules. This prevents retroactive changes in data handling and ensures legal certainty.

## ‚öñÔ∏è PROTECTION OF RIGHTS AND FREEDOMS

The Act emphasizes that national security activities must not infringe on fundamental rights and freedoms protected under the **Charter of Rights and Freedoms**.

- The Government commits to:
  - Conducting all activities in a manner that respects civil liberties.
  - Ensuring that oversight mechanisms are accessible and transparent.
  - Encouraging international partners to uphold similar standards.

> üîç This is particularly important in light of public concerns about the **Anti-terrorism Act, 2015**, which included provisions allowing broad surveillance and data access without sufficient oversight.

## üìö LEGAL CONTINUITY AND TRANSITION MECHANISMS

The Act includes detailed provisions to ensure **legal and administrative continuity** during the transition:

| Aspect | What Happens |
|-------|--------------|
| **Pending Proceedings** | Any legal action or administrative proceeding against the former institutions (SIRC, CSE) can continue against the new ones. |
| **Unexpended Funds** | Appropriated but unused funds are automatically redirected to the new entities. |
| **Contracts** | Contracts with the former institutions are deemed to be with the new ones. |
| **Complaints and Reports** | All complaints and reports made to the old bodies are automatically transferred to the new bodies. |
| **Personnel Status** | Employees and officials retain their status, positions, and compensation rights (except in cases of transition-related claims). |

> These provisions ensure that the reform process does not disrupt ongoing operations or legal obligations.

---

## üö® EXCEPTIONS AND LIMITATIONS

- **No Compensation Claims**: Former officials of the SIRC, CSE, or other replaced bodies **cannot claim compensation, damages, or indemnity** due to the abolition or reorganization of their roles.
- **Exclusions from Legal Continuity**: Legal actions related to **complaints or reviews** before the former bodies are preserved; however, other liabilities may be redirected to the new bodies.
