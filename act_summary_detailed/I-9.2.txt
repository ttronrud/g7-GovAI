**Detailed Summary of the *Department of Industry Act* (Canada)**

---

### **Overview**
The *Department of Industry Act* (1995) is a foundational piece of Canadian federal legislation that establishes the **Department of Industry** as a central government entity responsible for promoting economic growth, innovation, and competitiveness across Canada. It consolidates and reorganizes various functions previously scattered among multiple departments, streamlining responsibilities related to industry, technology, trade, investment, consumer affairs, and regional economic development—particularly in Ontario.

This Act came into force on **March 29, 1995**, and was enacted by Her Majesty in Right of Canada, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Establishment of the Department of Industry**
- The Act formally establishes the **Department of Industry** as a department of the Government of Canada.
- The **Minister of Industry** is appointed by commission under the Great Seal of Canada and presides over the Department.
- The Minister holds office at the pleasure of the Governor in Council (i.e., the federal Cabinet) and has full management and direction of the Department.

> **Note:** The Minister also serves as the **Registrar General of Canada**, a role that gives them administrative oversight of official documents and registrations.

---

### **2. Deputy Minister**
- The Governor in Council may appoint a **Deputy Minister of Industry**, who serves at the pleasure of the Governor in Council and acts as the deputy head of the Department.
- This structure ensures continuity and operational leadership within the Department.

---

### **3. Powers, Duties, and Functions of the Minister**

The Minister has broad authority over all matters within Parliament’s jurisdiction that are not assigned to other departments or agencies. These include:

#### **Core Areas of Responsibility:**
- **Industry and Technology**: Promoting innovation, R&D, and technological advancement.
- **Trade and Commerce**: Facilitating domestic and international trade.
- **Science and Technology**: Supporting scientific research and development.
- **Consumer Affairs**: Protecting consumer rights and interests.
- **Corporations and Corporate Securities**: Regulating corporate governance and securities.
- **Competition and Restraint of Trade**: Enforcing anti-monopoly laws and merger regulations.
- **Bankruptcy and Insolvency**: Overseeing insolvency frameworks.
- **Intellectual Property**: Administration of patents, copyrights, trademarks, industrial designs, and integrated circuit topographies.
- **Standards of Identity, Packaging, and Performance**: Setting standards for consumer products (excluding safety).
- **Legal Metrology**: Regulation of measurement standards.
- **Telecommunications**: Except for:
  - Planning and coordination of telecom services for government bodies.
  - Broadcasting (excluding spectrum management and technical aspects).
- **Investment Promotion**: Encouraging both domestic and foreign investment.
- **Small Businesses**: Supporting small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs).
- **Tourism**: Promoting Canada as a destination.

#### **Regional Economic Development in Ontario**
- The Minister has specific powers and duties to promote **economic development in Ontario**, especially in areas with:
  - Low income levels
  - Slow economic growth
  - Inadequate employment opportunities
- The focus is on **long-term sustainable development**, **entrepreneurial talent**, and **support for SMEs**.

---

### **4. Policy Objectives and Strategic Priorities**

The Minister must exercise their powers in a manner that achieves the following objectives:

| Objective | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Strengthen National Economy | Promote sustainable economic growth. |
| Promote Trade and Mobility | Facilitate the free flow of goods, services, and factors of production. |
| Enhance International Competitiveness | Improve Canada’s global competitiveness in industry and services. |
| Foster Science & Technology | Encourage innovation and effective use of science and technology. |
| Improve Market Efficiency | Strengthen the efficiency and fairness of the Canadian marketplace. |
| Develop Communications Systems | Promote Canadian communication infrastructure. |
| Stimulate Investment | Attract and support investment in key sectors. |
| Protect Consumers | Safeguard consumer interests through regulation and information. |

---

### **5. Key Actions and Responsibilities of the Minister**

The Minister is empowered to:

- **Initiate, coordinate, promote, and implement** national policies, programs, and projects aligned with the objectives in Section 5.
- **Collect, analyze, and disseminate information** on industry trends, both domestically and internationally.
- **Provide support services and investment** to Canadian industry, science, and technology.
- **Foster cooperation** with:
  - Provincial governments
  - Business and labor organizations
  - International agencies and other nations
- **Secure Canada’s rights** in international communications regulation.

---

### **6. Inspection Powers**

- The Minister may provide **inspection services** to enforce Acts under their administration.
- The Minister may **designate inspectors** to carry out these duties.

---

### **7. Regional Economic Development in Ontario (Specific Provisions)**

In Ontario, the Minister must:

- Work **with other ministers and agencies** to develop integrated federal policies.
- Coordinate federal programs and initiatives.
- Lead and coordinate federal efforts in building **cooperative relationships** with Ontario and stakeholders (business, labor, public/private).
- Collect and analyze data relevant to regional economic development.

The Minister may:
- Provide and coordinate services to:
  - Develop entrepreneurial talent
  - Support local business associations
  - Stimulate investment
  - Support SMEs
- Initiate and implement **regional development programs and projects**.

---

### **8. Regulatory Authority and Flexibility**

The Governor in Council may make **regulations** to:
- Implement policies, programs, and projects referenced in Section 9.
- General provisions for carrying out the purposes of Sections 8 and 9.

These regulations provide flexibility in operational implementation.

---

### **9. Registrar General of Canada**

- The Registrar General registers all documents issued under the Great Seal or requiring registration.
- The Registrar may appoint **Deputy Registrars General** from within the Department to assist with registration and certification.
- Deputy Registrars can **sign and certify** documents and copies of records.
- **Transition provision**: If a pre-1967 Act required filing with the Secretary of State, such filing now goes to the Registrar General unless otherwise directed by the Governor in Council.

---

### **10. Special Assistance Programs**

Where the Governor in Council determines it is in the national interest, the Minister may develop and implement **special assistance programs** for:
- Specific industries
- Industrial establishments or organizations
- Certain categories of persons (as defined by order)
- To aid in:
  - Restructuring
  - Modernization
  - Expansion or contraction
  - Rationalization or re-establishment

These programs may involve:
- Loans
- Guarantees
- Credit insurance
- Grants and contributions

> The Governor in Council must also **specify or authorize** the actions taken under such programs.

---

### **11. Financial Instruments and Financing Powers**

The Minister may:
- **Make loans** to individuals or organizations
- **Guarantee repayment** or provide **loan insurance/credit insurance**
- **Make grants and contributions**

Subject to regulations, the Minister may:
- Acquire, exercise, assign, or sell **stock options or other financial instruments** obtained as conditions for loans, guarantees, or insurance.

> **Regulatory Oversight**: The Governor in Council may regulate:
> - Types of loans and guarantees
> - Conditions under which financial instruments may be acquired or sold

> **Financial Administration Act Note**: Loan insurance or credit insurance provided under this Act is treated as a **guarantee** for the purposes of the *Financial Administration Act*.

---

### **12. Advisory Committees**

- The Minister may establish **advisory and other committees** to provide guidance or perform specific functions.
- The Governor in Council may specify the powers and duties of these committees.
- The Minister may set **remuneration and expenses** for committee members.

---

### **13. Access to Customs Data**

- The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** must, upon request, provide to the Minister:
  - Copies of **invoices and other data** collected under the *Customs Act* on imported and exported goods.
- This data is used to support duties related to trade, investment, and competitiveness.

> **Confidentiality Clause**: No employee in the federal public service may disclose such information about a particular person, organization, or business **without written consent**.

> **Exception**: The Minister may **publish lists of importers/exporters** (with aggregated trade values) to protect individual confidentiality.

---

### **14. Use of Other Government Resources**

- The Minister may:
  - Use the **services, facilities, information, and expertise** of other departments, boards, or agencies.
  - Organize **conferences** with representatives from provincial governments, business, labor, and private entities.

---

### **15. Agreements with Provinces and Entities**

- The Minister may enter into **agreements** with:
  - Provincial governments
  - Provincial agencies
  - Private entities
- The Minister may make **disbursements up to the total amount contributed by all parties**, even before those contributions are received.

> This allows for flexible, collaborative funding arrangements.

---
