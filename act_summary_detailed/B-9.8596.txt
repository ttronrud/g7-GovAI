**Detailed Summary of the *Budget Implementation Act, 2024, No. 1***

---

### **Overview**
The *Budget Implementation Act, 2024, No. 1* is a federal legislative instrument enacted by the Parliament of Canada—by and with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons—on the authority of His Majesty. It serves as a key component of the 2024 federal budget, implementing various financial, regulatory, and policy changes across multiple areas of government. The Act is formally cited as the *Budget Implementation Act, 2024, No. 1*.

While the text contains numerous repeated entries labeled "[Amendments]" and notes indicating that certain provisions are "not in force," the Act contains substantive provisions that address:

- Regulatory repeals  
- Financial programs and funding mechanisms  
- Labour and pension law amendments  
- Indigenous initiatives and loan guarantee programs  
- Environmental assessment reforms  
- Administrative and procedural adjustments  

Below is a detailed, organized summary of the key provisions and their implications.

---

## **1. Repeal of Tobacco Regulations**
### **Provisions:**
- The **Regulations Relieving Special Duty on Certain Tobacco Products** are repealed.
- The **Regulations Respecting Prescribed Brands of Manufactured Tobacco and Prescribed Cigarettes** are repealed.

### **Effect:**
- These regulatory measures, which previously imposed special duties or restrictions on specific tobacco products, are formally removed from the legal framework.
- This reflects a shift in federal tobacco policy, likely aimed at simplifying regulations or aligning with broader public health goals, such as reducing tobacco consumption or promoting market access.

### **Commencement:**
- Both repeals come into force on the **first day of the month after the month in which the Act receives royal assent**.

---

## **2. Bilateral Agreement on School Food Programs**
### **Provisions:**
- The **Minister of Families, Children and Social Development** may enter into a **bilateral agreement** with a province to implement a **national program for providing food in schools**.
- Payments under such agreements are to be made **out of the Consolidated Revenue Fund**.
- The **maximum aggregate amount** payable across all provinces is **$70,100,000** for the fiscal year beginning April 1, 2024.

### **Purpose:**
- To improve access to nutritious meals in schools, particularly in underserved or rural communities.
- Promotes intergovernmental cooperation between the federal government and provincial authorities to support child nutrition and well-being.

### **Implementation:**
- The agreement must be structured and funded within the specified financial cap.
- The federal government retains control over funding through the Consolidated Revenue Fund.

## **4. Labour Code Amendments (Canada Labour Code)**
### **Key Provisions:**

#### **(a) Exemption from Application to Pre-existing Proceedings**
- **Sections 6.1, 6.2, 123.2, 123.3, 167.01, and 167.2** of the Canada Labour Code **do not apply** to any legal proceeding (e.g., complaints or claims) that was **commenced before the Act received royal assent**.

> **Implication:** Workers who initiated claims before the Act came into force are protected from being subject to new or stricter rules introduced under these sections.

#### **(b) New Rules Apply Only to Post-Royal-Assent Contraventions**
- **Section 167.1** (relating to severance and termination pay) applies **only to contraventions alleged to have occurred on or after the day the Act receives royal assent**.

> **Implication:** This ensures that changes to severance and termination benefits are not retroactively applied to past employment terminations.

#### **(c) Transitional Rules for Ongoing Complaints**
- For complaints made under **subsection 251.01(1)** of the *former* Canada Labour Code (relating to severance pay):
  - If the complaint is **ongoing on the commencement day**, the **Head or Board** (or court) must treat **subsections 230(1.01) and 235(1.1)** of the *new* Act as if they were in force at the time of termination.
  - A complaint is considered "ongoing" until **all available legal appeals and reviews are exhausted**.

> **Purpose:** Ensures consistency and fairness in handling ongoing employment disputes, particularly regarding severance entitlements, by applying updated standards without retroactive penalties.

## **5. Indigenous Loan Guarantee Program**
### **Provisions:**
- A **wholly owned subsidiary of the Canada Development Investment Corporation (CDIC)** is authorized to provide **loan guarantees** as part of an **Indigenous loan guarantee program**.
- The **aggregate principal and interest** on all guarantees must not exceed **$5,000,000,000**, or a higher amount authorized by the Governor in Council on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance.

### **Funding and Obligations:**
- The **Minister of Finance** must pay necessary amounts from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund** to cover:
  - Principal and interest payments
  - Operational costs
  - Legal or administrative obligations

### **Legal Status of the Subsidiary:**
- The subsidiary is treated as an **agent of His Majesty in right of Canada**.
- **Sections 91 and 100(1) of the Financial Administration Act do not apply** to the subsidiary or its wholly owned subsidiaries.
- However, **subsections 89(1), (4), (6)** and **section 89.1** of the Financial Administration Act **do apply**, as if the subsidiary were a **parent Crown corporation**.

> **Significance:** This establishes a clear legal and financial structure for Indigenous financial inclusion, allowing the federal government to support Indigenous-owned businesses through loan guarantees without violating financial administration rules.

---

## **6. Red Dress Alert Pilot Project Funding**
### **Provisions:**
- The federal government may pay out of the **Consolidated Revenue Fund** a sum **not exceeding $1.3 million** during the period **September 1, 2024 – March 31, 2027**, for:
  - Developing and launching a **Red Dress Alert**, a public alert system for **missing Indigenous women, girls, two-spirit persons, and gender-diverse Indigenous individuals**.
  - Making **direct payments** to participating entities or individuals involved in the project.

### **Purpose:**
- Addresses the ongoing crisis of missing and murdered Indigenous women and girls (MMIWG) by creating a proactive, community-based alert system.
- Supports Indigenous-led solutions and data collection.

> **Note:** This is a significant investment in Indigenous safety and justice, emphasizing a public health and human rights approach.
