### 1. **Core Electoral and Administrative Terms**

| Term | Definition & Purpose |
|------|------------------------|
| **Election** | Refers to the election of a member to serve in the House of Commons. This is the central event governed by the Act. |
| **Polling Day** | The official day fixed for voting, as set under section 57(1.2)(c), 59(4), or 77(2). Marks the end of the voting period. |
| **Election Period** | Begins when the writ is issued and ends on polling day. If the writ is withdrawn (e.g., under section 59(1) or 31(3)), the period ends on the withdrawal date. |
| **Polling Division** | A geographic subdivision within an electoral district where voting occurs. Defined in section 538. |
| **Polling Station** | A physical location established under various sections (e.g., 120, 122, 205, etc.) where voters cast their ballots. |
| **Advance Poll** / **Advance Polling Station** | A special voting option held before polling day, allowing eligible voters to vote earlier. Advance polling stations are established under subsection 168(3). |
| **Writ** | A formal legal document issued to initiate an election. It officially begins the election period. |
| **Official List of Electors** | The list prepared by the returning officer under section 106, containing names, addresses, and identifiers of registered voters. |
| **Preliminary List of Electors** | The initial list compiled by the Chief Electoral Officer under subsection 93(1), used before finalization. |
| **Revised List of Electors** | A corrected version of the official list prepared by the returning officer under section 105, after adjustments are made (e.g., due to errors or changes). |

> ‚úÖ These terms define the **spatial, temporal, and procedural framework** of voting and electoral administration.

### 2. **Political Parties and Candidate Status**

| Term | Definition & Purpose |
|------|------------------------|
| **Political Party** | An organization whose fundamental purpose is to participate in public affairs by endorsing members as candidates and supporting their election. |
| **Registered Party** | A political party formally registered in the registry of political parties (section 394). Must meet criteria for registration (e.g., structure, membership, activities). |
| **Eligible Party** | A party that satisfies the registration requirements set out in section 387. Used in contexts like party funding or advertising. |
| **Electoral District Association (EDA)** | An association of members of a political party within a specific electoral district. Can act as a local organizing body. |
| **Registered Association** | An EDA that is officially registered in the registry of electoral district associations (section 455). |
| **Candidate** | A person whose nomination has been confirmed under subsection 71(1), but who has not yet fulfilled all reporting or compliance obligations under sections 477.59 to 477.84. |
| **Potential Candidate** | A person who has not yet been formally confirmed as a candidate but may become one through: <br> - Selection in a nomination contest <br> - Deemed candidate under section 477 <br> - Being a current member of Parliament (or former member before dissolution) <br> - Having support from a political party. |
| **Nomination Contest** | A competition to select a person to be proposed as a candidate to a political party for endorsement in an electoral district. |
| **Nomination Contestant** | A person named in a report under subsection 476.1(1)(c) who has not yet complied with compliance obligations under sections 476.75 to 476.94. |
| **Leadership Contest** | A competition to select the leader of a registered party. |
| **Leadership Contestant** | A person registered in the leadership registry (section 478.4) who has not yet fulfilled compliance obligations under sections 478.8 to 478.97. |
| **Chief Agent** | The chief agent designated in a party‚Äôs application to become registered (section 385(2)(h)), or a replacement appointed under section 400(1). Acts as the principal representative of the party. |
| **Registered Agent** | A person appointed under subsection 396(1), including the chief agent of a registered party. Responsible for administrative and compliance duties. |
| **Official Agent** | A person appointed under subsection 477.1(1), or a replacement under section 477.42. Represents a candidate in official matters. |

> üîç These definitions clarify **who can run, how parties operate, and the responsibilities of key individuals** in the political process.

### 3. **Funding and Contributions**

| Term | Definition & Purpose |
|------|------------------------|
| **Contribution** | Encompasses both **monetary** and **non-monetary** contributions. |
| **Monetary Contribution** | Money provided that is not repayable. |
| **Non-Monetary Contribution** | The **commercial value** of a service (excluding volunteer labour), property, or use of property or money ‚Äî provided at **less than or without commercial value**. |
| **Commercial Value** | The lowest amount charged at the time for the same kind and quantity of property/service or usage, either: <br> - By the provider, if in business; or <br> - By another provider in the area, if not in business. |

> ‚öñÔ∏è This definition ensures **fair and transparent financial accountability**, preventing disguised or inflated contributions. It is critical for **funding rules, disclosure requirements, and compliance**.

> üí° Example: A candidate receives a car worth $20,000. If the car is normally rented for $1,000/month, its commercial value is $1,000/month ‚Äî so the contribution is valued at that rate, not the full purchase price.

### 4. **Advertising and Communication Rules**

| Term | Definition & Purpose |
|------|------------------------|
| **Election Advertising** | Transmission during the **election period** of messages that promote or oppose a registered party or candidate ‚Äî including taking positions on issues associated with them. <br> ‚ùå **Excluded** from this definition: <br> - Editorials, debates, speeches, interviews, columns, letters, news <br> - Distribution of books planned for public sale regardless of election <br> - Internal communications to members/employees/shareholders <br> - Personal political views shared non-commercially online <br> - Telephone calls to encourage voting |
| **Partisan Advertising** | Transmission during the **pre-election period** of messages that promote or oppose a registered party, eligible party, or a potential candidate, leader, or nominee ‚Äî **excluding** taking positions on issues. <br> ‚ùå **Excluded** (same as above). |
| **Election Advertising Expense** | Costs incurred in producing or transmitting election advertising messages. |
| **Partisan Advertising Expense** | Costs incurred in producing or transmitting partisan advertising messages. |

> üö® These definitions are crucial for **regulating political messaging** and preventing undue influence. They establish **strict boundaries** on when and how political messages can be disseminated.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è The distinction between **pre-election** (partisan) and **election period** (election advertising) helps prevent **early campaigning** and **unregulated messaging**.

### 5. **Personal Information and Data Protection**

| Term | Definition & Purpose |
|------|------------------------|
| **Personal Information** | As defined in section 3 of the *Privacy Act*. Includes data such as name, address, and identifiers of electors. |
| **Prescribed Form** | A form authorized by the Chief Electoral Officer. Any form used at a polling station, advance polling station, or returning officer‚Äôs office that contains personal information must be prescribed. |
