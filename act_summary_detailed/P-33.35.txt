### **Detailed Summary of the *Federal Public Sector Labour Relations and Employment Board Act***

This Act establishes and governs the **Federal Public Sector Labour Relations and Employment Board (the "Board")**, a key institution responsible for resolving labour disputes and overseeing employment relations in the federal public service. It consolidates and modernizes the functions of previous labour relations bodies and introduces new procedural, structural, and protective provisions to ensure fairness, impartiality, and efficiency in public sector employment matters.

---

## **1. Title and Citation**
- The Act is titled the **Federal Public Sector Labour Relations and Employment Board Act**.
- It replaces or continues the legacy **Public Service Labour Relations and Employment Board**, preserving its name and mandate under a new legal framework.

---

## **2. Definitions**
Key terms are defined to ensure consistency with related legislation:
- **Bargaining agent**: Defined as per subsection 2(1) of the *Federal Public Sector Labour Relations Act* — refers to a certified employee organization (e.g., union) that negotiates on behalf of employees.
- **Employer**: Defined as per subsection 2(1) of the same Act — refers to federal government departments or agencies that employ public servants.
- **Minister**: Refers to a federal minister designated under Section 3. The Governor in Council may designate any federal minister (excluding Treasury Board members) to serve as the Minister.

---

## **3. Structure and Composition of the Board**
The Board consists of:
- **One Chairperson** – holds office on a full-time basis and exercises overall supervision.
- **Not more than two Vice-chairpersons** – also full-time.
- **Not more than 12 other full-time members** – each must be eligible and appointed through a structured process.
- **Part-time members** – may be appointed if deemed necessary by the Governor in Council.

### **Eligibility Requirements for Members**
To qualify for appointment, a person must:
1. Be a **Canadian citizen** or a **permanent resident** (as defined in the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act*).
2. **Not hold any other office or employment** under the employer (i.e., cannot work directly for the government entity in dispute).
3. **Not be a member of or hold office in a certified bargaining agent** (union) that represents employees.
4. **Not engage in activities inconsistent with their duties** (e.g., advocacy for specific unions or employers).

> **Exception**: A person is **not ineligible** solely for holding office on a board in the Northwest Territories, Yukon, or Nunavut if that board has similar powers and functions.

### **Appointment Process**
- The **Chairperson** prepares a list of eligible candidates after **consultation with both the employer and the bargaining agents**.
- The list must include:
  - Names recommended by the employer.
  - Names recommended by the bargaining agents.
  - Any other names the Chairperson considers suitable.
- **Appointment must ensure an equal number** of members from employer-recommended and agent-recommended lists, where possible.
- Members **do not represent** either the employer or employees and must act **impartially**.
- **Residency requirement**: Full-time members must reside in the **National Capital Region** (as defined in the *National Capital Act*) or within a distance set by the Governor in Council.

### **Term of Office and Reappointment**
- Full-time members: **Maximum 5 years**; part-time members: **Maximum 3 years**.
- Members are **eligible for reappointment** after their term ends.

### **Continuity of Duties After Leaving**
- A person who ceases to be a member may, upon request by the Chairperson, within **8 weeks**, carry out and complete any duties related to matters before the Board during their tenure. For this purpose, they are **deemed a part-time member**.

---

## **4. Appointment and Removal**
- All members (except Chairperson and Vice-chairpersons) are appointed by the **Governor in Council**, on the **Minister’s recommendation**, and serve during **good behaviour**.
- Members may be **removed for cause** by the Governor in Council.

---

## **5. Oaths and Affirmations**
- Before beginning duties, all members must take an **oath or solemn affirmation**:
  > *"I, [name], do swear (or solemnly affirm) that I will faithfully, truly and impartially, to the best of my judgment, skill and ability, execute and perform the office of member (or Chairperson or Vice-chairperson) of the Federal Public Sector Labour Relations and Employment Board."*

---

## **6. Compensation and Expenses**
- Members are paid **remuneration set by the Governor in Council**.
- Members are entitled to **reasonable travel and other expenses** incurred while performing duties:
  - For full-time members: while absent from their **ordinary place of work**.
  - For part-time members: while absent from their **ordinary place of residence**.
- **Legal Status**:
  - Full-time members are deemed **employed in the public service** under the *Public Service Superannuation Act*.
  - Members are deemed **employees** under the *Government Employees Compensation Act* and **employed in federal public administration** under regulations under the *Aeronautics Act*.

---

## **7. Board’s Head Office and Access to Government Resources**
- The Board’s **head office is located in the National Capital Region**.
- The Board may use **any appropriate services and facilities** of Government of Canada departments, boards, and agencies.

---

## **8. Meetings and Quorum**
- Meetings may be held at any time and place deemed appropriate by the Chairperson.
- Meetings may be conducted via **telecommunication** (e.g., video conferencing), and participants are deemed present if they can communicate adequately.
- **Quorum**:
  - A **majority of full-time members**, including the Chairperson and one Vice-chairperson, constitutes a quorum.
  - A **part-time member is not entitled to attend** meetings but may do so at the Chairperson’s invitation.
- A **decision by a majority of members present** is binding on the Board.

---

## **9. Powers and Functions**
The Board has broad powers to conduct proceedings, including:
- **Summoning and enforcing attendance of witnesses** and compelling them to give oral or written evidence on oath (like a superior court).
- **Ordering pre-hearing procedures**, including private pre-hearing conferences, and setting hearing dates and places.
- Conducting hearings via **telecommunication**.
- **Administering oaths and solemn affirmations**.
- **Accepting any evidence**, even if not admissible in court.
- **Compelling production of documents or things** relevant to a proceeding at any stage.

### **Summary Dismissal Power**
- The Board may **dismiss a matter summarily** if it is:
  - Trivial,
  - Frivolous,
  - Vexatious, or
  - Made in bad faith.

### **Discretion to Avoid Hearings**
- The Board may **decide a matter without holding an oral hearing**, if appropriate.

### **Mediation and Settlement Assistance**
- With **party agreement**, the Board or its authorized employees (including those from the *Administrative Tribunals Support Service of Canada*) may assist parties in resolving disputes at any stage — without prejudice to the Board’s authority to make final decisions.

### **Delegation of Powers**
- The Board may:
  - Authorize the **Chairperson** to exercise any powers or perform duties (except regulation-making).
  - Authorize **any person** to exercise powers under specific paragraphs (20(d)–(f)) and require them to **report back** to the Board.

---

## **10. Chairperson’s Supervisory Role**
The Chairperson has **supervision and direction over**:
- Assignment and reassignment of matters to panels.
- Composition of panels.
- Determination of hearing dates, times, and places.

### **Vice-Chairperson Authority**
- The Chairperson may authorize a Vice-chairperson to exercise any of their own powers or duties, including delegated ones.

### **Succession in Case of Absence or Vacancy**
- If the Chairperson is absent or unable to act, a **designated Vice-chairperson** acts as Chairperson.
- If **both Chairperson and Vice-chairpersons are absent or unable to act**, the **Minister may designate a member** to act as Chairperson — but **only for up to 90 days**, unless the Governor in Council approves an extension.

> *(Note: This provision was previously repealed in 2014 but appears here as a retained clause.)*

---

## **11. Advisory Services**
- The **Chief Administrator of the Administrative Tribunals Support Service of Canada** may, on a **temporary basis**, engage **mediators or experts** to provide advisory support to the Board.
- Their remuneration is subject to **Governor in Council approval**.

---

## **12. Privilege Against Testimony**
- No member of the Board, nor any person engaged under Section 30 (e.g., mediators or experts), may be **compelled or competent to testify** in any civil or criminal proceeding about information obtained in the course of their duties or performance of powers.
- Similarly, the **Chief Administrator or employees of the Support Service** are protected from being compelled to testify about information provided to the Board.

---

## **13. Confidentiality of Notes and Drafts**
- **Notes, drafts, or preliminary orders** of the Board or its members are **not to be disclosed** without the consent of the person who prepared them.
