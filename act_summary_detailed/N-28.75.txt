### 4. **Inuit Participation and Representation**

The Act emphasizes **meaningful Inuit involvement**, particularly through:
- **Designated Inuit Organizations** (Tunngavik and Makivik),
- **Representation in Commission and Board appointments**,
- **Inclusion of Inuit knowledge and traditions** in decision-making.

#### ðŸ“Œ Representation in the Commission:
- At least one member appointed by the **federal Minister**,
- At least one by the **territorial Minister**,
- **Half** of the members appointed by **Tunngavik** (the designated Inuit organization in Nunavut).

> **Special provision for areas of equal use and occupancy**:  
> In such areas (shared use zones), **Makivik** may nominate a number of persons equal to **half** the number of members appointed by Tunngavik. These nominees are appointed by the federal Minister and act in place of the Tunngavik-appointed members.

#### ðŸ“Œ Representation in the Board:
- Two members appointed by the **federal Minister**,
- Four members appointed by the federal Minister **on nomination by Tunngavik**,
- One by the **territorial Minister**,
- One by **one or more territorial ministers**.

> Again, if a project is in an area of equal use and occupancy, **Makivik** can nominate a number of persons equal to half of the Tunngavik-appointed members, who are then appointed by the federal Minister.

> **Preference for local residents**: If equally qualified, the federal Minister gives preference to nominees who **reside in the designated area**.

### 5. **Appointment of Commission and Board Members**

#### Commission:
- **Chairperson**: Appointed after consultation with the territorial Minister from among nominated members.
- If a member becomes Chairperson, the federal Minister must appoint **another member** to maintain balance.
- **Term extensions**: If a member's term ends before a project review is complete, they **continue to serve** until the review concludes.
- **Residency requirement**: At least **half** of the appointed members must reside in the designated area.
- **Eligibility**: Employees of federal or territorial departments are **not eligible** to serve.

#### Board:
- Same rules apply for appointment and continuity.
- Chairperson is selected from among nominated members by the federal Minister, with preference for residents of the designated area.
- If a member becomes Chairperson, their appointing minister must appoint a **replacement**.

> All appointments are made **in writing**, and the federal Minister must **notify the designated Inuit organization** of any delegation or appointment.
