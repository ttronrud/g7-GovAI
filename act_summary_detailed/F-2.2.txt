### **Detailed Summary of the Farm Credit Canada Act**

---

#### **1. Introduction and Citation**
The *Farm Credit Canada Act* is an Act of Parliament of Canada that establishes and reorganizes the **Farm Credit Canada (FCC)** as a statutory corporation. It is enacted by Her Majesty, with the advice and consent of both the Senate and the House of Commons. The Act may be cited as the *Farm Credit Canada Act*.

This legislation serves to modernize, strengthen, and provide a clear legal framework for a financial institution dedicated to supporting rural and agricultural economies in Canada.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**
The Act defines several key terms used throughout:

- **Board**: Refers to the Board of Directors of Farm Credit Canada.
- **Business related to farming**: Includes businesses that produce, transport, store, distribute, process, or add value to inputs or outputs of farming operations. This broadens the scope beyond direct farming to support services.
- **Chairperson**: The Chairperson of the Board appointed under subsection 7(1). (Only in English)
- **Corporation**: Farm Credit Canada, continuing from the original Farm Credit Corporation established under the *Farm Credit Act*.
- **Director**: A member of the Board.
- **Farming**: Includes livestock raising, beekeeping, dairying, fruit growing, tillage, and any other husbandry activity that the Corporation determines (under subsection 4(3)) to constitute farming.
- **President**: The chief executive officer of the Corporation, appointed under subsection 7(1). (Only in English)
- **Subsidiary**: Defined as per subsection 83(6) of the *Financial Administration Act* — a company or entity controlled by or under the control of the Corporation.

---

#### **3. Continuation of the Farm Credit Corporation**
The Act continues the **Farm Credit Corporation** as a body corporate under the new name **Farm Credit Canada**. This ensures continuity of operations, legal standing, and financial obligations.

- The **head office** may be located in Regina or elsewhere in Canada, as determined by the Governor in Council by order.
- Farm Credit Canada is an **agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada**, meaning it acts on behalf of the Crown in its capacity as a government entity.

---

#### **4. Purpose and Mandate**
The primary purpose of Farm Credit Canada is to **enhance rural Canada** by providing **specialized, personalized financial and business services** to:

- Farming operations (including family farms), and  
- Businesses related to farming (e.g., agri-processing, agri-logistics, farm supply).

The **primary focus** of the Corporation’s activities remains on **farming operations**, especially family farms, with a strong emphasis on rural economic development.

---

#### **5. Powers and Authorities of the Corporation**
Farm Credit Canada is granted broad powers to fulfill its mandate, including:

| Power | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Lending and Guaranteeing** | Can make loans or guarantee loans for purposes such as: <br> - Acquiring, developing, or improving farmland, buildings, or personal property used in farming <br> - Discharging liabilities or consolidating debts <br> - Covering operational costs of farming or farming-related businesses <br> - Supporting diversified enterprises on farmland <br> - Acquiring or improving real or personal property used in farming-related businesses |
| **Security Interests** | Can acquire and hold any type of security interest (e.g., mortgages, liens) for loans, guarantees, or agreements. Can also pursue judicial or other means to acquire, exchange, lease, sell, or dispose of such interests. |
| **Property Management** | Can acquire, exchange, lease, sell, or otherwise dispose of real and personal property used in managing its business. |
| **Government and Private Agreements** | May enter into agreements with federal or provincial government departments, agencies, or private entities. |
| **Counselling Services** | Can provide specialized business and financial counselling to farmers and rural businesses. |
| **Complementary Services** | Offers business services and products that complement those available from public and private sectors. |
| **Subsidiary Management** | Can incorporate, dissolve, amalgamate, or acquire/dispose of shares in subsidiaries. |
| **Lease Financing** | Provides lease financing for assets used in farming or farming-related businesses. |
| **Investments** | Can acquire and dispose of investments in farming operations or businesses related to farming, subject to approval by the Minister of Finance. |
| **Farmland Disposal** | May dispose of farmland it acquires, but only at **fair market value** and within **five years** of acquisition. |
| **Service Delivery** | Can deliver financial services (loans, guarantees, lease financing, equity investments) directly, through partnerships, or as a member of a financing syndicate. |
| **Interest and Fees** | Has the authority to determine interest rates on loans and fees for guarantees and services. |
| **Other Duties** | May carry out duties assigned by the Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food or under other Acts, provided it can recover the associated costs. |
| **Incidental Powers** | May do any other acts incidental to the exercise of its powers. |

> **Note**: The Corporation may, by written determination, define additional husbandry activities as "farming" for the purposes of the Act — allowing flexibility in adapting to evolving agricultural practices.

---

#### **6. Legal Capacity and Personhood**
Farm Credit Canada is granted **the capacity of a natural person** — meaning it can enter into contracts, sue and be sued, and hold property in its own name, as if it were a person.

---

#### **7. Board of Directors Structure**
The Corporation is governed by a **Board of Directors** composed of:

- One **Chairperson**
- One **President**
- Not fewer than **three** and not more than **ten** other directors

**Appointment Process**:
- Directors (excluding Chairperson and President) are appointed by the **Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food**, with **Governor in Council approval**.
- Terms are **not to exceed four years**, designed so that **no more than half of the directors** expire in any single year — ensuring continuity and stability.
- If a director is not appointed upon term expiry, the incumbent **continues in office** until reappointed or a successor is named.

**Governor in Council may appoint** a director temporarily in case of absence or inability.

**Reappointment**: Directors may be reappointed to the same or a different role.

**Vacancies**:
- If the Chairperson is absent or unable to act, the Board may appoint a director to act as Chairperson, who has full powers and duties.
- If the President is absent or unable to act, the Board may appoint an officer or employee to act as President.
- A person may **not act as President for more than 90 days** without **Governor in Council approval**.

---

#### **8. Board Meetings and Quorum**
- The Board must hold **at least one meeting every four months**.
- The Chairperson may designate the location of each meeting.
- A **quorum** consists of **five directors**.

---

#### **9. Appointment of Chairperson and President**
- The **Governor in Council** appoints both the Chairperson and President for terms considered appropriate.
- The **President** is the **chief executive officer** and responsible for supervising the day-to-day operations of the Corporation.
- The **Chairperson** presides over Board meetings and performs any duties or powers assigned under by-laws or Board resolutions.

---

#### **10. Committees**
- The Board may establish **executive committees** or other committees as it deems advisable.
- The Board may delegate its duties and powers to such committees.
- The Board may determine the composition, duties, and tenure of committee members.

---

#### **11. Employment and Authority Delegation**
- The Corporation may employ officers, consultants, agents, and employees as needed.
- It may fix their **remuneration and terms of employment**.
- The Board may delegate authority to officers, employees, or agents to act on matters **not specifically reserved** for the Board or a committee.

---

#### **12. Personal Liability Protection**
- **No director or employee** of Farm Credit Canada, nor any person acting under their instructions or authority under this Act, is **personally liable** for actions taken or omitted in **good faith** in the exercise of powers granted by the Act.

This provides a strong legal shield against personal financial exposure in the performance of official duties.

---

#### **13. Funding and Financial Support**
The Minister of Finance may provide financial support to the Corporation from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund**:

- **Before October 1, 2020**: Funding available up to the amount the Minister determines during the period from the Act’s coming into force to September 30, 2020. If redetermined during that period, the most recent amount applies.
- **After October 1, 2020**: Funding available up to the amount previously determined (or last redetermined), with the same rules applying.

> The Minister must **publish a notice** in the *Canada Gazette* as soon as feasible after determining or redetermining the amount.

---

#### **14. Capital Structure**
- The **capital** of the Corporation consists of:
  - All amounts paid to it under funding provisions, and
  - Its **retained earnings** (which may be positive or negative).

> [Note: A provision related to a specific cap on capital was repealed in 2020 (c. 5, s. 53).]
