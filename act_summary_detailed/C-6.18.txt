**Detailed Summary of the *Canada Infrastructure Bank Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Canada Infrastructure Bank Act* establishes the **Canada Infrastructure Bank (CIB)** as a federal Crown corporation designed to promote public-private partnerships in infrastructure development across Canada. The Bank’s primary mission is to **invest in and attract private-sector investment** in infrastructure projects that generate revenue and serve the public interest—such as supporting economic growth, improving sustainability, and enhancing national resilience.

The Act provides a comprehensive legal framework outlining the Bank’s structure, powers, governance, financial operations, accountability mechanisms, and operational limits.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Name and Citation**
- The Act is titled the *Canada Infrastructure Bank Act* and may be cited as such.
- It establishes the **Canada Infrastructure Bank (CIB)** as a corporate entity.

---

### **2. Definitions**
Key terms are defined to ensure clarity and consistency throughout the Act:
- **Bank**: The Canada Infrastructure Bank established under section 5(1).
- **Board**: The board of directors of the Bank.
- **Chairperson**: The Chair of the Board.
- **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**: The head of operations of the Bank.
- **Designated Minister**: A member of the Queen’s Privy Council designated by the Governor in Council to act as the Minister responsible for the Bank.
- **Director**: A member of the Board.
- **Infrastructure Projects**: Projects specified in Section 6 (e.g., transportation, energy, water, digital infrastructure, etc.).
- **Joint Venture**: An association of persons that does not constitute a corporation, partnership, or trust.
- **Person**: Includes trusts, partnerships, joint ventures, and associations of individuals or corporations.
- **Subsidiary / Wholly-Owned Subsidiary**: Defined under the *Financial Administration Act* (F.A.A.), meaning a company controlled by the Bank.

---

### **3. Appointment of the Designated Minister**
- The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet) may designate a member of the Privy Council to serve as the **designated Minister** for the Bank.
- This Minister is the **appropriate Minister** under **Part X of the Financial Administration Act**, which governs the administrative and financial responsibilities of Crown corporations.

---

### **4. Establishment of the Bank**
- A **corporation** is formally established as the Canada Infrastructure Bank.
- The **head office** is located in Canada, designated by the Governor in Council.
- The Bank has the **capacity and rights of a natural person**, meaning it can enter into contracts, own property, and incur obligations, subject to the provisions of this Act and regulations.

---

### **5. Status and Role of the Bank**
#### **Not an Agent of the Government**
The Bank is **not an agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada**, except in the following specific circumstances:
- Providing **investment advice** to government ministers, departments, agencies, and Crown corporations.
- **Collecting and disseminating data** on infrastructure (as per paragraph 7(1)(g)).
- Acting on behalf of the government in delivering **specific financial assistance programs** listed in paragraph 18(h).
- Carrying out any activity **conducive to its purpose** that the Governor in Council may specify by order.

> *Note: This distinction ensures the Bank operates independently while still serving public interest goals.*

#### **Purpose of the Bank**
The Bank’s purpose is to:
- Invest in infrastructure projects (in Canada or partially in Canada).
- Attract investment from **private sector and institutional investors**.
- Support projects that:
  - Generate revenue.
  - Contribute to economic growth.
  - Promote sustainability and resilience.

---

### **6. Powers and Functions**
The Bank may carry out the following activities to fulfill its purpose:
1. **Structure proposals and negotiate agreements** with project proponents and investors regarding government support.
2. **Invest directly** in infrastructure projects using innovative financial tools (e.g., green bonds, blended finance).
3. **Receive unsolicited proposals** from private or institutional investors.
4. **Support evidence-based decision-making** in infrastructure planning.
5. **Act as a centre of expertise** for projects involving significant private investment.
6. **Provide advice to all levels of government** on infrastructure matters.
7. **Collect and disseminate data** with federal, provincial, and municipal governments to monitor infrastructure health and inform investment decisions.
8. **Perform any other function** conducive to its purpose, as specified by the Governor in Council by order.

> *Important: Any order under paragraph (1)(h) ceases to have effect on the second anniversary of its issuance.*

---

### **7. Governance Structure**
#### **Board of Directors**
- Composed of:
  - The **Chairperson**.
  - **Not fewer than 8 and not more than 11 other directors**.
- Each director (except Chairperson) is appointed by the Governor in Council for a term of **up to four years**, with a rotation policy to ensure no more than **half of the directors** serve in any one year.
- The **Chairperson** is appointed by the Governor in Council for a term deemed appropriate.
- The **designated Minister** may establish a **committee** to advise on director appointments, including representation from the Board.
- The Minister may also consult with provinces and other stakeholders.

#### **Term Extensions and Vacancies**
- If a director is not appointed before the end of an incumbent’s term (except Chairperson), the incumbent **continues in office** until a successor is named.
- If the **Chairperson is absent or unable to act**, the Board may appoint one of the other directors to act temporarily, **but only for up to 180 days without Governor in Council approval**.

#### **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**
- Appointed by the **Board**, with term to be fixed by the Board.
- Appointment and term require **approval by the Governor in Council**.
- The CEO must serve **full-time**.
- The Governor in Council may terminate or suspend the CEO after consulting with the Board; the Board may also terminate or suspend the CEO with Governor in Council approval.
- In the absence or incapacity of the CEO, the **designated Minister** may appoint a temporary acting CEO.

#### **Board Committees**
- The Board may establish committees for specific purposes.
- Committees may be delegated powers over **all matters not specifically reserved to the Board**.

#### **Eligibility Requirements**
- **Ineligible individuals** for appointment as CEO, Chairperson, or director:
  - Under 18 years of age.
  - Status of bankrupt.
  - Employed in federal public administration or by provincial/municipal authorities.
  - A member of Parliament (House of Commons or Senate) or provincial legislature.
- **Cannot hold both Chairperson and CEO roles simultaneously.**

#### **Remuneration**
- **Directors** and **officers** are paid by the Bank:
  - Remuneration rates for **Chairperson and directors** are set by the Governor in Council.
  - Remuneration for the **CEO** is set by the Governor in Council **on the recommendation of the Board**, which considers skills and market comparables.
- Directors receive **reasonable travel and living expenses** when absent from their ordinary residence.
- The CEO receives **reasonable travel and living expenses** when absent from their ordinary place of work.

#### **Employment Status**
- Directors and officers are deemed **employees** under the *Government Employees Compensation Act*.
- They are considered **employed in the federal public administration** for purposes of regulations under the *Aeronautics Act*.

---

### **8. Financial and Operational Requirements**
#### **Annual Reporting**
- The Bank must submit annually to the designated Minister:
  - A **corporate plan** (for approval by the Governor in Council).
  - An **operating budget** (for approval by the Treasury Board).
  - A **capital budget** (for approval by the Treasury Board).

#### **Financial Powers**
The Bank has broad powers to manage its investments and finances, including:
- Making equity investments or loans to persons.
- Extending credit or providing liquidity.
- Acquiring and holding securities, including rights in securities (especially in Quebec).
- Surrendering or exchanging securities.
- Realizing or disposing of investments.
- Entering into agreements as an agent or mandatary for government bodies or private entities.
- Accepting real or personal property as security.
- Charging interest and fees for services rendered.
- Acquiring, holding, exchanging, selling, or leasing interests in entities or real/personal property.
- Retaining proceeds from property dispositions.

> These powers are designed to enable flexible, innovative financing models.

#### **Loan Guarantees**
- The Bank **may recommend** to the designated Minister that the Minister of Finance approve a **loan guarantee** for an infrastructure project.
- If the designated Minister concurs, they **recommend the Minister of Finance** to approve the guarantee.
- The **Minister of Finance must approve** any loan guarantee.
- The Bank may also recommend that the Minister of Finance make a **direct loan** to a project.
- The Minister of Finance may approve such loans or guarantees **on the recommendation of the designated Minister**.

#### **Funding Authority**
- The **Minister of Finance** may pay the Bank up to **$35 billion** from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund**, or a higher amount authorized by appropriation acts.
- At the Bank’s request, the Minister of Finance may **lend money** to the Bank on terms set by the Minister.
