The **Budget Implementation Act, 2022, No. 1** is a comprehensive piece of legislation enacted by the Parliament of Canada to implement key provisions of the federal budget, primarily focusing on fiscal policy, regulatory changes, and the modernization of certain laws. Below is a **detailed and structured summary** of the Act, organized by thematic sections and provisions.

### 5. **Transitional Provisions for the Special Import Measures Act (SIMA)**

This section ensures continuity of legal proceedings related to trade complaints under the now-repealed *Special Import Measures Act*.

#### âœ… Key Transitional Rules:
- If a **complaint** was filed **before the commencement day** (date of royal assent):
  - Any **proceedings, actions, or processes** related to dumping, subsidizing, or circumvention of duties **continue under the former Act**.
- If the **Tribunal** received a request for an **interim review** before the commencement day:
  - The review is **continued or initiated** under the former Act.
- If the Tribunal initiated an **interim review on its own**:
  - It continues under the former Act.
- If a **notice of expiry** was published before the commencement day:
  - Any expiry review is **continued or initiated** under the former Act.

> This provision prevents disruption to ongoing trade disputes and ensures legal certainty for stakeholders.
