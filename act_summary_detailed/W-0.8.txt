**Detailed Summary of the *Wage Earner Protection Program Act***

### **Overview**

The *Wage Earner Protection Program Act* (WEPPA) establishes a federal program designed to protect workers who have been employed by a company that becomes insolvent â€” such as through bankruptcy, receivership, or foreign insolvency proceedings. The Act ensures that eligible employees receive payment for **wages owed** to them, up to a statutory maximum, in cases where the employer is unable to meet its financial obligations.

This legislation operates within the broader framework of Canadaâ€™s bankruptcy and insolvency laws, particularly the *Bankruptcy and Insolvency Act (BIA)* and the *Companiesâ€™ Creditors Arrangement Act (CCAA)*, and is administered by the **Canada Industrial Relations Board (CIRB)**, with oversight from the **Minister of Employment and Social Development**.

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Title and Citation**
- The Act is titled the **Wage Earner Protection Program Act** and may be cited as such.

---

### **2. Definitions**

#### **Board**
- Refers to the **Canada Industrial Relations Board (CIRB)**, established under Section 9 of the *Canada Labour Code*.  
- The Board plays a central role in reviewing appeals and ensuring fair administration of the program.

#### **Eligible Wages**
This is a critical definition that determines what wages can be paid under the program.

- **Eligible wages** include:
  - **Wages earned during a specific period**, which is the **longer of two periods**:
    1. The **six-month period ending on the date of bankruptcy or the first day a receiver is appointed**.
    2. A **six-month period beginning six months before** one of the following key dates:
      - Filing of a proposal (or notice of intention) under **Division I of Part III of the BIA**.
      - Commencement of proceedings under the **Companiesâ€™ Creditors Arrangement Act (CCAA)**.
      - A courtâ€™s determination under subsection 5(5) of the BIA.
    - This second period runs from **six months before** the event to the date of bankruptcy or the end of the receiverâ€™s duties.
  - **Termination pay and severance pay** related to employment that ended during:
    - The above six-month period, **or**
    - The period immediately following that period (from the day after it ends until the **trustee is discharged or the receiver completes their duties**).

> âš ï¸ **Important Exclusion**: A proposal that has a **certificate under section 65.3 of the BIA** is **not considered** a valid proposal for purposes of triggering the program. Similarly, a notice of intention made in relation to such a proposal is excluded.

#### **Wages**
- Broadly defined to include:
  - Salaries, commissions, compensation for services rendered
  - Vacation pay
  - Termination pay and severance pay
  - Any other amounts prescribed by regulation

#### **Trustee and Receiver**
- **Trustee**: Includes a **monitor** under the CCAA (as defined in subsection 2(1) of the CCAA).
- **Receiver**: Defined as a receiver under subsection 243(2) of the BIA.
- An employer is considered to be in **receivership** when any of its property is under the control or possession of a receiver.

> All words and expressions used in the Act have the same meaning as in the *Bankruptcy and Insolvency Act*, unless otherwise specified.

### **3. Eligibility for Payment**

An individual may receive a payment under the program if **all of the following conditions are met**:

#### **(a) Employment Ended for a Prescribed Reason**
- As defined by regulation (e.g., termination, resignation, layoff).

#### **(b) One of the Following Applies:**
1. The former employer is **bankrupt**.
2. The former employer is **subject to a receivership**.
3. The employer is the subject of a **recognized foreign proceeding** under subsection 270(1) of the BIA, and:
   - A court determines under subsection (2) that the foreign proceeding meets criteria prescribed by regulation.
   - A **trustee is appointed** for the purposes of this Act.
4. The employer is subject to:
   - Proceedings under **Division I of Part III of the BIA**, or
   - Proceedings under the **CCAA**, and
   - A court determines under subsection (5) that the criteria prescribed by regulation are met.

> âœ… **Key Provision**: If all conditions in (b)(iii) are met, the former employer is **deemed bankrupt**, and the **date of bankruptcy is deemed to be the day all conditions are satisfied**.

#### **(c) The Individual is Owed Eligible Wages**
- The individual must have a legitimate claim to eligible wages.

- Occupied a **managerial position** with the employer;
