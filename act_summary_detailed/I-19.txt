**Detailed Summary of the *International Development Research Centre Act***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *International Development Research Centre Act* establishes the **International Development Research Centre (IDRC)** as a Canadian non-governmental, non-profit research institution dedicated to advancing scientific and technical knowledge to address economic and social challenges in developing regions of the world. The Act outlines the structure, governance, powers, financial mechanisms, and reporting obligations of the Centre, emphasizing its role in promoting international cooperation, innovation, and sustainable development.

---

## **1. Name and Definitions**  
The Act may be cited as the *International Development Research Centre Act*. Key definitions include:

- **Board**: Refers to the Board of Governors of the Centre.  
- **Centre**: The International Development Research Centre established under Section 3.  
- **Chairperson**: The head of the Board (note: the former "Chairman" was repealed in 2010).  
- **Governor**: A member of the Board.  
- **Minister**: A member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada designated by the Governor in Council to oversee the Centre.  
- **President**: The chief executive officer of the Centre.  
- **Research**: Includes scientific or technical inquiry aimed at discovering new knowledge or applying existing knowledge to solve economic and social problems.  
- **Science**: Encompasses both natural and social sciences.

---

## **2. Establishment of the Centre**  
The Centre is established as a **corporation** with a **Board of Governors** composed of:
- A Chairperson,  
- A President,  
- Not more than 12 other governors (total of up to 14 members).

This structure ensures a balance between leadership, governance, and representation across disciplines and regions.

---

## **3. Objects and Mandate**  
The primary objectives of the Centre are:

1. **Initiate, encourage, support, and conduct research** focused on:
   - Problems in developing regions globally,
   - Methods for applying and adapting scientific, technical, and other knowledge to promote economic and social development.

2. **Enlist talent** from natural and social scientists and technologists in Canada and abroad.

3. **Help developing regions** build:
   - Research capabilities,
   - Innovative skills,
   - Institutions capable of solving local development challenges.

4. **Encourage international coordination** of development research.

5. **Foster cooperation** between developed and developing regions to achieve mutual benefits.

These objectives reflect a strong focus on **capacity building**, **knowledge transfer**, and **global equity** in development.

---

## **4. Powers of the Centre**  
The Centre is granted broad powers to fulfill its mandate, including:

- **Establish and operate** information and data centres and research facilities.
- **Initiate and carry out** research and technical development, including pilot projects or plant operations, until results can be applied.
- **Support or assist** research conducted by:
  - Governments,
  - International, public, or private organizations,
  - Individuals.
- **Enter into contracts or agreements** with governments, organizations, or individuals.
- **Recognize and publicize** outstanding contributions to international development (e.g., awards, publications).
- **Sponsor conferences, seminars, and meetings** to promote dialogue and knowledge exchange.
- **Acquire, hold, and dispose of real property** (land, buildings) at its discretion.
- **Hold, invest, and expend funds** (money, securities, property) as permitted under the Act.
- **Expend funds** allocated by Parliament or earned through operations.
- Perform **other activities** that support its mission.

These powers give the Centre significant autonomy in managing its operations and research activities.

---

## **5. Governance Structure and Appointment Rules**

### **Chairperson**
- Appointed by the Governor in Council.
- Holds office during "pleasure" (at the discretion of the government).
- Maximum term: **5 years**.

### **President**
- First President: Appointed by the Governor in Council for up to 5 years.
- Subsequent Presidents: Appointed by the Governor in Council **on the recommendation of the Board**, for up to 5 years.
- Serves as **chief executive officer** and oversees all operations and staff.

### **Governors**
- Appointed by the Governor in Council.
- Maximum term: **4 years**.
- To ensure continuity, no more than half of the governors may have their terms expire in any one year.
- **Re-appointment**: Retiring governors may be reappointed.

### **Vice-Chairperson**
- Elected by the Board from among the governors.
- Assumes duties of the Chairperson in case of:
  - Absence,
  - Incapacity,
  - Vacancy of the Chairperson’s office.

### **Temporary Substitutes**
- The Governor in Council may appoint a **temporary substitute governor** if a governor (not Chairperson or President) is unable to perform duties.
- A temporary appointment may be made for the remainder of the term if a vacancy occurs during a term.

### **Remuneration**
- Governors are paid **remuneration and expenses** as determined by the Governor in Council.

---

## **6. Eligibility and Composition Requirements**

To ensure diversity, expertise, and legitimacy, the Board must include:

- **Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, and at least six other governors** must be **Canadian citizens**.
- **At least eight governors** must have:
  - Experience in international development, or
  - Training or experience in natural or social sciences or technology.
- **Two Canadian citizen governors** (excluding Chairperson and Vice-Chairperson) may be appointed from:
  - The **Senate** or
  - The **House of Commons**.
  - These members are **not paid remuneration**, but may receive **expenses**.
  - They remain eligible to **sit and vote** in their respective Houses, even while serving on the Board.

---

## **7. Executive Committee**

- Composed of:
  - Chairperson,
  - President,
  - At least **three other governors** elected annually by the Board.
- Must include a **majority of Canadian citizens**.
- Meets **at least four times per year**.
- Requires a **quorum of three members**, at least two of whom must be Canadian citizens.
- Powers:
  - May be assigned by the Board via by-law.
  - Must submit minutes of proceedings to the Board after each meeting.
- Chairperson of the executive committee is appointed from among its members.

---

## **8. Fellowships**

- The Board may appoint individuals who have made **outstanding contributions** in international development as **Fellows**.
- The Centre may prescribe:
  - Duration of fellowship,
  - Stipend (if any).
- This recognizes excellence and encourages leadership in development research.

---

## **9. Committees and Staff**

- The Board may establish **advisory or other committees** under by-laws.
- The Board may appoint **officers, agents, and employees** as needed.
- By-laws may regulate:
  - Committee composition, salaries, and expenses,
  - Duties and conduct of staff,
  - Employment conditions and remuneration,
  - Meeting procedures,
  - Assignment of powers to the executive committee,
  - General management of the Centre.

---

## **10. Head Office and Meetings**

- The **head office** is initially designated by the Governor in Council and may be changed by by-law approved by the Governor in Council.
- The Board meets **at least twice per year**, with **at least one meeting at the head office**.
- Meetings are presided over by the **Chairperson**.

---

## **11. Quorum Requirements**

- A **quorum of the Board** is **seven governors**, at least **four of whom must be Canadian citizens**.
- However, **proceedings are invalid** if more than seven governors attend but **a majority are not Canadian citizens**.

This ensures that decisions reflect Canadian interests and values, especially in matters involving national policy or funding.

---

## **12. By-Laws and Governance Authority**

With the **approval of the Governor in Council**, the Board may adopt by-laws covering:

- Committee structure and compensation,
- Staff duties and conduct,
- Employment terms and pay,
- Meeting procedures,
- Delegation of powers to the executive committee,
- General management.

This provides flexibility while maintaining accountability and oversight.

---

## **13. Public Service Status and Tax Status**

- The Centre is **not an agent of Her Majesty** and **not part of the public service**.
- However:
  - Officers and employees are **deemed to be employed in the public service** for the purposes of the **Public Service Superannuation Act**.
  - The Centre is **deemed a public service corporation** under Section 37 of that Act.
- The **Public Service Superannuation Act does not apply to governors**, unless the Governor in Council specifically directs otherwise.
- The Centre is **deemed a registered charity** under the *Income Tax Act*, allowing it to receive tax-exempt status and donations.

---

## **14. Financial Mechanism: The IDRC Account**

To ensure financial stability and transparency, the Centre establishes a dedicated account:

- **Name**: *International Development Research Centre Account* (the "Account").
- **Location**: Managed in a Canadian bank or an authorized foreign bank **not subject to restrictions under Section 524(2) of the Bank Act** (i.e., no limitations on Canadian business operations).
- **Funding Sources**:
  - All **revenues** from research, technical development, or services provided under contracts or agreements.
  - All **expenditures** related to research or services are charged to the Account.
- **Initial Grant**:
  - The **Minister of Finance
