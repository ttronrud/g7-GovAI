### **Detailed Summary of the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada Act**

---

#### **1. Introduction and Citation**
The **Financial Consumer Agency of Canada Act** (FCACA) establishes the **Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC)** as a federal government agency responsible for protecting consumers in the financial sector. The Act may be cited as the *Financial Consumer Agency of Canada Act*.

This legislation was enacted by Her Majesty, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons of Canada, to ensure that financial institutions, payment card networks, and related entities operate in a manner that safeguards consumers, promotes financial literacy, and ensures the safety and security of consumer-driven banking.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

The Act provides a comprehensive set of definitions that apply throughout the legislation, particularly in sections 3 to 34. These include:

- **Agency**: Refers to the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada established under Section 3.
- **Bank Holding Company / Insurance Holding Company**: Defined in accordance with the *Bank Act* and *Insurance Companies Act*, respectively.
- **Commissioner**: The head of the Agency, appointed by the Governor in Council.
- **Consumer Provision**: A broad list of regulatory provisions from key financial acts (e.g., *Bank Act*, *Insurance Companies Act*, *Cooperative Credit Associations Act*, and *Green Shield Canada Act*) that are relevant to consumer protection. These include specific sections and subsections related to transparency, disclosure, and customer rights.
- **Financial Institution**: Includes banks, authorized foreign banks, trust and loan companies, retail associations, insurance companies, and Green Shield Canada.
- **External Complaints Body**: As defined in the *Bank Act*, refers to bodies that handle consumer complaints (e.g., the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions).
- **Payment Card Network Operator**: Entities that manage or operate payment card networks (e.g., Visa, Mastercard), including setting standards for transaction processing and information transfer.
- **Penalty**: Refers to administrative monetary penalties imposed for non-compliance.
- **Prescribed**: Means prescribed by regulation.
- **Senior Deputy Commissioner**: A senior officer specifically appointed to oversee consumer-driven banking.
- **Technical Standards Body**: A body designated under the *Consumer-Driven Banking Act* to develop standards for financial products and services.
- **Participating Entity**: Entities involved in consumer-driven banking, as defined in the *Consumer-Driven Banking Act*.

---

#### **3. Purpose of the Act**

The primary purpose of the FCACA is to:

- Ensure effective **supervision** of financial institutions, external complaints bodies, payment card network operators, participating entities, and technical standards bodies.
- Protect the **rights and interests of consumers** of financial products and services and the general public.
- Enhance the **safety and security** of consumer-driven banking.
- Strengthen **financial literacy** among Canadians.

This is achieved through a combination of oversight, monitoring, public awareness, and the promotion of responsible business practices.

---

#### **4. Establishment of the Agency**

- The **Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (FCAC)** is formally established as a government agency.
- The **Minister of Finance** presides over and is ultimately responsible for the Agency.
- The Agency operates independently but under the ministerial oversight framework.

---

#### **5. Core Objectives of the Agency**

The Agency has several key objectives, grouped into three main areas:

---

##### **A. General Consumer Protection and Financial Institution Oversight**

The Agency must supervise financial institutions and the external complaints body to determine compliance with:

- **Consumer provisions** applicable to them (as defined in the "consumer provision" list).
- **Terms and conditions or undertakings** imposed or required by the Minister under Acts listed in Schedule 1 or directions issued under this Act.

**Additional Objectives:**
- Protect consumer rights while allowing financial institutions to efficiently manage operations.
- Promote the adoption of **voluntary codes of conduct** and **public commitments** by financial institutions to protect customer interests.
- Monitor implementation of these voluntary measures.
- Monitor and evaluate **emerging trends and issues** affecting consumers and make findings public.
- Strengthen **financial literacy** and consumer awareness about:
  - Obligations of financial institutions and external complaints bodies.
  - Consumer rights and protections.
- Foster collaboration with federal and provincial departments, agencies, financial institutions, and consumer organizations to improve understanding of financial services.

> *(Note: Some of these subsections were repealed in 2018 via Bill C-27, s. 338 — the summary reflects the original content as enacted.)*

---

##### **B. Oversight of Payment Card Network Operators**

The Agency has specific responsibilities regarding payment card networks:

- Supervise compliance with the **Payment Card Networks Act** and its regulations.
- Promote the adoption of policies and procedures to implement the Act’s provisions.
- Monitor **voluntary codes of conduct** and public commitments made by payment card operators.
- Promote public awareness of the obligations of payment card operators under these codes or the Act.

---

##### **C. Oversight of Consumer-Driven Banking**

Consumer-driven banking (e.g., consumer-directed banking products like personal loans, credit cards, and savings tools) is a key focus area. The Agency must:

- Supervise **participating entities**, the **external complaints body**, and the **technical standards body** to ensure compliance with:
  - The *Consumer-Driven Banking Act*.
  - Any terms, conditions, or undertakings imposed or required by the Minister under Schedule 1 or directions under this Act.

- Monitor and evaluate **trends and emerging issues** in consumer-driven banking (e.g., product innovation, market developments, consumer behavior).

- Foster **participation in consumer-driven banking** through collaboration with:
  - Federal and provincial government bodies.
  - Financial institutions.
  - Consumer and advocacy organizations.

- Promote **public understanding** of consumer-driven banking and related issues.

---

#### **6. Appointment of Key Officers**

##### **Commissioner**
- Appointed by the **Governor in Council**.
- Has the rank and powers of a **deputy head of a department**.
- Holds office for **up to five years**, renewable, unless removed for cause.
- Upon term expiration, eligible for reappointment.
- In case of absence or incapacity, the **Minister may appoint a qualified person** to act for up to **90 days** with **Governor in Council approval**.
- Remuneration is fixed by the Governor in Council.
- Entitled to **reasonable travel and living expenses**.
- Deemed to be **employed in the public service** for purposes of:
  - *Public Service Superannuation Act*.
  - *Government Employees Compensation Act*.
  - *Aeronautics Act* (section 9).

##### **Senior Deputy Commissioner for Consumer-Driven Banking**
- Appointed by the Commissioner with **Minister’s concurrence**.
- Responsible for all matters related to **consumer-driven banking**.
- Acts under the instructions of the Commissioner.
- If absent or incapacitated, a qualified person may be appointed to act for up to **90 days**, with **Minister’s approval**.
- Supervises consumer-driven banking under the *Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions Act*, subject to that Act’s supervision.
- May collect personal information relevant to consumer-driven banking objectives.
- Required to **publish prescribed information** about consumer-driven banking in a timely and appropriate manner.

##### **Deputy Commissioners**
- The Commissioner may appoint one or more **Deputy Commissioners**.
- These officers act under the Commissioner’s instructions.

##### **Employee Authority**
- Employees of the Agency (including Deputy Commissioners) may be authorized to exercise powers or perform duties of the Commissioner or Senior Deputy Commissioner **if appointed in a capacity appropriate** to the function.
- This includes:
  - Human resources management powers (e.g., setting terms and conditions of employment).
  - Employer-employee relations.
- The Commissioner may authorize individuals in the federal public administration to exercise powers related to human resources management.
- Such authorizations may be **revoked or modified** at the Commissioner’s discretion.

---

#### **7. Business Planning and Ministerial Directions**

- The **Commissioner must submit a business plan annually** to the Minister for approval.
  - The plan must include:
    - Agency objectives.
    - Strategies to achieve those objectives.
    - Operating and capital budgets.
- The **Minister may issue written directions** to the Agency to:
  - Strengthen consumer protection.
  - Increase public confidence in consumer protection.
  - Enhance safety and security in consumer-driven banking.
  - Improve financial literacy.
- After implementing a direction, the Commissioner must **notify the Minister immediately**.
- Compliance with a direction is considered to be in the **best interest of the Agency**.
- The **Statutory Instruments Act does not apply** to these directions.
- A notice must be published in the *Canada Gazette* when a direction is implemented.

> This mechanism allows the Minister to guide the Agency’s priorities without requiring legislative change.

---

#### **8. Agreements and Collaborations**

- The Agency may enter into **agreements or arrangements** with:
  - Federal or provincial departments/agencies.
  - Private entities or organizations.
- Agreements with **provincial agencies** require **Governor in Council approval**.
- The Agency may enter into agreements with **payment card network operators**, including:
  - Sharing information.
  - Paying fees.

> This enables the Agency to collaborate effectively with industry stakeholders.

---

#### **9. Advisory Committees**

- The Commissioner, on advice of the **Senior Deputy Commissioner**, may establish **advisory committees**.
- These committees will advise or assist on matters related to **consumer-driven banking**.
- Committees may be established to provide expert input, facilitate dialogue, and support policy development.
