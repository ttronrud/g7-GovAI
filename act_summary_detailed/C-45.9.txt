### ðŸ” Definitions of Core Crimes

#### 1. **Genocide**
- An act or omission committed **with intent to destroy**, in whole or in part, an **identifiable group of persons**.
- The act must constitute genocide under:
  - Customary international law
  - Conventional international law
  - General principles of law recognized by the community of nations
- **Note**: The definition includes intent, and does not require a violation of domestic law at the time.

#### 2. **Crime Against Humanity**
- Any act or omission committed against:
  - A **civilian population** or an **identifiable group**
- That constitutes a crime against humanity under:
  - Customary or conventional international law
  - General principles of law recognized by the community of nations
- Includes specific acts such as:
  - Murder
  - Extermination
  - Enslavement
  - Deportation
  - Imprisonment
  - Torture
  - Sexual violence
  - Persecution
  - Any other inhumane act or omission
- **Note**: The crime is not limited to breaches of domestic law at the time of commission.

#### 3. **War Crime**
- An act or omission committed **during an armed conflict** that constitutes a war crime under:
  - Customary international law
  - Conventional international law applicable to armed conflicts
- Again, the crime is recognized independently of whether domestic law was violated.

### 1. **Military Commander Liability (Section 5)**

A military commander commits an indictable offence if:

- The commander **fails to exercise proper control** over a person under their **effective command and control**.
- As a result, that person commits:
  - A **crime against humanity** (Section 4)
  - A **war crime** (Section 6)
- The commander:
  - **Knows** or is **criminally negligent in failing to know** that the person is about to commit or is committing such an offence.
- The commander **fails to take, as soon as practicable**, all necessary and reasonable measures to:
  - Prevent or repress the offence
  - Submit the matter to competent authorities for investigation and prosecution

> Applies **both inside and outside Canada**.

> **Includes**:
> - A person effectively acting as a military commander
> - A person commanding police with authority comparable to a military commander

---

### 2. **Superior Liability (Section 6)**

A **superior** (non-military) commits an indictable offence if:

- Fails to exercise proper control over a person under their **effective authority and control**.
- As a result, the person commits:
  - A crime against humanity or war crime
- The superior:
  - **Knows** the person is about to commit or is committing the offence, or
  - **Consciously disregards** information clearly indicating such an offence is being committed
- The offence relates to activities within the superiorâ€™s **effective authority and control**
- The superior fails to:
  - Prevent or repress the offence
  - Submit the matter to competent authorities for investigation and prosecution

> Applies **outside Canada** and **before or after** the Actâ€™s coming into force.

> **Note**: "Superior" means a person in authority **other than a military commander**.
