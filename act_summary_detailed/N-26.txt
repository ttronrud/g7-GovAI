**Detailed Summary of the *Northern Pipeline Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Northern Pipeline Act* is a federal statute enacted in Canada to implement and enforce the **1977 Canada–United States Agreement on the Northern Pipeline**, which governs the construction and operation of a natural gas pipeline that transports natural gas from Alaska across Canada to Alberta. The Act establishes a dedicated federal agency, the **Northern Pipeline Agency (NPA)**, to manage and coordinate all aspects of the pipeline project, ensuring compliance with international obligations, promoting national energy interests, and addressing regional and Indigenous concerns.

This Act is binding on Her Majesty and is designed to fulfill both **international commitments** and **domestic policy objectives** related to energy security, economic development, environmental protection, and Indigenous rights.

---

## **Key Definitions and Key Terms**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Administrator** | A senior official (either appointed by the Governor in Council or designated from the Canadian Energy Regulator) who exercises powers under the Minister’s direction. |
| **Agency** | The Northern Pipeline Agency, established to oversee the pipeline project. Presided over by the Minister. |
| **Agreement** | The Canada–U.S. Agreement dated September 20, 1977 (Schedule I), including amendments related to pipeline specifications (e.g., 56-inch diameter pipe at 1,080 psi between Whitehorse and Caroline, Alberta). |
| **Commissioner** | The head of the Agency, appointed by the Governor in Council, serving as deputy to the Minister. |
| **Designated Officer** | A commissioner of the Canadian Energy Regulator (Regulator) who is appointed or designated to act as Administrator or deputy. |
| **Hearing** | Refers to the 1977 National Energy Board decision titled *“Reasons for Decision, Northern Pipelines”*, which approved the pipeline project and included key technical specifications. |
| **Pipeline** | The full infrastructure system for transporting natural gas from Alaska through Canada, including all associated facilities (tanks, compressors, storage, communication systems, etc.). |
| **Regulator** | The Canadian Energy Regulator (CER), established under the *Canadian Energy Regulator Act*. For purposes of this Act, it is treated as equivalent to the former *National Energy Board* (now defunct). |
| **Company** | Any entity issued a certificate of public convenience and necessity under this Act. |

> **Note:** The *Board* (National Energy Board) was **repealed in 2019**, and the *Regulator* now serves as the successor body.

---

## **Core Objectives of the Act**

The Act has five primary objectives:

1. **To carry out and give effect to the Canada–U.S. Agreement**  
   Ensures Canada fulfills its international obligations regarding the pipeline, including technical specifications and cross-border cooperation.

2. **To carry out federal responsibilities**  
   Through the Agency, the Government of Canada assumes responsibility for planning, construction, and operation of the pipeline.

3. **To facilitate efficient planning and construction**  
   Balances development needs with local, regional, and Indigenous interests. Requires **just and equitable treatment** of Indigenous claims related to land use.

4. **To promote consultation and coordination**  
   Mandates coordination with the governments of:
   - British Columbia
   - Saskatchewan
   - Alberta
   - Yukon
   - Northwest Territories  
   Through formal mechanisms such as the **Federal-Provincial Consultative Council** and advisory councils.

5. **To maximize social, economic, and industrial benefits**  
   - Prioritizes **Canadian employment** and **industrial participation**  
   - Minimizes adverse social and environmental impacts on affected communities  
   - Ensures **competitive procurement** of goods and services while maximizing Canadian involvement

## **Establishment and Governance of the Northern Pipeline Agency**

- The **Northern Pipeline Agency** is established as a federal agency under the Minister’s authority.
- The **Minister** (designated by the Governor in Council) presides over the Agency and has overall management and direction.
- The **Commissioner** is appointed by the Governor in Council as a deputy to the Minister and holds the rank and powers of a deputy head of a federal department.
- The **Administrator** may be:
  - Appointed directly by the Governor in Council, or
  - Designated from among the commissioners of the Regulator.
- If no Regulator commissioner is designated, the Governor in Council may appoint a temporary substitute.

> **Key Provision:** Paragraph 29(c) of the *Canadian Energy Regulator Act* does **not apply** to any commissioner designated as Administrator or deputy — meaning they are not subject to certain restrictions on authority.

---

## **Powers and Duties of Key Officials**

### **Administrator / Designated Officer**
- May exercise **powers of the Regulator** under the *Canadian Energy Regulator Act*, **except** for specific provisions (e.g., sections 80–86, 92, 95–98, 100, 101(3), 102(3), 103(2)–(4), 109–112, 115–173, 181, 183(1), (2), (4)–(6), (8)–(11), 184, 186, 187, 195–197, 213, 214, 225–240, 241(1), (4), 245, and Parts 7 and 9).
- May **certify**:
  - Approved plans, profiles, and reference books (for use under section 198(d) of the CER Act)
  - Permits issued under subsection 208(2) of the CER Act
- Can issue **orders and directions** to pipeline companies to enforce compliance with conditions in Schedule III.

### **Minister’s Powers**
- Can transfer powers and duties to the Minister from any member of the Queen’s Privy Council or federal department/agency.
- May enter into **agreements** with provincial governments or territorial authorities to facilitate coordination.
- May **consult** with provincial and territorial governments.
- Oversees **planning, construction, and procurement** of the pipeline.
- Must **consult with U.S. authorities** on any matter arising under the Agreement.

---

## **Employment and Resources**

- The Agency may employ professional staff (technical, scientific, administrative) and hire **temporary experts** with specialized knowledge.
- Employees are deemed to be in the **federal public service** for:
  - *Public Service Superannuation Act*
  - *Government Employees Compensation Act*
- The Agency may **second officers** from other departments and access their advice, subject to confidentiality rules.

---

## **Reporting and Oversight**

- The **Minister must prepare and submit an annual report** on the Agency’s operations by **December 31 of the year following each fiscal year**.
- The report must be laid before **both Houses of Parliament** within 15 days of the start of parliamentary session, or on the first 15 days after the session begins.

---

## **Federal-Provincial Consultative Council**

Established under the Governor in Council’s authority:

- **Composition**:
  - The Commissioner of the Agency
  - A representative of Yukon (nominated by the Legislature of Yukon)
  - One representative each from:
    - British Columbia
    - Saskatchewan
    - Alberta

- **Functions**:
  - Meet **at least every three months**
  - Coordinate activities of the Agency and provincial/territorial governments
  - Ensure **consistent policy approach** across jurisdictions
  - Establish **committees** to advise on specific issues related to the Act’s objectives

> This structure ensures intergovernmental cooperation and shared responsibility.

---

## **Advisory Councils**

- The Governor in Council may establish **one or more advisory councils** (up to 10 members each), selected from outside the federal public service.
- These councils provide expert advice on key issues.
- One council, the **Yukon Advisory Council**, must include representation of:
  - Geographic areas
  - Indigenous interests
- Members are entitled to:
  - Be paid **attendance fees**
  - Receive **reasonable travel and living expenses** when performing duties away from home

---

## **Certificates of Public Convenience and Necessity**

- A certificate is **declared issued** to each company listed in **Schedule II** for the portion of the pipeline route specified in the Agreement.
- These certificates are **deemed to be issued under section 52 of the National Energy Board Act** on **April 13, 1978**.
- All certificates are subject to the **terms and conditions** set out in **Schedule III**.

> This legal mechanism provides the legal basis for pipeline companies to operate under federal oversight.

---

## **Terms and Conditions in Schedule III**

Schedule III contains critical operational and compliance requirements, including:

- **Provisions on Indigenous rights and claims**
- **Prohibitions on termination or amendment** of shareholder agreements without prior approval from:
  - The **Governor in Council**
  - The **Commission of the Regulator**

> Specifically, the shareholder agreements of:
> - Westcoast Transmission Company Limited  
> - Alberta Gas Trunk Line Company Limited  
> - Foothills Pipe Lines (Yukon) Ltd.  
> - Alberta Natural Gas Company Ltd.  
> ... are **protected from termination or amendment** without such approvals.
