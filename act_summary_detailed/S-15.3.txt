### **3. Governance and Leadership Structure**

#### A. **Canadian Endangered Species Conservation Council (CESCC)**
- **Composition**:  
  - Minister of the Environment  
  - Minister of Fisheries and Oceans  
  - Minister responsible for Parks Canada Agency  
  - Provincial and territorial ministers responsible for wildlife conservation  

- **Functions**:
  - Provides **general direction** to COSEWIC on assessments and classifications.
  - Oversees the **preparation and implementation** of recovery strategies and action plans.
  - Coordinates activities among federal, provincial, and territorial governments to ensure consistent and effective conservation efforts.

#### B. **National Aboriginal Council on Species at Risk**
- Established by the Minister to include **six representatives from Aboriginal peoples of Canada**, selected based on recommendations from Aboriginal organizations.
- **Roles**:
  - Advises the Minister on administration of SARA.
  - Provides advice and recommendations to the CESCC.
- Emphasizes **Indigenous knowledge and participation** in species conservation.

#### C. **Advisory Committees**
- The Minister may establish one or more **advisory committees** to assist with:
  - Administration of the Act
  - Specific technical or policy issues related to the CESCC

### **4. Key Provisions and Mechanisms**

#### A. **Assessment and Classification of Species (COSEWIC Role)**
- COSEWIC evaluates each wildlife species to determine its status using the best available scientific, community, and **Aboriginal traditional knowledge**.
- Classifications include:
  - Extinct
  - Extirpated
  - Endangered
  - Threatened
  - Species of Special Concern
- COSEWIC must:
  - Determine when species should be assessed (with priority on those most at risk)
  - Reassess and reclassify species as needed
  - Identify whether species migrate across Canadaâ€™s borders
  - Develop and review classification criteria
  - Provide advice to the Minister and CESCC

> **Note**: COSEWIC must consider **treaties and land claims agreements** when assessing species status.

#### B. **Recovery Strategies and Action Plans**
- **Recovery Strategy**: A comprehensive plan outlining how a species can be recovered. Must be developed and registered in the public registry.
- **Action Plan**: A detailed, site-specific plan that includes specific actions, timelines, and responsibilities. Also registered in the public registry.
- These plans are **living documents** and can be amended with public input.

#### C. **Delegation of Powers**
- The Minister (or relevant competent minister) may delegate enforcement and administrative powers to:
  - Other federal ministers
  - Provincial governments
  - Government employees or agencies
- Delegation requires:
  - A formal agreement between delegating and delegate
  - Annual reporting to the delegating minister
  - Copy of the agreement and annual report must be filed in the **public registry within 45 days**

#### D. **Stewardship Action Plan**
- A mechanism to **encourage and support voluntary conservation efforts** by individuals, communities, and organizations.
- Must include:
  - Examinations of incentives and programs supporting stewardship
  - Public awareness campaigns about species at risk
  - Methods for sharing **community and Indigenous traditional knowledge** (with respect and approval of knowledge holders)
  - Recognition programs (awards, honors)
  - Information on stewardship agreements, land conservation easements
  - Access to technical and scientific support

> This promotes **community involvement** and recognizes that all Canadians have a role in species conservation.

#### E. **Conservation Agreements**
- Competent ministers may enter into **conservation agreements** with governments, organizations, or individuals to:
  - Benefit species at risk
  - Enhance survival in the wild
- Agreements may include:
  - Monitoring species status
  - Developing education and public awareness programs
  - Protecting critical habitat
  - Conducting research
  - Implementing recovery strategies and action plans

> These are **flexible, collaborative tools** that allow for shared responsibility and funding.

#### F. **Agreements for Non-At-Risk Species**
- Competent ministers may also enter into agreements to:
  - Protect wildlife species that are **not currently at risk**
  - Prevent them from becoming at risk
- Includes measures like:
  - Habitat protection
  - Public education
  - Monitoring
  - Preventing threats

> This shows a **proactive, preventive approach** to biodiversity conservation.

#### G. **Cost-Sharing Agreements**
- The government may enter into agreements to **share the financial costs** of conservation programs and measures.
- Must specify:
  - Amount and timing of contributions
  - Who will operate and maintain the program
  - Revenue-sharing arrangements
  - Terms and conditions of operation

> Ensures **equitable distribution of costs** across jurisdictions and stakeholders.

### **5. Legal Authority and Enforcement**

- The Act applies to:
  - All wildlife species and individuals (living or dead)
  - Sedentary organisms on or under the **continental shelf outside the exclusive economic zone**
- Applies to **federal lands**, internal waters, territorial seas, and Indigenous reserves.

- **Offences** under SARA (e.g., harming, killing, or disturbing a species at risk) are punishable under the Act.
- **Alternative measures** (non-judicial actions) may be used to address violations (e.g., fines, orders, warnings).

### **6. Integration of Knowledge Systems**

- **Scientific knowledge** is the foundation of assessments.
- **Community knowledge** and **Aboriginal traditional knowledge** are explicitly recognized and required to be considered in:
  - Assessing species at risk
  - Developing recovery strategies
  - Monitoring species status
- Knowledge sharing must:
  - Respect and preserve Indigenous knowledge
  - Obtain **consent from knowledge holders**
  - Promote wider application of such knowledge

> This reflects a modern, inclusive, and culturally respectful approach to biodiversity conservation.
