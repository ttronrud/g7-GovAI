### **Detailed Summary of the Canadian Institutes of Health Research Act**

---

#### **Overview**
The **Canadian Institutes of Health Research Act (CIHR Act)** is a foundational piece of legislation enacted by Parliament to transform and strengthen Canada’s health research system. It establishes the **Canadian Institutes of Health Research (CIHR)** as a national, independent, and scientifically rigorous agency tasked with leading and advancing health research in Canada. The Act reflects a national commitment to improving public health through excellence in research, equity in access, ethical integrity, and collaboration across sectors and communities.

---

## **Key Provisions and Objectives**

### **1. Foundational Principles and Motivations**
The Act begins with a series of "Whereas" clauses that establish the philosophical and practical rationale for creating CIHR:

- **Health as a core value**: Canadians value health as central to happiness and fulfillment, and aspire to be among the healthiest people globally.
- **Leadership in global health research**: Canada should be recognized internationally as a leader in health research.
- **Excellence as a necessity**: High-quality, internationally recognized health research is essential to improving health outcomes for Canadians and the global community.
- **Care as a societal vision**: Investment in health and healthcare is part of Canada’s vision as a caring society.
- **Provincial collaboration**: Provinces are responsible for delivering health care, and the federal government works with them to support health research and services.
- **Equity and inclusivity**: Health research must address the unique needs of children, women, men, and diverse populations.
- **Transparency and ethics**: Research must be transparent, accountable, and ethically sound.
- **Adaptability and responsiveness**: The health research system must evolve with changing health challenges and societal needs.

---

### **2. Establishment of the Canadian Institutes of Health Research (CIHR)**

- **Legal Status**: The CIHR is established as a **corporation**, operating as an **agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada**.
- **Head Office**: Located at a site designated by the Governor in Council.
- **Mission Objective**:
  The CIHR aims to:
  - Excel internationally in creating **new scientific knowledge** and translating it into **improved health outcomes**, better health services, and stronger health systems.
  - Lead the Canadian research community through **collaboration** with provinces, researchers, and stakeholders nationally and internationally.
  - Build a **robust, high-quality research environment** that attracts, develops, and retains top-tier researchers.
  - Develop an **integrated health research agenda** across disciplines and sectors to respond to emerging health needs.
  - Promote **interdisciplinary research** that includes:
    - Biomedical and clinical research
    - Health systems and services
    - Population health
    - Sociocultural and environmental influences on health
  - Accelerate discoveries in **cures, treatments, prevention, and wellness**.
  - Address **ethical issues** in research and ensure public trust.
  - Promote **knowledge dissemination** and application in policy and practice.
  - Foster **innovation and commercialization** to drive economic growth and job creation in Canada’s knowledge-based economy.
  - Support the **capacity building** of researchers, especially through career development and sustained funding.
  - Encourage **international partnerships** to enhance global impact.
  - Ensure **transparency and accountability** in how public funds are used.

---

### **3. Powers and Functions of the CIHR**

The CIHR is granted broad powers to achieve its mission, including:

- **Promoting, assisting, and undertaking health research**.
- **Supporting scientific careers** of both women and men in health research.
- **Collaborating with provinces and other stakeholders** (private sector, NGOs, international partners).
- **Monitoring and evaluating** health research issues, including ethical concerns.
- **Advising the Minister** on health research and policy matters.
- **Engaging with the public**, governments, and research communities to promote understanding and trust.
- **Performing any other function assigned by the Governor in Council**.

---

### **4. Governance Structure**

#### **The President of the CIHR**
- Appointed by the Governor in Council for a term of **up to five years**, with eligibility for reappointment.
- Serves as the **chief executive officer** and is responsible for **day-to-day management and strategic direction**.
- Is **not eligible** to serve as Chairperson of the Governing Council.
- Remuneration, travel, and living expenses are covered as per Government standards.
- Deemed an employee of the **federal public administration** for purposes of compensation and superannuation laws.

#### **The Governing Council**
- Composed of **no more than 18 members**, including a Chairperson.
- **Initial appointments**: Each member serves a term of **up to three years**, with a rotation policy ensuring that no more than one-third of members serve in any given year.
- **Subsequent appointments**: Also serve three-year terms.
- **Reappointment**: Members may serve up to **two consecutive terms**.
- **Eligibility**: Members must be individuals who can contribute to the achievement of CIHR’s objectives and who reflect **scientific excellence, diversity of background, and disciplinary expertise**.
- **Ex officio members**:
  - The **President** and the **Deputy Minister of Health** are non-voting members.
- **Vice-Chairperson**: Elected by the Governing Council from among its members (excluding President and Deputy Minister).
  - Acts as Chairperson in the absence or incapacity of the Chairperson.

#### **Committees**
- The Governing Council may:
  - Establish **executive committees** and **other committees**.
  - Create **standing committees** to advise on health research issues, especially related to interdisciplinary research and policy.
- Committees may include **non-Governing Council members**, who may be paid for services based on fees set by the Governor in Council.

#### **Meeting Requirements**
- Must meet **at least twice a year**.
- Can meet at times and places deemed necessary.

---

### **5. Management and Oversight Responsibilities of the Governing Council**

The Governing Council holds full responsibility for the management of the CIHR, including:

- Setting **strategic directions and goals**.
- Evaluating the **overall performance** of the organization.
- Approving the **annual budget**.
- Establishing a **peer review process** for research proposals.
- Approving **funding for research** and other expenditures.
- Developing **policies and procedures** related to staffing, ethics, and operations.
- Reviewing and potentially amending or terminating **Health Research Institutes**.

#### **Delegation of Powers**
- The Governing Council **may delegate** certain powers and duties to its members, committees, or the President.
- **Exclusions**: Powers related to budgeting, major expenditures, and strategic direction (e.g., under sections 14(a), 16, 19–21, and subsections 22(3) and 32(1)) **cannot be delegated**.

---

### **6. Health Research Institutes (HRIs)**

A central innovation of the Act is the creation of **Health Research Institutes** to coordinate and integrate health research across disciplines and sectors.

#### **Key Features of HRIs**:
- Designed to address **all aspects of health**, including:
  - Biomedical and clinical research
  - Health systems and services
  - Population health
  - Social, cultural, and environmental determinants of health
- Must **collaborate with provinces** to advance research and apply findings to improve health services.
- Must **engage with private sector, voluntary organizations, and international partners**.
- Each HRI is established, funded, and managed by the Governing Council.

#### **Advisory Boards and Scientific Directors**
- Each HRI has:
  - An **Advisory Board** composed of experts in relevant fields, appointed by the Governing Council.
    - Members serve **without pay**, but may receive reimbursement for travel and living expenses.
    - May be paid additional fees if performing duties beyond board membership.
  - A **Scientific Director** appointed by the Governing Council to lead research activities within the institute.

#### **Review and Evaluation**
- The Governing Council reviews each HRI’s **mandate and performance at least every five years**.
- May decide to:
  - Amend policies or mandates
  - Merge institutes
  - Terminate an institute

---

### **7. Financial and Operational Authority**

The CIHR has significant autonomy in financial and operational matters:

- **Funding**:
  - Can receive funds from gifts, bequests, and government appropriations.
  - May use funds from operations with **Treasury Board approval**.
- **Procurement**:
  - May procure goods and services **outside the federal public administration**.
  - May procure **legal services only with approval** from the Attorney General or Governor in Council.
- **Property Rights**:
  - Acquired property is owned by **Her Majesty in right of Canada**.
  - Title may be held in the name of the CIHR or the Crown.
- **Legal Capacity**:
  - Can enter into **contracts, agreements, memoranda of understanding**, or even **incorporate subsidiaries** (with Governor in Council approval).
  - May **acquire, lease, or dispose of real and personal property**.
  - May **license, assign, or sell intellectual property** (patents, copyrights, trademarks, etc.).
- **Dissemination**:
  - Can publish, sell, or distribute studies, reports, and other documents.

> **Note**: Section 9 of the Department of Public Works and Government Services Act does **not apply** to CIHR, allowing greater flexibility in procurement.
