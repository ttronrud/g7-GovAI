### **Detailed Summary of the *Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency Act***  

The *Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency Act* establishes and outlines the structure, mandate, powers, responsibilities, and operational framework of the **Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency (ACOA)** — a federal agency created to promote economic development and improve employment and income opportunities in Atlantic Canada. This summary provides a comprehensive and structured analysis of the key provisions, objectives, functions, governance, financial mechanisms, and legal framework of the Act.

---

## **1. Name and Citation**
- The Act is formally titled: **"Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency Act"**.
- It may be cited as such, and its provisions govern the creation, operation, and authority of the Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**
The Act defines several core terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Agency** | The Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency, established under Section 10. |
| **Atlantic Canada** | Refers to the four provinces: **Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, and Newfoundland and Labrador**. |
| **Designated Area** | A specific geographic area within Atlantic Canada designated by the Minister via order under Section 7, where exceptional circumstances support local economic improvement. |
| **Minister** | A member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada appointed by the Governor in Council to oversee the Agency’s operations. |
| **President** | The chief executive officer of the Agency, appointed by the Governor in Council to serve as the deputy to the Minister. |

> *Note: The "Board" was repealed in 2014 (s. 175), and references to it are no longer active.*

---

## **3. Purpose and Objectives**
The primary purpose of the Act is to:
- **Increase economic opportunity** in Atlantic Canada.
- **Enhance earned incomes and employment** opportunities, particularly in small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs).

The Agency is specifically tasked with advancing economic development with a **focus on SMEs**, entrepreneurship, and regional prosperity.

---

## **4. Authority and Powers of the Minister**
The Minister is granted broad powers to carry out economic development initiatives in Atlantic Canada, including:

### **a. Policy Coordination**
- Coordinates federal government policies and programs related to economic development in Atlantic Canada.
- Ensures alignment between federal initiatives and regional needs.

### **b. Establishment of Advisory Boards**
- May create advisory boards to provide guidance and expertise.
- Can define membership, duties, and operational procedures for these boards.

### **c. Funding for Advisory Board Members**
- May pay reasonable travel and living expenses for members of advisory boards, subject to **Governor in Council approval**.

### **d. Inter-Provincial Agreements**
- Can enter into agreements with provincial governments to implement Agency programs or projects.

### **e. Designation of Designated Areas**
- By order, may designate specific areas in Atlantic Canada as "designated areas" for a set period, based on the belief that they have **exceptional potential for locally based productive employment growth**.

### **f. Financial Instrument Management**
- May acquire, exercise, assign, or sell stock options, share warrants, or other financial instruments obtained as conditions of loans, guarantees, or credit insurance under Section 13 or during enforcement of debts.

### **g. Cross-Act Fund Transfers**
- If a program or project authorized under another Act would further the purpose of this Act, the Minister may **conditionally or unconditionally transfer funds** from the Agency’s budget to supplement funds already committed by the responsible minister.
- These transferred funds can be used **on the same terms and conditions** as the original funds.
- Annual reports must include full disclosure of all such fund transfers.

---

## **5. Establishment and Governance of the Agency**

### **a. Creation of the Agency**
- The Act formally establishes the **Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency** as a federal agency of the Government of Canada.

### **b. Leadership Structure**
- The **Minister** is the head of the Agency and responsible for its overall direction.
- The **President** is appointed by the Governor in Council as the **deputy to the Minister** and serves as the **chief executive officer** of the Agency.
- The President has **control and supervision over all Agency work, officers, and employees**.

### **c. Succession and Tenure**
- In the absence or incapacity of the President, the Minister may appoint an interim person to act as President.
- No individual may serve as acting President for **more than 90 days without Governor in Council approval**.

### **d. Compensation**
- The President’s remuneration is set by the Governor in Council.

---

## **6. Object of the Agency**
The Agency’s central mission is to:
- Support and promote **economic development in Atlantic Canada**, with a **specific emphasis on small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs)**.
- Achieve this through:
  - Policy and program development.
  - Advocacy for Atlantic Canada in national economic planning.
  - Implementation of targeted programs and projects.

---

## **7. Powers and Functions of the Agency**
The Agency has wide-ranging powers to achieve its objectives, including:

| Function | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Develop Integrated Federal Approaches** | Formulate coordinated plans with other federal departments to support economic development in Atlantic Canada. |
| **Support Enterprises** | Plan and implement programs to establish, develop, support, and promote enterprises — especially SMEs. |
| **Develop Entrepreneurial Talent** | Promote training, education, and capacity-building to foster entrepreneurial skills. |
| **Improve Business Environment** | Support business associations, trade shows, market research, business data banks, communication networks, and scholarship programs related to business and investment. |
| **Investor Attraction** | Assist investors (especially SMEs) in locating and establishing operations in Atlantic Canada, consistent with federal and regional investment requirements. |
| **Provide Financing** | Make **loans** to individuals or entities for enterprise development in Atlantic Canada. |
| **Guarantee and Insurance Services** | Provide **guarantees** or **loan insurance/credit insurance** to support financial obligations of enterprises. |
| **Grants and Contributions** | Make grants or contributions under Treasury Board-approved terms to support Agency or Minister-led programs. |
| **Enter into Agreements** | Sign contracts, memoranda of understanding, or other arrangements in the name of Her Majesty in right of Canada or the Agency. |
| **Assume Assets and Obligations** | Acquire assets and assume liabilities of the **Enterprise Cape Breton Corporation (ECC)** and its subsidiaries. |
| **Manage Security Interests** | Hold, surrender, realize, or dispose of security interests acquired from ECC. |
| **Hold Financial Instruments** | Hold, exercise, or dispose of stock options, shares, or similar instruments acquired from ECC. |
| **Other Necessary Actions** | Perform all actions necessary or incidental to managing the Agency’s assets, obligations, or operations. |

> *Note: The Agency is not restricted to these powers — it may do “all such other things” necessary to fulfill its mandate.*

---

## **8. Support to the Minister**
The Agency assists the Minister in:
- Exercising powers and fulfilling duties under this Act or other Acts.
- Coordinating federal policies on economic development.
- Administering agreements with provincial governments.
- Exercising powers under Section 8.
- Compiling detailed data on programs and projects to track trends and progress in economic development.

---

## **9. Staffing and Operations**
- Officers and employees are appointed in accordance with the **Public Service Employment Act**.
- The Agency may utilize the services and facilities of other federal departments, boards, or agencies when appropriate.

---

## **10. Office Locations**
- The **principal office** is located in **Moncton, New Brunswick**.
- The Agency must maintain **at least one office in each of the other three Atlantic provinces** to ensure regional accessibility and presence.

---

## **11. Legal Standing and Jurisdiction**
- Contracts, memoranda, and arrangements entered into by the Agency **are binding on Her Majesty in right of Canada** to the same extent as on the Agency itself.
- Legal actions or proceedings related to rights or obligations of the Agency may be brought **in the name of the Agency** in any court with jurisdiction — even though the Agency is not a corporation acting as an agent of the Crown.

---

## **12. Regulatory Authority**
The Minister may make regulations under the Act to:
- Specify additional programs or projects to improve the business environment.
- Define key terms such as:
  - “Small and medium-sized enterprises”
  - “Project” and “demonstration project”
  - Eligible classes of SMEs and operations for support.
- Generally implement the purposes and provisions of the Act.

Additionally:
- With **Minister of Finance approval**, the Minister may regulate:
  - Loans, guarantees, and credit insurance under the Act.
  - Conditions for acquiring, exercising, assigning, or selling stock options from loans or guarantees.

> **Area-Specific Regulations**: Regulations can be made for designated areas that differ from general Atlantic Canada rules, allowing tailored responses to local economic conditions.

---

## **13. Reporting Requirements**
- The **President** must submit an annual report on the Agency’s operations to the Minister within **four months after the end of each fiscal year**.
- The **Minister** must lay before each House of Parliament, within the first 15 days after September 30 following each fiscal year, a **comprehensive report** on the administration of the Act.
- This report includes a copy of the annual report submitted to the Minister.

> These reporting requirements ensure transparency, accountability, and parliamentary oversight.
