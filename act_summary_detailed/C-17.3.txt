**Detailed Summary of the *Department of Canadian Heritage Act* (1996)**

---

### **Overview**
The *Department of Canadian Heritage Act*, enacted on **July 12, 1996** (as noted in the note), establishes the **Department of Canadian Heritage** as a central government agency responsible for promoting and safeguarding Canada’s cultural identity, values, and heritage. It consolidates and reorganizes responsibilities previously scattered across several federal departments, including the Department of Communications, the Department of Multiculturalism and Citizenship, and parts of the Department of the Secretary of State of Canada.

This Act provides a comprehensive framework for the structure, powers, duties, functions, financial mechanisms, and administrative transitions associated with the new department.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Establishment of the Department of Canadian Heritage**

- The Act formally establishes the **Department of Canadian Heritage** as a department of the Government of Canada.
- The **Minister of Canadian Heritage** is appointed by commission under the Great Seal of Canada and serves at the pleasure of the Crown.
- The Minister holds **overall management and direction** of the Department and is responsible for its operations and strategic vision.

> *Note:* The Minister is not bound by fixed terms and can be removed by the Governor in Council at any time.

---

### **2. Appointment of Deputy Minister**

- The Governor in Council (i.e., the federal Cabinet) may appoint a **Deputy Minister of Canadian Heritage**.
- The Deputy Minister serves at the pleasure of the Crown and acts as the **deputy head** of the Department, assisting the Minister in administrative and operational leadership.

---

### **3. Powers, Duties, and Functions of the Minister**

The Minister’s jurisdiction is broad and encompasses **all matters within Parliament’s authority** that are not assigned to another department, board, or agency — specifically those relating to:

#### **Core Areas of Responsibility:**

| Area | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Human Rights & Fundamental Freedoms** | Promoting understanding of constitutional rights, freedoms, and related values. Includes funding for significant court test cases to clarify human rights issues. |
| **Multiculturalism** | Advancing multicultural values and supporting policies that promote equity and inclusion among Canada’s diverse populations. |
| **Arts & Cultural Development** | Oversight of all artistic fields: performing arts, visual arts, audio-visual media, literature, publishing, sound recording, film, and video. Includes recognition of the status and role of artists. |
| **Cultural Heritage & Industries** | Management of cultural property, including conservation, exportation, and importation. Supports cultural industries and innovation. |
| **National Battlefields** | Preservation and management of sites of historical significance related to Canada’s military history. |
| **Sport** | Encouraging, promoting, and developing sport across Canada as a means of national unity and social enrichment. |
| **Official Languages (English and French)** | Advancing equality in status and use of both official languages. Supports the development of linguistic minority communities in both English and French. |
| **State Ceremonial & Canadian Symbols** | Oversight of national symbols (e.g., flags, anthems, royal ceremonies), and state functions that reflect Canadian identity. |
| **Broadcasting** | Policy formulation in broadcasting (excluding technical spectrum management). |
| **Cultural Policy** | Formulating national cultural policy, including aspects related to **foreign investment** and **copyright**. |
| **National Museums, Archives, and Libraries** | Oversight of major national institutions preserving Canada’s historical and cultural memory. |
| **Public Events in the National Capital Region** | Organizing, sponsoring, and promoting public cultural and social events in the National Capital Region (as defined in the *National Capital Act*), ensuring they reflect Canada’s federal character, linguistic equality, and cultural heritage. |

> ✅ This jurisdiction is **not exhaustive** — it is described as "encompassing but not limited to" the listed areas.

---

### **4. Minister’s Responsibilities in Policy and Implementation**

The Minister must:

- **Initiate, recommend, coordinate, implement, and promote** national policies, projects, and programs related to Canadian identity, cultural development, and heritage.
- Specifically, regarding **public events in the National Capital Region**, the Minister must **coordinate** federal policies and programs related to such events, ensuring alignment with federal values and inclusivity.

> This ensures centralized leadership and consistency in cultural programming across federal departments.

---

### **5. Agreements with Provincial Governments**

- The Minister may enter into **agreements with provincial governments or their agencies** to collaborate on cultural initiatives, programs, or projects.
- This facilitates intergovernmental cooperation while maintaining federal leadership in cultural policy.

---

### **6. Financial and Administrative Powers**

To implement its programs, the Department may:

- **Provide financial assistance** in the form of:
  - Grants
  - Contributions
  - Endowments
  to individuals, organizations, or institutions.

- **Acquire or seek to acquire property** (e.g., land, buildings) through:
  - Gifts
  - Bequests
  - Donations  
  *(Subject to the Federal Real Property and Federal Immovables Act and Treasury Board directions)*

- **Hold, administer, invest, expend, sell, exchange, or dispose of property** (subject to applicable laws and regulations).

- **Make gifts, awards, prizes, souvenirs, and mementos** on behalf of the Department or another government department.

> These powers give the Department flexibility in funding and asset management.

---

### **7. Funding for Human Rights Test Cases**

- The Minister may provide **funding to independent organizations** to support **funding of national significance test cases** in court.
- These cases are intended to **clarify and assert constitutional human rights** (e.g., freedom of expression, equality rights).
- This reflects a proactive role in upholding constitutional values through judicial engagement.

---

### **8. Fee Setting Authority**

The Minister may **set fees** for various services, facilities, products, rights, and approvals, **subject to regulations made by the Treasury Board**.

#### Types of Fees:
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Service or Facility Use Fees** | For services or access to facilities provided by the Department. |
| **Product/Right/Privilege Fees** | For access to rights or privileges (e.g., licensing, membership). |
| **Regulatory Process Fees** | For approvals or regulatory processes (e.g., permits, licenses). |

#### Key Conditions:
- Fees must **not exceed the actual cost** to Her Majesty in right of Canada for providing the service or process.
- Fees must be **reviewed annually** and published in the *Canada Gazette* and via authorized electronic means.
- Any **change in fees** must be published within **30 days** of effective date.
- Fees are **subject to review** by the **Committee referred to in section 19 of the Statutory Instruments Act**, treating them as if they were statutory instruments.

> This ensures transparency, accountability, and public scrutiny of fee structures.

---

### **9. Consultation and Regulatory Process**

- Before setting any fee under sections 8, 9, or 10, the Minister must **consult with relevant stakeholders or organizations** deemed interested in the matter.

> This promotes inclusive decision-making and stakeholder engagement.

---

### **10. Status and Transition of Employees**

To ensure a smooth transition of personnel, the Act provides **protections for employees** who were working in the following departments **before the Act came into force**:

#### Employees Protected:
- In the **Department of the Environment** (specific portions)
- In the **Department of National Health and Welfare** (specific portions)
- In the **Department of the Secretary of State of Canada**, **excluding**:
  - Native Citizens Directorate
  - Canadian Studies and Special Projects Directorate
  - Youth Participation Directorate
  - Student Assistance Branch
  - Office of the Assistant Under Secretary of State (Social Development and Regional Operations)
- In the **Department of Communications**, **excluding**:
  - Specific branches (e.g., related to broadcasting or telecommunications)
- In the **Department of Multiculturalism and Citizenship**

> These employees **retain their positions** and are **transferred to the new Department of Canadian Heritage**.

#### Key Provisions:
- They are deemed to occupy their positions **under the authority of the Deputy Minister of Canadian Heritage**.
- Their **status, salary, and benefits** are preserved.
- Any **unexpended funds** allocated to the former departments (Communications, Multiculturalism and Citizenship) in the fiscal year of the Act’s coming into force are **deemed appropriated** to the new Department.

> This ensures continuity of service and avoids disruption in public service operations.

---

### **11. Transfer of Powers, Functions, and Assets from Former Departments**

Wherever any **power, duty, or function** previously vested in or exercisable by any of the following officials or departments is within the scope of the Minister’s new responsibilities, it is **transferred** to:

- The **Minister of Canadian Heritage**
- The **Deputy Minister of Canadian Heritage**
- The **appropriate officer** of the Department

> **Exception**: The Governor in Council may, by order, designate another Minister or officer to exercise a specific function.

#### Entities Covered:
- Ministers: Environment, National Health and Welfare, Multiculturalism and Citizenship, Secretary of State, Communications
- Deputy Ministers and officers of those departments

> This creates a **legal and administrative transfer** of authority, ensuring no gap in service.
