### **1. Purpose and Legislative Background**
The *Canada Cooperatives Act* (CCA) was enacted to:
- **Modernize and unify** the legal framework governing cooperatives across Canada.
- Establish a **clear, consistent, and democratic** legal regime that reflects internationally recognized cooperative principles.
- Provide a **distinct legal identity** for cooperatives separate from traditional corporate or non-profit entities.

This Act replaces the outdated *Canada Cooperative Associations Act* (prior legislation), which was last amended in 1970â€“72. The new Act aims to support the **social and economic development of communities** through cooperative enterprise by enabling democratic participation, equitable ownership, and community-focused business models.

### **3. Scope and Applicability**

#### âœ… Applies To:
- Cooperatives incorporated under this Act that have **not been discontinued**.
- Cooperatives that carry on business in **two or more provinces** and have a **fixed place of business in more than one province**.

#### âŒ Excludes:
- **No provisions** of the *Canada Business Corporations Act*, *Canada Not-for-Profit Corporations Act*, or *Winding-up and Restructuring Act* apply to cooperatives.
- Cooperatives **cannot** carry on business as:
  - A bank;
  - An insurance company (under the *Insurance Companies Act*);
  - A trust or loan company (under the *Trust and Loan Companies Act*);
  - A cooperative credit association (under the *Cooperative Credit Associations Act*).
- Cooperatives **cannot** operate as degree-granting educational institutions **unless expressly authorized** by a federal or provincial agency with such authority.

> This ensures cooperatives remain distinct from financial institutions and educational bodies.

### **4. Core Principles of Cooperative Operation**

A cooperative is deemed to be **organized and operated on a cooperative basis** if it meets the following criteria:

1. **Open Membership**  
   - Membership is open, in a non-discriminatory manner, to individuals who can use the cooperativeâ€™s services and are willing and able to accept membership responsibilities.

2. **One Vote per Member or Delegate**  
   - Each member or delegate has **only one vote** at meetings.  
   - **Exception**: The articles of a **federation** may allow more than one vote per member or delegate (subject to compliance with section 379(2)).

3. **No Proxy Voting**  
   - Members or delegates **cannot vote by proxy** (unless otherwise permitted in the articles or by prior agreement).

4. **Limits on Interest and Dividends**  
   - Interest on **member loans** is capped at a maximum percentage set in the articles.  
   - Dividends on **membership shares** are limited to a maximum percentage fixed in the articles.

5. **Member Capital Contribution**  
   - Members should provide capital when feasible.  
   - Returns on member capital (e.g., interest or dividends) must not exceed the maximum percentage specified in the articles.

6. **Surplus Fund Usage**  
   Surplus funds must be used for:
   - Developing the business;
   - Improving common services for members;
   - Providing reserves or interest on member loans/dividends;
   - Community welfare or promoting cooperative enterprises;
   - Distributing **patronage returns** to members or patrons.

7. **Education and Awareness**  
   The cooperative must **educate members, officers, employees, and the public** about cooperative principles and practices.

> **Exception**: Restrictions on membership eligibility may exist if they are reasonably related to:
> - Business restrictions in the articles;
> - The cooperativeâ€™s commercial capacity to serve prospective members;
> - And remain consistent with human rights laws.

The **Director** (appointed under section 371) has significant regulatory authority:

- Can determine whether a cooperative is or was a **distributing cooperative** (a cooperative that offers shares to the public).
