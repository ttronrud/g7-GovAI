**Detailed Summary of the *Canadian Space Agency Act***  
*(As enacted in 1990, with amendments and repeals noted)*

---

### **Overview**

The *Canadian Space Agency Act* (often referred to as the CSA Act) is a foundational piece of legislation that establishes the **Canadian Space Agency (CSA)** as a federal agency of Canada. Enacted on December 14, 1990, and coming into force by order of the Governor in Council, the Act provides the legal framework for the creation, governance, mandate, powers, and operations of the CSA. It is designed to promote Canada’s peaceful engagement in space activities, advance scientific knowledge, and ensure that space technologies benefit Canadian society and the economy.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Citation and Definitions**

- **Title**: The Act is cited as the *Canadian Space Agency Act*.
- **Key Definitions**:
  - **Agency**: Refers to the Canadian Space Agency established under Section 3.
  - **Minister**: The designated member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada responsible for overseeing the Agency (typically the Minister of Science or Minister of Innovation, Science and Industry).
  - **President**: The chief executive officer of the Agency, appointed by the Governor in Council (subject to approval and term limits).
  - **Board / Executive Vice-President**: Both were **repealed in 2010** (under *c. 12, s. 1712*), indicating a shift from a board-based governance model to a presidential leadership structure.

---

### **2. Establishment of the Canadian Space Agency**

- The Act formally establishes the **Canadian Space Agency** as a government agency of Canada.
- It is created to operate independently while remaining under the overall direction of the Minister.

---

### **3. Core Objectives of the Agency**

The CSA is mandated to:
1. **Promote the peaceful use and development of space**;
2. **Advance knowledge of space through science**;
3. **Ensure that space science and technology provide social and economic benefits to Canadians**.

These objectives reflect a balance between scientific exploration, national interest, and practical applications for Canadian industry and society.

---

### **4. Jurisdiction and Authority**

- The Agency has **exclusive authority over all matters concerning space** within the jurisdiction of Parliament.
- It may exercise its powers and perform duties in areas **not assigned to any other department, board, or agency** of the Government of Canada.

This ensures the CSA has autonomy in space-related policy and operations.

---

### **5. Functions and Responsibilities**

To fulfill its objectives, the Agency is empowered to:

#### ✅ Coordinate Government Space Policy
- Assist the Minister in coordinating Canada’s space policies and programs across federal departments.

#### ✅ Plan and Implement Programs
- Plan, direct, manage, and implement scientific or industrial space research and development, including applications of space technology.

#### ✅ Promote Technology Transfer
- Promote the transfer and diffusion of space technologies to Canadian industry to foster innovation and competitiveness.

#### ✅ Encourage Commercial Exploitation
- Encourage private-sector development and use of space capabilities, technologies, facilities, and systems.

#### ✅ Additional Functions
- The Governor in Council may assign **other functions** by order.

---

### **6. Powers and Authorities**

The Agency is granted broad powers to carry out its mandate, including:

| Power | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Construct, acquire, manage, maintain, and operate** space vehicles, facilities, and systems | Enables the CSA to build and operate its own space infrastructure (e.g., satellites, launch systems). |
| **Support government departments** | Helps other federal agencies use and market space technologies. |
| **Make grants and contributions** | Funds research and development projects aimed at testing, evaluating, or applying new space technologies with a view to assessing commercial potential. *Note: Excludes projects solely focused on commercial exploitation.* |
| **Cooperate internationally** | Engages with space agencies of other countries in peaceful space activities. |
| **Provide services and facilities** | Offers access to its infrastructure or expertise to individuals, organizations, or governments. |
| **License, sell, or make available IP rights** | Can license, sell, or otherwise distribute patents, copyrights, industrial designs, trademarks, trade secrets, or similar rights held by the Minister. |
| **Enter into agreements** | Can enter into contracts, memoranda of understanding, or other arrangements in the name of Her Majesty in right of Canada or in its own name. |
| **Acquire and manage property** | Can receive gifts or bequests and manage financial or personal assets accordingly. |
| **Administer loans/guarantees** | Administers loans or guarantees made by the Minister under Section 9. |

> These powers are essential to enabling the CSA to act as both a research institution and a commercial enabler.

---

### **7. Use of Government Resources**

- The Agency may **use the services and facilities** of other federal departments, boards, or agencies where appropriate — promoting interdepartmental collaboration.

---

### **8. Ministerial Responsibilities**

- The **Minister is responsible for the operations of the Agency** and oversees its strategic direction.
- The Minister may:
  - Enter into **agreements with provincial governments** to implement Agency programs (with provincial cooperation).
  - Make **loans** to individuals or entities for commercial space activities (subject to approval from the Minister of Finance).
  - Provide **guarantees** for loans related to commercial space technology (again, with Minister of Finance concurrence).

> These provisions support public-private partnerships and economic development in the space sector.

---

### **9. Fees, Charges, and Financial Management**

#### **Fee Imposition**
- The Governor in Council may, by order, prescribe:
  - Fees or charges for services or facilities provided by the Agency.
  - Fees or charges for access to IP rights (e.g., patents, trademarks).
- The **interest** on overdue fees may also be prescribed.

#### **Imposition of Fees**
- The Agency is required to **impose** the prescribed fees and charges (including interest on overdue amounts), unless waived or reduced.

#### **Waiver or Reduction of Fees**
- With **Governor in Council approval**, the Minister may waive or reduce fees or interest.

#### **Use of Fees**
- With **Treasury Board approval**, fees collected can be used to offset operational costs incurred in the fiscal year.

#### **Legal Status of Fees**
- All fees, charges, and interest are **debts due to Her Majesty in right of Canada**.
- They are **recoverable in federal courts**, including the Federal Court.

> This ensures financial accountability and legal enforceability of the CSA’s revenue streams.

#### **Exemption from Statutory Instrument Rules**
- An order applying to only one person or body is **not considered a statutory instrument** under the *Statutory Instruments Act* — simplifying administrative procedures for targeted actions.

---

### **10. Delegation of Authority**

- Any power or duty of the Minister (except those related to fee orders or authorizations under this section) may be exercised by **authorized officers or employees** of the Agency.
- When exercised, such actions are **deemed to have been exercised by the Minister** — allowing delegation without loss of accountability.

---

### **11. Appointment of the President**

- The **Governor in Council appoints the President** of the Agency.
- The President serves a **term not exceeding five years**.
- The President may be **reappointed** for further terms.

> This ensures a stable leadership structure with clear accountability.

#### **Role and Responsibilities**
- The President is the **chief executive officer** of the Agency.
- Reports to the Minister and has **control and supervision over all work, officers, and employees**.

#### **Vacancy or Absence**
- In the event of the President’s absence or incapacity, the Minister may appoint an acting President.
- However, **no acting President may serve more than 90 days without Governor in Council approval** — preventing prolonged interim leadership.

#### **Remuneration**
- The President is paid a salary fixed by the Governor in Council.

---

### **12. Employment of Staff**

- Employees necessary for the Agency’s operations are appointed in accordance with the **Public Service Employment Act (PSE Act)**.
- **Exception**: The Agency may appoint and employ **astronauts** in special positions.
  - Employment terms, including remuneration, require **Governor in Council approval**.
- **Exemption from Labour Relations Act**:
  - The *Federal Public Sector Labour Relations Act* **does not apply** to astronauts employed under this provision.
- **Applicability of Other Acts**:
  - Astronauts are considered employees under:
    - The *Government Employees Compensation Act*;
    - The *Aeronautics Act* (for regulation purposes);
    - The *Public Service Superannuation Act* (for pension benefits).

> This provides flexibility in staffing while ensuring proper legal protections and benefits.

---

### **13. Principal Office Location**

- The **principal office of the Agency is located in the greater Montreal area** — reflecting strategic geographic and logistical considerations.

---

### **14. Legal Binding Effect of Agreements**

- Any contract, memorandum of understanding, or agreement entered into by the Agency **is binding on Her Majesty in right of Canada** to the same extent as it is binding on the Agency itself.

> This ensures legal continuity and accountability when the CSA acts on behalf of the Crown.

---

### **15. Jurisdiction in Legal Proceedings**

- Actions, suits, or legal proceedings involving rights or obligations of the Agency — whether in its own name or in the name of Her Majesty — may be brought **in the name of the Agency** in any court with jurisdiction.
