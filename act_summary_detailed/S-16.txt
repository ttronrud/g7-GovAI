**Detailed Summary of the *Standards Council of Canada Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Standards Council of Canada Act* establishes the **Standards Council of Canada (SCC)** as a federal statutory body responsible for promoting and coordinating voluntary standardization activities across Canada. The Act defines the structure, mandate, powers, governance, financial capacity, and operational procedures of the Council, with the overarching goal of advancing national economic growth, public safety, consumer protection, trade facilitation, and technological innovation through effective standards development and implementation.

---

## **1. Name and Citation**
- The Act may be cited as the **Standards Council of Canada Act**.
- It provides the legal foundation for the establishment and operation of the Standards Council of Canada.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**
Several key terms are defined in the Act to ensure clarity in interpretation:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Council** | Refers to the Standards Council of Canada established under Section 3. |
| **Minister** | A member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada designated by the Governor in Council to oversee the Council’s operations. |
| **Conformity Assessment** | Any activity that determines whether relevant requirements (e.g., safety, performance) are met — directly or indirectly. |
| **National Standards System (NSS)** | The system of voluntary standards development, promotion, and implementation in Canada. |

> *Note: The Act also allows the Governor in Council to designate any country for purposes of international cooperation under the Council’s mandate.*

---

## **3. Establishment of the Council**
The Council is established as a **corporation** composed of the following members:

1. **Chairperson and Vice-Chairperson** of the **Provincial-Territorial Advisory Committee** (established under subsection 20(1)).
2. **Chairperson** of the **Standards Development Organizations Advisory Committee** (established under subsection 21(1)).
3. **Not more than 10 other individuals** representing the private sector, including non-governmental organizations.

> *Note: A provision that previously allowed for additional members (repealed in 2010) is no longer in force.*

---

## **4. Mandate of the Council**
The Council’s primary mandate is to **promote efficient and effective voluntary standardization in Canada**, particularly where standardization is not mandated by law. Specific objectives include:

- Promoting Canadian participation in voluntary standards activities.
- Encouraging public-private cooperation in standardization.
- Coordinating and overseeing efforts within the **National Standards System (NSS)**.
- Fostering quality, performance, and technological innovation in goods and services through standards.
- Developing long-term strategies and objectives for standards-related activities.
- Advancing the national economy, supporting sustainable development, protecting health and safety, assisting consumers, facilitating domestic and international trade, and enhancing international standardization cooperation.

---

## **5. Powers and Functions of the Council**
The Council is empowered to carry out a wide range of functions to fulfill its mandate:

| Function | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Promote cooperation** | Between standardization organizations and government bodies at all levels to ensure compatibility and common usage of standards. |
| **Establish criteria and procedures** | For the preparation, approval, acceptance, and designation of voluntary standards. |
| **Accreditation of organizations** | Accredits organizations in Canada or designated foreign countries engaged in **conformity assessment** (e.g., testing, certification) and **standards development**. Maintains records of accredited organizations and their marks. |
| **Approve national standards** | Approves standards submitted by accredited organizations as **national standards** and maintains an index of approved standards. |
| **Identify standards needs** | Evaluates the need for new standards, revisions, or additional conformity assessment services. If not met through existing organizations, promotes the creation of new ones. |
| **Use of marks and branding** | Establishes and registers its own **trademarks** under the *Trademarks Act*, and authorizes their use in relation to standards developed by accredited organizations (subject to agreements). |
| **International representation** | Represents Canada as the official member in key international bodies such as:  
  - International Organization for Standardization (ISO)  
  - International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC)  
  - Any other similar international standardization organizations  
  Ensures effective Canadian participation in their activities. |
| **International cooperation** | Promotes information exchange and cooperation with similar organizations in other countries. |
| **Financial assistance** | Provides funding to Canadians and Canadian organizations to meet national and international standardization requirements. |
| **Information dissemination** | Collects, translates, and distributes information on standards and standardization activities domestically and internationally via electronic or other means. |
| **Promote standard use** | Encourages the adoption and use of standards approved by the Council. |
| **Provide policy advice** | Makes recommendations to the Minister on standards-related matters, including those suitable for incorporation by reference in legislation. These recommendations may be included in the Council’s annual report. |

> *Note: The Council may act independently or in coordination with government departments and agencies.*

---

## **6. Operational Principles**
To ensure efficiency and avoid duplication:

- The Council must, **to the greatest extent practicable**, use the services and facilities of **existing organizations** engaged in standards development and conformity assessment.
- Before launching new services, the Council must **consider all alternatives** and avoid creating redundant or overlapping functions.

---

## **7. Financial and Property Powers**
The Council has broad financial autonomy:

- May **expend funds** appropriated by Parliament or received through operations.
- May **acquire and dispose of real property or interests** in property at will.
- May **receive gifts, bequests, or other property** and manage or dispose of such assets.
- May **publish and sell information** on standardization.
- May carry out **incidental or supportive activities** related to its mandate.

> These powers enable the Council to operate independently and sustainably.

---

## **8. Membership and Appointment**
### **Membership Composition**
- Members are appointed by the **Governor in Council**, on the **recommendation of the Minister**.
- **Term of office**: Up to **four years**, with no more than **half of the members** expiring in any one year to ensure continuity.

### **Representation**
- Members must represent a **broad spectrum of private sector interests** and possess relevant knowledge or experience.
- The Chairperson of the Standards Development Organizations Advisory Committee is a **non-voting member**.

### **Leadership Roles**
- A **Chairperson** and **Vice-Chairperson** are designated by the Governor in Council from among the members.
- The **Chairperson presides over Council meetings** and performs other duties assigned by the Council or its by-laws.
- If the Chairperson is absent or unable to act, the **Vice-Chairperson acts**.
- If both offices are vacant, a **member elected by the Council** acts as Chairperson.

> A retiring member is eligible for **reappointment** in the same or another capacity.

---

## **9. Remuneration and Employment Status**
- The **Chairperson** is paid a fixed remuneration set by the Governor in Council.
- All other members serve **without salary**, but are entitled to **reasonable travel and other expenses** incurred while performing duties.
- With **Council approval**, members may be paid additional remuneration for duties performed beyond their ordinary responsibilities.

> **Legal Status**:
- Members and the Executive Director are **deemed employed in the federal public administration** for the purposes of:
  - The *Government Employees Compensation Act*
  - Regulations under the *Aeronautics Act*
- However, the Council is **not an agent of Her Majesty**, and **members are not part of the federal public administration** under normal circumstances.
- The **Public Service Superannuation Act applies only to the Executive Director and other officers/employees**, not to members of the Council.

> **Tax Status**:  
The Council is deemed a **registered charity** under the *Income Tax Act*, which provides tax benefits and enables it to receive donations and grants.

---

## **10. Governance and By-Laws**
- The Council may adopt **by-laws** to govern its operations, including:
  - Formation of **ad hoc, standing, or special committees**.
  - Criteria and procedures for **accreditation and revocation** of organizations (for conformity assessment and standards development).
  - A **membership scheme** to allow broader public participation in Council activities.
- Committees may include **non-Council members** to ensure diverse perspectives.

---

## **11. Executive Director**
- The Council appoints an **Executive Director** who serves as the **chief executive officer**.
- The Executive Director:
  - Has **supervision and direction** over the Council’s work and staff.
  - Is appointed by the Governor in Council for a term as determined by the Governor.
  - Is paid a fixed remuneration and expenses by the Governor in Council.
- The Council may appoint other officers and employees, whose duties and terms are prescribed by the Council with **Treasury Board approval**.

> The Executive Director and other officers are **deemed employed in the public service** under the *Public Service Superannuation Act*.

---

## **12. Temporary Expert Engagement**
- The Council may engage **specialists or experts** for specific projects or temporary periods.
- Such engagement requires **Treasury Board approval** for remuneration and expenses.

---
