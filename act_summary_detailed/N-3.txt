**Detailed Summary of the *National Arts Centre Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *National Arts Centre Act* establishes the legal framework for the **National Arts Centre Corporation (Corporation)**, which is responsible for operating and promoting the **National Arts Centre (Centre)** in Ottawa, Canada. The Act defines the governance structure, powers, responsibilities, financial operations, and accountability mechanisms of the Corporation. It ensures that the Centre fulfills its mission of advancing the performing arts in Canada—particularly in the National Capital Region—and supports broader national cultural development through collaboration with federal agencies like the Canada Council for the Arts.

---

### **Key Definitions**

- **Board**: Refers to the Board of Trustees of the Corporation. This body governs the Corporation and includes key members such as the Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, mayors of Ottawa and Gatineau, and six other appointed members.
  
- **Centre**: The National Arts Centre located in Ottawa, serving as a central hub for performing arts.

- **Corporation**: The National Arts Centre Corporation, established and continued under this Act. It operates as a legal entity with corporate powers and responsibilities.

- **Minister**: A member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada designated by the Governor in Council (i.e., the federal Cabinet) to oversee the Act’s implementation. This Minister is responsible for accountability to Parliament.

- **President**: The chief executive officer of the Corporation, appointed by the Board, responsible for day-to-day management and leadership.

- **Performing arts**: Defined broadly to include drama, music, and dance—both the creation and performance of these arts.

---

### **Governance Structure**

#### 1. **Board of Trustees**
- The Board consists of:
  - A **Chairperson** and a **Vice-Chairperson**, appointed by the Governor in Council for terms not exceeding **four years**.
  - The **Mayors of Ottawa and Gatineau**, who are automatically included.
  - **Six additional members**, appointed by the Governor in Council for terms not exceeding four years.

#### 2. **Term Limits and Eligibility Restrictions**
- A person who has served **two consecutive terms** in any of the following roles:
  - Chairperson
  - Vice-Chairperson
  - Member (non-mayor)
  is **ineligible for reappointment** for **12 months** after completing their second term.

> This provision promotes diversity and prevents long-term dominance by any individual or group.

#### 3. **Vacancies**
- A vacancy in the Board does **not affect the authority or ability** of the remaining members to carry out their duties.

#### 4. **Board Remuneration and Expenses**
- Members (excluding those receiving fixed salaries) receive:
  - **Daily remuneration** for attending Board or committee meetings, as set by Board by-law.
  - **Travel and living expenses** incurred in performing duties, also fixed by by-law.
- The Board may establish by-laws to regulate:
  - Meeting procedures, including committee creation and delegation of duties.
  - Quorums required for decision-making.
  - Remuneration and expense allowances for members (subject to Ministerial approval).
  - General conduct and management of operations.

---

### **Appointment of the President**

- The **President** is appointed by the Board for a term of **up to five years**.
- Reappointment is **eligible**, allowing continuity in leadership.
- The President is the **chief executive officer** of the Corporation and has **supervision and direction** over all staff and operations.

#### Powers and Responsibilities:
- Oversees the work and staff of the Corporation.
- In the event of the President’s absence or incapacity, the Board may designate an **officer of the Corporation** to temporarily act as President.

---

### **Powers and Objectives of the Corporation**

The Corporation’s **primary objectives** are:

1. **Operate and maintain** the National Arts Centre.
2. **Develop the performing arts** in the **National Capital Region** (as defined in the *National Capital Act* schedule).
3. **Support the Canada Council for the Arts** in developing performing arts across Canada.

#### Specific Activities Permitted:
To achieve these objectives, the Corporation may:
- **Sponsor or arrange** performing arts events at the Centre.
- **Encourage and support** resident performing arts companies.
- **Broadcast performances** via radio or television from the Centre and show films.
- **Provide accommodation** at the Centre (on terms set by the Corporation) for national and local organizations focused on promoting the performing arts.
- **Arrange performances**:
  - Elsewhere in Canada (at request of the Government of Canada or Canada Council).
  - Outside Canada (by Canadian-resident performing arts companies).

> These provisions enable the Centre to serve as both a local cultural hub and a national platform for artistic exchange.

---

### **Financial and Property Powers**

The Corporation has broad authority to manage its financial and property affairs:

- **Acquire and dispose of property**:
  - Real, personal, movable, immovable property, including securities.
  - Can hold, manage, or sell such assets as it deems necessary.

- **Lease property** as lessee (including real estate and facilities).

- **Receive gifts, bequests, or devises** of property and may **use or dispose of such property** even if restricted by the donor’s terms—**unless prohibited by law**.

- **Operate commercial facilities**:
  - Run restaurants, lounges, parking, shops, and other public facilities within the Centre.
  - Lease or make available space for such facilities on terms set by the Corporation.

- **Expend funds**:
  - All moneys appropriated by Parliament or received from operations (including rental income) may be used for the Corporation’s purposes.

- **General authority**:
  - May do and authorize any other acts deemed necessary to fulfill its mission and exercise its powers.

> This grants significant operational flexibility while remaining within the bounds of public accountability.

---

### **By-Laws and Governance Flexibility**

The Board may adopt by-laws to:
- Establish **standing and special committees**.
- Delegate responsibilities to committees.
- Set **quorum requirements** for meetings.
- Fix **remuneration and expenses** for members (subject to Ministerial approval).
- Govern overall operations and management.

> These by-laws ensure adaptability and efficient governance while maintaining oversight by the Minister and Parliament.

---

### **Head Office and Public Service Status**

- The **head office** of the Corporation is located in **Ottawa**.

#### Public Service Status:
- The **President and officers/employees** are **deemed to be employed in the public service** under:
  - The *Public Service Superannuation Act*.
  - Section 37 of that Act (which grants certain benefits and protections).
- They are also **deemed employees in the federal public administration** under:
  - The *Government Employees Compensation Act*.
  - Regulations under the *Aeronautics Act*.

> This status provides access to public service benefits, including pension plans and compensation protections.

#### Important Limitation:
- The Corporation is **not an agent of Her Majesty**.
- The President and employees are **not considered part of the federal public administration** **except as specified in Section 13** (which is not detailed in the provided text).

> This distinction is crucial—it means the Corporation operates with **autonomy** from direct government control, despite being funded and accountable to Parliament.

---

### **Tax and Financial Accountability**

- The Corporation is **deemed a registered charity** under the *Income Tax Act*, which allows it to receive tax-deductible donations and operate without corporate income tax on qualifying activities.

- **Annual audit** of financial records and transactions is conducted by the **Auditor General of Canada**.

- A **report of the audit** is submitted to the **Chairperson of the Board**.

- The Chairperson must submit to the **Minister**:
  - A full report of all proceedings for the fiscal year.
  - The financial statements of the Corporation.
  - The Auditor General’s audit report.

> This ensures **transparency and accountability** to federal oversight.

---

### **Reporting to Parliament**

- The Minister is required to **lay the annual report before Parliament**:
  - Within **15 days** of receiving it, or
  - On any of the first 15 days after Parliament resumes sitting (if not currently in session).

> This ensures that Parliament remains informed about the Corporation’s activities, finances, and performance.

---

### **Key Principles and Implications**

| Principle | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| **Autonomy** | The Corporation is independent from direct government control, allowing artistic freedom and operational flexibility. |
| **Accountability** | Strong reporting and audit requirements ensure transparency to Parliament and the public. |
| **Cultural Mission** | Focus on advancing performing arts in the National Capital Region and nationally. |
| **Governance Balance** | Inclusion of mayors and term limits ensures civic representation and prevents stagnation. |
| **Public Service Benefits** | Officers receive public service protections, enhancing stability and retention. |
| **Commercial Flexibility** | Ability to operate commercial facilities supports sustainability and public access. |

---

### **Conclusion**

The *National Arts Centre Act* creates a robust, balanced, and accountable framework for a national cultural institution. It empowers the Corporation to:

- Operate a world-class performing arts centre.
- Promote Canadian arts both locally and nationally.
- Manage its finances and property with flexibility.
- Maintain transparency and public accountability.

By combining **civic representation**, **artistic freedom**, and **fiscal responsibility**, the Act ensures that the National Arts Centre serves as a vital cultural asset for Canada—both as a regional hub and a national leader in the performing arts.
