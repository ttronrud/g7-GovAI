**Detailed Summary of the *Legislation Revision and Consolidation Act***  

---

### **Overview**

The *Legislation Revision and Consolidation Act* is a foundational piece of Canadian legislation designed to **modernize, streamline, and improve the accessibility of Canada‚Äôs statutory and regulatory law**. It establishes a formal process for the **revision and consolidation of public general statutes and regulations**, ensuring that the legal framework of Canada is clear, consistent, up-to-date, and accessible to all stakeholders ‚Äî including government officials, legal practitioners, and the public.

While the Act is comprehensive, it is important to note that **many of its provisions have been repealed or superseded**, particularly as of 2000 (e.g., sections 63 and 66). The core of the Act remains relevant in principle, especially in shaping how laws are codified and maintained. This summary focuses on the **substantive structure, purpose, and key mechanisms** of the Act, as it was originally intended to function.

---

## **Key Definitions and Key Terms**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Commission** | The Statute Revision Commission, established by the Minister of Justice, consisting of three employees appointed to revise and consolidate laws. |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Justice and Attorney General of Canada; responsible for appointing the Commission and overseeing its work. |
| **Regulations** | Includes statutory instruments, orders, and documents published in the *Canada Gazette, Part II*, or in the *Consolidated Regulations of Canada, 1978*, that are of continuing effect or apply broadly. |
| **Revision** | The process of organizing, revising, and consolidating laws ‚Äî either statutes (Part I) or regulations (Part II). |
| **Consolidated Statutes / Regulations** | The final, published, and legally binding versions of statutes or regulations that replace outdated or fragmented versions. |
| **Regulation-making authority** | The body authorized to create a specific regulation (e.g., a department or minister). |
| **Revised Statutes of Canada** | The official, consolidated version of public general statutes, which takes effect upon proclamation. |

---

## **Core Objectives of the Act**

1. **Improve Clarity and Accessibility**  
   By revising and consolidating statutes and regulations, the Act aims to eliminate confusion caused by overlapping, outdated, or fragmented laws.

2. **Ensure Legal Consistency and Uniformity**  
   The Act mandates uniform language and structure in laws, eliminating inconsistencies and improving legal predictability.

3. **Modernize Legal Infrastructure**  
   It enables the use of modern tools such as electronic publishing and indexing to make laws more accessible.

4. **Maintain Legal Continuity**  
   The Act ensures that repealed laws do not revive, invalidate saving clauses, or disrupt prior legal applications ‚Äî preserving legal certainty.

5. **Facilitate Parliamentary Oversight**  
   Draft revisions are subject to review by appropriate parliamentary committees before being introduced as a bill.

---

## **Structure and Key Provisions**

---

### **1. Establishment of the Statute Revision Commission**

- The Act establishes a **three-member Statute Revision Commission**, composed of employees of the Department of Justice.
- The **Minister appoints one member as Chairman**.
- The Commission operates **under the direction of the Minister** and is responsible for:
  - Revising public general statutes and regulations.
  - Making editorial improvements without altering the legal substance of laws.

---

### **2. Powers of the Commission in Revising Statutes (Part I)**

The Commission may, during a revision, perform the following actions:

| Action | Purpose |
|-------|--------|
| **Omit expired, repealed, or suspended laws** | Remove obsolete or no longer valid enactments. |
| **Exclude laws with limited scope** (e.g., local or private Acts) | Focus only on laws with general application to the public. |
| **Include private or local laws with public impact** | Retain laws that impose duties or limit rights on the public. |
| **Reorganize numbering and structure** | Improve logical flow and navigation (e.g., by subject or part). |
| **Revise language** | Ensure consistent, clear, and uniform expression across statutes, without changing legal meaning. |
| **Resolve inconsistencies** | Harmonize contradictory provisions in existing statutes. |
| **Correct errors** | Fix grammatical, typographical, or editing mistakes. |

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: All changes must preserve the **substance** of the original law.

---

### **3. Parliamentary Review and Approval Process**

- Before finalizing a revision, the Minister must **lay drafts of the revised statutes before parliamentary committees**:
  - A **House of Commons Committee**
  - A **Senate Committee**
  - Or a **Joint Committee** (if both Houses are involved)
- These committees examine and approve the revisions.
- Only after approval can the Minister introduce a **bill** in Parliament that reflects the revised statutes.

> üîç *Note: This ensures democratic accountability and legislative scrutiny of law revision.*

---

### **4. Revision of Regulations (Part II)**

The Commission may revise regulations in the same way as statutes, with the following powers:

| Power | Purpose |
|------|--------|
| **Exercise same editorial powers** as in statute revision | Including reorganization, language improvements, and error correction. |
| **Prepare and maintain the "Revised Regulations"** | A unified, updated version of all relevant regulations. |
| **Publish a "Roll" of regulations** | A printed document attested by the Minister and President of the Privy Council, deposited in the Clerk of the Privy Council‚Äôs office. |
| **Include schedules** | To list:
  - Regulations **superseded** by the new version.
  - Regulations **inconsistent** with the new version.
  - Regulations whose **temporary force has expired**. |

> ‚úÖ The Roll becomes the **original legal source** of the regulations.

---

### **5. Coming into Force of Revised Regulations**

- The Governor in Council may issue an **order declaring the date** when the Roll comes into force.
- On that date:
  - The regulations in the Roll **take effect as law**.
  - All listed repealed or superseded regulations are **repealed to the extent specified** in the schedule.
  - The regulations are treated as if they were **originally made** by the appropriate authority, with full compliance with all legal requirements.

> üìå **Key Principle**: The revised regulation is not a new law ‚Äî it is a **consolidation and declaration of existing law**.

---

### **6. Legal Effect and Continuity of Repealed Laws**

- **Repeal does not revive** any law.
- **Saving clauses** in repealed laws remain in force.
- **Prior application** of repealed laws to transactions **before repeal** continues to apply.
- For **transactions before the effective date**, the **repealed law prevails**.
- For **transactions after the effective date**, the **newly consolidated law prevails**.

> üîó **Reference Rule**: Any reference in a current law or document to a repealed regulation is **deemed to refer to the corresponding provision in the Revised Regulations**, with the same effect.

---

### **7. Publication and Distribution**

- The Revised Regulations may be published in:
  - **Printed form**
  - **Electronic form** (e.g., online databases)
- The **Queen‚Äôs Printer** may publish electronic versions.
- **Electronic copies are legally valid** and are **deemed to be published** unless proven otherwise.
- In case of **inconsistency** between electronic and printed versions, the **printed version prevails**.

> üí° The Act allows for **format differences** in electronic form if they do not alter the legal substance.

---

### **8. Distribution to the Public**

- The Minister may **direct free distribution** of consolidated statutes and regulations to:
  - Specific persons or classes of persons
  - In any form or manner deemed appropriate
- Distribution is **mandatory** under Governor in Council direction.

---

### **9. Legal Status of Consolidated Statutes and Regulations**

- **They do not create new law** ‚Äî they are **consolidations and declaratory statements** of existing law.
- A copy of a consolidated statute or regulation is **evidence of its contents**.
- In case of **inconsistency** with the original statute or a later amendment:
  - The **original statute or amendment prevails**.

> üìö Example: If a consolidated version of a law contains a typo, and the original statute (certified by the Clerk of Parliament) says otherwise, the original wins.

---

### **10. Agreements for Production and Distribution**

- The Minister may enter into **agreements** for:
  - Producing, publishing, selling, or distributing consolidated laws.
- These agreements can include partnerships with publishers, legal databases, or government agencies.

---

### **11. Special Provisions on Repeal and Continuity**

- If the Clerk of the Privy Council, after consultation with the Deputy Minister of Justice, believes that a regulation should be **replaced by a new one** rather than revised:
  - The Clerk may **request** the regulation-making authority to create new regulations.
  - If the authority fails to act within a **reasonable time**, the Governor in Council may **direct them to comply** via order.

> üõ†Ô∏è This provides a mechanism to **replace outdated or ineffective regulations** with new, better-designed ones.

---

### **12. Indexing and Public Access**

- The Commission may prepare and publish **indices** to the Revised Regulations for public convenience.
