**Detailed Summary of the *Canada Innovation Corporation Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Canada Innovation Corporation Act* establishes the legal framework for the **Canada Innovation Corporation (CIC)** — a federal Crown corporation created to promote innovation-driven economic growth by maximizing business investment in research and development (R&D) across all sectors and regions of Canada. The Act continues the legacy of the former *Canada Innovation Corporation*, which was previously incorporated under the *Canada Business Corporations Act*, and restructures its governance, mandate, and operational powers.

This Act provides comprehensive provisions on the **definition of key terms**, **organizational structure**, **governance**, **financial support**, **transfers of assets and programs**, and **legal status and reporting obligations**.

---

## **1. Key Definitions**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Appropriate Minister** | As defined in section 2 of the *Financial Administration Act* (typically interpreted as the Minister of Industry, or another designated federal minister). |
| **Board** | The board of directors of the Corporation. |
| **Corporation** | Refers to the Canada Innovation Corporation as continued under section 5 of this Act. |
| **Federal Institution** | A department (as defined in the *Financial Administration Act*) or a Crown corporation (as defined in subsection 83(1) of that Act). |
| **Minister** | Initially the Minister of Industry; may be designated by the Governor in Council under section 4. |

> **Note:** In case of conflict between this Act and Part X of the *Financial Administration Act*, this Act prevails.

---

## **2. Continuation and Legal Status of the Corporation**

- The Canada Innovation Corporation is **continued as a corporation under this Act**, not dissolved or replaced.
- It is incorporated under the *Canada Business Corporations Act* and continues under this new legislative framework.
- The **head office** is located in Canada and must be designated by the Governor in Council.

### **Legal Capacity**
- The Corporation is **not an agent of His Majesty in Right of Canada** unless performing specific functions:
  - Providing advice to a federal institution at the request of the appropriate Minister;
  - Developing, delivering, or administering programs under paragraph 10(e);
  - Negotiating arrangements or agreements (or carrying out related activities) under paragraph 10(f);
  - Carrying out any activity conducive to its purpose that the Governor in Council may specify by order.

> This ensures the Corporation operates with **independent authority** while remaining accountable to the federal government.

### **Purpose of the Corporation**
To **maximize business investment in R&D** across all sectors and regions of Canada to **promote innovation-driven economic growth**.

---

## **3. Powers and Functions**

The Corporation may carry out the following activities to achieve its purpose:

1. **Act as a centre of expertise** on national and international industrial and technology trends.
2. **Promote ownership and retention of intangible assets** (e.g., patents, IP) in Canada.
3. **Monitor, analyze, and disseminate R&D information** to support program evaluation and improvement.
4. **Provide financial support** (grants, loans, or advice).
5. **Develop, deliver, or administer programs** on behalf of a federal institution — only if the Corporation can recover specified costs (after consultation between the appropriate Minister and the Minister of Industry).
6. **Negotiate arrangements or agreements with foreign entities** on behalf of a federal institution.
7. **Carry out any other function** conducive to its purpose that the Governor in Council may specify by order.

> These powers emphasize **strategic advisory, data-driven policy support, and international engagement**.

---

## **4. Governance Structure**

### **Board Composition**
- Consists of:
  - A **chairperson**;
  - **Not fewer than 4 and not more than 11 other directors**.
- Total board size: **Minimum 5, maximum 12 members**.

### **Appointment of Directors**
- Each director (except chairperson) is appointed by the **Minister**, after:
  - Consultation with the **Minister of Finance** and the **Board**;
  - Approval by the **Governor in Council**.
- Term of office: **No more than four years**, with a requirement that **no more than half of the directors** have their terms expire in any one year (to ensure continuity and stability).

> **Limitation**: No more than **two directors** may be from the federal public administration.

### **Chairperson Appointment**
- Appointed by the Minister after:
  - Consultation with the Minister of Finance and the Board;
  - Approval by the Governor in Council.
- **Eligibility**: A person **cannot be a director and chairperson simultaneously**.
- **Ineligibility**: A person occupying a position in federal public administration **cannot serve as chairperson**.

### **Continuation After Term Ends**
- The chairperson may continue in office for up to **180 days** after their term ends, until:
  - Reappointment, or
  - Appointment of a successor.

> Same rule applies to the **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**.

### **CEO Appointment**
- Appointed by the Minister after consultation with the Minister of Finance, from among nominees by the Board.
- Term is set by the Minister.
- CEO may continue for up to **180 days** after term ends (same as chairperson).
- If absent or unable to act, the **Board may appoint an officer or employee** to act as CEO — but only for up to **180 days** without Ministerial approval (which can be extended).

> CEO holds office at the **discretion of the Minister** and may be removed after consultation with the Minister of Finance and the Board.

### **Board Meetings and Participation**
- The CEO may attend Board meetings or committee meetings, subject to any by-laws.

---

## **5. Employee Status and Compliance**

- Directors, CEOs, and employees of the Corporation are:
  - **Deemed employees** under the *Government Employees Compensation Act*;
  - **Employed in the federal public administration** for purposes of regulations under section 9 of the *Aeronautics Act*.

> This ensures they are covered by federal employee benefits and protections.

---

## **6. Conflict of Interest and Confidentiality**

- The Board **must establish guidelines** on:
  - Conflicts of interest;
  - Confidential information.
- These guidelines must be **submitted to the Minister** after establishment or revision.
- The Minister may, with Board consultation, **modify the guidelines** specifically for the CEO.
- The guidelines must comply with **sections 116 and 117 of the Financial Administration Act** (which govern conflicts of interest and disclosure).

> Ensures transparency, accountability, and ethical conduct.

---

## **7. Evaluation Committee**

- The Board must establish an **evaluation committee** to advise the Corporation on matters related to **financial support**.
- **Directors are ineligible** to serve on this committee.
- The Board may create by-laws governing:
  - The scope of advisory responsibilities;
  - The qualifications and expertise of members.

> This provides **independent oversight** of financial decisions.

---

## **8. Prohibition on Directives**

- **No directive** under subsection 89(1) of the *Financial Administration Act* may require the Corporation to:
  - Provide financial support to a specific entity or group;
  - Provide advice to a specific entity or group.

> Prevents arbitrary or politically driven mandates; maintains autonomy in program design and support.

---

## **9. Information Sharing**

- The Corporation may **disclose information** to a federal institution if it relates to carrying out its purpose.
- A federal institution may **disclose information** to the Corporation if it relates to its purpose.

> Facilitates **collaborative, purpose-driven information exchange** between institutions.

---

## **10. Funding and Appropriations**

### **Annual Funding Allocations**
The Minister must pay from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund** the following amounts:

| Financial Year | Amount |
|---------------|--------|
| Ending March 31, 2024 | $198,000,000 |
| Ending March 31, 2025 | $775,000,000 |
| Ending March 31, 2026 | $800,000,000 |
| Ending March 31, 2027 | $800,000,000 |
| Each subsequent year | $525,000,000 |

> This reflects a **significant increase in funding** over time, with a **sustained level of investment** post-2025.

### **Withholding of Funds**
- The Minister may **withhold all or part** of a payment, **with the approval of the Minister of Finance**.
- Must **consult the Board** before withholding.
- If funds are withheld, the Minister must **table a report explaining the reason** in each House of Parliament within **15 days** of the withholding.

> Ensures **transparency and accountability** in financial decisions.

### **Excess Funds Reporting**
- Within **three months after each financial year ends**, the Corporation must pay to the **Receiver General** any amount in excess of **$66,000,000** at the end of that year.

> Prevents accumulation of unspent funds and ensures fiscal discipline.
