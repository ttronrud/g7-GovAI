**Detailed Summary of the *Western Economic Diversification Act***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *Western Economic Diversification Act* (WEDA) is a federal Canadian law enacted on **June 28, 1988** (as noted in the note), with the primary objective of promoting the **economic development and diversification of Western Canada**. The Act establishes a dedicated federal department — the **Department of Western Economic Diversification (DWED)** — to advance the economic interests of the Western provinces: **Manitoba, British Columbia, Saskatchewan, and Alberta**.

This legislation was developed to address regional economic imbalances and to ensure that Western Canada has a stronger voice in national economic policy, investment, and program development.

---

### **Key Definitions**  

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Department** | The Department of Western Economic Diversification, established under subsection 4(1). |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Western Economic Diversification, appointed by commission under the Great Seal of Canada. |
| **Western Canada** | Refers specifically to the provinces of **Manitoba, British Columbia, Saskatchewan, and Alberta**. |

---

### **Core Purpose**  
The central goal of the Act is to:  
- Promote the **development and diversification of the economy** of Western Canada.  
- Advance the **interests of Western Canada** in national economic policy, programs, projects, and implementation.  

This includes fostering economic growth beyond traditional sectors (such as resource extraction) by encouraging innovation, entrepreneurship, and business competitiveness.

---

### **Establishment of the Department**  

- A new **Department of Western Economic Diversification** is created as a department of the Government of Canada.  
- The **Minister of Western Economic Diversification** presides over the Department and holds office at the pleasure of the Governor in Council.  
- The Minister has **full management and direction** of the Department and is responsible for all matters within its mandate that fall under Parliament’s jurisdiction and are not assigned to another federal body.  

> *Note: The Department was created by consolidating or replacing earlier bodies such as the Department of Regional Industrial Expansion and elements of the Department of Indian Affairs and Northern Development, with a reorganization to focus on Western Canada.*

---

### **Key Powers, Duties, and Functions of the Minister**  

The Minister has broad authority to guide and implement policies and programs related to Western Canada’s economic development. These include:

#### 1. **Policy Guidance and Coordination**  
- Guide, promote, and coordinate federal government policies and programs related to industrial development in Western Canada.  
- Lead and coordinate cooperation between the federal government and:  
  - The provinces of Western Canada  
  - Business and labor organizations  
  - Public and private sector stakeholders  

#### 2. **Program Planning and Strategy Development**  
- Formulate **long-term plans and integrated strategies** to support economic diversification.  
- Oversee implementation of programs and projects aligned with these strategies.  
- Where no other Minister or agency is responsible, the Minister may **initiate or implement** such programs directly.  

#### 3. **Support for Enterprises (Especially SMEs)**  
- Develop and manage programs to:  
  - Establish, develop, support, and promote **small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs)** in Western Canada.  
  - Enhance the **overall economic prosperity** of the region.  

#### 4. **Improving the Business Environment**  
- Implement initiatives to improve business conditions, including:  
  - Support for **business associations, trade shows, market research, and conferences**  
  - Development of **business opportunity data banks and networks**  
  - Enhancements in **business communication and cooperation**  

#### 5. **Policy Research and Economic Analysis**  
- Initiate, promote, and coordinate **policy research, economic analysis, and development** to inform decision-making and support regional growth.  

#### 6. **Agreements with Provincial Governments**  
- Enter into **agreements with provincial governments or their agencies** to carry out its duties and functions.  

#### 7. **Other Necessary Actions**  
- Perform all other actions reasonably required to achieve the Act’s objectives.  

---

### **Financial Tools and Funding Authority**  

The Minister has significant financial powers to support economic development:

#### 1. **Loans**  
- May make **loans** to individuals or entities for the establishment or development of enterprises — especially SMEs — in Western Canada.  
- Loans must be approved by the **Treasury Board** and require the **approval of the Minister of Finance**.  

#### 2. **Guarantees and Insurance**  
- May **guarantee repayment** or provide **loan insurance/credit insurance** for financial obligations related to enterprise development.  
- This helps reduce risk for lenders and encourages investment in small businesses.  

#### 3. **Grants and Contributions**  
- May make **grants and contributions** to support programs and projects undertaken by the Minister.  
- These funds are used to finance initiatives that promote economic growth and business development.  

> *All financial activities must comply with Treasury Board regulations and fiscal oversight.*

---

### **Geographic Presence and Operational Structure**  

- The **principal office** of the Department is located in **Edmonton, Alberta**.  
- The Department must maintain **at least one office in each of the other three Western provinces** (BC, SK, MB) to ensure regional accessibility and local engagement.  

- The Department may **utilize services and facilities** of other federal departments, boards, or agencies when appropriate to enhance efficiency and coordination.

---

### **Regulatory Authority**  

The Minister is empowered to make **regulations** to:  
1. Specify additional programs and projects to improve the business environment in Western Canada (beyond those already listed).  
2. Provide general rules and procedures for carrying out the purposes and provisions of the Act.  

These regulations must be issued in accordance with the legislative process and are subject to parliamentary scrutiny.

---

### **Reporting Requirements**  

- The Minister is required to **submit an annual report** to each House of Parliament.  
- The report must be laid before Parliament on **any of the first 15 days** after the House begins sitting in the **first fiscal year following the end of each fiscal year**.  
- The report details the administration, performance, and outcomes of the Department’s activities for that year.  

This ensures transparency, accountability, and parliamentary oversight.

---

### **Transfer of Powers and Responsibilities**  

- Any powers, duties, or functions previously vested in the **Minister of Regional Industrial Expansion**, the **Deputy Minister of Regional Industrial Expansion**, or other officers of that department — **in relation to matters in Western Canada** — are now **transferred** to the Minister of Western Economic Diversification and the Deputy Minister of Western Economic Diversification.  

> *Exception:* The Governor in Council may, by order, designate another Minister, deputy head, or officer to exercise such powers if deemed appropriate.

---

### **Public Service Employment Provisions**  

- Any **indeterminate employee** (i.e., employee without a fixed term or appointment) transferred to a position within the Department **within 90 days** of the Act coming into force is deemed to have been **appointed under the Public Service Employment Act**.  
- This ensures continuity of staffing and protects the rights of public servants during the transition.

---

### **Transition and Implementation**  

- The Act comes into force on a date or dates to be **determined by the Governor in Council**.  
- The transition provisions ensure that existing financial allocations and responsibilities from prior departments (like Regional Industrial Expansion and Indian Affairs and Northern Development) are **reallocated** to the new Department.  
- The Governor in Council has discretion to assign such allocations to specific classifications within the new Department.

---

### **Amendments and Future Flexibility**  

- The Act includes a provision allowing for amendments, enabling future adjustments to reflect changing economic conditions, technological advances, or policy priorities.  

---

### **Significance and Impact**  

The *Western Economic Diversification Act* represents a major shift in how Canada supports regional economic equity. Key impacts include:

- **Strengthening regional autonomy** by giving Western Canada a dedicated federal voice in economic planning.  
- **Targeted investment** in SMEs, innovation, and business ecosystems.  
- **Improved coordination** between federal, provincial, and private stakeholders.  
- **Enhanced regional development** through data-driven policy, market research, and business support.  

It laid the foundation for future federal initiatives such as the **Western Economic Diversification Program (WEDP)**, which continues to support regional economic resilience and diversification.

---

### **Conclusion**  

The *Western Economic Diversification Act* is a comprehensive legislative framework designed to **stimulate sustainable economic growth** in Western Canada by creating a dedicated federal department, empowering the Minister with strategic and financial tools, and ensuring accountability through reporting and oversight. By focusing on SME development, business environment improvement, and intergovernmental cooperation, the Act plays a vital role in reducing economic disparities and fostering long-term prosperity across the Western provinces.

---

> **Note:** The Act was passed under the **Statutory Instrument SI/88-119** and came into force on **June 28, 1988**. It remains a foundational piece of legislation in Canada’s regional economic policy.
