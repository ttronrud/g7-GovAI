**Detailed Summary of the *Canadian Association of Former Parliamentarians Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Canadian Association of Former Parliamentarians Act* is a federal statute enacted by the Parliament of Canada to formally establish the **Canadian Association of Former Parliamentarians (CAFP)** as a **non-profit corporation**. The Act provides a comprehensive legal framework governing the structure, purpose, operations, governance, and accountability of the Association. It is designed to enable former members of Parliament—both in the Senate and the House of Commons—to contribute their experience and knowledge to the advancement of parliamentary democracy in Canada and beyond.

---

### **Key Definitions**
The Act defines several key terms to ensure clarity and consistency in its application:

- **Association**: Refers to the Canadian Association of Former Parliamentarians established under this Act.
- **Board**: Denotes the Board of Directors responsible for managing the Association’s affairs.
- **Former parliamentarian**: A person who previously served as a member of the Senate or House of Commons but is no longer currently serving.
- **Member**: A person who is a full, active participant in the Association (excluding honorary members).

---

### **Primary Purpose**
The central objective of the Act is to **establish a non-profit, non-partisan organization** that leverages the experience and expertise of former parliamentarians to strengthen and support Canada’s parliamentary democracy.

---

### **Objects and Mission of the Association**
The Association has five core purposes:

1. **To utilize the knowledge and experience of its members** for the benefit of parliamentary democracy in Canada and internationally.
2. **To serve the public interest** by providing non-partisan support for Canada’s parliamentary system of government.
3. **To foster a sense of community** among former parliamentarians.
4. **To promote good relations** between current members of the Senate and House of Commons and former parliamentarians.
5. **To protect and promote the interests** of former parliamentarians.

> **Crucially, the Association is strictly prohibited from pursuing any partisan political objectives.** This ensures its independence and commitment to non-partisanship.

---

### **Legal Capacity and Powers**
The Association is granted the **capacity of a natural person**, allowing it to act independently in a wide range of ways, including:

- Initiating, financing, and administering programs and activities (including those involving government, public, or private entities).
- Entering into contracts with governments, organizations, or individuals.
- Publishing or disseminating information relevant to its mission.
- Establishing and awarding scholarships or fellowships related to parliamentary studies.
- Recognizing individuals for outstanding contributions to Canada’s parliamentary system.
- Borrowing money, acquiring property (including real estate, money, or securities), and investing such assets.
- Creating security interests in its assets to secure obligations.
- Establishing charitable or benevolent foundations as deemed appropriate.
- Expenditures from contributions must be used solely for the Association’s activities.

> These powers are broad but are strictly limited to fulfilling the Association’s non-partisan, democratic mission.

---

### **Geographic Scope**
- The Association may carry out its activities **throughout Canada**.
- It also has the **capacity to operate in any jurisdiction outside Canada**, provided such activities are permitted by the laws of that jurisdiction.

---

### **By-Laws and Governance Structure**
The Board of Directors is empowered to create **by-laws** covering a wide range of administrative and operational matters, including:

- Membership criteria and procedures.
- Election and term limits of directors.
- Appointment, duties, remuneration, and expenses of officers, employees, and agents.
- Meeting procedures, voting rules, and quorum requirements.
- Appointment of patrons and honorary officers.

> **Key Requirement**: No by-law is valid unless it is **approved by at least two-thirds of the members present and entitled to vote** at a general meeting.

> The existing by-laws and rules of the unincorporated association (prior to incorporation) **remain in effect** unless they conflict with this Act or are formally amended or repealed.

---

### **Head Office and Meeting Requirements**
- The **head office** of the Association may be located in any place in Canada, as determined by the Board.
- The **Board must meet at least twice per year**, with **at least one meeting held at the head office**.
- Meetings can be conducted **electronically**, and directors participating remotely are considered fully present for the purposes of this Act.
- If directors are scattered, the meeting location is determined by where a **majority of directors are physically gathered**, or where the largest number is located, or where the presiding officer is present.

---

### **Remuneration and Financial Management**
- Any **profits or appreciation in the value of the Association’s assets** must be used exclusively to further its mission.
- **No profits or assets may be distributed** to any member of the Association—ensuring that the organization remains financially independent and member-free in terms of dividends or personal benefits.

---

### **Dissolution and Succession of Assets**
- The Association may be dissolved **by petition** submitted to the **Minister of the Government of Canada responsible for federal corporations**.
- Upon dissolution:
  - All debts and liabilities must be settled.
  - Any remaining assets after those payments must be transferred to a person or institution **designated by the Association in the petition**.
- A **notice of dissolution** must be published in the *Canada Gazette*.

> Importantly, the Association is **not an agent of Her Majesty** or of the federal government. Its officers, directors, and employees are **not part of the federal public administration**, preserving its independence and autonomy.

---

### **Transition from Unincorporated Body**
- Upon coming into force, the **Executive Committee** of the pre-existing unincorporated association known as the Canadian Association of Former Parliamentarians **acquires all the powers of the Board**.
- The current members of the Executive Committee and its officers **continue to serve in their roles** under the new Act and by-laws, as if they had been formally elected or appointed under this legislation.
- They remain in office until their successors are duly appointed or elected.

---

### **Effective Date**
The Act comes into force on the day it is **assented to by Parliament**.

---

### **Key Principles and Implications**

| Principle | Significance |
|--------|-------------|
| **Non-Partisanship** | Ensures the Association remains a neutral, public-interest body, not aligned with any political party. |
| **Independence** | The Association is not part of government administration and operates independently of political influence. |
| **Community Building** | Encourages former parliamentarians to remain connected and engaged, promoting shared values and mutual respect. |
| **Public Service Mission** | The Association serves as a bridge between past and present parliamentary leadership, supporting democratic education and institutional health. |
| **Transparency & Accountability** | By-laws require approval by a supermajority (2/3), and financial distributions are prohibited, ensuring responsible governance. |

---

### **Conclusion**
The *Canadian Association of Former Parliamentarians Act* establishes a robust, legally recognized, and independent non-profit organization dedicated to preserving and enhancing Canada’s parliamentary democracy. By formalizing the role of former parliamentarians, the Act creates a sustainable platform for civic engagement, intergenerational knowledge transfer, and public service. It emphasizes **non-partisanship, transparency, financial responsibility, and democratic values**, ensuring that the Association remains a trusted and valuable institution in Canada’s political landscape.

This legislation reflects a recognition of the enduring value of parliamentary experience and the importance of maintaining a vibrant, informed, and connected community of former legislators who can continue to serve the public good long after their time in office.
