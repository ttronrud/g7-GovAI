### 3. **Enactment of New Legislation**
Two major pieces of new legislation are enacted under this Act:

#### A. **Greenhouse Gas Pollution Pricing Act**
- **Enacted:** As a standalone Act with full text and **Schedules 1 to 4** included in **Schedule 4** of this Act.
- **Purpose:** Establishes a federal carbon pricing system to reduce greenhouse gas emissions through a pollution pricing mechanism.
- **Key Features:**
  - Sets a floor price for carbon emissions (initially $50 per tonne of COâ‚‚ equivalent).
  - Allows provinces to set their own carbon pricing systems, with a floor to ensure national consistency.
  - Provides mechanisms for compliance, monitoring, and reporting.

#### B. **Fuel Charge Regulations**
- **Made:** Under section 166 of the Greenhouse Gas Pollution Pricing Act (as enacted by section 186 of this Act).
- **Purpose:** Implements the carbon pricing system by setting fees on fuel (e.g., gasoline, diesel) based on emissions.
- **Key Legal Effects:**
  - The regulations are **deemed to have been made under section 166** of the Greenhouse Gas Pollution Pricing Act.
  - They are **deemed to have been transmitted** to the Clerk of the Privy Council for registration under the *Statutory Instruments Act*.
  - They **meet publication requirements** under subsection 11(1) of the Statutory Instruments Act.

### 5. **Transitional Provisions**

#### A. **Public Sector Restructuring: Canadian Cyber Incident Response Centre (CCIRC) to Communications Security Establishment (CSE)**
- **Key Change:** Positions within the **Canadian Cyber Incident Response Centre (CCIRC)** and the **Security Operations Centre (SOC)** of Shared Services Canada are **transferred to the Communications Security Establishment (CSE)**.
- **Effective Date:** The Division comes into force on a day fixed by the Governor in Council (initially set to **October 1, 2018**).
- **Transitional Clarity:**
  - The status of affected employees (including managerial or confidential roles) is preserved.
  - Employees' **status** includes whether they hold managerial or confidential positions.
  - The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** may disclose information from CCIRC to CSE.
  - The **Minister for Shared Services Canada** may disclose SOC-related information to CSE.
