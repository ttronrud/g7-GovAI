**Detailed Summary of the *Canadian Dairy Commission Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *Canadian Dairy Commission Act* establishes and governs the structure, functions, powers, and financial framework of the **Canadian Dairy Commission (CDC)** — a federal agency tasked with supporting the stability, quality, and market viability of Canada’s dairy industry. The Act was originally enacted to ensure fair returns for dairy producers and a consistent supply of high-quality dairy products to consumers, particularly in the context of fluctuating market conditions and international competition.

Although the Act has been significantly amended and partially repealed over time (notably in 2006 and 2009), the version provided here reflects a current and updated interpretation of the legislation, including provisions related to modern governance, regulatory oversight, financial accountability, and enforcement mechanisms.

---

## **Key Definitions**

The Act begins by defining key terms used throughout:

- **Board**: A provincial body responsible for regulating the production or marketing of dairy products within a province.
- **Commission**: The Canadian Dairy Commission, continued under Section 3 of this Act.
- **Cream**: Cream derived from milk.
- **Dairy Product**: Includes milk, cream, butter, cheese, condensed milk, evaporated milk, milk powder, dry milk, ice cream, malted milk, and sherbet — any product manufactured wholly or mainly from milk.
- **Market**: Refers to interprovincial or export trade.
- **Milk**: Milk from cows only.
- **Place**: Includes any vehicle, vessel, railway car, or aircraft.
- **Regulated Product**: A dairy product whose marketing is controlled or banned by regulations made under this Act.

These definitions provide clarity on the scope of the Commission’s authority and the types of products it regulates.

---

## **Structure and Governance of the Canadian Dairy Commission**

### **1. Composition of the Commission**
- The Commission is a **corporation** composed of:
  - A **Chairperson**
  - A **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**
  - One other member

> *Note: The Chairperson position is now effectively replaced by the CEO, as outlined in the final section of the Act.*

### **2. Appointment and Tenure**
- Members are appointed by the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet).
- Office is held **during pleasure** (at the discretion of the government).
- Term of office is determined by the Governor in Council, with no fixed duration.
- A member ceases to serve upon reaching **age 70**.
- In case of absence or incapacity, a **temporary substitute** may be appointed by the Governor in Council for a specified period and under specified conditions.

### **3. Compensation**
- Members receive salaries and **travel/living expenses** as set by the Governor in Council.
- The Commission may appoint officers and employees, whose duties and employment conditions are subject to **Treasury Board approval**.

### **4. Head Office and Jurisdiction**
- The **head office is in Ottawa**.
- Meetings may be held at other locations as decided by the Commission.

### **5. Legal Status**
- The Commission is considered an **agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada**, giving it certain legal standing and authority in administrative and regulatory matters.

---

## **Consultative Committee**

- The Minister appoints a **Consultative Committee** consisting of:
  - One **Chairperson**
  - Eight other members
- Terms:
  - 3 members for **2 years**
  - 3 members for **3 years**
  - 3 members for **4 years**
- The committee advises the Commission on matters related to **dairy production and marketing**.
- Members may be paid for services and expenses as approved by the Governor in Council.

> This committee serves as a bridge between government and industry stakeholders, ensuring that policy decisions reflect both producer interests and market realities.

---

## **Core Objectives of the Commission**

The primary goals of the Commission are:

1. To ensure **efficient dairy producers** (especially small and mid-sized farms) receive a **fair return** on their labor and investment.
2. To provide **consumers** with a **continuous, adequate, and high-quality supply** of dairy products.

These objectives guide all policy and operational decisions made by the Commission.

---

## **Powers and Functions of the Commission**

The Commission has broad powers to carry out its mandate, including:

### **1. Purchase and Disposition of Dairy Products**
- May **buy** dairy products and **sell, package, process, store, ship, insure, import, or export** them.

### **2. Financial Protection for Producers**
- Can make **payments to milk and cream producers** to protect their income.
- Payments may be based on:
  - Volume
  - Quality
  - Other criteria deemed appropriate by the Commission.

> This function is central to the Commission’s role in stabilizing producer incomes during periods of low market prices.

### **3. Investigations**
- May conduct investigations into:
  - Production, processing, or marketing of dairy products
  - Costs associated with producing or marketing such products

> The Commission has **all the powers of a commissioner under the Inquiries Act**, enabling it to conduct formal investigations, gather evidence, and issue reports.

### **4. Promotion and Information**
- May promote the use of dairy products.
- Improve quality and variety.
- Publish information about dairy products to inform consumers and producers.

### **5. Establishment and Operation of Pools**
- Can establish **marketing pools** for milk or cream.
- These pools:
  - Collect money from sales of milk or cream
  - Distribute funds to producers
  - Deduct necessary operating expenses from distributions

> This mechanism helps stabilize prices and ensures producers share in the benefits of market success.

### **6. Price Setting and Management**
- May establish:
  - **Minimum or maximum prices** for milk or cream
  - The **basis** for payment (volume, quality, etc.)
  - **Terms and manner** of payment

> This power allows the Commission to manage market volatility and protect producers from price crashes.

### **7. Collection of Prices**
- Can **collect prices** paid to producers or the Commission.
- May recover such amounts in **courts of competent jurisdiction** if necessary.

### **8. International Trade Support Program**
- Under an agreement under **Section 9.1**, the Commission may:
  - Establish programs to support **quantities and prices** of milk/cream for international trade
  - Distribute funds to **equalize returns** to producers
  - Deduct operating costs from these distributions

> This enables Canada to maintain competitiveness in global dairy markets while protecting domestic producers.

### **9. General Authority**
- May do all acts and things **necessary or incidental** to carrying out its powers or functions.

---

## **Regulatory Powers (Governor in Council)**

The Governor in Council may make regulations to regulate the marketing of dairy products, including:

- **Quota-based marketing** (limiting how much a producer can sell)
- **Designation of authorized marketing agencies**
- **Licensing requirements** for production or processing:
  - Fees for licenses
  - Suspension or cancellation of licenses
- **Prohibitions** on marketing without a license
- **Books and records** that producers must maintain
- **Levies or charges**:
  - Can be imposed on producers or marketers
  - Can classify producers into groups and set different levies
  - Levies are used to fund Commission functions
- **Seizure and disposal** of dairy products marketed in violation of regulations
- General rules to implement the Act’s purposes

> These regulations give the federal government flexibility to respond to market conditions, ensure compliance, and maintain fair competition.

---

## **Registration and Accountability of Producers**

- The Governor in Council may require **producers of milk and cream** to register as a condition of receiving payments under the income protection program.
- Producers must maintain and provide **books and records** to the Commission.

> This ensures transparency and accountability in claims for producer support.

---

## **Funding and Financial Accountability**

### **Special Account – Canadian Dairy Commission Account**
- A dedicated account in the federal accounts to track all CDC-related funds.

### **Credits to the Account**
- Revenue from:
  - Commission operations (sales, processing, etc.)
  - Licence fees, levies, and charges
  - Loans from the Minister of Finance
  - Payments under the **Farm Income Protection Act** (to support producer income)

### **Payments from the Consolidated Revenue Fund**
- All expenditures (except those directly tied to income protection) are charged to this account.
- Expenditures include:
  - Salaries, travel, administrative costs
  - Repayment of loans
  - Excess levies or charges

> **No disbursement exceeds the balance in the Account** — ensuring fiscal responsibility.

### **Loans from the Minister of Finance**
- The Minister may lend money to the Commission **with Governor in Council approval**.
- Purpose: To support operations under Sections 9(1)(a) and (b) (purchase/disposal of products).
- **Aggregate loan limit**: Maximum of **$500 million** outstanding at any time.

> This financial flexibility allows the Commission to respond to emergencies or market downturns.

### **Use of a Line of Credit**
- With **Minister of Finance approval**, the Commission may establish a **line of credit** with a member of the **Canadian Payments Association** to support its operations under Sections 9(1)(f) to (i).

> This provides liquidity for marketing and price stabilization programs.

---

## **Inspection and Enforcement Powers**

### **Appointment of Inspectors**
- The Commission may appoint or designate **inspectors** to enforce the Act.

### **Inspector’s Powers**
- May enter any place (including vehicles, vessels, aircraft)
