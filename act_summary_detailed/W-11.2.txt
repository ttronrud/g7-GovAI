### **Detailed Summary of the Witness Protection Program Act**

---

#### **1. Introduction and Purpose**
The *Witness Protection Program Act* establishes a national framework to protect individuals who provide critical assistance in law enforcement, national security, or public safety matters. The Act is enacted by Her Majesty in right of Canada, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons, and is formally cited as the **Witness Protection Program Act**.

The **primary purpose** of the Act is to:
- Promote law enforcement, national security, national defence, and public safety.
- Facilitate the protection of individuals who:
  - Are directly or indirectly involved in providing information or evidence in investigations or prosecutions involving the **Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)** or other law enforcement agencies (excluding certain arrangements under the RCMP Act);
  - Assist or have agreed to assist a **federal security, defence, or safety organization**;
  - Have been admitted to a **designated provincial or municipal witness protection program**.

This protection is intended to ensure the **security of witnesses** and support their **re-establishment or self-sufficiency**.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**
The Act defines several key terms to ensure clarity and consistency:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Commissioner** | Refers to the Commissioner of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP). |
| **Designated Program** | A provincial or municipal witness protection program listed in a schedule. |
| **Designated Program Protectee** | A person receiving protection under a designated program. |
| **Federal Security, Defence or Safety Organization** | Includes federal departments, agencies, or services with mandates related to national security, defence, or public safety. |
| **Force** | Refers specifically to the Royal Canadian Mounted Police. |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness. |
| **Program** | The federal Witness Protection Program administered by the Commissioner. |
| **Protected Person** | Includes current or former protectees or designated program protectees. |
| **Protectee** | A person receiving protection under the federal Program. |
| **Protection** | May include relocation, accommodation, identity change, counselling, and financial support to ensure security and reintegration. |
| **Protection Agreement** | A formal agreement between the witness and the Commissioner outlining obligations of both parties. |
| **Provincial Official** | The designated official within a province or municipality responsible for a designated program. |
| **Substantial Harm** | Any physical or psychological injury that significantly interferes with a person’s health or well-being. |
| **Witness** | A person who: (a) gives or agrees to give evidence in a criminal inquiry or prosecution; (b) assists a federal security organization; or (c) is related to someone who does so. |

---

#### **3. Establishment of the Witness Protection Program**
- A **Witness Protection Program** is established under Section 4 and administered by the **Commissioner of the RCMP**.
- The Commissioner has **discretionary authority** to determine:
  - Whether a witness should be admitted to the Program.
  - The type and extent of protection to be provided.

---

#### **4. Admission Requirements for a Witness**
A witness may only be admitted to the Program if:
1. A **recommendation** has been made by:
   - A law enforcement agency,
   - A federal security, defence, or safety organization, or
   - An international criminal court or tribunal.
2. The **Commissioner receives sufficient personal information** from the witness (as per regulations) to assess risks and suitability.
3. A **protection agreement** is signed between the witness and the Commissioner.

> **Exception for Emergencies**: In cases of emergency, the Commissioner may provide protection **without a formal agreement** for up to **90 days**. If the emergency continues, an additional **90-day period** may be granted.

---

#### **5. Factors Considered by the Commissioner in Admission Decisions**
The Commissioner must evaluate the following factors when deciding whether to admit a witness:
- The **nature and severity of the risk** to the witness’s security.
- The **danger to the community** if the witness is admitted.
- The **importance of the witness** in the investigation or assistance provided.
- The **value** of the witness’s participation or information.
- The **likelihood of the witness adjusting** to the Program, based on maturity, judgment, and family dynamics.
- The **cost** of maintaining the witness in the Program.
- The **availability of alternative protection methods** (e.g., non-admission-based safeguards).
- Any **other relevant factors** deemed appropriate by the Commissioner.

---

#### **6. Obligations Under the Protection Agreement**
The protection agreement includes the following **obligations**:

| Obligation | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Commissioner’s Duty** | To take reasonable steps to provide the agreed-upon protection. |
| **Protectee’s Duties** | To: <br> • Provide evidence or participate in proceedings as required; <br> • Meet all financial obligations not covered by the agreement; <br> • Fulfill legal obligations (e.g., child custody/maintenance); <br> • Avoid activities that constitute offences or compromise security; <br> • Accept and comply with reasonable directions from the Commissioner. |

---

#### **7. Termination of Protection**
- A protectee may **request termination** of protection through a personal meeting with the Commissioner.
- Termination takes effect on the day the request is confirmed or on a later date specified by the protectee.
- The Commissioner may **terminate protection** if there is:
  - A **material misrepresentation** or failure to disclose relevant information during admission; or
  - A **deliberate and material breach** of the protectee’s obligations.
- Before termination, the Commissioner must:
  - Notify the protectee;
  - Allow the protectee to make representations.

> **Written Reasons Required**: In both cases (refusal of admission or termination), the Commissioner must provide **written reasons** to the recommending body or the protectee to ensure transparency.

---

#### **8. Designation of Provincial and Municipal Programs**
- The **Minister of Public Safety** may, by regulation, **designate** a provincial or municipal witness protection program by adding it to a schedule.
- A **provincial official** is appointed to oversee the program.
- The provincial minister may **replace** the official and must **notify the federal Minister** promptly.
- The Governor in Council may **remove** a program from the schedule on the Minister’s recommendation.

---

#### **9. Coordination Between Federal and Provincial Agencies**
- The **Commissioner** must coordinate with federal departments, agencies, and services to facilitate a **change of identity** for a designated program protectee.
- The **provincial official** must provide any necessary information to the Commissioner for this purpose.

---

#### **10. Confidentiality and Disclosure Restrictions**
This section is critical to protecting the integrity and security of the program.

##### **Prohibited Disclosures (Section 11(1))**
No person may directly or indirectly disclose:
- The **location or identity change** of a protected person;
- Information about **means and methods** of protection (e.g., covert operations, administrative procedures, locations of facilities), **knowing or recklessly** that such disclosure could cause **substantial harm**;
- The **identity or role** of someone providing protection, or their family members, or other protected persons, **knowing or recklessly** that disclosure could cause substantial harm.

> **Exceptions**:
- A protected person may disclose personal information **without causing harm**.
- A person may disclose information **received from a protected person**, provided it does not result in substantial harm.

##### **Permitted Disclosures by the Commissioner**
The Commissioner may disclose protected information under the following conditions:

| Scenario | Conditions |
|--------|------------|
| **(a)** For protection purposes | To provide protection to a protectee. |
| **(b)** For identity change | To facilitate a change of identity for a designated program protectee. |
| **(c)** For justice, security, or defence | If the disclosure is essential for: <br> • Investigating a serious offence (if the person can provide material evidence); <br> • Preventing a serious offence; <br> • Establishing innocence in criminal proceedings; <br> • National security or national defence; <br> • Under an agreement (subsection 14.1). |

> **Special Rules for Designated Program Protectees**:
- Disclosure under (c) is only allowed if:
  - The situation is **urgent**, or
  - The **provincial official consents**.

##### **Disclosure by Provincial Officials**
A provincial official may disclose information if:
- The protectee **consents**;
- The protectee has **previously disclosed** the information;
- There is a **reasonable basis** for the disclosure in relation to justice (e.g., investigating a serious offence, preventing crime, or establishing innocence);
- The disclosure is made under an agreement (subsection 14.1(2)).

> **Provincial Officials’ Disclosure Criteria**:
- Must have reasonable grounds for the disclosure.
- Must consider the **risk of harm**, **likelihood of misuse**, and **availability of alternative means**.

##### **Disclosure by Third Parties**
Any party that has entered into an agreement or arrangement under subsection 14.1(1) or (2) may disclose information **in accordance with that agreement**.

- Take reasonable steps to **notify the protected person**;
- Allow them to make **representations**.
