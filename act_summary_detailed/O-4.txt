**Detailed Summary of the *Official Residences Act***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *Official Residences Act* is a Canadian statute that establishes and governs the official residences for key parliamentary figures: the **Prime Minister**, the **Leader of the Opposition**, and the **Speaker of the House of Commons**. It ensures that these individuals have designated, maintained, and funded residences within the National Capital Region (NCR), primarily in Ottawa and surrounding areas. The Act provides for the legal status, maintenance, staffing, funding, and operational rules of these residences, while emphasizing their role as official living accommodations rather than personal property.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Legislative Authority and Purpose**  
- The Act may be cited as the *Official Residences Act*.  
- It **supersedes or modifies provisions** of the *Parliament of Canada Act* to ensure that specific lands and buildings are maintained as official residences for designated political leaders.  
- The purpose is to provide dignified, secure, and properly maintained residences for the Prime Minister, the Leader of the Opposition, and the Speaker of the House of Commons—ensuring they can carry out their duties effectively and with appropriate recognition.

---

### **2. Designated Residences by Office**

#### **(a) Prime Minister’s Residence**  
- Lands and buildings described in **Schedule I** are maintained as the official residence of the **Prime Minister of Canada**.  
- This includes both land and structures on that property, which are to be preserved and managed as a residence.

#### **(b) Leader of the Opposition’s Residence**  
- Lands and buildings described in **Schedule II** are maintained as the official residence of the **Leader of the Opposition** (the person recognized as the head of the official opposition in the House of Commons).  
- This residence is distinct from the Prime Minister’s and is intended to provide similar facilities and dignity to the opposition leader.

#### **(c) Speaker of the House of Commons’ Residence**  
- Lands and buildings described in **Schedule III** are maintained as the official residence of the **Speaker of the House of Commons**.  
- The Speaker, as a neutral and ceremonial figure, is entitled to a residence to support their role in parliamentary operations.

---

### **3. Summer Residence for the Leader of the Opposition**  
- The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet, acting on ministerial advice) may, by **order**, allocate **additional lands** in the National Capital Region (as defined in the *National Capital Act*) for use as a **summer residence** for the Leader of the Opposition.  
- This provision allows flexibility in providing seasonal accommodation, possibly for health, climate, or diplomatic reasons.  
- **Mandatory Parliamentary Notification**:  
  - Any such order must be **laid before Parliament** not later than the **fifteenth sitting day** of Parliament after it is made.  
  - A "sitting day" is defined as a day on which either House of Parliament is in session.  
  - This ensures transparency and parliamentary oversight of such decisions.

---

### **4. Maintenance and Management Responsibilities**

#### **(a) Maintenance of Buildings and Land**  
- The **Minister of Public Works and Government Services** is responsible for:  
  - Furnishing, maintaining, heating, and keeping in repair the buildings on the lands described in Schedules I–III or allocated under Section 5.  
- The **National Capital Commission (NCC)** is responsible for:  
  - Maintaining the land and, **as needed**, improving it (e.g., landscaping, infrastructure, or structural upgrades).

#### **(b) Staffing and Employment**  
- The **Governor in Council** may appoint:  
  - A **steward or housekeeper** and such other employees as deemed necessary for the management of the **Prime Minister’s residence**.  
  - The **Prime Minister may approve** casual employees to assist the regular staff.  
- The **Leader of the Opposition** may appoint:  
  - A steward or housekeeper and **no more than three other employees** to manage their residence.  
  - This limitation ensures cost control and avoids excessive staffing.  

#### **(c) Prime Minister’s Chauffeur**  
- The Prime Minister’s **chauffeur may be provided with lodging at no cost**, recognizing the logistical and practical needs of the office.

---

### **5. Financial and Fiscal Provisions**

#### **(a) Funding for the Prime Minister’s Residence**  
- Moneys appropriated by Parliament for the operation of the Prime Minister’s residence may be used for:  
  - Payment of staff remuneration (under Section 7).  
  - Purchase of food and supplies for meals.  
  - Cleaning, laundering, and ordinary maintenance.  
  - Official hospitality costs incurred by the Prime Minister.  

#### **(b) Funding for the Leader of the Opposition’s Residence**  
- Moneys appropriated for the operation of the House of Commons may be used to:  
  - Pay the remuneration of employees under Section 8 (i.e., the Leader of the Opposition’s staff).  
  - Cover other related expenses of their employment.

> **Note**: All benefits provided under this Act (including housing, staff, and services) are **treated as living expense allowances**, explicitly fixed and authorized by the Act. This legal classification ensures that such expenses are not considered personal income or gifts, but rather legitimate and transparent official costs.

---

### **6. Detailed Land Descriptions (Schedules I–IV)**  

The Act includes **four specific parcels of land** with precise legal descriptions. These are the official residences for the Prime Minister, the Leader of the Opposition, and the Speaker. Each is described using traditional surveying terminology (bearings, distances, and landmarks).

---

#### **(1) Prime Minister’s Residence – Ottawa (Lot 3, Gloucester Township)**  
- Located in **Ottawa, County of Carleton, Ontario**.  
- Part of **Lot 3 in the Ottawa and Rideau Junction Gore**.  
- **Area**: 3.98 acres (approximately 16,000 m²).  
- **Description**:  
  - Begins at a point on the **westerly limit of Ottawa Street (Sussex Street)**, 173 ft 8 in south of the intersection with MacKay Street.  
  - Runs **450 ft westward** parallel to MacKay Street to the **high water mark of the Ottawa River**.  
  - Follows the riverbank to the westerly limit of Ottawa Street.  
  - Then proceeds **563 ft 8 in south** back to the starting point.  
- This is one of the oldest and most historically significant official residences.

---

#### **(2) Leader of the Opposition’s Residence – Gatineau (Lot 2B, Eardley Township)**  
- Located in **Gatineau, County of Gatineau, Quebec**.  
- Part of **Lot 2B, Range 10** in the cadastre of the Township of Eardley.  
- **Area**: Not explicitly stated, but described in detail via coordinates and bearings.  
- **Description**:  
  - Begins at the **westerly limit of Philippe Road**, intersecting the southerly shore of a creek flowing from Harrington Lake (Lac Mousseau).  
  - Travels **1,089.73 ft** on a bearing of S.15°44′E.  
  - Then follows a series of bearings (N.72°33′W, N.56°22′W, N.48°32′W, N.46°25′W) totaling approximately 1,000 ft.  
  - Ends at a point on a small drainage creek flowing into Harrington Lake.  
  - Proceeds **northerly** along the creek to the lake’s southerly shore, then **easterly** to the point of origin.  
- This property is located in a scenic area near the Ottawa River and is likely used for summer or seasonal accommodation.

---

#### **(3) Prime Minister’s Residence – Rockcliffe Park (Block A8, Ottawa-Carleton)**  
- Located in **Rockcliffe Park, Regional Municipality of Ottawa-Carleton, Ontario**.  
- Part of **Block A8** on Plan M22, filed with the Land Titles Office.  
- **Area**: 45,560 square feet (~4.2 acres).  
- **Description**:  
  - Begins at the **south-westerly angle of Block A12**.  
  - Proceeds **205.2 ft south** along the westerly limit of A12.  
  - Turns **247.3 ft east** along the northerly limit of Hillcrest Road.  
  - Moves **209.25 ft north** along the westerly limit of Acacia Avenue.  
  - Then **228.42 ft west** back to the start.  
- This residence is in a residential neighborhood and may serve as a primary or secondary official residence.

---
