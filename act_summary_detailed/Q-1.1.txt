**Detailed Summary of the *Quarantine Act* (Canada)**

---

### **1. General Overview**

The *Quarantine Act* is a federal law enacted by Her Majesty in Right of Canada, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons. It serves as a comprehensive legal framework to **protect public health** by preventing the **introduction and spread of communicable diseases** into Canada. The Act empowers the Government of Canada—specifically the **Minister of Health**—to implement measures related to the screening, assessment, detention, and management of individuals and goods arriving in or departing from Canada.

The Act applies to all persons, conveyances (vehicles), and locations involved in international or domestic travel, particularly where there is a risk of disease transmission.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

The Act establishes precise definitions of key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Communicable disease** | A disease caused by an infectious agent or biological toxin that poses a significant risk to public health; includes diseases listed in a schedule. Also includes the infectious agents causing such diseases. |
| **Conveyance** | Any means of transportation (e.g., aircraft, train, vehicle, cargo container) arriving in or departing from Canada. |
| **Entry point** | A designated point in Canada where customs offices are located or where the Minister designates a point for entry screening. |
| **Departure point** | A point designated by the Minister for departure screening, especially to prevent disease spread. |
| **Health assessment** | Evaluation of medical history, travel history, and physical examination (head, neck, extremities), including vital signs like temperature, heart rate, and respiratory rate. |
| **Medical examination** | Includes medical history, physical exam, and diagnostic tests (lab, X-ray) to determine if a person has a communicable disease. |
| **Medical practitioner** | A person licensed to practice medicine in a province. |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Health. |
| **Operator** | The person in charge of a conveyance, including crew members. |
| **Owner** | Includes lessees (tenants or renters). |
| **Peace officer** | As defined in the *Criminal Code* (e.g., police officers). |
| **Prescribed** | As defined by regulation. |
| **Quarantine facility** | A place used to detain travelers. |
| **Quarantine station** | A place used for administering and enforcing the Act. |
| **Screening officer** | A person designated by the Minister or a customs officer. |
| **Traveller** | Any person arriving in or departing from Canada, including conveyance operators. |
| **Vector** | An insect or animal capable of transmitting a communicable disease (e.g., mosquitoes, ticks). |

---

### **3. Purpose of the Act**

The **primary purpose** of the Act is to:
- Prevent the **introduction** of communicable diseases into Canada.
- Prevent the **spread** of such diseases within Canadian communities.
- Protect public health through timely detection, screening, isolation, and medical evaluation of potentially infected individuals.

---

### **4. Designation of Officers and Personnel**

The Minister has broad authority to designate qualified individuals to perform specific roles:

- **Analysts, screening officers, and environmental health officers** – Can be designated by the Minister.
- **Quarantine officers** – Must be medical practitioners or other qualified health professionals.
- **Review officers** – Medical practitioners designated to review detentions and recommend release.

All designated officers receive a **certificate of designation**, which must be produced upon request during inspections or questioning.

---

### **5. Establishment of Quarantine Stations and Facilities**

- The Minister may **establish a quarantine station** at any location in Canada.
- The **operator of a facility with a customs office** must provide free, usable space (and fixtures) to the Minister if required to set up a quarantine station.
- The Minister may **designate any place as a quarantine facility** by order, and may amend, cancel, or reinstate such designations.
- If the Minister deems temporary use of a place necessary for public health, the place is **deemed designated** as a quarantine facility.
- The Minister may **compensate** individuals or entities whose property is temporarily used.
- Before taking possession, the Minister must **consult with the provincial public health authority**.

---

### **6. Entry and Departure Points**

- The Minister may designate any point in Canada as an **entry point** (where travelers must present themselves upon arrival).
- The Minister may designate a **departure point** (where travelers must present themselves before leaving) if necessary to prevent disease spread.

---

### **7. Screening Requirements**

#### **Upon Entry to Canada**
- Every person subject to the *Customs Act* must:
  - Present themselves **immediately upon entry** to a screening officer at the **nearest entry point**.

#### **Before Departure from Canada**
- Every person leaving Canada through a **departure point** must:
  - Present themselves **immediately before departure** to a screening officer or quarantine officer.

#### **Use of Screening Technology**
- Authorized persons may use **non-invasive screening technologies** (e.g., thermal scanners, pulse oximeters) that do not enter the body.
- If a traveler **refuses screening** and the person using the technology is **not a screening or quarantine officer**, they must **immediately inform** a qualified officer.

#### **Traveler Obligations**
- Travelers must:
  - Answer relevant questions.
  - Provide any records or information reasonably required.
  - Disclose if they have **reasonable grounds** to suspect:
    - They have or might have a communicable disease listed in the schedule.
    - They are infested with vectors (e.g., insects).
    - They have been in close contact with someone who has such a disease or vectors.

#### **Compliance with Measures**
- Travelers must comply with any **reasonable measure** ordered by a screening or quarantine officer to prevent disease spread.

---

### **8. Role of Screening Officers**

A screening officer must:

- **Immediately inform** a quarantine officer if:
  - They suspect a traveler has or might have a communicable disease or is infested with vectors.
  - The traveler refuses screening.
  - The traveler refuses to answer questions or provide information.
  - The traveler refuses to comply with a measure.

- **Isolate** a traveler (individually or in a group) **without waiting for quarantine officer directives**, if they have reasonable grounds for suspicion.

- **Inform the traveler** of any measure before it is taken (if reasonably possible).

- **Request arrest** (without warrant) from a peace officer if a traveler refuses to be isolated or refuses to comply with a measure.

---

### **9. Health Assessment**

A quarantine officer may require a traveler to undergo a **health assessment** if:

- The officer has reasonable grounds to suspect the traveler has or might have a communicable disease or is infested with vectors.
- The traveler refused screening.
- The traveler violated screening or compliance requirements.

- The assessment must be completed **within 48 hours** of being required.

- The assessment applies to:
  - Any person at an entry or departure point who has been in close contact with a suspected case or vector-infested individual.

> **Note**: "Traveller" includes any person required to undergo a health assessment.

---

### **10. Medical Examination**

If a health assessment raises concerns, a quarantine officer may require a **medical examination** by a licensed medical practitioner.

- Must be conducted **within 48 hours** of the requirement.
- The traveler has the **right to request a medical examination by their own chosen practitioner** (at their own expense).
- The quarantine officer may accept this request only if it would **not unduly delay** public health measures.

---

### **11. Notification and Public Health Authority Involvement**

If a quarantine officer has reasonable grounds to suspect a traveler has a communicable disease but **does not believe the person poses an immediate risk**, they may:

- Order the traveler to **report to a provincial public health authority**.
- Send a copy of the order to the authority immediately.
- The authority must inform the officer whether the traveler reported.

If a traveler is found to have a communicable disease or vector infestation, the officer may:

- Order the traveler to **comply with treatment or other preventive measures** (e.g., medication, quarantine, disinfection).

---

### **12. Detention Powers**

A quarantine officer may detain a traveler if:

- They refused to be disinfested or undergo a health assessment.
- They were required to undergo a medical examination.
- They failed to comply with a treatment or reporting order.
- They have or might have a communicable disease or vector infestation and are **capable of infecting others**.
- They were arrested under Section 18 (without warrant) or Section 27.

> **Detention is only justified if the traveler poses a risk of significant harm to public health.**

---

### **13. Right to Review and Legal Safeguards**

To prevent arbitrary detention:

- A traveler detained under Section 28 must be informed of their **right to a review** of the detention decision.
- The traveler must be given the opportunity to undergo a medical examination **at least every seven days**.
- The quarantine officer must **confirm every seven days** that continued detention is necessary based on medical findings or other information.
- If the officer finds no risk of harm, the traveler may be released.
