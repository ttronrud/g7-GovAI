### **Detailed Summary of the Laurier House Act**

The **Laurier House Act** is a Canadian statute enacted to fulfill the final wishes of **William Lyon Mackenzie King**, Canada’s longest-serving Prime Minister (1921–1948), regarding the preservation, administration, and use of **Laurier House** in Ottawa and the associated **$225,000 bequest** to the Government of Canada. The Act ensures that the property and funds are managed in accordance with King’s expressed intentions, emphasizing historical preservation, public access, and scholarly use.

---

## **I. Background and Legislative Context**

The Act is rooted in the **last will and testament of William Lyon Mackenzie King**, who, in his final years, expressed a deep desire to leave Laurier House—a historic residence occupied by two of Canada’s most prominent Prime Ministers (Sir Wilfrid Laurier and himself)—to the Government of Canada for the benefit of the Canadian people.

Key motivations in King’s will included:
- Honoring his personal friendship with Sir Wilfrid Laurier.
- Promoting national unity and historical continuity.
- Preserving a tangible connection to Canada’s political past.
- Ensuring the residence serves as a living museum and center for historical research.

The Act was passed by **His Majesty in right of Canada**, with the **advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons**, affirming its constitutional legitimacy and public significance.

---

## **II. Key Provisions of the Act**

### **1. Ownership and Custody of Laurier House**

- **Custodian**: The **Parks Canada Agency** (now known as Parks Canada) is designated as the primary body responsible for:
  - The **care, custody, and control** of Laurier House and its contents.
  - Administering the property in accordance with the **specific provisions of King’s Will**.
- The Agency is empowered to manage the property with a focus on **historical integrity**, **preservation**, and **public access**.

> *Note*: This provision establishes Parks Canada as the steward of a national heritage site, aligning with its mandate to protect and interpret Canada’s cultural and natural heritage.

---

### **2. Maintenance Responsibilities**

- **Buildings**: The **Minister of Public Works and Government Services** is responsible for:
  - Maintaining, heating, and keeping in repair the **buildings** on the Laurier House property.
- **Grounds**: The **Federal District Commission** is responsible for:
  - Maintaining the **grounds** surrounding the property.

> *Note*: This division of responsibilities ensures that both structural and environmental aspects of the site are properly managed.

> *Repealed provision (1998)*: A former clause that allowed the Minister of Public Works to manage the grounds was repealed in 1998 (Section 52 of *c. 31*), indicating a shift in administrative oversight over time.

---

### **3. Financial Provisions – The Mackenzie King Trust**

- The **$225,000** bequeathed by King is:
  - **Deposited** into the **Consolidated Revenue Fund**.
  - Placed in a dedicated account called the **Mackenzie King Trust Account**.
- **Interest** on the principal sum is:
  - **Credited annually** to the Trust Account.
  - Computed at a rate equal to the **average interest rate on long-term Canadian Government bonds** for that fiscal year.
  - For the first year: interest is calculated **from the date of deposit**.
  - For all subsequent years: interest is calculated for the **entire fiscal year**.

> *Purpose*: The interest provides a sustainable, non-exploitative source of funding for the upkeep and maintenance of Laurier House.

---

### **4. Use of Funds**

- The **Agency** may **expend funds** from the Trust Account for the purposes set out in King’s Will.
- The total amount that can be spent is limited to:
  - The **cumulative interest earned** in the Trust Account **minus** any prior expenditures.
- Any expenditure must be **approved by the Governor in Council** (the federal Cabinet).
- Funds are **debited directly from the Trust Account** upon disbursement.

> *Key Principle*: This ensures that spending is strictly tied to the original intent of the bequest and is subject to accountability and oversight.

---

### **5. Consultation and Oversight**

- The **Chief Executive Officer of Parks Canada** may consult the **Historic Sites and Monuments Board (HSMB)** on matters related to the management of Laurier House.
- This provides a formal mechanism for expert advice on heritage interpretation, conservation standards, and public use.

> *Purpose*: Ensures that the site is managed with the highest standards of historical and cultural sensitivity.

---

### **6. Compliance with Government Directions**

- The **Minister of Public Works**, the **Federal District Commission**, and the **Parks Canada Agency** are required to comply with any **general or special directions** issued by the **Governor in Council** regarding:
  - The administration of Laurier House.
  - The realization of King’s desires and purposes.

> *Significance*: This provision gives the federal government the authority to intervene or direct policy if needed, ensuring flexibility and responsiveness to evolving public or administrative needs.

---

### **7. Legal Description of the Property**

The property comprises:
- **Lot 31** and the **west half of Lot 32** on the north side of Theodore Street (now Laurier Avenue), Ottawa.
- Located at **335 Laurier Avenue East**, Ottawa, Ontario.
- Registered in the **Registry Office of Ottawa on June 17, 1859**.

This precise legal description ensures clarity and enforceability in property rights and boundaries.

---

## **III. King’s Wishes as Set Out in the Will (Second Schedule)**

The Act incorporates and implements the following key intentions from King’s Will:

### **1. Bequest of the House**
- Laurier House (including all its contents) is bequeathed to the **Government of Canada** in **trust for the people of Canada**.
- **Exclusions**: Certain papers, books, furnishings, and items are specifically designated for disposal elsewhere in the Will.

> *Significance*: The residence is not merely a private estate but a **national heritage asset**.

### **2. Preservation of Key Features**
- The **dining room and its furnishings, paintings, and decor** are to be **retained as they are**.
- The **top-floor library and adjoining rooms**—where King conducted much of his work—are to be preserved for **scholarly research**.

### **3. Connection to the Public Archives of Canada**
- King specifically hoped that the house would be **linked to the Public Archives of Canada**.
- The **Dominion Archivist** (the head of the Archives) would have responsibility for:
  - Supervising the preservation and use of the house.
  - Facilitating access for researchers.
- This linkage ensures that the residence becomes a **living archive**, where historical documents and artifacts are preserved and accessible.

> *Impact*: This transforms Laurier House from a static monument into a dynamic center for **historical research and public education**.

### **4. Purpose of the $225,000 Bequest**
- The sum was contributed by **Peter C. Larkin and other friends** after the house was rebuilt and refurnished.
- It was intended to:
  - Cover the **ongoing maintenance** of the house.
  - Support King’s **personal and public needs**, especially during retirement.

> *Note*: The bequest is not for personal benefit but for **public good and historical continuity**.

---

## **IV. Significance and Legacy**

The **Laurier House Act** is more than a piece of legislation—it is a **living testament to Canadian political history and national identity**. It embodies several important values:

| Value | Reflection in the Act |
|------|------------------------|
| **Historical Preservation** | The house is preserved in its original form, with specific emphasis on authenticity. |
| **Public Access** | Scholars and the public can engage with the residence as a site of learning and remembrance. |
| **National Unity** | King’s emphasis on unity between Laurier and himself reflects a broader vision of national cohesion. |
| **Accountability and Oversight** | Clear financial and administrative controls ensure transparency and responsible use. |
| **Cultural Heritage** | The house functions as a bridge between past and present, linking political history with public memory. |

---

## **V. Conclusion**

The **Laurier House Act** successfully fulfills the enduring legacy of William Lyon Mackenzie King by:
- Establishing a formal, legally binding framework for the **management of a national heritage site**.
- Ensuring the **preservation of a historically significant residence**.
- Allocating a **sustainable financial mechanism** (the Trust Account) to support its upkeep.
- Linking the property to the **Public Archives of Canada** to promote research and public education.
- Maintaining a strong **commitment to historical authenticity and public benefit**.

Today, **Laurier House** stands as a national monument, a place where Canadians can reflect on the nation’s political evolution, the personal lives of its leaders, and the enduring values of unity, service, and historical memory.
