**Detailed Summary of the *Canada Worker Lockdown Benefit Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Canada Worker Lockdown Benefit Act* is a temporary legislative measure introduced to provide financial support to workers who experienced income loss due to public health restrictions related to the **COVID-19 pandemic**, specifically during a defined period between **October 24, 2021, and May 7, 2022**. The benefit was designed to assist individuals who lost income or faced significant reductions in earnings because of **lockdown orders** imposed by various levels of government during that time.

The Act establishes eligibility criteria, administrative procedures, payment mechanisms, and enforcement provisions for a **Canada Worker Lockdown Benefit (CWLBB)**, which pays **$300 per week** to qualifying individuals. It also includes provisions for compliance, repayment of overpayments, and appeals.

---

## **Key Components of the Act**

---

### **1. Definitions (Section 2)**

The Act provides clear definitions of key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Benefit Period** | The time frame set by a government order under subsection 3(1), during which a lockdown order is in effect. |
| **Competent Authority** | Any entity legally authorized to impose public health measures, including: <br> - Government of Canada <br> - Provincial or territorial governments <br> - Municipalities <br> - Public health authorities <br> - Indigenous groups with constitutional rights under Section 35 of the *Constitution Act, 1982* |
| **COVID-19** | The coronavirus disease of 2019. |
| **Her Majesty** | Refers to the monarch in right of Canada. |
| **Lockdown Benefit** | A Canada Worker Lockdown Benefit, paid under Section 8. |
| **Lockdown Order** | A public health measure imposed by a competent authority that includes: <br> - Closure of non-essential commercial premises <br> - Limiting public access to premises (by at least 50%) <br> - Mandatory stay-at-home orders (except for essential needs) <br> These must apply for **at least 14 consecutive days** (or a lower number if regulated). |
| **Lockdown Region** | A geographic area designated as a lockdown region in an official order. |
| **Medical Practitioner** | A licensed physician or medical professional under provincial law. |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Employment and Social Development. |
| **Return of Income** | A tax return filed under Part I of the *Income Tax Act*, excluding certain specific types (e.g., Canada Recovery Benefits, maternity/parental allowances). |
| **Week** | A seven-day period beginning on Sunday and ending on Saturday. |

> *Note: The Act is time-bound and applies only to weeks within the specified period (Oct 24, 2021 â€“ May 7, 2022), with possible extension via regulation.*

### **2. Designation of Lockdown Regions (Section 3)**

- The **Governor in Council**, on the recommendation of the **Minister of Employment and Social Development**, may designate a region as a **lockdown region**.
- The Minister can only recommend such a designation if:
  - It is in the **public interest**; and
  - The lockdown order (as defined) applies in the region for **at least 14 consecutive days** (or a lower number if set by regulation).
- The **benefit period** begins on the **Sunday** when the lockdown order starts and ends on the **Saturday** when it ends.
- This ensures alignment between the start/end of public health restrictions and the eligibility window for the benefit.

---

### **3. Eligibility Criteria (Section 4)**

To qualify for the lockdown benefit in a given week, a person must meet **all of the following conditions**:

#### **(a) Basic Requirements**
- Has a **valid Social Insurance Number (SIN)**.
- Is **at least 15 years old** on the first day of the week.
- Is **resident and present in Canada** during the week.

#### **(b) Income Requirement**
- Must have had **total income of at least $5,000** from the following sources in either:
  - 2020 (for applications in 2021)
  - 2020 or 2021 (for applications in 2022)
  - Or in the 12-month period before the application date.

> Sources include:
> - Employment income  
> - Self-employment income  
> - Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB) or Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB) payments  
> - Employment Insurance (EI) benefits  
> - Provincial maternity, parenting, or adoption allowances  
> - Any other income prescribed by regulation  

> *Income from self-employment is calculated as revenue minus expenses.*

#### **(c) Loss of Income Due to Lockdown Measures**
The person must have:
- **Lost employment** during the week due to the lockdown measures (i.e., the measures began before the week and ended after it), **and were unemployed during the week**;
- **Been unable to perform self-employment work** as they normally did before the lockdown began; or
- **Had a 50% or greater reduction** in their average weekly employment or self-employment income compared to:
  - 2020 (for 2021 applications), or
  - 2020 or 2021 (for 2022 applications)

> This reduction must be **relative to income earned before the lockdown**.

#### **(d) Exclusion of Other Income**
No other income may have been received during the week from:
- Maternity/parenting/adoption allowances
- Canada Recovery Sickness Benefit (CRSB) or Caregiving Benefit (CRCB)
- Employment Insurance benefits
- Any other income prescribed by regulation

> *This ensures the benefit is only for those who are truly financially impacted and not receiving other forms of support.*

#### **(e) No Voluntary Exit from Work**
The individual must not have:
- Quitted employment or voluntarily stopped working unless it was **reasonable** to do so;
- Failed to return to work when requested by employer;
- Failed to resume self-employment when reasonable;
- Declined a reasonable offer to work during the week.

> This prevents fraud or abuse of the system by individuals who chose to leave work for personal reasons.

#### **(f) No Quarantine or Isolation (with exceptions)**
- The person must **not have been required to quarantine or isolate** upon entering Canada due to a public health order **during the week**, unless:
  - They were outside Canada to receive **medical treatment certified by a medical practitioner**; or
  - They were accompanying a person with a medical condition requiring care; or
  - Their isolation would not have occurred had they not entered Canada.

> This rule excludes individuals who entered Canada under quarantine conditions unless medically justified.

#### **(g) Tax Return Requirement**
- Must have filed a **return of income for the 2020 taxation year**.

> *This is a critical compliance requirement to verify income history.*

---

### **4. Exclusions and Disqualifications**

- **Refusal to be vaccinated** against COVID-19 is a **disqualifying factor**:
  - A person who lost income because they refused vaccination is **not eligible**.
  - Such refusal is **not considered a reasonable excuse** for quitting work or failing to return to employment.

> This reflects the government's stance that public health compliance is a condition of eligibility.

---

### **5. Application Process (Section 5)**

- Any eligible person may apply **in the form and manner established by the Minister**.
- Applications must be submitted **within 60 days** of the end of the week for which the benefit is claimed.
- **Exception**: For weeks ending before the Act came into force, applications may be submitted within 60 days of the Act's effective date.
- Applicants must **attest** that they meet all eligibility conditions.
- If a person has previously received a lockdown benefit, they **do not need to attest to income** from that prior benefit.

> *The Minister may request additional information or documentation to verify claims.*

---

### **6. Payment of the Benefit (Section 6)**

- The **amount of the lockdown benefit is $300 per week**.
- Payments are made **only to those who are eligible** and have submitted a valid application.
- The benefit is **not subject to**:
  - Bankruptcy or insolvency laws
  - Assignment, charging, or garnishment
  - Deduction or set-off under any other Act (except this Act)
  - Garnishment under the *Family Orders and Agreements Enforcement Assistance Act*

> This ensures the benefit remains accessible and protected from external financial claims.

---

### **7. Regulatory Authority (Section 7)**

The **Governor in Council** may, by regulation:
- Amend the definition of "lockdown order"
- Fix a **lower number of days** for the 14-day threshold (e.g., 10 days)
- Add **additional sources of income** to the eligibility list
- Add **additional income types** that are excluded from eligibility

> This allows flexibility in response to evolving public health situations.

---

### **8. Extension of the Final Deadline (Section 8)**

- The Minister may recommend, and the Governor in Council may regulate, extending the final eligibility deadline from **May 7, 2022** to **July 2, 2022**.
