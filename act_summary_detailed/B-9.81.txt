**Detailed Summary of the *Budget Implementation Act, 1997* (Canada Foundation for Innovation)**

---

### **Overview**
The *Budget Implementation Act, 1997* establishes the **Canada Foundation for Innovation (CFI)**, a non-profit, non-governmental entity created to support and enhance Canada’s capacity for high-quality research by providing grants to eligible institutions. The Act outlines the structure, governance, funding mechanisms, operational rules, and accountability framework of the foundation. It is a key piece of legislation in Canada’s strategy to strengthen scientific research infrastructure and innovation across the country.

---

## **1. Purpose and Objectives of the Foundation**
- **Primary Objective**: To make grants to eligible recipients for eligible projects that advance research infrastructure in Canada.
- **Scope of Support**: Grants are intended to fund:
  - Modernization, acquisition, development, operation, or maintenance of **research infrastructure** within Canada.
  - Access to **world-leading research facilities outside Canada** or participation in **major international collaborative research projects** (including contributions toward capital costs).
- The foundation aims to increase Canada’s overall capability in conducting high-quality research in key fields:
  - Science
  - Health
  - Engineering
  - Environment

> The foundation is **not an agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada**, and is not considered part of the federal public administration.

## **3. Legal and Structural Framework**
### **Corporate Status and Legal Powers**
- The foundation is a **corporation without share capital**, established by this Act.
- It has **the capacity and rights of a natural person**, subject to the provisions of this Act.
- The **Canada Business Corporations Act (CBCA)** applies to the foundation and its directors, members, officers, and employees, **with modifications** to fit the foundation’s unique structure.
- The **Canada Not-for-profit Corporations Act does not apply** — confirming its distinct legal status.

### **Applicable CBCA Provisions (with modifications)**
The Act incorporates key CBCA sections, including:
- **Section 16**: Powers of the foundation (no by-law required to confer powers; restrictions on powers).
- **Section 21(1)**: Right of members and creditors to access foundation records.
- **Section 23**: Corporate seal not required to validate instruments.
- **Sections 103–105**: Authority to make and amend by-laws; qualifications of directors; resignation.
- **Section 114**: Place of meetings and quorum requirements.
- **Section 116**: Validity of acts of directors and officers.
- **Section 120**: Conflict of interest rules.
- **Section 122(1)**: Duty of care for directors and officers.
- **Section 124**: Indemnification and insurance for directors.
- **Section 155–159**: Financial reporting and disclosure obligations.
- **Section 171**: Duties of audit committee and penalties for non-compliance.
- **Section 257(1)–(2)**: Certificates issued by the foundation are evidence in court.

> These provisions ensure transparency, accountability, and governance while allowing flexibility in operations.

## **4. Governance Structure**

### **Board of Directors**
The board supervises the foundation’s management and exercises its powers, subject to by-laws.

#### **Composition (Total: 13 Directors)**
1. **Chairperson** – Appointed by Governor in Council on recommendation of the Minister.
2. **Five Directors** – Appointed by Governor in Council on Minister’s recommendation.
3. **Seven Directors** – Appointed by the members of the foundation, in accordance with by-laws.

> **Exclusions**: Directors appointed under paragraph (c) must be:
> - Resident in Canada
> - Not employees or agents of Her Majesty (federal or provincial)
> - Not members of Parliament or provincial legislatures

#### **Appointment Process**
- The Chairperson and directors (if any) appointed under (b) serve **provisionally** until the seven members under (c) are appointed.
- During this transitional period, the board may:
  - Organize the foundation
  - Make banking arrangements
  - Enact organizational by-laws
  - Receive funds
- **But**: The board **cannot make grants, enter into agreements, or review grant applications** during this time.

> This ensures that only fully constituted and properly governed leadership can initiate grant decisions.

#### **Term of Office**
- All directors serve **three-year terms**, unless removed earlier.
- Directors may be **reappointed** for additional terms.
- Directors cease to serve upon:
  - Death
  - Resignation
  - Ceasing to reside in Canada
  - Disqualification under CBCA (e.g., conflict of interest)
  - Appointment to Senate or House of Commons
  - Becoming an employee or agent of federal or provincial government

#### **Diversity and Representation**
- The board must ensure **approximate equal representation**:
  - **Half** from the research community
  - **Half** from the business or non-profit sector
- Directors should also reflect **regional diversity** across Canada.

---

## **5. Membership Structure**

### **Total Members: 15**
- **Initial Appointment**: Governor in Council appoints **six members** on recommendation of the Minister.
- **Next Phase**: At a meeting (or soon after), these six members appoint **nine additional members**.
- Subsequent appointments are made at **general meetings** of members.

### **Term of Office**
- Members serve **five-year terms**, but may be removed by a **special resolution** (2/3 majority).
- Terms are staggered:
  - One-third serve **5 years**
  - One-third serve **4 years**
  - One-third serve **3 years**
- Members may be **reappointed**.

### **Vacancies**
- A member appointed to fill a vacancy serves for the **unexpired portion** of the original term.

### **Eligibility Requirements**
A person is **not eligible** to be a member if:
- A member of Parliament or provincial legislature
- An employee or agent of Her Majesty (federal or provincial)
- A director of the foundation
- Does not reside in Canada
- Fails to meet CBCA qualifications (e.g., no conflict of interest)

### **Diversity and Representation**
- At all times, approximately **half** of members should represent research institutions and **half** represent business or non-profit organizations.
- Members should be drawn from **various regions** of Canada.

### **Remuneration**
- Members are **not paid** for their service.
- They may be **reimbursed** for reasonable out-of-pocket expenses (e.g., travel, meeting costs).

> Unlike directors, members do **not profit** from the foundation or its activities.

---

## **6. Officers, Employees, and Agents**
- The board may appoint **officers, employees, and agents** as needed.
- Directors and members **cannot be employed** by the foundation.
- The foundation’s personnel are **not part of the federal public administration**.
- The foundation may pay:
  - Salaries and wages
  - Rent
  - Director and agent remuneration
  - Reimbursement for expenses

---

## **7. Funding and Financial Operations**

### **Funding Sources**
- Grants to eligible recipients (for eligible projects)
- Donations of money (subject to conditions)
- Investment income from funds

### **Donations**
- The foundation may accept donations of money.
- **Conditions on donations**:
  - **Not allowed** if the donation restricts use to a purpose outside the foundation’s objectives.
  - **Permitted** if the condition only restricts **investment** until funds are used for grants.
- All donations and investment income must be used **exclusively** for the foundation’s purposes.
