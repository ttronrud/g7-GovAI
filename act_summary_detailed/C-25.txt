**Detailed Summary of the *Cape Breton Development Corporation Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *Cape Breton Development Corporation Act* establishes the legal framework for the **Cape Breton Development Corporation (CBDC)**, a public corporation created to manage and operate coal mining activities in the Sydney coal field of Nova Scotia. The Act outlines the structure, governance, powers, financial provisions, and operational objectives of the Corporation. However, it is important to note that **significant portions of the Act have been repealed**, most notably in **2000** (via *C. 23, s. 6* and *s. 11*) and further in subsequent amendments, particularly regarding the **Industrial Development Division**, **Coal Division**, and **pension and funding provisions**.

This summary focuses on the **remaining substantive content** of the Act as it stands in its current form, with clear annotations on what has been repealed or superseded.

---

## **1. Definitions and Key Terms**

- **Board**: Refers to the Board of Directors of the Corporation.  
- **Chairperson**: The Chairperson of the Board. *(Note: The term "Chairman" was repealed in 2000.)*  
- **President**: The chief executive officer of the Corporation.  
- **Corporation**: The Cape Breton Development Corporation established under Section 3.  
- **Minister**: A member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada designated by the Governor in Council to oversee the Corporation.  
- **Governor in Council**: The federal government (via the Cabinet), responsible for appointing key officials and approving major decisions.

> ⚠️ *Note: Several terms such as "Coal Division," "companies," and "Industrial Development Division" have been formally repealed and are no longer in force.*

---

## **2. Structure and Governance of the Corporation**

### **Board Composition**
- The Board consists of:
  - A **Chairperson**
  - A **President**
  - Up to **five additional directors** (maximum)

### **Appointment Process**
- **Chairperson and President**:
  - Appointed by the **Governor in Council**.
  - Hold office at the **Governor’s pleasure** (i.e., for a term deemed appropriate).
- **Other Directors**:
  - Appointed by the **Minister**, with **approval from the Governor in Council**.
  - Serve for a maximum term of **four years**.

### **Re-appointment Rules**
- The **President** may be reappointed after completing a term.
- Any **other director who has served two consecutive terms** is **ineligible for appointment** (except as President) for **twelve months** following the end of their second term.

### **Presiding Authority**
- The **Chairperson presides** at Board meetings.
- In the absence or incapacity of the Chairperson, or if the office is vacant, the **President presides**.

---

## **3. Powers and Authority of the President**

- The **President is the chief executive officer** of the Corporation.
- Has **full authority** to manage and conduct business of the Corporation **except where specifically reserved** by this Act or the Corporation’s by-laws for the Board.
- This includes making decisions on operations, contracts, and day-to-day management.

### **Succession in Case of Vacancy**
- If the President is absent or incapacitated, or if the office is vacant:
  - The **Board may authorize an officer or director** to act as President temporarily.
  - Such interim authority is **limited to 60 days**.
  - **Extension beyond 60 days requires approval from the Governor in Council**.

---

## **4. Compensation and Expenses**

- **President**:
  - Paid a **salary** set by the Governor in Council.
- **Chairperson and other directors (excluding President)**:
  - Paid **fees for attending meetings** (Board or committees), fixed by the Governor in Council.
- **All directors**:
  - Entitled to reimbursement for **travel and living expenses** incurred while performing duties — these are to be fixed by **Corporation by-law**.

> ⚠️ *Note: The original provision for travel and living expenses was repealed in 2000 (s. 11), so this may not currently be enforceable unless reinstated.*

---

## **5. Employment of Personnel**

- The Corporation may employ:
  - Officers, employees, and technical/professional advisers as needed.
- These employees are paid:
  - **Remuneration** fixed by the Board.
  - On **terms and conditions** established by by-law of the Corporation.

> ⚠️ *Note: This provision was repealed in 2000 (s. 11), so its current applicability is uncertain.*

---

## **6. Core Objectives of the Corporation**

The primary purpose of the CBDC is to:
> "Conduct coal mining and related operations in the Sydney coal-field on a basis that is consistent with efficient mining practice and good mine safety."

### **Powers to Carry Out Objectives**
The Corporation may:
1. **Rehabilitate, improve, open, operate, and maintain**:
   - Coal mines
   - Related fixtures, machinery, equipment, or property
2. **Enter into contracts** with:
   - Individuals or entities (domestic or foreign)
   - The federal government (Her Majesty in right of Canada)
   - Provinces or their agents
   - Relating to:
     - Production of coal
     - Marketing and shipping of coal
3. **Do all other things** incidental or conducive to achieving its objectives.

> ⚠️ *Note: The original coal mining focus has been largely abandoned due to the closure of the Sydney coalfield and the repeal of key sections (2000). The Corporation no longer operates as a coal producer.*

---

## **7. Pension Arrangements**

- The Corporation must establish, manage, and administer **pension plans** for:
  - Employees
  - Dependents of employees
- The plan must cover:
  - Contributions by the Corporation
  - Investment of pension fund money

> ⚠️ *This provision was originally included but may have been superseded by later legislation or regulatory changes.*

---

## **8. Financial Support (Working Capital Advances)**

- The **Minister of Finance** may authorize **advances** from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund** to the Corporation.
- These advances are:
  - For **working capital needs**
  - Subject to terms agreed upon
- **Maximum total outstanding advances**:
  - Initially capped at **$50 million**
  - Can be adjusted by **Parliament via appropriation Act**

> ⚠️ *This provision was repealed in 2000 (s. 12), so it is no longer active.*

---

## **9. Head Office and Meetings**

- The **head office** of the Corporation must be located on the **Island of Cape Breton, Nova Scotia**.
- Board meetings may be held **anywhere in Canada**, at the discretion of the directors.

---

## **10. By-Laws**

The Board may make by-laws covering:
- Calling and conducting meetings (including committee formation)
- Quorums and delegation of duties
- Travel and living expenses for directors
- Duties, conduct, and employment terms for directors, officers, and employees
- Pension fund administration (contributions and investments)
- General management of the Corporation’s affairs

> ⚠️ *Note: These by-laws are essential for operational governance but may have been revised or replaced in practice.*

---

## **11. Legal Status and Relationship with Federal Government**

### **Agent of Her Majesty in Right of Canada**
- For all purposes under this Act, the Corporation is considered an **agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada**.
- This status grants it certain legal privileges and responsibilities.

### **Employees Not Considered Federal Officers or Servants**
- Employees of the Corporation are **not officers or servants** of Her Majesty.
- However, for **regulatory purposes** (e.g., under the *Aeronautics Act*), officers and employees are **deemed to be in federal public administration**.

> ✅ This provision ensures that the Corporation operates under federal oversight but maintains a distinct administrative identity.

---

## **12. Tax Grants to Municipalities**

- The Corporation may make **grants in lieu of taxes** to municipalities on Cape Breton Island.
- Grants are **limited to the amount** that would otherwise have been levied on the Corporation if it were not an agent of the Crown.

> ⚠️ *This provision is likely inactive today due to the Corporation’s non-operational status.*

---

## **13. Funding and Expenditure**

- All expenditures not otherwise provided for in this Act must be funded by **Parliamentary appropriation**.

---

## **14. Repeal and Transitional Provisions**

### **General Repeal**
- The entire Act, or any of its provisions, may be **repealed** by order of the Governor in Council on a date or dates to be fixed.

> ✅ This gives the federal government the authority to formally end the Act when it is no longer needed.

---

## **15. Continuation of the Industrial Development Division (Key Transitional Provision)**

A critical and unique provision in the Act (Section 30):

> The Act treats the **Industrial Development Division (IDC)** of the CBDC as a **separate entity** — a "continued corporation" — which continues under the name **Enterprise Cape Breton Corporation** ("new corporation").
