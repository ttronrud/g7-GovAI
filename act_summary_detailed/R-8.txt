**Detailed Summary of the Revolving Funds Act**

---

### **Overview**
The *Revolving Funds Act* is a legislative instrument that authorizes specific Ministers of the Government of Canada to make expenditures from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund** (CRF) for designated purposes. These expenditures are governed by strict financial limits based on the revenues generated from those same purposes‚Äîeffectively creating a "revolving fund" model where spending is capped at a fixed multiple of incoming revenues. The Act also includes provisions for amendments, repeals, and oversight by the Treasury Board.

Importantly, **many of the provisions in this Act have been repealed or replaced over time**, particularly through subsequent legislation. This summary focuses on the **original content and structure** of the Act as presented, noting where repeals occurred and their implications.

---

## **Key Provisions by Minister and Purpose**

---

### **1. Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food**

#### **Purposes of Expenditure:**
- Race track supervision in Canada  
- Reimbursement of race associations for supervisory services provided under regulations made under **section 204 of the Criminal Code**  
- Research into:
  - The use of drugs on horses  
  - Race surveillance techniques  

#### **Spending Authority:**
- May spend any revenue received in respect of the above purposes, including revenue from section 204(4) of the Criminal Code.

#### **Financial Limitation:**
- The total expenditures under this section **shall not exceed by more than $2 million** the total revenues received for these purposes.

> **Note:** This section was **repealed in 2002** (c. 5, Sch. 1, votes 111b and 112b) by the *Public Safety and National Security Act*.  
> ‚Üí **Current Status: Repealed.** No longer in force.

---

### **2. Minister of Citizenship and Immigration**

#### **Purpose of Expenditure:**
- Providing passport and other travel document services in Canada and at Canadian posts abroad.

#### **Spending Authority:**
- May spend any revenue received in respect of this purpose.

#### **Financial Limitation:**
- The aggregate expenditures must not exceed **$1.00 (one cent)** more than the revenues received for this purpose.

> This is an extremely tight limit‚Äîeffectively requiring that expenditures be equal to or slightly below revenues, with no meaningful surplus allowed.

> **Note:** This section was **repealed in 2002** (c. 5, Sch. 1, votes 111b and 112b).  
> ‚Üí **Current Status: Repealed.** No longer in force.

### **3. Minister of Public Works and Government Services (PWGSC)**

#### **Provision 1 (Repealed) ‚Äì Previously Authorized Purposes:**
- Expenditures for:
  - Paragraphs 6(e) or (h) of the *Department of Public Works and Government Services Act*
  - Paragraphs 15(f) or (g) of section 10 of that Act
  - Section 24 of the same Act  
- Spending power: May spend any revenue received for these purposes or others listed.
- Financial limit: Expenditures must not exceed **$150 million** above revenues.

> **Status:**  
> This section was **repealed in multiple stages**:
> - 2013 (c. 4, Sch. 1, vote 6c)  
> - 2012 (c. 3, Sch. 1, votes 21c and 7c)  
> - 1998 (c. 2, Sch., vote 18b)  
> ‚Üí **Current Status: Repealed.**

#### **Provision 2 (Active at Time of Text) ‚Äì Current PWGSC Powers**

> **Purpose:**  
> Expenditures for:
> - Paragraphs 6(a) and (b) of the *Department of Public Works and Government Services Act*  
> - Section 6 of the *Surplus Crown Assets Act*

> **Spending Authority:**  
> May spend any revenue received in respect of these purposes.

> **Financial Limitation:**  
> Expenditures must not exceed **$45 million** more than the revenues received for these purposes.

> **Additional Provision:**

> ‚Üí This means that the Minister‚Äôs power to make expenditures under this section **includes** the ability to fund purposes under section 16 of the PWGSC Act, even if not explicitly listed.

> **Status:**  
> This provision **remains in force** as of the text provided, though it may be subject to future repeal or amendment.

### **4. Secretary of State of Canada**

#### **Purpose of Expenditure:**
- Operation of the **National Film Board of Canada (NFB)**

#### **Spending Authority:**
- May spend any revenue received in respect of the NFB‚Äôs operations.

#### **Financial Limitation:**
- Total expenditures must not exceed **$25 million** more than the revenues received for the purpose.

> **Note:** This section was **repealed in multiple stages**:

## **General Provisions**

### **1. Treasury Board Oversight**
> All expenditures made under sections 2 to 10 and all revenues spent under those sections are subject to **terms and conditions specified by the Treasury Board**.  
> ‚Üí This gives the Treasury Board broad authority to impose administrative, procedural, or performance-based requirements on how funds are used.

### **2. Amendments and Repeals**
> The provisions of this Act **may be amended or repealed by an appropriation Act**.  
> ‚Üí This means that Parliament can modify or remove any of the powers outlined in the Act through ordinary legislative process (e.g., through annual appropriations or budget bills).

## **Key Themes and Implications**

| Theme | Explanation |
|------|-------------|
| **Revolving Fund Model** | The Act enables a "revolving" mechanism where funds are spent and then replenished, as long as expenditures do not exceed a defined multiple of revenues. This allows flexibility without requiring new appropriations each time. |
| **Strict Financial Caps** | Most provisions impose very tight limits (e.g., $1.00 over revenue, $2M, $45M, $25M), ensuring that spending remains closely aligned with income and minimizing fiscal risk. |
| **Purpose-Specific Authority** | Each Minister has clear, narrowly defined powers tied to specific government functions (e.g., agriculture, immigration, film). |
| **Oversight by Treasury Board** | Ensures accountability and transparency in how funds are used. |
| **High Degree of Repeal** | The Act contains **multiple repealed sections**, indicating that many of these revolving fund powers were deemed obsolete or redundant over time due to changes in government structure, legislation, or policy priorities. |

## **Conclusion**

The *Revolving Funds Act* was designed to provide **flexible, revenue-linked funding mechanisms** for specific government departments and programs. It enabled Ministers to spend from the Consolidated Revenue Fund for purposes such as race track oversight, travel document services, public infrastructure, and film operations‚Äîsubject to strict financial caps and Treasury Board oversight.

However, **the Act has been largely repealed** since its enactment, with most of its provisions removed in the early 2000s through various legislative amendments and budgetary reforms. As a result:

> ‚úÖ **The Act is largely inactive today** and does not currently govern any major government expenditures.  
> ‚ùå **It is not a current source of funding authority** for any federal department.

Its historical significance lies in demonstrating how Canada has experimented with revolving fund models to provide operational flexibility in specific areas‚Äîthough such models have since been replaced by more traditional appropriation-based funding.
