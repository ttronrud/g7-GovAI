### **Detailed Summary of the *European Bank for Reconstruction and Development Agreement Act***  

---

#### **1. Introduction and Purpose of the Act**  
This Act, officially titled the *European Bank for Reconstruction and Development Agreement Act*, establishes legal authority in Canada to implement and support the **European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD)** under international treaty obligations. It is enacted by Her Majesty, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons of Canada, and serves as Canada’s legislative endorsement of the EBRD Agreement.

The primary objective of the Act is to **approve and operationalize Canada’s participation in the EBRD**, a multilateral financial institution created to promote economic reform, market-oriented development, and sustainable growth in Central and Eastern Europe (and later expanded to other regions).

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**  
- **Agreement**: Refers to the *Agreement Establishing the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development*, detailed in the Schedule.  
- **Bank**: The European Bank for Reconstruction and Development.  
- **Minister**: The Minister of Finance of Canada.  
- **Governor in Council**: The Cabinet of Canada, responsible for executing the Bank’s functions on behalf of Canada.

---

#### **3. Approval and Implementation of the Agreement**  
- The **Agreement is hereby approved** by Canadian law.  
- The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal government) has broad authority to:  
  - Authorize Canada’s acceptance of the Agreement;  
  - Make appointments, issue orders, and create regulations necessary to fulfill Canada’s obligations under the Agreement;  
  - Grant the **privileges and immunities** specified in the Agreement.  
- The Governor in Council may **amend the Schedule** (which contains the text of the Agreement) to reflect updates or modifications to the original Agreement.

---

#### **4. Financial Arrangements for Canada**  
- **Initial Subscription**:  
  - Canada may contribute up to **$120 million USD** (in U.S. dollars) to the Bank’s initial capital stock.  
  - This amount must be paid from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund**.  
- **Additional Financial Assistance**: The Minister may provide financial support through:  
  - Direct payments;  
  - Non-interest-bearing, non-negotiable demand notes;  
  - Purchase of shares on behalf of Canada;  
  - Issuance of guarantees;  
  - Any other method deemed appropriate by the Minister.  
- **Budgetary Limitations**:  
  - Payments under these provisions are capped by annual appropriations approved by Parliament.  
  - The total amount paid in any given period cannot exceed the amount specified in a parliamentary appropriation.

---

#### **5. Reporting Requirements**  
- The **Minister must lay before each House of Parliament** (Senate and House of Commons) a **report on the Bank’s operations** within one year after the end of each fiscal year.  
- If either House is not sitting, the report must be laid on one of the first five days when that House resumes sessions.  
- The report must include:  
  - A general summary of actions taken under this Act;  
  - An assessment of the **sustainable development** aspects (as defined in Article 2 of the Agreement);  
  - An analysis of the **human rights** implications of the Bank’s activities.

---

#### **6. Core Objectives of the EBRD (as outlined in the Agreement)**  
The EBRD is established to:  
- **Foster transition to open, market-oriented economies** in Central and Eastern Europe;  
- **Promote private and entrepreneurial initiative**;  
- Support **democratic governance**, the **rule of law**, and **human rights**.  

> **Expanded Scope**:  
> The term “Central and Eastern European countries” now includes:  
> - **Mongolia**;  
> - Member countries of the **Southern and Eastern Mediterranean** (e.g., Egypt, Jordan, Tunisia, etc.), **if approved by a two-thirds majority of Governors representing at least three-fourths of total voting power**.

---

#### **7. Operational Functions of the Bank**  
The Bank shall assist recipient countries in achieving economic transformation through:  

| Function | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Structural & Sectoral Reforms** | Support for: <br> • Demonopolization <br> • Decentralization <br> • Privatization |
| **Private Sector Development** | Encourage investment in: <br> • Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) <br> • Competitive, productive private sector activity |
| **Capital Mobilization** | Attract domestic and foreign capital and experienced management to support private investment |
| **Infrastructure Investment** | Invest in service and financial sectors and infrastructure where needed to support private enterprise |
| **Technical Assistance** | Provide support for project planning, financing, and implementation |
| **Capital Market Development** | Stimulate development of capital markets |
| **Cross-Border Projects** | Support sound, viable projects involving multiple recipient countries |
| **Sustainable & Environmental Development** | Promote environmentally sound and sustainable development practices |

---

#### **8. Cooperation with International Bodies**  
The Bank shall work closely with:  
- **International Monetary Fund (IMF)**  
- **World Bank (IBRD)**  
- **International Finance Corporation (IFC)**  
- **Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA)**  
- **OECD**  
- **United Nations and its specialized agencies**  
- Any public or private entity involved in economic development or investment in Central and Eastern Europe  

This ensures alignment with global standards and promotes coordinated policy implementation.

---

#### **9. Membership Criteria**  
Membership is open to:  
1. **European countries**  
2. **Non-European countries** that are members of the **IMF**  
3. **The European Economic Community (EEC)** and the **European Investment Bank (EIB)**  

> **Admission of Non-EEC Countries**:  
> Countries not joining under Article 61 may be admitted **only with a two-thirds majority vote of Governors representing at least three-fourths of total voting power**.

> **Protection of EEC/EIB Interests**:  
> No admission shall reduce the **majority share** of capital held by EEC members and the EIB below a majority of the total subscribed capital.

---

#### **10. Capital Structure of the Bank**  
- **Authorized Capital**: 10 billion ECU (European Currency Unit), divided into **1 million shares** of **10,000 ECU each**.  
- **Paid-in Shares**: Initial aggregate value of **3 billion ECU** (30% of total capital).  
- **Callable Shares**: The remainder (70%) are callable — meaning they can be called upon by the Bank when needed.  

> **Subscription Rules**:  
> - Each member subscribes in a **3:7 ratio of paid-in to callable shares**.  
> - **Minimum subscription**: 100 shares per member.  
> - **Initial allocation** for signatories is set in **Annex A**.  
> - Subscriptions are **non-transferable** except to the Bank itself (as per Chapter VII of the Agreement).  

> **Share Issuance**:  
> - Shares are issued at **par value** unless a **two-thirds majority of Governors** (representing at least two-thirds of voting power) approves special terms.  

> **No Pledges or Encumbrances**: Shares cannot be pledged or used as collateral.  

---

#### **11. Payment and Denomination of Subscriptions**  
- **Payment Schedule**:  
  - For initial subscriptions: **5 equal instalments of 20% each**.  
  - First instalment due within **60 days** of the Agreement’s entry into force or ratification.  
  - Subsequent instalments due **one year after the previous one**.  
- **Payment in Currencies**:  
  - Payments may be made in **ECU, USD, or JPY**.  
  - The exchange rate used is the **average rate from 30 September 1989 to March 1990**.  
- **Promissory Notes**:  
  - Up to **50% of each instalment** may be paid via **non-negotiable, non-interest-bearing promissory notes** issued by the member.  
  - Notes are payable **on demand** at par value.  
  - The value of each note must reflect the **proportional share** of the member’s paid-in shares.  

> **Payment to Trustee**:  
> The first instalment is paid to the **European Investment Bank (EIB)** as **trustee** for the Bank until the inaugural Board meeting determines the final payment location.

---

#### **12. Ordinary Capital Resources (Primary Funding Source)**  
These are the core financial resources used for regular operations and include:  
1. Authorized capital stock (paid-in and callable shares);  
2. Borrowings authorized under Article 20(i);  
3. Repayments of loans or guarantees;  
4. Proceeds from disposal of equity investments;  
5. Income from loans, equity investments, and guarantees (excluding special operations);  
6. Any other funds not part of the **Special Funds** (Article 19).

> These resources are **used exclusively** to implement the Bank’s purpose and functions under Articles 1 and 2.

---

#### **13. Special Funds (for Specific Projects)**  
- Separate from ordinary capital resources.  
- Used only for **special operations** (e.g., high-risk projects, transitional support).  
- Financial statements must **separately report** ordinary and special operations.
