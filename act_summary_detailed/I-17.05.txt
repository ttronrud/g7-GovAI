### ðŸ”¹ **5. Approval Requirements for Construction or Alteration**
#### Core Principle:
> **No person may construct or alter an international bridge or tunnel without prior approval from the Governor in Council.**

#### Process:
1. An **application must be submitted to the Minister** for approval by the Governor in Council.
2. The Minister may, if necessary, **consult** with:
   - Other levels of government (provincial, territorial) with jurisdiction over the site.
   - Any person with a **direct interest** in the project.
3. Applicants must provide:
   - All documents and information required under **Ministerial guidelines**.
   - Any additional information requested after submission.
4. **Guidelines are not statutory instruments** under the *Statutory Instruments Act* â€” they are administrative tools, not subject to parliamentary scrutiny.

#### Governor in Council Authority:
- May **approve** the project **subject to terms and conditions**.
- May **vary, rescind, or impose new conditions** at any time.
- All parties subject to conditions must **comply fully**.

#### Enforcement Powers (if Unauthorized Construction Occurs):
If construction or alteration occurs without approval:
- The Minister may:
  - Order the person to **cease work**.
  - Order the **owner to remove or alter** the structure.
  - If the owner fails to comply, the Minister may **remove, destroy, and dispose of** the bridge/tunnel and its materials.
- **Costs incurred** by the Minister (after deducting proceeds from disposal) are **recoverable as a debt due to Her Majesty in right of Canada**.

> This creates a strong **enforcement mechanism** to prevent unauthorized development.

### ðŸ”¹ **6. Offences and Penalties**
#### Offences Covered:
- Contravention of **Section 6** (construction/alteration without approval).
- Failure to comply with a Ministerial order under **Section 9**.
- Violations of **Section 23 or 25** (ownership/control without approval).

#### Penalties:
| Conviction Type | Individual | Corporation |
|----------------|----------|-------------|
| **On indictment** | Fine up to $500,000 or imprisonment up to 2 years (or both) | Same |
| **Summary conviction** | Fine up to $200,000 or imprisonment up to 6 months (or both) | Same |

> **Multiple offences**: If the violation occurs over multiple days, a separate offence is created for each day.

> **Corporate liability**: Officers, directors, agents, or mandataries of a corporation who **directed, authorized, or participated** in the offence are personally liable â€” even if the corporation is not prosecuted.

#### Forfeiture of Property:
- On conviction for an offence under Section 10, the **bridge/tunnel or its components** may be **forfeited to Her Majesty**.
- **Right of appeal**:
  - Any person with a **right or interest** in the property may apply within **30 days** to a superior court judge.
  - Judge must hold a hearing **at least 30 days after filing**.
  - Applicant must serve the Minister with notice at least **15 days before** the hearing.
- **Entitlement to relief**:
  - If the judge finds the applicant is **innocent** of complicity and **exercised reasonable care** to prevent unauthorized use, the applicant may obtain a declaration that their right is **not affected**.
  - The judge must **declare the nature and extent** of the interest.
- **Appeal**:
  - An appeal lies to the **court of appeal** in the province where the forfeiture occurred.
- **Outcome if no appeal or refusal**:
  - The property is disposed of as directed by the Minister.

> This provides a **fair and structured process** for affected parties to protect legitimate interests.
