**Detailed Summary of the *Privacy Act* of Canada**

---

### **Overview**

The *Privacy Act* is a foundational piece of Canadian legislation designed to protect the **privacy of individuals** with respect to **personal information held by government institutions**. Its primary purpose is to:

- Extend and strengthen existing privacy protections in Canada;
- Ensure that individuals have a **right of access** to personal information held about them by federal government institutions;
- Establish clear rules governing how personal information is collected, used, disclosed, retained, and disposed of;
- Provide accountability mechanisms through oversight by the **Privacy Commissioner**.

This Act applies specifically to **federal government institutions**, including departments, ministries, Crown corporations, and their subsidiaries.

---

## **Key Definitions and Key Provisions**

### **1. Definitions (Core Terms)**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Government Institution** | Includes any department or ministry of the Government of Canada, any body or office listed in the Schedule, and any parent Crown corporation or its wholly-owned subsidiary (as defined under section 83 of the Financial Administration Act). |
| **Head of Institution** | For departments/ministries: the member of the Queen’s Privy Council who presides over it. For others: either a person designated under subsection 3.1(2), or if none, the chief executive officer. |
| **Personal Information** | Information about an identifiable individual recorded in any form. Includes: race, religion, age, medical/employment history, identifying numbers, addresses, fingerprints, opinions (with exclusions), and correspondence. <br> **Exclusions**: Information about former employees (e.g., salary, title, job function), contract workers, discretionary benefits, or individuals deceased over 20 years. |
| **Personal Information Bank** | A collection or grouping of personal information used for administrative purposes. |
| **Sensory Disability** | A disability affecting sight or hearing. |
| **Administrative Purpose** | Use of personal information in a decision-making process that directly affects the individual (e.g., hiring, benefits, eligibility). |
| **Alternative Format** | A format (e.g., audio or braille) enabling individuals with sensory disabilities to access personal information. |

> **Note**: The Act applies to parent Crown corporations (e.g., Canadian Race Relations Foundation, Public Sector Pension Investment Board) and their subsidiaries.

> **Paragraph (j.1)** of personal information only applies to records created *on or after* the date it comes into force.

---

### **2. Principles Governing Collection and Use of Personal Information**

#### **(a) Collection Must Be Relevant and Direct**
> *No personal information shall be collected unless it relates directly to an operating program or activity of the institution.*

- Collection must have a legitimate and direct connection to a government function.

#### **(b) Direct Collection from the Individual**
> *A government institution shall, wherever possible, collect personal information directly from the individual.*

- The individual should be the source of the information.
- Exceptions:
  - Where the individual gives **prior consent**;
  - Where information is disclosed to the institution under **subsection 8(2)** (e.g., through third-party disclosure).

#### **(c) Purpose Disclosure Requirement**
> *The institution must inform the individual of the purpose for which the information is being collected.*

- Transparency is required — individuals must know why their data is being gathered.

#### **(d) Exceptions to Disclosure Rules**
> Compliance is not required if:
- It would result in the collection of **inaccurate information**, or
- It would **defeat the purpose** or **prejudice the use** for which the information is collected.

---

### **3. Retention and Disposal of Personal Information**

> *Personal information used for administrative purposes must be retained for a period prescribed by regulation to ensure the individual has a reasonable opportunity to access it.*

- The retention period must allow sufficient time for access requests.
- After this period, information must be **disposed of** in accordance with:
  - Regulations;
  - Directives or guidelines issued by the **designated Minister**.

---

### **4. Use and Disclosure Restrictions**

> Personal information **may not be used or disclosed** without the individual’s consent **except** for:
- The original purpose for which it was collected;
- A use **consistent with that purpose**;
- Specific exceptions listed below.

#### **Permitted Disclosures (with conditions)**

| Exception | Conditions |
|--------|-----------|
| **For original or consistent purpose** | Must align with the purpose of collection. |
| **Under statutory authority** | As permitted by another Act of Parliament or regulation. |
| **Compliance with court orders** | Subpoena, warrant, or court rule requiring production. |
| **To Attorney General** | For legal proceedings involving the Crown. |
| **To investigative bodies** | On written request, specifying purpose and information to be disclosed; must be for law enforcement or investigation. |
| **To foreign governments or international bodies** | Under formal agreements between Canada and such entities. |
| **To provincial governments** | For administrative or enforcement purposes. |
| **To First Nations or Aboriginal governments** | For research, validation of claims, grievances, or disputes. |
| **To locate individuals** | To collect debts owed to Her Majesty or make payments due. |
| **Public interest override** | When public interest clearly outweighs privacy concerns. |
| **Benefit to individual** | When disclosure clearly benefits the individual. |

> **Note**: In all cases where disclosure is made, the institution must ensure **no unreasonable invasion of privacy** occurs.

---

### **5. Special Provisions for Aboriginal Peoples**

The Act recognizes the unique relationship between governments and Indigenous communities.

- **"Indian band"** includes bands under the *Indian Act*, Naskapi/Cree-Naskapi Commission Act, Shíshálh Nation, and First Nations named in Yukon schedules.
- **"Aboriginal government"** includes:
  - Nisga’a Government
  - Westbank First Nation Council
  - Tlicho, Nunatsiavut, Tla’amin, Tsawwassen, Cree Nation Governments
  - Whitecap Dakota Government
  - Participating First Nations (e.g., in BC or Anishinabek Nation)
- Personal information may be shared with these entities for:
  - Researching claims, disputes, or grievances;
  - Validating rights or historical records.

> These provisions support **self-determination** and **Indigenous rights** in accessing and using personal information.

---

### **6. Accountability and Transparency – The Index Requirement**

The **designated Minister** (a senior federal official) must **periodically publish a public index** of all personal information banks and classes of personal information.

#### **What the Index Must Include**
For each personal information bank:
- Identification and description of the bank;
- Registration number assigned by the Minister;
- Class of individuals the information relates to;
- Name and address of the institution that controls the bank;
- Statement of purposes for which information was obtained;
- Statement of consistent uses (i.e., how the information is used);
- Retention and disposal standards;
- Whether the bank is **exempt** under section 18 or 21/22 (e.g., due to national security or public interest).

For classes of personal information **not in a bank**:
- A detailed description enabling access;
- Contact information for requests.

> The index must be **published at least once per year** and made **available throughout Canada**.

> The Minister may **add additional uses** to the index if they are regularly occurring and not already listed.

> **Records of disclosures** must be retained for a period prescribed by regulation and made available to the **Privacy Commissioner** upon request.

> **Notification to Privacy Commissioner**: Any disclosure under paragraph 8(2)(m) must be reported **within a reasonable time** or **immediately**. The Commissioner may then notify the individual.

---

### **7. Right of Access to Personal Information**

Every Canadian citizen or permanent resident has a **right to access** personal information held by a government institution.

#### **Access Rights Include**:
- Access to **all personal information in a personal information bank** about them;
- Access to **any other personal information** under the institution’s control, provided the individual can provide **sufficiently specific details** (e.g., name, date, type of information).

> This right is fundamental and enforceable. Institutions must respond to access requests in a timely and transparent manner.

> **Note**: The right of access is not limited to information that is currently in use — it includes historical or archived data.

---

### **8. Use of Personal Information in Personal Information Banks**

> The head of a government institution must:
- **Include all personal information** that:
  - Has been used, is being used, or is available for use in an administrative purpose;
  - Is organized or intended to be retrieved by name or identifying number.
- **Maintain a record** of any use or disclosure **not included** in the official "statement of consistent uses" in the index.
- **Notify the Privacy Commissioner** immediately when such a use occurs.
- **Ensure the use is included** in the next statement of consistent uses.

> This ensures transparency and accountability in how information is used.

---

### **9. Exclusions and Exceptions**

- **Library and Archives of Canada (LAC)**:
  - Personal information transferred to LAC for **historical or archival purposes** is **exempt from some access and use restrictions**.
  - However, such information **may still be disclosed** to researchers or statistical bodies under strict conditions:
    - The purpose cannot reasonably be achieved without identifying the individual;
    - The recipient gives a **written undertaking** not to re-disclose the information in a way that identifies the individual
