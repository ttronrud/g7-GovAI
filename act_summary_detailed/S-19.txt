**Detailed Summary of the *Statistics Act***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *Statistics Act* is a foundational piece of Canadian legislation that establishes the legal framework for the collection, management, and dissemination of official statistics in Canada. It provides the mandate, structure, and operational guidelines for **Statistics Canada**, the national statistical agency, and ensures the integrity, confidentiality, and independence of the statistical system. The Act emphasizes **professionalism, transparency, data quality, and public trust** while balancing the need for accurate data with individual privacy rights.

---

## **Key Definitions and Terms**

1. **Chief Statistician**  
   - The head of Statistics Canada, appointed by the Governor in Council.  
   - Serves as the deputy head of the agency and is responsible for all statistical operations.  
   - Holds office for up to five years and may be reappointed.

2. **Department or Departments of Government**  
   - Includes federal, provincial, and territorial departments, boards, bureaus, or agencies.

3. **Identifying Information**  
   - Any data that can identify an individual, business, or organization (e.g., name, address, phone number, employee count).

4. **Minister**  
   - The designated member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada (typically the Minister of Statistics or Finance) responsible for overseeing the Act’s implementation.

5. **Respondent**  
   - Any individual, business, or organization that provides information or is subject to a request for data under the Act.

---

## **Core Mandates and Responsibilities of Statistics Canada**

### 1. **Primary Duties**
- Collect, compile, analyze, abstract, and publish statistical information on:
  - Commercial, industrial, financial, social, economic, and general activities and conditions of Canadians.
- Collaborate with government departments to avoid duplication in data collection and promote integrated national statistics.
- Conduct the **Census of Population** and the **Census of Agriculture** as required by the Act.
- Promote the development of **integrated social and economic statistics** for Canada and each province.

> This ensures comprehensive, reliable, and timely data to support policy-making, research, and public understanding.

---

## **Appointment and Tenure of the Chief Statistician**

- Appointed by the Governor in Council.
- Term: Up to **five years**, renewable once for another five-year term.
- Can be removed **only for cause**.
- In case of absence or incapacity, the Governor in Council may appoint an **interim officer** for up to **six months**.
- Interim appointee receives the same salary and benefits as the Chief Statistician.

> The independence of the Chief Statistician is protected, with strong emphasis on professional judgment.

---

## **Authority and Powers of the Chief Statistician**

The Chief Statistician has significant autonomy in statistical operations:

1. **Decision-Making Authority**  
   - Decides on all methods, procedures, content, timing, and dissemination of statistical programs and publications.
   - Must base decisions on **professional statistical standards**.

2. **Advisory Role**  
   - Advises government departments on statistical programs and collaborates with them to improve data collection and quality.

3. **Operational Control**  
   - Oversees all operations and staff of Statistics Canada.

4. **Rules and Instructions**  
   - May prescribe rules, instructions, and requests for information necessary for carrying out the Act’s functions.
   - These are **not** considered "regulations" under the *Statutory Instruments Act*, thus bypassing certain regulatory processes.

5. **Mandatory vs. Voluntary Requests**  
   - **Censuses** (population and agriculture) are **mandatory**.
   - All other requests are **voluntary**, unless otherwise specified.
   - Mandatory requests must be **published at least 30 days before implementation**.
   - The Minister must be notified of new mandatory requests at least 30 days in advance.

> This ensures transparency and public awareness of data collection activities.

---

## **Oaths and Confidentiality Obligations**

All employees, agents, and contractors performing duties under the Act must take an oath or affirmation:

> **Oath of Fidelity**  
> “I will faithfully and honestly fulfil my duties... and will not disclose any matter known to me by reason of my employment without due authority.”

- Applies to:
  - Employees of Statistics Canada.
  - Contractors performing special services.
  - Officers and employees of a corporation retained under contract.

> This creates a strong legal and ethical obligation to **protect confidentiality**.

---

## **Canadian Statistics Advisory Council (CSAC)**

Established to enhance the quality and accountability of the national statistical system.

### Functions:
- Advise the Minister and Chief Statistician on:
  - Relevance, accuracy, accessibility, and timeliness of national statistics.
- Publish an **annual public report** on the state of the national statistical system.

### Composition:
- Up to **10 members**, appointed by the Governor in Council.
- Includes:
  - Chief Statistician (ex officio).
  - One **Chairperson** (5-year term, renewable for 3 years).
  - Other members (3-year terms, renewable for 3 years).
- Members are paid remuneration and reasonable travel/living expenses.

> The CSAC promotes **transparency, accountability, and public trust** in statistical outputs.

---

## **Inter-Governmental Cooperation**

### 1. **Provincial Agreements**
- The Minister may enter into agreements with provincial governments to:
  - Enable provincial officers to carry out duties under the Act.
  - Allow provincial departments to collect required statistical information.
  - Permit provincial agencies to supply data to Statistics Canada.

> Provincial officers acting under such agreements are **deemed employed** under the Act.

### 2. **Data Exchange Agreements**
- Statistics Canada may enter into agreements with provincial agencies to:
  - Exchange replies to specific inquiries.
  - Share tabulations and analyses based on collected data.
- Conditions for eligibility:
  - The provincial agency must have **statutory authority** to collect the data and impose penalties for refusal or falsification.
  - The agency must be **prohibited from disclosing** information that Statistics Canada cannot disclose (to protect privacy).
  - Penalties must apply to disclosures of such information.

> **Limitation**: Agreements do not apply to data collected **before** the agreement is signed or is effective (whichever is later).

> **Notification Requirement**: When collecting data, Statistics Canada must **advise respondents** of any provincial agencies to which the data may be shared.

---

## **Municipal and Departmental Agreements**

- The Minister may enter into agreements with **departments, municipalities, or corporations** to:
  - Share information collected jointly.
  - Conduct joint tabulations or publications.

> Requirements:
- Respondents must be **informed in writing** that their data will be shared.
- If a respondent **objects in writing**, the data will **not be shared** unless the receiving entity has legal authority to require the information.

> Shared data may include:
- Original responses to inquiries.
- Supplementary information provided by respondents.

---

## **Access to Records and Information**

Any person in charge of records that could assist in fulfilling a data collection request must:
- Grant access to authorized personnel from Statistics Canada.

> This ensures data collection is efficient and complete.

---

## **Legal Validity of Communications**

- A letter signed by the Chief Statistician or an authorized representative:
  - Is **evidence of appointment, removal, or instructions**.
  - Is presumed valid and binding.

- A request for information presented by an authorized officer:
  - Is **presumed to be authorized** by proper authority, unless proven otherwise.

> This streamlines administrative processes and reduces legal challenges.

---

## **Remuneration and Payment**

- The Minister (subject to Governor in Council approval) prepares **tables of remuneration** for:
  - Commissioners, enumerators, and other staff.
  - Payment may be fixed, per diem, or based on a fee scale.
- **Full payment is only made** after the service is **faithfully and entirely performed**.

> Ensures accountability and performance-based compensation.

---

## **Confidentiality and Disclosure Restrictions**

### **General Rule (Section 18):**
- **No unauthorized disclosure** of returns or identifying information.
- Applies to **non-employees** who are not sworn under Section 6.
- Sworn individuals (employees or contractors) **cannot disclose** identifying information in a way that links it to a specific person, business, or organization.

> This protects individual privacy and prevents misuse of sensitive data.

### **Permitted Disclosure (Section 17)**  
The Chief Statistician may authorize disclosure of information under specific conditions:

1. **Pre-1971 Data**  
   - Information shared before May 1, 1971, may be disclosed if:
     - It was previously subject to confidentiality.
     - The original collector and Chief Statistician agree on disclosure terms.

2. **Consent-Based Disclosure**  
   - Disclosure is allowed if **written consent** is obtained from:
     - The individual or organization.
     - The owner of a business.

3. **Publicly Available Information**  
   - Information already accessible under law (e.g., public records) may be disclosed.

4. **Non-Identifiable Information**  
   - Information about:
     - Hospitals, mental institutions, libraries, schools, welfare facilities — **only if it cannot be linked to individuals**.
