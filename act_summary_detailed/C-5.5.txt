**Detailed Summary of the *Canada Foundation for Sustainable Development Technology Act***

---

### **Overview of the Act**
The *Canada Foundation for Sustainable Development Technology Act* establishes the **Canada Foundation for Sustainable Development Technology (the "Foundation")** as a not-for-profit entity dedicated to advancing sustainable development through the funding and support of innovative technologies. The Act provides a comprehensive legal framework for the Foundation’s structure, governance, funding mechanisms, operational powers, and accountability.

This legislation was enacted by Her Majesty in right of Canada, with the advice and consent of both the Senate and the House of Commons, and it replaces the previous corporate structure of the Foundation with a modern, transparent, and independent governance model focused on environmental sustainability, technological innovation, and equitable representation.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Name and Purpose of the Foundation**
- **Citation**: The Act may be cited as the *Canada Foundation for Sustainable Development Technology Act*.
- **Purpose**: The Foundation's primary objective is to **provide funding to eligible recipients** for **eligible projects** that promote sustainable development.
- **Sustainable Development Definition**: Defined as development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

---

### **2. Definitions (Key Terms)**
The Act defines key terms used throughout the legislation:
- **Board**: The board of directors of the Foundation.
- **Chairperson**: The Chairperson of the board appointed by the Governor in Council on the Minister’s recommendation.
- **Corporation**: Refers to the original entity, now legally merged into the Foundation.
- **Director**: A member of the board, including the Chairperson.
- **Eligible Project**: A project carried out or planned primarily in Canada to develop and demonstrate new technologies promoting sustainable development, including those addressing climate change, air, water, and soil quality.
- **Eligible Recipient**: An entity established in Canada (or capable of carrying out such projects) that meets eligibility criteria set in funding agreements with the Government of Canada and has legal capacity.
- **Employee or Agent of Her Majesty in Right of a Province**: Excludes individuals whose duties are restricted to universities or educational institutions.
- **Foundation**: The Canada Foundation for Sustainable Development Technology, as referred to in Section 3.
- **Member**: A person who is a member of the Foundation.
- **Minister**: The member of the Queen’s Privy Council designated by the Governor in Council to oversee the Foundation.
- **Not-for-profit Organization**: An entity where no part of its income is available for personal benefit of proprietors, members, or shareholders.
- **Special Resolution**: A resolution passed by at least two-thirds of voting members or signed by all entitled members.
- **Sustainable Development**: As defined above.

---

### **3. Legal Continuity and Transfer of Rights**
The Act ensures a seamless transition from the original corporation to the Foundation:
- The **corporation is dissolved**, and all its rights, powers, obligations, and property are **transferred to the Foundation**.
- All references to the corporation in contracts, deeds, or legal instruments are **automatically read as references to the Foundation**.
- The Foundation inherits all legal rights and liabilities of the corporation, including the right to be sued and the obligation to fulfill existing commitments.
- **No liability is transferred to Her Majesty** — the Foundation is **not an agent of the Crown**.
- The Foundation is treated as a **natural person** for legal purposes, meaning it has the **capacity, rights, powers, and privileges** of a person under the law, subject to this Act.

---

### **4. Governance Structure**

#### **Board of Directors**
- The board consists of:
  - **1 Chairperson**, appointed by the Governor in Council on the Minister’s recommendation.
  - **Six directors**, appointed by the Governor in Council on the Minister’s recommendation.
  - **Eight directors**, appointed by the members of the Foundation in accordance with Section 13(5) and the Foundation’s by-laws.

> **Appointment Process**:
> - The Chairperson and six directors are appointed initially by the federal government (via the Minister).
> - The remaining eight directors are elected by the members of the Foundation after the first meeting of the initial seven members.

> **Eligibility Criteria for Directors**:
> - Not a member of Parliament (Senate, House of Commons, or provincial legislature).
> - Not an employee or agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada or a province.
> - Does not ordinarily reside in Canada.
> - Not disqualified under Section 105(1) of the *Canada Business Corporations Act*.

> **Term of Office**:
> - Directors serve during "good behaviour" for up to **five years**.
> - Can be reappointed for additional terms of up to five years.
> - A director who resigns or dies ceases to serve.
> - A director may be removed for cause by:
>   - The Governor in Council (for those appointed under paragraph 9(2)(b)).
>   - A **special resolution** (for those appointed under paragraph 9(2)(c)).

> **Vacancies**:
> - A person appointed to fill a vacancy serves only for the **unexpired portion** of the term.

> **Board Powers**:
> - Oversees management and affairs of the Foundation.
> - May appoint officers, employees, and agents.
> - May designate offices and define duties.
> - May enact organizational by-laws (subject to approval by members).
> - May organize the Foundation, including making banking arrangements and receiving funds, **until full board is appointed**.

> **Provisional Powers (During Transition)**:
> - The Chairperson and any directors appointed under paragraph (2)(b) may:
>   - Organize the Foundation.
>   - Make banking arrangements.
>   - Enact organizational by-laws.
>   - Receive funding.
> - However, **the Foundation shall not provide any funding or enter into agreements or reviews of funding applications** until the full board is in place.

> **Non-Ownership Clarification**:
> - Despite the interim governance, the Foundation is **not considered wholly owned by Her Majesty** under Part X of the *Financial Administration Act*.

---

#### **Membership of the Foundation**
- The Foundation has **15 members** in total.
- **Initial Appointment**:
  - The Governor in Council appoints **seven members** on the recommendation of the Minister of Natural Resources and the Minister of the Environment, after consultation with the Minister of Industry.
- **Subsequent Appointment**:
  - At the first meeting of these seven members, they appoint **eight additional members**.
- **Final Appointment**:
  - The members appoint the first directors at a meeting after the eight are appointed.

> **Eligibility Criteria for Members**:
> - Not a member of Parliament or provincial legislature.
> - Not an employee or agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada or a province.
> - Not a director.
> - Does not ordinarily reside in Canada.
> - Not disqualified under Section 105(1) of the *Canada Business Corporations Act*.

> **Term of Office**:
> - Members serve during "good behaviour" for up to **five years**.
> - Can be reappointed.
> - Vacancies filled for the unexpired term.

> **Removal**:
> - A member may be removed for cause:
>   - By the Governor in Council (for those appointed under subsection 13(2)).
>   - By a **special resolution** (for those appointed under 13(4), (6), or (7)).

> **Ceasing to Be a Member**:
> - Death, resignation, appointment to Parliament, becoming an employee of the Crown, ceasing to reside in Canada, disqualification, or removal.

> **Appointment Considerations**:
> - Ensures representation of:
>   - Individuals involved in sustainable tech development (including climate, air, water, soil).
>   - The business community.
>   - Not-for-profit organizations.
> - Geographic diversity across Canada.
> - Gender balance (men and women).
> - Knowledge of sustainable technologies.

> **Remuneration**:
> - Members serve **without pay**.
> - Are entitled to **reasonable travel and living expenses** incurred while performing duties.

---

### **5. Operational Powers and Funding Mechanisms**

#### **Funding to Eligible Recipients**
- The Foundation may provide **funding to eligible recipients** for eligible projects.
- Funding is used **solely for eligible projects** and must comply with:
  - Terms and conditions specified by the Foundation.
  - Conditions on repayment (if applicable).
  - Intellectual property rights.
  - Maximum amounts and proportions of funding.

#### **Agreements with Recipients**
The Foundation must enter into agreements that include:
- The **manner and timing** of funding advances.
- **Terms and conditions** of funding (including repayment, IP rights).
- **Performance evaluation** of the project (objectives, outcomes, technology potential).
- For recipients composed of multiple organizations, **joint and several liability** for obligations.

> **No Ownership Interest**:
> - The Foundation **does not acquire any interest** (e.g., shares, partnership interests) in any research infrastructure acquired by the recipient.

---

### **6. Financial and Administrative Powers**

#### **Use of Funds**
- The Foundation may use its funds for:
  - Salaries and wages of officers and employees.
  - Rent for office space.
  - Remuneration for directors and agents.
  - Reimbursement of **reasonable travel and living expenses** for directors and members.
  - Other operational costs.
