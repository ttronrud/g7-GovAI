### **Detailed Summary of the *Anti-Personnel Mines Convention Implementation Act***  
*(Canada‚Äôs Implementation of the Ottawa Convention)*

---

## **1. Overview and Purpose**

The *Anti-Personnel Mines Convention Implementation Act* (commonly referred to as the **Anti-Personnel Mines Convention Implementation Act**) is a Canadian law enacted to fulfill Canada‚Äôs international obligations under the **Convention on the Prohibition of the Use, Stockpiling, Production and Transfer of Anti-Personnel Mines and on their Destruction**, commonly known as the **Ottawa Convention**.

This Act provides the legal framework within Canada to:
- Prohibit the use, production, stockpiling, transfer, and possession of **anti-personnel mines**;
- Ensure compliance with the Ottawa Convention;
- Facilitate the destruction of existing stockpiles;
- Allow for authorized exceptions related to mine detection, clearance, and training;
- Enable inspections by international fact-finding missions;
- Protect the rights of individuals and ensure transparency in enforcement.

The Act came into force on **March 1, 1999**, and is binding on both the federal government and provincial governments in Canada.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**

The Act establishes precise definitions essential to understanding its scope and application:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Anti-personnel mine** | A mine designed, altered, or intended to be exploded by the presence, proximity, or contact of a **person**, capable of incapacitating, injuring, or killing one or more individuals. Mines that detonate due to **vehicle contact** and are equipped with **anti-handling devices** are **not** considered anti-personnel mines. |
| **Anti-handling device** | A device attached to or linked with a mine that activates when someone attempts to tamper with or disturb it. These are included in the definition of anti-personnel mines only if the mine itself is designed to harm people. |
| **Mine** | Any munition placed under, on, or near the ground or surface, intended to explode upon contact with a person or vehicle. |
| **Dwelling-house** | Includes any building or structure used as a permanent or temporary residence, including parts connected via doors or passageways, and mobile units used as residences. |
| **Place** | Includes any conveyance (e.g., vehicle, ship, aircraft). |
| **Transfer** | Includes both physical movement and transfer of **title or control** over anti-personnel mines. It **does not** include the transfer of territory containing emplaced mines. |

---

## **3. Core Prohibitions (Section 6)**

No person may:
- **Place** an anti-personnel mine under, on, or near the ground with the intent to detonate it by human presence;
- **Develop, produce, acquire, possess, or transfer** anti-personnel mines;
- **Stockpile** anti-personnel mines.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Exception**: These prohibitions do **not** apply to:
> - Possession or transfer of mines **authorized under Section 10** for training in mine detection, clearance, or destruction;
> - Acquisition, possession, or transfer of mines for **destruction purposes**;
> - Mines that have been **deactivated** according to regulation (e.g., removal of all explosives, priming charges, detonators, or rendering mechanisms inoperable);
> - Participation in military operations with non-Convention signatory forces, **if** it does not constitute active assistance in prohibited activities.

---

## **4. Export and Import Restrictions (Section 6)**

- **No export or import** of anti-personnel mines is permitted **unless authorized under the Export and Import Permits Act**.
- This ensures Canada does not facilitate international trafficking of these dangerous weapons.

---

## **5. Exceptions for Authorized Use (Section 10)**

The Minister may authorize the placement, acquisition, possession, or transfer of a **limited number** of anti-personnel mines for:
- Mine detection,
- Mine clearance,
- Mine destruction techniques.

> üîç The number of mines authorized must be the **absolute minimum necessary** for these purposes.

This allows Canada to maintain capacity for defense and humanitarian mine response while strictly limiting exposure to prohibited activities.

---

## **6. Information Gathering and Enforcement (Section 13‚Äì14)**

The Minister may issue a **notice** to any person who has relevant information or documents about:
- Compliance with the Act;
- Enforcement activities;
- Reporting obligations under **Article 7 of the Convention** (to the UN Secretary-General).

If a person **fails or objects** to providing information:
- The Minister may apply to a **superior court judge** for an order to compel disclosure.
- The judge may grant the order **only if**:
  - The information is necessary for Canada‚Äôs compliance with the Convention;
  - The public interest **outweighs** the individual‚Äôs privacy interest.

> ‚úÖ **Notice period**: At least **seven days** before the hearing.

## **7. Fact-Finding Missions (Section 14)**

When authorized under **Article 8 of the Ottawa Convention**, Canada must:
- Issue a **certificate** to each member of the fact-finding mission, which includes:
  - Name and status of the member;
  - Privileges and immunities under the **UN Privileges and Immunities Convention (1946)**;
  - Any other conditions applicable to their mission.

### **Rights and Powers of Fact-Finding Members:**
- May **enter and inspect** any place that:
  - Is a military or weapons facility;
  - Has the capacity to develop, produce, or stockpile anti-personnel mines.
- May **inspect** areas, containers, records, files, electronic systems, take photographs, interview personnel, and collect samples.
- May **take copies** of documents and remove them from the site.

### **Entry Requirements:**
- **Dwelling-house**: Entry requires **consent of the occupant**.
- **Non-dwelling-place**: Entry requires **consent of the person in control**, **unless**:
  - A **warrant** is issued by a justice (on ex parte application).
  - The justice is satisfied that:
    - There are **reasonable grounds** to believe relevant information exists;
    - Entry is **necessary** for the mission;
    - Entry has been refused or is likely to be refused.

> üö® **Exigent circumstances**: A warrant is not required if immediate action is needed due to urgent circumstances.

> ‚öñÔ∏è **Use of force**: Only permitted if:
> - Accompanied by a **peace officer**;
> - Specifically authorized in the warrant.

> üìå **No search/seizure powers** under the Criminal Code are excluded ‚Äî they **apply** in parallel.

---

## **8. Confidentiality and Disclosure (Section 17)**

- **Confidential information** obtained under this Act or the Convention **cannot be disclosed** without written consent of the source.
- **Exceptions** to confidentiality:
  - Disclosure is **permitted** if:
    - It serves the **public interest** (e.g., public health, safety, environmental protection), and this interest **clearly outweighs** financial loss or privacy harm;
    - It is **necessary** for enforcing this Act, another law, or implementing the Convention.

> ‚ùå No person may be compelled to testify or produce records containing confidential information **unless** the proceeding relates to enforcement of this Act or another Act of Parliament.

---

## **9. Designation of Ministerial Powers (Section 18)**

- The Governor in Council may **designate one or more persons** (e.g., officials in the Department of National Defence or Public Safety) to:
  - Exercise powers of the Minister;
  - Perform duties under the Act or Convention.
- These designated persons act **subject to specified terms and conditions**.

---

## **10. Regulatory Authority (Section 19)**

- The Governor in Council may make **regulations** to:
  - Implement the Convention;
  - Prescribe specific rules (e.g., deactivation standards, reporting requirements).
- Regulations may **make it an offence** to violate them.
- The Minister must **amend the schedule** to the Act to reflect any changes in the Convention and **lay the amendment before Parliament within 15 days** of making the order.

---

## **11. Penalties and Offences (Section 20)**

Violations of the Act are criminal offenses, punishable as follows:

| Violation | Penalty |
|---------|--------|
| Section 6 (placing, possessing, transferring mines) or Section 8 (failure to deliver mines for destruction) or Section 13(3) or 17(1) | **Summary conviction**: Fine up to **$5,000** or imprisonment up to **18 months**, or both.<br>**Indictment conviction**: Fine up to **$500,000** or imprisonment up to **5 years**, or both. |
| Contravention of a regulation made under Section 19 (and declared an offence) | Punishable on **summary conviction** only. |

> üìç **Real property**: The forfeiture provision **does not apply** to real property unless it was built or significantly modified **to facilitate** the offense.

> ‚è≥ **Time limit for prosecution**: Summary proceedings must be initiated **within two years** of the incident.
