**Detailed Summary of the National Wildlife Week Act**

**Act Title:**  
*The National Wildlife Week Act*

**Citation:**  
This Act may be cited as the *National Wildlife Week Act*.

---

**Purpose and Key Provision:**

The primary objective of the National Wildlife Week Act is to establish and recognize a dedicated period of time each year for promoting awareness, education, and public engagement in wildlife conservation.

**Establishment of National Wildlife Week:**

- The Act designates the week in which **April 10** occurs each year as **National Wildlife Week**.
- April 10 is specifically chosen because it is the **birthday of Jack Miner**, a respected figure in Canadian wildlife conservation. Jack Miner was a dedicated advocate for wildlife protection and played a significant role in fostering public interest in environmental stewardship, particularly in the context of Canadian natural heritage.

**Observance and Recognition:**

- The week is to be **known and observed** throughout Canada as National Wildlife Week.
- This designation is not merely symbolic; it serves as an official recognition by the federal government to promote public awareness and involvement in wildlife-related issues.

**Activities and Engagement:**

- The Act empowers **interested clubs, associations, and organizations** (such as environmental groups, wildlife societies, educational institutions, and community groups) to use the week to:
  - Disseminate information about wildlife conservation.
  - Organize educational events, public programs, exhibitions, or outreach initiatives.
  - Highlight the importance of protecting native species, habitats, and ecosystems.
- These activities may occur on **the day of that week most suitable to the organization**, allowing flexibility in scheduling based on regional needs, resources, and community interests.

**Scope and Application:**

- The Act applies **throughout Canada**, ensuring nationwide recognition and participation.
- It is intended to be inclusive and accessible, encouraging participation from a wide range of stakeholders, including schools, Indigenous communities, government agencies, and non-profits.

**No Legal Enforcement or Mandatory Requirements:**

- The Act does **not impose mandatory obligations** on individuals or organizations.
- Participation is voluntary, and the focus is on **encouraging awareness and fostering a culture of conservation** rather than enforcing compliance.

**Significance:**

- The Act reflects a commitment to environmental education and public engagement.
- By linking the observance to the legacy of Jack Miner, it honors a key contributor to Canada’s conservation movement and provides a meaningful historical and cultural anchor for the week.
- National Wildlife Week serves as a platform to promote sustainable practices, biodiversity, and respect for Canada’s rich natural heritage.

---

**Conclusion:**

The *National Wildlife Week Act* is a non-binding, symbolic piece of legislation that establishes a national observance in Canada to promote wildlife conservation. By designating the week containing April 10—Jack Miner’s birthday—as National Wildlife Week, the Act fosters public interest in environmental stewardship and empowers community-based organizations to deliver educational content and conservation messages during a designated period each year. While it does not mandate actions, it provides a formal, recognized opportunity for Canadians to come together and celebrate and protect Canada’s wildlife.
