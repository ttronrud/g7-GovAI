**Detailed Summary of the *College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants Act* establishes a regulatory body — the **College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants** — to oversee and regulate individuals who provide immigration and citizenship advice in Canada. The Act is designed to protect the public by ensuring that consultants operate with professional integrity, competence, and accountability. It sets out the structure, functions, governance, licensing requirements, disciplinary mechanisms, and public access provisions for the College.

---

## **1. Key Definitions**

- **Board**: The board of directors established under subsection 17(1), responsible for managing the College’s affairs.
- **College**: Refers to the College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants, either continuing under section 84 or newly established under section 86.
- **Immigration and Citizenship Consultant**: A person (excluding certain exempted individuals under federal legislation) who directly or indirectly advises or offers to advise individuals on matters related to:
  - Citizenship Act applications or proceedings;
  - Expressions of interest under section 10.1(3) of the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act* (IRPA);
  - IRPA applications or proceedings.
- **Licence**: A formal authorization issued by the College to practice as an immigration and citizenship consultant.
- **Minister**: A federal minister designated under section 3; may be appointed by the Governor in Council.
- **Prescribed**: As defined in regulations (i.e., rules set by the Minister or the College).
- **Privileged**: Information protected under evidence law, litigation privilege, solicitor-client privilege, or professional secrecy (e.g., advocates and notaries).
- **Registrar**: The official appointed under section 30 to administer licensing, compliance, and record-keeping.

---

## **2. Purpose of the College**

The primary purpose of the College is to serve the **public interest** by:

1. **Establishing and enforcing qualification standards**, standards of practice, and **continuing education requirements** for licensed consultants;
2. **Providing training and development programs** for licensees;
3. **Ensuring compliance with a Code of Professional Conduct**;
4. **Conducting public awareness campaigns** to inform the public about the role and responsibilities of consultants.

This ensures that only qualified, ethical, and competent professionals advise individuals on complex immigration and citizenship matters.

---

## **3. Governance and Structure**

### **Head Office**
- Located at a place in Canada designated in the College’s by-laws.

### **Legal Capacity**
- The College has the **rights, powers, and privileges of a natural person**, allowing it to act independently in legal and administrative matters.
- These powers extend **outside Canada** to the extent permitted by local laws.

### **Not an Agent of the Government**
- The College is **not part of the federal public administration**.
- Its directors, officers, employees, and agents are **not considered public servants**.
- This distinction ensures independence from government oversight and reduces conflicts of interest.

### **Applicability of Laws**
- The **Canada Not-for-Profit Corporations Act does not apply** to the College — it operates under a different legal framework.
- The **Official Languages Act applies**, ensuring that the College provides services in both official languages (English and French).

---

## **4. Membership**

- The **members of the College are the licensed consultants**.
- The College must hold an **annual general meeting** of members within six months of each fiscal year.
- Members must be notified of the time and place of the meeting in accordance with the by-laws.

---

## **5. Financial and Record-Keeping Obligations**

- The College must:
  - Establish a **compensation fund** to reimburse individuals who suffer harm due to a licensee’s misconduct.
  - Maintain and publicly make available records, including:
    - By-laws and amendments;
    - Minutes of public Board meetings;
    - Register of directors;
    - Register of officers;
    - Audited annual financial statements.
- Records must be retained for a **prescribed period**, unless longer retention is required by another Act.
- The College must submit a **fiscal year report** to the Minister within **120 days** of the end of each fiscal year.
- The Minister must **table a copy of the report** in both Houses of Parliament within the first 15 days of sitting after receiving it.

---

## **6. Board of Directors**

### **Composition**
- Minimum of **seven directors**, including a Chairperson.
- The Minister may, by order, **fix the number of directors** and **appoint some**.
- The number of **appointed directors** must not exceed the minimum required to form a majority.
- The **remaining directors are elected by licensees** in accordance with by-laws.

### **Term of Office**
- Directors serve **no more than three years** and may be re-elected.
- Terms may begin or end on different dates — not required to align.
- Appointed directors serve **during good behaviour** and may be removed for cause by the Minister.
- Elected directors may be removed per by-laws.

### **Eligibility Requirements**
An individual is **ineligible** to serve as a director if:
- Under 18 years of age;
- Not a Canadian citizen or permanent resident (as defined in IRPA);
- Has a bankruptcy status;
- In the case of appointment:
  - Is a licensee;
  - Is employed in the federal public administration;
- In the case of election:
  - Has a suspended licence;
- Or is ineligible under other regulations.

> **Note**: An act of a director or officer remains valid even if they are ineligible or elected irregularly.

---

## **7. Chairperson**

- Elected by the Board from among directors.
- Responsible for presiding over Board meetings and performing duties as assigned.
- May be removed by the Board per by-laws.

---

## **8. Board Meetings**

- The Board must meet **at least once per calendar year**.
- A **quorum** is a majority of directors fixed by the Minister.
- The Chairperson must give **notice of meetings** to directors and any designated observers.
- Meetings are **open to the public**, unless otherwise specified in by-laws.
- Directors and observers may participate **remotely** via telephone or electronic means.
- A **written resolution signed by all voting directors** is valid as if passed at a meeting.
- Copies of such resolutions must be kept with public meeting minutes (subject to by-law exceptions).

---

## **9. Committees**

Two key committees are established:

### **Complaints Committee**
- Appointed by the Board in accordance with regulations.
- Has jurisdiction over complaints related to **professional misconduct or incompetence** by licensees (including former licensees).
- May:
  - Consider complaints referred by the College or Registrar;
  - Conduct investigations;
  - Initiate proceedings if there are reasonable grounds to suspect misconduct or incompetence.
- May **refer complaints to the Discipline Committee** or to another regulatory body with statutory authority.

### **Discipline Committee**
- Also appointed by the Board.
- Members serve **no more than five years** and may be reappointed.
- Hold office **during good behaviour** and may be removed for cause.
- **Cannot serve on both the Complaints and Discipline Committees simultaneously**.

> **Note**: A member of the Discipline Committee may conclude any proceeding they have initiated even if their term expires.

---

## **10. Registrar**

- Appointed by the Board for a term of **up to five years**, with reappointment possible.
- Responsible for:
  - Issuing licences;
  - Maintaining a **publicly accessible register of licensees** (searchable on the College’s website);
  - Ensuring compliance with the Act.
- The register must be updated **timely** and made available in a searchable format and, where appropriate, via other channels.
- The Registrar must notify the Minister in writing when:
  - A licence is suspended;
  - A licence is revoked;
  - A licence is surrendered;
  - Any other prescribed circumstance occurs.

### **Licensing Process**
- Applications must be made in the form and manner specified by the Registrar and include required information.
- On application, the Registrar issues a licence **if the applicant meets eligibility criteria** set in the by-laws.
- Licences are subject to **conditions or restrictions** as defined in the Act.
- The Registrar may **approve surrender** of a licence upon licensee application (as per by-laws).

### **Enforcement Powers**
- May enter a licensee’s **business premises without notice** at any reasonable time to:
  - Require production of documents;
  - Examine or copy documents;
  - Obtain information from the licensee, employees, or business partners.
- If premises are in a **dwelling-house**, entry requires **occupant’s consent**.
- Powers **do not apply to privileged information** (e.g., solicitor-client privilege).
- If the Registrar suspects professional misconduct or incompetence, they may **initiate a complaint** and refer it to the Complaints Committee.

### **Sanctions**
- If a licensee violates the Act, regulations, or by-laws, the Registrar may:
  - **Suspend** the licence;
  - **Revoke** a suspended licence;
  - Take any other action prescribed in regulations.
- A licensee must **comply** with such decisions.
- The College may **file a certified copy** of a decision in the **Federal Court**, which then becomes an **enforceable order**.
- The Registrar must **notify all licensees** of any decision to revoke or suspend a licence.
- If a complaint is dismissed by the Discipline Committee, the Registrar must **notify all licensees** of the dismissal.
