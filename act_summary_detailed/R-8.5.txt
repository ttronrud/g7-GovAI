### **Detailed Summary of the Roosevelt Campobello International Park Commission Act**

---

#### **1. Introduction and Purpose**
The *Roosevelt Campobello International Park Commission Act* is a Canadian statute enacted to provide the legal framework for the establishment and operation of the **Roosevelt Campobello International Park**, a joint Canadian–U.S. memorial park located on Campobello Island, New Brunswick. The Act was passed on **August 14, 1964**, and came into force via proclamation by the Governor in Council.

Its primary purpose is to:
- Formalize the creation of a **joint Canadian–U.S. commission** (the Roosevelt Campobello International Park Commission) to manage a shared memorial site dedicated to **President Franklin D. Roosevelt**.
- Symbolize and strengthen **bilateral relations between Canada and the United States** through a unique cultural and historical initiative.
- Ensure the preservation, restoration, and public accessibility of the former summer home of President Roosevelt, now open to the public as a monument to his life and legacy.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**
The Act establishes clear definitions for key terms:
- **Agreement**: Refers to the bilateral agreement between Canada and the United States (set out in the Schedule) that forms the basis of this Act. This Agreement outlines the structure, functions, and responsibilities of the Commission.
- **Commission**: The Roosevelt Campobello International Park Commission — a joint body composed of members from both Canada and the United States.
- **Minister**: Refers to the **Minister of Foreign Affairs of Canada**.
- **Park**: The Roosevelt Campobello International Park, located on Campobello Island, New Brunswick.

---

#### **3. Legal Status and Juridical Personality**
The Commission is granted **full corporate status in Canada**, including:
- The legal powers and capacities of a body corporate under **Section 30 of the Interpretation Act**.
- This includes the ability to:
  - Acquire and dispose of property (except the Roosevelt home and its land),
  - Enter into contracts,
  - Sue or be sued in either Canada or the United States,
  - Appoint staff and set their terms of employment,
  - Adopt internal rules of procedure,
  - Charge admission fees,
  - Grant concessions,
  - Accept donations, bequests, or devises.

This corporate status enables the Commission to function independently and efficiently in managing the Park.

---

#### **4. Property and Tax Exemptions**
To ensure financial stability and operational freedom, the Act provides significant exemptions:
- **Exemption from attachment, seizure, or execution**: All property of the Commission in Canada is protected from court orders or judgments that would allow creditors to claim it.
- **Exemption from customs and excise duties**: No duty or tax under Canadian customs or excise laws applies to any property imported into Canada by the Commission for use in the Park.
- **Charitable status**:
  - The Commission is recognized as a **charitable organization** under:
    - **Section 62(1)(e) of the Income Tax Act** (for income tax purposes),
    - **Section 7(1)(d)(i) of the Estate Tax Act** (for estate tax purposes).
  - This status provides tax benefits and allows the Commission to accept donations and bequests without immediate tax implications.

---

#### **5. Composition of the Commission**
The Commission consists of **six members**, with:
- **Three appointed by the Government of Canada**
- **Three appointed by the Government of the United States**

Additional appointments:
- One Canadian member is to be **nominated by the Government of New Brunswick**.
- One U.S. member is to be **nominated by the Government of Maine**.

**Alternates** may be appointed for each member in the same manner. These alternates can serve in place of a member if needed.

**Leadership and Quorum**:
- The Commission elects a **Chairman** and **Vice-Chairman** from among its members.
- Each serves a **two-year term**.
- **No member of the same nationality** may serve as both Chairman and Vice-Chairman simultaneously.
- The **chairmanship alternates every two years** between Canadian and U.S. nationals.
- A **quorum** requires at least **four members** (or their alternates), including **at least two from Canada and two from the United States**.
- Decisions require the **affirmative vote of at least two Canadian and two U.S. members** (or their alternates).

---

#### **6. Employment and Staffing**
- The Commission may employ both **Canadian and U.S. citizens**.
- Employment is subject to applicable **Canadian labor laws**.
- The Government of Canada agrees to take **necessary measures** to ensure that U.S. citizens can work with the Commission on **equal terms** as Canadian citizens.

---

#### **7. Financial and Administrative Obligations**
- The Commission must:
  - Submit an **annual report** to the Minister of Foreign Affairs (Canada) within **three months** of the end of each fiscal year.
  - The report includes:
    - A financial statement,
    - Auditor’s report,
    - Summary of operations.
  - The Minister must lay the report before **Parliament within 15 days** of receiving it, or on the first day after Parliament resumes if it is not then sitting.
- The Commission maintains **insurance** (e.g., liability and property insurance) to protect against risks.
- The Commission holds **at least one meeting per calendar year**.
- Records are **open to inspection** by the accounting agencies of both Canada and the United States, ensuring transparency and accountability.

---

#### **8. Tax and Financial Provisions**
- The Commission is **exempt from all taxes** in both Canada and the United States on:
  - Real or personal property,
  - Gifts, bequests, or devises,
  - Income (from admissions, concessions, donations, or government appropriations).
- This exemption is intended to preserve the Commission’s financial independence and ensure it can operate without fiscal burdens.
- The **Governments of Canada and the United States share equally** the costs of:
  - Developing the Park,
  - Operating and maintaining it annually.
- **Revenue from admission fees or concessions** is shared **equally** between the two governments within **60 days** of the end of the fiscal year.
- Other funds (e.g., donations) may be used to further the Commission’s mission, as per the Agreement.

---

#### **9. Budget and Operational Oversight**
- The Commission submits an **annual budget** to both governments, outlining anticipated expenditures.
- The budget must be **approved jointly** by the two governments before implementation.
- The Commission operates strictly in accordance with the approved budget.

---

#### **10. Compensation for Members**
- Commissioners receive **no salary** from the Commission.
- However, they may be paid **reasonable per diem and travel expenses** to cover the costs of attending meetings and performing duties.

---

#### **11. Implementation and Effective Date**
- The Agreement (the foundation of this Act) requires **legislative implementation** in both Canada and the United States.
- The Act comes into force on a date **to be fixed by proclamation** of the Governor in Council (i.e., the Canadian government).
- The Agreement itself was signed on **January 22, 1964**, in Washington, D.C., by:
  - **Lester B. Pearson**, Prime Minister of Canada,
  - **Lyndon B. Johnson**, President of the United States.

The Act was passed in response to the **Hammer family’s generous offer** to donate their summer home on Campobello Island — a home once used by President Roosevelt — to be opened as a public memorial.

---

#### **12. Historical and Symbolic Significance**
The Act reflects a broader diplomatic goal:
- To **honor the personal and historical ties** between President Roosevelt and the Canadian summer home.
- To **promote mutual understanding and goodwill** between Canada and the United States.
- To create a **shared international cultural space** that fosters peace, education, and public engagement.

The Park serves as a living memorial that emphasizes:
- The **international nature** of the site,
- The **shared values** of democracy, leadership, and peace,
- The **importance of cross-border cooperation** in preserving history and heritage.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Roosevelt Campobello International Park Commission Act* is a landmark piece of Canadian legislation that:
- Establishes a legally robust, autonomous, and financially protected international commission,
- Provides comprehensive legal and financial protections for the Park,
- Promotes transparency, accountability, and shared governance between Canada and the United States,
- Embodies a powerful symbol of **bilateral friendship and historical continuity**.

It remains one of the most enduring examples of **diplomatic cooperation through cultural heritage**, and the Roosevelt Campobello International Park continues to serve as a significant public and educational site for visitors from both nations.
