**Detailed Summary of the National Philanthropy Day Act**

The *National Philanthropy Day Act* is a legislative measure enacted by the Parliament of Canada to formally recognize and celebrate the spirit of philanthropy within Canadian society. The Act is rooted in the values of generosity, community engagement, and civic responsibility, and it establishes a national day dedicated to honoring individuals and organizations that contribute to the well-being of others through voluntary giving and service.

### Key Provisions and Purpose:

1. **Definition of Philanthropy**  
   The Act begins with a series of "whereas" clauses that establish the foundational values of philanthropy:
   - Philanthropy is defined as the spirit of giving without expectation of reward.
   - It highlights the role of Canadian volunteers who selflessly dedicate their time and resources to improving the lives of others.
   - It emphasizes how philanthropy strengthens communities by fostering civic participation and bringing people together around shared goals.
   - It acknowledges the tangible benefits that countless Canadians have received through charitable actions and the generosity of individuals and organizations.

2. **Establishment of National Philanthropy Day**  
   The core provision of the Act is the formal designation of **November 15th each year as "National Philanthropy Day"** throughout Canada. This day is recognized in all provinces and territories, creating a unified national observance.

3. **Purpose of the Recognition**  
   The Act underscores the importance of publicly honoring Canadians who demonstrate the spirit of giving. By designating this day, the government aims to:
   - Promote awareness and appreciation of philanthropy.
   - Encourage greater civic engagement and volunteerism.
   - Inspire future generations to embrace generosity and community service.
   - Strengthen the cultural and social fabric of Canada by celebrating the positive impact of voluntary action.

4. **Legal Citation and Effect**  
   The Act may be cited as the *National Philanthropy Day Act*, providing a formal legal identity to the legislation. This ensures that the day is recognized in official records, public communications, and governmental programs.

### Significance and Implications:

- **Cultural and Social Impact**: The Act reflects Canada’s commitment to values such as compassion, service, and social cohesion. It embeds philanthropy into the national consciousness, reinforcing the idea that giving is a fundamental part of Canadian identity.
  
- **Public Awareness and Engagement**: By establishing a national day, the Act encourages schools, community groups, businesses, and media to organize events, campaigns, and educational initiatives that promote volunteerism and charitable giving.

- **Honoring Individuals and Organizations**: The day serves as a platform to recognize both large-scale charitable institutions and individual volunteers whose contributions have made a meaningful difference in people’s lives.

- **Alignment with Existing Practices**: The Act formalizes a day that had already gained cultural recognition in Canada—November 15th—through grassroots efforts and public sentiment, elevating it from a tradition to a legally recognized national observance.

### Conclusion:

The *National Philanthropy Day Act* is a symbolic yet meaningful piece of legislation that celebrates the enduring spirit of generosity in Canadian society. It does not impose new obligations or financial requirements but instead serves to recognize, honor, and promote the values of volunteerism, community service, and altruism. By formally designating November 15th as National Philanthropy Day, Canada affirms its appreciation for those who give their time, talents, and resources to build stronger, more compassionate communities.
