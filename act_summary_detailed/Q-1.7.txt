**Detailed Summary of the Act: "An Act to Establish the Office of the Port Warden of the Harbor of Quebec"**

---

### **Overview**

This Act, enacted by Her Majesty in Canada with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons, establishes the **Office of the Port Warden of the Harbor of Quebec** in response to the growing volume of maritime trade and commercial activity in the city. The Act provides a comprehensive framework for the appointment, duties, powers, oversight, and accountability of the Port Warden and his deputies, with a strong emphasis on impartiality, transparency, and the protection of maritime interests.

The Act was designed to ensure the proper handling of cargo, vessel safety, damage assessments, and dispute resolution within the port of Quebec, particularly in the context of increasing international trade. It also incorporates mechanisms for public accountability, procedural fairness, and legal enforceability.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Creation of the Office of the Port Warden**
- The Act formally establishes the **Port Warden of the Harbor of Quebec** as a key administrative and regulatory officer within the port.
- The office is created due to the **increasing trade volume** and business activity in the harbor, indicating a need for a dedicated official to manage port-related operations and disputes.

---

### **2. Appointment Process**
- **Appointment Authority**: The Governor in Council (the federal government) appoints the Port Warden.
- **Recommendation Body**: The appointment must be based on a recommendation from the **Board of Trade and Industry of the Metropolitan Quebec**.
- **Control and Oversight**: The **Board of Directors** of the Board of Trade and Industry retains ultimate control over the office.

---

### **3. Board of Examiners**
- The Board of Directors shall appoint a **Board of Examiners (five members)** annually, starting immediately after the Act's passage, and again in **April each year**.
- The Examiners are responsible for:
  - Evaluating all candidates for the **Port Warden** position.
  - Evaluating candidates for **Deputy Port Wardens**, as needed.
- The Board of Directors then makes the final appointments based on the Examiners’ recommendations.

> *Purpose*: Ensures transparency, fairness, and merit-based selection of officers.

---

### **4. Oath of Office**
- The Port Warden must take and subscribe a solemn oath before a **Justice of the Peace** in the District of Quebec.
- The oath reads:
  > *"I, A.B., do solemnly swear that I will faithfully and impartially, to the best of my judgment and ability, perform the duties of the office of Port Warden of the Harbor of Quebec, without fear, favor or affection for any person or party whomsoever."*

> *Purpose*: Establishes a legal and ethical commitment to impartiality and public service.

---

### **5. Compensation and Fees**
- The Port Warden **receives no salary**.
- He may receive **fees strictly related to the business of his office** (e.g., for surveys, certifications, or evaluations).
- All such fees must be:
  - Recorded in official books.
  - Reported annually in a **certified return** to the Board of Directors.

> *Note*: The Act explicitly prohibits any personal financial gain from the office, emphasizing public service.

---

### **6. Removal from Office**
- The Port Warden or any Deputy may be **removed for misconduct or neglect of duty** at the discretion of the Board of Directors of the Board of Trade and Industry.

---

### **7. Rules and Regulations**
- The Board of Examiners may:
  - **Make, amend, or repeal** rules and by-laws governing the office.
- Any such rules must be **approved by the Board of Directors** before implementation.

> *Purpose*: Allows for adaptive governance while maintaining oversight.

---

### **8. Office Operations and Hours**
- The Port Warden must maintain an **open office**:
  - **Season of navigation (active shipping season)**: 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.
  - **Off-season**: 10 a.m. to 2 p.m.
- The office must have:
  - A **seal of office**.
  - **Official books** to record all acts, decisions, and fees.
- All actions and fees must be recorded in accordance with instructions from the Board of Examiners.

---

### **9. Cargo and Vessel Inspections**
#### **(a) Examinations of Damaged Cargo**
- Upon request from any party, the Port Warden or Deputy must:
  - Proceed on board to examine **condition and stowage of cargo**.
  - If damage is found, **investigate, determine cause**, and **record findings in official books**.

#### **(b) Primâ Facie Evidence of Damage**
- If a vessel arrives from abroad and:
  - Its hatches are opened by a **non-Port Warden**.
  - Cargo is found damaged as a result.
- This constitutes **primâ facie evidence** that damage occurred due to **improper stowage or negligence** by the vessel’s crew or owners.
- The **owner, master, or part-owner** is liable until proven otherwise.

> *Purpose*: Prevents unjust liability and establishes clear standards for damage attribution.

---

### **10. Surveyor Authority**
- The Port Warden is empowered to act as a **surveyor** for:
  - Vessels that have **suffered wreck or damage**.
  - Vessels deemed **unfit to proceed**.
- Duties include:
  - Examining **hull, spars, rigging, and appurtenances**.
  - Specifying damage and recording it in official books.
  - Calling in **skilled professionals** (e.g., shipwrights, riggers) for assistance.
- **No surveyor may have a financial interest** in the case.
- The Port Warden may also certify **repairs** to ensure the vessel is seaworthy; such a certificate is **admissible as evidence** in court.

---

### **11. Pre-Voyage Fitness Checks**
- Any vessel carrying **grain for ports outside inland navigation** must:
  - Notify the Port Warden **before departure or clearing at customs**.
- The Port Warden must:
  - Board the vessel and assess its **fitness to proceed**.
  - If unfit, specify **conditions under which it may leave**.
  - If the master refuses to comply, the Port Warden must **notify the Collector of Customs** to prevent clearance.
- A **certificate of fitness** must be issued before departure.

> *Purpose*: Ensures maritime safety and prevents vessels from being sent to sea in unsafe conditions.

---

### **12. Stowage and Dunnage Requirements**
- The Port Warden may determine:
  - The **amount of dunnage** needed:
    - Below cargo.
    - Between wheat and other grain.
    - Between grain and flour.
- His **certificate on dunnage use** is **primâ facie evidence** of proper stowage.

> *Purpose*: Prevents cargo damage due to poor storage or shifting.

---

### **13. Vessel Inspections for Shippers**
- If a shipper pays for inspection, the Port Warden may:
  - Board the vessel and assess its fitness.
  - Issue a report if unfit.

---

### **14. Valuation and Measurement**
- The Port Warden may:
  - Estimate the **value and measurement** of a vessel.
  - Record such estimates in official books.
- Used in disputes or legal proceedings.

---

### **15. Auctions of Damaged or Condemned Property**
- Any auction of:
  - Condemned vessels.
  - Ship materials.
  - Damaged cargo.
  - Must be conducted in the harbor or city of Quebec.
- **Mandatory requirements**:
  - **File a statement** with the Port Warden’s office within **10 days** of sale.
  - **Public advertisement** in at least **two English and one French newspapers** for **at least two days**.
  - Sales must occur between **11 a.m. and 3 p.m.** (no earlier than 11, no later than 3).
- **No sale can occur without a formal survey and condemnation**.
- The Port Warden must be **one of the surveyors** in all such cases.

> *Purpose*: Ensures transparency, fairness, and legal legitimacy in auctions.

---

### **16. Arbitration of Disputes**
- The Port Warden may:
  - Hear and **arbitrate disputes** between:
    - A vessel’s master or consignee.
    - A cargo owner, shipper, or consignee.
- All disputes must be recorded in official books.

> *Purpose*: Provides a neutral, accessible forum for resolving commercial conflicts.

---

### **17. Notice and Due Process**
- The Port Warden must:
  - Provide **reasonable notice** to all interested parties before acting.
  - Require **written notice** and a **reasonable time** before any action is taken.
- This ensures procedural fairness and prevents surprise decisions.

---

### **18. Access to Records and Certificates**
- Any party may:
  - Request a **certificate** in writing from the Port Warden, under his hand, regarding matters in his records.
  - Request **copies of entries** in official books or filed documents.
- The Port Warden must supply a **copy of the regulations** relating to his office to any master of a vessel arriving in Quebec **once per year**.

> *Purpose*: Promotes transparency and public access to official records.
