### üìå **1. Title and Purpose**
- **Official Name**: *The Broadcasting Act*  
- **Purpose**: To establish a modern, inclusive, and independent Canadian broadcasting system that serves all Canadians‚Äîregardless of language, culture, ability, or background‚Äîby ensuring access to diverse, high-quality, and culturally relevant programming.

This Act aims to:
- Regulate broadcasting in Canada.
- Promote Canadian content and creative expression.
- Protect freedom of expression and journalistic independence.
- Strengthen national identity, linguistic duality (English/French), and cultural sovereignty.
- Support marginalized communities, including Indigenous peoples, racialized groups, persons with disabilities, and official language minority communities.

| Case | Explanation |

| **Online Services Providing Social Media** | If an online undertaking provides a social media service, it **does not exercise programming control** over user-uploaded content (unless it is the provider or affiliate). |

> **Note**: This section ensures that platforms like Facebook or Instagram are not treated as broadcasters simply because users upload content.

### üèõÔ∏è **4. Core Broadcasting Policy for Canada**

The Act declares a comprehensive **national broadcasting policy** with the following foundational principles:

#### ‚úÖ Ownership and Control
- The Canadian broadcasting system must be **effectively owned and controlled by Canadians**.
- Includes **foreign broadcasting undertakings** that serve Canadian audiences.

#### ‚úÖ System Structure
- The system operates primarily in **English and French**.
- Comprises **three key elements**:
  1. **Public** (e.g., CBC)
  2. **Private** (commercial broadcasters)
  3. **Community** (local, grassroots stations)

> These elements work together to serve diverse audiences.

#### ‚úÖ Language and Cultural Context
- English and French broadcasting operate under **different conditions** due to the **minority status of French in North America**.
- French-language programming must be **supported and developed** to reflect its unique position.

#### ‚úÖ Key Objectives of the System

| Objective | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Cultural Vitality** | Safeguard, enrich, and strengthen Canada‚Äôs cultural, political, social, and economic fabric. |

| **Inclusivity** | Reflect the needs of all Canadians, including: <br>‚Ä¢ Black and other racialized communities <br>‚Ä¢ Persons with disabilities <br>‚Ä¢ Indigenous peoples <br>‚Ä¢ Diverse ethnocultural backgrounds <br>‚Ä¢ Different ages, abilities, gender identities, and sexual orientations |

| **French-Language Programming** | Prioritize the creation and broadcast of original French-language programs, including from minority communities. |

| **Innovation and Adaptability** | Encourage innovation and adapt to technological change (e.g., streaming, AI, mobile devices). |

#### ‚úÖ Canadian Content Requirements
- All broadcasting undertakings must:
  - **Employ and make maximum use (predominant use)** of Canadian creative and human resources in programming.
  - Use **Canadian talent** in production, writing, direction, music, etc.
  - **Exception**: If specialized content (e.g., foreign language, niche formats) makes full use impractical, the use must still be **as great as practicable**.

> **Foreign Online Undertakings** (e.g., Netflix, YouTube, Amazon Prime):

> - Must **contribute equitably** to Canadian programming.
> - Must ensure **equitable visibility** of Canadian content, especially in French.

#### ‚úÖ Programming Standards

  - **Balanced in English and French** ‚Äî strive for equivalent quality.
  - **Reflective of Canada‚Äôs diversity** ‚Äî include regional, local, Indigenous, and multicultural perspectives.
  - Include **educational and community programming** (especially through independent authorities).
