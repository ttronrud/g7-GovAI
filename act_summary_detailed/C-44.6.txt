### üìå **1. General Overview and Citation**
- The Act is officially titled the **Corrections and Conditional Release Act**.
- It establishes the legal framework governing the **operation of the Correctional Service of Canada (Service)**, including the **custody, supervision, rehabilitation, and release** of offenders.
- It aligns with broader goals of justice, public safety, and reintegration into society.

### üë• **3. Representation of Victims**
- If a victim is deceased or unable to act:
  - Spouse (at time of death)
  - Cohabitant (for at least one year)
  - Relative or dependent
  - Person with legal or factual custody or care of the victim or their dependent
- **Exclusion**: The offender cannot act on behalf of a victim.

### üéØ **4. Purpose and Guiding Principles of the Correctional System**
#### ‚úÖ **Primary Purpose**
> To maintain a **just, peaceful, and safe society** by:
> - Safely and humanely carrying out court-imposed sentences
> - Rehabilitating offenders and helping them reintegrate as law-abiding citizens through programs

#### ‚úÖ **Core Principles (Guiding the Service)**
1. **Sentence Implementation**  
   - Must consider: sentencing judge‚Äôs recommendations, nature of the offence, offender responsibility, victim input, Parole Board policies, and other justice system feedback.

2. **Transparency & Communication**  
   - Timely information exchange with victims, offenders, and the public  
   - Public communication about policies and programs

3. **Least Restrictive Measures**  
   - Use the minimum level of control necessary to protect society, staff, and offenders.

4. **Alternatives to Custody**  
   - Promote alternatives such as community-based sentences, electronic monitoring, or conditional supervision (as per sections 29 and 81).

5. **Effective Rehabilitation Programs**  
   - Deliver correctional, educational, vocational, and volunteer programs to support rehabilitation and reduce recidivism.

6. **Offender Rights**  
   - Offenders retain all rights of citizens **except those legally and necessarily removed due to their sentence**.

7. **Public Involvement**  
   - Encourage public participation in Service operations.

8. **Fair and Transparent Decisions**  
   - Correctional decisions must be fair, with access to an effective grievance process.

9. **Equity and Inclusivity**  
   - Policies must respect gender, ethnicity, culture, religion, language, sexual orientation, and gender identity.  
   - Must respond to the special needs of women, Indigenous persons, visible minorities, and those with mental health needs.

10. **Offender Responsibility**  
    - Offenders must obey rules and participate actively in programs aimed at rehabilitation and reintegration.

11. **Staff Support and Development**  
    - Staff must be properly selected, trained, and supported with:
      - Career development opportunities  
      - Good working conditions (no practices that undermine dignity)  
      - Opportunities to contribute to policy and program design

### üè¢ **5. Structure and Operations of the Correctional Service**

#### ‚úÖ **Continuity of the Service**
- The **Correctional Service of Canada (Service)** continues as the national correctional agency responsible for:
  - Care and custody of inmates  
  - Delivery of rehabilitation programs  
  - Preparation for release  
  - Parole, statutory release, and long-term supervision  
  - Public education about the Service  
  - Implementation of agreements with the Canada Border Services Agency (under section 94.3)

#### ‚úÖ **Appointment of the Commissioner**
- The Governor in Council may appoint a **Commissioner of Corrections**.
- The Commissioner operates under the direction of the **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness**.
- Has control and management over all aspects of the Service.

#### ‚úÖ **Headquarters and Regional Offices**
- National headquarters and Commissioner‚Äôs offices are located in the **National Capital Region** (as per the Schedule to the National Capital Act).
- The Commissioner may establish **regional headquarters**.

#### ‚úÖ **Declaration of Penitentiaries**
- The Commissioner may, by order, declare any prison or hospital as a **penitentiary** for any person or class of persons.
- The Governor in Council may also declare a place as a penitentiary.
- **Provincial Approval Required**: No provincial prison, hospital, or facility under provincial jurisdiction may be declared a penitentiary **without approval from an officer designated by the Lieutenant Governor of that province**.

#### ‚úÖ **Evidence of Penitentiary Status**
- In court proceedings, a **certificate signed by the Commissioner** stating the location or description of a penitentiary is **admissible in evidence** and **deems to be proof** of that status, unless contradicted by evidence.

#### ‚úÖ **Lawful Custody of Inmates**
- A person who is an inmate under subsection (b)(ii) of the definition in section 2 is **deemed to be in lawful custody** of the Service.
