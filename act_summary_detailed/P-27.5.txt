**Detailed Summary of the *Public Complaints and Review Commission Act***  

---

### **Overview**

The *Public Complaints and Review Commission Act* establishes the **Public Complaints and Review Commission (Commission)** as an independent body responsible for investigating and reviewing complaints related to the conduct of officers and employees of two key federal law enforcement agencies: the **Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)** and the **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)**. The Act provides a comprehensive framework for the Commission’s structure, mandate, operational procedures, access to information, service standards, and protections for its members.

This legislation is designed to enhance accountability, transparency, and public trust in the operations of the RCMP and CBSA by creating a formal, accessible, and impartial mechanism for handling complaints about misconduct, poor treatment, or service delivery during detention or policing activities.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Title and Purpose**
- The Act is known as the **Public Complaints and Review Commission Act**.
- Its primary purpose is to establish a **public complaints and review mechanism** for individuals who have been detained or otherwise affected by the operations of the RCMP and CBSA.

---

### **2. Definitions (Core Terms)**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Agency** | Refers to the **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)**. |
| **CBSA employee** | Any officer or employee of the Agency, including those who assist in carrying out duties under the *Canada Border Services Agency Act*, **except** those who assist solely under agreements referenced in subsection 13(3) of that Act. |
| **Child** | A person under the age of 18, unless evidence suggests otherwise. |
| **Commission** | The independent body established under subsection 3(1), consisting of a Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, and up to three other members. |
| **Commissioner** | Refers to the **Commissioner of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police** (a senior official). |
| **Employee of the Agency** | Includes any person assisting the Agency in performing its powers or duties under the CBSA Act, **excluding** those assisting only under specific contractual arrangements. |
| **Minister** | The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness**. |
| **President** | The **President of the CBSA**. |
| **Proceedings** | Any investigation or hearing conducted by the Commission in response to a complaint under Part 2 or 3. |
| **Program legislation** | Defined as in *section 2 of the CBSA Act*, meaning laws governing the operations of the CBSA and RCMP (e.g., immigration, border control, policing). |
| **RCMP** | The **Royal Canadian Mounted Police**. |
| **RCMP employee** | Any member (as defined in the *RCMP Act*) or person appointed under Part I of that Act. |

> **Note**: A person designated under subsection 9(2) of the CBSA Act is treated as an officer or employee of the Agency for the purposes of this Act, even if not formally employed.  
> Similarly, decisions made by an RCMP employee regarding service levels are deemed to be conduct under the *RCMP Act*, even if made by a provincial minister.

---

### **3. Establishment and Composition of the Commission**

- The Commission consists of:
  - **One Chairperson**
  - **One Vice-Chairperson**
  - **Up to three additional members**
- Appointments are made by the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet).

#### **Appointment Guidelines**
- The **Minister of Public Safety** must ensure that appointments reflect **diversity in Canadian society**, including:
  - Gender equality
  - Overrepresentation in the criminal justice system (particularly Indigenous peoples and Black persons)
- **Eligibility Requirements**:
  - Cannot be a member of the RCMP (as defined in *RCMP Act*).
  - Cannot be an officer under the *Customs Act* or a person designated as an officer under the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act* who interacts with the public.
  - Must be a **Canadian citizen or permanent resident** (as defined in the *IRPA*).

#### **Term and Removal**
- Members serve for a **maximum of five years**.
- Can be **reappointed** after their term ends.
- May be **removed at any time by the Governor in Council for cause**.

#### **Work Status and Compensation**
- **Chairperson**: Full-time member with **deputy head of department rank** and authority over all Commission activities.
- Other members: Can be **full-time or part-time**.
- **Remuneration** is set by the Governor in Council.
- Members are entitled to **reimbursement** for travel, living, and other expenses incurred while performing duties.
- Full-time members are deemed **public servants** under the *Public Service Superannuation Act*.
- All members are considered **federal public administration employees** under the *Government Employees Compensation Act* and *Aeronautics Act*.

#### **Succession and Leadership**
- In the absence or incapacity of the Chairperson, the **Vice-Chairperson** assumes all powers and duties.
- If the Vice-Chairperson is also absent or vacant, the **Minister may authorize another member** to act as Chairperson for up to **90 days**, subject to **Governor in Council approval**.

---

### **4. Office and Operations**

- The **head office** must be located in Canada, with a designated location set by the Governor in Council. If no location is specified, it must be in the **National Capital Region** (as defined in the *National Capital Act*).
- The Commission may establish **regional offices** across Canada to improve access and responsiveness.

#### **Staffing**
- Officers and employees necessary for the Commission’s work are appointed in accordance with the **Public Service Employment Act**.
- With **Treasury Board approval**, the Commission may:
  - Temporarily engage **specialists or experts** (e.g., legal, psychological, human rights) to advise on matters.
  - Fix and pay their remuneration and expenses.

---

### **5. Mandate and Powers**

The Commission is empowered to:
- Exercise all powers and perform all duties assigned under this Act.
- Establish **service standards** jointly with:
  - The **RCMP** and **union representatives of RCMP employees** (for reviews under s. 28(1) or s. 29).
  - The **CBSA** and **union representatives of CBSA employees** (for reviews under s. 28(2)).
- These standards must specify:
  - Time limits for reviews and complaint responses.
  - Circumstances under which time limits may be extended or do not apply.
- The Commission must **publish these service standards on its official website**, particularly those related to communication with complainants.

---

### **6. Public Education and Research**

- The Commission must:
  - Implement **public education and information programs** to raise awareness of its mandate.
  - Conduct **research**, consult with stakeholders (individuals, organizations, both domestic and international), and cooperate in matters related to its mandate.

---

### **7. Rules and Procedures**

- The Commission may make **rules** on matters such as:
  - Sittings and meeting schedules
  - Quorum requirements
  - Procedures for handling business
  - Work apportionment among members
  - General performance of duties
- Before adopting any rule:
  - A **copy must be published in the Canada Gazette**.
  - A **reasonable opportunity** must be given for interested parties to make representations.
  - A rule may be proposed only once, even if amended.

> **Note**: Rules are not subject to parliamentary scrutiny but are subject to public consultation.

---

### **8. Immunity and Legal Protection**

- **No criminal, civil, or administrative action** may be brought against:
  - Members, officers, or employees of the Commission
  - Any person acting on behalf or under direction of the Commission
  - For anything done, reported, or said in **good faith** in the exercise of powers or performance of duties under this Act.

> **Exception**: This protection does **not apply** to actions taken in connection with a prosecution for:
> - An offence under this Act
> - An offence under the *Security of Information Act*
> - An offence under sections 132 or 136 of the *Criminal Code*

> **Observer Status**: A person designated as an observer under:
> - Section 45.83(3) or 45.98 of the *RCMP Act*
> - Section 14.5 of the *CBSA Act*
> is **deemed to be acting on behalf of the Commission** and thus entitled to immunity.

---

### **9. Witness Privilege and Non-Compellability**

- Members, officers, or employees of the Commission are **not competent or compellable witnesses** in any proceeding **outside** of:
  - Prosecutions for offences under this Act
  - Prosecutions under the *Security of Information Act*
  - Prosecutions under sections 132 or 136 of the *Criminal Code*

> This means they **cannot be required to testify** in court about matters known through their Commission work.

---
