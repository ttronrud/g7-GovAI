**Detailed Summary of the *Intelligence Commissioner Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Intelligence Commissioner Act* establishes a new federal office — the **Intelligence Commissioner** — to provide **independent oversight** of key intelligence and security authorizations in Canada. The Act is designed to ensure **transparency, accountability, and reasonableness** in decisions made by intelligence agencies, particularly those involving surveillance, data access, and foreign intelligence operations. It balances the need for national security with democratic accountability and legal safeguards.

The Act is structured around the appointment, powers, duties, and responsibilities of the Intelligence Commissioner, including authority over human resources, review of intelligence authorizations, and reporting obligations to Parliament.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Name and Citation**
- The Act is officially titled the **Intelligence Commissioner Act**.
- It may be cited as such in legal and administrative contexts.

---

### **2. Definition of Key Terms**
- **Commissioner**: Refers to the individual appointed under subsection 4(1) of the Act.
- **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet): Has broad authority to designate a minister as the **Minister responsible** for the Act, though this is not a direct role in day-to-day operations.
- **Retired Judge**: The Commissioner must be a retired judge of a superior court (e.g., Supreme Court or Court of Appeal), ensuring judicial independence and impartiality.

---

### **3. Appointment of the Commissioner**
- **Appointment Process**:
  - The Governor in Council, **on the recommendation of the Prime Minister**, appoints a retired superior court judge as the Intelligence Commissioner.
  - The term of office is **not more than five years**.
- **Reappointment**:
  - A person may be reappointed for **one additional term** of up to five years.
- **Part-Time Basis**:
  - The Commissioner serves on a **part-time basis**, indicating a role focused on oversight rather than active intelligence operations.
- **Remuneration**:
  - The Commissioner is paid a fixed salary set by the Governor in Council.
  - In addition, they are entitled to **reasonable travel and living expenses** incurred while performing duties, as per Treasury Board directives.

---

### **4. Status and Employment Rights**
- The Commissioner is:
  - **Deemed an employee** under the *Government Employees Compensation Act*.
  - **Employed in the federal public administration** for the purposes of regulations under section 9 of the *Aeronautics Act*.
- This status ensures access to employee benefits, compensation, and administrative protections.

---

### **5. Acting Commissioner**
- In the event of:
  - Absence,
  - Incapacity, or
  - Vacancy of the office,
  - The Governor in Council may appoint a **retired superior court judge** to **act as Commissioner**.
- The acting commissioner has **full powers, duties, and functions** of the Commissioner under this Act or any other Act of Parliament.

---

### **6. Authority and Rank**
- The Commissioner has the **rank and powers of a deputy head of a department**.
- This includes:
  - **Control and management** of their office and all matters connected to it.
  - **Exclusive authority** over:
    - Appointment or lay-off of employees;
    - Termination of employment (not for cause);
    - Setting standards and procedures for staffing, including recruitment, retention, and performance management.

> **Note**: This authority is **not restricted by the Federal Public Sector Labour Relations Act**, ensuring the Commissioner can manage human resources independently.

---

### **7. Human Resources Management Powers**
The Commissioner may, with consultation with the President of the Treasury Board, determine and regulate:
- **Human resource needs** and allocation;
- **Position classifications**;
- **Pay, hours, and leave** for employees;
- **Travel and expense reimbursements and allowances**;
- **Learning, training, and development** programs;
- **Performance awards** (e.g., for excellence or innovation);
- **Disciplinary standards** (including suspension, demotion, or termination for non-disciplinary reasons);
- **Termination or demotion** without misconduct;
- **Policies** on human resource management;
- Any other matters deemed necessary for effective HR management.

> This gives the Commissioner **broad discretion** in managing staff, including setting internal policies on pay, conditions, and performance.

---

### **8. Collective Bargaining Requirements**
- Before entering into **collective bargaining** with a bargaining agent for employees, the Commissioner must obtain **approval from the President of the Treasury Board**.
- This ensures that major labor negotiations are subject to cabinet-level oversight and alignment with broader public service policies.

---

### **9. Engagement of Specialists**
- The Commissioner may **temporarily engage experts** with technical or specialized knowledge (e.g., cybersecurity, data science) to assist in decision-making.
- These individuals may be paid and compensated with **Treasury Board approval**.

---

### **10. Oath and Compliance Obligations**
Upon taking office, the Commissioner must:
- **Oath or affirmation**:
  - All employees and contracted persons serving under the Commissioner must maintain **security clearances** required by the Government of Canada.
- **Secure Handling of Information**:
  - Must follow established procedures (including Treasury Board policies) for the **secure handling, storage, transportation, and transmission** of information.
- **Non-Disclosure of Sensitive Information**:
  - The Commissioner, former Commissioner, employees, or contractors may **only disclose information** obtained in the course of their duties:
    - For the purpose of exercising those powers or performing duties; or
    - As required by another law.
  - **No disclosure for personal or external purposes**.

> This provision ensures information is not misused or leaked outside of official oversight.

---

### **11. Review of Intelligence Authorizations**
This is the **core function** of the Intelligence Commissioner — reviewing and approving authorizations issued by intelligence agencies.

#### **Review Scope (Under Sections 13–20)**

| Authorization Type | Review Trigger | Reasonableness Assessment |
|-------------------|----------------|----------------------------|
| **Foreign Intelligence Authorization (FIA)** | Under subsections 34(1) & (2) of the *CSE Act* | Must review whether conclusions are reasonable before approving the FIA |
| **Cybersecurity Authorization (CSA)** | Under subsections 34(1) & (3) of the *CSE Act* | Must assess reasonableness of conclusions before approving CSA |
| **Amendments to CSE authorizations** (Section 13 or 14) | Under paragraphs 39(2)(a) & (b) of the *CSE Act* | Must determine if the conclusion is reasonable |
| **Class of Canadian datasets** | Under subsection 11.03(2) of the *CSIS Act* | Must assess reasonableness of classification decision |
| **Retention of foreign datasets** | Under subsection 11.17(1) of the *CSIS Act* | Must assess reasonableness of retention authorization |
| **Query/exploitation of datasets in exigent circumstances** | Under subsection 11.22(1) of the *CSIS Act* | Must assess reasonableness of authorization |
| **Class of acts or omissions** | Under subsection 20.1(3) of the *CSIS Act* | Must assess reasonableness of determination |

> All reviews are based on whether the **conclusions** made by the intelligence agency are **reasonable** — not on whether they are legally valid or politically acceptable.

---

### **12. Decision-Making Process**
After review, the Commissioner must issue a **written decision**:
- **If the conclusions are reasonable**:
  - Approve the authorization, amendment, or determination;
  - Provide **clear reasons** for approval.
- **If the conclusions are not reasonable**:
  - Reject the authorization;
  - Provide **clear reasons** for rejection.

#### **Special Case – Foreign Dataset Authorization (Section 17)**
- If the authorization involves **querying or exploiting a foreign dataset** or **retention/destruction of data**, the Commissioner may:
  - Approve with **conditions** (e.g., time limits, scope restrictions);
  - Provide reasons for the conditions;
  - Or reject the authorization entirely.

> This allows for **proportionality and accountability** in data use.

---

### **13. Timeliness and Delivery of Decisions**
- **For authorizations under Section 18** (e.g., FIA/CSA): Decision must be provided **as soon as feasible**.
- **For all other authorizations, amendments, or determinations**: Decision must be provided **within 30 days** of receiving notice, or within any agreed-upon period.

> Ensures timely oversight and prevents indefinite delays.

---

### **14. Non-Statutory Nature of Decisions**
- A Commissioner’s decision is **not a statutory instrument** under the *Statutory Instruments Act*.
- This means it does **not require parliamentary approval** or public notice as a statutory instrument would, but is instead a **binding administrative decision**.

---

### **15. Reporting to Parliament**
- The Commissioner must submit an **annual report** to the **Prime Minister**.
- The report must include:
  - Statistics on:
    - Authorizations approved;
    - Authorizations not approved;
    - Amendments or determinations reviewed.
- The report must be **prepared in consultation** with:
  - The **Director of CSIS**;
  - The **Chief of CSE**.
- **Sensitive information** (e.g., national security, defence, international relations, litigation privilege)
