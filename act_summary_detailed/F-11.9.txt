### **Detailed Summary of the *First Nations Oil and Gas and Moneys Management Act***

---

#### **1. Purpose and Context of the Act**

The *First Nations Oil and Gas and Moneys Management Act* (FNOGMMA) is a landmark piece of federal legislation designed to **empower First Nations** — specifically the **White Bear, Blood, and Siksika (Blackfoot)** — to **assume greater control over oil and gas exploration, exploitation, and the revenues generated** from such activities on their reserve lands.

This Act is rooted in:

- **Historical treaties**:  
  - The **White Bear First Nation** entered into **Treaty No. 4** on September 23, 1875.  
  - The **Blood and Siksika First Nations** entered into **Treaty No. 7** on September 22, 1877.  
  These treaties established early relationships between Indigenous peoples and the Crown, acknowledging traditional rights and land use.

- **Section 35 of the *Constitution Act, 1982***:  
  This constitutional provision recognizes and affirms **aboriginal and treaty rights**, including the right to self-governance and control over natural resources.

The Act aims to **realize those rights** by enabling First Nations to manage oil and gas operations and associated revenues directly, rather than having such decisions and financial benefits managed solely by the federal government.

---

#### **2. Key Principles and Objectives**

- **Self-Determination**:  
  First Nations are granted the right to **manage oil and gas exploration and exploitation** on their reserve lands, including making decisions about development, environmental assessments, and operations.

- **Financial Autonomy**:  
  First Nations can **receive and control revenues** (called "oil and gas moneys") that would otherwise be held by the federal government for their benefit.

- **Co-Management Background**:  
  Since 1994, these First Nations have been involved in co-managing oil and gas activities with the Government of Canada. This Act formalizes and expands that partnership into full control.

- **Potential for Broader Application**:  
  The legislation is structured so that **other First Nations may also benefit** if they choose to participate, promoting a model of equitable resource governance across Canada.

---

#### **3. Definitions (Key Terms)**

The Act provides clear definitions to ensure legal clarity:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Contract** | Includes licenses, permits, leases, easements, or rights-of-way related to oil or gas exploration/exploitation. |
| **Decision-Making Authority** | Refers to individuals or bodies empowered to approve projects under First Nation laws or Section 34 of the *Indian Act*. |
| **Environmental Assessment** | Conducted in stages under First Nation oil and gas laws to evaluate environmental impacts. |
| **Exploration** | Includes surveying, mapping, test drilling, and geophysical/geochemical studies. |
| **Exploitation** | Extraction, production, storage, refining, or use of oil/gas for energy. |
| **First Nation** | A band as defined in the *Indian Act*. |
| **First Nation Member** | A person listed on the band list or entitled to be listed. |
| **Gas** | Natural gas (including coal bed methane), condensate, and non-oil components. |
| **Managed Area** | Lands set apart as reserves, as of the transfer date, and any lands added later (unless otherwise agreed). |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Indigenous Services. |
| **Oil** | Any hydrocarbon extractable from surface/subsurface deposits (excluding condensate). |
| **Oil and Gas Law** | Laws made by a First Nation under Section 35 of the *Constitution Act, 1982*. |
| **Oil and Gas Moneys** | Revenues from exploration/exploitation after the transfer date, including fines. |
| **Project** | Any proposed construction, operation, modification, decommissioning, or exploration activity requiring environmental assessment. |
| **Transfer Date** | The date a First Nation’s name is added to Schedule 1 under Section 22(1). |
| **Financial Institution / Trust Company** | Banks or trust companies meeting regulatory criteria. |

> *Note:* "Environment" and "environmental effects" follow definitions from the *Impact Assessment Act*.

#### **4. Key Provisions and Procedures**

##### **Section 6: Request for Transfer or Payment**
- A First Nation’s **council may submit a written resolution** to the Minister requesting:
  1. Transfer of management and regulation of oil and gas in reserve lands.
  2. Payment of moneys currently held by Her Majesty (Canada) for the First Nation’s benefit — both existing and future.

##### **Section 7: Minister’s Obligations Upon Request**
Upon receiving a resolution, the Minister must provide:
- Copies of all **active contracts** in the First Nation’s reserve lands.
- Documents showing **designations** of lands under the *Indian Act*.
- Details of **moneys payable** under those contracts.
- Information about **contaminated sites** on reserve lands.

> **Exclusions**:
> - Information protected by **solicitor-client privilege**.
> - Information required to be withheld under **federal laws** (e.g., privacy, national security).
> - Information provided by a **contract holder** is exempt from disclosure under subsection (4).

> The Minister must also provide a **timetable** for delivering the documents.

---

##### **Section 10: Pre-Vote Requirements – Oil and Gas Code**
Before a vote on transfer can occur, the First Nation must prepare and adopt an **oil and gas code** that includes:

1. **Procedure for making, amending, and publishing oil and gas laws**.
2. **Accountability mechanisms** for the council’s management of oil and gas.
3. **Conflict-of-interest policies** for council members and employees.
4. **Coordination mechanisms** if the First Nation shares a reserve with another First Nation.
5. **Amendment process** for the code.

> This ensures transparency, governance, and community oversight.

---

##### **Section 11: Pre-Vote Requirements – Financial Code**
Before a vote on payment of moneys, the First Nation must prepare a **financial code** that specifies:

1. **Mode of holding moneys**:
   - Deposit in a financial institution OR
   - Payment into a trust (with First Nation as settlor and sole beneficiary).
2. **Conditions for changing modes** (e.g., from trust to account).
3. **Manner of collecting and spending moneys**.
4. **Accountability to members**.
5. **Conflict-of-interest procedures**.
6. **Amendment process**.

> The financial code ensures responsible stewardship and financial transparency.

---

##### **Section 15 & 16: Transfer and Payment Agreements**
- After preparing the codes, the **Minister and First Nation may enter into**:
  - A **Transfer Agreement** (Section 15): for management and regulation of oil and gas.
  - A **Payment Agreement** (Section 16): for receipt of moneys.

> These agreements define the terms, responsibilities, and governance structures.

---

##### **Section 17 & 18: Ratification by Vote**
- After agreements are signed, the First Nation may hold a **voter-approved vote** on:
  - Ratification of the oil and gas and financial codes.
  - Approval of the transfer or payment.

> **Eligible Voters**:
> - Must be on the band list.
> - Must be **18 years or older** on the date of the vote.
> - For First Nations not under Section 74 of the *Indian Act*, eligible voters must also meet the **customary age** for council elections.

> **Affirmative Vote Criteria**:
> - A **majority of participating voters** must vote "yes", **or**
> - A **majority of registered voters** who express intent to participate vote "yes".
> - **More than 25% of all eligible voters** must vote affirmatively.
> - The council may **increase the threshold** (e.g., raise the percentage) by resolution before the vote.

---

##### **Section 23: Transfer Date and Assignment of Rights**
- Once a vote is affirmative, the **Minister adds the First Nation’s name to Schedule 1** (the list of eligible First Nations).
- On the **transfer date**, all rights and obligations under **existing oil and gas contracts** are **transferred to the First Nation**.
- The Minister must **notify all contract holders** of this assignment.

> **Key Legal Effects**:
> - Designations under the *Indian Act* regarding oil and gas **cease to have effect** after the transfer date.
> - The First Nation assumes full rights and responsibilities under this Act.
> - **No impairment** of contract holders’ rights under new laws (Section 24).
> - Contracts remain valid and enforceable.

> **Exception**: If a designation includes rights unrelated to oil and gas, only the oil/gas portion is superseded.

---

##### **Section 25: Definition of Managed Area**
- The **managed area** consists of:
  - Lands set apart as reserves **as of the transfer date**.
  - Any lands **added later**, unless otherwise agreed.
- Lands surveyed under the *Canada Lands Surveys Act* may be **excluded** if the transfer agreement allows it.
- Any exclusion can be **reversed with mutual agreement** between the First Nation and the Minister.
