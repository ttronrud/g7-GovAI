**Detailed Summary of the *Injured Military Members Compensation Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Injured Military Members Compensation Act* establishes a statutory framework for providing financial compensation to members of the Canadian Armed Forces who suffer injuries during service â€” whether in the regular force or in the reserve forces â€” that result in specific physical or sensory losses. The Act is designed to ensure that eligible service members and their estates receive timely, fair, and equitable compensation based on clear criteria, while also protecting the integrity of the claims process and respecting privacy and administrative efficiency.

The Act was enacted by Her Majesty in Right of Canada, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons, and may be cited as the *Injured Military Members Compensation Act*.

### **3. Who Is Eligible for Compensation?**

#### **(a) Eligibility for Class B or C Reserve Service Members**
A person who:
- Was serving in the **regular force**, or in the **reserve force** performing:
  - **Class B Reserve Service for more than 180 days**, or
  - **Class C Reserve Service**,
- Suffered an injury resulting in a loss listed in **Column 1 of the Schedule** during the corresponding period in **Column 2**,
- And meets all of the following conditions:
  1. The injury is **attributable to service with the Canadian Forces**;
  2. The loss resulted **directly from the injury**;
  3. The loss was **not caused by self-inflicted injury** (including insanity or improper conduct such as wilful disobedience or criminal behavior);
  4. The person **survived at least 30 days after the injury**;
  5. The loss occurred **within 90 days of the injury**;
  6. The person was **not entitled to a lump-sum payment** under a Government of Canada insurance plan (e.g., the Veteransâ€™ Health Insurance Plan).

### **4. Caps on Total Benefits**
To prevent excessive payouts and ensure fiscal responsibility:
- For individuals referred to in **subsection 4(1)** (longer-term reserve service): **Maximum total benefit = $250,000**
- For individuals referred to in **subsection 4(2)** (shorter-term reserve service): **Maximum total benefit = $100,000**

> These caps apply to **all losses resulting from a single injury**, regardless of how many separate losses occur.

---

### **5. Exclusions from Compensation**
No benefit is payable if the loss results from:
- Any **disease**, **bodily or mental infirmity**, or **medical treatment** for such conditions;
- **Ptomaines or bacterial infections**, **except** those that are **directly caused by the injury**.

> This exclusion protects against claims arising from pre-existing medical conditions or infections unrelated to service.

---

### **6. Application Process**
- Any eligible person may apply to the Minister for benefits in the manner required by the Minister.
- If the person **died before the Act came into force**, or **died after the Act came into force without making an application**, their **estate or legal successor** may apply and has the same rights and obligations as the deceased individual.

---

### **7. Ministerâ€™s Obligations in Reviewing Applications**
The Minister must:
- **Consider applications without delay**.
- Investigate the facts and gather relevant information from government departments and agencies.
- May require the applicant to provide:
  - Documentation to substantiate the claim;
  - Relevant personal or medical information;
  - An **affidavit or statutory declaration** confirming the truth of the information.

> The Minister has the authority to access records from various federal departments and agencies as needed.

---

### **8. Decision-Making Principles**
When making decisions, the Minister must:
- Draw **every reasonable inference in favour of the applicant**;
- Accept **uncontradicted evidence** as credible;
- Resolve **any doubt in favour of the applicant** in the weighing of evidence.

> This creates a strong presumption of fairness and protects applicants from being disadvantaged due to procedural or evidentiary gaps.

---

### **9. Review of Decisions**
- An applicant dissatisfied with a Ministerial decision may **request a review**.
- The Minister may also **initiate a review on their own motion**.
- A review may result in:
  - Confirmation of the original decision;
  - Amendment or rescission of the decision **if**:
    - There was an **error in fact-finding**;
    - There was a **misinterpretation of the law**;
    - **New evidence** is presented.

> The review process is **informal and expeditious**, balancing fairness with administrative efficiency.

---

### **10. Finality of Decisions**
- Decisions of the Minister regarding reviews are **final and binding**.
- They are **not subject to appeal or judicial review** under the *Federal Courts Act*, except in limited circumstances (e.g., where a constitutional or legal error is involved).
- The decision **does not constitute a grievance** under Section 29 of the *National Defence Act*.

> This limits the avenues for appeal, emphasizing administrative finality and reducing legal overhead.

---

### **11. Representation and Legal Rights**
- An applicant may, at their own expense, be represented by a **chosen representative** (e.g., lawyer or advocate) in the process.

---

### **12. Access to Personal Information**
The Minister may access certain personal information from other government agencies to assess eligibility:

- From the **Department of Veterans Affairs** (under the *Pension Act* or *Veterans Well-being Act*);
- From the **Department of Transport** (in relation to *Aeronautics Act*, *Canada Shipping Act*, or related legislation);
- From the **Library and Archives of Canada** (under its own Act).

> This allows for cross-agency verification of medical, service, or administrative records.

---

### **13. Disclosure of Information**
Personal information collected under this Act may be disclosed to:
- Any person or body **only if necessary** to administer the Act;
- Any officer or employee of the **Department of National Defence** or **Canadian Forces**, if required for administration.

> Disclosure is strictly limited and governed by necessity.

---
