### ðŸ”¹ **7. Enforcement Powers**
The Act grants **strong enforcement powers** to park wardens and enforcement officers, modeled on peace officer authority.

#### Powers Include:
- **Arrest without warrant** if:
  - A person is committing or about to commit an offence under the Act.
  - There are reasonable grounds to believe an offence has occurred.
- **Enter and search private property** with a warrant (or in exigent circumstances).
- **Seize items** related to offences (e.g., fishing gear, illegal wildlife products).
- **Obtain search warrants** via ex parte application by a justice of the peace if there are reasonable grounds to believe:
  - An animal, fish, or hunting device is present.
  - Evidence of an offence exists.

> These powers are limited to **activities under the Act or regulations**.

#### Seizure and Custody:
- Seized items are held by the warden or officer.
- Under **sections 489.1 and 490 of the Criminal Code**, the seized item may be forfeited if ownership cannot be established within 30 days.
- **Perishable items** (e.g., fish) may be destroyed, and proceeds go to the owner unless proceedings are started within 90 days.

#### Liability for Seizures:
- The **owner**, person in charge, or possessor at the time of seizure are **jointly and severally liable** for all costs of seizure, detention, and forfeiture (beyond proceeds).
