### **Detailed Summary of the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Superannuation Act***

---

#### **1. Title and Purpose**
The *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Superannuation Act* establishes the superannuation (pension) framework for members of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP), ensuring financial security upon retirement or death. It replaces and modernizes the previous pension system, providing clarity on contributions, pensionable service, eligibility, and benefits.

This Act is formally cited as the **Royal Canadian Mounted Police Superannuation Act** and is designed to provide equitable treatment for both male and female contributors.

---

#### **2. Equality and Rights of Contributors**
- **Equality of Status and Rights**: Male and female contributors have **equal status and equal rights and obligations** under the Act.
- This reflects a commitment to gender equity in the RCMP’s pension system, ensuring no discrimination based on gender.

---

#### **3. Key Definitions (Definitions Section)**
The Act provides precise definitions of key terms used throughout:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Active Service** | Service specified in regulations as active; terminates upon discharge or release from a veterans’ hospital. |
| **Child** | A biological, stepchild, or adopted child who was dependent on the contributor at the time of their death. |
| **Contributor** | A person required to contribute to the Superannuation Account or RCMP Pension Fund. Includes those who have ceased contributing or who were previously covered under the old Act and now receive a pension or have died. |
| **Disabled** (except in Part II) | Capable of performing duties as a member of the Force; for individuals, incapable of pursuing any substantially gainful occupation. |
| **Force** | Refers to the Royal Canadian Mounted Police. |
| **Former Act** | The *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act*, Chapter 241, as it existed before April 1, 1960, including other laws providing pensions based on length of service. |
| **Member of the Force** | Includes commissioned officers and other members designated by regulations. |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness. |
| **Officer** | A commissioned officer in the RCMP. |
| **Pay** | For members of the Force: includes base pay, allowances, and compensation as prescribed. For public service or Canadian Forces: salary and allowances under applicable superannuation laws. |
| **Provincial Pension Plan** | Same meaning as in the Canada Pension Plan. |
| **Public Service** | As defined in the *Public Service Superannuation Act*. |
| **Regular Force** | Includes pre-1968 components of the Canadian Armed Forces (e.g., Royal Canadian Navy, Canadian Army, etc.). |
| **Retirement Age** | Age set by regulations for a given rank or class. |
| **RCMP Pension Fund** | Fund established under section 29.2. |
| **RCMP Superannuation Investment Fund** | Fund established under section 29.1 for investment of pension assets. |

| **Supplementary Benefit** | Additional benefit payable under Part III (e.g., survivor or disability benefits). |

---

#### **4. Public Service Employment and Service Recognition**
- A person employed under the authority of the *RCMP Act* but not as a member of the Force is **deemed to be in the public service**.
- Any service during which they were employed under the RCMP Act (but not a member) or during which they were subject to Part VII of the former Act is **deemed to be public service** for pension purposes.

> **Exception**: Employment as a member of the Force is **not excepted** from the Canada Pension Plan (CPP) unless otherwise specified.

---

#### **5. Annuity and Benefit Structure**
- **General Principle**: Upon ceasing to be a member of the Force or upon death, every contributor is entitled to an annuity or other benefit **based on the number of years of pensionable service**.
- The benefit is **not subject to reduction** unless otherwise specified in the Act or regulations.

---

#### **6. Continuation of the Former Superannuation Account**
- The **RCMP Superannuation Account** established under the former Act is **continued** under the new name: *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Superannuation Account*.

---

#### **7. Contribution Requirements (Effective January 1, 2013)**

##### **(a) General Contribution Obligation**
- All members of the Force are required to contribute to the **RCMP Pension Fund** from their pay (by reservation or otherwise) at rates set by the **Treasury Board**, jointly recommended by:
  - The President of the Treasury Board
  - The Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness

##### **(b) Exception for 35+ Years of Pensionable Service**
- A person who has **at least 35 years of pensionable service** on or after January 1, 2013 is **not required** to contribute under section 5(1).
- However, they **must contribute** to the Pension Fund from the **later of**:
  - January 1, 2013
  - The date they accumulate 35 years of pensionable service
- This contribution is in addition to any other required contributions under the Act.

##### **(c) Contribution Rate Cap**
- Contribution rates **cannot exceed** those paid by **Group 1 contributors** under the *Public Service Superannuation Act* (as defined in subsection 12(0.1) of that Act).

##### **(d) Limit on Annual Pay Contribution**
- No contribution may be made in respect of any portion of the annual rate of pay that exceeds the **annual rate of pay fixed or determined by regulations**.
- This prevents over-contribution, especially in cases of significant salary increases.

##### **(e) Contribution for Part-Time Service**
- A member working **fewer than a prescribed number of hours per week or days per year** (as set in regulations) is **not required** to contribute.

---

#### **8. Pensionable Service: What Can Be Counted?**

Pensionable service is categorized into two types:

---

##### **(A) Non-Elective Service**
- **For contributors who were under Part V of the former Act before April 1, 1960**:
  - Any period of service that would have been countable for pension, allowance, or gratuity **if they had retired** at that time.
  - **Excludes** periods for which they elected to pay (i.e., paid voluntarily to count service).
- **For all contributors**:
  - Periods during which they were required to contribute to the Superannuation Account or the RCMP Pension Fund (as of December 31, 2012).
  - Periods covered under **section 23** (service in certain public roles).
  - Periods covered under **subsection 24.1(9)** (service in police roles counted as pensionable).

---

##### **(B) Elective Service**
Contributors can **choose to pay** for certain periods of service to make them countable. These include:

1. **Service before April 1, 1960** under Part V of the former Act:
   - Periods for which they elected to pay.
   - Periods for which they *might have elected* to pay, if they choose within prescribed time limits.

2. **Service with Provincial or Municipal Police Forces**:
   - If the Minister has an arrangement under section 20 of the RCMP Act.
   - Must elect **within one year** of becoming a contributor.

3. **Service during World War I or II**:
   - Must elect **within one year** of becoming a contributor.

4. **Service in the Canadian Army Special Force (P.C. 3860, August 7, 1950)**:
   - Must elect within one year of becoming a contributor.

5. **Service in the Regular Force**:
   - Must elect within one year of becoming a contributor.

6. **Continuous full-time service (6 months or more)** in:
   - Canadian Forces (non-regular)
   - Navy, Army, or Air Forces (non-regular)
   - Must elect within one year.

7. **Service not required under the former Act**:
   - If the contributor elects within one year of becoming a contributor.

8. **Public Service Employment**:
   - Full-time employment with federal public service (after December 31, 1980).
   - Must elect within one year of becoming a contributor.
