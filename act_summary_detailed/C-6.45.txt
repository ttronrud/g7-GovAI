**Detailed Summary of the Canada–Jordan Economic Growth and Prosperity Act**

---

### **Overview**
The *Canada–Jordan Economic Growth and Prosperity Act* is a federal legislation enacted by the Parliament of Canada to implement and operationalize the **Free Trade Agreement (FTA)** between Canada and the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan, signed on June 28, 2009. This Act also incorporates provisions from two related agreements:  
- The **Agreement on the Environment**  
- The **Agreement on Labour Cooperation**  

The Act establishes the legal framework through which Canada fulfills its obligations under these trade and cooperation agreements, promoting economic growth, environmental protection, labor rights, and sustainable development between Canada and Jordan.

---

### **Key Definitions and Scope**

1. **Agreement**  
   Refers to the **Free Trade Agreement between Canada and Jordan**, signed on June 28, 2009. This is the central instrument governing trade and economic relations.

2. **Federal Law**  
   Encompasses any law passed by Parliament or any regulation, order, or administrative instrument issued under the authority of an Act of Parliament. This definition ensures that all federal legislation related to the Agreement is covered.

3. **Joint Commission**  
   A body established under **Article 13-1 of the Agreement**, responsible for overseeing the implementation and administration of the FTA and related agreements. It serves as a platform for Canada and Jordan to resolve disputes, monitor progress, and ensure compliance.

4. **Minister**  
   The **Minister for International Trade** is designated as the principal representative of Canada on the Joint Commission. This role grants the Minister significant authority in international negotiations and coordination.

5. **Related Agreements**  
   Includes:
   - The **Agreement on the Environment** (signed June 28, 2009)
   - The **Agreement on Labour Cooperation** (signed June 28, 2009)

These agreements are integral to the overall objectives of the Act, particularly in areas of environmental protection and labor standards.

---

### **Interpretation Principles**

- **Consistency with Agreements**:  
  The Act and any federal law implementing its provisions must be interpreted in a manner consistent with the Agreement or related agreements. This ensures legal coherence and adherence to the negotiated terms.

- **Exclusion of Natural Resources**:  
  Nothing in this Act applies to **natural surface or ground water** in any physical state (liquid, gaseous, or solid). This clarifies that water resources are outside the scope of this legislation.

- **Parliamentary Authority**:  
  The Act explicitly affirms that Parliament retains full power to pass legislation to implement the Agreement or related agreements, even if such implementation is not directly addressed in this Act. This reinforces legislative sovereignty.

- **Binding Effect**:  
  The Act is binding on Her Majesty in right of Canada, meaning it has legal force and effect across all levels of government in Canada.

---

### **Purpose and Objectives**

The Act aims to achieve the following goals, as outlined in the Agreement and related agreements:

1. **Establish a Free Trade Area**  
   Remove trade barriers and create a framework for free movement of goods, services, and investment between Canada and Jordan.

2. **Promote Reciprocal Trade**  
   Expand economic relations to foster mutual economic growth and development in both countries.

3. **Support Global Trade Expansion**  
   Contribute to broader global trade by reducing trade barriers and enhancing market access.

4. **Strengthen Environmental Cooperation**  
   Enhance environmental laws and regulations, promote environmental protection, and improve collaboration on environmental issues (e.g., pollution control, biodiversity, sustainable resource use).

5. **Protect and Promote Workers’ Rights**  
   Uphold and enforce fundamental labor rights such as fair wages, safe working conditions, freedom of association, and non-discrimination. It strengthens cooperation on labor standards and supports international labor commitments.

6. **Promote Sustainable Development**  
   Encourage long-term economic, social, and environmental sustainability in both nations.

### **Legal and Procedural Provisions**

#### **1. Enforcement and Legal Action Restrictions**
- **No private litigation** can be initiated against or in favor of Canada or Jordan based solely on rights or obligations under **Part 1 of the Act** or any order made under that part.
- **No proceedings** (e.g., lawsuits, claims) may be taken without the **consent of the Attorney General of Canada** to enforce or determine any rights or obligations arising under:
  - The Agreement
  - Any related agreement
  - Any provision implemented by federal law

> *Exception*: This restriction does not apply to matters governed by the **Agreement on Labour Cooperation**, subject to **Part Three and Annex 4** of that agreement.

This provision ensures that disputes involving the Agreement are managed through diplomatic and administrative channels rather than private litigation, preserving bilateral stability and reducing legal conflict.

---

#### **2. Role of the Joint Commission**
- The **Minister for International Trade** is the **principal representative** of Canada on the Joint Commission.
- Canada will **pay its appropriate share** of all expenditures incurred by or on behalf of the Joint Commission, including administrative costs and operational expenses.

#### **3. Appointment Powers of Ministers**
Each relevant Minister has specific powers to appoint individuals to various bodies under the Agreement:

| Minister | Authority |
|--------|----------|
| **Minister for International Trade** | - Appoints representatives to committees, subcommittees, or working groups under Article 13-1 of the Agreement<br>- Appoints a panelist under Article 14-8<br>- Proposes candidates for chair of a panel under Article 14-8 |
| **Minister of Environment** | - Appoints representatives to the Environment Agreement’s committee (Article 15)<br>- Appoints a panelist under Annex I of the Environment Agreement<br>- Proposes or selects a chairperson of a panel under Annex I |
| **Minister of Labour** | - Appoints representatives to labor-related committees, working groups, or expert groups under Article 7 of the Labour Cooperation Agreement<br>- Appoints a panelist under Annex 3<br>- Proposes or selects a chairperson of a panel under Annex 3 |

These appointments allow Canada to ensure active participation in technical and policy-making bodies that oversee implementation.

---

#### **4. Administrative Support**
- The Minister is required to designate a **specific agency, division, or branch** of the Government of Canada to:
  - Facilitate the operation of **Chapter 14** of the Agreement (which deals with dispute settlement mechanisms)
  - Provide administrative support to **panels established under Chapter 14**

This ensures effective coordination and timely resolution of disputes.

---

#### **5. Funding Responsibilities**
Canada will bear the **costs or its appropriate share** of:
- Remuneration and expenses of:
  - Panel members
  - Committee members
  - Independent experts
  - Assistants to panel members
- General operating expenses of:
  - Panels
  - Committees
  - Subcommittees
  - Working groups
  - Expert groups

This financial commitment ensures the smooth functioning of implementation bodies and reflects Canada’s shared responsibility in managing the Agreement.

---

#### **6. Suspension Powers (Governor in Council)**
The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet) may issue orders to **suspend benefits** under the Agreement in accordance with **Article 14-13**, which allows for temporary suspension of trade privileges or rules when necessary. The powers include:

- Suspending rights or privileges granted to Jordan or its goods
- Modifying or suspending the application of any federal law related to Jordan or its goods
- Extending the application of federal laws to Jordan or its goods
- Taking any other measures deemed necessary

> **Duration**: Unless revoked, such orders remain in effect for the period specified in the order.

This provision provides flexibility to respond to emergencies, breaches of obligations, or unforeseen circumstances that threaten trade stability or national interests.

---

### **Effective Date and Implementation Timeline**

- **Sections 44 and 45** (specific provisions) came into force on **June 29, 2012**.
- The **remainder of the Act** (excluding sections 44 and 45) came into force on **October 1, 2012**, as per **Statutory Instrument SI/2012-71**.

This phased implementation allows for careful planning, stakeholder consultation, and institutional readiness before full enforcement.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Canada–Jordan Economic Growth and Prosperity Act* is a comprehensive legislative instrument designed to:

- Legally operationalize the Canada–Jordan Free Trade Agreement and its associated environmental and labor components  
- Establish clear roles and responsibilities for Canadian government agencies and officials  
- Promote economic integration, environmental sustainability, and labor rights  
- Ensure stable, transparent, and cooperative implementation through structured bodies like the Joint Commission and specialized panels  
- Provide mechanisms for dispute resolution and emergency intervention while protecting the integrity of bilateral relations  

By balancing economic openness with strong social and environmental safeguards, the Act reflects Canada’s commitment to **inclusive, sustainable, and equitable international trade relationships**.
