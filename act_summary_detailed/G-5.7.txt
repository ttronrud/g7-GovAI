The **Government Organization Act, Atlantic Canada, 1987** is a foundational piece of legislation that establishes the legal framework for the structure, operation, and governance of government organizations in the Atlantic provinces of Canada—specifically Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, and Newfoundland and Labrador. While the Act itself is not a comprehensive statute detailing every aspect of public administration, it serves as a key enabling law that supports the creation, regulation, and oversight of government bodies, particularly in the context of regional development and economic growth.

---

### **Summary of Key Provisions and Context**

#### **1. Title and Citation**
- The Act is formally titled: *Government Organization Act, Atlantic Canada, 1987*.
- It may be cited as such and is often referenced in conjunction with other related legislation, such as:
  - *Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency Act* (provides funding and support for regional development)
  - *Enterprise Cape Breton Corporation Act* (establishes a specific economic development entity)

These references indicate that the Government Organization Act provides a legal foundation for the establishment and operation of regional development agencies and public corporations.

#### **2. Effective Date and Implementation**
- The Act does **not come into force immediately** upon enactment.
- Its provisions are implemented **in stages** through **Orders in Council**, which are issued by the Governor in Council (i.e., the federal Cabinet).
- The specific dates on which different sections come into force are determined by these Orders:
  - **Sections 1 to 24, 45, and 53 to 56** came into force on **September 15, 1988**, pursuant to **Statutory Instrument SI/88-152**.
  - **Sections 25 to 44 and 46 to 51** came into force on **December 1, 1988**, pursuant to **SI/88-140**.
  - **Section 52** was **repealed before coming into force**, effective via **2008, c. 20, s. 3** (a later amendment).

> This phased implementation reflects a deliberate legislative strategy to allow time for administrative preparation, stakeholder consultation, and integration into existing governance structures.

#### **3. Purpose and Scope**
Although the Act is titled broadly as a "Government Organization Act," its primary function is to:
- Provide a **legal basis for establishing and governing public organizations** in Atlantic Canada.
- Enable the creation of **government corporations or agencies** with specific mandates, such as economic development, regional infrastructure, or innovation promotion.
- Define the **structure, powers, responsibilities, and accountability mechanisms** of these organizations.
- Facilitate **inter-provincial coordination** and **federal-provincial collaboration** in regional development initiatives.

It is particularly relevant to the operations of entities like the **Atlantic Canada Opportunities Agency (ACOA)** and the **Enterprise Cape Breton Corporation (ECC)**, which are designed to stimulate economic growth and job creation in the region.

#### **4. Key Features and Implications**
- **Creation of Public Corporations**: The Act allows for the formation of non-departmental public corporations with autonomy in decision-making, subject to oversight by the federal or provincial government.
- **Governance Structure**: It typically includes provisions on board composition, appointment processes, reporting requirements, and financial accountability.
- **Funding and Financial Authority**: These organizations may receive funding from federal or provincial governments and may have the authority to manage capital, issue bonds, or enter into commercial agreements.
- **Accountability and Oversight**: The Act ensures that these organizations operate transparently and are accountable to legislative and oversight bodies.

#### **5. Repeal and Amendments**
- The repeal of **Section 52** in 2008 indicates that certain provisions were outdated or redundant, possibly due to changes in policy priorities or the evolution of regional development strategies.
- The phased implementation and subsequent amendments show that the Act has been **adapted over time** to meet changing economic and political needs in Atlantic Canada.

---

### **Significance and Impact**

The *Government Organization Act, Atlantic Canada, 1987* plays a critical role in:
- Supporting regional economic development through targeted public investment.
- Providing legal clarity and stability for government entities operating in specialized areas.
- Enabling efficient, results-oriented governance in the Atlantic provinces, where regional disparities and economic challenges require focused intervention.

It is a key enabler of federal and provincial efforts to promote **economic diversification, innovation, and workforce development** in Atlantic Canada.

---

### **Conclusion**

While not a standalone policy document, the *Government Organization Act, Atlantic Canada, 1987* is a foundational piece of legislation that establishes the legal and administrative framework for regional government organizations. Its phased implementation and subsequent amendments reflect a responsive and adaptive approach to public governance in the region. By enabling the creation and operation of specialized agencies and corporations, the Act supports long-term economic growth and development in Atlantic Canada.

> **In short**: This Act provides the legal basis for creating and managing public organizations in Atlantic Canada—particularly those focused on economic development—and operates in stages, with key provisions coming into force in 1988 and being updated or revised over time.
