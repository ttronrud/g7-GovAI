### **Detailed Summary of the *Canadian Air Transport Security Authority Act***  

The *Canadian Air Transport Security Authority Act* establishes the **Canadian Air Transport Security Authority (CATSA)** as a federal body corporate responsible for ensuring the safety and security of Canada’s air transport system. This Act defines the structure, mandate, governance, financial framework, operational powers, and accountability mechanisms of CATSA, while aligning it with broader aviation security regulations and government oversight.

---

## **1. Purpose and Mandate**

The primary purpose of the Act is to **ensure the effective and efficient screening of persons, property, and baggage** that access aircraft or restricted areas at airports and other designated locations. The Authority’s mandate includes:

- Conducting or authorizing screening of individuals, their belongings, and property in transit through **screening points**.
- Ensuring consistency in screening practices across Canada.
- Delivering screening either **directly** or **through contracted screening contractors**.
- Performing other air transport security functions assigned by the Minister of Transport.

> **Key Definition**:  
> A *restricted area* is any area designated under the *Aeronautics Act* at an aerodrome or other location, including airports.

---

## **2. Definitions and Key Terms**

The Act provides clear definitions for key terms used throughout:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Authority** | The Canadian Air Transport Security Authority established under section 5(1). |
| **Authorized Aerodrome Operator** | An airport operator authorized by CATSA to deliver screening, subject to conditions. |
| **Board** | The board of directors of the Authority, established under section 10. |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Transport (primary responsible minister). |
| **Screening** | Includes searches conducted in accordance with aviation security regulations, emergency directions, or interim orders under the *Aeronautics Act*. |
| **Screening Contractor** | A third-party contractor retained by CATSA or an authorized aerodrome operator to deliver screening. |
| **Screening Officer** | A personnel member (employed by CATSA, an operator, or a contractor) who performs screening. |
| **Screening Point** | Any location where screening occurs, whether directly by CATSA or via a contractor. |

> All words and expressions not otherwise defined have the same meaning as in the *Aeronautics Act* and related aviation security regulations.

> **Important Note**: This Act does *not* override or diminish air transport security responsibilities of other entities (e.g., airlines, airport operators) imposed under the *Aeronautics Act*.

---

## **3. Governance Structure**

### **Board of Directors**
- Composed of **11 directors**, including a **Chairperson**, appointed by the **Governor in Council** on the recommendation of the Minister.
- **Two directors** must be nominated by **representatives of the airline industry**.
- **Two directors** must be nominated by **representatives of aerodrome operators**.
- Directors serve **up to five years**, with no more than half of the board members’ terms expiring in any one year to ensure continuity.
- If a quorum is not met, the appointed directors may still exercise full board powers.

### **Eligibility Requirements for Directors**
A person may not serve as a director if:
- Not a Canadian citizen or permanent resident (under the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act*);
- Is a member of Parliament (Senate or House of Commons) or a provincial legislature;
- Is employed full-time in the federal or provincial public service;
- Is a mayor, councillor, or municipal officer.

> The Governor in Council may **renew** a director’s term for a maximum of one additional five-year term.

### **Director Responsibilities and Compensation**
- Must serve on a **part-time basis**.
- Receive **daily remuneration** set by the Governor in Council for attending meetings or fulfilling duties.
- Entitled to **reasonable travel and living expenses** when performing duties away from home.

### **Chairperson**
- Presides over board meetings.
- Exercises powers assigned by the Authority’s by-laws.

---

## **4. Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**

- Appointed by the **Governor in Council** for a term considered appropriate by the Council.
- Responsible for the **day-to-day management** of CATSA.
- Must serve **full-time**.
- Receives remuneration and travel/living expenses as set by the Governor in Council.
- In case of absence or incapacity, the **board may appoint an employee** to act as interim CEO, but only for **up to 90 days** without Ministerial approval.

> The CEO **cannot serve as a director**.

---

## **5. Powers and Authority**

### **Operational Powers**
- CATSA has the **capacity, rights, powers, and privileges of a natural person** for all purposes, subject to this Act.
- Can **enter into contracts** with Her Majesty as if it were not an agent of the Crown.
- May enter into agreements with:
  - The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** or the **Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)** for services (including on aircraft).
  - **Airport operators** to help cover policing costs (with Treasury Board approval).
- Can **enter into agreements** with air carriers to contribute to screening costs incurred after the Act’s coming into force.

### **Contracting and Procurement Policies**
- Must establish **contracting policies** specifying:
  - Minimum wage standards.
  - Terms and conditions of employment.
- Must ensure **transparency, fairness, openness, and value for money** in procurement.
- Must ensure that **operational requirements** are met through all contracts.

### **Certification of Contractors and Officers**
- Must establish **stringent qualification, training, and performance criteria** for screening contractors and officers — **at least as rigorous as** those in the aviation security regulations.
- Must **certify** all contractors and officers against these criteria.
- May **suspend, vary, or cancel certification** if a contractor or officer fails to meet the standards.

---

## **6. Financial and Operational Framework**

### **Headquarters and Fiscal Year**
- Headquarters must be located in Canada, as designated by the Governor in Council.
- The **financial year** runs from **April 1 to March 31**, unless otherwise directed.

### **Funding and Appropriation**
- **$340 million** is appropriated from the Consolidated Revenue Fund for CATSA’s operating and capital expenditures in the fiscal year **2002–2003**.
- This includes payments to authorized aerodrome operators and contributions to designated airport authorities.

### **Budget Submission Requirements**
- Must submit a **corporate plan, operating budget, and capital budget** within **six months** of the Act coming into force — despite standard timelines under the *Financial Administration Act*.
- Until these budgets are approved, the Authority may **expend funds** with the **Minister’s approval** to become fully operational.

---

## **7. Transfers of Assets and Contracts**

The Governor in Council may transfer to CATSA, on terms deemed appropriate:

| Transfer Type | Details |
|-------------|--------|
| **Equipment** | Screening equipment (e.g., explosive detection devices) from the **Air Transport Security Corporation (ATSC)**. |
| **Contracts** | Rights, titles, interests, or obligations under contracts entered into by ATSC or the Minister before the Act came into force, specifically related to screening. |
| **Rights of Air Carriers** | Rights under screening contracts from air carriers, despite contractual restrictions. |
| **Government Assets** | Any screening equipment or contracts held by Her Majesty. |

> These transfers are **automatic and binding**; CATSA must accept them.

> The transfer **does not affect** rights or obligations under the *Canada Labour Code* held by contractors, employees, or trade unions.

---

## **8. Confidentiality and Security**

- The Authority, authorized aerodrome operators, and screening contractors must **keep confidential** any information whose **publication would harm air transport or public security**.
- This includes financial and operational data.
- The **Auditor General of Canada** is the official auditor of CATSA.
- No information may be required to be **tabled in Parliament** if the Minister determines that its disclosure would be detrimental to security.

> This provision ensures operational flexibility and protects sensitive intelligence and operational details.

---

## **9. Oversight and Review**

- A **review of the Act and its operation** must be completed by the **Minister within five years** of the Act coming into force.
- The Minister must **lay a report before each House of Parliament** on one of the first fifteen days after the report is completed.

---

## **10. Suspension and Transitional Provisions**

- Sections 6 to 9, 27 to 30.1, and 34 of the Act are **suspended** from the **transfer date** (the date the Authority officially takes over operations).
- Regulations made under paragraph 34(a) of the previous *Air Transport Security Corporation Act* are **deemed to have been made under section 50** of this Act.
- Any aerodrome designated under subsection 6(1.1) of the prior Act is **deemed to be designated under subsection 18(3)** of this Act.
- The **original section 2 of chapter 9 of the Statutes of Canada, 2002** (the predecessor Act) is **repealed** on a date or dates to be fixed by the Governor in Council.
