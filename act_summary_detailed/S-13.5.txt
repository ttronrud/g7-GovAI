**Detailed Summary of the Act and Memorandum of Agreement:**

The document in question is a **statutory enactment** passed by the Parliament of Canada, titled an *Act confirming a land transfer agreement between the Government of Canada and the Government of British Columbia*, specifically concerning the **Songhees Indian Reserve** and related land arrangements. It includes both a formal legislative provision and a detailed **Memorandum of Agreement** between the two governments. Below is a comprehensive, structured summary of the key elements and implications of the Act and the agreement.

---

### **1. Legislative Context and Purpose**

- **Enactment by Parliament**:  
  The Act was passed by **His Majesty, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons of Canada**, indicating it is a formal piece of federal legislation enacted through the standard legislative process.

- **Primary Objective**:  
  To **confirm and validate** a private agreement (the Memorandum of Agreement) between the **Government of Canada** and the **Government of British Columbia** regarding the **sale or transfer of the Songhees Indian Reserve** in Victoria, British Columbia.

- **Key Provision Overriding the Indian Act**:  
  Despite the general restrictions imposed by *The Indian Act*, this Act **explicitly overrides** those provisions to allow for the **full payment of compensation to individual heads of Indian families** under the terms of the agreement.  
  → This means that **each head of an Indian family** (as defined in the agreement) is entitled to receive the full amount of compensation **in the manner specified in the agreement**, rather than being limited by the usual rules of the Indian Act (e.g., lump-sum payments or restrictions on how funds can be distributed).

---

### **2. The Memorandum of Agreement (Key Provisions)**

This is a legally binding agreement between two government entities:

#### **Parties Involved**:
- **Government of the Dominion of Canada**, represented by:
  - **Honourable Frank Oliver**, Superintendent General of Indian Affairs.
- **Government of the Province of British Columbia**, represented by:
  - **Honourable William Roderick Ross**, Minister of Lands.

---

#### **Core Agreement: Transfer of the Songhees Indian Reserve**

1. **Transfer of Land to British Columbia**:
   - The **Songhees Indian Reserve**, located in **Victoria, BC**, will be **conveyed or transferred** to the **Government of British Columbia**.
   - The transfer will occur **only after**:
     - The **Songhees Band of Indians formally surrenders** the reserve under the provisions of *The Indian Act*.
     - **Appropriate legislation** is passed by the **Parliament of Canada** confirming the agreement.

2. **Compensation to Songhees Band Members**:
   - In exchange for the transfer, the Province of British Columbia agrees to provide the following compensation:
     - **$10,000.00 (ten thousand dollars)** to be **deposited in the Canadian Bank of Commerce in Victoria**.
     - The amount is to be paid **to each head of family** of the Songhees Band.
     - The list of eligible heads is based on:
       - The **census of the Songhees Band conducted from November 21–25, 1910**, conducted by **Inspector Ditchburn**.
       - Any **additional bona fide heads of families** who existed at the time of payment, **certified by the Superintendent General** to the Minister of Lands.

3. **Payment and Documentation**:
   - Each deposit must be accompanied by a **receipt from the Canadian Bank of Commerce**.
   - The receipt must be **countersigned by the individual head of family** to whom the deposit is made.

4. **Value of Improvements to the Band**:
   - The value of **Indian-owned improvements** (such as the schoolhouse, water pipe, and other infrastructure) will be **deposited to the credit of the respective heads of families**.
   - The **schoolhouse** (which was used as a church), **water pipe**, and other improvements will be **valued and divided equally** among the heads of families.
   - If an agreement cannot be reached on valuation:
     - An **arbitration process** will be initiated.
     - The **Superintendent General of Indian Affairs** and the **Minister of Lands** each appoint one arbitrator.
     - The two arbitrators then appoint a **third arbitrator**.
     - The decision of **any two arbitrators** or the **three arbitrators collectively** is **final and binding**.

5. **Conveyance of Land at Esquimalt (Section 2A)**:
   - In exchange for the transfer of the Songhees Reserve, the **Government of Canada** conveys to **His Majesty, the King**, in fee simple, a parcel of land at **Esquimalt**, specifically:
     - **Section 2A**, Esquimalt District, Vancouver Island.
     - **Legal Description**:
       - Begins at a post on the **northern shore of Constance Cove** at high water mark.
       - Runs **north 36°28′ east (magnetic)** for 80.90 chains and 90 links (≈ 80.90 ch + 90 ln).
       - Then west along the **southerly boundary of Craigflower Road**.
       - Then south along the **easterly boundary of Admiral’s Road**.
       - Then south to the **east boundary of the Esquimalt Indian Reserve**.
       - Then follows the **east boundary of the reserve** to its southeast corner.
       - Then west along the **south boundary of the reserve** to its southwest corner.
       - Then follows the **shoreline of Esquimalt Harbour** back to the point of origin.
     - **Total area**: Approximately **163.42 acres** (more or less), as measured.
     - **Exclusion**: The portion of the land used as a **right-of-way** by the **Esquimalt and Nanaimo Railway Company**, conveyed in a deed dated **July 4, 1905**, registered in Victoria (Vol. 22, Folio 385, No. 115080), is **excluded** from the conveyance.

6. **Rights and Appurtenances**:
   - The conveyance includes **all mines royal**, **mines and minerals**, and **all rights, memberships, and appurtenances** associated with the land.

7. **Removal and Reinterment of Burials**:
   - All **dead bodies**, **monuments**, and **tombstones** from the Songhees Reserve in Victoria will be:
     - **Removed** by the Government of British Columbia.
     - **Re-interred** at the new reserve in Esquimalt.
     - The reinterment must be done in a manner **satisfactory to the Superintendent General**.
   - The **entire cost** of removal and reinterment is borne by the **Government of British Columbia**.

---

### **3. Signatures and Witnesses**

- The agreement was **signed, sealed, and delivered** on **March 31, 1911**.
- Both parties affixed their **seals of office** and were witnessed by:
  - **Frank Pedley** (for Frank Oliver)
  - **R. F. Child** (for William R. Ross)

This formalizes the agreement as a legally binding document between the two governments.

### **4. Historical and Legal Significance**

#### **Key Historical Context**:
- The Songhees people are one of the **original Indigenous nations** in the Victoria area, with long-standing ties to the land.
- This agreement reflects a **complex period of colonial land dispossession and government-led relocations** of Indigenous communities in British Columbia during the early 20th century.
- The transfer of the Songhees Reserve to the provincial government was part of broader policies to **integrate Indigenous peoples into settler society**, often at the expense of traditional land rights and self-governance.

#### **Legal and Ethical Implications**:
- **Override of the Indian Act**: The Act explicitly allows full payment to individual heads of families, which was a significant departure from standard practices under the Indian Act, which often restricted compensation or distribution.
- **Arbitration Clause**: The inclusion of a formal arbitration process for property valuation reflects a **desire to avoid disputes** and ensure fairness, though it still occurs within a colonial framework.
- **Cultural Disruption**: The removal of graves and reinterment at Esquimalt represents a **serious cultural and spiritual disruption**, with long-term impacts on Indigenous community identity and heritage.
- **Land Transfer Without Consent**: The agreement does not require the **consent of the Songhees Band** in a democratic or representative way. The surrender is conditional on compliance with the Indian Act, which may have been applied in a manner that undermined Indigenous sovereignty.
