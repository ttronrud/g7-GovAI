### üîç **Overview and Purpose**

The *Canada Business Corporations Act* (CBCA) is the primary federal statute governing the formation, operation, governance, and regulation of business corporations in Canada. It replaces a patchwork of prior provincial and federal corporate laws with a **uniform, national framework** for business corporations.

#### Key Objectives:
1. **Modernize and reform** the law applicable to business corporations operating across Canada.
2. **Promote uniformity** in corporate law to ensure consistency in how corporations are governed and regulated.
3. **Facilitate a smooth transition** of federal companies incorporated under older Acts (e.g., the *Bank Act*, *Cooperative Credit Associations Act*) into a single, modern legal regime.
4. Provide **clarity and predictability** in corporate governance, shareholder rights, and regulatory compliance.

> The CBCA applies to all corporations incorporated or continued under it and not discontinued.

### üåê **Affiliation and Control Rules**

These rules define how corporate relationships are structured and governed under the CBCA.

#### Affiliation:
- Two or more bodies are **affiliated** if:
  - One is a subsidiary of the other, or
  - Both are subsidiaries of the same parent, or
  - Each is controlled by the same person.

> If two bodies are affiliated with the same third body at the same time, they are **deemed affiliated with each other**.

#### Control:
- A body corporate is **controlled** by a person or group if:
  - They hold **more than 50% of the voting rights** (not just as security) in the corporation.
  - Those voting rights are sufficient to elect a majority of directors.

> This is a **strict threshold** (over 50%) and applies only to voting rights, not fair market value.

#### Subsidiary:
A company is a **subsidiary** if:
- It is controlled by another body corporate, or
- It is controlled by that body corporate and one or more bodies that are also controlled by it, or
- It is a subsidiary of a body that is itself a subsidiary of the other.

> This creates a **nested control structure** ‚Äî e.g., A controls B, B controls C ‚Üí A controls C.

### üìú **Incorporation Process**

#### Who Can Incorporate?
- One or more individuals or bodies corporate may incorporate a corporation.
- An individual must:
  - Be at least **18 years old**,
  - Not be **incapable**, and
  - Not be a **bankrupt**.

> No restrictions on nationality ‚Äî anyone meeting these criteria can incorporate.

#### Articles of Incorporation
Must be signed by incorporators and follow a form prescribed by the Director.

**Mandatory Contents:**
1. **Name** of the corporation.
2. **Registered office** location (province).
3. **Classes of shares** and any maximum number authorized.
4. If multiple classes exist:
   - Rights, privileges, restrictions, and conditions for each class.
   - Authority to issue shares in **series** (with rights, conditions, and designations).
5. **Restrictions** on share transfer or ownership (if any).
6. **Number of directors**:
   - A fixed number, or
   - A minimum and maximum number (subject to section 107(a)).
7. **Business restrictions** (e.g., prohibited activities).

> Articles may include any provisions permitted by the Act or by by-laws.

#### Precedence of Articles or Agreements
- If articles or a **unanimous shareholder agreement** require a **greater number of votes** than required by the Act to take an action (e.g., approve a merger), those provisions **prevail**.
- However, **articles cannot require more votes than required by section 109** to remove a director.

> This ensures that shareholders have a floor of protection against excessive director removal requirements.

#### Submission and Approval
- Incorporators must **send articles and required documents** to the Director.
- The Director **must issue a certificate of incorporation** upon receipt, **unless**:
  - A required notice (under sections 19 or 106) indicates the corporation would violate this Act.

> The corporation **comes into existence on the date shown in the certificate**.
