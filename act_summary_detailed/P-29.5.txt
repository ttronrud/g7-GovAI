**Detailed Summary of the *Public Health Agency of Canada Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *Public Health Agency of Canada Act* (PHACA) establishes the **Public Health Agency of Canada (PHAC)** as a standalone federal institution responsible for leading and coordinating public health policy, programs, and emergency preparedness across Canada. The Act was enacted to strengthen Canada’s capacity to protect and promote public health through science-based decision-making, intergovernmental collaboration, and improved coordination between federal, provincial, territorial, and international stakeholders.

It also creates key leadership roles — the **President** and the **Chief Public Health Officer (CPHO)** — to provide strategic direction and expert public health advice to the federal government.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Purpose and Mandate of the Agency**

> *"The Public Health Agency of Canada is established for the purpose of assisting the Minister in exercising or performing the Minister’s powers, duties and functions in relation to public health."*

- The PHAC exists to support the **Minister of Health** in fulfilling federal responsibilities related to public health.
- Its mandate covers a broad range of public health areas, including:
  - Health protection and promotion
  - Population health assessment
  - Health surveillance
  - Disease and injury prevention
  - Public health emergency preparedness and response

This ensures that public health policy is evidence-based, coordinated, and responsive to emerging threats.

---

### **2. Leadership Structure**

#### **A. The Minister of Health**
- Presides over the PHAC and has overall **management and direction** of the Agency.
- May delegate certain powers, duties, and functions to officers or employees of the PHAC, **subject to specified terms and conditions**.
- **Important Limitation**: The Minister **cannot delegate** the power to make regulations or to delegate under this section itself.

#### **B. The President of the PHAC**
- Appointed by the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet).
- Holds office for a **renewable term of up to five years**.
- Is the **chief executive officer** of the PHAC.
- Has the **rank and status of a deputy head of a department**, placing it among the highest-ranking positions in federal government.
- Remuneration is set by the Governor in Council.

> *Role*: Provides strategic leadership, oversees operations, and ensures alignment with national public health priorities.

#### **C. The Chief Public Health Officer (CPHO)**
- Appointed by the Governor in Council.
- Must be a **qualified health professional with expertise in public health**.
- Is the **lead health professional** of the Government of Canada in public health matters.
- Provides **scientifically grounded public health advice** to both the Minister and the President.

##### **Key Powers and Responsibilities of the CPHO**:
- Advises the Minister and President on public health issues.
- Can communicate with:
  - Provincial and territorial governments
  - Public health authorities
  - International organizations and foreign governments
  - The public, voluntary organizations, and private sector stakeholders
- May **prepare and publish reports** on public health issues.
- Must, where possible, **disclose sources of data and methodology** used in reports.
- May identify public health problems, their causes, and recommend preventive or corrective measures.

##### **Term and Reappointment**:
- Holds office during **pleasure** (at the discretion of the Governor in Council).
- May be **reappointed for one or more additional terms** of up to five years.

##### **Remuneration**:
- Paid by the Governor in Council for performance of duties.

##### **Delegation of Powers**:
- May delegate powers, duties, and functions to other PHAC officers or employees — **except** the power to delegate under this section.

##### **Annual Reporting Obligations**:
- Must submit a **report on the state of public health in Canada within six months after each fiscal year ends**.
- The Minister must lay this report before **each House of Parliament on any of the first 15 days** after receiving it when Parliament is sitting.

> This ensures transparency and accountability to Parliament and the public.

---

### **3. Operational and Administrative Framework**

#### **A. Staffing**
- Officers and employees required for the PHAC’s operations are appointed in accordance with the **Public Service Employment Act**.
- Ensures that staffing follows public service standards and merit principles.

#### **B. Advisory Committees**
- The Minister may establish **advisory and other committees** on public health.
- Committees may have:
  - Defined membership
  - Specific duties and functions
  - Operational procedures
- Members are paid:
  - Any remuneration fixed by the Governor in Council
  - For reasonable travel, living, and other expenses (as per Treasury Board directives), depending on whether they are full-time or part-time.

---

### **4. Information Management and Confidentiality**

> The Governor in Council may make regulations regarding:
- The **collection, analysis, interpretation, publication, and distribution** of public health information
- The **protection of confidential information**, including **personal information** (as defined in the *Privacy Act*)

#### **Enforcement of Regulations**:
- Any person who **knowingly possesses, uses, or discloses information in violation** of such regulations is guilty of an offence.
- Penalties:
  - **Fine not exceeding $50,000**, or
  - **Imprisonment for up to six months**, or
  - **Both**.

> This provision protects sensitive data (e.g., disease outbreak data, individual health records) while allowing necessary public health information sharing.

---

### **5. Transition from the Former Agency (Seamless Continuity)**

This section ensures a smooth transition from the **former Public Health Agency of Canada** (which existed prior to the 2006 reform) to the new, restructured PHAC.

#### Key Transitional Provisions:
- **Continuity of Office**: The person who held the office of Chief Public Health Officer on the day the Act came into force **remains in office** and is deemed to have been appointed under section 6(1).
- **Employee Status**: All employees who held positions in the former agency **automatically transfer** to the new agency upon the Act’s coming into force.
- **No Change in Status**: Employees’ status, job titles, and roles remain unchanged, except for the name of the agency.
- **Funding Continuity**: Any unexpended appropriations for the former agency in the fiscal year of transition are **deemed to be appropriated for the new agency**.
- **Transfer of Powers and Functions**: All powers, duties, and functions previously vested in officers or employees of the former agency are **automatically transferred** to the appropriate officers in the new agency.
- **Exception**: The Governor in Council may, by order, designate a deputy minister or other federal officer to exercise a specific function.

> This ensures that public health operations continue without disruption during the transition.

---

### **6. Reference to the Former Agency in Legal Documents**

- References to the "former agency" in various federal legislation and orders are **deemed to refer to the new PHAC**, including:
  - Schedule I to the *Access to Information Act*
  - Schedules I.1 and IV to the *Financial Administration Act*
  - The schedule to the *Privacy Act* under “Other Government Institutions”
  - Access to Information and Privacy Act designation orders
  - Auditor General Act sustainability strategy schedules
  - Privacy Act heads of institutions orders
  - Any order made under the Public Service Employment Act definition of "department"

> This ensures legal and administrative consistency across all federal statutes and regulations.

#### **Designation of Deputy Head**:
- Any designation of a person as deputy head of the former agency is **deemed to be a designation of the CPHO as deputy head of the new agency**.

> This strengthens the CPHO’s leadership position within the federal public service.

---

### **7. Effective Dates and Implementation**

- **Section 23** (which deals with the CPHO’s reporting and other obligations) came into force on **December 12, 2006**.
- The **rest of the Act** (excluding Section 23) came into force on **December 15, 2006**, as per Order in Council SI/2006-145.

> This phased implementation allowed time for administrative setup, staffing, and transition planning.

---

## **Key Objectives and Outcomes of the Act**

| Objective | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Strengthen Public Health Governance** | Creates a centralized, independent, and expert-led body to guide national public health policy. |
| **Improve Coordination** | Enhances coordination between federal, provincial, and territorial governments and international partners. |
| **Enhance Scientific Basis** | Requires all advice and reports to be based on scientific evidence. |
| **Increase Transparency and Accountability** | Mandates annual public health reports to Parliament and requires disclosure of data sources and methods. |
| **Ensure Continuity and Stability** | Provides a smooth transition from the old agency to the new structure, preserving expertise and operations. |
| **Protect Privacy and Confidentiality** | Establishes clear rules for handling sensitive health data. |

---

## **Significance and Impact**

The *Public Health Agency of Canada Act* represents a major structural reform in Canada’s public health system. It:

- Elevates public health to a central government priority.
- Establishes a single, authoritative voice for public health advice at the federal level.
- Provides a framework for proactive disease prevention, emergency preparedness, and health equity.
- Promotes collaboration across levels of government and sectors.
- Supports evidence-based policy development and public trust.
