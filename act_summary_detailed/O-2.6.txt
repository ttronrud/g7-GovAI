## **Detailed Summary of the Act: "An Act to Repeal Certain Acts and to Establish the Office of the Port Warden of the Harbour of Montreal"**

### **1. Repeal of Prior Legislation**
The Act formally **repeals** several earlier laws:
- The **Act of the Province of Canada, 26th year (1849), Chapter 52**  
- The **Act of the Province of Canada, 29th year (1856), Chapter 59**  
- Specific sections (1st, 2nd, 5th, 6th, 7th) of the **Parliament of Canada Act, 36th year (1873), Chapter 11**  
- The **Parliament of Canada Act, 37th year (1874), Chapter 33**

**Exception:**  
These repealed laws are **not fully abolished** with respect to the following offices:
- The **Port Warden of the Harbour of Montreal**
- The **Deputy Port Warden**
- The **Board of Examiners** appointed under the last-mentioned 1874 Act

These offices **continue to exist and function** until their successors are formally appointed under this new Act.

---

### **2. Establishment of the Office of the Port Warden**
A new, centralized office is created at the **City of Montreal**:
- A **Port Warden** shall be appointed.
- The **Council of the Board of Trade at Montreal** may appoint **as many Deputy Port Wardens as it deems necessary** for the efficient operation of the harbour.

**Authority and Supervision:**
- Any Deputy Port Warden may **exercise powers and duties** conferred upon the Port Warden, **under the general supervision** of the Port Warden.

---

### **3. Appointment Process for Port Warden and Deputy Port Wardens**
The process is structured and merit-based:

1. **Board of Examiners**:
   - The Council of the Board of Trade annually appoints **five persons** to serve as a **Board of Examiners**.
   - This Board is responsible for **examining all candidates** for the positions of Port Warden or Deputy Port Warden.

2. **Recommendation and Appointment**:
   - After conducting the examination, the Board of Examiners **reports its findings** to the Council.
   - The Council, acting on behalf of the Board of Trade, **recommends to the Governor in Council** one candidate for appointment as **Port Warden**.
   - The **Governor in Council** has the authority to **appoint** the recommended individual.

3. **Appointment of Deputy Port Wardens**:
   - The Council may appoint Deputy Port Wardens from among those reported by the Board of Examiners as **fit and proper**, based on its judgment.

### **4. Oversight and Removal of Port Wardens and Deputies**
- The **Council of the Board of Trade** has **control and supervision** over the Port Warden's office.
- If a **complaint is made** about the conduct of a Port Warden or Deputy:
  - The Council **investigates** the complaint.
  - If the complaint is **well-founded** and the circumstances warrant it:
    - For the **Port Warden**: The Council reports to the **Governor in Council**, who may **remove** the Port Warden and appoint a successor.
    - For a **Deputy Port Warden**: The Council may **remove** the individual **at its discretion**, without needing to report to the Governor.

### **5. By-Laws and Regulations**
- The **Board of Examiners** may create, amend, or repeal **by-laws, rules, and regulations** governing:
  - The duties of the Port Warden
  - The duties of Deputy Port Wardens
- These by-laws **must be approved by the Council of the Board of Trade**, which may:
  - Confirm them
  - Amend them
  - Reject them
- A **certified copy** of such by-laws, signed by the Secretary of the Board of Trade, is **primâ facie evidence** in any court in Canada of their validity and existence.

---

### **6. Oaths of Office**
- The **Port Warden** and each **Deputy Port Warden** must:
  - Take and subscribe an **oath of office** before a **Justice of the Peace** in the District of Montreal.
  - The oath is:
    > *"I, A. B., do solemnly swear that I will faithfully and impartially, to the best of my judgment and ability, perform the duties of the office of Port Warden of the Harbour of Montreal, without fear, favour or affection for any person or party whomsoever."*
- After taking the oath, both the Port Warden and Deputy must **deposit the oath with the Secretary of the Board of Trade**, who serves as custodian.

---

### **7. Compensation and Financial Reporting**
- The **Port Warden receives no fees** beyond those strictly related to his office.
- All fees collected are:
  - Recorded in official books
  - Credited to the office
- The Port Warden must submit a **certified annual return** to the Council of the Board of Trade within **seven days after December 31** of each year, detailing all **receipts and expenses**.

---

### **8. Office Hours and Record-Keeping**
- The Port Warden’s office is open:
  - **From 7 a.m. to 6 p.m.** during the **season of navigation**
  - **From 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.** during the **rest of the year**
- The Port Warden must have a **seal of office** and maintain **official books**.
- All acts, decisions, and fees of the Port Warden and his deputies must be **recorded in these books** as directed by the by-laws.

### **9. Survey of Damaged Cargo**
- **Master of any sea-going vessel** arriving in Montreal with cargo that has **not previously broken bulk** in Canada must:
  - **Notify the Port Warden** to be present at the **opening of the hatches**.
  - **Immediately upon discovering cargo damage**, request the Port Warden to **survey** the damage.
- If the Port Warden is **not notified or requested**, and the cargo is **landed in a damaged condition**, this is **primâ facie evidence** that:
  - The damage occurred due to **improper stowage or negligence** of the vessel’s crew.
  - The **master or owners** bear the burden of proving otherwise.

### **10. Examinations of Vessels and Warehouses**
- Upon request from any interested party, the Port Warden or a Deputy:
  - May go **on board a vessel** to examine cargo condition and stowage.
  - May inspect **warehouses, wharves, or dwellings** where goods are claimed to be damaged.
- The Port Warden must:
  - Notify the **master, agent, or representative** of the vessel.
  - Enquire into and **ascertain the nature, cause, and extent of damage**.
  - Make a **full memorandum** and record it in the office books.
- The Port Warden may **call in one or two experts** (e.g., shipwrights, carpenters) to assist.
  - Experts are entitled to a **fee**.
  - The **cost of the survey** is paid by the party requesting it and is a **lawful charge** against any party liable for the damage.
- The party requesting the survey must **notify the liable party** of the time and place of the survey.

### **11. Survey of Vessels in Need of Repair**
- The Port Warden may survey a vessel claimed to be damaged or unfit for sea.
- He must:
  - Examine the **hull, spars, rigging, and appurtenances**.
  - Specify the damage and the vessel’s condition.
  - Record a **full and particular account** in the office books.
- He may call in **skilled professionals** (carpenters, riggers, etc.) to assist.
  - These experts are entitled to a fee.
- **No expert may be chosen who has a financial interest** in the outcome.
- In cases of **wreck or serious damage**, the Port Warden must **consult the regular surveyor of Lloyd’s** or a similar classification society.
- His **certificate of repairs** is **primâ facie evidence** that the vessel is seaworthy.
