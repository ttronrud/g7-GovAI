**Detailed Summary of the *Canada Post Corporation Act***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *Canada Post Corporation Act* establishes and regulates the **Canada Post Corporation (Corporation)**, a federal Crown corporation responsible for providing postal services across Canada. The Act defines the Corporation’s structure, objectives, powers, responsibilities, and operational framework, while ensuring it operates with financial sustainability, service quality, security, and alignment with government priorities.

This Act serves as the foundational legislation for Canada Post, replacing earlier postal statutes and modernizing its governance and operations in response to technological change, economic realities, and evolving public expectations.

---

## **Key Definitions and Interpretations**

The Act begins by defining key terms used throughout:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Board** | The Board of Directors of the Corporation. |
| **Chairperson** | The Chairperson of the Board, appointed by the Governor in Council. *(Note: The original "Chairman" designation was repealed in 2006.)* |
| **Corporation** | The Canada Post Corporation established under section 4. |
| **Library material** | Books, magazines, records, CDs, DVDs, and other audiovisual or similar materials. |
| **Mail** | Mailable matter from the time it is posted until delivered to the addressee. |
| **Mail bag** | Any container or covering used to transmit mail, even if empty. |
| **Mail contractor** | A person with an active, unexpired contract with the Corporation to transmit mail. |
| **Mail conveyance** | Any physical, electronic, optical, or other method of transmitting mail. *(Only in English version)* |
| **Mailable matter** | Any message, information, funds, or goods that can be sent by post. |
| **Minister** | The designated member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada responsible for postal affairs. |
| **Post** | Leaving mail in a post office or with someone authorized to receive it. |
| **Post office** | Any place, device, or conveyance authorized by the Corporation for posting, receiving, sorting, handling, or delivering mail. |
| **Postage** | The charge for collection, transmission, delivery, and special services (e.g., insurance). |
| **Postage stamp** | Any stamp, impression, or postage meter authorized by the Corporation. |
| **Postal remittance** | Instruments authorized by the Corporation for remitting funds. |
| **President** | The chief executive officer of the Corporation, appointed by the Governor in Council. |
| **Transmit** | To send or convey mail via any physical, electronic, or optical means. |
| **Transmit by post** | To send mail through or via the Corporation. |
| **Undeliverable letter** | A letter that cannot be delivered due to refusal, legal prohibition, or unpaid postage. |

### **Delivery and Postal Course**

- **Delivery** is deemed to occur when mail is:
  - Left at the addressee’s residence or business,
  - Deposited in a post office lockbox, rural mailbox, or other designated receptacle,
  - Left with the addressee, their servant, agent, or someone reasonably authorized to receive it — **in the usual manner**.

- A thing is considered **in the course of post** from the moment it is posted until it is delivered or returned to the sender.

---

### **Applicability of the Act**

- The Act is binding on **Her Majesty in right of Canada or any province**.

---

## **Establishment of the Canada Post Corporation**

- A **corporation named "Canada Post Corporation"** is created under this Act.

---

## **Objects of the Corporation**

The Corporation is empowered to achieve the following objectives:

1. **Establish and operate a postal service** for the collection, transmission, and delivery of:
   - Messages, information, funds, and goods
   - Both within Canada and internationally

2. **Manufacture and provide products and services** that are necessary or incidental to postal operations.

3. **Provide services** to:
   - Government departments and agencies (federal, provincial, regional, municipal)
   - Private individuals and organizations
   - Services that are “capable of being conveniently provided” in the course of fulfilling the above objectives.

---

## **Guiding Principles in Operations**

While maintaining **basic customary postal service**, the Corporation must consider:

1. **Improving and extending products/services** in light of developments in communications technology.
2. **Operating on a self-sustaining financial basis** while maintaining a **consistent standard of service** across communities of similar size.
3. **Ensuring the security of mail** throughout its journey.
4. **Utilizing human resources effectively** to meet objectives and retain employee commitment.
5. **Maintaining a corporate identity program** approved by the Governor in Council that reflects Canada Post’s role as a government institution.

---

## **Board of Directors**

- The Board consists of:
  - **Chairperson**
  - **President**
  - **Nine other directors**

### Appointment and Tenure:
- **Directors (excluding Chairperson and President)**:
  - Appointed by the **Minister**, with **Governor in Council approval**.
  - Serve at the **discretion of the Minister** for up to **four years**.
  - No more than **half of the directors** may have their terms expire in any one year to ensure continuity.
- **Vacancy**:
  - If a director’s term ends prematurely, the Governor in Council may appoint a replacement for the remainder of the term.
- **Reappointment**:
  - Directors may be reappointed after their term ends.

### Fees and Expenses:
- Directors (excluding Chairperson and President) may receive **fees for attending Board or committee meetings** as fixed by the Governor in Council.
- Directors are entitled to **reasonable travel and living expenses** incurred while performing duties.

### Chairperson:
- Appointed by the Governor in Council for a term deemed appropriate.
- Paid a salary or remuneration set by the Governor in Council.
- If absent or unable to act, the **Minister may designate another director** to act temporarily for **up to 90 days**, subject to **Governor in Council approval**.

### President:
- Appointed by the Governor in Council for a term considered appropriate.
- Serves as the **chief executive officer**.
- Paid a salary or remuneration set by the Governor in Council.
- If absent or vacant, the **Board may appoint a director or officer** to act temporarily for **up to 90 days**, again requiring **Governor in Council approval**.

---

## **Head Office and Governance**

- The **head office** of the Corporation may be located in any place in Canada, as designated by **order of the Governor in Council**.

- The **Board has full authority** to:
  - Direct and manage the affairs of the Corporation
  - Exercise all powers and perform all duties of the Corporation

- The Board may:
  - Appoint and fix salaries for **Vice-Presidents**
  - Make **by-laws** covering:
    - Property and affairs management
    - Board and committee procedures (meetings, quorum, time, place)
    - Functions, duties, and powers of officers, directors, and employees
    - Administration of contracts

---

## **Employment and Staffing**

- The Corporation may employ **officers, employees, agents, advisers, and consultants** as needed.
- It may **fix terms, conditions, and remuneration** for these roles.
- **Except as provided in specific exceptions**, employees engaged under this Act are **not considered part of the federal public administration**.

### Public Service Superannuation:
- The **Public Service Superannuation Act does not apply** to directors (except Chairperson, President, or those from federal public administration) unless the Governor in Council otherwise directs.

### Exception for Aeronautics Act:
- For **regulations under the Aeronautics Act**, Chairperson, President, officers, and employees of Canada Post are **deemed to be in the federal public administration**.

### Labour Code Exception:
- **Mail contractors** (those who contract with Canada Post to transmit mail) are **not considered dependent contractors or employees** under subsection 3(1) of the *Canada Labour Code*.

---

## **Exclusive Postal Privilege**

- The Corporation has the **sole and exclusive right** to:
  - Collect, transmit, and deliver letters within Canada.

### Exceptions to the Exclusive Privilege:
The privilege **does not apply** to:

1. **Letters carried incidentally** by a friend of sender or addressee.
2. **Court documents** (commissions, affidavits, writs, processes).
3. **Letters lawfully brought into Canada and posted immediately**.
4. **Letters carried by common carriers without profit or reward** (e.g., for goods).
5. **Urgent letters** transmitted by messenger for a fee at least **three times the regular postage rate** for a 50g letter.
6. **Letters of merchants or cargo owners** carried by vessels or employees without profit.
7. **Internal organizational letters** transmitted between offices of the same organization by employees.
8. **Letters transmitted via electronic or optical means** (e.g., email, digital transmission).
9. **Letters from foreign armed forces** in Canada with the **Government of Canada’s consent**.

> ⚠️ **Important**: No person is authorized to collect or receive letters for the purpose of transmitting or delivering them under these exceptions.

### Exclusion for International Mail:
- The exclusive privilege **does not apply** to letters intended for delivery **outside Canada**.
