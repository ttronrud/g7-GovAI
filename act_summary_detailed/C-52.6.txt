**Detailed Summary of the Customs Act**

---

### **Overview**

The *Customs Act* is a foundational piece of Canadian legislation that governs the administration, enforcement, and regulation of customs duties, importation, exportation, and the movement of goods across Canada’s borders. It establishes the legal framework for the Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) to regulate trade, ensure compliance with tax and tariff laws, and enforce national security, health, and safety standards.

This summary provides a comprehensive breakdown of the key definitions, concepts, and references found in the introductory section of the Act — particularly the definitions of key terms used throughout the legislation. These definitions are essential for interpreting the scope and application of the Act in various regulatory and enforcement contexts.

---

## **Key Definitions and Interpretations**

### **1. Agency**
- **Definition**: The *Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)*.
- **Purpose**: The CBSA is the primary federal body responsible for administering and enforcing customs regulations, including border control, customs duties, and trade compliance.
- **Note**: This definition ensures that all references to "the Agency" refer unambiguously to the CBSA.

---

### **2. Bonded Warehouse**
- **Definition**: A place licensed by the Minister under subsection 91(1) of the *Customs Tariff*.
- **Purpose**: Used to store imported goods without paying duties until they are released for use in Canada. These warehouses are critical for businesses involved in international trade, especially those with deferred payment or inventory needs.
- **Regulatory Oversight**: Requires licensing and compliance with CBSA rules.

---

### **3. Cannabis and Cannabis Products**
- **Cannabis**: Defined in accordance with *subsection 2(1) of the Cannabis Act*.
- **Cannabis Product**: Defined under *section 2 of the Excise Act, 2001*.
- **Purpose**: Ensures consistent legal interpretation of cannabis-related goods, especially in relation to excise duties, licensing, and control.
- **Note**: This reflects the integration of the *Cannabis Act* with customs procedures, particularly for import and export controls.

---

### **4. Carrier Code**
- **Definition**: A unique identification number issued by the Minister either under *subsection 12.1(4)* or prior to its coming into force.
- **Purpose**: Identifies carriers (e.g., shipping companies, airlines) involved in international transport for customs and compliance tracking.
- **Use**: Critical for tracing goods through the supply chain and verifying compliance with customs procedures.

---

### **5. Free Trade Agreements (FTAs)**
The Act defines several major free trade agreements, each with specific names and abbreviations:

| Agreement | Abbreviation | Purpose |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Canada–Chile Free Trade Agreement | CCFTA (ALÉCC) | Enables preferential tariff treatment for goods from Chile |
| Canada–Colombia Free Trade Agreement | CCOFTA (ALÉCCO) | Same as above |
| Canada–Costa Rica Free Trade Agreement | CCRFTA (ALÉCCR) | Same as above |
| Canada–EFTA Free Trade Agreement | CEFTA (ALÉCA) | Covers EFTA states (Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway) |
| Canada–European Union Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement | CETA (AÉCG) | Grants preferential treatment to EU countries and other beneficiaries |
| Canada–Honduras Economic Growth and Prosperity Act | CHFTA (ALÉCH) | Supports trade between Canada and Honduras |
| Canada–Israel Free Trade Agreement | CIFTA (ALÉCI) | Facilitates trade between Canada and Israel |
| Canada–Jordan Economic Growth and Prosperity Act | CJFTA (ALÉCJ) | Trade facilitation between Canada and Jordan |
| Canada–Korea Economic Growth and Prosperity Act | CKFTA (ALÉCRC) | Trade agreement with South Korea |
| Canada–Panama Economic Growth and Prosperity Act | CPAFTA (ALÉCPA) | Trade between Canada and Panama |
| Canada–Peru Free Trade Agreement | CPFTA (ALÉCP) | Trade with Peru |
| Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership | CPTPP (PTPGP) | Multilateral trade agreement with Pacific Rim countries |
| Canada–Ukraine Free Trade Agreement | CUFTA (ALÉCU) | Trade between Canada and Ukraine |
| Canada–Ukraine Free Trade Agreement (2017 version) | CUFTA 2017 (ALÉCU de 2017) | Pre-2023 version of the Ukraine agreement |
| Canada–United Kingdom Trade Continuity Agreement | CUKTCA (ACCCRU) | Post-Brexit trade continuity arrangements |
| Canada–United States–Mexico Agreement | CUSMA (ACEUM) | Tripartite trade agreement with the U.S. and Mexico |

> **Note**: These agreements are central to the Act’s framework for *preferential tariff treatment*, which allows goods from member countries to enter Canada at reduced or zero tariffs under specific conditions.

---

### **6. Free Trade Partner**
- **Definition**: A country or territory listed in *column 1 of Part 1 of the Schedule*.
- **Purpose**: Identifies eligible countries for preferential tariff treatment under applicable FTAs.

---

### **7. Certificate of Origin**
- **Definition**: A document proving the country of origin of goods to claim preferential tariff treatment under an FTA.
- **Regulatory Basis**: Prescribed under *subsection 35.1(1)* and subject to regulations under *paragraph 35.1(4)(b)*.
- **Purpose**: Required for importers to qualify for reduced duties under free trade agreements. Must be properly completed and verified.

---

### **8. Goods**
- **Definition**: Broadly includes:
  - Conveyances (vehicles, aircraft, watercraft)
  - Animals
  - Any document in any form
- **Purpose**: Ensures that all types of items moving through Canadian borders are subject to customs control, including non-tangible records and documents.

---

### **9. Conveyance**
- **Definition**: Any vehicle, aircraft, water-borne craft, or other contrivance used to move persons or goods.
- **Purpose**: Covers all forms of transportation, including commercial freight, private vehicles, and aircraft, for customs and security monitoring.

---

### **10. Courier**
- **Definition**: Assigned by regulation.
- **Purpose**: Defines who qualifies as a courier for customs purposes (e.g., private delivery services), which may affect customs clearance and documentation requirements.

---

### **11. Designated Goods**
- **Definition**: Includes:
  - Aviation fuel
  - Aviation gasoline
  - Beer or malt liquor
  - Diamonds
  - Diesel fuel
  - Gasoline
  - Pearls
  - Precious and semi-precious stones
  - Spirits
  - Wine
- **Additional Provision**: The Minister may, by order, designate other goods for specific provisions of the Act.
- **Purpose**: These goods are subject to special rules, including excise duties, licensing, and customs controls. This allows flexibility in responding to market changes or policy needs.

> **Note**: Several entries (e.g., *cigar*, *manufactured tobacco*) have been **repealed** as of 2002.

---

### **12. Duties**
- **Definition**: Taxes or levies imposed on imported goods under:
  - *Customs Tariff*
  - *Excise Act, 2001*
  - *Excise Tax Act*
  - *Special Import Measures Act*
- **Exclusion**: Does **not** include taxes under *Part IX of the Excise Tax Act* (e.g., GST/HST or other indirect taxes), specifically for subsections 3(1), 59(3)(b), 65(1)(b), sections 69 and 73, and subsections 74(1), 75(2), 76(1).
- **Purpose**: Clarifies what constitutes a "duty" for customs purposes, distinguishing it from other taxes.

---

### **13. Duty Free Shop**
- **Definition**: A place licensed by the Minister under *section 24*.
- **Purpose**: Allows travelers to purchase goods without paying import duties, subject to specific rules and licensing.

---

### **14. Inland Waters**
- **Definition**: All rivers, lakes, and fresh water bodies in Canada, including the St. Lawrence River up to specific geographical boundaries:
  - From Cap-des-Rosiers to the westernmost point of Anticosti Island
  - From Anticosti Island to the north shore of the St. Lawrence River along 63°W longitude.
- **Purpose**: Defines jurisdictional boundaries for inland water transport and customs enforcement.

> **Note**: "Internal waters" and "territorial sea" have been **repealed** due to changes in maritime law.

---

### **15. Excise Stamp**
- **Definition**: As defined in *section 2 of the Excise Act, 2001*.
- **Purpose**: Used to indicate compliance with excise regulations on specific goods (e.g., tobacco, alcohol).

---

### **16. Export and Import**
- **Export**: Movement of goods *from* Canada.
- **Import**: Movement of goods *into* Canada.
- **Purpose**: Fundamental terms for tracking trade flows and applying duties.

---

### **17. Forfeit**
- **Definition**: A forfeiture to *Her Majesty in right of Canada*.
- **Purpose**: Applies to goods or assets seized due to violations of customs law (e.g., smuggling, fraud).
