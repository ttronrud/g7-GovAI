### **Detailed Summary of the Canada Border Services Agency Act**

The **Canada Border Services Agency Act** is a foundational piece of federal legislation that establishes, defines, and governs the **Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA)** as a key body within the Canadian federal public administration. This Act provides a comprehensive legal framework for the structure, functions, responsibilities, leadership, operational authority, and transitional arrangements associated with the CBSA — particularly in the context of its formation through the amalgamation and reorganization of previous border and customs agencies.

Below is a detailed, section-by-section summary and analysis of the Act:

---

## **1. Title and Citation**
- The Act is officially titled **"The Canada Border Services Agency Act"**.
- It may be cited as such, establishing a clear legal identity for the legislation.

---

## **2. Definitions**

Key definitions are introduced to ensure clarity and consistency throughout the Act:

### **Agency**
- Refers to the **Canada Border Services Agency** established under subsection 3(1).
- The Agency is a **body corporate**, meaning it has legal personality and can enter into contracts, own property, and be sued.

### **Minister**
- The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** is responsible for the Agency.
- This reflects the strategic alignment of border security with national public safety and emergency preparedness priorities.

### **President**
- The **President of the Agency**, appointed by the Governor in Council, is the chief executive officer.
- The President holds the rank and powers of a **deputy head of a department**, indicating a high level of authority and administrative responsibility.

### **Program Legislation**
A broad and critical definition encompassing all laws and instruments that the Agency administers or enforces. This includes:
- **Customs and Excise laws**: *Customs Act*, *Excise Act*, *Customs Tariff*, *Excise Act, 2001*, *Select Luxury Items Tax Act*.
- **Immigration and Refugee Protection laws**: *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act*.
- **Agriculture and Agri-Food laws**: *Agriculture and Agri-Food Administrative Monetary Penalties Act*, *Feeds Act*, *Fertilizers Act*, *Health of Animals Act*, *Plant Protection Act*, *Safe Food for Canadians Act*, *Seeds Act*.
- **Agreements or arrangements** between the Government of Canada and foreign states or provincial governments to administer programs or enforce regulations.
- **Duties or taxes** collected under the *Customs Act*.

This definition ensures the Agency has wide-ranging authority to enforce a wide spectrum of federal laws related to trade, immigration, agriculture, food safety, and animal/plant health.

---

## **3. Establishment and Legal Status of the Agency**

- The **CBSA is established as a body corporate**, meaning it has legal rights and obligations independent of its government parent.
- It is an **agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada**, meaning it operates under the authority of the Crown and is subject to the rules and oversight of the federal government.

### **Head Office Location**
- The head office is located in the **National Capital Region**, as defined in the *National Capital Act* (via a schedule). This ensures central coordination and proximity to key government institutions.

---

## **4. Core Mandate and Responsibilities**

The Agency is responsible for delivering **integrated border services** that balance three primary objectives:

1. **National Security and Public Safety**  
   - The Agency supports efforts to protect Canada from threats such as terrorism, smuggling, illegal immigration, and cross-border crime.

2. **Facilitation of Free Flow of Persons and Goods**  
   - Enables legal trade and travel by ensuring that individuals and goods (including animals and plants) meet all regulatory requirements under program legislation.

3. **Key Functions**  
   The Agency performs the following functions:
   - Administers or enforces program legislation (either or both).
   - Implements **agreements** with:
     - Foreign governments or international organizations.
     - Provincial or territorial governments.
     - Other federal departments or agencies.
   - Provides **cooperation and support** (e.g., advice, information) to other departments to assist them in policy development and implementation related to program legislation.
   - Offers **support services** to departments for which the Minister is responsible, under formal agreements.

---

## **5. Leadership and Governance**

### **Appointment of President and Executive Vice-President**
- The **Governor in Council** appoints:
  - A **President** for a term of **up to five years**, renewable.
  - An **Executive Vice-President** for the same term.
- Both are appointed during "pleasure," meaning the government can terminate their appointment at any time.

### **Authority of the President**
- The President has **control and management** of the Agency and all related matters.
- Acts as the **deputy head of a department**, reflecting a senior leadership role.
- Can **delegate powers** to any person (including employees or officers).
- Can **designate officers** under the *Customs Act* or other laws to perform specific duties, such as:
  - Officers (for customs enforcement).
  - Inspectors (including veterinary inspectors) for agriculture, animal, or plant health enforcement.
- May also **exercise powers** granted to the Minister under the *Immigration and Refugee Protection Act* to designate officers.

### **Remuneration and Benefits**
- Paid by the Governor in Council.
- Entitled to **reasonable travel and living expenses** when performing duties outside their ordinary place of work.
- Deemed to be **employed in the public service** under:
  - *Public Service Superannuation Act*.
  - *Government Employees Compensation Act* (and related regulations).
- This ensures access to public service benefits and protections.

---

## **6. Staffing and Authority of Officers and Employees**

- Officers and employees necessary for the Agency’s operations are **appointed under the Public Service Employment Act**.
- They may exercise powers or perform duties related to program legislation **only if**:
  - They are appointed in a capacity appropriate to the function.
  - They comply with any **directions from the Minister**.

> **Important Limitation**:  
> Subsection (1) does **not** include:
> - Powers or duties of the Minister under this Act.
> - The power to make regulations.

> **Note**:  
> Directions given by the Minister under this Act are **not considered statutory instruments** under the *Statutory Instruments Act*, meaning they do not require formal publication or parliamentary approval.

---

## **7. International and Inter-Governmental Agreements**

- The Agency may enter into:
  - **Agreements** with foreign states or international organizations.
  - **Arrangements** with provincial/territorial governments or federal departments.
- These are permitted **with the approval of the Governor in Council**, on the recommendation of:
  - The **Minister**.
  - The **Minister of Foreign Affairs**.

> **Fiscal Arrangements Exception**:  
> Parts III and III.1 of the *Federal-Provincial Fiscal Arrangements Act* **do not apply** to such agreements, allowing greater flexibility in fiscal arrangements.

---

## **8. Financial Management and Appropriations**

- An **appropriation Act** may provide that any **unexpended balance** of funds for the Agency at the end of a fiscal year **lapses** in the following year.
- This promotes fiscal discipline and prevents surplus funding from being carried forward.

---

## **9. Reporting Obligations**

- The **Minister** must, as soon as possible after each fiscal year ends (but no later than the end of the calendar year), **table a report** before each House of Parliament on:
  - The Agency’s **operations and performance**.
- This report may be satisfied by **existing Treasury Board reports** that contain the required information.

This ensures **transparency and accountability** to Parliament.

---

## **10. Transitional Provisions (Key for Continuity)**

This section is critical for ensuring a **smooth transition** from the **former Canada Customs and Revenue Agency (CCRA)** to the new CBSA.

### **Definition of Key Entities**
- **Former Agency**: The portion of the federal public administration known as the **Canada Customs and Revenue Agency**.
- **New Agency**: The **Canada Border Services Agency** established under subsection 3(1).
- **Order P.C. 2003-2064**: A prior order that transferred control of certain parts of the CCRA to the former agency.

### **Continuity of Leadership**
- The individuals who held the positions of **President and Executive Vice-President** of the former agency **automatically become** the President and Executive Vice-President of the new agency.
- They are **deemed to have been appointed** under section 7, ensuring immediate continuity of leadership.

### **Continuity of Employees**
- Employees who held positions in the former agency **retain their positions** in the new agency, under the direction of the President.
- This avoids disruption and ensures continuity of expertise and institutional knowledge.

### **Continuity of Financial Appropriations**
- Any unexpended funds allocated to the former agency in the fiscal year when this Act comes into force are **deemed to be appropriated to the new agency**.

### **Continuity of Powers, Duties, and Functions**
- All powers, duties, and functions previously vested in or exercisable by the **President or employees of the former agency** are now vested in the **President or employees of the new agency**, **unless otherwise designated** by the Governor in Council (e.g., to a deputy minister).

### **Continuity of Legal Proceedings**
- Any **pending legal or administrative actions** against or involving the former agency or its President may be **continued against or by the new agency** in the same manner and to the same extent.
