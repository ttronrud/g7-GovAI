**Detailed Summary of the *Bridge to Strengthen Trade Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *Bridge to Strengthen Trade Act* (the "Act") is a federal legislative instrument designed to facilitate the construction, operation, and management of a major infrastructure project: a new or expanded bridge across the **Detroit River**, connecting **Windsor, Ontario** and **Detroit, Michigan**. The Act provides a comprehensive legal framework that streamlines regulatory processes, grants exemptions from certain environmental and procedural requirements, and establishes clear responsibilities for planning, mitigation, public consultation, and governance‚Äîparticularly in light of cross-border cooperation between Canada and the United States.

It is intended to accelerate the development of this critical transportation link, which supports trade, economic growth, and regional connectivity between Canada and the U.S., while minimizing environmental and regulatory barriers.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Definitions**

The Act establishes key definitions that guide its interpretation:

- **Bridge**: Refers specifically to a bridge spanning the Detroit River connecting Windsor, Ontario, to Detroit, Michigan, with piers located within a defined territorial boundary (as detailed in the schedule) and includes its approaches.
- **Construction**: Encompasses all activities related to building the bridge, parkway, or related works.
- **Operation**: Includes maintenance, repair, and management of the bridge or related infrastructure.
- **Parkway**: A roadway linking Highway 401 to the bridge or related works, located within the defined territory, and including ancillary structures.
- **Related Work**: Broadly defined to include:
  - Border services facilities in Michigan or within the territory;
  - Facilities useful to bridge operation (e.g., toll booths, duty-free shops, parking);
  - Roads or interchanges connecting Interstate 75 to the bridge or related works;
  - Accessory works;
  - Any other work specified by regulation.
- **Person**: Encompasses individuals, corporations, partnerships, trusts, joint ventures, or unincorporated associations.

---

### **2. Exemptions from Federal Environmental and Regulatory Laws**

This Act grants significant exemptions from key federal environmental and administrative laws to expedite project delivery:

#### **Exemption from Environmental Assessment Laws**
- **Impact Assessment Act (IAA)**: Does *not apply* to the construction, expansion, decommissioning, or abandonment of the bridge, parkway, or related works‚Äî**subject to subsection (2)**.
  - *Exception*: The **expansion, decommissioning, or abandonment** of these projects **are considered "projects" under section 81 of the IAA** and are therefore **subject to sections 82 to 91** (i.e., environmental assessment requirements) for such events.
- **Canadian Environmental Assessment Act (CEAA)**: The responsible authority (e.g., Minister of Transport) is **exempt from obligations under subsections 20(2) and 38(1)** regarding environmental assessments.
- **Canada Port Authority Environmental Assessment Regulations**: The **Windsor Port Authority** is exempt from its environmental assessment obligations.

#### **Exemption from Other Federal Acts**
- The **Fisheries Act**, **Canadian Navigable Waters Act**, **Species at Risk Act (SARA)**, **section 6 of the International Bridges and Tunnels Act (IBTA)**, and **Port Authorities Operations Regulations** do **not apply** to the construction or operation of the bridge, parkway, or related works.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important Note**: These exemptions are not absolute. They apply only to the *construction phase* and are subject to specific conditions (e.g., mitigation plans, public consultation).

---

### **3. Exemption Authority and Flexibility via Governor in Council**

- The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet) may, by **order**, exempt any person from requirements under any federal Act (e.g., permits, licenses, approvals) for construction or operation of the bridge or related works‚Äî**if the Governor in Council determines it is in the public interest**.
- This power allows flexibility in responding to unforeseen circumstances or economic justifications.

> üîç **Statutory Instrument (SI) Exception**: The Statutory Instruments Act does **not apply** to such orders. However, the order **must be published in the Canada Gazette** to be effective.

---

### **4. Deemed Authorization**

- If an exemption under subsection (1) above is granted, then **any authorization that would have been required under a federal Act (but was not obtained due to the exemption) is deemed to have been issued** for the purpose of applying that Act.
- This ensures legal continuity and avoids procedural delays in project execution.

---

### **5. Mandatory Environmental and Public Consultation Plans**

Before any construction or activity begins, a **detailed plan** must be filed with the **Minister of Transport**. These plans must include:

#### **A. Mitigation Measures**
- For **navigation impacts** (bridge construction);
- For **fish mortality or habitat disruption** (Fisheries Act);
- For **impacts on listed wildlife species or their critical habitats** (Species at Risk Act);
- For **adverse environmental effects** (general construction);
- For **results listed in the Port Authorities Operations Regulations** (e.g., port-related impacts).

#### **B. Public Consultation Plans**
- A process for **consulting the public** must be included in all relevant plans.

#### **C. Consultation Requirements**
- **Minister of Transport** must be consulted before filing a plan related to construction.
- **Minister of Fisheries and Oceans** must be consulted for fish-related activities.
- **Minister of the Environment** must be consulted for wildlife-related activities.
- **Windsor Port Authority** must be consulted for any activity in the Port of Windsor related to construction.

#### **D. Continuity and Deemed Compliance**
- Any authorization issued **before** the coming into force of this section is **deemed to be a filed plan**.
- If a plan was filed under the old version of the law, it **continues to apply** after the new version comes into force.

#### **E. Amendments**
- Any person who files a plan may **amend it** at any time.
- Amendments must be:
  - Filed with the Minister;
  - Include the same content and consultation requirements as the original plan;
  - **Replace** the original plan once filed.
- The plan must be **implemented and fully complied with**.

---

### **6. Designation of Initial Operator**

- The Minister may **designate a person as the initial operator** of the bridge and related works.
- **Paragraph 23(1)(b) of the IBTA** (which normally requires a government entity to operate the bridge) **does not apply** to the designated initial operator.
- The designation is **not subject to the Statutory Instruments Act** (i.e., no formal SI process), but must be in writing.

---

### **7. Financial and Corporate Authority**

#### **Financial Administration Act (FAA)**
- Any person may, with **Governor in Council approval**, perform actions under **paragraphs 90(1)(a) to (e)** of the FAA‚Äîsuch as issuing loans, grants, or financial instruments‚Äîfor purposes related to construction or operation of the bridge or related works.

#### **Parent Crown Corporations**
- A parent Crown corporation may, with **Governor in Council approval**, **sell or dispose of all or substantially all of its assets** for purposes related to the bridge or related works.

#### **Corporate Entity for Bridge Operations**
- A **corporation** established under **section 29 of the IBTA** (for purposes of bridge construction/operation) is authorized to:
  - Construct and operate the bridge or related works;
  - Authorize another person to construct or operate the bridge.
- **Establishment of pre-2012 corporations** is deemed to have been authorized under the **amended IBTA** (via section 180 of the *Jobs and Growth Act, 2012*).
- Actions taken by such corporations **before the Act came into force** are deemed to have been taken **as if sections 16, 17, 19‚Äì21 of the IBTA were in force** at the time.

#### **Corporate Status and Legal Standing**
- The corporation is **not an agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada**.
- It is **deemed to be a public agency** under the **Urban Cooperation Act of 1967 (Michigan law)**.
- The corporation may enter into agreements with:
  - The **government of Michigan** or its political subdivisions;
  - Any of their agencies or agents;
  - To establish a **public body corporate and compact entity under U.S. law**.

---

### **8. Ministerial Agreements with U.S. Entities**

- The **Minister of Transport** may enter into agreements with:
  - Any person;
  - The **U.S. government** or its political subdivisions;
  - Their agencies or agents.
- These agreements may include:
  - Financial assistance (e.g., grants, loans, guarantees);
  - Joint development or operational responsibilities.
- The Minister may take any measures necessary to:
  - Carry out the agreement;
  - Protect Canada‚Äôs interests;
  - Accept and hold security (e.g., collateral) granted under the agreement;
  - Release or realize on such security.
