**Detailed Summary of the *Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council Act***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council Act* establishes the **Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council (NSERC)** as a statutory corporation in Canada, tasked with promoting and supporting research in the **natural sciences and engineering**, excluding health sciences. It outlines the structure, functions, governance, financial operations, and reporting obligations of the Council, while ensuring it operates as an agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada.

This Act provides the legal foundation for NSERC’s role in advancing scientific knowledge, advising government on research policy, and managing its operations independently within defined boundaries.

---

### **Key Definitions**  
The Act defines several key terms:  
- **Council**: The governing body of NSERC, established under section 3.  
- **Minister**: A designated member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada, responsible for oversight and policy direction.  
- **President**: The chief executive officer of the Council, appointed by the Governor in Council.  
- **Vice-President**: Elected by the Council; acts as President in their absence or incapacity.

---

### **Establishment of the Council**  
- NSERC is established as a **corporation** composed of:  
  - One **President**  
  - Up to **18 other members**  
- Members are appointed by the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet).  
- The Council is intended to be independent in its operations while remaining accountable to Parliament and the Minister.

---

### **Core Functions of the Council**  
The Council has two primary responsibilities:  
1. **Promote and assist research** in the natural sciences and engineering (excluding health sciences).  
2. **Advise the Minister** on matters related to such research when referred by the Minister.

These functions include funding research, supporting innovation, and providing strategic guidance to the federal government on science and engineering policy.

---

### **Financial Powers and Operations**  
- The Council may **expend funds** appropriated by Parliament or earned through its activities (e.g., publishing scholarly materials).  
- It may **publish, sell, or distribute** scholarly, scientific, and technical information relevant to its work, ensuring broader dissemination of knowledge.

---

### **Appointment and Tenure of Members**  
- **President**:  
  - Appointed by the Governor in Council.  
  - Term of office **not exceeding five years**, as determined by the Governor in Council.  
- **Other Members**:  
  - Appointed for terms **not exceeding three years**.  
  - Terms are structured so that **fewer than half of the members retire each year**, ensuring continuity and stability.  
- **Re-appointment**:  
  - Retiring members (President or otherwise) are eligible for re-appointment in the same or a different capacity.

---

### **Vice-Presidency**  
- The Council may **elect one of its members** to serve as Vice-President with the approval of the Governor in Council.  
- In the event of the **absence or incapacity of the President**, or if the office becomes vacant, the **Vice-President assumes all powers and responsibilities** of the President.

---

### **Compensation and Expenses**  
- **President**:  
  - Paid a salary and expenses fixed by the Governor in Council.  
- **Other Members**:  
  - Serve **without remuneration**.  
  - Entitled to **reasonable travel and other expenses** incurred while performing duties.  
- **Additional Compensation**:  
  - With Council approval, members may be paid **extra remuneration** for duties performed beyond their ordinary responsibilities.

---

### **Head Office and Meetings**  
- The **head office** is located at a place in Canada designated by the Governor in Council.  
- The Council must **meet at least twice per year**, and may meet at any time and place it deems necessary.

---

### **Leadership and Executive Structure**  
- The **President** is the **chief executive officer** and has full supervision and direction over the Council’s work and staff.  
- An **Executive Committee** is established, consisting of:  
  - The President  
  - The Vice-President  
  - At least **six other members** elected by the Council  
- This committee may:  
  - Exercise powers and perform functions assigned by the Council via by-law  
  - Submit **minutes of its proceedings** to the full Council at each meeting

---

### **By-Laws and Committees**  
- The Council may make **by-laws** with the approval of the Governor in Council to govern its internal operations.  
- By-laws may establish:  
  - Standing, special, or other committees  
  - Advisory committees that may include **non-members** (e.g., experts, academics, industry representatives)  
- Members of **advisory committees** who are not Council members may be paid **remuneration and expenses** by the Governor in Council.

---

### **Employment and Staffing**  
- The Council may:  
  - Appoint **officers and employees** necessary for its operations  
  - Define their **duties and employment conditions**  
- Salaries and expenses for these staff members are set by the Council **with the approval of the Governor in Council**.  
- The Council may also authorize the President or another officer to appoint **temporary personnel** for up to **six months** to perform short-term duties.

---

### **Legal Status and Authority**  
- The Council is **an agent of Her Majesty** (the Crown) for all purposes.  
- It may:  
  - Enter into **contracts** in the name of Her Majesty or in its own name  
  - Acquire property (e.g., land, equipment, funds)  
  - Exercise powers only as an agent of the Crown  
- Any property acquired by the Council becomes the **property of Her Majesty**, though it may be held in the name of the Council.  
- Legal actions (suits, claims) regarding rights or obligations of the Council may be brought **in the name of the Council**, even though it acts on behalf of the Crown.

---

### **Superannuation and Legal Exclusions**  
- The **Public Service Superannuation Act does not apply** to Council members **unless** the Governor in Council specifically directs otherwise.  
- This ensures flexibility in compensation and retirement arrangements for Council members.

---

### **Property and Asset Management**  
- The Council may acquire money, securities, or property through **gifts, bequests, or other means**.  
- It may **expend, administer, or dispose of** such assets **subject to any conditions** attached to the gift or bequest.  
- This provision allows NSERC to build endowments or long-term funding sources without violating donor restrictions.

---

### **Reporting Requirements**  
- The **President** must submit a **fiscal year report** to the Minister within **four months** after the end of each fiscal year.  
  - The report includes:  
    - Summary of activities  
    - Financial statements  
- The Minister is required to **lay the report before Parliament** within **15 days** of receiving it, or on one of the first 15 days after Parliament resumes sitting if it is not then in session.

---

### **Transition Provisions (Repealed Section)**  
- A transitional clause (now repealed) stated that obligations under sections 17 and 18 of the Act (related to reporting and financial accountability) **continued for the fiscal year beginning April 1, 2012**, but **did not apply to any subsequent fiscal years**.  
- This reflects a temporary adjustment during a legislative transition, now obsolete.

---

### **Key Principles and Implications**  
1. **Independence with Accountability**:  
   - NSERC operates independently in promoting science and engineering, but remains accountable to Parliament through reporting and ministerial oversight.

2. **Expert Governance**:  
   - The Council is composed of experts in science and engineering, ensuring technical credibility in research decisions.

3. **Transparency and Public Access**:  
   - The Council’s ability to publish and distribute scholarly information promotes open science and public access to knowledge.

4. **Flexibility in Operations**:  
   - By-laws and advisory committees allow adaptation to evolving research needs and stakeholder input.

5. **Crown Agency Model**:  
   - The structure ensures that NSERC functions as a Crown entity, maintaining fiscal and legal accountability while enabling effective research support.

---

### **Conclusion**  
The *Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council Act* provides a comprehensive legal framework for the establishment, governance, and operation of NSERC. It balances **scientific independence**, **government accountability**, and **financial sustainability**, enabling NSERC to fulfill its mission of advancing research in natural sciences and engineering across Canada. The Act ensures that the Council can operate efficiently, with expert leadership, transparent reporting, and flexibility in adapting to emerging challenges in science and technology policy.
