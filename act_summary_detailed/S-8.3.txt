### **Detailed Summary of the *Seized Property Management Act* (Canada)**

---

#### **1. Introduction and Citation**
The *Seized Property Management Act* is an Act of Parliament of Canada that establishes a comprehensive legal framework for the **seizure, custody, management, forfeiture, and disposal of property** associated with designated offences or proceeds of crime. It is designed to ensure efficient and accountable handling of such property by the federal government, while also promoting cooperation among law enforcement agencies and enabling the sharing of financial benefits from forfeitures.

The Act may be cited as the **Seized Property Management Act**.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**
The Act defines several key terms to ensure clarity and consistency in its application:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Attorney General** | Refers to the Attorney General of Canada and their lawful deputy. This role is central to initiating seizures and applications for management orders. |
| **Designated Offence** | Has the same meaning as in subsection 462.3(1) of the *Criminal Code*. These are serious offences such as drug trafficking, terrorism, or money laundering. |
| **Offence-Related Property** | Property used, intended for use, or connected to the commission of a crime — defined under the *Controlled Drugs and Substances Act* or the *Cannabis Act*. |
| **Proceeds of Crime** | As defined in subsection 462.3(1) of the *Criminal Code*, includes assets derived from criminal activity. |
| **Seized Property** | Any property seized under federal or provincial law in connection with a designated offence. |
| **Restraint Property** | Property subject to a legal restraint order (e.g., under the *Criminal Code* or drug acts). |
| **Management Order** | A court-issued order allowing the Minister to take control and manage seized property. |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Public Works and Government Services, who is responsible for managing the property. |
| **Proceeds Account** | A government account established to hold proceeds from forfeited property. |
| **Working Capital Account** | A separate account to fund operational costs (e.g., maintenance, improvements) related to managed property. |
| **Terrorism Offence** | As defined in the *Criminal Code*, includes acts of terrorism or terrorist financing. |

> *Note:* Certain outdated definitions (e.g., "designated drug offence," "enterprise crime offence") have been repealed.

---

#### **3. Primary Purposes of the Act**
The Act serves five main purposes:

1. **To authorize the Minister to provide consultative and operational support** to law enforcement agencies regarding:
   - Seizure or restraint of property in connection with designated offences.
   - Property that may be proceeds of crime or offence-related property.

2. **To provide services to federal, provincial, and municipal public officials** involved in:
   - Seizure, restraint, custody, management, forfeiture, or disposal of property linked to criminal activity.

3. **To authorize the Minister to manage property** that is:
   - Seized or restrained under federal or provincial laws;
   - Forfeited under federal or provincial statutes;
   - Paid under subsection 18(2) of the *Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act*.

4. **To enable sharing of proceeds** from forfeitures or fines under specific conditions:
   - When law enforcement agencies participated in investigations leading to forfeiture or fines.
   - When property is forfeited to Her Majesty in right of a province, proceeds are shared with that province’s government, as directed.

5. **To allow the Minister to manage and dispose of forfeited property** in a way that supports public interest — including preserving economic value and maintaining operational viability.

---

#### **4. Minister’s Responsibility for Custody and Management**

The Minister is responsible for the **custody and management** of property under the following circumstances:

- Property seized under warrants issued by the Attorney General under:
  - Sections 83.13, 462.32, 462.321, 487 of the *Criminal Code*;
  - Sections 11 (Controlled Drugs and Substances Act) or 87 (Cannabis Act).
- Property subject to restraint orders under:
  - Sections 83.13, 462.33, 490.8 of the *Criminal Code*;
  - Sections 14 (Controlled Drugs and Substances Act) or 91 (Cannabis Act).
- Property forfeited under:
  - Subsections 14(5), 39.03(5), 18(1), or 39.06(1) of the *Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act*;
  - Subparagraph 715.34(1)(e)(i) of the *Criminal Code*.
- Property forfeited under other federal or provincial laws **if the Minister agrees** to manage it.
- Property subject to a **management order** issued under this Act.

> **Important**: Once the Minister takes possession, they remain responsible for the property **until it is disposed of**, even if it is later forfeited.

Additionally, the Minister is responsible for managing:
- Proceeds of crime,
- Offence-related property,
- Property forfeited as a result of proceedings by the Attorney General (not previously under the Minister’s control).

> **Note**: Nothing in this Act overrides existing laws (e.g., *Criminal Code*, *Controlled Drugs and Substances Act*) regarding property rights or criminal procedures.

---

#### **5. Transfer of Control to the Minister**

Every person who controls property subject to a **management order** must:
- **Transfer control to the Minister as soon as practicable**, unless:
  - The property is needed as evidence, or
  - It is necessary for ongoing investigations.

> **Exception**: Evidence or investigative materials may not be surrendered.

---

#### **6. Reporting Requirements**

If the Minister detains property **outside the location specified in the required court report** (under paragraph 462.32(4)(b) of the *Criminal Code*), they must:
- Prepare a report in the **prescribed form**;
- File it with the **clerk of the issuing court** in the prescribed manner.

> This ensures transparency and accountability in property location and custody.

---

#### **7. Power to Issue Management Orders**

The **Attorney General** (or with written consent) may apply to a **judge or justice** for a **management order** over seized property — **excluding**:
- Controlled substances (as defined in the *Controlled Drugs and Substances Act*),
- Cannabis (as defined in the *Cannabis Act*).

> **Key Provisions**:
- Applications may be heard **simultaneously** with orders for seizure under paragraph 490(1)(b) of the *Criminal Code*.
- A judge or justice may issue a management order **if they believe the property may be needed for forfeiture purposes** under any Act of Parliament.

---

#### **8. Powers of the Minister under a Management Order**

The Minister has specific powers to manage property under a management order:

1. **Interlocutory Sale**:
   - May sell perishable or rapidly depreciating property (e.g., food, clothing) before final disposition.

2. **Destruction of Property**:
   - May destroy property with little or no value, **but only after**:
     - Applying to a court for a **destruction order**;
     - Giving **notice** to any person with a potential interest in the property;
     - The court determines the property has **little or no financial or other value**.

3. **Forfeiture of Property**:
   - May apply to a court to have **non-real property or conveyances** forfeited to Her Majesty and disposed of, provided:
     - A public notice is published (60 days);
     - No one files an objection during that period.

> **Note**: Real property or vehicles are excluded from this forfeiture power.

---

#### **9. Termination of Management Orders**

A management order **ceases to apply** when:
- The property is **returned** in accordance with law;
- It is **destroyed**;
- It is **forfeited to Her Majesty**.

> **Clarification**: If property is sold, the management order applies to the **net proceeds** of the sale.

---

#### **10. Conditions and Modifications**

- A management order may include **conditions** (e.g., preservation, use restrictions) set by the judge or justice.
- The Minister may **apply to cancel or vary** any condition at any time.

---

#### **11. Minister’s Authority to Carry Out the Act’s Purposes**

The Minister may, subject to applicable laws and regulations, do the following:

1. **Provide consultative services** to law enforcement and public officials on:
   - Seizure, restraint, custody, management, forfeiture, and disposal of criminal property.

2. **Manage property** (as defined in subsections 4(1)–(3)) in a manner deemed appropriate, including:
   - Advancing money at **commercial interest rates** to:
     - Maintain operations;
     - Meet environmental, labor, or industrial standards;
     - Make improvements to preserve value.

3. **Dispose of forfeited property** to Her Majesty **without restriction**, even if other laws would otherwise prohibit it.

4. **Dispose of property forfeited to a province** only **with the consent** of that province’s government.
