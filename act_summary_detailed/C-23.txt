### **IV. Definition of "Threats to the Security of Canada"**

This is a **comprehensive and legally precise** list of activities that constitute threats to national security. The Act explicitly **excludes lawful advocacy, protest, or dissent**, unless carried out in conjunction with prohibited activities.

#### Threats Include:
1. **Espionage or sabotage**:
   - Against Canada or detrimental to Canadian interests.
   - Activities supporting such acts.

2. **Foreign Influenced Activities**:
   - Clandestine, deceptive, or threatening activities by foreign actors within or related to Canada.
   - Harmful to Canadian interests.

3. **Activities Directed at Serious Violence**:
   - Threats or use of violence against persons or property for political, religious, or ideological goals:
     - Within Canada
     - In foreign states
   - Includes acts intended to achieve such goals through violence.

4. **Undermining the Constitutionally Established Government**:
   - Covert unlawful acts aimed at:
     - Destroying or overthrowing Canadaâ€™s democratic system by violence.

> âœ… **Exclusion**: Lawful advocacy, protest, or dissent is **not** a threat â€” **unless** it is combined with one of the above activities.

### **V. Establishment and Structure of CSIS**

#### 1. **Establishment of the Service**
- CSIS is formally established by the Act.
- Composed of the **Director** and **employees** of the Service.

#### 2. **Headquarters**
- Principal office located in the **National Capital Region**, as defined in the *National Capital Act*.
- The Director may, with **Ministerial approval**, establish **additional offices** across Canada.

#### 3. **Appointment of the Director**
- Appointed by the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet).
- Term of office: **Up to five years**.
- Eligibility for reappointment: Yes, for further terms of up to five years.
- **Maximum cumulative term**: **Ten years** in total (no individual may serve more than ten years combined).

#### 4. **Vacancy or Incapacity**
- If the Director is absent or incapacitated, the Governor in Council may appoint an **interim officer**.
- This person serves for **up to six months** and has **full powers and duties** of the Director.
- Paid at a rate fixed by the Governor in Council.

#### 5. **Salary and Benefits**
- Director receives a salary set by the Governor in Council.
- Paid reasonable **travel and living expenses** during duty performance.

#### 6. **Superannuation and Pension**
- The **Public Service Superannuation Act** applies to the Director, **except**:
  - A Director appointed from outside the public service may **opt into the Diplomatic Service (Special) Superannuation Act**.
  - If so, the provisions of that Act (excluding tenure) apply from the date of appointment.

### **VI. Direction and Oversight by the Minister**

- The **Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness** provides **directions** to the Director.
- These directions may cover operational policies or specific actions.
- A **copy of any such direction must be immediately provided to the Review Agency** (National Security and Intelligence Review Agency â€” NSIRA).
- Directions issued under this provision are **not considered statutory instruments** under the *Statutory Instruments Act* â€” meaning they do not require parliamentary approval or public notice.

### **VII. Reporting Requirements**

The Director must submit **regular operational reports** to the Minister and to the Review Agency.

#### Reporting Frequency:
- Every **12-month period** (or a shorter period as specified by the Minister).
- Reports may be submitted at any time the Minister specifies.

#### Required Content of Reports:
1. **General description and number of measures** taken in response to each of the four types of threats to national security (as defined in section 2).
2. **Number of warrants issued** under Section 21.1(3) and **refused applications** under Section 21.1(1).
3. **Details of measures taken under issued warrants** (for each threat).
4. **Description of intelligence collection activities** involving employees designated under Section 20.1(6) or (8), where such acts or omissions would otherwise constitute offences.
5. **Details of the acts or omissions**.
6. **Information under paragraphs 20.1(24)(a) to (e)** (likely relating to training, compliance, or conduct).
7. **Training records** of designated employees and senior employees.

> These reports ensure transparency and accountability while allowing for operational discretion.

### **VIII. Consultation Requirements**

The Director must **consult the Deputy Minister** on:
- General operational policies of the Service.
- Any matter requiring consultation under directions issued by the Minister.

#### Pre-Application Consultation for Warrants:
- Before applying for a warrant under:
  - Section 21 (interception)
  - Section 21.1 (warrant for surveillance)
  - Section 22.21 (foreign influence)
  - Section 23 (production order)
- The Director or designated employee must **consult the Deputy Minister**.
- Same applies to:
  - Judicial authorization under Section 11.12
  - Production order under Section 20.4

> This ensures that sensitive intelligence activities are subject to **inter-agency review** and oversight.

### **IX. Human Resources and Management Authority**

The Director has **exclusive authority** over:
- Appointment of employees (except those attached or seconded).
- Terms and conditions of employment.
- Human resources management functions (including recruitment, training, discipline, and grievances), **subject to regulations**.

#### Exceptions:
- The Director **cannot** exercise powers under the *Federal Public Sector Labour Relations Act* (FPLRA) without limitations.

#### Grievance Procedures:
- For employees **not attached or seconded**:
  - Disputes are resolved by **arbitration**, not by internal or union-based processes.
  - Adjudication must be conducted by a **full-time member** of the **Federal Public Sector Labour Relations and Employment Board**.
