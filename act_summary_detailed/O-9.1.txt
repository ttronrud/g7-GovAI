### **Detailed Summary of the Olympic (1976) Act**

---

#### **1. Purpose and Context of the Act**

The *Olympic (1976) Act* was enacted to facilitate and commemorate the **first-ever hosting of the Summer Olympic Games in Canada in 1976**, specifically in Montreal. The Act serves multiple key objectives:

- To **finance the organization and hosting** of the Games.
- To **promote and generate public and international interest** in the event through commemorative products.
- To **stimulate domestic and international markets** for coins and stamps as souvenirs and collectibles.
- To **overcome legal limitations** in Canada’s existing lottery laws that previously restricted the use of lotteries to fund such large-scale events.

The Act is a comprehensive legislative framework that governs the issuance of commemorative coins and stamps, establishes financial mechanisms for revenue generation, and protects intellectual property rights related to the Olympic event.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

The Act introduces several key definitions to clarify its scope:

- **Olympic**: Refers to the **1976 Summer Olympic Games** held in Montreal.
- **Olympic coin**: A special commemorative coin issued under section 3.
- **Olympic Corporation**: The **Organizing Committee of the 1976 Olympic Games**, incorporated under Quebec law as a body corporate.
- **Olympic stamp**: A special postage stamp commemorating the Olympic, issued under section 6.

---

#### **3. Issuance and Legal Tender Status of Olympic Coins**

##### **(a) Authorization to Issue Coins**
- The **Governor in Council**, on recommendation of the **Minister of Finance**, may by **proclamation** authorize the issue of:
  - **Silver coins** of $5 and $10, bearing the years 1973–1976.
  - **Gold coins** of **$100**, bearing the date 1976.

##### **(b) Standards for Coins**
- The **dimensions, design, weight, fineness (purity), and remedy allowance** (i.e., tolerance for wear or damage) of Olympic coins can be prescribed by proclamation.

##### **(c) Legal Tender Status**
- **Gold Olympic coins** are legal tender for amounts **up to $100**.
- **Silver Olympic coins** are legal tender for amounts **up to $20**.
- If multiple payments are due on the same day, the total is treated as a single amount.
- Coins that are **bent, mutilated, defaced, or reduced in weight** (other than by normal wear) are **not legal tender**.
- The **Governor in Council may call in** (i.e., withdraw from circulation) any Olympic coin at any time.

##### **(d) Price and Sales Restrictions**
- Olympic coins **cannot be sold below face value** or under any arrangement where the **net proceeds to the Government are less than face value**.
- The **total face value** of all issued or sold Olympic coins **cannot exceed $450 million**, excluding coins redeemed.
- If the Minister of Finance determines that the circulation of Olympic coins could **undermine the Canadian currency**, he may **redeem** a portion of them.

##### **(e) Prohibition of Subsidies**
- No individual involved in the promotion or operation of the Olympic or with a financial interest in it may offer **subsidies, commissions, or benefits** to encourage purchases of Olympic coins that would result in **net proceeds below face value**.

---

#### **4. Promotion and Distribution of Olympic Coins**

- The **Postmaster General** is authorized to administer the **promotion, distribution, and merchandising** of Olympic coins within and outside Canada.
- The **minting and distribution** of Olympic coins is permitted during 1973–1976.

---

#### **5. Olympic Stamps and Postal Products**

##### **(a) Authorization to Issue Stamps**
- The Postmaster General may issue:
  - **Special commemorative postage stamps**.
  - **Postal-related products** (e.g., philatelic items, souvenirs featuring stamps or reproductions).

##### **(b) Definition of Postal-Related Products**
- Includes:
  - Philatelic products (e.g., stamp books, albums).
  - Souvenirs or articles featuring stamps or reproductions, even if non-functional.

##### **(c) Pricing and Revenue**
- Olympic stamps may be sold at:
  - The **postage rate** indicated on the stamp.
  - An **additional amount** approved by the Governor in Council and regulated by the Postmaster General, to fund the Olympic Games.
- The **net proceeds** from sales (after production costs) are **not considered postal revenue** under the *Post Office Act* and are **paid into the Consolidated Revenue Fund**.

##### **(d) Net Proceeds Calculation**
- For **stamps**: Net proceeds are derived from:
  - The additional amount above postage.
  - If the sale is for **collecting purposes**, not postage, the full net proceeds are considered.
- For **postal-related products**: Net proceeds are determined by the Postmaster General.

##### **(e) Sales Authorization**
- The Postmaster General may authorize **agents** (inside or outside Canada) to sell Olympic stamps and products.
- Agents may receive **commissions** approved by regulation and Governor in Council (not restricted by standard Post Office Act provisions).

---

#### **6. Lottery Scheme for Financial Assistance**

##### **(a) Legalization of Lotteries**
- Despite existing prohibitions in the *Criminal Code* on gaming and betting, the Act **legalizes a lottery scheme** operated by the **Olympic Corporation** or its agents in any province.
- The lottery may be conducted **only during periods approved by the lieutenant governor in council** of that province.
- **Proceeds must be used exclusively** for:
  - Financial assistance to the Olympic Games.
  - Development of **amateur sport** in that province.

##### **(b) Legal Activities**
- Any person involved in the lottery scheme may perform acts listed in **paragraphs 189(1)(a) to (f)** or subsection 189(4) of the *Criminal Code*, as long as the scheme is operating lawfully.

##### **(c) Definition of "Lottery Scheme"**
- As defined in section 190 of the *Criminal Code*.

---

#### **7. Financial Management: The Olympic Account**

- A dedicated **Olympic Account** is established in the **accounts of Canada**.

##### **(a) Credits to the Account**
- Proceeds from:
  - Sale of Olympic coins (after deducting production costs).
  - Net proceeds from sale of Olympic stamps and postal-related products.

##### **(b) Payments from the Consolidated Revenue Fund**
- The following are charged to the Olympic Account and paid from the Consolidated Revenue Fund:
  - Administrative, merchandising, distribution, and promotion costs (as determined by the Postmaster General).
  - Net costs of any redemption of Olympic coins.
  - Payments to the Olympic Corporation (as per section 9).

##### **(c) Disbursement to the Olympic Corporation**
- The Minister of Finance may authorize **payments** from the Olympic Account to the Olympic Corporation **up to $260 million**.
- Payments are **limited to the amount available in the account**.
- **Exception during first 12 months**:
  - Payments may be made even if the account has a deficit.
  - The deficit is capped at **$500,000**.
  - After 12 months, no further deficits are allowed.

##### **(d) Gold Pricing**
- The **selling price of gold** used in producing Olympic coins is based on the **market price** of gold at the time of sale, as determined by the Minister of Finance for the Exchange Fund Account.

---

#### **8. Intellectual Property Rights**

##### **(a) Trademark Protection**
- The Olympic Corporation is deemed a **registered Canadian amateur athletic association** under the *Income Tax Act*.
- It is a **public authority** under the *Trade Marks Act*.

- The following are **protected trademarks**:
  - Words: “Olympic,” “Olympics,” “Montreal,” “Jeux,” “1976,” “76,” “XXI,” etc.
  - The **two beaver representations** in Schedule I.
  - The **symbol in Schedule II** is the **official Olympic symbol**.

##### **(b) Prohibition on Unauthorized Use**
- From **June 13, 1975, to January 1, 1977**:
  - No person may **adopt, use, or sell** any mark or design that:
    - Is a mark of the Olympic Corporation.
    - Is similar to or implies a reference to the Olympic.
    - Is likely to be mistaken for an Olympic mark.
  - This applies to **businesses, premises, trademarks, or advertising**.

##### **(c) Prior Adoption Exception**
- If a person adopted such a mark **before June 13, 1975**, they may continue to use it **only**:
  - For goods/services in the **same class**.
  - Or in connection with **another business**, **if authorized** by a license from the Olympic Corporation or by a by-law.
