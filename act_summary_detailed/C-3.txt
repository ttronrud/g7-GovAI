### **Detailed Summary of the *Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation Act***  

The **Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation Act** establishes and governs the **Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC)**, a federal agency responsible for protecting consumer deposits in Canadian financial institutions, ensuring financial stability, and acting as a resolution authority when necessary. This Act provides a comprehensive legal framework for the structure, governance, powers, responsibilities, and operational mechanisms of CDIC.

---

## **1. Purpose and Objectives of the Act**

The primary purpose of the Act is to:

- **Provide deposit insurance** to protect individuals and businesses against the loss of part or all of their deposits in member institutions.
- **Promote the stability of Canada’s financial system** by minimizing the risk of bank failures and maintaining public confidence in the banking sector.
- **Act as a resolution authority**—that is, to step in and manage or resolve failing financial institutions to prevent systemic risk.
- **Minimize CDIC’s exposure to loss** while achieving its objectives, subject to exceptions in times of financial crisis.

> The Act explicitly states that CDIC must act in a manner that minimizes its own losses, unless circumstances require otherwise to preserve financial stability.

---

## **2. Key Definitions and Terminology**

The Act defines key terms used throughout:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Member Institution** | Any corporation that has deposit insurance under this Act (e.g., banks, credit unions, trust companies). |
| **Bank** | A bank listed in Schedule I or II to the *Bank Act*. |
| **Affiliate** | For banks: defined under the *Bank Act*; for non-banks: would be considered affiliated if the institution were a bank under the *Bank Act*. |
| **Federal Institution** | Includes banks, companies under the *Trust and Loan Companies Act*, and retail associations under the *Cooperative Credit Associations Act*. |
| **Provincial Institution** | A company authorized to accept public deposits and engaged in a business substantially similar to those covered by the *Trust and Loan Companies Act*. |
| **Deposit and Depositor** | As defined in the schedule; includes all insured deposits and those individuals or entities holding them. |
| **Deposit Insurance** | The insurance coverage provided under paragraph 7(a) of the Act. |
| **Premium Year** | The period from May 1 to April 30, used for calculating and collecting insurance premiums. |
| **Professional Trustee** | A person or entity acting as a trustee of funds for others, including public trustees, governments, lawyers, notaries, and regulated trust companies. |
| **Nominee Broker** | A person who makes deposits on behalf of another individual. A **nominee broker deposit** is one made through such an arrangement. |
| **Subordinated Debt** | Debt that ranks below deposit liabilities in insolvency; includes conversion privileges or rights to acquire such debt. |
| **Shareholder** | Includes holders of membership shares in federal credit unions. |
| **Bridge Institution** | A federal institution designated by order as a "bridge" institution to support transition during financial restructuring. |
| **Representation** | Any oral or written statement, including advertisements, trade names, or signs. |
| **Returned Payment** | Any payment made by CDIC that is returned or remains under its control. |

## **3. Establishment and Governance of CDIC**

### **Establishment**
- The Act establishes the **Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC)** as a corporation under the authority of Her Majesty in right of Canada.
- CDIC is **an agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada** for all purposes under the Act.

### **Head Office**
- The head office is located in **Ottawa, Ontario**.
- CDIC may establish offices or employ agents in **any part of Canada**.

### **Board of Directors (Board)**
The Board consists of:

1. The **Chairperson** (appointed by the Governor in Council).
2. The **President and Chief Executive Officer** of the Corporation (as per section 105(5) of the *Financial Administration Act*).
3. The following appointed officials:
   - Governor of the Bank of Canada
   - Deputy Minister of Finance
   - Superintendent of Financial Institutions
   - Commissioner of the Financial Consumer Agency of Canada
4. A **Deputy Superintendent of Financial Institutions**, appointed by the Minister.
5. **Not more than six other members**, appointed by the Minister with **Governor in Council approval**.

#### **Eligibility Requirements for Board Members**
- **Not eligible** if:
  - Employed in federal public administration or receiving public salary.
  - A member of the Senate, House of Commons, or provincial legislature.
  - A director, officer, or employee of a federal, provincial, or local cooperative credit society.
- **Alternate directors** may be designated by a director for attendance in their absence.

#### **Vacancies and Acting Roles**
- A vacancy on the Board does **not impair** the ability of remaining directors to act.
- If the Chairperson is vacant, the Minister may appoint an **acting Chairperson** for up to **90 days**.

#### **Remuneration**
- Directors (except those in paragraph 1(b)) receive **only reasonable travel and living expenses**.
- Directors in paragraph 1(b) (e.g., senior officials) may receive **fixed remuneration** approved by the Governor in Council.
- The Chairperson receives remuneration set by the Governor in Council.

#### **Appointment of Chairperson**
- The Chairperson must be:
  - A **Canadian citizen** residing in Canada.
  - Not a member of Parliament or provincial legislature.
  - Not an employee of a financial institution.
- Appointed for a term of **good behaviour**, with potential for reappointment and removal at any time by the Governor in Council.

## **4. Powers and Authorities of CDIC**

CDIC is granted broad powers to fulfill its mandate, including:

### **Core Powers**
- **Provide deposit insurance** to members.
- **Acquire assets** from member institutions (e.g., during insolvency or resolution).
- **Make or guarantee loans** to member institutions.
- **Make deposits** with member institutions.
- **Assume liabilities** of member institutions.
- **Act as liquidator, receiver, or inspector** of member institutions or subsidiaries.
- **Assume winding-up costs** when acting as liquidator or receiver.
- **Guarantee fees and costs** of liquidators or receivers.
- **Acquire shares and subordinated debt** of member institutions.
- **Make advances** to pay claims against a member institution and become subrogated as an unsecured creditor.
- **Conduct inspections** of member institutions as authorized.
- **Acquire, hold, and dispose of real and personal property**.
- **Settle or compromise claims** involving CDIC.

### **Special Powers (Under Governor in Council Order)**
CDIC may:
- **Procure the incorporation** of a new corporation whose shares are held by, on behalf of, or in trust for CDIC.
- **Acquire all shares** of a corporation that will be held by, on behalf of, or in trust for CDIC.

> These incorporated entities are **not considered agents** of CDIC or of Her Majesty in right of Canada.

### **Compliance with Ministerial Directions**
- CDIC must comply with **directions of general application** issued in writing by the Minister, especially when carrying out its powers under paragraph 10(1)(b).

### **Contract Administration**
- CDIC may administer any contract related to deposit insurance entered into by the Minister under **section 60.2 of the Financial Administration Act**.

---

## **5. Exception to Loss-Minimization Principle**

While CDIC is required to minimize its exposure to loss, the **Governor in Council may issue an order** exempting CDIC from this requirement **only when**:

- The Minister, after consulting the **Board, Governor of the Bank of Canada, and Superintendent**, determines that **failing to minimize losses** would:
  - **Harm financial system stability**, or
  - **Undermine public confidence** in that stability.

> The order is **effective immediately upon issuance** and is **not subject to the Statutory Instruments Act**.

### **Publication and Premium Recovery**
- The Minister must publish a notice in the *Canada Gazette* if the order is made or repealed, unless doing so would harm financial stability.
- After publication, CDIC must **collect a special premium** from member institutions to recover the losses incurred due to the deviation from loss-minimization.

> This ensures accountability and financial sustainability during exceptional circumstances.

---

## **6. Exemptions from Regulatory Provisions**

To facilitate the acquisition and management of shares and subordinated debt, the **Minister may issue an order** exempting specific persons or shares from certain provisions of:

### **Bank Act** (Sections 47.03, 47.04, 47.06, 47.09, 47.11, 47.15, 47.17, 47.18, 60.1, 79.2(1), and others)
### **Insurance Companies Act** (Sections 407, 407.01–407.03, 407.1, 407.2, 408, 411, 428, 430)
### **Trust and Loan Companies Act** (Sections 375, 375.1, 376, 379, 396, 399)

#### Conditions and Duration
- Exemptions are **subject to conditions**
