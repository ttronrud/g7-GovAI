### **Detailed Summary of the Feeds Act**

---

#### **1. Purpose and Scope of the Act**
The *Feeds Act* establishes a comprehensive regulatory framework governing the **manufacture, sale, importation, exportation, packaging, labelling, and distribution of feeds** in Canada. The primary objectives of the Act are to:

- Ensure that feeds are safe for livestock consumption;
- Protect human and animal health;
- Prevent environmental harm;
- Maintain food safety and quality standards;
- Provide a clear system of oversight, inspection, and enforcement.

The Act applies to **any substance or mixture** intended for use in livestock nutrition — including amino acids, vitamins, proteins, minerals, fats, and additives — and to any activity directly related to such feeds.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Feed** | Any substance or mixture containing nutrients (e.g., proteins, vitamins, minerals) or additives used to meet the nutritional needs of livestock, prevent nutritional disorders, or improve animal health. Includes pelletizing, colouring, foaming, or flavouring agents. |
| **Establishment** | Any place — including conveyances like trucks or ships — where feed is manufactured, stored, packaged, or labelled. |
| **Livestock** | Animals designated by regulation as livestock (e.g., cattle, pigs, poultry, sheep). |
| **Document** | Any record or marking that contains information readable by a person or machine (e.g., invoices, lab reports, labels). |
| **Environment** | All natural components of Earth — air, land, water, living organisms, and their interacting systems. |
| **Inspection Mark** | A prescribed mark, stamp, seal, symbol, or design used to indicate compliance with feed safety standards. |
| **Inspector/Analyst** | Designated officials (under section 6) responsible for inspecting feed and verifying compliance. |
| **Item to Which This Act Applies** | Includes feeds, materials used in feed production, and documents related to those activities. |
| **Label** | Any text, symbol, or design on or attached to a feed or package. |
| **Penalty** | An administrative monetary penalty under the *Agriculture and Agri-Food Administrative Monetary Penalties Act* for violations. |

---

#### **3. Core Provisions and Obligations**

##### **(1) Approval and Registration Requirements**
- **No feed may be manufactured, sold, or imported into Canada unless:**
  - It has been **approved by the Minister** or **registered**;
  - It **conforms to prescribed standards**;
  - It is **packaged and labelled in accordance with regulations**.
- **Exemption**: Whole seeds or grains from cultivated farm crops are exempt from approval if they are free from prescribed deleterious substances (e.g., toxins, contaminants).

##### **(2) Prohibition of Harmful Feeds**
- No person may manufacture, sell, import, or export any feed that:
  - Presents a **risk to human or animal health**;
  - Poses a **risk to the environment**.
- This includes feeds that could cause disease, contamination, or ecological damage.

##### **(3) Authorization for Handling Imported or Inter-Provincial Feeds**
- A person may only conduct prescribed activities (e.g., manufacturing, selling, distributing) regarding:
  - Feeds imported for sale;
  - Feeds destined for export;
  - Feeds being transported between provinces — **if authorized** by:
    - A **registration** under subsection 5.2(1), or
    - A **licence** issued under that subsection, or
    - Both a registration and a licence (as per regulations).

##### **(4) Establishment Registration**
- All activities involving imported or inter-provincial feeds must occur in an **establishment registered under section 5.3(1)**.
- Registration ensures the facility meets safety, hygiene, and quality control standards.

##### **(5) Prohibition on Unauthorized Use of Inspection Marks**
- **No person may:**
  - Apply or use an **inspection mark** without authorization;
  - Advertise or sell a product that bears an inspection mark or is associated with one.
- **Prohibition on imitation**: No person may use something that **resembles an inspection mark** in a way that could mislead consumers.
- **Presumption of intent**: A person found in possession of an item with an inspection mark is presumed to be **in possession for the purpose of advertising or selling** unless proven otherwise.

##### **(6) Recall and Market Withdrawal**
- Feeds subject to a **recall order** under the *Canadian Food Inspection Agency Act* (section 19(1)) **cannot be sold**.

##### **(7) Exemption for Livestock Producers**
- Feeds **manufactured by livestock producers** (e.g., farmers making their own feed) are **exempt from this Act** if:
  - They are **not sold**;
  - They do **not contain drugs or substances** that pose a risk to human or animal health or the environment.

---

#### **4. Regulatory Powers of the Minister**

The Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food (Minister) has broad authority to make **regulations** under this Act, including:

| Regulatory Area | Details |
|----------------|--------|
| **Feed Approval & Registration** | Sets criteria, application requirements, fees, and timelines for registration/approval. |
| **Standards** | Prescribes form, composition, safety, and quality standards for feeds. |
| **Inspection Marks** | Defines what marks can be used and how they must be applied. |
| **Packaging & Labelling** | Specifies requirements for labels, packaging, and information content. |
| **Sampling & Analysis** | Requires sampling and testing of feeds and related documents. |
| **Quality Management Programs** | Mandates implementation of quality control, preventive control, and safety programs. |
| **Designation of Livestock** | Can designate specific animals (e.g., birds) as livestock. |
| **Seizure & Forfeiture** | Authorizes seizure of non-compliant feeds and allows forfeiture to the Crown. |
| **Notice Requirements** | Requires entities to report if a feed presents a health or environmental risk. |
| **Incorporation by Reference** | Allows regulations to reference existing documents (e.g., scientific reports, foreign evaluations) without needing to reproduce them. |

> **Important Note on Incorporation by Reference**:  
> - Documents incorporated by reference must be **accessible** to the public at the time of alleged violation.  
> - A person is **not liable** for a violation if the relevant document was not accessible when the alleged contravention occurred.  
> - The document does **not need to be published in the Canada Gazette** just because it is referenced.

---

#### **5. Registration and Licensing System**

##### **(a) Person Registration (Section 5.2)**
- The Minister may register individuals to conduct activities related to imported or inter-provincial feeds.
- A registration may be issued **with or without conditions**.
- **Conditions** may include compliance with quality standards, record-keeping, or reporting requirements.
- The registration is **non-transferable** and must be maintained by the holder.

##### **(b) Establishment Registration (Section 5.3)**
- Establishments (e.g., processing plants, warehouses) may be registered to handle specific feed activities.
- The **holder of the registration** is the applicant (typically the business owner).
- Like individual registrations, these are **non-transferable** and subject to conditions.

##### **(c) Suspension, Cancellation, Renewal**
- The Minister may **amend, suspend, cancel, or renew** registrations or licences.
- Decisions are based on compliance, safety, or risk assessments.

##### **(d) Certificates for Export**
- The Minister may issue certificates to support the **export of feeds**, including details about composition, safety, and compliance.

---

#### **6. Inspector Powers and Duties**

##### **(a) Entry and Inspection Authority**
- An inspector may enter any place where feed is stored or processed **if there is reasonable grounds** to believe feed is present.
- Inspectors may:
  - Open packages;
  - Examine feed and take samples;
  - Require access to books, shipping documents, mixing instructions, or other records;
  - Remove items for testing or analysis.

##### **(b) Entry into Dwelling-Houses**
- Inspectors **cannot enter private dwellings without consent**.
- However, a **warrant** can be issued by a justice of the peace if:
  - There is reasonable belief that feed is present;
  - Entry is necessary for enforcement;
  - Entry has been refused or is likely to be refused.
- Force may only be used if accompanied by a **peace officer** and specifically authorized in the warrant.

##### **(c) Duty of Cooperation**
- Owners or persons in charge of premises must:
  - Provide **all reasonable assistance**;
  - Furnish **any information** needed to enforce the Act.

##### **(d) Orders and Seizure**
- An inspector may **order** a person to provide:
  - Documents;
  - Information;
  - Samples.
- If an inspector believes a violation has occurred, they may **seize** the involved item.
- If the feed complies with regulations, it must be **released**.
- If a violation is found (by Tribunal or court), the item may be **forfeited** to Her Majesty in right of Canada.

##### **(e) Notice to Remove or Destroy Feeds**
- If an inspector believes feed does not meet regulations or was imported illegally:
  - They may issue a **notice** to remove or destroy it
