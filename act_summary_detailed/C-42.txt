### ğŸ“š **Core Definitions of Protected Works**

The Act defines several categories of protected **works**, which are the subject of copyright protection under Canadian law.

#### 1. **Literary Work**
- Includes:  
  - Tables  
  - Computer programs  
  - Compilations of literary works  
- *Note:* This broad definition ensures that even functional or data-driven works (like software or databases) are protected if they are original and fixed in a tangible form.

#### 2. **Dramatic Work**
- Includes:
  - Any piece for recitation, choreography, or mime, with a fixed form (in writing or otherwise)
  - Any cinematographic work (film)
  - Any compilation of dramatic works
- *Key point:* The definition includes performances and choreography, regardless of narrative content.

#### 3. **Musical Work**
- Any work of music or composition, with or without lyrics
- Includes any compilation of such works
- *Note:* This includes both original compositions and arrangements.

#### 4. **Artistic Work**
- Includes:
  - Paintings, drawings, maps, charts, plans, photographs, engravings, sculptures
  - Works of artistic craftsmanship
  - Architectural works
  - Compilations of artistic works
- *Note:* Engravings includes etchings, lithographs, woodcuts, prints â€” but excludes photographs.
- *Clarification:* A photograph is defined separately as a work analogous to photography.

#### 5. **Architectural Work**
- Refers to:
  - Any building or structure
  - Any model of a building or structure
- *Note:* This category is distinct from artistic works and is protected as a separate category of original creation.

#### 6. **Cinematographic Work**
- Includes any work expressed by processes analogous to cinematography (e.g., film, video)
- Does **not** require a soundtrack
- *Note:* Excludes soundtracks that accompany a cinematographic work (these are treated separately under sound recordings)

#### 7. **Compilations**
- A work resulting from:
  - The **selection or arrangement** of literary, dramatic, musical, or artistic works or parts thereof
  - The **selection or arrangement of data**
- *Important:* A compilation is protected as a distinct work, even if it contains pre-existing works. However:
  - The mere inclusion of a work in a compilation **does not** increase or reduce the copyright protection of the original work.
  - If a compilation contains two or more types of works, it is deemed a compilation of the category that makes up the **most substantial part**.

#### 8. **Computer Program**
- A set of instructions or statements expressed, fixed, embodied, or stored in any form (e.g., code, firmware, databases)
- Must be used directly or indirectly in a computer to achieve a specific result
- *Note:* This is a foundational definition for software protection under Canadian copyright law.

#### 9. **Work of Joint Authorship**
- A work produced by two or more authors where the contributions are **not distinct** from one another
- Implies collaborative effort where individual authorship cannot be clearly identified
- *Example:* A group of artists creating a mural where each contribution blends into the whole

#### 10. **Work of Sculpture**
- *Repealed (1997)*: This definition was removed. The term "sculpture" is now covered under the broader definition of "artistic work" and includes casts or models.

### ğŸ›ï¸ **Key Entities and Roles**

#### 1. **Board (Copyright Board)**
- The **Copyright Board** is established under section 66(1)
- Acts as an independent body to resolve disputes related to copyright, particularly in matters involving:
  - Licensing
  - Royalty distribution
  - Enforcement of rights
- Provides a mechanism for fair and equitable resolution between rights holders and users.

#### 2. **Collective Society**
- A society, association, or corporation that:
  - Administers copyright on behalf of multiple authors, performers, sound recording makers, or broadcasters
  - Operates a **licensing scheme** for specific classes of use (e.g., public performance, reproduction)
  - Collects and distributes royalties for such uses
- *Purpose:* Enables efficient management of rights when multiple creators are involved
- *Examples:* Organizations like SOCAN (for music), CRIA (for radio), or others managing rights in music, sound recordings, or broadcasts.

#### 3. **Broadcaster**
- A body that **broadcasts a communication signal** in accordance with the law of the country where it operates
- **Excludes** bodies whose primary activity is **retransmission** of signals (e.g., cable companies that simply re-broadcast existing signals)

#### 4. **Educational Institution**
- Includes:
  - Non-profit institutions providing pre-school, elementary, secondary, or post-secondary education
  - Institutions offering continuing, professional, or vocational training
  - Government departments or agencies that supervise education
  - Any other non-profit institution prescribed by regulation
- *Purpose:* To identify institutions that may be eligible for exceptions or limitations in copyright use (e.g., for teaching, research)

#### 5. **Library, Archive or Museum**
- Institutions that:
  - Are not for profit
  - Do not belong to or operate under a profit-making body
  - Maintain public collections of documents and materials
  - Are open to the public or researchers
- *Purpose:* To support fair use in research, preservation, and public access.

#### 6. **Exclusive Distributor**
- A person who:
  - Is **formally appointed in writing** by the copyright owner or exclusive licensee
  - Is the **only distributor** of a book in Canada (or a part of Canada) or in a specific market sector
  - Meets criteria established by **regulations under section 2.6**
- *Note:* If no such regulations exist, **no one qualifies** as an exclusive distributor
- *Purpose:* To define distribution rights and prevent unauthorized resale or distribution

#### 7. **Maker**
- Refers to:
  - For a **cinematographic work**: the person who undertakes the arrangements necessary for its creation
  - For a **sound recording**: the person who undertakes the arrangements necessary for the **first fixation** of sound
- *Note:* This is distinct from the author or performer; it focuses on the entity responsible for the initial production and recording
