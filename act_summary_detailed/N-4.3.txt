#### **Overview**

### **1. Legislative Background and Rationale**

The Act begins with a series of **"whereas" clauses** that establish the historical, constitutional, and moral foundation for reconciliation:

- **Historical continuity**: First Nations, Inuit, and MÃ©tis have long inhabited, governed, and managed their lands and cultures.
- **Colonial harm**: Centuries of assimilationist policies (e.g., residential schools, forced displacement) have caused deep trauma and systemic injustice.
- **Constitutional recognition**: Section 35 of the *Constitution Act, 1982* affirms Indigenous rights and recognizes reconciliation as a core purpose.
- **Mandate for action**: Reconciliation requires sustained, multi-generational efforts across all sectors and levels of government.
- **Commitment to international standards**: Canada commits to implementing the **United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (UNDRIP)**.
- **Language revitalization**: Indigenous languages are recognized as essential to cultural survival and reconciliation.
- **Need for independent oversight**: The Council is created to ensure transparency, accountability, and Indigenous leadership in monitoring reconciliation progress.

---

### **2. Definition of Key Terms**

- **Council**: The independent, non-governmental corporation established to advance reconciliation.
- **Governments**: Includes the Government of Canada, provincial and local governments, and Indigenous governing bodies.
- **Indigenous governing body**: Any entity authorized to act on behalf of an Indigenous group with rights affirmed under Section 35 of the *Constitution Act, 1982*.
- **Indigenous peoples**: As defined in subsection 35(2) of the Constitution Act, 1982 (i.e., "aboriginal peoples of Canada").
- **Minister**: The Minister of Crown-Indigenous Relations.
- **Transitional Committee**: A temporary body established under the *Department of Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Act* to oversee the Councilâ€™s formation.

> **Note**: In case of conflict, this Act takes precedence over the *Canada Not-for-Profit Corporations Act*.

### **3. Establishment and Incorporation of the Council**

- The Council is incorporated via **articles of incorporation** submitted by a member of the **Transitional Committee** to the Director of the Canada Not-for-Profit Corporations Act.
- The Council is **not an agent of the Crown** nor is it governed by the *Financial Administration Act*.
- It is recognized as a **qualified donee** under the *Income Tax Act*, meaning it can receive tax-exempt donations and grants.

### **6. Information Access and Government Accountability**

To ensure transparency and accountability, the Act establishes a **mandatory information-sharing protocol**:

- Within **six months of incorporation**, the **Minister of Crown-Indigenous Relations** must, in collaboration with the Council, develop a **protocol** for disclosing information relevant to reconciliation.
- The protocol must allow the Council to access **all information it deems necessary** for its work.
- If the Minister fails to comply, the Council may **apply to the Federal Court** for a declaration or order enforcing compliance.
