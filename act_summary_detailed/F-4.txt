### **Detailed Summary of the Farm Products Agencies Act**

---

#### **Overview**
The *Farm Products Agencies Act* is a federal statute designed to establish and regulate **marketing agencies** and **promotion-research agencies** to support the efficient, competitive, and sustainable marketing and promotion of farm products in Canada. The Act provides a structured framework for the creation, operation, oversight, and accountability of these agencies, with a strong emphasis on **producer participation**, **democratic decision-making**, and **economic efficiency** in interprovincial and export trade.

It is divided into three main parts—**Part I (Marketing Agencies)**, **Part II (Marketing Agencies for Specific Products like Eggs and Poultry)**, and **Part III (Promotion-Research Agencies)**—each addressing different types of farm products and the scope of agency powers.

---

## **Key Definitions and Scope**

### 1. **Agency**
- **Part I**: Refers to either a *marketing agency* or a *promotion-research agency*.
- **Part II**: Refers only to a *marketing agency*.
- **Part III**: Refers only to a *promotion-research agency*.

> These agencies are established under specific sections of the Act and are tasked with managing the marketing, pricing, distribution, or promotion of designated farm products.

---

### 2. **Farm Product**
- **Part I**: Any natural agricultural product (processed or unprocessed) and any part thereof.
- **Part II**: Specifically includes:
  - Eggs and poultry, and any part of such products.
  - Any other natural agricultural product (and its parts) where the **Governor in Council** determines—based on **plebiscites or provincial declarations**—that the **majority of producers in Canada** support the creation of a marketing agency for that product.
- **Part III**: All natural agricultural products (processed or unprocessed), regardless of type.

> This definition ensures that only products with broad producer support are eligible for agency establishment.

---

### 3. **Marketing**
- For **non-regulated products**: Includes all activities necessary to prepare and deliver the product for sale:
  - Selling, buying, pricing, assembling, packing, processing, transporting, storing.
- For **regulated products**: Only those acts specified in the **marketing plan** or **promotion and research plan**.

> This distinction ensures that agencies operate within clearly defined boundaries and do not overreach.

---

### 4. **Marketing Plan**
A comprehensive plan governing the marketing of a regulated product in interprovincial or export trade, including:
- Identification of producers and exemptions.
- Definition of marketing activities and who is involved.
- Quantity and price controls (e.g., limits on how much a producer can sell and at what price).
- Pooling of receipts from sales (with systems for initial, interim, and final payments to producers).
- Licensing of producers (with fees and conditions for suspension or cancellation).
- Imposition and collection of levies or charges, grouped by producer categories.

> This plan enables centralized control over supply, pricing, and revenue distribution.

---

### 5. **Promotion and Research Plan**
A plan focused on promoting farm products and conducting research for interprovincial, export, or import trade, including:
- Determination of who is involved (producers, importers) and exemptions.
- Definition of marketing activities.
- Imposition and collection of levies or charges, categorized by producer groups.

> Unlike marketing plans, this plan emphasizes **promotion** and **research**, not direct pricing or supply control.

---

### 6. **Regulated Product**
- **Part II**: A farm product grown or produced anywhere in Canada, **if**:
  - A marketing agency is authorized to act in Canada, or
  - In a designated region (e.g., a province or area), or
  - In any region outside that region but shipped into it (for interprovincial trade, not export).
- **Part III**: A farm product for which a promotion-research agency is authorized to operate in interprovincial, export, or import trade.

> This defines the geographic and functional scope of agency authority.

---

### 7. **Research**
- Includes **development** of new methods, technologies, or products.

> Expands the scope of promotion-research agencies beyond marketing to include innovation.

---

## **Establishment of the National Farm Products Council (Council)**

### **Structure and Composition**
- The Council consists of **3 to 7 members**, appointed by the **Governor in Council**.
- At least **50% of members (excluding the chairman)** must be **primary producers** at the time of appointment.
- The Governor in Council designates:
  - One **Chairman** and one **Vice-Chairman**.
  - One of the two must be a **primary producer**.
- Appointments aim for **equal representation** across:
  - Four Western provinces
  - Two Central provinces
  - Four Atlantic provinces
- **Eligibility**: No member over **70 years of age**; members cease service upon reaching age 70.

> The Council is designed to be **producer-driven**, ensuring that those most affected by agency decisions have a voice.

---

### **Temporary Substitutes**
- If a member (other than the chairman) is absent or unable to act, the Governor in Council may appoint a **temporary substitute** on prescribed terms.

> Ensures continuity in Council operations.

---

### **Leadership and Meetings**
- The **Chairman presides** at meetings.
- In the absence or incapacity of the chairman, the **Vice-Chairman acts**.
- The Council meets regularly and may conduct public hearings.

---

### **Compensation**
- Members not employed in federal public administration receive a **salary** set by the Governor in Council.
- Members are entitled to **travel and living expenses** incurred in carrying out duties, as provided by **by-law**.

> Ensures fair compensation and practical ability to perform duties.

---

## **Duties of the National Farm Products Council**

The Council has a wide range of responsibilities to ensure effective, fair, and transparent agency operations:

1. **Advisory Role**:
   - Advises the **Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food** on establishing or operating agencies.
   - Ensures agencies operate efficiently and competitively.

2. **Review of Agency Operations**:
   - Reviews agency operations annually or more frequently if warranted.
   - Reports findings to the Minister.

3. **Review of Plans and Proposals**:
   - Reviews:
     - Amendments to existing marketing or promotion-research plans.
     - Proposed new plans submitted by agencies.
   - Considers all representations and makes recommendations to the Minister.

4. **Review of Orders and Regulations**:
   - Approves or sets aside agency orders and regulations:
     - If they are **necessary** for implementing the relevant plan.
     - If not, the Council may **set them aside** in whole or in part.

5. **Public Hearings**:
   - Conducts public hearings on:
     - Whether to establish a new agency.
     - Whether to expand an existing agency’s authority.
     - Any other matter affecting agency operations.
   - Hearings may be held **at any place in Canada**, with notice in the *Canada Gazette* and major farm journals.

6. **Inquiries and Research**:
   - May conduct studies or research into marketing, promotion, or research issues.
   - May require producers or marketers to:
     - Register with the Council or agency.
     - Maintain production or marketing records.
     - Submit data on production, sales, or importation.

7. **Support for Producer Input**:
   - Can require data submission when assessing the **advisability of establishing** an agency or expanding its powers.

8. **Other Powers**:
   - May take any action incidental or conducive to fulfilling its duties.

---

## **Mandatory Conditions for Establishing or Expanding an Agency**

The Council **cannot recommend** establishing or expanding an agency unless it is satisfied that:

- **For a Marketing Agency**:
  > The **majority of producers** of the product (in Canada or a specific region) support the agency.

- **For a Promotion-Research Agency**:
  > The **majority of producers** (or producers and importers, if import trade is involved) support the agency.

> This ensures **democratic legitimacy** and **producer consent** is a prerequisite.

---

## **Public Hearings**

- Required in key situations:
  - When evaluating establishment or expansion of an agency.
  - When reviewing a proposed marketing or promotion-research plan.
  - When directed by the Governor in Council or Minister.
- May be held **on the Council’s own initiative** if in the public interest.
- The **Chairman** may designate two or more members to conduct a hearing on behalf of the Council.
- These members have **full powers of the Council** during the hearing.
- Hearings are **public**, with notice published in:
  - The *Canada Gazette*
  - Major farm journals
  - In areas where producers are likely to be affected

> Enhances transparency, public accountability, and stakeholder engagement.

---

## **Rules and Governance**

- The Council may make **by-laws** on:
  - Calling meetings.
  - Conducting business, including committee formation and quorum rules.
  - Fixing travel and living expenses (subject to **Treasury Board approval**).
- Officers and employees are appointed under the **Public Service Employment Act**.
- Members who devote full time to duties are considered:
  - **Employed in the public service** (for superannuation).
  - **Employed in federal public administration** (for compensation and benefits).

---
