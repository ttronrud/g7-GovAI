**Detailed Summary of the *Special Retirement Arrangements Act***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *Special Retirement Arrangements Act* (SRAA) is a federal legislation designed to address gaps in retirement benefits for certain public servants, officers, and individuals who are excluded from or prevented from joining standard pension plans due to specific legal, administrative, or structural reasons. It establishes two primary mechanisms:  
1. **Special Pension Plans** – For individuals who are otherwise ineligible for standard pension plans.  
2. **Retirement Compensation Arrangements (RCAs)** – To provide equivalent retirement benefits to those who would have been eligible under existing pension plans but are barred by law or policy from participating.  

The Act ensures these arrangements are financially sound, transparent, and equitable, with oversight by Parliament and compliance with income tax and pension regulations.

---

## **Key Definitions and Core Concepts**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Benefit** | Includes refunds of contributions and interest on those contributions. |
| **Pension Benefit** | A periodic payment payable under a special pension plan to a member or beneficiary. |
| **Pension Benefit Credit** | The total value of accrued pension benefits and other benefits under a special pension plan. |
| **Special Pension Plan** | A pension plan established under an order made under paragraph 3(1)(b). |
| **Retirement Compensation Arrangement (RCA)** | A plan or arrangement established under an order under paragraph 10(a) or subsection 11(1) of the Income Tax Act, providing retirement benefits to individuals excluded from standard pension plans. |
| **Retirement Compensation Benefit Credit** | The aggregate value of benefits accrued under an RCA. |
| **Registered Pension Plans Account (RPPA)** | A government account established to fund special pension plans. |
| **Retirement Compensation Arrangements Account (RCAA)** | A government account established to fund retirement compensation arrangements. |
| **Minister** | The President of the Treasury Board. |
| **Governor in Council** | The federal Cabinet, empowered to make orders under the Act on the Minister’s recommendation. |

## **Core Provisions and Functions**

---

### **1. Exemption from Existing Superannuation Acts**  
The Governor in Council may, on the recommendation of the Minister, by order:  
- **Exempt** any person or class of persons from the *Diplomatic Service (Special) Superannuation Act* or the *Lieutenant Governors Superannuation Act*.  
- **Establish or authorize** a pension plan to provide benefits to those exempted individuals.  
- **Designate** individuals or classes as members of a **special pension plan**.  

> *Purpose*: To allow certain public servants (e.g., diplomats, lieutenant governors) who are excluded from standard pension schemes to access retirement benefits through alternative plans.

---

### **2. Compliance with Income Tax and Pension Regulations**  
Every special pension plan must:  
- Comply with the *Income Tax Act* and regulations governing **registered pension plans**.  
- Provide **supplementary benefits** (e.g., health, disability) at the **same rates and methods** as under the *Supplementary Retirement Benefits Act*.  

> *Purpose*: Ensures financial transparency, tax compliance, and consistency with broader pension frameworks.

---

### **3. Amendments to Special Pension Plans**  
- The Governor in Council may amend or authorize amendments to a special pension plan.  
- Amendments may be **retroactive**, but only if the order specifies this and the prior date is not earlier than the plan’s establishment date.  
- **Amendments that reduce accrued pension benefits or benefit credits are void**.  

> *Purpose*: Prevents retroactive reduction of benefits already earned.

---

### **4. Contributions to the Registered Pension Plans Account (RPPA)**  
- All members designated by regulation must contribute to the RPPA through salary deductions or other prescribed means.  
- Contribution rates are set by regulation.  

> *Purpose*: Ensures ongoing funding of the special pension plan.

---

### **5. Funding and Accounting of the RPPA**  
Each fiscal year, the RPPA is credited with:  
- An amount estimated by the Minister to cover **accrued benefits** for each month.  
- Interest calculated and credited at the **same rate and timing** as interest on the Superannuation Account under the *Public Service Superannuation Act*.  

> *Purpose*: Maintains financial stability and ensures interest accrual aligns with standard pension accounts.

---

### **6. Reporting and Oversight**  
- A **valuation report** and **assets report** must be prepared and submitted to the Minister and laid before Parliament under the *Public Pensions Reporting Act*.  
- The first actuarial review occurs on **December 31, four years after the Act comes into force**.  
- Subsequent reviews must occur **no more than every three years**.  

> *Purpose*: Ensures financial accountability and transparency to Parliament.

---

### **7. Funding Adjustments After Valuation**  
Following the submission of a valuation report, the Minister may credit the RPPA with additional funds to ensure that, by the end of the prescribed period, the account will meet the full cost of benefits chargeable against it.  

> *Purpose*: Prevents underfunding due to actuarial or economic changes.

---

### **8. Payment of Benefits**  
- All benefits and amounts payable under a special pension plan are paid from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund** and **charged to the RPPA**.  

> *Purpose*: Ensures public funding and fiscal responsibility.

---

## **Retirement Compensation Arrangements (RCAs)**

### **Who Is Covered?**  
The Governor in Council may establish RCAs for individuals who:  
1. Are required to contribute to the **Public Service Superannuation Account**, **Canadian Forces Superannuation Account**, or **Royal Canadian Mounted Police Superannuation Account**;  
2. Are subject to the *Diplomatic Service (Special) Superannuation Act* or *Lieutenant Governors Superannuation Act*;  
3. Are members of a special pension plan;  
4. Would have accrued benefits under a pension plan **but for** provisions in:  
   - Subsection 5(6) of the Public Service Superannuation Act;  
   - Subsection 5(5) of the Canadian Forces or RCMP Superannuation Acts;  
   - Or Section 11 of the *Act to Amend Certain Acts in Relation to Pensions* (which introduced the SRAA);  
5. Are **prevented by law** from joining a pension plan under those Acts.  

> *Purpose*: Provides retroactive or equivalent benefits to those who were legally barred from joining standard pension plans.

---

### **Mandatory Establishment of RCAs (as of the Act’s Coming Into Force)**  
Within a specified timeframe, the Governor in Council **must establish or authorize** RCAs for:  
- Public servants whose **annual salary exceeds** the threshold set by regulations (e.g., under Public Service Superannuation Act, para. 42.1(1)(a));  
- Canadian Forces members whose **annual pay exceeds** the regulatory threshold (para. 50(1)(g));  
- RCMP members whose **annual pay exceeds** the threshold (para. 26.1(1)(a));  
- **Deputy heads** of departments with **at least 10 years of pensionable service** who elect to join an RCA;  
- Individuals who, **immediately before the Act came into force**, were deemed to be employed in the public service under the old Public Service Superannuation Act.  

> *Purpose*: Ensures fairness and inclusivity for high-earning public servants and senior officials who were previously excluded.

---

### **Features of RCAs**  
- Benefits must be **equivalent in value** to what the individual would have received under the standard pension plan had they not been excluded.  
- RCAs must provide **supplementary benefits** at the **same rates and methods** as under the *Supplementary Retirement Benefits Act*.  

> *Purpose*: Maintains parity with standard retirement benefits.

---

### **Amendments to RCAs**  
- Amendments may be **retroactive** (if ordered) but not before the plan’s establishment date.  
- Amendments that reduce **accrued benefits or benefit credits are void**.  

> *Purpose*: Protects individuals’ existing rights.

---

### **Advisory Committees**  
- The Governor in Council may establish **advisory committees** to advise on RCAs.  
- Committees consist of **eight members**:  
  - **Four selected from persons subject to or potentially subject to an RCA**.  
- Members serve **no more than three years**.  

> *Purpose*: Ensures stakeholder input and accountability in design and implementation.

---

### **Contributions to the Retirement Compensation Arrangements Account (RCAA)**  
- Individuals subject to an RCA must contribute via salary deductions or other means as prescribed.  
- **Public Service corporations** must pay into the RCAA, at times determined by the Minister, for benefits accrued by their employees under RCAs.  

> *Purpose*: Ensures sustained funding of the RCA.

---

### **Funding of the RCAA**  
Each fiscal year, the RCAA is credited with:  
- An estimated amount for **monthly accrued benefits**.  
- Interest calculated and credited at the **same rate and timing** as in the Superannuation Account.  
- An amount **refundable under subsection 207.7(2) of the Income Tax Act**, if any.  
- An amount of **tax payable under subsection 207.7(1)**, charged annually.  

> *Purpose*: Ensures financial sustainability
