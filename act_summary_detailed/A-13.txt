**Detailed Summary of the *Asia-Pacific Foundation of Canada Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *Asia-Pacific Foundation of Canada Act* establishes the **Asia-Pacific Foundation of Canada (the “Foundation”)** as a statutory corporation with a broad mandate to strengthen ties between Canada and the Asia-Pacific region. The Act outlines the Foundation’s purpose, governance structure, financial operations, accountability mechanisms, and reporting obligations. It is designed to foster mutual understanding, promote cooperation, and support sustainable development through cultural, economic, educational, and policy-related initiatives.

---

## **1. Establishment of the Foundation**

- The Foundation is formally established as a **corporation under Canadian law**, with the full legal capacity of a natural person.
- It is named the **Asia-Pacific Foundation of Canada** and is referred to throughout the Act as the "Foundation."
- The Foundation operates independently of the federal government, though it receives funding and guidance from it.

---

## **2. Purpose and Objectives**

The Foundation’s primary mission is to **develop closer ties** between Canada and the peoples, institutions, and economies of the Asia-Pacific region. Its specific objectives include:

1. **Cultural and Social Understanding**  
   Promote mutual awareness and understanding of:
   - Cultures, histories, religions, philosophies, languages, lifestyles, and aspirations  
   - The interplay between these elements and their effects on both Canadian and Asia-Pacific societies.

2. **Foreign Policy Dialogue**  
   Facilitate dialogue and understanding on foreign policy issues affecting Canada and the Asia-Pacific region.

3. **Development Cooperation**  
   Support collaborative programs and initiatives between organizations and institutions in Canada and across the Asia-Pacific region.

4. **Cross-Sector Collaboration**  
   Encourage partnerships among private and public sector entities in both Canada and the Asia-Pacific.

5. **Economic and Commercial Ties**  
   Promote closer economic and commercial relationships between Canada and the Asia-Pacific region.

6. **Scholarship and Expertise**  
   - In Canada: Promote scholarship on Asia-Pacific topics (economic, cultural, social, etc.)  
   - In the Asia-Pacific region: Promote scholarship on Canada and Canadian issues  

7. **Information Collection and Dissemination**  
   Collect, analyze, and share information and ideas about Canada and the Asia-Pacific region with stakeholders across both regions.

---

## **3. Legal Capacity and Powers**

The Foundation has extensive powers to carry out its mission, including:

- Initiating, financing, and administering programs relevant to its purpose  
- Supporting or implementing programs by government, private, or public organizations  
- Entering into contracts with governments, organizations, or individuals  
- Publishing and disseminating information related to its mandate  
- Sponsoring conferences, seminars, and meetings  
- Establishing research centers and information/data facilities  
- Awarding scholarships and fellowships for study on Asia-Pacific topics  
- Recognizing individuals or organizations for outstanding contributions to Canada–Asia-Pacific relations  
- Acquiring, holding, investing, and disposing of real property or financial assets  
- Receiving gifts, bequests, or donations and managing them in accordance with donor conditions  
- Expenditure of funds from appropriations by Parliament or from operational income  
- Using all grants, contributions, and donations solely for its purpose  

> *Note:* The Foundation may operate **throughout Canada** and **outside Canada** to the extent permitted by local laws.

---

## **4. Governance Structure**

### **Board of Directors (the “Board”)**

The Board is the governing body responsible for overseeing the Foundation’s activities.

#### Composition:
- **Chairperson** and up to **four directors** appointed by the **Governor in Council** (federal cabinet), on the recommendation of the **Minister of Foreign Affairs**, after consultation with the Board.
- Up to **eighteen directors** appointed by the **Board itself**, following consultations with:
  - Provincial governments  
  - Interested individuals, corporations, and organizations  
- The **President of the Foundation**, appointed under Section 17.

> *Note:* When considering appointments from provincial governments, the Board must take into account the **amounts contributed** by that province to the Foundation.

#### Appointment Criteria:
- Directors must have backgrounds or expertise that support the Foundation’s mission.
- At least **half** of the Board must have experience in Canada–Asia-Pacific relations.
- The Board must ensure representation in areas such as:
  - Corporate governance
  - Investment management
  - Auditing and evaluation
- The Board must reflect **diversity and representation of Canadian society**.

#### Eligibility:
- **Not eligible** to serve as a director if a member of:
  - The Senate  
  - The House of Commons  

#### Term of Office:
- Maximum term: **three years** for directors appointed under (a) and (b).
- Directors may be **reappointed**, but **no individual may serve more than three times** in total.

#### Removal:
- Directors appointed under (a) (federal appointments) may be removed **for cause** by the Governor in Council.
- Directors appointed under (b) (Board-appointed) may be removed **for cause** by the Board.

#### Chairperson and Vice-Chairperson:
- The **Chairperson presides** over Board meetings and may be assigned other duties.
- A **Vice-Chairperson** may be elected to act in the Chairperson’s absence or in case of vacancy.

---

## **5. Office and Operations**

- The **principal office** is located in **Vancouver, British Columbia**.
- The Board must meet **at least twice per year**, with at least one meeting held at the principal office.
- Meetings can occur at other times and places as deemed necessary by the Chairperson.

> **Voting Rights**:  
> A director who is part of the federal public administration **does not have voting rights** on Board matters.

---

## **6. President of the Foundation**

- The President is appointed by the Board and has **supervisory authority over staff and activities**.
- Requirements for appointment:
  - Must have **experience or expertise in Canada–Asia-Pacific relations**
  - Must meet any other qualifications set by the Board
- Appointment process must be **transparent and competitive**
- If the President is a Board member, another director must be appointed in their place.

#### Term and Reappointment:
- Maximum term: **three years**
- May be reappointed, but **no individual may serve as President more than three times**
- The Board may appoint an **acting President** if the President is absent or incapacitated or if the office is vacant

#### Compensation:
- The **Chairperson and other directors** serve **without salary**, but may receive **travel and living expenses** approved by by-law.
- The **President** and **acting President** are paid a **fixed remuneration and expenses** set by the Board.

---

## **7. Committees**

- The Board may establish:
  - An **Executive Committee**
  - **Advisory or other committees**
- These committees may have specific roles and responsibilities as defined by by-law.
- The Board must establish an **Audit and Evaluation Committee** consisting of **at least three directors**.
  - Responsibilities:
    - Conduct internal audits
    - Ensure compliance with management systems and controls
    - Evaluate performance and financial integrity
- The committee may be paid for its members' expenses.

---

## **8. By-Laws and Management**

- The Board may adopt **by-laws** covering:
  - Meeting procedures
  - Board member expenses
  - Committee structure and duties
  - Employment and remuneration of officers and employees
  - Membership rules
  - Appointment of patrons and honorary officers
  - General conduct and management of affairs

> **Important Limitations on Delegation**  
> The Board **cannot delegate** the following powers:
- Making, amending, or repealing by-laws  
- Appointing directors or filling committee vacancies  
- Appointing officers or setting their salaries  
- Accepting grants, contributions, or donations  
- Approving annual financial statements or reports  

---

## **9. Legal and Financial Status**

### **Charitable Status**
- The Foundation is **deemed a registered charity** under the *Income Tax Act*, for all purposes, **subject to section 29**.
- This status is **conditional**:  
  - If the Foundation issues receipts with prescribed information (as required by law), and **fails to use the funds for charitable purposes**, it will **cease to be considered a registered charity**.

### **Property and Profits**
- All **profits and appreciation in property** must be used **exclusively** to further the Foundation’s purpose.
- **No part** of the Foundation’s property or profits may be distributed to:
  - Board members
  - Employees
  - Any member of the Foundation

### **Funding and Investments**
- The Foundation may accept **grants, contributions, and unconditional donations** from:
  - The Government of Canada
  - Provincial governments
  - Private individuals or organizations
- All funds must be used **strictly in accordance with the Foundation’s purpose** and any conditions attached to the funding.
- The Foundation **cannot accept funding** for purposes inconsistent with its mission.
- The Board must establish **investment policies** that are prudent, risk-managed, and aligned with the Foundation’s obligations.
- Funds may be invested and reinvested **in accordance with these policies**, unless restricted by donor conditions.

> **Prohibition on Incorporation**  
> The Foundation **may not**, without prior written authorization from the Minister of Foreign Affairs:
> - Cause any corporation to be incorporated  
> - Participate in incorporation  
> - Become a partner in a partnership
