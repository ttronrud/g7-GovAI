**Detailed Summary of the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act* (RCMP Act) is the foundational legislation that establishes, governs, and regulates the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP), Canada’s national police force. It outlines the structure, powers, duties, appointment processes, disciplinary mechanisms, operational responsibilities, and accountability frameworks for the Force. The Act also addresses issues related to civilian oversight, conduct standards, peace officer authority, and the relationship between the RCMP and federal, provincial, and municipal governments.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Citation and Definitions**

- **Act Title**: The Act is known as the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Act*.
- **Definitions**: The Act provides clear definitions of key terms used throughout:
  - **Child**: A person under 18 years of age, unless evidence suggests otherwise.
  - **Code of Conduct**: Regulations established under Section 38 governing ethical and professional standards for RCMP members.
  - **Commission**: The *Civilian Review and Complaints Commission for the RCMP*, established under Section 45.29(1), responsible for reviewing complaints about RCMP members.
  - **Commissioner**: The head of the RCMP, appointed by the Governor in Council and serving at the pleasure of the Minister.
  - **Committee**: The *Royal Canadian Mounted Police External Review Committee*, established under Section 25, which reviews internal matters and provides oversight.
  - **Conduct Authority**: A person designated by the Commissioner to oversee disciplinary actions against a member.
  - **Force**: Refers to the Royal Canadian Mounted Police as a whole.
  - **Guardian**: A person (other than a parent) legally responsible for a child’s care or custody.
  - **Member**: Any individual appointed to work with the Force, including officers and non-officers (e.g., administrative staff).
  - **Minister**: The Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness.
  - **Officer**: A member appointed under Section 5 or subsections 6(3) or (4).
  - **Proceedings**: Investigations or hearings conducted by the Commission related to complaints under Part VII or VII.2.
  - **Representative**: A person representing or assisting a member or conduct authority under Section 47.1.

> **Note**: Several roles (e.g., Commission Chairman, Committee Chairman) were repealed in 2013 (c. 18, s. 2), reflecting modernization and restructuring of oversight bodies.

---

### **2. Structure and Hierarchy of the RCMP**

#### **Leadership Structure**
- The **Commissioner** is the head of the Force, appointed by the Governor in Council and reports to the Minister.
- The Commissioner may delegate powers to other officers, **except**:
  - The power to delegate (i.e., delegate delegation),
  - The power to make rules (under this Act),
  - Powers under subsections 45.4(5) and 45.41(10) (related to internal discipline and investigations).

#### **Officer Ranks**
- Officers include:
  - Deputy Commissioners
  - Assistant Commissioners
  - Chief Superintendents
  - Superintendents
  - Inspectors
  - Other ranks as prescribed by the Governor in Council.

> **Note**: The rank of "Inspector" was previously listed but was **repealed in 1985** (R.S., 1985, c. 8 (2nd Supp.), s. 3). The current structure reflects a streamlined hierarchy.

#### **Maximum Numbers**
- The maximum number of officers in each rank is set by the **Treasury Board**.

#### **Appointment of Officers**
- The **Governor in Council** may appoint a person to the rank of **Deputy Commissioner** during pleasure.
- The **Commissioner** appoints individuals to other ranks and may promote officers to higher ranks (except Deputy Commissioner).
- **Commissioners’ Standing Orders**: Rules made by the Commissioner under this Act are known as "Commissioner’s standing orders."

#### **Appointments to Other Positions**
- The **Governor in Council** may designate a **Commanding Officer** for each Division of the Force, based on the **Minister’s recommendation**, which itself is based on the Commissioner’s recommendation.
- If a Commanding Officer is absent or incapacitated, the Commissioner may appoint another officer to act as Commanding Officer.
- An officer may act as Commanding Officer for **no more than 180 days** without the Governor in Council’s approval.
- Upon leaving the Force, the officer automatically ceases to be a Commanding Officer.

#### **Appointment of Non-Officer Members**
- The Commissioner may appoint **non-officer members** (e.g., civilian staff) to various ranks and levels.
- Ranks and maximum numbers for such members are set by the **Treasury Board**.
- Appointments are **indeterminate** unless specified otherwise.

> **Note**: The term of appointment for non-officer members is **indeterminate** unless fixed. If fixed, the term ends upon expiry or extension (which does not constitute a new appointment).

---

### **3. Appointment Requirements and Probation**

#### **Qualifications for Appointment**
- All members must be:
  - Canadian citizens,
  - Of good character,
  - Possess necessary physical qualities.
- For non-officer members, additional qualifications may be prescribed by the Commissioner via rule.
- In rare cases, **non-citizens** may be appointed if they meet other required qualifications.

#### **Commissioner’s Authority**
- The Commissioner has the power to:
  - Revoke appointments,
  - Take corrective action if an error or improper conduct influenced the appointment process.

#### **Probationary Period**
- All newly appointed members undergo a **probationary period** established by Commissioner’s rules.
- This period **does not end** due to:
  - Appointment,
  - Demotion,
  - Transfer.
- During probation:
  - The Commissioner may **discharge** the member:
    - By notifying them of discharge at the end of a notice period (as per rules), or
    - By specifying a discharge date and paying the member the salary they would have earned during that period.
- The member ceases to be a member upon the end of the notice period or the specified discharge date.

#### **Resignation**
- A member may resign by giving the Commissioner written notice.
- The member ceases to be a member on the date specified in the resignation letter.

---

### **4. Supernumerary Special Constables**

- The Commissioner may appoint **supernumerary special constables** for up to **12 months** to assist in maintaining law and order.
- These appointments are made:
  - At the request of a federal department (defined under the *Financial Administration Act*), or
  - When deemed necessary or in the public interest.
- Supernumeraries are **not entitled** to any pecuniary benefits or privileges under the Act.
- The Commissioner may **revoke** such appointments at any time.

---

### **5. Civilian Employees**

- Civilian employees necessary for the Force’s operations are hired under the **Public Service Employment Act**.

---

### **6. RCMP Reserve**

- The Governor in Council may create a **Royal Canadian Mounted Police Reserve** through regulations.
- Regulations may define:
  - Appointment of reservists,
  - Their powers, duties, and functions.
- **Important Exception**: The RCMP Act **does not apply** to reservists unless specifically provided for in the regulations.
- For superannuation purposes:
  - A reservist is **not deemed to be employed in the public service**, even under the *Royal Canadian Mounted Police Superannuation Act*, *Public Service Superannuation Act*, or *Canadian Forces Superannuation Act*.

---

### **7. Peace Officer Powers**

- Every **officer** is a **peace officer** throughout Canada and has all the powers, authority, protection, and privileges of a peace officer under applicable law until they cease to be an officer.
- The Commissioner may designate **other individuals** as peace officers, including:
  - Non-officer members,
  - Supernumerary special constables,
  - Reservists,
  - Other persons under the Commissioner’s jurisdiction.
- Such designated individuals have **the same powers and privileges** as officers until:
  - The designation expires,
  - Is revoked, or
  - The person ceases to be a member, special constable, or reservist.

#### **Certificates**
- The Commissioner may issue:
  - A certificate stating that a member is a member of the Force and/or a peace officer,
  - A certificate to a designated individual confirming their status as a peace officer.
- These certificates are **evidence in all courts and legal proceedings**.

---

### **8. Disciplinary Powers and Conduct**

- A member who:
  - Contravenes, is found contravening, or is suspected of contravening:
    - The **Code of Conduct**, or
    - Any Act of Parliament or provincial law
  may be **suspended from duty** by the Commissioner.

> This provision enables immediate action against misconduct, particularly in cases involving ethical violations or legal breaches.

---

### **9. Headquarters and Oaths**

- The **headquarters and main offices** of the RCMP are located in **Ottawa**.

- Before assuming duties, every member must take:
  - The **oath of allegiance**,
  - The oaths specified in the Schedule.

- Oaths may be administered by:
  - A judge, provincial court judge, or justice of the peace in any part of Canada, or
  - Any member before the Commissioner or an authorized officer/person.
