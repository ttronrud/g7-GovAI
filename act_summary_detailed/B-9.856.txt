**Detailed Summary of the *Budget Implementation Act, 2007***  

The *Budget Implementation Act, 2007* is a federal statute enacted by Her Majesty, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons of Canada, to implement key provisions of the federal government’s 2007 budget. The Act serves as a legislative instrument to authorize financial transfers, establish new funding mechanisms, support provincial and territorial initiatives, and advance national priorities in areas such as environmental sustainability, public health, education, climate change adaptation, and international development.

---

### **1. Citation and General Provisions**
- The Act is formally titled: **Budget Implementation Act, 2007**.
- Sections 55 and 56 are **deemed to have come into force on July 1, 2000**, indicating retroactive application for certain provisions related to fiscal or administrative matters.

---

### **2. Financial Transfers to Provinces and Territories**

#### **A. Ontario – Corporate Tax Administration Transition**
- **Fiscal Year 2007 (beginning April 1, 2007):**  
  A one-time payment of **$250 million** is authorized from the Consolidated Revenue Fund to the Province of Ontario to assist in transitioning to a **single corporate tax administration system**.
- **Fiscal Year 2008 (beginning April 1, 2008):**  
  An additional payment of **$150 million** is authorized for the same purpose, supporting Ontario’s efforts to streamline corporate taxation.

> *Purpose:* Improve efficiency, reduce duplication, and ensure a unified approach to corporate tax collection across Ontario.

---

#### **B. British Columbia – First Nations Economic Development**
- A payment of **up to $30 million** is authorized to the Province of British Columbia to promote **fair and equitable economic development** in the **Spirit Bear Rainforest area** and the **Queen Charlotte Islands**, with a focus on **environmentally sustainable and ecologically integrated** growth for First Nations communities.

> *Purpose:* Support Indigenous-led economic development while protecting the environment.

---

#### **C. Climate Change and Greenhouse Gas Reduction Trust**
- The Minister of Finance may make direct payments, up to **$1.519 billion**, to a trust established to fund **provincial and territorial projects** that:
  - Reduce greenhouse gas emissions and air pollutants.
  - Support adaptation to climate change.
- The amount allocated to each province or territory is determined by the **trust indenture** and based on specific criteria (e.g., emission reduction potential, vulnerability to climate change).

> *Purpose:* Advance climate action and build resilience in communities most affected by environmental change.

---

#### **D. Post-Secondary Education and Training Trust**
- A total of **$1.519 billion** is authorized (note: likely a typographical error — likely intended to be $1.519 billion for the climate trust; this section appears to be a separate allocation)  
  **Correction:** This section states:
  - Up to **$1.519 billion** is available to a trust for **post-secondary education and training** in Ontario, and **training** in Manitoba and Saskatchewan.
  - Allocation:
    - **$574 million** to Ontario
    - **$21.7 million** to Manitoba
    - **$18.4 million** to Saskatchewan
- Payments are made based on the **trust indenture** and are subject to the Minister of Finance’s discretion in timing and method.

> *Purpose:* Strengthen education and workforce development, particularly in post-secondary and vocational training.

---

#### **E. Human Papillomavirus (HPV) Immunization Trust**
- Up to **$300 million** is available to a trust to fund **HPV immunization programs** across provinces and territories.
- Allocation is determined by the **trust indenture** and is based on need, population, and program effectiveness.

> *Purpose:* Improve public health by increasing access to HPV vaccines and reducing cervical cancer rates.

---

#### **F. Patient Wait Times Guarantee Trust**
- Up to **$614 million** is available to a trust to support the implementation of a **patient wait times guarantee**.
- Funds are distributed based on the terms of the trust indenture and are intended to improve access to healthcare services.

> *Purpose:* Address long wait times in healthcare and ensure timely access to medical services.

---

#### **G. Child Care Spaces Fund**
- For the fiscal year beginning April 1, 2007, the Minister of Finance may make direct payments totaling **up to $250 million** to provinces and territories to support the **creation of child care spaces**.
- The amount allocated to each jurisdiction is calculated as:
  > (Total amount) × (Population of the province/territory / Total population of all provinces and territories)

> *Purpose:* Expand access to affordable, quality child care to support working families and early childhood development.

---

#### **H. Direct Payments to Specific Territories**
- **Yukon:** $3.5 million
- **Northwest Territories:** $54.4 million  
> *Purpose:* Targeted support for regional development and infrastructure.

### **3. Transitional and Implementation Provisions**

#### **A. Tax-back Guarantee Act Amendments**
- **Sections 79 and 82** come into force on **April 1 of the first fiscal year** in which Newfoundland and Labrador makes an election under subsection 3.7(3) of the *Federal-Provincial Fiscal Arrangements Act*.
- The Minister of Finance must **publish the effective date** in the *Canada Gazette*.
  - *Note:* Sections 79 and 82 came into force on **April 1, 2010**.
- **Section 81** comes into force on **April 1, 2008**.

> *Purpose:* Provide clarity and implementation timelines for tax policy reforms in Newfoundland and Labrador.

#### **B. Timing of Other Provisions**
- **Subsections 91(2), 103(2), 104(2), and 113(2)** come into force on a day or days to be fixed by **order of the Governor in Council**.
  - *Note:* These came into force on **November 17, 2007**, as per SI/2007-105 and SI/2007-106.

> *Purpose:* Allow for phased implementation of fiscal and administrative reforms.

#### **C. Part 7 (General Provisions)**
- This Part (likely covering administrative and procedural rules) comes into force on a day to be **fixed by order of the Governor in Council**.
  - *Note:* Came into force on **October 26, 2007**, via **Statutory Instrument SI/2007-95**.

> *Purpose:* Establish governance and enforcement mechanisms for the Act.

---

### **4. Repealed Provisions**
Several sections of the Act are marked as **repealed**:
- Sections 150 of *2007, c. 29* (likely related to prior budget measures or transitional rules).
- These sections were repealed in 2007, indicating that certain earlier provisions were no longer in force.

> *Note:* Repeal ensures the law reflects current policy and avoids outdated or redundant clauses.
