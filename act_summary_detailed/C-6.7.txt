## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Canada Marine Act**

### üîπ **Overview and Purpose**
The *Canada Marine Act* is a foundational piece of legislation that establishes the legal framework for the development, management, and operation of Canada‚Äôs marine infrastructure ‚Äî particularly ports and port facilities. Enacted by Her Majesty in right of Canada, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons, the Act aims to modernize and strengthen Canada‚Äôs marine transportation system to support economic growth, trade competitiveness, and environmental safety.

#### **Core Objectives of the Act:**
1. **Implement Marine Policies**  
   To ensure Canada has the necessary marine infrastructure to meet national, regional, and local social and economic goals and to maintain its global trade competitiveness.

2. **Promote Port Success**  
   To enhance the efficiency, competitiveness, and prosperity of Canadian ports, which are vital to the national economy.

3. **Harmonize with International Standards**  
   To align with international practices and standards of Canada‚Äôs major trading partners (e.g., U.S., U.K., EU), promoting consistency and interoperability in maritime operations.

4. **User-Centric Services**  
   To organize marine transportation services to meet user needs and offer them at reasonable costs.

5. **Safety and Environmental Protection**  
   To ensure high standards of safety and environmental stewardship in all port and marine operations.

6. **Local Autonomy and Responsiveness**  
   To allow local or regional authorities significant autonomy in managing port operations and responding to local needs and priorities.

7. **Commercial Management**  
   To manage marine infrastructure in a commercially sound manner, incorporating input from users and local communities.

8. **Port Disposition and Transfer**  
   To enable the transfer or disposition of certain ports and port facilities as needed.

9. **Integration with Other Transport Systems**  
   To coordinate and integrate marine activities with surface (rail, road) and air transportation systems for seamless logistics.

## üîπ **Agency Status and Legal Authority of Port Authorities (Section 3)**

### üö® Key Principle:
> A port authority is **not** an agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada **except** for the specific port activities listed in paragraph 28(2)(a) of the Act.

### Exceptions:
- A **wholly-owned subsidiary** of a port authority is **not** an agent of Her Majesty **unless**:
  1. It was an agent of Her Majesty on **June 10, 1996**; and  
  2. It is an agent under **another enactment**, not this Act.

### Prohibited Actions:
- A port authority or its wholly-owned subsidiary **may not borrow money** as an agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada.

> This limits the financial liability of port authorities and ensures they operate independently and commercially.

## üîπ **Issuance of Letters Patent (Section 8)**

Letters patent are the legal instruments that formally **incorporate** a port authority and define its powers, structure, and responsibilities.

### üìù Criteria for Issuing Letters Patent (for new port authorities):
The Minister may issue letters patent to incorporate a port authority **without share capital** if the port meets these criteria:
1. It is and will remain **financially self-sufficient**.
2. It is of **strategic significance** to Canada‚Äôs trade.
3. It is **linked to a major rail or highway infrastructure**.
4. It has **diversified traffic** (e.g., freight, cargo, passengers, etc.).

### Contents of Letters Patent:
- Corporate name and registered office location  
- Jurisdictional navigable waters  
- Federal real property and immovables under management  
- Non-federal real property and immovables held or occupied  
- **Board composition**: Between 7 and 11 directors, appointed as follows:
  - 1 nominated by the Minister  
  - 1 appointed by municipalities (as listed in letters patent)  
  - 1 appointed by the province (or provinces, e.g., Alberta, Saskatchewan, Manitoba in Vancouver)  
  - Remaining directors: nominated by the Minister in consultation with users or classes of users  
- A **code of conduct** for directors and officers  
- A **charge on gross revenues** to be paid annually to the Minister to maintain the letters patent in good standing  
- Scope of port activities (paragraphs 28(2)(a) and (b))  
- Maximum term of lease or licence of federal real property  
- Limits on contracting as agent for Her Majesty  
- Limits on borrowing money (with governing code)  
- Any other provisions deemed appropriate and not inconsistent with the Act  

> ‚öñÔ∏è **Approval Requirements**:
> - Provisions on **activities (28(2)(b))** must be approved by the **President of the Treasury Board and the Minister of Finance**.
> - Provisions on **borrowing limits** must be approved by the **Governor in Council**, on recommendation of the Minister and Minister of Finance.

### üîÅ Amendments via Supplementary Letters Patent
- The Minister may issue **supplementary letters patent** to amend existing letters patent.
- Must be issued **after notice to the board** and within a time limit for board comment.
- Amendments must be **consistent with the Act** and take effect on the date stated.

> ‚úÖ These amendments allow flexibility in adapting to changing economic or operational needs.

## üîπ **Transition from Harbour Commissions to Port Authorities (Section 10)**

This section governs the **transition** of existing harbor commissions (under the *Harbour Commissions Act*) into port authorities.

### When a Harbour Commission Becomes a Port Authority:
- Upon issuance of letters patent, the harbor commission **ceases to exist**.
- It becomes a **port authority** with full legal rights and obligations.

### Rights and Obligations of the New Port Authority:
1. **Corporate Name**: Replaced in all contracts, leases, permits, and agreements.
2. **Federal Property**: Management rights over federal real property and immovables are transferred to the port authority.
3. **Non-Federal Property**: All real property and immovables (excluding federal) previously held or occupied by the harbor commission become the property of the port authority.
4. **Personal Property**: Rights and title to personal property or movable assets held on behalf of Her Majesty are transferred to the port authority.
5. **Existing Legal Claims**:
   - Existing lawsuits, claims, or liabilities remain unaffected.
   - **Judgments or orders must be satisfied first by the port authority**.
6. **Pending Proceedings**:
   - Civil, criminal, or administrative actions must be continued **only by or against the port authority**.
7. **Enforcement of Convictions or Judgments**:
   - Subject to paragraph (e), only the port authority can enforce rulings or judgments against or in favor of the former harbor commission.

> ‚ùå **Commissioners lose office** and have **no right to compensation, damages, or indemnity** upon transition.
