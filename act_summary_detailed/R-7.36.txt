### ğŸ” **Purpose and Legislative Context**

The **Retail Payment Activities Act (RPA Act)** was enacted to ensure the **safety, efficiency, and integrity** of retail payment systems in Canada. It responds to several critical concerns:

- The **growing complexity** of digital payment technologies.
- The **expansion of payment service providers (PSPs)** beyond traditional banks.
- The need to **protect end-user funds** and mitigate operational risks.
- The importance of **national security** and financial stability.
- The promotion of **competition, innovation, and consumer confidence** in the payment sector.

The Act establishes a **supervisory framework** for retail payment activities â€” defined broadly â€” to ensure that all entities performing such activities in Canada are held to consistent standards of safety, transparency, and accountability.

## ğŸ”§ **Key Definitions**

Understanding the Act begins with its core definitions:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Payment Service Provider (PSP)** | An individual or entity that performs payment functions as a core business activity (not incidental). |
| **Retail Payment Activity** | A payment function related to an electronic funds transfer (EFT) made in Canadian currency or another currency using a prescribed unit. |
| **Payment Function** | Includes: account holding, fund holding until withdrawal, initiating transfers, authorizing transfers, and providing clearing/settlement services. |
| **End User** | Any individual or entity that uses a payment service as a payer or payee. |
| **Operational Risk** | Risk of disruption due to: system deficiencies, human error, management failure, or external events. |
| **Incident** | An unplanned event or series of events that could lead to breakdown or deterioration of retail payment activities. |
| **Third-Party Service Provider** | A non-employee, non-agent entity that provides a service to a PSP related to a payment function. |
| **Affiliation** | Entities are affiliated if one controls another, or both are subsidiaries of the same entity, or are controlled by the same individual/entity. |

> *Note: "Prescribed" means defined by regulation; "Registered" means under section 25.*

## ğŸŒ **Scope and Application**

### âœ… **When Does the Act Apply?**

The RPA Act applies to **retail payment activities** performed by a PSP under the following conditions:

1. **PSP has a place of business in Canada**.
2. **PSP does not have a place of business in Canada but directs payment activities at individuals/entities in Canada** (e.g., foreign-based PSPs targeting Canadian users).

> âš ï¸ **Exceptions** (Act does *not* apply):

| Exception | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| **Merchant-issued instruments** | Payments made via cards or instruments issued by a merchant (e.g., store cards) that allow purchases only from that merchant or a group of merchants. |
| **Eligible financial contracts** | Payments tied to financial contracts under the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation (CDIC) Act. |
| **Cash withdrawals at ATMs** | Payments made for ATM cash withdrawals. |
| **Prescribed retail payment activities** | Activities specifically exempted by regulation. |
| **Systems designated under the Payment Clearing and Settlement Act (PCSA)** | E.g., major payment rails like Interac, SWIFT, or the Clearing House. |
| **Payments between affiliated entities** | If the PSP is one of the affiliated entities and no other PSP is involved. |
| **Specific exempted entities** | Includes banks, credit unions, co-ops, insurance companies, trust companies, loan companies, the Canadian Payments Association, and prescribed individuals/entities. |

> ğŸ“Œ **Note**: The Act does *not* apply to **agents or mandataries** of registered PSPs if they are acting within their authorized scope and are listed by the PSP.

### 1. **Supervision by the Bank of Canada**

The **Bank of Canada (Bank)** is the primary regulator under this Act and has three key objectives:

1. **Supervise** PSPs to ensure compliance with the Act.
2. **Promote adoption** of policies and procedures to meet obligations.
3. **Monitor and evaluate** trends and risks in the retail payment sector.

> The Bank must balance **efficiency of payment services** with **end-user protection**.

#### Powers and Authorities of the Bank:
- May enter into agreements with **government authorities** (e.g., RCMP, CSIS, CSEC).
- May issue **guidelines** on how to apply the Act (except for specific sections).
- May delegate powers to officers of the Bank (with public notice in the *Canada Gazette*).
- May assess a PSPâ€™s **risk management and incident response framework**.
- May issue **corrective measures** if deficiencies are found.
- May require **assistance, documents, and data access** from PSPs.

> âš–ï¸ **No liability** for actions taken in good faith by the Bank, its officers, or the Minister.

### 2. **Risk Management and Incident Response Framework**

Every PSP performing retail payment activities must:

- Establish, implement, and maintain a **risk management and incident response framework** that meets **prescribed requirements** (as set by regulation).
- This includes identifying operational risks and preparing for incidents.

#### Incident Notification Requirements:
If a PSP becomes aware of an incident with **material impact**, it must:
- Notify the **affected end user(s)**.
- Notify the **Bank of Canada**.
- The notice must be in **prescribed form and manner**, containing **prescribed information**.

> The Bank may issue a follow-up order directing the PSP to:
- Notify additional parties (e.g., other PSPs, clearing houses).
- Specify **when**, **how**, and **what information** to provide.

---

### 3. **Safeguarding End-User Funds**

A critical principle: **end-user funds must be protected**.

When a PSP holds funds on behalf of end users (e.g., pending transfers or deposits), it must:

- Hold funds in a **trust account** not used for other purposes.
- Hold funds in a **prescribed account** with specific safeguards.
- OR hold funds in a non-dual-use account **and provide insurance or guarantee** equal to or greater than the amount held.

> âš ï¸ **No set-off or compensation rights** can be exercised against funds held in these accounts.

> ğŸ“Œ **Exception**: If the PSP accepts deposits insured/guaranteed under a provincial Act, the rule does not apply.

### 4. **Annual Reporting and Change Notifications**

#### Annual Report:
PSPs must submit an annual report to the Bank that includes:
- Details of their **risk management and incident response framework**.
- Information about **accounts holding end-user funds** and associated insurance/guarantees.
- Any other **prescribed information**.

#### Pre-Change Notification:
Before making a **significant change** to a payment activity or launching a new one, a PSP must:
- Notify the Bank **in advance**.
- Provide the notification in **prescribed form and manner**.
- Include **prescribed information**.

> A change is considered "significant" if it could **materially impact operational risks or fund safety**.

---

### 5. **Registration Requirements**

All PSPs must be **registered with the Bank of Canada** before performing any retail payment activities.

#### Registration Process:
- Applicants must submit a **prescribed application** including:
  - Name, address, and contact details.
  - Declaration about operating from a dwelling house.
  - Organizational structure (incorporation, ownership, affiliations).
- The Bank must **register the applicant as soon as feasible**.
- The Bank maintains a **public registry** of:
  - Registered PSPs (name, address, activities, registration status).
  - Refusals or revocations (with reasons).

> âš ï¸ The Bank **cannot add** an entity to the refusal list unless:
- The review period under section 41, 46, 50, or 53 has expired, **or**
- The refusal/revocation has been confirmed.

#### Special Rules for Acquisitions or Changes:
- If a **planned acquisition** will result in the Act no longer applying to the PSP (due to section 9 or 10), the registration requirement **does not apply**.
- If a PSP plans a **prescribed change** (e.g., new technology, new service), it must:
  - Submit a **new registration application** before the change takes effect.
  - Be re-registered accordingly.

### 6. **Interplay with Other Legislation**

- If a **federal or provincial law** applies to a PSP and is **substantially similar** to a provision in the RPA Act, the **Governor of the Bank** may issue an order to **specify** that provision.
- In such cases, the **specified provision does not apply** to the PSP or class of PSPs involved.

> This allows for **harmonization** with existing laws (e.g., deposit insurance, anti-money laundering rules).
