**Detailed Summary of the *Law Commission of Canada Act* (1997)**

---

### **Overview**

The *Law Commission of Canada Act*, enacted on April 21, 1997 (Statutory Instrument SI/97-46), establishes the **Law Commission of Canada (LCC)** as an independent, non-partisan body tasked with reviewing, modernizing, and reforming Canada’s legal system. The Act is grounded in extensive national consultations and reflects a commitment to inclusivity, innovation, and responsiveness to the evolving needs of Canadian society.

The primary goal of the Commission is to ensure that Canada’s legal system remains **just, efficient, accessible, and relevant** in a rapidly changing social and economic context—while respecting both common law and civil law traditions.

---

## **Key Provisions and Components**

---

### **1. Purpose of the Law Commission of Canada**

The Commission is established to:
- Study and systematically review the **law of Canada** and its **effects on individuals and society**.
- Provide **independent, evidence-based advice** on improvements, modernization, and reform.
- Promote a **just legal system** that meets the needs of all Canadians, including marginalized and underrepresented groups.

Specific objectives include:
- Developing **new legal concepts and approaches** to address contemporary issues.
- Enhancing the **efficiency, economy, and accessibility** of the legal system.
- Stimulating **critical debate and collaboration** among academic, legal, and community institutions.
- Identifying and eliminating **obsolete laws and legal anomalies**.

> *This purpose emphasizes not just legal technicality but also social equity and practical effectiveness.*

---

### **2. Authority and Functions of the Commission**

The Commission has broad powers to carry out its mandate, including:

| Function | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Conduct Research & Studies** | Undertake, promote, initiate, and evaluate studies and research on various aspects of Canadian law. |
| **Publish and Disseminate Reports** | Support, publish, sell, and distribute reports, studies, and documents to the public and stakeholders. |
| **Organize Events** | Sponsor or support conferences, seminars, and other meetings to foster dialogue and knowledge sharing. |
| **Foster Partnerships** | Facilitate cooperation with governments, legal professionals, academia, and civil society. |
| **Acquire and Manage Assets** | Receive gifts, bequests, or donations and manage financial and property resources. |
| **Generate Revenue** | Earn income through goods or services (e.g., licensing, consulting). |
| **Expend Funds** | Use funds provided by Parliament or other sources to conduct operations. |

> These powers enable the Commission to operate with financial flexibility and autonomy while remaining accountable.

---

### **3. Governance Structure**

#### **The Board of Commissioners**
- Composed of:
  - **One President**
  - **Four other Commissioners**
- Appointed by the **Governor in Council** on the **recommendation of the Minister of Justice**.

#### **Appointment Criteria**
- **Not restricted to legal professionals** – encourages diversity in expertise and background.
- Must reflect **socio-economic and cultural diversity** across Canada.
- Should represent **multiple disciplines** (e.g., law, sociology, economics, policy, Indigenous knowledge).
- Must have **knowledge of both common law and civil law systems**.

#### **Terms of Office**
- **Maximum term**: Five years.
- **No more than two terms per year** to prevent prolonged tenure and ensure regular renewal.
- The **President serves full-time**; others serve part-time.
- **Reappointment is allowed** for any Commissioner.

#### **Resignation**
- A Commissioner may resign by **written notice** to the Minister of Justice.
- Resignation becomes effective upon receipt or as specified in the notice—whichever is later.

#### **Leadership Roles**
- Commissioners **elect a Vice-President**.
- The **President** is the **chief executive officer** and presides over meetings.
- In the President’s absence or vacancy, the **Vice-President assumes all powers and duties**.
- The **President must reside in the National Capital Region** (or in a designated area) to ensure proximity to operations.

#### **Compensation**
- President: Paid by the Governor in Council.
- Other Commissioners: Paid fees (set by the Governor in Council).
- All Commissioners are entitled to **reasonable travel and living expenses** when performing duties outside their ordinary residence.

---

### **4. Head Office and Meetings**

- **Head office**: Located in the **National Capital Region** (as defined in the *National Capital Act*) or may be relocated by the Governor in Council.
- **Meetings**: Held at times and places determined by the President.
- **Remote Participation**: Commissioners may participate in meetings via **telephone or other communication tools**, and such participation counts as being physically present for all purposes.

---

### **5. By-Laws and Internal Management**

The Commission may adopt **by-laws** governing:
- Formation and duties of **committees** (which may include non-Commissioners).
- Quorum and meeting procedures.
- General management and conduct of operations.

> This allows flexibility in operational design while maintaining accountability and transparency.

---

### **6. Executive Leadership and Staff**

- An **executive director** and other necessary employees are appointed in accordance with the **Public Service Employment Act**.
- The **executive director**:
  - Reports directly to the **President**.
  - Manages day-to-day operations and staff.
  - Oversees research, project implementation, and administrative functions.

> This ensures professional, efficient management while maintaining oversight by the Commissioners.

---

### **7. Advisory Council**

A **Law Commission of Canada Advisory Council** is established to provide strategic guidance.

#### **Composition**
- **12 to 24 members** (inclusive).
- Appointed by the Commission.
- Includes the **Deputy Minister of Justice**.
- Not restricted to legal professionals.
- Must reflect **diversity in socio-economic, cultural, and disciplinary backgrounds**.

#### **Roles and Responsibilities**
- Advise the Commission on:
  - Strategic direction.
  - Long-term program of studies.
  - Performance review.
- May advise on other matters related to the Commission’s mission.

#### **Remuneration**
- Members serve **without pay**.
- Entitled to **reasonable travel and living expenses** when away from home.

> The Advisory Council acts as a **strategic advisory body**, enhancing legitimacy and public engagement.

---

### **8. Study Panels**

For specific projects, the Commission may establish **study panels**:
- Presided over by a Commissioner.
- Composed of individuals with **specialized knowledge or personal experience** in the subject matter.
- Panel members serve **without pay** but receive **reasonable travel and living expenses**.

> This ensures that research is informed by real-world perspectives and lived experiences.

---

### **9. Legal Status and Accountability**

#### **Legal Status**
- The Commission is an **agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada**.
- It may act in the name of the Crown or in its own name.
- All property acquired by the Commission belongs to **Her Majesty in right of Canada**, though it may be held in the name of the Commission.

#### **Legal Proceedings**
- Actions, suits, or claims involving the Commission may be brought **in the name of the Commission** in any court with jurisdiction.

#### **Financial Oversight**
- The **Auditor General of Canada** audits the Commission’s accounts and financial transactions.
- Audits are conducted at times deemed appropriate by the Auditor General.
- A **report of the audit** is provided to:
  - The Commission.
  - The Minister of Justice.

#### **Annual Reporting**
- The **President submits an annual report** to the Minister of Justice within **three months** of each fiscal year.
- The report includes:
  - Summary of activities.
  - Financial statements.
  - Auditor General’s audit report.

#### **Parliamentary Transparency**
- The Minister of Justice is required to **table copies of the Commission’s report** in each House of Parliament within **15 days** of receiving it.
- The Minister must also **table a response to the report** within 15 days of providing it.

> This ensures **public accountability and transparency** to Parliament and citizens.

---

### **10. Principles Guiding the Commission**

The Act explicitly establishes five guiding principles:

| Principle | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Inclusivity and Accessibility** | The Commission must engage all Canadians and ensure its findings are accessible and understandable to the public. |
| **Multidisciplinary Approach** | The law must be studied within broader social, economic, and cultural contexts—not in isolation from society. |
| **Responsiveness and Accountability** | The Commission must collaborate with diverse stakeholders, including academia, civil society, and government. |
| **Innovation and Technology** | Modern tools and methods (e.g., digital research, online consultations) should be used to improve efficiency and effectiveness. |
| **Cost-Effectiveness and Equity** | Recommendations must consider cost, impact on different groups, and fairness across populations. |

> These principles form the **ethical and operational foundation** of the Commission’s work.

---

### **11. Implementation and Entry into Force**

- The Act comes into force on a date **to be fixed by order of the Governor in Council**.
- It was officially enacted on **April 21, 1997**.

---
