#### **1. Overview and Purpose**
The *Canada â€“ United Arab Emirates Tax Convention Act, 2002* establishes the legal framework in Canada for implementing the **Canadaâ€“United Arab Emirates (UAE) Tax Convention**, signed to avoid **double taxation** and prevent **fiscal evasion** with respect to taxes on income and capital. The Convention is a bilateral agreement designed to ensure fair and equitable treatment of taxpayers from both countries, particularly in cross-border tax matters.

This Act provides the statutory authority and procedural mechanisms for Canada to give effect to the Convention within its domestic legal system.

#### **2. Key Definitions and Interpretations**

##### **(a) "Convention"**
- Refers to the **Convention between the Government of Canada and the Government of the UAE**, as set out in **Schedule 1**, and amended by the **Protocol in Schedule 2**.
- The Convention is **approved and has the force of law in Canada** during the period it is in force, as stipulated under its terms.

##### **(b) Precedence of Convention and Act**

- The **Minister of National Revenue** may issue **regulations** to implement the Convention or give effect to its provisions.

  - The date the Convention **enters into force**,

#### **3. Scope of Application**

##### **(a) Who Is Covered?**
- Applies to **persons who are residents of one or both Contracting States** (Canada or UAE).

##### **(b) Types of Taxes Covered**
- Applies to **taxes on income and capital**, regardless of how they are levied.
- Includes:
  - Taxes on **total income or capital**,
  - Taxes on **elements of income or capital**,
  - Taxes on **gains from alienation** (sale) of movable or immovable property,
  - Taxes on **capital appreciation** (increase in value of assets).

##### **(c) Specific Taxes Identified**
- **In Canada**: Taxes under the *Income Tax Act* (i.e., Canadian tax).
- **In the UAE**:
  - Income tax,
  - Corporation tax (collectively referred to as "UAE tax").

##### **(d) Future Taxes**
- The Convention applies to **any new or substantially similar taxes** introduced **after the date of signature** of the Convention, whether replacing or supplementing existing taxes.
- The **competent authorities** of both countries must **notify each other** of significant changes in domestic tax laws.

#### **4. Definitions in the Convention**

##### **(a) Geographical Terms**
- **Canada**:
  - Includes territorial waters, seabed, subsoil, and natural resources under international law.
  - Includes airspace above these areas.
- **United Arab Emirates (UAE)**:
  - Includes the mainland, islands, territorial sea, airspace, and submarine areas.
  - Sovereign rights extend to natural resource exploration and exploitation.

##### **(b) Key Terms**
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Contracting State** | Either Canada or UAE |
| **Person** | Individual, trust, company, partnership, or any other body of persons |
| **Company** | Any body corporate or entity treated as such for tax purposes |
| **Competent Authority** | In Canada: Minister of National Revenue or authorized representative; in UAE: Minister of Finance and Industry or authorized representative |
| **National** | Individual or legal entity derived from the laws of a Contracting State |
| **Enterprise of a Contracting State** | A business carried on by a resident of that State |
| **International Traffic** | Voyage of ship or aircraft operated by a Contracting State for transport, **excluding** voyages primarily between places within the other State |

#### **5. Residency Rules**

##### **(a) Residency in Canada**
- A person is a resident if they are liable to tax in Canada due to:
  - Domicile, residence, place of management, place of incorporation, or similar criteria.
- **Exclusion**: Not a resident if taxed **only** on income from sources within Canada.

##### **(b) Residency in the UAE**
- An **individual** is a resident if:
  - They are a **UAE national**,
  - Have a **substantial presence**, **permanent home**, or **habitual abode** in the UAE,
  - Their **personal and economic relations** are closer to the UAE than any other country.

- A **company** is a resident if:
  - All shares are beneficially owned by UAE residents, **or**
  - All or substantially all of its income comes from **active trade/business** in the UAE (not investment), and
  - All or substantially all of its property value is attributable to assets used in that trade.

##### **(c) Dual Residency â€“ Individual**
If an individual is a resident of both countries, residency is determined in this order:
1. **Permanent home available** â†’ Resident only in the State where it exists.
   - If in both, resident in the State with **centre of vital interests** (closest personal and economic ties).
2. If centre of vital interests cannot be determined â†’ Resident in the State with **habitual abode**.
3. If habitual abode in both or neither â†’ Resident in the State of **nationality**.
4. If national of both or neither â†’ **Mutual agreement** between competent authorities.

##### **(d) Dual Residency â€“ Non-Individuals**
- A person (e.g., company) is a resident only in the **State of which it is a national**.

#### **6. Permanent Establishment (PE)**

##### **(a) Definition**
- A **fixed place of business** through which an enterprise carries on business.
- Includes:
  - Place of management,
  - Branch,
  - Office,
  - Factory,
  - Workshop,
  - Mine, oil/gas well, quarry, or any site related to exploration/exploitation of natural resources.

##### **(b) Exclusions**
A fixed place is **not** a permanent establishment if it is used solely for:
- Storage, display, or delivery of goods,
- Maintaining stock for storage/display/delivery,
- Stock for processing by another enterprise,
- Purchasing goods or collecting information,
- Preparatory or auxiliary activities (e.g., administrative, logistical).

> âš ï¸ **Exception**: If the fixed place is used for **preparatory or auxiliary activities**, it does **not** constitute a PE.

##### **(c) Agent Exception**
- An individual acting **on behalf of an enterprise** with authority to **conclude contracts** in a Contracting State:
  - **Deems to create a PE** in that State **unless** the activities are limited to those excluded above.
- **Exception**: If the agent is acting in the **ordinary course of business** (e.g., broker, general agent), no PE is created â€” **unless** their activities are **wholly or almost wholly devoted** to the enterprise.

> âœ… **Note**: A PE is **not** created simply because an enterprise operates through a broker or agent.

##### **(d) Control or Ownership Does Not Create PE**
- Merely **controlling or being controlled** by a company in the other country, or carrying on business there, **does not** create a PE.

#### **7. Taxation of Income from Immovable Property**

- Income from **immovable property** (land, buildings, agricultural/forestry land) located in the **other country** may be taxed in that country.
- **Definition of immovable property**:
  - Includes property **accessory** to land,
  - Livestock and equipment used in agriculture/forestry,
  - Rights to variable/fixed payments for working mineral deposits or natural resources,
  - Usufruct (right to use property),
  - But **excludes** ships and aircraft.

> âœ… Applies to:
> - Direct use, letting, or use of immovable property,
> - Income from enterprises using immovable property,
> - Income from independent personal services performed using such property.

- Profits of an enterprise are taxable **only in its home country**.
