**Detailed Summary of the *Canadian Centre for Occupational Health and Safety Act***

---

### **1. Act Title and Citation**
The Act is titled the **Canadian Centre for Occupational Health and Safety Act** and may be cited as such. It establishes a national institution dedicated to advancing occupational health and safety in Canada.

---

### **2. Purpose of the Act**
The primary objective of the Act is to **promote the fundamental right of Canadians to a healthy and safe working environment**. This is achieved by creating a national institute—the *Canadian Centre for Occupational Health and Safety (CCOHS)*—that fosters research, cooperation, and policy development in occupational health and safety across all levels of government and sectors.

The Act emphasizes **inclusive governance**, ensuring that the interests of workers, employers, trade unions, federal and provincial authorities, professional communities, and the general public are represented in the Centre’s decision-making processes.

---

### **3. Key Definitions**
The Act defines several key terms:
- **Centre**: The Canadian Centre for Occupational Health and Safety established under Section 4.
- **Chairman**: The Chair of the Council (only in English).
- **Council**: The governing body of the Centre.
- **Executive Board**: A body established under subsection 14(1), responsible for day-to-day operations and strategic oversight.
- **Governor**: A member of the Council.
- **Minister**: The federal minister designated by the Governor in Council for the purposes of this Act.
- **President**: The chief executive officer of the Centre.

---

### **4. Establishment of the Centre**
The Act establishes the **Canadian Centre for Occupational Health and Safety** as a **corporation** governed by a **Council** composed of the following members, appointed by the Governor in Council:

| Group | Composition |
|------|-------------|
| **Chairman** | One individual appointed to preside over the Council. |
| **13 other governors** | One nominated by each of the ten provincial lieutenant governors in council; one by the Commissioner of Yukon; one by the Commissioner of the Northwest Territories; and one by the Commissioner of Nunavut. |
| **4 governors (workers’ representation)** | Appointed after consultation with worker-representative organizations deemed appropriate by the Governor in Council. |
| **4 governors (employers’ representation)** | Appointed after consultation with employer-representative organizations deemed appropriate by the Governor in Council. |

> *Note: The provision for appointing governors after consultation with worker and employer organizations was repealed in 2010 (s. 1684), but the structure remains in the summary as originally drafted.*

---

### **5. Objects and Mandate of the Centre**
The Centre has the following core objectives:

1. **Promote health and safety in workplaces** and protect the **physical and mental well-being** of working Canadians.
2. **Facilitate consultation and cooperation** among:
   - Federal, provincial, and territorial governments;
   - Labour and management (workers and employers).
3. **Support the development and maintenance of high occupational health and safety standards** tailored to Canada’s unique context.
4. **Assist in developing and implementing policies and programs** to reduce or eliminate occupational hazards.
5. **Serve as a national repository** for statistics and information on occupational health and safety.

---

### **6. Powers and Activities of the Centre**
The Centre is authorized to carry out a wide range of functions to fulfill its mandate:

- **Promote, assist, initiate, and evaluate research** in occupational health and safety.
- **Establish and operate systems** for collecting, recording, analyzing, evaluating, and disseminating occupational health and safety data.
- **Publish and share scientific, technological, and other relevant information** with stakeholders.
- **Provide advice, information, and services** to:
  - Workers, unions, employers;
  - Government bodies;
  - National, provincial, and international organizations;
  - The general public.
- **Support training** of personnel in occupational health and safety.
- **Sponsor public meetings, conferences, and seminars** to promote dialogue and knowledge sharing.
- **Expend funds** appropriated by Parliament or earned through operations.
- **Recognize** public or private organizations or individuals for outstanding contributions in the field.
- **Engage in other activities** that further its objectives.

Additionally, the Centre:
- May **consider written briefs or representations** submitted on occupational health and safety issues.
- May **arrange public forums** to discuss these matters.

---

### **7. Property and Financial Capacity**
- The Centre may **acquire money, securities, or property** by gift, bequest, or other means.
- It may **expend, administer, or dispose of such property** even if restricted by the terms of donation, without violating the Act.
- For operational purposes, the Centre has the **capacity, rights, powers, and privileges of a natural person**, allowing it to act independently in carrying out its duties.

---

### **8. Leadership Roles and Tenures**

#### **Chairman**
- Appointed by the Governor in Council for a term **not exceeding five years**.
- May be removed for "cause."
- Presides over Council meetings and may perform other duties assigned by the Council.
- If absent or incapacitated, a governor may act as Chairman for up to **90 days** with Governor in Council approval.
- A person who serves **two consecutive terms** as Chairman is **eligible for reappointment to the Council**, but **cannot serve as Chairman again for 12 months** after completing their second term.

#### **Governors (non-Chairman)**
- Appointed for terms **not exceeding four years**, with staggered appointments to ensure that **fewer than half of governors serve in any one year**.
- A person who serves **two consecutive terms** as a governor is **ineligible for reappointment to the Council for 12 months** after completing their second term, **except as Chairman**.
- A vacancy in the Council does **not impair the functioning** of the remaining members.

#### **President**
- Appointed by the Governor in Council **after consultation with the Council**, for a term **not exceeding five years**.
- Holds office during **good behaviour** and may be removed for "cause."
- If a governor becomes President, they **cease to be a governor** upon assuming office.
- The President is the **chief executive officer** of the Centre, responsible for:
  - Supervising staff and operations;
  - Chairing the Executive Board;
  - Performing duties assigned by the Council.
- Must **devote full time** to the role.
- If absent or incapacitated, a Centre officer may act as President for up to **60 days** with Governor in Council approval.
- **Eligible for reappointment** after term ends.

---

### **9. Executive Board**
- Composed of the **President** and **at least six governors** elected annually by the Council.
- Must meet the following criteria:
  1. The number of governors appointed under **workers’ representation (4c)** must equal the number appointed under **employers’ representation (4d)**.
  2. The total number of governors appointed under (4c) or (4d) must be **at least 50%** of the total executive board membership.
- Powers:
  - Assigned by by-laws to carry out specific functions of the Centre.
  - Must submit at each Council meeting:
    - Minutes of its proceedings since the last meeting;
    - A progress report on the Centre’s work.
- **Quorum**: A majority of members (including the President) constitutes a quorum.
- Must meet **at least six times per year**.

---

### **10. Committees and Staffing**
- The Centre may establish **advisory or other committees** as needed, subject to by-laws.
- May **employ officers, employees, and agents** necessary for its operations, as defined in by-laws.
- By-laws may regulate:
  - Committee appointments, allowances, and expenses;
  - Employment and remuneration of staff;
  - Meeting procedures;
  - Assignment of powers to the Executive Board;
  - How briefs and representations are considered.

---

### **11. Compensation and Benefits**
- **President and Acting President**:
  - Paid a salary and expenses fixed by the Governor in Council.
- **Chairman and other governors**:
  - **Not paid a salary**.
  - May receive allowances for:
    - Time spent on Centre work;
    - Extra duties (with Council approval);
    - Travel and living expenses when away from home due to Centre duties.
  - All allowances are set by the Governor in Council.

---

### **12. Head Office and Meetings**
- The **head office** is located in Canada at a site designated by the Governor in Council.
- The **Council meets at least three times per year**, with **at least one meeting at the head office**.
- Additional meetings may occur as deemed necessary by the Executive Board.
- The **Chairman presides** at Council meetings.
- The **President is entitled to attend and participate** in all Council meetings.

---

### **13. By-Laws**
- The Council may adopt **by-laws** to govern the Centre’s internal operations, including:
  - Appointment and operation of committees;
  - Employment and compensation of staff;
  - Meeting procedures;
  - Assignment of powers to the Executive Board;
  - Procedures for reviewing briefs and written submissions.

---
