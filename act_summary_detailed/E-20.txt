**Detailed Summary of the *Export Development Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *Export Development Act* establishes **Export Development Canada (EDC)**, a federal Crown corporation tasked with supporting and expanding Canada’s domestic and international economic activities. The Act provides a comprehensive legal framework governing the structure, powers, responsibilities, financial limits, governance, and operational procedures of EDC. It emphasizes strategic development financing, risk management, environmental accountability, and alignment with Canada’s broader economic and foreign policy objectives.

---

## **1. Name and Citation**
- The Act is formally titled the **Export Development Act**.
- It may be cited as such in legal and administrative contexts.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**

The Act defines several core terms used throughout:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Board** | The Board of Directors of EDC (13 members). |
| **Body corporate** | Any incorporated entity, trust, partnership, fund, unincorporated association, or government body (federal, provincial, or foreign). |
| **Chairperson** | The Chair of the Board; appointed by the Governor in Council. |
| **Director** | A member of the Board (excluding Chairperson and President); appointed by the Minister with Governor in Council approval. |
| **Entity** | Broadly defined to include individuals, corporations, trusts, government bodies, and foreign political subdivisions. |
| **Executive Committee** | A subgroup of the Board consisting of the Chairperson and four other directors. |
| **Minister** | The designated member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada (specifically for trade or finance) responsible for overseeing EDC’s mandate. |
| **Person** | Natural persons, entities, or personal representatives (e.g., executors, trustees). |
| **Personal representative** | Includes executors, administrators, guardians, trustees, receivers, attorneys, etc. |
| **President** | Chief executive officer of EDC; appointed by the Governor in Council. |
| **Property** | Includes all tangible and intangible assets—real and personal, legal and equitable—along with interests, easements, and future rights. |
| **Security interest** | A charge or lien on property to secure payment or performance of an obligation. |
| **Vice-chairperson** | Elected from among directors; acts as Chairperson in absence or vacancy. |

> *Note:* The offices of "Chairman" and "Vice-Chairman" were repealed in 2001.

---

## **3. Governance Structure**

### **Board of Directors**
- Composed of **13 directors**:
  - 1 **Chairperson** (appointed by Governor in Council for a term as determined by that body).
  - 1 **President** (appointed by Governor in Council).
  - The remaining 11 directors are appointed by the **Minister**, with **Governor in Council approval**.
- **Term limits**:
  - Directors (non-Chairperson, non-President): up to **four years**, ensuring **no more than half** of the Board is term-limited in any one year.
- **Alternate directors**:
  - Appointed from federal public service employees to serve when a director is absent or incapacitated.

### **Executive Committee**
- Composed of the **Chairperson** and **four other directors** selected by the Board.
- May **exercise any powers or duties delegated** by the Board.
- Acts as a key decision-making body for day-to-day operations.

### **Vice-Chairperson**
- Elected by the Board.
- **Presides over meetings** in the absence or incapacity of the Chairperson.
- If both Chairperson and Vice-Chairperson are absent/incapacitated, the **remaining directors** may elect a temporary Chairperson during a meeting (if a quorum is present).

---

## **4. Appointment and Remuneration**

- **Chairperson and President**:
  - Appointed by the **Governor in Council**.
  - Salary set by the Governor in Council.
- **Other Directors**:
  - Paid by EDC a salary, fees, or remuneration fixed by the Governor in Council.
  - Entitled to **reasonable travel and expenses** incurred in carrying out duties.

> *Note:* The President has **full executive authority** over EDC’s operations, except where powers are explicitly reserved to the Board or its committees.

---

## **5. Core Purposes of EDC**

EDC is established to achieve three primary objectives:

1. **Support domestic business** (at the request of the Minister and Minister of Finance):
   - To assist Canadian businesses in developing and growing their operations.
   - Must **complement** services offered by commercial financial institutions and insurers.

2. **Support and develop Canada’s export trade**:
   - Promote Canadian capacity to participate in international markets.
   - Respond to global business opportunities.

3. **Provide development financing and support**:
   - In a manner consistent with **Canada’s international development priorities** (e.g., poverty reduction, sustainable development, trade equity).

> *Notice Requirement*: Upon receiving a request under (1)(a), the Minister must **publish a notice in the Canada Gazette** specifying the request period.

---

## **6. Powers and Authority of EDC**

EDC is granted broad powers to carry out its mandate, including:

- **Acquire and dispose of property** (real or personal) via any means.
- **Enter into arrangements** that:
  - Provide insurance, reinsurance, indemnity, or guarantees.
  - Extend credit or provide undertakings to pay money.
- **Take security interests** in property.
- **Prepare, publish, and distribute information** and offer **consulting services**.
- **Incorporate, dissolve, or amalgamate subsidiaries**.
- **Make investments** or enter into transactions (including portfolio risk management).
- **Act as agent** for others or authorize others to act as agents.
- Take such **steps as necessary** to protect EDC’s interests.
- Utilize **government services and facilities** (e.g., departments, agencies of the Government of Canada).

> *Note:* These powers are subject to **regulations** made under Section 6 (see below).

---

## **7. Contingent Liability and Financial Limits**

To ensure fiscal responsibility, the Act imposes **strict limits on EDC’s contingent liability**:

### **Before October 1, 2020**:
- Maximum contingent liability = amount determined by the **Minister of Finance** during the period from the Act’s coming into force to September 30, 2020.
- If redetermined during that period, the **most recent amount** applies.

### **On and after October 1, 2020**:
- Maximum contingent liability = amount determined by the Minister of Finance under paragraph (a), or the last redetermined amount.

> **Exclusion**: Amounts that are:
> - Insured or reinsured by EDC, or
> - Covered by an agreement for indemnification  
> are **not included** in the calculation.

> **Regulatory Compliance**: EDC must comply with **conditions prescribed by the Minister of Finance** when exercising powers under paragraph (1.1)(h).

---

## **8. Regulatory Authority**

The **Governor in Council** may make **regulations** under the following areas:

1. **Disposal of property** (sale or lease).
2. **Credit and insurance arrangements** that are **not related to international business**.
3. **Credit to persons for acquiring interests in entities** (excluding security interests).
4. **Insurance/guarantees for financing acquisitions of interests in entities**.
5. **Fees for consulting services**.
6. **Acquisition of interests in entities** (excluding security interests or those arising from realization of security interests).

> **Approval Requirements**:
> - Regulations may require **approval** from:
>   - The Minister alone,
>   - The Minister jointly with the Minister of Finance,
>   - Or the Governor in Council.
> - Where such approval is required, the relevant authority is authorized to grant it.

> **Public Consultation**:
> - At least **60 days before implementation**, the Minister must publish a proposed regulation in the *Canada Gazette*.
> - Interested parties are given a **reasonable opportunity to make representations**.
> - **Exemption**: No publication is required if:
>   - The regulation has already been published (even after amendments), or
>   - The Minister determines it makes **no material substantive change** to existing regulations.

---

## **9. Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) Requirement**

To ensure environmental sustainability, EDC must conduct a **determination** before entering into any transaction related to a project:

### Required Determinations:
1. Whether the project is **likely to have adverse environmental effects**, even after mitigation measures.
2. If so, whether EDC is **justified** in entering into the transaction.

### Board Directive:
- The Board issues a **directive** that:
  - Defines key terms: *transaction*, *project*, *adverse environmental effects*, *mitigation measures*.
  - Establishes **criteria** for assessing environmental risks.
  - May create **exceptions** (by class or category) to the obligation to assess environmental effects.

> **Important**: This directive is **not a statutory instrument** under the Statutory Instruments Act and does not require parliamentary approval.

---

## **10. Authorized Capital and Share Structure**

- **Authorized capital**:
  - From the date of the Act’s coming into force to **September 30, 2020**: Amount set by the Minister of Finance (or redetermined).
  - **After October 1, 2020**: Amount equal to the last amount determined by the Minister of Finance.
