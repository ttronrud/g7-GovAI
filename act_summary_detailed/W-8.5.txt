**Detailed Summary of the *Wild Animal and Plant Protection and Regulation of International and Interprovincial Trade Act* (Canada)**

---

### **1. Introduction and Citation**
The *Wild Animal and Plant Protection and Regulation of International and Interprovincial Trade Act* (commonly referred to as the **WAPR Act**) is a federal law enacted by Her Majesty in right of Canada, with the advice and consent of both the Senate and the House of Commons. It is designed to protect wildlife and plant species from overexploitation through the regulation of international and interprovincial trade.

This Act is formally cited as:
> *The Wild Animal and Plant Protection and Regulation of International and Interprovincial Trade Act.*

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

The Act establishes clear definitions for key terms used throughout:

- **Animal**:  
  Any living or dead specimen of a species listed as “fauna” in an appendix to the *Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora* (CITES), including eggs, sperm, tissue cultures, or embryos.

- **Plant**:  
  Any living or dead specimen of a species listed as “flora” in a CITES appendix, including seeds, spores, pollen, or tissue cultures.

- **Convention (CITES)**:  
  The international treaty adopted on March 3, 1973, in Washington, D.C., which regulates the international trade of endangered species. Canada ratified it on April 10, 1975, and the Act applies only to amendments that are binding on Canada.

- **Conveyance**:  
  Any vehicle, aircraft, watercraft, or other device used to transport persons or goods.

- **Distribute**:  
  Includes the act of selling.

- **Minister**:  
  The Minister of the Environment (as of the time of enactment).

- **Officer**:  
  A person designated by the Minister under section 12 to enforce the Act. May include government employees or designated individuals.

- **Prescribed**:  
  Means as defined or specified in regulations.

- **Transport**:  
  Includes sending or moving goods, including interprovincial movement.

---

### **3. Purpose of the Act**
The primary purpose of the Act is:
- To **protect endangered and vulnerable species of animals and plants**.
- To **implement the CITES Convention** in Canadian law.
- To **regulate international and interprovincial trade** in wildlife and plant specimens, ensuring such trade does not threaten species survival.

---

### **4. Cooperation Between Federal and Provincial Governments**

The Minister may enter into agreements with provincial governments to:
- Coordinate enforcement efforts.
- Avoid duplication or conflict between federal and provincial regulations.
- Promote cooperative management of species protection.

This ensures a unified and efficient approach to conservation across jurisdictions.

---

### **5. Prohibitions on Illegal Trade**

#### **(a) Prohibition on Importing Contraband**
No person may import into Canada:
- Any animal or plant that was taken, or
- Any part or derivative of an animal or plant that was possessed, distributed, or transported in violation of **any foreign country’s laws**.

#### **(b) Prohibition on Unauthorized Import/Export or Interprovincial Transport**
Subject to regulations, **no person may import, export, or transport (interprovincially)** any animal, plant, or part/derivative **unless authorized by a permit** issued under subsection 10(1).

#### **(c) Provincial Permit Requirements**
Where a province requires a permit for transport out of its territory, **no person may transport an animal or plant from that province to another province without the permit** issued by the competent provincial authority.

#### **(d) Prohibition on Transport in Violation of Provincial Law**
No person may transport an animal or plant from one province to another if:
- It was taken, possessed, distributed, or transported in **violation of a provincial law or regulation**.

#### **(e) Prohibition on Possession for Illegal Purposes**
No person may **knowingly possess** an animal or plant (or part/derivative) if:
- It was imported or transported in contravention of this Act;
- It is intended for transport between provinces in contravention of this Act; or
- It is intended for distribution or offering for distribution **if the species is listed in Appendix I of CITES** (which includes species threatened with extinction).

> *Note: Appendix I includes species that are most endangered and are subject to the strictest trade restrictions.*

---

### **6. Recordkeeping Requirements**

All persons involved in importing, exporting, or interprovincial transport must:
- Keep **required documents** in Canada.
- Maintain them in the **prescribed manner**.
- Retain them for the **prescribed period** (as set by regulation).

These records support compliance monitoring and enforcement.

---

### **7. Permits and Permitting Process**

The Minister may issue permits to allow:
- Importation
- Exportation
- Interprovincial transport

#### **Application Requirements**:
- Must be submitted in the form and with the conditions required by the Minister.
- Must contain all required information.
- Must include prescribed fees.

#### **Revocation and Suspension**:
- The Minister may revoke or suspend a permit if a holder violates any condition of the permit.
- A person must be given a chance to respond before action is taken.

#### **Delegation of Permit Authority**:
- The Minister may delegate permit authority to:
  - Other federal ministers
  - Provincial ministers
  - Government employees
- Delegated authorities must operate under conditions set by the Minister.

---

### **8. Enforcement Powers: Officers and Analysts**

#### **Designation of Officers**
- The Minister may designate individuals or classes of persons as **officers or analysts**.
- If the designated person is a government employee, the Minister must obtain **agreement from the provincial government**.

#### **Powers of Officers and Analysts**
- Officers have **all the powers of a peace officer** under the Act.
- The Minister may limit these powers for certain officers, and such limitations must be specified in a certificate.

#### **Certificate Requirement**
- Officers or analysts must show a **certificate approved by the Minister** when entering a place.

#### **Prohibitions Against Obstruction**
- No person may:
  - Make a **false or misleading statement** orally or in writing to an officer or analyst.
  - **Obstruct or hinder** an officer or analyst in carrying out their duties.

#### **Liability and Immunity**
- Officers and analysts are **not personally liable** for actions taken in good faith.
- A document signed by an analyst is **admissible in evidence** without proof of the signature or official status.
- The party against whom the document is presented may require the analyst to appear in court with leave of the court.

#### **Notice Requirement**
- A document may only be presented in evidence if the party intends to produce it gives **reasonable notice** and provides a copy to the opposing party.

---

### **9. Search, Inspection, and Seizure Powers**

#### **General Inspection Authority**
An officer may enter and inspect any place where:
- There is a thing subject to this Act
- Or documents related to its administration are stored

#### **Powers During Inspection**
- Open or cause to be opened any container believed to contain a protected item.
- Inspect the item and take **free samples**.
- Require any person to produce documents relevant to the Act.
- **Seize** any item believed to be in violation of the Act or likely to prove a violation.

#### **Analyst Accompaniment**
- Analysts may accompany officers during inspections and may enter the premises and exercise the same powers as described above.

#### **Conveyance Stops**
- Officers may stop a conveyance and direct it to be moved to a location where inspection can occur.

#### **Entry into Dwelling Places**
- Officers may **not enter private dwellings without consent or a warrant**.
- However, a **justice of the peace** may issue a warrant if:
  - There are reasonable grounds to believe the item is in a dwelling.
  - Entry is necessary for enforcement.
  - Entry has been refused or is likely to be refused.

> *Note: This power is limited to exigent circumstances and is not a general warrant.*

#### **Use of Private Property**
- Officers and analysts may enter and pass through private property **without liability or objection** when carrying out duties under this Act.

---

### **10. Obligations of Persons Under Inspection**

The owner or person in charge of a place being inspected must:
- Provide **all reasonable assistance** to officers or analysts.
- Provide any information reasonably required for enforcement.

---

### **11. Search and Seizure Without Warrant (Exigent Circumstances)**

An officer may exercise the powers of **search and seizure under section 487 of the Criminal Code without a warrant** if:
- The conditions for a warrant exist, but
- Obtaining a warrant would be **impractical due to exigent circumstances**.

This ensures timely enforcement in urgent situations.

---

### **12. Detention, Seizure, and Disposal of Items**

#### **Detention**
- Officers may **detain** any item that appears to be in violation of the Act or regulations.

#### **Retention and Disposal**
- The officer may retain custody or transfer it to another person.
- If the item is **perishable**, it may be disposed of or destroyed.
- Any proceeds from disposal go to the **lawful owner**, **unless**:
  - Proceedings under the Act are not initiated within **90 days** of seizure.

#### **Abandonment**
- The owner, importer, or exporter may **abandon** a detained or seized item to Her Majesty in right of Canada.
