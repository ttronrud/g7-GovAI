**Detailed Summary of the *Animal Pedigree Act***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *Animal Pedigree Act* establishes a legal framework for the **registration, identification, and maintenance of animal pedigrees** in Canada. Its primary objectives are to **promote breed improvement** and to **protect breeders and buyers** by ensuring transparency, traceability, and integrity in the breeding and ownership of livestock. The Act governs the creation and operation of **animal pedigree associations**, which are responsible for maintaining genealogical records and issuing official certificates for animals of both established ("distinct") and emerging ("evolving") breeds.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Animal Pedigree Association (Association)** | A body incorporated under this Act to manage breeding records. Includes associations amalgamated under section 25 or those subject to the Act via section 68. |
| **Board** | The Board of Directors of the Canadian Livestock Records Corporation (Corporation). |
| **Certificate of Identification** | Issued for animals of an *evolving breed* to confirm their identity during the transition phase. |
| **Certificate of Registration** | Issued for animals of a *distinct breed* to confirm their registration as a purebred. |
| **Corporation** | The *Canadian Livestock Records Corporation*, established under section 35. |
| **Embryo Certificate** | A certificate issued for embryos that are part of breeding programs. |
| **Evolving Breed** | A group of animals in the process of developing into a new, distinct breed. |
| **Foundation Stock** | The original animals recognized by the Minister as the basis of a distinct breed. |
| **Identification** | Recognition that an animal belongs to an evolving breed. |
| **Individual Identification** | Physical marking (e.g., tagging), photography, or other means used to distinguish an individual animal. |
| **Minister** | The Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food, who has regulatory authority. |
| **Pedigree** | Genealogical information showing an animal’s ancestral lineage. |
| **Prescribed** | As defined in regulations. |
| **Purebred** | An animal of a distinct breed that meets genetic standards set by the association authorized to register it. |
| **Registration** | Recognition that an animal belongs to a distinct breed. |
| **Semen Certificate** | A certificate issued for semen used in breeding programs. |

---

## **Purposes of the Act**

1. **Promote breed improvement** through scientific breeding practices and record-keeping.
2. **Protect individuals** who raise and purchase animals by providing reliable, verifiable records of lineage and quality.

---

## **Establishment of Animal Pedigree Associations**

### **Eligibility to Incorporate**
An association may be incorporated only if the Minister is satisfied that:
- The animals of each distinct or evolving breed have **significant value**.
- The applicants represent **breeders throughout Canada** for the relevant breed(s).
- Maintaining pedigrees and records will be **beneficial to breeders and the public**.

### **Scientific Basis Requirement**
- For a **distinct breed**: Must be based on scientific genetic principles.
- For an **evolving breed**: Must demonstrate that the breed can develop into a genetically stable new breed.

### **Limitations on Incorporation**
- Only **one association** may be incorporated per distinct breed or evolving breed.
- If an association serves multiple breeds, all must be of the **same species** (e.g., all cattle, all sheep, etc.).

### **Application Process**
- **Minimum Requirement**: Five or more qualified persons.
- **Qualified Person**: Must be at least 18 years old and a Canadian citizen or permanent resident.
- **Articles of Incorporation** must:
  - Be in **prescribed form**.
  - Include:
    - Name of the association.
    - Names and addresses of incorporators.
    - Names and addresses of first directors/officers.
    - Names of all distinct and evolving breeds covered.
    - For evolving breeds: Physical and genetic description of the intended new breed.
  - Be submitted in **triplicate** in the prescribed manner.

### **Minister’s Approval**
Upon satisfaction, the Minister:
- Issues a **certificate of incorporation**.
- Endorses the articles of incorporation.
- Files two copies with the Department of Agriculture and Agri-Food.
- Returns one copy to the association.
- Publishes a notice in the *Canada Gazette*.

> **Effective Date**: The association comes into existence on the date specified in the certificate of incorporation.

---

## **Corporate Status and Powers of Associations**

- Every association is a **body corporate** with perpetual existence.
- It may:
  - Acquire, hold, and dispose of real and personal property.
  - Borrow money and issue negotiable instruments (e.g., promissory notes).
  - Mortgage or secure property for obligations.
- Funds may be used for:
  - Breed improvement programs.
  - Grants to exhibitions.
  - Services for breeders (e.g., record-keeping, registration support).

### **Profit Distribution**
- All profits or appreciation in value must be **used to further the association’s purpose**.
- **No distributions** may be made to members, directors, officers, or employees—directly or indirectly.

### **Liability of Members**
- Members are **not personally liable** for debts of the association beyond:
  - Fees owed to the association.
  - Fees due for services provided by the association.

### **Liability of Officers and Employees**
- No individual is personally liable for acts done **in good faith** in the course of their duties.

## **Mandatory By-Laws**

Every association must adopt by-laws within **one year of incorporation**. These by-laws must cover:

1. **Membership Requirements**  
   - Eligibility criteria, membership fees, and procedures for joining or leaving.

2. **Election and Governance**  
   - Procedures for electing directors and officers.
   - Vacancy filling, powers, duties, and term limits.

3. **Meetings**  
   - Schedule, quorum, and procedures for general, annual, and special meetings.

4. **Amendments and Dissolution**  
   - Process for changing by-laws, merging with other associations, or dissolving.

5. **Head Office and Branches**  
   - Location of head office and branch offices.

6. **Fiscal Year**  
   - Definition of the association’s fiscal year.

7. **Records and Audits**  
   - Requirements for maintaining records of articles, by-laws, business, and financial affairs.
   - Annual audit and submission of audited financial reports.

8. **Registration and Identification Procedures**  
   - Rules for eligibility, application processes, and issuance of certificates (registration or identification).

9. **Individual Identification**  
   - Methods for identifying animals (tagging, photography).
   - Supervision of practices and enforcement of standards.

10. **Pedigree and Breeding Records**  
   - Requirements for maintaining pedigree records.
   - Right of the association to inspect records of members.

11. **Ownership Transfers**  
   - Recording of transfers of ownership of registered or identified animals.

12. **Fees**  
   - Determination of fees for registration, identification, transfers, and services.

13. **Administration**  
   - General management and operational rules.

### **Additional By-Laws (Permitted)**
Associations may adopt additional by-laws for:
- Promoting breed improvement programs.
- Requiring inspection of animals before registration/identification.
- Setting performance standards (e.g., health, conformation).
- Resignation, suspension, or expulsion of members.

---

## **Regulation of Semen and Embryo Records**

If an association records or issues certificates for:
- Semen
- Embryos

It must do so **only in accordance with its by-laws**, which must include:
- Rules for individual identification and supervision of practices.
- Procedures for issuing, transferring, and canceling semen/embryo certificates.
- Recording of ownership transfers of such biological materials.

---

## **Ministerial Approval of By-Laws**

- **No by-law is effective without Ministerial approval**.
- An application for approval must include **three copies** of the proposed by-law, amendment, or repeal.
- If a by-law requires a formal process (e.g., member consultation), the Minister may require **evidence by affidavit or statutory declaration** that the process was followed.

> Upon approval, the Minister issues:
> - A **certificate of by-law approval** (for new or amended by-laws).
> - A **certificate of by-law repeal** (for repealing by-laws).

### **Access to By-Laws**
- By-laws must be **available for inspection and copying** by all members at all reasonable times.

### **Prohibition on Fines**
- No by-law may impose **fines or monetary penalties**.
- No association may impose such penalties.
> *Exception*: Fees under section 15(1)(q) are **not considered fines**.

---

## **Amendments to Articles of Incorporation**

Associations may apply to amend their articles of incorporation for the following reasons:
1. Change the association’s name.
2. Add or delete a distinct or evolving breed.
3. Convert an evolving breed association into a distinct breed association.

### **Requirements for Amendment**
- Must be in prescribed form.
- Must include a **statutory declaration** stating:
  - Members were **consulted in writing**.
  - At least **25% of members responded**.
  - At least **two-thirds of responding members approved** the amendment.
- Must be submitted in **triplicate**.
