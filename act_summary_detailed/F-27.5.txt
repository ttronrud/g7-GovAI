**Detailed Summary of the *Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development Act***

---

### **1. Act Title and Citation**
The Act is formally titled the *Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development Act* and may be cited accordingly. This legislation establishes and restructures the federal department responsible for Canada’s external affairs, integrating foreign policy, international trade, and international development under a single, unified department.

---

### **2. Continuation and Structure of the Department**
- The **Department of Foreign Affairs and International Trade (FAIT)** is continued under the new name: **Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development (DFATD)**.
- The **Minister of Foreign Affairs** is appointed by commission under the Great Seal of Canada and serves at the pleasure of the Governor in Council (i.e., the federal Cabinet).
- The Minister holds **overall management and direction** of the Department both in Canada and abroad.

---

### **3. Key Appointments and Leadership Roles**

#### **(a) Minister for International Trade**
- Appointed by commission under the Great Seal.
- Holds office during pleasure.
- Assists the Minister in carrying out responsibilities related to **international trade**.
- Acts **only with the concurrence of the Minister** and uses the Department’s services and facilities.

#### **(b) Minister for International Development**
- Also appointed by commission under the Great Seal.
- Holds office during pleasure.
- Assists the Minister in matters relating to **international development, poverty reduction, and humanitarian assistance**.
- Acts **with the concurrence of the Minister** and has access to Departmental resources.

> Both Ministers for International Trade and Development are subordinate to the Minister of Foreign Affairs and must act in coordination with him or her.

---

### **4. Advisory Committees**
- The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet) may establish **advisory and other committees** to:
  - Advise or assist the Minister;
  - Exercise or perform specific powers, duties, or functions as specified.
- The Governor in Council may also **set the remuneration and expenses** for members of these committees.

---

### **5. Deputy and Associate Deputy Minister Appointments**
- **Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs**:
  - Appointed by the Governor in Council.
  - Serves at pleasure.
  - Acts as the **deputy head** of the Department and supports the Minister in administrative and operational leadership.

- **Three Associate Deputy Ministers of Foreign Affairs**:
  - Each holds the **rank and status of a deputy head of a department**.
  - Appointed under the Deputy Minister’s authority.
  - Exercise powers and duties as specified by the Minister.
  - One of these is designated as **Deputy Minister for International Trade**.
  - One is designated as **Deputy Minister for International Development**.

- **Coordinator, International Economic Relations**:
  - Appointed from the federal public service.
  - Has the **rank and status of a deputy head of a department**.
  - Operates under the Minister’s direction and performs duties as assigned.

---

### **6. Scope of Powers and Duties of the Minister**
The Minister has broad authority over all matters related to Canada’s external affairs **not assigned to another department**, including:

- Conducting **all diplomatic and consular relations** on behalf of Canada.
- Managing **official communications** between Canada and other countries or international organizations.
- Leading and coordinating **international negotiations** involving Canada.
- Coordinating Canada’s **international economic relations**.
- Promoting the **expansion of Canada’s international trade and commerce**.
- Fostering **sustainable international development** and **poverty reduction** in developing countries.
- Providing **humanitarian assistance** during global crises.
- Directing the **heads of Canada’s diplomatic and consular missions** (e.g., embassies, consulates).
- Managing **Canada’s diplomatic and consular missions** globally.
- Administering the **foreign service** of Canada.
- Promoting the **development and application of international law** in Canada’s foreign policy.
- Performing any other duties assigned by law.

> This gives the Minister a comprehensive mandate across foreign policy, trade, and development.

---

### **7. Restrictions on International Trade Commitments**
To prevent adverse impacts on Canadian agricultural protection, the Minister **shall not make any commitment** (e.g., through trade treaties or agreements) that would:
- **Increase the tariff rate quota** (as defined in subsection 2(1) of the *Customs Tariff*) for **dairy products, poultry, or eggs**; or
- **Reduce the tariff** applicable to those goods when imported **in excess of the tariff rate quota**.

> This provision protects Canadian agricultural interests from trade agreements that could harm domestic producers.

---

### **8. Promotion of Canada’s Interests Abroad**
The Minister may develop and implement programs to:
- Expand Canada’s **international trade and commerce**.
- Support **sustainable international development** and **poverty reduction**.
- Provide **humanitarian assistance** during emergencies.

These programs are designed to advance Canada’s national interests globally.

---

### **9. Fees for Travel Documents**
- The **Governor in Council** may, on the recommendation of the Minister and the **Treasury Board**, issue regulations specifying:
  - Documents issued by the **Minister of Citizenship and Immigration** for travel (e.g., visas, travel permits) that require fees.
  - The **amount, timing, and method** of fee payment.
- The fees are intended to **recover the costs** incurred by Her Majesty in Right of Canada in providing **consular services**.
- These fees are **additional** to any fees already required under **Section 19 of the Financial Administration Act**.

---

### **10. Agreements with Provincial Governments**
- The Minister may, with **Governor in Council approval**, enter into **agreements** with:
  - Any **province** or
  - Any **agency of a province**
  to carry out programs related to the Minister’s powers, duties, and functions (e.g., trade promotion, development initiatives).

> This allows for **cooperation and collaboration** between federal and provincial governments in foreign affairs and development.

---

### **11. Specific Responsibilities of the Ministers for Trade and Development**

#### **Minister for International Trade (Subject to Section 5)**
- Promotes the expansion of Canadian international trade and commerce by:
  - Supporting Canadian exporters in **international marketing** and **export sales**.
  - Improving access to foreign markets through **trade negotiations**.
  - Building and strengthening **trade relations** with other countries.
  - Contributing to **improving global trading conditions**.

#### **Minister for International Development (Subject to Section 5)**
- Promotes sustainable development and poverty reduction in developing countries and provides humanitarian assistance during crises by:
  - Undertaking activities in international development and humanitarian aid.
  - Ensuring the **effectiveness** and **accountability** of Canada’s aid programs.
  - Building relationships with other countries and organizations involved in development and humanitarian work.
  - Ensuring that Canada’s contributions align with **Canadian values and priorities** (e.g., human rights, gender equality, environmental sustainability).

---

### **12. Definition of "Head of Mission"**
- A **head of mission** includes:
  - An ambassador, high commissioner, or consul-general.
  - Any other person appointed to represent Canada in a country, region, or at an international organization, and designated as head of mission by the Governor in Council.
- **Except as instructed otherwise by the Governor in Council**, a head of mission:
  - Has **full management and direction** of their mission.
  - Supervises the official activities of all Government departments and agencies operating in that country or at the international body.

> This grants significant operational autonomy to diplomatic missions.

---

### **13. Transition of Key Positions from the Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA)**

#### **(a) Transition of Office of Minister for International Cooperation**
- Any person who held the office of **Minister for International Cooperation** on the day this Act came into force is **deemed to have taken on the office of Minister for International Development**.

#### **(b) Transition of President of CIDA**
- Any person who was the **President of the Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA)** on the day the Act came into force is **deemed to have become the Deputy Minister for International Development**.

#### **(c) Employee Status and Position Continuity**
- Employees of CIDA who held positions immediately before the Act came into force **retain their positions** in the new Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development.
- They serve under the authority of the **Deputy Minister of Foreign Affairs**.

#### **(d) Appropriation of Funds**
- Any **unexpended funds** appropriated by Parliament for CIDA in the fiscal year when this Act came into force are **deemed to have been appropriated** to the new Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development.

#### **(e) Transfer of Powers, Duties, and Functions**
- Any power, duty, or function previously vested in or exercisable by:
  - The **Minister for International Cooperation**,
  - The **President of CIDA**, or
  - Any other CIDA employee
  is now vested in or exercisable by:
  - The **Minister for International Development**,
  - The **Deputy Minister for International Development**, or
  - The appropriate officer in the new Department.

> This ensures a **smooth transition** of responsibilities and avoids gaps in service or legal authority.

---

### **Key Implications and Significance**

1. **Integration of Trade and Development**:  
   The Act formally merges foreign affairs, international trade, and international development into a single department, reflecting a shift toward a more holistic approach
