### **Detailed Summary of the *Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank Agreement Act***

---

#### **1. Title and Citation**
The Act is titled the **Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank Agreement Act** and may be cited as such. It serves as the legal framework in Canada for implementing Canada’s obligations under the **Agreement respecting the Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB)**.

---

#### **2. Definitions**
Key terms are defined:
- **Agreement**: Refers to the full treaty or agreement establishing the Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB), as set out in the Schedule.
- **Bank**: Refers to the **Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank**, a multilateral financial institution focused on infrastructure development in Asia.

---

#### **3. Approval of the Agreement**
The Agreement is formally **approved** by Canadian authorities, marking Canada’s ratification and commitment to the AIIB.

---

#### **4. Authority of the Governor in Council**
The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet) has broad powers to:
- Authorize the **acceptance of the Agreement** on behalf of Canada.
- Take **any necessary measures** to fulfill Canada’s obligations under the Agreement, including:
  - Making appointments.
  - Issuing orders and regulations.
  - Granting **privileges and immunities** as specified in the Agreement.
- Amend the **Schedule** (which contains detailed provisions) to reflect changes to the AIIB Agreement, provided such amendments are consistent with the Bank’s purpose and functions.

> This grants Canada flexibility to adapt to evolving circumstances while remaining aligned with the original intent of the AIIB.

---

#### **5. Role of the Bank of Canada**
The **Bank of Canada** acts as the **depository for the Bank’s assets** in Canada. This ensures financial oversight and secure custody of funds held within Canada.

---

#### **6. Canadian Financial Contribution**
The **Minister of Finance** may make payments from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund** to the AIIB for Canada’s **initial subscription of shares**, up to:
- **US$375 million**, or
- A higher amount specified in an **appropriation Act** passed by Parliament.

> This reflects Canada’s commitment to participate financially in the AIIB, though the amount is capped and subject to legislative approval.

---

#### **7. Purpose of the AIIB**
The AIIB is established to:
1. **Foster sustainable economic development** in Asia by investing in infrastructure and productive sectors.
2. **Improve regional connectivity and integration**, thereby promoting economic growth and social development.
3. **Enhance regional resilience** against global financial and external shocks.
4. **Promote regional cooperation** and collaboration with other multilateral and bilateral development institutions.

> The Bank aims to mobilize private and public capital to address long-term infrastructure needs across Asia.

---

#### **8. Scope of "Asia" and "Region"**
- "Asia" and "region" refer to the geographical regions and composition classified as **Asia and Oceania by the United Nations**, unless otherwise decided by the **Board of Governors**.
- This ensures alignment with international standards and allows flexibility in membership classification.

---

#### **9. Functions of the AIIB**
The Bank shall:
- Promote investment in **public and private capital** for development, especially in infrastructure and productive sectors.
- Finance projects that contribute to **harmonious economic growth**, with special attention to the needs of **less developed member countries**.
- Encourage **private investment** and supplement it when private capital is unavailable on reasonable terms.
- Undertake **other activities or services** that further these objectives.

> These functions emphasize both financial support and strategic development outcomes.

---

#### **10. Membership Criteria**
- **Eligibility**: Open to members of:
  - The **International Bank for Reconstruction and Development (IBRD)**, or
  - The **Asian Development Bank (ADB)**.
- **Regional vs. Non-Regional Members**:
  - **Regional members**: Those listed in **Part A of Schedule A** and other countries in Asia per Article 1.
  - **Non-regional members**: All others.
- **Founding Members**: Countries listed in Schedule A that signed the Agreement and met all membership conditions by the deadline in Article 58.
- **Admission of Non-Regional Members**: Any member of IBRD or ADB not joining under Article 58 may be admitted by a **Special Majority vote** of the Board of Governors (as per Article 28).
- **Application Process**: For non-sovereign entities (e.g., provinces, local governments), application must be made by the member state responsible for international relations.

---

#### **11. Authorized Capital Stock**
- Total authorized capital: **$100 billion USD**.
- Divided into:
  - **1 million shares** at **$100,000 par value each**.
  - **Paid-in shares**: $20 billion (20% of total) — already subscribed.
  - **Callable shares**: $80 billion (80% of total) — can be called upon when needed.
- The capital can be **increased** by the Board of Governors via a **Super Majority vote**, with terms and proportions (paid-in vs. callable) determined as appropriate.

> This structure allows for flexibility in capital expansion while maintaining a strong foundation of committed capital.

---

#### **12. Subscription of Shares**
- Each member must subscribe to shares in the ratio of **2:8 (paid-in to callable)**.
- Initial shares available are specified in **Schedule A**.
- For members admitted later:
  - Subscription amounts are determined by the Board of Governors.
  - **No reduction in regional members’ shareholding below 75%** of total subscribed capital, unless approved by a **Super Majority vote** of the Board of Governors.

> This protects regional interests and ensures equitable representation.

---

#### **13. Subscription Payments**
- Initial subscription payments are made in **five installments of 20% each**.
- **First installment**: Due within **30 days** of entry into force of the Agreement or upon ratification (whichever is later).
- **Subsequent installments**: Due annually thereafter (one year apart).
- **Payment currency**: Dollars or other convertible currencies; the Bank may convert payments into USD at any time.
- **Rights suspension**: Voting and other rights are **suspended** until full payment is received.
- **Less Developed Countries (LDCs)** have two options:
  1. Pay in **10 equal installments** (10% each), with first two due as per standard schedule.
  2. Mix payments in **dollar and local currency** (up to 50% of each installment), with:
     - Member must **declare** the local currency portion at time of subscription.
     - Local currency payments must be **adjusted to full USD equivalent** within 90 days.
     - If currency **depreciates significantly**, the member must pay additional amounts to maintain value.
     - If currency **appreciates significantly**, the Bank will pay back the member in local currency to adjust value.
- **Waivers**: The Bank or member may waive rights under certain conditions (e.g., currency adjustments).

> This provides flexibility for LDCs with volatile exchange rates and financial constraints.

---

#### **14. Share Issuance and Transfer**
- Shares are issued at **par value** unless the Board of Governors (by Special Majority) decides otherwise.
- Shares **cannot be pledged or encumbered**.
- Shares are **transferable only to the Bank** (i.e., not freely transferable to third parties).
- **Liability of members** is limited to the **unpaid portion** of their share price.
- No member is **personally liable** for the Bank’s obligations.

> This protects member states from undue financial exposure.

---

#### **15. Resources of the Bank**
The Bank’s **ordinary resources** include:
1. Authorized capital stock (paid-in and callable shares).
2. Funds raised through lending powers (subject to call commitments).
3. Repayments from loans or guarantees.
4. Income from loans or guarantees.
5. Any other funds not part of **Special Funds** (as defined in Article 17).

> These are used strictly for **ordinary operations** and are distinct from Special Funds.

---

#### **16. Use of Resources**
- Resources are used **exclusively** to achieve the Bank’s **purpose and functions** (Articles 1 and 2).
- Operations are divided into two types:
  - **Ordinary operations** (financed from ordinary resources).
  - **Special operations** (financed from Special Funds).
- The two types are **separately managed, accounted for, and used**.
- **Ordinary resources are not liable** for losses or liabilities arising from special operations.
- Expenses are charged accordingly:
  - Direct costs of ordinary operations → ordinary resources.
  - Direct costs of special operations → special funds.
  - Other expenses → determined by the Bank.

> Ensures financial transparency and risk isolation.

---

#### **17. Eligibility for Financing**
The Bank may provide or facilitate financing to:
- Any member or its agencies, political subdivisions, or enterprises.
- International or regional development agencies.

> Exception: The Bank may provide assistance to **non-listed recipients** only if:
1. The Board of Governors (by **Super Majority**) determines that:
   - The assistance serves the Bank’s purpose and functions.
   - It is in the interest of the membership.
2. The type of assistance is specified under Article 11.

> This ensures that all financing remains aligned with strategic goals.
