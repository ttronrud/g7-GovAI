**Detailed Summary of the *Department of Fisheries and Oceans Act***

---

### **1. Title and Citation**
The Act is formally titled the **Department of Fisheries and Oceans Act** and may be cited accordingly. This establishes the legal foundation for the creation and governance of the Department of Fisheries and Oceans (DFO) within the Government of Canada.

---

### **2. Establishment of the Department**
The Act creates a **new federal department** of the Government of Canada known as the **Department of Fisheries and Oceans (DFO)**. This department is established to oversee all matters related to Canada’s fisheries, oceans, and marine environments.

---

### **3. Appointment of the Minister**
- The **Minister of Fisheries and Oceans** is appointed by the Governor in Council (i.e., the federal Cabinet) **by commission under the Great Seal of Canada**.
- The Minister serves at the **discretion of the Governor in Council**, meaning their term is not fixed and can be terminated at any time by the government.
- The Minister is responsible for the **overall management, direction, and administration** of the Department.

---

### **4. Appointment of the Deputy Minister**
- The Governor in Council may appoint a **Deputy Minister of Fisheries and Oceans**.
- The Deputy Minister holds office during **pleasure** (i.e., at the discretion of the Governor in Council).
- The Deputy Minister serves as the **deputy head** of the Department, supporting the Minister in administrative and operational leadership.

---

### **5. Scope of Powers, Duties, and Functions**
The Minister’s powers and responsibilities are broadly defined and include:

#### **Core Jurisdictional Areas:**
- **Sea coast and inland fisheries**: Oversight of both marine and freshwater fishing activities, including regulation, sustainability, and conservation.
- **Fishing and recreational harbours**: Management of ports, harbours, and facilities used for fishing and public recreation.
- **Hydrography and marine sciences**: Conducting and supporting research in ocean mapping (hydrography), marine biology, climate change, and environmental monitoring.
- **Policy coordination**: Leading and coordinating the Government of Canada’s national policies and programs related to the **oceans**, ensuring consistency and effectiveness across federal agencies.

#### **Additional Matters:**
- The Minister also has authority over **any other matters relating to oceans** that are **within the jurisdiction of Parliament** and **specifically assigned by law** to the Minister.

> This broad mandate ensures that the DFO plays a central role in Canada’s ocean governance, integrating scientific research, environmental protection, and economic development.

---

### **6. Access to Security-Related Information**
In the course of carrying out its duties in the maritime domain, the Minister may **receive information** that:
- Relates to **activities that undermine the security of Canada**, as defined in **subsection 2(1) of the Security of Canada Information Disclosure Act (SCIDA)**.
- Is **relevant to supporting a Government of Canada institution** (as defined in SCIDA) that has jurisdiction or responsibility over such activities — including the **detection, identification, analysis, prevention, investigation, or disruption** of those threats.

> This provision allows the DFO to access sensitive security-related data to support national security objectives, particularly in maritime contexts (e.g., illegal fishing, smuggling, or threats to marine infrastructure), while respecting legal and procedural safeguards under SCIDA.

---

### **7. Interprovincial Agreements**
- The Minister may, **with the approval of the Governor in Council**, enter into **agreements with provincial governments or their agencies**.
- These agreements may pertain to the **carrying out of programs** under the DFO’s responsibility.
- This enables collaboration between federal and provincial authorities on shared responsibilities, such as fisheries management, coastal protection, or marine research.

> This provision supports **cooperative federal-provincial governance**, especially in areas where provinces have significant jurisdiction over coastal and inland waters.

---

### **8. Annual Reporting to Parliament**
- The Minister is required to submit an **annual report to Parliament**.
- The report must be submitted:
  - **On or before the 31st day of January** following the end of each fiscal year; **or**
  - If Parliament is not in session, **on any of the first five days after the start of a session** of either House of Parliament (i.e., when either House is sitting).
- The report must detail the **operations, achievements, expenditures, and performance** of the Department for that fiscal year.

> This ensures **transparency, accountability, and public oversight** of the DFO’s activities and budget.

---

### **Key Implications and Significance**
- The Act establishes the DFO as a **central, multidisciplinary agency** responsible for Canada’s marine and aquatic resources.
- It grants the Minister broad authority over **ocean-related policy, research, and enforcement**, while maintaining a clear separation between its core mandates and other departments.
- The inclusion of **national security provisions** reflects the growing importance of maritime security in Canada’s strategic interests.
- The **annual reporting requirement** ensures that the Department remains accountable to elected representatives and the public.

---

### **Conclusion**
The *Department of Fisheries and Oceans Act* creates a robust, legally grounded framework for the establishment and operation of Canada’s primary federal agency responsible for fisheries, ocean science, and maritime policy. By defining clear roles, responsibilities, and accountability mechanisms — including intergovernmental cooperation and transparency — the Act ensures that the DFO can effectively manage Canada’s vast ocean resources while contributing to national security, environmental sustainability, and economic development.
