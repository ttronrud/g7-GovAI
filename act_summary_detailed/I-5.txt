### ðŸ”¹ **Governance and Decision-Making Requirements**

> **General Principle**: Powers conferred on a band or its council are **not exercised unless approved by a majority** of relevant electors or councillors.

- **Band Powers**: Must be exercised with the consent of a **majority of the electors** of the band.
- **Council Powers**: Must be exercised with the consent of a **majority of the councillors present** at a duly convened meeting.

> **Note**: This ensures democratic accountability within band governance, even when decisions are made by councils or bands.

### ðŸ”¹ **Role of the Minister and Governor in Council**

- The **Minister of Indigenous Services** is the **superintendent general of Indian affairs**, providing overall oversight.
- The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet) may, by **proclamation**, declare that:
  - This Act (or parts of it) **does not apply** to certain Indians, groups, reserves, or surrendered lands.
  - Such declarations can be **revoked** at any time.
- **Special Authority**: The Governor in Council is deemed to have had the authority to make declarations regarding **sections 11, 12, and 14** (which relate to registration and eligibility) **prior to April 17, 1985** â€” a critical historical reference point.

> **Purpose**: Allows flexibility in applying the Act to specific communities or circumstances, especially where cultural or legal differences exist.

### ðŸ”¹ **Application of the Act to Non-Resident Indians**

> **Sections 114 to 117 and sections 42 to 52 do NOT apply** to any Indian who:
- Does **not ordinarily reside** on a reserve or on Crown-owned lands (in Canada or a province).

> **Implication**: The Act's provisions on land rights, band membership, and governance are **not automatically extended** to non-resident Indigenous individuals. This reflects a shift from broad application to more localized, community-based governance.

## ðŸ”¹ **Indian Register: Rules and Procedures**

### 1. **Establishment and Continuity**
- The Indian Register is maintained in the Department and contains the names of all persons entitled to be registered as Indians.
- The names in the Indian Register **as of April 17, 1985** are the official ones on that date â€” a key **legal reference point**.

### 2. **Addition or Deletion**
- The **Registrar** may add or delete names from the Register based on applications or legal determinations.
- Each entry includes the **date of addition or deletion**.

### 3. **Application Process**
- A name is **not required** to be recorded unless an **application is submitted** to the Registrar.

### 4. **Determining Ancestry When Parental Identity is Unknown**
> A major innovation in the 1985 amendments.

If a parent, grandparent, or ancestor of an applicant is **unknown or unstated** on a birth certificate:
- The Registrar **does not need to establish identity**.
- Can make a determination **based on credible evidence** presented by the applicant.
- Must **draw every reasonable inference in favour of the applicant**.
- **No presumption** that the ancestor was not entitled to be registered.

> **Key Principle**: Prevents disqualification due to missing documentation. Recognizes Indigenous knowledge and oral traditions.
