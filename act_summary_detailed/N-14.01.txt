**Detailed Summary of the Canada National Parks Act**

---

### **Overview**
The *Canada National Parks Act* is a foundational piece of legislation that establishes the legal framework for the creation, management, protection, and administration of Canada’s national parks and park reserves. Enacted by Her Majesty in right of Canada, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons, the Act ensures that national parks are preserved for the benefit, education, and enjoyment of present and future generations, while maintaining ecological integrity and respecting Indigenous rights.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Citation and Definitions**
- **Citation**: The Act is known as the *Canada National Parks Act*.
- **Key Definitions**:
  - **Community Plan**: A land use plan for a designated "park community" (e.g., visitor centres or towns within parks).
  - **Ecological Integrity**: The condition of a park that reflects its natural region, including abiotic factors, native species composition, biological communities, and natural processes. It is the **primary management priority**.
  - **Enforcement Officer**: A person designated under section 19 (e.g., park wardens or officers with authority to enforce rules).
  - **Minister**: The Minister responsible for the Parks Canada Agency.
  - **Park**: A national park named and described in **Schedule 1**.
  - **Park Community**: Specific communities within parks, including:
    - Visitor centres in Yoho, Banff (Lake Louise, Waterton Lakes), Jasper, Prince Albert, and Riding Mountain.
    - The town of Banff itself.
  - **Park Reserve**: A designated area in Schedule 2 established where aboriginal rights claims have been accepted for negotiation.
  - **Park Warden**: A person designated under section 18 to manage park enforcement.
  - **Public Lands**: All lands (including submerged areas) belonging to Her Majesty or subject to Canadian government disposal, regardless of provincial agreements.
  - **Superintendent**: An officer appointed under the *Parks Canada Agency Act*, including those authorized to act on behalf of a superintendent.

> *Note*: A prior provision (repealed in 2024) was removed, reflecting legislative updates.

---

### **2. Purpose and Mandate**
- The national parks are dedicated to the **public for benefit, education, and enjoyment**.
- They must be **maintained and used in a way that leaves them unimpaired for future generations**.
- The **Minister retains full authority** to set fees under existing Parks Canada Agency Act provisions (section 23 or 24), even if not explicitly stated here.

---

### **3. Establishment and Amendment of Parks and Park Reserves**
#### **Establishing or Enlarging a Park (Schedule 1)**
- The **Governor in Council** (i.e., Cabinet) may amend Schedule 1 to:
  - Add or alter the name and description of a park.
- **Conditions for amendment**:
  1. Her Majesty in right of Canada must have **clear title or unencumbered ownership** in the lands.
  2. The **provincial government** must have **agreed to the use** of those lands for park purposes.

#### **Removing or Altering a Park**
- If a court finds that the Crown does **not have clear title**, the Governor in Council may:
  - Remove or alter the park’s name or description.
- **No portion of a park may be removed** unless explicitly permitted (exception noted in subsections below).

#### **Establishing or Enlarging a Park Reserve (Schedule 2)**
- The Governor in Council may amend Schedule 2 to:
  - Add or alter a park reserve.
- **Condition**: Provincial government must agree to use the land for the reserve.

#### **Settlement of Aboriginal Claims**
- When an aboriginal claim is settled:
  - The Governor in Council may:
    1. Remove or alter the park reserve.
    2. If the settlement includes a transition to a park, and Crown title is clear, add or alter the park in Schedule 1.
- If a court finds Crown lacks title in a reserve, the reserve may be removed or altered.

> **No removal of any portion of a park or reserve** is allowed unless specifically permitted by law or settlement agreement.

---

### **4. Parliamentary Oversight and Public Consultation**
- Before any amendment to Schedule 1 or 2:
  - The proposed amendment must be **tabled in both Houses of Parliament**.
  - A **report must accompany it**, detailing:
    - Consultations conducted.
    - Agreements reached.
- The **standing committee** (or designated committee) has **30 sitting days** to report disapproval.
- If disapproval is reported, a **motion to concur** is put to the House.
- If **no disapproval motion** is passed within **31 sitting days**, the amendment may proceed.
- If **either House passes a disapproval motion**, the amendment **cannot proceed**.

> This ensures democratic accountability and public input in park expansion or reorganization.

---

### **5. Ministerial Responsibilities and Management**
- The **Minister is responsible** for:
  - Administration, management, and control of parks.
  - Use and occupation of public lands within parks.
- **Ecological integrity** is the **first priority** in all park management decisions.
  - This includes protecting natural resources, processes, and biodiversity.

---

### **6. Land Use Planning and Development in Park Communities**
- **Local governments cannot exercise land use powers** in park communities.
- Exceptions:
  - Agreements under **section 35** (general land use agreements).
  - The **Jasper Agreement** (signed 2001 between the Crown and the Municipality of Jasper), which allows local government functions in Jasper.

> This prevents local authorities from over-developing or altering park communities without federal oversight.

---

### **7. Agreements with Other Entities**
The Minister may enter into agreements with:
- **Federal and provincial ministers/agencies**.
- **Local and Indigenous governments**.
- **Bodies established under land claims agreements**.

#### Specific Agreement Types:
1. **Hydro-electric power development**:
   - For use within parks, under the *Dominion Water Power Act*.
2. **Water supply**:
   - To adjacent lands or communities (e.g., for domestic or tourism use).
   - Must **respect traditional water supply systems** used by Indigenous peoples.
3. **Use of public lands**:
   - Agreements may authorize use of public lands in parks for:
     - Schools, hospitals, churches, tourism, recreation.
   - **Termination rights**: The Minister may terminate such agreements if lands are no longer used as authorized.

---

### **8. Management Plans**
- Within **five years of a park’s establishment**, the Minister must prepare a **comprehensive management plan** that includes:
  - A long-term **ecological vision**.
  - **Ecological integrity objectives and indicators**.
  - Provisions for:
    - Resource protection and restoration.
    - Zoning.
    - Visitor use.
    - Public awareness.
    - Performance evaluation.
- The plan must be **tabled in both Houses of Parliament**.
- The plan must be **reviewed at least every 10 years**, and any amendments must also be tabled.

---

### **9. Public Participation**
- The Minister must provide **opportunities for public participation**, including:
  - Aboriginal organizations.
  - Bodies under land claims agreements.
  - Representatives from park communities.
- Participation is required in:
  - Policy development.
  - Park establishment.
  - Management planning.
  - Land use planning in park communities.
  - Other relevant matters.

> This promotes inclusive governance and Indigenous engagement.

---

### **10. Reporting Requirements**
- **At least every two years**, the Minister must table in both Houses:
  - A report on the **state of the parks**.
  - Progress toward establishing **new parks**.

> Ensures transparency and accountability to Parliament.

---

### **11. Restrictions on Disposal and Use of Public Lands**
- **No public land in a park may be disposed of** (sold, leased, or transferred) unless permitted by law or regulation.
- **No person may use or occupy public lands** in a park without authorization.
- Exceptions exist only under specific provisions (e.g., for infrastructure, services, or emergency access).

---

### **12. Wilderness Areas**
- The Governor in Council may, by regulation, declare areas of a park as **wilderness areas** if:
  - They exist in a natural state or can return to one.
- **No activity may impair the wilderness character** of such areas.
- **Exceptions** (with ministerial approval and conditions):
  - Park administration.
  - Public safety.
  - Basic facilities (e.g., trails, rudimentary campsites).
  - Activities under regulations (section 17).
  - Access by air to remote areas.
- If a management plan designates a wilderness area, the Minister must **recommend the declaration** to the Governor in Council within **one year** of the plan being tabled.

---

### **13. Leases, Easements, and Servitudes**
The Minister may enter into:
- **Leases** or **easements** over public lands for:
  1. **Existing infrastructure**:
     - Railways (right-of-way, stations).
     - Oil/gas pipelines (right-of-way, tanks, facilities).
     - Telecommunication/electrical transmission lines (right-of-way, substations).
  2. **Alterations or relocations** of such infrastructure.
  3. **Radio/TV repeaters, weather stations, scientific monitoring stations** (e.g., cosmic ray detectors
