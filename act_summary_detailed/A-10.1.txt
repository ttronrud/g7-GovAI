### **Detailed Summary of the *Air Canada Public Participation Act***

The *Air Canada Public Participation Act* (the "Act") is a legislative instrument that governs the corporate structure, operations, governance, and regulatory obligations of **Air Canada**, transitioning it from a government-owned entity under the *Air Canada Act* to a fully incorporated corporation under the *Canada Business Corporations Act* (CBCA). It establishes a framework for public participation, ensures continuity of operations, and preserves key policy objectivesâ€”particularly related to aircraft maintenance, official languages, and competitionâ€”while modernizing Air Canadaâ€™s legal status.

## **1. Purpose and Scope of the Act**

The primary purpose of the Act is to:
- **Modernize Air Canadaâ€™s legal structure** by transitioning it from a special-purpose corporation governed by the *Air Canada Act* to a standard business corporation under the *Canada Business Corporations Act*.
- **Ensure continuity of operations** and key service obligations, especially in aircraft maintenance and air services.
- **Preserve public interest and accountability** through provisions on governance, official languages, and competition.
- **Facilitate public participation** in decision-making and ensure transparency in corporate activities.

The Act is **not a standalone regulatory statute** but rather a transitional and enabling law that allows Air Canada to operate as a corporation while preserving its national significance and operational requirements.

## **2. Key Definitions**

- **Corporation**: Refers to Air Canada or any corporation continued by the *Air Canada Act*.
- **Minister**: Initially the President of the Queenâ€™s Privy Council, but may be designated by the Governor in Council as the Minister for the purposes of this Act. This role grants the Minister authority over key decisions regarding the Corporation.
- **Words and expressions** used in the Act have the same meaning as in the *Canada Business Corporations Act*, unless otherwise defined.
- **Inconsistency with CBCA**: If there is a conflict between this Act and the CBCA, **this Act prevails** to the extent of the inconsistency.
- **Competition Act**: This Act does **not affect** the operation of the *Competition Act* in relation to any acquisition of interest in Air Canada.

## **3. Transfer of Shares to the Minister**

A key provision of the Act is the **transfer of shares** held by the **Minister of Transport in trust for Her Majesty in right of Canada** to the **Minister personally**.

- The shares are transferred **notwithstanding subsection 13(3) of the Air Canada Act**.
- The shares are **registered in the name of the Minister** and are **held in trust for Her Majesty in right of Canada**.
- This ensures continuity of ownership and government oversight while allowing the Minister to manage and act on behalf of the Crown.

---

## **4. Continuance of the Corporation under CBCA**

The Corporation must submit an application for a **certificate of continuance** under **section 187 of the CBCA** to the **Minister for approval**.

Once approved:
- The Corporation must submit the approved application to the **Director of Corporations**.
- The application is **deemed to have been made under section 187(1) of the CBCA**, giving it legal standing.

This process ensures that Air Canadaâ€™s legal status as a corporation is formally recognized and maintained under federal corporate law.

Despite the transition to CBCA, the Corporation may **continue to use and be legally designated as "Air Canada"** on and after the date it becomes a CBCA corporation. This preserves brand identity and public recognition.

## **9. Official Languages Act Obligations**

The *Official Languages Act* applies to the Corporation.

### **Key Duty: Service Provision in Official Languages**

- If a **subsidiary** of Air Canada provides air services (including incidental services), the Corporation has a **duty to ensure** that:
  - Customers can **communicate with the subsidiary** in either official language.
  - Customers can **obtain services** from the subsidiary in either official language.
  
> This duty applies **only when** such services would otherwise be required under **Part IV of the Official Languages Act** (which governs service in official languages).

### **Definition of a "Subsidiary"**

A body corporate is considered a subsidiary if:
- It is **controlled** by Air Canada, or by Air Canada and one or more other bodies controlled by it, or by two or more bodies each controlled by Air Canada.
- Or, it is a subsidiary of a body that is itself a subsidiary of Air Canada.

> **Control** is defined as:  
> More than **50% of the voting rights** in the subsidiary are held **directly or indirectly** by another body corporate (not just via security), and those votes are sufficient to elect a majority of directors.

### **Geographic and Temporal Application**

- Applies to subsidiaries operating in:
  - Manitoba, British Columbia, Saskatchewan, Alberta, Yukon, Northwest Territories, or Nunavut.
- **Effective dates**:
  - For existing subsidiaries: **one year after the subsection comes into force** (July 5, 2000).
  - For new subsidiaries or Canadian Airlines International Ltd. / Canadian Regional Airlines Ltd.: **three years after becoming a subsidiary**.

> ðŸ” **Exception**: The Governor in Council may, by order, **extend the three-year period to a maximum of four years** for specific routes or facilities.

### **Successor Service Provision**

If **Canadian Airlines International Ltd. or Canadian Regional Airlines Ltd.** replaces Air Canada or one of its subsidiaries in providing an air service (on or after December 21, 1999), the Corporation has a duty to ensure that:
- Customers of the successor can **communicate with and obtain services from the successor** in either official languageâ€”**if the service would otherwise require such language under Part IV of the Official Languages Act or under this section**.

> âš ï¸ **Clarification**: These duties **do not override** any other duties under **section 25 of the Official Languages Act** (which relates to language use in government services).

> âœ… **For greater certainty**: The duties in subsections (2) and (7) are **deemed to be duties under Part IV of the Official Languages Act**.
