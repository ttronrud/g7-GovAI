**Detailed Summary of the *Canada-Mexico Tax Information Exchange Convention Act, 1991***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *Canada-Mexico Tax Information Exchange Convention Act, 1991* is a legislative instrument that enables Canada and the United Mexican States (U.M.S.) to establish a formal framework for the **exchange of tax-related information** between the two countries. The Act provides the legal foundation in Canada for implementing the **Canada-Mexico Tax Information Exchange Convention**, which was signed in 1990 and entered into force on **April 27, 1992** (as noted in the Schedule).  

This Act ensures that the Convention is legally binding in Canada and gives effect to its provisions through regulatory and administrative mechanisms.
