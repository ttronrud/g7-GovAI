**Detailed Summary of the *Telefilm Canada Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Telefilm Canada Act* establishes **Telefilm Canada** as a federal corporation tasked with fostering and promoting the development of Canada’s audio-visual industry. This Act serves as the foundational legislation for Telefilm Canada, replacing the previous *Canadian Film Development Corporation (CFDC)* and consolidating its functions under a new legal framework. The Act outlines the structure, mandate, financial provisions, governance, and operational powers of the corporation, ensuring it operates independently while remaining aligned with broader Canadian cultural and economic policies.

---

### **Key Definitions and Terminology**
- **Corporation**: Refers to *Telefilm Canada*, established by section 3.
- **Minister**: A member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada designated by the Governor in Council to oversee the Act’s implementation.
- **Canadian audio-visual production**: Defined as a production that either:
  - Has significant Canadian creative, artistic, and technical content, and ensures copyright ownership by an individual resident in Canada, a Canadian corporation, or a combination thereof; **or**
  - Is produced under a co-production agreement between Canada and another country.
- *Note*: Terms such as "film activity," "film-maker," "film production," and "film technician" have been **repealed** (removed) as of 2005 under *c. 14, s. 1*, indicating the Act focuses on broader audio-visual content (including television, digital media, and sound recordings).

---

### **Establishment and Governance of Telefilm Canada**

#### **1. Structure and Membership**
- Telefilm Canada is a **corporation with six members**, appointed by the **Governor in Council**.
- Members are selected from a pool of qualified individuals, with the following term structure:
  - **Three members appointed for a 3-year term**
  - **Three members appointed for a 5-year term**
- The **Government Film Commissioner** (from the *National Film Act*) is one of the members and is **not subject to the same term limits**.
- A **chairperson** is designated by the Governor in Council from among the members. The chairperson serves at the **discretion of the Governor in Council** and may receive a **yearly honorarium** set by the Corporation.
- A **vice-chairperson** may be appointed to act as chairperson in the absence or incapacity of the chairperson.

#### **2. Re-appointment and Vacancies**
- Retiring members are eligible for **re-appointment**.
- If a member resigns or dies before the end of their term, the Governor in Council appoints a replacement for the **remainder of the term**.
- **Eligibility**: No person who has a **direct or indirect pecuniary interest** (as shareholder, partner, or otherwise) in the audio-visual industry may serve as a member.

#### **3. Remuneration and Expenses**
- Members (excluding federal public administration officials) are paid:
  - A daily fee for attending meetings, determined by **by-law**.
  - **Reasonable travel and living expenses** incurred while fulfilling duties.
- The **Public Service Superannuation Act does not apply** to members, meaning they are not entitled to public service pension benefits.

#### **4. Quorum and Decision-Making**
- A **majority of members** constitutes a quorum for conducting business.
- A vacancy in membership **does not impair the ability of the remaining members to act**.

### **Mandate and Core Functions**

Telefilm Canada’s primary mandate is to:
> *"Foster and promote the development of the audio-visual industry in Canada."*

To fulfill this mandate, the Corporation has the **capacity, rights, powers, and privileges of a natural person**, enabling it to act independently and with full legal authority.

#### **Specific Powers and Activities**
1. **Invest in Canadian productions**:
   - Can invest in individual Canadian audio-visual productions in exchange for a **share of the proceeds**.
   - Liability is **limited to the amount invested** — it is not considered a partner in the production.

2. **Provide loans to producers**:
   - May lend money to producers of Canadian audio-visual works.
   - Loans may be **charged interest**.

3. **Make awards**:
   - Can provide awards recognizing **outstanding achievements** in Canadian audio-visual production.

4. **Grant funding to professionals**:
   - Offers grants to industry professionals (e.g., directors, technicians) to help them **improve their craft**.

5. **Advise on distribution and administration**:
   - Advises and assists producers in:
     - Distributing their works
     - Managing administrative functions of production

### **Financial Provisions**

#### **Funding and Appropriations**
- A total of **$25 million** is **appropriated from the Consolidated Revenue Fund** to support the Corporation’s operations.
- A **special account** called the *Telefilm Canada Advance Account* is established in the federal accounts.

#### **Sources of Funding**
- Amounts required for:
  - Investments in productions (para 10(3)(a))
  - Loans (para 10(3)(b))
  - Loan guarantees (para 10(5))
  → Are paid **from the $25 million appropriation or from the Advance Account** and are **charged to the Advance Account**.

- All other expenses (salaries, administration, etc.) are paid from the **$25 million appropriation** and are **charged to budgetary expenditures**.

#### **Revenue Collection and Accounting**
- The Corporation must **pay all proceeds** from:
  - Productions in which it has invested
  - Principal or interest on loans
  - Fees from loan guarantees
- These amounts are **paid to the Receiver General**, deposited into the **Consolidated Revenue Fund**, and **credited to the Telefilm Canada Advance Account**.

### **Legal and Administrative Framework**

#### **Legal Status**
- Telefilm Canada is an **agent of Her Majesty** (the Crown).
- It may enter into contracts **in the name of Her Majesty or in its own name**.
- Property acquired by the Corporation is **owned by Her Majesty**, though it may be held in the name of the Corporation.
- Legal actions (suits, claims) related to rights or obligations of the Corporation may be brought **in the name of the Corporation**, even though it acts as an agent of the Crown.

#### **Compliance with Financial Administration Act**
- **Part VIII of the Financial Administration Act** (as it existed before September 1, 1984) continues to apply to Telefilm Canada.
- Sections 90–93, subsection 94(2), and sections 95, 99, 100, and 102 of the Financial Administration Act apply to the Corporation, with **necessary modifications**.

#### **Audits and Reporting**
- The Corporation’s **financial records and transactions are audited annually** by the **Auditor General of Canada**.
- The **chairperson** must submit a **comprehensive annual report** to the Minister within **three months** of each fiscal year. The report includes:
  - Summary of activities
  - Financial statements
  - Auditor General’s audit report
- The Minister is required to **lay this report before Parliament** within **15 days** of receiving it, or on the first of the next 15 days when Parliament is sitting.

---

### **Transition from the Canadian Film Development Corporation (CFDC)**

This Act formally **replaces and succeeds** the CFDC. Key transitional provisions ensure continuity:

- All **powers, duties, and functions** previously vested in the CFDC are **transferred to Telefilm Canada**.
- Any **reference to the CFDC** in existing deeds, contracts, or legal documents is **automatically read as a reference to Telefilm Canada**.
- All **rights, property, obligations, and liabilities** of the CFDC are **deemed to belong to Telefilm Canada**.
- **Pending legal proceedings** against or involving the CFDC **may continue** against or in favor of Telefilm Canada **in the same manner and to the same extent**.
- All actions taken by the CFDC **before the Act came into force** are **valid and enforceable** as if they had been taken after the Act’s implementation.

---

### **Advisory and Support Structures**

- The **Minister may appoint an Advisory Group**, composed of:
  - Professional associations
  - Exhibitors
  - Distributors
  - Unions
  - Other qualified individuals
- The group advises the Corporation on matters referred by the Minister or the Corporation.

- The Corporation may **employ officers, employees, and technical advisers** as needed, at remuneration and terms it deems appropriate.

- The **executive director**:
  - Is appointed by the Governor in Council on the **recommendation of the Corporation**.
  - Serves at the **discretion of the Governor in Council** (during pleasure).
  - Is the **chief executive officer** of the Corporation.
  - Has **supervisory and managerial authority** over staff and operations.
  - May attend Corporation meetings.

- The Corporation may adopt **by-laws** to regulate its internal operations, subject to **Ministerial approval**.

---

### **Strategic Alignment with Government Policy**

Telefilm Canada is required to:
- Act within the **broader context of Government of Canada’s cultural policies**.
- **Consult and cooperate** with:
  - Federal departments, boards, and agencies
  - Provincial governments
  - Entities with mandates related to culture or the audio-visual industry
