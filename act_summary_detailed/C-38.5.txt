## üìú **Detailed Summary of the Act: Incorporation of the Continental Bank of Canada**

### **1. Purpose and Background**
This Act is enacted to incorporate a new financial institution ‚Äî the **Continental Bank of Canada** (in English), known as *Banque Continentale du Canada* in French ‚Äî under Canadian corporate and banking law. The incorporation is based on a petition by **IAC Limited**, a Canadian corporation, seeking to transform it into a chartered bank. The Act is considered expedient due to the strategic and financial significance of the transition.

The core objective is to **establish a chartered bank** that will eventually be fully integrated with IAC Limited through a statutory amalgamation, ensuring regulatory compliance while preserving existing rights, structures, and financial obligations.

### **2. Incorporation of the Bank**
- **Name**:  
  - English: *Continental Bank of Canada*  
  - French: *Banque Continentale du Canada*  
  - Referred to throughout as the ‚ÄúBank‚Äù

- **Structure**:  
  The Bank is incorporated as a corporation with shareholders who are individuals or entities who own shares in the Bank at any given time.

- **Head Office**:  
  Located in **Metropolitan Toronto, Ontario**.

- **Authorized Capital Stock**:  
  - Total authorized capital: **$100 million**  
  - Par value per share: **$10**  
  - Shares are issued in the form of common shares with a fixed par value.

### **3. Provisional Directors**
- The **directors of IAC Limited** on the day the Act comes into force become the **provisional directors** of the Bank.
- This serves as a **declaration of names, addresses, and occupations** for purposes of **subsection 8(1) of the Bank Act**.
- **Eligibility for Provisional Directorship**:
  - A director of IAC Limited must own **at least 100 common shares** of IAC Limited in their **individual capacity** (not as trustee or on behalf of another).
  - This requirement **exempts** the Bank from the standard Bank Act rule that provisional directors must be subscribers to the bank‚Äôs stock (subsection 10(2) of the Bank Act).

> ‚úÖ *This allows for a smoother transition without requiring immediate shareholder subscriptions.*

### **4. Corporate Control and Subsidiary Definitions**
- A corporation is considered a **subsidiary** of another only if it is **controlled** by that parent corporation or by a chain of corporations ultimately controlled by the parent.
- **Control is defined as owning more than 50% of the issued shares with full voting rights** in all circumstances.
- This definition applies to all corporate relationships relevant to the Bank and IAC Limited.

---

### **5. Powers, Privileges, and Liabilities**
- The Bank is granted **all powers, privileges, immunities, and liabilities** under the **Bank Act**, **except** where specifically excluded by this Act or the Bank Act itself.
- This ensures the Bank operates under the full regulatory framework of the Bank Act as a chartered bank.

### **6. Exemptions and Special Provisions for the Transitional Period**
The Act includes a **ten-year transitional period** (or until amalgamation, whichever occurs first) during which certain provisions of the Bank Act are **modified or suspended** to facilitate the transition from IAC Limited to the new Bank.

#### Key Exemptions:
| Provision | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Public Notice of Stock Books & Meetings** | The provisional directors are **not required** to give public notice of opening stock books or shareholder meetings (under subsections 11(1) and 12(1) of the Bank Act). |
| **Validity of Meetings** | Shareholder meetings are valid even if **only proxy holders of IAC Limited** are present ‚Äî no public notice is required (under section 19 of the Bank Act). |

### **7. Shareholding and Directorship ‚Äì IAC Limited as the Holding Entity**
- **Until amalgamation**, all voting shares of the Bank are **held in the name or right of IAC Limited**.
- **IAC Limited shall ensure that a majority of the Bank‚Äôs directors are also directors of IAC Limited** ‚Äî a key structural alignment.
- This ensures continuity of governance and decision-making during the transition.

### **8. Prohibitions on Lending**
- The Bank **shall not lend money to IAC Limited**.
- The Bank **shall not lend to any subsidiary of IAC Limited** (but not a subsidiary of the Bank) **if** that subsidiary is carrying on business or owning assets that would **not be permissible for a chartered bank under the Bank Act** ‚Äî unless such lending is permitted under **Section 18** of the Bank Act.

> üîí This prevents conflicts of interest and ensures that all lending activities remain compliant with banking regulations.

### **9. Mandatory Amalgamation**
- IAC Limited and the Bank **must amalgamate within ten years** of the Act coming into force (or earlier if an amalgamation under subsection 10(1) takes effect).
- The amalgamation must be conducted **in accordance with the Bank Act**.
- After amalgamation, the **resulting entity shall be the continuing corporation** ‚Äî the Bank ‚Äî and will be subject to **all provisions of the Bank Act**.

> ‚úÖ This ensures full regulatory compliance and long-term stability.

### **10. Share Transfer and Continuity After Amalgamation**
- **Shareholders of IAC Limited** who were entitled to hold shares before amalgamation may **continue to hold shares in the new Bank** after amalgamation.
- **Section 110.1 of the Bank Act applies to IAC Limited** and its shares.
- A person who was allowed under section 110.1(2) to hold shares in contravention of paragraph (1)(a) may continue to hold shares issued to them after amalgamation **for the same period** as they would have been allowed under the original provision.

> ‚úÖ This protects existing shareholders from sudden loss of rights due to regulatory changes.

### **11. Amalgamation Without Shareholder Approval**
- If the amalgamation involves **only IAC Limited and the Bank or a wholly owned subsidiary**, the **Governor in Council** may, by order, **exempt the amalgamation from requiring shareholder approval**.
