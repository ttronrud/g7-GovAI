### **1. General Overview and Purpose**

The **Conflict of Interest Act** is a Canadian law designed to:

- Establish **clear rules** governing conflicts of interest and post-employment responsibilities for public office holders.
- Minimize conflicts between a public officialâ€™s **private interests** and their **public duties**.
- Ensure decisions are made in the **public interest**, free from undue influence or personal gain.
- Provide the **Conflict of Interest and Ethics Commissioner (CIEC)** with the authority to:
  - Determine appropriate measures to prevent conflicts.
  - Investigate and adjudicate potential violations.
- Encourage qualified individuals to enter public service by promoting transparency and trust.
- Promote **interchange between the public and private sectors**.

This Act applies to all individuals in positions of public authority and seeks to uphold integrity, accountability, and public confidence in government operations.

### **2. Key Definitions**

#### **Public Office Holder**
A person who holds or has held a position of public responsibility. Includes:
- Ministers of the Crown, Ministers of State, or Parliamentary Secretaries

- Ministerial advisers
- Governor in Council appointees (with specific exclusions)

- Any person or class designated under sections 62.1 or 62.2

> *Note:* Some roles are excluded (e.g., judges, military judges, RCMP Deputy Commissioners, members of the NSICP) due to their distinct independence.

#### **Family Members**

#### **Private Interest**
An interest that could interfere with public duties. **Excluded** if:
- The matter is of general application
- Affects a broad group of people
- Concerns remuneration or benefits of office

> *Private interest is defined narrowly to exclude general public matters.*
