**Detailed Summary of the *Expropriation Act***  
*(Cited as the Expropriation Act)*

---

### **Overview**

The *Expropriation Act* establishes the legal framework under which the **Government of Canada (the Crown)** may acquire private property or rights in land for **public works or public purposes**. It provides a structured, transparent, and accountable process for expropriation, including notice, public hearings, and compensation mechanisms. The Act applies to **all of Canada except Quebec**, where separate rules govern immovable real rights. It also includes specific provisions for Indigenous lands, railway companies, and special circumstances involving national security or urgent public needs.

This summary breaks down the key components, definitions, procedural steps, and exceptions within the Act.

---

## **1. Key Definitions**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|--------|
| **Court** | Refers to the **Federal Court** (a tribunal for judicial review). |
| **Crown** | Means *Her Majesty in right of Canada*. |
| **Expropriated** | Property or interest taken by the Crown under **Part I** of the Act. |
| **Expropriated interest or right** | Any estate, interest, or right that is lost or diminished due to the registration of a **notice of confirmation** under Part I. |
| **Land** | Includes: <br> - Mines, buildings, structures, fixtures (under Quebec civil law); <br> - Minerals (precious or base), whether above or below surface; <br> **Excludes** minerals above surface in Quebec. |
| **Minister** | <br> - For provisions other than Part II: Minister of Public Works and Government Services, or a delegated minister; <br> - For Part II: A Minister presiding over a department listed in **Schedule I to the Financial Administration Act**. |
| **Notice of Confirmation** | A formal notice registered under section 14 confirming an expropriation. |
| **Notice of Intention** | A preliminary notice published and sent to notify the public and interested parties of an intended expropriation. |
| **Register / Registrar** | Includes filing or deposit; the official body responsible for recording land titles and immovables. |
| **Interest in land** | Applies to land outside Quebec. |
| **Immovable real right** | Applies to land in Quebec (includes leasehold rights). |
| **Owner** | Repealed (2011); no longer used. |
| **Holder of a right** | A person with a right in Quebec land, including lessees. |

## **2. General Provisions and Scope**

### **Scope of Application**
- The Act applies to **all land in Canada outside Quebec**.
- In **Quebec**, the concept of *immovable real rights* governs expropriation, and the rules are adapted accordingly.

### **Public Purpose Requirement**
- The Crown may expropriate an interest in land **only if it is required for a public work or public purpose** (e.g., infrastructure, transportation, utilities).
- The Minister must determine that the interest is needed for such a purpose.

### **Expropriation Prohibitions (Consent Required by Governor in Council)**

Certain lands are protected from expropriation without **prior consent from the Governor in Council**:

| Land Type | Legal Basis |
|---------|------------|
| **Category IA-N land** | *Naskapi and the Cree-Naskapi Commission Act*, s. 2(1) |
| **Category IA land** | *Cree Nation of Eeyou Istchee Governance Agreement Act*, s. 2(2) |
| **Shíshálh lands** | *shíshálh Nation Self-Government Act*, s. 2(1) |
| **Settlement land** | *Yukon Surface Rights Board Act*, s. 2 |
| **Tetlit Gwich’in Yukon land** | *Comprehensive Land Claim Agreement* between Canada and Gwich’in Tribal Council (Annex B, Appendix C) |

> These protections recognize Indigenous rights and self-governance agreements.

## **3. Process for Expropriation (Step-by-Step)**

### **Step 1: Minister’s Request to Register a Notice of Intention**

- If the Minister believes an interest is required for a public purpose, they may request the **Attorney General of Canada** to register a **notice of intention**.
- The notice must include:
  - A **description of the land**;
  - The **nature of the interest or right** intended to be expropriated (including whether it is subject to existing rights);
  - The **public purpose** (e.g., railway, road, public utility);
  - A statement that the interest will be expropriated by the Crown.

> **Exception**: If the purpose relates to **national security or safety** and disclosure would be contrary to public interest, only a statement of such purpose is required.

### **Step 2: Registration and Reporting**

- The **Attorney General of Canada** registers the notice and a **plan of the land** with the **registrar** in the relevant jurisdiction.
- The Attorney General conducts **title investigations and searches** and provides the Minister with:
  - Names and latest known addresses of all persons with an estate, interest, or right in the land.

### **Step 3: Public Notification**

After registration, the Minister must:
- **Publish the notice** in at least one general-circulation publication in the area where the land is located (within **30 days**);
- **Send copies by registered mail** to persons named in the Attorney General’s report;
- **Publish the notice in the Canada Gazette** immediately after sending it by mail.

> The notice is deemed **given on the day it is published in the Canada Gazette**.

> **Omissions or errors** in the notice can be corrected, and the corrected version **relates back** to the original publication date.

> If the notice does not specify the nature of the interest or whether it is subject to existing rights:
- The interest is presumed to include **all interests** in the land;
- It is presumed **not subject** to any existing interest unless otherwise stated.

### **Step 4: Objection Period (30 Days)**

Any person who believes their interest is affected may:
- Serve a **written objection** on the Minister within **30 days** of the notice being given.
- Include:
  - Name and address;
  - Nature of the objection;
  - Grounds for the objection;
  - Nature of their interest in the matter.

> This objection must be filed **before** a public hearing is ordered.

---

## **4. Public Hearing (If Objections Are Filed)**

### **Trigger**
- If the Minister receives an objection, they must **order a public hearing** within 30 days of the objection period ending.

### **Appointment of Hearing Officer**
- The Minister requests the **Attorney General of Canada** to appoint a **hearing officer**.
- The officer must:
  - Not be employed in the **public service** (as defined in the *Public Service Superannuation Act*).
  - Be independent and impartial.

### **Hearing Officer’s Duties**
- Fix a **time and place** for the hearing within **7 days** of appointment (not later than 7 days).
- Publish notice of the hearing in a general publication and send it to:
  - Persons named in the Attorney General’s report;
  - All persons who filed objections.
- Provide an opportunity for each person who filed an objection to **be heard**.
- Conduct inspections of the land.
- Receive and consider **written representations** filed before or during the hearing.
- Prepare and submit a **written report** to the Minister within **30 days** of appointment.

> The hearing officer may **ignore frivolous, vexatious, or unfounded objections**.

### **Representation and Costs**
- Any person appearing at the hearing may be **represented by legal counsel**.
- The hearing officer may assess **reasonable costs** incurred by a person asserting an objection (up to a maximum set by a tariff approved by the Governor in Council).
- The **Minister of Finance** pays these costs from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund** upon certification.

> **Extension of Deadline**: The Attorney General may extend the 30-day reporting deadline by up to **30 days** at the request of the hearing officer.

> **Failure to Perform**: If the hearing officer fails to conduct the hearing or submit a report, the Minister must appoint a **replacement hearing officer** immediately.

---

## **5. Exceptions and Special Provisions**

### **Urgent Expropriation (No Public Hearing Required)**

- If the **Governor in Council** determines that:
  - Physical possession or use of the land is **urgently needed**;
  - Delaying a public hearing would **harm the public interest**;
- Then the Governor may **direct that no public hearing be held**.
- A statement explaining this decision must be included in the notice of intention.

> This applies only when **special circumstances** justify urgency (e.g., emergency infrastructure, disaster response).

> **Note**: This exception **does not apply** to land described in subsections 4(4) or (5) (i.e., protected Indigenous lands), **unless** the relevant Indigenous group (e.g., Yukon First Nation or Gwich’in Tribal Council) **waives the requirement** for a public hearing.
