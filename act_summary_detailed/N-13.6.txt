**Detailed Summary of the National Organ Donor Week Act**

**Overview:**  
The *National Organ Donor Week Act* is a legislative measure enacted by the Parliament of Canada to promote public awareness, education, and engagement regarding organ donation across the country. The Act establishes an annual national observance dedicated to encouraging Canadians to consider and pledge organ donation as a meaningful part of their personal and societal health choices.

---

**Key Provisions:**

1. **Purpose and Background (Whereas Clauses):**  
   - The Act begins with two "whereas" clauses that establish the rationale for its enactment:
     - It recognizes the critical importance of increasing public education and awareness about organ donation throughout Canada.
     - It emphasizes the value of encouraging all Canadians to formally pledge their organs for donation upon death, thereby addressing the ongoing shortage of available organs and improving transplant outcomes for patients in need.

2. **Official Designation of National Organ Donor Week:**  
   - The Act formally designates the **last full week of April** each year as **"National Organ Donor Week"**.
   - This week is to be recognized and observed across all provinces and territories of Canada.
   - The name "National Organ Donor Week" is to be used officially in government communications, public campaigns, educational materials, and media outreach.

3. **Scope and Application:**  
   - The designation applies **throughout Canada**, ensuring nationwide consistency and visibility.
   - The week is intended to serve as a platform for:
     - Public education campaigns about organ donation and transplantation.
     - Promotion of organ donor registration through official registries.
     - Engagement of communities, schools, healthcare providers, and media to raise awareness.
     - Encouraging individuals to register as organ donors, either through official government or provincial donor registries.

4. **Legislative Citation:**  
   - The Act may be cited as the *National Organ Donor Week Act*, providing a formal legal reference for future use in policy, administrative, or judicial contexts.

---

**Objectives and Impacts:**

- **Increase Public Awareness:** By establishing a dedicated national week, the Act aims to reduce misconceptions and stigma surrounding organ donation.
- **Encourage Donor Pledges:** The timing of the week—during a period when people may be reflecting on personal health and end-of-life decisions—provides a strategic opportunity to promote registration.
- **Strengthen National Unity:** The shared observance fosters a sense of national solidarity around a vital public health issue.
- **Support Health System Goals:** By addressing the organ shortage, the Act contributes to improving patient survival rates and quality of life through successful organ transplants.

---

**Implementation and Responsibilities:**  
While the Act itself does not prescribe specific activities or funding, it creates a framework for:
- Government agencies (such as Health Canada and provincial health ministries) to lead or support public education initiatives.
- Non-governmental organizations (e.g., Organ Donor Canada, Canadian Red Cross) to develop outreach programs during the week.
- Schools, hospitals, and community groups to participate in awareness events, such as donor pledge drives, informational sessions, and public forums.

---

**Conclusion:**  
The *National Organ Donor Week Act* is a symbolic yet impactful piece of legislation that advances public health by institutionalizing awareness and education about organ donation. By designating the last full week of April as a national observance, it provides a consistent, annual opportunity for Canadians to learn more about organ donation, reflect on their personal choices, and take action to become donors—ultimately supporting a more compassionate and effective healthcare system.

This Act reflects Canada’s commitment to addressing organ shortages through public education, civic engagement, and a culture of giving, where individuals contribute to saving and improving lives through informed, voluntary donation.
