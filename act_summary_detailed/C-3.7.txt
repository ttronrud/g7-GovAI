### **Detailed Summary of the Canada Emergency Response Benefit Act (CERB Act)**

The **Canada Emergency Response Benefit Act (CERB Act)** is a temporary legislative measure introduced by the Government of Canada in response to the public health emergency caused by the **coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19)**. The Act establishes the **Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB)**, a short-term income support program designed to assist individuals who lost income due to the pandemic, particularly those who were unable to work for reasons directly linked to the spread of the virus.

---

## **1. Purpose and Scope of the Act**

- The Act provides **financial support** to eligible workers who have lost income due to **reasons related to COVID-19**.
- It was intended to **mitigate economic hardship** during the initial wave of the pandemic, specifically between **March 15, 2020, and October 3, 2020**, with a strict deadline for applications.
- The benefit is **not a long-term welfare program**, but a **targeted, time-limited emergency measure**.

## **3. Application Process**

- **Eligible Period**: Applications can be submitted for **any four-week period** falling between **March 15, 2020, and October 3, 2020**.
- **Deadline for Applications**: **No applications will be accepted after December 2, 2020**.
- **Application Method**: Must be submitted in the **form and manner established by the Minister**.
- **Required Information**: Applicants must provide all information the Minister may reasonably require to verify eligibility.

---

## **4. Eligibility Criteria**

A worker is eligible for a CERB payment **if and only if**:

1. **Ceased working due to COVID-19**:
   - The worker **stops working for at least 14 consecutive days** within the four-week period for which they apply.
   - This cessation must be **due to reasons related to COVID-19** (e.g., lockdowns, business closures, illness, quarantine).

2. **No other income during the days of cessation**:
   - The worker must **not receive income** during the days they ceased working, except for:
     - Income from **employment or self-employment**;
     - **Employment Insurance (EI) benefits** (including emergency response benefits under section 153.7 of the EI Act);
     - **Provincial benefits** for pregnancy or childcare;
     - Any other income **prescribed by regulation**.

## **5. Payment Amount and Duration**

- **Weekly Payment Amount**:
  - Fixed by **regulation**.
  - The Minister may, with the **consent of the Minister of Finance**, set the weekly amount.
  - Regulations may **distinguish between different classes of workers** (e.g., self-employed vs. employed).

- **Maximum Duration**:
  - A worker can receive CERB payments for a **maximum of 16 weeks**.
  - This can be adjusted by regulation (e.g., if a higher or lower number is deemed appropriate).
  - The 16-week limit applies **per individual**, not per week.

## **7. Legal Protections for CERB Payments**

To protect recipients and ensure the benefit is not subject to general debt collection mechanisms:

- **Not subject to bankruptcy or insolvency laws**.
- **Cannot be assigned, charged, attached, or used as security**.
- **Cannot be deducted, offset, or garnished** under any other federal or provincial law, **except under this Act**.
- **Not garnishable** under the *Family Orders and Agreements Enforcement Assistance Act*.

> This means CERB payments are **protected from creditors** and cannot be seized for other debts.

---

## **8. Overpayments and Repayment Obligations**

- If the Minister determines that a person:
  - Received a CERB payment they were **not entitled to**, or
  - Received **more than their entitled amount**,

- The overpayment is treated as a **debt due to Her Majesty in right of Canada**.
- The Minister may **certify the amount of the debt**, and such certification has the **same legal effect as a judgment** in the **Federal Court**, including all related registration costs.

> This allows the government to pursue repayment efficiently and legally.

---

## **9. Limitation Period for Recovery of Overpayments**

- **General Rule**: No legal action can be taken to recover money owed under this Act **after six years** from the date the money became due and payable.
- **Exception**: If a person **acknowledges liability** (in any form), the limitation period is **reset**:
  - Acknowledgment includes:
    - A promise to pay;
    - An admission of debt (even without a promise);
    - A partial payment;
    - An acknowledgment during bankruptcy or debt proceedings.
  - Once acknowledged, the **time before acknowledgment does not count** in the six-year period.
  - If acknowledgment occurs **after the six-year period**, recovery may still be pursued **within six years of the acknowledgment**.

> âš ï¸ **Suspended Periods**: If it is prohibited to pursue legal action against a person (e.g., due to bankruptcy), the limitation period **suspends** during that time.

---

## **10. Interest and Enforcement**

- **No interest** is payable on any overpayment or debt arising under this Act.
- The government **cannot recover** the debt from income or benefits **under section 122.61 of the Income Tax Act** (i.e., tax credits or refunds).

---

## **11. Special Provisions for Employment Insurance Emergency Benefits**

- If a worker received an **Employment Insurance Emergency Response Benefit (EIERB)** during the four-week period:
  - The CERB payment is **not considered ineligible** **if** the **Canada Employment Insurance Commission (CEIC)** notifies the Minister that the EIERB should not disqualify the worker.
  - In such cases, the worker is **deemed eligible** for CERB, despite receiving EI benefits.

> This creates a **protective mechanism** to avoid double-dipping or overpayment, while ensuring support reaches those who genuinely lost income.

---

## **12. Special Formula for Repayment in Cases of Non-Eligibility Due to Benefits**

If a worker received **benefits, allowances, or money** (e.g., EI, provincial childcare) during the four-week period, and this is the **only reason** they were ineligible for CERB:

- The amount the worker must repay is calculated using the formula:

> Example: If a worker received 3 weeks of EI benefits during a 4-week period, and the weekly CERB was $500, they would repay $1,500.
