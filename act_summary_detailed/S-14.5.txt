### **Detailed Summary of the *Special Economic Measures Act***  
**(Canada)**

---

#### **1. Introduction and Citation**
The *Special Economic Measures Act* (SEMA) is a Canadian legislative instrument enacted by Her Majesty, with the advice and consent of both the House of Commons and the Senate. It may be cited as the **Special Economic Measures Act**.

This Act provides the legal framework for the Government of Canada to impose **economic sanctions** on foreign states, individuals, or entities in response to specific international or domestic crises—particularly those involving **grave breaches of international peace and security**, **gross human rights violations**, or **acts of significant corruption**.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

The Act establishes precise definitions of key terms used throughout:

- **Canadian**:  
  A citizen of Canada or a body corporate (e.g., corporation, trust, partnership) incorporated or continuing under Canadian or provincial law.

- **Entity**:  
  Broadly defined to include any body corporate, trust, partnership, fund, unincorporated association, or foreign state.

- **Foreign State**:  
  Any country other than Canada, including:
  - Political subdivisions (e.g., provinces, municipalities)
  - Government bodies and departments
  - Agencies and their subdivisions

- **National (of a foreign state)**:  
  An individual who holds citizenship of that foreign state, or a body corporate incorporated under its laws.

- **Person**:  
  Either an individual or an entity.

- **Property**:  
  All types of tangible or intangible assets, including:
  - Real and personal property
  - Money, funds, currency
  - Digital assets and virtual currency

- **Technical Data**:  
  Includes blueprints, software, engineering designs, formulas, manuals, and any technical know-how.

> **Control Principle**:  
> If a person controls an entity (directly or indirectly), any property owned, held, or controlled by that entity is deemed to be owned by the controlling person.  
> A person controls an entity if they:
> - Hold 50% or more of shares/voting rights;  
> - Can change the board composition or powers;  
> - It is reasonable to conclude they can direct the entity’s activities (based on all circumstances).

---

#### **3. Purpose of the Act**

The primary purpose of SEMA is to enable the Government of Canada to take **targeted economic measures** against foreign states or individuals when one of the following conditions occurs:

1. An **international organization of states** (e.g., UN, NATO, G7) to which Canada belongs calls on its members to impose economic sanctions on a foreign state.  
2. A **grave breach of international peace and security** has occurred, leading to or likely to result in a serious international crisis.  
3. **Gross and systematic human rights violations** have been committed in a foreign state.  
4. A **national of a foreign state** (a public official or associate thereof) is responsible for or complicit in **acts of significant corruption**, such as:
   - Bribery
   - Misappropriation of public/private assets
   - Transfer of proceeds to foreign states
   - Corruption related to government contracts or natural resource extraction  
   → These acts are deemed "significant" based on impact, scale, influence, and government complicity.

---

#### **4. Powers of the Governor in Council**

The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet, acting on ministerial advice) may:

- Make **orders or regulations** restricting or prohibiting certain activities involving:
  - A foreign state
  - Nationals of that state (not ordinarily residing in Canada)
  - Non-Canadian persons outside Canada

- **Seize or restrain property** in Canada that is:
  - Owned, held, or controlled directly or indirectly by:
    - A foreign state, or
    - A person identified in an order or regulation

> These orders can apply to a wide range of activities, both inside and outside Canada.

---

#### **5. Scope of Restricted Activities**

Under the Act, the Governor in Council may restrict or prohibit the following activities involving a foreign state:

| Activity | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Dealing in property** | Any transaction (buying, selling, holding) in property (real or personal) owned by the foreign state, its nationals, or non-Canadians. |
| **Exportation/supply of goods** | Exporting, selling, supplying, or shipping goods to the foreign state, its nationals, or non-Canadians. |
| **Transfer of technical data** | Sharing technical data (e.g., software, designs, blueprints) with the foreign state or its nationals. |
| **Importation of goods** | Importing goods exported from the foreign state after a specified date. |
| **Financial services** | Providing financial services, loans, or other services to or for the benefit of the foreign state or its nationals. |
| **Transfer of property** | Transferring non-good property (e.g., funds, real estate) to the foreign state or its nationals. |
| **Shipping and aircraft operations** | Docking in the foreign state by Canadian-registered ships; landing in Canada by foreign-registered aircraft or ships; passage through Canada by foreign vessels or aircraft. |

> These restrictions apply whether the activity occurs in Canada or abroad.

---

#### **6. Exclusions and Permits**

- **Exclusions**:  
  Orders or regulations may **exclude** specific persons, property, goods, services, ships, or aircraft from being subject to the restrictions.

- **Permit System**:  
  The Minister (Minister of Foreign Affairs) may issue:
  - **Specific permits**: For individual transactions or activities restricted under the Act.
  - **General permits**: Allowing broad classes of activities to proceed.

> Permits are subject to **terms and conditions** deemed appropriate by the Minister and must be consistent with the Act and any related orders.

> The Minister may **amend, suspend, revoke, or reinstate** permits at any time.

---

#### **7. Seizure and Forfeiture of Property**

- When property is seized under an order:
  - **Costs incurred** by the Government (e.g., legal, enforcement) are the **liability of the owner** and become a **debt due to Her Majesty in right of Canada**, recoverable in court.

- A person affected by a seizure may **apply in writing** to the Minister to request that the order be lifted.

> The Minister must assess whether there are **reasonable grounds** to recommend the Governor in Council lift the order.

- **Rights of Secured and Unsecured Parties**:
  - All rights and interests in the seized property (including mortgages, liens) are **protected** and ranked as they would have been had no order been made—**unless**:
    - The person is the foreign state or a person named in the order; or  
    - The property is forfeited to the Crown.

---

#### **8. Forfeiture Process**

- The **Minister** may apply to a **judge of a superior court** in the province where the property is located to order its **forfeiture to Her Majesty in right of Canada** if:
  - The property is described in a seizure order; and  
  - It is owned or directly/indirectly controlled by the person named in the order.

- Before making the order, the court must:
  - Provide **notice** to any person who may have a legitimate interest in the property.
  - Allow a **30-day window** for any such person to apply to the court asserting their interest.

- After forfeiture, any person (except those named in the order) who claims an interest may:
  - Apply within **30 days** to the judge for a declaration that their interest is not affected.
  - Request that the Minister pay them the **value of their interest**.

> **Important Exception**:  
> If a corporation's **entire shareholding** is forfeited, the corporation is **not deemed a Crown corporation** under the *Financial Administration Act*.

---

#### **9. Use of Forfeited Proceeds**

- The Minister (with approval from the Governor in Council) may **disburse funds** from the **Proceeds Account** (as defined in the *Seized Property Management Act*) for the following purposes:
  1. **Reconstruction** of a foreign state affected by a grave breach of peace or security.
  2. **Restoration of international peace and security**.
  3. **Compensation** to victims of:
     - Grave breaches of peace and security
     - Gross and systematic human rights violations
     - Acts of significant corruption

> These funds are limited to the **net proceeds** from the disposal of forfeited property.

---

#### **10. Administration and Enforcement**

- The **Minister of Foreign Affairs** is primarily responsible for administering and enforcing the Act.

- The Governor in Council may designate **one or more Ministers** to take on specific enforcement responsibilities.

- A designated Minister may also be responsible for:
  - Receiving and assessing **claims for compensation** from individuals harmed by the Act.

---

#### **11. Inter-Ministerial Collaboration**

The following ministers may assist the Minister in enforcing the Act and share information:
