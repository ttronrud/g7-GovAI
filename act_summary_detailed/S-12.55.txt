## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act, 2006**

### üîç **Overview and Purpose**
The *Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act, 2006* (SLPECA) was enacted by the Government of Canada to impose a **tariff-like export charge** on softwood lumber products exported to the **United States**. The primary objective was to:

- **Protect the Canadian softwood lumber industry** from unfair competition due to lower-priced U.S. lumber.
- **Generate revenue** to offset potential losses from exports that could otherwise flood the U.S. market.
- **Encourage sustainable and fair trade practices** by adjusting charges based on market conditions and export volumes.

The Act was introduced in response to concerns that U.S. softwood lumber producers were undercutting Canadian prices, especially through the use of lower-cost, less-regulated products. It applied specifically to **softwood lumber products** exported to the U.S. and introduced a **tiered, market-responsive charge system**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important Note**: The Act was **phased out** in 2015, with most of its provisions ceasing to apply on **October 12, 2015**, as noted in *SOR/2016-155*. Some provisions were later replaced or modified under subsequent trade agreements (e.g., the U.S.-Canada Free Trade Agreement and related trade policy shifts).

### 3. **Scope and Application**

#### ‚úÖ **Applies to**:
- Exports of **softwood lumber products** to the **United States** after **October 11, 2006**.
- Applies to **all exports** except those explicitly excluded.

#### ‚ùå **Exclusions**:
The following exports are **exempt** from the charge:
1. Exports from:
   - **Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, Newfoundland and Labrador**
   - **Yukon, Northwest Territories, Nunavut**
2. Exports by a person referred to in **section 16** (likely referring to certain domestic or government entities).

> üìç **Deemed Export Origin**:
> - A product is deemed exported from **Nova Scotia, NB, PEI, NL** if its **first primary processing** occurred in one of those provinces using **softwood sawlogs from the same province or Maine**.
> - A product is deemed exported from **Yukon, NWT, Nunavut** if first primary processing occurred in one of those territories using local sawlogs.

### 4. **Charge Calculation and Rates**

#### A. **Base Rate Determination (Section 12)**

The **rate of charge** depends on:
- Whether an **export allocation** is required (under section 6.3(3)(b) of the *Export and Import Permits Act*).
- The **reference price** (based on U.S. market data).

##### üîπ **Reference Price**:
- Defined as the **most recent four-week average** of the **Framing Lumber Composite Price (FLCP)** published by **Random Lengths Publications Incorporated**.
- Must be available at least **21 days before the start of the month**.
- If Random Lengths changes methodology or stops publishing, a **prescribed formula** applies.
- Reference price is **rounded to the nearest dollar**, with ties going to the **higher multiple**.
