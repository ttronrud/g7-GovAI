**Detailed Summary of the *Historic Sites and Monuments Act***

---

### **Overview of the Act**

The *Historic Sites and Monuments Act* establishes a comprehensive framework for the identification, commemoration, preservation, and management of places of national historic significance in Canada. The Act is designed to ensure that Canada’s cultural heritage—particularly sites, buildings, and structures of national importance—is recognized, protected, and maintained for future generations.

The Act is officially cited as the **Historic Sites and Monuments Act** and is administered primarily through the **Historic Sites and Monuments Board of Canada (the "Board")**, which advises the Minister and carries out key responsibilities related to heritage conservation.

---

### **Key Definitions**

- **Board**:  
  Refers to the *Historic Sites and Monuments Board of Canada*, established under Section 4. This body is responsible for advising the Minister on all matters related to historic places and museums.

- **Historic Place**:  
  Defined broadly as any site, building, or other place of **national historic interest or significance**. This includes:
  - Structures of historical, architectural, or cultural value.
  - Places recognized for their age, design, or role in Canadian history.
  - Any location that contributes to Canada’s national heritage.

- **Minister**:  
  Refers to the **Minister responsible for the Parks Canada Agency**, who holds the primary authority to act under the Act and is accountable to the Governor in Council.

---

### **Powers and Responsibilities of the Minister**

The Minister has several key powers under the Act:

1. **Marking and Commemorating Historic Places**  
   - Can place **plaques, signs, or other markers** on historic places to commemorate their significance.
   - May also use **other suitable methods** (e.g., digital media, interpretive signage) to highlight heritage value.

2. **Entering into Agreements**  
   - Can enter into agreements with individuals, organizations, or institutions to:
     - Mark or commemorate historic places.
     - Ensure the **care, preservation, and maintenance** of such places.

3. **Establishing Historic Museums**  
   - With **Governor in Council approval**, the Minister may establish **historic museums** to preserve and exhibit heritage materials.

4. **Acquiring Historic Places or Lands**  
   - With **Treasury Board approval**, the Minister may:
     - Purchase, lease, or otherwise acquire:
       - Historic places.
       - Lands associated with historic museums.
       - Any interest in such properties.
   - These acquisitions are made on behalf of **Her Majesty in right of Canada**.

5. **Administration and Maintenance**  
   - The Minister is responsible for:
     - Administering and maintaining any historic places acquired under the Act.
     - Managing and preserving any historic museums established under the Act.

---

### **Establishment of the Historic Sites and Monuments Board of Canada**

The Board is a key advisory and decision-making body created under the Act.

#### **Composition of the Board (16 Members)**

The Board consists of the following members:

1. **The Librarian and Archivist of Canada**  
   - Provides access to archival and historical records, ensuring that heritage decisions are informed by documented evidence.

2. **An Officer of the Canadian Museum of History**  
   - Designated by the **Minister responsible for the Canadian Museum of History**, who is appointed by the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada under the *Museums Act*.

3. **An Officer of Parks Canada Agency**  
   - Designated by the **Minister of Parks Canada**.

4. **One Representative per Province or Territory**  
   - Each province and territory is represented by one individual.
   - **Eligibility Requirement**: A person must **reside in the province or territory** they represent to be eligible for appointment.
   - Appointments are made by the **Governor in Council**.

#### **Term of Office**

- Members appointed by the Governor in Council serve at the **discretion of the Governor in Council**.
- Maximum term: **Five years**.
- Members may be **reappointed** after retirement.

#### **Chairmanship**

- The Governor in Council designates **one member** of the Board as **Chairman**.

#### **Meetings**

- The Board must meet **at least once per calendar year**, at the call of the Chairman.
- The **time and place** of meetings require **Ministerial approval**.
- Additional meetings may be called at the **Minister’s request**.

#### **Quorum**

- A **quorum** of **seven members** is required for the Board to conduct business.

---

### **Support Staff and Administrative Arrangements**

- The **Minister** may designate an officer or employee of Parks Canada as the **Secretary of the Board**.
  - The Secretary manages the administrative and operational functions of the Board.
- The Minister may also provide **other employees or assistants** from Parks Canada to support the Board’s work, as needed.

---

### **Board Responsibilities and Advisory Role**

The Board has a core mandate to:

- Receive and consider recommendations on:
  - The **marking or commemoration** of historic places.
  - The **establishment of historic museums**.
  - The **administration, preservation, and maintenance** of historic places and museums.
- Advise the Minister in carrying out his powers under the Act.

---

### **Remuneration and Expenses**

#### **For Members Appointed by the Governor in Council**

- **Daily Remuneration**:  
  Paid for each day a member is **necessarily absent** from their ordinary residence to attend meetings or perform Board duties. Amount is set by the Governor in Council.

- **Travel and Living Expenses**:  
  Covered for **actual, necessary expenses** incurred in connection with Board business.

#### **For Other Board Members (e.g., provincial/territorial representatives)**

- Entitled to **actual travel and living expenses** necessary for Board duties.

#### **Clerical and Stenographic Support**

- **Chairman**: $75 per year.
- **Other members appointed by the Governor in Council**: $30 per year.

> These allowances ensure that members can participate effectively in Board activities without undue financial burden.

---

### **Regulations and Reporting Requirements**

- The **Governor in Council** may make **regulations** to implement and interpret the Act’s provisions.

- **Annual Reporting**:
  - The Board must submit a **report on its proceedings** to the Minister **as soon as practicable after each calendar year**.
  - The report must be in a form prescribed by the Minister.

- **Additional Reports**:
  - The Board may be required to provide **further statements or reports** at any time and in any manner as the Minister may direct.

---

### **Key Principles and Objectives**

1. **National Heritage Recognition**  
   - Ensures that places of national significance are formally recognized and preserved.

2. **Public Engagement and Education**  
   - Through plaques, signs, and museums, the Act promotes public awareness and appreciation of Canada’s history.

3. **Collaborative Governance**  
   - Balances federal oversight with regional representation (provincial/territorial members), ensuring inclusivity and local input.

4. **Preservation and Maintenance**  
   - Provides a clear pathway for the acquisition and long-term care of historic properties.

5. **Transparency and Accountability**  
   - Requires regular reporting and oversight by the Minister, ensuring accountability to government and the public.

---

### **Significance and Impact**

The *Historic Sites and Monuments Act* is foundational to Canada’s national heritage policy. It:

- Establishes a formal, structured, and accountable process for identifying and protecting historically significant places.
- Promotes national unity by recognizing heritage across regions and communities.
- Supports the work of Parks Canada, museums, and other institutions in preserving Canada’s cultural identity.
- Provides a legal basis for both commemoration and preservation, ensuring that historical memory is not lost to time or development.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Historic Sites and Monuments Act* is a vital piece of legislation that enables Canada to identify, honor, and protect its most important historical sites and places. By creating a balanced, representative, and empowered Board, and by empowering the Minister with clear tools for action, the Act ensures that Canada’s national heritage is actively managed, preserved, and shared with the public. It reflects a commitment to cultural continuity, historical awareness, and democratic participation in heritage stewardship.

--- 

**In short**:  
This Act creates a national framework for recognizing, commemorating, and preserving places of national historical significance through a well-structured Board, clear ministerial powers, and ongoing accountability.
