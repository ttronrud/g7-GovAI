**Detailed Summary of the *Economic Action Plan 2013 Act, No. 1***

---

### **Overview**

The *Economic Action Plan 2013 Act, No. 1* is a legislative instrument enacted by Her Majesty in Canada, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons, on April 1, 2013. It serves as a foundational piece of legislation under the broader **Economic Action Plan 2013**, a government initiative aimed at stimulating economic growth, supporting key sectors, and improving public services through targeted investments and policy reforms.

The Act is structured into several divisions, each addressing distinct policy areas such as fiscal funding for public institutions, reorganization of state-owned enterprises, and amendments to existing statutes to support economic objectives. Key provisions include direct financial allocations to Canadian organizations, structural changes to government entities, and transitional mechanisms for implementation.

### **3. Amending the Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development Act**

This section revises and modernizes references to the **Department of Foreign Affairs and International Trade (DFATI)**, which was restructured into the **Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development (DFATD)** in 2013.

#### Key Amendments:
- **"Department of Foreign Affairs and International Trade"** is replaced with **"Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development"** in all Acts of Parliament (except where explicitly referenced).
- **"Minister for International Cooperation"** is replaced with **"Minister for International Development"**.
- **"Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA)"** is replaced with **"Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development"**.

### **4. Definitions and Legal Framework (Division 5)**

The Act introduces or clarifies key definitions used throughout the legislation:

- **Assets**: Includes securities and incorporeal property (e.g., intellectual property, rights) held by or on behalf of an entity.
- **Security**: Includes shares, debt obligations, and conversion/exchange rights (e.g., options to buy shares).
- **Corporation, Share, Wholly-Owned Subsidiary**: Defined consistently with the *Financial Administration Act* (section 83(1)).
- **Inconsistency Resolution**: In case of conflict between this Act and the *Canada Business Corporations Act*, this Act **prevails**.
- **Competition Act Exception**: The *Competition Act* does **not apply** to transactions under this Act, allowing for government-led reorganizations without antitrust scrutiny.

### **5. Reorganization and Divestiture of Ridley Terminals Inc. (Key Policy Initiative)**

This is the **most significant and complex section** of the Act, outlining a government-led reorganization of **Ridley Terminals Inc.**, a major Canadian port and terminal operator.

#### **Purpose**:
To allow the Government of Canada to:
- Obtain **best value** for the business,
- Ensure long-term, **sustainable operations**,
- Promote **open access** to terminal services,
- Improve efficiency and competitiveness.

#### **Key Powers Granted to the Minister** (with Governor in Council approval):

1. **Sell or dispose of securities** of Ridley Terminals Inc.
2. **Modify the companyâ€™s objects, purposes, or business restrictions** (e.g., change its charter or activities).
3. **Procure amalgamation** (merger) of the company.
4. **Procure dissolution** (liquidation or closure).

#### **Expanded Powers for New Entities**:
The Minister may also:
- Create a **new corporation or entity** whose securities will be held by Her Majesty in right of Canada (on trust), then sell or dispose of them.
- Acquire securities of such a new entity and then dispose of them.

> This mechanism allows the government to **separate the business from its current ownership**, potentially privatize or restructure it under more favorable terms.

#### **Powers for the New Entity (or Subsidiary)**:
After the creation of a new corporation or entity (whether through incorporation, formation, or acquisition), the entity may:
- Sell or dispose of its **assets or liabilities**,
- Issue and sell securities,
- Reorganize its capital structure,
- Acquire assets from other corporations or entities,
- Amend its charter or business restrictions,
- Merge or dissolve itself,
- Take any action necessary to implement a reorganization.

> These powers are **extensive** and designed to allow full operational flexibility during transition.

#### **Governor in Council Directives**:
- The Governor in Council may **direct** Ridley Terminals or its successor entity to take specific actions (e.g., sell assets, merge).

  - Any of its wholly-owned subsidiaries have had their securities sold or disposed of.
- **Compliance is mandatory** for directors or officers of the entity.

- After implementing a directive, the entity must **notify the Minister** within a reasonable timeframe.
