### **Detailed Summary of the Investment Canada Act**

---

#### **Overview**

The **Investment Canada Act (ICA)** is a federal law enacted to balance the benefits of foreign investment in Canada with the need to protect **national security** and **public interest**. It establishes a regulatory framework for reviewing significant investments by **non-Canadians** in Canadian businesses, ensuring that such investments contribute to **economic growth, employment, and innovation**, while also safeguarding Canada‚Äôs strategic and sensitive sectors.

The Act is designed to **encourage foreign investment** while providing a transparent, predictable, and fair process to assess whether a proposed investment could pose a threat to national security, public order, or economic stability.

---

## **Key Purposes of the Act**

1. **To promote investment and economic growth**  
   By creating a clear and efficient review process, the Act encourages foreign investment in Canada, which brings capital, technology, and expertise that can enhance productivity, innovation, and job creation.

2. **To protect national security**  
   The Act allows for the review of investments that may have **national security implications**, such as those involving critical infrastructure, defense, energy, telecommunications, or technologies with dual-use (civil and military applications).

3. **To ensure transparency and fairness**  
   The process is structured to provide non-Canadians with clear notice, opportunities to respond, and a fair review mechanism, promoting confidence in the investment climate.

## **Key Definitions (Crucial to Understanding the Act)**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Canada** | Includes the **exclusive economic zone (EEZ)** and **continental shelf** of Canada. This expands the geographic scope of the Act beyond land borders. |
| **Canadian** | Includes: <br> - Canadian citizens or permanent residents (residing in Canada for ‚â§1 year after eligibility for citizenship); <br> - Canadian government or agencies; <br> - Entities that are **Canadian-controlled** (as defined under section 26), with no prior determination or declaration under certain subsections indicating foreign control. |
| **Canadian business** | A business that: <br> - Has a place of business in Canada; <br> - Employs individuals in Canada; <br> - Uses assets in Canada. |
| **Non-Canadian** | Any individual, government, or entity that is **not Canadian**. |
| **Own (beneficial ownership)** | Refers to **actual beneficial ownership**, not just legal title. |
| **Voting interest** | A stake that gives the holder rights to vote (e.g., shares in a corporation), receive profits, or share in assets upon dissolution. |
| **Voting share** | A share with voting rights and profit-sharing rights. |
| **Joint venture** | An association of two or more parties where ownership interests are collectively held and not structured as a corporation, partnership, or trust. |
| **Voting group** | Two or more individuals/entities acting together in the exercise of voting rights (e.g., through contracts, control, or shared interests). |
| **State-owned enterprise (SOE)** | A foreign government or entity controlled or influenced by a foreign government. |
| **New Canadian business** | A business not previously operated by the non-Canadian, and either unrelated to other operations or related to Canada‚Äôs cultural heritage or national identity. |

## **Applicability and Exemptions**

The Act **does not apply** to certain types of investments that are considered routine or already regulated by other laws. These include:

### **Exempt Transactions (Where the Act Does Not Apply)**

1. **Acquisitions in the ordinary course of trading in securities**  
   - E.g., buying shares as a trader or dealer in financial markets.

2. **Venture capital investments**  
   - Provided the investment is in the ordinary course of business and complies with Ministerial conditions.

3. **Acquisitions linked to loan security (not for investment purposes)**  
   - If the acquisition is subject to approval under the **Bank Act**, **Insurance Companies Act**, or **Cooperative Credit Associations Act**.

4. **Financing-related acquisitions**  
   - Acquisitions made to facilitate financing, with a requirement to **divest control within 2 years** (or longer period approved by the Minister).

5. **Corporate reorganizations**  
   - Acquisitions resulting from mergers, amalgamations, or consolidations where **control remains unchanged**.

6. **Government or Crown corporation activities**  
   - Activities carried out by **agents of Her Majesty in right of Canada**, provinces, or **Crown corporations** under the Financial Administration Act.

7. **Tax-exempt businesses**  
   - Businesses whose income is exempt under **paragraph 149(1)(d)** of the Income Tax Act.

8. **Transactions under the Bank Act (Part XII.01)**  
   - Certain banking or financial transactions are exempt.

9. **Involuntary acquisitions**  
   - Acquisitions due to estate devolution or operation of law.

10. **Insurance companies and foreign SOEs**  
   - Specific conditions apply for insurance companies or foreign SOEs acquiring control, including:
     - Gross investment revenue must be included in taxable income.
     - Voting interests or assets must be held in **trust** under Part XIII of the Insurance Companies Act.

11. **Agricultural businesses**  
   - Acquisitions where revenue comes from farming on real property acquired in the same transaction.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important Note**: If any exemption condition is **not met**, the exemption **does not apply**, and the transaction is subject to review under the Act.

## **Notification and Review Process**

### **1. Investments Subject to Notification (Mandatory Notice)**

The following investments require **advance notice** to the Director of Investments:

- **Establishing a new Canadian business** (i.e., a business not previously operated by the non-Canadian).
- **Acquiring control** of a Canadian business in any manner listed in **subsection 28(1)** ‚Äî **unless** the investment is already reviewable under **section 14** (national security review).

> ‚úÖ **Notice must be given**:
> - Before implementation, or
> - Within **30 days** after implementation.

> üîç **Content of Notice**: Must include information prescribed by regulation (e.g., investment details, financials, business operations, etc.).

---

### **2. Receipt and Determination of Review Status**

When a notice is submitted:

- **If complete or sufficient**:  
  The Director sends a **receipt** certifying:
  - The date the notice was received, or
  - The date required information was received.

  The receipt also advises the non-Canadian whether:
  - The investment is **not reviewable under Part IV**, or
  - A **review under section 15** will be initiated within **45 days** (or prescribed period).

> ‚úÖ **No review if**:  
> - The information provided is accurate, and  
> - No notice for review is issued within the 45-day window.

- **If incomplete**:  
  The Director sends a **notice of deficiency**, specifying missing information and requesting its completion.

---

### **3. Investments Subject to Review (Reviewable Under Part IV)**

The following investments are **reviewable** under the Act:

| Type of Investment | Conditions for Review |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Acquisition of control** via: <br> - (a) direct purchase of voting interests <br> - (b) indirect control via ownership <br> - (c) control through a joint venture | Review applies if the **value of assets acquired** is **$5 million or more**. |
| **Acquisition via a joint venture** (subparagraph 28(1)(d)(i)) | Same threshold: **$5 million**. |
| **Acquisition via a joint venture** (subparagraph 28(1)(d)(ii)) | Review applies **only if**: <br> - The **total value of assets** (Canadian business + other entities acquired) exceeds **50% of the total value of all assets acquired** in the transaction. <br> - **Value threshold**: $5 million. |
| **Acquisition via a joint venture** (subparagraph 28(1)(d)(ii)) where **circumstances do not apply** (i.e., asset threshold not met) | Review applies if **total asset value** exceeds **$50 million**. |

> üîç **Asset value calculation**: Determined in a manner prescribed by regulation (e.g., fair market value, adjusted for specific factors).

> üìå **Note on thresholds**:
> - **$5 million** is the threshold for most acquisitions.
> - **$50 million** applies to joint ventures where the acquisition represents a large share of the total transaction value.

---

### **4. Special Provisions for WTO Investors**

Despite the standard thresholds, **a WTO investor** (a foreign investor participating in the World Trade Organization) may have a **reviewable investment** only if:

- The **enterprise value** (calculated per regulation) of the assets involved is **equal to or greater than** a specified amount.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è This provision is **repealed** as of 2017 (per section 192 of *c. 20*), meaning **no longer applies**.

> ‚ùå **Repealed**: The clause regarding WTO investors is **no longer in force**.
