**Detailed Summary of the *Invest in Canada Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Invest in Canada Act* establishes a new federal entity — the **Invest in Canada Hub (IICH)** — with the primary objective of promoting and facilitating **foreign direct investment (FDI)** in Canada. The Act provides a comprehensive legal framework for the creation, governance, operational powers, and accountability of this agency, enabling it to act independently and effectively in supporting Canada’s economic growth, innovation, and global competitiveness.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Name and Citation**
- The Act may be cited as the **Invest in Canada Act**.
- It creates a dedicated corporate body to promote Canada as a premier destination for foreign investment.

---

### **2. Definitions**
Key terms are defined:
- **Invest in Canada Hub (IICH):** A corporation established under section 4(1) to promote FDI and coordinate investment-related efforts.
- **Minister:** A member of the Queen’s Privy Council for Canada designated under section 3. The Governor in Council may designate a Minister by order.

---

### **3. Establishment of the Invest in Canada Hub**
- A **corporation** is formally established to operate as the **Invest in Canada Hub**.
- The IICH is **an agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada** for all purposes, meaning it acts on behalf of the Crown.
- Its **head office** is located in Canada and is designated by the Minister.
- The IICH has a **dual mandate**:
  1. To **promote foreign direct investment in Canada** and attract and facilitate such investment.
  2. To **coordinate government, private sector, and stakeholder efforts** related to FDI.

---

### **4. Mandate and Core Functions**
The IICH is authorized to carry out the following functions in fulfilling its mandate:

| Function | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Develop and implement a national strategy** | Formulate a comprehensive, long-term plan to attract FDI to Canada. |
| **Build partnerships** | Establish collaborations with federal departments, provincial/territorial governments, private sector actors, and other stakeholders to maximize the effectiveness of investment programs and services. |
| **Plan and manage events** | Organize conferences, trade missions, investment fairs, and outreach programs to showcase Canada as an attractive investment destination. |
| **Collect and disseminate information** | Gather and share data with foreign investors to support informed investment decisions. |
| **Provide coordinated services** | Deliver consistent, efficient, and responsive support to foreign investors throughout the investment lifecycle (pre-investment, due diligence, post-investment). |

---

### **5. Powers and Authorities**
The IICH is granted broad powers to operate effectively:

- **Enter into contracts, agreements, or memoranda of understanding**:
  - With federal departments, other governments, or private entities — either **in the name of Her Majesty in right of Canada** or **in its own name**.
  - This includes entering into agreements with the Crown as if it were not an agent, giving it significant autonomy.

- **Acquire, hold, manage, lease, or dispose of property**:
  - The IICH may acquire personal property (movables), including office space, equipment, or technology, and manage these assets.

- **Perform any other actions necessary or incidental** to achieving its mandate.

> **Note on Title to Property**: Property acquired may be held in the name of **Her Majesty** or in the name of the **IICH**, providing flexibility in ownership and accountability.

---

### **6. Exemptions from Federal Public Administration Rules**
Despite being an agent of the Crown, the IICH is **exempt from certain regulations** under the *Financial Administration Act (FAA)* and the *Department of Public Works and Government Services Act*:

- **Exemption from Treasury Board regulations** (under FAA) **except**:
  - For **financial management** matters (e.g., budgeting, accounting, financial reporting).
  - For **other matters** (policies on contracts, communications, travel, events, etc.), **only to the extent provided by the Governor in Council by order**, on the recommendation of the President of the Treasury Board.

- **Exemption from section 9 of the Department of Public Works and Government Services Act**:
  - The IICH may **procure goods and services from outside the federal public administration**.
  - **Exception**: Legal services must be procured **only with the approval of the Attorney General of Canada**.

> This allows the IICH to hire external consultants, contractors, and service providers, enhancing flexibility and access to specialized expertise.

---

### **7. Legal Proceedings**
- Any legal actions, suits, or proceedings arising from rights or obligations of the IICH (whether in its own name or in the name of the Crown) may be **brought or defended in the name of the IICH**.

---

### **8. Oversight and Accountability**
- The **Minister** is **responsible** for the IICH.
- The IICH must comply with **all directions** given by the Minister.
- The Minister may:
  - Inquire into any activities of the IICH.
  - Require the IICH to provide information or reports on its operations.

---

### **9. Governance Structure**
#### **Board of Directors**
- Maximum of **11 directors**, including:
  - A **Chairperson** and a **Vice-Chairperson**.
  - The **Deputy Minister** of the department responsible for the Minister is an **ex-officio director**.

- **Appointment**:
  - Chairperson, Vice-Chairperson, and other directors (excluding Deputy Minister) are appointed by the **Governor in Council**.
  - Appointed on a **part-time basis**, during **pleasure**, for a **maximum term of three years**.
  - To ensure **rotational leadership**, no more than **half the directors** may serve in any one year.

- **Reappointment**:
  - Directors may be reappointed in the same or different roles.

- **Remuneration and Expenses**:
  - Paid by the Governor in Council.
  - Entitled to **reasonable travel and living expenses** incurred while performing duties.

- **Responsibilities**:
  - Supervise and manage the IICH’s business and affairs.
  - Advise the Minister and Chief Executive Officer on matters related to the IICH’s mandate.
  - May adopt **by-laws** governing internal operations.

- **Quorum**:
  - A **majority of directors** or **five directors**, whichever is greater, constitutes a quorum.

- **Chairperson Leadership**:
  - Presides over board meetings.
  - In the absence or incapacity of the Chairperson, the **Vice-Chairperson acts**.

---

#### **Chief Executive Officer (CEO)**
- Appointed by the **Governor in Council** for a term of **up to five years**.
- Eligible for **reappointment**.
- Remuneration and expenses are set by the Governor in Council.

- **Responsibilities**:
  - Oversees **day-to-day operations** of the IICH.
  - Has the **rank and powers of a deputy head of a federal department**.

- **Reporting Requirements**:
  - Must submit to the Board:
    - An **annual business plan**.
    - An **annual report** on operations and performance.

- **Succession Plan**:
  - In the absence or incapacity of the CEO, the **Minister may appoint a temporary CEO**.
  - This appointment may only last **longer than 90 days with the approval of the Governor in Council**.

---

### **10. Human Resources Management**
The IICH has **exclusive authority** over its human resources policies and practices:

- **Determine staffing needs** and allocate human resources effectively.
- **Plan training and development** programs and set conditions for such programs.
- **Classify positions** and employees.
- **Set pay, working hours, leave entitlements**, and other employment terms — **after consulting with the President of the Treasury Board**.
- **Establish performance awards** for outstanding achievements.
- **Define disciplinary standards** and penalties (including suspension or termination) for misconduct.
- **Provide for termination or demotion** (not due to misconduct) under defined circumstances and procedures.

> These powers allow the IICH to manage its workforce with flexibility and responsiveness to its mandate.

---

### **11. Employee Benefits and Insurance**
- The IICH may:
  - Establish or enter into contracts to acquire **group insurance or benefit programs**.
  - Set **all terms and conditions** (premiums, benefits, management, expenditures).
  - Audit and make contributions to such programs.

> **Exemption from the Financial Administration Act** applies to:
- Contributions and premiums paid by the IICH or its employees.
- Benefits received under such programs.

> This ensures operational flexibility in managing employee benefits without being constrained by standard public service financial rules.

---

### **12. Staffing Program and Collective Bargaining**
- The IICH must develop a **staffing program** covering:
  - Appointment of employees.
  - Recourse for employment disputes.

> **Matters governed by this staffing program are not subject to collective agreements**.

- Before entering into **collective bargaining** with a bargaining agent:
  - The IICH must obtain **prior approval from the President of the Treasury Board**.

> This ensures oversight and alignment with broader federal public service standards.

---

### **13. Employment Status and Legal Implications**
- **Public Service Status**:
  - The **Chief Executive Officer** and **senior vice-presidents and account executives** are **not considered employed in the public service** for the purposes of the **Public Service Superannuation Act**
