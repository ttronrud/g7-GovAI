**Detailed Summary of the *Weather Modification Information Act***

---

### **1. Title and Citation**
The Act is officially titled the **Weather Modification Information Act** and may be cited as such. It establishes a regulatory framework for activities involving weather modification in Canada, emphasizing transparency, accountability, and public access to information.

---

### **2. Key Definitions**

- **Administrator**:  
  This is a designated official from the public service appointed by the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet). The Administrator is responsible for overseeing compliance with the Act and managing the regulatory process related to weather modification activities.

- **Weather Modification Activity**:  
  This broadly defines any action intended to alter atmospheric conditions through **physical or chemical means**, with the goal of:
  - Increasing or decreasing precipitation
  - Redistributing precipitation
  - Decreasing or suppressing hail or lightning
  - Dissipating fog or clouds  
  *Examples include cloud seeding using silver iodide or other substances to induce rain or reduce hail.*

---

### **3. Pre-Activity Notification Requirements**

Any individual or entity proposing to conduct a weather modification activity in Canada must:
- Submit **detailed information** to the Administrator in the form and manner prescribed by regulations.
- The information must include:
  - Details about the person proposing the activity
  - A description of the proposed weather modification activity

This ensures that the government is aware of all planned operations before they occur, enabling oversight and risk assessment.

---

### **4. Mandatory Pre-Implementation Notification**

Before initiating any weather modification activity, the proposer must provide **written notice** to the Administrator specifying:

1. **Date and time** of the activity  
2. **Location** where the activity will take place  
3. **Names and addresses** of individuals or entities responsible for carrying out the activity  
4. **Purpose** of the activity (e.g., inducing rain for agriculture, suppressing hail for protection)  
5. **Equipment, materials, and methods** to be used  
6. **Geographic area(s)** that may be affected by the activity  

This requirement ensures transparency and allows the Administrator to assess potential environmental, health, or safety impacts.

---

### **5. Post-Activity Recordkeeping and Reporting Obligations**

Every person who carries out a weather modification activity must:

#### **(a) Maintain a Daily Record**
A detailed daily log must be kept, including:
- Location and operational details of all equipment used
- Meteorological observations (e.g., temperature, humidity, cloud type, wind speed) taken in the affected area
- Chemical composition, physical properties, and **quantities** of any substances emitted into the atmosphere (e.g., silver iodide, dry ice)

These records serve as a scientific and legal record of the activity and its environmental context.

#### **(b) Submit a Monthly Report**
Within **15 days after the end of the month** in which the activity occurred, the person must submit a report to the Administrator that includes:
- The date the activity was carried out  
- A description of the nature and scope of the activity  
- All relevant meteorological observations  
- Any other information specified by the Administrator or an authorized representative  

This ensures ongoing monitoring and data collection for scientific analysis and public accountability.

---

### **6. Access to Records**

Any individual or entity that holds a record required under the Act must allow the **Administrator or an authorized representative** to inspect it at **reasonable times**. This provision ensures that regulators can verify compliance and conduct audits.

---

### **7. Public Access to Information**

Information collected by the Administrator or its authorized representatives under this Act may be:
- **Made public**, or  
- **Provided upon request** to any member of the public  

This promotes transparency and public trust, allowing citizens to access data about weather modification activities, their locations, and their environmental effects.

---

### **8. Regulatory Authority**

The **Governor in Council** (federal Cabinet) has the power to make **regulations** that prescribe:
- The form and manner of submissions and reports  
- Specific requirements for records  
- Conditions under which activities may be permitted  
- Other matters that the Act allows to be prescribed  

This flexibility enables the government to adapt the rules to technological advances, scientific understanding, or emerging risks.

---

### **9. Enforcement and Penalties**

#### **Offence for Non-Compliance**
Any person who fails to comply with any provision of this Act is guilty of an offence and may be:
- **Fined up to $1,000**, or  
- **Imprisoned for up to six months**, or  
- **Both fined and imprisoned**

This creates a clear legal consequence for failure to follow procedural and reporting obligations.

#### **Corporate Liability and Individual Accountability**
If a **corporation** violates the Act:
- Any **officer, director, or agent** who:
  - Directed, authorized, assented to, or acquiesced in the violation  
  is **personally liable** for the offence  
- This liability applies **even if the corporation itself is not prosecuted**  
- The individual is subject to the same penalties as described above  

This ensures that corporate responsibility is not absolved by corporate structure — individuals in positions of authority are held personally accountable.

---

### **Purpose and Significance of the Act**

The *Weather Modification Information Act* serves several key functions:

1. **Transparency and Public Accountability**  
   By requiring detailed pre- and post-activity reporting, the Act ensures that the public is informed about weather modification efforts.

2. **Environmental and Scientific Oversight**  
   The requirement to document meteorological observations and chemical emissions supports scientific research and helps assess environmental impacts.

3. **Safety and Risk Management**  
   By identifying affected areas and methods used, the Act helps prevent unintended consequences such as unintended precipitation, chemical dispersion, or interference with other weather systems.

4. **Legal and Regulatory Clarity**  
   The Act establishes a clear legal framework for weather modification, distinguishing it from ordinary weather phenomena and setting boundaries for acceptable use.

5. **Public Trust and Democratic Oversight**  
   By allowing public access to information and enabling inspections, the Act strengthens democratic accountability in government-regulated scientific activities.

---

### **Conclusion**

The *Weather Modification Information Act* is a foundational piece of Canadian legislation designed to regulate, monitor, and ensure accountability for weather modification activities. It balances the potential benefits of such technologies — such as enhancing water supply or protecting crops from hail — with the need for environmental protection, public safety, and transparency. Through mandatory reporting, recordkeeping, and public access, the Act ensures that these powerful atmospheric interventions are conducted responsibly and with full societal oversight.

--- 

**Note**: While the Act does not prohibit weather modification, it imposes strict procedural and informational requirements to ensure that such activities are conducted in a safe, transparent, and scientifically sound manner.
