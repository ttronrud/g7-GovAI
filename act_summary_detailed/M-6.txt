**Detailed Summary of the *Merchant Seamen Compensation Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *Merchant Seamen Compensation Act* is a Canadian statute designed to provide **compensation to seamen and their dependants** in the event of injury, disability, or death arising from an accident during the course of their employment. It establishes a comprehensive framework for determining eligibility, calculating compensation, and enforcing claims, while also ensuring that the rights of seamen are protected from employer interference or waiver.

This Act applies to seamen employed on ships engaged in **foreign or home-trade voyages**, and it operates independently of other workers' compensation laws, though it includes provisions for overlapping claims.

---

## **Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Accident** | Includes both **wilful/intentional acts** (not by the seaman) and **fortuitous events** caused by physical or natural causes. |
| **Compensation** | Covers medical and hospital expenses, as well as any other benefits, allowances, or services authorized under the Act. |
| **Dependants** | Family members who were wholly or partly dependent on the seaman’s earnings at the time of death, or who would have been dependent had the seaman’s incapacity occurred. |
| **Employer** | Any person having a seaman in service under a written or oral contract (express or implied), including through hiring or apprenticeship. |
| **Foreign Voyage** | A voyage extending beyond the boundaries of a home-trade voyage and not involving inland or minor waters. |
| **Home-Trade Voyage** | A voyage between specified Canadian and neighboring regions (e.g., U.S. excluding Hawaii, Mexico, Central America), without going south of the 6th parallel north latitude. |
| **Inland Voyage** | A voyage on inland waters of Canada, including parts of U.S. lakes or rivers forming part of those waters. |
| **Invalid** | A person physically or mentally incapable of earning. |
| **Medical Aid** | Includes surgical, dental, hospital, skilled nursing, and artificial limb services as defined in section 46(1). |
| **Minister** | Refers to the **Minister of Labour**, who has primary administrative and judicial authority under the Act. |
| **Minor Waters of Canada** | Inland waters excluding major lakes (Ontario, Erie, Huron, Superior, Georgian Bay), and sheltered coastal waters designated by the Minister of Transport. |
| **Minor Waters Voyage** | A voyage within the minor waters of Canada and adjacent U.S. portions. |
| **Seaman** | A person (excluding pilots, apprentices, and fishers) employed on a **Canadian-registered ship** or a **ship chartered to a Canadian resident**, engaged in foreign or home-trade voyages. If ordered by the Governor in Council, also includes seamen working on foreign-registered ships operated by Canadian residents. |
| **Ship** | As defined in the *Canada Shipping Act, 2001*. |
| **Survivor** | A spouse or a person cohabiting with the seaman for at least one year prior to death. |

## **Key Provisions and Legal Principles**

### **1. Disability as an Accident (Section 3)**
- A seaman who suffers a **non-accident-related disability** (e.g., illness, pre-existing condition) arising from employment is **deemed to have suffered the disability as a result of an accident**.
- For compensation purposes, the **accident date is deemed to be the day the disability first became known to the employer**.

> *Purpose*: Ensures that seamen are not denied compensation due to non-accident-related injuries that occur in the course of duty.

---

### **2. Exclusion of Claims (Section 4)**
Compensation **shall not be payable** if:
- The seaman or dependants are already entitled to compensation under:
  - The *Government Employees Compensation Act*;
  - Any provincial workers’ compensation law;
  - The Order in Council of April 30, 1942 (P.C. 104/3546), or any similar foreign or domestic law.

> *Rationale*: Prevents duplication of benefits and ensures a single, clear compensation system.

---

### **3. Foreign Law Election (Section 5)**
- If a seaman or dependant is entitled to compensation under a foreign country’s law, they must **elect** whether to claim under that law or under this Act.
- **Notice of election must be given to the Minister within 3 months** of the accident or death (or a longer period allowed by the Minister).
- If no election is made, it is **presumed the claimant has chosen not to claim under this Act**.
- A **waiver of foreign claims** must be submitted in a form approved by the Minister to receive compensation under this Act.

> *Purpose*: Resolves conflicts between domestic and foreign compensation systems.

---

### **4. Scope of Application**
- The Act applies to accidents occurring **within or outside Canada**.

> *Note*: This broad scope ensures protection for seamen regardless of location.

---

### **5. Employer Responsibility for Compensation (Section 6)**
- The employer must pay compensation **in the manner and amount specified in the Act**, **except** in these cases:
  - The injury does **not disable the seaman for at least three days** from earning full wages; or
  - The injury results solely from the **serious and wilful misconduct** of the seaman (unless it results in death or serious disability).

> *Exclusion rationale*: Prevents compensation for minor or self-inflicted injuries.

---

### **6. Timing and Notice Requirements (Section 7)**
- Compensation is payable **from the date of disability**.
- **Notice of accident** must be given **as soon as practicable** before the seaman voluntarily leaves employment.
- A **claim must be made within six months** of the accident or death.
- Failure to meet notice or claim deadlines **does not bar compensation** if:
  - The employer was not prejudiced; or
  - The claim is just and should be allowed.

> *Flexibility*: Recognizes practical difficulties in timely reporting.

---

### **7. Employer Reporting Obligations (Section 8)**
- Employers must **notify the Minister in writing within 30 days** of an accident involving:
  - Disability or need for medical aid.
- Required information includes:
  - Time, place, nature of the accident;
  - Name and address of the seaman;
  - Medical aid received.

> *Penalty*: Failure to comply is an **offence**, punishable by:
> - Fine up to **$5,000**, or
> - Imprisonment up to **12 months**, or both.

> *Defence*: A person is not guilty if they can prove **due diligence** was exercised.

> *Minister’s discretion*: The Minister may issue an order relieving an employer from reporting obligations.

---

### **8. Medical Examinations and Referrals (Section 27)**
- A seaman must submit to medical examination **if required by the employer or the Minister**.
- The **employer pays** all costs of medical exams or referrals.
- If the seaman has been examined by a qualified practitioner (selected by employer or self), and the report is shared, the Minister may refer the case to a **medical referee**.
- The **medical referee’s certificate** is **conclusive** on matters of fitness and unfitness for work.

> *Exception*: A seaman may **reasonably refuse** surgical aid — refusal does not suspend compensation.

> *Consequence of non-compliance*: 
> - Right to compensation or periodic payments is **suspended** until examination occurs.
> - The Minister may **diminish or suspend** payments if the seaman:
>   - Continues dangerous/sanitary practices;
>   - Refuses necessary medical treatment.

---

### **9. Review of Compensation Payments (Section 28)**
- Weekly or periodic payments may be **reviewed** by the employer or seaman.
- The Minister may:
  - **End or reduce** payments;
  - **Increase** them up to the maximum prescribed in the Act.

> *Purpose*: Ensures that compensation remains appropriate and fair over time.

---

### **10. Insurance Requirement (Section 29)**
- Every employer must **cover the risks of compensation** through insurance or other means satisfactory to the Minister.

> *Ensures financial responsibility and prevents employer default.*

---

### **11. No Waiver or Agreement (Section 30)**
- **No agreement** between a seaman and employer to waive or forego benefits under this Act is valid.
- Such agreements are **absolutely void**.

> *Protects seamen from exploitation or coercion.*

---

### **12. Jurisdiction and Finality of Ministerial Decisions (Section 31)**
- The **Minister has exclusive jurisdiction** to:
  - Examine, hear, and determine all matters under the Act.
  - Reconsider decisions or amend orders.
  - Require production of relevant information.
- Ministerial decisions and findings are **final and conclusive**.

> *Emphasis on administrative authority and finality.*

---
