### 3. **Powers of the Minister**
The Minister (designated by the Governor in Council) has broad powers to:
1. **Undertake research and investigations** in the field of nuclear energy.
2. **Utilize nuclear energy**, with the **approval of the Governor in Council**.
3. **Acquire nuclear substances**, including:
   - Mines, deposits, or claims related to nuclear substances;
   - Patent rights or certificates of supplementary protection under the *Patent Act*;
   - Works or property used for:
     - Production or preparation of nuclear energy;
     - Research or investigations into nuclear energy.
4. **License or dispose of**:
   - Discoveries and inventions;
   - Improvements in processes, apparatus, or machines related to nuclear energy;
   - Patent rights and certificates acquired under the Act.
5. **Collect royalties and fees** from such licenses, discoveries, inventions, and improvements.

> This grants the Minister significant authority over the development, ownership, and commercialization of nuclear technologies and materials.

## ðŸš« Important Context: Repeal in 1997

- The **entire Act was repealed in 1997** under **Bill C-9, section 88**.
- The repeal was part of a broader legislative reform to:
  - Streamline nuclear regulation;
  - Transfer responsibilities to more specialized legislation;
  - Recognize Indigenous rights and land claims;
  - Improve accountability and transparency.

> The functions previously governed by the *Nuclear Energy Act* were transferred to:
> - The *Nuclear Safety and Control Act*;
> - The *Atomic Energy Control Act*;
> - The *Crown Corporation Act*;
> - And other federal laws governing Indigenous rights and land use.
