### ðŸ”¹ **Overview and Purpose**
The *Excise Act, 2001* is a foundational piece of Canadian legislation that establishes the framework for the **levying, collection, and administration of excise duties** on specific goods such as **alcohol, tobacco, cannabis, and vaping products**. It consolidates and modernizes excise regulations, aligns them with current economic and regulatory practices, and introduces new provisions related to the **cannabis market**, **vaping**, and **international trade**.

This Act is **cited as the "Excise Act, 2001"** and operates under the authority of Her Majesty in right of Canada, enacted with the advice and consent of both the Senate and House of Commons.

The Act begins with a comprehensive list of **definitions** that apply throughout the legislation. These definitions ensure clarity and consistency in interpreting tax obligations, classifications, and administrative procedures.

#### 1. **Alcohol & Alcohol-Related Terms**
- **Alcohol**: Refers to **spirits or wine**.
- **Bulk alcohol**: Alcohol that is **not packaged** (i.e., sold in unsealed or uncontainerized form).
- **Marking (mark)**:
  - Spirits containers must be **marked** to indicate whether they are for:
    - Delivery to a **registered user**, or
    - Use at a **bottle-your-own premises** (a retail space where consumers can purchase and package alcohol themselves).
- **Bottle-your-own premises**: A location where consumers can **purchase and package alcohol** from a marked special container.
- **Alcohol licensee**: A person holding a **spirits or wine licence**.
- **Alcohol registrant**: A person holding an **alcohol registration** issued under section 17.

> *Purpose*: Ensures traceability, prevents illegal distribution, and supports consumer safety and regulatory oversight.

#### 2. **Tobacco-Related Definitions**
- **Cigar**: Includes cigarillos, cheroots, and any tubular product made from natural/reconstituted leaf tobacco (filler, binder, wrapper).
- **Cigarette**: Any roll or tubular construction intended for smoking, **excluding cigars or tobacco sticks**. If a cigarette exceeds 102 mm in length, **each portion of 76 mm or less is treated as a separate cigarette**.
- **Manufactured tobacco**: Any product made from raw leaf tobacco (excluding cigars or raw leaf tobacco) through processes like packing, reconstituting, or converting.
- **Partially manufactured tobacco**: Tobacco that is cut filler or cut rag, or less fully processed than those forms.
- **Tobacco dealer/licensee**: A person holding a **tobacco dealerâ€™s licence** issued under section 14.

> *Purpose*: To standardize tobacco classification and ensure compliance with production, sale, and distribution rules.

#### 3. **Cannabis-Related Definitions (Aligned with Cannabis Act)**
- **Cannabis**: Defined under subsection 2(1) of the *Cannabis Act*.
- **Fresh cannabis**: As defined in subsection 1(1) of the *Cannabis Regulations*.
- **Dried cannabis**: As defined in the *Cannabis Act*.
- **Flowering material**: The whole or part (excluding viable seeds) of an inflorescence at any stage of development (including the infructescence stage).
- **Industrial hemp**:
  - Cannabis designated as industrial hemp under the *Cannabis Act* or *Industrial Hemp Regulations*.
  - Includes **hemp plants, by-products, and processed materials**.
- **Industrial hemp by-product**:
  - Flowering material (excluding viable achenes) or non-flowering material removed from a plant.
  - Must not have been disposed of via retting or other methods rendering it unusable.
- **Industrial hemp grower**: A person with a licence or permit to grow industrial hemp.
- **Industrial hemp plant**: A cannabis plant (including seedlings) that is classified as industrial hemp.

> *Purpose*: To distinguish between recreational, medical, and industrial uses of cannabis and to regulate the production and movement of both.

#### 4. **Cannabis Duty & Excise Stamps**
- **Cannabis duty**: Imposed under **section 158.19 or 158.21**.
- **Cannabis excise stamp**: A stamp issued by the Minister under section 158.03(1) and not yet cancelled.
- **Dutiable amount**:
  - Calculated based on:
    - Consideration paid for the cannabis product,
    - Consideration for the container,
    - Any additional charges (e.g., advertising, commissions),
    - A percentage set in **Schedule 7, paragraph 2(a)** (typically 10%),
    - **Provincial adjustments** if applicable (e.g., additional duty in a specific province),
    - Or **0%** in certain cases.
  - In prescribed circumstances, the amount may be determined differently (e.g., for personal use or exports).

> *Purpose*: To create a transparent, proportional, and equitable tax system on cannabis sales, with flexibility for provincial policy.

#### 5. **Vaping Products**
- **Vaping product**: Includes any product made with or containing:
  - Cannabis (including dried or fresh),
  - Industrial hemp by-products,
  - A prescribed substance/material/thing.
- **Vaping substance**: The liquid or material used in a vaping device.
- **Immediate container**: The container that **directly contacts** the vaping substance (does **not** include the vaping device).
- **Additional vaping duty**: Imposed under **section 158.58**.

> *Purpose*: To regulate the growing market of e-cigarettes and vaping devices, ensuring tax compliance and preventing illicit trade.

#### 6. **Duties & Taxation Framework**
- **Duty**:
  - Generally refers to excise duties under this Act.
  - Includes duties under **section 21.1 or 21.2 of the Customs Tariff** (e.g., ad valorem duties).
  - **Excludes special duty** in Parts 3 and 4.
- **Duty-free shop**: A retail location licensed under the *Customs Act* to sell goods without duty.
- **Duty-free shop licensee**: Person authorized to operate such a shop.
- **Duty-paid market/value**:
  - For imported cigars: Value of cigars + value of container + applicable duties.
  - Includes both ad valorem and excise duties.
- **Non-duty-paid**: Refers to alcohol or cannabis products **not subject to excise duty** (except special duty for alcohol).

> *Purpose*: To establish clear thresholds and values for importation, taxation, and customs compliance.

---

#### 7. **Administrative & Enforcement Roles**
- **Agency**: The **Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)**, continued under subsection 4(1) of the *CRA Act*.
- **Commissioner**: The Commissioner of Revenue appointed under the *CRA Act*.
- **Officer**:
  - Anyone appointed to administer or enforce the Act.
  - Includes members of the **Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP)** or designated police forces.
  - For unreleased imported goods, includes officers under the *Customs Act*.
- **Analyst**: A person designated under section 11 to analyze compliance, samples, or product composition.

> *Purpose*: To ensure effective enforcement, inspection, and compliance with excise laws.

#### 8. **International & Cross-Border Terms**
- **Accredited representative**: A person entitled to tax exemptions under international treaties (e.g., Articles 34 or 49 of the Foreign Missions and International Organizations Act).
- **Listed international agreement**: Includes:
  - The *Convention on Mutual Administrative Assistance in Tax Matters* (Strasbourg, 1988), as amended.
  - Comprehensive tax information exchange agreements with other countries.
- **Foreign duty-free shop**: A store abroad that sells goods free of duty to individuals leaving the country.

> *Purpose*: To support international cooperation, facilitate trade, and ensure tax fairness in cross-border transactions.

#### 9. **Time Periods and Financial Terms**
- **Fiscal year**: Same as under *Part IX of the Excise Tax Act*.
- **Fiscal month/half-year**: Defined under sections 159 and 159(1.1).
- **Month**: A period beginning on a specific day and ending on the day before the same calendar date in the next month (or last day of the next month if no such date exists).

> *Purpose*: To standardize financial reporting and tax assessment cycles.
