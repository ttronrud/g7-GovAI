**Detailed Summary of the *Department of Employment and Social Development Act***  

---

### **Overview**  
The *Department of Employment and Social Development Act* is a foundational piece of Canadian legislation that reorganizes and redefines the structure, mandate, and operational powers of the federal government’s agency responsible for employment and social development. This Act establishes the **Department of Employment and Social Development (DESDD)**, consolidates key functions previously held by the Department of Human Resources and Skills Development (HRSD), and strengthens the roles of the **Canada Employment Insurance Commission (Commission)**, the **Social Security Tribunal (Tribunal)**, and the **Board of Appeal**. It also outlines the powers, responsibilities, financial mechanisms, and administrative frameworks governing the Department and its associated bodies.

The Act was enacted by Her Majesty, with the advice and consent of both the Senate and the House of Commons, and is intended to improve the standard of living, promote inclusive labor markets, and ensure income security for Canadians.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Name and Citation of the Act**  
- The Act is formally titled: **"The Department of Employment and Social Development Act"**.  
- It may be cited as **DESDD Act**.

---

### **2. Definitions**  
The Act introduces several key definitions that govern its application:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Board of Appeal** | Refers to the **Employment Insurance Board of Appeal**, established under section 43.01. Responsible for reviewing decisions in employment insurance matters. |
| **Commission** | Refers to the **Canada Employment Insurance Commission**, continued under this Act (Section 20). Oversees employment insurance administration, policy, and compliance. |
| **Minister** | The **Minister of Employment and Social Development**, appointed by commission under the Great Seal. Holds executive authority over the Department. |
| **Partner Entity** | Broadly defined to include: <br> - Departments or bodies (excluding parent Crown corporations); <br> - Parent Crown corporations and their wholly-owned subsidiaries; <br> - Provincial governments, public bodies, municipalities; <br> - Indigenous bands, First Nations, or Aboriginal organizations (including those in land claim or self-government agreements); <br> - Non-profit organizations serving any of the above entities. |
| **Tribunal** | The **Social Security Tribunal**, established under section 44, which handles appeals in social security matters. |

---

### **3. Continuation and Renaming of the Department**  
- The **Department of Human Resources and Skills Development (HRSD)** is **continued under the new name**: **Department of Employment and Social Development (DESDD)**.  
- The **Minister of Employment and Social Development** is appointed by commission under the Great Seal and presides over the Department.  
- The expressions **"Employment and Social Development Canada"** and **"Emploi et Développement social Canada"** are deemed to refer to the **DESDD** and its French counterpart.

---

### **4. Ministerial Powers, Duties, and Functions**  
The Minister has broad authority over matters related to:

#### **Human Resources and Skills Development**  
- Focus: Promoting a **highly skilled, mobile workforce** and an **efficient, inclusive labor market**.  
- Objective: Improve the **standard of living and quality of life** of Canadians.

#### **Social Development**  
- Focus: Promoting **social well-being and income security**.

> These powers extend to all matters within Parliament’s jurisdiction that are not assigned to another Minister, department, board, or agency.

---

### **5. Service Delivery and Public Engagement**  
The Minister may provide various services and facilities to **partner entities** and other designated bodies:

#### **To Partner Entities**  
- Support in delivering programs and services.  
- Any other service authorized by the Governor in Council.

#### **To Federal Departments and Partner Entities**  
The Minister may provide the following digital services:  
- Administration of public-access websites  
- Management of social media accounts  
- Publication of mobile applications on third-party stores and the Government of Canada website  
- Online citizen engagement  
- Any other related electronic or digital service  

#### **Administration of the Government of Canada Website**  
- The Minister administers the **official Government of Canada website**, ensuring accessibility and functionality.

#### **Information Services**  
- Provides information services about Government programs and services, as well as those of authorized third parties.

> The Minister may extend these services to **any other person, organization, or government** if authorized by the Governor in Council.

---

### **6. Charging and Revenue Use**  
- The Minister may **charge fees** for services or facilities provided under section 5.1, **except** for services under paragraphs (c) and (d) (digital services and website administration).  
- Fees must not exceed the **cost to Her Majesty in right of Canada** of providing the service.  
- Revenues from fees may be **spent by the Minister** for operational purposes.

---

### **7. Data Collection and Coordination**  
- The Minister may **collect, analyze, interpret, publish, and distribute** data on:  
  - Human resources and skills development  
  - Social development  
  - Service delivery to the public  
> This is subject to the **Statistics Act**.

- The Minister may **cooperate with provincial authorities** to coordinate efforts in human resources, social development, and service delivery.

---

### **8. Program Development and Funding**  
- The Minister may **establish and implement programs** to support:  
  - Human resource development  
  - Skills enhancement  
  - Social development  
  - Public service delivery  
- The Minister may make **grants and contributions** to support these programs.

---

### **9. Collection of Identifiers**  
- The Minister may **collect and use**:
  - A person’s **Business Number** (as defined in the Income Tax Act) as an identifier for administrative or enforcement purposes.  
  - A person’s **Social Insurance Number (SIN)** to verify identity in connection with administration or enforcement of programs under the Minister’s responsibility.

> These powers are critical for verification, eligibility checks, and compliance.

---

### **10. Committees and Advisory Bodies**  
- The Minister may **establish advisory and other committees** with defined membership, duties, and operations.  
- Members are entitled to:  
  - **Remuneration** fixed by the Governor in Council  
  - **Reimbursement** for travel, living, and other expenses (in accordance with Treasury Board directives)

---

### **11. Agreements and Delegation of Powers**  
- The Minister may enter into **agreements with provinces, financial institutions, and other entities** to coordinate policy and program implementation.  
- The Minister may **authorize other bodies** (e.g., the Minister of Labour, the Commission, or other persons) to **exercise powers or perform duties** of the Minister.

---

### **12. Oaths and Affidavits**  
- Employees of the Department (authorized by the Deputy Minister or Minister) may:  
  - Administer oaths  
  - Take and receive affidavits, declarations, and affirmations  
> These individuals have all the powers of a **commissioner for administering oaths**.

---

### **13. Fee Setting and Regulation**  
The Minister may, subject to **Treasury Board regulations**, set fees for:  

| Service Type | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Services/Facilities** | Provided by the Minister, Department, Commission, or other government bodies under the Minister’s responsibility |
| **Products/Privileges** | E.g., access to services, licenses, or benefits |
| **Regulatory Processes/Approvals** | E.g., applications, reviews, compliance checks |

> **Fee Limits**:  
> - Fees for services/facilities must not exceed the **cost to Her Majesty** of providing the service.  
> - Fees for regulatory processes must not exceed the **reasonable outlays** incurred by the Government.

> **Publication Requirement**:  
> - Within **30 days** of setting a fee, the fee must be **published in the Canada Gazette**.  
> - The fee is treated as a **statutory instrument** and is referred to the **Committee under the Statutory Instruments Act** for review and scrutiny.

> **Regulatory Authority**:  
> - The Treasury Board may make regulations to implement these fee-setting provisions.

---

### **14. Minister of Labour – Appointment and Powers**  
- A **Minister of Labour** may be appointed by commission under the Great Seal.  
- If no Minister of Labour is appointed, the **Minister of Employment and Social Development** **exercises the powers and duties** of the Minister of Labour.  
- All references to the Minister of Labour in legislation are **read as references to the Minister** unless context requires otherwise.

#### Powers of the Minister of Labour  
- Covers all matters related to **labour** within Parliament’s jurisdiction.  
- Objective: Promote **safe, healthy, fair, stable, cooperative, and productive workplaces**.

> The Minister may:  
> - Use the services and facilities of the DESDD  
> - Authorize Department employees to perform duties of the Minister of Labour  
> - Establish and fund programs to support safe and productive workplaces  
> - Collect Business Numbers and SINs for enforcement purposes

---
