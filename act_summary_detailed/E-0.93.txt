**Detailed Summary of the *Economic and Fiscal Update Implementation Act, 2021***

---

### **Overview**

The *Economic and Fiscal Update Implementation Act, 2021* is a Canadian federal statute enacted by Her Majesty, with the advice and consent of both the Senate and the House of Commons. The Act serves as a legislative implementation tool to provide specific provisions related to the administration and enforcement of certain economic and fiscal measures introduced during the early stages of the COVID-19 pandemic, particularly those involving business support programs and public health initiatives.

While the Act does not introduce new major policy frameworks, it consolidates, clarifies, and operationalizes key elements of financial recovery mechanisms and targeted government funding for provinces and territories.

#### **1. CEBA Loan Recovery Limitations (Section on Debt Collection)**

This section establishes a clear and time-bound framework for recovering money owed under **Canada Emergency Business Account (CEBA) loans**, which were part of the governmentâ€™s emergency financial assistance program for small and medium-sized businesses during the pandemic.

##### **Core Elements:**
- **Recovery Deadline**:  
  Any legal action or proceeding to recover money owed under a CEBA loan must be initiated **no later than six years after the date of default**.

- **Definition of "Date of Default"**:  
  The date of default is defined as the **earlier of**:
  - The day the person making the claim first **knew** the default had occurred, or  
  - The day they **ought reasonably to have known** the default had occurred.  
  This establishes a reasonable standard for when the obligation begins to accrue.

- **Right to Set-Off or Deduction**:  
  A person who owes money under a CEBA loan may have that debt **deducted, set off, or compensated** against any other amount payable by the Government of Canada to them â€” **except** amounts payable under **section 122.61 of the Income Tax Act** (which relates to tax credits or refunds).

- **Effect of Acknowledgment of Liability**:  
  If a debtor acknowledges their liability (in any form) **during or after** the six-year limitation period, a legal action may be brought **within six years of the acknowledgment date**, subject to certain conditions.

  **What constitutes "acknowledgment of liability"?**  
  This includes:
  - A **promise to pay** the debt (by the debtor or their representative);
  - An **acknowledgment of the debt** (even if no payment is promised);
  - A **partial payment** of the debt;
  - An acknowledgment made during **bankruptcy or insolvency proceedings**, including by a trustee or administrator.

- **Suspension of Limitation Periods**:  
  The six-year limitation period is **suspended** during any period when it is **prohibited to commence or continue legal action** against the debtor (e.g., due to court closures, pandemic-related restrictions, or other legal moratoria).

- **Exemption from Section Application**:  
  This section **does not apply** to actions related to:
  - The **execution, renewal, or enforcement of a court judgment**.

- **Applicability Regardless of Timing of Default**:  
  The rules apply **regardless of whether the default occurred before or after** the Act came into force. Furthermore, even if the prior limitation period expired before the Act was passed, the new rules still apply.

> âœ… **Purpose**: This provision provides **predictability and fairness** in debt recovery for businesses that received CEBA loans. It prevents undue hardship while ensuring that creditors have a reasonable window to pursue recovery.

#### **3. Funding for Provincial Proof-of-Vaccination Initiatives (Section on Public Health Compliance)**

The **Minister of Health** may provide financial support to provinces and territories to help implement **coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) proof-of-vaccination initiatives**.

##### **Key Details**:
- Total funding ceiling: **Not to exceed $300 million**.
- The amount allocated to each province/territory is **determined by the Minister of Health**, based on local needs and implementation plans.

##### **Funding Mechanism**:
- Payments are made from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund**.
- The Minister has discretion over **timing, method, and conditions** of disbursement.

> âœ… **Purpose**: To assist provinces in enforcing or promoting vaccination requirements in public spaces (e.g., schools, workplaces, public transport), supporting public health goals during the pandemic.

#### **4. Funding for COVID-19 Testing Expenses (Section on Laboratory and Testing Costs)**

The **Minister of Health** may provide funding for **expenses incurred on or after April 1, 2021**, related to **coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) testing**.

##### **Key Details**:
- Total funding ceiling: **Not to exceed $1.72 billion**.
- Funds are to be used **specifically for testing-related expenses** (e.g., purchase of tests, lab costs, distribution, logistics).

##### **Reporting Requirements**:
- Within **three months** of the section coming into force, and **every three months thereafter**, the Minister of Health must:
  - Prepare a report that includes:
    - The **number of payments made** and the **total amount paid**;
    - The **number of tests purchased**;
    - How the tests were **distributed** (e.g., by region, facility, population group).
  - Tabulate the report in **each House of Parliament** on any of the **first 15 days** of sitting after the report is completed.

> âœ… **Purpose**: To ensure **transparency, accountability, and public trust** in the use of public funds for testing, while enabling timely and efficient pandemic response.
