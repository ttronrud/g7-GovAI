**Detailed Summary of the *Tax Court of Canada Act***

---

### **Overview**

The *Tax Court of Canada Act* establishes the legal framework for the **Tax Court of Canada (TCC)**, a superior court of record with **exclusive original jurisdiction** over specific tax-related appeals and references. The Act defines the structure, composition, appointment, powers, procedures, and jurisdiction of the Court, while also outlining rules governing judicial conduct, hearings, discovery, and costs. It ensures that tax disputes involving federal taxation legislation are resolved in a fair, transparent, and legally sound manner.

---

## **1. Citation and Definitions**

- **Citation**: The Act is known as the *Tax Court of Canada Act*.
- **Key Definitions**:
  - **Associate Chief Justice**: Refers to the Associate Chief Justice of the Court (repealed in 2002).
  - **Chief Justice**: The Chief Justice of the Tax Court of Canada.
  - **Judge**: Includes any judge of the Court, including the Chief Justice and Associate Chief Justice.
  - **Registry**: A registry established by the **Chief Administrator of the Courts Administration Service** under the *Courts Administration Service Act*, responsible for administrative functions of the Court.
  - **Aggregate of all amounts**: The total of all amounts assessed or determined by the **Minister of National Revenue** under the *Income Tax Act*, **excluding interest or losses**.
  - **Aggregate of supplies for the prior fiscal year**: The total value of all supplies (as defined in *Part IX of the Excise Tax Act*) made by a person in the last complete fiscal year that ended at least six months before the filing of an appeal.
  - **Amount in dispute in an appeal**: Varies depending on the Act involved:
    - *Customs Act (Part V.1)*: Total amounts assessed under section 97.44.
    - *Excise Act, 2001*: Includes duty, refund, relief, and interest in issue; also any amount likely to be affected in other assessments.
    - *Excise Tax Act (Part IX)*: Includes tax, net tax, rebate, penalties, and any amounts likely to be affected in other appeals or assessments.

## **2. Structure and Composition of the Court**

The Tax Court of Canada consists of:

- **One Chief Justice** (Chief Justice of the Tax Court of Canada)
- **One Associate Chief Justice** (Associate Chief Justice of the Tax Court of Canada)
- **Not more than 23 other judges**

> **Note**: The Chief Justice and Associate Chief Justice are the highest-ranking judicial officers in the Court.

### **Appointment**
- Judges are appointed by the **Governor in Council** via commission under the Great Seal.
- Eligibility criteria:
  - Former judge of a superior court in Canada; **or**
  - At least 10 years as a barrister or advocate (at the bar of any province), with **full-time judicial duties** in a legal position under federal or provincial law.
- **Quasi-requirement**: Either the Chief Justice or Associate Chief Justice must be a member of the **bar of Quebec**.

### **Rank and Precedence**
- Chief Justice > Associate Chief Justice > Other judges.
- Among other judges, precedence is based on **seniority** (time of appointment to the Court or the Tax Review Board).

### **Vacancies and Succession**
- If the **Chief Justice is unable to act**, the **Associate Chief Justice** assumes duties.
- If the Associate Chief Justice is also unable to act, the **Chief Justice may designate a judge**.
- If no designation is made, the **senior judge in Canada** (able and willing to act) steps in — provided they have not elected to be a **supernumerary judge**.

---

## **3. Judicial Tenure and Retirement**

- **Tenure**: Judges serve during **good behaviour**.
- **Removal**: By the Governor General on an address from both the **Senate and House of Commons**.
- **Retirement age**:
  - All judges retire at **75 years of age**.
  - Judges who held office on **March 1, 1987**, may retire at **70 years of age**.
- **Oath of office**:
  - Required before entering office.
  - Administered to the Chief Justice by the **Governor General**; to other judges by the Chief Justice or another judge in their absence.

---

## **4. Deputy Judges**

- A **deputy judge** may be appointed under the following conditions:
  - Must be a former judge of a superior court in Canada or a former judge of a provincial court.
  - Must have been appointed under a provincial statute.
  - Request must be made by the **Chief Justice**, with **approval of the Governor in Council**.
- **Consent requirement**: No request can be made to a judge of a provincial court without the **consent of the chief justice of that court or the provincial attorney general**.
- The Governor in Council may approve requests **generally or for specific periods/purposes** and may limit the number of deputy judges.
- **Compensation**:
  - Paid the **same salary** as a regular judge (excluding Chief Justice or Associate Chief Justice), plus **travel allowances**.
- Deputy judges have **full judicial powers** while acting.

---

## **5. Supernumerary and Associate Judges**

- **Supernumerary Judge**:
  - A judge may elect to hold this office under the *Judges Act*.
  - Does not perform active duties but retains immunity and status.
- **Associate Judge**:
  - Appointed by the Governor in Council for **specific duties** (e.g., handling certain types of cases).
  - Must be a **barrister or advocate** in a province.
  - Appointment subject to **regulatory limits** on number (by regulation).
  - **Powers and duties** are defined by the **rules of the Court**.
  - Paid full salary, allowances, and annuities under the *Judges Act*.
  - Workload may be regulated as a **percentage of an associate judge's workload** (by regulation).
  - Has **full judicial immunity**.
  - Removable by the Governor in Council for cause.
  - Retires at **75 years of age**.
- **Supernumerary Associate Judge**:
  - An associate judge may elect to hold this office under the *Judges Act*.

## **6. Jurisdiction of the Tax Court**

The Court has **exclusive original jurisdiction** to hear and determine:

### **Appeals and References**
- Under the following Acts:
  - *Income Tax Act* (e.g., assessments, deductions, credits)
  - *Excise Tax Act* (Part IX – taxes, rebates, penalties)
  - *Customs Act* (Part V.1 – duties, refunds)
  - *Old Age Security Act*, *Canada Pension Plan*, *Employment Insurance Act*
  - *Air Travellers Security Charge Act*
  - *Excise Act, 2001*
  - *Softwood Lumber Products Export Charge Act, 2006*
  - *Disability Tax Credit Promoters Restrictions Act*
  - *Greenhouse Gas Pollution Pricing Act* (Part 1)
  - *Underused Housing Tax Act*
  - *Select Luxury Items Tax Act*
  - *Digital Services Tax Act*
  - *Global Minimum Tax Act*

### **Specific References**
- Under the **Canada Disability Benefit Act** (referred to in s. 66(2) of the *Department of Employment and Social Development Act*)
- Under the **War Veterans Allowance Act** and **Civilian War-related Benefits Act** (referred to in s. 33 of the *Veterans Review and Appeal Board Act*)
- Under specific sections of various Acts (e.g., s. 310/311 of *Excise Tax Act*, s. 97.58 of *Customs Act*, s. 173/174 of *Income Tax Act*, etc.)

### **Applications**
- For **extensions of time** under:
  - Canada Pension Plan (s. 28(1))
  - Cultural Property Export and Import Act (s. 33.2)
  - Excise Tax Act (s. 304/305)
  - Customs Act (s. 97.51/97.52)
  - Income Tax Act (s. 166.2/167)
  - Employment Insurance Act (s. 103(1))
  - Air Travellers Security Charge Act (s. 45/47)
  - Excise Act, 2001 (s. 197/199)
  - Greenhouse Gas Pollution Pricing Act (s. 115/117)
  - Underused Housing Tax Act (s. 39/41)
  - Select Luxury Items Tax Act (s. 99/101)
  - Digital Services Tax Act (s. 74/76)
  - Global Minimum Tax Act (s. 88/90)

### **Special Application**
- A registered charity may apply for a **postponement of suspension of receipt authority** under s. 188.2(4) of the *Income Tax Act*.
