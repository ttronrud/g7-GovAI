### ðŸ”¹ **1. Purpose of the Act**
The primary objective of the *Competition Act* is to **maintain and promote healthy competition in Canada**. This is achieved through several key goals:
- To enhance the **efficiency and adaptability** of the Canadian economy.
- To **expand opportunities** for Canadian businesses to participate in global markets.
- To **recognize and respect foreign competition** within Canada.
- To ensure that **small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs)** have **equitable access** to the Canadian market.
- To provide **consumers** with **competitive prices** and a **wide range of product and service choices**.

This foundational purpose underscores that competition is not only about market dominance but also about fairness, innovation, and consumer welfare.

### ðŸ”¹ **3. Affiliation and Control (Key Structural Provisions)**

#### âœ… **Affiliation Rules**
Two entities are considered **affiliated** if:
- One is a **subsidiary** of the other,
- Both are subsidiaries of the **same entity**, or
- Each is **controlled by the same entity or individual**.

> If two entities are affiliated with the same third entity at the same time, they are **deemed affiliated with each other**.

#### âœ… **Control (Definition of "Controlled by")**
An entity is **controlled** by another if:
- **For a corporation**:
  - More than **50% of the voting rights** in the corporation are held directly or indirectly (through subsidiaries), and
  - Those votes are sufficient to **elect a majority of directors**.
- **For a corporation without share capital**:
  - A majority of directors (excluding ex-officio directors) are appointed by:
    - The Governor in Council (federal) or Lieutenant Governor in Council (provincial), or
    - A Minister of Canada or a provincial government.
- **For non-corporate entities** (e.g., partnerships, trusts):
  - An individual or entity holds an interest that entitles them to **more than 50% of the profits** or **more than 50% of assets upon dissolution**.

> This definition is critical for assessing **market concentration**, **anti-competitive behavior**, and **merger review**.

#### âœ… **Application to Government Agents**
The Act applies to **agents of Her Majesty in right of Canada or a province** (e.g., government-owned corporations) **in respect of commercial activities** they engage in **competing with other entities**, whether actual or potential.

> This ensures that even government bodies must comply with competition rules when operating in markets.
