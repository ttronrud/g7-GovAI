### **Detailed Summary of the *Lieutenant Governors Superannuation Act***

---

#### **1. Purpose and Citation**
The *Lieutenant Governors Superannuation Act* establishes a comprehensive superannuation (pension) system for former Lieutenant Governors of Canadian provinces. It ensures financial security for individuals who have served in this role, including provisions for retirement pensions, disability benefits, survivor benefits, and returns of contributions. The Act may be cited as the **Lieutenant Governors Superannuation Act**.

---

#### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Contributor** | A Lieutenant Governor who is required to contribute to the Consolidated Revenue Fund, or who has ceased to hold office but is entitled to a pension or return of contributions under the Act. Includes those who have stopped contributing due to five years of service or disability. |
| **Immediate Pension** | A pension payable immediately upon becoming entitled to it (e.g., upon reaching age 60 or upon disability). |
| **Deferred Pension** | A pension payable at age 60 or later. Available to contributors who have not yet reached 60 years of age. |
| **Disabled** | A person with a permanent infirmity rendering them unable to perform duties of office or pursue a gainful occupation commensurate with qualifications. |
| **Survivor** | A spouse or cohabiting partner (in a conjugal relationship for at least one year) of a deceased Lieutenant Governor or former Lieutenant Governor. Applies both before and after retirement. |
| **Contribution Year** | A calendar year during which contributions were made by a contributor. |

---

#### **3. Pension Entitlements for Contributors**

##### **(a) Upon Ceasing Office (After 5 Years of Service)**  
Every contributor who has served **five or more years** as Lieutenant Governor is entitled to one of the following, upon ceasing office:

- **Immediate Pension** if:
  - Age ≥ 60 years, **or**
  - Disabled (even if under 60).

- **Deferred Pension** if:
  - Age < 60 years, **unless** they choose to return contributions.

- **Return of Contributions + Interest** at the contributor’s option (if under 60 and not disabled).

> ✅ **Optionality**: Contributors may choose between a deferred pension or return of contributions.

> ⚠️ **Default Rule**: If no decision is made within **six months** of ceasing office, the contributor is **deemed to have chosen a deferred pension**.

##### **(b) Pension Calculation**
- The pension amount is **three-tenths (30%) of the average salary** earned during the **last five years** of service.

##### **(c) No Pension? Return of Contributions**
- If a contributor does **not qualify** for a pension (e.g., less than five years of service), they are entitled to a **return of all contributions made**, plus **interest**, as calculated under subsection (5).

---

#### **4. Contribution Requirements**

- **Mandatory Contribution**: Every Lieutenant Governor must contribute **6% of their salary** to the **Consolidated Revenue Fund**, by reservation from their salary.

- **Cap on Contributions**: After completing **five years of service**, no further contributions are required under this Act.

- **Exemption via Election**:
  - A Lieutenant Governor may **elect in writing**, within **six months of appointment**, to **not contribute** under section 4.
  - Once elected, the individual is **exempt from future contributions** under section 4.
  - Such an election is **irrevocable**.

- **Consequences of Election**:
  - Contributions made prior to the election are **not eligible for interest**.
  - The contributor is entitled to a **return of all contributions made**, **without interest**.
  - Sections 3, 4, 7, 8, and 8.1 **do not apply** to:
    - The contributor who made the election.
    - The survivor of such a contributor.

---

#### **5. Interest on Contributions**

- When a contributor becomes entitled to a pension or return of contributions, **interest is calculated** at **4% per annum, compounded annually**, on the **aggregate amount of contributions** for each contribution year.

- Interest is calculated from **December 31 of each year** (the contribution year) to **December 31 of the year immediately before** the year in which the contributor ceased office.

> Example: If a contributor ceased office in 2025, interest is calculated from Dec 31, 2015 to Dec 31, 2024, for each year of service.

---

#### **6. Survivor Benefits**

##### **(a) Upon Death of a Contributor**
- **Surviving Spouse** (married at time of ceasing office or cohabiting for ≥1 year before death):
  - Entitled to **half** of the immediate or deferred pension the contributor would have received.
  - Pension commences **immediately upon death**.

- **If no spouse is entitled**:
  - The **total contributions** (plus interest) are paid to the **estate**.
  - If the total exceeds the amount already paid to the contributor and spouse, the **excess is paid as a death benefit**.
  - If the excess is less than **$1,000**, the President of the Treasury Board may direct its distribution.

##### **(b) Election to Reduce Pension for Spousal Entitlement**
- If a former Lieutenant Governor **does not have a spouse or cohabiting partner** entitled to a pension under section 7, they may make an **election** (subject to regulations) to **reduce their own pension** so that the spouse or cohabiting partner becomes eligible.
- The reduction is governed by:
  - Regulations.
  - The amount of the pension reduced.
  - The amount paid to the beneficiary under subsection (2).
- Once made, the election is **binding** and **non-revocable**.
- The beneficiary is entitled to the pension only if:
  - The election is **not revoked**.
  - The person was **married** or **cohabiting in a conjugal relationship for ≥1 year** immediately before death.

> ❌ **Important**: A person who receives a pension under section 7 (survivor benefit) **cannot also receive** a pension under section 8.1 (reduction election).

---

#### **7. Payments and Administration**

- **Pension Payments**:
  - Paid in **equal monthly instalments in arrears**.
  - For periods less than a month: paid **pro rata**.
  - Continues **during the lifetime of the recipient** and **until the end of the month in which the recipient dies**.

- **Funding Source**: All pensions and returns are paid from the **Consolidated Revenue Fund**.

---

#### **8. Supplementary Retirement Benefits Account (SRBA)**

- A separate account established under the **Supplementary Retirement Benefits Act**.

- **Contribution Requirements**:
  - **Before 1977**: 0.5% of salary (half of 1%).
  - **From January 1977 onward**: 1% of salary (by reservation from salary).

- **Tax Treatment**: Contributions to the SRBA are deemed to be contributions to a **registered pension plan** under the *Income Tax Act*.

- **Accounting**: Any amount paid into the SRBA is **credited to that account**.

> Note: This is **in addition to** the main superannuation fund under this Act.

---

#### **9. Regulatory Authority**

The **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal government) may make regulations to:

- Specify **installment payment terms** for service periods.
- Determine **amounts recoverable** from pensions (e.g., for unpaid installments).
- Prescribe **medical examinations** to determine disability.
- Provide **forms** required under the Act.
- Regulate **elections under section 8.1**, including:
  - Timing, method, and conditions for making, revoking, or deeming revocation of an election.
  - The **amount of pension reduction**.
  - The **amount of pension payable** under subsection 8.1(2).
  - Any other matter necessary to implement the Act.

> These regulations are essential for operational clarity and enforcement.

---

#### **10. Diversion of Pension Payments (Garnishment)**

- If a court orders a former Lieutenant Governor to pay **financial support** (e.g., spousal or child support), **pensions payable under this Act may be diverted** to the recipient of the support order.
- This applies under **Part II of the Garnishment, Attachment and Pension Diversion Act**.

> This ensures that financial obligations to family members are prioritized over pension entitlements in certain legal contexts.

---

#### **11. Key Principles and Implications**

| Principle | Summary |
|--------|--------|
| **Fairness & Security** | Provides retirement security for long-serving Lieutenant Governors, recognizing their public service. |
| **Age and Disability as Key Triggers** | Immediate pension at age 60 or upon disability; deferred pension otherwise. |
| **Flexibility** | Option to return contributions (especially for those under 60). |
| **Spousal Protection** | Surviving spouses receive half of the pension; cohabiting partners may qualify. |
| **Election Mechanism** | Allows contributors to protect partners' pension rights through a strategic reduction. |
| **Funding & Compliance** | Contributions are mandatory until five years of service, then exempt; interest accrues on returns. |
