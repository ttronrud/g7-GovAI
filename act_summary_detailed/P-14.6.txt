### ðŸ”¹ **3. Qualifying Criteria for a New Variety**

A plant variety may only be granted plant breederâ€™s rights if it meets **all four conditions**:

1. **Novelty**  
   - The variety must not have been sold or publicly available by the breeder or their legal representative:
     - **In Canada**: Within the prescribed period before the filing date:
       - **Recently prescribed categories**: Period preceding filing date.
       - **All other varieties**: One year before filing date.
     - **Outside Canada**: 
       - **Trees or vines**: Six years before filing date.
       - **All other plants**: Four years before filing date.
   - *Exception*: Regulations may exclude certain classes of sales from being considered "sales" for novelty purposes.

2. **Distinctness**  
   - The variety must be clearly distinguishable from all known varieties at the time of filing (i.e., at the filing date).

3. **Stability**  
   - The variety must remain true to type after repeated propagation.  
   - If a specific propagation cycle is defined, the variety must remain consistent after each cycle.

4. **Homogeneity**  
   - The variety must be sufficiently uniform so that variations in characteristics are:
     - Predictable,
     - Describable, and
     - Commercially acceptable.

> âœ… A variety is "sufficiently homogeneous" if, when propagated in large quantities, variations are not significant enough to affect commercial viability.

### ðŸ”¹ **4. Exclusive Rights of the Holder**

The holder of plant breederâ€™s rights has **exclusive rights** to perform the following acts (under sections 5 to 5.2):

#### (a) To Produce and Reproduce Propagating Material  
- The right to produce and reproduce seeds, cuttings, or other propagating material of the variety.

#### (b) To Condition Propagating Material  
- To prepare or treat propagating material for propagation purposes.

#### (c) To Sell Propagating Material  
- To sell, offer for sale, or exchange propagating material.

#### (d) To Export or Import  
- To import or export propagating material of the variety.

#### (e) To Use Propagating Material for Breeding  
- To use propagating material repeatedly to produce another plant variety (e.g., in breeding programs), if necessary.

#### (f) Use in Ornamental Plants  
- For ornamental plants marketed for non-propagation purposes, the holder may use the plant or part as propagating material for cut flowers or ornamental plants.

#### (g) To Stock Propagating Material  
- To store propagating material for the purpose of carrying out any of the above acts.

#### (h) To Authorize Others  
- To conditionally or unconditionally authorize others to perform any of the above acts.
