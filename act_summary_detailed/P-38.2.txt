**Detailed Summary of the *Department of Public Works and Government Services Act***  

---

### **Overview**

The *Department of Public Works and Government Services Act* (commonly referred to as the **DPWGS Act**) establishes and defines the structure, powers, duties, and functions of the **Department of Public Works and Government Services (DPWGS)** — a central federal agency within the Government of Canada. Enacted by Her Majesty, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons, this Act provides a comprehensive legal framework for the department’s role in supporting the efficiency, economy, and integrity of federal operations.

The Department functions as a **common service agency**, providing shared services across various federal departments, boards, and agencies. It is tasked with managing the procurement of goods, services, and infrastructure, as well as delivering critical administrative and technical support.

---

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Title and Definitions**

- **Title**: The Act is officially cited as the *Department of Public Works and Government Services Act*.
- **Key Definitions**:
  - **Appropriate Minister**: As defined in section 2 of the *Financial Administration Act*; refers to a minister with responsibility over a specific department or function.
  - **Crown Corporation**: As defined in section 2 of the *Financial Administration Act*.
  - **Department**: As defined in section 2 of the *Financial Administration Act*.
  - **Federal Real Property / Federal Immovable**: As defined in the *Federal Real Property and Federal Immovables Act*.
  - **Materiel**: Includes goods, equipment, machinery, and supplies.
  - **Minister**: Refers specifically to the **Minister of Public Works and Government Services** (only in English).
  - **Public Work**: Any work or property under the management or control of the Minister.

> These definitions ensure consistency with existing federal administrative and property laws.

---

### **2. Establishment of the Department**

- The Department of Public Works and Government Services is formally established as a department of the Government of Canada.
- The **Minister of Public Works and Government Services** is appointed by commission under the **Great Seal of Canada**.
- The Minister holds office at the **discretion of the Governor in Council** (i.e., during "pleasure").
- The Minister serves as the **Receiver General for Canada**, a key financial and administrative role.

> This dual role underscores the department’s central role in both operational and financial governance.

---

### **3. Deputy Minister**

- The Governor in Council may appoint a **Deputy Minister of Public Works and Government Services**.
- The Deputy Minister serves during pleasure and acts as the **deputy head** of the Department.
- The Deputy Minister is also the **Deputy Receiver General**.

> This ensures a clear chain of command and administrative continuity.

---

### **4. Role as a Common Service Agency**

- The Department operates as a **common service agency** for the federal government.
- Its primary mission is to **provide support services** to other departments, boards, and agencies, including:
  - Procurement of materiel and services (including construction)
  - Printing and publishing services
  - Accommodation and facilities
  - Telecommunications planning and coordination
  - Architectural and engineering advice
  - Translation and language services

> This model promotes **efficiency, cost savings, and standardization** across government operations.

---

### **5. Powers, Duties, and Functions of the Minister**

The Minister has broad powers over matters within Parliament’s jurisdiction, **not assigned to other departments**, including:

| Function | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Procurement** | Acquire articles, supplies, machinery, equipment, and services (including construction) for departments. |
| **Planning & Organization** | Plan and organize the supply of materiel and services, including preparation of specifications, standards, cataloging, quality assurance, and inventory management. |
| **Construction & Maintenance** | Oversee construction, maintenance, and repair of public works, federal real property, and federal immovables. |
| **Facilities** | Provide accommodation and other facilities for departments. |
| **Telecommunications** | Plan and coordinate telecommunications services for federal institutions. |
| **Architectural/Engineering Services** | Provide advice on design, codes, standards, and technologies affecting public works. |
| **Translation Services** | Deliver translation and related services to support multilingual government operations. |

> These powers are designed to **centralize procurement and service delivery** to improve efficiency and reduce duplication.

---

### **6. Environmental Considerations in Procurement**

- When planning construction, maintenance, or repair, the Minister **must consider potential reductions in greenhouse gas emissions** and other environmental benefits.
- The Minister may **allow the use of wood or other sustainable materials** (e.g., renewable products, sustainable resources) if such use contributes to environmental improvements.

> This reflects Canada’s commitment to **sustainable development and climate action** in public infrastructure.

---

### **7. Exclusions from Materiel Management Powers**

- The Minister’s authority to manage materiel **does not extend to exclusively military supply systems**.
- This exclusion ensures that **military operations are not subject to civilian procurement oversight**.

> A critical safeguard for national defense and military logistics.

---

### **8. Delegation of Powers**

The Minister may delegate powers, duties, or functions to:

1. **An appropriate minister** (e.g., for a department under their jurisdiction).
2. **The chief executive** of a department (if the Minister is the appropriate minister).
3. **Sub-delegation**:
   - The appropriate minister may sub-delegate to the department’s chief executive.
   - The chief executive may further sub-delegate to any person under their jurisdiction.

> This creates a **flexible delegation structure**, enabling tailored management while maintaining accountability.

> *Note: Delegation is subject to conditions and oversight, particularly under Treasury Board regulations.*

---

### **9. Exclusions from Section 9 (Delegation Rules)**

Section 9 (delegation rules) **does not apply to**:
- Departments listed in **Part I of Schedule VI to the Financial Administration Act** (e.g., departments with specific governance structures).
- The **Department established under the Shared Services Canada Act**.

> This recognizes the unique status and operational model of certain departments.

---

### **10. Exemptions from Section 9**

The Minister may, by **order**, exempt the following from the delegation rules:
- Departments **not named in Schedule VI** to the Financial Administration Act.
- Departments **over which the Minister does not preside** but for which the Minister is the **appropriate minister**.

> Provides flexibility in managing non-core or decentralized departments.

---

### **11. Administration of Federal Real Property and Immovables**

- The Minister has **exclusive administrative authority** over all **federal real property and immovables** **except**:
  - Those under the administration of another minister, board, or agency.
  - Those in **Yukon, the Northwest Territories, or Nunavut** (which are managed separately).
- The Minister may:
  - Incurred expenditures
  - Perform or have services performed
  - Carry out work on federal real property or immovables
  - Enter into agreements with private owners (with their consent)

> This consolidates control over federal assets and ensures centralized management.

> *Note: This does not affect the operation of the Seized Property Management Act.*

---

### **12. Powers as Receiver General**

- In this capacity, the Minister exercises **all powers and duties assigned to the Receiver General by law**.
- Includes responsibilities related to **payroll administration**, **employee benefits**, and **pension plan services**.
- The Minister must provide administrative services for:
  - Disbursement of pay to federal employees
  - Employee benefit and superannuation/pension plans

> This strengthens the department’s role in **human resources and financial administration**.

---

### **13. Production and Distribution of Film and Media**

- The Minister may provide **film, video, and cinematographic works** services to departments.
- **No department may initiate the production of a motion picture film** without the Minister’s authority.
- All film production must be undertaken by the Minister **unless** the Minister determines it is in the public interest and authorizes otherwise.

> Ensures **centralized control over national film production**, promoting consistency and public interest.

---

### **14. Provision of Consulting and Support Services**

The Minister may provide the following services to departments, boards, and agencies:

| Service | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Management consulting | Strategic advice on operations |
| Information management & IT | Systems, data, and digital services |
| Accounting & auditing | Financial oversight |
| Financial services | Budgeting, forecasting |
| Real property services | Acquisition, management, disposal |
| Architectural & engineering services | Design, codes, standards |
| Other services | Any within the Minister’s powers |

> This makes the Department a **one-stop support hub** for technical and operational needs.

---

### **15. Authority to Act on Behalf of Others**

If authorized under any Act of Parliament, the Minister may:
- Act **for or on behalf of any department, board, or Crown corporation**
- Act **on behalf of any government, body, or person in Canada or abroad**, with **Governor in Council approval** (general or specific)

> This broadens the department’s **inter-agency and international reach**.

---

### **16. Charging for Services**

- The Minister may **charge fees** for services provided under this Act or other laws.
- Charges are subject to **regulations made by the Treasury Board**.

> Introduces a **revenue-generating model** to fund services and ensure cost recovery.
