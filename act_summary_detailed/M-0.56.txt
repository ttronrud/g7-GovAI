**Detailed Summary of the *Marine War Risks Act***

### **Overview**
The *Marine War Risks Act* is a Canadian statute designed to ensure the continuity of maritime commerce by establishing a government-backed insurance mechanism to cover vessels and cargo against risks arising from war, civil unrest, piracy, and related hostilities. The Act creates a dedicated financial account â€” the **Marine War Risks Insurance Account** â€” to fund insurance or reinsurance obligations in cases where private insurers may be unwilling or unable to provide coverage, particularly during times of conflict or instability.

The primary objective is to **prevent disruptions in international trade and transportation** by ensuring that ships and goods are protected from war-related losses, even when private insurers are unwilling or unable to provide coverage.

## **Core Provisions and Functions**

### **1. Insurance/Reinsurance by the Minister**
- The **Minister of Transport** may enter into agreements (either directly or through a person or association) to **insure or re-insure vessels or cargo** against war risks.
- These agreements may be structured in any form and include terms and conditions as prescribed by **regulations** or approved by the **Governor in Council**.
- The purpose is to **prevent vessel layups** and **ensure uninterrupted commerce** due to lack of private insurance coverage during times of conflict.

### **2. Regulatory Authority of the Governor in Council**

- The types of insurance or reinsurance allowed under the Act;
- The form, terms, and conditions of agreements;

  - Specific or general classifications;
  - When cargo begins or ends (e.g., at loading/unloading, at port entry/exit);
