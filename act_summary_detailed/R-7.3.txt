**Detailed Summary of the *Restoring Rail Service Act*, 2012**

---

### **Overview**

The *Restoring Rail Service Act* (2012) is a Canadian federal statute enacted to address a critical disruption in rail service operations caused by a prolonged labor dispute between the **Canadian Pacific Railway (CPR)** and the **Teamsters Canada Rail Conference (TCRC)**. The Act was passed by the Parliament of Canada with the advice and consent of both the Senate and the House of Commons and came into force on **June 1, 2012**.

Its primary objective is to **restore and maintain essential rail service operations** by ensuring that employees return to work and that no further disruptions occurâ€”such as strikes or lockoutsâ€”during a legally defined transitional period. It also establishes a structured, neutral arbitration process to resolve outstanding disputes over collective agreements, thereby preventing further industrial action.

## **Key Provisions and Detailed Summary**

---

### **1. Citation and Definitions**

- **Title**: The Act is known as the *Restoring Rail Service Act*.
- **Key Definitions**:
  - **Arbitrator**: An individual appointed under subsection 8(1) to resolve disputes over collective agreements.
  - **Collective Agreement**: Any agreement between CPR and the TCRC that expired on **December 31, 2011**.
  - **Employee**: A person employed by CPR and bound by a collective agreement.
  - **Employer**: The **Canadian Pacific Railway**.
  - **Union**: The **Teamsters Canada Rail Conference**, representing two bargaining units:
    - Rail traffic controllers
    - Running trades employees
  - **Minister**: The **Minister of Labour**.
  
> *General Rule*: Unless otherwise stated, terms used in the Act have the same meaning as in **Part I of the Canada Labour Code**.

---

### **2. Immediate Restoration of Rail Service (Section 3)**

Upon coming into force:
- The **employer (CPR)** must **continue or resume rail service operations without delay**.
- Every **employee** must **continue or resume their duties without delay**, if required.

---

### **3. Prohibition of Dismissals or Disciplinary Actions (Section 4)**

It is **prohibited** for:
- The employer or any of its officers/representatives to:
  - Impede an employee from complying with their work duties after the Act comes into force.
  - Discharge or discipline any employee **because** they participated in a strike **before** the Act came into force.

ðŸ‘‰ This protects employees from retaliation for prior strike activity, ensuring a safe return to work.

---

### **4. Union Responsibilities (Section 5)**

The union and its officers must:
- **Immediately notify employees** that rail service will resume and that employees are required to return to work.
- Take **all reasonable steps** to ensure employees comply with the duty to return to work.
- **Refrain from any conduct** that might discourage or discourage employees from complying (e.g., public statements or campaigns).

ðŸ‘‰ This promotes cooperation and avoids undermining the restoration of service through internal pressure or misinformation.

---

### **5. Extension of Collective Agreements (Section 6)**

- The term of each expired collective agreement is **automatically extended** to include the period from **January 1, 2012**, until a **new collective agreement** comes into effect.
- The extended agreement is:
  - **Effective and binding** on both parties, even if it contradicts earlier terms in the original agreement or the Canada Labour Code.
  - **Treated as if it were the original term of the agreement**, so that Part I of the Canada Labour Code applies to the extended period as if it were the original term.

ðŸ‘‰ This prevents immediate breakdown of employment rights and provides legal continuity during the transition.

---

### **6. Prohibition of Strikes and Lockouts (Section 7)**

Until the new collective agreement is finalized:
- **No lockout** can be declared by the employer or its representatives.
- **No strike** can be declared or authorized by the union or its representatives.
- **No employee** in the affected bargaining unit may participate in a strike.

ðŸ‘‰ This creates a **temporary freeze on industrial action**, ensuring stability and uninterrupted rail service.

---

### **7. Arbitration Process (Section 8)**

- The **Minister of Labour** must appoint an **arbitrator** for each expired collective agreement.
- The arbitrator will be responsible for resolving **all disputes** related to the amendment or revision of the collective agreement that were in dispute at the time of appointment.

#### Powers of the Arbitrator:
- Has all powers and duties of an arbitrator under:
  - Paragraphs 60(1)(a) and (a.2) to (a.4)
  - Section 61 of the **Canada Labour Code**
- Must deliver a decision **within 90 days** of appointment (or a longer period approved by the Minister).
- Must submit a **report to the Minister** detailing the decision.

ðŸ‘‰ This establishes a neutral, legally binding process to resolve disputes without further strikes or lockouts.

### **15. Penalties for Violations (Section 16)**

- **Individual Offence**:
  - Anyone who violates the Act is guilty of an offence punishable on **summary conviction**.
  - Fine: 
    - **Up to $50,000** if the individual was acting as an officer/representative of the employer or union.
    - **Up to $1,000** in all other cases.
  - Fine is assessed **per day or part of a day** the offence continues.

- **Corporate Offence**:
  - If the **employer or union** violates the Act:
    - Guilty of an offence punishable on summary conviction.
    - Fine: **Up to $100,000 per day or part of a day** the violation continues.

ðŸ‘‰ These penalties serve as strong deterrents against violations such as lockouts, strikes, or retaliation.
