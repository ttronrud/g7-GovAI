**Detailed Summary of the *Canadian Radio-television and Telecommunications Commission Act***

---

### **Overview**
The *Canadian Radio-television and Telecommunications Commission Act* (CRTC Act) establishes, governs, and outlines the structure, powers, responsibilities, and operational procedures of the **Canadian Radio-television and Telecommunications Commission (CRTC)** — a federal regulatory body responsible for overseeing broadcasting, telecommunications, and related services in Canada. The Act is foundational in ensuring that Canada’s media and communication sectors operate fairly, efficiently, and in the public interest.

This summary provides a comprehensive, section-by-section analysis of the key provisions of the Act, focusing on governance, eligibility, duties, reporting obligations, and regulatory scope.

---

## **1. Title and Citation**
- The Act is formally titled: **"The Canadian Radio-television and Telecommunications Commission Act"**.
- It may be cited as such, establishing its legal standing within the Canadian legal framework.

---

## **2. Key Definitions**
The Act defines several critical terms used throughout the legislation:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Broadcasting** | Same meaning as in the *Broadcasting Act* (radiodiffusion) |
| **Chairperson** | Designated member by the Governor in Council under subsection 6(1); the chief executive officer of the Commission |
| **Commission** | Refers to the CRTC itself |
| **Member** | A member of the Commission; includes both full-time and part-time members |
| **Minister** | A member of the Queen’s Privy Council designated by the Governor in Council for the purposes of this Act |
| **Telecommunications undertaking** | Any business operating in the telecommunications field within Canada or on Canadian-registered vessels/aircraft |
| **Vice-Chairperson** | Any member designated by the Governor in Council to serve as a Vice-Chairperson |

> **Note**: Several older offices such as "Executive Committee," "Chairman," "Vice-Chairman," and "radiocommunication" have been **repealed** (removed) over time, reflecting modernization and restructuring of the Commission’s governance.

---

## **3. Structure and Appointment of the Commission**

### **Number of Members**
- The Commission consists of **not more than 13 members**, appointed by the **Governor in Council** (i.e., the federal Cabinet).

### **Term of Office**
- Each member serves for a term **not exceeding five years**.
- Members may be **removed at any time by the Governor in Council for cause** (e.g., misconduct, breach of duty).
- **Reappointment is eligible**, subject to section 5 (eligibility conditions).

### **Duties and Commitment**
- A member must **devote their full time** to the performance of duties under the Act.

### **Eligibility Requirements**
A person is **not eligible** to be appointed or continue as a member if:
1. They are **not a Canadian citizen** who is **ordinarily resident in Canada**.
2. They are **directly or indirectly**:
   - Engaged in a **telecommunications undertaking** (as owner, shareholder, director, officer, partner, etc.), or
   - Have **any pecuniary or proprietary interest** in:
     - A telecommunications undertaking, or
     - The **manufacture or distribution of telecommunication apparatus**.

> **Exception**: Distribution of such apparatus is allowed if it is **incidental to general merchandising** (e.g., retail goods).

### **Disposal of Prohibited Interests**
- If a member acquires a prohibited interest (by will or succession) **for their own benefit**, they must **absolutely dispose of that interest within three months**.

---

## **4. Leadership and Governance**

### **Designation of Leadership**
- The Governor in Council designates:
  - **One member** as **Chairperson**
  - **Two members** as **Vice-Chairpersons**

### **Role of the Chairperson**
- The **Chairperson is the chief executive officer** of the Commission.
- Has **supervision and direction** over the Commission’s work and staff.
- Presides over meetings of the Commission.

### **Succession and Delegation**
- If the Chairperson is absent or incapacitated, or if the office is vacant:
  - The Commission may authorize **one Vice-Chairperson** to act as Chairperson.
- If both Chairperson and Vice-Chairpersons are absent or unable to act:
  - The Commission may authorize **one or more members** to act as Chairperson.

> This ensures continuity of leadership and operational stability.

---

## **5. Compensation and Expenses**

### **Salary**
- Each member is paid a **salary fixed by the Governor in Council**.

### **Travel and Living Expenses**
- Members are entitled to receive **travel and living expenses** incurred in performing duties.
- These are **fixed by Commission by-law**, subject to approval by the Minister (as required under section 10(4)).

---

## **6. Staff and Public Service Status**

### **Appointment of Officers and Employees**
- Officers and employees necessary for the Commission’s operations are appointed in accordance with the **Public Service Employment Act**.

### **Public Service Status**
- Members of the Commission are **deemed to be persons employed in the public service** for the purposes of:
  - The **Public Service Superannuation Act** (entitlement to pensions)
  - Regulations under the **Aeronautics Act** (treated as federal public administration employees)

> This grants them benefits and protections equivalent to other federal public servants.

---

## **7. Location and Regional Offices**

### **Head Office**
- The Commission’s head office must be located in the **National Capital Region** (as defined in the *National Capital Act* schedule), or in another location designated by the Governor in Council.

### **Regional Offices**
- The Governor in Council may direct the Commission to establish **regional offices** across Canada.
- The Commission must comply with such directions.

### **Residency Requirements**
- Full-time members must reside:
  - In the National Capital Region, or
  - Within a distance determined by the Governor in Council.

> If a member is assigned to a **regional office**, they must reside in that region and within a distance set by the Governor in Council.

---

## **8. Meetings and Quorum**

### **Frequency of Meetings**
- The Commission must meet **at least six times per year**.

### **Quorum**
- A **majority of members in office** constitutes a quorum.

### **Remote Participation**
- Members may participate in meetings via **telephone or other communication facilities** that allow all participants to hear each other.
- Such participation is **deemed as being physically present** for all purposes of the Act.

> This supports flexibility and remote engagement, especially for members in regional offices.

---

## **9. By-Laws**

The Commission may make by-laws on the following matters:

1. **Calling of meetings**  
2. **Conduct of business**, including:
   - Establishment of **special and standing committees**
   - Delegation of duties to committees
   - Fixing quorums for committee meetings  
3. **Travel and living expenses** for members

> **Requirement**: By-laws under paragraph (1)(c) (travel/living expenses) **must be approved by the Minister** before coming into effect.

---

## **10. Powers and Objectives**

### **Powers Under the Broadcasting Act**
- The Commission’s powers and duties in relation to **broadcasting** are **fully defined and delegated** by the *Broadcasting Act*.

### **Powers Under the Online News Act**
- The Commission exercises powers and performs duties under the **Online News Act**, which governs online news content and access.

### **Powers Under the Telecommunications Act and Related Acts**
- Full-time members and the Chairperson exercise powers and duties under:
  - The **Telecommunications Act**
  - Any **special Act** defined in subsection 2(1) of the Telecommunications Act
  - An Act promoting economic efficiency by regulating activities that discourage reliance on electronic means (e.g., paper-based commerce)

> This reflects the Commission’s evolving role in regulating digital and electronic commerce.

---

## **11. Committee Authority**

Members may make by-laws to:
- Establish **special and standing committees**
- Delegate powers, duties, and functions to those committees
- Fix quorums for committee meetings

> **Key Provision**: Any action taken by a committee in the exercise of delegated powers is **deemed to be an action of the full Commission**.

> This allows for efficient delegation and operational flexibility.

---

## **12. Annual Reporting Requirements**

The Commission is required to submit an **annual report to the Minister** within **three months after the end of each fiscal year**.

### **Content of the Report**
The report must include:

### **A. Broadcasting and Accessible Canada Act Inquiries and Orders**
- Number of inquiries conducted under subsection 12(1) of the *Broadcasting Act*:
  - Related to **barriers to accessibility** (identification, prevention, removal)
  - Related to **sections 42–44 of the Accessible Canada Act**
- Number of orders issued under subsection 12(2) of the *Broadcasting Act*:
  - On accessibility barriers
  - Under sections 42–44 of the Accessible Canada Act
