**Detailed Summary of the *Budget Implementation Act, 2009***  

The *Budget Implementation Act, 2009* is a comprehensive piece of federal legislation enacted by Her Majesty in Right of Canada, with the advice and consent of the Senate and House of Commons. It serves as the enabling and implementing legislation for key components of the federal government’s 2009 budget, particularly focusing on fiscal policy, infrastructure investment, economic development, social programs, and regulatory reform. The Act is cited as the *Budget Implementation Act, 2009* and contains numerous amendments to existing statutes, the creation of new regulatory frameworks, and specific funding allocations to provinces and territories.

---

### **1. General Provisions and Cite**
- The Act is formally titled the *Budget Implementation Act, 2009*.
- It is structured as a legislative instrument that implements fiscal and policy measures outlined in the 2009 federal budget.

---

### **2. Transitional Provisions and Effective Dates**
The Act includes detailed transitional provisions specifying when various sections come into force, ensuring continuity and clarity in implementation:

- **Sections 123 to 127 and subsection 220(1)**: Deemed to have come into force on **September 8, 2008**.
- **Sections 122 and 128 to 219 and subsection 220(2)**: Deemed to have come into force on **January 28, 2009**.
- **Subsection 224(1)**: Deemed to have come into force on the **second Sunday before royal assent** (a key date for implementation).
- **Subsection 224(2)**: Comes into force on **September 12, 2010**.
- **Section 254**: Deemed to have come into force on **January 27, 2009**.
- **Provisions of Division (excluding sections 235, 254, and subsection 257(1))**: Come into force on a day or days to be fixed by **order of the Governor in Council**.
  - Sections 235 and 257(1): In force on **March 12, 2009**.
  - Sections 233, 234, 236–244, 245(1)–(6), 246–253, 255, 256, 257(2), and 258: In force on **July 1, 2009** (via Statutory Instrument SI/2009-57).
  - Subsection 245(7): In force on **November 1, 2010** (via SI/2010-80).
- **Division (specific provisions)**: Comes into force on **April 1, 2009**, or **later**, if the Act receives royal assent on a later date.
- **Sections 270, 273, 277, 280, 283, 285, and 290**: Come into force on a day to be fixed by order of the Governor in Council.  
  - These were implemented on **July 1, 2010** (via SI/2010-31).

### **3. Employment Insurance (EI) Reforms**
A significant focus of the Act is on modifying the Employment Insurance program, particularly regarding benefit duration and eligibility:

- **Benefit Periods and Maximum Weeks**: For claimants whose benefit period has not ended before the **second Sunday before royal assent** and who do not begin benefits after **September 11, 2010**, the maximum number of weeks of benefits is determined according to **Schedule 10**.
- This reform aims to ensure fairness and stability in EI benefit calculations during transitional periods, especially for those who may have been affected by changes in eligibility or benefit duration.

---

### **4. Canadian Securities Regulation Regime**
The Act establishes a framework for a **national securities regulation regime** and the creation of a **Canadian regulatory authority** to harmonize and strengthen financial regulation across provinces and territories.

- **Canadian Securities Regulation Regime Transition Office Act** is enacted as part of this initiative.
- **Section 297** (a key transitional provision) comes into force on a day to be fixed by the Governor in Council.  
  - It was implemented on **July 13, 2009** (via SI/2009-60), establishing a transition office to manage the shift toward a unified national securities regulatory body.

- The **Minister of Finance** has authority to:
  - Make **direct payments up to $328 million** (or an amount specified in an appropriation Act) to provinces and territories for establishing the Canadian securities regulation regime and regulatory authority.
  - Enter into **agreements with provinces and territories** regarding securities regulation.

> This reflects a major policy shift toward centralized, consistent financial regulation across Canada, reducing regulatory fragmentation and improving investor protection.

### **6. Expenditure Restraint and Public Sector Compensation**
The Act includes provisions related to public sector financial discipline and compensation:

- The **Expenditure Restraint Act** is enacted with **Schedules 1 and 2** attached in Schedule 11 of the Act.  
  - This Act likely imposes spending limits or fiscal constraints on certain public sector entities, reflecting fiscal responsibility measures introduced in the 2009 budget.

- The **Public Sector Equitable Compensation Act** is enacted, with transitional provisions to ensure fair and equitable compensation adjustments for public servants.  
  - Several sections (e.g., 394, 399–405) are subject to a future effective date set by the Governor in Council.  
  - Notably, **Section 400 was repealed before coming into force** (2013, c. 40, s. 469), and **Section 399 was repealed before coming into force** (2008, c. 20, s. 3).  
  - Sections 410, 429, and 442 come into force **one year after royal assent** (i.e., **March 12, 2010**).

> These provisions aim to balance public sector pay equity with fiscal constraints and ensure long-term financial sustainability.
