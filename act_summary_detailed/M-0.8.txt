## üìö **Detailed Summary of the Marine Transportation Security Act**

### **1. Purpose and Scope of the Act**
The *Marine Transportation Security Act* (MTSA) was enacted to enhance the security of marine transportation in Canada by establishing a legal framework for the prevention of unauthorized threats‚Äîsuch as weapons, explosives, and incendiaries‚Äîon vessels and at marine facilities. It applies to both Canadian-registered and foreign vessels operating in Canadian waters and offshore structures, with the goal of protecting people, goods, vessels, and critical infrastructure from acts of terrorism, sabotage, or unlawful interference.

The Act is designed to:
- Ensure the security of marine operations through screening, inspection, and monitoring.
- Provide flexibility for operators to develop their own security rules, subject to ministerial review and approval.
- Empower the Minister of Transport to take emergency action when threats are suspected.
- Establish clear accountability for non-compliance and security failures.

### **2. Key Definitions**

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Authorized Screening** | Any inspection, control, or search of persons or goods to prevent unauthorized weapons, explosives, or incendiaries on board vessels or at marine facilities. This includes measures under regulations, security measures, or rules. |
| **Canadian Ship** | A vessel registered under the *Canada Shipping Act, 2001*, or registered in Canada before August 1, 1936, under the *Merchant Shipping Act, 1894*. Includes all amendments to that Act. |
| **Goods** | Anything that may be taken on board a vessel‚Äîincluding personal belongings, baggage, and cargo. |
| **Marine Facility** | Any area, structure, or equipment used for vessel arrival, departure, movement, servicing, or handling of goods. Includes land, water, buildings, storage, and offshore installations. |
| **Minister** | Refers to the **Minister of Transport**; may also include an appointed official in the Department of Transport. |
| **Operator** | For vessels: owner (registered or not), beneficial interest holder, lessee, charterer, or master in charge. For marine facilities: person with charge, management, and control. |
| **Proposed Security Rule** | A rule submitted to the Minister for approval under section 10. |
| **Restricted Area** | A zone where access is limited to authorized individuals only. |
| **Screening Officer** | A person designated by the Minister to carry out authorized screening activities. |
| **Security Inspector** | A person designated by the Minister to inspect compliance with security rules and measures. |
| **Security Measure** | A measure formulated by the Minister (e.g., directives, inspections, restrictions). |
| **Security Rule** | A rule approved by the Minister, which may be adopted by operators as an alternative to ministerial measures. |
| **Tribunal** | The *Transportation Appeal Tribunal of Canada*, which handles appeals from decisions on designations and rule approvals. |
| **Vessel** | Includes all types of marine craft: ships, boats, rafts, seaplanes, dredges, floating homes, oil rigs, etc., regardless of propulsion method. |

> **Note**: Where a provision requires or authorizes the Minister to act, the Minister may delegate such authority to an appointed official in the Department of Transport. References to the Minister are interpreted to include such officials.

### **3. Application of the Act**

#### ‚úÖ Applies To:
- Vessels and marine facilities **within Canada**;
- **Canadian ships operating outside Canada**;
- Marine installations and structures **offshore**, as defined in sections 5 and 6 of the *Canadian Laws Offshore Application Act*.

#### ‚ùå Does Not Apply To:
- Vessels and facilities operated under the authority of the **Minister of National Defence** (e.g., military vessels);
- **Foreign military vessels**, to the extent the Minister of National Defence may exempt them.

> The Act **does not require or authorize** any person outside Canada to violate the laws of a foreign country, nor does it compel a Canadian ship to operate in violation of foreign law.

### **4. Regulatory Powers of the Governor in Council**

The Governor in Council (i.e., the federal government) may make **regulations** on matters including:
- Preventing unlawful interference with marine transportation;
- Requiring or authorizing **authorized screening**;
- Establishing **restricted areas**;
- Maintaining records of security operations (including copies of regulations, measures, and rules);
- Requiring records to be filed with or provided upon request by the Minister;
- Protecting and returning evidence seized without a warrant or vessels detained under the Act.

> **Penalty for contravention**:  
> - Individuals: fine up to **$5,000** or **6 months‚Äô imprisonment**;  
> - Corporations: fine up to **$100,000**.

### **5. Minister‚Äôs Powers and Responsibilities**

#### a) **Formulation of Security Measures**
- The Minister may **formulate security measures** (e.g., inspection protocols, access controls) to protect marine transportation.
- These measures may be **in addition to or instead of** regulations.

#### b) **Requirement to Carry Out Security Measures**
- The Minister may, by **written notice**, require operators of vessels or marine facilities to carry out security measures.
- The Minister may **carry out security measures directly** if it determines that current security is inadequate or non-compliant.

#### c) **Liability of Operators**
- Operators are **jointly and severally liable** to Her Majesty in right of Canada for **all costs incurred** by the Minister in carrying out security measures.

#### d) **Offences for Non-Compliance or Obstruction**
- **Failure to comply** with a security measure or **wilful obstruction** of a security officer is an offence.
- Penalties:
  - **On indictment**:
    - Individual: fine up to **$10,000** or **1 year imprisonment**;
    - Corporation: fine up to **$200,000**.
  - **On summary conviction**:
    - Individual: fine up to **$5,000** or **6 months‚Äô imprisonment**;
    - Corporation: fine up to **$100,000**.

### **6. Operator-Developed Security Rules (Alternative to Ministerial Measures)**

This section provides **operator flexibility** to develop and implement their own security rules, which can serve as an alternative to government-mandated security measures.

#### Key Provisions:
- Operators may **formulate rules** on any security-related matter.
- Before submission, the operator must **consult** individuals likely to be affected (e.g., crew, port staff).
- Must submit:
  - A **statement of persons consulted**;
  - A description of any **disagreements** expressed.

#### Submission and Approval Process:
- Within **120 days**, the Minister must decide whether to approve the rules.
- If approved:
  - Approval may be **subject to conditions** (e.g., reporting requirements).
  - The operator must **notify all consulted parties**.
  - The rules must be **implemented and maintained** until revoked.

#### If Not Approved:
- The Minister must provide **written reasons**.
- The operator may **revise and resubmit** within a period specified by the Minister.

> **Amendments** to existing rules follow the same process.

#### Revocation:
- The Minister may **revoke** approved rules at any time, either at the operator‚Äôs request or independently.

#### Offences for Non-Compliance with Approved Rules:
- Failure to implement approved rules or **wilful obstruction** of security rule enforcement is an offence.
- Penalties are **identical** to those for non-compliance with security measures:
  - On indictment: up to **$10,000 fine or 1 year imprisonment** (individual); up to **$200,000** (corporation).
  - On summary conviction: up to **$5,000** or **6 months‚Äô imprisonment**.

> ‚ö†Ô∏è **Important**: This provision **does not limit** the Minister‚Äôs authority to impose security measures. The Minister can still require measures even if rules are in place.

### **7. Exemptions and Disclosure Restrictions**

#### a) **Exemptions**
- The Minister may **exempt** a person, vessel, or facility from regulations, measures, or rules **if**:
  - The exemption is in the **public interest**; and
  - It is **not likely to compromise marine security**.
- Exemptions are granted on a case-by-case basis and require Ministerial judgment.

#### b) **Disclosure of Security Information**
- No person may disclose the **substance** of a security measure, rule, or proposed rule **unless**:
  - Authorized by the Minister;
  - Ordered by a court or body under section 14;
  - Required by law; or
  - Necessary to give effect to the rule.
- **Penalty for unauthorized disclosure**:
  - Summary conviction: up to **$5,000 fine or 6 months‚Äô imprisonment** (individual); up to **$100,000** (corporation).
