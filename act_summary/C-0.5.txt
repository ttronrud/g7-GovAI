The Canada and Taiwan Territories Tax Arrangement Act, 2016 establishes a tax agreement between Canada and Taiwan to avoid double taxation and prevent fiscal evasion, defining the tax treatment of income, dividends, and permanent establishments, while giving the Arrangement legal force in Canada during its effective period. It provides rules for determining residency, taxing income from property and business activities, and limits on tax on dividends, with provisions for mutual adjustments and compliance through competent authorities.
