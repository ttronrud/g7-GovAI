The National Research Council Act establishes the National Research Council of Canada as a corporation with broad powers to conduct scientific and industrial research, procure goods and services (including outside federal public administration), manage intellectual property, and operate research facilities, all while being accountable to the Governor in Council and subject to oversight by a Procurement Oversight Board. The Council is tasked with advancing research in key areas such as technology, agriculture, materials science, and public health, and operates as an agent of Her Majesty in right of Canada.
