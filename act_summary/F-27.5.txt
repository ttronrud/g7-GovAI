The Department of Foreign Affairs and International Trade is restructured and renamed the Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development, with a Minister of Foreign Affairs overseeing its operations, supported by separate Ministers for International Trade and International Development, who assist in promoting trade, development, and humanitarian assistance. The Act establishes a comprehensive framework for the Department’s powers, responsibilities, and administrative structure, including the management of diplomatic missions, international negotiations, and the coordination of Canada’s foreign policy, trade, and development activities.
