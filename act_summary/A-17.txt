The Auditor General Act establishes the appointment, duties, and independence of Canada’s Auditor General, who serves as the federal government’s principal auditor, responsible for examining financial statements, ensuring accountability of public funds, and reporting to Parliament on financial integrity, efficiency, and sustainable development practices. The Auditor General has broad powers to access information, conduct inquiries, and report on misuse of funds, while being protected from legal liability for actions taken in the course of their duties.
