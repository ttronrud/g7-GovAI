The Gwich’in Land Claim Settlement Act approves and gives effect to the 1992 Comprehensive Land Claim Agreement between the Government of Canada and the Gwich’in Tribal Council, recognizing and confirming Gwich’in rights to land, resources, and treaty rights, while vesting title to certain lands in the Gwich’in Tribal Council. The Act ensures the Agreement's validity under Section 35 of the Constitution Act, 1982, and establishes the legal framework for its implementation, including the powers of the Renewable Resources Board and the obligation to fund monetary commitments under the Agreement.
