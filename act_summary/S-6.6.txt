The shíshálh Nation Self-Government Act recognizes and enables the shíshálh Nation to exercise its inherent right of self-government, including full legal capacity to manage its lands, resources, and governance through a constitutionally established Council and District, while upholding Aboriginal rights and the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples. It formalizes the transfer of fee simple title to the Nation over its reserve lands and provides a framework for self-governance, land management, and legislative authority, subject to constitutional procedures and certain conditions.
