The Forgiveness of Debts (sub-Saharan Africa) Act forgives official development assistance debts owed by several sub-Saharan African countries to Canada under specific loan agreements, and the forgiven amounts are written off as budgetary expenditures. The Act applies to 12 loan agreements with Cameroon, 2 with the Congo, and similar agreements with Ivory Coast, Gabon, Ghana, Kenya, Madagascar, Nigeria, Senegal, Swaziland, Zaire, and Zambia, among others.
