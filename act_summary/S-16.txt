The Standards Council of Canada Act establishes the Standards Council of Canada as a federal corporation tasked with promoting voluntary standardization across Canada, coordinating private and public sector efforts, developing standards, accrediting organizations, and advancing economic and social benefits through quality and innovation. The Council is composed of representatives from provincial-territorial bodies, standards development organizations, and the private sector, and it operates with broad powers to develop policies, accredit organizations, and provide advice to the government on standards-related matters.
