The Sahtu Dene and Metis Land Claim Settlement Act approves and gives legal effect to the Comprehensive Land Claim Agreement between the Government of Canada and the Sahtu Dene and Metis, recognizing their rights to land, resources, and self-governance, while establishing the framework for implementation and enforcement of those rights. It confirms the validity of the agreement under Section 35 of the Constitution Act, 1982, and provides for the transfer of land title to designated Sahtu organizations.
