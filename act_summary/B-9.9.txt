The Business Development Bank of Canada Act establishes the Business Development Bank of Canada as a body corporate to support Canadian entrepreneurship by providing financial and management services, issuing securities, and making loans or investments to small and medium-sized enterprises, with specific eligibility criteria and governance structures. The Bank operates as an agent of the Crown, is governed by a Board of Directors with strict eligibility and term requirements, and has broad powers to lend, invest, provide services, and manage risks, all aimed at promoting economic development in Canada.
