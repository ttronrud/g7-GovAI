This Act gives legal effect to the Anishinabek Nation Governance Agreement, enabling the Anishinabek Nation and its member First Nations to make their own laws in areas such as governance, citizenship, culture, and language, while affirming their inherent right to self-government under Section 35 of the Constitution Act, 1982. The Agreement supersedes certain provisions of the Indian Act and is binding on all parties, with specific procedures for judicial review and interpretation.
