This regulationproclaims that the Canada-India Agreement on Social Security enters into force on August 1, 2015, enabling citizens of both countries to have their social security benefits—such as pensions and disability payments—recognized and paid based on creditable periods of contribution or residence, with specific rules for eligibility, totalization of periods, and mutual assistance between the two governments. It ensures that individuals who have worked or resided in either country can access benefits without reduction or suspension due to their presence in the other country.
