These regulations prohibit or regulate the export of certain hazardous substances listed in the Export Control List under the Canadian Environmental Protection Act, 1999, implementing international agreements like the Rotterdam, Stockholm, and Minamata Conventions. They require exporters to provide a notice of proposed export at least 7 or 15 days in advance, obtain specific export permits for most substances, and impose conditions based on the destination, use, and type of substance, with limited exceptions for research, personal use, or specific industrial purposes.
