These regulations establish zoning restrictions at Hall Beach Airport in the Northwest Territories, limiting the height of any structures or buildings on adjacent lands to the approach surfaces, outer surface, or transitional surfaces, ensuring safe aircraft operations. The regulations define key geographical elements such as the airport reference point, approach and transitional surfaces, and the outer surface, all based on the 1989 zoning plan, and apply to lands within a 4,000-meter radius of the airport reference point.
