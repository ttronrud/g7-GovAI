This regulationproclaims that the Canada-Philippines Agreement on Social Security enters into force on March 1, 1997, establishing reciprocal rights to social security benefits (such as pensions, disability, and death benefits) for nationals and residents of both countries, with specific rules on creditable periods, benefit calculation, and mutual cooperation between their respective authorities. Benefits are payable without reduction due to residency in the other country, and the agreement includes provisions for information sharing, dispute resolution through arbitration, and the use of a common currency or freely convertible currency for payments.
