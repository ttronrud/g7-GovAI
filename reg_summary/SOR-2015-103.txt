The Canada Agricultural Review Tribunal has established detailed rules governing the conduct of review proceedings, including language requirements, filing and service of documents, hearing procedures, evidence presentation, and timelines for decisions, all aimed at ensuring fairness, efficiency, and accessibility. These rules apply to administrative penalty reviews under the Agriculture and Agri-Food Administrative Penalties Act and provide clear procedures for admissibility, submissions, hearings, and decision-making, with provisions for confidentiality, language access, and impartiality.
