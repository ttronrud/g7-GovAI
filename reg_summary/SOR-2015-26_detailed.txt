**Detailed Summary of the *Railway Safety Management System Regulations, 2015* (S.C. 2012, c. 7, s. 30; S.C. 2012, c. 7, s. 37; R.S., c. 32 (4th Supp.))**

---

### **Overview**

The *Railway Safety Management System Regulations, 2015* are a set of administrative regulations made under the *Railway Safety Act* (RSA) by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Transport. These regulations establish **minimum safety management requirements** that all railway companies must develop, implement, and maintain to achieve the **highest level of safety** in their operations.

The regulations are structured into **three main parts**:

1. **Part 1**: General requirements applicable to all railway companies.
2. **Part 2**: Specific requirements for two types of local railway companies:
   - Those operating on **main tracks** (lines where railway equipment movement is authorized).
   - Those operating **exclusively on non-main tracks**.
3. **Part 3**: Consequential amendments, repeal of previous regulations, and enforcement date.

These regulations are designed to promote a **proactive, systematic, and evidence-based approach** to railway safety, emphasizing accountability, continuous improvement, employee involvement, and compliance with legal and technical standards.

---

## **Key Definitions**

The following terms are defined and used throughout the regulations:

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Accountable Executive** | The senior executive responsible for the operations of the railway company and accountable for the effectiveness of the safety management system (as per subsections 8(1) or 43(1)). |
| **Act** | Refers to the *Railway Safety Act*. |
| **Dangerous Goods** | As defined in the *Transportation of Dangerous Goods Act, 1992*. |
| **Main Track** | A railway line where the movement of railway equipment is authorized by the railway company. |
| **Non-Main Track** | Any railway line that is not a main track. |
| **Railway Occurrence** | A reportable event under the *Transportation Safety Board Regulations* (i.e., an incident that meets reporting thresholds). |

---

## **Core Safety Management System Requirements (Parts 1 & 2)**

The regulations mandate that every railway company establish and maintain a comprehensive **Safety Management System (SMS)** that includes the following **12 key processes**:

1. **Process for Accountability**  
   - A designated **accountable executive** must be appointed who is responsible for ensuring the safety management system is effective and achieves the highest level of safety.  
   - The company must provide the Minister with the name of this executive as soon as possible.  
   - Within 30 days of designation, the accountable executive must submit a **signed declaration accepting accountability**.  
   - The accountable executive may designate one or more individuals to manage specific processes, provided they hold a management position with relevant responsibilities and decision-making authority.  
   - These designated persons must report **annually** to the accountable executive on:  
     - Problems encountered in implementing procedures/plans/methods and how they are being resolved (or not).  
     - The effectiveness of the procedures in improving safety.

2. **Safety Policy**  
   - The company must develop and maintain a **written safety policy** approved and signed by the accountable executive.  
   - The policy must reflect the companyâ€™s commitment to promoting railway safety.  
   - It must be **reviewed annually** and communicated to all employees.

3. **Compliance with Instruments**  
   - The company must maintain a **list of all applicable safety instruments**, including:  
     - Regulations under the RSA  
     - Engineering standards approved by the Minister  
     - Rules established or approved by the Minister  
     - Exemptions granted under sections 22 or 22.1  
     - Notices under section 31 (containing orders)  
     - Ministerial orders or emergency directives under sections 32 or 33  
   - For each instrument, the list must include the **date of approval or issuance** (for standards/rules/exemptions/notices).  
   - The list must be **kept up to date** and show the date of the last revision.

4. **Procedure for Compliance Verification**  
   - A formal procedure must be in place to:  
     - Review and update the list of instruments.  
     - Verify compliance with all applicable regulations, standards, rules, notices, and exemptions.

5. **Railway Occurrence Reporting and Review**  
   - A procedure must be established for:  
     - Reporting railway occurrences to management.  
     - Reviewing the occurrence.  
   - The procedure must be **communicated to employees** so they know how and when to report incidents.

6. **Safety Concerns Analysis**  
   - The company must **continually analyze** its operations to identify safety concerns, including:  
     - Reports of railway occurrences  
     - Internal documentation related to incidents  
     - Injury reports  
     - Inspection results (by company or inspector)  
     - Employee reports of contraventions or hazards  
     - Safety complaints  
     - Data from safety monitoring technologies  
     - Findings from annual reports and audit reports  
   - A written procedure must be included in the SMS for conducting these analyses.

7. **Risk Assessment**  
   - A risk assessment must be conducted in the following circumstances:  
     - When a safety concern is identified through operational analysis.  
     - When the company proposes to begin transporting **dangerous goods** (new types or increased volume).  
     - When there is a proposed change that may affect public safety, personnel, property, or the environment, such as:  
       - Introduction/elimination of technology or railway work  
       - Changes to routes or volumes of dangerous goods  
       - Changes in workforce size or responsibilities  
   - The risk assessment must:  
     - Describe the triggering circumstances  
     - Identify and describe associated risks  
     - Specify affected persons and whether property/environment is impacted  
     - Assess the **likelihood** and **severity** of each risk  
     - Identify risks requiring **remedial action**  
     - Propose specific remedial actions  
   - **Consultation Requirement**: Before identifying risks requiring action, the company must consult with:  
     - Bargaining agents (if applicable), or  
     - Employees directly affected, or  
     - A representative selected by employees  
   - The identified risks and proposed actions must be **communicated to affected employees**.

8. **Remedial Action Implementation and Evaluation**  
   - The company must **implement** remedial actions for risks identified in the assessment.  
   - A procedure must be in place for:  
     - Selecting remedial actions  
     - Implementing them and evaluating their effectiveness  
   - **Consultation is required again** when evaluating effectiveness (same as in risk assessment).  
   - The company must ensure the actions reduce or eliminate the identified risks.

9. **Safety Targets and Initiatives**  
   - Each calendar year, the company must:  
     - Establish **specific, measurable safety targets** based on prior analyses and trends.  
     - Develop **initiatives** to achieve each target.  
   - Each initiative must be:  
     - Clearly described in writing  
     - Explained in writing as to how it contributes to achieving the target  
   - The targets and initiatives must be **communicated to all employees**.

10. **Employee Reporting of Contraventions and Hazards**  
   - A procedure must be established enabling employees to report:  
     - Violations of the Act or regulations  
     - Safety hazards  
   - This must be done **without fear of reprisal**.  
   - The company must develop a **written policy** to protect employees from reprisals.  
   - This policy and procedure must be developed **in collaboration with bargaining agents or employees** (or a representative).  
   - The policy and procedure must be **communicated to all employees**.

11. **Duty, Position, and Competency Requirements**  
   - The company must create a list that specifies:  
     - The **essential duties** required for safe operations  
     - The **positions** responsible for performing those duties  
     - The **skills and qualifications** required to perform each duty safely  
   - Every employee performing such duties must:  
     - Possess the required skills and qualifications  
     - Have knowledge of:  
       - Relevant instruments (regulations, standards, rules)  
       - Federal legislation affecting railway safety  
       - Internal procedures, standards, instructions, or bulletins that impact safety  
   - For **non-employee personnel** (e.g., contractors, visitors) authorized to access the railway, the same knowledge requirements apply.

12. **Verification, Supervision, and Training Methods**  
   - The company must include in its SMS:  
     - A **plan** to ensure employees have the required skills and knowledge  
     - A **method** to verify that employees have the necessary skills and knowledge  
     - A **method** to supervise employees performing critical duties  
     - A **method** to verify that non-employee access personnel have the required knowledge
