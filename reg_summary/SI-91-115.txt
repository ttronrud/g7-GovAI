This regulation establishes that the Canada-Netherlands Agreement on Social Security, along with its Supplementary Agreement, came into force on October 1, 1990, enabling the mutual recognition and totalization of social security creditable periods for nationals and other eligible persons of both countries, including entitlements to pensions, disability benefits, and survivor benefits without reduction due to residence in the other country. It also outlines specific rules for calculating benefits, including partial pensions and adjustments based on periods of residence or employment.
