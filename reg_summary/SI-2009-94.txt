This regulation withdraws specific tracts of territorial land, including subsurface rights, from disposal to establish the Saoyú–Æehdacho (Grizzly Bear Mountain and Scented Grass Hills) National Historic Site in the Northwest Territories, with the purpose of protecting and preserving the area's cultural and historical significance. The withdrawal applies to designated parcels totaling approximately 3,525 km², and certain mineral claims or leases related to these areas are exempted under specific conditions.
