These regulations establish strict licensing, safety, and security requirements for the handling of human pathogens and toxins in Canada, including definitions of controlled activities, licensing terms based on risk levels, mandatory reporting obligations, and stringent conditions for security clearances, including background checks and prohibitions on certain activities. The regulations prioritize public health and safety by requiring biosafety oversight, regular compliance monitoring, and strict controls on transfers, exports, and modifications to facilities involving high-risk biological materials.
