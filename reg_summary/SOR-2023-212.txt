These regulations establish the conditions under which goods shipped directly from beneficiary countries (such as Least Developed Countries, Australia, New Zealand, and others) can be imported into Canada at preferential tariff rates, requiring either a through bill of lading or documentary evidence of the shipping route and customs control. The regulations, which come into force on January 1, 2025, repeal the Mexico Deemed Direct Shipment Regulations and are made under subsection 17(2) of the Customs Tariff.
