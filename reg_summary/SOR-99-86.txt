This Canadian proclamation designates several countries as "designated states" under the Visiting Forces Act, making the Act applicable to those nations (except Part VI) and recognizing their civilian personnel employed by their forces as a civilian component of a visiting force, provided they are not stateless, nationals of non-designated states, or citizens/residents of Canada. The designation includes countries such as Albania, Austria, Bulgaria, and others, with specific references to earlier designations for Central and Eastern European states.
