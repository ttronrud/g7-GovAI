These regulations establish that, under subsection 178.23(1) of the Criminal Code, the Attorney General or Solicitor General must certify to a court by filing a signed certificate detailing the intercepted person's information, the expiration date of the authorization, any granted delays, and the date, place, and method of giving the notice. This certificate is treated as confidential and must be sealed and kept with the authorization packet as required by section 178.14.
