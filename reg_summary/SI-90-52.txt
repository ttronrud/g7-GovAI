This proclamation declares that the Agreement on Social Security between Canada and Iceland, signed in 1988, came into force on October 1, 1989, in accordance with the provisions of the Old Age Security Act and the mutual ratification of the agreement. It establishes rules for how residents of either country can qualify for social security benefits, including pensions, disability benefits, and survivor benefits, through the totalization of creditable periods of residence, employment, or contributions, and ensures reciprocal eligibility and payment of benefits under Canadian and Icelandic legislation.
