This proclamation confirms that the Agreement on Social Security between Canada and the Federal Republic of Germany, signed in 1985, came into force on April 1, 1988, and establishes reciprocal pension rights for nationals of both countries, including credit for periods of residence and contribution in one country toward eligibility and benefits in the other. It also outlines specific rules for calculating benefits, such as partial pensions, and ensures equal treatment for certain categories of individuals, including refugees and stateless persons.
