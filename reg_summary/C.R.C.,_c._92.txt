These regulations establish height restrictions for buildings and structures near Lethbridge Airport, Alberta, limiting all constructions to the elevation of specified surfaces—primarily a horizontal surface 150 feet above the airport reference point (3,002 feet above sea level), approach surfaces, and transitional surfaces—defined by precise geographic boundaries and measured from the airport’s runways. The restrictions apply to specific parcels of land adjacent to or within the airport’s vicinity, as detailed in a 1958 plan, excluding certain areas within a defined perimeter.
