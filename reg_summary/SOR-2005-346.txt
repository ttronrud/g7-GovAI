These regulations establish the conditions under which the Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) can collect, retain, use, and disclose passenger name record information for purposes related to national security, terrorism prevention, and the investigation of serious transnational crimes, with strict limits on retention and access to protect privacy and ensure accountability. Information may be retained for up to six years if related to suspected terrorism or serious transnational crime, and access is tightly controlled and subject to authorization and record-keeping requirements.
