This regulationproclaims that the Canada-Brazil Agreement on Social Security entered into force on August 1, 2014, establishing rules for recognizing and combining social security benefits (such as pensions and disability benefits) for individuals who have worked or resided in both countries, including provisions for period of coverage totalization and the non-reduction of benefits due to residence in the other country. It also outlines administrative cooperation, information exchange, and eligibility rules between the two countries.
