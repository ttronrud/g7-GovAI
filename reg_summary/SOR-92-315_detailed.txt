**Detailed Summary of the Subsidiaries Holding Company Shares (Insurance Companies) Regulations**

---

**Regulatory Context and Authority**  
These regulations were issued by His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance, under **paragraph 703(h) of the Insurance Companies Act** (*Act*). The regulations came into force on **June 1, 1992**, and are formally titled the **Subsidiaries Holding Company Shares (Insurance Companies) Regulations**. They are cited as **S.C. 1991, c. 47**.

The purpose of these regulations is to **allow insurance companies to permit their subsidiaries to hold shares in the parent company**, under specific conditions designed to ensure financial stability, prevent conflicts of interest, and maintain regulatory oversight.

---

**Key Definitions in the Regulations**

1. **Act**  
   Refers to the *Insurance Companies Act*, which provides the legal framework governing the operations, capital requirements, and regulatory obligations of insurance companies in Canada.

2. **Distribution**  
   Defined as "distribution" as set out in **section 296 of the Act**. This refers to the issuance or sale of shares or other securities by a company to the public or to specific investors. The term is critical because it triggers special rules regarding shareholding limits and disclosure.

3. **Regulated Securities Entity**  
   A financial institution whose primary business is dealing in securities (e.g., underwriting, brokerage, investment services), and which is regulated in its securities activities by:
   - An Act of Parliament,
   - A provincial legislature, or  
   - The laws of a foreign country.  
   This definition ensures that only entities operating under recognized regulatory regimes are eligible to hold shares in the parent insurance company.

---

**Main Provisions and Conditions**

**1. General Shareholding Limit (Section 3)**  
A company (parent insurance company) may permit a **subsidiary** that is a **regulated securities entity** to hold shares of the company **only if** the **aggregate value** of all such shares held by all subsidiaries (excluding shares referred to in section 76 of the Act) does **not exceed 1% of the companyâ€™s regulatory capital**.

- **Regulatory Capital**: This is the minimum capital required by law to ensure the financial soundness and solvency of the insurance company.
- **Aggregate Value**: The total value of the shares held across all subsidiaries must not exceed 1% of this regulatory capital.
- **Exclusion of Section 76 Shares**: Shares that are specifically mentioned in **section 76 of the Act** (which may relate to shares held by certain affiliates, such as in connection with mergers or capital restructuring) are excluded from this 1% cap.

---

**Purpose and Regulatory Objectives**

These regulations aim to:
- **Balance operational flexibility** with **regulatory prudence**.
- Allow insurance companies to leverage their subsidiaries (especially those involved in securities activities) in capital-raising efforts without compromising financial stability.
- Prevent potential misuse of shareholding to create conflicts of interest or to manipulate the companyâ€™s financial position.
- Ensure that any shareholding by subsidiaries remains within reasonable and transparent bounds.

---

**Key Limitations and Safeguards**

- The 1% cap applies to **all subsidiaries** that are regulated securities entities.
- The cap is based on **regulatory capital**, not market capitalization or book value, ensuring alignment with solvency and risk management standards.
- The exception for underwriting is **temporary and conditional**, not a permanent relaxation of the rule.
- The rules are designed to be **transparent and enforceable**, with clear definitions and conditions.

---

**Conclusion**

The *Subsidiaries Holding Company Shares (Insurance Companies) Regulations* provide a **structured and risk-managed framework** allowing insurance companies to permit their regulated securities subsidiaries to hold shares in the parent company. The core principle is **strict limitation** (1% of regulatory capital) to safeguard financial stability, with a **temporary exception** during share distributions when a subsidiary acts as an underwriter.

These regulations reflect a balanced approachâ€”enabling operational efficiency in capital markets while maintaining the integrity and independence of insurance companies under Canadian insurance law.
